**Headquarters at 606 Studios** : The band maintains its headquarters at 606 Studios, which they refer to as the "House of Foo." ## curiosities
**Self-Perceived Luck** : Despite his talent and ambition, Dave Grohl believes he is one of the luckiest humans alive, acknowledging that many factors beyond his control had to align perfectly for his career to unfold as it has. "He knows he's among the luckiest humans alive."
**Father's Career and Influence** : His father, Jim Grohl, was a journalist in Warren, Ohio, who gained recognition for covering the Kent State University Anti-Vietnam War student protests on May 4, 1970, where four students were killed by the National Guard. This led to a move to Washington, DC, in 1972 (specifically Springfield, Virginia). Jim later became a speechwriter and campaign manager for Republican politicians, including Senator Robert Taft, Jr., and contributed to the Republican National Convention. "His reporting was so good that it got him on the fast track to a bigger gig in Washington, DC."
**Parents' Divorce (1976)** : His parents, Jim and Virginia Grohl, divorced in 1976 when Dave was approximately seven years old, a separation that limited the influence of his father's conservative political views on him. "The marriage to Virginia fell apart when Dave was about seven. This was 1976."
**Progressive Political Stance** : Growing up under the influence of his hardcore conservative, Reaganite father, Dave Grohl ultimately adopted a more progressive political approach, partly because his parents' divorce in his early childhood prevented his father's lifelong conservatism from fully impacting him. "Therefore, it's safe to say that Dad's lifelong political conservatism didn't really have a chance to rub off on Dave, who eventually adopted a more progressive approach."
**Father's Death (2014)** : His father, Jim Grohl, passed away from cancer at the age of 75 on August 6, 2014. "Dave saw his father in a regular basis until he died of cancer at the age of 75 on August 6, 2014."
**Early Drum Set at Father's Home** : A bedroom at his father's house always contained a drum set, ready for Dave to play whenever he visited, indicating an early exposure and encouragement for his drumming. "there was a bedroom at Jim's house with a drum set waiting for Dave every time he visited."
**Mother's Profession and Support** : His mother, Virginia Grohl, worked as an English and drama teacher at Thomas Jefferson High School. She notably supported his decision to drop out of high school to pursue music, albeit with conditions. "Mum, Virginia Grohl, got a job as an English and drama teacher at Thomas Jefferson High School."
**First Album Acquisition** : The first album Dave Grohl ever acquired was a K-Tel "hits of the 70s" record, which he obtained after his mother brought home a standard school-issue record player from Fairfax County. "the first album that I got was this K-Tel hits of the 70s record."
**Early Musical Influences** : His musical journey began with "Frankenstein" by the Edgar Winter Group, which ignited his passion for "big guitar rock." He then explored The Beatles, followed by Rush (given by a cousin), then Devo and Kiss (before B-52s and Devo). Around age 12, he discovered hardcore and punk rock, immersing himself exclusively in these genres for years until around age 16, when he became solely focused on Led Zeppelin. "I just thought, this is the greatest. Then we went out and got some Beatles records. After that, my cousin gave me a Rush record."
**Pseudo-Stepdad's Record Collection** : His mother's boyfriend, Chip Donaldson, who was an English teacher and Vietnam veteran, moved in with his own record collection featuring music from the late 1960s and early 1970s, including artists like The Rolling Stones, Bob Dylan, and Grateful Dead. "It featured a lot of stuff from the late 60s and early 70s. Stones, Dylan, grateful dad, that kind of thing."
**Intensified Led Zeppelin Fascination** : Although he'd heard Led Zeppelin on AM radio, his true fascination with the band intensified after Chip Donaldson moved in. This was further solidified when neighborhood stoners gave him the first two Zeppelin albums, leading him to consider them "his band" and becoming a huge fan of John Bonham. "And it was through Chip that Dave was first properly exposed to Led Zeppelin."
**Led Zeppelin Tattoos** : He has three Led Zeppelin tattoos. The first, John Bonham's three-circle runic symbol from the fourth Led Zeppelin album, he tattooed himself at age 16. The second was done by an amateur artist in Amsterdam, and the third was paid for with his first check from Nirvana ($400), which he also used to buy a Nintendo and a BB gun. "Not only did he become a huge fan of drummer John Bonham, but today he has three Led Zeppelin tattoos, the first of which he did himself when he was 16."
**Pivotal B-52s Moment (1980)** : At age 11, on January 26, 1980, while watching Saturday Night Live with his older sister Lisa, he witnessed The B-52s perform "Rock Lobster." This performance profoundly impacted him, changing his life and inspiring him to embrace "weirdness" in music. "I remember that moment like some people remember the Kennedy assassination. That moment changed my life."
**Cousin Tracy Bradford's Punk Introduction** : His non-blood relative "cousin," Tracy Bradford, who was two years his senior and lived in Evanston, Illinois, introduced him to authentic punk rock culture. She appeared in bondage pants and motorcycle boots with an anti-P.C. t-shirt, showcasing a record collection including first singles from Misfits and Minor Threat, and connected him to the underground network of fanzines and show flyers from across the country. "She had an antiposti t-shirt and two's punk. I was just like, oh my God, I was so into it."
**First Concert Experience (1982/83)** : Attended his first live band performance at around age 13 (either 1982 or 1983) at the Cubby Bear in Chicago, where he saw Naked Ray Gun and Rights of the Accused, an experience facilitated by his cousin Tracy. "So the first show I'd ever seen was, it was Naked Ray Gunn and rights of the accused at a place called the Cubby Bear in Chicago."
**First Guitar (1981)** : Received his first guitar for Christmas in 1981, a 1963 Silvertone model from Sears that notably had an amplifier built directly into its case. "Mom got him his first guitar for Christmas in 1981. It was a 1963 Silvertone from Sears with the amp built into the case."
**First Band Naming (HG Hancock band)** : He came up with the name for his first band, the HG Hancock band, a Grade 5 bedroom project with Larry Hinkel. The name was a derivation of their gym teacher's name, Hinkel, combined with an initial for himself, inspired by Lynyrd Skynyrd. "Well H for Hinkel, G for Grohl and Hinkel because, well, that was the name of their gym teacher. It was Dave's idea."
**Early Multi-track Recording Method** : At ages 11 or 12, he devised a method to write and record four-track songs about his dog (Bee-Gee) and school. He used a boombox to record guitar, then played that tape through his mom's stereo while re-recording with the boombox to add drum tracks, layering instruments one by one. "I'd actually write and track songs with my mom's home stereo and my little boombox."
**Early Song Style** : His self-recorded songs from age 11 or 12, created as he was getting into punk rock, likely sounded more like bands such as DOA or Naked Ray Gun rather than The Beatles. "they probably sounded more like, I don't know, DOA or naked ragan or something like that than the Beatles or anything."
**Dog's Name** : His dog was named Bee-Gee, an homage to the Bee Gees. "By the way, Dave's dog's name was Bee-Gee as in the Bee-Gee's."
**First Public Performance (Treebeards)** : His first performance for strangers occurred at an open mic night at a local restaurant called Treebeards, alongside his friend Nick Christie. "This is where they got the performance from strangers for the very first time."
**Transition to Drums (1985)** : Around his 16th birthday in January 1985, as Freak Baby evolved into Mission Impossible, Dave Grohl transitioned from guitar to playing the drums. "By this time, David moved to Drums, and this is where we finally get to Dave's earliest recordings that we can find."
**Joining Scream (1987)** : In 1987, he became a fan of the DC hardcore band Scream. Upon seeing a notice for a new drummer at a music store, he contacted the band, auditioned, and secured what he considered his "dream gig" by the end of the month, replacing their long-time drummer. "He tore the note down, contacted the band, auditioned, and got what he thought was his dream gig by the end of that month."
**Departure from Dane Bramage** : He abruptly left his band Dane Bramage to join Scream, an offer he "couldn't refuse," which led to hard feelings within Dane Bramage and the band's dissolution. "that band died because Dave bailed. He got an offer that he just couldn't refuse."
**Age Deception to Play Bars** : At age 17, when he joined Scream, he was underage for playing in bars, so he lied about his age, claiming to be 22, to legally perform. "Dave was just 17 at the time, which meant he was under age when he came to playing at bars. So he lied. He said he was 22."
**Dropping Out of High School** : With his mother's blessing, he dropped out of high school to tour with Scream. His mother agreed on two conditions: he pay rent and attend night school, though he spent the night school tuition on marijuana instead. "Mum, I'm going to quit high school. I'm not going to graduate. That, as you might guess, did not go over well with the English teacher."
**Academic Performance in High School** : While he achieved straight A's when younger, his academic performance declined significantly by age 17 due to a lack of interest and being a part-time stoner, leading him to transfer schools without improvement. "It wasn't that he wasn't smart. It's just that he didn't care. When he was younger, he got straight A's. Now at age 17, not so much."
**Dissatisfaction with Part-time Jobs** : He found no happiness in his part-time jobs at Shaky Speeds and Tower Records, as his ultimate desire was to pursue music as a full-time career. "He also wasn't happy working at a couple of part-time jobs at shaky speeds and tower records."
**First Touring Experiences (Scream)** : Touring America and Europe (including Amsterdam) with Scream at age 17 or 18 marked his first significant travels beyond Chicago. He cherished these experiences, seeing them as freedom and a clear path to his musical passion, despite the lack of money or large audiences. "And then to Europe for the first time. And wow, I'm an Amsterdam, and I'm 18, and I'm free, man. It was cool. It was great."
**Singing Lead in Scream** : He sang lead vocals on the song "God's Look Down" for Scream's fifth album, *Fumble*, despite feeling "totally vulnerable," "naked," and "exposed" when singing lead, preferring the anonymity of a drummer. "I just always felt really weird about singing, like I didn't mind singing backups, but when it's my voice out front, that's when it gets creepy."
**Remix of Scream Album (2017)** : He remixed Scream's fourth album, *No More Censorship*, which was subsequently reissued in 2017 as part of a Record Store Day event. "That album, by the way, was remixed by Dave, and then reissues part of a record-store day thing in 2017."

**Communal Atmosphere** : The concert environment at the Cubby Bear was characterized by its non-violent, friendly, and communal nature, where audience members and bands seemed to know each other, fostering a sense of camaraderie. "it wasn't violent at all. It was all these people hanging out, everyone's friends, everybody knew each other, you know, everybody knew the band, the band knew everybody, and just playing this show."

# instrument - Silvertone Guitar

## curiosities
**Dave Grohl's First Guitar** : Dave Grohl received a 1963 Silvertone guitar from Sears for Christmas in 1981, notable for its unique design which incorporated an amplifier directly into the instrument's case. "It was a 1963 Silvertone from Sears with the amp built into the case."

# instrument - Boombox

## curiosities
**Early Recording Device for Dave Grohl** : An 11 or 12-year-old Dave Grohl ingeniously used a boombox, in conjunction with his mother's home stereo, as a multi-track recording device to layer guitar and drum parts for his early original songs. "I'd actually write and track songs with my mom's home stereo and my little boombox."

# instrument - Improvised Drum Kit

## curiosities
**Larry Hinkel's Makeshift Percussion** : For the HG Hancock band, drummer Larry Hinkel creatively assembled his percussion setup using common household items: a laundry basket for the bass drum, kitchen pots for cymbals or toms, and knitting needles as drumsticks. "Larry cobbled together a jumpsuit consisting of a laundry basket, some pots from the kitchen, and a couple of mom's knitting needles for sticks."

# curiosity

**Saturday Night Live Guest Host (1980)** : Terry Gar served as the guest host for Saturday Night Live on January 26, 1980, the night The B-52s' performance of "Rock Lobster" deeply affected an 11-year-old Dave Grohl. "Terry Gar was the guest host that night."
**Dave Smith on Foo Fighters Road Crew** : Dave Smith was employed as a member of the Foo Fighters' road crew for a period. This detail was mentioned in the context of indicating that despite Dave Grohl's abrupt departure from his previous band Dane Bramage, not all personal connections were severed. "Dave Smith ended up working as a member of the Fouffleiters Road Crew for a time, so not all the bridges were burned."
**Senator Robert Taft, Jr.** : The grandson of a former U.S. President, Robert Taft, Jr., was a Republican politician for whom Dave Grohl's father, Jim Grohl, worked as a speechwriter and campaign manager. "a senator named Robert Taft, Jr., the grandson of a former U.S. president."
**Bob Dole's Political Career** : Republican politician Bob Dole was offered Jim Grohl's services as a consultant after Robert Taft, Jr. left politics, an offer Jim Grohl declined. Dole later lost the 1996 presidential election to Bill Clinton, effectively ending his career as a political consultant. "Jim Grohl was offered a job working for Republican Bob Dole, but he turned that down. And that was just as well because Dole got killed by Bill Clinton when he ran for president in 1996."
**Ronald Reagan's Political Context** : The presidency of Ronald Reagan is highlighted as the political backdrop against which the hardcore punk genre emerged, serving as a direct response to his conservative policies. "a genre that was created in response to Ronald Reagan."
**Leonard Skinnerd (Gym Teacher)** : The name of a gym teacher who inspired the naming convention of the band Lynyrd Skynyrd, a precedent that Dave Grohl used when naming his first band, HG Hancock. "Leonard Skinnerd's name is a derivation of Leonard Skinnerd, their gym teacher's name."
**Tracy Bradford's Record Collection** : Dave Grohl's cousin, Tracy Bradford, owned an "incredible" record collection featuring valuable punk singles, including first pressings from Misfits and Minor Threat, which would be worth a "mint" today. "She had this incredible record collection, which it would be worth a mint today, I'm sure, because it's all, you know, the first misfit single, the first minor threat single, these things that you go down to bleaker bobs, you see like 400 bucks on pop."
**Underground Punk Network** : Dave Grohl was captivated by the underground punk network revealed to him by his cousin Tracy, which included fanzines, pen pals, and flyers from shows extending across the country, even from distant locations like San Diego and Alaska. "I just fell in love with the network, like the fanzines, and just this network, the underground network, and how everyone was connected, and everyone, you had pen pals, and she had flyers from shows, and you know, San Diego."
**Band Breakups Due to Parental Divorce** : A recurring pattern in Dave Grohl's early bands (HG Hancock band and Nameless) was their dissolution due to the parents of band members divorcing, leading to the members moving away. "Okay, you're probably starting to see a pattern here."
**Barrett Jones, Recording Engineer** : Barrett Jones was a member of the local band 11th Hour who had a recording studio in his parents' laundry room, which he named Laundry Room Records. He recorded eight songs for Freak Baby and later recorded Mission Impossible. "Dave met someone named Barrett Jones, a member of a local band called 11th Hour, who had a recording studio set up in his parents' laundry room and he called it laundry room records."
