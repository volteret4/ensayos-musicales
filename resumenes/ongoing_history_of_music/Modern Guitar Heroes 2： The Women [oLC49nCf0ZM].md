## curiosities
**Early Influences** : From childhood, Chrissie Hynde loved the guitar and, due to a scarcity of female role models, drew inspiration from James Brown's guitarist. She also admired artists like Alice Cooper, The Stooges, and Jeff Beck. "She loved the guitar from the time she was a kid. With few female role models, she looked to the guitarist employed by James Brown. She loved Alice Cooper and the stooages and Jeff Beck."
**Move to London and Punk Scene Immersion** : In her early 20s, Hynde moved from Akron, Ohio, to London, where she deeply engaged with the burgeoning early punk scene. She contributed writing to the music magazine The NME and frequently associated with figures from bands like the Sex Pistols and The Clash while trying to form her own band. "When she was in her early 20s, she moved from Akrona, Ohio to London and immersed herself in the early punk scene. She wrote for the Music Magazine, The NME, and she hung out with people like the sex pistols in the clash, while trying to get a band off the ground."
**Band Formation Year** : The Pretenders finally solidified in 1978 after several earlier attempts by Chrissie Hynde to establish a band. "There were several before the pretenders finally took hold of 1978."
**Fighting Sexism and Becoming a Role Model** : Despite the "tremendous bias against female players" and "awful" sexism prevalent in the 1970s, Chrissie Hynde persevered, using the "Free For All" attitude of punk to prove her guitar-playing abilities were on par with men, eventually becoming a role model herself. "The sexism was awful. But Chrissy fought through it and in the process became a role model herself."
**Chrissy Hynde's Advice to Chick Rockers (1984)** : In 1984, Chrissie Hynde published a piece titled "Chrissy Hines Advice to Chick Rockers," which is noted as making for "interesting reading." "In 1984, she published something called Chrissy Hines Advice to Chick Rockers. Google it. It certainly makes for some interesting reading."
**Continued Activity** : Despite being nearly 70 years old, Chrissie Hynde remains active in music. "even though she's nearly 70, she is still going."

"from the very beginning, she did everything herself through her own label, Righteous Babe Records. ... Ani wanted to be independent. Her acoustic guitar technique is highly percussive, and her vocal melodies, which "dance around" the guitar, draw comparisons to Joni Mitchell. "Ani has a passive aggressive style of playing. She uses all sorts of open tunings and often wears finger picks that she tapes to the fingers of her right hand. The guitar might be acoustic, but she's always finding ways to make things more percussive. And the way her vocal melodies dance around a guitar might make you think a little bit of Joni Mitchell."

# instrument - Electric Guitar

## curiosities
**Decline in Sales (Last Decade)** : The electric guitar has faced a "really rough decade," with global sales of new instruments decreasing by a third, from 1.5 million to roughly 1 million. This decline is attributed to younger generations favoring electronic music production tools like laptops and iPads over traditional instruments. "It's been a really rough decade for the electric guitar. Sales of new instruments have dropped by a third, from 1.5 million globally to around just 1 million. Why? Well, generations brought up on electronics are opting out of creating music with traditional instruments like the guitar."
