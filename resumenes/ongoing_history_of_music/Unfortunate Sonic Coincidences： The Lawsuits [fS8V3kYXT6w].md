**Bittersweet Symphony (1997)** : This song became the subject of a famous sampling lawsuit. The Verve initially cleared rights with Decca Records, but not with Abko Records, owned by Allan Klein, who held rights to earlier Stones material. Klein sued, leading Richard Ashcroft to sign over all songwriting credits and rights to the entire song to Klein, who credited Jagger/Richards. "But one of the most famous sampling lawsuits involves the Verve, and how they lost control of their song, Bittersweet Symphony for decades."

**US Court Ruling on Statute of Limitations (October 2020)** : An American court ruling in October 2020 established a statute of limitations for copyright infringement. This ruling states that if an individual is accused of copyright infringement, they must contest the claim, after which the accuser has three years to file a case. If no case is brought within that timeframe, the claim is permanently barred, potentially curbing opportunistic lawsuits based on old claims. "In October 2020, there was a ruling by an American court that set a statute of limitations on copyright infringement."
