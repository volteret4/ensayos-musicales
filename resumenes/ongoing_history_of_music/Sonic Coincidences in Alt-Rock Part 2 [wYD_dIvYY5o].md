**No-Two-Alike Conjecture (Snowflakes)** : This conjecture states that no two large snow crystals are ever alike. A snowflake, as a crystal, grows around a dust particle and increases in size through an infinite number of variations due to uneven crystal growth under varying temperatures. "A snowflake is a crystal, and this type of crystal begins with nucleation around a dust particle and continues to increase in size in an unparticularly infinite number of variations, thanks to uneven crystal growth as the flake is subjected to a variety of temperatures."
**Nanosto Flakes Exception to Conjecture** : The no-two-alike conjecture applies only to large snow crystals. Nanosto flakes, which are tiny and consist of a small number of water molecules, are believed by crystallographers to potentially be alike. "However, the no-two alike conjecture only applies to large snow crystals. Before snowflake can get big, it has to be small.
