## curiosities
**Bootleg-Friendly Policy** : Pearl Jam maintained a long-standing policy of being "bootleg-friendly," allowing fans to record their shows. This approach stemmed from frontman Eddie Vedder's personal experience of sneaking recorders into concerts as a child to re-experience live performances. **Official Bootleg Series (2000 onwards)** : In 2000, Pearl Jam initiated an innovative series of official bootlegs, releasing high-quality, soundboard recordings of their live shows. The first series launched during the Binaural Tour, making 72 shows from North America and Europe available as affordable double-disc CDs and later digital downloads, starting with a Lisbon gig on May 23rd, 2000. "They released a series of official bootlegs, all recorded at the soundboard on tour. The audio was high quality, and the CDs were affordable."
**Extensive Live Recording Archive** : Pearl Jam continued their official bootleg series for many years, releasing 73 shows in 2003, 8 from a Canadian tour in 2005, 50 in 2006, and more from tours in 2008, 2009, 2010 (Backspacer tour), 2011, 2012, 2013, 2014, 2018, and 2022. This prolific release schedule resulted in over 200 official bootlegs, documenting close to 6,000 live-performed songs, exemplified by a show recorded in Montreal on October 4th, 2000. "So how many of these official bootlegs exist? I think somewhere north of 200, with close to 6,000 songs performed live."
