## albums
**No One Sings Like You Anymore, Volume 1 (2020) – Posthumous Cover Album Released During Pandemic** : Cornell had been working on this album of covers for a couple of years prior to his death, planning it as a solo project. It features his interpretations of songs by Janis Joplin, John Lennon, Prince, and others. Released posthumously on December 11, 2020, its title comes from a line in the Soundgarden song "Black Hole Sun." It was previewed by a recording of "Patience" (originally by Guns N' Roses), released on July 20, 2020 — what would have been Chris's 56th birthday. Released during the depths of the pandemic, the album largely flew under the radar. "If that record escaped your attention, you're forgiven. We had other things going on."

## songs
**Spoonman – Origin from Cameron Crowe's Film Singles, Written from Discarded Band Names** : During the filming of Cameron Crowe's *Singles* (1991), Stone Gossard had written down discarded fictional band names for the movie, including "Spoonman." After filming wrapped, Chris Cornell took the list and used those discarded names as song titles to write for Soundgarden. The resulting song appeared on the *Singles* soundtrack. A full original version, slightly different from the album version, was not released until 2017. "Stone had written down discarded band names, and Cornell used them as song titles."

**Rusty Cage – Studio Outtake Version from Bad Motorfinger Sessions** : An alternate studio outtake recording of this song from the *Bad Motorfinger* sessions was preserved. The album was released on October 8, 1991, completing what was considered the "Holy Trinity of Grunge Records" alongside Pearl Jam's *Ten* (August 27, 1991) and Nirvana's *Nevermind* (a month later). "One of the most popular songs from that record."

## curiosities
**Origins at Ray's Boathouse – Kitchen Work Led to Singing Career** : In the early 1980s, long before Soundgarden existed, Cornell worked at a Seattle restaurant called Ray's Boathouse, starting as a teenager when his older brother Peter got him a job. He progressed from dishwasher to busboy to sous chef. At the restaurant's annual staff Christmas party, he spontaneously grabbed the microphone from a hired two-man band and launched into "Twist and Shout," astonishing everyone with the power of his voice. The bass player at Ray's Boathouse was Hiro Yamamoto; a maintenance worker named Scott Sundquist (later replaced by Matt Cameron) completed the original 1984 Soundgarden lineup with Kim Thayil on guitar. "Nobody had heard him before and nobody could believe the power of this kid's voice."

**Black Calvarros Restaurant, Paris (2006) – Rock Music Bar Near the Four Seasons, Closed After Two Years** : Cornell opened a high-end restaurant in Paris called Black Calvarros in 2006, located across from the Four Seasons hotel about five blocks from the Champs-Élysées. During renovations, a ceiling fresco dating to the 1780s was discovered. The joint venture with his wife Vicki and brother-in-law DJ Nick Blast offered food including caramelized quail, truffled mac and cheese, and gourmet hot dogs. Interior design was by the designer of the annual Victoria's Secret Fashion Show. The restaurant sometimes did not close until 7am but shut down after a couple of years. "A restaurant slash bar where you could go in Paris to hear rock music."

**Singles (1992) – Walk-On Cameo and Spoonman Song Origin** : Director Cameron Crowe cast Cornell in a brief, non-speaking walk-on role in *Singles* (1991, released 1992), appearing after a scene where Matt Dillon shows Bridget Fonda his new car stereo, immediately preceding a Soundgarden concert sequence. "Chris Cornell walks up to see what's going on. It doesn't say anything, but the very next shot is of Chris and Soundgarden on stage."

**Lifelong Struggles with Mental Health, Drugs, and Alcohol** : Cornell grew up as a loner, one of six children raised by a single mother who was an alcoholic; his estranged father was also an alcoholic. By age 12 he was drinking, smoking marijuana, and taking prescription medications. He tried PCP at 14 and suffered a very bad experience, deepening his depression and anxiety. He managed his mental health throughout Soundgarden's existence but after the band broke up in 1997 — coinciding with the failure of his first marriage — he went heavily into heroin and oxycodone. He entered rehab in 2002 and was entirely sober by 2005, except for prescription drugs. "No one really knows what one of the mill depression is. You'll think that someone has run of the mill depression and then the next thing you know, they're hanging from a rope."

**Motorcycle Accident (October 24, 2006) – Hit by Truck, Thrown 20 Feet, $50,000 Bike Destroyed, Returned to Studio Same Day** : While recording his second solo album, Cornell was riding his motorcycle through Studio City, Los Angeles, when he collided with a truck (reports conflict on whether it was a rear-end or front-end collision). He was thrown 20 feet into the air and his $50,000 bike was completely destroyed. Despite cuts and bruises, he dusted himself off and returned to the studio later the same day. "Given what could have happened to him."

**Soundgarden Breakup (1997) – Tension from Manager/Wife Susan Silver and Separate Tour Transport** : The band broke up in 1997 partly due to the unresolvable conflict of interest created by Cornell being married to Susan Silver, who was also the band's manager (and the manager of Alice in Chains and Screaming Trees). By the end of the band's existence, each member was reportedly traveling separately by their own bus or plane between shows. After the divorce in 2002, Cornell filed a complaint with the California Labor Commission in 2006 alleging Silver had performed booking work without the required talent agent's license, and sought return of money she made from Soundgarden activities in 2005. "No conflict of interest there."

**Death (May 17–18, 2017) – MGM Grand Detroit, Ativan, Unknown Barbiturates, Naloxone Found in System** : After a Soundgarden show at the MGM Grand in Detroit, Cornell returned to room 1136. His wife Vicki, 2,000 miles away in Los Angeles, noticed the house lights flickering via a remote app on Chris's phone and called him at 11:35pm — twenty minutes after he left the stage. His bodyguard Martin had given him two Ativan (lorazepam) tablets beforehand to help him sleep. Cornell told Vicki he was fine but sounded groggy and slurred. Martin was sent to check on him at 12:15am, found the room locked, kicked the door in, found the bathroom locked, kicked that door in, and found Chris on the floor with blood from his mouth and an exercise band around his neck. A hotel medic arrived at 12:56am; paramedics followed; Chris was pronounced dead at 1:30am. The official cause was suicide by hanging. Toxicology found Ativan, unknown barbiturates, and unexplained Nalozone (naloxone, an anti-opioid) in his system. A hidden injury was mentioned in two clinical reports but not in the autopsy. In 2018 Vicki sued Dr. Robert Coblin of Beverly Hills, who had prescribed the Ativan, alleging dangerous over-prescription to a known former addict; the lawsuit settled out of court in May 2021. "Many of us who know Chris well noticed that he wasn't himself during his final hours and that something was very off."

**Death Date Coincidence – 37 Years After Ian Curtis, Close to Chester Bennington** : Cornell died by hanging on May 18, 2017 — exactly 37 years to the day that Joy Division singer Ian Curtis hanged himself. Two months later, on July 20, 2017 (which would have been Cornell's 53rd birthday), his close friend Chester Bennington of Linkin Park died by suicide, also by hanging. Cornell is interred at Hollywood Forever Cemetery in Los Angeles; his tombstone reads "Voice of our generation and an artist for all time." Johnny Ramone's monument is nearby. "Two months after Chris died, July 20, 2017, which would have been Chris' 53rd birthday, his close friend Chester Bennington of Linkin Park died by suicide."

**Post-Death Lawsuits – Seven Demo Vocal Tracks, Royalties, Tribute Concert Proceeds** : After Cornell's death, Vicki Cornell filed suits over two primary issues: the rights to seven vocal demo tracks recorded in early 2017 on his laptop, apparently destined for a new Soundgarden album, and the disbursement of Soundgarden royalties. Soundgarden counter-sued, alleging Vicki misappropriated proceeds from a 2019 tribute concert. Both sides went to war over social media rights and Chris's posthumous participation in any band activities. Vicki's offer of $4 million per remaining member, then $7 million, was refused. A settlement was announced on April 17, 2023: the seven songs will be finished and released. "This dispute has never been about money or the band. This is their life's work and their legacy."

## curiosities
**Deep Six Compilation (1985) – First Soundgarden Recordings** : The original 1984 lineup of Soundgarden (Cornell, Yamamoto, Sundquist on drums, and Kim Thayil on guitar) recorded their first material in 1985. Three tracks appeared on a compilation called *Deep Six* issued by Seattle's CZ Records, including a track called "Here We Tick." "That would be the original lineup of Soundgarden from 1984."
