## albums
**Buddha (1994) – Pre-Name Change Album** : This album was recorded before the band changed its name from "Blink" to "Blink 182" due to a legal conflict with a band in Ireland. Mark Hoppus includes it in the band's discography, leading him to state that *Nine* (2019) was their ninth studio album despite it technically being their eighth. "Because Mark counted Buddha."
**Anema of the State (1999)** : This album was part of a "great run" of successful albums for the band in the late 1990s and early 2000s, contributing to their rise to prominence. "There is a great run of albums, including Anima of the State in 1999."
**Take Off Your Pants and Jacket (2001)** : This album was part of Blink 182's "great run" of successful albums during the late 1990s and early 2000s, cementing their popularity. "Take off your pants jacket in 2001..."
**Blink-182 (2003) – Self-Titled** : This self-titled record was part of the band's "great run" of albums that helped them blow up in the late 1990s and early 2000s, just before a period of internal friction. "and a self-titled record in 2003."
**Neighborhoods (2011) – Sixth Album** : Released after an eight-year hiatus, this album's performance was met with dissatisfaction by their label. This unhappiness ultimately led to the band being cut loose and forced to go indie in October 2012. "Their label wasn't happy with the performance of Neighborhoods, and in October 2012, the band was cut loose and forced to go indie, a pretty demoralizing thing after selling tens of millions of albums."
**Dogs Eating Dogs (2012) – EP** : A "quickie EP" released in November 2012, shortly after the band was forced to go indie by their label. It was well received by listeners. "In November 2012, they released a quickie EP entitled Dogs Eating Dogs."
**California (2016) – Comeback Album** : This album was the first to feature Matt Skiba after Tom DeLonge's departure. It achieved significant commercial success, becoming the group's second number one album on the Billboard 200 and their first in the UK, also hitting number one in Canada and the US, number two in Australia and Austria, and top 10 in six other countries. The album went gold, was nominated for a Grammy, and was described as Blink 182's biggest hit in a couple of decades, reinvigorating the band. "There was an album called California that became the group's second number one album on the Billboard 200 and their first in the UK."
**Nine (2019) – Eighth Studio Album** : Released on September 20, 2019. Mark Hoppus counted it as their ninth studio album, including *Buddha* (1994). It features 15 songs, many built around rhythms by Travis Barker with hip-hop influences, loops, and electronics, a new sound for the band. Collaborators included Miley Cyrus and Feral Williams, with a goal for a cross-format top 40 hit, though this was hindered by the COVID-19 pandemic. The album's lyrics were darker, reflecting Mark Hoppus's depression, anger over mass shootings, and political views. It received excellent reviews and reached number three on the American top 200 and was a top 10 rock album in Canada, UK, Australia, Germany, and Austria. "Nine was released on September 20th, 2019."
**One More Time (2023) – Reunion Album** : This album was released on October 20, 2023, after recording sessions began in January 2022. Travis Barker self-produced the album in his home studio. It was profoundly influenced by Mark Hoppus's battle with cancer, the band's rekindled relationships, and a sense of maturity. The recording was kept secret to ensure the reconciliation with Tom DeLonge would work. It includes 17 tracks, plus 10 bonus tracks, and a "cancer song" titled "You Don't Know What You've Got." The album debuted at number one on the Billboard Top 200, aided by 11 different vinyl versions, and charted highly internationally. "One more time is an interesting album for Blink 182 because it was obviously affected by Mark's cancer, relationships, friendships, and becoming a little more mature."

## curiosities
**Formation and Name Change (1992-1994)** : The band formed in 1992 outside San Diego, initially named "Blink." They later changed their name to "Blink 182" in 1994 due to a legal issue with another band in Ireland. "Blink 182 forms in 1992 outside of San Diego."
**Career Trajectory (Late 90s/Early 2000s)** : After taking an "Indie route" and later securing a major label deal, the band experienced a significant surge in popularity, "blowing up" in the late 1990s and early 2000s. "They take the Indy route, get some major label deal, and then blows up in the late 90s and early 2000s."
**Indefinite Hiatus (2005)** : An indefinite hiatus was announced on February 22nd, 2005, following a period of uncertainty and internal friction, during which Tom DeLonge and Mark Hoppus ceased communication for years. "Things got to the point where they couldn't continue, and an indefinite hiatus was announced on February 22nd, 2005."
**Jerry Finn's Death (2008)** : The sudden death of their long-time producer Jerry Finn in 2008, coupled with Travis Barker's near-fatal plane crash, "shocked everyone back to reality" and helped the band members sort out their issues, leading to their eventual reunion. "along with drummer Travis Barker's near fatal plane crash and the sudden death of their long-time producer Jerry Flynn, both in 2008. That shocked everyone back to reality and things got sorted out."
**Forced Indie Status (2012)** : In October 2012, following the unsatisfactory performance of their album *Neighborhoods*, the band's label severed ties. This forced Blink 182 to become independent, a "pretty demoralizing thing" for a band that had sold tens of millions of albums. "Their label wasn't happy with the performance of Neighborhoods, and in October 2012, the band was cut loose and forced to go indie, a pretty demoralizing thing after selling tens of millions of albums."
**Resurfacing Internal Issues (Late 2014)** : After their album *Dogs Eating Dogs* and more tours, the "old cracks returned" within the band. Issues between Tom DeLonge and Mark Hoppus, coupled with Travis Barker's fear of flying preventing overseas tours, came to a head in late 2014. "There were more tours, but the old cracks returned."
**Tom DeLonge's Departures (2014-2015)** : Tom DeLonge initially quit the band for one day in early 2014 but was persuaded to return. Tensions escalated again in early 2015 when he insisted on recording his parts in San Diego while Mark and Travis worked in LA. A week before studio convergence, Mark and Travis received an email from Tom's manager stating his unwillingness to participate, citing a desire to focus on non-musical projects. Another account suggests he was presented with a 60-page contract demanding sole focus on Blink, which he couldn't accept due to prior commitments. He consequently quit for a second time. "Tensions built, and a week before everyone was supposed to convene in the studio for the final push, Mark and Travis got an email from Tom's manager saying that he no longer wanted to participate in Blink 182."
**Matt Skiba's Recruitment and First Performance (2015)** : After Tom DeLonge's second departure, Mark Hoppus and Travis Barker decided against disbanding Blink 182 and recruited Matt Skiba from Alkaline Trio. Their first performance with Skiba on March 18, 2015, marked the first time Mark Hoppus played in Blink without Tom DeLonge, after which Skiba was made an official member. "Mark and Travis had no plans to kill Blink so they brought in Matt Skiba from Alkaline Trio."
**COVID-19 Tour Cancellation (2020)** : A significant world tour planned for the *Nine* album was canceled due to the COVID-19 pandemic lockdown in March 2020, likely impacting the album's potential commercial success and preventing them from achieving a desired top 40 hit. "A big tour had been planned but that of course was canceled."
**Last Show with Matt Skiba (2020)** : The band's last live performance before the COVID-19 pandemic, held in Los Angeles on January 18, 2020, also turned out to be the final show featuring Matt Skiba as a member of Blink 182. "Blinks last show before everything hit the fan was in Los Angeles on January 18, 2020. And that also turned out to be the last show with Mads Keeba."
**Tom DeLonge's Return and Reconciliation (2022)** : By mid-2022, rumors of a reunion circulated, leading to the band's social media accounts being wiped in October. On October 11, the official announcement was made that Tom DeLonge was back, and an album and a two-year world tour were planned. This reconciliation was spurred by Mark Hoppus's cancer diagnosis, which prompted Tom to reach out immediately. "And that's when news came down that Mark, Travis and Tom, came to the realization that it was time to bury all the animosities and get back to being Blink 182."
**Coachella Comeback Performance (2023)** : On April 14, 2023, Blink 182 was announced as a surprise replacement headliner for Frank Ocean at Coachella with only two days' notice. This marked the original lineup's (Mark, Travis, Tom) first performance together in nine years (since 2014). They played an hour-long set to an audience of 150,000, which was described as one big sing-along. "In the interim, they got to make a big comeback together at Coachella on April 14th, 2023."
**Hard Rock and One More Time Tours (2023-2024)** : The band embarked on a headline tour, known as the Hard Rock Tour for its first leg and the One More Time Tour for its second. It ran from May 2023 to November 8, 2024, spanning four legs and 91 shows, with each concert featuring approximately 25 songs. This tour achieved the "biggest box office take of any rock tour that year." Three dates were canceled: one due to a hurricane and two for routine illnesses. "The headline tour, known as the Hard Rock Tour and the One More Time Tour for the second leg, began in May 2023 and didn't end until November 8, 2024."
**Missionary Impossible Tour (2025)** : Following their extensive 2023-2024 tours, the band initiated the Missionary Impossible Tour in 2025. This tour was notable for featuring many songs that Blink 182 had not performed in decades. "That was followed by the missionary impossible tour in 2025, which saw the band play a lot of songs that they hadn't touched in decades."
**Fourth Decade and Resilience (2026)** : By 2026, Blink 182 was actively performing and showing no signs of slowing down, entering their fourth decade as a band. Despite numerous past challenges where things threatened to "break down completely," the members consistently managed to pull it back together. "Blink 182 is now into their fourth decade."

**Struggle with Depression and Anger (2018-2019)** : During the period leading up to and during the recording of Blink 182's *Nine* album, Mark Hoppus struggled severely with depression. His wife encouraged him to pursue activities that brought him happiness, leading to the formation of Simple Creatures in 2018. His lyrics for *Nine* were notably darker, reflecting his internal battles, his anger over mass shootings (particularly one near Travis Barker's home), and his frustration with Donald Trump's policies. He was also not sober at this time. "But hold up though, Mark was struggling with depression during this time. And it was bad."
**Justification for "Nine" Album Title (2019)** : Mark Hoppus considered Blink 182's 2019 album *Nine* to be their ninth studio album, explaining that he counted *Buddha*, a 1994 album released before the band's name change from "Blink" to "Blink 182." He also stated, "Nine is the number of universal love." "Because Mark counted Buddha."
**Large B Cell Lymphoma Diagnosis (2021)** : In June 2021, Mark Hoppus discovered a concerning lump on his shoulder that was quickly diagnosed as stage 4 large B cell lymphoma, a fast-growing cancer type previously experienced by his mother. Terrified and believing he would die, he immediately began intensive chemotherapy. "It was something called large B cell lymphoma. This is a fast growing type of cancer, because the same cancer his mother had, and Mark's was stage 4."
**Grueling Chemotherapy Experience (2021)** : Mark Hoppus underwent incredibly strong chemotherapy treatments. During one session, the drugs were so potent that his doctors advised his wife not to share a toilet with him for three days. He likened chemo to "being on the worst international overnight flight where you can't sleep or get comfortable," suffering from hair loss, constant pain, exhaustion, and persistent "brain fog" that made him feel "diminished mentally." "He says, chemo is like being on the worst international overnight flight where you can't sleep or get comfortable."
**Accidental Public Cancer Revelation (2021)** : On June 23, 2021, after three months of secrecy, Mark Hoppus accidentally posted a selfie from a chemotherapy infusion to his 1.2 million Instagram followers, instead of his private WhatsApp chat. The photo was captioned, "yes, hello, one cancer treatment, please." Though he deleted it immediately, the news spread, forcing him to issue a public statement confirming his diagnosis and treatment, which he later called "the best mistake I've ever made by far." "Instead, he posted it to Instagram and it went out to 1.2 million followers. It was captioned, yes, hello, one cancer treatment, please."
**Cancer-Free Declaration and Continued Screening (2022)** : After undergoing intensive treatment through the end of 2021 and into 2022, Mark Hoppus was declared cancer-free. He was required to continue with screenings every six months. "Mark was declared cancer free, but had to continue screening every six months."
**Healing of Friendships Through Cancer (2022)** : His battle with cancer facilitated the repair of damaged relationships, particularly his friendship with Tom DeLonge, who offered immediate support. Mark reflected that this experience helped him appreciate his life, stating, "The cancer brought back friendships that I hadn't had in years. It healed my friendship with Tom."
**Vocal and Physical Weakness Post-Chemo (2022)** : During the recording of the *One More Time* album in January 2022, Mark Hoppus was not yet fully recovered from chemotherapy. The treatment had impacted his vocal cords, and his physical strength was still diminished, though he gradually regained it with each day of recording. "The chemo had done a number on his vocal cords and his strength still wasn't where it needed to be."
**Fahrenheit 182 (2025) – Memoir** : Mark Hoppus released a memoir titled *Fahrenheit 182* in April 2025, detailing his personal journey. "If you want to read more about Mark's journey, I highly recommend the memoir Fahrenheit 182, which came out in April 2025."

**Near-Fatal Plane Crash and Fear of Flying (2008)** : Travis Barker was involved in a near-fatal plane crash in 2008, an event that, alongside producer Jerry Finn's death, "shocked everyone back to reality" and helped resolve internal band issues. That shocked everyone back to reality and things got sorted out."
**Prolific Collaborator and Producer (2015-2022)** : Over a seven-year period, Travis Barker became a highly active collaborator and producer, appearing on numerous records. His extensive list of collaborations includes artists such as Young Blood, The Game, M&M, Stevie O'Kee, LL Cool J, Pitbull, Lights, Kenny Hoopla, Machine Gun Kelly, Halsey, Willow Smith, and Dirty Heads. "Travis Barker seemed willing to collaborate and produce just about anyone, and he would appear in a ton of records over the next seven years."
**Personal Life Developments (Post-2015)** : During this period, Travis Barker's personal life saw significant changes; he got divorced, became involved with the Kardashians, and made more TV appearances. He was also involved in a car accident where his SUV collided with a school bus, though he was not injured. "He got divorced, got wrapped up with a Kardashians, appeared on more TV shows, and got into a bad car accident, whereas SUV collided with a school bus."
**Hip-Hop Influence on "Nine" (2019)** : Travis Barker's strong interest in hip-hop heavily influenced the rhythms he created for many of the 15 songs on Blink 182's *Nine* album. This rhythmic foundation, combined with the incorporation of loops and electronics, contributed a "different sort of flavor" to the band's sound. "Nine features 15 songs, most of which seem to have been constructed around rhythms created by Travis, which since Travis was so big into hip-hop, gave the album a different sort of flavor."
**Producer of "One More Time" (2023)** : Travis Barker took on the role of producer for Blink 182's *One More Time* album, with most of the recording conducted at his home studio. While initially a sensitive and unplanned arrangement, it became evident as sessions progressed that he was "up to the task." "There was no outside producer.
