# artist - Patti Smith

## songs
**Piss Factory (1974) - First Punk Rock Record** : This one-off seven-inch single, with "Piss Factory" as its B-side, was released in the summer of 1974 and is regarded by some music historians as the first-ever punk rock record. The song was recorded almost by accident after Patti Smith and Lenny Kay finished an earlier session for a Jimi Hendrix cover. It told the story of Smith's experiences working in a doll factory. A thousand copies were pressed and distributed to independent bookstores and record sellers, selling out almost instantly and launching Smith's recording career. "No one, man or woman, was making music quite like this back then."

## curiosities
**Early Career as Poet and Writer** : Patti Smith began her career as a poet and music writer, often performing readings. She was frequently accompanied by her friend, record store clerk Lenny Kay, who would improvise on guitar in the background. "Patty was a poet and music writer who often did readings with her friend, record store clerk Lenny Kay, noodling around on a guitar in the background."
**Accidental Recording of Piss Factory** : The recording of "Piss Factory" was somewhat accidental. Patti Smith and Lenny Kay had booked studio time to record a cover of the Jimi Hendrix song "Hey Joe," but they finished early. With the remaining time, they recorded "Piss Factory," a track that recounted Smith's experiences working in a doll factory. "Piss Factory was sort of an accident. They had booked time to record a cover of the Jimmy Hendrix song Hey Joe and finished early."
**Independent Release Success** : Following its accidental recording, "Piss Factory" was pressed as a one-off seven-inch single, with a limited run of a thousand copies. These copies were strategically placed in independent bookstores and record sellers, where they sold out almost instantly. This initial success was pivotal, setting Smith on the path to becoming a genuine recording artist. "They pressed up a thousand copies and seated it at independent bookstores and record sellers. It sold out almost instantly."
**Icon of New York Punk and Rock Scene** : The early success of "Piss Factory" enabled Patti Smith to secure a major label record deal and swiftly establish herself as a leading icon within the New York punk rock scene, and subsequently, the broader rock scene. She inspired many with her defiant perspective on life and her unprecedented vocal style. "This little bit of success set Patty on the road to becoming a genuine recording artist, nailing a major label record deal and becoming a leading icon on the New York punk rock scene and then, well, the rock scene in general."
**Inspirational and Unconventional Artist** : Patti Smith inspired both men and women with her distinctive "I don't give a crap view of the world, the universe, and beyond," and her uncompromising approach to her art. Her vocal delivery was described as unlike anything heard before, embodying a spirit of doing things her own way. "Patty was an inspiration to so many women and men with her techno prisoners I don't give a crap view of the world, the universe, and beyond."

## curiosities
**Discovery by David Gilmour** : At the age of 14, Kate Bush was discovered by David Gilmour of Pink Floyd. He immediately recognized her distinct talent, as she was already playing piano, violin, and organ, and writing her own songs. Gilmour took her demo tape to EMI Records, Pink Floyd's label. "David Gilmore of Pink Floyd, chanced upon a 14 year old girl who had been playing piano, violin, and organ as well as writing her own songs."
**EMI Records Deal and Artistic Development** : EMI Records, seeking artists who sounded and looked different in the then-hot UK prog rock market, quickly signed Kate Bush to a deal. This arrangement allowed her to continue her schooling while developing as a musician. Over the next couple of years, she wrote more than 200 songs and performed in various pubs around London. "EMI quickly signed her to a deal that allowed her to continue to go to school and develop as a musician."
**Unique and Ethereal Style** : Kate Bush's style, characterized by her distinct singing, vocal range, and the unconventional nature of her hit songs, was not universally understood but deeply appreciated by those who "got her." Her appeal was often described as "ethereal quirkiness." "Not everyone got Kate Bush, of course, but those who did love her for her, well, let's just call it her ethereal cuckiness."
**Long Hiatus from Live Performances** : After an initial tour, Kate Bush famously refrained from playing any live shows for 35 years. This decision contributed to an aura of mystery, myth, and legend surrounding her, captivating many and fostering one of the most loyal and fanatical fan bases globally. "because she refused to play any live shows for 35 years after one initial tour, she was surrounded by an aura of mystery and myth and legend."
