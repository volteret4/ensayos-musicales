# artist - Jack White

## member of
The White Stripes

## curiosities
**Priesthood Calling Abandoned for Guitar Amplifier – Jack White's Near-Seminary Life** : Jack White (born John Anthony Gillis) is the youngest of ten children of Gorman and Theresa Gillis, a devout Catholic couple in Detroit. Both parents worked for the Catholic Archdiocese of Detroit — his mother as a secretary, his father in maintenance. Jack served as an altar boy at the Church of the Holy Redeemer and, following in the footsteps of his six brothers, developed a sense of religious calling. He applied to and was accepted at a seminary in Wisconsin, but had a last-second change of heart when he realized he would not be permitted to bring his new guitar amplifier. He enrolled in public school instead. Had he gone, there would have been no White Stripes, no marriage and divorce to Meg White, and no subsequent marriage to model Karen Elson. "So talk about a decision that changes your life, huh?"

**The Rosary Murders (1987) – Uncredited Child Actor Appearance as Altar Boy** : While serving as an altar boy at the Church of the Holy Redeemer in Detroit, the young Jack Gillis (later Jack White) made an uncredited extra appearance in the 1987 film *The Rosary Murders*. He is visible in the film if you know where to look. This early exposure to acting contributed to his later interest in it as an adult, including a role in *Cold Mountain*. "Technically, this makes Jack White a child actor."

**Amazon Canoe Wedding (2005) – Karen Elson, Shaman Priest, Three Mystical Rivers** : When Jack White married British model Karen Elson in 2005, the ceremony was not held in a Catholic church as one might expect given his devout upbringing. Instead, they were married by a shaman priest in the Amazon basin, at the confluence of three mystical rivers in Brazil, in a canoe. A post-ceremony blessing was administered by a Catholic priest at a nearby church. "They were married by a shaman priest in the Amazon basin at the confluence of three mystical rivers in Brazil."


# artist - Joe Strummer

## member of
The Clash

## curiosities
**Diplomat's Son – Real Name John Miller, Born in Ankara, Raised Across Multiple Postings** : Joe Strummer's entire punk persona was built on a working-class streetwise identity that contradicted his actual origins. His real name was John Mellor (referred to as John Miller in the podcast). He was born in Ankara, Turkey, while his father, a British diplomat with the Foreign Service, was posted there. His father subsequently received postings to Mexico City, Cairo, and Bonn, West Germany — providing Joe and his brother with security, good schools, and comfortable accommodations. The family eventually settled in Surrey, England, where the boys attended private schools befitting a diplomat's salary. Joe transformed himself into a punk musician via art school and spent the rest of his life distancing Joe Strummer the punk from his privileged origins. "He considered his upbringing to be an embarrassment."


# artist - Eddie Vedder

## curiosities
**Child Modeling Career – Department Store Catalogs, Bozo the Clown (Early 1970s)** : Growing up in Chicago, Eddie Vedder's mother entered him in photo shoots and TV commercial auditions from a young age. He appeared in at least five department store catalogs modeling back-to-school clothing for five-year-olds. His TV commercial work included spots for Hallmark greeting cards and Chuckles candy. He appeared in a Big Wheel toy commercial that aired during Saturday morning cartoons. His most prominent childhood appearance was on the nationally broadcast *Bozo the Clown* show in the early 1970s. As a grunge icon with serious social, political, and environmental views, this chapter of his life is one he would rather keep quiet. "He would rather not be known as the cute child model for Bloomingdale's."


# artist - Dave Navarro

## member of
Jane's Addiction
Red Hot Chili Peppers

## curiosities
**Mother's Murder (March 3, 1983) – Boyfriend John "Dean" Ricardi Shot Connie and Friend, Caught via America's Most Wanted** : Dave Navarro was 15 years old when his mother Connie — one of the models on *Let's Make a Deal* with Monty Hall — was shot to death along with her best friend Sue by her ex-boyfriend, a bodybuilder named John Ricardi (who went by "Dean"). After the relationship deteriorated and Connie sought a restraining order, Ricardi broke into the house on the night of March 3, 1983, and waited. Dave narrowly escaped by spending the weekend with his father. Ricardi evaded capture for almost eight years until a tip from a viewer of *America's Most Wanted* led to his arrest in Houston, Texas, on January 4, 1991. Dave testified against him at trial; Ricardi was subsequently placed on death row at San Quentin. "Human beings are not equipped to know how to handle something like that."

# artist - Nine Inch Nails

## members
Trent Reznor

## curiosities
**Pre-NIN New Wave Bands (1983) – Option 30 and Others Disowned After Fame** : Before Nine Inch Nails, Trent Reznor played in several bands based in the Cleveland, Ohio area that released recordings and sounded markedly different — far softer and more new wave — than the industrial sound he later became known for. One group, called Option 30, recorded new wave material in 1983. After NIN broke through in the late 1980s, Trent reportedly had his sister scour record stores in eastern Ohio and western Pennsylvania to buy up all remaining copies of these earlier recordings, in an effort to erase evidence of that part of his career. "Trent did try to disown that part of his musical past."


# artist - Ramones

## members
Joey Ramone

## curiosities
**Joey Ramone's OCD – Lifelong Battle Barely Acknowledged, Finally Confirmed on 2002 Solo Album** : Joey Ramone suffered from obsessive-compulsive disorder that preceded the formation of the Ramones and is cited by those close to him as a major reason he was so shy, awkward, and withdrawn in private. His OCD manifested in compulsive repetitive behaviors — stepping off and on a curb dozens of times, repeating words over and over driven by voices in his head — and he was hospitalized at least once when it became unmanageable. Despite this, Joey vehemently denied having OCD whenever it was raised publicly, and the subject was almost never discussed. As he came to terms with his cancer and mortality, he relented slightly, and in the song "Like a Drug I Never Did Before" on his 2002 posthumous solo album *Don't Worry About Me*, he addressed his lifelong battle with OCD — the only time he ever mentioned it in his music. "This is the only time he ever brought it up in his music."

# artist - Nirvana

## members
Kurt Cobain

## songs
**Scentless Apprentice (1993) – Inspired by Jean-Baptiste Grenouille, the Fictional Perfume Murderer** : Kurt Cobain was fascinated by the story of an 18th-century French perfume maker whose goal was to create a scent that smelled like virginity, and who murdered 25 young women to steal their essence using methods involving dissolving bones and extracting fat. This story (later adapted into the 2006 film *Perfume: The Story of a Murderer*) appealed to Kurt and he wrote a song about it called "Scentless Apprentice" for the *In Utero* album. The attraction connected to his broader aesthetic interest in medical imagery, anatomy, and bodily excreta. "This story appealed to Kurt in some weird way and he even wrote a song about it for the In Utero album."

## curiosities
**Medical Fetish – Anatomy Art, Gynecological Instruments, Full-Sized Medical Dummy on Tour** : Kurt Cobain's artistic aesthetic included a deep preoccupation with medical imagery and human anatomy that dated back to his grade school drawings. The artwork for *In Utero* features disassembled dolls, fetuses, and embryo-like forms. He reportedly collected vintage medical equipment and surgical instruments, including old gynecological devices well beyond basic medical tools. One account claims he kept a full-sized medical dummy at home and took it on tour in its own trunk. His journals (published by Courtney Love in 2002) reveal an obsession with bodily functions, vomit, urine, mucus, and excrement — consistent with his naming of his first band Fecal Matter. "Kurt had a full-sized medical dummy. Not only did he keep it around the house, but he toured with it."


# artist - The Killers

## members
Brandon Flowers

## curiosities
**Pedestrian Accident (2001) – Hit Man at 50 mph on Las Vegas Freeway, Outcome Unknown** : In 2001, Brandon Flowers was driving in the Las Vegas area when a drunk pedestrian walked into his lane on the freeway. Despite honking and braking, he struck the man at approximately 50 mph. The pedestrian hit the windshield and his shoes flew off. Police questioned Brandon and released him. He has never found out whether the man survived. In a *GQ* interview he described it as "terrible" and "pretty traumatic." Brandon rarely discusses the incident. "Did he recover? Or is the singer of the Killers really a killer?"
