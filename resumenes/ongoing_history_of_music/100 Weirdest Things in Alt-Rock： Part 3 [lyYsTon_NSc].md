# artist - The Cure

## curiosities
**Fan Self-Stabbing on Stage – Los Angeles, July 27, 1986** : During a Cure concert in Los Angeles on July 27, 1986, attended by approximately 18,000 people, an audience member named John Morland — recently broken up with his girlfriend and severely depressed — jumped onto the stage, pulled out a knife, and began stabbing himself in what appeared to be some kind of final romantic gesture intended for his ex. Much of the crowd assumed the incident was part of the show and cheered him on. Security grabbed Morland before he could do serious damage; he survived. The incident profoundly disturbed the band. "Our crowd thought it was part of the show, so they cheered him on."


# artist - The Smiths

## curiosities
**Denver Radio Station Hostage Standoff – Fan Demanded All-Smiths Playlist at Gunpoint (1987)** : In 1987, a young man walked into a Denver radio station and took over the control room at gunpoint, demanding that the station stop playing mainstream rock and broadcast exclusively The Smiths. The standoff lasted four solid hours — four hours of uninterrupted Smiths — before a SWAT team negotiator talked him into surrendering. The man appeared to be acting out some kind of doomed romantic, idealistic gesture. "With his gun, [he] took over the control room of the radio station for four solid hours. And during that time, it was all Smiths all the time."

## curiosities
**Identity Theft by Nuclear Lab Technologist – Devon Townsend, Albuquerque, 2006** : In late winter 2006, Chester Bennington's wife Talinda began receiving threatening calls and texts, friends were called from the hacked number, and Talinda lost access to her eBay and PayPal accounts. Chester hired a former Secret Service specialist who traced the intrusion to a 27-year-old single mother named Devon Townsend, employed as a technologist at a nuclear weapons research facility in New Mexico. Townsend had initially found Chester's email address in a mass email accidentally sent out to promote a tattoo parlor Chester co-owned in Tempe, Arizona. She used his birthday and zip code to access his .Mac account, then guessed his password as "Charlie" (his middle name), gaining access to email addresses, photos, home addresses, social security numbers, and bank account details — though she did not steal any money. She was arrested and charged with multiple offenses. "She was a fan. And she was bored at her job."


# artist - Pearl Jam

Eddie Vedder

## curiosities
**Stalker (1994–1996) – Believed Eddie Was Jesus and Father of Her Children, Drove Car into His House** : Between approximately 1994 and 1996, a mentally ill woman became convinced that all of Eddie Vedder's Pearl Jam songs were written personally about her. She also believed that Jesus was the father of her two children via rape, and that Eddie Vedder was Jesus — therefore the father of her children. Physical threats were made, forcing the installation of fences, security systems, and 24-hour bodyguards. Reports indicated she was armed. The situation escalated when she drove her car at 50 mph directly into Eddie's house, at which point he relocated to an inaccessible location outside Seattle. Legal proceedings produced little result. The experience contributed to Eddie's reputation as a recluse and hermit during this period, and is reflected in the song "Lukin" from *No Code* (1996). "Eddie ended up with this reputation as a recluse and a hermit, but now you know why."

# artist - Björk

## curiosities
**Ricardo Lopez Acid Bomb (September 1996) – Sulfuric Acid Book Mailed from Hollywood, Florida; Intercepted by Scotland Yard** : On September 12, 1996, Ricardo Lopez — a 21-year-old former pest control worker from Atlanta, obsessed with Björk for months — mailed a hollowed-out book rigged to spray sulfuric acid in the face of whoever opened it to Björk's London home. Lopez had documented his obsession on video over nine months; some tapes were at least 11 hours long. His stated motive was rage that Björk had been dating Black men, specifically Goldie. After mailing the package, Lopez filmed himself shaving his head, painting his nipples red, and painting his face red and green, before shooting himself in the mouth with a .38 caliber pistol on camera. Florida police found his body on September 16. Reviewing his tapes, they saw him hold up the package to the camera long enough to read the address. They traced the parcel to a south London post office, where Scotland Yard intercepted it just before it was delivered. "I am the angel of death for her."


# artist - U2

## members
Bono

## curiosities
**IRA Hit List and FBI Assassination Threat – Denver 1987, Arizona 1992** : Bono's anti-IRA statement delivered from the stage at a Denver concert on November 8, 1987 — the day an IRA bomb killed 13 people at a Remembrance Day parade — led to rumors he had been placed on an IRA hit list. In 1992 a more concrete assassination threat came through the FBI: because of U2's long advocacy for a Martin Luther King national holiday (opposed by Arizona Governor Evan Mecham), a threat was made that Bono would be shot the next time U2 played Arizona, specifically during "Pride (In the Name of Love)" at Sun Devil Stadium in Tempe on October 24, 1992. The FBI advised U2 not to play the show; when refused, they suggested dropping "Pride" from the set. Bono refused that too. He performed the song with his eyes closed to concentrate on the lyrics; when it ended, he was still alive and opened his eyes to find Adam Clayton standing directly in front of him, prepared to shield him. "Bono sang the whole thing with his eyes closed, trying to concentrate on the lyrics."


# curiosity

**Cynthia Plaster Caster – Rock Star Penis Mold Collection Since 1968, Kiss Song, Documentary** : Chicago-born Cynthia Albritten (known professionally as Cynthia Plaster Caster) developed her unique hobby after a high school art class assignment in plaster casting around 1968. Her specialty is making plaster molds of the penises of willing rock musicians; confirmed subjects include Jimi Hendrix and Jello Biafra of the Dead Kennedys, among many others. She also casts willing women's breasts. She maintains records of those who attempted but were not "up to the task." Kiss wrote "Plaster Caster" about her on their *Love Gun* album. A documentary film about her was made, and she continues to exhibit her work in art galleries. "Her thing was getting plaster casts of rock star penises."

**Celebrity Skin and Body Fluids – Company Selling Rock Star Bacteria, Skin Cells, and Fecal Matter** : A company called Celebrity Skin and Body Fluids, founded anonymously by former Hollywood personal assistants with access to their employers' private spaces, offered for sale biological material attributed to various rock musicians. Documented inventory included: Dave Navarro's personal bacteria at $5.75 per batch; Michael Stipe of R.E.M.'s dead skin cells at $12.75; Flea of the Red Hot Chili Peppers' fecal matter at $27 per cubic inch; and Billy Corgan of the Smashing Pumpkins' fecal matter at $30 per cubic inch (priced higher due to scarcity). "Why would anyone want any of this stuff? Beyond me, unless of course you were engaged in some kind of weird cloning experiment."
