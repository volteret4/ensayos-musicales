## albums
**California (2016)** : This album was recorded with Matt Skiba as guitarist, replacing Tom Delonge. The band developed 28 songs quickly and completed the recording session within three months, releasing the album on July 1, 2016. The rapid and "old-school Blink" approach proved successful, with the album reaching the top 10 in 13 countries. "They came up with 28 songs in very short order, burned through the recording session in three months and released it on July 1, 2016."
**Neighborhoods (2011)** : This album, released on September 27, 2011, marked the band's first record together in nine years and nearly three years after Travis Barker's plane crash. Its creation was a long and emotionally challenging process, exacerbated by the members' busy individual schedules, Tom DeLonge's continued work with Angels & Airwaves, Mark Hoppus's TV show commitments, Travis Barker's grief over DJ AM's death, and the absence of their longtime producer Jerry Finn. The album was one of the most anticipated releases of 2011 but sold approximately 500,000 units worldwide, which was considered less than stellar. "They called it neighborhoods, and this was the first single."

## curiosities
**Hiatus and Internal Conflicts (2005)** : In February 2005, Blink-182 announced an official hiatus, which was effectively a breakup due to widespread fatigue and interpersonal friction among the members after nearly a decade of intense work. The band had transitioned from being teenagers to adults, leading to personal changes and a desire to pursue individual paths. The record label, despite the internal issues, strictly instructed the band not to publicly state that it was the end of the band. "Part two of the Blink 182 story ended with the announcement of the hiatus in February 2005."
**Tom Delonge's Desire for Break** : Tom Delonge's decision to take a break stemmed from feeling the intense grind of the band's schedule and needing at least six months to decompress. He was disappointed that Mark Hoppus and Travis Barker didn't fully understand his need for this extended time away. "The story seems to be that Tom just wanted a break from the grind and he was disappointed that the other two guys didn't understand why he needed at least six months to decompress."
**Dislike of Reality TV Presence** : Tom Delonge expressed his disapproval of Travis Barker's involvement in reality television, specifically the MTV show "Meet the Barkers," which extensively documented Travis and his wife Shannon Mochler. The presence of TV cameras, even in the Blink-182 rehearsal space, was a source of discomfort for Tom. "Tom also wasn't a fan of Travis getting into the reality TV show game."
**Jerry Finn's Push for Other Projects** : Longtime producer Jerry Finn, along with Mark Hoppus and Travis Barker, made it clear they wanted to work on another project during Blink-182's hiatus. This desire led to the formation of Plus 44 by Mark and Travis, further solidifying the band's fragmentation. "It also didn't help the situation that the other two thirds of the band and their long time producer Jerry Finn made it very clear that they wanted to work together on another project."
**Travis Barker's Plane Crash (2008)** : On September 19, 2008, less than a month after Jerry Finn's death, Travis Barker and DJ AM were involved in a Learjet 60 plane crash in Columbia, South Carolina, after a show. The chartered plane, which Travis was persuaded to take by DJ AM, suffered underinflated tires that burst during takeoff, causing the landing gear to collapse and the plane to crash through lights, a fence, across a highway, and into an embankment. Travis and DJ AM were the only two survivors from the six people on board. "Then came the plane crash. This was September 19th, 2008."
**Hospital Reunion (2008)** : Following Travis Barker's severe injuries from the plane crash, Mark Hoppus was among the first to visit him in the hospital, and Tom Delonge also made the trip. The three members of Blink-182 met in a "sobering reunion" at Travis's bedside, prompting a realization for all three about what truly mattered in their lives and relationships. "The three guys met up in the hospital in a pretty sobering reunion."
**Reunion Announcement (2009)** : The band officially announced their reunion at the 2009 Grammy Awards. This was followed by a reunion tour during the summer, signaling their return to active music creation and performance. "Their first appearance together was at the 2009 Grammy Awards, where they announced that they were getting back together."
**Band Bureaucracy Challenges** : During the recording of their "Neighborhoods" album, the band faced significant bureaucratic challenges. While they were apart, each member had acquired their own manager, lawyers, accountants, and publicists. At one point, the three-piece band collectively had four managers, making decision-making and project coordination incredibly difficult. "At one point during the recording of neighborhoods, this three-piece band had four managers."
**Departure from Interscope Records (2012)** : In October 2012, Blink-182 split from their major label, Interscope Records. This move allowed them to pursue independent projects, such as their five-song EP "Dogs Eating Dogs," giving them complete creative control without external interference. "They left Interscope Records in October 2012."
**Tom Delonge's Second Departure (2014-2015)** : Tom Delonge quit the band again around 2014, although he briefly returned proposing group therapy led by motivational speaker Tony Robbins, which did not resolve underlying issues. Communication ceased once more, leading to indefinite delays for a planned 2013 album. His manager eventually sent an email, one week before a scheduled studio session, stating Tom's indefinite unwillingness to participate in Blink-182 activities. The band issued a statement announcing his departure, to which Tom countered that the band demanded he put all other projects on hold for an album and tour within six months. He refused these terms and officially left. "Tom's manager sent an email... saying that Tom did not want to participate in any Blink activities indefinitely."
**Max Schieber Joins (2015)** : In March 2015, following Tom Delonge's second departure, Blink-182 played two club shows with Max Schieber (likely Matt Skiba) of Alkaline Trio filling in as guitarist. Lawyers worked to formalize Tom's exit and Max's integration into the band. "In March 2015, Blink played two club shows without Tom. Max Schieber, the guitarist for a band called Alkaline Trio filled in."
**Influence on Pop Punk** : Blink-182 is considered one of the most influential pop punk bands of the 21st century. Their style and career trajectory have left a significant mark on numerous artists across the genre, inspiring bands like Sum 41, Good Charlotte, Simple Plan, Panic! At The Disco, Fall Out Boy, Hedley, Billy Talent, You Me At Six, and Paramore. "Blink has to be considered one of the most influential pop punk bands of the 21st century."
**Surviving Multiple Lifespans** : Despite the general observation that bands have an average lifespan of seven years, Blink-182 has defied this trend, navigating through several such cycles of career highs, lows, and lineup changes. "Blink 182 has gone through several of those lifetimes."

## curiosities
**Formation and Origin of Name** : Plus 44 was formed by Mark Hoppus and Travis Barker during Blink-182's hiatus, initially conceived as a more electronic project. The band's name, "Plus 44," was inspired by the international dialing code for the United Kingdom, where the idea for the band first germinated among its members. "Mark Hoppiss and Travis Barker formed a band that they called Plus 44. That name comes from the code you have to use to call the UK."
**Electronic Sound Ambition** : The original artistic vision for Plus 44 was to explore a more electronic sound. However, after a period of trial and error in recording, the band's sonic direction evolved into something different from this initial concept. "The original idea was for Plus 44 to be more electronic in its approach, but after a series of false starts and a lot of recording and secrecy, a different sound emerged."
**Plus 44 Tour Experience** : The tour supporting the album "When Your Heart Stop's Beating" was a highlight for Mark Hoppus and Travis Barker (members of Plus 44), providing them the opportunity to play small clubs again after a long time, which was a re-energizing experience. During this tour, Travis Barker broke his arm during a video shoot, causing him immense pain and forcing him to miss several gigs. "The best part about it was the tour where Mark and Tom got to play small clubs for the first time in a long time. That was really energizing."

## curiosities
**Formation and Personal Struggles** : Angels & Airwaves was formed by Tom DeLonge during Blink-182's hiatus. This period was marked by personal struggles for Tom, including a nervous breakdown attributed to the Blink-182 breakup, a severe back condition, and addiction to painkillers, which affected his clarity of thought. He dedicated months to working on new material in his home studio, teaching himself piano and audio engineering, finding the process therapeutic. "Tom was completely invisible the entire time, Plus 44 was in the news. The breakup of Blink caused what he'd later call a nervous breakdown."
**Artistic Re-invention** : The formation of Angels & Airwaves represented Tom DeLonge's desire to evolve as an artist, moving away from the "sophomore goofy teenager" persona he had embodied for many years with Blink-182. This new project allowed him to explore different artistic avenues and express a more mature perspective. "it gave Machins to be another type of artist, something away from that sophomore goofy teenager rule that he'd played for so many years."
**Political and Utopian Philosophy** : Angels & Airwaves' philosophy was deeply influenced by Tom DeLonge's engagement with politics, sparked by his endorsement of John Kerry for president and his opposition to the Iraq War (where his brother was deployed), alongside his father's leukemia diagnosis. The band adopted a strong streak of utopianism and positivity, with their motto being "Friends and Family First, Band Seconds," reflecting Tom's personal priorities. "Tom became committed to the idea of reform, and the work it would take to make this a better world. And this became baked into the philosophy of this band, Angels, and Airwaves."
**Demos Leak and Rushed Release** : The project was initially developed in secret, but its cover was blown when a fan hacked into Tom DeLonge's email, stole five demo tracks, and leaked them online. This forced the band to rush the official release of their debut album. "then some fan hacked into his email and made off with five demos and leaked them online. So with the secret out, the decision was made to rush the release."
**Unfulfilled Movie Promise** : Following the release of their debut album, Tom DeLonge expressed significant satisfaction with Angels & Airwaves' initial output and promised fans a "mind blowing full length movie" in addition to new music. However, this full-length film project never materialized. "he promised that in addition to more new music there would be some kind of mind blowing full length movie. We never saw the movie."
**Prolific Multimedia Output** : Since his departure from Blink-182, Tom DeLonge has been highly productive, releasing seven Angels & Airwaves projects, many of which are integral parts of larger multimedia endeavors. These include albums accompanied by animated films, EPs linked to novels co-authored by him, and concepts that extend into documentary series, demonstrating his broad creative scope. "There are now seven angels and airwaves releases."

## curiosities
**Reality TV Show** : Travis Barker starred in a reality television series called "Meet the Barkers" on MTV. The show documented his life with his wife, Shannon Mochler, even extending to filming inside the Blink-182 rehearsal space, which was a point of contention for Tom DeLonge. "Remember, meet the Barkers? That was an MTV show that followed Travis and his wife Shannon Mochler everywhere, including into the Blink rehearsal space."
**Fashion Company** : Travis Barker ventured into the fashion industry by establishing his own company, Famous Stars and Strapz. The brand specialized in selling t-shirts, shoes, and various types of skate gear, reflecting his personal style and interests. "Travis got into fashion, starting up a company called Famous Stars and Strapz, a self-t shirts and shoes and all kinds of skate gear."
**Hip-Hop Collaborations** : Musically, Travis Barker delved deeper into beat-making and rhythm, collaborating with a range of hip-hop artists on both recording sessions and remixes. This expanded his musical repertoire beyond rock drumming. "And musically he got deeper into beats, working with a series of hip-hop artists on recording and remixes."
**Collaboration with DJ AM** : Travis Barker formed a collaborative project with DJ AM, aiming to merge live drumming with DJ mixing. DJ AM would spin classic songs on his decks, while Travis would augment the grooves with live drums, creating a unique hybrid performance style that they released online. "Their idea was to combine a DJ's mixing with live drums. DJ A.M. would spin classic songs and his decks and Travis would augment the groove."
**Five-Year Silence with Tom DeLonge** : By mid-2008, a significant communication breakdown had occurred between Travis Barker and Tom DeLonge, with Travis noting that he hadn't spoken a word to Tom in five years. This extended silence highlighted the deep rifts within Blink-182, making the prospect of a reunion seem unlikely. "Travis hadn't said a word to Tom in five years or would be the point of getting back together of nobody's talking."
**Pre-Crash Premonition and Message** : On the way to the airport for a chartered Learjet 60 flight with DJ AM, Travis Barker had a strong premonition that something was amiss. He consumed several joints, three Vicodin, and his Xanax. Before boarding, he sent a picture of the small plane to a friend with a message expressing his unease and detailing his wishes for his children if anything were to happen to him. "Travis had a bad feeling on the way to the airport... I'm just letting you know, something doesn't feel right. The plane is really small. If anything happens to me, make sure the kids get the house and they're taken care of."
**Plane Crash Injuries and Recovery (2008)** : Travis Barker sustained severe injuries in the September 2008 Learjet crash, suffering 30 re-burns to 65% of his body and developing blood clots. He spent four months in a Georgia hospital, undergoing 27 different surgeries. Amputation was considered, but he underwent tangential excision (scraping away dead skin). The agony was so intense that he offered friends a million dollars to end his suffering. He also developed PTSD and eventually divorced his wife after the ordeal. "Travis had 30 re-burns to 65% of his body. He had blood clots all over. He was in a Georgia hospital for four months and had 27 different surgeries."
**DJ AM's Death Impact** : When Travis Barker learned of DJ AM's death from a drug overdose, it caused a significant setback in his recovery and mental state, impacting him for months. This grief further complicated the already challenging process of recording the "Neighborhoods" album with Blink-182. "when Travis found out that DJA am died of a drug overdose, that set him back months."
**Autobiographical Reflection** : In his autobiography, Travis Barker reflects on how life-altering events, such as his near-fatal plane crash, clarify one's understanding of personal relationships. He articulates a realization about who truly matters in life. "He writes in his autobiography that comes to point in life when you realize who really matters, who never did, and who always will."

# curiosity

**Average Band Lifespan** : Don Letz, a DJ, filmmaker, and confidant of The Clash, posited that the typical lifespan for a band is seven years. This period is generally sufficient for a group to form, achieve stardom, develop creative differences, and ultimately disband. "The average lifespan of a band is seven years."
**Exceptions to the Seven-Year Band Lifespan** : While many bands follow a seven-year cycle from formation to breakup, several notable groups have defied this trend, surviving multiple such periods. These exceptions include U2, The Rolling Stones, Oasis, Green Day, and Foo Fighters, demonstrating remarkable longevity. "Groups that have survived multiple seven-year cycles."
**Meet the Barkers Reality TV Show** : "Meet the Barkers" was an MTV reality show that chronicled the lives of Travis Barker and his wife, Shannon Mochler. The show's filming extended into private spaces, including the Blink-182 rehearsal room, which was a source of discomfort for band member Tom DeLonge. "That was an MTV show that followed Travis and his wife Shannon Mochler everywhere, including into the Blink rehearsal space."
**Jerry Finn's Importance to Blink-182** : Jerry Finn was the longtime producer for Blink-182, considered by many to be the "fourth member" due to his crucial role in shaping the band's sound and extracting optimal performances. His influence spanned back to the 1990s, and he developed strong relationships with everyone associated with the group over a decade. "Jerry had been pretty much the fourth member of Blink 182. He was that important in getting the right sounds and the right performances out of the band, dating all the way back to the 90s."
**Jerry Finn's Tragic Death (2008)** : In July 2008, shortly after completing work on a Morrissey album, producer Jerry Finn suffered a cerebral hemorrhage followed by a massive heart attack, which ultimately proved fatal. He was taken off life support and died on August 21st at the age of 39. His death deeply affected all members of Blink-182, contributing to a reassessment of their own lives and relationships. "He was taken off life support and he died on August 21st at the age of 39."
**Fatal Learjet Crash (2008)** : On September 19, 2008, less than a month after Jerry Finn's death, a chartered Learjet 60 carrying Travis Barker and DJ AM (among others) crashed in Columbia, South Carolina. The crash was caused by underinflated tires that burst during takeoff, leading to the collapse of the landing gear. The plane veered off the runway, erupted in sparks, crashed through barriers, and exploded about 60 seconds after Travis and DJ AM managed to escape. Pilots James Bland and Sarah Lemon, along with two other passengers, perished, leaving Travis and DJ AM as the sole survivors. "The tires, which had been underinflated, had burst."
**Consequence of Removing Shoes During Flight** : During the Learjet 60 crash, Travis Barker had, unusually, taken off his shoes when he took his seat. This seemingly minor detail had severe consequences: when he became covered in jet fuel and caught fire, his fuel-soaked socks continued to burn on his feet and legs, exacerbating his injuries significantly. "This one time, this one time, he took a shoes off when he took a seat. And that meant that fuel soaked his socks and they just kept burning and burning."
**Tangential Excision Medical Procedure** : Travis Barker underwent tangential excision as part of his recovery from severe burns. This surgical procedure involves a surgeon scraping away dead, burned skin until viable, bleeding tissue is reached. While necessary for healing, it carries risks of significant blood loss and infection. "He ended up in something called tangential excision. This is where a surgeon scrapes away dead, burned skin until it starts to bleed."
**Band Management Bureaucracy** : A significant challenge for Blink-182 during the recording of their "Neighborhoods" album was managing the complex bureaucracy that developed during their hiatus. Each of the three members had acquired separate managers, lawyers, accountants, and publicists, leading to a situation where the three-piece band had four managers at one point, making any decision-making process incredibly cumbersome. "At one point during the recording of neighborhoods, this three-piece band had four managers. Imagine trying to get anything done."
**Music Monetization Software (Mod Life)** : Tom DeLonge developed a software company called Mod Life, which was specifically designed to help musicians effectively monetize the music and content they produce. This venture reflected his interest in supporting artists' financial independence. "Then there was his software company called Mod Life, which is aimed at helping musicians monetize what they produced."
**Greatest Hits Album as a Bad Omen** : The release of a greatest hits album for a band that is officially on hiatus is often perceived as a negative sign by the record company, typically indicating that the label has lost hope for the group's future or reunion. "Whenever you see a label release a greatest hits album for a group that's supposedly on hiatus, that's usually bad news."
**Toronto International Film Festival Award** : The animated film accompanying Angels & Airwaves' album "The Dreamwalker" garnered an award at the Toronto International Film Festival. This recognition highlighted Tom DeLonge's success in multimedia projects beyond traditional music albums. "An album called The Dreamwalker came with a short animated film that won an award at the Toronto International Film Festival."
