## albums
**Number Four (1999) - STP Relaunch** : This Stone Temple Pilots album was a project that had been put on hold due to Scott Weiland's various incarcerations. After Weiland's release from jail and rehab, he and Dean DeLeo embarked on an acoustic promotional tour across the US for its relaunch. "He was ready to relaunch the Stone Temple Pilots album number four, a project that had been put on hold because of Wyland's various incursorations."
**Shangri-La Dee Da (2001) - Fifth STP Record** : This Stone Temple Pilots album was worked on in a rented estate in Malibu during Scott and Mary's first three months as new parents. Released on June 19th, 2001, it was the fifth STP record in 10 years. "Things moved quickly, and the album was in the stores on June 19th of 2001."
**Contraband (2004) - Velvet Revolver Debut** : The debut album from Velvet Revolver, released on June 8, 2004, achieved a record-breaking number one debut on the U.S. charts and later won a Grammy Award. Scott Weiland claimed to have written all the melodies and lyrics for this album, a process that took the last six months of 2003, during which he kicked heroin in residential rehab. "A record-breaking number one debut on the U.S. charts and eventually a Grammy Award."
**Libertad (2007) - Velvet Revolver Second Album** : This was Velvet Revolver's second album, which was supposed to be supported by a large international tour. However, the tour dates were largely cancelled due to Scott Weiland's DUI arrest in November 2007, leading to significant financial losses and internal band tensions. "They were supposed to go on a big international tour for their second album, Libertad."
**Happy in Galoshes (2008)** : This was Scott Weiland's second solo record, released after the 2008 Stone Temple Pilots reunion tour. It was not a commercial success but provided Scott an outlet for individual creative expression. "Not a commercial success by any means, but it allowed Scott to be creative on his own."
**A Compilation of Scott Weiland Cover Songs (2009)** : This album, whose title is verbatim, was released through Scott Weiland's website. It featured covers of various artists including The Smiths, David Bowie, Nirvana, The Flaming Lips, and The Stone Roses. "He also released an album called A Compilation of Scott while in cover songs. That's actually the title."
**Stone Temple Pilots (2010) - Self-Titled Album** : This self-titled album was released on May 21, 2010, marking a return to recording for Stone Temple Pilots after a nine-year gap since "Shangri-La Dee Da." It featured the single "Between the Lines." "The result was a self-titled album released on May 21, 2010."
**Christmas Standards Album (Year Unknown)** : Scott Weiland released an album consisting of Christmas standards sometime between his 2010 self-titled STP album and his firing from STP in 2013. "And then there was an album of Christmas standards."
**Blaster (2015) - The Wildabouts Debut** : This album by The Wildabouts, featuring Scott Weiland, was crowdfunded and released on March 31, 2015. Its first single was "White Lightning." "They crowdfunded an album entitled Blaster, which was released on March 31st, 2015."

## songs

It carries multiple meanings, referencing a drug dealer Scott knew in Atlanta who supplied him with drugs while the band recorded there, and also his then-wife, Mary Forsberg, who is half Mexican. "It's about a drug dealer he knew in Atlanta, who kept him supply with drugs whenever the band recorded there."

"A single that was just too poppy for most fans. And if you paid attention to the words, yes, it was Scott singing about his heroin addiction."
**Coma (2001)** : A "rocker" that Stone Temple Pilots wanted as the first single from their "Shangri-La Dee Da" album, but the record label insisted on "Days of the Week" for commercial reasons. "We have this rocker called coma. That should be the first no argument."
**Let Me Free (2003)** : This was one of the first songs released by Velvet Revolver, appearing on the soundtrack for "The Hulk" movie in the summer of 2003. Its release occurred approximately a month after Scott Weiland was arrested for heroin possession. "Let me free the first example of Velvet Revolver, which saw the light of day about a month after Scott was arrested again for having heroin in his car."
**Slither (2004)** : A significant single from Velvet Revolver's debut album "Contraband." "Here's Velvara Volver from Contra Band with Slither."
**Fall To Pieces (2004)** : A major single from Velvet Revolver's "Contraband" album, written by Scott Weiland after his arrest on October 23, 2003, which was also his 36th birthday. "Another big single from Contra Band was a song that Scott wrote after he was arrested on October 23rd, 2003, which just happened to be his 36th birthday."
**Between the Lines (2010)** : The first single from Stone Temple Pilots' 2010 self-titled album. **White Lightning (2015)** : The first single from The Wildabouts' crowdfunded album "Blaster." "This was the first single. It's called White Lightning."

## curiosities
**Off the Charts Life** : Even by the abnormal standards of a rock star, Scott Weiland's life was considered extremely unusual. This was evident when Slash, a rock star known for his own wild past, expressed concern for Weiland's well-being. "I mean, when a guy like Slash takes you aside and says, dude, I'm worried. You'd better take it easy. You know that you're dealing with some extraordinary circumstances."
**Post-Rehab and Jail Relaunch (Early 2000)** : In January 2000, Scott Weiland was fresh out of jail and rehab, clean and sober, ready to relaunch Stone Temple Pilots' album "Number Four," which had been delayed due to his legal issues. He was accompanied by a minder during promotional activities to help him stay out of trouble. "At this point, Scott was fresh out of jail. He was fresh out of rehab. He was clean. He was sober."
**Meeting Mary Forsberg** : Scott Weiland met Mary Forsberg in his pre-Stone Temple Pilots days when he was paid $8 an hour to drive models to appointments in a beat-up Chrysler; Mary, then 16, was one of these models. They later reconnected and became a serious, codependent couple, both struggling with drug addiction. "Scott met Mary back in his pre-Stone Temple Pilots days when he was paid $8 an hour to drive models to their various appointments and auditions in a beat up Chrysler, and Mary was one of those models."
**Undiagnosed Bipolar Disorder** : A significant factor in Scott Weiland's and Mary Forsberg's struggles was their undiagnosed bipolar disorder, which led to extreme mood swings and poor judgment, often exacerbated by self-medication with drugs like crack. Doctors initially misdiagnosed him with severe depression, and the treatments did not control his manic episodes. "A big part of their problems coping with life is that they were both undiagnosed sufferers of bipolar disorder, and this is a really important part of our story."
**Marriage to Mary Forsberg (May 21, 2000)** : Scott Weiland and Mary Forsberg were married on May 21, 2000, at the Little Door Restaurant in Los Angeles during an "elucid moment" amidst their struggles. "On May 21st 2000 at the Little Door Restaurant in LA, they got married."
**Noah Weiland's Birth and Relapse (November 9, 2000)** : Scott Weiland's first son, Noah, was born on November 9, 2000. While Mary remained clean during her pregnancy and for 18 months postpartum, Scott relapsed shortly after Noah's birth, returning home from the hospital with a prescription for Vicodin after a dental appointment. "Scott was doing well too, but on the way home from the hospital where his wife had just given birth, he had a dental appointment, and he came home with a prescription for Vikiden."
**Malibu Residence and STP Album Recording (2001)** : For the first three months as new parents, Scott and Mary Weiland moved to a rented estate in Malibu, where Stone Temple Pilots began working on what would become the album "Shangri-La Dee Da." "For the first three months as new parents, Scott and Mary moved to a rented estate in Malibu, where STP began work on an album that would eventually be called Shangri La Di Da."
**Tensions and Fights on Tour (2001)** : During the tour for "Shangri-La Dee Da," Scott Weiland's unwell state led to increased tensions and physical altercations within Stone Temple Pilots, including at the final show of the year in Texas on October 30th. "And because Scott seemed so unwell most of the time, tensions went up. There were fights."
**Las Vegas Domestic Violence Arrest (Late 2001)** : In an incident in Las Vegas, Scott Weiland attempted to leave the Hard Rock Hotel to score drugs before an STP show. When Mary Forsberg tried to block him, he lifted her out of the way. She then called security and the police, leading to his arrest on domestic violence charges and another 30-day rehab stint. "Mary says that in a desperate attempt to keep Scott from using again, she called security, and they called the cops who had Scott arrested on domestic violence charges."
**Financial Strain from Rehab (Early 2000s)** : Scott Weiland's multiple 30-day rehab commitments, each costing up to $50,000 with no refunds for early departure, led to significant financial issues for him and Mary Forsberg. He had already spent millions on drugs. "Money started becoming a serious issue. When the two are finally ended, it was definitely time for a break."
**Velvet Revolver Audition and Trust (2003)** : Scott Weiland initially disliked the music sent by the future Velvet Revolver members but agreed to jam after hearing a second, improved CD. Despite arriving hours late due to drug use, he found the chemistry with Duff McKagan, Slash, Matt Sorum, and Dave Kushner to be "bang on" and trusted Matt Sorum from a shared rehab experience. "Besides, he'd been in rehab with Matt Sorom so he trusted him."
**Motivation to Join Velvet Revolver (2003)** : Scott Weiland was motivated to join Velvet Revolver not only by the promising musical chemistry and the fact that all other members were clean (offering a potentially supportive environment), but also by the "really good" money, given his previous extensive spending on drugs. "And besides, the money was really good. Really good. And Scott had no idea how many millions he'd already spent on drugs. Several."
**Duff McKagan's Intervention and Martial Arts Training (2003)** : After an initial rehab stint for Velvet Revolver's debut album didn't work, Duff McKagan took Scott Weiland to a rural retreat in Washington state. There, a martial arts expert's intense physical training regimen helped Weiland get "straight and healthy again," leading him to find his own trainer in LA. "Duff took him to a quiet rural place in Washington state to commune with his trainer, a martial arts expert who promised that Scott would work out his issues with him intense physical training."
**Arrest for Heroin Possession (Mid-2003)** : Approximately a month after Velvet Revolver's song "Let Me Free" was released for "The Hulk" soundtrack, Scott Weiland was arrested again for having heroin in his car. This resulted in probation, conditioned on his sobriety. "Let me free the first example of Velvet Revolver, which saw the light of day about a month after Scott was arrested again for having heroin in his car."
**Arrest for Hitting Parked Car (October 27, 2003)** : On October 27, 2003, after midnight in Hollywood, Scott Weiland hit a parked car and was arrested. He was sent to residential rehab to detox, with a judge allowing him four hours a day to leave the facility to finish recording Velvet Revolver's debut album. "While driving somewhere in Hollywood after midnight, Scott hit a parked car. He was arrested again and sent to what's called residential rehab to detox yet again."
**Kicking Heroin in Rehab (Late 2003)** : During his residential rehab stint in late 2003, which coincided with the final stages of recording Velvet Revolver's debut album, Scott Weiland reportedly kicked his heroin addiction. "However, stories from all corners do corroborate on one thing. That did kick heroin during this rehab stint."
**Relationship Improvement and Financial Woes with Mary (Late 2003)** : During this period, Scott Weiland's relationship with Mary Forsberg improved after being on the rocks. They sold their San Diego home to cover bills, including legal fees for a planned divorce that ultimately did not occur. "They sold their San Diego home to pay for a bunch of bills, including some lawyers for a divorce that they were going to get, but never happened."
**Press Conference Behavior (2004)** : At a press conference for Velvet Revolver's "Contraband" album, Scott Weiland was notably separate, aloof, and unapproachable, creating palpable tension. In contrast, the former Guns N' Roses members were friendly and engaging. "He exhibited an aloofness that resulted in a puppable tension when he was in the room. And while the rest of the band was only too happy to talk, Wyland was icy cold and completely unapproachable."
**Arrest on 36th Birthday (October 23, 2003)** : Scott Weiland was arrested on October 23, 2003, which also happened to be his 36th birthday. This incident inspired the Velvet Revolver song "Fall To Pieces." "Another big single from Contra Band was a song that Scott wrote after he was arrested on October 23rd, 2003, which just happened to be his 36th birthday."
**Impact of Brother Michael's Death (2007)** : Scott Weiland was deeply affected by the death of his younger brother, Michael Weiland, in March 2007 from cardiomyopathy, a disease often linked to heavy drug use, which both brothers shared. Scott had introduced Michael to drugs, leading to a profound sense of guilt and a relapse into heavy drinking and drug use, causing paranoia and difficulty. "Scott was hit hard. He'd introduced Michael to drugs. They partied hard together, and now he was dead. So Scott started drinking heavily. And then the drug started again."
**Daughter Lucy's Birth (Circa 2007)** : Scott Weiland had a second child, a daughter named Lucy, adding another pressure to his life during a period of increasing personal struggles. "There was the pressure of a second child, a daughter named Lucy."
**Diagnosis of Bipolar Disorder (2007)** : After Mary Forsberg's destructive breakdown and subsequent commitment to a psych ward, she was finally diagnosed with bipolar disorder. Shortly thereafter, Scott Weiland also received the same diagnosis, confirming both were severely bipolar, a dangerous combination with their access to money and drugs. "It was only then that she was diagnosed with bipolar disorder, as was Scott."
**DUI Arrest (November 21, 2007)** : On November 21, 2007, Scott Weiland was arrested for driving under the influence. This incident proved disastrous for Velvet Revolver, leading to significant tour cancellations for their "Libertad" album and demands from bandmates for Weiland to reimburse their lost revenue. "And then on November 21, 2007, Scott was arrested again for driving drunk."
**Unilateral Announcement of Velvet Revolver End (March 20, 2008)** : In a fit of anger over his bandmates' demands for reimbursement after his DUI-related tour cancellations, Scott Weiland unilaterally announced to the crowd at a gig at the Carling Academy in Glasgow on March 20, 2008, that it was the "last ever Velvet Revolver tour." "Scott was so angry that on March 20, 2008, during a gig at the Carling Academy in Glasgow, and he unilaterally announced to the crowd that this was the last ever velvet revolver tour."
**Sentencing for DUI (April 28, 2008)** : On April 28, 2008, Scott Weiland was sentenced to 192 hours in jail, an 18-month alcohol program, and a $2,000 fine for his November 2007 DUI. However, for unspecified reasons, he only served part of a day in jail. "On April 28, Scott was sentenced to 192 hours in jail for the DUI back in November."
**Stone Temple Pilots Reunion Performance (2008)** : During the 2008 Stone Temple Pilots reunion tour, Scott Weiland's performance was described as "vintage Scott," where he "became the song" and exhibited breathtaking command of the stage. Despite this, a palpable "chasm" existed between him and the other band members. "Scott became the song. At the same time though, you could feel that there was a chasm between him and the other three guys."
**Soft Drive Records (Circa 2010)** : During a period of stability around 2010, Scott Weiland owned and managed his own record label, Soft Drive Records, which appeared to be doing well. "His own record label was called Soft Drive Records. It seemed to be doing well."
**English Laundry Fashion Line (Circa 2010)** : Scott Weiland became involved in fashion, designing a line for English Laundry with designer Christopher Wicks. His vision for the line was a blend of sophisticated mid-to-late 70s David Bowie aesthetics with a mid-60s Rolling Stones look, while maintaining a Savile Row feel. "And he got involved in fashion, designing a line for a label called English Laundry with a designer named Christopher Wicks."
**Claimed Sobriety and Erratic Behavior (2013-2015)** : Although Scott Weiland claimed to have been drug-free for 13 years, there were numerous signs of his continued struggle with addiction, including YouTube videos of erratic performances with The Wildabouts. His third wife, Jamie, acknowledged his drinking but stated he promised to get clean after the 2015 tour. "Although Wyland claimed to have been off drugs for 13 years, there were plenty of indications that he was prone to slipping."
**Last Gig in Toronto (December 1, 2015)** : Scott Weiland and The Wildabouts performed at Adelaide Hall in Toronto on December 1, 2015. Weiland "did not look good" and conducted a couple of interviews before what would be his final performance. "It would be his last gig."
**Death on Tour Bus (December 3, 2015)** : Scott Weiland died on Thursday, December 3, 2015, less than 48 hours after his last gig. He was found unresponsive by his tour manager in the back bedroom of The Wildabouts' tour bus in the parking lot of a hotel in Bloomington, Minnesota. Rigor mortis indicated he had died up to six hours earlier. "Less than 48 hours after that performance, Scott Wyland would be dead."
**Autopsy Results and Cause of Death (Late 2015)** : An autopsy determined Scott Weiland's cause of death was mixed drug toxicity, specifically cocaine, ethanol (alcohol), and MDA (amphetamine). The coroner also noted contributing factors like cardiovascular disease, a history of asthma, and multi-substance dependency. His death was ruled an accident and had a cause very similar to that of his brother, Michael. "The result was mixed drug toxicity, cocaine, ethanol, which is alcohol, and that amphetamine known as MDA."
**Funeral and Burial (December 13, 2015)** : A small funeral for Scott Weiland was held on December 13, 2015, at the Hollywood Forever Cemetery, attended by friends and family. The location of his remains is currently unknown. "On December 13th, there was a small funeral at the Hollywood Forever Ceremony attended by friends and family."
**Exceptional Stage Presence** : Scott Weiland was considered one of the best frontmen, capable of captivating an audience with just a microphone, his clothes, and charisma. His stage presence was described as breathtaking, making it impossible to look away. "With nothing more than a microphone and the clothes on his back in his charisma, his moves demanded that all eyes be on him."
**Rock Star Transformation** : A label executive observed Scott Weiland's profound transformation into a rock star as he dressed for a show. With each piece of clothing, he physically and mentally shifted into a different persona, ready to perform at a higher level. "He transformed a little more into a rock star. By the time he was dressed, he was a different person, someone on a different level, ready to perform, was amazing to watch."
**Performance at Edgefest 2008** : During Stone Temple Pilots' reunion at Edgefest in Toronto in 2008, Scott Weiland arrived separately from his bandmates, a common practice to maintain peace. Despite internal tensions, his confidence and prowess as a performer were amazing, cementing his reputation as one of the best from the alternative revolution of the 90s. "Again, I was amazed at his confidence and his prowess as a performer. Dude was good."
**Decades of Personal Problems** : Scott Weiland's life was marked by decades of insurmountable personal problems, including severe drug abuse, repeated failed rehab attempts, DUIs, jail time, mental illness (bipolar disorder), intermittent band membership, marital issues, and co-dependencies. These issues ultimately contributed to his early demise. "Behind it all, though, were decades of insurmountable personal problems. ## albums
**Number Four (1999) - Post-Incarceration Relaunch** : This album was delayed due to Scott Weiland's various incarcerations. **Shangri-La Dee Da (2001) - Fifth Record in 10 Years** : Recorded in Malibu while Scott Weiland and Mary Forsberg adjusted to new parenthood, this album was released on June 19, 2001. It was originally conceived as a double album, a tribute to Andrew Wood of Mother Love Bone, but the record company intervened to simplify it to a single album to get their career back on track. The album received a lukewarm reception and was the band's fifth record in 10 years. "This record was supposed to be a double album, something about it being a tribute to Andrew Wood of the Seattle band Mother Love Bone, who died of an overdose back in 1980."
**Stone Temple Pilots (2010) - Self-Titled Reunion Album** : This self-titled album was released on May 21, 2010, marking the band's return to recording after a nine-year hiatus since "Shangri-La Dee Da." Its release followed a successful 2008-2009 reunion tour and was spurred by Atlantic Records' suggestion to capitalize on the regained momentum. "The result was a self-titled album released on May 21, 2010."

**Scott Weiland Arrived Separately at Edgefest (2008)** : During the 2008 reunion tour, Scott Weiland often arrived at gigs, such as Edgefest in Toronto, separately from his bandmates. This was "standard operating procedure" at the time, employed to help maintain a fragile peace within the group. "Scott arrived separately from his other bandmates, standard operating procedure in those days to help keep the fragile peace."
**Plan for New Album (Late 2008)** : After successfully completing their 73-show reunion tour in 2008, Stone Temple Pilots agreed to try and make another album. "And when it was done, it was agreed that they should try to make another album."
**Atlantic Records Suggests New Album (2009)** : Following the success of the 2008-2009 reunion tour, Atlantic Records, Stone Temple Pilots' former label, suggested it was time for the band to record another album, nine years after "Shangri-La Dee Da," due to renewed momentum. "The 2008-2009 STP reunion tour went well enough for Atlantic STP's old record label to suggest that it was time for another album."
**Firing of Scott Weiland (February 27, 2013)** : On February 27, 2013, Stone Temple Pilots officially terminated Scott Weiland, issuing a one-line statement. "Even temple pilots have announced they have officially terminated Scott Wyland. In other words, they fired him."

## curiosities
**Formation as a Supergroup (2003)** : Formed in 2003, Velvet Revolver began with former Guns N' Roses members Duff McKagan, Slash, and Matt Sorum, who then recruited Dave Kushner from Wasted Youth. They auditioned several singers, including Josh Todd of Buckcherry, Travis Meeks of Days of the New, and B-Real of Cypress Hill, before settling on Scott Weiland. "Susan told Mary that three guys from Guns and Roses had kissed and made up and were forming a new band."
**Scott Weiland's Initial Reluctance and Trust (2003)** : Scott Weiland initially disliked the music presented by the band, but a second demo convinced him to jam. He showed up late due to drug use, but found unexpected chemistry and a low ego factor, particularly trusting Matt Sorum due to a shared rehab experience. "Matt, to Scott's surprise, the ego factor was low and the chemistry was bang on. Besides, he'd been in rehab with Matt Sorom so he trusted him."
**Band Members' Sobriety (2003)** : The members of Velvet Revolver (McKagan, Slash, Sorum, Kushner) were all 100% clean from drugs at the time of their formation, providing a potential environment to help Scott Weiland get clean as well. "And all the other guys had been in trouble with drugs too and were all 100% clean. So maybe this was the environment that would help him get clean too."
**Scott Weiland's Rehab Before Debut Album (2003)** : Prior to recording their debut album, Scott Weiland underwent another trip to rehab to get straight. When that proved insufficient, Duff McKagan arranged for him to train with a martial arts expert in Washington state, which helped Weiland achieve sobriety and health. "First there was another trip to rehab to straighten out a bit before the band got to work on their debut album."
**Movie Soundtrack Debut (2003)** : The band's first public music release was a couple of soundtrack songs for "The Hulk" movie in the summer of 2003, approximately a month before Scott Weiland's arrest for heroin possession. "The first anyone ever heard of this new group was via the movie The Hulk, which came out on the summer of 2003."
**Manufactured Supergroup (2004)** : Velvet Revolver was openly acknowledged as a "manufactured product" assembled by managers, essentially a combination of Guns N' Roses members with Scott Weiland and another musician, rather than a naturally formed group. "It was a manufactured product put together as a supergroup by various managers. It was basically guns and roses with Scott Wyland and some other guy."
**Tense Press Conference Dynamics (2004)** : At a press conference for the "Contraband" album, the former Guns N' Roses members (Duff, Slash, Matt) were friendly and engaging, while Dave Kushner was quiet. Scott Weiland, however, was totally separate, aloof, and unapproachable, creating palpable tension and doubt about the band's longevity. "He exhibited an aloofness that resulted in a puppable tension when he was in the room. And while the rest of the band was only too happy to talk, Wyland was icy cold and completely unapproachable."
**Longevity Beyond Expectations (2004-2009)** : Despite initial skepticism about their durability (many doubted they would last more than one album and tour), Velvet Revolver remained together for five years and released two albums. "No one wanted to bet on this band lasting more than one album and one tour. That however turned out to be wrong. Velvara Volver held it together for five years in two albums."
**Rough Start to 2007** : The year 2007 began difficult for the band with the death of Matt Sorum's younger brother from brain cancer in early March, followed by the death of Scott Weiland's younger brother, Michael, a month later. "Things started off rough for Velvara Volver in 2007. Former Matt Sorum's little brother died of brain cancer the first week of March."
**Tour Cancellations Due to Scott Weiland's DUI (2007)** : Scott Weiland's DUI arrest on November 21, 2007, caused a "disaster" for Velvet Revolver, leading to the cancellation of many dates on their international tour for the "Libertad" album. Japan refused the band entry, and Australia dates were cancelled. "Japan refused the band entry outright. Australia was canceled."
**Band's Financial Demands on Scott Weiland (2008)** : Following the extensive tour cancellations caused by Scott Weiland's DUI, the other band members were "pissed" and demanded that Weiland reimburse them out of his own pocket for all the lost revenue, which further strained internal relations. "They demanded that since this was Scott's fault, that he reimburse all of them for the lost revenue out of his own pocket."
**Scott Weiland Declares Band's End (March 20, 2008)** : Angered by the financial demands, Scott Weiland unilaterally announced to the crowd at a Glasgow gig on March 20, 2008, that it would be the "last ever Velvet Revolver tour." The band performed eight more shows, with their final gig in Amsterdam on April 1, 2008. "Scott was so angry that on March 20, 2008, during a gig at the Carling Academy in Glasgow, and he unilaterally announced to the crowd that this was the last ever velvet revolver tour."
**End with Scott Weiland (April 1, 2008)** : Velvet Revolver's final gig with Scott Weiland as their singer took place in Amsterdam on April 1, 2008, effectively marking the end of his tenure with the band. "So velvet revolver was done, at least with Scott as their singer."
**Talk of Returning to Velvet Revolver (Circa 2010)** : After a period of stability for Scott Weiland around 2010, there was discussion about a potential return to Velvet Revolver, but the band ultimately did not want him back due to his continued struggles with addiction. "And there was talk about returning to Velvet Revolver. And frankly neither did STP. As far as they were concerned, Wylin had gone back to his old ways."

**Bipolar Disorder Symptoms** : Bipolar disorder is characterized by extreme mood swings, including severe depression and periods of very happy, high energy.
