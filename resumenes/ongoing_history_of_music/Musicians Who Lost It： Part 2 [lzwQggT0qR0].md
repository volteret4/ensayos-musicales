## curiosities
**Band Formation and Influence** : Killing Joke is described as a heavy post-punk group originating from England, which began releasing records in 1980. The band has been a significant influence on a diverse range of artists, including Tool, Nine Inch Nails, Metallica, Nirvana, the Foo Fighters, Jane's Addiction, Marilyn Manson, and Soundgarden. Additionally, Jimmy Page of Led Zeppelin is noted as a massive admirer of their work. "Killing joke has been a big influence on tool, 9-inch nails, Metallica, both Nirvana and the Food Fighters, Jane's Addiction, Marilyn Manson and Soundgarden."
**Jazz Coleman Quits for Apocalypse (1982)** : In February 1982, following a performance, singer Jazz Coleman announced his departure from Killing Joke and his intention to move to Iceland. He was convinced that the apocalypse was near and believed Iceland offered the safest refuge. This conviction stemmed from his deep fascination with the occult and a growing paranoia developed after investigating various conspiracy theories concerning the end of the world. Shortly after, guitarist Jordy Walker joined him in Iceland, seemingly persuaded by Coleman's apocalyptic reasoning. "After a gig, he announced he was quitting killing joke and moving to Iceland."
**Formation of Niceland** : During their time in Iceland, following Jazz Coleman's departure from Killing Joke due to his apocalyptic beliefs, he and Jordy Walker engaged with the local rock scene, assisting various musicians. It was during this period that they formed a new band, which they named Niceland, before eventually returning to England. "after spending time helping various people within the Icelandic rock scene and forming a band they called Niceland, jazz and Jordy returned to England."
**Jazz Coleman's Disappearance (2012)** : In July 2012, Jazz Coleman unexpectedly disappeared. He appeared to be upset about Killing Joke's impending tour with The Cult and expressed a strong desire to dissociate himself from both the tour and the band, which was publicly voiced in a scathing Facebook post. Approximately a month later, he was located living a "nomadic existence" in the Western Sahara. Coleman subsequently claimed that the controversial Facebook post was not authored by him but by an imposter. "Jazz seemed angry that killing joke was going on tour with the cult, and he didn't want to have anything to do with the road trip or the band."

His mental state deteriorated into delusional behavior, including watching the Weather Channel for 24 consecutive hours, painting his walls and floor black, and engaging in conversations with his collection of stuffed animals, a behavior that became concerning when he claimed the animals began to speak back to him. "He grew paranoid and carried a gun, even just to get from the house to the mailbox."
**House Looting Post-Rehab (1995)** : After a period at a rehabilitation clinic in the Arizona desert, Dave Gahan was released on August 27, 1995. "But when he checked out on August 27, 1995 and returned to his house, he found that it had been completely looted, burgl'd."
**Suicide Attempts and Overdose (1995-1996)** : While staying at the Sunset Marquis hotel in August 1995, Dave Gahan attempted suicide by slashing his wrists in the bathroom mirror. After waking in a psychiatric ward, his primary focus was getting high again, seemingly indifferent to the fact that a suicide attempt was a felony in California. He later made another unsuccessful attempt to hang himself in his bathroom. His struggles culminated on May 27, 1996, when after attending a party and checking back into the Sunset Marquis, he injected a mixture of heroin and cocaine, resulting in a massive overdose. At 1:15 AM on May 28, an unknown person called 911, and paramedics found him clinically dead for two minutes due to full cardiac arrest. "When he woke up in the psychiatric ward, all he could think about was getting high again."
**Post-Overdose Recovery (1996)** : Following his release from the hospital and subsequently from jail on drug charges after his massive overdose in May 1996, Dave Gahan immediately returned to using heroin ("smack"). However, this time, he experienced no "buzz" or effect from the drug. This profound lack of sensation prompted him to voluntarily admit himself to the Exodus Recovery Center in Marina del Rey, California, a facility notably known for having treated Kurt Cobain shortly before his death. At Exodus, Gahan embraced accountability for his actions, achieved sobriety, cleaned up, and has reportedly maintained a clean and straight lifestyle since late 1998. "But this time, there was no buzz. There was nothing."
