## songs

British psychologist Simon Manchip interpreted this and the subsequent line, "I don't feel the passion anymore," as indicators of mental illness. ## curiosities
**Discovery of Body (1994)** : Kurt Cobain's body was discovered at approximately 8:40 AM on April 8, 1994, by an electrician named Gary Smith. Smith subsequently contacted a local radio station, believing he had obtained a major news scoop. "He stumbled on the scene at about 840 on the morning of April 8, 1994."
**Official Cause of Death (1994)** : On April 8, 1994, the King County Medical Examiner issued a statement describing the cause of death as "A shotgun wound to the head and at this time the wound appears to be self-inflicted." Within 24 hours, his death was officially ruled a suicide. "24 hours later Kurt's death was officially declared a suicide."
**Divorce Intentions (1994)** : Prior to his death, there was evidence suggesting Kurt Cobain was attempting to divorce his wife, Courtney Love. This desire for divorce is considered by some investigators as a potential motive for foul play. "the evidence that Kurt was trying to divorce Courtney Love."
**Sense of Danger (1994)** : During the final weeks of his life, Kurt Cobain reportedly believed that he was in some form of danger. This belief has been cited by conspiracy investigators as a point of suspicion surrounding his death. "Kurt believed that he was somehow in danger of, well, something during the last weeks of his life."
**Disappearance from Rehab (1994)** : On April 1, 1994, Kurt Cobain disappeared from the Exodus Recovery Center in Los Angeles after spending two nights in the facility. He subsequently flew back to Seattle the following morning. "On April 1, 1994, Kurt disappeared from the Exodus Recovery Center in Los Angeles."
**Missing Credit Card (1994)** : Kurt Cobain's MasterCard was canceled by Courtney Love sometime on April 1, 1994. When his body was discovered, this credit card was missing. "When Kurt's body was discovered that card was missing."
**Unusual Credit Card Activity (1994)** : Following the cancellation of his MasterCard, multiple attempts were made to use the card on April 4, 1994, and several subsequent days. These attempts included significant cash advances of $5,000, $2,500, and $1,500 over the phone. "one time the holder of the card tried to take a $5,000 cash advance over the phone."
**Final Credit Card Attempt (1994)** : On the morning of April 8, 1994, the same day Kurt Cobain's body was found, an attempt was made to use his canceled credit card to purchase $43.29 worth of flowers. "somebody tried to use Kurt's credit card to buy $43.29 worth of flowers."
**Missing Persons Report (1994)** : On Monday, April 4, 1994, a missing persons report for Kurt Cobain was filed. Courtney Love, his wife, made this call, pretending to be Kurt's mother, Wendy Cobain. "It was Courtney that called that report in."
**Wording of Missing Persons Report (1994)** : The missing persons report filed for Kurt Cobain was worded as follows: "Mr. Cobain ran away from California facility and flew back to Seattle. He also body shotgun and maybe suicidal." This wording became a critical point in later investigations, particularly as it seemed to pre-emptively suggest the circumstances of his death. "Mr. Cobain ran away from California facility and flew back to Seattle. He also body shotgun and maybe suicidal."
**House Search Omission (1994)** : On April 7, 1994, at 2:15 AM, Detective Tom Grant and Kurt Cobain's best friend, Dylan Carlson, conducted a thorough search of Cobain's house in Seattle. Despite searching "top to bottom," they failed to check the greenhouse in the backyard, as Carlson reportedly forgot to mention its existence to Grant. "They didn't go out into the backyard to check the greenhouse."
**Presence of Diazepam and Morphine (1994)** : An autopsy on Kurt Cobain's body confirmed the presence of diazepam and a high concentration of 1.52 milligrams of morphine per liter of blood. Heroin, when injected, converts into morphine in the body, indicating a significant drug dose. "a concentration of 1.52 milligrams of morphine per liter of blood."
**Incapacitating Drug Dose (1994)** : Dr. Cyril Wecht, a pathologist noted for his work on the JFK assassination, stated that such a high dose of morphine would incapacitate even hardcore addicts within seconds. He maintained that Kurt Cobain would have been unable to aim a gun and pull the trigger with that amount of drugs in his system. "Kurt would have never, ever been able to aim the gun and pull the trigger with that kind of smack in his bloodstream."
**Questionable Method of Suicide (1994)** : Critics questioned why Kurt Cobain would use a shotgun to commit suicide, given the extremely high dose of heroin (morphine) in his system. It was suggested that a lethal injection of heroin would have allowed for a more peaceful death and a "better looking corpse." "Why not just inject a lethal dose of heroin, peacefully go to sleep, and die?"
**Combed Hair Appearance (1994)** : Several witnesses, including the electrician who discovered Kurt Cobain's body, reported that his hair appeared to have been combed. This observation was deemed unusual for someone who had just sustained a shotgun wound to the head. "it looked like Kurt's hair had been combed."
**Lack of Barricade (1994)** : Contrary to early reports, Kurt Cobain had not barricaded himself in the room above the greenhouse where his body was found. A stool was present by the door, but it was too short to effectively wedge the door shut. "Now there was a stool by the door, but it was too short to wedge the door shut."
**Shotgun Load (1994)** : The shotgun found with Kurt Cobain contained three loaded shells. This raised questions for some investigators, who wondered why a person intending to kill themselves would load more than one shell. "If Kurt bought the gun to kill just himself, why would he load it up with more than one shell?"
**Disputed Fingerprint Evidence (1994)** : Police reports claimed that "Marks Fund" on Kurt Cobain's hands were consistent with firing a Remington M11 shotgun, but Detective Tom Grant stated he was unable to duplicate these results with his own M11. Furthermore, a forensics report from May 6, 1994, by senior ID technician T. Geronimo, stated that "The four cards of lifted latent prints contain no legible prints" from the shotgun. "The four cards of lifted latent prints contain no legible prints."
**Greenhouse as Lookout (1994)** : Detective Tom Grant firmly believed that Kurt Cobain was using the greenhouse as a "little lookout tower" over his property and was in fear of his life. Grant hypothesized that Cobain intended to fly out of Seattle within hours or days, suggesting he was preparing to leave. "That was like a little lookout tower for him over his whole property. And I do firmly believe that he was in fear of his life."
**Suicide Note Analysis (1994)** : Detective Tom Grant did not believe the note found near Kurt Cobain's body was a suicide note, but rather a "retirement speech" to his fans. He noted that the last five lines, which included the phrase "which will be happier without me," appeared to be in a different handwriting, a conclusion supported by two handwriting analysts. "the last five lines of the note look like they were in someone else's handwriting."
**Absence of Explicit Suicide Wish in Note (1994)** : The alleged suicide note found with Kurt Cobain's body contains no explicit statement from him about wanting to kill himself. This observation is a key point for those questioning the official suicide verdict. "no where the note does Kurt say anything about wanting to kill himself."
**Second Note Discovery (1994)** : Courtney Love claimed that several months after Kurt Cobain's death, she discovered a second note from him in a sealed envelope under pillows in her bedroom. This discovery was considered odd because Tom Grant and Dylan Carlson had searched "everywhere including under pillows and mattresses" on April 7, 1994, without finding it. "This is rather odd because when Tom and Dylan searched the house on the morning of April the 7th, they looked everywhere including under pillows and mattresses, but Courtney insists that's where she found this letter."
**Content of Second Note (1994)** : The second note, allegedly found by Courtney Love, was not suicidal in nature. Instead, it purportedly stated that Kurt Cobain was leaving her and Seattle. "Kurt allegedly tells Courtney that he's leaving her and Seattle."
**Lack of Letters to Family (1994)** : A point of contention for investigators is why Kurt Cobain did not leave any letters or messages for his mother or his daughter, Frances Bean, if he intended to take his own life. "why didn't Kurt leave anything for his mum or his daughter?"
**Disputed Internet Photo (Post-1994)** : Detective Tom Grant asserts that an infamous internet photo circulating of Kurt Cobain's head after his death is a fake. He believes it is a "stock autopsy photo of someone who got caught in a lawnmower" and that the actual wounds, while fatal, were not as dramatic or severe as depicted. "He maintains it's just some stock autopsy photo of someone who got caught in a lawnmower."
**Alleged Mutilation at Funeral Home (2003)** : Independent journalist Richard Lee reported on April 13, 2003, that Kurt Cobain's body was "mutilated while at the funeral home by one or more employees." Lee described the body as being "horribly abused." "the body was mutilated while at the funeral home by one or more employees."
**Lack of Exit Wound (Post-1994)** : Lee Remington, an investigator from Seattle, elaborated on inconsistencies regarding the fatal head wound. He claimed that even with a light load in the shotgun, there should have been more damage and, critically, no exit wound. Remington concluded that it would have been impossible for someone to shoot themselves in the head in the manner Kurt Cobain was found. "he believes that it was impossible for someone to shoot themselves in the head the way Kurt Cobain was."
**Unsigned Suicide Note (Post-1994)** : Lee Remington pointed out that the suicide note attributed to Kurt Cobain was not signed with a full signature; instead, the name "Kurt" was merely "printed out in small letters." "the suicide note wasn't signed. Kurt was printed out in small letters, no signature."
**Financial Motive for Murder (Post-1994)** : Courtney Love's biological father, Hank Harrison, suggested a strong financial motive for Kurt Cobain's death. He posited that if Cobain had lived that weekend, he would have withdrawn all his money from bank funds, signed divorce papers, and other contracts, which would have significantly cut Courtney and other individuals out of substantial sums. "Kurt hadn't been chilled that weekend, he would have gone to the bank and taken all the money out of the funds."
**Mother's Dismissal of Tom Grant (Post-1994)** : Wendy Cobain, Kurt's mother, issued a statement dismissing Detective Tom Grant as an "inept private investigator" and a "sleaze bag." She accused him of trying to gain "15 minutes of fame" and noted that the family had unsuccessfully tried for months to get him to sign a confidentiality agreement. "Tom Grant is the same inept private investigator who was hired by the family to find Kurt the week he was missing."
**Father's Public Statement (2004)** : In April 2004, Kurt Cobain's biological father, Donald Cobain, published a letter to the editor in Us Weekly magazine. In this letter, he publicly dismissed those questioning Kurt's death, telling them to "get a life." "Donald Cobain, Kurt's biological father basically tells all these people to get a life."

**Alleged Overdose Story (1994)** : After Kurt Cobain's disappearance from rehab, Courtney Love, who was in Los Angeles promoting a new Hole album, was reportedly "freaked out." She allegedly planted a story in the press claiming she had overdosed, theorizing that this would prompt Kurt to call her. "She allegedly planted a story in the press, saying that she overdosed."
**Cancellation of Kurt's Credit Card (1994)** : Sometime on April 1, 1994, the day Kurt Cobain disappeared from rehab, Courtney Love had his MasterCard canceled. This action was later explained by her to Detective Tom Grant as an attempt to track Kurt's activities. "Sometime that day, she also had Kurt's master card canceled."
**Hiring Detective Tom Grant (1994)** : On Easter Sunday, April 3, 1994, Courtney Love hired Detective Tom Grant. She initially claimed to him that someone was using her husband's credit card, but later clarified that it was Kurt himself and she wanted to track his movements. "Easter Sunday, April 3, Courtney called my office in Beverly Hills and told me someone was using her husband's credit card."
**False Missing Persons Report (1994)** : On Monday, April 4, 1994, Courtney Love filed a missing persons report for Kurt Cobain, falsely identifying herself as Kurt's mother, Wendy Cobain. Tom Grant believed she did this to control the report's wording, which stated Kurt "ran away from California facility and flew back to Seattle. He also body shotgun and maybe suicidal." "On Monday April 4th she called in a missing persons report and she pretended to be Kurt's mother Wendy Cobain."
**Alleged Murder-for-Hire Offer (1993)** : Elden Hoke (Elduchet), singer for The Mentors, claimed in the April 1996 edition of High Times Magazine that in December 1993, Courtney Love offered him $50,000 to kill her husband, Kurt Cobain. Hoke quoted her as saying, "I need you to blow my old man's head off. I'll give you $50,000 to blow his effing head off." "Courtney Love offered him $50,000 to kill her husband."
**Corroborated Meeting Location (1993)** : Elden Hoke's claim about Courtney Love offering him money was partially corroborated by the owner of a record store called Rockshop on Wilcox Avenue in Los Angeles. Hoke stated Courtney pulled up in a limousine outside the store and exchanged car keys, a detail the owner confirmed. "The owner of the store corroborates Elduchet's story."
**Follow-Up Attempt for Hit (1994)** : Elden Hoke further claimed that in March 1994, Courtney Love called the Rockshop, looking to give him "the word to go ahead" with the alleged murder plot. However, Hoke was on tour at the time and missed the call, preventing them from connecting again. "Courtney until March 1994 when she allegedly called the store looking to give Elle the word to go ahead."
**Motive for Alleged Murder (Post-1994)** : Elden Hoke theorized that Courtney Love had Kurt Cobain "whacked" because he was preparing to divorce her on adultery charges. Hoke believed she needed him dead quickly to secure financial benefits. "I think Kurt was getting ready to divorce her for adultery charges. The theory goes, she had to have him whacked right away so she could get the money."
**Confidentiality Agreements (Post-1994)** : In the months following Kurt Cobain's death, Courtney Love was reportedly very concerned with having all her friends and associates sign confidentiality agreements. This action raised suspicions for some investigators. "why in the months after Kurt's death was Courtney so concerned about getting all her friends and associates to sign some kind of confidentiality agreement?"
**Discovery of Second Note (Post-1994)** : Courtney Love stated that several months after Kurt Cobain's death, she found a second note from him in a sealed envelope under pillows in her bedroom. This note allegedly indicated Kurt was leaving her and Seattle, and was not suicidal. "Courtney says she found it in a sealed envelope in her bedroom under her pillows."
**Father's Belief in Her Involvement (Post-1994)** : Hank Harrison, Courtney Love's biological father, publicly stated his belief that his daughter had "something to do with" Kurt Cobain's death, or at least benefited from it. He claimed she had confessed to "various aspects of it" in letters and interviews. "Do you believe your daughter had anything to do with Kurt's death? Oh, sure."
**Financial Gain from Death (Post-1994)** : Hank Harrison suggested that Courtney Love benefited significantly financially from Kurt Cobain's death. He claimed that under a prenuptial agreement, she would have received less, but after his death, she "got everything," and even the "baby's money is gone too." "whereas now she got everything and she now says that the baby's money is gone too."
