## albums
**In A Flat Field (1980) - Debut Album** : Released in the fall of 1980, this record set the pace for the British Goth movement with its distinctive sound and atmosphere. "The first Bajaus album was released in the fall of 1980. The album was called In A Flat Field."
**The Skies Gone Out (Year not specified) - Career Bestseller** : This, their third record, became the biggest seller of their career. "The Skies Gone Out, the biggest seller of their career."
**Burning from the Inside (Year not specified) - Last Official Album** : This album was the last official Bauhaus record and was largely recorded while singer Peter Murphy was hospitalized with pneumonia. The rest of the band proceeded with the recording, discovering they could work effectively without him, which contributed to the band's internal divisions and eventual split. "The resulting album was called Burning from the Inside. And this was the last official Bajaus singer."

## songs
**Bela Lugosi's Dead (1979) - Debut Single** : The band's first single, issued by indie label Small Wonder in August 1979. It was an atmospheric piece featuring a bossanova beat, a three-note bass line by David J, a reggae feel from Daniel Ash, and an erotic, vampiric vocal approach by Peter Murphy, inspired by the actor who played Dracula. The song was long, clocking in at over nine and a half minutes, and used minimal special effects like a cheap echo machine and Daniel's amplifier sound. It was later performed in the David Bowie film "The Hunger." "And in August 1979, Small Wonder issued the one and only Bajaus single. They called it Bella Legosi's Dead."

## curiosities
**Early Formation and Naming** : The band formed from a series of earlier projects like This Bag Is Not a Toy, The Submerged Tenth, The Craze, Jackplug, and The Sockets around 1977. They initially considered names like The Drugs or Frees before settling on Bauhaus 1919, inspired by a German art movement, four days before their first gig. Their name was shortened to just Bauhaus within a few weeks of their debut performance. "Before there was Bajaus, there were a series of bands. This bag is not a toy, the submerged tenth, the craze, jackplug, and the sockets, none of which went anywhere."
**Godfathers of Goth** : Bauhaus is often described as the "Godfathers of Goth" and was one of the founders and pioneers of the original British Goth movement. Their glamour, energy, art school sensibilities, and attitude of doom and gloom proved infectious, spreading their sound, look, and attitude globally. "The band often described as the Godfathers of Goth."
**Influential 80s Band** : Existing for barely four years and releasing just four albums, Bauhaus became one of the most influential bands of the 80s, with their work still affecting music at the dawn of the 21st century. "A group that existed for barely four years and released just four albums. But in that time, they became one of the most influential bands of the 80s."
**Band Mobile** : The group purchased a hearse from an undertaker and converted it into their official touring vehicle, affectionately naming it the "Bauhaus Mobile." "The group also bought a Hearse from a hunder taker and converted it into their official touring vehicle. They called it their Bajaus Mobile."
**David Bowie's Advocacy** : David Bowie became a significant fan of Bauhaus and actively lobbied for the band to be cast in his vampire movie, "The Hunger." Their performance of "Bela Lugosi's Dead" was featured in a scene filmed at the London club Heaven. "David Bowie had become a big fan and he lobbied for the band to be cast in a vampire movie he was doing called The Hunger."
**Internal Strife and Breakup** : The band's progress was halted by internal issues including egos, creative differences, and touring pressures. Peter Murphy's illness with pneumonia during the recording of their next album led to the rest of the band recording "Burning from the Inside" without him, which highlighted their ability to function independently and created a "battle of egos," leading to their eventual split. "Ego's got on the way. Creative differences, the pressures of touring. And then Peter got very sick with pneumonia, just as the group was scheduled to start recording their next album."
**Final Performance** : Bauhaus's final gig on July 5, 1983, took place at the Hammersmith Palais in London, where David J famously grabbed the microphone at the end of the show and declared, "rest in peace," signaling the band's demise. "It was one final gig. July 5th, 1983, at the Hammersmith Pele in London."
**Reunion Tour** : Fifteen years and four days after their last London show in 1983, Bauhaus reunited for a performance at the Hollywood Athletic Club, with Peter Murphy finally agreeing to a reunion after years of requests, capitalizing on the "80s retro craze." This successful gig was followed by several sellout shows at the Hollywood Palladium, where 7,000 tickets sold in just over 15 minutes, leading to a full-tilt reunion tour. "15 years and four days after that last show in London, Baha's appeared on stage at the Hollywood Athletic Club."

## albums
**Deep (1990) - Solo Album** : Released in the spring of 1990, this album sold enough copies to allow Peter Murphy to move his family to Turkey, where he subsequently began to study Islam and became a Muslim. ## curiosities
**Iconic Maxell Commercials** : In 1981, Peter Murphy was chosen to appear in TV commercials for Maxell Cassettes, receiving 10,000 pounds for two shoots. He became the recognizable figure in the enduring magazine ads depicting a person sitting in a chair being "blown away" by music from a stereo, an image that remained in use for over 15 years. "The guy in the chair? That's Peter Murphy."
**Pneumonia and Band Division** : Peter Murphy became severely ill with pneumonia just before Bauhaus was scheduled to record their next album. This led to the rest of the band recording "Burning from the Inside" without him, a process that revealed they could work effectively independently and contributed to the internal split of Bauhaus into two factions: Peter Murphy and everyone else. "And then Peter got very sick with pneumonia, just as the group was scheduled to start recording their next album."
**Post-Bauhaus Projects and Dali's Car** : After Bauhaus splintered on July 5, 1983, Peter Murphy briefly formed the band Dali's Car, which released one album before he embarked on a solo career. "Peter Murphy briefly formed a band called Dali's Car, which lasted for one album before he went solo."
**Withdrawal to Turkey and Islam** : In the early 1990s, Peter Murphy began to withdraw from Western society, moving his family to Turkey after the success of his 1990 album "Deep." There, he dedicated himself to studying Islam and eventually converted, financing his full-time Arabic studies and other pursuits through earnings from his music and other ventures, though these paled in comparison to potential reunion earnings. "In the spring of 1990, he released an album that sold enough copies to allow him to move his family to Turkey, where he began to study Islam, eventually becoming a Muslim."
**Support for Nine Inch Nails** : Peter Murphy introduced thousands of people to the then-emerging band Nine Inch Nails from Cleveland, who served as his opening act during his tour to promote the "Deep" album. "he introduced thousands of people to his opening act. Little band out of Cleveland called Nine-inch Nails."
**Continued Loyal Fanbase** : Peter Murphy continues to maintain a loyal fanbase for his solo work. "Peter Murphy continues to have his loyal fans."
