# artist - Rush

## albums
**2112 (1976)** : A concept album inspired by Ayn Rand, the objectiveist philosopher who wrote books like Atlas Shrugged. The album tells a complex narrative involving the Red Star, the Solar Federation, priests who run the temples of Syrikes, and their command over existence, featuring a rebel guitar player who rises up and commits suicide.

# artist - Smashing Pumpkins

## albums
**Machina/The Machines of God (2000)** : A double album released in 2000. It was conceived during a period when Billy Corgan was bitter and disillusioned by the band's media portrayal, leading to the group's breakup by the end of the year. # artist - Queens of the Stone Age

## albums
**Songs for the Deaf (2002)** : Released in the summer of 2002, this album was conceived as a concept record. It is intended to be the soundtrack of a drive from Los Angeles through to Joshua Tree National Park in the desert, with the listener flipping through small-town radio stations along the way. The artwork of some versions of the album depicts the dashboard of a Fiat 124 Spider to emphasize this concept.

# artist - The Streets

## albums

# artist - Green Day

## genres

## labels

## albums
**American Idiot (2004)** : Released in the fall of 2004, this album was designed as a full-blown rock opera. Its creation was accidental, occurring after the band mysteriously lost the master tapes for a planned record titled "Cigarettes and Valentines." They decided to start from scratch, with each band member contributing short musical fragments. These fragments were then strung together, and the band drew inspiration from The Who's rock operas and Andrew Lloyd Webber's Jesus Christ Superstar. The album tells the story of Jesus of Suburbia, a powerless anti-hero who despises his existence and the world, eventually leaving his town for the city. There, he encounters St. Jimmy, a punk rock kid, and "what's her name," a riot girl type. The libretto's interpretation suggests St. Jimmy commits suicide and that Jesus of Suburbia and St. Jimmy are ultimately the same person. The album sold approximately 10 million copies (CDs and DVDs), becoming the most successful alt-rock concept album ever and one of the most successful concept records of all time.
**21st Century Breakdown (2009)** : Released on May 15, 2009, this was Green Day's second consecutive rock opera. Set in Detroit, it features main characters Christian and Gloria as they navigate life in America in the post-George W. Bush era. To make the narrative more comprehensible, the album is divided into acts: Heroes and Cons, Charlatans and Saints, and Horseshoes and Hand Grenades. Billy Joe's frequent first-person singing suggests that Christian and Gloria serve as vehicles for telling his autobiography.

## songs
**Boulevard of Broken Dreams (2004)** : The second single from American Idiot, sung from the perspective of Jesus of Suburbia, who finds the city overwhelming.
**Good Riddance (Time of Your Life)** : A song that is re-enacted by the cast at the conclusion of the stage musical adaptation of American Idiot, providing a sense of acceptance and hope.

## members

## curiosities
**Cigarettes and Valentines Lost Masters** : The band mysteriously lost the master tapes for a record titled "Cigarettes and Valentines," which led them to start over from scratch and ultimately create the concept album American Idiot.
**American Idiot Stage Musical Adaptation** : The album was adapted into a stage musical, which rearranged and expanded the narrative for improved clarity. In this adaptation, Jesus of Suburbia is named Johnny, St. Jimmy's suicide is portrayed metaphorically, and new characters such as Toney and a "De Nue mom" (embodying acceptance and hope) were introduced.

# artist - The Antlers

## albums
**Hospice (2009)** : Released in August 2009, this independent recording is described as quiet and poignant. The album tells a story through the analogy of a relationship between a terminally ill patient, suffering from bone cancer and exhibiting anger and abusive behavior, and a male hospice worker. The worker becomes attached and falls in love with the patient despite her abuse, seemingly unconcerned by her impending death. The complete story is written down in the album's liner notes. The events inspiring the ten songs appear to be drawn from the life of frontman Peter Silberman, though he prefers not to discuss them. It is the speaker's second favorite concept album of the 2000s.

## songs
**Kettering (2009)** : A track from the concept album Hospice, named after the Sloan Kettering Cancer Center in New York.

## members
Peter Silberman

# curiosity

**Definition of Concept Album** : A record where a central story or a series of interconnected themes link all the songs together, functioning like a "musical novel" that expands beyond a standard four-minute song into an hour-long musical narrative.
**Concept Album Popularity Cycle** : Concept albums were a significant trend in the late 1960s and 1970s, particularly within Progressive Rock, but their popularity declined with the rise of Punk Rock. They were subsequently resurrected in the 21st century, primarily by Alt Rock bands.
**iTunes and Downloading Impact on Album Sales** : The proliferation of iTunes and single-song downloading led record labels and artist managers to embrace concept albums, as these projects theoretically encouraged fans to purchase entire albums rather than just individual tracks, thereby helping to sustain the album format.
**Ayn Rand's Influence on Rush (1976)** : The objectiveist philosopher Ayn Rand, author of `Atlas Shrugged`, served as a direct inspiration for Rush's 1976 concept album `2112`.
**The Who's Rock Operas (1969)** : The Who pioneered the rock opera format in 1969, a style that later served as a historical reference point and inspiration for bands like Green Day when developing their own conceptual works.
**Andrew Lloyd Webber's Jesus Christ Superstar as Influence** : Green Day consulted Andrew Lloyd Webber's `Jesus Christ Superstar`, among other works, for guidance and inspiration while developing the rock opera structure for their album `American Idiot`.
**George W. Bush as Song Inspiration** : The former U.S. President is referenced in Nine Inch Nails' song "Capital G" (where 'G' stands for Greed and George W. Bush) and is mentioned as providing the contextual setting for Green Day's album "21st Century Breakdown" (post-George W. America).
**Alain Kras Podcast Host** : Alain Kras is the host of "The Angling History of New Music" podcast.
**Rob Johnston Technical Productions** : Rob Johnston is credited with the technical productions for "The Angling History of New Music" podcast.
