**Oxygen Analogy for Music's Importance** : Music is compared to oxygen in its integral role in human lives, often overlooked despite its profound importance. Just as an ideal 21% oxygen level is crucial for physical and mental health, music is a fundamental component of human experience. "Well, music is such an integral part of our lives that we have no idea how important it is, just like oxygen."
**Biological Basis of Music Appreciation** : Despite no apparent biological or evolutionary need, human brains are "pre-wired" for music. Every known human culture throughout history has had music. Specific areas in the brain are custom-designed for music making, appreciation, and memory, with some even 100% devoted to music processing, the reasons for which remain unknown. "There doesn't seem to be a biological or evolutionary need for our brains to come pre-wired for music, at least as far as scientists have so far been able to determine yet it is."
**Brain Processing of Music - Melody and Pitch** : The brain first processes melody and pitch using the same neurons that are active in speech processing, indicating a shared neural pathway for these auditory functions. "The first thing that our brains track is melody and pitch using the same neurons that we use to process speech, make sense."
**Brain Processing of Music - Note Prediction** : A distinct set of neurons, separate from those for speech, is dedicated solely to predicting what musical notes will come next. These neurons access a database of musical memories stored in their own brain section, constantly anticipating upcoming notes while also processing the context of these notes. "Second, there's another set of neurons that do nothing but predict what musical notes are coming next. They are completely separate from the neurons that deal with speech."
**Cannabis Effect on Music Perception** : Cannabis enhances musical pleasure by inhibiting the brain's ability to predict upcoming notes. Even with familiar songs, every note becomes a "pleasurable surprise" because the brain struggles to anticipate, thereby amplifying the inherent enjoyment humans derive from pleasurable musical surprises. "Cannabis inhibits the brain's ability to predict what notes come next, even if you're listening to a song that you've heard a million times before."
**Role of Expectation in Music Enjoyment** : Research published in the journal Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society B indicates that both the sensory aspect of music (actual sound) and cognitive understanding (how music works) independently contribute to musical enjoyment and expectation. Pleasure is derived from how music meets, violates, delays, or surprises expectations. The more familiar subjects are with a musical style, the greater their expectations. "Bottom line is that we derive pleasure from music from the way it meets, violates, delays or surprises us in terms of what we're expecting from it."
**Cognitive Familiarity and Enjoyment of New Music** : Enjoyment of music stems not just from the music itself but also from one's knowledge of it. The more one knows about a genre, the higher the chance of liking it. This explains why new, unfamiliar music can be difficult to appreciate; the brain lacks the resources to predict patterns or find pleasurable repetition. Learning about a new genre, or having an enthusiast explain it, can facilitate enjoyment. "Extrapolating that, the more we know about a certain genre of music, the greater the chances that will like it."
**Three Rationales for Disliking Music** : There are three primary reasons for disliking a song or genre: 1) not liking its sound (composition, lyrics, aesthetics); 2) not liking how it feels (emotions, physical reactions, disconnect from self-image); and 3) social reasons (dislike influenced by peer group's taste). "Well, there were three rationals for disliking a certain song or even an entire genre of music."

"When it comes to background music and maximum concentration, learning ability, and imagination, we need to get our brains into that alpha state."

The goal is for the mind to connect the songs to previous dreams in a relaxed, sleepy state, potentially granting dream control. Binaural beats are suggested as a type of music that may work best for this. "One trigger could be music. When you wake up in the morning, take a few minutes to remember your dreams.
