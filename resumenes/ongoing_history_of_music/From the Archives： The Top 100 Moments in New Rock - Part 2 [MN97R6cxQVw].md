## songs
**Bitter Sweet Symphony (1997)** : This song, intended as the band's big comeback single, features a 4.5-second sample from an obscure orchestral cover of The Rolling Stones' "The Last Time" from the mid-1960s. Despite initial permission requests to Mick Jagger, Keith Richards, and Andrew Loog Oldham being met with little concern, former Stones manager Allen Klein, who controlled the copyright to Stones songs up to 1970, refused sampling on principle. A deal was eventually struck where Klein would split royalties 50/50, but this meant 50% for Mick Jagger and 50% for Keith Richards, leaving The Verve with only a flat thousand dollars for the lyrics. "Much of Bitter Sweet Symphony is built around a 4.5 second sample from an orchestral version of a Rolling Stones song called The Last Time."
