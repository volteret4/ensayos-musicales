## albums
**Holy Roller Novakaine (EP) (2003)** : This was the band's first release, recorded in Memphis a month after Nathan and Caleb signed their deal. The EP was influential, as their record label showed it to members of The Strokes, who were intrigued, leading to the band's first UK tour. It features songs like "Wicker Chair" and "Molly's Chambers", and its title track "Holy Roller Novocaine" gained significant exposure through video games and the soundtrack for the NASCAR comedy film "Talladega Nights: The Ballad of Ricky Bobby". "We were making our EP like a month later and Memphis."
**Youth and Young Manhood (No Year Mentioned, Released after 2003 EP)** : This was Kings of Leon's first full-length album, released approximately six to eight months after their debut EP. The title was inspired by a phrase found in the back of their preacher father's Bible, specifically from an illustration of the "tree of Moses' life" detailing stages like "Youth and Young Manhood". This album was particularly successful in the UK and Europe. It included material from their first EP, with some songs appearing as-is and others re-recorded, notably featuring "Molly's Chambers". "Youth and Young Manhood was the album that I guess really caught on the UK and Europe and then kind of went from there."
**Aha Shake Heartbreak (UK: ~2004, North America: 2005)** : This album, their second full-length release, adhered to the band's five-syllable album title rule. It was initially released in the UK and then in North America in February 2005, roughly five months later. This staggered release was due to the band's early success in the UK, where they were associated with a "new rock revival" that included bands like The Strokes. The album yielded three major singles in the UK, all of which made the Top 20, including "The Bucket" and "King of the Rodeo". "This record got a British release first and an North American release second."
**Because of the Times (2007)** : This was Kings of Leon's third full-length album. It was released in 2007 and, like their other full albums, followed the band's self-imposed rule of having a five-syllable title. "We'll pick things up with a third album because of the Times, because of the Five Cylables, which came out in 2007."
**Only by the Night (2008)** : This album, Kings of Leon's fourth, was the record that truly propelled the band to mainstream success, with the single "Notion" being a major hit. For some fans, it seemed the band "came out of nowhere" with this album, though the band points out it took five years, one EP, and four albums to reach this level of fame. They found it particularly satisfying to reach big venues with their fourth album, having "worked their way up" rather than achieving instant success. "One of the big singles from their 2008 album, Only by the Knights, the record that really pushed him over the top."

## songs
**Notion (2008)** : This song was one of the significant singles released from Kings of Leon's fourth album, "Only by the Night," which came out in 2008. The album and its singles, including "Notion," were instrumental in pushing the band to mainstream recognition and fame. "Kings of Leon and Notion, one of the big singles from their 2008 album, Only by the Knights."
**Wicker Chair (2003)** : This track appeared on Kings of Leon's 2003 debut EP, "Holy Roller Novacaine." It holds the distinction of being the first song that Caleb Followill wrote on a guitar. The song's genesis occurred in the band's garage, inspired by Caleb seeing his brother Jared sitting in a white wicker chair, presumably while they were under the influence. The lyrics delve into themes of helplessness and people who struggle to help themselves. "That was the first song I wrote on a guitar."
**Holy Roller Novocaine (2003)** : Serving as the title track for their debut EP, "Holy Roller Novacaine," this song garnered considerable attention and wider exposure for Kings of Leon. It was featured in multiple video games and was also included on the soundtrack of the NASCAR comedy film "Talladega Nights: The Ballad of Ricky Bobby," which starred Will Ferrell and John C. Riley. "This track got them into a couple of video games."
**Tranny (Year Not Specified)** : Caleb Followill wrote this song after the band's first UK tour, which provided him with his initial experience of the touring "lifestyle." This inspiration led him to write several new songs, including "Tranny," before the completion of their debut album. "So I came home and I wrote, Tranny and Spiral Staircase".
**Spiral Staircase (Year Not Specified)** : This song was composed by Caleb Followill after Kings of Leon returned from their inaugural UK tour. Having had his "first taste of the lifestyle" of touring, Caleb was creatively inspired and wrote "Spiral Staircase" along with "Tranny" before the recording of their first album was finalized. "So I came home and I wrote, Tranny and Spiral Staircase".
**Molly's Chambers (Appears on EP (2003) and Youth and Young Manhood)** : "Molly's Chambers" is a significant early track for Kings of Leon, featured on both their debut EP, "Holy Roller Novacaine," and their first full-length album, "Youth and Young Manhood." It was one of the songs that played a key role in attracting early attention to the band. "Molly's Chambers from both the debut EP, Holy Roller Nova Kane and the first CD, Youth and Young Manhood."
**The Bucket (UK: ~2004)** : Released as the first single from Kings of Leon's second album, "Aha Shake Heartbreak," this song achieved chart success by hitting number 16 on the British singles charts. It was one of three singles from the album that reached the UK Top 20. The song's narrative is rooted in a personal situation experienced by Caleb Followill, who was 17 years old at the time the events inspiring the song unfolded. "The first single was a track called The Bucket."
**King of the Rodeo (UK: ~2004, North America: 2005)** : "King of the Rodeo" was released as a single from Kings of Leon's second album, "Aha Shake Heartbreak." While the album saw an earlier release in the UK, the North American release of "King of the Rodeo" occurred in February 2005, approximately five months after its initial debut in the United Kingdom. "One of the singles from their second album, A Ha Shake Heartbreak."

## curiosities
**Early Band Formation and Improvised Lineup (2001)** : In 2001, after Nathan and Caleb signed a record deal, the label sought to assemble a band for them. The brothers proposed their younger brother, Jared, then 15, play bass despite his lack of experience, and their cousin, Matthew, play guitar, based on a few lessons he had taken. The label, amused by this unconventional proposal, challenged them by providing a Led Zeppelin box set. The four then spent two weeks in their mother's garage, emerging with five songs that thoroughly impressed the label and secured their blessing to proceed. "We signed our deal in 2001, me and Caleb. Then they were trying to help us put our band together. We were like, we'll just buy a little brother a bass... We'll just, you know, we'll let him be the guitar player."
**Nomadic, Pentecostal Upbringing** : The Followill brothers and their cousin were raised in a strict, hardcore Pentecostal family, with their father serving as a preacher. The family, including their mother Betty Ann, lived a nomadic existence, traveling across the American South in an old Chrysler as their father delivered sermons. Due to a lack of funds for hotels, the family frequently camped out between services. The boys gained their foundational musical skills by singing gospel songs and playing drums during these church services. "The boys were brought up in a hardcore Pentecostal family. Dad was a preacher, and he, mother Betty Ann and the boys were pretty nomadic for a while."
**Childhood Education and Residency** : For the majority of their early lives, the Followill brothers were homeschooled, adapting to their family's itinerant lifestyle. However, there was a period when they settled down in Jackson, Tennessee, where they were briefly enrolled in a parochial school. "For the most part, the brothers were homeschooled, although for a while they were able to settle down in Jackson, Tennessee where they were enrolled in a Perrokerial school."
**Preacher Father's Secret Rock Tastes** : Although their preacher father was staunchly "anti-rock and roll" and had been a "hippie pothead" who later came "full circle," he occasionally indulged in rock music. During long drives, particularly when their mother was asleep around 3 AM, he would tune into AM radio stations playing artists like The Rolling Stones or Jimi Hendrix. He sometimes shared anecdotes about his past, including stories of "smoking pot" with his friends. "Dad liked to rock. I mean, he would put on the stones and deal young and the car with mum wasn't there, right?"
**Observation of Religious Radio Scams** : The band members, reflecting on their experiences with religious broadcasts, recounted observations of various individuals on the radio attempting to sell dubious items. Nathan Followill specifically recalled an incident where a preacher was trying to sell "the tears from Jesus that he wept on the cross" for $3,999, with people actually purchasing them. "I actually saw one guy at Hellbangeless one time that was actually trying to sell the tears from Jesus that he wept on the cross, like the most ridiculous like. And there were people that were literally for $3,999 you could actually get."
**Preacher Father's Unique Party-Ending Tactic** : The band's preacher father, despite his conservative background, was remarkably lenient, allowing his sons to host parties at their home. However, when he decided the festivities had run their course and it was time for guests to depart, he employed an unusual method: he would load jalapeno peppers into the microwave, turn it on high, and effectively "tear gas" everyone out of the house. "He'd load up the microwave with jalapeno peppers, turn it on high and kind of tear gas everybody out of the place."
**Pre-Music Career Paths** : Before Kings of Leon formed, the members had various paths. Nathan Followill was the only one to graduate from high school. Caleb Followill left school in grade 11 to pursue work as a construction worker, but he later quit this job to fully commit to music. Jared Followill, the youngest brother, "just tagged along" during the band's formation, becoming the bassist by default. "Nathan, being the oldest, graduated from high school first, Caleb bailed on school in grade 11 to become a construction worker. But then once he got deeper into music, he quit that too."
**Members' Real Names** : The Followill brothers and their cousin all use their middle names professionally. Nathan was originally named after his father, Ivan. Caleb's first name is Anthony. Jared's official driver's license name is Michael Jared Falwell. Matthew's full name is Cameron Matthew Followill. "Nathan was actually named after Ivan, his father. Caleb's first name is Anthony. Jared's driver's license reads Michael Jared Falwell, and we'll get to Matthew in just a minute."
**Lack of Formal Musical Training and Technical Knowledge** : The members of Kings of Leon openly admit to a striking lack of technical knowledge about their instruments, despite their proficiency as performers. Nathan Followill, for instance, requires his drum tech to create "cheat sheets" detailing the sizes, types, and heads of his drums for magazine interviews because he has "literally no idea." The band sees rock music as an art form where sometimes less technical knowledge can foster creativity, and they consider themselves "probably the least technical band in the world to ever reach this level." "I probably only band in the world that they do a special on him for bass magazine or guitar or drum magazine that we have to have our texts there with us that give us cheat sheets."
**Performance Over Technicality** : Despite their confessed lack of technical understanding of their instruments, Kings of Leon emphasizes their ability to perform live. They assert that the "magic happens" when they are on stage with instruments in hand, contrasting with their admitted status as "the least technical band in the world" to achieve their level of success. They also rarely soundcheck, with the first chord of the show often being the very first they play. "But you stick an instrument in our hands and stick us on the stage and we'll, you know, that's when the magic happens."
**First Gig Embarrassment** : Kings of Leon's debut performance was opening for Billy Joe Shaver, playing to an audience primarily comprised of "cowboy hats" expecting Old Western music. The band performed behind a curtain that would open for their set and close upon their final chord. After their performance, they celebrated backstage with high-fives, hugs, and tears, only to discover that the curtain was see-through, meaning the entire audience had witnessed their emotional post-set reactions. "And we realized that the curtain was see through. So the whole place saw us high five and then like hugging and crying."
**Career-Threatening Fire on 9/11/2001** : On September 11th, 2001, while in San Diego to perform for radio and TV programmers, Kings of Leon inadvertently caused a fire that nearly destroyed a penthouse suite. After playing a requested gig to help programmers cope with the shock of the 9/11 attacks, Nathan Followill placed a cigarette butt in an ashtray on a balcony covered with flammable astroturf. A gust of wind blew the cigarette onto the turf, igniting a fire that led to the fire department breaking down the door and extensively damaging the suite. "It happened on September 11th. The September 11th. Really? Oh yes. We were in San Diego playing a singing for a bunch of radio and TV programmers and stuff like that."
**Album Title Naming Convention** : Inspired by phrases they found in their preacher father's Bible, such as "Youth and Young Manhood" from a "tree of Moses' life" illustration, Kings of Leon adopted a distinct naming rule for their albums: every full-length album title must consist of exactly five syllables. Their debut EP is the sole exception to this rule. "Then we got into this five syllable thing and so we just kind of kept that going. Every album title has five syllables."
**Record Label's Strategic Patience** : Kings of Leon attribute their ability to develop as a band over five years, one EP, and four albums to their record label's patience. They suggest the label allowed this development because of the band's inconsistent but notable success, achieving "surprisingly successful" sales of 500,000 units in markets like the UK and Australia, which made the label reluctant to drop them despite being "under the radar" elsewhere. "They kind of had to let us develop because they didn't want to drop us from the label when they knew they were guaranteed 500,000 sold in the UK and Australia and all this."
**Unexpected Early International Success** : Contrary to their initial expectations, Kings of Leon's first significant success came not in North America, but predominantly in the UK and Europe, which was unusual for a band originating from Tennessee. The band had believed their sound would first resonate in the American South, only to find the South was "one of the last places to get it," while audiences in places like Ireland were already singing their songs. "Your first seat of success, it wasn't in America, it wasn't in North America, it was in the UK and Europe, which is unexpected for a band from Tennessee."
**Surprise UK Magazine Tour** : During the recording of their first album, Kings of Leon's label abruptly booked them on a UK tour sponsored by a prominent magazine, featuring "all the hottest bands." Despite having only played ten shows as a band and being "scared to death" to perform alongside older, more experienced musicians, their first UK show and all subsequent dates were sold out. This experience provided Caleb Followill with his "first taste of the lifestyle" of touring, inspiring him to write more songs upon his return. "The tour that they had booked this on, it was like this magazine had this tour in the UK and it was all the hottest bands."
**U2's Mentorship on Tour** : While on tour with U2, Kings of Leon received invaluable guidance on the intricacies of the "business side of touring," including lessons on lighting and creating a distinctive stage show. Jared Followill noted U2's meticulous attention to detail, such as washing lights before every performance. This contrasted sharply with Kings of Leon's own practice of often foregoing soundchecks, with their first chord of a show often being their initial chord of the evening. "They kind of took us under their wings for that whole tour and taught us a lot of stuff that we still use to this day. On the business side of touring stuff. As far as lighting and just having a really big, unique show."
**Band Name Origin (Grandfather Leon)** : Kings of Leon is named in honor of the band members' grandfather, Leon. Coincidentally, their father, Ivan, also shares the middle name Leon and, due to the band's success, has adopted and proudly embraced it as his own identity. "But we named it after our grandfather because we're all, you know, his grandsons."

## curiosities
**Rock Music and Untrained Musicianship** : Rock music is highlighted as a unique art form where a lack of formal musical training or extensive technical knowledge can actually enhance a musician's ability to innovate and contribute positively. This perspective suggests that intuition and raw talent can sometimes be more beneficial than classical training in the rock genre. "Rock music is probably the only art form where the less you know, the better the chance you have of improving it."
