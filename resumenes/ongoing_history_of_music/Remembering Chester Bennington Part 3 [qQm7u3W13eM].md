## albums
**Hybrid Theory (2000) – Debut Album** : Released in 2000, this album marked the band's debut and achieved significant commercial success, selling 30 million copies worldwide. It established the band's initial presence in the music industry. "Hybrid Theory, the first album, sold 30 million."
**Meteora (2003) – Second Studio Album** : Released in 2003, this was the band's second album. It continued their commercial success, selling 27 million copies globally. "The second, 2003's Meteora sold 27 million."
**Minutes to Midnight (2007) – Third Studio Album** : Released in 2007, this album sold 20 million copies. Its release followed a contentious contract renegotiation with Warner, which resulted in a $15 million advance and a high 20% royalty rate for the band, granting them greater creative control. "After that, minutes to midnight was released in 2007, and sold 20 million."
**A Thousand Sons (2010) – Fourth Studio Album** : Released on September 10, 2010, this album had worldwide sales of around 6 million. It polarized fans and critics due to an evolving sound, featuring guitars in the background, fewer raps for Mike Chinota, more electronic elements, and programmed beats, alongside a concept dealing with nuclear war. The band anticipated the pushback, viewing the changes as a necessary creative evolution. "A Thousand Sons, which came out on September 10, 2010. Very successful record."
**Living Things (2012) – Fifth Studio Album** : Released on June 27, 2012, this album was slightly behind the band's planned 18-month release schedule. It was generally less polarizing than its predecessor, "A Thousand Sons," and was often described in reviews as returning to "more familiar territory" with "more anger." "On June 27th 2012, a little bit behind schedule, there it was, a fifth album called Living Things."
**Recharged (?) – Remix Album** : This was a remix album mentioned as one of the side projects that band members indulged in. "And then there was another remix album called Recharged."
**The Hunting Party (2014) – Sixth Studio Album** : Released on June 13, 2014, while Chester Bennington was performing double duty with Stone Temple Pilots, this album's worldwide sales barely reached a million copies, reflecting significant changes in the record industry. "The Hunting Party and it came out on June 13th, 2014."
**One More Light (2017) – Seventh Studio Album** : Released in the spring of 2017, this album achieved a number one chart position. It generated pushback from some fans over its sound, prompting Chester Bennington to vigorously defend the band's artistic direction against accusations of "selling out." "leading up to the release of the One More Light album in the spring of 2017, he seemed pretty cheerful and optimistic."

## curiosities
**Early Career Success & Control** : By 2011, approximately 10 years after forming, Lincoln Park had sold over 80 million albums and amassed millions of fans globally. They exercised firm control over their career, dictating a schedule of releasing a new album every 18 months, rather than their record label. "In the 10 years since the band was formed, they'd sold over 80 million albums."
**Members' Side Projects** : The band members, all in their 30s by 2011, found time to pursue various side projects, encompassing both musical and non-musical endeavors. These included remix albums, contributions to soundtracks, and even movies, with DJ Johan notably starting to direct films. "Plus, they had time to indulge in all kinds of side projects, some musical, some not, like remix album, soundtracks, even movies, DJ Johan had started to direct films."
**2005 Warner Music Group Dispute** : In spring 2005, Warner, the band's record label, requested they perform at the New York Stock Exchange to celebrate its IPO following a significant financial restructuring that included $250 million in cuts. Lincoln Park was incensed by the cuts, believing they would hinder the label's ability to promote their music and other artists, and publicly refused to play, highlighting that they received no share from the IPO despite having sold 57 million records with just two albums. "Lincoln Park, though, was pissed at this one."
**Public Contract Negotiation** : The band issued a press release criticizing Warner Music Group for an expected $1.4 billion windfall from their IPO, with only $7 million going to company operations and nothing to artists, while Warner fired back accusing the band's management of using "fictitious numbers" and "inflammatory threats" as a negotiating tactic. This public dispute lasted for months, drawing significant media attention. "This very public fight went on for months before an agreement was reached."
**Favorable Contract Renegotiation (2005)** : After a public dispute with Warner Music Group, Lincoln Park secured a $15 million advance for their next album, "Minutes to Midnight," and an option for up to five additional albums with an exceptionally high royalty rate of 20%. Warner had little choice but to agree, given their new presence in financial markets and the risk of losing "the biggest rock band in the world at the time." "Lincoln Park had a $15 million advance for the next album which was Minutes to Midnight. They were also given an option for up to 5 more albums with a royalty rate of 20%, which is a pretty insanely high rate."
**Record Industry Evolution & Sales Decline** : Over their career, the band witnessed a drastic shift in the record industry; their first album sold 30 million copies, but by their sixth album in 2014, worldwide sales barely reached a million. This decline in physical sales was attributed to streaming taking over, yet the band sustained itself through touring and a deep catalog. "By the time the numbers started coming in for their sixth album in 2014, World Wide Sales barely added up to a million."
**Sophisticated Online & Social Media Strategy** : Lincoln Park developed an exceptionally smart and effective online and social media organization, recognized for its modern 21st-century approach to engaging and maintaining their fanbase. The band members were deeply involved, constantly interacting, providing special content, and releasing music directly to fans. "I have never seen an organization smarter than the one behind Lincoln Park."
**LP Recharge Online Game** : Chester Bennington was involved in the creation of "LP Recharge," an online action game based on the band itself. The game was launched through Facebook, demonstrating the band's engagement with new digital platforms. "He was also involved in the creation of LP Recharge, which was an online action game based on the band and launched through Facebook."
**Chester Bennington Tribute Concert (2017)** : On October 27, 2017, a three-hour tribute concert for Chester Bennington was held at the Hollywood Bowl. The event featured friends from Blink 182, System of a Down, Korn, and Avenged Sevenfold, and was streamed live, reaching millions of viewers as Linkin Park paid tribute to their fallen bandmate. "On October 27th, 2017, there was a three hour tribute to Chester featuring his friends from Blink 182, system of a downed corn, a bench, seven fold and more."
**One More Light Fund Establishment** : In memory of Chester Bennington, Linkin Park established the "One More Light Fund," an organization dedicated to offering help and support to individuals grappling with mental health issues. "And in Chester's memory, Lincoln Park has the one more light fund, an organization that offers help to those who need it."
**Carpool Karaoke Appearance (2017)** : On July 14, 2017, the band filmed an episode of "Carpool Karaoke" with James Corden. Footage from the event showed Chester Bennington appearing happy and healthy just days before his death. "On July 14, 2017, the band shot an episode of Carpool Karaoke with James Corden. That seemed to be loads of fun."

## curiosities
**Early Warning Signs** : Despite Linkin Park's outward success, there were subtle, slow-burning, almost undetectable internal struggles within Chester Bennington that, in hindsight, pointed to a tragic end six years later. "But if anyone had taken the time to really get to know Chester Bennington, there might have been some early warning signs."
**Acting Career** : Chester pursued acting roles, including playing a stoner in a pharmacy in the 2006 movie "Crank" and a character in its sequel. He also had a minor role in the 2010 movie "Saw 3D" and played himself in the 2012 film "Art of Fact." "Acting, for example, he played a stoner and a pharmacy in the 2006 movie Crank."
**Addiction and Depression Struggles** : Chester experienced significant battles with drugs and alcohol, notably a crisis during his 2005 divorce. Although he married Talinda in 2006 and claimed to be "straight ever since," he contended with a persistent depression. In August 2016, he had a three-day bender involving alcohol and continued drinking until October 2016, and in June 2017, he confided to Ryan Schuck about an "hour by hour battle with addiction" following a relapse earlier that year where alcohol was the primary issue. "The drugs and alcohol were long behind them. Okay, there was that crisis in 2005 when he got divorced. But by marrying Tallinda in 2006, that was the best thing for him. He'd been straight ever since."
**Defense of "One More Light" Sound** : Facing fan criticism for the evolving sound of Linkin Park's "One More Light" album, Chester fiercely defended the band, pushing back against critics who accused them of "selling out" or "going soft." He expressed deep personal offense, later apologizing for his harsh words. "When you make it personal, like a personal attack who we are as people, like, dude, shut up. That means I can actually have feelings about it. And for most of my time, the feelings are, I want to kill you."
**Openness about Mental Health** : Chester was remarkably honest about his struggles with mental health and actively encouraged others to speak out and seek help. This transparency was a significant aspect of his public persona and legacy. "Chester was really, really good about being honest about his mental health. He encouraged many, many people to take that first step to talk about things."
**Rock to Recovery Event (2016)** : In October 2016, Chester attended "Rock to Recovery," an event focused on sober musicians. He greeted attendees at the door with his new puppy, appearing very happy and optimistic. "There's a story about Chester working an event called Rock to Recovery, which is all about sober musicians. This was in October 2016."
**Friendship with Chris Cornell** : Chester shared a close and long-standing friendship with Chris Cornell. They toured together, socialized frequently, and Chester was the godfather to Chris's son, Christopher. Their bond was evident in their joint performances and mutual support. "Chester and Chris had a friendship that went back years."
**Devastation by Chris Cornell's Death** : Chris Cornell's suicide on May 18, 2017, was a profound shock and devastating blow to Chester, who was already in an emotionally fragile state. Cornell was found dead in Detroit, just hours after a show. "The big shock was Chris Cornell's suicide. He was found dead early in the morning of May 18, 2017 in the MGM Grand Hotel in Detroit, just a couple of hours after the band played a show at the Fox Theatre the night before."
**Tribute Performance at Cornell's Funeral** : At Chris Cornell's funeral, Chester performed Leonard Cohen's "Hallelujah." He later tweeted about Cornell's voice, describing it as encapsulating joy, pain, anger, forgiveness, love, and heartache, and helping him understand these complexities within himself. "At the funeral, Chester sang Leonard Cohen's hallelujah, and he wrote this on his Twitter, your voice was joy and pain, anger and forgiveness, love and heartache all wrapped up into one."
**Creative Burst Post-Cornell Memorial** : The day after Chris Cornell's memorial, Chester tweeted about feeling highly creative and having written six new songs. He also told a friend, "we have to stick together and we have so much to live for," indicating a sense of hope and a focus on survival. "The day after the memorial for Chris, Chester tweeted again, saying that he was feeling very creative and had written six brand new songs."
**2017 Europe Tour** : In June and July 2017, Chester toured Europe with Linkin Park. Observers noted that he appeared to be in the best physical condition of his life during this period. "In June and July 2017, Lincoln Park toured Europe. The band was on fire. People remarked that Chester seemed to be in the best physical condition of his life."
**North American Tour & Gray Days Reunion Plans** : Following the Europe tour, a short break was planned before a 29-date North American tour in support of the number one album "One More Light." Around this time, Chester was also discussing a potential reunion with Gray Days, his pre-Linkin Park band from Arizona. "Chester was talking about a reunion with Gray Days, a band from his pre-Lincoln Park days in Arizona."
**Personal Life Prior to Death** : Chester had a very close relationship with his wife, Talinda, and his family, who meant everything to him. They had recently purchased a new house in a beautiful, gated community. Before the planned Linkin Park tour, he and Talinda enjoyed a two-week vacation, and he exchanged happy, positive texts with members of Stone Temple Pilots, expressing optimism for the future. "Chester and his wife and his family were super tight. They meant everything to him."
**Final Interactions and Plans** : On July 14, 2017, Chester filmed "Carpool Karaoke" looking great. On July 18, he spoke with longtime friend Sean Doudel of Gray Days, sounding great, and was later seen laughing and joking with his family in a video. On July 19, he emailed Matt Sorum about reforming the all-star covers band Kings of Chaos, expressing excitement. "On July 19, he sent an email to Matt Sorum, the former drummer with guns and roses, saying that he would like to reform kings of chaos and all-star covers band. That'd be so much fun, he said."
**Death by Suicide (July 20, 2017)** : Chester Bennington was found dead on July 20, 2017, around 9 AM, by his housekeeper at his home in Pollock's. He was alone, as his wife Talinda and children were away. The date tragically coincided with Chris Cornell's 53rd birthday. An empty bottle of vodka was found nearby, though nothing conclusive appeared in toxicology reports. "He was alone in the house the night of July 19, to Linda and the kids were away. Around nine in the morning of July 20, what would have been Chris Cornell's 53rd birthday, the family's housekeeper ran out to an arriving limo driver who was there to pick up Chester for a photo shoot. She'd found a body."

## curiosities
**Financial Restructuring & IPO (2005)** : In spring 2005, Warner Music Group underwent a significant organizational and financial restructuring, which involved cuts of $250 million. They planned an Initial Public Offering (IPO) to raise $750 million, expecting a $1.4 billion windfall from a $2.6 billion purchase made 18 months prior. "The new owners of Warner Music Group will be reaping a windfall of $1.4 billion from the $2.6 billion purchase a mere 18 months ago if their planned IPO moves forward."
**Dispute with Lincoln Park (2005)** : Warner Music Group asked Lincoln Park, their biggest act, to perform at the New York Stock Exchange to celebrate their IPO. However, the band publicly refused, criticizing the $250 million cuts which they believed would hurt artist promotion, and pointed out that only $7 million of the IPO funds were slated for company operations, with no money designated for Warner artists. "Lincoln Park, their biggest act, will get nothing. Of that plan, $750 million raised by an IPO, only about 7 million will be put towards the company's own operations with no money going to Warner artists."
**Public Response to Lincoln Park** : Warner Music Group publicly fired back at Lincoln Park's statements during contract renegotiations, labeling the band's claims as a "ploy." They asserted that Linkin Park's management was using "fictitious numbers" and making "baseless charges and inflammatory threats," emphasizing their significant investments in the band and generous compensation for their success. "Warner fired back, saying that this was just a ploy during a time of contract re-negotiation."
