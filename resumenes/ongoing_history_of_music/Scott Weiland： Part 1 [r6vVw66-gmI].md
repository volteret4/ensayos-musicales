## curiosities
**Early Life - Birth and Name Change** : Born Scott Richard Kline in San Jose, California. His mother, Sharon, divorced his biological father, Kent, when Scott was 12. A year later, when Sharon remarried David Weiland, David formally adopted Scott, leading him to take the Weiland surname. "He was Scott Richard Klein back then."
**Childhood Dream of Stardom** : At eight years old, Scott Weiland reportedly told his mother that he intended to become both a rock star and a millionaire, foreshadowing his future career and aspirations. "He remembers telling his mom when he was eight years old that he was going to be both a rock star and a millionaire."
**Family Environment and Substance Abuse** : Scott's biological father, Kent Kline, was described as a Pepsi truck driver by day and a frequent dope smoker by night. His mother, Sharon, secretly became an alcoholic herself, often carrying a bottle of vodka in her purse. This environment contributed to Scott and his younger brother, Michael, getting into alcohol and drugs very early, often raiding friends' parents' liquor cabinets. "Not exactly the kind of people to supervise these kids."
**Early Trauma - Family and Personal Attack** : Weiland experienced significant trauma in his youth. The son his biological father adopted upon remarrying was tragically hit by a car while riding his bike. Additionally, Weiland claimed he was raped by an older, bigger boy after school one day, contributing to a dark period in his life. "Then Wyland says an older, bigger boy raped him after school one day."
**Teenage Years and Move to California** : After a stint in Cleveland, Weiland's family moved west to Huntington Beach, California, in 1982 when he was 14. This area of Orange County, situated on the Pacific Ocean just south of Los Angeles, became his new home. "It was 1982 and Scott was 14."
**Music as Escape and Teenage Influences** : Following a football injury that sidelined him for much of a season, music became Scott Weiland's primary escape. His favorite bands during this period included Depeche Mode, The Clash, Social Distortion, The Sweet, Echo & The Bunnymen, and Queen. "Music became his escape, especially after a football injury forced him to sit out much of a season."
**Teenage Style and Drug Use** : As a teenager, Scott Weiland adopted a distinct style, wearing mascara and dressing in black. During this time, he also delved deeper into various types of drugs, reflecting a period of increasing experimentation and rebellion. "It began wearing mascara, dressing in black and got deeper into various types of drugs."
**Commitment to Mental Hospital** : At the age of 16, after his stepdad discovered he was doing cocaine, Scott Weiland was committed to a mental hospital for three months following a major family conflict. "The blowup that followed resulted in Scott being committed to a mental hospital for three months."
**Early Adulthood and Graphic Artist Job** : After attending college for a period, Scott Weiland moved to Los Angeles where he worked as a graphic paste-up artist for a legal publication called Los Angeles Daily Journal, holding a steady job before his music career took off. "Scott W island ended up in Los Angeles working as a graphic paste-up artist for a legal publication called Los Angeles Daily Journal."
**Relationship with Mary Ann** : While living in Los Angeles, Scott Weiland was involved with a girl named Mary Ann. Their relationship was volatile, with one notable incident occurring when she became so angry with Scott that she set his car on fire. "She was with a girl named Mary Ann who once got so mad with Scott that she said his car on fire."
**Meeting Robert DeLeo** : Scott Weiland met Robert DeLeo in 1986 at a Black Flag concert. Their initial acquaintance evolved into friendship after they discovered they were both romantically involved with the same woman, who subsequently moved to Texas. "In 1986, W island meant Robert Delayo at a black flag concert."
**Introduction to Heroin** : Scott Weiland was allegedly introduced to heroin by Gibby Haynes, the singer of the Butthole Surfers, during a tour in June and July of 1993. Weiland later claimed his first heroin use occurred in New York on August 4, 1993, following a show at the Roseland Ballroom. "It's alleged that this is when Gibby introduced Scott to heroin."
**Views on Heroin Culture** : Weiland openly admitted his love and intrigue for heroin culture, referencing influences from William S. Burroughs, Charlie Parker, the aesthetic of The Rolling Stones, John Lennon's heroin period, and the mid-80s impact of Perry Farrell and Jane's Addiction. He associated heroin with romance, glamour, danger, and rock and roll excess, and was curious about its connection to creativity. "The truth is that I loved heroin culture."
**Heroin as an Equalizer** : Weiland described heroin as his "savior" and "the ultimate equalizer," using it to overcome extreme self-consciousness. He felt that while on dope, he could be "Superman" or "any man," no longer caring what anyone thought of him. "Dope was my savior, the ultimate equalizer. Or so I thought."
**Death on Tour Bus** : Scott Weiland died on December 3rd, 2015, at the age of 48, in the back bedroom of his tour bus. The bus was parked outside a Country Inn and Suites hotel northwest of Minneapolis, where he had intended to rest before a show at the Medina Ballroom. Police reports indicated an unresponsive male, possibly from an overdose. "He never came out alive."
**Survival Against Odds** : Despite a tumultuous life marked by health issues, family problems, alcohol, drugs, rehab, legal issues, and bad luck, Scott Weiland managed to live to 48 years old, a fact noted with some surprise given his lifestyle. "It was that he had managed to live to 48."

## curiosities
**Founding and Early Lineup** : The band formed through a series of connections starting with Scott Weiland meeting Robert DeLeo in 1986. Robert played bass on Scott's demos, and they later recruited drummer Eric Kretz. Robert's brother, Dean DeLeo, initially hesitant, was eventually persuaded to join, completing the lineup. "And that was the beginning of Stone Temple Pilots."
**Original Name: Mighty Joe Young** : Before becoming Stone Temple Pilots, the group was known as Mighty Joe Young, named after a character in a 1940s gorilla movie. They performed their first gig under this name. "At this point, the group was known as Mighty Joe Young after the character in that gorilla movie from the 1940s."
**First Gig Location and Date** : As Mighty Joe Young, the band played their first ever gig in August 1990 at The Whiskey on Sunset Boulevard in Los Angeles. "The first gig was in August of 1990 at the whiskey on Sunset Boulevard in Los Angeles."
**Sound Development and Influences** : For about two years, the band worked on their sound by playing numerous gigs, developing their chops. In the late 1980s, hair metal was prevalent, and grunge was yet to fully emerge. Robert DeLeo's admiration for Flea and the funkiness of Red Hot Chili Peppers significantly influenced their early sound, particularly as Mighty Joe Young. "Took a while to figure out their sound too."
**Record Deal with Atlantic Records** : An agent who saw the band play tipped off Atlantic Records, who observed the group multiple times. Following a show at The Shamrock in Silver Lake, an offer was made, and Mighty Joe Young signed their major label deal on April 1st, 1992 (April Fool's Day). "And on April 1st 1992, April Fool's Day, Mighty Joe Young signed their major label deal."
**Name Change Due to Legal Dispute** : Just two days before delivering their debut album to Atlantic Records, the band was informed by their lawyer that they had a problem: an old Chicago blues singer named Joe Young objected to them using the name Mighty Joe Young. This forced the group to find a new name within two weeks. "An old Chicago blues singer named Joe Young, objected to the band using the name Mighty Joe Young because that's the name he used to use."
**Origin of the Name "Stone Temple Pilots"** : After two weeks of brainstorming, the band settled on Stone Temple Pilots. The initials "STP" came from an old STP oil treatment can that Scott Weiland had as a "shrine" in his living space, reminding him of a childhood STP sticker on his bicycle and NASCAR driver Richard Petty, who was sponsored by STP. The full words "Stone Temple Pilots" were chosen while the band members were "drunk and stoned" as they sounded the best among many combinations, despite meaning "absolutely nothing." "From there was just a matter of matching up words to the letters."
**Critical Reception and Grunge Label** : Stone Temple Pilots faced significant critical backlash, particularly with the song "Plush," which was a "big critical flop." Many critics categorized them as a grunge band or, worse, a "blatant Pearl Jam rip off." Others, especially after hearing songs like "Sex Type Thing," labeled them as a metal band. "When most people first heard the song, they were quick to categorize STP as a grunge band or worse, a blatant Pearl Jam rip off."
**Scott Weiland's Response to Grunge Label** : Scott Weiland intensely disliked being compared to grunge bands, particularly Pearl Jam and Eddie Vedder. He felt that the obvious differences in his fashion sense and stage presence, which lacked any flannel, should have distinguished them. To counter these criticisms, Weiland deliberately adopted a more flamboyant, performance art aspect to his stagecraft, incorporating dresses, lipstick, and mascara. "There was certainly no flannel in Scott's wardrobe."
**First Tour and Touring Decisions** : The first Stone Temple Pilots tour lasted 14 months. After opening for Megadeth, they received an offer to open for Aerosmith. However, the band declined this opportunity, as they did not consider themselves to be in the same genre as Aerosmith during the early 90s alternative music scene. Instead, they opted to tour with the Butthole Surfers in June and July of 1993. "They didn't consider themselves in the same genre as Erosmith."
**MTV Unplugged Performance** : Stone Temple Pilots performed for MTV Unplugged, with their episode broadcast on November 17, 1993. Notably, this performance took place in the same studio where Nirvana recorded their famous MTV Unplugged performance, just one day prior to Nirvana's session. Both bands chose to cover semi-obscure songs by David Bowie as part of their sets. "On Temple Pilots, performing for MTV with an unplugged episode broadcast on November 17, 1993."
**Band's Stance on Scott Weiland's Illness** : Robert DeLeo and Eric Kretz spoke about the "uncontrollable" nature of Scott Weiland's illness, acknowledging "two different people" sometimes existed within him. They stated that the band had made it clear years ago that they would not work under circumstances where Weiland was struggling with his issues, choosing instead to deal with "Scott" and only when he was "sober" in order to conduct their art. "We want to deal with Scott and deal with him sober."

## curiosities
**First Gig** : The band's inaugural performance took place in August 1990 at The Whiskey on Sunset Boulevard in Los Angeles. "The first gig was in August of 1990 at the whiskey on Sunset Boulevard in Los Angeles."
**Development and Opening Slots** : Mighty Joe Young spent about two years playing numerous gigs to develop their sound and performance skills, eventually drawing audiences of a couple hundred people on their own. This success led to opening slots for bands such as Solar Sylem and the Rollins Band. "This band worked out their chops by playing gig after gig for about two years, eventually drawing a couple hundred people on their own which led to opening slots for bands like Solar Sylem and the Rollins band."
**Influence of Red Hot Chili Peppers** : During the late 1980s, when hair metal was popular and grunge was still emerging, Robert DeLeo, a big fan of Flea and the Red Hot Chili Peppers' funkiness, influenced Mighty Joe Young's sound, as evident in some of their early songs like "Dirty Dog." "Robert Delayo was a big fan of flea in the funkiness of the Red Hot Chili Peppers who were finally starting to do well."
**San Diego Misconception** : The band got a bunch of gigs in San Diego, partly due to Dean DeLeo's help, which led to the misconception that Stone Temple Pilots originated from San Diego. "Dean Delayo wasn't much interested in being in a band at all, but he did help his brother and Scott and Eric get a bunch of gigs in San Diego, which is where the misconception of STP being a San Diego band started."
**Forced Name Change** : The band was compelled to change their name from Mighty Joe Young because an old Chicago blues singer, also named Joe Young, objected to their use of the name. This happened just two days before they were scheduled to submit their debut album to Atlantic Records. "An old Chicago blues singer named Joe Young, objected to the band using the name Mighty Joe Young because that's the name he used to use."
