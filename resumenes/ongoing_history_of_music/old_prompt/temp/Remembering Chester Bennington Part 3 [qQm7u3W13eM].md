## Artists & Members

artist @Linkin Park **Band Status (2011)** : In 2011, the band had sold over 80 million albums, garnered millions of global fans, maintained firm control over their career, and planned to release a new album every 18 months, indulging in musical and non-musical side projects.
member @Joe Hahn **Filmmaking Career (2011)** : Linkin Park's DJ, Joe Hahn, had started directing films around 2011.
member @Chester Bennington **Tragic End (2017)** : His life ended tragically around six years after 2011, specifically in 2017.
artist @Linkin Park **Creative Control** : Achieved unique creative control over their music following a contract renegotiation with Warner Bros. Records.
artist @Linkin Park **Post-Contract Renegotiation Outcome (2005)** : Warner Bros. Records granted Linkin Park a large advance and assurances of creative freedom, wanting to avoid losing the "biggest rock band in the world" at the time.
artist @Linkin Park **Album Release Schedule** : Planned to release a new album every 18 months.
artist @Linkin Park **Artistic Philosophy** : Known for insisting on musical evolution, unafraid to experiment, confuse, or annoy, prioritizing the music above all else.
member @Chester Bennington **Acting Roles**: Appeared as a stoner in a pharmacy in the 2006 movie *Crank*, had a minor role in the sequel *Crank: High Voltage*, a minor role in the 2010 movie *Saw 3D*, and played himself in the 2012 movie *Art of Fact*.
artist @Dead by Sunrise **Chester Bennington's Side Project** : A side project featuring Chester Bennington.
artist @Stone Temple Pilots, Chester Bennington **Chester Bennington Joins (May 2013)** : Chester Bennington became the full-fledged lead singer of Stone Temple Pilots in May 2013, performing with them in Los Angeles, following Scott Weiland's departure.
artist @Stone Temple Pilots **Scott Weiland Departure (February 2013)** : Scott Weiland was removed from Stone Temple Pilots in February 2013 due to issues with drugs, alcohol, and drama.
artist @Stone Temple Pilots **Dean DeLeo's Statement on Chester Bennington**: Dean DeLeo praised Chester Bennington's "one-of-a-kind voice" upon him joining the band, acknowledging Linkin Park would remain Bennington's priority.
member @Chester Bennington **Tenure with Stone Temple Pilots (May 2013 - November 2015)** : Toured and recorded with Stone Temple Pilots for two and a half years.
artist @Stone Temple Pilots, Chester Bennington **Chester Bennington Departs (November 2015)** : Chester Bennington amicably left Stone Temple Pilots in November 2015 due to time constraints with Linkin Park and family.
member @Chester Bennington **Statement on Leaving Stone Temple Pilots (November 2015)** : Stated he could not dedicate enough time to Stone Temple Pilots alongside Linkin Park and family obligations, choosing to focus 100% on Linkin Park.
artist @Linkin Park **Post-Sales Decline Strategy** : Maintained financial viability through extensive touring and a deep catalog, despite a dramatic decline in album sales by 2014.
artist @Linkin Park **Social Media & Fan Engagement** : Praised for having an exceptionally smart online and social media team, with the band actively engaging with fans, providing special content and releases in the 21st-century digital landscape.
member @Chester Bennington **Personal Life & Sobriety (2005-2017)** : Experienced a personal crisis and divorce in 2005, but became sober after marrying Talinda in 2006, remaining straight until a relapse in 2016.
member @Chester Bennington **Mental State Prior to One More Light (2017)** : Despite lingering depression, appeared cheerful and optimistic leading up to the release of "One More Light" in spring 2017.
member @Chester Bennington **Defense of One More Light & Public Comments** : Defended "One More Light" against critics accusing the band of "selling out" or "going soft," controversially stating that personal attacks made him "want to kill you."
member @Chester Bennington **Apology for Harsh Comments** : Later apologized for his harsh comments directed at critics of "One More Light."
member @Chester Bennington **Relapse into Alcohol Abuse (August-October 2016)** : Experienced a three-day drinking bender in August 2016 and continued to drink until at least October 2016.
member @Chester Bennington **Impact of Chris Cornell's Death**: Deeply devastated Chester Bennington, who was already in an emotionally fragile state.
member @Chester Bennington **Post-Cornell Memorial Creativity**: The day after Chris Cornell's memorial, Chester Bennington tweeted about feeling very creative and having written six new songs.
member @Chester Bennington **Statement on Life After Cornell's Death**: Told a friend, "we have to stick together and we have so much to live for," following Chris Cornell's death.
member @Chester Bennington **Potential Gray Daze Reunion**: Discussed a reunion with Gray Daze, his pre-Linkin Park band from Arizona.
member @Chester Bennington **Family Life & Preparations (July 2017)** : Had a close family with wife Talinda, bought a new house, and went on vacation prior to a Linkin Park tour in July 2017.
member @Chester Bennington **Battle with Addiction (June 2017)** : Admitted to Dead by Sunrise bandmate Ryan Shuck in June 2017 that he was in an "hour by hour battle with addiction."
member @Chester Bennington **Relapse & Sobriety (Early 2017)** : Was approximately six months sober in June 2017, following an early-year relapse primarily involving alcohol.
member @Chester Bennington **Last Known Positive Interactions (July 18, 2017)** : Spoke with long-time friend Sean Dowdell of Gray Daze, sounding great, and was later seen on video laughing and joking with his family on July 18, 2017.
artist @Chester Bennington **Kings of Chaos Reunion Discussion (July 19, 2017)** : Emailed Matt Sorum (ex-Guns N' Roses) on July 19, 2017, expressing interest in reforming the all-star covers band Kings of Chaos.
member @Chester Bennington **Death (July 20, 2017)** : Died by suicide on July 20, 2017, at his home, the morning of Chris Cornell's 53rd birthday, discovered by his housekeeper.
member @Chester Bennington **Circumstances of Death**: A bottle of vodka was found near his body, but toxicology reports reportedly showed no substances.
member @Chester Bennington **Posthumous Impact**: His death caused profound shock among fans, leading to numerous tributes, memorials, and acknowledgements of his influence across rock, metal, and rap genres.
member @Chester Bennington **Vocal Legacy**: Possessed an "undeniably amazing voice" that, like Chris Cornell and Scott Weiland, helped define alt-rock for a generation.
member @Chester Bennington **Advocacy for Mental Health Openness**: Was known for his honesty about his own mental health, encouraging many people to discuss their struggles.

## Albums

album @Linkin Park **Hybrid Theory (2000) – Debut Album** : Released in 2000, sold 30 million copies.
album @Linkin Park **Meteora (2003) – Second Studio Album** : Released in 2003, sold 27 million copies.
album @Linkin Park **Minutes to Midnight (2007) – Third Studio Album** : Released in 2007, sold 20 million copies.
album @Linkin Park **A Thousand Suns (2010) – Fourth Studio Album** : Released on September 10, 2010, selling around 6 million copies worldwide.
album @Linkin Park **A Thousand Suns (2010) – Sales Context** : Sales were impacted by the decline in physical music sales and the rise of streaming.
album @Linkin Park **A Thousand Suns (2010) – Critical & Fan Reception** : Polarized fans and critics due to evolving sound, with opinions ranging from best to worst record of the year.
album @Linkin Park **A Thousand Suns (2010) – Concept & Musical Approach** : A concept album dealing with nuclear war, featuring changes like background guitars, fewer rap verses from Mike Shinoda, more electronic elements, and programmed beats.
album @Linkin Park **Living Things (2012) – Fifth Studio Album** : Released on June 27, 2012, slightly behind the band's planned 18-month schedule.
album @Linkin Park **Living Things (2012) – Reception & Sound** : Described as less polarizing than "A Thousand Suns," returning to "more familiar territory" with "more anger."
album @Linkin Park **Recharged (Remix Album)** : A remix album released by Linkin Park.
album @Linkin Park **The Hunting Party (2014) – Sixth Studio Album** : Released on June 13, 2014, recorded and released while Chester Bennington was also active with Stone Temple Pilots.
album @Linkin Park **Sales Decline (2000-2014)** : Linkin Park experienced a dramatic decline in album sales, from 30 million copies for their 2000 debut to barely a million worldwide for their sixth album in 2014, reflecting changes in the music industry.
album @Linkin Park **One More Light (2017) – Fan Pushback** : Faced fan pushback over the sound of pre-release songs from the album.
album @Linkin Park **One More Light (2017) – Tour & Chart Performance** : A number one album, supported by a planned 29-date North American tour, with the single "Heavy" performing well on radio.

## Curiosities

curiosity @Linkin Park **Dispute with Warner Bros. Records (2005)** : Linkin Park publicly protested Warner's $250 million cuts during their financial reorganization, fearing it would harm promotion for their music and other artists.
curiosity @Linkin Park **Warner IPO Revenue Discrepancy (2005)** : Linkin Park highlighted that Warner would reap a windfall from their IPO largely due to the band's 57 million album sales (from first two albums), while artists received no direct share of IPO funds.
curiosity @Linkin Park **Public Press Release Against Warner (2005)** : The band released a statement alleging Warner Music Group's new owners would gain $1.4 billion from a $2.6 billion purchase, while only $7 million of the $750 million IPO proceeds would go to operations, with nothing to artists.
curiosity @Linkin Park **Warner's Response to Linkin Park Dispute (2005)** : Warner Brothers Records dismissed Linkin Park's claims as a negotiating tactic during contract renegotiations, stating they had invested significantly in the band since signing them in 1999 and compensated them generously.
curiosity @Linkin Park **LP Recharge (Online Game)** : An online action game based on the band, launched via Facebook, in which Chester Bennington was involved.
curiosity **Mental Health Awareness Legacy** : Chester Bennington's life and death highlight the importance of discussing mental health issues, depression, and addiction.
curiosity @Linkin Park **One More Light Fund** : An organization established in Chester Bennington's memory by Linkin Park, dedicated to providing mental health support.
curiosity **Podcast Information** : The "Ongoing History of New Music" is a podcast hosted by Alan Cross, with technical production by Rob Johnston, available on iTunes and other on-demand audio platforms, and has a companion website ajournalofmusicalthings.com.
