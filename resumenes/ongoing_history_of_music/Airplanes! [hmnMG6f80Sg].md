# curiosity

**Ongoing History of New Music Podcast (1993-Present)** : Hosted by Alan Cross, this weekly podcast explores stories and characters in modern music. It began in 1993 and has produced over 700 one-hour episodes. Show number 747, for example, focused on civil aviation and alt-rock. The podcast is available early and ad-free on Amazon Music for Prime members.
**Alan Cross's Aviation Enthusiasm** : Host Alan Cross identifies as an "airline nerd," stating he would be a travel agent for an airline if not in music. He flies approximately 150,000 miles a year and enjoys discussing all aspects of air travel, including SkyTRAX ratings, SeatGuru tips, airport shopping, duty-free lessons, and finding airport lounges.
**Show Idea Generation** : Alan Cross describes his process for coming up with podcast ideas as akin to a student procrastinating an assignment, often hitting "writers block" despite hard deadlines for radio stations. The idea for show #747, on alt-rock and airplanes, came from noticing the show number matched the Boeing 747 airliner.
**Air Rage as a General Problem** : The podcast highlights air rage as a real problem, with common causes including cramped seats, excessive recline by other passengers, alcohol consumption, insufficient smoking, drug issues, sexual escapades, and general belligerence.
**"The Day the Music Died" (1959)** : This historical event refers to the plane crash on February 3, 1959, in a cornfield outside Mason City, Iowa, which killed rock and roll pioneers Buddy Holly, Richie Valens, and The Big Bopper.
**Pan Am Flight 103 Lockerbie Bombing (1988)** : On December 21, 1988, Pan Am Flight 103, a Boeing 747 en route from London to New York, was destroyed by a bomb secreted inside a cassette player in the forward luggage compartment. The plane disintegrated at 31,000 feet, flying at around 500 miles per hour over Lockerbie, Scotland, resulting in the deaths of all 243 passengers, 16 crew members, and 11 people on the ground. The bombing was attributed to a Libyan agent.
**Notable Individuals Who Missed Pan Am Flight 103 (1988)** : Several public figures narrowly avoided the Lockerbie bombing on December 21, 1988, including actress Kim Cattrall (changed her flight for last-minute shopping), tennis player Matt Zvielandr (did not board), the R&B group The Four Tops (overslept after performing on BBC's Top of the Pops), and Johnny Lydon of Sex Pistols/Public Image Ltd (delayed by his wife Nora's packing, an argument, and traffic to Heathrow).
**Highest Gig in the World Competition** : A trend emerged among musicians to perform concerts at the highest possible altitude.
**Highest Gig on Ground Record (Pre-2007)** : Oz Beldon, founder of Music for Children, held the record for the highest gig performed with feet still on the ground, delivering a 40-minute show for hikers on the summit of Mount Mera in the Himalayas at an altitude of 21,825 feet.
**Highest Gig in Air Record Evolution** :
    *   **Jamiroquai (2007)** : Set a Guinness World Record on February 27, 2007, by performing at 35,000 feet on a private Boeing 757.
    *   **The Black Eyed Peas (2009)** : Broke Jamiroquai's record on September 8, 2009, performing at 41,000 feet on a Virgin Blue Boeing 737.
    *   **James Blunt (2010)** : Surpassed The Black Eyed Peas on June 28, 2010, with a performance at 42,080 feet on a Boeing 767.
    *   **Tony Hadley & Kim Wilde (2013)** : Achieved the highest known passenger jet gig on March 10, 2013, performing at 43,000 feet on a British Airways Boeing 767, close to the operational ceiling of current passenger jets.
**Zero G Music Videos** : The concept of filming music videos in zero gravity. Commander Chris Hadfield released a Zero G video aboard the International Space Station in 2013. OK Go later created the first Zero G music video *within the atmosphere* for "Upside Down and Inside Out" with S7 airline.
**Non-insane Automatism (Legal Term)** : A legal term describing certain types of non-voluntary behavior, notably used in Peter Buck's defense. In his case, it was caused by an unusual reaction between a prescription drug (Ambien) and alcohol.
