## albums
**Lungs (2009)** : This album featured early songs by Florence, including "My Boy Builds Coffins," which was inspired by her first real boyfriend, and "Kiss With A Fist," which was originally a song by her earlier band Ashock. Matt Alchon, her ex-boyfriend, received royalty income from songs on this album. "A proper version is on the Lungs album."
**Ceremonials (2011)** : This album was recorded at Abbey Road Studios, specifically in studio three. The recording setup for Florence & Company in the studio was notable for its exotic instruments, carpets, and candles. "When that album was being recorded at Abbey Road Studios, I was given a tour of the facility. Florence & Company were set up in studio three with all kinds of exotic instruments and carpets and candles."
**How Big, How Blue, How Beautiful (2015)** : Florence took a year off between the *Ceremonials* album and this one due to a nervous breakdown, which was attributed to "too much too fast" and relationship issues, specifically a string of breakups and excessive drinking. "Florence had to take a year off between the Ceremonials album and how big, how blue, how beautiful, because she had a nervous breakdown of sorts, a case of too much too fast."

## songs
**My Boy Builds Coffins (2009)** : This was Florence's very first proper song, written about her first real boyfriend who was actually building a coffin for the cover of an album his indie band was making. It later appeared on her *Lungs* album and was recorded with the help of Isabella Summers. "It was called My Boy Builds Coffins, which later turned up in her 2009 album Lungs."
**Girl with One Eye** : This was the first song Florence Welch co-wrote and worked on with Isabella Summers, who would become her partner in Florence + The Machine. The original demo had a co-writing credit for Matt Alchon, Florence's first boyfriend. A proper version of the song later appeared on the *Lungs* album. "The name of that demo is Girl with One Eye. It was the first song Florence Welch ever worked on where Thurssoon to be partner Issa Summers."
**Kiss With A Fist** : This song was the first official Florence + The Machine single when the *Lungs* album was released in 2009. It originated as a track called "Happy Slap" from an album by Florence's previous band, Ashock, and was co-written by Matt Alchon, her ex-boyfriend. "The original demo of Kiss With A Fist, the first official Florence and the machine single, when the lungs album was put together in 2009."
**Little Donkey (2008)** : This track was part of an elaborate puppet show video project created by Florence and Issa Summers under the name Flora Bull and Miss A Rebella. The video was described as "very weird and violent and kind of bloody" and was an early exploration of taking on characters during performance, which contributed to the evolution of the "Florence and the machine" concept. "It's called Little Donkey, and appears online under the name Flora Bull and Miss A Rebella."
**Dog Days Are Over** : The demo for this song was instrumental in helping Florence Welch and Isabella Summers secure their first record deal. The song's title was inspired by an art installation in South London called "The Dog Days Are Over," which Florence frequently passed on her way to art school. "The demo of Dog Days are over that helped Florence Welch and her machine partner is a summer's Get That First Record deal."
**No Light, No Light** : This song, featured on the *Ceremonials* album, prominently showcases both big drums and the harp played by Tom Munger. The inclusion of harp in such a distinctive way highlights the band's use of interesting and unusual instrumentation. "He used to track from the ceremonials out in the features both big drums and harp. It's called No Light, No Light."

## curiosities
**Early Artistic Inspiration** : Florence's mother was a New York academic and a professor of Renaissance studies, which exposed Florence to a wealth of Renaissance material during her childhood. Her favorite painting was "Saint Agatha of Sicily" by Pierro de la Francesca (c. 1450), which depicts Agatha with her breasts cut off, reflecting an early inclination towards dark and evocative imagery. "Her mum is an academic from New York and is an actual professor of Renaissance studies. So you can imagine the kind of material that lay around the house as Florence was growing up."
**Family Background and Influences** : Florence's father, Nick, was an "old punk" who had associations with Joe Strummer before The Clash formed, and later became an ad executive for Aero Chocolate Bars, resembling Andy Warhol. Her paternal grandfather hung out with the Warhol crowd at Studio 54, and another grandfather, a deputy editor of the Daily Telegraph, gave her the soundtrack to *The Rocky Horror Picture Show*, which became her first favorite record and sparked a crush on its creator, Richard O'Brien, at age 10. "And then there's her father, Nick. He was an old punk who once hung out with Joe Strummer in the days before he joined the clash."
**Bipolar Grandmother and Childhood Trauma** : Florence's grandmother, who was bipolar, committed suicide when Florence was 13 years old. This significant personal tragedy likely contributed to her emotional landscape and artistic perspective. "This would also be the time to mention that Florence had a bipolar grandmother who committed suicide. Florence was 13 at the time."
**Dyspraxia Diagnosis and Self-Consciousness** : While living in New York, Florence was diagnosed with dyspraxia, a genetic neurological condition affecting physical coordination, balance, and potentially social skills. This diagnosis, combined with being uprooted from the UK, being tall, and having bright red hair (dyed at age 10), made her feel "even more different" and very self-conscious, leading her to find escape in music. "This is a genetic neurological condition that makes it tough for someone to plan and coordinate their physical movements."
**Early Singing at Funerals** : At age 10 or 11, Florence's strong voice led her to become the "go-to person for singing at funerals," an experience that would have had a profound impact on her as a young child. "She had such a lovely voice that she became the go-to person for singing at funerals."
**Literary and Dark Interests** : During her time at an exclusive private school, Florence developed a deep interest in literature, particularly Emily Dickinson and Emily Brontë. She also cultivated a fascination with Jack the Ripper and murder, with "the grizzlier, the better," indicating an early attraction to macabre themes. "She devoured all sorts of literature, especially Emily Dickinson and Emily Bronte. Oh, and she also developed a thing for anything to do with Jack the Ripper and anything to do with murder, the grizzlier, the better."
**Parental Split and Blended Family Dynamics** : Florence's parents separated, leading to her mother moving in with a new dad who already had three children. This transition shifted Florence from being the eldest child to the middle child in a blended family that "didn't blend so well," causing her to retreat into her bedroom for reading, music, singing, and dancing, finding solace in Billy Holiday's music. "And then her parents split. More serious emotional drama. She, who was a new dad, was a neighbor with three kids of his own."
**The Florence Commandments** : Isabella Summers attempted to organize Florence's energies by helping her construct a list of guiding principles known as "The Florence Commandments." These ten rules included directives like "always carry seeds," "always carry a book," "dance to all the music, dance hall style," and "climb anything," reflecting Florence's quirky and eccentric approach to life. "There are ten of them. Number one, always carry seeds. Number two, always carry a book. Number three, support your local charity shop."
**Bathroom Audition for Manager Mayrid Nash** : In late 2006, after many drinks at the Soho Review Bar in London, Florence cornered Mayrid Nash, a "seenster" and influential figure in the indie scene, in the women's bathroom. She drunkenly sang an Etta James song to Nash to prove her vocal prowess, an impromptu audition that led to Nash becoming her manager and securing her record deal. "Once she got Mayrid cornered in the Lou, she began singing an edit James song at her with the soul intent of proving that she had a big, strong voice."
**Equipment Trailer Fire (October 2009)** : During a European tour in October 2009, the band experienced a disaster when their equipment trailer caught fire, destroying their gear. They stood by the roadside and listened as the harp strings snapped due to the heat. "In October 2009, there was a disaster when the band's gear was destroyed when their equipment trailer went up in flames during a European tour."
**Barefoot Performances and Injuries** : Florence Welch consistently performs barefoot on stage, a preference stemming from her lifelong enjoyment of dancing without shoes. This habit has led to several injuries, including a broken foot sustained from a stage dive during a Coachella gig in April 2015. "Florence, like Dolores Arreared into the cranberries, is one of those people who likes to perform barefoot."
**Subbing for Dave Grohl at Glastonbury** : Florence subbed in for Dave Grohl at Glastonbury after he broke his leg falling off a stage in Sweden. This performance highlighted her resilience and ability to step into major roles. "So it's sort of fitting that she subbed in for Dave Grohl at Glastonbury after he broke his leg falling off the stage in Sweden."
**Past Struggles with Alcohol and Sobriety** : Florence experienced a period of severe drinking, which contributed to a nervous breakdown between the *Ceremonials* and *How Big, How Blue, How Beautiful* albums. An anecdote includes waking up with a chipped tooth after a night of partying with Kanye West, with no memory of how it happened. She now states that she no longer drinks. "The drinking was pretty severe for a while. After a night of parting with Kanye West, she woke up at the hotel with a chip tooth. No idea."

## curiosities
**Non-Traditional Harpist** : Tom Munger began his musical journey with classical harp lessons in college, but his instructors became "tired of him getting all jazzy and rocky" with the instrument, prompting him to leave. He then experimented by adding various effects pedals to his harp, establishing himself as a unique and unconventional harpist, which appealed greatly to Florence Welch and Isabella Summers. "He'd begun with classical harp lessons in college, but when instructors grew tired of him getting all jazzy and rocky with such a serious instrument, he knew it was time to leave."

# curiosity

**Peter O'Toole Film Quote - "My Favorite Year" (1982)** : A memorable quote from the 1982 film *My Favorite Year*, starring Peter O'Toole as Alan Swan, highlights the fan's desire for heroes to be larger than life. When a junior writer, Benji Stone, defends his "hero worship," he states, "Whoever you were in those movies, those silly heroes meant a lot to me. What doesn't matter if it was an illusion? It worked. So don't tell me this is you life size. I can't use you life size. I need Alan Swan's as big as I can get them." This sentiment is used to introduce the idea that fans often seek fantasy and escape from their musical heroes, preferring them to be "bigger and cooler and larger than life." "So don't tell me this is you life size. I can't use you life size. I need Alan Swan's as big as I can get them."
**Fan Desire for Larger-Than-Life Musical Heroes** : Many music fans desire their musical heroes to be more than just "life-sized," seeking a fantasy land populated by "exotic creatures who do and say things far, far beyond what we could ever dream of doing." This creates a tricky balance where fans want both heroism and a degree of accessibility for personal connection, preferring the fantasy but with a "door crack" open to reality. "And that's how a lot of us feel about our heroes. And I include our musical heroes in that number."
**Evolution of Audience Preference for Rock Stars** : Following the Grunge era, where authenticity and relatability were key, audience preferences shifted towards desiring rock stars who were "bigger and cooler and larger than life." This desire for spectacle, theater, and escape became paramount, ensuring fans felt they were getting their "money's worth" from live performances. "But after a while, we decided that we didn't want our rock stars to be like us so much. We wanted them bigger and cooler and larger than life."
**Contemporary Balance of Spectacle and Relatability in Music** : Current audience preferences in music represent a balance between desiring grand spectacle and also appreciating relatability. While stripped-down performances allowing the music to speak for itself (like with Dave Grohl) are valued, there is also a continued need for "larger than life personalities" who are "weird and coo-key and careless and experimental" to keep dreams alive and maintain interest. "Today, we're currently in a phase where spectacle and relatability pretty much balance each other out."
