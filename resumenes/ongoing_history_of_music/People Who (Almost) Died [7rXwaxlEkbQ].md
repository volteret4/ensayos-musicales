## curiosities
**1995 World Tour Incident** : In January 1995, R.E.M. embarked on their first world tour in six years, beginning in Australia and moving through Asia before reaching Europe. During a show on March 1, 1995, at the Demali Ice Rink in Lausanne, Switzerland, drummer Bill Berry collapsed on stage about 90 minutes into the gig while singing a falsetto part in the song "Tongue." "During that show, Bill developed a bad headache... And then about 90 minutes into the gig as he was singing the falsetto part in a song called Tung, Bill recalls feeling like somebody dropped the bowling ball on his head."
**Bill Berry's Brain Aneurysm and Surgery** : Following his collapse on stage in Switzerland, Bill Berry was hospitalized. An MRI revealed not one, but two aneurysms on the right side of his brain, one of which had ruptured. He underwent a craniotomy where part of his skull was removed, performed by a world-renowned surgeon. Berry suffered no lasting effects on his brain function or motor skills. "an MRI revealed that there was not one but two aneurysms on the right side of his brain. One had ruptured and the other was about two. It was Russian Dissertry where he underwent a criniotomy."
**Tour Interruption and Recovery** : After Bill Berry's emergency surgery, all R.E.M. shows were canceled for over two months. Berry recovered in the hospital for about a month, with the band members keeping him company and even smuggling in fresh donuts. The band returned to the stage with Berry on May 15, 1995, in Mountain View, California, and the official tour program for that leg featured an image of Bill's brain showing the aneurysms. "All shows were canceled until May 15th 1995 when Bill in the band returned to the stage in Mountain View California. The official program for that part of the tour featured an image of Bill's brain showing the aneurysms."
**Bill Berry's Departure and New Life** : The brain aneurysm incident served as a significant wake-up call for Bill Berry. In 1997, two years after the event, he resigned from R.E.M. and has since been living in Georgia, where he works as a gentleman hay farmer. "The incident served as something as a wake-up call for Bill. In 1997 he resigned from the band and lives in Georgia as a gentleman hay farmer."
