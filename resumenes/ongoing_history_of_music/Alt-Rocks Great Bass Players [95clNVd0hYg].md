# artist - Ramones

## members
Dee Dee Ramone (Douglas)
Johnny Ramone (John Cummings)

## instruments
Danny Electro bass
Fender basses
Ampeg amp

## albums
**Ramones (1976) – First Album** : This album, released in 1976, featured basic rock n' roll that was built on Dee Dee Ramone's super basic bass line.

## curiosities
**Band Formation (1974)** : Dee Dee Ramone and John Cummings (later Johnny Ramone) got drunk after work one day in 1974 and decided to form a band. Neither guy knew the first thing about music, but they wanted to make it, even though they couldn't play a note on their new instruments.
**Early Punk Ethos** : The appeal and genius of the Ramones was showing everyone that anyone could do what they were doing, requiring only the guts to do it.
**Dee Dee Ramone's Role** : Dee Dee Ramone became the main songwriter and lyricist for the Ramones for 15 years.
**Dee Dee Ramone's Playing Style** : His playing consisted of long runs of the same notes. He was considered one of the most primitive and basic bass players in history, but his spirit was what counted. His approach inspired many young, alienated, and disenfranchised kids.

# artist - The Clash

## members
Paul Simonon
Mick Jones

## genres
Punk
Reggae
Ska

## instruments
Fender Precision
Ampeg amplifiers and cabinets

## albums
**London Calling (1979)** : Released at the tail end of 1979, this was a hugely important album that advanced the cause of punk with its sounds, attitudes, influences, and complexities. It is described as one of the most influential rock albums, period.

## songs
**Guns of Brixton (1979)** : This song was written by Paul Simonon and featured on the album 'London Calling'. It reflects his absorption of deep bass sounds.

## curiosities
**Paul Simonon's Artistic Background** : Paul Simonon initially wanted to be an artist and a painter.
**Learning the Bass** : When Mick Jones offered to teach him guitar to join his new band, Paul tried but found guitar too hard, complaining, "This is too hard." Mick then suggested he get a bass, stating, "There's two less strings." Paul learned to play the bass on his own.
**Early Skill Level** : When The Clash recorded their very first album in early 1977, Paul still really didn't know what he was doing. However, by the time the band recorded their third album, a little more than two years later, he had become proficient.
**Influence of Jamaican Music** : Paul Simonon absorbed the deep bass sounds he heard coming from the windows of new Jamaican immigrants, which he described as "fat and warm and had a groove unlike anything that came out of Britain." This influence shaped his playing style.
**London Calling Album Cover** : The iconic cover of the 'London Calling' album features Paul Simonon smashing his bass. This is considered one of the most iconic rock photos ever.
**Unique Playing Technique** : As time went on, Paul's reggae and ska-influenced rhythms set him apart from most other bass players. Unlike many bassists who plucked strings with their fingers, Paul tended to use a pick, which gave his Fender Precision bass a more percussive sound. He also preferred the warmth of big Ampeg amplifiers and cabinets.

# artist - Joy Division

## members
Peter Hook (Hookie)
Barney (Bernard Sumner)

## genres
Punk
Rock and Roll

## curiosities
**Band Formation Inspiration** : After seeing the Sex Pistols play in Manchester, Peter Hook and his friend Barney decided they could make similar noise. Barney got a guitar, and Peter found a bass.
**Improvised Equipment** : Lacking money for an amplifier, Peter Hook and Barney figured out how to wire their gear through the tone arm of the turntable in a console stereo owned by one of their families.
**Unconventional Playing Style Origin** : When Joy Division formed, Peter Hook, like other kids inspired by punk, made it up as he went along. He didn't know what he was doing or that his technique was "wrong" — his bass was slung far too low, his stance was incorrect, and his amp was severely underpowered.
**Developing a Signature Sound** : The band rehearsed in a room of a local pub where the drums and guitars were too loud, preventing Hookie from hearing himself through his underpowered amp. To cut through the din, he started playing his parts far higher on the fretboard than normal. This combination of "mistakes" ultimately advanced the cause of rock and roll.
**Influence Despite Technical Skill** : Peter Hook was not the most adept musician, but his approach to his instrument was so different for his era that he became an idol to many.

# artist - The Stranglers

## albums

## songs
**Peaches (1977)** : This song, released in 1977, is highlighted as a prime example of Jean-Jacques Burnel's unique and aggressive bass style, showcasing his melodic lines played with a pick and distorted amplifier.

**Redefining Bass Role** : His playing style, combining melodicism with aggression and distortion, redefined what a bass player could do within a band.

# artist - Talking Heads

## members
Tina Weymouth

## genres
New Wave
Funk

## curiosities
**Breaking 70s Stereotypes** : Tina Weymouth broke many stereotypes prevalent in the 1970s, when girls were typically expected to only sing or strum an acoustic guitar. Punk rock's ethos, "if you have something to say, say it. All you need is the guts to do it," helped to dismantle these rules.
**Pioneer Female Bassist (1976-1977)** : When Talking Heads moved to New York in 1976-1977, people were shocked to discover their bass player was a woman, as very few had ever seen such a thing before. This was considered "unladylike" and almost as scandalous as a woman playing the drums.
**Impact on Female Musicians** : Tina Weymouth's very appearance as a bass player with the Talking Heads exploded numerous stereotypes and broke down many silly preconceptions. She was a leading "suffragette" for the cause of the female bassist back in the day.
**Musical Skill and Funkiness** : Beyond her visual impact, Tina Weymouth was also a very good musician; few new wave bands had a bass player as funky as she was.
**Inspiration for Future Female Bassists** : Her influence started a trend, inspiring many female bassists who followed, including Melissa Auf der Maur (Whom, Smashing Pumpkins), D'Arcy Wretzky (Smashing Pumpkins), Kim Deal (Pixies, The Breeders), Kim Gordon (Sonic Youth), Nikki Monninger (Silversun Pickups), and Sean Yseult (White Zombie).

## songs
**Life During Wartime** : This song features the dream bass playing of Tina Weymouth, showcasing her distinctive funky style.

# artist - Public Image Limited

## members
Jah Wobble (John Wardle)
Johnny Lydon (John Lydon)

## genres
Punk
Dub
World Music

## songs
**Public Image (1978)** : This 1978 song features Jah Wobble on bass, showcasing his distinct playing style.

## curiosities
**Real Name and Nickname Origin** : Jah Wobble's real name is John Wardle. He was one of "four Johns" who hung out in the punk days, alongside John Lydon (Johnny Rotten) and John Ritchie (Sid Vicious). His nickname, "Jawwabel," came early, possibly due to his "wobbly" playing style, or supposedly given by Sid Vicious when drunk.
**Joining Public Image Limited** : After the Sex Pistols broke up, Johnny Lydon chose Jah Wobble to be the bass player in his new group, Public Image Limited. This was considered a risky choice because Wobble tended to be "a little prickly."
**Drummer Anecdote** : There is a story that Jah Wobble hated a particular drummer so much that he "set him on fire."
**Dub Influence** : Jah Wobble's playing was heavily influenced by dub, a Jamaican style characterized by slow, low rhythms with a deep, deep groove.
**Pioneering World Music** : After leaving Public Image Limited, he delved deeply into world music long before many others, working with musicians from as far away as China. This made him very important in this area, and people have sought him out for collaborations and to play on their recordings due to his skilled and versatile approach.

**Unique Style** : Simon Gallup is noted for his unique style and approach to the bass, which more than compensated for him not being the most technically adept player.

**Influence on The Cure's Sound** : The Cure is considered one of the most influential Alt Rock bands of all time, and its rhythm section, especially the bass, has been a significant part of their most important songs.

# artist - Red Hot Chili Peppers

## members
Flea (Michael Peter Balzary)
Anthony Kiedis
Hillel Slovak

## genres
Jazz
Rock
Funk
Hardcore
Alt Rock

## albums
**Mother's Milk (1989)** : This album, released in 1989, represents the peak of Flea's slap-based playing style.

## songs
**Higher Ground (1989)** : Featured on the 'Mother's Milk' album, this song exemplifies Flea's slap-based style at its peak.

## curiosities
**Early Musical Background** : Born in Australia, Flea's stepfather was a jazz musician, leading to many musicians jamming at his home. His first instrument was the trumpet. He initially did not like rock music and was serious about pursuing jazz professionally.
**Introduction to Rock** : Flea began to listen to rock with seriousness only after meeting Anthony Kiedis and Hillel Slovak at school.
**Joining Hillel's Band** : Hillel Slovak was in a band called Anthem. After they lost their bass player, Hillel asked Flea if he would like to try playing bass.
**Developing Slap Bass Style** : Flea developed his distinctive style of "slapping his bass" from his years of jazz study, which gave everything a "new jumpy funky sound." He studied Bootsy Collins, a former member of George Clinton's band, and Sly & The Family Stone became a favorite influence.
**Offer from Public Image Limited** : After Anthem, Flea found work in a hardcore band called Fear. He was even offered a job by Johnny Lydon to join Public Image Limited after Jah Wobble's departure but ultimately returned to his friends Hillel and Anthony to form the Red Hot Chili Peppers.
**Evolution of Playing Style** : Flea's playing style has dramatically changed since 'Mother's Milk'; his slap-style has been almost entirely abandoned in favor of a more melodic approach, characterized by fewer notes, more emotion, and less frantic playing.
**Influences and Impact** : He became a fan of Simon Gallup's playing with The Cure and Peter Hook's work with Joy Division. In the process, Flea became one of the most copied bass players not only in Alt Rock but in all of Rock music.
**Philosophy on Music** : Bass Player magazine quoted Flea as saying, "Any instrument is just a vehicle to express who you are and your relationship to the world, no matter what level you're doing it on, playing music is an opportunity to give something to the world."
**Collaborations** : Flea has played with various artists, including Jane's Addiction and Young MC, driven by his desire to simply play music.

# artist - Primus

## members
Les Claypool

## instruments
Whammy bar
Processors
Distortion pedals
Four-string basses
Six-string bass
Stand-up basses

## albums
**Sailing the Seas of Cheese (1992)** : This 1992 album features the track "Tommy the Cat," which showcases Les Claypool's unique technique and includes a guest vocal performance from Tom Waits.

## songs
**Tommy the Cat (1992)** : From the 1992 album 'Sailing the Seas of Cheese', this track is noted for its demonstration of Les Claypool's technique and features a great guest vocal performance from Tom Waits.

## curiosities
**Aggressive and Versatile Playing Style** : Les Claypool is known for "attacking a bass" with a style that can be both rocking and super funky. He employs various techniques including strumming strings, slapping strings, and tapping them with his fingers.
**Unique Instrumentation and Effects** : Some of his guitars feature a whammy bar, allowing him to bend notes in unusual ways. He also processes his notes through a series of processors and distortion pedals.
**Multi-Instrumentalist and Vocalist** : Live, he performs all these playing techniques while simultaneously singing lead vocals.
**Diverse Bass Collection** : Claypool uses standard four-string basses, a six-string bass when the need arises, and a variety of stand-up basses.
**Metallica Offer** : There is a story that he was offered the job of bassist in Metallica after the death of Cliff Burton, but he reportedly did not get the job because, as quoted, he "was too good."
**Guest Vocalist** : Tom Waits provided a guest vocal performance on the track "Tommy the Cat."

# curiosity

**Importance of the Electric Bass in Rock and Roll** : While rock and roll took off with the electric guitar, it would not have become what it is today without the low, steady thump of an electric bass played through big cabinets powered by warm-sounding tube amplifiers, and without the lockstep rhythms produced by the bass player and the drummer.
**Criteria for Influential Bass Players** : The primary criterion for inclusion on the list of influential bass players is influence itself. The individuals chosen may not be the most technically adept at their instrument, but they inspired many kids.
**Exclusions from Influential Bass Player List** : The list focuses exclusively on Alt Rock and deliberately excludes "classic rock guys" like John Paul Jones, John Entwistle, Jack Bruce, Geddy Lee, and Roger Waters, as well as anyone from the Motown scene or the jazz scene, such as Jaco Pastorius.
**Early Punk Ethos** : The fundamental premise of punk was "if you have something to say, say it." It demonstrated that anyone could make music; all that was needed was the guts to do it.
**Honorable Mention – Mark King (Level 42)** : Mark King is recognized for his "dunning, slot-based technique" and is considered deserving of more respect.
**Honorable Mention – Tony Levin (Peter Gabriel's Band)** : Tony Levin is praised as "a joy to listen to and to watch," often drawing significant attention during Peter Gabriel's concerts.
**Honorable Mention – Kim Gordon (Sonic Youth)** : Kim Gordon is acknowledged for inspiring many women and men with her musicianship.
**Honorable Mention – Kim Deal (Pixies)** : Kim Deal is recognized for her inspirational impact on many musicians.
**Honorable Mention – Cliff Burton (Metallica)** : Cliff Burton is mentioned as a bass player who could also be included on a list of influential bassists.
