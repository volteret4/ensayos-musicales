## curiosities
**Early Life & Family Background** : Born John Simon Richie on May 10, 1957. His mother, Ann, was a high school dropout who became a pot dealer and later a hardcore heroin addict. His father, John, a guardsman at Buckingham Palace and semi-pro trombone player, never supported them after Sid's birth in Ibiza, Spain. "His mother Ann was a high school dropout who met Sid's father while she was enlisted in the army. His father, John, was, believe it or not, a guardsman at Buckingham Palace."
**Nickname Origin** : John Beverly's nickname "Sid" came from an incident where he was bitten by John Lighten's pet hamster, also named Sid, which led to the observation, "Who knew that a hamster could be vicious?" The "Vicious" part was added later, inspired by a Lou Reed song. "From that day on, John Beverly had the nickname Sid. The vicious came later after the Lureed song."
**Early Musical Ventures** : Before joining the Sex Pistols, Sid and John Lighten (Johnny Rotten) busked, often playing Alice Cooper covers on acoustic guitar and tambourine. Their method was to play until they were paid to stop. "Sid and John Lighten picked up some spare change busking, usually by playing covers by Alice Cooper."
**Assault on Nick Kent** : In June 1976, Sid Vicious assaulted music journalist Nick Kent of the NME with a rusted bicycle chain after disliking his trousers. Despite the severity of the attack, Sid escaped charges. "said when up to him and said, boy, I don't like your trousers. And bashed him up pretty good with the rusted bicycle chain that he carried with him."
**Glass-Throwing Incident at 100 Club** : On the second day of the 100 Club Punk Special in September 1976, while The Damned were performing, a speed-intoxicated Sid Vicious threw a glass aimed at Dave Vanian, the singer. He missed Vanian but hit a woman in the face, blinding her in one eye, which led to Sid's first prison sentence. "He missed hitting a woman in the face and blinding her in one eye. That led to Sid's first taste of prison."
**Failed Marriage to Chrissie Hynde** : Sid was paid two pounds by Chrissie Hynde, who was working illegally in the UK, to marry her for a work permit. The marriage almost happened, but the office was closed on the day they intended to go through with it. "She paid Sid two pounds to marry her so she could get a work permit. And it almost happened too, but the day they went to get married, the office was closed."
**US Tour Self-Destruction** : During the Sex Pistols' 1977 US tour, Sid was frequently strung out on drugs and alcohol. Roadies often didn't bother plugging in his bass, and he engaged in self-mutilation on stage, carving himself with broken beer bottles and writing "give me a fix" on his chest. "Sid was often so strung out that the roadies didn't even bother plugging in his base."
**Infamous Truck Stop Encounter** : During the ill-fated US tour, Sid Vicious, heavily involved in drugs, engaged in shocking behavior at a truck stop restaurant near San Antonio. After a family complained about his smell and made rude comments, Sid approached the father, slashed his arm with a penknife, causing blood to splatter over the man's steak. "And all the blood blew out like ketchup over the man's steak."
**Post-Pistols Coma and Hospitalization** : On January 19, 1978, while flying to London after the Sex Pistols disbanded, Sid fell into a coma due to an overdose of diazepam, methadone, and alcohol. He was rushed to a hospital upon landing and warned that continued drinking would lead to his death within six months. "But on the flight, he fell into a coma. Too much dyes of Pam, methadone, and alcohol."
**Self-Harm Attempt During Detox** : Shortly after midnight on October 23, 1978, while attempting to detox with methadone at the Hotel Saville in London with his mother, Sid went into the bathroom and cut his arms and wrists with a razor and a crushed light bulb. He later stated he had made a pact with Nancy and tried to jump out a window when paramedics arrived. "He was attempting to detox with methadone. But then that night he went into the bathroom and cut up his arms and wrists with a razor and a crushed light bulb."
**Assault on Todd Smith's Girlfriend** : On December 5, 1978, while supervised by Rockets Red Glare at a club, Sid Vicious got into a fight using a beer bottle with the girlfriend of Todd Smith, the brother of singer Patti Smith. He was arrested, charged with assault, and sent to Riker's Island. "On December 5th, he went to a club under the supervision of Rockets Red Glare and got into a beer bottle fight with the girlfriend of Todd Smith, the brother of singer Patty Smith."
**Final Overdose and Suicide Note** : On February 2, 1979, after a celebratory party at Michelle Robinson's apartment following his detox program and release from Riker's Island, Sid Vicious overdosed on heroin. He left a suicide note stating, "bury me in my leather jacket, jeans, and motorcycle boots. Goodbye." He was 21 years old. "Sid left a note that read, bury me in my leather jacket, jeans, and motorcycle boots. Goodbye."
**Alleged Spreading of Ashes** : After his cremation, Sid's mother reportedly spread some of his ashes at Nancy's grave in Philadelphia. A possibly apocryphal story claims she tripped at Heathrow airport, spilling the remaining ashes, which were then sucked into the air conditioning ducts. "She took the rest home to London, but there's a story, possibly apocryphal, that she tripped when she arrived at Heathrow, spilling some of Sid's ashes, and watching them get sucked up into the air conditioning ducts."

# artist - Nancy Spungin

## curiosities
**Birth Complications & Early Trauma** : Born Nancy Lauren Spungin on February 27, 1958, in Philadelphia, she nearly died during birth because her umbilical cord was wrapped around her neck, causing cyanosis. She required immediate blood transfusions, suggesting possible oxygen deprivation. "She almost didn't survive her own birth because the umbilical cord was wrapped around her neck, almost choking her, on her way out into the world."
**Childhood Emotional Disturbances** : Nancy displayed emotional problems from an early age, throwing tantrums at three months. By age three, a doctor prescribed barbiturates to control her constant screaming, and she began psychiatric treatment at four. She exhibited violent behavior, including threatening a babysitter with scissors and physically attacking her psychiatrist. "A doctor was worried enough to prescribe baby Nancy barbituates when she was just three in hopes that would stop her from always screaming."
**Expulsion and Suicide Attempt** : She was expelled from school at age eleven and later ran away from her next school, attempting suicide by cutting her wrists with scissors. She escaped from a home for troubled kids where she had been enrolled. "She was expelled from school at eleven. She ran away from her next school and attempted suicide by cutting her wrists with scissors."
**Teenage Diagnoses and Ban from Colorado** : Diagnosed with schizophrenia at age fifteen, Nancy was also brilliant and enrolled in university in Colorado at sixteen. However, she was thrown out due to "bad and weird behavior," including an arrest for trying to buy pot and accusations of theft. As a result of her issues, she was banished from the state to avoid legal consequences. "Nancy was diagnosed with schizophrenia when she was 15."
**Early Adult Life in New York** : At seventeen, Nancy moved to New York City where she worked as a stripper and occasionally as a prostitute to earn extra money. She became involved in the punk scene, frequenting clubs like CBGB and Max's Kansas City, sometimes carrying drugs for bands. "She moved to New York, where she worked for a while as a stripper, and when she needed extra money, she turned tricks. She was just 17."
**Move to London and Groupie Lifestyle** : In February 1977, Nancy moved to London, believing it was "where the action was." She fully embraced the groupie lifestyle, with the Sex Pistols as her primary target. "She deterred that that's where the action was. She needed to be there. And Nancy embraced everything she could about the groupie lifestyle."
**"Nauseating Nancy" by the Press** : Due to her loud American attitude and constant presence around the infamous Sex Pistols, Nancy was dubbed "nauseating Nancy" by the press. "Nancy who inserted herself everywhere was dubbed nauseating Nancy by the press because of her loud American attitude."
**Wrist-Cutting Incident in Paris** : While Sid was filming parts for "The Great Rock and Roll Swindle" in Paris, Nancy, upset at being left alone in their hotel room, cut her wrists. This was not a serious attempt but rather an act to "make a point." "Nancy was upset at being left alone so she cut her wrists. Nothing serious though. She just wanted to make a point."
**Murder in the Chelsea Hotel** : Nancy Spungin died in Room 100 of the Chelsea Hotel between 8 and 10 AM on October 12, 1978. She was found in the bathroom under the sink, having been stabbed in the lower abdomen with a Jaguar Wilderness K-11 knife, causing fatal external and internal hemorrhaging. The medical examiner concluded it was murder, not suicide. "It was 10.45 on the morning of Thursday, October 12th, 1978. Sid was in shock, initially in a panic. He confessed, I stabbed her, but I never meant to kill her."

## curiosities
**Formation and Vision** : The Sex Pistols were formed when clothing shop owner Malcolm McLaren recruited John Lighten (who became Johnny Rotten) to be the singer for a group intended as a "living breathing advertisement" for his store, "Sex." "Malcolm McLaren recruited John Lighten to be the singer of a group that would be a living breathing advertisement for his store. He called them the Sex Pistols."
**Glenn Matlock's Departure** : In February 1977, original bassist Glenn Matlock was "thrown out of the group" by Malcolm McLaren. His alleged crime was "liking the Beatles," a sentiment seen as antithetical to the band's punk ethos. "Glenn Matlock was thrown out of the group for allegedly committing the crime of liking the Beatles."
**US Tour Visa Troubles** : The band faced significant difficulties obtaining working visas for their planned late 1977 American tour due to the criminal records of its members. Johnny Rotten had a minor drug charge, Steve Jones and Paul Cook had past theft charges, and Sid Vicious had a record including drug charges, assault on a police officer, attempted grand theft auto, criminal damage, and possession of a dangerous weapon. "Getting working visas for these yobbs proved to be very difficult."
**Saturday Night Live Cancellation** : As a direct result of their visa delays, the Sex Pistols were unable to appear on "Saturday Night Live" as scheduled. Their slot was instead filled by Elvis Costello and the Attractions. "As a result of all the visa delays, the sex pistols weren't able to appear on Saturday night live as scheduled."
**Subversive US Tour Strategy** : Instead of hitting major markets, manager Malcolm McLaren designed a "subversive" strategy for the US tour, planning a major swing into the punk-unfriendly Southern US. This tour, however, "did not go well," with hostile crowds and Sid Vicious often being too strung out to perform effectively. "But instead of hitting all the major markets, McLaren thought it would be very subversive, very punk, if the pistols made a big swing into the punk, unfriendly, southern US."
**Final Performance and Disbandment** : The band's infamous US tour "limped" into the Winterland Ballroom in San Francisco on January 14, 1978. Running on fumes, the Sex Pistols played their final song, with Johnny Rotten's anger and frustration evident in his closing remarks: "Have I yet to feel that you've been cheated? Good night." With that, the band was officially done. "By the time the tour limped into the Winterland Ballroom in San Francisco in January the 14th, the Sex Pistols were running on fumes. And then, for the final song, they came to an end."

# artist - Malcolm McLaren

## curiosities
**Owner of "Sex" Clothing Shop** : Malcolm McLaren owned the clothing shop named "Sex" on Kings Road in Chelsea, London. This shop served as a hub for the early punk scene and was where he recruited John Lighten for the Sex Pistols. "All four hung around a clothing shop called Sex on the Kings Road in Chelsea, which is where owner Malcolm McLaren recruited John Lighten to be the singer of a group."
**Vision for Sex Pistols** : McLaren recruited John Lighten to be the singer of a group that would serve as a "living breathing advertisement" for his clothing store, "Sex," which he then named the Sex Pistols. "Malcolm McLaren recruited John Lighten to be the singer of a group that would be a living breathing advertisement for his store. He called them the Sex Pistols."
**Reaction to Sid Vicious's Violence** : After Sid Vicious assaulted journalist Nick Kent with a bicycle chain in June 1976, McLaren expressed dismay, calling Sid a "psychopath" and promising that he would never be allowed at their concerts again. "Malcolm McLaren though was mortified. He told Kent, oh God, that guy's a psychopath. He'll never be one of our concerts again. I promise that."
**Strategy for Sex Pistols US Tour** : For the Sex Pistols' 1977 US tour, McLaren conceived a "subversive" strategy to tour the punk-unfriendly Southern US, believing it would be "very punk." "McLaren thought it would be very subversive, very punk, if the pistols made a big swing into the punk, unfriendly, southern US."
**Payment Strategy for Sid Vicious** : During the Sex Pistols' chaotic US tour, McLaren put Sid Vicious on a weekly stipend of $14, hoping that a lack of readily available cash might help him "keep him straight" from drug use. "McLaren had him on a weekly stipend of $14, thinking that maybe a lock of cash would keep him straight."
**Advocacy for Sid Vicious after Nancy's Death** : After Nancy Spungin's murder and Sid Vicious's arrest, Malcolm McLaren and Sid's mother immediately sought a high-profile lawyer for his defense. McLaren strongly believed Sid was innocent, theorizing that an unknown guest had robbed the couple and then stabbed Nancy, noting that $20,000 in royalty money was missing from their room. "McLaren thought that Sid was innocent. He had a theory. He thought that the couple was robbed by an unknown guest who then stabbed Nancy."

# artist - Rockets Red Glare

## curiosities
**Actor, Drug Dealer, and Key Witness** : Rockets Red Glare was a drug dealer, bit actor, club bouncer, stand-up comedian, occasional roadie, and sometime police informant. He appeared in over 30 films, including "Desperately Seeking Susan" and "After Hours." He was called by Nancy Spungin for drugs the night she died and is a central figure in alternative theories of her murder, with some alleging he killed her during a robbery. "Let's talk about him for a second. Rockets was an actor who ended up in more than 30 films."
**Alternative Murder Suspect** : The most popular alternative theory states that Rockets Red Glare murdered Nancy Spungin while Sid Vicious was incapacitated by drugs, in the process of robbing the couple of $1400 (and an alleged $20,000 in royalties). Author Phil Strongman's book "Pretty Vacant" advances this theory, citing Red Glare's sudden acquisition of money and new clothes shortly after Nancy's death, despite previously being broke. "The most popular alternative theory is that Nancy was killed by Rockets Red Glare."
**Death and Unanswered Questions** : Rockets Red Glare denied all accusations of murder, though he reportedly blabbed about what he did to friends and even tried to sell a snuff film of Nancy's murder. He died on May 28, 2001, from kidney failure, liver failure, cirrhosis, and Hepatitis C, exacerbated by morbid obesity, taking with him any potential answers regarding Nancy's death. "Rockets, Red Glare, died of kidney failure, liver failure, cirrhosis, and hepsi on May 28, 2001."

**Chelsea Hotel - Historical Significance** : Completed in 1884 at 222 West 23rd Street in Manhattan, the Chelsea Hotel became a famous hangout and home for "colorful characters," including New York City eccentrics, bohemians, and celebrated figures across arts, literature, and music, such as Arthur C. Clarke, Stanley Kubrick, Robert Mapplethorpe, Jack Kerouac, Tennessee Williams, Arthur Miller, Sam Shepard, Dennis Hopper, Uma Thurman, Elliott Gould, Jane Fonda, William Burroughs, Allen Ginsberg, Andy Warhol, Jackson Pollock, Bob Dylan, Patti Smith, Leonard Cohen, Janis Joplin, Jimi Hendrix, Tom Waits, Jim Morrison, Jeff Beck, Joni Mitchell, Alice Cooper, and Pink Floyd. "The Chelsea Hotel sits at 222 West 23rd Street in Manhattan. Since it was completed in 1884, the place has been a hangout for some very colorful characters."
**Chelsea Hotel - "Junky Floor"** : The first floor of the Chelsea Hotel was notoriously designated as the "junky floor." This was where guests with drug problems, like Sid Vicious and Nancy Spungin, were intentionally placed, so the hotel staff could "keep an eye on things." "But the most notorious floor was the first one. It was designated the junky floor."
**Chelsea Hotel - Deaths and Hauntings** : The Chelsea Hotel has been the site of numerous deaths, including poet Dylan Thomas spending his dying days there. Incidents include suicides by jumping from windows or the 11-story roof, and a woman named Nadia who threw herself out a window in 1922 after cutting off her right hand, leading to stories of her one-handed ghost. A photographer named Billy Maynard was beaten to death in his 8th-floor room in the mid-1970s. "And a lot of people have died at the Chelsea. poet Dylan Thomas spent his dying days there."
**Chelsea Hotel - Room 100** : Room 100 on the "junky floor" of the Chelsea Hotel was where Sid Vicious and Nancy Spungin were moved after a fire in their initial room. This room became the scene of Nancy Spungin's murder on October 12, 1978. "They ended up in room 100 after a fire of some sort broke out in their first room."
**Chelsea Hotel - Eccentric Residents and Animals** : The hotel was known for eccentric guests, such as composer John Cale, who transformed his room into a jungle with twelve-foot trees from Madagascar, birds, a monkey, and an eight-foot snake. Dancer Catherine Dunham was evicted after bringing two full-grown lions into the hotel for a rehearsal. "Composer John Klein singer brought in 12-foot trees from Madagascar, along with birds, a monkey, and an eight-foot snake to turn his room into a jungle."
**"Sex" Clothing Shop** : Located on Kings Road in Chelsea, London, "Sex" was a clothing shop owned by Malcolm McLaren. It served as a central hub for the emerging punk scene and was where McLaren recruited John Lighten (Johnny Rotten) for the Sex Pistols. "All four hung around a clothing shop called Sex on the Kings Road in Chelsea, which is where owner Malcolm McLaren recruited John Lighten to be the singer of a group."
**"The Great Rock and Roll Swindle" Film** : Sid Vicious and Nancy Spungin flew to Paris in January 1978 to film parts for a movie titled "The Great Rock and Roll Swindle." Much of their time there was spent shooting up drugs in their hotel room. "Instead of continuing home, Nancy collected him and the two flew to Paris to film parts of a movie called The Great Rock and Roll Swindle."
**Nancy Spungin's Murder - The Crime Scene** : On the night of October 11, 1978, in Room 100 of New York City's Chelsea Hotel, Nancy Spungin was fatally stabbed in the abdomen with a Jaguar Wilderness K-11 knife (13cm blade) owned by Sid Vicious. She died in the bathroom under the sink. The room was found "drenched in blood." "Something brutal, bloody, and deadly happened in room 100 of New York City's Chelsea hotel on the night of October 11, 1978."
**Nancy Spungin's Murder - Forensic Examination & Autopsy** : The forensic team's initial investigation of Room 100 was described as "really bad," reportedly missing evidence like three syringes, a bottle of Two-in-all, and brown flaky powder. Dr. Gita and the Tyrajohn conducted the autopsy, determining that Nancy died of external and internal hemorrhaging from a one-inch incision that cut arteries. She also had multiple track marks and bruises on her face, consistent with a prior fight. The medical examiner concluded it was murder, not suicide, with death occurring between 8 and 10 AM on October 12, 1978. "Some accounts say that forensics did a really bad job, missing things like three syringes, a bottle of two and all, and some kind of brown, flaky powder."
**Nancy Spungin's Murder - Missing Money** : Very little money was found in Room 100 after Nancy's murder, despite Nancy reportedly having $1400 and an alleged $20,000 in Sex Pistols royalty money from Virgin Records being in a bedside drawer. This missing money fueled robbery theories, particularly concerning Rockets Red Glare. "Very little money was found. Where was Nancy's $1,400?"
**Sid Vicious's Arrest and Bail** : Following Nancy's murder, Sid Vicious was found wandering the hallway, initially confessed to stabbing her, then recanted due to memory loss from the previous 12 hours. He was charged with second-degree murder by early afternoon on October 12, 1978. He was later released on $50,000 bail, paid by Virgin Records, on the condition he not leave New York City. "By early afternoon, he was charged with second-degree murder in the death of Nancy Spungeon."
**"Pretty Vacant" Book's Theory** : Author Phil Strongman, in his book "Pretty Vacant," advances the theory that Rockets Red Glare murdered Nancy Spungin during a robbery while Sid Vicious was heavily sedated. Strongman cites Red Glare's sudden display of blood-stained bills and new clothes shortly after the murder, despite previously being broke, as supporting evidence. "In a book called Pretty Vacant, author Phil Strongman advances this theory, saying that Rockets killed Nancy, took all the money, and was seeing the following days flashing a lot of bloodstayed bills and brand new clothes, less than 48 hours after he professed to being broke and begging people to buy him drinks and then around Greenwich Village."
**Unresolved Mystery of Nancy's Murder** : Despite Sid Vicious being officially charged with Nancy Spungin's murder, significant questions and alternative theories persist decades later. The most popular alternative theory points to drug dealer Rockets Red Glare as the killer, who denied accusations but reportedly blabbed to friends and allegedly tried to sell a snuff film of the murder before his death in 2001. "Punk's most notorious murder will forever have questions attached to it."
**Two-in-all (Sedative)** : This sedative, which is no longer manufactured, was used for sleep since the 1940s and recreationally in the 1970s to come down from other drugs. Also known as "blue tips" or "jeeps," it was addictive and could cause fatal poisoning. Sid Vicious reportedly took 32 Two-in-all tablets (at least 50mg each) on the night Nancy Spungin died, causing him to become groggy and pass out repeatedly. "2-in-all, which they don't make anymore, had been used as a sedative for sleep since the 1940s."
**Deluded (Synthetic Morphine)** : This synthetic morphine was specifically requested by Nancy Spungin from drug dealer Rockets Red Glare on the night she died. She was willing to pay $40 per pill and needed at least six to get high, while Sid could get by on four. "When Nancy called, she wanted drugs, specifically deluded the type of synthetic morphine and some new syringes."
