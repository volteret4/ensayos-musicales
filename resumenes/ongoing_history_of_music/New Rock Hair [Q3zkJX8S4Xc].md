## albums

## curiosities
**Spokesman for International League for the Preservation of Animal Filaments** : In the early 1960s, before achieving widespread fame, David Bowie (then David Jones) acted as a spokesman for a group called the International League for the Preservation of Animal Filaments, advocating for men's right to wear long hair. "In the early 1960s, long before anybody knew who he was, Bowie was a spokesman for a group called the International League for the Preservation of Animal Filaments."
**1964 British Television and Newspaper Appearance for Long Hair Rights** : In 1964, under his birth name David Jones, Bowie appeared on British television and in newspapers alongside other members of the International League for the Preservation of Animal Filaments to publicly defend the right of men to have long hair. "In 1964, Bowie, who was still known as David Jones at the time, appeared on British television and also in the newspapers with a few members of this association to defend the right of a man to have long hair."
**Advocacy for Pop Musicians with Long Hair** : David Bowie passionately articulated his activism for long hair, stating it was "for the protection of pop musicians and those who wear their hair long," acknowledging the "hell" that individuals with shoulder-length hair endured and urging unity. "It's really for the protection of pop musicians and those who wear their hair long, he said, anyone who has the courage to wear hair down to his shoulders has to go through hell."
**Ziggy Stardust's Orange Puffball Haircut** : Around eight years after his early advocacy, Bowie, heavily influenced by his wife Angie, debuted the iconic orange puffball with a tail haircut, which became central to his Ziggy Stardust persona and is regarded as one of rock's most influential hairstyles. "The orange puffball with a tail, sported by Bowie and his Ziggy Stardust face."
**Origin of Ziggy Stardust's Haircut** : A popular narrative recounts that during the "Ziggy Stardust and the Spiders from Mars" album sessions, Bowie expressed a desire for a different look, telling his wife Angie, "I want to cut my hair and I want to look different," and suggested dyeing it red. "One version of the story says that during the sessions for what would become the Ziggy Stardust and the spiders from Mars album, Bowie woke up one morning and said to Angie over his orange juice, I want to cut my hair and I want to look different."
**Vogue Magazine Inspiration for Ziggy's Hair** : After deciding on a new look, David and Angie Bowie searched fashion magazines, eventually finding a version of the desired "new hair thing" in a Vogue advertisement, which served as the blueprint for Ziggy's iconic style. "It was then that neither Bowie or Angie, nobody remembers who, found a version of this new hair thing in an ad in Vogue magazine."
**Susie Fussy - Hairdresser for Ziggy Stardust** : Susie Fussy, a London hairdresser, was instrumental in bringing Ziggy Stardust's look to life; she received the Vogue magazine inspiration from Bowie and Angie, then executed the distinctive cut and dyed it a reddish-orange hue for dramatic effect. "And they took the magazine and showed it to a London hairdresser named Susie Fussy who then gave Bowie the cut and died at that reddish orange for added effect."
**Haircut's Impact on Bowie's Music and Persona** : Angie Bowie believed that the high maintenance of the Ziggy Stardust puffball haircut, contrasting with his previous easy-to-manage hippie hair, compelled David Bowie to become more self-conscious and focused, thereby influencing his musical direction. "But keeping a puffball like this looking good takes effort."
**Haircut Leading to New Experiments in Style** : According to Angela Bowie, the demands of maintaining the Ziggy Stardust haircut heightened David Bowie's self-awareness and focus, consequently leading to extensive new experiments with clothes and makeup, which further shaped his evolving persona. "Angela Bowie says that the haircut forced David Bowie to be more focused and more self-conscious."
**Formation of Ziggy Stardust Character** : The meticulous attention to his appearance, driven by the Ziggy Stardust haircut, culminated in the fully realized Ziggy Stardust character, a persona widely recognized as one of the most historically significant and influential in rock and roll. "And the end result was the fully formed Ziggy Stardust character, one of the most important and influential personas in the history of rock and roll."
**Bowie's Influence on Rock and Roll** : David Bowie, particularly through his Ziggy Stardust alter ego, profoundly altered the trajectory of rock and roll's development and served as a powerful source of inspiration for countless aspiring rock musicians globally. "How Bowie and Ziggy changed the course of the development of rock and roll."
**One of Rock Music's Most Important Individuals** : David Bowie is positioned as one of the three or four most crucial figures in the entire history of rock music, suggesting that contemporary music would be drastically different without his contributions. "In fact, you can make an argument that Bowie was one of the three or four most important individuals in the history of rock music."
**Haircut as Catalyst for Musical Change** : It is posited that David Bowie's immense impact on music, which fundamentally reshaped its future, might have had its genesis in the seemingly simple act of getting a new haircut. "And to think that it might have all started with a haircut."
**Puffball Haircut as Creative Catalyst** : Bowie's "puff ball" haircut transcended mere fashion, appearing to propel him onto an entirely new and exceptionally creative musical trajectory, which subsequently unleashed a wave of influence across all of rock and roll. "So there's Bowie and his puff ball, a hugely important haircut, not just in terms of style and fashion sense, but it seems to have sent Bowie down an entirely new ultra creative musical path, which in turn created a wave of influence that ended up affecting all rock and roll."
**Mullet as Evolution of Ziggy Stardust Haircut** : David Bowie's Ziggy Stardust haircut is noted for possessing mullet-like qualities, suggesting the possibility that the mullet hairstyle itself evolved from Bowie's distinctive "Ziggy puffball." "If you look at David Bowie's Ziggy Stardust haircut, you can see it has a mullet-like attribute."
