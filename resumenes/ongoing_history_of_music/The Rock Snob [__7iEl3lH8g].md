# genre - Alternative

## curiosities
**North American Term Origin (1980)** : The term "alternative" in North America dates back to at least 1980, emerging as rock began to fragment. It originally described music from artists rejected by major labels due to smaller audiences, finding homes with small regional labels like Berserkli and Rounder. These labels and their music were seen as an "alternative" to the mainstream. "Now alternative dates back to at least 1980, a time when rock was really starting to fragment."
**Supplanted "College Rock"** : The term "alternative" eventually replaced "college rock," which was used to describe music popular on campus radio stations. "This word alternative eventually supplanted the term college rock, which was being used to describe the music that found favor with campus radio stations."
**Meaning by Grunge Era (Early 90s)** : By the early 1990s and the grunge era, "alternative" described a new generation of music descended from punk and new wave, rather than from bands like Led Zeppelin or The Beatles. It signified an aesthetic and sensibility, not merely a record label origin, and largely lost its countercultural significance. "by the time we got to the grunge era of the early 90s, alternative was being used to describe a new generation of music that had descended from punk and new wave and not from Led Zeppelin and the Beatles."

# genre - Punk Rock

## curiosities
**Impact on Rock World (Late 1970s)** : Punk emerged around 1977, simultaneously with disco, and "tore asunder the rock world," leading to fragmentation and the rejection of more challenging music by major multinational labels. "At the same time punk hit, tearing us under the rock world."
**Precursor to Post-Punk** : Punk rock is identified as the foundational genre without which post-punk could not have existed. Post-punk bands were not punk rock themselves, but their sound was clearly a descendant, indicating that punk had "happened" first. "In the case of post punk, for example, the term is used to describe a band or sound, which isn't punk rock, but listening to them, you could tell that punk had happened."

# genre - Indie

## curiosities
**British Equivalent of Alternative** : Originally, "indie" in the UK was the equivalent of the North American term "alternative," referring to music from small record labels not owned by major multinational companies. The UK maintained this usage even as North America embraced "alternative nation" in the 1990s. "See originally indie was the British equivalent of the North American term alternative."
**UK Record Charts Influence** : The UK music scene favored the term "indie" partly due to their love for record charts, with an official independent record chart existing since at least 1980, publicizing the progress of indie artists weekly. "An official independent record chart had been part of the music culture since at least 1980."
**Modern North American Meaning** : Today in North America, "indie" essentially refers to music from artists working within an expanding network of labels not associated with the four major labels. However, it has also evolved to describe a certain aesthetic, sensibility, and even a sound, regardless of label origin, sometimes even originating from a major label, especially given distribution deals between independents and majors. "Well essentially it means any music from artists who work within an expanding network of record labels that aren't associated with the four remaining major labels."
**Underground Cred and Self-Promotion** : Some artists aim to maintain "original underground cred" by avoiding traditional label structures, instead using platforms like blogs, Myspace, Facebook, YouTube, SoundCloud, and Bandcamp for promotion and distribution. "They want to keep their original underground cred and find alternate ways of promoting themselves and distributing their music."

**360 Deal Business Model** : The 360 deal is a prevalent business model in the contemporary music industry, necessitated by declining CD sales and insufficient digital sales. Unlike traditional deals focused on music sales revenue, labels now seek multi-rights agreements to recoup investment, taking a share of revenue from merchandise, touring, ringtones, songwriting royalties, and video game placements. In return, artists often gain greater security, touring subsidies, extended development time, and substantial upfront payments, which obligates the label to work hard for their success. "This is the favorite business model in the record business these days."
**Superstar 360 Deals** : A distinct type of 360 deal is reserved for superstar acts, often involving promoters like Live Nation. These deals typically guarantee massive payouts over many years for a share of touring, merchandising, digital, and branding rights, while allowing artists to retain music sales revenue in exchange for commitments to release new albums and tour. "There's another kind, and they only go to superstar acts."
**Mastering of Audio Recordings** : Mastering is the final polishing stage of a recording before it is transferred to its final format (CD, digital file, etc.). This crucial process involves mastering engineers with specialized equipment who fine-tune the sound by removing minor flaws, reducing hiss, ensuring consistent volume across tracks, adjusting stereo imaging, sweetening high frequencies, and tweaking bass, creating the definitive "master recording" from which all copies are made. "This polished up final version is the version from which all copies of the recording will be made in the future. It is the master recording."
**Remastering of Audio Recordings** : Remastering involves revisiting an old master recording to re-polish it and "give it new life" using modern studio technology. The goal is to improve clarity, bring elements like vocals forward, enhance instrumentation, and eliminate tape hiss, often resulting in a significantly better listening experience compared to the original master, prompting consumers to re-purchase the music. "Well, it's just like it sounds. You go back to an old recording, an old master recording and try to polish it up some more and give it new life."
