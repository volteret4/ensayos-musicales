## albums
**Pocket Watch (1992) – Solo Album as Late** : Recorded four songs in December 1990 and another six in July 1991, released on Simple Machines as a cassette-only album. All instruments credited to Dave G in the liner notes.
**Pre-Foo Fighters Demos (1994) – Sixteen Songs from Seattle Studio Session** : Recorded in October 1994 over six days with Barrett Jones at a small studio in Seattle, playing all instruments. Some songs later became Foo Fighters tracks, others were discarded. This material was later released as an unofficial bootleg CD in Japan in 1995.
**Pre-Foo Fighters Demos (1994) – Limited Vinyl and Cassette Distribution** : 100 copies of these sessions were pressed on vinyl and ultra-rare cassettes with rough mixes of 15 songs. These were distributed among friends, including Nirvana's tour manager Cristobus Selch and various record company confidants.

## songs
**Orbitting Persons in Space (1990)** : A track from the Harling Talks AD solo project, recorded in the summer of 1990 with assistance from Barrett Jones and Toss Newyn Huizen. The project was an experimental endeavor not intended for wide release and featured unusual song titles like "Recycle children never to be grown" and "Treason, Daddy, Brother, and Crime."
**Color Pictures of a Mera Gold (1992)** : The original version of this song appeared on Dave Grohl's "Pocket Watch" solo album, released under the alias "Late." This track later evolved into "Mera Gold" on the CD single version of Nirvana's "Heart Shaped Box."
**Friend of a Friend (1992)** : A song from the "Pocket Watch" solo album (as Late), written in Olympia, Washington, while Dave Grohl was sitting on Kurt Cobain's couch. It details the friendship between Kurt Cobain and Chris Novoselic. A re-recorded version would later appear on the acoustic portion of the Foo Fighters' "In Your Honor" album in 2005.
**Mera Gold (1992)** : A new version of "Color Pictures of a Mera Gold," recorded during the "In Utero" album sessions in February 1992. Dave Grohl sang and played drums and guitar on this track, with Chris Novoselic later adding the bass line. Though it did not make the "In Utero" album, it appeared as the last song on the CD single of Nirvana's "Heart Shaped Box" and was later included in the "With The Lights Out" box set. It is the only song in the entire Nirvana canon not written or sung by Kurt Cobain and has been performed live by Foo Fighters, appearing on their "Skin and Bones" album in 2006.
**Alone and Easy Target (1994)** : A demo version of this song was recorded in October 1994 as part of Dave Grohl's pre-Foo Fighters sessions. He played all instruments, completing the track in approximately 40 minutes.

## curiosities
**Early Demographics and Recording (1994)** : In October 1994, Dave Grohl recorded 16 songs at a small studio in Seattle with Barrett Jones, playing all instruments. These sessions were not intended for release, but the material was later bootlegged and released as an unofficial CD in Japan in 1995, shortly after the first official Foo Fighters album hit stores.
**Quitting School for Scream (Age 17)** : Dave Grohl quit school at age 17 to join the DC-based punk band Scream.
**Harling Talks AD Solo Project (1990)** : In the summer of 1990, Dave Grohl recorded new demos under the alias Harling Talks Angel Divine (or Harling Talks AD) with assistance from Barrett Jones and Toss Newyn Huizen. These experimental recordings were not expected to lead to anything, and some featured unusual titles like "Recycle children never to be grown" and "Treason, Daddy, Brother, and Crime," with the project described as sounding more like Megadeth than Foo Fighters.
**Nirvana Audition and Joining (September 21, 1990)** : After Scream broke up, Buzz Osborne of The Melvins recommended Dave Grohl for the open drummer slot in Nirvana. Dave flew to Seattle on September 21, 1990, and after an informal audition, simply became a member of the band without a formal "you're in the group" announcement.
**Legal Dispute with Scream (Post-Nirvana Signing)** : After joining Nirvana and signing a major label deal with DGC Records, Dave Grohl was sued for $40,000 by an entity associated with Scream. The claim alleged he was still under contract to Scream, despite the band having long broken up, and the issue was subsequently handled by his lawyers.
**"Pocket Watch" Cassette Collectibility (1992)** : Dave Grohl's solo album "Pocket Watch," released as "Late" in 1992 on Simple Machines, is a highly collectible cassette, with copies in good condition trading for $700 or more.
**Suppression of "Pocket Watch" (1992)** : Dave Grohl insisted that his solo album "Pocket Watch" did not receive much attention because, as Nirvana began to gain massive fame, he did not want to be seen as "crashing the party" with his own material. Simple Machines records had even requested to release the album on CD.
**Limited Songwriting in Nirvana (Reasons)** : Dave Grohl contributed minimally to Nirvana's songwriting, primarily because Nirvana was Kurt Cobain's band, with Cobain serving as the leader, singer, and songwriter. Additionally, Grohl lacked confidence in his own singing voice and songwriting abilities, often recording his material in secret and considering it "substandard."
**Post-Nirvana Depression and Contemplation (April 1994)** : Following Kurt Cobain's death in April 1994, Dave Grohl entered a deep depression. He contemplated giving up music entirely or joining another band, having received invitations from Danzig and Tom Petty. He resisted the idea of becoming "just a drummer" because it would constantly remind him of his time in Nirvana.
**First Post-Nirvana Performance (1994 MTV Movie Awards)** : Dave Grohl's first performance after the dissolution of Nirvana was with the fictional Backbeat Band at the 1994 MTV Movie Awards.
**Motivation to Continue Music (Seven Year Bitch Postcard)** : Dave Grohl's decision to continue making music after Kurt Cobain's suicide was significantly influenced by a postcard he received from the Seattle band Seven Year Bitch. Despite enduring profound tragedies (a member's overdose death in 1992, and another's kidnapping, rape, and murder in 1993), the band stuck together and continued their musical careers. The postcard conveyed a message of hope, stating, "We know what you're going through. The desire to play music is gone for now, but it will return. Don't worry."
**Pre-Foo Fighters Demos Purpose (1994)** : The sole purpose of Dave Grohl's intensive six-day recording sessions in October 1994, where he played all instruments, was to prove to himself that he could still create music after the trauma of Nirvana's end, rather than to secure a record contract.
**Retaining Ownership and Roswell Records (1995)** : Following the immense popularity and bootlegging of his pre-Foo Fighters demos, Dave Grohl was inundated with record offers. Determined to retain ownership of his music, he established his own record company, Roswell Records, named after the alleged UFO crash site in New Mexico, to protect his interests.
**Origin of Foo Fighters Name (WWII UFO Terminology)** : Dave Grohl chose the name "Foo Fighters" based on a term used by World War II pilots to describe mysterious balls of fire they encountered over Allied lines ("Feu" is French for fire). Fascinated by UFOs since childhood, he read about "Foo Fighters" as a slang term for UFOs in the book "Above Top Secret." He selected the name to create anonymity for his solo project, wanting listeners to perceive it as a band, similar to groups like The Ramones, The Pixies, or The Bangles.
**Dave Grohl's Dislike for Foo Fighters Name** : Despite its origin, Dave Grohl considers the name "Foo Fighters" to be "dumb" but acknowledged he is "stuck with it" due to the project's unexpected success. The name also led to numerous UFO-related questions and some mistakenly associated him with the Heaven's Gate cult.

## songs
**Love Buzz (1988) – First Ever Single** : This song was performed at Dave Grohl's first official gig with Nirvana on October 11, 1990, at the North Shore Surf Club in Olympia, Washington.
**While Pissing (1991)** : A song from the "Nevermind" album, where Kurt Cobain shares songwriting credit with Chad Powers of Quicksilver Messenger Service. This is due to the inclusion of yelping at the beginning of the song, which consists of lyrics from "Get Together" by The Youngbloods, a song written by Powers.
**Get Together (1967)** : Originally performed by The Youngbloods and written by Chad Powers. Lyrics from this song were incorporated into Nirvana's "While Pissing."
**Heart Shaped Box (1993) – CD Single** : The CD single version of this song included "Mera Gold" as its last track.

## curiosities
**Dave Grohl's First Gig (October 11, 1990)** : Dave Grohl's inaugural official performance with Nirvana took place at the North Shore Surf Club in Olympia, Washington. The 300-person venue sold out, Dave broke his snare drum due to intense playing, and the band caused the venue's power to blow out three times.
**Major Label Signing Lawsuit (1991)** : Upon signing their major label deal with DGC Records, Dave Grohl faced a lawsuit for $40,000 from an entity associated with his former band Scream, alleging breach of contract.
**Nevermind Royalty Dispute (Spring 1992)** : After "Nevermind" achieved massive global success, Kurt Cobain demanded a renegotiation of royalty arrangements in the spring of 1992. He initially sought 90% of songwriting royalties, backdated to the album's September 1991 release, controversially calling Dave Grohl and Chris Novoselic "greedy." After significant contention, Kurt was eventually bargained down to 75%.
**Limited Songwriting Credits (Nevermind)** : Kurt Cobain shares songwriting credit on only two tracks on the "Nevermind" album.
**"Mera Gold" – Only Non-Cobain Song** : "Mera Gold," recorded by Dave Grohl during the "In Utero" sessions, is the only song in the entire Nirvana canon that was neither written nor sung by Kurt Cobain. It did not make the album but was released as the last track on the "Heart Shaped Box" CD single and later on the "With The Lights Out" box set.

# label - Simple Machines

## curiosities
**Cassette-Only Label Experiment (1992)** : This label, run out of a house in Arlington, Virginia, by members of the band Sudami, operated as an experiment to exclusively release material on cassette. Their strategy involved dubbing new cassettes (typically five at a time) as demand dictated, aiming to ensure their releases would never go out of print.
**Release of Dave Grohl's "Pocket Watch" (1992)** : Simple Machines released Dave Grohl's solo album "Pocket Watch" under his alias "Late" in 1992, on a cassette-only basis, and later unsuccessfully lobbied him to release it on CD.

# label - Roswell Records

## curiosities

It became the label for the Foo Fighters project.
