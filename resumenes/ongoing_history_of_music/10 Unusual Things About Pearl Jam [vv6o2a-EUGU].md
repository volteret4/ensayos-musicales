## albums
**Ten (1991) – Audition Tape Recorded Over Merle Haggard's Greatest Hits** : Stone Gossard gave a cassette of instrumental demos to his friend Jack Irons (who would later drum for both Pearl Jam and the Red Hot Chili Peppers). Irons passed it to a San Diego surfer and bartender who sang for a band called Bad Radio: Eddie Vedder. Eddie wrote and recorded a three-part mini-opera called "Mama-San" (comprising "Alive," "Once," and "Footsteps") over Stone's demo, using his old Tascam 4-track recorder — but the only blank tape he had was a copy of Merle Haggard's *Greatest Hits*. He taped his audition material over Merle's music and sent it to Seattle. That tape got him the gig. "The entire 12 and a half minutes of audio on this cassette was taped over a pre-recorded copy of Merle Haggard's greatest hits."

**Vs. (1993) – Recorded at The Site Studio; Rearviewmirror Sticks Were Thrown Against the Wall** : Recorded at an expensive studio called The Site in the San Francisco area at the insistence of Epic Records. Eddie was in his most intense anti-rockstar phase and suffered severe writer's block; he often drove the 30 miles to San Francisco to hang out with homeless people, returning occasionally to work on five or six songs at a time. During the recording of "Rearviewmirror" — the first Pearl Jam song Eddie ever wrote on guitar — drummer Dave Abbruzzese, under intense pressure to get his part right, threw his sticks against the wall at the song's end, punched through the head of his snare drum, marched outside, and threw the snare drum over a cliff. "The tension Eddie felt about this whole rockstar thing spilled over to the rest of the band."

**Vitalogy (1994) – Released on Vinyl Two Weeks Before CD; 34,000 Copies in First Week** : Pearl Jam released *Vitalogy* on vinyl two weeks before the CD edition, underscoring their love of old-school formats. Despite vinyl being near extinction in 1994, demand was so high that the vinyl edition sold 34,000 copies in its first week — a record for first-week vinyl sales until Jack White's *Lazaretto* sold 40,000 in one week in 2014. The opening song, "Spin the Black Circle," is an ode to vinyl itself. "Pearl Jam may have thought they were fighting against the future with the vinyl release of Vitalogy, but they were actually predicting it."

**Backspacer (2009) – Album Named After Their Sponsored Race Turtle** : In 2009, Pearl Jam sponsored a leatherback sea turtle named Backspacer in a Conservation International/National Geographic Society race from the Gulf of St. Lawrence to nesting grounds in the Caribbean. Competing turtles were sponsored by the Red Hot Chili Peppers (entry name unreported) and REM (entry: "Knight Swimmer"). Backspacer won, crossing 2,442 miles and being cheered over the finish line by an American Olympic swimmer. The album released the same year was named after the turtle, promoting the band's eco-friendly agenda. "Backspacer was the winner, cheered across the finish line by an American Olympic swimmer."

## songs
**Jeremy – Two Videos Exist; Inspiration Was a Real School Shooting (January 8, 1991)** : The first Jeremy video was financed personally by photographer Chris Cafaro (a friend of Eddie's), who took out a loan and sold half his guitar collection to fund it; Epic initially had no interest in Jeremy as a single. Filmed in a warehouse in Los Angeles on October 4, 1991, this version has a subtler, less graphic treatment. Epic later commissioned the well-known Mark Pellington video in June 1992, which won multiple awards but featured graphic blood spatters and a suicide scene eventually censored. The experience soured Pearl Jam on making videos; they didn't release another until 1998 (*Do the Evolution* from *Yield*). The song was inspired by Jeremy Dell, a student who shot himself with a .357 Magnum in front of his English class in Richardson, Texas, on January 8, 1991. In 1996, the shooter in the Moses Lake, Washington school shootings (three killed) cited the Pellington video as an influence. "Epic Pearl Jam's record label, was not keen on the idea. They didn't think Jeremy had any potential whatsoever as a single."

**Better Man – Written in High School, Almost Given to Greenpeace and to Chrissie Hynde** : Eddie wrote "Better Man" in high school; it was played by his pre–Pearl Jam San Diego band Bad Radio as early as 1989. Pearl Jam recorded it for *Vs.* but it was cut; neither Eddie nor the band believed in the song, and they were prepared to donate it to Greenpeace for a benefit compilation. Producer Brendan O'Brien insisted it was a hit. Eddie then offered it to Chrissie Hynde of the Pretenders (who failed to show up to the meeting). O'Brien insisted the band re-record it; they did so twice more and a composite was used on *Vitalogy*, where it became one of their biggest hits — precisely what the band had dreaded. "That would end up becoming a much bigger hit for Pearl Jam about five years later."

**Lukin – Written About Matt Lukin, Who Sheltered Eddie from Stalkers** : This song from the *No Code* album (1996) is about Matt Lukin, former bassist for Mudhoney and the Melvins, who gave Eddie shelter during a period when he was being pursued by a dangerous stalker. "Pearl Jam and a track called Lukin from 1996 inspired by Eddie Vedder's dangerously crazy stalker."

## curiosities
**Band Name Origin – From Neil Young Concert, Not Eddie's Grandmother's Jam** : The story about Eddie's fictional grandmother "Pearl" and her peyote jam is completely fabricated — acknowledged as a joke. The real story: the band needed to abandon their initial name "Mookie Blallock" (after the New Jersey Nets point guard) because his lawyers feared trademark confusion. While brainstorming a new name at a Seattle restaurant, everyone liked "Pearl." They went to New York for record-deal business, attended a Neil Young concert, were impressed by his long improvisational instrumental jams featuring competing creative visions, and settled on "Pearl Jam." Eddie later added a metaphorical layer: that competing creative irritants can come together into something great, like a pearl. "They found themselves in New York on business involving the brand-new major label record deal."

**Eddie Vedder Was a Child Model – Department Store Catalogs, TV Commercials, Bozo the Clown** : Growing up in Chicago, Eddie's mother entered him in photo shoots and TV commercial auditions. He appeared in at least five department store catalogs (including Bloomingdale's) modeling back-to-school clothing as a five-year-old, and appeared in TV commercials for Hallmark greeting cards and Chuckles candy. He was one of the kids driving a Big Wheel in a commercial that ran during Saturday morning cartoons. His biggest triumph was an appearance on the nationally broadcast *Bozo the Clown* show in the early 1970s. "He would rather not be known as the cute child model for Bloomingdale's."

**Eddie Vedder Stalker (1994–1996) – Believed Eddie Was Jesus and Father of Her Children** : A mentally ill woman became convinced that all of Eddie's Pearl Jam songs were written about her, that Jesus was the father of her two children via rape, and that Eddie was Jesus — therefore the father of her children. Physical threats were made; fences, security systems, and 24-hour bodyguards were deployed. She ultimately drove her car at 50 mph directly into Eddie's house, forcing him to move to an inaccessible location outside Seattle. Little came of legal proceedings. This explains Eddie's reputation as a recluse during the mid-1990s and is reflected in the song "Lukin." "Eddie ended up with this reputation as a recluse and a hermit, but now you know why."

**Eddie Vedder, Chris Cornell, and Al Jourgensen – Bile Beer Drinking Contest at Lollapalooza 1992** : The 1992 Lollapalooza tour featured the Jim Rose Circus sideshow, including performer Matt "the Tube" Crowley, who would pump beer, ketchup, chocolate, and Pepto-Bismol into his stomach via a seven-foot swallowed tube and then pump it back out as "bile beer" for audience members to drink. Eddie Vedder, Chris Cornell (Soundgarden), and Al Jourgensen (Ministry) entered a competition for who could drink the most bile beer. Cornell tried and retched; Jourgensen managed it two days in a row; Eddie was there every single night and claimed to have drunk two quarts more than anyone. When asked why, Eddie said: "Just looking for attention." "Eddie won this contest. He called it Wayne."

**Singles Cameo (1992) – Eddie, Stone, and Jeff as Citizen Dick Members** : In Cameron Crowe's Seattle-set film *Singles* (filmed 1991, released 1992), Eddie Vedder, Stone Gossard, and Jeff Ament appear as the backing band for fictional singer Cliff Poncier (played by Matt Dillon), in a band called Citizen Dick. Their longest appearance is just over a minute, showing them reading a review of their record. By the time the film was released, Pearl Jam was enormous, which worked greatly in the film's favor. Pearl Jam also contributed two songs to the soundtrack: "Breath" and "State of Love and Trust." "By the time the movie was released in 1992, the band was huge."

**Backspacer Sea Turtle Sponsorship (2009) – Won Race from Canada to Caribbean** : Pearl Jam sponsored leatherback turtle "Backspacer" in a Conservation International/National Geographic race across 2,442 miles of ocean. It beat turtles sponsored by the Red Hot Chili Peppers and REM. The sponsorship raised awareness for endangered leatherback turtles and helped publicize their same-named 2009 album. "Backspacer was the winner, cheered across the finished line by an American Olympic swimmer."
