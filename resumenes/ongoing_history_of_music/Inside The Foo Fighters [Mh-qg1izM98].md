## albums
**Sonic Highways (2014)** : An album where eight songs were recorded in eight different cities in eight different studios, documenting the influences and processes for an HBO TV series.
**Sonic Highways (2014) – Something From Nothing** : The first single from the album, recorded at Electrical Audio in Chicago. Features guest guitarist Rick Nielsen of Cheap Trick.
**Sonic Highways (2014) – Outside** : A track made in the desert at Joshua Tree, California. Features guest guitar contributions from Joe Walsh of The Eagles.
**Sonic Highways (2014) – Congregation** : A track made at Southern Ground Studios in Nashville, featuring a discernable "Nashville" sound.
**Sonic Highways (2014) – Feast or Famine** : A track recorded at Inner Ear Studios in Arlington, Virginia, featuring Bad Brains as guest musicians.
**Sonic Highways (2014) – Subterranean** : A track recorded at Robert Lang Studios north of Seattle, featuring Ben Gibbard of Death Cab for Cutie as a guest musician.
**First Foo Fighters record** : Made at Robert Lang Studios north of Seattle.

## curiosities
**Sonic Highways Recording Process (2014)** : For the album, the band set up in a new city for each of the eight songs, absorbing local vibes and talking with musicians. Dave Grohl then cut and pasted words and phrases from these conversations into the lyrics for each city's song.
**HBO TV series "Sonic Highways" (2014)** : A TV series that ran over eight weeks, documenting the entire recording process of the "Sonic Highways" album.
**Live Performance Joy** : Known for the immense joy and fun displayed by all five members on stage at every gig.
**Rush Influence on Dave Grohl's Guitar** : Dave Grohl admits to "ripping off" Alex Lifeson's guitar playing from Rush in many Foo Fighters songs, specifically sustained notes and riff patterns (e.g., in "Winnebago" and "Times Like These").
**Rush Influence on Taylor Hawkins' Drums** : Taylor Hawkins admits to "ripping off" Neil Peart's drum fills from Rush records, especially "crazy fills" and open parts that become a big part of the song.
**"Sound City" Documentary (2013)** : Dave Grohl's previous filmmaking project which he describes as having "fell into my lap," assembling a team of like-minded people. Its success inspired the "Sonic Highways" concept.
**Dave Grohl's Filmmaking Journey** : Following "Sound City" and "Sonic Highways," Dave Grohl has developed a reputation as a filmmaker, though he considers rock and roll his "day job" and prefers not to quit it due to enjoying it and being well-paid.

# genre - Punk Rock

## curiosities
**Simplicity and Accessibility** : Dave Grohl describes punk rock as being like folk music in its simplicity (often three chords), making it accessible for aspiring musicians who don't feel they can emulate virtuosos like Jimmy Page or Peter Frampton.
**Focus on Local Issues** : Punk rock acts like folk music by having kids write about things important to them, such as neighborhood or home life, rather than abstract or distant topics.
**Pat Smear's Entry into Music** : Pat Smear started his musical journey at the same time punk rock was emerging, finding it an open scene for bands like The Germs who initially "didn't know how to play."
**Dave Grohl's Life-Changing Experience** : Seeing Naked Raygun perform live in a small, dingy club (The Cubby Bear in Chicago) at age 13 completely changed Dave Grohl's life and inspired him to pursue this style of music.

# genre - Hardcore

## curiosities
