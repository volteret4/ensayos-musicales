## curiosities
**First Live Gig – St. Martins (November 6, 1975)** : The band's debut performance took place at an art school in London, St. Martins, as an unexpected opening act for Bizzouca Joe. They reportedly lasted only about two songs before the school's social programmer halted their performance by pulling the plug. This event, despite its unceremonious ending, was considered a pivotal moment that changed music. "music was never to be quite the same."
**TV Show Scandal (December 1, 1976)** : The Sex Pistols appeared on a popular television program where they used "bad words," leading to a significant scandal across Britain. This incident was widely discussed and served as a catalyst for future musicians, inspiring Declan McManus (later Elvis Costello) to pursue a career in music. "All of Britain was talking about it. Terrible, terrible. These young men on TV."
**Manchester Gig Inspiration (Spring 1976)** : A performance by the Sex Pistols in Manchester, though described as "terrible" by attendees Bernie and Peter, was nonetheless found to be "great" in its raw energy. This concert directly inspired Bernie and Peter to form their own band, which would eventually become Joy Division. "He says, they were terrible. I thought they were great. I wanted to get up there and be terrible too."
**Opening Act for The 101ers – Nashville Club** : The band opened for The 101ers at a club called Nashville. Despite their "awful" musicianship, their attitude and energy profoundly impressed Joe, the guitarist for The 101ers, motivating him to leave his band and form The Clash. "It was their attitude and their energy that grabbed his attention."
**Early Fan Base** : Siouxsie Sioux was noted as one of the Sex Pistols' first fans and was present in the audience during their scandalous TV appearance where they swore at the host. "Susie Su is another person with connections to the sex pistols. She was one of their first fans."

# artist - R.E.M.

## curiosities
**Band Name Origin** : The name "R.E.M." was chosen from a dictionary around three o'clock in the morning, the night before the band's first show, because they didn't have a name and thought "nobody else in the world would ever use it." While in the dictionary it means "rapid eye movement," the band simply grabbed it because it was short. "We had our first show the next night and we still didn't have a name and we couldn't think of one so we picked that one up because we figured nobody else in the world would ever use it."
**Live Performance Status** : In their early days, R.E.M. frequently played clubs, a stark contrast to their later career where they ceased playing live concerts altogether. "That's a very young REM from the days when they were still playing clubs, which is a whole lot better than these days when they don't play live at all."

# artist - Depeche Mode

## genres
Synth-pop (implied)

## instruments
Synthesizer
Guitar

## curiosities
**Shift from Guitar to Synthesizer** : Depeche Mode transitioned from being a guitar band primarily due to the perceived limited sound of guitars and the greater interest they found in the diverse sounds of synthesizers. A practical reason was the ease of transport for synthesizers, which could be carried "under our arms on a train" and plugged directly into a PA system, circumventing the logistical problems of moving amplifiers for guitar-based setups in their early days. "We found synthesizers very interesting, just the sounds that you could get on them."

## curiosities
**Inspiration from Sex Pistols (December 2, 1976)** : Declan McManus, then a computer operator, was deeply inspired by the Sex Pistols' scandalous appearance on a TV show where they used "bad words." Feeling they had "shaken up the system," he decided to quit his data entry job, picked up a guitar, and changed his name to Elvis Costello, embodying the attitude, anger, and energy he saw in the Sex Pistols. "He wanted to be involved in whatever it was that the sex pistols embodied, the attitude, the anger, the energy."

# genre - Punk

## curiosities
**Foundational Influence** : Punk music, exemplified by bands like the Ramones, Sex Pistols, and Blondie, marked the beginning of a significant shift in the music scene, leading to the development of numerous subsequent genres and artists. "It started with bands like the Ramones and the Sex pistols and Blondie."
**Domino Effect on New Music** : The punk scene created a "domino effect" that profoundly shaped the new music prevalent today, inspiring musicians like Elvis Costello, the founders of Joy Division, and The Clash to create their own music. "Here's one more story about how the punk scene set up a domino effect that helps explain the new music that we have today."
