# artist - Sex Pistols

## curiosities
**First Live Gig (November 6, 1975)** : The band's inaugural live performance took place in London at the St. Martins art school, in a small upstairs room. They were the opening act for Bizzouca Joe, featuring singer Adam Ant. The school's social programmer pulled the plug after only two songs, but despite this initial reception, their appearance was pivotal for music history.
**TV Show Scandal (Late 1976)** : Prior to December 2, 1976, the band appeared on a popular TV show where they used "bad words," causing a national scandal. All of Britain was discussing it. This event greatly inspired Declan McManus, who later became Elvis Costello, and Siouxsie Sioux was noted as being in the audience.
**Inspiration for Joe Strummer** : When the Sex Pistols opened for The 101ers at a gig at the Nashville club, their "attitude and energy" profoundly impressed Joe Strummer, the guitar player for The 101ers, despite their musicianship being described as "awful." This led Joe Strummer to leave his band and form The Clash.
**Inspiration for Joy Division** : In the spring of 1976, Bernie and his friend Peter saw the Sex Pistols play in Manchester. Though Bernie initially found their performance "terrible," he also thought it was "great" and was inspired to "get up there and be terrible too," leading them to form their first group, Warsaw, which later became Joy Division.

# artist - Depeche Mode

## instruments
Guitar
Synthesizer
Amplifier

## curiosities
**Transition to Synth Band** : Depeche Mode switched from being a guitar band primarily for two reasons. Firstly, they felt the sound of guitars was "very limited" and found synthesizers "very interesting" due to the sounds they could produce. Secondly, synthesizers offered significantly easier transport in the early days, as they could be carried "under our arms on a train" and plugged directly into a PA system, unlike the logistical problems associated with transporting guitars and amplifiers.
