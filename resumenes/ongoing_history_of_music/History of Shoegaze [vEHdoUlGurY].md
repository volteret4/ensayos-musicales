"Meanwhile, the band created all sorts of controversy by getting arrested for drugs and playing short 20-minute concerts that often devolved into violence."

## albums
**Isn't Anything (1988) - Shoegaze Precursor** : Released in 1988, this record is considered to have arrived "at the very beginning of the whole shoegaze thing." It was characterized by "guitar drones and layers and layers of feedback" that generated a "near-trance-like soundscape," an "impenetrable atmosphere of sound," and made extensive use of effects pedals. "In 1988, they released a record called, Isn't Anything, which was filled with guitar drones and layers and layers of feedback that created near-translikes soundscape."
**Loveless (1991) - Influential Masterpiece** : Released in 1991, *Loveless* is considered the band's "biggest contribution" to music. It was recorded over two years in 19 different studios, a process so extensive and costly that it "nearly bankrupted their record label." Despite not selling in "appreciable numbers," its influence significantly "far outweighed its commercial performance," deeply impacting artists like Trent Reznor, Billy Corgan, Robert Smith, Courtney Love, and The Edge of U2. "It was an album called Loveless that was recorded over two years in 19 different studios. And it was so expensive that it nearly bankrupted their record label."

## curiosities
**Pioneering Noise Soundscape** : My Bloody Valentine from Dublin were central to developing the shoegaze sound, focusing on "layers of noise, psychotic amounts of noise." They created an "impenetrable atmosphere of sound" with guitars, often making vocals difficult to discern. Their sound featured "weird harmonics" and "near-trance-like soundscapes" from drones and feedback, achieved through extensive use of effects pedals. "Rarely had so many people use so many effects pedals in the guitars to create such an impenetrable atmosphere of sound."
**Influence on Contemporary Artists** : The album *Loveless* had a profound and widespread impact, blowing away musicians such as Trent Reznor, Billy Corgan, Robert Smith, Courtney Love, and The Edge of U2. Its influence far exceeded its commercial sales, cementing its status as a seminal work in alternative music and being frequently cited by contemporary bands as an inspiration. "Everyone from Trent Resner to Billy Corgan to Robert Smith to Courtney Love to the edge of YouTube were blown away by what they heard on that album."

# genre - Shoegaze

## genres
Alt Rock (branch of)
Post-Punk (roots)

## curiosities
**Origins and Spread** : Shoegaze emerged in Scotland in the 1980s, then expanded southward to the Thames Valley, eastward to Ireland, and by the 1990s, it had reached audiences worldwide. It is characterized as a distinct branch of the Alt Rock family tree, rooted in the experimental tendencies of post-punk. "It's called Shoegaze, a sound that began in Scotland in the 1980s, migrated south to the Thames Valley over to Ireland and by the 1990s around the world."
**Commercial Performance vs. Impact** : Despite the fact that "no one involved sold a lot of records or content tickets," the genre exerted a profound influence. Its music is still referenced today by many who consider it formative, indicating that its impact "is still being felt today" and "far outweighed its commercial performance." "No one involved sold a lot of records or content tickets, but the impact this music had is still being felt today."
**Etymology of the Term** : The term "shoegaze" originated from the observed stage behavior of these new bands. Unlike mainstream frontmen who performed frenetically, shoegaze musicians often looked down at their feet while playing. This was partly due to a desire for the "music to do all the talking" and, more practically, because they had to "keep their eyes on the foot pedals they were using" for their heavily treated guitar parts. "To the audience they appeared to be looking at their shoes all the time, hence the label shoegasing."
**Core Aesthetic and Sensory Experience** : Shoegaze music is designed to deliver an "intense sensory experience" that focuses attention inward. It is characterized by loud, fuzzy sounds that "hit you hard in the chest and savaged your ears," creating an immersive atmosphere that promotes introspection rather than external showmanship. "The music hit you hard in the chest and savaged your ears, creating a very intense sensory experience that focused attention inward."
**Peak Era and Decline** : The "original shoegaze era" was relatively short-lived, primarily spanning "late 1989 through to late 1992," a "narrow gap between Madchester and Britpop." During much of this period, it was overshadowed by established Manchester bands. It fell out of favor when Britpop emerged, as the British music press diverted its attention first to American Grunge and then to Britpop, effectively leaving shoegaze "to rot." "The best years for the original shoegase era were the early 1990s, that narrow gap between Madchester and Britpop."
**Introspection and Community ("The Scene That Celebrates Itself")** : In contrast to outwardly performative music, shoegaze is fundamentally about "introspection and sensory immersion." Its practitioners preferred being "the underdogs," a sentiment that led some adherents to call shoegaze "the scene that celebrates itself," suggesting a self-contained community unconcerned with external validation from the British music press. "Now, shoegase was, and he is more about introspection and sensory immersion. Its practitioners liked being the underdogs."
**Female Vocal Presence** : A notable and often "overlooked aspect of shoegaze" was its significant "sexual equality" within the scene, with approximately 50% of the featured songs utilizing female vocals. Many bands included at least one female member playing a major role in vocals, creating an "interesting balance" between the "male aggression, manifested in the noise of guitars," and "soft female vocals." "Most bands had at least one female who played a large role in the vocals, and the result was an interesting balance."
**Enduring Legacy and Contemporary Influence** : Although the original shoegaze era was brief, its influence persists strongly in contemporary music. It can be heard in the production work of Alan Moulder with bands like The Smashing Pumpkins and Nine Inch Nails, and directly in the sound of groups like Silver Sun Pickups, A Place to Bury Strangers, Die of Crocodiles, Toy, Yuck, Auto-Locks, The Black Angels, Sigur Rós, M83, Mogwai, Ulrich Schnauss, The Pains of Being Pure at Heart, and Hookworms, many of whom "name check my bloody Valentine as an influence." "The shoogas legacy can be felt all over the place."

# instrument - Guitar effects pedals

## curiosities
**Central to Shoegaze Sound** : Guitar effects pedals were a crucial element in creating the characteristic shoegaze sound, enabling guitarists like Robin Guthrie of the Cocteau Twins to explore new sounds and My Bloody Valentine to create "layers and layers of feedback" and "near-trance-like soundscapes." The extensive use of these pedals meant musicians had to "keep their eyes on the foot pedals they were using," contributing to the "shoegazing" stage aesthetic. "Rarely had so many people use so many effects pedals in the guitars to create such an impenetrable atmosphere of sound."

# curiosity

**Alan Moulder - Shoegaze Producer and Influencer** : Alan Moulder was a renowned producer who "loved the whole shoegaze sound" and played a pivotal role in shaping many bands of the genre, starting with Ride. His expertise involved helping to "tame these walls of noise" while simultaneously "uncover[ing] the material's inherent pop sensibilities." Moulder later applied his knowledge of "how to ring melody out of noise and thicker arrangements" to a diverse range of projects outside shoegaze, including Nine Inch Nails' *The Downward Spiral*, The Smashing Pumpkins, The Killers, A Perfect Circle, Death Cab for Cutie, and the Foo Fighters. He met and later married Toni Halliday while working with her band Curve. "He was a producer who loved the whole shoegase sound. And starting with Ride, he dug in, helping tame these walls of noise these bands were creating, and uncover the material's inherent pop sensibilities."
**British Music Press Influence and Betrayal of Shoegaze** : The British music press, which held "hugely influential" power during the shoegaze era, initially supported the scene but later "turned on shoegaze bands." They redirected their attention and coverage first to American Grunge and subsequently to Britpop, effectively leaving shoegaze "to rot." This shift significantly contributed to the genre falling out of mainstream favor. "After initially being on board with the whole shoogas scene, the British music press, which was hugely influential back then, turned on shoogas bands, diverting their attention first to American Grunge, and then to Britpop."
**Shoegaze Revival Status** : Despite falling out of favor, shoegaze "never really went away," instead folding "in on itself and occasionally threatened to make a comeback." While a "comeback has never really been properly manifested," the genre's legacy endures, influencing numerous contemporary artists across various genres. "That comeback has never really been properly manifested, but the shoogas legacy can be felt all over the place."
