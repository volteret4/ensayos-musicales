## albums
**Hybrid Theory (2000)** : Released on October 24, 2000, this debut album sold over 18 million copies in the US and 1.3 million in Canada, with global sales estimated between 35 and 38 million records, making it the best-selling debut rock record of the new century. It was completed in just four weeks and, despite being critically dismissed as "more of the same old new metal," it was embraced by fans for its blend of rapping, scratching, screaming, big riffs, beats, and a catchy pop sensibility. "Since their first album was released on October 24, 2000, Lincoln Park is sold over 18 million albums in the US and another 1.3 million in Canada."
**Reanimation (2002)** : Released in 2002, this remix album featured reinterpretations of songs from *Hybrid Theory*. It served to capitalize on the success of the debut and provided the band with crucial time before recording their second studio album, *Meteora*. "Lincoln Park, Hossalotto Remix, album entitled Reanimation."
**Meteora (2003)** : Released on March 25, 2003, this second studio album was titled after monasteries in a region of Greece. It was a challenging follow-up, with producer Don Gilmore pushing the band, including making them work on 40 different choruses for its first single. Within a week, it sold over 810,000 copies in the US alone, reaching nearly 6 million in America, 500,000 in Canada, and 250,000 in Australia. Worldwide, sales totaled around 13 million copies, even as the recording industry faced a collapse in CD sales due to file sharing. "Meteora, the second Lincoln Park album was released on March 25th of 2003, and within a week it sold more than 810,000 copies in the US alone."
**Live in Texas (2003)** : This concert album was released in 2003. It was designed to buy the band some time amidst intense recording sessions, remixes, DVDs, and world tours, providing fans with new material while the band managed their demanding schedule. "Lincoln Park from their 2003 concert album Live in Texas."
**Collision Course (2004)** : This collaboration CD with rapper Jay-Z was released in 2004. It was one of the many creative ventures pursued by band members during the group's extended hiatus between *Meteora* and *Minutes to Midnight*. "And then there was that collision course collaboration CD with Jay-Z."
**Minutes to Midnight (2007)** : Released on May 15, 2007, this third studio album arrived more than four years after *Meteora* due to a contentious dispute with Warner Brothers Records. Super producer Rick Rubin was brought on board, and the album's creation involved 14 months of work spread over three years. It achieved the biggest opening week of any album in 2007, selling 625,000 copies in its first seven days and 3.3 million within the first month in the US. By year-end, it sold 4.3 million records, becoming the 7th best-selling record of 2007, with worldwide sales reaching about 6 million. "Studio album number three minutes to midnight did not come out until May 15th, 2007."

## curiosities
**Best-Selling American Rock Band of the 21st Century** : As of 2008, Linkin Park was recognized as the best-selling American rock band of the 21st century, having sold 35-38 million records worldwide since their debut album in 2000. Nickelback was noted as the only other band to approach these sales figures in a comparable timeframe. "This is the story of the best-selling American Rock Band of the 21st Century."
**Emergence from New Metal** : Linkin Park was one of the few groups to emerge from the late 1990s' "new metal" scene bigger and stronger, distinguishing themselves with a pop sensibility that made their angry and powerful music highly catchy. "Lincoln Park is pretty much the only group to emerge from the whole new metal thing of the late 90s bigger and stronger."
**Band Origin in Agoura Hills** : The core of Linkin Park began with three high school friends—Mike Shinoda, Brad Delson, and Rob Bourdon—who attended Agoura Hills High School, located approximately 30 miles northwest of downtown Los Angeles. "Everything with his band starts at a high school in Agora Hills, which is about 30 miles northwest of downtown Los Angeles."
**Name Change from Hybrid Theory** : The band changed its name to "Linkin Park" from "Hybrid Theory" after being inspired by an actual park of the same name in Santa Monica that they drove by daily. They believed it to be a universally recognizable name, as many American towns reportedly have a Lincoln Park. "Hybrid Theory became the deliberately misspelled Lincoln Park."
**Record Label Dispute with Warner Brothers (2005)** : Between 2003 and 2007, Linkin Park was embroiled in a public and contentious dispute with Warner Brothers Records, which significantly delayed their third album. The band alleged that only a small portion of the money from Warner's stock offering would benefit artists, while the label accused the band of "extortion" for demanding a $60 million advance. Linkin Park sued Warner to exit their seven-album contract, but the lawsuit was dropped in December 2005 after negotiations, with both sides agreeing to keep the specifics confidential. "A rift had opened between the band and their management and Warner Brothers records."
**Extended Hiatus (2004-2007)** : Following intense touring and recording through 2004, Linkin Park took an almost four-year break from releasing proper studio albums. During this hiatus, individual band members pursued various side projects and collaborations to maintain their creative output. "by the end of 2004 Lincoln Park needed a break. So they disappeared for almost four solid years."
**Decreased Rap in Minutes to Midnight** : For their *Minutes to Midnight* album, the band intentionally reduced the amount of rap, with Mike Shinoda rapping on only two of the 12 songs. This decision was made because the "new metal" genre was perceived as "dead," and the band was uncertain about fan acceptance of rap in the evolving music landscape. "The new metal was dead so the amount of wrapping that fans might find acceptable was uncertain. So taking the safe route, they toned it down."
**Charity Work** : Linkin Park actively engaged in various charity efforts, including supporting victims of the Asian tsunami and Hurricane Katrina, and participating in the Live 8 event. "Not to mention some charity work with everything from Asian tsunami and hurricane Katrina victims to participating in the Live 8 event."

## curiosities
**Early Life Struggles** : Chester Bennington came from a broken home and struggled with substance abuse early in his life, reportedly spending money earned at Burger King on "Coke and Crystal meth." His primary motivation during this difficult period was his dream of becoming a rock star. "He was from a broken home. He spent the money he made at Burger King on Coke and Crystal meth."
**Musical Influences** : Bennington cited Scott Weiland of Stone Temple Pilots and Dave Gahan of Depeche Mode as his musical heroes, though the transcript notes these were "not exactly guys whose lifestyle should be emulated." "Although his heroes were Scott Wyland of the Stone Temple Pilots and Dave Gahn of Depeche Mode, not exactly guys whose lifestyle should be emulated."
**Audition for Zero (1999)** : In March 1999, Chester was contacted by Jeff Blue to audition for Zero. He received two demo tapes—one with Mark Wakefield's original vocals and one instrumental—with the instruction to "Do your thing!" He wrote new lyrics, recorded his vocals, and sent them back, successfully passing the audition. "Chester wrote some new lyrics, recorded his vocals, and sent everything back. And that seal that he passed the audition."
**First Marriage and Divorce** : Chester Bennington first married Samantha in 1996 at the age of 20. They had a son together before divorcing in 2005. "Mr. Bennington was married for the first time at age 20. This was back in 1996."
**Second Marriage and Family** : Following his divorce from Samantha, Chester married former Playboy model Talinda Bentley. They had a son named Tyler and resided in Newport Beach, California. "he since married a former playboy model named Talinda Bentley. They have a son named Tyler."
**Identity Theft Incident** : Chester and his wife Talinda experienced identity theft when a superfan from New Mexico managed to hack into Talinda's cell phone to steal their identities. "They live in Newport Beach, California, where they're still reeling from the time a superfan and New Mexico managed to steal their identities by hacking into Talinda's cell phone."
**Death (2017)** : Chester Bennington died by suicide via hanging before 9 a.m. Pacific time on Thursday, July 20, 2017. This date also marked Chris Cornell's 53rd birthday, with whom Bennington shared a very close bond. Cornell's death was "devastating" to Bennington, as both men battled drugs, alcohol, depression, and the pressures of being in a superstar band, ultimately described as "kindred spirits." "Chester Bennington died by his own hand, a hanging. One time before 9 a.m. Pacific time on Thursday, July 20th."

# label - Warner Brothers Records

**Initial Rejection of Linkin Park** : Warner Brothers Records initially turned down Linkin Park (then known as Hybrid Theory) "many, many times" before eventually signing them, indicating their initial skepticism despite later success. "Warner Brothers was under new management and was trying to cut both costs and raise money."
**Dispute with Linkin Park (2005)** : Warner Brothers was involved in a public and contentious dispute with Linkin Park in 2005, occurring during a period of financial instability for the label. The label accused the band's management of "extortion" and "making baseless charges" in their negotiation tactics, while Linkin Park sought a $60 million advance. "executives were brawling with Lincoln Park, one of the label's biggest assets, and one of the label's biggest sources of revenue."
