## curiosities
**Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)** : Joey Ramone suffered from a severe case of OCD, which manifested in ritualistic behaviors like repeatedly checking his apartment door or stepping on and off a curb a specific number of times before moving on. Doctors, lacking modern understanding of the condition, once told him he would never be able to care for himself, and he was hospitalized at least once as a result. "It was something that was always there and he always had to struggle with. These voices in his head would say, you didn't close the door right, you got to do it again, and he would do it 20 times until it was right."

**OCD's Role in His Death** : In December 2000, Joey was in remission from lymphoma. On an icy cold December 30th, 2000, he was overcome by an OCD compulsion to re-check a door across town. While leaving his apartment to fulfill this urge, he slipped on ice and broke his hip badly, an injury exacerbated by bones weakened and brittle from chemotherapy. This required hospitalization and removal from his anti-lymphoma medication, leading to the return of the lymphoma and overwhelming his immune system, ultimately causing his death on April 15, 2001. "But then one icy cold day after Christmas 2000, it was December 30th, 2000 to be exact. He was overcome by this feeling that he hadn't closed a door properly on the other side of town."

**Marfan Syndrome Diagnosis** : Joey also had Marfan Syndrome, a genetic disorder affecting connective tissue. This condition often results in individuals being very tall and thin with long limbs, fingers, and toes, and can lead to scoliosis and certain heart and circulatory issues. The syndrome's characteristic height made his fall more dangerous, contributing to the severity of his injuries. "People with Marfan's tend to be very tall and very thin with long limbs, fingers and toes."
