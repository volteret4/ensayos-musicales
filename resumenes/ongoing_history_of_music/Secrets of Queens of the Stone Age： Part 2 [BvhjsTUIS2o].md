## albums
**Rated R** : The second Queens of the Stone Age record, which marked a shift towards robotic repetition of riffs in a staccato style, with the intention of pounding ideas into listeners' heads. It was created by Josh Homme and Nick Oliveri, featuring contributions from at least 15 guests, including Rob Halford from Judas Priest and Mark Lanegan from Screaming Trees. "we try to take a riff and pound it into people's heads by doing it over and over."
**Songs for the Death** : This album was recorded with Josh Homme, Nic Oliveri, and Mark Lanegan, and featured Dave Grohl as a full-time drummer who joined halfway through the sessions. The record incorporated interstitial bits with fake radio DJs to simulate listening to a car radio driving in the desert at night, where every station change would play a Queens of the Stone Age song. Despite the concept, the record label reportedly disliked the album from the demo stage. The title was inspired by the band's sound engineer, who played music at dance parties for deaf people, where attendees enjoyed feeling the bass by holding large balloons. "The audience loved it when he pumped up the bass, which they could really feel by holding big balloons."
**Lullabies to Paralyze** : The fourth studio album, primarily featuring Josh Homme, drummer Joey Castillo, and a returning Mark Lanegan. This album was notable for its extensive list of guest contributors including Shirley Manson of Garbage, Jesse Hughes from Eagles of Death Metal, Jack Black, Billy Gibbons of ZZ Top, and Brody Dalle, who had recently married Josh Homme. "Everything else was filled in by various guests and friends."
**Over the Years and Through the Woods (Live)** : A live album released to bridge the gap between studio albums, serving as a placeholder after `Lullabies to Paralyze` and before `Era Vulgaris` while Josh Homme dealt with the aftermath of Nick Oliveri's departure. "To buy time, there was a live album entitled Over the Years and Through the Woods."
**Era Vulgaris (2007)** : The fifth studio album, released on June 12, 2007, built around a core of Josh Homme, drummer Joey Castillo, and multi-instrumentalist Troy Van Leeuwen. It included numerous guest appearances such as Julian Casablancas of The Strokes, Mark Lanegan (contributing harmonies), and Brody Dalle Homme. Trent Reznor and Jesse Keeler of Death from Above were approached to participate but encountered scheduling conflicts. "The core of Queens was now Josh, drummer Joey Castillo, and multi-instrumentalist Troy Van Lovyn."
**...Like Clockwork (2013)** : Released in 2013, this album was profoundly influenced by Josh Homme's near-death experience in 2010. His recovery from a botched knee operation, cardiac arrest, and a severe MRSA infection led to a shift in his perspective on life, which was reflected in the album's creative direction. "And the album Queens was finally able to release was very much influenced by what happened."

The band performed this song live on Saturday Night Live in 2005, famously joined by guest host Will Ferrell contributing "more cowbell" approximately 90 seconds into the performance. ## curiosities
**Evolving Sound** : Queens of the Stone Age is known for taking chances and risks with each new record, actively avoiding repetition and embracing a "curve ball" approach to their sound. This commitment to change means fans never quite know what to expect, and the constantly shifting lineup contributes to this sonic evolution. "The last thing Josh Hommie and whatever his crew is in Queen of the Stone Age want to do is repeat themselves."
**Genre Rejection** : The band firmly rejects being labeled as a "Stone Rock band" or a "Grunge band." While acknowledging that the popularity of Grunge and the influence of late 90s/early 2000s new metal helped shape their career path, they maintain that they are not, nor have they ever been, a Grunge band. "please do not call Queen's a Stone Rock band."
**Songs for the Death - Secret Track** : The CD release of `Songs for the Death` contains a hidden track titled "Real Songs for the Death," which can be accessed by immediately pressing the skip-back button when the CD is inserted. "There's a secret track that can be found if you immediately press the skip button back when you put the CD in."
**Nick Oliveri's Firing (Early 2004)** : Bassist Nick Oliveri was fired by Josh Homme in early 2004 due to a series of "incidents occurring over the last 18 months." These included being arrested in Brazil for public nudity (performing in only sneakers), getting the band banned from a hotel in Australia, and, most critically, throwing full Corona beer bottles at the audience during a gig, pitched like fastballs. "A number of incidents occurring over the last 18 months have led to the decision that the two can no longer maintain a working partnership in the band."
**Josh Homme as Sole Member (Early 2004)** : In early 2004, following the firing of Nick Oliveri and the departure of Mark Lanegan, Josh Homme became the only remaining member of Queens of the Stone Age. He continued the band, given his primary role in writing 90% of the music and serving as the lead singer. "So this meant that in early 2004, Queen of the Stone Age was down to just one member, Josh Hommie."
**Josh Homme's Near-Death Experience (Fall 2010)** : In the fall of 2010, Josh Homme underwent a routine knee operation that led to a critical intubation error. This caused him to asphyxiate, and his heart stopped, resulting in full cardiac arrest, from which he was revived with a defibrillator. During his 13-day hospital stay, he contracted MRSA, a dangerous superbug, in his leg, leading to four months of being bedridden, extreme pain, disorientation, and isolation from his young daughter, Camille, which severely impacted his mental health and creative drive. "He started to asphyxiate. First he stopped breathing, then his heart stopped beating. Full cardiac arrest."
**Trent Reznor's Role in Josh Homme's Recovery** : During Josh Homme's recovery from his profound depression and near-death experience, Trent Reznor offered significant support, meeting with Homme for hours of conversation that Homme described as therapeutic and instrumental in his turnaround. "He credits Trent Rezner with helping him turn things around."
**Josh Homme's Rave Work (1991-1993)** : Between 1991 and 1993, Josh Homme worked rave gigs, where he would set up the lights in exchange for a supply of ecstasy. "He'd set up all the lights in exchange for a supply of ecstasy."
**"Free Tag 415" Tattoo** : Josh Homme has one of his more than 20 tattoos, "Free Tag 415," which is a permanent reminder of a particularly bad show the band played in Germany on Friday, April 15th, 2001, to ensure they never performed so poorly again. "It was so bad that each member of the group got that tattoo to remind them to never perform that poorly ever again."
**Josh Homme's Marriage and Separation** : Josh Homme married Brody Dalle on December 5th, 2005, and their separation occurred on November 15th, 2019. "Josh and Brody Doll were married on December 5th, 2005, but they separated on November 15th, 2019."
**Josh Homme's Hobbies** : Josh Homme has a known affinity for guns, motorcycles, and muscle cars. "Josh likes guns. Josh likes motorcycles and he likes muscle cars."
**Josh Homme's Physical Confrontations** : Standing at six feet, five inches, Josh Homme has been involved in several physical altercations, including fights with members of other bands, encounters with fans, and an incident during a show where he kicked a photographer. "Josh is a big guy, six, five. And he's been in a few fights, members of other bands, a couple of fans."
**Josh Homme & Mark Lanegan - Anthony Bourdain Theme** : Josh Homme and Mark Lanegan collaborated to create the theme music for the television show "Anthony Bourdain: Parts Unknown." "It was Josh and Mark Lennigan who came up with a theme music for the Anthony Bourdain show Parts Unknown."
**Josh Homme's Voice Acting** : Josh Homme has ventured into voice acting, making appearances in episodes of "American Dad" and "Aqua Teen Hunger Force," as well as live-action roles on "Portlandia" and the BBC comedy "House of Fools." "He's appeared in an episode of American Dad and Aquatine Hunger Force."
**Sweet Stuff Foundation** : Josh Homme founded the Sweet Stuff Foundation, a charity dedicated to assisting musicians, producers, engineers, and their families who are facing hardships due to illness or disability. "And finally, Josh has something called the Sweet Stuff Foundation, which is a charity that helps up musicians, producers, engineers and their families when they run into times of illness and disability."
**Reconciliation with Nick Oliveri** : Despite his departure, Josh Homme and Nick Oliveri have reconciled their differences, even though Oliveri has not significantly rejoined the band's activities. "Nicola Vary has never re-entered the picture much, but he and Josh have long since reconciled."

## curiosities
**Band Name Origin (1998)** : Co-founded in 1998 by Josh Homme and Jesse Hughes, the band's name arose from a night spent listening to the Polish metal band Vader, leading someone to suggest playing "Death Metal, but like the Eagles." An alternative account states Josh Homme coined the phrase after someone in a bar mistakenly referred to Scorpions' "Winds of Change" as "Death Metal." "The name came from a night listening to a Polish metal band called Vader. And someone, no one is sure who, said, we should play Death Metal, but like the Eagles."
**Josh Homme's Touring Flexibility** : While Josh Homme is officially a full-time member of Eagles of Death Metal, he does not consistently tour with the band, which explains his absence from certain performances. "although Josh is officially a full-time member of that band, he doesn't always tour with him."
**Bataclan Attack (November 13, 2015)** : Josh Homme was not present at the Bataclan theater in Paris on November 13, 2015, when terrorists attacked an Eagles of Death Metal concert, resulting in the deaths of 89 people, due to his flexible touring schedule with the band. "That's why he wasn't at the Bataclan theater in Paris when terrorists took over the place on November 13th, 2015 and killed 89 people who were all at an Eagles of Deathmale show."

Volumes 9 and 10 of these recordings, released in 2003, notably featured PJ Harvey and were the creative birthplace of the song "Make It With Choo." "the recording zone as the desert sessions"

**Nick Oliveri's Domestic Dispute and Arrest (July 12, 2011)** : On July 12, 2011, a SWAT team responded to a domestic dispute report at Nick Oliveri's house. After a two-hour standoff during which he released his girlfriend, a loaded rifle was found upon his arrest. He subsequently entered a plea agreement, resulting in community service and a six-year probation period. "On July 12th, 2011, a SWAT team was called to his house over a report of some kind of domestic dispute."
**MRSA Superbug** : Methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA) is identified as a "super bug" due to its immunity to nearly all known antibiotics. Josh Homme contracted this dangerous infection in his leg following a knee surgery in 2010. "Methasillin resistance, staphococcus, auras. It's a super bug that's immune to just about every antibiotic known to man."
**Public Nudity Law in Brazil** : The transcript highlights that public nudity is illegal in Brazil, a fact learned by Nick Oliveri when he was arrested for performing on stage wearing only sneakers. "Who knew that public nudity in Brazil was against the law?"
**Pink Duck Studio** : Josh Homme owns a recording studio named Pink Duck, located in a modest, older house within a residential area of Burbank, California, close to the airport.
