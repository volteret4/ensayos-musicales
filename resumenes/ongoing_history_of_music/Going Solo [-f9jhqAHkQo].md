# instrument - Accordion

## curiosities
**Unexpected Use in Rock Music** : The accordion was notably played by Sheryl Crow as a guest musician on Scott Weiland's solo album, 12 Bar Blues. Its inclusion was considered unusual and interesting for a rock record, diverging from what would typically be heard in Weiland's band, Stone Temple Pilots. "She was brought in to play of all things the accordion. This wasn't STP, but I'm sure as hell was interesting."

# curiosity

**Creative Differences Leading to Band Breakup** : When individual band members develop new musical ideas that diverge from the group's established vision, it can lead to "fractures and tension and confusion," often resulting in a band's breakup. Such splits are frequently attributed to "creative differences." "The band can break up using the ever popular creative differences issue"
**Solo Sabbaticals for Creative Expression** : Artists in bands sometimes take temporary breaks or "sabbaticals" between tours and albums to pursue individual musical muses and get new ideas "out of their system" that may not align with the band's overall vision. This allows them to return to the group "purged and cleansed." "So between tours and albums, they take a little sabbatical to pursue their individual muses."
**Pursuit of Individual Recognition** : A motivation for a band member to go solo can be the desire for personal recognition, seeking to be known as an "individual artist" rather than merely a part of a collective "team" or "group." This often leads to them not returning to the band. "the person in question wants a little extra recognition of their talents. Instead of continuing to be seen as a part of a whole, a team, a group, they would like to be known as an individual artist."
**Band Disintegration as a Catalyst for Solo Careers** : One reason for an artist to go solo is when their band, or the relationships within it, completely "blows up" and goes "to hell," leaving the individuals with no choice but to "pick up the pieces" and embark on their own careers. "the band or the relationship with the rest of the band blows up. Things go to hell and the individuals left to pick up the pieces."
**Limited Portable Skills for Rock Stars** : For rock stars, if their band dissolves, they often find themselves lacking other "portable skills" outside of music. This situation forces them into a "sink or swim" scenario, compelling them to pursue a solo career out of necessity. "as he rockstar, you really don't have a lot of portable skills. It becomes sync or swim as a solo artist."
**The Whole Is Greater Than the Sum of Its Parts** : In certain musical contexts, the collective synergy and talent of a band as a unit far exceeds the individual output or success of its members once they pursue solo careers. This phenomenon is observed when individual solo endeavors are "spotty" and do not approach the level of the group's achievements. "the whole is actually greater than the sum of its parts."
