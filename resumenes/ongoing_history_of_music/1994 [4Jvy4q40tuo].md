# artist - Green Day

## albums
**Dookie (February 1, 1994) – Only 9,000 Copies Shipped Initially; Exploded After Kurt Cobain's Death; 20 Million Worldwide** : Warner Bros. expected so little from Green Day's major label debut that only 9,000 copies were sent to stores on release day. The label's ceiling for success was 200,000 copies. The first single "Longview" had its MTV video debut on February 22; by the end of March the album was rising on the charts. When Kurt Cobain died on April 8, 1994, the shock created a vacuum in the alt-rock world — and *Dookie*'s punk energy helped fill it. By year's end it had gone multi-platinum; it was later certified Diamond (10 million in the US alone); worldwide sales reached approximately 20 million. It set off the mid-1990s punk pop revival, influencing the Offspring, Rancid, Sum 41, Blink-182, Fall Out Boy, and others. *Dookie* was also the first album Lady Gaga ever bought with her own money. Time Magazine ranked its influence alongside *Nevermind*. "This record acted as something of a bomb to the hearts broken by the end of Nirvana."


# artist - Nirvana

## members
Kurt Cobain

## concerts
Zenith Munich (final show, March 1, 1994)

## curiosities
**Final Show March 1, 1994; Death April 8, 1994 – The Vacuum That Reshaped 1994's Rock Landscape** : Nirvana played their final concert in Munich on March 1, 1994 — Kurt's voice was shot, the band was exhausted, and the rest of the European tour was canceled. On March 3, he deliberately overdosed on tranquilizers in a Rome hotel room. On March 18, police were called to his Seattle house after he locked himself in a room with guns. He agreed to enter rehab after an intervention, then escaped. His body was found April 8. The news was read on air: "Nirvana lead singer Kurt Cobain shot himself to death at a Seattle home yesterday." The effect on music fans was profound and prolonged. The loss of Nirvana's central narrative created a temporary opening that Green Day, The Offspring, Blur, and Oasis each rushed to fill. "Kurt's death was extremely traumatic. And the mourning continued for months."


# artist - The Offspring

## labels
Epitaph Records

## albums
**Smash (April 11, 1994) – Released 3 Days After Kurt's Death; $20,000 Budget; 18 Million Copies; Biggest-Selling Indie Record Ever** : The Offspring released *Smash* just three days after the world learned of Kurt Cobain's death, on April 11, 1994. Recorded on a $20,000 budget in North Hollywood for Epitaph — the Orange County indie label co-founded by Bad Religion — it was expected to sell perhaps 15,000 copies if lucky. The timing was fortunate: Green Day's *Dookie* had already established that pop punk had an audience. *Smash* broke out via radio in Southern California and spread nationally and internationally, leaving Epitaph dazed as sales hit 500,000, then a million, two million, five million, ten million, and ultimately approximately 18 million worldwide — the biggest-selling independent rock record in history. "Would Green Day and the Offspring have become as big as they did had Nirvana continued?"


# artist - Alice in Chains

## albums
**Jar of Flies EP (January 25, 1994) – First EP in History to Enter Billboard Album Charts at Number One** : Released on January 25, 1994, while singer Layne Staley's heroin addiction was limiting the band to EP-length output, *Jar of Flies* became the first EP in history to enter the Billboard album charts at number one. By year's end it had sold more than two million copies and was considered a major critical success. "This was the first time in history that an EP entered the charts at number one."


# artist - Blur

## albums
**Parklife (April 25, 1994) – Starting Point for Britpop; Food Records Called It a Mistake; 90 Weeks on UK Charts** : Blur had released two albums without setting the world alight. By 1993, frontman Damon Albarn was increasingly angry that American alt-rock was smothering British indie music; Manchester was over, and raves and dance clubs dominated. *Parklife* — a loose concept album capturing the essence of British life, initially to be titled "London" — was called a mistake by their own label Food Records. It proved anything but: an immediate hit with audiences young and old, it stayed on British album charts for 90 weeks and is now considered one of the best British albums of the 1990s. It was released three weeks after the news of Kurt Cobain's death. Along with Oasis's *Definitely Maybe* later that year, it is credited as the starting point of Britpop. "If we're looking for a starting point for Britpop, this is probably it."


# artist - Oasis

## albums
**Definitely Maybe (August 29, 1994) – Fastest-Selling UK Debut Ever; 100,000 Copies in 4 Days** : Oasis formed when Noel Gallagher — then a roadie for the Inspiral Carpets who wrote songs during downtime at his day job behind a British Gas parts counter — took over his brother Liam's band Rain and renamed it after a local venue. After singles "Columbia," "Supersonic," and "Shakermaker," their Creation Records debut *Definitely Maybe* dropped on August 29, 1994. It became the fastest-selling debut album in UK history at that point, moving 100,000 copies in four days and debuting at number one, outselling its nearest competitor by 50%. Critical acclaim was universal; worldwide sales eventually exceeded 10 million copies. Together with Blur's *Parklife*, it launched the Britpop era that consumed British music for the following years. "It had outsold its nearest competitor by 50%."


# artist - Nine Inch Nails

## members
Trent Reznor

## albums
**The Downward Spiral (March 8, 1994) – Recorded at Sharon Tate Murder House; Reached #2 in US** : Trent Reznor recorded NIN's second album in a house at 10050 Cielo Drive in the Bel Air area of Los Angeles — the same property where Charles Manson's followers murdered actress Sharon Tate and her friends on August 9, 1969. The murders were so brutal they still resonated through LA culture decades later. Reznor was descending into depression, alcoholism, and drug addiction during recording. The album used cutting-edge digital techniques and hardware. Despite being an intensely heavy industrial record, it reached number two on the Billboard album chart, was top 10 in the UK, and eventually sold north of five million copies worldwide. Many consider it the best Nine Inch Nails album ever made and one of the greatest albums of the 1990s. "It was a testament to how popular alt-rock had become that this heavy industrial record reached number two."


# artist - Soundgarden

## albums
**Superunknown (March 8, 1994) – Number One in US, Canada, Australia, New Zealand; Two Grammys; 10 Million Copies** : Released the same day as *The Downward Spiral*, Soundgarden's fourth studio album debuted at number one in the US, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand, reached the top ten in the UK, Denmark, Finland, Norway, and Switzerland, spawned five successful singles, won two Grammy Awards, and sold approximately 10 million copies worldwide. Most fans and critics consider it Soundgarden's best record. "Considered by most fans to be Sound Garden's best record."


# artist - Hole

## albums
**Live Through This (1994) – Established Hole as a Serious Force, Released Just After Kurt's Death** : Courtney Love and Hole had an earlier album and a high profile through her relationship with Kurt Cobain, but *Live Through This* (1994) established Hole as a musically serious entity in their own right. The album arrived just after Kurt's death, adding unavoidable biographical context to its already charged content. "There's nothing like success to spur imitation."


# artist - Weezer

## albums
**Blue Album (May 10, 1994) – Nerdy Slacker Power Pop; 15 Million Worldwide; Laid Foundations for Emo** : Weezer's self-titled debut (known as the Blue Album) was released on May 10, 1994, and defied easy categorization: somewhat grungy but not, nerdy and slacker-ish in a good way, rooted in power pop but with a punk spirit. It was a major critical and commercial success and laid groundwork for emo in the years that followed. Worldwide sales exceeded 15 million copies — a figure many people do not realize. "A lot of people don't realize that Weezer's Blue Album sold at least 15 million copies worldwide."


# artist - Jeff Buckley

## albums
**Grace (1994) – His Only Album; Died Accidentally 1997** : Jeff Buckley released *Grace* in 1994 — his one and only studio album. Three years later he accidentally drowned in the Mississippi River. The album is now considered a classic and a testament to what might have been. "Three years later he'd be dead, accidentally drowning in the Mississippi River."


# curiosity

**1994 Woodstock 25th Anniversary – Alt-Rock's Triumphant Peak; Lineup Proved Genre's Dominance** : The 25th anniversary of Woodstock was held in Saugerties, New York, with a lineup that demonstrated just how thoroughly alternative rock had taken over popular music: Red Hot Chili Peppers, Peter Gabriel, Green Day, Nine Inch Nails, Live, Collective Soul, Violent Femmes, Metallica, Blind Melon, the Cranberries, Primus, Porno for Pyros, and more. The conditions were muddy and cold. The same summer's Lollapalooza featured the Smashing Pumpkins, Beastie Boys, the Breeders, the Verve, Green Day, Flaming Lips, and others. "If you were a fan of rock and you were around in the early 1990s, you probably remember that era very, very fondly."

**August 11, 1994 – First Item Ever Sold Securely Over the Internet** : On August 11, 1994, Sting made history when a CD copy of his album *Ten Summoner's Tales* became the first item ever sold securely over the internet, for $12.48 plus shipping and handling. "It sounds quaint now, but back then this was revolutionary."
