## albums
**Billy Talent III (2009)** : This was the band's third studio album, produced by Brendan O'Brien. The production involved spontaneous additions like xylophones and background vocals, which O'Brien would improvise and tuck into the mix. This approach taught the band that not everything needs to be perfectly calculated during recording.
"And in Saveronic, there's this part that it's just me and him singing background and harmonies. And he just literally would come up with it on the spot."

**Dead Silence (2012)** : The band's fourth album, released on September 11, 2012. It marked a departure from the band's previous numbered album titles, a decision made due to a desire to shift interview focus from numbering to the music itself. The album debuted at number two on the charts, behind all of Michael Jackson's albums, who had passed away that same week. For this album, the band collaborated with artist Kent Taylor for the artwork, reflecting their commitment to physical art accompanying their releases.
"That album came out number two because Michael Jackson died. And on the same week, so all of Michael Jackson's albums... Dead Silence is number two behind all of them."

**Afraid of Heights (2016)** : This fifth album was released after a four-year break, during a period (2012-2013) when rock bands were struggling to make waves amidst the dominance of indie rock, characterized by bands like Mumford & Sons. The band expressed initial uncertainty about how the record would be received by Canadian rock stations. Its themes touch upon global political and social realities like Brexit, the refugee crisis, the rise of the right, and Donald Trump. The band views it not as a "political record" but a reflection of current global realities affecting people they know. Drummer Aaron was unable to perform on this album due to an MS relapse, with Jordan Hastings filling in.
"And I don't know if it kind of relates to back in the day when we first came out, where it was this, how much and I, it's kind of very pedestrian middle of the road, kind of thing that was happening, and we were not that, so it just kind of jumped out a little bit more."

**First Live Album (2006)** : In 2006, Billy Talent released their first live album, a two-CD set recorded during two performances in England: London on September 8th and Manchester on September 16th. These raw, 75-minute sets were sold at concerts and online.
"They recorded a couple of sets in England, one in London on September the 8th, and another in Manchester on September the 16th."

**666 (Live Album/DVD)** : This live album and DVD features material from the Rock am Ring Festival, a large event held at the Nürburgring in Germany, which attracts over 50,000 people.
"There have been other live albums, including 666, which is also a DVD featuring material from the Rock M Ring Festival, which is a giant event at the Nürburgring in Germany."

## curiosities
**Band Formation and Longevity (1993-present)** : Billy Talent formed in 1993, with the same four core members staying together since then. This long-standing lineup is considered an exception in the music industry, comparable to bands like Radiohead, U2 (since 1978), and ZZ Top (since 1969). The band members feel their identity is deeply intertwined with the band, having started at a young age, around 17.
"Billy Talent. Same four guys since 1993."

**Evolution of Music Consumption (2003-2012)** : The band has witnessed significant technological shifts in the music industry since the birth of the internet, including the rise of iPhones, streaming services, Facebook, MySpace, and Twitter by 2012. They acknowledge that record sales have never been their primary source of income, instead relying heavily on touring since their inception in 2003.
"We never made money off of record sales. And it's always touring is that's how we made our money."

**Commitment to Artwork and Physical Sales** : From day one, Billy Talent has prioritized creating great artwork to accompany their albums. For "Dead Silence," they worked with Kent Taylor, known for his band-related artwork and silk screen art. This focus is particularly relevant in markets like Germany, South Korea, and Japan, where physical music sales (vinyl, deluxe box packages) still account for a significant portion of the market (e.g., 67% in Germany).
"But the one thing that's worked for us, I think, is from day one, we always wanted to have great artwork that accompanied the album."

**Importance of Live Performance** : The band has always prided itself on its live shows and touring. They believe that a live performance cannot be downloaded, requiring fans to buy a ticket and experience it in person. Their dynamic on stage has been a source of significant pride since their beginnings, from playing small venues to large festivals.
"And you can't really download a live show. You have to go and buy a ticket and see a band live."

**Early Career Venues** : In their early days, Billy Talent performed in a wide variety of settings, including bars, clubs, Chinese food restaurants, and even house parties, emphasizing their roots and consistent dedication to live performance. They originate from Streetsville, Ontario.
"We literally have played every bar, every club, every, you know, from Chinese food restaurants to people's house parties."

**Aaron Solowoniuk's MS Diagnosis (1998)** : Drummer Aaron Solowoniuk was diagnosed with Multiple Sclerosis (MS) in 1998 at the age of 24. His initial symptoms included buzzing legs, difficulty walking to his job at Chrysler, optic neuritis in his left eye (leading to vision loss), and confusion. He managed the disease for over a decade with medication, needle therapy, and a positive outlook.
"Buzzing legs from my hips down. I could barely walk to my job at Chrysler. I had an optic neuritis on my left eye, so I lost a lot of vision in my eye."

**Aaron Solowoniuk's MS Relapse and Album Absence (2016)** : On January 15, 2016, Aaron announced on YouTube that he was experiencing an MS relapse, which had been slowing him down for six to seven months. The symptoms made it difficult to perform daily tasks and play drums. He decided to step away from recording drums for the "Afraid of Heights" album to focus on his health, as he was unsure how long his recovery would take. The band fully supported his decision and brought in Jordan Hastings as a fill-in drummer.
"I'm not going to be able to play drums on this record. And I just need to focus on my health."

**Aaron Solowoniuk's Treatment and Recovery** : After his relapse, Aaron decided to stop taking his MS medication for three months and explore alternative therapies, focusing on healthy eating and exercise. He expressed a goal to come back stronger, listening to his body more carefully, after having previously pushed himself too hard, to the point of walking with a cane. In 2017, he made a return to the stage, playing one song in Poland, his first performance in a year.
"I'm three months into no drugs at all. I'm going the eating healthy and exercising route to see how that works because the drugs that I've been taking, the newer ones haven't really worked for me, and they kind of made me feel worse."

**Band Support for Aaron Solowoniuk and FUMS (2016)** : Billy Talent, their management, and their record label (Warner Music Canada) are 100% supportive of Aaron in his struggle with MS. They established a charity called FUMS (Fuck U MS) to raise money for MS research. At a music festival on May 29, 2016, a band member (Ben Kowalewicz) addressed the crowd, expressing the band's love, care, and commitment to Aaron, and thanking Jordan Hastings for filling in. The speaker referred to Aaron as "Mr. A. S. Lua," which appears to be a mis-transcription of his full name, Aaron Solowoniuk.
"And that included setting a posterity called FUNMS that raises money for research."

**Unforeseen Rock am Ring Show (2016)** : During a Rock am Ring Festival performance in 2016, bad weather and lightning strikes led to the evacuation of the grounds. The band was initially told they wouldn't play, with headliners Deftones and Red Hot Chili Peppers scheduled before them. Unexpectedly, the Red Hot Chili Peppers requested that Billy Talent go on after them, resulting in Billy Talent headlining. The next day, the festival was canceled due to weather. This was Jordan Hastings' third show with the band, with his first two having been in Russia.
"So now all of a sudden, it's our first show back in Germany, it goes DevTones, then the Chili's, and then us. We had lined the night, and then the next day they canceled it because of the weather."

**Strong German Market** : Germany is a country where Billy Talent performs exceptionally well, demonstrating a robust fanbase there, particularly evident at events like the Rock am Ring Festival.
"Billy Towns live at the Rock M Ring Festival in Germany, a country where they do really, really well."

**Best Territory in Canada** : Despite international success, Canada remains Billy Talent's best territory, with hometown shows holding significant special meaning for the band.
"But their best territory is of course in Canada."

# person - Brendan O'Brien

## curiosities
**Production Style** : Brendan O'Brien, known as Pearl Jam's favorite producer, employs an improvisational and spontaneous production style. He frequently adds unexpected instrumentation, like xylophones, or layers background vocals on the spot, tucking these elements into the mix to create subtle effects. He also operates his own recording studio in Atlanta, filled with various gear.
"We would record stuff. And at the end, he'd be like, something needs a fitness pocket right here and literally walk into the other room and pick up like, exylophones and like weird instrumentation."

**Mellotron Usage** : O'Brien owned and used a Mellotron, a "taped piano" instrument, famously on the Red Hot Chili Peppers' album "Blood Sugar Sex Magik" and specifically on their song "Breaking the Girl." He later used this same Mellotron on Billy Talent's songs "Saint Veronica" and "White Sparrows" from the album "Billy Talent III," after the band expressed their admiration for its sound.
"And I was like, is that a melaton? And he's like, yeah, I mean, pulled it out and I said, is this the melaton from, he's like, yeah, that's from, that's from the chili peppers."

# instrument - Mellotron

## curiosities
**Description and Sound** : The Mellotron is described as a "taped piano" that, when played as a piano chord, can produce sounds like an actual recorded flute or even a string arrangement.
"There's like this thing called a melaton, which is a taped piano. So it would have the sound of like a flute, like an actual recorded flute, but you would play it as a piano chord."

**Famous Uses** : The Mellotron is most famously known for its background flute sound on Led Zeppelin's "Stairway to Heaven." It was also used by Brendan O'Brien on the Red Hot Chili Peppers' "Blood Sugar Sex Magik" album, particularly on the song "Breaking the Girl," and later on Billy Talent's "Saint Veronica" and "White Sparrows."
"And then melaton is most famous. You probably already know this from Stairway to Heaven. Is that background flute on Stairway to Heaven?"

# curiosity

**Challenges of Band Longevity** : Maintaining a band's lineup over many years presents significant challenges, including the constant close proximity of members, cramped conditions on tour, record company issues, personal issues, and the general fragility of human relationships. Despite these difficulties, some bands, like Billy Talent, Radiohead, U2, and ZZ Top, manage to maintain a consistent lineup for decades.
"Still wonder so many groups break up. For a while, it's just too much trouble to stay together."

**Adaptation to Digital Music Landscape (2012)** : By 2012, the music industry was grappling with the rise of digital consumption, including iPhones and streaming, while still struggling to adapt from a physical unit-based sales model. Bands like Billy Talent found that they couldn't compete with free content and focused instead on maintaining the quality of their creative output, as touring remained their primary revenue stream.
"I think our approach to it... You can't compete with free, right?"

**Global Political and Social Context for Art** : Around the time of Billy Talent's "Afraid of Heights" album, global events such as Brexit, the European refugee crisis, the rise of the right, and the political climate surrounding Donald Trump created a demand for "angry, aggressive, outspoken art of all sorts." The band believes their music, which addresses these realities, fit the timing.
"And now, you know, the time is right for angry, aggressive, outspoken art of all sorts."

**Multiple Sclerosis (MS) in Canada and UK** : Canada has one of the highest, if not the highest, rates of Multiple Sclerosis in the world, with the UK also experiencing a high incidence. A theory, though unproven, suggests a possible link between MS and a lack of sunlight and vitamin D, suggesting that daily vitamin D supplementation from a young age might help prevent it.
"Canada has one of the highest MS rates in the entire world. If not the highest MS rate, I can't believe how many people I know from even my circle of my family."

**FUMS (Fuck U MS)** : FUMS is a charity initiative created by Billy Talent, their management, and record label to raise money for Multiple Sclerosis research, demonstrating the music community's support for those affected by the disease.
"And that included setting a posterity called FUNMS that raises money for research."
