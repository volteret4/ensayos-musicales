# artist - The Smiths

## albums
**The Queen is Dead (1986)** : The artwork for this album prominently features the band standing in front of The Salford Lads' Club on Coronation Street in Manchester. This photo, taken by the band, transformed the club into an alt-rock shrine, despite initial objections from the club itself. The album's artwork appropriation of the club's image caused the club to hire lawyers and state their disapproval, but later they began to embrace their status. "The Smiths and Bigmouth strikes again from the Queen is Dead, the album featuring artwork that turned The Selford Ladd's Club into an alt-rock shrine."

## curiosities
**The Salford Lads' Club Photo Shoot** : The Smiths posed in front of The Salford Lads' Club, located on Coronation Street in Manchester, for the artwork of their album *The Queen is Dead*. The club, founded in 1904 by Robert Baden Powell, initially disapproved of this appropriation, hiring lawyers to state that the band's music and lyrics were not congruent with their values and that the band was not connected to the club. Despite their unwelcoming stance towards fans recreating the photo, the club eventually lightened up and accepted 20,000 pounds for renovations, recognizing its status as a Smiths shrine. "The Smiths decided to pose there because of the club's history."

## albums
**The Joshua Tree (1987) – Named After Desert Tree** : This album was originally conceived under the working title "Desert Song." Just before Christmas in 1986, U2, accompanied by photographer Anton Corbijn, traveled to the Mojave Desert to shoot photos for the album. During discussions on day one of the shoot, Corbijn and Bono talked about Joshua trees and their mythology, leading Bono to suggest naming the new record *The Joshua Tree*. The album's iconic cover features a photograph of a solitary Joshua tree. "Bono suggested that the new record be called the Joshua Tree."

## songs
**"Have You Found What You're Looking For?" (Not a Song, but Album related quote)** : A concrete slab placed in the Mojave Desert near the site of the original Joshua Tree features a plaque inscribed with the question, "have you found what you're looking for?". This phrase is a lyric from their song "I Still Haven't Found What I'm Looking For" from *The Joshua Tree* album, and serves as a poignant reference for pilgrims to the tree. "And you'll know you're in the right place when you find a concrete slab in the desert floor with a plaque that reads, have you found what you're looking for?"

## curiosities
**Formation at Mount Temple Comprehensive School** : U2 was formed in 1976 at Mount Temple Comprehensive School on Malahide Road in Dublin. The Edge, as seen in the movie *It Might Get Loud*, shows the bulletin board where Larry Mullen first posted a note seeking band members, and the room where U2 used to rehearse. This school is considered a "Bethlehem" for U2 fans. "This is where the band was formed in 1976."
**First Public Performance as Feedback** : U2's very first public performance took place at Mount Temple Comprehensive School on an elevated slab of concrete outside the school. At the time, they were known as Feedback. This performance was part of a school talent contest where they played "Show Me The Way" by Peter Frampton, some Bay City Rollers songs, and Beach Boys songs. "They were called feedback then."
**The Joshua Tree Album Cover Shoot** : In late 1986, U2 traveled with photographer Anton Corbijn into the Mojave Desert to capture images for their new album, then provisionally titled "Desert Song." Over three days, they stayed in motels and posed for shots. On the second day, they found a distinct, solitary Joshua tree on the south side of State Road 190. They spent approximately 20 minutes photographing around this tree, which became the iconic image for *The Joshua Tree* album. "Suddenly Anton spotted a single tree from his window on the south side of the bus while they were on State Road 190."
**The Original Joshua Tree Pilgrimage Site** : The specific Joshua tree photographed for U2's *The Joshua Tree* album cover in the Mojave Desert (west of Death Valley on Highway 190) became a pilgrimage site for fans. The band initially kept its location secret to prevent souvenir hunters from damaging it. The tree, however, died of natural causes and fell over around 2000. Despite its demise, fans continue to visit, leaving messages by arranging stones on the ground and inside a weathertight container called "the YouTube." A concrete slab with a plaque marking the spot reads, "have you found what you're looking for?" "The tree, sadly, is dead. It died of natural causes and fell over some time in 2000."
**World Tour Gross** : After a recording was made in 1981, U2 was able to stage a world tour that grossed three quarters of a billion dollars. "this band would be able to stage a world tour that would gross three quarters of a billion dollars."

# artist - Beastie Boys

## members
Ad-Rock

## albums
**Paul's Boutique (1989)** : Released on July 25, 1989, this second album by the Beastie Boys sought to embody their New York identity through its artwork. Ad-Rock was inspired by an old radio commercial for a Brooklyn men's clothing store named Paul's Boutique, which was no longer in business. To create the cover, a sign for "Paul's Boutique" was fabricated and hung over a store called Leigh Sportswear at the corner of Ludlow and Rivington streets on Manhattan's Lower East Side. Photographer Jeremy Chutan took a panoramic shot for the cover. Initially, the album was met with disappointment from fans expecting more frat boy anthems like those on their first album, and was branded a failure. However, it was later recognized as a landmark recording praised for its production, sampling, and clever use of beats, becoming an essential part of music collections. "When the album was released on July 25, 1989, the whole thing landed with a thud."

## songs
**Fight for Your Right to Party (1986)** : This song is mentioned as an example of the "frat boy stuff" from the Beastie Boys' first album, which disappointed fans who were expecting more of the same when *Paul's Boutique* was released. "Everyone who was hoping for more songs like Fight for Your Right to Party and Girls, and all the other frat boy stuff from the first album was very disappointed."
**Girls (1986)** : This track from the Beastie Boys' debut album is cited as one of the "frat boy stuff" songs that set an expectation for their second album, *Paul's Boutique*, an expectation that was not met upon its initial release. "Everyone who was hoping for more songs like Fight for Your Right to Party and Girls, and all the other frat boy stuff from the first album was very disappointed."

## curiosities
**Paul's Boutique Album Artwork Location** : The iconic panoramic album cover for the Beastie Boys' 1989 album *Paul's Boutique* was shot at the corner of Ludlow and Rivington streets (99 Rivington) on the Lower East Side of Manhattan. The original Paul's Boutique store that inspired the album's name was out of business, so a sign was created and hung over Leigh Sportswear for photographer Jeremy Chutan's shoot. After the album gained fame, the sportswear store was replaced by a restaurant also named Paul's Boutique, which operated until 1997 when it became The Three Monkeys. A fire in January 2011 destroyed the building, leaving little to see today. "If you want to make a pilgrimage to where the building used to stand, it's at 99 Rivington in Manhattan."

**Last House in Seattle** : Kurt Cobain's last residence was located at 171 Lake Washington Boulevard East in the Denny Blaine District of Seattle. This substantial house, measuring 7,000 square feet with four bedrooms, five bathrooms, and five fireplaces, was originally built in 1902 by Albert Blaine. Cobain's body was discovered in the greenhouse on the property in 1994, which Courtney Love subsequently had torn down in 1996. After Love sold the house in 1997, security measures like a large fence and cameras were installed, and more landscaping was added to preserve privacy. "Kurt Cobain's last house. The address is 171 Lake Washington Boulevard East in the Denny Blaine District of Seattle."

When Courtney Love sold the house, she included a contractual clause stipulating her right to return and remove that specific willow tree to replant it elsewhere. # genre - Punk

## curiosities
**924 Gilman Street Club – All-Ages Punk Hub** : Located at 924 Gilman Street in West Berkeley, California, this venue is recognized as arguably the most important all-ages punk club globally. Founded in 1986, it operates as a member's only establishment, requiring a $2 annual fee for membership, which includes voting rights at bi-monthly meetings where club business is discussed. The club enforces strict rules: no drugs, alcohol, violence, racism, homophobia, or major label bands. Due to the major label ban, successful alumni such as Green Day, The Offspring, AFI, Primus, and Bad Religion are no longer welcome to perform there. "Probably the most important, all-ages punk club in the world."
