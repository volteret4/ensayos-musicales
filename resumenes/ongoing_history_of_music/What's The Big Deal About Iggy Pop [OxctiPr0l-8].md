## albums
**The Idiot (1977) - First Solo Album** : Iggy Pop's first solo album, work on which began in June 1976 with David Bowie, and was ready by March 1977. Bowie played all instruments except Iggy's vocals. It performed "all right sales-wise," surpassing all Stooges releases, partially due to the Bowie connection. In 1994, Trent Reznor sampled its opening beats from "Nightclubbing" to create a new sound. "The Idiot did all right sales-wise. Certainly way better than anything the stooges had ever released."
**Lust for Life (1977) - Career Best Album** : Recorded in West Berlin with David Bowie in just 13 days after their tour together, this album was made in celebration of Iggy Pop's renewed prospects. It is considered the best work of his entire career and features the song "The Passenger." "in celebration of Iggy's renewed prospects, the album was called Lust for Life, and it remains the best work of his entire career."
**Blah Blah Blah (1986) - Bowie-Inspired Comeback** : Released in October 1986, this album was recorded after David Bowie encouraged Iggy Pop and ex-Sex Pistol guitarist Steve Jones to make new music from rough tapes. A recording contract was secured, and it became Iggy's best album in years, introducing him to a third generation of music fans with the song "Real Wild Child." "A recording contract was secured, and the result was Iggy's best album in years. He called it Blah Blah Blah."
**Instinct (1988) - Steve Jones Collaboration** : Released in July 1988, this album saw Iggy Pop continue his collaboration with ex-Sex Pistol guitarist Steve Jones. It featured the song "Cold Metal." "Iggy sucked with Steve Jones for his next album, from July 1988, and the record called Instinct."
**Brick by Brick (1990) - Top 40 Hit Album** : This 1990 album featured a duet with Kate Pierson of the B-52s and yielded Iggy Pop's first honest-to-god top 40 hit single. The success of this album contributed to Iggy being declared an alt-rock icon in the early 90s. "It came from his 1990 album, Brick by Brick, and it featured a duet with Kate Pearson of the B-52s."

## songs
**Lust for Life (1977)** : A song released in September 1977, co-written with David Bowie, and used extensively to tell stories in various media, from movies to cars to beer commercials. It is the title track of Iggy's second solo album, which is considered his best work. "from September 1977, Iggy Pop with Lust for Life."
**China Girl (1977)** : Co-written by Iggy Pop and David Bowie, this song was originally released on Iggy's 1977 album The Idiot. David Bowie later had a major hit with his own recording of the song in the early 1980s, which earned Iggy Pop close to a million dollars. "China Girl from 1977. A song co-written by David Bowie who of course had a big hit with his own recording of that song in the early 1980s."
**Nightclubbing (1977)** : A track from Iggy Pop's 1977 album The Idiot, notable for its opening beats. In 1994, Trent Reznor famously sampled these beats, loading them into a Macintosh computer, turning the waveform upside down, and adding effects to create a new sound. "Seventeen years later, another part of that record was made famous. And it began with the opening beats of a track called Nightclubbing."
**The Passenger (1977)** : A song from the 1977 album Lust for Life, later covered by Siouxsie and the Banshees and performed by Bau Wow. It was allegedly inspired by The Doors singer Jim Morrison, with Iggy Pop reportedly believing Morrison's spirit had transferred to him from Jim's old leather pants. "The passenger, a song later covered by and performed by Susie in the Banshee's and Baw House."
**Real Wild Child (1986)** : Released on the album Blah Blah Blah in October 1986, this song introduced Iggy Pop to a third generation of music fans. It is a cover of a 1957 song by Australian rockabilly performer Johnny O'Keefe. "Real wild child. Few people realized this, but that's a cover."
**Cold Metal (1988)** : A song from the July 1988 album Instinct, which continued Iggy Pop's collaboration with ex-Sex Pistol guitarist Steve Jones. "Here's Iggy and Steve with Cold Metal."

## curiosities
**Birth and Early Life** : James Juel Asterberg was born on April 21, 1947, and grew up in the Coachville Mobile Home Park in Ypsilanti, Michigan, residing in a 50 by 100 foot trailer. His father was a highly strung English teacher, and his mother worked as a secretary. "His real name is James Juel Asterberg. He was born on April 21, 1947 and grew up in the Coachville Mobile Home Park in Yipsalani, Michigan."
**Academic Achievements** : As a student, James Juel Asterberg was "pretty good in school," serving as president of the student council and vice president of the debating team. In grade 9, his classmates voted him "most likely to succeed." "He was president of the student council and vice president of the debating team. And when he was in grade 9, he was voted most likely to succeed by his classmates."
**Name Origin - Iggy Pop** : The name "Iggy" was taken from his first band, The Iguanas, while "Pop" was inspired by a junkie friend named Jim Pop. Jim suffered from a medical condition that caused him to lose all his hair, including his eyebrows, which Iggy thought was "pretty cool," leading him to shave his own eyebrows and cement his new stage name. "Iggy, after the iguanas, and Pop, after a junkie friend named Jim Pop."
**Early Persona and Police Encounters** : Iggy Pop embraced a deliberately "weird" persona, favoring strange haircuts and large, baggy overalls. His appearance sometimes led to police stopping him on the street, believing he might be an "escape mental patient or something." "Iggy enjoyed being weird for weird sake. He liked strange haircuts and like wearing these big baggy overalls. And everyone's in a while the cops would stop him on the street thinking that he was an escape mental patient or something."
**Infamous Stage Antics - General** : Iggy Pop's live performances were legendary for their extreme and often self-destructive behavior, which pushed boundaries far beyond what was acceptable at the time, particularly during the "peace and love hippie thing" of 1967-1968. Before shows, he would consume acid, crank, and large amounts of pot, then appear on stage in dresses or maternity clothes, often stripping completely naked. "Iggy got weirder and weirder whenever the band played live. And it's important to put this into context. Some of what Iggy did back then would still get him busted today."
**Infamous Stage Antics - Self-Mutilation** : During performances, Iggy Pop would smear his body with peanut butter and raw meat, carve his chest with broken beer bottles or steak knives, resulting in "long, bleeding open wounds." He also had The Stooges guitarist Rob Ashton whip him with a real leather bullwhip and would smash himself in the head with a microphone. "He'd smear his body with peanut butter and raw meat. He'd carve up his chest using a broken beer bottle or maybe mutilate himself with a steak knife, drawing long, bleeding open wounds."
**Arrests for Indecent Exposure** : Iggy Pop was frequently arrested for indecent exposure due to his habit of stripping naked on stage during performances. This was part of his extreme and confrontational performance style with The Stooges. "No wonder Iggy kept getting arrested for indecent exposure."
**Post-Performance Stage Condition** : After The Stooges' performances, the stage was typically "slick with blood" due to Iggy Pop's self-mutilation and violent antics. This visual aftermath underscored the raw and dangerous nature of their live shows. "And no wonder when the stooages left the stage, it was slick with blood."
**Financial Mismanagement and Eccentric Behavior (1969-1973)** : During the period of The Stooges' three major label albums, Iggy Pop was "totally off the deep end," spending $100,000 of his record company advance on a suite at a Beverly Hills hotel. He developed a substantial "coconut hair one habit" and was arrested multiple times, including for impersonating a female (which was a crime then), and once took an axe to a wealthy one-night stand's Mercedes. "He spent $100,000 of his record company advance on a suite at a Beverly Hills hotel. He had a huge coconut hair one habit that needed to be fed."
**Confrontation at Michigan Palace (1974)** : Following a brutal confrontation with a Detroit gang, Iggy Pop performed at Michigan Palace where he provocatively demanded that the gang come on stage and "beat him to a pulp," with some friends believing he sought to die on stage. The audience responded by throwing beer bottles, not all of them empty, at the stage, an incident captured on the album "Metallic KO." "Years later a cassette recording of that performance would be released on an album called Metallic KO."
**Post-Stooges Mental Health Crisis** : After The Stooges disbanded, Iggy Pop's mental and physical state deteriorated, leading him to a psychiatric hospital in Los Angeles. He was described as a "babbling, strung out freak prone to blockouts and random acts of violence," eventually disappearing and being found in a "heap under the counter at a hamburger joint," where police gave him the choice of jail or a long stint in a mental unit. "When the Stooages finally blew apart, Iggy had nowhere to go but a psychiatric hospital in Los Angeles."
**David Bowie's Intervention** : David Bowie, who had admired Iggy Pop's energy for years and even wrote a song about him in 1972, was the only person who cared enough to visit Iggy during his psychiatric confinement. Bowie "finally convinced Iggy that he didn't need to kill himself," thereby playing a crucial role in his career's rebirth. "Bowie was the only person in the world who cared about what happened to Iggy Pop and it was him who finally convinced Iggy that he didn't need to kill himself."
**Bowie's Role in Career Rebirth** : David Bowie not only got Iggy Pop a new management deal and a record contract but also actively collaborated on his first two solo albums, The Idiot and Lust for Life, and even toured as his backup keyboard player. This intervention was pivotal in Iggy's career comeback. "Thanks to Bowie Iggy's career was reborn."
**Financial Windfall from China Girl** : Iggy Pop earned a significant amount of money, estimated close to a million dollars, from David Bowie's commercially successful cover version of "China Girl." Iggy had co-written the song, originally appearing on his 1977 album The Idiot. "In fact, a good guess would be somewhere close to a million dollars."
**Post-Bowie Career Slump and Resurgence** : After a strong start with Bowie, Iggy Pop's solo career sagged by 1982, leaving him without a record contract and forced to tour to survive. However, David Bowie again intervened with his recording of "China Girl," which provided a financial turnaround, leading to the phrase, "This guy's got more lives in a cat, doesn't he?" "Fortunately though, his old pal David Bowie came through right about then with his recording of China Girl, and things turned around again."
**Marriage to Suci** : Iggy Pop met and married a Japanese woman named Suci during a period of renewed stability in his life, following a career boost from David Bowie's recording of "China Girl." "Iggy then met and married a Japanese woman named Suci."
**Acting Career Expansion** : Iggy Pop expanded his career into acting, securing roles in notable films such as "The Color of Money" alongside Tom Cruise and Paul Newman, "Sid and Nancy" with Gary Oldman, and even an episode of "Miami Vice" with Don Johnson. "Over the next few years, Iggy picked up roles in films like The Color of Money with Tom Cruise and Paul Newman."
**Alt-Rock Icon Status (1990s)** : By the early 1990s, Iggy Pop was cleaned up, sounding better than ever, and recognized as a legendary alt-rock pioneer alongside influential bands like The Velvet Underground, The Ramones, The Sex Pistols, and The Clash. His continued acting roles and a top 40 hit single cemented his iconic status. "Iggy was once again named as a legendary alt-rock pioneer right out there with a velvet underground on the rambones and the sex pistols and the clash."
**Godfather of Punk Legacy** : Iggy Pop is universally known as the "Godfather of Punk" and is considered one of the most important historical figures in New Rock. His work with The Stooges provided the blueprints for the punk movement of the 1970s and 80s, acknowledged by bands like the Sex Pistols. "icons don't get much bigger than Iggy Pop, who is universally known as the Godfather of Punk."
**Pioneer of Unconventional Music** : Iggy Pop is recognized for insisting on doing the unconventional, pushing the envelope to an extent that few have since matched. His influence created a "straight line" from his early work through the punk of the 70s, indie rock of the 80s, grunge of the 90s, to today's punk and underground music, demonstrating how "undiluted, straight from the rock and roll" should be done. "Iggy was one of those guys who insisted on doing the unconventional. Who pushed the envelope so far that few people since have managed to stand that far out on the edge."

## curiosities
**Debut Performance (October 31, 1967)** : The band, initially called Iggy and the Psychedelic Stooges, made their debut on October 31, 1967, in Ann Arbor, Michigan. They were characterized by a loud, spontaneous, sloppy, fuzzy, arrogant, violent, angry, and powerful sound. "And on October 31, 1967, Iggy and the psychedelic studios made their debut in Ann Arbor, Michigan."
**Name Change to The Stooges** : Shortly after their debut as Iggy and the Psychedelic Stooges, the band's name was shortened to simply "The Stooges," marking the beginning of the legend of Iggy Pop. "The psychedelic studios soon became just the stooages. And here begins the legend of Iggy Pop."
**Blueprint for Punk** : The Stooges' three studio albums and the famous live bootleg "Metallic KO" served as crucial blueprints for the punk music that emerged in the 1970s and 80s. Their nihilistic and violent style profoundly influenced future bands. "Those three studio albums and that famous bootleg became blueprints for the punk that was to follow in the 1970s and 80s."
**Acknowledged by Sex Pistols** : Fans, including members of the Sex Pistols, later acknowledged that Iggy Pop was indeed the "Godfather of Punk," recognizing The Stooges' foundational role in the genre. "Fans like the Sex Festels would later acknowledge that Iggy was, yes, the godfather of punk."
**Band Disintegration** : The Stooges "finally blew apart," leading to Iggy Pop's severe mental health crisis and eventual hospitalization, described as a "babbling, strung out freak prone to blockouts and random acts of violence." "When the Stooages finally blew apart, Iggy had nowhere to go but a psychiatric hospital in Los Angeles."

# artist - David Bowie

## curiosities
**Admiration for Iggy Pop** : David Bowie admired Iggy Pop's raw energy for many years, even writing a song about him in 1972, demonstrating a long-standing appreciation for his artistic force. "Bowie had admired Iggy's energy for years. In fact, in 1972 Bowie wrote this song about Iggy."
**Savior of Iggy Pop's Career** : Bowie visited Iggy Pop in a psychiatric hospital when no one else cared, convincing him not to kill himself and subsequently securing him a new management deal and record contract. This intervention was crucial for Iggy's professional and personal survival. "Bowie was the only person in the world who cared about what happened to Iggy Pop and it was him who finally convinced Iggy that he didn't need to kill himself."
**Collaboration on The Idiot** : Bowie was instrumental in Iggy Pop's first solo album, "The Idiot," starting work in June 1976 and completing it by March 1977. He played "everything else" on the album, while Iggy handled vocals, establishing a key creative partnership. "Then in June 1976 Bowie began working with Iggy on Iggy's first solo album. It was Iggy on vocals and Bowie on everything else."
**Hit Version of China Girl** : David Bowie released his own recording of "China Girl," a song he co-wrote with Iggy Pop in 1977, in the early 1980s. His version became a big hit, generating significant royalties (close to a million dollars) for Iggy Pop. "A song co-written by David Bowie who of course had a big hit with his own recording of that song in the early 1980s."
**Touring as Keyboard Player** : Bowie toured with Iggy Pop not as a headlining act, but humbly as the keyboard player in Iggy's backup band, showcasing his dedication to fostering Iggy's career. "And Bowie even toured with Iggy, not as a headline act, but just as the keyboard player in his backup band."
**Collaboration on Lust for Life** : After their tour, Bowie and Iggy Pop went to West Berlin and recorded Iggy's "Lust for Life" album in just 13 days. This quick, collaborative effort produced what remains the best work of Iggy's career. "Once that tour was over, the two of them went back to West Berlin, where they made another record for Iggy in just 13 days."
**Later Encouragement for Blah Blah Blah** : In 1985, Bowie again came to Iggy Pop's aid by listening to rough tapes of new songs and encouraging Iggy and Steve Jones to make another album, leading to "Blah Blah Blah." "In 1985, it was Bowie to the Rescue again. After listening to some rough tapes of some new songs, he encouraged Iggy and his new buddy, ex-sex pistol guitar Steve Jones, to make another album."
