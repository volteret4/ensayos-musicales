**Career vs. Health Benefits Dilemma (Early 2000s)** : As Billy Talent gained traction, Aaron faced a choice between continuing his factory job at Chrysler, which offered good benefits covering his expensive MS drugs, and pursuing music with the band, which typically lacked such benefits. He ultimately chose the band, initially keeping his condition a secret. "Working at the Chrysler plant meant good benefits, benefits that covered the cost of the expensive drugs he needed. Meanwhile, rock bands tend not to have any benefits."
**MS Awareness Advocacy (2006 onwards)** : In 2006, after learning about children as young as three being diagnosed with MS, Aaron decided to publicly share his diagnosis to raise awareness. He actively works with the MS Society of Canada and is involved with FUMS, an organization that hosts benefit concerts and golf tournaments to support MS charities. "he thought it was maybe time that he did what he could to raise awareness of MS."
**Ongoing MS Treatment** : Aaron's MS is managed with three weekly injections of an expensive drug, and he is committed to supporting MS charities until a cure is found. "Aaron's own health is under control thanks to three weekly injections of a hideously expensive drug."

**Back Injury and Emergency Surgery (May 10, 2010)** : On his 50th birthday, Bono experienced severe back pain, followed by leg numbness and partial paralysis. He was rushed for emergency surgery, where he was diagnosed with a herniated disc, severe compression of the sciatic nerve, an 8-millimeter tear in the disc's ligaments, and disc fragments in his spinal canal. Delicate surgery was required to prevent permanent paralysis. "He was rushed to the hospital for emergency surgery. The problem was discovered to be a herniated disc and a severe compression of the sciatic nerve."
**360 Tour Postponement and Financial Impact (2010)** : Due to Bono's back injury, 16 scheduled North American dates on U2's massive 360 world tour had to be postponed for a year. This resulted in an estimated loss of $100 million in ticket sales, marking the largest insurance claim in the history of canceled concerts. "The 16 canceled dates represented a loss of about $100 million in ticket sales. It was the biggest insurance claim in the history of canceled concerts."
**Munich Clinic Recovery (2010)** : After his initial back surgery, Bono was sent to a clinic in Munich for recovery. He recovered well, joking about being "rebuilt by German engineering." The tour was able to resume in Italy that August. "After the initial surgery, he was sent to a clinic in Munich."
**Bicycle Crash and Multiple Fractures (November 2014)** : While preparing for the Innocence and Experience tour, Bono crashed his bike in Central Park, sustaining severe injuries. These included a left facial fracture (affecting his eye orbit), a left scapula (shoulder blade) fractured in three places, a left compound distal humerus fracture (where the bone broke in six pieces and pierced his skin), and a fracture of his fifth metacarpal in his left hand. "He was riding his bike through Central Park and he crashed. Hard. And the injuries were ugly."
**Extensive Arm and Hand Surgery (November 2014)** : The bicycle crash necessitated five hours of surgery to repair his arm, where a nerve was moved, and the bone was fixed with three metal plates and 18 screws. Additional surgery was required for his fractured left hand. "There was five hours of surgery where the album [likely 'arm'] was washed out and reset. A nerve trapped in the break was moved and the bone was repaired with three metal plates and 18 screws."
**Lingering Guitar Playing Difficulty (2014 onwards)** : Following his extensive arm and hand injuries, Bono still lacked feeling in two fingers and expressed concern that doctors believed he might never play the guitar properly again due to the inability to form chords with all his fingers. "I still don't have any feeling in two fingers. Doctors say I might never play the guitar properly again because I can't use all my fingers to form chords."
**Debunked "Hassidic Jew" Crash Story** : A rumor circulated that Bono was dressed as a Hassidic Jew when he crashed his bike. The Edge clarified this was a joke he had made in a radio interview, suggesting it as a reason why Bono might not have been recognized, and confirmed it was not true. "He told me that when Bono crashed his bike, he was dressed as a hersteaded Jew. That was a joke and I'm responsible for it."
**Glaucoma Diagnosis and Sunglasses (Early 1990s)** : Bono was diagnosed with glaucoma, a chronic eye condition that causes pressure buildup on the eyeball, in the early 1990s. One of its symptoms is sensitivity to light, even indoors, which is the medical reason he started wearing sunglasses consistently around that time. He has undergone various treatments like eye drops and lasers to prevent optic nerve damage. "Bono was first diagnosed with Glacoma in the early 1990s, which, as you might remember, is exactly when he started wearing sunglasses all the time."

## curiosities
**Cerebral Hemorrhages (February 18, 2005)** : On February 18, 2005, during a BBC visit, Edwin Collins felt unwell, experiencing dizziness and nausea. Two days later, he was in intensive care after suffering a major cerebral hemorrhage, described by doctors as "catastrophic." He then suffered a second, equally catastrophic hemorrhage. "Two days later though, he was an intensive care having suffered a major cerebral hemorrhage, bad bleeding of the brain. And then another one. And both were described as catastrophic."
**Brain Surgery and Post-Stroke Speech (2005)** : Less than a week after his hemorrhages, Edwin underwent brain surgery to relieve pressure in his skull. For weeks afterward, he could only utter four phrases: "Yes," "No," "Grace Maxwell" (his wife's name), and "The possibilities are endless." "for weeks afterwards, he could only say four things. Yes, no. Grace Maxwell, which is the name of his wife, and weirdly, the possibilities are endless."
**Recovery with Wife's Support** : Despite a poor prognosis, Edwin recovered with the unwavering support of his wife, Grace Maxwell, who encouraged him to draw and write in notebooks with his left hand, as his right hand was rendered useless by the strokes. "His wife, and this was as close to a saint as you'll find, was there every step of the way. It was her idea to help his recovery by getting Edwin to draw."
**Speech vs. Singing Anomaly** : Following his strokes, Edwin experienced significant stuttering when speaking. However, remarkably, when he sang, his stutter completely disappeared. "He still has trouble speaking, Edwin stutters a lot. But when he starts to sing, there's not a stutter to be heard."
**Musical Comeback (After 4 Years)** : It took four long years for Edwin Collins to slowly return to music. Although his right hand was impaired, preventing him from strumming a guitar, he could still form chords, and he eventually resumed recording, releasing a couple of new albums. "Slowly he was able to get back into music. It took four long years. He couldn't strum his guitar anymore because of that bum right hand, but he can still form chords."
**Wife's Book on Recovery** : His wife, Grace Maxwell, documented Edwin's recovery journey in her book titled "Falling and Laughing, The Restoration of Edwin Collins." "Grace wrote a book about Edwin's recovery called Falling and Laughing, The Restoration of Edwin Collins, which is extremely good."
**Documentary "The Possibilities Are Endless" (2014)** : A documentary about Edwin Collins's recovery from his brain injuries, titled "The Possibilities Are Endless," was released in 2014. "And then there's the documentary from 2014 entitled Appropriately, The Possibilities Are Endless."
