**Dominant Musical Driver** : In the 21st century, hip hop became the "main musical driver of popular culture" and "king," extending its reach with strength and depth that previously belonged to rock. "Hip hop is king. Now, this does not mean that rock is dead, that it doesn't have a place in our lives, but it isn't going to be around for decades to come. But if you are honest, you will have to admit that hip hop has extended its reach into popular culture with the strength and depth that used to belong to rock."
**Most Growth in the 1990s** : Hip hop was the genre with the most growth in the 1990s, a decade when rock still ruled everything but saw hip hop's effects on alt rock begin to change the musical vibe. "It's just that other genres have exploded hip hop being the genre with the most growth."
**Crossover to White Audiences** : Hip hop, through groups like Body Count, Living Color, the Beastie Boys, and Rage Against the Machine, successfully reached "more white kids" (the rockers) and people of color who embraced the genre early. "rap and hip hop was able to reach both more white kids, the rockers, and people of color, those who embraced hip hop early."
**Infiltration of Mainstream Culture** : Hip hop and rap began to "slowly infiltrate the mainstream and start driving culture on all levels," evidenced by songs like Massive Attack's "Teardrop" going down smoothly with audiences. "You see what I mean about how hip hop and rap began to slowly infiltrate the mainstream and start driving culture on all levels?"
**Expansion of Alt Rock's Sound Palette** : By 1995, hip hop had dramatically increased the variety of sounds available to alt rockers, moving beyond just grunge punk and electronic sounds. "As we reached 1995 and thanks to hip-hop, the palette of sounds available to alt rockers had increased dramatically beyond just grunge punk in the various electronic sounds that had brought us this far."
**Impact on Alt Rock Evolution** : Hip hop and rap were crucial in allowing alt rock to "stretch even further" and "make the tent for fans even bigger," marking it as one of the most important musical evolutions of the 1990s. "hip hop and rap allowed alt rock to stretch even further and to make the tent for fans even bigger. That will go down as one of the most important musical evolutions of the 90s"

# genre - Rap

## curiosities
**Evolution in the 1990s** : In the 1990s, rap rhymes "got tougher and more complicated," and the overall vibe of the genre began to change, influencing alt rock. "Rhymes got tougher and more complicated. The whole vibe began to change."
**Reaching White Ears and Performers** : Rap, while huge on its own, increasingly saw its sound and attitude reach "white ears" and be adopted by "white rock performers," leading to a new sound that was part rock and part rap. "Rap was huge on its own, but more and more of that sound and attitude not only began to reach white ears, but that sound and attitude was adopted by white rock performers."
**Straight Up Rap Artists** : The 1990s included "straight up rap" artists such as Tupac, Biggie, Dre, and Snoop, alongside various blends and hybrids that crossed into the alternative world. "in addition to straight up rap like Tupac and Biggie and Dre and Snoop."

"One of the cool things about the people who were into alternative music was that they didn't carry some of the same perceptual baggage that mainstream rock fans had about rapping hip-hop."
**Acceptance of Hybrid Sounds** : As alternative music grew in popularity, more and more fans were willing to embrace the hybrid of rock and hip-hop, signaling "cool, bring it on!" "As their numbers grew and as alternative music became more and more popular, more and more people were willing to look at this hybrid of rock and hip-hop and go, cool, bring it on!"

"the palette of sounds available to alt rockers had increased dramatically beyond just grunge punk in the various electronic sounds that had brought us this far."

# genre - New Metal

## curiosities
**Polarizing Form of Alt-Rock** : New metal was characterized as "one of the most polarizing forms of alt-rock ever conceived," with some of it aging well and "the rest of it, not so much." "new metal, which was one of the most polarizing forms of alt-rock ever conceived. Some of it has aged okay. The rest of it, not so much."
**Late 1990s Band Signings** : The late 1990s saw numerous new metal bands being signed, indicating a peak in its commercial interest and label activity. "The late 1990s saw signing after signing after signing of new metal bands."
**Categorization Challenges** : Some bands initially categorized as new metal, such as Incubus, Puddle of Mud, Evanescence, Orgy, Papa Roach, and 3Days Grace, were easily digestible and eventually "escaped that classification," which many fought hard to do. "Some were easily digestible. And maybe some of them should have never been categorized under the heading of new metal to begin with."
**Intense Metal Leanings** : Other new metal bands, including Disturbed, Mudvayne, Lip knots, and System of a Down, were "much more intense because of their pronounced metal leanings." "Other bands were much more intense because of their pronounced metal leanings, disturbed, mud veins, lip knots, system of a down."
**Extremes of the Genre** : Some bands, like Limp Biscuit, pushed new metal "to its extremes and beyond." "And then there were those who took new metal to its extremes and beyond. Bands like Limp Biscuit."
**Industry Belief of Demise** : By the end of 1999, record labels had turned down Linkin Park's demos almost 50 times, reflecting the music industry's belief that new metal was "over." "that's how much the industry believed that new metal was over."

# curiosity

**Rapprochement Between Rockers and Rappers** : After years of mutual wariness, rockers and rappers in the 1990s discovered a significant amount of common ground and shared interests, leading to notable collaborations. "After years of being wary of each other, rockers and rappers found that they had a substantial amount of common ground and shared interests."
**Rap/Hip Hop's Expanding White Audience** : Thanks to pioneering groups like Body Count, Living Color, the Beastie Boys, and Rage Against the Machine, rap and hip hop successfully broadened its reach to encompass both white rock fans and people of color who were early adopters of the genre. "rap and hip hop was able to reach both more white kids, the rockers, and people of color, those who embraced hip hop early."
**Mystery of Crossover Songs** : The phenomenon of why certain individual songs, but not entire albums, managed to successfully cross over into the alternative music scene, while many others failed to do so, remained a hard-to-explain aspect of the era. "I still find it very hard to explain why some songs, and just songs and not full albums, managed to cross over into the alternative universe and why so many others did not."
**The 1990s as a Pre-Digital, Rock-Dominant Era** : The 1990s were marked as the final decade where rock music held a dominant position, preceding the widespread adoption of the internet, iPods, iTunes, and smartphones. "the 90s were the last decade where rock was the dominant form of music. It was also the last pre-internet decade, and the last decade, without things like iPods, iTunes and smartphones."
**Post-Gen Xer Music Consumption** : The emerging generation of "post-Gen Xers" approached music without preconceptions about rock's sound, having grown up with rap as an omnipresent genre. They favored loud, energetic, and angry music that sounded good on car stereos and headphones, often specifically enjoying music that their parents despised. "Fans ate this up, especially the emerging post-gen exers who had zero preconceptions about what rock should sound like."
**Generational Divide in Music Taste** : The preference of younger generations for music that their parents dislike, thereby establishing their own distinct musical identity, is a recurring "parent child dynamic over music" that has been observed for over a century. "This is all part of the parent child dynamic over music that's been repeated every generation for the past 100 years or longer."
**Modern Ubiquity of Rock-Rap Hybrids** : Today, the fusion of alt rock and hip hop is so commonplace that artists blending these styles are ubiquitous, and listeners often don't even recognize they're experiencing a hybrid that was considered radical and new until the 1990s. "It's so common that we often don't even notice that we're listening to a hybrid of styles that didn't exist until the late 80s and one that was considered radical and weird until we got into the 90s."
**Hip Hop's Role in Alt Rock's Expansion** : The advent of hip hop and rap significantly broadened the scope of alt rock, enabling it to "stretch even further" and enlarge its fanbase, making this influence one of the most critical musical evolutions of the 1990s. "hip hop and rap allowed alt rock to stretch even further and to make the tent for fans even bigger. That will go down as one of the most important musical evolutions of the 90s"
