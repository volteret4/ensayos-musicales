## curiosities
**First Hiatus (1995-1996)** : From September 1995 to some time in 1996, the band experienced a period that appeared to be a dissolution but was later considered a hiatus rather than a formal breakup. "There was a time from September 1995 to some time in 1996 where it looked like they were done, but they weren't."
**Guitarist's Exit Before First Breakup (1998)** : On June 7, 1998, after a show in Rysho, Dusseldorf, Germany, singer Richard Ashcroft had a physical altercation with guitarist Nick McCabe, resulting in McCabe suffering a broken hand. McCabe subsequently decided to leave the band and was replaced by session guitarist B.J. Cole for the remaining tour commitments. "Which face took a pounding and Nick ended up with a broken hand. And with that, Nick decided he was done."
**First Official Breakup (1999)** : The band's proper breakup was officially announced on April 28, 1999, though they had effectively ceased to exist since their final show in Ireland on August 29, 1998, which was performed with only most of the band members. "The proper breakup of the verve came on April 28th of 1999. That's when the official announcement was made."
**Eight-Year Separation and Reunion (2007)** : For eight years following their 1999 breakup, all members were adamant that a reunion would not happen. However, Nick McCabe began working with drummer Pete Salisbury, which prompted Richard Ashcroft to reconsider. Bass player Simon Jones was available, but guitarist Simon Tongue was deliberately excluded. The reunion was announced on June 26, 2007, after a period of eight years, one month, and 30 days apart. "For the next eight years, everyone was adamant that the verve would not reunite. The time tends to mellow people out."
**Second Breakup Over Solo Career Ambitions (2008)** : The reunion was initially successful, especially regarding live performances, but it dissolved again around August 10, 2008. The breakup allegedly occurred because Nick McCabe and Simon Jones believed that Richard Ashcroft was using the reunion to re-establish his solo career, leading to a major conflict. "Why? Well, because Nick McCabe and Simon Jones allegedly believed that Richard Ashcroft was using this reunion just to get his solo career happening again."

## curiosities
**Turbulent Early 90s and Breakup Factors** : The Pixies experienced a contentious period in the early 1990s marked by creative differences, power struggles, and an exhausting cycle of recording and touring. Tensions escalated during their 1992 stint as an opening act for U2's "Achtung Baby" tour, partly due to bassist Kim Deal's boyfriend, Jim Greer (a writer for Spin Magazine), documenting the band's experiences, and partly because the Pixies were a poor fit for U2's stadium audiences. "Tensions built all through the early 90s. There were a lot of creative differences. There were power struggles."
**Unceremonious Breakup via Fax (1993)** : After their U2 tour concluded in Vancouver on April 25, 1992, the band agreed to a one-year hiatus. However, on January 15, 1993, singer Black Francis unilaterally decided to leave and officially break up the band by sending a fax to their management company, a decision he later regretted. "But then on January 15, 1993, singer black france is decided that he wanted out for good. So how did he do it? Well he sent a fax to the management company saying that he was out and that the band is officially breaking up."
**Post-Breakup Careers and Challenges** : Following the breakup, members pursued diverse paths: Black Francis became Frank Black, releasing solo albums; Kim Deal transformed her side project, The Breeders, into a full-time band with her sister Kelly (who later faced legal issues after being busted for heroin); drummer David Lovering became "the scientific phenomenalist," a magician, but struggled financially; and guitarist Joey Santiago found success as a composer for TV and film, contributing to shows like "Weeds." "Everybody scattered. Black frances renamed himself Frank Black and released a string of solo albums, Kim Deel remade the breeders, her Pixies side project, into a full-time thing with her sister Kelly."
**Increased Fame While Apart** : During their separation, The Pixies paradoxically grew more famous than they had been when active, largely due to prominent bands like The Smashing Pumpkins and Nirvana publicly praising them, which helped build their "myth and legend." "And while they were apart, the Pixies had become more famous than they ever had been when they were together."
**Financial Motivation for Reunion (2004)** : The reunion was driven by both time-healed wounds and financial necessity, as members were not making substantial money individually. The band received lucrative offers for a reunion tour due to their elevated myth and legend. "Well, it was another case of time-healing wounds and besides no one separately was making much money."
**First Reunion Show (2004)** : The Pixies played their first show together since the 1992 U2 Vancouver concert on April 13, 2004, at the Fine Line Music Cafe in Minneapolis, marking a gap of 11 years, two months, and 30 days since Black Francis' breakup fax. "On April 13, 2004, the Pixies played together for the first time since that U2 show in Vancouver in 1992. The gig was at a club in Minneapolis."

## curiosities
**Post-Sex Pistols Experimental Vision** : After the Sex Pistols, Johnny Lydon formed Public Image Limited (PiL) with the aim of exploring more experimental rock, incorporating influences from reggae, dub, 1970s German rock, and funk musicians. "He was looking for a more experimental way of dealing with rock. He got deep into reggae and dub."
**Profound Influence on Multiple Genres** : PiL had a significant impact on several music genres, including industrial and punk funk. Bands such as Nine Inch Nails, Massive Attack, Manic Street Preachers, Ministry, and Primal Scream have acknowledged PiL's importance. "He hung out with some funk musicians, and eventually pulled together a band that ended up having a profound effect on several genres of music, including industrial and punk funk."
**Final Gig and Label-Induced Breakup (1992)** : PiL had a stormy existence from Christmas Day, 1978, until their last gig on September 18, 1992. The band effectively broke up because their label, Virgin Records, refused to provide further tour support. "It became their last show when their label, Virgin Records, refused to pay for any more tour support, so that was it."
**Reunion Motivated by Album Anniversary and Personal Tragedy (2009)** : In the summer of 2009, Johnny Lydon decided to revive Public Image Limited. This decision was influenced by the 30th-anniversary re-release of their influential "Metal Box" album, and also by emotional factors: the loss of his father to cancer in 2008 and the recurrence of his brother's cancer. These events reminded him of his mother's death during PiL's active period, which had inspired their song "Death Disco," suggesting "unfinished business." "The actual announcement came out on September the 7th of 2009. That was one reason. Another was a little more emotional."
**Reunion After Nearly 17 Years (2009)** : The band's reunion was officially announced on September 7, 2009, meaning they had been apart for 16 years, 11 months, and 20 days since their last gig. The reunion featured Johnny Lydon out front, but with different musicians, essentially making it "Johnny's band." "The time between that last show and the reunion was 16 years, 11 months, and 20 days. I guess we should play something for Metal Box, shouldn't we?"

**Financial Motivation for Reunions** : A common thread in many band reunion stories is money. Promoters are willing to pay significant sums for old bands to tour again, as there is substantial profit to be made from nostalgia. Reunions offer an excellent and quick way for musicians, who often lack a traditional pension plan, to replenish their retirement funds, with longer separations often leading to larger financial rewards. "There was a lot of money to be made in nostalgia. If you can keep everyone together long enough, and if you can keep everyone from killing each other, a reunion is an excellent way to top up the retirement fund very, very quickly."
**Time Healing Wounds for Reunions** : The passage of time often helps to mellow out interpersonal conflicts and allows band members to reconsider past animosities, paving the way for potential reunions. "The time tends to mellow people out."
**Post-Breakup Professional Diversification** : After band breakups, members often pursue varied careers outside of music, some finding success in new fields like television or even professional pumpkin growing, while others continue within the music industry in different capacities like composing or production.
