**Lust for Life (1977) – Title Track** : The title track features one of the "coolest rock beats ever recorded." The drum beat was inspired by the theme song of a German news program that Iggy Pop and David Bowie watched while trying to "straighten out their lives in Berlin." Bowie picked up on the theme and gave Iggy the idea for the beat. "Still one of the coolest rock beats ever recorded."

## curiosities
**Early Drumming Career – Motown Gigs** : In the mid-sixties, Iggy Pop began his musical career as a drummer for the Detroit band The Iguanas, playing gigs around Michigan and recording several songs. He was also hired locally by Motown acts like The Shangri-La's, The Temptations, and The Four Tops for their live gigs, as they toured with minimal musicians and hired bassists and drummers on location. He was known for being able to pick up almost any song within seconds. "Iggy was one of the guys These Motown acts would hire to play drums for them."
**Laying Punk Rock Foundations – Post-Drumming Career** : Iggy Pop eventually moved from behind the drum kit to start writing songs, leading to the formation of The Stooges. His career further evolved with involvement from David Bowie and significant personal experiences, which collectively laid the foundations for modern punk rock. "He started writing songs moved on to the stooges got involved with David Bowie took a lot of LSD and pretty much laid the foundations for modern punk rock."
**Godfather of Punk – Influence** : Iggy Pop, originally a drummer, is recognized as the "godfather of punk" and had a huge influence on the development of new rock music. "there's no disputing that Iggy pop a drummer had a huge influence on the development of new rock."

He acquired a snare drum and high hats by trading supermarket stamps his mom collected, allowing him to play along with Beatles records. At 13, his grandmother gave him a full drum set, which he expanded to match the size of Keith Moon's kit, who was his drumming hero. "Jeff got his mom to trade her stamps for a snare drum and a set of high hats so he could play along with his Beatles records."
**Transition from Drummer to Singer – Founding The Ramones** : In early 1974, Jeff Hyman was playing drums and singing in a band called Sniper. He then connected with John Cummings (guitar) and Douglas Colvin (bass), forming a new group that played its first gig on March 30, 1974. However, Hyman's drumming was too loud and fast for the band, and he proved to be a better singer than his bandmates. He moved to frontman, adopting the name Joey Ramone, while a friend named Tommy took over on a $100 drum set. "Jeff moved down front and became the singer and a friend named Tommy was forced to keep time using a hundred dollar drum set."
**Influential Punk Figure – Original Drummer** : Joey Ramone is recognized as one of the most influential and important figures in the history of punk rock. His foundational role in The Ramones began as the band's original drummer, before he transitioned to lead vocals. "Joey Ramon one of the most influential and important figures in the history of punk rock Was actually a drummer."

**Early Drumming Education – Joe Bonny and Lessons** : Larry Mullen started playing drums at the early age of nine. His first teacher was Joe Bonny, who was recognized at the time as Ireland's best-known drummer. Larry's mother supported his interest by purchasing a secondhand drum set for 17 pounds. Despite extensive formal lessons, he grew bored with the strict formality, rudiment exercises, sight-reading charts, and playing along to others' music. "his first teacher was a guy named Joe Bonny who at the time was Ireland's best known drummer and he taught Larry well."
**Pre-U2 Performances – Local and National Gigs** : Before he even entered high school, Larry Mullen was actively performing. He played in a band associated with the local post office that traveled around the country. He also had the honor of playing in the significant St. Patrick's Day parade in Dublin. "Before Larry even made it to high school. He was playing at a band at the local post office Which actually traveled around the country He also played in the big St. Patrick's Day parade in Dublin, which is a huge honor."
**Forming U2 – The Bulletin Board Note** : At age 15, frustrated with formal lessons, Larry Mullen pinned a note to the bulletin board at Mount Temple High School in Dublin. The note sought out other musicians who had "wasted money" on guitars. This initiative led him to approach Adam Clayton, and later attracted Dave and Dick Evans (The Edge) and Paul Husson (Bono), leading to the band's first rehearsal in Larry's kitchen in the autumn of 1976. "one day when he was 15 Larry pinned a note to the bulldozen board at Mount Temple High School in Dublin The note said money wasted on a drum kit Were there others out there who had wasted money on guitars."
**Father's Advice – Irish Army Band** : Larry Mullen's father, Larry Mullen Senior, was very supportive of his son's drumming ambitions. However, he once advised Larry to join the Irish army for seven years, suggesting that this path would allow him to play in the army band and learn to play all percussion instruments. "Larry Mullen's senior was very supportive of his son's drumming ambitions but his advice was uh Well, he almost had his son convince to join the Irish army for seven years so he could play in the band And learn to play all the percussion instruments."

Instead of sitting behind a conventional drum kit, she stood up to play. She used felt mallets instead of traditional sticks and laid her bass drum on its side to play it with mallets, similar to a marching bass drum. She also "hardly used symbols." "Most style was On orthodox instead of sitting down behind a conventional kit she stood up and played everything with felt mallets instead of sticks."
**Inspiration for Style – Nigerian Drum Virtuoso** : Maureen Tucker stated that her distinctive drumming style and setup, which included playing while standing and using felt mallets, were inspired by a Nigerian drum virtuoso. "Mo says her style and setup was inspired by a Nigerian drum virtuoso."
**Breaking Gender Barriers – First Female Rock Drummer (Retrospectively)** : When Maureen Tucker joined The Velvet Underground in 1965, there were no known women drummers in rock music; playing drums was widely considered a "man's job." She therefore broke significant gender barriers, though this pioneering role was largely unnoticed at the time, possibly due to her unconventional drumming setup. Only in retrospect has she received due recognition as a musical pioneer. "Mo broke down some serious gender barriers But oddly enough no one really took any notice at the time probably because she wasn't setting upper drums properly anyway So only in retrospect that Mo got her due as a musical pioneer."
**Joining The Velvet Underground – Unique Circumstances** : Maureen Tucker was the last member to join The Velvet Underground. She was drafted into the band after their initial drummer quit and relocated to Nepal, where he later died of malnutrition. "She was drafted after the velvet's first drummer quit and moved to Nepal Where he would eventually die of male nutrition, but that's another story."

**Early Life and Engineering Background – War and Drums** : Jim Marshall was born in England in 1923. He initially worked in his family's fish and chip shop. Due to health reasons, he was unable to enlist in the war. Instead, he studied to become an engineer. During this time, he also turned to playing the drums and quickly became very proficient. "Jim Was born in England back in 1923 and for a while he worked in the family's fish and chip shop Unable to enlist in the war for health reasons he studied to be an engineer Any turn to the drums and Jim became very very good very very quickly."
**Influential Drum Teacher – Students Became Legends** : Jim Marshall became a highly respected drum teacher, and his students included several notable drummers. Among them were Mitch Mitchell, who would later become the drummer for Jimi Hendrix, and Mickey Waller, who played in bands with Jeff Beck, Rod Stewart, and Jimmy Page. "Over the years his students included Mitch Mitchell the guy who would later go on to back up Jimmy Hendrix There was Mickey Waller who played in bands with Jeff Beck and Rob Stewart and Jimmy Page."
**Drum Shop Owner and Gear Innovator – London, 1960** : In 1960, Jim Marshall opened his own drum shop in London. In addition to continuing to perform and teach lessons, he began selling musical gear. As an engineer with an interest in sound and amplification, he notably created and sold giant speaker cabinets that he made at home. "Things were going so well that in 1960 Jim opened his own drum shop in London Not only did he continue to perform and teach lessons But it began to sell gear including some giant speaker cabinets that he made at home."
**Invention of the Marshall Amplifier – Responding to Rock and Roll Needs** : Marshall's store sold American amplifiers like Fender, but customers began complaining that these were inadequate for the emerging sound of "rock and roll." In 1963, Jim Marshall and his staff developed their own amplifier design, aiming for the "loudest, cleanest, most rugged amplifier they could make." This amplifier, along with speaker cabinets featuring 12-inch and 19-inch speakers, became hugely popular with British musicians and established Marshall as standard gear. "in 1963 Jim and his staff came up with the loudest cleanest most rugged amplifier they could make."
**Legacy of the Marshall Stack – Drummer Invented Guitar Icon** : Jim Marshall, a drummer by profession and an engineer by training, conceived, invented, manufactured, and marketed the renowned Marshall amplifier and the iconic Marshall stack. This gear became the "dream gear for millions of guitar players around the world," solidifying his legacy as an inventor. "Did I mention that Jim's last name is Marshall? Yeah, the famous Marshall amplifier the famous Marshall stack the dream gear for millions of guitar players around the world Was conceived invented manufactured and marketed by a drummer."

# instrument - Amplifier

## curiosities
**Marshall Amplifier Innovation – Responding to Rock and Roll Demand** : The renowned Marshall amplifier was conceived, invented, manufactured, and marketed by Jim Marshall, a drummer and engineer. In 1963, in response to customer complaints that American amps like Fender were insufficient for the "new-ish thing called rock and roll," Marshall and his staff created the "loudest, cleanest, most rugged amplifier they could make." This invention, along with associated speaker cabinets, became standard gear for rock guitarists. "This amp and the speaker cabinets with 12 inch and 19 inch speakers became a big hit with the new generation of British musicians and Jim stuff is still standard gear for guitar players who want to go big or go home."
