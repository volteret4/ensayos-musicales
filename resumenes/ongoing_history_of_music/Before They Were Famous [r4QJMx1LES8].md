# curiosity
**Challenges of Archiving Early Music**: Historically, finding early musical material was difficult due to tapes being locked away, erased for reuse, lost in personal storage, or going out of print. Furthermore, legal disputes over ownership often kept recordings unheard, while fires and water damage also destroyed archives. "Finding this material used to be hard. Tapes were locked away in vaults. Other early music on tape was erased, recorded over so that the tape could be reused."
**Digital Technology's Role in Music Preservation**: The advent of the internet, file-sharing sites like Napster, YouTube, and the trend of box sets and reissues by record labels has significantly transformed the preservation of early music. This digital era allows demos, alternate takes, and lost recordings to be found and preserved for posterity, making less material disappear. "Then the internet hit. Slowly, first through file sharing sites like Napster, these demos and alternate takes and long lost recordings started changing hands. And then came YouTube. That's a treasure trove of old music."
**Record Labels' Catalog Exploration**: As physical music sales decline, record labels are increasingly delving deep into their back catalogs to unearth old material for box sets and reissues, aiming to entice fans to purchase high-margin physical products. "As physical music sales drop, record labels are looking deep into the catalogs to find stuff that might entice fans to buy high-march and physical product."
