Ad-Rock picked up a guitar and Mike D sat behind the drums to hash it out, with a working title of "Chris Rock" because a sound engineer named Chris thought the song "really rocked." ## curiosities
**"Sabotage" Music Video (1994) – 70s Crime Show Parody** : The music video for "Sabotage" was directed by Spike Jonze, who had previously photographed skateboarders, and featured a concept devised by the Beastie Boys themselves. It parodied 1970s crime shows, complete with all the clichés and "bad moustaches." The video was shot with a skeleton crew, fitting everyone into one van, and without permits, allowing for quick filming on the streets of Los Angeles with a relatively tiny budget compared to other videos of the era. "The Beastie Boys came up with the concept that parodied 70's crime shows, complete with all the clichés and bad moustaches."
**"Sabotage" Video Awards (1994)** : The music video for "Sabotage" was a major hit on all video channels and garnered significant industry recognition. It won five trophies at the 1994 MTV Video Music Awards, in addition to awards from Billboard and at the Music Video Production Awards. "It won five trophies at the 1994 MTV Video Music Awards."
**"Sabotage" Video Influence on Film** : The "Sabotage" video, directed by Spike Jonze, is credited by some with creating a template for future film and television projects. This influence is seen in movies such as *Anchorman*, projects like Lonely Island, and even the entire concept of the Adult Swim cable channel. "Some people even credit the sabotage video as creating the template for movies like Anchorman, projects like Lonely Island, and even the entire concept of the Adult Swim cable channel."
