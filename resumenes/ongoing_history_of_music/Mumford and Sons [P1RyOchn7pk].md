## albums
**I Will Waits (from Babel, 2nd album)** : This song is noted as one of Mumford & Sons' major hits from their second album, *Babel*. "one of their major hits from Babel, their second album."
**Self-titled or Lennie Your Eyes (2008) - Debut EP** : Released on July 7th, 2008, by the boutique London label Chess Club. It was issued on 10-inch vinyl in very limited quantities, estimated at around 500 copies. The 4-track EP was recorded in a basement studio called House of Strange, located underneath a dog biscuit factory. "Mumford & Sons with Lire from that ultra-rare vinyl copy of their 4-track debut EP, released July 7th, 2008."
**Love Your Ground (2008) - Second EP** : Released on November 3rd, 2008, as a 10-inch vinyl record, again on Chess Club, and also limited to 500 copies. This record was made with more production time than their debut EP and was recorded at a country mansion owned by Ben Lovett's parents. It featured four listed tracks and one hidden song. "Not Early Mumford & Sons this time from the Love Your Ground EP, released as a 10-inch vinyl record on November 3rd, 2008."
**The Cave and the Open Sea (2009) - Third Indie EP** : Issued on April 6, 2009, on Chess Club as a 10-inch vinyl, with an even more limited run of only 300 copies. The first 100 copies were numbered and signed by every member of the group, making them a "holy grail" for superfans. This EP contained only two songs on Side 1, "The Cave" and "Hold On to What You Believe," with Side 2 featuring artwork etched into the vinyl. "That recording was taken from the Cave and the Open Sea EP, released on April 6, 2009."
**Sigh No More (2009) - Debut Studio Album** : Released on October 2nd, 2009, this album was recorded at Eastcoat Studios with producer Marcus Draves. The title is derived from a song performed by Balthazar in Shakespeare's *Much Ado About Nothing*. The album incorporates themes and references from Shakespeare (including direct lines from the 1623 text), the Bible, Homer's *The Odyssey*, a biography of St. Francis of Assisi, the teachings of Socrates, and John Steinbeck's *The Grapes of Wrath* and *East of Eden*. It became a massive hit, selling over a million copies in the UK, more than 2 million in the US, and approximately 200,000 copies in Canada where it reached number two on the charts. Worldwide sales exceeded 6 million. "Sinomore was released on October 2nd, 2009."

## songs
**White Blank Page (2007)** : A song recorded by the proto-Mumford & Sons group (Marcus, Ted, Winston, with Ben) in the fall of October 2007. Ben Lovett reportedly found playing this material more fulfilling than his work with his then-band, Hot Rocket. "That's Proto Mumford & Son's from October 2007 and White Blank Page."
**Lire (2008)** : A track from Mumford & Sons' ultra-rare 4-track debut EP, which was either self-titled or called *Lennie Your Eyes*, released on July 7th, 2008. "Mumford & Sons with Lire from that ultra-rare vinyl copy of their 4-track debut EP, released July 7th, 2008."
**Feel the Tie (2008)** : This song was featured on the *Love Your Ground* EP, which was released as a 10-inch vinyl record on November 3rd, 2008. "Not Early Mumford & Sons this time from the Love Your Ground EP, released as a 10-inch vinyl record on November 3rd, 2008."
**Hold On to What You Believe (2008/2009)** : This song appeared on two of Mumford & Sons' early EPs: *Love Your Ground* from the Fall of 2008 and *The Cave and the Open Sea*, released on April 6, 2009. "Hold On to What You Believe from Mumford & Sons."
**The Cave (2009/2010)** : A crowd favorite, "The Cave" was included on *The Cave and the Open Sea* EP (April 6, 2009) and later released as the third single from the *Sigh No More* album on February 26, 2010. The song contains religious references and imagery and received four Grammy nominations. "From the number three from Sino More, Mumford & Sons and The Cave."
**Little Lion Man (2009) - Re-recorded Version** : Originally featured on the *Love Your Ground* EP, this song was re-recorded for the *Sigh No More* album at the suggestion of producer Marcus Draves. It was almost omitted from the album but Winston Marshall insisted on its inclusion. Released as the first single on August 11, 2009, it initially had limited chart impact but has since sold over three million digital copies. Marcus Mumford states its genesis is rooted in a personal story he is not proud of, though it appears to be inspired by Alexander Hamilton. "Little Lion Man became the first single released on August 11, 2009."
**Winter Winds (2009)** : Released as the second single from *Sigh No More* on December 6, 2009. Upon its release, it accumulated 250,000 views on YouTube within approximately 12 hours, breaking a previous record held by Lady Gaga by more than 25%. "Winter Winds from Mumford & Sons, the second to four singles from that debut record."

## curiosities
**Early Perception at Island Records (2008)** : In an early 2008 meeting at Island Records, Mumford & Sons were initially dismissed as an unconventional "banjo band" or "dustbin merchants" whose use of an accordion was seen as highly unorthodox, reflecting a label's skepticism about their potential in the alternative music market. "Next thing you'll tell me is that they have an accordion player."
**Band Formation and Scouting (2007)** : The band was formed in December 2007 and was scouted by Lewis Bloom of Island Records shortly after. Bloom repeatedly saw them perform until he deemed them ready for a record deal. "He scouted Mumford and son, surely after the band was formed in December 2007."
**Multi-Award Winning Status** : Mumford & Sons have achieved significant success, becoming a multi-platinum, Grammy-winning, and Brit-winning band. "A multi-platinum, Grammy winning, Brit winning band that plays the banjo."
**Marcus Mumford's Family Background** : Marcus Mumford's English parents, John (a former "drug-taken hippie" turned ordained minister) and Eleanor (a proponent of faith healing), lived in Orange County, California, where they established a branch of the Vinyard Churches, a Christian evangelical organization, before moving back to London. "His English parents, John, a former drug-taken hippie turned ordained minister, and Eleanor, a proponent of faith healing, were living in Orange County, California at the time, right in the hearts of American punk rock country."
**Marcus Mumford's Musical Influences** : Growing up, Marcus Mumford was exposed to Bob Dylan's music (especially his "God Love and Era" and the *O Brother, Where Art Thou?* soundtrack) and various jazz. He played in a jazz group as a teenager. "As he grew up, Marcus was exposed to the music of Bob Dylan, especially his God Love and Era, the old brother were at the O Soundtrack, and a variety of jazz."
**Marcus Mumford's Career Path Change** : After attending Captain Kick and the Cowboy Ramblers' performance at The Bosens Locker, Marcus Mumford was so impressed that he dropped out of Edinburgh University, where he was expected to follow his parents into the ministry, and committed to music as his vocation. "Marcus was so impressed by what he heard and saw that he dropped out of university and vowed to make music his vocation."
**Marcus Mumford's Crisis of Faith (2009)** : During the recording of *Sigh No More* in 2009, Marcus Mumford experienced a "crisis of faith," struggling to reconcile the allure of the "rock and roll world" with his parents' evangelical Christian beliefs. "And to make things even weirder, Marcus was having a crisis of faith."
**Winston Marshall's Elite Upbringing** : Winston Marshall is the son of a highly successful hedge fund manager who managed over 100 million pounds in assets and advised Rupert Murdoch on acquisitions. Despite attending a private school with 30,000 pounds annual tuition, Winston adopted a distinctive "rastaffarian hip-hop gangster" style, complete with dreadlocks. "Winston Marshall, the son of a tremendously successful hedge fund manager that looked after more than 100 million pounds in assets."
**Ted Dwane's Musical Education** : Ted Dwane is the son of a self-made construction mogul and, in addition to playing the banjo, holds a music school diploma in bass performance. "He also played the banjo, but he actually had a music school diploma in bass performance."
**Ben Lovett Leaves Hot Rocket for Mumford & Sons (2007)** : In October 2007, despite his band Hot Rocket having a successful indie EP and a sold-out UK tour, Ben Lovett chose to leave Hot Rocket, his record deal, and the tour to join the nascent "foaky thing" that would become Mumford & Sons, feeling a stronger connection to their music. "So he made a decision."
**Name Origin and Meaning** : The band evolved from "Marcus Mumford and his Mary Men" to "Mumford & Sons." This name choice, influenced by a trend of bands using "Sons" in their names, was appealing because it evoked the image of a traditional, family-owned general store or antique furniture shop. "So it came to pass that Mumford and his merry men became Mumford & Sons."
**Self-Financed Debut Album (2009)** : For their debut album, the band was required to finance the entire production themselves, an estimated 20,000 pounds, and create their own indie label as part of a deal with Lewis Bloom and Island Records. "The total for everything will be about 20,000 pounds and it will be all your money."
**Unique Sound in 2009 Musical Landscape** : In 2009, Mumford & Sons' English folk sound, featuring accordions, banjos, and ukuleles, was considered "a million miles away from what anyone else was offering," providing a stark contrast to the dominant alt-rock bands and pop stars like Beyonce, Katy Perry, and Justin Bieber. "What Mumford was offering the world in 2009 was a million miles away from what anyone else was offering."
**Debut Album Cover Photo (2009)** : The *Sigh No More* album cover, featuring all four band members in the window of Pimpernell & Partners (a 19th-century furniture shop at 596 The Kings Road, London), was shot by Max Knight in Summer 2009. The photograph took nearly eight hours to capture due to challenges such as a very hot day, the band arriving late, a busy street requiring clear shots (no cars, pedestrians, or cats), sun reflections ruining takes, and distractions from drunk Wimbledon watchers in a neighboring pub. "It's all four members of the band in the window of a shop at 596, The Kings Road, and the full area of London."
**Overall Career Success** : Beyond their debut, Mumford & Sons have sold well over 10 million albums and accumulated numerous awards and major festival appearances. "Now they've sold well over 10 million albums and have a ton of awards and major festival appearances to their credit."

# label - Island Records

## curiosities
**Initial Skepticism for Mumford & Sons (2008)** : In early 2008, an unnamed executive at Island Records expressed significant doubt about signing Mumford & Sons, dismissing them as a "banjo band" and "dustbin merchants" and questioning their potential as "the next big alternative band" due to their unconventional instrumentation. "Good Lord Smith. This is 2008. We need the next big alternative band."
**Lewis Bloom's Persistence and Scouting** : Lewis Bloom of the A&R department at Island Records relentlessly scouted Mumford & Sons after their formation in December 2007. He observed them repeatedly until he was confident they were ready for a deal, eventually convincing the label despite initial reservations. "He scouted Mumford and son, surely after the band was formed in December 2007."
**Unconventional Debut Album Deal** : Island Records, impressed by the self-financed *Sigh No More* album, negotiated a licensing deal where they licensed the album from Mumford & Sons' indie label, Gentlemen of the Road. Island agreed to cover all manufacturing, distribution, and promotion costs, while the band received only a licensing fee without subsequent profits, deemed the "safest way forward" at the time as it required no upfront money or liability from the band. "Island then negotiated a deal whereby they licensed the album from Mumford through their gentlemen of the road indie label."

# curiosity

**Alexander Hamilton - "The Little Lion of the American Revolution"** : One of the American founding fathers, depicted on the US$20 bill, and known by the nickname "The Little Lion of the American Revolution." He was gay during a period when homosexuality was considered a crime, and his story is a suggested inspiration for Mumford & Sons' song "Little Lion Man." "He was known as The Little Lion of the American Revolution."
**Shakespeare - Much Ado About Nothing** : The play from which the title of Mumford & Sons' debut album, *Sigh No More*, is derived. The phrase comes from a song performed by the character Balthazar, "Sino More, ladies, Sino More, men were deceivers ever." The album also incorporates lines and phrases directly from the 1623 text. "If you know your Shakespeare, you may know that the phrase Sino More is taken from a song performed by Balthazar in Muchadu about nothing."
**O Brother, Where Art Thou? Soundtrack** : This soundtrack was a significant musical influence on both Marcus Mumford and Winston Marshall, particularly its "God Love and Era" aspects and bluegrass elements, serving as a common ground for their early musical discussions. "Marcus was exposed to the music of Bob Dylan, especially his God Love and Era, the old brother were at the O Soundtrack, and a variety of jazz."
**Lewis Bloom - Island Records A&R Scout** : A key figure in Mumford & Sons' early career, Bloom, from the A&R department at Island Records, scouted the band in late 2007. He patiently nurtured them, waiting until they had a strong body of songs and stage confidence before proposing an unconventional deal where they self-financed their debut album, which Island then licensed. "First, you really have to hand it to Lewis Bloom of the A&R department at Island Records in London."
**Adam Tuthoep - Band Manager** : Mumford & Sons' manager whose ambition played a crucial role in the band's breakthrough. Even before they secured a record deal, he managed to obtain a promise from renowned producer Marcus Draves to produce their debut album, a feat that ultimately convinced Island Records to engage with the band. "The thing that really did it for him though was manager Adam Tuthobe's ambition."
**Marcus Draves - Producer** : A highly regarded producer known for his work on two Coldplay albums, one Bjork album, and Arcade Fire's *Neon Bible*, and his involvement in *The Suburbs 2* by early 2009. He produced Mumford & Sons' debut album, *Sigh No More*, and was instrumental in suggesting the re-recording of "Little Lion Man." "Even before the band had been signed to any label, he had somehow managed to get a promise from producer Marcus Draves to produce Mumford's debut album."
**The Bosens Locker - London Bar** : A tiny, 40-person bar located beneath a pastry shop in London where Winston Marshall's band, Captain Kick and the Cowboy Ramblers, regularly performed. Marcus Mumford was so impressed by a performance there that he dropped out of university to pursue music. The venue was eventually shut down. "Captain Kick and Crew played in a tiny 40-person bar called the Bosens Locker, which was located below a pastry shop in London."
**Communion - Ben Lovett's Bar** : After The Bosens Locker closed, Ben Lovett opened his own small bar named Communion, which had a capacity of around 200 people. It became a venue for his band, Hot Rocket, and also for Marcus Mumford and his Mary Men, fostering the environment where Mumford & Sons would eventually form. "That's when Ben launched a new place himself, another small bar called Communion."
**House of Strange - Recording Studio** : The basement studio where Mumford & Sons' first EP (Self-titled or *Lennie Your Eyes*) was recorded in 2008. The studio was built underneath a factory that manufactured dog biscuits, which is noted as the reason for its unusual name. "It was recorded in a basement studio built underneath the factory that made dog biscuits."
**Pimpernell & Partners - Antique Furniture Shop** : Located at 596 The Kings Road in London, this shop specializes in 19th-century furniture and served as the backdrop for the *Sigh No More* album cover photo. The shop's proprietors reportedly do not appreciate discussing the album cover, deeming it "not relevant to our business." "The place is called Pimperno on Partners, it's a shop that specializes in 19th century furniture."
