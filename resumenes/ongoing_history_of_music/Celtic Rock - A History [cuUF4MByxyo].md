The Celts, and there are a group of Celtic ethnicities stretching across the British Isles under this umbrella term, had shared histories, but also developed their own languages and traditions and cultures. The Celtic nations are considered to be Ireland, Scotland, Wales, the Isle of Man, Cornwall, and Brittany in France."
**Pen-celticism Identity** : Despite the Isle of Man and Cornwall falling under English jurisdiction, and Brittany being a region of France (which meant Celtic traditions were less emphasized in their local scenes), these three places were recognized as part of the broader Celtic community, fostering an identity known as "pen-celticism." "However, these three places were nevertheless recognized as part of the Celtic community, and thus an identity that became known as pen-celticism."
