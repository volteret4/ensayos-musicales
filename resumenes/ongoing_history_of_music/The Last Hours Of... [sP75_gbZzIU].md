Back at the hotel, his bodyguard, Martin Kirsten, gave him two Ativan pills, for which Chris had a prescription. Shortly after, Chris spoke to his wife Vicki, who noted his slurred speech; he claimed to have taken "too much Ativan" and was "just tired." He abruptly hung up, alarming Vicki. Martin Kirsten, called by Vicki, found Chris's room latched from the inside. After kicking open both the room and bathroom doors, he found Chris. **Events Leading to Discovery (Late May 17 - Early May 18, 2017)** : After the Soundgarden show, Chris Cornell returned to his hotel room (1136 at the MGM Grand) with his bodyguard, Martin Kirsten. Kirsten helped with Cornell's laptop and gave him two prescribed Ativan pills before leaving at 11:35 PM. Soon after, Cornell spoke with his wife Vicki on the phone, his speech slurred, saying he "probably took too much Ativan" and was "just tired," before abruptly hanging up. Alarmed, Vicki called Kirsten, who returned to the room at 12:15 AM. Finding the door latched from the inside, Kirsten, after being denied entry by security, kicked open the door. He found the bathroom door also locked and kicked that open, discovering Cornell on the floor with blood from his mouth and a red exercise band around his neck, attached to a carabiner jammed into the doorframe. "Martin left Chris' room at around 11.35. Soon after that, Chris spoke with his wife Vicki on the phone. She says his speech was slurred."
**Toxicology Report and Contributing Factors (Post-May 2017)** : Chris Cornell's toxicology report revealed several substances in his system: four doses of Ativan (an anti-anxiety medication he had a prescription for), pseudoephedrine (a common over-the-counter decongestant), butylbital (a sedative and strong painkiller), caffeine (from No-Doz tablets), and Narcan (likely administered by paramedics, who might have initially suspected an opioid overdose).
