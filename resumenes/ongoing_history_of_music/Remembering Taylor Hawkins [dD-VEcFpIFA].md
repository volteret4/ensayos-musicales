## curiosities
**606 Studios - Band Headquarters** : The Foo Fighters' headquarters, hangout, and nerve center located in Van Nuys, California. This studio was the setting for an interview regarding the "Sonic Highways" album and TV series. "This is 606 Studios, the band's Hangout and Nerve Center in Van Nies, California."
**William Goldsmith's Departure** : Drummer William Goldsmith was replaced by Taylor Hawkins after difficulties during the recording sessions for "The Colour and the Shape" album. Dave Grohl was reportedly unhappy with Goldsmith's performance and re-recorded all his drum parts, prompting the change. "Dave even went in and re-recorded all of Goldsmith's parts."
**Taylor Hawkins Joins the Band (1997)** : On March 18, 1997, it was officially announced that Taylor Hawkins was joining the Foo Fighters. He transitioned from being the drummer for Alanis Morissette, who at the time was a bigger act, to an up-and-coming rock band featuring the former Nirvana drummer, Dave Grohl. "So on March 18th, 1997, it was announced that Taylor was officially a food fighter."
**Taylor Hawkins' Unique Drumming Style** : Taylor Hawkins brought a distinct drumming style to the Foo Fighters, influenced by prog rock. His approach was subtly different and gave the band an "extra" dimension compared to other drummers like Dave Grohl, who was known for a hard-hitting style akin to John Bonham. "Taylor could play just as hard, but his style brought in a few prog rock influences, something that gave the foods something extra."
**Taylor Hawkins as the "Most Important" Member After Dave Grohl** : Taylor Hawkins was considered by many, next to Dave Grohl, to be the most important member of the Foo Fighters, a beloved fan favorite. Despite Dave Grohl being the chief songwriter and frontman, Taylor was allowed to "steal the show" and was a crucial personality within the group. "And next to Dave, Taylor was, I dare say, the most important, a fan favorite, beloved by fans."
**Taylor Hawkins' Lead Vocals** : Taylor Hawkins was the only member other than Dave Grohl to sing lead on Foo Fighters songs. He sang on covers like Pink Floyd's "Have a Cigar" (with Brian May on guitar), Cream's "I Feel Fine," and Joe Walsh's "Life of Illusion," as well as original songs like "Cold Day in the Sun" and "Sunday Rain." "Just to show you how special a place Taylor Hawkins had within the food fighters, he is the only guy other than Dave Grohl to sing lead on a food song."
**Band Cancels All 2022 Shows After Taylor Hawkins' Death** : Following Taylor Hawkins' death on March 25, 2022, the Foo Fighters canceled all their scheduled shows for the remainder of the year. This decision reflected a period of healing, grieving, and reorganizing for the band, leaving their future uncertain. "The Fufighters have canceled all their shows for the rest of 2022."
**Uncertain Future Post-Taylor Hawkins' Death** : The Foo Fighters face an uncertain future following the death of Taylor Hawkins, who was considered irreplaceable due to his chemistry and friendship with Dave Grohl and his essential role in the band's "family nature." Comparisons are drawn to Led Zeppelin, which disbanded after John Bonham's death, and Rush, which retired after Neil Peart's death. "What do the Fufighters do now? We don't know."
**Dave Grohl and Taylor Hawkins' Close Friendship** : Dave Grohl and Taylor Hawkins shared an exceptionally close bond, described as "brothers from another mother," with Dave stating he'd "take a bullet for Taylor." Their friendship was essential to keeping Dave grounded and played a significant role in the overall well-being and dynamics of the band. "Second, Taylor was Dave's best friend in the whole world."

## curiosities
**Early Life and Move to California** : Taylor Hawkins was born in Fort Worth, Texas, on February 17, 1972. His family relocated to California when he was approximately four years old, shaping his upbringing in Orange County. "He's a Texas boy born in Fort Worth on February 17th, 1972."
**First Concert: Queen (1982)** : At the age of 10 in 1982, Taylor Hawkins attended his first concert, which was Queen. This experience profoundly impacted him, sparking his lifelong obsession with rock and roll and his dream of being in a huge rock band. "In 1982, when he was 10, he attended his first concert. And that was Queen."
**Beginnings as a Drummer** : Taylor Hawkins started playing drums in 1982, the same year he attended his first Queen concert. He consistently credited his mother for her encouragement, which fostered his ambition to dream big in his musical pursuits. "That was the year he also started playing drums."
**Sylvia - First Group in Orange County** : Taylor Hawkins' first musical group was called Sylvia, formed in Orange County, California. This marked his initial foray into playing with a band, navigating a local music scene that predominantly favored reggae. "His first group was in Orange County, if it called Sylvia."
**First Paid Music Gig** : Taylor Hawkins got his first paid gig playing music at a wedding. Although the band initially wasn't paid, his mother intervened to ensure they received their money, a moment he remembered as when he realized he could make a living from music. "That was the first time I got paid to play music."
**Influence of Jane's Addiction** : Taylor Hawkins cited Jane's Addiction as a major influence that changed his musical direction in high school. Prior to hearing them, he listened to older classic rock and new wave, but Jane's Addiction's raw, "disgusting," and "awesome" style resonated with him, inspiring him to be like them. "And then when I heard Jane's addiction, because I was really... It was all warrant and all that kind of crap, you know."
**Joining Sass Jordan's Backup Band (1994)** : In 1994, Taylor Hawkins secured a gig in the backup band for Canadian singer Sass Jordan after meeting someone at a music store who connected him. This opportunity allowed him to go on tour to Europe, marking his first experience in a rock and roll touring band. "Eventually he got a gig in the backup band for Canadian singer, Sass Jordan."
**Drummer for Alanis Morissette (1995)** : After Alanis Morissette's "Jagged Little Pill" album blew up in 1995, Taylor Hawkins became her drummer at age 23 for her "Can't Not" tour. He appeared in several of her music videos and performed with her during her debut on David Letterman in 1995. "That Sass Jordan job morphed into a gig with Alannis Morissette."
**Leaving Alanis Morissette for Foo Fighters** : While touring with Alanis Morissette, Taylor Hawkins frequently crossed paths with the Foo Fighters at summer festivals and quickly bonded with Dave Grohl. Alanis eventually asked him when he was going to quit and join the Foo Fighters, which he did, despite Alanis being a bigger act at the time. "It got to the point where Alannis asked, when are you just going to quit and join the food fighters?"
**Drumming Influences** : Taylor Hawkins' drumming style was heavily influenced by progressive rock drummers such as Neil Peart of Rush, Stewart Copeland of The Police, and Phil Collins during his time with Genesis. Other influences included Stephen Perkins of Jane's Addiction, Ringo Starr, and his hero, Roger Taylor of Queen. "His heroes were Neil Pirate of Rush, Stuart Copeland of the police, and Phil Collins when he was with Genesis."
**Guest Appearances and Collaborations** : Taylor Hawkins was known for his willingness to assist other bands and artists. He performed on albums by Coheed and Cambria (also touring with them), Eric Avery (Jane's Addiction bassist), Chris Shiflett (Foo Fighter, for Jackson United project), Slash, Ozzy Osborne, and Elton John. "And like his boss and friend, Taylor was always willing to help out other bands."
**Taylor Hawkins and the Co-Tale Riders (2006)** : In 2006, Taylor Hawkins formed his own band, Taylor Hawkins and the Co-Tale Riders, a name reflecting his sense of humor and humbleness. The band featured crack session musicians and released three albums, collaborating with figures like Roger Taylor and Brian May (Queen), Nancy Wilson (Heart), Joe Walsh (Eagles), Chrissie Hynde (Pretenders), and LeAnn Rimes. "The first came in 2006 when he formed a band called Taylor Hawkins and the Co-Tale Riders."
**SOS All Stars (2007) - One-Off Drummer Supergroup** : Taylor Hawkins participated in SOS All Stars, a special one-off drummers' group formed for the Live Earth show in London in 2007. He was joined by Roger Taylor of Queen and Chad Smith of the Red Hot Chili Peppers, showcasing his connections within the drumming community. "In addition to the Coat Tale writers, there was SOS All Stars."
**The Birds of Satan (2014) - Prog Rock Side Project** : In 2014, Taylor Hawkins formed another side project called The Birds of Satan, a band with a strong prog rock sound. The group introduced itself with a 10-minute epic song titled "The Ballad of the Birds of Satan" and featured appearances from Dave Grohl, Pat Smear, and Foo Fighters keyboardist Rami Jaffee. "In 2014, he formed a side project called The Birds of Satan, a very prog rock band that introduced itself with a 10-minute epic song entitled The Ballad of the Birds of Satan."
**NHC (2022) - Most Recent Project** : Taylor Hawkins' most recent musical project was NHC, where 'H' stood for Hawkins. The 'N' was Dave Navarro and the 'C' was Chris Cheney, both from Jane's Addiction. Taylor described the band's sound as landing "somewhere between Rush and the Faces," and they released the song "Feed the Cruel" in early 2022. "His most recent project was called NHC."
**Death (March 25, 2022)** : Taylor Hawkins died on Friday, March 25, 2022, in Bogotá, Colombia, at the Four Seasons Casa Medina. An ambulance was called after reports of severe chest pains, but a private doctor could not help, and he was already gone by the time EMS arrived. "Taylor left us. The Fouce fighters were touring South America, and were about to perform at the Estarial picnic in Boca-Tac, Colombia on Friday March 25th, 2022."
**Toxicology Report Findings** : A preliminary toxicology report after Taylor Hawkins' death indicated the presence of 10 different substances in his system, including weed, opioids, and antidepressants. While many of these could have been prescription medicines, the report also noted an enlarged heart. "A preliminary toxicology report said that there were 10 different substances in Taylor's system, ranging from weed and opioids to antidepressants."
**Enlarged Heart** : The early report following Taylor Hawkins' death stated that his heart was enlarged, weighing 600 grams, which is twice the normal size for a person of his age and size. An enlarged heart is a symptom of various underlying conditions and, if untreated, can lead to serious cardiac issues including sudden cardiac arrest. "The early report also said that Taylor's heart was enlarged."
**Heroin Overdose and Coma (2001)** : In 2001, Taylor Hawkins experienced a heroin overdose at a party in London, which resulted in a two-week coma. Dave Grohl stayed by his side throughout this period, and Taylor later stated that the incident "scared him straight" regarding drug use, although he never elaborated on his sobriety. "He ingested some heroin at a party in London back in 2001 and landed in a coma for two weeks."
**Distaste for Rock Star Trappings** : Taylor Hawkins expressed a personal indifference to the stereotypical trappings of rock stardom. He prioritized playing music for the love of it rather than for wealth or fame, maintaining a grounded perspective despite his success. "If you want to play music, play because you want to play music, not because you want to be rich and famous."
**Recognition of Luck and Hard Work** : Taylor Hawkins acknowledged that his successful career in the Foo Fighters was a combination of both luck and hard work. He was humble about his achievements, understanding that hard work alone doesn't guarantee success in the music industry. "if you get lucky like me, you may make a really good living."
**Joy and Gratitude for His Career** : Taylor Hawkins consistently conveyed immense joy and gratitude for his profession, feeling incredibly lucky to "get paid tons of money to go play rock and roll." He viewed his life as awesome, emphasizing the simple, pure enjoyment of what he did for a living. "Now we're really lucky."
**Beat-Up 1986 Toyota 4x4 Pickup Truck** : Taylor Hawkins drove a beat-up 1986 Toyota 4x4 pickup truck, which he had bought for $400 in high school. This choice of vehicle, alongside his 2005 Subaru Baja, highlighted his down-to-earth personality and lack of concern for flashy rock star possessions. "He was driving the same beat up 1986 Toyota 4x4 pickup truck that he bought for $400 when he was in high school."
