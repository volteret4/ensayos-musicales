## instruments

## albums
**Blood Sugar Sex Magik** : Features Flea's slap bass technique, which he later became annoyed with due to its widespread copying after the band's breakthrough.

## curiosities
**Flea's Real Name** : Flea's real name is Michael Balzary.
**Flea as Studio Musician** : Flea has been hired as a studio musician by artists including Alanis Morissette, Mick Jagger, and Johnny Cash.
**Flea's Early Musical Background** : Flea grew up in a household where jazz was the main music. His first instrument was the trumpet, and he didn't start playing bass until high school.
**Flea's Unique Bass Style** : Due to his hardcore jazz background and fascination with funk, Flea quickly developed a unique style that has been copied by millions worldwide.
**Chad Smith's Audition (1989)** : Chad Smith, a hard-hitting drummer from Detroit, was the last of 30 people to audition for the gig in 1989. He almost didn't make it, but a "click" happened when he jammed with Flea, and he has been a Chili Pepper ever since.
**Chad Smith's Musical Influences** : Chad Smith grew up listening to the relaxed rhythms of Motown.
**Hyde Park Concert (June 2004)** : The band performed a concert in Hyde Park in London in June 2004.

# artist - The Smiths

## members
Mike Joyce
Andy Rourke
Morrissey
Johnny Marr

## curiosities
**Mike Joyce's Drumming Style** : Mike Joyce, the drummer, admitted to being a "ham-fisted" player, focused on power and volume.
**Andy Rourke's Bass Style** : Andy Rourke, the bass player, was known for funk and subtlety, and he taught Mike Joyce how the bass and bass drum should work together.
**Unique Sound** : The combination of Mike Joyce and Andy Rourke created a unique sound for its time, described by Mike as a "weird kind of communion."
**Mike Joyce's Post-Smiths Career** : Mike Joyce went on to play with a variety of bands, including Badly Drawn Boy and Suede.
**Andy Rourke's Post-Smiths Career** : Andy Rourke worked as a hired gun for a while before reuniting with Mike Joyce in a group called Vinnie Peculiar.
**Royalty Lawsuit (1996)** : In 1996, both Mike Joyce and Andy Rourke sued Morrissey and Johnny Marr for non-payment of royalties. Andy settled for £80,000, while Mike was awarded £1.2 million. Morrissey has reportedly continued to appeal the judgment.

## genres

## instruments

## albums
**Rage Against the Machine (1992)** : The band's major label debut, which was essentially a slightly altered version of their 12-song cassette recorded in 1991.

## curiosities
**Rhythm Section's Importance** : Singer Zack de la Rocha and guitarist Tom Morello recognized the need to give the rhythm section room to breathe for the band to truly work.
**Tim Commerford's Upbringing** : Tim Commerford grew up south of Los Angeles in Irvine. His mother was a mathematician, and his father was an aeronautical engineer who worked on the space shuttle.
**Tim and Zack's First Band** : Tim Commerford and Zack de la Rocha formed their first band, Juvenile Expression, in grade seven. Zack taught Tim how to play bass using the Sex Pistols' *Never Mind the Bollocks* album.
**Tim Commerford's Bass Influences** : Tim spent hours learning Geddy Lee's parts from Rush albums.
**Brad Wilk's Early Musical Interests** : Brad Wilk was a Kiss fan from Chicago who started on guitar. He later became a fan of The Who drummer Keith Moon.
**Brad Wilk's Drumming Education** : Brad Wilk took lessons from serious jazz professionals and considered Elvin Jones his hero, from whom he learned very funky grooves.
**Brad Wilk's Groove Technique** : Brad Wilk studied James Brown and George Clinton records and learned how to insert a microsecond delay into his timekeeping, which is essential for a good groove.
**Brad Wilk's Pre-Rage Band** : While living in the San Diego area, Brad Wilk played in a band called Indian Style, which featured Eddie Vedder as its singer.
**Band Formation** : Everyone in Rage Against the Machine found each other through musicians wanted classified ads.
**Political Reputation** : Over eight years, Rage Against the Machine gained a reputation as one of the most political and funky bands in the alternative nation.
**First Recordings (1991)** : The band recorded a 12-song cassette in 1991.

# curiosity

**Reasons to Join a Band** : People often get into bands for reasons such as free beer and more sex.
**Discrepancies in Band Attention** : Lead singers and "hot" guitarists tend to receive the most attention, adoration, and "action" within a band, leaving bass players and drummers to get "scraps."
**Importance of Rhythm Section** : Bass players and drummers form the fundamental foundation of any band's sound, providing the bass and the beat, which are essential for "swing" and groove.
**Definition of a Good Rhythm Section** : A good rhythm section can transform an average or mediocre band into something special by establishing the heartbeat, pace, bounce, slinkiness, and foundation for melody and lead instruments.
**Characteristics of Great Rhythm Sections** : The greatest drum and bass combinations are seamless, with complementary styles and timing. They can be very tight players with machine-like accuracy or have a slight lag or relaxed feel, but they always exhibit a "psychic link" that makes everything flow once the beat and groove start.
**Standard Rhythm Section Composition** : In the vast majority of cases, the rhythm section job falls to two people: the drummer and the bass player.
**Microsecond Delay Technique** : A microsecond delay in timekeeping is identified as something essential for a good groove.
