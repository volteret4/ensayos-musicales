## curiosities
**Exceptional Rhythm Section** : The band is known for having one of the great rhythm sections in New Rock, formed by Flea on bass and Chad Smith on drums. This "funky rhythm section that rocks" provides a strong foundation for the band's sound. "The Red Hot Chili Peppers featuring the backbone of Flea on bass and Chad Smith on drums and together they form one of the great rhythm sections in all of New Rock."
**Flea's Early Life and Influences** : Flea, whose real name is Michael Balzary, grew up in a household steeped in jazz music. His first instrument was the trumpet, and he only started playing bass in high school. His hardcore jazz background and fascination with funk led him to develop a unique and widely copied bass style. "Flea grew up in a household where the main music was jazz."
**Flea's Studio Work** : Flea is considered one of the greatest bass players in the world, leading to his hiring as a studio musician for diverse artists including Alanis Morissette, Mick Jagger, and Johnny Cash. He also played bass on Young MC's hit "Busta Move." "He is so good that he has been hired as a studio musician by everyone from Alannis Morse set to Mick Jagger to Johnny Cash."
**Chad Smith's Audition and Background** : Chad Smith, a hard-hitting drummer from Detroit, was influenced by the relaxed rhythms of Motown. He was the last of 30 people to audition for the drummer position in 1989. Despite almost not making it through, a connection "clicked" in Flea's mind when they jammed, and Smith has been a member ever since. "He was the last guy in a line of 30 people to audition for the gig when it became available in 1989."

He also studied James Brown and George Clinton records, mastering the technique of inserting a microsecond delay into his timekeeping, crucial for a good groove. "He also studied James Brown and George Clinton records and learned how to insert a microsecond delay into his time keeping something that's essential to a good groove."
**Band Formation and Political Stance** : Rage Against the Machine formed after finding members through musicians-wanted classified ads. Over eight years, they earned a reputation as one of the most political and funkiest bands in the alternative nation, largely due to the contributions of Tim Commerford and Brad Wilk. "Over the next eight years rage against the machine got a rep as one of the most political bands in the alternative nation and one of the funkiest thanks to Tim Bob and Brad Wilk."
