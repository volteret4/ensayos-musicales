# artist - Florence Foster Jenkins

She believed herself to be a brilliant opera singer, despite lacking a sense of rhythm, pitch, tone, or pacing, or any discernible musical talent. "She persisted using the funds from an inheritance to further her singing career, beginning with some recitals in 1912."
**Inheritance and Persistence** : Upon her mother's death in 1928, Florence inherited additional money, which she immediately reinvested into her singing career. "When her mother died in 1928, she inherited even more money, which she then plowed right back into her singing career."

"She made a number of recordings between 1941 and 1944, which have been reissued several times since then, including on CD and through iTunes, of course."

"The indie musicians of Chicago and beyond took note, and they took a liking to him."
**Major Label Distribution** : Friends helped Wesley form The Wesley Willis Fiasco, which toured with various indie bands. "He even picked up a record deal with Rick Rubin's American recordings, which means he actually had major label distribution for some of his material."
**Death and Legacy** : Wesley Willis's schizophrenia worsened over time, and he was later diagnosed with leukemia, which led to his death in August 2003. "Sadly, Wesley schizophrenia became worse and then he was diagnosed with leukemia and he died in August of 2003."

# genre - Outsider Music
## curiosities
**Definition and Attributes** : Outsider music is described as the most extreme form of alternative music, far removed from the mainstream, akin to punk rock in its ethos that anyone with something to say should create music. It often features artists with an intense desire to create but without conventional talent, characterized by deep sincerity and authenticity, and frequently made for the sheer joy of the creative act itself. "Welcome to the ultra-strange world of outsider music."
**Artist Motivation** : Artists, including outsider artists, are driven by an internal burning desire to create music, a need described as "as inseparable from the person as the color of their eyes." This creative urge is fulfilled regardless of external validation or perceived talent, emphasizing the profound, intrinsic joy of making music for oneself. "Artists, and I mean all artists, have something that burns inside them, something that demands that they create music."
**Comparison to Punk Rock** : Outsider music is directly compared to punk rock, sharing a central tenet that anyone with something to say and the guts to express it should be able to do so, regardless of whether the material possesses conventional aesthetic value or technical skill. This parallels punk's DIY and accessible approach to music-making. "In fact, it's not unlike punk. One of the central tense of punk rock was that anyone with something to say and the guts to say it should be able to do it."
