# artist - Chalamutu

## curiosities
**Singing into a Cassette Recorder (1979)** : In Utah, during Christmas time in 1979, a small boy, Chalamutu, sang into a cassette recorder, enjoying the sound of his voice on the tape and the feeling of having an audience. This recording later became a central artifact in his life story. "a small boy is singing into a cassette recorder."
**Recording a Secret Message (1979)** : After singing, Chalamutu stopped to record a secret, painful, and shocking message in his own language onto the cassette tape, knowing it was something he had been warned not to speak about. He felt compelled to leave a trace of his memory before it was too late, making it the most important speech of his life. "There's something really important the child needs to say, something he wants put on record in his own language, but he's frightened because it's a secret he's not supposed to tell."
**The Lost Recording – Language Disconnect** : The magnetic coating of the cassette tape preserved the "last grainy flecks and wisps" of Chalamutu's memory, including scratched sounds and muffled phrases that once made sense to him. These were the last words he would speak in his mother tongue before he eventually forgot the language. "And buried somewhere in the hiss and drop out of the recording are the clues to who this boy is and how he got here."
**Adult Recollection of Tape** : Decades later, as Taj, Chalamutu still hears his "scratchy voice" floating up from the tape, but it whispers in a language he no longer speaks or understands, creating a profound disconnect from his past identity. "Yes his scratchy voice still floats up from the tape, but it whispers in a language that Taj no longer speaks or understands."

# instrument - Cassette Tape

## curiosities
**Medium for Lost Memories** : A cassette tape, with its magnetic coating, became the repository for a child's last words in his mother tongue in 1979, preserving "grainy flecks and wisps" of his memory. This tape held scratched sounds and muffled phrases that, at the time, made sense to him but were later lost to his understanding. "imprinted onto the magnetic coating of the tape are the last grainy flecks and wisps of the child's memory."
**Decades of Storage and Degradation** : After the child's recording, the cassette was put back into its "hard little plastic box and tidied away," remaining unheard for many years. The recording itself contained "hiss and drop out," indicating natural audio degradation over time, further challenging the retrieval of its embedded clues. "And buried somewhere in the hiss and drop out of the recording are the clues to who this boy is and how he got here."

# curiosity

**Stop Rewind, the Lost Boy – Podcast Series** : This is a ten-part podcast documentary series that unravels the true story of a child from India who was kidnapped, falsely declared an orphan, and then adopted by a family in Utah. The series uses rare recordings, immersive sound design, and unforgettable first-person testimony to tell the story of a stolen childhood, the quest for truth, and the long road back home. "It tells the story of a child from India who is kidnapped, falsely declared an orphan, and then adopted by a family in Utah."
