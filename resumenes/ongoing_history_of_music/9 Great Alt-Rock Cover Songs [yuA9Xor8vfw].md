## songs

**Zombie (2018) – The Cranberries Cover** : Bad Wolves, an LA-based metal supergroup formed in 2017, recorded their version of The Cranberries' 1994 song "Zombie" in November 2017 while gathering material for their debut album. The cover featured a few lyrical changes, most notably a reference to drones, and was heavier than the original but remained largely true to it. The band's label, 11-7, had an employee named Dan Wait who was a friend of The Cranberries' singer Dolores O'Riordan; he suggested sending her the recording for her blessing. Dolores not only loved the version but offered to come to a studio in London on January 15, 2018, to contribute "sweetening" like guitar or background vocals. She left an excited voice message the night before, but was found dead the next morning. Despite the tragedy, Bad Wolves released the song, promising all profits to Dolores' three children, with the first check amounting to $250,000. This cover became the first rock song to be declared platinum in 2018.

## curiosities

The numbers she smears from a glass in the video, 1/15/18, signify the day Dolores O'Riordan died.
**Dolores O'Riordan's Planned Collaboration** : Dolores O'Riordan loved Bad Wolves' cover of "Zombie" and offered to contribute to the final version in a London studio on January 15, 2018. She left a voice message the night before, expressing excitement, but was tragically found dead in her London hotel room the morning of the planned session.
**Bad Wolves Description** : Bad Wolves is described as a metal supergroup.

## songs

**Hard Sun (2008) – Indio Cover** : Sean Penn, working on the movie "Into the Wild," suggested Eddie Vedder cover "Hard Sun" by Canadian artist Indio (Gordon Peterson) after finding an out-of-print CD. Vedder, unfamiliar with Indio, loved the song and included it on the soundtrack, bringing in Corin Tucker of Sleater-Kinney for backup vocals. The song was released as a single when the soundtrack came out in September 2008 and became a hit. When I stayed to pillage her, she just throws it back at me"). However, a judge ruled in April 2010 that Vedder did not materially infringe on the song, as he kept the general structure and melody.

## albums

**Big Harvest (1989)** : This record was issued in 1989 on N.M. Records in Canada and featured "Hard Sun" as its main single. After its release, Gordon Peterson (Indio) "walked away from the music industry," causing the album to go out of print and largely be forgotten. Decades later, following Eddie Vedder's hit cover, "Big Harvest" was reissued and made available again.

## songs

**Hard Sun (1989)** : Released in 1989, this song became a top 10 single in Canada for Indio (Gordon Peterson). Its original lyrics included lines such as "when she comes to greet me, she is mercy at my feet. When I stayed to pillage her, she just throws it back at me." After its success, Peterson quit the music industry due to his dislike of its workings. The song later gained renewed fame when Eddie Vedder covered it for the "Into the Wild" soundtrack in 2008, leading to a lawsuit from Peterson regarding lyrical changes, which Vedder ultimately won in April 2010. Despite the legal dispute, Peterson received significant royalties from the cover.

## curiosities

**Retirement from Music Industry** : Gordon Peterson, known as Indio, walked away from the music industry after the release of his 1989 album "Big Harvest" because he disliked how it operated, how it treated people, and how it made him feel.
**Lawsuit Against Eddie Vedder (2010)** : Gordon Peterson sued Eddie Vedder for covering "Hard Sun" without explicit permission (which is not legally required under blanket license rules) and for "eroding the integrity of the song" by changing some of the lyrics. The case went through the courts until April 2010, when a judge ruled that Eddie Vedder did not materially infringe on Peterson's song, noting that Vedder kept the general structure and melody largely the same.

# artist - Gloria Jones

## songs

**Tainted Love (1965)** : Written in 15 minutes by Ed Cobb in 1964, Gloria Jones released "Tainted Love" in May 1965 as the B-side to "My Bad Boys Come In Home," which was intended to be the big single. Both songs failed commercially, and the record disappeared. However, in 1973, British club DJ Richard Seerling discovered it while vacationing in the U.S. and began playing it in Northern Soul clubs, where it became a dance floor sensation. Word of its popularity reached Jones, who re-recorded the song, but this attempt also failed.

## songs

**I Will Survive (1978)** : Released in the fall of 1978, this "glorious breakup disco song" became a dance hit and an anthem for female empowerment, also embraced by gay culture. It was originally released as a B-side to "Substitute," a cover of a Righteous Brothers single that "stiffed." DJs discovered "I Will Survive" on the flip side and began playing it in clubs, launching its success. In 2016, it was selected for preservation in the National Recording Registry of the United States. The song has been covered "dozens of times by artists around the world."

## curiosities

**Original B-side Status** : "I Will Survive" was initially released as a B-side in 1978. The intended A-side, a cover of The Righteous Brothers' "Substitute," failed to gain traction, but DJs discovered and began playing "I Will Survive," leading to its immense popularity.
**Opinion on Cake's Cover (1996)** : Gloria Gaynor "hates" Cake's 1996 cover of "I Will Survive" because it includes a swear word in the first verse that was not present in her original recording. Radio versions of Cake's cover have since been edited to remove this word.
**National Recording Registry of the United States (2016)** : "I Will Survive" was selected for preservation in the National Recording Registry of the United States in 2016.

The concept was for Cash to cover contemporary songs in his distinctive style. The album features material originally written by Paul Simon, Depeche Mode, Sting, The Beatles, The Eagles, a duet with Nick Cave, and most notably, his rendition of "Hurt" by Nine Inch Nails.

## songs

**Hurt (2002) – Nine Inch Nails Cover** : Featured on his 87th studio album, "American IV: The Man Comes Around," Johnny Cash's cover of Nine Inch Nails' "Hurt" is described as "one of the most painfully honest recordings of all time." The accompanying video intersperses shots of a young Cash in his prime with footage of him as an older, frail man (he was 71 and in "very poor health" when the video was shot), a portrayal that brought Trent Reznor of Nine Inch Nails to tears. This cover transformed Cash into an "alternative hero," solidifying his image as "the original man in black." It became the biggest hit of his career, selling nearly 3 million downloads, garnering tens of millions of YouTube views, and winning numerous music awards from both the rock and country establishments. Cash died seven months after the video was filmed.

## curiosities

**Career Resurgence with Rick Rubin (2000s)** : In the early 2000s, with his career having "run aground," Johnny Cash connected with producer Rick Rubin. This collaboration led to the concept of Cash covering contemporary songs in his unique style, resulting in his acclaimed "American IV: The Man Comes Around" album.
**"Hurt" Music Video Impact and Legacy (2002)** : The music video for Johnny Cash's cover of "Hurt" (2002) was deeply impactful, showing Cash, then 71 and in very poor health, interspersed with images from his younger years. This video brought Trent Reznor (Nine Inch Nails) to tears, cemented Cash's status as an "alternative hero" and "the original man in black," and was pivotal to the song becoming his biggest hit. Cash died seven months after the video was shot.
**Cash Home in Hendersonville, Tennessee (Video Location)** : The house featured in Johnny Cash's "Hurt" video was his home for 30 years in Hendersonville, Tennessee, which later became a museum. Tragically, the house was in rough shape and burned to the ground in 2007.
**"Hurt" Video Chair Displayed in Nashville** : The chair in which Johnny Cash is seen sitting in the "Hurt" music video is on permanent display behind glass at the Johnny Cash Museum in Nashville.

# venue - Hendersonville, Tennessee

## curiosities

**Johnny Cash's Home and Museum** : The house in Hendersonville, Tennessee, was Johnny Cash's home for 30 years and later became a museum. It was featured in his "Hurt" music video but tragically burned to the ground in 2007.

# curiosity

**The Power of a Great Song – Interpretability** : A truly great song can be interpreted multiple times by different artists and still resonate effectively. The best compositions can sound impressive when performed by a full band or by a single individual with just an acoustic guitar around a campfire, demonstrating their inherent strength and adaptability.
**Audience Appreciation for Cover Songs** : Fans often enjoy hearing songs performed in different ways by various artists, sometimes even preferring the cover to the original. This reveals new dimensions or aspects of the song that might not have been apparent in its initial rendition.
**Royalty System for Cover Songs – The Blanket License** : In the music industry, when covering a song, artists are not required to ask explicit permission from the original composer. Instead, they must provide proper credit to the original artist, ensuring that royalties are paid. This mechanism is known as the blanket license.
**Learning Through Cover Songs** : Learning to play and deconstruct a favorite song, understanding its lyrics, chords, rhythm, and production decisions, can deepen appreciation for the artistry involved and significantly improve one's own musical skills.
**The Impact of Slowing Down a Cover** : For some songs, slowing down the tempo in a cover version can make it "even better than the original," as demonstrated by the success of Michael Andrews' "Mad World" and The Flaming Lips' "Borderline." This technique can provide a fresh perspective and emotional depth.
