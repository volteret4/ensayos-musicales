## curiosities
**Early Career and Influence** : In the late 1970s, Joy Division was a highly regarded English indie band with no money, described as "skint" in the British way. Years later, they were recognized for being a groundbreaking band, becoming legendary and one of the most influential bands in the entire history of Alt Rock. "The Division is now legendary, one of the most influential bands in the entire history of Alt Rock."
**Ian Curtis's Suicide (1980)** : Singer Ian Curtis killed himself on May 18, 1980, at the age of 23. He had been suffering from severe depression, epilepsy, and substance abuse problems related to his illnesses, and had attempted suicide at least once before. His deteriorating condition was compounded by marital problems. The day before the band's first North American tour, Ian stayed up all night watching TV, listening to music, drinking whiskey and coffee, then hanged himself in his kitchen with a clothesline. His wife, Deborah, found him the next morning. "Ian had been suffering from severe depression, epilepsy, and all manner of substance abuse problems related to his illnesses."
**Band Pact and Dissolution** : The other three members were devastated by Ian Curtis's death and had made a pact that if any member left for any reason, the band would automatically self-destruct. This meant Joy Division was officially over, but the surviving members chose to create something new together. "And they had all made a pact. Should any member leave for any reason, the band would automatically self-destruct."

## curiosities
**Formation and Name Origin** : After Ian Curtis's death, surviving Joy Division members Bernard Sumner, Peter Hook, and Stephen Morris discussed their future. Encouraged by their progress as Joy Division, they decided to move forward under a new name. Their manager, Rob Gretton, suggested "New Order" after finding the phrase in a newspaper headline about mass killings in Pol Pot's Cambodia. "Their manager, my name Rob Greten, is the one who came up with it. He found the name in a newspaper headline about the mass killings in Pol Potts, Cambodia. The phrase was new order."
**Bernard Sumner as Lead Singer** : During rehearsals, each member took a turn on lead vocals, with Bernard Sumner ultimately taking on the role. He had to learn to sing and play guitar simultaneously. "And in the end, it was Bernard Sumner. He just had to learn to sing and play guitar at the same time, something that he had to do."
**Jillian Gilbert Joins** : Stephen Morris's girlfriend, Jillian Gilbert, was brought in to play keyboards to fill out the band's sound. "To fill out the sound, Stephen's girlfriend, Jillian Gilbert was brought in to play some keyboards."
**Musical Evolution (1982)** : Initially, New Order sounded similar to Joy Division. However, a trip to New York, where they were exposed to post-disco, early house, and electro music, changed their direction. They became fascinated with synthesizers, drum machines, and sequencers, shifting by 1982 from Joy Division's guitar-based style to a more electronic and dance-oriented sound with Bernard Sumner fronting the band. "And by 1982, New Order had moved away from the guitar-based material that Joy Division had become famous for into something more electronic and dancing, all with Bernard out front."
**Influence** : New Order became extremely influential in the history of Alt Rock, evolving into a distinct style compared to Joy Division. "The result was that New Order became extremely influential in the history of Al Rock in a different style than what made Joy Division what they were."
**Longevity** : New Order continues to be active today, with Bernard Sumner still serving as the band's singer. "And New Order is still with us today, and Bernard Sumner is still singing for the band today."

## curiosities
**Early Singer Changes** : By 1983, the band, originally named Sharp Young Men, changed their name to Faith No More and brought in Chuck Mosley as singer. Mosley had replaced Courtney Love, who was part of the group for approximately 15 minutes. "He replaced a woman named Courtney Love, yes her, who was part of the group for about 15 minutes."
**Chuck Mosley's Erratic Behavior** : While Chuck Mosley was the frontman for the band's first two albums, his behavior became increasingly problematic. He got into fights with other band members, sometimes even on stage. He had his own roadies who also fought with the band's roadies. Incidents included falling asleep on stage and insisting the band only perform acoustic bass songs, which led to the other members losing "faith" in him and quitting. "Then there was the time he fell asleep on stage, and then he got it into his head that all the band should do is acoustic bass songs, and that's when everybody around him in Faith No More had no more faith."
**Mike Patton Joins** : After Chuck Mosley's departure, the remaining members reconvened and approached Mike Patton, who was the singer for Mr. Bungle and had met Faith No More in 1986. Patton joined as lead singer by January 1989, and promptly wrote all the lyrics for their next album in two weeks. "After the Mosley Fiasco, he was approached to become the lead singer, and by January 1989, he was in."
**90s Alternative Success** : With Mike Patton as the new singer, Faith No More's 1989 album featuring Patton's lyrics was released in June, received Grammy nominations, and within a year, the band became a prominent fixture on video channels, achieving early success in the alternative 90s. "And within a year, Faith No More was all over the video channels, and we're on their way to becoming one of the biggest early successes of the alternative 90s."

## curiosities
**Founding Members and Early Lineup** : Guitarist Jerry Cantrell and drummer Sean Kinney were the founders of Alice in Chains. Layne Staley was not a founder; he was drafted in early from a glam band called Sleeze, for whom he played drums. "That was guitarist Jerry Cantrell and drummer Sean Kinney. But he was drafted in early from another band, a glam group called Sleeze, for whom he played drums."
**Formation and Name Origin** : Layne Staley had been living with a homeless Jerry Cantrell at their rehearsal space when Sleeze, Staley's band, was eventually renamed Alice in Chains. After Staley's other band (a funk outfit) and Cantrell's separate project both broke up, Cantrell and Sean Kinney asked Staley to be the vocalist for their new group, adopting the name of Staley's former band, Alice in Chains, for the new project. "Sleeze was eventually renamed Alice in Chains, while Lane was living with a homeless Jerry Cantrell at their rehearsal space."
**Early Sound and Image Shift** : The original Alice in Chains had a sound more akin to Van Halen and Mötley Crüe than Grunge. However, they underwent a change of image and sound tweaks when the Seattle grunge scene began to emerge, which proved to be a successful move for the band. "The original Allison Chains was more Van Halen and Motley Crude than Grunge. But then, when everybody realized what was going on around them in Seattle, there was a change of image and a couple of sound tweaks."
**Layne Staley's Vocal Style** : Layne Staley's voice and his harmonies with Jerry Cantrell were central to the band's sound, considered the best hard rock harmonies since David Lee Roth and Michael Anthony in Van Halen's early days. Their vocal arrangements often featured dual lead vocals, strategically overlapped, with the two voices typically separated by a major third. Staley, with the more powerful delivery, could transition from a menacing monotone to sharp, cutting explosions, and also had a great scream, appealing to both metal and alternative fans. "Technically, the two voices were separated by a major third with Lane having the more powerful of the two deliveries."
**Layne Staley's Drug Addiction and Decline** : Around 1995, Layne Staley's drug habit spiraled out of control, leading to his virtual disappearance from 1996 onward. His last performance with the band was on July 3, 1996, in Kansas City, during a tour with KISS. He became a severe addict, barricading himself in his Seattle condo, where he painted, played video games, and had drugs delivered, leaving the band paralyzed. "Lane turned into an irreparable junkie, barricading himself in his Seattle condo where he painted, played video games, and had all his drugs delivered to the door."
**Layne Staley's Death (2002)** : Layne Staley was found dead on April 19, 2002, after his parents called the police when they hadn't heard from him for two weeks. The coroner determined he had died two weeks earlier, on April 5. A needle was still in his arm when he was discovered. "That was April 19th, 2002. The corner said he died two weeks earlier on April 5th. A needle was still hanging from his arm when they found him."
**Dropped by Label (2004)** : After 15 years, Alice in Chains was dropped from their record label in 2004, following Layne Staley's death and years of inactivity. "In 2004, they were dropped from the record label after 15 years."
**Reunion for Tsunami Benefit (2005)** : In 2005, drummer Sean Kinney initiated the idea of reuniting the band for a benefit concert to aid victims of the tsunami that struck Thailand, Indonesia, and other parts of Southeast Asia. The concert was well-received. "In 2005, drummer Sean Kinney came up with the idea of reuniting the band for a benefit concert for the victims of the tsunami that hit Thailand, Indonesia, and other places in Southeast Asia."
**William DuVall Joins as Lead Vocalist** : Following a successful show in March 2006, where William DuVall (singer for Comes with the Fall) performed as a guest vocalist, the band considered him for a permanent role. They had known and respected DuVall for five or six years. Other candidates, like Vinny Dombrowski of Sponge, were considered but didn't fit, and Scott Weiland was deemed unsuitable due to his drug issues. DuVall was chosen because he sounded close to Layne Staley without being a clone. His integration was gradual, leading to a full commitment by late 2008. "One of the guest vocalists that evening was a guy named William Duval, the singer with a group called Comes with the Fall."
**Equal Album Output with New Singer** : The version of Alice in Chains featuring William DuVall has released as many full albums as the band did with Layne Staley on vocals. "This version has now released as many full albums with Duval as they had with lane."

**Audioslave Naming Rights Payment** : When the band decided on the name "Audioslave," they discovered another band from Liverpool already held the name. To resolve this, they made a payment of $30,000 to the English group. "A quick $30,000 payment to the English groups solved that problem."
**Ed Sullivan Theater Marquee Performance** : Audioslave's debut performance on November 25, 2002, was unconventional, taking place on top of the marquee of the Ed Sullivan Theater as part of a *Late Show with David Letterman* appearance. "They played on top of the marquee on the Ed Sullivan theater as part of an appearance on the late show with David Letterman."
