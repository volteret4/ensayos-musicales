"In the beginning, all rock and roll was shock rock. These kids and their music inspired by black musicians, their swiveling hips and sneering lips. It was the stuff of heathens, music of the devil. Elvis, Jerry Lee Lewis, little Richard, shocking awful stuff, sent by Satan to corrupt the youth."
**Evolution to Deliberate Shock** : As early rock and roll became mainstream and less shocking, the concept of "shock rock" emerged as a deliberate strategy to create outlandish music and performances guaranteed to make people recoil in fear, disgust, and horror, while simultaneously attracting fans who found it "cool." "Eventually, though, early rock and roll became acceptable mainstream music and not nearly as shocking as it once was. This gave birth to the idea of using rock music to shock, period."
**Pushing Boundaries in the 1960s** : Throughout the 1960s, numerous performers sought to test societal limits with their music and behavior, pushing the boundaries of what was acceptable in rock culture. "As the rock scene grew throughout the 1960s, there were plenty of performers who wanted to see just how far society would let them take things"
**Shock for Shock's Sake (Early 1970s)** : By the early 1970s, a new dimension of shock rock emerged where the primary motivation was "shocking for shock's sake," emphasizing provocation as an end in itself. "By the early 1970s, there was this idea of shocking for shock's sake."

# genre - Punk Rock
## curiosities
**Kicking Against the Status Quo** : British punk rock was fundamentally about rebelling against the established social order, expressing defiance towards the class system, the monarchy, and the perceived normality and humdrum lives of mainstream society. "British punk rock was all about kicking against the status quo, down with the class system, down with the monarchy, down with all you normal people in your humdrum lives."
**Desire to Shock Complacency** : Fueled by a sense of hopelessness about their future and the future of their country, British punks aimed to shock people out of their complacency. "British punks felt hopeless about their future and the future of their country. They wanted to shock people out of their complacency."

**Art as Provocation** : Some artists believe the purpose of art is to challenge thinking, face unpleasant realities, speak truth disturbingly, and provoke people out of their safe notions, while others simply seek attention by pushing boundaries. "They believe that the purpose of art is to challenge the way people think, to get them to face unpleasant realities, to speak truth regardless of how disturbing it may be, to be provocative in ways that will shake people out of their safe notions and preconceptions."
