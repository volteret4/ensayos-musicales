## curiosities
**Barcelona Show Cancellation and Sibling Brawl (2000)** : On May 20th, 2000, an Oasis show in Barcelona was canceled because drummer Alan White suffered from severe tendonitis. During the band's unplanned downtime, Liam Gallagher made a derogatory remark about Noel's then-wife, Meg Matthews, questioning the paternity of their daughter, Anais. This led to Noel physically assaulting Liam, punching him in the face, splitting his lip, and delivering a serious headbutt, before declaring he quit the band. "At some point in the evening, Liam said something nasty about Null's then-wife Meg Matthews. Let's just say that he questioned the paternity of Null's daughter, Anase. This was a bad idea. Null leapt on Liam, punched him in the face, split his lip, and then gave him a serious headbutt."
**Noel's Temporary Departure (2000)** : After a physical altercation with Liam Gallagher in Barcelona, Noel Gallagher quit Oasis, leading the band to perform 18 subsequent shows without him. A replacement guitarist, Matt Deighton, played with the band in Milan on May 30th. Noel eventually returned on July 15th for UK outdoor festivals, though a press release stated he would no longer tour overseas with the band. "Null did not return to the band until July 15th when it was time to play some big outdoor festivals across the UK. And even then, a press release said that Null would no longer tour with a waste overseas."
**Noel's Unforgiveness for Paternity Remark (2005)** : Five years after Liam's controversial remark about Noel's daughter Anais, Noel broke his silence on the incident, stating he had never forgiven Liam because he had never apologized. He emphasized that Liam remained "at arm's length" until an apology was offered. "I've never forgiven him because he's never apologized. He's my brother, but he's at arm's length until he apologizes for what he's done."
**Liam's Mid-Set Departures (2002)** : In August 2002, Liam Gallagher walked off stage during a Fort Lauderdale, Florida, show only four songs into the set, claiming his voice had failed due to the flu, which had previously caused a show cancellation in Spain. By the fall of that year, he had abandoned three other shows mid-set, contributing to significant tension within the band, including a six-week period where he and Noel did not speak. "In August 2002, Liam walked off the stage just four songs into a set in Fort Lauderdale Florida. He said his voice had gone bad, a continuation of a case of the flu that had caused a cancellation of a show in Spain 10 days earlier."
**Munich Bar Brawl and Aftermath (2002)** : On December 1st, 2002, during a tour supporting "Heathen Chemistry," Liam Gallagher, Alan White, and two other band members became heavily intoxicated at the Bayer Sherhoff hotel bar in Munich, Germany. They provoked five Italians by throwing peanuts, and after an Oasis song played, Liam and Alan White jumped on stage for "unwanted karaoke." A fight erupted after one of them jumped on a table, causing it to collapse. The brawl escalated into the hotel lobby, resulting in broken furniture and smashed windows. Police arrived with ten cars to find Alan White bleeding from an ashtray to the head, and security details injured, one unconscious. Liam lost several teeth and was bruised, and was arrested after kicking a police officer. Toxicology confirmed his extreme intoxication and the presence of cocaine. Liam required $30,000 in emergency dental surgery, and he and two band members spent 12 hours in jail. The rest of the European tour was canceled, costing millions in revenue, and management paid over $300,000 for Liam to leave Germany. Noel, who was in his room during the incident, was furious upon learning of it. Liam could have faced two years in jail. "Liam had lost several teeth and was bruised and battered all over. The police spokesman was quoted as saying, the boy didn't look good. Several of his teeth were punched out. But that didn't stop him from launching himself at one of the cops kicking him in the ribs as hard as he could."
**Noel's Disregard for Liam's Legal Troubles (2002)** : Following Liam's arrest and potential jail time after the Munich brawl, Noel Gallagher's only public comment on the incident was his concern for Liam's ability to continue singing. "All Null would say about the incident was, all I'm bothered about is that he can still sing."
**Enduring Sibling Hostility (2004)** : By 2004, Noel and Liam Gallagher were the only remaining original members of Oasis, as other band members were unwilling to mediate their escalating conflicts, leading to worsening tensions. Their animosity frequently played out in media interviews. "By 2004, Null and Liam were the last remaining original members of the band. None of the other guys were willing to step in between them when things got heated, so that's how things got worse."
**Noel's Media Attacks on Liam (Mid-2000s)** : Noel Gallagher frequently used the media to disparage Liam, claiming Liam was "frightened to death" of him and that he could manipulate Liam's decisions. He also referred to Liam as a "knobhead" and described him as "rude, arrogant, intimidating, and lazy," likening him to "a man with a fork in a world of soup." "He's rude, arrogant, intimidating, and lazy. He's the angriest man you'll ever meet. He's like a man with a fork in a world of soup."
**Noel's Virgin Festival Attack (2008)** : During an 18-month tour supporting "Dig Out Your Soul," Noel Gallagher was attacked by a fan on stage at the Virgin Festival in Toronto on September 8th, 2008. He suffered three broken ribs, which necessitated the cancellation of several scheduled shows. "Null was attacked by a stupid fan on stage at the Virgin Festival in Toronto on September 8th, 2008. He broke three ribs, and a bunch of shows had to be canceled."
**Liam's V Festival Cancellation and Slander Lawsuit (2009)** : On August 23rd, 2009, Liam Gallagher canceled Oasis's performance at the V Festival, citing laryngitis. Two years later, Noel publicly claimed Liam was "massively hungover" at the time, which prompted Liam to sue his brother for slander, though the lawsuit was later dropped. "The basis was supposed to play the V Festival on August 23rd, but Liam canceled because he had laryngitis. **Paris Dressing Room Altercation and Oasis Breakup (2009)** : On August 28th, 2009, backstage in Paris, Liam Gallagher, enraged by negative press he attributed to Noel, stormed out of the dressing room, throwing a plum at the wall. He then returned, wielding one of Noel's guitars "like an axe" after Noel made a joke. Liam swung the guitar, narrowly missing Noel's head and ultimately trashing the instrument. Noel immediately left, and five minutes before showtime, announced he could no longer continue. Two hours later, a statement was posted on the Oasis website by Noel, confirming his departure due to his inability to work with Liam any longer, effectively ending Oasis after 18 years. "Noel left the dressing room in a huff, just as someone declared that it was five minutes to show time. Noel sat in a car backstage for all of those five minutes, and when he came out, he said, that's it. I can't do this anymore."
**Noel's Detailed Account of Paris Brawl (2011)** : At a press conference two years after the Oasis breakup, Noel Gallagher recounted the Paris dressing room incident, describing Liam's behavior as "quite violent" and akin to "WWE wrestling" or "macho man Randy Savage." He detailed Liam's expletive-laden rant, the throwing of a plum, and Liam's return wielding a guitar like an axe, which nearly struck Noel's face before Noel destroyed it. This incident, witnessed by other band members who remained silent, was the final straw that led Noel to quit the band minutes before a scheduled performance. "Liam was quite violent. At that point, there hadn't been any physical violence, but it was a bit like WWE wrestling. And he was like the macho man Randy Savage. He was like, oh yeah, and all that going on. It's like, effing hell. Liam does the FU and FU and FU, and then he kind of storms out of the dressing room."
**Post-Breakup Slander Lawsuit (2011)** : Following Noel Gallagher's press conference two years after Oasis's breakup, in which he detailed Liam's violent behavior during their final altercation, Liam Gallagher sued Noel for slander. "It was after this press conference that Liam sued Noel for slander."
**Liam's Post-Oasis Sniping (2010s)** : After the breakup of Oasis, Liam Gallagher continued to publicly mock Noel, tweeting "High Flying turds" in response to Noel's new band, posting a picture of Noel with the word "Potato," and tweeting "F Oasis." **Oasis Reunion Speculation and Supersonic Premiere (2016)** : The English press occasionally reported on a potential Oasis reunion, citing reasons such as Liam's financial needs or their mother Peggy's desire for reconciliation. At the October 2016 London premiere of the Oasis documentary "Supersonic," Liam attended, but Noel was absent. Liam publicly remarked on Noel's absence, sarcastically suggesting he was in one of his "really big houses" having a "face peel." Despite the snipes, Liam also expressed openness to a reunion at this time. "In October 2016, the Oasis documentary Super Sonic had its premiere in London. Liam was there, but Noel wasn't."
**Liam's Ambivalence Towards Reunion (2016)** : At the premiere of the Oasis documentary "Supersonic" in October 2016, Liam Gallagher acknowledged that it was good for people to be speaking about Oasis again, but he did not believe a reunion would result from the documentary or ongoing discussions. "I think it is good that people are speaking about Oasis, so I'll agree, but not about just being actually having a little bit of sheep and bigs at each other.
