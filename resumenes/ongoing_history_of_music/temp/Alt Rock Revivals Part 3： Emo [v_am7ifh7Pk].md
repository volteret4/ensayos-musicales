## Emo Waves & Periods
genre **First Wave Emo (1984-1994) – Birth and Development** : A 10-year span during which emo was born and developed, characterized by being difficult to find and hiding in the corner, with bands not attracting much attention.
genre **Revolution Summer (Mid-1980s) – DC Hardcore Movement** : A period when Washington D.C. hardcore bands, including proto-emo groups like Embrace, attempted to break free from the limiting dogma of traditional hardcore music.
genre **Second Wave Emo (1994-Late 1990s) – Evolution and Spread** : Began after the first wave ended, characterized by the traditional hardcore community viewing its adherents as "wimps" while others appreciated its raw emotional honesty. Emo spread and evolved with more dynamic songs.
genre **Third Wave Emo (2000-2008/2010) – Peak and Mainstream Crossover** : A period when emo "blew up" into the mainstream, splintering into many variations (like emo pop, screamo), annoying purists. Characterized by commercial success, major label interest, radio play, and chart performance.
genre **Fourth Wave Emo (2008/2010-Present) – Underground Resurgence** : Began as the third wave declined, remaining mostly underground and regional throughout the 2010s. Bands were inspired by 90s emo, developed independent sounds, or twisted existing emo elements.
genre **Garage Rock Revivals – Upcoming Topic** : The next chapter of "Alt Rock Revivals" will cover Garage Rock, which emerged in the early 1960s and has constantly ebbed and flowed, influencing music each time it resurfaced.
