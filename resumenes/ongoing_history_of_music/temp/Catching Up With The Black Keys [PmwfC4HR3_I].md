## Music Industry Context
influence @U2 **Early Career Development Example** : U2 demonstrated that a band could stumble through its first two records before achieving significant success with its third album, *War*.
influence @Red Hot Chili Peppers **Extended Development Example** : The Red Hot Chili Peppers developed their sound over three albums, achieving a breakthrough with *Mother's Milk* and then a major breakthrough with *Blood Sugar Sex Magik*.
influence @R.E.M. **Independent Success Leading to Major Deal Example** : R.E.M. released five independent albums, each progressively better, before securing a deal with a major record label, enduring a demanding cycle of recording and touring.
curiosity **Modern Music Industry Challenges (2000s-Present)** : In the current music landscape, a new band with a debut record has a narrow 6-13 week window to gain significant traction with radio, retail, and fans after releasing their first single; failure often results in labels withholding support for a second single.
curiosity **Historical Artist Development Practices** : Historically, when music was less abundant, record labels could invest in a band's development, allowing them to mature over 2, 3, 4, or 5 albums.

## The Black Keys - Biography & Career
artist @The Black Keys **Band Formation (2001)** : Formed in 2001 in Akron, Ohio, by Dan Auerbach and Patrick Carney, after an attempt to record a demo with multiple musician friends resulted in only the two of them showing up, leading them to form a duo.
artist @The Black Keys **Hometown Roots** : Both Dan Auerbach and Patrick Carney hail from Akron, Ohio, a city historically known as a center for tire manufacturing.
artist @The Black Keys **Early Friendship** : Dan Auerbach and Patrick Carney met around age 8 or 9, becoming friends in high school despite their different social groups (Dan was a jock, Patrick a nerd).
artist @The Black Keys **University of Akron Enrollment** : Both Dan Auerbach and Patrick Carney attended the University of Akron for a period.
artist @The Black Keys **Early Career Struggles** : The band's first four albums garnered almost no attention, with their fifth offering hope, and their sixth album marking their commercial breakthrough after a long period of hard work.
artist @The Black Keys **Musical Evolution** : Over their career, The Black Keys demonstrated an evolution in their sound, characterized by changes in production, Dan Auerbach's playing style, Patrick Carney's drum sound, and their songwriting ability.
curiosity @The Black Keys **The Grey Ghost Touring Van** : In their early touring days, the band traveled between gigs in a 1994 Chrysler van affectionately named "The Grey Ghost."
curiosity @The Black Keys **Initial Anti-Commercial Stance** : The band initially turned down an offer of £200,000 from a UK mayonnaise manufacturer for a song in a TV commercial, fearing it would damage their artistic credibility, much to their parents' dismay.
curiosity @The Black Keys **Shift in Licensing Policy** : Later in their career, The Black Keys changed their stance on commercial licensing, allowing their songs to be used in movies, TV shows, video games (Sony), products (jeans, Nissan, American Express, Victoria's Secret), prioritizing financial stability for rent and touring.
influence @The White Stripes **Benefit from Peer Success** : The Black Keys' profile grew alongside the rising popularity of The White Stripes, another "garagy, punky, two-piece band," as interest in this type of duo increased.
curiosity @The Black Keys **Internal Strife During Brothers Album (2010)** : Around the time of recording *Brothers*, Dan Auerbach and Patrick Carney were "a bit sick of each other," with Patrick stating they weren't friends outside of band activities.
curiosity @The Black Keys **Near Breakup Experience (2010)** : The band was on the verge of breaking up due to significant personal conflicts, including Dan Auerbach's solo project and Patrick Carney's severe depression and drinking stemming from marital issues.
artist @The Black Keys **Strong Canadian Market** : Canada proved to be one of The Black Keys' most successful territories, achieving triple platinum sales for *El Camino*, nearly matching UK sales despite Canada having half the population.

## The Black Keys - Discography Overview
album @The Black Keys **Extensive Catalog (Pre-2014)** : By 2014, The Black Keys' catalog included 8 full studio albums, 2 EPs, 1 live album, and nearly two dozen singles.

## The Black Keys - Albums

album @The Black Keys **The Big Come Up (2002) – Debut Studio Album** : Recorded between January and March 2002 in Patrick Carney's parents' unfinished basement in Akron, Ohio, using an 8-track Digi recorder. Released on May 14, 2002, on CD and in ten different colors of vinyl (standard black, orange, pink, white), each limited to under a thousand copies and now considered collector's items. It was their only release on the Los Angeles-based indie label, Alive. The album was well-received by critics.
song @The Black Keys **Leven Trunk (2002) – Track from The Big Come Up** : An early track from the band's debut album, *The Big Come Up*.

album @The Black Keys **Thickfreakness (2003) – Second Studio Album** : Recorded in a single 14-hour session in Patrick Carney's parents' basement. The recording was financed by Dan Auerbach and Patrick Carney themselves by mowing lawns. Released on the indie label Fat Possum. The album was praised by critics.
album @The Black Keys **Inezuma Rock and Blues (2003) – Japanese Title for Thickfreakness** : For its Japanese release, *Thickfreakness* was retitled *Inezuma Rock and Blues* because the original name did not translate well; "Inezuma" means lightning in Japanese.
song @The Black Keys **Set You Free (2003) – Single from Thickfreakness** : A single from the album, recognizable from its use in the movie *School of Rock*.

album @The Black Keys **Rubber Factory (2004) – Third Studio Album** : Recorded over five months in an abandoned General Tire factory (which ceased operations in 1982) in East Akron, Ohio, where the band rented the second floor for $500 a month. The title references Akron's history in tire manufacturing, and the band members even attended Firestone High School. The recording process was challenging due to glitches with the space and equipment, including a "crap" recording console bought on eBay (formerly owned by someone who engineered sound for Loverboy) and recycled recording tape "baked" from their label. The album received good reviews, secured the band a slot at Lollapalooza, and led to an appearance on *David Letterman*. It was their first album to chart on the Top 200, peaking at #143.
song @The Black Keys **10AM Automatic (2004) – Single from Rubber Factory** : A single from the album.
curiosity @The Black Keys **10AM Automatic (2004) – Music Video Director** : The music video for "10AM Automatic" was directed by comedian David Cross.

album @The Black Keys **Magic Potion (2006) – Fourth Studio Album** : Released on the Nonesuch label. Recorded back in Patrick Carney's basement using approximately $5,000 worth of inexpensive gear, chosen for the unique "vibe and weird sounds" it produced. The band expressed dissatisfaction with the album, particularly its mastering, which they felt lacked low end. Despite their reservations, the album outsold their first three records, reaching the Top 100 on the American album charts and peaking at #12 in Australia. This period marked their transition to playing larger venues, such as theaters accommodating 1,000 or more people.
song @The Black Keys **Your Touch (2006) – Single from Magic Potion** : A single from the album.

album @The Black Keys **Attack & Release (2008) – Fifth Studio Album** : Recorded in the summer of 2007 at Summa, a studio located in the small college town of Painsville, Ohio. This marked their first time working with an outside producer, Danger Mouse, who also contributed by playing keyboards and bass on the album. Other musicians were brought in to play guitars, provide vocals, and even clarinets.
member @Danger Mouse **Producer & Multi-Instrumentalist for Attack & Release** : Danger Mouse produced the album, playing keyboards and bass. He became available after a prior commitment to produce an album for Ike Turner fell through.
member @Patrick Carney **John Bonham Drumming Style (2007)** : During the recording of *Attack & Release*, Patrick Carney was influenced by John Bonham, adopting Bonham's (Bonzo's) drum kit setup, which resulted in a larger, deeper, and tighter drum sound.
song @The Black Keys **I Got Mine (2008) – Second Single from Attack & Release** : The second single from the album, considered the band's biggest hit from their pre-superstar era. It is a staple in their live performances and has been widely used as a theme song for various TV shows, including the opening theme for CBC's *The Bridge*.

album @The Black Keys **Brothers (2010) – Sixth Studio Album & Commercial Breakthrough** : Released on May 18, 2010. While the physical album cover displayed a longer, nonsensical title, *Brothers* is the proper name, with the extended title serving as a tribute to a 1969 album by Howlin' Wolf. Danger Mouse produced only one song on this album. The album was recorded in part at the legendary Muscle Shoals Sound Studio in Alabama, a venue used by artists like The Rolling Stones, Lynyrd Skynyrd, Bob Dylan, and Bob Seger. Despite the studio's legacy, it presented challenges due to 30 years of disuse, requiring new digital equipment that was subsequently damaged by electrical issues; however, its acoustics and natural echo chambers in the basement were exceptional, particularly emphasizing the bass on the record. This album was created amidst significant personal turmoil and strain between Dan Auerbach and Patrick Carney. It received five Grammy nominations, winning for Best Alternative Album and Best Artwork.
influence @Howlin' Wolf **Brothers (2010) – Album Title Inspiration** : The longer, nonsensical title on the album cover was a tribute to a 1969 album by blues artist Howlin' Wolf.
curiosity @The Black Keys **Brothers (2010) – Heat-Sensitive CD Artwork** : The physical CD for *Brothers* was designed to change color: black becoming white when heated by a CD player's laser; in Australia, it went from white to black; and in Europe, it changed from silver to black.
song @The Black Keys **Tighten Up (2010) – Danger Mouse Produced Hit Single** : This major hit from *Brothers* was the only song on the album produced by Danger Mouse. It earned The Black Keys a Grammy Award.
event @The Black Keys **2011 Grammy Award for Best Rock Song by Duo or Group with Vocal** : "Tighten Up" won this Grammy award.
event @The Black Keys **2011 Grammy Award for Best Alternative Album** : *Brothers* won this Grammy award.
event @The Black Keys **2011 Grammy Award for Best Album Artwork** : *Brothers* won this Grammy award.

album @The Black Keys **El Camino (2011) – Seventh Studio Album & Global Success** : Released just 19 months after *Brothers*, following an intense touring period. Recorded in spring 2011 in Nashville at Dan Auerbach's newly acquired Easy Eye Sound studio, which provided a relaxed working environment with fully functional equipment. Danger Mouse served as a co-writer and producer for all 11 tracks. The songs were developed from short hooks inspired by bands such as The Beatles, The Clash, The Cramps, Elvis, and The Ramones. The album cover features a simple image of an old Chrysler van, a nostalgic reference to "The Grey Ghost," their original touring vehicle. The album achieved immense global success, including platinum sales worldwide and triple platinum status in Canada.
member @Danger Mouse **Co-Writer & Producer for El Camino** : Danger Mouse co-wrote all 11 tracks on *El Camino* and produced the entire album.
influence @The Beatles, The Clash, The Cramps, Elvis, The Ramones **El Camino (2011) – Songwriting Inspiration** : Songs on *El Camino* were composed around short hooks inspired by these influential bands.
curiosity @The Black Keys **El Camino (2011) – Album Cover Homage** : The album cover, featuring an old Chrysler van, paid homage to "The Grey Ghost," the band's original touring van.
song @The Black Keys **Lonely Boy (2011) – Award-Winning Hit Single** : A single from *El Camino* that garnered two Grammy awards.
curiosity @The Black Keys **Lonely Boy (2011) – Viral Music Video** : The music video for "Lonely Boy" featured a single, continuous shot of security guard Derek T. Tuggle dancing and lip-syncing, a spontaneous decision made after an initial video with 48 extras proved ineffective. It quickly achieved nearly half a million views on YouTube within its first 24 hours.
event @The Black Keys **2012 Grammy Award for Best Rock Album** : *El Camino* won this Grammy award.
event @The Black Keys **2012 Grammy Awards for Lonely Boy** : The song "Lonely Boy" received two Grammy awards.
member @Dan Auerbach **2012 Grammy Award for Producer of the Year** : Dan Auerbach received this individual Grammy award following the success of *El Camino*.

album @The Black Keys **Turn Blue (2014) – Eighth Studio Album** : Recorded intermittently over more than 1.5 years and released on May 12, 2014. The album's lyrics were deeply influenced by Dan Auerbach's divorce. Its existence was initially announced by Mike Tyson via tweet. The title was borrowed from a catchphrase used by Gullardy, a host of a 1960s Cleveland late-night horror movie show, and the album's cover art also alluded to Gullardy's interest in themes of mind control, brainwashing, and hypnotism. Five singles were released from the album.
curiosity @The Black Keys **Turn Blue (2014) – Divorce-Inspired Lyrics** : The lyrics of the album were heavily informed by Dan Auerbach's personal experience with divorce.
curiosity @The Black Keys **Turn Blue (2014) – Misconceived Divorce Anecdote** : A widely reported story about Dan Auerbach and his wife battling over custody of "a lock of Bob Dylan's hair" in their divorce was a misunderstanding; the dispute was actually over a poster they referred to as "Bob Dylan's hair."
curiosity @The Black Keys **Turn Blue (2014) – Mike Tyson Album Announcement** : The album's release was uniquely announced via a tweet from Mike Tyson.
influence @Gullardy **Turn Blue (2014) – Title and Cover Art Homage** : The album title *Turn Blue* was inspired by a catchphrase from Gullardy, a host of a 1960s Cleveland late-night horror movie show. The cover art also served as an homage to Gullardy's fascination with movies about mind control, brainwashing, and hypnotism.
song @The Black Keys **Weight of Love (2014) – Opening Track from Turn Blue** : The album's opening track, described as having a sound reminiscent of Pink Floyd from the 1970s. Its title gains additional meaning when considered in the context of Dan Auerbach's divorce during its creation.
influence @Pink Floyd **Weight of Love (2014) – Sound Comparison** : The song "Weight of Love" from *Turn Blue* is described as sounding similar to Pink Floyd's music from the 1970s.

## Venues & Studios
venue @Patrick Carney **Carney's Parents' Unfinished Basement, Akron** : This location served as the primary recording space for *The Big Come Up*, *Thickfreakness*, and *Magic Potion*, noted for its cinder block walls and the "weird sounds" that came from it.
venue **Abandoned General Tire Factory, East Akron** : The second floor of this former tire plant (closed in 1982) was rented for $500 a month and used as the recording location for *Rubber Factory*.
venue **Summa, Painsville, Ohio** : The studio where *Attack & Release* was recorded, located in a "tiny college town."
venue **Muscle Shoals Sound Studio, Alabama** : A historic studio utilized by numerous renowned artists, including The Rolling Stones, Lynyrd Skynyrd, Bob Dylan, and Bob Seger, where a portion of *Brothers* was recorded, noted for its amazing acoustics and natural echo chambers.
