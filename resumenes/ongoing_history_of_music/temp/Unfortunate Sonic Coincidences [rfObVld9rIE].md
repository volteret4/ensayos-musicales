## Music Theory and Copyright Principles
curiosity @None **"Western Music Theory - 12-Note Scale"** : Western music is based on a scale of 12 notes, mathematically related to each other by a series of ratios known as intervals of a semitone.
curiosity @None **"Musical Building Blocks - Chords, Melodies, Harmonies"** : Notes played in certain combinations or patterns reveal things like chords, melodies, and harmonies, which are the fundamental building blocks of music, from Mozart symphonies to pop songs.
curiosity @None **"Finite Combinations of Musical Notes"** : While seemingly infinite, the number of ways 12 notes can be used is finite, estimated to be around 479 billion, 1,600 if each is played once, or over one quintillion (1 followed by 18 zeros) when considering different note durations. Playing a new combination every second would take 33 billion, 63 million, 236 thousand, 360 years.
curiosity @None **"Music Composition - Constraints of Pleasing Sounds"** : Music must sound pleasing to the ear and reach into the soul, meaning there are only so many ways to order sounds effectively, especially within specific genres like rock.
curiosity @None **"Songs Are Not Like Snowflakes - Inevitable Duplication"** : Due to the finite nature of pleasing musical combinations and structures, duplication in songwriting is inevitable at some point.
curiosity @None **"Copyrightable Elements - Song Titles"** : Song titles are not protected by copyright, meaning one could legally title a new song "Smells Like Teen Spirit" without being sued.
curiosity @None **"Copyrightable Elements - Rhythms and Beats"** : Rhythms and beats are not protected by copyright.
