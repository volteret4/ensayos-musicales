## Artists & Members
artist @Kings of Leon **Origin and Family** : American rock band formed by three brothers (Caleb, Nathan, Jared Followill) and their cousin (Matthew Followill).
member @Kings of Leon **Nathan Followell – Drummer** : Drummer for Kings of Leon, identified as the oldest brother. His birth name is Ivan, after his father, but he goes by his middle name, Nathan.
member @Kings of Leon **Caleb Followill – Singer, Guitarist** : Singer for Kings of Leon. His birth name is Anthony, but he goes by his middle name, Caleb. Initially played guitar.
member @Kings of Leon **Jared Followill – Bassist** : Bassist for Kings of Leon. His birth name is Michael Jared, but he goes by his middle name, Jared. Was 15 years old when the band signed their first deal and had never played bass before joining the band.
member @Kings of Leon **Matthew Followill – Guitarist** : Guitarist for Kings of Leon and the cousin of the three brothers. His full name is Cameron Matthew Followill. He had only taken "a couple guitar lessons" before joining the band.
member @Kings of Leon **Ivan Followill (Leon) – Father** : Preacher father of Caleb, Nathan, and Jared. His birth name is Ivan, but he is also known as Leon and has embraced this name since the band's success.
member @Kings of Leon **Betty Ann Followill – Mother** : Mother of the Followill brothers.

## Band History & Formation
artist @Kings of Leon **Record Deal (2001)** : Caleb and Nathan Followill signed a record deal in 2001.
artist @Kings of Leon **Early Instrumental Assignments** : Following their record deal, Caleb and Nathan suggested their younger brother Jared (who had no experience) play bass and their cousin Matthew (who had a few lessons) play guitar, forming the band's lineup.
artist @Kings of Leon **Formation in Mom's Garage** : The band formed and wrote their first five songs over two weeks in their mother's garage (referred to as a "shed") after receiving a Led Zeppelin box set from their label.
artist @Kings of Leon **Upbringing – Preacher Family** : The Followill brothers were raised in a hardcore Pentecostal family by their preacher father, Ivan, and mother, Betty Ann.
artist @Kings of Leon **Nomadic Childhood** : The family traveled extensively across the American South in an old Chrysler due to their father's preaching, often camping out between services due to a lack of money for hotels.
artist @Kings of Leon **Education** : The brothers were primarily homeschooled but were enrolled in a "progril school" for a period in Jackson, Tennessee.
artist @Kings of Leon **Early Musical Training – Church** : Learned to sing gospel songs and play instruments (guitar, bass, drums) by participating in their father's church services.
member @Kings of Leon **Caleb Followill – Education & Work** : Dropped out of school in 11th grade to work in construction before quitting to pursue music.
member @Kings of Leon **Nathan Followell – Education** : Graduated from high school.
member @Kings of Leon **Jared Followill – Role in Formation** : "Tagged along" with his brothers as the band formed.
artist @Kings of Leon **Gradual Rise to Success** : Achieved mainstream success and began playing large venues only by their fourth album, "Only by the Night," rather than with an early "fluke pop hit."
artist @Kings of Leon **Initial International Success** : Gained their first widespread success in the UK and Europe, which was unexpected for a band from Tennessee, as the American South was one of the last regions to embrace their sound.

## Albums & EPs
album @Kings of Leon **Holy Roller Novocaine EP (2003) – Debut EP** : Their first official release, recorded in Memphis approximately one month after signing their record deal. "Wicker Chair" and "Holy Roller Novocaine" are featured on it.
album @Kings of Leon **Youth and Young Manhood – Title Origin** : The title for their first full album was inspired by text on the back of their father's Bible, specifically from a "tree of Moses' life" diagram.
album @Kings of Leon **Youth and Young Manhood – International Breakthrough** : This album was responsible for their initial widespread success in the UK and Europe and contained material from the "Holy Roller Novocaine EP," some as original recordings and some re-recorded.
album @Kings of Leon **Aha Shake Heartbreak – Second Album** : Their second full-length album, adhering to the five-syllable album title rule.
album @Kings of Leon **Aha Shake Heartbreak (2005) – Release Strategy & Performance** : Received a British release first, charting at number 3 on the British album charts, before its North American release in February 2005 (nearly five months later).
album @Kings of Leon **Because of the Times (2007) – Third Album** : Their third album, released in 2007, adhering to the five-syllable album title rule.
album @Kings of Leon **Only by the Night (2008) – Breakthrough Album** : Their fourth album, released in 2008, which "really pushed them over the top" and led to significant mainstream success.

song @Kings of Leon **King of the Rodeo – Single from "Aha Shake Heartbreak"** : A single from their second album, "Aha Shake Heartbreak."

## Influences & Collaborations
influence @Kings of Leon **Early Influence – Led Zeppelin** : The record label gave the band a Led Zeppelin box set, which they listened to while forming the band and writing their first five songs in their garage.
influence @Kings of Leon **Early Exposure – Rock Music** : Had early, clandestine exposure to rock music, including The Rolling Stones and Neil Young, through their father listening to AM radio at night.
influence @Kings of Leon **Late Introduction to Rock** : Due to their preacher father's anti-rock and roll stance, they did not hear the first Led Zeppelin record until 2001.
influence @Ivan Followill **Musical Taste – Rock** : Their father, Ivan (Leon) Followill, would listen to rock artists like The Rolling Stones and Neil Young when his wife was asleep.
influence @Ivan Followill **Anti-Rock Stance** : Their father, Ivan, was "anti-rock and roll," restricting their exposure to the genre during their upbringing.
influence @Kings of Leon **Early Endorsement – The Strokes** : The band's music was shown to members of The Strokes by their record label, leading to The Strokes' intrigue and subsequent touring together.
influence @Kings of Leon **Acclaim from Major Artists** : Received praise from artists such as Bob Dylan, Elvis Costello, and Pearl Jam.
influence @U2 **Influence on Kings of Leon's Touring** : Kings of Leon learned about the business side of touring, lighting, and unique show production while on tour with U2.

## Curiosities & Anecdotes
curiosity @Kings of Leon **Use of Middle Names** : All band members go by their middle names rather than their given first names.
curiosity @Kings of Leon **Father's Tactic for Music** : Their father would play sports radio to make their mother fall asleep during long drives, allowing him to then switch to rock music on AM stations.
curiosity @Kings of Leon **Listening to Radio Preachers** : The family would also listen to other radio preachers and find humor in their sermons.
curiosity **Radio Preacher Gimmicks** : Anecdotes include hearing radio preachers selling unusual items like a "lucky thing for your shoe" or "tears from Jesus."
curiosity @Ivan Followill **Unique Party Tactic** : Ivan (Leon) Followill would clear parties at his house by microwaving jalapeno peppers to "tear gas" guests out.
curiosity @Kings of Leon **Self-Taught Musicianship** : Caleb Followill learned guitar by observing a friend playing C and F chords, then practicing on his own.
curiosity @Kings of Leon **"Wicker Chair" – Song Origin Story** : Caleb Followill wrote "Wicker Chair" in the garage, inspired by Jared sitting in a white wicker chair while they were "stoned," with lyrics subtly referencing this and "helpless situations."
curiosity @Kings of Leon **Lack of Technical Knowledge** : The band members, particularly Nathan Followill (drums), admit to having very little technical knowledge about their instruments, relying on their technicians for "cheat sheets" during interviews or magazine features.
curiosity @Kings of Leon **First Gig Embarrassment** : At their first gig, they mistakenly believed the stage curtain was opaque and celebrated with high-fives and hugs after their set, only to realize the audience could see through it.
curiosity @Kings of Leon **San Diego Fire Incident (September 11, 2001)** : On September 11, 2001, in San Diego, while performing for radio and TV programmers, Nathan Followill accidentally started a fire on a penthouse balcony by dropping a cigarette butt onto flammable Astroturf, causing significant damage.
curiosity @Kings of Leon **Five-Syllable Album Title Rule** : The band adopted a rule that all their full-length album titles must have exactly five syllables (the EP is an exception).
curiosity @Kings of Leon **Band Name Origin – Grandfather Leon** : The band is named after their grandfather, Leon.
curiosity @Kings of Leon **Ivan Followill – Adopting "Leon"** : Their father, Ivan, has embraced "Leon" (his middle name) since the band's success, even introducing himself by it.
curiosity @Kings of Leon **Lack of Soundchecks** : The band often does not perform soundchecks before their shows, trusting their crew, in contrast to bands like U2 known for meticulous preparation.

## Genres & Movements
genre @Kings of Leon **Early Genre – Gospel Music** : Their initial musical experience came from singing gospel songs in their father's church services.
genre **Country Music** : The genre of Billy Joe Shaver, an artist Kings of Leon opened for at their first gig.
genre **New Rock Revival** : A musical movement in the UK that Kings of Leon were associated with, alongside bands like The Strokes.

event @Kings of Leon **First Gig – Opening for Billy Joe Shaver** : Their very first Kings of Leon gig was opening for country artist Billy Joe Shaver, alongside a band called The Skeeters, to an audience primarily interested in "Old Western music."
event @Kings of Leon **Early UK Tour** : Booked on a UK tour as part of a magazine-sponsored tour featuring "all the hottest bands" after playing fewer than ten shows, halfway through recording their first album.
event @Kings of Leon **Tour with The Strokes** : Toured with The Strokes in the same year their first album ("Youth and Young Manhood") was being completed.
event @Kings of Leon **Tour with U2** : Toured with U2 the year following the release of "Youth and Young Manhood."
event @Kings of Leon **Tour with Bob Dylan** : Toured with Bob Dylan.
