## Members & Biography
member @Mike Smith **Sandbox Guitarist & "Bubbles" Actor** : Mike Smith was a guitarist for Sandbox. After the band broke up, he became a sound man, developed the character "Bubbles" using thick glasses, and was written into the 1999 movie *The Trailer Park Boys*, later becoming a singing member of the show's cast.
member @Paul Young **Street Band Keyboardist** : Paul Young was the keyboardist for Street Band from its formation in 1977 until its breakup in 1979, subsequently launching a successful solo career.
member @D'arcy Wretzky **The Smashing Pumpkins Original Bassist** : D'arcy Elizabeth Wretzky met Billy Corgan and James Iha in Chicago in 1988 and became the original bassist for The Smashing Pumpkins. She was romantically involved and engaged to James Iha from 1988-1991. She left the band in 1999 to pursue acting, was arrested on crack cocaine charges in 2000, and later moved to Watervliet, Michigan, where she raises Arabian horses, owns antique shops, and is a trained massage therapist.
member @John Anthony Gillis (Jack White) **The White Stripes Co-Founder** : John Anthony Gillis, known as Jack White, met Meghan Martha White in Detroit in 1994. They married on September 12, 1996, with Jack taking Meg's last name. They formed The White Stripes and divorced on March 24, 2000, but continued as bandmates.
member @Meghan Martha White (Meg White) **The White Stripes Co-Founder** : Meghan Martha White, known as Meg White, met John Anthony Gillis in Detroit in 1994. They married on September 12, 1996, and formed The White Stripes. They divorced on March 24, 2000, but continued as bandmates.

## Curiosities & Anecdotes
curiosity @Street Band **Bakers' Strike Influence on "Toast" Success** : The success of Street Band's "Toast" single in 1978 was influenced by a series of national strikes in England, including a bakers' strike which made bread scarce, ironically boosting a song about making toast.
curiosity @Street Band **"Toast" as a Career Liability** : While "Toast" was Street Band's biggest hit, it also became their biggest liability, as audiences expecting a quirky English outfit were disappointed by their more conventional rock sound, leading to the failure of subsequent releases and the band's breakup.
curiosity @The Smashing Pumpkins @D'arcy Wretzky **Post-Band Arrest and Rehab** : On January 25, 2000, D'arcy Wretzky was arrested in Chicago on crack cocaine charges, found with three bags of crack after her passenger car made illegal U-turns, leading to a court-ordered drug rehab program.
curiosity @The Smashing Pumpkins @D'arcy Wretzky **Post-Band Life** : After leaving The Smashing Pumpkins, D'arcy Wretzky pursued acting (unsuccessfully) and now lives on a horse farm near Watervliet, Michigan, raising Arabian horses, owning three antique shops, and working as a trained massage therapist.
curiosity @The Extras **Post-Breakup Careers** : After The Extras broke up, its members scattered, with at least one individual becoming a professional chef or cook.
curiosity **Collectible Record Value Factors** : The value of a collectible record depends on its condition, rarity, and market demand, with resources like *Record Collector* price guides, eBay, gemm.com, and 991.com assisting in valuation.
curiosity @The White Stripes **"Brother and Sister" Misconception** : The widely believed story that Jack and Meg White were brother and sister originated as a joke they perpetuated when The White Stripes first started, despite them actually being ex-husband and wife.
curiosity @Radiohead **"Just" Video Mystery's Intent** : The mystery of what the man says at the end of Radiohead's "Just" music video is intentional, designed to leave viewers wondering and prevent the "deadening" of the mystery, as confirmed by the band and video creators.
curiosity @Nirvana @Kurt Cobain **Kurt Cobain Murder Conspiracy Theories** : Numerous conspiracy theories question the official narrative of Kurt Cobain's death, citing unanswered questions about the logistics of his suicide, changes in handwriting on his note, credit card use after his death, and investigative oversights. The host dismisses these as "chasing shadows," favoring the Occam's Razor principle.
