## Foundational Influences & Early Music History

influence @African American Culture **Foundational Influence on Rock Music** : Rock music's origins and development are inextricably linked to the innovations and accomplishments of Black culture, tracing back centuries to important innovations and accomplishments.
event **Arrival of First Slave Ship in North America (1619)** : Approximately 20 African captives arrived in the British colony of Virginia in 1619, marking the beginning of forced migration to the West.
genre **African American Music Traditions (Historical)** : Songs and rhythms brought by enslaved Africans sustained them through brutal times, including work songs, protest songs, satirical songs, and game songs, featuring regular and syncopated beats from traditional dance.
genre **Spirituals (Historical)** : A genre of music that evolved from African American traditions over centuries.
genre **Gospel Music (Historical)** : A genre of music that evolved from African American traditions over centuries.
genre **Blues (Historical)** : An African-American invention from the South, characterized by simple songs telling of hard lives, hard luck, real life, and difficult relationships, with a structure traceable to Mississippi Delta field songs.
genre **Boogie-Woogie (Historical)** : A genre of music that evolved from African American traditions over centuries.
genre **Ragtime (Historical)** : A genre of music that evolved from African American traditions over centuries.
genre **Jazz (Birth and Development)** : A genre created by Black musicians in New Orleans, with its birth and development occurring in the first two decades of the 20th century.
genre **Rhythm and Blues (R&B) (Historical)** : A genre of music that evolved from African American traditions over centuries.
genre **Bebop (Historical)** : A genre of music that evolved from African American traditions over centuries.
genre **Folk Blues / Rural Blues (Post-Civil War)** : Music that spread across the Deep South following the American Civil War, originating from African-American invention.
influence **Blues Structural Foundation**: The three-line stanza structure of blues songs traces back to Mississippi Delta field songs and forms the foundations for rock, R&B, funk, and hip-hop.
genre **Rock and Roll (Origins)** : Formed in the early 1950s by incorporating music with rich Black history and traditions, country, western, hillbilly, R&B, and other ingredients.
influence **African American Artists as Rock Pioneers**: Landmark contributions by Black artists changed everything, making what we call rock today and so much of its culture exist.
event **Black History Month (Podcast Focus)** : The episode was prepared specifically to acknowledge rock and roll "firsts" by Black artists.

## Early Rock & Roll "Firsts"

artist @Living Colour **Formation and Style (1988)** : A band formed in 1988 with four Black members from New York City who brought together metal, funk, punk, hip-hop, jazz, and alt-rock into one fresh package, mating it with straight commentary on politics and race.
genre @Living Colour **Funk Metal Pioneers**: Their resulting funk metal sound laid the groundwork for many groups of the 1990s, including Rage Against the Machine, 311, Primus, and Korn.
song @Living Colour **Cult of Personality (1988)** : A song that exemplifies their blend of genres and political/racial commentary.

artist @George W. Johnson **First Recording Star**: The first person to have a hit record and the first Black performer to make the music charts.
artist @George W. Johnson **Biography (Pre-Recording)** : Former slave, freed around 1853, who sang on the streets of New York City starting sometime in the 1870s.
event @George W. Johnson **First Recordings (1890)** : Recruited by Charles Marshall in spring 1890 to make recordings for the Metropolitan and New Jersey Phonograph companies.
curiosity @George W. Johnson **Recording Process (1890s)** : Recorded on Thomas Edison's wax cylinders for coin-operated phonographs; each cylinder was a master, requiring individual recording. Johnson's strong voice allowed capture by up to five recorders at a time, repeating songs up to 50 times per song.
curiosity @George W. Johnson **Marketing as a Black Man**: His record label marketed him as a Black man, which was unusual at a time when slavery was still fresh in many minds and segregation was strong.
song @George W. Johnson **The Laughing Song (1890)** : His most popular track, recorded in 1890.
chart @George W. Johnson **First Number One Hit (1894)** : By 1894, The Laughing Song was the best-selling song ever and reached number one on the brand new music charts of the day.
chart @George W. Johnson **Sales of The Laughing Song (Late 1890s)** : Said to have sold up to 50,000 copies, which would have required him to perform the song 12,000-15,000 times (approximately 300 days of singing at 50 performances a day).
artist @George W. Johnson **Major Vaudeville Star**: Became a major Vaudeville star as a result of his recordings.
artist @George W. Johnson **World's First and Biggest Pop Star**: Recognized as the world's first and biggest pop star in the late 1890s.
song @George W. Johnson **The Laughing Song (1897 Recording)** : A later recording of The Laughing Song from 1897 is arguably the first ever hit record.
artist @George W. Johnson **Later Life and Death**: His popularity faded; he ended up working as an office doorman and died in 1914 at the age of 67, buried in an unmarked grave in Queens, New York.

artist @Trixie Smith **Blues Singer Biography**: A blues singer originally from Atlanta.
label @Trixie Smith **Black Swan Records**: Recorded for Black Swan, a Harlem-based record label.
song @Trixie Smith **My Man Rocks Me with One Steady Roll (1922)** : Recorded in September 1922.
curiosity **Etymology of "Rock and Roll"**: Originally meant the movement of a ship on rough seas.
event @Johnny Morton's Minstrels **First Public Performance of "Rock and Rural" (1881)** : A white group, Johnny Morton's Minstrels, performed a song called "Rock and Rural" in Victoria, BC, on April 24, 1881.
curiosity **"Rock and Roll" - Early Meanings**: Also described how animated things could become in Black churches when singing gospel, later evolving into a euphemism for sex.
song @Trixie Smith **First Secular Use of "Rock and Roll" (1922)** : My Man Rocks Me with One Steady Roll is cited as the first secular use of the words "rock and roll" in a song, carrying multiple meanings.
influence @Trixie Smith **First to Use "Rock and Roll" on Record**: Credited as the first to use the phrase "rock and roll" on record in 1922 (or 1931).
song @Lil Johnson **Rock That Thing**: Followed Trixie Smith's use of "rock and roll."
song @Ike Robinson **Rock Me Mama**: Followed Trixie Smith's use of "rock and roll."
song @The Boswell Sisters **Rock and Roll**: Followed Trixie Smith's use of "rock and roll."

artist @Alan Fried **Radio DJ Career (Cleveland)** : A white radio DJ who started his career in Cleveland.
curiosity @Alan Fried **Playing "Race Records" (1951)** : Made his name in 1951 by playing "race records" (songs by Black artists), which was "forbidden fruit" for many young white teenagers.
curiosity @Alan Fried **Coining "Rock and Roll" (Radio Term)** : Plugged into the culture, he knew the phrase "rock and roll" and started referring to the records he played with that term.
curiosity @Alan Fried **"Rock and Roll" Definition**: Defined rock and roll as "swing music with a modern name," noting its origins in levees and plantations, incorporating folk songs, blues, and rhythm, while likely knowing its coded sexual connotations.

artist @Charlie Christian **First Electric Guitar Soloist**: Appears to be a Black man from Oklahoma, who performed the first ever electric guitar solo.
instrument @Charlie Christian **Guitar Innovation (1931)** : Started soloing during jam sessions with fellow musicians in 1931, believing the guitar could be used for more than just chords and rhythm.
instrument @Charlie Christian **Acquisition of Electric Guitar (1936)** : Acquired a new electric guitar in 1936.
artist @Charlie Christian **Joining Benny Goodman's Big Band (1939)** : Joined Benny Goodman's Big Band in 1939, where he was allowed to improvise and solo during performances.
influence @Charlie Christian **Foundation of Lead Guitarist Role**: Recognized for taking the new amplified guitar out of the shadows and creating the foundations of the lead guitarist as we know it today.
artist @Charlie Christian **World's First Guitar Hero**: By every measure, he was the world's first guitar hero.
artist @Charlie Christian **Death (1941)** : Died of tuberculosis on March 3rd, 1941, at the age of 25.

artist @George Barnes **First Electric Guitar Solo Recording**: Recorded the first electric guitar solo on March 1st, 1938.
album @George Barnes, Big Bill Broonzy **Recording Session (1938)** : Recorded two songs with blues guitarist Big Bill Broonzy.
song @George Barnes, Big Bill Broonzy **A Low-Down Dirty Shame (1938)** : One of the two songs recorded, featuring Barnes' solo when he was just 16 years old.

artist @Jack L. Cooper **Biography**: A Black former boxer and semi-pro baseball player.
event @Jack L. Cooper **Host of The All-Negro Hour (1929)** : Got a job at WBSC in Chicago as the host of The All-Negro Hour, first broadcast on November 3rd, 1929.
show @Jack L. Cooper **The All-Negro Hour**: A variety show featuring music and comedy bits, which was extended to 9.5 hours a week by mid-1936.
curiosity @Jack L. Cooper **First Professional Radio DJ (Possible)** : Possibly the first to play records as part of his show, bringing his own phonograph and collection of jazz and gospel records to play on air, making him the first ever professional radio DJ.
venue @WBSC Chicago **Broadcast Venue**: The radio station where Jack L. Cooper hosted The All-Negro Hour.

event **Shift in Radio Broadcasting (Late 1940s/Early 1950s)** : Local radio stations lost network programming (from NBC, CBS, Mutual) as networks pivoted to television, leading them to improvise new content.
curiosity **Local Radio Programming (Post-1940s)** : Stations reclaimed the period after 7 pm to create local programming, often hiring individuals to play records, shifting radio's focus from soap operas and dramas to popular music.
instrument **Transistor Radio (Rise of)** : A new technology that allowed young people to take their music with them, away from their parents.
genre **Birth of Rock and Roll (Gradual Process)** : Emerged around 1951, a "stew" of R&B, Blues, Country Western, Hillbilly, and other elements, aided by radio stations playing a hodgepodge of material. Its birth happened gradually, not with a single song.

song @Roy Brown **Good Rockin' Tonight (1947)** : A 1947 song by a Black blues singer from Louisiana, considered a landmark recording by some, with lyrics including "Rock and Roll's night."
song @Wynonie Harris **Good Rockin' Tonight (1947)** : The same song released by a Black performer, contributing to the notion of "rockin'" as a musical concept entering public consciousness.
song @Jimmy Preston **Rock the Joint (1949)** : A song that contributed to the notion of "rockin'" as a musical thing.
song @Stick McGhee **Drinking Wine Spo-Dee-O-Dee (1949)** : A song that contributed to the notion of "rockin'" as a musical thing.

song @Jackie Brenston and His Delta Cats **Rocket 88 (1951)** : Released late March 1951, nominated as the first true rock and roll record.
artist @Jackie Brenston **Role in Ike Turner's Band**: Jackie was the sax player in Ike Turner's band (the same Ike Turner who later married and abused Tina).
event @Jackie Brenston and His Delta Cats **Recording Session Journey (March 2, 1951)** : The Delta Cats packed an old station wagon at the Riverside Hotel in Clarkstale, Mississippi, for a 75-mile drive north to Sun Studios in Memphis along Highway 61.
venue @Riverside Hotel Clarkstale, Mississippi **Rehearsal Location**: Location where Jackie Brenston and His Delta Cats rehearsed before their recording session.
venue @Sun Studios Memphis **Recording Location**: The studio where Jackie Brenston and His Delta Cats recorded "Rocket 88."
curiosity @Jackie Brenston and His Delta Cats **Busted Bass Amp Innovation**: During the drive, bass player Willie Kizart's amp was dropped, causing the speaker cone to come loose and produce a buzz/vibration instead of a clean sound. Attempts to fix it made it worse.
curiosity @Jackie Brenston and His Delta Cats **Sam Phillips' Decision**: Producer Sam Phillips (a white guy) liked the distorted sound and decided to use it as is, potentially creating the first rock and roll record.
album @Jackie Brenston and His Delta Cats **Rocket 88 Production**: Featured upfront vocals, power from piano and two saxophones, a good time backbeat from drums, and a new distorted bass sound from Willie Kizart's amp.
influence @Jackie Brenston and His Delta Cats **First Rock and Roll Record Claim**: Sam Phillips often touted "Rocket 88" as the first rock and roll record (Phillips also discovered Elvis Presley), which helped the legend become fact.
influence **Proto Rock and Roll Songs (Black Artists)** : The vast majority of these proto rock and roll songs were from Black artists.

## Guitar Heroes & Innovators

artist @Sister Rosetta Tharpe **First Female Guitar Hero**: Considered by many to be the first female guitar hero and revered by Johnny Cash, Jerry Lee Lewis, Elvis Presley, Little Richard, and Chuck Berry.
instrument @Sister Rosetta Tharpe **Electric Guitar Pioneer**: A guitarist who graduated to the electric guitar.
artist @Sister Rosetta Tharpe **Godmother of Rock and Roll**: Called the "godmother of rock and roll."
artist @Sister Rosetta Tharpe **Biography**: A queer Black woman from Cottonplant, Arkansas.
influence @Sister Rosetta Tharpe **Bridge Between Genres**: Provided an important bridge between gospel blues and a loose type of swing music, contributing to the development of rock and roll.
event @Sister Rosetta Tharpe **European Tour (1964)** : Embarked on a European tour in spring 1964, including a stop in Manchester.
influence @Sister Rosetta Tharpe **Influence on British Rockers**: Her Manchester performance inspired Eric Clapton, Jeff Beck, and both Keith Richards and Brian Jones of The Rolling Stones, who became her disciples.
curiosity @Sister Rosetta Tharpe **Queer Identity (Private)** : Kept her queer identity quiet during her career.

artist @Little Richard **Rock's First Openly Gay Rock Star (Argument)** : An argument can be made that he was Rock's first openly gay rock star.
artist @Little Richard **Sexuality and Flamboyance**: Identified as gay or at least bisexual with a leaning towards gay, playing up effeminate mannerisms, wearing make-up, working in "rough trade," and once arrested for spying on men in public toilets.
artist @Little Richard **Self-Proclaimed "Queen"**: Proclaimed, "Elvis may have been the king of rock and roll, but I and the queen."
influence @Little Richard **Impact and Flamboyance**: His flamboyance, energy, and music reverberated through all of rock and roll, having a massive effect on artists from The Beatles to James Brown, David Bowie, and even Lemmy of Motorhead.
chart @Little Richard **First Black Crossover Artist**: The first Black artist to cross over, appealing to all races and breaking the color barrier, with his shows attracting both Black and white audiences, which helped change culture.
song @Little Richard **Tutti Frutti (Original Lyrics)** : One of his best-known songs, whose original, uncensored lyrics hint at his sexuality.

artist @Chuck Berry **Father of Rock and Roll**: Named the "father of rock and roll."
influence @Chuck Berry **Rock's Separate Identity**: Credited more than any other person with taking rhythm and blues and "rocking it up" to make rock into a separate and distinct thing.
instrument @Chuck Berry **Signature Style**: Riffed through two and three-chord songs on his electric guitar with style and showmanship, laying the foundation for much of standard rock and roll.
artist @Chuck Berry **Musical Characteristics**: Possessed swagger and attitude, with lyrics aimed squarely at young people and their troubles; known for his guitar solos.
instrument @Chuck Berry **Pre-distortion Sound**: His early career sound had a roughness and rawness that presaged the use of giant amplifiers and distortion pedals almost a decade later.
curiosity @Chuck Berry **Voyager Golden Disk (1977)** : The only rock and roll song included on the Voyager Golden Disk, a phonograph record of gold launched on Voyager I and II (1977), meaning if alien civilization finds it, their first exposure to rock will come from Chuck Berry.

artist @Jimi Hendrix **Electric Guitar Innovations**: Provided rock with many firsts regarding the sounds an electric guitar could make.
instrument @Jimi Hendrix **Wah-Wah Pedal Pioneer (1967)** : The first, or one of the very first, to incorporate the wah-wah effects pedal into his sound in 1967.
influence @Jimi Hendrix, Keith Richards **Distortion Pedal Popularization (1965)** : Keith Richards of The Rolling Stones popularized the distortion pedal on "(I Can't Get No) Satisfaction" in 1965.
curiosity **Wah-Wah Pedal Mechanical Precursors**: The wah-wah effect had been reproduced mechanically by brass players as far back as the 1920s.
song @Jimi Hendrix **Burning the Midnight Lamp (1967)** : B-side released in spring 1967, one of the first recordings featuring a wah-wah pedal.
song @Eric Clapton **Tales of Brave Ulysses (1967)** : Released within days of Hendrix's "Burning the Midnight Lamp" in spring 1967, also claimed as the first wah-wah pedal recording.
curiosity @Jimi Hendrix **Wah-Wah Pedal Acquisition Stories**: Stories include buying a Vox unit from Manny's Music on West 48th Street in New York, Frank Zappa giving him his wah-wah pedal, or Noel Redding being told by a London music store employee to bring Jimi by for a listen.
instrument @Jimi Hendrix **Extensive Wah-Wah Use**: Used the wah-wah pedal extensively in his music.

artist @Tom Morello **Wah-Wah Pedal User**: A great guitarist of African descent who frequently uses the wah pedal.
song @Rage Against the Machine **Killing in the Name**: Features Tom Morello's wah-wah pedal use, appearing about 30 seconds into the song.

artist @Bo Diddley **Pioneer of "Bo Diddley Beat"**: Used a particular rhythm so effectively that it is now called the "Bo Diddley beat."
influence @Bo Diddley **Introduction of Beat to Rock and Roll**: Did not invent the beat (its history goes back at least 50 years) but was the one who introduced it to rock and roll.
influence @Bo Diddley **Impact of Beat**: Everyone from Iggy Pop to Jett has used that rhythm in their hit songs.

artist @Peggy Jones **Mother of Rock and Roll (Suggested)** : Suggested as the "mother of rock and roll."
member @Peggy Jones, Bo Diddley **Guitarist in Bo Diddley's Band (1957)** : Started working as a guitarist in Bo Diddley's band in 1957.
artist @Peggy Jones **First Female Rock Guitarist in Famous Band**: Recognized as one of the first female rock guitarists in a famous rock band.
instrument @Peggy Jones **Guitar Synthesizer Experiments**: Recognized for her experiments with guitar synthesizers, which nobody used in R&B music at the time.

## Punk & Grunge Pioneers

artist @Death **First Black Punk Band (Detroit Argument)** : An argument can be made for this band, composed of three Black brothers from Detroit, as the first punk band.
member @Death **Hackney Brothers**: Comprised of David, Bobby, and Dennis Hackney.
event @Death **Formation Inspiration (1964)** : The Hackney brothers were inspired after seeing The Beatles perform on Ed Sullivan in February 1964.
artist @Death **Original Name (Rockfire Funk Express)** : Originally called Rockfire Funk Express.
artist @Death **Name Change (Death)** : Changed their name to Death after their father died in a car accident.
album @Death **Early Recordings (1970s)** : Started making records that largely went unnoticed, typically released in runs of maybe 500 copies.
event @Death **Breakup (1977)** : The band broke up in 1977.
event @Death **Rediscovery (2009)** : Rediscovered in 2009, with early 1970s recordings making the rounds and sounding "pretty punky."
curiosity @Death **Pre-Ramones Punk Music**: Possibly making punk-like music before The Ramones played their first gig in 1974.

artist @Tina Bell **Godmother of Grunge (Suggested)** : Needs to be remembered as someone who helped invent Grunge, one of the founders of the sound that defined Rock in the 1990s, and is called the "godmother of Grunge."
artist @Tina Bell, Bam Bam **First Female Grunge Front Person**: Was the genre's first ever female front person in the early stages of Grunge.
artist @Bam Bam **Formation (1983)** : Tina Bell and her husband Tommy Martin formed Bam Bam in 1983.
member @Bam Bam **Matt Cameron (Drummer)** : Featured Matt Cameron (later of Soundgarden and Pearl Jam) as their drummer.
curiosity @Tina Bell **Challenges as Black Female Punk Singer**: Faced a lot of racial abuse as a Black female punk singer but also had fans.
curiosity @Tina Bell **Kurt Cobain as Roadie**: Kurt Cobain once worked as a roadie for Bam Bam.
album @Bam Bam **First Record (1984)** : Made their first record in 1984.
venue @Reciprocal Recording **First Grunge Band to Record There**: Bam Bam used Reciprocal Recording, making them the first Grunge band to make music there.
influence @Reciprocal Recording **Later Grunge Bands**: Reciprocal would later be used by Nirvana, Alice in Chains, Soundgarden, The Melvins, Mudhoney, and a ton of others.
album @Bam Bam **Villains EP (1984)** : Their EP, "Villains," was released at least a year before other early Grunge groups started releasing records.
event @Bam Bam **Local Seattle Scene (1980s)** : Part of the local Seattle scene for several years, opening shows for future stars like Soundgarden, but had a hard time gaining traction and was largely ignored.
artist @Tina Bell **Later Life and Death**: Left Bam Bam in 1990, moved to England, then the Netherlands (deported back to the US due to an immigration crackdown). Quit music, got divorced, battled depression and alcoholism, and was diagnosed with cirrhosis of the liver. Died in Las Vegas on October 10, 2012, at the age of 55, with her body undiscovered for weeks and her possessions (lyrics, music, diaries, poems) later thrown out.

## Other Notable Contributions

artist @Big Joe Turner **Shake, Rattle, and Roll (1954)** : His 1954 song was one of the template songs for Rock and Roll during that era.
artist @Nona Hendryx **Biography**: A former member of Labelle and a founding member of Black Rock Coalition.
organization **Black Rock Coalition**: A non-profit organization that provides help for Black artists.
artist @Poly Styrene **First Black Female Punk Front Person (UK)** : Singer for Britain's X-Ray Spex, probably the first Black female front person for a punk band.
artist @Don Letts **Biography**: A member of Big Audio Dynamite with Mick Jones of The Clash.
influence @Don Letts **Pioneering Punk DJ**: Got involved in the early punk scene as a DJ, spinning dub and reggae records in clubs and introducing those genres to pioneers in the punk era, which was a "first."
