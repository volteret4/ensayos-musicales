## Albums & EPs
album @USS **Questamation (2009) – Debut Full-Length Album** : USS's first full-length album, released on March 9, 2009.
album @USS **Welling the Sea Drive (EP) – First EP** : USS's first extended play (EP), noted by the band for having sounds similar to later work by Twenty One Pilots.
album @USS **Approved (2011) – EP** : An EP released by USS in 2011, marking a significant shift in the band's collaborative and songwriting process after working with producer Tog Salter.
album @USS **Advanced Basics (2014) – Album (Two Versions)** : Album released by USS in 2014, notable for having both a regular and an international version. It emerged during Ash's recovery from a drug bender, with the title reflecting a philosophy of simple things being the hardest.
album @USS **New World Alphabet (2017) – Long EP** : A seven-song EP released by USS in 2017, where the creative process and mood of the songs were influenced by geographical location (California vs. Canadian winter).
album @Various Artists **Judgment Night Soundtrack** : Soundtrack album featuring the song "You're Just Another Victim" by Helmet and House of Pain.
album @Pink Floyd **Dark Side of the Moon (1973)** : Legendary album, recorded using a specific drum set that was later serendipitously encountered by Ash Boo Shultz while writing "Who's With Me."

## Influences
influence @Kurt Cobain **Comparison for Ash Boo Shultz** : Jay Parsons compared Ash Boo Shultz to Kurt Cobain upon first hearing his music (the "Cloudboy" demo), seeing him as "the second coming of like, Kirk O'Bane in Canada."
influence @Nirvana **Ash Boo Shultz's Favorite Band** : Nirvana was Ash Boo Shultz's favorite band growing up, and producer Tog Salter encouraged him to embrace their intensity in USS's music during the "Approved" EP sessions.
influence **Amadeus Movie as Creative Process Analogy** : The movie "Amadeus" and Salieri's observation of Mozart's "direct download" creative process is used by Ash Boo Shultz as an analogy for USS's evolved, highly receptive songwriting method.
influence @Imagine Dragons **Radio Success Paving the Way** : The simultaneous radio success of Imagine Dragons, particularly with "Radioactive," helped open a lane for USS's sound and momentum on rock radio.
influence @Twenty One Pilots **Musical Similarities/Ahead of the Curve** : USS noted that their first EP, "Welling the Sea Drive," contained sounds similar to what Twenty One Pilots had been doing in later years, suggesting USS was "a couple of years ahead of the curve."
influence @Neil Young **Inspiration for Drug Recovery Analogy** : Neil Young's lyrical themes (e.g., "every junkie has a setting sun") were referenced by Ash Boo Shultz when describing his recovery from a drug bender.

## Curiosities
curiosity @Ash Boo Shultz **Chlorophyll Harmonic Stage Setup** : Ash Boo Shultz's early solo performances included having ten plants on stage, which he called the "chlorophyll harmonic," with microphones set up to capture "plant solos."
curiosity @Ash Boo Shultz **Neuroplasticity Experiment Performance** : In early solo performances, Ash Boo Shultz would play two cassette tapes simultaneously (one self-help, one heavy metal from the Judgment Night soundtrack) and make prolonged eye contact with the audience, exploring neuroplasticity and disrupting the brain.
curiosity @Jay Parsons **Origin of "Human Kabob" Nickname** : Jay Parsons' nickname originated from his university thesis paper on Vlad Drakul (Vlad the Impaler), whom he humorously likened to a "human kabob" for impaling enemies' heads on stakes. He adopted it as his handle for DJ gigs.
curiosity @USS **Abstract Correlations as Songwriting Method** : The core of USS's music creation initially involved Ash and Jay making each other laugh with "lucid abstract correlations" that they would then spontaneously start singing.
curiosity @USS **"Word Jams" in Song Titles** : USS creates unique song titles, such as "Neurochemical Warfare Gas Masquerade," by deliberately combining words that "have never sat on the bus beside each other in the history of language."
curiosity **Sony ATV Publishing Company's Challenge to USS** : Sony ATV signed USS but challenged them to write songs with choruses, noting the band's prior success without traditional song structures.
curiosity @Tog Salter **Producer for Approved EP** : Tog Salter is identified as a producer and songwriter who worked with USS on their "Approved" EP and previously with Josh Groban and Lights. He encouraged Ash to "go big" and embrace his inner Nirvana.
curiosity @Ash Boo Shultz **Initial Resistance to Collaboration** : Ash Boo Shultz initially reacted hysterically and nearly walked away from collaborating with producer Tog Salter, revealing deep-seated control issues and perfectionism linked to childhood abandonment. This experience ultimately led to personal growth and collaborative breakthroughs.
curiosity @Jay Parsons **A&R-like Role within USS** : Jay Parsons perceived himself as performing an A&R (Artist and Repertoire) function within USS, recognizing Ash's talent and helping to develop the band, despite also being a core member.
curiosity @USS **Fragmented vs. Receptive Creative Process Evolution** : USS's songwriting process transformed from a "fragmented creative experience" (like assembling 10,000 puzzle pieces from 700 puzzles) to an almost "direct download of information," compared to Mozart's effortless composition, following Ash's personal healing and increased receptivity.
curiosity @USS **"Advanced Basics" Life Philosophy** : The title of USS's 2014 album, "Advanced Basics," embodies a life philosophy that "the simplest things are the hardest things."
curiosity @USS **Two Versions of Advanced Basics Album** : The 2014 "Advanced Basics" album was released in two versions: a regular version and an international version.
curiosity @Ash Boo Shultz **Drug Bender During "This Is The Best" Recording** : Ash Boo Shultz recorded the breakthrough single "This Is The Best" at the culmination of an eight-month drug bender, admitting to being on "every alphabet drug" in the studio.
curiosity @Ash Boo Shultz **Recovery Challenges During "Advanced Basics"** : Ash Boo Shultz faced severe personal challenges and a period of intense recovery ("lights out for a while") after recording "This Is The Best," struggling significantly to complete the "Advanced Basics" album while at "1% power."
curiosity @Jay Parsons **Patience with Ash's Struggles** : Jay Parsons demonstrated extraordinary patience and support for Ash Boo Shultz during Ash's drug recovery and struggles while making "Advanced Basics," a fact Ash attributes to Jay being a "saint" and "winning the lottery of life" by meeting him.
curiosity @USS **Seven Songs as a "Good Number" for EPs** : USS considers seven songs to be an ideal length or "good number" for their extended plays, as exemplified by "New World Alphabet."
curiosity @USS **Geographic Influence on New World Alphabet's Mood** : The creative environment significantly impacted "New World Alphabet": the first four songs, written in California, have a distinct feel, while the later three songs became "really dark" after the band returned to Canada in winter.
curiosity @Ash Boo Shultz **Bowling as a Post-Drug Hobby** : After ceasing drug use, Ash Boo Shultz actively sought out conventional human activities, taking up bowling as a new hobby to understand "what human beings did."
curiosity @USS **"Something's Happening Here" Song Inspiration** : The phrase "something's happening here," frequently used by Ash Boo Shultz's bowling buddy, directly inspired the writing of the earwormy song "Who's With Me."
curiosity @Ash Boo Shultz **Overcoming Stage Anxiety** : Ash Boo Shultz previously performed wearing a hat and sunglasses, with his eyes closed, due to an "incapability of connection with other humans." After personal growth, he stopped this practice, marking a significant personal and performative shift.
curiosity @Jay Parsons **Radio DJ Career Beginnings (2012)** : In 2012, while USS was in Mexico with Sonic in Edmonton, Al Ford from Sonic offered Jay Parsons an hour-long weekend radio show playing modern electronic remixes from the rock world.
curiosity @Jay Parsons **"The Military Mixtape" Radio Show (2017)** : Jay Parsons hosts "The Military Mixtape" on Saturday nights on 102.1 The Edge, starting in 2017, which he considers one of the greatest experiences of his life outside of USS.
curiosity **Alan Ford, Radio Personality** : Al Ford, from Sonic in Edmonton, offered Jay Parsons his first radio show opportunity in 2012.
