## Members
member @Tom DeLonge **Blink-182 Co-founder/Guitarist/Vocalist**: From Poway, San Diego suburb; skateboarder, C student; played trumpet badly; performed solo acoustic as "Big Oily Men"; co-founded Blink with Scott Raynor and Mark Hoppus; agreed with signing to MCA; had vocal problems during Dude Ranch sessions; wrote "All the Small Things" for his girlfriend Jennifer.
member @Mark Hoppus **Blink-182 Co-founder/Bassist/Vocalist**: Born in Washington, D.C.; moved to San Diego due to father's Navy transfer and parents' divorce; discovered The Cure and The Smiths; worked at Little Caesar's pizza; expelled from school in grade 8 for being drunk; co-founded Blink; at 18, he was older than Tom and Scott (15); his sister dated Tom's friend; signed the initial contract with Cargo Records as he was old enough; had a day job hauling cement; agreed with signing to MCA; strained his voice badly during Dude Ranch sessions while trying to sing "Dammit"; met The Aquabats members at a Wendy's in Ottawa, starting a friendship; was single and felt lonely on tour, inspiring "Adam's Song".
member @Scott Raynor **Blink-182 Original Drummer**: Met Tom DeLonge at a party in grade 8; was in a band that was falling apart; played covers of Misfits, Danzig, Gwar with Tom; 15 years old when the band started; family moved to Reno, Nevada, so he moved in with his sister in San Diego to make records; was under 18 and ineligible to sign the Cargo Records contract; opposed signing with MCA, calling it an "unpunk move"; had issues with living arrangements, leading the band to pay for his flights to Reno; broke both his heels jumping drunk, recorded parts for Dude Ranch on crutches/wheelchair; problems with drinking continued after May 1998 return; eventually fired by Tom and Mark, reportedly over the phone, for his behavior hurting performances.
member @Mark Kroll **Blink-182 Temporary Drummer**: Scott Raynor's high school buddy who temporarily replaced Scott when his family moved to Reno.
member @Travis Barker **Blink-182 Drummer (from 1998)**: Drummer for The Aquabats (alias "The Baron Von Tito") before joining Blink-182 in 1998; worked as a trash collector; learned 20 Blink-182 songs in 45 minutes (legend); mother died when he was 15, leading him to throw himself into music; took jazz and funk rhythm lessons, practiced rudiments extensively; played for both Blink and The Aquabats for a period; asked to be a hired musician for "Enema of the State" sessions before becoming a full-time member.

## Albums
album @Blink **Fly Swatter – Debut Demo Tape**: Recorded in Scott Raynor's bedroom, distributed on cassette, a mix of original songs and covers, and led off with "Rebok Commercial".
album @Blink **Demo Number Two – Second Demo Tape**: Recorded in Scott Raynor's bedroom, featured the track "Marble Man", and released in 1993, beginning to show the band's developing sound.
album @Blink **Boota – First Professional Recording**: A 15-track indie release recorded in about two days in January 1994, essentially a collection of everything the band had written with much better audio quality, but sold poorly with almost zero distribution; featured the song "Carousel".
album @Blink **Cheshire Cat – Debut Album on Cargo Records**: Released February 17, 1995, recorded in six days (16 tracks, many live), some recycled from previous releases and some new; initially on cassette then CD due to bootlegging; sold about 3,000 copies; featured "M&M's" and "Wasting Time". Early versions were credited to "Blink" before the name change to Blink-182.
album @Blink-182 **Dude Ranch – Major Label Debut**: Released June 17, 1997, recorded around Christmas 1996 into the New Year under rushed conditions; initially not well-received by MCA; sold ~40,000 copies initially, but sold half a million copies within a year after "Dammit" became a radio hit; featured four singles including "Dammit" and "Josie".
album @Blink-182 **Enema of the State – Breakthrough Album**: Recorded January-March 1999 with producer Jerry Finn; originally titled "Turn Your Head and Cough"; described by Tom DeLonge as "nursery rhymes on steroids"; sold over 15 million copies globally; featured hit singles "What's My Age Again?", "All the Small Things", and "Adam's Song"; cover featured porn star Janine Lindemueller, with three versions due to Red Cross symbol controversy.
album @Green Day **Dookie – Landmark Album**: Produced by Jerry Finn, the same producer who worked on Blink-182's "Enema of the State".
album @Blink-182 **California – 2016 Album**: Referenced as the source of the single "She's Out of My Mind" from the current version of Blink-182.

## Songs
song @Blink **Rebok Commercial – Debut Demo Track**: The leading track from Blink's first cassette demo, "Fly Swatter", recorded in Scott Raynor's bedroom.
song @Blink **Marble Man – Second Demo Track**: Featured on Blink's second demo tape, "Demo Number Two", recorded in Scott Raynor's bedroom in 1993, beginning to show the Blink-182 sound.
song @Blink **Carousel – First Professional Recording Track**: A song from Blink's first professional recording, the indie release "Boota" (January 1994).
song @Blink **M&M's – Cheshire Cat Single**: Written in Tom DeLonge's parents' garage, released February 17, 1995, and became a minor hit single in some parts of California from the "Cheshire Cat" album.
song @Blink **Wasting Time – Cheshire Cat Single**: The second single released from the "Cheshire Cat" album.
song @Blink-182 **Dammit – Dude Ranch Breakthrough Single**: Promoted to radio in September 1997, leading to "Dude Ranch" selling half a million copies; sung by Mark Hoppus (whose strained voice during sessions gave it a distinct sound); Tom DeLonge sang the chorus live; it's about a fictional breakup.
song @Blink-182 **Josie – Dude Ranch Single**: The fourth and final single from "Dude Ranch"; about a dog owned by Elise Rogers of Dance Hall Crashers; the video featured actress Alyssa Milano and a real San Diego restaurant, Sombrero's.
song @Blink-182 **What's My Age Again? – Enema of the State Single**: First single from "Enema of the State", released June 1, 1999; originally titled "Peter Pan Complex"; a pop radio hit (#58 US, #17 UK); video featured the band running naked (in flesh-colored speedos) in L.A., which led to the band wanting more creative control over their image; part of an early digital distribution experiment.
song @Blink-182 **All the Small Things – Enema of the State Hit Single**: Written by Tom DeLonge for his girlfriend, Jennifer, and deliberately crafted to be catchy and a hit; sold over 500,000 copies in the US and UK; a top 40 hit (#6 US, #2 UK, Top 10 in multiple other countries), significantly contributing to "Enema of the State" selling over 15 million copies globally.
song @Blink-182 **Adam's Song – Enema of the State Serious Single**: Mark Hoppus's personal song about loneliness on tour and inspired by ideas of depression and suicide from a magazine letter; intended as an anti-suicide song; became controversial when linked to the Columbine murders, specifically a suicide where it was played on repeat; received mail from teens stating it helped them choose not to take their own lives.
song @Blink-182 **She's Out of My Mind – California (2016) Single**: A single from the 2016 album "California", representing the current version of Blink-182.
song @Big Oily Men **Who's Gonna Shave Your Back – Tom DeLonge's Solo Performance**: A solo acoustic song performed by Tom DeLonge under the name "Big Oily Men" at a battle of the bands contest.

## Curiosities
curiosity @Blink-182 **"The Millennium Falcon" Band Van**: The name given to Blink-182's first own tour van, purchased after they earned enough money from gigs.
curiosity @Blink-182 **Band Name Origin (182)**: The number "182" in the band's name has no true meaning, debunking myths about Scarface's F-bomb count or a connection to the movie "Turk-182", with the band stating any connection was unconscious.
curiosity @Blink-182 **Correct Spelling of Name**: The band's name should be spelled with a lowercase 'b' and hyphenated ("Blink-182").
curiosity @Blink-182 **"Enema of the State" Nudity in Video**: For the "What's My Age Again?" video, the band ran around L.A. appearing naked, though they wore flesh-colored Speedos; they later regretted the "joke band" reputation and vowed to take more creative control over their public image, preferring to "leave nudity to the Red Hot Chili Peppers."
curiosity @Blink-182 **"Enema of the State" Digital Distribution Experiment**: In 1999, 30-second clips of "What's My Age Again?" and two other songs were made available online via the now-defunct A2B Music, a radical move for digital distribution at the time.
curiosity @Blink-182 **"Enema of the State" Album Cover (Janine Lindemueller)**: The cover of "Enema of the State" featured porn star Janine Lindemueller as a sexy nurse; the band claimed they were unaware of her profession, simply choosing her photo from a stack provided.
curiosity @Blink-182 **"Enema of the State" Album Cover (Red Cross Controversy)**: There were three versions of the "Enema of the State" album cover due to a dispute over the use of the Red Cross symbol; the American Red Cross cited a violation of the Geneva Convention for its commercial use, leading to the symbol being completely removed from later versions.
curiosity @The Aquabats **Punk Rock Superheroes Persona**: The band's unique "stick" of performing as punk rock superheroes, complete with costumes, special effects, pyro, and audience participation, including a "Chicken Man" who handed out fried chicken.
