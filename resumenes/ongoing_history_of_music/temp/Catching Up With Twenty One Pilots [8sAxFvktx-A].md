## 21 pilots - Early Career and Formation
artist @21 pilots **Pre-Breakthrough Period (2009-2015)** : The band endured six years of "hard slogging" with minimal public recognition before their significant breakthrough with the 'Blurryface' album in 2015.
member @Tyler Joseph **Hometown and Family Background** : The central figure and frontman of 21 pilots, originating from Columbus, Ohio; his mother was a teacher, and his father was a high school principal and basketball coach.
member @Tyler Joseph **Early Musical Exploration** : Began experimenting with an old keyboard, leading to his initial foray into music.
instrument @Keyboard **Tyler Joseph's First Instrument** : An old keyboard that Tyler Joseph started "missing around with" in his early days.
member @Tyler Joseph **Athletic Background** : Was deeply involved in basketball for a period, playing as a point guard.
album @Tyler Joseph **No Fun Intended (2008) – Solo Debut** : A solo record made by Tyler Joseph at approximately 17 or 18 years old, during his senior year of high school, and recorded in his parents' basement.
member @Nick Thomas **Guest Appearance on Solo Album** : Contributed some guitar parts to Tyler Joseph's solo album, 'No Fun Intended', prior to the formation of 21 pilots.
song @Tyler Joseph **Blasphemy (2008) – Solo Track** : A track from Tyler Joseph's 2008 solo album, 'No Fun Intended', created during his high school years.
artist @21 pilots **Initial Formation (c. 2009)** : Formed approximately a year after Tyler Joseph's solo record, with original members Tyler Joseph, Nick Thomas (bass), and Chris Salih (drums), after Tyler won a university scholarship. They moved in together to write music and perform grassroots, DIY gigs.
member @Chris Salih **Original Drummer** : One of the founding members of 21 pilots, serving as the band's first drummer.
curiosity @21 pilots **Grassroots Promotion by Tyler's Mother** : Tyler Joseph's mother actively supported the band's early career by distributing tickets to their shows outside Ohio State University games.
artist @21 pilots **Early Performance Strategies** : Participated in "battle of the bands" events and shared stages with hardcore bands to secure performance opportunities.
curiosity @21 pilots **Considered Band Names** : The band contemplated using "Chill Coat" and "Bicycle Thief" as their name before deciding on "21 pilots."
curiosity @21 pilots **Origin of Band Name (Arthur Miller Play)** : The band's name is derived from Arthur Miller's 1947 play 'All My Sons', which tells a true story about an Ohio man who conspired with the army to approve faulty aircraft engines in World War II, leading to the deaths of 21 pilots.
curiosity @21 pilots **Stylized Non-Hyphenated Name** : The deliberate omission of a hyphen between "20" and "1" in "21 pilots" is a design choice by Tyler Joseph, focused on its visual appearance in text and adaptability for logos and graphic design.
curiosity @21 pilots **Early Logo Elements** : Early representations of the 21 pilots logo included diagonal lines running through the O's in the band's name.

## 21 pilots - Debut Album and Lineup Changes
album @21 pilots **21 Pilots (2009) – Self-Titled Debut Album** : A self-produced indie record, released on December 29, 2009, and recorded at home. The lineup for this album included Tyler Joseph, Nick Thomas, and Chris Salih.
album @21 pilots **21 Pilots (2009) – Initial Sales Performance** : Despite being a low-key, home-recorded indie release, the debut album sold approximately 115,000 copies.
song @21 pilots **Oh Ms Believer (2009)** : A track featured on the band's self-titled, self-released debut album.
member @Chris Salih **Departure from 21 pilots (2011)** : Left the band in 2011 to prioritize his employment at a music store.
member @Nick Thomas **Departure from 21 pilots (2011)** : Departed from the band a month after Chris Salih in 2011 to concentrate on his university studies. He later assisted with the band's merchandise but not as a musician.
member @Tyler Joseph **Period as Sole Member (July 2011)** : After the departures of both Chris Salih and Nick Thomas in 2011, Tyler Joseph remained as the sole member of 21 pilots for a period in July.
member @Josh Dun **Joining 21 pilots (2011)** : A drummer who knew Chris Salih from a music store, Josh Dun joined Tyler Joseph in 21 pilots after hearing his music, around the time the second album 'Regional at Best' was released. He had previously considered pursuing studio drumming work in Nashville.
instrument @Drums **Josh Dun's Instrument** : Josh Dun became the drummer for 21 pilots.
album @21 pilots **Regional at Best (2011) – Second Indie Album** : Released on July 8, 2011, this indie album was primarily Tyler Joseph's project, featuring only a couple of small guest appearances. Tyler described it as a "glorified mixtape."
album @21 pilots **Regional at Best (2011) – Discontinuation** : The album was "completely discontinued, deleted, wiped from the face of the earth" as a condition of the band's subsequent record deal. Copies were initially distributed to fans.
album @21 pilots **Regional at Best (2011) – Current Availability and Value** : The album notably lacks a Wikipedia page. It was released digitally and as a CD. Today, physical copies sell for $35 to $900 online, and it is primarily available for streaming only on Deezer (a French service) and in odd tracks on YouTube.
song @21 pilots **Forest (2011)** : A song from the discontinued album 'Regional at Best', recorded during the period when Tyler Joseph was the sole member of 21 pilots.
curiosity @Tinderbox **Dual Album Package** : A label that issued a hard copy edition packaging both 'Regional at Best' and the self-titled debut album. This package's purpose was to attract the attention of record labels, and it is now listed as "blocked from sale" online.
artist @21 pilots **Signing with Fueled by Ramen (April 2012)** : The band was signed by Fueled by Ramen, an indie label based out of New York, in April 2012, after the label became aware of their significant indie fan base and sampled material from their first two albums.
curiosity @Fueled by Ramen **Condition for 'Regional at Best' Disappearance** : A specific condition of 21 pilots' signing deal with Fueled by Ramen was that the band had to ensure the 'Regional at Best' album was made to disappear.
artist @21 pilots **Strong Ohio Fanbase** : By the time they signed their record deal, the band had cultivated a substantial fan base in Ohio, with some shows attracting nearly 2,000 attendees.
event @21 pilots **Low-Attendance Out-of-State Gig (July 2011)** : An early out-of-state performance in July 2011 was attended by only 12 people, illustrating their limited reach beyond Ohio at that time.
album @21 pilots **Three Songs (2012) – Fueled by Ramen EP** : The first release under the Fueled by Ramen label, an EP released in July 2012. Two of its three songs were re-recorded versions from 'Regional at Best'.
song @21 pilots **Guns for Hands (2012)** : A song from the 'Three Songs' EP, also notably one of five tracks from 'Regional at Best' that was re-recorded for the subsequent album 'Vessel'.

## 21 pilots - Vessel Album Era
album @21 pilots **Vessel (2013) – Third Studio Album** : Released on January 8, 2013, this album garnered good reviews and played a key role in expanding the band's fan base.
curiosity @21 pilots **The Skeleton Clique – Fanbase Name** : The band's growing fan base became officially known as "The Skeleton Clique" during the 'Vessel' album era.
album @21 pilots **Vessel (2013) – Touring and Festival Exposure** : The album's success led to international tours and appearances at major festivals, including Lollapalooza and Bonnaroo.
event @Lollapalooza **21 pilots Performance (Vessel Era)** : 21 pilots performed at Lollapalooza as part of their touring in support of the 'Vessel' album.
event @Bonnaroo **21 pilots Performance (Vessel Era)** : 21 pilots performed at Bonnaroo as part of their touring in support of the 'Vessel' album.
album @21 pilots **Vessel (2013) – Radio Play** : The album received a "little bit of radio play," contributing to its growing popularity.
song @21 pilots **Car Radio (2013)** : A successful single from the 'Vessel' album, directly inspired by Tyler Joseph's car being broken into and his radio and CDs stolen, which prompted him to reflect on driving in silence.

## 21 pilots - Blurryface and Trench Mythology
album @21 pilots **Blurryface (2015) – Fourth Album & Breakthrough** : This fourth album, released in 2015, marked the band's major breakthrough and was notable for introducing more mystical and complex narrative elements.
album @21 pilots **Blurryface (2015) – Core Themes** : The album's narrative and lyrical content are primarily centered on themes of doubt, insecurity, anxiety, and incorporates a "dollop of religion."
curiosity @Tyler Joseph **Blurryface Character Concept** : Tyler Joseph conceived the character "Blurryface" as an avatar to embody his personal feelings of doubt, insecurity, and anxiety during that period.
curiosity @Tyler Joseph **Visual Representation of Blurryface** : Tyler Joseph frequently used black paint on his hands and neck during live performances and video shoots as a physical representation of the Blurryface character.
curiosity **Blurryface Twitter Account** : An official Blurryface Twitter account exists with over 150,000 followers, notably without having posted any tweets.
curiosity @21 pilots **Blurryface Era New Logo** : A new band logo was introduced around the release of 'Blurryface', characterized by a bar, a hyphen, and a slash, symbols found on a standard computer keyboard.
curiosity @21 pilots **Fan Engagement with New Logo** : Fans were encouraged to interpret and create their own versions of the new Blurryface era logo.
album @21 pilots **Blurryface (2015) – Special Limited Editions** : The album was released in various formats, including a limited edition, "very, very red" cassette. It also featured "inkblot special vinyl editions," which are highly collectible, with no two being identical, and can trade for hundreds of dollars.
album @21 pilots **Blurryface (2015) – Instrumental Album Anomaly** : An instrumental version of 'Blurryface' surfaced in the UK, described as very difficult to find and possibly a bootleg, contributing to the band's enigmatic image.
curiosity @21 pilots **Reddit Blurryface Narrative Theories** : Members of the Skeleton Clique, the band's fanbase, actively developed numerous theories on Reddit attempting to connect all 14 songs on 'Blurryface' into a single overarching narrative.
curiosity @21 pilots **The Skeleton Clique – Fanbase Culture** : The band's "hardcore fans," known as the Skeleton Clique, are recognized for their welcoming and inclusive nature, referring to each other as "frens" (spelled F-R-E-N-S) and often using the phrase "Stay Street."
curiosity @21 pilots **The Skeleton Clique – Online Growth** : The Skeleton Clique's Twitter account was approaching 350,000 followers.
album @21 pilots **Blurryface (2015) – Significant Commercial Success** : The album sold close to 2 million copies, a notable achievement in the era of streaming music.
album @21 pilots **Blurryface (2015) – Number of Singles** : Six singles were released from the 'Blurryface' record.
song @21 pilots **Stressed Out (2015)** : A prominent single from 21 pilots' breakthrough album, 'Blurryface'.
artist @21 pilots **Hiatus for Creative Focus (July 2017 - April 2018)** : The band entered a "dark" period on July 6, 2017, after completing two world tours for 'Blurryface', allowing Tyler Joseph and Josh Dun to focus on creating new music without interruption for nearly a year.
curiosity @21 pilots **dmaorg.info – Hidden Website Discovery (April 2018)** : In April 2018, a hidden website address, dmaorg.info, was discovered embedded within a gift image on the 'Vessel' album page of the 21 pilots online store.
curiosity @21 pilots **dmaorg.info – Initial Warning Message** : The landing page of dmaorg.info displayed a cryptic warning message: "404 error. You are in violation. They mustn't know you were here. No one should ever find out about this. You can never tell anyone about this. For the sake of the other survival, you must keep this silent. We must keep silent. No one can know. No one can know. No one can know. Violation code 1539862_14."
curiosity @21 pilots **dmaorg.info – Elaborate Narrative Clues** : The website led fans down an "insane rat hole of clues," revealing images of a new character named Clancy who was "struggling with dma," and featured a series of posts from "Clancy's Journal" containing clues about the upcoming 'Trench' album and its songs.
curiosity @21 pilots **Clancy – Character in 'Trench' Lore** : A character in the elaborate storyline of the 'Trench' album, who posted a file named "17-35.4527" prior to the release of the "Chlorine" single. The text in this file was later altered to spell out "Ned."
curiosity @21 pilots **Ned – Character in 'Chlorine' Video** : A "weird little alien animal character" who debuted in the music video for the single "Chlorine," and whose name was revealed through an Easter egg on the dmaorg.info website.
curiosity @21 pilots **Chlorine Single Chemical Easter Egg** : Before the release of "Chlorine" as a single, Clancy posted a file named "17-35.4527" on dmaorg.info, where the numbers 17 and 35.427 correspond to the atomic number and atomic weight of the element chlorine.
album @21 pilots **Trench (2018) – Fifth Studio Album** : The band's fifth album, released in 2018, which significantly expanded their intricate, narrative-driven lore.
album @21 pilots **Trench (2018) – Thematic Exploration** : The album's themes delve into doubt, insecurity, anxiety, faith, and incorporates elements of suicide.
curiosity @21 pilots **Dema – Fictional City in 'Trench' Lore** : The 'Trench' album introduces a fictional world built around the city of Dema, a name translated from Zoroastrianism to mean "Tower of Silence."
curiosity **Tower of Silence – Zoroastrianism Concept** : In Zoroastrianism, a "Tower of Silence" is a structure where followers traditionally placed dead bodies to be consumed by vultures.
curiosity @21 pilots **Niko – Chief Bishop of Dema** : The first character introduced within the 'Trench' world, depicted as one of nine "bishops" and serving as the chief bishop of Dema.
curiosity @21 pilots **Niko's Identity and Blurryface Connection** : The name "Niko" is derived from "Nicolas Bourbaki," a pseudonym used by a group of French mathematicians in the 1930s. In the 'Trench' lore, Nicolas Bourbaki is revealed to be the true name of Blurryface, the central character from the previous album.
curiosity @21 pilots **The Banditos – Resistance Group in 'Trench'** : A group within Dema portrayed as "the good guys" who seek to liberate the city's inhabitants.
curiosity @21 pilots **Banditos' Distinctive Yellow Color** : The Banditos are uniquely identified by a specific shade of yellow that the bishops, including Niko, are unable to perceive.
curiosity @21 pilots **'Trench' World Imagery** : The intricate lore surrounding the 'Trench' album includes recurring imagery of vultures and cheetahs, along with coded messages hidden within Clancy's Journal posts.
song @21 pilots **Jumpsuit (2018) – First Single from 'Trench'** : The lead single from 'Trench', introduced to fans on July 6, 2018, via an email containing a closing eye image and the caption, "Are you still sleeping?"
curiosity @21 pilots **Praise for World-Building and Mystery** : The band is highly commended for their extensive and immersive fantasy world creation, which is seen as a way to revive the sense of mystery in music lost in the modern, information-saturated internet era.
