## albums
**In A Flat Field (1980) - Debut Album** : Released in the fall of 1980, this record established the stylistic groundwork for the emerging British Goth movement. "A record that set the pace for the British Goth movement."
**The Sky's Gone Out (Year unspecified) - Biggest Seller** : This album became the biggest seller of Bauhaus's career, largely due to a combination of factors including David Bowie's fandom, the band's appearance in his movie "The Hunger," Peter Murphy's role in Maxell commercials, and their cover of David Bowie's "Ziggy Stardust." "The Sky's Gone Out, the biggest seller of their career."
**Burning from the Inside (1983) - Final Album with Peter Murphy** : Released in June 1983, this was the last official Bauhaus album to feature Peter Murphy. Its recording commenced while Peter was hospitalized with pneumonia, forcing the rest of the band to work on the album without him, and during this process, they found that things worked "very well without Peter." "The resulting album was called Burning from the Inside. And this was the last official Bajaos singer."

**Bela Lugosi's Dead (1979) - Debut Single** : Issued in August 1979 by indie label Small Wonder, this atmospheric piece served as Bauhaus's one and only single. It featured a Bassenova beat, a distinctive three-note bass line by David J, a reggae feel from Daniel Ash, and an erotic, vampiric vocal approach by Peter Murphy, inspired by the actor who played Dracula. Clocking in at over nine and a half minutes, its unusual length for a debut single was a risk taken by the label. ## curiosities
**Early Band Formations - Pre-Bauhaus** : Before forming Bauhaus, the members were involved in a series of unsuccessful bands with names like This Bag Is Not A Toy, The Submerged Tenth, The Craze, and Jack Plug and The Sockets, none of which achieved significant recognition. "Before there was Bajaos, there were series of bands."
**Band Naming - Bauhaus 1919** : Initially, the band considered names like "The Drugs" or "Freeze." Four days before their first gig, they settled on "Bauhaus 1919," drawing inspiration from the name and founding year of a German art movement. "But four days before their first gig, they settled on Bajaos 1919, the name and year of the beginning of a German art movement."
**Name Shortening - From Bauhaus 1919 to Bauhaus** : Within a few weeks of their inaugural performance on December 31, 1978, the band decided to shorten their name from "Bauhaus 1919" to simply "Bauhaus." "Within a few weeks, their name had been shortened to just Bajaos."
**The Bauhaus Mobile - Band Touring Vehicle** : The group purchased a hearse from an undertaker and customized it into their official touring vehicle, affectionately naming it their "Bauhaus Mobile" for their travels. "The group also bought a Hearse from a Hunder taker and converted it into their official touring vehicle."
**Peter Murphy's Maxell Commercial - Iconic Ad Appearance** : In 1981, Peter Murphy was selected to appear in a TV commercial for Maxell cassettes, earning him 10,000 pounds. The iconic magazine ads based on these commercials, featuring a person in a chair being "blown away" by music, continued to be used for more than 15 years after their initial release. "The guy in the chair? That's Peter Murphy."
**The Hunger - Vampire Movie Appearance** : David Bowie, a significant fan of Bauhaus, successfully lobbied for the band to be cast in his 1981 vampire movie, "The Hunger." In a scene filmed in a London club ironically named Heaven, Bauhaus performs their signature song, "Bela Lugosi's Dead." "Bowie just loved what Bajaos had done with one of his old songs."
**Band Breakup - Hammersmith Palais (1983)** : Bauhaus concluded their initial run with a final performance on July 5, 1983, at the Hammersmith Palais in London. The breakup was attributed to rising egos, creative differences, the pressures of touring, and Peter Murphy's severe bout of pneumonia, which forced the band to record an album without him, leading to internal divisions. "As the gig at the Hammersmith Pele came to an end, David J grabbed the mic and said, rest in peace. And that was that. Bajaos was dead."
**Band Reunion - 15 Years Later (1998)** : After 15 years and four days since their last London performance, Bauhaus reunited on stage at the Hollywood Athletic Club, with thousands paying $55 US for admission. This was followed by successful gigs at the Hollywood Palladium, selling 7,000 tickets in just over 15 minutes, leading to a full reunion tour, most of which were sellouts. "15 years and four days after that last show in London, Baha's appeared on stage at the Hollywood Athletic Club."
**Godfathers of Goth - Enduring Influence** : Bauhaus is widely recognized as one of the founding groups and pioneers of the original British Goth movement. Their distinctive glamour, energy, art school sensibilities, and attitude of "doom and gloom" had a profound and infectious impact globally, influencing numerous dark bands, including Nine Inch Nails, Marilyn Manson, White Zombie, Courtney Love, and The Smashing Pumpkins. "The band often described as the Godfathers of Goth."
**Crackle (1998) - Greatest Hits Compilation** : For those interested in learning more about the band's music, a 1998 greatest hits CD called "Crackle" is recommended, noted for how well the music still sounds today. "you should pick up a comp of a 1998 greatest hit CD called Crackle."

## curiosities
**Devout Catholic Upbringing** : Peter Murphy was raised in a devout Catholic family, an aspect of his background that shaped his early life. "raised in a devote Catholic family."
**Distinctive Appearance** : Murphy was characterized by his elegant, impossibly thin physique and prominent cheekbones, contributing to his distinctive visual identity. "elegant, impossibly thin, great cheekbones."
**Relocation to Turkey - Study of Islam** : In the spring of 1990, funded by the success of his "Deep" album, Peter Murphy moved his family to Turkey, where he began an intensive study of Islam and eventually converted to the faith, becoming a Muslim. "he began to study Islam, eventually becoming a Muslim."
**Financial Struggles in Turkey** : By the mid-1990s, Peter Murphy faced financial difficulties in Turkey, as the costs of full-time Arabic study, combined with earnings from translating books and composing music for his wife's dance company, proved insufficient compared to his potential income from reuniting with his former bandmates. "Peter Murphy was running out of money in Turkey."

# genre - Goth

## curiosities
**British Goth Movement - Defining Elements** : The British Goth movement, pioneered by bands like Bauhaus, was characterized by its glamour, energy, art school sensibilities, and an overriding attitude of "doom and gloom." This particular sound, look, and attitude became highly infectious and spread globally. "The glamour, the energy, the art school sensibilities, and the attitude of doom and gloom proved to be infectious."
**Cultural Context - Escapism in Early 80s** : The theater, romance, horror, and angst of Goth provided an escape that went "far beyond the real life doom and gloom of the early 80s," a period marked by the Cold War and a global recession. Goth fans embraced the dark aesthetic, concluding that "since we're all going hell anyway, we might as well dress the part and dance towards the apocalypse." "The theater and romance and horror and angst of God went far beyond the real life doom and gloom of the early 80s, even with the Cold War and the global recession."
