# genre - Nerd Rock

## curiosities
**Definition and Scope** : Nerd rock is identified as a genre of music that falls squarely within the world of alternative music. The term can refer to a specific sound and style, as well as the look, attitude, and personalities of the musicians who create it. "even nerds have their own genre of music. And as you might guess, it falls squarely in the world of alternative music."
**Characteristics and Themes** : Nerd rock, along with its later iteration Geek Rock, does not possess a common sound but is unified by themes of humor, fun, self-deprecation, an appreciation of the mundane, and extensive use of irony. "There wasn't really a common sound, but they were all about humor and fun and self-deprecation, appreciation of the mundane and loads and loads of irony."
**Key Bands of the 90s** : The early and mid-1990s witnessed a significant rise in nerd rock bands. Besides Weezer and They Might Be Giants, notable groups included The Rentals (a Weezer spin-off), Fountains of Wayne, Ween, The Aquabats, Man or Astroman, Andrew W.K., Ben Folds Five, and "Weird Al" Yankovic, who holds a distinct position in the genre. "The early in mid-90s were a great time for Nerd Rock. We got Weezer in the spin-off band The Rentals."

# genre - Filke Rock

## curiosities
**Origin and Characteristics (1950s)** : Filke rock, a precursor to nerd rock, emerged in the 1950s as a form of folk music. Its name originated from a typo (I next to O on the keyboard). It was favored by fans of science fiction, horror movies, and fantasy, and typically involved musicians playing acoustic guitars at sci-fi conventions in gatherings known as "House Filks" or "Filke circles." "The name itself is a typo... These were folk songs, favored by certain fans of science fiction and horror movies and fantasy."
**Cultural Impact** : Filke music has demonstrated enduring cultural significance, evidenced by its dedicated awards program, the Pegasus Awards, and the existence of a Filke Hall of Fame. "Filke music is so enduring that it has its own awards program called the Pegasus Awards, and there's a Filke Hall of Fame."

# curiosity

**Early History of the Word "Nerd" (1500s-1970s)** : The word "nerd" first appeared around 1500 in a Spanish book, "A True Discourse of the Assault Committed upon the most noble prince Prince William of Orange," though its specific meaning in that context is unknown. It fell out of use by 1725, re-emerging in Dr. Seuss's work in 1950 to describe a creature. In 1951, Newsweek Magazine reported its use in Detroit as slang for an "awkward sort of dude." The term gained wider popularity in 1974 with the TV series "Happy Days," where Fonzie used it to describe uncool characters like Richie and Potsie. "According to these nerds, nerd, the word, shows up for the first time in a book called A True Discourse of the Assault Committed upon the most noble prince Prince William of Orange..."
**Evolution of "Nerd" as a Compliment** : By the late 1970s, coinciding with the rise of personal computers, consumer technology, and science fiction (like Star Wars), the use of "nerd" became more widespread, including in films like "Revenge of the Nerds" in the 1980s. In contemporary technological society, "being a nerd is actually a compliment," with individuals aspiring to be like figures such as Bill Gates, Elon Musk, and Mark Zuckerberg, and TV shows like "The Big Bang Theory" and "Silicon Valley" celebrating "nerddom." "But now in our technological society, being a nerd is actually a compliment."
**Influence of 1980s Music Videos** : The 1980s, particularly the era of MTV and MuchMusic, played a crucial role in fostering a love for music videos due to their storytelling, dancing, and distinctive styles. Iconic videos from artists like Madonna, Peter Gabriel, Phil Collins, Michael Jackson, and Aerosmith fascinated audiences and inspired an appreciation for the art form, prompting curiosity about the creative process behind them. "I came up in the 80s era where that's what made me fall in love with music videos, right? The MTV, much music era, watching videos by like Madonna and Peter Gabriel and like Phil Collins and Michael Jackson and Errol Smith."
