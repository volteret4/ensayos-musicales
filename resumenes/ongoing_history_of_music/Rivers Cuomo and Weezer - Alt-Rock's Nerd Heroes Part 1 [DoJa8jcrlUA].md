## albums
**The So-called Blue Record (1994) - Debut Album** : Released on May 10th, 1994, this debut album was recorded at Jimmy Hendrix's Electric Lady Studio in New York for about six weeks starting in August 1993. It sold over 15 million copies worldwide and gained additional revenue from appearances in video games. The band lineup changed during recording as guitarist Jason Cropper departed. "The blue album started to sell and sell and sell."
**Pinkerton (1996) - Second Album** : Released on September 24th, 1996, about two and a half years after the Blue Album, this record had high expectations but delivered a darker, self-produced sound, reflecting Rivers Cuomo's disillusionment with the rock star lifestyle and his painful leg surgery. It was only 34 minutes long, composed of material left over from Rivers' abandoned rock opera project "Songs From the Black Hole," and covered themes of former girlfriends, groupies, fan letters, and a search for identity. The album was considered a "proto-emo" record, ahead of its time, and initially sold less than one million copies, facing a restraining order from Pinkerton Inc. over its title. "It was, if anything, a proto-emal record, something that people weren't quite ready for in 1996."
**Alone, the Home Recordings of Rivers Cuomo (2007)** : This series features 121 songs written by Rivers Cuomo during a period of self-isolation and depression in a blacked-out apartment in Culver City. Geffen Records initially refused to release this material for years, fearing it would tarnish Weezer's reputation, but Rivers eventually won the right to release it in 2007. "Geffen refused to release that material for years because they didn't want substandard material sullying the good name of Weezer."
**The Green Album (2001) - Third Album** : The release of this album in 2001 marked a significant turning point, effectively kickstarting the "Weezer we know today." It featured a lineup change with Mikey Welsh replaced by Scott Schreiner due to Welsh's severe mental health issues and drug use, leading to his hospitalization. "The Weezer we know today really began in 2001 when they released their third record, the one known as the Green Album for obvious reasons."
**Maladroit (2002)** : This album features songs like "Dope Nose," which originated from Rivers Cuomo's Ritalin and tequila experiments, composed entirely in his head in an altered state. "This one, for example, would be heard in a finished form on the Maladroid album in 2002."

## songs
**Say It Ain't So (1994)** : This song from the debut "Blue Record" addresses Rivers Cuomo's parents' divorce, specifically his hippie parents. The line "wrestling with Jimmy" refers to his brother, James, who was born Leaves Cuomo and has appeared in other Weezer songs, like "My Name is Jonas." "And a song about learning his hippie parents getting a divorce."
**My Name is Jonas (1994)** : Featured on the "Blue Album," this song contains a reference to Rivers Cuomo's little brother, James, concerning issues James was having with an insurance claim after a car crash. "For example, there's a reference in, My Name is Jonas about River's little brother, and that had to do with the time James was having issues with an insurance claim after a car crash."
**I Can't Forget The Way (1992)** : Considered the first proper Weezer song, it was recorded on February 16, 1992, in a scummy Hollywood rehearsal space. This early recording demonstrates a significant difference from the band's later sound that emerged within a few months. "Recorded in a scummy Hollywood rehearsal space on February 16, 1992, it's called I Can't Forget The Way."
**Buddy Holly (1994)** : This song was originally hated by Rivers Cuomo, who thought it was "cheesy" and had written it on June 25, 1993, as a goofy new wave type song using a small synthesizer. The chorus came to him while walking on a college campus, and the subject was making fun of his friend, Cuyen Hee, who disliked the band making fun of his girlfriend. Despite Rivers' initial aversion, the rest of the band and producer Ric Ocasek loved it, leading to studio notes reading "we want Buddy Holly," and it eventually became the most performed song in Weezer's history, known for its legendary Happy Days-themed video directed by Spike Jonze. "Rivers had written it on June 25, 1993."
**Buddy Holly (1994) - Original Lyrics and Mastering** : The original lyrics of this song did not mention Buddy Holly and Mary Tyler Moore but instead referred to Fred Astaire and Ginger Rogers. The version recorded for the album was performed at a slower speed, similar to its demo, and was only sped up during the mastering process, meaning the released version is not how the band initially intended it to sound. It was released as a single on September 7, 1994, which would have been the real Buddy Holly's 58th birthday. "There was originally no mention of Buddy Holly and Mary Tyler Moore in the lyrics. The name checks were Fred Astaire and Ginger Rogers."
**Buddy Holly (1994) - Windows 95 Inclusion** : This song was included on the installation disk for Windows 95 in a folder called "Fun Stuff," alongside Edie Brickell and New Bohemians' "Good Times." Weezer had no idea about its inclusion in what was the biggest software launch at the time and were initially furious because they weren't informed, but it proved to be excellent promotion as Windows 95 sold 40 million copies in its first year. "Buddy Holly was included on the installation disk for Windows 95."
**El Scorcho (1996)** : This song from the "Pinkerton" album was written by Rivers Cuomo as a homework assignment for one of his music classes at Harvard. He wrote it because he lacked the courage to speak to a girl he had a crush on at Harvard, being too shy. The title was inspired by a local hot sauce called Del Scorcho. "The song was written as a homework assignment for one of his music classes."
**Island in the Sun (2001)** : This song, one of the singles from "The Green Album," notably does not feature bassist Mikey Welsh in its music video because he was hospitalized at the time for severe mental health issues and a drug overdose. "This explains why we don't see Mikey in The Weezer video for Island in the Sun because he was hospitalized at the time."
**Hashpipe (2001)** : The first single from "The Green Album," this song was described by Rivers Cuomo as being about a "homosexual transvestite prostitute." Its creation stemmed from Rivers' "catalog of riffs" experiments, which involved plugging in a guitar, taking a Ritalin pill with three shots of tequila, and then going into the backyard to see what musical ideas emerged. The record company initially did not want to release it, but the band fought and won, proving it to be a big hit. "It was written about a homosexual transvestite prostitute."
**Dope Nose (2002)** : This song, later appearing on the "Maladroid" album, originated from a Ritalin and tequila experiment by Rivers Cuomo where he overdid it, sitting in his dark apartment at six in the morning, "foaming at the mouth." In this altered state, he composed the song entirely in his head within three minutes, without any instruments. "This was another Ritalin and Tequila experiment."

## curiosities
**Longevity and Stability** : Weezer has been together for more than 30 years, notably without any breakups, reunions, or official hiatuses, showcasing unusual stability for a rock band. "Weezer has been together for more than 30 years. There have been no breakups and no reunions."
**Band Name Suggestions** : Before settling on Weezer, the band considered several unconventional names including "60 wrong sausages," "Fuzz," "Bush" (not the famous one), "Goat Punishment," "Outhouse," "Hummingbird," and "the big Jones." They even recorded a four-track demo as "60 wrong sausages" called "The Colustraal EP." "One of the names they considered was 60 wrong sausages."
**Early Rehearsal Conditions** : After moving into a house at 2226 Amherst Avenue in Los Angeles, the band spent hours rehearsing in the garage under stark conditions, including no refrigerator and only outdoor lawn furniture for seating indoors, reflecting their poverty at the time. "There was no refrigerator. And the only thing they had to sit on inside was outside lawn furniture."
**Rivers Cuomo's Pizza Crust Habit** : During the band's early, poor days, Rivers Cuomo would save pizza crusts in a fanny pack to eat later, demonstrating the band's financial struggles. "They were so poor that Rivers would save pizza crusts in a fanny pack."
**First Gig and Band Naming** : The band's first offer to play came on March 19, 1992, at a club called Rodgies, headlining Dog Star, which featured Keanu Reeves on bass. Needing a proper permanent name for the gig, Rivers Cuomo, Patrick Wilson, Jason Cropper, and Matt Sharp chose "Weezer," a nickname Rivers had been given by his father, referencing a character in "The Little Rascals." "And eventually Rivers, Pat, Jason, and bass player Matt Sharp now went with the name Weezer."
**Producer Selection for Debut Album** : When asked by Geffen Records who they wanted to produce their debut, Weezer initially boldly suggested they would do it themselves. When pushed for names, someone jokingly suggested Ric Ocasek of The Cars, not expecting it to happen, but Ocasek turned out to be interested. They recorded a practice demo with him on August 11, 1993, and got along great. "Someone in Weezer said, you know, we really like the cars. Get us Ricokasic. That was meant as a joke."
**Jason Cropper's Departure from Blue Album Sessions** : Guitarist Jason Cropper left the group during the recording sessions for "The Blue Album" due to "legal issues" surrounding his departure. One story suggests he was asked to leave because his girlfriend was pregnant, and he wasn't handling it well, raising concerns about his commitment to the band. Brian Bell, an early fan, was invited to replace him. "Guitarist Jason Cropper left the group during the recording sessions. And it's unclear what happened because there are, well, legal issues surrounding his departure."
**Mickey and Carly Allen Car Accident** : Following their tour for "Pinkerton" through late 1996 to mid-1997, Weezer postponed everything after early fans Mickey and Carly Allen, who ran the official fan club, died in a car accident with their sister Trista while driving home from a Weezer show in Denver. After mourning, the band decided to take a three-year break. "Everything had to be postponed, though, when Mickey and Carly Allen, two early fans who ran the official Weaser fan club, died in a car accident with their sister Trista."
**Unofficial Album Leak** : In 2001, Rivers Cuomo intentionally leaked an unofficial album online. This included at least four recording sessions from rehearsals featuring 18 new songs, and about two dozen more recorded live during the Warp Tour using an eight-track mobile unit. "They were also part of an unofficial album that was intentionally leaked online by Rivers in 2001."
**Ritalin and Tequila Songwriting Experiments** : Rivers Cuomo's creative process for some songs, including "Hashpipe" and "Dope Nose," involved unusual experiments he called his "catalog of riffs." These entailed plugging in a guitar, taking one Ritalin pill with three shots of tequila, going into the backyard, and sometimes closing his eyes to see if songs appeared in his mind. "The creation of a lot of this material involved plugging in a guitar, taking one riddle in pill with three shots of tequila and then going out into the backyard to see what happened."

## curiosities
**Unlikely Rock Star Persona** : Rivers Cuomo is described as an "unlikely sort of rock star," committed to maintaining his status. He is highly educated, deeply introspective, very private, and constantly seeking to learn new things, from songwriting to computer programming, intense meditation, and music industry studies. "If Forever's Cuomo of Weezer were to walk past you on the street, you probably wouldn't notice him."
**Desire for a Weezer Movie** : Rivers Cuomo harbors a long-term goal to create a Weezer movie, not just a tour film, but an actual cinematic production. "And one day he wants to make a Weezer movie, not a tour film, but some kind of actual movie."
**Diverse Interests** : Beyond music, Rivers Cuomo is an avid pickleball player, a very good chess player, and is deeply fascinated with Japanese culture. "River's has also taken a pickleball with a vengeance. He's a very good chess player, and he's fascinated with Japanese culture."
**PETA's Sexiest Celebrity Vegetarian** : PETA once voted Rivers Cuomo the sexiest celebrity vegetarian, though he personally confesses to hating carrots. "Pita once voted him the sexiest celebrity vegetarian, although he does confess to hating carrots."
**Lack of a Middle Name** : Rivers Cuomo does not have a middle name because his parents intended for him to choose one when he was old enough, but he never got around to it. "He doesn't have a middle name because his parents wanted him to choose one when he got old enough, but he just never got around to it."
**Harvard TV Show "The Pilot"** : A film called "The Pilot" for a TV show was based on the period when Rivers Cuomo attended Harvard University. "This film, The Pilot, for a TV show based on the year's Rivers Went to Harvard."
**Pet Squirrel Mr. Peanut Butter** : Rivers Cuomo once had a pet squirrel named Mr. Peanut Butter. "And once he had a pet squirrel named Mr. Peanut Butter."
**Birth and Childhood** : Rivers Cuomo was born in New York City to a hippie musician father and a hippie mother. He was raised in several Buddhist communities for the first ten years of his life, including an ashram called Yogaville, before his parents divorced in 1975, and the family settled in Connecticut. "Rivers was born in New York City. Dad was a hippie musician, and mum was a hippie hippie hippie."
**Origin of His Name "Rivers"** : There are two stories about his name. One states his father, a soccer fan, named him after three players from the 1970 World Cup (Revilino of Brazil, Luigi Riva, and Gianni Rivera of Italy). The other story attributes it to his birth in a hospital between the East and Hudson Rivers in New York City, with his mother reportedly hearing one of the rivers outside her window. "Well, there were two stories."
**Stepfather Stephen Kits** : Rivers Cuomo gained a stepfather named Stephen Kits when his family lived in the Yogaville ashram. "This is where Rivers gained a stepfather named Stephen Kits."
**Temporary Name Change to Peter Kits** : For a couple of years in the mid-1980s, Rivers Cuomo changed his name to Peter Kits. This name appeared in one of his very first bands, a glammy metal group called Among Guard, before he reverted to Rivers. "He also, for a time, changed his name to Peter Kits."
**Roadie for King's Eyes** : For a period, Rivers Cuomo worked as a roadie for a band called King's Eyes, alongside his college enrollment and music writing. "He also, for a while worked as a roadie for a band called King's Eyes."
**Tower Records Employment and Influences** : Rivers Cuomo had a part-time job at the famous Tower Records on Sunset Boulevard, which was crucial as Alt Rock was emerging. This exposed him to bands like Pixies, Nirvana, Sonic Youth, and Nine Inch Nails, and also led him to meet drummer Patrick Wilson, who would become a Weezer member. "Working in the record store exposed him to the pixies and Nirvana, Sonic Youth, and Nine Inch Nails."
**Aversion to Punk Funk** : In the early 90s, Rivers Cuomo was talked out of forming a punk funk band because he worried it would hinder the band's chances, especially with the growing emphasis on authenticity in the alternative scene. As a former metalhead, he feared this background could be detrimental to the group. "Rivers had been talked out of forming a punk funk band."
**Leg Lengthening Surgery** : Born with his right leg 44 millimeters (1.75 inches) shorter than his left, Rivers Cuomo underwent expensive and painful leg lengthening surgery in March 1995, funded by earnings from "The Blue Album." The procedure involved breaking the bone, fixing it with a brace, and gradually stretching it with screws over months, causing significant pain and affecting his mental state and songwriting for the second album. "But as he grew, his right one grew to be 44 millimeters, one and three quarter inches shorter than his left."
**Harvard University Enrollment (First Time)** : In September 1995, while still healing from his leg surgery and walking with a cane, Rivers Cuomo enrolled at Harvard University to study classical composition. His studies consumed a lot of his time, impacting his songwriting. He tried out for a Harvard choir but did not make it and kept to himself, growing a beard. He found it amusing that classmates wearing Weezer T-shirts had no idea he was Rivers Cuomo. "In September 1995, still healing and walking with a cane, Rivers enrolled at Harvard University to study classical composition."
**Disillusionment with Rock Star Lifestyle** : The creation of "Pinkerton" reflected Rivers Cuomo's deep disillusionment with the rock star lifestyle, which contributed to the album's darker themes. "Pinkerton was...darker and reflected Rivers' disillusionment with the rock star lifestyle."
**Leg Injury's Impact on Guitar Playing** : His leg injury significantly affected Rivers Cuomo's guitar playing, as he couldn't stretch his leg out. This constraint forced him to write most of the songs on the "Pinkerton" album with limited mobility on the fretboard. "His leg affected his guitar playing too. He couldn't stretch it out so he had to write most of the songs on the album with limited mobility on the fretboard."
**Harvard University Enrollment (Second Time)** : After Weezer's break following the "Pinkerton" tour, Rivers Cuomo returned to Harvard, completing semesters in 1997 and 1998. During this time, he played in a campus band called Homey. "Rivers went back to Harvard, completing semesters in 1997 and 1998."
**Self-Isolation and Depression (1998-1999)** : In February 1998, after completing his studies at Harvard, Rivers Cuomo moved back to LA and into an apartment under a freeway in Culver City. Still hurt by "Pinkerton"'s poor reception, he sought self-isolation, unplugging his phone, sealing all light by covering windows with fiberglass insulation, painting the walls black for ultimate darkness, and only communicating with his pet gecko for a year. He was very depressed and without a car, became immobile in Los Angeles. "Rivers was very depressed. He didn't own a car either."
**Productivity During Isolation** : Despite his severe depression and self-isolation in a blacked-out apartment under a freeway, Rivers Cuomo was incredibly productive, writing 121 songs during this period. Some of these songs were later released as part of the "Alone, the Home Recordings of Rivers Cuomo" series. "In that blacked out apartment, under the freeway, he wrote 121 songs."
**Japanese Culture and Fuji Rock Festival** : Rivers Cuomo's long-standing interest in Japanese culture, combined with an invitation to perform at the Fuji Rock Festival in Japan, rekindled the band's excitement for making music after a hiatus. "Rivers, Spanish Japan, okay? And that got them excited about making music again."

**Pinkerton Inc. - Copyright Infringement Lawsuit** : One day before Weezer's album "Pinkerton" was released, the band was served with a restraining order from Pinkerton Inc., a detective agency formed in 1850. The company alleged federal copyright infringement and demanded $2 million in damages, plus a prohibition on the album's sale, distribution, and advertising. Rivers Cuomo provided a six-page essay explaining the album's title referred to B.F. Pinkerton from "Madame Butterfly," and the case was dismissed by a judge who ruled the detective agency was "just being silly." "One day before Pinkerton went on sale, they were served with a restraining order from Pinkerton Inc. The detective agency that was formed in 1850."
**Puccini Opera 'Madame Butterfly' - Pinkerton Album Inspiration** : Rivers Cuomo's interest in Japanese culture and his studies in classical composition led him to become very acquainted with Puccini's opera "Madame Butterfly." This opera influenced the title of Weezer's "Pinkerton" album, specifically through the character B.F. Pinkerton, who is featured in the story. "Rivers had also become interested in Japanese culture. He became very acquainted with the Puccini opera Man and Butterfly."
**Del Scorcho Hot Sauce - "El Scorcho" Song Title Inspiration** : The title of Weezer's song "El Scorcho" was inspired by a local hot sauce called Del Scorcho, described as a "Pucco text." "And the title was inspired by hot sauce called Del Scorcho from a local Pucco text."
**Mikey Welsh's Mental Health Crisis and Death** : Weezer bassist Mikey Welsh experienced a severe breakdown in mid-2001 due to excessive drug use and undiagnosed mental health issues, leading to his sudden departure from the band and hospitalization. He was diagnosed with bipolar disorder, PTSD, and borderline personality disorder, and suffered a suicide attempt via overdose, falling into a coma before being transferred to a psych ward. Welsh later made a comeback as a musician, painter, and sculptor, with his art even appearing on Burton Snowboards, but he ultimately died of a heart attack in a Chicago hotel on October 8, 2011, stemming from a suspected heroin overdose. "Mikey Welsh was out as the band's bass player. Already, he was in rough shape."
**James Cuomo (Rivers' Brother) - Sociologist and Musician** : Rivers Cuomo's brother, born Leaves Cuomo, now goes by James and has been referenced in several Weezer songs, including "Say It Ain't So" (as "Jimmy") and "My Name is Jonas." James is a professor of sociology at UMass Amherst, teaching courses like new religious movements, and uses the last name Kits, from his and Rivers' stepfather. He also used to be a rhythm guitarist in a Kiss cover band. "James is a professor of sociology, and he has taught courses in things like new religious movements."
