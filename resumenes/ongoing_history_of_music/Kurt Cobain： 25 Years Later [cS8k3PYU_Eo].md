## albums
**Cobain's Disease (fictional concept)** : A concept for a double album rock opera about vomiting gastric juice, being a borderline anorexic Auschwitz grunge boy, accompanied by an endoscope video, as written in Kurt's personal journals. "just let me have my very own unexplainable rare stomach disease named after me. And the title of our double album is Cobain's disease, a rock opera about vomiting gastric juice, being a borderline anorexic Auschwitz grunge boy with an accompanying endoscope video."
**In Utero (1993)** : This album, the follow-up to "Nevermind," initially fizzled upon its release in the fall of 1993, selling less than 200,000 copies in its first week. Walmart stores refused to stock it due to its artwork and some lyrical content. Fans had reportedly grown bored of Kurt's issues and his anti-star stance. "When in Yudrow came out on the fall of 1993, it really kind of fizzled."

## songs
**Marigold (1993)** : This was the only song Dave Grohl contributed to Nirvana during his tenure from fall 1990 to April 1994, found deep within the CD single release for "Heart-Shaped Box." "he got to contribute just one song to the band and to find it, you have to go deep into the CD single release for Heart Shape Box in 1993 for a song called Marigold."
**My Best Friend's Girl (The Cars cover)** : Performed by Nirvana as a cover song during their last-ever show in an airport hangar in Munich, Germany, in March 1994. "One of the songs that evening was a cover of the cars my best friend's girl."
**You Know You're Right (recorded January 30, 1994)** : This was the last full, proper song recorded by Nirvana. Initially an unreleased live recording from Chicago in 1993, a studio version was recorded on January 30, 1994. Courtney Love took the song for Hole, titling it "You've Got No Right," and it was performed on MTV Unplugged on February 14, 1995. The Nirvana recording was later bogged down in a lawsuit because Courtney wanted it on a single disc greatest hits compilation, while Dave Grohl and Chris Novoselic wanted it for a box set; it was eventually released both ways. Kurt never gave the song a title, listing it as "Kurt's song number one" in studio notes. "You Know Your Right, The Last Full, Proper Song, Recorded by Nirvana."
**Untitled Beatles song (home recording)** : A home recording of an unnamed Beatles song was uncovered after Kurt's death, hinting at his potential future musical ambitions for quiet songs with string arrangements and a more acoustic feel. "Here for example is a home recording of a Beatles song uncovered before he died."

## curiosities
**Ongoing Stomach Issues** : Kurt suffered from unending stomach issues, seeing nine different specialists on both coasts who could not provide a definite diagnosis, ruling out Crohn's disease and IBS. Doctors considered a pinched nerve as a possible explanation, which might have led to his reliance on opioids for pain relief. "Well, there were a lot of doctor visits to determine the cause of his unending stomach issues, nine different specialists on both coasts."
**May 9, 1993 Overdose** : Kurt was rescued from an overdose on May 9, 1993, when paramedics administered a shot of Narcan just in time. This was not an isolated incident, as he overdosed almost a dozen times in the last ten months of his life. "On May 9, 1993, he was rescued from an overdose when paramedics gave him a shot of narcan just in time."
**"Rapunzel" Nickname** : Friends began calling Kurt "Rapunzel" during the last ten months of his life because his frequent heroin use led him to rarely come down from his room. "His friends began calling him Rapunzel because he rarely came down from his room."
**June 1993 Gun Call** : Police were called to Kurt's home on Lake Washington Boulevard in June 1993 to diffuse a situation involving a gun. "There was a gun call to the home on Lake Washington Boulevard in June 1993."
**Concerns for Finances and Death** : Kurt had fights with Nirvana's management over the band's finances and became obsessed with writing his will. During a European tour in February and March 1994, he was worried about Nirvana not having tour cancellation insurance and repeatedly wondered what would happen to "everybody and everything if I died." "More than once he wondered, what would happen to everybody and everything if I died?"
**Last Nirvana Show (Munich, March 1994)** : Nirvana's final concert took place in an airport hangar in Munich, Germany, in March 1994, in front of just over 3,000 people. The gig was described as "okay," featuring a power failure that led to a quick acoustic set, despite Kurt feeling very sick with a sore throat. "Nirvana's last ever show was held in an airport hangar in Munich, Germany. In March of 1994, in front of just over 3,000 people."
**Cancellation of European Tour** : After the Munich show, the rest of Nirvana's European tour was cancelled because Kurt was too sick to continue, suffering from what doctors diagnosed as chronic bronchitis and laryngitis. "After the show in Munich, the rest of the tour was cancelled. Kurt was just too sick to continue with what doctors called chronic bronchitis and laryngitis."
**Rome Overdose (March 4, 1994)** : While in Rome, Kurt checked into room 541 of the Excelsior Hotel with Courtney Love. Sometime after midnight on March 4, he wrote a three-page suicide note, ingested over 50 Rohypnol pills with champagne, and slipped into a coma. Courtney found him and called an ambulance, and he remained in a coma for 20 hours, with CNN even reporting his death. "Sometime after midnight on March 4, Kurt wrote a three-page suicide note, washed down more than 50 pills of a tranquilizer called Rohipno with a bottle of champagne and slipped into a coma."
**March 18, 1994 Domestic Dispute** : Following his return home to Seattle on March 12, another domestic dispute occurred on March 18, a Friday night. Kurt locked himself in a room and threatened to kill himself, prompting Courtney to call the police, who confiscated four guns and 25 boxes of assorted bullets. "On March 18, there was another domestic dispute of some kind. At Friday night, Kurt locked himself in a room and was threatened to kill himself, so Courtney called the cops again."
**Last-Ditch Intervention (March 25, 1994)** : On March 25, Courtney organized an intervention at their Seattle home with doctors and friends, including Chris Novoselic, Dave Grohl, Pat Smear, Nirvana's management and legal team, and drug counselor James Burr. The meeting lasted five hours, and Kurt eventually agreed to enter rehab. "On March 25, Courtney gathered a bunch of doctors and friends together at the house in Seattle to try to scare Kurt straight. He had to deal with his problems."
**Gun Purchase Through Dylan (March 30, 1994)** : Before flying to Los Angeles for rehab, Kurt and his friend Dylan visited Standbaker Sports, a Seattle gun shop, on March 30. Unable to buy a new gun himself due to police knowledge of his previous confiscations, Kurt gave Dylan $308.37 to purchase a Remington C-11 shotgun and a box of shells, claiming he needed it for protection. "So he gave Dylan the $308.37 to buy a Remington C-11 shotgun in the box of shells."
**Exodus Recovery Center Escape (April 1, 1994)** : Kurt checked into the Exodus Recovery Center in Los Angeles on March 30, but after two nights, on the evening of Good Friday, April 1st, he scaled a garden wall, took a cab to the airport, and caught Delta flight 788 back to Seattle. "But on the evening of Good Friday, April 1st, he scaled the wall in the garden, took a cab to the airport where he caught Delta flight 788 to Seattle."
**Encounter with Duff McKagan (April 1, 1994)** : On Delta flight 788 to Seattle, Kurt sat in seat 2F, next to Duff McKagan of Guns N' Roses. Upon landing, Duff offered him a ride home, which Kurt declined. "He ended up in seat 2F, next to Duff McCagan of Guns & Roses."
**Last Autograph (April 2, 1994)** : Around 1 AM on Saturday, April 2nd, 1994, at Sea-Tac Airport, Kurt was recognized by a Canadian Nirvana fan who asked him to sign a copy of Jane Austen's "Mansfield Park." The autograph was strangely dated April 5th, the day Kurt ultimately died. "The weird thing about this autograph is that Kurt dated it as April 5th, which is the day he ended up killing himself."
**Missing Persons Report & Detective Search (April 1994)** : Courtney Love, frantic and fearing Kurt would harm himself, hired an LA detective named Tom Grant to track him down. Kurt's mother also filed a missing persons report with the Seattle police. "She hired an LA detective named Tom Grant to track him down. His mother filed a missing persons report with the Seattle police."
**Last Known Sighting and Activities (April 2-5, 1994)** : Kurt made it back to his Seattle home shortly before 2 AM on Saturday, April 2nd. One of the last people to see him alive was Callie, his former nanny, at about 6 AM that morning. He then attempted to call Courtney in Los Angeles but couldn't get through due to a "do not disturb" order on her phone. Over the next two days, there were "Elvis-like sightings" of Kurt around Seattle, including at a park, another house he owned in Carnation, with an unidentified friend at a dealer's house, at a couple of hotels, and reportedly at a store called CL Guns where he bought another box of 20 gauge shotgun shells. "One of the last persons to see him alive was Callie, the co-vain's former nanny who was looking after the house while everybody was supposed to be a Los Angeles."
**Suicide and Discovery (April 5-8, 1994)** : Sometime on April 5th, 1994, Kurt went to a room above the greenhouse in his backyard, wrote a suicide note, loaded a shotgun, injected himself with heroin, and shot himself. His body was found three days later on Friday, April 8th, 1994, by an electrician who saw him through a window. "One time on April 5th, 1994, he went to the room above the greenhouse in the backyard of his house. He wrote out a suicide note, loaded up a shotgun, loaded himself up with heroin, and shot himself."
**Suicide Note Excerpt** : The suicide note included a significant passage expressing his inability to fake enthusiasm for beautiful people and the music industry, stating he couldn't "take up with beautiful people off by thinking and pretending as if I'm having a hundred percent flood." It also contained the line "It's better to burn out than to fade away." "It's better to burn out than to fade away. Peace, love, empathy, Kurt Cobain."
**Official Identification and Cause of Death** : The King County Medical Examiner's Office positively identified Kurt Cobain's body by fingerprints, and an autopsy showed he died of a shotgun wound to the head, which appeared to be self-inflicted. "The King County Medical Examiner's Office has positively identified the body of Kurt Cobain by fingerprints."
**Age at Death** : Kurt Cobain was 27 years old when he died. "When he died, Kurt Cobain was 27."
**Funeral and Ashes** : After being turned down by several Seattle churches due to the suicide, a Unitarian church held Kurt's funeral. His body was cremated, with some ashes sprinkled on the banks of the Wishkah River in his hometown of Aberdeen, Washington. "Some of the ashes were sprinkled on the banks at the Wishcaw River in Kurt's hometown of Aberdeen, Washington."
**Courtney Love's Use of Ashes** : Courtney Love reportedly had some of Kurt's ashes made into objects for her Buddhist shrine and some were apparently stuffed into a teddy bear backpack she carried until it was stolen from her Los Angeles home in 2008. "Kurt and I apparently had some made into objects for her Buddhist shrine. Some were apparently stuffed into a teddy bear backpack that she carried with her everywhere until 2008 when it was stolen out of her home in Los Angeles."
**Natasha Stelmock's Ashes Claim** : In October 2008, Australian performance artist Natasha Stelmock claimed to have acquired some of Kurt's ashes and announced plans to smoke them in a joint, stating this act would free him from commercialism and exploitation. "In October 2008, a performance artist from Australia named Natasha Stelmock announced that she had come into possession of some of Kurt's ashes and was going to smoke them in a big joint."
**Posthumous Impact and Legacy** : After his death, Kurt's image elevated him to the status of icons like Elvis, Buddy Holly, John Lennon, and Tupac, profoundly influencing rock, pop, and even hip-hop through his music, attitude, and image. "From that point on, Kurt was up there with Elvis, and Buddy Holly, and John Lennon, and Tupac."
**Posthumous Business Deals** : Courtney Love sold a piece of the Nirvana Empire to Primary Wave, leading to increased merchandising like t-shirts, action figures, lunchboxes, and shoes, and the company now manages future licensing of Nirvana songs. Courtney also authorized the publication of Kurt's private diaries and was involved in the documentary "Montage of Heck." "Courtney sold a piece of the Nirvana Empire to a company called Primary Wave, which explains why we see more Kurt Cobain t-shirts and action figures and lunch boxes and shoes."
**Speculation on Undied Life** : If Kurt had not died, it is speculated that he would have separated from Nirvana for a solo project with different musicians, possibly Buzz Osborne of the Melvins and Michael Stipe of R.E.M., with whom he had discussed working. This solo record would likely have had a more acoustic feel with string arrangements. "Well my guess would be that he would have separated himself from Nirvana for his next project using different musicians. Maybe people like, I don't know, Buzz A's, One of the Melvin's and most likely Michael Stipe of R.E.M."
**Cancelled Michael Stipe Meeting** : Kurt had a plane ticket to meet Michael Stipe to discuss a musical project with quiet songs and string arrangements but cancelled it at the last minute. "And he even had a plane ticket to see Michael Stipe to talk about this project. But he cancelled it the last minute."
**Nirvana Dissolution if Alive** : Speculation suggests that if Kurt had lived, Nirvana might have dissolved undramatically, with Kurt pursuing his non-grunge, acoustic musical ideas, similar to later unplugged shows by Eddie Vedder and Chris Cornell. "maybe he would just sort of dissolve Nirvana. Not a breakup, but just an undramatic fade to black."
**Speculated Divorce from Courtney Love** : It is speculated that Kurt and Courtney would have divorced at some point within five years if he had lived. "At some point over the next five years, he and Courtney would have been divorced."
**Speculated Nirvana Reunion** : An inevitable Nirvana reunion at Coachella around 2005 is speculated, possibly leading to a brief, high-money tour, but the band would then break up for good, with Kurt realizing they were heading down the same dark road. "Except for an inevitable Nirvana reunion at Coachella at about, uh, old 2005 or something."
**No More Unreleased Music** : It is believed that there is no more unreleased music from Kurt Cobain left to be discovered. "And we know that there's no music left hiding anywhere. That well is completely bone dry."

**April 8, 1994 - Regular Friday Transformed** : April 8, 1994, began as an ordinary Friday with news about the Toronto Blue Jays and military smoking bans, but it dramatically transformed around 1:45 PM with news of something "going on in Seattle" involving Kurt Cobain, eventually revealing his death.
