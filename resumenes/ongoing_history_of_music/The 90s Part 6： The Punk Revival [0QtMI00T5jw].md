## genres

## albums
**In Utero (1993) - Sales Disappointment** : The album, released as the follow-up to the highly successful *Nevermind*, was considered a sales disappointment, contributing to a sense of "Nirvana fatigue" by early 1994 amidst drama involving Kurt Cobain and Courtney Love. "in utero, the follow-up to never mind had been a, well, a sales disappointment."

## curiosities
**Lead Singer Kurt Cobain's Death (April 1994)** : Kurt Cobain died in April 1994, a tragic event that created a significant void and brought hurt and confusion to the Grunge and Grunge-Lite communities, prompting a reluctant shift in the music scene to find "the next, next big thing." "And then in April 1994, Kurt died, and that brought nothing but hurt and confusion to the whole Grunge and Grunge-Lite community."
**Punk Rock Attitude** : Despite being primarily a Grunge band, Nirvana frequently expressed a punk rock attitude, drawing from traditions of bands like The Ramones, The Clash, and various 1980s hardcore groups, which helped to prepare audiences for the punk revival that followed. "Nirvana was primarily of the genus Grunge, but whenever they could, they made it known that their attitude was total punk rock in the tradition of the Ramones, the clash, and any number of hardcore bands from the 1980s."
**Nirvana Fatigue** : By early 1994, public fatigue with Nirvana had set in due to too much public complaining by Kurt Cobain, extensive drama surrounding Kurt and Courtney Love, and the commercial underperformance of their album *In Utero*. "Many people forget this, but Nirvana fatigue had set in. Too much complaining by Kurt, too much drama with Kurt and Courtney, and in utero, the follow-up to never mind had been a, well, a sales disappointment."

# artist - Ramones

## curiosities
**Continued Activity Through Obscurity** : Despite being at their peak in the mid-to-late 1970s, The Ramones spent most of the 1980s in obscurity, considered "washed up" and a "joke," yet they persistently continued releasing albums and touring. "In fact, they were not only considered washed up, but a joke. But the Ramones kept at it, releasing album after album and going on tour after tour."
**Rediscovery by Grunge Bands** : In the early 1990s, new Grunge bands, notably led by Kurt Cobain and Eddie Vedder, began publicly praising The Ramones, "name-checking" them and wearing their t-shirts, which initiated a rediscovery of the band by a new generation of fans. "All these new bands, led by kids like Kirk Cobain and Eddie Better, started name-checking the Ramones. They were seen wearing Ramones t-shirts."
**Pioneers and Creators of Punk Rock** : The Ramones were widely acknowledged by subsequent bands like Green Day and The Offspring, and many others, as pioneers and creators of the punk rock genre, a fact largely considered true. "Whatever they could, they'd sing the praises about the Ramones and how they were pioneers and the creators of punk rock, which to a large extent is very, very true."
**Mainstream Comeback and Lollapalooza 1996** : The outpouring of affection and attention from a new generation brought The Ramones back into the mainstream, culminating in their appearance as main stage stars at the Lollapalooza festival in 1996. "The outpouring of affection and attention was so strong that by 1996, they were one of the mainstaged stars of the Lollapalooza festival."
**Final Show and Legacy (1996)** : After their appearance at Lollapalooza in the summer of 1996, The Ramones played their last show in Hollywood, concluding a career that spanned 2,263 performances and finally receiving deserved recognition for their contributions before the deaths of Joey and Johnny Ramone. "After that summer, they would play their last show in Hollywood, bringing the curtain down on a career that included 2,263 shows."

# artist - Green Day
## members
Billy Joe Armstrong
Mike Dirnt

## genres
Punk Rock

## labels
Warner's

## albums
**Dookie (1994) - Breakthrough Album** : Released on February 1, 1994, *Dookie* initially received good reviews but truly exploded after Kurt Cobain's death two months later, filling a void in the music scene. Its sound combined the rebelliousness of grunge with a fun, snotty attitude, distinguishing it from the doom and gloom of its predecessor genre. The album stayed on the charts for two years, selling over 20 million copies worldwide, won a Grammy for Best Alternative Album, and became a defining record for a generation, often cited as a life-changing album and a "gateway drug" to punk rock. "On February 1, 1994, a record called Duckey appeared in stores. And it got some good reviews. Then two months later, Kurt dies. A void. A vacuum opened up. And Green Day got sucked into it."

## curiosities
**Oakland Origins and Indie Releases** : Green Day originated in Oakland, California, and had released several indie records prior to their major label success, though these earlier efforts did not achieve significant widespread attention. "A band from Oakland at the right sound. They also had the right attitude and the right album at exactly the right time, and that band was Green Day. They had a couple of indie releases, but weren't exactly setting the world on fire."
**Signing with a Major Label - A Risky Move** : Producer Rob Cavallo convinced Green Day to sign with a major label, a decision that went against the core ethos of the punk culture they emerged from. This controversial move led to the band being banned from some of their usual venues and labeled as "sell-outs" by many Bay Area punks. "He convinced Green Day that the only way forward was for them to sign with a major label. So going against the ethos of the punk culture they had grown up in, Green Day signed. That was a huge risk. And it got them banned from some of the places they used to play. As far as a lot of Bay Area punks were concerned, Green Day was dead to them. There were sell-outs."
**Rapid Recording of Dookie** : The band completed the recording sessions for their album *Dookie* with producer Rob Cavallo in an exceptionally short period of three weeks. "The group burned through the recording sessions with Cavallo in three weeks."
**Admiration for Rancid and Operation Ivy** : Green Day members were significant fans of both Rancid and Operation Ivy, the latter being Rancid's predecessor. Billy Joe Armstrong and Mike Dirnt frequently attended Operation Ivy shows at the renowned 924 Gilman Street punk club during their youth. "This pleased Green Day to no end because everyone in the band had been big fans of Ranset as well as Operation Ivy, Ranset's immediate ancestor. As kids, Billy Joe Armstrong and Mike Durant used to go see Op Ivy all the time at the infamous 924 Gilman Street punk club."
**Role in the Mid-90s Punk Revival** : Green Day emerged as the most significant band of the 1990s rock revival, playing a pivotal role in popularizing California punk and creating a "huge vortex" that drew other bands like Rancid into the mainstream. "Green Day was the biggest band of the 90s rock revival, but they were hardly the only one."

# artist - Rancid
## genres
Punk Rock
American Punk Rock
British Punk

## labels
Epitaph Records

## albums
**Let's Go (1994) - Second Album** : Released five months after Green Day's *Dookie*, *Let's Go* benefited from the burgeoning appetite for resurrected American punk rock. While not reaching the massive sales of *Dookie*, it peaked at number 97 on the album charts and sold over half a million copies, a significant achievement for an indie band. "Ranset from Let's Go, their second album which came out five months after Duckey. Good timing because by then there was this growing appetite for this new resurrected form of American punk rock."

## curiosities
**Origins and Green Day's Support** : Rancid, a Bay Area band, formed in 1991 following the disbandment of Operation Ivy, which Green Day members Billy Joe Armstrong and Mike Dirnt had been big fans of. As Green Day gained prominence, they actively promoted Rancid, drawing them into the mainstream spotlight. "Ranset came together in 1991 once Op Ivy disbanded after a couple of years, and as Green Day was blowing up, they chatted up Ranset whenever they could."
**Commitment to Indie Label** : Unlike Green Day, Rancid opted to remain an independent band, recording their music for Epitaph Records, the label founded by members of Bad Religion. "But unlike Green Day, Ranset chose to stay indie, recording for Bad Religion's Epitaph Records."
**Mainstream Exposure via MTV** : Despite being an indie band, Rancid was propelled into the mainstream "stratosphere" due to the immense popularity created by Green Day. MTV, searching for more songs similar to Green Day's hits, discovered Rancid and their song "Salvation," giving them significant exposure. "But because of the huge vortex formed around Green Day, Ranset was sucked up into the stratosphere anyway, thanks largely to MTV. They went looking for more songs like Basket Case and Longview, and that's when they found Ranset and Salvation."
**Elder Statesmen of the Scene** : With some members approaching 30, Rancid was perceived as the "older brothers" or "elder statesmen" of the mid-90s punk scene, which garnered them a certain level of respect among younger fans. "Ranset were a little older too, with a couple of members pushing 30. They were seen as the older brothers, the elder statesmen of the scene, and that brought them a certain amount of respect."
**Connecting Fans to Original Punk** : Through Rancid's influence, many younger fans were introduced to the original British punk bands of the 1970s, such as The Clash. This connection also led to a resurgence of previously out-of-fashion styles, like big spiky mohawks, at Rancid's shows. "And through Ranset, a lot of kids were led back to the original British punks of the 1970s, like the clash. Big spiky mohawks were seen at a lot of Ranset shows, something that had gone out of fashion for a while."

# artist - The Offspring

## genres

## labels

## albums
**Smash (1994) - Indie Blockbuster** : Released on April 8, 1994, *Smash* started slowly, initially gaining traction among skaters and snowboarders through songs featured in boarding videos. However, it soon took off when one of its songs received massive radio play in LA, leading to an MTV video. By August 1994, the album was selling thousands of copies every few hours, dwarfing previous sales figures (their *Ignition* album sold 60,000 total, while *Smash* sold that every three days). With estimated worldwide sales of around 20 million records, it became the second-highest-selling indie release in history for a decade and a half, trailing only Adele's *XL* Recordings release. The album produced four radio and video singles and, alongside Green Day's *Dookie*, played a crucial role in introducing millions to a new era of punk rock, displacing grunge as the top alt-rock sound by 1995. "When the Smash record was released on April 8th, 1994, it started slow. And the only people who noticed it were those skaters and snowboarders who heard an offspring song or two that had been picked up for a couple of boarding videos."
**Ignition (1992) - Pre-Smash Sales** : *Ignition* was an earlier album by The Offspring that sold a grand total of 60,000 copies, a number later surpassed by *Smash* which sold that many every three days during its peak. "Their ignition album sold a grand total of 60,000 copies."
**Self-titled Album (1989) - Epitaph Debut** : The Offspring's first album released on Epitaph Records was self-titled and received decent reviews from hardcore fans, skaters, and snowboarders. "Their first album with them was self-titled and, like everything else that had come before, got some decent reviews from the hardcore faithful."

## songs
**Come Out and Play (1994)** : The first single from *Smash*, "Come Out and Play" gained massive traction after being played on an LA radio station, leading to a quick $5,000 music video funded by Epitaph Records. The song famously features Jason McClain, the band's number one fan and a forklift operator, contributing the line "you gotta keep them separated." "Come out and play the first single from the offspring's 1994 album, Smash. And one of their songs, one of the offspring songs, had somehow made it onto an LA radio station and was getting a massive response. Then MTV called, where's the video? So Epitaph sunk $5,000 into making a quickie clip."

## curiosities
**Formation as Manic Subsidal** : The band initially formed under the name Manic Subsital in the Huntington Beach area of Orange County, south of Los Angeles, comprising three high school students and the school's janitor. "They'd first come together as a group called Manic Subsital in the Huntington Beach area of Orange County, south of Los Angeles."
**Noodles' Entry into the Band** : Noodles, the guitarist, reportedly joined the band because he was the only member cool or old enough to purchase beer for the rest of the high school-aged group. "Apparently he got into the band because he was the only one cool enough or old enough to buy everyone else beer. That's how he got into the group."
**California Hardcore Scene** : The Offspring were deeply rooted in the California hardcore scene, a fierce but small community dedicated to punk that evolved from the original 1970s punk. This music became a soundtrack for skaters and snowboarders, including bands like Dead Kennedys, The Adolescents, The Vandals, Black Flag, and Bad Religion, which was niche and rarely played on radio. "Everyone was into California hardcore. The punk that evolved out of the original punk of the 1970s. The scene was small but fierce and its adherents stuck together. And some of this music became the soundtrack for skaters and snowboarders, dead candidates, the adolescence, the vandals, black flag, bad religion, really niche stuff at the time, and certainly stuff that never made it on the radio."
**Drummer Change and Early Demos** : After their original drummer left to pursue a career as a gynecologist, the band began recording demos and selling cassettes, such as the collectible "six songs and tarantica sets," at their gigs. "After a drummer change, the original dude went off to learn how to be a gynecologist. Seriously, they began recording demos and tossing off cassettes that they'd sell at gigs. If you can find original copies of the six songs and tarantica sets, you've actually got something pretty collectible."
**Alaska Snowboarding Gig** : Prior to their mainstream breakthrough, The Offspring secured a gig playing for snowboarders in Alaska, a trip that coincided with their song "Come Out and Play" beginning to explode on radio. "It got the band a sweet gig playing for a bunch of snowboarders up in Alaska. And it was while they were away that things got kind of crazy."
**Jason McClain - Number One Fan** : Jason McClain, a forklift operator and The Offspring's devoted number one fan, was recognized by the band with a small vocal part in their hit song "Come Out and Play," where he says the line "you gotta keep them separated." "Jason McClain. He was a forklift operator who was the offspring's number one fan. No matter where the band played, Jason was guaranteed to be in the audience. So as a reward, the band gave him a small part in that song. And that's Jason that says, you gotta keep them separated."
