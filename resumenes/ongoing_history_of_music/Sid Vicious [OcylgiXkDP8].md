## curiosities
**Birth and Early Life – May 10, 1957, London** : Simon John Richie was born in London on May 10, 1957. His father, John Richie, was a soldier in the Royal Guard, and his mother, Anne McDonald (later Anne Beverly), was a free-spirited hippie who had worked for the Royal Air Force. He was born without his parents ever being married. "Simon John Richie was born in London on May 10, 1957."
**Name Confusion – Simon John Richie vs. John Simon Richie** : While widely known as John Simon Richie, his birth certificate actually states Simon John Richie. The mix-up of his names remains a mystery, and he also referred to himself as John Richie, John Beverly, or "Simon Something" at different times. "I know a lot of people say John Simon Richie, but it's Simon John Richie on his birth certificates."
**Childhood in Ibiza and UK – Early 1960s** : Sid, his mother Anne, and his biological father John Richie planned to move to Ibiza. Anne and Sid went ahead, but John Richie never joined them. In Ibiza, Anne worked as a typist and sold pre-rolled joints to tourists, eventually getting deported back to the UK due to lack of money to settle. "The three of them moved to the Spanish island of Ibiza, or at least that was the plan."
**Mother's Heroin Addiction – By Spring 1962** : After returning to the UK, Sid's mother Anne scammed the National Health Service by pretending to be a junkie to get free housing. She then fully embraced the role and became a heroin addict by the spring of 1962, when Sid was about five years old. "Anne ended up being a heroin addict by the spring of 1962."
**Stepfather's Death – 1965** : In late 1964, his mother Anne married Christopher Beverly, which explained why his mother was known as Anne Beverly while Sid's birth name remained Richie. Christopher Beverly died of cancer about six months after the wedding, a few weeks before paperwork could be filed for him to adopt Sid. "Christopher Richie died of cancer about six months after the wedding and a few weeks before the paperwork would be filed so he could adopt Sid."
**Life in Hackney, London – Late 1960s** : Around five years after Christopher Beverly's death, Sid and his mother moved to Hackney in the East End of London, where they lived without many luxuries. His mother made extra money by rolling joints for dealers she knew and continued her occasional heroin use. "Mom made some extra dodge by rolling joints for the dealers that she knew."
**Intellectual Interests – Reading and Space Race** : Despite his later reputation, Sid was not unintelligent. He enjoyed reading books by Edgar Allan Poe and followed the American Space Race with significant interest. "He loved reading books by Edgar Allan Poe and watched the American Space Race with great interest."
**Art School and Meeting Future Punks – Before Dropping Out** : Before dropping out of art school, Sid met John Liden (later Johnny Rotten) and John Wardle (later Jah Wobble) in his classes. He also hung out with a fourth John, John Gray, who later lived a more traditional life. "Before he dropped out of art school, John met another couple of John's in some of his classes."
**Origin of "Sid Vicious" Name – From John Liden's Hamster** : John Liden gave him the name Sid Vicious. The "Sid" part came from Liden's pet hamster, who bit Sid one day, prompting Sid to exclaim, "Oh, Sid is really vicious!" "The Sid parts came from John Liden's pet hamster who bit Sid one day."
**Squatting in London – With Three Johns** : Sid and the three Johns (Liden, Wardle, Gray) eventually left home and squatted in an abandoned building in the Hampstead area of London. This period preceded the formation of the Sex Pistols. "Sid and the three John's eventually left home and squatted in an abandoned building in the hamstered area of London."
**Fashion Victimness – David Bowie and Roxy Music Influence** : Sid was initially very focused on fashion, particularly styles inspired by David Bowie's Ziggy Stardust and Aladdin Sane periods. He was also a huge fan of Roxy Music and would steal Vogue magazines, often getting fashion details wrong. "He was really into clothes, especially the styles inspired by David Bowie through his Ziggy Stardust in the Latin St period."
**Aspiring Male Model – Despite Appearance** : Despite having bad skin and awful acne, Sid seriously wanted to become a male model. He would wear sandals in winter to show off his weird nail polish and dyed his hair red like David Bowie. "He wanted to be a model. Seriously, he wanted to be a male model."
**Limited Musical Ability – Tambourine and Acoustic Guitar** : Before joining the Sex Pistols, Sid was not much into playing music. When busking with the other Johns, he could only play the tambourine. When given an acoustic guitar, he could not play it. "Sid really couldn't do anything but play the tambourine, although they did once give him an acoustic guitar, which he could not play."
**Role in Sex Pistols Formation – Led to McLaren's Shop** : Sid's interest in fashion was key to the Sex Pistols' origin. It was he who led his friends into Malcolm McLaren's clothing shop in King's Road, the very place where the Sex Pistols were formed. "Sid's fashion victimness is key to the Sex Pistols story because it was he who led everyone into Malcolm Clarence's clothing shop in the King's Road, the place where the Sex Pistols were formed."
**Sex Pistols' Number One Fan – Bromley Contingent Member** : Sid was the Sex Pistols' biggest fan and a member of the "Bromley Contingent," a group of dedicated followers that also included future stars like Billy Idol and Siouxsie Sioux. He followed the band everywhere. "Sid became the Sex Pistols number one fan."
**Drummer for Siouxsie and the Banshees Debut – September 1976** : Sid played drums for Siouxsie and the Banshees at their debut performance in September 1976. This was one of his early musical involvements before joining the Sex Pistols. "He was the drummer for Susie in the Banshees when they made their debut in September of 1976."
**Member of Flowers of Romance – Early Band** : Sid was also part of a group called Flowers of Romance, another one of his early band projects in the burgeoning punk scene. "And he was also part of a group called Flowers of Romance."
**Missed Audition for The Damned – Lead Singer Opportunity** : Sid was considered for the role of lead singer in the new punk band The Damned but failed to show up for the audition and did not get the gig. "It's also possible that he was considered to be the lead singer of another new punk band called The Damned."
**Violent Incident – Glass Thrown at Pistols Gig** : Sid's violent tendencies were evident early on, including an incident where he threw a glass at a Sex Pistols gig, resulting in a woman losing an eye. "There was an incident where he threw a glass at a Pistols gig and a woman ended up losing an eye."
**Attack on NME Journalist Nick Kent – Over Clothing** : Sid once physically attacked Nick Kent, a journalist with the NME, with his motorcycle chain simply because he disliked Kent's pants. "In other case, he took his motorcycle chain to Nick Kent, a journalist with the NME because he didn't like his pants."
**Threat Against BBC TV Host – The Old Grey Whistle Test** : Sid also threatened the host of the BBC TV show "The Old Gray Whistle Test," showcasing his confrontational nature. "And then there was a time he threatened the host of a BBC TV show called The Old Gray Whistle Test."
**Joining Sex Pistols as Bassist – February 1977** : After Glenn Matlock was fired from the Sex Pistols in February 1977, manager Malcolm McLaren decided to replace him with a fan. Sid was chosen because he was always around and was Johnny Rotten's friend, embodying the punk ethos of the ultimate fan joining the ultimate band. "Malcolm McLaren came up with the idea of hiring one of the group's fans as his replacement."
**Self-Taught Bass Method – Ramones and Speed** : Legend claims Sid taught himself bass by staying up one night on speed, listening to a Ramones record. By morning, he could play along, supposedly qualifying him to be the Sex Pistols' bassist. "He stayed up one night on speed while listening to a Ramon's record. By morning, he could play along."
**Pogo Dance Inventor – Public Proclamation** : After joining the Sex Pistols, one of the first things Sid Vicious did was publicly declare himself the inventor of the Pogo, a punk dance characterized by jumping up and down in place. "One of the first things he did after joining the band was publicly proclaim himself as the inventor of the Pogo."
**Playing Bass with Sex Pistols – Doubts about Musicianship** : Sid played bass for the Sex Pistols through most of 1977 and during tours in Britain and Scandinavia. However, he was notoriously inept, often drunk and high, leading to significant doubt whether he ever actually played notes on stage. For some shows, a roadie was hired to play his parts offstage and unseen. "Basically Sid was so inept as a musician, often so drunk and high that there's real doubt whether he ever played a note on stage with the pistols."
**American Tour Disaster – Uncontrollable Behavior** : Sid was completely uncontrollable during the Sex Pistols' ill-fated 12-day American tour through the conservative South. He got into an altercation with Georgia police, was repeatedly beaten by his own bodyguards after baiting them, and his major heroin dependency, exacerbated by Nancy Spungen, made him miserable and strung out. "Sid was uncontrollable for the entire 12 days the van was on the road."
**Incident at a Texas Truck Stop – Slashed a Man's Arm** : During the American tour near San Antonio, Sid, strung out and high, slashed a man's arm with a pen knife at a truck stop restaurant because the man and his family made rude comments about Sid's appearance. Blood sprayed "like ketchup" over the man's steak, nearly inciting a riot among long-distance truck drivers. "He went round to the back of the gentleman that was eating his steak and chips and pulled his sleeve back and threw his arm over and above the man's head and over the man's plate up fairly high and withdrew a pen knife from his pocket and slashed his arm and all the blood blew out like ketchup over the man's steak."
**Violence Towards Audience Member – Bass Attack** : In San Antonio, Sid violently attacked an audience member, braining them with his bass in a "big two-hander to the head." "There were other incidents like the time in San Antonio when Sid brained a member of the audience with his base, big two-hander to the head."
**Self-Mutilation – Carving "Give Me a Fix"** : Sid frequently carved himself whenever a knife was handy. At one point, he carved "give me a fix" with a razor across his chest, illustrating his severe drug addiction. "At one point you could read where he crawled, give me a fix with a razor across his chest."
**Road Crew's Job to Bathe Him – Due to Drug-Induced Stupor** : Sid was so severely impacted by drugs that bathing him became a regular task for the road crew whenever he passed out. "He was so screwed up that it became part of the road crew's job to bathe him whenever he passed out."
**Bass Strings Broken on Stage – Dallas Gig** : During a Sex Pistols gig at the Longhorn Ballroom in Dallas on January 10, 1978, three of the four strings on Sid's bass were broken. He either didn't notice or didn't care, or it was irrelevant because a roadie was playing his parts backstage. "At one point during the gig, three of the four strings on Sid's bass were broken."
**Banned from American Airlines and Holiday Inn – Due to Self-Destruction** : By the time the Sex Pistols reached San Francisco on their American tour, the band had been banned from both American Airlines and the entire Holiday Inn chain, largely because Sid had redefined self-destructive behavior. "The group had been banned from American Airlines and the entire holiday in chain. Partly because Sid had rewritten the book on self-destruction."
**Interview with Johnny Rotten – Before Final San Francisco Show** : Before the final Sex Pistols show in San Francisco, Sid was coherent enough to do an interview with Johnny Rotten at a local radio station. However, the interview became ugly and aggressive. "Sid was straight enough to do an interview with Johnny at a local radio station. But it got ugly."
**Disappearance and Overdose After Breakup – January 1978** : Hours after the Sex Pistols broke up on January 14, 1978, Sid disappeared until he was found comatose in a closet in the Haight-Ashbury district. While attempting to fly back to London, he managed to overdose on the plane during a connection in New York. "He disappeared until someone found him comatose in a closet somewhere in the high-dashberry district."
**Filming for "The Great Rock and Roll Swindle" – Paris Trip** : Malcolm McLaren had the idea for Sid to continue as the new singer for the Sex Pistols. Sid traveled to Paris to film sequences for the movie "The Great Rock and Roll Swindle," performing Eddie Cochran songs. This trip was awful due to Sid and Malcolm's animosity and Nancy's continued drug supply. "The Sex Pistols could carry on with Sid as the new singer. So it was off to Paris in a cloud of junk to film a couple of sequences for a film that would end up being called the Great Rock and Roll Swindle."
**Evicted by Jah Wobble – With an Axe** : After the Paris trip, Sid and Nancy were evicted from a flat they were sharing with others by Sid's old friend, Jah Wobble. Wobble, fed up with Nancy especially, chased them out of the apartment with an axe. "So he chased the two of them out of the apartment with an axe."
**Move to New York City – August 1978** : With nowhere to go after their eviction, Sid and Nancy decided to move to New York City in August 1978, a place where Nancy claimed to know people. This move was partly funded by a £10,000 loan from Malcolm McLaren. "With nowhere to go, they decided that it was time to move to New York."
**Stay at Chelsea Hotel – Under Mr. and Mrs. Richie** : In New York, Sid and Nancy checked into the Chelsea Hotel on West 23rd Street in Manhattan, using the name Mr. and Mrs. Richie. They were placed on the first floor, a section unofficially reserved for junkies and "low-life." "In August of 1978, they checked into the Chelsea Hotel on West 23rd Street in Manhattan under the name Mr. and Mrs. Richie."
**New Band in New York – With New York Dolls and The Clash Members** : Within a week of arriving in New York, Sid was serious about his music career and formed a new band. This group included two members from the New York Dolls and Mick Jones of The Clash. "Within a week, he had a band together, consisting of two guys from the New York Dolls and Mick Jones of the Clash."
**Nancy's Managerial Interference – Scaring Off Labels** : Nancy Spungen acted as Sid's manager in New York but her outrageous demands and behavior alienated the press and scared away record companies. This made it impossible for Sid to form a lasting band. "As Sid's manager, she started throwing her weight around, depending on all sorts of outrageous things."
**Deeper Drug Stupor – Marijuana, Methadone, Heroin, Dilaudid** : As Sid's musical endeavors stalled due to Nancy's interference, both of them sank deeper into a stupor of multiple drugs, including marijuana, methadone, heroin, and Dilaudid. "Both of them sank deeper into a stupor of marijuana, methadone, heroin, and delauded."
**Hospitalization for Overdose – New York** : Sid overdosed at least once in New York and was subsequently sent to the hospital. "Sid OD, at least once that month, and was sent to hospital."
**Meeting Nancy's Parents – Philadelphia** : Sid and Nancy traveled to Philadelphia for Sid to meet Nancy's parents. The meeting was strained, and after a few hours, it was suggested the couple stay at a Holiday Inn instead of the family guest room. "They also went to Philadelphia, so Sid could meet the parents. That must have been interesting."
**Moved to Room 100, Chelsea Hotel – After Mattress Fire** : By October 11, 1978, Sid and Nancy had moved into room 100 at the Chelsea Hotel because the mattress in their previous room had caught fire. "By October 11th, 1978, Sid and Nancy had moved into room 100 at the Chelsea Hotel. See the mattress in their old room caught fire, wonder how that happened, and they had to move."
**Nancy Spungen's Murder – October 12, 1978** : On the morning of October 12, 1978, Nancy Spungen was found dead in the bathroom of room 100 at the Chelsea Hotel, stabbed in the stomach with a 007 brand knife. Sid claimed to have no memory of the event, as he had passed out after taking 22 Tuinals. "Nancy was lying dead on the bathroom floor of the hotel room. She had been stabbed in the stomach and had blood to death."
**Arrested for Second-Degree Murder – October 13, 1978** : Sid Vicious was charged with second-degree murder for Nancy Spungen's death on October 13, 1978, and sent to the detox unit at Rikers Island Prison. Bail was set at $50,000. "Sid was charged with second-degree murder and sent to the detox unit at Rikers Island Prison."
**Malcolm McLaren Posts Bail – October 17, 1978** : Malcolm McLaren posted Sid's $50,000 bail, leading to his release from Rikers Island on October 17, 1978. "October 17, 1978. Malcolm McLaren posts Sid's bail and he's released."
**Suicide Attempts After Bail – Slashing and Jumping** : After his release, Sid made two suicide attempts: first by slashing himself with a broken light bulb, and then by trying to jump out of a window at the Seville Hotel in New York. Both attempts were unsuccessful. "Sid attempts suicide by slashing himself with a broken light bulb. He messed that up so he tried to jump out of a window at the Seville Hotel in New York."
**Assault on Patti Smith's Brother – December 9, 1978** : On December 9, 1978, Sid got into a fight with the brother of singer Patti Smith during a night out and slashed his face with a beer bottle. He was arrested on felony assault charges and sent back to Rikers Island. "They get into a fight and Sid slashes the guy's face with a beer bottle."
**Mother Smuggles Drugs to Rikers Island – In Her Boots** : While Sid was imprisoned at Rikers Island, his mother Anne was a regular visitor. She smuggled dope into the prison inside her boots, which Sid would conceal and then use to get high in his cell. "She smuggled in dope in her boots. Sid took the drugs and stuck them, well you know where, and then he went back to his cell and got high."
**Release from Jail and New Girlfriend – February 1, 1979** : After 55 days in jail, Sid was released on bail on February 1, 1979, having completely detoxed. While incarcerated, he had managed to find a new girlfriend, Michelle Robinson. "After 55 days in jail, Sid is out on bail and by this time he is completely detoxed."
**Fatal Heroin Overdose – February 2, 1979** : To celebrate his freedom, Sid's mother arranged for a drug dealer, Peter Kodak, to deliver heroin to Michelle Robinson's apartment after a spaghetti dinner. Sid took a "taste" of 99% pure heroin. Being fully detoxed, his body was super vulnerable, and he overdosed, dying in his sleep as his heart rate slowed and lungs filled with fluid. "Apparently, the heroine he took the night before was 99% pure."
**Death at 21 Years Old – Before Murder Trial** : Sid Vicious died at the age of 21 on February 2, 1979, from a heroin overdose. His death occurred before he could stand trial for the murder of Nancy Spungen, leading the New York police to close the case. "Sid was dead before he got to go to trial for the murder of Nancy Spungin."
**Cremation and Ashes Scattered – February 7, 1979** : Sid Vicious was cremated on February 7, 1979, after receiving death certificate 156-799-102078 from the City of New York. His mother spread some of his ashes on Nancy's grave in Philadelphia and took the rest back to England, with an apocryphal story suggesting some spilled at Heathrow Airport. "His mom spread some of his ashes on Nancy's grave in Philadelphia and took the rest back to England."
**Punk's James Dean and Symbol of Self-Destruction – Legacy** : Sid Vicious became an icon after his death, selling millions of t-shirts annually. He is remembered as "punk's answer to James Dean" for his "live fast, die young, die hard" mentality and as a symbol of the original punk movement's inherent drive towards self-destruction. "First of all, he was punk's answer to James Dean. Live fast, die young, die hard."
**Doubts About Nancy's Murder – Alternate Theories** : Despite being charged, doubts persist about whether Sid actually murdered Nancy. Theories include Nancy plunging the knife into herself while high, or a drug dealer named Rockets Redgler (or Michael) being responsible, as suggested by the 2004 book "Too Fast to Live" and the film "Who Killed Nancy." "I think it's one of those things where, you know, when you're nodded out and you're, you know, on smack that... She could have as easily plunged it into herself and not even felt it."

"The group had been banned from American Airlines and the entire holiday in chain. Partly because Sid had rewritten the book on self-destruction."
**Band Breakup – January 14, 1978, San Francisco** : The Sex Pistols broke up hours after their final show with Sid Vicious at the Wintergarden Ballroom in San Francisco on January 14, 1978. The atmosphere within the band had become too violent, self-destructive, and strange to continue. "Hours after the show in San Francisco on January 14th 1978, the Sex Pistols broke up."
**Post-Breakup Activities of Members – Jones, Cook, Rotten** : After the breakup, guitarist Steve Jones and drummer Paul Cook went to South America and recorded with an exiled British criminal. Johnny Rotten (John Liden) traveled to Jamaica to relax, immerse himself in reggae music, and smoke ganja. "Guitar Steve Jones and drummer Paul Cook ended up in South America where they recorded with an exiled British criminal. Johnny Rotten eventually turned up in Jamaica to decompress, soak up some reggae and smoke some ganja."
