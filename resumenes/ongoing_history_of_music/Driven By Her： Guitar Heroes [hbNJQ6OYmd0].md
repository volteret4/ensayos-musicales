**Early Life and Busking Career** : Born in 1904, Memphis Minnie received her first guitar at age seven and was performing guitar and banjo at parties by age 11. In 1910, she ran away from home, surviving by busking on Beale Street in Memphis, followed by a stint with the Ringling Brothers Circus before returning to immerse herself in the burgeoning Memphis blues scene. "In 1910, she ran away from home and survived by busking on street corners along Beale Street in Memphis."
**Discovery and Extensive Recording** : Around 1930, Memphis Minnie and her second husband, Joe McCoy, were discovered by a Columbia Records talent scout, which launched her recording career. **Cutting Contest Victories** : Memphis Minnie was known for participating in and winning "cutting contests," musical duels where musicians competed to demonstrate the best licks and greatest skill, often triumphing against male competitors. "Mini won her share of those and they were all against dudes."
**Unique Blues Style and "First Punk Rocker" Label** : Her playing style offered a distinct take on the blues, characterized by more swing, force, and bounce, coupled with confidence and swagger. Her songs were frequently described as fierce and angry, leading some to call her the "first ever punk rocker" based on her attitude. "If you listen carefully to her playing style, she took the blues in a different direction. She played with a little more swing, a little more force, maybe a little more bounce."

"In 1945, she recorded a song called Strange Things Happening Every Day."
## curiosities
**Pioneering Electric Guitarist – First Woman Transition** : Starting in the 1930s, Sister Rosetta Tharpe began making gospel records, initially on acoustic guitar, then transitioning to the electric guitar. She is noted as potentially the first woman to make this shift and was using distortion to her advantage even before distortion pedals were invented. "As far as I can tell, she was the first woman to make that transition from acoustic to electric."
**Profound Influence on Blues and Rock Pioneers** : Sister Rosetta Tharpe had a deep impact on the trajectory of the blues and on the pioneers of rock and roll, including major figures like Little Richard, Chuck Berry, Jerry Lee Lewis, and Johnny Cash, all of whom were ardent fans. Her work also captivated British musicians such as Keith Richards, Eric Clapton, and Jeff Beck. "What is not in dispute is that sister Rosetta Tharp had a profound effect on the course of the blues, and then with the pioneers of rock."
**"Godmother of Rock and Roll" Title** : Due to her significant influence on early rock and roll artists and her innovative use of the electric guitar, Sister Rosetta Tharpe has been affectionately christened the "Godmother of Rock and Roll" by some. "So no wonder some people have christened her the godmother of rock and roll."
