## curiosities
**Early Musical Obsession** : Ian Thornley became obsessed with music from a very young age, starting around four or five years old. He began his musical journey on piano after a family friend had one, showing him a few things that he quickly picked up. His mother, who was also very musical and wanted to learn piano, decided they would both take lessons together around the age of five. "I've been obsessed with music from a very young age, from about four or five."
**Teenage Keyboard Whiz** : Up until about age 14, Ian played piano and synthesizers in pop bands around Toronto. He used a Juno 60 or 106, a Roland, and a Korg, and even an ironing board as a keyboard stand for gigs. He was considered a "whiz kid, keyboard kid" and would sometimes open sets with Bach's Toccata and Fugue in D minor before transitioning to a reggae vibe. "I was a synth guy."
**Cabbagetown Gig Experience** : As a 13-year-old, Ian Thornley filled in as a keyboard player for a band with a singer named Chrissy (a cousin). The band needed a replacement keyboardist for a gig at the Cabbagetown band after their previous player bailed. Despite his young age, he looked the part and managed to play the "pretty straightforward music" effectively. "I used an ironing board as a keyboard stand."
**First Recording Experience - Phase One** : As a teenage keyboardist in the band Tula, Ian went to Phase One studio to record his synth parts. This was an eye-opening and significant experience for him. "Went up to phase one and recorded and did on the synth parts."
**Transition from Keyboards to Guitar** : Ian stopped playing keyboards and piano after his mother became sick and subsequently passed away, a correlation he realized much later. For about a year and a half to two years, he gravitated towards athletics, cycling, and basketball. His father bought him an acoustic guitar, which he initially put in a closet after learning only a couple of chords. "that's really when I stopped playing keyboards and piano and all that."
**Guitar Inspiration from Dan** : While working a summer job at Sounds Interchange in the dub room, Ian befriended Dan, a talented guitar player. Dan played Zappa riffs and songs like Led Zeppelin's "Stairway to Heaven," which deeply impressed Ian and ignited his passion for the guitar. This experience made him realize he could recreate the music he loved with accuracy, leading him to fully embrace the instrument. "once I figured out that correlation of how it came pretty quickly, came pretty easily, but I think the real bug was being able to recreate things that I'd heard and things that I loved."
**Early Electric Guitar Influences** : Ian Thornley quickly acquired an electric guitar, a delay pedal, and a whammy bar, eager to emulate his influences. He was particularly obsessed with Bruce Cockburn's song "If a Tree Falls" and also practiced U2's music, using the gear to achieve similar sounds. "I was obsessed with if a tree falls or, yeah, if a tree falls. And that that, he used the whammy bar and the delay pedal."
**Berklee College of Music Admission** : Ian Thornley was admitted to the Berklee School of Music in Boston after submitting a 4-track demo featuring his compositions and various instrumental parts. His prior musical training, including piano studies with jazz musician Darwin Grosse, contributed to his foundational understanding of harmony and theory, which likely aided his admission. "I just, I had put together, you know, I had a little foretrack thing and I had written a bunch of compositions and, and don't know all these parts, put them all together and just sent those down."
**Berklee Experience and Departure** : Ian attended Berklee for three years but did not graduate. He realized that pursuing a degree often led to careers in teaching or session work involving chart reading, which didn't align with his aspirations for creating his own music. By his third year, he focused on taking courses with professors from whom he could gain practical insights. "I decided after, I think after two years, I had decided like I don't want to really, I'm not here to graduate."
**Roxbury Living Experience** : While attending Berklee, Ian moved into the old Tremont Towers in Roxbury, Boston, with Brian Doherty and Dave Henning. This was an eye-opening and "scary" experience for a kid from Toronto, as they were living in a rough neighborhood. He quickly learned to "shut up, keep my head down and get where I was going, especially if it was dark." "it was scary is what it was. It was scary."
**Country Music Gig** : After his student visa ran out, Ian was denied entry into the US and got a gig as a country guitar player for a few months in Canada. While he found the experience "great" in terms of general musicianship, he quickly realized that country guitar was "a different language" and not his forte, despite being an accomplished guitarist in other genres. "I am not a country guitarist."
**Velvet Revolver Audition** : Ian Thornley was offered the opportunity to become the lead singer and frontman for Velvet Revolver on two separate occasions. "he was offered the opportunity to become the lead singer in frontman for Velvet Revolver."

## albums
**In Loving Memory Of... (1997) - Debut Album** : Big Wreck's first album, released in 1997, consisted mostly of demos that Atlantic Records purchased. It marked the first time Ian Thornley heard his voice through speakers. The album was praised for sounding polished and fully formed, defying typical debut record expectations, and sold a quarter million copies in Canada. "that first record was a result of searching for a particular..."
**The Pleasure and the Greed (2001) - Second Album** : Released in June 2001, this was Big Wreck's second album, coming four years after their debut. The label, Atlantic Records, reportedly pressured the band to sound like Nickelback. The band, however, wanted to move in the opposite direction, leading to creative clashes and contributing to the eventual breakup of the original lineup. "The second album, the difficult second record comes out. The pleasure of the Greed in 2001."

## curiosities
**Formation in Boston** : Big Wreck was formed in Boston, Massachusetts, out of collaborations between Ian Thornley, Brian Doherty, Dave Henning, and Forest Williams, who had met while attending Berklee School of Music. "It was formed in Boston."
**Search for a Singer** : Initially, Big Wreck struggled to find a suitable singer, leading to Ian Thornley reluctantly taking on the vocal duties himself. They even tried out one singer who turned out to be associated with a "gang" and was suspected of stealing from the band's van, leading to his swift dismissal. "How did you end up singing? Because we couldn't find one."
**Management Trust Affiliation** : Big Wreck was managed by The Management Trust, a Canadian management company known for also representing The Tragically Hip, The Watchmen, and Andrew Cash at the time. The Management Trust played a crucial role in securing studio time and facilitating the band's early career development. "We had, we had management. We had the management trust."
**Creative Conflict with Atlantic Records** : For their second album, Atlantic Records pressured Big Wreck to adopt a sound similar to Nickelback, aiming for a more "packaged" and commercially viable product. The band, however, desired to move in a more experimental direction, creating significant tension and contributing to the eventual dissolution of the original lineup. "They needed to nickel back. They wanted, that's totally what they wanted."
**Version 1.0 Dissolution** : The original lineup of Big Wreck (version 1.0) lasted for exactly two albums, with its breakup influenced by factors including record label interference, extremely long touring periods (up to 18 months straight), and internal conflicts within the band. "So big rec version 1.0 lasts exactly two albums."

**Sounds Interchange - Summer Job Location** : Ian Thornley and his friend Dan worked summer jobs at Sounds Interchange, located on Adelaide, in the dub room. Their task involved creating quarter-inch dubs of commercials to send to various radio stations. "We'd get these summer jobs at a place, you know, sounds interchange."
**The Management Trust - Canadian Artist Management** : The Management Trust was a Canadian management company that represented several prominent Canadian bands, including The Tragically Hip, The Watchmen, Andrew Cash, and Big Wreck. They played a significant role in securing opportunities and funding for their artists. "We had, we had management. We had the management trust."
**Presence Studio - Big Wreck Demo Recording** : Big Wreck recorded their initial demos at Presence studio, a "great sounding room," with producer Chris Wardman and engineer Matti Mateo. These sessions formed the basis of their debut album. "we went into presence, great sounding room and, and we were all dazzled, loved, particularly the engineer who was Matti Mateo."
