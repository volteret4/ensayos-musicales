# genre - Post-Rock

## curiosities
**Definition and Philosophy** : Post-rock implies music that comes "after rock," but since rock is still present, it is better understood not as a genre or a sound, but as a philosophy or aesthetic. It explores "what happens if rock instruments stop behaving like rock instruments," focusing on experimentation, unusual uses of traditional rock instruments, texture, atmospherics, dynamic range, repetition of musical motifs, and often instrumental or cinematic scope. "This term implies that there is already something that comes after rock. But rock is still very much with us, so what's so post about it? This requires a substantial amount of deconstruction, and this is part two of our attempt to define post-rock."
**Core Characteristics** : It is rock music focused on experimentation, unusual uses of traditional rock instruments, an emphasis on texture and atmospherics, wide dynamic range, attention to repetition of musical motifs, often completely instrumental, and cinematic in scope and nature. "To review, it's rock music that focuses on experimentation, unusual uses of traditional rock instruments, a focus on texture and atmospherics, lots of dynamic range, attention to repetition of musical motifs, and often, but not always, completely instrumental and very cinematic in scope and nature."
**Regional Approaches - UK** : Post-rock bands from the UK tend to be spacey while also exhibiting restraint in their music-making. "Bands from the UK tend to be rather spacey while at the same time exhibiting some restraint in their music making."
**Regional Approaches - Montreal/Chicago** : Post-rock from Montreal and, to a certain extent, Chicago, is often more political and intellectual in its expression. "Montreal Post Rock, and to a certain extent, Chicago, is more political and intellectual."
**Regional Approaches - Texas** : Texas post-rock bands are distinguished as among the most cinematic in their musical approach. "And Texas Post Rock bands are among the most cinematic."
**Regional Approaches - Japan** : The Japanese post-rock scene is largely characterized by precision in its musical execution. "We could also look to Japanese Post Rock. That scene is largely about precision."
**Network and Global Reach** : Post-rock functions as a network, spreading through word-of-mouth, touring, and platforms like Bandcamp, YouTube, and Discord. It is a global phenomenon, with artists from the US, Canada, UK, Japan, Scandinavia, Eastern Europe, China, Latin America, and Israel. "It's also a network. This music travels by word of mouth. It travels by touring and sites like Bandcamp and YouTube and Discord all play a big part."
**Hybridization Potential** : The genre lends itself well to hybridization with other forms of rock because it is largely instrumental and thus free of language barriers. "It does lend itself to much hybridization because it's largely instrumental free of language barriers."
**Non-Traditional Outcomes** : Despite using traditional rock instruments like guitars, bass, drums, keyboards, and outboard gear, post-rock produces non-rock outcomes, avoiding typical verse-chorus structures in favor of atmospheric, experimental, and cinematic compositions. "What we're doing is producing non-rock outcomes. That means compositions without the usual verse chorus, verse structure, and more atmospheric, some more experimentation, and more of a cinematic approach to audio."
**Structure and Dynamics** : Post-rock often features long tracks that slowly and leisurely evolve, incorporating a wide dynamic range from whisper-quiet to overwhelmingly loud, frequently creating an emotional arc or oral narrative. "Long tracks that slowly and leisurely evolve with plenty of dynamics ranging from whisper-quiet to overwhelming loudness. Many pieces are wrapped in a sort of oral narrative that can be best described as an emotional arc."
**Lyrical and Riff Emphasis** : In post-rock, riffs are often secondary to texture, and while lyrics can be nice, they are not necessary, with the focus often on instrumentation to guide the listener on a wordless or nearly wordless musical journey. "Riffs, not smart about texture. Lyrics, nice but not necessary. Much of the time, the focus is on the instrumentation, and the goal is to take the listener on a wordless or nearly wordless musical trip."
**Enduring Nature** : Post-rock shows no signs of abating, continuously exploring new areas and sounds, with its experiments often trickling down to influence the broader rock world. "One thing is for sure though, post-rock shows no sign of a baiting. There are always areas and sounds to explore, and some of those experiments will continue to trickle down to the rest of the rock world."
**Recommended Bands for Exploration** : To explore post-rock further, one can look into bands such as Talk Talk, Slint, Bark Psychosis, Tortoise, Mogwai, Stereolab, Godspeed You! Black Emperor, Sigur Rós, Explosions in the Sky, Mono, This Will Destroy You, God Is An Astronaut, and Russian Circles. "If you want to go deeper, look into bands like Talk Talk, Slint, Barxide Coses, Tortoise, Magwai, Stereolab, Godspeed you, Black Amper, we always keep coming back to them. Singer Rousse, Explosions in the Sky, Mono, this will destroy you, God is an astronaut and Russian circles."

# genre - Shoegaze

## curiosities
**Overlap with Post-Rock** : Shoegaze bands have explored post-rock elements, both genres offering immersive sound experiences where listeners can get lost in effects and distortion. However, some post-rock can be clean and clear, contrasting with shoegaze's typical wall of sound. "Shoegaze bands have played with post-rock. Both are immersive sound experiences. You get lost in the effects in the distortion, but some post-rock is clean and clear."
**Melody in the Mix** : Shoegaze often features more melody than post-rock, though it is typically deeply embedded within the mix, requiring attentive listening. "Shoegaze has more melody, but it's often very deep in the mix."

# genre - Math Rock

## curiosities
**Overlap with Post-Rock** : Math rock can overlap with post-rock, but it is typically very challenging to play due to its complicated and precise nature, demanding a lot of precision. "Math rock can overlap with post-rock. This is music that's very challenging to play because it's so complicated and precise. It requires a lot of precision."
**Time Signatures and Tension** : A key difference from post-rock is math rock's preference for weird time signatures, in contrast to post-rock's smooth beats (almost always 4/4, occasionally 6/8). Math rock songs often feature significant tension, arising from their angularity and the physicality of the playing. "A big difference is that math rock loves weird timesignatures. Post-rock tends to prefer smooth beats, almost always 4-4, but 6-8 comes up once in a while."
**Structural Philosophy** : Math rock focuses on building complicated structures, in contrast to post-rock's emphasis on long, flowing fields of sound. "Math rock is about building complicated structures. Post-rock is about long flowing fields."

# genre - Ambient Music

## curiosities
**Distinction from Post-Rock** : Ambient music aims to create a musical environment that soothes listeners but differs from post-rock in its lack of climax or defined end. While ambient music drifts, post-rock typically takes the listener on a journey with a specific destination. "Ambient music drifts. There's almost no climax or defined end. Post-rock takes you on a journey with a destination."

# genre - Post-Metal

## curiosities
**Characteristics and Riff Focus** : Post-metal is described as heavy, crushing, slow, doom-laden music that evokes a feeling of dread. While there is some post-rock that shares these characteristics, post-metal distinguishes itself by making riffs a central part of the song structure, unlike post-rock where riffs are often secondary to texture. "This is heavy, crushing, slow, doom-laden stuff that comes with a feeling of dread. There's post-rock like that, but post-metal gets us back into riffs as a central part of the song structure."
