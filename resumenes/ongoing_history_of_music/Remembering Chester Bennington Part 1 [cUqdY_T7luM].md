## curiosities
**Date of Death and Cause** : Chester Bennington, the vocalist for Linkin Park, was confirmed dead by his own hand on Thursday, July 20, 2017. His death came as a shock and was widely considered unexpected, even among a series of other musician deaths around that time. "Chester Bennington, vocalist with Lincoln Park, was not only dead, but dead by his own hand."
**Family Life** : At the time of his death, Chester Bennington had a loving family which included six children. "He had a loving family with six, six kids."
**Early Life and Birthplace** : Chester Bennington was born on March 20, 1976, in Phoenix. His mother was a nurse, and his father was a police detective whose work primarily focused on child sex abuse cases. "Chester was born on March 20th, 1976 in Phoenix."
**Parents' Divorce and Custody** : When Chester was 11 years old, his parents divorced, and his father was granted custody. This event significantly impacted him, leading him to delve deeply into drug use. "Mom and dad divorced when Chester was 11, dad got custody."
**Teenage Drug Abuse** : As a teenager, Chester Bennington experimented with a wide range of drugs, including pot, coke, various opioids, LSD, and meth. His father, a police officer, was unable to manage his drug abuse, leading Chester to move in with his mother at the age of 17. "As a teenager, he tried everything. Pot, Coke, various opioids, LSD, meth, basically the whole buffet."
**High School Bullying and Abuse** : Chester's high school experience was difficult, marked by bullying and physical altercations. More profoundly, he was sexually abused by an older male friend from the age of seven until he was 13, an experience he kept secret due to shame and fear of not being believed. "High school was hell where he was bullied and beaten up. But that paled in comparison to the sexual abuse that was subjected upon him by an older male friend."
**Restrictions and Early Job** : After moving in with his mother at 17 due to his drug use, Chester was essentially permanently grounded, with his only approved outings being for his shifts at Burger King. "he was basically permanently grounded, except for his shifts in Burger King."
**Music as an Escape** : Music provided a crucial escape for Chester Bennington during his difficult teenage years, becoming a significant and positive outlet. "Fortunately, music became a bigger and better escape."
**Marriage and Career Frustration** : By 1998, a young Chester Bennington was married, despite both he and his wife being barely 20. Feeling completely frustrated with his lack of progress in the music industry, he quit his band Grey Daze and took a job as an assistant with a digital services company. "But by then, he was completely frustrated with his lack of progress in the music biz."
**Audition for Zero** : Chester was contacted by a VP of A&R at Zomba Music and asked to audition for a promising band in LA called Zero, which needed a frontman. He received two tapes from Zero—one with existing vocals by Mark Wakefield and another with instrumentals. He recorded his vocal interpretations over the instrumental tape and sent it back, successfully securing the position. "Chester was asked to provide some vocal interpretations for the music, record them over the instrumentals and then send the tape back."
**Contribution to Linkin Park's Name** : Chester Bennington was credited with suggesting the name "Linkin Park" after driving past a real Lincoln Park in Santa Monica. The band also appreciated the national appeal of a name associated with Abraham Lincoln. "But it was Chester who came up with the name that stuck. He was driving past a real Lincoln Park in Santa Monica."

## curiosities
**Founding and Early Recordings** : Zero was founded in LA by three high school friends: Mike Shinoda, Brad Delson, and Rob Borden, who, along with other members, began playing gigs and recording. They released a 4-track cassette in 1997, available in two versions with different covers, which are now highly sought after by collectors due to their rarity. These early recordings, including the track "Rine Stone," were made in singer Mark Wakefield's bedroom using a basic computer setup. "This was a group founded by three high school friends, Mike Shinoda, Brad Delson and Rob Borden."
**Diverse Musical Influences** : The members of Zero had a wide array of musical tastes that influenced their sound, including bands like Alice in Chains, The Roots, and The Prodigy. "Everyone in the band was into different things. Allison Chains, the Roots, the Prodigy, you mix it all together and it all came out like this."
**Lack of Initial Record Label Interest** : Despite being a promising band and creating "okay stuff," Zero struggled to garner interest from record companies during their early years. "Some okay stuff, but it wasn't finding any love at the record company level."

# artist - Hybrid Theory

## member of
Zero
Linkin Park

## curiosities
**Name Change from Zero** : After Chester Bennington joined the band, Zero changed its name to Hybrid Theory. "The band changed their name to Hybrid Theory and they recorded a self-titled EP."
**Record Label Rejections** : Hybrid Theory faced significant challenges in securing a record deal, being turned down by record labels at least 42 times, which was explicitly stated as not an exaggeration. "The third theory was turned down by record labels at least 42 times."
**Early Internet Promotion** : Hybrid Theory was an early adopter of internet-based self-promotion, establishing an online street team consisting of volunteers. These volunteers engaged in various chat rooms and music forums to publicize the band's record, although this strategy initially did not lead to widespread success. "They must have been one of the first bands to properly use the internet to publicize themselves."
**Mandatory Name Change to Linkin Park** : The band was forced to change their name from Hybrid Theory in 1999 to avoid legal issues, as another band with the same name already existed in Wales and was signed to Warner Brothers Records. This legal necessity led to the adoption of the name Linkin Park. "they really didn't have much choice because it turned out that there was already a band in Wales called Hybrid Theory, so they had to change their name to avoid any messy legal situations."
**Rejected Band Names** : Before settling on Linkin Park, the group considered other names such as "Clear" (as in transparent), "Probing Loggers," and "Platinum Lotus Foundation." "The group went through a bunch of possibilities, clear as in transparent, probing loggers. No. Platinum Lotus Foundation."

## albums
**Meteora (2003)** : This was Linkin Park's second studio album, released in the spring of 2003. "Lincoln Park from their second album, Mediaora."
**Hybrid Theory (2000) – Debut Album** : Released on October 20, 2000, this album was a monumental success, selling five million copies in its first year and over 30 million worldwide. It topped charts in New Zealand (number one), and reached number two in the US, Germany, and Australia, also charting in the top ten in Canada, Austria, Belgium, Denmark, Finland, Hungary, Norway, Scotland, Sweden, and Switzerland. The album earned three Grammy nominations, winning one for "Crawling" in the Best Hard Rock Performance category. Its success was attributed to the band's strong music, Mike Shinoda's rapping, and their firm stance against various "stupid suggestions" from label representatives regarding their image and sound, such as removing rap elements or altering their logo. "That record was released on October 20th of 2000."
**Reanimation (2002) – Remix Album** : Released in 2002, this was a 20-track remix album co-produced by Mike Shinoda. Creating a remix album so early in their career was unusual, but Warner Brothers Records approved it due to the strong sales of their debut, Hybrid Theory. The album went on to sell two million copies and is recognized as the third best-selling remix album of all time. "Reanimation is officially the third best-selling remix album of all time."

## songs
**Somewhere I Belong (2003)** : This song is featured on Linkin Park's second album, Meteora. The lyrics express a desire for authenticity and belonging, including lines like "I want to feel like I'm close to something real. I want to find something I want it all along. Somewhere I belong." "Lincoln Park from their second album, Mediaora."
**Untitled (Demo Version) (pre-2000)** : This demo track, which would later become the hit song "In the End," was part of a nine-song demo created by Hybrid Theory before they officially became Linkin Park. This particular demo version was circulated and rejected by record labels at least 42 times. The lyrics, even in this early form, included the memorable line, "But in the end, it doesn't even matter." "In the end, the demo version, before it even had a title by Hybrid Theory just before they changed their name to Lincoln Park."
**Crawling (2000)** : The second single from Linkin Park's debut album, Hybrid Theory. Chester Bennington composed the lyrics drawing directly from his experiences with drugs and alcohol. The track features minimal rapping from Mike Shinoda, a decision made by the label to test the waters regarding the presence of a rapper in the band. The song's musical style was influenced by the band's collective appreciation for Depeche Mode, and it went on to win a Grammy for Best Hard Rock Performance. "Crawling, the second single from Lincoln Park's debut album, Hybrid Theory."
**In the End (2000)** : Featured on their debut album Hybrid Theory, this song became a significant hit for Linkin Park. It reached number one on the singles charts in the US and climbed to number two in Canada, solidifying the band's mainstream success. "In the end became a genuine top 40 hit reaching all the way to number one on the singles charts in the US and all the way to number two in Canada."
**One Step Closer (2000)** : A single from Hybrid Theory, this song originated from the band's frustration with producer Don Gilmore, who was intensely pushing them in the studio. Chester Bennington's lyrics, such as "I cannot take this anymore. I'm saying everything I've ever done before, all these words make no sense" and the chorus "Everything you say takes me one step closer to the edge and I'm going to break," were explicitly directed at Gilmore for dismissing his lyrical submissions. The "shut up" part was also 100% aimed at him. Despite the band's initial reluctance to include it on the album, it was released as a single, became a hit, and effectively launched Linkin Park's career. "One step closer, which is basically Chester Bennington screaming at hybrid theory producer Don Gilmore because Chester was tired of being pushed around in the studio."
**Papercut (2000)** : One of the four singles released from the debut album Hybrid Theory. A live recording of "Papercut" was made at the Docklands Arena in London for the BBC, and this version was subsequently included on a bonus disc accompanying the special two-disc Japanese edition of the *Hybrid Theory* album. "It's a version of PaperCut recorded of the Docklands Arena in London for the BBC."
**Points of Authority (2002 Remix)** : A reinterpreted mix of this song is featured on Linkin Park's 2002 remix album, Reanimation. The new vocal contribution on this particular track comes from Jay Gordon, who is the lead singer of the San Francisco-based band Orgy. "This is the reinterpreted mix of points of authority from Lincoln Park's Reanimation album from 2002."

## curiosities
**Sales Figures** : Linkin Park has sold approximately 100 million records throughout their career, highlighting their significant commercial success. "This guy was the frontman for a band that sold somewhere around 100 million records."
**Strategic Name Change for Domain** : In 1999, Linkin Park deliberately misspelled "Lincoln" in their name as "Linkin" to secure the readily available domain name, Lincolnpark.com. This approach to online branding was a forward-thinking strategy for a band at that time. "The reason they deliberately misspelled Lincoln, so they could get the domain, Lincolnpark.com."
**Record Deal with Warner Brothers** : Linkin Park signed with Warner Brothers Records in late 1999, a deal facilitated by an A&R executive named Jeff who had previously recommended Chester Bennington to the band (then Zero) and later became a VP at Warner Brothers. This signing occurred despite Warner Brothers having rejected the band three times previously. "they got a meeting, and they were signed in late 1999."
**Band's Stand Against Label Interference** : In an unusual move for a new band, Linkin Park directly met with Warner Brothers executives and firmly rejected various label suggestions, including demands to remove rapping, change Mike Shinoda's rap style to mimic Fred Durst, bring in a reggae vocalist named Matt Lyons, or introduce gimmicks for DJ Joe Hahn like wearing a cowboy hat and lab coat. They also refused to change their logo, which some at the label found "too Asian." "And the meeting was risky because new bands just don't show up in the boardrooms of major record labels and start dictating terms for how they were to be marketed and how their fan club should be treated."
**Initial Label Disdain for Sound** : Warner Brothers Records executives, including reportedly the label president, initially struggled to understand and even disliked Linkin Park's sound, characterized by "screaming and rapping." There was even internal debate about whether to release their debut record at all. "Warner did not know what to do with Lincoln Park and some, including apparently the president of the label just hated their sound."
**Intense Touring Schedule** : In the year immediately following the release of their debut album, Hybrid Theory, Linkin Park demonstrated an extraordinary work ethic by performing 320 shows. "In the first year after hybrid theory came out, they played 320 shows."
**Project Revolution Tour** : Linkin Park took the initiative to create and headline their own tour, "Project Revolution," which notably featured popular artists such as Snoop Dogg and Cypress Hill. "They created their own tour called Project Revolution, which featured Snoop Dogg and Saipers Hill."
**Pioneering Online Fan Club** : Linkin Park established a high-level fan club and street team called "Linkin Park Underground," which focused on serving fans online. This dedication to online fan engagement was a novel approach for bands at the time. "They created a high-level fan club slash street team called Lincoln Park Underground that strove to serve the fans online, something that few bands were doing at the time."
**Japanese Special Edition of Hybrid Theory** : A unique two-disc version of Linkin Park's debut album, Hybrid Theory, was sold in Japan. The first disc contained the standard album, while the second disc featured a collection of unreleased live tracks and additional studio recordings. "there was a special two-disc version of hybrid theory sold in Japan."
**Extensive Documentation** : Linkin Park's journey and story are exceptionally well-documented by various entities, including the band members themselves, their management company, their record label, and their dedicated fanbase. "Lincoln Park is a very 21st century band and the sense that their story is so very well documented by themselves, their management company, their label, and their fans."
