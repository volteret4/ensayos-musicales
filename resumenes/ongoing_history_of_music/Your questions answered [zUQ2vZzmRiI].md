## albums

**21 Pilots (2009) – Self-Titled Debut Album** : A self-produced indie record recorded at home and released on December 29, 2009, featuring the original lineup of Tyler Joseph, Nick Thomas, and Chris Salih. Despite its low-key nature, it sold approximately 115,000 copies.

**Regional at Best (2011) – Discontinued Second Album** : Released on July 8, 2011, this project was primarily a solo effort by Tyler Joseph, who described it as a "glorified mixtape". As a condition of their signing with Fueled by Ramen, the album was completely discontinued and wiped from digital platforms. Physical copies have become highly collectible, trading for between $35 and $900.

**Vessel (2013) – Third Studio Album** : Released on January 8, 2013, as their Fueled by Ramen debut, this album received positive reviews and significantly expanded the band's fanbase. Its success led to international tours and major festival slots at Lollapalooza and Bonnaroo.

**Blurryface (2015) – Fourth Album & Breakthrough** : This 2015 release marked the band's major commercial breakthrough, selling close to 2 million copies in the streaming era. The album introduced a complex narrative centered on the character "Blurryface," representing frontman Tyler Joseph's insecurities.

**Trench (2018) – Fifth Studio Album** : Released in 2018, this album expanded the band's lore into a fictional world featuring the city of "Dema" and the "Banditos". Its themes explore doubt, anxiety, faith, and suicide.

## songs

**Blasphemy (2008)** : A track from Tyler Joseph's high school solo album, _No Fun Intended_.
**Oh Ms Believer (2009)** : A song from the band's self-titled indie debut.
**Forest (2011)** : A track from _Regional at Best_, recorded while Tyler Joseph was the sole member of the band.
**Guns for Hands (2012)** : One of five tracks re-recorded from _Regional at Best_ for the _Vessel_ album.
**Car Radio (2013)** : A hit single inspired by a real-life incident where Tyler Joseph's car was burglarized; the theft of his radio and CDs forced him to drive in silence and reflect.
**Stressed Out (2015)** : A prominent lead single from the breakthrough _Blurryface_ era.
**Chlorine (2018)** : A single from _Trench_ whose title was teased with chemical codes (atomic number 17 and weight 35.427) corresponding to the element chlorine.
**Jumpsuit (2018)** : The lead single from _Trench_, announced via an email featuring a closing eye image.

## curiosities

**Origin of Band Name** : The name is derived from Arthur Miller’s 1947 play _All My Sons_, which tells the story of 21 pilots who died because a businessman approved faulty aircraft engines during WWII.
**Stylized Branding** : The omission of a hyphen in "21 pilots" was a specific design choice by Tyler Joseph for visual adaptability in logos and graphic design.
**The Skeleton Clique** : The band's dedicated fanbase, known for an inclusive culture where members call each other "frens" and use the phrase "Stay Street".
**dmaorg.info Mystery** : In April 2018, fans found a hidden website address embedded in a gift image on the band's online store. The site contained cryptic warnings and journal entries from a character named Clancy, leading to the _Trench_ album reveal.
**Banditos' Color** : In the _Trench_ mythology, the resistance group known as the Banditos uses a specific shade of yellow because the oppressive "bishops" of Dema are unable to perceive it.

## instruments

**Keyboard** : Tyler Joseph's first musical instrument, which he began experimenting with in his early days.
**Drums** : The primary instrument played by Josh Dun, who joined in 2011.
**Bass** : Played by original member Nick Thomas.

## instruments

**8-track Digi Recorder** : Used to record their debut album, _The Big Come Up_.
**eBay Recording Console** : A "crap" console previously owned by a Loverboy engineer, used during the _Rubber Factory_ sessions.
**Inexpensive Gear ($5,000)** : Approximately $5,000 worth of "crappy gear" was intentionally used for _Magic Potion_ to achieve a specific "weird" vibe.

## albums

**The Big Come Up (2002) – Debut Studio Album** : Recorded in Patrick Carney's parents' basement, this indie release featured limited edition colored vinyl and was well-received by critics.
**Thickfreakness (2003) – Second Studio Album** : Recorded in a single 14-hour session financed by the members mowing lawns; it was retitled _Inezuma Rock and Blues_ in Japan.
**Rubber Factory (2004) – Third Studio Album** : Recorded in an abandoned tire factory, this was the band's first album to chart on the Top 200.
**Brothers (2010) – Commercial Breakthrough** : Recorded at Muscle Shoals during a period of intense personal friction, this album won three Grammys and achieved massive commercial success.
**El Camino (2011) – Global Success** : Produced by Danger Mouse in Nashville, this album went platinum worldwide and was inspired by 60s and 70s rock hooks.

## curiosities

**Band Formation (2001)** : The duo formed in Akron, Ohio, after an attempt to record a demo with friends resulted in only Dan and Patrick showing up.
**The Grey Ghost** : Their original 1994 Chrysler touring van, which was later honored on the cover of _El Camino_.
**Initial Anti-Commercial Stance** : The band famously turned down £200,000 for a mayonnaise commercial early in their career to protect their artistic credibility.

# genre - Rock

## curiosities

**Superstar Rock Act Definition (Traditional)** : Defined by selling tens of millions of units, drawing tens of thousands to concerts, and creating a deep catalog of hits deemed great by a consensus of millions.
**The "Fourth Album" Rule** : Historically, many major acts like Red Hot Chili Peppers, Muse, and R.E.M. did not achieve true mainstream success until their fourth or fifth album.
**Streaming Disadvantage** : Rock songs are rarely found in the top 200 weekly streamed songs, which are dominated by pop, R&B, and hip hop.

# curiosity

**Spotify 30-Second Payout Rule** : Spotify only pays royalties for a stream after 30 seconds have passed.
**Shortening Intros** : To combat high skip rates, instrumental song intros have shrunk from 17 seconds in the 1980s to under 5 seconds today.
**Music Industry Transformation (Post-2000)** : Since 2000, metrics like album sales and chart positions have become less relevant for defining superstardom.
**End of Traditional Gatekeepers** : The internet has removed the power of traditional gatekeepers (labels, radio, print media) to manufacture consensus, shifting power to the individual fan.
