## instruments

**8-track Digi Recorder** : Used to record the band's debut album, _The Big Come Up_.
**eBay Recording Console** : A "crap" console previously owned by a Loverboy engineer, used during the _Rubber Factory_ sessions.
**Inexpensive Gear ($5,000)** : Purposefully used for _Magic Potion_ to achieve a specific "weird" vibe.
**John Bonham Drum Setup** : Adopted by Patrick Carney during the _Attack & Release_ sessions for a larger, tighter sound.

## albums

**The Big Come Up (2002) – Debut Studio Album** : Recorded in Patrick Carney's parents' basement, this indie release was well-received by critics and featured limited edition colored vinyl.
**Thickfreakness (2003) – Second Studio Album** : Recorded in a single 14-hour session and financed by the members mowing lawns; it was retitled _Inezuma Rock and Blues_ in Japan.
**Rubber Factory (2004) – Third Studio Album** : Recorded in an abandoned tire factory, this album was the band's first to chart on the Top 200 and secured them a slot at Lollapalooza.
**Magic Potion (2006) – Fourth Studio Album** : The band's first major-label release on Nonesuch, it reached the Top 100 in the US and the Top 12 in Australia despite the band's dissatisfaction with its mastering.
**Attack & Release (2008) – Fifth Studio Album** : Their first collaboration with producer Danger Mouse, featuring additional musicians and a more polished sound.
**Brothers (2010) – Sixth Studio Album & Breakthrough** : Recorded at Muscle Shoals during a period of intense personal friction, this album won three Grammys and achieved massive commercial success.
**El Camino (2011) – Seventh Studio Album & Global Success** : Produced by Danger Mouse in Nashville, this album went platinum worldwide and was inspired by 60s and 70s rock hooks.
**Turn Blue (2014) – Eighth Studio Album** : A psych-rock leaning record influenced by Dan Auerbach's divorce, announced uniquely via a tweet from Mike Tyson.

## songs

**Set You Free (2003)** : A single from _Thickfreakness_ that gained fame after its inclusion in the film _School of Rock_.
**I Got Mine (2008)** : Considered the biggest hit of their pre-superstar era, frequently used in TV themes.
**Tighten Up (2010)** : The only Danger Mouse-produced track on _Brothers_, it earned the band a Grammy for Best Rock Song.
**Lonely Boy (2011)** : A global hit featuring a viral music video of security guard Derek T. Tuggle dancing in a single shot.
**Weight of Love (2014)** : A Pink Floyd-inspired opening track that reflects the emotional weight of Auerbach's divorce.

## curiosities

**Band Formation (2001)** : The duo formed in Akron, Ohio, after no other musicians showed up to a scheduled demo recording session.
**The Grey Ghost** : The band's original 1994 Chrysler touring van, which was later honored on the cover of _El Camino_.
**Initial Anti-Commercial Stance** : The band famously turned down £200,000 for a mayonnaise commercial early on to protect their "indie cred".
**Internal Strife** : By 2010, the duo was on the verge of breaking up due to depression, drinking, and a lack of communication regarding solo projects.

# artist - Twenty One Pilots

## members

Tyler Joseph
Josh Dun
Nick Thomas (Original Bassist)
Chris Salih (Original Drummer)

## genres

Indie
Alternative Rock

## labels

Fueled by Ramen
Tinderbox

## albums

**21 Pilots (2009) – Self-Titled Debut Album** : A home-recorded indie release that sold 115,000 copies despite its DIY origins.
**Regional at Best (2011) – Discontinued Second Album** : Primarily a Tyler Joseph solo project, this album was wiped from existence as part of their major label deal and is now a rare collector's item.
**Vessel (2013) – Third Studio Album** : Their Fueled by Ramen debut, which launched them into the international festival circuit.
**Blurryface (2015) – Breakthrough Album** : A massive success selling 2 million copies, centered on themes of insecurity and mental health.
**Trench (2018) – Fifth Studio Album** : A concept album that expanded the band's mythology into the fictional city of Dema.

## songs

**Car Radio (2013)** : A hit single written after Tyler Joseph’s car was burglarized, leaving him in silence without his music.
**Stressed Out (2015)** : A global breakthrough single that defined the _Blurryface_ era's success.
**Chlorine (2018)** : A track from _Trench_ whose title was teased through chemical atomic numbers on a hidden website.

## curiosities

**Origin of Band Name** : Derived from Arthur Miller's play _All My Sons_, concerning 21 pilots who died due to faulty parts.
**The Skeleton Clique** : The band's dedicated fanbase, known for their inclusive culture and calling each other "frens".
**Lore and Mythology** : The band created an immersive world involving "Bishops" and the "Banditos" to bring mystery back to music in the internet age.

# genre - Rock

## curiosities

**Superstar Definition** : Traditional rock superstardom requires tens of millions in sales, sold-out arenas, and a deep catalog deemed "great" by a consensus of millions.
**Aging Demographic** : Most of the world's biggest rock acts are now middle-aged or older, with few new "superstars" emerging to replace them.
**Streaming Disadvantage** : Rock music is rarely found in the top 200 weekly streamed songs, which are dominated by hip-hop and pop.
**The "Fourth Album" Rule** : Historically, many rock bands—including Red Hot Chili Peppers, Muse, and R.E.M.—did not achieve true mainstream success until their fourth or fifth album.

# curiosity

**Spotify Payout Rule** : Spotify only pays royalties if a song is listened to for at least 30 seconds, leading producers to front-load songs with hooks.
**Shortening Intros** : To combat high skip rates, instrumental song intros have shrunk from 17 seconds in the 1980s to under 5 seconds today.
**Napster and iTunes Impact** : Napster began the "death of the album" by offering an a la carte buffet of songs, a trend legalized and cemented by the launch of the iTunes Store in 2001.
