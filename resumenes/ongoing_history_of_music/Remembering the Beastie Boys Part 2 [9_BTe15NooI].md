## albums
**License to Ill (1986) - Parody of Hip Hop** : The debut album was initially perceived as a parody of hip hop, leading to the band being pigeonholed as a novelty act for the first eight years of their career. Despite its humorous nature, Rolling Stone magazine acknowledged its significant impact. Profits from this album were later used to fund their G-Sun studio. "License to ill was a parody of hip hop. It was a good one, but it was still a parody."
**Paul's Boutique (1989) - Commercial Sales Disaster** : Their second album was a commercial failure upon its release, confusing the majority of fans attracted by "License to Ill" and even their record company. Despite its poor initial reception, it is now considered a masterpiece of production and engineering. "Part one ended with the, shall we say, underwhelming commercial reception of their second album, Paul's boutique. It was a sales disaster."
**Check Your Head (1992) - Comeback Album** : Released after a three-year gap, this album marked a significant shift in the band's sound and approach, moving away from heavy sampling due to artistic desire and economic necessity (cost of clearing samples). Recorded at their self-built G-Sun studio in Los Angeles with Money Mark on keyboards and Mario Caldato Jr., the album solidified their musical credibility and sold well, reaching the top 10 of the US album charts. "Check Your Head not only solidified the Beastie Boys' musical credibility, it also sold big time reaching into the top 10 of the US album charts."
**Ill Communication (1994)** : The band's fourth album, which was largely finished when Mike D wrote "Sabotage." The album's lead single and video helped elevate its profile significantly. "Sabotage from the Beastie Boys 1994 album, Ill Communication."
**Aglio E Olio (1995) - Hardcore EP** : A low-key EP released after the Quadrophonic Joystick Action Tour, featuring eight hardcore songs in just 11 minutes. "There was a quickie hardcore EP called Aglio Ioleo, eight songs in just 11 minutes."
**The In Sound From Way Out (1995) - Funky Jazz CD** : A low-key CD released after the Quadrophonic Joystick Action Tour, comprising a collection of funky jazz tracks. "plus there was a CD full of funky jazz called The In Sound From Way Out."
**Hello Nasty (1998) - Chart-Topping Success** : Released on July 14, 1998, this album was a major commercial success, debuting at number one and selling 681,000 copies in its first week in the U.S. alone. It featured the single "Intergalactic," known for its intricate sampling. "It was ready by July 14, 1998, and they called it Hello Nasty. Another number one. This time selling 681,000 copies in its first week in the U.S. alone."
**To the 5 Boroughs (2004) - New York Tribute** : This album was conceived as a love letter and tribute to New York City, reflecting the events and atmosphere in the city following the 9/11 attacks in the early 2000s. The recording process involved building tracks around beats, with band members contributing rhymes and then refining vocals with "nicer mics" before handing off to Duro for mixing. The album faced controversy in Europe due to copy protection software. "They call the album to the five boroughs."
**The Mix-Up (2007) - Instrumental Grammy Winner** : An instrumental album released on June 26, 2007, an idea the band had considered since their "Check Your Head" days in 1991-1992. Although it didn't sell very well, it won a Grammy for Best Instrumental Album and was intended as a statement about the status of hip-hop music. "It didn't sell very well, but it did win a Grammy for Best Instrumental Album."
**Hot Sauce Committee Part One (2009) - Unreleased Album** : This album was originally slated for release on September 15, 2009, as the first part of a two-part set. Its release was postponed due to Adam Yauch's cancer diagnosis in July 2009 and was subsequently shelved. "It was going to be called Hot Sauce Committee Part One, the first of a two-part set."
**Hot Sauce Committee Part Two (2011) - Final Beastie Boys Album** : Released on April 27, 2011, over a year and a half after its originally planned predecessor, "Hot Sauce Committee Part One," was shelved. This ultimately became the last Beastie Boys album. Adam Yauch was too ill to tour or appear in videos for this album. "Make some noise from the last ever Beastie Boys album, Hot Sauce Committee Part Two."

## curiosities
**Early Career Perception - Novelty Act** : For the first eight years of their career, the Beastie Boys were not taken seriously, often viewed as spoiled, snotty frat boys making goofy songs and funny videos, despite the commercial success of "License to Ill." "No one took them seriously for the first eight years of their career."
**Record Label Disinterest in Paul's Boutique** : After the commercial failure of "Paul's Boutique," the president of their record label informed Adam Yauch that no more money could be spent promoting the album, citing the upcoming release of a new Donny Osmond record as a priority. "Adam Yaka remembers being told by the president of their label that they couldn't afford to sink any more money into promoting the album because, wow, they had a new, donny, osman record coming out."
**Move to Los Angeles and Studio Construction (G-Sun)** : The band moved to Los Angeles and used profits from "License to Ill" to build their own studio, G-Sun, in the Atwater Village area (3218 1/2 Glendale Boulevard). The name "G-Sun" originated from a plumbing store next door, Gilson's, which had lost the "I" and "L" from its sign, leading owners to place a hyphen between the "G" and "S-O-N." The studio also served as their business office, private skate park, and home basketball court, with rent less than $1,500 a month. "The Beastie Boys moved to LA. They set up their own label called Grand Royal. They took some big profits from license to ill and built a studio called G-Sun in the Atwater Village area north of downtown and east of Hollywood."
**Shift from Sampling to Live Instruments** : The band decided to reduce their reliance on sampling for "Check Your Head," partly due to artistic desire to explore a different approach after taking sampling "as far as they could," and partly due to economic reasons, as clearing samples was becoming prohibitively expensive. This led to Adam Horowitz on guitar, Adam Yauch on bass, and Mike D on drums, marking their most punk rock sound since the early 80s. "The thinking was, they'd taken the sampling thing about as far as they could, so it was time for a different approach. And their accountant also told them not to be so heavy with the sampling because the cost of clearing those samples would break them."
**"Check Your Head" Recording Conditions** : Much of "Check Your Head" was recorded at night at G-Sun studio because the drugstore on the ground floor complained about noise during business hours. The band intentionally used "crappy mics" to achieve a distinct, gritty vocal sound. "Most everything was done at night because the people at the drug store on the ground floor kept complaining about all the noise during business hours. They used deliberately crappy mics to get that greedy vocal sound."
**Lack of Record Label Supervision on Check Your Head** : During the recording of "Check Your Head," the band operated with almost no record company supervision because they had been "written off" by their label, which took little interest in the project. This allowed them complete creative freedom without figures like Rubin or The Dust Brothers guiding them. "They've been written off by the label, so they didn't take a lot of interest in the project."
**X-Large Clothing Company** : The Beastie Boys created their own clothing company, X-Large, specializing in skate and hip hop wear. This venture stemmed from their frustration with finding suitable cool clothes, leading them to design their own. "A clothing company called X-Large was created specializing in Skate and Hip Hop wear. The guys were tired of looking around for cool clothes, which is why they started designing their own."
**Adam Yauch's Activism - Tibet and Milarepa Fund** : Following a trip to Tibet, Adam Yauch became a Buddhist and a vocal activist for pro-Tibet, anti-China political causes. He founded the Milarepa Fund, a non-profit organization dedicated to raising awareness of Chinese oppression in Tibet. The fund received donations from Beastie Boys tours and profits from the Tibetan Freedom Festivals. "Meanwhile, Adam Yacht took a trip to Tibet, that changed his life. He became a Buddhist and a strong activist for pro-Tibet anti-China political causes. That was the start of the Milarepa Fund, a non-profit organization designed to promote awareness of Chinese oppression in Tibet."
**Broader Activism** : Beyond Tibet, the Beastie Boys were involved in several AIDS awareness groups and supported pro-choice causes. "The Beasties also became involved in several AIDS awareness groups, along with a pro-choice cause."
**Mike D's "Sabotage" Beard Challenge** : For the Spike Jonze-directed "Sabotage" video, band members were supposed to grow hair and beards. However, Mike D, being "folically challenged," struggled to grow facial hair, leading to the decision to use fake, glue-on beards for everyone. This initial risk was ultimately seen as contributing to the video's charm. "Mike D is um... folically challenged. He had a hard time growing any facial hair, and that's why everyone decided to go with the fake glue on stuff."
**"Sabotage" MTV Music Video Awards Protest** : At the 1994 MTV Music Video Awards, the "Sabotage" video lost to R.E.M.'s "Everybody Hurts." In protest, a "drunk freak" named Nathaniel Hornblower (later revealed to be Adam Yauch in disguise, officially his uncle) rushed the stage. "And you might remember how some drunk freak named Nathaniel Hornblower rushed the stage in a drunk and protest. Officially, that was Adam Yox's uncle, but if you look past the beard, he looks suspiciously like Adam himself."
**Quadrophonic Joystick Action Tour Donation** : During their successful Quadrophonic Joystick Action Tour, which included a sold-out show at Madison Square Garden in 30 minutes, one dollar from each ticket was donated to the Milarepa Fund. "Madison Square Garden sold out 30 minutes. And even more important was that one dollar from each ticket was donated to the Miller Rapa Fund."
**Remixing and Producing for Others** : Between 1995 and 1999, when not focused on activism or their own albums, the Beastie Boys members were actively engaged in remixing and producing records for other artists. "Meanwhile, the guys were busy doing remixes and producing records for other people."
**Early Internet Presence** : The Beastie Boys were pioneers in the digital realm, spending significant time on the nascent internet and becoming one of the first bands to establish a sophisticated official website. "The BC Boy spent a lot of time getting into this new thing called the Internet, and we're in fact one of the first bands to set up a proper sophisticated website."
**Mike D's Bike Accident** : Mike D suffered a bike accident that resulted in a separated shoulder, requiring surgery and subsequent rehabilitation. "Mike D had a bike accident, a separated shoulder that needed surgery and rehab."
**Adam Yauch's Film Endeavors** : Adam Yauch delved deeper into the film industry, establishing a studio named A Siloscope Laboratories in Manhattan. "Adam Yock started getting deeper into film, setting up a studio called A Siloscope Laboratories in Manhattan."
**Impact of 9/11** : The terrorist attacks of September 11, 2001, deeply affected the band members and significantly influenced the creation of their album "To the 5 Boroughs," which served as a tribute to New York City. "There was Coachella, and there was 9-11, which messed them up a lot."
**Adam Yauch's Cancer Diagnosis (2009)** : On July 20, 2009, Adam Yauch publicly announced his diagnosis of a treatable form of cancer in his parotid gland and a nearby lymph node. This led to the cancellation of upcoming shows and the postponement of their album "Hot Sauce Committee Part One" for surgery and radiation treatment, though at the time he expressed optimism for a full recovery. "Because recently, about well about two months ago, I started feeling this little lump in my throat."
**Rock and Roll Hall of Fame Induction (2012)** : The Beastie Boys were inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in April 2012. Adam Yauch, too ill to attend, had his letter of dedication read by Adam Horowitz and Mike Diamond from the stage. "When the Beastie Boys were inducted into the Rock and Wohala fame in April 2012, MCA was too sick to attend."
**Adam Yauch's Death (2012)** : Adam Yauch passed away at the age of 47 on May 4, 2012, at nine in the morning, at New York Presbyterian Hospital in Manhattan, where he had been admitted on April 14, 2012. The official cause of death was Salivary Gland Neoplasm, cancer of the salivary glands. His death marked the undisputed end of the Beastie Boys as a continuing group. "He went downhill very, very quickly. And at nine in the morning, on May 4th, 2012, he died at the age of 47."
**Adam Yauch's Will Stipulation - No Advertising** : Adam Yauch's will contained a handwritten stipulation that no Beastie Boys song should ever be used in advertising, a directive that his bandmates, Mike D and Adam Horowitz, fully support. "His will stipulates that no Beastie Boy song should ever be used in advertising. He wrote that bit in by hand right near the end."
**Adam Yauch Park** : A park in Brooklyn Heights, a few blocks from where Adam Yauch grew up, was named in his honor. It was the place where he learned to ride his bike as a child. "And if you go to Brooklyn Heights, you'll find Adam Yox Park, a few blocks from where he grew up."
**Adam Yauch's Post-Band Activism** : Beyond his music career, Adam Yauch remained dedicated to his fight for the people of Tibet and was a strong advocate for feminist causes and LGBT rights. "Adam Yox was committed to his fight for the people of Tibet. He was a big supporter of feminist causes and LGBT rights."
**Mike D's Post-Band Career** : Following the end of the Beastie Boys, Mike D diversified his career, becoming an interior designer and expressing a desire to work more as a record producer. He is married to Tamara Davis, a director of television, film, and music videos. "Now Mike D isn't a real estate. He's an interior designer as well. He also wants to work more as a record producer."
**Adam Horowitz's Post-Band Career** : After the Beastie Boys concluded, Adam Horowitz became a remixer under the moniker "41 Small Stars." He also plays bass with New York cabaret singer Bridget Everett and is open to more acting roles. He is married to Kathleen Hanna, a prominent figure from the Riot Grrrl band Bikini Kill. "And Adam Horowitz is a remixer working under the name 41 Small Stars."
**Early Practice Locations** : Adam Yauch's letter at the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame induction mentioned early band practices taking place at John Barry's loft on 100th Street and Broadway, where John's father would interrupt them, and also at Adam Yauch's home in Brooklyn during hot summer days, with windows open, disturbing the neighborhood. "To Keith Sheldonbach, to John Barry, to John Barry's loft on a hundredth street and Broadway, where John's dead would come busting in during our first practice as screaming when you turn that f*** off already."

# label - Capitol Records

## curiosities
**Copy Protection Controversy** : In Europe, Capitol Records, the Beastie Boys' label, utilized MacroVision CDS2000, a copy protection software on "To the 5 Boroughs" CDs. While it didn't install malware like the Sony rootkit, its resemblance to a virus caused controversy and problems for the band. "In Europe, capital records, the Beastie's label, used something called MacroVision CDS2000, which was a rather insidious form of copy protection software that mimicked a virus."

# label - Sony

## curiosities

This incident contributed to widespread public distrust of copy protection software, which then affected other labels, including Capitol Records and the Beastie Boys. "Sony was busted for this."

# concert - Tibetan Freedom Festival

## curiosities
**Adam Yauch's Vision** : The concept for the Tibetan Freedom Festival originated from Adam Yauch's desire for the Beastie Boys to do more to raise awareness about Chinese oppression in Tibet, envisioning a large-scale event featuring major musical acts. "Adam Yock was the guy who really started it. He felt that the Beasties could somehow do more to raise awareness about what was going on. The lineup featured the Beastie Boys, Smashing Pumpkins, Bjork, Sonic Youth, and the Chili Peppers, among others. "More than 100,000 people paid 30 bucks a pop to the first annual Tibetan Freedom Show at Golden Gate Park in San Francisco on July 15, 1996."
**Subsequent Festivals (1997, 1998)** : Following the success of the first event, a two-day festival was held in New York in 1997, which was also a big success. Another two-day event took place in Washington, D.C., in 1998, continuing the mission of raising awareness for Tibet. "A year later, a two-day event was held in New York, another big success. And after that in 1998, there was another two-day event in Washington, D.C."

# curiosity

**Difficulty of Transitioning from Novelty Act to Serious Artist** : It is extremely challenging for artists pigeonholed as a novelty act to transition into a respected artist with gravitas and serious artistic merit, a feat that the Beastie Boys managed to achieve over their career. "it's even more difficult to move from being pigeonholed as a novelty act to one that carries gravitas and serious artistic merit."
**Beastie Boys' Evolution and Influence** : The Beastie Boys transformed from a perceived novelty act to respected artists, growing as musicians, businessmen, and humans. They took risks, experimented, and branched out, ultimately cementing their reputation as one of the most important and influential bands across multiple genres for at least two generations by the time of Adam Yauch's death in 2012. "And by the time it all came to an end with the death of Adam Yaka, the spring of 2012, the Beastie Boys had cemented a reputation as one of the most important bands of not one, but at least two generations."
**Milarepa Fund** : A non-profit organization co-founded by Adam Yauch, dedicated to promoting awareness of Chinese oppression in Tibet and supporting pro-Tibet causes. It received funding through donations from Beastie Boys' tours and the Tibetan Freedom Festivals. "That was the start of the Milarepa Fund, a non-profit organization designed to promote awareness of Chinese oppression in Tibet."
**Internet and Early Band Websites** : The advent of the internet in the mid-1990s captured the Beastie Boys' attention, and they were among the very first bands to establish a sophisticated and proper website, demonstrating their early adoption of new technologies. "The BC Boy spent a lot of time getting into this new thing called the Internet, and we're in fact one of the first bands to set up a proper sophisticated website."
**Music Industry's Copy Protection Crisis (2004)** : In 2004, the music industry faced rampant illegal file sharing and responded by implementing various copy protection methods, some of which, like Sony's rootkit, installed malware on users' computers. This era of insidious copy protection software, such as MacroVision CDS2000 used by Capitol Records, caused significant problems and accusations for artists like the Beastie Boys, even if their specific software didn't install malware. "Illegal file sharing was rampant, and the industry was looking to do something, anything, to keep people from stealing music."
