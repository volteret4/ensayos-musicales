## albums
**12 Bar Blues (1998) – Solo Debut** : Released in May 1998, with Scott playing all instruments himself. Described as an "artistic time away from STP." While not a hit, it received kind reviews. At the time of release, Weiland had been clean and sober for six months and spoke candidly about spending over $6 million on drugs between 1994 and early 1998.

## curiosities
**Early Heroin Addiction Revelation (1993)** : During Stone Temple Pilots' major label tour following their debut album *Core*, it was discovered that Scott Weiland had become a heroin addict while the band was selling millions of records.
**Attempted Detox in Los Angeles (Spring 1994)** : Upon returning to L.A., Weiland intended to check into the Exodus recovery center in Marina del Rey for his first detox attempt but was advised against it due to the presence of other "junkies," including Gibby Haynes and Kurt Cobain.
**Marriage to Janina Costenda (September 17, 1994)** : Married his longtime girlfriend, Janina Costenda, in a lavish ceremony in a rich area of Pasadena. Weiland reportedly used cocaine with groomsmen in the limo before the ceremony.
**On Tour Lies to Wife (Late 1994)** : While hooked on heroin during the *Purple* tour, Weiland made numerous phone calls to Janina, falsely claiming he was not using drugs, despite feeling guilty about the deception.
**Drug Arrest in Pasadena (May 15, 1995)** : Arrested at approximately 3:30 AM after dropping away from the back of a motel with his lights off. Cops found a chunk of Mexican black tar heroin in his wallet. He spent the night in jail before his wife posted $10,000 bail.
**Post-Arrest Drug Seeking Behavior (May 1995)** : On the way home from jail in the couple's 1965 Mustang, Weiland demanded his wife drive to his dealer's house. When she refused, he jumped out of the moving car at 15-20 mph.
**Solace with Courtney Love (May 1995)** : After his wife refused to let him come home following his arrest, Weiland sought refuge with Courtney Love, who was staying at the Chateau Marmont Hotel.
**Public Statement of Remorse (May 1995)** : Courtney Love called a local radio station in the middle of the night and read a tearful statement of remorse from Weiland live on air, acknowledging his disease and desire to get well and make more music.
**Rehab Stint and Drug Charges Dropped (Summer 1995 - October 1996)** : Agreed to complete a drug treatment program in Colorado, after which his May 1995 drug bust charges were dropped on October 29, 1996, with the judge satisfied he had completed the court-prescribed treatment.
**Missed Counseling and Hospitalization (April 23, 1996)** : Missed a mandatory drug counseling session, leading police to attempt arrest. His friends intervened, sending him to the hospital, from which he was transferred to the medical unit of the L.A. County jail.
**Escape from Rehab and Arrest Warrant (June 26, 1996)** : Disappeared from Impact House, a residential drug treatment facility where he was meant to stay for six months, leading to an arrest warrant. He later reappeared safe and sober at Pasadena Municipal Court, explaining he left to talk to his wife.
**13 Rehab Stints in Three Years (1994-1997)** : Between 1994 and 1997, Weiland entered rehab 13 times. On December 30, 1996, he checked into rehab again voluntarily, marking his 13th time in three years.
**Proposed Restitution for Canceled Tour (1996)** : After the cancellation of the *Tiny Music* tour, it was suggested that Weiland pay millions in restitution for the lost dates.
**Divorce from Janina Costenda (Late 1996)** : His marriage to Janina ended around late 1996 because "she just couldn't take it anymore." The divorce was still not finalized by the end of 1999.
**Guarded Touring Conditions (November 1996)** : For a six-week road trip starting November 4, 1996, Weiland was required to be accompanied by a guardian, meet a nightly curfew, and avoid crossing international borders due to the hassle of customs.
**New Year's and Hawaii Show Cancellations (Late 1996 - Early 1997)** : A New Year's show in Anchorage, Alaska, and two shows in Hawaii in early January 1997 were canceled without explanation after a successful six-week tour, impacting over 30,000 ticket holders.
**Relapse Confession (December 29, 1996)** : Called Dean DeLeo to confess he had "fallen off the wagon. Hard," leading to his voluntary check into rehab the next day.
**$6 Million Drug Spending (1994-1998)** : Candidly admitted to spending over $6 million on drugs between 1994 and early 1998.
**Narcotics Anonymous Attendance (1998)** : His new management company ensured he attended Narcotics Anonymous meetings.
**Second Drug Arrest in New York City (June 1, 1998)** : Two weeks after his solo album release, and the day before a New York City gig, Weiland was arrested at 3 PM coming out of a building known to house drug dealers on Avenue D in Manhattan, found with ten decks of heroin. The arresting officer, a huge STP fan, was heartbroken.
**Disappearance After NY Arrest (June 1998)** : Disappeared after being released on bail, missing his July 31st court date in New York.
**Missing LA Court Appearances and Arrest Warrant (June-July 1998)** : Missed a June 11th court hearing (claiming he was in rehab) and a subsequent appearance in early July. Judge Larry Fiddler issued a $250,000 arrest warrant on July 9, 1998, after his attorney stated, "Nobody knows" where he was.
**On the Run (July 9-22, 1998)** : Was on the run for almost 13 days after hearing about the arrest warrant while standing outside the Los Angeles County Courthouse, opting to vanish rather than be arrested.
**Arrested While Buying Drugs (July 22, 1998)** : Arrested at 6th and Burlington Street near downtown Los Angeles for buying drugs, not specifically due to the warrant. Drugs were found on him. He was released the next day on $250,000 bail.
**Coast-to-Coast Legal Battles (July 1998)** : Faced drug felony charges in both California and New York, requiring his lawyers to coordinate court dates across the country.
**NY Court Date Delays (July 1998 - February 1999)** : His NY court date was repeatedly delayed (July 31, 1998; November 4, 1998; December 10, 1998; January 22, 1999; February 16, 1999) due to his being in rehab in California and continuances.
**Probation Violation and Jail in LA (January 15, 1999)** : Picked up in Los Angeles for a probation violation after failing to provide a urine sample to his parole officer. Spent Friday night to Wednesday morning in jail and was sent back to rehab after a scolding from Judge Fiddler.
**Plea Deal in New York (February 16, 1999)** : Plead guilty to his drug charge in New York in exchange for no jail time, conditioned on staying out of trouble for one full year. Any failure would result in a 30-day sentence at Riker's Island.
**Relationship with Mary Forsberg (February 1999)** : Accompanied to his New York court appearance by model Mary Forsberg, his new girlfriend.
**Dropped from Impact House (April 16, 1999)** : Sentenced to another rehab facility after Impact House informed Judge Fiddler that Weiland had been dropped from treatment for failing to comply with the program (either missed urinalysis or failed a drug test).
**Las Vegas Show and Jail Sentence (August 12-13, 1999)** : Performed what the band considered one of their greatest gigs in Las Vegas. The next day, he voluntarily turned himself in for leaving the state, facing an impending jail sentence.
**One-Year Jail Sentence (September 3, 1999)** : Sentenced to one year in LA County jail for a probation violation stemming from August 1998. The verdict reportedly caused "sobs" in the courtroom.
**Jail Routine (Late 1999)** : Spent a month in a dormitory in jail, waking at 5:30 AM, attending meetings and drug counseling at 7 AM and 7 PM, with free time after supper and lights out at 10 PM. Music listening was not allowed.
**Early Release from Jail (December 31, 1999)** : Rumblings in January indicated he was doing well and would be released early; he was.
**Move to Halfway House (January 13, 2000)** : Moved from jail to a halfway house.
**Snowboarding with Mary (February 2000)** : Allowed to go snowboarding with his girlfriend Mary.
**Plans for Marriage (May 2000)** : Despite his divorce from Janina not yet being finalized, plans were in place for Scott and Mary to get married in May.
**Death in Tour Bus (Unknown Year)** : Died in the back of a tour bus, following years of drug, alcohol, and mental health issues.

## albums
**Core (1993) – Debut Record** : The band's debut record. Its release was followed by a tour during which lead singer Scott Weiland became a heroin addict while the band was selling millions of records. Sold about four times as much as *Tiny Music Songs from the Vatican Gift Shop*.
**Purple (1994) – Second Album** : Recorded in Atlanta during a break in the band's touring schedule, completed quickly in four weeks partly due to Scott Weiland's access to a local drug dealer. Released on June 7, 1994, it was noted for being less grunge and more "straight ahead alt rock" compared to *Core*. It debuted at #1 on US charts, #1 in Australia, #2 in Canada, and #10 in the UK. Sold about three times as much as *Tiny Music Songs from the Vatican Gift Shop*.
**Tiny Music Songs from the Vatican Gift Shop (1996) – Third Album** : Recorded with basics on the West Coast (at actor Jimmy Stewart's mansion in Santa Barbara) and finished in Atlanta with producer Brendan O'Brien. Scott Weiland consciously attempted to channel David Bowie in melodies and lyrics. Released on March 26, 1996, it was a big radio hit but sold less in stores, about two million copies, a third of *Purple*'s sales and a quarter of *Core*'s.
**Number 4 (1999) – Fourth Album** : The band's fourth album, with Brendan O'Brien as producer, on which they started working in February 1999 after Scott Weiland's return. Released on October 26, 1999.

## songs
**Plush (1993)** : From the band's debut album *Core*. Reportedly a song about death, inspired by a newspaper story of a girl found dead in San Diego. It won a Grammy for Best Hard Rock Performance and remains a staple on radio station playlists worldwide.
**Big Empty (1994)** : A hit song that preceded the *Purple* album, originally given to the producers of *The Crow* movie soundtrack (released March 1994) and later included on *Purple*. Its style was consistent with songs from *Core*.
**Down (1999)** : The very first song the band tackled when re-entering the studio with producer Brendan O'Brien in February 1999 for their fourth album, *Number 4*.
**Sour Girl (1999)** : A single from the fourth album, *Number 4*, written about Scott Weiland's ex-wife, Janina (though their divorce was not finalized at the time).

## curiosities
**Heroin Addiction During Early Success (1993)** : While selling millions of records and touring following their debut album *Core*, the band discovered their lead singer, Scott Weiland, had become a heroin addict.
**Cancelled Tiny Music Tour (1996-1997)** : The band canceled a significant portion of their planned tour to support *Tiny Music Songs from the Vatican Gift Shop* through late 1996 and into 1997 due to Scott Weiland's drug dependency, stating "Our singer is a junkie, and we can't go on the road with him until he's well."
**Official Statement on Scott Weiland's Drug Dependency (1996)** : The band issued a rare public statement explicitly stating that Scott Weiland was unable to perform or rehearse due to drug dependency, not typical issues like exhaustion.
**Scott Weiland's Temporary Departure (1996)** : Weiland was effectively "out" of the band, especially after the suggestion that he pay millions in restitution for the canceled tour dates.
**Reunion Press Conference (October 1, 1996)** : In the same place they had previously announced Weiland's departure, the band declared they were "fit and ready to rock," with Scott Weiland expressing his renewed commitment and feeling like a "young kid."
**Six-Week Comeback Tour (November 4 - December 14, 1996)** : Embarked on a six-week road trip starting in Los Angeles, during which Scott Weiland was accompanied by a guardian, had a nightly curfew, and no international borders were crossed to simplify customs. The tour was successful, with Weiland performing well.
**Unexpected Tour Cancellations (Late 1996 - Early 1997)** : Following a successful six-week tour, a New Year's show in Anchorage, Alaska, and two January shows in Hawaii were canceled without explanation, impacting over 30,000 fans.
**Manager's Statement on Scott Weiland's Relapse (January 6, 1997)** : Manager Steve Stewart issued a statement confirming Scott Weiland was "having problems again" and receiving care, but stressed the band had not broken up and no one had been fired.
**Album Chart Rebound (1997)** : Due to the successful six-week tour, *Tiny Music Songs from the Vatican Gift Shop* jumped 78 spots on the album charts in one month.
**Rumors of Permanent Split (May 25, 1997)** : After a show in Toronto, rumors circulated that the band was "finished for good" due to Scott Weiland's ongoing issues.
**Other Members Form Talk Show (1998)** : While Scott Weiland was working on his solo album, the other three members (Robert DeLeo, Dean DeLeo, Eric Kretz) formed a side project called Talk Show with singer David Coots, releasing an album that did not perform well.
**Reunion for Fourth Album (February 1999)** : Dean DeLeo initiated a call to Scott Weiland, leading to their reunion and entry into the studio with producer Brendan O'Brien to work on their fourth album, *Number 4*.
**Surprise Gig at Vyper Room (March 16, 1999)** : Performed a surprise gig in Los Angeles, signaling their return to live performances.
**Rumored Lollapalooza Headliners (March 1999)** : There were rumors that the band would headline Lollapalooza if the festival were to be reactivated.
**New York City Gig Cancellation (June 11, 1999)** : A scheduled gig in New York City was canceled due to Scott Weiland's probation issues.
**Las Vegas Show as Scott Weiland's "Last Night of Freedom" (August 12, 1999)** : The band performed a show in Las Vegas, which Scott Weiland referred to as his "last night of freedom" before he had to turn himself in the next day due to court conditions for leaving the state. The band described his performance as "insanely great."
**VH1 Special Taping (March 8, 2000)** : All four members traveled to New York to tape a special for the VH1 video channel.
**Plans for Comeback Activities (March 2000)** : Plans were in place for more showcase gigs, a tour, and even a pay-per-view event, indicating a strong positive outlook for the band's future.
