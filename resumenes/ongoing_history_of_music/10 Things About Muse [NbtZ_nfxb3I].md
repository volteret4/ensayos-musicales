## albums
**Showbiz (1999) – First Album Featuring Material from 1997 Demo** : The band's debut album included a more polished version of "Do We Need This?", which first appeared on a 1997 demo tape. "Do We Need This? would later show up in a more polished form on the band's 1999 album Showbiz."

**Absolution (2003) – Album That First Registered in North America** : Muse's third studio album produced "Time Is Running Out," described as the first Muse song to register in any meaningful way in North America. "The first song that registered in any meaningful way in North America. It's a very important record."

**Black Holes and Revelations (2006) – Astronomy, Cosmology, and Conspiracy Themes; Knight of Cydonia** : The album's title track and lead single "Knights of Cydonia" takes its name from a region on Mars (Cydonia) where some people believe they can see an ancient humanoid face from orbit — reflecting Bellamy's interest in astronomy and fringe science. "The title was taken from a region on Mars, that's known as Cydonia."

**The Resistance (2009) – Recorded Almost Entirely at Matt's Lake Como Villa** : The album, including "Uprising," was recorded largely at what Muse called Studio Bellini — Matt Bellamy's villa in the village of Moltrasio on Lake Como, Italy, once owned by Italian composer Vincenzo Bellini. George Clooney, Sting, and Richard Branson also have properties in the area. Winston Churchill once lived there and used the villa for painting. The location was also used in *Star Wars: Attack of the Clones* as the planet Naboo. Bellamy later put the two Lake Como apartments on the market for €2.5 million after splitting with his Italian fiancée. "Recorded almost entirely in Matt's villa on Lake Como in Italy."

**The Second Law (2012) – Themed After the Second Law of Thermodynamics** : The album takes the Second Law of Thermodynamics — involving the increasing entropy of the universe — as its conceptual framework. "The band's 2012 album is themed after the Second Law of Thermodynamics, which involves the increasing of entropy in the universe."

**Drones (2015) – Recorded Largely in Vancouver; Themes of Unthinking Obedience** : Recorded largely in Vancouver, the album deals not only with unmanned aerial vehicles but with people who act without thinking or do the bidding of others without questioning. Themes include deep ecology and the empathy gap. "Both origin of symmetry from 2001 and black holes and revelations from 2006 were about astronomy and cosmology and quantum physics."

## songs
**Neutron Star Collision (Twilight Soundtrack)** : One of several Muse songs that appeared on *Twilight* movie soundtracks, written as a direct concession to author Stephenie Meyer's fandom. Meyer acknowledged Muse repeatedly in her novels — in *New Moon* she thanked "the talented musicians who inspire me, particularly the band Muse"; in *Eclipse* called them "rock gods"; in *Breaking Dawn* called them "the very aptly named Muse." "I don't think Muse supports either Team Edward or Team Jacob."

## curiosities
**George Bellamy (Matt's Father) – Played on "Telstar" (1962), Second British Song to Hit #1 in America** : In 1961, Matt Bellamy's father George Bellamy, then a 20-year-old country singer, answered an ad in *Melody Maker* placed by legendary UK producer Joe Meek, who was assembling a studio group called the Tornados. George played rhythm guitar on "Telstar," an instrumental single released summer 1962, capitalizing on the excitement around the launch of the Telstar communications satellite. It became just the second British song to reach number one on the American charts and the second instrumental to top charts on both sides of the Atlantic, selling over 5 million copies worldwide. Joe Meek subsequently "went extra crazy" and the Tornados disintegrated; George ended up working as a plumber. "Tell Star was a massive hit, becoming just the second British song to reach number one on the American charts."

**Formation – Won Battle of the Bands as Rocket Baby Dolls (1994, Teignmouth)** : In 1994, at the Broadmeadow Sports Centre in Teignmouth, Devon (population ~15,000), 16-year-olds Matt Bellamy, Christopher Wolstenholme, and Dominic Howard competed in a battle of the bands as "Rocket Baby Dolls" (evolved from "Fixed Penalty," itself evolved from "Gothic Plague"). They entered halfheartedly, thinking the concept idiotic, but the violence of their performance drew such a strong crowd response that they won — surprising themselves enough to take music seriously. About a week later they renamed themselves Muse. "They played with such violence and because the crowd responded so well to this violence, they ended up winning."

**Name Origin – Occult Witchcraft Sessions with Three Girls** : The name "Muse" may have come from a teacher introducing Bellamy to the word, but the preferred explanation involves three girls from school who practiced witchcraft in abandoned houses, casting spells. Matt — who was attracted to one of them — tagged along and played guitar during the sessions. "He thought one of them was kind of hot, so he went with her and the other two friends to abandoned houses where they cast spells."

**First Demo Tape (May 1995) – Cassette Worth ~$1,000 Today** : In May 1995, the band recorded four songs possibly under the guidance of Matt's father George ("Copyright Geo Bell Music" appears on the cassette label). The tape, titled *This Is a Muse Demo*, was distributed to promoters and venues across the northwest of England by their first manager Phil Corthals, securing more gigs and their first overseas show (in France). Very few copies were made; the cassette now sells for close to $1,000. "If you can find one, the asking price is close to $1,000."

**Matt Bellamy's Car Theft Ring – Stole a Ford Escort from the Wrong Guy** : At 18, having moved from Teignmouth to Exeter to live above a porn store with a painter/drug dealer friend, Bellamy got involved in a small-time car theft ring, stealing cheap vehicles for modest profit. The scheme fell apart when he stole a Ford Escort belonging to someone dangerous, who showed up demanding £500 or he would harm Bellamy and his family. Lacking the money, Bellamy handed over the band's touring van. "So much for a career in Grand Theft Auto."

**Cryptic Fan Codes and Hidden Messages – Bicycles Hidden Across America (2005 Tour)** : On a 2005 tour, Muse printed coded set lists each night using fake song titles that were actually anagrams and cipher clues. Solving these led to email addresses, then to four autographed bicycles — the band's tour fitness bikes — hidden under abandoned railway bridges across America, with combination locks whose codes were embedded in the puzzles. One clue required fans to write "Christ" on their body, photograph it, and email the image to unlock the next clue; only four people worldwide solved that one. A harder puzzle directed the solver to a specific recording studio in England to claim a signed Muse guitar. For the launch of "United States of Eurasia," USB sticks were hidden in Berlin, Tokyo, Hong Kong, Moscow, Paris, and Dubai, each containing a code to enter on a website; once all codes were entered, parts of the new song were revealed. "Only four people worldwide succeeded in breaking that one."

**Conspiracy Theories and Scientific Obsessions – Bilderbergs, 9/11 Truthers, Alien Reptile Royals** : Bellamy at one time believed 9/11 was an inside job (he later recanted), is concerned about the Bilderberg Group and Trilateral Commission, and based "Unnatural Selection" on the Babylonian Brotherhood theory — the idea that the British Royal Family are descendants of giant alien lizards, as proposed by British writer David Icke. Right-wing conspiracy theorists began using Muse music in their internet videos, which greatly annoyed Bellamy — not because of the conspiracy usage but because he felt the right wing had "hijacked all the best conspiracies." A Muse fan-compiled reading list includes both fringe (Zecharia Sitchin's ancient alien theories) and legitimate scientific works (Brian Greene's *The Elegant Universe*, Orwell's *1984*). "He felt that the right wing had hijacked all the best conspiracies."

**World Record Guitar Destruction – 140 Guitars Smashed on 2010 Resistance Tour** : Matt Bellamy holds the world record for the greatest number of guitars smashed on a single tour, destroying 140 during the 2010 leg of The Resistance Tour. "Matt Bellamy holds a world record for the greatest number of guitars smashed on a single tour."
