## curiosities
**Josh Saviano Myth** : A common myth claims that Marilyn Manson is actually a grown-up version of child actor Josh Saviano, who played Paul Fyfer on the TV show "The Wonder Years." This is factually incorrect; Saviano is now a lawyer. "The most common Manson myth that he's actually a grown-up version of child actor Josh Saviano, who played Paul Fyfer on the Wonder Years. That is totally incorrect, not true."
**Mr. Belvedere Child Actor Myth** : Another variation of the child actor myth falsely claims that Manson played a character in "Mr. Belvedere," a role actually played by Danica McKellar, who is now a respected mathematician. "And no, he did not play when he cooped with Mr. Belvedere either. That was Danica Mckeller, who is now a well-respected mathematician."
**Satanism Allegations** : While Manson has been associated with Satanism, he does not consider himself a spokesperson for it or for any organized religion, viewing his ordination as a minister in the Church of Satan by Anton LaVey as purely honorary with no practical power or meaning. He describes his beliefs as a personal interpretation incorporating diverse influences. "I'm not and never have been a spokesperson for Satanism. It's simply part of what I believe in along with Dr. Seuss, Dr. Hook, Nietzsche, and the Bible. I just have my own interpretation."
**Ribs Removal Myth** : A persistent myth claims that Marilyn Manson had ribs removed so he could orally pleasure himself, a claim that is untrue despite his known interest in body modification. "Did Manson have some ribs removed so he could orally pleasure himself? Well, no. Well it is true. He does have an interest in body modification. He's never gone that far."
**Body Modification Myths** : Several myths circulate about Marilyn Manson's body, including claims that he tattooed his private parts black, had breast implants, pulled audience members on stage for sex, or distributed puppies to the audience to be torn apart on cue. All of these claims are false. "Manson also did not tattoo his private bits black. He did not have breast implants, pull someone from the audience to have sex with on stage, or distribute puppies to the audience to be torn apart on cue."

# artist - The White Stripes

## members

Meg White

## curiosities
**Brother and Sister Myth** : A persistent myth claimed that Jack and Meg White were brother and sister, which is entirely false. Jack Gillis (his birth name) was the tenth of ten children from a Catholic family in Detroit, with no sister named Meg, while Meg White was from Gross Point Farms with one sister, Heather, and no brothers. This false narrative was deliberately cultivated by Jack White in early interviews, where he referred to Meg as his "little sister," even after their marriage certificate was posted online in 2001. "When you see a band that's two pieces, husband and wife, boyfriend and girlfriend, you think, oh, I see. When their brother and sister, you go, oh, that's interesting. You care more about the music, not the relationship, whether they're trying to save their relationship by being in a band together."
**Marriage and Divorce** : Jack and Meg White were married on September 21, 1996, with Jack Gillis adopting Meg's last name to become Jack White. They divorced in March 2000 but continued to be bandmates and friends, even attending and serving in each other's subsequent wedding parties. "The truth is that Jack and Meg were married on September 21, 1996, but then divorced in March of 2000."
