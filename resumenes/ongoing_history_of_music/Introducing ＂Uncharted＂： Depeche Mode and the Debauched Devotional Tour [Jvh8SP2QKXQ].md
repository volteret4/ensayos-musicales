## albums
**Music for the Masses (1987) - Sixth Record** : This album marked Depeche Mode's rise to superstardom, leading to global hits and cementing their breakthrough into stadium-filling acts. "Their rise to superstardom began in 1987 when they released the Music for the Mass' Album, their sixth record."
**Violator (1989) - Seventh Album** : Released on March 19, 1989, this album was an immediate hit, coinciding with a shift in music culture as Gen X began to assert itself. It led to an infamous autograph session in Los Angeles that turned into a riot. "The 7th album was entitled Violator, and it was a hit from the moment it was released on March 19, 1989."
**Songs of Faith and Devotion (1993) - Eighth Album** : This album was recorded in Madrid starting in February 1992 after an 18-month break following the World Violation Tour. The recording process was difficult, marked by internal band tensions, Dave Gahan's drug use, and Andy Fletcher's severe depression, with no pre-production and reliance on Martin Gore's demos and jam sessions. "Making the songs of Faith and Devotion album was difficult."
**Ultra (1997)** : Demos for this album were sent to Dave Gahan by Martin Gore while Dave was still struggling with severe drug addiction. The song "Sister of Night" from this album was notably recorded while Dave was high on heroin, and he could not manage to get through the full song in one take. "The story is that the song's sister of night, one of the tracks from the 1997 ultra album, was recorded while Dave was high on heroin."

## curiosities
**Early Career and Rise to Stardom (1977-1988)** : Depeche Mode formed between 1977 and 1980, developing a synth-based approach that was trendy at the time. For about six years, they maintained a mid-level status with a cult following until their sixth album, "Music for the Masses," released in 1987, propelled them to superstardom with global hit singles. "Depeche Mode came together between 1977 and 1980."
**Music for the Masses Tour Culmination (June 6, 1988)** : The tour promoting the "Music for the Masses" album concluded at the Rose Bowl in Pasadena, where Depeche Mode demonstrated their breakthrough by filling the stadium with 60,000 people. This landmark performance was documented by both a live album and a concert film. "By the time the Music for the Mass is to erode into the Rose Bowl in Pasadena on June 6, 1988, the band was capable of filling a stadium with 60,000 people."
**Violator Album Signing Riot (March 20, 1989)** : The day after "Violator" was released, 17,000 fans (more than 5,000 initially) turned up for an autograph session at a warehouse record store on Lossiannigabullavard in Los Angeles. The event quickly devolved into a riot due to inadequate security, leading to seven police units clearing the area after an hour, bricks and bottles flying, two neon signs destroyed, and 150 LAPD officers in riot gear being called in. "The band was so crazy that the next day, 17,000 people turned up at an autograph session at a Los Angeles record store and a riot broke out."
**Post-Riot Apology and US Tour (March 1989)** : Following the riot at their Los Angeles autograph session, Depeche Mode issued an apology, stating they left to prevent serious injuries to their fans. The band confirmed they would return for a US tour that summer, starting at the end of May and ending in August on the west coast. "Peche Mode issued an apology in the wake of all that, saying they'd chosen to leave after only an hour at the scene in order to ensure that none of their fans got seriously injured."
**World Violation Tour (May 28, 1990)** : Depeche Mode commenced their global tour for the "Violator" album in Pensacola, Florida. The tour comprised 88 sold-out gigs worldwide, solidifying their status as one of the biggest bands globally by its conclusion. "A few months later, on May 28th, 1990, Depeche Mode began their world violation tour in Pensacola, Florida."
**Post-World Violation Tour Hiatus and Fan Intrusion (1990-1992)** : After the demanding World Violation Tour, Depeche Mode took an 18-month break. During this period, Dave Gahan faced extreme fan intrusion, having to move twice because fans frequently appeared on his lawn, with one even hiring a private detective to find his home address. "Once that tour wrapped up, Depeche Mode took a little time off and they needed it."
**Devotional Tour - Most Debauched Rock Tour Ever (1993-1994)** : The tour promoting "Songs of Faith and Devotion" lasted eight months, featuring extreme alcohol and drug use that led to members suffering heart attacks, seizures, severe mental illness, and an overdose that left one member clinically dead for two minutes. It employed 100 people, including a full-time psychiatrist, and a dedicated drug sourcer. "It has gone down in history, as the most debauched rock tour ever."
**Devotional Tour Logistics (1993)** : The initial plan for the Devotional Tour was ambitious, encompassing 158 dates across 27 countries, covering approximately 100,000 miles. It required almost 100 tons of equipment and featured a stage designed by photographer and artist Anton Corbijn, who was undertaking such a design for the first time. "The initial plan was for 158 dates through 27 countries covering about 100,000 miles and requiring almost 100 tons of equipment with a stage designed by photographer and artist Anton Corbin, something that he'd never done before."
**Devotional Tour European Leg (May 19 – July 31, 1993)** : The Devotional Tour kicked off on May 19, 1993, in Lille, France, marking the beginning of an 11-week European leg that concluded on July 31 in London. Throughout this period, relations among the four band members remained highly strained, with significant concern focused on singer Dave Gahan. "Depeche Mode's devotional tour began on May 19, 1993 in Lil France, the start of an 11-week European tour that ended in London on July 31."
**Band Isolation During Devotional Tour (1993)** : During the North American leg of the Devotional Tour, Dave Gahan's increasingly insufferable ego and "rock god" persona led the other band members to insist on staying on separate floors in hotels to manage the extreme tension. "He became so unsufferable that when it came to staying in hotels, the other members of the group insisted on being on separate floors from Dave."
**Banned from Berlin's Intercontinental Hotel (June 1993)** : During the Devotional Tour's stop in Berlin in June 1993, Depeche Mode was banned from the Intercontinental Hotel, their usual accommodation, due to Dave Gahan's behavior, which included spending days injecting heroin in his room. "In June 1993, when the tour rolled into Berlin, the Pesh Mode was banned from the intercontinental hotel, which was their usual place."
**Devotional Tour Vampire Phase (1993)** : Dave Gahan reportedly entered a "vampire phase" during the Devotional Tour, keeping his dressing room dark and filled with candles. As a joke, a coffin was delivered to the tour, which Dave Gahan used to take naps before each show. "There was a rumor that Dave went through a vampire phase, his dressing room was dark and filled with candles, and as a joke, someone delivered a coffin to the tour."
**Primal Scream's Shocking Experience (1993)** : Dave Gahan reportedly insisted that Primal Scream open for Depeche Mode on the Devotional Tour because he knew they enjoyed partying. However, Primal Scream was so appalled by the level of drug use and excess they witnessed that they allegedly gave up drugs entirely, despite their prior extensive use. "The story goes that primal scream was so shocked by what they saw that they gave up drugs entirely."
**Near-Death Plane Incident (Nov 1993)** : During a break in the Devotional Tour, a private jet chartered for the band and entourage to travel from Dallas to the Caribbean experienced a pressurization problem. The plane struggled to return to Texas, an incident that deeply unnerved everyone on board. "There was a pressurization problem in the plane struggle to get back to Texas, which freaked everybody out."
**Kurt Cobain's Death Impact (April 8, 1994)** : While in Buenos Aires on April 8, 1994, Depeche Mode learned of Kurt Cobain's death. Dave Gahan was reportedly taken aback by the news for a day or two before reverting to his usual drug-fueled habits. "De Peshmoan was in Buenos Aires on April 8, 1994 when they heard about the death of Kurt Cobain."
**Post-Devotional Tour Dispersal (1994)** : After the Devotional Tour concluded, the band members scattered, disinclined to see each other due to the severe strains and personal struggles experienced during the tour. "After the tour, everyone scattered. No one wanted to see each other."
**Long-Term Recovery and Current Status (1997-Present)** : Depeche Mode eventually overcame the tumultuous period between 1991 and 1997. Dave Gahan and Martin Gore are now sober, and Alan Wilder is living a quiet life. While Andy Fletcher died in 2022, Martin Gore and Dave Gahan continue to record and tour together, showing intent to move forward almost 50 years after the band's formation. "The good news is that the Pesh mode eventually came out on the other side of all things that happened between 1991 and 1997."

# artist - Dave Gahan

## member of
Depeche Mode

## curiosities
**Early Life and Family (1962-1991)** : Dave Gahan's biological father, Jack, left the family when Dave was only six months old and died in 1991, just as Dave was attempting to reconnect with him. He was married to Joanne Fox, who helped establish the first official Depeche Mode fan club, and they had a son named Jack, but Dave rarely saw his son during the first five years of his life due to touring. "Dave also lost his biological father, Jack, who left the family when Dave was 6 months old."
**Post-World Violation Tour Mental State (1990-1992)** : Following the World Violation Tour, Dave Gahan became notably angry and irritable, compounded by undiagnosed ADD and autistic traits. He was forced to move twice due to relentless fan intrusion at his home. "Dave Gahn was especially angry and irritable."
**Move to Los Angeles and Drug Initiation (Early 1990s)** : After a personal crisis and his first divorce from Joanne Fox, Dave Gahan moved to Los Angeles, where he fully embraced the "Rock God" fantasy. He began using heroin around 1991 and quickly escalated his drug use after meeting Teresa Conroy, who introduced him to the drug-heavy Jane's Addiction crowd. "Right around the time he first started using heroin."
**Relationship and Marriage to Teresa Conroy (Early 1990s)** : Dave Gahan became involved with Teresa Conroy, Depeche Mode's former press officer, who introduced him to the Jane's Addiction social circle where drugs were readily available. They were described as "rock and roll soulmates," indulging in the rockstar lifestyle and having a baby daughter together. They married at the Graceland Chapel in Las Vegas by an Elvis impersonator in April 1992. "He also hooked up with Teresa Conroy, the Pesh Mode's former press officer."
**Escalating Drug Use and Physical Decline (Early 1990s)** : Dave Gahan's drug intake, primarily cocaine and heroin, escalated to intravenous use, causing him to stop eating and become dangerously thin. He grew out his hair and acquired numerous tattoos, often while so high he couldn't remember getting them, including one that read "T.C. TTM-FG" for Teresa Conroy. "His body had become so hardened to the drug that it had no effect, but he had to keep using just to keep from getting dope sick."
**Heroin Addiction and Distinctive Tattoos (Early 1990s)** : Dave Gahan and Teresa Conroy became recognizable heroin junkies, with one of their favorite wedding presents being a lump of black tar heroin. Dave's body bore multiple tattoos, including one on his shoulder blades that took a single 10-hour sitting, often acquired while he was heavily intoxicated. "There's one that reads T.C. TTM-FG, and stands for Teresa Conroy to the Mother Effor Dave God."
**Devotional Tour Behavior and Arrest (1993)** : During the Devotional Tour, Dave Gahan's ego spiraled, prompting other band members to insist on separate hotel floors. He engaged in heavy heroin use, was rumored to have a "vampire phase" involving coffin naps, and was arrested on September 8, 1993, in Quebec City for fighting with security guards at a hotel. "Dave and the band's road manager were arrested after fighting with security guards at a hotel in Quebec City on September 8, 1993."
**Overdose and Heart Attack in New Orleans (October 8, 1993)** : In New Orleans, Dave Gahan overdosed between the main set and the encore, suffering a heart attack that required paramedics to rush him to the hospital, leading Martin Gore to perform a solo song for the encore. Dave recalled hearing the song "Death's Door" in the background during this near-fatal incident. "At the end of the gig, I couldn't go back for the encore."
**Physical Decline and Stage Injuries (1993-1994)** : Despite training for the Devotional Tour with workouts, running, and martial arts, Dave Gahan looked terrible due to his drug use, with needle marks visible on his arms. Later in the tour, his stage performances became erratic, leading to forgotten lyrics and miscues, and he needed cortisone shots for his voice. He weighed no more than 100 pounds by the summer of 1994 and was hospitalized after a stage dive in Detroit. "So great was the abuse on his body that by the summer of 1994, Dave Gein weighed no more than 100 pounds."
**Post-Devotional Tour Relapse and Home Invasion (1994-1995)** : After the tour, Dave returned to Los Angeles, continuing his heavy drug use. He eventually woke up on a dealer's lawn wearing only pants, shoes, and socks, having been robbed of his wallet, watch, jewelry, and shirt. Later, his house was completely looted while he was away at rehab, losing all his possessions, including recording gear and kitchen cutlery. "One morning he woke up on a dealer's lawn wearing nothing but pants, shoes and socks."
**First Suicide Attempt (1995)** : At the Sunset Marquis Hotel, Dave Gahan slashed his wrists with two-inch cuts while on the phone with his mother. An ambulance was called, and paramedics had to stitch him up on the spot without anesthetic due to the critical situation. This was not his first rescue by the same paramedic team, who began calling him "the cat" for his multiple near-death experiences. "While looking in the bathroom mirror, slashing his wrists seemed like a good idea."
**Psychiatric Ward and Second Suicide Attempt (1995)** : After his first suicide attempt, Dave Gahan woke up in a straightjacket in a padded room at the psychiatric ward of Cedar Sinai Hospital, his only thought being to get high again. He later attempted to hang himself in his bathroom, but that attempt also failed. "When he woke up in a straight jacket in a padded room in the psychiatric ward at nearby Cedar Sinai Hospital, all he could think about was getting high again."
**Paranoia and Escalating Drug Use (1995-1996)** : Dave Gahan's paranoia intensified, leading him to carry a gun, even to check his mailbox. He developed bizarre habits like watching the Weather Channel for 24 hours, painting his walls and floors black, and conversing with stuffed animals that he believed talked back. He also began injecting powerful speedballs (heroin and cocaine) to feel anything. "He grew paranoid and carried a gun with him even if it were just outside to check the mailbox."
**Near-Fatal Overdose and Clinical Death (May 27-28, 1996)** : After flying back to Los Angeles from recording sessions for "Sister of Night," Dave Gahan injected a mixture of heroin and cocaine at the Sunset Marquis Hotel. He suffered a massive overdose, turning blue and his breathing slowing. At 1:15 AM on May 28, 1996, paramedics found him clinically dead for two minutes due to full cardiac arrest. He was revived by an adrenaline needle to the heart, similar to a scene from "Pulp Fiction." "Dave gone was dying."
**Post-Clinical Death Relapse and Recovery (1996)** : Despite being released from the hospital and jail on $10,000 bail for drug charges after his near-fatal overdose, Dave Gahan immediately returned to using heroin, though it no longer provided any buzz. This realization prompted him to check into the Exodus Recovery Center in Marino Del Rey, California, a facility where Kurt Cobain and Shannon Hoon also sought treatment. In Dave's case, the treatment was successful, leading to his sobriety. "But in Dave's case, the treatment worked."
**Divorce from Teresa Conroy (Late 1990s)** : After Dave Gahan achieved sobriety, his second wife, Teresa Conroy, filed for divorce, seeking a significant financial settlement. This marked Dave's second divorce. "Meanwhile, Teresa filed for divorce and wanted some big money."
**Reflections on Addiction and Sobriety (Ongoing)** : Dave Gahan reflected on his period of addiction as a "negative way of a permanent solution to a temporary problem," akin to living a constant suicide. He acknowledged the fear of change and the comfort found in misery, and the struggle to regain a sense of normalcy and happiness without drugs. He credited working with a vocal coach for providing confidence, self-worth, and daily structure in his recovery. "Trying to destroy myself is a really negative way of a permanent solution to a temporary problem."

# artist - Martin Gore

## member of
Depeche Mode

## curiosities
**Songwriting Dominance and Personal Challenges (Early 1990s)** : Martin Gore became the sole songwriter for Depeche Mode, which created tension with Alan Wilder. He claimed to be autistic and indulged heavily in partying and drinking, often withdrawing into video games like "Sonic the Hedgehog." Becoming a father for the first time in June 1991 added further stress. "He wanted to contribute more as a songwriter, but that had pretty much become the sole domain of Martin Gore."
**Devotional Tour Behavior and Arrest (1993)** : During the Devotional Tour, Martin Gore reportedly drank vast amounts of beer and often stripped naked. He relied on very strong sleeping pills to get any sleep. On November 3, 1993, he was arrested in Denver for playing loud music in his hotel room at 4 AM and spent the night in jail. "Martin Gore would allegedly drink vast amounts of beer and then strip naked."
**Panic Attacks and Seizures (1993)** : Martin Gore experienced severe panic attacks, leading him to believe he was dying, and he really should have been taking it easy. In November 1993, ahead of five nights at the Great Western Forum in Los Angeles, he suffered a grand mal seizure during a business meeting at the Sunset Marquis Hotel. This was his second such attack, attributed to stress, alcohol, and drugs, in addition to numerous minor panic attacks. "Then came a grand mall seizure in the middle of a business meeting at the Sunset Marquis Hotel in Los Angeles in November 1993, a head of five nights at the Great Western Forum."
**Sobriety and Continued Music Career (Late 1990s-Present)** : Martin Gore achieved sobriety midway through the recording of the "Ultra" album, which eased the rest of the recording process, particularly the vocal tracks. He continues to record and tour with Dave Gahan, maintaining a sober lifestyle. "Halfway through the recording of this record, Dave got clean and sober."

# artist - Andy Fletcher

## member of
Depeche Mode

## curiosities
**Coping Mechanisms and Personal Life (Early 1990s)** : Andy Fletcher attempted to decompress from band stress by playing chess, becoming quite skilled. He also ventured into entrepreneurship by opening a restaurant named Guesconi near Abbey Road Studios in London, which ultimately failed. He became a father for the first time in August 1991, adding more pressure to his life. "He also opened a restaurant in London called Guesconi near Abbey Road Studios, but that failed to arrest some mental health challenges he was having."
**Mental Health Struggles (Early 1990s)** : Andy Fletcher was diagnosed with OCD, which he believed was inherited from his father, and also struggled with anxiety and depression, requiring professional treatment. He found Dave Gahan's ego trip particularly difficult to manage, contributing to a "three against Dave" dynamic within the band. "He was diagnosed with OCD, something he believed he inherited from his father."
**Devotional Tour Breakdown and Replacement (1993)** : During the recording of "Songs of Faith and Devotion," Andy Fletcher exhibited signs of severe depression and was sent home from sessions. On the Devotional Tour, he was depressed, homesick, and hypochondriac, suffering a nervous breakdown that led to a month-long hospitalization, causing him to miss the entire second leg of the tour. He was replaced by keyboardist Darryl Bata Montey for 39 shows. "He suffered a nervous breakdown and had to be checked into the hospital for a month, missing the entire second leg of the tour."
**Post-Tour Financial Issues and Death (1994-2022)** : After the tumultuous 90s, Andy Fletcher managed to regain stability and continue as a functioning member of Depeche Mode, despite facing financial issues from bad investments, which he also survived. Sadly, he died at his home on May 26, 2022, at the age of 60, from an aortic dissection, a sudden and massive internal bleeding event. "But then Fletcher died of an aortic dissection in his home on May 26, 2022."

## curiosities
**Joining Depeche Mode and Creative Frustration (1982-1991)** : Alan Wilder was the last to join Depeche Mode in 1982, serving as a synth player and programmer. By 1991, he grew frustrated as songwriting became almost exclusively Martin Gore's domain, limiting his creative contributions. "He was the last person to join Depeche Mode, signing on in 1982 as a synth player and programmer."
**Personal Life and Outside Projects (Early 1990s)** : Alan Wilder got married and purchased a 30-acre, eight-bedroom estate in the English countryside. He also pursued projects outside Depeche Mode, working with a group called Recoyl and the English Electronic Body Music (EBM) band, Knights of Rab. "He got married and bought a 30-acre eight-bedroom estate in the English countryside."
**Decision to Leave and Kidney Stone Attack (1992-1994)** : Alan Wilder decided during the recording of "Songs of Faith and Devotion" that he would eventually leave Depeche Mode. During a break between the first and second legs of the Devotional Tour, while in South Africa, he suffered an excruciating kidney stone attack. "At some point during the recording process, Alan Wilder decided that when the time was right, he was going to leave the group."
**Near-Death Experience (September 1, 1994)** : On September 1, 1994, approximately two months after the Devotional Tour concluded, Alan Wilder was driving through the Scottish countryside when an RAF jet fighter trainer almost crashed onto his car, missing him by just over 200 meters. Had his girlfriend not seen the plane approaching and alerted him to pull over into a farm road, his car would have been hit. The two pilots onboard the jet were killed. "Had his girlfriend not seen the plane coming, allowing Alan to pull over into a farm road, his car would have been hit by this plane falling out of the sky."
**Resignation from Depeche Mode (June 1, 1995)** : Alan Wilder officially resigned from Depeche Mode on June 1, 1995, after 13 years with the group. His official statement cited increasing dissatisfaction with internal relations, working practices, and a consistent imbalance in the distribution of workload, stating that his input "never received the respect and acknowledgement that it warrants." "On June 1, 1995, almost a year after the tour ended, Alan Wilder resigned from Depeche Mode after serving 13 years with the group."
**Post-Depeche Mode Life (Present)** : Alan Wilder now lives quietly, having owned a 30-acre, eight-bedroom country estate with his own recording studio. He also possessed a vacation property in Vestfold, Norway. "Alan Wilder is living quietly."

**Los Angeles Warehouse Riot Cleanup Cost (March 1989)** : Following the riot that erupted at Depeche Mode's "Violator" album signing event in Los Angeles, the record store was left with a bill for $25,000 for the cleanup, indicating the extensive damage caused by the out-of-control crowd. "When the dust finally settled, seven people had been sent to hospital and the record store was left with a bill for $25,000 for the cleanup."
**Exodus Recovery Center (Marino Del Rey, California)** : This facility in Marino Del Rey, California, was where Dave Gahan eventually checked himself in to get clean from his drug addiction, and where the treatment worked. It was also the same place where Kurt Cobain had attempted to get straight the week before he died, and where Shannon Hoon of Blind Melon also sought help. "He checked himself into the Exodus Recovery Center in Marino Del Rey, California."
