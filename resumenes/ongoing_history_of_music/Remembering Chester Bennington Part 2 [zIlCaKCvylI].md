## albums
**Hybrid Theory - Debut Album** : This album was initially "absolutely hated" by their record company, yet it went on to sell 30 million copies worldwide. It was one of the two albums released during the peak of illegal file sharing that combined for 57 million sales. "Hybrid 3 was on its way to selling 30 million copies around the world."
**Reanimation - Remix Album** : This remix album was released between the band's first and second studio albums, Hybrid Theory and Meteora, contributing to their high productivity during that period. "And let's not forget that reanimation remix album that was dropped in there too."
**Meteora (2003) - Second Studio Album** : Released in the spring of 2003, Meteora was considered a "difficult second album" but achieved commercial success comparable to Hybrid Theory, selling 27 million copies worldwide by 2003 despite the prevalence of illegal file sharing. The band demoed at least 80 different songs for it over an eight-month period while constantly on tour, resulting in a tight collection of 13 tracks under 37 minutes. "Mediaora was as big a hit as Hybrid 3."
**Live Album & DVD (2003) - Live in Texas** : Released for Christmas 2003 and retailed by November 18, this live album and DVD was recorded during a performance at Reliant Stadium in Houston on August 2, 2003. While the audio is entirely from the Houston show, the video footage was shot both in Houston and the following night in Dallas, leading to visible continuity changes in band members' appearances. "All the video we see was shot in Houston and the next night in Dallas. The audio though is just from Houston."
**Collision Course (2004) - Mashup Album with Jay-Z** : This mashup album was a collaboration between Linkin Park and rapper Jay-Z, undertaken during a period when the band members were pursuing other projects to restore their creative juices after intense touring. "to do something like a mashup album with Jay-Z called Collision Course."
**Minutes to Midnight (2007) - Third Studio Album** : Produced by Rick Rubin, this album took about 18 months to complete, with an original release date in mid-2006 that was delayed. It was finally released on May 14, 2007, debuting at number one in 16 countries and eventually selling around 20 million copies worldwide. The band made a conscious decision to move away from pure new metal, with Mike Shinoda rapping on only two of 12 songs but singing lead on three others. "Minutes to Midnight came out on May 14, 2007, debuting at number one in 16 countries, and it would eventually sell somewhere around 20 million copies."

## curiosities
**High Productivity Early Career** : During the first couple of years of their career, Linkin Park maintained an "insane level of productivity," constantly touring while simultaneously writing and recording. For their second album, Meteora, they managed to demo at least 80 different songs over eight months, dodging in and out of the studio between shows. "That's an insane level of productivity."
**Radio Friendly Sound** : Linkin Park was recognized for its broad appeal and "super radio friendly" sound, which distinguished them within the New Metal genre, even as the genre itself was largely declining. "Lincoln Park was super radio friendly."
**Early Success Amidst Piracy** : Despite the "all-time peak" of illegal file sharing platforms like Napster, LimeWire, and Kazaa when their first two albums were released, Linkin Park achieved immense commercial success, selling a combined 57 million copies worldwide from Hybrid Theory and Meteora. "But for God's sake, 57 million in sales from just two albums."
**Live Album Production Challenges** : The live album and DVD released for Christmas 2003 featured audio recorded solely in Houston on August 2, 2003, but incorporated video footage from both the Houston show and another performance in Dallas the following night. This mixing of visual sources with a single audio track resulted in "subtle changes in appearances by the band members" and other continuity issues. "All the video we see was shot in Houston and the next night in Dallas. The audio though is just from Houston."
**Intense Workload and Toll** : The band's initial four years were characterized by an "insane" pace of continuous touring, writing, and recording, which inevitably took a significant toll on their mental well-being, physical health, and personal lives. "You can't keep up this kind of pace with other being some kind of toll on your mind, your health and your personal life."
**Creative Break and Side Projects** : After the intense touring cycle for Meteora, the band collectively requested and took downtime. This period allowed members to rest and "pursue some other projects" (like Chester Bennington's Dead by Sunrise and the Jay-Z collaboration "Collision Course") to revitalize their creative energies. "Besides, it would be good to rest and then maybe pursue some other projects to restore the creative juices."
**Album Release Delays** : The release of their third album, Minutes to Midnight, was originally slated for mid-2006 but was delayed until May 14, 2007. The band's substantial success afforded them the ability to delay the release without adverse consequences. "The original release date was in mid 2006, but because they were such a very successful band, they could get away with delaying it."
**Genre Evolution with Minutes to Midnight** : With "Minutes to Midnight," Linkin Park made a deliberate choice to shift their sound away from "pure new metal" into "a different sort of headspace." This change was partly evidenced by Mike Shinoda's increased singing roles and reduced rapping, appearing on only two of the album's 12 songs. "Lincoln Park made a conscious decision to move away from pure new metal into a different sort of headspace."
**Project Revolution CD Initiative** : During the Project Revolution tour in 2007-2008, Linkin Park implemented an innovative merchandise strategy: they sold blank CDs at their merch booths for $11, each containing a download code that allowed fans to download and burn that specific Linkin Park show for personal use. "Each came with a download code that allowed any fan to download and burn that particular Lincoln Park show for their personal use."
**8-bit Rebellion iOS Game** : In April 2009, Linkin Park launched an iOS application called "8-bit Rebellion," which was a video game featuring the band members as characters. The game included an exclusive song, "Blackbirds," obtainable only by successfully completing the game. "In April 2009, they released an iOS app called 8-bit Rebellion, which was a video game featuring band members as characters."
**Commercial Success by 2009** : By the end of 2009, after releasing just three studio albums, Linkin Park had sold an impressive 80 million albums, establishing them as "the most successful new rock band to emerge in the 21st century." "Lincoln Park was on its way to selling 80 million albums, making them the most successful new rock band to emerge in the 21st century."

# artist - Chester Bennington

## member of
Linkin Park
Dead by Sunrise

## curiosities
**Musician's Expressiveness** : Chester Bennington's ability to articulate deep feelings and concepts through his music resonated with millions of fans, significantly contributing to Linkin Park's sales success. "many on the strength of Chester's abilities to express how he felt, feelings that resonated with so many others."
**Early Relationships and Children** : Chester's first partner was Elka Brandt, with whom he had a son named Jamie in 1996. After their separation, he married Samantha Olett and they had a son named Draven. "His first partner was a woman named Elka Brandt. They had a child in 1996 named Jamie. When they split, Chester married Samantha Olett and they had a child named Draven."
**Health Scare and Surgery (2003)** : In May 2003, Chester was hospitalized for severe gastrointestinal issues, which were initially misdiagnosed as a viral infection or a complication from a spider bite received in 2001. The condition worsened dramatically during the filming of the "Numb" music video in Europe, leading to his return to the U.S. for surgery on a hiatal hernia, a condition causing extreme pain, heartburn, and vomiting, and potentially leading to esophageal cancer for someone who frequently screams. "He had to fly back to the States for surgery on a hiatal hernia, a condition that can lead to extreme pain, heartburn and lots of vomiting."
**Dedicated Music Video Performance** : Chester was renowned for never lip-syncing during the filming of music videos, always performing his vocals live and intensely for each take, even when suffering from severe gastrointestinal problems during the "Numb" video shoot. "Chester never lip-synced when filming music video. So he was going all out again and again."
**Creation of Dead by Sunrise** : Chester initiated his side project, initially named Snow White Tan and later Dead by Sunrise, specifically to accommodate songs he had written that "weren't right for Lincoln Park," collaborating with friends from the bands Julian K and Orgy. "The group began because Chester had written some songs that really weren't right for Lincoln Park."
**Later Marriage and Extended Family** : Following the dissolution of his first marriage, Chester met and married Talinda Ann Bentley, a former Playboy model who became a schoolteacher, a relationship that endured for the remainder of his life. Together, they had three children—a boy and twin girls—expanding his family to include six children, alongside Jamie (from Elka Brandt), adopted Isaiah (Elka Brandt's other son), and Draven (from Samantha Olett). "He later met and married Talinda Ann Bentley, who was a former playboy model, turned schoolteacher, and that relationship held together for the rest of Chester's life."
**Accident-Proneness and Tour Interruptions** : Chester experienced a series of physical misfortunes, including breaking a wrist during a Melbourne show in 2007, falling ill while touring Asia in 2011 (causing five Linkin Park show cancellations), sustaining a shoulder injury requiring immediate surgery in 2011, and damaging his ankle playing basketball in 2015, which also necessitated surgery and further show cancellations. "he was rather accident prone. In 2007, he broke a wrist during a show in Melbourne. In 2011, he got sick while touring Asia, which affected five Lincoln Park shows. And then he injured his shoulder, which required immediate surgery."
**Cyber-Stalking Incident (2006-2008)** : Starting in 2006, Chester and his wife Talinda were subjected to a severe cyber-stalking campaign, which led to their cell phones, email, and PayPal accounts being compromised. The stalker, Devon Townsend, sent threatening messages, made silent calls, impersonated Chester's ex-wife, and repeatedly hacked their online accounts. The investigation, which involved a former Secret Service computer forensics expert, traced the activity to Sandia National Laboratories and eventually led to Townsend's arrest and conviction, resulting in a two-year jail sentence in February 2008 after her guilty plea in June 2007. "But the worst period was when he and Talinda were cyber-stocked."
**Friendship with Chris Cornell** : By the end of 2009, Chester Bennington and Chris Cornell had become "best friends," a close bond made tragic by their deaths within weeks of each other years later. "By the end of 2009, Chester had become good friends with Chris Cornell. Best friends, actually."
**Struggles with Addiction and Depression** : Despite public perceptions, Chester relapsed in 2006, requiring a trip to rehab for drug and alcohol problems. He also battled depression, a condition not always externally visible, but one he openly discussed alongside substance abuse and childhood sexual molestation, hoping to inspire others to seek help. "The truth was that he had relapsed in 2006, requiring a trip to rehab. He was also suffering from depression, a condition that's not always apparent to people on the outside."
**Openness about Mental Health** : Chester was remarkably candid about his struggles with mental health and depression, describing his internal world as a "bad neighborhood" he shouldn't navigate alone. He expressed that actively engaging in his roles as a father, husband, bandmate, and friend helped him cope with the challenges of life on life's terms. "He talked about depression, substance abuse, being sexually molested as a kid. His thinking was that if he could talk about it, well, maybe that would encourage others to do the same thing, maybe get some help."
**Tragic Death** : A few months after giving a radio interview in early 2017 where he openly discussed his mental health battles, Chester Bennington died by suicide. "A few months after that interview, Chester would be gone, taking his own life."

# curiosity

**Impact of Musician Deaths** : The death of a musician creates a profound sense of loss for fans because the art they created expresses "feelings and concepts and thoughts that we could not articulate ourselves," integrating deeply into listeners' lives and psyches. "When a musician dies, there's a light that goes out in fans."
**Illegal File Sharing Peak** : The early 2000s marked an "all-time peak" for illegal file sharing through platforms like Napster, LimeWire, and Kazaa. Despite the significant sales lost to piracy during this period, bands like Linkin Park still achieved tens of millions of album sales. "both those albums came out when illegal file sharing was at an all-time peak. We had Napster and Lime Wire and Kazah."
**Cyber-Stalking Forensics and National Security** : A sophisticated cyber-stalking incident targeting Chester and Talinda Bennington in 2006-2008 escalated to the point where a former Secret Service computer forensics expert traced the source of malicious emails to Sandia National Laboratories, a highly secure nuclear weapons research facility, briefly making the situation "a matter of national security." "If the emails were coming from there, it wasn't just somebody stalking a rock star. This was a matter of national security."
**Stalker's Identification and Capture** : The cyber-stalking campaign against the Benningtons was eventually traced to Devon Townsend, a 27-year-old single mother who worked at Sandia National Laboratories (where her mother also worked in security). Townsend confessed that she gained access to Chester's email by guessing his middle name, "Charlie," after seeing his address in a mass email from Club Tattoo. She pleaded guilty in June 2007 and was sentenced to two years in jail in February 2008. "In the end, everything was traced to a woman named Devon Townsend, a 27-year-old single mother living with her mom in Albuquerque."
