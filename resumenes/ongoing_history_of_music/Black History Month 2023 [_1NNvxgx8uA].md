# artist - The Selecter

## members
Pauline Black

## genres
Two-Tone Ska

## curiosities
**Mixed-Race and Mixed-Gender Lineup** : The Selecter was one of the original two-tone ska bands from Coventry, England. Similar to their contemporaries The Specials, they featured a mixed-race lineup, but further distinguished themselves by also being a mixed-gender band. "Like their mates, the specials, the selector had a mixed race lineup. But they went further by being mixed gender."
**Pauline Black's Identity and Stage Name** : Frontwoman Pauline Black was born to an Anglo-Jewish mother and a Nigerian father, and was raised as Pauline Vickers by a white middle-class couple. She adopted the stage name Pauline Black both to conceal her musical activities from her boss, who disapproved of such pursuits, and to acknowledge her African roots. "She was brought up as Pauline Vickers, but adopted the stage name Pauline Black for a couple of reasons. First of all, she didn't want her boss to know that she was playing in a band. He apparently frowned on things like that. And to acknowledge her African roots."
**Pauline Black's Diverse Career** : Beyond her more than 40 years in music, Pauline Black has also hosted a TV quiz show for children, acted on both stage and television, and contributed to radio programming for the BBC. "Pauline has been active in music for more than 40 years, but she also hosted a TV quiz show for kids, acted on both stage and television, and has even done some work on the radio for the BBC."

## songs
**Ground Zero (1984)** : This song was recorded in 1984 and is featured on BamBam's album "Free Fall from Space," representing their early grunge sound released years before the genre gained widespread recognition. "Here's just a song called Ground Zero. That's Seattle's BamBam featuring singer Tina Bell and it's from 1984."

## curiosities
**Pioneers of Grunge** : BamBam, fronted by Tina Bell, was a Seattle-based band performing their "grunge thing" as early as 1983, predating the widespread recognition of the sound by a couple of years. Tina Bell is rightly considered one of the inventors of Grunge, as their sound existed before the term was even commonly used. "Tina was a Black woman who fronted a very grungy sounding band from Seattle called BamBam, and they were doing their Grunge thing in 1983, a couple of years before that sound really began to catch on."
**Exclusion from Grunge History** : Despite their pioneering role, Tina Bell and BamBam were never given the credit they deserved and are almost completely expunged from the history of Grunge. Information about them online is often incorrect, with some mentions even omitting Tina entirely. "Tina and BamBam were Grunge before anyone even started using that word, but Tina and the band were never given the credit they deserve. In fact, they're almost completely expunged from the history of Grunge."
**Reasons for Lack of Recognition** : Several factors may have contributed to BamBam's historical oversight, including Tina Bell being a Black woman fronting a punk-rock band in a male-dominated world, their sound being five years too early, issues with bad management, a record deal that went very wrong, or the band stopping at an inopportune moment. "Maybe because this was a Black woman, fronting this kind of punky rock band in a man's world, or it could be that BamBam sound was five years too early. Bad management might be another problem, a record deal then went very wrong, or maybe they just stopped at the wrong time."
**Early Cessation of Activity** : Tina Bell fronted BamBam until 1990, just one year before the grunge movement exploded into mainstream popularity. "Tina fronted BamBam until 1990, one year before Grunge exploded."
**Significant Early Career Highlights** : Despite their obscurity, BamBam played important gigs and festivals, recorded at the same studio where Nirvana made their first records, won local music awards, and even had The Melvins open for them. "It's very strange considering that they played some important gigs in festivals recorded at the same studio where Nirvana made their first records, won some local music awards, and had the Melvins open for them."
**Kurt Cobain's Early Involvement** : At one point, the band BamBam featured a "rowdy" named Kurt Cobain, indicating an early connection to the future Nirvana frontman. "And get this, BamBam once featured a rowdy named Kurt Cobain."
**Tina Bell's Biography** : Tina Bell passed away in 2012. Her son, an Academy Award-winning documentary director, authored a biography about her titled "Conversations with the Grunge Queen." "If you want to know more about Tina who died in 2012, by the way, there's a biography written by her son called Conversations with the Grunge Queen."

"Although these bands covered songs like Led Zeppelin, Bachminton, Overdrive, and Black Sabbath, Tom was profoundly affected by run DMC in public enemy. That's how his guitar style developed, scratching the strings, like a DJ would scratch on a turntable."
**Academic Achievements and Political Leanings** : Morello's political leanings were evident from high school, where he was sometimes considered an anarchist. He graduated with honors and went on to Harvard University, where he studied political science and earned a bachelor's degree in social studies. "Tom's politics have always leaned left, going as far as being a little anarchist at times even in high school. He graduated with honors and enrolled in Harvard as a political science student. "Leaving Harvard with a bachelor's in social studies, he moved to Los Angeles in 1986, where times were so tight that he supported himself working as a male stripper for bachelor-rette parties. "Meanwhile, Adam Jones, who had also moved west, was looking to form his own band. Tom introduced Adam and his new friend, singer, Maynard James Keenan, to a drummer named Daddy Kerry. **Disillusionment with Politics** : Morello briefly worked in the office of a U.S. Senator, an experience that greatly disillusioned him and led him to abandon any aspirations of a political career, choosing instead to focus on music. "For a while, he worked in the office of a US Senator, but what he saw really disillusioned him. He was never, after this, going to have a career in politics.
