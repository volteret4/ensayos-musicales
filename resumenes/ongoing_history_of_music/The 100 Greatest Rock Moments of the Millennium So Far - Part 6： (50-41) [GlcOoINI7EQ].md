"The song first came out on September 29, 2003 and would later appear on the Hot Fuss album."

**Global Release Day / New Music Friday (2015)** : In 2015, a fundamental shift occurred in music release timing with the establishment of Global Release Day, also known as "New Music Friday." Historically, record release days varied by country (e.g., Monday in the UK, Tuesday in North America), which worked for physical media but led to widespread piracy in the digital age when a record purchased in one country could be ripped and distributed globally before its release elsewhere. To combat this and streamline operations, 45 countries agreed to release all new music simultaneously at 12:01 AM local time on Fridays, with only Japan maintaining a slight difference for domestic artists to pre-empt international releases. This change has simplified logistics for labels, stores, streamers, and fans, and has made chart compilation more accurate by standardizing the charting week from Friday to Thursday, preventing artists like Beyoncé from "gaming the system" with surprise releases to artificially boost first-week sales. While some indie record stores lamented the loss of weekday traffic from older release schedules, New Music Friday is now the standard for the foreseeable future. "In 2015, a decision was made to create global release day, or as some people call it, new music Friday."

This disaster, which resulted in 100 deaths and 230 injuries, served as a grim catalyst for the implementation of new, recommended and ordered safety measures, making indoor shows demonstrably safer for attendees than they were before that tragic date. "The result of the disaster was increased insurance requirements and a long list of recommended and ordered safety protocols that have since gone into effect not just in the US, but around the world."
