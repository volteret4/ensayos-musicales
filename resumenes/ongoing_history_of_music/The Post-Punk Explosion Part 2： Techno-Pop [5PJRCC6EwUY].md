# artist - Gary Numan

## instruments

Mini-Moog

## songs

# genre - Technopop (Synth Pop/Electro Pop)

## curiosities

**Emergence and Definition** : Technopop emerged in earnest around 1979 as the synthesizer-driven second half of the New Wave era. It is defined as post-punk music where electronic keyboards and drum machines are the dominant instruments, often with real guitars and drums being completely absent.
**Vibe and Lyrical Themes** : Early Technopop often had a robotic, cold, and deliberately artificial feel, sometimes described as "Blade Runner-ish". Lyrically, the genre focused on futurism, alienation, isolation, and dystopian views of life, sharing a sense of nihilism with guitar-driven punk.
**Commercial Peak (1981-1982)** : The genre reached its commercial zenith in the winter of 1981-82. This period was marked by massive global hits from bands like The Human League, Duran Duran, Soft Cell, and ABC that were both accessible and danceable.
**Evolution and Decline** : By the mid-1980s, the genre's popularity as a distinct movement declined as synthesizer technology became common. Synths transitioned from being "new and futuristic" to the "common present," making it harder to distinguish between real and synthesized sounds.

# genre - New Wave

## curiosities

**Era and Division (1978-1984)** : This era is divided into two distinct periods. The early period featured jerky, twitchy power pop based on guitars, while the later period was dominated by the synthesizer-driven Technopop.

# genre - Post-Punk

## curiosities

**Defining Term** : Post-punk is a blanket term for all the new music made possible by the punk rock explosion of the 1970s. While it did not sound like punk, the movement could not have occurred without the attitude and doors opened by punk.

# instrument - Synthesizer

## curiosities

**Early Development (1940s-1960s)** : Work on synthesizers began in the 1940s and accelerated in the 1950s with the invention of the transistor. Early machines were massive; one model was so large it required a rail car for transport.
**Miniaturization** : By the 1960s, engineers like Bob Moog and Don Buchla built smaller units. However, these early models remained expensive, temperamental, and difficult to program.
**Polyphonic Advancement (1980-1981)** : Around 1980-1981, newer synthesizers emerged that allowed musicians to play multiple notes simultaneously (polyphony). This allowed for the creation of chords without needing to layer individual notes on separate tracks.

---

# instrument - MIDI (Musical Instrument Digital Interface)

## curiosities

**Global Communication Standard** : Originating from a paper titled "Universal Synthesizer Interface," MIDI became the worldwide standard for electronic instrument communication. It allowed a single cable to carry 16 channels of information, enabling one controller to trigger events on up to 16 different devices simultaneously.

# curiosity

**UK Musicians Union Grievances (1982)** : On May 23, 1982, the UK Musicians Union went public with grievances against synthesizers, fearing job losses for traditional musicians. This date coincided with Bob Moog's 48th birthday.
**Motion to Ban Synthesizers (1981)** : In May 1981, a motion was passed at a Musicians Union meeting calling for a total ban on synthesizers. This proposal was widely dismissed by the music press and fans as the "product of a bunch of loonies".
**Technopop DNA in the Late 80s** : As the genre's pure popularity faded, its influence remained in the "DNA" of various late-80s groups such as Camouflage, When in Rome, Information Society, and Kon Kan.
