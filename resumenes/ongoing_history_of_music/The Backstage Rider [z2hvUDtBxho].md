# curiosity

**Concert Rider Function and Importance** : The concert rider is a crucial part of the contract with a promoter, specifying what an act must be provided with before, during, and after a show. Its purpose is to ensure artists are in a "good headspace" to play a great show, covering both mundane logistical details and specific artist requirements. "This brings me to the concert rider, the part of the contract with the promoter that specifies what an act has to be provided with before, during, and after a show."
**Road Manager's Role in Rider Enforcement** : The road manager serves as the point person once the band arrives at a venue, responsible for ensuring that the promoter adheres to all the terms outlined in the rider, from logistical details to artist demands. "Once the band arrives, the road manager is the point person. He or she has to make sure that the promoter adheres to all the terms outlined in the rider."
**Impact of Unmet Rider Requests** : If a promoter fails to meet the artist's requests specified in the rider, it can lead to a "pouty, petulant, and otherwise pissed off performance," as artists may feel disrespected if their needs are ignored. "They mess up the rider and the promoter runs the risk of having a pouty, petulant, and otherwise pissed off performance."
**Complexity of Rider Negotiation and Enforcement** : Crafting a list of requests in a rider that can be consistently enforced night after night, across various venues, cities, and countries, is described as a "delicate and essential thing," with some bands being more adept at this than others. "Coming up with a list of requests that can be enforced night after night, venue after venue, city after city and country after country, is it delicate and essential thing?"
**Touring Hardships and Backstage Sanctuary** : Touring is depicted as arduous, involving months away from home, little sleep, bad food, substance use, late nights, temptations, and difficult interpersonal dynamics. In this environment, the dressing room becomes a "sanctuary," offering artists a measure of control and comfort amidst the chaos of the road. "For that time on the road, your sanctuary really becomes the dressing room, the one place over which you have some measure of control."
**Promoter Test Riders** : Many bands incorporate unusual or seemingly trivial requests into their riders, such as Van Halen's brown M&M's clause or Queens of the Stone Age's map of Ireland, as a test. These clauses help determine whether a promoter has thoroughly read the entire document, indicating their attention to detail and likelihood of fulfilling more critical requirements. "This was a test to see if the promoter actually read through the entire document, which is something that had happened far too few times in the past, resulting in, well, let's just call them fractious relations."
