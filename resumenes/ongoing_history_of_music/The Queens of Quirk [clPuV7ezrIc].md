## genres

## songs
**Piss Factory (1974)** : Regarded by some music historians as the first ever punk rock record, this track was the B-side of a one-off seven-inch single released in the summer of 1974. The song was recorded accidentally after Smith and her collaborator Lenny Kaye finished early while recording a cover of Jimi Hendrix's "Hey Joe." It recounts Smith's experiences working in a doll factory. A thousand copies were pressed and distributed to independent bookstores and record sellers, selling out almost instantly, which helped launch Smith's career as a recording artist and icon on the New York punk rock scene. "No one, man or woman, was making music quite like this back then."

## curiosities

This informal collaboration eventually led to their first recording. "Patty was a poet and music writer, who often did readings with her friends, record-storkler Lenny Kay, noodling around on a guitar in the background."
**Career Breakthrough** : The unexpected success of her debut single "Piss Factory" propelled Patti Smith towards a major label record deal. This marked her transition into a genuine recording artist and established her as a leading icon in the burgeoning New York punk rock scene, and subsequently, the broader rock scene. "This little bit of success set Patty on the road to becoming a genuine recording artist, nailing a major label record deal and becoming a leading icon on the New York punk rock scene, and then, well, the rock scene in general."
**Influence and Unique Style** : Patti Smith was an inspiration to both men and women due to her defiant "I don't give a crap" attitude and her pioneering vocal style. Her approach to music was entirely unique, distinguishing her from any vocalist heard before her time. "Patty was an inspiration to so many women and men with her techno prisoners I don't give a crap view of the world, the universe, and beyond."

"David Gilmore of Pink Floyd, chanced upon a 14-year-old girl who had been playing piano, violin, and organ as well as writing her own songs."
**Early Career Development** : EMI Records signed Kate Bush to a deal that allowed her to continue her schooling while developing her musical talents. "EMI quickly signed her to a deal that allowed her to continue to go to school and develop as a musician."
**Unconventionality and Reception** : Kate Bush's music, particularly her debut single "Wuthering Heights," was considered highly unusual even during the punk years due to her singing style, range, and the literary basis of her hit song. She was adored by those who appreciated her "ethereal quirkiness," consistently refusing to conform to conventional or traditional music practices. "Nothing Kate did was conventional or traditional."
**Live Performance Absence** : After an initial tour, Kate Bush refused to play any live shows for 35 years, which contributed to an aura of mystery, myth, and legend around her. "And because she refused to play any live shows for 35 years after one initial tour, she was surrounded by an aura of mystery and myth and legend."

However, she was expelled at age 11 because she preferred to play music by ear and favored rock and pop songs over classical masters, which was considered "bad form" for a classical program. "Like Kate, she was a child prodigy, more so actually, since she was accepted into the music program at John Hopkins University at age five, five, youngest ever."
**Diverse Early Performances** : As a teenager, around the same time Kate Bush was performing in pubs, Tori Amos honed her skills by performing at various events including country teen talent shows and gay bars. "At around the time Kate was performing in pubs as a teenager, Tori was performing at things like country teen talent shows and playing gay bars."
**Family and Philosophical Influences** : Tori Amos grew up absorbing and rejecting diverse religious, spiritual, and philosophical ideas, a process influenced by her father, who was a minister, and a Cherokee Indian element in her family tree. This rich background likely contributed to the depth of her lyrical themes. "She was also absorbing and rejecting various religions, spiritual and philosophical ideas, which can happen when your dad's a minister and there's a Cherokee Indian element to your family tree."
**Early Career Challenges and Successes** : Before her solo debut, Tori Amos was part of a failed pop band called Why Can't Tori Read and won a songwriting competition organized by the Baltimore Orioles. Her first solo record was initially rejected by her label, but a revised version was eventually released to critical acclaim. "By the time she got to her first solo album, she had been part of a failed pop band called Why Can't Tori Read and won a songwriting competition staged by the Baltimore Orioles."
**Comparisons to Kate Bush** : From the moment she began her solo career, Tori Amos drew comparisons to Kate Bush, particularly for their shared fearless approach to self-expression and their unique artistic "headspace." "In fact, the comparisons between her and Kate began from the moment she started working as a solo artist."
**Revered Singer-Songwriter** : Tori Amos initially gained recognition for her quirky style, but over time, she became one of the world's most respected female singer-songwriters, adored by those who understood her distinctive artistic vision. "She has become one of the world's most respected female singer songwriters, regarded as quirky at first, but then revered and adored by those who got her."
