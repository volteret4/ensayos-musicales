## curiosities
**Barcelona Concert Cancellation & Paternity Question (2000)** : On May 20, 2000, Oasis was scheduled to perform in Barcelona, but the show was cancelled because drummer Alan White suffered from severe tendonitis in his hands and arms. This unexpected downtime led to significant drinking among the band members. During this period, Liam Gallagher made a disparaging comment about Noel Gallagher's then-wife, Meg Matthews, specifically questioning the paternity of their daughter, Anais. "On May 20th of that year, Oasis was supposed to perform in Barcelona. However, there was a problem. Former Alan White had bad tendonitis in his hands and arms, which is a very common thing for drummers. And there was no choice but to cancel the show."
**Noel Quits After Physical Altercation (2000)** : Following Liam Gallagher's comments questioning his daughter's paternity, Noel Gallagher physically assaulted Liam, punching him in the face, splitting his lip, and delivering a serious headbutt. Immediately after the altercation, Noel declared he quit the band, stating he was "done." "Noel leapt on Liam, punched him in the face, split his lip, and then gave him a serious headbutt. Then he stopped off saying that he quit. He was done with the band."
**Continued Touring Without Noel (2000)** : Despite Noel Gallagher's immediate resignation after the fight in Barcelona, Oasis decided to continue their tour. On May 30, 2000, they performed in Milan with Matt Dytin serving as a replacement guitarist and completed 18 subsequent shows without Noel. "On May 30th, they played a show in Milan with a replacement guitarist named Matt Dytin. And they continued without Noel for the next 18 shows."

"Noel did not return to the band until July 15th when it was time to play some big outdoor festivals across the UK. "It wasn't until 2005 that Noel broke the silence, saying, quote, I've never forgiven him because he's never apologized. He cited vocal issues, claiming his voice had gone bad, which he attributed to a lingering case of the flu that had previously led to a concert cancellation in Spain ten days earlier. **Munich Bar Brawl (December 1, 2002)** : On December 1, 2002, while on tour supporting their album "Heathen Chemistry," Liam Gallagher, Alan White, and two other band members became heavily intoxicated at the nightclub bar of the Bayer Sherhoff hotel in Munich, Germany. They began throwing peanuts at five Italian individuals. When the DJ played an Oasis song, Liam and Alan White spontaneously jumped on stage for "unwanted karaoke." Subsequently, one of them (unclear if Liam or Alan) jumped on a table in front of the Italians, causing it to collapse and initiating a violent brawl. "It happened in a bar in Munich, Germany. This was December 1st, 2002. Oasis was on tour in support of their Heathen chemistry album."
**Consequences of Munich Brawl (2002)** : The fight, which began in the bar, escalated into the hotel lobby, resulting in broken furniture and smashed windows. Police responded with ten cars, finding Alan White bleeding from an ashtray blow to the head, and two security personnel injured, one of whom was unconscious. Liam Gallagher sustained significant injuries, losing several teeth and suffering extensive bruising. Despite his injuries, he resisted arrest by kicking a police officer in the ribs. Toxicology reports confirmed he was highly intoxicated with a substantial amount of cocaine in his system. "From the bar, the fight quickly moved out into the hotel lobby. Furniture was broken. Several windows were smashed. Hotel staff called the police, and they responded with ten cars."
**Financial and Legal Repercussions of Munich Brawl (2002)** : Liam Gallagher required $30,000 worth of emergency dental surgery following the Munich brawl. He and two other band members were jailed for 12 hours. The entire remaining European tour had to be cancelled, resulting in millions of dollars in lost revenue for the band. Additionally, management was forced to pay a fine exceeding $300,000 on Liam's behalf before any band member was permitted to leave Germany. Liam faced the possibility of two years in jail. "Liam required $30,000 worth of emergency dental surgery, and he and two other members of the band spent 12 hours in jail. Oh, and the rest of that European tour had to be cancelled, of course, costing the band millions in revenue. And if that weren't enough, management had to pay a fine on Liam's behalf of more than $300,000 before anybody in the band could leave Germany."

The Italian men involved in the brawl were later identified as four real estate agents and a computer nurse, dispelling initial rumors that they were mafia members. "No, it was upstairs in his room when all this happened. And when he found out, he was furious, and yes, there was a confrontation afterwards. Meanwhile, Liam could have faced two years in jail. **Final Backstage Altercation in Paris (August 28, 2009)** : Five days after the V Festival cancellation, a final confrontation occurred backstage in Paris. Liam Gallagher, furious about negative press he believed Noel was involved in, ranted and threw a plum at the wall. He then returned with one of Noel's guitars, wielding it like an axe and swinging it violently, nearly striking Noel's head. The guitar was ultimately destroyed. "Five days later, they were in Paris, backstage in the dressing room. Liam was furious about the bad press he had been getting. Nothing to know. **Noel's Departure and End of Oasis (August 28, 2009)** : Immediately following the violent backstage incident in Paris, Noel Gallagher left the dressing room in a huff. Despite a tour manager announcing "five minutes to show time," Noel sat in a car backstage, then declared, "That's it. I can't do this anymore." Two hours later, a statement was posted on the Oasis website confirming his departure: "It's with some sadness and great relief to tell you that I quit Oasis tonight. People will write and say what they like, but I simply could not go on working with Liam a day longer." This marked the end of Oasis after 18 years. "Noel left the dressing room in a huff, just as someone declared that it was five minutes to show time. Noel sat in a car backstage for all of those five minutes, and when he came out, he said, that's it. I can't do this anymore."

He detailed Liam's expletive-laden tirade, the throwing of a plum, and Liam returning with a guitar, wielding it "like an axe" and nearly hitting Noel's face before Noel "put it out of its misery." Noel criticized the other band members for their inaction during the event, which ultimately solidified his decision to leave. "Liam was quite violent. At that point, there hadn't been any physical violence, but it was a bit like WWE wrestling, and he was like the much-o-man Randy Savage."
