# artist - Sinéad O'Connor

## albums
**The Lion and the Cobra (1987) - Debut Album** : Sinéad O'Connor's debut album appeared in 1987 and was a sensation. At the age of 20 and seven months pregnant, a situation arising from a relationship with a session drummer, she was astonishingly granted the power to self-facilitate and self-produce this major label release, a level of control rarely afforded to artists her age. "The album was stunning."

## songs
**Troy (1987)** : Featured on her first album, The Lion and the Cobra, this song resonated particularly with her new female fanbase. At the time of its release, it was considered the angriest and most honest breakup song many had ever heard, and it remains an intensely powerful track. "It was, to that point, the angriest and most honest breakup song that any of us had ever heard."

## curiosities
**Early Appearance and Reputation (1980s)** : Prior to her debut album in 1987, in the pre-internet era, rumors circulated about Sinéad O'Connor as a tiny, bald woman possessing a massive voice and a punk rock "screw you" attitude, making her a "breath of fresh air." "There were rumors of a tiny bald woman with this huge voice and this punk rock screw you attitude."
**Collaboration with The Edge (1986)** : In 1986, when The Edge was commissioned to write the soundtrack for the film *Captive*, he enlisted 19-year-old Sinéad O'Connor to co-write and provide vocals for one of the songs. "When the edge was tapped to write the soundtrack for a movie called Captive in 1986, he got Shenato co-write and sing on one song."
**Major Label Control (1987)** : Despite her young age of 20 and being seven months pregnant, Sinéad O'Connor was uniquely allowed to self-facilitate and self-produce her debut album for a major label, an unprecedented degree of power and control. "Nobody at that age was given that kind of power and control for a major label release, but Shaneid got it."
**Rebellious Nature** : Sinéad O'Connor was known for her tendency towards self-sabotage and her refusal to conform to the record company's expectations, characteristics that earned her admiration from many women and music fans alike. "Shenato of course went on to become one of the strongest female voices of the early 1990s, but she also had this pension for self-sabotage and not wanting to play the record company game."

# artist - Tori Amos

## member of

## albums
**Little Earthquakes (1992) - First Solo Album** : Released as one of the first albums of 1992, *Little Earthquakes* initially puzzled many. At a time when grunge, spearheaded by Nirvana's *Nevermind*, was ascendant and labels sought similar-sounding bands, Tori's piano-based songs presented a stark contrast to the prevalent "big guitar and testosterone" sound. She broke through due to the early 90s alt-rock explosion dismantling prejudices against female musicians, her embrace by old-school alt-rock fans as a spiritual successor to Kate Bush, and her music's quiet vulnerability resonating with the underlying fear and confusion also present in grunge, but from a female perspective. "Her first solo album, Little Earthquakes, was one of the first releases of 1992, and at first nobody really knew what to make of it."

## curiosities
**Child Prodigy and Religious Upbringing** : Tori Amos was a piano prodigy who grew up in Baltimore within a strict religious family, an environment that often clashed with her personal inclinations. "Now Tori was a piano prodigy who grew up in a strict religious family in Baltimore, which is something that really didn't sit well with her."
**Defiance in Music School** : During her training at a formal music school, she reportedly irritated her instructors by refusing to practice scales or learn classical pieces by composers like Chopin, instead insisting on playing rock songs. "And when she was sent to a formal music school for training, she annoyed everybody by insisting on playing rock songs instead of practicing scales and learning about Chopin."
**Brief Hair Metal Phase** : Tori Amos had a short-lived and "really weird" experience as the lead singer of a hair metal band called Why Can't Tori Read, which eventually came to an end, allowing her to pursue her distinct musical muse. "There was a brief spin as the lead singer in a hair metal band called Why Can't Tori Read, which was really weird, but then that came to a blessed end."
**Spiritual Successor to Kate Bush** : Early alt-rock fans, familiar with Kate Bush's style, readily embraced Tori Amos as her "spiritual successor" due to her similarly highly emotional, confessional, and powerful musical output. "Back in some old school alt-rock fans remembered Kate Bush and embraced Tori as her spiritual successor."
**Anti-Grunge Appeal** : Her piano-based music, characterized by its quieter nature and female perspective, stood in direct opposition to the dominant guitar-driven grunge sound of the early 1990s. This "anti-grunge" approach proved highly effective in the musical landscape of the time. "Tori's music was much the same, except quieter and from a female perspective."
**Record Label Openness** : Record labels, caught off guard by the surge of alternative music, were willing to gamble on anything considered "left of the mainstream," including signing a classically trained redhead known for her expressive, physical piano playing. "Red labels were so surprised at the rise of alternative. They were willing to take a chance at signing anything that might be considered left of the mainstream."

"She studied classical music from the age of 6, recorded her first album at the age of 12 and was on Icelandic radio and TV all the time."

She also immersed herself in the Goth subculture during this period. "Then she discovered punk and all bets were off. She was in all girl bands with names like Spit and Snot."

"The Sugar Cubes were the right brand of weirdness for the Gen X rock community and they were embraced from the time they were formed in 1986 until they break up in 1992."

"This pixie-ish thing with a massive singing voice that was often punctuated by shrieks and howls and growls and then back down to little girl whispering."
**Move to London and Experimentation** : Feeling that Iceland was too small for her ambitions, Björk relocated to London, where she commenced extensive experimentation with music, sounds, and visuals, a creative process she continues to this day. "Iceland was way too small for Bjork so she moved to London where she began to experiment with music and sounds and visuals, something that continues to this day."
**Unconventional Artistic Identity** : Björk is renowned for her highly unconventional artistic approach; virtually nothing she produces is considered "normal," a characteristic that endeared her to alt-rock enthusiasts in the 1990s and established a recognizable "Bjorkish" style. "Nothing Bjork does and I mean nothing. She does is what anyone would call normal and that's exactly what made her such a darling to the alt rock kids in the 1990s."
**Pop Culture Icon** : Following the release of her 1993 album *Debut*, Björk ascended to the status of a pop culture darling, maintaining this influence throughout the remainder of the decade and beyond. "Bjork became a pop cultured darling for the rest of the decade and beyond and let me tell you something."
