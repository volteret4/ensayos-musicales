## albums
**Yesterday and Today (1966) – Infamous Butcher Cover** : An album issued in 1966 featuring original artwork of the Beatles posing with headless baby dolls and raw meat, known as the infamous "butcher cover." This photo, part of a collection by Robert Whitaker, was conceived as a protest against the Vietnam War and possibly a "dig" at EMI for "butchering" the Beatles' UK albums for North American release. A limited number of these albums were sent to stores, causing record store owners to be appalled. Capitol Records, the band's American label, immediately recalled all 60,000 albums distributed and held back another 750,000 in warehouses. A new cover was chosen, depicting the Beatles sitting around a steamer trunk looking annoyed. Many recalled albums simply had the new cover pasted over the old one, which could be carefully steamed off to reveal the original. A sealed copy of the original butcher version in mint condition can be worth $125,000. "In 1966, they issued the Beatles yesterday and today, an album where the original artwork featured the Beatles posing with some headless baby dolls in a bunch of raw meat."
**Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band (1967) – Autographed Mono Parlophone Pressing** : An original 1967 pressing of the "Sergeant Pepper album," specifically the mono version on the original Parlophone label, autographed by all four members of the Beatles. One such copy was auctioned in 2013, selling for $290,000. This price, while significant, was not an all-time high for a Beatles album at the time. "Number four on the master list is an original 1967 pressing of the Beatles Sergeant Pepper album, autographed by all four members."
**The White Album (1968) – Ringo Starr's Serial Number 0000001** : An original copy of "The White Album" from 1968, distinguished by its unique sequential serial number embossed on the front. EMI's numbering system meant the very first copy, 0000001, was owned by Ringo Starr. In 2015, Starr sold his perfect-condition copy at auction, with the proceeds benefiting his charity, The Lotus Foundation. It fetched $790,000 US dollars, setting an all-time record for an album at that time. "His serial number on his white album was 000000000001."
## curiosities
**EMI Audition Demo (Undated) – "Hello Little Girl" / "Till There Was You" Acetate** : A one-of-a-kind, 10-inch acetate test pressing recorded as an audition for EMI Records, representing the first record featuring the Beatles as they would become known. Side A features "Hello Little Girl," credited to Paul McCartney and the Beatles, while Side B has "Till There Was You," credited to John Lennon and the Beatles; manager Brian Epstein wrote the titles in blue pen to ensure fairness. This record disappeared for decades before being found in the attic of Les Maguire, a member of Gerry and the Pacemakers. It was sold in March 2016 for 77,500 pounds, equivalent to $95,000 American or $130,000 Canadian. "It's a 10-inch acetate, a test pressing in other words, and is a one-of-a-kind thing Hello Little Girl on the A side, and Till There Was You on the B side."
**1958 Percy Phillips Home Recording – "That'll Be the Day" / "In Spite of All the Danger"** : An ultra-rare, two-sided 78 RPM disc from 1958, recorded by John Lennon, Paul McCartney, George Harrison, pianist John Lowe, and drummer Colin Hanton at the home of Percy Phillips, who specialized in "down and dirty" recordings in his front room. Side one features Buddy Holly's "That'll Be the Day," and side two contains "In Spite of All the Danger," the first-ever Lennon-McCartney recording. This record is owned by Paul McCartney and is estimated to be worth at least 100,000 pounds if it were ever to be sold. "The result was a two-sided 78 RPM disc with Buddy Holly's That'll Be the Day on Side One, and the first ever Lennon McCartney recording on the other, in spite of all the danger."

## albums
**Double Fantasy (1980) – Autographed by Lennon on Assassination Day** : John and Yoko's recently released album, which John Lennon autographed for Mark David Chapman around 5 PM on December 8, 1980, before heading to a recording studio. This is believed to be Lennon's last autograph and bears both Chapman's and Lennon's fingerprints. In the chaos following Lennon's assassination five hours later, Chapman left the record in a planter at The Dakota, Lennon's New York apartment. It was found by an unknown individual who kept it under their bed for 18 years. The cover features Lennon's signature in black marker and police evidence markings. It first sold in 1998 for $150,000, then for $520,000, and again in November 2020 for $900,000, making it the most expensive piece of vinyl in the world. "John Lennon was assassinated on December 8, 1980. The shooter, of course, was Mark David Chapman. Before he killed Lennon, Chapman hung around the Dakota, Lennon's New York apartment, with a copy of John and Yoko's just released album, Double Fantasy."

# artist - Wu-Tang Clan
## albums
**Once Upon a Time in Shaolin (2015) – $4 Million CD** : A unique album that was conceived as an art project and a statement about the disposability of music, created over six years before its unveiling in 2015. Only one copy exists, which was auctioned off in 2015 with the stipulation that the buyer could not exploit it commercially for 88 years, until 2103. The contract also included an extraordinary clause allowing active members of the Wu-Tang Clan and/or actor Bill Murray to legally attempt a heist to steal it back without legal repercussions. It initially sold for $2 million US dollars to Martin Shkreli. "There is just one copy. Call it an art project. A statement about how music has become so dispensable."

# instrument - Dead Wax
## curiosities
**Identification of Collectible Vinyl** : The "dead wax" refers to the space on a vinyl record between where the grooves run out and the center label. It can contain inscriptions, such as "Why don't you trade those guitars for shovels?" on the collectible Nirvana "Bleach" singles, which helps to authenticate valuable copies and differentiate them from counterfeits. "So look at the dead wax, which is that space between where the grooves of the record run out and the label of the center."

# curiosity
**Vinyl Record Revival (2008-present)** : The vinyl record format, once "all but dead," experienced a significant revival starting in 2008 with the invention of Record Store Day, leading to double-digit increases in sales year after year. This boom is attributed to factors like audio quality, the ability to display music, and the tactile experience of holding the disc, artwork, liner notes, and lyrics. "The format was all but dead until some desperate record store owners invented record store day in 2008. And since then, we've seen double-digit increases in vinyl sales year after year after year."
**Vinyl Revenue Surpassing CD Sales** : As a result of the ongoing boom, revenue generated by vinyl record sales in several countries has surpassed the revenue from compact disc sales, a phenomenon not seen since the late 1980s. "Things are so good that in several countries, the revenue brought in by selling vinyl is greater than the revenue generated by compact disc sales."
**Factors for Vinyl Collectibility** : The two primary factors that make a vinyl record collectible are extreme rarity and high demand. A record, even if it's the only copy, is worthless if no one desires it. "Well, first of all, it has to be very rare. And second, it has to be in demand."
**Martin Shkreli's CD Purchase and Confiscation (2015-onward)** : Disgraced pharmaceutical CEO Martin Shkreli purchased the sole copy of the Wu-Tang Clan's "Once Upon a Time in Shaolin" CD for $2 million in 2015, only for it to be confiscated by the U.S. government after his arrest and conviction for securities fraud, eventually selling for $4 million to an NFT company. "And the buyer ended up being disgraced farmer bro Martin Shrekeli, the guy who became famous or infamous, I guess, by hiking the price of an anti-HIV drug by 5,000 percent just because he could."
