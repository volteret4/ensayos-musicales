## albums
**Enema of the State (1999)** : This album was tremendously important to the development of pop punk, selling at least 15 million copies globally. Despite criticism for becoming "too slick and too glossy," it served as a blueprint for many upcoming bands, influencing acts like Fall Out Boy and Panic! at the Disco, and contributed to Emo bands shifting towards more melodic, poppy sounds. "No Enema of the States, and we probably wouldn't have heard too much from Fallout Boy or Newfound Glory or a simple planner panic at the disco."
**The Mark Tom and Travis Show, The Enema Strikes Back (2000) – Live Album** : Released on November 7th, 2000, this live album captured highlights from the band's first three studio albums. It also included extensive in-between song banter from Mark and Tom, and featured one new studio single at the very end. "It was released on November 7th, 2000, and it featured highlights from the first three albums, plus a lot of the in-between song band here for Mark and Tom."
**Take Off Your Pants and Jacket (2001)** : Released on June 12th, 2001, this album was created under pressure from their record label to deliver another pop punk hit, leading to creative tensions within the band. It was slightly heavier than "Enema of the State" and became the first punk record to debut at number one on the American album charts, selling around 15 million copies. The album's success was pivotal in the growth of pop punk and pop emo in the early 2000s. "It became the first punk record to debut at number one on the American album charts."
**Blink 182 (2003) – Self-Titled Album** : Released on November 18, 2003, this fifth album marked a significant departure for the band. After individual side projects by Tom and Travis, the band members were growing up and sought a more mature sound. The recording sessions were extensive, involved trying out numerous instruments and recording techniques, and featured Robert Smith of The Cure on guest vocals. It was more experimental, darker, and incorporated new sounds. "This fifth album, the self-titled one that was released on November 18, 2003, had a different sound."
**California (2016)** : The 2016 album from which the song "Bored to Death" was released. "From the 2016 album California, there's Blink 182 with bored to death."

## songs
**Man Overboard (2000)** : This was the sole studio track included at the end of Blink 182's first live album, "The Mark Tom and Travis Show, The Enema Strikes Back." "Man Overboard, the one studio track from Blink 182's first live album."
**F the Dog (Unreleased/Joke Song)** : One of two joke songs played for record label executives to express the band's rebellion against pressure to deliver a specific type of album. "One was called F the Dog, and the other one was when you F'd Hitler, which freaked out the label people."
**When You F'd Hitler (Unreleased/Joke Song)** : Another joke song, played for record label executives as an act of rebellion during creative struggles. "One was called F the Dog, and the other one was when you F'd Hitler, which freaked out the label people."
**The Rock Show (2001)** : Written by Mark Hoppus in about 10 minutes as a deliberate "F you" to the record label, who were pressuring the band for a commercial hit. Intended as a cheesy, disposable pop punk song, it unexpectedly became the album's first single and a major hit, backfiring on Mark's intention. The song refers to the real-life club Soma in San Diego. "The Rock Show from Blink 182 written in 10 minutes and intended as an F you to the record label, which kind of backfired, but in a good way, I guess."
**First Date (2001)** : Tom DeLonge, also infuriated by label pressure, wrote this song in about 10 minutes, intending it as his own "F you" to the label. It's about a date he went on with his now-wife to SeaWorld and became the second single from "Take Off Your Pants and Jacket," also a major hit. "It's about a date he went on with his now wife to see world. It ended up becoming the second single from takeoff and another major hit."
**Stay Together for the Kids (2001)** : This song from "Take Off Your Pants and Jacket" represents the band's effort to push into more serious territory, with Tom DeLonge writing about the impact of his parents' divorce. Its accompanying video, shot on September 10, 2001, initially featured a wrecking ball demolishing a house as a metaphor for family breakup. However, after the events of September 11, 2001, the wrecking ball concept was deemed too destructive and the video was reshot to show young people shouting in an empty house. "This song is also an example of the band pushing themselves into new territory."
**Feeling This (2003)** : The first single from the self-titled "Blink 182" album, it was the first song the band wrote digitally, piecing it together with software. It featured four edited drum tracks and a cowbell that was initially a joke but kept. Tom DeLonge's off-key, loud vocals before the chorus were recorded with him at the end of a 30-foot room, 15 feet from the microphone. The song's unintended fade-out effect on the vocals was kept as a mistake, reminding the band of The Beach Boys. "The first single was Feeling This, and it's the first song the band wrote digitally, which is to say that it was pieced together using software."
**I Miss You (2003)** : The second single from the self-titled album, it sold half a million copies as a standalone track. Its rhythm track was inspired by The Cure's 1983 hit "Love Cats." This song notably used no electric instruments, despite having up to 70 acoustic layers. "If the rhythm track sounds familiar, it's because the inspiration came from the Cures 1983 hit Love Cats."
**Always (2005)** : The last single released from the self-titled "Blink 182" album. It served as a tribute to 1980s music, featuring prominent synth sounds, and was considered by some fans to be the best song on the album. "The song was their tribute to the 80s, with its synth sounds."
**Bored to Death (2016)** : A song from their 2016 album "California." At almost four minutes in length, it is noted as one of the longest Blink 182 songs in history, as the band rarely extends beyond the three-minute mark. "That's one of the longest Blink songs in history."

## curiosities
**Early 2000s Success and Decline** : By 2000, Blink 182 had reached the top, selling albums by the tens of millions, but subsequently faced a career nose dive. "By 2000, they'd made it to the top and were selling albums by the tens of millions."
**Creative Struggles and Label Pressure (Early 2000s)** : After "Enema of the State," the band faced significant internal tensions and creative struggles. Their record label pressured them to continue producing pop punk hits, leading to rebellion from the band members who desired to explore new sounds. "This did set up tensions within the band. Call it creative struggles if you want."
**Saving Green Day (Pop Disaster Tour)** : Blink 182 joined Green Day on the Pop Disaster Tour, a pairing intended as a last-ditch effort to revitalize Green Day's career, which seemed to be on the decline. The tour successfully introduced Green Day to a new, younger audience and forced Blink 182 to improve their live performances. "So Blink 182 saved Green Day."
**Post-Take Off Your Pants and Jacket Break** : After fulfilling obligations for "Take Off Your Pants and Jacket," the band desperately needed a break due to intense pressure and internal tensions. Tom DeLonge also had serious back issues, including a herniated disc requiring surgery, necessitating time off from Blink 182. "They desperately needed some time apart."
**Mark Hoppus's Isolation During Side Projects** : While Tom DeLonge and Travis Barker pursued side projects like Box Car Racer and The Transplants, Mark Hoppus "stayed on the sidelines quietly fuming," feeling betrayed that Tom had asked Travis to drum for Box Car Racer, a project that had an "old DIY vibe of early Blink." "Mark left the studio and wrote what he thought would be an instant rejection."
**Evolution of Sound (Self-Titled Album)** : For their self-titled 2003 album, the band sought to grow beyond "goofy adolescent" songs. Influenced by their side projects, Tom and Travis brought a new attitude to Blink 182, resulting in a more experimental, darker, and mature sound. "Like all musicians, they wanted to grow."
**Meticulous Recording Process (Self-Titled Album)** : The recording sessions for the self-titled album were prolonged and exceptionally meticulous. The band tried over 70 different guitars, 30 different amplifiers, at least six drum kits, and rotated 40 different snare drums. They incorporated unusual instruments like Polynesian Camel and Bals, turntables for scratching/looping, old organs, and cellos. An engineer who worked on Pink Floyd's "The Wall" was hired to experiment with various microphones and mic placement techniques. "They were also very, very picky when it came to recording each sound."
**The Dollar Bill Tour (2004)** : The tour following the release of the self-titled album began with an All Ages Club Tour, where tickets were sold for $8, and included a golden ticket contest for a private concert. The tour ran throughout 2004, concluding in Dublin on December 16th, with a planned 2005 leg ultimately canceled due to renewed internal tensions. "The tour that followed the release of the fifth album was called The Dollar Bill Tour."
**Indefinite Hiatus and Breakup (2005)** : Over Christmas 2004, old tensions resurfaced, leading to a breakdown in communication among band members. On February 22, 2005, management announced an "indefinite hiatus." The statement explained the band's need for time with loved ones after over a decade of non-stop work. However, this was effectively a breakup, initiated when Tom DeLonge's demand for a six-month break was deemed too long by Mark and Travis, leading to Tom quitting the band. "On February 22, 2005, announcing that the group was going on, quote, indefinite hiatus."

## albums
**Box Car Racer (2002) – Self-Titled Concept Album** : Released on May 21st, 2002, this was Tom DeLonge's side project, initially conceived to satisfy his desire to play dirtier, more aggressive punk while recovering from back surgery. It was not intended to be a real band. The album is a concept album that explored themes of the end of the world, conspiracies, religion, the apocalypse, and the Masons. It started acoustically but became louder and more electric during its creation. Produced by Jerry Finn, who also worked with Blink 182. "They released an album, a concept album, that dealt with the end of the world."

## songs
**I Feel So (2002)** : One of the singles released from the "Box Car Racer" album. "And this was one of them, is Boxcar racer and I feel so."

## curiosities
**Origin and Purpose** : Box Car Racer was Tom DeLonge's project, driven by his need to play more aggressive punk music and to occupy himself while recovering from back surgery. He did not envision it as a permanent band. "This was Tom's product and his chance to exercise his need to play punk that was dirtier and more aggressive."
**Travis Barker's Involvement** : Travis Barker joined Tom DeLonge on drums for Box Car Racer, a decision that deeply upset Mark Hoppus of Blink 182, who felt betrayed. Travis only sang on a couple of tracks. "But then he asked Travis to be his drummer, which was okay for Travis, but it greatly pissed off Mark."
**Name Origin** : The name "Box Car Racer" was shared with a previous band Travis Barker was in before Blink 182. The common story that it alluded to the B-52 bomber that dropped the atomic bomb on Nagasaki is incorrect; its true origin is Travis's old band. "The real origin goes back to Travis's old band."
**Hidden CD Artwork Messages** : The CD artwork for the "Box Car Racer" album contains hidden messages. For example, a sequence "LNW-1301" is the longitudinal location of the Twin Towers/World Trade Center. "If you have a copy of the CD, there are all kinds of hidden messages in the artwork."

# curiosity

**Band Careers with Two Acts** : It is rare but not impossible for a band to have a career with two distinct acts, exemplified by bands like Joy Division/New Order, Barenaked Ladies, Black Sabbath, Green Day, and Blink 182. "It's rare that a band has a career with two acts."
**Record Label Pressure for Hits** : Record labels often demand specific musical directions from artists, especially when quarterly revenues are looking bleak, pushing for hits to secure executive jobs and bonuses. This pressure can lead to creative struggles and rebellion from the artists. "Mark claims it was because the label's quarterly revenues were looking bleak and they needed a hit so that the executives could keep their jobs and maybe get some bonuses."
**Soma (Club in San Diego)** : The San Diego club called Soma is referenced in Blink 182's song "The Rock Show." "By the way, the song refers to a real-life club in San Diego called Soma."
**September 11, 2001 Events' Impact on Music Video** : The events of September 11, 2001, had an immediate impact on creative content, leading Blink 182 to reshoot a music video for "Stay Together for the Kids." An original concept featuring a wrecking ball going through a house was deemed "too destructive" after the attacks. "But after the events of the following day, the wrecking ball idea was considered too destructive."
**DIY (Do It Yourself) Vibe** : Tom DeLonge's side project Box Car Racer was noted for having an "old DIY vibe" reminiscent of early Blink 182, which resonated with Mark Hoppus. "It had that old DIY vibe of early Blink."
**Pink Floyd Engineer for Blink 182** : For their self-titled 2003 album, Blink 182 hired an engineer who had previously worked on Pink Floyd's "The Wall" to assist with the recording. "To engineer part of the record, they hired one of the guys who helped sort up the wall for Pink Floyd."
**Unintentional Musical Element** : The ending of Blink 182's "Feeling This" included an engineer's mistake where a fade-out command was forgotten for the vocals. The band chose to keep the error, noting it sounded "like the beach boys." "That extra by the way was a mistake. The engineer had set up the studio to fade out the song at the end, but forgot to apply that command to the vocals."
