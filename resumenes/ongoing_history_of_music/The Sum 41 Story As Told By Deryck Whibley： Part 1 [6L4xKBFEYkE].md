## albums
**Half Hour of Power (2000) - First Record Company Release** : This EP was the band's first official release on a record company, specifically Aquarius Records in Canada. Its success helped get the band on the road, touring North America (Canada and US). "The first real release that was on a record company was half our power."
**All Killer No Filler (2001) - Debut Full-Length** : This album was recorded in a couple of periods throughout mid-2000, late 2000, and early 2001. It achieved widespread international success, surprising the band, and sold around four million copies. "All Kill and All Fillar comes out. And again, well received."
**Does This Look Infected (2002) - Rushed Production** : Released on November 26, 2002, this album was created under extreme time pressure, with the band having only six weeks off between tours to write and record. Recording continued even while the band was touring Europe, with sessions at Lipic Studios in London after playing festivals like Reading and Leeds. The gross album cover image was decided before the title, and the title itself was chosen at the last minute by the band members looking at the cover. "Does This Look Infected was a funny record for us because we didn't give ourselves any time to make a new record coming to try to follow up from all killer."
**Chuck (2004) - Congo-Influenced Album** : Recorded partly before the band's trip to the Congo, this album was named after Chuck, a UN official who saved the band from an African civil war. The recording took place in a bunker-like studio located across the street from the Hells Angels headquarters in Toronto. The album was noted for its heavy yet melodic sound, likely influenced by the traumatic experience in Congo. "So we get to the Chuck record, which before you get there, you end up in the Congo."

## curiosities
**Early Influences - The Monkees and The Beatles** : Derek Whibley's interest in music began around age six or seven in 1986, heavily influenced by The Monkees Television Show and The Beatles' music. "I was really obsessed with the Monkeys Television Show, the Beatles music."
**Early Influence - Gas Station Cassettes** : Around 1986, at age six, Derek Whibley discovered "50s and 60s revival hits" on cassette tapes obtained from a gas station promotion, which profoundly contributed to his early love for music. "I just fell in love with those cassettes."
**Early Influence - La Bamba Movie** : The 1987 movie "La Bamba," which tells the story of Ritchie Valens, significantly influenced Derek Whibley at age seven, making him want to play guitar. "I just fell in love with that movie and I just thought I want to play guitar."
**First Guitar at 13** : Derek Whibley received a fake guitar around age eight, but it wasn't until he was 13 that he got a real guitar, immediately forming his first band in Ajax. "when I got a real guitar I was about 13."
**Formation Origin - 41 Days into Summer Vacation** : Sum 41 was formed 41 days into the band members' summer vacation in 1996, coinciding with the day the Warp Tour came through their area, which became the inspiration for the band's name. "Because you formed the band 41 days into your summer vacation? 41 into the summer vacation, true."
**Early Mentorship and Demos** : Greg Norrie (from Trevor Charger) and Marcos Stanzo (from Len) became friends with Sum 41 after Derek Whibley invited Norrie to their first show. Over a year or two, they helped the band by recording demos, offering advice, and bringing them on tour, playing shows with both Trevor Charger and Len. "Over the next year or two, they both just kind of helped us out. Recorded us, gave us, you know, we did some demos and stuff like that."
**Bidding War for Record Deal** : After Michael McCarty at EMI Publishing signed Derek Whibley to a publishing deal at age 17 and began sending out Sum 41's demos, interest from record companies grew into a bidding war. Despite mixed initial reactions, the band eventually signed a worldwide deal with Island Def Jam, while Aquarius Records (the first label to show interest) retained the Canadian rights. "it turned into a bidding war for some reason. Like everything just sort of changed from what it was."
**Collaboration with Iggy Pop** : In late 2002, Iggy Pop invited Sum 41 to contribute to his "Skull Ring" album, an opportunity Derek Whibley considered a "privilege." Whibley, along with producer Greg Norrie, co-wrote the track "Little Know It All" with Iggy Pop. Whibley felt an immediate connection with Pop during their first phone call and they have maintained contact since, sometimes performing at the same festivals, such as in France in 2016. "Iggy Pop was a great experience, and sort of a privilege."
**Caught in African Civil War** : During the recording of the "Chuck" album, Sum 41 traveled to the Democratic Republic of Congo with the group War Child to film a documentary on the effects of the Civil War. Ten days into their trip, a ceasefire ended, and they found themselves trapped in a hotel between two warring rebel factions, subjected to mortar rounds, RPGs, and stray bullets. "we were just caught in the middle of it."
**Album Named After Rescuer** : During the three days Sum 41 was trapped in a Congolese civil war zone, a UN official named Chuck bravely moved the band and 40 other people to safety within the hotel. Derek Whibley promised Chuck that if he got them out, they would name their next album after him. Chuck subsequently organized armored tanks to evacuate them. "Chuck, if you get us out of this, we're going to name this record after you."
**Chuck Album Recording Location** : The "Chuck" album was recorded in a unique, bunker-like studio situated across the street from the Hells Angels headquarters in Toronto. "now we can get to recording the Chuck album, which was recorded in a bunker like studio, across the street, from the hell's angels headquarters in Toronto."
**Derek Whibley's Chronic Back Injury** : Derek Whibley first suffered a herniated disc in 2007 on stage in Calgary, an injury he attributes to years of jumping around with a guitar. The condition causes excruciating pain and can temporarily render his legs useless by affecting his spinal cord nerves. Initially, he managed the pain with alcohol and continued touring, preventing proper healing. "I have a herniated disc, which is not a crazy injury, but it does the way... For whatever reason, they're always different for everybody."
**Back Injury Re-aggravated in Japan** : Derek Whibley's chronic back injury flared up again after he was attacked by two or three guys from behind in a small bar in Japan, forcing the cancellation of the remaining tour dates. He returned home, but continued to cope with the pain through drinking instead of proper care, leading to ongoing chronic issues. "I was just... it was a friend of ours has this bar we've been going to for, you know, since we've been going to Japan."
**Meteoric Rise and Burnout** : Sum 41 experienced a rapid and intense rise to fame from 1996 to 2005, becoming an international multi-platinum act. However, this period of constant touring and recording, coupled with Derek Whibley's personal health struggles, eventually led to physical and mental exhaustion. "Some 41 went on a meteoric rise from 1996 through to 2005. But you can only burn that hard, that bright for so long."

# curiosity

**Derek Whibley's Childhood in Ajax** : Derek Whibley was born and raised in Scarborough, Ontario, before moving to Ajax at the age of 12, where he spent most of his high school and teenage years. He formed his first bands in Ajax around age 13. "I was born and raised in Scarborough and then moved out to Ajax when I was about 12."
**1990s Canadian Rock and Punk Growth** : The 1990s witnessed a rapid expansion of Canadian rock and punk music, creating a vibrant scene that helped bands like Sum 41 emerge and gain traction quickly. "at that time of the 90s, Canadian rock and punk was growing really, really quick."
**Sum 41's Congo Documentary with War Child** : Sum 41 traveled to the Congo with the humanitarian organization War Child to shoot a documentary detailing the devastating effects of the Civil War. This trip, intended for filming, unexpectedly placed the band in the middle of renewed conflict. "We went with a group called War Child. And we were to come back and sort of finish up the album."
**Chuck (UN Official)** : Chuck was a UN official who played a heroic role in saving Sum 41 and 40 other individuals when they were trapped in a hotel during an African civil war in the Congo. He continuously moved the group to safer rooms as the hotel was under attack from mortar rounds, RPGs, and stray bullets, eventually organizing armored tanks for their extraction after three days. "Chuck was the guy he happened to be at the hotel with us. He was with the UN and he was the one who kept moving us around and keeping us safe from room to room."
