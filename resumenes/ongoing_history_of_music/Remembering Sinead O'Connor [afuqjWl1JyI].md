## albums
**The Lion and the Cobra (1987) - Debut Album** : Released when Sinead was 20, this album was self-produced by her after she fired the initial producer two days into recording, despite being pregnant at the time. The album went gold, received a Grammy nomination, and established her as a strong, independent, and fierce artist of the late 1980s. It contained college and alternative radio hits and was instrumental in building her North American fan base. The title is a line from Psalm 91. "The line in the cobra, the title is a line from Psalm 91, by the way, established Shanade as one of the hottest and most interesting singers of the late 1980s."
**I Do Not Want What I Haven't Got (1990) - Second Album** : Released on March 20, 1990, this album was a massive global success, shooting to number one worldwide and selling millions, earning four Grammy nominations. Engineer Chris Birkett was brought in last-minute to produce it after Sinead walked out on the initial producer, Nellee Hooper, after two days, despite the record company initially not wanting to release it, deeming it "too personal." Sinead's contract gave her complete creative control, forcing its release. The title comes from a spiritualist session where she contacted her deceased mother, signifying her mother's spirit wasn't seeking forgiveness. There were two album covers; the European edition featured an angry, screaming shot, while the North American division opted for a more demure photo from the same session. "She forced them to release it."
**Universal Mother (1994) - Mostly Original Material** : Released in 1994, this album primarily featured original material, marking her return to original songs after covering standards and showtunes. It included the song "In The Sun." "After releasing an album where she covered standards and showtunes from the old days, Shenade put out Universal Mother in 1994."
**Throw Down Your Arms (2005) - Double Reggae Album** : Recorded in Jamaica with the production crew Sly & Robbie, this double reggae album allowed Sinead to finally record "War" by Bob Marley, a song that previously got her into trouble on *Saturday Night Live*. "In 2005, she released Throw Down Your Arms, a double reggae album recorded in Jamaica with the production crew of Slian Robby."

## songs
**Take My Hand (1984)** : Written by Sinead O'Connor at age 15 and recorded with the band Intuanoa. It became their first single, though Intuanoa eventually decided she was too young to be a permanent member. "This is called Take My Hand and it's from 1984. Shaneid was 15 at the time."
**Heroine (1986)** : This track was co-written and sung by Sinead O'Connor for the soundtrack of the movie *Captive*, with music composed by The Edge of U2. This collaboration marked an early and significant step in her burgeoning career, leading to her quick move to London. "The track is called Heroin and this is from 1986."
**Troy (1987)** : Written by Sinead O'Connor when she was 18, recorded at 19, and released at 20 on her debut album *The Lion and the Cobra*. The song was inspired by her mother and an affair with a minister. While it didn't perform well as a single, it became a beloved track for fans of the album. "It was inspired by both her mother and the affair with the minister. It's called Troy."
**Mandinka (1988)** : This was the second single released from her debut album, *The Lion and the Cobra*. It was instrumental in building her North American fan base, showcasing the raw emotion and power of her voice. "This was the second single from the record and was instrumental in building her North American fan base."
**Nothing Compares 2 U (1990)** : Originally demoed by Prince in 1984 and recorded by his funk band, The Family, in 1985, this song was suggested to Sinead by her manager Steve Fargnoli. Engineer Chris Birkett produced the track, which was recorded with a hi-hat loop, programmed strings, and notably no bass. Sinead recorded the vocal in one take using Birkett's "go-to mic," an AKG 414B, and then double-tracked it perfectly. The accompanying music video, mostly filmed in London and featuring genuine tears, helped propel the song to number one globally after initially struggling. "But the vocal went down extraordinarily in one take."
**Myles Stones (2022, unreleased)** : This song was written by Sinead O'Connor about her mental health battles, the therapy, institutionalizations, and trauma she experienced in the mid-2010s. She had intended for it to be on a new album she was working on shortly before her death in 2023. "She wrote this song about her mental health battles and the experiences she went through in the middle 2010s, with the therapy, the institutionalizations, and the trauma."

## curiosities
**Early Life and Abuse (1966-1980s)** : Born Sinead Marie Bernadette O'Connor in Dublin, she was the third of five children. Her mother, described as very strict and Catholic, frequently beat Sinead and her siblings, locking her in her room or a cupboard. This abuse, coupled with her mother's mental issues (leading to hospitalization after deliberately crashing a car), caused Sinead to rebel, skip school, and engage in shoplifting. "Mom was very strict in a scary, catholic sort of way, and often beat Shaneid in her siblings."
**Magdalene Asylum Stay (1980s)** : Due to her shoplifting, Sinead spent approximately 18 months in a Magdalene Asylum, specifically the Grinian Training Center, which were Irish institutions for "fallen women" and "uncontrollable daughters." Despite the harsh rules, punishments, and abuse from nuns, this period was significant for her as she began writing and performing music, with one nun, Sister Margaret, buying her first guitar. "Shaneid's pension for theft led to about 18 months in something called a Magdalene Asylum."
**Mother's Death and Identity Crisis (1985)** : On February 18, 1985, her mother died in a car crash on an icy road while on her way to Mass. This event profoundly affected Sinead, leaving her even more confused about her identity and her relationship with her deceased mother. "But then, on February 18, 1985, Mom died in a crash on her way to Mass."
**Shaved Head Protest (1987)** : While preparing for her debut album, Ensign Records strongly suggested she stop cutting her hair short and dress "more like a girl your age," with short skirts, babydolls, and jewelry. Angered by this, Sinead followed her manager's suggestion to shave her head, which she did the next day at a Greek barber, then marched to the record company to demand repayment for the haircut. "His response was, I think you should shave your head. So the next day, she did."
**Debut Album Self-Production (1987)** : Despite being 19, pregnant, and an unproven singer-songwriter, Sinead O'Connor fired the original producer of her debut album, *The Lion and the Cobra*, after just two days because they "didn't mesh." She then took over production herself, effectively "binning 100,000 pounds worth of studio time" and restarting the recording process. "The producer didn't mesh, so Shanade binned 100,000 pounds worth of studio time and started again with herself as producer, firing the original guy after just two days."
**Childbirth During Recording (1987)** : Sinead recorded the latter half of her debut album, *The Lion and the Cobra*, while she was pregnant. She gave birth to her son, Jake, with John Reynolds, the drummer in her band, when she was 20, three months before the album's release. "Oh, and she found out that she was pregnant. The father was John Reynolds, the drummer in her band."
**Collaborations and Film Role (Late 1980s)** : Between the ages of 16 and 20, Sinead's career accelerated rapidly. She co-wrote and recorded with The Edge, toured with INXS and David Bowie (with Andy Rourke and Mike Joyce of The Smiths in her band), had songs placed in movies, and starred in a Northern Irish film called *Hasha-Veibaby*. "She performed on David Letterman, participated in more collaborations with other artists, had her songs placed in movies. And she starred in a movie from Northern Ireland called Hasha-Veibaby."
**Grammy Boycott and Anti-Competition Stance (1990)** : When *I Do Not Want What I Haven't Got* was nominated for Grammys, Sinead boycotted the awards program, asserting, "I'm a punk, not a pop star. It shouldn't be a competition, it's about art." She made it a policy to refuse every award for which she was nominated or won, using the resulting controversy to draw attention to the issue of child abuse. "When I do not want what I haven't got was nominated for Grammys, she boycotted the awards program."
**Red Cross Donation and Move to England (1990)** : Following the negative backlash and accusations of "ungratefulness" for boycotting awards, Sinead donated her house in Hollywood to the Red Cross. This enabled a family of refugees from Somalia to live there, and she subsequently moved back to England. "She was so appalled at the way she was treated for this so-called ungratefulness that she gave her Hollywood house to the Red Cross."
**Star-Spangled Banner Controversy and Frank Sinatra Feud (1990)** : In 1990, Sinead declined to have "The Star-Spangled Banner" played before a show in New Jersey, which was misconstrued as a refusal to perform if it was played. Upon hearing this, Frank Sinatra famously declared his desire to "kick her in the ass." "And when Frank Sinatra heard that, he famously said he would like to, quote, kick her in the ass."
**Saturday Night Live Pope Photo Tearing (1992)** : On October 13, 1992, as a musical guest on *Saturday Night Live*, Sinead performed an a cappella version of Bob Marley's "War" instead of her scheduled second song. During the performance, she held up a picture of Pope John Paul II (found on her mother's wall after her death) and tore it to pieces, declaring, "Fight the real enemy." This act, which she stated was planned to destroy a symbol of "lies and liars and abuse" associated with the Catholic Church and her mother, generated immense controversy. "Instead, she held up her mom's picture of the Pope and tore it to pieces."
**SNL Fallout and CD Steamrolling (1992)** : The Pope photo incident led to immediate and intense backlash: she was egged outside Rockefeller Center, NBC received nearly 5,000 complaint calls, and she was banned from the network. A pile of her CDs and tapes were publicly steamrolled outside her New York record company's offices, an event she and a friend found amusing enough to attend in disguise and even be interviewed by a local reporter. "A pile of her CDs and tapes were literally steamrolled in front of the offices of her New York record company."
**Career Reassessment after SNL (1992)** : Sinead viewed the *Saturday Night Live* incident not as a derailment, but as a corrective course for her career. She believed having a number one record had "derailed" her, and tearing the photo put her "on the right track," allowing her to embrace her role as a protest singer and perform live without the pressures of pop stardom or compromise. "I feel that having a number one record derailed my career, and my tearing of the photo put me on the right track."
**Peter Gabriel Relationship and Music (Early 1990s)** : Sinead O'Connor was involved in a relationship with Peter Gabriel that included recording and touring, though it reportedly ended on a sour note. "There was a relationship with Peter Gabriel that involved recording and touring and seems to have ended on some kind of a sour note."
**Activism and Charity Work (1990s-2000s)** : Sinead continued her activism by working with the Red Hot and Blue organization, raising money for AIDS research and supporting people living with AIDS. She also actively spoke out on the political situation in Ireland, advocating for the reunification of North and South, and collaborated with artists like Dolly Parton and Mary J. Blige on related projects. "She continued her work with a red hot and blue organization, raising money for AIDS research and people living with AIDS, as well as speaking out on the political situation in Ireland, calling for the North and South to be reunited."
**Portrayal of Virgin Mary (1997)** : In 1997, Sinead O'Connor took on an acting role, playing the Virgin Mary in a movie titled *The Butcher Boy*. "In 1997, she played the Virgin Mary in a movie called The Butcher Boy."
**Complex Family Life (1991-2015)** : Sinead had four children with four different men and married only one of them. She divorced her first husband, John Reynolds (father of Jake), in 1991. She had a daughter, Róisín, with Irish journalist John Waters in 1995, leading to a long and contentious custody battle. Her son Shane was born in 2004 with musician Donal Lunny, which created a scandal due to Lunny being married at the time. Her fourth child, Jeshua, was born in 2006 with Frank Bonadio. She also experienced three miscarriages and one abortion, later becoming a grandmother in 2015 at age 49. "Family life, though, was complicated. Shenade had four children with four different men, only one of whom she married."
**Multiple Marriages (1991-2012)** : After divorcing John Reynolds in 1991, Sinead O'Connor married British journalist Nick Sommerlad in 2004, a union that lasted less than a year. She married musician and longtime collaborator Steve Cooney in 2010, also for less than a year. Her fourth marriage was a Las Vegas wedding to Irish psychotherapist Barry Herridge in 2012, reportedly lasting only a few weeks. "Her third marriage was in 2010 to Steve Cuny, a longtime friend and musical collaborator who played in her band."
**Drug Use and Alcohol Allergy** : Sinead used various drugs, including weed, a fair amount of acid, some ecstasy, a little cocaine, and smoked heroin once, with speed being her all-time favorite. She was allergic to alcohol and therefore did not drink. "She did a lot of drugs, a lot of weed, a fair amount of acid, a little ecstasy, a little cocaine. She smoked heroin once, but speed was her all-time favorite."
**Mental Health Struggles and Suicide Attempts (1993-2016)** : Sinead's mental health issues were public, including a denied suicide attempt involving sleeping pills in late 1993, and a definite attempt on her 33rd birthday (December 8, 1999) where she took 20 Valium pills following a custody court case involving her daughter Róisín. She was initially misdiagnosed with bipolar disorder, later receiving diagnoses of complex post-traumatic stress disorder and borderline personality disorder. In 2016, she disappeared from her Chicago suburb residence amidst rumors of a suicide attempt, but was located safely. "There was definitely a suicide attempt under 33rd birthday, which was December 8, 1999."
**Fibromyalgia and Retirement (2002)** : Around 2002, Sinead O'Connor suffered from fibromyalgia, which caused such severe pain and fatigue that she retired from music entirely for a period of two years. "Another problem was fibromyalgia, which said in sometime around 2002, the pain and the fatigue was so bad that she retired from music entirely for two years."
**Hysterectomy and Post-operative Issues (2015)** : In 2015, Sinead underwent a hysterectomy to address endometriosis. She attributed a subsequent "four-year breakdown" and suicidal ideation to the lack of post-operative treatment, specifically the absence of hormone replacement therapy. During this period, she also developed agoraphobia. "interstitially at a hysterectomy in 2015 to deal with endometriosis. And that, according to her and the lack of post-operative treatment, no hormone replacement therapy, made her suicidal."
**Rehab for Cannabis Dependence (2016)** : In 2016, Sinead O'Connor entered rehab in San Francisco with the aim of breaking her dependence on cannabis. "And also that year, she entered rehab in San Francisco to break her dependence on cannabis."
**Public Pleas for Mental Health Help (2017)** : In the summer of 2017, Sinead went public on Facebook from a Travelodge room in New Jersey, stating she was still suicidal and frequently in and out of hospital psych wards. She later made genuine attempts to destigmatize mental illness through appearances on TV shows like Oprah and Dr. Phil. "The following summer, Shanade went public on Facebook from her room at a travel lodge in New Jersey, saying that she was still suicidal with only her doctor and psychiatrist standing in her way."
**Conversion to Priesthood (1999)** : In 1999, Sinead O'Connor met Bishop Michael Cox of the Latin Tridentine Order, a rebel offshoot of the Catholic Church not recognized by Rome. He ordained her a priest, and she adopted the name Mother Bernadette Marie, an event that generated vicious press coverage. "In 1999, she met Bishop Michael Cox of the Tindan Dean Order, a rebel offshoot of the Catholic Church not recognized by Rome. He ordained her a priest, and she took the name Mother Bernadette Marie."
**Criticism of Vatican and Catholic Church (1999-2018)** : For 15 years after her ordination as a priest, Sinead O'Connor consistently attacked the traditional Catholic Church, referring to the Vatican as a "nest of devils" and advocating for Catholics to create an alternative church to reclaim control from Rome. She wrote to at least three popes, requesting formal excommunication. "For the next 15 years, she continued to retax on the traditional Catholic Church, calling the Vatican a, quote, nest of devils, and the Catholics should rise up and create an alternative church to rest control away from Rome."
**Conversion to Islam (2018)** : In October 2018, after studying both Christian and Judaic texts, Sinead O'Connor converted to Islam, adopting the name Shuhada Davitt. This conversion was followed by some "inflammatory tweets directed at non-Muslims," for which she later apologized. "Then in October 2018, after studying both Christian and Judaic texts, she converted to Islam, taking the name Shuhada Davit."
**Son Shane's Death and Her Hospitalization (2022)** : On January 7, 2022, the body of her son, Shane, from whom she had lost custody in 2013, was found after he had taken his own life. A week later, after posting a series of tweets indicating she intended to follow him, Sinead was voluntarily hospitalized. "On January 7, 2022, the body of her son, Shane, someone she'd lost custody of in 2013 was found. He had taken his own life."
**Incomplete Memoir and Final Projects (2023)** : Sinead O'Connor authored an "excellent yet incomplete" memoir titled *Remembrings*, which sold very well. At the time of her death, she had an album of new material "almost finished" and was planning tour dates for 2024 and 2025. "She wrote an excellent yet incomplete memoir called Remembrings That Sold Very Well."
**Death (July 26, 2023)** : Sinead O'Connor passed away on July 26, 2023, at approximately 11:20 AM, found unresponsive in her flat on the south side of London. Police pronounced her dead at the scene, and her death was not considered suspicious. She was 56 years old. "But at about 1120 in the morning of July 26, 2023, police were called to her flat. She was found unresponsive and pronounced dead at the scene."
**Anti-Exploitation Stance** : Sinead O'Connor consistently spoke out against the patriarchy of the recorded music business and the exploitation of women. She believed in female liberation and used her art and public persona to convey her message without compromise. "She did not believe in the exploitation of women. She did not agree with it."
**Comparison to Other Female Artists (1990s)** : Unlike artists such as Alanis Morissette, Liz Phair, and Courtney Love, Sinead O'Connor was viewed as too intense to be "contained and marketed and categorized." Her fierce independence and uncompromising nature set her apart, and her influence in the 1990s was widespread. "Unlike, say, Alanis Morissette, Liz Fair, Courtney Love, and a few others, Shanade could never be contained and marketed and categorized."

# label - Ensign Records

## curiosities
**Signing Sinead O'Connor (1985)** : Ensign Records contacted Sinead O'Connor two weeks after her mother's death, having heard about her talent. Following successful demos with Carl Wallinger, she signed a deal with the label on August 5, 1985, at the age of 18. "Two weeks after her mom died, a label called Ensign Records called. We heard a lot about you, they said, and we'd like to make more demos with you with Carl Wallinger."
**Creative Control Dispute (1990)** : Ensign Records initially resisted releasing Sinead O'Connor's second album, *I Do Not Want What I Haven't Got*, believing it was "too personal" and wouldn't connect with a broad audience. However, her contract granted her complete creative control, and she forced them to release the album, which subsequently became a massive global hit. Nigel Grainge, an executive at Ensign Records, was instrumental in securing Chris Birkett as producer after Sinead fired the initial producer. "Believe it or not, though, her record company didn't want to release it. Well, this is too personal, they said. It won't connect with anyone."

# instrument - AKG 414B

## curiosities
**"Nothing Compares 2 U" Vocal Recording (1990)** : Engineer Chris Birkett used his "go-to mic," an AKG 414B, to record Sinead O'Connor's vocal for "Nothing Compares 2 U." She flawlessly sang the entire vocal in one take and then perfectly double-tracked it using the same microphone. "I put up my go-to mic with 414B, AKG, which I use all the time. And she nailed the whole vocal in one take."

# curiosity

**Magdalene Asylum - Irish Institutions (18th Century)** : These unique Irish institutions, originating in the 18th century, were established to house and "reform" what were termed "fallen women," including unwed mothers, prostitutes, and "uncontrollable daughters." Characterized by strict rules, punishments, and abuse by nuns, they were places of confinement, but for some, like Sinead O'Connor, they also became unexpected settings for self-discovery and artistic development. "These were uniquely Irish institutions, born in the 18th century that dealt with, well, it's called them fallen women, unwed mothers, prostitutes and so-called uncontrollable daughters."
**Rapp Award Grammy Controversy (1990)** : The Rapp Award, a category at the Grammy Awards, garnered controversy in 1990 when it was not televised. Sinead O'Connor protested this decision by having the Public Enemy logo shaved into her head, ensuring her protest would be visible whenever she appeared on television. "When the Rapp Award wasn't televised during the Grammy Awards, she had the public enemy logo shaved into her head, so it would be seen by people anytime she was on TV."
**Catholic Church Sexual Abuse Revelations (Early 1990s)** : The early 1990s marked the beginning of public revelations concerning widespread sexual abuse within the Catholic Church. Sinead O'Connor's controversial act of tearing a picture of Pope John Paul II on *Saturday Night Live* in 1992 was a highly visible, early protest against these abuses, representing "lies and liars and abuse" associated with the Church. "These were the early days in the revelations of sexual abuse in the Catholic Church, and not everybody was ready to hear the truth, especially from this weird, angry, bald Irish woman."
