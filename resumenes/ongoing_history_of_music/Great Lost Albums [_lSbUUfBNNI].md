**Songs from the Black Hole (1994-1995) - Unfinished Concept Album** : After the success of their 1994 debut record, Rivers Cuomo conceived this science fiction rock opera, set in the year 2126. **Rat Patrol from Fort Bragg (1982) - Unreleased Double Album** : This was the working title for The Clash's intended follow-up to *Sandinista!* in 1981, initially planned as another double album. Recorded in New York, the project was hampered by creative clashes between Mick Jones (who favored longer songs heavily influenced by Dub, Reggae, and world music) and Joe Strummer (who preferred a tight, single album to manage label debt). Mick Jones's vision was a 15-track, 65-minute double album that the rest of the band disliked. It was ultimately re-sequenced and cut down by producer Glenn Johns into *Combat Rock*. "The working title of this record was Rat Patrol from Fort Bragg... Mick presented his vision of the project. A 15-track 65-minute double album entitled Rat Patrol from Fort Bragg. Mick thought it was marvelous. The rest of the band hated it."
**Combat Rock (1982) - Studio Album** : Released on May 14, 1982, this album was the result of producer Glenn Johns cutting, editing, and re-sequencing the material from the *Rat Patrol from Fort Bragg* sessions. This was Joe Strummer's preferred outcome, a "nice tight single album" that CBS could effectively promote, contrasting with Mick Jones's original double album vision. "In April 1982, producer Glenn Johns was brought in to sort it all out. He cut and edited and re-sequenced the whole thing. And the final result was an album called Combat Rock, which was released on May 14, 1982."

**Mellon Collie and the Infinite Sadness - Sprawling Album** : This album was a successful "sprawling album" by the Smashing Pumpkins, which their record company referenced when advising against another similar-length record for their fifth album. "It was like, look guys, you did find with Melon Colley in the Infinite Sadness, so take your weddings and just run."
**Adore (1998) - Commercial Flop** : Released in 1998, *Adore* was the album preceding *Glass and the Machines of God*. It was considered "something of a commercial flop," which made the record company reluctant to support another ambitious, potentially double album project. "especially since the last album, Adore, which came out in 1998, was something of a commercial flop."
**Glass and the Machines of God (1999) - Unfinished Rock Opera** : Intended as the Smashing Pumpkins' fifth and final studio record in 1999, this project was envisioned as a giant rock opera song cycle. It revolved around a rock star named Zero (renamed Glass by God), his true love June, and their collective rise, fall, and redemption. Band members were to assume these characters, with live performances presented as theater. The album was abandoned before completion due to bassist D'arcy Wretzky's departure (or firing), Chris James Eha's disengagement, and the record company's reluctance to release another sprawling album, especially after *Adore* was a commercial flop. "Its proposed title was Glass and the Machines of God. Never heard of it? Not surprised... The best guess is that glass and the Machines of God would have been divided into two halves of 14 songs each."
**Machina, the Machines of God (2000) - Salvaged Album** : This album was released in 2000, collecting the "best material" from the abandoned *Glass and the Machines of God* project in an attempt to salvage something from the effort. It won a Grammy for best artwork and was promoted with an early viral online marketing campaign encouraging fans to decipher its themes and storylines, though the campaign was abandoned unfinished. Despite critical re-evaluation, it became the second lowest-selling album in Pumpkins history. "In the end, Glass and the Machines of God was abandoned. The best material was then collected into a record called Machina, the Machines of God, in an attempt to salvage something, anything from the effort... Want a Grammy? For best artwork."
**Machina II/The Friends & Enemies of Modern Music (2000) - Free Online Release** : Released in the fall of 2000, this record featured much of the remaining tracks from the *Glass and the Machines of God* project. It was notably given away for free online, a pioneering move at the time, and had an extremely limited physical release of only 25 copies, whose whereabouts are unknown. "In the fall of 2000, the Pumpkins released a record called Machina 2, the Friends and Enemies of Modern Music. It featured much of the rest of the tracks from glass and the Machines of God, and was given away for free online. Again, nobody was doing this kind of thing back then, so it was really cool."

# curiosity

**Lost Albums Phenomenon** : Many artists and groups have made, or attempted to make, albums that were never officially released for various reasons, such as creative abandonment, group breakups, or record label intervention. These "lost albums" often sit in vaults or on hard drives, with some remaining completely lost while others have physical evidence of their existence. "Plenty of artists and groups have made or tried to make albums that for a host of reasons never ever saw the light of day. Sometimes things just weren't working out and they were abandoned."
**Wonderland Amusement Park - Unfinished Project** : Located north of Beijing in China, Wonderland amusement park was designed to attract millions of visitors. However, investors pulled out in 1998, leaving 120 acres of a "half finished, abandoned, fairy tale themed amusement park" empty. This serves as an analogy for creative projects, like albums, that are started but never completed or released. "But then in 1998, investors pulled out. 120 acres of half finished, abandoned, fairy tale themed amusement park. Empty. Very weird."
**Record Label Discretion on Releases** : Even with a record contract, a record label is not obligated to release an artist's record. This can be a reason why albums remain unreleased. "And believe it or not, just because you have a record contract doesn't mean your record label has to release your record."
