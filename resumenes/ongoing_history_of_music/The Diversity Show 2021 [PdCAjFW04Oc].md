Dannis Hackney

## genres

## curiosities
**Formation and Early Sound** : Formed in Detroit in 1971 by two brothers and a friend, who were black men. The band initially started as a funk band.
**Musical Epiphany** : After seeing The Who and Alice Cooper, the band had an epiphany that shifted their musical direction.
**Ahead of Their Time** : Despite their talent, Death was considered too far ahead of their time, leading to their breakup in 1977 and subsequent obscurity.
**Rediscovery and Reissue** : The band was rediscovered when the three sons of one of the members, forming a band called Rough Frances, found their father's previous musical life online. This led to a reissue of the original tapes in 2009.
**Pre-Punk Pioneers** : Retroactively, Death is considered one of the most important pre-punk bands in America.

## curiosities

# artist - The Specials

## members
Jerry Dammers (keyboards)
(mixed black and white musicians, some Caribbean immigrants)

## genres
Ska
Reggae
Dub
Rock Steady
Post-Punk

## curiosities
**Formation Context** : Formed amidst racial tensions and the rise of the hard-right National Front in the UK during the 1970s, which led to street demonstrations, rioting, and injuries in British cities.
**Founding Vision** : Formed by white keyboardist Jerry Dammers, who was captivated by the music West Indian immigrants brought to Coventry, especially reggae, dub, rock steady, and ska.
**Anti-Racism Inspiration** : Dammers hated the racism he observed in his multi-racial city and was inspired by the Rock Against Racism movement that began in 1978.
**Integrated Lineup** : From its inception, the band was designed to be integrated, always featuring a mix of black and white musicians, including some Caribbean immigrants.
**Band Logo and Iconography** : The band's logo featured a stylized Jamaican rude boy named Walt Japsco, depicted with a black suit, white shirt, black tie, pork pie hat, white socks, and black shoes. Walt Japsco was based on a photograph of Jamaican musician Peter Tosh, formerly of Bob Marley's Wailers.
**Message of Harmony** : The Specials consistently preached racial and social harmony during their performances.
**Ska Revival** : They were instrumental in introducing ska into the post-punk world.

## albums
**(Debut Album) (1979)** : Their self-titled debut album.

## songs
**A Message to You, Rudy (1979)** : A cover of the 1967 Jamaican song "Rudy, A Message to You" by Dandy Livingstone. This track is from their debut album.

## curiosities
**Genre Blending Pioneers** : Recognized as one of the most important groups for their innovative blending of punk, funk, ska, hard rock, soul, and elements of hip hop and rap.
**Formation and Early Sound** : Formed in 1979 by four black musicians from South Central Los Angeles, later growing to six members. Their music was socially conscious, anti-racist, clever, witty, and generally fun.
**Influence on Other Bands** : Played alongside punk bands and were particularly admired by a group that eventually formed the Red Hot Chili Peppers. The Beastie Boys later recruited Fishbone to open one of their tours.
**Festival Presence** : Were main stage performers on the 1993 Lollapalooza tour.
**Peak Years** : Their most impactful period spanned from 1988 to 1994.
**Personnel Problems** : The band experienced internal issues, including one member quitting to join a religious cult. When another member attempted to retrieve him, that member was charged with attempted kidnapping.
**Label Disputes** : Faced various record company problems.
**Legal Troubles** : Sued by a woman in Philadelphia for $1.4 million after one of the members stage-dived on top of her.
**Enduring Legacy** : Despite challenges, Fishbone perseveres and maintains a fanatical following. They are cited as a significant influence by bands such as No Doubt, Sublime, 311, Goldfinger, Rage Against the Machine, and Faith No More.
**Pioneering Status** : Described as all-black punk-funk pioneers.

# artist - Body Count

## members
Ice-T
Ernie C. (guitar)
(various high school friends from Los Angeles)

## genres
Metal
Rap
Thrash Metal
Hip Hop
Nu Metal
Rock

## curiosities
**Ice-T's Transition** : Formed by Ice-T, who was primarily known as a gangster rapper before this project.
**Rock Influences** : Ice-T's personal rock influences included Led Zeppelin, Black Sabbath, and Judas Priest, and he appreciated the speed of thrash metal, with Slayer being a favorite.
**Formation** : Ice-T discussed forming a metal band on the side with his friend, guitarist Ernie C., around 1989. They gathered a group of friends they had gone to high school with in Los Angeles.
**Band Ethos** : Ice-T described Body Count as "a rock band with a rap mentality," focusing on social and political issues such as racism, police brutality, and inner-city crime.
**Live Performance Approach** : Typically, their live sets were split, with half devoted to Body Count material and the other half to hip-hop.
**Audience Bridge** : This approach served to introduce hip-hop to white audiences and metal to black audiences.
**African American Roots of Rock** : Ice-T often emphasized that rock music originated with African American artists like Chuck Berry and Little Richard.
**Controversy from Black Audiences** : Ice-T was accused by some of his black audiences of "selling out" by venturing into rock music.
**Controversy from White Audiences** : The presence of an all-black metal band was not always appreciated by white audiences.
**"Cop Killer" Controversy** : Their song "Cop Killer" sparked significant controversy, being widely interpreted as an attack on police forces. This ignited a major First Amendment debate in the U.S., which shared many elements with the later Black Lives Matter movement.
**Corporate Pressure** : The controversy became so intense that shareholders threatened to withdraw all their money from Warner Brothers Records if the label did not address the issues surrounding Body Count.
**Enduring Impact** : Despite the controversies, the band continued and was a significant force in the emergence of nu metal in the mid to late 1990s.
**Musical Lineage** : A direct line can be drawn from Body Count to bands such as Korn, Slipknot, and Limp Bizkit.
**Ice-T's Acting Career** : Ice-T is also known for his extensive acting career, including his role on "Law & Order" and dozens of other acting gigs.
