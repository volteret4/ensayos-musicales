## albums
**Gorillaz (2001)** : The debut album, recorded in both London and Jamaica, featuring an experimental sound blending trip-hop, alt-rock, Brit-pop, dub, reggae, and hip-hop. Its release was heralded by the single "Tomorrow Comes Today" and launched Phase 1: Celebrity Take Down. The album sold approximately 1 million copies in the UK, over 2 million in North America, and more than 7 million worldwide. "That single propelled the album to sales of about a million at home in the UK, over two million in North America, and beyond seven million worldwide, which is not a bad start for a fake band."
**Demon Days (2005)** : The second Gorillaz album, released on May 11, 2005, following the reopening of their website in December 2004. Produced by Danger Mouse, it featured a dark theme about the dangers of ego and a world descending into darkness, partly inspired by Damon Albarn's train trip from Beijing to Mongolia. The album explored hip-hop influences and followed a musical arc from calm to progressively darker, selling at least 8 million copies worldwide. "As successful as the first album was, this one was even bigger, moving at least 8 million copies around the world."
**Plastic Beach (2010)** : The third album, initially titled "Carousel," was released on March 3, 2010. Its final title and concept were inspired by Damon Albarn's depression over plastic trash on a beach, tying into the environmental issue of the Great Pacific Garbage Patch. The album's music was designed to be much more pop-oriented than its predecessors and featured an extensive list of collaborators. "As for the music, it's much more pop than the first two records. That was by design from the outset."
**The Fall (2010)** : A fan club album recorded entirely by Damon Albarn on his iPad during the 2010 World Tour, released during the "Phase 4: Humans After All" era. "This era included a fan club album entitled The Fall, which was recorded entirely by Damon on his iPad during that 2010 World Tour."
**Humans (2017)** : The fifth Gorillaz album, released on April 28, 2017, after a seven-year hiatus and a prolonged social media buildup. It featured numerous collaborators, with some notable artists declining to participate or having their contributions unused. "After seven long years, the fifth album was finally ready. Humans was released on April 28th, 2017 after a long, long buildup on social media."

## songs
**Tomorrow Comes Today (2000)** : The first single released by Gorillaz on November 27, 2000, serving as a test for the band's concept. It aligned with the prevalent trip-hop sounds of the era and marked the beginning of "Phase 1: Celebrity Take Down." "The first ever Gorilla single, Tomorrow Comes Today, released as a standalone on November 27, 2000."
**Clint Eastwood (2001)** : The first single from the band's debut album, released on March 27, 2001, named after the American actor Clint Eastwood, and subtly incorporating themes from his 1966 film "The Good, The Bad, and The Ugly." Del the Funky Homosapien contributed rap vocals, appearing as "Del the ghost rapper," one of the spirits inhabiting Russell Hobbs, and is depicted as a blue phantom in the video. "The first single from the album was Clint Eastwood. And yes, it's about the American actor."
**Feel Good Inc. (2005)** : The first single from the "Demon Days" album, featuring additional contributions from De La Soul. It earned a Grammy Award for Best Pop Collaboration. "On the Magrammy Award for Best Pop Collaboration 2, the whole Demon Days project came together as Jamie Hilo was trying to come up with a script for Gorilla's movie, meanwhile Damon was off recording the Think Tank album with Blur."
**Dare (2005)** : A song from the "Demon Days" album featuring Shaun Ryder of Happy Mondays. Originally intended to be titled "It's There," the name was changed to "Dare" because Ryder's thick Mancunian accent made "It's There" sound like "Dare" during recording. "The song was supposed to be called It's There, but because Sean had such a thick man and coony an accent, nobody could understand what he was saying when he said, it's there."
**Kids With Guns (2005)** : One of four singles from the "Demon Days" album, featuring singer Neneh Cherry. It was part of a collaboration project stemming from the "Search for a Star" talent contest, where three winners contributed animation, remixes, or artwork. "Gorillas with Kids with Guns featuring singer Nina Cherry, and that's one of the four singles from the Demon Days album."
**Stylo (2010)** : The first single released from the "Plastic Beach" album. "Steelo or Stylo from Gorillas, one of the singles from the third album, Plastic Beach, which was released on March 3, 2010."

## curiosities
**Guinness World Record** : Gorillaz are listed in the Guinness Book of World Records as the most successful virtual band of all time, having achieved hit singles, multiple albums, and tours despite their members being fictional. "They're even listed in the Guinness Book of World Records as the most successful virtual band of all time."
**Creation and Concept** : Formed by Damon Albarn and Jamie Hewlett in 1997, the band originated from their observations of MTV artists' perceived vacuity and lack of substance. The idea was to create a "fake band" with fictional characters to comment on contemporary music. "Bloody hell, somebody said, we should just form a fake band. You make some music, all draw the characters."
**Original Name** : The band was initially called "Gorilla" before the "Z" was added to the name for aesthetic reasons, as it "looked cooler." "Damon and Jamie first called the band Gorilla, but then they later added the Z because it looked cooler."
**Phase 1: Celebrity Take Down** : This was the first era in Gorillaz's history, marked by the release of their debut single "Tomorrow Comes Today" in 2000. It represented the band's perceived "war on the celebrity culture of the era." "This marked the beginning of an era in Gorilla's history known as Phase 1 Celebrity Take Down, which I guess was basically the band's war on the celebrity culture of the era."
**First Live Performances (2001-2002)** : Early live shows involved 3D animation projected onto a screen across the stage, with a real-life band performing hidden behind it. The first performance was in London in March 2001, followed by tours in the UK, Paris, Japan, and North America. "At first, live performances were conducted by using 3D animation on a screen that stretched across the stage, accompanied by a real-life band set up behind the screen."
**Lore of Phases** : The concept of "phases" is central to Gorillaz lore, representing different eras or steps in the group's journey towards "world domination." "The idea of phases is very important to Gorilla's lore. These are the steps, the eras that the group has taken towards world domination."
**Phase 2: Slowboat to Hades** : This era followed the debut album, with the band's website shutting down for nearly a year and Kong Studios appearing abandoned, guided by a robot. It concluded after the release and success of the "Demon Days" album. "After shutting down the website for what must have been close to a year, you could still go there, but Kong Studios was closed."
**Failed Movie Project** : Jamie Hewlett worked on a script for a Gorillaz movie during the "Demon Days" period, with interest from Harvey Weinstein and Terry Gilliam, and a working title "Bananas" (spelled with a Z). However, the movie never materialized, though ideas were integrated into the album, songs, and music videos, with fans eventually receiving an online documentary. "The movie never happened, but ideas were integrated into the album, the songs, and the music videos."
**Search for a Star Contest** : A talent contest launched during the "Demon Days" era, inviting global entries of minute-long audio, video, or image files. The prize was a chance to collaborate with the band and have a virtual room in Kong Studios. While the virtual room never happened, collaborations, such as for the song "Kids With Guns," did occur. "The prize was a chance to collaborate with the band and have your own virtual room inside Kong Studios."
**Phase 3: Escape to Plastic Beach** : This era was associated with the "Plastic Beach" album. The Gorillaz headquarters became "Plastic Island" in the Pacific Trash Vortex, modeled after Tracy Island from "Thunderbirds," and the website offered a virtual tour of this island. "Plastic Island was the new Gorillas headquarters. It was out in the middle of the Pacific Trash Vortex."
**2010 World Tour Performance Method** : During their first "proper" world tour in 2010, the nine-person live band (including Mick Jones and Paul Simonon) performed in front of a screen with visuals projected behind them, a shift from their earlier method of hiding behind the screen. "Instead of hiding behind a screen, like the first tour, they were out front with visuals projected on the screen behind them."
**Phase 4: Humans After All** : This era followed the "Plastic Beach" period and included the release of "The Fall" album. "And after Resolve done, it was Time 4 Phase 4, which was called Humans After All."
**Damon Albarn and Jamie Hewlett Dispute (Post-2010)** : After "The Fall" album, Damon Albarn and Jamie Hewlett had a falling out and did not work together for almost four years. Hewlett reportedly felt his comic creations were taking a backseat to the music's increasing scale. "Jamie, the music was getting bigger while his comic creations were taking more of a back seat. Didn't like that very much."
**Character Lore During Hiatus (c. 2015)** : During the band's hiatus, the characters became active on social media and a virtual book explained their individual stories: Murdoc was captured by a record company and forced to make an album; Noodle returned to Japan and took over the Yakuza; 2D was swallowed by a whale named Massive Dick; and Russell, who had become a giant, washed up on a North Korean beach, was imprisoned, starved, and shrunk back to normal size. "Murdoch had been captured by the record company and was ordered to make another album under duress."
**Music's Daring Nature** : Due to their nature as cartoon characters, Gorillaz as a manufactured band has been able to produce music described as "daring and interesting" over two decades, suggesting their fictional status allows for greater creative freedom than real human artists. "because cartoons can get away with more than real humans can, the music we've heard from this particular manufactured band has turned out to be some of the most daring and interesting at the last 20 years."

## curiosities
**Co-Creator of Gorillaz** : Along with Jamie Hewlett, Damon Albarn is one of the two human creators at the center of Gorillaz, responsible for the music of the virtual band. "The two humans at the center of all this are Damon Albarn of Blur, a very good musician with a pedigree and track record, and Jamie Hewlett."
**Inspiration for Gorillaz** : While sharing a London flat with Jamie Hewlett in 1997, Damon Albarn watched MTV and was struck by the perceived vacuity and lack of substance in many contemporary artists, leading to the idea of forming a "fake band." "The TV was on MTV a lot and both of them were fascinated by how vacuous so many of the artists were, how empty the music was, how little substance there seemed to be."
**Blur's Influence on Gorillaz's Sound** : Touring the world with Blur exposed Damon Albarn to diverse musical influences, which he wanted to experiment with. Gorillaz provided the perfect vehicle for this experimentation, as Blur was not the appropriate outlet for these new sounds. "Damon says that touring the world with blur brought him to contact with all kinds of music. He wanted to fiddle with these new influences, but blur wasn't really the place to do that."
**Demon Days Inspiration** : The thematic depth of the "Demon Days" album, which explored themes of ego and a dark world, was partly informed by a train trip Damon Albarn took from Beijing to Mongolia. "concepts that were released partly informed by a train trip Damon Albarn took from Beijing to Mongolia."
**Recording "The Fall" on an iPad** : Damon Albarn entirely recorded the 2010 fan club album "The Fall" on his iPad while on the Gorillaz World Tour. "This era included a fan club album entitled The Fall, which was recorded entirely by Damon on his iPad during that 2010 World Tour."
**Dispute with Jamie Hewlett (Post-2010)** : After 2010, Damon Albarn and Jamie Hewlett had a dispute and stopped working together for approximately four years, reportedly because Hewlett felt his comic creations were sidelined as the music gained prominence. "Surely after that, Damon and Jamie had some kind of dispute and weren't talking to each other for a while."
**Feud with Noel Gallagher** : Damon Albarn and Noel Gallagher were once considered "mortal enemies," but this rivalry had ended by the time of the "Humans" album, with Gallagher contributing to the record, though his vocals were ultimately unused without malice. "He and Damon used to be mortal enemies, but that's all in the past."

# artist - Jamie Hewlett

## member of
(None explicitly mentioned)

## curiosities
**Co-Creator of Gorillaz** : Jamie Hewlett is one of the two human creators of Gorillaz, a comic book artist responsible for creating the band's fictional characters and visual universe. "Now he's not a musician. He's a comic book artist, which in this case is just as important a role as being the music guy."
**Early Career** : In the late 1980s, Jamie Hewlett worked for the British magazine "Deadline," known for comic strips and drawings. He co-created the strip "Tank Girl" with Alan Martin. "By the late 1980s, he was working for a British magazine called Deadline, which featured comic strips and drawings by a variety of English artists."
**Tank Girl Movie** : His comic strip "Tank Girl" was optioned for a movie, which was made in 1995. Prior to this, Steven Spielberg had expressed interest in working with Hewlett. "It was option for a movie that was made in 1995."
**Album Artwork** : Hewlett's "Tank Girl" work led to commissions for album artwork, including the EP "You Got It!" by the British group Senseless Things, featuring a distinctive drawing of an alien. "He and his buddy, Alan Martin, had their own strip called Tank Girl."
**Diverse Creative Projects** : Beyond comics, Hewlett designed nightclub decor, opened a clothing store, wrote for a computer magazine, and created a comic strip about a gay Buddhist kung fu cop. "He also designed the decor for a nightclub. He opened a clothing store. He wrote for a computer magazine."
**Pulp's "Common People" Comic Strip** : In 1995, Hewlett illustrated a comic strip based on the story of Pulp's song "Common People," which was included with the French version of the single and an Australian box set. This connection highlights his acquaintanceship with Pulp's Jarvis Cocker. "He also illustrated a comic strip in 1995 that was based entirely on the story told in this song by Pulp."
**Inspiration for Gorillaz Characters** : While sharing a London flat with Damon Albarn in 1997, Hewlett, inspired by MTV's perceived lack of substance, created the four fictional members of Gorillaz: 2D, Murdoc, Noodle, and Russell. "Jamie came up with 2D, Murdoch, Noodle and Russell."
**Aging of Characters** : Hewlett became "a little bored with the characters" and decided to age them up, most notably Noodle, who transitioned from a 10-year-old girl to a 17-year-old teen. "Jamie had grown a little bored with the characters, so he decided to age them up a bit."
**Dispute with Damon Albarn (Post-2010)** : Following "The Fall" album, Jamie Hewlett and Damon Albarn had a falling out for about four years. Hewlett reportedly felt that the music was becoming too prominent, overshadowing his comic creations, leading to his dissatisfaction. "The Jamie, the music was getting bigger while his comic creations were taking more of a back seat. Didn't like that very much."

# curiosity

**Virtual Artists (General)** : Before Gorillaz, a variety of virtual artists and performers existed, including the Archies, Josie and the Pussycats, Dr. Teeth and the Electric Mayhem (from The Muppets), Death Clock, The Chipmunks, Prozac, Crazy Frog, Banana Splits, Miss Tula (from the Philippines, represented by female dolls), The Bots (CG creations), and Hatsune Miku (Japanese hologram), as well as Jem and the Holograms. "Back in the 1960s, we had the Archies, who were followed by Josie and the pussycats, then Dr. Teeth and the Electric Mayhem on the Muppets."
**Kong Studios** : The fictional headquarters of Gorillaz, located "high in a mountain in Essex, which is northeast of London." Despite not existing in reality, its appearance could be viewed on gorillas.com. It was depicted as closed and abandoned during the "Slowboat to Hades" phase, with a robot guiding visitors around. "By the way, the gorillas headquarters is called Kong Studios. It's supposedly high in a mountain in Essex, which is northeast of London."
**Gorillaz.com** : The official website for Gorillaz, which at one point allowed users to see what Kong Studios looked like. It was temporarily shut down or abandoned during Phase 2 (Slowboat to Hades), but reappeared in December 2004 to announce the "Demon Days" album. During Phase 3 (Escape to Plastic Beach), it offered a full tour of the virtual Plastic Island. "We can, however, see what it looks like at gorillas.com."
**Great Pacific Garbage Patch / Pacific Trash Vortex** : According to environmental legends, this is an area where Pacific Ocean currents collect waterborne plastic pollution. It served as a significant inspiration for the Gorillaz album "Plastic Beach," with "Plastic Island" becoming the band's new headquarters within this vortex. "Now according to environmental legends, Pacific Ocean currents drive all kinds of waterborne plastic pollution into an area that's become known as the Great Pacific Garbage Patch or the Pacific Trash Vortex."
**Thunderbirds (TV Show)** : The fictional "Plastic Island" headquarters for Gorillaz, depicted in the "Plastic Beach" album, was explicitly modeled after "Tracy Islands" from the classic Supermarionation TV show "Thunderbirds." "It was modeled after Tracy Islands. Now, if you know anything about the old Super Mario Nation TV show called Thunderbirds, you know exactly what Damon and Jamie were thinking about as this album came together."
**The Good, The Bad, and The Ugly (1966 film)** : This Clint Eastwood film, released in 1966, provided thematic and musical inspiration for the Gorillaz song "Clint Eastwood." Bits of the film's theme are audible throughout the song. "He started that movie back in 1966. And as far as anybody knows, Clint Eastwood has never actually commented on the song, but he certainly never complained about it either."
**How to Write a Hit Song (Book)** : Rapper Del the Funky Homosapien claimed he used tips from this book to help write the Gorillaz song "Clint Eastwood," purchasing it with a coupon from his mother. "Del the funky homo sapien says he helped write the song using tips from a book called, How to Write a Hit Song, which she bought thanks to a coupon his mom gave him."
**London Gospel Choir** : The London Gospel Choir contributed to the Gorillaz album "Demon Days," along with a children's choir, adding to the diverse range of collaborators on the project. "and even the London Gospel Choir along with the Children's Choir."
**Children's Choir** : A children's choir contributed to the Gorillaz album "Demon Days," alongside the London Gospel Choir. "and even the London Gospel Choir along with the Children's Choir."
**Yakuza** : In Gorillaz lore, during the band's hiatus, the character Noodle returned to Japan and took over the Yakuza. "Nudel went back to Japan and took over the Yakuza."
**Massive Dick (Whale)** : In Gorillaz lore, the character 2D was swallowed by a whale named Massive Dick during the band's hiatus. "2D was swallowed by a whale called Massive Dick and Russell, who ended up becoming a giant as part of the plastic beach story."
**North Korea (Russell Hobbs lore)** : According to Gorillaz lore, the character Russell Hobbs, after becoming a giant as part of the "Plastic Beach" story, washed up on a North Korean beach where he was imprisoned and nearly starved, an ordeal that eventually shrunk him back to normal size. "Washed up on the shore of some North Korean beach where he was imprisoned and starved nearly to death."
**Deadline Magazine** : A British magazine from the late 1980s that featured comic strips and drawings by various English artists, including Jamie Hewlett, who co-created "Tank Girl" for the publication. "By the late 1980s, he was working for a British magazine called Deadline, which featured comic strips and drawings by a variety of English artists."
