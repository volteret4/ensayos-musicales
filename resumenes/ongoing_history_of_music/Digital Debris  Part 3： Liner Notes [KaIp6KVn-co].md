"When you listen to music through a screaming music service, how aware are you of what you're actually listening to?"
**Disappearing Music Features** : The digital era has seen the disappearance of traditional liner notes along with other concepts like B-sides, bonus tracks, and album artwork. This transformation is viewed as part of the ongoing evolution of music culture, impacting how listeners engage with and learn about music. "But as we get deeper and deeper into the digital era, liner notes are disappearing. Along with the concepts of B-sides and bonus tracks and album artwork, it's all part of the evolution of music culture."
**Discogs.com as Music Resource** : Discogs.com, sometimes referred to as "Discox," is recognized as a very good online resource for providing basic information about albums, songs, and artists. It is noted for obtaining clean, accurate data directly from record labels, which is crucial for its reliability. "There's Discox.com, which does a very good job of providing the basics."
**Dynaflex Recording Technology** : Dynaflex recording was a new development in record manufacturing promoted by RCA for albums like David Bowie's "The Man Who Sold the World" in 1971. This lightweight record technology aimed to provide a smoother, quieter surface, improve sound quality, and virtually eliminate warpage and turntable slippage. "RCA was very proud to mention that this album was a dino-flex recording, a new development and record manufacturing that provides a smoother quieter surface and improves sound quality to reproduce a musical sound."
**Evolution of Album Packaging and Notes** : The concept of album text originated in the early 20th century with flat rotating discs, which were initially sold in plain packaging. The practice evolved from printed music programs for symphonies and operas, first appearing as basic information on fancy "record albums" (packages of multiple discs), then as ads on inner sleeves (sleeve notes), before the term "liner notes" became popular with the advent of the single-package LP. "This goes back to the beginning of the 20th century and the very, very start of the recorded music industry, specifically when we started using flat rotating discs to store our music."
**Fella Production Company** : Fella is a music video production company co-founded by Director X and Taj Khrushlo, which gained a significant breakthrough with the music video for DJ Khaled, Drake, and Justin Bieber's "Popstar." The company represents the creative efforts of the two directors from Brampton, Ontario. "We broke our production company, Fella, with this music video for DJ Khaled, Drake and Bieber called Popstar."
**Future of Liner Notes and Box Sets** : In the all-digital era, box sets are identified as a primary format where liner notes will continue to thrive, as record labels use elaborate collections—featuring production information, lyrics, essays, and various books—to keep older catalogs profitable. This strategy caters to a desire for physical artifacts and comprehensive contextual information. "If there's one place liner notes will live for a long time to come, it's with box sets."
**Global Repertoire Database (GRD) Initiative** : The Global Repertoire Database (GRD) was an ambitious project envisioned as a single-point registration site for all songs globally, intended to facilitate the discovery of liner notes, artwork, and crucial credit information for proper payment. Endorsed by the Grammy Awards, the initiative aimed to establish a music equivalent of an international movie database but collapsed in 2015 due to the lack of statutory registration requirements and the overwhelming complexity and expense of compilation. "This was supposed to make it easy to find out everything about a given song, including who needed to be credited and therefore paid for use of these songs."
**Grammy Award for Best Liner Notes** : A Grammy Award specifically for Best Liner Notes was established in 1964 and continues to be awarded, primarily recognizing classical recordings, collections, and anthologies. While rock music received limited recognition, Bob Dylan and Big Star (in 2013 for a box set) were notable exceptions. "Liner notes were such a big deal that a Grammy Award was established for them in 1964 and continues even today."
**Importance of Credits and Payments** : In the fragmented digital music world, accurately tracking and attributing credit to all individuals who worked on songs is crucial. This ensures that people are recognized for their contributions and receive appropriate payment, making liner notes a business imperative, regardless of the format they ultimately take. "People need the official credit so they can be recognized for their work and get paid."
**Initial CD Booklets (1983)** : When CDs first entered the market in 1983, the accompanying booklets were often "wretched," frequently consisting of minimal glossy paper, sometimes with blank pages or only basic cover art. This initial lack of effort contrasted sharply with the rich liner notes found on vinyl LPs, though booklets later expanded into more informative formats. "And when CDs first appeared in the marketplace in 1983, the booklets that came inside were often just wretched things."
**iTunes LP (Project Cocktail)** : Codename "Cocktail," iTunes LP was an initiative launched by Apple's iTunes Store in the fall of 2009 to create interactive album artwork and provide digital extras like liner notes, videos, and lyrics. Despite its innovative concept, Apple ceased publishing this material in March 2018 and has been actively removing existing iTunes LPs since then. "Apple's iTunes Store really got into this with something called the iTunes LP. This was codamed cocktail and first appeared in the fall of 2009."
**iTunes Metadata** : In digital music, metadata is encoded directly into files, allowing users to access information like artist, song, album, and other details by using features like the "get info" option in iTunes. While songs purchased from the iTunes music store often have these fields filled, they are frequently left blank in other cases. "In iTunes, you can right click and choose the get info option and a dialog box will pop up with several tabs of metadata."
**Jaxsta Initiative** : Jaxsta is an Australian website dedicated to bringing back official liner notes by creating a comprehensive database of all required information. Their mission is to enhance music discovery by allowing users to click through detailed credits, and they aim to gather clean, accurate data directly from record labels. "And finally, there's an Australian site called Jacksta, J-A-X-S-T-A. They're on a mission to bring back official liner notes by creating a big database of, well, everything that requires liner notes."
**Liner Notes Concept** : Liner notes, also known as album notes or sleeve notes, refer to the textual information found on vinyl jackets, inner sleeves, CD booklets, or J-cards (for cassettes). They can range from basic details like song titles and credits to extensive essays, providing context about the music, artists, and recording process. "First of all, this text and these writings can also go by the name's album notes or sleeve notes."
**Liner Notes in the 1970s** : The 1970s were considered a "best time" for liner notes, especially for vinyl LPs, serving as comprehensive "textbooks" for listeners in the pre-internet and pre-video channel era. These notes were crucial for learning about producers, recording studios, session players, instrument manufacturers, and endorsements, enriching the understanding of the music. "The time we got to the 1970s, which was probably the best time for liner notes, at least as far as vinyl LPs went, studying the text of an album revealed so much about the artist and the music in those pre-internet pre-video channel days."
**Media Sound Studio B** : Lou Reed's 1989 album "New York" was recorded and mixed at Media Sound Studio B in New York City. Reed's liner notes for the album specifically mention this studio, along with details about the recording process and his intention for the listening experience. "The sample was recorded in mixed-ed media sound Studio B, New York City, and essentially the order you have it here."
**Metadata in Digital Music** : In contemporary digital music, information equivalent to traditional liner notes, such as instrumentalists, producers, engineers, and studios, is categorized as metadata. This data is encoded directly into digital files, although its completeness can vary, with many fields often left blank. "In today's world, we would call this metadata. It's encoded right into the digital file."
**Music Video Storytelling** : Music videos are recognized as a powerful medium for storytelling, imagery, and developing storylines, often originating from unique creative headspaces. The "Art Context" podcast aims to explore the behind-the-scenes processes, storyboards, and collaborations that bring these visual narratives to life, moving beyond just the final product. "when you watch a music video, you're just seeing the end result. But you don't see what went into to make that product and that piece of art"
**Ralph Gleason's Liner Note Philosophy** : Ralph Gleason, an executive at Fantasy Records, was a strong proponent of detailed liner notes, frequently using them to articulate his personal opinions on albums and to discuss the music's social or political significance. His approach contributed to making music an active experience by adding another editorial dimension to recordings. "He'd write his opinion of the album and sometimes go off on tangents about why this music was important from a social or political point of view."
**Rhapsody/Napster Digital Liner Notes** : In 2013, Rhapsody, which later rebranded as Napster, began integrating digital liner notes into its song data. This initiative received significant endorsement from the Grammy Awards, indicating a desire to formalize and standardize the provision of detailed music information in the digital streaming landscape. "In 2013, Rhapsody, which has since been renamed Napster and sold a couple of times, started including digital liner notes in its song data."
**Spotify Songwriter Pages** : In February 2020, Spotify introduced "Songwriter Pages," a feature designed to offer fans, collaborators, and industry partners a deeper insight into the creators behind their favorite songs. This involves clickable songwriter names in the credits, allowing users to explore other works written by them. "In February 2020, Spotify launched something called Songwriter Pages, which they call a new way for fans, collaborators and industry partners to dive deeper into the creators behind their favorite songs."
**Streaming Music Benefits and Drawbacks** : Streaming music offers immense convenience and instant access to a vast catalog, making it the dominant future of music distribution. However, this transition from physical media to digital means has resulted in the loss of certain elements, collectively referred to as "digital debris," such as comprehensive liner notes. "Streaming is here, it is the future, it is super convenient and gives us instant access to more music than we ever thought possible."
**Trident Studios** : David Bowie's 1971 album "The Man Who Sold the World" was recorded and mixed at Trident Studios in London. This studio played a crucial role in the creation of an album that featured Bowie's early rock and roll sound and innovative use of instruments like the Moog synthesizer. "the album was recorded and mixed with Trident Studios in London"
**Vinyl Revival and Liner Notes** : The current resurrection of vinyl records plays a role in the continuation of liner notes, as consumers often seek the tactile experience of holding the album jacket while the record plays. This physical interaction reinforces the demand for printed information, complementing efforts by third-party sites like Discogs and Jaxsta to fill the information gap. "New vinyl is a throwback to the days of old vinyl where people want to hold the jacket in their hands while the record spins on the turntable."
**Wikipedia as Music Resource** : Wikipedia is frequently used as a source for learning about songs and albums, often providing surprisingly accurate details on producers, engineers, studios, and supporting musicians. However, as crowdsourced information, its accuracy is not always guaranteed, and it can feel "lacking" compared to traditional liner notes. "Wikipedia is surprisingly accurate when it comes to learning more about a song or an album, really, usually, who produced it, the engineer, the name of the studio, the supporting players, and so forth."
