**Bile Beer Drinking Contest – 1992 Lollapalooza, Jim Rose Circus, Matt "the Tube" Crowley** : The 1992 Lollapalooza tour featured the Jim Rose Circus sideshow, including Matt "the Tube" Crowley, who would swallow a seven-foot hose connected to a hand pump, force beer, ketchup, mustard, chocolate, and Pepto-Bismol into his stomach, then pump it all back out into glasses for audience members to drink. At one stop on the tour featuring both Pearl Jam and Soundgarden, Eddie Vedder and Chris Cornell entered a competition to see who could consume the most of this bile cocktail. Eddie won decisively and named his victory "Wayne." "Eddie won this contest. He called it Wayne."


# artist - Dave Matthews Band

## curiosities
**Chicago River Raw Sewage Incident – August 8, 2004, Kinzie Street Bridge, 800 lbs of Waste onto Tourist Boat** : At 1:18 pm on August 8, 2004, a Dave Matthews Band tour bus driven by Stephen Wooll crossed the Kinzie Street Bridge in Chicago — a metal grate structure over the Chicago River. Wooll took the opportunity to empty the bus's septic system through the grate. Directly beneath the bridge at that moment was the sightseeing boat *Little Lady*, loaded with approximately 100 tourists looking up at the Chicago skyline. Security cameras on the bridge captured the entire incident. Wooll was sentenced to 18 months probation, 150 hours of community service, and fined $10,000; a $70,000 lawsuit followed for violation of environmental laws. Dave Matthews and the band cooperated fully with the investigation, offering DNA samples to positively identify the waste, and donated $50,000 to Friends of the Chicago River. "800 pounds of human waste... there was a sightseeing boat called the Little Lady."


# artist - The Clash

## members
Joe Strummer

## curiosities
**Hepatitis from Gobbing – 1979, Spat in Mouth During Performance** : In the early British punk scene, audience members competed to spit at bands on stage as a sign of enthusiasm following a newspaper article depicting the practice. During a 1979 Clash performance, Joe Strummer was singing with his mouth open when an audience member's spit landed directly in it. The result was a significant case of hepatitis. The practice of spitting at performers, known as "gobbing," was common enough that performers were routinely covered in phlegm and mucus during shows. "Joe Strummer was singing. His mouth was open and the result was a really nasty case of hepatitis."


# artist - R.E.M.

## members
Bill Berry

## curiosities
**Dual Brain Aneurysms – March 1, 1995, Lausanne, Switzerland, Emergency Surgery; Tour Resumed May 15** : Ninety minutes into the 27th show of the Monster world tour — which had started on November 13, 1994 in Perth, Australia — drummer Bill Berry felt as if "somebody dropped a bowling ball on his head" and had to be carried from the stage in Lausanne, Switzerland. He had suffered not one but two potentially fatal brain aneurysms. Emergency brain surgery was performed, and Berry spent eight weeks recovering with the outcome uncertain. The tour was postponed; it resumed on May 15 in San Francisco. To demonstrate the band's dark humor about the episode, the tour program subsequently featured an X-ray of Bill's head showing where the aneurysms had been. "It was not one, but two potentially fatal brain aneurysms."

**Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever – April 1989, Munich, Near-Fatal Illness Misidentified by European Doctors** : In April 1989, during a European tour, Bill Berry collapsed on stage in Munich with a fever of 103°F, chills, muscle aches, severe headache, nausea, red eyes, and a characteristic rash spreading from his wrists and ankles. German doctors, uncertain of the cause, prescribed tetracycline — the only antibiotic effective against Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever, a tick-borne illness virtually unknown in Europe that Berry had almost certainly contracted while gardening before the tour. Had the German doctors chosen penicillin (the standard first choice), Berry would have received no benefit and might have suffered organ failure, coma, and death. The correct diagnosis was only confirmed after his recovery. "It wasn't until later that it was determined that Bill had come down with something called Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever."


# artist - Iggy Pop

## member of
The Stooges

## curiosities
**Elton John Gorilla Suit Incident – Richards, Atlanta, October 1973** : In October 1973, Iggy Pop and the Stooges were playing at Richards in Atlanta at the same time Elton John was touring America. Elton — engaged in a semi-friendly rivalry with David Bowie, who was a known Stooges supporter — decided to crash the show dressed in a full gorilla suit to surprise Iggy on stage. A photographer from Detroit music magazine *Creem* was tipped off. No one warned Iggy that this was planned, and no one warned Elton that Iggy was heavily intoxicated on speed that night. When Elton bounded onto the stage in the gorilla suit, Iggy genuinely believed it was a real gorilla, was terrified, and fled the stage. The incident was captured on film. After Elton removed the gorilla head and multiple people calmed Iggy down, the show resumed and the two performed several songs together — Iggy bare-chested, Elton still in the suit. "Iggy thought it was an actual gorilla. And this freak out is captured on film."


# artist - Metallica

Cliff Burton

## curiosities
**Cliff Burton Death by Card Draw – September 27, 1986, Sweden, Winch Cable Slip** : During Metallica's European tour in support of *Master of Puppets*, the band drew cards to determine who got which bunks on the tour bus — Cliff Burton drew the Ace of Spades, a high card, and chose first, going to bed. Before dawn on September 27, 1986, between Stockholm and Copenhagen, the bus hit black ice, the driver lost control, and the vehicle left the road. Cliff was thrown from his bunk through a window and the bus rolled on top of him, badly injuring him. When rescue crews used a winch to lift the bus off Cliff, the cable slipped and he was crushed a second time — this almost certainly caused his death. He was 24. "And to think it all started with a cut of the cards."


# artist - Linkin Park

## curiosities
**Malaysia Seated Show – October 15, 2003, Kuala Lumpur, 50,000 Fans, No Screaming, No Movement** : When Linkin Park was booked to play Merdeka Stadium in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, on October 15, 2003, significant religious opposition arose from groups arguing that rock music corrupted youth. Negotiations with the government produced an "artist performance ethics document" requiring: audiences to remain seated at all times with no dancing or jumping; stage decoration limited to a simple backdrop with the band's logo; the band dressed from chest to knees with no exposed skin and no clothing linked to drugs; no swearing, violent, or erotic behavior; no "leaping around or throwing something from the stage"; the band seated during the entire performance; and no screaming during any songs. Linkin Park accepted all conditions. The show proceeded before 50,000 similarly seated fans, with police present both inside and for the religious demonstrators outside. "Can you imagine a Linkin Park show without screaming and jumping and dancing and swearing?"


# artist - U2

Bono
Adam Clayton

## curiosities
**Mexican Secret Service Incident – December 3, 1997, Foro Sol Stadium, Mexico City, Security Chief Run Over** : After playing a 25-song set at Foro Sol Stadium in Mexico City on December 3, 1997, U2 were approached backstage with an urgent request to leave immediately. Three sons of Mexican President Ernesto Zedillo had arrived unannounced without tickets; venue security had waved them and their secret service detail through in a van. When the president's sons and their bodyguards tried to exit through a restricted backstage camera crane area — dangerous due to counterweights — a camera operator told them to leave, causing a confrontation. U2's road crew intervened, not knowing who the group was. Jerry Mele, U2's head of security, placed himself in front of the van to detain it pending police arrival; the van drove over him, resulting in serious back injuries from which he never fully recovered. The next day, U2 were summoned to the president's palace where the sons produced albums for signing. President Zedillo asked that the incident be kept quiet, stating it could have been much worse. "So what could U2 do?"


# artist - Marilyn Manson

## curiosities
**Blizzard Stranded with Sesame Street Cast – Allentown, Pennsylvania, January 6, 1996** : After a Marilyn Manson show at Stars in Allentown, Pennsylvania, on January 6, 1996, a severe blizzard dumped three feet of snow on the area, stranding everyone in their hotel. Also stranded in the same hotel were Shaquille O'Neal and members of the Orlando Magic basketball team, and the entire cast of *Sesame Street Live*, including performers playing Bert, Ernie, Oscar the Grouch, the Cookie Monster, and Big Bird. With nowhere else to go, the band, the basketball team, and the Sesame Street characters gathered in the hotel bar, drank heavily, and sang songs together until three in the morning. "With nowhere else to go, the band, the team, the cast of Sesame Street... they all gathered in the bar, drank very heavily and sang songs together until three in the morning."


# artist - Blur

## members
Alex James
Dave Rowntree

## songs
**Beagle II (2002) – Instrumental Recorded for British Mars Probe, Lost on Christmas Day 2003** : Blur bassist Alex James and drummer Dave Rowntree, both astronomy enthusiasts, approached the British Space Agency when they heard Britain was launching a Mars probe and proposed providing music to be broadcast upon a successful landing. The agency agreed, and on January 30, 2002, Blur recorded a specially commissioned instrumental called "Beagle II," based loosely on a mathematical sequence. The probe — named Beagle II — was launched on June 2, 2003, designed to land on the Martian surface in the equatorial region known as Isidis Planitia on Christmas Day 2003. After successfully separating from the parent spacecraft Mars Express in Martian orbit on December 19, Beagle II began its descent at 2:47 am London time on December 25 and was never heard from again, joining the long list of failed Martian probes. Blur's debut performance on Mars was never heard. "Essentially, we are going to play the first gig on Mars."
