## albums
**First Record (Year not specified)** : Jared Followell was 15 years old when the band created their first record, which contributed to the band's natural musical evolution over time. "my little brother was Jared, was 15 when we did the first record."
**AHA Shake Heartbreak (2005)** : This second album was largely influenced by the band's extensive touring in Europe and the UK between their first and second records, during which they experienced the "highs of rock and roll." The lyrical content explicitly reflected themes of excess, including boozing, drugging, and women. "AHA Shake, the second record, which was pretty much about boozing and drugging and women and all that good stuff."
**Because of the Times (Year not specified)** : The band made this third album at Blackbird studio in Nashville, a conscious decision to avoid the numerous distractions of Los Angeles and focus intently on the music. This creative period followed a critical family intervention prompted by their mother's concern over their unhealthy appearance after a tour, leading the band to "chill out" and prioritize their well-being. The album surprisingly debuted at number one in Russia. "we really just got to focus on the music and the kind of record we wanted to make. And that was because of the times."
**Only by the Night (2008)** : This fourth album represented a significant breakthrough for Kings of Leon, serving as a payoff for the patience shown by their record label in allowing them to develop. It notably featured the hit song "Sex on Fire," which Nathan Followell initially voted against for inclusion on the album but was ultimately outvoted by the other members. "All of this patience really paid off with the fourth album only by the night."

## curiosities
**Musical Growth Over Time** : The band's sound evolved naturally from one record to the next, a direct result of their personal growth and changing musical influences, rather than a conscious effort for drastic change. Jared Followell, for example, grew from 15 years old on the first record to 21 on the fourth, naturally impacting their style. "It was just us growing musically. ...we've changed as people and musicians and our musical influences have changed."
**Early European Success** : Kings of Leon experienced immediate significant success in Europe and the UK, being "big from day one" there. This phenomenon was partly attributed to a fascination with their unique backstory as preacher sons engaging in rock and roll, contrasting with their slower reception in their native American South, which was "one of the last places to get it." "over there we were big from day one over there. ...I think they were kind of fascinated with the story. The way we were raised and how the whole good versus evil we grew up. Preacher sons and now we're out doing stuff that preach our sons shouldn't do."
**Label Patience and Development** : The band was afforded an unusual amount of time and freedom by their record label to develop their sound and fan base, avoiding the typical pressure placed on new acts to achieve immediate commercial success. This patience proved beneficial, allowing them to build a sustainable career and eventually play large venues on their fourth album. The label's willingness to wait was partly due to guaranteed sales of 500,000 units in the UK and Australia. "The label didn't panic or threaten them or otherwise freak out. They just let things happen. ...they didn't want to drop us from the label when they knew they were guaranteed 500,000 sold in the UK and Australia."
**Backstory as a Novelty and Hook** : The band's unique narrative—comprising three brothers and a cousin, raised on the road by a preacher father—initially captivated audiences and sustained their interest. This allowed sufficient time for the band's musical potential to be recognized beyond their intriguing personal story, proving they were "something more than just a novelty." "the story definitely sucked them in. But then, you know, it kept their attention long enough to realize that, you know, this band might have some potential to, you know, actually make great music and be something more than just a novelty."
**"Socks and Shoes" Perception in England** : Upon their first arrival in England, the band observed that people seemed surprised they were wearing socks and shoes, suggesting an expectation of a more stereotypical "wild, crazy, hillbilly redneck" image. "when we first got to England, I mean, I honestly think they were surprised that we had socks and shoes on."
**Family Intervention for Well-being** : After returning home from a tour, the band's mother became visibly distressed by their appearance, noting they were "super skinny and pale" from excessive activities, which prompted the band to reflect and decide to "chill out." This led to a period of focusing on music and making their third album, "Because of the Times," in Nashville to avoid the distractions found in places like Los Angeles. "our mom busted out crying when she saw us. We were all super skinny and pale and been doing way too much stuff that we shouldn't have been doing and she was like, I don't even recognize my boys."
**Five-Syllable Album Title Rule** : Kings of Leon maintain a unique and consistent rule where every album title they release must consist of exactly five syllables, a detail exemplified by their album "Because of the Times." "That's the Kings of Leon rule for every album title. It must have five syllables."
**Goal for Remix and Cover Publishing** : The band aims to be the first to earn more publishing money from remixes and covers of their songs than from their original compositions. This strategy also serves a practical purpose, allowing them to play their own music at parties by using remixes rather than the original versions. "We're going to try to be the first band that makes more publishing money off of. Remixes and covers. Then we actually did the original songs."
**Preference for Diverse Remixers** : Kings of Leon typically select remixes that are "completely different" from the original versions and are usually created by artists who are not primarily associated with rock bands. This preference for non-rock artists ensures a fresh and unique interpretation of their music. "we usually only choose the ones that are completely different. And usually it's someone that isn't, you know, in a rock band or anything like that, we kind of want something different."
**Aversion to Political Career Revival** : The band intentionally avoided using political "anti-Bush" songs to revive their careers, viewing the practice as "uncool" and believing musicians should focus on music rather than politics. They later included some lyrics that "could have been taken as political" but primarily reflected a general desire for change. "that made us so sick as a band. All these bands, reviving their careers by these political anti-bush songs and anti, you know, you're a band, you're a musician. You make music. You're not a politician."
**Dream Collaboration with Radiohead** : Nathan Followell expressed a strong desire to collaborate with Radiohead, envisioning an "experimental" blend of Kings of Leon's "Southern country boy thing" with Radiohead's contrasting musical style to see what unique outcome would emerge. "Probably, uh... Radio Had a lot. I'd be fine just to get freaky, experimental with those guys."
