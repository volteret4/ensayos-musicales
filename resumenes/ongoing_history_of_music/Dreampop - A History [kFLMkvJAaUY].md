# artist - The Velvet Underground

## members
John Cale

## albums
**The Velvet Underground & Nico (1967)** : Released in March 1967, this debut album featured repetitive, drony passages created by John Cale, which had an almost hypnotic effect. Some of the music from this album could be consumed in the dark to create an immersive, carrying-away experience.
"The band's John Kale had a knack for creating repetitive drony passages that had an almost hypnotic effect."

## songs
**Venus in Furs** : This song is highlighted as an example of The Velvet Underground's immersive sound. Played at a low volume, it creates an atmosphere conducive to sleep, while at extreme volume, its drone can alter one's mental and physical state. It showcases the band's ability to envelop listeners in sound and texture.
"At a low volume, a song like Venus and Furs, for example, creates an atmosphere that could allow you to sleep."

## curiosities
**Immersive Effect Pioneers** : The Velvet Underground was one of the first rock bands to create an immersive effect with their music, whether intentionally or not. Their music was described as enveloping the listener in sound, texture, and noise in a mesmerizing, narcotic, transcenducing, and soothing way, best consumed in the dark.
"Velvet Underground was one of the first rock bands to, whether on purpose or not, to create an immersive effect with their music."

# artist - Cocteau Twins

## members
Elizabeth Fraser (vocals)
Robin Guthrie (multi-instrumentalist)

## member of
This Mortal Coil

## genres
Dreampop

## labels
4AD

## albums
**Heaven or Las Vegas (1990)** : Released in early 1990, this was the Cocteau Twins' most successful album.
"The most successful album was Heaven or Las Vegas, which was released in early 1990."

## songs
**Pearly-Dewdrops' Drops (1984)** : This song is from the 1984 3-track EP "The Spangled Maker." It achieved success as a top 30 mainstream hit on the singles charts and a number one indie hit. Elizabeth Fraser's vocals, delivered in her unique made-up language, prioritize atmospheric and sonic textures over lyrical meaning.
"The song was a top 30 mainstream hit on the singles charts and a number one indie hit."

## curiosities
**Formation and Lineup Changes (1979)** : The Cocteau Twins formed in Central Scotland in 1979. After a challenging start, they stabilized as a duo comprising vocalist Elizabeth Fraser and multi-instrumentalist Robin Guthrie.
"The cocktail twins were from Central Scotland and came together in 1979."
**Influences** : The band incorporated elements from Bauhaus and Siouxsie and the Banshees (referred to as "Suzy in the band she's in") and Joy Division, adding their distinctive style.
"They took bits from Bajaos and Suzy in the band she's in Joy Division and added their own spin."
**Elizabeth Fraser's Vocal Style** : Fraser was known for singing in a self-invented language, a style described as almost operatic or "angelic" by listeners. Her vocals emphasized atmospheric and sonic textures, with the lyrical content being less important.
"She sang in some made up tongue in a style that was almost operatic, or in the words of some listeners and fans, and Jellic."
**Robin Guthrie's Guitar Technique** : Robin Guthrie focused on achieving specific guitar tones, sounds, and textures rather than technical precision. He openly admitted to technical limitations but layered guitar tracks with reverb and echo to create a rich sound, effectively covering up his perceived deficiencies.
"Robin was more concerned about getting the right guitar tones and sounds than he was about playing the correct notes, which is not a slam against him."
**Heyday and Decline (1984-1997)** : The Cocteau Twins experienced their most successful period from 1984 to 1994. The band's dynamic changed after the collapse of Elizabeth Fraser and Robin Guthrie's romantic relationship, though they released a few more albums. The group officially disbanded in 1997.
"The Cocktoes best years were from 1984 through to about 1994."
**Failed Coachella Reunion (2005)** : A reunion for the Coachella festival in 2005 was nearly organized, but the lingering emotional pain from Fraser and Guthrie's past relationship prevented it from happening. A planned 55-day world tour following Coachella was consequently canceled.
"But there was almost a Coachella reunion in 2005, but the relationship pain was just too much for the two of them and it never happened."

# genre - Dreampop

## curiosities
**Origins in Early 80s** : Dreampop emerged from the post-punk era in the early 1980s, influenced by the experimentation and new electronic technologies of the time, such as synthesizers, drum machines, and samplers. It has had a profound and lasting effect on music.
"And from its origins in the early 80s, Dreampop has had a profound effect on music that has felt even today."
**Characteristics** : Defined by atmosphere, sonic textures, and sometimes sheer volume, Dreampop maintains a melodic core. It ranges in volume from barely audible softness to extreme loudness. Its history traces back to the immersive sounds of the 1960s, like The Velvet Underground, and the "Wall of Sound" techniques of Phil Spector.
"atmosphere, sonic textures, and in some cases, sheer volume reigns supreme."
**Overlap with Sub-genres** : Dreampop significantly touches on and overlaps with other alternative rock sub-genres, including shoegaze and modern psychedelic material.
"Dreampop is an interesting case and that it touches on and overlaps with other alt-rock sub-genres, including shoegase, and anything resembling modern psychedelic material."
**Volume Continuum** : The genre's volume range is vast, spanning from "barely there softness" to an intensity "beyond a jet engine," yet it consistently retains a melodic quality.
"It has a volume continuum that ranges from barely their softness to somewhere beyond a jet engine."
**Goth Influence (Late 70s)** : The emergence of Goth music in 1978-1979, characterized by an ethereal, spooky, dark, and minor-key feel, was a necessary precursor for proper Dreampop to form. This style, sometimes called ethereal wave or ethereal Goth, later morphed into something distinct but related to Dreampop.
"And before it could be born, we need Goth."
**Late 1980s Characteristics** : In the late 1980s, Dreampop was characterized by a dominance of female vocalists (though males also featured), breathy and quiet vocals, and a hazy, blurry mood. It was often associated with relaxation and a mind-blanking state, and could be enhanced by "recreational chemicals."
"Female vocalists dominated, although dudes did have their chances too. Vocals were breathy, quiet. The mood was hazy, blurry."
**Coined by Alex Ayuli (Late 1980s)** : The term "Dreampop" itself was coined by Alex Ayuli of AR Kane around the late 1980s. He used it as a way to define his band's sound and push back against journalists applying unwanted labels.
"It was around this time that Alex started referring to what the band did as Dreampop, as a way of pushing back on journalists giving them labels that they didn't like."
**UK Origins and US Expansion** : Early Dreampop was primarily confined to the UK. However, American artists and collaborators like David Lynch, Angelo Badalamenti, and Julee Cruise, and bands like Galaxie 500, soon began exploring the genre's territory.
"The first Dreampop was largely confined to the UK, but there were others in other countries who wanted to explore this territory."
**Heyday and Decline (Late 80s to Mid 90s)** : The heyday of Dreampop extended from the late 1980s through to the mid-1990s. It was subsequently overshadowed by other 90s genres like Madchester, Britpop, Grunge, and the punk rock revival, receding to the margins but never entirely disappearing.
"And the genres heyday extended from the late 80s through to the mid 1990s."
**21st Century Revival and Technology (2000s)** : Dreampop, and its cousin Shoegaze, experienced a significant revival in the 21st century, partly driven by technological advancements. Laptops made it easier for artists to create densely textured, studio-quality music in bedrooms using synthesizers and software, facilitating a surge of new material by the time streaming services like Spotify launched in 2008.
"The advantage Dreampop had was a technological one, and that technology was the laptop."
**COVID-19 Influence (2020s)** : Dreampop made significant inroads in the 2020s, potentially due to the COVID-19 pandemic. Lockdown conditions encouraged many artists to create music in their bedrooms, increasing the supply of this type of music. Additionally, the genre's relaxing qualities offered a needed antidote to the stress and isolation of the time.
"Why did Dreampop make such inroads in the 2020s? I'm a theory that has something to do with COVID."
