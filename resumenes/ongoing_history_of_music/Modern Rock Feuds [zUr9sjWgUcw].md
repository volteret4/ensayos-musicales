## curiosities
**Political and Personal Ideology Clash** : Joey Ramone was described as a liberal with sympathies for hippie types and progressive outlooks, while Johnny Ramone was conservative, supporting Ronald Reagan and disliking hippies. Despite these opposing views, they managed to form the band and find common ground in music. "Joey Ramon was a liberal sort of dude with sympathies for hippie types and those with progressive outlooks on life. The stars, Joey Ramon hated hippies. He was about as conservative as it got when it came to politics."
**Love Triangle and Intra-Band Feud** : In 1976, Joey Ramone met 16-year-old fan Linda Danielle from Queens at CBGB. They reconnected in Los Angeles a couple of years later and became a couple, with Linda joining The Ramones on tour. Johnny Ramone, who initially made Linda sit in the back of the van, eventually discovered common ground with her (neither drank or did drugs, she liked to cook, he liked home-cooked meals) and they became best friends. Joey and Linda became engaged, but after a dispute over a ring price, Johnny realized he was in love with Linda. Johnny intimidated Joey, who hated conflict, leading Linda to move towards Johnny over the next 18 months. They broke up, and Johnny and Linda became a couple in the early 1980s, marrying in 1984. "In 1976, just as the Ramones were starting to become known, Joey met a young 16-year-old fan named Linda Danielle."
**Enduring Animosity** : Despite Johnny Ramone and Linda Danielle marrying in 1984, The Ramones remained together and continued touring until 1996. Joey Ramone did not speak to Linda again until 1993, and he and Johnny almost never spoke again until the band's breakup. The feud was intense and quiet, prioritizing the band's business over personal issues. Johnny did not contact Joey when he was dying in early 2000 nor attend his funeral. "Joey didn't speak to Linda again until 1993. And Joey and Johnny almost never spoke again."
**Johnny Ramone's Post-Mortem Sentiments** : Johnny Ramone expressed his lack of desire to interact with Joey Ramone even in death, stating he wouldn't want Joey at his funeral and wouldn't want to hear from him if he were dying, preferring to be left alone by friends. "I was in California, said Johnny. I wasn't going to travel all the way to New York, but I wouldn't have gone anyway. I wouldn't want him coming to my funeral and I wouldn't want to hear from him if I were dying."

**Perceived Feud with Pearl Jam** : Kurt Cobain was initially critical of Pearl Jam, viewing them as "careerists" who were too commercial and corporate, appealing to "jocks" whom Kurt disliked, and not true to punk rock or alternative roots. "That never was a feud. I slagged them off because I didn't like their band. It was my fault. I should have been slagging off the record company instead of them."
**Feud with Guns N' Roses** : The bad blood between Nirvana and Guns N' Roses, particularly with Axl Rose, started when Kurt Cobain refused an offer for Nirvana to open for Guns N' Roses on a major world tour. Kurt viewed Guns N' Roses as "too big" and was concerned that aligning with them would be perceived as selling out, stating Nirvana was not a "typical Guns n' Roses type of band that has absolutely nothing to say." Axl Rose, who was a fan of Nirvana (even wearing a Nirvana hat in the "Don't Cry" video), felt snubbed and began publicly insulting Kurt and Courtney Love as "junkies," claiming their daughter had birth defects due to their drug use. "It began when Kurt refused an offer by the Gunners to have Nirvana open for them on one of their massive world tours."
**1992 MTV Music Video Awards Altercation** : The animosity between Nirvana and Guns N' Roses culminated in a backstage altercation at the 1992 MTV Music Video Awards. Axl Rose confronted Kurt Cobain and Courtney Love, reportedly telling Kurt to "keep his woman in line." Simultaneously, Nirvana bassist Chris Novoselic and Guns N' Roses bassist Duff McKagan were also arguing. "This boiled over to an altercation backstage at the 1992 MTV Music Video Awards between Axel, Kurt and Courtney Love."
**Public Insults Against Axl Rose** : In late 1992, Kurt Cobain gave an interview where he publicly denounced Axl Rose, calling him "a sexist and a racist and a homophobic" and stating that one could not be on Guns N' Roses' side and Nirvana's side simultaneously, further adding that Guns N' Roses "can't write good music." "Axel is a sexist and a racist and a homophobic. And you can't be on his side and be on our side."
**Reconciliation with Duff McKagan Before Death** : Days before Kurt Cobain's death, after leaving a rehab center in Los Angeles, he encountered Duff McKagan of Guns N' Roses on a Delta flight back to Seattle. They had a pleasant conversation, and Duff even offered Kurt a ride home from the airport, though Kurt vanished before taking it. "After walking away from a rehab center in Los Angeles, Kurt found himself on a Delta flight back to Seattle, sitting right next to Duff McKagan in business class."
**Dave Grohl's On-Stage Taunt** : Following the backstage confrontation at the 1992 MTV Music Video Awards, Nirvana performed. Towards the end of their performance, Dave Grohl reportedly taunted Axl Rose from the stage on live television. "Now, hold on to the very end when Dave Grohl comes to the mic and taunts Axel from the stage on live TV."

Damon Albarn and Noel Gallagher have since mellowed with age and have performed together on stage, acknowledging their shared experiences of the 1990s without focusing on past conflicts. "Age has meld both Noel and Damon to the point where they've performed together on stage."

He expressed a wish for a warning or a chance to fix things, noting it took him another five years to get sober. "I had been physically reckless, and there had been a lot of pushing and shoving on my part with everybody in the band. I was very unpredictable. I embarrassed everyone around me."
**Continued Bad Blood with Metallica** : Despite Megadeth's success, a lasting tension between Dave Mustaine and Metallica, particularly Lars Ulrich, has been noted.
