**Earworm** : This term describes a phenomenon "when a clip of a song keeps running through your head over and over and over again on a loop and a drive you crazy." "Everybody's had one of those."
**Mondegreen** : This term refers to a "misheard lyric," with Jimi Hendrix's "Purple Haze" ("excuse me while I kiss the sky" often heard as "excuse me while I kiss this guy") provided as a "great example." "That's a misheard lyric and a great example is in Jimmy Hendrix's purple haze."
**Western Music Theory - 12-Note Scale** : Western music is fundamentally structured around "a scale of 12 notes." These notes are "related to each other mathematically by a series of ratios known as intervals of a semitone." "Western music is based on a scale of 12 notes."
**Western Music Theory - Building Blocks** : Combinations and patterns of the 12 notes in Western music reveal "chords, melodies, harmonies, and so on," which are considered "the building blocks of our music," from symphonies to pop songs. "Playing those notes in certain combinations or patterns reveal things like chords, melodies, harmonies, and so on."
**Finite Number of Musical Combinations** : Despite the initial impression of infinite possibilities, the number of ways notes can be used is "very, very big but definitely finite." For playing each note once, it's "around 479 billion, 1,600." When considering different note durations (whole, half, quarter, etc.), the number exceeds "one quintillion possible combinations," which would take "33 billion, 63 million, 236 thousand, 360 years" to play if a new combination was played every second, more than five times the age of the universe. "The numbers are very, very big but definitely finite. Somewhere around 479 billion, 1,600."
**Constraints on Pleasing Music** : Despite a vast number of potential note combinations, "you just can't stick a bunch of tones and semi-tones together and expect them to sound good." Music needs to be "pleasing to the ear and has to reach into the soul," which significantly narrows down the number of musically viable combinations, especially within specific genres like rock. "Music has to sound pleasing to the ear and has to reach into the soul, right?"
**Inevitable Musical Duplication** : Due to the finite nature of pleasing musical combinations, "songs are not like snowflakes" and "at some point, there's going to be duplication."
