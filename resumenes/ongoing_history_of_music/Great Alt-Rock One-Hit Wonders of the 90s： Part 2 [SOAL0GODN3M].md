## curiosities
**1990s Boom** : In 1991, Nirvana's *Nevermind* sparked an explosion in alt-rock, leading to massive sales for bands like Pearl Jam, Soundgarden, Smashing Pumpkins, Stone Temple Pilots, and Red Hot Chili Peppers. This era was driven by Generation X's "insatiable appetite" for the genre, prompting record labels to sign "everyone and anything alternative sounding." **One-Hit Wonders Phenomenon** : The 90s alt-rock boom, characterized by rapid label signings, also produced many "one-hit wonders." Labels would sign bands if they sounded "alternative and halfway decent," and consumers would buy full CDs for just one big hit song, regardless of the album's overall quality. Sometimes labels would bail on promoting subsequent singles after an initial hit, or the band's sound would simply fall out of cultural favor. "But there were some misses along the way. And some of those signings only had one good song."

# curiosity

**The One-Hit Wonder Phenomenon** : The concept of a "one-hit wonder" describes an artist or band that achieves significant success with a single song, often struggling to replicate that level of impact throughout their career. This phenomenon is observed across all genres, including classical, jazz, R&B, country, pop, and especially alt-rock in the 90s. Despite the stigma, achieving even one hit can provide sustained income and recognition. "But for whatever reason, the magical pixie dust that they managed to harness that one time disappears forever."
**Record Label Strategy in the 90s** : During the 1990s alt-rock boom, record labels adopted an aggressive strategy of signing any band that sounded "alternative and halfway decent." This "herd mentality" often meant investing in artists for just one potential hit, sometimes abandoning further promotion if subsequent singles didn't immediately take off, even if other good songs existed on the album. "Instructions went out to all the talent scouts at the record labels. If it sounds alternative and halfway decent, sign it."
**Consumer Behavior and CD Sales** : In the 1990s, the popularity of a single hit song could drive consumers to purchase an entire CD, even at prices like $15 or $20.
