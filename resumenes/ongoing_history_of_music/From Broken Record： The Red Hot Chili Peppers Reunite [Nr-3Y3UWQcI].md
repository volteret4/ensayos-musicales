**John Frusciante's Multiple Reunions** : John Frusciante has rejoined the band for a second time, marking his third stint as a member, following previous departures and returns. The discussions around his latest return involved a cautious approach to ensure communication from every angle and prevent future regrets. "Oh for those people who don't know, John left the band, rejoined the band, left the band, and has now rejoined the band. So this is the second time you've rejoined."

**Chad Smith's Reunion Catalyst** : The idea for John Frusciante's return was sparked by Chad Smith, who told John at Flea's wedding that he missed playing with him and suggested they, along with Flea, should jam sometime. This casual comment initiated the chain of events leading to John's rejoining the band. "Chad had said something to me when I saw him at Flea's wedding. And he was like, he misses playing with me and him and Flea should jam sometime."

**Flea's Direct Rejoining Offer** : After John Frusciante mentioned Chad Smith's suggestion about jamming, Flea, within seconds, directly asked John if he would consider being in the Red Hot Chili Peppers again. This swift proposition quickly moved the conversation from a casual jam to a potential full-time reunion. "It was within seconds after I suggested that Flea was like, you ever think about being in the Red Hat Chili Peppers again?"

**Anthony Kiedis's Initial Reunion Concerns** : Anthony Kiedis was initially apprehensive about John Frusciante rejoining the band, worrying that unresolved past resentments could get in the way of their future success. He felt the need to understand if John's return stemmed from a place of love and creation rather than bitterness. "Anthony was initially worried that past resentments would get in the way of their success."

**Anthony Kiedis's Pre-Reunion Sensation** : Anthony Kiedis observed that Flea was not "on fire" or feeling his "best self" in the band's previous incarnation, which led Anthony to independently wonder about John Frusciante's potential involvement again, initially thinking of him writing or producing rather than fully rejoining. This sensation grew despite no communication with John for some time. "I just got the sensation like, it would be really nice if John would get involved."

**Simultaneous Reunion Discussions** : Anthony Kiedis and Flea both approached each other at the same time, independently, to discuss John Frusciante's potential return, unaware of the other's thoughts. Flea, however, was a step ahead, having already jammed with John by that point. "Basically, we were both saying the exact same thing at the exact same time, which was what do you think about John?"

**John Frusciante's Destiny Declaration** : During a visit to John Frusciante's house to gauge his intentions, Anthony Kiedis felt a significant resolution of past animosity. John then declared, "I was born to be in this band," which deeply convinced Anthony that the reunion was meant to be, regardless of any potential challenges. "And he said, I was born to be in this band."

**Starting from Scratch for Reunion Rehearsals** : John Frusciante made a key suggestion for the reunion: instead of trying to pick up where they left off, the band should go "all the way back" and learn old blues songs and early Red Hot Chili Peppers songs. This approach helped them build basic rock and roll foundations, which made the subsequent writing and playing together feel more natural. "He was like, let's learn some old blue songs and let's learn some really old red hot chili pepper songs."

**Collaborative Songwriting Process** : The creative process for new material involved a natural flow of ideas among the members. John Frusciante would bring "gems" (ideas, chord progressions, arrangements, vocal melodies) to practice, which would inspire Flea to bring his own contributions. Anthony Kiedis would then go for long drives in his Chevy to generate his parts and add them to the collective "stew." "It's like every time John would bring a gem into practice, you know, flee would feel like, okay, I'll bring a gem."

**Second Rejoining of Red Hot Chili Peppers** : John Frusciante has rejoined the Red Hot Chili Peppers for the second time, marking his third period as a member of the band. This reunion followed his previous departures and returns. "So this is the second time you've rejoined."

**Initiator of Reunion Talks** : The conversation about John Frusciante rejoining the band was initiated by him after Chad Smith expressed missing playing with him at Flea's wedding. John then suggested jamming to Flea, which quickly led to Flea asking him to rejoin. "Chad had said something to me when I saw him at Flea's wedding. And he was like, he misses playing with me and him and Flea should jam sometime. So I mentioned it to Flea."

**Musical Activities During Hiatus** : During his time away from the Red Hot Chili Peppers, John Frusciante was primarily focused on making electronic music. However, he continued practicing guitar consistently, maintaining his instrumental skills. "Practicing guitar is something I was always doing even though I'm making electronic music."

**Cautious Approach to Rejoining** : Before fully committing to rejoining the band, John Frusciante had several reservations and wanted to discuss the decision from "every angle" with the other members. His aim was to ensure clear communication and prevent any regrets later. "I wanted to be sure that we were communicating and seeing it from every angle just to make sure that none of us were going to wind up regretting it later."

**Delayed First Jam with Band** : Despite agreeing to rejoin, John Frusciante did not play with the Red Hot Chili Peppers until approximately a month later, during their first rehearsal. Flea had prioritized John's commitment to joining over an immediate "test" jam session to see if the "old magic" was still present. "We didn't play together until maybe a month later or something at rehearsal."

**Reconciliation with Anthony Kiedis** : Anthony Kiedis visited John at his long-time residence to gauge his feelings about rejoining, specifically looking for any lingering bitterness. Anthony reported feeling "a great deal of resolution," indicating that previous animosity and resentment had largely dissipated. "I felt like whatever sort of animosity or resentment or all this stuff had more or less been resolved."

**Declaration of Purpose** : During his meeting with Anthony Kiedis, John Frusciante made a powerful statement about his connection to the band, declaring, "I was born to be in this band." This statement deeply resonated with Anthony, making him believe that the reunion was an inevitable and destined path. "But he said, I was born to be in this band."

**Reunion Rehearsal Strategy** : John Frusciante proposed a specific strategy for the band's initial reunion rehearsals: learning old blues songs and early Red Hot Chili Peppers songs. This foundational approach aimed to relearn "basic rock and roll kind of building blocks" from scratch, which proved instrumental in making the new writing process feel more natural. "John made some really wise suggestions in the very beginning. He was like, let's learn some old blue songs and let's learn some really old red hot chili pepper songs."

**Reservoir of New Material** : Having not written a significant amount of rock music for some time, John Frusciante found himself with a "reservoir of ideas," including chord progressions, arrangements, and vocal melodies. These creative elements began to flow naturally once the band started writing again, contributing significantly to their new material. "He had in him a reservoir of ideas and chord progressions and arrangements and vocal melodies and all these things that he must have been kind of quietly keeping in his back pocket that started to flow."
