**Formation of SST Records** : In a true demonstration of the "do-it-yourself" ethos, Black Flag decided to form their own record label, SST Records, when no major label would sign them. The label was named after an old electronic company and was initially managed directly by the band's guitarist, bassist, and two roadies. "So in true, do it yourself, Sphere, the flag formed their own record label."

**Struggles and Legal Battles** : Black Flag endured several years of struggle and financial difficulty. They nearly lost a significant legal battle with a major label, an event that could have potentially ended both the band and their newly formed independent label. Despite these obstacles, they eventually mastered the complexities of running an independent company and achieved considerable success. "Black Flag struggled for a few years, almost losing a legal battle with a major label that would have killed both the band and their label, but eventually they got the hang of running an independent company and did quite well."

# genre - Alternative
## curiosities
**Emergence as Umbrella Term** : The term "alternative" became the preferred word among fans and critics to describe a new wave of music that diverged from the mainstream. This term gained prominence as collective sales of these distinct bands grew into millions of records and concert tickets annually. "The word that fans and critics gravitated to was alternative."

**Murky Etymology of "Alternative Music"** : The precise origin and etymology of the phrase "alternative music" are considered uncertain or "murky." Several theories exist regarding how the term came to be widely adopted. "Well, the etymology of the phrase alternative music is murky, but let's try."

**Theory 1: 1970s FM Radio** : One theory suggests that "alternative" initially described certain FM radio stations in the 1970s. These stations would play music that was "slightly different" from the corporate rock staples like Styx, Journey, and Boston, occasionally integrating punk or New Wave tracks, thus offering an "alternative" programming choice. "Some say it originally described certain forms of FM radio in the 1970s, the kind of stations that would play slightly different music than all the rest."

**Theory 2: Independent Record Labels** : Another theory posits that the term originated from independent record labels. Bands whose sound was considered too "weird" or "radical" to secure deals with major labels had "no alternative" but to sign with smaller independent companies like SST, Epitaph, Berserkeley, Rounder, IRS, or Alternative Tentacles. Consequently, music recorded for these "alternative labels" became known as "alternative rock." "Another theory involves independent record labels."

**Distinct from Mainstream** : Regardless of its precise origin, the music labeled "alternative" was fundamentally seen as an alternative to the mainstream. It represented sounds and artists that were not part of the commercial establishment. "And yes, this music was an alternative to the mainstream."

**Campus Radio's Role in Popularization** : Campus radio stations played a critical role in the growth and networking of the alternative scene. They specialized in playing music from independent labels and, as they became more influential and connected through magazines and conventions, they began consistently referring to this music as "alternative." "Campus stations specialized in this stuff."

**Lineage Outside Mainstream Rock** : The word "alternative" eventually became a broad umbrella term for rock music that was distinct from the lineage of bands like The Beatles, The Rolling Stones, and Led Zeppelin. Instead, its roots were traced to artists such as David Bowie, Iggy Pop, The Velvet Underground, and The Ramones. "the word alternative became more widely used as this umbrella term for rock music that was different that did not descend from the Beatles and the Stones and Led Zeppelin, but from Bowie and Iggy Pop and the Velvet Underground and the Ramones."

**Birth of the "Alternative Nation"** : The proliferation and acceptance of this music in the 1990s led to the coining of the term "alternative nation," signifying the widespread cultural impact of this genre. "It was the birth of what we call in the 90s the alternative nation."

# genre - New Wave
## curiosities
**Marketing Plan for Less Threatening Punk** : New Wave was conceived by record labels as a marketing strategy to sell the "less threatening cousins" of punk rock. It aimed to make the edgy energy of punk more palatable and commercially viable for a wider audience. "Now remember that this is a marketing plan, a way for the record labels to sell punk's less threatening cousins."

**Crucial for Music Videos and MTV** : New Wave played an extremely important role in the history of rock and roll by fostering the development of music videos. It is argued that without New Wave, the form of music videos and the existence of MTV as we know it might never have materialized. "And it was certainly extremely important to the history of rock and roll because you can make the argument that without new wave, there might have never been music videos in the way that we had music videos."

**Visual Emphasis and Image** : New Wave was significantly influenced by MTV, as the channel's existence made visual appearance a critical factor in an artist's success. Marketers and directors began to emphasize "looks and glitz," often leading to style surpassing substance, with New Wave bands being particularly well-suited to this visual medium. "MTV quickly began to affect the course of music because it began to really matter what you looked like."

**Antidote to Punk Nihilism and Recession** : Emerging after the "nihilism of punk" and during the "brutal recession of the early 80s," New Wave offered an antidote. It embraced "fun and romanticism," took elements from the past with a "modern sheen," and sang about "love in the future" rather than present-day despair, providing an escape and optimism. "After the nihilism of punk and all of this talk about burning down the past and rebuilding the present, people were actually ready for something new."

**Explosion in North America Post-Duran Duran** : Following the commercial success of Duran Duran through their music videos, New Wave rapidly "exploded in North America." **Crossover of Punk and Domestic Bands** : Once the initial wave of British New Wave bands gained acceptance, other acts with punk roots, such as The Clash, The Cure, and Billy Idol, managed to break through. "The New Wave era started in 1979 and had a good run before it was done by, oh at least 1985."

"Instead the major record labels picked off the most commercially acceptable bits called it New Wave and proceeded to bleed a dry."
