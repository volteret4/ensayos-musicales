## albums
**Day for Night (1994)** : Featured the first track performed by the band on Saturday Night Live. It spawned six singles.
**Trouble at the Henhouse (1996)** : This album followed "Day for Night" and produced five singles.
**Live Between Us (1996)** : A concert album recorded at Cobo Hall in Detroit.
**Phantom Power (1999)** : Released in mid-1999. It features the Juno Single of the Year, "Bobcaygeon."
**Road Apples (c. 1991)** : The bulk of this, their second album, was written on the stage at Queen's University approximately 25 years before 2016.
**Man Machine Poem (2016)** : An album that was completed and scheduled for release in 2016, preceding their final tour.
**Now For Plan A (undated)** : This album was released four years before "Man Machine Poem," marking a significant break in their album release schedule.
**Fully Completely (Reissue)** : Mentioned as one of two box sets, including a reissue of this album.
**EP (undated)** : The band released one EP as part of their extensive body of work spanning three decades.
**Album Release Frequency** : The band released a new album roughly every two years between 1987 and 2006, then every three years until 2012, followed by a four-year break before "Man Machine Poem."
**Sales Performance** : Every album released by the band sold hundreds of thousands of copies in Canada, with at least three records selling over a million copies.

## songs
**Bobcaygeon (1999)** : From the album "Phantom Power," this song was awarded the Juno Single of the Year in 2000. Gord Downey described it as being about a stressed-out city cop who loved visiting the Kawartha Lakes region of Ontario, with the title chosen merely because it rhymed with "constellations."
**Montreal (unreleased)** : An unreleased track that the band occasionally performed live. It addresses the events of December 6, 1989, when Mark Lapine shot 27 people and killed 14 women at the École Polytechnique in Montreal.

## curiosities
**"Cactus Relationship" Philosophy** : Gord Downey described the band's dynamic as a "cactus relationship," requiring little maintenance or TLC, thriving in "extremely dry environments," and characterized by dropping or fewer expectations of each other.
**Early Performance Venues** : The band learned to perform in front of students and locals at campus pubs and local dives in Kingston, such as Elftees and Lake Femaine.
**Saturday Night Live Lyrical Mishap (undated)** : During a performance on Saturday Night Live, Gord Downey forgot a lyric, mistakenly sang "grades two," before correcting himself to "I come from downtown."
**Saturday Night Live "11" Signal (undated)** : Before their Saturday Night Live performance, Gord Downey agreed to give his 11-year-old nephew a secret signal (two index fingers raised), which preoccupied him during the broadcast.
**"Golden Years" of Success (undated)** : The band experienced a period of "golden years," marked by a glorious run of singles, albums, tours, and appearances at festivals like Edge Fest, Eden Fest, and their own Another Roadside Attraction.
**Creative Evolution** : Over time, the band's song structures became more complex, Gord Downey experimented with vocal styles and phrasing, and their music increasingly incorporated references to Canadian people, events, and places, embodying a distinctive Canadian identity.
**Air Canada Centre Opening Performance (February 1999)** : The Tragically Hip was the very first band to perform at the brand new Air Canada Centre in Toronto.
**Command Performance for the Queen (undated)** : The band gave a command performance for the Queen.
**Grey Cup Halftime Show (undated)** : Performed at halftime for the Grey Cup.
**Canada's Walk of Fame Induction (2002)** : Inducted onto Canada's Walk of Fame.
**Juno Hall of Fame Induction (2005)** : Inducted into the Juno Hall of Fame.
**Royal Conservatory of Music Honorary Fellowship (2006)** : Received an honorary fellowship from the Royal Conservatory of Music.
**Governor General's Performing Arts Award (2008)** : Received a Governor General's Performing Arts Award.
**Tragically Hip Way in Kingston (2012)** : A part of Baruch Street in Kingston was renamed Tragically Hip Way.
**Canada Post Stamp (2013)** : Canada Post issued a Tragically Hip stamp.
**Queen's University Honorary Degrees (2016)** : The band members received honorary degrees from their alma mater, Queen's University in Kingston.
**Order of Canada Appointment (2017)** : The band was appointed to the Order of Canada, with Gord Downey specifically recognized for his leadership in Indigenous issues.
**Modest Public Demeanor** : Despite their significant accomplishments and good fortune, the band members were noted for their modesty and did not expect to be treated like stars.
**Support for Opening Acts** : The band was known for taking their favorite bands on tour as opening acts, including The Rheostatics, Crash Vegas, Eric's Trips, Spirit of the West, Ron Sexsmith, The Inbreds, Change of Heart, and Ashley MacIsaac, to help boost their exposure.
**Social Activism** : The band was involved in social activism, addressing issues related to the environment, social justice, equality, racism, disaster relief (e.g., Haiti), feminist causes, decolonization, and the concerns of Canada's Indigenous people.
**Man-Machine Poem Tour Announcement (May 25, 2016)** : The tour was announced the day after the public learned of Gord Downey's incurable brain cancer diagnosis.
**Man-Machine Poem Tour Ticket Sales (June 3, 2016)** : Tickets for the tour sold out nationwide within minutes.
**Man-Machine Poem Tour Rehearsals (Early May 2016)** : Despite Gord Downey's memory loss from cancer treatment, the band undertook intensive rehearsals of 90 songs, a first for them, with Gord relying on repetition and lyrics displayed on a screen.
**Man-Machine Poem Tour Medical Support** : A dedicated EMS team was hired for Gord Downey, and his surgeon, Dr. Douglas DJ Cook, was present backstage to monitor for potential seizures due to dehydration and sleep deprivation.
**Man-Machine Poem Tour Performance** : Gord Downey completed all scheduled and additional shows, exhibiting more subdued physical movements and using teleprompters for lyrics, but his personality remained at 100%, and audiences often sang along to assist him.
**Man-Machine Poem Tour National Impact** : The tour "galvanized a nation," bringing Canadians together and inspiring numerous cancer treatment fundraisers.
**Man-Machine Poem Tour Final Show Broadcast (August 20, 2016)** : The final show in Kingston was broadcast commercial-free on the CBC, attracting an estimated 12 to 20 million viewers.
**Post-Gord Downey Projects** : Rob Baker and Gord Sinclair have pursued musical projects with other artists; Johnny Fay is involved with country projects; and Paul Langlois produced a record for Kingston country rock artist Greg Ball.
**Cassadora Band** : Two sons of Tragically Hip members formed the band Cassadora, with their fathers serving as advisors.

# artist - Gord Downey

## albums
**Koch Machine Glow (2001)** : His debut solo record, characterized as quirky, with spoken word elements and a low-fi recording style, drawing comparisons to Tom Waits. It reached number 26 on the Canadian charts.
**The Battle of the Nudes (2003)** : His second solo album, which featured a less conceptual and heavier musical approach than his first. It includes the fan-favorite song "Pasco Submarine."
**The Grand Bounce (2010)** : His third solo album, produced by Chris Walla of Death Cab for Cutie. The album features the song "The East Wind."
**Secret Path (2016)** : Released on October 18, 2016, this album was part of a larger multimedia project that included a film and a graphic novel, all dedicated to telling the story of Chanie Wenjack.
**Introduce Yourself (2017)** : A surprise double album released on October 27, 2017. Its recording sessions were completed in February 2017. The album features 23 songs, each dedicated to a specific person, including "The Bruins Kept Us Tight."

## songs
**Nothing but Heartache in Your Social Life (2001)** : A track from "Koch Machine Glow," co-written with Canadian Film Director Adam Goyin.
**Pasco Submarine (2003)** : A fan-favorite song from "The Battle of the Nudes," notable for featuring Gord Downey himself playing the drums.
**The East Wind (2010)** : Featured on "The Grand Bounce," this song includes astute meteorological observations attributed to a farmer, and incorporates quotes from literary figures such as Al Purdy and Walter von Tilburg Clark (specifically, a line from "The Oxbow Incident").
**Son (2016)** : A song from the "Secret Path" album, released October 18, 2016.
**The Bruins Kept Us Tight (2017)** : A track from the "Introduce Yourself" album, specifically directed at his brother Pat, highlighting their shared fandom for the Boston Bruins hockey team.

## curiosities
**"Cactus Relationship" Philosophy** : Gord Downey applied his "cactus relationship" philosophy – characterized by low maintenance and reduced expectations – to his relationships with bandmates and family.
**Hockey Fanatic** : A lifelong hockey fan and player, with Boston Bruins coach and GM Harry Sinden as his godfather.
**Anonymous Goaltending** : To maintain anonymity while playing hockey at a very good outdoor rink in a Toronto East End park, he would dress in full goalie gear, including his mask, at home before walking to the rink.
**Creative Drive Beyond The Hip** : Felt a need to pursue creative projects outside of The Tragically Hip.
**Collaborations with Other Artists** : Collaborated with various artists including Dallas Green, Buck 65, Blue Rodeo, The Sadies, The Rheostatics, and F.D.
**Lake Ontario Waterkeeper Board Member** : Served as a board member for Lake Ontario Waterkeeper, an organization focused on protecting Lake Ontario and its watershed.
**Indigenous Reconciliation Advocacy** : Became deeply involved in advocating for Canada's Indigenous people, leading to the establishment of the Chanie Wenjack Fund.
**Chanie Wenjack Fund** : A project dedicated to supporting reconciliation between Canada's First Nations and other Canadians, inspired by the tragic story of Chanie Wenjack.
**Glioblastoma Diagnosis (December 2015)** : Suffered his first seizure in Kingston in December 2015, leading to a diagnosis of incurable primary glioblastoma, an aggressive brain cancer in his left temporal lobe.
**Treatment Choice** : Opted for a treatment plan that prioritized a better quality of life for a shorter duration, enabling him to accomplish more in his remaining time.
**Brain Surgery (Early 2016)** : Underwent a full temporal lobectomy performed by Dr. Douglas DJ Cook at Kingston General Hospital, involving the removal of his left temporal lobe, hippocampus, and surrounding tissue.
**Post-Surgery Treatment (Early 2016)** : Received 30 rounds of radiation (twice daily, five days a week for six weeks), supplemented by the oral chemotherapy drug Tolozolamide.
**Cognitive Impairments** : Experienced significant memory loss, including forgetting his children's names, confusion, and difficulty forming sentences, often resorting to writing reminders on his hands.
**Public Announcement of Incurable Cancer (May 24, 2016)** : Dr. James Perry of Sunnybrook Hospital in Toronto publicly announced Gord Downey's incurable brain tumor, news that profoundly affected Canada.
**Home Sold, Relocation to Kingston** : His home in the Beaches area of Toronto was sold, and he spent most of his final time residing in Kingston.
**Assembly of First Nations Appearance (December 2016)** : Appeared at a gathering in Gatineau, where he was presented with an eagle feather and given the Lakota spirit name A’sokomanii ("the man who walks among the stars").
**Last Public Singing Performance (February 2, 2017)** : Joined Blue Rodeo and The Sadies on stage at Massey Hall in Toronto to sing "Lost Together," marking his final public vocal performance.
**Order of Canada Investiture (June 2017)** : Attended an investiture ceremony in Ottawa to receive the Order of Canada for his leadership in Indigenous issues.
**We Day Address (July 2, 2017)** : Appeared in Ottawa with Pearl and Daisy Wenjack to deliver a powerful address on the necessity of reconciliation and the historical impact of residential schools.
**Final Days and Death (October 18, 2017)** : Passed away at age 53 on October 18, 2017, the night before, surrounded by family, friends, and bandmates, following rumors of declining health. His death prompted widespread national mourning.
**Posthumous Album Context** : His double album "Introduce Yourself" was released posthumously on October 27, 2017, but its recording sessions had been completed in February 2017.
**Plan for Final Days** : He and his brother Mike had planned to build a log cabin next door to Chanie Wenjack's family as a place for him to live out his final days when he became very sick.

# curiosity

**Chanie Wenjack Story and Reconciliation (undated)** : The tragic story of Chanie Wenjack, a First Nations boy who died of exposure attempting to walk 600 kilometers home after escaping a residential school in Kenora, Ontario, was discovered by Gord Downey's brother Mike and became the genesis for Gord Downey's "Secret Path" project and his advocacy for Indigenous reconciliation.
**Walter von Tilburg Clark's "The Oxbow Incident" (undated)** : A novel by Walter von Tilburg Clark, from which a line ("night is like a room. It makes the little things inside your head too important") was quoted in Gord Downey's song "The East Wind." The novel was adapted into a film.
**The Oxbow Incident Film Adaptation (1943)** : The film adaptation of Walter von Tilburg Clark's novel was directed by William Wellman and released in 1943.
**Casablanca Oscar Win (1943)** : The film "Casablanca," released in 1942, won the Best Picture Oscar for 1943.
**Harry Sinden as Godfather (undated)** : Harry Sinden, then coach and General Manager of the Boston Bruins, served as Gord Downey's godfather.
**Gord Downey's Cancer Diagnosis Public Announcement (May 24, 2016)** : Dr. James Perry, head of the Division of Neurology at Sunnybrook Hospital in Toronto, publicly announced that Gord Downey's brain tumor was incurable, news that deeply impacted Canada.
**Canadian National Mourning for Gord Downey (October 18, 2017)** : Following Gord Downey's death, Canada experienced widespread mourning, characterized by national tributes, flags lowered to half-mast, dimmed lights, moments of silence, and a televised appearance by the Prime Minister in tears.
**"Canada Closed, Death in the Family" Tweet (October 2017)** : A widely circulated tweet that captured the national sentiment of grief following Gord Downey's passing.
**Gord Downey Private Memorial (October 20, 2017)** : A very private memorial service for Gord Downey was held for family and friends two days after his death.
**Dr. Seuss Quote on Loss (undated)** : The quote "Don't cry because it's over. Smile because it happened" was used by Alan Cross to commemorate Gord Downey's life and legacy.
**Polaris Prince Cruise Ship Reconciliation Room (undated)** : The Polaris Prince, a cruise ship operating in the Canadian North, features a designated area called the "Gord Downey Chanie Wenjack Room," specifically reserved for facilitating "tough and sensitive conversations" about reconciliation issues.
