## genres

Punk Rock

## venues

## songs

**One Way or Another (1978)** : This track is identified as an exemplary New Wave song that, while possessing the right attitude and "do-it-yourself" philosophy, transcended pure punk rock. # artist - Devo

## genres

New Wave

## albums

**Are We Not Men? We Are Devo! (1978) – Debut Album** : This album served as a major introduction to the New Wave sound. It featured a cover of The Rolling Stones' "Satisfaction" that highlighted the genre's unique direction by departing from the expected speed, intensity, and anger of punk.

## songs

**(I Can't Get No) Satisfaction (1978)** : A cover of the Rolling Stones classic that caused confusion among listeners due to its departure from punk norms. The track's unique arrangement served to highlight how New Wave differed from the angry, high-speed style of traditional punk.

# artist - The Pretenders

## members

Chrissie Hynde

## genres

New Wave
Punk

## curiosities

**Chrissie Hynde's Punk Roots** : Originally from Akron, Ohio, Hynde moved to the UK and became deeply embedded in the early punk scene, working at Malcolm McLaren's "Sex" shop and writing for British music magazines. She played guitar in early iterations of bands that would become The Clash and The Damned, and even considered a marriage of convenience with Sid Vicious to stay in the country.
**Bridge Between Eras** : Formed in 1978 with three English musicians, the band was viewed as a crucial bridge between old-school punk and New Wave. They were described as an "edgy, punky band with a gift for songwriting," epitomizing the New Wave sound through Hynde's strong understanding of melody.

# artist - The B-52s

## genres

New Wave

## albums

**The B-52's (1979) – Debut Album** : This album featured the song "Planet Claire" and established the band's quirky, silliness-driven approach to rock music.

## songs

**Planet Claire (1979)** : This track features a mysterious Morse code sound which is actually a "call tape" or "ZKR tape" from a Canadian forces base in Nova Scotia. The code informed ships about optimal radio frequencies for communications, specifically "NAWSDECFH-ZKRF13394".

## curiosities

**Formation and Philosophy** : The band formed over cocktails in a Chinese restaurant in Athens, Georgia, with the specific goal of countering the seriousness of rock music with silliness. They were a mixed-gender and multi-sexual band that rehearsed in the blood-letting room of an abandoned mortuary.
**DIY Instrumentation and Image** : To achieve their specific sound, their guitarist sometimes replaced lower guitar strings with bass strings. Visually, they cultivated a unique look using 1950s thrift-shop clothing and bouffant hairdos, which inspired their name.
**Impact on Kurt Cobain** : While initially dismissed by some as a novelty act, their 1980 performance on _Saturday Night Live_ reportedly transfixed a young Kurt Cobain.

# artist - The Knack

## genres

New Wave

## albums

**Get The Knack (1979) – Debut Album** : Recorded for Capitol Records on a modest budget of $18,000, the album was completed in only two weeks. Released on June 11, 1979, it sold 1 million copies by June 24, making it Capitol's fastest-selling debut since The Beatles in 1964.

## curiosities

**Economic Industry Impact** : The "astounding" profits generated by the band's debut proved to record labels that New Wave could be a path out of the late-70s economic downturn. However, the band faced a severe industry backlash due to their rapid success, and American radio skepticism often hindered the traction of their subsequent work.

# genre - New Wave

## curiosities

**Term Origins** : The term was first coined in the late 1950s to describe French filmmakers and was later applied by music critics in the early 1970s to artsy bands like The New York Dolls and The Velvet Underground. In the UK, it was used by fanzines like _Sniff and Glue_ and managers like Malcolm McLaren to describe punk-adjacent bands with artsy ambitions.
**Musical Characteristics** : New Wave is defined by more melody and pop sensibilities than punk, often incorporating quirkiness, nerdist tendencies, and a less political tone. Structurally, it moved away from blues, favoring twitchy, spiky, and agitated arrangements with "jittery" or "yelpie" vocal deliveries.
**Visual and Social Identity** : The genre is associated with a suburban vibe, robotic/jerky dance styles, and a notable presence of sexual fluidity rather than traditional sexual bravado. Its fashion is famously defined by skinny ties and large glasses.
**Economic Advantage** : During the global recession of the late 1970s, record labels found New Wave bands significantly cheaper to produce and market than established mainstream acts, which led to a surge in signings.
**American Radio Resistance** : Despite its popularity, American FM Rock Radio was often hostile toward the genre, with some stations prohibiting DJs from even using the term "New Wave" on the air.

# genre - Post-Punk

## curiosities

**The Direct Aftermath** : Spanning roughly from 1978 to the mid-1980s, post-punk was the musical fallout of the punk explosion. While it did not sound like traditional punk, it relied on the barriers punk had already dismantled, eventually laying the groundwork for the 1990s alternative revolution.

---

# genre - Punk Rock

## curiosities

**The 1976 Explosion** : Defined by an angry, thrashing, two-to-three chord style, punk fundamentally changed music by dismantling social and artistic barriers. By 1978, the original sound felt "so 1976," but it triggered a chain reaction that birthed New Wave and Post-Punk.

# label - Sire Records

## curiosities

**Marketing Strategy** : Headed by Seymour Stein, the label struggled to get punk acts like The Ramones on American radio because the term "punk" was perceived as dangerous and violent.
**The "Don't Call It Punk" Campaign** : Around Christmas 1976, Sire launched an advertising campaign to rebrand their artists as "New Wave" to wipe away the stigma of punk and gain mainstream radio access.

# curiosity

**The 1978 Pivot** : 1978 is cited as a pivotal year where popular music began to segment into hundreds of distinct streams. Before this, music was largely categorized into four buckets: rock, pop, country, and R&B.
**MTV Launch (1981)** : MTV launched on August 1, 1981, with a limited library of only 250 videos. Rod Stewart's videos appeared 17 times on the first day alone. The channel's need for content led it to import high-fashion British videos, fueling a "second British invasion".
**John Hughes Soundtracks** : Director John Hughes helped popularize New Wave by using "edgy" (often British) bands in films like _16 Candles_ and _The Breakfast Club_, largely because these tracks were inexpensive to license.
