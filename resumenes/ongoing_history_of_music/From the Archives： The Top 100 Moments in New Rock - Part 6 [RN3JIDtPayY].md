## curiosities
**Formation Origin - Pickup Basketball Game (1989)** : The band's genesis began in Seattle when Stone Gossard and Jeff Ament formed Love Company and sought a singer by circulating a demo. Jack Irons, a former Red Hot Chili Peppers drummer, received a copy of this demo and passed it to Eddie Vedder, a surfer and night shift worker at a Chevron tank farm in San Diego, after a pickup basketball game in 1989. Eddie then wrote lyrics to three of the demo's five songs, dubbed them onto a new cassette, and sent it to Seattle, leading to his invitation to jam with the group and the formation of Pearl Jam. "This cassette passed out of a gym bag sometime in 1989 might have resulted in the most profitable pickup basketball game ever played."
**Eddie Vedder's Audition Tape Creation** : After receiving a demo tape from Jack Irons, Eddie Vedder listened to it repeatedly. Following a night shift and a surfing session, he sat down at home and wrote lyrics for three of the five songs on the tape. He then recorded his vocals and the new lyrics onto a cassette and mailed it to Seattle, where Jeff Ament and Stone Gossard immediately recognized his talent, solidifying his role as their frontman. "As soon as Eddie got the tape from Jack, he started listening to it over and over."

## songs
**Smells Like Teen Spirit (1990)** : The title of this iconic song originated from an act of vandalism by Kathleen Hanna, a friend of Kurt Cobain and member of Bikini Kill. During a night of drinking in the winter of 1990, Hanna spray-painted "Kurt smells like teen spirit" on Cobain's bedroom wall in Olympia, Washington, referencing a trendy girls' deodorant used by Toby Vail (another Bikini Kill member whom Kurt liked). Kurt, who didn't wear deodorant, misinterpreted this as a compliment about embodying teenage rebellion, only learning the true, less profound inspiration after the song's writing, recording, and release. "It wasn't until after the song was written, recorded, and released that Kurt found out what had actually inspired one of the greatest rock and roll songs of all time."

## curiosities
**UK Influence and Guitar-Based Pop Revival (1982-1987)** : Between 1982 and 1987, The Smiths were one of the most influential UK bands, credited with almost single-handedly rescuing guitar-based pop music from extinction in the UK at a time when synthesizers dominated. They distinguished themselves by crafting guitar-driven songs featuring strong hooks and intense lyrics, becoming icons and heroes to a generation of disaffected and alienated alternative fans. "Between 1982 and 1987, the Smiths almost single-handedly rescued guitar-based pop music from extinction in the UK."
**Breakup Announcement (September 12, 1987)** : Internal conflicts, including personality clashes, drug use, and disputes with their record label, led to the inevitable breakup of The Smiths. Rumors circulated in the British press throughout the spring of 1987, and on August 18, 1987, singer Morrissey informed producer Steven Street of his intention to leave for a solo career. The official public announcement of the band's dissolution came on September 12, 1987, a traumatic event for hardcore fans who deeply connected with the band's music, comparable in impact to the Beatles' 1970 breakup for a specific generation. "On August 18th, 1987, Morrissey, the singer, sent a letter to Smith's producer, Steven Street, telling him that he intended to break up the band so we could pursue a solo career."

# concert - Woodstock 1999

## curiosities
**Riots and Unsanitary Conditions (July 26, 1999)** : Woodstock 1999, held on July 26th, 1999, at an old Air Force base in Rome, New York, to commemorate the original festival's 30th anniversary, devolved into widespread riots. The event was plagued by extreme heat (38 degrees Celsius), exorbitant prices for basic necessities like food and water (a small pizza cost $12), and severely unsanitary campground conditions. These factors, combined with testosterone-fueled performances, pushed the crowd to a breaking point, resulting in bonfires, destruction of equipment and fences, looting of vendor booths and a nearby hardware store, the forced evacuation of a TV crew, and the burning of 12 tractor trailers. The aftermath included charges of rape and sexual assault, marking it as "not Rock's finest hour." "The riots at Woodstock, July 26th, 1999, New Rock Moment number 50."

# instrument - Precision Bass

## curiosities
**Introduction by Fender Guitar Company (1951)** : In 1951, Leo Fender's guitar company quietly launched the Precision Bass, a revolutionary solid-body electric bass guitar. Designed to be lightweight and portable, it was worn around the neck, featured four thick strings, and had frets for precise note placement, distinguishing it from the bulky, fretless acoustic stand-up double bass. Its introduction was a bold move before the rise of rock and roll, with early models bundled with a 26-watt amp and 15-inch speaker to create a market for it. "In 1951, Leo Fender's guitar company quietly released a brand new invention. They called it the precision bass."

# instrument - Electric Bass Guitar

## curiosities
**Revolutionizing Bass in Rock and Roll (1950s)** : The electric bass guitar, particularly the Precision Bass introduced by Fender in 1951, transformed music by offering a fat, heavy bottom end in a portable, amplified format. As rock and roll gained traction in the 1950s, the electric bass became increasingly popular, gradually pushing the upright double bass out of rock music by 1957 because, as music grew louder, the demand for electric instrumentation increased. By the 1960s, many companies, including those offering six-string models, were producing electric bass guitars, making the instrument indispensable to modern music. "The introduction of the solid body electric bass guitar by the Fender Guitar Company in 1951."

# instrument - Stand-up Double Bass

## curiosities
**Predecessor to Electric Bass (Pre-1950s)** : Before the 1950s, bass notes in music were primarily produced by the stand-up double bass, a large, bulky acoustic instrument often taller than the musician playing it. While it provided the necessary low end, its size and lack of amplification made it less practical as music styles, particularly rock and roll, demanded louder and more portable instruments, leading to its eventual replacement by the electric bass guitar in rock music. "But up until the 1950s, bass notes came from a big, bulky acoustic instrument that was often taller than the person playing it."

**Napster's Origin and Internet Disruption (June 1, 1999)** : Napster was created by Sean Fanning, a bored computer science student at Northeastern University, in his dorm room using a $7,000 notebook computer in 1999. Frustrated by the difficulties of finding and downloading MP3s, Fanning, encouraged by his uncle John, developed a superior music application with help from Sean Parker and Jordan Ritter. Launched on June 1, 1999, Napster was initially shared freely with 30 friends on the condition of secrecy. It rapidly spread to over 15,000 users in days, fundamentally altering the entire music industry and initiating a continuous transformation. "Sean gave the program the nickname people had given him because of his short, curly, nappy hair.
