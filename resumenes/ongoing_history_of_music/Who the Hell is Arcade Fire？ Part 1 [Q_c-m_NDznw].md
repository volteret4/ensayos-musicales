## curiosities
**Birthplace and Early Life (1980)** : Win Butler, whose real name is Edwin, is the singer for Arcade Fire. He was born in Truckee, California, a remote location in the northeast corner of the state on the border with Nevada, before his family relocated to The Woodlands, a planned community north of Houston, Texas. "He was born into a place called Truckee, California."
**Religious Upbringing** : Win Butler was raised Mormon, a detail that provides background to his early life and potential influences. "he was raised Mormon."
**Family of Musicians - Grandfather Elvino Ray** : Win Butler comes from a family with a deep musical heritage. His grandfather, Elvino Ray, was a jazz steel guitarist and band leader who worked as a musician for over 70 years, significantly contributing to the invention of the modern electric guitar pickup while employed by Gibson Guitar Corporation. "A guy named Elvino Ray was one of his grandfather's. He was a jazz steel guitarist and band leader who worked as a musician for more than 70 years."
**Family of Musicians - Grandmother King Sisters** : Win Butler's grandmother, Elvino Ray's wife, was a member of The King Sisters, a prominent big band singing group. "A grandmother, Elvino's wife, was a member of the King Sisters, a big band singing group."
**Family of Musicians - Mother Liza** : Win Butler's mother, Liza, was featured on The King Family Show, a variety program that aired on ABC in 1965, linking her directly to the family's musical television presence. "Wins Mom Liza was also on that show."
**Boston Prep School Exposure** : Both Win and his younger brother, William Butler, attended prep school in Boston, where they were exposed to influential bands like Joy Division, New Order, James, The Cure, Echo and the Bunnymen, and The Smiths, shaping their early musical tastes. "And this is where they were exposed to music from. Joy Division, New Order, James the Cure, Echo and the Bunnieman and the Smiths."
**Montreal Religious Studies** : After prep school, Win Butler moved to Montreal to pursue religious studies, first at Concordia University and then at McGill University, which is where he would eventually meet his future bandmate and wife, Regine Chassagne. "Meanwhile, Wins moved to Montreal because he wanted to study religion, first to Concordia, then a McGill."
**Canadian Outsider Perspective - Neon Bible Themes** : After living in Canada for several years, Win Butler began to view the United States from an outsider's perspective, a feeling he says influenced the themes of "dissonance and the occasional feeling of dread" that permeated Arcade Fire's *Neon Bible* album. "When Butler felt a little weirded out after having lived in Canada for a few years. He began to look at the US from an outsider's perspective, something that he says resulted in the record having themes of dissonance and the occasional feeling of dread."

## albums
**Us Kids No (2003) - Debut EP** : This EP was recorded at the Butler family farm in Maine in the summer of 2002 after initial lineup changes. It was known by fans as "Us Kids No" and featured the original recording of "No Cars Go." This release also attracted the attention of Merge Records. "At the time, and this is 2003, the EP was known amongst fans as Us Kids No."
**Funeral (2004) - Breakthrough Debut Album** : Recorded quickly in about a week in the summer of 2003 at Hotel to Tango studio in Montreal and finished by early 2004, this album was released on September 14, 2004. It was titled "Funeral" due to the deaths of several band members' relatives (grandfathers, grandmothers, aunts) during its creation. The album received immediate critical acclaim and attention, leading to club shows, festival appearances globally, a Time Magazine cover, and friendships with figures like David Bowie. It became the best-selling album ever for Merge Records at the time, garnering three Grammy and three Brit Award nominations, and a Juno for Songwriters of the Year, making it the most significant debut album of the decade. "The band called it funeral, because so many of the band's relatives died during its making."
**Neon Bible (2007) - Critically Acclaimed Sophomore Album** : Recorded mostly in the band's self-purchased church headquarters in Farnham, Quebec, with some sessions in New York, this album was released on March 7, 2007. It explored themes of dissonance and dread, influenced by Win Butler's outsider perspective on the US while living in Canada. *Neon Bible* debuted at No. 1 in Canada and Ireland, No. 2 in the US and UK, No. 7 in Australia, and No. 9 in France. It garnered "unanimously ecstatic" reviews (except for Rolling Stone, which felt "a little cold"), became Merge Records' best-selling album, was a finalist for the 2007 Polaris Music Prize, received a Grammy nomination for Best Alternative Album (losing to The White Stripes' *Icky Thump*), and won a Juno in the same category. "They called this album Neon Bible."
**The Suburbs (2010) - Grammy Album of the Year Winner** : This album surprisingly won the Album of the Year award at the 53rd annual Grammy Awards on February 13, 2011, presented by Barbara Streisand. Its win caused a significant public reaction, with many expressing confusion and outrage on social media due to the band's relative unfamiliarity to a mainstream audience, leading to an explosion on Twitter. "And the Grammy goes to The Suburbs, Archie Pires."
