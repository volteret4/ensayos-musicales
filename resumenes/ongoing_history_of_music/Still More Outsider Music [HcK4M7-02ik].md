# genre - Outsider Music

## curiosities
**Origin and Definition** : Outsider music is a genre that evolved from outsider art, also known as art brute, which originated in France in the 1920s and referred to art created by children or the mentally ill. The concept of outsider music began to take hold in the 1950s within the fringes of the jazz world before moving into rock and pop. It is characterized by self-taught musicians who often lack conventional training or adherence to music theory, driven by the sheer joy of expression without concern for external opinions. "No two outsider musicians sound alike."
**Characteristics** : Outsider musicians are often self-taught and may exhibit a naive approach to musical conventions. They do not care about traditions, music theory, or the opinions of others, prioritizing the joy of creation. Many may have intellectual disabilities, mental illnesses like schizophrenia, or simply a left-of-center view of reality, which manifests in their music. A common trait is a childlike quality in their creations. "There is often a childlike quality to what they create."
**Shaping Modern View** : The modern understanding of outsider music was significantly shaped by Irwin Chisett, a music journalist and DJ at WFMU. Chisett championed these artists, featuring their music and releasing compilation CDs titled *Songs in the Key of X*. "Our modern view of outsider music was shaped by Irwin Chisett, a music journalist and a DJ at WFMU, an independent community radio station in East Orange, New Jersey."
**Historical Precedents/Comparisons** : Artists previously covered in discussions of outsider music include Wesley Willis, The Shags, The Legendary Stardust Cowboy, Jandek, Captain B. Fart, Daniel Johnson, Florence Foster Jenkins, and Charles Manson. Brian Wilson of The Beach Boys is also considered a potential inclusion, given his life story. "On those programs, I covered Wesley Willis, the Shags, the legendary Stardust cowboy, Jandic, Captain B. Fart, Daniel Johnson, Florence Foster Jenkins, and even Charles Manson."
**Admiration for Spirit** : The spirit of outsider musicians is to be admired due to their unbridled authenticity and their insatiable need to express themselves through music, regardless of how it sounds to others. They are seen as "unintentional renegades" who should be celebrated for their joy and human spirit in action. "Their spirit is to be admired, even if the music is a little challenging."

## curiosities
**Background and One-Man Band Style** : Hassel Adkins hailed from a very poor rural area of West Virginia. He often performed as a one-man band, simultaneously playing guitar and drums. He is considered a pioneer of psychobilly, an amplified version of rockabilly. "He was from very poor rural West Virginia and dabbled in rock, country, blues, and rock ability, most often performing as a one-man band."
**Lyrical Themes** : His music featured diverse and unusual lyrical content. He sang about sex, but also frequently about chickens and various types of poultry. Other topics included eating peanut butter on the moon, aliens, hot dogs, and occasionally decapitation. "He liked to sing about sex, but he also had songs about chickens and various types of poultry."
**Hero and Idiosyncrasies** : Adkins declared Colonel Sanders of KFC as his hero. He was known to drink eight liters of coffee daily and toured with gogo dancers who would imitate chickens during his performances. "He also declared that his hero was Colonel Sanders of KFC."
**Dance Creations** : Each of his songs was paired with a dance of the same name. He created numerous "chicken dances" like the chicken hop, chicken flop, and chicken wobble. He also pioneered a dance called "the Slop," specifically designed for drunks, involving staggering and falling down. "He pioneered a dance called the Slop, which was designed specifically for drunks."
**Cult Stardom and Record Deal** : After years of obscurity, Hassel Adkins secured an indie record deal, which propelled him to cult stardom. Miles Copeland, head of IRS Records, showed interest in producing an album with him, though it never materialized. "After years of being an unknown, he ended up with an indie record deal that turned him into a cult star."
**Incidents and Health Issues** : Adkins had a history of unusual incidents, including driving off a 70-foot cliff with friends for fun and being jailed for statutory rape. He may have suffered from manic depression and other mental illnesses. "We learned about the time he and a bunch of friends drove off a 70-foot cliff for fun."
**Death and Legacy** : In 2005, just before his 68th birthday, he was run over by a teenager on a quad bike in his front yard and died eleven days later. In 2018, he was inducted into the West Virginia Hall of Fame. "Eleven days after that, he died just short of his 68th birthday. That was, like I said, 2005."
**Documentary** : A documentary about him, titled *The Wild World of Hassel Adkins*, is available for those interested in learning more. "If you want to know more, there's a documentary called The Wild World of Hassel Adkins that is rather interesting."

# curiosity

**Outsider Art Origin (Art Brut)** : The concept of "outsider art," originally known as "art brute," first emerged in France during the 1920s. This term specifically referred to art that transcended the avant-garde, created by individuals who were either children or those suffering from mental illness. "The progenitor of outsider music is outsider art, a concept that first arose in France in the 1920s when it was simply known as art brute."
**Irwin Chisett's Compilations (Songs in the Key of X)** : Irwin Chisett, a music journalist and DJ at WFMU, played a pivotal role in shaping the modern view of outsider music. He championed these artists by featuring their music and releasing a couple of compilation CDs under the title *Songs in the Key of X*. "Chisett loved to feature the music of these artists and even issued a couple of compilation CDs entitled Songs in the Key of X."
