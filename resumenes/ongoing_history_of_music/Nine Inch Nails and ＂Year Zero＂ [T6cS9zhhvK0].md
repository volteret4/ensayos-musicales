## albums
**Year Zero (2007) - ARG Launch Album** : This album is considered one of the most brilliant records of the 21st century, primarily due to its innovative marketing strategy rather than solely its musical content. The release was an incredibly complex undertaking that integrated an Alternate Reality Game (ARG), making risky assumptions about human behavior. Clues were intricately buried in various unconventional places, which fans successfully discovered and connected. The album's launch commenced on February 7, 2007, in Lisbon, Portugal, coinciding with the first night of a European tour, and was officially released on April 17, 2007. The physical CD itself held a hidden clue: if the black label was warmed by a hand, binary codes would appear, leading to more secret websites. Upon its release, the album was uploaded to peer-to-peer networks, prompting immediate global analysis by fans searching for embedded clues, including a mysterious toll-free number.
"The result was one of the coolest, most innovative launches of an album ever."

## songs
**Capital G (2007)** : This song, featured on the "Year Zero" album, was later released on April 26, 2007, in the GarageBand format for Mac computers. This release specifically encouraged fans to remix the track, with its multi-tracks containing additional hidden clues integral to the Alternate Reality Game.
"April 26th, three songs, capital G, my violent heart, and me I'm not were released in the garage band format for Mac computers."
**My Violent Hearts (2007)** : This track was "leaked" when a fan named Anna discovered a USB key containing the song in a bathroom at one of Nine Inch Nails' Lisbon gigs and promptly posted it online. A spectral analysis of the song file subsequently revealed a peculiar image of an alien-looking hand reaching down, which fans quickly christened "The Presence." On April 26, 2007, it was also made available in the GarageBand format for Mac computers to encourage remixing.
"It contained one new 9-inch nails song called My Violent Hearts, which she promptly posted."
**Me I'm Not (2007)** : Another track "leaked" on February 19, 2007, during the European tour when a USB key containing the song was found in Barcelona, Spain. The song file was notably labeled "2432.mp3," and when subjected to a spectrograph, the static within the audio resolved into waveforms that clearly spelled out a phone number (216-338-10) for Cleveland. Calling this number led to a wiretap message about a fictional nightclub massacre. On April 26, 2007, it was released in the GarageBand format for Mac computers, with its multitracks containing further clues for the ARG.
"They contained another song called Me I'm Not, another supposedly leaked or lost song."
**In This Twilight (2007)** : This song was discovered on a USB key found on February 25, 2007, in a bathroom at a gig in Manchester, England. The same USB key also contained an image depicting the famous Hollywood sign completely destroyed, linking the song to the dystopian narrative of the "Year Zero" campaign.
"One is a song called In This Twilight and the other is a picture of the famous Hollywood sign completely destroyed."
**Survivalism (2007)** : This song was released as a single on March 7, 2007, at a concert held at Brixton Academy in London, England. It was distributed to fans via pin drives, which included both an audio file and a video file of the track. Fans later discovered that time codes embedded within the video itself revealed additional websites, serving as further clues in the Alternate Reality Game.
"Survivalism, which was leaked as an audio file and is a video file on March 7, 2007 at a gig in London, England."
**The Great Destroyer (2007)** : As track 13 on the "Year Zero" album, this song contained a pivotal hidden clue. When played back in mono, the audio revealed the phrase "Red Horse Vector," which subsequently led fans to a website called RedHorseVector.net. This website introduced the fictional Red Horse virus, responsible for the mass psychosis that, within the narrative, resulted in the Cleveland nightclub massacre.
"It ended up being track 13 on the album and is called The Great Destroyer."

## curiosities
**Year Zero Alternate Reality Game (ARG) Conception**: Motivated by boredom and a desire to innovate, Trent Reznor conceptualized an Alternate Reality Game to revitalize Nine Inch Nails' music, concerts, fan engagement, and public image. Drawing inspiration from his previous work on video game music for "Quake" and his interest in ARGs like "Mist" and "Halo," he envisioned an immersive experience for the band set 15 years in the future.
"Trent came up with an idea for a concept that placed 9-inch nails about 15 years in the future."
**Collaboration with 42 Entertainment**: Trent Reznor partnered with 42 Entertainment, a company renowned for its "weird, unconventional word of mouth advertising" that specializes in creating immersive experiences through puzzles and scavenger hunts for clients ranging from movie studios to Microsoft. This collaboration was crucial in designing the "Year Zero" ARG for Nine Inch Nails fans.
"Trent contacted a company called 42 Entertainment."
**T-Shirt Clue Discovery in Lisbon**: On February 7, 2007, during the opening night of Nine Inch Nails' European tour in Lisbon, Portugal, local photographer Nuno Foros noticed bolded, seemingly random letters on the back of tour t-shirts. These letters, when deciphered, spelled out "iamtryingtobelieve.com," marking the official beginning of the "Year Zero" Alternate Reality Game.
"Nuno Foros... Notice that random letters in the back of the shirts... were in boldface."
**iamtryingtobelieve.com Website**: This was the inaugural website discovered through the t-shirt clue. It presented a stark warning about a fictional drug called Parapin, which was purportedly introduced into the American water supply. While the government claimed it was an antidote to a bioterror weapon, the website alleged it was part of a more sinister government plot against its own citizens. An email sent to the site's address triggered a chilling automated reply: "I am drinking the water. So should you."
"What they got was a site warning of the dangers of a drug called Parapin, which had been introduced into the American water supply."
**T-Shirt Phone Number Clue**: A week after the Lisbon concert, on February 14, 2007, a fan named Sue discovered bolded numbers on her Nine Inch Nails t-shirt. These numbers formed a phone number for Los Angeles, which, when dialed, played a recorded message featuring a brief presidential address accompanied by background music. This discovery rapidly disseminated among fans worldwide.
"She noticed that the 9-inch nails t-shirt that she had bought in Lisbon had to numbers in boldface."
**USB Key "Leaks"**: As part of the ARG, USB keys containing new songs were deliberately "lost" in bathrooms at various European tour venues. The first instance involved a fan named Anna finding a key in Lisbon with "My Violent Hearts," and another key was found simultaneously in the men's bathroom. Later, on February 19, 2007, a key found in Barcelona contained "Me I'm Not," and another found in Manchester, England, on February 25, 2007, held "In This Twilight" along with an image of the destroyed Hollywood sign.
"A woman named Anna came forward and said that she had found a USB key in the bathroom at one of the Lisbon gigs."
**The Presence Image**: A distinctive, alien-looking hand reaching downwards was revealed through a spectral analysis of the audio file for the song "My Violent Hearts." Fans quickly adopted the moniker "The Presence" for this mysterious image.
"Spectral analysis of the song revealed a weird image of an alien looking hand reaching down."
**Interactive Wheat Field Website**: A meta tag within the "My Violent Hearts" file led to a website that initially displayed a picturesque wheat field. However, moving the mouse across the screen dramatically transformed the serene landscape into a desolate post-nuclear holocaust scene, intensifying the dystopian narrative.
"But if you slid your mouse across the screen, everything turned into some kind of post-nuclear holocaust."
**Cleveland Wiretap Phone Number**: The file "2432.mp3," found alongside the song "Me I'm Not" on a USB key, contained static that, when processed through a spectrograph, clearly resolved into a phone number (216-338-10) for Cleveland. Calling this number played a wiretap recording of a woman screaming about being trapped in a nightclub by police, setting the scene for a tragic event within the "Year Zero" future narrative. This number received nearly 2 million calls.
"When run through a spectrograph, that static resolved itself into waveforms that clearly spelled out a phone number."
**US Wiretap and Bureau of Morality**: The fictional company responsible for the Cleveland wiretap message was named US Wiretap. Its website identified it as a "partnership corporation of the Bureau of Morality." This fictional bureau claimed its agents had intentionally bolted the doors of the Cleveland nightclub because it was a "hangout for the resistance," leading to 112 deaths, which they justified as being "for the good of the nation" within the future timeline.
"The website explained that they were a partnership corporation of the Bureau of Morality."
**Hollywood in Memoriam.org Website**: A USB key discovered in Manchester included an image of the famous Hollywood sign in a state of complete destruction. This image was directly linked to the website hollywoodinmemoriam.org, further expanding the desolate vision of the "Year Zero" narrative.
"Oh, and what's this? Another website? Hollywood in memoriam.org."
**ARG Monitoring and Complexity**: Both Nine Inch Nails headquarters and 42 Entertainment meticulously monitored the Alternate Reality Game, ensuring continuous fan engagement and preventing dead ends. The intricate game involved over 30 interconnected websites, all actively tracked for fan discoveries. This global puzzle fostered collaboration among participants worldwide.
"There were no fewer than 30 websites connected to the game and every one of them had to be monitored for activity."
**Survivalism Video Clues**: The music video for "Survivalism," distributed via pin drives at a London concert, contained hidden time codes. Fans meticulously deciphered these codes to uncover additional websites, highlighting the layered and interactive nature of the ARG's clues.
"Then some fans noticed that time codes on the video revealed more websites."
**Red Horse Virus and Cleveland Massacre Link**: Playing "The Great Destroyer" in mono audio unexpectedly revealed the phrase "Red Horse Vector," which led to the website RedHorseVector.net. This site served as a narrative device, explaining that a fictional Red Horse virus was responsible for a "mass psychosis" that culminated in the Cleveland nightclub massacre, thereby connecting disparate clues within the ARG.
"That was responsible for the mass psychosis that resulted in that nightclub massacre in Cleveland."
**Year Zero CD Binary Code Clue**: The physical "Year Zero" CD itself contained a clandestine clue: applying warmth to its label, such as with a hand, would cause binary codes to appear. These codes, in turn, led to even more secret websites, all of which were continuously monitored by 42 Entertainment for fan activity and discoveries.
"Check out all the binary codes that suddenly appeared."
**Surprise Los Angeles Show (The Raid)**: On April 18, 2007, the day after "Year Zero" was released, a surprise Nine Inch Nails show took place in Los Angeles. Fans who had successfully cracked codes throughout the ARG were given cell phones and then transported on blacked-out buses to an old warehouse. There, they navigated a maze patrolled by armed guards (actors) and were addressed by a figure claiming to be the leader of the Open Source Resistance before the four-song performance began. The concert dramatically concluded with a staged raid by a fake SWAT team, featuring flash grenades, smoke, and screaming, serving as a "semi finale" to the entire game.
"The mission, however, was a surprise 9-inch nail show at the end of this maze."
**Remixing Songs with Hidden Clues**: On April 26, 2007, the songs "Capital G," "My Violent Hearts," and "Me I'm Not" were released in GarageBand format for Mac computers. This initiative explicitly encouraged fan remixes, but more importantly, their multi-tracks were embedded with additional hidden clues for the "Year Zero" ARG, prominently linked to the phrase "Google Exhibit 24."
"What was that hidden in the multi-tracks? More clues? Oh yeah."
**ARG Conclusion and "Hour of the Arrival"**: The "Year Zero" ARG concluded on April 27, 2007, when everything seemingly "went dark." Participants of the surprise LA show received a final, cryptic message on their cell phones: "We think these phones have been compromised. We are going dark for some time. It's up to you now. You are the resistance. This is the hour of the arrival." This message also directed them to hourofarrival.net, implying the continuation of the game's narrative through the actions of the fans themselves.
"This is the hour of the arrival."
**Year Zero TV Series Talks**: Trent Reznor entered discussions with HBO to develop a two-season television series based on the entire "Year Zero" scenario. This indicates a potential long-term expansion of the dystopian alternate reality narrative initially created through the album and its accompanying Alternate Reality Game.
"Ant is apparently in talks with HBO to create a two-season long series based around the whole year-zero scenario."
