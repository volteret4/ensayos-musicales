**Health Issues** : Amy Winehouse struggled with numerous health problems, including bulimia, self-harming, domestic violence issues, public drunkenness, and addiction to various substances like crack, heroin, ketamine, cocaine, and ecstasy. She underwent psychological and psychiatric counseling, and was prescribed drugs for alcohol withdrawal and anxiety. At 24, she was diagnosed with early stage emphysema. "People health problems, bulimia, cutting issues with domestic violence, public drunkenness, crack, heroin, ketamine, coke, ecstasy."
**Relapses and Death (2011)** : Despite going to rehab several times, including a two-week stay in late January 2008, Amy Winehouse experienced a major relapse after a brief period of sobriety, which led to her death by alcohol intoxication on July 23rd, 2011. She had been sober for almost two weeks but began drinking again due to boredom, telling her doctor this three days before her death. The coroner's report stated she spent her last hours drinking vodka alone in her bedroom, watching YouTube videos of herself, leading to her stopping breathing, slipping into a coma, and dying with a blood alcohol level 20% above what is usually fatal. Her security guard found her lying face down in her bed at 2:30 AM and she had not moved by 3 PM the following afternoon when paramedics were called. "She drank so much that she stopped breathing and she slipped into a coma and then she died."

**Anthony Kiedis's Childhood Exposure to Drugs** : Anthony Kiedis was sent to live with his father, Blocky, in Los Angeles, who was a bit actor with a side drug operation. **Anthony Kiedis's Heroin Addiction** : Upon the Red Hot Chili Peppers' formation, Anthony Kiedis's drug habit escalated, with heroin becoming his primary addiction, which he smoked, snorted, and injected. He would disappear from recording sessions for days, becoming a full-blown junkie buying drugs from gang members under a bridge in downtown Los Angeles. "Heroine really was his thing, and I'll smoke it, snort it, and ject it whatever it took."
**Anthony Kiedis's Rehab and Relapses (1994-1997)** : Kiedis's first serious rehab stint led to five years of sobriety, but he relapsed in January 1994 after a wisdom tooth extraction led to prescription narcotics (Vicodin and Percodan) and a return to cocaine and heroin. This cycle of getting straight and relapsing continued until 1997, including a stay at Exodus Recovery Center in Marina Del Rey, California. "His first serious trip to rehab left him sober for five years, but then there was a relapse in January 1994 when he had a wisdom tooth removed and the dentist put him on narcotics for the pain, which was a really bad idea."
**Anthony Kiedis's "Last Chance" Rehab (1997)** : A trip to India sparked a spiritual awakening for Anthony Kiedis. Upon returning to Los Angeles in 1997, he checked into a "last chance" rehab place called the Impact Drug and Alcohol Treatment Center in Pasadena for about a month, a facility known for hosting members of Mötley Crüe and Brent Gerowicz of Bad Religion. This visit initially seemed successful. "A trip to India put him in a spiritual place, and when he got back to LA, he checked into a last chance rehab place called the Impact Drug and Alcohol Treatment Center in Pasadena for about a month."
**Anthony Kiedis's Motorcycle Accident and Painkiller Addiction (1997)** : Following a severe motorcycle accident, Anthony Kiedis was prescribed Codeine, Percocet, and OxyContin. Due to his developed tolerance to chemicals, these medications were ineffective against the pain of nine months of physical rehabilitation for a shattered arm. On his 35th birthday, November 1, 1997, he checked into a rehab unit called Steps, but this attempt was also unsuccessful. "The pain of nine months of physical rehab for a shattered arm were extremely painful."
**Anthony Kiedis's Long-term Sobriety and Health Consequences** : By the end of the 1990s, Anthony Kiedis had successfully overcome his addictions and has reportedly remained clean since. However, his past intravenous drug use resulted in a case of Hepatitis C. "All that Ivy drug use resulted in a case of hep C, but at least he's still alive."
**Anthony Kiedis's Drug-buying Bridge** : Kiedis has never revealed the exact location of the bridge in Los Angeles where he used to buy drugs, fearing it might become an unusual fan shrine. Detective work suggests it is likely a bridge in MacArthur Park leading into Wilshire Boulevard. "He's never said where that bridge is because he's afraid that it might become some kind of weird fan shrine."

**Personal Struggles and Grandmother's Death (Mid-Late 1990s)** : Trent Reznor suffered from depression and a social anxiety disorder.
