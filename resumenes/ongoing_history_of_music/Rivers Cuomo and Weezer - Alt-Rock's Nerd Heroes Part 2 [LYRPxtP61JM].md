## albums
**Make Believe (2005)** : This was the band's fifth album, released in 2005. It was a very big album for the band, selling around 2 million copies, a notable achievement during the era of music piracy and file sharing. Following its album cycle, rumors circulated that Weezer was breaking up. "Make Believe was a very big album for the band, selling somewhere around 2 million copies."
**Deliverance Is At Hand** : This was a real album, teased by online clues and featuring a more experimental side of Weezer. All band members contributed songs, and Rivers Cuomo experimented with moving away from the traditional verse-chorus-verse songwriting structure. Eight tracks from the album were leaked, which forced the label to move up its release date. "Not only was everyone of the band contributing songs, but River was trying to get away from the traditional verse chorus, verse way of writing material."
**Red Album** : This was the band's sixth album, following the Blue Album (1994) and Green Album (2001). It was self-titled and simply known as the Red Album due to its color. The album marked the beginning of a very prolific period for the band and included an interview with the host incorporated into the liner notes. "The red album marked the beginning of a very prolific period for the band."
**Ratitude (2009)** : This album's title was suggested to Rivers Cuomo by actor Rainn Wilson. The cover features a dog named Sydney, whose photo was found in an August 2009 issue of National Geographic, and permission was obtained from the dog's owner to use it. For this record, Patrick Wilson moved from drums to guitar, and Josh Freese was hired as the drummer. "Little more than a year later, we got Ratitude, the title suggested to Rivers by Rain Wilson, the actor."
**Hurley (2010)** : This album was named after the character played by Jorge Garcia in the TV series Lost, with a cropped picture of Rivers Cuomo hugging Garcia on the cover. The photo was taken in the green room of the talk show "Lopez Tonight," where both were guests. While originally considered another self-titled, color-coded album, fans refer to it as Hurley. "Next up was the 2010 album entitled Hurley, named after the character in the TV series Lost."
**Death to False Metal (2010)** : This compilation, officially considered the band's ninth album, was a collection of odds and ends, featuring previously unreleased tracks. It was released around the same time as a re-issue of Pinkerton, which garnered more attention. The cover was inspired by a Jehovah's Witness pamphlet that Rivers Cuomo had kept since the Red Album days. "So instead, we ended up getting death-to-false metal."
**Pinkerton (1996)** : This was the band's second album, originally released in 1996. By 2010, when Death to False Metal was released, Pinkerton was finally receiving the recognition and praise that had eluded it upon its initial release. Its re-issue coincided with the release of Death to False Metal. "Pinkerton, of course, being the second album. And by now, Pinkerton was getting the recognition and praise that escaped it when it was first released in 1996."
**Everything Will Be All Right in the End (2014)** : Released in October 2014, this album followed a four-year break from proper records after Hurley (2010). It was the third and final album produced by Ric Ocasek of The Cars, who had previously worked on the Blue and Green Albums and brought Weezer's sound back to its early days. The album's creation was a lengthy process, involving the consideration of over 200 songs, 20 recorded, and about a dozen selected. "And they called it, Everything Will Be All Right in the End."
**White Album (2016)** : Weezer's tenth album, released on April Fool's Day 2016, marked a return to using colors for album titles. Following the mixed commercial success of "Everything Will Be All Right in the End," the band decided to experiment again. It featured a new producer, Jack Sinclair, a longtime Weezer fan who also fronted a tribute band called Wannabeezer, and who possessed deep insights into the band's sound. "Weezer's 10th album went back to using colors."
**Pacific Daydream (2018)** : This album followed the White Album, originally intended to be the "Black Album" but the songs weren't dark enough. Rivers Cuomo continued to use his spreadsheet songwriting technique, enlisting programmers to organize lyrical fragments with specific attributes in an even more granular way. The album received good reviews and a Grammy nomination for Best Rock Album. "Pacific Dream got good reviews and also got the band another Grammy nomination for Best Rock Album."
**Teal Album (2019)** : This album was released digitally in January 2019 without warning, consisting entirely of cover songs. Rivers Cuomo used a computer program that analyzed Spotify data to identify popular songs not tagged as Alt Rock or Classic Rock, released before 1994, to select the tracks. The band aimed to play the top 10 songs as accurately as possible, with TLC's "No Scrubs" being the only exception to the 1994 rule because "it was just cool." "The Teal album, and it showed up in January 2019 with no warning whatsoever."
**Black Album (2019)** : This was officially Studio Album No. 15, released after the Teal Album's two-month run. Its release was preceded by a clever internet marketing campaign: a Yelp profile appeared on October 9, 2018, shared by the official Weezer Twitter account, with reviews containing mysterious lyrics from the new album, all about places with "Black" in their name. A new song featuring these lyrics, "Can't Knock the Hustle," was released two days later. "Once the Teele album had its run, which was all two months, it was finally time for the Black Album."
**OK Human (2021)** : Issued on January 29, 2021, this studio record's title is a direct response to Radiohead's "OK Computer." To promote the album, Weezer collaborated with the creators of the Roomba Robot Vacuum, offering a contest to win one of five "Weezer Roombas" (spelled with a silent W). The campaign began with a social media message stating, "study show that nine out of ten humans have dreamed of Weezer cleaning their house." "Weezer issued OK Human, another studio record, and yes, the title is in response to Radiohead's OK Computer."
**Van Weezer (2019)** : This album was a return to Rivers Cuomo's metal band roots before Weezer, offering a heavier sound while still maintaining the band's distinctive style. The title itself suggests a blend of "Van Halen" and "Weezer," indicating its heavier, rock-oriented direction. "This was a return to that time, sort of."
**Seasons project (2022)** : This project involved the release of new EPs on the first days of spring, summer, fall, and winter in 2022. It represents a continuous stream of new material from the band. "For example, there was the Seasons project. New EPs released on the first days of spring, summer, fall, and winter of 2022."
**Album 6 (Fake)** : A mysterious website, not affiliated with Weezer, that appeared online and directed fans to the official Weezer website. It initially promoted a fake album called "Toot En Song" but was part of the build-up to the real "Deliverance Is At Hand." "Another clue appeared in the form of a mysterious website not affiliated with Weezer called Album 6, which directed fans to the official Weezer website."
**Toot En Song (Fake)** : A fake album promoted by the "Album 6" website, which turned out to be part of the misinformation surrounding the release of "Deliverance Is At Hand." "And it started promoting an album called Toot En Song, which turned out to be fake."

## songs
**Beverly Hills (2005)** : Rivers Cuomo conceived this song after attending the opening of a new phase of the Hollywood Bowl, where he saw pictures of the girl group Wilson Phillips in the program. This led him to ponder marrying an established celebrity. Initially approached seriously with a swing beat, it evolved into a sarcastic track with a chunky rhythm. It was the first Weezer song to use a talk box on a guitar. "It was also the first Weezer song to use a talk box on a guitar."
**Pig** : A demo that was leaked and then deleted online. Metadata analysis revealed it was a song from something called "Deliverance Is At Hand," serving as an early clue to the band's next project. "A demo called Pig was leaked and then deleted."
**The Greatest Man That Ever Lived** : Towards the end of this song, there is a vocal breakdown featuring a three-part counterpoint, which Rivers Cuomo described as sounding like Renaissance vocal polyphony. He learned this technique from studying music theory and composition, noting its rarity in modern rock and pop. "It almost sounds like Renaissance vocal polyphony."
**Pork and Beans** : This song was an instant favorite at radio stations due to its quirky nature. The distinctive "chipmunk sort of sound" in the song is Rivers Cuomo's voice, sped up an octave after saying "hey." The video for the song featured appearances by prominent internet stars of the day, capturing the zeitgeist of the moment. "It actually sounds like a timble and type of production."
**I Want To (If You Want To)** : This song had an "ungainly title" but performed well on alt-rock radio. It was featured on the Ratitude album. "Ungainly title, if you're wondering if I want to, I want to. But it did well in all rock radio, that Fallen Winter."
**Memories (2010)** : This single from the Hurley album was cross-marketed to promote the movie Jackass 3D, with cast members singing in the background. The video was shot using old Super-8 cameras, giving it a nostalgic feel. "In a bit of cross-marketing, it was also used to promote the movie, Jackass 3D."
**Turning Up the Radio (2010)** : The first song on the "Death to False Metal" album, this track was the result of a fan collaboration project called "Let's Write a Song" (spelled S-A-W-N-G). Fans submitted ideas for song titles, chord progressions, lyrics, and melodies through YouTube videos. The final song incorporated ideas from 16 people, in addition to Rivers Cuomo. "It's called Turning Up the Radio, and it's the result of fans submitting ideas for a new song."
**Back to the Shack** : This was the first single for the "Everything Will Be All Right in the End" album. It was debuted at a party in Nassau during the second Weezer Cruise in 2014, giving fans an early listen. "On the second cruise, the first single for the new album was debuted at a party on NASA."
**California Kids** : A song from the White Album, it was explicitly stated that this song was written with the aid of Rivers Cuomo's Excel spreadsheet songwriting technique. This highlights the band's innovative and analytical approach to composition. "Here's a song from the White Album that was written with the help of an Excel spreadsheet."
**Feels Like Summer** : One of the singles from the Pacific Daydream album, which earned good reviews and a Grammy nomination for Best Rock Album. This song was created using Rivers Cuomo's advanced spreadsheet technique, where programmers helped organize lyrical fragments with specific attributes. "Here's one of those singles. It's called Feels Like Summer."
**Africa (1982)** : Weezer's cover of the Toto hit from 1982. The cover originated from a persistent request, or "demand," from a 14-year-old fan in Cleveland named Mary Kinchen, who tweeted at the band. After initially covering Toto's "Rosanna," Weezer eventually recorded "Africa," releasing it digitally and as a limited edition 7-inch single. "The song that got the most attention was Weezer's version of Africa, the total hit from 1982."
**Rosanna** : A Toto song that Weezer initially covered after a fan's request for "Africa." This cover was released as a response to Mary Kinchen's tweet, before the band eventually recorded "Africa." "That, however, was not the end of it."
**No Scrubs (1999)** : A TLC song covered on the Teal Album. This was the only song on the album that broke the self-imposed rule of covering songs released before 1994, because the band simply found it "cool." "It made the cut because the band decided that it was, well, it was just cool."
**Can't Knock the Hustle (2018)** : This new song was released on October 11, 2018, and featured mysterious lyrics that had been embedded in Yelp reviews as part of the promotional campaign for the Black Album. The Yelp profiles appeared on October 9, 2018, on places with "Black" in their name. "On October 11th, 2018, a new song was released. It featured those mysterious lyrics in the Yelp reviews, and it was called Can't Knock the Husse."

## curiosities
**Quirky and Eccentric Band Type** : Weezer exemplifies the "quirky, eccentric, and weird" type of band that makes music fun and unpredictable. They balance this with widespread appeal, possessing "just enough of the nerd factor" to stand out without alienating audiences. This requires songwriting skills, careful image management, and creativity. "And among the very, very, very, very best of this class is Weezer."
**Formula for Success** : Weezer has perfected a formula that has worked for decades, combining musical talent, wit, self-deprecation, left-of-center thinking, a desire for fun, a willingness to experiment, clever marketing, and, crucially, an open approach with their fans. This inclusive strategy has been key to their longevity. "They have perfected a formula that includes musical talent, wit, self-deprecation, left of center thinking, a desire to have fun, a willingness to experiment, some very clever marketing, and above all, to let their fans in on everything."
**Demos Widely Shared** : As early as 2005, Weezer was sharing demos widely with fans through their website, which was a novel approach at the time. This practice demonstrated their commitment to transparency and involving their fanbase, as exemplified by a slightly different mix demo of "Beverly Hills" from "Make Believe." "Demos like that were widely shared by Weezer with fans through their website, something that was pretty novel for 2005."
**Post-Make Believe Breakup Rumors** : After the "Make Believe" album cycle, rumors circulated that Weezer was breaking up. This was fueled by Rivers Cuomo returning to Harvard, his marriage, and other band members focusing on side projects, along with Rivers' own quote in an interview suggesting the band was "done for the moment." "However, after this album cycle was completed, there were rumors that Weezer was breaking up."
**Rivers Cuomo's Personal Life & Hiatus** : Following the "Make Believe" album, Rivers Cuomo returned to Harvard to finish his English degree. He married Kyoko Ito on June 18, 2006, after meeting her in 1997, with a ceremony on a remote beach near Malibu and a honeymoon in Japan. During this period, he made a comment during his honeymoon interview suggesting the band might be "done," which contributed to breakup fears when no new album appeared in 2006 or 2007. "Rivers returned to Harvard to finish his degree in English. He got married to Kyoto, Ito on June 18, 2006."
**Band Members' Side Projects & Acting** : During Weezer's hiatus after "Make Believe," drummer Patrick Wilson and bassist Scott Shriner worked on a group called The Relationship. Brian Bell pursued his side project, The Special Goodness. Pat and Brian also ventured into acting in a film called "Factory Girl," with Pat playing John Cale of The Velvet Underground and Brian portraying Andy Warhol. "drummer Patrick Wilson and bass player Scott Schreiner went to work on a group called The Relationship."
**Online Clues and Disinformation for Deliverance Is At Hand** : Prior to the release of "Deliverance Is At Hand," several online clues emerged, including a leaked and deleted demo called "Pig" (metadata revealed its origin). A mysterious website, "Album 6," not affiliated with Weezer, also appeared, promoting a fake album ("Toot En Song") but ultimately directing fans to the official Weezer site. The "Weezer Zone" was flooded with fake news, rumors, leaks, and bogus lyrics, creating widespread disinformation. "But then some clues started appearing online. A demo called Pig was leaked and then deleted."
**Leaked Tracks Forcing Album Release** : Eight tracks from the real "Deliverance Is At Hand" album were leaked online, which compelled the record label to accelerate the album's release date. This demonstrates the impact of early online leaks on album rollout strategies. "Eight tracks from the album, the real album, or leaked, which actually forced the label's hand."
**Rivers Cuomo's Songwriting Inspiration** : Rivers Cuomo primarily draws inspiration from his own life and feelings, starting with personal experiences and then using "a little artistry" to make the songs more relatable. He confirmed that his songs for this particular album came directly from his life, though he couldn't speak for other band members' contributions. "My favorite source of inspiration is my own life and my own feelings."
**Band's Collective Songwriting on Red Album** : For the Red Album, Pat Wilson, Brian Bell, and Scott Shriner also contributed songs, marking a more collaborative songwriting effort beyond Rivers Cuomo's primary role. Scott Shriner described his musical contributions as "just based on feeling," with Rivers often writing the lyrics. "Pat Brian and Scott wrote songs in this album too, so I can't speak for them."
**"Pork and Beans" Video with Internet Stars** : The music video for "Pork and Beans" featured appearances by prominent internet personalities of the day, including the "dramatic chipmunk," the "leave Brittany alone guy," and Tay Zonday (singer of "Chocolate Rain"). This was a clever and timely way to capture the online zeitgeist. "It featured appearances by the biggest internet stars of the day, ranging from the dramatic chipmunk to the leave Brittany alone guy to Taze On Day, the singer of Chocolate Rain."
**Red Album Liner Notes** : The host was surprised to find his interview with the band incorporated into the liner notes of his CD copy of the Red Album. This was an unusual and unexpected inclusion. "Now imagine my surprise when I opened my copy, my CD copy, and saw that my interview with the band had been incorporated into the liner notes."
**Prolific Period After Red Album** : The Red Album marked the beginning of a highly prolific period for Weezer, with another album, "Ratitude," released little more than a year later. This suggested an increased creative output from the band. "The red album marked the beginning of a very prolific period for the band."
**External Collaborators and Multiple Producers for Ratitude** : For "Ratitude," Weezer brought in outside collaborators for the first time, including Jermaine Dupri, members of All-American Rejects, Lil Wayne, Dan Wilson from Semi-Sonic, and the guys from Sloan (though uncredited and parts recorded in 2003). The album also had four producers, including Dr. Luke and Jacknife Lee, plus Rivers Cuomo, totaling five. "For the first time outside collaborators were brought in, including Germain Dupree, a couple of guys from all American rejects, Lil Wayne, Dan Wilson from Semi-Sonic, and the guys from Sloan."
**Rivers Cuomo's Experimental Approach to Ratitude** : Rivers Cuomo embraced an experimental mindset for "Ratitude," telling the band, "this is just one more album out of many more that we're going to make." This indicated a willingness to take risks, suggesting that if an experiment didn't work out, they would simply try something different for the next album. "So in other words, if it works out great, if it doesn't, fine, we'll do something different for the next album."
**The Weezer Snuggie** : One of the band's most famous pieces of merchandise was the Weezer Snuggie, a blanket with sleeves similar to those advertised on late-night TV. The band appeared on David Letterman wearing them, and a commercial was produced, offering the Snuggie for $29.99 with a bonus copy of the "Ratitude" CD. "It was The Weezer Snuggie. You know those blankets with the sleeves that were advertised on late night TV?"
**YouTube Invasion Promotion for Hurley** : To promote the "Hurley" album on YouTube, Weezer launched an innovative campaign called "YouTube Invasion." They challenged 15 amateur online creators to produce something interesting in 30 minutes. One example involved "Hot for Words" trying to explain the origins of "Weezer" and "Hurley." "To promote the album on YouTube, the band gave 15 amateur online creators to come up with something interesting in 30 minutes."
**Fan Collaboration for "Turning Up the Radio"** : The project "Let's Write a Song" (SAWNG) for the track "Turning Up the Radio" involved fans submitting ideas for song titles, chord progressions, lyrics, and melodies through YouTube videos. This highly creative and interactive project utilized the ideas of 16 different fans, alongside Rivers Cuomo's contributions, for the final song on "Death to False Metal." "The project was called Let's Write a Song, and song is spelled S-A-W-N-G in this particular case."
**Four-Year Break Before Everything Will Be All Right in the End** : Weezer took a significant four-year break from releasing "proper records" between "Hurley" (September 2010) and "Everything Will Be All Right in the End" (October 2014). This was their longest hiatus between studio albums in a while. "Weezer took a four-year break from making proper records."
**Ric Ocasek's Production and Return to Early Sound** : Ric Ocasek of The Cars produced "Everything Will Be All Right in the End," making it his third and final album with Weezer before his death, having previously produced the Blue and Green Albums. Ocasek was specifically hired to guide Weezer back to their earlier sound. "This was the third and last album to be produced by Rico Cacic of the Cars before he died."
**Bus Accident on Ratitude Tour** : On December 6, 2009, while on tour for the "Ratitude" album, Weezer's bus hit a patch of ice on the New York State Thruway near Glen. The bus went over the median, hit posts, crossed the highway again, jumped a guard rail, and landed in a ditch. Rivers Cuomo, his wife Kyoko, daughter Mia, nanny, and assistant Sarah were on board. Rivers sustained three cracked ribs and internal bleeding, leading to the cancellation of the remaining tour dates. "On December 6th, 2009, Weezer was on tour for the Ratitude album."
**Personal Tragedies and Birth of Son Leo** : After the bus accident, Rivers Cuomo experienced further personal challenges, including the death of former bassist Mikey Welsh from an overdose in a Chicago hotel room on October 8, 2011. He also lost his grandfather, a former girlfriend, and his meditation teacher. In 2012, Rivers and Kyoko had a son, Leo, whose birth was difficult. These events prompted Rivers to seek a slower pace after a period of prolific releases. "On October 8th, 2011, former bass player Mikey Welsh was found dead of an overdose in a Chicago hotel room."
**Rivers Cuomo's Meditation Practice (Vipassanā)** : Rivers Cuomo practices Vipassanā meditation, a Sanskrit term meaning "to see things as they are," which has been around for at least 2,500 years. He practices at least two hours a day. Producer Rick Rubin first suggested it around 2003 when Weezer was working on the Red Album. Rivers initially feared it would rob him of angst but found it enhanced his creativity and made him "10% happier." "It's called Vispasana, that's Sanskrit for to see things as they are."
**Fan Focus Groups for Everything Will Be All Right in the End** : Weezer involved their fans directly in the creation of "Everything Will Be All Right in the End" by holding focus groups. Fans listened to demos and songs still in progress, and their feedback was incorporated into the final product. This level of fan involvement was unusual for a band. "The band once again turned to their fans, holding focus groups to listen to demos and songs that were still works in progress."
**Thematic Structure of Everything Will Be All Right in the End** : The album "Everything Will Be All Right in the End" is divided into three thematic sections: "The Panopticon Artist," which explores the relationship between Weezer and their fans; "Belodona," comprising classic girl group-style songs; and "Patriarchia," songs about father figures. The "Patriarchia" theme is particularly significant as Rivers reconciled with his birth father, who left the family in 1975 and became a Pentecostal minister. "The album can be divided into three themes."
**Ric Ocasek's Guitar Preference** : During the production of "Everything Will Be All Right in the End," Ric Ocasek insisted that the band use the same guitars they had on the "Blue Album." This was part of his effort to bring Weezer's sound back to their early days. "Rico Kasek insisted on using the same guitars as the band had on the blue record."
**Everything Will Be All Right in the End Cassette Release** : Weezer released "Everything Will Be All Right in the End" on cassette, making them one of the few bands to anticipate the eventual resurgence of cassettes as collectibles for hardcore fans. This demonstrates a forward-thinking approach to niche physical formats. "Weezer released a version on Kasek, making them one of the very few bands to predict that Kasek would eventually become kind of a thing again, at least when it comes to collectibles in the world of hardcore fans."
**24 YouTube Videos for Album Promotion** : The release of "Everything Will Be All Right in the End" was promoted with an extensive campaign involving 24 videos posted on YouTube. This demonstrated a heavy reliance on digital platforms for album marketing. "This release was promoted with 24 videos on YouTube."
**Wannabeezer Producer for White Album** : Jack Sinclair, the producer for the White Album, was a longtime Weezer fan and also fronted a tribute band called Wannabeezer. Brian Bell later noted that Sinclair understood the band's sound better than the band members themselves, bringing a unique perspective to the production. "Jack Sinclair, who a long time Weezer fan who also fronted a band called Wannabeezer. This gave him some really crazy insights as to how the band should sound."
**Rivers Cuomo's Stream of Consciousness Songwriting** : Rivers Cuomo generates a vast number of songs by embracing a "stream of consciousness" approach, letting ideas flow freely. While most of these ideas might be discarded, he believes some "gems" will emerge, contributing to his prolific output (e.g., sorting through 250 songs for the White Album, only one making the cut). "Well Rivers says he's all about stream of consciousness. He just lets the ideas flow."
**Tinder Account for Songwriting Inspiration** : For the White Album, Rivers Cuomo signed up for a Tinder account to meet people and then wrote about those experiences. Because he was living in California, these encounters often coalesced into themes related to the beach and ocean, including surfers, beach bums, and specific characters like "Harry Christmas." "Rivers signed up for a Tinder account and he met with people and then wrote about those experiences."
**Spotify Playlists for Chord Progressions** : Rivers Cuomo developed a unique songwriting process that began with creating Spotify playlists. These playlists featured songs with chord progressions he found interesting, providing a foundation for new melodies. "Rivers began by creating Spotify playlists featuring songs with chord progression that he found interesting."
**Vocalizing Guitar Solos** : As part of his songwriting process, Rivers Cuomo would first sound out guitar solos vocally. This technique allowed him to develop the melodic contours of solos before translating them to the instrument. "Guitar solos were sounded out vocally first."
**Journal and Spreadsheet for Lyrics** : Rivers Cuomo stored vocal thoughts in a journal, selecting the best bits to transfer to a spreadsheet. This spreadsheet was organized by the number of syllables in each line and by "strong weak emphasis," a concept likely learned from his English degree. He then ordered the data to fit melodies and tell stories. "Vocal thoughts were stored in a journal with the best bits making it onto a spreadsheet."
**Black Album Delay** : The Black Album was initially planned to follow the White Album, intended to be a darker counterpart. However, the songs Rivers Cuomo developed were not dark enough, leading to the Black Album being put on hold. What was released instead was Pacific Daydream. "So they had no choice but to put the Black Album on hold."
**Programmers for Pacific Daydream Spreadsheet** : For the Pacific Daydream album, Rivers Cuomo hired programmers to organize his songwriting data in an even more granular way. They tagged everything with specific attributes, and one tab in his spreadsheet contained about 5,500 "cool" lyrical fragments, which he used when stuck for inspiration. The programmers used Python for this task. "During this time, he hired some programmers to organize the data in an even more granular way, tagging everything with specific attributes."
**Prolific Output and "Too Much Weezer" Criticism** : Weezer has maintained a "furious pace" of releasing new material, leading some critics and fans to suggest they are "too prolific" and that the sheer volume of music might dilute its impact. Despite this, the band continues to move ahead with new projects. "In fact, some people say that they're maybe a little too prolific."
**Teal Album's Surprise Digital Release** : The Teal Album was released in January 2019 as a complete surprise digital release, without any prior warning or traditional promotional rollout. This unexpected drop was a marketing tactic in itself. "It just appeared as a digital release as a surprise, and it featured nothing but covers."
**Computer Program for Teal Album Covers Selection** : Rivers Cuomo wrote a computer program that analyzed Spotify data to select songs for the Teal Album. The program identified popular songs not tagged as Alt Rock or Classic Rock and narrowed the list to those released before 1994, generating 200 titles for the band to consider. "Rivers wrote a computer program that sucked in all kinds of Spotify data."
**Mary Kinchen's Africa Cover Request** : The band's cover of Toto's "Africa" originated from a persistent request, described as a "demand," from a 14-year-old fan in Cleveland named Mary Kinchen, who tweeted at Weezer. Her tweet, "it's about time you blessed the reins down in Africa," prompted the band to first cover Toto's "Rosanna" before eventually recording "Africa." "It grew out of a request, well, actually more of a demand. From a 14-year-old fan in Cleveland named Mary Kim, she tweeted at the band, it's about time you blessed the reins down in Africa."
**"Weird Al" Yankovic in Africa Cover** : Weezer's version of "Africa" featured an unexpected appearance by "Weird Al" Yankovic in the music video, a detail noted as not being in Toto's original 1980s version. "Although I don't remember weird Al being in the original back in the early 80s."
**Yelp Profile Promotion for Black Album** : To promote the Black Album, an official Yelp profile was created on October 9, 2018, and shared by the official Weezer Twitter account. This profile featured reviews of places with "Black" in their names, with each review containing mysterious lyrics from the upcoming album. "On October 9th, 2018, a Yelp profile appeared and was shared by the official Weezer Twitter account."
**Weezer Roomba Contest for OK Human** : For the release of "OK Human," Weezer partnered with the creators of the Roomba robot vacuum for a promotional contest. Five "Weezer Roombas" (spelled with a silent W) were offered as prizes, announced through a social media campaign claiming that "nine out of ten humans have dreamed of Weezer cleaning their house." "Weezer hooked up with the people who made the Roomba Robot Vacuum."
**Rivers Cuomo's Metal Band Past** : The album "Van Weezer" was noted as a return to Rivers Cuomo's musical roots, specifically referencing his past in a metal band before forming Weezer. This informed the heavier sound of the album. "Remember how Rivers was in a metal band before Weezer? This was a return to that time, sort of."
**Riverpedia / The Library / Drive Times App** : Rivers Cuomo created something called the "Riverpedia," now known as "The Library," which began as a homework assignment and was posted online on August 18, 2020. This evolved into an app called "Drive Times," designed to automate tour logistics like scheduling venue arrivals, road travel, and private plane flights. He learned to code in Python during the pandemic lockdown to create this. "He created something called the Riverpedia, which is now known as the Library."
**Rivers Cuomo's Coding Skills** : Rivers Cuomo dedicated part of the pandemic lockdown to learning to code himself, ultimately creating the "Drive Times" app for tour logistics. He achieved a 95 in his coding class and is described as a "pretty fair Python programmer." "In case you're wondering, Rivers got a 95 in his coding class."
**Extensive Demos and Unreleased Material** : Rivers Cuomo has an immense catalog of demos, possibly thousands, some dating back as far as 1979, which he has posted online or released on compilations. This extensive archive provides numerous "rabbit holes" for fans to explore. **Three Types of Bands** : There are three types of bands: the extreme (doing anything for attention), the traditional (earnest and honest), and the quirky, eccentric, and weird.
