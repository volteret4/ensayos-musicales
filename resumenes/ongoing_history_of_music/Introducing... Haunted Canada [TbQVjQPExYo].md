# artist - Joanne Jordan
## instruments
Harp
## curiosities
**Ghost Encounter During Performance** : Toronto musician and harpist Joanne Jordan experienced a terrifying encounter while performing at an event in the former Bank of Montreal building, several years before it transitioned into the Hockey Hall of Fame in 1993. While playing her harp and admiring the Great Hall's dome and magnificent stained glass, she spotted a woman (later identified as the ghost of Dorothy May Elliott) standing by the second-floor railing, staring down at her. Jordan was shocked and recounted this vivid experience to the Toronto Star, learning about the building's permanent spectral resident only after her sighting. "I remember it so vividly because it's one of those things you'll never forget."
**Avoidance of Second Floor** : Following her unsettling ghost encounter during a performance at the historic building (formerly the Bank of Montreal), harpist Joanne Jordan developed a strong reluctance to visit the second floor alone. Whenever she had subsequent engagements to play her harp at the venue, she made sure she was never by herself when needing to go upstairs, citing the area as "too spooky." "she made sure to never go up to the second floor by herself because she said that it's just too spooky up there."
