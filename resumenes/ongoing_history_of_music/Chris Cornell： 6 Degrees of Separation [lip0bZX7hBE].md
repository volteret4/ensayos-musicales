## albums
**Euphoria Morning (1999) – Debut Solo Album** : After Soundgarden broke up in 1997, Chris Cornell had a backlog of material and began working on his solo debut in 1998. He relied on Elaine Johannes and Natasha Schneider of the band Eleven to help produce the record. Despite receiving good reviews, the album was a sales disappointment. "Chris reliant on both of them to help produce his debut solo record Euphoria Morning, which came out in 1999."
**Scream (2008) – Collaboration with Timberland** : This album was recorded with hip-hop performer and producer Timberland. The collaboration was a significant departure from Cornell's grunge roots and was met with commercial disappointment. It connected Chris Cornell professionally to a wide range of R&B and hip-hop artists. "The result, which was called scream, how do we put this? Well, it was a commercial disappointment and more."
**Higher Truth (2015) – Fourth Solo Album** : Released in September 2015, this album received positive reviews. It was produced by Brendan O'Brien, a renowned producer who has also worked with artists like Pearl Jam, Billy Talent, Bruce Springsteen, and AC/DC. "when he released his fourth solo record in September 2015, got some great reviews."

**Casino Royale Title Theme (2006) – James Bond Theme Song** : Chris Cornell recorded the title theme for the James Bond movie Casino Royale in 2006. This project connected him to a long chain of previous Bond theme performers and artists. "In 2006, he got a job seeing the title theme for the James Bond movie Casino Royale."

## curiosities
**Death (May 18, 2017) – Passing in Detroit** : Chris Cornell passed away on May 18, 2017, in Detroit at the age of 52. His death marked the end of one of the pivotal figures whose bands made Grunge and Alt Rock possible, leaving behind a tremendous musical legacy. "On May 18th, 2017, Chris Cornell, singer for Soundgarden and audio slave, passed away in Detroit."
**Roommate of Andrew Wood – Impact on Temple of the Dog** : Chris Cornell shared an apartment with Andrew Wood, the singer of Mother Love Bone. Wood's unexpected death in March 1990 profoundly affected Cornell, leading him to write songs in tribute that eventually formed the basis of the Temple of the Dog project. "Andrew died the day Chris got back from a long tour with Soundgarden. A few days after the funeral, Chris and Soundgarden headed out on a European tour, and while on the road, Chris started running some songs in tribute to his old friend."
**Post-Soundgarden Solo Career Start – Free Agent** : Following Soundgarden's official breakup on April 9, 1997, Chris Cornell became a free agent. He had a backlog of material and by 1998, he began working on his debut solo album, Euphoria Morning. "Soundgarden broke up on April 9th, 1997 with an official announcement, making Chris a free agent."
**Audition for Rage Against the Machine – Rick Rubin's Suggestion** : After his solo album's disappointing sales, and with Rage Against the Machine's singer Zack de la Rocha having quit, producer Rick Rubin suggested Chris Cornell as a potential new vocalist for the remaining members. The chemistry between Cornell and the instrumentalists was instantaneous, leading them to write 21 songs in just 19 days. "Then producer Rick Rubin suggested that they ring up Chris Cornell. The chemistry was so instant that they wrote 21 songs in just 19 days."
**Audioslave Manager Conflict – Near Breakup** : The new group, which would become Audioslave, faced immediate challenges when their respective managers began brawling, causing the band to break up before playing their first gig. This administrative hurdle required both managers to be fired and a new management group to be brought in to resolve the issues. "Well, the new group broke up before they played their first gig because the two managers started brawling, and it looked like Rage Against the Machine version 2.0 was dead before it got a chance to do anything."
**Lawsuit Against Ex-Wife Susan Silver (2004) – Royalties and Possessions Dispute** : After divorcing Susan Silver in 2004, Chris Cornell sued her for a million dollars. He claimed she diverted Soundgarden royalties to the other band members and withheld valuable items, including a guitar, Grammy Awards, demo tapes, personal recordings, diaries, and journals. This legal dispute was cited as a reason for the prolonged delay in the Soundgarden reunion, although everything was eventually settled. "Chris sued Susan for a million dollars, claiming that she diverted Soundgarden royalties away from him and to the other three band members, words like fraud, oppression, and malice reused in the documents."

## curiosities
**Formation (1984) – Early Lineup Changes** : Soundgarden officially started in 1984 with Chris Cornell, Hiro Yamamoto, and Kim Thayil, and an initial drummer Scott Sunquist. Sunquist was later replaced by Matt Cameron due to family commitments involving a wife and child. "In 1984, they started calling themselves Soundgarden with a German name Scott Sunquist. But he had a wife and child and couldn't commit to the band full time, so he was replaced by Matt Cameron."
**Hiro Yamamoto's Departure (1989) – Pursuit of Education** : In 1989, bassist Hiro Yamamoto left Soundgarden to finish his master's degree in physical chemistry. After leaving the band, he continued his musical pursuits by playing in a band called Truly, which included members of Screaming Trees. "In 1989, there was a lineup change when bass player Hero Yamamoto quit to finish his master's degree in physical chemistry."
**Jason Everman's Brief Stint – Nirvana Connection** : Jason Everman replaced Hiro Yamamoto on bass but did not remain with Soundgarden for long. He was notable for his prior involvement with Nirvana, where he briefly served as second guitarist, toured behind their *Bleach* album, and even paid the $606.17 recording fee for that album, receiving fake credit for his financial generosity. "Jason didn't last very long in Soundgarden either and was eventually replaced by Ben Shepard."
**Ben Shepherd's Enduring Role – Roadie to Bassist** : Ben Shepherd eventually replaced Jason Everman as Soundgarden's bassist and has held the position since. Before joining Soundgarden, Shepherd was active in the Seattle scene, playing in bands like March of Crimes (briefly with Stone Gossard) and Tick Dolly Roe (with Chad Channing), and even freelanced as Nirvana's roadie before being briefly considered for their second guitarist role. "But that was still okay because he auditioned for Soundgarden again and got the job, and he still has that job today."
**Breakup (April 9, 1997) – Official Announcement** : Soundgarden officially broke up on April 9, 1997. This event made Chris Cornell a free agent, allowing him to pursue a solo career and other projects, including Audioslave. "Soundgarden broke up on April 9th, 1997 with an official announcement, making Chris a free agent."
**Susan Silver as Manager – Ex-Wife and Business Partner** : Susan Silver, Chris Cornell's ex-wife, served as the manager for Soundgarden. Her role as manager became a point of contention after their divorce in 2004 when Cornell sued her over alleged diverted royalties and disputed ownership of band-related items. "She is also the ex-manager of Soundgarden and Allison Chains."
**Reunion Delay – Linked to Lawsuit** : The legal dispute between Chris Cornell and his ex-wife and former manager, Susan Silver, regarding alleged diverted royalties and withheld band property, contributed to the delay in Soundgarden's reunion. This conflict involved claims of fraud, oppression, and malice. "So if you've ever wondered why it took so long for the Soundgarden reunion to come together, there's part of your answer."

## albums
**Apple (1990) – Debut Album Halted by Tragedy** : Mother Love Bone's debut album, *Apple*, was scheduled for release on March 20, 1990. However, just four days prior, singer Andrew Wood tragically overdosed on heroin and subsequently died, leading to the album's release being delayed and ultimately impacting the band's future. "That record was called Apple, and it was set for release on March 20, 1990. But just four days before that could happen, this was Friday March the 16th. Andrew Wood's girlfriend found him in his apartment completely comatose."

## curiosities
**Evolution from Malfunkshun – Group's Origin** : Mother Love Bone developed out of an earlier group named Malfunkshun, which notably featured Greg Gilmore, a musician who had also spent time in Skinyard with Jack Endino and Daniel House. "They had evolved out of malfunction, a group featuring a dude named Greg Gilmore, who, for a time, had been in Skinyard with Jack and Dino and Daniel House."
**Major Label Deal (November 1988) – Seattle Pioneers** : In November 1988, Mother Love Bone secured a major label record deal, becoming one of the first Seattle bands to achieve this milestone. Their debut EP was well received, setting the stage for their anticipated album *Apple*. "In November 1988, Mother Love Bone became one of the first of what would be a long line of Seattle bands to nail a major label record deal."
**Andrew Wood's Death (March 19, 1990) – Heroin Overdose** : Andrew Wood, the popular singer of Mother Love Bone, died on Monday, March 19, 1990, the day before their album *Apple* was due for release. He was found comatose on March 16 after a heroin overdose, and after brain activity ceased, his family chose to take him off life support. "Andrew was a really popular guy, and he had lots of friends in Seattle. His death hit Chris Cornell, his old roommate particularly hard."
**Transformation into Pearl Jam – Post-Tragedy Rebirth** : Following the tragic death of Andrew Wood, the surviving members of Mother Love Bone, Jeff Ament and Stone Gossard, collaborated with Chris Cornell and Matt Cameron to form Temple of the Dog. This collaboration eventually led Ament and Gossard to form a new band with Eddie Vedder, initially called Mookie Blaylock, which soon became Pearl Jam. "When Mother Lovebone collapsed and turned into Pearl Jam, Kelly became their full-time manager, leaving Susan to deal with Allison Chains."

## albums
**Temple of the Dog (1991) – Self-Titled Tribute Album** : This album was recorded in just 15 days and released on April 16, 1991, as a tribute to Andrew Wood, the deceased singer of Mother Love Bone. It featured Chris Cornell, Matt Cameron, Jeff Ament, Stone Gossard, Mike McCready, and an unproven Eddie Vedder. Although it initially received great reviews but little attention, it later went multi-platinum worldwide after Soundgarden and Pearl Jam achieved massive success. "The Temple of the Dog album was recorded in just 15 days and released on April 16, 1991."

## songs
**Hunger Strike (1990) – Unprompted Eddie Vedder Duet** : During the recording of this song, while Chris Cornell was laying down vocals, Eddie Vedder spontaneously picked up a microphone and began singing the low harmonies without being asked. This impromptu contribution solidified the legendary duet between the two future rock giants, recorded at a time when neither Soundgarden nor the nascent Pearl Jam were widely known. "But in the midst of Chris recording the vocals for the song that we're about to hear, Eddie just picked up a mic and started singing the low parts. Nobody really asked him to do it, he just kind of did it."

## curiosities
**Tribute to Andrew Wood – Origin of the Project** : Temple of the Dog was formed as a quick tribute to Andrew Wood, Chris Cornell's former roommate and the singer of Mother Love Bone, who tragically died in March 1990. Cornell wrote several songs in his friend's honor, which he then shared with the surviving members of Mother Love Bone, leading to the formation of this supergroup. "When he got back to Seattle, he showed the surviving members of Mother Love Bone, the songs. When other materials started flowing, they decided to record a quick tribute album under the name Temple of the Dog."
**Initial Lack of Attention, Later Multi-Platinum Success – Post-Hype Phenomenon** : Despite receiving great reviews upon its release in April 1991, the Temple of the Dog album was largely ignored by the public. However, once Mookie Blaylock changed its name to Pearl Jam and both Soundgarden and Pearl Jam achieved massive global success, interest in the album exploded, leading it to sell multi-platinum worldwide. "It got great reviews, but nobody paid much attention until Mookie Blaylock changed their name to Pearl Jam and, well, you know, that story."

## albums
**Audioslave (2002) – Debut Album Release and Label Deal** : Audioslave's self-titled debut album was released in 2002. A complex label deal was worked out: the first album was released jointly by Epic (Rage Against the Machine's label) and Interscope (Chris Cornell's label). "The first album would be released by both Epic, Rage Against the Machine's label, and Interscope, Chris's label."

## songs
**Cochise (2002) – First Single** : "Cochise" was the first single released by Audioslave in 2002, appearing on their self-titled debut album. It marked the public introduction of the supergroup formed by Chris Cornell and the instrumental members of Rage Against the Machine. "AudioSlave from 2002, and the first single from their first of three albums."

## curiosities
**Formation from Rage Against the Machine Remnants – Producer's Suggestion** : Audioslave formed when the remaining members of Rage Against the Machine (Tom Morello, Tim Commerford, Brad Wilk) sought a new singer after Zack de la Rocha's departure. Producer Rick Rubin suggested Chris Cornell, leading to an instant creative connection and the rapid composition of 21 songs. "Rage Against the Machine had imploded... All they needed to do was find a new singer... Then producer Rick Rubin suggested that they ring up Chris Cornell."
**"Civilian" Demos – Early, Unofficial Name** : Before Audioslave officially formed, 13 demos featuring Chris Cornell with the Rage Against the Machine instrumentalists surfaced online under the name "Civilian." This name was considered for the band but was ultimately found to be already taken. "It was then that 13 demos showed up online under the name civilian."
**Name Acquisition from Liverpool Band (October 2002) – $30,000 Payment** : The American supergroup faced a challenge when they chose the name "Audioslave," only to discover a Liverpool band already using it. After an initial offer of $5,000 was rejected, the American band's managers eventually paid the Liverpool group $30,000 in late October 2002 for the rights to the name, just weeks before their debut album's release. "In the end, they were paid $30,000 to go away. Liverpool AudioSlave changed their name to the most terrifying thing."

# artist - Susan Silver

**First Management Client (1981) – The U-Men** : Susan Silver began her career in artist management by managing The U-Men, a Seattle group formed in 1981 that predated the widespread Grunge movement by a couple of years. She also managed Screaming Trees before taking on Soundgarden. "Silver got her start managing the U-Men, a Seattle group that predated the whole Grunge thing by a couple of years in 1981."

Their relationship lasted through significant periods in the rise of both Soundgarden and Alice in Chains, as well as Chris's early solo career and personal struggles. "She and Chris started dating, which probably had its awkward moments with the rest of the band, and then they got married in September of 1990."
**Co-Manager of Alice in Chains – Partnership with Kelly Curtis** : Susan Silver co-managed Alice in Chains with Kelly Curtis through their shared management company. When Mother Love Bone collapsed and transformed into Pearl Jam, Kelly Curtis shifted his focus to become Pearl Jam's full-time manager, leaving Susan to manage Alice in Chains independently. "When Mother Lovebone started to get hot, Kelly wanted to spend more time with them. Meanwhile, Ken wanted out, so Susan and Kelly became co-managers of Allison Chains through their own management company."
**Divorce from Chris Cornell (2004) – Followed by Lawsuit** : Susan Silver and Chris Cornell divorced in 2004, after a relationship that spanned the rise of Soundgarden and Alice in Chains, Soundgarden's breakup, Cornell's initial solo career, and his struggles with addiction, as well as the death of Alice in Chains singer Lane Staley. Their divorce was followed by a contentious lawsuit initiated by Cornell. "She and Chris divorced in 2004."
**Lawsuit by Chris Cornell – Alleged Royalties Diversion and Withholding of Items** : Following their 2004 divorce, Chris Cornell sued Susan Silver for a million dollars, accusing her of diverting Soundgarden royalties to the other band members and withholding valuable items such as a guitar, Grammy Awards, demo tapes, personal recordings, diaries, and journals. The suit used strong terms like "fraud, oppression, and malice" in its documentation, and it was eventually settled. "Chris sued Susan for a million dollars, claiming that she diverted Soundgarden royalties away from him and to the other three band members, words like fraud, oppression, and malice reused in the documents."
**Atmosphere Artist Management (2005-2011) – Post-Divorce Venture** : After her divorce from Chris Cornell, Susan Silver established a new company called Atmosphere Artist Management in 2005. This venture, however, was later shut down in 2011 for unspecified reasons. "Susan created a new company called Atmosphere Artist Management in 2005, but it was shut down for some reason in 2011."

**Eddie Vedder's Pearl Jam Audition Tape – Networking via Jack Irons** : Eddie Vedder secured his audition for Mookie Blaylock (which became Pearl Jam) through an intricate networking chain. Chris Cornell passed a cassette tape to Jack Irons, who in turn gave it to Eddie Vedder after they played basketball. Vedder then added his lyrics and vocals to the tape and mailed it back to Stone Gossard, Jeff Ament, and Mike McCready in Seattle, ultimately landing him the gig. "Jack received a cassette tape, which he was asked to pass along to any promising young singer. It just so happened that Jack played basketball with this surfer kid from San Diego named Eddie Vetter.
