# genre - Rock and Roll

## curiosities
**Origin of the Term "Rock and Roll" – Moondog Lawsuit Forces Alan Freed to Coin the Phrase (1956)** : DJ Alan Freed popularized R&B music to white audiences using the name "Moondog" (borrowed without permission from blind musician Lewis Harden, who had used it since 1947) for his radio show and concerts. In 1956, Harden sued Freed for trademark infringement and won. Forced to abandon the name, Freed adopted "Rock and Roll" — drawn from African-American slang for sex, as heard in the 1951 song "60 Minute Man" — using it as a "secret code" for teenagers. "Freed adopted 'Rock and Roll' from African-American slang for sex, using it as a 'secret code' for teenagers."

**Birth of Rock and Roll in the 1950s – Convergence of Genre Mixing, Local Radio DJs, and the Teenager as Demographic** : The rise of rock and roll in the 1950s resulted from a convergence of forces: the blending of musical genres, the proliferation of local radio DJs filling voids left by national networks, and the emergence of the "teenager" as a distinct demographic with its own cultural and musical demands. "The rise of rock and roll was a confluence of genre mixing, local DJs, and the emergence of the 'teenager' as a new demographic."

**Transistor Radio (1948) – "Social Media" of Its Era, Catalyst for Rock and Roll** : The invention of the transistor in 1948 enabled the portable radio, which allowed teenagers to listen to rock and roll privately and independently of their parents. This device functioned as the social media of its era and was a direct catalyst for the cultural and musical revolutions of the 1950s and 1960s. "The transistor radio allowed teenagers to listen to rock and roll privately, functioning as the 'social media' of its era."


# genre - Gothic Rock

## curiosities
**Gothic Rock Lineage – From Tambora's Eruption to Mary Shelley to Bauhaus** : The genealogy of gothic rock traces back to the 1815 eruption of the Tambora volcano in Indonesia — the largest in recorded history — which caused the "Year Without a Summer" in 1816. The resulting gloom and cold inspired Mary Shelley to conceive *Frankenstein*, a foundational text of Gothic literature. That Gothic tradition, running through horror cinema and theatrical glamour of the 1960s, was eventually absorbed into rock music by bands like Bauhaus, Joy Division, and The Cure in the late 1970s. "The eruption of the Tambora volcano in 1815 caused the 'Year Without Summer' in 1816, inspiring Mary Shelley to conceive 'Frankenstein,' a cornerstone of Gothic literature."


# genre - Rhythm and Blues

## curiosities
**R&B's Origins in the Great Migration – Cotton Gin, Slavery, and Urban Demographics** : The origins of rhythm and blues trace back to the invention of the cotton gin in 1794, which entrenched slavery, and the subsequent dispossession of African-American land, which drove the Great Migration from the rural South to northern cities from the late nineteenth century through the early twentieth. This demographic shift was the crucible in which jazz and blues evolved into rhythm and blues. "The cotton gin (1794) entrenched slavery, dispossession drove the Great Migration, and the resulting demographic shift was the crucible in which jazz and blues evolved into R&B."

**Supernova Explosion ~41,000 Years Ago – Possible Cosmic Origin of Music** : A theory proposes that a supernova explosion approximately 41,000 years ago may have caused genetic mutations in *Homo sapiens*, resulting in enlarged brains capable of complex thought, language, visual art (as in cave paintings), and ultimately music. The oldest known musical instruments date to approximately 30,000 years ago, consistent with this timeline. "A supernova explosion about 41,000 years ago may have caused mutations in Homo sapiens leading to larger brains and, ultimately, music."
