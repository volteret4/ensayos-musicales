**Soundtrack vs. Score Definition** : A soundtrack is a collection of songs from or inspired by a movie, not necessarily written for it, and often pre-recorded. A score is typically instrumental music specifically composed for a movie to set the mood for scenes or the film as a whole. "A soundtrack is the collection of all songs that were in a movie or were, quote, inspired by the movie. They were not necessarily written for that movie and often were already recorded."

"The problem is that the movie FM was not a hit."

**Urban Cowboy Movie Origin (1978)** : The movie "Urban Cowboy" was based on a non-fictional essay written by Aaron Latham for Esquire magazine in 1978. The essay explored the lives of blue-collar workers in Houston who found recreation in a "playing cowboy" lifestyle at Gilley's nightclub. "Urban Cowboy was based on an essay written by Aaron Latham for Esquire magazine."
