"This is where the Jackson's were living with Motown president Barry Gordy and the woman he described as the love of his life Diana Ross."

**Early Career and Public Scrutiny** : Barry Manilow was a frequent target of ridicule in the 1970s and 80s. Before achieving stardom, he worked accompanying Bette Midler in New York bathhouses. Dick Clark, while initially underestimating Manilow's star potential based on his looks and voice, recognized his talent for arranging and producing emotionally resonant songs. "The most crushing blow came when I heard their theme. Their theme is played on a moog synthesizer now."

"If you didn't go on American bandstand, you just hadn't made it yet."
**Commercial Peak and 1970s Comeback** : Aretha Franklin reached her commercial peak in the 1960s through her collaboration with Jerry Wexler, which helped her cultivate her deep southern soulful sound. While she remained relevant, the "Sparkle" soundtrack in 1976 was seen as a significant comeback in the 1970s, preceding a further lean into pop music in the 1980s that constituted another major comeback. "maybe she had maybe reached her commercial peak in the 1960s when she teamed up with Jerry Wexler and kind of found that really deep southern soulful sound."

**Origins and Early Hosting (1952-1956)** : American Bandstand began in 1952 with Philadelphia DJ Bob Horn as host, despite often being associated with Dick Clark's 1957 takeover.
