**Kent State Shootings (May 4, 1970)** : On May 4, 1970, 13 students were shot by the Ohio National Guard on the campus of Kent State University, resulting in deaths and woundings. This tragedy followed President Richard Nixon's announcement on April 30, 1970, that ground troops had been moved into technically neutral Cambodia as part of the Vietnam War, sparking protests on campuses nationwide, including Kent State. "In today's episode, we will examine the music that mattered to some of the students of Kent State University in some of the music that resulted from the tragic shooting of 13 students on their campus by the Ohio National Guard on May 4, 1970."
**Richard Nixon's Cambodia Invasion (April 30, 1970)** : On April 30, 1970, President Richard Nixon announced the expansion of the American War with Vietnam by moving ground troops into Cambodia, which was technically neutral but served as part of the Ho Chi Minh Trail used by the Viet Cong. This announcement, following Nixon's 1968 promise of a "secret plan to end the war" and combined with the unpopular draft, directly sparked protests on campuses across the country, including Kent State. "He announced that ground troops had been moved into Cambodia just over the western border of Vietnam on April 30th, 1970."
**Ho Chi Minh Trail's Role in Vietnam War** : The Ho Chi Minh Trail, a crucial supply route for the Viet Cong, cut through northern Cambodia, which was technically neutral in the Vietnam War. The US had been secretly bombing it for a year prior to President Nixon's public announcement of ground troop movement into Cambodia in April 1970. "Cambodia was technically neutral in the war, but it was also part of the Ho Chi Minh Trail, which cut through northern Cambodia and the Vietnam Kong were using it to move weapons and other supplies."
**Blame on Kent State Students (Decades)** : For decades after the May 4, 1970, shootings, students of Kent State University were unfairly blamed for the deaths and woundings of 13 of their peers. This blame came from their governor, their university, and even their own parents. "For years, scratch that for decades, the students of Kent State University were blamed for the deaths and the wounding of 13 of their own."
**Ohio National Guard Occupation (May 1-4, 1970)** : The protests at Kent State from May 1st to May 4th, 1970, eventually became as much about the campus being occupied by the Ohio National Guard as they were about the Cambodia bombings. Ohio Governor James Rhodes declared a state of emergency in Kent and was responsible for deploying 1200 National Guard members around the university. "The protests of the weekend of May 1st through Monday, May 4th eventually became as much about the fact that their campus was occupied by the Ohio National Guard as it was about the bombings."
**Governor James Rhodes' Denunciation of Students (1970)** : Ohio Governor James Rhodes publicly condemned the Kent State protesters, labeling them "the worst kind of Americans" and likening them to the Nazi paramilitary force, the brown shirts. He was responsible for declaring the state of emergency and deploying 1200 National Guard members to the university. "Ohio Governor James Rhodes called them the worst kind of Americans and likened them to the brown shirts, the Nazi paramilitary force."
