## albums

## songs

The song also crossed over to the Billboard Hot 100, reaching number 15. "It won the CMA Song of the Year, the CMA single of the year, a songwriter Kenny O'Dell won a Grammy for a country song of the year, Rich won a Grammy for country vocal performance, all by the way Behind Close Doors also crossed over to the Billboard Hot 100 and made it to number 15."
**The Most Beautiful Girl (1973)** : Released in late summer of 1973 as a follow-up single, this song went to number one on both the Billboard country chart and the Billboard Hot 100. "It also went to number one on the Billboard country chart but you know what? It also went to number one on the Billboard Hot 100."

## curiosities
**1974 CMA Entertainer of the Year Win** : Charlie Rich won the CMA Entertainer of the Year award in 1974, reaping rewards from his successful 1973 work. "As the 1974 winner, he was reaping the rewards of his work that he had done in 1973."
**1975 CMA Awards Incident - Card Burning** : As the reigning CMA entertainer of the year, Charlie Rich staggered onto the stage at Nashville's Grand Ole Opry in 1975, fueled by gin and tonics, and announced John Denver as the winner. He then pulled out a lighter and set the card with Denver's name on it on fire, an act widely interpreted as a statement that John Denver was not welcome in country music. Rich later claimed it was an accident caused by pain killers. "Rich reaches into his pocket, takes out a lighter and sets the card with Denver's name on it on fire."
**Drinking Habits** : It was well-known that Charlie Rich frequently drank heavily, which was cited as a factor in his behavior at the 1975 CMA Awards. "It was no secret then or now that Charlie Rich drank and he drank a lot."

# genre - Country-politin

## curiosities
**Emergence and Characteristics** : Country-politin, also known as the Nashville sound, emerged in the 1960s as a style that removed traditional country elements like banjo and fiddle, instead incorporating choirs, orchestras, amplified instruments, and sophisticated arrangements with adult lyrical themes. It was geared towards middle-class adults who found rock and roll too aggressive but disliked "twangy old school hillbilly country-western," seeking relatable stories about love and work. "What do you get when you take the banjo and the fiddle out of country music and you add in a choir and or an orchestra? You get the Nashville sound or country-polletin."
**Pop Chart Success (Early 1980s)** : Country-politin was poised to replace disco on the pop charts in the early 1980s, exemplified by the "Urban Cowboy" era. "Country-polletin is going to take the place of disco on the pop charts in the early 1980s."

# curiosity

**Country Music Association (CMA) Founding Purpose** : The CMA was founded in 1958 because many radio stations were abandoning country music in favor of rock and roll, largely due to Elvis Presley. Its purpose was not to protect the "purity" of country music, but rather to find ways to get more people to listen to and buy country music. "It exists because in 1958, too many radio stations were abandoning country for rock and roll largely due to the hip-shaking kid named Elvis out of Memphis. It was created to figure out how to get more people to listen to and of course buy more country music."
**Academy of Country Entertainers Formation** : Formed after a gathering hosted by George Jones and Tammy Wynette following the 1975 CMA Awards, this organization aimed to "save real country music" by addressing the issue of non-country singers winning country music awards. However, it did not last long due to the eventual dissolution of Jones and Wynette's marriage. "Still, shots were fired. The so-called establishment said that something needed to be done to save, quote, real country music."
**Radio's Influence on Record Sales (1970s)** : In the 1970s, before the era of streaming, radio play was paramount to record sales; if a record wasn't heard on the radio, it wouldn't sell. "No radio play? No record sold. In the 1970s, you only sold records if you were heard on the radio."
**Country Radio's Programming Strategy (1968-1974)** : Between 1968 and 1974, the number of full-time country music stations doubled, using the same template as rock stations. Many country music DJs of the era lacked knowledge about country music but were proficient in programming radio stations effectively. "Between 1968 and 1974, the number of full-time country music stations had doubled, and they used the same template as rock stations. Nobody cared if the DJ's knew a damn thing about country music."
**The Americanization of Dixie, the Southernization of America (1974)** : This thesis, published by John Edgerton in 1974 and maintained until his death in 2013, argued that regional distinctions between the American South and other regions were becoming lost. While the South was becoming more like the rest of the nation, the rest of the nation was also adopting aspects of Southern culture, evident in areas like politics (e.g., a President from Georgia in 1976), religion, and movies. "John Edgerton published the Americanization of Dixie, the Southernization of America. This thesis was that the regional distinctions between the South and everywhere else in America was becoming lost."
**The Myth of the American Cowboy** : The mythical American West and its independent cowboy figure represent an enduring national myth, distinct from the historical reality of cowboys primarily engaged in herding cattle and the closure of the open prairie by barbed wire fences before the 20th century. "The mythical American West is one of this country's most enduring of myths."
**Blending of Popular Music Formats (1970s)** : The 1970s are considered the beginning of a significant blending of popular music formats, resulting in a highly diverse Billboard pop chart that continued until the early 1980s, when a period of segregation in music genres began. "The 1970s were arguably the beginning of a blending of popular music formats that gets to the point where we get a very diverse billboard pop chart, and it kind of stays like that until the early 1980s when I'm going to say unfortunately we get very segregated for about four or five years in the early 80s."

**Homogenization of America in the 1970s** : America in the 1970s experienced a trend towards homogenization, where regional differences began to blur, as observed by John Edgerton in his book. "In America in the 70s was becoming homogenized."
