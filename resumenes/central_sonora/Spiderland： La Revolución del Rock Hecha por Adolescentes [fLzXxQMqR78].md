## albums
**Fritz (1987) - First Album** : This album was recorded in 1987 with engineer Astifal Vinny. Slint initially wrote to Touch and Go Records hoping for a release, but receiving no response, they self-released the album. The resulting sound was not entirely to the liking of bassist Ethan Buckler, who subsequently left the band. "Fritz se grabó en 1987 con Alvinie y pese a que escribieron a la Tao Chango Records, para que lanzaran el disco, no recibieron respuesta de ellos, por lo que se le autoaditaron."
**Spiderland (1990) - Definitive Work** : Recorded in August 1990 over a weekend in Chicago, this album was produced by Brian Paulson, a deliberate choice to move away from the abrasive sound associated with Astifal Vinny. Paulson was known for recording bands live, achieving a sound faithful to rehearsals with minimal post-production. All the music was recorded live and was well-rehearsed, but the vocals were left for the very end of the recording process and completed in just two takes. Brian McMahan's soft vocal style is attributed to his lack of confidence as a singer, and Britt Walford wrote the lyrics. During mixing, Slint and Paulson attempted to add different effects but ultimately discarded them, resulting in a very clear and minimalist production sound. Despite the clean sound, the band later commented they were not entirely happy with the recordings, feeling they sounded more natural and less tense in their rehearsals. The album, with its suggestive and mysterious black-and-white cover, received no promotion, tours, interviews, videoclips, or photo sessions due to the band's separation almost immediately after recording, leading to very discrete sales. Over the years, its impact spread by word-of-mouth, solidifying its place as a fundamental pillar of 90s music and the main catalyst for post-rock and math rock. "Este disco, con una sugerente y misteriosa portada en blanco y negro, no tendrá ningún tipo de promoción."

## curiosities
**Formation and Early Hardcore Experience** : Slint formed in Louisville, Kentucky, in 1986. At the time of their formation, the members were around 16 years old. Despite their youth, they were not novices, having already spent several years participating in the American hardcore punk scene. "Es lind se formaron en Luisville, Kentucky en 1986. La banda estaba formada por el guitarrista de la vocalista Brian Magmahan, el batería Brit-Wallford, el guitarrista David Pajoy y el bajista Ethan Buckler. En el momento de formar la banda, los miembros rondaban los 16 años, pero no eran unos novatos, llevaban ya varias años formando parte de la escena hardcore punk americana."
**Childhood Bands and Influences** : Britt Walford and Brian McMahan formed their first hardcore punk band, Lanky-Tan-Flas-It, when they were only 11 years old, so small that their parents had to carry their equipment. By age 14, they were part of Squirrel Bait, described as a seminal hardcore band that influenced the sound of "mocor" (grunge). These early musicians, with their nerdy appearance, literally grew up alongside and rubbed shoulders with bands like Hüsker Dü, Black Flag, and Big Black. "Brit-Wallford y Brian Magmahan formaron su primera banda de hardcore punk, Lanky-Tan-Flas-It, cuando tenían tal sólo 11 años. Era el tan pequeño que sus padres tenían que cargar con el equipo. Con 14 formaron parte de Squire-El-Bate, la seminal de hardcore y el sonido de mocor."
**Band's Humorous Side** : Despite the aura of mystery and darkness that formed around Slint's public image, the band members reportedly spent their days playing jokes on each other and did not hesitate to tease prominent musicians like David Yow of The Jesus Lizard or engineer Astifal Vinny. An anecdote tells of them approaching Vinny's door to propose recording their album, playfully armed with "Carlson Cillos" and pointing drinks directly at his face. "Pese a la hora de misterio y oscuridad que se formó en torno a su figura, se pasaban el día que en dobromas entre ellos y no se cortaban en vacilar a músicos de la talla de David Joe, de Gisos Liza o Astifal-Vini."
**Rigorous Rehearsal Practices** : After the release of their first album and with band members attending university, they would periodically return to Louisville to practice and write new material. During these sessions, held in the Walford family basement, they could spend up to nine hours rehearsing a single riff. Over three years, they meticulously polished their sound, incorporating complex time signature changes and dark textures, progressively distancing themselves from hardcore. "En esas sesiones, se podrían pasar nueve horas ensayando al mismo riff, ensayaban en el sótano de los Walford."
**Post-Spiderland Breakup** : Slint separated almost immediately after the recording of *Spiderland*. The morning after completing the recording, Brian McMahan was admitted to a hospital where he was diagnosed with depression. He subsequently left the band, a life-changing decision he attributed to the disorientation of a mind overwhelmed by anxiety. "Sin embargo, prácticamente el día después de la grabación, la banda se separará."
**Iconic Album Artwork** : Joe Aldam, a friend of the band, took several photographs of the group to be considered for potential album covers. From these sessions emerged the striking, evocative black-and-white cover of *Spiderland*, which perfectly captures the mysterious aura that envelops the record. "De esas sesiones surge la llamítica portada, la cual captura se aura de misterio que envuelve el disco."
**Lasting Influence and Legacy** : Over the years, *Spiderland* sold an increasing number of copies and significantly influenced many subsequent bands. Slint is often described as "the Velvet Underground of the 90s" in terms of their profound influence. While their sound wasn't purely post-rock, it marked the entire scene, with bands like Tortoise and Mogwai declaring that without *Spiderland*, everything would have been very different. It also influenced other styles such as Math Rock and "Pro de Sifemo" (Progressive Math). "Con los años, el disco fue vendiendo cada vez más copias, influyendo a muchas bandas que vendían detrás. Se podría decir de Slin, que son la velvetander round de los 90 en cuanto a influencia."
**Critical Acclaim from Steve Albini** : The first review for *Spiderland* came from Steve Albini for the British publication *Melody Maker*. He awarded the album a score of "10 damn stars," describing it as "unsettling, sinister, and beautiful." "La primera reseña del disco vino de la mano de Steve Albini para el medio británico Melody Maker, dijo de él, y le dio una puntuación de 10 jodidas estrellas."

# curiosity

**Steve Albini's Critical Review** : The acclaimed musician and producer Steve Albini contributed the first review of Slint's *Spiderland* for the British publication *Melody Maker*, where he awarded it a perfect score of "10 damn stars." His assessment of the album highlighted its unique, haunting, sinister, and beautiful qualities. "La primera reseña del disco vino de la mano de Steve Albini para el medio británico Melody Maker, dijo de él, y le dio una puntuación de 10 jodidas estrellas."
**Brian Paulson's Production Style** : Producer Brian Paulson was chosen for Slint's *Spiderland* album specifically for his reputation for recording bands live. His method involved capturing a sound that was very faithful to their rehearsals, with minimal post-production, a stark contrast to the abrasive sound associated with previous engineers. "Como productor, eligieron a Brian Paulson, quien era conocido por la barra a las bandas en directo, con un sonido muy fiel a los ensayos en muy poca posproducción."
**Astifal Vinny's Abrasive Sound** : Engineer Astifal Vinny recorded Slint's first album, "Fritz," in 1987. He was known for an "abrasive" sound, a characteristic the band wished to avoid for their subsequent album, *Spiderland*, leading them to choose a different producer. "Pese a estar contentos con Alvinie, decidieron cambiar de productor en Spider-Land para alejarse del sonido abrasivo que caracterizaba el ingeniero."
**The Unconventional Recording of Vocals on Spiderland** : On *Spiderland*, while all the music was meticulously rehearsed and recorded live, the vocals were left until the very end of the recording sessions. They were then completed in just two takes, with Brian McMahan's soft vocal style attributed primarily to his lack of confidence as a singer at the time. "No había el escritor al letras ni practicado, lo dejaron para el final de la grabación y lo cieron en dos tomas. Es estilo vocal suave, se debe, principalmente a la falta de confianza de magmajan como cantante."
