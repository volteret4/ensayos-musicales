# genre - Phonk
## curiosities
**Origins and Naming (2011)** : The genre originated in 2011, pioneered by Miami producer and rapper Space Goshpar and his collective Raider Clan. They fused the "dirty and lo-fi techniques" of Memphis rap with the "narcotic slowness" of Houston's Chop 'N' Screw, adding a modern, digital sensibility influenced by West Coast G-Funk. Space Goshpar dubbed this new sound "authentic Miami funk." "Space Goshpar llamó a esta música auténtico funk de Miami y en 2011 lanzó el tema Filda Funk 1990."
**Early Spread via SoundCloud** : Following the release of "Filda Funk 1990" in 2011, the "Phonk" label quickly gained traction on SoundCloud. A new global scene emerged, with artists worldwide paying homage to the Memphis and Houston pioneers while adding a new aesthetic filter. "La etiqueta empezó a resonar rápidamente en SoundCloud, y nuevos artistas de diferentes partes del mundo, como Lila Glimain, DJ Sacred o DJ Smokey, empezaron a generar una nueva escena global, venderando a los maestros de Memphis y Houston, pero añadiendo voluntariamente un nuevo filtro estético."
**Aesthetic Impact of Low Fidelity** : Phonk heavily incorporates the "low fidelity" aesthetic, drawing parallels with low-poly graphics in video games. Just as low-poly visuals create unsettling atmospheres in horror games, Phonk uses the "low fidelity of cassette tapes" to create sonically disturbing and dark environments. "Si el Low Poly usa la baja resolución para inquitar visualmente, el Funk usa la baja fidelidad del caset para inquitar sonoramente y evoca las atmósferas turbias y oscuras."
**Artistic Style and Imagery** : The visual art associated with Phonk mirrors its low-fidelity sound, featuring elements such as photocopied material, deformed generic typography, harsh shadows, low-poly and cropped images. These visuals are often tinted with a purple hue and heavily draw upon the excessive imagery of Houston and Memphis car culture. "todo ello te he ido del color por porad del lín, y con toda la imagen hería excesiva en arcúutica de Houston y Memphis."
**Initial Internet Distribution** : Like other internet-born genres of its era, such as vaporwave and cloud rap, Phonk initially found its audience and spread on platforms like SoundCloud. "Al igual que otros hijos de internet de su época, como el vapor wave o el cloud rap, el Funk empezamos a dar en plataforma como SoundCloud."
**Commercialization and Loss of Artistic Sense** : The rapid global growth of Phonk, especially Drift Phonk, led to its commercialization, particularly among teenagers who produced tracks "emptying it practically of any artistic sense." The genre transformed into a quick way to make money, with many new producers openly admitting their sole motivation was profit, leading to the "dehumanization" of its aesthetic. "el fonc se había olvidado de solencia cultural, y se convertió en una vía de ganar dinero rápidamente."
**Dehumanization of Aesthetic** : As Phonk grew commercially, its artistic identity deteriorated. Album covers became "AI-generated images," losing the original distinctiveness. Only "purple tones, heavy cowbells, and increasingly exaggerated sound saturation" remained, reducing Phonk to "musical nothingness" or "white noise without any personality," akin to music generated by ChatGPT. "De esa identidad original, solo quedaban los tonos por puras, los centeros machacones, y una saturación sonora cada vez más exagerada."
**Spotify's Official Playlist Controversy** : When Spotify finally created an official Phonk playlist, it "ignored pioneers" like Spinsy Gospar, Hollywood, and Lila Glimain, instead filling it with "commercial Russian sound," further cementing the public's misunderstanding of the genre's origins. "Cuando Spotify por fin creó su lista oficial de fonc, obvió a los videonejos como Spinsy Gospar, Hollywood o Lilac Limane llenando la desesonido ruso comercial."
**Adaptability and Longevity** : Phonk has shown remarkable longevity for an internet trend, surviving memes, algorithm changes, and its own saturation, earning it the moniker "the cockroach of the music industry." This is attributed to its producers' ability to "adapt to current trends," making it the "perfect genre to mold" for any "shitpost or brain drop." "Es el genero perfecto para moldear al entojo de cualquier shit post o brain drop."
**Music for Consumption, Not Listening** : The ubiquitous Phonk heard on social media is characterized as "music to consume, not to listen to." In a world saturated with stimuli and devoid of silence, the genre has found its "ideal place" by providing four-by-four, oversaturated beats designed to grab attention for three seconds in an infinite scroll. "Este fonc que ha indundado las redes no es música para escuchar sino para consumir, y es que en esta vida saturada de estímulos, vivacia de silencios, el género han encontrado su lugar ideal."

# genre - Drift Phonk
## curiosities
**Russian/Eastern European Origins (Late 2010s)** : Drift Phonk emerged in the late 2010s, primarily from producers in Russia and Eastern Europe. These artists were drawn to the aggressive elements of Memphis sound, focusing on "metallic cowbells," "distorted sub-basses," and "primitive electronic production," rather than the slow aspects. They intensified these elements, speeding them up, and added a "saturated kick drum" with a tempo close to house music. "La finales de la década de 2010, productores de Rusia y Europa del este empezaron a posicionarse con ese sonido de Memphis. Pero no les interesaba la parte lenta, sino lo más agresivo."
**Connection to Street Racing Culture** : Drift Phonk found its ideal audience in Russia's strong street car racing culture. The aggressive sound of its cowbells perfectly complemented visuals of Japanese cars drifting sideways, creating an "indivisible visual and sonic aesthetic" for the asphalt. "En Rusia existía una cultura de carreras callejeras de coches muy potente, y estos productores encontraron en esos ritmos la mandas son la perfecta para las faltó."
**Explosion via TikTok Algorithm (2019)** : The popularity of Drift Phonk exploded in 2019, especially through the TikTok algorithm. Its short, explosive sounds proved to be the "perfect complement" for the platform, quickly becoming ubiquitous as background music for content aiming to appear tough, stoic, or "internet-based." "en el algoritmo de Tik Tok, ambidento de sonidos cortos y explosivos en control del dirifunk, el complemento perfecto."
**Devouring the Original Phonk** : Drift Phonk grew so rapidly that it "devoured its father" – the original Phonk. For the vast majority of new audiences, Drift Phonk *was* Phonk, completely overshadowing the SoundCloud scene that had emerged just 10 years prior. "El dirifunk creció tanto que devoró a su padre. Para la inmensa mayoría del público nuevo, eso era fonc, y punto."

# curiosity

**Underground Distribution in 90s Southern US** : The early 90s hip hop scenes in Houston and Memphis were deeply underground, completely independent of mainstream East and West Coast hip hop, and lacked conventional radio play or distribution. Artists personally recorded their music onto cassettes and sold them directly, "hand-to-hand," from car trunks, in neighborhood stores, or at local concerts. "Era los propios artistas que les grababan su música en casets y las vendían mano a mano, en maleteros, entiendas de barrio con los conciertos locales."
**Resurgence of 90s Southern Underground** : The hermetic and artisanal nature of 90s Houston and Memphis hip hop scenes caused them to fade around the new millennium. However, starting in the 2010s, with the rise of online forums and social networks, many of their cassette recordings were digitized and uploaded, sparking an "unexpected cult" following worldwide for these "cursed works." "Esto provo conculto y nesperado alrededor de estas obras malditas."
**Cassette Fidelity as Aesthetic** : Many digitized Memphis tapes uploaded online were not originals but pirate copies, often re-recorded and resampled to the point of saturation. This physical wear created layers of "cracks and distortion," which, rather than being defects, enhanced their "cursed works" aura. This "patina of dirty noise" became a signature aesthetic, with Phonk producers intentionally seeking to recreate this atmosphere. "Esa patina de ruido sucio dejó de ser un defecto para convertirse en una firma estética, y los productores de funk buscarán recrear esa atmósfera."
**Low Fidelity Parallel to Low-Poly Graphics** : The aesthetic of low fidelity in Phonk is compared to low-poly graphics in PlayStation 1 and 2 video games. Just as low-poly visuals (with their characteristic trembling, jagged edges, and low-resolution textures) are resurfacing as an artistic medium, particularly effective in horror for their "expressionistic capacity," Phonk uses the "low fidelity of cassette tapes" to create sonically disturbing and dark atmospheres, aiming to visually and sonically unsettle the audience. "Si el Low Poly usa la baja resolución para inquitar visualmente, el Funk usa la baja fidelidad del caset para inquitar sonoramente y evoca las atmósferas turbias y oscuras."
**Visual Aesthetics of Phonk** : The low-fidelity aesthetic of Phonk extends to its visual art, which features elements like "photocopied material," "deformed generic typography," "harsh shadows," "low-poly and cropped images." These visuals are typically tinged with a "purple hue" and draw heavily from the "excessive imagery" of Houston and Memphis car culture. "Al igual que otros hijos de internet de su época, como el vapor wave o el cloud wrap, el Funk empezamos a dar en plataforma como SoundCloud."
**Brazilian Funk Visuals** : The visual aspect of Brazilian Funk (Fonc Brasileno) is noted for its lack of originality, frequently featuring "characters and heroes transformed into Super Saiyans." Despite this, these visuals typically retain the "purple hue" characteristic of the original Phonk. "Personajes y joyosconía convertiendes en supersayan al menos conservan el tono por fuera del fonc original."
