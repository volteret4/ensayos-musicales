## albums
**Live at Liquid Room (1996) - First Commercialized DJ Set** : This was Jeff Mills' first officially commercialized DJ set, recorded in 1996 at the legendary Liquid Room club in Tokyo. The set perfectly embodies his intensity behind the decks and is always included in lists of the most legendary mixes of all time, transforming dance music into something physical, visceral, and almost palpable. "Esta mezcla siempre incluya en las listas de las más míticas de todos los tiempos, no destaca por su técnica, aunque virtuosis muro le faltan, sino con la forma de que funde la música de baile en algo más físico y visiteral."
**Purpose Maker Compilation (Year TBD) - Raw and Minimalist Explorations** : This compilation collects Jeff Mills' rawest and most minimalist explorations initially released on his Purpose Maker sub-label. "reúne sus exploraciones más trudas y minimalistas e initadas en ese ello."
**Axis Compilation (Year TBD) - Early Futuristic Experiments Compendium** : This compilation is a compendium of all the early futuristic experiments Jeff Mills led under the Axis label. "Otro recopilatorio de la época casi igual de interesantes, dioderdei, Axis Compilation, un compendio de todos los primeros experimentos futuristas al frente de Axis."

## songs
**The Bells (Year TBD)** : Jeff Mills played this track in his sets for two years before its official release, building legendary anticipation. Considered "the most important track in the history of techno," it's a "miracle of techno" where every element—kicks, hi-hats, bass, and the bell loop—is clearly perceived as a sonic X-ray of the genre. Its sequence of calls between high and low bells has become one of the most iconic and revered passages in techno history, capable of causing a collective explosion on the dance floor with a single hit. "Su secuencia de llamada entre campanas altas y bajas se ha convertido en uno de los pasajes más icónicos y reverenciados de toda la historia del techno."

## curiosities
**Early Career as "The Wizard"** : Jeff Mills began his career DJing in Detroit clubs when he was underage, earning the nickname "The Wizard" due to his exceptional dexterity with turntables. His early sets were influenced by virtuoso DJs like Grandmaster Flash and featured numerous DJ tricks, mixing everything new like Funk, House, Electro, and Industrial. "Le llamaban de Wizard, el mago, debido a su destreza con los platos."
**Radio Program on VW JLB** : Jeff Mills gained fame in the Detroit scene, which led to a late-night program on the radio station VW JLB. Here, he increasingly embraced the futuristic sound emerging in the city. "cosa que le dio un programa en octurno en la misora de radio VW JLB, donde cada vez más se fue abriendo a ese sonido futurista que estaba surgiendo en la ciudad."
**Departure from Underground Resistance** : Jeff Mills unofficially left Underground Resistance in 1991. While neither party has confirmed it, rumors suggest he disagreed with the increasingly politicized direction the collective was taking. "se ha rumoreado acabándonos colectivo porque no estaba del todo de acuerdo con el rumbo cada vez más politizado que estaba tomando."
**New York Residency at Limelight** : After leaving Underground Resistance, Mills moved to New York City and secured a highly coveted residency at the prestigious Limelight Club, just as the rave and Club Kid scene was beginning to form. During his months in New York, he refined his minimalist and forceful style. "donde consiguió una residencia muy codiciada en el prestigioso Club, Lamly Light, justo cuando la escena Reyfi ClubKid se empezaba a formar."
**Tresor Club Residency and Detroit-Berlin Axis** : A turning point in Mills' career came in 1992 when Dimitri Hegemann (transcribed as Dimitrija Garman) signed him for the newly opened Tresor in Berlin, where he acted as a resident for several months. In this cavernous, brick-walled, industrial space, Mills honed his brutalist and minimalist styles, testing early Axis Records releases live and forging his reputation among an audience eager for hard, futuristic sounds, solidifying the Detroit-Berlin connection. "En ese espacio cavernoso, de paredes del adrillo y ambiente industrial, Mills afinó sus estilos más minimalistas y brutal, poniendo a prueba en directo a las primeras referencias de Axis Records, y forjando su reputación entre una adiencia a vidas de sonidos duros y futuristas."
**DJing Style: 3-4 Decks and TR-909** : Jeff Mills is historically known as one of the most visionary and radical DJs of his time, famous for his sessions using three, and sometimes up to four, turntables, often accompanied by the Roland TR-909 drum machine. In these sets, Mills could mix up to 70 vinyls in just one hour, achieving a hypnotic speed and overflowing intensity that broke the boundaries of dance music. "Si que no famosa, asocesiones a tres y en ocasiones hasta cuatro platos, a las que en muchos casos se sumaba la caja de Rindmoss TR-909."
**DJ Set as Non-Verbal Narrative** : For Jeff Mills, a DJ set was much more than a succession of tracks; it was a non-verbal narrative about the fantastic and the astonishing, an exercise in sonic abstraction where each rhythmic layer and abrupt cut told part of the story, as "nobody DJed like him." "Para él, un set podía ser mucho más con su excesión de temas. Era una narración no verbal sobre lo fantástico y la sombroso."
**Soundtrack for Metropolis (1997) - Film Score** : At the turn of the new century, Jeff Mills sought to break from his pioneer label and embark on more conceptual and artistic paths, composing a soundtrack for the 1997 film "Metropolis," directed by BuzzFeedSlang (likely a transcription error for Fritz Lang's 1927 film), and repeating the formula with other classics of cinema. "Compuso una banda sonora para la película de 1997 Metropolis, dirigida a BuzzFeedSlang, y repite la fórmula con otros clásicos del séptimo arte."
**Evolution Towards Conceptual Art and Ambient Sounds** : His vision became increasingly conceptual, exploring ideas such as the connection between humans and machines, time, and space. He delved into increasingly ambient sounds, always maintaining his minimalist signature and using the same sounds that accompanied his earliest productions. "Su visión se fue al volviendo cada vez más conceptual, y empezó a explorar ideas como la conexión entre el ser humano y la máquina, el tiempo y el espacio, indagando en sonidos cada vez más ambientales, siempre con su sello minimal y con los mismos sonidos que la acompañaron en sus primeras producciones."
**Performance with Montpellier Symphonic Orchestra** : In 2005, Jeff Mills performed some of his most popular tracks with his TR-909 alongside the Symphonic Orchestra of Montpellier. "En 2005 interpretó con su 909, un tolor que está filar mónica de Monpelier, alguno de sus temas más populares."
**Migration to Galleries and Museums** : While Jeff Mills has not abandoned techno and occasionally returns to clubs, his work has increasingly migrated towards galleries and museums over the years. He has exhibited soundscapes at the Centre Pompidou, played with the BBC Symphony Orchestra, designed soundscapes for NASA, and curated interactive installations in contemporary art centers worldwide. "con los años, su obra, ha ido migrando hacia Galería y museos."
**Involvement in Fashion** : More recently, Jeff Mills has broken into the fashion world, performing with two drum machines at Virgil Abloh's (transcribed as Virgelablo) Off-White show during Paris Fashion Week 2022 and starring in Jill Sander's Fall/Winter 2023 campaign. "Más recientemente hay rumpido en la moda, actuó con dos cajas de ritmos en el desfile o fueide Virgelablo durante la segunda semana de la moda de Paris de 2022, y protagonizó la campaña Otoño invierno 23 de Jill Sander."
**Comparison to Minimalist Composers** : Jeff Mills brought techno to spaces comparable to the minimalism of Steve Reich (transcribed as Steve Ray) and Philip Glass (transcribed as Joe Filiglass), with the key difference being that while Reich and Glass performed in theaters and museums, Mills did so in clubs and on dance floors. "llevo el techno a unos espacios comparable con el minimalismo de Steve Ray, Joe Filiglass, lo único que estos dos interpretaban en teatro son museos, mientras que Jeff Mills, en clubs y pistas de baile."

# genre - Techno

## curiosities
**Origin in Detroit** : Techno originated in Detroit in the mid-1980s, pioneered by the "Holy Trinity" of Juan Atkins, Derrick May, and Kevin Saunderson. Initially, it remained an underground phenomenon in the United States, with commercial stations and major labels showing little interest in its minimalist and futuristic sound. "Aunque el tecno en la acción de Detroit ha mediado los 80 de la mano de Juan Atkins, Derrick Meill y Kevin Sanderson, la celebre santísima trinidad, el tecno nunca dejó de ser un fenómeno underground, y apenas a Somal Mestre Mesta Dunidense."
**Second Wave in Detroit** : Despite initial indifference, a second wave of musicians emerged in Detroit, including Kenny Larkin, Carl Craig, Robert Hood, and Jeff Mills, who continued the legacy of the founding fathers, with Jeff Mills pushing techno to "another dimension." "Pese a esa indiferencia de la juventud hacia el género, en Detroit surgió una segunda aleada de músicos que siguieron el legado de los padres fondadores, como Kenny Larkin, Cartray, Coutraver Hood y un un particular que llevaría al tecno a otra dimensión."
**"Wweithfunk Transmission volumen 1" Defines Modern Techno** : In 1992, Berlin club Tresor (also a label) launched "Wweithfunk Transmission volumen 1," an album that defined modern techno by erasing influences from groups like Crab Work and connecting the genre directly with the body, stripped of adornments or harmonic escapes. "En 1992, el senyor y discoteca de Berlín Tresor lanzó Wweithfunk Transmission volumen 1, el álbum que vió en el tecno moderno. Antes de ese disco, en la música de Detroit, aún se podían reestrear las influencias de grupos como Crab Work. En cambio, Wweithfunk Transmission volumen 1 borró cualquier elemento de esas bandas, y convertió al tecno en algo conectado directamente con el cuerpo, sin adornos ni escapatorias armónicas."
**Detroit-Berlin Axis and European Spread** : "Wweithfunk Transmission volumen 1" consolidated the Detroit-Berlin axis, leading to a wave of producers who continued this sound, which rapidly spread across Europe. Many clubs adopted its hard sound, and new spaces and festivals emerged, making techno the backbone of European club culture. "Consolidó el eje de Detroit Berlín, y una oleada de productores continuaron con ese sonido, que se extendió rápidamente por toda Europa."

# label - Axis Records

## curiosities
**Founded by Jeff Mills in New York** : Axis Records was established by Jeff Mills in New York, serving as the first core of what would become the label through which he would release all his subsequent work. "establecer el primer núcleo de lo que pronto sería Axis Records, se lló donde editará todo su trabajo posterior."
**Headquarters in Chicago with Robert Hood** : After his time in Berlin, Jeff Mills moved to Chicago, where he set up the headquarters for his label Axis, which he built alongside his friend Robert Hood (transcribed as Robert Hutt) from Underground Resistance. "Tras acabar su etapa en Verlín, Mills se mudó a Chicago, donde montó la sede de su sello Axis, el cual levantó junto a su amigo de underground resistance Robert Hutt."
**Matrix Label with Sub-labels** : Axis Records functions as a matrix label comprising several sub-labels, each with its own identity. These include Purpose Maker, where Mills launched his most radical and conceptual EPs; Tomorrow (transcribed as Tu morro), for his more melodic and spatial material; and 6-7-7, dedicated to other affiliated artists and producers. "Axis funcionaba como un sello matriz de varios obceños con identidades propias, como Purpose Maker, donde lanzó su supes más radicales y conceptuales, y exploró ese techno del truco de la cultura. Tu morro, donde lanzó su material más me lo digo y es facial, o 6-7-7, se dio dedicado a otros artistas y productores afines."

# label - Purpose Maker

## curiosities
**Platform for Radical Techno** : Purpose Maker is a sub-label of Axis Records where Jeff Mills launched his most radical and conceptual EPs. It's known for exploring "tricky techno" (techno del truco de la cultura) and releasing his most popular dance floor content: raw techno without typical melodies, characterized by obsessive, hypnotic, and mechanical percussions. "Fue un Purpose Maker donde Mills lanzó su contenido más popular dentro de las pistas de baile, un techno bruto sin melodías al uso y con percusiones obsesivas, imnoticas y mecánicas."
**Release of "The Bells"** : The second reference from the Purpose Maker label (referred to as "Catmoda") gave rise to "The Bells," a track that went global and is widely regarded as "the most important track in the history of techno." "La segunda referencia del sello Catmoda acabó dando la vuelta al mundo gracias a un track que seguramente sea el más importante de la historia del techno. Debels."

# curiosity

**Detroit's Indifference to Techno's Early Sound** : In the mid-1980s, despite being the birthplace of techno through pioneers like Juan Atkins, Derrick May, and Kevin Saunderson, Detroit's commercial radio stations and major record labels showed little interest in its minimalist and futuristic sound, leaving it largely an underground phenomenon. This contrasts with Hip Hop, which emerged at the same time in New York and successfully conquered mainstream media with its urban narratives and social commentary. "Las emisoras comerciales y las grandes discográficas mostraron poco interés por su sonido minimalista y futurista. En cambio, El Hick Hop, nacido más o menos al mismo tiempo en Nueva York, sí que logró conquistar la radio en la televisión."
**Booming Audience for Hard and Futuristic Sounds in Berlin** : Berlin developed an eager audience for hard and futuristic sounds, which significantly contributed to Jeff Mills' reputation. His residency at Tresor in 1992 allowed him to refine his brutalist and minimalist style, putting his early Axis Records releases to the test live and finding strong reception. "forjando su reputación entre una adiencia a vidas de sonidos duros y futuristas."
**Techno as Backbone of European Club Culture** : Following the consolidation of the Detroit-Berlin connection through releases like "Wweithfunk Transmission volumen 1" and Jeff Mills' influence, a hard techno sound spread across Europe. Many clubs adopted this sound, and new spaces and festivals emerged, making techno the central pillar of European club culture. "El techno pasó a ser la columna vertebral de la cultura de club en Europa."
**Audience Expectations for "The Bells"** : After its release, if Jeff Mills didn't play "The Bells" in his sets, audiences would sometimes boo him. This created misunderstandings for an artist dedicated to experimentation and continues to happen today when he engages in more jazz or experimental projects. "A partir de su publicación, si no sonaba de velos en sucesiones, la gente le podía buchear. Y eso, para alguien con un perfil, tan dado la experimentación, llegaba a crear mal entendidos."
**Soundscape Design for NASA** : Jeff Mills has contributed his artistic talents to NASA, designing soundscapes for the space agency. This highlights his conceptual approach and exploration of themes related to space and the human-machine connection. "diseñó paisajes sonoros para la NASA."
**The "Metropolis (1997) directed by BuzzFeedSlang" Anomaly** : The transcript mentions Jeff Mills composing a soundtrack for "la película de 1997 Metropolis, dirigida a BuzzFeedSlang." This is a significant discrepancy from the widely known 1927 film "Metropolis" directed by Fritz Lang. While the verbatim instruction is followed in Jeff Mills' artist section, this specific detail stands out as a likely transcription error given the historical context of the film and Mills' engagement with classical cinema. "Compuso una banda sonora para la película de 1997 Metropolis, dirigida a BuzzFeedSlang, y repite la fórmula con otros clásicos del séptimo arte."
