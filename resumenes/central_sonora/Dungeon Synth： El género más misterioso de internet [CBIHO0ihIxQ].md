## genres

## albums
**Dauði Baldrs (1997) - Prison Album** : Recorded while Varg Vikernes was incarcerated, this album utilized a MIDI keyboard and a cheap recorder. The MIDI wind instruments sounded unrealistic, giving the album a "cheap" quality similar to a Super Nintendo JRPG of the era. It was initially heavily criticized, especially as it followed "Filosofem," but its perception has changed over time with the rise of micro-genres like Dungeon Chiptune. "Los instrumentos de viento, Midi estaban años luzes en la realistas y acabaron dando el en toque cutre."
**Filosofem (1996) - Predecessor to Prison Albums** : This album preceded "Dauði Baldrs" and was mentioned in the context of the criticism received by the subsequent prison albums due to their perceived lower quality sound. "y más después de ser el siguiente disco tras filósofem."

## curiosities
**Imprisonment of Varg Vikernes** : Varg Vikernes was sent to prison for several years following the 1993 murder of Euronymous, leader of Mayhem. During his incarceration, he recorded what are known as his "prison albums." "Este suceso envió a Bekerne esa prisión durante varios años."
**Admiration for Dark Wave Neoclassical** : Varg Vikernes acknowledged his admiration for Anglo-Saxon dark music derived from post-punk, specifically Dark Wave Neoclassical artists like Dead Can Dance and Death in June, which notably influenced the atmospheric approach of some of his works. "Entre ellos, el propio Wurzum, que reconoció su admiración por estas propuestas."

## genres

## albums
**First Album (1993)** : This was the first album to incorporate the aesthetic and imagery of Black Metal but was entirely composed using synthesizers. It marked a turning point in the genre. "En 1993 lanzó su primer disco. Esta fue la primera vez que un disco con la estética y el imaginario del Black Metal estaba completamente realizado con sintetizadores."
**First Four Albums (1993 onwards) - Foundational Works** : These initial four albums laid the groundwork for Dungeon Chiptune. While they received a polarized reception, with some Black Metal fans dismissing them, others found them to be a significant source of inspiration. "Sus primeros cuatro discos pusieron los cimientos del Lan-Jone Chiptune."

## curiosities
**Departure from Black Metal** : After recording the first album with Emperor, Håvard Ellefsen abandoned Black Metal, expressing weariness with the genre's direction, to explore a more atmospheric and introspective musical path with his solo project, Mortiis. "Tras grabaron el primer disco con la banda, abandonando el Black Metal, cansado del rumbo que estaba tomando el genero, para explorar un camino más atmosférico e introspectivo."
**Berlin School Influence** : Mortiis cited the Berlin School of electronic music and artists such as Tangerine Dream and Klaus Schulze as his main references, which is logical given their creation of layered, synthesizer-based textures to evoke spatial and futuristic journeys. "mortiscito a la escuela de Berlin y artistas como Tangier Indry, Mikraus Shullz, como sus principales referencias."

## curiosities
**Origin and Naming by Andrew Werner (2011)** : The genre officially received its name, Dungeon Synth, in 2011 when enthusiast Andrew Werner collected disparate recordings on a blog. He defined it as "the sound of an ancient crypt, the breath of the tomb, that can only be adequately transmitted with primitive, black, low-fi music, forgotten, dark and ignored by mainstream society." This act created a manifesto and expanded the musical scene. "En su primer post lo define así, el Dungeon Synth es el sonido de una criptantigua, el aliento de la tumba, que solo puede transmitirse adecuadamente con música primitiva, negro, low fey, olvidada, oscura e ignorada por la sociedad dominante."
**Roots in Black Metal Instrumentals (Late 1980s / Early 1990s, Norway)** : Dungeon Synth emerged from the extreme and dark Black Metal scene, particularly from the instrumental passages in albums by bands like Mayhem, Darkthrone, and Emperor, which sought to add an epic and atmospheric dimension to their work. These instrumental interludes, notably in Burzum's early records and especially his prison albums, gained more prominence and eventually led to dedicated synthesizer-based projects. "Noruega fue el epicentro de un género musical extremo, oscuro y radicalmente contracultural. El Black Metal Vandas como Maigen, Dark Throne o Enperor y cieron de la crudeza a su marca personal."
**Influences from Fantasy and Role-Playing Games** : The genre is deeply influenced by fantasy novels and role-playing games that its creators enjoyed in their youth, such as "The Lord of the Rings" and "Dungeons & Dragons." Many Dungeon Synth albums are conceived as unofficial soundtracks for stories of dragons, goblins, and abandoned castles, making it a highly narrative genre often favored by role-playing gamers. "Las principales referencias eran claras, el señor de los anillos y, como no, danjos andragons."
**"Bubble Burst" in the 2000s and Revival in the 2010s** : After expanding across Europe in the 1990s, the Dungeon Synth scene experienced a decline in the 2000s, with very few projects releasing new material due to lack of widespread interest and a clear genre label. This led to a period of obscurity and near-forgetfulness until Andrew Werner's blog in 2011 sparked a renewed interest, causing its popularity to skyrocket. "Pero al llegar los 2000, la burbuja se desinfló. Muy pocos fueron los proyectos que siguieron editando material."
**Evolution and Diversification (2010s onwards)** : Initially following the dark aesthetic of predecessors like Mortiis, Dungeon Synth gradually matured and solidified as its own genre, moving away from its Black Metal roots. It opened up to less dark, more overtly medieval sounds, integrating elements from retro video games and internet culture, even finding common ground with Vaporwave. This led to the emergence of projects offering different visions, from medieval baroque (Sorcerian Myth, Ophif) to enveloping ambient (Old Sorcery, Erang), and synthwave/retro gaming aesthetics (Questmaster). "Poco a poco, el dan Jon Synth comenzó a lejarse de sus raíces en el Black Metal, madurando y consolidándose como un generoso lido, con una estética propia cada vez más definida."
**Expansion through Bandcamp and YouTube** : Bandcamp became the primary distribution platform for Dungeon Synth, facilitating its ramification into numerous micro-genres (with over 50 variations documented). Later, YouTube channels like "The Dungeon Synth Archive" further democratized access, making hundreds of previously niche albums available to a much broader audience and allowing the genre to appear directly in users' feeds through algorithms. "Van Camm se convirtió en la principal plataforma de distribución del género, y fue allí donde el dan Jon Synth comenzó a ramificarse."

## curiosities
**Norwegian Epicenter (Late 1980s / Early 1990s)** : Norway became the central hub for this extreme, dark, and radically countercultural musical genre, with bands like Mayhem, Darkthrone, and Emperor defining its rawness. "La finales de los 80 y principios de los 90, Noruega fue el epicentro de un género musical extremo, oscuro y radicalmente contracultural."
**Instrumental Passages for Atmosphere** : Many Black Metal albums incorporated instrumental pieces to imbue the overall work with an epic and atmospheric, often cinematic and somber, quality. These instrumentals, notably in bands like Slay (likely a typo for a Black Metal band, or 'Slate' as per transcript) and Emperor, began to gain more notoriety and were foundational for the emergence of Dungeon Synth. "Muchos de los discos de la escena contendían piensas instrumentales que buscaban darle una idea épico y atmosférico al conjunto del disco."

# genre - Berlin School

## curiosities
**Influence on Dungeon Synth** : The Berlin School, with artists like Tangerine Dream and Klaus Schulze, significantly influenced Dungeon Synth, particularly artists such as Mortiis. These pioneers created layered, synthesizer-based textures designed to evoke futuristic and spatial journeys, a concept translated into the medieval and dungeon imagery of Dungeon Synth. "la escuela de Berlin y artistas como Tangier Indry, Mikraus Shullz, como sus principales referencias, y tiene lógica, ya que estos proyectos creaban capas de texturas basadas en sintitizadores para evocar viajes espaciales y futuristas."

# genre - Dark Wave Neoclassical

## curiosities
**Influence on Atmospheric Dungeon Synth** : This genre, derived from Anglo-Saxon post-punk (e.g., Dead Can Dance, Death in June), noticeably influenced the atmospheric approach of some Dungeon Synth artists, including Burzum, who explicitly admired these works. "La música oscura anglosajona derivada del postfunk, como el Dark Waze Neoclásico de Dead Candends o Death in June, influyeron notablemente en el enfoque atmosférico de algunos artistas."

# genre - Coldysynth

## curiosities
**Comfortable Nostalgia and Peaceful Aesthetics** : Also known as Cowsysynth, this subgenre seeks to evoke comfortable nostalgia, deliberately moving away from the dark and somber tones of classic Dungeon Synth. Its goal is to transmit calm, warmth, and peace. "Este Super Stilock busca abocar una nostalgia confortable, alejándose por completo de los tonos oscuros y sombridos del dan Jon Synth clásico para transmitir calma, calidad y paz."
**Childlike Melodies and Inspirations** : The sound of Coldysynth is built upon soft and emotional melodies, frequently reminiscent of those created with a child's keyboard or toys. Its aesthetic draws heavily from illustrations by Beatrix Potter and Arthur Rackham, featuring enchanting fables of bunnies, goblins, and hares. "Su sonido se construye a partir de melodías suaves y emocionantes, que a menudo parecen sacadas de un teclado infantil o hechas con juguetes."
**Connection to Kotashkor** : This microgenre is strongly associated with an internet trend known as "Kotashkor," which, despite its potentially whimsical sound, effectively delivers on its promise of cozy comfort. "Está muy relacionado con una tendencia en internet llamada Kotashkor, y si, puedes sonar revículo, pero realmente cumple lo que promete."

# genre - Dinosynth

## curiosities
**Prehistoric and Dinosaur-Themed Imagery** : Dinosynth's imaginative world revolves around dinosaurs and prehistoric life, or more specifically, a nostalgia for the earliest popular culture depictions of dinosaurs found in comics, stop-motion films, and trading cards. "Su imaginario giran tonos los dinosaurios y la vida prehistórica, o más bien hacia la nostalgia por las primeras representaciones en la cultura popular de los dinosaurios, de sus cómics, películas en stop motion y cromos."
**Sound of Prehistoric Epics** : The music of Dinosynth incorporates caveman percussion, roars, and melodies that evoke those ancient epics, creating a distinctive prehistoric soundscape. "Su música incluye percusiones que averniculas, rugidos y melodías que recuerdan a esas epopeillas."

# curiosity

**Early Hidden Recordings (1990s)** : In the 1990s, strange recordings circulated, seemingly destined for eternal clandestinity. These recordings were often found on old, hand-made cassettes in very limited editions, shared within private circles. "Refugiados en viejos caséis, hechos a mano de entidadas limitadísimas y compartidos en círculos privados."
**Andrew Werner's Vision (2011)** : Andrew Werner, an enthusiast, was powerfully drawn to these dispersed recordings and became convinced that they shared too many commonalities to remain nameless. In 2011, he decided to compile them on a blog, where he finally coined the genre "Dungeon Synth," creating a manifesto and giving life to a musical scene that continues to expand. "En 2011 decidió recopedarlos en un blog, el cual acabó bautizando al género, Dungeon Synth."
**Impact of Naming a Genre (Like Acid House)** : Similar to what happened with Acid House, once Dungeon Synth was given such an appealing label, its popularity soared unprecedentedly. Its evocative style, resembling forgotten video game music, and its temporal, sonic, and aesthetic ambiguity made it highly magnetic. "Al igual que pasó con el Eckpank, una vez el genero fue nombrado bajo una tiqueta tan atractiva, su popularidad se disparó como nunca antes."
**Influence of Video Game Music** : The music from video games became a significant reference for the new generation of Dungeon Synth musicians. RPGs from Super Nintendo like Terranigma, Secret of Mana, and The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time (referred to as "los In of Time"), Final Fantasy games from the first PlayStation, and PC games like Daggerfall (referred to as "Darkerfall"), Hexen, and Diablo offered rich and atmospheric soundscapes that resonated deeply with the language of Dungeon Synth. "RPGs de Super Nintendo como Terranigma, Secret of Mana o los In of Time, los Final Fantasy de la primera PlayStation, ojos de PC como Darkerfall, Exeno Diablo, ofrecían un mensaje salidos tan ricos y atmosfericos que parecían estar hechos con el mismo lenguaje que el dan Jon Synth."
**The Dungeon Synth Archive (YouTube Channel)** : This YouTube channel is dedicated to archiving and disseminating Dungeon Synth works, significantly facilitating access to hundreds of albums that were previously hidden on Bandcamp or specialized forums. Thanks to this initiative, Dungeon Synth has reached a much wider audience. "Canales como de Danyon synth archive se han dedicado a archiva a arribi fundir estos trabajos, facilitando el acceso a cientos de álbumes que antes estaban escondidos en bancam, buenforos especializados."
**Shifting Music Consumption Trends** : There is a broader cultural trend of renewed interest in medieval and dark fantasy, coinciding with the rise of Dungeon Synth. This shift reflects a change in how music is perceived and consumed, moving beyond mere style or genre to emotions, atmospheres, and worlds people want to inhabit. Music is increasingly listened to for what it makes listeners feel rather than just what it is. "Parece que está cambiando la forma en la que percibimos y consumimos la música. Ya no se trata solo de estilo o generos, sino de emociones, atmósferas, mundos que queremos evitar."
