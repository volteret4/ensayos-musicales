## genres

## albums
**King Knight (2010) - Manifest Sonoro y Estético** : Salem's first album became a sonic and aesthetic manifesto for Witchhouse. It marked the genre's "explosion," moving the music from blogs and forums to capture the attention of the music press and early alternative festivals. "Su primera álbum, King Knight, se convirtió en un manifiesto sonoro y estético, y fue el gran momento de explosión del Witchhouse, cuando esa música que se gestaba en foros y blogs, pasa a captar la atención de la prensa musical y de los primeros festivales alternativos interesados en la escena."

## curiosities
**Founding Band of Witchhouse** : Formed in Michigan in 2007, Salem is considered the foundational band of Witchhouse, being the first to give real shape to its dark and dense sound. The current lineup includes Jack Donoghue and John Holland. "Formada en Michigan en 2007, por Jack Donogue, Geder Marlatt y John Holland, actualmente solo sí que en Jack y John en la formación, fueron las primeros en dar forma rea al este sonido cure y denso."
**Distinctive Sound** : Their music combined the slowed bases of Southern Kinghops with dense synthesizers, infinite reverbs, and voices that floated between the angelic and the demonic. Their sound was compared to what might result if Cocteau Twins and Lil' Jon Escriu abandoned a John Carpenter film score. "Su música combinaba las bases ralentizadas del Kinghops suréño con sintetizadores denso, reverbs infinitos y voces que flotaban entre el Angelical y lo demoniaco. El sonido que había surgido si cocto Twins y Lil'Jay Escriu hubiera dicho un abandono era para una película de John Carpenter."
**Controversial Public Image** : Salem has always been a controversial band. Members like John and Jack are proud of their Midwest origins and are known to associate with various characters. They have also had relationships with celebrities such as Julia Fox, Cornilo, and Follana del Rey. "Los cierto es que salen siempre han sido una banda polémica. Se ha seguido el Instagram de John, Walt de Jack, pero es que están orgullosos de pertenecer al Midwest y de relacionarse con todo tipo de personajes. Además de haber tenido relaciones con celibitiz como Julia Fox, Cornilo, Follana del Rey."

## curiosities
**Originator of Chopped & Screwed** : DJ e-Screw is a legendary figure who originated the Chopped & Screwed G-Hop scene in Houston during the 1990s. His celebrated "screwtapes" were influential and collected by musicians like Robert Disarro. "Originada por el legendario DJ e-Screw, esta técnica consistía en ralentizar trásticamente los debates, repetir fragmentos de canciones y rapear de forma restaurada, casi son ámbola, hasta lograr un efecto inmótico y casi narcótico."
**Hypnotic Sound Technique** : His signature technique involved drastically slowing down beats, repeating song fragments, and rapping in a stretched, almost somnambulant manner to achieve a hypnotic and nearly narcotic effect. Witchhouse producers later adapted this exact technique to invoke a ghostly atmosphere. "Esta técnica consistía en ralentizar trásticamente los debates, repetir fragmentos de canciones y rapear de forma restaurada, casi son ámbola, hasta lograr un efecto inmótico y casi narcótico."

# artist - Travis H.J.D.

## member of
Victor Plane

## curiosities
**Coined "Witchhouse" Name** : In 2009, Travis H.J.D., known for his project Victor Plane, coined the term "Witchhouse" during an interview with Pitchfork magazine. He defined the genre as "Witchhouse" as a joke, playing with the idea of "haunted" (brujo/witch) and House music, despite the genre having little to do with traditional House music. "En 2009, Travis H.J.D, conocido por su proyecto Victor Plane, en una entrevista con la revista Pitchfork, definió el genero como Witchhouse, jugando con la idea del hombrujado y la música House, aunque dejamos ya sa delante que no tenía nada."
**Name Adoption and Dislike** : Despite originating as a joke about the darkness and "cursed" nature of these bands, the name "Witchhouse" quickly caught on with the press and became consolidated as the genre's official name, even though most groups within the scene disliked it. "El nombre, pese a que nació como un chiste sobre la oscuridad y el malditismo de estas bandas, caló rápido en la prensa y terminó consolidándose como el nombre del genero, a pesar de que la mayoría de los grupos no les gustaba."

# genre - Witchhouse

## curiosities
**Origin and Early Characterization** : Witchhouse began gestating over 15 years ago in the deepest corners of the internet, emerging as one of the darkest, most hypnotic, and mysterious micro-genres of the digital era. It had a brief lifespan of just two years before almost disappearing, but it was enough to mark the musical landscape of its time with darkness. "La historia es la historia del Witchhouse, el primer fantasma son los internetos."
**Opposite to Luminous Trends** : Emerging at the end of the 2000s and beginning of the 2010s, Witchhouse developed as a deliberately dark and opposite scene to the prevalent luminous alternative music (like New Rave, Rainbow Rock, Walshiel Wave). It deformed pop codes to invoke ghostly atmospheres and digital rituals, characterized by occult symbolism, dragged tempos, and a spectral aura. "Mientras esta corriente dominaba la superficie, en los márgenes de la rey, te empezaba a gestarse una escena opuesta, una escena deliberadamente oscura, que deformaba los códigos del pop para invocar atmósferas fantasmales y rituales digitales. Un movimiento cargado de simbolismo ocultista, tiempos arrastrados y una hora espectral. El Witchhouse"
**Influence of Southern G-Hop** : Around 2009, producers with dark sensibilities, drawn to gothic and industrial sounds, began to focus on Southern G-Hop, specifically Houston's Chopped & Screwed scene. They saw that the technique of slowing down sounds to induce a narcotic state could be used to invoke a ghostly atmosphere, forming the core sound of Witchhouse. "Estos productores de sensibilidad oscura vieron que esa misma técnica de ralentizar los sonidos para inducir un estado casi narcótico, podían plearse también para invocar una atmósfera fantasma."
**Post-Geographic Nature** : Witchhouse is considered one of the first truly post-geographic musical genres, meaning it did not originate in a specific city or local scene, but rather across different parts of the world simultaneously among producers sharing a dark sensibility. "El Witchhouse es uno de los primeros géneros musicales, verdaderamente posqueográficos, es decir, no nació una cidad concreta o una escena local."
**Influences Beyond G-Hop** : Besides Southern G-Hop, Witchhouse drew influences from Darkwave and Field Post-Punk (for dark sounds and powerful basslines), Industrial (for noise, experimental, and provocative spirit), and Dreampop and Shoegaze (for ethereal textures and wall of sound). All these elements were layered with dense synthesizers, often close to Synthwave. "Además del Kinghops suréño, el genero tuvo otras influencias, por ejemplo, Del Larway y Fielpost Punk, en el 2 sonidos cure los bajos contundentes, del industrial, como el ruidismo y suspirito experimental y provocador, y del Dreampop y el sugeys, tomó las texturas etéreas y son muro de sonido."
**Rich Visual World and DIY Ethos** : Beyond its sound, Witchhouse cultivated a rich visual world that complemented its dark imaginary. This included occult symbols, arcane characters, broken typographies, disturbing images (appearing to be from nightmares or the Deep Web), glitched digital effects, and a strong DIY spirit reminiscent of punk and extreme metal. "Sin bonos ocultistas, chicarácteres sonicodes, tipografías rotas, e imágenes perturbadoras que parecían sacadas de una pesadilla o directamente de la Deep Web. Todo ello repleto de glises digitales y efectos subí HSM."
**Inspiration from Film** : The visual aesthetic of Witchhouse drew inspiration from films such as "The Blair Witch Project" and the cinema of David Lynch. "Se inspiraron en películas como de Blar Witch Project o el Cine de David Lynch."
**Tumblr as a Showcase** : Tumblr became a major showcase for the Witchhouse genre, intertwining the gothic, the digital, and the ironic to construct an influential visual identity on the platform. "També se convertió en el gran escaparate del genero, un lugar donde logótico, lo digital y lo ironico se entrelazaban para construir una identidad visual que fue un influyente en la plataforma."
**Evolution into Other Genres** : Just a few years later, the dark sensibilities initiated by Witchhouse spread and mutated, giving rise to trends like Soft Grunge or Nugoth, which dominated platforms like Tumblr between 2013 and 2015. "Tán solo unos años después, estas sensibilidades cura que habían iniciado Witch House se extendió inmutó, dando origen atendencias como el Soft Branch o el Nugoth, que dominaron la plataforma entre 2013 y 2015."
**Internet-Native Genesis** : Witchhouse was one of the first truly internet-born genres. It emerged on MySpace, consolidated on blogs and forums, was shared on Tumblr, and distributed via SoundCloud and Bandcamp, marking a new paradigm in music creation and dissemination. "Sorgieron en Maya Space, se consolidaron en Blocks y Foros, se compartían en Tumblr, y se destruyan por SoundCloud y Bankham."
**Enduring Legacy as a "Ghost"** : While Witchhouse faded as a defined genre, its spirit empowered an entire generation of music, and its legacy extends through labels like Triangle and into mainstream artists. It is best seen as a "ghost" rather than truly dead. "El witchhouse se desmanició como un género definido. Sí, pero Suspirito había posibilidad a toda una generación de música. El legado del witchhouse entiende a través de selles como Triangle."
**Mainstream Influence** : The spectral influence of Witchhouse can be appreciated in artists like Grimes, the sonic grimness of Death Grips, the atmosphere of Katy Perry's "Dark Horse," and the dark textures adopted by many trap producers. Billie Eilish's sound, deep bass, and dark melancholic aesthetic are seen as a modern combination of Witchhouse codes in the mainstream. "De repente, su influencia espectral estaba por todas partes. Se podía apreciar en artistas como Grimes, o en la grisimidad sónica de desgrips, incluso en el pop de Kate Perry, escuchar la atmósfera de Dark Horse. Muchas productores de trap se fijaron en las texturas oscuras de salen."

# genre - Chopped & Screwed

## curiosities
**Origin and Technique** : Chopped & Screwed was a G-Hop scene prominent in Houston during the 1990s, originated by the legendary DJ e-Screw. The technique involved drastically slowing down beats, repeating fragments of songs, and rapping in a drawn-out, almost somnambulant way, creating a hypnotic and nearly narcotic effect. "El Chopin e-Screwed fue una escena de G-Hop que fue la profeición Houston durante los años 90. Originada por el legendario DJ e-Screw, esta técnica consistía en ralentizar trásticamente los debates, repetir fragmentos de canciones y rapear de forma restaurada, casi son ámbola, hasta lograr un efecto inmótico y casi narcótico."
**Inspiration for Witchhouse Atmosphere** : Witchhouse producers adapted the core technique of Chopped & Screwed—slowing sounds to induce a near-narcotic state—to serve a different purpose: invoking a ghostly atmosphere. "Estos productores de sensibilidad oscura vieron que esa misma técnica de ralentizar los sonidos para inducir un estado casi narcótico, podían plearse también para invocar una atmósfera fantasma."

**Enabling Advanced Home Production** : Increasingly sophisticated VST (Virtual Studio Technology) programs, used in conjunction with home studio software like FL Studio, Ableton, and Reason, revolutionized music production. They allowed musicians to virtually recreate any synthesizer, drum machine, bass, or guitar, and apply professional-grade mixing and mastering effects from a home computer, empowering unprecedented creative freedom and the rise of unconventional genres. "programas UBCT cada vez más sofisticados, estaba cambiando las reglas del juego."

# curiosity

**Rise of Internet-Born Genres** : The late 2000s and early 2010s saw a paradigm shift with Witchhouse and Vaporwave emerging as the first truly internet-native genres. They began on MySpace, found consolidation on blogs and forums, were widely shared on Tumblr, and distributed through platforms like SoundCloud and Bandcamp, fundamentally altering the landscape of music creation, dissemination, and reception outside traditional industry channels. "Tanto el Witchhouse como el Bapar Way, fueron los primeros géneros verdaderamente nacidos en internet. Sorgieron en Maya Space, se consolidaron en Blocks y Foros, se compartían en Tumblr, y se destruyan por SoundCloud y Bankham."
**Artists' Names with Unpronounceable Characters** : Many artists within the Witchhouse scene adopted names featuring unpronounceable characters, serving both an aesthetic purpose aligned with the genre's dark imagery and a practical function of hindering searchability. This deliberate choice fostered a clandestine and exclusive atmosphere, reinforcing the perception of Witchhouse as a "cursed" and underground genre, accessible only to those privy to its secret codes. "Muchas artistas usaban caracteres e imponunciables en sus nombres, y no solo por su estética, sino también para dificultar su voz que da ir a fortar ese a lo clandestino, como si formar parte de la escena implicara conocer cientos códigos secretos."
**Dismissal by Traditional Music Press** : The nascent internet-born genres, including Witchhouse and early Vaporwave (e.g., "Floral Shoppe," "Chuck Person's Eccojams Vol. 1"), were largely dismissed by the mainstream music press. Critics often viewed these projects as "jokes" or "memes" due to their unconventional names and aesthetics, overlooking their musical seriousness and innovative contributions. This initial misjudgment highlighted a disconnect between traditional critical frameworks and the rapidly evolving digital music landscape. "Esto también ocurrió con la primera etapa del Bapar Way, discos como Floral Chopo, Chuck Person Secochán, Volumen 1, fueron dejados de lado por gran parte de la prensa musical, quienes lo veían como memes."
**Influence of the 2009 Global Crisis** : Around 2009, amidst a global economic crisis affecting almost every corner of the world, various producers with a dark sensibility (drawn to gothic and industrial sounds) began looking towards genres like G-Hop. This backdrop of crisis and pervasive darkness likely contributed to the emergence and appeal of Witchhouse's somber aesthetic. "Al rededor de 2009 y con la crisis, ya golpeando prácticamente en cualquier rincon del mundo, varios productores de distintos lugares con una sensibilidad oscura, cercan algo o sea y al industrial, empezaron a fijarse en un genero que hasta entonces parecía moverse en un universo completamente distinto."
**Home Studio Revolution** : The accessibility and sophistication of home studio software like FL Studio, Ableton, and Reason, coupled with advanced VSTs, revolutionized music production. This allowed musicians to recreate virtually any instrument and apply professional-grade mixing and mastering effects from a personal computer, granting total creative control and enabling a surge of experimental and unconventional genres that broke from traditional industry norms. "Desde un ordenador doméstico era posible recrear cualquier sentitizador, caja de ritmos, bajo, guitarra, y aplicar efectos de mezcla y masterización, que antes solo existían en estudios profesionales. Este control total permitió hacer a los músicos cualquier locura que les pasara por la cabeza, y es por eso que en esa época, Floricieron tantos géneros que se escapaban de lo convencional."
**Traditional Press Initial Misunderstanding** : The music press initially struggled to understand and categorize the strange new proposals coming from internet-born genres like Witchhouse. They perceived these movements as having their own rules, not belonging to the traditional industry, and destined for the margins of the internet. However, time eventually showed that these margins were gestating truly interesting music and figures who would ultimately change the mainstream. "La prensa andaba perdida. Parecía que estas propuestas tan extrañas tenían sus propias normas y leyes, y no tenían que vida en la industria, sino que era algo destinado a los márgenes de internet, pero con el tiempo se ha visto que no era así. En esos márgenes, estaba gestando música realmente interesante, con figuras que acabarían cambiando el mainstream."
