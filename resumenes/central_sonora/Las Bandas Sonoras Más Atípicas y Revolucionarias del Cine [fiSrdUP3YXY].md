# artist - Genio y Yamashiro Bumi
## curiosities
**Akira Soundtrack - Diegetic Music and Unique Sound** : The music in *Akira*, composed by the collective Genio y Yamashiro Bumi, initially appears as diegetic when Kaneda places a CD into a "yuki box." This music, distinct from typical science fiction scores, is characterized by colorful and textural percussions, mysterious sounds, organic and tribal elements, layers of synthesizers, electronic percussions, and Japanese ritualistic voices. It created a sense of the unknown that fused perfectly with the film's urban, overpopulated setting and narrative. "La música que surge desde el yuki box, originalmente parte de la película, como música diágetica, se convierte en un componente sencial de la banda sonora."
**Musical Influences - Traditional Balinese Music (Gamelan)** : The unique percussive and mysterious sounds featured in *Akira*'s soundtrack were later discovered to be derived from, or heavily influenced by, traditional Balinese music, specifically Gamelan. Genio y Yamashiro Bumi, the collective behind the score for Katsuhiro Tomo's masterpiece, sourced some of their distinctive sounds from this tradition. "Por fin descubrí donde sacaron algunos a de sus sonidos, Genio y Yamashiro Bumi, el colectivo encargado de realizar la banda sonora de la obra maestra de Katsuhiro Tomo."

## curiosities
**Forbidden Planet (1956) Score - First Electronic and Magnetic Tape Soundtrack** : For the 1956 film *Forbidden Planet*, the musical duo Bebe, Luis Barron created the first entirely electronic film score. This work also marked a historical milestone as the first score to be recorded on magnetic tape. Their avant-garde composition was ahead of its time, serving as an inspiration for numerous musicians seeking to create sounds for unknown worlds and extraterrestrial spacecraft, thereby immersing audiences in a fascinating and pioneering sonic universe. "Para la película de 1956, Forbidden Planet, la pareja de músicos, Bebe, Luis Barron, crearon la primera banda sonora totalmente electrónica y marcaron un hído en ser también la primera partito de la realizada en Cinta Magnética."

# artist - Juanitri Expon-Never

"La banda sonora influenciada por figuras como Tangier Indrimi Vanjelly se molde a través del filtro inagógico de Lopatín."

# artist - Bernadjema
## curiosities
**Psycho Shower Scene Score - Avant-Garde Strings** : Bernadjema (Bernard Herrmann) composed the score for the iconic shower scene in *Psycho*, employing all violins to create a screeching and piercing sonic movement. This innovative approach positioned the score closer to contemporary musical avant-garde than the conventional film music of its era, drawing comparisons to John Cage rather than Max Steiner. It is widely considered one of the most famous and imitated film scores in history. "Como hizo Bernadjema en la partitura de psicosis, que en la escena de la ducha, pone a todos los violines en un movimiento sonoro chisriante y punzante."

# artist - reconio Morricone
## curiosities
**Dollar Trilogy Scores - Iconic Western Sound and Budget Innovation** : reconio Morricone (Ennio Morricone) composed the unforgettable soundtracks for Sergio Leone's Dollar Trilogy films. For the first film, *Por un poñado de dólares* (*A Fistful of Dollars*), a limited budget meant an orchestra couldn't be afforded, prompting Morricone to compose a score for electric guitar, whistles, shouts, and percussions. While he refined this approach in *En la muerte tenía un precio* (*For a Few Dollars More*), it was for *El bueno soyol y el malo* (*The Good, the Bad and the Ugly*) that he wrote one of the most iconic scores in cinematic history. "Otras bandas sonoras que han dejado una huella imorrable en la historia del cine y han indefinido el sonido de lo este son las de la trilogía del dólar, compuestas por en el riconio Morricone dirigida a Sporzey, Leone."
**A Fistful of Dollars Score - Resourceful Instrumentation** : Faced with a minimal budget for *Por un poñado de dólares*, reconio Morricone innovatively composed the score using an electric guitar, whistles, shouts, and percussions, as a full orchestra was not an option. This creative constraint resulted in a distinctive and genre-defining sound for the Western film. "Para la primera de ellas, por un poñado de dólares, apenas tenían presupuesto para la música y al no poder permitirse un arque está Morricone compuso una partitura para guitarra eléctrica, silbidos, gritos y percusiones."
**The Good, the Bad and the Ugly Score - Landmark Achievement** : The score composed by reconio Morricone for *El bueno soyol y el malo* is recognized as one of the most iconic film scores in the history of cinema. This work cemented his innovative approach to scoring Westerns, building on his previous successes in the Dollar Trilogy. "pero fue en el bueno soyol y el malo donde escribió una de las partituras más icónicas de la historia del cine."
**The Thing (1982) - Partial Usage and Director's Preference** : For the film *The Thing* (1982), reconio Morricone was hired with a larger budget than his previous works with John Carpenter. However, the director was not entirely satisfied with the outcome, and only a minimal portion of Morricone's extensive composition was ultimately used, primarily what became the main theme. This theme was noted for its close resemblance to Carpenter's own synthesized music. "De toda la música que compuso el italiano, apenas usaron el que sería el tema principal de la película, el cual poco difiere de la música sentitizada de Carpenter."
**The Hateful Eight - Redesigned Discarded Music** : Quentin Tarantino specifically requested "descartes" (discarded pieces) from reconio Morricone for the soundtrack of his film *Los odiosos 8* (*The Hateful Eight*). These "forgotten notes" found redemption and a new place within the narrative, highlighting how musical compositions can find their appropriate moment even after being set aside. "cuenten Tarantino le pidió esos descartes a Eniomorricone para la banda sonora de la película de los odiosos 8."

# artist - un carpeto
## curiosities
**Early Film Scores - Budget-Driven Electronic and Minimalist Approach** : Due to a lack of budget, un carpeto (John Carpenter) composed the soundtracks for his early films himself. For *Assalto a la comisaria del distrito 13* (*Assault on Precinct 13*), he created a dark, eerie, and minimalist score primarily using synthesizers and drum machines. This resourcefulness defined his signature sound. "También por falta de presupuesto, de un carpeto en realizó la banda sonora de sus primeras películas."
**Assault on Precinct 13 Score - Dark, Eerie, and Minimalist** : For *Assalto a la comisaria del distrito 13*, un carpeto composed a score characterized by its dark, eerie, and minimalist qualities. This music was built fundamentally on synthesizers and drum machines, a choice that established his distinctive electronic style. "Con asalto a la comisaria del distrito 13, compuso una música oscura, espeluznante y minimalista, base de sentitizador y caja de ritmos."
**Halloween Theme - Iconic Modern Horror Piece** : In his subsequent film after *Assalto a la comisaria del distrito 13*, un carpeto created the most iconic song of his career, which became one of the most recognizable pieces in modern horror. His music is considered a "sign of identity," celebrated for its "escarpente de Diana" quality when blending genres such as Rosin, progressive electronic music, and minimal synth. "Pero fue en la siguiente película donde realizó la canción más icónica de su carrera y una de las piezas más reconocibles del terror moderno."
**Genre Blending - Rosin, Progressive Electronic, Minimal Synth** : un carpeto's musical style is distinguished by its ability to blend genres like Rosin, progressive electronic music, and minimal synth. This fusion creates a unique "sign of identity" in his compositions, noted for its "escarpente de Diana" quality. "mezcla generos como la Rosin, la música electrónica progresiva y el sin voz más minimal."
**The Thing (1982) - Morricone Collaboration and Thematic Resemblance** : For *The Thing* (1982), un carpeto had a larger budget and hired reconio Morricone to compose the score. Despite this, un carpeto was not entirely pleased with the final result. Only the film's main theme, which closely resembled un carpeto's own synthesized music, was predominantly used from Morricone's composition. "Para la cosa, Carpenter dispuso de más presupuesto y se permitió el lujo de contratar a Eniomorricone, aunque el resultado no fue del todo de la grado del director."
