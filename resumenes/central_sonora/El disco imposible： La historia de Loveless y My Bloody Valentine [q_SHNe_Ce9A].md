## albums
**Isn't Anything (1988) - Debut Album** : Released in 1988, this album, along with the "You Made Me Realise" EP, marked a significant period for the band. Released only four months apart, these two works showcased My Bloody Valentine beginning to find their distinctive sound, characterized by extreme experimentation with guitars, extensive use of effects pedals, and the integration of noise as a natural component of their music. These works were very well received within the community and were considered the almost official starting point for the emerging sound of Shoegaze. "Estos trabajos fueron muy bien acogidos dentro de la comunidad y serían considerados como el punto de partida casi oficial de este sonido que estaba emergiendo. El Show Guys."
**Loveless (1991) - Shoegaze Masterpiece** : The band's second album, released in 1991, is widely regarded as the absolute classic of Shoegaze, a record that redefined guitar-based music, and one of the most influential works of the 1990s. Its production was an odyssey of chaos, extending over two and a half years and passing through 19 recording studios, bringing Creation Records to the brink of bankruptcy. Despite selling well, it initially fell far short of recouping its investment, partly due to its release only a month and a half after Nirvana's market-breaking album "Nevermind." Today, it is an undeniable and timeless classic. "Lofless nace del caos, la confusión y el agotamiento, según lo que se podría haber hecho de una forma mucho más fácil y con menos cabezonería, si Charles se hubiese dejado ayudar, pero este proceso no es un disco único y repetible."
**m b v (2013) - Unexpected Return** : Released in 2013, against all odds, this album demonstrated that beneath layers of noise and beauty, the genius of My Bloody Valentine still pulsed. Its existence proved that a miracle could emerge from chaos, confirming the band's enduring creative power after a long hiatus following their seminal "Loveless" album. "En 2013, contra todo pronóstico, lanzaron Mbume, un disco que nos hizo ver que bajo capas de ruido y belleza, o un latil al genio de Maible de Valentine."

## curiosities
**Band Formation and Early Search for Sound** : My Bloody Valentine formed in Dublin and Orlando in 1983, founded by guitarist Kevin Shields and drummer Colm Ó Cíosóig. In their early years, they released a series of singles and EPs that showed them actively searching for their distinctive sound, leaning towards a more garage-rock style with production reminiscent of Spector's "wall of sound" and influenced by The Cramps. These initial works were released on small labels and had little impact. "La banda se informó en Dublin y Orlando en 1983 por el guitarrista Kevin Shiles y el batería Colmo Kisoyk."
**Signing with Creation Records and Move to London (1988)** : In 1988, the independent label Creation Records saw something special in My Bloody Valentine and released their EP "You Made Me Realise." Impressed by this EP, Alan McGee of Creation Records recognized their enormous potential for pushing guitar experimentation into unexplored territories. Consequently, the band signed with Creation Records in 1988 and relocated to London. "Por lo que en 1978, la banda firmó a concriación records y se mudaron a Londres." (Note: Corrected 1978 to 1988 based on context).
**"Loveless" Production Nightmare - Duration and Studios** : The recording process for My Bloody Valentine's second album, "Loveless," began in February 1989 with an initial five-day booking at Blackwing Studios in London. However, this process dramatically extended to two and a half years, involved 19 different recording studios, and pushed Creation Records to the brink of bankruptcy. "Lo que nadie imagina es que el proceso de grabación se extenderá durante dos años y medio, pasará por 19 estudios de grabación y llevará al borde de la quibra al sello discográfico."
**Kevin Shields' Authoritarian Control During "Loveless"** : During the recording of "Loveless," Kevin Shields took authoritarian control, allowing no one but himself to play the instruments, despite other members being credited. An engineer from Elephant Studios noted that Shields made it clear from the beginning that technicians were primarily there to teach him how to use the machinery and press buttons. Bilinda Butcher and Debbie Googe, though credited, mostly did not appear in the studios except for the recording of the EPs. "Charles había tomado las riendas de forma autoritaria y no dejaba que nadie salvo el tocar a los instrumentos."
**Unusual Vocal Recording Sessions for "Loveless"** : The vocal tracks for "Loveless" were eventually recorded at Britannia Row studio, in sessions described as quite strange. Curtains were installed between the control room and the vocal booth, and the band members communicated with the technicians by simply sticking a hand through the curtain if a take was good. "Las sesiones eran un tanto extrañas, instalaron cortinas entre la sala de control y la cabina de voces."
**Band Equipment Seized Due to Creation Records' Financial Woes** : A significant setback during the "Loveless" recording occurred at Eastcote Studios when Creation Records ran out of funds and could not pay the previous studio. This led to the retention of the band's equipment by the unpaid studio. Alan McGee personally advanced money from his own pocket to retrieve the gear and allow recording to continue. "Creation Records no puede pagar el anterior estudio, se han quedado sin fondos, por lo que el estudio retiene el equipo de la banda, que Michel estuvo a avanzar el dinero de su bolsillo para recuperarlo."
**Kevin Shields and Bilinda Butcher Suffer Tinnitus** : Just when it seemed the recording of "Loveless" was back on track, both Kevin Shields and Bilinda Butcher suffered from tinnitus due to the deafening volumes at which they played their concerts. This condition forced them to postpone the recording sessions for several more weeks, adding to the project's already significant delays. "Cuando parecía que la grabación al fídeo va a seguir adelante, tanto Charles como Bacher sufra en detenitos por los ensorcecedores volumenes a los que toca en sus conciertos, por lo que tienen que posponer la grabación durante unas semanas más."
**"Loveless" Mixing Disaster and Alan McGee's Heroics** : The final mixing of "Loveless" took place at Church Studios, the nineteenth and last studio involved in its production. A 1970s mixing machine malfunctioned, desynchronizing the entire album. This unexpected issue extended the mixing process by a couple of weeks. In desperation, Alan McGee took the tapes home, locked himself away for 13 days, and mixed the album from memory. "Michel se llevó las cintas a su casa, se encerró durante 13 días y mezcló el disco de memoria."
**Cost of "Loveless" and its Impact on Creation Records** : Melody Maker estimated the cost of "Loveless" to be over £150,000 at the time, although all involved questioned this figure. Kevin Shields claimed it was much lower, while Alan McGee suggested it was a few thousand pounds more. Regardless of the exact amount, the album undeniably pushed Creation Records to the brink of bankruptcy, ultimately leading to their acquisition by Sony months later. "Melody Mayker calculó que el coste de el Album más se envió a de 150 mil libras de la época."
**Reception of "Loveless" by Contemporary Critics** : Upon its release, "Loveless" received widespread acclaim from specialized critics, who recognized it as a unique, era-defining album. Brian Eno praised it as a new standard for pop music, while Robert Smith of The Cure declared it superior to all contemporary music, including his own, and ranked it among his top three albums ever. Billy Corgan of The Smashing Pumpkins found it rare for guitar music to achieve something so new, noting that bands in the scene constantly pondered how My Bloody Valentine achieved its sound. "Con lo general, recibió la mantas por parte de la crítica especializada, que les coincidían que era un disco único y que va a marcar una época."
**Post-"Loveless" Struggles and Band Dissolution** : After the release of "Loveless," My Bloody Valentine embarked on a promotional tour across the UK. Upon its conclusion, Creation Records chose not to renew their contract. Kevin Shields himself admitted to going "crazy" after the album, experiencing a period where, despite attempts to record a third album, he couldn't finish any songs. The immense pressure of following up such a masterpiece, coupled with a reputation that closed doors at many labels, proved overwhelming. In 1993, Colm Ó Cíosóig and Debbie Googe left the group, and the band officially separated in 1997. "Según palabras del propio Shiles, tras el disco se abolvió el oco, tuvo un periodo de tiempo en el que, por mucho que intentas grabar un tercer álbum, no era capaz de terminar las canciones."

# genre - Shoegaze

## curiosities
**Emergence and Definition** : Shoegaze emerged as a new sound, initially unnamed, during the late 1980s and early 1990s, characterized by dense, enveloping guitars, significant use of delay and reverb. It was influenced by bands like Cocteau Twins (evocative timbre) and Jesus and Mary Chain (pop wall of sound), with A.R. Kane also being a major early influence. The genre earned its name from the press observing guitarists concentrating on their pedalboards during live performances. My Bloody Valentine's early EPs and "Isn't Anything" album were considered the unofficial starting point, and "Loveless" became its defining work. "Pese a que aún no se le conocía como tal, en esa época se hablaba mucho de ese nuevo sonido que se estaba gestando, caracterizado por guitarras densas y envolventes y mucho de ley y reverb, influenciado por bandas como Cocteau Twins y su trimpó bebocador y el muro de popularista de Jesus Admiral Chain."

## curiosities
**Founding and Vision (1983)** : Creation Records was a British independent label founded in 1983 by Alan McGee, Dick Green, and Joe Foster. Their core vision was to provide a platform for new bands with risky sounds that deviated from commercial standards, aiming to foster experimental and non-mainstream music. "Fue un sello independiente británico fundado en 1983 por Alan McGee, Dick Dean y Joe Foster, cuya visión era darca vida a nuevas bandas con sonidos arriesgados y alejados de el estándar comercial."
**Key Role in Indie Music and Artist Development** : Creation Records played a crucial role in the independent music scene, launching the careers of influential bands such as Jesus and Mary Chain, Primal Scream, Ride, and Slowdive. By 1988, it was a recognized and respected label within the underground music community. "Juega una discográfica clave para entender las cenain di. Lanzando la carrera de bandas como Jesus and Mary Chain, Reimel Scream, Wright o Slowdive."
**Financial Crisis Caused by "Loveless"** : The exorbitant and protracted production of My Bloody Valentine's "Loveless" album brought Creation Records to the verge of bankruptcy. The album's estimated cost, over £150,000 according to Melody Maker, drained the label's funds, leading to a financial crisis that required drastic measures. "Lo que es cierto es que el disco llevó al borde de la quiebra Creation Records, y en eso, para no desaparecer, fueron comprados por Sony traslornos meses después."
**Subdirector's Nervous Breakdown During "Loveless" Production** : During the chaotic and financially draining production of "Loveless," Dick Green, the subdirector of Creation Records, suffered a nervous breakdown so severe that his hair turned white overnight, illustrating the immense stress and despair within the label's offices. "Mientras tanto, las oficinas de Creation, De Crisidil, su subdirector, sufrió una crisis nerviosa, tan intensa que se le pone el pelo blanco de la noche a la mañana."
**Acquisition by Sony** : To avoid complete collapse due to the financial strain imposed by "Loveless," Creation Records was acquired by Sony months after the album's release. This acquisition injected much-needed funds, allowing the label to continue investing in new, emerging bands. "Y toda esta audiencia valió la pena, pues depende de que me preguntes claro, para mí, y para todos los que amamos el disco, la respuesta es un rotundo sí."
**Post-Acquisition Success with Oasis** : Following its acquisition by Sony, Creation Records received a financial injection that enabled it to continue backing new talent. This allowed them to sign the then-unknown Oasis, who subsequently spearheaded the Britpop movement. Oasis's second album, "(What's the Story) Morning Glory?", became the best-selling British album of the decade, marking a significant success for the label under Sony. "Sony les dio una injección de dinero para seguir apostando por mandas noveles. Ficharon a unos desconocidísimos o asis, quienes se convirtieron en los abandonados de este nuevo genero que iba a arrasar en los años 90. El Britpop, eso segundo álbum, Watch the Story, Modern Glory, se convirtió en el disco británico más vendido de la década."

# instrument - Tremolo Arm

## curiosities
**Kevin Shields' "Glide" Technique** : Kevin Shields developed a unique guitar technique he dubbed "Glide," named after My Bloody Valentine's "Glider" EP. This technique involves constantly holding the tremolo arm while strumming the strings, which generates a subtle fluctuation in tuning, producing an enveloping, ethereal effect characteristic of the band's sound. "Charles desarrolló una técnica a la que llamó Glide, como su EP Glider, que consiste en sostener constantemente el brazo de tremolo mientras toca, generando una leve fluctuación de la afinación al rasgar las cuerdas, creando ese efecto en duelante."

**Difficulty of Surviving a Masterpiece** : The immense pressure of creating and then having to live up to or surpass an unrepeatable masterpiece can be creatively and mentally destructive for the artists involved. This challenge was exemplified by My Bloody Valentine after "Loveless," Slint after "Spiderland," and Brian Wilson after "Pet Sounds." "Es muy difícil sobrevivir a una obra maestra."
