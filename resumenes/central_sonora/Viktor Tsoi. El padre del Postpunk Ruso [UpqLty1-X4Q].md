## curiosities
**Birth and Early Life (1962-1979)** : Victor Tsoy was born in 1962 in Leningrad. His mother was a physical education teacher, and his father was a Russian-Korean engineer with ancestry tracing back to present-day North Korea. He studied at art school but quickly lost interest, being expelled in 1979 at the age of 17 for low performance. In the Soviet Union of that era, stopping studies meant having to work, as unemployment could lead to arrest and imprisonment for "social parasitism." "Victor Tsoy nació en 1962 en el engrado. Su madre era profesora de educación física y su padre, un ingeniero ruso coreano cuya sentencia se remota al actual corear del norte."
**Physical Appearance and Bruce Lee Influence** : Tsoy possessed peculiar Asian features that distinguished him from the majority of Soviet society. He greatly admired Bruce Lee and began to emulate his image and practice martial arts, skills he would later showcase in films. "Físicamente, Tsoy tenía unos rasgos asiáticos muy pecoleares que le hacían esta carta de la mojenia sociedad soviética. Admiraba a Bruce Lee y empezó a imitar su imagen y a pertecar artes marciales."
**Avoiding Military Service (1983)** : In 1983, Victor Tsoy was recruited by the Soviet army, as the USSR was embroiled in conflicts in Afghanistan and needed soldiers. To avoid going to war, he feigned a mental illness. He spent a month and a half institutionalized, receiving treatment he did not need, but which ultimately led to him being declared unfit for the army. Those close to him noted that something changed in him during this period, and he was never the same again. "En 1983 fue reculotado por el ejército En esa época, LaURS estaban plenoconflictos de Afganistán, necesitaba soldados."
**Kamchatka Boiler Room - Artistic Refuge** : While his music began to circulate, Victor Tsoy worked in a boiler room of a residential block, where he did night shifts but also had a lot of free time. He named this place "Kamchatka," and it eventually became a significant refuge for Russian bohemians. Many musicians worked there, and it has since been converted into a museum dedicated to Tsoy. "Mientras son músicas en vez de circular, Victor trabajaba en una sala de calderas de un bloque residencial, donde hacía las rosturnos, pero también tenía mucho tiempo libre."
**Cameo in "Asa" (1987)** : Victor Tsoy had a brief but very powerful cameo in Sergei Solovyov's cult film "Asa" in 1987, portraying himself. In a memorable scene, as a restaurant manager reads a list of rules he must follow before performing, Tsoy rises mid-sentence, removes his coat, and takes to the stage, singing "Khochu Peremen!" "Soy tomo un breve pero muy poderoso cameo, interpretándose a sí mismo."
**Acting Role in "Igla" (1988)** : In 1988, Victor Tsoy demonstrated his martial arts abilities in the cult film "Igla" (The Needle). In this movie, Tsoy's character confronts a corrupt world, fighting against drug dealers, drug addiction, and the challenging times they lived in. By this point, he was already a star. "Años más tarde, en 1968, puedo demostrar sus habilidades en la película de Culto Igla. En esta película, Victor Tsoy se enfrenta un mundo corrupto, luchando contra los escapos de la droga, la misma droga adicción y los tiempos que han tocado vivir."
**Stardom Cemented by "Igla" (1988)** : The premiere of the film "Igla" in 1988 definitively cemented Victor Tsoy's status as a star, solidifying his reputation as the greatest rock star Russia has ever had. "El otroito de ese año fue el estreno de la mencionada película Iglá, que le acabó convertiendo definitivamente en una estrella."
**Final Retreat and Demos (Summer 1990)** : After playing to tens of thousands at the Luzhniki Stadium, Victor Tsoy felt the need to disappear for a time. He retreated to a quiet country house in Tonya to spend part of the summer, where he fished and prepared demos (voice and guitar tracks) for Kino's next album. It was here that he was recording just before his death. "Después de tocar el cielo, soy necesitada desaparecer un tiempo, así que se retiró a una tranquila casa de campón de Tonya para pasar parte del verano."
**Death (August 15, 1990)** : On August 15, 1990, at 12:28 PM, on the Slocatalsim road in Latvia (then part of the USSR), Victor Tsoy's car crossed into the opposing lane while taking a curve and collided head-on with a bus. The musician died instantly. An autopsy revealed no traces of drugs or alcohol, and the official conclusion was that he simply fell asleep at the wheel due to exhaustion. "15 de agosto de 1990, 12 y 28 del medio día. En la carretera de Slocatalsim, en Letonia, por entonces a un parte de Laurs, un más Covid-2141 o invadé al carri-contario al tomar una curva y choca frontalmente contra un autobús."
**Age at Death** : Victor Tsoy died at the age of 28. "Víctor Tsoy murió los 28 años."
**Post-Mortem Impact and Enduring Myth** : The news of Victor Tsoy's death opened national news and had such a profound impact on Soviet society that there were reportedly "dozens of registrations of young people who had taken their own lives" across the country, though this is likely an exaggeration. Shortly after his death, a Moscow street wall began to be covered with photos of Tsoy and lyrics from his songs. Today, this wall continues to accumulate tributes, and every August 15th, people gather to sing his songs and write "Tsoy está vivo" (Tsoy is alive), illustrating the enduring reach of his figure. "El hígado, en muchos sitios, que al conocer la historia de su muerte, decenas de jóvenes se quitaron la vida. Todo pareciindicar que es una exageración."

## albums
**45 (1982) - Debut Album** : Kino's debut album, recorded in 1982 with the assistance of musicians from the band Aquarium. The title, "45," referred to the 45-minute duration of the tape. Lacking a dedicated drummer, the band utilized an electronic drum machine, which, combined with acoustic guitars, contributed to a curious and primitive sound. The lyrics were a novel element for Soviet society, filled with contained poetry that elevated everyday life to the heroic, offering a "radiography of their time" by singing about existential emptiness rather than direct political statements. "El nombre era porque la cinta duraba con 25 minutos."
**46 (Unofficial Album)** : This album followed "45" and was distributed unofficially. Despite its informal release, many fans consider it the band's second work, indicating its importance within Kino's discography even without official backing. "45 vino una semana en el año que fue el año para su siguiente trabajo, pero se distribuyó uno oficialmente y muchos fans lo consideran el segundo trabajo de la banda."
**Gruppa Krovi (Blood Group) (1988) - Breakthrough Album** : Released in 1988, this album marked the peak of Kino's popularity. It was massively distributed across the entire USSR through "magnetizdat," a system of pirated cassette copies made by fans for each other in their homes. This grassroots distribution allowed Kino to become the country's biggest band despite lacking official support. The album was recorded concurrently with the filming of Sergei Solovyov's movie "Asa." "El disco se movió masivamente por toda la urs a través de la magnetistad, copias piratas en caset que los fans grababan de unos a otros en sus casas."
**Chyorny Albom (The Black Album) (1991) - Posthumous Release** : Following Victor Tsoy's death, police recovered a miraculously intact cassette from the car wreck. This cassette contained the voice and guitar tracks that Tsoy and guitarist Yuri Kasparyan had recorded in Tonya. Tsoy's bandmates composed the remaining music around these foundational tracks, releasing a posthumous album with a completely black cover, which subsequently became known as "The Black Album." "Sus compañeros de banda, composieron la música sobre el alrededor, y lanzaron un disco post-tumor que no la portaba completamente negra."

## songs
**Khochu Peremen! (I Want Changes) (circa 1987)** : An iconic Kino song whose lyrics explored themes of existential void rather than direct politics. It gained immense popularity when it was featured in the final scene of the cult film "Asa" in 1987. Subsequently, the masses appropriated the song, transforming it into a political anthem during the Perestroika era, as it resonated with calls for changes and reforms. Victor Tsoy, however, consistently rejected this political label for his lyrics. "Cuando la canción son en la escena final de la película de Culto Asa de 1987, las masas apropiaron de ella y la convirtieron en un digno político en plena perestroica, viviendo cambios y reformas."
**Gruppa Krovi (1988)** : This song, the single from the album of the same name, became widely recognized and was later notably used in the video game Grand Theft Auto 4. Its inclusion in the game accompanied the narrative of an Eastern European immigrant arriving in Liberty City, pursuing the American dream only to find a corrupt and heartless urban environment. "el single de Grupa Crovy, que es un tema, si usó para el videojuego Grandes Fauto 4."

## curiosities
**Founding (1981)** : Victor Tsoy and guitarist Alex S. Rybin founded their first band, Garin i Giperboloidy, in June 1981, before eventually changing its name to Kino. "Punto, en vez de a uno de 1961, fundaron su propia banda, Gary Nighy pervolo Di."
**Name Change (1982)** : The band Garin i Giperboloidy changed its name to Kino in 1982, establishing the identity under which they would become renowned. "Y ya, en 1982, se cambiaron el nombre áquino."
**Internal Conflict and Lineup Change (Post-45)** : Following their debut album, Kino experienced a challenging period marked by creative differences between Victor Tsoy and Alex S. Rybin. Their clashing ideas regarding the group's direction and leadership ultimately led to Rybin abandoning Kino, leaving Tsoy to continue the band alone. "Soy Ribin Chocaban y tenía ni ideas contraria sobre el rumbo y el liderazgo del grupo, por lo que Ribin acabó abandonando Quinoa."
**Musical Evolution and Influences** : For their subsequent albums, Kino expanded their lineup by recruiting more musicians and increasingly incorporated electronic instruments into their sound. Musically, they were considered close to bands like The Cure and Joy Division, and Victor Tsoy particularly admired The Smiths. Their distinctive dark and danceable sound was notably filtered by the brutalist architecture, coldness, and crudeness of Leningrad, creating a unique sonic identity that has since been embraced by the new wave of post-punk in Eastern Europe. "Posicalmente, que no estaba cerca de bandas como de Kyu, Royo y División, una de las bandas que más amigaba Victor Toy eran desmits."
**Rebirth on the Internet** : Decades after their initial success, Kino has experienced a significant rebirth on the internet. Hundreds of their songs have been translated into Spanish and numerous other languages by young people who, despite the time difference, feel a deep connection with the band's music, demonstrating its timeless appeal. "seguramente sea por eso que Quinoa ha vivido un renacimiento en internet. De escenas de canciones suyas han sido traducidas al español y muchos otros idiomas por jóvenes que deca las despues, sin tener una profunda conexión con la banda."

# genre - Rock

## curiosities
**Underground Movement in Late 70s USSR** : In the late 1970s, rock music in the Soviet Union was primarily an underground movement, largely limited to the city of Leningrad. It contrasted sharply with the government-backed and more controllable "Pop Moscovita" and "Música Folclórica" that dominated the official charts. "En la uso de finales de los años 70, el robo que era un movimiento muy underground. Y estaba limitado a una zona, leningrado."

# concert - Leningrad Rock Club

## curiosities
**Creation and Control (Early 1980s)** : In the early 1980s, the Soviet regime undertook an unusual experiment of openness by permitting the creation of the legendary Leningrad Rock Club. This venue allowed rock concerts to take place, alongside "seminars on aesthetic and rhythmic aspects of the genre." Despite being controlled by the KGB and the Communist Party, it fostered unprecedented creative freedom within the USSR. "Sin embargo, en principios de los años 80, el régimen hizo un extraño experimento de apertura y permitió la creación del mítico club de rock del engrado, un local donde se podían hacer conciertos de rock a parte de las seminarias odestética y rímpica del genero."
**Catalyst for Russian Rock Revolution** : The Leningrad Rock Club played a pivotal role in fostering the Russian rock revolution. It provided a space where bands like Aquarium, Alisa, and DDT could perform and develop, despite the tight control exerted by the KGB and the Communist Party, allowing for a creative surge. "Sin embargo, allí se vivió una libertad creativa sin precedentes en la URSS y ayudó a conducir la revolución del rock ruso de mandas como aquario, alisa o DDT."

# curiosity

**Social Parasitism in the USSR** : In the Soviet Union of the late 1970s, failing to continue one's studies meant being required to work. If an individual did not hold a state-recognized job, they could be arrested and sentenced to prison for the crime of "social parasitism," illustrating the strict governmental control over individual lives and employment. "En la unil soviética de esa época, dejar de estudiar significaba tener que trabajar, ya que si no tenías un empleo reconocido por el Estado, podía ser arrastado y condenado prisión por el delito de parasitismo social."
**KGB and Communist Party Control of Rock** : Even with the creation of the Leningrad Rock Club, the Soviet authorities maintained strict control. The club, a hub for emerging rock music, was directly controlled by the KGB and the Communist Party, demonstrating the pervasive surveillance and ideological oversight of artistic expression during that era. "El club estaba controlado por la KGB y el partido comunista."
**Perestroika's Political Context** : Perestroika was a period of political and economic reforms in the Soviet Union during the late 1980s. This era of living changes and reforms provided a potent backdrop for songs like Kino's "Khochu Peremen!" to be adopted by the masses as political anthems, even if the artist intended no such message. "las masas apropiaron de ella y la convirtieron en un digno político en plena perestroica, viviendo cambios y reformas."
**Magnetizdat - Underground Music Distribution** : "Magnetizdat" was an informal system of disseminating music in the Soviet Union through pirated cassette copies. Fans would record tapes from one another in their homes, allowing bands like Kino to achieve massive popularity across the USSR despite lacking official support or proper distribution channels. "El disco se movió masivamente por toda la urs a través de la magnetistad, copias piratas en caset que los fans grababan de unos a otros en sus casas."
**"Asa" (Film by Sergei Solovyov)** : "Asa" is a cult film from 1987 by Sergei Solovyov, notable for its connection to the Soviet rock scene. The final scene featured Kino's song "Khochu Peremen!", which contributed significantly to the song's widespread popularity and its adoption as a political anthem during Perestroika. "Cuando la canción son en la escena final de la película de Culto Asa de 1987, las masas apropiaron de ella y la convirtieron en un digno político en plena perestroica, viviendo cambios y reformas."
**Grand Theft Auto 4 - Cultural Impact** : The video game Grand Theft Auto 4 used Kino's song "Gruppa Krovi" as part of its soundtrack. This inclusion brought Soviet rock music to a global audience within the context of the game's narrative about an Eastern European immigrant navigating a corrupt American city. "el single de Grupa Crovy, que es un tema, si usó para el videojuego Grandes Fauto 4, de un inmigrante de Europa del Este llega a Liberticity, persiguiendo el sueño americano que le ha prometido a su primo, pero lo que encuentra es una ciudad corrupta y desalmada."
**Collapse of the Soviet Union (1991)** : The Soviet Union officially collapsed in 1991, just one year after the death of Victor Tsoy. This historical event marked the end of an era that had profoundly shaped Tsoy's life and the context of Kino's music. "Un año después, en 1991, la Unión Soviética con la exón definitivamente."
