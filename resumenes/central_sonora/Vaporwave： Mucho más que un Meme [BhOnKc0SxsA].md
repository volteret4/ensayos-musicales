## albums
**Chuck Person's Eccojams Vol. 1 (2010)** : Daniel Lopatin, operating under the alias Chuck Person, released this collection of his earlier works in 2010. The album built upon his initial YouTube experiments, further developing the Eccojams style by presenting popular song extracts reduced in tempo and pitch, and assembled in loops. This release was foundational for the early vaporwave scene. "Al año siguiente, el mismo Lopatín publicó una colección de esos trabajos a los cañadio otros, bajolálias de Chuck Person en Chuck Person Seco-Jumps volumen 1."

## curiosities
**First Vaporwave Recordings (2009)** : In 2009, Daniel Lopatin, then known by his YouTube handle SunsetCorp and later also as Oneohtrix Point Never, uploaded the earliest recordings of what would become vaporwave to his channel. These videos featured extracts of popular songs, slowed down, pitch-shifted, and looped, accompanied by unsettling and nostalgic images related to technology. "Las primeras grabaciones del género las podemos escuchar en el año 2009, cuando los veros de YouTube, San SetCorp, quien más tarde se descubrió que se trataba de Daniel Dopatín, más conocido por su proyecto, One-Etricks, Ponnevar, subió los vídeos en su canal."
**Pivotal Figure in Vaporwave's Origins** : Daniel Lopatin is considered, alongside James Ferraro and Vektroid, one of the most important individuals in the development of vaporwave, having been responsible for some of its foundational musical experiments and releases. "Sin duda, junto a Ferraro y Lopatín, la persona más importante dentro del vaporwave."

## curiosities
**First Internet-Born Music Genre** : Vaporwave holds the distinction of being the first musical genre to be entirely conceived and developed on the internet, demonstrating a new paradigm for the emergence of music scenes beyond physical clubs and streets. "Fue el primer género musical en la serie y desarrollarse en internet, demostrando que las escenas podían surgir más allá de los clubs y las calles."
**Virtual Counterculture and Resilient Genre** : Vaporwave has evolved into a virtual counterculture that resists easy categorization, and notably, it has "died" and "resurrected" multiple times, continually taking on new forms through its subgenres. "Un género que se ha convertido en una especie de contragultura virtual, que se reúsa a ser encasillado y que ha muerto y resucitado varias veces, tomando nuevas formas en subgeneros como el malls off, el dream-pang y el slash-wave."
**Core Musical Principles** : The musical foundation of vaporwave is based on sampling extracts of pop, funk, new age, or city pop songs from the 1980s and 1990s, which are then slowed down, looped, and heavily drenched in reverb to create a hypnotic atmosphere. "El no musical, la base del género, se basa en el sample de extractos de canciones de pop, funk, new age, o city pop de los años 80s y 90s, reducidos y pegados en Lop. A los cuales se le aplica un pesado river, lo cual genera un aire innotico."
**Distinctive Visual Aesthetics (A E S T H E T I C S)** : Vaporwave adopts a unique visual aesthetic rooted in digital nostalgia and internet culture, incorporating elements such as low-resolution graphics, glitch art, Greco-Roman iconography, pastel and neon colors, and design styles from 1990s websites. This aesthetic, known as "A E S T H E T I C S," aims to evoke a sense of fascination with an idealized digital past where consumerism, technology, and pop culture merge into an evocative and unsettling visual collage. "Esta estética, conocida como a estética, busca evocar una sensación de fascinación por un pasado digital y idealizado."
**Declared Dead in 2013, Leading to Subgenre Explosion** : In 2013, merely four years after its inception, the vaporwave community itself declared the genre "dead" due to the saturation and repetitiveness of its visual tropes (sculptures, pastel colors, old operating system windows). However, this "death" ironically marked the beginning of its branching into numerous subgenres. "En 2013, con apenas cuatro años de vida, declararon al vaporwave muerto."
**Enduring Importance Beyond Meme Status** : Despite often being perceived as just a meme, vaporwave has proven to be profoundly significant, altering how music scenes emerge and spread (moving from clubs to the internet), expanding its boundaries through diverse subgenres, offering a critique of consumerism and digital alienation, forging an unprecedented link between music and visuals, and fostering a DIY spirit in the digital realm. "Hoy en día el vaporwave, aunque sigue restrando esa imagen de Meme, ha demostrado ser mucho más importante de los que muchos creyeron."

## curiosities
**Vaporwave's Muzak with Nostalgia (Originated 2012)** : Mallsoft emerged in 2012 as a subgenre akin to vaporwave's version of Muzak, but imbued with a strong nostalgic element. It uses background music, such as easy listening, smooth jazz, and Muzak, typically associated with shopping malls. "El mallsoft es como el muzac del Bapur Wave, pero con un fuerte componente nostálgico. El surgió en 2012"
**Recreating Commercial Atmospheres** : Through extensive reverb and editing, Mallsoft meticulously recreates the ambiance of large commercial spaces, often integrating field recordings from these locations. This technique intensifies its dreamlike environment and evokes a sense of capitalist nostalgia, specifically recalling the consumerism prevalent in the 1980s and 1990s. "el mallsoft recrea la almohsfera de grandes espacios comerciales, a menudo incluyendo grabaciones de campo de estos lugares para intensificar su ambiente unírico y la nostálgia capitalista, evocando el consumismo de los años 80's y 90's."
**Darker Tone and Liminal Spaces** : In recent years, Mallsoft has evolved to adopt a darker tone, suggesting the solitude of empty shopping malls. This shift has contributed to the popularization of concepts like liminal spaces and backrooms within the genre's aesthetic. "En los últimos años el género ha ido adoptando un tono más oscuro, sugiriendo la soledad de los centros comerciales vacíos, deminó la popularización de los espacios liminales y los back rooms."

# genre - Dreampunk

## genres

Ambient

## curiosities
**Dreamlike Sound and Cinematic Influence (Originated 2014)** : Dreampunk emerged around 2014, characterized by its dreamlike sound and influences from ambient music and progressive electronic. Unlike traditional vaporwave, it avoids the typical sampling and looping techniques of Plunderphonics, focusing instead on abstract soundscapes. "El Dreampunk surgió alrededor de 2014 y se caracteriza por su sonido onírico, influencias de la música ambiental y la electrónica progresiva."
**Urban, Cinematic, and Cyberpunk Inspired** : This subgenre is described as much more cinematic and urban than Slashwave, integrating elements of Cyberpunk and exploring the atmospheres of futuristic metropolises. It draws significant inspiration from iconic films such as *Blade Runner* and *Ghost in the Shell*. "Es mucho más cinematográfico y urbano, integrando elementos de Cyberpunk y explorando atmósferas de metropolis futuristas, tomando inspiración de películas como Blirran Erigos y De Shell."
**Popular Vaporwave Subgenre** : Dreampunk has successfully consolidated its position as one of the most popular subgenres within the broader vaporwave movement. "Este subgeniero se ha consolidado como uno de los más populares dentro del vaporwave."

# genre - Slashwave

## genres
Vaporwave

## curiosities
**Echoey, Atmospheric, and Emotive Sound** : Slashwave is characterized by its echoey and atmospheric sound, achieved through manipulated samples and generous application of effects like reverb, delay, and especially phaser. Unlike other vaporwave branches focused on nostalgia and consumerism, Slashwave evokes themes of love, melancholy, and dreams, making it the most romantic and emotional subgenre. "El Slash Wives se caracteriza por su sonido de encho y atmosférico, logrado a través de la manipulación de Samples y el uso generoso de efectos como el River, el delay y especialmente el Faser."
**Shift Towards Original Compositions** : While primarily using samples from Asian genres like Japanese and Chinese pop, Slashwave has recently experienced a surge in popularity by beginning to incorporate original compositions without samples, similar to Utopian Virtual, thereby gaining increased presence online. "el género experimentado en pico de popularidad recientemente gracias a empezar a incluir composiciones originales sin Samples. Ganando cada vez más presencia en la red, gracias artistas como Desert Sandfield's Warman Night."
**Popularized in 2013** : The genre gained significant popularity in 2013, notably aided by artists such as Telepath. "Se popularizó en 2013 gracias a Telepath."

# genre - Eccojams

## genres
Vaporwave
Chop 'n' Screw
Plunderphonics

## curiosities
**First Form of Vaporwave** : Eccojams is identified as the earliest form of vaporwave, characterized by its use of simple, repetitive loops typically centered on lyrical fragments of songs, creating an echo and reverberation-laden collage. "El Eco Yams fue la primera forma del vaporwave. Se caracteriza por el uso de lup simples y repetitivos, generalmente centrados en fragmentos líricos de canciones, que crean un colach cargado de eco y reverberación."
**Inspired by Audio Manipulation Techniques** : The genre draws inspiration from techniques like Chop 'n' Screw and Plunderphonics, involving the slowing down and fragmentation of tracks to introduce a glitch-like effect. "El Eco Yams toma inspiración de técnicas como el Chopeta N' Escriway o el Plonder Phonics, ralentizando y fragmentando las pistas para darle un toque glitch."
**Raw and Chaotic Precursor** : Eccojams is considered the raw and chaotic precursor to vaporwave, having emerged directly from *Chuck Person's Eccojams Vol. 1* and further developed by artists such as MediaFired and Bodycode. "Es el precursor crudo y caótico del vaporwave, surgido a raíz del citado álbum Jack Persons Eco Yams volumen 1"

# genre - Utopian Virtual

## genres
Vaporwave
Electronic
Muzak

## curiosities
**Sophisticated, Corporate, and Technological Vaporwave (Originated Mid-2010s)** : Utopian Virtual emerged in the mid-2010s as a more sophisticated, corporate, and technological iteration of vaporwave, focusing on the aesthetics and sounds of 1980s and 1990s commercial electronic music, particularly corporate Muzak. "El Utopía Invirtual es una forma más sofisticada, corporativa y tecnológica del vaporwave. Emejió mediados de 2010, centrado en la estética y el sonido de la música electrónica comercial de los años 80 y 90, especialmente al muza corporativo."
**MIDI-Centric and Evocative of Obsolete Tech** : The style is characterized by the use of monophonic synthesizers and, notably, MIDI sounds, which evoke the "kitschy" aspects of that technological and obsolete era—the time of CD-ROMs, encyclopedias, Hi-Fi equipment, and early 3D artistic renders. "El estilo se caracteriza por el uso de sintetizadores monofónicos y especialmente sonidos MIDI que evoca el locúrsi de esa era tecnológica y a obsoleta."
**Emulation Over Sampling** : Unlike many other vaporwave subgenres, Utopian Virtual emulates these sounds rather than using original samples, creating an unsettling sensation by blending the human and the artificial. "A diferencia de los otros superstilos, lutopía virtual emula esos sonidos en lugar de usar samples originales, generando una sensación de vayan quietante al mezclar lo humano y lo artificial."
**Named After Ferraro's Album** : The genre's name is derived from James Ferraro's seminal album *Far Side Virtual*. "El nombre deriva del álbum Fyre's Eye Virtual de Ferraro."
**Recent Peak in Popularity** : The genre has experienced a recent peak in popularity, thanks to artists like Crypto Volans and Highliner. "El genero está teniendo los últimos años un pico de popularidad gracias a artistas tan geniales como Cripto Volans o Highliner."

**Vaporwave with Trap Rhythm** : Vapor Trap does not significantly differ from pure Vaporwave in its artistic or sonic aspects, except for the inclusion of the characteristic rhythm of trap music. "La influencia de la cibercultura y de la tecnología de los 90, sigue siendo un aspecto importante del género como podemos ver en muchas de sus portadas."
**Divergence and Footwork Influence** : As the decade progressed, Vapor Trap began to distance itself from pure Vaporwave, particularly as artists (such as the implied Vaperror, from "va a perro" in transcript) incorporated influences from other genres like Footwork. "A medida que avanzaba la década, se fue distanciando del Bapur Wave, ya que artistas como va a perro, incorporaron influencias de otros géneros, como el footwear."
**Popular Album: BlackBank-C-0 by Black Bank C** : The album *BlackBank-C-0* by Black Bank C is highlighted as likely the most popular release in the Vapor Trap genre. "Su edico más popular seguramente sea BlackBank-C-0, de BlackBank-C."

# instrument - Samplers

## curiosities
**Foundational Tool for Vaporwave** : Samplers are fundamental to the core musical basis of vaporwave, used to extract and manipulate fragments of songs from 80s and 90s pop, funk, new age, or city pop. "la base del género, se basa en el sample de extractos de canciones de pop, funk, new age, o city pop de los años 80s y 90s, reducidos y pegados en Lop."
**Less Used in Far Side Virtual** : James Ferraro's album *Far Side Virtual* distinguished itself by employing fewer samplers compared to other early vaporwave works. "Este álbum usá menos samplers"
**Essential to Future Funk and Slashwave** : Future Funk is a sample-based genre, and Slashwave relies on the manipulation of samples for its sound. "En realidad, nos masco una forma de house, con mucha influencia del French House, basado en Samples."
**Avoided by Dreampunk** : Dreampunk deliberately avoids the typical sampling techniques associated with Plunderphonics, focusing instead on abstract soundscapes. "A diferencia del vaporwave, evita las técnicas de Sampleo y Lupéo típicas del Plonder Phonics"

# instrument - Reverb

## curiosities
**Core Effect in Vaporwave's Hypnotic Air** : Heavy reverb is a defining characteristic applied to sampled extracts in vaporwave, generating a distinctive hypnotic atmosphere. "A los cuales se le aplica un pesado river, lo cual genera un aire innotico."
**Central to Eccojams and Mallsoft** : Eccojams creates echo and reverberation-laden collages, making reverb a central element. Mallsoft employs "mucho reverb" to recreate the atmosphere of large commercial spaces. "crean un colach cargado de eco y reverberación."
**Frequent in Future Funk and Generous in Slashwave** : Reverb is described as frequent in Future Funk and is generously used as an effect in Slashwave. "los reveros son frecuentes"

# instrument - Pitch/Tempo reduction

## curiosities
**Fundamental for Early Vaporwave** : A key technique in early vaporwave, as demonstrated by SunsetCorp (Daniel Lopatin), involved reducing the tempo and pitch of popular song extracts. "reducidos de tempo y pitch y pegados en bucle"
**Eccojams Technique** : This technique is also fundamental to Eccojams, which involves slowing down and fragmenting tracks to give them a glitch effect, drawing inspiration from Chop 'n' Screw and Plunderphonics. "ralentizando y fragmentando las pistas para darle un toque glitch."
**Used in Broken Transmission** : Slowdowns (ralentizaciones) are explicitly mentioned as a manipulation technique in Broken Transmission to create fragmented and ethereal soundscapes. "A través de bucles, ralentizaciones y distorsiones"

# curiosity

**Internet History and Evolution** : Internet began in the 1960s as ARPANET, a military and academic project designed to connect computers and facilitate information exchange. By the 1980s, technology expanded commercially, and in the 1990s, the arrival of the World Wide Web transformed it into a new, free world where forums, chats, and browsers fostered the first digital communities. However, this initial freedom eventually became regulated, leading to an evolution toward a centralized ecosystem dominated by social media, streaming platforms, e-commerce, algorithms, and artificial intelligence in subsequent decades. "Internet comenzó en los años 60 con ARPANET, un proyecto militar y académico, creado para conectar ordenadores y facilitar el intercambio de información."
**The Rise of Digital Nostalgia** : As the internet evolved towards a centralized and commercial ecosystem, users who experienced its initial, freer stages began to feel a curious sensation: a nostalgia for the visual and sonic elements of that early era of the network and technology. This "digital nostalgia" longed not only for a past time but also for a specific aesthetic and a sense of discovery and freedom that vaporwave ultimately encapsulated and reinterpreted. "Una nostalgia digital que no solo añora una época, sino también una estética y una sensación de descubrimiento y libertad."
**Subgenres Prioritizing Visuals Over Sound** : Some vaporwave subgenres developed their visual imaginary before their sonic identity, often resulting in them not progressing beyond meme status. Examples like Simpsons Wave and Fudge Wave contributed to a superficial public perception of the genre, indicating a disconnect between visual concept and musical depth. "en varios casos se creó antes el imaginario visual que el sonoro y muchos no pasaron del meme"
**Vaporwave as "The Punk of the Digital Age"** : Given its transformative impact on how music scenes emerge and spread (shifting from physical spaces to the internet), its role in expanding musical boundaries through diverse subgenres, its critique of consumerism and digital alienation, its unique connection between music and visuals, and its fostering of a DIY spirit, it is reasonable to consider vaporwave, in many senses, "the punk of the digital age." "no creo que se ha descabellado decir que el vaporwave es, en muchos sentidos, el páncano de los traer a."
