# artist - John Carpenter

## curiosities
**Budgetary Innovation (1974)** : As a young film student, Carpenter faced budget limitations for his first film, *Dark Star*, which forced him to use an unconventional tool: a synthesizer. This decision not only saved his film but also inadvertently defined the sound of 1980s horror cinema, and by extension, contemporary horror. "la solución este problema, aparte de salvar a su película, va a definir el sonido del cine de terror de los años 80 y, por extensión, el actual."
**Signature Sound Forged** : After his initial experiment with *Dark Star*, Carpenter truly forged his signature style in *Assault on Precinct 13*. This style, characterized by synthetic and dark minimalism, would become a hallmark of his entire body of work and be widely imitated by almost all videoclippish horror cinema of the 1980s and many current works. "donde Carpenter forjó su firma, un minimalismo sintético y oscuro que caracterizará toda su obra."

## films
**Dark Star (1974)** : Carpenter's first film as a student, featuring a fictional alien audience and an alien that was literally a painted beach ball, ran into budget issues for music, leading to the use of a synthesizer. "su primera película, una audiencia de ficción de un alien que es literalmente una pelota de playa pintada, causa el caos."
**Assault on Precinct 13 (1976)** : This film was where Carpenter solidified his distinct synthetic and dark minimalist style, which later became characteristic of his work and widely influential. "fue en asalto a la comisaría del distrito 13, donde Carpenter forjó su firma, un minimalismo sintético y oscuro que caracterizará toda su obra."
**Halloween (1978)** : Carpenter composed one of the most iconic soundtracks in history for this film, not just within horror cinema but in universal cinema, demonstrating how simplicity could translate into absolute terror. "En Halloween, Carpenter compuso una de las bandas sonoras, más icónicas de la historia, ya no solo del cine de terror, sino del cine universal."

## songs
**Halloween Main Theme (1978)** : Created by John Carpenter, this theme was as terrifying as the film's antagonist, Michael Myers, itself. "John Carpenter creó un tema tan aterrador como el propio Michael Myers."

# genre - Horror Synth

## curiosities
**Genre Naming**: "Horror Synth" was the chosen name to encompass both the pioneering artists and the new wave of musicians creating dark, synthetic sounds, recognizing a unified aesthetic and passion. "Poner un nombre a ese sonido que agrupara tanto a los pioneros como a los nuevos músicos y el nombre elegido, fue Horror Synth."
**Diverse Influences**: Horror Synth is not a closed genre; its new musicians draw from a variety of scenes, including extreme metal, house, progressive rock, and even Dungeon Synth, all united by a profound passion for 1980s horror cinema. "estos nuevos músicos no vienen de una escena concreta, sino de varias, del metal extremo, del house, pero todo le suele la misma religión, una pasión sagrada por el cine de terror de los 80."
**Modern Application**: This sound, which originated from a budgetary necessity, now serves as the atmospheric backdrop for highly successful blockbuster films like *It Follows*, *The Substance* (as per transcript), and phenomena series such as *Stranger Things*, establishing itself as a distinct language beyond a mere revival. "este sonido que nació como una necesidad acaba ambientando películas tan daquilleras y exitosas como It's Follows, The Substance, o series que se han convertido en un fenómeno, como Stranger Things."

# instrument - Sintetizador

## curiosities
**Early Film Adoption (1974)** : Initially dismissed as an unconventional and unserious tool in cinema, a young film student (John Carpenter) first used a synthesizer in 1974 to score his debut film, which was suffering from budget constraints for orchestral music. "una máquina llena de cable y potenció metros que casi nadie en el cine se toma en serio, un sintetizador."
**Ideal for Horror**: Synthesizers became the perfect instrument for horror films due to their unparalleled versatility to create virtually any sound, including those inherently unpleasant or unsettling; deep tones effectively built tension, while piercing high notes evoked terror and simulated anxiety. "por ser un recurso relativamente barato, innovador y realmente funcional, que se convirtió en el instrumento ideal para inventar esas películas de terror."
**1990s Decline**: During the 1990s, the rise of more polished, adult, and psychologically realistic horror films, alongside the emergence of new computer technologies like samplers and MIDI libraries, led to synthesizers being seen as excessive and outdated, effectively burying their widespread use in film scoring. "Así que el cine de terror de los 80 y los sintetizadores pasaron a ser vistos como algo excesivo y cosa de frequís."
**2000s Revival**: Towards the late 2000s, particularly ignited by the 2011 film *Drive*, synthesizers made a significant comeback, becoming a central element in the revival of 80s aesthetics, stylized violence, and neon lighting in popular culture. "En 2011, una película trajo de vuelta a las luces de neon, la violencia estilizada, y sobre todo, los sintetizadores."

# curiosity

**Halloween Financial Success (1978)** : The film *Halloween*, produced with a modest budget of $300,000, achieved an impressive gross of $47 million solely in the United States, instantly making its formula a highly sought-after model for replication. "La película, que contó con un presupuesto de 300 mil dólares, logró recaudar 47 millones de dólares tan solo en Estados Unidos."
**VHS Impact on B-Horror (1980s)** : The introduction of the VHS format in the 1980s dramatically increased the number of B-grade horror productions, providing a new distribution channel and market that was receptive to innovative, synthesizer-driven soundtracks. "Al entrar los años 80, llegó un nuevo formato que disparó el número de producciones de terror de Siria B, el VHS."
**Italian Horror Cinema (1970s-1980s)** : During the 1970s and 1980s, Italy became a prolific factory of film genres, including Poliziottesco, supernatural terror, cannibal movies, zombie movies, and especially Giallo, distinctively recycling and exaggerating international trends with remarkable creativity, largely owing its cult status to its unique music. "Durante los 70 y 80, el país se convirtió en una auténtica fábrica de géneros, Polichotesco, Teroza Oren Natural, Canibal Moobies, películas de Zombies y especialmente el Jalo."
**Italian Musicianship (1970s-1980s)** : Italy boasted an exceptional array of musicians, many with backgrounds in progressive rock, experimental music, or academia, who found synthesizers to be fertile ground for sonic experimentation and revolutionizing horror scores. "Italia tenía una plenidad de músicos excepcional, muchos venían del roco por desivo, del día experimental o de la música académica."
**American vs. Italian Horror Sound (1970s-1980s)** : American horror, exemplified by John Carpenter, tended to be cold and cerebral, often using synthesizers to create the sound of the killer, whereas Italian horror, through artists like Goblin, was hot and visceral, employing synths to embody the victim's madness. "Mientras Carpenter usaba los sintetizadores para crear el sonido del asesino, Goblin los usaba para crear el sonido de la locura de la víctima. El terror americano era frío y cerebral, el italiano era caliente y visceral."
**Decline of Synth Horror (1990s)** : The 1990s saw a shift in public demand for more polished, adult, and psychologically realistic horror, like *The Silence of the Lambs*, leading to the overexposure of B and Z-grade films and the perception of synthesizer music as excessive and "freaky," ultimately leading to its decline in mainstream horror. "La sobre exposición de obras de serie V o Zeta acabó quemando al público, quienes exigían producciones más pulidas y adultas."
**Synth Revival (late 2000s - 2010s)** : The "monster" of synth horror resurfaced in the late 2000s, with the 2011 film *Drive* notably bringing back neon lights, stylized violence, and synthesizers, transforming the once-considered "tacky" 80s aesthetic into "the coolest thing in the world" and igniting the Synthwave trend. "a finales de la vez cada de los 2000, el monstruo asomó la mano. En 2011, una película trajo de vuelta a las luces de neon, la violencia estilizada, y sobre todo, los sintetizadores."
**Early Retro-Valorization**: The cultural groundwork for the 80s revival had been laid for several years before the explicit explosion with *Drive*, with earlier works like *Grand Theft Auto: Vice City* (as interpreted from "grande fauto, baísito") contributing to this burgeoning retro appreciation. "se lleva a va generando un caldo de cultivo de este revés valo unos años. Por ejemplo, grande fauto, baísito, insalió de años antes de dry."
**VHS Aesthetic Revival**: In conjunction with the synth sound revival, the VHS aesthetic also experienced a resurgence, with an increasing number of works embracing and reinterpreting its visual style. "Cada vez más obras empezaron a reivindicar esa estética WHS."
**Pioneers as Demigods**: Within the context of the synth revival, original pioneers like John Carpenter and Goblin were elevated to an almost "demigod" status, serving as major inspirations for new artists in the genre. "Inspirados por Carpenter y Goblin, quienes habían sido elevados casi a la categoría de semidioses dentro de este rival."
**Independent Synth Artists**: Inspired by the original masters, various artists began to create and publish their own dark, synthetic "imaginary soundtracks" on platforms such as Bandcamp and SoundCloud, giving rise to musicians like Umberto, Perturbator, Carpenter Brut, and GosT, who further explored and evolved the sound. "Musicos como un verto, perturbator, Carpenter Brut, Ghost y muchos más, llevan otros terrenos ese sonido."
