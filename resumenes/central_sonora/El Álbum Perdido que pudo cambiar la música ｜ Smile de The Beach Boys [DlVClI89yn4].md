## albums
**Pet Sounds (1966)** : This album, released in 1966, was described as a masterpiece that elevated Brian Wilson to the stature of a genius, carving an exciting new path for music alongside The Beatles' Rubber Soul. Before its release, The Beach Boys were known for adolescent hits about surfing, Californian girls, and cars. Inspired by Rubber Soul, Wilson created a deeply introspective and stimulating album, introducing orchestral arrangements and unusual sounds that challenged the pop and rock conventions of the time, with lyrics exploring profound themes like love, loss, and the search for meaning. "En 1966 vio la Luz Petzhounds de los Beach Boys, una obra maestra que lebo a Bryan Wilson a la altura de Gienio y Cabrío, junto con Raver Soul de los Beatles, un nuevo exitante camino para la música."
**Smile (1966-1967) - Unreleased Project** : Brian Wilson conceived this project as an attempt to record the "best album in history," aiming to define the decade and expand music to new levels before The Beatles could. Following the success of Pet Sounds and "Good Vibrations," The Beach Boys reached an agreement with Capital Records for a release on January 15, 1967. However, after 10 months of intense work, the band was forced to cancel the album in May 1967, due to Brian Wilson's deep depressions, mental disorders, growing tensions within the band, and his increasing paranoia. Over 150 hours of recordings were archived. "Esta es la historia de Smile, el disco perdido que pudo cambiar la historia de la música."
**Smiley Smile (1967)** : After the cancellation of the original Smile project, the rest of the band adapted some of the recorded songs into a more pop format, resulting in this album. It was a much less ambitious record, closer to their earlier style before Pet Sounds. "Tras la cancelación, el resto de la banda adaptó algunas de las canciones en un formato más pop y grabado en Smiley Smile, un álbum mucho menos ambicioso y más cercano a lo que venían haciendo antes de Pet Sounds."
**Wild Honey (1967)** : Released in July 1967, this album went almost unnoticed, especially when compared to The Beatles' monumental Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band. The lukewarm reception led to The Beach Boys being perceived as a conservative band, and Brian Wilson significantly reduced his compositional role, retreating further into reclusion. "Por el contrario, el siguiente disco de los Beach Boys, Wild Honey, pasó casi desapercibido y la banda comenzó a ser vista como conservadora."
**Smile (2011) - Official Archival Release** : In 2011, an official edition of Smile was finally released, compiling the recordings made by the band during those 10 months of intense work in the 1960s. This release allowed the public to experience the original studio work from the legendary unreleased album. "Esto ha permitido que en 2011, se lanzara al fin un edición oficial de Smiley con las grabaciones que hizo la banda durante esos 10 meses de los años 60."

## songs
**Good Vibrations (1966)** : Intended as the perfect pop song and the first preview of Smile, its recording incurred a record cost of $50,000, the highest for a single track at the time. It was meticulously recorded in five different studios with up to 26 musicians, involving Wilson creating 28 variations of the verse and 37 of the chorus before selecting the best takes for the final version. The single, released on October 10, 1966, was a huge success, reaching number 1 in both the United States and England, and selling 400,000 copies in just four days. It was described as something "new and exciting," a psychedelic and hip-hop song in equal parts. "Good Vibrations se grabó en cinco estudios diferentes con la participación de hasta 26 músicos, la complejidad del proceso fue inmensa."
**FIRE (1966)** : This song was part of the Smile project. During its recording, Brian Wilson reportedly dressed all the musicians in children's firefighter helmets while filling the studio with smoke. On the same day, a building adjacent to the studio caught fire. Wilson believed his music was cursed and that "FIRE" was the cause of the fire, leading him to discard the song from the album. "Bryan creyó que su música estaba en brujada y que esa canción era la causante del incendio, por lo que decidió descartarla."

## curiosities
**Initial Hype for Smile**: Capitol Records actively promoted Smile, even hiring Derek Taylor, former press chief for The Beatles, to market Brian Wilson as a musical genius. Taylor suggested to the press that Smile would not only be a masterpiece but possibly the best album ever recorded. Capitol predicted over a million copies would sell by January 1967 and even printed more than 400,000 album covers. "Capito le empezó a promocionar el disco en prensa, llegando a baticinar que en enero venderían más de un millón de copias."
**Doubt and Changes during Smile Production**: During the final months of 1966, while the band was on a promotional tour in Europe, the Smile project became uncontrolled. Brian Wilson began to doubt the album, making incessant changes and corrections that endlessly prolonged recordings. He would change recording studios if a place didn't give him "good vibes" and canceled sessions with assembled musicians, incurring significant costs for Capitol. In his pursuit of precise sound, he sometimes made studio musicians play the same chord for hours. "Mulsaron empezado a dudar del disco y no paraba de hacer cambios y correcciones que eternizaban las grabaciones."
**Tensions within the Band during Smile Production**: Growing tension between Mike Love and other members of the band, and Brian Wilson's close circle led by Van Dyke Parks, exacerbated the difficult situation surrounding the Smile project. This internal conflict contributed significantly to the album's eventual cancellation. "La tensión creciente entre Mike Love, otros miembros de la banda y el círculo cercano a Wilson encabezado por Bandic, exacerbó la situación."
**Post-Cancellation Decline**: After the cancellation of Smile and the release of the largely unnoticed Wild Honey, The Beach Boys began to be seen as a conservative band. Brian Wilson significantly reduced his compositional role and increasingly retired to the reclusion of his home, contrasting sharply with The Beatles' growing success. "Brian Wilson redujo significativamente su rol compositivo y se retiró cada vez más a la reclusión de su hogar. Mientras tanto, la leyenda de Smiley comenzó a crecer."
**Warner Bros Agreement and Fine**: After breaking their agreement with Capitol Records, the band signed with Warner Bros., who imposed a clause requiring them to publish Smile before May 1973. Failing to meet this condition, they had to pay a fine of $50,000, which further added to the mystique surrounding Wilson's genius and the legend of the lost album. "No lograron cumplir con esta condición y tuvieron que apagar una multa de 50 mil dólares."
**Pirated Smile Recordings (1990s)** : In the 1990s, bootleg recordings featuring various songs from the Smile sessions began circulating in the pirate market. Smile had become the most prized album for collectors, the "Holy Grail of music," with each pirated copy offering only hypotheses about how Wilson had conceived the album. "Durante los años 90 empezaron a correr por el mercado pirata, Bollex con varias de las canciones de esas grabaciones."

## curiosities
**Genius and Maestro Status**: Following the release of Pet Sounds, Brian Wilson was elevated to the stature of a musical genius and maestro, acknowledged for his pivotal role in creating new and exciting paths for music. "ha elevado a Bryan Wilson, la cabeza detrás del álbum, a la altura de Gienio de la música."
**Panic Attacks and Stage Fright**: In late 1964, Brian Wilson suffered an extremely intense and distressing panic attack during a flight. This incident led him to stop touring and playing live with The Beach Boys due to stage fright, allowing him to concentrate entirely on compositional tasks. "Wilson sufrió un ataque de panico durante un vuelo, un evento que fue para él extremadamente intenso y angustioso."
**Childhood Trauma and Mental Health**: Wilson faced profound personal challenges stemming from pressure and mistreatment by his father during childhood, which left deep roots affecting his mental well-being. These issues, combined with immense pressure to surpass the successes of Pet Sounds and "Good Vibrations," were exacerbated by his abuse of hallucinogenic substances, likely contributing to his mental problems. "La presión y los maltratos que sufrió de niño por parte de su padre le dejaron raíces profundas que afectaron a su bienestar mental."
**Creative Obsession and Perfectionism**: Wilson was consumed by the desire to be the best in composition, arrangement, and recording, aiming to surpass Paul McCartney in composition, Phil Spector in production, and compete with the finest arrangers of his era. His meticulous process, evident in "Good Vibrations," was a preview of his aspirations for Smile. "Wilson se encargaba de la composición, arreglo, sigrabación y querías ser el mejor en todos ellos."
**"Teen Symphony to God" (Smile Description)** : Brian Wilson described the Smile album he was recording as a "teen symphony to God," reflecting his ambitious and spiritual vision for the project. "Describió el disco que estaban grabando como una esinfoñadolescente a Dios."
**Paranoia during Smile Production**: As the Smile project unraveled, Brian Wilson became increasingly paranoid. He feared that The Beatles might steal his songs and even believed he was the target of a conspiracy led by Phil Spector. "Bryan estaba cada vez más paranoico, temía que los Beatles pudieran robarle las canciones, incluso llegó a pensar que era el blanco de una conspiración encabezada por Fille Spector."
**Impact of "Strawberry Fields Forever"**: The release of The Beatles' single "Strawberry Fields Forever" in February 1967 is often cited as a breaking point for Brian Wilson and the Smile project. He reportedly felt that The Beatles had achieved what he had so desperately yearned for. "Otro punto que se comenta que fue el desencadenante del fíntil disco es el lanzamiento del single Strawberry Hills Forever de los Beatles, en febrero de 1967, al sentir que ellos habían logrado lo que él tanto ansiaba."
**Retreat and Reclusion**: Following the cancellation of Smile and the subsequent perceived decline of The Beach Boys, Brian Wilson significantly reduced his compositional role and increasingly withdrew into the reclusion of his home, marking a period of deep personal and professional retreat. "Brian Wilson redujo significativamente su rol compositivo y se retiró cada vez más a la reclusión de su hogar."
**Recognition of Sgt. Pepper's Supremacy (2014)** : When asked in 2014 if Smile, had it been published, would have "beaten" The Beatles, Brian Wilson candidly replied that Sgt. Pepper's had "kicked their ass," acknowledging The Beatles' immense impact. "el Sargent Papers les había apateado el culo."

# curiosity

**Album Era (1967)** : The release of The Beatles' Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band in July 1967 marked a pivotal moment in music history, initiating the "album era," where the album gained prominence as a cohesive artistic statement. "Marcando el inicio de la era del álbum."
**Summer of Love (1967)** : The release of The Beatles' Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band in July 1967 played a significant role in catalyzing the "Summer of Love" of 1967, a major cultural phenomenon. "catalizando el verano de l amor de 1967."
**"Lonely Genius" Pattern in Music History**: The collapse of Smile established a recurring pattern in music where artists like Brian Wilson, Syd Barrett of Pink Floyd, and Kevin Shields of My Bloody Valentine are portrayed as solitary geniuses whose creativity seemingly thrives in studio isolation. This romanticized image, however, often conceals a darker burden: the intense pressure to innovate can exacerbate personal struggles and mental health issues, making isolation not just a choice but often a consequence of the relentless pursuit of artistic perfection. "El colapso de Smiley es un patron recurrente en la música, el cual vamos a ver durante los siguientes años en otras bandas."
**Derek Taylor's PR Campaign for Brian Wilson**: Derek Taylor, formerly The Beatles' press chief, was hired by Capitol Records to promote Brian Wilson as a musical genius and build immense anticipation for the Smile album. Taylor reportedly suggested to the press that Smile would not only be a masterpiece but potentially the greatest album ever recorded. "Capital inicialmente respaldó a Wilson, incluso contra toda direct Taylor, ex jefe de prensa de los Beatles para promover a Bryan Wilson como un genio musical."
**Melody Maker Interview (Brian Wilson)** : In an interview with Melody Maker, Brian Wilson affirmed that Smile would be an improvement over Pet Sounds, showcasing his confidence in the ambitious new project. "En una entrevista con Melio de Maker, Bryan Wilson afirmo que Smile sería una mejora de Pet Sounds."
**Hit Parade Magazine (Dennis Wilson Quote)** : Dennis Wilson was quoted in Hit Parade magazine stating that the album Brian Wilson was recording (Smile) was so exceptional that it made Pet Sounds "stink," underscoring the high expectations surrounding the new work. "Denis Wilson llegó a decir en Hit Parade que el disco que estaba grabando era tan bueno que hacía que Pet Sounds apestaba."
