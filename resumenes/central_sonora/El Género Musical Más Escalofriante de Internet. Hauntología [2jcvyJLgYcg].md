# artist - The Caretaker

## member of
Kirby Kear (alias)

## albums
**Selected Memories for the Haunted Ballroom (1999) - Debut Album** : This was The Caretaker's first album, inspired by the ballroom scene from Stanley Kubrick's "El Resplandor" (The Shining). It reinterprets old, ghostly melodies from a ballroom where everyone has died, and only a photograph remains as a memory. The album is an early example of hauntology, aiming to evoke a nostalgia for something the listener has never truly experienced. "Selected Memories for the Haunted Ballroom, un disco que reinterpreta cómo sonaban esas viejas melodías fantasmales de ese salón, donde ya todo el mundo ha muerto y tan solo queda una foto como recuerdo."

**Everywhere at the End of Time (2016-2019) - Dementia Simulation Series** : This project, released under the alias Kirby Kear, is a series of six albums. Across these discs, the artist collects blurry old orchestral songs and progressively deteriorates them through layers of noise, echoes, and other effects, simulating the effects of dementia. While the melodies on the first disc are still recognizable despite being blurry, by the sixth and final album, melody is completely absent, as everything has become blurred, symbolizing the patient's approach to death. "En ellos recogen unas viejas canciones orquestales antiguas borrosas que paulatinamente, disco a disco, se van deteriorando, mediante capas de ruido, ecos y otros efectos para simular los efectos de la demencia."

## curiosities
**Alter Ego's Metaphorical Death** : Following the completion of the *Everywhere at the End of Time* series, Leland Kirby metaphorically "killed" his most well-known alter ego, The Caretaker. This act coincided with a significant surge in popularity for The Caretaker's music, particularly online, leading to numerous analyses, reflections, and sensationalist videos on YouTube. "Tras eso, Leland mató a su más conocido alter ego de Kirteiker."

**TikTok Challenge Phenomenon** : The *Everywhere at the End of Time* series spawned a viral trend on TikTok known as the "Kirteiker Challenge." This challenge involved users listening to all the albums in the series consecutively and posting their reactions. As a result, the artist's work inadvertently adopted a creepypasta aesthetic, with his music frequently used to accompany short videos on platforms like TikTok featuring liminal aesthetics, dreamcore, weirdcore, and analog horror, which introduced his work to a broad young audience. "En TikTok suyo al Kirteiker Challenge, que consistían escucharlosis, discos del tirón y postear tu reacción."

# artist - Boards of Canada

## genres
Hauntology
Electronic

## albums
**Music Has the Right to Children (1998) - Hauntology Masterpiece** : Released in 1998, this album presented a stark contrast to the dominant electronic music of the 1990s, which was characterized by clean, digital production and rhythmic prominence. Boards of Canada instead utilized slightly detuned analog synthesizers, sampled old television jingles, and recordings of children playing, all processed with a "patina of dust and time." The album's sonic aesthetic evokes a sense of nostalgia for a pre-internet world and an eternal summer. Its cover art, an old, blurred photograph of a family seemingly on vacation with their faces erased, intentionally looks backward to a distorted, ghostly past. It is considered a masterpiece of hauntology, even before the term became widely popularized. "Sintetizadores analógicos ligeramente desafinados, viejas sintonías de televisión sampleadas, voces de niños jugando, todo ello procesado con una pátina de polvo y tiempo."

**Geogaddi (2002) - Most 'Photological' Work** : Released in 2002, this album is described as the band's "most photological work," (which can be understood in the context of hauntology as evoking ghostly images and distorted memories). It is characterized as almost a "ghost album," an artifact from an era that never arrived, viewed through a kaleidoscopic prism. The album is highlighted as a key influence for artists on Ghost Box Records. "Geogadi es casi un disco fantasma, un artefacto de una era que nunca llegó."

# genre - Hauntology

## curiosities
**Definition by Theorists** : Hauntology is a concept that defines "ghostly music" capable of evoking nostalgia for something one has never truly experienced. Its musical characteristics often include slightly detuned analog synthesizers, sampled old television jingles, and recordings of children playing, all processed to sound aged and dusty. Artists achieve these "ghostly" or "haunted" sounds by using layers of noise to simulate old vinyl or cassette recordings, along with reverbs, echoes, and distortion. The instrumental palette commonly incorporates low-quality samples from cassettes or old recorders, field recordings, old sound libraries, and analog synthesizers. "un concepto que definió una música fantasmal que logra bocar una nostalgia por algo que realmente nunca viviste."

**Mark Fisher's Philosophical Definition** : According to writer and theorist Mark Fisher, hauntology is "the perception that we live in a present invaded by remnants of the past and by futures that never arrived." These echoes are not manifested as gentle nostalgia but as unsettling presences and marks of the unfinished. Fisher observed a constant repetition of images and sounds in culture (music, cinema, art) that once promised a better future but now survive as mere echoes, forming "the aesthetic of lost futures." "Según el escritor y teloso formarckfisher, la jontología es la percepción de que vivimos en un presente invadido por restos del pasado y por futuros que nunca llegaron."

**Cultural Collapse of Creativity** : Mark Fisher theorized that around the turn of the millennium, there was a "collapse of musical and cultural creativity." While previous eras looked towards the future, suddenly most musical media began reformulating the past. The promise of a better tomorrow lost its strength and hope, prompting artists to look back and delve into the remnants of the past. "Para la fisher, el onces, se marcó una especie de colapso de la creatividad musical y cultural."

**Movement for Mysterious Nostalgia** : Both Mark Fisher, in his blog K-Punk, and music writer Simon Reynolds framed hauntology within music as a movement designed to induce a mysterious nostalgia. This movement aims to create sounds that evoke a sense of the past that is both familiar and alien, often through lo-fi sampling and sonic degradation. "Tanto Fisher, en su blog, K-Punk, como el escritor y escrito musical Simon Reynolds, tiene un forma la jontología dentro de la música como un movimiento que hemos que conducir una nostalgia misteriosa."

**Origin of the Term in Music** : The term "hauntology" was first applied to music in 2006, specifically in reference to the record label Ghost Box Records. This label perfectly encapsulated the core ideas of hauntology through its aesthetic and sound, making the term "fit like a glove." "La primera vez que sus son la música Gwendo 2006 para recibirse al siguió discográfico Gwosbox y en realidad, liba como nillo al dedo, ya que este se lló en capsula como nadie esa idea."

**Expansion and Application on the Internet** : The concept of hauntology expanded freely on the internet, being applied to various genres. These include Vaporwave, which evokes nostalgia for the 1980s; Witch House, with its spectral aesthetic born from the fringes of the web; and even Dungeon Synth, which originally sought to recreate medieval fantasy atmospheres but gained a new internet interpretation, going beyond merely imitating a sound to evoke non-existent times and worlds. "El concepto de hauptología se expandió por internet y empezó a usarse de forma más libre, aplicado a géneros como el vapor wavycis en hostarcia por los años 80, el witch house, con sostéticas espectral nacida en las margenes de la red, o incluso el dungeon synth."

# label - Ghost Box Records

## curiosities
**Founding and Aesthetic Mission (2004)** : Ghost Box Records was founded in 2004 and quickly established itself as an aesthetic and sonic laboratory. The label built a mythology centered around library music, public service jingles, educational documentaries, and television iconography from past decades, particularly drawing influence from the BBC Radiophonic Workshop. Its artists embraced the feeling of failed futures and transformed it into a fictional sound archive. "Fundado en 2004, Gwosbox actúa como un laboratorio estético y sonoro, construyó una mitología basada en la música de biblioteca, las intonías de servicio público, los documentales educativos y la conografía televisiva de décadas pasadas, especialmente del taller de fónico de la BBC."

**Embodiment of Hauntology** : Ghost Box Records is credited with perfectly encapsulating the idea of hauntology. The term "hauntology" was first explicitly used in a musical context in 2006 to refer to this record label, highlighting its foundational role in popularizing the concept within music. "y en realidad, liba como nillo al dedo, ya que este se lló en capsula como nadie esa idea."

**Influence of Boards of Canada** : The artists on Ghost Box Records drew significant inspiration from the works of Boards of Canada, especially their album *Geogaddi*, in developing their distinctive sound and aesthetic. "Además de la tallierra de fónico de la BBC, los artistas de Gwosbox también se basaron las trabajos de Wors off Canadá, especialmente en Geogadi."

**Ballroom Scene Inspiration** : The text opens with a vivid description of people enjoying themselves in an elegant ballroom on September 1, 1939, with an orchestra playing cheerful melodies, couples dancing, and an overall atmosphere of a perfect night. This vivid, yet potentially non-existent memory, is stated to be inspired by the ballroom scene from "El Resplandor" (The Shining), serving as an initial illustration of hauntological themes and the evocation of a past that may never have been. "Uno de septiembre de 1939, la gente se está divirtiendo en una legante sala de baile, las mesas están decoradas con manteles y el techo reprito de intrincadas lámparas de araña."

**Mark Fisher - Theorist and Writer** : Mark Fisher, a renowned writer and theorist, is a pivotal figure in the development and popularization of the concept of hauntology. He defined it as "the perception that we live in a present invaded by remnants of the past and by futures that never arrived," viewing cultural expressions like music, film, and art as repetitions of images and sounds that once promised a better future but now exist as echoes, forming an "aesthetic of lost futures." His blog, K-Punk, was also a platform for discussing hauntology. "Según el escritor y teloso formarckfisher, la jontología es la percepción de que vivimos en un presente invadido por restos del pasado y por futuros que nunca llegaron."

**Simon Reynolds - Music Critic and Writer** : Simon Reynolds is a prominent music critic and writer who, alongside Mark Fisher, framed hauntology within music as a movement intended to evoke a mysterious nostalgia. His work contributed significantly to the understanding and discussion of this musical aesthetic and its cultural implications. "el escritor y escrito musical Simon Reynolds, tiene un forma la jontología dentro de la música como un movimiento que hemos que conducir una nostalgia misteriosa."

**BBC Radiophonic Workshop - Influential Entity** : The BBC Radiophonic Workshop is identified as a significant source of inspiration for artists associated with Ghost Box Records. The Workshop's innovative use of sound and its contributions to past television iconography heavily influenced the mythology and sound archive created by the label, which focused on evoking failed futures. "especialmente del taller de fónico de la BBC."

**Liminal Spaces - Aesthetic Concept** : Liminality refers to the state of inhabiting a threshold or being in transit between one place and another, exemplified by spaces like airports, hotels, or waiting rooms which are designed for passage, not permanence. These places become unsettling when found empty or in a penumbra because they appear simultaneously familiar and strange. When these images are combined with the music of artists like Aphex Twin, Boards of Canada, or The Caretaker, the sensation of being trapped in a memory that was never real is intensely multiplied. "La liminalidad significa evitar un umbral, estar en tránsito entre un lugar y otro."

**Dreamcore - Aesthetic Concept** : Dreamcore is an aesthetic trend that deforms nostalgia, creating an unsettling feeling. Music from hauntology artists is used to accompany short videos featuring this aesthetic on platforms like TikTok, contributing to the sense of uncanny familiarity. "destéticas liminales, y escenas de drink or, word core y terror analógico."

**Weirdcore - Aesthetic Concept** : Weirdcore is an aesthetic trend that deforms nostalgia, creating an unsettling feeling. It is often combined with other internet aesthetics to evoke a sense of unease and distorted memory. "el weircord."

**Backrooms - Aesthetic Concept** : The Backrooms is an aesthetic concept identified alongside liminal spaces, Dreamcore, and Weirdcore, contributing to internet trends that distort nostalgia into something unsettling. It creates a sense of familiar yet disorienting spaces. "o sobre todo la estética de los espacios liminales y los macrooms."

**Internet as a Non-Place (No Lugar)** : The internet, particularly cyberspace, is described as being, in essence, a "non-place" (no lugar). This characteristic has facilitated the development and expansion of hauntology within its framework. In an era of uncertain future, marked by AI reformulations, reinterpretations of past eras, and recycled nostalgia, time itself blurs within this digital "non-place," filling digital memory with constantly pursuing specters. "Hoy la jantología se ha delvido en ese marquise internet, porque el ciber espacio es, en esencia, un no lugar."
