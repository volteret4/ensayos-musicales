# artist - Sophie
## genres

## albums
**Oil of Every Pearl's Un-Insides (Year not specified)** : This album propelled hyperpop to an entirely new level, revolutionizing it forever. It is a deconstructed club music album that alternates between almost industrial moments and deeply emotional landscapes. Loaded with glitches and avant-garde electronic textures, the album balances the experimental with the accessible, remaining profoundly pop. In this work, Sophie explores themes such as identity, gender dysphoria, and acceptance, making it a key work for a new generation of artists. "Sophie llevó el hyperpop a un nivel completamente nuevo, revolucionándolo para siempre."
## curiosities
**Pioneering Pop Production**: Before this album, glitches and industrial production were not common in pop, but Sophie changed the rules of the game. "Antes de este álbum, los glitches y la producción industrial no eran algo común en el pop, pero la artista cambió las reglas del juego."
**Tragic Passing (2021)** : Sophie passed away in 2021 due to a tragic accident, but her legacy continues to grow each year. "Por desgracia, Sophie nos dejó en 2021 otra sontragico accidente, pero su legado sigue creciendo cada año."
**Modern Electronic Classic**: This album has, without a doubt, become a modern classic of electronic music. "Este discazo se ha convertido, sin ningún tipo de duda, en un clásico moderno de la música electrónica."

## albums
**1000 Gecs (2019) - Debut Album** : This debut album is described as a "sonic collapse" that draws influences from trap, EDM, dubstep, J-Pop, and even monophonic mobile phone melodies. All of this is passed through an autotune filter and enveloped in an absolutely colorful and "chiclosas" (chewing gum-like) aesthetic. Far from being just a joke, the album resonates surprisingly seriously, encapsulating the essence of hyperpop as a genre where anything goes, and its impact has been enormous. "Un colasso honoro que recoge influencias del trap, el EDM, el DAFSTEP, el J-POP y hasta melodías monofónicas de teléfonas móviles."
## curiosities

# artist - Dorian Electra
## genres
Hyperpop
Pop
## albums
**Flamboyant (Year not specified)** : With this album, Dorian Electra establishes themselves as a key creative figure in hyperpop. This album invites exploration of gender, masculinity, and queer identity, blending humor and provocation. Musically, "Flamboyant" is the album that strays least from the margins of hyperpop, being very close to the early "chiclosas" (chewing gum-like) and 2000s productions of PC Music. The album is an explosion of baroque synthesizers, glitchy beats, and arrangements that leap from melodic to chaotic in seconds. It is arguably the most pop and accessible album on this list, full of adolescent hits, and had a considerable impact on the less experimental and more theatrical side of the genre. "Este álbum te atraa a la exploración como el género, la masculinidad y la identidad queer, mezclando humor y provocación."
## curiosities

# genre - Hyperpop
## curiosities
**Generational Sound of Gen Z**: Hyperpop is a genre that encapsulates the immediacy, constant stimuli, and "internet-infused" adolescent nostalgia so characteristic of Generation Z. "Si hay un genero que encapsula la inmediatez, nos estímulos constantes y la nostalgia interneterada lescente tan propios de la generación Z. SES el hyperpop."
**Origins and Popularization**: It emerged at the end of the last decade, particularly around the fascinating PC Music label, founded by producer A. G. Cook, and popularized by artists like Sophie and Charli XCX. "Suscido a finales de la década pasada alrededor del interesantísimo sello PC Music, fundado por el productor Aikikuk y popularizado por artistas homo-sofí, o Charlie XX."
**Blurring Pop Boundaries**: This movement has blurred the boundaries of traditional pop, drawing it into an experimental, chaotic, and deeply emotional territory. "Este movimiento ha desdibujado en los límites del pop tradicional, llamándola a un terreno experimental, caótico y profundamente motivo."
**Accelerated and Experimental Characteristics**: It is an accelerated and experimental pop subgenre with auto-tuned vocals, simple melodies, many rhythm changes, and an exaggerated bass inherited from Bavarian Bass. "Es un sub-género del pop acelerado y experimental. Con voces cargadas de autotune, melodía simples, muchos cambios de ritmo y un bajo exagerado heredado del Bavarian Base."
**Designed for Digital Attention Spans**: This genre is designed to capture the attention of an audience accustomed to endless scrolling, where every beat, glitch, and rhythm change is another stimulus in the organized chaos of the digital era. "Es un genero diseñado para captar la atención de una diencia acostumbrada al escuro del infinito, donde cada beat, glitch y cambio de ritmo es un estímulo más en el caos organizado de la era digital."
**Nostalgic and Distorted Influences**: It is laden with a particular nostalgia for genres that defined this generation's adolescence, including Euro Trance, Happy Hardcore, emo music, video games, and even Dance Metal. Hyperpop takes these intense sounds, distorts and exaggerates them, passing them through an internet filter, transforming them into something deeply connected with a culture that blends nostalgia, irony, the queer movement, and excess as a lifestyle. "Todo ello cargado de una nostalgia particular de los géneros que definieron la adolescencia de esta generación. Euro Trends, Happy Hardcore, música emo, videojuegos y hasta Dance Metal."
**Evolution and Commercialization Impact**: This movement has evolved from its experimental and niche roots to become a global phenomenon, redefining what pop can be. As the genre has gained recognition and become more commercial, it may have also lost its quality as a genre for "weirdos and misunderstood," leaving behind some of the essence that defined its beginnings. "Tal vez, ahora que el género ha ganado reconocimiento y se ha vuelto más comercial, ha perdido también su calidad de género para raros y incontrendidos, dejando atrás parte de la sencilla que lo definían sus inicios."
