# artist - Aphex Twin

## albums
**Artificial Intelligence (1992)** : Featured as the opening artist on the seminal Warp Records compilation, which played a crucial role in redefining electronic music listening. His presence on this album helped shift the genre from club-focused dance music to a more contemplative, home-listening experience and was instrumental in shaping the emerging IDM sound. "el que abre el disco, Affix Twin."
**Selected Ambient Works 85-92 (1992)** : Released a few months after the Warp compilation on Apollo, the ambient division of the influential Belgian label R&S Records. This album is considered by some to be the fundamental record for EDM, presenting a series of ambient tracks that are more like a soft, bubbly techno, allegedly composed between 1965 and 1992 (with some tracks dating back to his early teenage years). Its release paved new pathways for EDM in subsequent years and served as a direct influence for a second wave of artists. "El disco salió apenas unos meses después de la recopelación de Warp en el Sello Apollo, la división ambiental del super influyente Sello Belga, Ryes y Records."
## curiosities
**Childhood Musical Exploration** : As a child, Richard D. James was known as the "weird kid" at school, drawn to peculiar sounds. Instead of playing the piano conventionally, he preferred to open its lid and experiment with the strings to create his own noises. "en su casa tenía un piano, y él, en vez de tocar una de forma natural, refería abrir la tapa a jugar con las cuerdas."
**Synthesizer Deconstruction** : Dissatisfied with the sounds of 1980s synthesizers, he would frequently dismantle, tinker with, and reassemble them. This hands-on approach allowed him to learn electronics and develop his unique sound, leading to his first musical compositions. "No le gustaban los sonidos de los sintetizadores de los 80, así que los desmontaba, cacharreaba, y los volvía a montar."
**Discovery of Detroit Techno and Chicago House** : His musical path was significantly altered upon discovering Detroit Techno and Chicago House, realizing that other artists were exploring similar sonic territories and validating his experimental approach. "El día que descubrió el Técno de Detroit, y a las hijaos de Chicago le cambió la vida al descubrir que había más gente, explorando caminos similares a los suyos."
**IDM Naming Contribution** : In 1993, a fan mailing list for Warp and Aphex Twin enthusiasts, known as the IBM list, played a crucial role in consolidating the terms "intelligent music" and "EDM." Aphex Twin himself proposed an alternative name for this new genre: "cerebral music." "Affextwing, por ejemplo, propuso llamar la música cerebral."

# genre - Intelligent Dance Music
## curiosities
**Origin of the Name (1993)** : The terms "Intelligent Music" and "EDM" (IDM) became consolidated in 1993 through the IBM list, a dedicated mailing list for fans of Warp Records and Aphex Twin. This occurred after some segments of the press had initially referred to this new sound as "intelligent techno." "hasta que en 1993, a través de una lista de correo de fans de Warp y FX Twin, la IBM list, el término intelligent music y EDM acabó consolidándose."
**Controversial Label** : Since its emergence, the "IDM" label has been contentious, often perceived as pretentious because it implicitly suggests that other forms of electronic music are not intelligent. Consequently, many musicians associated with the scene have openly disavowed this tag. "Desde entonces, la tiqueta ha sido polémica. Claramente suenas no. Es como si sugeriese que el resto de música electrónica no fue inteligente."
**Reflecting a Deeper Intent** : Despite its controversial and pretentious nature, the term "IDM" managed to reflect a genuine sentiment within a segment of the electronic community. This group sought a deeper, more profound layer in techno music beyond its purely functional role for dancing, aiming for a more conscious, attentive, and thoughtful listening experience. "Está claro que es una tiqueta pretenciosa, pero aun así, de alguna manera, lograr reflejar una sensación."
**Evolution in the 1990s** : Throughout the 1990s, IDM continuously mutated. A "second wave" of artists and labels emerged, distinguishing itself from the first wave (which focused more on melody and textures) by placing a greater emphasis on percussion, influenced by the sounds of jungle and drum and bass. "Durante los años 90 lide, me no dejó de imutar, y varios generos de artistas, como la misma Bjork, empezaron a incorporar esas sensibilidades en sus trabajos."
**Contemporary Diffuseness** : Today, the IDM label has become increasingly vague and diffuse as its styles have blended with alternative pop, hip-hop, and other contemporary musical currents. Despite this, many of the original producers continue to release new material, and fresh artists still emerge carrying the spirit of IDM. "Hoy en día, la mayoría de productores que dieron original y de mén siguen publicando discos, todavía se sigue editando material interesante, aunque la tiqueta es cada vez más difusa."

# genre - Tecno
## curiosities
**Detroit Techno as Core Influence** : The more futuristic strains of Detroit Techno, particularly the work of artists like Derry May and Carc Drake, were considered by the founders of Warp Records to be at the core of a new type of music and listening. This provided a fundamental aesthetic and conceptual basis for the emergence of IDM. "Esta gente creía que el Tegno de Detroit más futurista, el de Derry May y o Carc Drake, estaba en el núcleo de un nuevo tipo de música y de escucha."
**Shift from Functional Dance Music** : Before 1992, electronic dance music was almost exclusively experienced in clubs, primarily for dancing. The "Artificial Intelligence" compilation and the subsequent IDM movement fundamentally challenged this paradigm, proposing that techno could be listened to while seated, occupying a cultural space comparable to progressive rock, spiritual jazz, or contemporary classical music. "Hasta ese momento la electrónica de club tenía una función muy clara, a Cerva Ylar."
**"Intelligent Techno" as Precursor to IDM** : Before the term IDM became widely accepted and consolidated, some segments of the music press began referring to this new, more contemplative and intricate form of electronic music as "intelligent techno." "Partida de la prensa, empezó a llamar la inteligen techno."

# genre - Reif
## curiosities
**UK Rave Scene in 1991-1992** : In 1991 and 1992, the rave scene in the United Kingdom transformed from a niche subculture into a widespread mass phenomenon. Illegal parties grew significantly in both size and reach, prompting sensationalist press to publish articles alarming society about the perceived dangers of these events. "El año 1991 y 1992, la escena Reif, del reno unido, había dejado de ser algo mineralitario para convertirse en una moda de masas."
**Dilution of Rave Spirit** : The initial utopian and communal spirit of the rave scene began to dissipate as an increasing number of young people, drawn merely by the party atmosphere, started attending. This influx included dealers and gangs who sought higher BPMs and more aggressive sounds like Break Bichar corei (Breakbeat hardcore), moving away from the minimalist techno of Detroit and the acid house of Chicago. "El Brick Bichar corei era lo que más le mandaba a la gente, y ese espíritu tópico con el que había empezado la escena, empezaba a diluirse."
**"Rave" as a Pejorative Term** : Due to the commercialization of the scene by the music industry, which started mass-producing diluted tracks on white labels, and the changing demographics of attendees, the word "Reif" (rave) began to acquire a pejorative connotation. "Es por eso que la palabra Reif empezó a sonar pejorativa."

# curiosity
**Evolution of Electronic Music Listening (Pre-1992)** : Before 1992, electronic dance music was almost exclusively consumed in clubs and raves, typically in a standing position, under strobe lights, and amidst crowds. Singles were primarily sold on white labels and intended for DJs. Deep, contemplative listening was not the priority, rather it was often a brief interlude between energetic dance sessions. "Antes de 1992, la música electrónica de Vyles escuchaba casi exclusivamente en los club y las reis. De pie, iluminado por luces estroboscópicas, inrodeado de gente."
**"Artificial Intelligence" Compilation Cover Art (1992)** : The cover of Warp Records' 1992 "Artificial Intelligence" compilation featured a provocative, computer-generated 3D image, which, in its primitive style, was considered futuristic at the time. It depicted a metallic humanoid figure, a blend of a mannequin and a cyborg, sprawled on a sofa, smoking, and seemingly immersed in music. Surrounding it on the floor were records by Kraftwerk and Pink Floyd, a beer can, and rolling papers, forming a kind of high-tech altar to the ritual of listening. "Una imagen generada por ordenador en ese 3D primitivo que ya sociamos a la nostalgia, pero que en aquel momento representaba el futuro."
**Shift in Electronic Music Purpose** : The "Artificial Intelligence" compilation introduced a revolutionary concept: techno could be listened to while seated, aspiring to occupy the same cultural space as progressive rock, spiritual jazz, or contemporary classical music. This challenged the prevailing notion of electronic music as solely a functional tool for DJs, asserting its potential for narrative, authorial intention, and profound artistic expression. "El inteligencio cambió ese marco, proponía que el tecno podía escucharse sentado, que podía ocupar el mismo espacio cultural de un disco de rock progresivo, de ya su espiritual, podíamos llegar con Temporania."
**Individual vs. Collective Listening** : This new music, while sharing sonic characteristics with the rave scene (such as 4x4 kicks and acid basslines), was designed to cultivate an internal state and an individual journey. It moved beyond the need for a collective physical ritual, instead fostering a personal mental trance. This sound was perceived as futuristic but with an introverted character, crafted for private listening in rooms. "No menos corporal y más mental. Y ahí estaba la verdadera revolución."
**Enduring Legacy of IDM (30+ Years On)** : More than 30 years after the release of the "Artificial Intelligence" compilation with its metallic mannequin on a sofa, the fundamental act of listening to this type of electronic music remains unchanged. The genre continues to invite listeners to close their eyes and enter a unique, introspective space that bridges the physical body and the mind, evolving beyond its initial "intelligent music" label. "Hoy, más de 30 años después de aquel mannequin metalico tirado en un sofá, el gesto sigue siendo el mismo."
