# artist - Nirvana

## labels
DGC/Geffen Records (implied)

## albums
**In Utero (Year not specified) - Third Album** : Nirvana (specifically Kurt Cobain) chose Steve Albini to produce their third album, *In Utero*, because he had previously produced *Surfer Rosa* by Pixies and *Pod* by The Breeders, two of their favorite records. Albini's specific technical approach, which involved capturing natural room ambiance with strategically placed microphones, had been rejected by previous producers but was essential to Nirvana, leading them to insist on his services. "Corco vain quiso Steve Alvini para producir su tercer álbum minútero."

## curiosities
**Albini's Recording Agreement for In Utero** : After Steve Albini sent Nirvana a four-page letter detailing his minimalist approach, expressing his resistance to record label pressure, and emphasizing his determination to preserve artistic integrity during the recording process, they agreed to record *In Utero* together. This letter outlined Albini's philosophy and conditions for the project. "Alvini tras enviarles una carta de cuatro hojas donde los detalles son foque minimalistas y expresó su resistencia a la prisión de la discográfica y su determinación de preservar la integridad artística en el proceso de grabación acordaron grabar juntos."
**In Utero Recording Location** : Following the agreement, Nirvana recorded *In Utero* at Pachyderm Studios, a secluded studio intentionally chosen to keep the record label at a distance from the recording process. This decision underscored the band's desire for creative freedom and minimal interference during the album's creation. "Tras aceptar los acuerdos grabaron en paquí de arme estudios, un estudio es lado para mantener a la discográfica dejada de la grabación."
**Initial Record Label Reaction to In Utero** : When Nirvana's record label first heard *In Utero* in Urbana, they were initially pleased with the result and even planned to re-record the album with another producer. However, the band's evolving perspective on Albini's mix led to a different outcome. "Cuando escucharon el disco, en Irbaná, nega discográfica quedaron contentos, por lo que planearon volver a grabar con otro productor."
**Nirvana's Evolving Preference for In Utero** : After listening to *In Utero* for a month, the band grew to appreciate the album more, though they had one specific reservation: they desired greater emphasis on the vocals and bass. This slight dissatisfaction led to a request for a remix. "Tras escucharlo durante un mes, el disco les fue gustando más, salvo en un punto. Querían darle más enfasis a la voz y al debajo."
**Albini's Refusal to Remix In Utero** : Nirvana asked Steve Albini to remix *In Utero* to address their concerns about vocal and bass prominence, but he refused their request, stating he was satisfied with the original final result. This refusal led to external changes being made to the album. "Le pedieron alvini que remezclara el álbum, pero este es enegó, ya que estaba contendo con el resultado final."
**In Utero Remastering and Remixes** : During the remastering of *In Utero*, mastering engineer Bob Ludwig (referred to as "Bob Blowwind") increased the volume of the vocals and bass to meet the band's preferences. Additionally, an unnamed producer associated with R.E.M. remixed some of the album's songs, significantly altering Albini's original production. "Durante la remastarización del disco, en el género Bob Blowwind sobriel volumen de las voces y el bajo. Además, el productor de re, mezcó al lit, remezcó algunas canciones."
**In Utero's Detrimental Impact on Albini's Career** : Despite *In Utero* being frequently cited in discussions of Steve Albini's production work, it doesn't sound like his initial production due to subsequent changes. According to Albini, this album ultimately harmed his career rather than helping it, as he struggled to find work for the following year because major record labels became unwilling to collaborate with him. "Este disco más que beneficiarle, según él, le perjudico. Ya que durante el siguiente año le costo encontrar trabajo debido a que las grandes discográficas se negaban a trabajar con esto."
**Rid of Me as a Reference for In Utero** : During the recording of PJ Harvey's *Rid of Me*, Steve Albini was concurrently negotiating with Nirvana for *In Utero*. He sent Nirvana a copy of *Rid of Me* to demonstrate his desired sound for *In Utero*, offering it as a sonic blueprint for their upcoming project. "Es envío una copia de Ritof Meig para mostrarles como quería que son así inútero."
**Albini's Letter to Kurt Cobain** : Steve Albini famously viewed himself as a "plumber" rather than a "guru" like Rick Rubin, emphasizing his technical role and direct, paid-for service. This self-perception was articulated in a letter he sent to Kurt Cobain. "él se percibe más como un fontanero que hace su trabajo y le pagan lo que vale, como aferse una carta enviada a Kurt Cobain."

# artist - Pixies

## genres
American indie

## albums
**Surfer Rosa (1988) - Debut Studio Album** : Steve Albini produced *Surfer Rosa*, the first full-length studio album for the then-unknown Pixies in 1988. The album instantly established the band as pioneers of American indie music and was widely regarded as Album of the Year by many media outlets. Its sound was remarkably distinctive, being innovative, experimental, contagious, luminous, and raw in equal measure, and the band never achieved such a strong and thrilling sound again. "En 1988, Steve Albindli producería unos entonces desconocidos Pixies en el que sería su primilalbum largo de estudio, surferrosa."

## songs
**Gigantic (1988)** : As an illustration of Steve Albini's unconventional recording methods, for bassist Kim Deal's vocals on "Gigantic," he eschewed traditional distance and echo effects. Instead, he had her sing the track in a bathroom, using the natural acoustics of the space to achieve the desired sound. "En lugar de usar efectos de distancia y eco para la voz de Kim Dill, bajista de la banda, le hizo cantar Gigantic de selvaño."

## curiosities
**Kim Deal's Frustration with Black Francis** : Kim Deal, the bassist for Pixies, decided to work with Steve Albini to record *Pod* for her new band, The Breeders, because she and the band were frustrated that Pixies' singer, Black Francis, was not taking her songs seriously. This artistic disagreement led her to seek an outlet for her own compositions. "Informó la banda artada de que Black Francis, cantante de Pixies, no toman en seriosos canciones."

## curiosities
**Birth and Early Musical Influence** : Born in 1962 in Pasadena, Steve Albini's musical journey began after he heard The Ramones' album *Wut*. This experience prompted him to buy a bass guitar and subsequently play in various punk bands throughout his adolescence. "Nacido en 1962 en Pasadena al Vinitra, se escuchar el disco de Wut de los Ramones se compró un bajo y toco varias bandas de pongo durante la asociencia."
**Formation of Big Black** : Steve Albini formed his band Big Black in 1981, marking an early milestone in his career where he began experimenting with his distinctive production approach. "En 1981 formó Big Black."
**Formation of Rapeman** : Steve Albini formed the band Rapeman in 1997. "En 1997 Reimen."
**Formation of Shellac** : Steve Albini formed Shellac in 1992, and the band remains active. This project has allowed him to continue exploring his unique recording and performance philosophies. "en 1992 Shalak, con los que siguen activo."
**Obsession with Raw Sound** : Albini's strong desire to capture the raw and direct sound of rock music led him to experiment extensively with production techniques, particularly within his own bands. This obsession has been a defining characteristic of his work throughout his career. "Su obsesión por capturar la crudeza del sonido más directo del rock le hizo experimentar con la producción de sus bandas."
**Specialization in Live Recording** : Steve Albini is renowned for his expertise in recording bands live, a practice that he notes is becoming increasingly rare in modern music production. This method is central to his philosophy of capturing authentic performances. "Es un especialista en grabar a las bandas en directo, cosa que cada vez es más rara."
**Minimal Effects and Preservation of Imperfections** : In his recordings, Steve Albini favors using few effects and intentionally retains errors made by musicians during sessions. This approach underscores his commitment to natural sound and authentic performance, avoiding over-polished production. "De gusta usar pocos efectos en las grabaciones y dejar errores que tengan los músicos durante las sesiones."
**One-Week Recording Philosophy** : Albini adheres to a philosophy that an album can be recorded in approximately one week. He believes this timeframe is crucial for capturing the immediacy of the initial takes, which he feels best preserve the spirit and energy of live concerts. "Sus tiene que son la rota de armas de una semana en grabar un disco, ya que es parte de adió de la inmediatez de las primeras tomas, las cuales son las que mejor recogen el espíritu de los conciertos."
**Critique of Modern Production Trends** : Steve Albini asserts that 99% of contemporary music sounds identical, a phenomenon he attributes to producers' pervasive obsession with excessive compression and equalization. He views these practices as detrimental to sonic diversity and integrity. "Afirma que el 99% de la música son igual y con los productores están obsesionados con la compresión y la cualización."
**Refusal of Royalties** : Distinct from many other producers, Steve Albini consistently refuses to accept royalties for the music he records. He prefers to be paid a flat fee for his engineering work, rather than benefiting from the ongoing sales of the music he helps create. "Se nega recibe regalías por la música que raba, a diferencia de cómo hacía en otros productores."
**Dislike of "Producer" Title and Arrangements** : Steve Albini dislikes being referred to as a "producer" and never involves himself in musical arrangements. He sees his role as a technical engineer, focused solely on capturing a band's sound rather than shaping its artistic direction. "No le gusta que le llame un productor y además nunca hace arreglos."
**Self-Perception as a "Plumber"** : In contrast to "guru" producers like Rick Rubin, Steve Albini views himself more as a "plumber" who simply performs his job and is compensated for his technical skill. This analogy highlights his pragmatic and unpretentious approach to his craft, as he famously articulated in a letter sent to Kurt Cobain. "él se percibe más como un fontanero que hace su trabajo y le pagan lo que vale, como aferse una carta enviada a Kurt Cobain."
**Artists Produced by Albini** : Steve Albini has worked with a diverse array of artists, including The Stooges, The Jesus Lizard, Mogwai, Fugazi, Joanna Newsom, Superchunk, and Coil. These collaborations showcase his wide-ranging influence and consistent application of his distinctive production style across various genres. "Ha trabajado con destuges, de armyscoker, Monkwife, Fugaci, Slim, Johanna Newsome, Super Choo, Colo, y vamos a analizar siete de los mejores y más importantes discos de su carrera como productor."
**Career Philosophy: Challenging Conventions** : Throughout his career, Steve Albini has actively challenged musical conventions, consistently operating against prevailing trends. He firmly adheres to his core musical principles: a minimalist, analog, and homemade approach, coupled with an unwavering devotion to sonic honesty. These principles are deemed more critical today than ever, in a world where music increasingly becomes compressed, equalized, and uniform. "Este falbínia, a lo largo de su carrera, ha deshacado las convenciones musicales a leer a contra corriente, acerándose siempre a sus principios musicales, son foques minimalistas, analógico y casero, y su devoción por la unistiraz sonora, son más importantes hoy que nunca en un mundo que está volviendo ese cada vez más comprimido, más equalizado y más uniforme."
