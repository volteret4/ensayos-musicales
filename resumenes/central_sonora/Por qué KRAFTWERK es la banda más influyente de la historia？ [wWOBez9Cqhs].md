## albums
**Autobahn (1974) - First Worldwide Success** : This album marked Kraftwerk's first worldwide success and was a pivotal point where the band shed any remaining traces of rock culture. It was highly impactful, notably influencing David Bowie, who played the album in full before his "Station to Station" tour shows. The album was a success in both the United States and Europe, solidifying their conceptual trajectory. "Autobahn fue un éxito en Estados Unidos y Europa, y comprendieron que esa senda conceptual era la que tenían que seguir."
**Radio-Activity (1975) - Pure Electronic Pop** : Following "Autobahn," this album saw Kraftwerk abandon all traditional instruments in favor of a purely electronic sound. It also diverged from the progressive rock character of their earlier work, moving towards a new form of pop music, leading it to be considered the first electronic pop album in history. "no es nada descaballado de decir que Radioactivity es el primer disco de pop electrónico de la historia."
**Trans-Europe Express (1977) - Definitive Electronic Pop** : This album, along with "The Man-Machine," solidified Kraftwerk's electronic pop language and became a worldwide success. Its evocative melodies were famously sampled by Afrika Bambaataa for "Planet Rock," a foundational track in electro and hip-hop. The album dealt with terrestrial communications, anticipating digital communications explored in "Computer World." "Translíope Express, lanzado en 1977 y demand machine de 1978, acabado de dar la forma definitiva a esa idea y fueron un éxito en medio mundo."
**The Man-Machine (1978) - Worldwide Dance Pop Success** : Released in 1978, this album, alongside "Trans-Europe Express," defined Kraftwerk's electronic pop sound and achieved global success. It featured dance-pop hits like "Showroom Dummies" and "The Model," marking a breakthrough in creating universally appealing and danceable electronic music. "funcionó nindos del Dance Pop como Translíope Express, showroom dumis, de model, de robots, Crafwerke había logrado lo que muchas artistas llevaban años señando."
**Computer World (1981) - Futurist Conceptual Work** : This album was a futuristic conceptual work that accurately predicted the omnipresence of computing in daily life, anticipating digital communications after earlier albums explored terrestrial ones. It gained significant popularity in US ghettos and showcased Kraftwerk's continued leadership in electronic music, even as other electronic bands emerged. "En 1981 lanzaron Computer World, una obra conceptual futurista que predijo la omnipresencia de la informática en la vida cotidiana."
**Electric Café (1986) - Continued Electronic Exploration** : Released after a five-year hiatus, this album continued to explore sequencers, drum machines, and electronic textures. However, it did not achieve the same impact as their previous works, as the electronic music scene had become more democratized, with new artists and genres gaining prominence in popularity and innovation. "no tuvo el mismo impacto que esos álbumes anteriores."
**The Mix (Early 1990s) - Reinterpretation for New Trends** : This album was a reinterpretation of Kraftwerk's most successful tracks, adapted to the contemporary electronic music trends of the early 1990s. By this point, Kraftwerk were revered as "gods" by the entire electronic music scene. "A principios de los 90 lanzaron el disco de Mix, una revisión de sus temas más exitosos adaptados a las tendencias del momento."

## songs
**Autobahn (1974)** : The title track of the 1974 album, this over 20-minute piece features a self-made electronic drum machine providing a metronome-like pulse. Florian Schneider, a band founder, described its trance-like state as akin to the mental clarity experienced while driving on a highway, dispelling notions of a psychedelic journey. "comparó con el estado de lucidez mental que se alcanza al conducir por un autopista, desmintiendo el mito de viaje psicodélico que los aficionados al rock esperaban."
**Trans-Europe Express (1977)** : A prominent track from the album of the same name, its evocative melodies were a key element sampled by Afrika Bambaataa for his foundational electro-hip hop track "Planet Rock." This track contributed significantly to the album's success and influence in popularizing machine-generated, danceable music. "le metió las evocadoras melodías de transduropexpress para crear Planet Rock, el tema afundacional del electro, ya sabéis, uno de los pilares del gig-hop."
**Showroom Dummies (1978)** : A track from "The Man-Machine" album, it is cited as one of the dance-pop hits that emerged from Kraftwerk's works, showcasing their ability to craft simple, memorable melodies and mechanical grooves into highly bailable and popular electronic music. "funcionó nindos del Dance Pop como Translíope Express, showroom dumis, de model, de robots, Crafwerke había logrado lo que muchas artistas llevaban años señando."
**The Model (1978)** : A successful track from "The Man-Machine," it is listed as one of the dance-pop songs that solidified Kraftwerk's impact on popular music. Its cold, precise yet deeply physical sound, combined with repetitive patterns and simple melodies, made it highly memorable and bailable. "funcionó nindos del Dance Pop como Translíope Express, showroom dumis, de model, de robots, Crafwerke había logrado lo que muchas artistas llevaban años señando."
**The Robots (1978)** : Featured on "The Man-Machine," this song exemplifies the band's identity as "robots" and their embrace of mechanization. It contributed to the album's success in popularizing electronic dance music and showcased their unique, dehumanized yet catchy approach to pop. "funcionó nindos del Dance Pop como Translíope Express, showroom dumis, de model, de robots, Crafwerke había logrado lo que muchas artistas llevaban años señando."
**Tour de France (1983) - Cycling-Inspired Single** : This single, released in 1983, was a demonstration of the band's love for cycling and was well-received upon its release. It showcased their continued exploration of electronic sounds and themes, despite a looming five-year hiatus. "En 1983 lanzaron el single Tour de France, donde plasvaron su amor por el ciclismo, fue muy bien recibido."

## curiosities
**Influence and Legacy** : Kraftwerk is recognized as one of the most influential and mysterious bands in modern music, fundamentally changing the language of music by reinventing composition, reprogramming new sounds, and redefining musical representation. Their influence is evident in genres like techno, industrial, synth-pop, progressive rock, post-punk, and hip-hop. "una de las bandas más influyentes y misteriosas de la música moderna."
**"Four Robots from Düsseldorf"** : The band is colloquially referred to as "these four robots from Düsseldorf," highlighting their unique, mechanized image and origin. "Estos cuatro robots de Dasseldorf cambiaron el idioma de la música."
**Teutonic Discipline and Image** : Kraftwerk adopted a profoundly German, Teutonic, and austere discipline, which served as an image of unity for the band. They were admired for their determination to differentiate themselves from American sequences and chords, and to break away from the traditional rock image. "adoptaron esa disciplina teotónica y austera como una imagen de unidad."
**Band Name Meaning** : The name "Kraftwerk" means "power station," symbolizing concepts like electricity, industry, mechanization, and automation. This reflected their embrace of industrial aesthetics and technology in music. "El nombre de la banda significa centrar eléctrica, electricidad, industria, mecanización, automatización."
**Machines as Creativity** : Unlike traditional rock, which often reacted against industrialization, Kraftwerk embraced machines as a form of creativity. They integrated themselves into industrial logic to forge a new language of resistance through music. "en vez de huir de las máquinas, las vieron como una forma de creatividad."
**Early Sound and Evolution** : Kraftwerk's initial phase was vastly different from their later historical image. Their first three albums featured a more psychedelic and rock-anchored sound, incorporating drums, guitar, and flutes, with members often wearing long leather coats. Florian Schneider later referred to these early works as "sonic geology." "Sus primeros tres discos tienen un sonido más, psicodélico y anclado al rock, combatería, guitarra, flautas, y un duca corde a ese sonido, pero los largos chupas de piel."
**Autobahn as Turning Point** : The release of "Autobahn" in 1974 marked a significant turning point, where the band deliberately abandoned traces of rock culture to build a quasi-corporate identity. This shift involved cutting their hair, adopting suits and ties, and cultivating a retro-futurist technocrat image that was anti-rock. "El verdadero punto de inflexión llegó con Autobahn, donde dejaron atrás cualquier rastro de la cultura rock y empezaron a construirse como una entidad casi corporativa."
**Suppression of Ego** : The band intentionally suppressed individuality and ego, presenting themselves as a unified system rather than individual artists. "Moraron al individuo y el ego, y se presentaron como un sistema unificado."
**Collaboration with Emil Schult** : Emil Schult joined the band and is often considered the "fifth Kraftwerk." He, along with Florian Schneider, designed the band's canonical image for both albums and live performances. Schult was instrumental in gradually transforming the band into a more robotic, AI-controlled entity, which redefined the future of music. "Fue un encargado de ir convirtiendo poco a poco a la banda, prácticamente en robots."
**Breakthrough with Radio-Activity** : By the release of "Radio-Activity" in 1975, Kraftwerk were no longer unknown artists, having garnered recognition for their innovative electronic sound. "Cuando lanzaron sus higien de trabajo, Radioactivity, editado en 1975, ya no eran los desconocidos."
**Danceable Dehumanized Music** : Their music, characterized by repetitive patterns, simple, memorable melodies, and mechanical grooves, created a sound that was cold and precise yet deeply physical and truly danceable. "Esa música de sumanizada sonaba muy pop y era realmente bailable."
**Transcending Borders** : Kraftwerk's music quickly transcended geographical and cultural borders, finding unexpected resonance with diverse sensibilities worldwide. "Son música no tardó entre los pasados fronteras."
**Detroit Techno Influence** : In Detroit, during the emergence of techno, Kraftwerk was a primary source of inspiration, listened to alongside tracks like "Zohyfield of Demoroder." Techno pioneer Juan Atkins cited Kraftwerk, Parliament, and Funkadelic as his main influences. "En Detroit, cuando estaba gestándose el tecno, lo que más escuchaba era Crafwerk, junto al tema Zohyfield of Demoroder."
**Influence on American Black Music** : Despite their disciplined, austere, and industrial German aesthetic, Kraftwerk left a profound mark on the passionate and physical Black American music scene, including electro, hip-hop, and Miami Bass. "acabará dejando una huella tan profunda en la música negrasta de un idiense, mucho más pasional y corporal."
**"So Rigid They Became Funky"** : "Carclerc" (as transcribed) described Kraftwerk in an interview by stating they were so rigid in their approach that they ultimately became "funky." "Crafwerk era tan rígidos que llegaban a ser fanqui."
**Surpassed by Emerging Scenes** : After their groundbreaking work with "Computer World," Kraftwerk observed various groups, producers, and scenes catching up to and even surpassing them in the mid-1980s, despite having been years ahead in the 1970s and early 1980s. "vieron como varios grupos, productores y escenas, empezaban a alcanzarlos e incluso adelantarlos."
**Five-Year Hiatus** : After consistently releasing an album almost every year since their formation, Kraftwerk experienced a five-year hiatus between "Tour de France" (1983) and "Electric Café" (1986). "vieron un parón de cinco años hasta su siguiente trabajo."
**Pioneering Sound Reinterpreted** : During their hiatus and thereafter, Kraftwerk's pioneering sound was widely reinterpreted by other artists and pushed to new commercial and experimental heights. "Su sonido pionero empezaba a ser reinterpretado por otros, y llevado nuevas cotas comerciales y experimentales."
**Legacy in the 90s** : Despite losing some of their earlier relevance, Kraftwerk's legacy remained potent, notably influencing artists like Aphex Twin, who cited "Computer World" as an inspiration, and shaping the direction of electronic music in the 1990s. "aunque habían perdido relevancia su legado seguía vivo, marcando el rumbo de la música electrónica de los 90."
**Admiration Across Scenes** : By the early 1990s, Kraftwerk was considered legendary and admired by practically everyone in the electronic music scene, transcending specific subgenres or factions. Their music served as an unexpected meeting point for diverse sensibilities. "Fue una de esas raras bandas admiradas por prácticamente todo el mundo, sin importar la escena."
**Created a New Language** : Kraftwerk created a new musical language that each electronic music scene adopted and spoke with its own accent, fostering unique expressions without requiring allegiance to a specific tribe. "Crawford creó un idioma nuevo, y cada escena lo habló con su propio acento."

# artist - David Bowie

## member of
Iggy Pop (friend and collaborator)

## concerts
Station to Station Tour (1976)

## curiosities
**Admiration for Kraftwerk** : David Bowie was deeply impacted by Kraftwerk's album "Autobahn," frequently calling them his favorite band in interviews. During his "Station to Station" tour, he would play the entire "Autobahn" album before his shows. "decía que CRAVWER que era su banda preferida."
**Berlin Era Inspiration** : Bowie's profound admiration for Kraftwerk, combined with his love for German Expressionist art, was a key reason he chose Berlin as a refuge with Iggy Pop. This move was intended to escape the excesses of Los Angeles and to steer his artistic output in a new direction that aligned with the minimal and deeply German character of Kraftwerk's work. "uno de los principales motivos, junto a su amor por el arte expresionista alemán, por el que eligió Berlín como cido para difugiarse junto a su amigo Yguipop."
**Awareness of Musical Divergence** : Bowie recognized that his own rock artistry and music were significantly different from Kraftwerk's minimal and distinctively German style. "Bowie era consciente de que era una artista de rock, y que su música estaba muy alejada del carácter minimal y profundamente alemán de CRAVWER."

**Planet Rock (1982) - Foundational Electro Track** : In the Bronx, Afrika Bambaataa combined a Roland TR-808 drum machine with evocative melodies from Kraftwerk's "Trans-Europe Express" to create "Planet Rock." "África van batada a la Báser en Ambers, a una Roll-Anterrey 808, y le metió las evocadoras melodías de transduropexpress para crear Planet Rock, el tema afundacional del electro, ya sabéis, uno de los pilares del gig-hop."

## curiosities
**Pioneer of Electro and Hip Hop** : Afrika Bambaataa's creation of "Planet Rock" in the Bronx, by blending Kraftwerk's melodies with a Roland TR-808, established him as a pioneer of electro and a key figure in the development of hip-hop. "el tema afundacional del electro, ya sabéis, uno de los pilares del gig-hop."

# genre - Traditional Rock

## curiosities

# genre - Electronic Pop

## curiosities
**Radio-Activity as First Album** : Kraftwerk's "Radio-Activity," released in 1975, is considered the first electronic pop album in history due to its complete abandonment of traditional instruments and its shift towards a new, pop-oriented electronic sound. "no es nada descaballado de decir que Radioactivity es el primer disco de pop electrónico de la historia."

# genre - Dance Pop

## curiosities
**Kraftwerk's Early Contributions** : Kraftwerk's albums "Trans-Europe Express" and "The Man-Machine" produced foundational dance pop tracks like "Trans-Europe Express," "Showroom Dummies," "The Model," and "The Robots." These tracks featured repetitive patterns, simple melodies, and mechanical grooves, making electronic music universally danceable. "funcionó nindos del Dance Pop como Translíope Express, showroom dumis, de model, de robots, Crafwerke había logrado lo que muchas artistas llevaban años señando."

# concert - The Blitz Club

## curiosities
**London's New Music Incubator** : Opened in a small London basement in the late 1970s by Steve Strange and Rusty Egan, who didn't fit with the punk scene, The Blitz Club was a venue where music spoke a new language. Kraftwerk's music was a constant presence there, making it an influential hub for emerging electronic sounds. "dos jóvenes que no encajaban del todo con la rodeaza del punk, Steve Strange y Rastiegan abrieron el blitz."
**Birthplace of New Romantics and Synth-pop** : The Blitz Club is recognized as the cradle of the New Romantics movement and, by extension, the seedbed for the synth-pop genre. The endless nights spent dancing to Kraftwerk deeply influenced attendees and nascent bands like Ultravox, Visage, and Spandau Ballet. "El blitz fue la cuna de las tenaníos románticas y de rebote, el semillero del synth pop."

# instrument - Electronic drum machine

## curiosities
**Kraftwerk's Self-Made Innovation** : Kraftwerk famously manufactured their own electronic drum machine, which they used to establish the metronomic pulse for the title track of their 1974 album "Autobahn." This self-made instrument was a key component in their early electronic sound. "con una batería electrónica que ellos mismos habían fabricado."

# instrument - Flute

## curiosities
**Traditional Element in Early Kraftwerk** : The flute was a traditional instrument used in Kraftwerk's early works, particularly their first three albums, which had a psychedelic and rock-anchored sound. It was even maintained as a traditional instrument during the initial shift towards an electronic sound on "Autobahn." "aunque mantenían algún instrumento tradicional, como la flauta, fue el primer gran giro hacia un sonido electrónico."

# instrument - Vocoder

## curiosities
**First Use by Kraftwerk on Autobahn** : The vocoder was included for the first time by Kraftwerk on their album "Autobahn," specifically for processing voices. This marked a significant step in their evolution towards a fully electronic sound and a departure from traditional vocal techniques. "incluyeron por primera vez un poco de er para procesar las voces."

# instrument - Synthesizers

## curiosities
**Core of Kraftwerk's Later Sound** : From "Trans-Europe Express" and "The Man-Machine" onwards, synthesizers became a fundamental component of Kraftwerk's sound, creating the cold, precise, yet deeply physical and danceable music they were known for. "Todo el sonido haciado a escaladores, secuenciadores y cajas de ritmos."
**Joy Division's Adoption** : Synthesizers were incorporated into Joy Division's album "Closer" due to the significant influence of Kraftwerk on Ian Curtis. "En ese álbum, mi corporaron sintetizadores por influencia de la banda."
**New Order's Full Embrace** : Following Ian Curtis's death, the remaining members of Joy Division formed New Order and fully embraced synthesizers, alongside drum machines and sequencers, becoming an electronic music band. "abrazaron completamente la electrónica, cajas de ritmos, sintetizadores, secuenciadores, convertiéndose casi por completo en una banda de música electrónica."

# curiosity

**Kraftwerk's Influence vs. The Beatles** : The transcript opens by posing the question of whether Kraftwerk was more influential than The Beatles, immediately establishing the immense scope of their impact on music. "Fueron un CRAVWER que más influyentes que los Beatles?"
**Aura of Mystery** : An aura of mystery has always surrounded the German band Kraftwerk, contributing to their enigmatic appeal and distinct image in modern music. "Siempre ha habido un alo de misterio entorno a la banda alemana."
**Global Spread of Kraftwerk's Influence** : Kraftwerk's influence was not confined to a single region; their sound marked artists and scenes across the globe, including England (Depeche Mode, Human League, Gary Numan), the United States (The B-52's), Japan (Yellow Magic Orchestra), and Spain (Aviador Dro). "todos ellos estaban marcados de una manera o otra por los alemanes, y no solo en Inglaterra, de Bob en los Estados Unidos, y a los Magikorchestra en Japón, habiador drogo en España."
**Computer World's US Ghetto Popularity** : Kraftwerk's album "Computer World" gained significant popularity in the ghettos of the United States, demonstrating the unexpected reach and appeal of their futuristic electronic sound in diverse communities. "Computer World se movió mucho por los ghitos de los Estados Unidos."
**Tension Between Disciplined German and Passionate Black American Music** : It is noted as striking that Kraftwerk, arguably the most German band in its discipline, austerity, and industrial imagery, left such a profound mark on the passionate and physical Black American music scene. "Llamas mucho la atención que la que probablemente sea la banda más alemana de todas, en su disciplina, en su austeridad, en su imaginaría industrial, acabará dejando una huella tan profunda en la música negrasta de un idiense, mucho más pasional y corporal."
