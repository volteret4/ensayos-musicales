## curiosities
**Microsoft Windows 95 Startup Sound (Mid-90s)** : In the mid-1990s, Microsoft approached Eno, the "father of ambient music," to compose a startup sound for their new operating system, Windows 95. They provided a demanding list of adjectives—sexy, inspiring, nostalgic, sentimental—all to be compressed into just over three seconds. Eno delivered a piece nearly double the requested length, which Microsoft nevertheless adopted because it perfectly conveyed the desired feeling of welcome, hope, and advancement. "Microsoft le dio una lista interminable de adjetivos para describir lo que buscaban. El sonido debía ser sexy, inspirador, nostálgico, sentimental, y todo ello, comprimido un poco más de tres segundos."
**Microsoft Windows 95 Startup Sound Payment Donation** : Brian Eno later revealed that he composed the Windows 95 startup sound using a Mac. More recently, he donated the payment he received from Microsoft for this composition, doing so in protest of the company's support for the Israeli army. "y no confiso que la había compuesto con un Mac, y recientemente ha donado lo que le pagó Microsoft en protesta por el apoyo de la empresa al ejército de Rayleigh."
**Coined the Term "Ambient" (1978)** : Brian Eno coined the term "ambient" with the release of his 1978 album, "Music for Airports." This work laid the foundational philosophy for the genre. "Al fin de al cabo, él había coñado el término ambient en su disco Music for I report, en 1978."
**Philosophy on Music (1978)** : In his 1978 album "Music for Airports," Eno articulated his philosophy that music should be "as ignorable as it is interesting." This concept proved to be ideal for the background atmosphere required by the operating system of the future, Windows 95. "En este disco, y no defendía que la música debía ser tan ignorable como interesante."
**Collaboration on David Bowie's Berlin Trilogy** : Brian Eno played a significant role in helping David Bowie create his influential and epic Berlin Trilogy of albums. "Al hombre que ayudó a David Bowie, ha creado su épica trilogía de Berlin"
**Creator of Oblique Strategies** : Brian Eno is also recognized as the creator of "Oblique Strategies," a set of cards designed to prompt creative thinking. "al creador de las obliguees estratégias"

**Megadrive Startup Sound (Early 90s)** : The famous "Sega!" intro sound, which became synonymous with the Megadrive console, was not integrated into the console's hardware but rather embedded within the first Sonic game. This strategic decision was effective for branding, as nearly every Megadrive owner possessed the game, making the two-second audio, consisting of a digitalized and compressed chorus of voices, a de facto console boot sound that consumed a significant portion of the game's memory. "Esta famosísima intro de Megadrive. ¡Sica! No estaba dentro de la consola, sino que se quería operar el primer Sonic."
**Dreamcast Startup Sound by Ryuichi Sakamoto (1998)** : For the Dreamcast console, Sega commissioned renowned electronic musician and Oscar winner Ryuichi Sakamoto to compose its startup sound. Sakamoto created a "crystalline, aquatic, and full of light" sound that aimed to transmit immense hope for the future. Paradoxically, the Dreamcast became Sega's "swan song" in hardware, eventually being outcompeted by PlayStation 2, GameCube, and Xbox. "Para Dreamcast, se gaga contra todo nada más, y nada menos que a la llorado Richie saca moto. Y en el ánimo de la música electrónica con Jeloma y Corquestra y ganador de un Oscar para componer su sonido de arranque."

# artist - Sony

## curiosities
**First Major Foray into Video Games (1994)** : The PlayStation represented Sony's inaugural significant venture into the video game industry, moving beyond its previous role of supplying components for other consoles. As a global giant in electronics and sound, Sony's expertise was immediately apparent, making a strong auditory impact from the moment the console was powered on. "Play Station supuso la primera encursión en el mundo de los videojuegos para Sony, más haya algún componente para otra consola. Sony era un gigante de la electrónica y del sonido, y esto se iba a notar desde que encendías la consola."
**PlayStation 1 Startup Sound (1994)** : Designed by Takafumi Fujisawa, the PlayStation 1 startup sound took only two days to create. It served a technical check function, commencing with a deep, saturated synth, transitioning into distant bell-like sounds that created a sense of expansive space, and concluding with a celestial confirmation. Fujisawa aimed to evoke the feeling of entering a cinema, drawing parallels to the THX "Rivient Timpanos" sound. "Takafumi Fujisa, Huawei, diseñó el sonido en dos días. También cumplió una función de check."
**PlayStation 1 Error Sound ("PS4" - Internet Nickname)** : If the PlayStation 1 failed to read a disc, an error sound would play, which the internet humorously dubbed "PS4." This sound was a slight, unsettling variation of the regular startup's second part, entering late and transforming into a "nightmarish" auditory experience. "El que internet botizó como PS4. La segunda parte entraba tarde, como con la leve variación, la justa para convertirse en un sonido pesadillésco."
**PlayStation 2 Startup Sound (2000)** : Inspired by then-PlayStation head Ken Kutaragi's vision of a "floating world with a vast space and Earth in the background," Takafumi Fujisawa and his team designed the PlayStation 2 startup sound to represent a spaceship landing in an undefined cosmic location. If the disc successfully loaded, a brief confirmatory sound played, which, unlike the PS1, was not a climactic moment. "Para realizarles de la segunda consola de Sony, en Cutaragi, el capo de PlayStation por ese entonces, les describió lo que iban a ver los jugadores. Un mundo olido aflojante con un vasto espacio con la tierra de fondo. Y así, el y su equipo, diseñaron el sonido como una nave terrizando en alguna parte indefinida del espacio."
**PlayStation 2 "Red Screen of Death" Sound (2000)** : If the PlayStation 2 failed to read a disc, it famously displayed the "red screen of death," a terrifying sight for many. The accompanying sound enhanced this dread, conveying the impression of the spaceship drifting aimlessly, intensifying the sense of failure. "Si no lo grababa de Er il disco, venía una de las pantallas más temidas de la historia de las consolas, la pantalla roja de la muerte. Este sonido daba la sensación de que la nave había quedado aflojando a la deriva."
**PlayStation 3 Startup Sound (2006)** : For the PlayStation 3, Sony adopted a simpler, more functional startup sound. It employed the audio of a band tuning up before a concert, symbolizing that a significant event was about to commence, thus setting an expectation of excitement and grandeur. "En PlayStation 3 hicieron algo más sencillo y funcional. Usaron el sonido de una banda afinando antes del concierto. Para mostrar que algo grande estaba a punto de comenzar."

"Para realizarles de la segunda consola de Sony, en Cutaragi, el capo de PlayStation por ese entonces, les describió lo que iban a ver los jugadores. # artist - Nintendo

## curiosities
**GameCube Philosophy (2001)** : Nintendo deliberately distanced itself from the intense console war between Xbox and PlayStation 2, which had already claimed the Dreamcast as its first victim. Instead of focusing on technical advancement, Nintendo prioritized "fun over technical advancement," aiming for the GameCube to be perceived as "a box full of toys." "Nintendo se posicionó la favor de la diversión más cala de la avance técnico. Entender que en Cube era de una caja llena de juguetes."
**Wii and Wii U Marketing Shift (2006, 2012)** : With the advent of the Wii and Wii U, Nintendo shifted its strategy to target the "casual" or "uninitiated" player. This involved designing a profoundly clean and friendly user experience, transforming the console's startup into a comfortable and welcoming space with a white environment, soft sounds, and muzak-like music, akin to entering a digital spa. "Pero con la llegada de Wii y Wii U, ya el objetivo cambió. Nintendo quería conquistar al jugador casual. Al no iniciado. Y para ello, diseñó una experiencia completamente distinta, profundamente polcra llamable. La arranque dejó de ser un espectaculo para convertirse en un espacio confortable."

# genre - Vaporwave

## curiosities
**Virtual Utopia Subgenre** : Virtual Utopia is identified as a particularly interesting branch of vaporwave. This subgenre is characterized by its soundtracking of the melancholy associated with a promised, but ultimately unfulfilled, future. It achieves this by recontextualizing old startup sounds, Messenger notifications, CD-ROM aesthetics, and Wii menus, transforming them into corporate muzak that captures the obsessive, and at times cheesy, essence of that bygone era. "En Lutopía Virtual, una de las ramas más interesantes al mi parecer del vaporwave, pone banda sonora a ese futuro. Recuperas a sonidos de arranque, las notificaciones de Messenger, la estética de Ceroom, los menos de la Wii y la traslada un muza corporativo que retrata la obsesiva, me llaveces cursi que fuese a época."

# curiosity

**Early Console Startup Sounds (Pre-1990s)** : In their initial iterations, most consoles did not feature a distinct startup sound but rather a simple monophonic beep or melody. This sound primarily served a technical function, indicating that the software was loading correctly. "Las primeras consolas, por lo general, no tenían un sonido de arranque como tal, sino un pedido monafónico, una simple melodía que indicaba que el software estaba cargando correctamente."
**1980s PC Startup Sounds** : During the 1980s, turning on a PC typically resulted in a strident beep. This sound had a technical purpose, acting as a check. However, on many machines, the same beep also signaled a critical error and system crash, making the startup experience often a reminder of potential failure rather than a satisfactory beginning. "En los años 80, se encendía su PC, lo que escuchaba será esto. Este pedido estridente, me en realidad, tenía una función técnica, servía como un check que le dició al usuario, el problema es que, en muchas máquinas, ese mismo pedido, eran que sonaba cuando el ordenador detectaba un error crítico y se colgaba."
**Console Startup Sounds as Branding** : The incorporation of a startup sound into a console's design quickly became a potent branding tool. When combined with a logo, brand colors, or an animation, these auditory cues helped solidify the console's identity throughout its entire lifespan, leaving a lasting impression on users. "Pronto se envió que meter un sonido en la ranqueda, una consola, ayudaba crear marca. Era una forma de branding, que a la acompañarlo del logo, los colores de la marca o una animación, si vas a acercar a la consola durante toda su vida."
**32-bit Console Era and Loading Times** : The transition to 32-bit consoles and the adoption of CD-ROMs brought about a significant change in user experience: loading times. These delays, almost nonexistent with earlier cartridge-based systems, created a new "waiting room" between powering on the machine and beginning gameplay, which sound engineers cleverly leveraged to psychologically prepare and immerse players. "llegaron los 32 bits, y pasamos del cartucho, a CD. La consola necesitaba un rato para leer el CD. Los tiempos de carga, antes casi inexistentes en el formato cartucho, llegaron para quedarse. Y ahí, es cuando se creó un espacio vacío entre encender la máquina y empezar a jugar."
**Auditory Memory vs. Visual Memory** : The sense of hearing is one of the earliest to develop in the maternal womb, allowing a fetus to recognize its mother's voice. Sound is capable of forming a deeper and more direct connection with emotional memory than visual stimuli alone, making music and sounds exceptionally effective at evoking powerful recollections. "El oído es uno de los primeros sentidos que desarrollamos en el lútero materno. Un bebé en gestación puede llegar a reconocer la voz de su madre. El sonido puede llegar a tener una conexión con la memoria emocional más profunda y directa que la vista."
**Startup Sounds as Auditory Madeleines** : Drawing a parallel to Marcel Proust's observation that the aroma of a madeleine can trigger a flood of childhood memories, the startup sounds of personal computers or consoles from one's youth can act as an "auditory equivalent." These brief sonic cues are capable of instantaneously transporting individuals back to a more innocent and carefree period of their lives. "Mars el Proust escribió que el aroma de una madalena puede causar un torbellino de emociones que pueden llevarte a tu más cierre la infancia. Bien, pues los sonidos darán que de tus ordenadores o consolas de la infancia, pueden ser el equivalente auditivo."
**Modern Minimalism in Startup Sounds** : Contemporary major brands have largely embraced a minimalist aesthetic, leading to startup sounds losing much of their past relevance and memorability. These sounds have evolved into mere utilitarian formalities, sacrificing grandeur and theatricality for the sake of efficiency and immediacy, a trend also observed in current AAA video games, where photorealism is perceived as diminishing past creativity. "Hoy en día, todas las grandes marcas están inversas dentro del minimalismo. El sonido de arranque está perdiendo en la elevancia, y es mucho menos memorable que los de antes. Se ha convertido en un trámite utilitario."
**Nostalgia for the "Future Past" of Technology** : Older systems like Windows 95, PlayStation, and Dreamcast once promised expansive worlds and global connectivity, embodying a distinct "narrative of the future." In contrast, today's permanent digital immersion means there's no longer a need for majestic welcome sounds, as "the doors are always open." Technology, once an unknown journey demanding an epic soundtrack, is now ubiquitous. "Buenas 95, PlayStation, Dreamcast, todas estas máquinas nos prometían mundos infinitos, conexiones globales, había una narrativa del futuro. Hoy ya no necesitamos un sonido majestuoso que nos dé la bienvenida a un entorno digital futurista, porque ya vivimos permanentemente dentro del entorno digital."
