# genre - Drama Bass
## genres
Liquid Funk
Atmospheric Drama Bass
Neurofunk
## curiosities
**Dominant Video Game Genre (90s-2000s)** : Drum and bass emerged as the most prominent club music genre in video games during the late 1990s and early 2000s. Its rapid beats and deep basslines perfectly complemented the speed and tension that many racing and futuristic action titles aimed to convey. "pero hubo un generó que sonó con más fuerza que el resto, el drama bass."
**Evolution from Jungle (1995)** : Drum and bass was defined in 1995 as a more polished and technical evolution of jungle. It featured more elaborate and precise basslines, cleaner, often produced rather than sampled drums, and incorporated broader influences, from pop to experimental genres, fostering its growth. "A este nuevo género le llamaron drama base."
**Key Differences from Jungle** : Drum and bass distinguishes itself from jungle by being a more refined and technical evolution. It employs more elaborate and precise basslines, cleaner and often custom-produced drum patterns (rather than sampled breaks), and integrates a wider array of influences including pop and experimental sounds. "El drama base en cambio nace como una evolución más púlida y técnica, sus bajos son más elaborados y precisos, las baterías más limpias y muchas casas producidas y nos empleadas."
**Decline in Video Games (2010s)** : Around the 2010s, drum and bass started to diminish in prevalence within video games, becoming almost exclusively restricted to established franchises with a strong existing association, such as driving games like Forza or Dirt Rally and extreme sports titles. The industry shifted towards other electronic and pop styles that resonated better with the market. "el género comenzó a desgastarse dentro del medio."
**Resurgence and "PlayStation Sound" (Recent Years)** : In recent years, drum and bass has experienced a significant rediscovery, particularly among new generations. The atmospheric variant is often colloquially referred to as "PlayStation sound," inspiring millions of YouTube views for mixes and tutorials on how to recreate this specific aesthetic, which is deeply tied to late 90s/early 2000s video game aesthetics. "Mucha gente se está refiriendo al drama base más atmosferico como sonido a PlayStation."

# genre - Atmospheric Drama Bass
## curiosities
**Early Adoption in Video Games (PlayStation 2, GameCube)** : Atmospheric Drama Bass, described as "advoqueric drama bass" in the transcript, was adopted by video games on platforms such as PlayStation 2 and GameCube. **Characteristics and New Age Inspiration** : Atmospheric drum and bass emerged around the same time as conventional drum and bass but with a distinct ethos. While club-oriented drum and bass focused on power and impact, atmospheric drum and bass drew inspiration from New Age to craft ethereal, cinematic soundscapes. It maintained tempos around 170 BPMs but utilized softer drums, long pads, and heavy reverb, creating a sensation of floating in open space. "La sensación era de flotar en un espacio abierto más que la de moverse en una pista de baile."

# genre - Jungle

## curiosities
**Emergence and Characteristics (1992)** : Jungle emerged around 1992 as a distinct genre, characterized by tempos oscillating between 160 and 170 BPMs, deep bass lines, and strong influences from dancehall and ragga. It was deeply connected to sound system culture and gained popularity in the British scene through pirate radio and illegal parties. "Hacia 1992, de ese caldo de cultivo surgió el jungle."
**Scene Reputation and Evolution to Drama Bass (1995)** : Despite its growing popularity, jungle's reputation suffered by 1995 as the scene became associated with increased physicality, harder drugs, fights, and conflicts. This negative perception led several musicians to distance themselves and refine the jungle sound, which eventually gave birth to drum and bass. "A la aumentar la velocidad de la intensidad, la escena se volvió mucho más física, entraron drogas más duras y fue común ver peleas y conflictos."
**Key Differences from Drama Bass** : Jungle is described as more raw and chaotic compared to drum and bass. It features profound bass, percussions with numerous breaks, dark ambient sounds, and a strong influence from dancehall and ragga, remaining heavily tied to rave culture. "El jungle es más crudo y caótico, bajos profundos, percusiones con muchos breaks, ambientadas con sonidos escuros y una fuerte influencia del dexhol y el raga."

# curiosity

**Video Games Embrace Club Music (Mid-90s to Early 2000s)** : During the latter half of the 1990s and the first half of the 2000s, video games became saturated with club music. Genres such as techno, trance, and Big Beat frequently appeared in racing and futuristic action sports games, but drum and bass ultimately dominated, with its fast beats and deep bass perfectly conveying the speed and tension these titles aimed for. "Durante la segunda mitad de los años 90 y la primera de los 2000, los videojuegos se llenaron de música de club."
**Evolution of British Rave Scene BPMs (Early 90s)** : In the early 1990s, the British rave and club culture scene grew significantly in both popularity and intensity. The 120 BPMs of It House, which characterized the "Summer of Love" in 1988, were rapidly superseded. DJs and producers pushed tempos higher, reaching 130, 140, and even 150 BPMs, leading to extensive experimentation with cut and manipulated breakbeats. "Los 120 BPMs de las It House habían marcado aquel verano de la mor de 1988 comenzaron a quedar atrás."
**Rise of Sample Culture in Early 90s Electronic Music** : The culture of sampling profoundly impacted the early 1990s electronic music scene. It facilitated the reintroduction of old drum fragments, most notably the legendary Amen break. This 7-second drum loop, recorded by a soul band in 1969, became one of the most influential rhythmic patterns in modern music, forming the core rhythmic foundation of both jungle and drum and bass. "La cultura del sample hoy rompió de lleno en la escena, trayendo de vuelta a viejos fragmentos de batería, como el legendario A&M break."
**Jungle's Scene Reputation and the Birth of Drama Bass (1992-1995)** : Jungle emerged around 1992, gaining traction in the British scene through pirate radio and illegal parties. However, as its popularity grew, its reputation suffered due to increasing speed and intensity leading to a more physical scene, with hard drugs, fights, and conflicts. By 1995, several musicians distanced themselves from this scene, opting to experiment and refine the jungle sound, which led to the creation of drum and bass. "A la aumentar la velocidad de la intensidad, la escena se volvió mucho más física, entraron drogas más duras y fue común ver peleas y conflictos, posiblemente por eso y por muchos otros motivos, seguro, en 1995 varios músicos se desvincularon de la escena y empezaron a experimentar y polir el sonido del jungle. A este nuevo género le llamaron drama base."
**Early Console Gaming in British Clubs (1995)** : In 1995, an unusual phenomenon occurred in British clubs: instead of everyone dancing, groups of people gathered around consoles that had not yet been released. This scene irrevocably changed the relationship between club music and video games. "en algunos cruzos británicos ocurría algo inusual, no todo el mundo estaba bailando, varias personas hacían con las rededor de una consola que todavía no había salido al mercado, y que cambiaría, para siempre, la relación entre esta música y los videojuegos."
**PlayStation's Adult Marketing Strategy (1994)** : Upon its launch in Japan in 1994, Sony's PlayStation sought to break away from the childish image associated with 8-bit and 16-bit consoles. Sony aimed to market it as an entertainment system for all ages, projecting a more adult and sophisticated image. This strategy involved collaborating with leading studios like the British developer Psygnosis. "Desde el principio la empresa japonesa quiere romper con la imagen infantil de las consolas de 8 y 16 bits y quiere venderla como un sistema de entretenimiento para todas las edades."
**Wipeout's Groundbreaking Promotion and Soundtrack (1995)** : Wipeout, a futuristic racing game, featured a highly atypical promotion for its era: playable demo units were installed in UK clubs even before the PlayStation console was available. It was also the first game to feature licensed music from renowned British club artists such as The Chemical Brothers, Orbital, and Leftfield, with graphic design by Designes Republic (known for Warp Records covers). "su promoción fue totalmente atípica para la época. Antes de que la consola llegara al mercado, instalaron caminas jugables en clubs del Reino Unido."
**PlayStation Launch Success and Identity (September 1995)** : When the PlayStation console launched in Europe in September 1995, Wipeout quickly became its best-selling launch title. This success helped define PlayStation's identity as a more adult platform, with electronic music becoming a crucial pillar of its sound and overall aesthetic. "White Pout se convirtió en el título del lanzamiento más vendido, y Playstation, y para extensión una gran parte de su catalogo, comenzó definido una identidad mucho más adulta y con la música electrónica como uno de los espilares de su sonido y estética."
**Early EDM in Amiga Games (Late 1980s - Early 1990s)** : The presence of Electronic Dance Music (EDM) in video games predates PlayStation. Many titles developed for Amiga computers in the late 1980s and early 1990s already embodied a rave aesthetic. Some of the composers working on these games were also active DJs in the electronic music scene, although the Amiga system had a very compressed sound quality. "A finales de los años 80 y principes de la 90, muchos titulos para ordenadores amiga ya respiraban la estética Rafe."
**First Official Drama Bass in Video Games (Wipeout 2097)** : The distinction of being the first video game to officially incorporate drum and bass largely belongs to Wipeout 2097, the second installment of the Wipeout series. This title featured "Circe Coins" by Fotec, marking a pivotal moment for the genre's integration into gaming. "Investigando sobre cuál fue el primer videojuego en incluir drama base de forma oficial, todo apunta que el honor recae en la segunda entrega de la saga White Pout. White Pout 2017, con el espectacular de Circe Coins de Fotec."
**Drama Bass Integration in PlayStation Soundtracks** : Following Wipeout 2097, both jungle and drum and bass began to naturally integrate into PlayStation soundtracks. Initially, their natural habitat was racing games like Gran Turismo, Ridge Racer Type 4, Need for Speed 2, and Rollcage, where the genres' speed and energy perfectly complemented the sensation of adrenaline and velocity. "A partir de ahí, tanto el jungle como el drama base empezaron a filtrarse de forma natural en las bandas honoras de Playstation."
**Drama Bass Expansion Beyond Racing Games (Late 90s)** : During the latter half of the 1990s, drum and bass quickly expanded beyond racing circuits into other video game genres. It became particularly prominent in sports and snowboard games, where its energy aligned well with the excitement of competition. The genre also made the jump to other platforms, including the Nintendo 64. "Pero rápidamente el género saltó más allá de los circuitos. El drama base empezó con las y en otros géneros, especialmente en los juegos deportivos y de Snowboard."
**Japanese Approach to Drama Bass Soundtracks** : Japanese producers approached drum and bass for video game soundtracks with a distinctive, "almost academic and deeply aesthetic" vision, often treating it akin to cinematic genres. Unlike many British counterparts who emerged from club and rave scenes, these Japanese musicians were often trained in conservatories or programming, rather than through club culture. "Parece que los productores japoneses se acercaban al drama base como si fueran casi géneros cinematográficos."
**Ape Escape's Influential Drama Bass Soundtrack** : Ape Escape, one of PlayStation's most beloved platformers, is remembered for its iconic drum and bass soundtrack. The game's developers, inspired by Soichi Terada's album "Sumo Jungle," commissioned him to compose a three-and-a-half-hour score that blended jungle and drum and bass energy with playful melodies, tropical textures, synthesizers, marimbas, and maracas, designed to evoke various player emotions. "Episcaype fue uno de los juegos de plataformas más queridos y recordados de PlayStation. Los desarrolladores estaban dando vueltas a la banda sonora que tendría a acompañar al juego."
**Drama Bass Decline in Video Games (2010s)** : As the 2010s progressed, the use of drum and bass in video games began to wane.
