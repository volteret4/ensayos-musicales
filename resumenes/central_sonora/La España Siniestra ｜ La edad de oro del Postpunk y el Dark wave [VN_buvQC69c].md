# artist - Eduardo Benavente

## member of
Parálisis Permanente
Alaska y los Pegamoides

## curiosities
**Tragic Death (May 1983)** : Eduardo Benavente died in a car accident in May 1983 while en route to Zaragoza for a concert with his band, Parálisis Permanente, for the Isis Permanente event. His untimely passing led to the dissolution of the band but marked the beginning of his legend. "su trágica y repentina muerte, con ella desaparecía la banda, pero empezaba la leyenda."
**Pioneer of Spanish Post-punk** : Benavente and his band, Parálisis Permanente, became the main references for post-punk and Spanish gothic music, known as Afterpunk or "música siniestra" in the peninsula. Their dark and radical sounds offered a tenebrous contrast to the Movida Madrileña. "Benavente y su grupo se convirtieron en los principales referentes del post-pank y la música gotica española."
**Generational Figure and Counter-Cultural Leader** : The legacy of Benavente and his band marked a generation and defined a counter-cultural movement that is still longed for and championed today. He possessed an innate charisma, making him an enigmatic and natural leader who quickly acquired iconic and controversial status. "En legados de Benavente y su banda marcó una generación y definió un movimiento contra cultural que sigue siendo añorado y reivindicado hoy en día."
**Influence on Alaska y los Pegamoides** : Upon joining Alaska y los Pegamoides, alongside Ana Curra, Eduardo Benavente brought a "siniestro" (sinister/dark) side to the band. His fascination with British gothic bands led to changes in his personal style. "quienes aportaron ese lado siniestro a la banda."
**Travels to London and Gothic Influence** : After meeting Ana Curra, Eduardo Benavente went to live with her a week later, and they made several trips to London. There, they were fascinated by concerts of bands like Killing Joke and Bauhaus, which influenced them to start styling their hair and dressing in leather pants and creepers. "hicieron varias viajes en la terra, donde quedaron fascinados con los conciertos de Killin Joke y Bout House, comenzaron a cardarse el pelo y a vestir con pantalones de piel y creepers."
**Charisma and Enigmatic Presence** : Eduardo Benavente, though neither tall nor particularly handsome, possessed an innate charisma unmatched by many in the scene, perhaps only comparable to Alaska's. His magnetism attracted both followers and fellow musicians, generating admiration, rivalries, and envy due to his growing fame. "Eduardo benavente no era ni alto ni especialmente guapo, pero poseía un carisma inato que pocos en la escena podían igualar. Quizás solo compararle con el Alaska."

## albums
**Split with Gabinete Caligari (1981)** : Parálisis Permanente's first work was a split single with Gabinete Caligari, a band influenced by The Cure and Eduardo Benavente's charisma. Parálisis Permanente contributed "A tu suficiencia" and "Tengo un pasajero" to this release, which quickly became an underground gem. "Su primer trabajo fue un split con Gabinete Caligari."
**Quiero Ser Santa EP (1982)** : Following the success of their split single, Parálisis Permanente released the EP "Quiero Ser Santa" in 1982. This release further cemented their status within the underground scene. "Tras el split lanzaron el EP Quiero Ser Santa en 1982."
**El Acto (1982)** : A few months after their "Quiero Ser Santa" EP, the band released their album "El Acto," which catapulted them to the forefront of the national underground scene. Its success was short-lived due to the tragic death of Eduardo Benavente in May 1983. "y unos meses después el disco el acto, el cual lo situó en la cima del underground estatal."

## songs
**A tu suficiencia (1981)** : This song was chosen by Parálisis Permanente for their debut split single with Gabinete Caligari. It contributed to the split's status as an underground gem. "Las canciones legidas por paralisis permanente fueron a tu suficiencia y tengo un pasajero."
**Tengo un pasajero (1981)** : Featured on their first release, a split single with Gabinete Caligari, this song helped establish Parálisis Permanente's dark sound in the Spanish underground. The split's success led to increased concert and television appearances for both bands. "Las canciones legidas por paralisis permanente fueron a tu suficiencia y tengo un pasajero."

## curiosities
**Formation and Lineup Changes (1981)** : Parálisis Permanente was formed in 1981, initially including brothers Eduardo and Javier Benavente, along with Nacho and Johnny Canut. Javier Benavente later left the band, and Ana Curra joined, bringing her distinctive keyboard sound. "La banda se formó en 1981 para Lelo Apegamo y des, con los hermanos Eduardo y Javier benavente por un lado y Nacho y Johnny Canut por otro. Finalmente Javier abandonó la banda y se les unió a Nakura."
**Self-Release Strategy through Tres Cipreses** : Due to Hispavox's reluctance to release material from such a dark-sounding band, Parálisis Permanente was inspired by British groups who self-edited their works. This led them to open their own independent label, Tres Cipreses, establishing a norm in the Spanish underground. "Ispavox tenía muchas reticencias en sacar disco a una banda tan oscura, por lo que inspirados por todos estos grupos británicos que se autoditaban sus trabajos, habrían su propio sello, tres cirpreses."
**Underground Gem Status and Media Exposure** : Their split single with Gabinete Caligari quickly became a highly valued release within the underground scene. This success enabled both bands to perform at more concerts and secure invitations to television programs. "El disco se convertió en una joya dentro del underground, y pronto ambas bandas empezaron a tocar en cada vez más conciertos y ser invitados a programas de televisión."

# artist - Olvido Gara

## member of
Caca de Lux
Alaska y los Pegamoides
Alaska y Dinarama

## curiosities
**Early Exposure to Punk in London (1976)** : Olvido Gara, known as Alaska, was in London in 1976 at the age of 13, where she witnessed the burgeoning punk scene. This early exposure significantly shaped her musical path. "Olvido a Gara, quien adoptó el nombre artístico de Alaska, estuvo en la capital inglesa en 1976 cuando tenía tan solo 13 años y pudo ver todo lo que se estaba gestando."
**Pioneer of Spanish Punk** : Alaska is considered, almost with total certainty, to be the first punk in Spain, a title she shares with Paloma Romero 'Palmolive' of Des Leeds. Her early involvement was crucial in establishing the punk aesthetic in Spain. "Se podría decir casi con total seguridad que fue la primera punk de España con permiso de Paloma Romero Palmolyf, miembro de Des Leeds."
**Charismatic Frontwoman** : Alaska possessed a charisma so powerful that few in the scene could equal it, with Eduardo Benavente being a possible comparison. Her stage presence contributed to her bands' success and iconic status. "Quizás solo compararle con el Alaska."

## curiosities
**Formation After Caca de Lux (Undated)** : After the dissolution of Caca de Lux, Nacho Canut, Carlos Berlanga, Alaska, and Manolo Campoamor, who shared a stronger affinity, formed Alaska y los Pegamoides. This new project allowed them to explore different musical directions. "quienes tenían mejor afinidad entre ellos cuando se disolvió Caca de Lux fundaron Alaska los pegamos y des."
**Introduction of a Darker Sound** : The band's sound evolved significantly with the addition of Eduardo Benavente on drums and Ana Curra on keyboards. They brought a "siniestro" (sinister) side to the band, influencing its musical direction towards gothic and darker themes. "poco después se unieron unos convencísimos, se duardo venamente a la batería y han ocurra a los teclados, quienes aportaron ese lado siniestro a la banda."
**Internal Tensions and Carlos Berlanga's Departure** : Despite the band's success, internal tensions arose due to the darker, gothic turn the music took following the arrival of Eduardo Benavente and Ana Curra. This disenchantment led Carlos Berlanga to leave the band and form Dinarama. "ese éste éxito en la banda bien crepanzas internas, desencantado por el giro hacia el oscuro y gotico que había tomado a la banda tras la llegada al Eduardo Yana, Carlos Berlanga dejó la banda y formó Dinarama."

## albums
**Décima Víctima (Undated)** : This album was one of the two genial records released by Décima Víctima through their self-founded label, Grabaciones Accidentales (GASA). It contributed to their reputation for a cold, dark, and minimalist sound. "Allí editaron todo su material, entre los que se encuentran sus dos discos. Los geniales, Dicima Víctima y un hombre solo."
**Un Hombre Solo (Undated)** : As one of Décima Víctima's two seminal albums released on GASA, "Un Hombre Solo" further showcased the band's distinctive sound. The label was instrumental in disseminating their work. "Allí editaron todo su material, entre los que se encuentran sus dos discos. Los geniales, Dicima Víctima y un hombre solo."

## curiosities
**Formation of "Onda Siniestra" Pioneer (1981)** : Décima Víctima formed in 1981, becoming another major band of the initial phase of the "onda siniestra" in Spain. Their sound was described as extremely cold and dark, drawing parallels to Joy Division. "Al igual que para el Isis permanente y Gabinete Caligari, en 1981 también se formó la otra gran banda de la primera etapa de la onda siniestra, décima víctima."
**Joy Division Influence and Distinctive Sound** : The band was notably close in style to Joy Division and possessed arguably the coldest and darkest sound within the "onda siniestra." Their music was minimalist and characterized by a melancholic and anxious atmosphere. "una banda muy cercana a Joey División y posiblemente los que tenían el sonido más frío y oscuro."
**Existentialist Lyrics and The Cure Comparison** : Carlos Entrena's lyrics were a blend of existentialism and depression, contributing to a melancholic and angst-ridden atmosphere in their music. This lyrical style drew comparisons to The Cure's album "Faith." "Las letras de Entrena, entre existencialistas y depresivas, aportaban ese ambiente melancólico y angustioso que los emparejaban con el Faith de Dickyour."
**Minimalist Aesthetic and Rejection of "Siniestro" Look** : Décima Víctima was known for its minimalist approach, consistently dressing austerely and rejecting the characteristic "siniestro" look, differentiating them from other bands of the movement. "Pciaezo era una banda minimalista y siempre vestían de forma austera, renegando del look característico de los iniestros."
**Founding of Grabaciones Accidentales (GASA)** : The band established their own label, Grabaciones Accidentales (GASA), in collaboration with Paco Trinidad and members of Esclarecidos. This label was created to self-release their material and became a significant platform for other scene artists. "Ellos también crearon su sello para editar su propio material. Ravaciones accidentales, abreviado como gasa, fundado junto con paco trinidad y miembros desclarecidos."

# artist - Derribos Arias

## members
Poch (leader)

## labels
Grabaciones Accidentales (GASA)

## albums
**Branquias bajo el agua (Undated)** : This LP followed the successful single "La Flor" and showcased Derribos Arias's unique and risky musical proposition. Despite its unconventional nature, the album gained significant notoriety within the scene. "seguido de LP Branquias bajo el agua."
**La Guía del Espíritu (Undated)** : This was Derribos Arias's only LP which, despite prior notoriety, featured a "maquetero" (demo-like) and out-of-tune sound, in contrast to their earlier works. Many listeners considered it a step backward in their career. "cuando sacaron en la guía del Distin, su único LP, ofrecieron un disco con un sonido a maquetero y desafinado, en contraposición a sus trabajos anteriores, por lo que mucha gente lo consideró un paso atrás en su carrera."

## songs
**La Flor (Undated)** : This single was Derribos Arias's first release and successfully introduced their unique and somewhat risky musical concept to the scene. Its reception helped them gain considerable notoriety. "Su primer lanzamiento fue el Genearsingle La Flour."

## curiosities
**Impact and Uniqueness on GASA** : Derribos Arias was the band with the most significant impact on the Grabaciones Accidentales (GASA) label. They were led by the charismatic and extravagant Poch, distinguishing them within the "onda siniestra" scene. "Dribos áreas fue la banda que más repercusión tuvo de gasa, liderada por el carismático y extravagante poche."
**Comedic and Absurd Extreme of Afterpunk** : While Décima Víctima represented the melancholic extreme of Afterpunk, Derribos Arias occupied the comedic and absurd extreme. They were considered a rarity in the scene, balancing chaos and genius, reminiscent of Cabaret Voltaire but also embracing a "CUTRE" (trashy/DIY) aesthetic. "Si Dicima Víctima era el extremo melancólico del Afterpunk, ella serán el extremo cómico y absurdo, una de esas rarezas de la escena entre el caos y la genialidad, entre Cávarit Volter y lo CUTRE."
**Expected Success and Career Setback** : Everyone anticipated Derribos Arias would become the next big band after achieving notoriety and performing on television multiple times. However, the release of their LP "La Guía del Espíritu" with its demo-like and out-of-tune sound was widely seen as a step backward, diminishing their potential breakthrough. "Todo el mundo pensaba que iba a ser la gran banda del momento, pero cuando sacaron en la guía del Distin, su único LP, ofrecieron un disco con un sonido a maquetero y desafinado, en contraposición a sus trabajos anteriores, por lo que mucha gente lo consideró un paso atrás en su carrera."

## curiosities
**Pioneers of Electronic Pop (1979)** : Aviador Dro formed in 1979 and were the pioneers of electronic pop in Spain. Their music drew influences from influential international bands like The Residents, Devo, and Kraftwerk. "A viador drogo fueron los pioneros del pop electrónico en el país. La banda se formó en 1979 y tomó influencias de bandas como de Residence, Divo o Crafwerk."

**Formation of DRO (1982)** : Discontent with the musical direction and the lack of a label willing to release his album after most band members left, Servando Carballar founded his own label, DRO (Discos Radioactivos Organizados), in 1982. This label would become highly influential. "Miendo que ninguna discográfica quería editarle ese disco, Servando Creon, 1982, es el sello drogo."
**Departure of Members to Form Esplendor Geométrico** : The majority of the band, excluding Servando Carballar, became dissatisfied with the evolving sound and decided to leave Aviador Dro. These members then went on to form the experimental electronic group Esplendor Geométrico. "pero el grueso de la banda, salvo Servando, descontenta con el sonido que estaba tomando, decidió abandonar el grupo para formar esplendorgeó métrico."
**Continued Activity** : Aviador Dro continues to tour and release new material to this day, demonstrating their enduring presence in the electronic music scene. "Ambas bandas siguen haciendo giras y editando material a día de hoy."

# artist - Esplendor Geométrico

## members
Arturo Lanz
Gabriel Riaza
Juan Carlos Astray

## genres
Música Electrónica
Industrial

## curiosities
**Formation from Aviador Dro Members** : Esplendor Geométrico was formed by Arturo Lanz, Gabriel Riaza, and Juan Carlos Astray, who had previously been members of Aviador Dro but left due to dissatisfaction with that band's musical direction. They quickly established their own label for self-release. "Esplendorgeó métrico, por su parte, hizo lo mismo y obtenidos sus propios discos."
**Embrace of Experimentation and Noise** : After separating from Aviador Dro, Esplendor Geométrico remained in electronic music but distanced themselves from pop, embracing pure experimentation. Their sound became characterized by harsh, hypnotic, rhythmic mantras where noise took precedence over melodies. "Se mantuvieron en la música electrónica, pero para distanciarse del sonido de aviador drogo se alejaron del pop y abrazaron la experimentación. Crearon mantras límicos, imnoticos y asperos, donde el ruido adquirió más importancia que las melodías."
**International Recognition in Industrial Genre** : Considered "oddballs" within the Movida Madrileña, Esplendor Geométrico gained more recognition outside Spain, particularly in cultures interested in noise music like Germany and England. # curiosity

**Movida Madrileña Cultural Context** : Following the end of the dictatorship, Spain experienced a period of cultural fervor characterized by a strong desire to break with tradition and the past. Young people eagerly sought new cultural expressions, embracing movements like punk and post-punk that arrived from abroad. "Tras el final de la dictadura, España vivía una etapa de fervercencia cultural marcada por la necesidad de romper con lo aniejo y todo lo que representaba el pasado. La juventud estaban seosa de probar y descubrir nuevas expresiones culturales y abrazo nuevos movimientos que llegaban del extranjero."
**Trend of Self-Editing in the Underground** : Inspired by British groups who self-released their works, the practice of opening independent labels became a norm in the Spanish underground music scene. Many bands, including Parálisis Permanente and Décima Víctima, edited their albums independently. "A partir de este momento los sellos independientes se convirtieron en una norma en el underground español, y la gran mayoría de las bandas que vamos a nombrar elitarán sus discos de manera independiente."
**Spanish Underground Independent Labels Landscape** : The Spanish underground scene saw the emergence and consolidation of several key independent labels like Tres Cipreses (founded by Parálisis Permanente), Grabaciones Accidentales (GASA, founded by Décima Víctima), and DRO (founded by Servando Carballar of Aviador Dro). These labels became crucial for the distribution of alternative music. "A partir de este momento los sellos independientes se convirtieron en una norma en el underground español."
**Onda Siniestra Decline and Shift to Hedonism (Late 1980s-1990s)** : The "onda siniestra" declined by the late 1980s, primarily persisting in Valencia's "Ruta del Bakalao" nightclubs. The advent of the 1990s brought a cultural shift towards a more colorful and hedonistic environment, reflecting a desire to move past the profound concerns and darkness that had defined previous decades, particularly in Spain. "A finales de la década de los 80, la onda siniestra estaba en decadencia...O la llegada de la década de los 90, el enfoque se desplazo hacia un ambiente más colorido y edonista, reflejando un cambio en las prioridades culturales, el deseo de disfrutar y dejar atrás las curidad y las preocupaciones profundas que habían definido a las décadas anteriores, en especial en este país."
