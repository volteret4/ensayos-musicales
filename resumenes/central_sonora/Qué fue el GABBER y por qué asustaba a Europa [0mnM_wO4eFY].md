# genre - Gáver (Gabber)

## genres
Electronic Music
Hardcore
Hardcore Techno

## curiosities
**Early Radical Music Identity** : Gabber emerged in the 1990s as one of the first electronic music genres to successfully transform music into a radical identity, mirroring the impact of punk in England or black metal in Norway. It is characterized by its exceptionally hard and violent sound, played at speeds reaching 200 BPMs. "uno de los primeros generos en lograr lo que el punk en Inglaterra vuelve la meta en Noruega, convertir la música en una identidad rarical."
**Musical Characteristics** : Gabber tracks are defined by their reliance on a distorted kick drum sound, often sourced from a classic TR-909 drum machine, accelerated to extreme tempos of 180, 190, or even 200 BPMs, turning it into a "sonic hammer." These tracks typically feature minimal melody, focusing instead on pure energy and impactful rhythms. "Las pistas de Cábera apenas requerían melodía. Se centraban en la energía pura y el impacto."
**Name Origin and Meaning** : The term "Gabber" originates from Amsterdam's Yiddish slang, where it means "friend" or "colleague." This nomenclature reflects the sense of community and camaraderie among its early adherents. "Asinación Gáber, que significa amigo o colega en el Argo Gidis de Amsterdam."
**Identity for Working-Class Youth** : Gabber became a crucial identity and a refuge for many working-class young people, predominantly boys but with a significant presence of girls, who felt unrepresented by mainstream culture or more sophisticated electronic scenes. It provided a space for belonging, worker's pride, and an embrace of marginality. "el Gáber se convirtió en una identidad para muchas jóvenes de clase obrera que no se reconocen en la cultura dominante ni las escenas electrónicas más sofisticadas y pijas, el Gáber fue en refugio."
**Media Scrutiny and Social Alarm** : Due to its growing visibility and distinctive aesthetic, Gabber began to draw distrust from mainstream society and quickly became an object of social alarm. Media outlets frequently portrayed it as a dangerous subculture associated with violence, drug consumption, and, in some cases, the far-right. "Desde fuera a la cultura Gáber empezó a despertar de es confianza, y pronto se conrirtió en un objeto de alarma social."
**Commodification and Decline by 2000** : The music industry eventually absorbed Gabber, commodifying it into a mass product through numerous "soulless" compilation CDs and oversaturating the market, to the point where even pop hits received hardcore versions. This commercialization, combined with its poor public image, led to Gabber being "practically a corpse" by the year 2000. "para el año 2000, el Gáber, fuera prácticamente un cadaver."
**Enduring Legacy as Identity** : Despite its commercial decline, Gabber left an enormous footprint by solidifying the idea that electronic dance music, much like punk or heavy metal, could serve as a powerful identity, a form of belonging, and a statement of resistance. It established a lineage that continues through genres like hardcore, speedcore, frenchcore, and uptempo hardcore. "el Gáber había dejado un anorme huella, ya que había consolidado la idea de que la música electrónica de baile, al igual que el panc, el heavy metal, también podía ser una identidad, una forma de pertenencia y resistencia."
**Contemporary Revival and Aesthetic Reinterpretation** : Gabber is currently experiencing a "strange and contradictory" revival. While 90s participants saw it as a profound identity beyond extreme sounds and aesthetics, Generation Z often perceives Gabber as a purely aesthetic product, similar to "idosca" or vaporwave, primarily valued for its viral potential. "el Gáber vive un rey ball, extraño y un tanto contradictorio."

# genre - Música Máquina

## genres

## curiosities
**Spanish Gabber Parallel** : Música Máquina, originating in Spain, particularly Valencia and later strongly developed in Catalonia, shared a very similar spirit with Gabber. Musically, it was more melodic and epic, yet almost as powerful and fast. Its aesthetic, including shaved heads and tracksuits, was also quite similar. "Este género, nacido en España, en concreto en Valencia, luego desarrollado con especial fuerza en Cataluña, compartía con el Gáber un espíritu muy parecido."
**Working-Class Adoption and Political Stigma** : Música Máquina was adopted by working-class sectors in Spain and, similar to Gabber, was identified with political extremism. This association contributed to reinforcing its public stigma. "la máquina fue adoptada por sectores de clase trabajadora, y también fue un colectivo al que se identificó con el extremismo político, lo que reforzó su estigma público."

# concert - Rave

## genres
House
Acid House
Hardcore

## curiosities
**Early UK Rave Culture (Late 1980s)** : The first raves in the United Kingdom, particularly during the "Second Summer of Love" in the late 1980s, were characterized by an "empathetic and hedonistic spirit" imported by DJs from clubs like Nesea. These parties primarily featured Chicago House and Acid House, mixed with local productions, with participants dancing for hours at 120 BPMs, often under the influence of ecstasy, aiming for a trance state while bars served only water. "Esas primeras raves eran muy diferentes a lo que asociamos hoy en día a una rave."
**Evolution and European Expansion (Early 1990s)** : Within a few years, the rave scene transitioned from an underground movement to a mass phenomenon, expanding across Europe to countries like Germany, the Netherlands, Belgium, and Spain. This growth brought changes, including the introduction of alcohol and new drugs such as amphetamine (femtamina/Westpith), which offered a cheaper and more nervous effect compared to ecstasy. "En pocos años la Rave pasó de ese de un movimiento underground a convertirse en un fenómeno de masas."

# concert - Thunderdome

## genres
Gabber
Hardcore

## curiosities
**Foundational Gabber and Hardcore Macro-Party (1992)** : Thunderdome originated in 1992 as a series of large-scale parties held in pavilions and stadiums across the Netherlands. It became a focal point for the most extreme Gabber and hardcore sounds in the country, defining the early scene. "Thunderdon nació en 1992 como una serie de macrofistas en pavillones y estadios, y puso al foco del Gáber y el hardcore más extremo del país."
**Commercial Success and Icon Status** : Many Thunderdome events were recorded and commercialized, and compilation CDs of their parties were widely distributed across Europe. The Thunderdome logo eventually achieved recognition comparable to any major 1990s pop icon. "llegó un momento en el que luego de Thunderdon era casi tan reconocible como cualquier iconopop de los 90."
**Contemporary Revival Events (2017, 2022-Present)** : After several years of inactivity, Thunderdome returned in 2017 to celebrate its 25th anniversary. In 2022, it celebrated its "teneta aniversario Nutrek" with all tickets sold out. Due to growing demand, there is now an annual Thunderdome event, demonstrating a strong revival. "ThunderDome volvió en 2017 para celebrar su 25 aniversario. En 2022, celebro su teneta aniversario Nutrek, con todas las entradas agotadas, y ahora ya cada año hay un evento debido al crecimiento rival."

# instrument - TR-909

## curiosities
**Core of Gabber Sound** : The classic kick drum sound from the TR-909 rhythm box was a fundamental element in the creation of Gabber music. Producers would significantly accelerate this sound to 180-190 or even 200 BPMs and heavily distort it, transforming it into the genre's characteristic "sonic hammer." "cogieron el clásico bombo de la caja de ritmos TR-9-09, lo aceleraron hasta los 180, 190, o incluso 200 BPMs. Y lo distorsionaron hasta convertirlo en un martillo sónico."

# curiosity

**Segundo Verano del Amor (Second Summer of Love)** : This refers to one of the most significant cultural revolutions in history, which erupted in the United Kingdom in the late 1980s. It marked the beginning of modern rave culture and a period of collective hedonism and empathy. "A finales de los años 80 se vivió una de las revoluciones culturales más importantes de la historia, el llamado Segundo Verano de la More explotó en el Reino Unido."
**Early Rave Culture Distinctions** : Early raves, influenced by an empathetic and hedonistic spirit from clubs like Nesea, were distinct from later iterations. Attendees focused on dancing and letting go, not on dating or showing off. Bars typically served only water, and the then-new substance Ecstasy fostered a sense of collective empathy, enabling dancers to reach a true state of trance for hours at 120 BPMs. "En aquellas fiestas la gente no iba a ligar ni alucirse, sino bailar y dejarse llevar."
**Rave Scene Fracturing (1993)** : By 1993, the rave scene had grown so immense that it began to fragment. New "tribes" emerged within the movement, each demanding faster, harder, and more radical sounds, which ultimately led to the diversification into various hardcore genres across Europe. "Para 1993 la escena era ya tan grande que empezó a fracturarse."
**Dutch Electronic Scene Division (Early 1990s)** : In the early 1990s, the Dutch music scene was distinctly divided between Amsterdam, known for its more artistic and sophisticated club culture, and Rotterdam. The industrial, port city of Rotterdam viewed Amsterdam's scene as "precious" and felt unrepresented, seeking a cruder, more honest, and unadorned sound that captured their working-class, industrial spirit. "En Amsterdam había una cultura jajos más artística y podríamos decir sofisticada. En Rotterdam... no se sentían identificados con ella."
**Gabber Youth Culture Aesthetic** : Gabber youth, predominantly boys but with a notable presence of girls from working-class backgrounds, adopted a distinctive and unmistakable aesthetic. This included shaved heads, colorful Nike and Australia brand tracksuits, and Air Max sneakers, which became a visible expression of their identity. "Un identidad que se expresaba con una estética inconfundible, cabezas rapadas, chandas coloridos Nike, Australia, las patillas Air Max."
**The Haken Dance - Gabber's Signature Move** : The Haken was the unique dance of the Gabber scene, characterized by a straight, almost rigid upper body while the legs executed frenetic patterns to the music's inhuman speed. The essence of the dance was not technique or grace, but rather endurance and resistance, demonstrating the ability to keep pace with the extremely fast music. "El cuerpo permanecía recto, casi rígido, mientras las piernas dibujaban patrones frenéticos a ritmo de la música, más que la técnica o la gracia, lo que importaba era aguantar, resistir, demostrar que podía seguir la velocidad inhumana de la música."
**Gabber's Fascination with Dark Imagery** : Visuals from the Gabber era, particularly flyers, revealed a widespread fascination with terror and darkness. This aesthetic, featuring skulls, demons, and witches (most notably the Wizard of Thunderdome), captivated 16-year-olds as a way to engage with the forbidden and the obscure. "los flaños de la época muestran a la fascinación del general hacia el terror y los curo."
**Gabber's Association with Far-Right Elements** : The association of Gabber culture with the far-right was partly attributed to the shaved head aesthetic adopted by some participants. While there were indeed neo-Nazi groups present, for the majority of young Gabbers, there was no political agenda; their "politics and religion was the party." "Todo esto fue en parte por la estética de cabeza rapada que adoptaron, y sí, es cierto que había grupos neonacis, pero para la mayoría de los jóvenes no había política detrás."
**Gabber as a Lifelong Identity** : The strong negative perception from mainstream society ironically became a "seal of authenticity" for many Gabbers. Being disliked by society affirmed their genuine belonging to something significant, leading to the saying that "a Gabber is for life," indicating it was a deep-rooted identity, not a fleeting trend. "ser odiado por el resto de la sociedad significaba que, realmente, estabas dentro de algo, y es por eso que se dice que un Gáber lo es para toda la vida."
**Gabber's Commodification and Market Oversaturation** : As the music industry absorbed Gabber, it became a mass-market product. Television aired numerous ads for "soulless" compilation CDs, and the market became oversaturated, even extending to hardcore versions of mainstream pop hits. This commercialization, coupled with its already poor public image, contributed to Gabber's near demise by 2000. "Al mismo tiempo, la industria musical absorbió al género y lo convertió en un producto de masas."
**Haken Dance Revival and Viral Trend** : The Haken dance, once a marginal cultural element, has experienced a resurgence in popularity, becoming as widespread as a Fortnite dance, especially through TikToks and Reels. Many engage with it from a "huge social and cultural distance," primarily appreciating its aesthetic exaggeration and viral potential. "Lo de Jaken se ha vuelto en alocura, un baile marginal ha llegado a ser tan popular como, no sé, un baile de Fortnite."
