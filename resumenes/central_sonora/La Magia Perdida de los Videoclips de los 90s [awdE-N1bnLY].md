# artist - Elvis

## curiosities

# curiosity

**The Videoclip as a Medium** : Initially conceived as a short film to accompany a musical composition primarily to boost record sales, the videoclip evolved significantly in the 1990s and early 2000s, becoming a powerful audiovisual medium with a profound impact on youth and serving as inspiration for many film directors. "El videoclip se podría definir como un corte metrájeca compaña una composición musical creado originalmente para favorecer la venta de discos. Pero los años 90 y a principios de los 2000, esa definición se quedó corta."
**Origins of the Videoclip** : While the creation of MTV is commonly associated with the birth of the videoclip, the format predates the channel. Films featuring artists like Elvis and The Beatles, particularly "Let It Be," established many of the visual standards, such as camera plane changes matching rhythm and embedding small stories within performances. "El nacimiento del videoclip se suele asociar con la creación del canal MTV. Pero el videoclip de existía antes."
**Pop Clips - 70s US Program** : In the 1970s in the United States, a program called "Pop Clips" pioneered the broadcasting of videoclips, specifically from the Warner catalog, to promote their artists. The success and effective functioning of this program directly inspired the concept of a television channel dedicated entirely to 24-hour music video programming. "El buen funcionamiento de este programa inspiró a la creación, nunca en el de televisión que programas en las 24 horas del día videoclips."
**Birth of MTV (1981)** : The success of 1970s programs like "Pop Clips" in showcasing music videos directly led to the conceptualization and launch of MTV in 1981, a channel dedicated to broadcasting videoclips 24 hours a day, thus marking a new era for the medium. "Era 1981, y ahora sí, nacía MTV."
**90s Videoclip Golden Age** : The 1990s represented a magical and unrepeatable period for videoclips, driven by the era's musical diversity across genres like grunge, alternative rock, and electronic music. Record labels, seeing the clips' broad appeal, invested heavily and granted artists substantial creative freedom in selecting directors, creating a high demand for short filmmakers, cinema students, and audiovisual artists. "Viendo a la buena agujera que estaba manteniendo esos clips hacia un público que abarcaba muchos gustos, las discográficas destinaban cada vez más recursos a ellos y dejaban la tiba en libertad a los artistas para elegir el director de su videoclip."
**Michel Gondry - Director Profile** : Michel Gondry is hailed as arguably the most imaginative director of his generation, possessing a highly creative and visually innovative vision. He is a master of techniques like stop motion and practical effects, employing optical illusions to create unique and surreal visual worlds with a distinct "homemade" approach. He also pioneered the "bullet time" technique, later famously used in films like *Matrix*, and transitioned into feature films with works such as *Human Nature* (2001) and *Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind*. "Es seguramente el director más imaginativo de su generación, pues si una visión altamente creativa y visualmente innovadora, son manejos de las ilusiones apticas dan como resultado un mundo visual surrealista y unítico único."
**Spike Jonze - Director Profile** : Spike Jonze's distinctive style is described as fluid and documentary-like, stemming from his background in skate videos, which imbues his works with an "amateur" finish. He is known for exploring profound themes of human emotions and connections, consistently incorporating a humorous style. Beyond directing, he is one of the creators of *Jackass* and has successfully ventured into cinema, notably winning an Oscar for Best Original Screenplay for *Her*, in addition to directing *Being John Malkovich*. "Va ser un estilo, Doiddjullseth, que era donde su tapa de foto era fuido con mentalista de videos de skate, cosa que la torga a sus vídeos una acabado a mater."
**Chris Cunningham - Director Profile** : The most enigmatic of the acclaimed directors, Chris Cunningham began his career creating animatronics and prosthetics for films like *J.D.D.* and *Alien 3* (the Fincher film). His videoclip work, starting with Aphex Twin's "Tecres" for Warp Records, is characterized by a disturbing style, creating dystopian worlds where organic elements blend with mechanical ones. Although he was long rumored to direct an adaptation of William Gibson's *Neuromancer*, the project never materialized, and he maintains a lower profile compared to his peers today. "A raíz de su experiencia con los animatronicos, crean mundos distópicos donde el orgánico se mezcla con lo mecánico."
**Decline of the Videoclip** : The videoclip medium suffered a significant decline from its past splendor, primarily due to piracy forcing record labels to reinvent themselves and scale back videoclip production. This, combined with the general decline of traditional television and the rise of social media platforms with short-form videos like TikTok, led to a redistribution of economic resources towards more profitable alternatives, as videoclips lost their prominence in television programming, with MTV even abandoning them for reality shows and YouTube failing to generate expected traffic. "La peratería forzó a las discográficas a reinventarse y una de las series afectadas fue la producción de videoclips."
**The "Videoclipero" Term** : During the golden, intense period of videoclip production, the term "videoclipero" was ironically used in a pejorative sense, despite the fact that this era served as an excellent breeding ground for future acclaimed film directors. "Ahora me río cuando he recuerdo que en esa época, el término videoclipero se usaba de forma pejorativa."
**Videoclip Directors as Film Talent** : The intense, magical period of videoclip creation served as a remarkable nursery for cinematic talent, producing a cohort of directors who went on to become major figures in film, including David Fincher, Spike Jonze, Michel Gondry, Tarsem Singh, Anton Corbijn, and Jonathan Glazer. "Este periodo nos vende una de las mejores canteras de cine, con autores como David Fincher, Spike Jones, Michelle Gondrie, Tarsam Singh, Anton Korming o Jonathan Glazer."
