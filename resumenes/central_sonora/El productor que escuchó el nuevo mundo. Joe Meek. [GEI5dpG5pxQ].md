## albums
**I Hear A New World (1960) - Conceptual Space Album** : Conceived and produced by Joe Meek in 1960, this album is possibly the first conceptual rock album in history. It presents a musical fantasy about outer space, heavily employing echoes, reverbs, and pitch modulation of voices to simulate alien sounds. It also uniquely uses modified household sounds to evoke the vastness of space, and this album significantly predated the later success of Space Music. "Es el mismo año mi concebió y produjo a Hira New World, posiblemente el primer disco conceptual de la historia del rock, una fantasía musical sobre el espacio exterior, repleta de ecos, reverbs, modulación del pitch de las voces para simular la voz extraterrestre o el uso de sonidos caseros modificados para simular el espacio."

## songs
**Johnny's Remember Me (1961)** : Produced by Joe Meek, this song, sung by John Leyton, became Meek's first number one hit in 1961. The track tells the story of a young man being pursued by his deceased lover, and it features Meek's characteristic unsettling sound, which is accentuated by ghostly, premonitory female voices forming the chorus. The soprano Lisa Gray famously recorded her part from the studio bathroom to achieve this specific phantasmagorical effect. "En 1961 logró por fin su primer número 1 con el tema Johnny's Remain Burmick, cantado por John Layton, una canción que relata la persecución de un joven por parte de su amante muerta."
**Telstar (1962)** : Produced by Joe Meek and interpreted by The Tornados, "Telstar" became Meek's biggest success in 1962. This song achieved a significant milestone by becoming the first British song to reach number one in the United States, thereby predating the broader phenomenon of the British Invasion. Its distinct main melody was played on a clavioline, identified as a primitive synthesizer. "En 1962 produce su mayor éxito, Del Star, interpretada por detornados. La canción fue todo unito al convertirse en el primer número 1 en Estados Unidos, proveniente de Inglaterra, adelantándose también a la British Invasion."
**Angela Jones (Year Not Specified)** : Produced by Joe Meek for his own Triumph Records label, this song came very close to becoming a number one hit. Although it peaked at number 7 on the English charts, Meek believed it could have achieved higher success. However, its potential was limited by the small pressing plants of the time which were unable to satisfy the unexpected demand. "Con su propia disco gráfica, estuvo a punto de conseguir un número 1 con Angela Jones, pero las pequeñas plantas de prensado no pueden satisfacer la demanda. La canción llegó al número 7 en las listas inglesas, aunque él creía que podía haber llegado más alto."
**Night of the Vampire (Year Not Specified)** : Produced by Joe Meek for The Demon Trackers, this song serves as an example of Meek's fascination with dark and esoteric themes. It is recognized as one of the earliest demonstrations of what would later become known as Gothic Rock. "Esa fascinación por los curos y los otéricos se podrá apreciar en canciones como Night of the Vampire, de Demontrackers, o Till the Fall of the Wind Knight, de Screaming Loves Hatch, siendo una de las primeras muestras de lo que luego sería conocido como Roc Gotico."
**Till the Fall of the Wind Knight (Year Not Specified)** : Produced by Joe Meek for Screaming Lord Sutch, this track illustrates Meek's interest in the obscure and the occult. Alongside other works, it is considered one of the pioneering examples of what would later develop into Gothic Rock. "Esa fascinación por los curos y los otéricos se podrá apreciar en canciones como Night of the Vampire, de Demontrackers, o Till the Fall of the Wind Knight, de Screaming Loves Hatch, siendo una de las primeras muestras de lo que luego sería conocido como Roc Gotico."

## curiosities
**Early Electronic Aptitude** : Born in 1923 in Newwind, England, Joe Meek was said to have built the first television in his neighborhood as a child. He reportedly assembled it from electronic components scavenged from his neighbors' appliances, showcasing an early and undeniable talent for gadgets and electronics. "Se cuenta que Joe Mick nacido en 1923 en Newwind, en la Tierra, creó el primer televisor de su vecindario a base de componentes electrónicos que acumulaba de aparatos de sus vecinos cuando era tan solo un niño."
**RAF Radar Technician** : During his military service, Joe Meek served as a radar technician in the Royal Air Force (RAF). This experience in electronics and technology further deepened his interest in both the field of electronics and the concept of space. "cuando hizo el servicio militar en la RAF, fue técnico en radar, documentos interés en la electrónica y el espacio."
**Pioneer of Electronic Rock** : Joe Meek is widely acknowledged as the pioneer of electronic rock, revolutionizing the recording market from his home studio. He achieved this by inventing groundbreaking recording techniques and creating unique sonic universes that set him apart from his contemporaries. "Considerado por muchas como el pionero del rock electrónico, este hombre, desde su estudio casero, revolucionará al mercado discográfico creando nuevas técnicas de grabación y universos sonados únicos."
**Non-Musician Producer** : Despite his profound influence on music, Joe Meek famously could not play any instruments, nor could he read or write music. To compose, he would verbally convey his musical ideas, often by humming or singing, to a musician who would then interpret his precise directions. "Y todo ello, sin saber tocar un instrumento."
**Innovative Recording Techniques** : Meek was renowned for his unorthodox production methods, which included the exaggerated use of self-made compressors, direct bass input without an amplifier, sampling, and extensive overdubbing on one or two-track recorders to craft new and complex textures. He also utilized a distinctive technique of recording songs at slower speeds and then accelerating them during production to achieve his unique sound. "donde destacó por sus técnicas poportodoxas, como el uso exagerado de compresores que el mismo fabricaba, la entrada directa de bajo sin amplificador, el sampling, o el sobredoblaje en grabadoras de una o dos pistas para crear nuevas texturas."
**Studio as an Instrument** : Joe Meek was a trailblazer in using the recording studio itself as an instrument. He manipulated the studio's capabilities to shape and transform sounds and textures in highly innovative ways, effectively making the studio an integral part of the creative process, despite his own lack of formal musical training. "Pese a esa carencia, fue piano yero en usar el estudio de grabación como instrumento más."
**Production Company and Studio Location** : Joe Meek established a production company initially named James Hound, which later became known as Mixville Sound. His studio was uniquely located within his residence, situated in a three-story building in London, directly above a leather shop. This shop was managed by his landlady, Violet Shenton, who also owned the property. "La sexta experiencia creó la empresa de producción de James Hound, más tarde conocida como Mixville Sound, la cual hubi con su vivienda, en un edificio de tres plantas en Londres sobre una tienda la cual estaba rejentada por Valle le Chentón, su casera."
**Obsession with the Dead and Occult** : Joe Meek harbored a deep fascination with communicating with the dead. He would install tape recorders in cemeteries, hoping to capture voices from the afterlife, and once believed a cat's meow was a human-toned plea for help. He was also profoundly obsessed with Buddy Holly, who had died in a plane crash, claiming to communicate with him in dreams. "Amiglio fascinaba la idea de comunicarse con los muertos, instalaba grabadoras de cinta a los cementerios con la esperanza de capturar voces de ultratumba."
**Dealing with Landlady's Complaints** : During recording sessions, when his landlady, Violet Shenton, would complain about the noise by banging on his ceiling with a broom, Meek would retaliate. His response was to place speakers on the stairwell and significantly increase the volume, aiming to annoy her further. "Durante esas grabaciones, cuando sus caseros creían que había hecho mucho ruido, golpeaban su techo con una espoba. El respondía poniendo los altavoces en las caleras, subiendo el volumen para molestar más."
**Disbelief in The Beatles' Talent** : Prior to their global fame, Brian Epstein, a friend of Meek, presented a demo tape of a nascent band called The Beatles to Joe Meek, seeking his advice on whether to represent them. Meek famously dismissed their talent, advising Epstein not to pursue them. "Brian Epstein, amigo de Mick, demostró una maqueta de una banda que estaba empezando para pedirle consejos sobre si representarle sonó. La banda se llamaba Beatles, la respuesta de Mick fue que Nisemo le estáse."
**Missed Opportunity with Rod Stewart** : Joe Meek once expressed interest in hiring a particular group but made it a condition that they dismiss their 16-year-old singer, Rod Stewart. Meek stated that he simply did not perceive any talent in the young vocalist. "En otra ocasión contrató un grupo con la condición de que se disiciaran de su cantante, al cual no le veía talento. Se trataba de rote stewar que tenía 16 años."
**Missed Opportunity with David Bowie** : Joe Meek also famously passed on the opportunity to work with a very young David Bowie. This decision, along with others, highlighted that while Meek was a genius at recording, his ability to discover new musical talent was not his strong suit. "También dejo pasar la oportunidad de trabajar con un jovenquísimo David Bowie. Talento en las grabaciones les abraba, pero descubrir músicos no era su fuerte."
**Independent Producer Success** : Despite operating as an independent producer, Joe Meek achieved remarkable success. He worked on 245 singles, with an impressive 45 of these reaching the Top 50 on the charts, a considerable feat for any independent producer. "Trabajó en 245 sencillos, 45 de los cuales alcanzaron el Top 50 para ser un productor independiente no está nada mal."
**Decline in Relevance post-Beatles** : The meteoric rise and widespread popularity of The Beatles significantly impacted Joe Meek's career. After their emergence, his distinct productions began to be perceived as old-fashioned and outdated. His last number one hit was achieved in 1964. "Pero la fe de Pondos Beatles hizo que sus producciones fueran listas como antiguas y pasadas de moda. Su último número 1 lo lograría en 1964."
**Mental Health Deterioration and Paranoia** : Joe Meek's mental health underwent a severe decline, leading him to experience psychotic delusions. He began to believe his flat was inhabited by poltergeists and that his mind was being controlled by extraterrestrials. In one instance, he furiously hung up on Phil Spector, accusing him of stealing his musical ideas, and was convinced that his landlady had installed microphones in his home with the same intention. "El deterioro de esos salud mental fue creciendo. Empezó a creer que en su piso había Paul Tergay's y que los extraterrestres controlaban su mente. En una ocasión recibió una llamada de Phil Spector, Mick le colgó un farado acusándole de robarle sus ideas."
**Further Delusions and Repressed Sexuality** : In his final years, Joe Meek's psychotic delusions intensified. He refused to use the telephone, convinced that his landlady was listening to his calls and mentally controlling the musicians in his studio. This combination of commercial pressure, repressed sexuality, and profound paranoia severely impacted his already fragile mental health. "En sus últimos años, Mick empezó a experimentar de lídios psicóticos. Se negabó a usar el teléfono ya que creía que su casera escuchaba sus llamadas y controlaba la mente de los músicos del estudio."
**Murder-Suicide** : On February 3, 1967, Joe Meek committed a tragic act, killing his landlady, Violet Shenton, before taking his own life. This devastating event followed an argument between them regarding noise levels and a significant amount of unpaid back rent that Meek owed her. "El 3 de febrero de 1967, Mick mataba su casera Vallele Chentón, luego un psiquito la vida. Ese día habían discutido por los niveles de ruido y por la cantidad del que le des atrasados que aún le bebía."
**Posthumous Recognition** : Following his death, Joe Meek's immense contributions to music largely faded into obscurity until the 1990s, when his figure as an innovative musician and producer began to be re-evaluated. In 2014, the English magazine NME (New Musical Express) named Meek the most important producer of all time, placing him above highly revered figures such as Phil Spector, Gwen Cillons (Glyn Johns), George Martin, and Rick Rubin. "Tras un muerte cayó en el olvido hasta los años 90, cuando empezaron a valorar su figura como músico y productor. En 2014, la revista inglesa a Neil Musical Express y leció a Mick como el productor más importante de todos los tiempos. Por encima de Phil Spector, Gwen Cillons, George Martin o Rick Rubin."
**Modern Influence** : Joe Meek's innovations were so influential and personal that many contemporary audio plugins and processors bear his name, serving as a direct testament to his lasting impact and the continued relevance of his unique recording techniques in modern music production. "Sus innovaciones fueron tan influyentes y personales que muchos plugins y procesadores de audio actuales llevan su nombre."
