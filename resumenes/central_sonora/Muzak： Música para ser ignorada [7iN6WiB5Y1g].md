# artist - Brian Eno
## albums
**Music for Airports (1978)** : This conceptual album, released in 1978, saw Brian Eno coin the term "ambient music" and create a record that, while appearing to be Muzak, was not simply intended as background music. Eno's vision for ambient music was for it to be adaptable to various levels of auditory attention, capable of being both ignorable and interesting without imposing itself. "La música ambiental debe poder adaptarse a muchos niveles de atención auditiva, sin imponer o en particular. Debe ser tan ignorable como interesante."
## curiosities

"Vayanero coño el término también para la música, creando en apariencia un disco de Muzak, el cual no está creado para sonar simplemente de fondo."

# artist - Erik Satie

His intention was for this music to blend into daily life, serving to mask ordinary sounds like the clatter of cutlery during a meal, fill awkward silences, and generally become part of the ambient environment. "Eric Satilla pensó algo similar hace más de 100 años. ## curiosities
**Characteristics and Reception** : Easy Listening, also known as "Isleysening" or "música ligera," is a genre of instrumental music designed to be relaxing. It incorporates elements from jazz, swing, Latin music, or folk, but unlike jazz, it is intended to remain in a secondary plane, not demanding the listener's full attention. This characteristic led many critics and listeners to question its artistic merit, often categorizing it as mere entertainment, despite the contributions of ingenious arrangers and directors like Martin Denny, Les Baxter, and Esquivel. "La música que creaba y distribuía la empresa era similar a Lise Lysening o la música ligera. # genre - Muzak
## curiosities
**Origin as a Corporate Strategy** : The company Muzak, initially Guárez Radio, pivoted from home electrical wiring music distribution to specializing in meticulously programmed background music for commercial spaces. This music, often composed anonymously and devoid of expressive human elements, was designed in laboratories for specific functions, such as influencing productivity or consumer behavior, deliberately avoiding the complexities and demands of "real" music. "Más con una manifestación auténtica de la expresión artística, el Muzak se rige como una copia hyperrealista, una proyección idealizada y neutra de la música, como los son los parques temáticos de los lugares que representan."
**Progressive Stimuli Concept (1950s)** : In the 1950s, as the Muzak company expanded into diverse venues like medical offices, hair salons, shopping centers, and factories, it introduced the concept of "estímulos progresivos." This involved a series of music blocks, typically 15 minutes long, with gradually increasing intensity, followed by 15 minutes of silence, aimed at boosting worker productivity and stimulating consumerism. "Una serie de música diseñada para aumentar la productividad de los trabajadores y el consumismo entre los clientes, que consistían elevar gradualmente la intensidad de la música."
**Association with Technology and Predictability** : With the advent of synthesizers and electronic music, the concept of Muzak became even more popularized as "elevator music." The inherent "cold" quality of computer-generated music and the absence of human interpretation aligned perfectly with Muzak's standardized and predictable nature, further reinforcing its image as artificial and uniform sound in the digital age. "El elemento frío que aporta la música por computadora y el hecho de prescindir del componente humano en interpretación encajaron de manera peculiar con la naturaleza estandalizada y predecible del Muzak."
**Ubiquity in Modern Media** : The term "Muzak" has expanded beyond its original corporate context, commonly heard in documentaries, educational videos, corporate presentations, and even video games. The adoption of the CD format for game composers facilitated the use of "imperceptible synthesized sounds," making it an ideal choice for background music in games lacking a strong narrative. "El término Muzak se estendió y ya me da raro o irlo en documentales, vídeos educativos o corporativos."

# genre - Mallsoft
## curiosities
**Atmosphere of Commercial Spaces** : Mallsoft is a subgenre designed to evoke the ambiance of large, bustling shopping centers, characterized by expansive spaces and nebulous atmospheres. Artists frequently incorporate field recordings from actual commercial spaces to enhance this sound, creating a dreamlike and hazy environment that often expresses nostalgia for the popular shopping malls and consumer culture of the 1980s and 1990s. "El Malsop está diseño para aparecerse al ambiente de cientos comerciales grandes y llenos de gente, con espacios enormes de atmóseras nebulosas, incorporando a veces grabaciones de campo de cientos comerciales reales para lograr aún más ese sonido."

# label - Guárez Radio
## curiosities
**Precursor to Muzak Company (1934)** : Founded by Yotchow Goodesquayer, Guárez Radio was an American company established in 1934 that initially focused on distributing music through electrical wiring to homes. In the same year, fascinated by the name Kodak, Goodesquayer decided to rename the company to Muzak, a portmanteau of "música" and "Kodak." "En 1934, Guárez Radio, una empresa que se dedicaba a llevar la música a través del cableado eléctrico de los hogares estadounidenses, decide cambiarse un nombre a Muzak."

# label - Muzak
## curiosities
**Name Origin and Evolution** : The company's name, Muzak, was adopted in 1934, derived by its founder Yotchow Goodesquayer who fused the first syllable of "música" with the second syllable of "Kodak." Initially, Muzak provided music via electrical wiring to homes, but as radio gained prominence, the company shifted its focus to registering and commercializing background music specifically designed for hotels and restaurants, marking its transition into a pervasive commercial sound provider. "Su fundador, Yotchow Goodesquayer, fascinado por el nombre de Codak, fusionó la primera silabra de la palabra música con la segunda de Codak."
**Executive Perspective on Silence** : Navi, a director at the Muzak company, famously articulated the company's core mission by stating that its biggest competitor was silence itself. This perspective underscores Muzak's objective to fill auditory voids and prevent discomfort, aiming to provide a constant, familiar, and friendly sonic environment that averts perceived awkwardness or threat from silence. "Como dijo Navi, son directivo de la empresa, el mayor competidor de Muzak es el silencio."

# curiosity
**Origin of "Elevator Music" Term** : The term "música de ascensor" (elevator music) is believed to have originated from the early skyscrapers, where people often felt fear while riding in the metallic cages ascending to great heights. To alleviate this anxiety, a musical thread was installed, playing pleasant and tranquil melodies to soothe passengers during their ascent. "Se dice que se llama música de ascensor, por cantivamente, en los primeros rascapielos, a la gente le daba miero mientras he sacado las metálicas que subían tan alto."
**Muzak in Space Travel (NASA)** : Muzak's calming properties were even utilized by NASA during early space travels. The music was employed to help soothe and relax astronauts, demonstrating its perceived effectiveness in managing stress and creating a tranquil environment in unusual and high-pressure situations. "En esa época se llegó a usar incluso los viajes espaciales de lana-sa para calmar a los astronautas."
**The Sims Game Soundtrack** : The popular video game "The Sims" successfully captures the "way of life" characteristic of 1950s and 1960s America through its soundtrack, which is described as "pure Muzak." The game utilizes this style of background music to avoid interfering with the gameplay experience, immersing players in a setting where unobtrusive, ambient sound is central to the atmosphere. "Midejuegos como los Sims consigue capturar ese guay of life propia de la América de los años 50 y 60 con una banda sonora que es puro Muzak."
**Modern Society's Fear of Silence** : Contemporary society exhibits a widespread aversion to silence, fueling a constant need for stimuli to occupy thoughts. Music has transformed into a kind of comfort blanket, mitigating an "auditory vacuum" and becoming a necessity for everyday activities such as working, commuting on the subway, or even mundane tasks like taking out the trash. "Hoy en día nos angúste al silencio y necesitamos constantes estímulos para hogar nuestros pensamientos."
**Music as a Universal Background** : Music has become so deeply intertwined with modern existence that its absence would now feel strange. It serves as an omnipresent backdrop, from specially composed or carefully chosen library music in YouTube videos and podcasts to a general hum that fills every moment, making silence a rare and precious commodity. "Se ha entrelazado de tal manera con nuestras vidas que la ausencia de su eco podría apreciernos extraña."
**CD Format and Video Game Music** : The adoption of the CD as a format in video game development was crucial for composers, as it enabled the use of "imperceptibly synthesized sounds" (sonidos impercentitizados). This made the CD an ideal medium for background music, particularly in games lacking a strong narrative, where the goal was to provide an immersive sonic environment without distracting from the gameplay experience. "El hecho de adoptar el CD como formato permitió a los compositores de videojuegos usar esos sonidos impercentitizados, siendo un escapar ideal para la música de fondo, especialmente un juego sin traman narrativa."
