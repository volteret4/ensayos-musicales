# artist - K.D.K.in

## member of
Los Corredores del Bloque
PXXR GANG
La Mafia del Amor

## songs
**No me identifico (2011)** : This YouTube track was published on April 14, 2011, by the rapper from Comet. With this song, K.D.K.in made it clear that he did not identify with the existing Spanish hip-hop scene and definitively detached himself from it. "Con ello, ponía de manifiesto que no se se se enciera identificado con esa escena y se deslegaba definitivamente de ella."
**Perdedores del barrio** : This song was K.D.K.in's swift response to C. Tangana's track "No te pegarás" from the album "Ídolo." K.D.K.in uploaded the video just a few days after C. Tangana's diss, delivering a famous line and reminding C. Tangana that he had no style before becoming famous. "Caíde caí, tardó tan solo unos días en subir el videoperdedores del barrio, donde les soltó la frase mítica. Y le recordó que antes de ser famoso, no tenía estiva."

## curiosities
**Move to El Raval** : After friendly encounters between Los Corredores del Bloque and Kefta Boyz in Barcelona, K.D.K.in left Madrid and settled in El Raval. This neighborhood functioned as the "true Spanish trap house" and was where he co-founded the band PXXR GANG with John Biff, Khaled, and Steve Lean. "K.D.K.in de jamas, R.I.T. y se instalan todos en el rival, que funcionará como la verdadera trap house española."
**Confrontation with Enry-K** : During a concert in 2014, K.D.K.in slapped Enry-K, a member of the group N.E.N.O. DE CLICCO. The tension arose because Enry-K reportedly stopped speaking to K.D.K.in after the latter gained fame, despite their previous collaborations. "La atención estalló cuando Caíde Caín les soltó un guantazo a Enwise, uno de sus miembros."
**Spaghetti Video After Confrontation** : The day after the confrontation with Enry-K, K.D.K.in and John Biff uploaded a video to YouTube calmly explaining the slap while eating a plate of spaghetti. This incident became a legendary moment in Spanish trap history. "Al día siguiente, Caíde y Unbef subieron un video explicando el tortazo con total calma mientras se comían un plato de espaguetis."
**"No te pegarás" Diss from C. Tangana** : C. Tangana indirectly challenged PXXR GANG and K.D.K.in in his song "No te pegarás" from the album "Ídolo," criticizing them for repetitive ideas. K.D.K.in quickly responded with his own video, "Perdedores del barrio." "C tangana tiraba una pollita a la purga en cuando dice, o si vais a quedar con todo, lo repetíáis como los horos, todo lo mismo como bobos."
**Artistic and Commercial Differences at Primavera Sound 2018** : At the Primavera Sound festival in 2018, K.D.K.in participated in a discussion with C. Tangana and Bad Gyal. This debate highlighted significant artistic, commercial, and ideological differences between K.D.K.in (representing Granada's street-oriented trap) and C. Tangana (representing Madrid's more business-minded approach). "Y se dejaron patentes las diferencias de criterio artístico, comercial y diológico ante el madrileño y el gran adino."

## albums
**Los Pobres (2015) - Sony Music Debut** : PXXR GANG signed a contract with Sony Music in 2015 to release this single album. According to them, they sent Sony a handful of songs they considered mediocre, intending to mock the label and cash the check. "Según ellos, enviaron a Sony un puñado de canciones que consideraban medio crees para reírse de la discográfica y cobrar el cheque."
**Mixtape Gratuito (2015)** : Shortly after the release of "Los Pobres" with Sony Music, PXXR GANG launched a free mixtape. This act was seen as unthinkable if they had signed an exclusivity contract with the label, further underscoring their rebellious approach to the music industry. "A los pocos días lanzaron un mixtape gratuito, algo intensable si hubiese firmado un contrato de exclusividad con ellos."

## curiosities
**Foundation in El Raval** : PXXR GANG was founded in El Raval, Barcelona, by John Biff, Khaled, K.D.K.in, and Steve Lean. The group's formation in this neighborhood, which they called the "true Spanish trap house," was a pivotal moment for the Spanish trap scene. "Y una vez allí, junto a Steve Lin, fundan la banda que lo cambiará todo, la pur gang."
**Legend of Cecilio G's Involvement** : Legend has it that Cecilio G, a chaotic figure, was an initial member of PXXR GANG and gave the group its name as a parody of Rich Gang. The veracity of this story is debated, blurring the lines between fact and myth. "La leyenda cuenta que hubo un miembro más en sus inicios, una figura caótica, la que le expuso el nombre como una parodia de la Rich Gang, el indomable Ececiliogé."
**Early Negative Reception** : Initially, PXXR GANG's videos were met with negative comments online. Viewers criticized the excessive use of Auto-Tune, accused them of misogynistic and violent lyrics, and claimed their music wasn't "real rap." "En un principio se llenaron de comentarios negativos. Haciendo referencia al exceso de Autotune, acusandoles de que tenían letras machistas y violentas, y acusandoles de no ser rap."
**Influence of 90s Aesthetics and Internet Culture** : The group, being digital natives, drew heavily from 90s aesthetics, internet culture, and video games. They blended these influences with street aesthetics, connecting deeply with urban youth. "Se inspiraron mucho en la estética de los años 90, la cultura de internet y los videojuegos."
**Connection to Vaporwave and Street Culture** : PXXR GANG adopted and popularized a specific aesthetic that combined elements of vaporwave (previously popularized by Yung Lean and Sad Boys) with gritty street culture. This blend allowed them to connect with a wider audience in Spanish neighborhoods. "Esa estética con referencias al vape orwave, la habían puesto de moda John Lin su colectivo asadvois, pero la pur gang logró conectar más con la comentitud de los barrios al mezclar, todo esto con lo que a llegero."
**Glorification of Street Hustle** : Their aesthetic glorified "Joseo," which refers to the act of finding ways to make money, whether through music or street hustling. This narrative resonated with their audience. "Esa es la glorificación de Joséo, de buscarte la vida y generar dinero como sea, ya sea con la música o con el trapicheo."
**Digital Distribution Strategy** : PXXR GANG leveraged the internet, abandoning physical formats and using YouTube to monetize their tracks directly. They understood social media and recognized the music video as a crucial format, producing material often filmed with mobile phones in the street. "Entendían perfectamente a las redes, y sabían que el videoclip era un formato clave."
**Confrontation with N.E.N.O. DE CLICCO** : In 2014, PXXR GANG and N.E.N.O. DE CLICCO clashed at a concert, leading to a physical altercation where K.D.K.in slapped Enry-K. The next day, K.D.K.in and John Biff posted a legendary video calmly explaining the incident while eating spaghetti. "Los espaguetis. En 2014, la purga niem de clicco incidieron en un concierto."
**Reconciliation with Cecilio G** : Despite a long-standing "war" with their former associate Cecilio G, which played out on Instagram stories and DMs, PXXR GANG and Cecilio G eventually reconciled. "Con el tiempo la purga no y Cecilio G se han reconciliado y me alegro."
**Performance with Salsa Combo at Primavera Sound** : PXXR GANG had a memorable performance at festivals like Sónar and Primavera Sound, where they played some of their most iconic songs with a salsa combo. "donde tiene un sofamoso concepto, tocando algunas de sus piezas más míticas con un combo de salsa."
**Parallel Reggaeton Project: La Mafia del Amor** : PXXR GANG also formed a parallel reggaeton project called La Mafia del Amor. This demonstrated their willingness to blend genres, mixing trap with Latin music. "la porgang de hecho formó el proyecto paraleno del regetón la mafía del amor."

## member of

## curiosities
**Legendary Initial Member of PXXR GANG** : Legend has it that Cecilio G was an initial, chaotic member of PXXR GANG and that he named the group as a parody of Rich Gang. The extent of his involvement at the very beginning is debated. "una figura caótica, la que le expuso el nombre como una parodia de la Rich Gang, el indomable Ececiliogé."
**"Rey del Bogatello"** : Cecilio G is known as the "Rey del Bogatello" (King of Bogatello), indicating his unique and often controversial status in the scene. "Cercilio G el rey del bogatello tuvo una guerra larga con sus antiguos socios, la porgan."
**Long-standing Conflict with PXXR GANG** : Cecilio G had a long "war" with his former associates, PXXR GANG. This conflict primarily played out on Instagram stories and DMs, rather than through diss tracks. "Pero no fue una guerra de canciones, sino una guerra que se vivió en historias de Instagram y Duáles."
**Reconciliation with PXXR GANG** : Over time, Cecilio G and PXXR GANG reconciled, ending their long-standing beef. "Con el tiempo la purga no y Cecilio G se han reconciliado y me alegro."
**"One of the Most Punk Figures in Spain"** : Cecilio G is described as one of the most punk figures in Spain, drawing comparisons to Eskorbuto's Alma. He was often at odds with almost everyone, leading to dozens of feuds. "Lo cierto es que Cecilio G es una de las figuras más pan de España desde yo su hijo alma de escorbuto."
**Conflict with Pinflaco and Kinder Malo** : Cecilio G also had a significant beef with Pinflaco and Kinder Malo, which led to the famous "yellow car incident." "Por nombrar otro, también acabó mal con Pinflaco y quinder malodando pie al famoso incidente del coche amarillo."
**Embrace of "The Cause" to its Consequences** : While other musicians from his generation professionalized or became fashion icons, Cecilio G fully embraced "the cause" to its ultimate consequences. This stance has repeatedly led him in and out of jail. "En la brazo alcausa, las sus últimas consecuencias, lo que le ha llevado a entrar y salir de la cárcel en las repítidas ocasiones."

# genre - Trap

## curiosities
**Emergence in the Southern US (80s-90s)** : Trap originated in various rap scenes in the peripheries of different cities in the Southern United States during the 1980s and 1990s. These scenes had a distinct philosophy and sound, independent of the dominant East and West Coast styles. "Durante los años 80 y 90, en la perifería de diferentes ciudades del sur de los Estados Unidos, surgen varias escenas de rap, con una filosofía y un sonido muy distinto al dominante de las costas este y yo este."
**Early Influences and Evolution (Late 90s - 2000s)** : By the late 1990s, influences from various Southern rap scenes (Memphis' dark sound, Houston's chopped & screwed) converged in Atlanta under the name "Dirty South." In the 2000s, this dirty mix distilled into trap, a genre that eliminated funk and soul, focusing on the hardest, most synthetic, and street-oriented elements. "A finales de los 90, todas estas influencias y la actitud chula es cada enlanta, empezaron a la chocaria mezclarse bajo un mismo nombre, el Disney House."
**Sonic Characteristics** : Trap sound was "plastic," distinct from the organic boom-bap of dominant hip-hop. It used Roland TR-808 drum machines, creating frantic triplet hi-hats and deep sub-bass instead of samples and real drums. Rhythms typically ranged from 60 to 90 BPM, often doubled for more hi-hats, accompanied by dark synth melodies and heavy Auto-Tune. "El trap directamente sonaba plástico. En vez de sample si baterías reales usaba la rola ante R08 y similares, que en lugar de marcar el ritmo lo ametrellaban con tresillos freníticos y sub graves profundos."
**Role of Autotune** : While Auto-Tune was designed for pitch correction, trap artists exaggerated its effect, deforming the human voice in a peculiar way to create an abrasive, narcotic-like sound. It was seen as anti-rap—a cold, synthetic music for a similarly cold world. "El autotune se había creado para correcir tonos a la hora de cantar, pero si los exageras, deforma la voz humana de una forma muy peculiar y así lo soltrab para que sonar al estridente y sin arcóticas."
**Etymology: "Trap Houses" of Atlanta** : The genre's name comes from the "trap houses" in Atlanta—abandoned houses where crack cocaine was cooked and sold 24/7. This origin reflects the genre's lyrical content, which often chronicled street dealings. "El género tomó el nombre de las trap houses dan lanta, casas abandonadas donde se cocinaba y se vendía crack las 24 horas."
**Lyrical Themes** : Trap lyrics, popularized by artists like T.I., Jeezy, and Gucci Mane, were direct chronicles of street life and hustling. They did not seek moral or political lessons, but rather depicted the day-to-day realities of the street. "marcaron al género con letras que no buscaban de elecciones morales ni políticas, sino que eran crónicas del trapicheo y el día a día en la calle."
**Spanish Trap's Beginnings (Prototrap)** : In Spain, the "prototrap" scene emerged after El director Sao introduced Southern rap in 2002. Spanish rappers and producers began experimenting with these sounds and referencing trap. "Y poco a poco, diferentes raperos y productores, empezan a coquetear con este rap soleño y a referenciar al trap, generándose así una escena que podríamos llamar Prototrap."
**Viral Moment in Spain (2011)** : In 2011, trap became viral in Spain with Lori Money's parodic song "Santa Claus" on YouTube. This was the first time the general public in the country heard trap music. "Pero ese mismo año el trap se volvería a viral por un proyecto inesperado. Y Lourimoni, publican su YouTube Santa Claus."
**Definitive Eruption in Spain (2013)** : Trap made its definitive eruption into the Spanish music scene in 2013, marked by key encounters between collectives like Los Corredores del Bloque and Kefta Boyz, leading to the formation of PXXR GANG. "Pero sería en 2013, cuando el trap haría su errocción definitiva en el panorama musical español."
**Commercial Diversification and Pop Integration (2015-2016)** : By 2015-2016, trap had diversified significantly. Artists like C. Tangana began to incorporate pop and commercial aesthetics, Yvng Beef mixed it with flamenco, and groups like Los Santos brought ironic proposals. "Para 2015 y 2016, el género ya había hallado."
**Post-Pandemic Evolution and "Música Urbana"** : After the pandemic, the dark and dangerous sound of early trap began to fade. A new, less confrontational label, "Música Urbana," emerged to encompass the evolving mix of reggaeton, flamenco, and dembow with a cleaner, more pop vision. "El sonido oscuro y peligroso del trap de 2013 tras la pandemia y ha empezado a perder fuerza."
**Lasting Impact on Music Production** : While pure trap as a genre largely disappeared, its aesthetic changes and rhythms conquered the music scene. Auto-Tune, triplet hi-hats, and trap slang became ubiquitous in commercial pop and indie music. "El autotune, los jai-jachs entre sillos, la jerga, están en todas partes, desde el pop más comercial hasta Lindy."
**Influence on New Sounds** : Today's youth look to sounds from London or Chicago, like Drill—an even rawer and more violent genre. Others, inspired by early pioneers, take digital heritage to more virtual styles. "Hoy los chávales de los bloques se fijan en sonidos de Londres Ochicago, como el Drill, un sonido aún más crudo y violento."

"Esta es la historia de una guerra generacional, de la calle y contra la industria, de los pobres contra los ricos, bienvenidas a la historia del trapo español."

They maintained a direct relationship with the street and an underground character. "la independencia de la eje nueva York los Ángeles, una relación muy directa con la calle y un carácter totalmente underground."

**Cultural Impact of Internet and Video Games** : Internet culture was key to trap's success in Spain. Artists were digital natives who drew heavily from 90s aesthetics, internet culture, and video games. "La cultura de internet fue clave en el éxito del trap en España."
**Collaboration for Interaction** : Trap artists frequently collaborated to generate more interaction online, filling YouTube with self-produced material shot on mobile phones in the street. **Widespread Adoption of Trap Sound** : Suddenly, everyone, from young people to older listeners, from "pijos" (posh kids) to street people, started listening to trap. **Two Opposing Factions Emerge (2018)** : The debate at Primavera Sound 2018 solidified the existence of two opposing factions within trap: one defending the street and creative freedom, and the other advocating for business integration and changing the game from within. "Se habían creado dos bandos opuestos. Uno cierra la calle y la libertad creativa. Otro, defendiendo la postura del negocio, la de coger el dinero y cambiar el juego desde dentro."
**Trap's Legacy and Rule Change** : Although trap practically disappeared as a pure genre, it was enough to change the rules of the game. It demonstrated that young people from neighborhoods, with just a computer and a lot of attitude, could turn the music industry upside down. "El trap prácticamente desaparició como generopuro, pero le bastó para cambiar las reglas del juego.
