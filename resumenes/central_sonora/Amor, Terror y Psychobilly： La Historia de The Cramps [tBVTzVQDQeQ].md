## albums
**Songs the Lord Taught Us (1980) - Debut Album** : The band's long-awaited first album, released in 1980. It featured Nick Knox, who would become their most iconic drummer. Due to high anticipation from their cult following, it was an immediate success among underground enthusiasts. "La expectativa era tan grande que fue un éxito entre los seguidores del underground."
**Psychedelic Jungle (1981) - Second Album** : Released in 1981, this album's release was quickly followed by a legal dispute with their record label over unpaid royalties. This legal battle prevented the band from recording new material for two years, during which they focused on touring. "Ese mismo año tuvieron una disputa legal con su discográfica por derechos de autónopagados, lo cual les impidió grabar nuevo material durante dos años, por lo que se dedicaron a tocar y hacer giras."
**Smell of Female (1983) - Return Album** : This album marked The Cramps' return to recording in 1983, following a two-year hiatus caused by legal issues with their record label. "En 1983 regresaron con Esmelos Fimel."
**A Date with Elvis (1986) - Rockabilly Homage** : Released three years after their previous album, this work is considered one of the best of their career. It served as a direct homage to rockabilly music. Notably, for this album, Poison Ivy changed from playing guitar to bass. "Tardaron tres años en sacar su siguiente disco, Adate with Elvis, un homenaje al Riddel Rock, el cual está considerado uno de los mejores trabajos de su carrera, y fue el primero en el que Ivy cambió la guitarra por el bajo."
**Stay Sick! (1990)** : Recorded in 1990. "En 1990 graban Stysheek."
**Look Mom No Head! (1991)** : Released in 1991, the year following *Stay Sick!*. "al año siguiente locomon Nohead."
**Flamejob (1994)** : Released in 1994. "en 1994 Flemjop."
**Big Beat from Badsville (1997)** : Released in 1997. "en 1997 Big Bit from Biceville."
**Fiends of Dope Island (2003)** : Released in 2003, as part of their output in the 2000s decade. "Ya en la década de los 2000 lanzaran Cents of the Opieland en 2003."
**How to Make a Monster (2004)** : Released in 2004, also within the 2000s decade. "y J. Mike Amonster en 2004."

## curiosities
**Founding Encounter in Sacramento (1972)** : In 1972, Cristiuala (later Poison Ivy) met Gilsher (later Lux Interior) while hitchhiking on a road in Sacramento. They discovered a mutual affinity for music, cinema, and spiritual topics, laying the foundation for their future collaboration and intense love story. "1972, una chica llamada Cristiuala se hace autostop en una carretera de sacramento. La recoger y clip por gilser, un estudiante de la misma ciudad."
**Shared Passions and Early Influences** : After their initial meeting, Cristiuala and Gilsher reconnected at an art and shamanism course at the University of Sacramento, after which they remained inseparable. They shared profound passions for the spiritual, the subversive, and the primitive and dark rock and roll of the 1950s, alongside low-budget monster movies, 1950s and 60s science fiction, and Hammer horror films. "Ellos aún no lo saben, pero formarán una de las bandas más influyentes y únicas del punk rock, popularizarán el término psychobili y tendrán una de las historias de amor más intensas de apasionadas de la historia de la música."
**Move to "The Cramps" (1973)** : In 1973, Cristiuala and Gilsher moved in together to a place they referred to as "The Cramps," or "O Hallo." This predated the official formation of their band but may have served as an early symbolic identifier or shared living space. "En 1973 se fueron a vivir juntos a Crams, o hallo."
**New York Dolls Concert Catalyst (1973)** : Attending a New York Dolls concert in 1973 proved to be a life-changing experience for Cristiuala and Gilsher. Following this event, Poison Ivy stated they began to "consume anything that was sexy, wild, and played rock and roll." "Veren con cierto a New York Doll, ese mismo año les cambió la vida."
**Rejection of Mainstream Music (Mid-1970s)** : In the mid-1970s, Cristiuala and Gilsher found record stores to be filled with progressive rock and singer-songwriters, which they openly "abhorred." This led them to extensively explore second-hand bins, where they developed a fetish for doo-wop, 60s garage rarities, and especially rockabilly, becoming avid collectors of its obscure B-sides. "En esa época estaban llenas de rock, prodecibo y cantáultores, cosa que aborrecían."
**Move to New York and CBGB Immersion (1975)** : In 1975, they relocated to New York, immediately immersing themselves in the burgeoning punk scene centered around the legendary CBGB. They frequented the venue almost nightly, forging connections and friendships with bands like the Ramones and developing an admiration for Suicide. "En 1975 se mudan a New York."
**Adoption of Stage Names (Poison Ivy Rorschach and Lux Interior)** : Cristiuala adopted the stage name Poison Ivy Rorschach, inspired by her passion for comics and psychology. Eric Gilsher chose Lux Interior, a name he found in a car advertisement. These names were part of their effort to shape a 1950s aesthetic filtered through the emerging punk style of New York. "Cristi se bautizó como Poison Ivy Roshach, inspirada por su pasión por los cómics y la psicología. Mientras que Eric se rebautizó como lux interior, un nombre tomado de un anuncio de coches."
**Visual Aesthetic and Inspirations** : The Cramps' visual image was heavily influenced by a 1950s aesthetic, drawing strong inspiration from publications such as *Famous Monsters*, *Shock Theater*, and *Tales from the Crypt*. This vintage horror and sci-fi imagery was then filtered through the punk ethos emerging in New York. "Asi inspiraron fuertemente en publicaciones como Famos Monster, Soft Finland y Tales from the Creed, todo ello pasado por el filtro punk que estaba emergiendo en Nueva York."
**First Concert at CBGB (1976)** : The Cramps performed their first concert in 1976 at CBGB, sharing the stage with the Dead Boys. They quickly became regulars at the venue, frequently appearing on bills with bands like Suicide and Ramones. "Su primer concierto fue en el CBGV junto con los Dead Boys, era 1976."
**Early Sound and Stage Presence** : In their early days, The Cramps' musical proposition was clear: a sexy blend of rockabilly and protopunk, even if their technical musicianship had not fully developed. Poison Ivy's lead guitar work was characterized by a unique, raw, and psychedelic sound, complemented by her serious stage attitude. Bryan Gregory contributed a hypnotic rhythm guitar. Early drummer Pam was soon replaced by Miriam Lin, who laid down primitive rhythms. Lux Interior commanded the stage with his spasmodic vocals, described as a "bastard mix between Iggy Pop and an Elvis Presley resurrected from hell." "Por ese entonces no eran tan buenos músicos como Acabrían siendo, pero su propuesta ya estaba clara, una mezcla sexy entre en el Rockabilly y Protopunk."
**Refusal to Identify with Punk Movement** : The Cramps explicitly stated they did not feel identified with the punk movement, arguing that punk "disavowed the origins of rock and roll and wanted to destroy everything." In contrast, The Cramps saw themselves as "mythomaniacs," embracing and reinterpreting rock and roll history. "Ellos decían no sentirse identificados con el movimiento, ya que el punk renego a los orígenes del rock and roll y quería acabar con todo, en cambio de Crams eran unos mitómanos."
**Cult Status Before Debut Album (Pre-1980)** : The press initially struggled to categorize The Cramps, and they did not release their first album until 1980. This unique situation contributed to their growth into a significant cult phenomenon within the underground music scene. "La prensa a tardón a hacerles caso ya que eran una banda difícil de englobar y nos sacaron disco hasta 1980, cosa que los convirtió en un fenómeno de culto."
**Coining the Term "Psychobilly"** : The Cramps coined the term "Psychobilly" and used it to promote their concerts on posters and flyers. The term quickly gained traction and became a recognized musical genre. They also employed other descriptive terms for their music, such as "Voodoo Rockabilly" and "Gothabilly." "El término Psycho Bill Lilloy con Yoron para promocionar sus conciertos en carteles y flyers y rápidamente se convirtió en un género musical."
**The Cramps' Definition of Psychobilly vs. Others** : When The Cramps toured the United Kingdom, they were surprised to discover other bands identifying with the psychobilly genre. However, The Cramps asserted that their only commonality with these bands was shared references, not their fast and violent musical sound. "Cuando viajaron al reino unido de gira, para su sorpresa, había varias bandas que se englobaban dentro del género, pero ellos defendían que lo único que tenían en común con esas bandas eran las referencias, no compartían su sonido rápido y violento."
**Bryan Gregory's Mid-Tour Departure (1980)** : Shortly after the release of *Songs the Lord Taught Us* in 1980, guitarist Bryan Gregory abandoned the band mid-tour, reportedly taking the band's van and equipment with him. Rumors circulated that he had joined a satanic cult, but the truth was he was experiencing a crisis exacerbated by heroin use. "Poco después de sacar el disco, Reyangre goria abandonó la banda amplia en la gira, llevándose consigo a la forgoneta y el equipo. Corrió el rumor entre las cena y los fans de que se había unido en la secta satanica. Lo cierto es que estaba pasando por una crisis potenciada por la heroína."
**Kid Congo Powers' Amicable Departure (Post-1983)** : After The Cramps' 1983 album *Smell of Female*, Kid Congo Powers amicably left the band to rejoin his original band, Gun Club. "Poco después, Kid Kongo dejó la banda de forma amistosa, y volvió con Gun Club."
**Poison Ivy Rorschach: The Architect of Sound** : Poison Ivy was considered the architect of The Cramps' distinctive sound and a "wildest guitar heroine" who redefined how rock and roll was played. She and Lux Interior were the only consistent members from the band's inception until its dissolution. "Fue al arquitectar de su sonido, y una aerobina de la guitarra más salvaje que redefineo la forma en la que se toca el Rock and Roll."
**Lux Interior's Death (February 4, 2009)** : Lux Interior passed away on February 4, 2009. His death marked the loss of one of rock music's most charismatic frontmen and effectively ended The Cramps. "Luxe Interior murió el 4 de febrero de 2009."
**Poison Ivy's Retirement** : Following Lux Interior's death, Poison Ivy, who had turned 70 the previous year, retired to her home in California and largely withdrew from public life, disappearing from the scene. "Con su muerte, el mundo perdió una de los Fronman con más personalidad del Rock, pero no sólo le perdimos a él, junto a Lux, Cristín Terroa de Poison Ivy, y ella, que cumplió 70 años el pasado año, se retiró a su casa de California, donde prácticamente desapareció."
**Passionate 37-Year Marriage** : The relationship between Poison Ivy and Lux Interior was a passionate, reverent, excessive, and creative love that sustained them throughout their shared career, recordings, and "craziness." They were soulmates who became rock and roll legends, having been married for 37 years. "De Krabs fue un amor apasionado y reverente, excesivo y creativo, que los mantuvo juntos en los accionarios, en cada grabación y en cada locura que compartieron. Los almas gemelas que se convirtieron en leyendas del Rock and Roll, llevaban 37 años casados."
