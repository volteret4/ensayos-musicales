# artist - DJ Screw

## genres
Hip Hop
Chopped and Screwed

## curiosities
**Early Experiments with Hip Hop (Early 1990s)** : DJ Screw, a pivotal figure in the Houston, Texas music scene, began experimenting with his hip hop records in the early 1990s. He developed a technique of mixing two copies of the same record simultaneously at a significantly slower tempo, typically around 60-70 BPMs, resulting in a unique, languid sound. "Empezó a experimentar con sus discos de Hic Hop, al mezclar dos copias del mismo disco en paralelo a un tiempo más lento del habitual, alrededor de los 60-70 MPMs."
**Creation of Chopped and Screwed Sound** : DJ Screw's experimentation involved repeating long phrases with pauses and stretching beats until the songs seemed to "melt," giving the music a new, dreamlike and narcotic meaning. This unique manipulation of tempo and pitch became known as "Chopped and Screwed," with "Chop" referring to the cuts, repetitions, and jumps in phrases, and "Screw" to the dragged, deep tempo. "La música tomamos significado nuevo, más onídico y narcótico."
**Legendary Mixtapes and Local Scene Growth** : His now-legendary mixtapes on cassette began circulating, with people sharing and re-recording them. This dense, heavy sound quickly spread throughout Houston, fostering a vibrant local scene comprising rappers, producers, and a distinctive aesthetic characterized by gold chains, vintage cars, and even its own associated substance, "lean" or "purple drunk"—a mixture of cough syrup with codeine and soda—which amplified the hypnotic and narcotic effect of the slowed mixes. "Empezaron a circular sus ahora legendarios mixtapes en cassette, la gente se pasaba y regravaba esas cintas, y pronto, ese sonido denso y pesado se extendió por todo Houston."
**Death and Enduring Legacy (2000)** : Although initially a local phenomenon, DJ Screw's legacy expanded significantly after his death in 2000 due to an overdose of "lean." His pioneering work ultimately influenced artists across the United States and beyond, even impacting culturally distant genres like vaporwave. "Pero tras la muerte de DJI Escriu, en el año 2000, victimado en las sobretosis de Lin, su legado se extendió y terminó influyendo artistas de todo el país y más allá."

# artist - Macintosh Plus

## albums
**Floral Shoppe (2011)** : Released just a year after Daniel Lopatin's pioneering work, Floral Shoppe became arguably the most popular release within the vaporwave genre. It transcended its musical origins to become a significant internet meme and defined the recognizable aesthetic of vaporwave for a wide audience. "Tensó lo un año después del lanzamiento de Coyams, salió el que seguramente sea el lanzamiento más popular del vaporwave."
**Floral Shoppe (2011) - Influence and Example** : Compared to Lopatin's more heavily fragmented work, Floral Shoppe featured less extensively chopped and manipulated sounds. The album's famous second track serves as a prime example of how manipulating speed can imbue an existing song with entirely new meaning and emotional resonance. "El famusísimo segundo tema del disco, va a servir para ejemplificar cómo la manipulación de la velocidad puede dotar de un nuevo significado a una canción existente."
**Floral Shoppe (2011) - Diana Ross Transformation** : The opening track of Floral Shoppe is a slowed and partially manipulated version of Diana Ross's "It's Your Move." By slowing down Ross's originally optimistic and danceable song, Macintosh Plus transformed it into something nostalgic, melancholic, and even ghostly, demonstrating how tempo and pitch changes profoundly alter a piece's emotional impact. "La canción que abre el disco es una versión ralentizada y parcialmente manipulada de It's Your Move de Diana Ross."

# artist - Gabriel Dueñez

## curiosities
**Accidental Origin of Cumbia Rebajada (Early 1990s)** : In Monterrey, Mexico, around the same time DJ Screw was developing his sound, Gabriel Dueñez, a "solidero" (sound system operator), inadvertently created a new subgenre. During a party where he had been playing Colombian cumbia and vallenato records for over six hours, his sound equipment failed, causing the music to play slower and heavier. "Más o menos al mismo tiempo que DJ Escriu creaba sus primeros mixtaifs de Chopin Escriut, el Monterrey, México, el solidero Gabriel Duñez tras estar reproduciendo discos de convia colombiana a sí ballenatos."
**Audience Reception and Continuation** : Despite repairing his equipment for subsequent parties, Dueñez was surprised when the audience began requesting the slowed-down music once again. Far from perceiving it as an error, listeners were delighted with the heavy and "calencioso" (warm/passionate) sound, finding it offered an almost psychedelic and more danceable quality for the local public. "La gente empezó a pedirle que pusiera de nuevo la música arralentizada."
**Birth of Cumbia Rebajada** : This accidental discovery and its positive reception led to the birth of "cumbia rebajada" (slowed cumbia), a crucial subgenre for understanding Colombian and "cholo" culture. Similar to Houston, a distinctive scene and aesthetic developed around these parties in Monterrey, all without any direct physical or cultural connection to the movements happening elsewhere. "Asinación la cumbia rebajada, un subgenero clave para entender la cultura colombia, ocho lombiana."

# artist - DJ Dickie Ronny

## curiosities
**Origin of New Beat in Antwerp** : At the Club Hansen Beljick in Antwerp, Belgium, DJ Dickie Ronny accidentally sparked the New Beat genre. He played the EBM track "Flesh" by A Split Second at 33 revolutions per minute instead of its intended 45 RPM, with the pitch control set to maximum. This resulted in a denser, darker sound that captivated the clubgoers. "En el club Hansen Beljick de Amberes, el DJ Dickie Ronny pinchó el tema de EBMF-LACE, de AsplitSecond a 33 revoluciones por minuto en lugar de 45 con el control del pitch al máximo."

# artist - Kurt Eiker

## curiosities
**Decomposition of Memory Through Sound** : Kurt Eiker, described as a favorite musical project of recent years, constructs his entire work around the ambiguity of manipulated music to explore the decomposition of memory. He uses old recordings of salon orchestras, which are then slowed down to the point of sounding ghostly. "Por ejemplo, de Kurt Eiker, uno de mis proyectos musicales preferidos de los últimos años, explora la descomposición de la memoria a través de viajas grabaciones de orquestas de salón, ralentizadas hasta sonar fantasmales, evocando la pérdida y el olvido."
**Re-signification of Celebratory Music** : The resulting sound from Kurt Eiker's process evokes feelings of loss and oblivion. Music originally created for celebration and dancing is re-signified to sound heartbreaking, demonstrating how manipulating tempo can profoundly alter the emotional landscape of a piece. "Esa música, creada en un principio para ser celebrada y bailar, se resignifica hasta sonar descarradora."

While her tracks can be danced to, they are not characterized as cheerful music, highlighting the complex emotional impact achieved through her sonic choices. # genre - Vaporwave

## curiosities
**Origin on the Internet (Early 2010s)** : Vaporwave emerged in the early 2010s, with its origins rooted in the internet rather than in physical streets or neighborhoods. Its initial references showed strong similarities to DJ Screw's "Chopped and Screwed" mixtapes that circulated in Houston during the 1990s. "Un genero que surgió a principios de la década de 2010 en Internet y no en las calles bondos barrios."
**Core Technique and Emotional Impact** : At its core, vaporwave involves taking popular songs, slowing them down, and applying a reverb effect, which also lowers the pitch and expands the chords. This simple manipulation profoundly changes the song's meaning, transforming music into something new and often evoking nostalgia or a sense of distortion. "Una corriente nación de los márgenes del vaporwave, que consiste básicamente en tomar canciones populares, ralentizarlas, y aplicarles un efecto de riverba."
**Cultural Deconstruction and Nostalgia** : A key aspect of vaporwave is its ability to deconstruct pop culture, turning it into a distorted mirror of itself. It creates a feeling that everything once brilliant about the 1980s and 1990s is now shrouded in a mist, perceivable only through the filter of memory, evoking a wistful dream-like state. "No se trata solo de ralentizar, de construir la cultura pop, para convertirla en un espejo distorsionado de sí mismo."

# genre - Drum & Bass

## curiosities
**Origin from "Amen, Brother" Break (Early 1990s)** : In the early 1990s in the United Kingdom, various musicians collectively agreed to accelerate and chop the same "Amen, Brother" drum solo by The Winstons that Dr. Dre had slowed for N.W.A. This creative manipulation gave rise to Drum & Bass, one of the most exciting genres of that decade. "A principios de los 90 en la tierra, varios músicos se posieron de acuerdo en acelerar y trocear ese mismo solo de batería, dando origen a uno de los géneros más apasionantes de esa década."

# genre - Nightcore

## curiosities
**Accelerated Music Phenomenon** : Nightcore is a genre characterized by accelerated music and typically features high-pitched vocals. It gained popularity on platforms like YouTube years ago and, far from dying out, has spread significantly on TikTok. "El Nightcore se popularizó en la plataforma hace años y lejos de morir se está extendiendo en TikTok."
**Visuals and Evolution to "Speed Up"** : Virtually any song searched online can be found in a Nightcore version, often accompanied by images of anime-style drawn girls. Today, the term "Nightcore" is gradually being superseded by the more general "Speed Up" as the preferred label for these new accelerated versions, reflecting a shift in terminology on platforms like TikTok. "Hoy en día el término Nightcore ha ido perdiendo peso frente al término Speed Up y es más habitual encontrar nuevas versiones bajo esa monetilla."

# curiosity

**Slowed + Reverb and Speed Up Phenomena** : The "Slowed + Reverb" and "Speed Up" phenomena are currently dominating platforms like YouTube and TikTok. An artist is not considered reputable until their music has been remixed in a "Speed Up" version, indicating a significant shift in how music achieves viral popularity and recognition in the digital age. "Los fenómenos es lo de Tan River, Pee Speed Up, están rompiendo YouTube o Fitik Tok. No eres un artista reputado hasta que no te hacen un remix es lo de To Speed Up."
**Record Labels Adapting to Tempo Manipulation** : Record labels are increasingly recognizing the importance of these trends. Alongside new releases from their catalogs, they now include slowed or accelerated versions of songs, acknowledging that platforms like TikTok serve as crucial windows for exposure and viral discovery. "parece que las discográficas están empezando a darse cuenta, y ya, junto con los nuevos lanzamientos de Socatalogo, incluyen la versión ralentizada o acelerada."
**Transformation by Tempo Change** : The simple act of changing a song's speed, whether slowing it down or speeding it up, can completely transform the emotional experience for listeners. This phenomenon has a fascinating origin, emerging almost simultaneously in different cultures and parts of the world. "El simple acto de cambiar la velocidad de una canción. ¿Puedes transformar por completo lo que sentimos a les escucharla?"
**YouTube and TikTok's Influence on Music Consumption** : Platforms like YouTube and TikTok are fundamentally transforming how music is consumed. On YouTube, traditional albums are no longer the primary accumulators of views; instead, playlists, mixes, and edited versions with suggestive titles designed to evoke emotions are gaining prominence. "Plataformas como YouTube o Fatik Tok, están transformando la forma en la que consumimos música."
**Slowed + Reverb as a Dominant Trend** : Among the various phenomena on digital platforms, "Slowed + Reverb" stands out. Originating from the margins of vaporwave, this trend involves taking popular songs, slowing them down, and applying a reverb effect. This manipulation lowers the pitch, drags the vocals, and expands the chords, imbuing the songs with new meanings. "El es lo de Tan River, una corriente nación de los márgenes del vaporwave, que consiste básicamente en tomar canciones populares, ralentizarlas, y aplicarles un efecto de riverba."
**Popularity of Fan-Made Versions** : Many of these manipulated versions, often created by anonymous users, become more popular than the original songs, accumulating millions of views. Beyond the initial curiosity, there's a deeper engagement, as altering the tempo can make listeners feel vastly different emotions than those intended by the original composition. "Bien, pues alguna de estas versiones, creadas de la gran mayoría de veces por usuarios anónimos, como podéis ver, pueden volverse más populares que en las versiones originales, y acumular millones de views."
**Universal Fascination with Tempo and Pitch** : The idea of manipulating music speed has always been present; from placing a finger on a turntable platter to slowing down a Walkman with dying batteries, or changing YouTube playback speed, curiosity about tempo and pitch is universal. This explains why so many people from diverse backgrounds and cultures have independently felt this same fascination. "La idea de jugar con la velocidad de la música nos ha acompañado siempre, que no ha puesto el dedo en el plato del tocadisco, para reducir su velocidad."
**Global, Independent Origins of Tempo Manipulation** : It's a curious fact that in the early 1990s, in vastly different regions and cultures worldwide, several individuals independently conceived the idea of slowing down records to create new, more evocative sounds, highlighting a shared human response to auditory manipulation. "Lo curioso es que a principios de los años 90, en diferentes regiones del mundo y con culturas muy diferentes entre sí, varias personas tuvieron la misma idea, ralentizar un discop para crear un sonido nuevo mucho más evocador."
**Impact of Tempo and Pitch on Emotional Load** : Changing the tempo and pitch of a song does more than just modify its sonic texture; it completely alters the emotional load of the piece. It can transform an optimistic, danceable track into something nostalgic, melancholic, or even ghostly, making it feel less like listening to the present and more like a blurry memory of the past. "Cambiar el tempo y el pitch no solo modifica la textura sonora, sino que cambia por completo la carga emocional de la pieza."
**Ubiquity of Tempo-Based Subgenres in Electronic Music** : The electronic music scene is replete with subgenres whose primary differences often lie in speed. Simplifying it, merely increasing or decreasing a track by a few BPMs can transmit an entirely different emotion or even categorize it into a completely new style. "La escena electrónica está réplita de subgenerosco y a principal diferencias de la velocidad."
**Brain Perception of Music** : Modern understanding shows that our brain doesn't perceive music as a simple succession of notes or beats. Our listening experience is profoundly influenced by time, tone, timbre, and emotional context. Slowing or speeding up a song can transform what once sounded joyful into something nostalgic or even disturbing, as altering the rhythm changes our perception of its meaning. "Hoy sabemos que nuestro cerebro no percibe la música como una simple sucesión de nota a Sobits."
**Music Tempo's Link to Bodily Rhythms** : The tempo of music directly affects our feelings because it is intimately linked to our bodily rhythms, such as how we walk, breathe, or dance. When sad or discouraged, we tend to move slower with lower heart rates, entering a state of lethargy, whereas when active or joyful, our heart rate accelerates, and we breathe more intensely. "El tiempo de la música afecta directamente a cómo nos sentimos, porque está íntimamente ligado a nuestro cuerpo."
**Music Synchronizing with Body Rhythms** : Music, by synchronizing with these bodily rhythms, can intensify or modify our mood. Generally, we associate fast music with energy and euphoria, while slow music tends to convey calm, melancholy, or sadness. Thus, tempo not only accompanies our emotions but actively shapes them. "La música, al sincronizarse con estos ritmos corporales, puede intensificar o modificar nuestro estado de ánimo."
**Post-Vaporwave Evolution of Tempo Modification on Internet** : Following the explosion of vaporwave in the first half of the 2010s, the phenomenon of modifying music tempo has gained a new dimension on the internet, evolving into novel listening forms. On YouTube, "Slowed + Reverb" has become a new emotional phenomenon, with some versions surpassing original songs in views due to heightened curiosity. "Tras la colosión del vapor wave durante la primera mitad de 2010, el fenómeno de modificar el tiempo ha adquirido una nueva dimensión en internet, donde están mutando a nuevas formas de escucha."
**Search for Sensation over Artists** : These new listening trends on digital platforms are no longer solely about discovering a specific artist but about finding a particular sensation or atmosphere. This is evident not just with slowed music but also with accelerated music, which is becoming equally or more viral. "En estas nuevas tendencias de escucha ya no se trata sólo de buscar a un artista, sino de encontrar una sensación."
**Record Labels' Marketing Strategy (Post-2020)** : From around 2020, observing the high engagement numbers these fan-made versions generated, record labels began officially creating and releasing their own slowed or accelerated versions. They understand that platforms are vital for exposure, and these remixes often serve as the initial point of discovery for many listeners. "Más o menos a partir de 2020, viendo los números que generaban estas versiones, las discográficas empezaron a crear las soyas propias y alanzarlas de forma oficial."
**Algorithm-Driven Remixes** : These label-produced remixes are purely marketing-driven, designed to align with algorithmic logic, rather than artistic reinterpretation. Tracks are accelerated or slowed because they fit better with platforms like TikTok, leading to increased recommendations, clicks, retention, and viral potential, thus diluting artistic intent in favor of consumer product optimization. "La relectura artística se diluye totalmente a favor de un producto puramente de consumo."
