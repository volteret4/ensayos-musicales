# artist - Tony Iommi

## members
Tony Iommi

## instruments
Guitar
Homemade prosthetics (melted plastic, leather)

## curiosities
**Work Accident and Injury (Age 17)** : At just 17 years old, while working in Birmingham, Tony Iommi suffered an industrial accident with a metal cutting machine that severed two phalanges from his right hand. This was his fretting hand, crucial for playing the guitar. "Con tan solo 17 años, Tony Ayomi, un joven guitarrista de Birmingham, en la terra, acaba de tener un accidente laboral con un acordador a metal que le ha reban a dos falanges de su mano derecha, justo la mano con la que controlan los trascés."
**Innovative Playing Solution** : To continue playing after his accident, Iommi created his own prosthetics by melting plastic and lining them with leather. However, playing with these was difficult and painful. To make it more comfortable, he switched to thinner strings and reduced their tension, which naturally resulted in a lower tuning. This painful solution led directly to a dark, slow, and almost tenebrous sound that defined his band. "Para poder seguir tocando, se fabrica unas protechis, fundiendo plástico y forrándolas con cuero, pero la resulta complicado y doloroso tocar con ellas. Para que les sea más cómodo, cambia las cuerdas por una más finas y reduce su tensión, logrando si una finación más grave."

## albums
**First Two Albums (1970)** : Black Sabbath's initial two albums, released in 1970, introduced a sound unlike anything heard before at the time. These records marked a pivotal moment, being a point of rupture that defined the birth of Heavy Metal. Simultaneously, the almost physical gravity and the slow, ritualistic darkness of their music laid the groundwork for what would become Doom Metal. "Cuando su banda, Black Sabbath, publicó esos dos primeros discos en 1970, su sonido donde se parecía nada de lo que se había escuchado entonces. Estos álbumes fueron un punto de ruptura y marcaron el nacimiento del Jebi metal."

## curiosities
**Pioneers of Esoteric Symbology** : From their very beginnings, Black Sabbath incorporated esoteric symbology into their work, filling their albums with occultist and apocalyptic references. This tradition of incorporating mysticism, mythology, and alchemy extended throughout the Metal genre, serving to explore dimensions beyond mere satanic provocation and to create a coherent visual and musical discourse. "De hecho, ya lo cían Black Sabbath, quienes plagaron sus discos de referencias ocultistas y apocalípticas, y el Doom siguió esa misma tradición que se extendió por todo el Metal."

# genre - Doom Metal

## genres
Heavy Metal (origin)

## curiosities
**Accidental Birth from Injury** : Doom Metal did not originate from a happy idea but from an accident and a wound. This paradoxical origin gave rise to the heaviest and darkest sound in music, a direct result of Tony Iommi's injury and his innovative solution to continue playing guitar. "El Doom Metal funciona de la misma manera, un genero que no nació de una idea feliz, sino de un accidente, de una herida que, paradogicamente, dió luz al sonido más pesado y oscuro de la música."
**Emergence in the 1980s** : While its roots trace back to Black Sabbath's early sound, Doom Metal began to truly take shape in the 1980s. This was a reaction against the prevailing trend in metal which favored faster, more technical, and virtuosic sounds, as some bands consciously chose slowness and atmosphere. "Pero en Paláleo ese éxito, empezás a surgir una reacción opuesta. Algunas bandas decidieron apartarse de esa aceleración constante y poner el peso, la lentitud y la atmósfera en el centro de la propuesta."
**Defining Musical Characteristics** : Doom Metal is characterized by slow, heavy tempos, with riffs becoming longer and repetitive, and sustain stretching almost infinitely, multiplying the inherent heaviness of metal. These bands aimed not to dazzle with speed, but to trap the listener in an oppressive atmosphere where each beat feels like a ton. "Los riffs se volvieron más largos y repetitivos, el susto en se estiraba hasta el infinito, la pesadier del metal se multiplicó. Era como si cada compás, es ser una tonelada."
**Ritualistic and Ancestral Connection** : The deliberate slowness of Doom Metal imbues it with a ritualistic quality, connecting more with the ancestral than with spectacle. It represents a return to the essential and the earthly, seeking to immerse and envelop the listener through distortion and the suspension of time, differentiating itself through the depth of its atmosphere rather than superficial darkness. "Esa lentitud deliberada acercaba al metal algo ritual que conectaba más con lo ancestral que con el espectáculo, un retorno al asencial y lo terrenal que buscaba sumergir y envolver mediante la distorsión y la suspensión del tiempo."
**Consolidation in the 1990s and 2000s** : The genre solidified and began to attract more fans worldwide during the 1990s and 2000s. Bands during this period expanded the genre's atmosphere, adding new layers of tension, desperation, and terror to its proposals, leading to the exploration of even more extreme territories. "Durante los 90 y los 2000 el genero se consolido y empieza a tener cada vez más fans por todo el mundo."
**Paradoxical Peace and Comfort** : Despite its dark themes of death and the void, people often find peace, clarity, and even comfort in Doom Metal. This darkness, far from destroying, transforms into something akin to light, functioning as a refuge—a space to pause and breathe, where the music opens up an avenue for deep feeling. "Pero si el Doom Metal y estos subgéneros son tan oscuros, tan lentos y tratan temas como la muerte, vuelva a cío, porque la gente encuentra en ellas paz, claridad y hasta con suelo. Que es lo que ocurre para que esa oscuridad lejos de un dierte, tiene vuelve algo parecido a la luz."
**Structure as Prolonged Sonic Ritual** : Unlike faster metal subgenres, Doom Metal is structured as a prolonged sonic ritual where time dilates, notes are suspended, and the music becomes a state of mind. The use of drone, repetition, and silences creates a trance-like effect, similar to religious chants or shamanic meditations, enveloping the listener rather than progressing. "A diferencia de otros subgéneros del Metal más acelerados, el Doom se estructura como ritual sonoro prolongado. El tiempo se dilata, las notas se suspenden y la música se convierte en un estado mental."
**Academic Perception and Reevaluation** : Traditionally ignored or not taken seriously in academic circles due to its extreme symbologies and perceived noisiness, Doom Metal is increasingly being analyzed by scholars and theorists. They are approaching it from theological, mystical, and philosophical perspectives, recognizing its deep intellectual readings, much like minimalist and drone music have received. "El Doom Metal suele ser ignorado en el ambito académico, o al menos tomado por conserio en parte por todas estas invologías extremas y ser demasiado ruidoso. Pero cada vez más estudiosos y teóricos están empezando a analizarlo desde perspectivas teológicas, místicas y filosóficas."
**Connection to Eastern Philosophy and Transformation** : The philosophy of Alan Watts, who bridged Eastern philosophy with the West by advocating that negativity or darkness is a path to understanding and personal transformation, can be directly applied to Doom Metal. The genre's darkness is seen not as something to avoid, but as a teacher that opens up deeper life experiences, leading the listener through negativity to find something affirmative. "Alan Watts, conocido por acercar la filosofía oriental a Occidente, defendía que lo que solemos percibir como negativo o oscuro, no es algo que debamos evitar, sino un camino hacia la comprensión y la transformación personal. Y esto se puede trasladar al Doom Metal."
**Profound Esoteric Symbology** : From its inception with Black Sabbath, Doom Metal has embraced profound esoteric symbology, incorporating occultism, mysticism, mythology, and alchemy. This imagery extends beyond mere satanic provocation, serving as a visually coherent discourse with the musical content to generate a contemplative, introspective, and almost spiritual atmosphere that connects the listener with primordial emotions, the sublime, the ancestral, and the transcendent. "Desde sus inicios, el Doom Metal ha sido de simbología sotérica. Ocultismo, místicismo, mitología, alquimia, todo ello para explorar dimensiones que van más allá de la provocación satanica."
**Search for the Sacred in the Profane** : Doom Metal actively reclaims and gives meaning to the esoteric within a disenchanted world, seeking the sacred in the profane and the spiritual in the marginal. Its visual and sonic symbology aims to open the experience toward the inexplicable, consciously inviting the listener to participate in a spiritual quest. "Hay una búsqueda de los agrados en lo profano, de lo espiritual en lo marginal. El Doom no se toma la ligera a lo de sotérico, sino que lo remindica para darle sentido dentro de un mundo desencantado."
**"Dark Equivalent of New Age"** : In a metaphorical sense, Doom Metal is considered the "dark equivalent of New Age within Metal." Experiencing a genre album or attending a concert is likened to a collective ceremony, where the body is traversed by deep vibrations reminiscent of ancient rituals, transforming feedback, sustain, and distortion into symbolic tools of invocation. "En cierto modo, el Doom ventriase del equivalente oscuro del New Age dentro del Metal. Escucharon un disco del género, Mo'Mohora One, asistir a un concierto, al final, no dejas de ser como una ceremonia colectiva donde tu cuerpo es atravesado por vibraciones graves que recuerdan antiguos rituales."
**Cathartic and Hopeful Experience** : A study interviewing 74 fans of Doom Metal concluded that, despite its association with dark themes like depression and death, the genre can be profoundly cathartic and hopeful. For many listeners, Doom Metal does not generate sadness but provides meaning to deep emotional experiences, with some even stating it helped them through difficult times. "Preparando el vídeo he dado con un estudio en el que su autor, entrevista a 74 fans del género, y llega la conclusión de que el Doom Metal, a pesar de estar asociados con temas oscuros como la depresión y la muerte, puede ser profundamente catártico y esperanzador."
**John of the Cross Analogy** : The experience of Doom Metal can be likened to Saint John of the Cross's concept of the "dark night of the soul," where one faces absolute darkness not for destruction, but for transformation. The darkness is not a punishment but a necessary process for reaching a higher form of understanding, emphasizing walking through pain, feeling it, and finding strength in the experience. "Lo que predicaba es cuando la cruce en la noche oscura, ese momento en el que da el matrabi es una oscuridad absoluta, no para destruirse, sino para transformarse. Para él, la oscuridad no era un castigo, sino un proceso necesario para alcanzar, una forma más alta de comprensión."
**Journey Without Destination** : The prolonged riffs, slow tempos, and dense atmospheres of Doom Metal create a space suspended in time that invites reflection. Feeling the electric guitars resonate through the body becomes an almost primitive experience, where for a moment, all notion of progress and the frantic rhythm of daily life is lost. The genre is described as a journey without a destination, with its objective being to inhabit the sound itself. "El Doom Metal es un viaje que no tiene destino, porque es objetivo, no es llegar a ningún sitio, es evitar el sonido."

# genre - Funeral Doom

## curiosities
**Extreme Slowness and Melancholy** : Funeral Doom is a subgenre that pushes the characteristics of Doom Metal to their extremes, featuring very slow riffs and notes that are sustained for durations that seem almost eternal. The sound often resembles a funeral chant, adopting a mournful and melancholic image, and generating an expansive atmosphere of contemplative despair where the mood becomes almost physical. "Por un lado el funeral Doom lleva los rices y las notas adoraciones que parecen casi eternas, llegando a aparecer un cantofonebre. Adopto una imagen y un sonido más lúbredo y melancólico, generando un espacio sonido de desesperación contemplativa, donde la atmósfera se vuelve casi física."
**Instrumentation for Contrast** : To achieve its unique atmospheric depth, Funeral Doom relies not only on distortion but frequently incorporates organs and clean guitars. These instruments are used to create crucial contrasts within the dense soundscape, enhancing the sense of despair and contemplation. "Para ello no se valen solo de la distorsión, sino que suelen recorrir a órganos y guitarras limpias para crear contraste."

# genre - Drone Metal

## curiosities
**Purest Essence of Doom** : Drone Metal distills the Doom genre to its purest essence, stripping away any superfluous elements. This subgenre is characterized by its minimalist soundscape, often eliminating vocals and sometimes even drums, leaving only sustained notes that intertwine and resonate for minutes. "Pero hay un estilo que distila al género hasta asociencia más pura, despojándolo de cualquier elemento superfluo, ese es el Drone Metal."
**Tectonic Vibrations and Stilled Time** : In Drone Metal, notes are held for minutes and interweave, creating vibrations that are described as tectonic. The musical time practically stops, reducing Doom to its maximum simplicity by focusing on extended sonic textures rather than conventional rhythm or melody. "Las notas se sostienen durante minutos y se entrelazan, provocando liberaciones que parecen tectónicas. Aquí el tiempo musical prácticamente se detiene, es el Doom reducido a su máxima sencilla."
