# artist - The KLF

## members
Bill Drummond
Jimmy Cauty

## genres
Ambient House

## albums
**Chill Out (1990) - Genre-Defining Album** : This album became instrumental in defining the chill-out genre, offering a relaxing and evocative sonic experience. It was designed to simulate a nocturnal journey along the southern coast of the United States, utilizing layers of ambient sounds, samples, and music that unfolded like a narrative. The recording was notable for being captured live in a single take, and its cover art drew direct inspiration from Pink Floyd's *Atom Heart Mother*. While Bill Drummond stated it was intended as a parody of progressive rock, which they found pretentious, the album paradoxically achieved cult classic status within electronic music. "El disco describó una experiencia sonora relajante y evocadora, diseñada para simular un viaje nocturno por la costa sur de Estados Unidos, con capas de sonidos ambientales, sampler y música que fluye como una narrativa."

## curiosities
**Origin of "Ambient House" Term** : Jimmy Cauty, along with Bill Drummond, began referring to their productions, which were inspired by the relaxing sessions at "The White Room," as "ambient House." This term was an early designation for the evolving sound that would later be known as Chill Out. "Fue un Jimmy Couty junto a Bill Drummond, quien estenía en el otro grupo seminal del género de KLF, quienes, a sus producciones inspiradas en esas sisiones, empezaron a llamarlas ambient House."
**Album Cover Inspiration and Intent** : The cover art for The KLF's album *Chill Out* was directly inspired by Pink Floyd's *Atom Heart Mother*. According to Bill Drummond, this was intended as a mockery of progressive rock, a genre they considered pretentious. Despite this satirical intent, the album itself went on to become a cult classic in electronic music. "su portada está directamente inspirada en el Atom Gersmoder de Pink Floyd. Según Drummond era una burlal, rock progresivo que ellos consideraban pretencioso y, lónicamente, el disco se volvió un clásico de cultón de música electrónica."
**Live Recording in One Take** : The KLF's seminal album *Chill Out* was recorded live in a single take, a remarkable feat that contributes to its flowing and immersive narrative style. This recording method enhanced the album's natural, unedited feel, crucial for its ambient design. "Como curiosidad, se grabó un directo en una sola toma."

## albums
**Café del Mar (1994) - First Compilation in Series** : José Padilla compiled and released the first album in the iconic *Café del Mar* series in 1994. This collection successfully captured the unique sonic experience of the sunsets at the Café del Mar bar in Ibiza, allowing visitors to take that atmosphere home with them. Its release, alongside compilations from Buda Bar, initiated a global craze for chill-out compilations. "En 1994 José Padilla recopilo el primer álbum de la serie Café del Mar, una colección que capturaba esa experiencia sonora en un formato que los visitantes podían llevarse a casa."

## curiosities
**Defining the Ibiza Sound at Café del Mar** : As the resident DJ at Café del Mar in Ibiza, José Padilla became renowned for his sessions that perfectly encapsulated the essence of the island's sunsets. His musical selections blended ambient sounds, World Music, "Jazz Swab" (likely Jazz Suave), and "Rindmoss Electrónicos" (likely Ritmos Electrónicos), significantly contributing to the relaxed and contemplative spirit of Ibiza. His work redefined Chill Out for a wider audience, turning the sunset experience at the bar into a near-spiritual event for tourists. "Padilla contribuyó a definir el espíritu relajado y contemplativo de Ibiza, redifiniendo para gran público el Chill Out."

# genre - Ambient House

## curiosities
**Precursor to Chill Out** : The term "Ambient House" was initially used by Jimmy Cauty and Bill Drummond (The KLF) to describe their productions, which were inspired by the relaxing sessions in early rave rest areas like "The White Room." This term quickly gained popularity, eventually deriving into other genres, and served as a direct precursor to the "Chill Out" genre as it became widely known. "Fue un Jimmy Couty junto a Bill Drummond, quien estenía en el otro grupo seminal del género de KLF, quienes, a sus producciones inspiradas en esas sisiones, empezaron a llamarlas ambient House."
**Popularity and Evolution in the 90s** : Ambient House rapidly gained popularity in the early to mid-1990s, evolving from dance music and giving rise to other subgenres such as Ambient Techno and Ambient Trance. During this period, the genre began to diversify, incorporating external influences beyond rave culture, including instrumental hip-hop, Dub, World, and Downtempo. "El Ambient House se volvió muy popular rápidamente, derivando a otros géneros que también tienen su origen en la música de baile, como el Ambient Techno o el Ambient Trance."

# genre - Chill Out

## curiosities
**Modern Perception vs. Origins** : The contemporary image of Chill Out is often seen as cosmopolitan, exclusive, chic, and sometimes outdated, associated with luxurious terraces and designer loungers. However, its true origin lies in the "sudorosos club y raves de finales de los años 80," far removed from these sophisticated settings. "La imagen que ha llegado nuestros días del chill-out es vista como cosmopolita, exclusiva, chique, llaveces, algortera y pasada de moda. Pero sabía que este género tan selecto tiene su origen en los sudorosos club y reis de finales de los años 80, muy lejos de las terrazas lujosas y las tumbonas de diseño."
**Evolution of the Term** : Initially, "Chill Out" was a colloquial expression used simply as a synonym for relaxing or cooling down. However, after The KLF's 1990 album titled *Chill Out*, the term began to be specifically associated with the dedicated rest areas within rave culture, which then became known as "Chill Out Rooms." "aunque el término Chill Out ya era una expresión coloquial lo cual se usaba como sin un hímo de relajarse o enfriarse, a partir de este álbum, comenzó a asociarse con las alas de descanso de la cultura rave, las cuales comenzaron a ser conocidas como Chill Out Rums."
**Integration into Interior Design and Real Estate** : As Chill Out reached its peak popularity in the late 1990s and early 2000s, it transcended its status as merely a musical phenomenon. It became an integral part of interior design and real estate, utilized to complement an ethnic and exclusive aesthetic, often with Mediterranean and Indian influences, found in beach bars, restaurants, and various establishments worldwide. "Se había convertido en algo más que un fenómeno musical, vas a formar parte del diseño de interior y es inmobiliario, creado para combinar con esa apariencia étnica y exclusiva en lo que se había convertido, con influencias mediterráneas y indias."
**Peak Popularity, Decline, and Digital Revival** : The Chill Out genre experienced its peak popularity in the late 1990s and early 2000s. Following this period, like many trends, it suffered a sharp decline and came to be perceived as old-fashioned. Today, thanks to its nostalgic value and the rise of digital platforms, the genre is being rescued from obscurity, with Chill Out playlists achieving impressive numbers as people seek a musical escape from noise. "El generó tuvo su pico de popularidad a finales de los años 90 y principios de los 2000, durante los años siguientes como se le ocurrió con todas las modas, cayó empicado y pasó a ser visto como algo carca. Hoy en día gracias a su valor nostálgico, el generó está siendo rescatado del olvido gracias a las plataformas digitales, donde el playlist de Chill Out logran números impresionantes."

# curiosity

**Origin of Rave Rest Areas** : During the Acid House and Rave parties of the late 1980s, which could last an entire weekend, organizers recognized the necessity of creating refuges for attendees to rest away from the intense ecstasy of the dance floors. This need led to the creation of the first dedicated chill-out spaces. "Fue estas fiestas cuando los organizadores vieron la necesidad de crear refugios que permitieran descansar a la gente fuera del ex-Tasis de las pistas de baile."
**Ruta del Bacalao Comparison** : The social phenomenon of Rave culture in England, where parties served as a refuge for young people of all classes who adopted it as a philosophy of life, is noted to be very similar to what was simultaneously occurring in Spain with the "ruta del bacalao." "Algo muy parecido a lo que estaba pasando en España con la ruta del bacalao, pero claro nosotros nos somos ángulos a hones y nos han escritorios de tinta sobre ello."
**Ibiza's Influence on Clubbing** : By the mid-1990s, Ibiza had established itself as the global capital of clubbing, having significantly influenced the Acid House scene throughout the 1980s. During this time, watching the sunset from the beach had become particularly popular among foreigners, setting the stage for the rise of chill-out culture. "A midiados de los años 90, Ibiza era la capital del mundo del Clothing, tenía las discotecas más grandes del mundo, ya había influenciado durante los años 80 a la escena asit House."
**Global Chill Out Compilation Fever** : The release of José Padilla's first *Café del Mar* album in 1994, combined with similar compilations from Buda Bar in Paris, sparked a global "fiebre" for Chill Out compilations. This led to a saturation of the market with numerous chill-out CDs. "Este álbum, junto a las recopelaciones del Buda Bar de París, dieron inicio a una fiebre global por los recopelatorios de Chill Out. El mercado de sesaturo de cedres recuperatorios."
**Mid-90s Rave Room Decoration** : As the 1990s progressed and club music accelerated, an increasing number of clubs began incorporating "Chill Out Rooms" into their venues. These spaces were typically decorated in a psychedelic style, featuring very dim lighting and an abundance of cushions, designed to provide a relaxing ambiance. "Conforme avanzaba la década de los 90, la música se fue acelerando y cada vez más club se empezaron a encular estas alas, las cuales se lean estar decoradas de forma psicodélica, con una luz muy tenue y cojines por todos los lados."
