## curiosities
**Early Influences and Environment** : As Jonathan Atkins, he, Derrick May, and Kevin Saunderson, all from a middle-class background in Belleville, less than 50 km from Detroit, spent their late 1970s youth listening to funk and European electronic music. Their ability to grow up in a prosperous environment, rather than the ghettos, was attributed to the job opportunities at Ford and General Motors for both Black and white residents. "Atkins la tribuye que por primera vez, en Detroit, tanto blancos como nigos, podían trabajar en las mismas condiciones."
**First Synthesizer Acquisition** : In a context of significant economic and social changes in Detroit, Atkins acquired his first synthesizer, a Korg MS-10, in 1980, marking a pivotal moment in his musical development. "Atkins adquiere su primer sintetizador, un cor que me es 10, y en 1980 forma a Cibotron."
**Cybotron Formation and Interests** : In 1980, Atkins formed Cybotron with Richard Davis, an older Vietnam veteran he met while studying music. They bonded over shared interests in science fiction, futurism, and electronic music, though Atkins's roots were in funk while Davis leaned towards rock. "Ambos eran la ficionadas a la fíjana ficción, a los futuros logos y a la música electrónica."
**Deep Space Soundworks DJing** : Juan Atkins and Terry McMillan began DJing under the name Deep Space Soundworks. They mixed their own early productions with new House music emerging from Chicago, traveling there frequently to establish connections with DJs like Ron Hardy and Frankie Knuckles. "En esas sesiones mezclaban la música de Baylor, Peacus, sus primeras producciones, y una música nueva que estaba soquiando de Chicago, El House."
**Founding Metroplex Records** : Following the split of Cybotron, Atkins, now working solo, founded his own record label, Metroplex. This label became a crucial platform not only for releasing his own music but also for showcasing other like-minded producers, helping to solidify the techno sound. "Para el elamente, establece su propio sello discográfico, Metroplex, donde no solo lanzaría su propia música, sino también la de otros productores afines."

# artist - Belleville Three
## members
Juan Atkins
Derrick May
Kevin Saunderson
## curiosities
**Formation and Early Influences** : In the late 1970s, Jonathan Atkins, Derrick May, and Kevin Saunderson, then students at Belleville school near Detroit, forged a friendship centered around music. They would gather at Atkins' home to intensely listen to artists like Parliament, Funkadelic, Kraftwerk, Gary Numan, and Italo Disco, often dimming the lights and discussing the sounds for hours. "Al salir de colegios se leían ir a casa de Atkins... escuchar música. Musica fan con bajos irrimos electrónicos como Parlameno, FUNCADELIC y sobre todo música europea electrónica, Crafwer, Gary Newman y lo que sería conocido más tarde como Italo Disco."
**Global Impact and Recognition** : Within a few years of their formative listening sessions, these three individuals profoundly transformed how music was heard and danced to worldwide, earning them the monikers "The Creators, The Innovators, and The Renovators of Techno music," and securing their place in history as the Belleville III. "En unos años, estos tres chicos cambiarían la manera de escuchar e bailar la música en todo el mundo, y serían conocidas como Decreator, Diel Eveitor y Dino Vator de la música Tegno, y pasarían la historia como Belville III."
**Socio-Economic Context of Detroit** : Their upbringing differed from that of Chicago House artists; they were raised in middle-class families, not the ghettos. This was due to the prosperity of Detroit, where Ford and General Motors factories offered numerous jobs under equal conditions for both Black and white residents, providing a good standard of living. "Atkins la tribuye que por primera vez, en Detroit, tanto blancos como nigos, podían trabajar en las mismas condiciones."
**Divergent Label Ventures** : Despite their close relationship, each member of the Belleville Three developed their own distinct vision for the techno genre and its business aspect. This led each of them to found their own independent record labels: Juan Atkins with Metroplex, Derrick May with Transmat, and Kevin Saunderson with KMS Records. "Esta divergencia se reflejo en el hecho de que cada uno de ellos fundó su propio sello discográfico, Derrick and Mike creó Transmat. Mientras que Kevin Sanderson estableció KMS Records."
**European Compilation Genesis** : Towards the end of the decade, the three reunited with Neil Rushton, a talent scout from Virgin Records, to curate a compilation album. This album was intended to introduce their new sound to a European audience, initially titled "The House Sound of Detroit" but ultimately renamed "Techno! The New Dance Sound of Detroit" after Atkins' track "Techno City." "Los tres se reunieron con Raeston para elegir las canciones que irían en el disco que presentase y se nuevos sonido a los europeos."

# genre - Techno
## curiosities
**Detroit Origins and Sound** : Techno emerged from Detroit with a distinctive futuristic sound, deeply rooted in funk and clearly influenced by the city's industrialization. It was primarily created by middle-class youth, distinguishing it from the ghetto origins of Chicago House. "El tecno de Detroit tiene ese sonido futurista, con raíces en el funk y una clara influencia de la industrialización de la ciudad."
**Global Spread and European Popularity** : Despite initial struggles in Detroit, where young people preferred gangsta rap, many techno producers and DJs found immense success in Europe. They became stars overnight within the new rave scene, particularly benefiting from British talent scouts seeking new sounds for European discos. "Muchos de esos productores y DJs y de Anauropa, donde de un día para otro, en la nueva escena Rafe, se habían convertido en estrellas."
**Naming and Definitive Compilation** : The genre officially received its name from the compilation album "Techno! The New Dance Sound of Detroit," released by Virgin Records. This title, suggested by Juan Atkins' song "Techno City," helped popularize the sound across European clubs in London, Berlin, Valencia, Brussels, and Ibiza, influencing an entire generation. "tras debatirlo entre los tres le llamaron Tengo, The New Dent Son of Detroit, por la canción de Adkins, Technos City."
**Enduring Spirit in Detroit** : Despite Detroit becoming a desolate city with abandoned neighborhoods, techno is described as a persistent force, resonating through the cracks of buildings and deserted streets, serving as a perfect soundtrack to its transformed landscape. "Hoy en día de Detroit es una sombra de lo que fue... pero el Tengo Flo tan el viento y resonante de las grietas de los edificios y las calles de soladas. Como una banda sonora perfecta."

# genre - House
## curiosities
**Chicago Origins and Sound** : House music emerged from Chicago with roots in gospel and as an evolution of disco, which was resisting decline in the city. Unlike techno, it originated from the city's ghettos and utilized rhythm machines and samples instead of traditional bands. "El House de Chicago tiene esos raíces en el gospel, y es una evolución de la música disco, la cual se estaba resistiendo a morir en la ciudad."
**Parallel Development with Techno** : House and techno developed in parallel, and while they might seem similar, they possess distinct elements. House has gospel roots and is a disco evolution, whereas techno is futuristic, funk-rooted, and influenced by industrialization. "Con las escenas nacieron de manera paralela, y si bien pueden parecer lo mismo, tienen elementos bien diferenciados."
**Beneficiary of Rave Scene Growth** : House music, along with the sitcom "La City House," benefited significantly from the growth of the rave scene in Great Britain, leading to increased popularity and export opportunities. "Evido a la popularidad de la música House y la sitcom La City House, en Gran Bretagne, la música de Chicago se benefició del crecimiento de la escena Rafe."

# curiosity
**Detroit's Economic Transformation** : Detroit, initially a prosperous "motor city" thanks to Ford and General Motors creating thousands of jobs for its inhabitants, later suffered massive job losses due to the rise of robotics and technology. This profoundly altered the city's social and economic fabric, setting the stage for the techno movement. "El turismo era la capital del motor, por Grizzler, General Motors, generaban miles de puestos de trabajos sus habitantes, por lo que se convirtió en una ciudad próspera para esas familias. Más tarde, en la ócebe de la robótica de la tecnología, provocó una pérdida masiva de puestos de trabajo, y alteró profundamente el tejido social y económico de la ciudad."
**Detroit vs. Chicago Scene Differences** : The techno scene in Detroit and the House scene in Chicago developed in parallel but had key differences. Detroit techno was futuristic, funk-rooted, and influenced by industrialization, created by middle-class individuals. Chicago House had gospel roots, evolved from disco, and emerged from the ghettos, utilizing rhythm machines and samples instead of bands. "El tecno de Detroit tiene ese sonido futurista, con raíces en el funk y una clara influencia de la industrialización de la ciudad, mientras que el House de Chicago tiene esos raíces en el gospel, y es una evolución de la música disco."
**British Record Agent Interest** : British record agents were highly keen on exporting the sounds of house and techno to European discos due to the growing rave scene. They discovered that many popular tracks in Chicago clubs originated not from Chicago itself, but from Detroit. "Los agentes discográficos británicos andaban como locos por exportar, ese sonido a las discotecas europeas, pero vieron que las canciones que más se pinchaban en los cruos de Chicago eran precisamente desacidad, sino de Detroit."
**Compilation Naming Debate** : A compilation album designed to introduce Detroit's sound to Europe was initially conceived as "The House Sound of Detroit." However, after deliberation among the Belleville Three and Neil Rushton, it was ultimately named "Techno! The New Dance Sound of Detroit," inspired by Juan Atkins' song "Techno City." "El disco es si va a llamar de House Son of Detroit, pero tras debatirlo entre los tres le llamaron Tengo, The New Dent Son of Detroit, por la canción de Adkins, Technos City."
**Detroit's Post-Industrial State** : Modern-day Detroit is depicted as a shadow of its former self, a desolate city with entire abandoned neighborhoods. Yet, techno music is described as a force that continues to echo through the city's dilapidated structures, serving as a fitting soundtrack to its transformed landscape. "Hoy en día de Detroit es una sombra de lo que fue, una ciudad de solada con barris enteros abandonados, pero el Tengo Flo tan el viento y resonante de las grietas de los edificios y las calles de soladas. Como una banda sonora perfecta."
