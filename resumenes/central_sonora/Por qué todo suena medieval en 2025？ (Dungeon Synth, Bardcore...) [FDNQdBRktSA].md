# genre - Medieval Profane Music

## curiosities
**Nature and Purpose of Medieval Profane Music** : Alongside religious music, there was a rich tradition of profane music created by troubadours and jongleurs, who sang about themes of love, satire, or war. This tradition, being more free and earthly, profoundly inspired how the modern world and video games later imagined and interpreted medieval sounds. "también había una música profana, hecha por trobadores y juguulares que cantaban al amor, a la sátira o a la guerra, y será precisamente esta tradición más libre y terrenal, la que inspire siglos después la manera en que el mundo moderno y los videojuegos imaginaran cómo sonábano medieval."
**Characteristics of Profane Music** : Medieval profane music was the music of the street, the tavern, and the courts. It did not adhere to the strict rules of the church, allowing for greater rhythmic and melodic freedom. This secular nature made it more accessible and relatable to the common populace. "La música profana medieval era la de la calle, la taberna y las cortes, no seguía las estrictas normas de iglesia y tenía más libertad rímpica y melodica."
**Troubadours and Jongleurs as Performers of Profane Music** : Troubadours were primarily composers who wrote songs about courtly love, chivalric deeds, and social satires. Jongleurs, on the other hand, were itinerant musicians who brought this music to the people, performing it with instruments such as lutes, fiddles, wooden flutes, psalteries, or drums. This closer, more rhythmic, and earthly music is what today is associated with popular medieval imagery, evoking banquets, journeys, and adventures. "Los trobadores escribían canciones sobre el amor cortes, a dañas cada balleresca, a sus sátiras sociales. Quienes la llevaban al pueblo eran los juguulares, músicos ambulantes que le interpretaban con la húdes, violas de arco, flauta de madera, saltérios o tambores."
**Preservation of Medieval Music Sources** : Although it is impossible to know with certainty how medieval music truly sounded due to the obvious lack of audio recordings, historical sources like manuscripts, scores, treatises, and even period instruments have been preserved. These artifacts offer invaluable clues for scholars and performers to reconstruct and interpret this ancient music. "Aunque no podemos saber cómo sonaba esa música medieval con certeza, ya que obviamente no tenemos registros sonoros, sí que se han conservado manoscritos, partituras, tratados o incluso instrumentos de la época."

# genre - Video Game Music

## curiosities
**Early Technical Challenges in Western RPG Music** : The music for early Western RPGs faced immense technical barriers. It was impossible to faithfully recreate instruments like a lute or a bagpipe on the limited sound chips of 8-bit and 16-bit consoles. This forced composers to ingeniously imitate their timbres and adapt melodies to sound minimally convincing within these severe limitations, inadvertently creating a new sonic language. "La música de los primeros RPG occidentales tuvo que enfrentarse a una barrera enorme, la de la técnica. No había forma de recrear fielmente un lauto o una guaita en un chip de sonido, así que los compositores tuvieron que imitar sus timbres y adaptar sus melodías para que sonas en mínimamente convincentes dentro de esas limitaciones."
**Development of "Sound Zones" in RPG Music** : Early RPG composers created a distinct musical language organized into what could be called "sound zones" to aid narrative. Dungeons typically featured dark and repetitive atmospheres, while cities and taverns had lighter, almost folkloric melodies evoking daily life. Temples and sacred places drew liturgical inspiration, and combat music would accelerate, serving as an emotional guide that narrated the story without words. "Con ello iban a crear un lenguaje musical propio, que pronto se iba a organizar en lo que podríamos llamar zonas sonoras."
**Japanese Approach to Video Game Music Narrative** : Japanese composers demonstrated a superior ability to adapt music into the narrative, treating it as a true protagonist. While Western games often emphasized dark and functional atmospheres, Japanese developers prioritized more melodic music, creating "compressed symphonies" despite the technical constraints of the era. "Y los que mejor supieron adaptar todo esto en la narrativa, a mi entender, fueron los japoneses."
**Musical Diversity in Japanese RPG Scores** : Japanese video game composers, despite technical limitations, managed to make their music sound like compressed symphonies. In their games, one could find pieces with medieval motifs, but also ballads and rock pieces, demonstrating that while medieval was a starting point, all musical styles had a place within their compositions. "En sus juegos podemos encontrar piezas con motivos medievales, pero también baladas, vuestras piezas de rock. Lo medieval era el punto de partida, pero todo tenía cabida."
**The "Fanfare" as a Key Medieval Resource** : Among all medieval resources, the fanfare shone with particular strength in video games. Traditionally a brief, energetic piece interpreted by trumpets to celebrate solemn acts, it was adapted in games as a triumphant celebration following combat, becoming an iconic musical cue. "De todos los recursos medievales hubo un goque brillo con especial fuerza, la fanfaria, una pieza breve, cargada de energía, interpretada tradicionalmente por trompetas y pensada para celebrar un acto solemne, y en los juegos se adduptó como una celebración tras un combate."
**Technological Adaptation and Exoticism in Game Music** : Composers emulated medieval sounds by using minor scales, simplified ecclesiastical modes, or arpeggios, but these were always filtered through the technology of the era. This approach, combined with the exoticized vision of European medieval themes held by a distant country like Japan, led to the creation of a unique aesthetic identity, born from the very impossibility of achieving true realism. "La imposibilidad de sonar realistas ha como creando en identidad estética."
**Impact of CD-ROMs and Sound Cards on Game Music** : The arrival of CD-ROM technology in consoles and advanced sound cards (such as Sound Blaster-compatible cards) in PCs dramatically opened up a new sonic horizon for video game music. This technological leap allowed for much richer and more complex soundscapes, setting a new benchmark for game audio quality. "la llegada al CD en consolas y las tarjetas de sonido como las ombras de RMPC abrió de golpeo un nuevo horizonte sonoro."
**"Morrowind" as a Sonic Turning Point** : "Morrowind," part of The Elder Scrolls saga, marked a significant turning point in video game music, with its main theme becoming deeply ingrained in the memory of a generation and defining the aesthetic of the entire series. This game's soundscape helped solidify a new standard for epic, orchestral music in RPGs. "Podíamos marcar un punto de inflexión con Morro Wind, el tema principal se quedó grabando en la memoria de toda una generación, y marcó la estética de la saga El de Descrols."
**Orchestral and Epic Sound in Post-Morrowind RPGs** : After "Morrowind," video games clearly demonstrated they were playing in the same league as "The Lord of the Rings" in terms of musical ambition. Practically all RPGs, including titles like "Neverwinter Nights," "Oblivion," "World of Warcraft," "Dark Souls," and "Skyrim," began to feature orchestral and epic scores, embracing this "false medieval music." "A partir de ahí, los videojuegos dejaron claro que jugaban en la misma ligada del señor de los anillos. Morro Wind prácticamente todos los RPGs empezaron a sonar orquestales y épicos con esa falsa música medieval. Deber Winternides, Oblivion, Wall of Warcraft, Dark Souls, Skyrim."
**Medievalized Music as Mainstream Cultural Resource** : The extensive groundwork laid by video games, cinema, and TV series built a clear idea that the medieval, whether fantastic or not, serves as a refuge. This space offers a narrative where heroes still exist, battles have meaning, and the fight against injustice seems possible, moving medievalized music from a niche resource to a part of the cultural mainstream. "La música medievalizada había dejado de ser un recurso de nicho y había empezado a a formar parte del Mestre Incultural."

# genre - Bardcore

## curiosities
**Emergence during the Pandemic** : Bardcore emerged during the pandemic, initially as a musical meme and later evolving into a widely popular phenomenon. It involves re-versioning current songs as if they were created in the Middle Ages, performed with medieval sounds and instruments. "En plena pandemia, sorción no sé si llamarlo a genero musical, un fenómeno más bien, conocido como barco."
**Concept and Covered Artists** : Bardcore is a simple concept: it involves covering current songs as if they had been made in the Middle Ages, using medieval instruments and sounds. Artists such as Hildegard von Blingin' and Algal the Bard have created medievalized versions of songs by diverse artists like Eminem, Dolly Parton, or Radiohead. "El barco es muy simple. Se trata de versionar canciones actuales como si hubieran sido hechas en la edad media. El barco es como pide el de barco, quid al garbón, blingingín o algal de barc, han versionado canciones de artistas andispares como minem, dollypartón o radiohead, asonidos instrumentos medievales."
**Meme Origin and Pandemic Popularity** : Bardcore began as a joke or a musical meme. The phenomenon became very popular and spread rapidly across social media, possibly because the pandemic era evoked memories of the Black Death. This blend of humor and anachronism deeply connected with millions of people. "El barco empezó como un chiste, como un bebé musical. Y no sé si es que la pandemia que nos tocó vivir recordaba la peste negra, pero lo cierto es que el fenómeno se volvió muy popular y se extendió rápidamente por redes."
**Visual Aesthetics** : Each Bardcore cover is often accompanied by visuals, such as illuminated manuscripts or medieval paintings, digitally manipulated with Photoshop. These visuals enhance the illusion, making it seem as though the music could genuinely have originated in the 14th century, further contributing to the shared medievalized aesthetic on the internet. "cada cover me dio acompañado de manuscritos iluminados o pinturas medievales initalas con Photoshop para hacer veros y miel que esa música hubiese surgido en el siglo XIV."

# curiosity

**Contemporary Fascination with the Medieval (2025)** : In 2025, there is a widespread craze for all things medieval, reflecting an aesthetic that has been cultivated for years in cinema, video games, and music. This trend is seen as a refuge in an era defined by screens and uncertainty, offering an idealized vision of medieval life that resonates deeply with contemporary audiences. "Definitivamente, en 2025 nos hemos vuelto locos por lo medieval. Este año acabo doculó siendo una estética que llevaba años cultivándose en el cine, en los videojuegos o la música."
**Medieval Era Definition** : The Middle Ages spanned nearly ten centuries, from the mid-400s to the mid-1400s. During this extensive period, the Church's influence permeated all aspects of life, including the majority of music, which was primarily destined for religious purposes and to honor God. "La teada me diaba a un periodo de casi 10 siglos, desde mediados del 400 hasta mediados del 1400."
**Medieval Filtering into 20th Century Pop Culture** : The fascination with the medieval, initially seen in the Romanticism of the 19th century, profoundly filtered into 20th-century pop culture. This included folk music reviving medieval forms and instruments, and a surge of groups and composers rescuing medieval repertoire, setting the stage for its later mass cultural impact. "En el siglo XX, esta fascinación se filteró en la cultura pop."
**Internet, Video Games, and Cinema as Drivers of Medieval Popularity** : With the advent of video games, cinema, and later the internet, the medieval transcended its perception as mere romantic nostalgia to become a massive cultural phenomenon. These mediums played a crucial role in popularizing fantastic medieval imagery and bringing it into the mainstream. "con la llegada de los videojuegos, el cine y más tarde internet, lo medieval pasará de ser visto como una nostalgia romántica a convertirse en todo un fenómeno cultural masivo."
**Dungeons & Dragons Release (1974)** : The tabletop game Dungeons & Dragons, released in 1974, is a key reference point for popularizing the fantastic medieval imagery of castles, dragons, and dungeons. It drew inspiration from J.R.R. Tolkien's "The Lord of the Rings" and its innovative systems later significantly influenced video games, particularly RPGs. "Vamos a marcar 1974 como un punto de referencia, que es cuando sale el mercado el juego de mesa a Danyos andragons."
**"The Lord of the Rings" as Inspiration for D&D** : The tabletop game Dungeons & Dragons drew its fantastical medieval imagery, including castles, dragons, and dungeons, directly from the inspiration provided by J.R.R. Tolkien's seminal book, "The Lord of the Rings." This literary work established a foundational framework for modern fantasy. "al inspirarse en el libro El Señor de los Anillos."
**The Lord of the Rings Film Trilogy Release (Early 2000s)** : The premiere of "The Lord of the Rings" film trilogy at the beginning of the 2000s was a pivotal moment, igniting a widespread and fervent passion for the medieval and fantasy genres. This led to a subsequent boom in related tabletop games, video games, movies, and series that has only continued to grow over the years. "El único que terminó de cender la fiora por los medieval y la fantasía, ese es el estreno de la trilogía del señor de los anillos al principio de la década de los 2000."
**Impact of "Game of Thrones" and "Vikings" TV Series** : Following the success of "The Lord of the Rings" films, television series like "Game of Thrones" became an unprecedented global phenomenon, while "Vikings" particularly fostered a cult following for pagan and Viking themes. These shows further propelled the rise of genres like Pagan Neofolk and intensified the general cultural fascination with the medieval. "Juego de Tron se convertió en un fenómeno televisivo sin precedentes, y Vikins despertó un culto por lo pagano y lo vikingo."
**The Medieval as a Refuge in the Modern Era** : The extensive groundwork laid by video games, cinema, and TV series built a very clear idea that the medieval, whether fantastic or not, serves as a refuge. It is perceived as a space where heroes still exist, battles have meaning, and the fight against injustice seems possible, offering solace in a present era defined by screens, algorithms, and immediate connectivity, yet often lacking grand narratives. "Todo este poso que nos dejaron los videojuegos, el cine y las series fue construyendo en idea muy clara, que lo medieval, fantástico o no, es un refugio."
**Medieval as a Reflective Mirror of Eras** : The medieval has never been solely about the past; rather, it has consistently served as a mirror in which each historical era has viewed itself. 19th-century Romantics imbued it with mystery and spirituality, video games transformed it into adventure, cinema presented it as orchestral spectacle, and social media repurposed it into a widely shared meme. "Al final lo medieval nunca ha sido solo pasado. Ha sido un espejo donde cada época se ha mirado."
**Contemporary Need for Medieval Narratives** : In 2025, people continue to listen to and imagine medieval worlds because, amidst screens and algorithms, there remains a fundamental human need for stories that remind us of the enduring possibility of heroes, community, and epic narratives. The attraction to the medieval might stem not from what it historically was, but from what it continues to tell us about what we aspire to be. "porque entre pantallas y algoritmos, aún necesitamos relatos que nos recuerden que pueden haber héroes, comunidad y épica. Quizás lo medieval no nos atrae por lo que fue, sino porque sigue diciéndonos lo que queremos ser."
