## members
Norman Cook

## genres
Big Beat

## labels

## albums
**Better Living Through Chemistry (1996)** : This album, released in 1996, defined what the Big Beat genre would become. It presented a less electronic and dark sound compared to its predecessors, opting for a more funky, psychedelic, and lysergic approach. "Batcher Living Throck Chemistry es una propuesta menos electrónica y oscura, y más fans psicodélica y lisaérgica."
**You've Come a Long Way, Baby (1998)** : Released in 1998, this was Fatboy Slim's most popular album. It featured several well-known tracks that became synonymous with the Big Beat sound. "Fat Boy Slim lanzó en 1998, Yoav Coma Long White Baby, su disco más popular."

## songs
**Rockafeller Skank (1998)** : This famous track by Fatboy Slim was featured in the soundtrack for the video game FIFA 99, becoming a highly recognizable example of Big Beat's integration into popular media. "FIFA 99, con la famosísima de Rockefeller's Canc de Fat Boy Slim."
**Praise You (1998)** : A notable song from Fatboy Slim's 1998 album, "You've Come a Long Way, Baby." "su disco más popular de cual de esta canción es como Press-Due."
**Gangster Trippin' (1998)** : Another prominent track from Fatboy Slim's 1998 album, "You've Come a Long Way, Baby." "Gas Statriping y la mencionada Rock Afeleres Kang."

## curiosities
**Big Beat Butik - Origin of Big Beat** : Norman Cook, known as Fatboy Slim, held a Friday night party in a Brighton club called Big Beat Butik. These parties, along with those led by The Chemical Brothers in London, were instrumental in popularizing the genre that would eventually be named Big Beat in honor of Cook's events. "El Dj Britannico Norman Cook, más conocido como Fat Boy Slim, tenía una fiesta a los viernes en un club de Brighton llamada Big Beat Butik."

## albums
**Exit Planet Dust (1995)** : Released in 1995, this album is considered the "year zero" of Big Beat. Its founders, Tom Rowlands and Ed Simons, originally called themselves Dust Brothers but had to change their name due to a lawsuit threat from another American band with the same name. While preserving elements of acid house and nocturnal club music, the album was well-received by critics as a "new breath of fresh air" and achieved unexpected commercial success. "El disco se llama Silla que Tom Roblanz y Ed Siamonds, sus fundadores, se hacían llamar Dust Brothers, pero tuvieron que cambiar lo debido a que otra banda americana que se llamaba igual, había amenazado con demandarles."
**Dig Your Own Hole (1997)** : Launched in 1997, this album featured some of the most notable tracks of the Big Beat genre, including "Block Rockin' Beats" and "Setting Sun." "Dick Durr on Hall, lanzado en 1997, que tendrá algunos de los temas más destacados del genero."
**Surrender (1999)** : This album was released in 1999, following their critically acclaimed "Dig Your Own Hole." "y su render, lanzado en 1999."

## curiosities
**Name Change - From Dust Brothers** : The founders of The Chemical Brothers, Tom Rowlands and Ed Simons, initially called themselves Dust Brothers. However, they were forced to change their name due to a threat of a lawsuit from another American band that already used the same name. "El disco se llama Silla que Tom Roblanz y Ed Siamonds, sus fundadores, se hacían llamar Dust Brothers, pero tuvieron que cambiar lo debido a que otra banda americana que se llamaba igual, había amenazado con demandarles."
**Live Performance Innovation - Stadium Fillers** : The Chemical Brothers were one of the first electronic music bands to successfully fill stadiums, incorporating light shows and all the typical paraphernalia associated with rock events. This achievement even preceded Daft Punk, who later adopted many elements from their show, helping to pave the way for subsequent acts like Diplo, Justice, and Skrillex to also perform in large venues. "De Camilla Brothers, su una de las primeras bandas de música electrónica en llenar estadios, con juegos de luces y toda la parafernalia propia de los eventos del rock."
**Post-Big Beat Evolution - Genre Shift** : Around the year 2000, as the Big Beat genre became over-exploited and perceived as old-fashioned, even creatively strong bands like The Chemical Brothers found themselves unable to bring anything new to it. Consequently, they began to move towards other genres. "Incluso bandas tan creativas como de chemical Brothers no supieron aportar nada nuevo, y acabaron de cantándose hacia otros generos."

## albums
**The Fat of the Land (1997)** : Released in 1997, this album is considered the most important for the expansion of the Big Beat genre and arguably the most beloved and remembered by the public. It sold approximately 10 million copies worldwide and was not the band's debut, but their third studio work. Although The Prodigy had previously been rooted in the hardcore rave scene, their transition to Big Beat allowed them to maintain a rebellious, hip-hop-like attitude. "Fatof de L'Ans Laura Maestra del genero y el principal causante de su éxito. Vendió alrededor de 10 millones de copias en todo el mundo."

## songs
**Smack My Bitch Up (1997)** : This single generated significant controversy due to its suggestive lyrics and music video, which was noted for having a "plot twist like in Game of Thrones." It was part of The Prodigy's album "The Fat of the Land," contributing to its notoriety and the genre's expansion. "Uno de sus singles, Smart My Beatchup, generó una gran controversia por su sugerente letra y video musical, el cual tiene un plot twist que en juego de tronos."

## curiosities
**Genre Evolution - From Hardcore Rave to Big Beat** : Before the release of "The Fat of the Land" in 1997, The Prodigy was primarily known within the hardcore rave scene. However, with their move to Big Beat, they successfully managed to preserve a rebellious, hip-hop attitude within their new sound. "Antes de este disco estaba dentro de la escena hardcore más raveira, pero en su paso al Big Beat, superó en mantener esta actitud rebel de hip-hop."

# genre - Electronic Music

## curiosities
**Mid-1990s Mainstream Rarity** : In the mid-1990s, electronic music was still rarely heard on the radio and even less commonly seen on the charts, despite experiencing its most significant creative peak to date. "A mediados de la década de los 90, aún era raro escuchar música electrónica en la radio, y mucho más verlan las listas de éxitos."
**Subgenre Emergence and Underground Nature** : During its creative peak in the mid-1990s, electronic music saw the emergence of numerous subgenres like drum and bass and goa trance. These styles, though influential, typically remained confined to nightclubs, given their experimental and raw nature, and were primarily aimed at urban subcultures and raves rather than a mass audience. "Y eso que estaba viviendo su mayor pico de creatividad hasta el momento, y no paraban de emergir sub-generos como el drama bass, el yucagara echualtrance."
**Origins in Nightclubs (1980s-1990s)** : During the 1980s and 1990s, the vast majority of new electronic music genres, including Big Beat, originated from nightclubs, in contrast to later trends where new genres often emerge from the internet. "Aunque hoy en día, los nuevos generos de música electrónica suelen surgir de internet durante la década de los 80 a 90, la gran mayoría de ellos surgía de los club nocturnos."
**Gateway for Rock Fans** : The inclusion of Big Beat tracks in movie and video game soundtracks, often mixed with metal groups, served as an entry point for many rock enthusiasts to discover and appreciate electronic music. "cosa que hizo que fue la puerta de entrada a la música electrónica de muchas aficionados al rock."
**Legacy of Machine-Made Acceptance** : Despite its later reputation as "easy music," Big Beat played a crucial role in enabling a legion of rock fans and followers of other alternative genres to accept that music made with machines was not "a sin." "pero no olvidemos que esto sirvió para que una legión de seguidores del rock y otros generos más alternativos admitieran que la música hecha con máquinas no es ningún pecado."

# genre - Big Beat

## curiosities
**Origin in Nightclub Parties** : Big Beat emerged from nightclub parties during the 1980s and 1990s, specifically from Friday night events like the Big Beat Boutique in Brighton hosted by Norman Cook (Fatboy Slim) and parties led by The Chemical Brothers in London. "el Big Beat no era una excepción. El Dj Britannico Norman Cook, más conocido como Fat Boy Slim, tenía una fiesta a los viernes en un club de Brighton llamada Big Beat Butik."
**Nomenclature Evolution** : Initially, the genre was sometimes referred to as Brit Hop or Chemical Beat, in homage to The Chemical Brothers. However, it eventually became known as Big Beat, a name that stuck in honor of Norman Cook's "Big Beat Butik" parties. "Si bien al principio se le llamó Brit Hop, ya está chemical beat en honor a la que mi calbrothers, finalmente el genero pasará la posteridad como Big Beat, en honor a las fiestas de Cook."
**Musical Influences** : Big Beat drew from a diverse range of non-electronic styles including rock, psychedelia, hip hop, punk, jazz, soul, and funk. It also incorporated elements from contemporary electronic music such as acid house and drum and bass. "Bebe de estilos no electrónicos como el rock, la psicodelia, el hip hop, el punk, el jazz, el soul y el funk, y toma elementos de la música electrónica del momento, como las hit house del drama bass."
**Rhythmic Characteristics** : The rhythms of Big Beat are similar to breakbeat but are more forceful and intense, typically consisting of sampled breaks from classic funk, soul, and hip hop records. These rhythms are generally played at a tempo between 110 and 135 BPM, which is faster than most hip hop and trip hop tracks but slower than the dominant techno and drum and bass of the era. "Las rímpicas del Big Beat son muy similares a las del Breque Beat, pero más contundentes y intensas, suelen ser brex ampliados de discos clásicos de funk, soul y hip hop, y están en un tempo en 310 y 135 BPMs."
**Bass Line Characteristics** : Big Beat bass lines are generally powerful and reminiscent of those found in drum and bass and acid house. This similarity is often due to the use of the Roland TB-303 synthesizer, which became popular in those genres, for the bass sounds. "Las líneas de bajo por lo general suelen ser también contundentes, y recuerdan a las del drama bass a las del hit house, ya que en muchos casos se empleaba al mismo sintetizador de bajos que popularizó a este genero, el Roland B303."
**Pop-Oriented Structure** : Unlike much of the experimental electronic music dominating the British nightlife, Big Beat adopted a structure closer to pop music. Its key artists valued the pop structure found in rock and hip hop and adapted it for the dance floor, which facilitated its mainstream appeal. "A diferencia de la música electrónica que dominaba la noche británica, la cual sería tener una estructura más experimental, la estructura del Big Beat está más cerca al pop."
**Criticism from Purists** : The pop-oriented structure of Big Beat was not well-received by purists of electronic music, who considered the genre to be the "lowest point of electronic music." They viewed its accessibility as a departure from the experimental roots of club music. "cosa que hará que no sea del todo bien visto por las más puristas del genero, quienes consideraron al Big Beat del punto más bajo de la música electrónica."
**Mainstream Breakthrough** : Despite criticisms from purists, Big Beat's pop-like structure proved instrumental in opening doors for the genre to be played on radio and channels like MTV, allowing it to reach a wider audience beyond the club scene. "Aunque este hecho, vuelquen les abrió las portas a sonar en radios y canales como MTV."
**"Year Zero" in 1995** : The year 1995 is considered the "year zero" for Big Beat, marked by the release of The Chemical Brothers' album "Exit Planet Dust." "Se podría considerar, en 1995 como el año cero del Big Beat, ese año en el que sale exit planetas desde que mi calbrothers."
**Peak Popularity (1997-1999)** : The Big Beat genre experienced its peak popularity between 1997 and 1999. During this period, it became common to see its music videos on music channels like MTV, and it topped charts in the UK and USA. "Su pico de popularidad era de 1997 a 1999, y será un genero recorrente en las bandas sonoras de películas."
**Recurring in Movie Soundtracks** : Big Beat became a recurring genre in movie soundtracks, particularly for a curious subgenre blending science fiction, hackers, sunglasses, dark trench coats, and large rave scenes. Films such as Matrix, Blade, Hackers, and Spawn notably featured Big Beat tracks. "será un genero recorrente en las bandas sonoras de películas, el especial de ese subgenero tan curioso que mezcla vaciencia ficción, hackers, gafas de sol, gabardinas oscuras y grandes love de rave."
**Presence in Video Game Soundtracks** : The popularity of Big Beat extended to video games, primarily those focused on racing and sports. Memorable instances include Gran Turismo 3, featuring "Aisha" by Dezin Vegas, and FIFA 99, which included Fatboy Slim's famous "Rockafeller Skank." "Era tal su popularidad que también llegó a los videojuegos, principalmente a los de carreras y deportes."
**Decline and Over-Exploitation** : The Big Beat genre experienced a significant decline after its peak, largely due to over-exploitation in action movies, video games, sports events, and advertisements. Around the year 2000, it began to be perceived as old-fashioned and simplistic. "Pero como todo lo que sube, algún día tiene que caer, y la caída del Big Beat fue muy grande. El genero se sobrexploto entre el de películas de acción, videojuegos, eventos deportivos y anuncios, y alrededor del año 2000 ya ha visto como algo antiquado y fácil."
**Shift of Artists Away from Genre** : As Big Beat's popularity waned, even creative bands like The Chemical Brothers were unable to bring new innovations to the genre and eventually transitioned to other musical styles. "Incluso bandas tan creativas como de chemical Brothers no supieron aportar nada nuevo, y acabaron de cantándose hacia otros generos."
**Current Perception as "Easy Music"** : Today, Big Beat is often remembered as a type of "easy music." "Hoy en día es recordado como un tipo de música fácilona."
