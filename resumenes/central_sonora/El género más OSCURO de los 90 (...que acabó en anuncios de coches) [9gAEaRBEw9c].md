# artist - Massive Attack

## members
DJ Milo
Masha Rume
3D

## albums
**Blue Lines (1991) - Landmark Electronic Album** : This debut album, released in 1991, marked a significant turning point in European electronic music. Critics hailed it as the "Kind of Blue of rave culture" because Massive Attack skillfully stripped Hip Hop's raw rhythms of their urban urgency, reduced BPMs, and infused them with a sensual, soulful atmosphere complemented by dub-influenced basslines, effectively inventing the introspective music style of the decade. "El disco supuso nantes y en después en la música electrónica europea, la crítica lo definió como el kind of blue de la cultura de ens."
**Protection (Year unknown but before 1994) - Delayed Sophomore Release** : The band's second album, which took three long years to release, was delayed due to internal disputes among the members. "Tardaron tres largos años en sacar protección, su siguiente disco."
**Mezzanine (1998) - Darker Direction** : Released in 1998, this album was a product of intense internal discord within the band, where they veered towards a darker, more industrial, and aggressive sound. It is possibly their most known album, uniquely capturing the sense of technological alienation felt at the close of the millennium. "El resultado de este discicio fue Mesa Nín, posiblemente el álbum más conocido de la banda donde dieron un giro a la oscuridad."

## curiosities
**Formation and Sound** : Formed by DJ Milo, Masha Rume, and 3D, the group aimed to capture Bristol's smoky atmosphere within a recording studio, a decision that led to their distinctive sound. "Estos tres últimos decidieron meter la atmósfera humeante de la ciudad en un estudio de grabación, pasaron a llamarse Masifatac, y en 1991 lanzaron Blue Lines."
**Hiring Tricky (1991)** : Following the success of their debut album *Blue Lines*, Massive Attack hired Tricky in 1991. "Sejando en su talento, Masifatac decidió ponerlo a sueldo en 1991 tras el éxito de su álbum."
**Internal Disputes and Studio Segregation (1998)** : In 1998, Massive Attack faced severe internal disputes, with Masha Rume favoring the warm, soulful Bristol sound of *Blue Lines* and *Protection*, while Tricky sought a radical shift towards an aggressive, industrial, and profoundly dark sound. The conflict escalated to the point where band members refused to speak or share the same studio room, forcing the co-producer to act as a messenger, shuttling audio tapes between separate rooms. "La banda dejó literalmente de hablarse, llegaron el extremo de negarse a compartir la misma sala de estudio, el co-productor del disco tuvo que actuar de mensajero y abandonar sentas de audio de una habitación a otra de la estudio, porque sus miembros era la niña capaces de mirarse a la cara."

## members

## albums
**Maxinquaye (Year unknown) - Paranoid Debut** : Tricky's brilliant solo debut album, which was the culmination of him channeling deep existential crisis, hallucinations, anguish, and terror into his music, largely fueled by cannabis use and paranoia. "El resultado fue Maxine Cuale, su genial álbum de debut en solitario."

## curiosities
**Vocal Contributions to Blue Lines** : Before embarking on his solo career, Tricky contributed his vocals to several tracks on Massive Attack's seminal album, *Blue Lines*. "uno de ellos fue Tricky, que empecé su voz a esperar algunas canciones."
**Post-Blue Lines Lifestyle and Crisis (Early 1990s)** : After being hired by Massive Attack in 1991, Tricky spent the next two years predominantly outside the recording studio. He instead used his salary to frequent clubs, drink in pubs, and consume "industrial quantities of weed," which led to "infernal hangovers," a profound existential crisis, and paranoia, where he claimed to see ghosts in his living room. "Durante los siguientes dos años, Tricky prácticamente no piso en estudio de grabación. Gastaba su salario en clubs, bebiendo en pubs y consumiendo cantidades industriales de hierba."
**Channeling Paranoia into Music** : Tricky emerged from his period of deep paranoia and existential dread as a "dark sorcerer," consciously deciding to channel all his hallucinations, anguish, and terror into his music, which directly informed his solo debut. "Tricky energió de esta etapa convertida en una especie de oscuro magosomuro que decidió canalizar todas sus alucinaciones, toda esta angustia y terror en su propia música."
**Dislike for "Trip Hop" Label** : Along with Geoff Barrow and 3D, Tricky openly disliked being categorized under the "Trip Hop" label. "Jo Farrow, Tricky o 3D, detestaban la tiqueta TripHop."
**Internal Conflict within Massive Attack (1998)** : During Massive Attack's *Mezzanine* recording, Tricky was obsessed with steering the band toward an aggressive, industrial, and radically dark sound, breaking away from their previous style, which led to significant internal disputes. "Tricky estaba obsesionado con llevar a la banda hacia un sonido agresivo, mindo estrial y radicalmente oscuro para romper con todo."

# artist - Geoff Barrow

## member of
Portishead

## curiosities
**Early Studio Work on Blue Lines** : Geoff Barrow worked in the studios where Massive Attack recorded *Blue Lines*, though not in a prominent role; he primarily changed reels and connected cables. "Yo varro a trabajar con los estudios donde se grabó blue lines."
**Observational Learning from Massive Attack** : Despite his minor role, Barrow meticulously observed Massive Attack's creative process, noting how they manipulated tempos, equalized bass, and transformed vinyl fragments into intricate sound textures. "Tuvo una sinta de primera fila para ver cómo trabajaban masifatac, cómo jugaban con los tempos, cómo cualizaban los bajos y cómo convertían fragmentos de vinilos en texturas sonoras."
**Accumulating Film Soundtracks and Samples** : Applying his acquired knowledge, Barrow began actively collecting film soundtracks and sampling their rhythms and textures, which would become a cornerstone of his future work. "Me empezó a comular mandas sonoras y samplear sus límicas y texturas."
**Dislike for "Trip Hop" Label** : Geoff Barrow, along with Tricky and 3D, expressed a strong dislike for the "Trip Hop" categorization of their music. "Jo Farrow, Tricky o 3D, detestaban la tiqueta TripHop."

# genre - Trip Hop

## curiosities
**Ubiquity in Late 90s** : By the late 1990s, trip hop was omnipresent, featuring in films, advertisements, design bars, and even video games, becoming synonymous with the "hidden" or "occult." "A finales de los años 90, esta música estaba en todos lados, películas, anuncios, bares de diseño y hasta videojuegos. El trip hop se había convertido en sinónimo del oculto..."
**Origins in Bristol** : This genre emerged not from luxury, but from the dark streets of Bristol, fueled by the city's isolation and a sense of paranoia. "pero este genero nació lejos del lujo se gestó en las oscuras calles de Bristol, alimentado por el aislamiento y la paranoia."
**Transformation into Luxury Music** : Despite its dark origins in the 90s, trip hop ultimately transformed into the musical luxury of its era. "uno de los géneros más oscuros de los 90, acabó convertido en el lujo musical de una época."
**Headphone Listening** : Unlike traditional rave music designed for mass consumption and dance floors, trip hop was conceived for a more intimate experience, primarily for listening with headphones. "a diferencia de la música rif no estaba pensado para consumir en masa, sino para escuchar con los cascos. El Trip Hop."
**Filmic Approach** : In Trip Hop, producers emulate film directors, utilizing samplers like editing tables to cut and paste musical fragments to construct clear narrative arcs and sustained tension, in contrast to classic Hip Hop where the MC is the star. "en el TripHop los productores quieren estar en la silla del director."
**"Perfect Mix of Computers and Cannabis"** : James Bradell (Funky Porcini) famously characterized Trip Hop as "the perfect mix between computers and cannabis," highlighting key elements of its production and culture. "definió el TripHop como la mezcla perfecta entre ordenadores y cannabis."
**Shift to "Bohemian" Music (Mid-90s)** : In the mid-90s, as rave culture's image became "plebeian" to some intellectuals, Trip Hop offered a sophisticated alternative, becoming the epitome of "bohemian" listening, associated with discerning individuals listening to vinyl on high-fidelity systems while enjoying cocktails. "se convirtió en el epito medio de lo goaimio. Sorgó un actitud esno, un tipo sofisticado que viacocteles escuchaba más exdejijo para estradas."
**Commercialization and Background Music** : The music industry, particularly advertisers, recognized that by removing its inherent anguish and retaining the slow rhythms and elegant basslines, Trip Hop made perfect background music. This led to its use in luxury advertisements, fashion shows, and hotel lobbies. "si le quitabas al TripHop, es angustia y dejabas el rinvo alento y los bajos elegantes tenia su música de fondo perfecta."
**"Mozart for Marijuana Smokers"** : Trip Hop gained a reputation as "the perfect Mozart for marijuana smokers," further cementing its association with a relaxed, introspective, and perhaps illicit listening experience. "el TripHop se convirtió en el Mozart perfecto para formadores de Marijuana."
**Name Origin (1994)** : The term "Trip Hop" was coined in 1994 by a journalist from Mixmag, combining "Hip Hop" with "Trip" to evoke both cinematic and psychedelic journeys. "se lo puso un periodista de Mix Mach en 1994 mezclando el terminógic Hop con el Trip, el viaje cinematográfico y también psicodelico."
**Decline and Resurgence in Video Games (New Millennium)** : By the new millennium, the genre had largely fallen out of fashion due to oversaturation. However, its melancholic sound found a new home in Japanese video games such as *Silent Hill*, *Resident Evil*, *Perfect Dark*, and *Persona*, which utilized it to create specific atmospheric moods. "el género se había quemado tanto que pronto pasó de moda, aunque esa melancolía encontró un segundo hogar en los videojuegos japoneses."
**Reflecting Technological Alienation and a Broken World** : Trip hop's sound and visual aesthetics, often featuring cold, empty urban album covers, captured the technological alienation prevalent at the end of the millennium and seemingly foreshadowed a world on the verge of breakdown. "Mesa Nín plasmó como muy pocas obras la alineación tecnológica que tendría al final del milenio."

# curiosity

**Bristol's Unique Cultural and Musical Landscape** : Bristol, historically a port city with a legacy of slave trade and a large black population, was also a cheap city attracting students and bohemians. This diverse demographic fostered a rich musical tradition with strong influences from reggae, soul, hip hop, and post-punk, providing fertile ground for the unique development of trip hop. "Por su historia como ciudad portuaria y antiguo puerto de comercio de esclavos, Bristol tiene mucha población negra. Además, mi históricamente alcio en una ciudad barata, siempre ha tenido mucha presencia de estudiantes y boímios, por lo que su tradición musical es muy variada, por una fuerte presencia del rígi, del soul, el gijob y el post-punk."
**Influence of Cannabis and Climate on Bristol Sound** : In Bristol, Hip Hop beats were drastically slowed down under the combined influence of cannabis and the city's cold, humid climate, which led producers to reduce sample times and integrate elements of soul, jazz, dub, and film soundtracks, distinct from the chemical euphoria of capital city dance floors. "En esta ciudad, los bits de gijob me fueron arrastrados por la uforia química de las pistas de baile de la capital, sino que se rara la enticiano drásticamente bajo los efectos del cannabis y el frío clima húmedo de lo este. Redujero en el tiempo de los amples y metieron más influencias del soul, el jazz, el dub y las bandas sonoras."
**American Urban Music's Journey to England (Mid-80s)** : In the mid-1980s, new urban music genres like House, Techno, and Hip Hop arrived in England from the United States. Upon crossing the Atlantic, they lost some of their original cultural message for many Britons, who perceived them as a unified part of a vibrant American urban music scene. "A mediados de los 80, empezó a llegar mucha música nueva de Estados Unidos a Indaterra, el Faú, el Tecnó, el fijo, estos géneros al conocer el charco, perdieron parte de su mensaje cultural y muchos británicos consideraban esa música parte de un todo, de la vibrante música urbana americana."
**Paranoia as a Creative Force** : A crucial, often overlooked factor in the formation of Trip Hop, particularly evident in Tricky's work, was paranoia. Excessive THC consumption led Tricky to a state of description where he claimed to see ghosts, and he channeled this anguish and terror into his music. "Era el álmito y el exceso de THC, empoharon la tricky hacia un estado de descripción, en el que aseguraba haber fantasmas rondando para el salón de su casa."
**Film as a Key Influence on Trip Hop** : Film, specifically the atmospheric and narrative qualities of film soundtracks, was a pivotal element in shaping Trip Hop, with producers actively seeking the "aura of celluloid" to evoke images and sustained tension. "Ya hemos visto varios de los elementos que dieron forma al trick hop, pero aún faltaba uno de los más importantes, el cine."
**Trip Hop Producers as Directors** : Unlike classical Hip Hop where the MC is the central figure, in Trip Hop, producers assume the role of film directors, meticulously crafting soundscapes to create a cinematic and evocative experience. "en el TripHop los productores quieren estar en la silla del director."
**Trip Hop Musicians Composing Film Scores** : Over time, several musicians associated with the Trip Hop scene transitioned into composing film soundtracks, reflecting the genre's inherent cinematic quality. "con el tiempo, varios músicos englobados en la escena TripHop acabarían haciendo bandas sonoras para películas despías."
**Shift in Rave Culture Perception (Mid-90s)** : By the mid-1990s, rave culture had evolved from the late 80s, with ecstasy consumption and dancing increasingly viewed by some intellectual circles as "plebeian," "pseudo-religious," "working-class," and "noisy." "A mediados de los 90, la cultura Rey ya no era como la de finales de los 80, y el consumo de Extasis y el baile empezaba a ver en algunas sectores intelectuales como algo, podríamos decir plebello."
**Urban, Cold, Empty Album Art** : The visual aesthetic of Trip Hop, characterized by cold, empty urban album covers with minimal human presence, conveyed a specific mood that mirrored the music's melancholia and the sense that "something was about to break." "Ese estado de ánimo no estaba solo en la música, estaba también en la imagen, en esas portadas urbanas, frías, vacías donde casi no hay nadie."
**Trip Hop as a Sound Chronicle of the Future** : Trip Hop is not merely considered a 90s soundtrack but also a "sound chronicle" that retroactively captures the essence of a world yet to come, reflecting an impending societal shift or feeling of technological alienation. "no veo solo al trick hop como una de las muchas bandas sonoras que tuviera las 90, sino que también lo veo como un retro sonoro de un mundo que estaba por llegar."
