## The Original Ramones

### Members

__artist__ **Joey Ramone** : Born Jeffrey Hyman (May 19, 1951), suffered from Marfan Syndrome, contributing to his tall lanky physique, and was diagnosed with OCD at 18, manifesting in rituals like repeatedly stepping on and off curbs or struggling to leave his apartment.

__artist__ **Joey Ramone** : Growing up in Forest Hills, Queens, he was a bullied loner whose eclectic musical tastes spanned The Beatles, The Stooges, The Who, Chuck Berry, Little Richard, and Motown, absorbed via AM radio stations of 1960s and early 70s New York.

__artist__ **Joey Ramone** : In his early twenties embraced glam rock, idolising David Bowie and Alice Cooper, adopting a flamboyant persona with satin jumpsuits and platform boots that elevated his height to 6'8".

__artist__ **Joey Ramone** : Before The Ramones fronted a band called Sniper (1972–1974) performing as "Jeff Starship", specialising in songs about "weirdness and perversion". One Sniper song, "I Don't Care", was carried over to The Ramones.

__artist__ **Joey Ramone** : Was initially the band's drummer, but his inability to play fast enough combined with his powerful voice led to him becoming lead vocalist.

__artist__ **Joey Ramone** : Died April 15, 2001, just shy of his 50th birthday, from lymphoma. A broken hip in 2000 forced him off chemotherapy, allowing the cancer to return. The corner of East Second Street and Bowery, near CBGB and his last apartment, is now officially "Joey Ramone Place". Buried at Hillside Cemetery in New Jersey, overlooking Manhattan.

__artist__ **Johnny Ramone** : Born John Cummings, attended two military schools and did not miss a single day of high school. After a "super delinquent" phase in early adulthood, by age 20 he became straight-edge and adopted the demanding demeanour of a drill sergeant.

__artist__ **Johnny Ramone** : Was a lifelong New York Yankees fan who had a TV set up just off-stage during gigs to watch important games.

__artist__ **Johnny Ramone** : Influenced by Elvis, Buddy Holly, Led Zeppelin and The Beatles, yet a famous anecdote claims he attended the Beatles' 1966 Shea Stadium concert with a bag of rocks which he then threw at the band.

__artist__ **Johnny Ramone** : Bought his iconic blue Mosrite guitar for $50. His rigid downstroke-heavy playing style became the defining sound of Ramones guitar.

__artist__ **Johnny Ramone** : Died October 8, 2004, at age 55, after a five-year battle with prostate cancer. An eight-foot bronze statue stands in Hollywood Forever Cemetery in his honour.

__artist__ **Dee Dee Ramone** : Born Douglas Colvin, an "army brat" from a military family marked by domestic violence. Grew up on American military bases in Germany. In New York sank into deep drug addiction, resorting to turning tricks to finance his habit. Reportedly diagnosed as bipolar but did not seek treatment, contributing to his reputation as a "very scary dude."

__artist__ **Dee Dee Ramone** : Purchased his Danelectro bass for $50. Initially handled lead vocals but quickly lost his voice, permanently establishing Joey as singer.

__artist__ **Dee Dee Ramone** : Left the band in 1989. Found dead of a heroin overdose on June 5, 2002, at age 50. Buried in Hollywood Forever Cemetery.

__artist__ **Tommy Ramone** : Born in Hungary, his family fled communism when he was four, settling in Forest Hills, New York. Was a skilled recording engineer who worked at The Record Plant studio, notably with Jimi Hendrix.

__artist__ **Tommy Ramone** : Was the catalyst for the band: he suggested the four friends form a group, took on initial managerial duties, and sat behind the kit in July 1974 after other drummers failed, immediately clicking. Also co-produced their first three albums.

__artist__ **Tommy Ramone** : Left the band New Year's Eve 1977, frustrated by lack of commercial success and Johnny's "hardcore discipline style". Each member was living on $125 a week, with Dee Dee spending $100 of his on heroin. A notable incident backstage — a makeshift humidifier (a teapot on a hot plate) exploded and scalded Joey's face with second and third-degree burns; he returned to play the show in tremendous pain.

__artist__ **Tommy Ramone** : After leaving continued as a producer (The Replacements, Red Kross) and later formed a bluegrass folk band called Uncle Monk. Died July 11, 2014, at age 65, from bile duct cancer — the last original Ramone to die.

__artist__ **The Ramones** : The last time all four original members were together was an autograph session at the Virgin Megastore in Times Square on July 20, 1999.

__artist__ **The Ramones** : Played 2,236 gigs over 22 years. Final show was August 6, 1996, at The Palace in Hollywood.

__artist__ **The Ramones** : Only achieved two Gold records in 22 years: their self-titled debut (which finally crossed 500,000 sales in early 2014) and a greatest hits compilation — a stark commercial paradox given their immense cultural influence.

__artist__ **The Ramones** : Considered the "Johnny Appleseeds of Rock", directly inspiring Metallica, Green Day, Blink-182, Guns N' Roses, The Clash, The Sex Pistols, Pixies, Beastie Boys, Alice in Chains, Pearl Jam, Soundgarden, The Strokes, The Offspring, and Red Hot Chili Peppers, among many others.

### Formation and Name

__general music curiosity__ **Ramones name origin** : The name "Ramones" was inspired by Paul McCartney's occasional use of "Paul Ramon" on hotel registers, a name Dee Dee had already adopted as "Dee Dee Ramone" for years. The plural form evoked the idea of a gang.

__general music curiosity__ **Ramones image** : To reinforce "gang mentality" and "solidarity", the band adopted a uniform of leather jackets, jeans, and t-shirts, each member taking "Ramone" as a surname like brothers. Jeffrey chose "Joey Ramone" believing it sounded "so much cooler" than "Jeff Ramone."

__event__ **First Ramones formal gig (March 30, 1974)** : Held at the Performance Studio, attended by 30 friends paying $2 each, and was by all accounts "terrible." Initial lineup: Jeffrey on drums, Johnny on guitar, Douglas on lead vocals and bass.

### CBGB and Early Career

__event__ **CBGB debut (August 16, 1974)** : On a sweltering Friday evening the four played their first CBGB gig at 315 Bowery at Bleecker — then a notorious slum. The entire audience consisted of owner Hilly Crystal, his dog, two members of a transvestite band (The Cuckettes), Television's manager, a neighbourhood artist, and scenester Legs McNeil. The set lasted 15 minutes, included on-stage arguments and broken guitar strings, and scared the owner's dog. Hilly Crystal said: "Nobody's going to like you. But you know what, I'll have you back." This night is considered the moment music "began to change forever."

__event__ **CBGB residency (1974–1975)** : After their debut The Ramones became regulars, playing CBGB 74 times by the end of 1974 and 33 times in 1975. Word spread about these "leather-jacketed weirdos" and crowds grew. A notable anecdote: Linda Ronstadt arrived in a limo to see them but left after less than five minutes.

__event__ **Sire Records signing** : Manager Denny Fields financed a 15-song demo for $1,000 at the end of 1974. Further demos in September 1975 caught the attention of Seymour Stein, head of Sire Records, who saw them at Mothers, a gay bar, and signed them for $6,000.

__venue__ **CBGB** : Located at 315 Bowery at Bleecker Street, New York City, then a desolate slum. The acronym stands for Country, Bluegrass, and Blues — an ironic home for the birth of punk rock. Owner Hilly Crystal's willingness to rebook The Ramones repeatedly despite their chaotic first performance was essential to the New York punk scene.

### Songwriting and Style

__general music curiosity__ **Ramones songwriting philosophy** : Songs were deliberately built as "amped-up '60s pop": catchy sing-along melodies, no overt politics, no guitar solos, nothing over three minutes long, and always loud, fast, and fuzzy. Tina Weymouth of Talking Heads observed they played an hour's worth of material in 45 minutes.

__general music curiosity__ **Ramones early song titles** : Early songs often had negative declarative titles: "I Don't Want to Get Involved With You," "I Don't Want to Walk Around With You," "I Don't Want to Be Learned, I Don't Want to Be Tamed," "I Don't Want to Go Down to the Basement." Lyrics drew from their near-do-well backgrounds — hanging out, drinking, fistfights, and drugs.

__album__ **53rd and 3rd** : Dee Dee wrote this song directly about his own experiences turning tricks for drugs at that specific New York City corner.

__album__ **Beat on the Brat** : Joey wrote this song inspired by a spoiled, undisciplined child in his apartment building's playground.

__album__ **I Wanna Sniff Some Glue** : Described as the band's first "positive" song, running a mere 94 seconds.

__general music curiosity__ **Hey Ho Let's Go** : The iconic Ramones chant was drawn from the Bay City Rollers' "S-A-T-U-R-D-A-Y night" chant — the band were unapologetic about "unself-consciously ripping off" elements from pop bands.

### Albums

__album__ **Ramones (1976)** : Recorded between February 2–19, 1976, at Plaza City Sound in Radio City Music Hall in only 17 days, for a total cost of $6,400 — $400 over their advance. The master recording was so loud it blew the cutter head off the machine at the record plant. Released April 23, 1976. Domestic radio stations largely refused to play it, dismissing it as "crap."

__album__ **Ramones (1976) cover** : The iconic cover — four members leaning against a brick wall — was shot by Roberta Bailey, a friend who often worked the CBGB door, in a vacant lot near the bar. She was paid $125 for three rolls of film. It became "an all-time rock and roll classic."

__album__ **Ramones (1976) UK impact** : On July 4, 1976, while America celebrated its bicentennial, The Ramones played second on the bill at the Roundhouse in North London. This is widely credited with doing "more to kickstart British punk than anything else." At that point The Sex Pistols hadn't recorded and The Clash had barely played. Dee Dee recalled being treated like royalty playing to 3,000 kids already "tuned in," and noted that future groundbreaking British punk bands were at their Dingwalls club gig.

__album__ **Leave Home (1977)** : Released January 10, 1977. Co-produced by Tommy Ramone and Tony Bongiovi — cousin of Jon Bon Jovi. Described as "a little more poppy" than the debut. Joey's OCD struggles directly inspired "Gimme Gimme Shock Treatment."

__album__ **Rocket to Russia (1977)** : Released November 4, 1977 — the band's third album in 18 months, with the total combined running time of all three albums barely exceeding 90 minutes. Released as punk rock reached its peak and is considered a "seriously important record." Johnny Ramone was reportedly "absolutely furious" that The Sex Pistols' "God Save the Queen" had "ripped off" their sound. Features permanent live anthems: "Rockaway Beach," "Sheena Is a Punk Rocker," "Cretin Hop," and "We're a Happy Family."
