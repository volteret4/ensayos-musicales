**Screaming Life (1987)** : This was one of Soundgarden's first two EPs, released on Sub Pop Records. It played a key role in codifying the distinct "Seattle sound," characterized by a blend of "punk aggression, metal doom, sludgy guitars, and here and there, pop hooks." **FOP (1988)** : This EP, also released on Sub Pop Records, was another foundational release for the "Seattle sound." Its title track was a cover of a mid-70s funk hit by The Ohio Players, which guitarist Kim Thayil intended to transform into something closer to AC/DC. "The title track of the FOP EP was a cover of a mid-70s hit by funk band The Ohio Players."
**Ultramega OK (1988)** : Soundgarden's full-length debut album, released on SST Records, garnered critical acclaim. Its sound was described as "Stooges meets Sabbath, crossed with 80s Indy Rock," with guitarist Kim Thayil characterizing it as "Sabbath-influenced punk." "Soundgarden's full length 1988 debut album Ultra Mega OK, one praise from critics, who described it sound as Stooges meets Sabbath, crossed with 80s Indy Rock."
**Louder Than Love (1989)** : This was Soundgarden's second album and their debut on major label A&M Records. It achieved a milestone by becoming the first Grunge LP to enter the Billboard album chart, reaching number 108 in early 1990. At this time, the band was still marketed as metal, and the Seattle scene had not yet become a mainstream phenomenon. "Louder Than Love, became the first Grunge LP to make the Billboard album chart, where it reached number 108 in early 1990."
**Badmotorfinger (1991)** : Soundgarden's third album, its release faced initial setbacks when MTV banned the video for its first single, "Jesus Christ Pose," due to controversial imagery. Despite the delay in chart breakthrough, the album received "near universal critical praise," leading the band to focus on its second single, "Outshined." "Jesus Christ Pose was the first single from Bad Motorfinger, Soundgarden's third album."
## songs
**Hunted Down (1987)** : This was Soundgarden's debut single, released in 1987 on Sub Pop Records. For several years, the track notably served as Sub Pop's telephone hold music. "Soundgarden's 1987 debut single, Hunted Down, served as Subpop's telephone hold music."
**Hands All Over (1990)** : This single reached number 82 on the UK chart in 1990, indicating early interest from British listeners in the Seattle sound, preceding its mainstream breakthrough in America. "including Hands All Over, which reached number 82 UK."
**Jesus Christ Pose (1991)** : The first single from "Badmotorfinger," its music video was banned by MTV due to imagery of a crucified girl. The band argued the video was a commentary on sexism, but the controversy and lack of airplay delayed the album's chart success. "the video for Jesus Christ Pose was banned by MTV for its imagery of a crucified girl."
**Outshined (1991)** : This was the second single from "Badmotorfinger." "Soundgarden redirected their focus on the album's second single, Outshine."
## curiosities
**Formation and Early Lineup** : Soundgarden was formed in 1984, taking its name from a local Seattle sculpture. The band originated with Chris Cornell (vocals, drums) and Hiro Yamamoto (bass). Kim Thayil joined on guitar, and Matt Cameron became the permanent drummer in 1986 after Cornell transitioned solely to vocals. Yamamoto later left in the late 80s, with Ben Shepherd eventually taking over bass duties. "Soundgarden formed in 1984. They named themselves after a local Seattle sculpture."
**Sub Pop Debut** : Soundgarden secured their first recording contract with the legendary Seattle label Sub Pop Records, co-founded by Bruce Pavitt. Their debut single, "Hunted Down," famously served as the label's telephone hold music for years. "Subpop wound up signing Soundgarden to their first recording contract."
**Genre Definition** : Soundgarden played a significant role in refining the Grunge template, notably fusing punk and new wave elements with hard rock and metal. Guitarist Kim Thayil characterized their 1988 album "Ultramega OK" as "Sabbath-influenced punk." "What made Grunge a term by the way that none of the bands tagged as Grunge either liked or adopted was how it fused punk and new wave into hard rock and even metal."
**Early Mainstream Breakthrough** : Their 1989 album "Louder Than Love" was the first Grunge LP to chart on Billboard, reaching number 108 in early 1990. Despite this, the Seattle scene had not yet become a mainstream phenomenon, and Grunge was not a recognized term in the music press, with the band still marketed as metal. "Louder Than Love, became the first Grunge LP to make the Billboard album chart, where it reached number 108 in early 1990."
**British Reception** : In 1990, British listeners showed greater initial enthusiasm for the Seattle sound than Americans, with two Soundgarden singles making the lower rungs of the UK chart. "At first, British listeners were more enamored of the Seattle sound than Americans were."
**Singles Soundtrack Cameo** : Members of Soundgarden made cameo appearances in Cameron Crowe's 1992 film "Singles," which was notably set within Seattle's burgeoning Grunge scene. "actually featured cameos by members of Soundgarden."
**Popularity in Late 1992** : By late summer 1992, Soundgarden had achieved widespread popularity, driving the success of a 1991 side project, Temple of the Dog. This collaborative tribute album to Andrew Wood broke into the album chart's top 10 and belatedly went platinum. "By the late summer of 92, both Pearl Jam and Soundgarden were so popular with new fans hungry for new material that a tiny side project they'd collaborated on back in 1991 called Temple of the Dog."

## curiosities
**Heavy Metal Roots** : Alice in Chains distinguished themselves from other Grunge bands with their "more obvious heavy metal roots," aligning with the "doomy end of the metal scene" rather than the punk-metal fusion of Soundgarden. Both Layne Staley and Jerry Cantrell had played in metal bands before forming Alice in Chains in 1987. "What distinguished them was their more obvious heavy metal roots."
**Lewis Carroll-inspired Name** : The band's name, Alice in Chains, was described as a "portentous play on Lewis Carroll's Alice in Wonderland," hinting at the dark and surreal themes in their music. "whose name was a portentous play on Lewis Carroll's Alice in Wonderland."
**Management and Major Label Deal** : Alice in Chains rose alongside Soundgarden in the Seattle scene, sharing managers Kelly Curtis and Susan Silver. They secured a direct major label deal with Columbia Records, bypassing the extensive independent label history that many of their contemporaries experienced. "Music managers Kelly Curtis and Susan Silver, who also oversaw Soundgarden, took on Alice as clients."
**Embraced by Traditional Rock Fans** : A distinguishing characteristic of Alice in Chains was their strong appeal to traditional rock fans, even more so than alternative rock fans. Their songs often charted on the Album Rock Chart before making significant headway on the Modern Rock Chart. "They were embraced by traditional rock fans, more than alternative rock fans."
**Ambassadors for Grunge** : With their hit "Man in the Box," Alice in Chains "proved to be ambassadors for the Grunge sound and the Seattle scene" by mid-1991, helping to introduce the new sound to a wider audience. "Man in the Box and Alice in Shains proved to be ambassadors for the Grunge sound and the Seattle scene."
**First Gold Seattle Album** : Their debut album "Facelift" achieved gold status by September 1991, marking it as the first Seattle scene album of the 1990s to reach this sales milestone. "By September, the Facelift album had gone gold, the first 90s Seattle scene album to do so."
