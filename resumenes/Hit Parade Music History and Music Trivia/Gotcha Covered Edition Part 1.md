**Teen Idol Charting Strategy (1970s)** : For 1970s teen idols such as Donny Osmond, Michael Jackson, David Cassidy, and Shaun Cassidy, hit remakes and covers presented an "easy way" to secure positions on the music charts. "Covers were also an easy way to get 70s teen idols onto the charts."
**Interpolation Benefits: Artistic & Financial** : The "clever and perhaps crass trick" of interpolations lies in enabling new artists to capitalize on the familiarity of an older song while infusing it with their unique personality. Crucially, the new artist typically retains a portion of the royalties for their contributions, offering branding and financial advantages over a straightforward cover. "The clever and perhaps crass trick of these interpolations and it holds true to this day is that the new artist leverages the warm familiarity of an old song but then adds their own personality to it. Not incidentally, the new artist usually gets to keep a portion of the new song's royalties, taking credit for the part they added."
**21st Century "Bummer Cover" Trend** : At the turn of the 21st century, straight covers primarily garnered attention by completely reimagining the original song's tone and tempo, often stripping it down to a "haunting skeleton." This "bummer cover" trend, exemplified by Michael Andrews & Gary Jules' "Mad World" and Johnny Cash's "Hurt," established a new template for slowed-down reinterpretations in film and television. "the only way a straight cover could draw attention anymore was a total reimagining of the tone and even the tempo of the original song, stripping it down to a haunting skeleton. Welcome to the era of the bummer cover."
**Cultural Impact vs. Chart Success (Bummer Covers)** : Early 2000s "bummer covers" like "Mad World" (which was a UK number one) and "Hurt" (which won a US Country Music Association Song of the Year award) achieved significant cultural impact, but neither typically became major radio or US chart hits, distinguishing them from more commercially driven interpolations. "These early-oughts covers of Mad World and Hurt did have considerable cultural impact... but neither recording was a major radio or US chart hit."
**Post-Hip Hop Interpolation Reboot (2020s Pop)** : Moving into the 2020s, the "post-hip hop interpolation reboot" has become the dominant form of remake on pop charts, with pseudo covers like Ariana Grande's "7 rings" and Drake's "Way 2 Sexy" frequently reaching number one. "on the pop charts moving into the 2020s, the post-hip hop interpolation reboot is far more common."

His strategic aim was to successfully cross rap over to a mainstream white audience, a gambit that proved highly effective. "when producer Rick Rubin chose Aerosmith's 1977 top 10 hit, Walk This Way, and convinced Run DMC to cover it, he was deliberately trying to cross-wrap over with a mainstream white audience."
