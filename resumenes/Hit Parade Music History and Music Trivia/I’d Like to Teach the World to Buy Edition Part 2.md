*   **Persistence of a Good Song** : Regardless of commercial origins, a "good song is a good song" and can transcend its initial purpose. For instance, The Carpenters' "We've Only Just Begun," originally a bank jingle, is now enjoyed unsinnically as a love song, illustrating how songs popularized by advertising can still become cherished parts of popular culture. "A good song is a good song, like for example, the bank jingle that songwriter Paul Williams bequeathed to the Carpenters back in 1970."
