## albums
**Misunderstood (Year not specified)** : This blockbuster album marked Pink's pivot to a more rock-oriented sound after emerging from the teen pop era as an R&B star. It spawned a string of hits and achieved quintuple platinum status. The album is noted as her second best-selling title after "Funhouse." "her blockbuster album, Misunderstood, which spawned a string of hits and went quintuple platinum."
**Try This (Year not specified)** : This album, following "Misunderstood," flopped and received a poor response. Its commercial underperformance led Pink to regroup in 2004 and 2005 to prepare material for her next release. "But it's follow up, try this, flopped, and we're now in the mid-2000s when pink is figuring out what to try next."
**I'm Not Dead (2006)** : Pink's fourth album, which initially opened strongly at number 6 on the Billboard 200 but sank relatively quickly due to a lack of further hit songs. By September, it had fallen off the chart entirely without even going gold, marking the weakest opening sales of Pink's career. However, its demise proved premature as singles like "You and Your Hand" revived its performance, helping it return to the top 40, go gold, then platinum, and eventually double platinum, restoring Pink's multi-platinum status. "The album, I'm Not Dead, similarly opened strongly at number 6 on the Billboard 200, but it too sank relatively quickly, especially when no further hit songs materialized."
**Funhouse (Year not specified)** : The title for this album was inspired by Pink's elaborate circus-like performances. Max Martin produced nearly half of the album. It generated a year and a half's worth of soul-bearing hits, many of which chronicled her separation from her husband. The album was later certified triple platinum, making it her second best-selling title after "Misunderstood." "These circus-like performances would inspire the name of pink's next album, Fun House, nearly half of which was produced by Max Martin."
**Greatest Hits, So Far (2010)** : Pink's first compilation album, cheekily titled with three exclamation points, was issued as a "victory lap" around the time of her pregnancy announcement. It notably defied the trend of bonus tracks on greatest hits albums rarely becoming an artist's biggest hits, instead spawning new number one and number two singles. The album went gold, then platinum a year later, achieving the "virtually unprecedented chart feat" of two major new hits from a compilation. "Pink also took a victory lap by issuing her first compilation album. Hit bore the cheeky title, Greatest Hits, So Far, with three exclamation points."
**The Truth About Love (2012)** : This album debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 album chart in September 2012, marking Pink's first career chart-topping album at the age of 33. Like its predecessors, it proved to be remarkably deep with hit songs. "The Truth About Love debuted on the Billboard 200 album chart at number one. At age 33, Pink scored the first chart topping album of her career."
**Beautiful Trauma (2017)** : This album achieved the number one position on the album chart. It generated the electropop hit "What About Us." "In 2017, her beautiful trauma album hit number one and generated a hit with the Electropop track What About Us."
**Hurts to Be Human (2019)** : This album also reached number one on the album chart and produced a number one adult pop radio hit with "Walk Me Home," whose remix topped the club play chart. "In 2019, her Hurts to Be Human album also reached number one on the album chart and produced a number one adult pop radio hit with Walk Me Home."
**Trust Fall (2023)** : Released in February 2023, this album debuted at number two on the Billboard 200 and number one on Billboard's top album sales chart. Its title is described as "very Pink-like." "This year, in 2023, Pink is back with another album, with the very Pink-like title Trust Fall."

## songs
**Lady Marmalade (2001) - Cover** : This cover song marked Pink's first experience reaching the number one spot on the charts (the "penthouse"). It was a significant early career achievement, preceding her first entirely solo number one hit. "It was pink's first trip to the penthouse since the Lady Marmalade cover in 2001, and her first ever number one hit, Entirely Solo."
**Stupid Girls (2006)** : This song was the lead-off single from Pink's album "I'm Not Dead." It served as a satire of mid-aughts reality show culture and a "rip on what pink called Pornopoparazzi Girls at the peak of Paris Hilton tabloid culture." The song's video, which was released ahead of the album, featured Pink portraying thinly veiled versions of celebrities like Paris Hilton, Mary-Kate Olsen, Lindsay Lohan, and Jessica Simpson engaging in provocative behaviors. Musically, it was uncategorizable, blending pop, rock, and reggae rhythms, which made it stand out on the charts. It achieved the highest debut of Pink's career to date in February 2006, arriving at number 24 on the Hot 100, driven purely by digital downloads. However, it only rose to number 13 before hitting a chart ceiling due to a lack of radio programmer support, falling out of the Top 40 by May. "Stupid Girls was a satire of mid-Auts reality show culture, a rip on what pink called Pornopoparazzi Girls at the peak of Paris Hilton tabloid culture."
**Dear Mr. President (2006)** : A protest-ballad featured on the "I'm Not Dead" album, this song was a scathing open letter addressed to then-President George W. Bush. It received critical acclaim and featured harmonies from folk rock veterans The Indigo Girls, but despite this, it did not help Pink regain radio airplay. "particularly for the protest-ballad Dear Mr. President, a scathing open letter to then-president George W. Bush that featured harmonies from folk rock veterans The Indigo Girls."
**You and Your Hand (2007)** : Issued as a single in January 2007 from the album "I'm Not Dead," this song features a provocative title and is described as Pink's quintessential "kiss-off" anthem to "no good men." It's a blunt dressing-down of a would-be suitor in a nightclub attempting to buy her a drink. Co-written and produced by Max Martin, its fiendishly catchy melody, with his famous "pleasurable melodic math," makes it surprisingly uplifting for its blunt subject matter. The song perfectly encapsulated Pink's emerging persona: a "foul mouthed take-no-shit attitude paired with life-affirming music." It climbed slowly for four months, finally cracking the Top 10 in May 2007 and peaking at number 9, ultimately saving the "I'm Not Dead" album by prompting its return to the charts and subsequent gold and platinum certifications. "In a career full of kiss-offs to no good men, You and Your Hand might be pink's quintessential kiss-off, a blunt dressing-down of a would-be suitor at a nightclub who is trying to buy her a drink."
**Who Knew (Year not specified, reissued 2007)** : This mid-tempo torch song, described as warm and catchy, wistfully chronicled the death of a friendship, showcasing an "especially mature theme" for Pink. It was co-written by Max Martin and Dr. Luke and was initially attempted for promotion in 2006 without success. Reissued as the fourth single from "I'm Not Dead" after the success of "You and Your Hand," it also cracked the Top 10, matching the number 9 peak of its predecessor. "The mid-tempo torch song Who New, warm and catchy, the song wistfully chronicled the death of a friendship and especially mature theme for pink."
**So What (2008)** : A driving electro rock song with a "stompy electro rock vibe" that Max Martin first applied to Katy Perry's "I Kissed a Girl." On this track, Pink channeled her emotions into bravado, even opening the song with a taunting chant. Despite its punchy sound, "So What" was surprisingly personal, addressing her trial separation from her husband, Carey Hart. Pink, a "great believer in music as therapy," poured her hurt feelings into this defiant song, in which she literally calls herself a "rock star." Released in August 2008, it was an instant smash, debuting at number 9 on the Hot 100 and climbing to number 1 just three weeks later, marking her first entirely solo number one hit and her first trip to the penthouse since the "Lady Marmalade" cover in 2001. "When So What landed in August 2008, it was an instant smash, debuting all the way up at number 9 on the Hot 100 and rising to number 1 just three weeks later."
**Sober (2009)** : A stately rock lament featured on the "Funhouse" album. This song, which reached number 15 in early 2009, was one of several soul-bearing hits from the album that addressed Pink's separation from her husband. "including the stately rock lament sober, a number 15 hit in early 2009."
**Please Don't Leave Me (2009)** : An "aching pop" song from the "Funhouse" album. It became a number 17 hit in the summer of 2009 and was another of the soul-bearing tracks addressing Pink's separation from her husband. "The aching pop of Please Don't Leave Me, a number 17 hit that summer."
**Glitter in the Air (2010)** : A dramatic ballad from the "Funhouse" album that became a late-breaking hit in early 2010 thanks to Pink's acclaimed performance at the Grammy Awards. On January 31, 2010, she performed the song live, beginning on stage and then being lifted into the Staples Center rafters by silks, all while singing live. During the song's climax, she was doused in water, literally upside down, and continued singing. The week after this showcase, which regularly appears on lists of the greatest Grammy performances, the song debuted on the Hot 100, selling over 100,000 downloads in a week and peaking at number 18. "Glitter in the Air, a dramatic ballad which became a late breaking hit in early 2010. Thanks to the Grammy Awards."
**Raise Your Glass (2010)** : The first single released from Pink's compilation album, "Greatest Hits, So Far." Co-written by Pink with Max Martin and his songwriting associate Carl Martin Schuster (Shellback), its lyrics were described as brilliant, celebratory, and self-referential, filled with gratitude for Pink's fans, referring to them as "all my underdogs, we will never be, never be anything but loud, and nitty-gritty, dirty little freaks." By December 2010, the song rose to number one on the Hot 100. "The first single from Greatest Hits So Far was Raise Your Glass, co-written by Pink with Max Martin and his songwriting associate Carl Martin Schuster, aka Shellback."
**F***ing Perfect (Pardon My French) (2011)** : This was the second smash hit spawned from "Greatest Hits, So Far," co-written by Pink, Max Martin, and Shellback. It's a self-empowerment anthem whose original title "literally couldn't be played on the radio." Despite its foul-mouthed title, this soft rock ballad conveyed an uplifting message of support and acceptance for anyone facing bullying or self-doubt. When it became a hit, Pink released a clean version for radio play titled "Simply Perfect," with a slightly re-recorded chorus. The song reached number two on the Hot 100 in February 2011 and rode the Top 10 alongside CeeLo Green's "Fuck You." "They called the song Pardon My French, Fucking Perfect."
**Blow Me (One Last Kiss) (2012)** : The lead single from Pink's 2012 album "The Truth About Love." The title included "One Last Kiss" in parentheses. The song soared into the top five on the Hot 100, topped radio playlists, and reached number one on Billboard's Radio Songs Chart. Following "F***ing Perfect," its title seemed "relatively tame to radio programmers." It reached its number five peak on the Hot 100 in September 2012. "Blow Me One Last Kiss...Was the lead single to Pink's 2012 album The Truth About Love?"
**Try (2013)** : A midtempo rocker and the second single released from "The Truth About Love." This song reached number nine on the charts in February 2013. "The second single, The Midtempo Rocker Try, reached number nine in February 2013."
**Just Give Me A Reason (2013) - Duet with Nate Ruess** : The third single from "The Truth About Love," released in March 2013. This marked the first duet Pink released as a single, aside from "Lady Marmalade." Pink insisted on it being a dialogue requiring a second voice, despite Nate Ruess's reluctance, ultimately "tricking him into doing it." The song took nine weeks to reach number one on the Hot 100, becoming Pink's fourth career chart-topper and Ruess's first as a soloist outside of Fun. "Released in March 2013 as the third single from The Truth About Love, Just Give Me A Reason took nine weeks to reach number one on the Hot 100."
**Just Like Fire (2016)** : A one-off single Pink recorded for an "Alice in Wonderland" movie. It reached number 10, marking her last Top 10 hit on the Hot 100 "so far." "In 2016, a one-off single she recorded for an Alice in Wonderland movie, Just Like Fire reached number 10."
**Setting the World on Fire (2016) - Duet with Kenny Chesney** : A duet recorded with country megastar Kenny Chesney later in 2016. Pink's husky voice proved "well suited to country music." In October 2016, this collaboration reached number one on both Billboard's Hot Country Songs and Country Airplay charts. "Later in 2016, she recorded a duet with Country Megastar Kenny Chesney, setting the world on fire."
**What About Us (2017)** : An electropop track from the "Beautiful Trauma" album. It reached number 13 on the Hot 100, becoming Pink's last Top 40 hit "so far," 18 years into her career. "It reached number 13 on the Hot 100. Pink's last top 40 hit so far, and it came 18 years into her career."
**Walk Me Home (2019)** : Featured on the "Hurts to Be Human" album. This song produced a number one adult pop radio hit, and its remix also topped the club play chart. "produced a number one adult pop radio hit with Walk Me Home. Its remix also topped the club play chart."
**Never Gonna Not Dance Again (2023)** : The first single from the "Trust Fall" album, co-written by Pink with Max Martin and Shellback. This track is a throwback that recalls the sound of vintage disco and is notably "more soul-flavored than any Pink single in years," serving as a reminder of Pink's early "genre crossing pop and B" beginnings. "The first single from the album, Never Gonna Not Dance Again, is a throwback in more ways than one."

"the radio in the mid-Auts seemed to vindicate pink, the original millennial pop rock hybridizer."

"One USA Today reporter, Gind Up Controversy, by seeking comment from the starlet's pink was satirizing."

"But when her label suggested they work together on songs for I'm Not Dead, pink reluctantly agreed."

"She no longer works with Dr. Luke, however, has called him, quote, not a good person, and supported singer Kesha in her later abuse lawsuit against Dr. Luke."
**Persona Defined by "You and Your Hand"** : The song "You and Your Hand" is cited as the perfect encapsulation of what would become Pink's enduring persona: "a foul mouthed take-no-shit attitude paired with life-affirming music." "It was the perfect encapsulation of what would become the pink persona, a foul mouthed take-no-shit attitude paired with life-affirming music."
**Elaborate Gymnastic Performances** : Beginning with her "I'm Not Dead" tour, Pink introduced elaborate gymnastic routines, including aerial silks, into her on-stage performances, which became a hallmark of her live shows, with audiences "gasping as pink spun from the arena's rafters." "adding elaborate gymnastic routines to her on-stage performances starting on the I'm Not Dead tour."
**Music as Therapy** : Pink views music as a form of therapy, which was evident when she channeled her personal pain from her trial separation from husband Carey Hart into the defiant song "So What." "A great believer in music as therapy, pink sank her hurt feelings into the defiant song, on which she literally calls herself a rock star."
**Grammy Performance of "Glitter in the Air" (2010)** : On January 31, 2010, Pink delivered an acclaimed live performance of "Glitter in the Air" at the Grammy Awards. Starting on the stage, she was then lifted into the Staples Center rafters by silks, performing with a trio of fellow gymnasts, singing live throughout. By the song's climax, her body was doused in water, and she performed upside down, making this a regularly cited "greatest Grammy performance." "Pink performed Glitter in the Air live on the broadcast, starting from the Grammy stage. Then she lifted into the staple center rafters by silks held by a trio of fellow gymnasts."
**Marriage Saved by "Funhouse" Album** : Pink's "unfiltered marriage on the rocks album" ("Funhouse") is suggested to have contributed to saving her marriage with Carey Hart, as they confirmed their reconciliation in February 2010. "pinks unfiltered marriage on the rocks album may have saved her marriage."
**Pregnancy Announcement (2010)** : Pink announced her first pregnancy later in 2010, around the same time she was issuing her "Greatest Hits, So Far" compilation album. "Pink became pregnant with their first child later that year."
**Ongoing Marriage (2023)** : More than a decade after their reconciliation in 2010, Pink and Carey Hart remain married and have two children. "They are still married with two children more than a decade later."
**"Greatest Hits So Far" Chart Feat** : Pink achieved a "virtually unprecedented chart feat" by having two new songs from a greatest hits album – "Raise Your Glass" (number one) and "F***ing Perfect" (number two) – become major hits, a rare occurrence for such compilations. "It was already remarkable that Pink had landed a new number one and a new number two from a greatest hits album, a virtually unprecedented chart feat."
**"Blow Me" Title Tamed** : After the controversy surrounding "F***ing Perfect," the title "Blow Me (One Last Kiss)" seemed "relatively tame to radio programmers," which allowed it to top radio playlists. "I guess after fucking perfect, Blow Me seemed relatively tame to radio programmers."
**Olympic Gymnast Ambition** : Before becoming a singer, Pink had aspirations of being an Olympic gymnast. "I wanted to be an Olympic gymnast before I was a singer."
**Continued Acrobatics (2023)** : Pink, at 43 years old in 2023, confirmed that her live acrobatics would continue on her tour for the "Trust Fall" album, asking, "once you get to fly, why would you not?" "In her promotion for her new album, the 43-year-old Pink has already affirmed not only that she is going back on tour this year, but that her live acrobatics will continue."
**Consistent Sound Over 20+ Years** : Pink's extensive list of hits is noted for its remarkable consistency in sound, despite evolving over two decades. Her songs are described as "vulnerable and defiant, self-deprecating and self-aggrandizing, rocking and soulful," embodying her unbranded style. "But the remarkable thing about Pink's long list of hits is how consistent they sound, how unbranded they are for her."
**No Longer Misunderstood (2023)** : After more than 20 years in the music industry, Pink is confident she is "not worried about" feeling misunderstood anymore. "It's been more than 20 years since Pink said she felt misunderstood. I'm pretty sure she's not worried about that anymore."

# curiosity

**Mid-Aughts Rock and Pop Hybrids** : The mid-aughts music scene was characterized by "new hybrids of rock and pop storming the charts." This trend included songs like Kelly Clarkson's "Since U Been Gone," which "grafted Indy Rock style onto Pure Pop," and the "danceable neo-garage" sound of post-punk revivalists such as The Killers. "While she was off the radar, new hybrids of rock and pop were storming the charts, ranging from Kelly Clarkson's smash since you've been gone, which grafted Indy Rock style onto Pure Pop. To the danceable neo-garage of post-punk revivalists, the killers."
**The Risk of "Part One" in Greatest Hits Titles** : Naming a greatest hits compilation "Volume One" or "Part One" is considered a commercial risk, as it implies future volumes that may not materialize. This is exemplified by Daryl Hall and John Oates' "Rock and Soul Part One," which never saw a "Part Two" despite them having more hits. "It's always a risk to name a greatest hits compilation volume one or part one."
