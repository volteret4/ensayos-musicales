# artist - Stevie Wonder
## albums
**Innervisions (1973)** : This album, described as "superlative," was a number four hit on the Billboard album chart and won Album of the Year at the 1974 Grammy Awards. This win was part of Stevie Wonder's acclaimed three-peat of Album of the Year Grammys. "1973's Superlative Intervisions, a number four billboard album, took album of the year in 74."
**Fulfillingness' First Finale (1974)** : Released in the summer of 1974 as a follow-up to "Innervisions," this album's momentum was boosted by the previous Grammy win, sending it to number one on the charts. It subsequently won the top prize at the 1975 Grammy Awards, marking Wonder's second consecutive Album of the Year win. "And then that summer, Wonder dropped his follow-up LP, Fulfilliness First Finale. And this time, the momentum from the Grammy win sent the album to number one. And then, Fulfilliness won the top prize at the 75 Grammy Awards."
**Songs in the Key of Life (1976)** : This "double LP Magnum Opus" was delivered 18 months after "Fulfillingness' First Finale" and was a landmark release, opening on the charts at number one, a first for Stevie Wonder. It went on to win the Grammy for Album of the Year in 1977, completing his unprecedented three-peat of Album of the Year awards. "18 months later, when Wonder belatedly delivered his double LP Magnum Opus songs in the key of life, the album opened on the charts at number one, a first for Stevie Wonder. And of course, took the Grammy the following way to."
## curiosities
**Grammy Awards Three-Peat - Album of the Year (1974, 1975, 1977)** : Stevie Wonder achieved an unparalleled Grammy streak, winning Album of the Year three times in just four years without public complaint, a feat described as "perhaps the most beloved Grammy streak of all time." His albums "Innervisions" (1973), "Fulfillingness' First Finale" (1974), and "Songs in the Key of Life" (1976) all secured the top prize, with the Grammys actively boosting his profile with each win. "The moment when a singular, wondrous artist went three for three in just four years and nobody complained."
**Chart Momentum from Grammys** : Stevie Wonder's Grammy wins significantly boosted his chart performance, with "Fulfillingness' First Finale" reaching number one after its win and "Songs in the Key of Life" debuting at number one, a first for him. "the Grammys were both good two and good four Stevie, boosting his profile with each win."
**Paul Simon's Acceptance Speech Mention (1976)** : During the 1976 Grammy Awards, Paul Simon, upon accepting his Album of the Year award for "Still Crazy After All These Years," offered a humorous and pointed thank you to Stevie Wonder for "not making an album this year," acknowledging Wonder's dominance. "And at most of all, I like to think Stevie Wonder who didn't make an album this year."

## albums
**Nick of Time (1989)** : This was Bonnie Raitt's tenth studio album, released just before her 40th birthday. Initially, Capital Records pushed the album onto adult contemporary radio, leading it into the top 30 in the summer of 1989, and it went gold by Grammy night 1990, on its way to platinum. Five weeks after her Grammy triumph, "Nick of Time" climbed to number one on the Billboard album chart and eventually achieved quintuple platinum status. The album is considered "arguably the most celebrated Grammy winning album of the year of all time" and "the recording Academy's best Cinderella story," profoundly changing her career. "Released in 1989, Nick of Time was Bonnie Rates' 10th studio album."

## curiosities
**Career Reboot - Grammy Win (1990)** : Bonnie Raitt's career underwent a dramatic transformation after her "Nick of Time" album won Album of the Year at the 1990 Grammy Awards, marking her as the "recording Academy's best Cinderella story." Prior to this, she had been toiling since the early 70s without a top 40 pop hit or top 20 LP, battling drug and alcohol addiction, from which she got sober in the late 80s. The Grammy win rebooted her career, turning her into a pop star at age 40, leading to consistent top 20 album chart placements and top 40 hits throughout the 90s. "You can literally divide Bonnie Rates' career into before she won the Grammy and after she won the Grammy."
**Sobriety and Label Change (Late 1980s)** : In the late 1980s, Bonnie Raitt battled and overcame drug and alcohol addiction, getting sober, and subsequently signed with a new label, Capital Records, just before the release of her career-defining album "Nick of Time." "She had battled drug and alcohol addiction, got sober in the late 80s and signed to a new label, Capital Records."
