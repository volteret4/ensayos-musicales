# curiosity

**Definition of Imperial Phase** : The "imperial phase" is a term developed by "chart nerds" to describe a moment in an artist's career when they seemingly can do no wrong, scoring hits with almost anything and widening the lane for what can be a hit, often trying something "outside of their wheelhouse." "The imperial phase is when an act is so big, they can score a hit with just about anything."
**Origin of "Imperial Phase" Term** : The term "imperial phase" was coined by Neil Tennant of Pet Shop Boys in the album notes for an early 2000s reissue of their 1987 album "Actually." He described feeling that they "had the secret of contemporary pop music" and "entered our imperial phase," providing an unwitting gift to music fandom by naming a concept pop critics had observed for decades. "This sentence turned out to be Neil Tennant's unwitting gift to music fandom and scholarship."
**Codification of "Imperial Phase" Term** : Music critic Tom Ewing, in his 2010 Pitchfork magazine column titled "Imperial," expanded on Neil Tennant's concept and codified the term "imperial phase."
