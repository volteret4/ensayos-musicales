## songs
**Yellow Submarine (1966)** : This song, one of The Beatles' most famous "kitty anthems," originally began as a morose composition by John Lennon that sounded "almost emo and sad." The box set outtakes allow listeners to literally hear the song evolving from its melancholic beginning into the fun, kid-friendly "Ringo Bop" it eventually became. It was primarily a Lennon-McCartney composition, with Ringo Starr performing the lead vocal. "You can literally hear them evolving, Yellow Submarine into the fun Kitty Ringo Bop that it became in the outtakes of this box set."
**Do You Want to Know a Secret (1963)** : Featured on their first album, *Please Please Me*, this song was sung by George Harrison but written by John Lennon and Paul McCartney (primarily a Lennon composition). "Song by George Harrison, but written by I think that was primarily, I want to say a Lenin composition, but it's anyway, it's Lenin McCartney in any case."
**Act Naturally (1965)** : This Buck Owens cover was sung by Ringo Starr, but it was not written by him. "Several of the covers like Buck Owens' Act naturally were sung by Ringo, but obviously not written by Ringo."
**A Little Help from My Friends (1967)** : Featured on *Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band*, this song was a Lennon-McCartney composition specifically written for Ringo Starr to sing. "with a little help from my friends on Sargent Pepper is another one, a Lenin McCartney composition that was sung by Ringo."
**Don't Pass Me By (1968)** : Featured on *The White Album*, this song marked Ringo Starr's first solo composition with The Beatles. Prior to this, John and Paul frequently wrote songs for Ringo to sing. "Ringo didn't start writing songs until the white album when he wrote Don't Pass Me By."
**Octopus's Garden (1969)** : Featured on *Abbey Road*, this was Ringo Starr's second solo composition for The Beatles. "The only two Ringo compositions are Don't Pass Me By on the White Album and Octopus is Garden on Abbey Road."

## curiosities
**50th Anniversary Box Set Remastering Series** : Between approximately 2017 and 2022, Apple Records released a series of 50th-anniversary collections of classic Beatles albums. These sets featured the original albums remastered by Giles Martin, the son of famed Beatles producer George Martin. Giles Martin's process involved going back to the original master tapes to deconstruct and reconstruct each album, aiming to make it sound as much like the original as possible but with a fresher quality and improved stereo separation, as stereo technology was new in the 1960s. Albums released in this series included *Sgt. Pepper*, *The White Album*, *Abbey Road*, *Let It Be*, and *Revolver*. "Apple Records, the Beatles still existent label, was putting out these collections, these 50th anniversary collections, where the classic Beatles albums were remastered by Giles Martin."
**Shift from Singles to Albums** : The Beatles played a pivotal role in shifting music commerce in the 1960s from a focus on singles to a focus on full-length albums. The album *Revolver* is considered a key pivot point for this change, occurring just before *Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band*. "I talked about how the Beatles kind of shifted music commerce from the single to the album in the 60s. And, you know, revolver is kind of the pivot point for that, just before Sargent Pepper."
**Ringo Starr's Songwriting Contributions** : Contrary to a common misconception that Ringo Starr composed songs he sang, he did not begin writing songs for The Beatles until *The White Album* (1968), where he penned "Don't Pass Me By." His only other composition for the band was "Octopus's Garden" on *Abbey Road* (1969). Before this, John Lennon and Paul McCartney frequently wrote songs for Ringo to sing, such as "A Little Help from My Friends," and he also sang cover tracks like Buck Owens' "Act Naturally." "Ringo didn't start writing songs until the white album when he wrote Don't Pass Me By."
