## curiosities
**Birth and Early Life** : Born Robert John Lang in Rhodesia, which is now Zimbabwe, and raised in Durban, South Africa. His parents were German expatriates, with his father being a mining engineer and his mother from a high society German family. "Born Robert John Lang, his friends and colleagues called him Mut Lang."
**Nickname Origin** : His parents nicknamed him "Mut" at an early age, though his friends knew him by his middle name, John. "his parents, both German expatriates, a mining engineer father and a mother from a high society German family, nicknamed him Mut at an early age."
**Musical Influence - Slim Whitman** : As a young boy, Mut loved country music, particularly the keening, yodeling vocals of American country and western star Slim Whitman. This influence would later echo in the high-pitched hard rock vocals featured in his production work. "young Mut loved country music and the keening vocals of Slim Whitman in particular."
**Career Start - Commercials** : After high school, Mut moved to the Johannesburg area and took a job producing commercials, which was his entry into recording before forming his first bands. "he only finally began recording after school when he moved to the Johannesburg area and took a job producing commercials."
**Brief Attempt as Frontline Artist** : Mut briefly tried being a frontman artist but later gave it up, choosing to focus on production. He intentionally kept his name off album titles, unlike other producer-artists. "He briefly tried being a front line artist then basically gave it up for life."
**Signature Sound - Anthemic Global Pop** : Lang established himself as the author of an anthemic brand of global pop, characterized by a crunching, pumping sound he mined for hits from the 1980s deep into the 21st century. His productions often made genres sound distinctively "Mut-like" regardless of the artist. "Mut would score many more hits across billboards charts, establishing himself as the author of an anthemic brand of global pop."
**Signature Sound - Rock Maximalism** : The overriding theme of every Mut Lang production is rock maximalism, even when working outside of the rock genre. He helped define and arguably perfected arena rock from ACDC's breakthrough into the 21st century. "The overriding theme of every Mut Lang production is rock maximalism, even when the genre is not rock."
**Media Reclusiveness** : Despite the ostentatiousness of his signature sound, Mut Lang has largely remained invisible throughout his career, driven by an intense desire for privacy. There is remarkably little photographic evidence of him, and he has sat for very few interviews. "Mut Lang has largely remained invisible throughout his career."
**Rare Interview and Quote** : One of his very few interviews was for a 1989 BBC documentary about Def Leppard, which is one of the only video footage of him still available online. In a quote attributed to him about a 90s hit he was working on with Brian Adams, he candidly expressed his commercial goal. "it's pretty good, but I can't see them dropping their knickers for it in Kansas."
**Long Path to Breakthrough** : Mut's start was humble, taking him nearly a decade to find his distinctive sound and break through as a chart force. "It took him nearly a decade to find his sound and break through as a chart force."
**Collaborative Reputation** : He earned a reputation for meticulousness in the studio, with artists like Graham Parker finding his instrument-by-instrument recording approach stiff. However, he was also lauded for his fastidious production and rich sound. "Mut was already earning a reputation for meticulousness in the studio and Parker didn't like Mut's stiff instrument by instrument recording approach."
**Pivotal Role in ACDC's Success** : Lang was pivotal in ACDC's massive success, particularly with the "Back in Black" album, where he honed their sound to a diamond-hard core and guided them through the catastrophe of Bon Scott's death. "Mut Lang was pivotal to that success."
**Breaking UK Chart Record** : In 1991, Mut Lang produced Brian Adams' hit, which spent 16 weeks at number one on the official UK singles chart, breaking Slim Whitman's 1955 record of 11 weeks at number one with "Rose Marie." "That chart record would not be beaten for 36 years until a hit by Brian Adams in 1991 spent 16 weeks official UK chart and that single was produced by you guessed it, Mut Lang."
**Long Gap in Album Production (1984-1987)** : Mut went almost four years between 1984 and 1987 without producing a full released album, not due to a change in his chart fortunes, but because of his total focus on a new LP for one of his previous clients, which would become one of the biggest albums in glam metal history and the most painstaking project of his career. "Mut went almost four years between 1984 and 1987 without producing a full released album."

## albums
**High and Dry (1981)** : The band's second album, where Mut Lang helped transform them into an efficient conduit for cybernetic, gleaming glam metal hooks. It didn't go gold or platinum in 1981 and didn't generate any pop hits initially, but MTV exposure helped it enter the top 40 by late 1981 and ride the album chart throughout much of 1982. "Mutt Lang helped transform them into an efficient conduit for cybernetic gleaming glam metal hooks."
**Pyromania (1983)** : The band's third album, produced by Mut Lang who "pulled out all the stops" to make them pop stars. Lang used innovative sampling techniques to make the drums sound bigger, co-wrote all the songs, and provided backing vocals as part of the masked chorus behind Joe Elliott. The album rose to number two on the LP chart, only blocked from number one by Michael Jackson's "Thriller," and spun off three top 40 pop hits, an unprecedented feat for a metal album at the time. "Pyromani rose all the way to number two on the LP's chart."
**Pyromania (1983) - Sales** : "Pyromani won one of the 10 best-selling albums of 1983, going sex-touple platinum in America in its first year." Two decades later, it achieved diamond status for sales of 10 million copies. "Two decades later, it went diamond for sales of 10 million copies."
**Hysteria (1987)** : The title track from this album rose to number 10 on Billboard's Hot 100 in March 1988, marking the band's first top 10 American pop hit. Mut Lang's production resulted in a sleek and polished sound, characterized as chiming hair metal with synth-pop overtones. "Hysteria, the title track from their latest smash album rose to number 10 on Billboard's Hot 100."

## songs
**Bringin' on the Heartbreak (1981)** : A single from the album "High and Dry," which, despite not becoming a chart hit for several years, helped break the band in 1981 due to exposure on the then-brand-new video channel MTV, where the band's polished and glossy music videos, led by frontman Joe Elliott, were frequently played. "Bring in on the heartbreak, a 1981 single from High and Dry that wouldn't become a chart hit for several years helped break the band in 1981 anyway thanks to the then brand new video channel MTV."
**Photograph (1983)** : The first single from "Pyromania," featuring Mut Lang's production, which included a soaring pre-chorus. It was a huge MTV hit, a massive rock radio hit (spending six weeks atop Billboard's album rock chart), and a sizable pop hit, reaching number 12 on the Hot 100 in the spring of 1983. "Photograph was a huge MTV hit."
**Rock of Ages (1983)** : The second single from "Pyromania," which reached number 16 on the pop charts. Mut Lang's pigeon German way of counting the band in, "Gunter Gleben-Glauton Globen," can be heard at the beginning of the song. The title of the song was later borrowed for a Broadway musical about 80s rock. "That by the way is Mutt Lang saying Gunter Gleben-Glauton Globen, his pigeon German way of counting the band in instead of one, two, three, four."
**Foolin' (1983)** : The follow-up single to "Rock of Ages," which reached number 28 on the pop charts, contributing to "Pyromania"'s unprecedented success of spinning off three top 40 pop hits for a metal album. "the follow-up single Fullen reached number 28."

"Lang was something of a hero to deaf leopard when a near tragedy befell the band in the 80s."

# artist - ACDC

## members

## genres

## labels

## albums
**Highway to Hell (1979)** : Released in the summer of 1979 and produced by Mut Lang, this album changed the fortunes of both the band and Lang. It peaked on the album chart at number 17 in November 1979, becoming the first top 20 LP and first US Platinum album for both. Lang condensed the band's sound to its essence, showcasing Bon Scott's piercing vocals with a crisp, sleek, and muscular sound, embodying the quintessence of the "Mut sound." "Released in the summer of 1979, High Way to Hell changed everyone's fortunes."
**Back in Black (1980)** : Produced by Mut Lang after Bon Scott's death, this album, featuring new vocalist Brian Johnson and a "non-more Black" album cover, served as both a funeral eulogy to Scott and a welcome party for Johnson. It became ACDC's biggest LP ever and one of the top-selling albums of all time, with producers considering it a reference standard for hard rock acoustics. "Back in Black would also wind up not only ACDC's biggest LP ever but one of the top-selling albums period of all time."
**Back in Black (1980) - Chart Performance and Sales** : This album peaked on the Billboard album chart at number four and, as of the recording, has been on the Billboard 200 for nearly 650 weeks. It is certified double diamond in America with sales and streams of 27 million, and globally, its sales are estimated at 50 million copies. "Back in black peaked on the Billboard album chart at number four."
**For Those About to Rock We Salute You (1981)** : The third consecutive LP produced by Mut Lang for ACDC, which dropped just before Christmas 1981 and immediately became ACDC's first American number one album, knocking Foreigner's "4" from the top spot. After three weeks, it gave the number one position back to the Mut-produced Foreigner LP. "The third consecutive LP he produced for ACDC for those about to rock we salute you, dropped just before Christmas of 81."

## songs
**You Shook Me All Night Long (1980)** : A "down and dirty, raunchy dance anthem" from the "Back in Black" album, which Mut Lang gave a compressed but crisp crunch. It received heavy album rock radio play in America and cracked the pop Top 40, reaching number 35. "You shook me all night long, which Mut gave a compressed but crisp crunch."
**Back in Black (1980)** : The "straining riff-tastic title track" of the album, which Mut Lang arranged with "spaced out syncopated grooves like a funk record." The song's funkiness led to it being sampled in over 50 hip-hop tracks. It received heavy album rock radio play and hit number 37 on the pop charts. "Back in Black, the song was so funky. It would later be sampled in more than 50 hip-hop tracks."

## curiosities
**Live Performance Aesthetic** : Guitarist Angus Young was known for appearing on stage in a schoolboy uniform, a distinctive part of the band's live image. "You may recall guitarist Angus Young appearing on stage in a schoolboy uniform."
**Early American Chart Struggles** : Through 1978, none of ACDC's LPs climbed higher than number 113 on the Billboard album chart, prompting Atlantic Records executives to seek a more radio-friendly producer to elevate them from a live draw to a major US chart-topper. "Through 1978, none of ACDC's LPs got higher on the Billboard album chart than number 113."
**Bon Scott's Tragic Death** : Just six months after "Highway to Hell" was released, lead singer Bon Scott was found dead of alcohol poisoning at age 33 in February 1980. The band considered disbanding, as Scott was considered critical to their image. "Just six months after the release of the High Way to Hell album in February 1980, Bonscott was found dead of alcohol poisoning at age 33."
**Mut Lang's Guidance After Tragedy** : After encouragement from friends and family, Mut Lang nudged ACDC to replace Bon Scott with another shrieking singer, Brian Johnson, formerly of the band Jordy, who possessed an even higher-pitched wail than Scott. "it was Mut who nudged them to replace Bonscott with another shrieking singer."
**Name Origin** : The band chose the initials ACDC, standing for alternating current and direct current, because they felt it told people they were electric. "They say they chose it because it tells people they're electric."
**End of Collaboration with Mut Lang** : After "For Those About to Rock" and "4," Mut Lang moved on from ACDC, never producing another LP for the group again. "After for those about to rock and for, Mutt Lang moved on from ACDC and Foriner."

It spent ten weeks stuck at number two on the Hot 100, parked behind Olivia Newton-John's "Physical" and Darryl Hall and John Oates' "I Can't Go For That," setting a Hot 100 record for the longest-running number two hit without ever reaching number one (tied with Missy Elliott's "Work It"). "Waiting for a girl like you was heartbreaking in more ways than one."

It became the most hit-packed album to date for both The Cars and Mut, generating five top 40 singles in 1984 and 1985, and going triple platinum by 1985. "The result was 1984's Heartbeat City, which became the most hit-packed album to date for both the Cars and Mutt."

## songs
**You Might Think (1984)** : One of the three biggest singles from "Heartbeat City," this song was a number seven MTV smash and remains a classic hit radio staple. "The number seven MTV Smash you might think."
**Magic (1984)** : Another major single from "Heartbeat City," this song was a number 12 Summer Anthem and is still considered a classic hit radio staple. "the number 12 Summer Anthem Magic."
**Drive (1984)** : The biggest hit from "Heartbeat City" and The Cars' first major power ballad. Written by Rick Ocasic for his bandmate Ben Orr to sing, it was set off by epic Mut Lang production and became a number three hit. "And the biggest of them all, the Cars' first major power ballad, a song written by Rick Ocasic for his bandmate Ben Oar to sing, set off by epic Mutt Lang production, the number three hit draw."

## curiosities
**Previous Producer** : Prior to Mut Lang, The Cars had worked with British producer Roy Thomas Baker, famed for producing Queen, on their first four platinum LPs from 1978 through 1982. "Through their first four platinum LPs from 1978 through 82, the Rick Ocasic-led quintet from Boston had worked with British producer Roy Thomas Baker, famed for producing Queen."
**Post-Mutt Challenges and Split** : The only potential downside to "Heartbeat City" was that The Cars found Mut Lang's meticulousness impossible to follow up, leading the band to split three years later. "If there was any downside to the album, it's that the cars found Mutt Lang's meticulousness impossible to follow up."

# genre - Arena Rock

## curiosities
**Mut Lang's Perfection of the Genre** : Although the term and subgenre of arena rock predated Mut Lang's heyday, he arguably perfected it, taking a "crunching, pumping sound" from ACDC's breakthrough right through his 21st-century work with bands like Maroon 5 and Nickelback, making his power ballads designed for stadiums. "Indeed Mut helped define what came to be known as arena rock, that term and the subgenre arena rock predated his heyday, but he arguably perfected arena rock."

# curiosity

**Billboard Hot 100 Longest Running Number Two** : Foreigner's "Waiting for a Girl Like You" holds the Hot 100 record for the longest running number two hit without ever reaching number one, having spent ten weeks stuck at number two. This record is tied with Missy Elliott's 2002 hit "Work It," which also stalled at number two for ten weeks. "To this day waiting for a girl like you still holds the Hot 100 record as the longest running number two hit without ever reaching number one."
**UK Singles Chart Longevity Record** : Slim Whitman's 1955 hit "Rose Marie" set a record by spending 11 weeks at number one on the UK singles chart, a benchmark that remained unbroken for 36 years until Brian Adams' 1991 hit, produced by Mut Lang, spent 16 weeks at number one. "That chart record would not be beaten for 36 years until a hit by Brian Adams in 1991 spent 16 weeks official UK chart and that single was produced by you guessed it, Mut Lang."
**Hip Hop Era Producer-Artists** : The hip hop era generated several producers who successfully moved from working behind the mixing board to performing at the mic, including the "Virginia Beach Polymaths": Pharrell Williams, Missy Elliott, and Timberland. "The hip hop era has generated several producers who moved from the mixing board to the mic."
**Broadway Musical from 80s Rock Title** : The title of Def Leppard's 1983 hit song "Rock of Ages" was borrowed two decades later for a Broadway musical about 80s rock, highlighting its lasting cultural impact. "Rock of Ages, whose title, by the way, would be borrowed two decades later for a Broadway musical about 80s Rock."
**"Gunter Gleben-Glauton Globen"** : This phrase, spoken by Mut Lang at the beginning of Def Leppard's "Rock of Ages," was his unique "pigeon German way of counting the band in instead of one, two, three, four." "That by the way is Mutt Lang saying Gunter Gleben-Glauton Globen, his pigeon German way of counting the band in instead of one, two, three, four."
