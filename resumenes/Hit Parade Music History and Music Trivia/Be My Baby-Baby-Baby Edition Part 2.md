"By 1995, the future Spice Girls had ditched the management team who had auditioned them out of concern that they weren't listening to the ladies' ideas."

# genre - Girl Group

## curiosities
**Early 60s Heyday** : The girl group concept experienced its heyday in the early 1960s, with vocal ensembles like The Shirelles, The Ronettes, and The Shangri-La's dominating the charts. "its early 60s heyday, when vocal ensembles like the charelles, the Ronets, and the Shangri-La's were commanding the charts."
**Survival Through the 70s and 80s** : After the dominance of The Supremes in the late 1960s, the initial girl group era receded, but the genre was kept alive through the 1970s and 1980s by disco, new wave, and freestyle dance music. "But disco, new wave, and freestyle dance music kept the Girl Group alive through the 70s and 80s."
**90s Renaissance** : The 1990s saw a "Girl Group Renaissance," driven by a new generation of vocalists who emerged after the rise of Hip Hop, infusing the pop charts with a fresh sound. "We're now entering the 90s, when a new generation of sisters with voices, who emerged after Hip Hop, are about to infuse the Hip-Parade with a Girl Group Renaissance."
**Producer-Driven Approach in 90s** : Unlike the more organically formed groups of the 1960s, girl groups in the 1990s and beyond were typically conceived and assembled within the music industry machine by producers, similar to the creation of boy bands like New Kids on the Block and Backstreet Boys. "This producer-driven approach would become the norm for girl groups in the 90s. While the 60s groups often formed organically in a specific place, but then were managed or sadly often manipulated in the studio by producers and other svingales, the girl groups of the 90s and beyond were conceived within the music industry machine."
**21st Century "Angle" Requirement** : In the 21st century, girl groups increasingly needed a unique "angle" or brand beyond just their music to break through, as exemplified by groups like The Pussycat Dolls and Danity Kane. "Even more than in the 90s, girl groups in the 21st century seemed to require an angle beyond music, a way to build a brand."
**American Dry Spell in 2000s** : There was an "American Girl Group Dry Spell" for the first half of the 2000s, with most significant girl group activity and number one hits occurring in the UK with groups like Sugababes and Girls Aloud. "The American Girl Group Dry Spell wasn't finally broken until halfway through the Aughts, when a troupe of burlesque dancers who called themselves the Pussycat dolls broke big on the Billboard charts."
**"Girl Power" Slogan** : Although not originated by the Spice Girls themselves, the slogan "Girl Power" was heartily embraced by them and, in retrospect, was acknowledged by critics to have had a profound impact on millennial girls' formative memories, akin to the Lilith Fair. "The girls came up with neither these monikers nor their eventual slogan Girl Power, but they heartily embraced them as essential to their brand."
**Solidarity and Friendship** : The underlying premise of girl groups throughout pop history, from The Shirelles to The Shangri-La's, TLC, and Destiny's Child, is that "women in harmony generate solidarity." This ethos is epitomized by the Spice Girls' debut single message, "Friendship never ends." "The premise of the Spice Girls is the premise of the girl group throughout pop history, from the sherelles to the Shangri-La's TLC to Destiny's Child. Women in harmony generate solidarity. Perhaps the Spice's debut single had it right. Friendship never ends."
