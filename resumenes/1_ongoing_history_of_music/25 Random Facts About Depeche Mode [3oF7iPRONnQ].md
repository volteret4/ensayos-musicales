## curiosities
**Band Name Origin and Mis-translation** : The name "Depeche Mode" was adopted from a French fashion magazine. For many years, it was commonly, but incorrectly, translated as "fast fashion." The more accurate translation, reflecting the spirit of the original French, should be "fashion news update." "Your name depech mode was taken from the title of a French fashion magazine. I heard that. And for years, that has been wrongly translated as fast fashion."
**Psychiatrist on Tour (Songs of Faith and Devotion Tour)** : During the tour for "Songs of Faith and Devotion," the band hired a psychiatrist as part of their 120-member road crew, paying him $4,000 a week. This was intended to help band members, particularly Dave Gahan, with depression and addiction during the grueling 156 shows over 15 months, although the psychiatrist ended up being used by everyone but Dave. "This shrink was part of a 120 member staff that accompanied the band on the tour for the album Songs of Faith and Devotion."
**Drug Dealer on Tour (Songs of Faith and Devotion Tour)** : Despite the presence of a psychiatrist, the 120-member staff on the "Songs of Faith and Devotion" tour also included a drug dealer. This individual was responsible for supplying members like Dave Gahan with cocaine and heroin, directly counteracting the psychiatrist's efforts to provide counseling for drug issues. "But then again, that 120 member staff also included a drug dealer to make sure that guys like Dave Gahan were well supplied with cocaine and heroin."
**Fighter Jet Crash near Alan Wilder (September 1, 1994)** : On September 1, 1994, while former keyboardist Alan Wilder was driving in the Scottish countryside, a Royal Air Force Tornado fighter jet on a training mission crashed less than 200 yards from his car. Wilder was covered in debris, and the two pilots were killed instantly, an event that profoundly affected his outlook on life. "It was September 1, 1994. Former keyboardist Alan Wilder was driving through the Scottish countryside when on a nowhere, a royal Air Force tornado, a fighter jet on a training mission roared over his car and crashed, less than 200 yards away."
**Martin Gore's Real Estate Investments** : Martin Gore, in addition to being the group's chief songwriter, is a successful real estate investor, with one commercial property in London's revitalized Docklands District. This property generates over a quarter of a million pounds in rent annually, and ironically, one of its tenants is a branch of the bank where Gore worked as a teller in the late 1970s. "One of his commercial properties is in the revitalized Docklands District of London. It generates more than a quarter of a million pounds in rent every year. And in a delicious piece of irony, one of the tenants is a branch of the bank where Martin used to work as a teller in the late 1970s."
**Andrew Fletcher's Restaurant in London** : Andrew Fletcher owns a restaurant in London called Gascones, located just north of the famous Abbey Road Recording Studios in St. John's Wood. The restaurant specializes in international cuisine and includes a bar known as the Lotus Lounge. "Alan Fletcher has a restaurant in London called Gascones. It specializes in international cuisine and can be found just north of the famous Abbey Road Recording Studios in St. John's Wood in North London."
**Martin Gore's Binge Drinking (Hamburg, Germany)** : Martin Gore was known for severe binge drinking, with one account stating he once consumed 67 pints of lager in a single 11-hour sitting in Hamburg, Germany. This extreme feat for a man who is five feet eight inches tall required many trips to the bathroom. "One report says that Martin Gore once drank 67 pints of logger in a single sitting in Hamburg, Germany."
**Dave Gahan's Second Marriage and Tattoo** : Dave Gahan married his second wife, Teresa Conway, at the Graceland Chapel in Las Vegas, with an Elvis impersonator providing the music. As a pledge of love, Dave commissioned a tattoo reading "TC TTM/FG," which stands for "Teresa Conway to the motherf***ing Gahan." "Dave Gahan married his second wife Teresa Conway at the Graceland Chapel in Las Vegas. The music for the service was provided by an Alvison personator as part of Dave's pledge of love. He had a new tattoo commissioned yet rent TC TTM slash FG. It stands for Teresa Conway to the mother Blanker Gahan."
**Early Deal with Mute Records (1980)** : In the fall of 1980, Depeche Mode was one of the first bands to sign with Mute Records, an all-synthesizer-based label. Their verbal agreement stipulated a 50/50 split of proceeds from domestic releases and a 70/30 split (band/label) for international sales, a handshake deal that remained unwritten until 1986 due to strong trust. "They were one of the first people to sign into a new all synthesizer base label called mute in the fall of 1980. They struck a deal whereby all proceeds from the domestic releases would be split 50 50 with the label and the money from international sales would be split 70 30."
**Religious Background of Members** : Founding members Andrew Fletcher and Vince Clark were born-again Christians as teenagers, and their singing of hymns and devotional songs initially attracted Martin Gore and Dave Gahan, who were described as "essentially pagan unbelievers." Spiritually, Gore and Gahan have since "lapsed." "Andrew Fletcher and founding member Vince Clark were born again Christians as teenagers. Martin Gore and Dave Gahan, both essentially pagan unbelievers, were drawn to Andrew and Vince because they liked the hymns and devotional songs that they had heard Andrew and Vince sing."
**Evolution of Instrument Use** : Guitars and real drums have periodically fallen in and out of favor with Depeche Mode; there was a significant period in their career where their sound was 100% electronic and synthesized. In their very early days in the late 1970s, guitars were part of their sound but were seen as restrictive and logistically difficult to transport compared to synthesizers. "One we felt that guitars restricted us. The sound of the guitars very limited. And we found synthesizers very interesting, just the sounds that you could get on them."
**Decadence of the Devotional Tour (1993-1994)** : The world tour of 1993-1994, known as the "Devotional Tour," was marked by gargantuan excess and decadence, including backstage VIP areas and hotel suites "outfitted like the set of a porn movie," where parties fueled by liquid ecstasy often lasted up to three days. Allegations of S&M were made, but widespread drunkenness and drug use made reliable record-keeping difficult. "Each night up to 20 of the most beautiful women in the audience were invited into special backstage VIP areas and hotel suites that were outfitted like the set of a porn movie."
**Dave Gahan and Darabomonte Arrested in Montreal (September 8, 1993)** : On September 8, 1993, Dave Gahan and touring keyboardist Darabomonte were arrested and jailed in Montreal after Dave punched a hotel concierge. This incident occurred after a loud party in their suite led to numerous complaints, prompting the hotel to cut their power. "September 8, 1993. Dave Cajon and Touring Keyboard as Darabomonte are arrested and jailed after Dave punched the concierge of a Montreal hotel in the mouth."
**Martin Gore Arrested in Denver (November 4, 1993)** : On November 4, 1993, Martin Gore was arrested at the Weston Hotel in Denver. The arrest happened after he refused to turn down his stereo at 4 in the morning following complaints about the noise. "November 4, 1993. Martin Gore was arrested at the Weston Hotel in Denver after refusing to turn down the stereo at 4 in the morning."
**Dave Gahan's Mid-Show Heart Attack (October 8, 1993)** : On October 8, 1993, Dave Gahan suffered a heart attack mid-show in New Orleans but remarkably managed to finish the set before being removed backstage by paramedics. "Dave is removed from backstage at a show in New Orleans by paramedics after he suffered a heart attack. He actually had a heart attack mid-show, but he somehow managed to finish the set."
**Dave Gahan's Stage Fall (July 1994)** : In July 1994, Dave Gahan fell 12 feet off the stage in Indianapolis, resulting in multiple broken ribs. "We might as well include the time in July of 1994 when he fell 12 feet off the stage in Indianapolis and broke a bunch of ribs."
**Miranda Sex Garden Audience Reaction (Devotional Tour)** : During the Devotional Tour, opening act Miranda Sex Garden quit after a particularly bad show where the audience pelted them with rotten meat and dog excrement. "One of the bands who opened for Depeche Mode on this tour was a group called Miranda Sex Garden. They quit the tour after the audience pelted them with rotten meat and dog excrement after one particularly bad show."
**Post-Tour Health Crisis (Devotional Tour Aftermath)** : The aftermath of the Devotional Tour saw several band members facing severe health issues: Alan Wilder was diagnosed with kidney and gall stones during the South African leg; Andrew Fletcher nearly suffered a nervous breakdown and left the tour for treatment after his sister died of stomach cancer; Martin Gore experienced a grand mal seizure during a business meeting due to excessive drugs and alcohol; and Dave Gahan's severe drug use led to extreme weight loss (under 100 pounds) and an incident where he bit a journalist on the neck. "During the South African leg, Alan Wilder was diagnosed with kidney stones and then gall stones."
**Vince Clark's Departure (December 1, 1981)** : On December 1, 1981, chief songwriter Vince Clark left Depeche Mode, believing the group had little future and that he could achieve greater success on his own. While his subsequent projects like Yazoo and Eurasia had hits, Depeche Mode's overall album sales (over 50 million) ultimately surpassed his post-band career. "The date was December 1, 1981. The first member and chief son writer Vince Clark decided that he wanted out. Why? Well, he didn't think the group had much of a future."
**Alan Wilder's Departure (June 1, 1995)** : On June 1, 1995, Alan Wilder officially left Depeche Mode, unable to tolerate the band's excesses, his limited role in songwriting, and his bandmates. The earlier fighter plane crash incident also contributed to his reassessment of his life and priorities. "On June 1st, 1995, Alan Wilder made it official. He could not take it anymore. He could not stand the excess. He could not stand not being able to write more of the music. And he could not stand any of his bandmates."
**Dave Gahan's Parental Issues** : Dave Gahan's childhood was affected by parental issues; his parents divorced when he was three, and his mother remarried. His stepfather was presented as his biological father until Dave discovered the truth at age 10, which profoundly impacted him. "Dave has had some issues regarding his parentage when he was three his parents divorced and his mom remarried. Stepdad was then present to Dave as his real dad. Stayed that way until he was 10, which is when he found out the truth, and that really messed him up for a while."
**Dave Gahan's Teenage Delinquency and Art College** : As a teenager, Dave Gahan appeared in juvenile court three times for offenses like car theft and vandalism, and he held 20 different jobs within six months. His enrollment in art college was the only thing that saved him from a path of delinquency. "As a teenager, he went to juvenile court three times, for things like stealing cars and vandalism. He went through 20 different jobs in just six months."
**Dave Gahan's British Display Society Award** : After three years of art college, Dave Gahan earned a British Display Society Award, qualifying him to design store display windows. This provided a practical skill should his music career ever falter. "After three years, he ended up with something called a British Display Society Award. Which means if the singing thing ever falls through, he has qualified to design display windows for stores."
**Dave Gahan's "Gesh" Piercing** : Dave Gahan once sported a "gesh," a piercing in the area behind the scrotum, which he believed was the most erotic place to be pierced, despite its pain and discomfort. He eventually removed it after one of his children innocently questioned its presence during a shower. "He once had something called a geesh, GEISH, a geesh. This is a piercing of the area. It's the behind the scrotum."
**Dave Gahan's Near-Death Experience and Recovery (1995-1996)** : From late spring 1995 through to a brief clinical death in 1996, Dave Gahan's life was consumed by heroin addiction, costing $3,000 daily, leading to extreme dissociation, conversations with stuffed animals (including a Bugs Bunny doll), and two suicide attempts. On May 27, 1996, after an attempt to record with Martin Gore, Gahan injected cocaine and heroin at the Sunset Marquis Hotel in Hollywood, leading to full cardiac arrest and two minutes of clinical death. He recalled a vision of his wife screaming for him to return and a voice stating, "This is wrong, you don't get to decide." His recovery began after summer 1996, largely influenced by the threat of green card revocation from the American government if he didn't enter rehab and remain sober. He has been sober since June 6, 1996, now runs four times a week, lives in New York's West Village, and is married to Jennifer, his third wife. "He was for all intents and purposes clinically dead for two full minutes. He now says he remembers the vision of his wife screaming at him to come back to this plane."
**Andrew Fletcher's Tour Hobbies** : When Depeche Mode is on tour, Andrew Fletcher enjoys organizing walking tours of the cities they visit. "When the band is on tour, Andrew Fletcher likes to organize walking tours of whatever city they happen to be in."
**Band Members' Current Residences** : Martin Gore resides in Santa Barbara, California, where his ex-wife owns a spa. Dave Gahan lives in New York, and Andrew Fletcher has his home in the Maidstone area of London. "So we got Martin living in California. There's Dave in New York and Andrew in London."
**Dressing Room Arrangements on Tour** : On the road, Martin Gore and Andrew Fletcher share a dressing room, while Dave Gahan has his own. This arrangement is primarily attributed to Dave's need to avoid the temptations of backstage life. "When they're on the road, Martin and Andrew share a dressing room. Dave gets his own. That always more to Dave's need to be away from the temptations of backstage life than anything else."
**Incident with Hells Angels in Bardemodrid** : Three members of Depeche Mode were once beaten up by a group of Hells Angels outside of Bardemodrid. The incident was reportedly Dave Gahan's fault, as he looked at a biker girl "kind of funny." "Three members of the band were once beaten up by a group of hell's angels outside of Bardemodrid. Apparently it was Dave's fault for looking at a biker girl kind of funny."
**Band's Enduring Longevity** : Despite a tumultuous history filled with personal struggles, lineup changes, and extreme touring conditions, no one involved with Depeche Mode – including the band members, their record company, management, or families – can believe that the band is still together. "No one, including the band, their record company, their management, and their families can believe that after all this, the Peshmo is still together."

# concert - Devotional Tour (1993-1994)

## curiosities
**Extreme Decadence and Excess** : The Devotional Tour, a world tour spanning 1993 and 1994, was characterized by "unbelievably decadent" excesses. It featured backstage VIP areas and hotel suites designed like porn movie sets, where parties fueled by liquid ecstasy (preferred over pills) could last up to three days, though the widespread intoxication made reliable accounts of events difficult. "This was the unbelievably decadent devotional tour. Item 13. Each night up to 20 of the most beautiful women in the audience were invited into special backstage VIP areas and hotel suites that were outfitted like the set of a porn movie."
**Staffing and Health Issues** : The tour's 120-member staff included both a psychiatrist and, controversially, a drug dealer, reflecting the band's struggles with depression and addiction. The tour's grueling nature and lifestyle led to significant health crises among the band members, including Alan Wilder's kidney and gall stones, Andrew Fletcher's nervous breakdown, Martin Gore's grand mal seizure, and Dave Gahan's severe drug abuse, which caused extreme weight loss and violent outbursts. "This shrink was part of a 120 member staff that accompanied the band on the tour for the album Songs of Faith and Devotion."
**Multiple Incidents and Arrests** : The tour was fraught with incidents, including the arrests of Dave Gahan and touring keyboardist Darabomonte in Montreal after Gahan punched a hotel concierge (September 8, 1993), and Martin Gore's arrest in Denver for refusing to turn down his stereo (November 4, 1993). Dave Gahan also suffered a mid-show heart attack in New Orleans (October 8, 1993) and fell 12 feet off stage in Indianapolis, breaking ribs (July 1994). "Dave Cajon and Touring Keyboard as Darabomonte are arrested and jailed after Dave punched the concierge of a Montreal hotel in the mouth."
