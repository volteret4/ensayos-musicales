## albums
**Questamation (2009)** : This was the band's first full album, released on March 9, 2009. It included their first song "Cloudboy" and the single "Three Purple Butterflies." "USS from their first full inked album, Questamation, which was released on March 9, 2009."
**Approved EP (2011)** : This record was considered a "long EP" or "short album" following "Welding the Sea Drive" and "Questamation." It marked a significant shift in Ash's creative process, born out of confronting his control issues and learning to collaborate with producer Tog Salter and Jay. This shift was critical for the band's growth. "NA OK from the approved EP, a record born out of a lot of angst and fear and growth and acceptance."
**Advanced Basics (2014)** : This album was released in 2014, two years after the initial European tour with Walk Off the Earth. It had two versions: a regular version and an international album. The creation process was challenging for Ash, who was recovering from an eight-month drug bender, making it a "one foot in front of the other" effort. "So our song shipwreck which is one of the most important songs to our band came out of that album came out of facing again another round of like you know the PhD dissertation with the demons."
**New World Alphabet (2017)** : Referred to as a "long EP" or "seven sevens," this album was written after a period of touring. The first four songs were written in California, reflecting a desire to shake up the process by being in the desert, mountains, and by the ocean. The album takes a darker turn from song five onwards, as the band returned to Canada in winter. "And again another album that does extremely well for you."

## curiosities
**Chlorophyll Harmonic Stage Setup** : In his earlier abstract performance life, Ash would have ten plants on stage, set up microphones in front of them, and include "plant solos," incorporating the concept of the "chlorophyll harmonic." "I would have like 10 plants on stage with me, which I called the chlorophyll harmonic. And I have put set microphones up in front of them and there'd be like plants, solos and stuff."
**Neuroplasticity and Brain Disruption Experiment** : Ash was deeply interested in neuroplasticity and disrupting the brain. He would perform on stage with a stereo playing two different cassette tapes simultaneously—one with "how to heal your inner child" and another with Helmet and House of Pain's "You're Just Another Victim" from the "Judgment Night" soundtrack—while holding the stereo to his head and staring at the audience to create discomfort. "I was just so interested in the whole idea of neuroplasticity and the whole idea of of rewind, like just disrupting, you know, just rupting the brain."
**Jay's "Human Kabab" Nickname Origin** : Jay's nickname originated from his first-year university History 120 thesis paper about Vlad Drakuhl (Vlad the Impaler) from 15th-century Transylvania/Romania. Vlad's brutal punishment of impaling enemies' heads on stakes outside his castle reminded Jay of a "human kabab scenario," which he later adopted as his DJ handle at Trent University after a local promoter asked him for one. "And I thought I would, I thought it was funny because to me it was like a human kabab scenario."
**Ash's Control Freak Tendencies** : Ash struggled with perfectionism and the need to control every aspect of the creative process, stemming from childhood trauma related to his father leaving. This manifested as an inability to collaborate, even with Jay, and caused a hysterical reaction during initial sessions with producer Tog Salter near Lake Erie, almost leading to the abandonment of the "Approved EP." "You start to learn a lot about yourself and situations like that. You go, oh my, and then all of a sudden, you just, you go, boom, boom, boom, boom, boom, you get right to the heart of it and you just go, oh my goodness. I'm like, I don't trust anybody else to do anything because if other people do something, it might not be perfect."
**Therapeutic Songwriting Process for "Approved EP"** : The writing sessions for the "Approved EP" with Tog Salter became a deep psychological experience for Ash. Salter, acting like a "songwriter shaman," helped Ash confront his control issues, past traumas, and "learned helplessness," enabling him to embrace collaboration and "go big" in his music. "That I learned a lot, that learned helplessness, that scared little boy, talk solter, like, extracted, like a friggin singer, singer, singer, songwriter, shaman, like, extracted this big voice out of me."
**Jay's Role as A&R and Creative Partner** : When Ash first approached Jay with demos like "Cloudboy" and "Antivenom," Jay felt like he was acting as an A&R (Artists and Repertoire) person, recognizing Ash's talent akin to "Kirk Cobain in Canada." Jay simultaneously developed his own creativity by learning from Ash and incorporating his influences from 80s pop and 90s hip hop into the band's sound. "I also felt like I was A and R in him."
**Evolution of USS's Creative Process** : The band's songwriting process dramatically changed after Ash's "armor was cracked" in 2011. It transformed from a "fragmented creative experience" of piecing together disparate ideas (like 10,000 puzzle pieces from 700 different puzzles) to a more receptive, direct "download of information," comparable to Mozart's seemingly effortless composition as described by Salieri in "Amadeus." "We've arrived at a point where enough of these layers, enough of the rust-gunked goop, has been polished from the antenna to allow this absolute receptivity that is allowing for these experiences with creativity that are all very similar to what Solary was describing with Mozart, where it's just a direct download of information."
**Inspiration from a Nashville Bird Sanctuary** : Ash's new creative process, described as "channeling," was exemplified during a trip to Nashville. After a profound, emotional experience in a bird sanctuary, where he cried due to its beauty and shared it with a couple, he directly channeled that conversation into their next single. "I just sang our conversation. I sang our, almost our whole conversation like this, our next single that's coming out is this song that is this conversation that from the serendipitous meeting in the woods in Nashville, Tennessee."
**Ash's Drug Bender and Album Completion Challenge (2013)** : After writing "This Is The Best" at the end of an eight-month "alphabet drug" bender, Ash went "lights out" for a while in November-December 2013, making the completion of the "Advanced Basics" album extremely difficult. He described being at "1% power" and unable to reach an "outlet." "Well, what was amazing Alan is that when we wrote and recorded this is the best, it was right at the end of like an eight month bender that I was on and I was on like every alphabet drug when we were in the studio and we recorded the song and just put it out."
**Jay's Patience and Band Cohesion** : Jay's extraordinary patience and unwavering commitment were crucial in keeping USS together, especially during Ash's personal struggles like his drug bender. Ash explicitly states he "could not believe that Jay stuck around" and credits him with the "patience of a saint," acknowledging that few others would have tolerated the difficulties. "I am a lucky I'm I'm I've like won the lottery of life like meeting him because there really isn't anyone. I don't know if there's another human that could have tolerated everything that's that's gone on."
**Bowling as a Post-Addiction Hobby** : After stopping drugs, Ash found he had no hobbies outside of music and drug use. To discover what "human beings did," he started bowling with friends every Tuesday night for $8.88 at World Bowl. This new social activity indirectly sparked the creation of the song "Who's With Me." "When I stopped doing drugs I couldn't I didn't know what human beings did I didn't understand because I didn't have any hobbies it was just in music and drugs and so I was like what do you do like what do what do people do and so I was like bowling."
**Ash's Stage Presence Transformation** : Ash revealed that until recently, he stopped wearing a hat and sunglasses on stage and had kept his eyes closed the entire time during performances, due to being "completely incapable of connection with other humans." The success of his "public experiment" in personal growth allowed him to shed these defenses. "I just stopped wearing a hat and sunglasses on stage because I would have my eyes closed the whole time really."
**Early EP Similarities to Twenty One Pilots** : The band noted that their first EP, "From Welding the Sea Drive," released years prior, contained sounds and styles that were later adopted by Twenty One Pilots, suggesting USS was ahead of its time. "You can go back to our first EP from welding the C drive and you can hear what 21 pilots is doing in the last couple of years."
**Jay Parsons' Radio Career** : Jay began his radio career in 2012 when Al Ford from Sonic in Edmonton asked him to host a show for an hour every weekend, where he played remixes from the rock world with a modern electronic take. In 2017, he joined 102.1 The Edge to host "The Military Mixtape" on Saturday nights, considering it one of the greatest experiences of his life outside USS, fulfilling a dream on a station he grew up listening to. "And so for an hour every weekend I would do remixes from all over the rock world and it was like a modern electronic take on what was happening at all rock fast forward to 2017 um 102.1 the edge uh came on board and uh gave me the military mixtape and that's on Saturday nights and it's been one of the greatest experiences of my life outside of USS."

"I was just so interested in the whole idea of neuroplasticity and the whole idea of of rewind, like just disrupting, you know, just rupting the brain."

"I was just crying because it was the most beautiful moment that I'd ever had in my life and a couple walked by and I stopped them and I said, can I tell you something?"
