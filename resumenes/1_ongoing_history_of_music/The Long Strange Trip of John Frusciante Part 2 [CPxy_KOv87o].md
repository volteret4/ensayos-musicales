## curiosities
**Teenage Guitar Phenom (1988)** : John Frusciante was a teenage guitar phenom who was a big fan of the Red Hot Chili Peppers before joining them at 19 years old in 1988. "John was this teenage guitar phenom, who became a big fan of the Red Haunchley Peppers."
**First Departure and Heroin Addiction (1992-1998)** : In May 1992, in the middle of a tour of Japan, John Frusciante quit the Red Hot Chili Peppers due to becoming angry and disillusioned with fame. For the next six years, he descended into a severe heroin addiction and abject poverty, which led to a house fire and health issues that nearly killed him. "So in May 1992, in the middle of a tour of Japan, he quit, and for the next six years, he slid into a terrible heroin addiction and abject poverty."
**Physical Deterioration During Addiction (Late 1996)** : An LA Times interview from late 1996 described John Frusciante's severe physical state: his upper teeth were nearly gone, replaced by tiny slivers peeking through rotten gums, and his lower teeth were thin and brown. His lips were pale, dry, and coated with thick spit, his hair shorn, fingernails blackened by blood or missing, and his feet and legs poked with burns from cigarette ashes. His flesh also bore bruises, scabs, and scars. "His upper teeth are nearly gone now. They have been replaced by tiny slivers of off-white peaked through rotten gums."
**Addiction as a Form of Control (Late 1996)** : During his addiction, John Frusciante claimed his life had improved since he became a junkie and found happiness, believing that without heroin, he had no control over his thoughts. He barely ate, preferring a high-protein supplement, and his room at the Chateau Marmont reeked of urine, feces, and death. "I just decided that without heroin, I have no control over what thoughts take over my brain."
**Loss of Home and Money (Year not specified)** : After his house burned down and was rebuilt, John Frusciante failed to pay for it, either due to lack of money or forgetfulness, leading the bank to foreclose on the property. He also accidentally gave his last $2,000 as a tip to a cab driver. "The bank foreclosed on the property and he lost it. He'd accidentally given his last $2,000 as a tip to a capi."
**Intervention and Recovery (Late 1990s)** : John Frusciante was eventually saved by the intervention of Flea and Anthony Kiedis, who provided tough love and facilitated extensive rehabilitation, leading to his return to the band. "It was only when Fle and Anthony intervened that he was saved, thanks to some tough love and a lot of rehab."
**Clean Living and Dedication (Late 1990s-2000s)** : After rejoining the Red Hot Chili Peppers in late 1998, John Frusciante became completely clean from drugs and alcohol, abstained from sexual activity (celibacy), and dedicated 45 minutes daily to yoga. He expressed feeling much higher and happier through his dedication to music, health foods, and yoga than he ever did on drugs. "Between my dedication to trying to constantly be a better musician and eating my health foods and doing yoga, I feel so much more high than I did for the last few years of doing drugs."
**Renewed Love for Music and Study (Early 2000s)** : John Frusciante's love for music was renewed, leading him to spend hours searching record stores for old doo-wop records to study harmonies. He also watched a lot of Star Trek and spent time with his girlfriend, Stella Schnabel, focusing on long stretches of songwriting instead of benders. "John's love of music was renewed. He spent hours coming through record stores looking for old dew-op records because he wanted to study harmonies."
**Learning from Other Guitarists (Early 2000s)** : During the period of the "By the Way" album, John Frusciante continued to learn about guitar by studying the styles of Johnny Marr of The Smiths, John McGeoch from Siouxsie and the Banshees, and Andy Partridge of XTC, seeking new chords, techniques, and textures. "He was learning even more about the guitar by studying the styles of guys like Johnny Mar of the Smiths, John from Susie in the Banshees and even Andy Partridge of XTC."
**Second Departure and Occult Interests (2007-2009)** : After the "Stadium Arcadium" tour wrapped up in August 2007, John Frusciante decided to quit the band on July 29, 2009. He stated that touring had made him mentally off-balanced, and he delved deeply into the occult as a way to escape tour life. The band members expressed relief at his departure, believing he was gone for good. "I had become quite off-balanced mentally those last couple of years we toured. As the tour went on, I got deep into the occult which became a way of escaping the mindset of tour life."
**Post-Second Departure Electronic Focus (2009-2019)** : Following his second departure from the Red Hot Chili Peppers, John Frusciante retreated into making solo albums that were heavily electronic. He declared he had lost interest in traditional rock band songwriting and wanted to explore music he knew nothing about, forming an electronic trio called Speed Dealer Mums and releasing an EP in December 2010. "He said he'd lost interest in traditional rock band songwriting and wanted to try something he knew nothing about."
**Online Music Releases (Post-2009)** : John Frusciante actively posted new music on SoundCloud and Bandcamp after his second departure from the Red Hot Chili Peppers, releasing material under his own name and also under the alias Trickfinger. "He was on SoundCloud and Bandcamp a lot posting new music. Some material was released under his own name, but there was also some material that used the name Trickfinger."
**Third Return to Red Hot Chili Peppers (2019)** : Sometime in 2019, John Frusciante re-engaged with Anthony Kiedis and Flea, proposing to work together again after becoming re-excited about playing guitar following his electronic music explorations. He started jamming secretly with Flea, and the musical connection was so strong that Flea quickly decided John needed to be back in the band. "After spending all that time working with electronic music, John had become excited about playing the guitar again. Could they possibly work together again?"
**Soul Searching and Growth (2019-Present)** : John Frusciante explained his third return to the Red Hot Chili Peppers by saying he had done soul searching, grown, and wanted another chance to "do it right," appearing strong, confident, and together, with words like "brothers" and "family" used to describe the band's relationship. He reflected on his desire to keep things fresh and avoid the "rockstar mentality." "John says he'd done some soul searching. He'd grown and he wanted another chance to do it right."
**Juggling Ego and Appreciation (Present)** : John Frusciante discussed the challenge of appreciating fans who put musicians on a pedestal while keeping one's ego in check, viewing ego as a "tricky opponent." "On one hand, you want to be appreciative of the people who put you on a pedestal. On the other hand, you've got to keep your ego in check."

## albums
**Blood Sugar Sex Magik (1991)** : John Frusciante's contributions to this album were immediate and significant, turning the band into global superstars. This album's approach, described as "punk funk," was revisited for the subsequent "Californication" album. "His impact was immediate, and his contributions to the Blood Sugar Sex Magic album in 1991 turned the band into global superstars."
**Californication (1999)** : Released on June 8, 1999, this album marked John Frusciante's return as guitarist after a six-year absence due to heroin addiction. It was an even bigger hit than "Blood Sugar Sex Magik," selling almost 20 million copies worldwide and winning a Grammy, solidifying Frusciante's crucial role in the band's sound. "The California Cation album released on June 8th, 1999, was an even bigger hit than blood sugar sex magic, selling almost 20 million copies around the planet, along with winning a Grammy."
**By the Way (2002)** : Released on July 9, 2002, this album was considered by John Frusciante to be one of the best things he'd ever been involved in. Despite the era of Napster, it managed to sell close to 10 million copies globally. "The Chili Peppers with a title track of 2002's By the Way album and even in the era of Napster, that album managed to sell close to 10 million copies."
**Stadium Arcadium (2006)** : This massive double album, originally intended to be a triple album, was released on May 9, 2006, and showcased John Frusciante's new approaches to improvised solos, funky rhythms, and melodic lessons. It sold millions, became the first Chili Peppers album to reach number one on the American album charts, and won five out of seven Grammy nominations. "Stadium Arcadium came out on May 9 2006 and it sold in the millions. It was the first Chili Peppers album to reach number one on the American album charts and it was nominated for seven Grammys and it won five."
**I'm With You (2011)** : One of two albums released by the Red Hot Chili Peppers with Josh Klinghoffer on guitar after John Frusciante's second departure. "He's with a group for two albums, I'm with you in 2011 and The Getaway in 2016."
**The Getaway (2016)** : The second album released by the Red Hot Chili Peppers with Josh Klinghoffer on guitar. "He's with a group for two albums, I'm with you in 2011 and The Getaway in 2016."
**Unlimited Love (2022)** : This album, featuring 17 songs and running over 73 minutes, marked John Frusciante's third return to the band. Writing for it began almost immediately after his return, with Rick Rubin producing over a hundred songs during the pandemic. Released on February 4, 2022, it received mostly positive reviews and debuted at number one in 16 countries, their first number one since "Stadium Arcadium." "The Unlimited Love album contains 17 songs and ran for more than 73 minutes, the longest single album since Blood Sugar Six Magic."
**Return of the Dream Canteen (Year not specified)** : Announced shortly after "Unlimited Love," this album features another 17 songs spanning over 75 minutes, making it their longest single studio album. "Another 17 song spending a over 75 minutes making it their longest single studio album."

## curiosities
**Band's State in Late 1998** : When John Frusciante rejoined in late 1998, the band was in a difficult place, with Flea having issues, Anthony Kiedis battling addictions again, and creative blocks amid divorces and new babies. "The band was not in a good place. Fle was having issues. Anthony was battling addictions again. There were divorces and new babies and creative blocks."
**Return to "Punk Funk" Approach (Late 1990s)** : After failed attempts to create an electronic-tinged record with producers like The Chemical Brothers or David Bowie, the band decided to revert to the "punk funk" style that made "Blood Sugar Sex Magik" successful, an approach that proved fruitful for "Californication." "So it was decided that the peppers would go back to the approach of blood sugar sex magic. In other words, the punk funk that made them successful in the first place."
**Collaborative Songwriting Process** : The band views drum, bass, and guitar parts as equally important to vocals or song construction, emphasizing freedom and equality for each member to play their best. Songs often start with a riff or groove from one member, or sometimes a complete arrangement brought in by John Frusciante. "in our band, we just we feel that a drum part or a bass part is or a guitar part, it's equal to the vocal or to the or to the construction of the song."
**Band Hiatus and Confidence Crisis (2007-2019)** : After the "Stadium Arcadium" tour ended in August 2007, the band agreed to a long rest, with Anthony Kiedis being particularly tired. During John Frusciante's second absence, the band experienced a "crisis of confidence" and felt "lost" in terms of direction and inspiration. "After the long stadium Arcadium tour wrapped up in August 2007, the band agreed to take a nice long rest. Anthony was especially tired and needed the break."
**Firing of Josh Klinghoffer (2019)** : Flea initiated John Frusciante's third return after secret jamming sessions, and when he brought it up with Anthony and Chad, Josh Klinghoffer was fired within half a second to reinstate Frusciante. "When he brought it up with Anthony and Chad, it took another half second for Josh to be fired and for John to be reinstated in the Chili Peppers."
**COVID-19 Pandemic and Songwriting (2020)** : The COVID-19 pandemic provided an unexpected opportunity for the band, with nothing to do but write songs. They wrote constantly from January to October 2020, resulting in over a hundred songs for their new album. "Writing a new album started almost immediately...They wrote constantly from January to October 2020 as COVID raged all around them. As Anthony said, with the pandemic, there was nothing to do but write songs."
**Classic Lineup Returns (2019-Present)** : With John Frusciante back for his third stint, the "classic Red Hot Chili Peppers lineup" is reunited, described as cleaner, more sober, older, and wiser than ever before. "The classic Red Hot Chili Peppers lineup is back together. Everyone is cleaner and more sober than they've ever been. Everyone may be a lot older too but there's certainly a lot wiser."

# curiosity

**Artist vs. Musician Distinction** : A distinction is made between "musicians" who make art and have other interests and abilities (like Bono), and "artists" who live solely to create art and may be ill-equipped for day-to-day tasks or life in general. "Artists make art, of course, but they also have other interests. They have other pursuits, abilities, hobbies. Bono is an example of an artist."
**Pitfalls of the Artistic Brain** : Individuals wired as pure artists, with a supernatural ability to create beauty, often face pitfalls such as trouble with daily tasks (money, groceries, schedules, social situations), and susceptibility to depression, anxiety, bipolar disorder, autism spectrum, and addictions (alcohol, drugs, sex). "They might have trouble with day-to-day tasks like handling money, shopping for groceries, keeping a schedule, or being able to deal with everyday social situations."
**Exacerbation of Pitfalls by Fame** : For well-known artists, the insane pressure, crazy schedules, hedonistic lifestyle, and living in a bubble of fame can worsen these inherent pitfalls, leading to severe negative consequences. "And if they manage to become well known for their arts, the insane pressure, crazy schedules, hedonistic lifestyle, and living in a bubble of fame can exacerbate things until, well, until things get very, very bad."
**Rick Rubin as Producer** : Rick Rubin was approached to produce the Red Hot Chili Peppers' album following John Frusciante's second return and agreed to the gig, indicating a significant role in the band's post-addiction success. He was also brought back to produce "Unlimited Love." "But with John back and with Rick Rubin agreeing to produce the next album and a new management deal, things were starting to look up at least a little bit."
**Producer's Role in Songwriting** : According to John Frusciante, a producer like Rick Rubin does not write or play music but rather "organizes things" for the band. "Well, how about you and Rick Rubin? I mean, well, he doesn't write the music, he doesn't play anything. He more organizes things."
**Los Angeles as a Creative Influence** : John Frusciante expressed that he and the band tried to be sensitive enough to incorporate the good feelings and beauty of Los Angeles and California into their music, even acknowledging the city's "seeminess." He disliked Sunset Boulevard, preferring to drive down Hollywood Boulevard for inspiration. "Yeah, there's a lot of beauty to be found in Los Angeles. There's a lot of in California. There's a lot of good feelings in the air and we try to make ourselves sensitive enough for musicians and songwriters to be able to incorporate where we live into what we do."
**COVID-19 Pandemic as Creative Catalyst (2020)** : The pandemic, by limiting other activities, provided an environment where the Red Hot Chili Peppers could focus intensely on songwriting, leading to over a hundred new songs for "Unlimited Love." "They wrote constantly from January to October 2020 as COVID raged all around them. As Anthony said, with the pandemic, there was nothing to do but write songs."
