## albums
**Deadlines EP (2008) – Self-Released and Re-issued** : This EP was initially self-released by the band, featuring songs recorded around 2005-2006, some in Tim Oxford's basement. It was later re-issued by Dino Lone Records as both a physical CD and digital release. The re-issue included a flyer inserted into the City and Colour record that was distributed across Canada. "It was 2005, 2006 songs? Yeah, yeah, fine."
**Jackson Square (2008) – Debut Full Album** : This album is named after a somewhat dated, brutalist-architecture mall in downtown Hamilton, which for the band symbolized the city's core and its inspiring "fight." The album reflects the city's atmosphere before widespread gentrification, capturing stories that might take place there, such as the one in the song "Abigail." "It felt... I don't know. It was the core of Hamilton and I think for us, there was a part of it that we found to be really like inspiring about Hamilton, sort of like the fight in the city and around that time as well in 2008, I feel like the city was... There was like this light in the distance that we could sort of see that it was changing, that there was this creative community."

## songs
**The Boss Is Coming (2008)** : This song's title stemmed from the band members' experiences working "terrible jobs" during university summers, including Mike DeAngeles picking up needles at Jackson Square, working in a distribution warehouse, and being fired from East Side Marios. Musically, it reflected the guitar-driven indie rock of bands like the Constantine's and Winter Sleep that the band was listening to at the time. "Yeah, I mean, we all were working terrible jobs when we were either through the summers in university like Mike picked up needles on the top of Jackson Square. I'd run a real bad ones."
**Abigail** : This song is mentioned as an example of a story that, in the band's mind, could have taken place within the Jackson Square mall, reflecting the atmosphere of downtown Hamilton at the time. "There's a song called Abigail that in my mind, I don't know, it didn't take place in Jackson Square mall but it seemed like the sort of story that would take place there."
**The Ballad of Hugo Chavez (2008)** : This song was written about Hugo Chavez during a period when he was seen by many on the left as a hopeful leader, although the band acknowledges that history has since been less kind to his reputation. The song specifically focuses on his time in jail in the 1990s, where he learned about socialist revolutions, an experience that shaped the rest of his life. The band sought to write about interesting, complicated characters. "At the time, he was a bit of a darling of people on the left because I think people in Venezuela and around the world saw him as a real hopeful leader."
**John Lennon (2008)** : This song is focused on the Westdale area around McMaster University in Hamilton, painting a picture of summer life there. The title refers to "John Lennon '67," a period when Lennon was miserable despite being at the peak of his career, with the lyricist using this as a self-deprecating comparison to his own youthful confusion and self-pity, despite having no real reason for it. The song also features a line inspired by a friend's joke about getting Frank McCourt to write a foreword for a girl's book about her charmed life, contrasting it with McCourt's memoir "Angela's Ashes." "I'm kind of poking fun at myself, I think, in that song where I'm laughing about sort of just being young and confused and having no real, no real reason to feel sorry for yourself, but feeling that way."
**Whistleblower (2011)** : This track is from their 2011 album *Michigan Left* and was performed live at 102.1 The Edge in Toronto. "Arkels in a live version of Whistleblower, a track from their 2011 album Michigan Left."

## curiosities
**Band Formation at McMaster University (2004)** : The band's core began forming on the first and second days Max Kermann attended McMaster University in Hamilton in 2004. Max, who was primarily interested in finding bandmates, met Mike DeAngeles on day one by asking about his favorite bands (The Weakerthans) and if he played guitar. On day two, Nick Dica introduced himself to Max because Max was wearing a Sam Roberts Band T-shirt, and upon learning Nick played bass, he was also invited to join. "You're literally the first and second day that you were in university."
**Adoption of Hamilton as Home City** : Although the members are originally from different parts of Ontario (Newmarket, London, Mississauga, Guelph, and Toronto), the band adopted Hamilton as its official home. This is because the band was "born" there, all its formative experiences, first sold-out shows, and connections to the music scene happened in Hamilton, making it their true musical home. "But as a band all our formative experiences happened in Hamilton, you know, like we're never going to know the clubs in any other city the way we know the clubs in Hamilton or you know, our connections to the music scene and all those really important things for us happened in Hamilton, you know."
**Initial Band Name: Charlemagne (2004-2007/2008)** : The band initially performed under the name Charlemagne from their first gig at the McMaster Battle of the Bands until late 2007 or early 2008. They were forced to change their name after another band named Charlemagne on MySpace threatened to sue them. "We call ourselves Charlemagne as our first band name."
**Name Change to Arkells (Late 2007/Early 2008)** : The name change from Charlemagne to Arkells occurred under extreme pressure when Dino Lone Records needed a new name in 45 minutes for an EP flyer to be inserted into the City and Colour record. Max Kermann and Mike DeAngeles, who lived on Arkell Street at the time, proposed the name in a last-minute effort, noting its 60s Motown-esque sound. "So then we frantically got on the phone. I was at the library and McMaster, I still remember. And we called everyone, we got to change the name now because we've been sitting on this kind of decision for a long time and we just hadn't got around to it."
**Extensive Touring and Performances** : The band has a reputation for playing a vast number of shows, appearing "anywhere and everywhere" asked by their label or radio stations, including pop-up gigs and spontaneous busking. They estimate they've played hundreds of shows per year for over a decade, working between 175 and 200 days annually on musically directed activities. This volume of performance is suggested to have potentially surpassed the Ramones' lifetime total of 2250 shows. "We do say we do like a hundred a year for, we've been doing this for 12 years. 1200 shows."
**Stadium-Filling Status** : Arkells has achieved significant popularity, capable of filling stadiums for their performances. "Not only can they fill a stadium, more on that in a bit, they have a statue that allowed them to blag free airline tickets to the Olympics after doing nothing more than a quick tweet."
**Free Olympics Trip via Tweet** : The band leveraged their public profile and a "statue" (likely a metaphorical reference to their influence or popularity) to secure free airline tickets to the Olympics simply by sending a tweet. "They have a statue that allowed them to blag free airline tickets to the Olympics after doing nothing more than a quick tweet."
**Collaborative Songwriting Process** : While Max Kermann typically initiates the songs and is the chief lyricist, the songwriting process is highly collaborative. The other band members provide significant input through editing and questioning, ensuring clarity and improving the lyrics. All members share in the songwriting split, reflecting an "all for one, one for all" approach. "Yeah, I mean, I start the songs, but the more we do it, the more there's a pretty good heavy dose of editing going on from the guys where I go, is there a better way to say this? Or is this part clear? So it is very collaborative in that sense."

## curiosities
**Early Life and Musical Development** : Born in Quebec and grew up in Newmarket, Ontario. He decided to pursue music at a young age, starting on guitar but admitting he played it "very badly" for a couple of years before switching to drums around age 12, becoming "all in" immediately. "Yeah, I was born in Quebec, grew up in New Market, Ontario, and I did not go to McMaster. I wanted to be a musician at a pretty young age. So I started playing guitar very badly for a couple years and then moved over to the drums at about 12."
**Entry into Arkells and Professionalism** : Tim Oxford joined Arkells after attending audio school and networking through various pub gigs. He was introduced to the band by their previous keyboard player who mentioned they needed a drummer. His professionalism and working equipment (unlike the previous drummer's broken hi-hats) immediately impressed the band. He initially recorded a 5-song EP with them, some of which was done in his basement facilities. "Then when we met Tim, it was like, whoa, this guy is really professional because he was a very accomplished drummer, but his equipment also worked."
**Home Recording Studio** : Tim Oxford not only proved to be an accomplished drummer but also possessed recording skills from audio school and had facilities in his basement, which the band utilized for early recordings. "Yeah, because here's a drummer who's not only a great drummer, but also went to school for recording and had facilities in his basement."
**Initial Hesitation about Joining Arkells** : Despite his professionalism, Tim Oxford initially had reservations about joining Arkells. After his first tryout, he told his partner, Sarah, that he probably wouldn't go back, but changed his mind quickly when they began recording together shortly after. "If I'm being totally honest, I went home and I remember Sarah being like, hey, my lady saying, hey, how to go? You were trying out with that band or whatever. And I was like, I don't think I probably won't go back initially."
