"We've got the Red Star, the Solar Federation, and the priests who run the temples of Syrikes in their command over existence. And then we have the rebel guitar player who rises up and then commits suicide."

# artist - Green Day

## members
Billie Joe Armstrong

## genres
Rock Opera

## albums
**American Idiot (2004)** : This album, released in the fall, was conceived as a full-blown rock opera. The band had fallen out of favor with fans and was perceived as "old and tired," with talk of their potential demise; this rock opera was considered a last-ditch effort to revive their career. Legend states that the album was an "accident," originating after the band lost master tapes for a planned record titled *Cigarettes and Valentines*. Instead of re-recording, they started from scratch, with each member contributing short fragments of music. These fragments sounded good together and, when strung into longer pieces, inspired the band to revisit older rock operas like those by The Who and Andrew Lloyd Webber's *Jesus Christ Superstar* for guidance. "An American idiot was actually an accident."
**American Idiot (Stage Musical Adaptation)** : When *American Idiot* was adapted for the stage, elements were rearranged and expanded for greater clarity. The character Jesus of Suburbia was renamed Johnny, St. Jimmy's suicide became metaphorical, and new characters like Toney and a "De Nue mom" were introduced. The stage version's themes include acceptance and hope, concluding with a cast rendition of "Good Riddance (Time of Your Life)." "When American idiot was adapted for a stage musical, things were rearranged and expanded to help everything make more sense."
**21st Century Breakdown (2009)** : Released on May 15, 2009, this album is another rock opera, set in Detroit. It follows main characters Christian and Gloria as they navigate life in America after the presidency of George W. Bush. To enhance narrative clarity, the album is divided into three acts: "Heroes and Cons," "Charlatans and Saints," and "Horseshoes and Hand Grenades." Billie Joe Armstrong frequently sings in the first person, suggesting that Christian and Gloria serve as vehicles for his autobiography, with personal truths embedded in the lyrics, reflecting his life and the lives of those around him. "Twenty-first century breakdown which came out on May 15th 2009 is set in Detroit."

## songs
**Boulevard of Broken Dreams** : This was the second single released from *American Idiot*. The song is sung from the perspective of Jesus of Suburbia, reflecting his experience of finding the city overwhelming. "Boulevard of Broken Dreams was the second single from the record and it's sung from the point of Jesus of suburbia who finds the city a little too much for him."

## curiosities
**American Idiot Success** : *American Idiot* became not only the most successful alt rock concept album ever but also one of the most successful concept records across all genres. Its success, with about 10 million copies sold (including associated CDs and DVDs), demonstrated to other songwriters and record labels that concept albums could be a viable and popular format. "American Idiots, which is not only the most successful alt rock concept album ever, but one of the most successful concept records ever, period."
**Cigarettes and Valentines (Lost Album)** : Before *American Idiot*, Green Day had recorded an album titled *Cigarettes and Valentines*. However, the master tapes were mysteriously lost, leading the band to start from scratch and ultimately create *American Idiot* instead. "Legend has it that after the band mysteriously lost the master tapes to a record that was supposed to be called cigarettes and valentines, they decided to start from scratch instead of remaking that record."

# genre - Concept Album

## curiosities
**Definition and Structure** : A concept album is a record where a story or a series of themes link everything together, front to back. It transforms a four-minute musical short story into an hour-long musical novel, breaking a complex narrative into constituent parts spread across multiple songs. "A record where the story or a series of themes link everything together back to front and front to back."
**Historical Popularity (1960s-1970s)** : Concept albums were a "big business" in the late 1960s and 1970s, particularly embraced by progressive rock bands, with greater complexity often correlating to higher regard. "Concept albums were big business in the late 1960s and 1970s."
**Decline with Punk Rock** : The advent of punk rock, characterized by its two-minute, two-chord songs, led to concept albums falling out of favor for an extended period. "When punk rock came along with its two-minute two-chord songs of burning hate, the concept album fell out of favor for a very long time."
**21st Century Resurgence** : The concept album was slowly resurrected and experienced a significant surge in popularity by the early 21st century, with many coming from alt rock bands. "The concept album was slowly and carefully resurrected. And by the time we got to the first years of the 21st century, bam! We got tons of them."
**Market Strategy in Digital Age** : Record labels favored concept albums in the 21st century as a strategy to combat the trend of fans buying individual songs via iTunes and downloading services. The format inherently encourages consumers to purchase entire albums for the narrative to make sense, thereby helping to keep the idea of the album alive. "A concept record didn't make sense unless you bought the whole thing, right?"
**Nature of Conception** : Concept albums do not always have to be grand, complex, or highly theatrical projects; they can sometimes be quiet and poignant, as exemplified by The Antlers' album *Hospice*. "Concept albums don't have to be big, complex, highly theatrical projects. Sometimes they can be just quiet and poignant."
