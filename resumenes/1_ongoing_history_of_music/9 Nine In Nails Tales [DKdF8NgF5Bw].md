## albums
**Pretty Hate Machine (1989)** : This album was Trent Reznor's debut record for TVT Records. The label owner, Steve Gottlieb, initially thought he was signing a "dancing technopop band" like Depeche Mode, but found the music too "heavy, too dark, too loud, too angry," describing it as an "abortion." Despite the label's initial distaste, they issued the record, and it sold half a million copies, largely through word of mouth. The album was later allowed to go out of print by TVT Records, making copies very hard to get. "Because Steve had spent so much recording it, TVT issued the record anyway and much to his surprise the records sold quite well."
**Broken (EP)** : Described by Trent Reznor as "eight songs of ultra fast chunks of death." A disturbing video art project for this EP was made in collaboration with Peter Christopherson of Throbbing Gristle and Coil, intending to push boundaries of taste and tolerance. The video, starring performance artist Bob Flanagan, involved non-simulated acts of torture, which Trent found too disturbing to officially release. It was distributed on VHS tapes to friends and subsequently leaked as a bootleg. "A far more disturbing video was made on purpose for the nine inch nails EP called Broken, which Trent describes as eight songs of ultra fast chunks of death."
**The Downward Spiral (1994)** : This album was recorded in a house at 10,050 Cielo Drive in Benedict Canyon, Los Angeles, which Trent Reznor rented in July 1992. Unbeknownst to Trent at the time of signing the rental agreement, this was the site of the Manson family murders in 1969. The studio gear, installed over three months, was set up in the very room where Sharon Tate and Jay Sebring were murdered. Equipment mysteriously and frequently broke down. Trent named this temporary studio "Le Pig," and the word "pig" features prominently on the album, referencing the word written in Sharon Tate's blood on the living room wall by the Manson family. Trent described the recording period as "the blackest, the bleakest," stating, "I hate what I do. I don't like doing it anymore."
**The Fragile (1999)** : This album was recorded during a period when Trent Reznor was struggling with deep depression and severe addictions to alcohol and drugs like coke and heroin, following the release of "The Downward Spiral." Despite his personal struggles, he managed to complete the album. He later reflected that he "whitetnuckled it through the recording of The Fragile."
**With Teeth (2005)** : This album marked a turning point for Trent Reznor, following his intensive rehab and recovery from addiction in 2001. It contains 13 songs, seven of which feature Dave Grohl on drums. "This lead us to the With Teeth album in 2005."
**Year Zero (2007)** : This was Nine Inch Nails' fifth studio album and also an alternate reality game (ARG). The project incorporated concepts that offered a scathing critique of the US government and predicted a fundamentalist democracy by 2022. The ARG involved multiple websites, pre-recorded phone messages, emails, videos, murals, and MP3s. As part of its promotion, USB sticks containing clues and music were left in places like bathrooms at Nine Inch Nails concerts, and clues were also integrated into merchandise sold at these gigs. "This was both the fifth studio album for 9-H nails and an alternate reality game."
**Ghosts I–IV (2008)** : This collection of instrumental works, described by Trent Reznor as "soundtracks for daydreams," was first released online in March 2008 and later in various physical formats. Notably, it was released under a Creative Commons Attribution Non-Commercial Share-A-Like License, which permitted anyone to use the album's content for non-profit purposes, encouraging other composers to experiment and expand their skills. "The first called Ghosts 1-4 was released online in March 2008, and later released in a variety of physical forms."

## songs
**Get Down Make Love (1977)** : Originally a Queen song from their 1977 album "News of the World." Nine Inch Nails performed an "insanely heavy version" of this cover at the RPM Club in Toronto on April 17, 1990, which captivated the audience. This cover version was a bonus track on the CD single for Nine Inch Nails' song "Sin" and was produced by Hypo Luxa, the alias of Al Jourgensen of Ministry. "It was an insanely heavy version of the Queen song Get Down Make Love from their 1977 album News of the World."
**Down In It** : A song from Nine Inch Nails' first album. Its music video depicted Trent Reznor being chased through Chicago, pushed off a building, and lying in a pool of blood. Due to budget constraints, aerial shots were filmed by attaching a camera to a balloon, which subsequently detached and drifted 150 miles east to Burr Oak, Michigan. A farmer found the camera and gave it to police, who, misinterpreting the footage as an actual murder (thinking the body was genuine and rotting), launched an FBI investigation that lasted a year. The case was only solved when a university student, who had seen the video (without the death shot) on MTV and an FBI flyer, connected the dots. "The plot shows Trent being chased through the streets of Chicago by a couple of baddies."
**Head Like A Hole** : This song was part of Nine Inch Nails' setlist at the RPM Club in Toronto on April 17, 1990. "for the rest of the set, which consisted of ring finger down in it and head like a hole, the crowd went nuts and we were rewarded for our attention by the band smashing their gear to bits at the end."
**Happiness and Slavery** : This song is associated with the disturbing video art project for the "Broken" EP. "Here's a sample of the music that went along with that film for Broken. This is Happiness and Slavery."
**Capital G** : One of the singles from the "Year Zero" album. "This is one of the singles from Year Zero. It's Capital G."
**Immigrant Song** : A cover of the Led Zeppelin song, recorded by Trent Reznor and Atticus Ross with Karen O of the Yeah Yeah Yeahs. This version was created for the trailer of the film "The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo." "Trent and Atticus brought in Karen O of the Yeah, Yeah, yeah, to record this version of Led Zeppelin's immigrant song for the trailer of The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo."
**34 Ghosts 4 (2008)** : An instrumental track from the "Ghosts I–IV" album, released under a Creative Commons Attribution Non-Commercial Share-A-Like License. A Dutch beatmaker named YoungKio remixed this track and sold it online for $30. Montero Hill (Lil Nas X) purchased this remix, wrote lyrics over it, and released it independently as "Old Town Road" on December 3, 2018. Due to the license violation (commercial use), Trent Reznor and Atticus Ross were given official songwriting credits and shared in the song's chart records, including its 19 weeks at number one on the Billboard Hot 100. "A Dutch beatmaker named Young Kiel took up Trent on his offer. He messed with one of the tracks called 34 Ghosts 4 and put the results up for sale online for $30."

## curiosities
**Childhood and Early Life** : Grew up in Mercer, Pennsylvania. After his parents divorced, he lived with his maternal grandmother. He played the tuba in the school band and gravitated towards the piano. "He grew up in Mercer, Pennsylvania."
**First Concert (Eagles, 1976)** : Attended his first concert, seeing The Eagles in 1976, which inspired his decision to pursue a stage career. "A big moment came when he went to his first concert, the Eagles of all bands in 1976. And that's when he decided he wanted to be on stage."
**Janitorial Job at Right Track Studio** : Worked as a janitor at Right Track, a Cleveland recording studio. He convinced the owner to let him record demos during off-hours, which ultimately led to the birth of Nine Inch Nails. "Things started to get better when he got a job as a janitor at a Cleveland recording studio called Right Track."
**TVT Records Contract Dispute** : After signing with TVT Records, the label's owner, Steve Gottlieb, disliked Trent's debut album "Pretty Hate Machine" for being too heavy and dark. When Steve demanded a more commercially palatable follow-up, Trent refused and sought release from his contract. TVT denied his request, putting him in "limbo for a couple of years" and forbidding him from recording or releasing new music, even allowing "Pretty Hate Machine" to go out of print. This forced Trent to record secretly under fake names. "But when Steve demanded that the follow up record be more commercially palatable, Trent not so respectfully declined and demanded that he be led out of his contract."
**Secret Recording as 1000 Homo DJs** : During his contract dispute with TVT Records, Trent Reznor secretly recorded vocals on a cover of Black Sabbath's "Supernaut" for the Ministry spin-off band 1000 Homo DJs. There is a dispute whether his vocals actually appear on most versions; only versions specifically labeled "Trent Rezner Vocal Version" feature his voice, otherwise it's Al Jourgensen's voice with distortion. "The result was that Trent had to record secretly and under fake names. One such recording was a ministry spin-off band called 1000 homo DJs."
**Renting Manson Murder House (10,050 Cielo Drive)** : In July 1992, Trent rented a secluded house at 10,050 Cielo Drive in Benedict Canyon, Los Angeles, to record "The Downward Spiral." He only learned after signing that this was the site of the Manson family murders in 1969, where Sharon Tate and others were brutally killed. Despite being "freaked out" and trying to find an alternative, he moved his studio gear into the house, specifically the room where Tate and Jay Sebring died. He named his temporary studio "Le Pig." "It was apparently only after the rental agreement was signed, the Trent learned of the house's history. 10,050 C.L.O. Drive is where the first of the Manson family murders took place."
**Struggles with Addiction and Recovery (1994-2001)** : Following "The Downward Spiral," Trent fell into a deep, five-year depression, self-medicating with alcohol, coke, and heroin, leading to suicidal thoughts. His grandmother's death exacerbated his struggles. He recorded "The Fragile" while "on a pretty slippery slope," believing himself cured upon its release, but relapsed for a "year and a half testing to see how low I could go." He described this period as "a miserable time of being sick and sweating and vomiting and hiding and just being awful," and considered it a "miracle" he didn't die. In 2001, after the last tour, he committed to intensive rehab, learning humility and self-care, and confronting his creative fears about writing sober. "My soul had been crushed by being an alcoholic and an addict. And there was no denying at that point."
**Fear of Sober Creativity** : During his recovery from addiction, Trent was "very much afraid that I didn't know if I could write anymore. I didn't know if I could write sober. I didn't know if I've destroyed my brain." He took time off in 2001 to "get comfortable in my own skin" and confront these fears. "I didn't know if I could write sober."
**Academy Awards and Golden Globes** : Trent Reznor has won two Academy Awards, three Golden Globes, and an Emmy for scoring films and TV series. "Trent has also won two Oscars for scoring movies."
**Co-holder of Billboard Hot 100 Record** : As co-composer of "34 Ghosts 4," the source material for Lil Nas X's "Old Town Road," Trent Reznor (with Atticus Ross) shares the record for the pop song with the most weeks (19) at number one on the Billboard Hot 100. "So yes, Trent Rezner is the co-holder of the record for the pop song, the pop song, with the most weeks at number one on Billboard."

# label - TVT Records

## curiosities
**Founder and Origin** : Founded by Steve Gottlieb, TVT originally stood for "TVTunes" and initially released compilations of old TV theme songs from shows like "The Jetsons," "The Monsters," and "The Beverly Hillbillies." These compilations sold well enough for Gottlieb to sign "real bands." "Demos were sent out to a bunch of labels with TVT emerging the winner. And this was pretty unusual because TVT stood for TVTunes. This was a label founded by Steve Gottlieb."
**Expectations vs. Reality with Nine Inch Nails** : Steve Gottlieb signed Trent Reznor, expecting "the next Depeche Mode," a dancing technopop band. However, he "kind of hated" Nine Inch Nails' debut album "Pretty Hate Machine," calling it an "abortion" due to its heavy, dark, loud, and angry music. Despite his dislike, the album was issued and sold half a million copies. "Steve heard Trent's demos and thought he had the next depeche moan on his hands, a dancing technopop band capable of turning out hit singles. But when Trent turned in his record he called it pretty hate machine. He kind of hated it. Actually he called it an abortion quote unquote."
**Contract Dispute with Trent Reznor** : After the success of "Pretty Hate Machine," TVT demanded a more commercially palatable follow-up. Trent Reznor refused and sought to be released from his contract, but TVT declined, putting him "in limbo for a couple of years." During this time, TVT allowed "Pretty Hate Machine" to go out of print and forbade Trent from recording or releasing new music, threatening to confiscate any new material for its own use. "But when Steve demanded that the follow up record be more commercially palatable, Trent not so respectfully declined and demanded that he be led out of his contract. TVT said, nope. And put Trent in limbo for a couple of years."

# instrument - Camera

## curiosities
**"Down In It" Video Production Mishap** : For aerial shots in the "Down In It" music video, a camera was attached to a balloon due to budget constraints. A gust of wind tore the balloon and camera from its moorings, causing it to drift 150 miles east of Chicago to Burr Oak, Michigan. It was found by a farmer and mistakenly given to the police, who, upon reviewing the footage of a staged murder, believed it was evidence of a real gang or cult killing, leading to an FBI investigation that lasted a year. "The video director required some aerial shots but since there was no money in the budget for a crane and a helicopter and all that sort of stuff, a camera was attached to a balloon and sent aloft."

# curiosity

**Right Track (Cleveland Recording Studio)** : This Cleveland recording studio is where Nine Inch Nails was conceived. Trent Reznor, working as a janitor there, convinced the owner to let him record demos during unused studio hours, which led to the band's formation. "Things started to get better when he got a job as a janitor at a Cleveland recording studio called Right Track. Trent convinced the owner to let him record demos when the studio wasn't being used. And this is how and where Nine Inch Nails was born."
**10,050 Cielo Drive (Manson Murder Site)** : This address in Benedict Canyon, Los Angeles, was the site of the first Manson family murders on August 8, 1969. Tex Watson, Susan Atkins, Linda Kasabian, and Patricia Krenwinkel brutally murdered Abigail Folger, Woodjick Freierkowski, Jay Sebring, Stephen Parent, and the heavily pregnant actress Sharon Tate, who was renting the property with Roman Polanski. The word "pig" was written in Tate's blood on the living room wall. Trent Reznor later rented this house in July 1992 to record "The Downward Spiral," unknowingly at first, and established his studio in the room where Tate and Sebring were found. "10,050 C.L.O. Drive is where the first of the Manson family murders took place."
**Creative Commons Attribution Non-Commercial Share-A-Like License** : This license was used by Trent Reznor for Nine Inch Nails' instrumental album "Ghosts I–IV," released in 2008.
