# curiosity

**The Chromatic Scale and Musical Building Blocks** : The chromatic scale is the standard scale of pitches or notes used for centuries in Western music, consisting of 12 notes within an octave that then repeat. These notes are mathematically related through a set series of ratios, or semitone intervals, and form the basic DNA for all music, from Mozart's operas to punk songs. "For centuries, we've used a standard scale of pitches or notes, called the chromatic scale. 12 notes in an octave, then repeat."
**Possible Combinations of 12 Notes (Once Each)** : According to Frank Barons, who wrote in the Arts Times in 2004, there are 479,160,000 possible combinations if you play the 12 notes of the chromatic scale just once each. This figure highlights the vast but finite number of basic musical sequences available. "A quick bit of factoring... reveals that there are 479 million 1,600 possible combinations of those 12 notes. If you just play them once each, 479 million combinations."
**Possible Combinations of Notes (with Durations)** : When accounting for the various ways a single note can be played (e.g., whole, half, quarter, eighth, sixteenth, thirty-second notes), the number of possible note combinations expands exponentially to just over one quintillion (a one followed by 18 zeros). This illustrates the immense complexity and theoretical diversity in musical composition. "Something just north of one quintillion possible combinations. That's a one followed by 18 zeros, one quintillion."
**Time to Play All Unique Combinations** : If one were to play a new combination of notes every second, it would take an astounding 33 billion, 63 million, 236,360 years to exhaust all one quintillion non-repeating sequences using the 12 notes of the chromatic scale. This is approximately two and a half times the age of the universe, demonstrating the immense, though finite, potential of musical expression. "if you played a new combination of notes every second, it would take you 33 billion, 63 million, 236,360 years, which is about two and a half times the age of the universe."
**Aesthetic and Artistic Constraints on Note Combinations** : Despite the astronomical number of theoretical note combinations, music must ultimately sound pleasing to the ear and soul. This artistic and aesthetic requirement significantly narrows down the number of *workable* note combinations, increasing the likelihood of unintentional repetition in compositions. "Music has to sound pleasing to the ear and to the soul, too. ...there are only so many combinations of notes that work from an artistic and aesthetic point of view."
**Definition of Plagiarism** : Plagiarism in music, and in general, is defined as passing off someone else's original work as one's own without acknowledging the true creator. It is considered cheating, unethical, dishonest, and a form of theft, capable of ruining reputations if proven. "Plagiarism is when you pass off someone else's work as your own without ever acknowledging the original creator. And plagiarism is a nasty thing. It's cheating. It's unethical. It's dishonest and it's theft, plain and simple."
