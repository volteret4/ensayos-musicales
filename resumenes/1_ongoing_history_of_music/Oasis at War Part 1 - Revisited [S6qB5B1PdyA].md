## curiosities
**Oasis Reunion (Next Summer)** : After years of rumors, Liam and Noel Gallagher have agreed to reunite for a series of shows next summer across the UK, Ireland, and other locations. This reunion is timed to coincide with the anniversaries of their first two albums, *Definitely Maybe* (1994) and *What's the Story Morning Glory* (1995). "The Brothers Gallagher have agreed to reunite, and possibly buried the hatchet. This is for a series of shows next summer in the UK, Ireland, and beyond."
**Rain's Transformation into Oasis (1991)** : In 1991, after a gig by Liam Gallagher's band Rain, Liam and his bandmates asked Noel Gallagher to manage them. Noel refused to manage but offered to join as lead guitarist, stating his original songs were far superior to Rain's material, leading to the band accepting him and transforming into Oasis. "The band accepted, and that's when Rain started the Metamorphosis into Oasis."
**Noel Gallagher's Dictatorial Nature** : Noel's dictatorial approach to the band did not sit well with Liam or other members. An instance of this was when Noel used a record company advance to purchase new guitars for himself, despite the band's broader need for other equipment. "For example, there was a time when he took record company of money and advance and bought himself new guitars when the group needed all kinds of other gear."
**First Documented Public Fight (March 29, 1994)** : The initial recorded public altercation between Noel and Liam occurred at the Joiners Arms club in Southampton on March 29, 1994, where Noel punched Liam in the face and then chased him off stage during a show. "The first documented evidence of a public war between Noel and Liam was at a show at a club called Joiners Arms in Southampton on March 29, 1994."
**NME Interview Incident (April 1994)** : About a month after their Southampton fight, Noel and Liam were featured in their first major British music media interview by the NME, conducted by John Harris at a Glasgow hotel in April 1994. The interview quickly descended into a profanity-laced argument between the brothers within the first three minutes, highlighting their intense relationship early in their career. "The interview took place at a hotel in Glasgow, the writer, a guy with the name of John Harris, reported it and it went something like this."
**NME Interview Single (1994)** : The chaotic NME interview, filled with the Gallagher brothers' arguments, was later released as a single, demonstrating the public's fascination with their relationship by reaching number 52 on the British charts. "That interview was later released as a single, and it reached number 52 on the British charts."
**First North American Tour (Fall 1994)** : Oasis embarked on their inaugural North American tour in the fall of 1994, a period marked by escalating conflict and drug use within the band. "In the fall of 1994, Oasis headed out on their first North American tour."
**Whisky a Go Go Performance and Aftermath (September 29, 1994)** : During a performance at the Whisky a Go Go on Sunset Strip on September 29, 1994, Noel's insistence on singing harmonies alongside Liam's lead vocals agitated Liam. They repeatedly confronted each other on stage, culminating in Liam hitting Noel with a tambourine, leaving the stage, and refusing an encore. A backstage fight involving a thrown chair ensued, with Liam seen "skulking down Sunset Boulevard" and Noel departing with $8,000 of band money for Las Vegas and San Francisco, leading to the cancellation of more than half a dozen shows. "On September 29th, 1994, they played the whiskey a go-go on the sunset strip. Noel insisted on singing harmonies to lead lead vocals, which really annoyed Liam. They squared off on stage a bunch of times, and at the end of the set, Liam whacked Noel in the back of the head with a tambourine."
**Near Breakup in 1994** : Following the chaotic Whisky a Go Go incident and Noel's temporary departure, the band was on the verge of breaking up, necessitating Noel's eventual return in Austin, Texas. "The band came this close to breaking up then."
**Rockfield Studios Incident (May 1995)** : In May 1995, during the recording of their second album at Rockfield Studios in Wales, Liam returned from a pub extremely drunk with a large group of people from South Wales, who then invaded the studio causing damage. Noel locked the studio doors, embarrassing Liam, who subsequently battered Noel's bedroom door, breaking his foot. Noel retaliated by hitting Liam with a cricket bat, reportedly breaking Liam's arm, and then left the studio for a week to cool off. "Noel then grabbed a cricket bat, opened the door, and started whacking Liam with it."
**MTV Unplugged Incident (August 23, 1996)** : Oasis was scheduled to perform an MTV Unplugged set at the Royal Festival Hall in London on August 23, 1996. At the last minute, Liam pulled out, claiming a sore throat, but Noel suspected he was hungover. Noel took over lead vocals for the entire set, receiving good reviews, while Liam watched from the balcony, drinking beer and loudly heckling the band and spinning beer towards the stage. "At the very last second though, Liam says, no, can't do it, got a sore throat."
**Heathrow Airport Incident (August 25, 1996)** : Just two days after the MTV Unplugged event, on August 25, 1996, while Oasis was about to depart for another North American tour from Heathrow Airport, Liam, after boarding the plane while on the phone with his wife Patsy Kensit, suddenly announced he couldn't go and left the plane, stating he needed to "go buy a house." This resulted in the cancellation of their first major North American headlining gig at the 16,000-seat Rosemont Horizon in Chicago. "Suddenly, he says, can't go, gotta go buy a house."
**Noel's Claim of "Killing Oasis" in America** : Noel Gallagher asserted that Liam's "diva move" at Heathrow Airport in 1996, which led to the cancellation of a major US show, effectively "killed Oasis, stone dead in America." "Noel claims that this diva move went a long way to killing Oasis, stone dead in America."
**MTV Music Video Awards Performance (September 4, 1996)** : During Oasis's performance at the MTV Music Video Awards in New York on September 4, 1996, Liam deliberately sang off-key, made annoyed gestures towards Noel, spat beer all over the stage, and then stormed off. These actions further harmed the band's prospects in the United States. "Liam deliberately sang off key. As Noel played, Liam gestured towards him in some kind of annoyed way. And then he spat beer all over the stage and stormed off."
**Charlotte, North Carolina Fight (1996)** : The North American tour of 1996, already fraught with incidents, concluded with another fight in Charlotte, North Carolina, which led to Noel Gallagher flying home alone again to take a few weeks to calm down. "The tour continued for a little while longer, but then there was another fight in Charlotte North Carolina. And Noel ended up flying home alone."
**Be Here Now Recording Sessions (1997)** : The recording sessions for the third album, *Be Here Now*, in 1997 were a period of constant, low-level fighting between Noel and Liam. The band was burned out and heavily under the influence of cocaine, which Noel later believed affected the album's sound and led to decisions like layering excessive guitars and turning up the high end. "Meanwhile, Noel and Liam were constantly fighting throughout the whole thing."

## curiosities
**Early Life (Born 1967)** : Noel Gallagher was born in 1967, the middle child, two years after his older brother Paul and five years before Liam. He shared a bedroom with Liam in childhood. "Paul is the middle child. He was born in 1967, two years after older brother Paul, and about five years before Liam came along."
**Childhood Trauma and Personality** : Noel developed stuttering problems as a child due to his alcoholic and abusive father, Tommy. He was a "bad kid" involved in petty thefts and was kicked out of school at 15. As he grew, he became quieter, likeable, and sometimes moody, developing a "love-hate thing" with Liam. "Paul and Noel were so freaked out by this that they developed stuttering problems when they were young."
**Early Prank on Liam** : For years, Noel would prank his younger brother Liam by moving furniture around the house while Liam slept, then claiming ghosts were responsible, reportedly keeping Liam terrified during this period. "One of the things Noel liked to do was to freak out Liam by moving furniture around the house as he slept. And then he would tell Liam that ghosts were doing it."
**Early Career as Roadie** : By 1988, Noel was living independently and eventually secured a job as a roadie for the band Inspiral Carpets, before joining Liam's band, Rain. "Noel eventually got a job as a roadie with the Inspiral Carpets."
**Songwriting Prowess** : Before joining Rain, Noel had been writing songs for years and considered his own compositions "far better than Rain's rubbish," a sentiment that influenced his decision to join the band as lead guitarist. "Besides, he had been writing songs for years, and everything he'd written was far better than Rain's rubbish."
**Financial Decision for Guitars** : Demonstrating his dictatorial nature within Oasis, Noel once used a record company advance to buy himself new guitars, prioritizing his own gear over the band's collective need for other equipment. "For example, there was a time when he took record company of money and advance and bought himself new guitars when the group needed all kinds of other gear."
**Post-Whisky a Go Go Departure (September 1994)** : After a violent on-stage and backstage fight with Liam at the Whisky a Go Go on September 29, 1994, Noel left the band, heading to the airport with $8,000 of the band's money and flying to Las Vegas and San Francisco to cool off, leading to several canceled shows. "And Noel, while he headed to the airport, with $8,000 of the band's money. He flew to Vegas and the disanference isco to hang out with some friends to cool off."
**Rockfield Studio Retaliation (May 1995)** : In response to Liam's drunken invasion of Rockfield Studios and subsequent attempt to batter his bedroom door, Noel grabbed a cricket bat, opened the door, and began hitting Liam with it. Accounts suggest he may have broken Liam's arm, after which Noel left the studio for a week to calm down. "Noel then grabbed a cricket bat, opened the door, and started whacking Liam with it."
**Taking Lead Vocals for MTV Unplugged (August 23, 1996)** : When Liam pulled out of Oasis's MTV Unplugged performance at the Royal Festival Hall, citing a sore throat (which Noel suspected was a hangover), Noel declared he would sing the songs himself, as acoustic sets were already part of the band's repertoire. He received "pretty good reviews" for his performance. "If you won't sing my songs, I will sing them."
**Be Here Now Production (1997)** : During the recording of *Be Here Now* in 1997, Noel, under the influence of cocaine, insisted on making the record sound "bigger and louder" by layering numerous guitars and turning up the high end, a perception he later attributed to his drug use. "Noel kept wanting to make the record sound bigger and louder. So he insisted on layer after layer after layer of guitars. And he wanted the high end turned up because he didn't think there was enough."
