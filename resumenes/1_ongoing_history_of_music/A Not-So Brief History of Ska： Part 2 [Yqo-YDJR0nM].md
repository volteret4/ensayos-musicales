# artist - The Toasters

## members
Rob Hingley

## curiosities
**The Toasters – Founded by British Expat Rob Hingley in New York; Most Important American Ska Band of the 1980s; Founded Moon Ska Records** : The Toasters were the most important American ska band of the 1980s. Founder Rob Hingley was a Brit who moved to New York only to find almost no one knew about the British ska scene, so he formed a band based on Two-Tone traditions. Beyond touring and recording, Hingley also established Moon Ska Records, which eventually became the largest and most important independent ska label in America, distributing dozens of bands across the continent. Moon Ska Records had a business until 2000, though some European operations survived. "Out of all the Skah bands of the early and middle 80s, no American group did more to popularize this sound in the US than the Toasters."


# artist - Mighty Mighty Bosstones

## curiosities
**Mighty Mighty Bosstones – Formed 1983 Boston; Invented Skacore by Mixing Two-Tone with Hardcore; Major Label by 1993** : The Mighty Mighty Bosstones came together in 1983 when a group of guys became obsessed with the Two-Tone sound despite some members playing in hardcore bands influenced by Motorhead and AC/DC. This combination of Two-Tone ska with American hardcore became known as skacore — annoying to ska purists and initially baffling to hardcore kids, but eventually it clicked. By 1993 they had a major label record deal and a signature look involving plaid suits. "The Boss Tones took two-tone style Skah and made it with American hardcore to create something that was eventually called Skahcore."


# artist - Operation Ivy

## curiosities
**Operation Ivy (1987–1989) – Skacore Pioneers; Dissolved into Rancid** : Operation Ivy formed in 1987 and, like the Bosstones, embraced the blend of ska and hardcore known as skacore. They held it together for a couple of years and a couple of releases before dissolving and coalescing into a new band — Rancid, which became the first of the new wave of skacore bands to break out big. "Operation Ivy, they were formed in 1987 and like the muddy, muddy Boston's they were into that blend of Skah and Hardcore known as Skahcore."


# artist - Sublime

## members
Brad Nowell

## curiosities
**Sublime (Long Beach, 1988) – Co-Headlined First Vans Warped Tour 1995; Singer Brad Nowell Died of Heroin Overdose Two Months Before Major Label Album Came Out** : Sublime came together in 1988 and had their own label (Skunk Records) by 1992. They became wildly popular by playing anywhere and everywhere, co-headlining the first Vans Warped Tour in 1995 and the first Sno-Core tour. After landing a major label deal, their first major label CD sold millions. Unfortunately, singer Brad Nowell died of a heroin overdose just two months before it came out. "Shortly after they also snagged a major label record deal and that first big label CD sold in the millions."

## curiosities
**No Doubt (Anaheim, 1986) – Started as Applecore; Original Singer John Spence Committed Suicide Age 18; Tragic Kingdom (1995) 17 Million Copies; Eric Stefani Left for The Simpsons** : No Doubt was formed in 1986 as a band called Applecore by Eric Stefani and his sister Gwen, later joined by John Spence (who said "no doubt" when he agreed with you — that became the band's name). Spence was the main singer with Gwen on backup. Just before a big showcase for major labels, Spence committed suicide at age 18. After a brief breakup, the band reformed with a new singer named Alan Mead, who didn't last, and then Gwen took over vocals. A self-titled 1992 album flopped (maybe 30,000 copies). A second album called The Beacon Street Collection sold only a few more. Keyboardist Eric Stefani then left to become an animator for The Simpsons, preferring animation to music. Interscope paired the remaining band with producer Matthew Wilder; the resulting Tragic Kingdom came out October 10, 1995 and eventually sold north of 17 million copies worldwide, doing more for ska punk than any other CD. "At last count worldwide sales were somewhere north of 17 million copies."


# artist - Planet Smashers

## members
Matt Collier

## labels
Stomp Records

## curiosities
**Planet Smashers (Montreal, 1994) – Founded Stomp Records; Survived the "Ska Death" of 1999–2002** : The Planet Smashers formed in 1994 and created their own label, Stomp Records, which has promoted dozens of ska, ska-punk, and ska-friendly bands including Buckle 9, Bedouin Soundclash, The Flatliners, The New York Ska-Jazz Ensemble, St. Alvia, and even The Toasters. The ska scene largely crashed in the US between 1999 and 2002, described by Matt Collier as "brutal" — the band stopped touring in the US for three years. The recovery beginning in 2003 brought ska back to its natural home as a subculture. "You do not want to be Scott from 1999 to 2002. You just didn't do it."


# genre - Ska

## curiosities
**Third Wave Ska – California Radio Show "The Ska Parade" and CD Compilations Created Critical Mass** : Third wave ska gathered strength on both the East and West Coasts of the US in the late 1980s and early 1990s. Key to its spread in California was a radio show called The Ska Parade, which played classics alongside newer material by bands like Rancid, Skankin' Pickle, Real Big Fish, Let's Go Bowling, Buckle 9, and Goldfinger. CD compilations from the show further cemented ska into the alternative rock psyche of California, especially in Orange County and south of Los Angeles. "The ska became deeply entrenched into the new music psyche of California, especially in Orange County and to the area south of Los Angeles."

**Third Wave's California Roots – Bay Area Uptones (1981) Inspired Hardcore + Ska Fusion; Secret Sauce for Third Wave** : The third wave began in the Bay Area with The Uptones (1981), a group of 15 and 16-year-olds fascinated by the Two-Tone sound. They played tons of Bay Area shows for seven years, inspiring other groups. The next generation added hardcore punk DNA to the mix — this was the secret sauce that set the stage for the third wave. Fishbone (formed 1979) also kept ska alive through the mid-1980s in California, mixing punk, funk, soul, metal, and ska with social commentary about racism, oppression, and nuclear war. "Hardcore punk rock. And this was the secret sauce that set things up for what would be the third wave of Scott."

**Ska Is Eternal – Declared Dead Twice, Came Back Stronger Each Time; Subculture at Its Best** : Ska has been declared over at least twice — the first wave ended in the late 1960s as reggae took hold; the second wave burned out around 1985; and the third wave crashed in the US around 1999–2002. Each time it has returned stronger. Matt Collier of the Planet Smashers: "Ska music is a subculture form of music. It has a subculture style and fashion. It's not meant to be in the mainstream. When it does, generally, it's because someone's out to make some money and market something." "There aren't, however, certain styles that seem immortal... Ska is now into its third wave."

**Ska's US Midwest – Gangster Fun (Detroit, 1986); Mustard Plug; Skapone** : While the Toasters (New York) and Bosstones (Boston) established ska on the coasts, the Midwest was served by Detroit's Gangster Fun (formed 1986), who spread the Two-Tone sound in the same DIY spirit — their 1989 debut was released only in the UK. Other Midwest ska bands included Mustard Plug and Skapone. "Since no one knew about Skah, they decided to spread the word themselves."

**Less Than Jake (Gainesville FL) – Own Label Sleep It Off Records; Won't Quit; "All My Best Friends Are Metalheads"** : Less Than Jake from Gainesville, Florida morphed into a ska punk band and operate their own label, Sleep It Off Records. They are cited as an example of ska bands that simply will not quit no matter what the marketplace does. "Here's one more Scott band that just won't quit."
