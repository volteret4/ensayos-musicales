## albums
**Bleach (1989) – Recorded for $606.17, Paid for by Jason Everman** : Recorded in 1988 for a total cost of $606.17. Kurt Cobain didn't have the money, so his friend Jason Everman, a commercial fisherman from Alaska, paid the bill. As a thank-you, Everman was credited in the liner notes as having played guitar, even though he played no notes on the recording. "Kurt made it look like Jason played on the record, but let's be clear, he did not."

**In Utero (1993) – 16 Songs Recorded in 14 Days at Pachyderm Studios** : In mid-February 1993, Nirvana set up at Pachyderm Studios in Canon Falls, Minnesota, and recorded 16 songs in 14 days. Twelve ended up on the album; four found homes elsewhere, including Dave Grohl's "Marigold." "A dozen of them ended up on the in-utero album while the other four found homes elsewhere."

## songs
**Marigold (1993) – Only Nirvana Song Written and Sung by Dave Grohl** : Written by Grohl and originally recorded as a solo piece in 1990 — a full year before he joined the band. At Pachyderm Studios in 1993, Grohl recorded it apparently on his own after the others had gone home, playing drums, singing, and likely guitar. It appears only on the B-side of the "Heart-Shaped Box" CD single. A hint of future Foo Fighters can be detected in the recording. "The only Nirvana song written and sung by Dave Grohl."

**Love Buzz (1988) – Cover of a Dutch Song, First Sub Pop Single** : Nirvana's first Sub Pop single was a cover of a song originally recorded by the Dutch band Shocking Blue. The demo version was recorded when the band was still known as "Ed Ted Fred." "Which in case you didn't know, is a cover of a song by a Dutch band called Shocking Blue."

**Blandest (1988) – Lost Song Stolen from Studio and Recovered from Bootlegs** : Recorded on June 30, 1988, during a rushed two-day session. The song was underrehearsed and the recording failed; Kurt ordered it erased. Fifteen years later, when compiling the *With the Lights Out* box set, the band discovered the song had apparently been stolen from Reciprocal Studios on the day of the session — copies survived on bootleg CDs and records. The official release on *With the Lights Out* was sourced directly from the bootleg recordings. "The record company and Nirvana just stole the track back from people who had bootlegged this particular song."

## curiosities
**Kurt Cobain's Vaguely Canadian Ancestry** : Kurt was born on February 20, 1967, at Aberdeen Community Hospital in Aberdeen, Washington. His paternal ancestry traces to a family from Skytown, County Tyrone, Ireland (spelled "Cobane"), who emigrated around 1875 to Cornwall, Ontario, where patriarch Samuel Cobain worked as a shoemaker. Census records confirm a Samuel Cobain, wife, and seven children in Cornwall. They subsequently moved to Gray's Harbor County, Washington, where Aberdeen is located. Kurt's great-uncle Delbert, on his mother's side, was a respected Irish tenor who appears in the 1930 film *King of Jazz* featuring Paul Whiteman and the young Bing Crosby. "Hardly qualifies Kurt as Cancon, but it is still kind of cool."

**Krist Novoselic – Name History from Croatian Birth Spelling to English Variations** : Born May 16, 1965 to Croatian immigrant parents, Krist's birth name was spelled "Krste" in the Old World Croatian way (his father's name being Kristo). At school the name was Anglicized to "Chris" (C-H-R-I-S). At age 15 in 1980, he attended a Croatian high school in Zadar on the Adriatic coast, where classes included assembling and disassembling a machine gun as part of civil defense training. Returning to Aberdeen, he favored "Krist" (K-R-I-S-T) again. Early Nirvana materials often credited him as "Chris Novoselic" (likely a record company decision), but from *Nevermind* onward it has been "Krist." Anyone calling him "Chris" was corrected. "Novoselic was a fairly unusual name for Croatian and Serbian — it meant 'new villager.'"

**Band Name History – From Fecal Matter to Ed Ted Fred to Nirvana (March 19, 1988)** : Kurt's first band was called Fecal Matter (fall 1985). Subsequent incarnations included Brown Towel (sometimes misbilled as Brown Cow). The first Kurt-and-Krist performance was at a house party on March 7, 1987, as "Skid Row" (unrelated to the metal band), with drummer Aaron Burkhard. Names considered at the time included Batguano, Designer Drugs, Gut Bomb, Egg Flog, Fish Food, Puking Worms, Bliss, Whiskey Biscuit, Incompetent Fools, Poo Poo Box, Pen Cap Chew, Throat Oyster, Window Frame, Yin-Yang Valvstem, and Ed Ted Fred. They entered the studio on January 2, 1988, still as "Ed Ted Fred." The name Nirvana was first used at a show in Tacoma on March 19, 1988 — Kurt was studying Buddhism and liked that Nirvana meant a state free from pain, worry, and the troubles of the world, reflecting his ongoing stomach problems and the psychological damage of his parents' divorce. "He was dabbling in Buddhism at the time and learned that Nirvana meant a state or place that was free from pain, worry, and the troubles of the world."

**Radio Shack In-Store Video (January 24, 1988) – Miming to Demo Tape in Unplugged Guitars** : Friend and Radio Shack manager Eric Harder invited the band (still called Ed Ted Fred) into his Aberdeen store on January 24, 1988, and filmed them miming to their demo tape. The guitars are clearly not plugged in; only the drums were live. Kurt was upset at how unprofessional it looked but watched the footage repeatedly to study how to improve the band's presentation. "The video is really cheesy, really low-budget stuff, and Kurt was actually upset that it wasn't more professional-looking."

**Jason Everman – 30-Day Member of Nirvana (February–July 1989)** : When Kurt invited him to join as second guitarist for Nirvana's first major tour in February 1989, Everman had no fishing season to attend to and agreed. He made his debut on February 25, 1989, at the University of Washington. He was present when British journalists flew in to cover the Seattle scene, and when *Bleach* (which he'd financed) was released on June 15. His final show was at the Pyramid Club in New York City on July 18, 1989; the rest of the tour was canceled and the band drove 50 hours back to Seattle without speaking. He was let go or quit — either way it was fine with him. He subsequently joined Soundgarden and after that went on to become a Special Forces soldier. "The band deadheaded back home to Seattle. They were so pissed with Jason that they nearly left him in New York."

**Kurt Throwing Bottles at Drummer Chad Channing – Live at Lee's Palace, Toronto (April 16, 1990)** : By spring 1990, Kurt was profoundly unhappy with Chad Channing's drumming. At Lee's Palace in Toronto on April 16, 1990 — the band's first Toronto show — Kurt began hurling glass bottles at the wall behind Chad; the audience followed suit. A recording of the one-hour set captures the glass shattering behind the kit. "Kurt started throwing glass bottles at the wall behind Chad and the audience followed."

**Dave Grohl's Father Covered the Kent State Massacre (May 1970)** : In 1970, the Grohl family lived in Niles, Ohio (near Columbus). Dave's father James Grohl was a reporter for the Scripps News Agency and was assigned to cover anti-Vietnam War protests at Kent State University on May 1, 1970 — and was present when the National Guard opened fire, killing four students. James Grohl won several awards for his on-the-spot reporting, earning a major promotion and reassignment to Washington D.C., where Dave grew up and discovered music. "Had it not been for the National Guard killing four unarmed students at Kent State, who knows if we'd ever have heard of Dave Grohl?"

**Dave Grohl Joined Nirvana Because His Band's Bassist Broke Up with His Girlfriend (1990)** : Dave dropped out of high school to tour with DC punk band Scream for about three years. In the summer of 1990, bassist Skeater Thompson flaked out mid-tour in Los Angeles over the loss of his girlfriend, canceling multiple shows. Dave was left penniless (temporarily crashing with three female mud wrestlers) and doing cash tiling work for food money. He called Melvins leader King Buzzo (Kurt Cobain's idol), whom he'd met in Europe, who told him Kurt had a band needing a drummer. Dave flew to Seattle on September 21, 1990; four days later he auditioned and passed. Dave later wrote a song about Skeater Thompson called "Just Another Story About Skeater Thompson," available only on a cassette-only release made under the name "Late" in late 1990, titled *Pocket Watch*. "Dave has never forgotten Skeater Thompson and how thankful he is that Skeater had such bad girlfriend trouble."

**Suicide Runs in Kurt Cobain's Family Across Multiple Generations** : Kurt's great-great-grandfather John Cobain was a county sheriff who accidentally shot himself in 1938. His great-grandfather James Irving stabbed himself in the stomach in front of his family in 1935, was institutionalized, and died two months later after ripping out his own stitches. His great-uncle Burl Cobain shot himself in the stomach with a .38 in 1979; when that failed he shot himself in the head. At age 14, Kurt told friends he planned to kill himself one day after becoming a rock star. At 15 he and his stepbrother James made a short film called *Kurt Commits Bloody Suicide*. At 13 he wrote in his journals of planning to actually kill himself. At 16, drunk and stoned, he lay on train tracks with a large piece of cement on his chest waiting to be hit; the train went past on a different track. The famous March 1994 Rome hotel overdose was the last major attempt before his death on April 5, 1994 (aged 27) in his Seattle home. "He said he had suicide genes."

## songs
**Asking for It – Unofficial Version with Kurt Cobain on Vocals (1993, Rio de Janeiro)** : An unofficial version of this song exists featuring what is widely identified as Kurt Cobain on background vocals. It is believed to have been recorded at Ariola Studios in Rio de Janeiro between January 19–22, 1993, when Nirvana was in Rio for four days and Courtney was traveling with the band. This version did not appear on *Live Through This*; the only official outtake from those sessions known to exist is a demo of "Miss World" (on the rarities CD *My Body the Hand Grenade*). "It's really not that big a leap to assume that the version of asking for it that we just heard was also recorded during the Rio sessions."

## curiosities
**Dave Grohl Solo Cassette "Pocket Watch" (1990) – Under the Name "Late"** : Before joining Nirvana and years before the Foo Fighters, Dave Grohl released a cassette-only recording at the very end of 1990 under the name "Late," recording songs with his friend Barnett Jones. The tape is called *Pocket Watch* and includes "Just Another Story About Skeater Thompson," written about the bassist whose romantic troubles indirectly led Grohl to Nirvana. "The only place it can be found is on a cassette only release that Dave released under the name Late."
