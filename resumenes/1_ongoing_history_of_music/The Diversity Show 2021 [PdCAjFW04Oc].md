"Growing up in a multi-racial city, he hated the racism that he saw around him and was inspired by the rock against racism movement that began in 1978."
**Integrated Band Design** : From its inception, The Specials were designed to be an integrated band, consistently featuring a mix of black and white musicians, including Caribbean immigrants, over the years. "From the very beginning, the specials were designed to be an integrated band."
**Walt Japsco Logo** : The band's logo featured a stylized Jamaican rude boy character named Walt Japsco, depicted with a black suit, white shirt, black tie, pork pie hat, white socks, and black shoes, embodying their cultural influences. "Even the band's logo featured a stylized Jamaican rude boy called Walt Japsco. He had his black suit, the white shirt, the black tie, the pork pie hat, the white socks and the black shoes."
**Peter Tosh as Walt Japsco Inspiration** : The character Walt Japsco, central to The Specials' logo, was directly based on a photograph of the Jamaican musician Peter Tosh, a former member of Bob Marley's Wailers. "Walt was actually based on a photograph of Jamaican musician Peter Tosh, who was a former member of Bob Arley's Whalers."
**Advocates for Racial Harmony** : In their performances, The Specials consistently promoted messages of racial and social harmony. "Whenever the specials played, they preached racial social harmony."
**Ska's Introduction to Post-Punk** : The Specials were pivotal in introducing Ska music into the post-punk world, blending these genres effectively. "Plus, they were instrumental when it came to introducing Scott into the post-punk world."
