# artist - U2

## members
Bono

## curiosities
**Fan Hearing Loss Lawsuit (1994) – Judge Awarded $34,000 Because No Warning Signs Were Posted** : On July 14, 1994, a French fan named Mohammed Falfana attended a U2 Zoo TV show at Stade Vélodrome in Marseille, one of 55,000 people present. Four years later, in September 1997, he filed suit claiming he had lost 75% of his hearing in his left ear and 49% in his right after security guards dragged him into a restricted area next to a speaker tower. U2's position was that he had entered the restricted area on his own initiative and so bore personal responsibility. However, the promoter had failed to post any warning signs advising that standing close to a 100,000-watt speaker system could damage hearing. The judge ruled on this technicality in Falfana's favor and awarded the equivalent of $34,000. "Nice technicality."


# artist - The House Martins

Hugh Whitaker

## curiosities
**Hugh Whitaker Gasoline, Firebombing, and Axe Attack on Former Business Partner** : After the House Martins broke up, drummer Hugh Whitaker invested his life savings with a partner named James Hughitt in a car dealership. By 1993, Hughitt's mismanagement had left Whitaker completely broke. Whitaker responded by pouring gasoline into Hughitt's mailbox at least twice; when this produced no results, he firebombed his house; when that also failed, he attacked him with an axe, hacking him up significantly. Whitaker served a six-year sentence for assault. He now plays in local bands in Leeds and stays away from the car business. Meanwhile, another House Martins alumnus, guitarist Quentin Cook, renamed himself Fatboy Slim and sold a billion records. "He went a little crazy."

**Naked Urination on Vancouver Audience – October 31, 1993, Pacific Coliseum** : On Halloween 1993, Blind Melon were supporting Lenny Kravitz at a sold-out Pacific Coliseum in Vancouver (13,000 people). Singer Shannon Hoon stripped naked for the final three songs, then urinated on the audience. The band attempted to escape on their tour bus, but police cruisers blocked the exit. Hoon climbed onto the roof of the bus and began screaming at the officers. It took 15 minutes to talk him down; he was then arrested and charged with committing an indecent act. The promoter spent the following weeks apologizing to those who had been urinated upon. "Which kind of gives a whole new meaning to the title of this song."


# artist - 311

## curiosities
**KKK Conspiracy Theory – Band Name Investigated, T-Shirts Banned, Real Origin Is Indecent Exposure Charge** : In 1993, rumors spread that 311 was code for KKK, since K is the 11th letter of the alphabet and 3×K=KKK. The band's t-shirts were banned in high schools around their hometown of Omaha, Nebraska, and police investigated. The actual origin of the name: a former guitarist named Jim Watson was caught skinny dipping by the Omaha Police Department and charged with indecent exposure, which carries the offense code 311 in the Omaha PD's system. "This is all crap. The real story behind the name relates back to the time when a former guitarist named Jim Watson was caught skinny dipping."


# artist - The Charlatans UK

## members
Rob Collins

## curiosities
**Rob Collins Armed Robbery Conviction (1992–1993) – Friend Robbed Liquor Store "To Impress His Rock Star Friend"** : On December 3, 1992, keyboardist Rob Collins drove an old school friend named Michael Whitehouse to a liquor store and waited in the car. Michael robbed the store — reportedly to impress his rock star friend — and ran back to the car; Collins drove away without knowing the full extent of what had just happened. Witnesses got his license plate number. Three hours later, police arrested Collins; he spent five days in custody while Whitehouse confessed to armed robbery. Original charges of armed robbery and possession of a firearm were reduced to assisting an offender after the crime. At trial on September 21, 1993, Whitehouse was sentenced to four years; Collins received eight months and served time as prisoner RD1533 at Redditch Open Prison, where a fellow inmate hummed the melody of a Charlatans song at him in the showers. Collins died in a car crash on July 22, 1996. "His buddy Michael confessed to armed robbery and then Rob began to talk."

# artist - The White Stripes

## members
Jack White

## curiosities
**Magic Stick Assault (December 13, 2003) – Jack White Beat Von Bondies Singer Jason Stollsteimer** : On a Saturday night at the Magic Stick club in Detroit during the CD release party for a local band called Blanche, Jack White approached Von Bondies singer Jason Stollsteimer, spat in his face, and began punching him. When Stollsteimer fell to the floor, White continued hitting him until bystanders pulled him away. White filed a counter-complaint claiming self-defense. He initially pleaded not guilty, then changed his plea to guilty of misdemeanor assault and battery on March 9, 2004. Sentence: $500 fine, $200 court costs, one year of anger management. White maintained that Stollsteimer had grabbed him and pulled out his hair, and that his retaliation lasted less than 10 seconds. He also alleged that Stollsteimer refused to wash off the blood before posing for press photos sent to NME the next morning, and that the permanently damaged eye he claimed was a pre-existing injury from a contact lens incident as a teenager. White and Stollsteimer had not spoken for two years prior to the incident. "The entire event was completely exploited by him, his band and his managers and legal professionals."


# artist - Ramones

## members
Johnny Ramone

## curiosities
**Fractured Skull Attack (August 1983) – Street Brawl Led to Brain Surgery, Later Distrust of Doctors, and Death** : In August 1983, Johnny Ramone became involved in a brawl with Seth Maclin of a band called Sub Zero Construction on the street outside Johnny's East Village apartment at 4am. Johnny was beaten to the pavement and kicked in the head. He was rushed to hospital with a fractured skull and required brain surgery. He recovered, but the incident left him deeply distrustful of doctors. When he later began experiencing abdominal pain — roughly 15 years after the attack — his mistrust caused him to delay seeking treatment. The abdominal pain was aggressive prostate cancer that spread throughout his body. Chemotherapy failed, and Johnny died on September 15, 2004. "Had he not been beaten up in the summer of 1983 and still trusted doctors?"


# artist - The Stone Roses

## members
Ian Brown

## curiosities
**British Airways Air Rage Conviction (February 13, 1998) – Sentenced to Four Months in Strangeways** : Ian Brown boarded British Airways Flight 1611 from Paris to Manchester on February 13, 1998. During the short flight, a dispute arose with flight attendant Christine Cooper when she made what Brown interpreted as a dismissive wave. Brown allegedly shouted at her and threatened to chop off her hands; when the pilot came out to investigate, Brown reportedly repeated the threat. He was also said to have banged on the cockpit door for 20 to 30 seconds during descent. When the plane landed, he was arrested and charged with air rage. At trial in October 1998, Brown's defense — that his remarks were jokes and he never banged on the door — was not believed. On October 23, 1998, he was sentenced to four months at Strangeways prison. Brown maintained he was made an example because of a timing coincidence with a genuine air rage case in Spain, and claimed racism as a factor. "Ian and his friends and supporters maintained that he was set up as an example."


# artist - The Clash

## members

## curiosities
**Arrested for Stealing a Pillowcase and Towels – Newcastle, June 1977, Fined 100 Pounds** : On June 11, 1977, Joe Strummer and Topper Headon were in Newcastle. The following night they were arrested on robbery charges for allegedly stealing a pillowcase and several towels from a Holiday Inn. They were supposed to have appeared in court in May 1977 to answer these charges but had simply not turned up. A judge took a dim view of the non-appearance; in the end they were fined 100 pounds each. "I mean, the Clash. They were arrested for stealing a pillowcase."
