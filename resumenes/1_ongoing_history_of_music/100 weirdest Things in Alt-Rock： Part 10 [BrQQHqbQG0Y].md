# artist - Nine Inch Nails

## members
Trent Reznor

## songs
**Down in It (1989) – Original Video Mistaken by FBI as Snuff Film** : For the first NIN single from *Pretty Hate Machine*, Trent Reznor and directors Eric Zimmerman and Benjamin Stokes filmed a video in a Cleveland warehouse in September 1989. In the first draft, Trent appeared to fall from a building and die bloody in the street. A camera was attached to a helium weather balloon for aerial shots, but the rope snapped and the camera floated 200 miles away before landing in a farmer's field. The farmer took it to the police, who watched the footage and concluded it was a snuff film showing a real corpse decomposing. Forensic experts agreed the "body" had been there at least three weeks. The FBI was called. Eventually the situation was resolved; the original video was apparently never publicly released, and a new version was shot instead. "Forensic experts poured over the film and came to the conclusion that yes, it did show a real rotting corpse."


# artist - Ramones

## members
Joey Ramone
Johnny Ramone

## songs
**KKK Took My Baby Away (1981) – Joey Ramone's Political Shot at Johnny Over Stolen Girlfriend** : Though the Ramones were presented as a punk brotherhood, singer Joey and guitarist Johnny effectively stopped speaking after 1981 and communicated virtually not at all for over 20 years — through thousands of gigs and at least 12 albums. The rift had three dimensions: Johnny excluded Joey and Dee Dee from media interviews starting in 1978, believing they made the band look dumb; the two were polar political opposites (Johnny was a hardcore Republican who loved Reagan and both Bushes; Joey was a Democrat and anti-Reaganite); and most crucially, Johnny stole Joey's girlfriend Linda Danielle and married her. Joey never forgave him. "KKK Took My Baby Away" (1981) was Joey's coded strike against Johnny. The feud was never resolved: Joey died on April 15, 2001, and Johnny on September 17, 2004, with ill feeling still intact. "The singer and the guitarist, two of the founding members of one of the most important bands of all time, didn't speak to each other for 20 years through thousands of gigs and at least 12 albums."

## curiosities
**Johnny Ramone – Documented All 2,262 Ramones Gigs** : As the Ramones' de facto leader, chief strategist, and disciplinarian, Johnny Ramone made most business decisions, dictated what everyone wore on stage, controlled practice schedules, approved every set list, and scrupulously documented every single one of the 2,262 gigs the Ramones ever played. "Johnny was the one who scrupulously documented every single one of the 2,262 gigs the Ramones ever played."


# artist - Blur

## members
Alex James

## curiosities
**Alex James – Became Celebrated British Cheesemaker After Blur's 2004 Hiatus** : When Blur's extended hiatus began in 2004, bassist Alex James moved to the Cotswolds and immersed himself in artisan cheesemaking. His farm features more than 400 sheep, valued for their milk. His specialty is a soft goat's cheese wrapped in a vine leaf called "Little Wallop," and he also makes varieties called "Geronimo" (named after his eldest son), "Churchill Lady," "Rotten Bastard," and "Mad Bitch," plus a triangular variety he calls "Wedgie." He produced a video series called "The Cheese Diaries" for *The Guardian*. The origin of his cheese obsession traces to an early Blur Japan tour when, asked what fans should bring him as gifts, he drunkenly said "cheese" — and was deluged with Japanese cheese at every subsequent visit. "Alex became, in the words of some, the poster boy for the renaissance of British cheesemaking."


# artist - Radiohead

## albums
**In Rainbows (2007) / OK Computer (1997) – The "10-Spiracy"** : Radiohead fans identified an elaborate numerical pattern connecting the two albums over a decade apart. *In Rainbows* has 10 tracks, contains 10 letters in its title, was released on October 10 (the 10th day of the 10th month), was made available on an alleged 10 download servers, was announced on October 1 (10 days before release) via 10 cryptic fan messages each using the letter X (Roman numeral 10). Some fans believe the albums are meant to be listened to together by interleaving tracks — starting with "Airbag" from *OK Computer*, then "15 Step" from *In Rainbows*, alternating, with each song cross-faded by 10 seconds. A listener named Kevin applied the golden mean (1.618/divine proportion) to the total length of *In Rainbows* in seconds, producing a timestamp landing at 2 minutes 49 seconds into "Reckoner" — a major crescendo where Tom's vocals shift "from U to In Rainbows." A mole inside Radiohead reportedly confirmed Thom Yorke was "most amused" watching the theorists at work. "It sent chills down my spine."


# artist - Tool

## curiosities
**Lachrymology – Tool's Invented Pseudo-Science of Crying** : Tool promotes a fake philosophy called "lachrymology" (the study of crying), supposedly derived from a 1940s book called *A Joyful Guide to Lachrymology* by one Ronald P. Vincent — a former crop duster turned philosopher after his wife was "dismembered in a bizarre snowplow accident." The only source for the existence of this book and its author is Tool themselves, who have never confirmed or denied the hoax. The core thesis — that humans cannot evolve emotionally, spiritually, or mentally without experiencing pain, and that releasing repressed trauma (possibly through crying) improves mental, spiritual, and physical condition — has been elaborated by fans into something resembling Freudian psychoanalysis. "Tool maintains that they discovered this science through the writings of Ronald P. Vincent, who wrote a book back in the 1940s."


# artist - Nirvana

## curiosities
**Church of Kurt Cobain (1996) – Media Hoax Won a Contest to Fool The Oregonian** : On May 29, 1996, a man calling himself "Reverend Jim Dillon" announced the founding of the Church of Kurt Cobain in Portland, Oregon, using Nirvana song lyrics as liturgy. The story received front-page coverage in *The Oregonian*, was picked up by MTV, and led the ABC Evening News with Peter Jennings. In reality it was a hoax — a local radio station had run a contest to see if listeners could trick *The Oregonian* into printing a fake story, and the winner was a man named Jerry Catel, who became "the Reverend Jim Dillon." His secondary intent was to make a statement about media shallowness and celebrity idolatry. "It was a hoax. The truth is that all this originated with a local radio station which staged a contest to see if listeners could get the Oregonian to print a fake story."


# artist - Sigue Sigue Sputnik

## labels
EMI Records

## albums
**Flaunt It (1986) – Debut Album with Advertisements Embedded Between Tracks** : Sigue Sigue Sputnik built their entire career on selling out as an art form, with music secondary to hype, image, and money-making. They signed to EMI without even submitting a demo tape — only a video of science fiction film clips. Their main slogan was "Fleece the World." Before the album's release they auctioned advertising space between the tracks, with studio line from L'Oréal and *ID* magazine buying space on the external artwork and commercials embedded within the record itself. Several band members had almost zero musical ability. The album underperformed and the band broke up after their second release. "With the way the music industry is going these days, maybe they were just ahead of their time."


# artist - Sex Pistols

Sid Vicious

## curiosities
**Sid Vicious Ashes Allegedly Spilled at Heathrow Terminal 4 (February 1979)** : After Sid Vicious's death on February 2, 1979 (heroin overdose while awaiting trial for the murder of Nancy Spungen), his mother Anne Beverly had him cremated. Two weeks later, allegedly drunk while returning to London through Heathrow Airport, she tripped, fell, and spilled Sid's ashes in the terminal — some reportedly embedded in the carpet and sucked into the ventilation system. Rumor holds that Sid Dust still circulates through Terminal 4. However, those with knowledge say this is not what happened: what is confirmed is that before returning to England, Anne took his ashes to Nancy Spungen's grave in Philadelphia and spread them there. What is also confirmed is that New York police tipped off London police, who raided Anne's Notting Hill home, found drugs, and arrested her. Anne Beverly died on September 6, 1996, after carefully putting her affairs in order and then consuming a large quantity of painkillers, tranquilizers, and alcohol. "Rumor has it that Sid Dust still circulates throughout terminal four."


# artist - Hole

## curiosities
**Courtney Love's First Husband – Punk Transvestite Singer Falling James Moreland (1989)** : Before marrying Kurt Cobain, Courtney Love had a drunken impulse-wedding in Las Vegas in 1989 with punk transvestite singer Falling James Moreland, the lead vocalist of the Leaving Trains (who had released albums on Black Flag's SST label). Moreland was the boyfriend of Jennifer Finch, who was about to join LA band L7. After sobering up, Courtney realized the mistake and they divorced within months. Moreland produced the first single for Courtney's new band, which was supposed to be called "Sweet Baby Crystal Powered by God" but was released as Hole — and the single was called "Retard Girl." "Listen, they've seen stranger things in Vegas."


# artist - Depeche Mode

## curiosities
**Stephen Hawking Was a Depeche Mode Fan Who Repeatedly Failed to Get Backstage** : Throughout the 1990s, cosmologist Stephen Hawking had his assistant write letters to Depeche Mode's management requesting a backstage meeting. Letters were repeatedly ignored. He then escalated to contacting their record company, which finally reached the band — whose reply was: "We're always getting letters from this guy trying to get free tickets. "No word on whether Professor Hawking ever got to meet his musical heroes."
