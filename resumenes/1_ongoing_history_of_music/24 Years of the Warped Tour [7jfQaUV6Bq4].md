**Longest Running Touring Music Festival (1995-2018)** : The Vans Warped Tour began in 1995 and continued annually until the summer of 2018, making it the longest-running touring music festival. "The Vans Warp Tour began in 1995 and ran every year until the summer of 2018."
**Founder Kevin Lyman** : The Warped Tour was founded by Kevin Lyman, who conceived the idea while working as a stage manager for Lollapalooza in 1991, aiming to combine music with extreme sports like skateboarding. "The founder of Warped is Kevin Lyman and for a guy who has brought so much music to so many people, we really don't know that much about him."
**Initial Name "The Bomb" (1995)** : Kevin Lyman initially intended to name his new tour "The Bomb." However, on the very day of its announcement, April 19, 1995, the Oklahoma City bombing occurred, leading him to change the name to "Warped." "His would be called the bomb. Great name. However, on the very day, Kevin was going to announce his new venture, his very first tour. This would be April the 19th, 1995. Timothy McVey blew up the federal building in Oklahoma City."
**First Tour Details (1995)** : The inaugural Warped Tour took place in 1995, with the first date on August 4 in Salt Lake City. It comprised 25 dates, including stops in Toronto and Vancouver, Canada, featuring about 20 up-and-coming alternative bands on two stages. "And in 1995, the first Warped Tour went ahead. The first date was August 4 in Salt Lake City. There were 25 dates, including two in Canada, Toronto and Vancouver."
**Initial Financial Losses (1995)** : The first Warped Tour in 1995 (then just "Warped Tour") resulted in financial losses for Kevin Lyman, who couldn't pay everyone, leading him to seek a title sponsor for future iterations. "Kevin realized that if the festival were to continue, he would need a sponsor. He lost money and couldn't pay everyone that first time out, and things were pretty grim."
**Vans Sponsorship (1996)** : In 1996, Vans, a skateboard and shoe company, signed a $300,000 deal to become the title sponsor of the tour, officially renaming it the "Vans Warped Tour" from that year forward. This happened after a blizzard prevented a potential deal with Calvin Klein. "And before anything could be rescheduled, Vans, the skateboard company, the shoe company, stepped in during that 24 hour delay and signed a deal for $300,000. So from 1996 forward, it was officially the Vans Warped Tour."
**Content Pivot to Punk and Skate Culture (1996)** : With the new Vans sponsorship, the festival's content shifted from broad-range alternative music to a focus on punk and other genres that resonated with skate culture. "His new sponsorship also meant a content pivot. Kevin moved the festival from broad range alternative to focusing on punk and other music that went well with skate culture."
**"Punk Rock Summer Boot Camp"** : Founder Kevin Lyman frequently referred to the Warped Tour as his "punk rock summer boot camp," due to the demanding nature, low pay, grueling pace, tough crowds, and lack of luxuries for participants. "The Wonder Kevin Lyman often called Warped his punk rock summer boot camp."
**Expansion to Multiple Stages (1997)** : The Warped Tour expanded its setup, featuring a main stage, a side stage, and a "locals only" stage, starting around the third tour. He became involved in booking bands while attending California State Polytechnic University in Pomona, where he majored in recreation administration in the early 1980s. After graduation, he worked as a production manager at Golden Voice, an LA-based concert promotion company, before founding Warped Tour. "We know he's American. He was born in 1961.
