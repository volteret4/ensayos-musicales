# curiosity

**Band Name Origins – 60 Examples (Bandminology)** : A coinage attributed to the podcast for the study of band name origins, combining etymology with branding: "bandminology." The following entries document how specific bands got their names.

**30 Seconds to Mars – Harvard Professor's Thesis on Technological Progress** : Jared and Shannon Leto found the name in a subsection of a Harvard professor's thesis about how fast technology advances — the suggestion being that on an evolutionary scale, humans are close to reaching Mars. They felt the concept described their musical ambitions. "Such a grand thought described perfectly what they wanted to do with their band."

**Alexis on Fire – Real Person: World's Only Lactating Contortionist Stripper** : The name comes from a real woman named Alexis Fire, described as the world's only lactating contortionist stripper. She attempted to sue the band for copyright infringement, but because she had never registered the name, nothing came of it. "She's a real person who tried to sue the band for copyright infringement."

**Arcade Fire – Singer Win Butler's Childhood Story of a Burning Arcade** : Singer Win Butler insists the name comes from a story told to him when he was 12: a high school kid who beat him up kept referencing an arcade fire that allegedly killed a bunch of kids. "It was more of a threat, really."

**Arctic Monkeys – Guitarist Jamie Cook Chose It; Possibly Japanese Snow Monkeys** : The band dislikes the name, but guitarist Jamie Cook came up with it without ever fully explaining why, though some suggest he saw a picture of Japanese snow monkeys sitting in a hot spring. "The band hates that name, but that's what happens when you don't give things much thought."

**Billy Talent – Character in the Canadian Film Hardcore Logo** : Named after a character in the Canadian rock-and-roll film Hardcore Logo, played by Calum Keith Rennie — later known for Battlestar Galactica and Californication. In the film, "Talent" is spelled with two L's. "You know this story."

**Broken Social Scene – Kevin Drew's Jokey Gig as "John Tesh Jr. and the Broken Social Scene"** : Kevin Drew had a keyboard setup he likened to soft music king John Tesh. He played a jokey gig under the name "John Tesh Jr. and the Broken Social Scene." The second half of that name stuck for his next project. "The last half of that name stuck."

**Cage the Elephant – Multiple Conflicting Stories; Band Won't Tell** : The band offers conflicting origin stories — a mentally deranged man yelling "you gotta cage the elephant" at them, a fortune teller turning over a card and screaming it. They've said, "We'll never tell." "There's a bunch of others, the band's coy."

**City and Colour – Dallas Green; City = Dallas; Colour = Green** : "Terribly obvious yet not." Dallas Green. Get it? "City and color, which is terribly obvious yet not."

**Coldplay – Adopted from Another Band Who Had the Name from a Poetry Collection** : Originally called Starfish at university. Another Oxford group called Coldplay (two words) had taken their name from a poetry collection called "Children's Reflections Coldplay." That band was going defunct and agreed to give up the name. "That other band were in the process of going defunct so Starfish asked them if they could have their name."

**Death Cab for Cutie – 1967 British Novelty Song About a Woman Killed in a Taxi** : The Bonzo Dog Doodah Band recorded a song called "Death Cab for Cutie" on their 1967 album Gorilla, which was itself based on a tabloid headline about a woman killed in a taxi cab accident. "That in turn was based on a tabloid headline for a story about a woman who was killed in an accident involving a taxi."

**Foo Fighters – WWII Pilots' Term for Unexplained Balls of Fire** : In WWII skies over Europe, pilots on both sides reported mysterious fast-moving balls of fire they called Foo Fighters (from the French "feu" for fire). Dave Grohl considers the name a mistake and a stupid band name, but the UFO connection was appealing. "Dave Grohl thinks that's a stupid name for a band, but the band immunology is fascinating."

**Foster the People – Misheard Band Introduction Stuck** : Singer Mark Foster intended the name to be "Foster and the People" with himself as a named frontman. People kept missing the "and" when he introduced the group, and "Foster the People" stuck. "People kept missing the AND whenever he introduced the group."

**Kings of Leon – Tribute to Grandfather/Great-Uncle Leon Followill** : The Followill brothers (Caleb, Nathan, Jared) and cousin Matthew are all related to Leon Followill — grandfather of the brothers, great-uncle of Matthew. The name pays tribute to him. "They wanted to pay tribute to him by carrying on his name."

**Linkin Park – Chester Bennington Named After a Lincoln Park in Santa Monica; Spelling Changed for Domain** : Chester Bennington named the band after driving past Lincoln Park in Santa Monica. The spelling change from Lincoln to Linkin was made to secure the domain name "LinkinPark.com." Note: that park in Santa Monica has since been renamed Christine Emerson Reed Park. "By the way, don't go looking for that park in Santa Monica because it's been renamed."

**Nine Inch Nails – Trent Reznor Simply Liked It After Two Weeks** : Trent Reznor constantly doodled band name ideas; his rule was that if he still liked a name after two weeks, he kept it. Nine Inch Nails was the only name that made the cut. "There isn't a great story behind it."

**Pearl Jam – Named After Neil Young; Earlier Named After Basketball Player Mookie Blalock** : The fabricated story about Eddie Vedder's grandmother Pearl and her hallucinogenic jam spread was invented by Eddie himself, who has admitted it. The band was originally called Mookie Blalock after the New Jersey Nets point guard, but lawyers made noise about trademark use. They liked the word "Pearl" and after seeing Neil Young jam at a New Jersey concert, combined the two. "He made that all up."

**Queens of the Stone Age – Chris Goss Suggested Changing "Kings" to "Queens"** : Producer Chris Goss of Masters of Reality suggested the name during recording sessions. The band had been considering "Kings of the Stone Age." Goss's argument: "queens" implied they were heavy enough for boys and sweet enough for girls. "Which seems to have worked."

**Soundgarden – Named After a Sculpture at NOAA in Seattle** : There is a sculpture by artist Doug Hollis on the grounds of the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration in Seattle; when the wind blows, it makes "cool sinister noises." The sculpture is called Soundgarden. "In the wind blows, the sculpture makes some cool sinister noises."

**Sum 41 – Three Competing Stories; Random Number Most Likely** : Three theories: (1) band formed 41 days into summer 1996 (which would be September 28th, past summer's end — problematic); (2) a penny, nickel, dime, and quarter sum to 41 cents; (3) on a computer keyboard, Shift+4 gives a dollar sign and 1 gives an exclamation mark, together expressing a wish to get rich. No definitive answer. "Take your pick."

**Tragically Hip – From a Michael Nesmith VHS Skit (1981) and an Elvis Costello Song (1982)** : Michael Nesmith of the Monkees released a VHS tape of comedy sketches in 1981 called Elephant Parts, which included a piece called "The Tragically Hip." Gord Downie then heard the phrase in an Elvis Costello song called "Town Cryer" from the 1982 album Imperial Bedroom. Two references to the phrase felt like a sign. "The two references to the phrase seemed to be like some kind of sign."

**Alice in Chains – From a Stoned/Drunk Party Conversation About Alice in Wonderland; Layne Staley's Mom Was Upset** : Originally intended as a jokey drag side project where members would dress in drag and play metal. The name came from a stoned and drunk conversation about Lewis Carroll's Alice in Wonderland. Layne Staley's mother was especially upset with the name's connotations and preferred a different name, but when the band broke up and reformed as Alice in Chains it stuck regardless. "Lane's mom was especially upset with the connection."

**Blur – Label Hated "Seymour"; Told Them to Pick From a List** : When Blur signed their record deal they were called Seymour, but the label hated it. The band was presented with a series of alternatives and chose Blur. "So they were presented with a series of new names, told to pick one."

**The Clash – Newspaper Headline: "Clash with Police"** : When looking for a name, a story in the Daily Telegraph caught their attention: a headline about a clash with police. "Simple, newspaper headline."

**Depeche Mode – Name of a French Fashion Magazine; Translates as "Fast Fashion"** : The name is directly taken from a French fashion magazine called Dépêche Mode. "It translates as fast fashion."

**Green Day – A Day Spent Smoking Weed; From an Early Billy Joe Armstrong Song** : The name refers to a day spent smoking marijuana ("a green day"). It started as an early Billy Joe Armstrong song written when the group was still called Sweet Children. "That's all about a day, smoking weed, a green day."

**Jane's Addiction – Named After Perry Farrell's Real Roommate Jane Bainter** : There really was a woman named Jane Bainter who was Perry Farrell's roommate at a shared house on North Wilton Place in Los Angeles in the 1980s. When things went wrong at the house — parties, drugs, alcohol — the trouble was always blamed on "Jane's addiction." The Jane's Addiction logo was designed using Jane Bainter's own handwriting. Jane received no compensation. The song "Jane Says" tells her story: she had a smack habit, a dealer boyfriend named Sergio, and dreams of going to Spain. "How much did Jane get from the band for all her contributions? Nothing. Zero."

**Jimmy Eat World – Crayon Drawing by a Kid Named Ed of His Brother Jim Eating the World** : Guitarist Tom Linton had two younger brothers, Jim and Ed, who fought constantly. After one epic battle, Ed drew a picture of Jim shoving the Earth into his mouth, captioned "Jimmy Eat World." "They fought all the time."

**Oasis – Manchester Music Venue Oasis Leisure Center; Noel Gallagher Was a Roadie for the Inspiral Carpets** : Named after a Manchester music venue called the Oasis Leisure Center, where Noel Gallagher had worked as a roadie for the Inspiral Carpets. "Noel Gallagher was once their drum roady."

**R.E.M. – Michael Stipe Randomly Pulled It From a Dictionary** : The band needed a name before their first gig at a church in Athens, Georgia. Michael Stipe opened a dictionary at random and pulled out REM, which stands for Rapid Eye Movement and relates to dreaming. For the band, the letters don't stand for anything. "Singer Michael Stipe just randomly pulled it out of a dictionary."

**Sex Pistols – Named After Manager Malcolm McLaren's Bondage Clothing Store "Sex"** : The Sex Pistols were essentially a manufactured punk boy band put together by Malcolm McLaren, who ran a bondage clothing store in London called Sex. He also liked the word "pistols" for its dangerous connotation. The name was "basically a living, breathing advertisement for his store."

**Stone Temple Pilots – Reverse-Engineered from an STP Oil Treatment Sticker** : Originally called Mighty Joe Young, the band had to change names due to a blues musician using the same name. In their backyard was an STP oil treatment sticker on a shrine-like structure. They tried to make the initials stand for something: after considering "Stinky Toilet Paper" and obscene options, they landed on Stone Temple Pilots. STP is also slang for the hallucinogen 2,5-dimethoxy-4-methylamphetamine. "They went through a bunch of possibilities, including stinky toilet paper."

**The Killers – Named After a Fictional Band in a New Order Music Video** : The name came from the name of a fictional band appearing on the bass drum in the video for New Order's "Crystal." "That was the name of a fictional band in the video."

**The Stooges – Iggy Pop Named After an Iguana and a Drug Dealer Named Jim Pop** : Iggy was named "Iggy" because he once played drums in a band called The Iguanas. The "Pop" came after he shaved his eyebrows and someone pointed out he looked like a local drug dealer named Jim Pop. The "Stooges" came from the Three Stooges comedy group. "Then the Pop part came when he shaved off his eyebrows."

**Twenty One Pilots – From Arthur Miller's Play "All My Sons" About 21 Pilots Who Died** : Singer Tyler Joseph was studying Arthur Miller's World War II–set play All My Sons in film class, about a man who knowingly allowed defective plane parts to go into service, killing 21 pilots. The lack of a hyphen in the band name remains unexplained and annoying. "I've never found a reason why there is no hyphen in the band name 21 Pilots."

**U2 – Name Suggested by Steve Averill of Radiators from Space; Chosen from a List of Ten** : When Adam Clayton called Dublin ad agency worker Steve Averill asking for a band rename (from The Hype), Averill suggested a list including U2 because abbreviations were fashionable (XTC, etc.). The band chose U2. Averill is now U2's art director. "Steve is now working as U2's art director."

**Velvet Underground – Lou Reed Found a Sadomasochism Novel in a New York Gutter** : Lou Reed found a trashy paperback novel about sadomasochism in a New York City gutter. It was called The Velvet Underground. "That was the best thing that ever happened to author Michael Lee."

**Weezer – Rivers Cuomo's Childhood Asthma Nickname** : Rivers Cuomo had bad asthma as a kid and was called "Weezer" by friends. "Rivers Cuomo had bad asthma as a kid, so his friends called him Weezer."

**The White Stripes – Meg White's Love of Red and White Peppermint Candies** : When Jack met Meg, she was obsessed with red and white peppermint candies. The original band name idea was The Peppermints, but since their shared last name was White, they went with The White Stripes. "She really, really liked red and white peppermint candies."

**Alt-J – Mac Keyboard Shortcut for the Delta Symbol (Triangle)** : The band's actual name is Delta (Δ), the Greek letter that looks like a triangle. The keyboard shortcut to type a triangle on a Mac is Alt+J. "If you want to type that symbol on a Mac, the keyboard shortcut is, yes, Alt J."

**The Black Keys – Possibly from Patrick Carney's Father's Insult, or a Homeless Man's Farewell** : One story: Patrick Carney's father called him and Dan Auerbach "a couple of Black Keys" meaning they were slightly weird or off. Another story: a homeless schizophrenic man who asked people for crayons always said goodbye with "Don't be a Black Key. Be a B flat." "The best, most calming song that you can play for an anxious dog in the car."

**Daft Punk – Named After a Melody Maker Review That Called Their Previous Band "Daft Punky Thrash"** : They previously recorded under the name Darlin'. A bad review in the UK music weekly Melody Maker described their sound as "daft punky thrash." They killed Darlin' and adopted the insult as their new name. "After that they killed Darlin and went with Daft Punk, which you have to admit is a much better name."

**Phoenix – Either Named After a Daft Punk Song or After a Band Member's Cleft Palate Scar** : Two theories: the name comes from a song on Daft Punk's Homework album; or one of the band members was born with a cleft palate that required surgery, leaving a scar similar to actor Joaquin Phoenix's scar. "That's a bit of a stretch, but all right, fine."

**Fall Out Boy – From a Simpsons Character** : Named after a character in The Simpsons — the radioactive mascot sidekick to Radioactive Man. "Remember that Simpsons episode?"

**Imagine Dragons – An Anagram; Band Won't Reveal the Original Words** : The band has confirmed it is an anagram but has refused to say what words were scrambled to create "Imagine Dragons." They have hinted that apostrophes are required in the source phrase. Online generators suggest possibilities including "adoring images," "a roaming design," and "radioman egg sin." "They're still not saying."

**My Chemical Romance – From an Irvine Welsh Book Title Plus My Bloody Valentine** : Irvine Welsh (who wrote Trainspotting) also wrote a book called Five Tales of Chemical Romance. The "My" was added as a homage to My Bloody Valentine. My Bloody Valentine's name itself derives from the title of a 1981 Canadian slasher film. "That's a bonus for this list."

**Of Mice and Men – John Steinbeck Novel (1937)** : The band name comes from John Steinbeck's 1937 novella Of Mice and Men. "Still with literature."

**Vampire Weekend – Band Member Ezra Koenig Filmed a Low-Budget Horror Movie of That Name** : More than two years before the band formed, Ezra Koenig was the cameraman for an ultra-low-budget horror film called Vampire Weekend. "He was behind the camera for an ultra low budget horror film."

**White Zombie – Rob Zombie's Love of a 1932 Bela Lugosi Horror Film** : Rob Zombie (real name Rob Cummings) named the band after a 1932 Bela Lugosi horror film called White Zombie. "One of his favorite was a 1932 Bela Lugosi movie called White Zombie."

**Sloan – Named After a French-Speaking Boss's Mispronunciation of "Slow One"** : The band's friend Jason had a French-speaking boss who thought Jason wasn't very bright and called him "a slow one." With the French accent, it came out as "Sloan," which became Jason's nickname. The band asked to use the name, and Jason agreed on condition that he be on the cover of their first release. He is indeed on the cover of the Peppermint EP. "So if you have a copy of Sloan's peppermint EP, that's a picture of Jason on the front."

**Metric – Named After a Keyboard Preset** : The band was previously called Mainstream but changed it after one EP to Metric, named after a preset on one of keyboardist Jimmy Shaw's keyboards. "After a preset on one of Jimmy's keyboards."

**Modest Mouse – Passage from Virginia Woolf's Story "The Mark on the Wall"** : The name comes from a line in Virginia Woolf's short story "The Mark on the Wall": "and very frequent in the minds of modest, mouse-colored people." "It's a passage in a story called the mark on the wall by Virginia Woolf."

**Bastille – Dan Smith's Birthday, July 14; Bastille Day in France** : The band had a hard time finding a name until someone pointed out that singer Dan Smith's birthday is July 14 — Bastille Day in France. "That's Bastille Day in France."

**Joy Division – Section of a Nazi Concentration Camp Novel** : The name comes from the novel House of Dolls (1955) by Yehiel De-Nur, which describes a section of a Nazi concentration camp where female prisoners were kept as sex slaves for officers on leave. That section was called the Joy Division. "This is another reference to a novel."

**New York Dolls – Named After the New York Doll Hospital, a Real Doll Repair Shop** : The New York Dolls took their name from the New York Doll Hospital, a real shop in New York that repaired dolls. "A place that actually repaired dolls."

**Pogues – Short for the Irish Gaelic Phrase "Pog Mahon," Meaning Kiss My Ass** : The name is an abbreviation of the Gaelic phrase "póg mo thóin," which translates as "kiss my ass." "Which translates as Kiss My Ass."

**Ramones – Paul McCartney Once Checked Into Hotels as "Paul Ramone"** : When the Beatles were touring, Paul McCartney sometimes checked into hotels under the pseudonym Paul Ramone. That name was good enough for the Ramones. "When the Beatles were touring, Paul McCartney would often sign into hotels as Paul Ramone."
