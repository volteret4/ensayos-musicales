# artist - AC/DC

## members
Bon Scott
Brian Johnson
Dave Evans
Axl Rose

## curiosities
**Four Lead Singers – Bon Scott Died 1980, Brian Johnson Made Back in Black, Axl Rose Subbed in 2016** : AC/DC has had four lead singers. Dave Evans was the original, present from the band's 1973 formation through their glam-rock phase. Bon Scott replaced him after being discovered working as the band's chauffeur; he recorded seven albums cementing AC/DC's reputation but died of acute alcohol poisoning on February 19, 1980. Brian Johnson replaced him in time for *Back in Black* (1980), which has sold at least 50 million copies. When Johnson developed serious ear problems in 2016, Axl Rose of Guns N' Roses sang for approximately two dozen shows; Johnson subsequently received treatment and rejoined. "The swap worked way better than anyone could have dreamed."


# artist - Alice in Chains

## members
Layne Staley
William DuVall

## curiosities
**Layne Staley Found Dead April 19, 2002; William DuVall Joined, Three Albums Followed** : Layne Staley was the person around whom Alice in Chains was originally formed in 1984. By 1995, heroin had rendered him almost unable to function; the band could neither record nor tour. His body was found on April 19, 2002, in his Seattle condo after two weeks of no contact, still with a syringe in its arm. After a 2004 tsunami benefit with various substitute vocalists, the band decided to continue and eventually settled on William DuVall from a Los Angeles band called Comes with the Fall — who could blend his voice with guitarist Jerry Cantrell's harmonics and was also a capable guitarist. Three Alice in Chains albums followed. "He was also a pretty fair guitar player."


# artist - Black Flag

## members
Keith Morris
Henry Rollins

## curiosities
**Henry Rollins – Former 7-Eleven Clerk from DC Replaced Co-Founder, Took Band to New Level** : Black Flag was co-founded in 1976 with Keith Morris as singer, but Morris was gone by 1979 (creative differences, cocaine and speed). After brief stints by Ron Reyes and Dez Cadena, the band discovered Henry Rollins — a Washington, D.C. hardcore fan and former 7-Eleven clerk — who had attended their shows. Rollins took Black Flag to an entirely new level and remained their singer until they broke up in summer 1986. "His replacement was a former 7-11 clerk from Washington, D.C., named Henry Rollins."

# artist - Faith No More

## members
Mike Patton
Chuck Mosley

## curiosities
**Courtney Love Briefly Auditioned (1983); Chuck Mosley Fired After Falling Asleep at Showcase; Mike Patton Took Over** : Faith No More evolved from a band called Sharp Young Men, eventually cycling through several singers, including an approximately 20-minute audition by Courtney Love sometime in 1983. Chuck Mosley served as singer from 1983 and recorded their first two albums, but was fired in 1988 after getting into fights with bandmates and roadies, refusing to do anything but acoustic sets, and — most critically — falling asleep on stage during an important showcase performance. His replacement was Mike Patton, who fronted the band for 10 years (1988–1998) and again from 2009 onward. "Epic" remains their biggest song, from Patton's first album with them. "He actually fell asleep on stage during an important showcase event."

# artist - I Mother Earth

## members
Edwin
Brian Byrne

## curiosities
**Edwin Left 1997 Solo; Brian Byrne Tape Initially Thrown Out, Then Hired; Edwin Returned 2016** : I Mother Earth formed when Jagori Tanna and Christian Tanna met Edwin at their shared rehearsal space in 1990, creating a band immediately subject to a major-label bidding war. Edwin left in 1997 to go solo, finishing tour commitments that summer. The following year, auditions began; hundreds of demo tapes arrived, including one from a Newfoundlander named Brian Byrne that was immediately discarded. A mutual friend insisted they look again; Brian got a second chance, got the gig, and served as singer from 1998 to 2003. After a long hiatus, a 2012 reunion brought Brian back; Edwin was invited back again in 2016. Both occasionally share the stage on current tours. "His tape was immediately thrown out."


# artist - Iron Maiden

## members
Bruce Dickinson

Blaze Bayley

## curiosities
**Seven Lead Singers; Paul Di'Anno on First Two Albums; Bruce Dickinson Returned After Blaze Bayley's Vocal Issues** : Iron Maiden's singer history begins with Paul Day (about two years), then Dennis Wilcock (a performer who would put fake blood capsules in his mouth and draw a sword across his face to "bleed" on stage), then Paul Di'Anno — who recorded the band's first two studio albums before being fired for excessive drinking and drug use. Bruce Dickinson joined in 1981 and was with the band through 1993 before leaving to focus on solo work. "Dennis Wilcock had a thing about where he'd put capsules of fake blood in his mouth."


# artist - Joy Division / New Order

## members
Ian Curtis
Bernard Sumner
Gillian Gilbert
Stephen Morris

## songs
**Blue Monday (1983) – Band Agreed to Dissolve if Anyone Left; Reconstituted as New Order After Ian Curtis's Death** : Joy Division had a pact: if anyone left for any reason, the band would break up. When singer Ian Curtis hanged himself from his kitchen doorframe on May 18, 1980 — the night before a North American tour that could have broken the band internationally — the three surviving members honored the dissolution but immediately reconstituted under the name New Order. Curtis was 23. After everyone took turns at vocals, guitarist Bernard Sumner was designated singer in late July 1980; Stephen Morris's girlfriend Gillian Gilbert was added on keyboards that fall, completing the classic lineup. "Blue Monday" — their biggest single — was built by accident from a drum machine left running while the band tried to leave the stage early; a missed programmed note became a happy accident. The most successful 12-inch in history, it lost money on every copy sold due to the expensive die-cut packaging. The name Blue Monday refers to the Monday after Ian Curtis died by suicide the previous Sunday. "New order made it cool to dance again."


# artist - Kasabian

## members
Tom Meighan
Serge Pizzorno

## curiosities
**Tom Meighan Dismissed July 2020 After Assault Conviction; Serge Pizzorno New Frontman from October 2021** : Kasabian formed in 1997 and carved out a successful career in the UK over more than a decade. In July 2020, founding singer and member Tom Meighan announced he was leaving — a mutual decision following his admission in court that he had assaulted his ex-fiancée in front of their child, which was a clear red line for the rest of the band. He was permanently dismissed and severed from all band associations. Guitarist Serge Pizzorno took over as singer, with this new version of Kasabian making their public debut in October 2021. "That was a red line for everyone else."


# artist - Rage Against the Machine / Audioslave

## members
Zack de la Rocha
Chris Cornell

## curiosities
**Audioslave Formed After Rick Rubin Suggested Chris Cornell; Be Real of Cypress Hill Also Auditioned** : After Zack de la Rocha quit Rage Against the Machine in 2000, the remaining three members tried to continue under the RATM name with a new singer. Be Real of Cypress Hill auditioned. But after some jamming, they decided they didn't want a Zack sound-alike — they wanted something new. Producer Rick Rubin suggested they talk to ex-Soundgarden singer Chris Cornell. The chemistry was immediate. They began writing as "Civilian" or "the Civilian project" but had to rename when they discovered another group was using that name. They chose Audioslave. The group was essentially Rage with a different singer, and they were productive from 2001 through 2007. "The chemistry was immediate."


# artist - Small Faces / Faces

## members
Steve Marriott
Rod Stewart
Ronnie Wood

## curiosities
**Small Faces Became Faces When Rod Stewart and Ronnie Wood Joined – Both Later Became Rock Royalty** : When Steve Marriott left the Small Faces in 1969 to form Humble Pie, the rest of the group hired a new guitarist and a new singer. The guitarist was Ronnie Wood, who six years later would replace Mick Taylor in the Rolling Stones. The singer was a gravel-voiced former grave digger named Rod Stewart. The name changed from Small Faces to simply the Faces — because the two tall new members made the "small" descriptor irrelevant. "The new guitarist was Ronnie Wood. Six years later he'd replace Mick Taylor in the Rolling Stones."


# artist - Stone Temple Pilots

## members
Scott Weiland
Chester Bennington
Jeff Gutt

## curiosities
**Three Singers Post-Weiland – Chester Bennington Moonlighted from Linkin Park; Jeff Gutt Won Online Audition** : Scott Weiland was fired and rehired multiple times; the first replacement attempt involved David Coutts in a short-lived side project called Talk Show that failed entirely. Weiland was fired for good in 2013; he died of a drug overdose in 2016 without reconciling with the band. Chester Bennington of Linkin Park — a lifelong STP fan — was brought in for approximately two and a half years, was made an official member, toured, and recorded two tracks before returning to Linkin Park. His replacement was Jeff Gutt, found through an online audition that also briefly considered John Borja from the Philippines; Gutt had been a runner-up on Season 3 of *The X Factor* and was confirmed by fall 2017. "Chester was also a lifelong STP fan since, well, since he could remember."


# artist - Three Days Grace

## curiosities
**Matt Walst Replaced Adam Gontier in 2013; Band Subsequently Broke Van Halen's Billboard Record** : Adam Gontier, who had checked himself into rehab for OxyContin addiction in 2005, suddenly left Three Days Grace on January 13, 2013. "They have more number one hits on the Billboard mainstream rock songs chart than anyone else."

## curiosities
**Three Lead Singers – David Lee Roth, Sammy Hagar, Gary Cherone; Eddie Van Halen Died October 6, 2020** : Van Halen has had three lead singers: David Lee Roth (1973–1984), Sammy Hagar (1985–1996), Roth briefly in late 1996, Gary Cherone of Extreme (1996–1999), Hagar again (2003–2005), and Roth from 2006 onward.
