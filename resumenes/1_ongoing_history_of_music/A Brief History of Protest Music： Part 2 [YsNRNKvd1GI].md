# artist - U2

## songs
**Sunday Bloody Sunday (1983) – About the 1972 Bloody Sunday Massacre; 14 Killed; Written When Troubles Were Still Active** : "Sunday Bloody Sunday" from the spring of 1983 tells the story of the Bloody Sunday incident in Derry, Northern Ireland on January 30, 1972, when British soldiers opened fire on a protest march, shooting 26 unarmed people and killing 14, some while trying to help the wounded. The troubles had begun in the late 1960s, with violence between those loyal to the British monarchy and Republicans who wanted the North to join the Republic of Ireland. When Bono wrote the song, the troubles were still very much ongoing — peace was not declared until 1998 with the Good Friday Agreement, after more than 3,500 people had been killed. "That's an incident, the most famous incident in Northern Ireland. And it's the strongest way of saying, how long?"


# artist - The Cranberries

## songs
**Zombie (1994) – IRA Bombing Protest Song; Two Children Died; Often Mistaken for a Song About the Undead** : "Zombie" by the Cranberries is 100% a Troubles protest song, inspired by two IRA bombings in England in February 1993 in which two children died and 56 people were injured. Many listeners assume the song is about the undead. It was a major worldwide hit and remains one of the most recognizable protest songs of the 1990s. "How many people think that zombie from the cranberries is about the undead? Well, no. It is a 100% a Troubles protest song."


# artist - Frankie Goes to Hollywood

## songs
**Two Tribes (1984) – Cold War Protest Song; Video Featured Reagan vs. Chernenko Wrestling Match** : "Two Tribes" was one of the most popular anti-war protest songs of the 1980s. The video featured a wrestling match between Ronald Reagan and Soviet leader Constantine Chernenko, and it was released at a moment when the US and USSR had come dangerously close to nuclear war. In 1983 alone: NORAD ran a training tape on Soviet attack on the live systems causing a false alarm; a Soviet fighter shot down Korean Air Lines Flight 007; a Soviet early warning system falsely detected multiple US ICBM launches, with one submarine commander — Lieutenant Colonel Stanislav Petrov — unilaterally deciding not to launch a retaliatory strike. "The video featured a wrestling match between Ronald Reagan and Constantine Ternenko. It remains chilling to this day."

# artist - Rage Against the Machine

## songs
**Killing in the Name (1992) – Rodney King Episode and LA Riots; Four Albums Covering Volatile Topics** : "Killing in the Name" was generated by the Rodney King episode and the LA Riots of 1992 and remains one of the great angry rock and roll protest songs of all time. Rage Against the Machine released four albums between 1992 and 2000 covering many volatile topics — political disillusionment, institutional distrust, the growing gap between rich and poor, police brutality, and oppression of America's black population. "Rage kept everybody honest with four albums between 1992 and 2000, covering many volatile topics."


# artist - Bikini Kill

## members
Kathleen Hanna

## songs
**Rebel Girl (1992) – Riot Grrrl Anthem; Kathleen Hanna Told Kurt Cobain About Teen Spirit Deodorant** : "Rebel Girl" is one of the defining songs of the riot grrrl movement, led by Kathleen Hanna. Hanna was friends with Kurt Cobain and once noted that he smelled like a flowery girly deodorant. He misunderstood, thinking she was paying him a compliment about his punk attitude, and wrote "Smells Like Teen Spirit" — not knowing that Teen Spirit was the name of a fruity deodorant. Hanna is married to Adam Horowitz of the Beastie Boys and continues her activism today. "He had no idea that teen spirit was the name of a girly, fruity deodorant."


# artist - Sleater-Kinney

## members
Carrie Brownstein

## albums
**Call the Doctor (1996) – Key Riot Grrrl Record; Carrie Brownstein Later of Portlandia** : Sleater-Kinney came together in Olympia, Washington in 1994 and became a key component of the riot grrrl movement as well as the American indie rock scene, ultimately releasing about a dozen studio albums. The band broke up in 2006 but reunited for more tours and records in 2014. Guitarist Carrie Brownstein went on to become an acclaimed actor in Portlandia (with Fred Armisen), a Simpsons voice actor, a guest on Curb Your Enthusiasm, and a music writer with a memoir entitled Hunger Makes Me a Modern Girl. "Not only did they become a key component for the riot girl movement, they're also important to the entire American indie rock scene."

# artist - The Linda Lindas

## members
Mela De La Garza

## songs
**Racist Sexist Boy (2021) – Written Over Zoom During COVID Lockdown After Racist Incident at School** : The Linda Lindas are a young, all-female group from Los Angeles who started making music before any member was old enough to drive. Drummer Mela De La Garza, who is part Chinese, heard someone at her school make a racist remark — the boy's father had told him to stay away from Chinese people. She reported back to her bandmates and together they wrote a song over a Zoom call while in COVID lockdown. The song began as "Idiot Boy" but became "Racist Sexist Boy." "She reported back to her bandmates and together they wrote a song over a Zoom call while they were in COVID lockdown."


# genre - Protest Music

## curiosities
**Why Protest Songs Seem to Disappear – Media Fragmentation, Ultra-Short Attention Spans, 100,000 Songs Uploaded Daily** : The perception that protest songs have disappeared is explained by media fragmentation. The 24-hour news cycle was born June 1, 1980 when CNN signed on. Social media has shortened even that to 12 hours. By the time a moment arrives that might inspire a protest song, the world has already moved on. Meanwhile, 100,000 new songs are uploaded to streaming services every single day, making it nearly impossible for any protest song to gain the traction it once could. As a comparison, Neil Young heard about the Kent State massacre on May 4, 1970; saw pictures in Life magazine a week later; wrote Ohio; recorded it with CSNY on May 21; and it was in stores in early June — yet that one-month timeline produced one of the greatest protest songs ever. "How does an artist capture a moment with a protest song if that moment disappears in less than half a day?"

**Cold War Songs 1984 – OMD "Enola Gay," Rush "Distant Early Warning," Pink Floyd, Nena** : In addition to Frankie Goes to Hollywood's "Two Tribes," the nuclear fears of 1984 generated anti-war protest songs including "Red Skies" by The Fix, "Enola Gay" by OMD, "Two Suns in the Sunset" from Pink Floyd, "99 Luftballons" by Nena, and "Distant Early Warning" from Rush. This was a period when the US and USSR genuinely came close to nuclear conflict on multiple occasions. "It was a very scary time."

**Anti-Thatcher Protest Songs – Heaven 17, English Beat, Morrissey, Poll Tax Coalition** : Margaret Thatcher as Prime Minister generated numerous protest songs: "We Don't Need This Fascist Groove Thing" by Heaven 17, "Stand Down Margaret" by the English Beat, and Morrissey's "Margaret on the Guillotine." Towards the end of the 1980s, the Poll Tax — a new system of taxation widely seen as unfair to the poor — inspired anti-Poll Tax songs from The Jam, Billy Bragg, the Exploited, and others. "Musicians expressed fierce opposition to something known as the Pole Tax, a new system of taxation put in place by Thatcher."

**Riot Grrrl Movement – Olympia WA, Feminist Punk, Girls to the Front Policy, Third Wave Feminism** : The riot grrrl movement began around Olympia, Washington as a very indie, underground wave of feminist punk bands tired of the testosterone-filled rock scene. Bands included Bikini Kill, Bratmobile, Team Dresch, Heavens to Betsy, and British band Huggy Bear. Shows featured a "girls to the front" policy and were made into safe spaces. The movement is considered a branch of third wave feminism, which pushed for individualism, diversity, sex positivity, and women's roles in politics and society. It also generated zines, manifestos, and activism beyond music. "The first riot girls were tired of the testosterone-filled, dude-heavy rock scene and demanded that they get a voice."
