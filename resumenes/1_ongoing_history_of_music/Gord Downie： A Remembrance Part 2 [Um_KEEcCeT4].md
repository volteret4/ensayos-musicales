## curiosities
**Extensive Body of Work** : Over three decades, The Tragically Hip released 13 studio albums, one live album, one compilation, one EP, and one box set (or two if the "Fully Completely" reissue is counted). "That's 13 studio albums, one live album, one compilation, one box set, two if you count the reissue fully completely, and one EP, which is a pretty extensive body of work extending over three decades."

**Canada Post Stamp (2013)** : Canada Post issued a Tragically Hip stamp in 2013, commemorating the band's significance. "Canada Post issued a tragically obstamp in 2013."
**Honorary Degrees from Queen's University (2016)** : In 2016, the band members received honorary degrees from their alma mater, Queen's University in Kingston, where three of them had been students. "And in 2016 the hip received honorary degrees from their alma mater, Queen's University in Kingston."
**Kingston Roots** : The band members were native Kingstonians and formed in the vicinity of Queen's University, with three members being students there. They learned to perform in campus pubs and local dives, and wrote the bulk of their album "Road Apples" on a Queen's stage. "Our queens in Kingston Roots stew indeed run deep."
**Appointment to the Order of Canada (2017)** : Considered perhaps their biggest honor, The Tragically Hip was appointed to the Order of Canada in 2017. "And perhaps the biggest honor the tragically hip ever received was being appointed to the Order of Canada in 2017."
**Album Sales Success** : Every album released by The Tragically Hip sold hundreds of thousands of copies in Canada alone, with at least three selling over a million. "Meanwhile every single album they released sold hundreds of thousands of copies just in Canada. At least three sold more than a million."
**Touring Success** : The band consistently sold hundreds of thousands of tickets whenever they toured across Canada. "and whenever the band toured they sold tickets by the hundreds of thousands."
**Canadian Modesty** : Despite their significant accomplishments and good fortune, the band members were known for their modesty and did not expect to be treated like stars. "If you ever met any of them they certainly didn't expect to be treated like stars. Quite the opposite really."
**Supporting Other Bands** : The Hip frequently brought their favorite bands along to open shows, aiming for their "mojo to rub off" on their friends and fellow musicians. "The sense was that the hip really really wanted some of their mojo to rub off and some of their friends."
**Social Activism** : The band was actively involved in various social causes, including environmental protection, social justice, equality, anti-racism, disaster relief for places like Haiti, feminist causes, decolonization, and issues concerning Canada's Indigenous people. "The hip was also involved in social activism tackling issues involving the environment, social justice, equality, racism, disaster relief for places like Haiti, feminist causes, decolonization, and issues involving Canada's indigenous people."
**Album Release Cadence** : From 1987 to 2006, the band released a new album roughly every two years. This pattern shifted to once every three years until 2012, followed by a four-year gap between "Now for Plan A" and "Man-Machine Poem." "Canada got a new hip album roughly once every two years between 1987 and 2006. After that it was once every three years to 2012 and then there was a four year break between now for Plan A and the last record man machine poem."
**Never Rehearsed for Tours (Pre-2016)** : Prior to Gord Downie's final tour in 2016, The Tragically Hip had never rehearsed for a tour. The severity of Gord's condition necessitated this change for the "Man-Machine Poem" tour. "The hip had never, ever rehearsed for a tour, but this time they had to."
**"Man-Machine Poem" Tour Announcement (2016)** : The band announced the "Man-Machine Poem" tour on May 25, 2016, the day after Dr. James Perry publicly announced Gord Downie's incurable brain tumor. This announcement initially led many fans into a state of denial regarding Gord's health. "But we didn't have that much time to absorb everything because one day later, the hip announced the man-machine poem tour."
**Rapid Ticket Sell-Out (2016)** : Tickets for the "Man-Machine Poem" tour went on sale on June 3, 2016, and sold out across the country within minutes, demonstrating immense public demand. "Tickets went on sale June 3rd and sold out across the country in minutes."
**Medical Support for Final Tour** : For the "Man-Machine Poem" tour, a dedicated EMS team was hired specifically for Gord Downie, and his surgeon, Dr. Douglas DJ Cook, was present backstage as a precaution due to concerns about seizures from dehydration and sleep deprivation. "A separate EMS team was hired just for Gord to go on tour. Dr. Cook, Gord's surgeon, was in the wings."
**Gord's Subdued Performance on Final Tour** : During the "Man-Machine Poem" tour, Gord Downie's stage movements were noticeably more subdued, as he was not working at full physical capacity. His characteristic "mad, wild, jerky movements" were absent, and teleprompters were used to assist with lyrics. However, his powerful personality remained at 100%, compensating for his physical limitations. "But if you went to any of those shows, you'll have noticed that his movements on stage were much more subdued. He was not working at full power physically. The mad, wild, jerky movements where Gord seemed to be in a trans-we're absent."
**Audience Support During Final Tour** : During the "Man-Machine Poem" tour, whenever Gord Downie needed help with lyrics, thousands of audience members would sing them back to him, creating a profound collective experience. "And whenever he might have needed some extra help, there were thousands of people in the audience right there with him, ready to sing the lyrics back to him."
**National Impact of Final Tour** : The "Man-Machine Poem" tour united Canada, fostering a sense of community unlike almost any other event. It also inspired numerous cancer treatment fundraisers, many initiated by people new to charitable work. "That tour galvanized a nation, bringing us closer together than just about any other event I can think of in my lifetime."
**Final Kingston Show Viewership** : The Tragically Hip's final concert in Kingston, broadcast commercial-free on CBC on August 20, 2016, officially garnered about 12 million viewers. However, it's estimated that the true number, including those watching in communal settings like house parties and bars, was closer to 20 million out of Canada's 36 million population. "But I think that number is way too low because it did not account for all the people watching together, house parties, bars and restaurants, other public places where screens had been set up. I think the number is more like 20 million, and there's only 36 million of us."
**Post-Gord Legacy Management** : Following Gord Downie's passing, the band's legacy requires careful management, as The Hip functions as both a creative entity and a business composed of its remaining four musicians. "Beyond being a band is also a business and a business made up of four other musicians. The hip's legacy will need to be managed."
**Members' Post-Hip Projects** : Rob Baker and Gord Sinclair have been involved in musical projects with other artists, Johnny Fay is busy with country music projects, and Paul Langlois produced a record for Kingston-based country rock artist Greg Ball. "Rob Baker and Gord Sinclair have already been working on musical projects with other artists. Drama Johnny Faes busy with some country projects. Paul Langwaw produced a record for Greg Ball, a country rock guy from Kingston."

## albums
**Coke Machine Glow (2001)** : This was Gord Downie's debut solo album, released in 2001. It was described as "pretty quirky stuff" compared to The Tragically Hip's music, featured "The Goddamn Band" (which included members from Dinny Resruund, Eric's Trip, and The Skyteegers), and was compared to the work of Tom Waits due to its spoken word elements, low-fi recording, and difficult-to-categorize music. The album reached number 26 on the Canadian charts. "Koch Machine Glow has been compared to the work of Tom Waits. Lots of spoken word. Lots of low-fi recording and music that's really really hard to categorize in Pigeon Hole."
**The Battle of the Nudes (2003)** : Released in 2003 during an off-year for The Tragically Hip, this was Downie's second solo album. The backing band was again his ensemble, now known as "Country of Miracles." The music on this record was characterized as "less conceptual and heavier" than his previous solo effort. "The music on this record was less conceptual and heavier."
**The Grand Bounce (2010)** : This album, Gord Downie's third solo effort, was released in the summer of 2010, seven years after "The Battle of the Nudes." It featured the "Country of Miracles" as his backing band and was produced by Chris Walla of Death Cab for Cutie. "The Country of Miracles was back again for the grand bounts and the producer was Chris Walla of Death Cab for Cutie."
**Secret Path (2016)** : Released on October 18, 2016, this project encompassed an album, a film, and a graphic novel. Its genesis stemmed from Gord's brother Mike reading the story of Chanie Wenjack in McLean's magazine, leading to Gord's deep involvement in Indigenous issues and reconciliation efforts. "This became Gord's secret path, the album, the film, and the graphic novel."
**Introduce Yourself (2017)** : This double album was a surprise announcement on September 28, 2017, with a release date of October 27, 2017. Although the sessions for the album had been completed in February of that year, its unexpected announcement led to speculation that Gord's cancer treatments might be working. "There would be a new solo album entitled Introduce Yourself on October 27th. Surely this meant that the chemo and the radiation and all the other treatments were working. Gord's well enough to make a double album."
**Unspecified Final Solo Album (2017)** : This album, mentioned after Gord's death, featured 23 songs, each dedicated to a specific person. One song was directed at his brother Pat, a fellow Boston Bruins fan, reflecting Gord's lifelong passion for hockey. "A final solo album. 23 songs, each dedicated to a specific person."

## songs
**Nothing But Heartache In Your Social Life (2001)** : This track from "Coke Machine Glow" exemplifies the album's quirky style, incorporating spoken word elements and a low-fi recording aesthetic. It was co-written by Canadian film director Adam Goyin. "This is nothing but heartache in your social life. What are you thinking of disappearing? What are you falling off the map? What are you unknown that your feelings in the clearing? When your world's gone flat?"
**Pascale's Submarine (2003)** : Featured on Gord Downie's second album, "The Battle of the Nudes," this song is considered a fan favorite from the record. Notably, Gord Downie himself played the drums on this track. "If you had to pick a fan favorite from this album, it's probably this one. It's called Pasco Submarine. That's Gord in the drums by the way."
**The East Wind (2010)** : A song from "The Grand Bounce," this track opens with a poignant meteorological observation: "An East Wind is the laziest wind. It doesn't go around you. It goes through you." This line, almost verbatim, was shared with Downie by a farmer and incorporated into the song, which also contains quotes from Al Purdy and Walter von Tilburg Clark. "An East Wind is the laziest wind. It doesn't go around you. It goes through you. And that's absolutely true if you live in the Great Lakes, isn't it?"
**Son (2016)** : This song is from the "Secret Path" album, which was released on October 18, 2016, precisely 364 days before Gord Downie's passing. "Gord Downey and Son from Secret Path, which was released on October 18th, 2016, 364 days before Gord would finally pass away."
**Unspecified Song for Pat Downie (2017)** : Included on his final solo album, this song is dedicated to his brother Pat, with whom Gord shared a passion for the Boston Bruins. Gord's connection to the Bruins was significant, as their coach and GM, Harry Sinden, was his godfather. "It's directed at his brother Pat, a fellow Boston Bruin fan. Knowing that Gord was brought into this world with Bruin's coach and GM Harry Sinden as his godfather, and that he was a lifelong hockey fan and player, I just think it's kind of nice to go out."

## curiosities
**"Cactus Relationship" with Band** : Gord Downie described his relationship with his bandmates as a "cactus relationship," implying it didn't require much maintenance or "TLC" and thrived in challenging conditions. He believed this approach, with fewer expectations of each other, was healthy for longevity. "Well we have what I like to call a cactus relationship. It doesn't require a lot of water or TLC or anything. And we thrive in extremely dry environments and that's how I think how we do it."
**"11" Signal for Nephew** : While preparing for a Saturday Night Live performance, Gord promised his 11-year-old nephew a signal: holding up two index fingers to represent the number 11. This promise consumed his thoughts just before going on stage. "He said, Uncle Gord, can you give me a little signal? And I said, okay, I'll tell you what, I'm going to put my two index fingers up in front of me. You're turning 11 because you're turning 11."
**Resonance of "Tragically Hip" Lyric** : During a Saturday Night Live performance of "Grace, Too," Gord Downie became overwhelmed by the line "I'm tragically hip," which resonated so deeply with him that his "inner elevator went from the penthouse to the basement," causing him to forget the subsequent lyrics. "He said I'm tragically hip and it was like, because I just resonated with him and my inner elevator went from the penthouse to the basement. I'd break next speed and I thought I don't even know the next line."
**Experimentation in Vocal Style** : Over the course of The Tragically Hip's career, Gord Downie increasingly experimented with different vocal styles and phrasing, contributing to the band's evolving sound. "Gord started experimenting with different vocal styles and phrasing."
**Canadian Thematic References** : The longer The Tragically Hip stayed together, the more their music incorporated references to Canadian people, events, and places, making their sound increasingly distinctively Canadian. "the longer the band stayed together the more Canadian their music seemed to be with more and more references to people and events and places."
**Private and Quiet Demeanor** : Off stage, Gord Downie remained a quiet and private individual who preferred not to be the center of attention. "Gord remained a quiet private guy who did not want to be the center of attention off stage."
**Anonymity on the Rink** : To enjoy anonymity while playing hockey with friends at an outdoor rink in the east end of Toronto, Gord Downie would dress in his full goalie gear, including his mask, at home before walking to the park. He cherished playing at the "loneliest end of the rink." "Gord would dress in his goalie gear at home including his mask and then walk over to the park, total anonymity. Gord loved it playing there at the loneliest end of the rink."
**Solo Career Motivation** : Despite The Tragically Hip's immense success, Gord Downie felt a creative need to pursue projects outside the band, leading to his solo career. "The hip was a great success but creatively this wasn't enough for Gord. He had things he wanted to do outside the band."
**Philanthropic Work** : When not engaged in music, Gord Downie dedicated himself to philanthropic efforts. He served as a board member for Lake Ontario Waterkeeper, an organization focused on protecting the lake and its watershed. "For example, Gordown was a board member of Lake Ontario Waterkeeper, a group devoted to protecting the lake and its watershed."
**Indigenous Advocacy** : As he aged, Gord Downie deepened his involvement and concern for Canada's Indigenous people, which culminated in the establishment of the Chanie Wenjack Fund, dedicated to reconciliation between First Nations and other Canadians. "And the older he got, the deeper he got into the affairs and concerns of Canada's Indigenous people. This culminated with the establishment of the Cheney-Wenjack fun, a project dedicated to the supported reconciliation between the First Nations of Canada and the rest of us."
**Chanie Wenjack Story as Genesis** : The story of Chanie Wenjack, who died trying to walk home from a residential school, was recounted to Gord by his brother Mike after Mike read it in McLean's magazine. This story became the foundational inspiration for Gord's "Secret Path" project. "Mike Downey, Gord's brother, ran across the story in McLean's magazine and told Gord all about it. And that was the genesis of everything."
**Brain Cancer Diagnosis (2015)** : In December 2015, Gord Downie experienced his first seizure on a Kingston street, leading to a diagnosis of glioblastoma, an aggressive and incurable form of brain cancer found in his left temporal lobe. "Sometime at December 2015, Gord was on the street in Kingston when the first seizure came on. Something was very wrong. A trip to the doctor resulted in the worst possible news. He had a form of brain cancer called glioblastoma."
**Treatment Choice** : Faced with two grim treatment options—longer life with poor quality of life or a shorter life with better quality of life—Gord chose the latter, prioritizing the ability to accomplish his remaining goals. "Or you can choose to have a shorter time left, but your quality of life will be better during that time. Gord went with option B. That would give him the best chance to accomplish everything he wanted to do in the time he had."
**Surgery and Post-Surgical Prognosis (2016)** : In early 2016, surgeon Douglas DJ Cook at Kingston General Hospital performed a full temporal lobectomy, removing Gord's left temporal lobe, hippocampus, and additional tissue. The best-case scenario after surgery was 4-5 years without further neurological decline. "His surgeon was Douglas DJ Cook at Kingston General Hospital. Dr. Cook went in and removed Gord's left temporal lobe. This is called a full temporal abectomy."
**Radiation and Chemotherapy (2016)** : Following surgery, Gord underwent 30 rounds of radiation treatment over six weeks, twice a day, five days a week. This was supplemented by an oral chemotherapy drug called Tilosilamide. His brother Pat accompanied him, playing music, including The Bee Gees, during treatments. "After the surgery came 30 rounds of radiation treatment. Twice a day, five days a week for six weeks. He would go in for treatment with his brother Pat who would play music for Gord."
**Post-Treatment Cognitive Decline** : The cancer and surgery caused significant damage, leading to memory loss (he couldn't remember his children's names), difficulty forming sentences, and general confusion. He resorted to writing reminders on his hands. "Memory loss set in. He couldn't even remember the names of his children. Gord took to writing reminders on his hands. He couldn't form any sentences. There was lots of confusion."
**Will to Tour** : Despite his severe health challenges and cognitive decline, Gord's incredible will drove him to insist that The Tragically Hip rehearse and tour in spring 2016, even though he couldn't remember song titles, albums, or lyrics. "Gord's will had to have been incredible because he recovered enough through the spring of 2016 to insist that the hip get back together and start rehearsing."
**Brain Reboot for Tour** : To prepare for the final tour, Gord basically "rebooted his brain" through intense repetition of 90 songs, aided by written notes and lyrics displayed on a screen. Musical memories, stored in a separate part of the brain, were believed to assist his recovery. "But he powered through, basically rebooting his brain, repetition, repetition, repetition, aided by written notes and lyrics on a screen over and over and over again. 90 songs for the upcoming tour."
**Tour as Therapy** : His band, family, and organization indulged Gord's desire to tour, seeing it as a form of therapy to keep him engaged and happy, despite low expectations that he would actually be able to complete it. "If working towards a tour helps him, then let's use that at the very least as therapy to keep him engaged and happy."
**Incurable Tumor Announcement (2016)** : On May 24, 2016, Dr. James Perry of Sunnybrook Hospital publicly announced that Gord Downie's brain tumor was incurable, news that profoundly shocked Canada. "Good morning. My name is James Perry and I'm the head of the Division of Neurology here at Sunnybrook Hospital. I want to thank you for your attention and your concern in light of the difficult news that you all received this morning. It's my difficult duty today to tell you that Gord Downey's spring tumour is incurable."
**Miraculous Tour Completion** : Against all expectations, Gord Downie completed all scheduled shows of the "Man-Machine Poem" tour, and additional dates were even added due to high demand, surprising everyone involved. "But Gord surprised everyone. Not only did he make it through all the scheduled shows unscathed, new shows were added to a comedy demand."
**"Secret Path" Performances (2016)** : Following the "Man-Machine Poem" tour, Gord performed a few shows for his "Secret Path" project in Toronto, Ottawa, and Halifax. "After a couple of shows for Secret Path and Toronto Ottawa and Halifax, Gord remained largely out of sight."
**Move to Kingston and Home Sale** : After the "Secret Path" shows, Gord largely stayed out of the public eye. His home in the Beaches area of Toronto was sold, and he spent most of his remaining time in Kingston. "His home in the beaches area of Toronto had been sold, and he spent most of his time in Kingston."
**Lakota Spirit Name and Eagle Feather (2016)** : At an Assembly of First Nations gathering in Gatineau in early December 2016, Gord Downie was presented with an eagle feather and given the Lakota spirit name "Omani," meaning "the man who walks among the stars." "He did appear at a gathering of the Assembly of First Nations and Gattanoqua back at early December 2016, where he was presented with an eagle feather, and given a Lakota spirit name, a copy of Omani, the man who walks among the stars."
**Final Public Performance (2017)** : On February 2, 2017, Gord Downie made his last public singing appearance at Massey Hall in Toronto, joining Blue Rodeo and opening act The Sadies on stage to perform "Lost Together." "And that would be the last time Gord ever sang in public."
**Order of Canada for Indigenous Leadership (2017)** : In June 2017, Gord Downie was invested into the Order of Canada, not for his music (as The Hip already held that honor), but specifically for his leadership and advocacy on Indigenous issues. "In June 2017, Gord appeared in Ottawa for investment into the Order of Canada. Not for music, though, because the hip already had an order for that, but for his leadership with Indigenous issues."
**Powerful We Day Address (2017)** : On July 2, 2017, Gord appeared with Pearl and Daisy Wenjack at We Day in Ottawa, where he delivered a powerful address emphasizing the need for reconciliation and acknowledging the historical injustices of residential schools. "And on July 2nd, Gord appeared in Ottawa with Pearl and Daisy Wengek for We Day, where he gave a powerful address for the need for reconciliation."
**Declining Health Rumors (2017)** : Throughout the summer and fall of 2017, rumors of Gord's declining health circulated, though no official confirmations were made. "Yet there were rumors of declining health through the summer and the fall of 2017. No one would confirm anything, of course."
**Log Cabin Plan with Brother Mike** : There was a story that Gord and his brother Mike planned to build a log cabin adjacent to Chanie Wenjack's family home, where Gord intended to spend his final days when his illness progressed significantly. "There was a story that Gord and his brother Mike planned to build a log cabin next door to Cheney Windjaxe family, a place where when he got really, really sick, Gord would live out his final days."
**Death (2017)** : Gord Downie passed away on the night of October 17, 2017, at the age of 53, surrounded by his family, friends, and bandmates. His death plunged Canada into widespread mourning. "Until the morning of October 18th, 2017, when we heard that Gord had died the previous night surrounded by family, friends, and bandmates. He was 53."
**Family Members** : Gord Downie was survived by his four children, his brothers Mike and Pat, and his former partner, Laura-Lee Usher. "Gord left behind four children. His brother's mic had pat. His former partner, Laura Lee Usher, and his four bandmates."
**Private Memorial** : A very private memorial for family and friends was held on October 20, 2017, following Gord's death. "Meanwhile, there was a very, very private memorial for family and friends on October 20th."
**Harry Sinden as Godfather** : Gord Downie's godfather was Harry Sinden, who was the coach and general manager of the Boston Bruins, reflecting Gord's lifelong passion for hockey. "Knowing that Gord was brought into this world with Bruin's coach and GM Harry Sinden as his godfather, and that he was a lifelong hockey fan and player."
**Enduring Legacy in Reconciliation** : Gord Downie's name is expected to be associated with reconciliation efforts for a significant period. His work to foster understanding between Indigenous peoples and other Canadians created a momentum that many hope will continue to grow. "Gord's name will be associated with reconciliation efforts for a long, long time."

# concert - Man-Machine Poem Tour

## curiosities
**Response to Cancer Diagnosis** : This tour was announced on May 25, 2016, the day after Gord Downie's incurable brain tumor was publicly announced, leading to a period of denial for fans. Tickets sold out across Canada in minutes on June 3rd. "one day later, the hip announced the man-machine poem tour. Now, from our perspective, the news was dire, but hey, it couldn't be that bad yet, right?"
**Start Date** : The tour was set to begin in Victoria on July 22, 2016. "Everything was set to start in Victoria on July 22nd."
**Medical Precautions** : Due to Gord Downie's condition, a dedicated EMS team was hired for the tour, and his surgeon, Dr. Douglas DJ Cook, was present backstage to monitor for potential seizures. "A separate EMS team was hired just for Gord to go on tour. Dr. Cook, Gord's surgeon, was in the wings."
**Gord's Performance** : Despite severe health challenges, Gord surprised everyone by completing all scheduled shows and even adding new ones. However, his stage movements were subdued, and teleprompters were used for lyrics, though his powerful personality remained intact. "But if you went to any of those shows, you'll have noticed that his movements on stage were much more subdued. He was not working at full power physically."
**National Galvanization** : The tour became a profoundly unifying event for Canada, inspiring countless cancer treatment fundraisers and fostering a widespread sense of community. "That tour galvanized a nation, bringing us closer together than just about any other event I can think of in my lifetime."
**Final Kingston Show (2016)** : The tour concluded with a final show in Kingston on Saturday, August 20, 2016. This concert was broadcast commercial-free on CBC and attracted an official audience of approximately 12 million viewers, with an estimated total viewership closer to 20 million nationwide. "That final show in Kingston, shown commercial free on the CBC on Saturday, August 20th, officially attracted about 12 million viewers."

# curiosity

**Chanie Wenjack's Story** : Chanie Wenjack was a young Indigenous boy caught in the residential school system. He was sent to a residential school in Kenora, Ontario, from which he ran away. He tragically attempted to walk 600 kilometers back home but died of exposure alongside a railway track, a story that profoundly inspired Gord Downie's "Secret Path" project and the Chanie Wenjack Fund. "Cheney-Wenjack was caught up in the whole residential school's travesty. He was sent to Canora, Ontario, but he ran away. And he tried to walk the 600 kilometers home, but he'd never made it, dying of exposure alongside a railway track."
**Glioblastoma Brain Cancer** : Glioblastoma is a very aggressive form of brain cancer that typically forms in the cerebral hemispheres, with no known cause or cure. Gord Downie's case was primary glioblastoma, characterized by rapidly forming tumors that cause quick symptoms from within the brain itself, first found in his left temporal lobe. The tumors are invasive and infiltrative, making complete surgical removal impossible and leading to recurrence. "He had a form of brain cancer called glioblastoma. It usually forms in the cerebral hemispheres and is very, very aggressive. There was no known cause and no cure."
**"The Ox-Bow Incident" Quotes** : Gord Downie's song "The East Wind" incorporates "found objects quotes" from various sources, including Walter von Tilburg Clark, who wrote "The Ox-Bow Incident." A specific quote, "night is like a room. It makes the little things inside your head too important," appears in the song and is attributed to Clark, whose 1943 novel was adapted into a film by William Wellman. "So the second verse is night is like a room. It makes the little things inside your head too important. That's Walter von Tilburg Clark who wrote The Oxbow incident."
**Le Cold Polytechnique Massacre (December 6, 1989)** : This horrific event in Montreal saw Marc Lépine enter Le Cold Polytechnique with a rifle and hunting knife, shooting 27 people and killing 14 women. This tragedy served as the subject for The Tragically Hip's unreleased song "Montreal." "The track's called Montreal and it's about the events of December 6, 1989 when Marc Le Pen walked into Le Cold Polytechnique in Montreal with a rifle and a hunting knife shooting 27 people and killing 14 women."
**Canada's Reaction to Gord's Diagnosis** : The news of Gord Downie's incurable brain tumor, announced on May 24, 2016, had a profound impact across Canada, becoming the lead story everywhere and hitting the nation "like a bomb." "This news hit Canada like a bomb. Lead story everywhere."
**National Mourning for Gord Downie** : Upon Gord Downie's death on October 18, 2017, the entire country plunged into mourning. Flags were lowered, lights dimmed, moments of silence observed, and numerous tributes and fundraisers for cancer treatment and Indigenous causes were held. The sentiment was captured by a tweet that simply read: "Canada closed, death in the family." "The whole country plunged into mourning. The best tweet I saw was Canada closed, death in the family. The Prime Minister appeared on TV and tears. Flags were lowered, lights were dimmed, moments of silence were held."
**Dr. Seuss Quote** : A quote from Dr. Seuss, "Don't cry because it's over. Smile because it happened," was used as a fitting closing sentiment for a program dedicated to The Tragically Hip after Gord Downie's passing, encapsulating the appreciation for his life and work. "It goes like this. Don't cry because it's over. Smile because it happened."
