**Gasconi – Andy Fletcher's London Italian Restaurant** : Andy Fletcher of Depeche Mode, along with his wife Grania, opened an Italian restaurant called Gasconi in the mid-1990s in the St. John's Wood area of London, near Abbey Road recording studios and Violet Hill. ## songs
**Far Out (Year not specified)** : This song from Blur's Parklife album features bassist Alex James singing a rapid list of stars and moons for about 90 seconds. While it might sound like gibberish to some, it is actually a scientific recitation related to space. "Now, to the uninitiated, it sounds like a bunch of nonsense, but it's actually Alex singing a list of stars and moons for about 90 seconds."
**Beagle II Signature Tune (Year not specified)** : Blur specifically wrote and recorded a song to be played by the Beagle II probe upon its successful landing on Mars. Had the mission been successful, this would have made Blur the first band to have music played on Mars. However, the probe was lost, and the song was never broadcast from the Martian surface. "When it phoned home, it was going to play a song, and that song was written specifically for Beagle II by Blur."

## curiosities
**Alex James's Membership in British Astronomical Association** : Alex James, the bass player for Blur, has been a long-standing member of the British Astronomical Association for many years, indicating his deep interest in space and astronomy. "He's been a member of the British Astronomical Association for years."
**Alex James's Consultation on European Space Agency's Mars Express Mission (Beagle II)** : Alex James consulted on the construction of Beagle II, the British component of the European Space Agency's Mars Express Mission. The probe was launched from Kazakhstan on June 2nd, 2003, with an intended landing on Mars around Christmas Day of the same year. It was supposed to play a song written by Blur upon landing, but the mission failed, and the probe was declared lost. "Alex asked, and he was allowed to consult on the craft's construction."
**Alex James's Astrophysics Studies and Residency at Oxford** : During Blur's extended hiatus, Alex James dedicated two years to studying astrophysics at the University of Bath, where he earned an honors degree. Following this, he was appointed artist in residence in the astrophysics department at the University of Oxford. "During Blur's lengthy and maybe permanent break, Alex spent two years at the University of Bath studying astrophysics."
**Alex James's High-End British Cheese Making** : Alex James runs a farm in the Cotswolds where he produces high-end British cheese. He also contributes a column on cheese making for a major London newspaper and hosts tasting events for the public. "He's got this farm and the cut swalls. He makes some very high-end British cheese."
