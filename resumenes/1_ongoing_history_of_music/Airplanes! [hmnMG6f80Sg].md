**British Airways Flight Incident (1998)** : On February 13, 1998, not long after releasing his first solo album and while estranged from The Stone Roses, Ian Brown boarded British Airways Flight 1611 from Charles de Gaulle Airport to Manchester after an MTV event in Paris. Once airborne, he allegedly got into a row with a flight attendant, threatening to "cut your hands off," and then caused a disturbance by banging on the cockpit door and swearing at the crew during the plane's descent. Upon landing, he was arrested and charged with endangering the safety of an aircraft in flight. "Officially, Ian got into a row with a flight attendant over something, allegedly saying, I'm going to cut your hands off."
**Arrest and Imprisonment (1998)** : Following the British Airways incident, Ian Brown was arrested and, despite his testimony, a judge with alleged ties to the airline industry rejected his explanations and sentenced him to a four-month term in Strangeways prison in Manchester. His appeal, arguing that his UK tour would be canceled and over 100 people would lose work, was denied, with the judge aiming to "set an example." He was ultimately released unharmed before Christmas of that year. "The message to the public, he said, must be that anyone who behaves in this way will lose their liberty."
**Lifetime Flight Ban (1998)** : As a consequence of his actions and conviction, Ian Brown was permanently banned from flying with British Airways. "Fortunately, he was honed unharmed for Christmas of that year, but unfortunately, he'll never be permitted to fly British airways again."

# label - Pan Am

## curiosities
**Flight 103 Bombing (1988)** : Pan Am Flight 103, a 747 en route from London to New York, was bombed over Lockerbie, Scotland, on December 21, 1988, in one of the worst incidents of airline terrorism. A bomb, secreted inside a cassette player by a Libyan agent, detonated at 31,000 feet and 500 miles per hour, disintegrating the plane and killing all 243 passengers, 16 crew members, and 11 people on the ground. "One of the worst incidents of airline terrorism was the bombing of Pan Am Flight 103 over Lockerby Scotland on December 21, 1988."
