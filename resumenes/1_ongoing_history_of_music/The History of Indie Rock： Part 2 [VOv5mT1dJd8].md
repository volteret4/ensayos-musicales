# artist - Buzzcocks

## members
Pete Shelley
Howard Devoto

## labels
New Hormones

## genres
Punk

## concerts
Lesser Free Trade Hall, Manchester (arranged)

## albums
**Spiral Scratch (1977)** : This EP was the first punk record to be self-released, entirely by the Buzzcocks themselves, without any outside help. Released on January 29, 1977, it cost them 500 pounds to record and release it on their self-established label, New Hormones. It was a revelation to young musicians, proving that anyone could release a record on their own. "On January 29, 1977, they released an EP called Spiral Scratch. This was the first punk record to be self-released that did everything by themselves with no outside help."

## songs
**Boredom (1977)** : This track was the hit from the landmark indie release, the "Spiral Scratch" EP, which was self-released by the band on their New Hormones label in 1977. "The Buzzcocks and Boredom from their landmark indie release Spiral Scratch, an EP from 1977."

## curiosities
**Formation and Early Influences** : The band was formed in Manchester in 1976 by Pete Shelley and Howard Devoto, who were both fans of The Velvet Underground and becoming interested in punk music. "The Buzzcocks were formed in Manchester in 1976 by two guys, Pete Shelley and Howard Devoto. Both were big velvet underground fans and had started paying more attention to this thing that people were calling punk."
**Sex Pistols Manchester Show** : The Buzzcocks arranged for a still-unknown band, The Sex Pistols, to play the Lesser Free Trade Hall in Manchester on June 4th, 1976. This gig became mythical and legendary, inspiring about 40 attendees to form bands like Joy Division, Pet Shop Boys, Simply Red, and The Smiths, and to establish Factory Records. "They arranged for a still unknown band called The Sex Pistols to play the lesser free trade hall in Manchester on June 4th, 1976, a show that has gone down in myth and legend as one of the most important gigs in the history of rock."
**Establishing New Hormones Label** : Energized by the Sex Pistols show and facing a music scene where no labels were interested in releasing their kind of music, the Buzzcocks established their own label, New Hormones. This act demonstrated that inexperienced musicians from a "musical backwater" like Manchester could successfully release their own records. "But with the scene being so new, there were no labels interested in releasing this kind of music, so they established their own label."

# genre - Hardcore Punk

## curiosities
**Anti-Establishment Origins** : Hardcore punk emerged from the fast, hard, radical, and angry end of the punk rock spectrum, with its roots in late 1970s California. It was both anti-establishment and anti-hippie, deliberately anti-commercial, and positioned itself against what its proponents considered to be the corrupt music industry. "Hardcore comes from the fast, hard, radical, and angry end of the punk rock spectrum. Its roots can be found in the late 1970s in California."
**Impetus for Independent Labels** : Due to its radical and anti-commercial nature, existing record labels had no interest in working with hardcore acts. This forced these acts to start their own labels, such as SST, Epitaph, and Alternative Tentacles, becoming a crucial factor in the establishment of the indie music universe in the early 1980s. "Alright, so if this is the way it was going to be, there was no chance that the existing record labels were going to work with any hardcore acts. So the only thing these acts could do was start their own labels."
**Short, Sharp, and Fast Message** : Hardcore was characterized by its brevity and intensity, delivering messages in a short, sharp, and fast manner. "Hardcore was short, sharp, and fast. If that's all you needed to get out your message, well, so be it."

# label - Factory Records

## curiosities
**Founded by Tony Wilson** : This label was founded by Manchester TV host Tony Wilson, who was inspired after attending the Sex Pistols show in 1976. The name "Factory Records" was derived from a local nightclub night. "He called the company Factory Records after a local nightclub night."
**First Release** : Factory Records' inaugural release occurred on December 24, 1978. It consisted of two 7-inch singles bundled together, featuring samples from four bands on its roster, with Side A including two songs by Joy Division, notably "Digital." "The very first factory release was issued on December 24, 1978. Two 7-inch singles bundled together featuring samples from four bands in the roster."
**Influential British Indie Label** : Factory Records is recognized as one of the great British indie labels of all time, serving as the source for influential music from bands such as Joy Division, New Order, Happy Mondays, and Cabaret Voltaire. "Factory went on to become one of the great British indie labels of all time. So much music flowed from this company."
**Aesthetic Focus and Hacienda Nightclub** : The label was known for its strong aesthetic, meticulous attention to album artwork and packaging, and a quirky cataloging system. It also established the Hacienda nightclub, which became a central hub for British dance culture throughout the 1980s. "And with Factory, it was more than just about the music. It was all about the aesthetic, the care given to album artwork and packaging, the quirkiness of their cataloging system, the establishment of the Hasey end nightclub, which became ground zero for so much of British dance culture throughout the 1980s."
**Business Challenges and Bankruptcy** : Despite its musical influence, Factory Records faced significant business challenges, including never asking bands to sign formal contracts, dubious accounting practices, and a consistent lack of attention to cash flow. As a result, the label eventually went spectacularly bankrupt. "As a business, Factory was a basket case, and eventually they went broke in a spectacular way."

# label - Alternative Tentacles

## curiosities
**Dead Kennedys' DIY Label** : Alternative Tentacles, a California-based label, was formed by the Dead Kennedys after they initially just wanted to release one single. They found the experience enjoyable enough to transform it into a full-time business, mirroring the strategies of Black Flag with SST and Bad Religion with Epitaph. "Alternative Tentacles, another California label formed by a band who couldn't find anyone else to believe in them. This was the label of the dead Kennedys."
**Global Export of Hardcore** : This label distinguished itself by developing relationships with other independent labels around the world, enabling the export of American hardcore music to new international markets. "Because Alternative Tentacles managed to develop a relationship with other labels around the world, other indie labels, they were able to export American hardcore to places it had never been."
**First Release: California Über Alles** : The label's first release was the Dead Kennedys' self-produced single, "California Über Alles," in 1979. "This is the first Alternative Tentacles release, as the dead Kennedys, with their self-produced single, California Uber Alice."

# concert - Lesser Free Trade Hall, Manchester (June 4th, 1976)

## curiosities
**Legendary Sex Pistols Performance** : On June 4th, 1976, a still-unknown band called The Sex Pistols performed at the Lesser Free Trade Hall in Manchester, a show arranged by the Buzzcocks. Despite an audience of only around 40 people, this gig has achieved mythic and legendary status as one of the most important in rock history. "They arranged for a still unknown band called The Sex Pistols to play the lesser free trade hall in Manchester on June 4th, 1976, a show that has gone down in myth and legend as one of the most important gigs in the history of rock."
**Catalyst for New Bands and Labels** : The attendees of this sparsely attended concert were profoundly influenced, leading many to form highly influential bands like Joy Division, the Pet Shop Boys, Simply Red, and The Smiths. It also inspired the establishment of significant labels such as Factory Records. "Only 40 some people were there, but they left to form bands like George Division and the Pet Shop Boys and simply read and the Smiths and to establish factory records."

# curiosity

**Rise of Indie Labels** : Beyond the three major record labels (Universal, Sony, and Warner), there are hundreds, even thousands, of independent labels that operate autonomously. These "indie labels" and their "indie artists" are considered unaffiliated with the big three, representing the underground, non-mainstream, experimental, daring, outlier, unusual, and non-conformist segments of the music industry. "But beyond those three companies are hundreds, thousands of labels that do their own thing. And because they are unaffiliated with the big three, we consider them to be independent."
**DIY Ethic as Inspiration** : The radical act of two individuals in Manchester starting their own record label, as seen with the Buzzcocks and New Hormones, served as a massive inspiration for countless others determined to get underground music heard. In the four years following this event, over 900 independent records were released in Britain using a similar self-release model. "This was a massive inspiration for other people who were determined to get this underground music heard. In the next four years, more than 900 indie records were released in Britain this way."
**Post-Punk Segmentation** : In the early and mid-1980s, while major labels still controlled most of the music market, the market share for independent music steadily increased. As post-punk music continued to fragment and segment into an expanding array of styles, the independent music scene became increasingly interesting and diverse. "But started in the punk years just kept gaining in momentum. Let's stay in the UK for a bit. The left of center slash underground stuff that was being released by the indie labels kept on coming. And as post punk music continued to separate and segment into an ever growing number of streams, things kept getting more and more interesting."
**C86 Cassette Compilation** : In 1986, the NME (New Musical Express) resurrected the idea of offering a cassette with one of its issues, naming it C86 (a play on the year and cassette length labels like C60, C90, C120). This collection featured 22 bands from various UK indie labels, offering power pop and jangly guitar-based songs that reflected the contemporary state of new music in Britain. "They called it C86, a play on the year, 1986, and how cassette lengths were labeled. There was C60, C90, C120. That was based on the number of minutes that could be recorded on both sides."
**C86 as a Shorthand Term** : The C86 cassette proved so successful that its title became a shorthand term to describe a specific sound and attitude within a certain type of music. It showed independent musicians across the UK that they were not alone and that there was significant interest in their work, leading to an explosion of new bands, though most did not achieve widespread success. "It was so successful that C86 became a shorthand term to describe a certain sound and attitude when it came to a certain type of music."
**Ronald Reagan's Impact on Hardcore** : Ronald Reagan's presidency, characterized by deeply conservative and hawkish Republican policies, was a significant catalyst for the growth of independent music in North America. His policies were unpopular with many young people, leading to the manifestation of hardcore punk as an anti-establishment and anti-commercial response. "If we're going to be honest in telling this story, we have to give a substantial amount of credit to Ronald Reagan. You heard me. Reagan was a deeply conservative and hawkish Republican president and his policies did not sit well with a lot of young people."
**Hardcore as Foundation for Indie Labels** : Thanks to the political climate under Ronald Reagan, hardcore music was born and created a demand for a new generation of independent record labels. These labels, initially focused on hardcore, quickly expanded their scope far beyond their original genre. "From line to all this hardcore history is that thanks to Ronald Reagan, hardcore was born and helped create the need, desire, and demand for a new generation of independent record labels that would quickly extend far beyond what these labels were all about."
