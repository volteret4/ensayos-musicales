# artist - Ramones

## members
Dee Dee Ramone

## instruments
Bass guitar

## curiosities
**Dee Dee Ramone – Self-Taught "Primitive" Bassist and Primary Lyricist** : Dee Dee Ramone came to the bass without prior knowledge of how to play it, embodying the punk ethos of "anyone can do it." Despite his basic technique, he became the Ramones' primary lyricist and a massive influence on a generation of musicians. His primitive approach was not a limitation but a defining feature. "A 'primitive' bassist who, without initially knowing how to play, embodied the punk spirit of 'anyone can do it' and was the band's main lyricist."

## albums
**London Calling (1979) – Cover Image of Simonon Smashing His Bass** : The cover photograph of Paul Simonon destroying his bass guitar on stage has become one of rock's most iconic images, representing punk's confrontational energy. "His image smashing the bass on the cover of 'London Calling' is iconic."

## songs
**Guns of Brixton** : Written entirely by Paul Simonon, this song integrated reggae and ska influences into the punk context — reflecting Simonon's Jamaican musical influences — and is one of The Clash's most celebrated tracks. "He wrote 'Guns of Brixton' and integrated sounds of reggae and ska into punk."

## curiosities
**Paul Simonon – Self-Taught After Failed Attempt at Guitar, Integrated Reggae into Punk** : Paul Simonon taught himself bass after an unsuccessful initial attempt at guitar. He brought Jamaican reggae and ska sounds into The Clash's punk context, fundamentally expanding what punk bass could sound like. "Taught himself bass after trying guitar and integrated reggae and ska sounds into punk."


# artist - Joy Division

## members
Peter Hook

## member of
New Order

## instruments
Bass guitar

## curiosities
**Peter Hook – Invented High-Register Bass Style Out of Necessity** : Peter Hook developed his distinctive style of playing bass high up the neck — in registers typically occupied by lead guitar — out of the practical need to be heard in Joy Division's dense sound. This approach became a defining characteristic of post-punk and new wave bass playing and proved highly influential. "His unique style arose from the need to be heard, playing the bass in the highest notes of the neck."
