# curiosity

**Musical Tribalism of 70s-90s Rock** : During the 1970s, 80s, and well into the 90s, rock music was characterized by rigid tribal divisions between "mainstream rockers" and "alternative kids." Fans were expected to conform to strict musical ideologies, and admitting to liking music from the "other side," such as Led Zeppelin, Black Sabbath, or Van Halen for alt-rockers, was considered heresy, risking exile from one's tribe. "Once upon a time, music was incredibly tribal. Once you picked a tribe, you had to conform to serious, and rigid rules."
**"Cold War" Metaphor in Music** : The division between mainstream rock and alternative rock from the 70s to 90s was akin to a "cold war" of musical ideology, outlook, and aspirations. Crossing over was compared to taking a stroll across the DMZ between North and South Korea, highlighting the extreme rigidity of musical allegiances. "During this period, these two tribes were actually locked in a cold war. It was a war of musical ideology, musical outlook, and musical aspirations, and woe to any tribe member who tried to change tribes."
**Evolution of Musical Preferences Today** : The rigid tribal rules in music have largely broken down in the modern era, leading to a more "ecumenical" approach where the prevailing philosophy is to respect all music and listen to whatever one wants. This openness is seen as healthy for expanding musical vistas and influences. "Today, though, those rigid tribal rules have broken down. If you entered an era where we're very ecumenical about music, more and more the prevailing philosophy is luck. Respect all music, just listen to what you want."
**Musicians' Broad Musical Palettes** : Musicians, as professionals in their field, tend to possess a wider and broader range of musical tastes than the general public. They are constantly seeking new ideas, influences, and inspiration, often listening to a diverse array of genres beyond their own specific sounds. "Musicians tend to have a bigger and broader palette of musical tastes to draw upon. Music is their business, their livelihood. They're constantly looking for new ideas and new influences and new inspiration."
