# genre - Hardcore Punk

## curiosities
**Definition and Characteristics** : Hardcore punk is defined as a subgenre of punk rock, characterized by its creators' preference for faster and heavier music than original punk bands, used to express grievances against societal norms, capitalist exploitation, corrupt politicians, and mainstream music industry figures. It prioritizes playing harder, faster, and louder over technique or melody, with shouting and screaming being more important. "Hardcore punk or just hardcore, if you prefer, is a subgenre, obviously, of punk rock. Its creators love the faster and heavier end of the original punk rock bands, but they also had, well, they had some grievances that they needed to express."
**Geographical Origins** : Hardcore punk first took root in the late 1970s on the East and West Coasts of North America, specifically in Vancouver, San Francisco, Southern California, Washington, D.C., Boston, and New York City. It also had scenes in the UK and later spread to Italy and Japan. "Hardcore first took root on the East and West Coast of North America in the late 1970s, specifically in Vancouver, San Francisco, Southern California, Washington, D.C., Boston and New York City."
**Stripped-Down Sound** : The genre in Southern California, emerging around 1979, was even more stripped-down than original punk, often featuring only guitar, bass, drums, and a vocalist. Traditional verse-chorus song structures were often foregone, with songs sometimes lasting 32 seconds or less. "The traditional first-core's first song structure wasn't necessary. In fact, if everything could be said in 32 seconds or less, so be it."
**New York Hardcore Chug** : In the New York City hardcore scene, guitars were often played by muting chords with the entire palm, a technique informally known as the "New York Hardcore chug." "Guitars tended to play by muting chords with the whole palm. Some people called this the New York Hardcore chug."
**International Spread** : Hardcore punk caught on globally during the 1980s, driven by relentless touring of bands playing inexpensive, all-ages shows in underserved towns and cities. It spread to Canada (Toronto, Montreal, Edmonton, West Coast), Italy (Raw Power, Wretched), Sweden (Mob 47, The Refused), Eastern Europe, and Japan (as early as 1977). "It's amazing the number of places where hardcore punk caught on during the 1980s."

# genre - Grunge

## curiosities
**Origins in Pacific Northwest** : Grunge emerged in the Seattle area, resulting from a crossbreeding of the punk and hardcore scene (facilitated by touring bands from the West Coast) with influences from hard rock bands like Led Zeppelin, Black Sabbath, and Heart. The isolation created by the Rocky Mountains, which made East-West touring difficult for smaller bands, contributed to the unique local musical ecosystem. "And that's how it crossbred with Led Zeppelin and Black Sabbath and Hart. To eventually become Grunge, that would not have happened without the Rocky Mountains being in the way."

# label - SST

The label was created to release Black Flag's debut EP, "Nervous Breakdown," after they couldn't find a label to put it out. "Greg Ginn, the founder of the band, which had come together in 1977 under the name Panic, had a side hustle selling ham radio electronics through the mail. He called his company Solid State Tuners. # curiosity

**Early Hardcore Touring Network (The List)** : DOA compiled and circulated "the list," a comprehensive network of punk-friendly venues and places to stay across North America. This list was shared among dozens, potentially hundreds, of like-minded bands, greatly expanding the touring reach of punk and hardcore throughout the continent. "DOA compiled something called the list. This was a growing list of punk-friendly venues and places to stay across North America that was made available and then traded among dozens, maybe hundreds of other like-minded bands."
**Impact of Rocky Mountains on West Coast Touring** : The presence of the Rocky Mountains made touring North-South along the West Coast significantly easier and more affordable for bands compared to East-West routes. This geographical barrier created a semi-distinct ecosystem for West Coast bands, influencing how hardcore punk and other rock genres permeated the region from San Diego to Vancouver. "You can blame the Rocky Mountains. It takes time to cross the range and there aren't that many places to play between the western and eastern slopes. So that means for a certain level of band, you inhabit a semi distinct ecosystem."
**Underserved Cities and Towns as Tour Stops** : The North-South touring flow meant punk and hardcore bands frequently played in underserved cities and towns, including the Seattle area, which were often off the grid for major touring acts. This accessibility of punk shows in such regions contributed to the local music culture, eventually crossbreeding with hard rock influences to form grunge. "The North South flow of punk and hardcore bands meant that these groups played a lot of underserved cities and towns when it came to tours. They had to play somewhere so they would play anywhere."
**Moshpits and Slam Dancing Origins** : Moshpits, characterized by slam dancing, first emerged within the hardcore scene, initially observed in California and Washington, D.C. "It was with hardcore that we saw the first Moshpits with slam dancing and was first seen in California and Washington, D.C."
**DIY Ethic and Indie Labels Popularization** : The hardcore scene was instrumental in popularizing the concept of forming one's own record label (the "do-it-yourself" ethic), as many bands resorted to this to release their music due to lack of major label interest. "It was through hardcore that the idea of forming your own record label was popularized."
**Straight Edge Movement Philosophy** : The straight edge philosophy, which originated from a Minor Threat song, advocates for a lifestyle free from alcohol, drugs of any kind, and promiscuous sex. It continues to be a lifestyle choice for many people globally. "And the straight edge philosophy continues to this day. It's a lifestyle for a lot of people."
**Boston Crew** : In Boston, followers of Ian MacKaye's straight-edge philosophy were so intense that they would often brawl with anyone they saw drinking or doing drugs at their shows. These self-appointed "moral police" were known locally as the "Boston Crow." "Those self-affointed more police were locally known as the Boston Crow. You didn't want to mess with them."
