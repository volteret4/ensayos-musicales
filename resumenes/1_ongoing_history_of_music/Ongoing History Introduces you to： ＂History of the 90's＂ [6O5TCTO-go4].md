**Roseanne (1988-1997) – Sitcom Series** : Marcy Carsey and Tom Werner, executive producers of The Cosby Show, sought a no-frills family comedy in 1987, turning to Cosby writer Matt Williams to script a show about a blue-collar family. This was considered a radical idea given the opulence dominating TV with shows like Dallas, FalconCrust, The Cosby Show, and Empty Nest. Barr was signed for the lead role after appearing on The Tonight Show with Johnny Carson, where she was not nationally known. The show premiered on October 18, 1988, becoming an instant hit with 21.4 million households watching, making it the highest-rated debut of that season. It chronicled the tumultuous life of the working-class Conner family, featuring Barr's "brash, even caustic" character who often worried about money, yet thrived as a loving family. The series was regularly in the top three of the Nielsen ratings and remained in the top 20 until its final season. It hit its creative peak with provocative storylines such as DJ's masturbation obsession, Becky seeking birth control pills, Rosanne's PMS, and Darlene's first period. In its ninth and final season, the cash-strapped Conners won a $108 million Illinois State lottery prize, a moment critics and fans deemed "shark jumping." The series finale on May 20, 1997, revealed the entire ninth season was a fantasy of Barr's depressed blue-collar character; the Conners had not won the lottery, and Dan had died from a heart attack at the end of season 8. After the show finished, it lived on in syndication, drawing a new generation of viewers. "When the show went to air on October 18, 1988, the Connor family was an instant hit."
**Roseanne (2018) – Reboot Series** : The series returned to television in March 2018, with its debut attracting 18.2 million viewers, making it the highest-rated comedy episode on any broadcast network in nearly four years. However, the show was canceled two months later after Barr posted a racist Twitter rant against Valerie Jarrett, a former adviser to President Barack Obama. "The debut of the new and improved Drozan had 18.2 million viewers, which made it the highest rated comedy episode on any broadcast network in nearly four years."

## curiosities
**Appearance on The Tonight Show with Johnny Carson** : Barr gained attention after appearing on The Tonight Show with Johnny Carson to perform her stand-up routine. At the time, she was not nationally known, believed to be from Denver, and her appearance on the show, hosted by Johnny Carson, was considered the "holy grail" for comedians. "She was kind of not known at all, certainly not nationally. I believe she was out of Denver at the time when she went on the Tonight Show."
**Domestic Goddess Persona** : Her persona was described as the "domestic goddess," which, according to TV critic Paul Brownfield, was authentic and working-class, fitting what the network was looking for. "Everything about her seemed very authentic. And her persona was the domestic goddess."
**Lack of Acting Cred** : Despite her success as a comedian, she had no prior acting experience, which the production team considered a challenge and felt it was imperative to pair her with a skilled actor like John Goodman. "There was a comedian. She had no acting cred."
**Behind-the-Scenes Conflict with Matt Williams** : From the beginning, there was trouble behind the scenes. At the premiere screening party, Barr realized the "created by" credit went solely to writer Matt Williams, which enraged her as she felt the show was built around her actual life. This led to a "burning hatred" for Williams, constant pushback, refusal of lines, tantrums, and walking off set. The situation escalated until executive producers Carsey and Werner were forced to choose, resulting in Williams being asked to leave in the middle of the first season. "When the credits flashed up on the screen, Barr realized for the first time that the created by credit went solely to writer Matt Williams."
**Control and Backstage Drama with Tom Arnold** : After Matt Williams left, Barr added her new husband, Tom Arnold, to the writing team. Arnold also played a reoccurring character but was notorious for causing drama backstage. He acted as Barr's "henchman," executing her demands and routine firings. The couple exerted significant power over scripts, rewriting them after each read-through, which caused tension among the constantly changing writing staff. "He was basically served as Barr's henchman, executing her often crazy demands and routine firings."
**Tabloid Dominance** : Barr and Tom Arnold's "wild relationship" frequently dominated the tabloids during the show's run. "Barr also dominated the tabloids with her wild relationship with Arnold."
**Emmy Nominations and Wins** : Despite its sharp comedy and "segment-busting storylines," the series was never nominated for a best comedy series Emmy. However, Barr eventually won an Emmy for her performance on the series. "But both Barr and Laurie Metcalf, who played Rosanne's sister Jackie, eventually walked away with Emmys for their performance on the series."
**Season 5 Egg-Printed Shirt Running Joke** : After Barr and Arnold split in May 1994, the show entered "surreal territory," exemplified by a season-long running joke where each of the Conners appeared in different scenes wearing the same long-sleeved, egg-printed shirt with a large chicken on the front, with no explanation. "You can forget season 5's running joke in which each of the Conners appears in a different scene in the same long-sleeved, egg-printed shirt with a large chicken on the front."
**"All a Dream" Series Finale (1997)** : The final episode, aired on May 20, 1997, concluded with Barr delivering a voiceover revealing that the entire ninth season was a fantasy of her depressed character. The lottery win had not happened, and Dan had actually died from the heart attack he suffered at the end of season 8. Barr viewed this ending as representing her "total control" over the show, and the rest of the cast was unaware of this game-changing twist. "For Bar, this last episode represented her total control over the show."
**Cancellation of Reboot Due to Twitter Rant (2018)** : The 2018 reboot of Roseanne was canceled two months after Barr posted a racist Twitter rant against Valerie Jarrett, a former adviser to President Barack Obama. "The show was canceled two months later after Bar went on a racist Twitter rant against Valerie Jarrett, a former adviser to President Barack Obama."
**Character Killed Off in Spin-off** : Following the cancellation of the Roseanne reboot, ABC launched a spin-off called The Conners, which continued without the matriarch. In this spin-off, the character of Roseanne Conner was killed off by an opioid overdose. "In the end, Drozan was killed off by an opioid overdose."

**Seinfeld (1989-1998) – Sitcom Series** : Co-created with fellow stand-up comedian Larry David, the show was an adaptation of Seinfeld's observational comedy about the "madness of life's idiosyncrasies." Unlike Roseanne Barr, he essentially played himself, a stand-up comedian in New York. The pilot, called "The Seinfeld Chronicles," aired in 1989 as a late-night special and did not include Elaine, and Kramer was called Kessler. NBC was initially reluctant to make it a full series, but Rick Ludwin, head of NBC's entertainment division, funded a short four-episode first season from his own department and a scrapped Bob Hope special. The show, dubbed "the show about nothing," was built around fussy, self-absorbed people obsessing over daily life and became one of the most influential TV shows of all time. It took three years for the public to catch on, but it then became one of the biggest comedy hits in the USA, serving as the "linchpin" of NBC's "must-see TV" Thursday night lineup. It spawned 180 episodes across nine seasons, was nominated for 68 Emmy Awards, winning 10, and ranked either first or second in Nielsen ratings from 1994 to 1998. "The show about nothing was built around a group of fussy, self-absorbed people who obsessed over the minutia of daily life."

"Let's take a look at one of the most famous episodes, the contest."
