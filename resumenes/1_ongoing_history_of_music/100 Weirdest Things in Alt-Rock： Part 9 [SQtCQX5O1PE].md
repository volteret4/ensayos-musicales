# artist - U2

The Edge

## curiosities
**The Edge's 375 Hats – Hair Thinning Since Late 1980s, Appeared Hatless Once in Recent Memory** : The Edge began losing his hair in the late 1980s, becoming self-conscious enough that he has worn some form of headgear in public at all times ever since — including at his 2002 wedding to Morleigh Steinberg. As of fall 2007, his collection numbered 375 hats. His stated favorite is a mole-skin top hat worn on special occasions. The only confirmed recent public appearance without a hat was the 1995 Pavarotti and Friends concert. "The edge's headgear has become a very important and very distinctive part of his look."

**Bono's Hat Flown First Class London to Italy (May 2003) – Total Cost Approximately $1,800 for a Hat** : In May 2003, Bono was scheduled to appear with Luciano Pavarotti in Modena, Italy, at a benefit for Iraqi refugees displaced by the war. Realizing he had left his favorite hat in the UK, he phoned his assistant. The hat was put in a taxi to Heathrow (£100), then placed in a purchased first-class seat on a British Airways flight (£1,700). Because cabin crew worried the hat might be crushed, it was upgraded from first class to the cockpit jump seat. A driver then collected and delivered it to Bono at the benefit. "Did I mention that the event was designed to raise money for Iraqis made homeless by the war?"

## curiosities
**Supernova Heights – £2.5M Gaudy Mansion, £700,000 Yellow Submarine Kitchen, Meg Matthews's Spending Habits** : At the peak of Britpop, Noel Gallagher and then-partner Meg Matthews bought a house at 8 Steeles Road in Belsize Park, London, for £2.5 million in the mid-1990s, naming it Supernova Heights in stained glass over the front door. The décor included zebra-print couches, wild animal prints, a gothic bed, handcuffs, and an estimated £700,000 spent covering the kitchen walls with tiny paintings of yellow submarines. Meg became nationally famous for spending approximately £27,000 per month on clothes and beauty products and £50,000 on a birthday party for their daughter Anaïs. During the divorce, Meg sought £40,000 per month; the settlement gave her a Regent's Park house Noel bought for £1.2 million, plus £25,000 per month in support. In summer 2007, Meg auctioned Supernova Heights furnishings on eBay, netting approximately $30,000 — illustrating how badly bad taste depreciates. Noel remains worth north of £50 million. "Others described it as Taj Mahal pub kitsch."

# artist - KLF

## members
Bill Drummond
Jimmy Cauty

## curiosities
**Burned One Million Pounds in Cash – Jura, Scotland, August 23, 1994** : After a series of deliberately provocative stunts (including dumping a dead sheep and eight gallons of blood at an awards show after-party) and announcing their retirement from music until world peace was achieved, Bill Drummond and Jimmy Cauty of the KLF deleted their entire back catalogue and made what is believed to be the single largest cash withdrawal in UK banking history. They nailed one million pounds in £50 notes to a board, paraded it around England as an artwork called *Nailed to the Wall*, then on the evening of August 23, 1994, traveled to the island of Jura in Scotland with one journalist and one cameraman. In an abandoned boathouse, they set fire to the entire million pounds. A documentary called *Watch the KLF Foundation Burn a Million Quid* exists on YouTube. The pair have never fully explained their motivation beyond vague references to satire of materialism and contempt for the music industry. "They deliberately burned one million pounds of their own money."


# artist - Ramones

## members
Joey Ramone
Johnny Ramone

## curiosities
**Post-Retirement Wealth – Joey's Day Trading; Johnny's Baseball Cards and Movie Posters Sold for $250,000+** : The Ramones never sold many records during their career — only one album (a greatest hits collection) went gold. Most income came from touring and merchandise. However, after the band retired in 1996, both Joey and Johnny found financial success outside music. Joey discovered stock market day trading in the late 1990s, taking tips from financial TV channels and reportedly amassing a substantial fortune before his death in 2001. Johnny, a lifelong Yankees fanatic who ranked baseball above rock and roll on his personal top-ten lists, spent decades collecting vintage baseball cards and movie posters, including original posters from *Frankenstein* (1931) and *The Day the Earth Stood Still* (1951). Per his will, the poster collection was sold at auction in Dallas after his death in 2004, netting more than a quarter of a million dollars for his wife Linda. "Two of whom — Joey and Johnny — made a lot of money after the band retired on things other than music."


# artist - The Offspring

## members
Dexter Holland

## curiosities
**Anarchy Airlines – Dexter Holland's Private Jet Fleet, Solo Round-the-World Flight 2004** : *Smash* (1994) and subsequent Offspring albums generating more than 32 million in worldwide sales gave singer Dexter Holland the means to pursue his passion for aviation. He earned his first pilot's license in 1996, subsequently gaining more advanced certifications. He owns a Citation II twin-engine jet (approximately $2.5 million used), painted with the anarchy symbol on the tailfin and furnished with leopard-print interior — his own "Anarchy Airlines." He also flies an Aero Vodochody L-39 Czech/Russian jet fighter. He has enough simulator hours to pilot a 747. On November 25, 2004, Holland completed a secret solo circumnavigation: Los Angeles, New York, Iceland, Egypt, Dubai, Bangkok, Siberia, Russia, Alaska, and home — 11 days sustained almost entirely on beef jerky and Doritos, during which he encountered a volcanic eruption in Iceland, locusts in Egypt, and uncooperative customs officials in Russia. "Some people are into golf. Some people are into shooting deer. I'm into flying."
