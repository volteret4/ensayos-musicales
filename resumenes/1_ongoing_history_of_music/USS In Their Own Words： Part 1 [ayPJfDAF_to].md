## albums
**Welding the C-Drive (2007) – First Record/EP** : Ash compiled content for this first record, which was released in the summer of 2007. The band celebrated its release with a sold-out CD release party at The Gladstone Hotel. In January 2008, 102.1 The Edge began discussing the album, leading to "Hollowpoint in Hyperbole" being added to radio rotation as a single. The EP was notably brought to the station by Barry Taylor, who praised its maturity for an independent debut. "Ash started compiling all the content that would make our first record well in the C drive and that summer we put out that record and we played the Gladstone hotel sold out CD release party."
**Customization (2009) – Only Full-Length Record** : At the end of 2008, Ash dedicated himself to completing the band's only full-length album, *Customization*, by working intensely in a friend's studio in Etobicoke. The record was produced and engineered by Matthew von Wagner in collaboration with Ash. "ash buried himself from a friend studio in a tow bico to finish what would become our only full length record customization."

## curiosities
**Formation with Third Member** : When Ash and Jay initially played together around 2004-2005, their band included a third member, a friend of Jay's, who was deeply involved in Toronto's house, progressive house, and ambient music scenes. This individual's influence was significant in shaping USS's early aesthetic, particularly their departure from traditional song structures like choruses and verses. "When we met, there was a third member of our band because Jay and I had yet, he was just on turntables. I was just on the acoustic guitar and we had a friend of Jay's who was just hardcore into the house, ProvinCrossive House, Ambient Scene in Toronto."
**Singapore Electronic Gear Malfunction (2000s)** : During a performance at the Music Matters festival in Clark Quay, Singapore, USS encountered severe technical difficulties due to underestimating the local power situation. They blew fuses in their main electronic instruments and tripped breakers at two different clubs because of the 220-volt supply, contrasting with their 120-volt North American gear. This forced them to perform an unplanned acoustic set, with Ash playing acoustic guitar, Jay attempting to scratch on a non-spinning record player, and a drummer providing beats normally produced by a laptop. "Not only did we blow the fuses in our main electronic instruments, we also blew the breaker at two different clubs and so we had to DIY it and go back to our roots."
**The Smoothie Era (c. 2008 - Singapore Incident)** : Beginning around 2008, Ash, then a full-time musician, established a ritual of making and sharing protein and electrolyte smoothies on stage during USS shows. This unique practice became a signature element of their performances, often involving pouring smoothies for the audience. The "smoothie era" dramatically concluded in Singapore when the blender, due to voltage incompatibility, caught fire, sparked, smoked, and mildly electrocuted Ash on stage, marking its "grand finale." "Starting in about 2008 I was a full time rougher musician by night...it just became this thing that we did at every show and so yeah when we got to Singapore the blender actually caught on fire and started sparking and smoking and I got mildly electrocuted on stage and that was like the grand finale of the smoothie era."
**Ecstasy-Spiked Smoothie Incident at The Casbah** : After a show at The Casbah in Hamilton, Ash, who was deeply immersed in meditation at the time, was encouraged by audience members in the front row to finish his on-stage smoothie. After chugging it, he experienced what he initially perceived as a "flash of enlightenment" or "satori," characterized by intense joy, peace, and a feeling of unity. He later realized, while in the band van, that his smoothie had been secretly spiked with ecstasy, making it a "very fascinating experience" of unknowingly being under the influence of drugs. "And all of a sudden I went, oh I'm like it's happening. I'm like I'm here. I'm like I did it. I'm like this is enlightenment...And all of a sudden I realized in the van that someone had spiked my smoothie with ecstasy."

## curiosities
**Childhood Sickness and Transformation** : Ash grew up in a prosperous suburban environment, excelling as a hockey player. At nine years old, he became severely ill, leading to six years of hospitalizations, parental divorce, and his father's financial collapse. He remained physically stunted at 4'6" and 65 lbs for approximately eight years, unable to play hockey. This period of intense pain, isolation, and medical challenges, including mood-altering prednisone, profoundly shaped his appreciation for physical health and inspired his later lifestyle choices. "I got really, really sick. My parents got divorced. Empire crumbled...I got stuck at like four foot six, like 65 pounds."
**Early Musical Path and Mentor** : Unable to pursue hockey due to his illness, Ash's mother encouraged him to join a play, recognizing his love for making people laugh and being a show-off. He joined a musical, where he began singing for the first time. Later, while playing bass in his high school band, he was critically encouraged by Aubrey Mints, the singer of his brother's band, Church Bus, to become a lead vocalist after performing Beck's "Nightmare Hippie Girl." Ash considers this conversation a pivotal moment in his life, a realization he reflected on twenty years later during a reunion with Aubrey in Los Angeles. "Aubrey comes up to me after the show and he goes, you need to kick back out of the band. You need to be the singer."
**The "Ubiquitous Synergy Seeker" Manifesto** : During a period of personal stagnation working in a factory, Ash reflected on his "learned helplessness" stemming from childhood trauma and the inhibiting effects of being a "pothead." He decided to write a "script" for the person he aspired to become. While working, he discovered the word "ubiquitous" ("everywhere simultaneously") in a dictionary, which, combined with his studies of Eastern philosophy, led him to coin "Ubiquitous Synergy Seeker." This name encapsulates a paradox: the journey of seeking something that is inherently ever-present, serving as his personal manifesto and the foundational philosophy for his band. "So the name ubiquitous synergy seeker is actually a paradox because how can you seek something that's ever present but that's the journey."
**Solo Venture and Reunion with Jay (2007)** : After an initial period with Jay, Ash pursued a solo path as "Ubiquitous Synergy Seeker." In April 2007, he invited Jay to rejoin him for a show at Saltz Pub in The Junction, Toronto. Jay, who was working in the Northern Alberta oil fields at the time, returned to play, and they performed old songs from their pre-USS band, Team of Captains. This reunion performance was so enjoyable that it solidified their decision to formally continue their musical partnership as USS. "when Ash went solo and he became ubiquitous synergy seeker he asked me to rejoin forces with him in April 2007."
**Post-EP Work and Meditation (2007-2008)** : Following the release of the *Welding the C-Drive* EP in summer 2007, Ash traveled to Texas to meditate, while Jay returned to the oil fields to earn money. This period of external work and personal introspection was a necessary hiatus for both members before their music began to gain significant radio attention in early 2008. "Ash went down to Texas to meditate and to just basically you know we both like needed to do you know we needed to work to make money because Toronto rent is high."

## curiosities
**Early Life and Entertainment Drive** : Jay grew up in Stouffville, Ontario, and was an avid basketball player, drawn to its "show off" quality as an individual within a team setting. This inherent desire to entertain extended into his high school years, where he DJ'd school events, performed announcements, and served as student council president at Markham High. His immersion in 90s hip hop and DJ culture, embracing the "four elements" of hip hop, further fueled his natural knack for captivating audiences. "I played basketball my entire young life and for me, I feel looking back on it. Um I think I like basketball because as Ash pointed out, it has that show off quality to it where it's, it's you on a team, but it's your chance to really show who you are."
**University Punk Band Experience** : While attending Trent University in Peterborough in the early 2000s, Jay was invited to join a punk band as a turntablist. This reflected a contemporary trend where DJs scratched records for rock bands. This experience was transformative, revealing to him that he could actively participate in a band without playing traditional instruments like guitar, bass, or drums, thereby deepening his engagement with band life. "I got asked to join a punk band because uh back in the 2000, the early 2000s DJs were scratching records for uh rock bands. So um I was one of the few turntable lists on Trent University campus. And so I joined this punk band to scratch."
**Post-Graduation Struggles and Meeting Ash** : After earning a business degree, Jay faced the typical "university graduate quagmire" of being unable to secure a corporate job in downtown Toronto due to a lack of experience. He returned to his previous employment at the Angus Glen golf course in Markham. It was there, in the summer of 2004, that he serendipitously met Ash while loading beer, a meeting that led to Ash asking him to DJ his sister's wedding and ignited their future musical partnership. "the doors kept getting slammed in my face everywhere. I went because I didn't have any experience...I went back to my old job at a golf course in Markham called Angus Glen."
**Working in the Oil Fields (2007-2008)** : After Ash briefly pursued a solo path as "Ubiquitous Synergy Seeker," Jay was working in the oil fields of Northern Alberta, including Fort McMurray, when Ash invited him to rejoin for a show in April 2007. Following the release of the *Welding the C-Drive* EP, Jay returned to the oil fields, this time in Cold Lake, Alberta, to help construct a pipeline on an air weapons range, seeking income due to Toronto's high cost of living. It was during his time there, in January 2008, that he received the pivotal call about their EP gaining significant radio attention. "I was working in the oil fields in Northern Alberta I just returned from Fort McMurray...I went back to the oil fields this time I went up to cold Lake Alberta and to help build a pipeline on air weapons range."

# curiosity

**Two-Person Band Novelty (2004-2005)** : In the years 2004-2005, the concept of a two-person band, such as USS, was considered "a bit odd" and relatively new. Despite the emergence of groups like The White Stripes and The Black Keys, a minimalist lineup was still uncommon, making USS's eventual success as a duo particularly noteworthy. "the idea of a two person band was fairly new in 2004, 2005."
**"Learned Helplessness" and Artistic Aspiration** : Ash described experiencing a period of "learned helplessness" in his life, which he attributed to childhood trauma. During this time, he characterized himself as a "victim" and a "pothead" whose ambition and organizational skills had diminished. This psychological state, marked by shame over his unfulfilled potential, motivated him to create a personal "script" for a new identity, ultimately leading to the philosophical foundation of USS and its name. "Generally for the most part people either become overachievers or they become victims as a result of traumatic experience in childhood and in my case I went towards the victim side of things and that learned helplessness."
**The Canadian Indie Rock Scene (90s-2000s)** : The Canadian indie rock scene of the late 1990s and early 2000s was characterized by unique and influential bands such as Head, Hayden, Treble Charger, and The Killjoys. These artists, particularly Head with its personal songwriting and victory in the 102.1 New Music Search, profoundly influenced Ash's musical development and shaped the landscape for emerging musicians. "all that incredible, like unique Canadian, all that can rock stuff. Indy rock music. It just, it just came flooding in."
