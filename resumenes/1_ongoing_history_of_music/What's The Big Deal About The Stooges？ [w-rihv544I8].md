## albums
**The Stooges (1969) – Self-titled debut** : This album was produced by John Cale of The Velvet Underground. Due to the band's inability to rehearse for more than 20 minutes, half the material was allegedly written the night before their first day in the studio, with old avant-garde ideas reworked into more coherent songs. The initial mix was rejected by Elektra president Jack Holtzman, who then re-mixed it himself, but despite this, the album did not sell well as it was considered too raw and primitive for most people at the time. "Nobody was making music that sounded like this."
**Fun House (1970) – Second Album** : Named after their living quarters in Detroit, this album was recorded in Los Angeles. The recording process was characterized by extensive drug use, including the band's first experience with cocaine. The album was recorded largely live off the floor with minimal overdubs to capture their raw live sound, with its seven songs recorded in sequence like a live gig. Released in July 1970, *Fun House* is retrospectively considered the best representation of The Stooges' sound, though at the time it was savaged by critics and commercially unsuccessful. "That whole record was recorded live off the floor."
**Raw Power (1973) – David Bowie Collaboration** : This album resulted from David Bowie convincing Iggy Pop, the Ashton brothers, and James Williamson to come to England to make a record for Columbia Records. Despite Bowie's connection and influence, the album struggled commercially, peaking at 182 on the Top 200 charts. However, it deeply inspired many artists, including a 16-year-old Johnny Lydon and Mick Jones (The Clash) who were at Iggy's first UK show, and Kurt Cobain who later called it his favorite album. "But the people who bought this record were inspired."
**Skull Ring (2003) – Iggy Pop Solo Album featuring The Stooges** : This album was released under Iggy Pop's name after Virgin Records suggested he incorporate guest musicians, specifically hinting at the "Ashton Boys," to generate buzz for his next record and avoid being dropped. Ron and Scott Ashton appeared on four songs, marking a close, but not official, reunion of the band members. "Ron and Scott appeared on four songs in a record that was eventually released under the title Skull Ring."
**The Weirdness (2007) – Reunion Album** : This album was released on March 6, 2007, marking the first album of new material from The Stooges since *Raw Power* in 1973, a gap of 34 years. It featured Iggy Pop, Ron Ashton, and Scott Ashton, with punk stalwart Mike Watt on bass. The album was followed by extensive touring throughout the mid-2000s. "It was the first album of new stuages material since raw power in 1973, 34 years."

## curiosities
**Early Influences & Recognition** : The Stooges are frequently cited as a major influence by contemporary bands like The White Stripes and The Hives, and by 90s bands such as Jane's Addiction and Nirvana. They also shaped the sound of 80s bands like Sonic Youth and inspired 70s artists like the Sex Pistols, Ramones, and David Bowie to make music. "So the stooges seem to be on everybody's list even though they hardly sold any records in their day and were so messed up that their singer ended up in an asylum."
**Formation and Members (1967)** : The band was formed in 1967 by James Newell Osterberg (Iggy), brothers Ron and Scott Ashton, and Dave Alexander. Iggy, who was a known drummer in Ann Arbor, befriended Ron Ashton, leading to Scott joining, and then Dave Alexander, a high school dropout who had traveled the UK in search of the meaning of The Beatles. "These four guys decided to form a band with Iggy out front and on keyboards."
**Band Name Origin ("Psychedelic Stooges")** : The name originated during a drug-fueled session at the Ashton house in 1967 where the band members were watching "The Three Stooges" on TV while high on acid. Initially, they suggested "The Psychedelic Stooges" but it was later shortened to just "The Stooges" as no one could remember how to spell "psychedelic." "When they were very, very high, they were watching the three stuages on TV and somebody suggested I know we'll just call ourselves the psychedelic stuages."
**Iggy Pop's Name Origin** : James Newell Osterberg adopted the stage name "Iggy" from his previous band, The Iguanas. The "Pop" part was added after he shaved off his eyebrows in a "weird stoned gesture," leading the other band members to comment that he resembled a local drug user named Jim Pop. "The pop part came after Iggy shaved off his eyebrows in some weird stone to gesture."
**Jim Morrison's Influence (1967)** : On October 20, 1967, Iggy attended a Doors show at the University of Michigan and was greatly inspired by Jim Morrison's drunken behavior and provocative interactions with the audience, which influenced Iggy's own stage persona. "Iggy was there and he was inspired by Jim Morrison's drunkenness in the way he provoked the audience."
**First Gig (Halloween 1967)** : The Stooges' debut performance was on Halloween 1967 at their headquarters on State Street in Ann Arbor, described as "weird." Iggy wore an aluminum foil wig and played a Hawaiian guitar tuned only to E, Scott played oil drums with ball-peen hammers, and Dave created reverb effects by banging on Ron's amplifier. Despite the unconventional and "awful" performance, both band and audience, being high, considered it a success. "Iggy wore a wig made of strands of aluminum foil and played a Hawaiian guitar with every string tune to E."
**Early Professional Gigs and "Osterizer" (1968)** : The band's first professional gig was opening for the Amboy Dukes (featuring Ted Nugent) on January 20, 1968, followed by an opening slot for Blood Sweat and Tears on March 3, 1968. During these shows, Iggy notably played a homemade instrument called the "Osterizer," which was a vacuum cleaner attached to a blender, although he soon abandoned it due to amplification difficulties. "And was the singer playing a vacuum cleaner? Yes, yes it was. And was it attached to a blender? And yeah, it was."
**Confrontational Stage Presence & Stage Diving** : The Stooges developed a divisive live reputation, attracting audiences who either loved or hated them. Iggy Pop's performances were characterized by self-destructive, confrontational, and contortionist antics, often involving him being half-naked, and frequently diving into the audience without concern for his or their safety. He is considered a possible inventor of stage diving. "It's quite possible that Iggy was the inventor of stage diving."
**Discovery and Signing by Danny Fields** : The Stooges came to the attention of Danny Fields, a talent scout for Elektra Records known for signing hippie bands like The Doors. Fields discovered them while in Detroit to check out MC5, and despite his bosses being "stupified" by The Stooges, he convinced Elektra to sign them. "However, the stuages eventually came to the attention of Danny Fields, a talent scout for electro-records who was hired for his ability to find the right hippie bands."
**Commercial Failure and Punk Legacy** : The Stooges' albums, though critically acclaimed in retrospect, were largely commercial failures during their initial release due to their raw and primitive sound, which was ahead of its time. Despite this, their debut album is considered one of the most important pre-punk records, contributing to Iggy's legend as the "godfather of punk." "It has gone down on history as being one of the most important pre-punk records of all time."
**Extreme Stage Antics (1970)** : By the spring of 1970, The Stooges' live performances became even more extreme and out of control. Iggy would often get naked, cut himself with broken glass, and smear himself with substances like raw meat and peanut butter. Audiences attended primarily for the spectacle, rather than the music. "Iggy was just out of control, often getting naked, cutting himself with broken glass, and smearing things like raw meat and peanut butter all over himself."
**Drug Addiction and Lineup Changes (Late 1970)** : By late 1970, Iggy and Scott Ashton were severely addicted to heroin. Bass player Dave Alexander was fired for being too drunk to perform at a gig. New bass players cycled through, and a second guitarist, James Williamson, was added, but the band struggled with stability. "By the end of 1970, Iggy and Scott Ashton were serious hairwind junkies."
**Dropped by Elektra and Financial Ruin** : Due to their escalating drug problems, chaotic gigs (where Iggy was sometimes too stoned to stand), Elektra Records dropped The Stooges. The label also demanded an $11,000 refund on an advance given to the band. The Stooges then entered a nine-month period of inactivity, save for drug use, with Ron Ashton becoming estranged from the group, calling it a "death trip." "So Electra dropped them. Actually, they more than dropped them. They demanded a refund of $11,000 on an advance they had given the band."
**Iggy's Survival and Drug Dealing (Early 1970s)** : During the band's inactive period after being dropped by Elektra, Iggy Pop sustained himself by becoming a middleman for a heroin dealer. However, he often used the drugs himself, exacerbating his addiction. "Iggy paid the bills by becoming a middleman for a hairwind dealer, but sometimes he forgot his role and ended up using all the drugs himself."
**Financial Troubles and Truck Incident** : The Stooges were plagued by debts, including an IRS investigation for unpaid taxes, and bills resulting from an incident where Scott Ashton attempted to drive the band's 14-foot truck under a 13-foot bridge. "They were under investigation by the IRS for non-payment of taxes."
**First Breakup and Iggy's Hiatus** : Eventually, the band dissolved. Iggy Pop spent the next six months in Florida cutting lawns for retirees. "Eventually, the stuages were done. Iggy spent the next six months in Florida cutting lawns for retirees."
**Meeting David Bowie (September 7, 1971)** : While in New York "sponging off a friend," Iggy Pop met David Bowie on September 7, 1971. Bowie, who was on the verge of launching his Ziggy Stardust phase, was influenced by Iggy, choosing the name Ziggy partly due to Iggy's persona. "The date was September 7th of 1971. Bowie was about to launch into his Ziggy stardust phase. That character was to be in part inspired by Iggy."
**Bowie's Intervention and *Raw Power* Production** : David Bowie, then on a hot streak with significant industry power, bonded with Iggy Pop. He convinced Iggy, Ron and Scott Ashton, and James Williamson to move to England to record *Raw Power* for Columbia Records, effectively resurrecting the band. "Bowie was on a hot streak and thus he had power. And after bonding with Iggy, he convinced him, the Ashton brothers in James Williamson, to come to England where they'd all make a record for Columbia Records."
**Last Original Show (February 9, 1974)** : The final show played by The Stooges before their first long hiatus was on February 9, 1974, at The Rock'n'Roll Farm, a biker bar in Wayne, Michigan. Iggy, wearing a pink leotard, provoked the biker audience with insults during a 45-minute rendition of "Louis Louis," leading to eggs and bottles being thrown. Iggy then confronted a large biker, was punched in the face, and the band left the stage, effectively ending their run. "The last show the studios played together was at a club called The Rock'n'Roll Farm in Wayne, Michigan."
**Iggy's Arrest and Hospitalization (October 1974)** : In October 1974, Iggy Pop was arrested for being "aggressively strung out" at an LA burger joint. He was subsequently admitted to the UCLA Neuropsychiatric Hospital, where he detoxed and underwent psychiatric evaluation in an 8x4 room for several weeks. "Iggy was arrested for being aggressively strung out in an LA burger joint. He ended up at the UCLA Neurosyche Atric Hospital."
**Bowie's Support During Hospitalization** : During Iggy Pop's hospitalization in 1974, he had only two visitors: David Bowie and Bowie's costume designer, Ola Hudson (mother of Saul Hudson, later known as Slash). Bowie, despite his own struggles with drugs, played a crucial role as Iggy's and ultimately The Stooges' savior. "He had two visitors. One was David Bowie and the other was Bowie's costume designer, a woman named Ola Hudson."
**Ron Ashton's Estrangement and Side Projects** : After the initial breakup of The Stooges, Ron Ashton was estranged from the group due to the "death trip" drug use. He later played in several bands that didn't achieve significant success, though one group featured Thurston Moore of Sonic Youth, Mark Arm of Mudhoney, and J Mascis of Dinosaur Jr. He also pursued acting, appearing in the original *Texas Chainsaw Massacre* (1974). "Ron Ashton played in a series of bands, none of which did much, although there was the group that featured Thurston Moore of Sonic Youth, also Mark Arm of Mudhoney and Jay Mascus of Dinosaur Jr."
**James Williamson's Career Shift** : Following the initial breakup, guitarist James Williamson had a brief reunion with Iggy Pop in 1979 but then retired from music to become an electronics engineer. He earned an electrical engineering degree and eventually became the Vice President of Technology Standards for Sony. "He became an electronics engineer, although there was a brief reunion with Iggy in 1979. But then he retired from music, got an electrical engineering degree, and became the vice president of technology standards for Sony."
**Steve McCay's Post-Stooges Work** : Saxophonist Steve McCay, who performed on the *Fun House* album, continued his career working with a variety of avant-garde performers. "Steve McCay, he was the saxophoneist who turned on the funhouse album, while he worked with a series of on-guard performers."
**Reunion Overtures and Iggy's Reluctance** : For decades after their initial breakup, various attempts were made to reunite Iggy Pop and the Ashton brothers, but Iggy, whose solo career was flourishing and increasingly drug-free, felt revisiting the past would not be in his best interests. "But things were going so well for Iggy that he just felt that it wouldn't be in his best interests to revisit the past."
**Official Reunion at Coachella (2003)** : The Stooges officially regrouped and performed at the Coachella Festival in California on April 27, 2003. This reunion occurred 10,670 days, or 29 years, two months, and 19 days after their last gig at the biker bar on February 9, 1974. "And so it came to pass that Iggy Pop and the stuages regrouped and performed at the Coachella Festival in California on April 27, 2003."
**Ron Ashton's Death (2009)** : Ron Ashton died of a heart attack at the age of 60, approximately two years after the release of the reunion album *The Weirdness*. The band subsequently continued with James Williamson returning on guitar. "And then Ron Ashton died of a heart attack. He made it to 60 years old."
**Rock and Roll Hall of Fame Induction (2010)** : The Stooges were finally inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2010 after six previous unsuccessful attempts. This recognition came despite their historical lack of significant record sales. "And in 2010, they finally made it into the rock and roll hall of fame after six unsuccessful attempts."
**The Complete Fun House Session Box Set** : A deluxe release titled "The Complete Funhouse Session" offers a comprehensive look into the recording process of the *Fun House* album. Priced at $100, it includes all recordings made during the album's production, including conversations between takes. "The complete funhouse session. A hundred bucks, and you get to hear everything that went on during the recording of that album, including all the talking between takes."

These two albums helped transition him from a "deranged man in rock" to a respected figure. "Iggy's solo career really got moving with two albums, the Idiot and Lust for Life, both released in 1977."

## curiosities
**Early Drumming Talent** : Before forming The Stooges and becoming Iggy Pop, James Newell Osterberg (Jimmy) was known as a talented drummer, playing in his school marching band and later in several rock bands. His drumming talent was admired by the Ashton brothers. "They admired Iggy's town as a drummer. Not only did he play in the school marching band but he also eventually ended up in a couple of rock bands."
**Draft Scare and Physical Incident** : Iggy faced a potential draft into the Vietnam War. During his physical examination at the local draft office, an "incident" occurred while he was naked and about to undergo a close exam, which ultimately convinced officials he was "not army material," allowing him to avoid service. "Let's just say it happened when Iggy was naked and about to be subjected to a close exam."
**Post-Prime Movers Blues Focus** : After leaving The Prime Movers, Iggy initially focused on becoming a better blues player before reuniting with the Ashton brothers and Dave Alexander, which led to the formation of The Stooges. "He left the prime movers shortly after that to concentrate on being a better blues player."
**Middleman for Heroin Dealer** : During The Stooges' inactive period after being dropped by Elektra, Iggy Pop sustained himself financially by acting as a middleman for a heroin dealer. However, he often used the drugs himself, deepening his addiction. "Iggy paid the bills by becoming a middleman for a hairwind dealer, but sometimes he forgot his role and ended up using all the drugs himself."
**Cutting Lawns in Florida** : After The Stooges' initial dissolution, Iggy Pop spent six months living in Florida, earning money by cutting lawns for retirees. "Iggy spent the next six months in Florida cutting lawns for retirees."
**Detox and Berlin Relocation** : Following his arrest and hospitalization in October 1974, Iggy Pop, with support from David Bowie, moved to Berlin. There, they both cleaned up their drug habits and restarted their careers, with Iggy launching his solo career in 1977. "Iggy and Bowie moved to Berlin, cleaned up and restarted their careers."
**Transformation to "Godfather of Punk"** : Over 25 years after his initial struggles, Iggy Pop transformed from being "the most deranged man in rock" into one of the most respected figures, earning the title "Godfather of Punk" as his myth and legend grew. "Over the next 25 years, Iggy went from being the most deranged man in rock to one of the most respected. The Godfather of Punk and all that."

# genre - Punk

## curiosities
**Pre-Punk Influence** : The Stooges are widely recognized as a foundational "pre-punk" band, with their 1969 debut album going down in history as one of the most important pre-punk records of all time. Their raw and primitive sound significantly predated and influenced the punk movement. "It has gone down on history as being one of the most important pre-punk records of all time."
**Godfather of Punk** : Iggy Pop is widely regarded as the "godfather of punk" due to his pioneering stage antics, confrontational style, and the raw sound of The Stooges, which laid much of the groundwork for the punk rock genre. "And it's the beginnings of Iggy's legend as the godfather of punk."

# label - Elektra Records

## curiosities
**Signing of The Stooges** : Elektra Records, through talent scout Danny Fields, signed The Stooges despite the label's bosses being "absolutely stupified" by the band's unconventional sound. Fields was known for signing influential hippie bands like The Doors. "He, for example, was the guy who signed the doors. While in Detroit, to check out another bank called the MC5, Danny heard about the stuages. And even though his bosses were absolutely stupified by them, they agreed to sign the band."
**Rejection of First Album Mix** : Elektra Records rejected the initial mix of The Stooges' self-titled debut album. The label president, Jack Holtzman, personally stepped in to remix the record before its release. "The first mix was rejected by the record label. The lecture president, Jack Holtzman, came in to do it himself."
**Dropping The Stooges and Demanding Refund** : Elektra Records eventually dropped The Stooges due to the band's escalating drug problems and chaotic live performances. The label went further by demanding an $11,000 refund on an advance they had previously given to the band. "So Electra dropped them. Actually, they more than dropped them. They demanded a refund of $11,000 on an advance they had given the band."

# instrument - Osterizer

## curiosities
**Iggy Pop's Homemade Instrument** : The Osterizer was a homemade instrument created and played by Iggy Pop during The Stooges' early professional gigs in 1968. It consisted of a vacuum cleaner attached to a blender, but he soon gave it up due to the difficulty in amplifying its sound. "And was the singer playing a vacuum cleaner? Yes, yes it was. And was it attached to a blender? And yeah, it was. It was a homemade instrument that Iggy called the Osterizer."

# curiosity

**Early Recognition for Iggy Pop** : By grade 10, James Newell Osterberg (Iggy) was one of the most recognizable students at Ann Arbor High School near Detroit, known for his interest in politics and deep immersion in music, particularly Motown and Chicago blues. "By grade 10 he was one of the most recognizable kids at Ann Arbor High School near Detroit."
**Dave Alexander's Search for The Beatles' Meaning** : Dave Alexander, the original bassist for The Stooges, dropped out of high school 45 minutes into his senior year. He then embarked on a motorcycle trip across the UK, on a personal quest "to find the true meaning of the Beatles." "And Dave dropped out of high school 45 minutes into a senior year and ended up riding a motorcycle across the UK on a search to find the true meaning of the Beatles."
**Hippie Culture in 1967** : The formation of The Stooges in 1967 coincided with the peak of hippie culture, characterized by phrases like "turning on, tuning in and dropping out," alongside significant LSD use. This cultural backdrop influenced the band's initial acid-fueled formation. "It was 1967. Hippie culture was at its peak. He was turning on, tuning in and dropping out and dropping a lot of acid."
**Dave Alexander's Death** : Original Stooges bass player Dave Alexander contracted a severe case of pancreatitis due to his heavy drinking and died in a hospital at the age of 27 in February 1975, not long after the band's initial breakup. "Original bass player Dave Alexander contracted a serious case of pancreatitis due to his drinking. He died in hospital at age 27 in February of 1975."
**Zeke Zettner's Death** : Zeke Zettner, who was Dave Alexander's first replacement on bass for The Stooges for a time, died in 1973. "The exetner, a one-time rode in Alexander's first replacement on bass, died in 1973."
**Iggy's Valium Incident with Dogs** : During his period of mental instability and drug use, Iggy Pop once thought it was a good idea to share a stash of Valium with a friend's dogs. Fortunately, the dogs survived the incident. "He once thought it was a good idea to share a stash of value and with a couple of dogs that belonged to a friend. But don't worry, the dogs survived."
