## albums
**No Fun Intended (2008) – Tyler Joseph Solo Album** : This was a solo record made by Tyler Joseph in his parents' basement when he was about 17 or 18, still in his senior year of high school. Nick Thomas, a future 21 Pilots bassist, contributed some guitars to the project. "This is a track called Blast for Me and dates to about 2008."
**21 Pilots (2009) – Self-Titled Debut Album** : The band's first self-produced album, released on December 29, 2009, featured the lineup of Tyler Joseph, Nick Thomas, and Chris Salah. Despite being a low-key, home-recorded indie release, it sold approximately 115,000 copies. "The first 21 pilots album was a self-produced thing released on December 29, 2009."
**Regional At Best (2011) – Second Indie Release** : Released on July 8, 2011, this album was largely Tyler Joseph's project with minimal guest appearances, coming out shortly after Nick Thomas left and Josh Dunn joined. Tyler referred to it as a " glorified mixtape," and while initially distributed to fans, copies were later discontinued, deleted, and wiped from existence due to a record deal. "This project was pretty much Tyler's baby with the exception of a couple of small guest appearances."
**Regional At Best & 21 Pilots (2011) – Tinderbox Double Edition** : A hard copy edition released through the Tinderbox label that featured both the *Regional At Best* album and the band's first self-titled album. This package was specifically created with the purpose of attracting the attention of record labels. "Plus, there was also a second hard copy edition through a label called Tinderbox. It featured both Regional At Best and a copy of the first self titled album."
**Vessel (2013) – Third Studio Album** : Released on January 8, 2013, this album garnered positive reviews and significantly helped in building the band's dedicated fanbase, known as the Skeleton Clique. It included re-recorded versions of five songs originally from the *Regional At Best* album. "One of five songs from the missing Regional At Best album that was re-recorded for the third 21 pilots album they call Vessel, which came out on January 8th, 2013."
**Blurryface (2015) – Breakthrough Album** : This fourth record, released in 2015, marked the band's major breakthrough and began a more mystical and complicated phase for the group. It explored themes of doubt, insecurity, anxiety, and religion through the character Blurryface. The album sold close to 2 million copies and produced six singles. "They blew up with the blurry face album in 2015."
**Blurryface (Limited Edition Cassette)** : A very rare and distinctive limited edition cassette version of the *Blurryface* album, notable for its bright red color. "When the album was released, it came out in the usual ways, but there was also a limited edition cassette that was very, very red."
**Blurryface (Inkblot Special Vinyl Editions)** : Highly collectible special vinyl editions of the *Blurryface* album, each uniquely produced with an inkblot design so that "no two are the same." These editions are highly sought after by fans and can trade for hundreds of dollars. "But fans really treasure the inkblot special vinyl editions."
**Blurryface (Instrumental Album)** : An instrumental version of the *Blurryface* album that mysteriously appeared in the UK. It is described as very hard to find and its official status (whether bootleg or legitimate) is uncertain. "There's also a blurry face instrumental album that popped up in the UK for some reason. It's very hard to find."
**Trench (2018) – Fifth Studio Album** : Released in 2018, this album expanded the band's intricate mythology, exploring themes of doubt, insecurity, anxiety, faith, and suicide. It details a fictional world built around the city of Dema and introduces characters like the chief bishop Niko and the rebel group, the Banditos. "The first single from Trench was jumpsuit."

## songs
**Blast for Me (2008)** : A track from Tyler Joseph's solo album, *No Fun Intended*, which he recorded around 2008 while still in high school. "This is a track called Blast for Me and dates to about 2008."
**Oh Ms Believer (2009)** : A song featured on 21 Pilots' self-titled debut album, which was released in 2009. "This is called Ole Miss Believer."
**Forest (2011)** : A song from the *Regional At Best* album, recorded at a time when 21 Pilots consisted primarily of frontman Tyler Joseph. The album, and this song along with it, were later discontinued. "Here's a sample of Regional At Best. This is called Forest."
**Guns for Hands (2012)** : This song was originally from the *Regional At Best* album and was one of two tracks re-recorded for the *Three Songs* EP, released in July 2012. It was also one of five *Regional At Best* songs that were re-recorded for the *Vessel* album. "This is Guns for Hands."
**Car Radio (2013)** : One of the successful singles from the *Vessel* album, this song was inspired by an incident where Tyler Joseph's car was broken into and his radio and CDs were stolen. This event compelled him to consider the experience of driving in silence. "It's called Car Radio and was inspired by the time somebody broke into Tyler's car and stole his radio in all the CDs."
**Stressed Out (2015)** : This song was one of six singles released from the band's breakthrough album, *Blurryface*. "Dressed out from 21 pilots' breakthrough record, blurry face."
**Chlorine (2018)** : A single from the 2018 album *Trench*, which marked the introduction of a new "weird little alien animal character" named Ned, further enriching the band's complex mythology. Its release as a single was preceded by a file posted online containing numbers that corresponded to the atomic number and atomic weight of chlorine. "21 pilots in a single from their 2018 album, Trench, that's called chlorine, which featured the debut of a weird little alien animal character called Ned."
**Jumpsuit (2018)** : The lead single from the *Trench* album, it was initially teased on July 6, 2018, via an email to fans that showed a closing eye image with the caption, "Are you still sleeping?". "The first single from Trench was jumpsuit. It was introduced on July 6, 2018 via an email to fans that featured a closing eye with a caption, Are you still sleeping?"

## curiosities
**Early Career Slogging (2009-2015)** : For six years prior to their mainstream breakthrough with the *Blurryface* album in 2015, 21 Pilots experienced "really hard slogging" during which they were largely unknown to the general public. Despite building a dedicated indie fanbase, they continued to play smaller venues. "But before that, we're six years of really hard slogging when almost no one knew who they were."
**Hidden Website Address (dmaorg.info)** : An obscure website address, dmaorg.info, can be discovered by navigating to the *Vessel* album page on the 21 Pilots online store. This site serves as a central hub for the band's elaborate mythology, featuring messages and posts from a character named Clancy. "if you go to the 21 pilots online store and navigate to the page where their third album vessel, you might be able to find a hidden website address, which is dmaorg.info."
**Chlorine Single Pre-Release Clue (2018)** : Just prior to the release of the "Chlorine" single, the character Clancy posted a file on dmaorg.info titled "17-35.4527." These numbers represent the atomic number and atomic weight of the element chlorine, serving as an elaborate pre-release hint. "He and I assume it's a he, posted a file just before the release of chlorine as a single, with the name 17-35.4527."
**Hidden Message in Text Post (2018)** : A text posted on dmaorg.info, which preceded the "Chlorine" single, was subtly altered over several days. Fans who meticulously studied these changes could decipher that the alterations spelled out the name "Ned," the new character introduced in the "Chlorine" music video. "If you were paying attention and you studied the changes, you'd have discovered that the changes spelled out the name Ned, the name of the new character in the chlorine video."
**High School Origins (Columbus, Ohio)** : Frontman Tyler Joseph grew up in Columbus, Ohio, where his mother was a teacher and his father a high school principal and basketball coach. Tyler initially excelled at basketball, playing point guard, before turning his focus to music. "At the center of everything is Tyler Joseph of Columbus, Ohio. Mom was a teacher, dad was a high school principal and basketball coach."
**Grassroots Beginnings (Ohio)** : In their formative years, 21 Pilots operated in a very independent, "do it yourself" manner, playing gigs wherever possible. Tyler Joseph's mother would promote their shows by distributing tickets outside Ohio State University games. The band also participated in battle of the bands events and performed alongside hardcore groups. "Tyler's mum would actually stand outside Ohio State University Games, giving away ticket to her kid show."
**Band Name Origin ("All My Sons")** : The band's name is derived from Arthur Miller's 1947 play, "All My Sons," which is based on a true story. The play tells of an Ohio man who knowingly approved defective aircraft engines for use in World War II, leading to the deaths of 21 pilots. "This play is called All My Sons from 1947. And it's based on a true story about a man also from Ohio who conspired with the army to approve faulty and defective aircraft engines for use in combat in World War II. And as a result of this bad equipment, 21 pilots died."
**Band Name Spelling Choice** : Tyler Joseph intentionally omits the hyphen between "20" and "1" in the band's name, prioritizing its visual design and how it appears in text or can be adapted into a logo or other graphic element. "But for Tyler, it's all about design. All about how it looks in text and how it can be adapted into a logo or other graphic design."
**Early Member Departures (2011)** : In the spring of 2011, drummer Chris Salah left the band to prioritize his job at a music store, followed a month later by bassist Nick Thomas, who departed to focus on his university studies. Nick Thomas later returned to assist with the band's merchandise but not as a performing musician. "First of all, Chris Salah felt that he needed to spend more time at work. He had a job at a music store, and that seemed like something he needed to concentrate on."
**Josh Dunn Joining (2011)** : In July 2011, Josh Dunn, a drummer who had considered pursuing studio work in Nashville, joined Tyler Joseph. Dunn was introduced by Chris Salah and was convinced to join after hearing Tyler's music. "However, Chris, remember the guy at the music store, knew someone at work named Josh Dunn. Josh was a drummer who had thought about getting some studio work in Nashville."
**Discontinuation of Regional At Best** : The album *Regional At Best* was discontinued and effectively wiped from existence as a contractual condition of 21 Pilots' signing with Fueled By Ramen in April 2012. While physical copies are now rare and can fetch prices ranging from $35 to $900 online, a double Tinderbox version is listed as being blocked from sale. "A condition of the deal was that 21 pilots had to make the Regional At Best album disappear."
**Early Fanbase in Ohio (2011-2012)** : By the time the band signed with Fueled By Ramen, 21 Pilots had cultivated a substantial fanbase within Ohio, capable of drawing nearly 2,000 attendees to their shows. However, their appeal outside the state was minimal, evidenced by one July 2011 gig attended by only 12 people. "Anyway, by this time, 21 pilots had a decent fanbase in Ohio, sometimes attracting almost 2,000 people to a show. Outside the state, not so much."
**The Skeleton Clique** : The band's dedicated fanbase is known as the Skeleton Clique, who pride themselves on being "very, very welcoming" and "the most inclusive clique ever." Members refer to each other as "frens" (spelled F-R-E-N-S) and often use the phrase "Stay Street." "And it helped build the fanbase, which by the way, had become known as the Skeleton clique."
**Blurryface Character (Doubt, Insecurity, Anxiety)** : The *Blurryface* album's central concept revolves around a character named Blurryface, who serves as an avatar for Tyler Joseph's personal struggles with doubt, insecurity, and anxiety. Tyler physically embodies this character during live performances and video shoots by sporting black paint on his hands and neck. "The first thing we need to understand about blurry face is that it's centered on themes of doubt, insecurity, anxiety and a dollop of religion."
**Blurryface Twitter Account** : An official Blurryface Twitter account exists and has amassed over 150,000 followers, despite never having posted a single tweet. "There is a blurry face Twitter account with over 150,000 followers, even though there have been zero tweets."
**New Band Logo (Blurryface Era)** : Coinciding with the *Blurryface* album, a new band logo emerged, composed of a bar, a hyphen, and a slash, all standard computer keyboard characters. Fans are encouraged to interpret and adapt this symbol into their own versions of the 21 Pilots logo. "A new band logo appeared around this time. It's a bar, a hyphen and a slash from a standard computer keyboard."
**Blurryface Album Narrative Theories** : Members of the Skeleton Clique have actively developed and shared numerous theories on Reddit, attempting to connect all 14 songs on the *Blurryface* album into a coherent narrative. "If you dip into Reddit, which can be dangerous, you'll find a number of theories that attempt to link all 14 songs together into some sort of narrative."
**Band Hiatus (2017-2018)** : After completing two world tours in support of *Blurryface*, the band entered a period of hiatus starting July 6, 2017, which lasted almost a year. This break allowed Tyler Joseph and Josh Dunn to concentrate on creating new music without any interruptions or distractions. "After a couple of world tours in support of blurry face, the band went dark, on July 6, 2017, so Tyler and Josh could focus on music without any interruption or distraction."
**Demaorg.info Re-emergence (April 2018)** : In April 2018, the dmaorg.info website was rediscovered by fans who found its address concealed within a gift image on the *Vessel* album page of the band's online store. The site's initial landing page displayed a cryptic error message warning, "404 error. You are in violation. They mustn't know you were here." "At April 2018, somebody was poking around the 21 pilots online store and found a website address hidden in a gift image on the page for the vessel album."
**Clancy's Journal (dmaorg.info)** : Following the rediscovery of dmaorg.info, a series of posts from "Clancy's journal" began to appear on the site. These extensive journal entries are filled with complex clues pertaining to the upcoming *Trench* album and its songs. Clancy is also a significant character within the *Trench* storyline. "That was followed by a series of posts at dmaorg.info from Clancy's journal. These go on forever, but they're filled with clues about the album and songs to come."
**Dema City and Mythology (Trench Album)** : The *Trench* album introduces a deeply layered world centered around the city of Dema, a name that, in the religion of Zoroastrianism, translates to "tower of silence" (a place where dead bodies are exposed to vultures). This fictional world features nine "bishops," with Niko as the chief bishop. "It describes a world built around the city of dma, which translated from the religion of Zoroastrianism, means tower of silence."
**Niko's Name Origin** : The character Niko, one of the nine bishops of Dema, derives his name from Nicholas Bourbaki, a pseudonym used by a group of French mathematicians in the 1930s. Within the *Trench* mythology, Nicholas Bourbaki is revealed to be the true identity of Blurryface, the central character from the previous album. "The name Niko is derived from Nicholas Borbacchi, which was a pseudonym used by a bunch of real-life French mathematicians back in the 1930s, who did some really esoteric things with numbers that I can't even begin to understand."
**The Banditos (Trench Mythology)** : In the fictional world of Dema, the Banditos are portrayed as the heroic figures who seek to liberate the city's inhabitants. They are uniquely identifiable by a specific shade of yellow, a color that the bishops, including Niko, are unable to perceive. "Another within dma is a group called the Banditos. They're the good guys who want to liberate the people of the city."
**Intricate World Building** : 21 Pilots is noted for creating an immersive and complex fantasy world for their fans, filled with deep mythology, intricate clues, and Easter eggs across their music and online platforms. This level of detail encourages fans to explore and become deeply involved in the band's invented universe. "If you're looking to lose yourself in this fantasy world created by the band, you will be gone for a very, very long time."

# curiosity

**Patience in Music Industry** : Success stories of bands like Red Hot Chili Peppers, Muse, R.E.M., and The Black Keys emphasize the critical importance of patience and foresight from record labels, managers, and all parties involved in an artist's welfare. If genuine potential is recognized, a long-term strategy, potentially spanning years, is required. "These success stories underscore the need for patience and foresight on behalf of record labels, managers and everyone else associated with the welfare of a particular artist."
**Artist Perseverance** : From the artist's perspective, a path to success often necessitates a willingness to endure "lean and dark times" over an extended period. Bands must be prepared to navigate challenging phases on their journey to achieving some form of breakthrough. "Is the band prepared to live through some lean and dark times on their way to some kind of success?"
**Arthur Miller's "All My Sons" (1947)** : This 1947 play by Arthur Miller, also the author of "Death of a Salesman" and "The Crucible," is based on a true story. It depicts an Ohio man who conspired with the army to approve faulty and defective aircraft engines during World War II, leading to the deaths of 21 pilots. "This play is called All My Sons from 1947. And it's based on a true story about a man also from Ohio who conspired with the army to approve faulty and defective aircraft engines for use in combat in World War II."
**Mystery in Music in the Internet Age** : The pervasive nature of the internet has significantly diminished the sense of mystery that historically surrounded music artists. In earlier times, before music videos and widespread media, knowledge of artists was limited to still pictures and album artwork, requiring fans to infer details. "The internet has destroyed a lot of the mystery surrounding music that we used to have back in the olden days."
**Zoroastrianism (Religion)** : The ancient religion of Zoroastrianism is referenced in 21 Pilots' *Trench* album mythology through the city of Dema. In Zoroastrianism, Dema refers to a "tower of silence," a structure where followers traditionally placed dead bodies to be consumed by vultures. "It describes a world built around the city of dma, which translated from the religion of Zoroastrianism, means tower of silence."
**Nicholas Bourbaki (Pseudonym)** : Nicholas Bourbaki was a collective pseudonym adopted by a group of real-life French mathematicians in the 1930s who engaged in esoteric mathematical research. This name is incorporated into the *Trench* mythology as the true identity of the character Niko, who is also the Blurryface character from the previous album. "The name Niko is derived from Nicholas Borbacchi, which was a pseudonym used by a bunch of real-life French mathematicians back in the 1930s, who did some really esoteric things with numbers that I can't even begin to understand."
