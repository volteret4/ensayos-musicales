# artist - Smash Mouth

## members
Steve Harwell

## curiosities
**Steve Harwell Death (September 4, 2023) – Alcoholism, Son's Leukemia Death, Cardiomyopathy, Age 56** : Steve Harwell struggled with alcoholism for most of his adult life, a condition that worsened after his infant son Presley died at six months from leukemia. Alcohol destroyed his liver. In 2013 he was diagnosed with cardiomyopathy (heart muscle disease); by 2015 he had Wernicke encephalopathy from alcohol damage, causing balance problems, confusion, and vision impairment. By 2021, his health made continuing with Smash Mouth impossible. Following an erratic onstage performance at a New York festival, the band issued a statement confirming he was retiring to focus on his health. In August 2023, he began hospice care at his home in Boise, Idaho. He died September 4, 2023, age 56. "The truth was, nothing could be done for Steve."


# artist - Urge Overkill

## members
Blackie O'Nassis (John Rowan)

## curiosities
**Blackie O'Nassis Death (June 13, 2023) – Drummer on Pulp Fiction's "Girl, You'll Be a Woman Soon"; Age 57, Cause Unknown** : John Rowan, who played under the name Blackie O'Nassis, was the drummer for Urge Overkill — the Chicago band whose cover of Neil Diamond's "Girl, You'll Be a Woman Soon" (1967) appears in the famous dance scene in *Pulp Fiction*. The band was also a cult favorite in the alt-rock community and opened for Nirvana on the *Nevermind* tour, during which they befriended Kurt Cobain, Chrissie Hynde of the Pretenders, and Liz Phair. Blackie left the band in 1996; there were reports of heroin use and at least one arrest. The cause of his death on June 13, 2023, at age 57 was never disclosed. "As for the cause of his death on June 13th, 2023, we have no idea."


# artist - The Associates

## members
Alan Rankine

## curiosities
**Alan Rankine Death (January 2, 2023) – Heart Disease, Age 64; Launched Snow Patrol, Belle & Sebastian, Biffy Clyro via Electric Honey Records** : Alan Rankine co-founded the Associates with Billy MacKenzie in Dundee, Scotland in 1979 — evolving out of post-punk bands and following a Bowie-like direction. A former competitive tennis player (too short at 5'8" to go professional), Rankine also worked as a production collaborator with the Cocteau Twins. After the Associates broke up, he became a lecturer at Stow College in Glasgow teaching music production, and helped students set up a record label called Electric Honey, which launched Snow Patrol, Belle & Sebastian, and Biffy Clyro. He was diagnosed with heart disease in the 2020s and died at home with family on January 2, 2023, at age 64. "He helped him set up a record label called Electric Honey, and that label launched the careers of bands like Snow Patrol, Bell and Sebastian, and Biffy Clyro."


# artist - Luscious Jackson

## members
Vivian Trimble

## curiosities
**Vivian Trimble Death (April 4, 2023) – Cancer, Age 59; Bandmate Kate Schellenbach Was Briefly in Beastie Boys** : Vivian Trimble was keyboardist for Luscious Jackson, an early 1990s New York group mixing alt-rock, rap, and pop. Their connection to the Beastie Boys ran deep: drummer Kate Schellenbach had actually been an early Beastie Boys member before producer Rick Rubin had her ousted. Luscious Jackson toured with the Beasties and became the first signing to the Beastie Boys' Grand Royal record label. Their most successful album was *Fever In Fever Out* (1996, over 500,000 copies), featuring the alt-rock radio hit "Naked Eye." Vivian left in 1998 to settle down with a family, moved to New Hampshire, and worked as a music venue booker. She was diagnosed with cancer and died April 4, 2023, age 59. "Vivian left the group in 1998 because she was tired of touring."


# artist - Television

## members
Tom Verlaine

## curiosities
**Tom Verlaine Death (January 28, 2023) – Prostate Cancer, Age 73; Helped CBGB Become Ground Zero for Punk** : Tom Verlaine co-founded Television and in 1973 helped Hilly Kristal — owner of a scuzzy bar on Bleecker Street in a then-terrible part of New York — to let Television play a Sunday night residency. This decision catalyzed CBGB into becoming ground zero for New York punk and, by chain reaction, the entire international punk movement. Television produced two critically acclaimed albums before breaking up in 1978. Verlaine subsequently collaborated with Patti Smith, worked with James Iha of the Smashing Pumpkins, had a song covered by David Bowie, and was involved in a 1992 Television reunion album. He was scheduled to produce Jeff Buckley's second album — a project ended when Buckley drowned in the Mississippi River in 1997. Verlaine was diagnosed with prostate cancer at some point in the 2000s; it metastasized. He died January 28, 2023, age 73. "That was the catalyst for making CBGB ground zero for the world of New York punk."


# artist - Jim Gordon

## curiosities
**Jim Gordon Death (March 13, 2023) – Died of Natural Causes in Psychiatric Prison at 77; Killed Mother in 1983** : Jim Gordon was a highly respected session drummer in the 1960s who played on dozens of recordings and performed with Eric Clapton in Derek and the Dominoes, including on the classic "Layla." He then developed severe schizophrenia — voices told him to starve himself, he couldn't sleep, was alcoholic, and received the wrong treatment. On June 3, 1983, a voice told him to attack his mother with a hammer and stab her to death with a butcher knife. He did. He was sentenced to prison and denied parole at least 10 times due to the severity of his mental illness; he remained in a psychiatric facility until he died of natural causes on March 13, 2023, at age 77. "A voice told him to attack his mother with a hammer and stab her to death with a butcher knife. So he did."


# artist - Pulp

## members
Steve Mackey

## curiosities
**Steve Mackey Death (March 2, 2023) – Undisclosed Illness, Age 56; Produced Florence and the Machine's Lungs; Appeared in Harry Potter** : Steve Mackey joined Pulp in 1989 and was their bassist through the glory years of the 1990s Britpop era before leaving around 2002. He became a producer and remix artist, working with Cornershop, The Kills, and The Horrors, and co-produced Florence and the Machine's debut album *Lungs*. He also worked as a music director and sound designer for institutions including the Louvre, the Museum of Modern Art, and a Shanghai museum. Harry Potter fans may know him as one of the Weird Sisters in *Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire* — a group that also featured Jarvis Cocker of Pulp and Johnny Greenwood and Phil Selway of Radiohead. When Pulp announced a reunion in early 2023, Mackey did not want to participate — in retrospect, probably because he was already ill. His death was announced March 2, 2023, having been in hospital since Christmas 2022 with an undisclosed condition. He was 56. "In retrospect, that's probably because he was ill."


# artist - The Smiths

## members
Andy Rourke

## curiosities
**Andy Rourke Death (May 18, 2023) – Pancreatic Cancer, Age 59; Sued Morrissey, Later Played Two Morrissey Singles** : Andy Rourke was a founding member of the Smiths and is considered by fans one of the greatest bassists in the history of British indie rock. His time with the band ended badly: a heroin addiction, a lawsuit against Morrissey and Johnny Marr for unpaid royalties (he was broke), and a decade later a bankruptcy over tax issues. Post-Smiths, he worked with the Pretenders, Killing Joke, Stone Roses singer Ian Brown, Dolores O'Riordan and the Cranberries, and Peter Hook of New Order. In a remarkable reconciliation, he played on two Morrissey solo singles despite the money disputes. His father and sister were both diagnosed with cancer, which prompted him to start a cancer charity; he himself was later diagnosed with pancreatic cancer. He died in a New York hospital on May 18, 2023, age 59. "Ask any Smiths fan, and they'll tell you that he was one of the greatest bassists in the history of British Indie Rock."


# artist - Sinéad O'Connor

## curiosities
**Death (July 26, 2023) – Found Unresponsive in London Apartment; Coroner Ruled Natural Causes in January 2024** : Sinéad O'Connor led a troubled life with both physical and mental health struggles from the beginning. During her career she became an icon for refusing to bend to anyone or anything, always doing everything her own way. She survived multiple suicide attempts over the years, and friends and followers were regularly concerned about her well-being. She was found unresponsive in her London apartment on July 26, 2023, and was declared dead at the scene. In January 2024, the Coroner's report determined she died of natural causes — nothing suspicious, case closed. Her first two albums are considered stone-cold classics. "She became an icon as someone who refused to bend and insisted on doing everything and I mean everything her way."


# artist - The Band

## members
Robbie Robertson

## curiosities
**Robbie Robertson Death (August 9, 2023) – Prostate Cancer, Age 80; Musical Collaborator with Martin Scorsese** : Robbie Robertson was the guitarist, musical director, and chief songwriter of the Band — a Canadian group widely credited with helping create the genre of Americana. He was also a frequent and celebrated musical collaborator with director Martin Scorsese on several of his best-known films. He died of prostate cancer on August 9, 2023, age 80. "It's interesting how a Canadian band helped create a whole new genre of music called Americana."
