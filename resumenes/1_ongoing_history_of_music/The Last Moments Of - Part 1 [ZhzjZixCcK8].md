"Her body was found in the bathroom of room 100 at the Chelsea Hotel on the morning of October 12, 1978. She'd blight out after being stabbed in the abdomen with a knife owned by Sid."

Despite having just left detox, Sid injected heroin during the party and, after guests left, found and injected another quantity of exceptionally pure heroin (perhaps 80% pure) from his mother's purse, to which his detoxified body was no longer accustomed. His breathing slowed, his heart rate fell, and he died, being found by his mother at sunrise on February 2nd next to drug paraphernalia. "It read, We had a death pact referring to Nancy. I have to keep my half of the bargain. Please bury me next to my baby. Burry me in my leather jacket, jeans, and motorcycle boots. Goodbye."

**Epilepsy Diagnosis and Medication Struggle** : Ian Curtis began experiencing seizures in late 1978, with an official diagnosis of severe epilepsy confirmed on January 23, 1979. He was prescribed a heavy medication regimen, but many of the drugs were ineffective or caused severe side effects, including extreme mood swings, and his condition worsened due to late nights, touring pressures, heavy smoking, and large amounts of alcohol. "The official diagnosis came on January 23rd, 1979. Here's classified his case as severe and a heavy regime of medication was prescribed." "Many of the drugs did not work or only work for a short period of time."
**Performance-Related Seizures** : Ian Curtis experienced grand mal seizures during at least two performances, one triggered by strobe lights and another so violent that audience members feared for his life. His condition deteriorated to the point where even percussion effects could induce an epileptic episode, leading to a minder being assigned to ensure he took his medication, avoided alcohol, and got sufficient sleep. "There were at least two occasions when he had a grand mal seizure in the middle of a performance. One triggered by strobe lights and another that was so violent, people were genuinely afraid that he was going to die."
**Personal Turmoil and Suicide Attempt** : Ian Curtis faced significant personal challenges, including a difficult relationship with his wife Deborah, who had initiated divorce proceedings, and an affair with a Belgian fan, Enneke van Array. "There was a suicide attempt on Sunday, April 6, 1980 when Ian died a bunch of pills." "He told his mates that he had a terrible claustrophobic feeling like he was caught in a whirlpool of being sucked down and drowning."
**Final Days and Suicide** : In his final days, Ian Curtis, isolated and mentally/physically unwell, watched the Werner Herzog film "Strozek," about a struggling musician, drank strong coffee and whiskey, and wrote a long note to his estranged wife Deborah, with pictures of his wife and baby daughter nearby. On Sunday, May 18, 1980, at approximately 11 AM, Deborah returned home and found Ian had hung himself in the kitchen using an old-fashioned clothes rack. "Once Deborah was gone, Ian sat down to watch a movie on the telly. A 1977 Werner Herzog film entitled Strozek." "And within seconds, she ran into the street, screaming. **Posthumous Headstone and Theft** : Ian Curtis's body was cremated after his funeral on May 23rd, and a headstone was placed in Macklesfield Cemetery bearing the inscription "Ian Curtis, 18580, Love Will Terrace Apart." # artist - Bob Marley

## curiosities
**Assassination Attempt (December 1976)** : On the evening of December 3, 1976, around 8:30 PM, seven or eight armed men forcibly entered Bob Marley's house at 56 Hope Road outside of Kingston, Jamaica. Marley was shot twice, and his wife, manager, and a band employee also sustained gunshot wounds, though fortunately, no one died. The attack was reportedly orchestrated by elements within the Jamaican Labor Party (JLP) who perceived Marley as too politically progressive and influential, with some even suggesting CIA involvement. "Around 830 on the evening of December 3, 1976, 7 or 8 armed men burst into his house at 56 Hope Road outside of Kingston, Jamaica. Marley was shot twice."
**Bullet Retention for Guitar Playing** : Following the assassination attempt, a lead bullet remained lodged in Bob Marley's upper left arm. He chose not to have it removed, fearing that the removal surgery, due to its location, might cause damage that would make it difficult or impossible for him to play the guitar. "He could have had it removed, but he chose not to. Why? Well, because of its location, we're moving it might have caused damage that would have made it hard if not impossible to play the guitar, so it stayed."
**Toe Injury Leading to Cancer (May 1977)** : On May 9, 1977, in Paris, while playing soccer with bandmates between gigs, Bob Marley injured his big right toe when a French journalist wearing spikes stepped on it. This toe had been previously injured multiple times (by broken glass, a rock, almost cut off) and consistently struggled to heal, becoming a recurring issue. "One of the people he was playing with was a French journalist, and he was wearing spikes, and that guy stepped on Bob's foot. He injured his big right toe, and it didn't really seem to want to heal."
**Acral Lentiginous Melanoma Diagnosis and Refusal of Amputation (July 1977)** : In July 1977, a doctor in London diagnosed Bob Marley with acral lentiginous melanoma, a rare form of skin cancer discovered under his toenail that was not well documented in medical texts at the time. Despite the doctors' advice for toe amputation, Marley refused, citing his Rastafarian belief that removing any body part would be a sin, opting instead for a skin graft from his thigh performed by a Miami specialist. "In July, he visited a doctor in London, and they discovered he had something called Acro-leteginus melanoma, a pre-existing form of skin cancer under the nail that at the time wasn't well covered in the medical textbooks." "But Bob refused, saying that as a rastifarian, it would be a sin to have any body part removed."
**Central Park Collapse (September 1980)** : Before his second show at Madison Square Garden on September 20, 1980, while running with his friend Alan Cole in Central Park, Bob Marley experienced altered vision, difficulty speaking, tightened legs, and searing neck pain, culminating in a multi-minute seizure and collapse. Despite this, he insisted on checking himself out of the hospital to perform the concert that night. "But before the second show, Bob went running with his friend Alan Cole in Central Park. His vision began to alter. He had trouble speaking. Suddenly his legs tightened, and he felt a searing pain in his neck. He collapsed and went into a seizure that lasted for several minutes."
**Widespread Cancer and Unsuccessful Treatments (September 1980)** : After collapsing in Central Park, Bob Marley reported back to the hospital on September 26, 1980, where doctors discovered that the melanoma had spread from his toe to his brain, forming a massive tumor, and also to his liver and lungs. He underwent chemotherapy and radiation treatments at Sloan Kettering Hospital, which caused his dreadlocks to fall out, leading to his wife giving him his first haircut in years. However, these conventional treatments proved ineffective. "doctors reported that the cancer had spread from his toe to his brain. There was a massive tumor. They also found cancer in his liver and lungs." "But the chemo and radiation did not work. There was nothing more they could do."
**Experimental Treatments with Dr. Joseph Issels (November 1980 - February 1981)** : Discharged with only weeks to live, Bob Marley sought alternative treatments in Europe from Dr. Joseph Issels, a controversial figure described as a former Nazi who worked for Josef Mengele but resigned after refusing orders to stop treating Jewish patients. In Bavaria in November 1980, Marley, though weak, underwent Issels' experimental methods, including a special macrobiotic diet and drug-induced fevers using toxins to stimulate the immune system, along with the removal of decaying teeth and tonsils. By February 1981, after his 36th birthday, Issels attempted painful ozone injections into his navel, which only produced epileptic-like seizures and proved unsuccessful. "Through one of his Rastafarian contacts in Jamaica, he'd heard of a doctor named Joseph Isles, a former Nazi apparently, a guy who used to work for Joseph Mangalup." "Isles was offering some experimental cancer treatments."
**Death in Miami (May 1981)** : On May 8, 1981, Bob Marley decided to return home to Jamaica and asked Chris Blackwell, head of Island Records, to fly him there. However, his vital signs worsened during the flight, forcing the plane to land in Miami, where he was taken to Cedars of Lebanon Hospital. Critically ill and in immense pain, he died on May 11, 1981, surrounded by his family and weighing only 82 pounds, at the age of 36. "He was critically ill and in a tremendous amount of pain. But there was nothing the doctors could do except offer large amounts of powerful pain killers. And then, surrounded by his family and weighing just 82 pounds, Bob Marley died. It was May 11, 1981."
**Last Words to Son Ziggy** : Bob Marley's final words, spoken to his son Ziggy while surrounded by family, were a poignant message of support and expectation: "on your way up, take me up. On your way down, don't let me down." "He said, on your way up, take me up. On your way down, don't let me down."
