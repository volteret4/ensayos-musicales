# artist - Lou Reed

## labels
RCA Records

## albums
**Metal Machine Music (1975) – Double Album of Pure Guitar Feedback Delivered as Contract FU** : Unhappy with RCA Records and contractually obligated to deliver one more album, Lou Reed recorded a double album of nothing but guitar feedback in his New York apartment: two guitars given weird tunings, placed in front of amplifiers and left to feed back on themselves for 64 minutes, recorded on a small 4-track machine. Side four of the vinyl featured a locked groove that played infinitely. RCA retaliated by actually releasing it, hoping to destroy his career — and it did, sending his career into a decade-long decline. The record was later reissued on CD in 2000, re-evaluated as an important milestone in industrial, ambient, drone, and doom music. A German ensemble called Zeichkratzer performed and recorded it live around the turn of the millennium, arranged for strings, wind instruments, piano, and accordion. "What began as a really dumb career move turned into revered art."


# artist - Elvis Costello

## curiosities
**Columbus Holiday Inn Bar Fight (March 10, 1979) – Racist Remarks, Punch from Bonnie Bramlett, Career Nearly Ruined in America** : On March 10, 1979, Elvis Costello and his entourage were drinking heavily in a bar at a Holiday Inn in Columbus, Ohio, when they encountered Stephen Stills's party. Costello made blatant racist remarks about Ray Charles and James Brown. Stephen Stills's companion Bonnie Bramlett punched Costello, a full brawl broke out, and the incident made national news. American media sided with Stills; Costello became toxic and his American career took years to recover. He later described the incident in his memoirs *Unfaithful Music and Disappearing Ink* (2015) and acknowledged it without denial or excuse. "An old barfight from 37 years ago — but there are real serious things that haven't been fixed that are not my doing or my responsibility."


# artist - New Order

## curiosities
**The Haçienda Nightclub (1982–1997) – Millions Lost, Band Driven to Near-Bankruptcy** : In 1982, New Order were convinced to invest in building and running a nightclub in their Manchester hometown called the Haçienda. Having no knowledge of club management, they poured millions of pounds into the venture across 15 years, nearly bankrupting themselves. All their New Order and Joy Division album revenues were effectively swallowed by it. The club was culturally important as ground zero for the rave and dance culture explosion of the late 1980s, but was plagued by financial losses, security problems, and drug-related deaths (patrons preferring ecstasy over alcohol made the bar nearly unviable). It closed permanently in June 1997 and was converted to condominiums. Bassist Peter Hook wrote a book about the experience called *How Not to Run a Club*. "The profits of which were largely lost in the sinkhole that was the Haçienda nightclub."


# artist - The Clash

## members
Mick Jones
Joe Strummer

## curiosities
**Firing of Mick Jones (September 1, 1983) – Manager-Engineered Split That Destroyed the Band** : Manager Bernie Rhodes had spent years pitting Joe Strummer against Mick Jones, encouraging Joe to fire him. On September 1, 1983, Mick Jones's departure was announced in a formal "Clash Communiqué" stating he had "drifted apart from the original idea of the Clash." The resulting lineup recorded the critically panned *Cut the Crap* (1985), featuring two new members, after which the Clash simply dissolved with no official announcement. Mick Jones went on to major success with Big Audio Dynamite, while Strummer spent over a decade in record-company limbo and never again achieved the stature he had with the Clash. "Firing Mick Jones? Terrible. Joe Strummer thought he knew better than Mick, and he ended up paying for it."

## albums
**Cut the Crap (1985) – Critically Panned Post-Jones Album Effectively Ended the Band** : Recorded without Mick Jones and featuring two new members under heavy influence from manager Bernie Rhodes, this album was critically destroyed and a commercial failure. The band dissolved shortly after without a formal announcement. "Critically panned and a gigantic commercial disappointment."


# artist - Ramones

## curiosities
**Dee Dee King Rap Career (1987) – Hip-Hop Single "Funky Man" as Career Nadir** : In 1987, founding Ramones bassist Dee Dee Ramone abruptly left the band to pursue a solo hip-hop career under the name Dee Dee King, recording a track called "Funky Man." The attempt was widely considered disastrous. He tried to re-enter punk rock but never achieved significant success in any subsequent project. He died of a heroin overdose on June 5, 2002. "Dee Dee Ramon, the guy who played bass in all those legendary early Ramones songs, trying to be a rapper. Terrible career move, dude."


# artist - Nirvana

## members
Jason Everman

## curiosities
**Jason Everman – Paid for Bleach, Joined Nirvana, Quit, Joined Soundgarden, Quit, Became Special Forces Soldier** : Jason Everman financed the entire $606.17 recording cost of Nirvana's *Bleach* album as a favor and was falsely credited in the liner notes as having played on it. He later genuinely joined the band as second guitarist in February 1989, made his debut on February 25, 1989, and was dismissed or quit after a final show at the Pyramid Club in New York City on July 18, 1989. He subsequently joined Soundgarden as bassist, appearing on the EP *Loudest Love* (1990) and a world tour before leaving again. He then joined the US Army, served multiple tours in Afghanistan and Iraq as an elite Special Forces soldier comparable to SEAL Team 6, received an honorable discharge in 2006, enrolled in Columbia University to study philosophy, and now lives quietly in rural Washington State. "Two of the biggest grunge bands of all time, and he left both of them."


# artist - Sinead O'Connor

## curiosities
**SNL Pope John Paul II Photo Tear (October 3, 1992) – Career-Ending Television Moment** : After a series of controversies (refusing to appear on SNL while Andrew Dice Clay was host in 1990; refusing the national anthem before a New Jersey show in August 1990; skipping the 1991 Grammys despite four nominations; a perceived pro-Saddam comment in March 1991), Sinead O'Connor appeared on *Saturday Night Live* on October 3, 1992, singing Bob Marley's "War." In rehearsal she held a picture of a refugee child; live, she substituted a photo of Pope John Paul II — taken from her mother's bedroom wall — and tore it up at the word "evil," saying "fight the real enemy." There was complete silence from the studio audience. NBC received nearly 5,000 complaints and the resulting media blowback permanently ended her mainstream career. The inspiration was Bob Geldof tearing up a picture of John Travolta on *Top of the Pops* in the 1970s. "NBC received almost 5,000 complaints. The blowback in the media was intense and her career never, ever recovered."


# artist - Happy Mondays

## members
Shaun Ryder
Bez

## albums
**Yes Please (1992) – Most Expensive Indie Record Never Properly Made** : The Happy Mondays traveled to Barbados to record at Eddie Grant's Blue Wave Studios with Tina Weymouth and Chris Frantz (Talking Heads) producing. Shaun Ryder's methadone supply (four weeks' worth in 500ml vials) was dropped and shattered at the airport before departure. In Barbados he found crack cocaine as a substitute, eventually consuming up to 30 rocks a day (at 40 cents each vs. $50 back home). Bez broke his arm in a rented Jeep accident; Ryder drove another rental car through the front of a bar; keyboardist Paul Davis wrecked three rental cars; total vehicles destroyed numbered more than ten; band members sold Eddie Grant's furniture to buy more drugs. Tony Wilson flew in to find Shaun and Bez pushing a couch down the road to trade it for crack. Only one song was fully completed; Ryder forgot to write lyrics. The project cost £400,000. The one released single, "Stinking Thinking," peaked at number 31 in the UK. Factory Records filed for bankruptcy in November 1992, six weeks after the album's release. "Four hundred thousand pounds down the drain. The most expensive indie record never made."


# artist - Queens of the Stone Age

## members
Nick Oliveri

## curiosities
**Nick Oliveri Fired, SWAT Standoff (July 12, 2011) – Domestic Abuse Leading to Career Ejection** : Nick Oliveri was initially fired from Queens of the Stone Age after being physically abusive to his girlfriend. After a truce appeared to be arranged, a SWAT team descended on his house on July 12, 2011, following an assault on his girlfriend. He was arrested on charges including drugs and weapons, faced up to 15 years in prison, and received a plea deal for probation and community service. Despite this, he can be heard as a contributor on the band's 2013 album *…Like Clockwork*, though he is no longer a full member. "Even though he faced up to 15 years in jail, he got a plea deal for probation and community service."


# artist - Chris Cornell

## albums
**Scream (2007) – Hip-Hop Collaboration with Timbaland That Debuted at #10 and Dropped 55 Places** : After Audioslave broke up, Cornell teamed with hip-hop producer Timbaland for a solo album that replaced guitars and rock with beats and a pop/hip-hop approach. The album debuted at number 10 on the charts and then dropped 55 spots the following week — one of the biggest single-week falls from the top 10 in chart history. Even Trent Reznor publicly commented that Cornell had embarrassed himself. Cornell was eventually able to recover from the setback and reunite Soundgarden. "Even Trent Reznor felt he had to say something, quoted as saying that Chris had embarrassed himself."


# artist - Dexys Midnight Runners

## members
Kevin Rowland

## albums
**My Beauty (1999) – Solo Album That Sold 500 Copies Due to Controversial Cover** : Singer Kevin Rowland insisted on posing for the cover of his solo album wearing heavy makeup, panties, and stockings. The image alienated his fanbase so thoroughly that the record initially sold just 500 copies. It has since sold only around 20,000 total, and many of those purchases are attributed to curiosity about the cover image. "The image was such a turn-off to his fans that this record initially sold just 500 copies."
