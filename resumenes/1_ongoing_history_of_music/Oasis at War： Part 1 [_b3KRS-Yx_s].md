## albums
**Definitely Maybe (1994) – Debut Album** : This album featured "Columbia," which was the first track released by Oasis with Noel Gallagher in charge. It marked the formal beginning of the band's successful trajectory. "It would end up on the debut album definitely maybe."
**Be Here Now (1997) – Third Album** : Recorded during a period of heavy cocaine use among the band members, which significantly impacted its sound. Noel Gallagher, influenced by cocaine, insisted on layering many guitars and boosting the high end, resulting in a very long album (71 minutes) that he later recognized as excessively long. Constant low-level fighting between Noel and Liam also characterized these sessions. "Cocaine messes with your hearing and your audio perception. Noel kept wanting to make the record sound bigger and louder."

## songs
**Columbia (1993) – First Release with Noel** : This was the first ever release from Oasis after Noel Gallagher joined as lead guitarist, appearing as a promo single in 1993. It was later included on their debut album, "Definitely Maybe." "Here's the first thing Oasis released with Noel in charge. It's a 1993 promo single called Columbia."
**Do You Know What I Mean? (1997)** : A song from the "Be Here Now" album released in 1997, marking a period of intense internal conflict and drug use within the band during its creation. "Oasis. And do you know what I mean? From the Be Here Now album in 1997."

## curiosities
**Noel Gallagher Joins Rain, Band Becomes Oasis (1991)** : After a gig by Liam's band Rain in 1991, Liam and his bandmates asked Noel to manage them. Noel declined the management offer but instead proposed joining as their lead guitarist, citing his years of songwriting and the superior quality of his material compared to Rain's songs. The band accepted, initiating the transformation of Rain into Oasis. "The van accepted, and that's when Rain started the Metamorphosis into Oasis."
**Early Dictatorial Tendencies of Noel Gallagher** : From the outset of Noel joining Rain (which became Oasis), his dictatorial nature created tension with Liam and other band members. An early instance involved Noel using a record company advance to buy himself new guitars, despite the band needing other essential gear. "For example, there was a time when he took record company of money in advance and bought himself new guitars when the group needed all kinds of other gear."
**Joiners Arms Incident – Noel Punches Liam (March 29, 1994)** : The first publicly documented physical altercation between Noel and Liam occurred at a show at the Joiners Arms club in Southampton. For an undisclosed reason, Noel punched Liam in the face and then chased him off stage. "The first documented evidence of a public war between Noel and Liam was at a show at a club called Joiners Arms in Southampton on March 29, 1994."
**NME Interview – First Major Media Feature (April 1994)** : Approximately a month after the Joiners Arms incident, Noel and Liam were interviewed by the NME at a Glasgow hotel, their first major feature in the British music media. The interview, recorded by John Harris, quickly devolved into a heated argument, showcasing their volatile relationship. "This was their first major feature in the British music media."
**NME Interview Released as a Single (1994)** : The chaotic NME interview between Noel and Liam, which occurred in a Glasgow hotel, was later released as a single. This single reached number 52 on the British charts, demonstrating the early fascination the UK public had with the brothers' contentious relationship. "That interview was later released as a single and it reached number 52 on the British charts, which just goes to show you how much the UK was fascinated by this relationship this early in the band's career."
**First North American Tour – Offensive Lyrics and Whisky a Go-Go Fight (Fall 1994)** : During Oasis's first North American tour, Liam began changing song lyrics to be offensive, greatly annoying Noel, as they were his compositions. This culminated on September 29, 1994, at the Whisky a Go-Go in Los Angeles, where Noel's insistence on singing harmonies annoyed Liam, leading to multiple on-stage confrontations and Liam hitting Noel with a tambourine, refusing to return for the encore. "Liam started by changing the words of some of the songs into something rather offensive. This greatly annoyed noelle because of course they were his songs."
**Whisky a Go-Go Aftermath – Noel Leaves Tour (September 29, 1994)** : Following the fight at the Whisky a Go-Go, Noel flew to Las Vegas and then San Francisco with $8,000 of the band's money to cool off, resulting in the cancellation of over half a dozen shows. He only rejoined the band in Austin, Texas, after which the band nearly broke up. It was later revealed that the entire band, including both brothers, was using crystal meth at the time. "Noelle, while he headed to the airport with $8,000 of the band's money."
**Crystal Meth Use on First North American Tour (1994)** : During their first North American tour, the entire band, including Noel and Liam Gallagher, was heavily using crystal meth. This drug use contributed to the intense conflicts and instability that led to Noel temporarily leaving the tour after the Whisky a Go-Go incident. "What wasn't mentioned at the time was how the whole band, including Liam and Noelle, were off their nuts on crystal meth."
**Rockfield Studios Fight – Cricket Bat Incident (May 1995)** : While recording their second album at Rockfield Studios in Wales, Liam brought a large, drunk group from a pub back to the studio, leading to damage. Noel locked the studio doors, embarrassing Liam. Later, Liam battered Noel's bedroom door, breaking his foot. Noel then grabbed a cricket bat, opened the door, and allegedly broke Liam's arm with it, subsequently leaving the studio for a week to calm down. "Noelle then grabbed a cricket bat, opened the door, and started walking Liam with it."
**Noel's Cricket Bat Auction (1995)** : The cricket bat that Noel Gallagher allegedly used to hit Liam Gallagher during the Rockfield Studios incident in May 1995 later came into the possession of music writer Paolo Huitt. Huitt subsequently auctioned the bat, complete with a certificate of authenticity, for approximately a thousand US dollars. "He later auctioned it off, complete with a certificate of authenticity. And I think it sold for about a thousand US dollars."
**MTV Unplugged No-Show – Noel Sings (August 23, 1996)** : Liam Gallagher backed out of an MTV Unplugged performance at the Royal Festival Hall at the last second, claiming a sore throat, which Noel dismissed as a hangover. With no time to cancel, Noel stepped up to sing the entire acoustic set himself, receiving positive reviews. Liam watched from the balcony, drinking beer and loudly heckling the band, even spitting beer towards the stage. "If you won't sing my songs, I will sing them. Noelle had already made an acoustic set part of the band show, so stepping up to sing the full set list wasn't that big of a deal for him."
**Heathrow Airport No-Show – "Gotta Buy a House" (August 25, 1996)** : Just two days after the MTV Unplugged incident, Oasis was departing from Heathrow airport for another North American tour. Liam made it to the plane but, while on the phone with his wife Patsy Kensit, suddenly declared he "can't go, gotta go buy a house" and left the plane. This resulted in the cancellation of their first major headlining gig in North America at the 16,000-seat Rosemont Horizon in Chicago. "Suddenly he says, can't go, gotta go buy a house. He left the plane."
**MTV Music Video Awards Performance – Deliberate Off-Key Singing (September 4, 1996)** : At the MTV Music Video Awards in New York, Liam Gallagher deliberately sang off-key and made annoyed gestures towards Noel while he played. He then spat beer all over the stage before storming off, actions that further damaged Oasis's reputation and prospects in the United States. "Liam deliberately sang off key. As Noel played, Liam gestured towards him in some kind of annoyed way. And then he spat beer all over the stage and stormed off."
**Charlotte, North Carolina Fight – Noel Flies Home Alone (September 1996)** : Following a series of incidents that impacted Oasis's reputation in the US, another fight broke out in Charlotte, North Carolina, during the ongoing North American tour. This particular altercation led to Noel Gallagher flying home alone, once again needing time to cool off, and marking another significant disruption for the band. "The tour continued for a little while longer, but then there was another fight in Charlotte, North Carolina. And Noel ended up flying home alone."
**Be Here Now Recording – Constant Low-Level Fighting (1997)** : During the recording sessions for their third album, "Be Here Now," Noel and Liam were in a constant state of low-level conflict. While there were no dramatic "giant blowups," those present described it as a persistent, unyielding tension, like a "long low-level seizure," exacerbated by widespread cocaine use. "There were no giant blowups, but those who were there say it was like one of those long low-level seizures that just did not let up."

## curiosities
**Childhood Antagonism Towards Noel** : From a young age, Liam exhibited a cocky, extroverted, and antagonistic personality. He was even considered "kind of psycho" by some schoolmates. His older brother, Noel, was a frequent target of his aggression throughout childhood and his teen years, a behavior attributed by those close to him to emotional scars from their abusive father. "And of the two, Liam was probably the more antagonistic. He was this cocky, super extrovert."
**Expulsion from School (Age 15)** : Along with his brother Noel, Liam was expelled from school at the age of 15 due to a pattern of bad behavior that included petty thefts, skipping school, minor rebellions, fights, and sniffing glue. "Both were kicked out of school at age 15."
**Accusation of Crystal Meth Use Against Noel (1994)** : After Noel Gallagher left the first North American tour with $8,000 of the band's money following a fight, Liam accused Noel of taking the band's crystal meth with him. This comment highlighted the band's drug use during that period and the underlying animosity between the brothers. "But I think he took the band of crystal meth with him as well as the money."

## curiosities
**Childhood Trauma and Quiet Demeanor** : Born in 1967, two years after Paul and five years before Liam, Noel shared a room with Liam while Paul had his own. He and Paul developed stuttering problems as children due to their alcoholic and abusive father, Tommy. While he was a "bad kid" who engaged in petty thefts and was kicked out of school at 15, he eventually became more quiet and moody, in contrast to Liam's overt antagonism. "Paul and Noel were so freaked out by this that they developed stuttering problems when they were young."
**Expulsion from School (Age 15)** : Noel was expelled from school at the age of 15 due to a history of misconduct, including petty thefts, skipping school, minor rebellions, fights, and sniffing glue, mirroring his younger brother Liam's educational trajectory. "Both were kicked out of school at age 15."
**Pranks on Liam – Moving Furniture** : As children, Noel frequently pranked Liam by moving furniture around their shared bedroom while Liam slept. He would then convince a terrified Liam that ghosts were responsible for the rearrangement, a practice he maintained for years. "One of the things Noel liked to do was to freak out Liam by moving furniture around the house as he slept. And then he would tell Liam that ghosts were doing it."
**Roadie for Inspiral Carpets (1988)** : By 1988, after moving out of his family home, Noel Gallagher secured a job as a roadie for the band Inspiral Carpets. This experience gave him insight into the music industry before he joined Liam's band, Rain, which later became Oasis. "Noel eventually got a job as a roadie with the Inspiral Carpets."
**Response to Liam's MTV Unplugged No-Show (August 23, 1996)** : When Liam Gallagher claimed a sore throat to skip Oasis's MTV Unplugged performance, Noel angrily dismissed it as a hangover, declaring, "Bollocks do your throat, you're just hung over and you're being a prat." He then took over lead vocals for the entire set, performing his own songs. "'Right then, Bollocks do your throat, you're just hung over and you're being a prat. If you won't sing my songs, I will sing them.'"
