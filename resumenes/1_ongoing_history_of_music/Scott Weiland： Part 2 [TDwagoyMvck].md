## albums
**12 Bar Blues (1998) – Solo Debut Album** : Released in May 1998, this album was recorded with the help of Daniel Lanois, and Scott Wyland played all the instruments himself. He described it as "artistic time away from STP" and while it wasn't a major hit, it garnered kind reviews and showcased a side of Wyland few knew existed, featuring Cheryl Crowe on guitar for one track. "Scott played everything himself."

## curiosities
**1990s Era Voice** : Scott Wyland's voice was recognized as one of the great voices of the 1990s, making a significant impact on listeners despite his well-known personal struggles. "If you grew up in the 1990s, his was one of the great voices of that era."
**Heroin Addiction Discovery (1993)** : Following the success of Stone Temple Pilots' debut album "Core," it was discovered that Scott Wyland had become a heroin addict while the band was in the midst of selling millions of records. "STP signing their major label record deal, going on tour, and then finding out that their lead singer had become a heroin addict while the band was selling millions of records."
**First Detox Attempt (Spring 1994) – Exodus Recovery Center** : In spring 1994, Wyland intended to check into the Exodus Recovery Center in Marina del Rey for detox. He was advised against it because two other "major junkies," Gibby Haynes of the Butthole Surfers (who allegedly introduced Wyland to heroin) and Kurt Cobain, were already present. "There are two major junkies already on the premises and that will not help you kick your habit."
**Marriage to Janina Costenda (September 17, 1994)** : Scott Wyland married his longtime girlfriend, Janina Costenda, in a lavish ceremony held in a wealthy area of Pasadena on September 17, 1994. "And on September 17th, 1994, he and his longtime girlfriend, Janina Costenda, were married in a lavish ceremony in a rich area of Pasadena."
**Wedding Day Drug Use (September 17, 1994)** : Prior to his wedding ceremony, Scott Wyland consumed cocaine in the limo with some of his groomsmen. "Scott bit a little coke in the limo with some of his groomsmen before the ceremony, but Emes is wedding day."
**Post-Wedding Life (1994)** : After their honeymoon, Scott and Janina purchased the house where their wedding took place and began accumulating antiques and cars. "After the honeymoon, Scott and Janina bought that house, the house where they actually had the wedding and began collecting antiques and cars."
**Lying to His Wife While on Tour (1994)** : While on tour and addicted to heroin, Scott frequently called Janina and falsely claimed he was not using drugs. Despite feeling guilty about his deception, he continued his drug use. "He told her he wasn't using, which was a lie of course, and even though he felt bad about lying, he didn't stop using it."
**Rumors of Drug Problem (End of 1994)** : By the end of 1994, rumors began to circulate widely regarding Scott Wyland's condition, with many hinting at a significant drug problem and other "dark things." "By the end of 1994, rumors had begun to swirl about Scott's condition."
**Pasadena Drug Bust (May 15, 1995)** : At approximately 3:30 AM on Monday, May 15, 1995, in Pasadena, California, Scott Wyland was apprehended by police after being seen leaving the rear of a CD motel with his lights off. Officers, suspecting a drug transaction, found a chunk of Mexican black tar heroin in his wallet upon apprehension. "When they caught up with Scott, the cops found a chunk of Mexican black tar heroin in Wyland's wallet."
**Post-Arrest Drug Seeking (May 15, 1995)** : Immediately after being released on $10,000 bail (posted by his wife), Scott Wyland demanded that Janina drive him to his dealer's house to "score" drugs. When she refused, he jumped out of their 1965 Mustang while it was moving at 15-20 miles per hour. "When she refused, Scott jumped out of the car while it was still moving at 15 or 20 miles an hour."
**Seeking Solace with Courtney Love (May 1995)** : After Janina refused to allow him back home following his arrest and subsequent behavior, Scott Wyland sought refuge with Courtney Love, who was staying at the Chateau Marmont Hotel on Sunset Boulevard. "When Jeanine refused to let Scott come home, he sought solace in of all people, Courtney Love, who had a room with the Chateau Marmont Hotel on sunset bullifard."
**Public Statement of Remorse (May 1995)** : While at the Chateau Marmont, Scott Wyland prepared a tearful statement of remorse which Courtney Love then called a local radio station in the middle of the night to read live on air. In the statement, he apologized to his friends, band, wife, family, and acknowledged becoming a "hypocrite" to social ideals, expressing a desire to recover and create more music. "I have a disease it went. I want to say sorry to my friends, my band, my wife, and my family and to the social ideals to which I have become a hypocrite. I ache to get well, to feel, and to make more music."
**Drug Treatment Agreement (May 1995)** : Following his May 1995 drug bust, Wyland was informed by the court that he could avoid a criminal record if he successfully completed a drug treatment program, a condition he agreed to. "Wyland was told that if he completed a drug treatment program, he would be free to go without a criminal record."
**Rehab in Colorado (Summer 1995)** : Scott Wyland spent part of the summer of 1995 at a rehabilitation facility located in Colorado, returning to Los Angeles by the end of August that year. "He agreed and spent part of that summer at a rehab facility in Colorado."
**Repeated Relapses and Rehab Stays (1994-1997)** : Between 1994 and 1997, Scott Wyland entered rehabilitation facilities a staggering thirteen times, indicative of his persistent struggles with addiction and repeated relapses. "Between 1994 and 1997, he went to rehab 13 times."
**First Marriage Ends (1996)** : His marriage to Janina Costenda ended because she "just couldn't take it anymore" amidst his ongoing drug issues and legal troubles. "And to make matters even worse, his marriage to Janina was over."

Before officers arrived, friends intervened and sent him to a hospital, from where he was transferred to the medical unit of the LA County Jail. "What actually had happened was that Wyland had missed one of his mandatory drug counseling sessions and police moved in to arrest him on April 23, 1996."
**Escape from Rehab (June 26, 1996)** : After being transferred to Impact House, a residential drug treatment facility, for what was supposed to be a six-month stay, Scott Wyland disappeared on June 26, 1996. An arrest warrant was issued for him. "But then on June 26th, Wyland disappeared."
**Return to Court (1996)** : Before he could be located by the warrant, Scott Wyland reappeared "safe and sober" at the Pasadena Municipal Court, explaining his disappearance as a need to speak with his wife about a personal problem. "Wyland turned up safe and sober at the Pasadena Municipal Court."

"One dumb rumor was that he had bolted for Detroit so he could attend a kiss concert."

**$6 Million Drug Spending (1994-1998)** : In May 1998, Scott Wyland candidly revealed that he had spent over $6 million on drugs between 1994 and early 1998. "He spoke candidly of his drug problems, declaring that he had spent over $6 million on drugs between 1994 and early 1998."
**New Management and Sobriety Efforts (1998)** : A new management company took over Scott Wyland's affairs, ensuring he attended Narcotics Anonymous meetings. He was picked up leaving 20 Avenue D on Manhattan's Lower East Side, a building known for housing drug dealers, where police found ten $10 decks of heroin (valued at $100 each). "They found ten decks of heroin, ten dollar packages of smack, that were worth about a hundred dollars a piece."

**Missed LA Court Dates (June/July 1998)** : Judge Larry Fiddler in Los Angeles, assigned to Wyland's case, required him to appear at court hearings to monitor his sobriety. Wyland missed a June 11 hearing (excused as he was in rehab) and a subsequent early June appearance, prompting Judge Fiddler to issue a $250,000 arrest warrant. "Wylandt missed the first hearing on June 11th, but he had an excuse. He was in a drug rehab facility."

Upon hearing about the $250,000 arrest warrant, he vanished, knowing he would be arrested if he entered the building. "He knew that he'd be arrested the second he put inside, so he vanished."
**13 Days on the Run (July 1998)** : Scott Wyland was on the run for almost thirteen days, with a continent-wide APB (all-points bulletin) issued for his arrest. His manager and lawyer refused to disclose his whereabouts during this period. "Wylandt was on the land for almost 13 days."
**LA Drug Purchase Arrest (July 22, 1998)** : At 6:30 PM on Tuesday, July 22, 1998, Scott Wyland was arrested at 6th and Burlington Street near downtown Los Angeles for simply buying drugs, rather than on the outstanding arrest warrant. Drugs were found on his person. "The police weren't looking for him because of that arrest warrant. They picked him up because he was simply buying drugs."
**Dual Coast Legal Troubles (1998)** : Following his July 1998 arrest, Scott Wyland faced drug felony charges in both California and New York, necessitating his legal team to coordinate multiple court dates and hearings across the country. "Wylandt was up on drug felony charges in both California and New York."

Judge Thomas Farber and District Attorney Christine Hatfield agreed to postpone the hearing until November 4, 1998. ## albums
**Core (1993) – Debut Record** : The band's debut album, released in 1993, which featured the hit song "Plush." The album's success marked the period when the band signed their major label record deal and began touring. "It's apparently a song about death, inspired by a newspaper story about a girl who'd been found dead somewhere in San Diego."
**Purple (1994) – Second Album** : Recorded in Atlanta during a break in the band's touring schedule, this album was completed rather quickly in four weeks, partly due to Scott Wyland's drug use allowing him to avoid withdrawal symptoms. Released on June 7, 1994, it showcased a sound that was "less grunge and more straight ahead all rock" compared to their debut. "Purple was released on June 7th, 1994, and I remember when it came out thinking, wow, this sounds a lot different than the first album."
**Purple (1994) – Chart Performance** : "Purple" debuted at number one on the charts in the US, also reaching number one in Australia, number two in Canada, and number ten in the UK, indicating significant international success. "Hype was such that the album debuted at number one on the charts in the US, and made it as high as number one in Australia, number two in Canada, number ten in the UK."
**Tiny Music Songs from the Vatican Gift Shop (1996) – Third Album** : Released on March 26, 1996, this album represented a further evolution in the band's sound, described as even more distinct from "Purple." The band aimed for a stripped-back, "glam-esque" feel, with Scott Wyland consciously attempting to emulate David Bowie in his melodies and lyrics. "Tiny Music was released on March 26, 1996."
**Tiny Music Songs from the Vatican Gift Shop (1996) – Sales Performance** : While "Tiny Music" produced big radio hits, its store sales were less impressive, selling about a third of what "Purple" achieved and a quarter of "Core," totaling approximately 2 million copies. "It sold about a third of what purple did and a quarter of the total they saw with core."
**Number 4 (1999) – Fourth Album** : This album was released on October 26, 1999, following the band's reunion and their entry into the studio with producer Brendan O'Brien in February 1999. The first song they recorded for the album was "Down." "It was released on October 26, 1999."

## songs
**Plush (1993)** : From the band's 1993 debut album "Core," "Plush" is reportedly a song about death, inspired by a newspaper story concerning a girl found dead in San Diego. It won a Grammy Award for Best Hard Rock Performance and remains a staple on radio station playlists worldwide. "It won a Grammy for Best Hard Rock Performance and is still a part of radio station playlist around the world."
**Big Empty (1994)** : Originally featured on the soundtrack for the movie "The Crow," this song was written in a style similar to the tracks on "Core" and quickly became a hit while Stone Temple Pilots were still writing their second album. It was later included on their second album, "Purple." "That's big empty from the STP pilots, originally from the soundtrack of The Crow, but later included on the band's second album, Purple."
**Trippin' on a Hole in a Paper Heart (1996)** : This single from "Tiny Music Songs from the Vatican Gift Shop" is, according to Scott Wyland, about a dancer who endures a horrific gang rape. Despite the trauma, she ultimately falls in love because she finds herself unable to release the pain. "According to Scott, it's about a dancer who suffers a horrific gang rape, who ends up falling in love because she can't let go of the pain."
**Down (1999)** : This song was the very first single tackled by Stone Temple Pilots and producer Brendan O'Brien when they entered the studio in February 1999 to begin work on their fourth album, "Number 4." "The very first song they tackled was called Down."
**Sour Girl (1999)** : Featured on Stone Temple Pilots' fourth album, "Number 4," this song was written by Scott Wyland about his then-estranged wife, Janina. "Sour Girl from the fourth Stone Temple Pilots album written about his ex-wife, Janina."

## curiosities
**Drug-Fueled Recording of Purple (1994)** : The recording of the "Purple" album in Atlanta was notably swift, completed in just four weeks, largely because Scott Wyland's local dealer, a 25-year-old HIV-positive woman, supplied him with drugs that prevented him from becoming "dope sick," thus enabling continuous work. "Her stuff kept Scott from getting dope sick, which meant that the album was recorded rather quickly, four weeks."
**Isolation for Writing Tiny Music (1996)** : For their third album, the band rented actor Jimmy Stewart's mansion in Santa Barbara, believing the isolation would benefit everyone, particularly Scott Wyland, in their writing process. Despite this, Wyland frequently traveled down the Pacific Coast Highway to Los Angeles to purchase cocaine and heroin. "He was thought that the isolation would do everyone, especially Scott, a lot of good."
**Tour Cancellation Due to Scott Wyland's Addiction (1996-1997)** : The band was forced to cancel a substantial portion of their tour supporting "Tiny Music" throughout late 1996 and into 1997 due to Scott Wyland's persistent drug issues and his inability to rehearse or perform. The band stated, "Our lead singer is a junkie with the message and we can't go on the road with him until he's well."
**Public Statement on Tour Cancellation (April 1996)** : Stone Temple Pilots issued a public statement clarifying the cancellation of their tour dates, explicitly attributing it to Scott Wyland's "dependency on drugs" and his being "under a doctor's care in a medical facility." The statement emphasized that it was not due to typical reasons like exhaustion, dehydration, or a sore throat. "Our lead singer, Scott Wyland has become unable to rehearse or appear for these shows due to his dependency on drugs."
**Scott Wyland's Temporary Departure (1996)** : Scott Wyland was effectively "out" of Stone Temple Pilots after the band suggested he pay millions in restitution for the canceled tour dates, a period that coincided with the end of his marriage to Janina. "And that was it for STP as Scott was concerned. He was out, especially after he was suggested that he pay some kind of restitution for the canceled dates out of his own pocket, which meant millions."
**"Fit and Ready to Rock" Reunion Press Conference (October 1, 1996)** : At the same location where they announced Scott Wyland's departure, Stone Temple Pilots held a press conference on October 1, 1996, declaring that all members were "fit and ready to rock" after Wyland's drug treatment. Scott expressed the importance of his bandmates, stating, "It's an important thing to me to have these guys in my life. It's a relationship. It's not just about the band and business."
**Six-Week Road Trip with Guardian (November 4 - December 14, 1996)** : The band embarked on a six-week road trip beginning November 4 in Los Angeles, concluding in Cleveland on December 14. Scott Wyland was required to be accompanied by a guardian and adhere to a nightly curfew, with a decision made to avoid crossing international borders to minimize potential hassles with customs due to Wyland's history. "Wyland was to be accompanied by a guardian and was required to meet a nightly curfew."
**Post-Tour Cancellations (January 1997)** : Following a successful six-week tour, Stone Temple Pilots canceled a New Year's show in Anchorage, Alaska, without explanation, and two subsequent shows in Hawaii for the first week of January 1997, disappointing over 30,000 fans. "Then two more shows scheduled for the first week in January and Hawaii were also blown out."
**Manager's Statement on Scott's Relapse (January 6, 1997)** : On January 6, 1997, STP's manager Steve Stewart issued a statement confirming Scott Wyland was having problems again and was seeking care, but assured that he was safe, not arrested, and the band remained together. "Scott has made the band aware that he was having problems again and is taking care of himself at this point."
**Impact of Scott's Relapse on Album Charts (January 1997)** : Scott Wyland's relapse and subsequent undetermined rehab stay came at a time when "Tiny Music" had significantly climbed the album charts, jumping 78 spots in a month due to the recent tour, causing frustration for the other band members. "Tiny music had jumped 78 spots on the album charts in a month because of the tour."
**Rumors of Disbandment (May 25, 1997)** : After a show in Toronto on May 25, 1997, rumors began to circulate that Stone Temple Pilots were "finished for good." "After a show in Toronto on May 25th, 1997, the rumors were that the Stone Temple pilots were finished for good."
**Reunion and Renewed Chemistry (February 1999)** : After their separate projects, the other three members realized the importance of their collective "chemistry" and Dean DeLeo called Scott Wyland, who, feeling "out of options," agreed to rejoin. They entered the studio in February 1999 with Brendan O'Brien to work on their fourth album, "Number 4." "One of them was chemistry. We looked at each other in a different way and thought, this is what we mean to each other."
**Lollapalooza Headliner Rumors (March 1999)** : Following positive progress reports on Scott Wyland in March 1999, rumors suggested that Stone Temple Pilots would headline Lollapalooza if the festival were to be reactivated. "There were rumors that if Lola Paloza were to be reactivated, STP would be the headliners."
**Canceled Gigs Due to Probation Violation (June 1999)** : A gig scheduled for June 11 in New York City was canceled after Impact House reported Scott Wyland's probation violation, leading to Judge Fiddler ordering him back to rehab. "A gig in New York City, Schedule for June 11th was canceled, and there was a matter of a show set for Las Vegas in August the 12th."
**Las Vegas Show Before Incarceration (August 12, 1999)** : The band performed an acclaimed show in Las Vegas on August 12, 1999, which became Scott Wyland's "last night of freedom" before he had to turn himself in for sentencing. The band described the experience as surreal, witnessing their friend taken away by a bailiff the next morning after a great performance. "We watched our friend be taken away by a 200 pound bailiff, man. It was the most surreal four days you'd ever want to experience."
**VH1 Special (March 8, 2000)** : All four members of Stone Temple Pilots traveled to New York on March 8, 2000, to tape a special for the video channel VH1, marking a public appearance after Scott Wyland's recent release from jail and halfway house. "On March 8th, all four members of the band went to New York where they taped a special for VH1, the video channel."
**Comeback Plans (March 2000)** : By the end of March 2000, plans were being made for more showcase gigs, a tour, and even a pay-per-view event for the band, signaling a hopeful return to active touring and recording. ## albums
**Talk Show (1998) – Solo Album** : Released by the other three members of Stone Temple Pilots (Dean DeLeo, Robert DeLeo, Eric Kretz) with singer David Coots while Scott Wyland worked on his solo material. The album did not perform well, selling only around 60,000 copies, and did not receive the positive critical reception that Wyland's "12 Bar Blues" did. "It also didn't do very well, selling maybe 60,000 copies, and it did not get the good reviews that 12 bar blues got either."

## curiosities
**Therapeutic for Band Members (1998)** : The other Stone Temple Pilots members found the process of making the "Talk Show" record to be "therapeutic" for them, much like Scott Wyland found his solo album therapeutic. This experience taught them valuable lessons, particularly about their chemistry and mutual importance. "I think after the talk show thing, I think the talk show record was really therapeutic for all of us to make."

# concert - Las Vegas Show (August 12, 1999)

## curiosities
**Emotional Performance Before Jail** : This concert was described as one of Stone Temple Pilots' finest shows, with Scott Wyland delivering an "insanely great" performance, fully aware that he had to turn himself in the next day for a jail sentence. He told his bandmates it was an honor to spend his "last night of freedom" with them. "Man, he was insanely great. Just really. It was one of the bands' finest shows I think."

# curiosity

**Death of Brandon Lee (March 1994) – The Crow** : Brandon Lee, son of martial arts legend Bruce Lee, was accidentally shot and fatally wounded in the abdomen when an improperly loaded prop gun discharged during the filming of "The Crow." Despite his death, the movie was completed, and its soundtrack was released in late March 1994. "Brandon, if you remember, was accidentally shot when an improperly loaded prop gun went off, wounding him fatally in the abdomen."
**The Crow Soundtrack (March 1994)** : The soundtrack for "The Crow" movie, released at the end of March 1994, was highly acclaimed and described as "one of the best of the decade." It featured contributions from major artists including The Cure, Nine Inch Nails, Rage Against the Machine, and Stone Temple Pilots. "It's great soundtrack, too. One of the best of the decade, and at the cure in 9 inch nails and rage against the machine and other big names."
**Chateau Marmont Hotel – Sunset Boulevard** : This hotel on Sunset Boulevard served as a temporary refuge for Scott Wyland when his wife refused to let him come home in May 1995, where he sought solace with Courtney Love. It was also where Wyland recorded some of his solo material, like "Chateau Mars," on a four-track recorder. "Scott Wyland recorded at the Chateau Marmont Hotel on sunset bullifard."
**Impact House – Residential Drug Treatment Facility** : Impact House is identified as a residential drug treatment facility where Scott Wyland was transferred for a supposed six-month stay after a week in LA County Jail in April 1996. It later reported Wyland for non-compliance, leading to further probation violations. "From there, he was transferred to the medical unit of the LA County jail. After a week, he was transferred to Impact House, a residential drug treatment facility for what was supposed to be a six-month stay."
**Judge Larry Fiddler – Los Angeles Superior Court** : Judge Larry Fiddler was a Los Angeles Superior Court judge assigned to Scott Wyland's case, who imposed conditions such as regular court appearances to monitor Wyland's sobriety. He became visibly frustrated with Wyland's repeated missed hearings and probation violations, eventually ordering him back to rehab multiple times. "A Los Angeles Superior court judge named Larry Fiddler was assigned to the Wylandt case, and one of the conditions he imposed on Scott was that he appeared court hearings every so often."
**Robert Kalina – New York Attorney** : Robert Kalina was Scott Wyland's New York attorney, known for representing prominent rap artists such as Biggie Smalls and Run DMC. He successfully negotiated continuances for Wyland's court dates due to his client's repeated rehab stints in California. "Wylandt's New York attorney, Robert Kalina, who worked with rap artists like Biggie Smalls and run DMC."
**Rikers Island – Jail Warning** : Rikers Island was explicitly mentioned as the destination for Scott Wyland if he were to violate his probation in New York, facing a 30-day sentence. It was highlighted as a place "nobody wants to be sent to," with an anecdote about Sid Vicious being gang-raped there in 1979 after being sent on drug charges. "Any screw ups and Wylandt would be sentenced to 30 days at Reikers Island and believe me, nobody wants to be sent to Reikers Island."
