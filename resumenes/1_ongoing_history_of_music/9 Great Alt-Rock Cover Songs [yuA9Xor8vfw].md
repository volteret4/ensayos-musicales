## curiosities
**Dolores O'Riordan's Approval and Tragic Death** : Dolores O'Riordan, lead singer of The Cranberries, not only loved Bad Wolves' version of "Zombie" but offered to join them in London to add guitar or background vocals, leaving an excited voice message the night before the planned session. Tragically, she was found dead the next morning, January 15, 2018, having passed out in a bath after heavy drinking. "At the night before, she left a voice message, saying how excited she was about the project, and that she'd see everybody the next day."
**First Rock Song to go Platinum in 2018** : Bad Wolves' cover of "Zombie" achieved platinum status in 2018, marking it as the first rock song to do so that year. "Our first story involves the first rock song to be declared platinum in 2018."
**Music Video Details** : The music video for Bad Wolves' "Zombie" features a woman in gold who resembles Dolores O'Riordan from the original 1994 video, though it is not her. The numbers she smears from a glass, $1,15,18, represent January 15, 2018, the day Dolores O'Riordan died. "And the numbers she smears from the glass are $1,15,18. The day Dolores died."

# curiosity

**Great Songs and Multiple Interpretations** : A truly great song can be interpreted multiple times by different people and still sound good, whether performed by a full band or a single person. Fans appreciate hearing songs done in different ways, sometimes preferring the cover to the original or finding new dimensions in it. **Cover Song Permission and Royalties** : In the music industry, artists typically do not need to ask the original composer's permission to cover a song.
