## curiosities
**Adam Clayton Drug Arrest (August 6, 1989) – Possession with Intent to Traffic** : Bassist Adam Clayton was arrested in the parking lot of the Blue Light pub in Glen Allen, Ireland, for possession of 19 grams of marijuana. He was charged with intent to traffic under Irish law and fined £25,000, which was donated to the Women's Aid Refugee Center. Thanks to the Probation of Offenders Act, no official criminal conviction was recorded, allowing U2's scheduled tour to begin on September 15. "Paid a £25,000 fine and donated it to the Women's Aid Refugee Center."


# artist - Happy Mondays

## labels
Factory Records

## curiosities
**Barbados Recording Session Disaster – Collapse of Factory Records** : The Happy Mondays' drug binge in Barbados during the recording of their album proved catastrophic for Factory Records in the early 1990s. Band members consumed crack at a staggering rate, destroyed cars, a member broke his arm, and they sold their own possessions to fund drug purchases. They delivered an album devoid of lyrics and vocals. The incident directly accelerated the financial ruin of Factory Records. "Destroyed cars, a member broke his arm, and sold their belongings to buy drugs."

**Factory Records Collapse – No Legal Contracts with Artists** : Factory Records' attempt to sell its assets to London Records was derailed when it was discovered that the label held no legally binding contracts with any of its artists. This fundamental structural flaw made the label impossible to sell and hastened its complete collapse. "They had no legally binding contracts with their artists."
