## albums
**Never Mind the Bollocks (1977) – Definitive Punk Album** : Released on November 28, 1977. A radio commercial for the album was produced, with the word "bollocks" edited out to avoid offending listeners. This record would go on to define the original British punk rock era and is credited with launching a billion bands worldwide, despite being the band's only album. "It would be the record that defined the original British punk rock era and an album that launched a billion bands around the world."

## songs
**God Save the Queen (1977)** : Released on the Virgin Label on May 27, 1977. The song controversially connected the words "queen" and "moron." Virgin Records faced significant challenges pressing the record, as some workers at the manufacturing plant were so offended they refused to work. The BBC banned it, citing "gross bad taste" and a possible breach of the Broadcasting Act, effectively saying the song broke the law. Most radio and TV stations even refused to broadcast commercials for it, and major chain stores like Woolworths and Boots banned its sale. Despite all obstacles, it sold 150,000 copies in just five days, but was denied the number one spot on the official record charts due to alleged rigging, being listed as number two. The chart publishers further showed their disapproval by leaving a blank space where the band's name should have been printed at number two. "The Sex Pistols with God Save the Queen released on the Virgin Label on May 27th, 1977."
**Pretty Vacant (1977)** : Released in June 1977, around the time Johnny Rotten was subjected to two attacks. This single managed to make it into the top 10 on the charts. "The next one was Pretty Vacant, which came out in June."
**Holidays in the Sun (1977)** : This single was released in October 1977 and also achieved a top 10 position on the charts. "The single after that was Holidays in the Sun. That was in October."

## curiosities
**Bill Grundy TV Incident – December 1976** : Following their appearance with Bill Grundy on television in December 1976, the Sex Pistols became the recognizable face of punk rock in Britain. This event caused them to be viewed by many as symbolic of everything perceived as wrong with Britain's youth, igniting a media storm comparable to the intense public fascination around an unnaturally huge boy band or pop star today. "Ever since they were running with Bill Grundy on the tally back in December of 1976, the Pistols had become the face of punk rock in Britain."
**Record Label Dealings** : The band was fired from two record labels before securing a signing with a third. This signing ceremony was famously conducted on a table positioned directly in front of Buckingham Palace. "They were fired from two record labels before signing to a third."
**Glenn Matlock's Departure** : Original bass player Glenn Matlock left the band before the release of "God Save the Queen." His departure was reportedly due to his perceived excessive affection for The Beatles. "Original bass player Glenn Matlock was out, apparently because he professed too much love for the Beatles."
**Sid Vicious's Background and Entry** : John Simon Richie, known as Sid Vicious, replaced Glenn Matlock as bass player. He was described as a violent, semi-literate hanger-on and had previously been a sometime drummer and saxophone player. Before joining the group, he worked as a shop assistant in a sex shop. He considered Johnny Rotten an "old enemy" from the start, but after seeing the band perform, he thought they were "really wonderful" and admired their honesty, citing the Bill Grundy incident as genuine. "His replacement was a violent semi-literate hangar on and sometime drummer and saxophone player named John Simon Richie."
**Johnny Rotten's Attacks – June 1977** : Around the time "Pretty Vacant" was released, Johnny Rotten was attacked by monarchist hooligans armed with knives outside a pub called The Pegasus. His left arm was severely cut, damaging tendons to the extent that he was permanently unable to play the guitar properly. He was fortunate that his heavy leather pants deflected a blade from penetrating his thigh. Three days later, he was attacked again. "They cut up his left arm so badly that some tendons were ruined. He's never been able to play the guitar properly as a result."
**Paul Cook's Attack** : Following Johnny Rotten's attacks, drummer Paul Cook was also assaulted with a pipe in a manner described as "all medieval on his ass." "And then someone took a pipe to drummer Paul Cook and went all medieval on his ass."
**Malcolm McLaren's Unmade Movie** : Manager Malcolm McLaren was plotting to create a movie featuring the Sex Pistols. He intended to use a softcore porn director for the project and hired an unknown screenwriter, Roger Ebert, who would later become a renowned film critic. "Meanwhile Malcolm McLaren was scheming to make a movie with the pistols using a soft core porn director and an unknown screenwriter named Roger Ebert."
**Concert Ban Difficulties** : The band frequently encountered difficulties finding places to perform due to local councils banning their gigs. Johnny Rotten expressed frustration, stating that he was being denied his job while simultaneously being taxed 80% of his earnings by the very authorities that were preventing him from working. "Every time we get a gig, you get your local councils banning it and you get no complaints."
**Disastrous American Tour – January 1978** : Manager Malcolm McLaren's plan to "conquer America" with the Sex Pistols resulted in one of rock history's most disastrous road trips. Initially, the group's visa applications were denied, forcing them to cancel an important appearance on Saturday Night Live, where Elvis Costello was called in as a last-minute replacement. The drummer for Elvis Costello's performance notably wore a t-shirt that read "Thanks, milk," a sarcastic jab at Malcolm McLaren. McLaren made a bizarre miscalculation about American culture by routing the tour away from major cities and through the deep South, an area not receptive to punk rock values. "Instead, what he got was one of the most disastrous road trips in the history of rock."
**American Tour Audience Behavior** : The crowds during the American tour were both weird and violent. At the first show in Atlanta, the band was bombarded with pig snouts. In San Antonio, attendees threw seafood, and in Baton Rouge, tomatoes were hurled. At every venue, the band faced numerous "beer can bombs." "The crowds were both weird and violent."
**Sid Vicious's On-Tour Conduct** : During the American tour, Sid Vicious was notoriously problematic, spitting at people and instigating fights with anyone who looked at him in a way he perceived as offensive. He famously used his bass guitar to swing "like a cricket bat at people's heads." His behavior was so extreme that he was even beaten up by his own bodyguard a few days into the tour. Due to his drug use, he was considered "too stupider to stone to actually play," so his amplifier was never actually hooked up, with a roadie offstage playing all his bass parts. "Sid Vicious wasn't much help. He spit at everybody and he picked fights with anyone who looked at him funny."
**Sid Vicious Stabbing Incident** : Malcolm McLaren recounted an incident during the ill-fated American tour where Sid Vicious, deeply involved in drugs, became agitated in a motel restaurant near San Antonio. While sitting near a family eating, Vicious, wearing a dirty black t-shirt with a swastika and his feet black from not taking off his boots for six months, heard rude comments about him. He then walked behind the gentleman, pulled back his sleeve, threw his arm over the man's head and plate, and slashed his arm with a pen knife, causing blood to spurt out "like ketchup" over the man's steak. This occurred in a place full of "red necky guys" and "long distance truck drivers," leading McLaren to fear for their lives. "He went round to the back of the gentleman that was eating his steak and chips and pulled his sleeve back and threw his arm over and above the man's head and over the man's plate up fairly high and withdrew a pen knife from his pocket and slashed his arm and all the blood blew out like ketchup over the man's steak."
**Band's Self-Destruction** : The Sex Pistols were in the process of self-destructing by the time they reached San Francisco, just 12 days after their American tour began. "By the time the group made it to San Francisco, just 12 days after the tour began, the pistols were well into the process of self-destructing."
**Final Concert – January 14, 1978** : The band's last show took place at the Winterland Ballroom on January 14, 1978. A recording from that night's last song captured "the sound of a band dying violently on stage." Johnny Rotten concluded the performance with the memorable line, "Ah ha ha, have I get the feeling you've been cheated? Good night." "The last show was at the Winterland Ballroom on January 14, 1978."
**Art School First Gig** : The Sex Pistols played their first ever gig at an art school in England, highlighting the role of these institutions in British music. "Hey, their first gig was at an art school to blur and france ferdinand."

## curiosities
**Origins and Formation** : The Clash originated from a "stillborn" group called the London SS. They officially formed in late 1976 within a severely dilapidated squat located on Davis Road in West London. Their manager was a used car salesman. "The clash evolved out of a stillborn group called the London SS."
**Joe Strummer's Background** : The last member to join the group was Joe Strummer, whose real name was John Millers. He was a "refugee" from a pub rock band called The 101ers and insisted on being addressed by his new moniker. Millers, the son of an international diplomat, had a somewhat "posh" upbringing but intentionally abandoned it to become a busker. He earned the name "Strummer" due to the intense ferocity with which he played his guitar. "The last member of the group to join was a refugee from a pub rock band called the 101ers, and he insisted on being addressed as Joe Strummer."
**Camden Rehearsal Space** : The band rehearsed in a building in Camden, which notably featured a jukebox. This jukebox was stocked with the latest singles from contemporary bands like The Ramones, as well as reggae songs from Jamaican artists such as Bob Marley and dub pioneer Lee Scratch Perry. The Clash particularly favored Dub music, identifying it as the music of the immigrant communities with whom they shared their neighborhood. "The clash rehearsed in a building in Camden that featured a jukebox stock with the latest singles from bands like The Ramones and Reggae Songs from Jamaican artists like Bob Marley and Dub Pioneer Lee Scratch Perry."
**Spurred by Sex Pistols** : The Clash were significantly motivated and influenced by the Sex Pistols. "The clash were spurred on by the pistols."

**Formation in Woking** : The band was formed by three teenagers in the city of Woking during a shower. They established a routine of meeting daily at noon in one of their school rooms to jam, which ultimately led to them adopting the name "The Jam." "Up in the city of walking, three teenagers started a band during one shower."
**Early Career Progression** : The Jam's early career saw them advance from playing local pubs to securing opening slots for more established bands like the Sex Pistols and The Clash. "They soon graduated to playing the local pubs, which eventually led to opening slots for bands like the Sex Pistols and the Clash."
**Family Management** : The band members' parents were supportive, partly because the group was managed by the singer's father. "First of all, they were managed by the singer's father."
**Paul Weller's Influence** : Initially an R&B band, The Jam shifted direction after singer Paul Weller witnessed a Sex Pistols show. While embracing punk's energy, they distinguished themselves by also showing respect for British mod culture of the 1960s, rather than adhering to a pure "destroy everything" punk ethos. "After singer Paul Weller saw a Sex Pistols show, he steered things in a slightly different direction."
**Musical Identity** : Despite the music press categorizing them with other punk bands, The Jam intentionally carved out a distinct niche. They blended the energy, aggression, and attitude of punk with a strong understanding of melody and song structure. "This new group would end up carving out a specific niche all of their own."
**Commercial Success** : The Jam became the most popular band to emerge from the initial wave of British punk rock. By the time they disbanded in 1982, they had achieved significant chart success, placing nearly two dozen songs on the English singles charts. "The jam would become the most popular band to come out of that first way of a British punk rock."

# genre - Punk Rock

## curiosities
**1977 British Exposure** : Thanks to the notoriety of the Sex Pistols, punk rock culture became widely exposed across Britain in 1977, leading to the emergence of hundreds of new punk groups. "Thanks to the notoriety of the Sex Pistols, all of Britain had been at least exposed to punk rock culture."
**Definition as Attitude** : Punk was understood less as a specific sound and more as an attitude. The core idea was that if one possessed something important to say and the courage to express it, they should do so, regardless of their proficiency as a musician or singer. "It's important to remember that punk wasn't so much a sound as it was an attitude."
**Rapid Musical Evolution in 1977** : The rules for music underwent extremely rapid changes in 1977; indeed, it's more accurate to say that all established rules were quickly broken. This period opened up rock and roll to novel interpretations by a new generation. It was observed that rock is one of the rare art forms that can actually be enhanced by practitioners who are not technically skilled, suggesting that allowing passion to guide without adherence to rules can lead to wonderful outcomes. "The rules for music changed very fast in 1977. Actually it's probably more accurate to say that all the rules were broken very fast in 1977."
**Evolution and Segmentation** : By the close of 1977, punk was on the cusp of a significant evolutionary phase. This would lead to a vast array of new sounds that, while not strictly punk, unmistakably bore its influence, creating a "woozy, but in a good way" effect on listeners. "Punk was about to undergo a serious bit of evolution that would result in an array of sounds that was so big, it kind of made you woozy, but in a good way."

"And in November, Buffen's running a computer network called Arpanet successfully tested something called TCPIP, which lay the foundations for the internet."
**UK Socio-Economic Climate in 1977** : In 1977, particularly in the UK, the economy was severely struggling, unemployment rates were soaring, especially among young people, the British Empire was in decline, and the entrenched class system was under threat. "The economy was in the toilet, unemployment was skyrocketing, especially amongst young people."
**Queen Elizabeth II's Silver Jubilee – 1977** : A year-long celebration was staged in 1977 to mark Elizabeth II's 25th anniversary of her ascension to the throne. This event was intended as a way to "soothe the subjects" amidst the nation's economic and social challenges. "What better way to soothe the subjects than to stage a year-long celebration of Elizabeth II's 25th anniversary of her ascension to the throne?"
**British Art School's Role in Music** : English art schools play a unique and significant role in British music, acting as breeding grounds for new musicians. Students can enroll as young as 16, and these institutions prioritize creativity over the discipline emphasized in universities. This British phenomenon has produced bands ranging from the Sex Pistols (whose first gig was at an art school) to Blur and Franz Ferdinand. "This is where you could take courses in painting and photography and sculpture and so on. And you can get into an English art school as soon as you turn 16."
**New York City's Difficult Year – 1977** : The year 1977 was exceptionally challenging for New York City, marked by an unbearably hot summer and a major blackout on July 13th that led to widespread looting across half the city. The city itself was in a state of decay and facing potential bankruptcy, compounded by the terror caused by the Son of Sam killer. "1977 was not a good year for the city of New York."
**Son of Sam Killer – David Berkowitz** : New York City was terrorized by a serial killer known as the Son of Sam, identified as David Berkowitz. He committed shootings, claiming he was encouraged by a demon residing in his neighbor's dog. "Guy named David Berkowitz was going around shooting people because he said he was being encouraged by a demon who lived in his neighbor's dog."
**Music Genre Segmentation – Late 1977** : By the end of 1977, the music landscape was undergoing a significant segmentation into numerous genres. What was once simple R&B had diversified into funk and disco, and was on the verge of producing rap and hip hop. Defining "rock" also became more complex, leading to the identification of separate subgenres such as metal, California rock, hard rock, and punk rock, with these subgenres themselves beginning to fragment further. "As 1977 ended, it seemed that music was segmenting into a million different genres."
