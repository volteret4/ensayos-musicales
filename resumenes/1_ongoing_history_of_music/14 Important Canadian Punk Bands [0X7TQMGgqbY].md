# artist - Teenage Head

## genres
Punk rock

## labels
Attic Records

## concerts
Ontario Place Toronto (1980 riot)

## curiosities
**First Show October 17, 1975 – Pre-dates Sex Pistols and The Clash; 1980 Ontario Place Riot** : Teenage Head formed in Hamilton, Ontario, and played their first show on October 17, 1975 at Westdale High School — three weeks before the Sex Pistols played their first gig, and nine months before The Clash formed. They were a major force in Canadian punk, combining elements of the Ramones, New York Dolls, and 1950s rock and roll. Their profile peaked with a 1980 show at Ontario Place in Toronto where thousands tried to get in; what followed became an infamous riot. American radio programmers loved the music but balked at the band name (for obvious reasons), prompting a temporary rename to "Teenage Heads" for US purposes. Just before a crucial US showcase in September 1980, guitarist Gord Lewis was in a serious car accident, canceling the shows and closing the window of opportunity. Original singer Frankie Venom died of throat cancer in 2008; the band continues with Dave Rave on vocals more than 40 years later. "Long after most of their original punk rock contemporaries called it quits, or died off, Teenage Head still with us."


# artist - The Viletones

## members
Steve "Nazi Dog" Lucky

## curiosities
**Screaming Fist (May 1977) – Possible First Canadian Punk Vinyl; Song Named William Gibson's Operation in Neuromancer** : The Viletones formed in Toronto in December 1976 and were at the Ramones' first Toronto show on September 26, 1976. They released a three-track, seven-inch single possibly as early as May 1977, making them contenders for the first Canadian band on vinyl. The key track is called "Screaming Fist." Science fiction writer William Gibson later named a fictional covert operation in his landmark 1984 novel *Neuromancer* — in which hackers attack Soviet infrastructure — "Operation Screaming Fist," directly after the Viletones song. Gibson also coined the term "cyberspace" two years prior. "Gibson was also the person who first coined the term cyberspace."


# artist - The Diodes

## labels
CBS Records (Canada)

## curiosities
**Crash 'n' Burn Club (Summer 1977) – First Punk-Only Venue in Canada, Shut Down by Liberal Party Complaints** : The Diodes formed in late 1976 in Toronto and opened their first show supporting the Talking Heads in January 1977. That summer, they converted their rehearsal space at 15 Duncan Street in downtown Toronto into Crash 'n' Burn, the first punk-only club in Canada. Numerous young bands from Canada and the US played there over several months until the club's closure in the fall — the cause being that the Liberal Party of Canada had an office upstairs and kept complaining to the landlord about the noise and the strange people outside. The Diodes were the first Canadian band to sign with a major label, inking a deal in August 1977 and releasing their self-titled debut album in October. "The Liberal Party of Canada had an office, and they kept complaining to the landlords."


# artist - The B-Girls

## curiosities
**Friends with Blondie and Ramones; Almost Signed to IRS; Influenced B-52s; Background Vocals on Blondie's Autoamerican** : The B-Girls formed in Toronto in 1977 with a sound closer to British Invasion mid-1960s than hard punk, though their look and attitude were fully punk. They toured with and opened for Teenage Head, the Viletones, and eventually The Clash, then relocated to New York where they became close with Blondie, the Ramones, the Cramps, and the Dead Boys. Members sang background vocals on Blondie's *Autoamerican* (1980), had demos produced by Debbie Harry and Mick Jones of The Clash, and nearly signed with IRS Records — which chose a Los Angeles band called the Go-Go's instead. The Go-Go's were fans of the B-Girls. The B-Girls' existence and sound also influenced a band from Georgia called the B-52s. No album was released before the original lineup fell apart by 1981. "This Canadian punk band influenced not just Blondie and the Go-Go's, but a band visiting New York from Georgia called the B-52s."


# artist - The Demics

## curiosities
**New York City (1979) – Genuine Canadian Punk Classic About Toronto Bands Making Road Trips to CBGB** : The Demics formed in London, Ontario in 1977 and regularly made the drive to Toronto to participate in the scene. Drummer Marcy Saddy later left to join the B-Girls. Their EP *Talk's Cheap* appeared in 1978 on Toronto's Ready Records, followed by an album in 1980. Their best-known song, "New York City," is a tribute to the Toronto punk bands — the Viletones, the B-Girls, the Curse, the Diodes, and others — who would regularly drive nine hours to New York City to play CBGB and couch-surf. "The song is about those Toronto bands that would regularly drive the nine hours to New York."


# artist - The Curse

## curiosities
**Possible First All-Female Canadian Punk Band; Shoeshine Boy Written About Emanuel Jaques Murder (1977)** : The Curse are contenders with the B-Girls for the title of Canada's first all-female punk band, having been ready to perform as early as May 1977. The following month they played CBGB in New York on a bill with the Viletones, Diodes, and Teenage Head. They were more intense than the B-Girls — member Mickey Skin performed with a fake lobotomy scar and the band had an explicitly feminist agenda, often starting food fights at shows. Their most controversial song, "Shoeshine Boy," was written about the 1977 murder of 12-year-old Emanuel Jaques in Toronto: a boy shining shoes on Yonge Street who was offered $35 to help move photographic equipment, then tortured and raped for 12 hours before being strangled and drowned in a kitchen sink. The crime catalyzed the city's cleanup of its red-light district. "They were intent on destroying the patriarchy of music."


# artist - D.O.A.

## labels
Sudden Death Records

## albums
**Hardcore '81 (1981) – Album Title Credited with Naming the Genre "Hardcore Punk"** : D.O.A. evolved from a Vancouver band called The Skulls and played their first gig as D.O.A. on February 20, 1978 at the Japanese Hall. Their debut "Disco Sucks" EP in summer 1978 was funded by the band's pooled unemployment checks. Unlike Toronto bands (who could drive to New York), Vancouver's geography forced them south to LA and east from there; their constant touring led them to compile a list of punk-friendly venues and crash spaces across North America — "The List" — which they shared freely with other bands and which helped establish a coast-to-coast touring infrastructure for punk. Their 1981 album *Hardcore '81* and the tour supporting it are generally credited with giving the fast, heavy, aggressive variant of punk its name: hardcore. "DOA helped create an international punk community by compiling a list of punk-friendly places to play and places to stay."


# artist - The Dishrags (DD and the Dish Rags)

## curiosities
**Canada's First All-Female Punk Band – Victoria BC, Jamming 1976, Part of Vancouver's First Punk Show** : DD and the Dish Rags originated in Victoria, British Columbia, with three members who were around 15 years old and already Ramones fans when they started rehearsing around 1976. They were part of Vancouver's first punk rock show on July 30, 1977, before approximately 400 people — a large audience for that era. Their run ended by 1980, but they left behind two EPs and the distinction of being, by the available dates, Canada's (and possibly the world's) first all-female punk band. "One of the first all-female punk bands anywhere in the world."


# artist - Young Canadians (The K-Tels)

## members
Art Bergman

## curiosities
**Battle of Bands Win Led to Bob Rock's First Ever Production Credit** : The band started as the K-Tels in 1978 in Vancouver but were threatened with legal action by the cheap record company K-tel International and renamed themselves Young Canadians. Frontman Art Bergman would remain a presence in Canadian alt-rock for decades, ultimately being made a Member of the Order of Canada in 2020. The band won a local Battle of the Bands contest that awarded them studio time at a new facility called Little Mountain Sound. The engineer assigned to these unknown contest winners was a young man named Bob Rock — it was the first time Rock ever produced anything. He went on to produce Metallica, Mötley Crüe, Bon Jovi, The Cult, the Tragically Hip, and many others. "The engineer assigned to them was some kid named Bob Rock."


# artist - The Subhumans

## curiosities
**Jerry Hanna's Arrest and Litton Industries Bombing – Punk Activism Leading to Jail Time** : The Subhumans formed in Vancouver in 1978 with original members known as Useless, Dimwit, Wimpy, and Normal. Original singer Useless (real name Jerry Hanna) quit in 1981 and joined a group called Direct Action, also known as the Vancouver Five or Squamish Five. On October 14, 1982, Direct Action bombed a plant owned by Litton Industries in Toronto — which manufactured guidance systems for cruise missiles — injuring several workers and three police officers (no one was killed). Hanna was not part of the bombing itself but supported it and was involved in planning other actions, including a planned Brink's truck robbery using stolen automatic weapons. He was convicted and sentenced to ten years in prison, serving five. His arrest and trial rallied the punk community; multiple benefit concerts were organized. "He was convicted and sent to jail for ten years, serving five."


# artist - The Pointed Sticks

## labels
Stiff Records

## curiosities
**First Canadian Band to Sign to Stiff Records – Power Pop Side of Vancouver Punk** : The Pointed Sticks formed in Vancouver in 1978, five former members overlapping with D.O.A. and the Subhumans. Their sound was more melodic and power pop-leaning than their contemporaries — five members singing harmonies was unusual in punk. Their name came from a Monty Python sketch about self-defense against pointed sticks. They became the first Canadian band to sign to Stiff Records in the UK, home to the Damned, Nick Lowe, Elvis Costello, and Ian Dury. Several strong singles and one album followed. Their relative accessibility broadened the audience for Vancouver punk. "Stiff Records, one of the groundbreaking indie labels in the UK."


# artist - Forgotten Rebels

## members
Mickey DeSadist

## curiosities
**Hamilton Punk Provocateurs – "Surfin' on Heroin," Nazi Allegations, Cassette "Burn the Flag" (1978)** : The Forgotten Rebels formed in Hamilton in 1977, about 18 months after Teenage Head. Led by Mickey DeSadist, they were sarcastic, sometimes profane, and featured songs like "Surfin' on Heroin," "Bomb the Boats and Feed the Fish" (about Vietnamese refugees), "No Beatles Reunion," and a song called "Reich 'n' Roll" that drew allegations of anti-Semitism and homophobia; the band maintained it was all satire. Their first release was a six-song cassette called *Burn the Flag* in 1978, followed by *Tomorrow Belongs to Us*. They occasionally attracted crowds of several thousand. "They had a large cult following, although they were never really embraced to the level of Teenage Head."


# artist - SNFU

## members
Mr. Chi Pig (Ken Chin)

## labels
Epitaph Records

## albums
**...And No One Else Wanted to Play (1985) – Edmonton Hardcore Pioneers, Credited with Influencing Skate Punk** : SNFU formed in Edmonton in 1981 as one of the first bands to influence what would become known as Skate Punk. Vocalist Mr. Chi Pig (Ken Chin), the second youngest of 12 children from one of the last families in Edmonton to get running water, was known as one of the most energetic and athletic front men in punk anywhere. Like D.O.A., they toured relentlessly across North America, building an audience through constant gigging. Billy Joe Armstrong of Green Day cited them as an influence. They were signed to Epitaph (the label that also launched Bad Religion and The Offspring) and produced eight studio albums, plus live albums, EPs, and compilations. Mr. Chi Pig was the only constant member through nearly three dozen different musicians over the band's history. He was diagnosed with a fatal medical condition in 2018, spent his last months recording solo material, and died on July 16, 2020 — after which plans for a mural and statue were immediately floated by Edmonton's punk community. "Plans for a memorial and a statue were immediately floated by the punk community."


# artist - Propagandhi

## labels
Fat Wreck Chords

## genres
Punk rock
Skate punk

## curiosities
**Portage la Prairie, Manitoba to Global Punk Influence – Vegan Anarchist Politics and Fat Wreck Chords** : Propagandhi formed in 1986 in Portage la Prairie, Manitoba (population approximately 13,000), 50 miles west of Winnipeg. They built a reputation for strongly left-wing, sometimes anarchist, vegan politics targeting human rights violations, sexism, racism, capitalism, and organized religion. They aligned with American bands NOFX and Fugazi and were signed to Fat Mike's Fat Wreck Chords punk label out of California. Their debut record, *How to Clean Everything*, appeared in 1993. Still based in Winnipeg, they remain one of Canada's premier punk bands. "Very left-wing, sometimes anarchist, often vegan."
