"In 2000, Pearl Jam released 72 live albums from their tour as 'official bootlegs,' breaking sales records."

# label - Metropolitan Opera

## curiosities
**Lionel Mapelson – The Godfather of Bootlegging (Early 20th Century)** : Lionel Mapelson, librarian at the Metropolitan Opera in New York City, covertly recorded complete opera performances in the early twentieth century using two-minute wax cylinders. These recordings are considered the origin point of musical bootlegging. "Secretly recorded complete operas in wax cylinders of two minutes, considered the start of musical bootlegging."

**Jazz Archivists vs. Pirates – Fan Recordings as Historical Preservation (1930s–50s)** : Enthusiasts who recorded jazz performances in the 1930s through 1950s were not perceived as pirates but as archivists preserving unique and irretrievable historical moments. "Enthusiasts who recorded jazz performances were not seen as pirates but as 'archivists' preserving irretrievable historical moments."


# curiosity

**Thomas Edison's Phonograph (1877) – Not Originally Conceived for Music** : Thomas Edison's phonograph, patented in 1877, was not initially conceived as a device for music reproduction. Edison listed music very low on his enumerated potential uses, prioritizing speech preservation, dictation, and remote learning. "Edison listed music reproduction very low on his list of potential uses."

**19th-Century Copyright Law Left a Legal Vacuum for Sound Recordings** : Copyright laws of the nineteenth century were designed for the printed word and left a complete legal vacuum regarding audio recordings, allowing unauthorized recordings to exist without clear legal prohibition. The United Kingdom was the first country to extend copyright protection to sound recordings; the United States did not do so effectively until 1972. "Copyright laws were designed for the printed word, leaving a legal vacuum for audio recordings."

**Origin of the Word "Bootleg" – Prohibition-Era Hidden Alcohol** : The term "bootleg" dates to the Prohibition era in the United States, when illegal alcohol was transported and sold clandestinely, often hidden inside the shaft of tall boots. "The word 'bootleg' dates to Prohibition, when illegal alcohol was hidden in the shaft of boots."

**Two Types of Live Bootleg Recordings – Audience vs. Soundboard** : There are two primary categories of live bootleg recordings: audience recordings (captured with concealed microphones, raw and ambient) and soundboard recordings (tapped directly from the venue's PA system, offering clean, professional-quality audio). "There are mainly two categories: audience recordings (raw, with public atmosphere) and soundboard recordings (connected directly to the PA, offering clean audio)."

**Compact Cassette (Philips, 1963) – Democratized Tape Trading** : The compact cassette, invented by Philips in 1963, miniaturized magnetic tape technology and made it portable and affordable, triggering an explosion in tape-trading culture that would sustain the bootleg community through the pre-internet era. "Made tape technology portable and accessible, greatly boosting the 'tape trading culture.'"

**Tape-Trading Culture – Non-Commercial Code of Ethics** : Pre-internet tape-trading networks operated under an informal but widely observed non-commercial code of ethics centered on documentation, archiving, and love of the artist's legacy — not profit. "Operated under a non-commercial code of ethics focused on documentation, archiving, and love of the artist."

**Magnetic Tape Technology – Developed by Nazis, Adopted by US via Jack Mullin and Ampex** : Magnetic tape recording technology was developed in Nazi Germany and later brought to the United States by Jack Mullin, who worked with Ampex to commercialize it. This technology fundamentally transformed the capacity to record and manipulate audio and made unofficial recordings far easier to produce. "Developed by the Nazis and later adopted in the US by Jack Mullin and Ampex."

**FM Radio Broadcasts as Bootleg Sources (1970s–1990s)** : For several decades, FM radio broadcasts of concerts served as a major source of bootleg material. These broadcasts sometimes compelled record labels to issue official releases — as occurred with Elton John in 1970 and David Bowie in 1972 — to preempt the unauthorized recordings. "Radio broadcasts of concerts were an important source of bootlegs, sometimes forcing labels to release official versions (e.g. Elton John 1970, David Bowie 1972)."

**Artists That Allow Fan Recording – Metallica, Radiohead, Dave Matthews Band, Fugazi** : In addition to Pearl Jam, a number of other major acts have adopted policies permitting or actively encouraging fans to record their concerts, including Metallica, Radiohead, Dave Matthews Band, and Fugazi. "Metallica, Radiohead, Dave Matthews Band, and Fugazi have adopted policies that permit or encourage fan recording."
