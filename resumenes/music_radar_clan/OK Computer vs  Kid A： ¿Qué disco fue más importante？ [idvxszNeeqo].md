## albums
**Ok Computer (1997) - Pivotal 90s Album** : This album is widely considered one of the most important albums of the 1990s, not just for Radiohead but within the broader music context, noted for its thematic depth and technical recording quality. Despite its critical acclaim, the band, particularly Tom Yorke, expressed a sense of bitterness over its massive commercial success, feeling they had unintentionally created a popular mass-market album instead of the cultural and intellectual work they had intended. This success, however, granted the group complete creative freedom from their record label, which they used to pursue a path of intense experimentation. "tanto por su temática sino técnicamente como está grabado."

**Kid A (2000) - Career Turning Point** : Considered by Radiohead and Tom Yorke as their best album, "Kid A" marks a significant turning point in the band's career and their most important creative phase. Following the commercial success of "Ok Computer," the band aimed for a more conceptual, complex, and experimental sound, akin to Pink Floyd, intentionally moving away from mass appeal. Its recording process was lengthy, resulting in over 2,000 hours of new material, including songs, small compositions, canned sounds, and demos, which constituted the band's largest creative explosion. "ellos consideran el famoso quida que lo tenemos aquí en su edición el mejor disco de los 10 pulgadas como el álbum que marca un punto de infección en su carrera y el punto más importante."

**Amnesiac (2001) - Part of a Trilogy** : This album is explicitly described as forming part of a creative trilogy alongside "Kid A" and another album referred to as "i-MIDB-ROM." While "Kid A" itself is not just an album but part of this larger creative output, "Amnesiac" is a direct continuation of the vast experimental material generated during the extensive "Kid A" recording sessions. "el quidá en la NESIA y el i-MIDB-ROM."

**The King of Limbs (2011) - Derived from Kid A Sessions** : This album incorporated material that originated from the prolific and expansive recording sessions for "Kid A." The extensive creative output from those earlier sessions continued to be a rich source for the band's subsequent projects, illustrating the enduring impact and volume of material produced during that period. "tenemos el de King of Limbs."

**A Moon Shaped Pool (2016) - Features Kid A Era Material** : Radiohead's latest album is noted for including content that has its roots in the earlier "Kid A" recording sessions. Specifically, its first single, "Burn the Witch," was conceived, composed, and partially recorded during that highly experimental and productive period, highlighting the long-term influence of the "Kid A" era on their later work. "en el último disco a Moon Shake Pool que es su primer single, Bound of Witch."

## songs
**Burn the Witch (2016)** : This song, released as the first single from Radiohead's "A Moon Shaped Pool" album, has a unique origin story: it was conceived, composed, and partially recorded many years earlier during the extensive and creatively explosive sessions for the "Kid A" album. This demonstrates the profound and lasting impact of the "Kid A" recording period, which continues to supply material for the band's modern releases. "Fue pensado, fue compuesto y fue parcialmente grabado durante las grabaciones del quidá."

## curiosities
**Post-Ok Computer Creative Direction** : Following the unexpected and massive commercial success of "Ok Computer," Radiohead, particularly Tom Yorke, felt a sense of bitterness, as they had not intended to create such a popular, mass-market album. This reaction prompted them to deliberately shift their artistic direction towards making more conceptual, complex, experimental, and less commercially accessible albums, drawing inspiration from bands like Pink Floyd. "no habían pretendido hacer un álbum tan comercial y tan famoso y cuando tuvo tanto éxito se quedaron con una sensación un poco amarga."

**Creative Freedom Post-Ok Computer** : The immense success of the "Ok Computer" album provided Radiohead with complete creative freedom from their record label. This newfound liberty allowed them to embark on a phase of intensive, almost compulsive, experimentation, taking ample time to meticulously work on their subsequent projects, including "Kid A." "Gracias al éxito de lo que y computer el grupo había conseguido la total libertad creativa por parte de su disco gráfica."

**Kid A Recording Sessions - Creative Explosion** : The recording of "Kid A" was a period of immense and unprecedented creative explosion for Radiohead, taking a considerable amount of time. During these sessions, the band recorded over 2,000 hours of material, encompassing numerous songs, small compositions, canned sounds, and demos. This process generated an extraordinary volume and quality of content, unparalleled in Radiohead's subsequent career, and continues to be a source of material for their later works. "se produjo la mayor explosión creativa que fue dentro del grupo radioje."

**Kid A Trilogy Concept** : "Kid A" is considered not just a standalone album but rather part of a trilogy that also includes "Amnesiac" and an album referred to as "i-MIDB-ROM." This conception highlights the extensive and interconnected creative output of the band during this highly experimental period, with the "Kid A" sessions generating a vast amount of material that shaped multiple releases. "Realmente el quidá no es un álbum sino que es parte de una trilogía que forma en el quidá en la NESIA y el i-MIDB-ROM."

**Enduring Material from Kid A Sessions** : The overwhelming amount of material generated during the "Kid A" recording sessions has had a lasting impact on Radiohead's discography. The band has stated on many occasions that they continue to draw upon and reuse recordings, sounds, and compositions from these sessions for their subsequent albums, including "The Eraser" (Tom Yorke's solo album), "The King of Limbs," and even "A Moon Shaped Pool." "ellos siguen usando muchísimo material del que ha salido de ese día."

**Post-Kid A Recording Methodology** : The recording process for "Kid A" profoundly influenced Radiohead's subsequent recording methodology. After this period, their approach became much more centered on the use of canned sounds, samples, and extensive overdubbing during the production process. This marked a significant shift from their earlier techniques, laying the foundation for their future sound. "el quidad es el que siento las bases primero de la metodología de grabación de radio, es mucho más basada en sonidos en latados y en empleados en proceso de overdiving durante los procesos de grabación."

**Pink Floyd Influence** : In the period following "Ok Computer," Radiohead sought to create albums that were more akin to the work of Pink Floyd. This involved a deliberate move towards more completely conceptual, complex, highly elaborated, and experimental projects, aiming for a sound that would not appeal to a broad mainstream audience. "hacer un álbum más parecido a lo que venía a ser Pin Floyd, algo más completamente conceptuales, muy complejos, muy trabajados y muy experimentales que no llegan al gran público."

# artist - Tom Yorke

## member of
Radiohead

## albums
**The Eraser (2006) - Kid A Outtakes Album** : This solo album by Tom Yorke is composed almost entirely, approximately 90%, of material derived directly from the discarded recordings and creative surplus generated during Radiohead's "Kid A" sessions. It serves as a testament to the immense volume of quality content produced during that period, much of which was later repurposed. "el disponsoritario de Tom George, de Ereser que fue sacado en un 90% de manera casi integrada de las grabaciones desechadas del quidá."

## curiosities
**Cyclical Creative Process** : Tom Yorke is characterized by a distinctive creative pattern, marked by alternating phases of intense creative explosion and profound creative crisis or depression. The "Kid A" sessions, for example, represent the zenith of his creative output during such an explosive phase. "Tom George es una persona que combina enormes fases de explosión creativa con enormes fases de crisis creativa y de depresión."

**Interest in Electronic Music and Technology** : Tom Yorke developed a keen interest in electronics, exploring various new music systems. He learned from other electronic artists and became proficient in using sampling machines and recording canned sounds. These new interests and acquired skills became fundamental to the future direction and sound of Radiohead. "Él se ha interesado mucho por la electrónica, conocía muchos otras artistas electrónicos que le habían enseñado muchos sistemas de música nuevos, él aprendió a utilizar muchísimas máquinas desampleados, agravar sonidos en latados."

**NME Interview on Kid A Material** : In an interview with NME, Tom Yorke expressed the overwhelming volume of material generated during the "Kid A" sessions. He famously stated that he wished he could have released ten albums from the content produced during that period, indicating the difficulty he faced in selecting what to include. "En una entrevista a enemy Tom George llegó a decir que le hubiese gustado sacar 10 agúmes puntos con esa gollquidad porque realmente llevo un punto en que él no sabía que renunciar."
