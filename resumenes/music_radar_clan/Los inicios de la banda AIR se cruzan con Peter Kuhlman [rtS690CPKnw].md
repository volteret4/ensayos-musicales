## albums
**60.000 (1993)** : This electronic, instrumental album was released two years before Air was founded. Many of its titles were in French. It was initially released in Germany in very limited quantities, less than 1,000 units. A British independent label later re-released it in the UK, but it largely went unnoticed. This album was at the center of a "sangrante" (bloody/painful) conflict with Air due to its stylistic similarities and French titles preceding Air's debut. The album is not available on Spotify or iTunes today, and physical copies are extremely rare. "no llegaba a poder llegar menos de mil unitades de este disco."

## curiosities
**Early Career and Pseudonyms** : Peter Culman was an artist and producer who also owned his own graphic label in the 90s. Although he didn't achieve significant mainstream success outside Germany, his work was considered very interesting. Following a conflict related to his 1993 album and Air's debut, he continued his career under various pseudonyms, with "Pit Nanduke" being the most widely recognized. "Usaba nombres de fases grupos y sobre todo tenían sobre nombre que es Pit Nanduke con el que rateamente todo el mundo lo conoce."
**A Farts Record Label** : In the 1990s, Peter Culman founded his own record label in Germany called "A Farts." The label produced hundreds of works, none of which sold more than 1,000 units. The style of music produced by the label was described as being "ese rollo en el ronque de Gustav Rael." "La discográfica que había fundado en la alemania en los 90 que se ha dado a Farts, produjo, cientos de algo mas pero ninguno de ellos praniamente llevaba a los mil unitades."
**Collaborations and Posthumous Recognition** : Peter Culman was more widely known for his collaborations with other artists like Brian Eno and Richie Hawtin than for his own musical pieces, partly due to his extensive use of different names. After his death, some of his collaborative works and pieces from alternative projects, previously unreleased or obscure, were made available on Spotify. "hoy lo conocemos por que rajo con muchísimos artistas como Brian Nino, como Richie Houghton mas que por las piezas que hizo él."
**Death and Legacy** : Peter Culman passed away in 2002 from a heart attack. After his death, his record label went bankrupt. Due to the extremely small print runs of his works (often less than 1,000 units), much of his discography became exceptionally rare, described as "dead on video." While his primary album "60.000" remains elusive, some of his other productions and collaborations have since appeared on streaming platforms. "Murio Nino 2002 por un infarto y su discográfica de robo y prácticamente de todo lo que he dicho era que ha dado un etal de tiradas tan pequeñas que ha muerto en el vídeo."

## albums
**Premiers Symptômes (1997) - Debut Album** : This album was compiled from Air's first two singles, "Modular Miss" and "Seven Stars," along with other tracks. Despite their initial success in signing with Virgin, the album passed largely unnoticed upon its release. It struggled to find commercial success in France, where the electronic scene was predominantly "discotecquero," contrasting with Air's more ambient, Brian Eno-influenced style. "cuando salió realmente pasó muy desapercibido."

## songs
**Modular Miss (1995)** : Air's first independently produced single, released shortly after the group's formation in 1995. This track later became the opening piece of their debut album, "Premiers Symptômes." It was part of the initial works that Virgin (French branch) began to distribute, leading to a conflict due to its similarities with Peter Culman's earlier 1993 album. "sacaron su primar sigue para tener todo producido que es el Modular Miss."
**Seven Stars (1996)** : This was Air's second single, released a few months after "Modular Miss" in 1996. Like their first single, "Seven Stars" was also included in their debut album, "Premiers Symptômes." The early success of these singles quickly gained them recognition within French discographies. "A los pocos meses ya en 1996 sacaron el que hasta no va a 70, lo que fue su segundo síngel también incluido en este disco."

## curiosities
**Origins and Formation** : The two core members of Air, both French Parisians from Versailles, shared a remarkably similar background: they were the same age, studied solfège at the conservatory, learned a multitude of instruments, and even studied architecture at the same faculty. Their deep-rooted acquaintance and shared musical tastes solidified their bond, leading them to form Air in 1995 after the failure of their previous group, Orange. "los dos son franceses, por supuesto, pero son parísinos y encima son diversarias."
**Early Career and Virgin Signing** : After the release of their first single, "Modular Miss," Air quickly gained recognition, performing frequently. This led to their signing with a French branch of Virgin Records shortly after their debut. Within two years, Virgin compiled their early singles and other pieces into their first album, "Premiers Symptômes." "ya fijaron por una de las ramas de Virgin en francés."
**Market Reception and Conflict of Interest** : Air's ambient and symphonic electronic style, influenced by artists like Brian Eno, was not particularly commercial in the mid-90s French electronic scene, which was more "discotecquero" (club-oriented). Consequently, they found more success in the United Kingdom than in France. A significant problem arose when Virgin intended to properly release their early works, as it was discovered that Peter Culman had released an electronic, instrumental album titled "60.000" in 1993, which also featured many French titles, creating a "gran conflicto de intereses." "Tanto fue así que tuvieron más éxito en el reino unido que en francés."

**Formation and Disbandment** : Orange was an early group formed by five artists, including Tien de Crecy and the future members of Air, before Air officially came into existence. The band ultimately failed to coalesce, primarily because its members possessed widely disparate musical interests. The future members of Air were drawn to ambient and symphonic electronic music, influenced by acts like Banger and Brian Eno, while Tien de Crecy leaned towards a more "discotecquero" sound. # artist - Brian Eno

**Influence on Air** : Brian Eno was a significant influence on the two members of Air, who admired his ambient and symphonic electronic style. This influence shaped their musical direction, differentiating them from the more dance-oriented electronic music prevalent in France during the mid-1990s. "los dos miembros de él, que era un sentente muy seguidor, era lo que ganaba del Banger y se llan mis heridas por el Brian Nino, muy pasados a la automíquia ambiental y en la electrónica más de sinfonía."

These collaborations contributed to Culman's recognition, as he was often known more for his work with others than for his solo pieces. "rajo con muchísimos artistas como Brian Nino."

# genre - Electronic

## curiosities
**French Electronic Scene in the 1990s** : In the mid-1990s, the electronic music scene in France was largely characterized by a "discotecquero" (club-oriented or dance-oriented) style. This dominant trend meant that artists like Air, who pursued a more ambient and symphonic electronic sound, found it challenging to achieve commercial success within their home country, experiencing greater acclaim in the United Kingdom instead. "la electrónica francés era un poco tiraba por otros caminos. Era mucho mas discotefiero."
**Influence of Brian Eno's School** : The 1990s saw the "etapa de Brian Nino" (Brian Eno's stage/era) begin to heavily influence a "school" of electronic music that diverged from the discotheque sound. This period marked a growing appreciation for ambient and more symphonic forms of electronic music, particularly evident in German electronic music of the era, as exemplified by artists like Peter Culman. "un poco cuando la etapa de Brian Nino ya empezaba a generar mucha escuela en la electrónica que no era discotequera."

# curiosity

**The "Sangrante" Conflict between Peter Culman and Air** : A significant and "sangrante" (bloody or painful) conflict arose between Peter Culman and the group Air, stemming from the existence of Culman's 1993 album, "60.000." This electronic, instrumental work, featuring numerous French titles, predated Air's formation in 1995 and their subsequent release of similar-sounding material, such as the single "Modular Miss." The issue became pronounced when Virgin Records intended to properly distribute Air's early pieces, discovering the pre-existing work by Culman which created a "gran conflicto de intereses." "Este caso es especialmente sangrante. Entre otras cosas porque Peter Coleman también era un gran aficinado de la electrónica y este disco es de electrónica, también prácticamente son instrumental con muchos de sus titulos en francés."
**Rarity of Peter Culman's "60.000"** : Peter Culman's 1993 album, "60.000," is exceedingly rare today due to its initial limited release of less than 1,000 units and its subsequent disappearance from major streaming platforms like Spotify and iTunes. Despite a re-release in the United Kingdom by an independent British label, it passed completely unnoticed, and virtually no copies remain available. Its scarcity is such that finding a physical copy in a secondhand store or market is considered a remarkable discovery for music enthusiasts. "no se llegaron a evitar mas de mil copias y aunque se llegó de editar entre el en el reino Nino pasó completamente su apacidido y el en día no quedan prateante copias."
