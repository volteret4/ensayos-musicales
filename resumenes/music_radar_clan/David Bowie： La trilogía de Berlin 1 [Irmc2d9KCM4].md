## concerts
**Station to Station Tour (1976)** : During this tour, David Bowie displayed his deep admiration for the German electronic band Kraftwerk by playing recordings and video projections of their album *Radio-Activity*. This demonstrated his interest in the Kraurock sound that would later influence his "Berlin Trilogy." "durante los conciertos del station to station. ## albums
**Ziggy Stardust (1972) - Post-Tour Decline** : Following the enormous tour for *Ziggy Stardust*, David Bowie began experiencing a grave problem with drug addiction and numerous other personal and professional issues. This period marked the beginning of a progressive degeneration that would culminate in 1976. "David Bowie tenía ya un grave problema, no sólo de adición a las drogas sino muchos otros problemas personales y profesionales, que llevaba rastrando casi desde la salida de Figue Star Dust, casi desde esa enorme gira que había hecho cuando sigues Star Dust."
**Young Americans (1975)** : During the recording of this album, David Bowie entered a peculiar phase characterized by continuous changes in his entourage, including managers and close team members, and problems with his wife. He also began a period of constant changes in residence, indicating he was already becoming "out of control" despite his creative output remaining interesting and good. "Ya durante la grabación de John Americans, David Bowie entra en una fase entanto extraña en la que tiene continuos cambios de personas que le rodean, hay varios cambios de manajera, hay varios cambios de personas de su equipo de cercanía, tiene problemas con su mujer y además se empieza en una etapa de continuos cambios de residencia."
**Station to Station (1976)** : This album marks a period where David Bowie's personal life was completely out of control, characterized by extreme eccentricities, madness, and obsessive paranoias. Bowie himself reportedly could not recall composing, recording, or even touring for this album, indicating his severe state at the time. "Ese famoso disco que David Bowie nunca recordó ni haber compuesto ni haber grabado ni recuerda prácticamente nada de él, ni siquiera recuerda la gira de Station Tolstoyson."
**Low (1977)** : This album is often cited as the first of the "Berlin Trilogy," co-produced with Brian Eno. While associated with Berlin, it's noted that not all of its recordings actually took place in the city, challenging the simplified narrative of the trilogy. "muchas otras de agravaciones de David Bowie ni siquiera se grabaron en Berling."
**Heroes (1977)** : The second album in the "Berlin Trilogy," created with Brian Eno. It emerged from a period of intense personal and artistic transformation for Bowie, following his move to Berlin to address his drug addiction.
**Lodger (1979)** : The final album generally considered part of the "Berlin Trilogy," a collaborative effort with Brian Eno. This work further explored experimental sounds and approaches that characterized Bowie's output during his time in Germany.

## curiosities
**Drug Addiction and Personal Decline (Pre-1976)** : David Bowie suffered from a severe drug addiction and numerous other personal and professional problems, which he had been experiencing since the release and extensive tour of *Ziggy Stardust*. This progressive degeneration, culminating in 1976, was not always evident to the public due to his continued creative output of "very interesting" and "very good" projects like *Young Americans*. "David Bowie tiene un gravísimo problema de edición a las drogas casi desde el Pin-Avs. Y no sólo las drogas sino que David Bowie entra en una etapa en una de generación progresiva de muchas maneras, a que culminan en 1976."
**Nomadic Lifestyle (Pre-1976)** : Prior to moving to Berlin, David Bowie adopted a nomadic lifestyle, continuously changing residences between cities such as New York, Paris, Switzerland, and Los Angeles. This restless existence indicated a person already "practically out of control." "Ya llega un punto en que David Bowie no vive en ningún sitio concreto. Pasa temporadas, en Nueva York, pasas temporadas en París, pasas temporadas en Suiza, pasas temporadas en Los Ángeles, está continuamente cambiando residencia."
**The Thin White Duke Persona** : During the *Station to Station* era, David Bowie embodied the character of the Thin White Duke, a persona so intertwined with his declining mental state that it was unclear where the character ended and his own madness began. This figure made controversial, "Nazi, racistas, totalitarios" comments, highlighting a man completely "out of control" who had "hit rock bottom." "Como él también se había creado ese nuevo personaje, el Granduke nos sabemos que parte era del personaje que se había inventado y que parte era su propia locura."
**Economic Exploitation and Legal Troubles** : During his period of decline, David Bowie suffered significant financial exploitation from individuals in his entourage. He was involved in several legal disputes and was practically divorced, living separately from his wife. "David Bowie sufren auténtico expolio económico por algunas de las personas que les rodeaban, cambia varias veces de mana, ya se ven, en vuelto en varias trifulcas legales, con bastante gente, está casi prácticamente divorciado, ya vive separado."
**Swiss Residence and Obsessions** : Around the time he seemed to be at his lowest point, David Bowie moved to a millionaire house by a lake in Switzerland, which offered him a "hermetismo" or sense of privacy. During this period, he developed intense obsessions with painting (creating several canvases daily, often unfinished), contemporary art (amassing one of the world's most important collections), photography, dance, and classical music, seeking out expensive teachers for these pursuits. "se muda a una casa millonaria en suiza. Es la etapa en la que además de David Bowie sufren auténtico expolio económico por algunas de las personas que les rodeaban, cambia varias veces de mana, ya se ven, en vuelto en varias trifulcas legales, con bastante gente, está casi prácticamente divorciado, ya vive separado."
**Recording of Peter and the Wolf** : During his eccentric period in Switzerland, David Bowie recorded an orchestral version of Prokofiev's *Peter and the Wolf*, with himself narrating the story. This project illustrates his "un poco falla" (a bit off) mental state at the time. "La paranoia está que hizo David Bowie sobre Pedro y el obo de Procofir, en el cual grabó a un orquesto tocando Pedro y el obo y él se grababa narrando el cuento de Pedro y el obo, es justo de esta época."
**"Leche y Cocaína" Diet** : During his profound period of drug addiction and decline, David Bowie was famously described as subsisting on a diet of "leche y cocaína" (milk and cocaine). This detail underscores the severe state of his physical and mental health. "De aquí viene esa famosa frase de que David Bowie se alimentaba a base del hechico caína, es justo esta época."
**Berlin Relocation for Detoxification** : Against all odds, David Bowie decided to move to West Berlin with Iggy Pop to detoxify and start a new life. He successfully and quickly detoxed, though he frequently entered and exited Berlin, never staying for more than six months consecutively due to ongoing legal processes in Paris, divorce proceedings, and other commitments. "es justo cuando, en contra todo pronóstico, el yipop deciden mudar Seppardin para desintoxicarse y empezar una nueva vida."
**Admiration and Rejection by Kraftwerk** : David Bowie was a great admirer of Kraftwerk, even playing recordings and video projections of their album *Radio-Activity* during his *Station to Station* concerts. He proposed a collaboration with Kraftwerk for his Berlin projects, but they rejected his offer, as they famously refused to work with anyone, including Kanye West. "aunque David Bowie siempre quiso que Crabbro fue una de esas mandas que la ayudó a grabar la trilogía de Berlin porque era un grandísimo admirador de Crabbro ... aunque se lo propuso y le espide por favor que grabas en con el Crabbro, nunca llevó acceder a trabajar con David Bowie."
**The "Real" Berlin Trilogy Theory** : A theory suggests that the true "Berlin Trilogy" might not solely consist of Bowie's three albums, but rather David Bowie's *Low*, Iggy Pop's *The Idiot*, and Brian Eno's *Music For Airports*. This perspective accounts for the intense collaborative spirit and the fact that many of Bowie's recordings were not exclusively made in Berlin. "que posiblemente la gran trilogia de Berlin no eran los tres iscos de David Bowie sino que posiblemente la gran trilogia de Berlin fue el giro de David Bowie, fue ese de Hip-Hop y fue el mi-sik foro por Zebrai a Gennos, que posiblemente esos sí que fueron los tres grandes iscos que salieron de la trilogia de Berlin."

# artist - Iggy Pop

## member of
The Stooges

## albums
**The Idiot (1977)** : This album, a "grandísimo disco," was recorded in Berlin during the same period as David Bowie's "Berlin Trilogy" and marked the launch of Iggy Pop's successful solo career. Iggy Pop had moved to West Berlin with Bowie, seeking to detoxify from drugs, though he only managed to reduce his consumption and never fully detoxed. "el mismísimo Yipop también trae grabó de idiota en Berlin justo al mismo tiempo, ese grandísimo disco de Yipop con el quina pura su carrera en solitario, justo se grabó en plena, trilogía de Berlin de David Bowie."

## curiosities
**Post-Stooges Decline** : After leaving The Stooges, Iggy Pop found himself in a dire situation, described as "moribundo" (dying), similar to David Bowie's state before their move to Berlin. "Yipop, que ya había dejado a los stuks, estaba en una situación muy parecida. Los dos estaban en una situación de prácticamente estar moribundo, ya que su vida se había terminado."
**Berlin Relocation and Incomplete Detoxification** : Iggy Pop moved to West Berlin with David Bowie for detoxification and to start a new life. While he managed to reduce his drug consumption, he never fully detoxed, unlike Bowie who achieved a rapid and complete detoxification. "Y Chipob nunca se desintoxicaría, conseguía reducir su consumo de drogas, pero nunca se desintoxicó."
**Mention in Kraftwerk Song (1977)** : Iggy Pop is referenced in the lyrics of Kraftwerk's 1977 album *Trans-Europe Express*, along with David Bowie, indicating their presence in Berlin and the cultural impact of their residency there. "es como en el tranchiro Pespés de Crabbro en 1977, en la letra se hace referencia a que David Bowie y Hip-Hop estaban viviendo en Berlin y los iban a visitar."

# genre - Kraurock

## curiosities
**German Music Movement (1970s)** : Kraurock was a vibrant German musical movement experiencing a significant rise in popularity during the mid-1970s, precisely when David Bowie and Iggy Pop relocated to Berlin. The scene was producing "incredible albums" from bands like Kraftwerk, Neu, and Cluster, drawing the attention of artists seeking new creative impulses. "estaba a todo el auge del crowd rock, estaban seriendo discos increíbles, pues de gente como crowdwork, como Neu, estaba haciendo Cluster, estaba viendo un proyecto sinteresantísimos en la de Mania de Aquellas."

# curiosity

**The "Berlin Trilogy" Misconception (1976-1978)** : The widely known "Berlin Trilogy" period (1976-1978) in David Bowie's career, featuring the albums *Low*, *Heroes*, and *Lodger* with Brian Eno, is often presented in an overly simplistic and even inaccurate way. While commonly perceived as Bowie's complete immersion in Berlin to overcome drug addiction and create a revolutionary sound, the reality was much more complex, involved many more people than just Bowie and Eno, and not all recordings took place in Berlin. "La trilogía de Berlín que normalmente la conocemos como ese periodo de la carrera de David Bowie que va entre 1976 y 1978... realmente es una etapa muy compleja y muy difusa y a veces incluso llega a una cuestionarse hasta qué punto realmente existió la trilogía de Berlín o si existió realmente hasta qué punto es como la recordamos."
**Press Scrutiny of Artists** : A historical pattern existed where the press would "hunt for controversial articles" against prominent artists, similar to how they targeted The Beatles before their withdrawal from touring. This intense scrutiny also affected David Bowie during his *Station to Station* period (1974-1975), where interviews often sought to capture him in controversial situations, frequently while he was visibly in a very bad, drugged state. "Ya durante la etapa de Station Suestations con el 74 y el 75 ya había una obsesión también más en aporir a cazar a David Bowie en comentarios polémicos, en actitudes polémicas. Verlo, me refiero, tenemos entrevistas en las que está francamente drogado y en muy mal estado y de hecho muchas veces que así se buscaba ese tipo de situaciones."
**West Berlin as a Sanctuary (1976)** : West Berlin provided a discreet, remote environment for David Bowie and Iggy Pop to attempt detoxification and new beginnings. It was far from the world, with minimal drug trafficking, offered a certain degree of anonymity, and crucially, its vibrant musical movements, particularly Kraurock, greatly eclipsed other contemporary scenes, offering a fertile ground for inspiration. "Me refiero a una zona discreta, muy alejada del mundo, no había prácticamente contrabando drogas en esa parte del mundo, podían disfrutar de un cierta no animato. Y además estaban completamente eclizados por los movimientos musicales que estaba viendo en la de Mania."
**Alternative "Berlin Trilogy" Perspective** : An intriguing theory proposes that the true "Berlin Trilogy" might encompass three pivotal albums created by the collective of artists during their time in Berlin: David Bowie's *Low*, Iggy Pop's *The Idiot*, and Brian Eno's *Music For Airports*. This perspective acknowledges the deep collaboration and shared creative environment that defined the period, rather than solely focusing on Bowie's individual output. "Que decía que posiblemente la gran trilogia de Berlin no eran los tres iscos de David Bowie sino que posiblemente la gran trilogia de Berlin fue el giro de David Bowie, fue ese de Hip-Hop y fue el mi-sik foro por Zebrai a Gennos, que posiblemente esos sí que fueron los tres grandes iscos que salieron de la trilogia de Berlin."
