## albums
**Enter the Wu-Tang (36 Chambers) (1993)** : This was the debut album of the group, which emerged in 1993 from Staten Island, New York. Its production, handled without significant resources or budget, resulted in a characteristic raw, underground sound, akin to an "underground demo tape from a cassette" rather than a super-production. The album was a musical revolution, particularly in its distinct sampling technique, utilizing deconstructed and broken samples from 40s, 50s, and 60s soul, funk, jazz, and classic soul, setting a new standard for hip hop sampling that influenced later artists like J.C. and Kanye West. It established the group's unique lyrical style, where each of the eight members brought their own distinct attitude, from Ghostface Killah's "swaggering attitude" to Inspectah Deck's "reflective and quiet parts," using metaphors inspired by martial arts, Shaolin, chess, and warfare. The group had to resort to hustling drugs to finance the recording of this first album, as they received no money from RCA despite signing a landmark contract. "El disco se hizo, siempre he supuesto sin medios, sin recursos, sin ayuda y de hecho es lo que dio ese sonido tan característico de su mejor butanclan que trae todo ese sonido del Andernal New York inodil Hip Hop y lo trae a un disco de verdad."
**Wu-Tang Forever (1997)** : Released four or five years after their debut album, this album is considered by some to be even better than the first, and is recognized as one of the best albums in hip hop history and music overall. It was one of the best-selling hip hop albums in history and received critical acclaim as a masterpiece, earning some of the best ratings in rap history despite immense public expectation. Unlike their debut, this was a super-production with abundant resources, yet it successfully maintained the characteristic raw, underground essence of the Wu Tang Clan. While RZA continued to control the production, the album showcased a more cohesive and uniform group effort, with all members contributing significantly. The lyrics are considered the best the group ever wrote, deeply influenced by important Black liberation movements in the United States, such as the Black Panthers and FIPS, resulting in profound, committed, and explicit yet incredibly well-crafted verses. "es aún mejor que el primer disco y que está considerado, uno de los mejores iscos en el Hip Hop y uno de los mejores iscos en la historia de la música."

## curiosities
**Formation and Early Struggles - Staten Island** : The group originated in the late 1980s and early 1990s in Staten Island, New York, a somewhat remote and socio-economically disadvantaged area where its members resided. All members were already involved in the New York underground hip hop scene, having individually produced some works but struggling to achieve prominence against established acts like Public Enemy, N.W.A., Ice Cube, and Dr. Dre. The idea for the group came from RZA and GZA, who decided to unite the eight members to create a unique project distinct from the prevailing market trends. "nos tenemos que ir a final de los 80 primeros de los 90 a Stater Island. Esa isla que está en Nueva York con poco alejada que también es una de las zonas bastante malas de Nueva York, donde vivían los miembros del Wu Tang Clan."
**Early Funding through Drug Dealing** : During their initial period, the Wu Tang Clan financed their first album by dealing drugs, which is one of the many legends surrounding the group's origins. This contrasts with the often caricatured "gangsta rap" image of other artists who, despite rapping about street life, were already wealthy and operating from a position of privilege. "Y tenemos también historias de cómo pues drapicheando con drogas, conseguiron pagarse el primer disco."
**Unique Lyrical Perspective** : The Wu Tang Clan offered a more realistic portrayal of "gangsta rap" in their lyrics, focusing on drug dealing, insecurity, and police brutality from the perspective of the citizen who suffers it, rather than from the detached, super-produced viewpoint sometimes presented by groups like Public Enemy. Each of the eight members had a unique lyrical style, contributing to a rich and varied sound that could be sarcastic, ironic, or even humorous. "Los Wu Tang Clan en sus letras no sabían del rapicheo, no sabían de la inseguridad, no sabían de la brota de las policial, pero desde el punto de vista del ciudadano que lo sufre."
**Metaphorical Lyrical Approach** : The group's lyrics were highly innovative for 1990s hip hop, which was typically direct and explicit. They extensively used metaphors inspired by martial arts, Shaolin philosophy, chess, and warfare, adding a sophisticated and distinct layer to their songwriting. "Lo usaban mucha metáfora que habían traído de esa inspiración de las artes marciales, de los soblines y de todo esto, incluso tenían metáforas de las cedres y de metáforas bélicas."
**Revolutionary Sampling Techniques** : The Wu Tang Clan revolutionized hip hop sampling with their debut album. Lacking a budget, they sourced samples from classic soul, funk, and jazz records of the 1940s, 50s, and 60s. RZA would take fragments, deconstruct and "break" them to make them unidentifiable, creating slow, sophisticated, and distinct rhythmic bases that radically differed from the fast, agile beats prevalent in 80s hip hop. This approach profoundly influenced subsequent artists like J.C. and Kanye West. "Y generó una manera de hacer bases muy distintas, radicalmente distinta a lo que se estaba haciendo."
**RZA's Innovative Record Deal Strategy** : RZA, as the creative and business leader of the Wu Tang Clan, devised a highly intelligent and specific record contract strategy in the 1990s. Recognizing that generic contracts often trapped individual members after a band's breakup, RZA insisted on a deal that bound the Wu Tang Clan as a group but left its members free to pursue independent solo careers and sign separate record deals. This concept, common today, was revolutionary and difficult to secure in the 90s, but RZA ultimately achieved it with RCA. "Entonces Reisa quería hacer un contrato discográfico que afectase al butanclan como conjunto, pero no a sus miembros como entidades individuales."
**Post-Fame Underground Image** : Even after gaining immense wealth and becoming multimillionaires following the success of *36 Chambers*, the Wu Tang Clan consciously maintained their raw, "ground-level" and "thrown-out" image. Their music videos from that period were deliberately unpolished and "super trashy," showcasing a commitment to their underground New York roots and never losing the "essence of the lower depths" that other rappers had shed. "nunca quisieron dejar ese aspecto de grauma y tirado."
**RZA's Five-Year Economic and Business Plan** : Between their first and second albums, RZA orchestrated what is known in hip hop history as his "five-year plan." As the group's leader, he meticulously developed an economic and business strategy for the Wu Tang Clan. The plan involved releasing the group's debut album, followed by successful solo albums from each of the eight members, before reconvening for another Wu Tang Clan album. This strategy led to the release of critically acclaimed solo works from members like RZA (under the name Gaysjar, as stated in transcript), Method Man, and Old Dirty Bastard (with *Return to the 36 Chambers*), allowing the Wu Tang Clan to dominate the mid-90s hip hop market through both group and individual projects and collaborations. "reisar que era el líder, no solo creativo sino que era el líder casi empresarial del Guttankland estableció todo un plan para hacer un desarrollo económico empresarial de lo que sería el Guttankland."

# genre - Hip Hop

## curiosities
**Golden Age of Hip Hop (1990-1996)** : The period between 1990 and 1995-1996 is considered the greatest era in hip hop history, marked by seminal albums from artists like Public Enemy, Ice Cube, Nas, Notorious B.I.G., and Tupac, leading up to the Wu Tang Clan's debut. This era saw the emergence of various rapper archetypes, from solo artists to large "clans" of eight or ten members. "Fue posiblemente la mejor época de la historia del hip hop."
**Evolution of Sampling** : The method of sampling in hip hop underwent a radical transformation starting with the Wu Tang Clan's first album. While 80s hip hop featured fast, agile, and dynamic beats, the Wu Tang Clan introduced slow, deconstructed, and broken samples, often sourced from 1940s-60s soul, funk, and jazz, creating a more sophisticated sound that influenced later artists like J.C. and Kanye West. "la manera de trabajar el sampling del hip-hop, yo creo que cambia radicalmente desde el primer disco del butanclan"

# genre - Rap

## curiosities
**Types of Rappers** : The rap genre features various artist configurations, including solo rappers like Eminem, duos or trios, and large "clans" of eight to ten rappers, with the Wu Tang Clan being identified as the most important historical example of the latter. "Tenemos tipos de raperos, tenemos el rapero en solitario, como en Eminem tenemos dos raperos que son dos, o que son tres, y luego tenemos esos cruz o estos clanes tan enormes de ocho de diez raperos que son tan curiosos"
**Gangsta Rap Portrayal Differences** : While 1990s gangsta rap artists were often depicted as "tough" and involved in "gangsta" culture, this image was sometimes exaggerated or caricatured. Many successful rappers of the era were already wealthy and detached from the street struggles they described. In contrast, the Wu Tang Clan offered a more realistic, suffering citizen's perspective on drug dealing, insecurity, and police raids, maintaining their connection to the "low-income neighborhoods." "Todos los grandes raperos de los 90 eran muy chungos y eran muy metidos en el Gansara. Pero a veces era un poco de coña, ahora un poco caricatura."

# label - RCA

## curiosities
**Wu Tang Clan's Debut Contract (No Advance)** : The Wu Tang Clan secured a contract with RCA, one of the world's most significant record labels, for their debut album. However, despite the major label backing, RCA did not provide any financial advance ("not a single penny") to produce the record, forcing the band to fund it themselves. This unique contract, championed by RZA, was designed to protect the individual members' rights to pursue solo careers independently of the group's contract. "Al final lo consigo con la RCA y aunque lo firma con una de las esporafilas más importantes en el mundo es como si en la práctica no lo hubiera hecho porque no es dio ni un centimo para hacer el disco."

**Analog Sound of Debut Album** : The Wu Tang Clan's debut album *Enter the Wu-Tang (36 Chambers)* had a distinctive sound that resembled an "underground demo tape from a cassette." # curiosity

**Golden Age of Rap (1990-1996)** : The period between 1990 and 1995 or 1996 is widely considered the "golden age of rap," a highly significant era in hip hop history. This era saw the release of pivotal albums such as Public Enemy's *Fear of a Black Planet* and Ice Cube's *AmeriKKKa's Most Wanted* in 1990, followed by debut albums from Tupac, Nas (in 1994), Notorious B.I.G., and the Wu Tang Clan (in 1993). "Fue posiblemente la mejor época de la historia del hip hop."
**Black Liberation Movement Influence on Hip Hop Lyrics** : In the late 1990s, hip hop lyrics, particularly those of groups like the Wu Tang Clan, began to incorporate influences from significant Black liberation movements in the United States, such as the Black Panthers and FIPS (referring to various liberation groups). This integration resulted in highly committed and explicit, yet intricately crafted, lyrical content that resonated with the underground movement prevalent in hip hop at the time. "aquí ya tenemos influencias del de muchos movimientos negros que fueron muy importantes en Estados Unidos, como los panteras negras, o incluso el FIPS, y todos estos grupos de liberación negros que fueron tan importantes"
