# artist - Cerrone

## curiosities
**Influence on US Electronic Music** : Cerrone's work, along alongside Giorgio Moroder, significantly influenced Afrika Bambaataa's instrumental developments and mixtapes, helping to bring European disco elements to the US and lay the foundation for Detroit Techno and Chicago House. "tenía importantísimas influencias de gente como cerrone o que incluso como yorio modorier."
**European Disco Rhythmic Foundation** : Cerrone is cited as an example of European disco artists whose music was characterized by strong, electronic rhythmic bases, which formed a crucial pillar for the foundation of techno music. "solo hay que escuchar a Cerrone y a Jordi o Moroder para ver que toda la música disco europea muy rítmica tenía bases rítmicas muy electrónicas y muy fuertes."

# artist - Kraftwerk

## members
Wolfgang Flür
## genres
Electronic Music
## albums
**Autobahn (1974)** : Described as an early Kraftwerk album with a very melodic component, lacking the rhythmic focus that would later characterize their work. "si escuchamos los primeros discos de Grabbar que como el autobango son algo muy melodicos no son algo muy rítmicos."
**Trans-Europe Express (1977)** : This album was specifically envisioned by Kraftwerk, leading them to commission the development of the Syntanorma sequencer to achieve its distinctive sound and marking a significant stylistic shift for the band. "fue inventado específicamente para que Grabbar quisiés el trans-Europe express."
**Man-Machine (1978)** : Considered by many electronic music scholars as the first "Techno" album in its modern sense, "Man-Machine" demonstrates a radical shift in Kraftwerk's style, moving from melody-focused synthesizer work to complex sampling and sequencing, with modern techno still resembling it significantly. "Para muchos estudiosos de la electrónica de man-machine grabbar es el primer álbum de música tecnotálico como lo conocemos."
**Computer World (1981)** : This album further solidified Kraftwerk's shift towards sampling and sequencing, following the conceptual change initiated by "Man-Machine," and features "Numbers," one of the most influential songs for defining techno music. "Esa cambio radical que se da con demand masín para trabajar focalizado en sampling y secuenciación se hace todavía mas forten en el adon siguiente en computer world."
## songs
**Numbers (1981)** : Housed on the album "Computer World," this song is identified as one of the most influential for defining techno music, being remarkably ahead of its time. "tenemos una de las canciones mas influyentes para definir la música tecnot que fue la canción numbers."
**Timeless Modernity** : "Numbers" was so advanced for its era that it continues to sound modern even today for many electronic music movements, showcasing its pioneering nature and enduring influence. "Esta canción fue tan tan avanzada a su tiempo que aun a día de hoy sigue sonando demasiado moderna para muchos movimientos electrónicos."
## curiosities
**Pioneering Technological Innovation (1976)** : Kraftwerk, known for commissioning custom machines, spurred a significant technological advance in 1976 by tasking the sound engineering company Matten & Wiechers with developing the Syntanorma sequencer, a pivotal moment in music history. "En 1976 Grabbar hizo un avance tecnológico en la música que cambiaría la música para siempre."
**Commissioned Machine Development** : Kraftwerk was accustomed to manufacturing or commissioning the invention of the machines they used, which led to the creation of the Syntanorma sequencer by Matten & Wiechers. "Grabbar que eran tan adelantados que estaban acostumbrados a fabricar o a encargar para que se inventaran las máquinas que ellos mismos utilizaban."
**Radical Stylistic Transformation** : The introduction of the Syntanorma sequencer caused a radical stylistic change for Kraftwerk, shifting their focus from melodic synthesizer work to complex sampling and sequencing, as evidenced by albums like "Trans-Europe Express" and "Man-Machine." "Estilísticamente la banda grabar cambia radicalmente gracias a esta máquina."
**Conceptual Shift Documented by Wolfgang Flür** : The profound conceptual change in Kraftwerk's approach to music, particularly the shift to sampling and sequencing with "Man-Machine," is recounted in detail in Wolfgang Flür's autobiography. "Este cambio conceptual de la banda grabbar que el propio golfan-fur narra acojonantemente en su autobiografía es alucinante cuando cuenta esta parte en su autobiografía."
**Influence on Hip Hop and Electronic Music** : Kraftwerk's music heavily influenced Afrika Bambaataa, who directly sampled musical fragments from the German band in his seminal track "Planet Rock," establishing Kraftwerk as a pillar for US electronic music, modern Hip Hop, Techno, and House. "Esta canción está tan influenciada por la música alemana y por la banda crap arc que directamente tiene fragmentos musicales sacados ampliados de la banda crap arc."

# artist - Afrika Bambaataa

## genres
Hip Hop
## songs
**Planet Rock (1982)** : This seminal track is identified as one of the absolute musical pillars for all electronic music made in the United States, including modern Hip Hop, Techno, and House music. It was heavily influenced by German electronic music, specifically Kraftwerk, from whom it directly sampled musical fragments. "su grandísimo hip planet rock que es uno de los pilares de toda la música electrónica que se hizo en estados unidos del hip hop moderno y de la música tecnota de la música House, uno de los pilares musicales absolutos en estados unidos es planet rock."
## curiosities
**Intellectual Father of Hip Hop** : Afrika Bambaataa is recognized as one of the absolute intellectual fathers of the birth of Hip Hop music, significantly shaping its early development. "Este hombre que es uno de los padres absolutos padres intelectuales del nacimiento de la música hip hop."
**Bridge to European Influences** : He served as the crucial link connecting European electronic and disco music with influences in the United States, being enormously influenced by European disco music and especially by Kraftwerk. "el nexo que conecta la música electrónica de música disque europea con las influencias en estados unidos es este hombre africa van bata."
**Instrumental Development** : Afrika Bambaataa's mixtapes, songs, and instrumental work, vital for defining music in the United States, incorporated significant influences from artists like Cerrone and Giorgio Moroder, thereby introducing these "minority influences" to the US. "toda la parte instrumental que desarrolló africa van bata que fue importantísima para definir la música en estados unidos tenía importantísimas influencias de gente como cerrone o que incluso como yorio modorier."
**Foundation for Techno and House** : By bringing these European influences, which were considered minority in the US, to the United States, Afrika Bambaataa laid an important foundation for the later creation of Techno music in Detroit and House music in Chicago. "fue precisamente gracias a africa van bata que trajo esas influencias completamente minoritarias en estados unidos que sin una de las bases importantes para luego crear la música techno en Detroit y la música House en Chicago."

# genre - Techno

## curiosities
**Conceptual Breaking Music** : Techno was a conceptually groundbreaking form of music, making it challenging to identify its foundational pillars at times. "Esta música fue tan conceptualmente rompedora que a veces es difícil buscar los pilares sobre los que se basa."
**Simultaneous Birth with House** : Techno was born in Detroit practically at the same time as House music in Chicago, evolving in two "sister cities" as very similar movements in their early stages. "a la vez que estaban haciendo la música techno en Detroit estaban haciendo la música jausa en Chicago."
**Shared Early Identity with House** : In its early days, Techno was very similar to House music, making it difficult to differentiate between them, with many anthems being shared across both genres. "Cuando nacieron la música jausa en Chicago y la música en Detroit eran muy parecidos, eran movimientos muy hermanos y nos cuesta muchísimo marcar las diferencias entre uno y otros los inicios."
**Two Pillars: Stylistic and Technological** : The birth of Techno is founded upon two types of pillars: stylistic and technological, with technological innovations being as crucial as musical influences. "tenemos que hablar de dos tipos de pilares. Tenemos que hablar los pilares estilísticos y los pilares tecnológicos."
**European Stylistic Roots** : The musical and influential pillars of Techno are primarily found in Europe, not the United States, which historically was not a land of great electronic music. "Los pilares musicales, los pilares de influencia de la música techno están aquí en Europa y no están en Estados Unidos."
**Main European Pillars** : The two main pillars for the foundation of Techno music are European Disco and German electronic music, particularly the work of Kraftwerk. "los dos grandes pilares sobre los que se monten la música techno, que es la música de disco europea y la electrónica alemana."
**Rhythmic Emphasis in Development** : The development of Techno in the mid-1980s heavily emphasized rhythmic bases, utilizing machines like the Roland TR-808, and moving away from the prominence of melodies and vocals. "se daba un protagonismo total y absoluto a las bases rímpicas y esto nos habría una nueva manera de interpretar la música donde dejábamos de lado las melodías y los vocales."

# genre - House

## curiosities
**Simultaneous Birth with Techno** : House music was created in Chicago practically at the same time as Techno in Detroit, evolving in two "sister cities." It might have originated a few years earlier but emerged almost simultaneously. "a la vez que estaban haciendo la música techno en Detroit estaban haciendo la música jausa en Chicago."
**Shared Early Identity with Techno** : At its inception, House music in Chicago was very similar to Techno in Detroit, making them difficult to distinguish. Many anthems were considered part of both genres. "Cuando nacieron la música jausa en Chicago y la música en Detroit eran muy parecidos, eran movimientos muy hermanos y nos cuesta muchísimo marcar las diferencias entre uno y otros los inicios."
**Independent Status Today** : Although originating as a "sister movement" to Techno, House music is now completely independent, with clear distinctions from Techno. "a día de hoy la música jausa es completamente independiente, que no tienen nada que ver y que son muy fáciles de separar."
**Rhythmic Foundation on Roland TR-808** : House music, along with Hip Hop and Techno, was rhythmically based on the Roland TR-808 drum machine, which became accessible and affordable to producers in the mid-1980s. "la música jausa y la música techno se basaron ritmicamente en la rolante R8808."

# genre - Disco

## curiosities
**US vs. European Conceptual Differences** : There were significant conceptual differences between disco music made in the United States and that made in Europe. "había diferencias conceptuales muy grandes entre la música disco que se hacían Estados Unidos y la que se hacían aquí en Europa."

"casi todas las bases de la música disco europea eran muy de la electrónica europea."
**European Disco as Techno Pillar** : The very electronic, strong rhythmic bases of European disco, exemplified by artists like Cerrone and Giorgio Moroder, constitute one of the grand pillars that founded techno music. "todas esas bases electrónicas tan duras que tenían la música disco aquí en Europa."

# instrument - Syntanorma Sequencer

## curiosities
**Kraftwerk Commissioned Development (1976)** : This sequencer was specifically developed by the sound engineering company Matten & Wiechers in 1976, commissioned by the band Kraftwerk. "la banda Grabbar que le encargaría a la empresa de ingeniería de sonido Matanang Weichers. El desarrollo de una de las máquinas más importantes de la historia de la música que es el sequenciador Sintanoma."
**First Highly Complex Sequencer** : The Syntanorma is identified as the first highly complex sequencer ever manufactured, designed explicitly for Kraftwerk's "Trans-Europe Express" album. "es el primer sequenciador altamente complejo que se fabricó que fue inventado específicamente para que Grabbar quisiés el trans-Europe express."
**Advanced Capabilities** : This sequencer was groundbreaking, allowing for 32 steps and 16 simultaneous channels, a level of complexity previously unimagined in recording and multi-channel processes. "Fue el primer sequenciador que permitía hacer 32 pasos y 16 canales simultáneos."
**Revolutionary Impact on Music Production** : The Syntanorma sequencer changed the concept of recording and the understanding of music forever by elevating sequencing and multi-channel processes to an unprecedented level. "Esta máquina cambiaría el concepto de grabación para siempre, cambiaría la manera de entender la música para siempre."
**Stylistic Transformation of Kraftwerk** : The machine radically transformed Kraftwerk's style, enabling them to move from melody-focused synthesizer work to complex sampling and sequencing. "cambio estilísticamente la banda grabar para siempre. Gracias al Sintanoma grabbar podía hacer procesos de secuenciación muchísimo más complejos, podía coger muchísimos canales y hacer secuenciaciones de sampling muy complejas."

# instrument - Roland TR-808

## curiosities
**First Computerized Drum Machine** : The Roland TR-808 is celebrated as the first computerized drum machine ever created, marking a pivotal moment in music history. "la rolante R8808 la primera caja de ritmos computarizada que existe."
**Enduring Importance and Value** : The TR-808 remains incredibly important for music history and is still used today. Original units are highly valuable and expensive, leading to numerous modern machines and software emulating or cloning its sound. "sigue siendo tan importante al día de hoy para la historia de la música que aún a día de hoy se siguen utilizando y las que quedan todavía en funcionamiento son carísimas."
**Modern Emulations** : Despite being 40 years old, the TR-808's sound is so iconic that modern machines and software, such as the Roland TR-8 and Behringer RD-8808, continue to emulate it. "tenemos máquinas como la rolante R8 o la rolante R8 o la beringer R8808 tenemos un montón de máquinas y de software que mula una día de hoy una máquina que tiene 40 años."
**Pillar for Post-1980 Music** : This drum machine is considered an absolute stylistic pillar for a vast amount of music created after 1980, forming the rhythmic basis for early Hip Hop, House, and Techno music. "Esa caja de ritmos es uno de los pilares absolutos de mucha música posibilidad 1980 es casi la base en la que se empezaron a construir las primeras bases de hip hop y la música jausa y la música techno se basaron ritmicamente en la rolante R8808."
**Accessibility in the Mid-1980s** : Although originally expensive, by the mid-1980s, after the release of the TR-909 and TR-707, the TR-808 became very affordable on the second-hand market, often available for as little as $150 in perfect working condition. "para mitad de los 80 como había salido la 909 y después había lanzado a 707 esta máquina se podía comprar muy barata se podía comprar hasta por 150 dólares funcionando perfectamente."
**Democratization of Music Production** : Its accessibility allowed many producers of House, Techno, and Hip Hop music to acquire the machine, which then led to rhythmic bases becoming the absolute protagonists in these genres. "fue gracias a que esta máquina de segunda mano era bastante accesible que casi todos los mismos productores de música jausa y de música techno y de música hip hop casi todo el mundo empezó a hacerse con estas máquinas."
**Foundation of Techno Rhythm** : Techno music is fundamentally built upon this type of drum machine, to the extent that it's possible to create an entire techno track using only one such device. "la música techno se construye en este tipo de caja de ritmo."

# curiosity

**Techno and House Origins - Diffuse Beginnings** : While Techno emerged in Detroit and House in Chicago, both in "sister cities" and practically at the same time (House possibly a few years prior), their initial movements were so similar and diffuse that it's difficult to differentiate them. Many anthems are shared between both genres, and much of the information regarding Techno's foundations is also applicable to 90% of House music. "los inicios eran muy difusos. Así que gran parte de lo que voy a decir, aunque no hablo de las grandes bases que asientan la música techno, esto es aplicable también en el 90% de la música jausa."
**Technological Pillars in Music Creation** : In the birth of techno music, technological pillars and innovations were just as crucial as musical influences, fundamentally shaping the direction and sound of the genre. "los pilares tecnológicos, las novedades tecnológicas fueron tan importantes como las influencias musicales."
**Shift from Melodies to Rhythms in Electronic Music** : The rise of electronic music styles like House and Techno, driven by accessible drum machines like the Roland TR-808 in the mid-1980s, marked a new way of interpreting music. This approach prioritized rhythmic bases and structures, which had previously been in a secondary role, over melodies and vocals. "dejábamos de lado las melodías y los vocales para dar un protagonismo a toda la base rímpica y a todas las estructuras rímpicas que siempre habían estado en cierto modo en un segundo plano."
**German Producers in European Disco** : The influence of German producers in European disco is noted, for instance, in the context of "Boni" (likely Boney M.), highlighting the electronic and rhythmic foundations that characterized European disco. "de tras de Boni en me estaba un productor alemán."
