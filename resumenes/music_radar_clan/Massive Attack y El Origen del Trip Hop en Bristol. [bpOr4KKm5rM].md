## curiosities
**Bristol Hip Hop Association (80s-1989)** : The Wild Bunch was a small association in Bristol active during the 1980s, including 1989. It served as a hub where many young, almost adolescent individuals gathered around hip hop culture, encompassing graffiti artists, DJs, singers, and breakdancers. This collective is credited with giving birth to the foundational bases of Trip Hop. "The Wild Bunch venía a ser una asociación dentro del Bristol que muchos jóvenes, casi adolescentes tenían en torno a la cultura hip hop."
**Dissolution and Artistic Movements** : The group eventually dissolved, which subsequently led to a series of incredible artistic movements. From its members emerged the three founding members of Massive Attack, as well as Tricky and Nellee Hooper. "Porque en ese preño grupo de Wild Bunch, que se acabó disolviendo para que ha en 1969 [sic, likely 1989/early 90s], e que los llenó una serie de movimientos artísticos increíbles."
**Mid-Late 80s Legacy** : This small group of almost adolescent people from Bristol, active in the second half of the 1980s, established one of the most important pillars of 1990s culture. Crucially, it laid the foundational influences for music that continues to resonate today. "Entonces tenemos ahí ese pequeño grupo de gente, casi adolescente, bristo, que en la segunda mitad de los años 80, gestó uno de los pilares más importantes de la cultura de los 90, pero sobre todo que sentó bases de influencias de la música que aún seguimos teniendo en día."
**Artistic Fusion Point in Europe** : The Wild Bunch was considered one of the major artistic fusion points in Europe during its era, leading to incredible outcomes. It not only spawned Massive Attack, Tricky, and Portishead, but also producers who shaped the sound of Björk, a more electronic U2, and Madonna, and notably included the artist Banksy. "de Wild Bans fue uno de los grandes puntos de glóson artística que tuvimos en Europa durante esa época y acabo generando cosas increíbles."

## albums
**Blue Lines (1991)** : During the recording sessions for *Blue Lines*, the three founding members of Massive Attack were present in the studio, along with Tricky, described as a "strange member" of the group. Also in attendance was a young production assistant named Geoff Barrow, who would later found Portishead. This convergence of key figures in a single studio is considered a pivotal moment for the gestation of the Trip Hop movement. "Durante la grabación del Blue Line se habían juntado los tres miembros a Masyl Atac con Tricky, que era ese extraño miembro de Masyl Atac, pero también había un joven físimo ayudante de producción, que era músico que estaba empezando, que se llamaba Jeop Barrow."

## curiosities
**Founding Members from The Wild Bunch** : The three founding members of Massive Attack originated from The Wild Bunch, a Bristol-based hip hop association that was active in the 1980s. "Porque por un lado tenemos ahí a lo que fueron los tres miembros fundadores de Masyl Atac."
**Robert "3D" Del Naja - Graffitero Background** : Robert "3D" Del Naja, identified as the most talented leader of Massive Attack, was not originally a musician within The Wild Bunch community but rather a graffitero. This background highlights the diverse artistic roots within the collective. "el líder más tan entoso de Masyl Atac, para que antes le consideramos el líder que hais proper de Naja, realmente no era músico en la comunidad de Wild Bans, sino que era grafitero."

# genre - Trip Hop

## curiosities
**Birth in Bristol with The Wild Bunch** : Trip Hop is commonly asserted to have been born in the city of Bristol, specifically within a very concrete place: The Wild Bunch association. This group, active during the 1980s, fostered a vibrant hip hop culture that laid the essential foundations for the genre. "En el reino Unido se suele decir que el trip home nació en la ciudad de Bristol, y la verdad es que no es solo la ciudad de Bristol, sino que nació un sitio muy concreto."
**Gestation by Pioneers in One Studio** : Prior to the formal existence of Trip Hop, a unique event occurred in a small studio where all the genre's great pillars converged. During the recording of Massive Attack's *Blue Lines*, Massive Attack, Tricky, and Geoff Barrow (who later founded Portishead) coincided, beginning the gestation of the Trip Hop movement simultaneously in that very studio. "Entonces justo tenemos en ese estudio grabación como los tres grandes pilares del trip hop, que fueron la desmasiva atac por el otro lado Strixi y por el otro lado Sportishead, coincidieron y empezaron a gestar lo que sería el movimiento trip hop dentro del mismo estudio al mismo tiempo."
**Widespread and Lasting Influence** : The Trip Hop movement from the early 1990s is credited with initiating a rejuvenation that influenced all the music from the late 90s, the 2000s, and even contemporary music. This includes shaping the sounds of artists like Björk, the more electronic work of U2, Madonna, and subsequent groups such as DXX. "realmente el trip hop no solo viene de esa cultura de más ciudad aquí de Tricky y o de Porti-Jed, sino que realmente viene todo en reinzamiento que se basa en toda la música que tenemos durante el final de los 90, durante el 2000 y casi que tenemos hoy en día."

# curiosity

**The Bristol Scene - Crucible of Trip Hop** : Bristol, UK, is widely recognized as the birthplace of Trip Hop, stemming from a very specific and influential association called The Wild Bunch. This collective, active throughout the 1980s, cultivated a hip hop culture among young people that would form the foundational bases for Trip Hop. "En el reino Unido se suele decir que el trip home nació en la ciudad de Bristol, y la verdad es que no es solo la ciudad de Bristol, sino que nació un sitio muy concreto."
**Convergence of Trip Hop Pioneers** : A unique and pivotal moment occurred in a small studio, prior to the formal existence of Trip Hop, where all the genre's great pillars converged. During the recording of Massive Attack's *Blue Lines*, the three members of Massive Attack, Tricky, and a young Geoff Barrow (who later founded Portishead) were all present, simultaneously beginning the gestation of the Trip Hop movement. "un día en el que un pequeño estudio antes de existir el trip hop se juntaron todos los grandes pilares en el mismo lugar."
