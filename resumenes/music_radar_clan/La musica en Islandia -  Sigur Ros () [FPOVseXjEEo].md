## albums
** ( ) (2002) - Untitled Album** : Released in 2002, this album is considered a masterpiece and a significant musical reference. It consists of eight untitled songs, with the first four being more cheerful and the subsequent four sadder, separated by a 36-second silence meant to simulate the time required to flip a vinyl record. The physical edition is distinctive, featuring a blank onion paper booklet with all information presented in white, making it conceptually more complex than The Beatles' "White Album." The album incorporates Jónsi's invented language, "Bonnesca," and unique guitar sounds produced using cello bows. The Icelandic experimental string quartet Amina collaborated on its recording. It has been used extensively as a soundtrack for films, commercials, and awareness campaigns. "En el 2002 hicieron una grandísima obra maestra que está considerada como uno de los grandísimos referentes de la música que es el álbum sin título, el álbum de los paréntesis."
**Babatikatiddo (Unknown Year) - Dance Soundtrack** : This is a 20-minute symphonic piece, considered a continuation or "second part" of the "( )" album. It was created in collaboration with Radiohead for a dance work by Merce Cunningham. Sigur Rós utilized many discarded sounds from the recording sessions of their untitled album for this project, releasing three songs for the soundtrack on a separate disc. This disc had very limited units and was not intended for commercial sale. The vinyl version is particularly interesting for featuring symbols resembling cave paintings or stick figures on its unrecorded side. "una pieza sinfónica de apenas 20 minutos, que viene a ser la segunda parte de este disco."

## curiosities
**One of the Great Bands of the New Century** : Sigur Rós is regarded as one of the most important musical groups of the new century, epitomizing the distinct musical style emerging from Iceland. "está considerado uno de los grandes grupos del nuevo siglo y refleja muy bien toda esa música distinta que se hacen en Islandia."
**Jónsi's Invented Language - Bonnesca** : Jónsi, the band's vocalist, created his own language called "Bonnesca" for many of their songs. This language is an alteration of Icelandic, where a consistent sound is inserted between each syllable, aiming to make the language more harmonious and musical, a technique that has yielded successful results and serves as a significant reference in the industry. "su cantante John se inventó su propio lenguaje, el bonnesca para hacer muchas de sus canciones."
**Conceptual Packaging of ( ) Album** : The physical edition of their "( )" album features a blank onion paper booklet, with all information printed in white, making it a unique and conceptually intricate piece. This design choice is noted as being more complex than The Beatles' "White Album" due to its complete absence of titles for the album itself, its songs, or its lyrics. "su librillo por dentro está totalmente en blanco, entonces es un poco el álbum en blanco, algo más complejo que el álbum blanco de los Beatles."
**36-Second Silence on ( ) Album** : The "( )" album notably includes a 36-second silence that separates its two halves: four more cheerful songs followed by four sadder ones. This specific duration was chosen to represent the theoretical time it takes to pick up a vinyl record, flip it over, and place it back on the turntable. "están separadas por 36 segundos de silencio, que es el silencio que en teoría se tarda en coger el vinilo, dar la vuelta y volnerla a dar la alplay."
**Critical Acclaim for ( ) Album** : The "( )" album is celebrated as one of the best albums in history, earning a place on the renowned Rolling Stone list and receiving exceptionally high critical ratings on Metacritic, ranging from 85% to 87%, which is considered an extraordinary achievement for the platform. The album commands immense respect across the entire music industry. "está considerado uno de los mejores discos de la historia, por la lista famosa de los Rolling Stone y que conceptualmente es muy interesante, también Metacritic, ha tenido nada mejores críticas de la historia de Metacritic, como 87, 85 por ciento."
**Unique Vinyl Art for Babatikatiddo** : The vinyl edition of "Babatikatiddo" is remarkable for featuring symbols resembling cave paintings or stick figures etched onto the unrecorded side of the disc, adding a visually interesting and complex element to the rare release. "tuvieron la increíble idea de poner símbolos como de pintura rupestre y de monigotes en la cara que no está grabada en el vinilo."

# curiosity

**Iceland as a Global Musical Hub** : Iceland, despite its small size of only around 300,000 inhabitants (comparable to a small city), stands as one of the world's major centers for musical production. This is evident in its disproportionately high number of active bands. "hay uno de los grandes núcleos de producción musical que hay en el mundo."
**High Number of Active Bands in Iceland** : As of 2014, Iceland, with a population of just over 300,000, boasts more than 3,000 active music bands. This translates to approximately one band for every 100 inhabitants, a statistic unparalleled globally. "tiene más de 3000 bandas de música en activo."
**Broad Artistic Production in Iceland** : Iceland's remarkable artistic output extends beyond music to encompass painting, literature, and virtually all other artistic disciplines, demonstrating a widespread creative inclination within the small nation. "se da también tanto en la pintura, como en la literatura, como en prácticamente todas las disciplinas artísticas."
**Radically Distinct Icelandic Music** : The music created in Iceland is described as "radically distinct" from the music prevalent in most parts of the world, primarily due to its foundation in its unique national folklore rather than the widely influential British folklore. "la música que se hace en Islandia es radicalmente distinta lo que tenemos en gran parte del mundo."
**Bonnesca - Jónsi's Invented Language** : Bonnesca is a unique language invented by Jónsi, the singer of Sigur Rós, for use in many of the band's songs. It is an alteration of Icelandic where a consistent sound is inserted between each syllable, intended to make the language more harmonious and musical, and has been very successful. "el bonnesca no es más que una alteración del lenguaje normal, el coge listandés y lo que hace es que entre cada sílaba le mete un sonido."
**Rolling Stone's "Best Albums of All Time" List** : The famous list compiled by Rolling Stone magazine recognized Sigur Rós's "( )" album as one of the best albums in history. "por la lista famosa de los Rolling Stone."
**Metacritic's High Ratings for Sigur Rós** : The critical aggregator Metacritic awarded Sigur Rós's "( )" album exceptional ratings, reportedly between 85% and 87%. These scores are considered "an absolute madness for Metacritic," signifying immense critical acclaim and respect within the music industry for the album. "Metacritic, ha tenido nada mejores críticas de la historia de Metacritic, como 87, 85 por ciento, que es una auténtica locura para Metacritic."
