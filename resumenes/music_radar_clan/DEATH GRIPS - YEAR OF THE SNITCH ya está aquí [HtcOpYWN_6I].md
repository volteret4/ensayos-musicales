## albums
**Gear of the Sneech (Unknown Year)** : This album, launched progressively over two months, generated immense expectation, as is typical for any Dead Grips release. It is seen as a return to the band's roots, satisfying listeners who may not have been fully convinced by their more recent stages, such as *Buttonlesspeak*. The album's sound is reminiscent of their early work, incorporating samples and fragments from *Ismilitar* and *Monistor*. While not a radical musical revolution, it offers significant new elements while maintaining the band's core identity, notably through the predominant use of distorted strings and guitar, marking an interesting evolution for their electronic, drums, and rap sound. "Al fin tenemos Gear of the Sneech de los Z-Grips, llevábamos ya como dos meses el lanzamiento progresivo, estos lanzamientos raros que le gustan tanto la banda."
**Buttonlesspeak (Approx. 2 years ago)** : This album was followed by a period of incomprehensible pause for the band, leading to much speculation about new projects and the band's future direction. During this phase, MC Raik's lyrical style evolved to include more structured and worked verses, with choruses that were more identifiable and contained fewer invented words, which some fans found made the arguments easier to follow. However, this period also saw the band's sound lose some of its "spontaneous aggressiveness," becoming more studio-polished, which did not convince all listeners. "cuando la banda había tenido como esa pausa que nadie terminó entender con el botonlesspeak"
**Aysmilitary (Unknown Year)** : The first part of this mixtape is particularly beloved by some fans, who preferred it over the band's later material. Samples and fragments from *Aysmilitary* are being utilized again in *Gear of the Sneech*, signaling a return to the band's original sound. "hay quien ama la primera parte del Aysmilitary"
**Monistor (Unknown Year)** : Like other Dead Grips releases, *Monistor* generated significant anticipation and is highly regarded by some fans, especially those who appreciate the band's earlier work. Samples and fragments from *Monistor* are being reused in *Gear of the Sneech*, contributing to the album's return-to-roots sound. "sobre todo de Monistor y ya no le ha gustado mucho lo que han hecho al resto"
**No Love de Huevina delante (Unknown Year)** : This album marked the beginning of a later stage in Dead Grips' career, which has garnered a strong following among other segments of their fanbase. "hay gente que le encanta la última etapa después del No Love de Huevina delante"
**EP del estero (Approx. 1 year before Gear of the Sneech's launch)** : This EP was released approximately one year prior to the progressive launch of *Gear of the Sneech*. Its release timeline places it alongside another parallel group's album, marking a period of active output for some Dead Grips members. "justo un año después del EP que había sacado del estero"

## songs
**Hothead (from Buttonlesspeak)** : This track from the *Buttonlesspeak* album was notable for its considerable guitar sound. However, this prevalence of guitar was an exception within the album and the band's overall output at the time, as guitar sounds had not typically predominated in Dead Grips' music until the release of *Gear of the Sneech*. "teníamos canciones como el Hothead que ya tenía bastante sonido de guitarra, pero bueno, era la excepción."

## curiosities
**High Expectation and Controversy** : Dead Grips consistently generate significant expectation with any new release, especially since their album *Monistor*, and are considered possibly the most important experimental band of recent years. Every direction they take is highly relevant, but their work also routinely sparks controversy regarding its quality, leading to divided opinions among fans who often prefer different stages of their career. "cualquier disco de los Z-Grips genera muchísima expectación, yo creo que desde de monisto o cualquier cosa que hagan los Z-Grips, genera expectación"
**Unexplained Band Pause After *Buttonlesspeak*** : Approximately two years prior to the discussion of *Gear of the Sneech*, Dead Grips entered an unexplained pause following the release of *Buttonlesspeak*. This hiatus caused widespread speculation, as the band members spoke of new projects and ideas but also expressed uncertainty about the band's direction, while providing no interviews or information, leading to much public guesswork. "la banda había tenido como esa pausa que nadie terminó entender con el botonlesspeak"
**Shift Towards Audiovisual Projects** : Around two years ago, Dead Grips announced their intention to focus on more audiovisual projects beyond just music, including cinema, films, and video creations. This commitment became evident with the initial launch ideas for *Gear of the Sneech*, which included a peculiar YouTube video displaying songs as iPhone messages, signaling an ambition beyond purely musical endeavors. The collaboration with an independent film director, Austro Nero Finca, further underscores this strategic shift, which is anticipated to be a major revolutionary step for the band. "querían empezar a entrarse en el proyecto de Dead Grips como un trabajo mucho más audiovisual y no solo musical"
**Progressive Launch Strategy for *Gear of the Sneech*** : True to the band's preference for "rare launches," *Gear of the Sneech* was released progressively over approximately two months, with much of the album's most anticipated content made available during this period. This approach has allowed fans to gradually experience the material leading up to the full album release. "llevábamos ya como dos meses el lanzamiento progresivo, estos lanzamientos raros que le gustan tanto la banda"
**Musical Evolution with De Chambelor** : The participation of De Chambelor, a classical string member of Tull, on *Gear of the Sneech* represents a significant evolution for Dead Grips. His contribution led to a prominent use of distorted string sounds, which was a surprising and novel element for the band, given their usual electronic, drums, and rap-focused sound, demonstrating an interesting progression. "ha podido darlo en el bastante nuevo. Al final sabíamos ya desde hace un tiempo que iba a participar de Chambelor, uno de los miembros de Tull, uno de los miembros clásicos de cuerda de Tull"
**MC Raik's Return to Original Lyric Style** : In *Gear of the Sneech*, MC Raik returns to his signature lyrical composition style, characterized by a profusion of invented words and unconventional, critical structures that often blur the lines between verses, choruses, and stanzas. This return to his aggressive, almost improvised delivery is appreciated by fans who missed this aspect in the band's more recent, structured works. "MC Raik vuelve un poco a esos orígenes de su composición de letras, que vuelve muchísimas palabras inventadas, vuelve esas cosas tan críticas."
**Collaboration with Independent Film Director Austro Nero Finca** : Dead Grips has engaged an independent film director, Austro Nero Finca from the United States, in their project. This collaboration with a non-musician is particularly significant as it aligns with the band's stated ambition to move towards more ambitious audiovisual creations, potentially marking a pivotal shift in their artistic output. "han participado como un director de cine independiente que a Austro Nero finca en Estados Unidos"
**YouTube as a Key but Problematic Platform** : Dead Grips have a strong affinity for YouTube, frequently publishing exclusive content on the platform. However, they also have a habit of deleting content shortly after publishing it, often within three weeks, which frustrates fans. The band faces a growing challenge with YouTube's censorship policies regarding aggressive, complex, or politically incorrect material, raising concerns about the platform's suitability for their future audiovisual projects and potentially forcing them to seek alternative communication channels beyond mainstream social media. "los adgriffs siempre han sido muy amigos de YouTube, de hecho han publicado en YouTube muchas cosas que sólo estaban en YouTube."
**Physical Release Details for *Gear of the Sneech*** : The physical editions of *Gear of the Sneech* are scheduled for release on September 7th. Harvest will handle distribution in the United States and most of the world, while Carla International will manage a smaller distribution in Europe. The releases will be basic, economical CD and normal vinyl editions, deliberately eschewing limited or collector's editions, which the band views as an act of coherence with their artistic project. "Será el 7 de septiembre, ya tenemos fecha que será por Harvest en los Estados Unidos y en casi todo el mundo."

"Muchas bandas experimentales a los 3, 4 años ya empiezan a dar ciertas señales de lotamiento"

# instrument - Strings

## curiosities
**Introduction of Distorted Strings in *Gear of the Sneech*** : The album *Gear of the Sneech* features a surprising and significant presence of distorted string sounds, an element that was previously not dominant in Dead Grips' musical repertoire. While "Hothead" from *Buttonlesspeak* had some guitar, the extensive and distorted use of strings in the new album marks a notable sonic evolution for the band, heavily influenced by the involvement of De Chambelor. "Ha sorprendido, yo creo que mucho el hecho de que haya mucho sonido de cuerda distorsionada, pero mucha cuerda que es algo que nunca había predominado entre los Gips."

# instrument - Guitar

## curiosities
**Prominence in *Gear of the Sneech*** : The guitar sound is described as "muy predominante" in Dead Grips' latest album, *Gear of the Sneech*. This marks a shift from earlier works, where guitar presence, such as in the song "Hothead" from *Buttonlesspeak*, was considered an exception rather than a defining characteristic of their sound. "Ahora yo creo que el sonido de guitarra es muy predominante"

# curiosity

**YouTube Censorship Challenges for Artistic Expression** : YouTube is identified as a leading platform in the censorship of "comprometido, agresivo, complejo, violento, poco políticamente correcto" material. This presents a significant problem for artists like Dead Grips, whose artistic expression often falls into these categories, potentially creating a barrier between their creative output and what the platform will permit them to publish, possibly necessitating new communication channels. "YouTube está siendo un líder absoluto de censurar material que es al mejor comprometido, agresivo, complejo, violento, poco políticamente correcto."
**Coherent Approach to Physical Releases** : Dead Grips maintains a consistent approach to their physical album releases, opting for basic, normal, and economically priced CD and vinyl editions for *Gear of the Sneech*. This decision to forgo limited, rare, or collector's editions is presented as an act of coherence, aligning with their artistic project and the type of music they create, demonstrating a commitment to accessibility over exclusivity. "Así que en un arbato de coherencia tendremos ediciones completamente básicas, normales y económicas que saldrán el 7 de septiembre."
