# artist - Juan Atkins

## members
Juan Atkins

## labels
Metroplex

## albums
**No U Yufo (Year)** : This track is recognized as the starting point of techno music. Juan Atkins, who had the foundational idea for techno, released this significant theme. It eventually sold over 5 million copies. "él sacó el grandísimo tema No U Yufo que da el inicio a la música de techno."

## curiosities
**Father of Techno** : Juan Atkins is credited as the creator and "great father" of techno, having the original idea for the genre. He was also responsible for officially naming the music genre "Techno." "Juan Atkins fue el creador, fue el gran padre del techno, Juan Atkins tuvo la gran idea del techno."
**Metroplex Founding (1985)** : In 1985, Juan Atkins established Metroplex, the first record label dedicated to techno music. "él hizo la primera discográfica de música de techno en 1985 Metroplex."
**Introverted Nature and Career Impact** : Juan Atkins was a very withdrawn, introverted, and closed-off individual who never wanted to leave Detroit. This personal trait meant he is not as widely recognized as a visionary as he arguably should be, nor did he achieve the same level of fame or wealth as some of his contemporaries. "Juan Atkins siempre fue una persona muy retraída, muy introvertida, muy cerrada, nunca quisiera salir de Detroit."
**Enmity with Co-founders** : As techno records began selling millions of copies, Juan Atkins, along with the other two founders, developed significant animosity due to the commercial success and perceived abandonment of the underground spirit. "Hubo enormes enemistas entre los tres grandes fundadores del techno."
**Continued Detroit Focus** : Unlike some of his peers who became global stars and incredibly wealthy, Juan Atkins remained primarily in Detroit. Despite performing concerts worldwide today, he is considered less famous and less rich than he could have become. "John Atkins se quedaron siempre en Detroit y nunca acusionaron a Saer de Detroit y aunque John Atkins oí da conciertos por todo el mundo, yo creo que no es ni la mitad de famoso, ni la mitad de rico de lo que podría haberse convertido."

# artist - Derrick May

## members
Derrick May

## albums
**Rhythm Is Rhythm (1987)** : This song was crucial in bringing techno music out of Detroit and into the global spotlight. Released in 1987, it precipitated the genre's explosion beyond its origins, leading to its discovery in Europe, where it became incredibly popular. "Esa canción de The Rikmai fue la que precipitó todo y la que sacó el techno de Detroit."

## curiosities
**Techno's Global Visionary** : Derrick May is recognized as the individual who took techno out of Detroit. While he wasn't the originator of the idea (Juan Atkins) or the primary creative force (Kevin Saunderson), he possessed the vision to believe that techno needed to expand beyond its local scene. "The Rikmai, el tercero, el hombre que sacó el techno de Detroit."
**European Breakthrough** : His song, "Rhythm Is Rhythm," caused a sensation in Europe upon its arrival, particularly after London producer Neil Rushton heard it and recognized it as the future of dance music. This led to May traveling to London and realizing the immense European popularity of his music. "Cuando a Europa llegó Rydem y Sridem, desde The Rikmai, Europa se volvió loca."
**Music Institute Resident DJ** : Derrick May served as the resident DJ at the Music Institute in Detroit, the first major techno nightclub. "en la que el propio The Rikmai sería el DJ residente."
**Commercial Success and Fame** : Derrick May became an incredibly wealthy and famous individual, especially in Europe, contrasting with Juan Atkins's more reserved career path. "The Red Macy se convertió en una persona increíblemente rica y fue una persona que se hizo increíblemente famosa en Europa."
**Enmity with Co-founders** : As techno records began selling millions of copies, Derrick May, along with the other two founders, developed significant animosity due to the commercial success and perceived abandonment of the underground spirit. "Hubo enormes enemistas entre los tres grandes fundadores del techno."

# artist - Underground Resistance

## members

## curiosities
**Formation and Philosophy (1989)** : Underground Resistance emerged in 1989 as part of the second wave of techno. This collective of DJs and producers formed with the explicit goal of reclaiming the essential, underground spirit of Detroit techno, reacting against its commercialization by multinational corporations. "En 1989 aparece Ander Grand Resistans... quieren retomar la esencia en derrándole de Detroit."
**Anonymous Image and Sound** : Members of Underground Resistance were known for performing camouflaged, maintaining anonymity. They developed a very minimalist, pure, and dense techno sound, distinct from the earlier, more house-like techno, focusing heavily on rhythmic bases. "underground resistans no se sabía quienes eran por que iban camuflados y cogen ese sonido techno que antes era tan parecido a música Houting y lo convierte un sonido muy minimalista, muy puro y muy denso."
**Social and Political Activism** : Underground Resistance was deeply committed to its underground philosophy, linking with African-American liberation movements and anti-capitalist urban guerrilla movements. This commitment reflected their desire to return to the primal philosophy of techno, where people without resources united in trance states through rhythmic bases. "Underground resistans estaba tan concienciado con la filosofía underground que estuvo vinculado por ejemplo en los movimientos de liberación afraamericanos, a movimientos de guerrilla orban antica pitalista."
**Collective and Evolving Entity** : Underground Resistance is described as a "meta-group" or an independent philosophy rather than a fixed band. People have continuously joined and left, making it an evolving entity where no single individual embodies Underground Resistance. "nadie en concreto es underground resistans, underground resistans es una filosofía independiente de entender el techno."
**Influence on Modern Techno** : Along with Richie Hawtin, Underground Resistance is largely responsible for creating the minimalist, rhythm-heavy, monotonous, and constant techno sound that is known today, often without melody. This sound is considered more characteristic of the second wave of techno than the initial creations of the genre's founders. "la segunda gran generación del techno, creo el techno que conocemos hoy en día, el techno minimalista, cargadísimo de base rímpica, monótono, constante, donde la melodía a veces ya ni siquiera existe."

## curiosities
**Detroit Origins** : Techno music was forged in the abandoned urban core of Detroit by a generation of children who grew up in the 1970s and early 1980s. These young, predominantly African-American individuals, trapped in a decaying city affected by deindustrialization and robotization, used cheap machines to create music expressing their complex relationship with technology and their environment. "Y ese en ese no clorvano de un millón de personas sin recursos, mayoritariamente afroamericanos atrapados en el núcleo de Nacidad abandonada a su suerte, donde se gesta la música tengo."
**Foundational Artists** : The genre was initiated by three young adolescents: Juan Atkins, Derrick May, and Kevin Saunderson, who acquired inexpensive machines to create music with the limited resources they possessed. "3 jóvenes adolescentes, Juan Atkins, Derrick Main y Kevin Sanderson compraron unas máquinas baratas y decidieron empezar a hacer música con lo que bonamente tenían."
**Key Influences** : Early techno drew influences from various sources, including Afrika Bambaataa's hip hop revolution, European electronic music (notably Kraftwerk), synth-pop, new wave from the United States, and Chicago House music. "sus influencias, pues principalmente las que había traído África Van Bata a revolucionar las estructuras del hip hop... venía música electrónica alemana como Crawford y sobre todo venían las nuevas cajas de ritmos programables... También tuvieron muchas otras influencias como el sign pop y el new wave que se estaba haciendo en Estados Unidos o la música jausa chicago."
**Contrast with Chicago House** : While the Detroit techno and Chicago house communities were closely linked, they differed significantly in their origins. Detroit techno emerged from the depths of the city's ruined suburbs among resource-deprived people, whereas Chicago house originated within the financial context of disco producers evolving their sound. "al contrario de la música techno de Detroit que nacen las profundidades de los suburbios de Detroit de gente atrapada sin futura en una ciudad en ruinas, la música jausa se monta dentro del dinero de muchos productores de música discó que evolucionan hacer la música jausa."
**Early Philosophy and Leisure (1980s)** : Early techno represented a new philosophy of understanding music and leisure, prioritizing rhythmic bases, deep sounds, and hypnotic rhythms. Parties were held in abandoned factories, venues, and commercial centers, focusing on trance-like dancing for 12-15 hours without drugs or alcohol, in stark contrast to later associations with excessive parties and drug use. "el techno trajo una nueva filosofía para todos esos jóvenes, el techno era una nueva manera de entender la música, era una nueva manera de entender el sonido, donde la base ritmica era la protagonista, donde los sonidos graves eran los protagonistas y donde los ritmos hipnoticos eran una nueva manera de pasar tiempo."
**Early Sound Characteristics** : The initial sound of techno was distinct from what is commonly understood as techno today, bearing a closer resemblance to early house music. It was more melodic, simpler, and less monotonous. "Los primeros años del techno son muy distintos a lo que tendemos hoy por techno, y se parece mucho más a lo que entendemos hoy por música House, que lo que entendemos por música de techno hoy en día."
**Official Naming (1988)** : The term "Techno" was officially coined by Juan Atkins in 1988, at the request of London producer Neil Rushton, for the first global compilation, "Techno! The New Dance Sound of Detroit." "Juan Adkins le puso nombre oficial a la música techno. Porque por la petición de Juan Adkins, ese recopilatorio se llamaría techno de Neil Sound of Detroit y oficialmente en 1988 nacía la palabra techno."
**Pre-Global Breakthrough (before 1987)** : Prior to the release of Derrick May's "Rhythm Is Rhythm" in 1987, techno was an isolated phenomenon within Detroit, a "hidden bubble" and a marginal movement of resource-poor youths in an abandoned city, lacking commercial interest. "Hasta esa canción de The Rikmai de 1987, el techno era una burbuja escondida en Detroit."
**Rapid Commercialization and Disillusionment** : In less than two years, techno transformed from a marginal underground movement in Detroit factories into a multi-million-dollar industry. This rapid commercialization, particularly driven by producers like Neil Rushton, led to a profound sense of disillusionment and animosity among the original Detroit founders, who felt the underground spirit had been abandoned for profit. "en menos de dos años, el techno pasó de un movimiento en el gran completamente residual... a ser una fábrica de dinero a millones."
**Second Wave and Modern Definition (1989)** : The second major wave of techno emerged around 1989, led by artists like Richie Hawtin and Underground Resistance. This wave sought to reclaim the genre's essential underground roots, developing the minimalist, rhythm-heavy, monotonous, and constant sound that largely defines techno as it is known today, often without melodic emphasis. "En 1989 aparece Ander Grand Resistans, una serie de DJs y productores de música techno que vienen después de los grandes creadores, que se dan cuenta de que las grandes multinacionales han cogido el espíritu underground de Detroit y lo han convertido en una fábrica de dinero, quieren retomar la esencia en derrándole de Detroit."
**Split into Commercial and Underground (1990)** : By 1990, techno diversified into two distinct paths: a commercial, more danceable, and club-oriented style (represented by "Techno 2: The Next Generation") and a harder, more underground, minimalist style championed by artists like Underground Resistance and Richie Hawtin. "se separaba el techno comercial del techno puro underground minimalista."
**The Great Techno Diaspora** : The arrival of techno in Berlin, a city that had previously been home to pioneers like Kraftwerk, triggered the "great techno diaspora." This led to the creation of over 200 sub-styles of techno across Europe within less than 10 years, reflecting Berlin's unique interpretation and global spread of the genre. "el techno llega a Berling crear el estilo del techno de Berling y sobre todo empieza la gran día por el techno."

## curiosities
**First Major Techno Club (1988)** : The Music Institute opened in Detroit in 1988, marking the establishment of the first major techno nightclub in a city where techno had previously only been played in abandoned buildings and venues. "El mismo año, en una ciudad en la que el techno solo se tocaba en edificios en ruinas... aparece la primera gran discoteca de música techno, Music Institute."
**Unique Operating Model** : The club, where Derrick May was the resident DJ, operated for over a year. It was notable for not selling alcohol or beer (only water and juice), having no entry fee, and attracting a clientele who often couldn't afford to buy anything due to their lack of resources. "The Rikmai sería el DJ residente, estaría abierto un año y pico, y donde ni siquiera se vendía alcohol ni cerveza solo podías ver agua y zumo y donde no se pagaba por entrar y donde la mayoría de la gente no consumía nada porque no tenía dinero."
**Hub for Second Generation Artists** : Key artists of the second techno generation, such as Carl Craig, Richie Hawtin, and Jeff Mills, frequented the Music Institute. They would attend to enter trance-like states through the techno music, often dancing continuously for 8 to 15 hours. "Gente como Carl Craig, como Richie Houghton, como Jeff Maiz, la segunda gran generación del techno, y van a Music Institute a entrar en estado de trance con la música techno y estar bailando durante 8 o 15 horas seguidas."

# curiosity

**Detroit's Industrial Decline and Depopulation (1960s-1970s)** : Detroit, a major US industrial hub dominated by American automotive giants, experienced a drastic decline. In the 1960s and early 1970s, Cold War policies aimed at depopulating cities to disperse populations in case of nuclear attack led to a massive exodus. Unlike service-based cities, Detroit's workforce lived outside the city, leaving a million mostly African-American, working-class people trapped in an abandoned urban core without work, as factories were outside and the city center became a ghost town for 40 years. "Detroit vivía rodeada de fábricas y la gente trabajaba fuera de la ciudad... cuando cogieron una ciudad de cuatro millones de personas como Detroit y la vaciaron quedó un millón de personas abandonada en su suerte atrapada dentro de esa ciudad."
**Impact of Robotization on Detroit (Late 1970s-Early 1980s)** : The second major industrial wave of robotization in the late 1970s and early 1980s transformed Detroit's factories from labor-intensive to robot-intensive. This brought new prosperity and money to the city but simultaneously caused many of the one million trapped inhabitants to lose their jobs, exacerbating social inequality and poverty. "la robotización trajo una nueva oleada de prosperidad a la ciudad de Detroit, trajo muchísimo dinero a Detroit, pero hizo que mucha gente fuese cada vez más pobre."
**Youth's Relationship with Machines in Detroit** : Children growing up in Detroit in the 1970s and early 1980s, amidst the city's decay and robotization, developed a strange relationship of fascination and hatred towards the machines arriving in the city. While robots made the city a leading industry, they also caused their parents to lose jobs, forming a backdrop for the birth of techno music. "toda esa generación de niños solo 70 y primeros años de los 80 empezaron a vivir una extraña relación de fascinación y odio con las máquinas que llegaban a la ciudad."
**Neil Rushton's Role in Globalizing Techno (1987-1988)** : Neil Rushton, a London producer, discovered Derrick May's "Rhythm Is Rhythm" in 1987, recognized it as the future of dance music, and brought May to London. May then brought Rushton to Detroit, where Rushton was so impressed that he compiled and released "Techno! The New Dance Sound of Detroit" in 1988, officially launching techno globally and commercializing the genre. "Neil Raston era un productor long dinense que cuando escuchó Rydem y Sridem, pensó que será el futuro de la música de baile y localizó The Rikmai y lo llamó Detroit y le dijo que tenía que irse a Londres."
**Techno! The New Dance Sound of Detroit (1988) - Compilation Album** : This was the first techno music compilation album sold worldwide, created by London producer Neil Rushton in 1988, one year after Derrick May's "Rhythm Is Rhythm" was released. Juan Atkins officially named the music "Techno" for this compilation, giving the genre its official designation. "Así es como en 1988... Neil Raston creó el primer recopilatorio de música techno que se vendió en todo el mundo y sacó oficialmente el techno de Detroit."
**Techno 2: The Next Generation (1990) - Compilation Album** : Released in 1990, this compilation represented the commercial side of techno after the genre's global explosion. It showcased a more danceable and club-oriented style, distinguishing it from the harder, more underground techno being developed by artists like Underground Resistance. "en 1990 salía el segundo oro que gran recopilatorio de techno, techno 2 de Next Generation."
