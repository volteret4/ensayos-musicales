# curiosity

**Audiophiles vs. Melomaniacs Distinction** : The audiophile community is characterized by an obsession with sound quality, purity, frequencies, and audio zones, often described as a "fetishism for the purity of audio." In contrast, melomaniacs are primarily focused on the music itself. While audiophiles can offer interesting insights, some of their beliefs are based on false assumptions, a lack of technical knowledge, or a confusion between how things are and how they wish them to be. "Los que no se país la diferencia entre audio filos y melómanos, los audio filos. Es una parte de la gente que está asesinada con la calidad de sonido, la pureza de sonido, sonas de audio, las frecuencias de sonido, es como que buscamos un fetichismo por la pureza del audio y los malo melómanos están más enfocados a la música."

**Misconception of Psychoacoustics** : In the audiophile community and product reviews (headphones, sound systems, speakers, digital audio like MP3s), psychoacoustics is often incorrectly referenced. A common misconception is that it's used to "trick your brain" into perceiving higher sound quality than actually present, implying it's a method to make cheap equipment sound better or to "deceive your brain." "Normalmente se suele hacer referencia que muchos equipos de sonido como altavoces auriculares, grabaciones digitales en audio, MP3, etc. Abúsan la psicóacústica para que tu cerebro perciba una mayor calidad de sonido de la que realmente hay."

**True Nature of Psychoacoustics** : Psychoacoustics is a complex and fascinating science that studies how the human body collects, processes, and understands everything related to audio, sound, and frequencies, both within and outside of music. It involves a two-part process: a purely mechanical function by the ear and a cerebral cognitive processing by the brain. This science aids in developing sound quality, musical instruments, recordings, and audio equipment. "La psicóacústica es una complejísima ciencia apasionante que nos ayuda a trabajar muchísimas cosas que tienen que ver con el sonido con el audio y con la música y fuera de la música."

**Functioning of the Human Ear** : Contrary to popular belief that some individuals possess superior hearing, the healthy human ear operates in a surprisingly simple and mechanical fashion. All healthy ears function identically, transforming audio waves into pulses much like a telegraph. This mechanical precision means that the ear itself does not account for differences in sound perception among individuals. "De hecho, en contra de lo que piensa mucha gente, que cree que tiene el oído de Robocop, un oído Sanot tiene un funcionamiento sorprendentemente simple y mecánico. Todos los oídos Sanos funcionan igual de una manera increíblemente mecánica y precisa, funcionan como un telegrafo."

**Myth of "Special Ears" Debunked** : The notion prevalent among many audiophiles and sound purists that they possess "special, fantastic ears" capable of detecting or identifying frequencies beyond the normal human range is a myth. Scientific understanding of the ear's mechanical operation demonstrates that all healthy ears function similarly, refuting claims of unique auditory capabilities based on the ear itself. "Y esto por desgracia rompe el mito de muchos audiófilos y muchos fetichistas que creen que tienen oídos especiales, espaciales, fantásticos que recogen frecuencias que mucha gente no puede recoger o que identifican frecuencias que mucha gente no identifica, no tiene sentido."

**Brain's Role in Sound Perception** : The ability for individuals to possess a "perfect pitch," experience hyperacusis, or distinguish every musical note is not due to the mechanical function of the ear, but rather to how the brain processes sound. The ear collects a vast array of sounds, but it is the brain's cognitive function that interprets and makes sense of this auditory information, leading to varying levels of perception and discrimination. "¿qué es lo que hace que gente tenga un perfect pitch y que es la gente capaz de distinguir perfectamente todas y cada una de las notas? ¿qué es lo que hace que gente que tenga hiperacusia? Eso depende completamente de cómo nuestro cerebro procesa el sonido y no de cómo nuestro oído convierte las ondas en impulso."

**Brain's Discriminatory Processing of Sound** : Over millions of years of evolution, the human brain has developed an incredibly intelligent and discriminatory way of processing audio waves and sound collected by the ear. Rather than merely processing all sounds, the brain learns to actively discriminate between interesting and non-interesting audio information. This selective filtering explains phenomena like ignoring background street noise until one consciously focuses on it, and this processing ability can be trained and improved. "Nuestro cerebro, más que procesar, aprenda a discriminar que audio es interesante y que audio no es interesante. Fíjate en esto. Realmente en este vídeo hay un montón de ruido de fondo de la calle, esto es grabando al lado de la calle, pero tu cerebro hasta que no me callado y no te has fijado no lo has oído."

**Psychoacoustics in Stereo Processing** : The entire concept and development of stereo sound processing is completely based on the principles of psychoacoustics. This science enables engineers to manipulate sound in a way that, by understanding how the brain localizes and interprets auditory cues, creates the illusion of spatial separation and depth, making the sound appear to come from different directions. "por ejemplo todo el invento del procesado de exterior se basa completamente la psicóacústica."

**Psychoacoustics in Audio Compression** : A significant application of psychoacoustics is found in music encoding and compression codecs, such as MP3 and most other compression formats. These codecs work by eliminating frequencies that the human ear naturally discriminates against or doesn't perceive as critical, regardless of whether they are technically present. This allows for significant audio data reduction by focusing computational efforts on maintaining the purity of audio in frequencies that the brain actively processes, without a perceived loss of quality. "Una parte importante de los codex de compresión lo único que hacen es que eliminar las frecuencias que discrimina a tu oído porque independientemente de que está inno las escucha y centrar sus esfuerzos en dar la mayor pureza de audio en las frecuencias de audio que tu cerebro si procesa."

**Perceiving Compressed Audio Quality** : The ability to compress audio significantly without a noticeable loss of quality stems from the brain's selective processing. While technical differences might exist between, for example, a 96 kilobit MP3 and a high-quality 1400 FLAC audio file, once a certain compression quality (like 320 or 512 kbps) is reached, it becomes "impossible" for the human brain to distinguish the difference. This is because the frequencies eliminated during compression are those that, even if perceived by the ear and transmitted to the brain, the brain chooses not to process as they convey no essential information. "Entonces es la razón por la que aunque técnicamente puedas en muchos casos ver la diferencia de un MP3 de 96 kilobich con un audio flaggen de gran disima calidad de 1400. Cuando tú llegas a cierta calidad de compresión 320, 512 llega un punto en que es imposible."

**Psychoacoustics in Electronic Instruments** : Psychoacoustics plays a crucial role in the development and realistic sound reproduction of electronic musical instruments. Thanks to this science, electronic pianos and drums are able to work and process sound almost identically to a grand piano or a classic percussion set. This is achieved by identifying the specific frequencies and sound manipulation techniques that the brain uses to transmit sensations, thereby creating a virtual sound that produces practically the same final result as acoustic instruments. "Cómo es que somos capaces de tener pianos electrónicos o baterías electrónicas que son capaces de trabajar y procesar el sonido casi como un piano de cola como una batería clásica de percusión? ¿Por qué gracias a la psicóacústica?"

**Sound Perception Beyond the Ear** : An incredible aspect of psychoacoustics is its application beyond the realm of music and even beyond the auditory system. The human body is not limited to perceiving sound solely through the ears; it can also "hear" through the skin. This expanded understanding opens doors for future applications, such as influencing physical responses to sound in environments like concerts or cinemas. "Otro cosa increíble de la psicóacústica es que también tiene increíbles aplicaciones fuera de la ámbito musical e incluso fuera de lo hido, la psicóacústica trabaja el procesamiento del audio y nuestro cuerpo increíblemente no solo escucha sonido por los oídos sino que también lo escucha por la piel."

**Historical Use of Psychoacoustics in Ancient Rome** : Psychoacoustics, though not named as such, was implicitly understood and applied in ancient civilizations. For example, advanced psychoacoustic knowledge was utilized in ancient Rome during the construction of their amphitheaters. This allowed them to design structures with acoustics that effectively projected sound and engaged audiences, showcasing an early understanding of how sound interacts with human perception and architectural spaces. "La psicóacústica ya la descubren en la roma no se emparte porque son conocimientos bastante avanzados de psicóacústica para construir sus anfite atros."

**Psychoacoustics in Aviation and Police Security** : The science of psychoacoustics extends its applications to incredibly diverse and unexpected fields beyond music. It is extensively utilized in aviation, where it contributes to noise reduction and comfort, and even at a policing level for developing security instruments. This demonstrates its crucial role in engineering solutions that consider human perception of sound in various practical and safety-critical contexts. "Y tenemos trabajo de psicóacústica en sitios increíblemente extraños como por ejemplo la psicóacústica se trabaja muchísimo en los aviones, se trabaja incluso a nivel policial para acen instrumentos en seguridad."
