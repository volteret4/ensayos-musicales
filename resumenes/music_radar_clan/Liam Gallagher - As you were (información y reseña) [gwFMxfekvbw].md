## albums
**Solo Album (Upcoming)** : This is the first album released under Lee Amgala Gheri's solo name. It was reportedly composed from numerous half-finished ideas and small compositions he had accumulated over many years, including parts that originated during his time with Oasis and were left incomplete. The album's release was first revealed in an interview in May 2016. "este primer disco firmado con el nombre de Lee Amgala Gheri."
**Solo Album - Critical Reception** : Critics are expected to be biased due to Lee Amgala Gheri's controversial nature, past conflicts within the music industry, and numerous enemies in music criticism. This bias was previously observed with some BDI material, which was reportedly unfairly criticized despite its quality. "Liam Gallagher es una persona muy polémica que ha tenido bastantes peleas y enemistades con gente importante de la industria musical y que tiene grandes enemigos en la crítica musical y muchas revistas no creo yo personalmente que vayan a ser muy objetivos haciendo la crítica de este disco."
**Solo Album - Self-Review (11/10)** : Lee Amgala Gheri himself reviewed his entire discography for NME magazine, assigning incredibly high scores to his works. He rated Oasis's "Definitely Maybe" and "Morning Glory" a perfect 10 out of 10, but gave his own solo album an unprecedented 11 out of 10. "él le apuntó con un 11 sobre 10 a su propio trabajo en solitario."
**Solo Album - Production and Collaboration** : The album's production involved notable figures, with Greg Castin contributing significantly as a producer and instrumentalist, assisting Lee Amgala Gheri in recording, composition, and overall musical support. Dan Grets also played a major role in the album's production, providing expertise as a respected sound engineer to ensure high-quality sound. Despite Lee Amgala Gheri being the primary artistic force, he shared songwriting rights and ownership with all the supporting musicians and personnel, showcasing a collaborative approach. "aunque este disco es de Liam Gallagher sus canciones están firmadas por mucha gente aunque Liam Gallagher ha tenido la idea principal del disco y eso el principal compositor y realmente artífice artístico del disco él ha compartido los derechos y la titularidad de las canciones con todos los músicos y la gente que le ha dado soporte lo cual realmente es bastante loable."
**Solo Album - Sound and Influences** : The album is remarkably consistent and well-produced, avoiding an outdated 90s sound often feared from artists of that era. It is considered the most updated-sounding work from the Gallagher brothers since Oasis, including BDI and Noel Gallagher's solo efforts which still carried remnants of 90s Britpop and Oasis's classic sound. The album draws clear influences from The Beatles, 60s rock, Lof (likely The Kinks), and The Rolling Stones, often transporting listeners to that era, yet maintaining a fresh and contemporary feel. "no es un disco que suene viejo que suene noventero o que suene un tanto despasado que siempre el miedo que tenemos cuando esos artistas de los 90 tan marcados sacan material casi 20 años después de su época dorada."
**Solo Album - Overall Quality** : This album is praised for its ability to avoid sounding repetitive, long, or monotonous, with many unreleased tracks proving more potent than the singles. It features strong songs like "El Bold" and "El iPhone 9" and has no weak points or filler tracks, making it easy to listen to on repeat. The distinct voice of Lee Amgala Gheri, however, makes it difficult to separate the album's sound from his past work with Oasis. "el álbum no llega un punto en el que ni se te hace repetitivo ni se te hace largo ni siquiera tienen ningún punto bajo en el que te vengan a ser una canción hacia adelante porque parezca de relleno."
**Definitely Maybe (Year Unspecified)** : This Oasis album was rated 10 out of 10 by Lee Amgala Gheri himself in an NME article where he reviewed his own discography. "un 10 para los dos mejores discos o así es el defero y maybe."
**Morning Glory (Year Unspecified)** : This Oasis album was rated 10 out of 10 by Lee Amgala Gheri himself in an NME article where he reviewed his own discography. "un 10 para los dos mejores discos o así es el defero y maybe y el morning glory."

## curiosities
**Controversial Public Figure** : Lee Amgala Gheri is described as an enormously controversial personality who often seeks headlines and creates polemic situations. This tendency frequently overshadows his other activities and makes it difficult to objectively assess his musical material alone. "Lee Amgala Gheri, enormemente polémico, que hace que al mezclar las tres cosas pues sea muy difícil ser objetivo y señirse solo a lo que es el material en sí."
**Peanut Allergy Incident (May 2013)** : In May 2013, a newspaper called San published a headline stating "un cacahuete casi mata Lee Amgala Gheri" (a peanut almost kills Lee Amgala Gheri), which was deemed a surreal and hard-to-believe incident. "cuando el periodico de San en mayo de 2013 sacaba un titular que ponía un cacahuete casi mata Lee Amgala Gheri."
**Financial Troubles (Last 2-3 Years)** : In the two to three years preceding his solo album, Lee Amgala Gheri experienced significant financial problems, leading him to divest a substantial portion of his assets. Initial speculation suggested his solo album was driven by economic interest to escape this situation. "los problemas financieros que estaba pasando Lee Amgala Gheri en los últimos años que habían hecho que se deshiciése de una parte importante de su patrimonio."
**Physical and Vocal Improvement (Last Year)** : Over the past year, Lee Amgala Gheri has undergone significant physical improvement, reducing his alcohol consumption and adopting a calmer lifestyle. This transformation has notably enhanced his voice, which has become more powerful, confident, and in-tune, reminiscent of his "golden years." This improvement has led to a reduction in sensationalist opinions about his purported financial motives for his solo work. "Lee Amgala Gheri ha cambiado mucho, ha mejorado mucho físicamente él ha disminuido mucho el consumo del col y lleve una vida mucho más tranquila, su voz ha mejorado un montón no se nos recuerda mucho a esa voz más potente, más segura y más afinada que tenía un poco en los años dorados."
**Chicago Festival Walk-Off (Early August this year)** : During an important festival in Chicago in early August, Lee Amgala Gheri walked off stage approximately 15-20 minutes into his performance without explanation. He later claimed to have vocal problems preventing him from singing, although this explanation was not convincing to the press. "en primeros agosto de este año cuando daban concierto un muy importante festival de Chicago en el que él apenas 15-20 minutos después de empezar la actuación se larga de escenarios y en dar explicaciones luego él decía que tenía unos problemas de voz que le impedían cantarlo cual no tenía mucha pinta de que fue ese cierto."
**Manchester Terrorist Attack Fundraiser (May this year)** : Prior to the Chicago incident, in May of the same year, Lee Amgala Gheri performed a small acoustic concert in Manchester to raise funds for the victims of a fatal terrorist attack in the city. Despite the noble cause, this event received no press coverage. "en mayo también había hecho un pequeño concierto agosto y con Manchester para recodar fondos en honor a las personas que habían sido víctimas el fatal atentado terrorista de Manchester y nadie le había hecho caso."
**Identifying Artistic Gaps** : Lee Amgala Gheri is credited with positively identifying his own artistic shortcomings and effectively collaborating with top artists to bolster areas where he might have been less proficient. This approach, where he surrounded himself with the best, suggests a lack of purely economic motives behind his new solo album. "Liam Gallagher yo creo que en este caso tenemos que decir positivamente que ha sabido identificar un poco sus canencias que no es una persona que sepa hacer de todo y ha sabido completarlas con los mejores artistas que podían darle soporte."
