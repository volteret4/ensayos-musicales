# artist - Joe Brieaz

## member of
Pigeon

## labels
Elektra Records

## curiosities
**Early Life and Child Prodigy Status** : Joe Brieaz was an American artist who showed exceptional talent from childhood, being described as a true child prodigy. He is considered one of the great names lost to music history, with some referring to him as the true predecessor of glam. "Joe Brieaz es un artista americano que había sido un verdadero niño prodigio ya desde la infancia."

**Early Glam Behavior (1968-1969)** : As early as 1968 and 1969, Joe Brieaz exhibited behavior that was "absolutely glam," predating the mainstream glam movement by three or four years. His first work with his band Pigeon in 1969 already contained many elements that would define glam in the early 70s. "incluso hay sitios donde se refieren a él como el verdadero predecesor del glam porque realmente nos podemos remontar incluso a 1969 al primer trabajo que había hecho Joe Brieaz con su banda Pigeon en el cual he ya tenido un comportamiento verdaderamente glam."

**Military Service and Psychiatric Confinement** : After his first album with Pigeon failed in 1969, Joe Brieaz faced severe personal difficulties. He was called for military service, encountered numerous problems, escaped, was eventually caught by military police, arrested, and subsequently confined to a psychiatric hospital for a prolonged period due to his homosexuality. "lo llamaron para el servicio militar del tubo un montón de problemas acabó escapándose lo acabaron cogiendo la policía meditario lo acabó arrestando acabó encerrado en un psiquiátrico durante un montón de tiempo por ser homosexual."

**Life in California and Discovery by Jerry Brande (Post-1972)** : After his difficult experience with the band Pigeon and his time in a psychiatric hospital, Joe Brieaz lived in poverty in California, residing in a completely abandoned and ruined apartment. During this period, he also resorted to prostitution to survive. It was under these circumstances that he was discovered by Jerry Brande, a significant talent scout of the 1970s. "vivía o mal vivía como podía en California de hecho vivía con un piso completamente abandonado y en ruinas al que se tenía también que dedicar a la prostitución y ahí es donde lo descubrió Jerry Brande."

**Elektra Records Contract and Promotional Campaign** : After being discovered by Jerry Brande, Joe Brieaz secured a major contract with Elektra Records for half a million dollars, representing a significant commercial opportunity for the United States to join the exploding global glam movement. Elektra Records invested heavily in his promotion, launching an extensive campaign through posters, magazines, and television. "él consigue gracias a Jerry Brande un primer contrato con el extra recorso por medio millón de dólares y la verdad es que para Estados Unidos era una gran oportunidad porque con el movimiento glam y con David Bowie explotando de popularidad en todo el mundo."

**Societal Resistance and Perceived Lack of Talent** : Joe Brieaz's career faced immense challenges, primarily due to the conservative American society that viewed the glam movement with suspicion, particularly when it came from American artists with such an appearance. Additionally, he was perceived to lack the exceptional talent of David Bowie or the guitar prowess of Marc Bolan, sometimes reducing him to a "caricature." "Por un lado teníamos una sociedad muy conservadora todavía en Estados Unidos que miraba con recelo todo este movimiento glam que venía del otro lado del charco... Joe Brieaz posiblemente no tenía ese gran talento que tenía David Bowie o esa increíble habilidad para tocar la guitarra que tenía Marmolan y se quedó un poco en un personaje a veces un tanto de caricatura."

**Failed Tour Attempts** : Due to his explicit homosexuality, eccentric behavior, and appearance, Joe Brieaz gained many enemies, hindering his career from the outset. He was unable to undertake a significant tour, as numerous venues directly refused to host his concerts. He had aspirations to tour Europe, specifically the Opéra Jaws de Paris, where glam music was highly demanded. "no llegó a casi poder hacer una gira porque hubo directamente un montón de sitios que se negaron a coger los conciertos de Joe Brieaz por su aspecto. Él quiso hacer una gira por Europa por justo además por la opera Jaws de Paris."

**Television Performances** : Despite the struggles with touring and societal acceptance, Joe Brieaz did manage to perform on television. Two of these performances are described as "authentic artistic jewels," providing a glimpse into his work from before the main glam era of the early 70s. "Lo que sí llegó es actuar en televisión, tenemos dos actuaciones de la televisión que son dos auténticas joyas de arte."

**Forced Retirement and Later Life** : Following the commercial failure of his first and second albums with Elektra Records and the widespread societal rejection, Joe Brieaz was forced into retirement. He continued to live poorly, at one point staying in a luxury hotel but eventually returning to prostitution. He tragically died of AIDS in the 1980s, completely forgotten and buried from public memory. "Poco después él acababa haciendo su retirada pero bueno es una retirada prácticamente obligatoria ante el rechazo de la sociedad y él acabó un poco mal viviendo... acabaría moviendo de sidal primero en el 80."

**Posthumous Rediscovery by Morrissey** : Joe Brieaz's work remained largely forgotten until Morrissey, during his solo tour, discovered Brieaz's figure and was completely captivated by his material. Morrissey attempted to organize fundraising and provide artistic support, unaware that Joe Brieaz had been dead for many years. "Morris y durante la gina de solitario de llora Arsenal que descubría la figura de llobria así que ha completamente innotizado por su material y quiso hacer un poco de intentar ayudar a recodar fondos y de ayudarlo como artista sin darse de cuenta de que llobria llevaba ya muchos años muerto."

**Legacy as a Forgotten Genius and Documentary Controversy** : Joe Brieaz's records were uncatalogued, forgotten, and unlisted until later influential artists, such as Morrissey, began referencing him as an important, forgotten genius. A documentary made a few years prior regarding Joe Brieaz is noted for its controversial portrayal, presenting him as a somewhat caricatured "great loser" who lacked talent, attempted to achieve success in David Bowie's shadow, and ultimately failed, which is criticized as a simplistic and exaggerated view of a complex figure. "es catalogados, olvidados hasta que grandes artistas postiliores pues como Morris y yo como muchos otros han tenido referencias a llobrias como lo importante que fue como ese genio olvidado. Tristemente tenemos también un documental que se hizo sobre llobria hace unos pocos años que también es bastante triste y que la verdad es que tiene también cierta controversia porque presentaré imagen de llobria un tanto de caricatura, un poco de gran perdedor que no tenía talento y que intentó destacar un poco a la sombra de David Bowie que no lo consiguió y que un poco era como un personaje ciertamente patético."

**Authentic Androgyne Identity** : Joe Brieaz is considered perhaps the most authentic protagonist of the glam movement because his androgyne image was not merely a marketing component, unlike Marc Bolan or David Bowie. He had been a genuinely androgyne child, and this was his true personality from a very early age. "posiblemente Joe Brieaz fue el más auténtico de los protagonistas del movimiento glam porque no olvidemos que ni Marmola ni de Bid Bowie aunque tenían esa imagen andró general en verdad era mente homosexuales sino que era un muy importante componente de marketing pero Joe Brieaz era el verdadero andrógeno que había sido un niño andrógeno de ese niño y que realmente su personalidad era así y había sido así desde mucho antes."

**Pioneer of Glam Ahead of David Bowie** : His recordings from late 1968 and 1969 suggest that David Bowie's work in the early 70s might not have been as innovative as commonly believed. Joe Brieaz, along with Marc Bolan, was developing glam two or three years before Bowie, indicating that Bowie might not have been the "great inventor" of glam, though he did lead it to its "golden age." "uno se da cuenta de que a lo mejor lo que hacía de David Bowie a primeros de los 70 no era tan tan innovador y que de David Bowie posiblemente no fue el gran inventor de la música clãm porque ya no es o llobria, teníamos también a Marvolan dos, tres años antes que él."

**Glam Pioneer and Definitive Figure (Early 1970s)** : David Bowie is widely associated with the early 1970s origins of glam music, particularly with `Hunky Dory` and his character `Ziggy Stardust`. He is credited with catapulting and defining glam, ushering it into its "golden age." "cuando nosotros pensamos en lo que son los inicios del glam yo creo que todos nos remontamos a primeros de los años 70 con David Bowie con el Han Quidori y con el Ciguit Star Dust pero realmente los inicios del glam fueron bastante más complejos."

**Androgyne Image as Marketing** : While David Bowie presented an androgyne image, this was primarily a significant marketing component rather than a reflection of true homosexuality. This contrasts with Joe Brieaz, whose androgyne presentation was described as genuine personality. "ni Marmola ni de Bid Bowie aunque tenían esa imagen andró general en verdad era mente homosexuales sino que era un muy importante componente de marketing."

**Talent and Role in Glam History** : David Bowie is acknowledged for his "great talent" and his role in bringing glam to its "golden age" in the early 1970s. However, the transcript also suggests that he might not have been the "great inventor" of glam, given earlier developments by artists like Marc Bolan and Joe Brieaz. "Joe Brieaz posiblemente no tenía ese gran talento que tenía David Bowie."

## curiosities
**Glam Predecessor (1969-1970)** : Marc Bolan, as the leader of T-Rex, is considered a significant predecessor of glam music. By 1969-1970, he was already exhibiting theatrical behavior in his compositions and videos, defining much of the glam aesthetic, including an androgyne style. "posiblemente el gran predecesor del glam fue el gran disismo Marbola el líder de T-Rex porque ya en 1969 70 tenemos en Marbola incluso composiciones y vídeos de como él ya tenía un comportamiento realmente te atral ya iba definiendo gran parte de la estética del glam y de Zerraval un poco tan escénico con los dupartos con esa estera un poco androjena."

**Evolution from Psychedelic Rock/Folk** : While Bolan's early work still retained elements of psychedelic rock and folk, it represented a significant evolution towards what would become glam music. This demonstrates his transitional role in the development of the genre. "posiblemente Marbola fue todavía un poco restos de lo que era el róxico del yco pero ya era una enorme evolución hacia lo que sería la música glam."

**Androgyne Image as Marketing** : Marc Bolan's androgyne image was primarily a marketing component, not indicative of true homosexuality. This is contrasted with Joe Brieaz, whose androgyne presentation was described as genuine. "ni Marmola ni de Bid Bowie aunque tenían esa imagen andró general en verdad era mente homosexuales sino que era un muy importante componente de marketing."

**Guitar Ability** : Marc Bolan possessed an "incredible ability to play the guitar," a skill highlighted when comparing him to other artists in the glam movement. "esa increíble habilidad para tocar la guitarra que tenía Marmolan."

# genre - Glam

## curiosities
**Early Origins (Late 1960s - Early 1970s)** : The origins of glam music are more complex than commonly perceived, extending beyond David Bowie's work in the early 1970s with `Hunky Dory` and `Ziggy Stardust`. Key predecessors like Marc Bolan (from 1969-1970) and Joe Brieaz (from 1968-1969) were already developing the aesthetic and theatrical components of glam years prior. "cuando nosotros pensamos en lo que son los inicios del glam yo creo que todos nos remontamos a primeros de los años 70 con David Bowie con el Han Quidori y con el Ciguit Star Dust pero realmente los inicios del glam fueron bastante más complejos."

**Sexual Revolution and Androgyny** : Glam music represented a new sexual revolution, characterized by a highly theatrical and androgyne aesthetic, as exemplified by artists like Marc Bolan with his elaborate attire. This visual and performative aspect was central to the movement. "este nuevo movimiento y esta nueva revolución sexual que en Zerraval Clam sino que teníamos más nombres realmente posiblemente el gran predecesor del glam fue el gran disismo Marbola el líder de T-Rex."

**US vs. European Reception** : In the United States, glam music faced significant resistance due to a deeply conservative society that viewed the movement with suspicion, particularly when it came from American artists with a glam appearance. Conversely, Europe, especially places like Paris, showed a "great demand" for glam music. "Por un lado teníamos una sociedad muy conservadora todavía en Estados Unidos que miraba con recelo todo este movimiento glam que venía del otro lado del charco... es en Europa donde había un gran grandísima demanda de música glam."

He played a crucial role in discovering Joe Brieaz from a demo tape and recognized that glam music was flourishing in the United Kingdom while the United States was lagging behind commercially.
