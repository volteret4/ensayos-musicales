# artist - Richard Hell

## member of
Television

## curiosities
**First Aesthetic Punk** : Richard Hell is considered possibly the first aesthetic punk, emerging in New York in the early 1970s, predating the London punk scene by several years. His distinct style was a precursor to the widespread punk aesthetic. "Fue posiblemente el primer punk estético que tuvimos."
**Distinctive Aesthetic** : Richard Hell's look included ripped clothes, very skinny jeans, Dr. Martens, a dishevelled and pale appearance, and a fragile aesthetic often linked to his drug addiction. This look profoundly influenced Malcolm McLaren. "Era el primero que tenía como esa estética tan marcada de ropa rota, de pantalones como muy pitillo, de las Martins, ese look también como muy desalignado, muy pálido, el por desgracia del inómano con lo cual pues tenía esa estética como tan frágil de persona frágil."
**Malcolm McLaren's Discovery** : While performing with Television at CBGB in New York, Richard Hell's aesthetic caught the attention of Malcolm McLaren. McLaren saw him not just as a musician but as a youth leader who could inspire an interesting aesthetic movement. "Malcomma claren ve un enorme futuro en televisión, pero sobre todo con un enorme futuro en Richard Hill. No tanto como músico sino como líder juvenilán de underground que podría generar un movimiento estético interesante."
**Failed Solo Career Attempt** : Malcolm McLaren attempted to take Richard Hell to London to manage his solo career. However, Hell rejected the offer, and his severe drug addiction made the project unviable. "Por un lado fue rechazado por Richard Hill, por otro lado su estado de droga edición lo hizo inviable."

# artist - Malcolm McLaren

## curiosities
**New York Dolls Manager** : Malcolm McLaren served as one of the early managers for the New York Dolls. His time in New York placed him in a position to observe the emerging punk aesthetic. "Malcomma claren era el manager fracasador de los New Yorkers. El año y oro subieron como varios managers y uno de los primeros managers que tuvieron en el malcomma claren que era británico."
**Discovery of Richard Hell's Aesthetic** : During his time in New York, Malcolm McLaren visited CBGB and saw Television perform. He was particularly struck by Richard Hell's aesthetic, recognizing its potential beyond just music, seeing Hell as a potential youth leader for a new movement. "Bansi BGB y B a los televisión, pero sobre todo le llama la atención la estética de Richard Hill."
**Importer of Punk Aesthetic to London** : After failing to bring Richard Hell to London for a solo career, Malcolm McLaren returned to the United Kingdom, carrying with him the aesthetic ideas he had observed from Richard Hell in New York. This was a pivotal moment in transferring the punk look across the Atlantic. "Malcomma claren vuelve al reino unido con la idea estética que había tenido de Richard Hill y ahí es cuando la exporta a su esposa que es la gran diseñadora Vivian Westwood."
**Sex Pistols Manager and Stylist** : As the manager of the Sex Pistols, Malcolm McLaren decided that the band should adopt and wear the clothes being sold and designed by his wife, Vivienne Westwood, at their shop, making them key figures in popularizing the aesthetic. "Malcomma claren sería el manadier que haría famos usados a los 6 pistos y sería el que tomaría la idea de que los 6 pistos deberían llevar un poco la ropa y las ideas que se estaba vendiendo y que estaba haciendo Vivian Westwood."
**False Claim to Authorship** : Malcolm McLaren claimed to be the "true architect" of the punk aesthetic. However, it was later revealed that he had copied the ideas, primarily from Richard Hell, making his claim false. "Mal con Maclaren reclamó ser el verdadero artífice de la estética punk y el resultado que era mentira."

# artist - Vivienne Westwood

## curiosities
**Co-owner of SEX (later 6) Shop** : Vivienne Westwood co-owned a shop, initially named SEX and later known as "6," located in central London. This shop became a crucial hub for the development of the punk aesthetic. "Junto tenía una tienda que sería la tienda 6 que estaría en el centro de Londres."
**Early Shop Style Evolution** : Her shop initially sold rockabilly clothing. Over time, she began incorporating sadomasochistic elements and blending them with the aesthetic ideas Malcolm McLaren brought back from Richard Hell. This fusion led to the creation of the first punk aesthetic garments. "Donde vendía ropa mas sin andar rocabili y donde tendría ya unas pequeñas inspiraciones un poco de sado mas okismo y un poco mezclando la estética de Richard Hill con las ideas que tenía Vivian Westwood."
**Pioneer of Punk on Catwalks** : Vivienne Westwood is recognized for bringing the punk movement as an aesthetic to fashion runways and defining its style for a wider consumer audience. "Vivian Westwood, la diseñadora que yo creo que convirtió el punk como movimiento estético en las pasarelas."
**Expensive and Copied Clothing** : The clothes sold at her shop, SEX (6), were very expensive. Consequently, the Sex Pistols often stole or were given clothes, and many fans resorted to copying her designs at home because they couldn't afford them. "La ropa que vendía Vivian Westwood en la tienda 6 no era tan tan importante o tan relevante porque una tienda como muy muy cara a los 6 pistos muchas veces robaban la ropa de la tienda otra vez se arregalaban y muchos acaban copiando la idea que tenía Vivian Westwood en su casa porque no podían pagarla."
**False Claim to Authorship** : Vivienne Westwood claimed to be the originator of the punk aesthetic movement. However, this claim was later disproven, as she had imported and adapted ideas initially conceived by Richard Hell. "Vivian Westwood, la diseñadora que yo creo que convirtió el punk como movimiento estético en las pasarelas. También resultó ser una impostora."

# artist - Sex Pistols

## curiosities
**Shop "6" Visitors** : The Sex Pistols were young individuals who frequented Vivienne Westwood and Malcolm McLaren's shop "6" in central London. Their presence there connected them directly to the emerging punk fashion scene. "Unos jovencillos que se pasaban por la tienda que serían los 6 pistos."
**Managed and Styled by Malcolm McLaren** : Malcolm McLaren became their manager and made the strategic decision that the band should wear the clothes designed and sold by Vivienne Westwood, thereby popularizing the distinct punk look. "Malcomma claren sería el manadier que haría famos usados a los 6 pistos y sería el que tomaría la idea de que los 6 pistos deberían llevar un poco la ropa y las ideas que se estaba vendiendo y que estaba haciendo Vivian Westwood."
**Styled by Jordan** : Jordan, the shop assistant at "6" and a friend of the band, played a crucial role in solidifying their image. She was responsible for dressing, making up, and styling all the Sex Pistols for their concerts, giving the punk aesthetic its final definition. "Fue la que acabó vistiendo, maquillando y peinando a todos los 6 pistos en sus conciertos."
**Global Aesthetic Revolution** : The Sex Pistols spearheaded a massive aesthetic revolution that rapidly spread worldwide. Shortly after their emergence, their visual influence was seen in places like Japan, various parts of America, and Germany, eventually inspiring bands like the Dead Kennedys in the US. "Los 6 pistos son una gran disima revolución y muy rápido se extiende la inspiración en este día de los 6 pistos a todo el mundo."
**Clothing Acquisition Methods** : Due to the high cost of Vivienne Westwood's clothing at the shop "6," the Sex Pistols often resorted to stealing the garments. Alternatively, they were sometimes given clothes, or they would copy the designs themselves, contributing to the DIY ethos of punk. "Los 6 pistos muchas veces robaban la ropa de la tienda otra vez se arregalaban y muchos acaban copiando la idea que tenía Vivian Westwood en su casa porque no podían pagarla."
**False Claim to Authorship** : The Sex Pistols themselves claimed to be the aesthetic fathers of punk. However, this claim was later proven false, as the aesthetic originated from earlier influences. "Los expuestos reclamaron ser los padres estéticos del punk, el resultado que era mentira."

# artist - Jordan

## curiosities
**Shop "6" Assistant** : Jordan worked as a shop assistant at Vivienne Westwood and Malcolm McLaren's shop "6" in central London. Her position put her at the heart of the burgeoning punk fashion scene. "Jordan que es esta chica que era la dependienta de la tienda 6 de Vivian Westwood."
**Personal Friend of Sex Pistols** : She was a personal friend of the Sex Pistols even before they achieved fame, which gave her direct influence on their image. "Era amiga personal de los 6 pistos antes y que franfamosos."
**Final Definition of Punk Aesthetic** : Jordan is credited with providing the final impetus and definition to the punk aesthetic movement. She took the ideas from Vivienne Westwood and Malcolm McLaren (which originated from Richard Hell) and, through her DIY approach, solidified the iconic look. "Ella le daría el escoletazo final al movimiento estético del punk."
**Sex Pistols Stylist** : Jordan personally dressed, applied makeup to, and styled all members of the Sex Pistols for their concerts. Numerous interviews, backstage footage, and photographs show her actively working on the band's look. "Fue la que acabó vistiendo, maquillando y peinando a todos los 6 pistos en sus conciertos. Tenemos un montón de entrevistas de conciertos de backstage de fotografías de los 6 pistos en los que vemos a esta chica Jordan maquillando y vistiendo a los 6 pistos."
**DIY Pioneer** : Embodying the punk ethos, Jordan herself cut, tore, and added safety pins to garments. She then dressed the Sex Pistols in these modified clothes for their early performances, marking the aesthetic birth of the movement. "Jordan Solf había cortado las prendas, había rotas prendas, les había puesto los imperdibles y ella habría vestido a sitvicios y a todos los 6 pistos en sus primeros conciertos dando así lugar al nacimiento estético del movimiento punk."
**First Modern Aesthetic Punk** : She is considered possibly the first modern punk aesthetically as we know it, taking the imported ideas and transforming them into a definitive style. "Ella fue la verdaderamente posilmente la primera punk moderna estéticamente tal y como la conocemos."
**Early Personal Style** : Before her role in defining the punk aesthetic, Jordan describes her own early fashion choices, often involving frilly party dresses that would get tangled during games. "Sometimes about seven really, I used to wear those party dresses that you always get caught out when you're playing hide and seek in air frills and things and I was used to get caught and you get caught out."

# genre - Punk Rock

## curiosities
**Cultural Movement** : Punk is more than just a musical movement; it is a profound cultural movement that influenced many aspects of society. It manifested in literature, poetry, ideology, politics, artistic movements, and, most notably, a significant and enduring aesthetic. "El punk es un movimiento musical apasionante, entre otras cosas porque no es un movimiento musical, es como un movimiento cultural que lo influyó todo y que se reflojó muchas cosas."
**Enduring Aesthetic** : The punk aesthetic has persisted for decades, continuing to influence urban tribes, youth movements, and even re-inspiring fashion designers on catwalks today. "La estética punk, a un hoy en día, continúa para mucha gente, continúa para muchas tribus urbanas, continúa siendo la influencia de muchos movimientos estéticos en los jóvenes posteriores y, a un de vez en cuando aparece una pasarela de algún diseñador que ha decidido reinspirarse en el punk clásico."
**Diffuse and Heterogeneous Nature** : The punk movement is characterized by its diffuse and heterogeneous nature, with many distinct visions emerging from different cities and types of punk. This means there isn't a single, unified punk aesthetic or belief system. "El punk es un movimiento como muy difuso y muy heterogéneo, hubo como muchas ciudades, muchos tipos y muchas visiones del punk totalmente distintas."
**Predominant Aesthetic Image** : The most widely recognized punk aesthetic, largely popularized by the Sex Pistols in the early 1980s and exported globally, includes plaids, Dr. Martens boots, chains, leather, and ripped clothing. "La gran imagen que tuvieron los expuestos, que hicieron como famos a la estética punk y que acabó predominando a primeros de los 80, como la estética de los expuestos, acabó siendo como exportada todo el mundo. Cuando pensamos en el punk, pensamos en los cuadros, pensamos en las Martins, pensamos en las cadenas, pensamos en el cuero, pensamos en todo esa cosa estética bondada, pensamos en la ropa rota."
**False Claims of Authorship** : For many years, various figures, including the Sex Pistols, Malcolm McLaren, and Vivienne Westwood, claimed to be the originators of the punk aesthetic. However, these claims were later debunked, revealing they had copied or adapted earlier ideas. "Hemos ido desenmascarando a falsos padres de la estética punk durante muchos años, los expuestos reclamaron ser los padres estéticos del punk, el resultado que era mentira. Mal con Maclaren reclamó ser el verdadero artífice de la estética punk y el resultado que era mentira. Vivian Westwood, la diseñadora que yo creo que convirtió el punk como movimiento estético en las pasarelas. También resultó ser una impostora."
**Transatlantic Origin** : The punk aesthetic did not originate in London as commonly believed, but rather in New York in the early 1970s with Richard Hell. Through a series of coincidences, this aesthetic idea traveled from one place to another and from one person to another, eventually becoming a global movement. "La verdad es que sale como de un sitio muy lejano de Londres y muy altrilados expuestos. Y las casualidades hicieron que esa idea estética fue se viajando de un lugar a otro y de una persona a otra hasta convertirse en un movimiento estético."
**American Punk Divergence** : While the Sex Pistols' aesthetic had a significant global impact, punk in America eventually developed its own distinct path in many aspects, even as it was initially influenced by the Sex Pistols' visual revolution. "El panque en América tendría como su propio camino en muchos aspectos, pero sí que estéticamente los 6 pistos eran como la gran revolución estética."

# curiosity

**SEX (later 6) Shop in London** : The shop "SEX," later known as "6," located in the core of central London, was a crucial location for the development of the punk aesthetic. Co-owned by Vivienne Westwood and Malcolm McLaren, it initially sold rockabilly clothes before evolving to incorporate sadomasochistic elements and Richard Hell's aesthetic, becoming the birthplace of early punk garments. It was also the meeting point for key figures like Vivienne Westwood, Malcolm McLaren, Jordan, and the Sex Pistols. "La tienda 6 que estaría en el centro de Londres, en el centro en el núclea en el Nondelondres, donde vendía ropa mas sin andar rocabili y donde tendría ya unas pequeñas inspiraciones un poco de sado mas okismo y un poco mezclando la estética de Richard Hill con las ideas que tenía Vivian Westwood en la tienda 6 se empieza a gestar las primeras prendas estética punk."
