## songs
**Love Will Tear Us Apart (1980)** : This song is hailed as one of the most important modern songs ever written. Due to the band's strong belief in its future significance for their career, they recorded it extensively, "a million times in a million places." The definitive version was recorded at Strawberry Studios, although earlier versions were also made at BBC studios. Prior to its release, Joy Division had a relative critical presence but struggled to gain widespread recognition or significant record sales. The band invested enormous effort from the outset, convinced it would make them stars, especially as they were already gaining strong momentum and preparing for their first US tour. They tested the song live during tours in late 1979 and early 1980, with extant recordings revealing subtle nuances and differences in the famous background melody, which they continuously refined. Its lyrics are rooted in Ian Curtis's tragic emotional turmoil, caught between his wife and a lover whom he did not desire but who made his life difficult. The title of the song is famously inscribed on Ian Curtis's gravestone. It holds the unique distinction of being the only song where Ian Curtis played guitar, albeit poorly, just a few chords, as he was not a musician. It is also the only music video the group ever made. The song was released approximately five or six times, achieving notable success upon its 1980 release, but truly became an anthem throughout the 1980s. It is recognized as one of the most covered and enduring singles in music history, comparable to major works by Pink Floyd, Michael Jackson, or The Beatles. Following its publication, Ian Curtis lived for three more months, during which the single began to achieve relative success, but his subsequent death led to the cancellation of the band's impending US tour. "la grabaron un millón de veces en un millón de sitios."

## curiosities
**Pre-Success Status** : Prior to the release of "Love Will Tear Us Apart," Joy Division had achieved a certain level of critical acclaim but had not yet commercially "taken off." They faced challenges with record sales and public recognition, despite positive reviews, and considered "Love Will Tear Us Apart" to be an excellent and potentially breakthrough song. "nadie les compraba los discos, nadie se entraba de quienes eran y esta canción la consideraban buenísima."
**Recording Controversy** : There is considerable debate and controversy surrounding the precise timing and location of the recording sessions for "Love Will Tear Us Apart." This is largely attributed to the band's perception of the song as a career-defining piece, leading them to record it multiple times across various studios. "hay bastante controversias sobre cuando y donde se grabó el LoFwiki y la se apaga entre otras cosas porque como ellos ya la consideraba una pieza que iba a ser muy importante su carrera, la grabaron un millón de veces en un millón de sitios."
**Encounter with U2 at Strawberry Studios** : During the recording of the final version of "Love Will Tear Us Apart" at Strawberry Studios, Joy Division crossed paths with a small group of young "fan" boys who were seeking a producer for their prospective debut album. These young boys were U2, and their singer, Bono, had extensive conversations with Ian Curtis, leaving a profound impact. "cuando ellos están grabando que era la versión final en los estudios strawberry coincidieron que estaba un pequeño grupo de chavales fones que estaban buscando productor para lo que va a ser subrimer algo."

## curiosities
**"Control" Documentary Reliability** : The documentary film "Control," which depicts Ian Curtis's life, is widely regarded as not entirely reliable. This skepticism stems from the fact that his widow was both the producer and the financial backer of the film, which many believe compromised its objectivity. "su productora y la persona que pusó el dinero fue su vida. Entonces tampoco es una versión muy fiable de la que fue la vida de Iain Cortis."
**Final Months Mystery** : The period of three to four months that elapsed between the recording of "Love Will Tear Us Apart" and Ian Curtis's tragic suicide is described as particularly interesting, yet there is a scarcity of detailed information regarding this specific timeframe. "los tres, cuatro meses que pasaron entre que grabaron el LoFwiki y el tráfico suicidio de Iain Cortis, en el cual no hay mucha información."
**Tragic Emotional State as Song Inspiration** : The lyrics and theme of "Love Will Tear Us Apart" are directly rooted in Ian Curtis's tragic emotional situation. He found himself caught between two women, his wife and his lover, neither of whom he truly wanted, and both of whom caused him significant distress. "la historia va de la tránsica situación sintial que tenía y en curtes y enfrascada entre dos mujeres a las que no quería y que la cien al bien posible, que era su mujer y su avante."
**Gravestone Inscription** : The title of "Love Will Tear Us Apart" is famously inscribed on Ian Curtis's gravestone. Although his original gravestone was stolen in 2018 and subsequently replaced, the song title has remained on his memorial marker since the day of his death. "desde el día de su muerte su la pida pone el título de esta canción."
**Non-Musician Guitarist** : Ian Curtis was not a trained musician and lacked the ability to play instruments. "Love Will Tear Us Apart" is notable as the sole song where he played the guitar, doing so in a "very aggressive" style, using only a few chords. "no sabía tocar instrumentos, se le eran gran triste pero no era músico y en esa canción sí que es la única en la que tomo unos pocos acordes en toda su descordación."
**Profound Impact on Bono** : Bono, the lead singer of U2, was deeply affected by his encounter with Ian Curtis at Strawberry Studios when U2 was an unknown, nascent band. Bono, who was 26 at the time, described Curtis as a tall, thin figure who sang aggressively but, outside of recording, was remarkably articulate and possessed a very good vocabulary. This meeting left a lasting impression on Bono, who has recounted it numerous times in interviews. "le impactó un montón de Ian Cortis que han sido muy alto, muy delgado que cantaba de esa manera tan agresiva pero que sin embargo cuando salía de la grabación en auntía hable, en auntía muy correcto hablando, tenía muy buen vocabulario."
**Post-Song Life Details** : After the publication of "Love Will Tear Us Apart," Ian Curtis lived for an additional three months. During this period, the song began to achieve relative success, and he was reportedly listening to hip-hop at home. His subsequent death left his wife a widow with a small child and forced the cancellation of Joy Division's impending US tour, which was scheduled to commence a week later. "Al estuvo tres meses más en los cuales el disco salió y empezó a terrativo éxito, el sarco, el miembros se escuchaba en su casa en Diadol, de Yipop, su mujer se quedó vio de la comuna niña pequeña."

# artist - New Order

## curiosities
**Temporary Name "No Names"** : Following Ian Curtis's death and the effective dissolution of Joy Division, the remaining members continued to perform for a brief period under the provisional name "No Names," meaning "the nameless ones." They eventually adopted the name New Order, but during this interim phase of several months, no new material was released. "los miembros que finalmente acabaron formando mi orden, ante un pequeño periodo de tiempo, ha siguieron actuando con el nombre de No Names, los sin nombre."

# curiosity

**"Love Will Tear Us Apart" Music Video Lip-Sync Discrepancy** : The music video for "Love Will Tear Us Apart" exhibits a noticeable lip-sync issue, where Ian Curtis's mouth movements do not perfectly match the vocals. This discrepancy is attributed to poor editing practices and the fact that the final, exact version of the song was not available or used during the filming of the video. "hay un cierto de calaz entre cuando Ian Cortis canta y cuando mueve la boca en el video-clip y eso se dele a uno en montas hecha pucero que hicieron y es como bonamente podía ni otra en la que grabaron tantas versiones del logo y tiras a parte que la versión final no fue 100% exacta a la versión que tenían que hecha cuando grabaron el video-clip cuando volvió a la insuna así que no coincide cuando el muro de la boca y lo que está diciendo."
**"Love Will Tear Us Apart" Music Video Visual Effects Origin** : The distinct visual effects featured in the "Love Will Tear Us Apart" music video, characterized by segments of footage appearing burned or damaged, were not intentional artistic choices. Instead, these effects were a result of the poor condition of the film stock and a malfunctioning camera used during the video's production. "las famosas efectos visuales que tienen video-clip en la que parte de ellos en algunas cienas cienentrozos de acarra como quemados de la fotografía no se leban ningún efecto sino que se leban mal estado que tenía la película y la cámara con la que grabaron que no funcionaba bien."
**"Love Will Tear Us Apart" Title Meaning (Spanish Interpretation)** : The title of the song "Love Will Tear Us Apart" is interpreted by the speaker in Spanish as "el amor no es nuestro cerrado o no son empedazos," meaning "love is not our closed or not broken into pieces." "el amor no es nuestro cerrado o no son empedazos."
**First Edition of "Love Will Tear Us Apart" Single** : The initial release edition of the "Love Will Tear Us Apart" single is highly coveted by collectors, described as extremely expensive, invaluable, and exceedingly difficult to locate. "La primera edición se te porda las escarísimas impagable malísimas encontrar."
**Limited Edition of "Love Will Tear Us Apart" Single (2011)** : A visually appealing limited edition of "Love Will Tear Us Apart" was released in 2011. This particular edition comprised 4000 units. **"Love Will Tear Us Apart" as an Enduring Single** : "Love Will Tear Us Apart" is considered one of the most enduring singles in music history, placed in the same league as major works by artists such as Pink Floyd (referenced as "del fin"), Michael Jackson, and The Beatles (referenced as "David Víde los bíqueres"). "es uno de los ingles más dedicados de la historia, la altura del fin y la de Michael Jackson o el David Víde los bíqueres."
