## albums
**Early Albums (Pre-Nursery Cryme)** : These initial records were made when the band members were minors and teenagers, still attending high school and not yet professional musicians. Peter Gabriel was present on these albums, which were not considered revolutionary at the time. While they provide context for the band's history and are perceived today as having "aged well," the critic notes they are not masterpieces and haven't "traveled well" over the years. "eran los discos de unos chales jóvenes que empezaban, que eran aficionados, que no eran músicos profesionales."
**Nursery Cryme (1971)** : Released approximately one year before *Foxtrot*, this album marked the entry of Phil Collins into the band. It introduced very interesting musical propositions and showcased fresh ideas brought in by Collins, contributing significantly to Genesis's evolving sound. "Nourci Crime sí que es verdad que posiblemente ya tenga propuestas musicales muy interesantes y que Phil Collins fue una de las personas que trajo ideas muy frescas a Gensys."
**Foxtrot (1972)** : This album is regarded by the critic as potentially the first great work and major record by Genesis. It represented a distinct and personal branch of progressive music, differentiating Genesis from other bands through its unique progressive style, rich instrumental sections, and the prominent use of flutes. It also marked the beginning of Peter Gabriel's charismatic and theatrical stage persona, as he started to discover himself and become "pletórico" in his performance. "el Foxtrot posiblemente sea el primer gran disco de Gensys, posiblemente sea la primera primer gran trabajo de Gensys."
**The Lamb Lies Down on Broadway (1974)** : Described as a masterpiece and "fantastic," this album holds historical significance as Peter Gabriel's final studio recording with Genesis before his departure. "ese discos es una obra maestra, es fantástico."
**Mid-1970s Transition Albums (1976-1977)** : Following Peter Gabriel's departure, Genesis released a couple of albums in the mid-1970s (specifically 1976 and 1977) that constituted a period of transition rather than a radical shift. During this time, the band gradually moved towards new sounds, abandoning the long, 20-minute song structures characteristic of progressive rock, a trend also observed in other contemporary bands like Pink Floyd. "viene un par de discos en mitad de los 70, del 76 y 77, que es como una etapa de transición dentro de genesis en la que poco a poco vayan yendo hacia nuevos sonidos, bandes cargando, ya no tenemos las piezas de 20 minutos."
**1980s Albums** : During the 1980s, Genesis adapted to the prevailing musical trends, producing records that were more "easy listening," faster-paced, and, in the critic's view, at times "more banal" or "romantic." This evolution aligned with the broader market direction, allowing the band to consistently enter the top charts in both the United Kingdom and the United States, while delivering revolutionary concerts and spectacle. "tenemos ese nuevo genesis con Phil Collins mucho más Isilisening, mucho más rápido, mucho más. Bueno, la etapa es mejor tanto más banal, románticas."
**Calling All Stations (1997) - Last Album Without Phil Collins** : This album, released without Phil Collins, is characterized as "a bit of a disaster" by the critic. Its poor reception is attributed to the significant time elapsed since the members had last collaborated effectively, rendering it somewhat unnecessary in the band's discography. "el último disco que sacaron sin Phil Collins, el Colin Aldestégios, bueno ese disco ya era un poco desastre."

## curiosities
**Peter Gabriel vs. Phil Collins Duality** : A long-standing debate revolves around whether Genesis's era with Peter Gabriel or its 1980s era with Phil Collins was superior. The critic argues that this perceived duality is an oversimplification, believing that the band's evolution would have followed a similar path even if Peter Gabriel had remained, driven by broader market changes. "Hay una grandísima dualidad en el mundo sobre que Gensys es mejor, si el Gensys original de Peter Gabriel o Gensys casi de los 80 que tenía Phil Collins."
**Collective Creative Contribution** : Genesis is characterized as a band where all members consistently played an important role in its creative development and success, rather than being dominated by a single visionary leader. "Gensys siempre ha sido una de esas bandas en las que todos los miembros han tenido una parte importante del desarrollo creativo del éxito creativo de la banda. No ha sido de esas bandas que hay un gran líder que es un visionario que lo hace todo."
**Mid-1975 Evolution** : In mid-1975, Genesis began a significant evolution influenced by numerous factors beyond just Peter Gabriel's departure. The music market itself was shifting, and despite progressive rock's popularity (with bands like King Crimson and Pink Floyd filling stadiums), Genesis chose to evolve with these market changes and public taste. "a mitad de 1975, justo a mitad de los 70, se dan muchos factores que hacen que el genesis empieza a dar un cambio a dar una evolución."
**Adaptation to Market Trends** : Genesis successfully evolved with the musical market and its audience, avoiding remaining "anchored" in a progressive rock style that began to go out of fashion. This adaptation, though perhaps not appreciated by all "golden era" fans, allowed the band to remain relevant. "genesis fue una banda que sigo evolucionando con el mercado musical y precisamente con el público musical."
**Post-1980s Decline** : The band's later stages, particularly after the 1980s, were not as strong. This decline is attributed not to a decrease in musical quality, but to the increasing number of parallel projects undertaken by individual members (such as Phil Collins' solo career and Mike + The Mechanics), which meant Genesis was no longer their primary focus. The band experienced a "slow agony" with music described as increasingly "banal" and "easy," leading to an "unromantic" end. "la última etapa de Jagenesis, bueno ya no fue lo que era."
**Reunion Rumors and Future Uncertainty** : For 15 to 20 years, there have been rumors of a Genesis reunion, and while some concerts have occurred, a full return is considered increasingly improbable. The current members, including Peter Gabriel and Phil Collins, lack the musical relevance they once had, making a full-scale comeback difficult to close definitively. "llevamos 15 años, 20 años hablando o teniendo rumores sobre la posibilidad de Jagenesis aunque algunos conciertos se han dado pero yo creo que ha diado y yo creo que cada vez está más complicada la vuelta de Jagenesis."
**Rock and Roll Hall of Fame Absence** : Peter Gabriel notably did not attend Genesis's induction into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame. This absence highlighted the complex, unresolved relationships within the band, as members, despite their past stardom, are no longer active forces in the contemporary music scene. "cuando fueron al jol de la fama Peter Gabriel no fue lo cual ya no se prendió bastante."
**Preference for Peter Gabriel's Mid-Era** : The critic personally prefers the albums from *Foxtrot* onwards, up until Peter Gabriel's departure, highlighting these as the band's strongest works from that period. "si me quedo con uno o con otro sinceramente me quedo con los tres últimos discos antes de la marcha de Peter Gabriel del Foxtrot para adelante."

# artist - Peter Gabriel

## member of
Genesis

## genres
World Music

## albums
**1980s Solo Albums** : During the 1980s, Peter Gabriel's solo career mirrored the broader market trends seen with Genesis, as he too gravitated towards shorter songs (three to four minutes), simpler choruses, and "more banal" lyrics. He also ventured into incorporating world music elements into his work. This trajectory suggests that Genesis's evolution would have been similar even if he had remained in the band. "Peter Gabriel sus discos en solitario de los 80 también, pues bueno, se fue metiendo más en el buen music, pero también fue yendo a piezas de tres minutos, de cuatro minutos, con estribullos más sencillos, letras un poco más banales."

## curiosities
**Gradual Development of Stage Persona** : Peter Gabriel's iconic charismatic and theatrical stage presence, characterized by costumes and dramatic performances, was not an overnight development. He gradually gained confidence and self-discovery, with his "pletórico" stage persona first fully emerging during the *Foxtrot* era. "Es una persona que poco a poco va cogiendo con fíjense y mismo se va descubriendo a si mismo, va cogiendo presencia, va encontrando su camino y el focus trotto es cuando la por primera vez lo vemos pletórico."
**Departure from Genesis (Mid-1975)** : Peter Gabriel left Genesis in mid-1975. While significant, his departure was only one of several factors contributing to the band's subsequent evolution, which was already influenced by broader changes in the music market. "la marcia de Pitergaven el pero no creo que sea la única."
**Absence from Rock and Roll Hall of Fame** : Peter Gabriel did not attend Genesis's induction into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame. This non-appearance highlighted an unresolved and complex relationship among the former band members. "cuando fueron al jol de la fama Peter Gabriel no fue lo cual ya no se prendió bastante."

# artist - Phil Collins

## member of
Genesis

## instruments
Drums

## curiosities
**Joining Genesis** : Phil Collins joined Genesis for the album *Nursery Cryme* (1971), bringing fresh and interesting musical ideas that significantly influenced the band's sound. "en el Nourci Crime que ya entra a Phil Collins ya habían traído cosas muy interesantes."
**Leadership in 1980s Genesis** : During the 1980s, Phil Collins fronted the "new Genesis," leading the band's successful shift towards a more accessible, faster, and commercially oriented "easy listening" sound that aligned with market trends. "tenemos ese nuevo genesis con Phil Collins mucho más Isilisening, mucho más rápido, mucho más."
**Solo Career and Parallel Projects** : Phil Collins pursued a successful solo career simultaneously with his work in Genesis, which became a significant commitment and one of many projects undertaken by Genesis members outside the band. "Phil con la escenía a su trabajo, su carrera en solitario."
**Inability to Play Drums** : Phil Collins has stated that he is no longer able to play the drums. Speculation exists regarding the reasons, including potential physical limitations or a preference not to be overshadowed by Peter Gabriel in a potential reunion scenario. "Phil Collins que nos dice que ahora que ya no puede tocar la batería no sé por qué no sé si es que le falta un brazo y no nos lo ha dicho a nadie."

# artist - King Crimson

## genres
Progressive Rock

## curiosities
**Adherence to Progressive Rock Core** : Unlike Genesis, King Crimson is cited as an example of a progressive rock band that maintained its essential sound and identity, operating within a "small ecosystem" even as progressive rock began to fall out of fashion in the mid-to-late 1970s. "Hubo bandas en la música progresiva, pues como puede ser King Crimson, que se mantuvieron ahí en su esencia, en un pequeño ecosistema cuando el roprogresivo fue pasando de moda."
**Stadium-Filling Popularity (1975)** : In 1975, King Crimson was one of the progressive rock bands capable of filling stadiums, indicating the genre's significant popularity in the market at that time. "King Crimson llena a bastadios."

# artist - Pink Floyd

## genres
Progressive Rock

## curiosities
**Stadium-Filling Popularity (1975)** : In 1975, Pink Floyd was a prominent progressive rock band capable of filling stadiums, illustrating the genre's widespread appeal in the market during that period. "Pin Floyd llena a bastadios."

"hasta bandas como Win Floyd estaban empezando a pasar de esas piezas interminables de 20 minutos y volvían a empiar las canciones más cortas."

# artist - Radiohead

## curiosities
**Gradual Progression to Prominence** : Radiohead is presented as a perfect example of a band that achieved importance through gradual progression and continuous improvement across its albums, rather than having an instantly "explosive" debut. This trajectory is compared to Genesis's early development. "reidioje desde el caso perfecto, o sea reidioje es una banda que fue progresando poco a poco."

# genre - Progressive Rock

## curiosities
**Market Dominance (Mid-1970s)** : In 1975, progressive rock was a dominant trend in the music market, with leading bands like King Crimson and Pink Floyd consistently filling stadiums. "Genesis está en el roprogresivo cuando el roprogresivo llena a bastadios, marraffiro King Crimson llena a bastadios, Pin Floyd llena a bastadios, entonces, de aquella seran la música era la tendencia en el mercado."
**Decline in Popularity (Late 1970s)** : In the second half of the 1970s, progressive rock began to fall out of fashion. While some bands like King Crimson maintained their niche, others like Genesis adapted by evolving their sound. "el roprogresivo fue pasando de moda."

# genre - Easy Listening

## curiosities
**1980s Market Trend** : Easy Listening became a significant market trend in the 1980s, influencing many artists, including Genesis and Peter Gabriel in his solo career, to create shorter, more accessible songs. "La música de los 80, así, bueno, pues genesis podía haberse quedado anclada en su música de siempre o podía seguir evolucionando con la música."

# curiosity

**Band Trajectories: Explosive Debuts vs. Gradual Progression** : Bands generally follow one of two paths to prominence: either releasing a revolutionary debut album that instantly transforms the music market, or gradually building their importance through a series of albums and continuous improvement over time. Radiohead and early Genesis are cited as examples of the latter. "Hay bandas que sacan un primer disco con el que revolucionan el mercado discográfico y hacen una obra maestra y hay bandas que llegan a ser muy importantes pero bueno, van arrancando poco a poco."
**Mike + The Mechanics - Genesis Spin-off** : Mike + The Mechanics is mentioned as one of several parallel projects that emerged from members of Genesis, illustrating how the band's focus diffused as individual members pursued outside ventures, particularly in the late 1980s and 1990s. "salía a Mike and the mechanics, como otro de los proyectos palelos que salían de Jagenesis."
