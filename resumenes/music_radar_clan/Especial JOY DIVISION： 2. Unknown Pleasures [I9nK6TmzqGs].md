## albums
**Unknown Pleasures (1979) - Debut Album** : The recording of Joy Division's first album, *Unknown Pleasures*, began in 1979. It is considered a possible peak work of Post-Punk and a key origin point for the genre. The pure recording process was very fast, taking only three to five weekends, but the overall production was protracted because the band members had day jobs and could only record on Fridays, Saturdays, and Sundays. The band entered the recording sessions with a clear idea and polished vision for the songs, having already developed a detailed demo and experienced a failed recording contract with RCA. The album was released several months after its recording. It achieved relative success, selling well for an independent, almost self-produced album, though it was not a major chart hit and didn't reach the top 70s. Its success enabled the band to tour extensively and become full-time professional musicians. Despite its current critical acclaim, *Unknown Pleasures* was not widely reviewed at the time of its release, and many contemporary "reviews" found today are retrospective, often from the late 1990s or 2000s. The album's legacy is that it significantly influenced many bands, like The Cure, who in turn influenced the public, rather than directly influencing the general public itself, drawing a comparison to The Velvet Underground's debut. "Continuamos este pequeño especial sobre la vida y la carrera de J-Division en el punto en el que lo habíamos dejado el momento anterior, que es justo 1979 cuando comienza la grabación de No Pleasure's, el primer disco del J-Division."
**Closer (1980) - Second Studio Album** : Joy Division began recording their second album, *Closer*, in 1980. For many, this album is not as famous as their debut but is considered to be of higher quality, and some fans even regard it as possibly the best Post-Punk work. "primero en 1980 empiezan la grabación del Closer, lo que sería el segundo disco, para muchos no tan famos como el primero, pero sí que posiblemente va a estar de mejor calidad que el segundo."

## curiosities
**Early Sound and Aggression (1977-1978)** : In their early years, Joy Division's live shows in 1977 and 1978 were characterized by an aggressive, punk-influenced sound. Ian Curtis displayed an aggressive stage presence, and the instrumentation often featured excessive reverberation, contributing to a somewhat "contaminated" sound, reflecting a preference for first takes. "Cuando vemos los conciertos que había dado Joy Division posiblemente en los primeros años, 77, 78, seguían siendo una banda bastante agresiva, vemos ahí en Cortis Cantardo, una muy agresiva, vemos esa reverberación excesiva a veces de los instrumentos, vemos esa contaminación que hace que no llegue a Sprite a la ende nada, en claro, es amor por las primeras tomas que los iba a grabar tan rápido."
**Dual Identity - Live vs. Studio** : Joy Division exhibited a powerful duality between their live performances and their studio recordings. Live, the band retained strong punk reminiscences in their behavior and sound, showcasing force and potency. In the studio, their sound was much more polished, ambient, and reflective. This significant stylistic difference was a source of important discrepancies among the band members throughout their career. "Esa gran dualidad entre la banda con esa fuerza y esa potencia en directo y esa banda como tan reflexiva y ambiental en los discos ha estado presentando en toda carrera de la banda y de hecho, bueno, tenemos bastante constancia por entrevistas y por ellos mismos de que fue un objeto de importantes discrepancias entre ellos."
**Failed RCA Contract** : Prior to recording *Unknown Pleasures*, Joy Division had a failed recording contract with RCA, where they had already made a recording. This experience meant they entered the *Unknown Pleasures* sessions with a clear and refined vision for their songs, having already developed a blend of punk and new wave. "ellos ya venían como muy asteados de la grabación fracasada de la recia en las cuales se vayan como una especie de mezcla, no entre el panquil new wave."
**Internal Creative Discrepancies** : Throughout the band's career, there were significant creative discrepancies, particularly between Martin Hannett's vision, supported by Ian Curtis, and the rest of the band, especially Peter Hook. Hook strongly opposed losing the initial freshness and spontaneous aggression of the band's early sound, leading to ongoing problems. These internal issues deepened during the exhaustive and demanding touring schedule that followed the success of *Unknown Pleasures*. "sabemos que durante todo el proceso de vida de la banda hubo como dos grandes bandes, por un lado en Martin Hannett que está embujando la banda y que Ian Kurt y centrarían muy bien a esas ideas que estaba introduciendo Martin Hannett y por otro lado teníamos al restaurar la banda, sobre todo el grandísimo Peter Hook, fue como uno de los grandísimos abandonados en contra de perder esa froscura y esa agresividad espontánea que iba a ir en Andro la banda del principio y que les daría bastante sin problemas durante toda su carrera."
**Professionalization and Touring Issues** : Following the success of *Unknown Pleasures*, Joy Division officially became professional, full-time musicians. This led to a complex and exhausting process of continuous touring. Initially, the band did not earn much money from concerts, forcing them to maintain a relentless touring schedule. This intense touring exacerbated internal problems, personal issues for Ian Curtis and other members, and financial difficulties. "con este disco yo oficialmente yo de visión ya se profesionalizan, ya pasan a ser una banda profesional, ya se dedican a esto a tiempo completo, empiezan ya un complejo y sobre todo hago tador proceso de gira continuada, que no consiguen ganar mucho dinero con los concertos todavía, entonces estaban como muy forzados, se están dando concertos de manera continuada, lo cual le irió sonaría bastante la banda, empezaron ya como a ver disputas bastante importantes entre los miembros."
**Ian Curtis's Health Issues** : During the period of intense and insatiable touring that followed the release of *Unknown Pleasures*, Ian Curtis's health problems became increasingly evident. "y luego aquí ya empiezan a ser patentes los problemas de salud de Ian Cortis y ya en esa bora gira de gira insaciable de concertos."

# person - Martin Hannett

## curiosities
**Key Producer for Joy Division and Post-Punk** : Martin Hannett was a pivotal figure in the recording of Joy Division's *Unknown Pleasures* and became a key figure in the history of Post-Punk. His production and post-production work accounted for approximately 85% of the time and budget for *Unknown Pleasures*. He influenced recording methods for subsequent bands in the UK during the 1980s. "Martin Hannon acaba siendo una piza clave, y es curioso porque gracias a esto acaba siendo una piza clave durante todo lo que sería la historia del postpunk durante la carrera de la banda J-Division e incluso posiblemente de los métodos de grabación de lo que serían las bandas posteriores en el Reino Unido durante los 80."
**Unknown Prior to Unknown Pleasures** : Despite his later importance, Martin Hannett was relatively unknown before working on *Unknown Pleasures*. He possessed a sophisticated and advanced vision for recording methods, both technically and artistically, which was crucial for the album's sound. "Martin Hannon cuando grabó, a no plecias no era nadie. Entonces, digamos, que fue un trabajo especialmente complejo, pero lo curioso es que Martin Hannon tenía como dos cosas que lo harían una persona clave para la grabación de este álbum."
**Shaper of Joy Division's Sound** : Hannett was instrumental in guiding Joy Division away from their punk reminiscences towards a more experimental sound, incorporating influences from bands like Cabaret Voltaire and Can. He introduced unorthodox sounds, such as those from boxes and cans, to create the characteristic atmospheric environment of Joy Division, which complemented Ian Curtis's lyrics. "Martin Jañez lleva a la banda poco a poco a ese sonido un poco más heredado de bandas más experimentales como Cabret Volter, incluso como Cannes, que los aferca a veces incluso al Crab Rock, la música elitrónica experimental y que leis introducen sonidos como muy poco orthodoxos."
**Aligned with Ian Curtis** : Martin Hannett's innovative production ideas for Joy Division's sound were well-received and strongly aligned with Ian Curtis's artistic vision, particularly with the themes explored in Curtis's lyrics. This alignment contributed to creative tensions within the band, as other members, like Peter Hook, preferred a rawer, more aggressive sound. "Martin Hannett que está embujando la banda y que Ian Kurt y centrarían muy bien a esas ideas que estaba introduciendo Martin Hannett."

# genre - Punk

## curiosities
**Waning Influence (Late 1970s)** : By 1979, the punk movement, which had seen a massive wave in 1977, was described as "dying." This decline created an opportune moment for bands like Joy Division to forge new, more reflective and atmospheric sounds, making the decision to move beyond punk an intelligent and successful one. "fue una decisión como muy inteligente y muy acertada salirse de un pan que estaba muriendo para entrar en ese terreno."
**Emphasis on Freshness and Immediacy** : Punk music, prior to Joy Division's emergence, was largely characterized by a focus on freshness, agility, and a preference for raw, first-take recordings. Joy Division's more reflective, deep, atmospheric, and absorbing approach to music was a radical departure from this prevalent style. "el pan que había dado como una visión muy fresca y muy muy ágil y muy muy de primeras tomas en la música y que alguien viniese como esa manera tan reflexiva y tan profunda, tan atmosférica, tan absorbente de trabajar la música era como muy innovadora."

# curiosity

**Frenetic Musical Period (1977-1981)** : The years between 1977 and 1981 are identified as potentially the most frenetic in music history. During this period, numerous musical genres were born and diversified, leading to significant ramifications across the musical landscape. "los años en 1977 a 1981 fueron posiblemente los años más frenéticos a la historia de la música, nacieron y se ramificaron muchísimas cosas."
**Retrospective Music Criticism** : A significant practice in music criticism, particularly for albums that gain cult status, involves retrospective reviews. Many reviews of *Unknown Pleasures* found in contemporary magazines and publications are not from its original release but were written later, often in the late 1990s, 2000s, or even 2007, after the album had become widely acclaimed. NME, which initially gave the album a poor review, later updated it multiple times and eventually deleted the original critique, acknowledging its misjudgment. "Todas esas publicaciones han hecho sus reseñas posteriores una vez que el disco se ha consagrado, esto pasa muy a minuto, podéis ver muchas de estas reseñas que la mayoría son de los 2000, de finales de los 90 primeros del 2000, la mayoría algunas incluso son del 2007."
