# artist - Michael Field

## genres

Celtic Music

## labels
Virgin Records

## albums
**Tubular Bells (1973) - Revolutionary Debut** : This album, released in 1973, revolutionized the music industry. Its immense success was pivotal for the foundation and growth of Virgin Records, contributing significantly to Richard Branson's fortune as it was the breakthrough hit for his nascent record label, which had started in the early 1970s. "todos lo conocemos porque en 1973 sacaba el grandísimo tubular bells con el que revolucionaba la industria de la música."
**Ommadawn (1975) - Defining Soundscape** : Released in 1975, this album is identified as another major work by Michael Field and forms part of his "great trilogy" from the early stages of his career. It was during the creation of this album and the preceding works that Michael Field further developed his distinctive sound, exploring new instruments and incorporating diverse influences. "en el 1975 se sale el Omadam, que es también otra de su obra CUMBE."
**Amarok (1990) - Anti-Commercial Culmination** : An incredible and complex uninterrupted 60-minute piece, released in 1990, that encapsulates and culminates all the influences from Michael Field's initial creative period. This album features a blend of progressive rock, Celtic music, and romantic elements, presenting a monstrous fusion of influences, styles, and instruments that nonetheless sounds remarkably cohesive and solid. It was explicitly designed to be anti-commercial, outside of prevailing musical trends, and structured to prevent the extraction of any single, a deliberate move made in defiance of Virgin Records' pressure for a "Tubular Bells 2." "sacaba una obra que es el Amarok que viene a culminar todas esas influencias de la primera etapa."

## curiosities
**Early Start & Autodidacticism** : Michael Field began his musical career at the age of 15, entirely self-taught, forging his own path as a musician. "empezó ya con 15 años, completamente autodidacta, he hecho a sí mismo."
**Relationship with Richard Branson & Virgin Records** : Michael Field's relationship with Richard Branson and Virgin Records was notably contentious, described as being almost "at loggerheads." Despite this difficult dynamic, Virgin Records and Richard Branson owe a significant portion of their success and fortune to the immense commercial impact of Michael Field's "Tubular Bells." "Virgin, le debe gran parte de su fortuna, porque el gran éxito de Virgin como discográfica se la debe al éxito de Michael Field."
**"Great Trilogy" - Early Career Definition** : The most recognized and legendary period of Michael Field's career is considered to be his beginning, between the ages of 17 and 20, when he released a "great trilogy" of albums. This era, encompassing "Tubular Bells" (1973) and "Ommadawn" (1975), was crucial for him to progressively define his unique musical sound. "la parte más conocida o la parte más mítica de Michael Field es precisamente el inicio de su carrera cuando apenas tenía 17, 20 años, cuando saca esa gran trilogía."
**Influences in Early Career** : Across his first three albums, including "Tubular Bells" and "Ommadawn," Michael Field gradually shaped his sound by exploring and incorporating a wide array of new instruments and sonic textures. His influences during this period included distinct elements such as Celtic music and the rhythmic patterns of African drums. "va cogiendo influencias de cosas como de la música Celta, ritmos de tambores africanos, muchas cosas que va incorporando poco a poco en esos tres discos."
**Defiance of Virgin Records with Amarok** : Michael Field created "Amarok" partly as an expression of his frustration and anger with Virgin Records, who had spent years pressuring him to produce a "Tubular Bells 2." Although he never entirely dismissed the idea of a sequel, he was profoundly dissatisfied with his relationship with Virgin Records, openly criticizing both Richard Branson and the label in various interviews, and believing Virgin's actions in the 1980s negatively impacted his discographic quality. "fue una hora hecha por Michael Field, y para la mala leche o el cabrero que tenía con Virging."
**Anti-Electronic and Digital Stance** : In the era of 1990, when "Amarok" was released, Michael Field was a staunch opponent of electronic instruments, electronic music, and digital production methods. This aversion dictated the album's production, which entirely utilized "old school" systems, a choice considered truly admirable given the technological trends of the time. "Michael Field en esa época era un grandísimo atacante de todos los instrumentos electrónicos y de la música electrónica, incluso era un gran atacante de la producción digital."
**Solo Recording and Virtuosity** : "Amarok" was recorded almost entirely by Michael Field alone, who played dozens of instruments masterfully and largely self-produced the album, with only minor punctual assistance from a producer. The resulting sound is astonishingly rich, giving the impression of an enormous band or even an orchestra of 30-50 musicians who have rigorously rehearsed for years, serving as a testament to his exceptional musical virtuosity. "Michael Field lo graba totalmente solo, el toca todos los instrumentos a algo una pequeña sección puntual, se graba a sí mismo, prácticamente se produce a sí mismo."

# label - Virgin Records

## curiosities
**Founding and Early Success** : Virgin Records was founded by Richard Branson in the early 1970s. The label achieved its initial and significant financial success primarily through the release of Michael Field's "Tubular Bells" in 1973, which generated substantial profits and propelled Virgin towards becoming the large multinational corporation it eventually became. "Richard Branson empezó su discográfica justo a primeros de los 70 y tubular bells fue el gran éxito que lo hizo ganar muchísimo dinero."
**Pressure on Michael Field** : Virgin Records exerted considerable pressure on Michael Field over several years, specifically pushing for the creation of a "Tubular Bells 2." This persistent demand was a major factor contributing to Michael Field's growing dissatisfaction with the label and his subsequent decision to release "Amarok" as a commercially unviable project. "Virging llevaba muchos años presionando a Michael Field para hacer el tubular verse 2."
**Relationship with Michael Field** : Michael Field expressed significant discontent with his tenure at Virgin Records, openly criticizing both the label and Richard Branson in numerous interviews. He believed that Virgin's involvement played a "quite bad role" and potentially impacted the quality of his discography during the 1980s, despite his work during that period being considered very good. "estaba ya muy descontento con su tapa con Virging Records, me refiero a bastantes entrevistas en las que la habla francamente mal, tanto de Richard Ranson como de Virging Records."

# curiosity

**Old School Production in the 90s** : In 1990, despite the availability of numerous innovative and interesting production techniques in music, including for symphonic and classical genres, Michael Field and his producer chose to implement a classical, "old school" production approach for the album "Amarok." This decision was partly influenced by Michael Field's strong opposition to electronic instruments, electronic music, and digital production. The meticulous quality and precision of this production method are remarkable, as the album manages to sound as if performed by 50 highly rehearsed musicians. "Michael Field y su productor terminan haciendo una producción clásica de la vieja escuela y es una pena, pero bueno esta hecha con tan nivel de calidad y tan nivel de precisión."
