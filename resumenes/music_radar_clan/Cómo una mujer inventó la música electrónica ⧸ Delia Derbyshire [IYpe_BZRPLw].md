## albums
**Electric Storm (1968)** : Delia Derbyshire, as a member of White Noise (a group she formed with two other electronic experimenters), released this album. It is widely regarded and almost always credited as the first official, 100% electronic music album in history, preceding works by artists like Tangerine Dream, Kraftwerk, and Jean-Michel Jarre. This album is considered one of the "holy grails" of electronic music and a pivotal work in music history, yet the woman behind it often goes unrecognized. "uno de los santos grillales de la electrónica, el primer disco oficial que tenemos de música electrónica."

## songs
**Doctor Who Theme (1963)** : Delia Derbyshire created what is possibly the first 100% electronic piece of music in history for the "Doctor Who" television series. She took some basic melodies and pieces developed by Ron Grainer, another Radiophonic Workshop member, and transformed them into a fantastic, entirely electronic composition that Grainer himself reportedly did not recognize. This piece served as a musical revolution, inspiring many to explore fully electronic music, years before other pioneers emerged. "Es posiblemente la primera canción 100% electrónica de la historia."

## curiosities
**Absolute Mother of Electronic Music** : Delia Derbyshire is regarded as the absolute mother of electronic music, pioneering the genre as we know it today. She laid the foundational concepts for modern electronic music through her innovative work with pre-recorded sounds and sequencing. "madre absoluta de la música electrónica."
**Early Life and Education** : Delia Derbyshire was born in the United Kingdom between the two World Wars. She was exceptionally intelligent, a trait that allowed her to attend Cambridge University, one of the world's leading institutions, at a time when very few women had such opportunities. She studied mathematics and music, and conducted experimental work in acoustics and electricity. "Nace en el rinonido justo entre las dos guerras mundiales."
**Discrimination at Decca Records** : Despite her advanced education in mathematics and music, Delia Derbyshire faced significant gender discrimination in the professional world. She was rejected by Decca Records, one of the most important record labels of the era, because their company policy prohibited hiring women for engineering or sound production roles, limiting their employment to cleaning or assistant positions. "no la contrataron porque por política de empresa en deca recorso no contrataban a mujeres ni en ingeniería ni en producción de sonidos."
**Early Career Struggles** : After graduating, Delia Derbyshire initially struggled to find work in her field due to her gender. She ended up giving private lessons to wealthy children in mathematics and other subjects, and also worked as an assistant for an important music distributor, a role far beneath her qualifications. "acabó, pues hagas la pobre dando clases a niños ricos."
**Joining the BBC Radiophonic Workshop (1960)** : In 1960, Delia Derbyshire joined the BBC, initially as an assistant, a position that underutilized her Cambridge education in mathematics and acoustics. She eventually managed to secure a transfer to the BBC Radiophonic Workshop, a pioneering project established in 1958 to experiment with creating music using electricity, after a significant struggle to be allowed to work there. "Delia Derbyshire en 1960 entra a trabajar en la BBC y se da cuenta de que en la BBC hay un proyecto que había fundado un par de años antes de la BBC, en el 58 que se llama de Radiophonic Workshop."
**Pioneering Electronic Music Techniques** : While working at the Radiophonic Workshop, Delia Derbyshire revolutionized music by using pre-recorded sounds as musical notes and developing sequencing techniques. This groundbreaking approach laid the conceptual groundwork for modern electronic music, though the speaker notes that sequencing in a basic form might have existed slightly earlier. "Esta mujer está utilizando sonidos pregravados como notas y está secuenciendo, está creando secuenciación, con lo cual esta mujer está dando las bases de lo que luego más adelante sería la electrónica moderna."
**Lack of Recognition for Doctor Who Theme** : Despite her instrumental role in composing the groundbreaking 100% electronic "Doctor Who Theme" in 1963, Delia Derbyshire received no credit for her work. Ron Grainer, who provided only basic melodies, was the sole person credited initially and received all the financial compensation. Her immense contribution remained unrecognized for many years, highlighting a profound injustice. "Y nadie se lo reconoció ni siquiera salía en los créditos de la película."
**Post-1970 Experimental Career** : After 1970, Delia Derbyshire's work remained highly experimental, with her continuing to create thousands of electronic sound effects for films, series, and advertisements. She also gave pioneering live performances of electronic music in experimental theaters at a time when dedicated electronic music albums were virtually non-existent. "Ella hizo miles de zenas de miles de efectos de sonidos electrónicos que se utilizaron en películas en series en anuncios en un montón de cosas."
**Delayed Recognition and Financial Injustice** : For many years, Delia Derbyshire remained an overlooked figure, never appearing in the credits for the "Doctor Who Theme" or receiving any payment for her contributions. While her work garnered significant enthusiasm in 1965 and 1966, it was only many years later that efforts began to acknowledge her legacy and credit her properly, though tragically, much of her work was lost due to this initial neglect. "nunca apareció en los créditos de la banda sonada del Dr. Juni de la serie ni de ningún lado, y nunca recibió ni dinero, y nunca recibió nada por lo que hizo."
**Legacy Recovery and Documentation** : In recent years, there has been a significant effort to recover and recognize Delia Derbyshire's legacy. A documentary has been made about her life and work, and an official website dedicated to her provides a comprehensive biography. However, due to the historical lack of recognition, much of her pioneering work and information has unfortunately been lost. "En los últimos años sí que se ha intentado recuperar un poco el legado de lo que fue de Adervisari y se ha hecho el buen documental..."

**Doctor Who Theme (1963)** : Ron Grainer provided some basic, simple melodies for the "Doctor Who" theme, which Delia Derbyshire then extensively transformed into the final 100% electronic composition. Initially, Grainer was the sole person credited for the piece, despite not recognizing Delia's final version and only having contributed rudimentary elements. Today, the credit is shared equally between them. "Ron Granier una grabe de hecho era una melodía muy sencilla y ella fue la que compuso todo."

## curiosities
**Original Contributor, Later Credit Dispute** : Ron Grainer was a colleague of Delia Derbyshire at the BBC Radiophonic Workshop. He supplied rudimentary melodies for the "Doctor Who" theme. However, his contribution was so basic that he reportedly did not even recognize the final, fully electronic version after Delia Derbyshire's extensive compositional and production work. Despite this, he was initially the only one credited for the iconic theme. "el propio Ron Granier ni siquiera reconocía la pieza."

# genre - Electronic Music

## curiosities
**Definition of Electronic Music** : Electronic music is defined as any music created using electronic instruments. This distinguishes it from acoustic music, which generates sound through physical elements like vibrating strings (guitar), air columns (flute), or percussive vibrations (drums). Electronic instruments, in contrast, generate music through electronic circuits or electricity. "la música electrónica es toda aquella que está hecha con instrumentos electrónicos."
**Early Forms and Exotic Sounds** : Prior to the emergence of fully-fledged electronic music as a genre, there were early electronic instruments like the Theremin. These instruments were primarily used to generate "exotic sounds" or special effects rather than full musical compositions. It wasn't until later pioneers that the potential of electricity for creating entire musical pieces was fully realized. "era como una manera de generar sonidos exóticos, más allá de generar música."
**Impact of Doctor Who Theme** : The 1963 "Doctor Who Theme," a 100% electronic piece, was a musical revolution. It demonstrated that complete musical compositions could be made entirely with electronic means, inspiring many artists to pursue electronic music. This pivotal moment occurred years before the emergence of groups like Tangerine Dream, Kraftwerk, or the work of Jean-Michel Jarre. "la banda zona del doctor Jo y la canción del doctor Jo fue una revolución musical."

# curiosity

**BBC Radiophonic Workshop (1958)** : Founded by the BBC in 1958, the Radiophonic Workshop was a revolutionary project in music history. Its purpose was to allow individuals to experiment with creating devices and techniques to make music using electricity. It became a crucible for musical advancement in electronics, producing some of the most significant pieces in the field, initially focusing on generating sound effects for science fiction films and television series. "la BBC, consciente de ello, creó el Radiophonic Workshop, que es una de las cosas más revolucionarias en la historia de la música, para que gente experimentase creando artilugios para hacer música con la electricidad."
**Distinction Between Acoustic and Electronic Instruments** : The fundamental difference between acoustic and electronic instruments lies in their sound generation. Acoustic instruments produce sound through physical means, such as the vibration of a guitar string, the wind in a flute, or the percussion of a drum. Electronic instruments, conversely, generate music using electronic circuits or electricity. "Tenemos dos grandes grupos de instrumentos, instrumentos acústicos y instrumentos electrónicos."
**Electronic Music Milestones for Comparison** : Key electronic music acts like Tangerine Dream (whose first album was in 1970), Kraftwerk (formed in 1970), and Jean-Michel Jarre are used as benchmarks to highlight the pioneering nature of Delia Derbyshire's work. Her groundbreaking compositions and albums significantly predate the mainstream emergence and recognition of these later influential artists. "Este canción es en 1963, pero el primer disco de Tanjera in Dream es en 1970. Crabworth se fundan en 1970 con la cual esta mujer hizo esto antes de Crabworth, antes que Tanjera in Dream y antes que ya miseria y antes que todos..."
