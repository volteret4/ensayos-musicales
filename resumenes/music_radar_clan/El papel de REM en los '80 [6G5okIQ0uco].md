## albums
**Murmur (1983) - Debut Album** : Released in 1983, this album marked R.E.M.'s debut with a distinctly clean guitar sound and Michael Stipe's unique vocal delivery, contrasting with the much denser and distorted guitar sounds prevalent in other releases of that year. Despite earning extensive critical acclaim and being frequently named one of the albums of the year by numerous important publications, *Murmur* experienced very low sales figures. Nevertheless, it cultivated a deeply loyal following, particularly among university students in the Southern United States, establishing the band as a cult phenomenon on student and local radio stations. "El primer disco de R&M, el humor del, tuvo muchísimas buenas críticas, fue nombrado casi en los discos del año por muchas publicaciones, incluso importantes, pero se vendió muy poco."
**Document (1987) - Final Album with IRS Records** : Released in 1987, this album represented a pivotal moment for R.E.M., acting as their breakthrough success. It was their last recording released under the independent IRS Records label, as the band subsequently moved to Warner Music following various disagreements with IRS. Some critical perspectives consider *Document* as the foundational album of a "quadrilogy," extending R.E.M.'s acclaimed trilogy into four albums. "en 1987 se acaban el document último disco que harían con IRS records, que sería el disco que le darían el salto, el gran éxito."
**Green (1988) - "Great Trilogy" Album** : This album is widely regarded as the first installment in R.E.M.'s "great trilogy" of albums released with Warner Music. This collection of works is considered one of the best trilogies in the history of US music, signifying a new era for the band after their departure from IRS Records. "sería el green, el out of time y el automátil, for the people."
**Out of Time (1991) - "Great Trilogy" Album** : As part of R.E.M.'s "great trilogy" released through Warner Music, this album is a crucial component of what is frequently cited as one of the finest trilogies in US music history. Its release contributes significantly to understanding the band's stylistic evolution and impact within the broader musical timeline. "sería el green, el out of time y el automátil, for the people."
**Automatic for the People (1992) - "Great Trilogy" Album** : This album completes what is commonly referred to as R.E.M.'s "great trilogy" under Warner Music, a series celebrated as one of the best trilogies in American music history. While some critics advocate for expanding this to a "quadrilogy" by including the *Document* album, these three records remain critically important for tracing the historical trajectory of music. "sería el green, el out of time y el automátil, for de people."

## songs
**Radio Free Europe (1981/1983)** : Initially released as R.E.M.'s first single before their debut album, it was later re-recorded and included on *Murmur*. The original version featured Michael Stipe's vocals without defined lyrics; he would instead murmur, babble, and play with his voice, treating it as an expressive instrument rather than delivering concrete words. "la primer single de Rem el Radio Free Europe que sacaron antes del primer disco, que luego tenía incluido lo volverían a grabar en el murmur, no tenía letra definida."

## curiosities
**Formation and Early Reception (1980)** : R.E.M. emerged in 1980 during a period of immense musical ferment, when various genres like punk, electronic pop, and hip hop were evolving, and guitar sounds were becoming increasingly hard and distorted. Initially, the band struggled to gain interest from major labels, spending years sending out demos and performing frequent local university concerts where they cultivated a dedicated fanbase. "aparece la banda rem, defendiendo su propia visión de la música, su propia visión del sonido, y no la verdad es que no desperto mucho interés discográfico, ninguna multinacional prácticamente ninguna empresa se interesó demasiado."
**Elusive Musical Influences** : A persistent challenge for music historians and the band itself is identifying R.E.M.'s clear musical influences, especially in their formative years. Their sound was unique and not easily traceable to earlier acts, though some speculate about influences from 1960s and 1970s British bands and elements of folk, with The Smiths also mentioned despite appearing later. "es muy difícil racer las influencias de rem porque no son claras, no se parece mucho a cosas que tengamos y antes, la propia banda rem le cuesta bastante también de hablar de sus influencias sobre todo en los inicios."
**Michael Stipe's Early Vocal Enigma** : In their nascent career, Michael Stipe's singing was famously enigmatic and extravagant, with listeners often unable to discern the lyrics. Stipe himself confirmed that in the beginning, he didn't sing defined lyrics but rather mumbled and vocalized, aiming to experiment with his voice as an instrument. "al principio las letras de Rem que eran tan enigmáticas, que nadie tenía ni idea de lo que cantaban, es que ni siquiera el maquet, el maquet les trae sabía lo que cantaba, el murmuraba porque él simplemente quería jugar con su voz."
**Distinctive Guitar Sound and Production Style** : R.E.M. developed a signature guitar sound characterized by its cleanliness and lack of distortion, a stark contrast to the hard, dense, and distorted guitar prevalent in 1983 when their debut was released. Furthermore, their early albums were noted for minimal production work, aiming to replicate their live concert sound, which set them apart from the often overproduced and synthetic music common in the 1980s. "aparecen ellos con una guitarra completamente descargada, con un sonido completamente limpio, y uno de las cosas más curiosas, y es que prácticamente no hay trabajo de producción."
**Pillar of 90s Alternative Rock** : R.E.M. became an immensely significant pillar in shaping the sound of 1990s music, serving as a primary source of inspiration for the first major generation of Californian alternative rock bands. Their mid-to-late 1980s university concerts in the Southern United States were particularly influential, with members of future bands like Pixies, Mercury Rev, and Pavement drawing directly from their performances. "sería un pilar importantísimo para definir muchísima música de los años 90, porque serían en esos conciertos a mitad y finales de los 80, en esas universidades a los ordenes de Estados Unidos, donde se cogería muchísima inspiración."
**Catalyst for American Alternative Rock** : R.E.M.'s role in the development of American alternative rock is a subject of debate; some consider them "the father" or the initial "germ" of the genre, while others see them as a crucial precursor to the alternative rock movement that truly flourished in the 1990s. Regardless of their exact designation, they are indisputably an absolute pillar for the entire sound that defined the era. "hay quien considera REM, el padre, el Hermen, el primer de la primera banda del rock alternativo americano, hay quien lo considera un Hermen de lo que sí sería el rock alternativo americano que realmente nacen los 90."

# label - IRS Records

## curiosities
**Discovery of R.E.M.** : IRS Records was a small independent record label that gained historical notoriety for being the company that discovered and signed R.E.M. in the early 1980s, notably being the sole entity that initially showed interest in the band. R.E.M. maintained a close working relationship with IRS, releasing numerous albums with them up until 1987, concluding with the album *Document*. "sería una pequeña discográfica independiente, IRS Records, que pasaría la historia por ser la discográfica que descubrió a rem, la única que se entera sería por ellos."
**R.E.M.'s Departure (1987)** : R.E.M. concluded their tenure with IRS Records after the release of their 1987 album *Document*, moving on to Warner Music. This transition was prompted by unspecified "problems and certain disagreements" that arose between the band and the independent label. "justo después por si otros problemas y ciertos asenfados con la discográfica se pasaría a Warner."

**R.E.M.'s Multinational Era (Post-1987)** : R.E.M. transitioned to Warner Music after experiencing "problems and certain disagreements" with their previous independent label, IRS Records, around 1987. # curiosity

**Late 1970s and Early 1980s Musical Effervescence** : The period spanning the late 1970s and early 1980s is highlighted as an exceptionally effervescent and dynamic era in music history. This time was marked by a confluence of musical shifts, including the lingering presence of punk, the rise of diverse electronic pop genres like synth-pop and new wave, the emergence of hip hop, and a significant evolution in guitar sounds towards harder, denser, and more distorted textures. "Los últimos años de 1970 y primero se los 80 fueron con diferencia los años más fervescentes de la historia de la música funa, locura."
**1983 US Music Market Contradiction** : The year 1983 presented a peculiar anomaly in the US music market, where many critically acclaimed albums, some deemed historical, received enthusiastic praise from critics but paradoxically failed to achieve significant sales or broad commercial success among the general public. This meant that despite their artistic merit and critical recognition, these records were not widely purchased. "1983 fue un año muy raro para la música en Estados Unidos, porque hubo discos que la crítica puso muy bien, que la crítica se puso loca y que nos emendieron."
**1980s Overproduction in Music** : Many musical recordings from the early 1980s were characterized by extensive overproduction and excessive sound engineering. This prevalent trend in production techniques is often cited as a key factor that contributed to the distinctively synthetic and sometimes artificial sound often associated with much of the decade's music. "los discos de los primeros años los 80 están completa, absolutamente sobreproducidos, hay demasiada ingeniería de sonido, es una de las cosas que hizo que la música de los 80 sonetanas y tan sintéticas, a veces tan artificial."
