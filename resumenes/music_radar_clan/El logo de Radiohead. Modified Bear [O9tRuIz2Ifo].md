# artist - Radiohead

## albums
**Kid A (2000) - Television Launch and Comic Circulation** : The band's famous bear logo first gained public attention through television advertisements for the launch of this album, referred to as "Qidei" in the transcript. These ads featured a static image of the bear's face. Additionally, the bear comic, consisting of about 15 vignettes, was circulated informally across the web during the period of this album's release. "el anuncio de televisión del lanzamiento del Qidei era una imagen estática con esta cara de este osso."
**Amnesiac (2001) - Comic Circulation** : The bear comic, comprising approximately 15 vignettes, was disseminated unofficially across the internet during this album's era, referred to as "la menecia" in the transcript. "fueron colgadas de manera completamente perdida por la web durante el quide y en la menecia."
**Hail to the Thief (2003) - Comic Deletion** : Just before the release of this album, referred to as "Hiltu de Cis" in the transcript, the bear comic and other related artistic material were unfortunately deleted. This action was part of an effort to update the band's overall artistic work and visual identity, and the comic has not been officially republished since, remaining lost. "fue posteriormente porrado justo antes del lanzamiento del Hiltu de Cis para actualizar todo el trabajo artístico de la banda."

## curiosities
**Bear Logo - First Appearance and Origin** : The band's famous logo, which depicts a bear rather than a cat as sometimes mistaken, first appeared in television advertisements for the launch of the album "Kid A" (referred to as "Qidei"). These commercials prominently featured a static image of the bear's face. The bear itself originates from a macabre and psychotic comic, authored by Stanley Donwood and Dr. Shock (a pseudonym for Tom Yorke), which was an interpretation of "The Story of the Three Bears." "este logo que realmente no es un gato, es un osso, lo tenemos por primera vez tenemos constancia de él cuando se hace el lanzamiento en televisión del Qidei... sale de un cómic que habían hecho en su día, Stan Lee Donwood y el Dr. Shock."
**Pre-Kid A Creative Explosion** : The period immediately preceding the release of "Kid A" (Qidei) was identified by Tom Yorke as a significant explosion of artistic creativity for the band. This intensely creative phase yielded not only a large volume of music but also many other projects, including posters, short films, videos, animations, and even comics. "ese periodo justo anterior al Qidei que fue la mayor explosión de creatividad artística de la banda, como siempre ha referido a Tom York a ese periodo del que no solo salió muchísima música sino que salieron un montón de otros proyectos."
**Macabre Bear Comic Plot** : The comic that inspired the Radiohead bear logo features a dark, aggressive, violent, and semi-psychotic story. It begins with three genetically modified bears agreeing to be photographed by a fourth; however, the camera turns out to be a weapon, leading to the death of the three bears who had hoped to achieve fame. "tres osos modificados y van a ser fotografiados por un cuarto, resulta que la cámara era un arma y los tres osos murieron, pensaban que iban a ser famosos."
**Bear Motif Beyond Logo** : The conceptual story behind the bear comic not only gave rise to the famous bear logo but also inspired various photographs, drawings, and slogans. These visual elements continued to be utilized by Radiohead on subsequent album covers, in later liner notes, and featured prominently on many band posters that are still sold today. "De esta historia no solo salió el famoso osos sino que también salieron varias fotografías, dibujos y slogans que seguía usando radio jef en varias portadas, en varios libretos muy posteriores e incluso muchos posters de la banda que se venen hoy en día."
**Bear Comic Release and Deletion** : The comic, consisting of only about 15 vignettes, was never officially released for sale. It was informally distributed across the internet during the "Kid A" (Qidei) and "Amnesiac" (Amnesiac) periods. Unfortunately, this comic and related material were later deleted just before the launch of "Hail to the Thief" (Hiltu de Cis) as part of an update to the band's artistic work, and it has not been officially republished since, remaining lost. "Este comic realmente nunca llevó a salir a la venta como toda ese material porque es muy corto, son sólo como unas 15 vignetas que fueron colgadas de manera completamente perdida por la web durante el quide y en la menecia."
**Bear Logo Evolution into Band Standard** : The bear image was not initially conceived as an official band logo. Tom Yorke revealed in an interview that he was drawn to the macabre nature of the bear image and initially used it purely for advertising the album "Kid A" (referred to as "QD"). However, it garnered significant public acceptance and eventually became one of the band's standard and recognizable logos. "Realmente no estuvo pensado como logo... Pero luego le acabó gustando un montón, tuvo un montón de aceptación y que finalmente se quedó como uno de los logán se está en der de la banda."

# artist - Tom Yorke

## member of
Radiohead

## curiosities
**Dr. Shock Alias** : Tom Yorke used the pseudonym Dr. Shock for artistic expression projects that were not musical in nature. Under this alias, he signed various paintings and collaborated with Stanley Donwood on visual arts, including projections and the comic that inspired the Radiohead bear logo. "Dr. Shock realmente es el seudónimo con el que Tom York hacía ciertos trabajos de expresión artística que no era musical. Es el nombre como el que firmó algunos cuadros, incluso trabajos con Stan Lee Donwood sobre artes, sobre proyectiones y estas cosas."
**Pre-Kid A Creative Explosion Assessment** : Tom Yorke characterized the period immediately preceding the release of "Kid A" as the band's most significant explosion of artistic creativity. During this time, the band produced a wealth of music alongside numerous other projects, such as posters, short films, videos, animations, and comics. "la mayor explosión de creatividad artística de la banda, como siempre ha referido a Tom York a ese periodo del que no solo salió muchísima música sino que salieron un montón de otros proyectos."
**Bear Logo Acceptance and Evolution** : Tom Yorke confirmed in an interview that he initially admired the macabre image of the bear and used it exclusively for "Kid A" (QD) advertising. The bear's widespread acceptance by the public ultimately led to its adoption as one of Radiohead's standard logos, despite not being initially conceived as such. "Tom Yorke hacía referencia de que le había gustado un montón, esa imagen macabra de losos que la usó solo como publicidad para el QD. Pero luego le acabó gustando un montón, tuvo un montón de aceptación y que finalmente se quedó como uno de los logán se está en der de la banda."

# artist - Stanley Donwood

## member of
Radiohead

## curiosities
**Collaboration with Dr. Shock** : Stanley Donwood collaborated with Tom Yorke, under his pseudonym Dr. Shock, on various art projects. These collaborations included projections and the creation of a comic that became the origin of Radiohead's iconic bear logo. "trabajos con Stan Lee Donwood sobre artes, sobre proyectiones y estas cosas."
**Bear Comic Inspiration - Allegory of Childhood** : In an interview, Stanley Donwood explained that the inspiration for the macabre bear comic is an allegory drawn from children's fairy tales. He believes these stories often contain a dark, macabre illusion for children that is gradually forgotten as they grow older. The idea stemmed from a drawing of a bear he made with his two- or three-year-old daughter, which he interpreted as these innocent childhood figures aggressively and violently resurfacing from the past to torment them. "esta inspiración de los osos es una especie de alegoría que viene a sacarnos las historias de cuentos infantiles que en el fondo tienen una especie de ilusión macabra para los niños que poco a poco son olvidadas, sale de un dibujo de un oso que había hecho con su niña pequeña de dos de tres años y que el final le dio la interpretación de que un día esas pequeñas figuras en la infancia volvería a la infancia. A tormentarnos en el pasado de una manera agresiva y violenta, ahí viene a ser resurgir de esos osos de la infancia con ese aspecto tan macabro y tan violento."
