## albums
**Homework (1997) - Breakthrough Album** : This album, released approximately 20 years before the transcript's context, is considered a major turning point in 90s electronic music and music in general. It is a cult object and revolutionary because it went against the prevailing trend of complex, fast, and genre-blending electronic music of the mid-90s, returning to the slow, simple tempos characteristic of 80s techno. The album drastically reduced electronic music to its minimal expression, utilizing fewer than 30 sounds in its creation. It was initially not conceived for mass consumption, leading Virgin Records to print only 50,000 CD copies, as it was thought to be a niche album for professional rave culture, with the vinyl edition being considered more important. However, it quickly sold 650,000 CDs, exhausting the initial run immediately and prompting massive production. The album lacks a thematic or argumentative thread, being a compilation of material produced by two artists from the rave culture, eventually gathered into two vinyl discs. While its immediate influence in the late 90s and early 2000s was not substantial, as electronic music continued its complex path, its impact became evident around 2007 with the "second wave" of modern electronic music, showing significant influences on subsequent groups.
"Hombor de Daspán fue el único álbum de la cultura rey de los 90 que trascendió al gran consumo."

## curiosities
**Origin Story - Rave Culture Artists** : Daft Punk, as two artists working within the rave culture, began creating material and eventually amassed enough to combine it into two vinyl discs, which they then produced with Virgin Records. The album, Homework, was not designed with a narrative or thematic concept in mind.
"no tiene absolutamente ningún tipo de hilo argumental, no tiene ningún tipo de temáticas, simplemente es dos artistas que trabajaban en la cultura rey, que empezaron a hacer material y un día se juntaron con tanto material que lo juntaron en dos discos en vinilo y los se hagan produciendo con Virging."
**Cult Status - Album vs. Group** : While Daft Punk is recognized as a great group, the true cult status in specialized press and electronic music circles lies more with their album, Homework, than with the group itself.
"hay más culto al Homebord que Datpanke como grupo, Datpanke como grupo es un gran grupo pero realmente el culto nace alrededor de este disco y no tanto alrededor del grupo."

# genre - Electronic

## curiosities
**Evolution in the 80s** : In the early 80s, electronic music saw the birth of techno in Detroit and house music from disco producers. These styles served as a "shock therapy" against the elaborate disco music of the late 70s, introducing simpler, repetitive sounds.
"a primeras de los 80 con la nueva década nacieron los dos pilares en la música electrónica moderna que tenemos hoy en día, que por un lado es la música techno que nacen Detroit y por el otro lado es la música House que nace de los productores en la música disco. Y realmente vienen a hacer una terapia de choque sobre la música disco que se estaba haciendo anteriormente."
**Evolution in the 90s** : During the 1990s, electronic music increasingly intertwined with alternative and rock music. This was largely facilitated by the consolidation of modern, computerized recording systems that streamlined production processes, allowing independent and alternative groups to experiment with electronic sounds.
"durante los 90 se consoligan unos sistemas de grabación modernos, muy tecnológicos que generan unos procesos informáticos para toda la grabación de álbumes. Todo el proceso de grabación y de producción se vuelve completamente informatizado. Eso hizo que muchos grupos de música alternativa independiente se fuesen relazando con sonidos más electrónicos."
**Mid-90s Complexity** : By the mid-90s, electronic music had evolved from the minimalist techno and house of the 80s into a more baroque, complicated, rapid, and elaborate form, incorporating numerous styles that sometimes seemed unrelated, including jazz and black music influences.
"en una época de los 90 donde la música técnula y la música jajados más sencilla, había evolucionado a sistemas muy complicados, muy rápidos y muy trabajados."
**Early 2000s Oversaturation** : The complexity and baroque style of electronic music reached an "absolutely unsustainable" point in the early 2000s, as exemplified by albums utilizing thousands of distinct sounds.
"la música electrónica de los 90 sigue siendo cada vez más barroca hasta que a primeros del 2000 llevó un punto absolutamente insostenible."
**Second Wave of Modern Electronic Music (2007)** : The year 2007 marked a "second wave of modern electronic music," characterized by a resurgence of Daft Punk's influence, leading subsequent groups back towards a more minimalist, simpler, and purer path.
"en el 2007 cuando fue la segunda granola de la electrónica moderna, cuando vemos muchísimas influencias de Datpanke lo que vienen a hacer los grupos posteriores."

# genre - Techno

## curiosities
**Birth in Detroit (Early 80s)** : Techno music originated in Detroit in the early 1980s, establishing itself as one of the two foundational pillars of modern electronic music, contrasting sharply with the elaborate disco of the late 70s. Its sound was based on simple, repetitive elements.
"la música techno que nacen Detroit."
**Decline in the 80s** : Throughout the 1980s, techno music became increasingly minoritarian, gradually losing its initial momentum and influence as a popular genre.
"la música techno se va quedando en algo completamente minoritario y poco a poco va perdiendo fuerza."
**Evolution into Rave Culture (90s)** : In the 90s, the "pure techno" that remained evolved into the "Rave Maspura" (rave culture). This movement had a very minority audience and was largely concentrated in Europe.
"Lo poco que quedaba de música techno durante los 90 o al menos el techno puro fue lo que fue volucionando la cultura Rape Maspura que tenía un público muy minoritario y que estaba muy concentrado en Europa."
**80s Characteristics** : Early techno was characterized by "slow, classic tempos," which Daft Punk's Homework later revisited, contrasting with the faster, more complex electronic music of the mid-90s.
"vuelve a hacer los cimpos lentos clásicos en la música techno de los 80."

# genre - House

## curiosities
**Birth from Disco Producers (Early 80s)** : House music emerged from disco producers in the early 1980s, becoming the second pillar of modern electronic music alongside techno. It was developed as a "shock therapy" against the baroque disco of the late 70s, emphasizing simple and repetitive sounds.
"la música House que nace de los productores en la música disco."
**Evolution into Club Culture (90s)** : By the 1990s, house music evolved significantly into what became known as "club culture," adapting to faster tempos and giving rise to subgenres like "Isis House."
"la música House por otro lado a los 90 fue volucionando a lo que conocemos como la cultura de club y por el otro lado por el la Isis House que llaminó a tiempo es mucho más rápidos."
