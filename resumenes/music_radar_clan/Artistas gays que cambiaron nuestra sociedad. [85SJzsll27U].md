# artist - Janis Joplin

## curiosities
**Sexual Freedom Advocate in the 60s** : She was married to a man and never declared herself lesbian, but she always fiercely defended sexual freedom. She openly admitted to having been with many women and men, enjoying her sexual freedom, and many of her passionate, gut-wrenching songs are dedicated to women. This was challenging in the 1960s when music, especially rock, was male-dominated, and while homosexual culture existed in art, women were denied recognition and visibility in media. "Ella siempre admitió muy abiertamente que había estado con muchas mujeres que hombres que disfrutaba mucho de su libertad sexual, muchas de esas canciones amor tantes, garradoras que tienen están dedicadas a mujeres y no era fácil, era una época en la que la música y concretamente el rock estaban dominados por hombres durante los 60."

# artist - David Bowie

## curiosities
**Liberating Aesthetic Against Stereotypes** : He is considered an icon of culture who promoted a philosophy where "what you are doesn't matter, but what you feel and what makes you happy, and that you don't have to name things." This informed much of his aesthetic, which was very liberating, allowing men for the first time to dress like women, not be masculine, or not fit into existing stereotypes without being linked to specific sexual orientations. He was a great step forward against sexual stereotypes. "Que la llevó muchísimo a toda su estética y que fue una estética muy liberadora, porque por primera vez podíamos tener hombres que se desquien con mujeres o que no querían ser masculinos o que no querían encajar dentro de estos estereotipos y no por ello tenían que estar vinculados a unas opciones sexuales o a otras."

## curiosities
**Impact as a Grotesque Drag Queen and Cultural Icon** : Known as Divine, Harris Glenn Milstead was an important figure for 20th-century culture, described as the "drag queen par excellence." He challenged stereotypes with his enormous overweight physique and grotesque character, yet possessed incredible artistic capabilities. He collaborated extensively with John Waters on films like "Pink Flamingo" and "Harry's play," and achieved such respect that Disney recognized him as the direct inspiration for Ursula, the villain in "The Little Mermaid." "era una persona como un personaje de racuín muy grotesco, pero que tenía unas capacidades artísticas increíbles."
**Musical Influence: Post-Disco Grotesque** : Divine contributed significantly to music by generating a "grotesque and grungy, trashy post-disco" sound, which later became an important influence for electroclash and other styles. "Musicalmente también nos aportó muchísimo porque generó esa especie de post disco grotesco y gronchy, cutre, que después más adelante sería una influencia importante para el electroclares y muchos otros estilos."
**Unthinkable Mainstream Acceptance in the 80s** : Despite his difficult character and challenging situation in the 1980s, Divine achieved significant respect, even appearing on "Pryndtine" in a US late-night show, which was completely unthinkable at the time. He was invited not for ridicule, but out of absolute respect. "Ella llegó a salir en Pryndtine en un nai show en Estados Unidos, cosa que en los 80 era algo completamente impensable y sobre todo que no se le invitaba para arrirse de ellas sino que se le invito desde el más absoluto respeto."
**Unfinished Projects at Death** : Tragically, Divine left many unfinished projects upon his death, including appearances in several television series and even an installment of "A Nightmare on Elm Street." He was a character recognized and respected by the entire cultural industry, having earned respect in an era when it was not at all easy for a person with such a grotesque character and atypical physique in the often-ridiculed world of drag queens. "Desgraciadamente cuando morío dejó muchos proyectos inacabados y va a salir en varias series de televisión, incluso iba a salir en una de las entregas de pesadilla en el mestrid."

# artist - Freddie Mercury

## curiosities
**Proud Life and Public Harassment Before AIDS Death** : Throughout his life, Freddie Mercury proudly defended his way of living, full of parties and debauchery, never feeling uncomfortable or ashamed, emphasizing it was the life he enjoyed and that made him happy, independent of stereotypes. Tragically, he fell ill with AIDS, and British society's response was severely lacking. His diagnosis only became public two or three days before his death, after a year of intense harassment, including false blood tests confirming his illness. "Durante toda su vida, Freddie Mercury ha hecho una gran defensa de esa manera que le entendía de vivir la vida, de fiestas, de pasarlo bien, de libertinaje, nunca se sintió incomodado ni haber gonzado por la vida que llevaba y que le gustaba y que la llevó siempre con mucho orgullo."
**Tragic End and Societal Stigma** : Despite living in an advanced society, Freddie Mercury could not achieve his wish to die peacefully and without stigma among his loved ones. He died reclusive, embroiled in scandal, and pointed out by many, simply for having the misfortune of contracting a disease that was killing thousands. "o sea tuvo que morir, recluido, inmersen el escándalo, señalado por todos, simplemente por haber tenido la mala suerte de tener una enfermedad que estaba matando a miles de personas."
**Posthumous Impact on AIDS Awareness** : The only positive outcome of Freddie Mercury's death was the immense social commotion it caused, which arguably made it the most impactful AIDS-related death globally. It significantly helped change the perception of the disease among more conservative people, greatly contributing to awareness and visibility for those suffering from AIDS. "yo creo que con diferencia fue la muerte por sida que más conmozino al mundo y yo creo que ayudó muchísimo a cambiar la concepción que tenía mucha gente más conservadora sobre esta enfermedad."

# artist - Bédito

## member of
Debosi

## curiosities
**Challenging LGBTQ+ Stereotypes with Nude Magazine Cover** : Bédito, the singer of Debosi, is openly lesbian and comfortable with her identity. She was particularly revolutionary for appearing completely naked on a magazine cover. This action challenged the media's tendency to stereotype LGBTQ+ individuals, especially by showing that not all non-heterosexual people conform to the "handsome, fit, fantastic life" image often sold by the press, instead presenting a normal homosexual woman without those physical stereotypes or complexes. She spoke freely about her identity and made visible that not all parts of the homosexual community or non-heterosexual individuals fit into the stereotypes propagated. "Y yo de destacar a alguien en este siglo XXI, sin duda destacaría Bédito, cantante de Debosi, que ella es abiertamente lesbiana, a continuamente de ello no tiene ningún problema, pero sobre todo fue revolucionario cuando salía portada de una revista completamente desnuda."

# curiosity

**New Stereotypes for LGBTQ+ Individuals** : The struggle for LGBTQ+ rights initially aimed to eliminate prejudice and repression, but once sexual, bisexual, and other sexual communities could no longer be singled out, punished, or repressed, a new form of stereotyping emerged. The media began to create new stereotypes based on homosexuals who are well-groomed, handsome, muscular, fantastic, and lead modern, amazing lives. This often hides the reality that behind these communities are just normal, ordinary people. "se generó una nueva manera de hacer los estereotipos en base a los homosexuales que se cuidan, que son guapos, que están cachas, que son fantásticos, que llevan una vida fantástica y moderna, y al final detrás estos colectivos, lo único que hay es gente normal y corriente."
