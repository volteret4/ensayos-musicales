# artist - Sugarhill Gang

## albums
**Rapper's Delight (1979)** : This album is often considered the first and most important modern rap song, though the speaker notes this is sometimes an overvaluation, as it was just one of several significant Sugar Hill Records releases from the same year. It is frequently cited as the first modern rap release largely because it was Sugar Hill Records' inaugural offering, but other contemporary releases could equally mark this historical milestone. "normalmente consideramos este rap presidado como el primero porque efectivamente fue el primer lanzamiento de la discográfica su ágil pero usamos este por usar este y no por usar cualquier otro."

## songs
**Rapper's Delight (1979)** : This song is often mythologized as a completely groundbreaking invention, but the speaker argues it was part of a logical evolution of narrative music from the 1970s and one of many tracks released by Sugar Hill Records that gave visibility to a rap movement already developing in the Bronx. While highly important, its significance is sometimes overstated compared to other Sugar Hill releases from 1979. "muchas veces tendemos a mitificar lo que fue el lanzamiento de su argil gang de rap a zilite ni fue algo completamente rompedorto."

# artist - Millie Jackson

## albums
**Cocktop (1974)** : An album from 1974 described as "incredible." Millie Jackson's curious singing style on this record was a major influence on later artists like Grace Jones. On this album, she started reciting song lyrics with rhymes, moving beyond simple narration and representing a significant step in proto-rap development. "este disco de mil hijacción del 74 Cocktop es increíble y de hecho esta manera de cantar tan curiosa de mil hijacción sería una grandísima influencia para otra de las grandes divas posteriores grisos."

## songs
**The Rap (1974)** : A song from Millie Jackson's 1974 album "Cocktop," curiously titled "The Rap." In this track, she recites the lyrics with rhyme, which was a more advanced and interesting approach compared to earlier narrative styles, showing an evolution towards modern rap. "curiosamente esta canción se llama de rap."

## curiosities
**Influence on Grace Jones** : Millie Jackson's unique "half-singing, half-talking" style and her curious way of singing, particularly exemplified on her 1974 album "Cocktop," had a very important influence on the singing style of the later diva, Grace Jones. "grisos tuvo una importantísima influencia en su manera de cantar en mil hijacción."

# artist - Isaac Hayes

## albums
**Black Moses (1971)** : An album from 1971 that was a significant influence on later music. Melodic parts and rhythms from this album have been sampled and used by many hip-hop artists, and even modern artists like Portishead. This album predates Barry White's 1974 recordings by several years, making it a crucial precursor for both rap and much subsequent music. "este disco es incluso anterior a barri white me refiero esta grabación es barrios años anterior a barri white de 1974 con lo cual no sólo fue una importante influencia para definir lo que sería el rap posterior sino que pone importante influencia para mucha música posterior como barri white."

## songs
**Ike's Rap II (1971)** : A song from Isaac Hayes's 1971 album "Black Moses." The melodic elements and rhythms from this track are noted as sounding incredibly familiar to listeners because numerous hip-hop artists, and even contemporary acts such as Portishead, have utilized samples and instrumental bases from Isaac Hayes's recordings. "La canción Ike's rap 12 seguro que hay algunas partes melodicas y algunos rimos de este disco que os han sonado increíblemente familiares a vida artistas de hip hop que han usado bases e incluso artistas modernos como portis que han usado bases de estos discos de Lisa Kais."

## curiosities
**Influence on Barry White** : Isaac Hayes's 1971 album "Black Moses" and its recordings were an important influence on Barry White, predating White's own 1974 works by several years. "no sólo fue una importante influencia para definir lo que sería el rap posterior sino que pone importante influencia para mucha música posterior como barri white."
**Influence on Portishead** : Isaac Hayes's 1971 album "Black Moses" and its specific tracks, like "Ike's Rap II," were a direct source of samples and musical bases for modern artists such as Portishead. "artistas modernos como portis que han usado bases de estos discos de Lisa Kais."

# genre - Rap

## curiosities
**Early Development (1970s)** : The 1970s saw a strong trend and development of narrative music or "narrated songs" that were already referred to as "rap." References to "rap music" existed even before 1970, with songs titled "rap" serving as the "germ" for the later culture of rap and hip hop. "durante los años 70 hubo una moda y un desarrollo de la narrativa con música o de la canción narrada por así decirlo que ya se llama a Barrapp."
**Proto-rap Characteristics (1970s)** : During the 1970s, proto-rap often combined jazz and Afro-Jazz movements with narrative elements, sometimes incorporating influences from Jamaican and funky music. In this early form, the narrative voice typically did not strictly mark the rhythm but rather delivered a discourse. "es una base de afro y jazz combinada con la narrativa que no está marcando el ritmo, simplemente está narrando, está dando un discurso."
**Modern Rap Genesis (1979)** : The year 1979 marked a historic milestone with the first releases of modern rap. The significant evolutionary leap in 1979 involved the introduction of rhythms marked during singing and the development of powerful rhymes. "el gran salto volotivo que se hace en 1979 es el que es el primero que se marcan los ritmos al cantar y después que son las rimas tan potentes que tendría el rap."
**1979 Sound Profile** : While foundational, the first modern rap releases in 1979 still heavily featured background music rooted in funk, boogie, and late disco, and did not yet fully resemble the distinct musical bases that would later characterize hip hop culture. "muchas bases que tenemos de fondo recuerdan mucho más a la música fankey recuerdan sobre todo al buggy y no tanto al hip hop."
**Evolution 1979-1983** : Between 1979 and 1983, rap underwent a rapid evolution, gradually separating itself from its boogie and late disco influences to establish the distinct musical foundations of hip hop. This period involved a transition from rap with residual boogie elements to rap fully integrated with hip hop culture. "entre el 79 y el 83 es una rápidísima evolución que luego acabaría separando el rap con esos restos era música buggy y era música disco tardía a lo que sería lo que serían las bases del hip hop."
**1982-1983 Breakthrough** : The years 1982 and 1983 were possibly the two greatest evolutionary years for rap, marking the definitive leap from a style still retaining boogie elements to rap fully embodying hip hop culture. "el 82 y 83 posiblemente fueron los dos grandes años evolutivos que hicieron el salto del rap con restos del buggy al rap ya con la cultura hip hop."

# genre - Hip Hop

## curiosities
**Emergence of Distinct Musical Bases (1979-1983)** : The distinct musical bases that define hip hop culture emerged from a rapid evolution of rap between 1979 and 1983. Prior to this, early rap releases, despite being foundational, were still rooted in funk, boogie, and late disco, rather than the later characteristic sounds of hip hop. "no recuerda todavía esas bases que de lo que luego sería el hip hop."
**Full Cultural Integration (Post-1983)** : By 1983, and definitively by 1984, hip hop had fully established its distinct identity, encompassing the cultures of breakbeat and sampling. The majority of rap releases from 1984 onwards very clearly fit within what is considered hip hop culture. "a partir de 1983 os fijáis ya lo que se estoy enseñando ya no solo es el rap tal y como lo conocemos y en aquella son las bases del hip hop tal y como lo conocemos ya en 1984 la mayoría la gran mayoría de lanzamientos de rap encaja muy bien dentro de lo que consideramos la cultura hip hop la cultura del break beat la cultura del sampling."
**Advanced Concepts (1983)** : By 1983, tracks like West Street Mob's "Break Dance - Electric Boogie" were already showcasing highly advanced concepts of sampling and breakbeat, representing a very forward-thinking vision of mid-80s hip hop. "una pizza de hip hop casi instrumental que tiene conceptos avanzadísimos lo que es el sampling el break beat es una pizza avanzadísima de lo que luego sería el hip hop de mitad de los 80."

**Ancient Narrated Music** : Narratives or spoken songs accompanied by music have existed "since always," with recordings traceable back to the 1930s and even earlier, suggesting this form might be a timeless aspect of musical expression. "la verdad es que tenemos que narrativas o canción hablada con música desde siempre, tan en el pasado que nos perdemos rastreando la tenemos incluso grabaciones de los años 30, tenemos grabaciones anteriores entonces, posiblemente se algo que en cierto modo haya existido siempre."
**Pre-1970 Rap Mentions** : While not considered "modern rap," there are references to "rap music" and songs with "rap" in their titles from before 1970 and during the early 1970s. These served as the "germ" for the later development of rap and hip hop culture. "tenemos bastantes referencias a música rap incluso alguna anterior a 1970 tenemos canciones contítulos con la palabra rap ya en esa época que no se considera rap moderno pero que sí que son el germen de lo que más adelante sería la cultura del rap y del hip hop."
**Rap's Bronx Origins (1976-1978)** : Rap was likely already being created and performed in the Bronx, New York, between 1976 and 1978, even before it received its first discographic visibility through Sugar Hill Records in 1979. "ese rap ya se estaba haciendo en el bronce en el bronce en Nueva York durante el 67 y 78 y fue la primera visibilidad discográfica la que le dio su ágil a todo ese movimiento."
**Importance of Women in Early Rap/Hip Hop** : The significant contributions of women to defining the early years of rap and hip hop culture, exemplified by groups like The Sequence and Us Girls, are often forgotten or overlooked. "es una pena pero muchas veces olvidamos grandes nombres de mujeres de sequen y de hdb que fueron realmente importantes para definir los primeros años a la cultura rap y la cultura hip hop."
**Rap Evolution vs. Invention** : Modern rap was not a sudden invention in 1979 but rather the culmination of a logical and interesting evolution of narrative music throughout the 1970s. The "breakthrough" in 1979, largely due to Sugar Hill Records, was about providing visibility to a movement already happening in the Bronx, rather than creating it from scratch. "la ruptura sí que fue en 1979 con los primeros lanzamientos de la escorrafica su argil que no fue tanto inventar sino a dar visibilidad de escorrafica algo que ya está pasando en el bronx en Estados Unidos en el bronx en la york."
