## albums
**Is This It (2001) - Rock Revival Album** : This album is widely credited with revitalizing rock music in the 21st century upon its release in 2001. It created a significant stir, particularly for reinventing established concepts within industrial rock. A substantial amount of money was invested in its production, and despite the best professional resources RCA could provide, the album's release was delayed for months due to various problems. "Muchos conocen este álbum como el álbum que hizo resurgir el rock en el siglo XXI."
**Is This It (2001) - US Censored Cover** : The original cover for *Is This It*, depicting a hand on a naked buttock, which is known in Europe and Japan, was censored in the United States. US authorities deemed the cover "suggestive" and prohibited its use. "Estados Unidos consideraba la portada de un culo como un guante de maseros ojerte y acabaron prohibiéndola."
**Is This It (2001) - US Alternative Cover** : Due to censorship, the first album by The Strokes released in the United States featured an alternative cover. This version displayed a green and yellow image of colliding particles, reminiscent of those found in a particle collider. "con una foto como de verde y amarilla, de unas partículas que se colisionen a un colisionador de Andrones de estas cosas."
**Is This It (2001) - Post-9/11 Release Adjustments** : The release of *Is This It* coincided with the 9/11 attacks in the United States. This tragic event caused a major problem for the band, as the original, uncorrected edition of the album contained a song, "New York City Cops," with lyrics that were deemed highly inappropriate for the national sentiment at the time. "justo a punto de salir del disco se producen los atentados de los Ancés."
**Is This It (2001) - Considered Great Album** : *Is This It* is considered a significant album, described as "one of the great albums" of its era, marking a fresh direction for rock music. The financial investment in the band and this album ultimately proved The Strokes to be great artists. "uno de los grandes discos en esa adolescimiento."

## songs
**New York City Cops (2001)** : This song was originally featured as the fourth track on Side B of *Is This It*. Its lyrics contained choruses stating that New York police officers were "not excessively intelligent," which proved to be an unfortunate sentiment for a country that had just experienced the 9/11 attacks. "En esa canción decían en los coros que los policías de New York no eran excesivamente inteligentes."
**New York City Cops (2001) - US Replacement** : Due to the sensitive post-9/11 context, the release of *Is This It* had to be halted, and the song "New York City Cops" was replaced. In the United States, the CD version and the original vinyl release substituted this track with "When It Started." "acabaría reemplazando esa canción por buen instarte."
**New York City Cops (2001) - International Inclusion** : While removed from early US releases, "New York City Cops" was included in later vinyl versions of *Is This It* in the United States. Moreover, it was consistently featured in versions released in Europe, Japan, and other international markets. "nosotros en Europa, en Japón y en Pratín, en todos países de los mundos, si tenemos la canción de New York City Cox."
**When It Started (2001)** : This song was used to replace "New York City Cops" on the US CD and original vinyl releases of *Is This It*. The change was made after the 9/11 attacks, as the original song's lyrics were considered insensitive. "acabaría reemplazando esa canción por buen instarte."

## curiosities
**Julian Casablancas' Privileged Background** : Julian Casablancas is the son of the founder of Elite Model Management, one of the world's most important modeling agencies, and Miss Venezuela. He grew up in New York. "A Yulia en el Casablaca se sijo del fundador de élite o la fundada de las agencias de modelo más importantes del mundo y es hijo de mis Venezuela."
**Julian Casablancas' Youth and Early Connections** : Julian Casablancas, who grew up in New York, was sent to a boarding school in Switzerland due to behavioral issues, where he met "the other guitarist" who would later become a band member. "se tuvo que ir a un interrano suiza porque se portaba muy mal donde conocía lo otro guitarrista."
**The Strokes' Rapid Formation and Success** : Upon returning to New York, the band was formed and quickly achieved success. This rapid rise was attributed, in part, to the connections and social standing of its members. "cuando eres un tía, si defamos un importante, es fácil que te agarcaso."
**The Strokes' Early Amateur Approach** : In their initial stages, The Strokes' approach to music was somewhat "messy," as none of the members possessed professional musical knowledge. They operated by doing "a little bit of whatever came out." "ninguno de ellos tenía conocimientos profesionales de música y hacía un poco como él ha salido la cosa."
**The Strokes' Influential Musical Formula** : The band developed an appealing musical formula that blended themes from The Velvet Underground and The Ramones with elements of punk rock and garage rock. This distinctive sound was very well-received by audiences. "mezcaba un poco temáticas de la Belvetande de Brown, con los Ramones, con el pan cantigo, con el robo de gráje."
**The Strokes' Debut EP and Bidding War** : The Strokes' first release was an EP, which was put out by a small record label in the United Kingdom, as they recognized its potential more clearly. This EP generated considerable excitement upon its release, leading to an intense bidding war among major record labels for the band's signing. "saliendo por una discográfica pequeña en el Reino Unido, porque lo vieron mucho más claro, pero ya en cuanto salió tuvo muchísima il·sicreación de CP y al final hubo un enorme guerra de discográficas para ver que no se fichaba."
**The Strokes' Signing with RCA** : After a prolonged process involving a fierce bidding war among record labels, The Strokes ultimately signed with RCA. At the time, RCA was considered the second-largest record label in the world. "estos drogos acabaron fichando por la Recia, la Recia, después se vieron de la segunda escográfica más grande del mundo."
**The Strokes' Disappointment with Censorship** : The band was reportedly "very upset" by the censorship of their album cover in the United States, which led to a different cover being used for *Is This It* in that region. "Pero bueno, al final los Estros quedaron muy desafinados por la censura."
**The Strokes' Career and Investment** : A significant amount of money was invested in The Strokes, and their career ultimately demonstrated them to be great artists. "mucho dinero, pero la financianza demostrado ser una gran artista, la carrera de Chistrox."
