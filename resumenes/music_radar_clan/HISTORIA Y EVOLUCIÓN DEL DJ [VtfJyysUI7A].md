# genre - Tornabilismo

## curiosities
**Birth in Late 1970s** : The culture of turntablism emerged in the late 1970s, specifically around 1978-1979, closely associated with hip-hop culture. It represented a crucial leap in DJing, transforming the turntable from a playback device into a musical instrument. "nace la cultura alternabilismo, la cultura alternabilismo finales de los 70, 78, 79, muy unida, la cultura hip hop."
**Platter as an Instrument** : Turntablism revolutionized DJing by shifting the use of the turntable from a mere apparatus for playing records to a musical instrument. This was made possible by direct-drive turntables like the Technics SL-1200, which prevented damage to records during manipulation. "el DJ deja y deja de usar el plato como aparato y pasa usar el plato como instrumento musical."
**Techniques Developed** : The tornabilismo culture gave birth to techniques such as scratching, Brit beat (likely referring to breakbeat or beat juggling), and jungle beat (likely referring to breakbeats used in jungle music), where DJs use their turntables to generate new sounds. "allí nacen, gracias a estos platos de tracción directa que no rompen las cuerdas, nacen el tornabilismo que vienen a hacer el scratching, ven a ser el Brit beat, ven a ser el jungle beat."
**Defining Modern DJ Culture** : The speaker believes that modern DJ culture, despite its long history, was truly defined by the adoption of the Technics SL-1200 and the rise of tornabilismo culture. "yo creo que ahí es cuando se define la cultura del DJ moderno, yo creo que el DJ moderno, aunque si se o toda la vida nace con la implantación del técnico, se sele 1200 y con la cultura alternabilismo."

# instrument - CDJ

## curiosities
**Compact Disc Turntable Concept** : The CDJ concept emerged in 2001, representing a DJ turntable designed for compact discs. While not the very first of its kind, the Pioneer CDJ-1000 quickly became the industry standard. "nace el CDJ, nace el CDJ, que es el plato de DJ y versión disco compacto."
**Initial DJ Resistance** : DJs were initially reluctant to switch from vinyl to compact discs and MP3s around the early 2000s. "los DJs en esa época fueron muy reacios a salter al disco compacto de al MP3, en parte yo creo que por clasismo."

# instrument - Final Scratch

## curiosities
**Revolutionary DVS System** : Final Scratch is a system created by Richie Hawtin that revolutionized DJing by allowing DJs to import MP3 or computer-based sound files to turntables equipped with special vinyl records. These records, instead of containing music, simply used fixed rhythms and measures to control the sound loaded from the computer, effectively making digital files playable on traditional turntables. "Final Scratch es el sistema que nos permite importar sonido, de MP3 o del ordenador, a platos que tienen un vinilo que simplemente usa ritmos, que simplemente tiene compases fijo que reproducen el sonido que cargas es el ordenador."
**Return to Vinyl** : The introduction of Final Scratch profoundly impacted the DJ system, prompting many DJs to return to using vinyl records (albeit control vinyl) and move away from CDJs. "revoluciona para siempre todo el sistema y hace que muchos DJs vuelvan otra vez al vinilo y se canse en el CDJ."

# instrument - MIDI

## curiosities
**Data Transmission Standard** : MIDI (Musical Instrument Digital Interface) is described as a system for data transmission that enables the standardized control of musical equipment from computers or other devices. Although its origins date back to the 1980s, its practical adoption and integration into DJ booths didn't become widespread until the 1990s. "el sistema MIDI, el sistema de transmisión de datos que permite digamos usar cualquier maquinaria de manera a estandar para controlar musicales de los ordenadores o otros aparatos."
**Enabling DJ Producers** : The widespread adoption of MIDI in DJ setups during the 1990s was crucial for the emergence of "DJ musicians" and complex producers like The Chemical Brothers and Fatboy Slim. "gracias al MIDI, pues los chemical Proder, Fatboy Slim, un montón de esta gente importante."

# instrument - Pioneer CDJ-1000

## curiosities
**Setting the CDJ Standard** : The Pioneer CDJ-1000, released in 2001, is considered the most important CDJ in history, defining compact disc DJing similar to how the Technics SL-1200 defined vinyl DJing. While not the first CDJ, it offered advanced features that gave DJs compelling reasons to switch from vinyl. "Pioneer Mace saca esa máquina increíble que cambiaría el DJ para siempre, que es el CDJ 1000."
**Advanced Features for DJs** : The CDJ-1000 offered complex and advanced functionalities that were not possible with traditional turntables. These included looping capabilities, the ability to mark beats from 4 to 16 counts for loop creation, and various effect modules like echoes. These features provided a strong incentive for DJs to transition from turntables to CDJs. "el CDJ 1000 era el primero que tenía unas opciones muy complejas y muy avanzadas, que te permitía hacer cosas que no podías hacer como un plato, tenía los looping, tenía looping, tenía, podías marcar los compases de 4 a 16, para hacer todos los loops, tenía algunos módulos efectos, tenía ecos, tenía cosas muy complejas."

# curiosity

**MDJ - Renowned Spanish DJ** : MDJ is recognized as one of the best and most entertaining DJs in Spain, known for captivating audiences for hours at festivals, clubs, and discos. "MDJ y he bailado o sea horas en festivales, en salas de fiesta, en discotecas, pero es una de las DJs yo creo que mejores y más divertidas que tenemos en España."
**Six Stages of DJ Evolution** : The history of DJs is presented as having evolved through six distinct stages, primarily defined by the invention of new machines that continuously reshaped the DJ's work. "los DJs realmente han pasado por 6 etapas hasta la etapa en la que estamos hoy en día."
**Ancient Stage - Radio DJs** : The initial stage of DJing involved individuals who played music on the radio. This role evolved from playing entire concerts to playing fragments and individual songs, resembling a modern DJ. This era lasted until the 1960s. "la primera etapa de los DJs, que es la etapa antigua en lo que los DJs eran las personas que ponían música en la radio."
**Birth of Club Culture (Late 1960s / Early 1970s)** : The widespread availability of portable multi-track mixers in the late 1960s, coinciding with the rise of disco music, led to the birth of club culture. DJs moved from radio studios into nightclubs, becoming central figures in the club scene. "así es como a finales de los años 60, muy tarde en los años 60 casi primeros de los 70, coincidiendo con el nacimiento de la música disco, nace la cultura de club, el DJ y de club."
**Maxi Single Format (1970s)** : The Maxi-single, a 12-inch single format, emerged in the 1970s specifically for nightclubs. It was larger with deeper, wider grooves, making it easier for DJs to cue and mix. "nace el formato Maxi single, que es el single de 12 pulgadas, es específicamente pensado para las iscotecas."
**Disco Mix Format (1970s)** : The disco mix format, featuring extended versions of songs with long instrumental parts at the beginning and end, was created in the 1970s to facilitate smooth transitions and mixing for DJs. "nace la otra cosa importante, que es el formato disco mix."
**DJ as Sound Generator (1980s)** : The 1980s marked a new leap in DJing with the emergence of DJs who generated their own sounds, moving beyond simply playing or manipulating records. This was driven by inventions like Roland's drum machines. "entrando en 1980 inaugurando, 1980 nace el nuevo salto de los DJs, que son los DJs que generan sonido."
**Mid-1990s - A Passionate Era** : The second half of the 1990s is described as arguably the most exciting period in DJing, characterized by the coexistence of old-school turntablism and scratching with the rise of new-school DJing, musician-producers, and the advent of sampling. "la segunda mitad de los 90 posiblemente haya sido como la parte más apasionante del mundo del DJing."
**Second Wave of Club Culture (1990s)** : The 1990s experienced a "second wave of club culture," characterized by a "great invasion" of new dance music styles like Techno, Acid House, House, Progressive, and Euro Trance, including specific regional phenomena like Bacalao in Spain. "es la época como de la gran invasión de la nueva oleada de la música de las discotecas, lo que se llama la segunda oleada de la cultura de club."
**Modern DJ Era - Superstar Artists** : The contemporary era of DJing sees the DJ as a complex blend of DJ, composer, and musician, with the rise of "superstar DJs" like Hardwell, DJ Tiësto, and Avicii. These artists fill stadiums and feature on lists of the world's richest musicians, marking an "artist DJ" period. "nace el DJ grandísima estrella, pues como Harwell o como DJ Tiesto o como Vitchie, esos grandísimos DJs que ocupan ya las listas de los músicos más ricos del mundo, que llenan estadios, nace como la época de la DJ y artista."
**Technological Advancements as Drivers of DJ Evolution** : The evolution of the DJ's role has been consistently defined by technological inventions, with specific machines like the Technics SL-1200, the CDJ-1000, and the advent of Final Scratch fundamentally altering DJ culture. "creo que es interesante ver como los inventos tecnológicos han ido definiendo el trabajo del DJ y hasta el día de hoy."
