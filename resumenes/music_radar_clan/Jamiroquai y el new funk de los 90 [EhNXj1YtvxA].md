## curiosities
**Key Figure in 90s New Jazz/Funky Movement** : Jamiroquai was the leading exponent of a small, funky New Jazz revival movement that emerged in central London in the early 1990s. This movement, though somewhat isolated and ultimately short-lived, was significantly defined by Jamiroquai's work. "fue el máximo exponente de ese pequeño movimiento de resorgir de desastena New Jazz, un tanto fanchi que había surgido a primeros de los 90 en el centro de Londres."
**Initial Rejection by Brand New Heavies** : Jay Kay was initially considered to be the lead singer for The Brand New Heavies, another significant band in London's New Jazz Funky scene. However, he was rejected due to his "curious appearance"—being very short and not conventionally handsome—and his musical style, which was perceived as "too funky, too retro, and not jazzy enough" for the band. "Jaimiro Kuey realmente iba a ser el cantante de los Brand New Heavy... fue rechazado un poco por ese aspecto tan curioso que tenía, era muy bajito, no, era muy guapo, y porque tenía como una influencia demasiado fanchi, demasiado retro y notanjas."
**Hat Controversy with Indigenous Tribes** : Jay Kay faced significant complaints and nearly a lawsuit from American indigenous tribes who were offended by his use of hats typical of their cultures. This generated considerable friction and negative feedback regarding his appropriation of their traditional attire. "algunas tribus indígenas americanas acabaron casi tuviendo una denuncia, tuvieron bastantes quejas sobre él por usar los sombreros típicos esustribos."
**Automotive Collector and Semi-Professional Racer** : Jay Kay is an avid collector and semi-professional racer of automobiles, boasting a collection of over 100 sports and super luxury cars, some of which were custom-built exclusively for him. He notably holds the absolute speed record on the renowned Top Gear circuit, having surpassed many professional Formula 1 drivers. "Jaikay también es un grandísimo coleccionista y corredor semi profesional de automoción, tiene más de 100 deportivos y superlujos, algunas fabricados solo para él."
**90s Chart Performance** : During the 1990s, Jamiroquai achieved remarkable chart success, positioning between 20 and 30 singles that reached "nearly number one" status. This made the band one of the top 15-20 artists with the most highly charted singles of that decade. "le llevaba a posicionar, creo que entre 20 y 30 singles de casi número uno durante la época de los 90, debe ser uno de los 15, 20 artistas a lo máximo, que en más número uno ha posicionado."
**Creative Stifling Contract** : Jamiroquai signed an 8-album record contract that ultimately proved to be creatively suffocating. This "contractual strangulation" led to a gradual loss of industry interest and the artist's career becoming more relegated to other aspects. "Jaimiro Kuey tuvo un problema y es que fue víctima de su contrato, que firmó por 8 álbumes, el cual lo acabó agando a nivel creativo."
**Influence and Sampling Source** : Jamiroquai's music served as an extensive "sampling factory" for rap, electronic, and pop music. Artists like Madonna cited Jamiroquai as an influence on her album "Confessions on a Dance Floor," and Jamiroquai's tracks were directly sampled by figures such as Kaden Harriens (likely Kanye West), Missy Elliott, and Tupac for his song "How Do U Want It." "fueron una auténtica fábrica de sampling para el rap, para el electrónica, para muchísimas música pop."
**Disinterest in Career Progression** : Despite being incredibly wealthy, Jay Kay has openly expressed a lack of serious interest in continuing his music career actively. While he states he will not retire, he doesn't intend to take his career seriously, which results in new albums being released only sporadically, every three to five years, primarily for his dedicated fanbase. "En las entrevistas nos dice que no se piensa retirar, pero que tampoco se va a tomar en serio el resto de su carrera."

# genre - Funky

## curiosities
**Early 90s Commercial Revival** : The early 1990s saw a significant commercial return of funky influences within popular music. This resurgence, spearheaded by artists like Jamiroquai, offered an easy-to-listen-to retro sound that became widely successful. "ese retorno fanquy tan fácil de escuchar, teníamos ya bastantes como resurgir el retros de influencias de la música fanquy, dentro de la música comercial."
**Late 2000s Resurgence** : A relatively later resurgence of funky music influences occurred in the late 2000s. During this period, artists such as Madonna explicitly acknowledged Jamiroquai as a significant influence, highlighting the lasting impact of the earlier funky revival. "ese resurgir Funky, relativamente posterior, que tuvimos a finales en la década de 2000."

# curiosity

**The "Neo-Jazz Funky" Generation Quality** : The overall quality of the Neo-Jazz Funky generation of musicians that emerged in the 1990s was largely considered "half-credible" or mediocre. Beyond Jamiroquai and The Brand New Heavies, the movement was not seen as particularly strong or innovative. "quitándola él y quitándola lo mejor los brand new heavis, era un poco medio creíble, esa generación del Neo-Jazir, Funky que tuvimos."
