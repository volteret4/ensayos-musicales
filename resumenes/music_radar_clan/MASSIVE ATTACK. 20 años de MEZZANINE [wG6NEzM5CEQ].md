## albums
**Blue Lines (1991) - First Trip Hop Album** : Released as the first album by Massive Attack, it is almost consensually considered the first Trip Hop record, although some might disagree on its groundbreaking nature. It was heavily influenced by soul music, black music, downtempo, reggae, and American hip hop movements. "crean lo que es el primer disco de Trip Hop que es el Blue Lines."
**Protection (Year Not Specified) - Second Album** : This is the second album by Massive Attack, described as an "authentic masterpiece" alongside `Blue Lines`. It already showed influences from the Trip Hop style being made by Nellee Hooper with Björk. "protección ya tiene algunas influencias de todo el Trip Hop que estaban haciendo Neil Hopper con Bjork."
**Mezzanine (1998) - 20th Anniversary Album** : Celebrating its 20th anniversary, this album is highlighted as one of the most important records of the 90s. It is seen as the work that best crystallizes and culminates the fusion movement that Trip Hop became, bringing together all the influences the genre incorporated over the years. It marks a point of inflection for Trip Hop, after which the genre became very diffuse and complex. This album is noted for its emotional intensity, with moments described as "penetrant," and incorporates influences from classic American soul, deep downtempo rhythmic bases of Trip Hop, Björk's more melodic and intimate Trip Hop, synthesizers, new wave, and classical electronic music. Critically, it is considered by many to be Massive Attack's best work. "posiblemente esté disco del 98 al mesanín de Masi Batak. Si el disco que mejora, glutina, mejor, culmina, es ese movimiento de fusión que fue el Trip Hop."

## songs
**Angel (Year Not Specified) - Intense Reggae-Influenced Track** : This track is highlighted as one of the peak moments on the album, possessing an intensity that is almost "too intense to be intense." It features the acclaimed reggae singer Horace Andy on vocals and is heavily influenced by basic reggae and soul movements, played at a very slow tempo, often subtly. "Ingel es una canción muy influenciada por los movimientos básicos del rey de solo que están pasando a cámara muy lenta y no nosamos cuenta."
**Man Next Door (Year Not Specified) - Reggae Cover Featuring Horace Andy** : This track, another peak of the album, features Horace Andy singing a classic reggae song. Originally composed by John Holt, the song tells the story of Holt's problem with a party-loving neighbor who kept him and his children from sleeping. Massive Attack reinterpreted this ancient reggae track in a completely different way, transforming normally fast or simple reggae bases by playing with extremely slow tempos and deconstructing it complexly, making it almost unrecognizable while retaining stylistic foundations. "Realmente cuenta como John Holt tiene un vecino que es un juervista y que no deja dormir a sus hijos por las noches."

## curiosities
**Mezzanine Recording Context - Difficult Period and Member Departures** : The recording of `Mezzanine`, Massive Attack's third album, occurred during a challenging time for the band. The Trip Hop genre was no longer what it once was, compelling the band to evolve their sound by incorporating more elements, including synthesizers, new wave, and classical electronic music. Andrew Vowles left the band right after the recording was finished, with parts of the album already having been completed without his involvement. "Al final Andrew Wolves acababa abandonando la banda justo al terminar la grabación del mes anín y es que hubo parte entre las del disco donde Andrew Wolves ya no hacía nada."
**Pitch-shifting - Extensive Use on Mezzanine** : Massive Attack extensively employed pitch-shifting on `Mezzanine`, a technique where melodies are recorded at a different speed than they are reproduced, generating unique audio effects. While common among hip hop beatmakers, the band took this process to an extreme, with nearly all melodies on the album being recorded at a speed different from their playback. "casi todas las melodías que vemos en este disco masibata que en este disco esta grabada sonad de velocidad distinta de la que se está reproduciendo, lo cual genera efectos de audio muy interesantes."

# genre - Trip Hop

## curiosities
**Origin and Evolution** : Trip Hop was born in the UK, specifically Bristol, originating from the Wild Bunch cultural movement as a reinterpretation of hip hop and Afro-American music. Over time, it evolved by incorporating various elements, including world music, jazz (introduced by Portishead), and melodic and intense female vocals (contributed by Björk), and at times, it blurred the lines with dance music, as seen with Faithless. "El Trip Hub... bueno, existe bastante consenso de cómo nace el movimiento Trip Hub en el Reino Unido."
**Climax and Dispersal** : Massive Attack's `Mezzanine` album (1998) is considered the work that best crystallizes and culminates the fusion movement that Trip Hop became, bringing together its many influences. After `Mezzanine`, the genre did not die but became increasingly diffuse, dispersed, and hard to define, branching into numerous sub-styles and personal interpretations, making it difficult to view as a single, cohesive movement. "yo creo que esta es la obra que hace que el Trip Hop toque su punto climax y a partir de ahí ya todo se vuelve muy difuso y muy complicado."

# genre - Reggae

## curiosities
**Influence on Trip Hop** : Massive Attack extensively drew from Jamaican reggae music, deconstructing it and slowing down its often fast or simple bases to extremely slow tempos. This complex reinterpretation allowed them to maintain important stylistic foundations of reggae while rendering it almost unrecognizable, especially evident in their cover of John Holt's "Man Next Door." "Masibata consigue nutrirse muchísimo de toda esa música jamai canta, llevarla a tiempos tan extremadamente lentos y de construirla de una manera tan compleja que dejan todo el rege manteniendo unas bases estilísticas importantes y a la vez dejan lo que así reconocible."

# curiosity

**Wild Bunch - Bristol Cultural Movement** : The Wild Bunch was a significant cultural movement in Bristol, UK, centered around hip hop and Afro-black music. This collective is credited as the origin of almost all urban art that emerged in the United Kingdom during the late 1990s and early 2000s. Its influential members included Massive Attack, Tricky, graffiti artist Banksy, producer Nellee Hooper, and DJs from Soul II Soul. "De ahí nace prácticamente todo el arturbano que hay en el Reino Unido finales del 90 y primeros el 2000."
