## albums
**Music Has the Right to Children (1996) - Mythical Debut** : This album created a myth within electronic music, released on Skam / Super_IMG. It revolutionized the electronic scene in the United Kingdom because it strongly contrasted with the prevailing IDM sound of the mid-90s, pioneering a completely new sound. The album is imbued with contained emotions behind its instrumental sound, inspired by nature, and features a very nostalgic and romantic quality, sometimes even recalling dream pop. It also notably incorporates real instruments like bass, guitars, and classic drums, a departure from most IDM artists. "ese disco convocionó el electrónicamente al rin unido porque contra esta era muchísimo con lo que teníamos el idm."
**Geogaddi (2002) - Darker Evolution** : This, their second album, marked a significant evolution from their debut. It is described as darker, more depressing, sometimes sinister and uncomfortable, and less romantic than their first record, creating a more somber atmosphere. "el Ciudad y ese algo más oscuro es bastante más deprimente, a veces incluso es un poco siniestro, a veces es un poco incómodo, no es tan romántico como el primer disco."
**The Campfire Headphase (2005) - Natural & Dream Pop Infused** : This album saw Boards of Canada transition to a sound much closer to that of a band, reminiscent of Spiritualized, incorporating many more instruments. It is less electronic and synthetic, and significantly more natural, making it sometimes sound more like a dream pop band integrated into electronic music rather than an electronic band with dream pop influences. This record is considered a very romantic journey through the evolution of dream pop. "Borsov canada casi se convierte en una banda, en eso recuerda mucho a otra gente como espíritu a la insta, meten muchísimo más instrumento, no están elitrónico, no están sintéticos, es muchísimo más natural."
**Tomorrow's Harvest (2007) - Ambient Culmination** : Identified as their last album to date, this record is almost a culmination of ambient music. It features a very retro and ancient sound, at times recalling instrumental parts by Jean-Michel Jarre or Can, and sometimes appearing almost as a tribute to Brian Eno. It is described as an almost entirely ambient, completely romantic, beautiful, and wonderful album, considered one of the best albums of the 2010s decade. "un disco que es casi un cúlmende de la música ambiental, muy retro, con un sonido muy antiguo, a veces recuerda a esas partes instrumentales ya miseliar, a veces puede recordar a Ken, a veces parece casi un tributo de Brian Eno."

## curiosities
**Niche but Beloved Appeal** : Boards of Canada is considered a relatively niche group without widespread repercussion outside of a dedicated fan base, yet it elicits significant affection and is seen as a very special and emotional band by many. "es un grupo bastante minoritario que no tiene mucha repercusión fuera de los amantes de ese tipo de música."
**Scottish Origin and UK Mid-90s Emergence** : The band is an electronic music duo from Scotland, which emerged in the United Kingdom's vibrant IDM scene in the mid-1990s, a period bustling with new experimental electronic bands. "es un duro de música electrónica escocés que aparecían al reino nido en la gran escena del idm de mitad de los 90."
**IDM Classification Debate** : There is significant disagreement about whether Boards of Canada truly belongs to the IDM movement, despite their presence in the UK scene and being part of Warp Records, a core IDM label. This doubt arises because their music, unlike typical IDM artists such as Aphex Twin, is not solely driven by experimentation but also carries a strong emotional and communicative intent. "mucha gente discrepa de que borsov canada sea parte del movimiento idm."
**Distinct Sound and Influences** : Their sound is notably calmer, more relaxed, and has a very symphonic quality, drawing influences from Brian Eno, Tangerine Dream, and Krautrock. This contrasts with the harder, more purely experimental IDM artists of the time. "el sonido de borsov canadace mucho más tranquilo, mucho más relajado, tiene influencias de Brian Eno, de tangera indream del crowd rock, tienen una vertiente muy sinfónica."
**Vintage Gear Philosophy** : While many Warp IDM artists used the latest technologies or computers, Boards of Canada deliberately utilized very old, vintage, recycled, and sometimes even malfunctioning synthesizers and electronic elements. "borsov canadace usaban sintetizadores y elementos electrónicos muy antiguos, muy vintage, muy reciclados a veces incluso en mal estado."
**Profound Emotional and Symbolic Content** : A defining characteristic of their creative work is its immense emotional and emotive depth. Boards of Canada aims to transmit a wealth of emotions, feelings, and a connection with nature. Their song titles often carry double meanings and deeper intentions, evoking natural sounds, memories, sadness, and many other emotions in listeners, a stark contrast to artists focused purely on sound manipulation. "borsov canadace quería transmitir un montón de emociones, de sentimientos, de conexión con la naturaleza, muchos de sus títulos tienen doble sentidos y terceras intenciones."
**Early Self-Production and Label Interest** : Initially, their work was self-produced, a common starting point for many electronic artists of their generation. However, their unique output quickly attracted the attention of several record labels in the mid-90s UK scene. "empieza siendo tu auto-producido, prácticamente todos los artistas electrónicos de esa generación empezaron igual."
**Use of Real Instruments** : Unlike most other IDM artists, Boards of Canada incorporates real instruments such as bass, guitars, and classic drums into their electronic compositions, a trait that sometimes draws comparisons to the French band R, known for mixing electronic and instrumental music, or to Jean-Michel Jarre and Brian Eno. "borsov canada también se apoya en instrumentos reales, como por ejemplo los bajos las guitarras o baterías clásicas, cosa que nunca hicieron la mayoría de otros miembros que formaban parte del movimiento idm."
**Reclusive and Myth-Building Persona** : The band has cultivated an enormous myth around their elusive nature. They are described as timid, elusive, rarely giving interviews, never touring, performing very few concerts, and having scarce photos or information about their working methods. This extreme discretion contributes significantly to their enigmatic appeal. "borsov canada eran lentices, era gente de muy tímida, gente muy esquiva, no quería dar entrevistas nunca han dado giras, no han dado prácticamente conciertos, casi no tenemos conciertos de borsov canada, casi no tenemos fotos."
**Difficult-to-Track Discography and Hidden Material** : While consistently working, their output is often hard to trace. Besides their four main albums, they've released an enormous quantity of EPs and other material online, much of which has been lost, deleted, re-uploaded, or remains unverified. They are known for creating "hidden games" to help fans locate their music, a practice shared with Aphex Twin. "su trabajo se pierde un montón y no es fácil de rastrear, han sacado tres discos más a lo largo de estos años y han sacado una cantidad enorme de EPS y de material que han ido colgando por internet."
**Consistent Critical Acclaim** : All of Boards of Canada's albums have received incredible reviews, with critics consistently holding a special affection for the band's work. "Todos los discos de Borsov Canadá han tenido críticas increíbles, o sea, la crítica siempre ha tenido, yo creo que con cariño especial los Borsov Canadá."
**Broad Appeal to Music Lovers** : Their entire discography is considered essential for fans of electronic music and late 90s IDM. However, their unique sound also strongly appeals to listeners who enjoy rock, guitar music, and especially dream pop, with comparisons to My Bloody Valentine, Spiritualized, Slowdive, Ride, and Mogwai. "la carrera de Borsov Canadá entera es casi un paso obligado para los amantes de la electrónica y amantes de la de finales de los 90, pero incluso para gente que le gusta el roco, que le gusta la música de guitarra, es una parte interesante que le va a passionate, le va a encantar."
**Mystical and Metaphysical Vision of Music** : The band possesses a profound mystical vision of music, believing it can transport listeners and evoke memories, often referring to their own work as "the music of memory" because it reconnects people with their past. While not explicitly religious, their approach is deeply metaphysical, guiding listeners to reflect on deeper concepts. "Ellos tienen una visión muy mística de la música, en el que creen que la música te transporta, te lleva recuerdos, ellos definen muchas veces su música como la música del recuerdo, porque te lleva a recordar partes del pasado."
**Nostalgia, Memory, and Open Interpretation** : A central theme throughout their work is nostalgia and the constant evocation of past memories. Many song titles and messages are subtly critical, designed to make listeners ponder deeper meanings that the band never explicitly states, allowing for personal interpretation and association. "hay un hilo conductor enorme que es toda la nostalgia y el recuerdo del pasado, que te boca constantemente toda a Borsov Canadá, pero luego hay muchos títulos de canciones y mensajes, un tanto críticos que te llevan a pensar cosas que ellos nunca llevan a decir que eso."
**Extreme Perfectionism and Creative Process** : Despite the perception of infrequent releases due to their reclusiveness and long gaps between albums, Boards of Canada is an exceptionally hard-working band. Their album creation process is characterized by an "unhealthy fetishism" and extreme perfectionism, often taking years. For every album, which typically has 17-20 tracks, they might have worked on 200-300 cuts, discarding a vast majority (up to 90%) of their material, releasing only what they consider their absolute best. "Es una banda que trabaja muchísimo. Lo que pasa es que sus discos son un puro proceso de fetichismo que hacía enfermizo."
**Contrast with Other IDM Artists on Release Strategy** : Their highly selective release strategy stands in stark contrast to many other prominent Warp IDM artists like Autechre and Aphex Twin, who are known for releasing almost everything they create, including unfinished tracks or extensive archived sessions. "Esto es algo que contrasta mucho con los otros grandes artistas de Guarpe de Lidm. Al final, artistas como Tequero, incluso el propio FXTWING, una de las cosas por la que destacan muchos artistas de Lidm es porque sacan todo, absolutamente todo lo que hacen."

# artist - Aphex Twin

## curiosities
**Archetypal IDM Artist** : Aphex Twin is considered a prime example of IDM, creating music driven purely by experimentation, rather than overt emotional communication. In interviews, he often states his primary interest is experimenting with sound, creating and working with sound as a physical element, without an underlying emotional message. "era música que nacia por y para la experimentación... no hay una comunicación emotiva por detrás sino una experimentación pura del sonido como un elemento físico."
**Prolific Live Performer in Mid-90s** : In the mid-1990s, Aphex Twin was known for his continuous concert activity, performing extensively with 30 to 40 concerts in some years, contrasting sharply with the reclusive nature of Boards of Canada. "tenemos años a mitad de los 90 donde a fswing llevaba a dar más de 30 y 40 conciertos."
**Hiding Music from Fans** : Similar to Boards of Canada, Aphex Twin is known for playing games where he hides his music for fans to discover. "ellos les gusta hacer muchos juegos escondidos para localizar su música, en eso tienen que ver mucho con afexo y también ha hecho mucho eso."
**Extremely Prolific and Unfiltered Output** : Aphex Twin is renowned for releasing virtually everything he creates, including unfinished material. He has possibly released over 3000 tracks throughout his career, often publishing raw or unfinished songs on platforms like SoundCloud. He has openly confessed to releasing tracks he never completed, starting but not finishing them before uploading and distributing. "FXTWING posiblemente ha desagado más de 3000 temas a la roda de su vida. Y es que están desacarlo todo, que es que incluso en sus cuentas de SoundCloud va haciendo canciones interminarias y las va publicando."

# genre - IDM

## curiosities
**Controversial Naming Convention** : The term "IDM" (Intelligent Dance Music) is often disliked by many and considered pretentious, even though it emerged as "electronic music for smart people." "mucha gente no le gusta ese nombre, mucha gente considera que el término de idm es un poco pretencioso."
**Intellectual UK Electronic Movement (1990s)** : IDM appeared during the 1990s as an intellectual electronic movement in the United Kingdom, characterized by its focus on complex and experimental sound. "el idm es un movimiento de electrónica intelectual de hecho sale de inteligentas music como música electrónica para gente lista que aparece durante los 90."
**Core Artists on Warp Records** : Key artists associated with the IDM movement, particularly its "hard core," were signed to Warp Records, including Autechre, Aphex Twin, and The Future Sound of London. "los grandes nombres de lida n nácendela de escorrafía guard records como puede ser autéqueros, los fx swing, los tuelons formen toda esta generación."
**Experimentation over Emotion** : IDM music is defined as being born purely for experimentation, with artists primarily motivated by sound experimentation rather than explicit emotional artistic communication. "era música que nacia por y para la experimentación... no hay una comunicación emotiva por detrás sino una experimentación pura del sonido como un elemento físico."
**Technological Approach** : Many IDM artists associated with Warp Records tended to use the latest technologies or computers for their electronic music production, a practice that contrasted with Boards of Canada's use of vintage equipment. "la gente de war que hacia el idm es más duro utilizaba casi las últimas tecnologías, usaba material muy antiguo pero por ejemplo usaba los ordenadores para hacer el electrónica."
**Releasing All Material** : A notable characteristic of many IDM artists is their tendency to release almost all of their creative output, including unfinished material, often through various platforms or compilations, contrasting significantly with the highly selective release strategy of Boards of Canada. "una de las cosas por la que destacan muchos artistas de Lidm es porque sacan todo, absolutamente todo lo que hacen."

# genre - Dream Pop

## curiosities
**Romantic and Nostalgic Sound** : Dream pop is characterized by a nostalgic and romantic sound, which is present in Boards of Canada's music, particularly in "Music Has the Right to Children" and "The Campfire Headphase." "este disco tiene muchísimas emociones contenidas de tras el sonido instrumental, desde inspiraciones en la naturaleza tiene un sonido muy nostálgico, muy romántico, a veces incluso recuerda el dream pop."
**Integration with Electronic Music** : Boards of Canada's "The Campfire Headphase" is described as sounding like a dream pop band integrated into electronic music, rather than an electronic band influenced by dream pop, contributing to the evolution of the genre. "a veces parece más una banda de Drimpop integrada en la electrónica que una banda de electrónica influenciada... es un viaje muy romántico por lo que es la evolución del Drimpop."
**Wide Appeal and Influential Acts** : Dream pop has a broad appeal, with Boards of Canada being recommended for fans of established acts like My Bloody Valentine, Spiritualized, Slowdive, Ride, and Mogwai. "cualquiera avante del Drimpop de My Lord Ivan Lanta, de Spiritalize, de Slowdive, de Ride, incluso de Mokuey o de My Lord Ninjaked, va a pasarse, vamos, va a maravillarse con lo que es Borsov Canadá."

# label - Warp Records

## curiosities
**Hub of 90s IDM** : Warp Records was a central label for the "hard core" of the IDM movement in the 1990s, featuring prominent artists like Autechre, Aphex Twin, and The Future Sound of London, as well as Boards of Canada. "forman parte de warp, forman parte del núcleo duro del idm que era a escorrafía guard."
**Associated with Advanced Technology in IDM** : IDM artists on Warp were often noted for using advanced technologies and computers in their electronic music production, setting a standard that Boards of Canada diverged from with their vintage gear. "la gente de war que hacia el idm es más duro utilizaba casi las últimas tecnologías, usaba material muy antiguo pero por ejemplo usaba los ordenadores para hacer el electrónica."

# curiosity

**Mid-90s UK Electronic Scene Diversity** : The mid-1990s in the United Kingdom was a vibrant and diverse period for electronic music. It featured the "Bristol sound" of trip hop, exemplified by artists like Massive Attack, alongside the "hard core" of IDM, represented by purely experimental electronic artists such as Aphex Twin, Autechre, and The Future Sound of London. "la élite de la electrónica del rin unido a mitad de los 90, teníamos por un lado la gente que estaba haciendo el trip hub que tenía influencias del trip hub... teníamos ese sonido bristo el que estaba haciendo mas ibadac y lo teníamos el núcleo duro del idm."
