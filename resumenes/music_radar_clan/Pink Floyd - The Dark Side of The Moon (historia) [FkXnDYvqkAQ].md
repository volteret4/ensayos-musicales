## albums
**The Dark Side of the Moon (1973) - 43rd Anniversary, Multi-Release Concept Album** : This album recently marked its 43rd anniversary, an event tied to one of its three official releases. Its initial launch was considered a disaster, leading to multiple official release dates, driven by intense anticipation. The record company prematurely released the album before it was fully completed or out of post-production. The most significant official presentation, also commemorating its 43rd anniversary, took place at the London Planetarium, a venue chosen for its thematic alignment with the album's cover and overall concept. Despite the official presentation, the band, upset that the album was unfinished, boycotted the event. Specialized press attending the launch found only life-sized cardboard cutouts of the band members with the album playing, as the band refused to be present for its own release under these circumstances. Upon release, the album quickly reached number one on the charts but only held that position for a single week. Remarkably, it then spent approximately 15 years, or about 800 consecutive weeks, on the Billboard 200 list of best-selling albums in the United States, an extraordinary feat that, while not a record, came very close. It is recognized as one of the best conceptual albums in music history and a defining work in Pink Floyd's career. While the music itself is highly important, the band's primary focus during its creation was on the concept, not just the musical composition. The album integrates compositions, melodies, and even complete songs that pre-existed Pink Floyd's official albums, having been previously used in concerts but not recorded. The band aimed for a solid conceptual album with a very closed narrative, leading them to adopt a clearer and more direct lyrical language and to discard any material that didn't perfectly align with the album's themes. This represented an intensification of an evolutionary process within Pink Floyd, marking it as possibly their first truly great conceptual album and setting a new direction for the band's musical development. The complex development of its conceptual thread, rather than just the music, began almost a year prior; in February 1972, the band previewed a significant portion of the album's melodies and songs in London to specialized press and critics, who were amazed by what they heard but had to wait a full year for the album's release. The album's central concept is "the hardest aspects of the human condition." Two significant thematic influences were the mental deterioration of Syd Barrett, Pink Floyd's original leader, whose delicate emotional health and eventual retreat into reclusion due to severe mental problems (brought on by the band's success and record label pressures) deeply impacted the band's early career and ongoing exploration of madness and psychological evolution. Another important influence was philosophy, which the band began to seriously integrate as a mental thread and theme within their songs, expanding on their existing mystical and philosophical leanings. The album delves into themes such as madness (beyond Syd Barrett's specific case), hatred, greed, fear, consumerism, aggression, depression, and the fear of death, exploring these difficult aspects of human evolution throughout life. The album is divided into two parts, Side A and Side B. Side A approaches the harshness of the human condition from a personal and interior perspective: the first song addresses human madness or the loss of mental control; the second discusses regaining mental control and taking charge of one's life after losing direction; the third speaks of the refuge and reflection found once control is re-established; the fourth explores refuge and solitude after recovery and reflection; and the final song is an allegory of death at the end of life, once control has been regained. Side B examines the same themes but from a social viewpoint, focusing on how society and groups affect the individual. It touches upon greed, racial prejudice, consumerism, and the unhappiness induced by a society devoted to work and suffering. Side B analyzes this societal situation, offering a final conclusion, and again uses allegories, particularly mental illness, as a source of inspiration, but this time focusing on how a hostile environment can drive people to madness. Side B culminates in what the band called "catharsis," leading to a vision of liberation, a more humble understanding of the world, and a collective and cooperative perspective that champions unity for the common good over the societal competition that ultimately leads to madness and unhappiness. The album is musically incredible, considered one of the best in history, but its true significance lies in its profound conceptual nature, offering deep thoughts and a critical vision of society in the 1960s, 1970s, and 1980s, marking a new, revolutionary way of working for Pink Floyd. "el Darkseller de Moon fue el último álbum que acabaron grabando Pink Floyd con capito de récord, se aparte de la isa que borrón pinta su relación."

## curiosities
**Roger Waters' Vision and Label Conflicts** : Roger Waters, as the band's leader, possessed not only great musical talent but also an enormous business vision, leading him to have very clear ideas about the commercial development of Pink Floyd's discography. This often led him to intervene in areas typically handled by the record company, such as album launches and distribution, which generated significant controversy. His relationship with the record company was fraught for a long time. "Roger Waters, aparte de un gran músico, también tiene una enorme visión empresarial."
**Boycott of Album Launch Due to Unfinished State** : Pink Floyd became very annoyed when their album, *The Dark Side of the Moon*, was released while still in post-production and not yet finished. As a result, the group chose to boycott the official presentation of their own album at the London Planetarium. Attendees from the specialized press found only life-sized cardboard cutouts of the band members playing the album. "el grupo se molestó mucho y acabó boicoteando el lanzamiento de su propio disco."
**Conceptual Album Approach** : Pink Floyd, particularly with *The Dark Side of the Moon*, intensified their focus on creating conceptual albums. This meant that while music was important, the concept itself was the primary focus, aiming for a solid album with a closed argument. This led the band to develop a clearer and more direct language in their lyrics and to discard any musical material that did not 100% align with the album's central themes. "Lo que realmente estaban trabajando no era tanto la composición del álbum sino el trabajo conceptual de un álbum solido con un argumento muy cerrado."
**Pre-Release Presentation to Critics (February 1972)** : Nearly a year before the official release of *The Dark Side of the Moon*, in February 1972, Pink Floyd held a presentation in London where they showcased a significant portion of the melodies and songs that would form part of the album. This preview was for specialized press and critics, who were reportedly amazed by what they heard, yet had to wait a full year for the album's launch. "casi un año antes, en febrero de 1972 o un año antes de que saliese el álbum, la banda ya había hecho una presentación en Londres en la que había presentado una parte importante de las melodías y de las clasiones que formaban parte del dance, ahí lo hacemos, a toda la brecha especializada e incluso algunos críticos."
**Influence of Syd Barrett's Mental Deterioration** : A key thematic influence for *The Dark Side of the Moon* was the mental deterioration of Syd Barrett, Pink Floyd's original leader. Barrett had always been emotionally delicate, and the pressures of the record label, combined with the band's success, eventually led him to severe mental problems and complete reclusion at home with his family. This experience profoundly marked Pink Floyd's early career and became a recurring inspiration for themes of madness and psychological evolution within their music. "Aquí tenemos dos influencias temáticas bastante importantes que por un lado fue el deterioro mental, que sofre o si parre, que fue el líder original de Pink Floyd al principio antes de que acabas a retirar los primeros discos."

# curiosity

**Definition of a Conceptual Album** : A conceptual album is characterized by its emphasis on a central concept or theme, rather than solely on individual musical compositions. While the music remains highly important, the primary effort during its creation is dedicated to developing a solid, closed argument or narrative that permeates the entire work. This approach often leads to the use of clearer and more direct lyrical language, and the discarding of any material that does not 100% coincide with the album's overarching theme. "Evidentemente en este álbum la música es muy importante, pero lo que más trabajo Pink Floyd durante este álbum no fue la música sino el concepto."
**Philosophical Influence in Music** : Beginning notably with *The Dark Side of the Moon*, Pink Floyd started to seriously deepen their exploration of philosophy, integrating it as a fundamental intellectual thread within their songs and even as a direct lyrical theme. While the band had always harbored mystical and philosophical inclinations, this album marked a significant intensification of this influence in their work. "Ellos siempre habían tenido unas temáticas un tanto místicas y filosóficas, pero sobre todo en este ámo es cuando empiezan a profundizar a seriamente en la filosofía como ilor mental de las canciones o incluso como temática de las propias canciones."
