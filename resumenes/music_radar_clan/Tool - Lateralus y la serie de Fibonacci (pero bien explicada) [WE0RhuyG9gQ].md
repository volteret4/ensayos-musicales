## albums
**Lateralus (Year Unspecified) - Masterpiece of Complex Structure** : This album is considered an absolute masterpiece, one of the best in rock history, and one of the most complex ever made, comparable to the greatest works created. It uniquely combines a narrative theme with a structural-rhythmic theme, the latter being rare in music history, and is entirely based on the complex mathematical concepts of the Golden Ratio and the Fibonacci sequence. "obra absolutamente maestra, yo creo que con muchísima diferencia uno de los mejores discos a la historia del rock y uno de los discos más complejos jamás hechos."

## songs
**Lateralus (Year Unspecified) - Fibonacci Syllable Structure** : This song, sometimes referred to as "987," features a highly complex syllable structure in its verses that precisely follows the Fibonacci sequence, starting with one syllable, then one, two, three, five, eight, and subsequently reversing, creating an intricate mathematical pattern within the vocals. "toda la estructura de sílabas en sus versos, sigue la complejísima serie de Fibonacci de una sílaba, una sílaba 2, 3, 5, 8 y vuelve hacia atrás y hace un complejísimo juego siguiendo toda la serie de Fibonacci."

## curiosities
**Band's Secrecy and Fan Engagement** : Tul is known for not explicitly revealing the secrets and inspirations embedded in their albums, instead allowing fans to embark on a quest to discover them. This creates a "fun game" for fans, though it also leads to frustration when the band does not confirm fan-discovered secrets, a game that many still participate in years later. "La banda tampoco ayuda demasiado porque nos habla un montón de todos esos secretos inspiraciones y escondrijos que recorren todos los discos de Tul en general, pero tampoco nos da datos para que un poco peleemos para intentar encontrarlos, lo cual genera un juego muy divertido entre los fans."
**Mathematical Composition of Lateralus** : The album "Lateralus" is renowned for its structural and rhythmic complexity, where almost all instruments operate within an intricate mathematical order based on the Fibonacci sequence. This includes syllable counts in vocal stanzas, Golden Ratio application in verse/stanza time structures, and precise rhythmic patterns in drums, bass, and guitar, even influencing song lengths and overall album duration, with complex time signature changes derived from the Fibonacci series. "casi todos los instrumentos trabajan en un complejísimo orden matemático basado en la serie de Fibonacci."
**Years of Mathematical Experimentation** : The band Tul dedicated years to mathematically manipulating the rhythmic structures of the "Lateralus" album. This extensive experimentation aimed to integrate complex mathematical concepts into the music, resulting in one of the most rhythmically complex albums ever written, a feat considered almost impossible to achieve while maintaining musicality. "Tul se pasó años jugando matemáticamente con las estructuras ríntmicas del disco."
**Unconfirmed Fan Discoveries** : Over the years, dedicated fans have tirelessly searched for Golden Ratio proportions and Fibonacci sequences within the "Lateralus" album, uncovering highly complex patterns. While some claim to have even created new rhythmically coherent songs by reassembling parts of existing tracks based on these mathematical principles, the band typically refrains from confirming these findings, either due to their perceived inaccuracy or to maintain the ongoing "game" with their fanbase. "En parte porque yo creo que son mentira, en parte porque yo creo que le gusta seguir el juego a todos los afidianados."
**Band's Recent Activity and Confirmation Challenges** : Tul has not been very active in recent years, which makes it difficult to confirm any new discoveries made by fans regarding "Lateralus" from the past five or six years. This lack of recent presence contributes to the ongoing mystery and unverified nature of many fan theories. "Tul es una banda que no ha estado tampoco muy presente en los últimos años con lo cual yo creo que los últimos 5 o 6 años de gente que firma ver descubierto cosas en la teráluz de Tul pues ya están lejos de poder ser confirmadas."

# curiosity

**Golden Ratio (Proporción Áurea) - Ancient Greek Discovery** : Discovered by the ancient Greeks, the Golden Ratio describes a special rectangle where, if a square is drawn within it, the remaining rectangle has the same proportions as the original. This "beautiful" proportion found extensive mathematical and trigonometric applications and was widely used in sculpture, painting, and architecture. "A la proporción de estos rectángulos se le llamó la proporción aurea, que continúas a nuestros días."
**Golden Ratio - Applications and Presence** : The Golden Ratio, also known as the number of Phidias, extends beyond ancient art and architecture; Leonardo da Vinci based many works on it, and it's found in human body proportions (ears, eyes, nose, mouth, height, limb length). In nature, it appears in spirals (sea waves, shells, galaxies, sunflowers), and modern applications include identity documents, credit cards, Apple's iPod Classic, and televisions. The human brain is also highly capable of recognizing this proportion. "Hemos descubierto que todo nuestro cuerpo está basado en proporciones aureas, desde la forma de la oreja, los ojos, la nariz, la boca, la altura, la longitud de las piernas y brazos, un montón de cosas están basadas en esta proporción."
**Fibonacci Sequence (Sucesión de Fibonacci) - Discovery and Structure** : Discovered by Leandro de Pisa (Fibonacci) many years after the Golden Ratio, this sequence starts with 0 and 1, with each subsequent number being the sum of the two preceding ones (0, 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, and so on, to infinity). This series was found to have numerous practical applications in systems like Pascal's matrix and various trigonometric contexts. "Este hombre muy aficionado a hacer series de números y inventó una serie un tanto curiosa, partía del 1 y el 0 y a cada número iba asumando sus dos anteriores."
**Fibonacci Sequence - Connection to Golden Ratio** : Through experimentation, it was discovered that dividing any number in the infinite Fibonacci sequence by its preceding number approaches a familiar limit: the Golden Ratio (the number of Phidias). This revealed that the Fibonacci sequence and the Golden Ratio, discovered by the Greeks, are fundamentally the same concept, further found in natural spirals like sea waves, shells, and galaxies. "descubriendo que la serie de Fibonacci, la proporción aorea de los griegos en el fondo era la misma cosa."
**Johann Sebastian Bach - "Canón del Cangrejo"** : This classical work is cited as a very classic example of a piece with a structural theme in music history. "Tenemos ya casos muy clásicos de temática estructural, pues por ejemplo el canón del cangrejo de Bach."
**King Gizzard & The Lizard Wizard - Structural Theme** : Some of the recent works by King Gizzard & The Lizard Wizard are mentioned as contemporary examples that also feature a structural theme in their composition, akin to the mathematical complexity found in other works. "tenemos también recientemente alguna de las obras de Kingis and the Elisar Wizard, que también tiene una temática estructural."
**Leonardo da Vinci - Golden Ratio Application** : The renowned artist Leonardo da Vinci created numerous paintings and works based on the Golden Ratio, demonstrating its use in Renaissance art and design. "El Leonardo da Vinci haría un montón de cuadros y de cosas basadas en la proporción aurea."
**Leandro de Pisa (Fibonacci) - Discoverer of Fibonacci Sequence** : Leandro de Pisa, an ancient man with a strong interest in number series, is credited with discovering what would be named the Fibonacci sequence, a progression where each number is the sum of the two preceding ones. "un hombre muy viejo también, llamado Leandro de Pisa, habría descubierto lo que se llamaría la sucesión de Fibonacci."
**Ancient Greeks - Mathematical Discoveries** : The ancient Greeks, known for their deep appreciation for mathematics, discovered fundamental concepts such as the Pythagorean theorem, the number Pi, and extensively studied numbers and trigonometry, including the special properties of the Golden Ratio. "A los antiguos grigos les encantaban las matemáticas, descubrieron el teorema de Pitágoras, descubrieron el número P, descubrieron un montón de cosas sobre números."
