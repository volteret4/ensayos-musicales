## albums
**Filth (1983-84)** : Released early in the band's career, this album, along with *Cop*, is today considered a true gem and a masterpiece of guitar sound experimentation. At the time of its release, Michael Gira was not a professional musician and had another job. It was released on independent labels and received little to no repercussion, selling very few copies and failing to gain recognition, making it difficult for the band to secure concerts. Critics gave it very bad reviews, finding it radically different from the mass-consumed rock of the early 80s by bands like Metallica and Iron Maiden. Its sound was completely atmospheric, contrasting with the sharp, virtuoso, and fast guitar playing common in 80s rock. The lyrics, penned by Michael Gira, were disturbing, addressing themes such as sex, religion, cruelty, submission (sometimes voluntary), love leading to submission, vile behaviors, and aggression. These lyrics were so powerful that, had the band been famous, they likely would have been censored. "Hoy en día esos dos trabajos están considerados, obras maestras de la experimentación del sonido de guitarra."

**Cop (1983-84)** : Released very close to *Filth*, this album shares its characteristics as an early Swans work. It is now considered a masterpiece of experimental guitar sound, despite having almost no repercussion, low sales, and receiving very bad reviews upon release. The album featured Michael Gira's pioneering atmospheric guitar sound and disturbing lyrics that explored themes of sex, religion, cruelty, submission, and aggressive behaviors. Its radical departure from contemporary rock meant it was largely unknown and unappreciated at the time. "Hoy en día esos dos trabajos están considerados, obras maestras de la experimentación del sonido de guitarra."

**Holy Money (1985-86)** : This album, released in the mid-80s, marked a new revolution for Swans with the incorporation of Jarboe into the group. Jarboe's contributions in music and her presence as a singer and a woman helped make the band less noisy and aggressive, and more melodic, leading to increased popularity and more reviews (though not necessarily good ones until the band became truly famous). Despite the shift, the album maintained a strong artistic charge. "Holy Money" (referred to as "Jolly Money" in the transcript) is considered one of the most interesting albums for fans of Noise Rock and Industrial Rock origins. "Los discos en mitad de los 80 en los que se incorporan a Jarbo al grupo de 85, 86 como el Jolly Money son una nueva revolución dentro del campo de suans."

**The Burning World (1989)** : This album is widely considered an "absolute disaster" and the "worst album in Swans' entire career." It was the result of the band's unexpected popularity from their "Love Will Tear Us Apart" cover, which led them to sign with a major label and work with significant economic resources and a professional producer for the first time. The album sweetened and relaxed Swans' sound, introducing much more banal lyrics, including classic love songs that were completely out of character for the band. It featured much more melodic music with influences from country, Americana, and folk, which clashed with Swans' established discourse. The album failed to convince anyone: classic fans were horrified (even by the cover art), and the new audience gained from the single was neither loyal nor knew what to expect. Critically panned, disliked by the band, and a commercial failure, it is seen as the beginning of the end for the first iteration of Swans, which dissolved in the mid-90s. "El resultado, pues, Borning Wall de 1989, un absoluto desastre, el peor desastre y el peor disco de toda la carrera de suans."

**Soundtracks for the Blind (1996)** : Released as the band's final album before their separation, this double album is paradoxically considered by many to be the best of Swans' career, despite coming out of a period of immense creative, audience, and musical focus crisis. Michael Gira, believing Swans was "dead," compiled and reworked a multitude of ideas accumulated over the previous 12-13 years of the band's existence. The album masterfully collects all of Swans' diverse stages: from their early hard noise rock to the softer, more melodic period with Jarboe, and even the more banal melodic rock that followed the "Love Will Tear Us Apart" cover. It functions as a "farewell compilation," packaging the best of these often disjointed stages into a surprisingly cohesive and brilliant work. It received fantastic reviews in both Europe and the United States, leading many publications like Pitchfork and AllMusic to re-evaluate and update their initially horrible reviews of Swans' early albums. "En el 96 sale el que para muchos es el mejor disco de su carrera, curiosamente después de todo ese problema creativo."

**21st Century Albums (post-reunion)** : After a lengthy hiatus following their mid-90s dissolution, Michael Gira reformed Swans in the 21st century with new musicians. Despite Gira's age and the long gap, this reformed iteration of the band has produced "some of the best albums of the 21st century." This period is described as incredible, with the two most recent albums being "insane" and defying expectations for a band that had dissolved decades prior. Swans remains active and is expected to release more material. "nos ha dado algunos dos mejores discos al siglo XXI."

## songs
**Love Will Tear Us Apart (1988) - Joy Division Cover** : Released in 1988, this cover of Joy Division's iconic song is ironically considered by Michael Gira to be the "worst mistake of his entire career." Despite Gira's personal view, the single brought immense popularity to Swans, especially in the United States, and also significantly in Europe. It became, by far, their best-selling release until their later material in the late 90s. However, this popularity attracted a new audience that was largely disconnected from the aggressive, artistic, and performance-driven ethos Swans had always championed. The success of the single ultimately led the band to leave their independent labels and sign with a major label, a move that would subsequently lead to the critically panned and commercially disastrous album, *The Burning World*. "el propio Michael J. Laro definió como el peor error de toda su carrera."

## curiosities
**Michael Gira as a "Sound Explorer"** : Michael Gira, the leader and constant mastermind behind Swans, described himself not primarily as a musician but as a "sound seeker" or "sound explorer." This self-definition accurately reflects his artistic approach, which has always prioritized extracting new and unfamiliar sounds and creating novel sonic atmospheres and methods with instruments, rather than pursuing virtuosity. "se definía si mismo, más que un músico, un buscador del sonido como una especie de explorador del sonido."

**Revolutionary Early Lineup** : When Swans first started, their formation was highly revolutionary and deliberately moved away from classic rock structures. The band initially featured two drummers and two bassists, a configuration that was highly unusual and contributed to their unique, intense sound. "suans cuando arrancaba tenia dos baterías y dos bajos, por ejemplo, entonces se alejaba muchísimo de estas estructuras musicales de las bandas clásicas de rock."

**Constantly Evolving Identity** : Swans has been characterized by its constantly changing lineup, instruments, and working methods throughout its career. This continuous evolution means that "there isn't one Swans," but rather "four or five Swans" over time, with the band making drastically different works. The only constant member and guiding force through these transformations has been Michael Gira. "es una banda que ha cambiado muchísimo, ha evolucionado muchísimo, ha hecho trabajos absolutamente distintos o no sobre otros."

**Pioneering Atmospheric Rock Sound** : Michael Gira is possibly the "father" who invented the atmospheric sound in rock music. While atmospheric elements existed in psychedelia and progressive rock, Swans' approach, particularly in their early work, created a unique atmospheric guitar sound that blurred the lines between playing and noise. This specific vision is considered a direct precursor to the atmospheric rock sound found in later genres like shoegaze and dream pop. "Yo creo que es el padre, posiblemente es el padre que inventó el sonido atmosférica con el rock."

**Disturbing and Influential Lyrics** : The lyrics in Swans' early albums, penned by Michael Gira, were profoundly disturbing and overwhelming. They delved into themes of sex, religion, cruel attitudes, both voluntary and involuntary submission of people, love relationships devolving into submission, and vile, aggressive behaviors. These lyrical themes, described as a blend of aggressive performance and horror film, were highly influential and defined much of the early industrial rock movement. "las letras de suans serán muy sobrecogeoras, medio camino entre la performance agresiva y la película de terror fue casi la base argumental que definiría."

**Legendary and Visceral Live Performances** : Swans' concerts, especially in their early period (before 1985), were legendary and described as "insane" experiences, bordering between a musical concert and an artistic performance or "videoperformance." Michael Gira and the band sought to create a full sensory experience for attendees, often engaging in provocative actions such as insulting the audience, touching them, or performing non-physical but impactful aggressions like pulling hair. They also manipulated the environment by, for example, firing fire alarms, or plunging the room into darkness. With Jarboe's arrival in the mid-80s, these performances became even more exaggerated, sometimes leading to protests and local newspaper articles that decried them as "not music, but something much more grotesque." "los conciertos esuans estaban casi en el límite entre el concierto y la performance artística."

**Jarboe's Transformative Influence** : Jarboe joined Swans in the mid-80s, forming what was described as a "perfect tandem" with Michael Gira. Her arrival marked a significant turning point, bringing a new revolution to the band's sound. Jarboe contributed musically and was also adept at the band's videoperformances, making the live shows even more impactful. Her singing and presence as a woman helped to reduce the band's previously extreme noisiness and aggression, making their sound more melodic and contributing to an increase in their popularity. "Jarbo es la chica que sería casi el tanden perfecto con Michael Gira que le da una vuelta de tuerca a la siguiente etapa de suans, que es la de mitad de los 80."

**Worst Career Mistake - "Love Will Tear Us Apart" Cover** : Michael Gira explicitly stated that Swans' 1988 cover of Joy Division's "Love Will Tear Us Apart" was the "worst mistake of his entire career." While it brought the band immense popularity, particularly in the US, and became their best-selling single for a time, it attracted an audience that did not align with Swans' core artistic philosophy. This commercial success ultimately led the band to sign with a major label, a decision that resulted in the critically panned album *The Burning World* and initiated a period of creative struggle and audience alienation. "el propio Michael J. Laro definió como el peor error de toda su carrera."

**Mid-90s Dissolution and Unexpected 21st Century Reunion** : Swans dissolved in the mid-to-late 1990s, with *The Burning World* in 1989 possibly marking the beginning of the end. Following their acclaimed farewell album, *Soundtracks for the Blind* (1996), it was widely believed the band would never return. However, in the 21st century, Michael Gira unexpectedly reformed Swans with new musicians. Against all odds, this older, reunited iteration of the band, after nearly 15 years of inactivity, has gone on to produce "some of the best albums of the 21st century," delivering an "incredible" and "insane" new stage in their career. "Curiosamente en pleno siglo XXI, muchísimos años, una tarde que enseñó más tarde, Maikere Gira, refunda la banda suans a como un montón de músicos."

**Rumors of Jarboe Rejoining** : There are current rumors that Jarboe might rejoin Swans. For fans of the band's mid-80s period, especially the albums where she first incorporated into the group, her return would be an "incredible" development. "Son anrumores de que ya robó, va a volver a incorporarse al grupo."

**Pervasive Influence on Modern Rock** : Swans is considered one of the maximum expressions of the No Wave and noise music movements, and a seminal influence on modern guitar music. They are identified as the "absolute father" of numerous subsequent movements and bands, including The Pixies, grunge, shoegaze, and much of contemporary rock music. Their influence extends even to heavy metal bands like Tool and Dream Theater, and the thematic elements of industrial rock, exemplified by Nine Inch Nails. Their early work is essential for anyone seeking to understand the roots of much of today's music. "padre absoluto de movimientos posteriores como, por ejemplo, los pixis, el grancho, el sugays, incluso muchas música rock que tenemos hoy."

**Early Albums' Critical Re-evaluation** : Swans' first albums initially received "horrible" critical reception, with reviews containing harsh phrases like, "I doubt anyone could like this album." However, after the release of *Soundtracks for the Blind* in 1996 and their later resurgence, many modern music publications such as Pitchfork and AllMusic performed "modern revisions" of those early albums, acknowledging their previously overlooked significance and impact. "Los primeros discos son unas críticas horrible, o sea, tan horrible que tenían incluso frases muy fuertes como, dudo que hay alguien a quien le pueda gustar este disco."

## curiosities
**Late Appearance in No Wave Movement** : Sonic Youth is noted as one of the "two great bands" representing the No Wave movement, alongside Swans. However, like Swans, they appeared "very late" in the movement's history, forming after No Wave's peak in the mid-70s and releasing their first album around 1983. This late arrival complicated their strict classification as a pure No Wave band. "Estas bandas empiezan a primeras de los 80 sobre el merdisco de las doses de 83."

**Formed After Brian Eno's No New York** : Sonic Youth was curiously founded after the release of Brian Eno's influential 1978 compilation, *No New York*, which is considered one of the only existing compilations of the No Wave movement. "las dos bandas curiosamente se fundan después de aquel famoso recopilatorio que había hecho Brian N. No, de No New York, el único casi recopilatorio que tenemos del no wave que salía en el 78."

# genre - No Wave

## curiosities
**Peak in Mid-70s** : The No Wave movement reached its peak in the mid-1970s. Bands like Swans and Sonic Youth, while considered representatives, appeared later, in the early 1980s, complicating their strict classification within the movement. "el no wave tiene su punto álgido a mitad de los 70."

**"Pure" No Wave Bands** : The "pure" No Wave sound is exemplified by bands such as Suicide and Teenage Jesus and the Jerks, which are seen as distinct from the later incorporating influences of bands like Swans. "el no wave puro de suiza y de tines y sus ande jerks, pues es. Es relativamente diferente."

**Defined by Brian Eno's No New York (1978)** : The No Wave movement is largely defined by Brian Eno's 1978 compilation album, *No New York*, which is noted as almost the only existing compilation of the genre. Swans and Sonic Youth, two major bands associated with No Wave, were founded after its release. "el único casi recopilatorio que tenemos del no wave que salía en el 78."

## curiosities
**Swans' Signing and "The Burning World" Disaster** : Swans signed with a major label for the first time after their "Love Will Tear Us Apart" cover brought them significant popularity. This move provided them with important economic resources and the opportunity to work with a professional producer. However, the resulting album, *The Burning World* (1989), was an "absolute disaster," critically panned and commercially unsuccessful, signaling a difficult period for the band. # instrument - Guitar

## curiosities
**80s Virtuosity vs. Swans' Atmospheric Sound** : In the 1980s, rock guitar sounds were characterized by their sharpness, with virtuoso guitarists playing very fast and complex scores, often utilizing pedals and pitch-shifting effects. In contrast, Michael Gira of Swans pursued a radically different approach, aiming to create a completely atmospheric guitar sound that often blurred the line between playing and noise, rather than focusing on instrumental virtuosity. "en los 80 las guitarras del rock se hicieron muy puntagudas, y otra vez con esos grandes virtuosos de la guitarra que tocaba muy rápido tenían partituras muy complejas."

**Pioneering Use of Atmosphere** : Michael Gira is credited as a possible pioneer ("father") of the atmospheric sound in rock music, creating immersive guitar soundscapes. This distinctive approach to the guitar, departing from the prevailing virtuoso style of the 1980s, is seen as a direct precursor to later genres like shoegaze and dream pop. "Yo creo que es el padre, posiblemente es el padre que inventó el sonido atmosférica con el rock."

# curiosity

**Brian Eno's No New York (1978) - Defining No Wave Compilation** : This compilation, produced by Brian Eno and released in 1978, is considered almost the sole defining compilation of the No Wave movement. Both Swans and Sonic Youth, major bands associated with the genre, were formed after its release. "el único casi recopilatorio que tenemos del no wave que salía en el 78."

**Suicide - Pure No Wave Pioneer** : Suicide is identified as a representative of the "pure" No Wave sound, differentiating their style from the later, more complex influences integrated by bands like Swans. "el no wave puro de suiza y de tines y sus ande jerks, pues es. Es relativamente diferente."

**Teenage Jesus and the Jerks - Pure No Wave Pioneer** : Teenage Jesus and the Jerks are cited as an example of a "pure" No Wave band, distinct from the broader influences that characterized bands like Swans. "el no wave puro de suiza y de tines y sus ande jerks, pues es. Es relativamente diferente."

**Metallica - Contemporary 80s Rock Contrast** : Metallica, a band making music in the mid-1980s, serves as a point of contrast to highlight the radical distinctiveness of Swans' sound upon their debut. While Metallica represented the mass-consumed rock of the era, Swans' experimental and atmospheric approach was completely different from anything else at the time. "tú piensas por ese discos de metálica que se hacía justo a mitad de los 80... y de repente escuchas a Soans y es radicalmente distinto a todo lo que había."

**Iron Maiden - Contemporary 80s Rock Contrast** : Iron Maiden, another prominent rock band of the mid-1980s, is used as a benchmark to emphasize how radically different Swans' early sound was. Their music stood out completely from the popular rock consumed massively during that period. "tú piensas en los discos de Iron Maiden y de repente escuchas a Soans y es radicalmente distinto a todo lo que había."

**Pixies - Swans' Influence** : The Pixies are listed as one of the later movements and bands directly influenced by Swans, highlighting the enduring impact of Swans' unique sound and artistic approach on subsequent rock music. "padre absoluto de movimientos posteriores como, por ejemplo, los pixis."

**Grunge - Swans' Influence** : Grunge is cited as a musical movement that owes its roots to Swans, underscoring Swans' role as an "absolute father" figure in shaping the direction of later rock genres. "padre absoluto de movimientos posteriores como, por ejemplo, los [...] el grancho."

**Shoegaze - Swans' Atmospheric Influence** : Shoegaze is mentioned as a genre, along with dream pop, whose atmospheric sound aligns much more closely with Swans' pioneering atmospheric vision than with earlier atmospheric elements found in progressive rock. This positions Swans as a foundational influence for shoegaze. "el sugays [...] encajan mucho más con la visión atmosférica que tenía suans."

**Tool - Heavy Metal Influence** : Tool is named as a heavy metal band whose sound is absolutely based on Swans' albums, demonstrating Swans' far-reaching influence beyond their immediate genres, even into hard rock and heavy metal. "bandas como túl [...] se basan todas, absolutamente. En los discos es suans."

**Dream Theater - Heavy Metal Influence** : Dream Theater, another heavy metal band, is identified as having its foundations absolutely rooted in Swans' albums, illustrating the profound and diverse impact of Swans on various rock subgenres. "bandas como [...] como Dream Theater, se basan todas, absolutamente. En los discos es suans."

**Nine Inch Nails - Industrial Rock Influence** : The thematic elements of industrial rock, exemplified by bands like Nine Inch Nails, are explicitly stated to be based on Swans' albums. This highlights Swans' critical role in defining the lyrical and sonic landscape for the industrial rock movement. "casi la temática del rock industrial como a naïnichin, se basan los discos es suans."

**Pitchfork - Modern Music Reviewer** : Pitchfork is named as one of the modern music magazines that, years after their initial poor reception, conducted "modern revisions" of Swans' early albums. These publications had originally "destroyed" the albums with their critiques. "muchas críticas que veis hoy en día de revistas como pitchfork [...] ha hecho revisiones modernas de los discos primeros de suans porque en su día los destrozaron."

**AllMusic - Modern Music Reviewer** : AllMusic is cited as another contemporary music review outlet that performed "modern revisions" of Swans' early works, recognizing their significance after having initially given them devastating reviews. "muchas críticas que veis hoy en día de revistas como [...] el music y toda esta gente [...] ha hecho revisiones modernas de los discos primeros de suans porque en su día los destrozaron."
