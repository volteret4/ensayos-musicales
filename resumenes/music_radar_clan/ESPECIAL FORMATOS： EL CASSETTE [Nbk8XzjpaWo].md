# artist - The Strokes

This technique was employed to deliberately degrade the sound quality, aiming to emulate the distinctive "cut" and "scratched" sound characteristic of punk and 1980s rock music that was often heard on cassette tapes. "uno de los procesos que hicieron para ese sonido tan característico que tiene el primer disco de los Strokes, fue pasar toda la grabación de los formatos profesionales a un espectro electromagnético de la cinta de cassette para luego hacer la grabación final."

# label - Philips

## curiosities
**Cassette Tape Invention and Patent (1964)** : Philips was the company responsible for first introducing and patenting the cassette tape in 1964. However, its initial version suffered from very poor quality and high noise, which limited its use primarily to voice recordings and dictation rather than widespread musical applications. "fue en 1964 cuando Philis presentó el cassette y lo patentó."
**Initial Music Cassette Failure (1966)** : In 1966, Philips introduced the first "musicassette" specifically intended for musical applications. Despite this, it proved to be an absolute market failure due to its high cost and persistently poor sound quality, which was not sufficient for general music consumption. "Al ser hizo algún lanzamiento pero fue un fracaso absoluto porque era bastante caro pero también su calidad era muy mala."
**Cassette Patent Liberation (1971)** : Philips eventually conceded to market pressures and, significantly influenced by Sony, decided to liberate the cassette patent in 1971. This crucial decision allowed other manufacturers, including Sony, to further develop and improve the cassette format, setting the stage for its later commercial success. "Al final Philips se rindió y en 1971 decidió liberar la patente, sobre todo para que Sony la presionaba, para que Sony pudiese manejar también en la cinta de cassette."

# label - Sony

## curiosities
**Pressure for Cassette Patent Liberation (1971)** : Sony played a significant role in pressuring Philips to release its patent for the cassette tape in 1971. This liberation was critical, as it enabled Sony to manufacture and innovate with cassette tapes, which was a prerequisite for its future developments in portable audio. "sobre todo para que Sony la presionaba, para que Sony pudiese manejar también en la cinta de cassette."
**Introduction of the Walkman (1979)** : In 1979, Sony revolutionized the music industry with the introduction of the Walkman, the first truly portable device designed for listening to cassette tapes. This invention fundamentally transformed music consumption by enabling personal, on-the-go audio experiences, marking a pivotal moment in musical culture. "el grandísimo invento que cambiaría la cultura musical para siempre fue cuando en 1979 Sony presenta el Waltman, el primera para toportátil para escuchar casets."

# instrument - Walkman

## curiosities
**Revolutionary Portable Music Player (1979)** : Introduced by Sony in 1979, the Walkman was a groundbreaking portable device designed for playing cassette tapes. It differentiated itself from earlier portable record players by being significantly smaller (only slightly larger than a cassette case) and, crucially, by enabling users to listen to music while physically in motion, such as walking or exercising. "el grandísimo invento que cambiaría la cultura musical para siempre fue cuando en 1979 Sony presenta el Waltman, el primera para toportátil para escuchar casets."
**Profound Impact on Music Culture** : The Walkman's introduction in 1979 permanently altered music culture by popularizing personal, on-the-go audio consumption. It allowed listeners to enjoy cassettes while walking, playing sports, or driving, creating a musical revolution that prioritized portability despite the cassette's inferior sound quality. "Y eso fue el cambio de la cultura musical para siempre porque entonces podías escuchar a tus cintas de casets mientras andabas, mientras hacia el porte, podías llevárselas, podías poner cintas de casets en el coche."
**Contribution to Current Cassette Resurgence** : Urban Outfitters has actively contributed to the recent resurgence of cassette popularity by choosing to sell the Walkman in its stores worldwide. This initiative, alongside the sale of cassettes, aimed to reintroduce the format to a young audience, positioning it as a "bizarre" or fetishized object rather than a serious audiophile product. "Urban Outfitters decidió poner a la venta en sus tiendas de todo el mundo, decidió retomar el guelma y retomar el cassette, y eso disparó a la demanda del cassette entre las gente joven como un objeto bizarro, un objeto de fetichismo, un poco para hacer la gracia."

# curiosity

**Initial Cassette Introduction and Quality Issues (1964)** : The cassette format was initially presented and patented by Philips in 1964. Its early versions suffered from very poor sound quality and high levels of noise, rendering it largely unsuitable for music and primarily confined to voice recording and dictation applications. "El problema es que era de muy mala calidad tenía mucho ruido y se usaba prácticamente solo para hacer grabaciones de voz y dictado."
**First Music-Applicable Cassette (1966)** : A slightly improved version of the cassette, specifically marketed as a "musicassette," was released in 1966 to target musical applications. However, despite this improvement, it remained expensive and still suffered from poor sound quality, leading to its absolute failure in the market for music. "Al ser hizo algún lanzamiento pero fue un fracaso absoluto porque era bastante caro pero también su calidad era muy mala."
**Cassette as a Tool for Music Contraband (1970s)** : During the 1970s, despite being relatively expensive, the recordable nature of cassette tapes made them invaluable for the clandestine distribution of music. In countries such as India and China, cassettes were widely utilized to introduce censored or otherwise illegally distributed music, becoming a vital instrument in musical contraband. "la gran aplicación que tuvo el cassette durante la década de los 70 fue casi de contrabando de música."
**Invention of the Modern Cassette Tape (1979)** : A significant advancement in cassette technology occurred in 1979 with the invention of the modern cassette tape. This new design incorporated a metallic coating over the plastic tape, which dramatically improved the electromagnetic process and substantially reduced noise, thereby enhancing its viability for music recording and playback. "El primero es que se inventó la cinta de cassette moderna que es básicamente la que tiene un recubrimiento metálico sobre la cinta plástica que mejora un montón todo el proceso electromagnético y redujo muchísimo el ruido."
**Golden Age of the Cassette (1979-1980s)** : The "golden age" of the cassette officially commenced in 1979, a period significantly influenced by the invention of the modern metallic-coated tape and the introduction of the Sony Walkman. This era, which spanned throughout the 1980s, saw the cassette become a dominant and revolutionary format for portable music consumption. "la época dorada del cassette nace oficialmente y además lo tenemos muy claro en 1979 porque en 1979 tenemos los dos cambios que cambiarían para siempre la cultura del cassette."
**Inherent Sound Quality Limitations of Cassettes** : Cassette tapes inherently suffer from significant sound quality limitations, most notably an enormous reduction in the audible spectrum. The format performs a substantial compression of audio waves, removing a considerable portion of the sound to fit it onto the tape, resulting in a noticeably inferior sound quality compared to vinyl records. "la cinta de cassette tiene un gran problema y es que reduce enormemente el espectro audible."
**Persistent Noise Issues in Cassettes** : Despite various technological efforts to reduce it, cassette tapes always retained a small but perceptible level of noise originating from the electromagnetic reading process. This residual noise was an inherent characteristic of the format that could never be entirely eliminated. "El ruido se reducía enormemente pero seguía teniendo un pequeño ruido de la lectura electromagnética."
**Progressive Erasure of Cassette Tapes** : A major drawback of cassette tapes is their susceptibility to progressive erasure over time. Professional quality tapes could degrade within 30-35 years, while consumer-grade home cassettes often lost their recordings in as little as 10-15 years, with durability varying depending on their quality. "Las cintas de cassette se borran con el paso del tiempo de una manera muy rápida porque ya las profesionales se borraban con 30 a 35 años, las cintas caseras de cassette se borraban a veces incluso a los 10 o 15 años."
**Playback-Induced Erasure** : The very act of playing a cassette tape contributes to its degradation, as the electromagnetic read/write head progressively erases the recorded information with repeated use. This phenomenon further compounded the format's inherent durability issues. "el cabezal electromagnético con el uso también iba borrando la cinta."
**1980s Focus on Efficiency Over Quality** : During the 1980s, the development of cassette technology was characterized by a struggle between improving sound quality and enhancing efficiency. While efficiency improved enormously, the musical quality of cassettes saw very little advancement due to inherent limitations of the format's design, physical size, and fundamental electromagnetic process. "Donde el cassette mejoró muy poco su calidad musical y mejoró enormemente su eficiencia."
**Inherent Development Limitations of Cassettes** : Unlike formats such as vinyl, which progressively improved in quality, or compact discs (CDs), which started with limitations but evolved significantly, the cassette format faced severe inherent limitations in its development beyond approximately 1986. Its small size and fundamental electromagnetic process inherently restricted how much its musical quality could evolve. "por el simple proceso electromagnético estaba muy limitada su desarrollo."
**Start of Cassette Decline (Mid-1990s)** : The decline of the cassette format is generally marked from the mid-1990s, specifically around 1996. This period coincided with drastic price reductions in compact discs (CDs) and the introduction of modern portable CD players like the Discman in 1994, which offered superior sound quality and portability. "a mitad de los 90 en 1996 yo creo que ya empezó el declive, que fue cuando empezaron a ver las drásticas reducciones de precio en todo el disco compacto y además se presentó el disman, el disman moderno que es de 1994 que se llaman el declive."
**Drastic Sales Drop by 2000** : By the year 2000, the cassette market had experienced a precipitous collapse, with cassette tapes accounting for less than 5% of all music sales. This figure vividly illustrates the rapid obsolescence of the format in the face of digital and superior physical alternatives. "en el 2000 ya menos el 5% de las ventas eran cintas de cassette."
**Recent Resurgence and Demand-Supply Imbalance** : In recent years, there has been a noticeable resurgence in cassette popularity, with demand increasing over the last two to three years. However, this demand is far outstripped by a massive increase in supply, with product offerings growing 15 to 20 times faster than demand, raising concerns about market health and sustainability. "si bien ha subido la demanda de cassettes estos últimos dos y tres años, la oferta de cassettes se ha disparado, entonces lo mejor por cada punto que crece la demanda de compra de cassettes, está subiendo 15 o 20 puntos, la oferta de producto en cinta de cassette."
**Concerns over Modern Cassette Quality and Durability** : There are significant concerns regarding the quality and long-term durability of newly manufactured cassette tapes. Given the inherently high cost of producing good quality tapes, modern cassettes sold for low prices (e.g., 8-9 euros) are likely to be of inferior quality, raising questions about their sound fidelity and whether they will remain playable in ten years. "Con toda seguridad no se están fabricando de las buenas, porque las cintas de cassette de buena calidad son muy caras, incluso hoy en día, y que te estén vendiendo por ocho o nueve euros, cintas no vas de cassette, ya te da a pensar que muy buenas de calidad no van a ser."
**Urban Outfitters' Commercial Influence on Cassette Revival** : The recent "fever" for cassettes is largely attributed to the fashion retailer Urban Outfitters. By stocking Walkmans and cassettes in its global stores, the brand deliberately targeted young consumers (16-17 years old), presenting cassettes as a quirky or "bizarre" fetish object rather than a traditional music format, significantly driving demand in this demographic. "creo que tenemos un gran protagonista, que es la marca Urban Outfitters. Urban Outfitters decidió poner a la venta en sus tiendas de todo el mundo, decidió retomar el guelma y retomar el cassette, y eso disparó a la demanda del cassette entre las gente joven como un objeto bizarro, un objeto de fetichismo, un poco para hacer la gracia."
**Urban Outfitters' Dominance in Modern Cassette Sales (2016)** : In 2016, Urban Outfitters reportedly captured the vast majority of sales for modern cassette tapes. This highlights the brand's profound influence in shaping the contemporary cassette market and its role in reintroducing the format to a new generation of consumers. "Urban Outfitters durante el año 2016 copó la grandísima parte de venta de cintas de cassette modernas."
**Profile of Modern Cassette Buyers** : The primary purchasers of new cassettes are typically young people, aged 16-17, who frequent stores like Urban Outfitters. These individuals are generally not serious music collectors or ardent music lovers, suggesting their interest in cassettes is more superficial or trend-driven rather than audiophile-focused. "el tipo de comprador... chavales jóvenes, de 16 a 16 a 17 años, que no son coleccionistas de música, que no son tampoco en la mayoría de los casos grandes amantes de música."
**Limited Collector Demand for Cassettes (Discogs Data)** : Data from music marketplaces like Discogs indicates that demand for collectible or second-hand cassettes is growing very slowly and is highly focused on specific, niche items, rather than new releases. This trend contrasts sharply with the inflated supply and fashion-driven demand for new cassettes. "si nos vamos a la parte donde si tienes ese tipo de público, por ejemplo, disco G.S., ves que la demanda de cassette de coleccionismo o la demanda de cassette de segunda mano apenas está subiendo, está subiendo pero muy de espacio y, sobre todo, focalizada en cosas muy concretas y para nada en cosas nuevas."
**Commercial Pressure vs. Collector Authenticity in Cassette Revival** : The current "return" of the cassette tape is driven more by massive commercial pressure from fashion brands like Urban Outfitters, targeting a modern, young audience with little connection to traditional music collecting, rather than by genuine demand from dedicated audiophiles or collectors. This makes the long-term viability and value of newly purchased cassettes questionable. "detrás de este enorme vuelta de la cinta de cassette hay una enorme presión comercial de una marca de moda que no tiene nada que ver con la música, que se está refriendo a un público moderno, que poco tiene que ver con la música y con el coleccionismo y con la música antigua."
