## albums
**Operation: Doomsday (1999) - Debut Solo Album** : Released in 1999, this album was revolutionary within underground hip hop in the United States. It featured MF Doom's fantastic lyrics delivered in a completely calm and reflective manner, which was not aggressive. The rhythmic bases used in the album incorporated experimental hip hop elements mixed with jazz, creating a highly praised sound. "Operation Dunsay es un disco revolucionario dentro del underground del hip hop y en Estados Unidos."
**Madvillainy (2004) - Collaborative Masterpiece** : This album, a supergroup project between MF Doom and Madlib under the name Madvillain, was released in 2004. It is considered essential listening, a piece of music history, and something unique that leaves the hip hop world speechless. The album's creation involved MF Doom and Madlib working remotely before they had ever met, driven by mutual professional respect. It flowed rapidly during its initial development but was leaked online when almost finished, spreading virally and becoming a cult classic before its official release. The frustration from the leak led them to abandon the project for over a year and a half before finally reuniting to complete and release it, cementing its status as a foundational hip hop record, comparable in impact to the debut albums of Nas or Wu-Tang Clan. "Este disco sí que vale la pena para todo el mundo, es historia de la música."
**Madvillainy (2004) - Instrumental Version** : The album Madvillainy is so musically significant that it features its own instrumental version, showcasing the strength of Madlib's production independently of MF Doom's vocals. Even within the final version of the album, there are many instrumental parts that stand out and are considered excellent on their own. "el disco Mad Villani está importante que tiene su versión instrumental sin las canciones de Merdum solo en la parte instrumental porque incluso solo instrumentalmente se discos son referentes."

## curiosities
**Genius and Influence** : MF Doom is regarded as one of the great geniuses and best artists in rap and hip hop history. His work is often recommended as an excellent entry point for those who may not typically enjoy rap music. "uno de los grandes genios del Kaviar, de uno de los mejores artistas del rap que es Mr. Doom MF Doom."
**Non-Aggressive Flow** : Unlike many hip hop artists who use aggressive shouting, yelling, and swear words, MF Doom stands out for his lack of aggression. His flow is calm and measured, avoiding harsh endings to verses, often making him sound like he is reciting poetry rather than rapping, which appeals to a broader audience. "es un artista que no tiene esa agresividad, que a veces tienen muchos artistas de hip hop que a mucha gente que no le gusta el rap letir para atrás, estos artistas que gritan, que chican, que dicen tacos que son agresivos."
**Dual Musical Style** : MF Doom effectively bridges the gap between underground, experimental hip hop and more accessible forms, creating a unique sound that blends these often disconnected worlds. "es un exo muy interesante entre el hip hop underground, más de las profundidades o el hip hop más experimental y por el otro lado el hip hop más accesible."
**English-Peruvian Origin and International Life** : MF Doom was an artist and producer of English-Peruvian origin. He spent much of his life in the United States and, at the time of recording, was living between the United Kingdom and the United States. "un artista de hip hop y productor de origen inglesper, que ha vivido todas en el estado sonidos, ahora vuelve a estar a camino entre el reino o unido y el estado."
**Extreme Privacy and the Masked Persona** : MF Doom was incredibly secretive about his personal life, giving very few interviews where he would only discuss his music, making him a "ghost" in the public eye. He consistently wore a distinctive mask, inspired by Magneto and comic book villains, which meant he was rarely seen physically without it, though he occasionally appeared unmasked in music videos or photos. "una persona increíblemente celoso de su vida y de su intimidad, sabemos muy poco de él, da muy pocas entrevistas, hay poquísimas entrevistas suyas en las que solo habla de su música."
**Early Career and Resurgence** : In his early career, MF Doom was part of a small, unsuccessful hip hop band with his brother. He later experienced a period of indigence in his twenties, living as a homeless person, before resurfacing as a major artist in underground hip hop under the name MF Doom. "en los inicios tiene una pequeña banda de hip hop con su hermano y con alguien más que no tiene mucho éxito. Después acaba prácticamente viviendo en la indigencia, acaba tirado viviendo como un indigente sin casa y ahí cuando tiene 20 pico años resucita como uno de los artistas, uno de los grandes artistas del hip hop underground, ya con el nombre de MFDUM."
**Constant Name Changes and Collaborative Nature** : Throughout his career, MF Doom constantly changed his stage name, though most names contained "Doom" (e.g., Mr. Fud, Doom) or "Villain" (e.g., the villain). He was known for his extensive collaborations, with most of his albums being joint projects, and he worked with a wide range of artists both directly and indirectly. "ha cambiado de nombre, toda su carrera ha estado constantemente cambiando de nombre, casi todos tienen o el denominador comundum, MFDUM, MrFud, todo comdum o convilian."
**Dual Talent as Rapper and Producer** : MF Doom's comprehensive artistry stemmed from his equally strong abilities as both a rapper and a musical producer. "uno de las cosas que hace increíblemente completo a MFDUM es que es tan buen rapero como tan buen productor musical de hip hop."
**Jazz and Soul Influences in Production** : His production style, particularly in rhythmic bases, drew heavily from jazz and soul influences. He directly incorporated rhythmic bases and musical structures from jazz, distinguishing his approach from artists like Wu-Tang Clan who might use deconstructed samples. This made his style similar to early breakbeat DJs who were inspired by jazz, like Eran Katkemi and DJ Fút. "En la parte de los basses rímpicas, en la parte de productor de música, tiene muchas influencias del jazz, del soul, pero a lo mejor no tanto como podían tener a mejor esbo tan clan que tenían esos trozos de construido, sino que realmente usaba ser rímpicas y estructuras musicales sacadas del jazz."
**Revolutionary Poetic and Ironic Lyrical Style** : As a rapper, MF Doom was revolutionary due to his distinctive writing style. He refrained from using excessive explicit language, instead preferring a more measured approach that heavily utilized irony and double meanings, often embedding two or three meanings within a single verse. His writing and flow were so unique that he was often compared to a poet. "su forma de escribir es muy característica. No dice tantísimas palabras más sonantes ni tantísimos tacos, es una persona en eso mucho más pausada, que además juega muchísimo con las ironías, con los doble sentidos, a veces sus versos tienen dos y tres significados a la vez y no lo oculta con lo cual estás como haciendo tres lecturas distintas de la misma lectura."
**Innovative Use of Spoken Word Samples as Lyrics** : A hallmark of MF Doom's career was his unique method of incorporating fragments of telephone recordings, movie dialogues, or news reports directly into his lyrics. Unlike other hip hop artists who might sample spoken words for musical elements, MF Doom used these audio clips as the actual lyrics, presenting them as if the sampled voices were singing the song, making the fragments the central argument of the track. "coge fragmentos de grabaciones telefónicas o de películas o de diálogos que encuentra y lo usa como letra y los pone y lo reproduce como casi como si fueran el cantante de la canción."
**Short Song Format Philosophy** : MF Doom advocated for a philosophy in his music that emphasizes conciseness: "what you can say in two minutes, don't say in eight." This approach resulted in albums like Madvillainy featuring over 20 incredibly short songs, each with clear arguments and concise lyrics, in stark contrast to the longer, verse-heavy tracks common in rap. "la filosofía que siempre ha defendido el mezón es que lo que puedas decir en dos minutos no lo digas en ocho."
**Controversy Over Live Performances and Imposters** : Throughout his career, MF Doom was highly controversial regarding his live appearances, giving very few concerts and being highly reclusive on stage, especially since signing with Lex Records. A significant point of contention was the frequent discovery that imposters, wearing his signature mask, would perform in his place at many concerts. Initially, he would claim it was him, perhaps thinner or not clearly visible, but eventually admitted to using stand-ins, leading to widespread anger and frustration among fans, with some imposters even being booed off stage. "una cosa que siempre ha generado muchísima polémica de mezón es que ha habido muchos conciertos bastantes conciertos en los que se ha descubierto que el que estaba saliendo a cantar no era el propio mezón sino que era otro tío y como le ponía la máscara de magneto esta que se pone todo el día nadie sabía que encoño era."
**Performance Philosophy and Stage Fright** : MF Doom addressed the controversy of using imposters by presenting a philosophical argument about the nature of musical performance. He asserted that the artist who composed, worked on, and directed the performance is the true performer, and his physical presence on stage is irrelevant because audiences come to hear the music and material, not to see him physically. He compared it to attending "The Marriage of Figaro" without expecting to see Mozart. Over time, he admitted that his actions were partly due to his significant fear of stages, which he believed would be understandable if properly communicated to the audience. "él está diciendo que realmente es el artista que ha compuesto que ha trabajado y que ha dirigido y que ha hecho el ha hecho la performance del directo y que él salga o no salga no es relevante porque la gente va a escuchar su música va a escuchar su material va a escuchar su gracio y no va a escucharla a él."

**Talented Producer and Multi-instrumentalist** : Madlib is recognized as an exceptionally talented musical producer, musician, and multi-instrumentalist, widely respected for his skill in creating rhythmic bases. "Madlib es un productor musical, es un músico, un multistro mentalista que ha colaborado con muchísima gente y que es especialmente reconocido por lo bueno que es haciendo basarrismicas."
**Key Collaborations and Influence** : He played a crucial role in significant parts of J Dilla's career and also worked with Kanye West. Within hip hop, he is highly regarded, often compared to Flying Lotus, but noted for being more underground. "Madlib ha hecho partes muy importantes de la carrera de Jay-Dee La, por ejemplo, a trabajo de Bonca Ni West, es un tío muy reconocido dentro del hip hop, es como un flying Lotus, más o menos, pero más underground."
**Madvillainy Production Approach** : For the album Madvillainy, Madlib contributed impressive work, incorporating world music influences after traveling to Brazil during a break. He used classic breakbeat techniques and fragments from existing records. Notably, despite working on an album released in 2004, he employed completely classic manual sampling methods with a traditional sampler, resulting in slight, intentional desynchronization of samples, reminiscent of 1980s old-school hip hop. "Mad Libá hace un trabajo impresionante. Y cambia en el último momento porque Mad Libre en un momento de pausa se va viaja Brasil, viaja por el mundo y entonces reinterpreta todas las influencias que tenían musicales para convertir al disco con muchísimas influencias de discos al mundo."

# instrument - Sampler

## curiosities
**Manual, Old-School Sampling** : For the album Madvillainy, Madlib utilized classical, manual sampling methods with a traditional sampler. This technique, performed by hand, resulted in a slight desynchronization of samples, creating a sound reminiscent of 1980s old-school hip hop where sampling was done manually rather than digitally. "Mad Lib usa metodos completamente clásicos para hacer el sampling. Aunque estamos en el 2004 Mad Lib hace todo el proceso de sampling manual con un asampleador clásico, con lo cual a veces te recuerda muchísimo el sampling de la vieja escuela que podíamos tener en discos de los 80 en los que todo el sampling nos hacía informáticamente sino que se hacía pulsando manualmente con los dedos."

# curiosity

**Artist Performance Philosophy and Ethics** : MF Doom's controversial use of imposters at live concerts sparked an interesting reflection on the world of musical performance: to what extent should audiences detach their admiration for an artist from their admiration for the work itself, and how relevant is the artist's physical presence? While MF Doom argued that the true artist is the one who composed and directed the work, and physical presence is secondary, the ethical question remains whether audiences have the right to know who they are seeing perform, especially given his admitted fear of stages. "Es una reflexión muy interesante la que la hace hasta que punto deberíamos ir concierto a disfrutar de su música y no a disfrutar de ver físicamente una persona."
