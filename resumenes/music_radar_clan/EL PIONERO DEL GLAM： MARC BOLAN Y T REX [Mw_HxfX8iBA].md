## albums
**Ziggy Stardust - Inspiration** : This album, a significant work by David Bowie, was notably inspired by T-Rex's *Electric Warrior*, released in 1971. The influence extended to various aspects, including Marc Bolan's stage attitude, aesthetic, and numerous musical pieces created by T-Rex. "el álbum de sigue esta raza de David Bowie está inspirado en un álbum anterior que es el electric warrior que había hecho T-Rex en 1971."
**Early Albums (Late 1960s) - Acoustic Folk** : David Bowie's initial recordings were primarily acoustic folk, characterized by experimental and psychedelic elements. These early works recalled the sound of artists like Neil Young, Simon & Garfunkel, and Bob Dylan, embodying the late 1960s folk style. They achieved only moderate success, likely due to the market's saturation with folk music at the time. "Los primeros discos de David Bowie, aunque son muy interesantes, son principalmente discos acústicos de Folk, un tanto experimental, un tanto psicodélico."

## curiosities
**Glam Rock Pioneer Misconception** : Contrary to popular belief, David Bowie was not the pioneer of glam music. He was not the first individual to bridge the gap between late 1960s UK music and the 1970s glam movement, nor was *Ziggy Stardust* his first glam album. "David Bowie no fue el pionero de la música glam, él no fue esa primera persona, no fue la persona que conecta la música del reino nido que había en el reino nido a finales de los años 60, con lo que fue el movimiento de la música glam de los años 70."
**Maximum Expression of 70s White Movement** : David Bowie is recognized as having been the "maximum expression" of the "white movement" of the 1970s, serving as its great artist. "posiblemente fue la máxima expresión del movimiento blanco de los años 70, el fue el gran artista."
**Collaboration with Tony Visconti** : Throughout most of his career, David Bowie worked closely with producer Tony Visconti. However, Visconti did not produce Bowie's glam music albums, specifically *Ziggy Stardust*, *Aladdin Sane*, or *Pin Ups*. During this period, Visconti was actively involved in producing albums for Marc Bolan's band T-Rex, contributing to the birth of glam music from another angle. "David Bowie trabajó casi toda su vida con un productor que se llama Tony Biz Conti."
**Inspiration from Marc Bolan** : Bowie was significantly inspired by Marc Bolan of T-Rex. Bolan's stage attitude, aesthetic, and musical compositions deeply influenced Bowie's work in the 1970s, making him a central figure of inspiration. "Siempre se recuerda como la persona que inspiraba David Bowie."
**Androgynous Style** : David Bowie's distinctive androgynous dressing style, featuring colors and leopard prints, mirrored and was influenced by Marc Bolan's own eccentric stage attire and behavior. "Marvolan con ese vestimento tanto andróginal, con colores, con leopardos."

# artist - T-Rex

## members

## genres

## albums
**A Beard of Stars (1970) - Transition to Electric Sound** : This album achieved significant success, surpassing previous works. While still heavily linked to acoustic folk music, it began incorporating electric guitar sounds and psychedelic influences. It is not considered a glam or proto-glam album but marked an important evolution, moving away from the saturation of the folk music scene and satisfying a public eager for new sounds. "en 1970 sacan el álbum Averdo Festares, que tienen muchísimo más éxito que el anterior."
**T-Rex (1970) - Reinvention and Proto-Glam** : Released only a few months after *A Beard of Stars*, this self-titled album represented a complete reinvention of the band and introduced many elements of glam music. Some consider it the album that marks the beginning of glam, while others see it as an intermediate step connecting earlier music with the glam era. It had even greater success than its predecessor. "pocos meses después, Marvolan contires sacan un segundo álbum también en 1970, que es el álbum homónimo de T-Rex."
**Electric Warrior (1971) - Glam Rock Landmark** : This album is considered a complete glam music album and is frequently cited as officially inaugurating the "golden age" of glam, preceding David Bowie's *Ziggy Stardust* which it heavily inspired. It fully embraced glam clichés, including Marc Bolan's iconic persona, vibrant clothing, irreverent behavior, and provocative lyrics. The album features characteristic glam ballads and retains psychedelic guitar influences. Its biggest hit, "Get It On," is on this record. It marked a significant turning point in UK music, bridging the 60s and 70s sounds. "en 1971, saca una auténtica obra maestra que sería el electric warrior. Sí que el electric warrior ya son disco completamente de música glam."

## songs
**Get It On (1971)** : This song, featured on the *Electric Warrior* album, became T-Rex's biggest hit. It is a prime example of the glam rock sound that defined the album and the era. "Aquí hay uno de los grandes éxitos, el éxito más grande que tu poterex, esta en este disco que es Getty-None."

## curiosities
**Early Career - Acoustic Folk** : Marc Bolan, with his band T-Rex, began in the late 1960s with a sound far removed from glam music. Their early records were primarily acoustic folk, described as experimental and psychedelic, drawing comparisons to Neil Young, Simon & Garfunkel, and Bob Dylan. This phase had moderate success, likely due to the market's oversaturation with folk music. "Mar Bolanc, con su banda de David Bowie, comienza a finales de los años 60 y sus inicios no tienen nada que ver con la música glam."
**Reinvention in 1970** : In 1970, the band T-Rex underwent a significant reinvention, with Marc Bolan restructuring the group and embracing new sounds. This period saw Bolan incorporating influences from psychedelic music, particularly from eccentric figures like Syd Barrett, whose image and theatrical stage presence inspired Bolan's evolution. "Empieza a correr en 1970, que la banda Tirex prácticamente se reinventa, se reconstruye."
**Eccentric Persona and Androgynous Style (1970)** : Around the release of their self-titled album in 1970, Marc Bolan's public persona grew increasingly eccentric. His behavior and dressing style, featuring an androgynous look with colors and leopard prints, became reminiscent of David Bowie's later 1970s aesthetic. He also shifted from intellectual folk lyrics to more loving, sexually explicit, romantic, and melancholic themes, which became hallmarks of glam music. "Ya fuera de la música, Marvolan se convierte cada vez en una persona más excéntrica."
**"Missing Link" in Music Evolution** : T-Rex and Marc Bolan are considered the crucial "missing link" that perfectly connects the late 1960s psychedelic music (represented by early Pink Floyd) with the 1970s glam movement. Their albums, especially the three released around 1971, are seen as marking this perfect transition. "Si hay un nombre que conecta lo que es el antes y el después de la música glam de una manera magistral, es Mar Bolancos o banda T-Rex."
**Discovery by Tony Visconti** : Tony Visconti, a renowned producer, is credited with discovering Marc Bolan. Visconti played a significant role in producing T-Rex's albums during the period he was not working on David Bowie's glam records, thus actively participating in the birth of glam music. "Prácticamente, Tony Biz Conti es la persona que descubra Mar Bolanc."
**Inspiration for Future Rockers** : Marc Bolan and T-Rex, particularly through *Electric Warrior*, became a massive inspiration for numerous artists from the late 1970s onwards. He influenced figures like Rod Stewart, Kiss, Def Leppard, and the New York Dolls, making it "very difficult to understand late 70s and 80s rock" artistically and musically without their contribution. "Marvolan como personaje fue la gran inspiración, para gente como rock de estuar, para quís, para deflepar de los New York Dolls."
**Later Career and Tragic Death** : T-Rex continued releasing glam albums throughout 1972, 1973, and 1975. However, their popularity declined due to Marc Bolan's personal situation and issues within the band. Bolan later recovered and released more successful albums towards the end of his life. Tragically, he died around the age of 30 in a traffic accident, leaving the rock world to wonder what more he could have achieved as a "absolute father of glam music" and a major influence on subsequent rock generations. "Después, Marvolan se recuperaría y hacia el final de su vida sacaría algunos iscos que lo harían resodida. Pero la mala suerte es que cuando tenía más o menos unos 30 años, acababa muriendo por un accidente de tráfico."
**Creator of Glam Music** : Marc Bolan is widely regarded as one of the great pioneers of glam music, with the assertion that he "possibly the name with a capital letter, who created glam music." "uno de los grandes pioneros en la música glam, a posiblemente el nombre con Magiuscula, que creo la música glam."

# genre - Glam Rock

## curiosities
**Origin and Pioneers** : Glam music was not pioneered by David Bowie, who was merely its greatest exponent in the 1970s white movement. Instead, Marc Bolan of T-Rex is identified as the person who perfectly connected the late 1960s psychedelic era with the 1970s glam movement, making him arguably the genre's true originator. "David Bowie no fue el pionero de la música glam, él no fue esa primera persona, no fue la persona que conecta la música del reino nido que había en el reino nido a finales de los años 60, con lo que fue el movimiento de la música glam de los años 70."
**Musical Characteristics** : Glam rock was musically simpler and more spontaneous than the elaborate psychedelic and progressive rock of the late 1960s. Recordings were less polished, prioritizing spontaneity. Musically, it incorporated remnants of psychedelic guitar (distorted, eccentric), folk music influences (melancholic ballads), and the love for simple, catchy melodies from early pop-rock like The Beatles. "era musicalmente más sencillo, era muy espontáneo, las grabaciones no eran lo más pulido del mundo, primaba muchísimo lo que era la espontanidad."
**Performance and Aesthetic** : A defining characteristic of glam was the artists' stage behavior, which constituted "almost 90% of the show." This included eccentric, irreverent, explicit, and provocative conduct, coupled with an androgynous, colorful, often leopard-print aesthetic. Lyrics typically shifted from intellectual themes to more loving, sexually explicit, romantic, and melancholic ballads. "el comportamiento de los artistas en el escenario era casi el 90% del espectáculo."
**Influences and Complexity** : Despite its perceived simplicity, glam music was more complex than often assumed, amalgamating numerous musical influences and movements from the UK during the 1960s. These included psychedelic music, folk, early pop-rock, and the flamboyant personalities of rock legends. "la música glamera mucho más compleja de lo que muchas veces parece."
**Golden Age Inauguration** : T-Rex's *Electric Warrior* (1971) is widely considered the album that officially inaugurated the "golden age" of glam music. "el electric warrior es el álbum que inaugura oficialmente la etapa adorada de la música glam."
**Global Reach** : The glam movement was extensive, featuring artists not only in the United Kingdom but also in the United States and even Japan, demonstrating its widespread international appeal. "el movimiento de la Llan tuvo artistas por todo el mundo. Y fue mucho más extenso de lo que muchas veces parece."

# genre - Psychedelic Rock

## curiosities
**Late 1960s UK Dominance** : In the late 1960s, psychedelic rock, alongside progressive music, heavily dominated the music scene in the United Kingdom. This genre was characterized by its highly elaborate, worked, and perfectionist musical approach. "la música en el reino nido está dominada por el roxico delico y la música progresiva, era música muy recargada, muy trabajada, muy perfeccionista."
**Influence on Glam** : The remnants of psychedelic music, particularly its distorted and eccentric guitar sounds (exemplified by Syd Barrett), were a key influence on the development of glam rock. "En gran medida se basaban en los restos en la música psicodélica, de gente como podía ser sin barred de la quereda, esa guitarra tan distorsionada y a veces es un tanto excéntrica."

# genre - Progressive Rock

## curiosities
**Late 1960s UK Dominance** : Progressive music, along with psychedelic rock, dominated the UK music scene in the late 1960s. This genre was known for its highly elaborate, meticulously crafted, and perfectionist musical style, often featuring artists who preferred discretion. "la música en el reino nido está dominada por el roxico delico y la música progresiva, era música muy recargada, muy trabajada, muy perfeccionista."
**Contrast with Glam** : Progressive rock's characteristic emphasis on musical perfectionism and artists' discretion (e.g., King Crimson) stood in stark contrast to the glam movement, which prioritized spontaneity and flamboyant stage performance. "Y eso contrasta muchísimo con el glam, que era musicalmente más sencillo."

# genre - Folk

## curiosities
**Late 1960s Market Saturation** : Folk music, which began its rise in the early 1960s, experienced absolute market saturation by the end of the decade, making it challenging for new artists like early David Bowie and T-Rex to achieve significant success within the genre. "a finales de los años 60 había una absoluta saturación en el mercado de música folk."
**Influence on Glam** : Folk music, particularly its melancholic ballads, provided significant influences for glam rock. These balled themes became a cherished element within the glam sound, reminiscent of 1960s folk. "Teniendo influencias muchas influencias de la música folk, esas baladas que tanto gustaban dentro de la música glam, ese toque melancólico que tienen muchas veces algunos discos que recuerdan a veces a lo que era el foc de los años 60."
