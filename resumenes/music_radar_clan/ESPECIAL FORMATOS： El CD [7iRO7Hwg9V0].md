# artist - Herbert von Karajan

## curiosities

# genre - Classical Music

## curiosities
**Early Adoption of Compact Disc (1981-1985)** : Classical music aficionados and orchestra conductors, notably Herbert von Karajan, quickly embraced the Compact Disc in the early 1980s. This rapid adoption was due to the CD's ability to overcome inherent limitations of vinyl records, such as channel contamination and intrinsic noise, which significantly affected the clarity and fidelity of classical recordings. "Los grandes aficionados y directores Orquesta de la música clásica no eran unos grandes defensores del disco de vinilo."
**Discontent with Vinyl Records** : Classical music enthusiasts and conductors were not strong advocates of vinyl records despite their wide frequency range. They found vinyl problematic due to issues like channel contamination, intrinsic noise, and complex processes that compromised the clarity of classical music. "Eran grandes enemigos de un gran desproblemas que tenía el disco de vinilo, sobre todo en la contaminación de canales, son el ruido intrínseco, en ciertos procesos un poco complejos, que afectaban bastante a la nicidez de la música clásica."

# label - Philips

## curiosities
**Co-developer of the Compact Disc (1970s)** : Philips was a key titan in audiovisual culture during the 1970s, working jointly with Sony to develop the technology for the Compact Disc. Their collaboration focused on creating a new laser-reading system, laying the foundation for the digital audio format. "Se desarrolló prácticamente a medias entre Philips y Sony."
**Rigorous Cassette Patent Impact** : Philips' rigid patent system for the audio cassette significantly slowed its evolution and almost led to its failure. This experience influenced Sony and Philips' approach to the Compact Disc, leading them to adopt a more open licensing system to ensure widespread industry adoption. "La rigorosa patente que tenía Philips del sistema de cassette."

# label - Sony

## curiosities
**Co-developer of the Compact Disc (1970s)** : Sony, alongside Philips, played a pivotal role in the joint development of Compact Disc technology during the 1970s. This collaboration by two major audiovisual industry players led to the creation of a new laser-reading system that became the basis for the CD. "Se desarrolló prácticamente a medias entre Philips y Sony."
**Commercial Launch of the Compact Disc (1980)** : Sony and Philips jointly presented the new music reproduction system, the Compact Disc, commercially in 1980. They introduced it with a complex licensing system designed to allow any company to utilize the technology and develop CD systems. "El nacimiento comercial del disco compacto es en 1980 cuando Sony y Philips a la vez presentan este nuevo sistema de reproducción de música."
**Strategic Licensing for CD Standardization** : Learning from Philips' slow-evolving cassette patent and the video format wars (VHS vs. Beta), Sony and Philips joined forces to facilitate easy development for other companies. This strategy aimed to establish the Compact Disc as an industry standard quickly. "Sony y Philips decidieron sumar fuerzas para desarrollar de manera conjunta y sobre todo facilitar un montón el desarrollo a todas las otras empresas para convertir el disco compacto en un estandar de la industria."
**Introduction of the Discman (1994)** : Sony introduced the Discman in 1994, which was the Compact Disc's equivalent of the Walkman. This portable player is credited with kickstarting the official takeoff of the CD format, making it more accessible and popular. "El despegar oficial del disco compacto en 1994 cuando Sony presenta el dismin, el equivalente del Wormung pero para el disco compacto."

# instrument - Cassette

## curiosities
**Slow Evolution Due to Patent Issues** : The audio cassette experienced a very slow evolution and nearly failed due to Philips' rigorous patent on the system. This served as a cautionary tale for Sony and Philips when designing the licensing model for the Compact Disc. "una de las razones porque el cassette evolucionó muy despacio y casi fracaso fue precisamente por la rigorosa patente que tenía Philips del sistema de cassette."
**Rapid Growth Post-Walkman** : The cassette format saw an almost explosive development after the introduction of the Walkman. This contrasted with the Compact Disc's initially slow and progressive evolution. "El cassette que tuvo un desarrollo prácticamente explosivo después del nacimiento del Wormung."
**Limited Collector Value for Singles** : Unlike Compact Disc singles, cassette singles never gained significant collector's value. This highlights a difference in how the physical formats' rarities are perceived and valued by collectors. "en el caset nunca lo tuvieron [un gran valor los singles]."
**Lack of Professional Promotional Material** : Cassettes did not feature the same abundance of professional promotional material that was never released commercially, a factor that makes 1990s promotional CDs particularly valuable to collectors. "No pasaba con el caset y es que hubo un montón de material promocional que se hizo en disco compacto que no llegó a salir a la venta pero que se hizo de manera profesionalizada."

# instrument - CD-ROM

## curiosities
**Post-Audio CD Development (1984)** : The CD-ROM, a Compact Disc designed for data storage applications in computers, was introduced in 1984. This was almost four years after the commercial launch of the audio Compact Disc, clarifying that the music CD was not an inheritance of the CD-ROM system. "En 1984 se presentó el CD-ROM o el disco compacto con aplicaciones de almacenamiento de datos, que es casi 4 años posterior al disco compacto como formato de música."
**Not the Origin of Digital Audio CD** : Contrary to common belief, the audio Compact Disc did not become digital because it inherited the data storage system of the CD-ROM. The audio CD was developed specifically for music reproduction, and the CD-ROM for computers came later. "El disco compacto no es digital porque venga heredado del sistema de almacenamiento de datos del CD-ROM porque el CD-ROM de información de los ordenadores es posterior al disco compacto de música."

# curiosity

**Early Digital Formats Pre-CD** : Before the advent of the Compact Disc, other digital formats like Digital Audio Tape (DAT) already existed, demonstrating that the CD was not the first digital audio medium. "Incluso antes del disco compacto ya teníamos algún tipo de formato digitales como el famoso dad digital audio tape."
**CD Technology Development (1970s)** : The technology for the Compact Disc was extensively developed during the 1970s, making it a much older concept than its popular recognition in the 1990s suggests. Philips and Sony collaborated closely on this, pioneering a new laser-reading system. "Es durante los 70 en el que se desarrolla toda la tecnología del disco compacto."
**CD as an Evolution of LaserDisc** : The Compact Disc is a direct evolution of the LaserDisc, which was a significant failure in the audiovisual market for films. Extensive experiments were conducted in the 1970s to refine the technology from the LaserDisc into the CD. "El disco compacto realmente es una evolución del gran fallido laser disc que se había intentado importar para la cultura audiovisual para las películas y había fracasado."
**Early CD Prototypes (20cm, Analog)** : During its developmental phase in the 1970s, early experiments for the Compact Disc included tests with 20-centimeter discs, almost the size of a vinyl record. Curiously, many initial trials were conducted with analog systems, as the original idea was for the CD to be analog, not digital. "Se hicieron pruebas con un disco compacto de 20 centímetros que era casi del tamaño de un vinilo también se hicieron muchas pruebas antes de sistemas analógicos."
**CD's Primary Technological Advance: Error Correction** : The key technological innovation sought with the Compact Disc was not initially its digital nature, but an advanced error correction system. This system allowed the disc to be read at a much higher speed than playback, providing time for software to process and correct issues like skips or scratches before they became audible. "El gran avance tecnológico que se quería traer con el disco compacto era que tú podías tener un sistema en el que la lectura era mucho más rápida que la reproducción... un sistema de corrección de errores."
**IBM's Influence on Digital Culture** : The evolution of the Compact Disc eventually led it to become a digital system, a transition influenced by the nascent digital culture fostered by IBM. This shift moved the CD away from its initial analog conception. "Nació la cultura digital gracias a IBM, entonces finalmente se acabó mudando a un sistema digital."
**CD's Original Purpose: Music Playback** : The Compact Disc was originally conceived and designed for reproducing music. Its later applications in computing, such as the CD-ROM, were secondary developments. "El disco compacto nació para reproducir música y más adelante tuvo aplicaciones en la informática."
**CD Commercial Launch and Standardization Strategy (1980)** : The Compact Disc was commercially launched in 1980 by Sony and Philips, who presented it with a unique, open licensing system. This strategy was a direct response to past failures, such as Philips' restrictive cassette patent and the video format wars (VHS vs. Beta), aiming to swiftly establish the CD as an industry standard by facilitating development for other companies. "Sony y Philips decidieron sumar fuerzas para desarrollar de manera conjunta y sobre todo facilitar un montón el desarrollo a todas las otras empresas para convertir el disco compacto en un estandar de la industria."
**First Official CD Recordings (1981)** : Just months after its commercial presentation in 1980, the first official Compact Disc recordings were made in 1981, at the Salzburg Classical Music Festival. This rapid initial adoption highlighted the format's potential for high-fidelity audio. "Unos meses después de su presentación en 1981 ya tuvimos las primeras grabaciones oficiales en disco compacto."
**CD's Slow Initial Market Penetration** : The Compact Disc experienced a slow and progressive market development, unlike the rapid, almost explosive growth seen with the cassette after the Walkman's introduction. This was particularly true for pop music, where adoption lagged behind classical music. "Tuvo un desarrollo muy lento y muy progresivo."
**Early Challenges to CD Adoption: Cost and Quality** : The slow initial takeoff of the Compact Disc was primarily due to two factors: the exorbitant cost of both the discs themselves and the CD players, and the fact that the early audio quality was perceived as notably inferior to vinyl records. Many analog recording processes at the time did not adapt well to the new digital format, resulting in a less than ideal final sound. "Los discos compactos eran carísimos y no solo el disco compacto sino que los reproductores de disco compacto eran muy muy costosos... la calidad de audio era sensiblemente inferior a la de un disco de Benilo."
**Three Factors for CD's Market Takeoff (1994-1996)** : The Compact Disc's "official takeoff" and its establishment as the new music standard occurred between 1994 and 1996, driven by three major changes: the launch of Sony's Discman in 1994; a significant reduction in the cost and price of CDs during the mid-90s; and the consolidation of digital recording and mastering systems in the early to mid-90s, making it easier to transfer music to CD than to analog formats. "Se hacen tres grandes cambios que asentaron el disco compacto como el nuevo estandar de la música."
**CD Sales Peak and the "Hecatomb" (2000-2001)** : The demand for Compact Discs exploded between 1995 and 1996, leading to the year 2000 becoming the historical peak for CD sales. However, this same period marked the beginning of the "hecatomb" for the format, with sales starting a sharp decline in 2001. The speaker suggests the "beginning of the end" for the CD truly started in 1997. "El 2000 fue el año que más discos compactos se vendieron en toda la historia... en el 2001 se disparaba la ECATOMBE del disco compacto, yo creo que el principio del fin del disco compacto había empezado en 1997."
**Major Blows to CD Sales (2000-2001)** : The "hecatomb" for Compact Disc sales was accelerated by two major events in 2000-2001: the launch of Napster in 2000 as the first P2P file-sharing system, enabling widespread music downloads; and Steve Jobs' presentation of the iPod on October 23, 2001, which revolutionized user-friendly digital music management. "En el 2000 salió Nuster... Y luego fue el grandísimo 23 de octubre del 2001 cuando Steve Jobs presentó el iPod."
**CD Sales Decline Trajectory (2001-2012)** : Following the events of 2001, Compact Disc sales experienced a precipitous fall. This decline continued uninterrupted until approximately 2012, after which sales stabilized, albeit at unsustainably low levels today. "En el 2001 las ventas empezaron a caer en picado y a día de hoy están establez, pero hasta prácticamente el 2012 no han parado de bajar."
**Value of Early CDs vs. 90s Releases for Collectors** : Early Compact Disc recordings from the 1980s and early 1990s are generally not expected to gain significant collector's value. This is because music from this period was primarily released on vinyl or cassette, with the CD being a secondary format often with suboptimal audio quality. In contrast, releases from the mid-to-late 1990s—the "golden age" of the CD—especially those where the CD was the primary distribution method, are predicted to accrue substantial value over time. "Las primeras grabaciones de discos compactos durante los 80 y yo casi incluso durante primeros de los 90 no creo que van a tener nunca un gran valor... Los años 90, la mitad de los 90 y final de los 90, que fue la gran época dorada del disco compacto, yo creo que ahí si empezaremos a ver piezas que van a ir cogiendo valor con los años."
**Rising Collector Value of CD Singles (1990s)** : Compact Disc singles, particularly those from the 1990s, are rapidly increasing in collector's value. This phenomenon is attributed to small print runs and initially low demand, leading to many copies being damaged or discarded. Unlike cassette singles or 7-inch vinyl singles, CD singles are becoming highly sought-after rarities. "En el disco compacto sí se empieza a dar que los singles bien porque las tiradas eran pequeñas, bien porque había muy poca demanda y la mayoría se han destrozado, empieza a ver singles sobre todo de los 90 en disco compacto que empiezan a tener mucho valor."
**Collector Value of Promotional CDs (1990s)** : Promotional material released on Compact Disc during the 1990s, which was professionally produced but never made available for commercial sale, is acquiring significant collector's value. This trend is distinct from cassette and vinyl formats, where promotional items did not garner as much demand. "Hubo un montón de material promocional que se hizo en disco compacto que no llegó a salir a la venta pero que se hizo de manera profesionalizada... Todas estas piezas promocionales en disco compacto durante los 90, están empezando a coger mucha demanda dentro del coleccionismo."
**Future Resurgence of the CD Market** : While a major resurgence of the Compact Disc market may still be years away, there are indicators of a potential recovery. Second-hand CD prices, after reaching their lowest point a few years ago, are now beginning to rise. This potential comeback is driven by the growing collector interest in 90s primary releases, CD singles, and promotional materials. "El gran resurgir del disco compacto todavía se va a hacer esperar y tardar a unos cuantos años, si que es verdad que incluso el disco compacto de segunda mano ya hace unos años llegó a su punto más bajo y ahora está empezando a volver a subir."
