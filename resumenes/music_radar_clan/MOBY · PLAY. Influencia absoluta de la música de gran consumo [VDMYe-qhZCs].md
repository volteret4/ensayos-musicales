## albums
**Everything's Wrong (1995)** : This album, released before *Play*, received poor critical reviews and did not sell well. However, it did get relatively good airplay, especially in the United States. "no había tenido muy buena crítica no había vendido mucho pero sí que había sonado relativamente bien sobre todo en los Estados Unidos."

**Animal Rights (1996)** : Moby released this album after his electronic career stalled and guitar music remained strong in the US, shifting from his electronic sound to punk. It was a severe failure, almost ruining his career completely, with critics calling it a "horror." The album was themed around animal rights activism, a cause Moby is deeply committed to. "venía el peor de los fracasos arruinaba la carrera de movi casi por completo la crítica destrozaba el disco de animal rights lo consideraba un horror."

**Play (1999) - Initial Failure & Licensing Success** : Initially released in 1999, *Play* was a commercial failure, selling only a few thousand units in its first days and failing to chart, leading Moby to believe his career was over. To recoup costs, his representative licensed the album's tracks very cheaply for TV commercials, radio ads, and films due to their ambient nature. This strategy led to the album gaining immense popularity, especially in Europe initially, before becoming a global phenomenon and making Moby a millionaire. "movi assume ya que es el final de su carrera no consigue ya después de todo ese trabajo intimista dedicado dos años hípico no da ningún resultado."

**Play (1999) - Musical Legacy & Influence** : This album is described as a journey through 50-60 years of music, incorporating gospel, soul, funk, and blues, which was particularly remarkable for an American artist. It introduced a new, more subdued and ambient electronic sound to the United States, reaching millions of mainstream consumers who had never listened to electronic music before. Its rhythmic, sampled bases influenced many pop and mainstream artists, establishing Moby as a visionary who redefined electronic music's role in popular culture. "el primer legado que dejaría movi con este disco es que habría a los Estados Unidos a un nuevo campo de electrónica más pausada y más ambiental."

**Play (1999) - Reissue (2018)** : A special edition of *Play* was reissued in May 2018 by Vinyl Me, Please. This collectible version features two transparent turquoise vinyl discs. "se va a reditar este mes en hérode el 2018 en una edición chulísima uno de los discos yo creo que más importante es la historia que es el play de movi se dita por vinil mi please en un disco súper chulo de dos discos en color turqués transparente."

**18 (2002)** : Some of the 70-80 unreleased songs Moby composed during the prolific period before *Play* eventually appeared on his album *18*. "algunas saldrían el 18 en el álbum siguiente."

## curiosities
**Porcelain (Memoirs) (c. 2017)** : Moby released his memoirs, titled *Porcelain*, which is described as a very long but personal book. It offers significant insights into the creation and initial struggles of the *Play* album, making it an engaging read for Moby fans and those interested in the early 90s rave scene. "mobi lanzaba sus memorias a las que llamaba porcelain un tocho larguísimo pero súper personal."

**Early 90s Career Struggles** : Moby began his career in the early 90s electronic rave/disco scene but struggled to find significant success. Despite releasing some singles that gained some airplay and the album *Everything's Wrong*, he lacked critical acclaim, sales, and recognition, leading him to try different genres like punk. "Movi había comenzado en la escena electrónica un tanto rey un tanto discotequera de primeros de los 90 si mucho éxito."

**Animal Rights Activism** : Moby is deeply committed to animal rights activism, a cause that inspired his album *Animal Rights*. He frequently uses platforms like Facebook to advocate for veganism, nature conservation, and animal welfare. "movi es una persona muy concienciada en la lucha animalista los que los iráis en facebook vais a ver que está todo el puñete de lo día con rollo esbicanos de naturaleza de salva los cerros y todas estas cosas."

**Career Collapse (Mid-to-Late 90s)** : Following the disastrous reception of *Animal Rights*, Moby hit rock bottom by the mid-to-late 90s. He felt his career had collapsed, believing that his foray into punk had alienated him from the electronic scene and that his career was officially over. "Movi ya asumía que su carrera se había desplomado y que había fracasado."

**Prolific & Personal Creative Period (Pre-Play)** : After his career collapse, Moby retreated to his home studio, spending two and a half years working through his sadness and emotions by composing music. He created over 100 songs during this period, without a clear intention for commercial release, purely for personal expression. *Play* itself contained 18 of these tracks, and 70-80 more were left unreleased, some appearing on later albums or as B-sides. "compuso más de 100 canciones en esa época en la que sacó el play de hecho el play tiene 18 canciones."

**One-Man Orchestra Approach** : Moby is a multi-instrumentalist, capable of playing over 30 instruments. During the creation of *Play*, he recorded all the music himself in his home studio, layering instruments like drums and other parts, essentially acting as a "one-man orchestra." "el play de movi es movi tocando la batería se graba toca por encima de algo se graba toca por encima algo se graba es él el hombre orquesta prácticamente."

**Initial Rejection by Labels (1997-1998)** : After compiling his work for *Play*, Moby sent it to record labels and agents in 1997-1998, but it was rejected without being listened to. The ambient nature of the music was considered out of market trends at the time, and Moby's previous "disastrous trajectory" further fueled industry disinterest. "no consiguen ni que le ganan caso la discográfica."

**Industry Disinterest in Play (1997-1998)** : Industry professionals and critics, viewing Moby's previous career as a disaster and *Play* as a lengthy, eclectic "brick" of 18 tracks mixing genres like blues, soul, electronic, rave, and instrumental music, often didn't even bother to listen to the album initially. "directamente es que no consiguen ni que lo escuchen."

**Self-Financed Initial Pressing** : The label "uv2" eventually agreed to a small initial pressing of *Play*, which Moby largely had to finance himself. "su uv2 se cida a hacer una pequeña tirada que prácticamente acaba pagando movi de su bolsillo."

**Sample Clearance Nightmare** : Because Moby recorded *Play* in his home studio without commercial intentions, he used hundreds of samples, rhythms, and loops from various types of music. The process of identifying, licensing, and securing the rights for all these samples became a "nightmare" that took months, leading to some songs being excluded or modified. "recordar todo lo que había hecho movi licenciarlo buscar los derechos y poder trabajarlo fue una pesadilla llevó meses."

**Public Humiliation by Audience** : During his punk phase following the *Animal Rights* album, Moby opened for Soundgarden, where audiences threw objects at him and laughed, marking a low point of public humiliation in his career. "movi haría actuaciones como telonero de sound garden por ejemplo en el cual le tiraban cosas al escenario y se reían de él."

**Fan Alienation (Animal Rights)** : Moby's shift to punk with *Animal Rights* caused him to lose the small fanbase he had begun to build in the electronic/disco scene, as they did not understand the drastic change in musical direction. "los pocos fans que se había conseguido ganar en la escena electrónica no habían entendido el cambio."

**Delayed Critical Acclaim (2000)** : Despite its 1999 release, many positive critical reviews for *Play* did not appear until well into 2000, almost a year later. Critics only took notice and praised the album once its market success, driven by commercial licensing, was undeniable. "muchas en las críticas que tenemos son de muy avanzado en 2000 no bien ni siquiera se habían dignado hacerlo una crítica al disco play de movi hasta que ya estaba muy asentado en el mercado."

**Financial Success and Millionaire Status (2000s)** : By 2000, Moby became a millionaire through *Play*'s success, largely due to the sheer volume of placements in films, ads, and sketches, even though each individual licensing fee was very low. This financial success allowed him to restart and expand his tour, which he had initially cancelled due to the album's poor initial sales. "cuando se ve cuenta movis estaba haciendo millonario gracias a eso y no solo eso sino que tenía que empezar a renovar la gira que prácticamente había suspendido por el escaso es el inicial del play."

**Bitter Victory: Depression During Peak Success** : Despite *Play*'s monumental success, Moby experienced this glorious period poorly. After years of failure, humiliation, and battling depression and alcoholism (as revealed in his book), he found himself in a "very sad psychological state" during the peak of his career, describing it as a "very bitter and sad victory." "el pasa la etapa de mayor gloria y a oje de su carrera en un estado psicológico muy triste es una es una como una victoria muy amarga y muy triste para la para la vida de movi."

**Post-Play Popularity & Demand** : Following *Play*'s success, Moby became a highly sought-after artist. He was courted by magazines that had previously panned his earlier work, with everyone wanting interviews, photos, and collaborations. Artists like David Bowie, Metallica, and Beastie Boys incorporated his sounds, requested remixes, rhythmic bases, and commissioned compositions from him. "todo el mundo quería ir con movi todo el mundo quería hacerse fotos con movi todo el mundo quería hacer entre vistas y artículos de movi."

**Eclectic Future Career** : After *Play*, Moby continued his eclectic musical path, embracing disco, rock, punk, band-oriented music, and ambient sounds. He was then widely understood as a versatile artist, capable of surprising audiences with diverse offerings, whose work should be approached with an open mind. "movi en el futuro sin entrar a mojor demasiado en el resto de su carrera seguiría haciendo esa parte cléctica que tuvo movi con partes muy discotequeras volvería a hacer rock volvería a hacer punk volvería a tener casi una banda de música volvería a la música ambiental."

**Career Rescue by Play** : *Play* ultimately rescued Moby's career from the failure and industry rejection he had experienced, particularly in an environment that was initially unprepared for his innovative sound. "este disco al final consiguió rescatar a un gran genio que teníamos entra a música del fracaso que le había dado la industria y sobre todo un entorno que no estaba demasiado preparado a lo que le estaba haciendo."

**Best-Selling Electronic Album (Years)** : For several years, *Play* held the distinction of being the best-selling electronic album in history. "durante años fue el disco electrónico más vendido de la historia."

# genre - Ambient Music

# genre - Pop Music

## curiosities
**Influence of Moby's Play** : After Moby's *Play* gained popularity, many artists in the pop and mainstream scenes began incorporating his subdued rhythmic style. This led to the creation of ballads and romantic songs featuring electronic or pre-fabricated sampled bases, establishing Moby's approach as a new method for making mainstream music. "muchísimos artistas pasaría a incorporar en la estina pop y en la estina de gran consumo esa manera ritmica pausada que hacía movi."

**Redefining Electronic Work in Pop** : Moby's success and collaborations with major artists like David Bowie, Metallica, and Beastie Boys redefined the role of electronic work within the pop and mainstream scenes, solidifying his status as a highly respected star. "el redefinió el trabajo el electrónico dentro de la estina del pop y del gran consumo."

# curiosity

**European Electronic Scene - Openness to Moby's Sound (2000)** : By 2000, Europe had a more open electronic scene compared to the United States, which was more receptive to Moby's sound. European audiences were already familiar with instrumental and ambient electronic music from bands like Air and influenced by artists such as Jean-Michel Jarre, offering a market beyond just disco-oriented electronic music. "en Europa teníamos una estina leitrónica yo creo que mucho más abierta el sonido de movi."

**Global Chart Success of Play (2000s)** : Moby's *Play* achieved widespread international success, reaching number one in the United Kingdom (where Moby first discovered its popularity), France, Germany, and Australia. It also secured very high chart positions in Japan and eventually reached number one in the United States, though much later than its European success. "fue número uno en el reino unido pero después era número uno en Francia número uno en Alemania iría a número uno en Australia no ya haría número uno pero ya haría puestos altísimos en Japón."
