# artist - John Lennon

## curiosities
**Anthem of Pacifist Struggle (1969)** : On June 1, 1969, John Lennon and Yoko Ono recorded one of the greatest anthems of the pacifist struggle in a hotel room in Toronto. This act took place amidst escalating war and government efforts to suppress peace movements. "en esa habitación John Lennon y Jo Cono grabaron uno de los mayores ínnos de la lucha pacifista."
**Woodstock Performance Prohibited (1969)** : John Lennon was originally scheduled to perform at the Woodstock festival but was ultimately prohibited from entering the United States. This ban was due to declarations he had made against the American government's policies and the ongoing war. "John Lennon originariamente iba a actuar en el festival de Gustock, finalmente nunca actuó, porque John Lennon le fue prohibida la entrada a los Estados Unidos por algunas de las declaraciones que había hecho en contra de las políticas del gobierno americano y en contra de la guerra."

# concert - Woodstock

## curiosities
**Original Intent vs. Reality (1969)** : The Woodstock Festival, initially referred to as "Kutsu-Sok," was not originally conceived to be a massive event or an icon for the hippie and pacifist culture. Its eventual iconic status was born from a series of coincidences, severe organizational problems, and a critical need within American society for such an event. "originariamente el Festival de Kutsu-Sok ni siquiera estaba pensado para ser tal, originariamente ni siquiera se pretendía que fuese tan grande ni estaba pensado en convertirse en un icono para la cultura hippie."
**Permitting and Venue Struggles (1969)** : In the early months of 1969, the four young organizers faced immense difficulties, lacking a lineup, venue, and permits due to government and local opposition. They struggled to find a farmer willing to host, encountering significant community resistance and last-minute laws to prevent the festival. After months of frustration, they finally secured a farm in Bethel, nearly 70 kilometers from the originally planned Woodstock site, despite it lacking water, electricity, sanitation, and direct road access. "después de meses de frustración y impedimentos, acabarían encontrando una granja en vez del casi a 70 kilómetros de custoque, donde originariamente estaba pensado el festival."
**Media Mockery and Youth Resistance (1969)** : From March to June 1969, the festival's organization was continuously mocked and disqualified in television and press for its apparent lack of lineup, venue, and permits. Despite this, many young Americans eagerly followed the news, viewing the festival as a crucial act of resistance against a government that sought to silence pacifist movements. "La organización de este festival saldría en la televisión en la prensa durante todos los meses de marzo, abril, mayo y junio continuamente descalificándolo inmofándose como el festival que no se podía celebrar..."
**Permit Obtained Last Minute (1969)** : Through perseverance, patience, and some misrepresentation (claiming an expected attendance of 50,000 people), the organizers finally obtained the necessary permit to hold the festival on August 2, 1969, just two weeks before its scheduled start. "finalmente el 2 de agosto de 1969, apenas 2 semanas antes de la celebración del festival, obtenían el permiso para montarlo."
**Massive Over-Attendance (1969)** : While the permit was requested for an estimated 50,000 attendees, 168,000 tickets were sold two weeks before the festival. Ultimately, over 400,000 people, almost half a million, attended the event. Organizers considered themselves fortunate it wasn't 2 million, as that many youths across the country had expressed intent to go. "el último recuento de entradas que tenían vendida era de 168.000 personas, finalmente acodirían a gustos más de 400.000, casi medio millón..."
**Premature Arrival of Attendees (1969)** : A week before the festival, caravans of young people began traveling towards the site from across the United States. By August 10, roads within a 50-kilometer radius were already collapsed. By August 13, two days before the festival began, 50,000 people had already arrived at the un-fenced field, waiting for the event, with the stage still under construction. "El 13 de agosto a 2 días de la celebración del festival, con una organización en pleno montaje del escenario que ni siquiera estaba montado, se presentaron en el descampado de vez el 50.000 personas que habían acodido 2 días antes de esperar el concierto."
**Free Festival Decision (1969)** : The festival's organization, having lacked time to properly fence the grounds, found it impossible to control the entry of people. Many of the 50,000 early arrivals did not have tickets, and the original $18 entry fee was largely unpaid. Faced with uncontrollable crowds and the potential for riots if they tried to enforce payment, the organizers opted to surrender and allow everyone to enter freely. "optaron por rendirse y dejar entrar a todo el que quisiera."
**Traffic Chaos and Mass Migration (1969)** : By August 13, 1969, tens of thousands of cars were stuck on surrounding roads, unable to advance, as people attempted to access a venue without direct road entry. Frustrated and paralyzed, thousands abandoned their vehicles mid-road and chose to walk, in some cases up to 20 kilometers, to reach the festival. An estimated 180,000 of the nearly half a million attendees left their cars behind. "Se estima que 180 mil de las casi medio millón de personas que acudieron a gustoc dejaron sus coches tirados en medio de las carreteras para continuar el trayecto andando."
**Opening Day Attendance (1969)** : On August 14, 1969, the day before the festival officially began, over 200,000 people were already gathered at the site, watching the stage still being assembled. "El 14 de agosto de 1969, un día antes del comienzo del festival, ya son más de 200 mil personas las que están en el descampado viendo como todavía se está montando un escenario."
**Historical Significance (1969)** : The festival began on August 15, 1969, and lasted three magical days until August 18, becoming the most important music festival in history. It brought together an entire American generation that opposed military conflicts and felt discredited by the government, thus making history. "el festival de música más importante de la historia, que junto a toda una generación americana que estaba en contra de los movimientos bélicos en su país, que estaba siendo completamente desprestigiada y así es como se hizo historia."

# curiosity

**Vietnam War Escalation and Societal Impact (1960s)** : The 1960s generation in America was deeply marked by the Vietnam War, which began in the 1950s as a small, seemingly resolvable conflict but by the 1960s had become an intractable horror. The youth of the decade grew up rejecting this military conflict. "toda la generación americana de los 60 creció marcada por el horror de la Greir Vietnam."
**Pacifist Protests and Civil Unrest (1968)** : In 1968, as the Vietnam War escalated with hundreds of thousands of American troops deployed and the conflict expanding into neighboring countries like Laos and Cambodia, public support plummeted. This led to a phase of rejection that degenerated into widespread pacifist protests, some of which escalated into riots and popular revolts in various US cities, transforming the nation from discontent to street manifestations and popular unrest. "la sociedad americana estuvo marcada por una primera fase de rechazo al conflicto que fue degenerando en protestas pacifistas y que algunas de ellas en algunas ciudades de Estados Unidos acabaron degenerando pues en rejertas y en rebultas populares."
**Richard Nixon's Presidency and Vietnam Policy (1969)** : Richard Nixon became president of the United States on January 20, 1969, promising a swift end to the Vietnam War and a tough stance on pacifist protesters. However, in 1969, the conflict intensified, with American troop deployment peaking at over half a million. Nixon, realizing conventional military strategies were failing, implemented controversial counterintelligence policies in Southeast Asia, which further worsened the situation and popular opinion. "el 20 de enero de 1969 llega al gobierno de los Estados Unidos Richard Nixon."
**Government Counterintelligence Against Pacifist Movements (1969)** : In 1969, President Richard Nixon initiated a counterintelligence campaign to attack and discredit pacifist and hippie movements in the United States. Many negative perceptions of the hippie movement—that they were anti-American, lazy, and unhygienic—are largely attributed to this government pressure aimed at discrediting these peace-advocating groups. "Richard Nixon comenzó una campaña de contrainteligencia para atacar y desprestigiar algunos de los movimientos pacifistas que se estaban manifestando en Estados Unidos."
