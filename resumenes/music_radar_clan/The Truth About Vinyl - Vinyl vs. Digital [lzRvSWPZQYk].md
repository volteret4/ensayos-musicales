# artist - Thomas Edison

## curiosities
**First Playable Recording (1877)** : Thomas Edison made the first playable recording in 1877, following his invention of the telephone. He aimed to explore if telephone audio could be preserved for later reproduction. His device consisted of a rotating cylinder, a recording needle, and a diaphragm that translated sound pressure into analog indentations on the cylinder. "Este proceso fue primero colocado en 1877 por Thomas Edison."
**Phonograph Invention and Analog Voice Recording**: Edison designed a system where he spoke into a diaphragm, which moved a recording needle. As a cylinder was turned, the pressure from his voice was imprinted as analog indentations onto the cylinder's surface, creating a physical record of his speech. This invention marked the beginning of sound recording technology. "Edison habló de la presión de la presión, mientras se llevó el recorregimiento a un constante de la presión y, como lo han perdido, la presión de las sonrías se imprintó en una propuesta indentación que fue analógico para el recorregimiento de su voz."

# artist - Harry Nyquist

## curiosities
**Nyquist-Shannon Sampling Theorem Contribution (1928)** : Harry Nyquist, an electronic engineer, introduced the foundational concept of the sampling theorem in 1928. This idea was presented in a paper published by what is referred to as "el suelo de americanos americanos" (likely a journal like the Bell System Technical Journal). His work posited that to accurately reconstruct a frequency, it must be sampled at a rate of at least twice its frequency. "fue propósita en 1928 en un papel pívidolo publicado por el suelo de americanos americanos, el ingeniero electrónico, Harry Nyquist."

# artist - Claude Shannon

## curiosities
**Nyquist-Shannon Sampling Theorem Proof (1949)** : Claude Shannon provided a sufficient proof for the sampling theorem in 1949, building upon the theoretical framework established by Harry Nyquist in 1928. Together, their contributions form the basis of the Nyquist-Shannon Sampling Theorem, a cornerstone of digital audio and signal processing. "y fue suficientemente proveado por Claude Shannon en 1949."
