# instrument - Headphones (General)

## curiosities
**Types of Headphones - Button and Diadema** : Headphones are generally categorized into five types, grouped under two main categories: "button" headphones (earbuds) and "diadema" headphones (headband/over-ear). A third, specialized category for "sport headphones" is acknowledged but not discussed due to lack of expertise. "Button" headphones further divide into "on-ear" (like those for iPhone, resting on the surface of the ear) and "in-ear" (with silicone pads that insert into the ear canal). "Diadema" headphones are also split into "on-ear" and "over-ear" (which envelop the ear). "cinco tipos de auriculares que se agrupan en dos grandes grupos, tenemos los auriculares de botón y los auriculares de día de ma."
**Bluetooth vs. Wired Quality - Data Compression** : Bluetooth headphones offer convenience due to their wireless nature, but they face a technical limitation where the amount of information transmitted wirelessly is less than what a physical sound cable can carry. To compensate, Bluetooth headphones incorporate a compression system with a compression chip that processes the sound, regardless of the audio source's initial quality. While Bluetooth transmission has improved significantly, especially with modern versions like 4.1, it still transmits less information than a cable, making its sound quality generally inferior to traditional wired connections. This difference explains why professional home speakers often utilize Wi-Fi instead of Bluetooth. "los auriculares Bluetooth llevan un sistema de compresión con un chip de compresión dentro que comprime el sonido al margen de la calidad a la que sale el aparato."
**Bluetooth Evolution for Musical Applications** : It took several years from the discovery of Bluetooth technology until it could be effectively used in musical applications, primarily due to the initial limitations in data transmission quality. Despite improvements in modern Bluetooth versions (e.g., 4.1), wired connections still generally offer superior sound quality for demanding audio applications. Therefore, it is recommended that Bluetooth headphone purchases always include a cable input, allowing users to switch to a wired connection for better quality or a more consistent signal when desired. "Y esa es la razón por la que han pasado bastantes años desde que hemos descubierto el Bluetooth a que lo hemos podido usar en aplicaciones musicales."
**Unrecommended Brands - Philips** : Philips headphones are generally not recommended due to widespread consensus, including the speaker's own opinion, that the brand has become outdated in the headphone market. Their devices are often not considered the best available, and they are not competitively priced, leading to a poor value proposition. "Que Philips en demado auriculares se ha quedado bastante anticuado, con lo cual no son en ser los mejores aparatos y encima tampoco son baratos."
**Unrecommended Brands - Beats and Skullcandy (Specialized Equalization)** : While Beats and Skullcandy headphones are acknowledged as well-built products, they are not generally recommended due to their highly specialized and distinctive equalization profile. This unique sound signature, characterized by a subwoofer-like effect that over-saturates bass frequencies, is well-suited for genres like electronic music and hip-hop, giving a powerful sound. However, this same equalization proves detrimental to genres such as rock, classical music, or acoustic music, distorting the sound and making them unsuitable for audiophiles who prioritize pure sound quality. "Tiene una ecualización muy especial, muy propia. Que esta data muy bien, un tipo de sonido, pero muy mala otro tipo de sonido."
**Unrecommended Brands - Marshall (Design Over Sound Quality)** : Marshall headphones are noted for their attractive design and aesthetic appeal but are generally not recommended. The criticism stems from the belief that a significant portion of their cost is allocated to materials and visual styling rather than the quality of the speaker components. While not inherently bad, these headphones are considered disproportionately expensive for the audio performance they deliver, prioritizing appearance over sound fidelity. "Son muy caros de materiales y son muy vistosos y generalmente cuando te gastas el dinero en decorarlo no te gastas el dinero en la calidad de la altavoz."
**Personal Preference - On-Ear vs. In-Ear (Silicone)** : The speaker personally prefers the traditional "on-ear" button headphones, similar to those found with iPhones, which are becoming harder to find. A key reason for disliking silicone-tipped "in-ear" headphones is that while they offer superior sound isolation, they tend to over-saturate bass frequencies. Furthermore, their design can trap sound waves within the ear canal, causing small reverberation and echo effects as the sound cannot escape, leading to an undesirable listening experience. "Y no me gustan por un arrazón, porque aunque estos de silicon ha de oído, hais la mucho mejor el sonido, saturan los graves y generan pequeños efectos de reverberación y eco al no dejar escapar las zonas de audiencia fuera una vez que entra."

# curiosity

**Audiophile Preferences - Sound Purity** : Audiophiles are described as purists when it comes to sound quality, seeking the most faithful and undistorted reproduction of audio. They are critical of headphones, like Beats and Skullcandy, that employ specialized equalization or enhance bass in a way that deviates from a neutral and accurate sound presentation. For audiophiles, any distortion or coloration of the sound, even if appealing to some, makes the equipment undesirable. "Y desde luego no es nada recomendable a los audios filos que son puristas de la calidad de sonido porque distorsiona el sonido."
**Subjectivity of Sound Quality** : The perception of sound quality in headphones is highly subjective, extending beyond mere technical specifications. Each brand and model applies its own equalization and sound profile, meaning no headphone reproduces sound perfectly or identically. Ultimately, the best headphones for an individual are not necessarily those with the highest "pure quality" in objective terms, but rather those whose unique sound signature aligns best with the listener's personal preferences and how they enjoy hearing music. "muchas veces en estos los auriculares no es tanto como suelen si no cómo a ti te gusta cómo suelen cada marca suena su manera cada modelo cualiza su manera y a veces no es tanto la calidad pura si no lo que realmente a ti te gustan el oído."
**Dynamic Equalization in Bose QuietComfort 35** : The Bose QuietComfort 35 headphones feature an advanced dynamic equalization system that sets them apart. While all headphones apply some form of equalization (none reproduce sound 100% flat), Bose's system intelligently adapts its equalization in real-time. This adaptation occurs based on external environmental noise levels or the specific type of music the user is listening to. This intelligent adjustment helps maintain consistent high sound quality and ensures that the headphones perform optimally across different musical genres and listening environments. "todos los auriculares se cualizan o sea ninguno reproduce de 100% todos se cualizan y cada una su manera pero los boss tienen un sistema dinámico de ecualización cada adaptable ecualización al rohido externo o al tipo de música que tú escuchas."
