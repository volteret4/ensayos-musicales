# artist - The Rolling Stones

## members
Mick Jagger
Keith Richards
Brian Jones

## genres
American music
Psychedelia

## albums
**Between the Buttons (1967) - Shift to Original Material** : Released in early 1967, this album represented a significant change for The Rolling Stones, moving away from their earlier practice of covering other artists to featuring almost exclusively self-written material. Despite the band's intention to establish their own creative voice, the album did not achieve critical success, and while one song reached number 1, the album ultimately failed commercially. "este era un disco que ya prácticamente todo estaba hecho por ellos, pero sin embargo no tenía ningún éxito, la crítica no le gustaba, llegaron a número 1 con una canción, pero el disco finalmente acabó pinchando."
**Their Satanic Majesties Request (1967) - Self-Produced Failure** : Released at the end of 1967, this album was a self-produced effort following the departure of producer Andrew Loog Oldham. Although today it is remembered as a cult classic and a creative highlight of the 1960s, at the time of its release, it was a profound critical and commercial failure for the band. "pasan a producirse ellos mismos, y bajo su propia producción sacan a finales en 1967 de Satanic Ballastic West, es uno de los discos de culto por excelencia de la época de los 60, ahora todos lo recordamos como uno de los puntos más altos creativos a los Rolling Stones, pero en su día fue un atentico fracaso."
**Their Satanic Majesties Request (1967) - Commercial Failure and Censorship Issues** : This self-produced album garnered significant criticism, encountered numerous censorship problems, and was notably expensive to produce, all contributing to its commercial failure. It stands as possibly the greatest commercial flop in The Rolling Stones' history, despite its later reappraisal. "tuvieron un montón de problemas de censura, el álbum era muy caro, entonces también acabó pinchando, o fue posiblemente el mayor fracaso comercial de la historia de los Rolling Stones."
**Their Satanic Majesties Request (1967) - Career Low Point** : Despite some artistic merit, this album was ultimately the biggest commercial failure in The Rolling Stones' career, initiating a rapid and uncontrolled decline for the band. "fue posiblemente el mayor fracaso comercial de la carrera de los Rolling Stones y empieza a un poco esa cuesta bajo rápida y sin frenos."
**Beggar's Banquet (1968) - Swift Production** : Following the success of the "Jumpin' Jack Flash" single, The Rolling Stones quickly entered the recording studio. Within just a few months, they completed this album, which would go on to be considered by many as potentially the best album of their career. "Se cerraron en el estudio de grabación y en menú unas pocos meses se acababan posiblemente el mejor disco de la carrera de los Rolling Stones."
**Beggar's Banquet (1968) - Censorship and Delayed Release** : The album, released in 1968, faced substantial censorship problems concerning both its original cover art and certain songs. These issues led to a five-month delay in its release, and it ultimately came out with a completely blank white cover featuring only the band's name and the album title, a direct consequence of the censorship. "el vegan zwankett que es el grandísimo album que saldría en 1968 tenía problemas de censura de la portada, tenía un problema de censura con las canciones... al final con cinco meses de retraso con una portada que como fue censurada quedó completamente en blanco solo con el nombre de los Rolling Stones y con el título del disco salía el grandísimo vegan zwankett."
**Beggar's Banquet (1968) - White Album Coincidence** : The release of *Beggar's Banquet* with a minimalist white cover, forced upon the band by censorship, coincidentally occurred at the same time as The Beatles' self-titled "White Album." This led to an unexpected parallel between the two major bands' album aesthetics, though The Rolling Stones' choice was involuntary. "volvía a tener problemas porque coincidía con el lanzamiento del guay de Albueno de los Beatles y ellos acaban de un album en blanco no porque quisieran que fuese blanco sino porque su portada había sido completamente censurada."
**Beggar's Banquet (1968) - Critical and Commercial Triumph** : Despite the initial challenges with production and censorship, *Beggar's Banquet* was met with overwhelming critical acclaim and promptly returned The Rolling Stones to the number one position, solidifying their status as "the greatest" band once more. "tenía unas enormes críticas volvían o traezan número uno volvían a ser los más grandes."

## songs
**"2000 Light Years From Home" (1967) - Production Issues** : This track, featured on *Their Satanic Majesties Request*, is specifically noted for its "interminable introduction." This lengthy intro is cited as an example of the production problems inherent in the band's self-produced album, which made certain parts of the record difficult to endure. "el Sisarranbu que tiene esa interminable introducción que lo único que haces torturarte durante minutos."
**"Jumpin' Jack Flash" (1968) - Revival Single** : Released as the band's first new material after a period marked by low popularity and intense internal disputes, particularly between Keith Richards and Brian Jones. This single achieved immense success and signaled a powerful return to form for The Rolling Stones. "no esperan sacar un disco y simplemente sacan un primer material un primer sencillo, grandísimo Jean-Pin Jack Trash."
**"Jumpin' Jack Flash" (1968) - Critical and Commercial Success** : This single achieved widespread success and was met with critical acclaim, with many critics declaring the return of the quintessential Rolling Stones sound. It marked a distinct departure from an awkward psychedelic phase and other commercial efforts that had diluted their core identity, successfully restoring the band to prominence. "tuvo muchísimo éxito la crítica decía que volvían los Rolling Stones de toda la vida y nuestros Rolling Stones que estaban un poco arregufo entre una psicodelia entre la que no se movían cómodos y un intento por ser comerciales que no lo había hecho perder su esencia y con el Jean-Pin Jack Trash vuelven un poco por todo lo alto tuvieron muchísimo éxito."
**"Sympathy for the Devil" (1968)** : A prominent track from the album *Beggar's Banquet*, this song is highlighted as one of The Rolling Stones' significant and enduring successes within their career. "el grandísimo sinfacil de debil."
**"Street Fighting Man" (1968)** : Featured on the *Beggar's Banquet* album, this song is recognized as another one of the major and impactful hits in The Rolling Stones' extensive career. "al estrifighter van."

## curiosities
**Golden Age - Most Creative Period (1968-1973)** : The period between 1968 and 1973 is identified as possibly the most creative and successful era in The Rolling Stones' long career, which now spans over 50 years. This phase followed a low point and preceded further creative crises. "posiblemente su mayor etapa creativa fue entre 1968 y el 62."
**Pre-Golden Age Low Point (before 1968)** : Immediately preceding their golden age, the Rolling Stones experienced a period of significant decline, hitting rock bottom just before 1968. This was one of the lowest points in their career, even though the members were still relatively young. "justo antes del 68, cuando empezaba esa gran época, venía una pequeña época en que los Rolling Stones habían tocado fondo."
**Early 1967 High Point** : At the beginning of 1967, The Rolling Stones were experiencing a peak in their career. They had achieved numerous number-one hits and completed a massive tour across the United States, performing in many stadiums, including a notable occasion where they shared the stage with James Brown. "En el 67 los Rolling Stones, yo creo que estaban en uno de los puntos más altos, habían conseguido un montón de números, uno, habían hecho esa enorme gina por Estados Unidos en las que habían llenado un montón de estadios, incluso habían coincidido con el grandísimo James Brown, una actuación."
**1967 Drug Arrests** : Coinciding with the release of their album *Between the Buttons* in 1967, members of The Rolling Stones faced incarceration for a brief period due to drug possession and consumption. This event severely damaged their public popularity and marked the onset of a downturn in the band's fortunes. "los Rolling Stones acabaron encarcelados era un pequeño, durante un pequeño trozo de tiempo, por posesión y consumo de drogas, entonces hizo caer mucho su popularidad."
**End of Andrew Loog Oldham Era (1967)** : In late 1967, as a direct response to the commercial and critical failures of the early year, The Rolling Stones officially terminated their professional relationship with producer Andrew Loog Oldham. Oldham had been a pivotal figure in establishing their early importance and success. "se acabó oficialmente la etapa con André Olman, que fue el productor que los hizo tan importantes."
**Lack of Instrumental Prowess** : A significant challenge for The Rolling Stones during their self-production of *Their Satanic Majesties Request* was that they were not highly skilled instrumentalists, despite their talent. Their limited musical knowledge contributed to the album's perceived production disaster. "ellos no eran unos grandes instrumentalistas, eran gente con mucho talento, pero no con muchos conocimientos de música, entonces el producirse a sí mismos les generó un pequeño desastre."
**Dismissal of Manager and Legal Battle (1967)** : Before the end of 1967, The Rolling Stones fired their manager, which ignited a substantial legal battle. This dispute ultimately resulted in the band losing the rights to all their previously recorded discography from before 1967. "acaban despidiendo a su manaller, al enclenque era con el que acababan con un enorme litijo legal, con el que acababan perdiendo los derechos de toda su discografía anterior a 1967."
**Internal Conflict - Richards and Jones (Early 1968)** : At the very beginning of 1968, a significant and intense conflict erupted within the band between Keith Richards and Brian Jones, leading to a major internal struggle. "justo al inicio de 1968 fue cuando Kate Riches, Lerroso, no viabraya, y Jones, lo que generó aparte de una enorme pelea entre ellos."
**Brian Jones' Departure (Later) and Band Crisis** : The profound internal problems within the band, particularly the friction between Richards and Jones, were eventually resolved with Brian Jones' departure a few years later. This period represented a nadir for The Rolling Stones, during which the press even openly speculated about the band's potential dissolution. "Un enorme problema interno dentro de la banda que al fina de la cabaría se sanándose con la partida de Brian Jones unos años más tarde y ahí es un poco cuando los Rolling Stones tocan fondo y un poco están ahí que prácticamente incluso la prensa llegaba a hablar de la posible muerte de los Rolling Stones."
**Beginning of Jimmy Miller Era (1968)** : In response to their crisis, The Rolling Stones adopted a constructive approach, initiating a new and highly productive era with producer Jimmy Miller. This partnership marked a turning point for the band. "es cuando empieza la etapa de Jimmy Miller."
**Mick Jagger's Vision for American Sound** : Mick Jagger had a clear vision for the band, emphasizing the necessity of recapturing their "American essence" in their music, which he believed had been crucial to their prior success. This strategic focus led to their contact and subsequent collaboration with producer Jimmy Miller, marking a new chapter for the band that would last until 1973. "mi Jagger lo tenía muy claro. Necesitaba un productor que les yese, otra vez, esa esencia americana que tan importantes los había hecho."
**Jimmy Miller's Collaboration Period (1968-1973)** : Jimmy Miller's period of collaboration with The Rolling Stones began during a low point for the band, characterized by internal strife between members like Richards and Jones, and a dip in popularity. This partnership continued until 1973, defining what would become known as The Rolling Stones' "golden age." "empieza una nueva etapa que sería la etapa durada de los Rolling Stones hasta precisamente en 1973 cuando Jimmy Miller acaba su trabajo de colaboración con los Rolling Stones."
**Brian Jones' Departure (1968) and Golden Age Start** : The internal crisis within The Rolling Stones was finally resolved with Brian Jones' departure in 1968. This event was immediately followed by the commencement of an "incredible golden age" for the band, a four or five-year period of intense production with Jimmy Miller that yielded some of the most remarkable music of the past half-century. "esa pequeña crisis interna que tenían dentro la banda de los Rolling Stones acababa siendo sobsanada finalmente con la partida de Brian Jones y esa partida aquí en 1968 cuando empieces a increíble época dorada de cuatro o cinco años de producción con Jimmy Miller."
**Post-Golden Age Creative Crisis** : The golden age of The Rolling Stones, which began around 1968 with Jimmy Miller, lasted for several years before the band once again plunged into a significant creative crisis. "venía en la época dorada de los Rolling Stones que volvería a acabar unos años más tarde para volver a entrar en el enorme crisis creativa de la banda."

# artist - The Beatles

## albums
**The Beatles (The White Album) (1968) - Coincidental Release** : The Beatles' self-titled "White Album" was released coincidentally around the same time as The Rolling Stones' *Beggar's Banquet*. This synchronicity was notable because both albums featured minimalist white covers, though The Rolling Stones' white cover was a result of censorship. "coincidía con el lanzamiento del guay de Albueno de los Beatles."

# genre - Psychedelia

## curiosities
**Uncomfortable Period for The Rolling Stones (1967-1968)** : The Rolling Stones experienced a phase where they felt uncomfortable engaging with psychedelic music. This genre, combined with attempts at being overly commercial, led them to lose their essence, a situation from which they recovered with the success of "Jumpin' Jack Flash." "estaban un poco arregufo entre una psicodelia entre la que no se movían cómodos y un intento por ser comerciales que no lo había hecho perder su esencia."

# producer - Andrew Loog Oldham

## curiosities
**End of Collaboration with The Rolling Stones (1967)** : Andrew Loog Oldham, who had been an instrumental producer in establishing The Rolling Stones' importance, officially ceased his collaboration with the band in late 1967. This decision was a rapid response to the commercial and critical setbacks the band experienced earlier that year. "se acabó oficialmente la etapa con André Olman, que fue el productor que los hizo tan importantes."

# producer - Jimmy Miller

## curiosities
**Collaboration with The Rolling Stones (1968-1973)** : Jimmy Miller began his collaboration with The Rolling Stones in 1968, marking the start of a new, highly productive era for the band. His work as a producer with the band continued until 1973, a period recognized as The Rolling Stones' "golden age." "empieza una nueva etapa que sería la etapa durada de los Rolling Stones hasta precisamente en 1973 cuando Jimmy Miller acaba su trabajo de colaboración con los Rolling Stones."
**Tasked with Restoring American Essence** : Jimmy Miller was specifically sought out by Mick Jagger to help The Rolling Stones reclaim their "American essence" in their music. This cultural and sonic influence was perceived as vital for their continued importance and success. "Necesitaba un productor que les yese, otra vez, esa esencia americana que tan importantes los había hecho. Entonces, considen contactar con Jimmy Miller."
