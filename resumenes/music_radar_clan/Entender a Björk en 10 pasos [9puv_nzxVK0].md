## albums
**Stick Around for Joy (1992) - The Sugarcubes** : This album is presented as a key example of Björk's career before her solo work, showcasing early influences of trip hop, indie music, and rhythmic bases from Icelandic music, which remained important to her. It is considered the first point to understand Björk before she became the prominent artistic figure she is today. "este álbum de 1992 es el primer punto para entender qué fue Bjork antes de ser el personaje que eso hoy en día."
**Debut (1993) - Solo Career Launch** : This album marks the beginning of Björk's solo career and represents her first major musical evolution. It was a perfect example of her transition from a more indie sound to purely electronic music, featuring changes between ballads like "Venus as a Boy" and tracks with powerful rhythmic bases. "es ejemplo perfecto de la transición de la música que iba haciendo más indía a la música más puramente electrónica."
**Post (1995) - Electronic Exploration** : This album is significant for two reasons: Björk began collaborating with leading figures in mid-90s electronic music such as Nellee Hooper, Howie B, and Tricky, hardening her electronic sound. It also highlighted her dedication to remixes, which she viewed as genuine reinterpretations of her material, often re-recording entire parts of songs for them. "su sonido se empieza a hacer una electrónica más dura."
**Homogenic (1997) - Career Zenith** : Considered a career high point for many fans and a key piece of 90s music, this album further defined Björk's sound. It introduced collaborations with Mark Bell, leading to much harder and more experimental electronic sounds, moving away from trip hop. "el homogénic. Es el grandísimo disco que es una piza clave de los 90."
**Selma Songs (2000) - Soundtrack Album** : This album served as the soundtrack to the film *Dancer in the Dark*, in which Björk was the lead actress. She not only performed a harrowing role but also composed the entire soundtrack, which bridged the gap between film and musical. "No sólo tuvo un increíble papel dentro de la película, sino que compuso toda la canción que está medio camino dentro de la película y el musical."
**Vespertine (2001) - Organic Evolution** : A pivotal album in Björk's career, it marked a shift away from the harsh and aggressive electronics of her early work towards more organic sounds, incorporating influences from wind instruments, harps, and nature, moving away from industrial aesthetics. During this period, Björk also refined her vocal technique to be more harmonic and peaceful. "ya deja ese electrónica tan dura y tan agresiva que veníamos arrastando de sus inicios y se adentra en los sonidos cabez más orgánicos."
**Medúlla (2004) - Vocal Complexity** : This album is crucial in defining Björk's evolution from a singer to a museum artist or videocreator. It showcases her deepest exploration of vocal knowledge and work, presenting arguably her most vocally complex material to date. "el trabajo más complejo vocalmente que ha tenido Bjork hasta ahora."
**Volta (2007) - World Music & Popularity Peak** : *Volta* is noted for incorporating a rich array of world music sounds, making it possibly Björk's most globally influenced album. Released during a resurgence of new folk music, it marked a peak in her popularity, followed by an exhausting two-year tour that led to a significant hiatus. "incorpora muchísimos sonidos de músicas del mundo posiblemente se al disco de más enriquecido de músicas del mundo que ha tenido Bjork."
**Biophilia (Year not specified) - Museum Artistry** : This album belongs to a later stage in Björk's career where she is viewed more as a museum artist than solely a singer. Her work during this period, alongside *Vulnicura*, delves deeper into audiovisual projects, computer-based creations, and collaborations with other visual artists, solidifying her recognition in the art world. "Esta etapa yo creo que es la que más reconocimiento dentro del mundo del arte ladado al artista Bjork."
**Vulnicura (Year not specified) - Arca Collaboration** : Part of Björk's later artistic phase, this album, along with *Biophilia*, illustrates her transition into a prominent museum artist. It marks a significant collaboration with the Venezuelan singer, producer, and artist Arca, with whom she continuously works on audiovisual projects and video creations, further establishing her presence in modern art museums globally. "esta etapa ya podemos sacar pues por ejemplo el Black Lane o el último tema que acaba de sacar ahora mismo que nos deje esa situación en la que estamos ahora en la que Bjork ya ha dejado de ser una cantante para ser una de las artistas más importantes que tenemos."

## songs
**Venus as a Boy (Year not specified)** : Featured on the *Debut* album, this song is highlighted as a ballad, contrasting with other tracks on the album that have potent rhythmic bases. It exemplifies the diverse soundscapes Björk explored during her transition to electronic music. "cambios a veces un poco agresivos entre Vinos a Savoy por ejemplo que es como muy balada y esas canciones ya mucho más de bases muy potentes rítmicas."
**Human Behaviour (Year not specified)** : This song from the *Debut* album is cited as a perfect sonic transition. It bridges the indie music influences carried over from her Sugarcubes era with the powerful trip hop bases integrated into her new electronic sound. "Jim and Bjork, que nos trae el resto de la música indique traía de su Art Cube y esas bases tan potentes que venían incorporadas del trijop."
**Isobel (Year not specified)** : Featured on the *Post* album, this song is presented as a prime example of Björk's pioneering approach to remixes. It is noted for having around 20 remixes, with 10 of them being described as "truly incredible," demonstrating her dedication to reinterpreting her own material. "para entender esta parte, yo creo que nos tenemos que quedar sobretodo con Isabel, una de las mejores canciones remécladas."
**All Is Full of Love (Year not specified)** : From the *Homogenic* album, this track is described as the album's most romantic ballad. Despite its romantic nature, it features powerful, hard rhythmic bases and metallic sounds that evoke an industrial feel, showcasing the experimental and harder electronic sound of the album. "Oly's Full of Love, que aunque es una canción romántica y muy balada, tiene unas bases rítmicas realmente potentes, duras y de sonidos tan metálicos, que dan esa sensación de sonido industrial."
**I've Seen It All (2000)** : This song from the *Selma Songs* soundtrack is noted for being one of the most beautiful songs Björk has ever written. It features a collaboration with Thom Yorke, the singer of Radiohead, further elevating its artistic significance. "nos quedamos con el increíble tema Avesi Nirol de la bandas sonora de esta película, llamada Selma Songs, que canta con el mismísimo Tom Jor, cantante de Reibiojez."
**Pagan Poetry (Year not specified)** : A grand example from the *Vespertine* album, this "precious romantic song" illustrates Björk's move toward a more harmonious and peaceful vocal style, leaving behind the aggressive intensity of her earlier work. Its videoclip also highlights Björk's pioneering visual artistry, though it was censored by MTV due to partial nudity. "gran ejemplo es el pagan poetry. Esa grandísima canción preciosa romántica que nos deja una manera mucho más oce de trabajar Bjork."
**Who Is It (2004)** : The videoclip for this single from the *Medúlla* album premiered and was exhibited at the Museum of Modern Art in New York. This event underscores Björk's evolving role from a traditional singer to an artist focused on visual and museum-grade creations. "el videoclip de juicite uno de los ingues que traía el disco Médula que era directamente srenado y expuesto en el Museo de Arte Moderno en Nueva York."
**Dull Flame of Desire (Year not specified)** : Featured on the *Volta* album, this song is highlighted as one of Björk's best collaborations, featuring Anohni (from Antony and the Johnsons). It exemplifies the album's rich incorporation of world music sounds and its broad appeal. "esa increíble canción de full-dame of desire que cantaba con anzones y danzones de llonso es una de las mejores colaboraciones que ha tenido Bjork con otros artistas."
**Black Lake (Year not specified)** : This track, likely from *Vulnicura*, is presented as an example of Björk's current artistic stage, marked by her deep collaboration with the Venezuelan artist, producer, and singer Arca. It represents her continuous evolution towards audiovisual works and museum exhibitions, moving beyond conventional concerts. "esta etapa ya podemos sacar pues por ejemplo el Black Lane o el último tema que acaba de sacar ahora mismo que nos deje esa situación en la que estamos ahora en la que Bjork ya ha dejado de ser una cantante para ser una de las artistas más importantes que tenemos."

## curiosities
**Cultural Icon Status** : Björk has evolved beyond being just an important singer to become a significant artistic figure and a cultural reference for her generation. Her influence is so profound that the Museum of Modern Art (MoMA) in New York has hosted more exhibitions dedicated to her than to Picasso. "Bjork se ha convertido en un personaje artístico que es un referente cultural prácticamente de nuestra generación."
**Early Career Projects** : Before launching her solo career, Björk was involved in numerous projects since her childhood. These early ventures, particularly her work with The Sugarcubes, were crucial in shaping her artistic identity before she embarked on her solo path. "tuvo muchísimos proyectos antes de trabajar en solitario y para entender esa etapa que la definió a ella un poco como artista antes de empezar su carrera por solitario."
**Nellee Hooper Collaboration** : Björk's collaboration with Bristol-based producer Nellee Hooper was a pivotal moment in her musical evolution, introducing her to the rhythmic foundations of trip hop and electronic music. Hooper, known for his work with Massive Attack, Tricky, and Portishead, was a key figure in shaping the sound of the 90s, guiding Björk into a more electronic sound. "Bjork conoce a Nilly Hopper, importante productor musical de Bristol, que había sido amigo de Massive Attack, de Tricky, de la gente de Portishead y que fue una persona clave para entender parte del sonido de los 90, ayuda a introducirse a Bjork a lo que serían las bases rítmicas del trip Hop y de la electrónica."
**Remix Pioneer** : Björk developed a very early vision for remixes, understanding them as authentic reinterpretations of her composed material. She not only encouraged other artists to remix her tracks but also created her own, even re-recording entire parts of songs for these remix versions, a practice that foreshadowed their later popularity. "Bjork desarrolla una visión muy temprana de lo que vienen a hacer las reméclas que serían tan populares y que son hoy en día y ya no solo las reméclas. Ella regraba, se reace partes enteras de las canciones para hacer solo en las reméclas."
**World Music Influences in Post** : In her *Post* album, Björk significantly broadened her musical palette beyond electronic and indie influences. She began incorporating complex sounds, including Caribbean rhythms, African rhythmic bases, and even elements from Asia, which helped to richly define her evolving sound with global influences. "aquí vienen influencias de ritmos calibeños, de bases rítmicas africanas, incluso de algunas partes de Asia, empiecen incorporar muchos sonidos del mundo que empiezan a requerar mucho ese sonido de Bjork."
**Mark Bell Collaboration** : Starting with the *Homogenic* album, Björk began a significant and lasting collaboration with Mark Bell, who would work with her for nearly the remainder of her career. This partnership was instrumental in her shift towards harder and more experimental electronic sounds, moving away from trip hop. "ella empieza a trabajar con Mark Bell, persona con la que acabaría trabajando casi el resto de su carrera."
**Alexander McQueen Album Art** : The cover of *Homogenic* featured Björk styled and made up by Alexander McQueen, marking the beginning of her distinctive approach to staging and fashion. This visual identity played a crucial role in her transformation into a significant public figure and cemented her status as an important artist. "esa increíble portada de Bjork vestida y maquillada por el mismo Alexander McQueen. Es el inicio de esa Bjork con esa estenografía y esa manera de vestirse, que ya cambiaría y que es una de las razones por las que Bjork se ha convertido en un personaje tan importante."
**Film Industry Work** : Björk has an extensive history of working with the film industry, contributing to numerous soundtracks and taking on minor roles in films. Her most notable achievement in this field came in 2000, when she starred as the lead actress in Lars von Trier's *Dancer in the Dark*, earning her the Best Actress award at the Cannes Film Festival. "una de las cosas más increíbles que tiene Bjork es su trabajo con la industria cinematográfica."
**Dancer in the Dark Performance** : In Lars von Trier's 2000 film *Dancer in the Dark*, Björk delivered a powerful and acclaimed performance as a blind woman fighting to save her son's health. Her role was both heartbreaking and critically praised, contributing to her Best Actress win at Cannes. "Ella tendría un desgarrador papel y una increíble interpretación en bailar en las curidades, dancer en la edad de Las Bontrier, donde hace el papel de una mujer ciega que lucha y ahorra por salvar la salud de su hijo."
**Voice Perfection and New Recording Method (Vespertine)** : During the *Vespertine* era, Björk underwent extensive singing lessons to perfect her voice, resulting in a less aggressive, more harmonic, and peaceful vocal delivery. Concurrently, she developed a new, more solitary recording system, working primarily alone at home with a computer, moving away from complex studio setups to focus on her individual artistic process. "Bjork recibe muchas clases de canto para perfeccionar su voz y tenemos una manera de cantar de Bjork que cada vez es menos agresiva e intensa y es mucho más armónica y mucho más pacífica ahora, así decirlo."
**Pioneer of Audiovisual Formats** : Björk was recognized as the first artist to release a single on DVD, establishing her as a significant precursor in the adoption of audiovisual formats. Her deep engagement with visual work is evident in her videoclips, which are often considered works of art. "ella fue la primera artista en publicar un single en DVD y siempre ha sido una de las grandes precursoras de los formatos audiovisuales."
**Censorship of "Pagan Poetry" Videoclip** : The videoclip for "Pagan Poetry" faced censorship in various places, including MTV, due to its depiction of partial nudity. This incident highlighted Björk's unique and sometimes provocative vision of feminine sensuality, which often features in her lyrics and visual works, presenting a natural and raw state of being. "el videoclip de pagan poetry acaba siendo censurado en muchos sitios e incluso en la MTV porque salen imágenes parcialmente de desnudos."
**Videoclips as Art** : Björk's videoclips are celebrated as genuine works of art, with many having been exhibited in museums. Her artistic vision is so compelling that she is one of the few artists to have collaborated with three of the most renowned directors in the field: Spike Jonze, Chris Cunningham, and Michel Gondry. "prácticamente todos los videoclips de Bjork son auténticas obras de arte que a veces han llegado a estar expuestas en museos."
**Transition to Museum Artist (Medúlla onwards)** : From the *Medúlla* album onwards, Björk's career evolved significantly, marking her transition from primarily being a singer to becoming a "museum artist" or a "videocreator." This phase emphasizes her deepening engagement with visual arts and her work being presented in art institutions. "empieza posiblemente la parte de Bjork ya no tanto como una cantante sino como directamente una artista de museo o una videocreadora."
**Volta Tour Exhaustion** : Following the success of her *Volta* album, Björk embarked on an "incredible and exhausting" two-year tour. This demanding period left her completely drained, leading to a substantial break from music and touring activities for several years. "hizo esa increíble y agotadora gira de dos años que la dejó completamente agotada y después de la cual tendrían enorme pausa."
**Contemporary Art World Recognition** : In her most recent stage, characterized by albums like *Biophilia* and *Vulnicura*, Björk has gained immense recognition within the art world. She focuses increasingly on audiovisual works, computer-based art, and video creations in collaboration with other plastic artists, with her art frequently featured in museums rather than just concerts. "Esta etapa yo creo que es la que más reconocimiento dentro del mundo del arte ladado al artista Bjork."
**Arca Collaboration** : In her current artistic phase, Björk has forged an important collaboration with Arca, a Venezuelan singer, producer, and artist based in New York. They work together consistently, shaping her contemporary sound and visual projects, which are increasingly presented in modern art museums globally. "conoce esa amiga de arca este cantante productor y artista de nezolano que vio en Nueva York con el que ya trabajan prácticamente juntos todo el tiempo."
**Enduring Artistic Legacy** : Björk has transcended the role of a mere singer to become one of the most significant artists of the present day, whose work is filling modern art museums worldwide. It is anticipated that her contributions will continue to be exhibited in museums globally for decades to come. "Bjork ya ha dejado de ser una cantante para ser una de las artistas más importantes que tenemos y que está llenando los museos de arte moderno de todo el mundo y que posiblemente en 30 a 40 años sigamos teniendo cosas de Bjork expuestas en muchísimos museos de todo el mundo."

# genre - Electronic

## curiosities
**Evolution in Björk's Sound** : Björk's career is marked by a dynamic evolution within electronic music, transitioning from the raw energy of trip hop and harder electronic sounds in the mid-90s, influenced by collaborations with Nellee Hooper, to more experimental and industrial textures in *Homogenic*, before embracing organic, natural sounds in *Vespertine* and global influences in *Volta*. "ayuda a introducirse a Bjork a lo que serían las bases rítmicas del trip Hop y de la electrónica."

# genre - Folk

## curiosities
**21st Century Revival Context** : The release of Björk's *Volta* album in 2007 coincided with a resurgence in folk music in the 21st century, with artists like CocoRosie and Devendra Banhart experiencing a "small peak in popularity" for this new approach to folk. *Volta* itself incorporated many world music sounds, positioning Björk within this broader musical landscape. "esta etapa de siglo XXI era la etapa en la que tuvimos ese pequeño resorgir de una nueva manera de entender la música folk en el que salían gente como las Coco Rossi o de Ben Draván Harff un pequeño pico de popularidad de esta nueva música folk."
