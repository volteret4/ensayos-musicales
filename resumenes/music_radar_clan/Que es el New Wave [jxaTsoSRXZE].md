# genre - New Wave

## curiosities
**Ambiguity of Definition** : The term "New Wave" lacks a concrete, universally agreed-upon definition, with its interpretation often varying by country. It is recommended to view any understanding of New Wave as relative due to this lack of consensus. "ni siquiera hay un conceso. Hay muchas maneras de entenderlo, incluso cambias en el país en el que estéis lo que consideramos el new wave."
**Early Application to Mainstream Rock** : The expression "New Wave" was first used in music to refer to a new generation of bands like The Beatles, The Beach Boys, Rolling Stones, and The Kinks. It was applied to any new musical movement that deviated from mainstream consumption. "la primera vez que tenemos constancia de que hemos usado la expresión de new wave dentro de la música. Se usaba para hacer referencia a la nueva ganación de los Beatles, los Beach Boys, Rolling Stones, los Kings y de todas estas bandas."
**Transition to Rock Pop** : Shortly after its initial application to the "new generation" of rock bands in the 1960s, the term "New Wave" was quickly replaced by "rock pop" for those artists, as they became mainstream. The term then shifted to describe alternative movements. "pocos años después, ya dejó de llamársela así para llamarse el rock pop que ya conocemos hoy en día."
**Shift to Alternative/Underground** : Following its initial use for mainstream rock, "New Wave" quickly became associated with alternative and underground movements. However, once these movements were absorbed by mainstream consumption, the term was again dropped, only to be revived later. "Motoda esta generación de los 60 y de casi durante todos los 60 fue absorbida, por lo gran consumo muy rápido, pues pronto dejó de llamarse el new wave porque ya era la gente normal, ya era música normal."
**Resurgence in the Late 1970s** : The concept of New Wave re-emerged strongly at the end of the 1970s, around 1975, with new waves of posturing. In the United States, it was seen as underground music, while in the United Kingdom, it was almost synonymous with British underground music. "se retomó mucho el concepto con bastante fuerza durante el final de los 70. Cuando se empezó a llamar otra vez el new wave en sí con mucha potencia, cuando aparecían las nuevas oleadas de posturio de esa 1975."
**Broad Definition in Late 70s Underground** : During the late 1970s, the New Wave movement in the underground was a heterogeneous mix, encompassing both new punk bands and non-punk bands like Television. It served as a "catch-all" for various emerging sounds. "era un saco donde se metía de todo, no refiero, teníamos nuevas bandas del punk, pero lo teníamos bandas que no era punk, que eran sexy o que eran de cute, como eran los propios de televisión."
**UK vs. US Terminology in Late 70s** : In the United Kingdom, the term "punk" was initially avoided due to its negative, violent, and anti-establishment connotations, especially linked to anarcho-political movements. Thus, "New Wave" differentiated non-punk underground movements from punk. In the United States, "New Wave" continued to be used almost interchangeably with "punk" for several years. "en el reino nido se diferenciaba el punk, de lo que era el punk del new wave, era lo que todo el movimiento underground, que no era el punk, en Estados Unidos todavía eran reacios, y se seguía usando el new wave casi como sinónimo de punk durante muchos años."
**Defined by CBGB and New York Scene** : In the United States, the New Wave movement was significantly defined within the context of the CBGB club and the New York music scene. This specific geographical and cultural environment played a crucial role in its early understanding and evolution. "lo que si nos ayudaba era a delimitar muy bien el movimiento new wave dentro del civil y dentro de Nueva York, en los Estados Unidos."
**Separation from Punk in the US** : Similar to the UK, the US scene, particularly at CBGB, featured both pure punk bands like Ramones and non-punk bands like Talking Heads and Blondie. Over time, "punk" became associated with Ramones-style music, and bands that didn't fit this mold were categorized as New Wave. "poco a poco en Estados Unidos se le fue perdiendo el miedo a la parada a punk y se fue quedando el punk ya para la generación de los ramones y de la gente que hacía esas versiones, lo que conocemos en el punk. Y el resto de bandas que no encajaban dentro de esa nueva enclatura eran los que estaban considerados en New Wave."
**Early 1980s Heterogeneity** : In the early 1980s, New Wave remained a highly heterogeneous movement, with compilations featuring bands as diverse as Siouxsie and the Banshees, The Cure, Joy Division, and New Order. During this period, more specific sub-genres began to emerge. "Siguiendo un movimiento muy heterogeneo porque ahí teníamos todo. La refiro teníamos desde Xi Xi y me refiro como elatorio de New Wave que me tenía de Kyur, que me tenía Joe Y Division, que me tenía New Order, me tengo un poco de todo."
**Narrowing to Synth Pop** : As other alternative and underground genres like After Punk (Post Punk) and Dark Wave developed their own names in the early 1980s, the term "New Wave" increasingly became associated with Synth Pop. This narrowing of definition particularly applied to the first generation of Synth Pop. "poco a poco el New Wave se iba quedando en la parte que en la última parte que es la que más tarde cuentardó, en coger nombre que fue Xi In Pop."
**Modern Equivalence to Early Synth Pop** : Today, the most accepted nomenclature for New Wave is almost synonymous with early Synth Pop, encompassing artists like OMD, "expression Boy" (Depeche Mode, inferred), and New Order. This typically refers to music from approximately 1978 to 1984 or 1985, also including electro-pop and techno-pop. "hoy en día yo creo que casi la nomen que la dura más aceptada para tener para hacer referencia a New Wave es casi como un sino ni modo de un primer Xi In Pop. Lo que hacía a OEMD, lo que hacía en la expresión Boy y lo que hacía New Order."
**80s US Compilations as a Mixed Bag** : In the 1980s, US New Wave compilations were notable for their eclectic mix, including bands like The Smiths, Duran Duran, New Order, Siouxsie and the Banshees, and Culture Club. These compilations labeled anything non-mainstream from the UK as New Wave. "De hecho en los 80 es curioso porque tenemos recordadores de New Wave que están editados como New Wave dentro de Estados Unidos que mezclan de lo más barrio pinto. Tienen a los Smith, tienen a Durán, tienen a New Order, tienen todo un poco mezclado."
**Recurring Application for New Movements** : Despite its evolving definition, the term "New Wave" has persistently been used to describe new waves of music in later periods. Examples include the new wave of modern psychedelia (Tame Impala) and the rock revival (The Strokes, The White Stripes, Interpol, Bloc Party). "fue una palabra que se quedó ahí un tanto atascada pero que regularmente incluso más adelante hemos seguido usando cada vez que venían nuevas oleadas de música."
**General Definition for Independent Reinterpretations** : Fundamentally, "New Wave" as a nomenclature refers to any new interpretation or independent wave of music that builds upon practically existing genres. It signifies a fresh approach to established sounds. "el New Wave como no me inclatura simplemente hace referencia cada vez que aparece una nueva interpretación, una nueva oleada independiente de una música de prácticamente ya existía."

# genre - Punk

## curiosities
**Separation from New Wave** : In the late 1970s, Malcolm McLaren, manager of the Sex Pistols, played a key role in actively separating "punk" from the broader "New Wave" movement, advocating for the specific use of the term "punk" for his associated artists. "El fondo de las primeras personas que machaco mucho en instalar la versión punk, la versión de la palabra punk para todo este movimiento."
**Negative Connotations in the UK** : In the United Kingdom, the term "punk" was initially not widely used due to its strong negative connotations, including violence and anti-establishment sentiments, and its association with anarcho-political movements. This led to "New Wave" being used for non-punk underground music in the UK. "en el reino nido la palabra punk no se usaba mucho porque tenía connotaciones muy negativas, ni aconotaciones violentas, ciertas con otaciones antisistema, porque venía a dar un poco del reino nido donde el punk estaba asociado, incluso a ciertos movimientos políticos anarquistas."
**Synonymous with New Wave in US (Initially)** : For many years, particularly in the United States, "New Wave" was almost synonymous with "punk." This is evidenced by Sire Records' 1977 compilation titled "New Wave" that included bands like Ramones, who were pure punk. "en Estados Unidos todavía eran reacios, y se seguía usando el new wave casi como sinónimo de punk durante muchos años."
**Clarification of Term in the US** : Over time in the United States, especially influenced by the scene at CBGB, the term "punk" became more specifically associated with bands like Ramones and their particular style. Bands that did not fit this narrower definition were then classified under New Wave. "se fue quedando el punk ya para la generación de los ramones y de la gente que hacía esas versiones, lo que conocemos en el punk."

# label - Sire Records

## curiosities
**First "New Wave" Compilation (1977)** : Sire Records (referred to as "Fono Grande de Estados Unidos") released what is noted as almost the first compilation album titled "New Wave" in 1977. This album was a "catch-all" that included a diverse mix of artists such as Ramones, Talking Heads, and The Residents, highlighting the early, diffuse nature of the New Wave movement. "la discográfica de la Fono Grande de Estados Unidos en el 77 se acabó un recopilatorio llamado New Wave casi el primer recopilatorio que se llama New Wave, y que es un cajón de sáster donde está todo."

# concert - CBGB

## curiosities
**Crucial in Defining US New Wave** : The CBGB club in New York was instrumental in clearly delimiting the New Wave movement within the United States. It hosted both pure punk bands like Ramones and non-punk acts such as Talking Heads and Blondie, illustrating the diverse beginnings of New Wave in the US. "lo que si nos ayudaba era a delimitar muy bien el movimiento new wave dentro del civil y dentro de Nueva York, en los Estados Unidos."

**The New New New New Pop (Book)** : A book titled "el nuevo nuevo nuevo nuevo pop" (The New New New New Pop) reviews the history of New Wave and attempts to define it, exploring the etymology of the term and the reasons for its ambiguous definition. The book is not in Spanish, but in English, and is described as interesting and quick to read. "un viejo libro que tengo que ser el nuevo nuevo nuevo nuevo pop, que repasa toda la historia del new wave y que intenta dar nombre a lo que es el new wave."
