## genres

## albums
**The College Dropout (2004) - New Way of Understanding Rap** : This album, released in 2004, marked a pivotal moment in hip hop, introducing a new way of understanding the concept of a rap artist and the content of rap lyrics. It appeared at a time when listeners were growing tired of the prevalent gangster rap image. "The College Dropout" officially initiated a new generation and what is considered a new golden age of hip hop, characterized by more intellectual and complex approaches. "Kini West está aparecido en el 2004 con ese grandísimo álbum de College Dropout en la exposición de la Universidad, la exposición del colegio en Castellano en el que abre una nueva manera de entender el concepto de artista de rap y el concepto de letras del rap."

## curiosities
**Key Figure in 21st Century Music** : Kanye West is considered a crucial figure for understanding 21st-century music, widely recognized for his importance and quality. He plays a key role in connecting and explaining the timeline of hip hop's evolution, particularly bridging the gap between classic rap and the more experimental hip hop of the 21st century. "Kini West es una de las personas clave para entender la música de siglo XXI."
**Transition from Producer to Artist** : Before becoming a solo artist, Kanye West established himself as a highly significant producer in Black music, especially hip hop, starting in the mid-1990s. He worked with prominent artists like The Notorious B.I.G., Nas, and contributed to early Jay-Z albums, notably playing a major role in Jay-Z's resurgence with "The Blueprint." "Kini West empieza a trabajar a mitad de los 90 y muy rápido se convierte en un productor realmente importante en dentro de la música negra, principalmente el hip hop pero no solo del hip hop."
**Industry Resistance to His Artist Career** : Despite his strong reputation as a producer, the music industry was initially very reluctant to support Kanye West as an artist and promote his own material. This was primarily because his personal image — educated, well-dressed, articulate, and from an accommodated background (even studying fine arts at university) — did not fit the prevailing gangster rap profile of the mid-to-late 90s, which emphasized a delinquent, street-tough persona. "Toda la industria discográfica fue muy reacia desde un primer momento a trabajar en el propio material de Kenny West y a promocionarlo como artista."
**Rejection by Labels and Artists** : Kanye West faced numerous rejections from record labels when trying to launch his artist career in the late 1990s, and even from artists he worked with, such as Jay-Z initially. He had serious discussions with Columbia Records, but they eventually backed out due to pressure from Roc-A-Fella Records, who did not want to lose him as a highly valued producer. "Lo único que consigues son negativas, negativas de las discográficas, pero también negativas a los propios artistas para los que trabajaba como en un principio el mismo J.S.E."
**Pioneer of a New Hip Hop Image and Sound** : Kanye West emerged as an artist challenging the dominant gangster rap aesthetic of the late 90s and early 2000s, which was exemplified by artists like 50 Cent, Jay-Z, and Eminem with their gold chains and tough attitudes. Kanye presented himself as well-dressed, well-spoken, and cultivated, with an exquisite vocabulary. His approach to hip hop was inspired by literature, establishing a more complex lyrical foundation that moved away from classic gangster rap themes. "Kenny West aparece justo en un momento en el que yo creo que todos estamos ya un poco cansados de esa imagen de 50 cent de Jay-Selt de Eminem incluso pues con sus caguenas y oro, con sus actitudes chulas que a con su mal educación, con sus líos, con sus armas y a veces Kenny West bien vestido, bien arreglado, un tío que habla bien, que tiene un vocabulario esquisito."
**Catalyst for the "New School" of Hip Hop** : Kanye West is credited with being the figure who connects classic rap with the truly experimental hip hop of the 21st century. His debut ushered in a "new golden age" where a diverse range of artists, inspired by his movement, revolutionized the genre. This led to a clear distinction in hip hop, with artists predating his debut often referred to as "old school" (e.g., Jay-Z, 50 Cent) and those influenced by him or emerging after, like Kendrick Lamar, Run the Jewels, and Death Grips, termed "new school." "Tenemos aquí justo en el medio la persona que conecta estos dos movimientos generales y de estilo que es el que es el mismísimo Kenny West."

"Incluso hoy en día artistas que tenemos en activo como J.S.E.T. como 50 Cent, los seguimos llamando lo old school, la vieja escuela."

# genre - Gangster Rap

## curiosities
**Dominant in the 1990s** : Gangster rap absolutely dominated the hip hop scene from the mid-1990s, becoming the "great era" of mainstream hip hop. This period brought forth major artists but also became somewhat "caricature-like" by the late 1990s, leading to a sense of exhaustion. "El rap y del hip hop de mitad de los 90, la verdad es que la época de mitad de los 90 del hip hop es una etapa un poco curiosa porque está absolutamente dominada por lo que entendemos por el gangster rap."
**Characterized by Delinquent Imagery and Themes** : Rapers in this subgenre were often depicted with gold chains, portraying a delinquent image, discussing shootings, women, aggression, prison, and originating from suburbs. This prototype of rap became deeply ingrained in public perception. "Los raperos van con cadenas de oro, son delincuente, se hablan de tiros, de tías buenas, son agresivos, es entranza de la cárcel, tienen todos estos problemas, vienen de suburbios, este prototipo un poco del rap que todos tenemos tan asentado."
**Late 90s/Early 2000s Decline and Evolution** : Towards the end of the 1990s, after many years of prominence, gangster rap began to feel "relatively exhausted" and almost "caricature-like." Artists like 50 Cent and Jay-Z, while important, represented a late evolution of this style, maintaining the "almost delinquent" image with weapons and aggressive themes, although some, like Eminem, had more "evolutive" lyrics. "Empieza a estar relativamente agotado y empieza a ser un poco casi caricaturezco y cuando ya nos vamos hacia finales de los 90 que ya tenemos a 50 cent, tenemos a J7, tenemos a muchos otros artistas muy importantes, siguen siendo una evolución un poco tardía de lo que viene a ser esos restos en el gangster rap."

# genre - Hip Hop

## curiosities
**Mid-90s Creative Context** : The mid-1990s hip hop scene was predominantly defined by gangster rap, which, despite its dominance, coexisted with some more politically charged content and early experimental mixtures that had not yet reached mainstream consumption. "Los 90 no todo era gangster rap porque teníamos algunas cosas de contenido más político, incluso teníamos ya algunas variantes del hip hop mezcladas con ya, como otras cosas."
**Early 2000s Creative Crisis and Resurgence** : Around the year 2000, hip hop experienced a "small creative crisis" or "slump" after the long dominance of gangster rap. This period set the stage for Kanye West's emergence in 2004, which introduced a new direction for the genre. "En este pequeño punto en torno al 2000 de pequeña crisis creativa que tuvimos en el hip hop entre mitad de los 90 y primeros el año 2000 que hubáis ese pequeño bache creativo dentro de la línea de tiempo del hip hop aparece Kenny West."
**The "New Golden Age" of 21st Century Hip Hop** : The 21st century brought about a "new golden age" for hip hop, officially beginning with Kanye West's first album in 2004. This era saw the emergence of a wide array of incredible artists, partly inspired by West, who revolutionized the genre. This new wave featured an "incredibly intellectual, incredibly complex" approach to rap, drawing inspirations from jazz, electronic music, soul, and funk. "Empezaría oficialmente esta nueva generación del hip hop y lo que entendemos como la nueva época dorada del hip hop que empieza precisamente en esta época del siglo XXI."
**Shift in Lyrical Themes in the New Era** : In the new golden age of hip hop, lyrics moved away from the traditional themes of shootings, women, and the Bronx. Instead, they embraced complex and "baroque" subjects like human suffering, madness, the world, and politics, reflecting a more intellectual approach that had been relegated to the background during the gangster rap era. "Dejan de hablar de tiros, dejan de hablar de tías buenas, dejan de hablar del Bronx para hablar del sufrimiento humano, para hablar de la locura, para hablar del mundo, para hablar de política, para hablar de todos estos temas tan complejos."
**"Old School" vs. "New School" Distinction** : The advent of Kanye West's artistic career in the early 2000s created a clear division in hip hop. Artists who came before this period, such as Jay-Z and 50 Cent, are now largely referred to as "old school," while those who emerged after, or were significantly influenced by Kanye West's pioneering style, are classified as "new school." "Todo lo que viene a partir de que Kenny West lo llamamos el new school o la nueva escuela del hip hop."
