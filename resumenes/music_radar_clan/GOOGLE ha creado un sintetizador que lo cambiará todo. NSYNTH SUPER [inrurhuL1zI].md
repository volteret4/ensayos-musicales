# artist - Google

## curiosities
**Project Magenta - AI Software for Synthesizers** : Google has created Project Magenta, an open-source foundational software for synthesizers based on artificial intelligence. This software learns from user interaction and aims to assist musicians, not replace them. It is freely available for download, and its public website already showcases complex modules created by users. "el proyecto de software de inteligencia artificial que usa este sintetizador que ha hecho Google y que además es totalmente libre que o cualquiera de vosotros os lo podéis descargar."
**Ensay Super - AI Synthesizer** : Google developed its own synthesizer, the Ensay Super, which is powered by Project Magenta's artificial intelligence. It is physically very similar to Korg's Kaoss Pad, possibly more interactive and tactile. Critically, the Ensay Super is not sold, manufactured, or planned for commercial release, serving primarily as a demonstration of Magenta's capabilities. "ha creado su propio sintetizador, que es el Ensign Super."
**Official Stance on Musician Replacement** : Google consistently states in its presentations, conferences, and website that it does not intend to replace musicians or create artificial musicians with its AI tools. This position is maintained despite the rapid advancements in AI capabilities. "no pretenden sustituir a los músicos ni crear un músico artificial ni nada."
**Impact on Workflow - Predictive System** : Project Magenta's AI learns a user's preferences for effects modules, sound types, and overall music-making style. Based on this learning, it generates a predictive system that anticipates the user's next desired actions, thereby simplifying the often-tedious process of working with synthesizers, especially when pre-listening is not possible. "genera un sistema predictivo en el que ya predice lo que tú vas a creer a hacer y te ayuda un poco a ir de antemano."
**Impact on Workflow - Melody Generation** : The AI in Project Magenta learns to develop and compose melodies by analyzing the user's compositional style. It creates behavioral patterns from user-inputted melodies to predict and compose new ones. This allows a user to provide just a few tonal references (e.g., three or four notes), and the AI can then generate hundreds of prepared melodies or directly develop a melody based on those guidelines, radically changing the way melodies are created with synthesizers. "aprende a desarrollar melodías y a componer melodías en base a cómo tú trabajas y compones tus melodías."
**Open-Source Nature of Magenta** : Project Magenta is designed as a completely open and free software, akin to Google's Android operating system. This open-source approach allows independent developers and other companies to integrate and build upon Magenta's AI framework, fostering widespread adoption and innovation in music technology. "Además lo bueno es que será un software libre como Android o como es casi todo lo que hace Google."
**Future Adoption by Other Brands** : Due to Project Magenta's open-source and free nature, it is anticipated that within two to three years, independent synthesizer brands, or any brand, could begin using Google's Magenta software as their foundational AI. This is considered plausible given Magenta's intuitive, predictive capabilities and its rapid learning from user musical preferences. "es posible que en menos de dos o tres años ya tengamos nuestros propios intentizadores de marcas independientes o de cualquier marca que usen de base al software de magenta de Google."

# curiosity

**AI's Foundation: Neural Networks** : Modern artificial intelligence, particularly the kind used by Google for Project Magenta and the YouTube algorithm, operates on a complex system called neural networks. This system allows machines to learn not through logical language, but through a more intricate, observational process, mimicking the way biological brains learn. "Hoy en día las máquinas no aprenden por lenguaje lógico sino que aprenden como un complejo sistema que se llama el complejo sistema de redes neuronales."
**AI Learning by Observation** : Artificial intelligence, particularly that underpinning Project Magenta, learns by observing how humans work and create. This observational learning mechanism is the core principle enabling the AI to understand and subsequently mimic or assist in creative tasks like music composition. "la inteligencia artificial solo tiene que mirar y observar cómo trabaja la gente para aprender a hacer lo que ellos hacen."
**Copyright Challenges for AI Music** : The emergence of AI-generated music, particularly automatically composed computer music, introduces new and fundamental questions regarding intellectual property rights. It prompts a re-evaluation of basic concepts like registrable music and copyright ownership for works created by artificial intelligence. "hasta qué punto va a repercutirioso al registro de derechos hasta qué punto es música registrable, música generada automáticamente por ordenador."
**Rapidity of AI Progress in Music** : Observing Project Magenta and the Ensay Super in operation quickly reveals that the timeline for machines to compose their own creative and emotional music is far shorter than commonly perceived, suggesting a leap forward comparable to 30-40 years of progress. "cuando ves a trabajar a magenta y cuando ves a trabajar en lensa y super y lo ves después de tres horas te das cuenta de que está mucho más cerca de lo que jamás pensaste y que ya estás en un mundo en el que crisis te vas a tardar 30-40 años en llegar."
**AI as a Potential Independent Composer** : While Google's official stance is against replacing artists, the capabilities of Project Magenta raise questions about the extent to which AI learning systems could eventually become completely independent in their creative processes, similar to the evolution of self-driving cars. Currently, Google maintains that AI is still far from being a real-time, emotional music composer. "hasta qué punto el sistema de aprendizaje va a llegar a un punto en el que sea de manera completamente independiente, como nos ha pasado con los coches."
**AI and Self-Driving Cars Analogy** : The evolution of Google's AI in music is implicitly compared to the development of Google Maps and self-driving cars. This analogy highlights the potential for AI technology to advance beyond its initially stated limitations, suggesting that what begins as an assistive tool might eventually achieve significant autonomy. "dejaron lo mismo del Google Maps y ahora los coches caminan solo, así que uno ya no sabe qué pensar."
