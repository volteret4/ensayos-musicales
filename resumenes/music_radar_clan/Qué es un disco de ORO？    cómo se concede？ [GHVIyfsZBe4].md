# label - RCA

## curiosities
**Conferrer of the First Gold Record** : RCA was the record label that awarded the very first documented gold record in February 1942 to Glenn Miller for his single "Chattanooga Choo Choo," commemorating its sale of 1.2 million units.
"el que concedió la RCA a Glenn Miller en febrero de 1942 por el single de chatanova chuchu"
**Pioneer of Vinyl Matrix Gold Records** : RCA had a unique practice of awarding gold records in the early days of vinyl. When a record sold so many copies that the vinyl pressing matrix wore out and needed replacement, RCA would take the last unit pressed from the old matrix before switching to new ones. This "gold record" served to commemorate the immense sales that necessitated the matrix change.
"la RCA solía coger la última unidad empresa con las planchas viejas antes de cambiar a las nuevas planchas un poco para conmemorar que había vendido tantas unidades que la matriz había estropeado y había que hacer matrices nuevas"

# curiosity

**Gold and Platinum Records - Definition and Sales Recognition** : Gold and Platinum records are the most well-known awards given to artists to recognize their sales achievements. However, their certification of sales is often misleading, as the requirements for these awards vary significantly by country and year.
"Realmente los discos de oro y de platinos son los dos más famosos que hay, se torgan como todos sabéis para dar premios a los artistas por sus ventas, pero realmente los discos de oro son una manera muy tramposa de dar certificación de las ventas a los artistas."
**Gold and Platinum Records - Awarding Body** : These awards are not granted by objective or external organizations. Instead, they are conceded by record labels to their own artists to celebrate sales, effectively serving as a marketing and publicity tool. This inherently makes them highly questionable.
"En el fondo no lo otorgan ningún organismo objetivo ni externo sino que lo conceden las discográficas a sus artistas premiando las ventas."
**Gold and Platinum Records - Questionable Nature and Decline** : Gold records have always been a highly questionable award, primarily used by record labels for artist publicity and marketing. Their lack of objective criteria and transparency has led to their practical disappearance in modern times.
"es un galardón que siempre ha sido muy questionable y que prácticamente hoy en día ha desaparecido porque se lo han cargado."
**Gold and Platinum Records - Evolving Sales Requirements in Spain** : In Spain, the requirements for Gold and Platinum albums have significantly degenerated over time. Initially, 50,000 units were required for an album award, but this has since been lowered to 40,000 and even 20,000 units. These reductions were made to ensure artists could continue receiving awards, adapting to the needs of the labels rather than true artistic recognition.
"en España por ejemplo [...] se daban los discos de oro y platino las 50 y que 1.000 unidades vendidas de un álbum pero eso ha ido evolucionando y degenerando y hoy en día se dan a las 40.000 y 20.000 unidades vendidas."
**Gold and Platinum Records - Based on Distribution, Not Sales** : A curious aspect of gold records is that they are awarded based on the number of units *manufactured and distributed* by a record label, rather than the actual number of units sold to consumers. Whether these distributed units are eventually sold, promoted, destroyed, or remain unsold for years in stores is secondary.
"los discos de oro se conceden no por los discos vendidos como piensa la mayoría de la gente sino por los discos fabricados y distribuidos."
**Gold and Platinum Records - Impact of Distribution-Based Awards** : This practice means a record label can produce 100,000 units of an album and instantly qualify it for a gold record. The actual sales performance of the album after distribution is not a factor in the award, allowing labels to grant awards based on their own interests without objective sales verification.
"si una discográfica, fabrica, produce 100.000 unidades y un álbum es un disco de oro, luego que los venda o que no los venda o que los acabe promocionando o destruyendo o que se tiren años sin venderse en las distintas de discos eso es secundario"
**Gold and Platinum Records - Data Falsification and Devaluation in the 2000s** : In the early 2000s, during a "fever" for gold records, all requirements were practically abandoned, and data was frequently falsified. Since labels themselves count the manufactured records without objective verification, gold and platinum records were given to almost anyone, driven by label interests rather than verifiable sales. This severely undermined their credibility.
"sobre todo porque a primeros de los 2000 que fue la gran fiebre de los discos de oro fue cuando se tiraron por tierra todos los requisitos para conseguirse, se falsificaban un montón de datos porque al final la discográfica lo da, la discográfica cuenta los discos que fabrica"
**Gold and Platinum Records - Modern Irrelevance** : The degeneration of the gold record system, marked by relaxed requirements and data manipulation, has rendered them largely meaningless today. Possessing a gold record now holds little practical value or recognition.
"esa degeneración ha llevado a la situación de hoy en día que hoy tener un disco de oro es como no tenerlo ya no sirve prácticamente para nada."
**Diamond Record - Origin and Ambiguity** : The "Diamond record" is another rare award that was invented in the United States several years ago. Its exact criteria and significance remain unclear.
"hay el disco de diamante que también se inventaron en Estados Unidos hace unos años y que no sabemos muy bien lo que es"
**Uranium Record - Origin and Spread** : The "Uranium record" was the latest award invented in Spain by the record label Ispavox, specifically to commemorate Rafael's achievement of 50 million copies sold, making him the only Spanish artist to reach this milestone in Spain. Despite its initially localized and somewhat arbitrary origin, the idea appealed to international record labels, and there are now five "Uranium records" worldwide.
"el último que se ha inventado que es el disco de urano que se inventó además aquí en España se lo inventó la discográfica Ispavox un poco para comemorar los 50 millones de copias de la artista español Rafael"
**Uranium Record - List of Recipients** : There are only five "Uranium records" in the world. The first was awarded to Rafael, followed by Queen for a compilation album that sold over 50 million units, Michael Jackson for *Thriller*, and AC/DC for *Back to Black*. The fifth recipient is not specified.
"Hoy en el mundo pues hay solo 5 discos de urano, está el de Rafael que os comenté que fue el primero, está el de Queen por su recopulatorio que vendió más de 50 millones de unidades, está como no el thriller de Michael Jackson y está el Back to Black de ACDC, luego hay un quinto que no tengo ni idea de cuál es la verdad es que no me acuerdo."
**Uranium Record - Inconsistent Application** : The "Uranium record" is considered very strange due to its inconsistent application. Albums like Pink Floyd's *Dark Side of the Moon* and *The Wall* have sold over 50 million actual copies but have not received this award, making the criteria for its conferral unclear.
"son muy raros porque tenemos más discos que han llegado a los 50 millones de copias vendidas reales como puede ser el Dark Side of the Moon o the Wall a los 12 de Pink Floyd que no tienen ningún tipo de disco de urano entonces son premios, son tanto extraños que no sabemos muy bien en base a que los conceden."
**Historical Sales Figures - Discrepancy between Labels and Independents** : When discussing the best-selling artists in history, there's a significant difference between sales figures claimed by record labels (often estimates) and those determined by independent organizations. These discrepancies can amount to hundreds of millions of copies.
"aquí tenemos también una gran diferencia porque por un lado están las ventas que reclaman sus discográficas que se hacen estimaciones y por otro lado las ventas que se intentan hacer mediante los independentes para saber las unidades y en algunos casos hay cientos de millones de copias de venta de diferencia de unos a otros"
**Failed Attempts to Standardize Gold Records** : Throughout the 1980s, numerous attempts were made to standardize the process for awarding gold records to bring more sense and objectivity to the system. However, these efforts proved impossible due to manipulation by record labels, the concealment of real manufacturing figures, and a consistent disregard for actual sales data, ultimately confirming gold records as merely marketing tools.
"hubo un montón de intentos sobre todo durante la época de los 80 de están revisar el proceso de llegar a las donas de los discos de oro para intentar que tuviese un poco de sentido pero ha sido algo completamente imposible al final ha habido tantas treitas por las discográficas se ha ocultado tanto información sobre las unidades fabricadas reales o se ha hecho tampoco caso a la unidad realmente vendidas que al final aunque ha habido muchos intentos estandalizar el disco de oro finalmente ha sido imposible porven al fondo no era más con la herramienta de marketing"
