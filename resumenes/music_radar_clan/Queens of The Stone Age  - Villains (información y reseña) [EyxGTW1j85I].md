## albums
**New Album (2017) - Highly Anticipated Release** : The band's new album in 2017 was eagerly awaited and generated significant discussion and expectation globally, partly due to the band's period of less activity and the emergence of parallel projects. Its production was handled by Mark Ronson and co-produced by Mark Rankin, with mixing by Alan Moulder, assuring a quality sound. "es uno de los discos que más explicación ha generado en lo que llevábamos en 2017."
**New Album (2017) - Sound Evolution and Accessibility** : The album does not represent a major artistic evolution for the band after 20 years, but its sound has evolved technically, becoming cleaner, drier, and slightly sharper, departing from the deep, reverbed sounds of their early work. This updated, fresh, agile, and faster sound is easy to listen to and makes the album accessible for younger fans, offering an entry point into the band's music. "el disco no representa una gran evolución de la banda."
**New Album (2017) - Composition and Coherence** : The overall work and Josh Homme's composition on the album are commendable, resulting in a well-crafted, solid record that recalls the band's classic elements in an updated manner. While it lacks the narrative solidity, verbal aggression, and hard guitar agility of earlier albums like 'Rated R' or the conceptual depth of '...Like Clockwork', it remains a coherent album offering good music. "es un álbum muy bien trabajado, muy sólido, muy que nos recuerda la parte más clásica de la banda pero de una manera bastante actualizada."
**New Album (2017) - Critical Reception** : Early reviews from publications like NME, Pitchfork, and All Music (inferred from "old music") are very positive, generally giving scores of around 4 out of 5 stars or between 75 and 80+ out of 100. This reception is considered excellent for a rock album released in 2017. "ya tenemos la de pitch forky la de old music y todas están yendo un poco por el mismo camino le están dando una obra maestra pero si me iba bastante buena apunto a acción entre 4 sobre 5 estrellas y sobre el 80 entre el 75 y el 80 y pico sobre 100."
**Rated R (2000) - Early Sound Characteristics** : This album featured a distinctive early sound characterized by significant echo, depth, distortion, and reverberation. Later, the band's sound evolved towards cleaner, drier, and more acute tones, moving away from this original style. "sobre todo del Rated Art que era como con tanto eco, tan profundo, tan distorsionado, con tanta reverberación."
**Rated R (2000) - Aggression and Agility** : This album was praised for its verbal aggression and strong, hard guitar agility, which contributed to making it a "completely rounded" record. This specific quality is noted as missing from the band's 2017 album. "agresividad verbal y esa agilidad y tarera tan dura y tan fuerte que tenía la mejor el rated art que la hació un disco completamente redondo."
**...Like Clockwork (2013) - Dark and Enveloping** : The band's previous album, released in 2013, was noted for its conceptual solidity and charisma. It featured a very dark, somber, and gloomy atmosphere, with enveloping guitars that were compared to David Bowie's dark style and the powerful sound of Judas Priest. "el Light Locker que el álbum de 2013 que salía muy oscuro, tenebre, lúbre con esas guitarras tan envolventes que a veces parecían en ese momento así tan oscuro de la capa de David Bowie pero a la vez tan judaspris."

"cada vez que salen disco de los Queen of Stone Age nos preguntamos un poco a qué va a sonar y qué influencias va a tener de colaboraciones."
**Career Longevity and Rock's State** : With a career spanning over 20 years, Queen of the Stone Age has been a significant force in rock music. The release of their 2017 album was met with a mix of apprehension and affection, given that rock as a genre is currently undergoing a period of evolution and is not at its peak, leading to questions about whether the band would retain its classic sounds from the mid-90s. "son más de 20 años de carrera, de lo mejor que hemos tenido en el rock, han pasado muchos años, el rock ahora mismo no está pasando con su mejor momento, todo parece que está evolucionando algo."
**Evolution of Sound (Technical vs. Artistic)** : The band's 2017 album shows a technical evolution in its sound, moving towards cleaner, drier, and slightly sharper tones, as opposed to an artistic revolution. This shift is noted by early critics using descriptors like "caramelized," "pompous," and "dry," reflecting a general trend in rock music rather than a unique band development. "el sonido ha evolucionado bastante, ya tenemos las primeras críticas del disco y todas hacen referencia conme evoluciona un poco el sonido de la banda no tanto artísticamente sino a nivel técnico y la verdad es que se están poniendo un montón de adjetivos un tanto raros como a Caramelado, pomposo, seco."
**Fan Expectations and Past Sound** : Die-hard fans of the band often yearn for the "fantastic era" of 'Rated R' and its associated chaotic sound (inferred from "de caos"), but this sound, despite its quality, is now considered somewhat outdated. After 20 years, expecting the band to revert to this older style is deemed unviable. "los más fans aferrimos, seguimos echando a lo mejor de menos esa etapa tan fantástica del rated art y de caos pero bueno han pasado ya muchos años es un sonido que ha quedado a pesar de su calidad relativamente desactualizado."
**Absence of Grand Collaborations** : The band's 2017 album is noted for not featuring the extensive and "grand" collaborations that Queen of the Stone Age has historically been accustomed to, which was a defining characteristic of some of their previous works. "tampoco tienes esas grandísimas colaboraciones a las que estamos acostumbrados en la banda."
**Enduring Appeal** : The band's 2017 album is expected to please a wide audience, including long-time fans who will appreciate the maintained essence of Queen of the Stone Age and Josh Homme's updated sound, as well as newer 21st-century rock fans who might have found the band's earlier, harder sound too intense. "la esencia de Queen of the Stone Age se mantiene el sonido de Joe's Home se mantiene completamente firme pero a la vez son completamente actualizado y al mejor es a gente que era muy aficinada al rock del siglo XXI pero que se la hacía más duro el sonido del Queen of the Stone Age."
