# artist - Royce Immorce

## member of
Demoloco

## albums
**El Rabidud (2005) - Solo Debut** : This album began recording in 2004, prompted by Royce Immorce's severe depression and her desire to avoid professional and creative stagnation after the breakup of Demoloco. Feeling unwell, she relied on two main pillars: her record label, Eco Records, which offered total freedom upon hearing her solo idea, and her friend Matthew Gerber, who served as producer for the recordings. Despite Eco Records' initial concerns regarding its commerciality, lack of strong singles, and a request for more "commercial" changes, Royce Immorce flatly refused any alterations. The label ultimately released the album unconditionally, influenced by its high quality and the label's need for new material after Demoloco. The album's visual direction and cover art, which features a personal portrait of Royce Immorce by Seymour Genghor, were entirely handled by Genghor. Although "El Rabidud" achieved only regular sales in the UK and failed in the US (a market significant for Moloco), it received widespread critical acclaim, being described as an "authentic gem," which fulfilled Royce Immorce's ambition to be a respected artist rather than a commercial star. The album was released in normal and deluxe editions on CD and vinyl. "el resultado fue bastante extraño entender un poco la estepitoza morte que tuvo un gloco moloco pero sobre todo para coger de vuelta lo que vino a ser el rabibludo en el ese morcio y de los mey 5 y entender por qué este disco no es especialmente comercial pero porque la prioridad de este disco no era tanto vender copias sino tener una crítica y ganarse el respeto de la industria siendo una persona que no era músico profesional."

## curiosities
**Early Musical Knowledge** : When Royce Immorce first met Mar Brighton, she had no prior musical knowledge or background, in contrast to Brighton who was already an established musician. "Royce Immorce no sabían nada de música."
**Post-Breakup Depression** : Following the emotional and professional breakup of Demoloco in 2002, Royce Immorce experienced a severe and debilitating depression. This period was marked by significant personal problems and a struggle to recover, ultimately serving as a catalyst for her to attempt recording new material as a way to avoid professional and creative stagnation. "Ella se quedaron fatal y lo peor es que pasa por una depresión horriblemente fuerte de la que le cuesta mucho salir."
**Refusal to Compromise on "El Rabidud"** : When Eco Records expressed doubts about the commercial viability of her solo album "El Rabidud" and suggested working with Matthew Gerber to make it more "commercial" and "more Moloco" with stronger singles, Royce Immorce delivered a "completely flat" refusal. She stated she would not change anything on the album and instructed the label not to publish it if they were dissatisfied. "la respuesta de Royce y Boercy fue completamente pajante y les dijo que no iba a cambiar a su edad de nada del disco y sino que ni han que no lo publicase."
**Relationship with Seymour Genghor** : Seymour Genghor, a renowned painter and personal friend, became the third crucial pillar for the creation of "El Rabidud," overseeing all its audiovisual creative aspects, including videoclips and album design. He personally painted Royce Immorce for the album's cover. Their professional collaboration later extended into a personal relationship, and they eventually had a child together. "También se llevaba a Royce y Boercy Seymour Genghor que al final pues tuvieron el hijo juntos, así que os podéis imaginar un poco por donde van los tiros."
**Career Focus Post-Demoloco** : After the critical success of "El Rabidud" and the final dissolution of Demoloco, Royce Immorce deliberately shifted her career focus away from commercial stardom. Her primary ambition became to establish herself as a valued and respected artist within the music industry, a goal she successfully achieved through her solo work. "Royce y Boercy siguió su carrera insolitario muy enfocada, no tanto hacer comerciales y no ser un artista muy respetada profesionalmente que concreces algo que ha cumplido."
**Final Stance on Moloco Reunion** : In an interview given after the release of the "Aléol Cataló" compilation, Royce Immorce unequivocally closed the door on any future reunion with Mar Brighton. She stated that while "life is very long" and "Moloco may return," she personally did not want to go back with Mar Brighton, and they have had no contact since. "La vida es muy larga, puede tomar el moloco pero yo al menos no quiero volver con Marraikov y de hecho desde esas no tiene praten en contacto entre ellos."

# artist - Mar Brighton

## member of
Demoloco

## curiosities
**Pre-Demoloco Career** : Before co-founding Demoloco, Mar Brighton was already a relatively well-known musician. He had a notable history of working musically with several important figures in the industry, including George, Fr. Jimi Lippo, and Cabrero el Ter. "Mar Brighton ya había trabajado musicalmente con gente bastante importante como George, como Fr. Jimi Lippo como Cabrero el Ter, y empecé bastante conocido."
**Despotic Treatment of Royce Immorce** : One of the principal reasons cited for the breakup of Demoloco in 2002 was Mar Brighton's despotic behavior towards Royce Immorce. He reportedly did not consider her a "real musician," frequently prevented her from singing, and severely limited her ability to contribute creatively to the group. "Una de las principales razones por las que se separaron fue un poco el trato despota que Mar Brighton siempre había tenido a Royce Immorce, entre otras cosas porque como no la consideraba una música de verdad, de dejar a cantar y no dejaba a abortar prácticamente nada al grupo."
**Post-Demoloco Career** : Following the final separation of Demoloco, Mar Brighton's professional career significantly receded from the public eye. He worked independently with various artists, undertook some production roles, and performed as a DJ under different names. However, he is largely considered to have "died professionally" compared to his previous prominence. "Marraikov ha quedado un poco en la sombra, ha trabajado con algunos nombres de manera independiente, ha producido alguna gente, trabajo como DJ y como distintos nombres pero él prácticamente ha muerto en el animal."

## albums
**Estaus (2002) - Final Studio Album** : This album was recorded in 2002 under strained circumstances, following the personal and professional breakup of Royce Immorce and Mar Brighton. The recording sessions were described as chaotic, very short, tense, and fraught with arguments, largely driven by contractual obligations with Eco Records for two more albums. Despite the tumultuous production, "Estaus" sold quite well, partially due to the public interest generated by the band's prior internal conflicts. However, critical reception was predominantly negative, with many reviewers considering it the weakest album of Moloco's career, although some still found merit in it. The album's lyrics reportedly reflect the "hatred" and "rancor" between the band members. Its release was immediately followed by the band's official statement of dissolution. "El disco de estatus emoroco se vendió bastante bien, en parte por el dirón que habían tenido anteriormente, pero la crítica, si viendo lo puso mal fue un poco el disco que peor puso sobre la carrera de Moloco, aunque la resca a mí se hubiera gustado."
**Aléol Cataló (2006) - Official Compilation** : Released in 2006, "Aléol Cataló" stands as the only official compilation album by Moloco. Its creation was primarily driven by Eco Records to fulfill a remaining contractual obligation, after a brief and unsuccessful reunion attempt in 2006 proved recording new material impossible due to the intense animosity between Royce Immorce and Mar Brighton. Consequently, the compilation contained no previously unreleased tracks. To promote the album, the band performed a few small concerts and conducted a couple of interviews, though these promotional activities were often carried out separately, as the members "couldn't even see each other." "así es como en el 2006 aléol cataló, que fue el único recopelatólico oficial que sacó moloco."

## curiosities
**Formation and Early Success** : Demoloco (also referred to as Moloco) was founded by Royce Immorce and Mar Brighton shortly after they met, concurrently becoming a romantic couple. The band enjoyed a successful run of seven years, achieving both strong commercial sales and positive critical reception, before internal issues led to their initial breakup. "La cosa pasó bastante bien y el poco de conocerse fundaron Moloco, aparte de gran pareja sentimental, y durante 7 años tuvieron bastante éxito tantas mentas como en crítica."
**2002 Breakup and Contractual Obligations** : In 2002, the romantic and professional relationship between Royce Immorce and Mar Brighton dissolved, leading to the band's initial separation. Despite this, they remained contractually bound to Eco Records for two more albums. This obligation compelled them to reunite under duress to record "Estaus" and also contributed to their ambiguous messaging about a potential return in early interviews, as neither was confident in their ability to launch successful solo careers and wanted to avoid contract breaches. "El problema viene en 2002 cuando la pareja se rompe por motivos personales pero también profesionales."
**Ambiguous Reunion Messages** : Following the recording of "Estaus" and their initial breakup, Royce Immorce and Mar Brighton gave interviews where they stated they were separating but intentionally left the door open for a future return. This ambiguous stance was strategic, serving to avoid breaching their contract with Eco Records for a still-pending album and reflecting their uncertainty about their individual professional prospects outside of Moloco. "Y en esas entrevistas dieron un mensaje bastante ambigo, porque se separaban, pero dejaban mucho la puerta abierta a volver."
**2006 Strained Reunion** : In 2006, Demoloco briefly reunited for a few months, not to create new material, but primarily because Eco Records demanded a final album to fulfill their contract. The label, being relatively small, considered Moloco a crucial financial asset from the 1990s. However, the members' intense animosity, exacerbated by Royce Immorce's newfound "euphoria" after her solo success with "El Rabidud," rendered new recordings impossible. This reunion instead resulted in the compilation album "Aléol Cataló" and a few separate promotional appearances. "Se juntaron en el 2006 y no les del 2005 para empezar a hacer algunas cosas por la agración de material fue completamente imposible."
**Post-Aléol Cataló Interviews** : After the release of the "Aléol Cataló" compilation, interviews with Royce Immorce and Mar Brighton were largely conducted separately due to their extreme animosity, as they "couldn't even see each other." It was during these interviews that Royce Immorce definitively clarified her stance, stating she would not work with Mar Brighton again, effectively signaling the irreversible end of Moloco. "Tan más se llevaban que gran parte de las entrevistas que hicieron en el cataló con las hicieron los dos mil vósporos separados porque ya no se podía ni ver."

# artist - Matthew Gerber

## curiosities
**Collaborator and Producer for Royce Immorce** : Matthew Gerber is an influential artist, producer, and graphic designer who played a crucial role as a collaborator and producer for Royce Immorce's solo debut album, "El Rabidud." He maintained a strong working relationship with Royce Immorce, having previously contributed remixes for Demoloco, collaborated with the band, and attended various festivals and concerts alongside them. "Matthew Gerber que aparte de ser un artista es importantísimo, productor mientras música gráfica y ya tenía una buena relación con Royce and Boercy."
**Industry Reputation** : Within the music industry, Matthew Gerber is highly regarded for his pleasant demeanor and professional qualities. He is known for his "easy-going temperament," being "pleasant," "easy to work with," and possessing a "good character." These specific attributes were precisely what Royce Immorce sought and needed during the challenging period of recording her solo album. "Matthew Gerber para el que no lo sepa dentro de esta industria tiene una fama bastante buena, tiene jamás un teof fácil, les anteo a nadable, les anteo a cualquier espacin trabajar y tener muy muercalácter, con lo cual para ella era precisamente lo que necesitaba."

# artist - Seymour Genghor

## curiosities
**Painter and Audiovisual Director for "El Rabidud"** : Seymour Genghor, a personal friend of Royce Immorce and a well-known painter in the United Kingdom, was responsible for all the audiovisual creative work for Royce Immorce's solo album, "El Rabidud." His contributions included designing videoclips and personally painting Royce Immorce for the album's cover art, a painting that genuinely exists. "Justo cuando se está trabajando en la versión final de lo que va a ser el rabidud sale el tercer pilar sobre que se va a ser este disco, que es el pintoria artista Seymour Genghor, el es un amigo personal de Royce y Boercy aparte de un pintor bastante conocido dentro del rey unido y llevó todo el trabajo creativo a nivel audiovisual del disco."
**Personal Relationship with Royce Immorce** : Beyond his significant professional contributions to "El Rabidud," Seymour Genghor developed a deeper personal relationship with Royce Immorce. They eventually had a child together, indicating the evolution of their bond beyond artistic collaboration. "También se llevaba a Royce y Boercy Seymour Genghor que al final pues tuvieron el hijo juntos, así que os podéis imaginar un poco por donde van los tiros."

# label - Eco Records

## curiosities
**Contractual Leverage over Demoloco** : As a relatively small label, Eco Records held significant contractual leverage over Demoloco, which had been a major source of income for them throughout the 1990s. This financial dependence allowed the label to enforce the remaining two-album contract even after the band's initial breakup in 2002, leading to the strained recording of "Estaus" and later attempts to secure a final album. "Eco Records han tenido un disco pendiente como loco y teniendo en cuenta que era una escográfica bastante pequeña y que moloco la había dado muchísimo dinero de la narrativa de dos 90 no era algo que iba a dejar escapar tan fácilmente."
**Decision to Release "El Rabidud" Unconditionally** : Despite initial reservations about the commercial appeal of Royce Immorce's solo album "El Rabidud" and the absence of clear hit singles, Eco Records ultimately decided to release it unconditionally. This decision was influenced by two key factors: the label's need for new material following the collapse of Demoloco, making Royce Immorce's album their best chance for profit, and the undeniable high quality of the album, which indicated it would likely be quickly picked up by another label if Eco Records refused. "El primero es que el corrector se ha faltado de discos de moloco que ha tratado de importantes para ellos, el disco de Royce y Boercy era lo mejor a lo que se ponían a ganar y la segunda razón es que el disco era de muy buena calidad y era muy probable que en poco tiempo quedase en otra disco gráfica."
