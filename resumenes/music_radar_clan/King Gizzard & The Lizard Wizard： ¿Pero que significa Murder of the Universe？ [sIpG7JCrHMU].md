## albums
**Marderoth de Universe (2017) - Second of Five Albums** : This album, released recently in 2017, is the second installment of a projected five albums the band aimed to release within that year. It has left listeners deeply disconcerted, raising more questions than answers. The album is conceptual, divided into three seemingly independent stories, a format that was popular in the late 1960s but has since declined. Musically, it is described as incredibly complex and well-worked, featuring three distinct styles: epic progressive rock for the first part, epic heavy metal for the central part, and 70s psychedelic rock for the third part, all linked by an interesting musical thread despite the lyrical disconnections. A significant critique is the use of extensive spoken word narration by a man and a woman, which is said to detract from the music. "Este disco así solo te deja con una especie de cara de poquerde no entender nada."

## songs
**Taylor de Restlet Pist (2017)** : This is the first of three distinct stories featured on the album "Marderoth de Universe". It narrates the tale of a man-beast, where a bestial part of a man gradually possesses his human side, battling societal repression, ultimately leading to the beast consuming the man and the man-beast's demise. The narrative style is compared to the psychological themes explored by Pink Floyd, but the story is perceived as too short and lacking a solid concept, making it difficult to fully understand or enjoy its purpose. "Nos recuerda mucho a esa manera psicológica que tenía pinflos y de hablar de la psicología humana."
**Lord of Light y versus Balrog (2017)** : This is the second story on the album "Marderoth de Universe", completely unrelated to the first. It tells of a demigod with lightning powers who accidentally destroys a village he is supposed to protect. The villagers, believing him evil, witness him strike a dead man, who then transforms into a powerful beast named Balrog. A fierce battle ensues between the Lord of Light and Balrog, with the Lord of Light emerging victorious but deciding to leave the village to prevent further harm. The story is likened to epic heavy metal fantasy narratives, but its brevity and lack of coherence are criticized, making it feel more like a simplified clash such as Harry Potter versus Voldemort. "Parece más la batalla de Harry Potter contra Voldemort que realmente una historia épica."
**Han Tumía de Márderos de Universe (2017)** : This is the third story on the album, which also lends its name to the album itself. It is entirely unrelated to the previous two narratives. The story features a future robot living in a cyber-digital world who becomes obsessed with human conditions it cannot experience, specifically vomiting and dying. The robot, unable to die more than once, creates a "robot son" with the sole purpose of incessantly vomiting. The son rebels, leading to a robotic symbiosis between the two that mysteriously collapses, magically spreading vomit across the universe before they die. This story is described as surreal and artistic but suffers from a lack of argumentative coherence, leaving many questions about the motivations and events. "Es una historia como muy surrealista, muy artística, pero que al margen de todo eso no tiene escesiva coherencia argumental."

## curiosities
**Australian Origin and Rising Popularity** : The band is from Australia and, despite having primarily operated within alternative and independent circles, their popularity is rapidly increasing. Their growth is compared to the early stages of Arquit Fayer, a band known for being artistic, eccentric, and independent. "Es una banda australiana que todavía apenas ha salido de los tírculos alternativos e independientes, pero que la popularidad está creciendo como la espuma."
**Prolific Output and 2017 Album Goal** : King Gisart and Elisar Wysart are exceptionally prolific, releasing new material roughly every 8 to 9 months. In 2017, they set an ambitious goal to release five albums within the year, a target considered overwhelming by many. "Para este 2017 se ponían como objetivo hacer 5 álbumes en un año."
**Complex and Artistic Style** : The band is recognized for its highly artistic approach and creation of complex musical pieces. Their album "Marderoth de Universe" is cited as an example of their intricate and well-crafted musical compositions. "King Gisart es un grupo muy arti, que hace cosas verdaderamente complejas."
**Limited Interviews and Future Clarity** : Due to their relatively emerging status, there are very few interviews or public declarations available from the band. It is speculated that a deeper understanding of "Marderoth de Universe" and its interconnected stories, particularly regarding the narrative coherence, might only become clear with the release of the third album in their 2017 series. "Tenemos muy pocas entrevistas de ellos o muy pocas declaraciones de ellos porque todavía no son tan importantes."
**Detrimental Spoken Word Narration** : Despite the album's strong musicality, a significant criticism is directed at the extensive spoken word narrative segments featuring a man and a woman, which are integrated with the sung tracks. These narrative portions are perceived as detracting from the musical experience, overwhelming the otherwise impressive compositions. "Un hilo argumental musical increíble, potentísimo que se cargan con unas interminables para fadas de gente hablando."

# curiosity

**Conceptual Album Decline** : The conceptual album, a format where an album is built around a central theme or narrative, was particularly fashionable and produced many masterpieces in the late 1960s. However, its popularity significantly declined starting from the 1980s, making its return in modern music a risky venture that can either be brilliant or a complete disaster. "A partir de los 80, el álbum conceptual ha caído muy en declive."
