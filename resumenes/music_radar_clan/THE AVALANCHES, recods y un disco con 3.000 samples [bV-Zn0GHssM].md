## albums
**Sin-Sail-Few (2000) - Debut Album** : This album, referred to as "Sin-Sail-Few," was the first record released by the Australian band "Diabalanches" in 2000. It was considered extremely important in the music market, widely recognized as one of the best albums of that year, and in many rankings, it is cited as the best electronic music album in history. Its creation involved the unprecedented use of over 3000 fragments or samples from other existing records, a process that no one had ever taken to such an extreme. "Sin-Sail-Few" is still credited as the most complex sampling album ever made.
"Ese disco fue importantísimo del mercado musical y sobre todo fue uno de los mejores discos del año y está puesto en muchos rancos y el mejor disco es la historia de la electrónica sacan este disco de los abanches."

**Second Album (2016) - Follow-up After 16 Years** : After a 16-year hiatus following their debut "Sin-Sail-Few," "Diabalanches" released their second album in 2016. This album also received significant critical acclaim, though some critics remarked that it was perhaps too "continuist" or similar to the sound of their first record from 16 years prior.
"Hace dos años en el 2016 se acababan su segundo disco que también tuvo una grandísima crítica. Hubo quien era la mejor la chaco que era demasiado continuista con un disco que habían hecho hace 16 años."

## curiosities
**Australian Origins** : The band "Diabalanches" is an Australian group that emerged in the late 1990s, forming from members of other existing bands, a common occurrence for many musical groups.
"Diabalanches es una banda australiana que aparece a finales de los 90, de recilada de otras bandas como la mayoría."

**Guinness World Records Recognition for Sampling Complexity** : The album "Sin-Sail-Few" is recognized in the Guinness World Records for its unprecedented use of over 3000 fragments or samples from other existing records. This extensive sampling process had never been taken to such an extreme before, making it conceptually the most complex sampling album ever created.
"Este disco también está reconocido porque están en el recorgínes, porque se hizo con más de 3000 fragmentos y otros discos existentes."

**Act of Protest in the Music Industry** : The creation of "Sin-Sail-Few" with over 3000 samples inadvertently transformed the album into an act of protest within the recording industry. The sheer volume of samples made it impossible to trace and register all rights holders, highlighting the impracticality of traditional copyright licensing for such complex works. This circumstance made the album a significant point of contention regarding copyright management in electronic music.
"Ese disco se acabó convirtiendo casi en un acto de protesta dentro de la industria discográfica."

**Dozens of Album Versions Due to Copyright Challenges** : There is no single definitive version of "Sin-Sai-Lefiu" by "Diabalanches"; instead, dozens of versions exist. This phenomenon occurred because as rights were progressively credited and licensed, samples were either removed or added over time, often due to legal challenges. Consequently, different editions of the album purchased in the UK, America, or at various times, are subtly distinct.
"No existe un disco de diábalanches de Sin Sai Lefiu, existen de zenas de versiones, porque según se han ido acreditando los derechos y permitiendo los derechos han ido cambiando."

**Massive Copyright Management Attacks** : The album "Sin-Sail-Few" faced massive attacks related to copyright management that required extensive processing. Many rights holders found it frustrating to try and license parts of the album they couldn't even recognize, leading to a complex and often unresolved legal quagmire.
"El disco sufre ataques masivos de gestiones de derechos autor que tuvieron que ir tramitándose."

**Legal Outcome - Over 100 Credited, 2900 Uncredited Samples** : The album "Sin-Sail-Few" became an "absolute nonsense" in terms of rights management. While "Diabalanches" had to modify the album several times and ended up crediting over 100 individuals, approximately 2900 samples remained uncredited and unlicensed. This outcome was seen as a significant victory for those who advocated for the free use of music fragments.
"Tuvieron que reconocer a decenas a casi más de 100 personas estrenadentes en este disco por ejemplo, si han sido reconocidos. 2.900 nunca lo han sido."

**Victory for Free Use of Music Fragments** : The complex and often controversial journey of "Sin-Sail-Few" through copyright challenges was ultimately seen as a "grandísima victoria" for individuals who championed the free use of breakbeats and music fragments in artistic creation. The album's ability to largely persist despite numerous claims demonstrated the difficulties in strictly enforcing copyright on highly fragmented sampled works.
"Fue una grandísima victoria, yo creo que para la gente que defendía el uso libre del brecvito de los fragmentos de música."

**Highly Recognized in Australia and a Reference Point** : Despite the legal complexities and a long gap between albums, "Diabalanches" are incredibly well-recognized in Australia. For lovers of music from the late 1990s and early 2000s, they are considered a significant reference point, influencing many artists in the electronic music scene.
"Son gente increíblemente reconocida en Australia y para los amantes de la música de finales de 1990, primero es el 2000, son un referente."

**Broke Barriers of Musical Definition** : "Diabalanches" is highlighted as one of the bands that significantly helped "throw down many barriers" concerning what is considered music and what is not. Their innovative and extreme approach to sampling expanded the understanding of music composition and artistic creation.
"Una de las bandas que nos ha ayudado muchísimo a tirar muchas barreras de lo que consideramos música y lo que no consideramos música."

# artist - DJ Shadow

## albums
**Introducing (1996) - Pioneering Sample-Based Album** : Released in 1996, DJ Shadow's album, "Introducing," marked a pivotal moment in music history by being the first record made entirely from fragments of old, existing discs. This pioneering work fundamentally changed the trajectory of electronic music and, in part, the entire history of music, establishing a new conceptual framework for composition.
"DJ Shadow fue el primero que hizo un disco que solo estaba hecho con trozos y otros discos antiguos existentes."

## curiosities
**Historical Figure and Turning Point** : DJ Shadow is considered a historical figure in music, with his album "Introducing" serving as a significant "point of inflexión" or turning point in music history. His groundbreaking approach to creating an entire album solely from samples conceptually altered many perspectives within electronic music, inspiring numerous artists who followed his lead.
"DJ Shadow está considerado histórico, es un uno de sus puntos de inflexión en la historia de la música."

# artist - Madonna

## curiosities
**Mature Reaction to Sample Use of "Jolida"** : Madonna displayed a "surprisingly mature" reaction when informed that "Diabalanches" had used a fragment from her song "Jolida" in their album. Unlike many rights holders, she did not allow publication without first hearing the material, but once satisfied, she requested no accreditation, money, or any other claim, only wishing to approve how her work was utilized. This approach challenged public perceptions of her as being solely money-driven.
"Madonna tuvo una reacción sorprendentemente madura de cómo se deberían mejor gestionar los derechos de autor."

**Challenged Public Perception of Copyright Management** : Madonna's response to the sampling of her song "Jolida" by "Diabalanches" challenged the common perception of her as being solely focused on financial gain. Her willingness to approve the use of her material without demanding monetary or crediting compensation, provided she heard it first, offered a vision of a "healthier" and more collaborative approach to copyright management, differing from typical legal and aggressive reactions.
"Nos dio también gracias a Dios Madonna una visión de que no siempre tenemos que racionar de la misma manera legalizada y atacante ante el uso de derechos de autor y que puede haber una manera sanado sobre los derechos de autor."

# genre - Electrónica

## curiosities
**Transformative Impact of Sampling** : The emergence of albums like DJ Shadow's "Introducing" and "Diabalanches'" "Sin-Sail-Few" fundamentally transformed electronic music. DJ Shadow pioneered the idea of an entire album made from samples, while "Diabalanches" pushed this to an extreme with over 3000 fragments, establishing new conceptual perspectives and becoming a landmark in the genre's history.
"Este disco cambió para la historia de la electrónica y en parte toda la historia de la música."

# genre - Hip Hop

**Early Use of Sampling** : Hip hop music is recognized as one of the earliest genres to extensively utilize sampling. Its rhythmic bases, in particular, were born from the practice of incorporating fragments and existing records, laying foundational groundwork for later, more complex sampling techniques.
"Siempre se habían usado fragmentos y discos existentes en la música, el hip hop, todas las bases rímpicas nacieron así."

# genre - Vaporwave

## curiosities
**Definition as Musical Recycling** : Vaporwave is defined as a musical genre that involves taking "completely exhausted, outdated, and antiquated melodies," then giving them a "facelift" to renew and bring their essence to the present. This process is likened to the "recycling of music," representing a modern approach to composition by reinterpreting existing sounds.
"El vaporwave que es, el vaporwave es coger melodías completamente agotadas desfasadas y que se han quedado atrasadas, darles un lavado de cara a renovar la cita de la salsa al presente, es como el reciclaje de la música."

# curiosity

**Copyright Management Challenges with Extreme Sampling** : The use of over 3000 samples in a single album, as seen with "Diabalanches'" "Sin-Sail-Few," exposed a critical flaw in traditional copyright management. The sheer volume of fragments made it impossible to trace, register, and obtain licenses from thousands of rights holders, transforming the album into an unexpected act of protest against the existing legal framework and demonstrating the impracticality of applying conventional rules to highly fragmented works.
"3.000 samples no son rastreables, no son registrables o sea tú no puedes hablar con 3000 propietarios de derechos."

**Diverse Reactions to Copyright Claims** : The copyright claims against "Diabalanches'" album "Sin-Sail-Few" revealed a wide spectrum of reactions from rights holders, highlighting that not everyone responds to copyright infringement in the same way. Some initiated legal attacks, resulting in their recognition as co-authors and receiving economic compensation, while others recognized their material but took no action, or requested recognition without making any financial claims.
"Hubo gente propietaria de derechos que atacó legalmente a los ablanches y tuvieron que ser reconocidos y de hecho hay algunos artistas de ese estilo que aparecen reconocidos como coautores de las canciones y que reciben su acreditación económica."

**New Ways of Composing Music in the 21st Century** : Modern music composition, particularly in the 21st century, has evolved beyond traditional methods like playing a melody on a piano. It now extensively involves creating new sounds from existing ones, taking fragments of what already exists, reinterpreting it, and giving it a fresh perspective, akin to musical recycling. Albums like "Sin-Sai-Lefio" serve as prime examples of this innovative compositional approach.
"Hoy en día componer música se hace a base de crear sonidos nuevos en base a unos ya existentes o a coger trozos algo que ya existe, a reinterpretar lo que ya existe, a darle un lavado de cara."
