## albums
**Último Disco - Unnamed (Current)** : This recent album has caused extreme reactions within the music industry and among fans, leading to widespread disappointment and difficulty in assessing its quality. It represents a drastic stylistic change for the band, described by some as a "professional suicide," while others view it as a logical evolution if considering their other projects like The Last Shadow Puppets. The album's style, heavily influenced by Gainsbourg, Bowie, James Bond, and Kubrick films, moves between retro reinterpretation and a direct retro sound, sometimes bordering on "elevator music." Critics note it doesn't sound like a cohesive band effort, but rather like a solo project by Alex Turner, and that it loses some of the band's essential DNA, failing to maintain a core essence while reinventing. "Estabiendo reacciones muy extremas, está viendo como mucho opinión muy difícil de asumir, está viendo como un sentimiento de excepción muy generalizado en parte de la crítica y también en parte de los fans."
**Tranquility Base Hotel & Casino (Year Unspecified)** : This album is explicitly identified as a crucial reference point for understanding the new stylistic direction of the Arctic Monkeys, serving as a precursor to their latest work. The question is posed about its role within the band's timeline. "El primero es que papel juega el Tranquilitiba Houtel en casino último al bóndalo de los Artic Monkeys dentro de la línea temporal de los propios Artic Monkeys."
**AM (Year Unspecified)** : Considered by a significant portion of critics in Europe, and NME and El Rhino Nido magazines, as one of the band's best albums, almost on par with "Humbug," receiving 10/10 ratings. In Europe, it was a Top 5 album in nearly all publications for its year. However, in the United States, some critics viewed it as a "superstar" album that didn't contribute much creatively. "Aquí en Europa el AEM está considerado también uno de los mejores álbumes de los Artic Monkeys casi a nivel del Amburg, de hecho NEM. La propia revista NEM y el Rino Nido le daban 10 sobre 10."
**Humbug (Year Unspecified)** : This album is regarded as possibly the peak or "obra cumbre" of the Arctic Monkeys' discography. Following "Humbug," the band found itself in a situation where they had no more trajectory if they didn't try new things. "Después de haber hecho el Amburg, que es posiblemente la obra cumbre que han tenido los Artic Monkeys, está en una situación en la que ya no tenía mucho más recorridos y no probaban cosas nuevas."

## curiosities
**Early 2000s Indie Rock Revival Context** : The Arctic Monkeys emerged as part of the early 2000s rock revival alongside bands like Franz Ferdinand, Interpol, Block Party, and The Strokes. Like many of their contemporaries, the band initially operated within a predictable musical trajectory, solidifying and improving their style without venturing too far from their comfort zone. "Cuando pensamos en la generación en la que salían los Artic Monkeys, todas las bandas estaban un poco en esa situación. Afiró, Franfernan, Interpol, Block Party, los Strokes, todas estas bandas que salían a primeros del 2000, en este rival del Rock, el que formaban parte de los Artic Monkeys."
**Alex Turner's Dominance in Latest Album** : The recent work gives the strong impression that Alex Turner is predominating, and the album sounds more like a great musician (Turner) with a backing band rather than a solid, united group. This perception stems from the fact that Turner practically composed and recorded the album in solitary, and for the first time, he is credited as a producer without the involvement of other band members. "La sensación de que tenemos unos artimon que dicen los que tenemos a un líder al externer que está predominando, sobre todo. Y en el fondo nos está pasando el disco que no suena a un gran músico que le sea al externer con una banda de apoyo que podía ser el resto de artimon que es un líder."
**Live Performance on Jimmy Fallon** : During their first performance of the new material on Jimmy Fallon, the band of tracks appeared more like an accompanying orchestra or a backing band, similar to those David Bowie had, rather than the solid band sound that the Arctic Monkeys had always projected. This reinforced the idea of Alex Turner's dominant role. "Y ahora la primera actuación que hacía en los artichmon que hice este nuevo trabajo en el show de Jimmy Fallon y lo veíamos muy claro. Y una banda de tracks que parecía más una orquesta de acompañamiento parecía más una banda de acompañamiento como las que tuvo David Bowie y no tanto una banda sólida que es el sonido que siempre ha mientido en los artichmon que hice."
**Evolution vs. Radical Change Dilemma** : The band's drastic stylistic change is seen by some as a "professional suicide" if viewed solely through the lens of their previous albums. However, if one considers their side projects, such as The Last Shadow Puppets' recent album, the shift appears as a "not predictable but quite logical" evolution, indicating a broader vision for the band's trajectory. "Si nosotros consideramos que los álbumes del ASEADO PAPETS es parte de la escorrafía de Artic Monkeys, este álbum es un salto menos al vacío de lo que pueda aparecer."
**Creative Ambition vs. Public Expectation** : As artists, the Arctic Monkeys are still young and professionally ambitious, and they are not obligated to create music solely to please a 20-year-old audience, especially as established millionaires. Instead, they should produce works that they creatively consider professionally fulfilling, despite the importance of pleasing their audience. "Un artisan no tiene por qué vivir obsesionado con agradar a su público y es bastante sana exactitud."
**Loss of Band Essence in New Album** : While the band has evolved and taken a leap, they have lost a part of their essence, which is regrettable as many artists who reinvent themselves manage to retain their core DNA. Unlike Beck or Radiohead's Kid A, where a pure essence was maintained, the Arctic Monkeys failed to keep a thread of their unique identity in their latest work. "Es una pena que los artidmonks hayan hecho este salto al vacío sin saber mantener una parte de su ADN que es interesante para mantener un hilo conductor en la discuracia de la banda."
**Blurring Line of Retro Reinterpretation** : The new album navigates a very fine line between retro reinterpretation and directly adopting a non-reinterpreted retro sound. The band heavily relies on classic sounds from Gainsbourg and retro-futurism reminiscent of Kubrick films, but often lacks a reinterpretation vision. This places the album on the edge of sounding like "elevator music" rather than a fresh take on the past. "Es una línea muy fina en la que al estar en la banda se basa muchísimo en esos sonidos clásicos de Gamesburg y del retro futurismo que a veces recuerda a las películas de Kubrick, pero sin embargo no tiene muchas veces esa visión de reinterpretación, esa inversión reinterpretada."

# curiosity

**Bands Leaving Their Comfort Zone - The "Jump into the Void" Metaphor** : Many bands reach a point where they feel they have exhausted their creative path within their comfort zone, leading them to make a "jump into the void" or radical stylistic change. This can be seen as either a "professional suicide" or a necessary reinvention, with examples like U2's "Zooropa" and Radiohead's "Kid A" initially receiving severe criticism but ultimately leading to new, exciting trajectories. "Algunas lo deciden hacer progresivamente y otras saltando una manera más agresiva."
**Leader-Dominated vs. Solid Band Structures** : Bands can be broadly categorized into those with a very powerful leader who creatively drives the group, and those that are much more solid and united. Examples include Oasis with the Gallagher brothers as a strong tandem, versus the current perception of Arctic Monkeys as an Alex Turner-dominated project. "Tenemos bandas en las que hay un líder muy potente, que es el que día a la banda lleva la banda creativamente y tenemos bandas que son mucho más sólidas y mucho más unidas."
**Solo Work Under Band Name** : It is not uncommon for individual band members to release solo work, sometimes even under the band's name, while the band is on hiatus or in a period of transition. The unnamed keyboardist from The Prodigy released a solo album, "Lou e Sad Burn, Everton Gold," using the Prodigy name despite no other members participating, which highlights a contentious strategy. "Sacó el nombre de prodigian y los sacó el nombre de solitario."
**Long-Term Band Consistency vs. Caricature** : It is very difficult for a band to maintain musical relevance for more than 10 years while staying within their comfort zone without eventually becoming a caricature of themselves. While exceptions like The Pet Shop Boys and The Rolling Stones exist, more often, bands struggle with repetitiveness and creative stagnation if they don't evolve or reinvent. "Es muy difícil que una banda consigue estar más de 10 años haciendo música dentro de su tonal de confort y que no acabe haciendo casi una caricatura de sí misma."
**Current State of Rock Music Production** : There is a current scarcity of new classic rock or old-school rock production. When bands like the Arctic Monkeys, to whom many fans clung for new rock material, release a drastically different album, it contributes to a sense of disappointment and a perception that "rock is even more dead." "Cuando todo el mundo se estaba agarrando a los trabajos de los artidmonks como un clavo ardiendo de aquí iba a ser el nuevo material de rock clásico, un poco de rock de vieja escuela. Vienen con este y algo un poco una sensación debajo, es como si ya el rock estaba muy morto como que lo están entrando un poco más."
