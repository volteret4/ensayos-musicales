## albums
**Some Girls (1978) - Comeback Album** : This album was a crucial turning point in the band's career, released in 1978 after a decade of critically indifferent albums that had led them to be labeled "rock dinosaurs." It was an effort to regain critical acclaim and reassert their status. Keith Richards contributed minimally due to a pending drug trial in Canada, leaving Mick Jagger to largely compose it solo. The album incorporated strong influences from disco and punk, current genres at the time, blending them with their classic rock sound. It achieved tremendous critical and commercial success and is now considered their second-best album after "Exile on Main St." "Este disco es alucinante, fue un punto muy importante dentro de la carrera de los Rolling Stones."

## curiosities
**Critical Slump and "Rock Dinosaurs" Label in the 1970s** : In the decade leading up to "Some Girls," The Rolling Stones, despite having many hits, saw their albums largely ignored by critics. This period of critical indifference led to the band earning the "rock dinosaurs" label. "los Rolling Stones tuviese un montón de desitambentas, pero críticamente no hubiese entendido cesigacariación los discos."
**Keith Richards' 1977 Drug Arrest and Trial** : Keith Richards was significantly less involved in the creation of "Some Girls" because, in 1977, he experienced a severe drug relapse. He was facing a drug possession trial in Canada, which required him to travel frequently for declarations, making him largely unavailable for the album's composition, which Mick Jagger mostly handled. "Richard trabajó muy poco en el álbum, porque el año anterior en 1977 había tenido un revá con las drogas, estaba pendiente de un juicio, a dor posicion de Rolling Stones, en Canadá."
**Ron Wood Joins the Band** : A significant lineup change occurred before the recording of "Some Girls" with Mick Taylor's departure. Ron Wood, previously of the band Faces, joined as his replacement, bringing new ideas and a "breath of fresh air" to the group, despite not being considered as technically proficient a musician as Taylor. "mi telor había abandonado poco antes del grupo y había entrado en su sustitución Rockwood, que venía al grupo de Faces."
**"Some Girls" Original Album Cover Controversy** : The original cover for "Some Girls," designed by an American artist as a blend of Pop Art and Punk, featured images of famous women including Liza Minnelli's mother, Marilyn Monroe, and other actresses. This generated widespread criticism for being sexist, as it depicted women's faces with prices and suggestive messages. Liza Minnelli, in particular, was very displeased to see her mother's image used in such a context. "Esta portada le hizo un artista americano, que quisiese esperarse un poco un un una mezcla entre el pop art, que todavía traa bastante fuerte y el punk."
**Album Recall and Redesign Due to Cover Lawsuits** : Due to the significant controversy and threats of lawsuits from figures like Liza Minnelli and Marilyn Monroe's estate, the original "Some Girls" album was pulled from the market. A second, definitive version was then released where the faces of the actresses were literally cut out and replaced with photos of the band members, with some intense colored fragments remaining from the original design, reflecting a rock aesthetic. "tuvieron que retirar del mercado en disco y hacer una segunda versión que es en la que se queda la versión definitiva."
**Mick Jagger's Paternity Suit Threat** : One of the actresses depicted on the original "Some Girls" cover, a lesser-known American figure, was involved with Mick Jagger and threatened him with a paternity lawsuit. This was one of the first major paternity demands Mick Jagger faced, which ultimately concluded with an agreement out of court. "Fue una de las primeras grandes demandas de paternidad, es que se tuvo que enfrentar Mick Jagger que al finada acabó nada."
**Mick Jagger Apologizes to Liza Minnelli at Studio 54** : In 1979, Mick Jagger personally apologized to Liza Minnelli at Studio 54 for the controversial use of her mother's image on the "Some Girls" album cover. She eventually forgave him, preventing any lawsuit from proceeding. "me ya el se tuvo que disculpar a más tarde en 1979 en el estudio 54, cuando se la cruzó, ella le perdó no al final, no llegaba a mandarlos."
**Valuable US Edition of "Some Girls"** : The US edition of "Some Girls" released after the album recall featured literally cut-out faces on the cover, replaced with band members' photos, as a defiant response to the lawsuit threats. This edition is considered very valuable among collectors today. "Esta es la que hicieron en Estados Unidos, justo después de tener que retirarlo y un poco por recochineo y por frustración hicieron esta versión en la cual las caras salían literalmente recortadas."

# genre - Punk

## curiosities
**Revolutionary Impact and Mainstream Presence by 1978** : By 1978, punk music was in full swing, having already revolutionized the music industry since the 1976 releases by bands like the Sex Pistols. Other key bands like New York Dolls and Ramones had also emerged, making punk a strong influence on contemporary music, including The Rolling Stones' "Some Girls" album. "el panque estaba en pleno auge, ya había salido los New York 2, ya había sido los Ramones y en 1976 los expisto, la había reucinaró toda la industria musical."
