## albums
**Off the Wall (1979) - Pre-Thriller Zenith** : By 1979, with the release of *Off the Wall*, Michael Jackson was considered at his professional peak, having achieved an absolute sales record, scored five singles from a single album, and received maximum critical acclaim in nearly all major magazines. He was practically regarded as "the king of music," and this album was widely seen as his professional ceiling. "En 1979, Michael Jackson se acaba of the wall y ya no había conseguido todo, ya era su techo profesional."
**Thriller (1982) - Most Sold Album of All Time** : *Thriller*, acknowledged as one of the best and the most sold albums in history, was a product of Michael Jackson's extraordinarily demanding and professional work ethic, not solely his immense talent. The recording process for *Thriller* involved assembling an elite team, including over 50 of the best musicians, important rock artists, and direct collaborations with figures like Eddie Van Halen and Paul McCartney, all under the guidance of Quincy Jones. "Michael Jackson grabó el thriller gracias a un trabajo durísimo y terriblemente profesional que le permitió llegar a esos niveles de calidad."
**Thriller (1982) - Extensive Production and Collaboration** : For the *Thriller* album, Michael Jackson assembled an elite team, including over 50 of the best musicians available at the moment, important rock artists, and directly called upon Eddie Van Halen and Paul McCartney. He also relied on Quincy Jones, described as the best producer of Black music in history. Despite recording up to 30 songs, Jackson was so demanding that he felt none were good enough, ultimately selecting only nine tracks for the final album. "Michael Jackson para hacer el thriller cogió a más de 50 músicos que eran casi los mejores músicos que había en el momento. Me refiero cogió músicos ellas, importantes, cogió artistas de rock, importantes, incluso llamó al mismísimo Eddie Van Halen, llamó Paul McCartney, me refiero ser rodeo de lo mejor que pudo encontrar."
**Thriller (1982) - Post-Recording Dissatisfaction and Remixing** : Despite the immense effort and resources invested in recording *Thriller*, Michael Jackson was initially dissatisfied with the results, feeling the songs were not "good enough" or even "bad." He and Quincy Jones spent an entire extra week remixing every song on the album from scratch. Even after this extensive rework, Jackson remained unconvinced of the album's perfection, ultimately releasing it without his full satisfaction. "Michael Jackson terminó sacando el disco sin estar convencido de que fue ese suficientemente bueno para lo que quería hacer."
**Thriller (1982) - Genesis of Modern Pop** : The release of *Thriller* marked a pivotal moment, officially commencing the era of mainstream pop music and mass-market pop stars, preceding the emergence of other major pop figures like Madonna. Michael Jackson reinvented numerous aspects that would define pop music moving forward, from dance choreography to music videos and large-scale concert performances. "el pop de gran consumo o la época gradada de la música pop empieza oficialmente con el thriller, poco después empezaría a venir las grandes super estrellas del pop vendría madona, vendría a todas las grandes estrellas del pop, Michael Jackson abrió una nueva manera de hacer el pop de gran consumo."

## songs
**Beat It (1982) - Genre Expansion and Eddie Van Halen's Contribution** : For *Beat It*, Michael Jackson deliberately expanded his musical style, incorporating rock elements and synthesizer music, genres he hadn't extensively worked with before. After auditioning numerous guitarists for the electric guitar part, he called Eddie Van Halen. Jackson made Van Halen try 30 different guitars to achieve the exact sound he envisioned for the song's chords, a process Van Halen described as an unparalleled "fetishism of sound." "Michael Jackson acabó llamando al mismo de Divaan Hallen al cual lo hizo probar 30 guitarras distintas hasta que encontró a Eddie Van Hallen y encontró la guitarra que Michael Jackson quería para cómo sonaba en el beat."

## curiosities
**Michael Jackson's Career - Hard Work Over Talent** : Michael Jackson's legendary achievements are attributed more to his hard work, intense effort, and highly professional approach rather than solely to his immense talent. This highlights the profound dedication and relentless struggle behind his iconic status. "Michael Jackson llegó a hacer lo que era gracias al trabajo duro a la esforzo y a un completo trabajo profesionalizado y no tanto a su talento."
**Thriller (1982) - Barrier-Breaking Achievement** : The album *Thriller* is fascinating not just for its incredible quality, but for how Michael Jackson pushed beyond his own perceived limits and achieved something previously unimaginable in the music industry. It marked a significant personal and professional breakthrough for him, setting a new standard. "el thriller de Michael Jackson es apasionante, no tanto por lo increíble que es el disco, por lo bueno que es el disco sino por cómo Michael Jackson consigue romper sus propias barreras y llegar a algo que jamás nadie se imaginó."
**Breaking the Off the Wall Ceiling** : Despite *Off the Wall* being considered Michael Jackson's professional zenith in 1979, he chose not to rest on his laurels. Instead, he surpassed this perceived peak, achieving unprecedented success and creating *Thriller*, the best-selling album in history, which nobody thought possible after his prior success. "Michael Jackson en vez de tirar del hilo y dejarse llevar, consiguió romper su propio techo profesional y llegar a un más lejos a hacer lo que nadie había hecho nunca y hacer el disco más vendido de la historia."
**Thriller Challenge - Adapting to 1980s Music Shift** : Creating *Thriller* in 1982 was especially challenging because the musical landscape had drastically changed since 1979. Michael Jackson, who was considered the "king of disco and funky music" or "black music" in 1979, faced a market where disco was rapidly declining and new genres were emerging. He had to innovate beyond a style that was starting to go out of fashion. "Michael Jackson era el rey de un estilo que hacía aguas. Y para Michael Jackson romper las barreras del sonido, romper las barreras, el mercado musical en el 82 era muy difícil cuando era el rey de un sonido que estaba empezando a pasar de moda."
**First Major Depression During Thriller Recording** : During the recording of *Thriller*, Michael Jackson experienced what was possibly the first major depression of his career, a struggle he faced from childhood until his death. Despite achieving immense success with *Off the Wall*, he felt an "enormous emptiness" and recounted spending nights crying. This period saw him in a state of "enormous depression and personal devastation." "Michael Jackson de aquella ya estaba sufriendo posiblemente la que fue la primera grandísima de presión de la carrera de Michael Jackson."
**Extreme Recording Hours for Thriller** : Despite battling severe depression, Michael Jackson pushed himself to work in the studio for seven months, enduring "unbearable" recording sessions of 12, 15, 17, and even 20 consecutive hours. He would record until he almost lost his voice, meticulously studying and refining the sound rather than just performing. "Conseguía sacar fuerzas para irse el estudio durante siete meses y hacer jornadas de grabación insoportables de 12 de 15 de 17, incluso de 20 horas seguidas."
**Studio Acoustics Experimentation for Thriller** : The recording process for *Thriller* involved extreme attention to acoustic detail. Michael Jackson recorded entire songs, and even fragments, in different positions and locations within the recording studio to exploit the specific sonorities of each area. For certain fragments, he even had a platform over two meters high installed to leverage the unique acoustics provided by the studio's ceiling. "Michael Jackson mandó instalar una plataforma de más de dos metros de altura, porque lo gustaba la sonoridad que hacía el techo del estudio de grabación para fragmentos del disco."
**Obsessive Acoustic Detail for Thriller** : Michael Jackson's pursuit of perfection during the *Thriller* recordings extended to constantly changing his position, height, and location within the studio. This was done to capture imperceptible or subtle tonal changes and specific echoes generated by the recording environment, which he meticulously utilized. "Michael Jackson estuvo cambiando de posición de altura, de lugar y de todo durante todo el estudio de grabación para coger imperceptibles o ridículos cambios de tonalidad, eso de decos que generaban los estudios de grabación que él aprovechaba."
**Nightly Vocal Rehearsals at Home** : Beyond the 18-20 hour studio sessions, Michael Jackson's work ethic continued at home. Instead of sleeping, he would spend entire nights rehearsing vocal sounds with a recorder, often accumulating two hours of vocal material by morning. He studied specific screams and small vocalizations he wanted to reproduce, demonstrating an "unhealthy drive for perfection" that went beyond studio time. "había pasado toda la noche ensayando sonidos con su voz que venía con una grabadora y venía al mejor con dos horas de sonidos de su voz para reproducir la sí para ver estudiado cuáles eran los gritos y sonidos que le gustaba reproducir."
**Eddie Van Halen's "Fetishism of Sound" Comment** : Eddie Van Halen recounted that during the recording of *Beat It*, Michael Jackson exhibited an unprecedented "fetishism of sound." Jackson's meticulousness extended to having Van Halen test 30 different guitars to find the precise tone he wanted for just a few chords in the song. "el propio Divaan Hallen nos lo dijo que jamás en su vida había visto se mejante nivel de fetichismo del sonido como el que había tenido Michael Jackson para grabar unos simples acordes como en esa canción."
**Post-Thriller Reinvention of Dance Choreography** : After *Thriller*, Michael Jackson's work fundamentally rewrote the rules for dance choreography in pop and modern music. His influence on dance became a defining characteristic of the genre, transforming how dance was conceived and executed in popular music. "Michael Jackson después del thriller rescribe las coreografías de baile de la música, me refiero toda la coreografía de baile del pop, toda la coreografía en general de la música, moderna serra escribe después del thriller."
**Post-Thriller Reinvention of Music Videos** : The concept of the music video was fundamentally re-conceived and reinvented following Michael Jackson's groundbreaking work with the videos for *Thriller*. He opened up new ways of creating visual accompaniments for music, establishing a new paradigm for the medium. "El concepto de video clip se reescribe y se reinventa y se abro una nueva manera de video clips después del increíble trabajo de video clips que tuvo Michael Jackson con el thriller."
**Post-Thriller Reinvention of Superstar Concerts** : The idea of large-scale concerts for superstar artists was newly invented and redefined in the wake of the extensive concert tours Michael Jackson undertook following the release of *Thriller*. He set new standards for performance and spectacle in live music. "el concepto de conciertos en gran consumo de estrellas y conciertos se inventó de nuevo después de la agiría conciertos que hizo Michael Jackson con el thriller."
**Thriller - Birth of Modern Pop and Pop Star Archetype** : *Thriller* is considered not only the best-selling and one of the most perfect albums in history, but also the album that gave birth to modern pop music as we know it, and established the archetype of the modern pop star. It redefined performance, music videos, and live concerts, rewriting practically everything about contemporary pop. "Como el thriller ya no es que nace el disco más vendido de la historia, uno de los discos más perfectos y más increíbles de la historia, sino que nace el pop moderno como lo conocemos, nace la estrella pop como la conocemos y nace nuevas maneras de hacer video clips de bailar de la performance de los conciertos, se reescribe todo prácticamente."

# curiosity

**Talent vs. Professional Effort in Music Industry** : The music market often presents a "very unrealistic perception" that talent alone is sufficient for success. In reality, having an incredibly powerful voice or innate talent is often insufficient. Artists who achieve lasting success do so through "very hard and very professional work," a relentless and continuous struggle, rather than just their talent. For aspiring musicians, focusing on professional training, effort, and a determined approach is more crucial than relying solely on talent. "las artistas que llegan lejos, no llegan gracias a su talento o no solo gracias a su talento, sino que llegan gracias a un trabajo muy duro y muy profesional que les permite llegar dejos."
**1978-1982 Music Industry Revolution** : Between 1978 and 1982, the music industry underwent a significant revolution. Disco music rapidly declined, while new forms of rock, heavy metal, hip hop culture, New Wave, synth-pop, and post-punk emerged. This period was described as "very convulsive," with the punk movement still prominent. "entre el 78 y el 82 la industria musical se revolucionó. Me refiero con la música disco, la música disco, la calle en picado, nacían nuevas maneras de rock, nacían nuevas maneras de heavy metal, nacían la cultura hip hop, nacían el de New Wave, nacían el signpok, nacían el post-punk, estaba en la hoja del punk, eran momentos muy convulsos."
