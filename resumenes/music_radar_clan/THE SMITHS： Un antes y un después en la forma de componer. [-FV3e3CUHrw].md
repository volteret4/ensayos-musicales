## albums
**Four Albums - Poetry Books** : The band's four albums are noted for containing some of the best lyrics ever written, to the extent that they could be considered books of poetry. "Y hoy en esos cuatro discos de los esmíc tenemos algunas de las mejores letras jamás escritas que podrían ser perfectamente libros de poesía."

## songs
**Handsome Devil (1983)** : This song was wrongly accused by the press of inciting incest or pedophilia due to its lyrical content featuring strange, reprobate characters. However, these characters were influenced by Oscar Wilde's interest in such figures and John Waters' macabre themes, not actual incitement, and could be found in any of Wilde's short novellas. "De hecho teníamos letras que se les acusaban a incitar al incesto, incluso a la pedofilia como en Hanson Deville, pero realmente haber, Hanson Deville no va sobre incesto, ni va sobre pedofilia."
**Suffer Little Children (1984)** : This song addressed the macabre child murders that had occurred in Manchester during Morrissey's childhood. Morrissey later developed a positive relationship with some of the mothers of the victims of this incident. The macabre themes were influenced by John Waters and reflected British society at the time. "A ver, está el famoso caso de software little children que iba a de los asesinatos macabros en niños que había habido en Manchester cuando Morrisa irá pequeño."
**This Charming Man (1983)** : Even this seemingly lighter song, which touches upon the theme of going out and having a good time with someone you appreciate, showcases The Smiths' distinctive lyrical style. It uses a pompous and metaphorical writing manner, elevating simple ideas to a new level of writing previously unheard in pop music. "la famosa de desalaita Never Goes Out, que realmente no tiene casi el momento a hablar de salir de fiesta y pasarlo bien con alguien con quien aprecias, pero lo hace con una metáfora, con una manera de escribir tan pomposa que es que eso... Eso es otro nivel de escritura."

## curiosities
**Emergence and Impact (Early 1980s)** : The Smiths emerged in the early 1980s pop scene, marking a significant "before and after" in the trajectory of pop music over the past 50 years. Their impact was primarily due to the introduction of groundbreaking lyrics, rather than their musical style which was initially simpler, though it evolved later. "Los Smith y Rúmen a primeros de los 80 en la escena pop y suponen un antes y un después en toda la trayectoria de la música bombe de los últimos 50 años, no tanto musicalmente a pesar de los buenos que son musicalmente, pero sobre todo por las letras que introducen."
**Groundbreaking Lyrical Style** : The band pioneered a new way of writing lyrics in pop music by drawing influences from literature rather than previous musical styles. For the first time in a long while, pop lyrics featured metaphors, strange characters, and fantastical stories, offering something completely different from what had come before. "Por primera vez en mucho tiempo volvíamos a tener en las letras el pop, volvíamos a tener metáforas, volvíamos a tener personajes extraños, volvíamos a tener historias fantasiosas, algo completamente diferente a lo que había habido antes."
**Controversy and Media Conflict** : As Morrissey developed his innovative lyrics, the media, particularly sensationalist outlets like The Sun, often made unusual interpretations, leading to ongoing controversy and conflict between the band and the press. Morrissey contributed to this by giving incendiary quotes in interviews, which further fueled media scrutiny. "Y yo creo que ahí nación poco la famosa controversia y la famosa lucha que siempre hubo entre los Smith, sobre todo Morrisa y toda la prensa musical, y casi la prensa en general."
**Political Allusions in Lyrics** : The Smiths' lyrics incorporated political allusions to figures such as the Queen and Margaret Thatcher. These references were highly metaphorical, allowing for various interpretations by the audience. "Y tenemos también muchos sintes políticos, sale la reina, sale marga de tacheres, pero esa manera tan metafórica de escribir, pues te daba a piñar reterla como tú quisieras."
**Foundation for Britpop** : Looking back 25 years later, The Smiths are recognized for generating a new way of writing lyrics and understanding pop music, thereby laying the groundwork for the Britpop movement of the 1990s. "Pero al final, viendo la día de hoy, 25 años más tarde con perspectiva, los esmícitos generaron una nueva manera de escribir, generaron una nueva manera de entender la música pop y fue el gran cimiento sobre el que se basó la música del ripop de los 90."
**Unparalleled Lyrical Cultivation** : The Smiths' unique lyric-writing style, which was deeply rooted in classical literature, pompous, and cultivated, yet brought into mainstream pop music, is considered to have been unparalleled since their time. While there have been other great lyricists, this particular "ancient literature," "overloaded," "correct," almost "Shakespearean" approach has not been replicated. "pero sobre todo, yo creo que esa manera tan basada en la literatura clásica, tan pomposa, sobre todo de una manera tan culta, pero a la vez llevada la música de gran consumo, no la hemos vuelto a tener a día de hoy."

# artist - Morrissey

## member of
The Smiths

## curiosities
**Literary Background** : From his adolescence, Morrissey was a dedicated literature enthusiast. He actively wrote poetry, participated in literature clubs, and had even published a book about music and several poems, earning recognition for his literary pursuits during his teenage years. "Morrisa era un gran aficionado a la literatura, ya dolescente, escribía mucho, escribía poesía, estaban clubes de literatura, de hecho, ya había escrito algún libro sobre música y algunos poemas, era bastante reconocido por eso, ya da adolescente."
**Lyrical Influences** : When starting The Smiths, Morrissey deliberately sought lyrical inspiration from literature rather than relying on previous musical influences, which was the common practice. His key literary inspirations included Oscar Wilde, Walt Whitman, and classic British Victorian novels, shaping his distinct writing style. "cuando empieza el proyecto de los Smith, Morrisa inó busca sus influencias para las letras en la música anterior, como solía pasar, sino que busca sus influencias en la literatura."
**Controversial Interview Style** : Morrissey was known for his confrontational approach in interviews, particularly with sensationalist newspapers like The Sun. He would often make incendiary statements, which further fueled press controversies and scrutiny surrounding The Smiths. "Y digamos que Morrisa y entraba mucho al trapo. Morrisa y decía cosas muy incendiarias en muchas de sus entrevistas que incitaban todavía que las prensas hechas en más encima sobre la banda."

## albums

## curiosities
**Invention of Pop Music (1962)** : The Beatles emerged in 1962 and are credited with inventing pop music as it is known today. Their rise to fame in that year initiated a curious phenomenon that impacted the music landscape. "En 1962 salen los Beatles, inventan la música pop tal y como la conocemos."
**Early Lyrical Trivialization** : In the early part of the 1960s, particularly their initial phase, The Beatles' lyrics were considered to be quite uninspired and left much to be desired, often saying practically nothing. This trend contributed to a broader tendency to trivialize lyrics in popular music. "Las letras de los Beatles, no nos engañemos. Te dejaban bastante que desearon, eran letras que no decían prácticamente nada."

# artist - Ian Curtis

## curiosities
**Evolution of Punk Lyrics** : Ian Curtis, through his work with Joy Division, took the punk rock genre and elevated its lyrical content to a previously unexplored sphere. He introduced themes of psychology, intimacy, and profound personal suffering into rock and punk lyrics, transforming the depth of the genre's expression. "Y en Cortis, que con Joy Division, hereda el PANC y lleva las letras, todo este rock PANC, una esfera prácticamente desconocida, en la que se habla, pues, de aspectos de psicología, de intimismo, de sufrimiento personal, que son increíbles."

# artist - John Peel

## labels
Radio One

## curiosities
**Discovery of The Smiths (1983)** : John Peel, an influential DJ for Radio One and considered a "great patron" of pop music for 40 years, was completely amazed upon first seeing The Smiths perform live at one of their early concerts at London University in 1983. He noted their unique sound, remarking that he couldn't discern their musical influences. "Y John Pill, de hecho, una entrevista, comentaba que, cuando los vio por primera vez en ese concerto, se quedó alucinado, y se quedó alucinado porque decía que tuveías a los Smiths, y no tenías manera de encontrar de dónde habían sacado sus influencias para llegar a esa música."
**Launch of The Smiths' Career** : Just a few weeks after witnessing their concert, John Peel invited The Smiths to record a Peel Session for Radio One. This opportunity was pivotal, as it completely launched and propelled The Smiths' career forward. "a las pocas semanas John Pill invitaba a los Smiths, al Radio One, a grabar unas pills Sessions con las que la carrera de los Smiths despegaba completamente."

# genre - Pop

## curiosities
**Invention and Early Lyrical State (1960s)** : Pop music as we know it was invented by The Beatles in 1962. However, in its early stages, particularly the first part of the 1960s, its lyrics were often unimaginative and trivial, frequently saying very little, which initiated a trend of lyrical frivolity in mainstream pop music. "En 1962 salen los Beatles, inventan la música pop tal y como la conocemos."
**Lyrical Decline (1965-1980)** : From approximately 1965 to 1980, the lyrics in mainstream pop music were generally not considered noteworthy, with the late 1970s being described as a particularly "shameful era" for lyrical quality, encompassing genres like disco and early punk. "Pero bueno, en lo que es la música pop de gran consumo, la música pop, digamos, que del 65 al 80 prácticamente, sus letras, pues, no son precisamente lo más a destacar de esa temporada de la música de gran consumo."
**Transformation by The Smiths** : The Smiths, led by Morrissey, radically transformed the understanding of pop music by elevating its lyrical content. They moved beyond simple melodies and uninspired lyrics, introducing a new depth that included metaphors, strange characters, and fantastical stories, thereby taking pop music to "another level." "pero en la parte pop, que es la música de mayor consumo, tenemos la referencia de Morrisa y de los Smiths, por supuesto, que cambian radicalmente toda esa manera de entender la música pop, como lo díaz muy fáciles, pero con letras que dejan mucho que desean, en la mayoría de los aspectos, algo algunas excepciones, y lleva la música a otro nivel."

# curiosity

**Trivialization of Lyrics in Pop Music (1960s-1980s)** : Starting with the early Beatles in the first half of the 1960s, a significant trend emerged in pop music to trivialize lyrics, leading to songs that often said very little. This tendency persisted, making the lyrics of mainstream pop from 1965 to 1980 generally unremarkable, with the late 1970s, including disco and early punk, being particularly highlighted as a "shameful era" for lyrical quality. "Y lo curioso es que empezaron una tendencia a frivolizar muchísimo a la letra de la música."
**Shift in Lyric Writing Paradigm (1978-1982)** : Between 1978 and 1982, a fundamental shift occurred in the approach to writing lyrics for music, spearheaded by two distinct figures: Ian Curtis in the punk sphere and Morrissey in pop. This period marked a permanent change in how lyrical content was conceived and executed across genres. "Pero justo al principio de 1980, en el 78, 82, aparecen dos personas muy concretas, que cambian para siempre la concepción que tenemos de escribir letras para la música."
**Influence of British Victorian Novels on Lyrics** : Classic British Victorian novels were a direct source of influence for Morrissey's lyric writing, contributing to his unique approach that drew heavily from literature rather than previous musical styles. "Él toma sus influencias y Oscar White, de Walt Whitman, de todas las novelas clásicas victorianas británicas, y entonces generó una nueva manera de escribir las letras."
**Literal Interpretation of Lyrics by Media** : A recurring issue during The Smiths' career was the media's tendency to literally interpret their lyrics, leading to misrepresentations and controversies. This literal reading often ignored the metaphorical depth and literary influences behind Morrissey's songwriting, allowing the public to interpret the music however they wished. "Lo que pasa es que se hacía esa interpretación literal de la música, que al final la gente lo interpretaba como quería."
**The Sun's Sensationalism** : The sensationalist newspaper "The Sun" is specifically mentioned as a publication that often interviewed The Smiths, particularly Morrissey, and focused on the controversial interpretations of their lyrics, further fueling public debate and scrutiny around the band. "sobre todo con diarios sensacionalistas, como de San, donde se le hacían muchas preguntas sobre las letras o sobre los controvertidas que se podían interpretar."
**Unmatched Lyrical Style Since The Smiths** : The particular style of lyric writing introduced by The Smiths, characterized by its classical literary basis, pompousness, and cultivated nature—all brought into mainstream pop music—is considered to be unparalleled and has not been replicated since. Despite other great lyricists, their "ancient literature," "overloaded," "correct," almost "Shakespearean" approach remains unique. "pero sobre todo, yo creo que esa manera tan basada en la literatura clásica, tan pomposa, sobre todo de una manera tan culta, pero a la vez llevada la música de gran consumo, no la hemos vuelto a tener a día de hoy."
