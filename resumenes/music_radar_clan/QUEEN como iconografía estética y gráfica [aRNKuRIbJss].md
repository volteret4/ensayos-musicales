## curiosities
**Visual Iconography - Historical Importance** : Queen maintained a difficult balance between a faithful image and continuous innovation, leaving historical and relevant moments in rock iconography. The band is considered one of the most important in rock history, not only musically but also stylistically and as a visual icon. "Queen ha guardado un difícil equilibrio entre mantener una imagen fiel a su estilo y estar continuamente innovando y cambiando su iconografía."

**Queen Logo - Design and Symbolism** : The famous Queen logo features the zodiac signs of the four band members: two lions (representing two members), a Cancer, and two fairies representing Freddie Mercury (who was a Virgo). Other elements are primarily aesthetic, though some interpret the phoenix (ABFNix) as a reference to the phoenix on the emblem of Freddie Mercury's childhood boarding school, although there's uncertainty about this. Some elements, like a lion looking forward and a crown, are copies or adaptations of the British coat of arms, as they couldn't use the original royal crown. "En el logo de Queen figure no se odia a Calés de los 4 miembros de la banda. Dos leos, un cáncer y las dos adas que representan a Freddy Mercury es Virgo."

**Queen Logo - Creation** : Freddie Mercury, a talented draftsman who studied plastic arts, designed the main structure of the Queen logo. While it is widely believed he created the logo, it's most probable that he made a definitive or very close version, which was then finalized by a professional artist. The logo became famous with "A Night at the Opera" and "A Day at the Races" but has been present since Queen's first album. "Aunque lo más probable es que lo hizo Freddy Mercury no lo sabemos al 100%."

**News of the World (1977) - Album Cover Iconography** : The iconic robot on the "News of the World" album cover is a nearly identical copy of a sci-fi fanzine from 1953. The original artist from the fanzine was commissioned by the band to create the cover, and also developed all the iconography within the album, including original paintings in the booklet depicting people screaming in terror from the robot, and related posters. This visual has been highly influential, inspiring reinterpretations in comics like X-Men and even a subtle nod in a Family Guy episode. "Realmente es una copia casi idéntica de un fancine de ciencia afición de 1953."

**Jazz (1978) - Bicycle Race Nudism Controversy** : The iconography surrounding the first single "Bicycle Race" was highly controversial. Freddie Mercury, inspired by a photograph of nude women in a race, wanted to base the single and album's visual theme on this. Due to censorship, the single cover could not show full nudity, so a small cropped image of a nude woman on a bicycle had panties drawn onto her. The original "Jazz" album versions included a small poster of the nude women's race, which was censored in countries like the United States, forcing Queen to remove it. To circumvent this, a coupon was included, allowing fans to mail away for the poster. Queen subsequently created extensive iconography around the nude cyclists, including posters, concert visuals, and merchandise. This visual theme was significant in the 1970s, an era when nudism was a form of protest against the connection between nudity and sexuality. "Esta fotografía, acabo siendo censurada en muchos países como Estados Unidos donde no podía mandarse fotos de desnudo."

**Flash Gordon (1980) - Soundtrack Iconography** : While the "Flash Gordon" soundtrack album is considered one of Queen's lower artistic moments, its iconography is fantastic. The band masterfully integrated their image with Flash Gordon's superhero iconography, creating numerous intriguing posters. This era produced one of Freddie Mercury's most iconic looks: wearing a Flash Gordon t-shirt, leather shorts, and famously pictured atop a Darth Vader figure. "Nos lejó uno de los momentos icónicos más maravillosos de Freddy Mercury con la camiseta de Flash Gordon, unos y ors de cuero subido encima de Darth Vader."

**The Works (1984) - "I Want To Break Free" Videoclip** : Creatively, "The Works" is one of Queen's most questioned albums, but it yielded the visually epic music video for "I Want To Break Free." The video features all band members in drag, famously including Freddie Mercury vacuuming while crossing a doorway. The humor and impact came from seeing other band members like Roger Taylor and Brian May in drag, breaking taboos prevalent in the 1980s. The video also contains other memorable moments, such as a scene interpreted as a version of ancient Greek bacchanalian feasts, showcasing a graphically interesting aesthetic. "Lo divertido no era a Freddy Mercury travestido, era a ver los a los otros."

**A Kind of Magic (1986) - 80s Aesthetic** : The "A Kind of Magic" album embodies the pure essence of the 1980s aesthetic. This period blended various stylistic factors, including influences from David Bowie's 80s photography. The title track's music video is an apotheotic moment where animation and music interact fantastically, and the album also includes the soundtrack for "Highlander," featuring the song "Who Wants to Live Forever," which also has a wonderful aesthetic moment. "Aquí se mezclan un montón de factores estéticos que hacen que la iconografía de Queen esta época se interés antísima."

**The Miracle (1989) - Album Cover Technology** : Although "The Miracle" is not always considered the most critically acclaimed album in Queen's career, its cover features a highly interesting graphic element: a single image blending the four faces of the band members. Created in 1989, this computer-generated composite was a technological marvel for its time, executed using the famous Quantel Paintbox, a precursor to Photoshop and other video editing programs. At the time, this advanced photographic editing technique was revolutionary, even if it seems unremarkable today. "Este dibujo que mezcla en una sola imagen las cuatro caras de los miembros de Queen es uno de los momentos interesantes desde el punto de vista gráfico y estamos hablando en 1989 y hacer esta especie de montajes fotográficos no era sencillo."

**Innuendo (1991) - Graphic Complexity** : "Innuendo" is arguably the most graphically and iconographically interesting point in Queen's entire career due to its complex visual language. The music video for the title track is a highly intricate collage of various elements, including film footage, animation, and old recordings, all perfectly synchronized with the music, faithfully reflecting the song's complexity. "Si el Inuendo musicalmente es complejo, visualmente también es muy complejo."

**"I'm Going Slightly Mad" (1991) - Music Video Tribute** : The music video for "I'm Going Slightly Mad" is almost a tribute to the band's history and a beautiful aesthetic moment. Freddie Mercury embodies a version of the protagonist from "The Rocky Horror Picture Show" and delivered one of his most iconic looks by wearing a banana wig. "Freddy Mercury es y versional, el protagonista de roche y horror picture show."

**"Radio Ga Ga" (1984) - Music Video Inspiration** : The music video for "Radio Ga Ga" was entirely inspired by the classic film "Metropolis." "Hacen un video clip completamente inspirado en la película Metropolis."

**"The Invisible Man" (1989) - Music Video Inspiration** : The music video for "The Invisible Man" drew its inspiration from a video game, depicting the band members as video game characters, which was a very interesting visual concept. "Donde hacen un video clip inspirado en un videojuego donde ves a los personajes como videojobos."

# artist - Freddie Mercury

## curiosities
**Queen Logo Designer** : Freddie Mercury, known for his vocal talent, was also a skilled draftsman who studied plastic arts. He is credited with designing the core structure of the iconic Queen logo. "Freddy Mercury aparte de un gran cantante era un gran dibujante de hecho estudiar desplásticas."

**Flash Gordon Era Outfit** : During the "Flash Gordon" era, Freddie Mercury sported one of his most iconic looks, featuring a Flash Gordon t-shirt with contrasting borders, leather shorts, and was famously photographed standing atop a Darth Vader figure. This image is considered one of the coolest superhero-themed outfits and moments in rock history. "Ese momento de Freddy Mercury subido encima de Darth Vader es como lo más."

**39th Birthday Party in Munich (1985)** : Freddie Mercury's 39th birthday celebration in Munich in 1985 is considered one of the two greatest parties in rock history. He invited all his friends to a costume party, and the images from this event, including guests arriving in horse-drawn carriages and the extravagant costumes, are iconic. It is often compared to Grace Jones's party at Studio 54 where Mick Jagger ended up hospitalized. "La grandísima fiesta de cumpleaños quiso Freddy Mercury en 1985. Son las dos mayores fiestas de la historia de Rock."

**"I'm Going Slightly Mad" Music Video Outfit** : In the music video for "I'm Going Slightly Mad," Freddie Mercury famously appears in a memorable aesthetic moment, portraying a version of the protagonist from "The Rocky Horror Picture Show" and notably wearing a banana wig. "Nos dejó uno de esos momentos apatióicos en los que Freddy Mercury se pone, una peluca de plátanos que es uno de esos momentos maravillosos de la iconografía de Queen."

**Live Aid (1985) Iconic Outfit** : Freddie Mercury's outfit at the Live Aid concert in 1985, consisting of white trousers with a red stripe and a yellow military-style jacket, has become an iconic piece of rock fashion. This look has been reinterpreted and referenced by various designers, such as Yves Saint Laurent and Martin Margiela, over the past 30 years. "El propio Freddy Mercury en el concierto de Life Aid, desde el pantalón Blanco con una banda roja hasta la cazadora Casa Cada Marilla que han sido versionados por distintos diseñadores como Isalora Go Martin Margel en los últimos 30 años."
