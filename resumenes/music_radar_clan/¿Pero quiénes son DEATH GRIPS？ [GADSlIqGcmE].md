## albums
**Ex-Military (2011) - Debut Mixtape** : This self-produced mixtape, released a few months after the band's formation, is credited with revolutionizing and redefining underground culture. It features highly complex sampling from various sources, including David Bowie, Daniel Pink of Pink Floyd, the voice of Charles Manson, and music from video games like Half-Life. The mixtape is a complex work of cut-and-paste, characterized by powerful drums and MC Ride's aggressive, potent vocals. Its tape format spread rapidly at concerts and hand-to-hand, leading to Epic Records signing the band in early 2012 for two or three albums. "Hay sampleado David Bowie, hay sampleado Daniel Pink, de Pink Floyd, tenemos incluso la voz de Charles Manson, tenemos trozos de música de videojuegos como el Half-Life, es una complejísima trabajo de corta pega con baterías muy potentes y con la voz agresiva y potente de Enzy Ride."
**The Money Store (2012) - First Official Album** : This album, referred to as "demonistor" in the transcript, was the band's first official full-length release, positioned as something between an EP and an LP. The band caused a major dispute with Epic Records by controversially releasing it for free before its official market release as a physical record. Critics raved about the album, which further increased the popularity of their earlier mixtape, Ex-Military. "La crítica al Lucina con demonistor, de hecho eso hace subir todavía más la popularidad del mixtape de Ex-Military."
**No Love Deep Web (2012) - Controversial Album** : The release of this album was significantly delayed for months due to major censorship problems related to its controversial cover art. The band refused to change the cover, eventually resorting to releasing the album inside a black plastic sleeve that concealed the interior. Despite the issues, the album received critical acclaim upon its release and later became an underground icon. "el lanzamiento de No Love de Web quedaría redrasado durante meses por enormes problemas censurados con su portada."
**Government Plates (2013) - Free Online Release** : This album was initially released for free on the band's website and Facebook page at the end of 2013. It was made available on iTunes for download a short time later, accompanied by a humorous comment from the band on Facebook mocking anyone who would pay for something already offered for free. "salía publicado de manera gratuita en su web y en su Facebook para poco más tarde salir en iTunes para descarga, a lo cual ellos ponían además un comentario en Facebook muy divertido que es por si algún gilipollas quiere comprar algo que ya hemos puesto gratis."
**The Powers That B (2014-2015) - Ambitious Double Album** : Described as one of the band's most ambitious works, this is a double album released in two parts. The first part, "Niggas on the Moon," was released for free and met with overwhelming success and critical acclaim, showcasing many samples and contributions from Björk. The second part, "Jenny Death," was released gradually over time. "el trabajo más ambicioso que habían hecho los Dead Gryps, que es el doble álbum de Niggas, on Demon y de Jenis D."
**Niggas on the Moon (2014) - Part One of The Powers That B** : This was the first part of "The Powers That B" double album, released for free online. It achieved overwhelming success and garnered immense critical praise. The album features numerous samples and contributions from artists like Björk, and was admired by figures such as Pizorno of Kasabian and David Bowie, influencing many in the underground scene. "ellos publicaron de manera gratuita la primera parte de este doble álbum Niggas, on Demon que tuvo un éxito arrollador y la crítica alucinó con él."
**Jenny Death (2015) - Part Two of The Powers That B** : This was the second part of "The Powers That B" double album. Its release was gradual, with pieces of it made public little by little as the band continued working on it. "la publicación de la segunda parte de Jenis D fue publicada como muy poco a poco según la IBA manteniendo."
**Fashion Week (2015) - Instrumental Collection** : Released between the two parts of "The Powers That B," this was a conglomerate of instrumental pieces that the narrator admits was never fully understood. "lanzaron todo un conglomerado de piezas instrumentales llamado Faso & Wig, que nunca hemos terminado de entender muy bien."
**Bottomless Pit (2016) - Album with Normal Distribution** : This is the latest album mentioned, released with what was described as a "normal distribution," making it the first time a Death Grips album was distributed in such a conventional manner. "el botonlessbeat, que yo creo que ya como salía por cuarto tenía una distribución bastante normal, yo creo que era la primera vez que teníamos un disco de Dead Gryps distribuido como un disco normal."

## curiosities
**Rolling Stone's High Praise** : The band was once described by Rolling Stone magazine as "the first truly relevant band of the 21st century," highlighting their significant impact and unique artistic blend. "La revista Rolling Stone los definía una vez como la primera banda realmente relevante del siglo XXI."
**Formation and Immediate Recording (2011)** : Death Grips formed in 2011, and remarkably, on the very day they first met, they definitively recorded several tracks. This highlights their intense creative process and aggressive improvisation. "Arrancaban en 2011 y ya el mismo día que se juntaban por primera vez, ya grababan varios temas de manera definitiva."
**Aggressive Improvisation Creative Basis** : A core foundation of Death Grips' work is their "aggressive improvisation" approach, which is integral to their creative process and the sound they produce. "uno de las grandes bases que tienen los Dead Grips es el trabajo de improvisación agresiva, como ellos lo llaman."
**Early Concerts - Shocking Performances** : In their early California concerts, often held in abandoned places or small venues and captured in pirated recordings, Death Grips delivered impactful live shows. These performances included provocative elements like pornographic images in the background, MC Ride sometimes mildly self-mutilating, performing nude, screaming, and breaking things. "Hay videos muy impactantes de esos primeros conciertos de Enzy Ride y de los Dead Gryps en los que vemos cosas muy javís como imágenes pornográficas de fondo, Enzy Ride a veces automotilándose levemente, el desnudo gritando, rompiendo las cosas son espectáculos en vivo verdaderamente impactantes."
**Inconsistent Lineup** : While the core lineup is believed to be MC Ride, Zach Hill, and Andy Morin, their live performances have often seen only two of them on stage, not always the same two. There have also been instances where unknown individuals appeared, making the band's definitive membership unclear even to themselves. "hay parte de la carrera de Dead Grips y muchos conciertos en las que salen solo dos de ellos, no siempre los mismos, hay partes en las que sale gente que no sabemos quiénes son."
**MC Ride's Art Background** : MC Ride studied at the Faculty of Fine Arts (Villasarde) but was eventually expelled due to his controversial works. This background contributes to the band's strong artistic component. "MC Ride había estudiado en la facultad de Villasarde si había terminado siendo expulsado por sus polémicos trabajos."
**Zach Hill and Andy Morin's Art Background** : Zach Hill and Andy Morin also pursued studies in art, photography, and film. They were involved in experimental projects such as films and exhibitions in museums, reinforcing the band's connection to conceptual art. "Zag Hill y Andy Morning también habían trabajado, he hecho algunas estudios de arte y fotografía y cine, y además que tuvieron además proyectos como películas, royes experimentales, han trabajado con exposiciones en museos."
**Band as Conceptual Artists** : Death Grips is described as a unique band formed by individuals who exist "halfway between music and conceptual art," which is seen as key to understanding their work. They consider themselves more conceptual artists than musicians. "Es una especie de extraña banda formada por gente que está medio camino entre la música y el arte conceptual."
**Conflict with Epic Records over The Money Store Release** : The band engaged in a significant dispute with Epic Records when they decided to release "The Money Store" for free online before its official market release, much to the label's dismay. "tendrían una gran disputa por Epic Records porque ellos la publicarían de manera gratuita antes de salir al mercado como disco."
**Tour Cancellation for No Love Deep Web** : Shortly after "The Money Store" was released, the band announced their first tour in the United States, generating huge anticipation. However, just days before it was set to begin, the band canceled the entire tour at the last minute, choosing instead to focus on their new album, "No Love Deep Web." This decision further complicated their relationship with Epic Records. "gira que apenas unos días antes de comenzar era cancelada por la propia banda porque decidían el último momento pasar de dar conciertos para centros en su nuevo trabajo, el No Love de Web."
**Censorship of No Love Deep Web Cover** : The release of "No Love Deep Web" was delayed for months due to extensive censorship issues surrounding its controversial album cover. The band's refusal to change the artwork led to the album being packaged in a black plastic sleeve that obscured the image. "el lanzamiento de No Love de Web quedaría redrasado durante meses por enormes problemas censurados con su portada."
**Björk's Initial Contact and Admiration** : Shortly after the release of "No Love Deep Web," Björk, one of the band's earliest and biggest fans, reached out to them. She was captivated by their work and initiated a close relationship with the band. "contacta con ellos una de sus primeros grandísimos fans, que es Bjork. Bjork contacta con los Dead Gryps a Lucina con el trabajo que están haciendo."
**Collaboration with Björk** : Death Grips created several remixes for Björk's complementary remix albums of "Biofilia." In return, Björk recorded many of her vocals and various singing parts, which Death Grips subsequently used in their next album. "A Dead Gryps harían varias remeclas para esos albumes complementarios que hicieron de remeclas del biofilia de Bjork y por el otro lado Bjork grabaría un montón de sus voces y varias partes de ella cantando que luego harían en su siguiente trabajo los Dead Gryps."
**Conflict with Epic Records over Album Release Frequency** : After releasing two full albums in less than a year, Death Grips was working on new material for a third album. Epic Records refused to allow the release, stating that three albums in one year was excessive. "Epic Records se niega porque dice que sacar tres albumes en un año que ya empieza a ser un poco un abuso."
**Dismissal from Epic Records** : In response to Epic Records' refusal to release their new material, the band retaliated by releasing a large portion of their nearly completed third album for free. This infuriated Epic Records, leading them to ultimately expel Death Grips from the label. "La banda reacciona publicando de manera gratuita gran parte del trabajo que estaban terminando para su tercer album y Epic Records acaba muy cabreada con ellos y los acaba expulsando de la disco gráfica."
**Leaking Private Emails with Epic Records** : Following their dismissal from Epic Records, Death Grips further exacerbated the situation by publishing a large number of private emails between the band and the label. These emails exposed their complicated relationship and hinted at Epic Records' questionable attempts to further monetize Death Grips' music. "Dead Gryps reacciona publicando un montón de mis privados entre Epic Records y Dead Gryps en los que se veía esa complicada relación y asuntos son tanto turbios de Epic Records de cómo intentaba monetizar más todavía la música de Dead Gryps."
**Potential Lawsuit from Epic Records** : The publication of the private emails on Facebook nearly resulted in a significant lawsuit from Epic Records against the band, though ultimately nothing came of it. "Casi les vale una demanda bastante importante de Epic Records por la publicación de todos estos correos electrónicos en Facebook pero al final se queda en nada."
**Independent Career** : After being dropped by Epic Records and publishing the private emails, Death Grips became an independent band, operating without a record label for the remainder of their career. "Dead Gryps se quedan con todo eso publicado sin disco gráfica y a partir de ahí ya Dead Gryps trabajarían de manera independiente al resto de su carrera."
**Distribution Deals with Independent Labels** : Following their departure from Epic, Death Grips signed specific distribution contracts with independent labels such as Harvest and Warp. These agreements were solely for the distribution of their material and not for full label representation. "Cirmarían contratos de distribución puntuales con ciertas discográficas independientes como Harvest o como Warp pero solo para la distribución del material nada más."
**Ongoing Live Performance Issues** : Death Grips concerts have been characterized by significant behavioral issues, with intense and impactful performances captured in powerful videos. Although their shows are less aggressive now, the band views their concerts as the true artistic manifestation of their work, seeing them as aggressive and passionate. "tenemos unos enormes problemas de comportamiento y de bastante impactantes que son los conciertos en los que Dios hay vídeos muy fuertes de los conciertos que daban Dead Gryps."
**Lollapalooza 2013 No-Show** : In the summer of 2013, Death Grips gained notoriety for one of their most famous incidents: their no-show at Lollapalooza in Chicago, where they simply failed to appear. This event is considered a moment when their behavior escalated out of control. "el planton que le ganan al festival Lula Palusa de Chicago cuando directamente no se presentan."
**Consistent No-Show Incidents** : Following the Lollapalooza incident, the band began a pattern of not showing up for concerts, which was perceived by many fans as taunting. "empiezan a no presentarse en los conciertos, empiezan a un poco a tomarle el pelo a muchos fans."
**Fan Reactions to No-Shows** : Incidents occurred at several concerts where Death Grips fans, frustrated by the band's absence, stormed the stage and destroyed instruments before the band even arrived. This behavior is described as a calculated mix of chaos, aggression, and artistic representation orchestrated by the band. "en varios conciertos los fans de Dead Gryps arrasaron con el escenario antes de que ellos llegaran destrozando los instrumentos, una mezcla entre caos agresividad y representación artística calculada por la banda."
**Lack of Traditional Interviews** : Death Grips is known for not giving interviews, making it difficult to gain insight into their work. They provide information primarily through their Facebook page, which is considered the main source of information about the band. "Dead Gryps no dan entrevistas, no hacen ningún tipo, dan bastante información ellos por si mismos en su Facebook que yo creo que es la principal fuente de información que tenemos en la banda pero no tenemos entrevistas."
**NME Interview (2012)** : The band gave one of their most famous interviews to NME (Neo Musical Express) in 2012. Although published relatively recently and without a full transcription, NME recounted what the band said, providing the only known quasi-interview and affirming their self-perception as conceptual artists rather than just musicians. "la más famosa es la que hicieron en 2012 para NEO Musical Express, que además tampoco ha dado fue publicada hasta hacer relativamente poco y en la que sólo NEO Musical Express nos cuenta un poco lo que dicen pero no ponen realmente una transcripción de esa entrevista."
**Self-Identification as Conceptual Artists** : In their NME interview, Death Grips explicitly stated that they consider themselves more "conceptual artists, or whatever that may be," rather than simply musicians. This self-perception informs their artistic approach and chaotic public persona. "ellos nos hacen referencia a que ellos se consideran más unos artistas conceptuales o lo que quiera que eso sea que unos músicos se por sí."
**David Bowie's Admiration** : David Bowie was a notable admirer of Death Grips, particularly during the period of their double album, "The Powers That B." "con David Bowie que flipaba también con toda esta parte de los Dead Gryps."
**Pizorno (Kasabian) Admiration** : Sergio Pizzorno from Kasabian was greatly impressed by Death Grips, especially during the era of "The Powers That B." "el pizorno de los casavian que dice que flipa con los Dead Gryps, sobre todo con esta etapa."
**Fake Interview / Instrumental Release (2016)** : Around the time of a perceived "half-separation," the band posted a 30-minute video on their YouTube channel, misleadingly titled "Death Grips Interview." Fans rushed to watch it, only to discover it was a lengthy, unreleased instrumental piece, which was later uploaded to Spotify. "publicaron esa extraña entre vista que colgaron en su canal de YouTube de 30 pico minutos, que ponía entre vista de Dryps, que todos fuimos como locos, a verla y que realmente era mentira, era una especie de enorme pieza instrumental de 30 pico minutos inédita de mucho material de Dead Gryps."
**Chaotic Release and Distribution Strategy** : Acquiring a Death Grips album is notoriously difficult due to their unconventional and chaotic release methods. They engage in partial collaborations with distributors that don't result in normal production, lead to items never hitting the market, or cause material to be edited and withdrawn. They frequently upload and remove content from YouTube, SoundCloud, and Facebook, making it extremely hard to track their releases. "es complicadísimo seguir la pista de Dryps, ellos lo ponen muy complicado, porque además tienen varios que se retienen."
**Multiple Unverified Social Media Accounts** : Adding to the complexity, Death Grips maintains several YouTube channels, Facebook accounts, and Twitter accounts. It is often unconfirmed whether these accounts are officially run by the band, making their online presence exhausting to follow. "tienen varios canales de YouTube, varias cuentas de Facebook, varias cuentas de Twitter, que ni siquiera nos confirman si son ellos, sino son ellos, es verdaderamente agotadora."
**Overall Impact on Underground and Experimental Culture** : Death Grips is credited with completely rewriting underground culture and experimental culture, making them a profoundly relevant and impactful group. Their entire body of work is described as truly fascinating. "el grupo que rescribe toda la cultura underground, ha reescrito toda la cultura experimental, yo creo que prácticamente todo el trabajo de Dryps es verdaderamente apasionante."
**Comparison to 1970s Punk** : The band's relevance and impact are considered so significant that they are compared to the punk movement of the 1970s, suggesting a similar level of cultural disruption and influence. "no teníamos a un grupo tan relevante y sobre todo tan impactante, posiblemente desde el pan de los 70."
**2017 Unreleased/Free Material** : Throughout 2017, Death Grips continued to publish a significant amount of material for free, consistent with their usual approach. However, this material was not officially released in a conventional format. "Durante todo ese año 2017 hemos seguido teniendo muchísimo material publicado de Dead Gryps, pero como hacen ellos de manera gratuita pero no ha vuelto a salir publicado como material."

# label - Epic Records

## curiosities
**Signing of Death Grips (2012)** : Epic Records signed Death Grips in early 2012 for a contract spanning two or three albums, having been impressed by the rapid circulation and popularity of their "Ex-Military" mixtape. This signing marked the beginning of a "very complicated relationship" between the band and the label. "en cuanto la caza Epic Records a los pocos meses en el primeros del 2012, Fitch a los Dead Gryps por dos tres discos, una relación que sería muy complicada."
**Dispute over "The Money Store" Release** : A major dispute arose when Death Grips released their album "The Money Store" for free online before its scheduled market release, causing significant friction with Epic Records. "tendrían una gran disputa por Epic Records porque ellos la publicarían de manera gratuita antes de salir al mercado como disco."
**Conflict over "No Love Deep Web" Release and Censorship** : Epic Records faced further problems with Death Grips regarding the release of "No Love Deep Web," which was delayed for months due to the label's issues with its controversial and censored cover art. "Esto generaría más problemas todavía de Dead Gryps con Epic Records y al final el lanzamiento de No Love de Web quedaría redrasado durante meses por enormes problemas censurados con su portada."
**Refusal to Allow Rapid Album Release** : Epic Records refused to permit Death Grips to release a third album within a year, stating that releasing three albums in such a short period was "a bit of an abuse." This refusal led to further escalation of tensions. "Epic Records se niega porque dice que sacar tres albumes en un año que ya empieza a ser un poco un abuso."
**Dismissal of Death Grips from Label** : After Death Grips defiantly released portions of their unapproved third album for free, Epic Records became "very angry" with them and ultimately expelled the band from the label. "Epic Records acaba muy cabreada con ellos y los acaba expulsando de la disco gráfica."
**Leaked Emails and Monetization Efforts** : Following their dismissal, Death Grips published private emails between the band and Epic Records. These emails exposed the complicated relationship and revealed "somewhat murky" attempts by Epic Records to further monetize Death Grips' music. "se veía esa complicada relación y asuntos son tanto turbios de Epic Records de cómo intentaba monetizar más todavía la música de Dead Gryps."
**Threat of Lawsuit** : The publication of the private emails on Facebook almost led to a substantial lawsuit from Epic Records against Death Grips, though the legal action ultimately did not materialize. "Casi les vale una demanda bastante importante de Epic Records por la publicación de todos estos correos electrónicos en Facebook pero al final se queda en nada."

# curiosity

**Death Grips' Creative Output** : In a span of just six years, Death Grips experienced an "enormous explosion of creativity," producing a vast amount of material characterized by its truly artistic and rare blend of genres. "en apenas seis años ha sido una enorme eclosión de creatividad, han tenido muchísimo material en apenas seis años."
**Influence of Death Grips on Artists** : Death Grips has been influential to many artists, particularly in the underground scene. Notable figures like David Bowie and Pizorno of Kasabian expressed great admiration for their work, especially during the period of "The Powers That B" double album. "tenemos influencias en míos, tenemos influencias en muchísima gente de todo este trabajo underground."
