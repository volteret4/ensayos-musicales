## albums
**The Velvet Underground & Nico (1967) - Debut Album** : Released in March 1967, this album featured Nico and was produced by Andy Warhol, famously sporting a banana on its cover. It is widely considered by many as the beginning of underground, alternative, and experimental music. Recording began in April 1966 at Scepter Studios in New York. The production was quick as most material was already composed from John Cale and Lou Reed's demos. Integrating Nico proved challenging, ultimately leading her to sing some songs due to Warhol's insistence. The album was finished by mid-1966 but delayed due to significant censorship over its strong lyrics about masochism, sexual themes, and drugs (including the song "Heroin"). An executive from Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer initially called it "shit," but ironically, his Verve Records division ultimately released it. Further delays were caused by Warhol's insistence on a peelable banana sticker for the cover, making production expensive and complex. Despite the delays, it was a massive commercial failure upon release, with no promotional support from the record label. "se considera por muchos el inicio de la música underground, posiblemente el inicio de la música alternativa, música experimental."
**The Velvet Underground (post-1967) - Second Album** : Released after the departure of Nico and Andy Warhol, this album was described as much more intimate, measured, and poetic. It offered a more realistic portrayal of the band's original vision, uninfluenced by external actors. "sería mucho más intimista, mucho más pausado, mucho más poético y que nos da una visión mucho más realista de lo que realmente era la banda."

## curiosities
**Difficult Creative Triangle** : The debut album by The Velvet Underground was marked by a difficult creative triangle formed by the band (Lou Reed and John Cale), Nico, and producer Andy Warhol, a collaboration that never continued beyond that first album. "Es ese difícil triángulo que formaron la velbetander round nico y Andy Warhol en su primer album y que nunca más continuó."
**Book "Sí, In the Light, Inside the Velvet Underground"** : This book, available in a 2012 edition and still sold today, is considered a definitive source for the band's history. It details this period extensively through interviews and even parts narrated directly by Lou Reed, focusing on the more human, rather than purely musical, aspects of The Velvet Underground. "del libro Sí, In the Light, Inside the Velvet Underground, que es induda a uno de los libros por excelencia que nos habla de la historia un tanto bastante más humana, no tanto musical de la velbetander round."
**Early Band Names** : Between 1963 and 1967, before their debut album, the project led by John Cale and Lou Reed went through several name changes, including The Primitives, Ball Rocks, and Falling Spice. "se van llamando de primitives, luego ball rocks y Falling Spice."
**Falling Spice Demos (1965)** : Under the name Falling Spice, the band self-produced material, including a famous demo tape recorded in their apartment in 1965. This demo featured early versions of tracks like "All Tomorrow's Parties," "Heroin," and "Venus in Furs," and represented a more intimate, poetic, and slower original vision for the band, resembling their later second album more than their debut with Nico. "con el nombre de Falling Spice ya tenemos material auto producido por ellos, tenemos esa famosa cinta pirata o esa famosa maqueta que se grabó en 1965 en su propia apartamento."
**Demo Tape Journey to UK (1965)** : In 1965, John Cale took the Falling Spice demo tape to the United Kingdom, aiming to give it to Marianne Faithfull, Mick Jagger's partner, hoping she would pass it to Jagger or Andre Ollman (Andrew Loog Oldham) of The Rolling Stones. While the trip is confirmed, there is no evidence the tape ever left Faithfull's hands or reached Jagger. "John Kaley se la llevaría al reino unido en 1965 en uno de sus viajes para intentar darse la Marian Faithful."
**Re-release of Demos (1995)** : The famous 1965 demo tape, initially self-produced as Falling Spice, was later re-released in 1995 as part of a comprehensive box set edition for their debut album, The Velvet Underground & Nico. "luego acabaría reditar en 1995 en esa redición de caja tan potente que hicieron del disco de la velbetanda de Realme Inico."
**Origin of Band Name** : The name "The Velvet Underground" was adopted around late 1965, originating from a 1963 book about masochism titled "The Velvet Underground." However, the exact circumstances of how the band members acquired the book and officially adopted the name remain unclear, with conflicting accounts even from the members themselves regarding who delivered the book to them. "el nombre sale de un libro publicado en 1963 sobre masochismo que se llama de Ve train de Grom."
**Warhol's Patronage and The Factory Residency (Late 1965/Early 1966)** : Andy Warhol, impressed by The Velvet Underground's performance (likely in New York), immediately took the band, particularly John Cale and Lou Reed, under his wing, bringing them into his creative hub, The Factory. This marked the beginning of a profoundly difficult relationship between all parties. "Automáticamente coge la banda principalmente a John Kale y a Lou Reed y se los lleva a la factory."
**Aesthetic Focus vs. Musical Focus** : During their collaboration with Andy Warhol, Warhol was intensely focused on the aesthetic development of The Velvet Underground, having little interest in the music itself. In contrast, Lou Reed and John Cale were primarily dedicated to developing their own musical material. "Andy Warhol, más que en la música que no era un gran aficionada la música, estaba muy centrado en el desarrollo estético de la velvita underground, Lou Reed y John Kale estaban muy centrados en trabajar su propio material."
**Featured in "Exploding Plastic Inevitable" (February-March 1966)** : The band's rehearsals, featuring Nico for the first time, were captured in Andy Warhol's iconic documentary "Exploding Plastic Inevitable," filmed around February-March 1966. During this period, Warhol incorporated many aesthetic elements that would become iconic for the band. "se grababa ese fantástico documental que yo creo que es icónico que es el explodimplastic inevitable de Andy Warhol en el que vemos todos estos ensayos de la velvita underground."
**Iconic Sunglasses Look** : Andy Warhol mandated that the band members wear sunglasses during all their performances, a directive that became a highly recognizable and iconic aesthetic element of The Velvet Underground. "lo obliga a ir todo el tiempo con gafas de sol en las actuaciones que luego serían tan reconocibles por eso."
**Unilaterally Designed Posters (1966)** : As early as 1966, Andy Warhol began designing and selling posters for The Velvet Underground completely unilaterally, without informing the band or showing them the designs. Band members would sometimes discover their own posters being sold after finishing concerts, having never seen them before. "Andy Warhol ya empieza a diseñar posters de la velvita underground, ya vendía primero en 1966 ya tenemos posters diseñados por Andy Warhol que se vendían sobre la banda, esto lo había hecho de manera totalmente unilateral sin ni siquiera avisar a la banda y sin ni si quieren enseñarles los posters que estaba vendiendo."
**Andy Warhol's Chaotic Production Style** : John Cale and Lou Reed documented Andy Warhol's chaotic and frustrating approach during the recording of their debut album. Warhol, largely uninterested in the music, focused solely on the band's aesthetic, often changing their clothes during sessions if he disliked an outfit, such as John Cale's shirt. "tenemos muchos textos John Kaylee de Lurif que de esa época que nos hablaba del caótico trabajo que hacía Andy Warhol durante la grabación que molde estaba muchísimo, Andy Warhol la música no le interesaba especialmente."
**Censorship and Delay of Debut Album** : The debut album, "The Velvet Underground & Nico," faced immense censorship problems because of its strong lyrics discussing masochism, unusual sexual tendencies, and drugs, including the track "Heroin." This controversial content led to significant delays in its release, as many industry figures were reluctant to get involved. "el primero y evidente era un enorme problema de censura, las letras eran muy fortes, hablaban de masochismo, hablaban de tendencias sexuales, un tanto extrañas, hablaban de drogas, había la camión de heroin."
**Commercial Failure of Debut Album** : "The Velvet Underground & Nico" was a significant commercial failure, a fate largely predicted by the record label which refused to undertake any promotion due to the album's controversial themes. The band had hoped Andy Warhol's production and cover art would help, but it ultimately did not. "acá veo eso siendo un fracaso como la discurráfica había baticinado y la relación que tenía la velvetanda gran original que eran Lurri de John Kale con el resto de la banda, con Nico y Andy Warhol era cada vez más tensa."
**Dismissal of Warhol and Nico (Post-1967)** : The relationship between core members Lou Reed and John Cale and Andy Warhol and Nico became increasingly tense, leading Lou Reed to fire both Warhol as producer/manager and Nico as a band member. "Lurri despide Andy Warhol como productor y manaller de la banda y despide a Nico como miembro de la banda."
**"True Work" Began After Warhol & Nico's Departure** : For Lou Reed and John Cale, the band's "true work" and original vision began after the dismissal of Nico and Andy Warhol as manager/producer. Their subsequent second album reflected a more intimate, measured, and poetic style, providing a more realistic view of what the band truly was before external influences altered their project. "para la banda realmente para Lurri y para John Kale, ellos en durante mucho tiempo consideraron que fue a partir de ahí del despido de Nico y de Andy Warhol como manaller cuando empieza el verdadero trabajo de la velvete de Ground."

**Early Career as Professional Composer** : Before co-founding The Velvet Underground, Lou Reed had played in several local bands and worked as a professional composer for other artists and for a small record label. "trabajaba como compositor profesional para otros artistas y para una pequeña discográfica."

**Fires Andy Warhol and Nico** : Lou Reed eventually fired Andy Warhol as the band's producer and manager, and also dismissed Nico as a band member, due to the increasingly strained relationship within the group. "Lurri despide Andy Warhol como productor y manaller de la banda y despide a Nico como miembro de la banda."

**Child Prodigy and Classical Music Background** : John Cale, originally from England but residing in the United States, was an immense musical talent. He began studying music professionally at age five and was composing classical music by age eight, having emigrated to the U.S. to work on experimental classical music. "empezó a estudiar música profesionalmente con cinco años y componía música clásica desde los ocho y de hecho había emigrado Estados Unidos para trabajar en música experimental clásica, era un grandísimo genio."

# artist - Nico

## member of
The Velvet Underground

## instruments

## curiosities
**Background as Actress and Model** : Nico, of German origin, was an actress and model working within Andy Warhol's Factory before being introduced to and joining The Velvet Underground. "Nico era una de las actrices modelo realmente que estaba dentro de la factory y de origen alemán que trabajaba con Andy Warhol."
**Difficult Integration into The Velvet Underground** : Nico's integration into The Velvet Underground was challenging because she reportedly could not sing well and faced significant difficulties pronouncing English due to her German origin. Lou Reed famously quipped that it was normal for Nico to play the tambourine all the time because she couldn't do anything else. "Nico no sabía cantar, cantaba muy mal, no sablaba bien inglés, hecho tenía bastantes problemas para pronunciar inglés porque era origen alemán."

# artist - Andy Warhol

## curiosities
**Discovery of The Velvet Underground (Late 1965/Early 1966)** : Andy Warhol discovered The Velvet Underground between late 1965 and early 1966. There are contradictory accounts regarding who precisely introduced the band to Warhol, with cinematic collaborators Querantón Morricay (Gerard Malanga) and Barbara Rabin (Barbara Rubin) mentioned as possibilities. "entre final de 1965 y el principio de 1966 es cuando Andy Warhol encuentra la Albert Anderson."
**Insistence on Nico Joining The Velvet Underground** : Andy Warhol introduced Nico, a German actress and model from The Factory, to The Velvet Underground. He felt the band needed a "beautiful woman" and was obsessed with Nico at the time, thereby forcing her into the band against the opinions of John Cale and Lou Reed. "Andy Warhol le parecía que hacía falta una mujer guapa dentro de la velvita underground. Entonces para él la idea perfecta era Nico en ese tiempo estaba sesionado con ella y entonces pues la introduce un poco con calzador en contra de la opinión de John Kale y de Lou Reed."
**Promotion of The Velvet Underground (Early 1966)** : Andy Warhol secured numerous small concerts and art showcases for The Velvet Underground, actively promoting them within the New York art world. "Andy Warhol ya le consigue bastantes pequeños conciertos, ensaras artísticas, incluso ya los empiezan mover por la jefe del art de New York."
**Album Cover Design & Peelable Banana** : Andy Warhol designed the iconic album cover for "The Velvet Underground & Nico," famously insisting on a peelable banana sticker. This creative decision led to significant production challenges and delays, making the album costly and complex to manufacture, but Warhol refused to allow its release without this specific artistic element. "Andy Warhol había diseñado la portada, pero está posicionado con que el plátano de la portada fuese una pegatina que se pudiese quitar."
**MGM Executive's Reaction to Album** : After hearing "The Velvet Underground & Nico," a Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer executive famously told Andy Warhol, "Andy, esto es una mierda" ("Andy, this is shit"). Ironically, this same individual was responsible for the Verve Records division and ultimately oversaw the album's release. "un director, un director ejecutivo de la Metro Golden Mayor después de escucharlo, le decía Andy Warhol la famosa frase de Andy, esto es una mierda."
**Reaction to Being Fired** : According to John Cale, while Andy Warhol initially claimed to take his dismissal well after Lou Reed fired him, he directly called Lou Reed a "dirty rat" and never spoke to him again, indicating a very sour end to their relationship. "Andy Warhol se lo tomó muy bien pero que, de girectamente, le dijo a Lurri que era una sucia rata y nunca más le volvió a hablar."

## curiosities
**Release of The Velvet Underground & Nico** : Verve Records, a division of Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer, was the label responsible for finally releasing The Velvet Underground & Nico in March 1967, after significant delays due to censorship and Warhol's demanding production requirements, such as the peelable banana sticker. "después de aceptarse por la discurráfica berb que pertenecía a Metro Golden Mayor, la edición de ese disco acababa volviendo a tener meses de retraso."

# curiosity

**The Factory - Andy Warhol's Creative Hub** : The Factory was Andy Warhol's renowned creative space where he collaborated with a diverse group of artists, including actors, dancers, and filmmakers. It became a significant hub for artistic innovation and a central point for the early career of The Velvet Underground under Warhol's patronage. "la factory que era ese espacio donde él lacía toda la creatividad con actores, bailarines, directores de cine."
