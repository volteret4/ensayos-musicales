## albums
**Ultra (1997) - Emotional Introspection** : Released in the mid-90s, Ultra was recorded during a profoundly difficult and crucial period for Depeche Mode, following Alan Wilder's departure and amidst the severe personal struggles of Dave Gahan and Martin Gore. The album initially received mixed critical reviews, but its standing has significantly grown over the years, becoming a beloved work for many fans due to its intense emotional depth. It explores a more intimate sound, reflecting the members' personal challenges and their eventual journey toward recovery. "Es un disco que con los años ha ganado muchísimo carisma."
**Ultra (1997) - Production and Influences** : Ultra marked a significant shift in Depeche Mode's musical direction, benefiting from the departure of Alan Wilder, which allowed the band to open up to new sonic horizons. The album incorporated influences from genres like Trip Hop, Krautrock, and German industrial music, moving away from synthesizer-based structures towards more complex, computerized foundations. It also featured an extensive list of collaborating musicians, contributing to a more complex and musically rich sound that defined the band's post-Wilder era. "Tenemos influencias de muchas otras estilos de música que posiblemente no hubiese sido posible todavía con el álbum, como esas influencias del Trip Hub que era tan potente a la mitad de los 90."

## songs
**Home (1997)** : Sung by Martin Gore, "Home" was considered too good to remain an instrumental track and became one of the key emotional pieces on Ultra. Despite its potentially romantic or cheerful theme of finding refuge, Gore's rendition conveys a melancholic, sad, and exhausted tone, reflecting the difficult personal situation of the band members. The song speaks to the idea of a safe place where one is forgiven and can return to oneself, regardless of past mistakes or negative changes. "Es la canción que nos cuenta como a pesar de lo mal que hagas las cosas en la vida, los errores que cometas, lo mal que te comportes al final siempre hay un sitio al que puedes un poco huir y refugiarte."

## curiosities
**Band's Low Point (1995)** : The year 1995 marked the lowest point for Depeche Mode, characterized by internal turmoil and severe personal crises among its members. Dave Gahan was battling a life-threatening heroin addiction, Martin Gore was struggling with alcoholism and personal relationship problems, and Alan Wilder, citing the "unsustainable situation," decided to leave the band. This period led to widespread speculation in the press that the band was finished or dissolved. "1995 fue posiblemente el momento más bajo de la banda y yo creo que fue ese momento en el que tocaron fondo."
**Near Dissolution and Reforming (1996)** : Following Alan Wilder's departure in 1995, the remaining members, Andy Fletcher and Martin Gore, began working on new material in 1996. During this time, Dave Gahan's severe heroin addiction made recording with him nearly impossible, leading to serious discussions about expelling him from the group, finding a new singer, or even Martin Gore embarking on a solo career with Ultra potentially becoming his first solo album supported by Fletcher. The band's future was truly uncertain. "Ahí ya fue cuando prácticamente se llevaba plantear la disolución y que ultra fuese el primer disco hecho ya en la carrera Martin Gorko con el apoyo de Andrew Fishery."
**Emotional and Intimate Album** : Ultra is considered by many to be Depeche Mode's most emotional and intimate album, serving as a raw reflection of the personal struggles and eventual healing of its members. The album vividly captures Martin Gore's sadness, aggression, and exhaustion from years of personal battles, alongside Dave Gahan's physically deteriorated state and his remarkably sad, decadent, and subdued vocal performances, all indicative of the profound personal challenges they faced. "Es un álbum emocionalmente muy intimista porque tenemos aquí yo creo que los dos grandes momentos emocionales de los osotabonistas completamente plasmados."

# artist - Dave Gahan

## member of
Depeche Mode

## curiosities
**Severe Heroin Addiction (Mid-1990s)** : In the mid-1990s, Dave Gahan suffered from a life-threatening heroin addiction, which led to multiple hospitalizations due to overdoses. This addiction rendered him physically debilitated and made recording new music with Depeche Mode, particularly for the album Ultra, extremely challenging, almost leading to his expulsion from the band. His struggle was a major factor in the band's critical period and his appearance in the Ultra music videos reflected his poor physical state. "Él estaba en un tan nivel de adició a la heroína y un estado físico tan debleque que trabajar con él era prácticamente imposible."
**Near-Fatal Overdose and Court-Ordered Rehabilitation** : During the recording process for Ultra, Dave Gahan experienced a severe overdose that nearly cost him his life, resulting in another hospitalization. This terrifying event, combined with court intervention, compelled him to enter a mandatory rehabilitation program. This forced rehabilitation was a crucial turning point, not only saving his life but also the future of Depeche Mode. "Se produzo una de las enormes sobre dosis que tiene que valo hospital en la que casi se muere y empieza un poco el resurgir del cantante."
**Supervised Recording of Ultra** : Dave Gahan's rehabilitation process was so critical that judicial authorities supervised the recording sessions for Depeche Mode's album Ultra. This oversight was deemed necessary due to the significant presence of heroin, alcohol, and other drugs during the band's recording processes at that time, highlighting the severe environment they were in. The intervention ultimately helped save the band from dissolution and Gahan from potential death. "La sonidad de Santinar Códipo estuvieron supervisando la grabación de la Albuo Muttra porque había tal cantidad de heroína, de alcohol y de drogas en los procesos de grabación de Petchmode en esa época."
**Physical Deterioration and Vocal Performance on Ultra** : During the period of Ultra's creation and release, Dave Gahan was visibly deteriorated physically, as evidenced in the album's music videos where he appeared in a "truly lamentable state." His vocal performance on the album is described as the saddest, most exhausting, and most decadent of his entire career, reflecting his near-death experience and the profound emotional and physical toll of his addiction. "Vemos ahí un Dave Bajan tan deteriorado físicamente en los video clips pero ya incluso en su voz lo hemos tan apagado, tan rendido, tan alborde de la muerte."

# artist - Martin Gore

## member of
Depeche Mode

## curiosities
**Struggles with Alcoholism and Personal Problems (Mid-1990s)** : In the mid-1990s, concurrent with the band's lowest point, Martin Gore faced significant personal challenges, including problems with alcoholism and intense sentimental issues. These struggles put him in a very difficult period of his life and contributed to the overall tension within Depeche Mode. His emotional state, marked by sadness, aggression, and exhaustion from sleeplessness and relationship conflicts, is palpably present in the Ultra album. "Martin Gorko muchos problemas al colismo pero también con muchos problemas sentimentales, él estaba en un momento muy malo de su vida."
**Consideration of a Solo Career** : During the period of uncertainty surrounding Depeche Mode's future and Dave Gahan's addiction, Martin Gore came very close to initiating a solo career. There were discussions that the album Ultra might even become his first solo record, with support from Andy Fletcher. Although he eventually released solo work later, this period marked a serious consideration of branching out on his own. "Martin Gorko estuvo a punto de arrancar una carrera en solitario prácticamente desde el inicio de la banda que hoy si que la tenemos tenemos algunos trabajos en Martin Gorko."
**Increased Role Post-Wilder's Departure** : Following Alan Wilder's departure from Depeche Mode, Martin Gore stepped into a more prominent and comprehensive creative role within the band. He became "completely euphoric," engaging more deeply in composing, recording, and producing the band's music, particularly evident in the creation of Ultra. This shift allowed his talent to shine more brightly, shaping the band's new sound. "Vemos un martingor completamente pletórico, componiendo, grabando, produciendo."

# artist - Alan Wilder

## member of
Depeche Mode (former)

## curiosities
**Departure from Depeche Mode (1995)** : Alan Wilder, considered by some to be the "great talent" of Depeche Mode, left the band in 1995 due to what he perceived as an "unsustainable situation." This departure occurred amidst Dave Gahan's severe heroin addiction and Martin Gore's personal struggles, contributing to widespread media speculation about the band's imminent demise. His departure was a major turning point for the band, marking the end of an era. "Alan Wilder que era posiblemente de aquellas. El gran talento de Petchmode que había abandonado el grupo un poco en esa situación insostenible."
**Controversial but Beneficial Departure** : Alan Wilder's departure sparked significant controversy among Depeche Mode fans, particularly because he held a very clear and defined vision for the band's sound, especially concerning production. While his presence undeniably embodied a crucial part of the band's essence, his leaving ultimately proved beneficial in the long run. It allowed the remaining members to explore new musical horizons, incorporate diverse influences like Trip Hop and Krautrock, and adopt more complex, computerized electronic structures not previously possible under his more synthesiser-focused approach. "Mucha gente yo creo que también ha sido así, a la larga ha sido bastante beneficiosa porque por un lado a la banda le permitió abrirse a nuevos horizontes."

# curiosity

**Journalistic Reaction to Depeche Mode's Crisis (Mid-1990s)** : In the mid-1990s, as Depeche Mode faced a severe internal crisis marked by Dave Gahan's addiction, Martin Gore's personal struggles, and Alan Wilder's departure, the press reacted harshly. Journalists widely reported the band as "dead" or "dissolved," signaling what they believed to be the end not only of the group but possibly of their entire artistic careers. This intense media scrutiny underscored the critical state of the band. "La prensa se echa encima de la banda. Prácticamente ya lo sababa por muertos, lo sababa por disueltos, lo sababa como ya el final no solo de la banda sino posiblemente de la carrera artística de ellos."
