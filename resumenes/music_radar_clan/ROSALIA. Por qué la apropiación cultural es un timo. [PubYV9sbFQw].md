# genre - Flamenco

## curiosities
**Complex and Purist Style** : Flamenco is described as a very purist and closed style, particularly among its enthusiasts, and is often perceived as resistant to external influences. However, despite this perception, the genre has been consistently influenced by other cultures for decades. "el flamenco siempre ha sido un estilo muy purista y muy cerrado entre los grandes amantes del flamenco y a veces son tanto cerrado a las aperturas de otros lugares, lo cual no tiene mucho sentido teniendo en cuenta que el flamenco como tal, desde el más puro estilo flamenco lleva teniendo influencias culturales y otras culturas durante décadas."
**Historical External Influences** : Despite conservative views, flamenco has always received cultural exchanges. Examples include Raimundo Amador's collaborations with blues master BB King and Camarón de la Isla's landmark album *La Leyenda del Tiempo*, which incorporated funk, disco, and electronic keyboards. These instances demonstrate flamenco's intrinsic history of evolving through external inspiration. "el flamenco lleva recibiendo intercambios culturales desde siempre, nos gustó no está ahí."
**Damaging Styles (Pre-Rosalía)** : The speaker argues that if Rosalía is accused of damaging flamenco, previous styles caused more harm without significant criticism. These include a "cutre rumba flamenquera" mixed with child exploitation, and "flamenco chilao" (chill-out flamenco) from the early 2000s, which involved DJs and "Vicencos" (producers/DJs) and was tolerated by major flamenco artists because it generated significant income. "el flamenco solo cargó melodía con esa mezcla de rumba flamenquera cutre mezclada con explotación infantil. El flamenco se lo cargó el flamenco chila otra, primero es el 2000."
**Global Influence and Adaptation** : Despite being a culturally concentrated art form, flamenco has influenced half the world for many years due to its passionate and intense artistic movements. People from around the globe, such as Chinese and Japanese individuals, have become highly skilled flamenco guitarists, demonstrating its universal appeal and the ability of non-native practitioners to excel. "el flamenco lleva muchísimos años y décadas influenciando a medio mundo, porque a ver, nos gustó no de los movimientos artísticos más pasionales y más intensos del mundo."
**Origins Research and Complexity** : For over 50 years since the 1950s, extensive musical research in Spain has sought to trace the origins of flamenco, but no consensus has been reached due to its diffuse and complex beginnings. It is known to date back to the 18th century as a complex cultural mix primarily in Southern Spain (Andalusia, Murcia, Extremadura, southern Castilla-La Mancha) and also along the Moroccan coast, and even has nuclei in Latin America. "en España llevamos más de 50 años en rastreando muchísimo los origenes del flamenco. A nivel musical, a nivel de historia de la música en España es algo que ha ocupado muchísima investigación musical porque los inicios del flamenco son muy difusos, son muy complejos y se pierden en el tiempo."
**Diverse Cultural Influences in Origin** : The origins of flamenco in Southern Spain are described as a "crucible of cultural exchanges," drawing significant influences from the Roma (gitana) ethnicity, general Andalusian folklore, Moorish influences from North Africa, and Sephardic influences. While the Roma community has been crucial in developing, transforming, and deepening flamenco, it is inaccurate to claim exclusive ownership, as many cultures contributed. "los orígenes del flamenco se rastrean en el sur de España porque hay un crisis de intercambios culturales que cogen importantes influencias de la enniagitana, cogen importantes influencias del folclobre Andalucía en general de influencias moriscas que vienen de todo el norte de la África Média y de ciertas influencias se fardías."

# curiosity

**The Concept of Cultural Appropriation - Origins in Fashion** : The concept of cultural appropriation, while relatively new in music, has been a contentious issue in the fashion industry for decades. It first gained prominence in the 1970s when designers like Yves Saint Laurent faced criticism for collections inspired by cultures such as Siberia, Mongolia, China, the Himalaya, and Saharan Africa. "El concepto de apropiación cultural en no es nuevo, es bastante nuevo en la música, pero por ejemplo en la moda, donde yo trabajo lo llevamos ofriendo décadas, es un malendémico."
**John Galliano and Cultural Appropriation Accusations** : Fashion designer John Galliano has consistently faced accusations of cultural appropriation throughout his career. This includes criticisms for a collection inspired by the Sacred Heart (religious appropriation), for incorporating influences from Sidh culture in a collaboration with Oscar de la Renta, and for collections inspired by Mongolia and the Himalaya. "El pobre Yungaliano es algo que la verdad es que llevas frente a toda su vida, desde la colección que hizo inspirada en el sagrado corazón, mi símbolo religioso, que era el acuso de apropiación cultural religiosa."
**Legal Precedent for Cultural Appropriation - The Navajo Case** : A significant turning point for the concept of cultural appropriation, particularly its legal formalization, occurred in the early 2000s. Major brands like Christian Dior used the term "Navajo" and Navajo-inspired designs for their collections, leading to legal action by the Navajo Native Americans in the United States. The Navajo won, establishing an important precedent that intangible cultural values can be legally registered as rights and pursued judicially for economic benefits. "A primeros el 2000, muchas marcas importantes, por ejemplo Christian Dior, entre otras muchísimas, hicieron varias colecciones inspiradas en los nativos americanos, y no solo se inspiraron en los nativos americanos sino que usaron la palabra navajo para definir algunas de las colecciones y algunas de las prendas."
**Controversy of the Navajo Legal Precedent** : The legal process involving the Navajo case was highly controversial. It was the first time an intangible cultural value could be legally registered for monetary gain. The controversy also stemmed from the registration of the word "Navajo," which had long been used generically as a synonym for Native American, and the registration of certain generic Native American values under the "Navajo" designation, requiring payment and permission for its use today. "ese proceso legal y estas intensas fue normalmente polémica porque aparte de ser la primera vez que legalmente se podría registrar un valor cultural intangible por gente, también fue bastante polémica porque fue muy curiosa, porque se registró la palabra navajo, aunque ya desde siempre se había utilizado como sinónimo de indioamericano, y se registraron incluso ciertos valores nativos americanos que ni siquiera eran navajos, que eran genericos o los nativos americanos, pero que consiguieron registrar como navajos."
**The United Kingdom's Openness to Cultural Influences** : The United Kingdom is presented as an exemplary country for its open-mindedness towards cultural influences and its ability to effectively manage them throughout history. Examples include the Rolling Stones' reinterpretation of African-American Southern rock, The Beatles' adoption of Indian music, the integration of Jamaican music into British pop, and the influence of Detroit techno on British rave movements, all without significant accusations of appropriation. "el Reino Unido nos gustó, no nos guste, es el ejemplo perfecto de cómo saber gestionar bien las influencias culturales de otros lugares."
**Spain's Reluctance to Cultural Influences** : Despite its history as the largest colonial power, Spain is portrayed as a country that has been notably reluctant to import cultural influences and has not managed them well. This has led to historical examples of failing to properly value or integrate external cultural imports, even to the detriment of its own artists who engage in such fusions. "España por desgracia es el perfecto ejemplo de cómo no saber gestionar bien las influencias culturales."
**Critique of Cultural Appropriation Claims** : The speaker views the concept of cultural appropriation as a "senseless mess" that is fundamentally unjust and unrealistic about how cultural influences and musical development actually work. It is argued that such claims can be dangerous by incorrectly assigning cultural rights and are often driven by economic monopoly interests seeking to monetize cultural values. "el concepto de apropiación cultural es una auténtica mamá rachada sin sentido y hay ciertos intereses de monopolio económico que hay detrás, que deberíamos reaccionar ante ellos porque no deberíamos permitirlos."
**Spanish Language as Shared Cultural Property** : The Spanish language is presented as an example of a cultural element that, while originating in Spain, now belongs to half the world. This illustrates how cultural creations evolve beyond their points of origin and become shared property, challenging notions of exclusive ownership. "El español es hablar medio mundo hace mucho tiempo que es de todos."
