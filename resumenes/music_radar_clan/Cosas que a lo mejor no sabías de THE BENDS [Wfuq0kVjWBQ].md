## albums
**The Bends (1995) - Recording Challenges** : The recording of *The Bends* took almost a year, marked by numerous difficulties including Thom Yorke's first significant depression, internal band tensions, and interruptions for supporting concerts. The producer frequently had to leave the project for other commitments, and EMI's demands led to many changes and complete reinterpretations of songs. The album was recorded in three different studios and underwent multiple remixes before its release, resulting in several delays. "Entonces tras varios retrasos el proceso de grabación y producción del disco llevó casi un año."
**The Bends (1995) - Critical Reception** : While *The Bends* is now widely acclaimed with high scores (9/10 or 10/10) in 21st-century reviews, its initial reception was more modest, particularly in the United States, where reviews were relatively mediocre. Critics generally expressed a feeling of disappointment following the strong hype generated by "Creep." Some original reviews, such as one from Pittsburgh that attacked Thom Yorke and questioned Radiohead's future, were later removed by publications due to embarrassment, reflecting a common practice of rewriting historical music criticism. "En su día las críticas de Benz fueron bastante listas, fueron bastante buenas. En el reino unido fueron relativamente mediocres, en Estados Unidos pero todas coincidieron en un relativo sentimiento de decepción después del hype de Creep."
**The Bends (1995) - Most Singles** : *The Bends* holds the distinction of being Radiohead's album with the most singles released. It featured 5 primary singles, or 6 if "Planet Telex" is counted as a double A-side. Each single also had multiple versions (C1, C2, and sometimes C3), bringing the total number of single releases from the album to over 10, a unique feat in the band's discography. "Es el disco de Radiohead que más single ha tenido. Ha tenido 5 singles como muchos otros discos de Radiohead. 6, si contamos el plan Tlex que sale como cara doble cara a, del single doble cara a, que sacaron en este disco."
**The Bends (1995) - Multiple Producers** : *The Bends* is notable for having numerous accredited producers, although John Leckie was the official and primary producer credited in most places. The album legally registered 5 producers, including Nigel Godrich, who initially worked as a sound engineer but also performed minor production tasks, thus receiving a producer credit. This marked the beginning of his long-term collaboration with Radiohead. Additionally, the album was remixed a second time in the United States, under pressure from EMI and with the band's eventual consent, leading to the American remixers being credited as producers for the final market version. "Realmente el álbum lo produjo en lequí, era el productor oficial del álbum y es el que aparece acreditado en la mayoría de sitios en las que solo aparece acreditado. Un productor aunque tiene 5 legalmente registrados como productores."
**The Bends (1995) - Themes and Public Perception** : Upon its release, *The Bends* garnered significant media attention, particularly in the UK, not only due to the preceding hype from "Creep" but also prominently because of Thom Yorke's perceived depression. The album's lyrics, which were more critical and depressive compared to those on *Pablo Honey*, were widely interpreted as direct reflections of Yorke's mental state. Rumors about the difficult recording process and Yorke's complex emotional condition fueled numerous articles between 1995-1996, many of which focused on his depression and lyrical interpretations. "Las letras de Benz ya se empiezan a ser como más críticas y más depresivas, comparadas con las que teníamos en el Pablo Jonei y mucha gente de interpretaciones muy directas de la depresión que estaba teniendo Tom Jorke o a la que se le estaba tribuyendo."
**Pablo Honey (1993)** : This album featured lyrics that were notably less critical and depressive when compared to those found on its follow-up, *The Bends*. "comparadas con las que teníamos en el Pablo Jonei"

## songs
**Creep (1992)** : This song generated significant hype for Radiohead, which subsequently led to a feeling of disappointment among critics regarding the initial reception of *The Bends*. An acoustic version of "Creep" was also included on the Australian *My Iron Lung* EP, which later saw global distribution due to its success. "después del hype de Creep."
**My Iron Lung (1994)** : Released as a single significantly before *The Bends*, "My Iron Lung" served as a warm-up to gauge market reaction to Radiohead's evolving musical direction. While initially released as a normal single worldwide, an Australian EP version, featuring an acoustic version of "Creep" and eight songs, was later created. This EP's success led to its subsequent re-fabrication and distribution across the rest of the world, though its origin and initial global distribution timeline were distinct from the album. "Si es verdad que Mayeron Lang salió como single antes del disco, bastante antes del disco como calendamiento para ver cómo reaccionaba, un poco el mercado al pequeño cambio de tendencia que había hecho radioje."
**Planet Telex (1995)** : The opening track of *The Bends*, "Planet Telex" was recorded by the entire band one night while they were heavily intoxicated after a period of drinking. Thom Yorke was reportedly so drunk that he could not stand while recording the lyrics, which is reflected in some pronunciation and diction issues throughout the song. Although many elements were later supported and refined during the production phase, the original recording captured the band's inebriated state. "la grabaron estando borrachísimos. Esto es una historia bastante común que muchos artistas, sobre todo británicos, la grabaron una noche volviendo de estar a verdaderamente borrachos toda la banda."
**Fake Plastic Trees (1995)** : Considered one of Radiohead's finest songs and their second most covered track after "Creep," "Fake Plastic Trees" was stylistically inspired by a Jeff Buckley concert in Oxford that Thom Yorke attended while grappling with a creative block for the song. Its recording was a "nightmare," requiring numerous takes—possibly the most for any song on *The Bends*—due to Thom Yorke's severe depression, which caused him to weep frequently. Ultimately, Yorke recorded his part alone with a guitar, with other members and effects added later, as his emotional state made full band recording too challenging. "Es inspirar en Jeff Paky un concierto de Jeff Paky en Noffford, después de que la banda, sobre todo, Antonio Orcubi se entró en una especie de comatascamiento creativo para poder desarrollar bien la idea que tenía de la canción y grabar la fona pesadilla."
**Fake Plastic Trees (1995) - Secret US Remix** : When "Fake Plastic Trees" was submitted as a single in the United States, the US distributor deemed it insufficiently commercial, "grunge-like," or similar to "Creep" for the guitar-centric American market. Consequently, the distributor independently commissioned Bob Clearmountain to create an unofficial, "guitar-heavy" alternative remix. This version was distributed to US radio stations and media for airplay and was broadcast on several US radios and published in the press, all without the band's knowledge. Upon discovering this, particularly Thom Yorke, the band was horrified by the "guitar version" and immediately halted its distribution, never wishing to re-edit or re-release it. While many purported pirate versions exist, none have been definitively certified as the original Bob Clearmountain remix, though some cassette recordings from radio broadcasts are rumored to be held by individuals. "Entonces, la distribuidor de RID o jelen los Estados Unidos decidió hacer, por su cuenta, una remetcla o una versión alternativa remetclada en los Estados Unidos."
**Just (1995)** : This song originated as a "guitar-shredding competition" between Thom Yorke and Jonny Greenwood, challenging each other to create the most guitar distortion. Years later, Thom Yorke humorously admitted in an interview that Jonny Greenwood was the undisputed winner, acknowledging Greenwood as Radiohead's superior guitarist. "originalmente, hace como una competición de guitarreo entre Tom Jorke y John Gringwood, a ver quién era capaz de distorsionar más la guitarra."

## curiosities
**Thom Yorke's Depression during The Bends** : Thom Yorke experienced his first significant professional depression during the recording of *The Bends*. This profound emotional state significantly impacted the album's production, leading to numerous problems and influencing the more critical and depressive nature of its lyrics. This depression became a major topic of media discussion upon the album's release, with journalists interpreting the lyrics as direct reflections of his condition. "Y fue la depresión que tenía Tom Jorke. Las letras de Benz ya se empiezan a ser como más críticas y más depresivas, comparadas con las que teníamos en el Pablo Jonei y mucha gente de interpretaciones muy directas de la depresión que estaba teniendo Tom Jorke o a la que se le estaba tribuyendo."
**Journalistic Speculation on Thom Yorke's Depression** : In late 1995, *Melody Maker* published a highly sensational article titled "Radio Yorke," which directly compared Thom Yorke to Ian Curtis and Kurt Cobain. The article suggested that Yorke might be the "next martyr of rock," predicting his suicide was "imminent" due to his depression and that he would die at 25, reflecting intense media focus on his mental health during *The Bends* era. "Y yo creo que el cúlmen de todo esto llegaba en finales en 1995, cuando en el Melody Maker sacaban un artículo sobre Radio Jorke, en el que directamente decían que posiblemente Tom Jorke sería el próximo martir del rock comparándolo con Ian Curtis o con Panándolo con Curco Ben, diciendo que posiblemente el suicidio de Tom Jorke era inminente ante su depresión y que sería el siguiente martir de morir con 25 años, a causa de sus depresiones."

# artist - Mark Ronson

## albums
**Version (2008)** : This album featured Mark Ronson's highly popular remix of Radiohead's "Just." The version, which initially appeared on the tribute album *Exit Music: Music for Radio Heads*, was released on Ronson's own album in 2008, gaining widespread popularity and subsequently charting, accompanied by a music video that served as both an homage and a continuation of Radiohead's original "Just" video. "en el 2008, el sacaría un álbum en el que sacaría esta canción y se había la versión que hizo la remesa que hizo muy, muy popular."

## songs
**Just (2008) - Mark Ronson Version** : Twelve years after its original release, "Just" re-entered music charts thanks to a highly popular official remix by producer Mark Ronson. This alternative version first appeared on the tribute album *Exit Music: Music for Radio Heads* and was later featured on Ronson's 2008 album, gaining significant popularity. Its success led to the production of a music video that paid homage to and acted as a continuation of Radiohead's original "Just" music video. "volvió a las listas y a los charts, 12 años más tarde. Y es que hay una segunda versión oficial que hizo el productor Mac Ronson."

# label - EMI

## curiosities
**Interference during The Bends Production** : EMI caused significant issues during the recording of Radiohead's *The Bends*. Their demands led to numerous changes and even entire reinterpretations of several songs, contributing to the album's prolonged and difficult production process and multiple delays. "Emmy les dio bastante por saco, hubo bastantes cambios, incluso reinterpretaciones enteras, varias canciones durante el proceso de grabación."
**Pressure for US Remix of The Bends** : EMI exerted pressure on Radiohead for *The Bends* to undergo a second remix specifically for the United States market. The band eventually acceded to this demand, which resulted in the American remixers being credited as producers for the final version of the album that was released. "Es una versión que fue remetclada por segunda vez en los Estados Unidos, al parte de la margen de la banda por presión de Amy y al que la propia banda acaba accediendo."

# curiosity

**Rewriting of Historical Music Reviews** : It is a common practice for music publications to rewrite or erase older reviews of classic albums, especially those from the 90s, when they initially received less enthusiastic critical reception (e.g., scores below 9/10 or 10/10). This happens over time as albums gain "charisma" and are recognized as transcendent works, allowing publications to align their historical narrative with contemporary views, sometimes out of "embarrassment" for earlier opinions. "Con el paso del tiempo y con el carisma que ha ido ganando el tiempo posiblemente para el gran consumo de los álbumes más trascendentales de la época de los 90, muchas críticas fueron reechas y fueron borradas las anteriores después por vergüenza."
**Martyr of Rock Trope** : A sensationalistic journalistic trope in the mid-1990s involved comparing struggling artists to deceased rock icons. *Melody Maker*'s 1995 article, "Radio Yorke," exemplifies this by directly comparing Thom Yorke to Ian Curtis and Kurt Cobain, suggesting he would be the "next martyr of rock" and that his suicide was "imminent" due to his depression, speculating he would die at 25. "en el que directamente decían que posiblemente Tom Jorke sería el próximo martir del rock comparándolo con Ian Curtis o con Panándolo con Curco Ben, diciendo que posiblemente el suicidio de Tom Jorke era inminente ante su depresión y que sería el siguiente martir de morir con 25 años, a causa de sus depresiones."
**Music Production under Influence** : It is a recurring anecdote among many artists, particularly British ones, to record music while heavily intoxicated. Notable instances include Radiohead's "Planet Telex," where Thom Yorke was reportedly too drunk to stand while recording vocals, and Blur's "Boys & Girls," which was also recorded by the band while completely drunk during a trip to Ibiza. "Esto es una historia bastante común que muchos artistas, sobre todo británicos, la grabaron una noche volviendo de estar a verdaderamente borrachos toda la banda."
**Exit Music: Music for Radio Heads (2006)** : This album is described as a "half-tribute" to Radiohead, featuring an alternative version of "Just" by producer Mark Ronson. This particular version became very popular and contributed to the song re-entering the charts twelve years after its original release. "Tenemos un disco como medio tributo a Radio Jorke, que se llama Exit Music, Music for Radio Hets, en el que aparece una versión alternativa de Mac Ronson"
