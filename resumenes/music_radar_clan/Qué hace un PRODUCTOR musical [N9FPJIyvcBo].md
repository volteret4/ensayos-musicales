# artist - Rick Rubin

## curiosities
**Producer Role Definition** : Rick Rubin, recognized as one of the most important producers in history, defines the work of production in a straightforward manner: "the music producer converts the artist into a product." This perspective views music, beyond its artistic value, as a product intended for consumption and sale, aiming to reach and appeal to the public without being derogatory. "el productor musical convierte el artista en producto."
**Commercial Vision as a Producer** : Rick Rubin is highlighted for his exceptional commercial vision in production. He possesses a deep understanding of what the audience desires and "will devour," identifying preferences for specific elements such as choruses, tempos, rhythmic basslines, and ideal song durations, often suggesting that a song should not exceed five minutes. His expertise lies in transforming an artist's creative concepts into a finished product specifically adapted to public taste. "En Rick Craving es el hombre que sabe, por ejemplo, a la gente le gustan ese tipo de coros, a la gente le gustan ese tipo de tiempos, a la gente le gustan ese tipo de vaso-rismicas, a la gente le gusta que la canción no dura más de cinco minutos."

# artist - Brian Eno

## curiosities
**Creative Producer Role** : Brian Eno is presented as the quintessential example of a creative producer, actively involved in the artistic development of a record. He frequently acts almost as an additional band member, contributing to composition and even physically playing instruments during recording sessions. His unique role involves finding innovative solutions to an artist's creative challenges, such as achieving a specific violin sound or incorporating echo and delay modules to enhance a song's presence. "el ejemplo por excelencia es Brian Haneyne, que es una de las personas que por excelencia donde es productor trabaja activamente en el desarrollo creativo, ayuda incluso a componer, ayuda a tocar instrumentos, llega físicamente a tocar instrumentos en las grabaciones."
**Problem Solver for Artists** : As a producer, Brian Eno excels at providing creative and technical solutions for artists. For example, if an artist envisions a particular violin sound but lacks the ability to create it, Eno finds the means to achieve it. Similarly, if an artist desires specific echo or delay effects to give a song more volume or presence, Eno possesses the knowledge to implement these modules effectively. "Brian Eno sabe cómo hacerlo, entonces Brian Eno sería el trabajo de broción la persona que sabe hacer lo que tú no sabes hacer."

# curiosity

**Music Producer's Core Function** : A music producer's fundamental role is to transform an artist's creative ideas, which may be raw or vague, into a polished, finished product ready for public consumption. Artists often enter the studio with concepts that are not fully developed or lack the resources and skills to execute certain elements, such as specific instrumental parts or vocal harmonies. The producer acts as the bridge, converting this artistic vision into a commercially viable and complete recording. "El productor coge ese arte y la convierte en un producto acabado, impactado y listo para consumir."
**Dual Components of Production Work** : Music production generally involves two distinct but often intertwined components: a creative aspect and a technical aspect. A creative producer focuses on the artistic development of the record, sometimes actively participating in composition or playing instruments. A technical producer, conversely, prioritizes the logistical and engineering elements, ensuring correct recording processes, coordinating resources, managing budgets, and adhering to timelines. "generalmente hay dos tipos de productoros, dos tipos de trabajo de producción en un disco. Una es un componente creativo y el otro es un componente técnico."
**Producers Who Are Not Musicians** : Many successful music producers are not professional musicians and may not even play instruments, with a notable number being DJs. This phenomenon occurs because the producer's primary role is not to perform music but to adapt an artist's creative ideas for public appeal, manage the project, and ensure its commercial viability. This reality can be contentious among traditional instrumentalists, who sometimes perceive it as a "heresy" or "lack of respect." "muchos productores no son músicos o no son músicos profesionales o incluso son DJs, pero es que el trabajo que hace un productor no es el trabajo de músico, entonces el trabajo de un productor no es el de un músico y no tiene que saber tocar instrumentos."
**DJs as Effective Producers** : DJs frequently transition into successful music production due to their professional experience in observing real-time audience reactions to music. They gain an acute understanding of public preferences regarding tempos, sounds, transitions, and overall engagement. This unique insight into what resonates with listeners enables DJs to effectively adapt an artist's creative vision into a product that the public will enjoy, often without needing instrumental proficiency themselves. "En lo que los DJs viven profesionalmente de poner música y viven de ver cómo la gente reacciona a la música que ponen, lo que les gusta, lo que no les gusta, cómo les gusta, qué tipo de velocidad de reproducción les gustan, qué tipo les únicos, lo les gustan cómo los enganchas, cómo les ha subido, son profesionales de la reacción en tiempo real de cómo la gente reacciona a la música y eso es lo que ha hecho que muchos DJs han sido muy buenos productores y adiós y a día de hoy sean muy buenos productores."
**Technical Management by Producers** : A crucial aspect of a producer's job is technical management, which involves coordinating the recording process to ensure technical correctness, even if sound engineers handle the specific execution. This role includes finding and allocating resources, balancing the creative demands of a project with its economic realities. Producers manage budgets, set schedules for musicians (such as violinists or saxophonists) and engineers, supervise recording takes, and track costs and progress. "la otra parte muy importante que desarrolla un productor es un alabor muy técnica y tenemos ejemplos de productores que son muy técnicos y que ayudan en la voz técnica a los artistas, esto es desde convertir el proceso de grabación en un proceso técnicamente correcto aunque para ellos están los ingenieros en sonido, pero el productor es el que coordina todo ese trabajo y el otro lado es conseguir hacer lo que el artista no se va a hacer, encontrar los recursos y gestionar los recursos."
**Project Management by Producers** : Recording an album is a business endeavor requiring a budget and careful resource allocation, and the producer serves as the central figure managing this entire project. They are responsible for transforming the creative recording process into an economically viable one, overseeing costs, establishing timelines, and delegating technical tasks. This involves creating schedules for musicians and sound engineers, deciding which takes to repeat, and monitoring financial expenditure, akin to a project manager overseeing the construction of a house. "el productor es la persona que gestiona todo eso, es la persona que convierte el proceso creativo de grabación en un proceso económico que tiene un coste que tiene un presupuesto y que tiene que seguir si tiene que guiarse."
**Multiple Producers on an Album** : It is common for albums to involve more than one producer, sometimes two or three, due to the diverse skills required for a successful recording. This can include a producer specialized in financial and project management, a creative producer who assists with composition and artistic development (like Brian Eno), and a producer focused on ensuring the creative idea translates into a commercially successful product appealing to consumers (like Rick Rubin). "hay muchos discos que tienen más de un productor o más de dos, tres productores porque a veces tienes un productor de gestión económicamente el proyecto, tienes un productor creativo que ayuda a la banda en el trabajo de composición como puede ser gente como Brian Hano y tienes productores que consiguen convertir una idea creativa en un producto comercialmente de éxito, que se adapta a lo que los clientes van a querer comprar."
**Artist Limitations and Producer Necessity** : Most artists are not "one-man orchestras" and lack the comprehensive skills needed to transform a raw demo or initial idea into a fully realized, finished product. Artists often have a clear vision for elements like backing vocals or specific instrumental solos (e.g., violin or saxophone) but may not possess the ability to perform or secure these components themselves. This is precisely where the producer becomes indispensable, sourcing necessary musicians, providing technical expertise, and managing the overall project to bring the artist's vision to fruition. "muy pocos artistas son capaces de desarrollar una maqueta que se aplatea en un producto acabado porque no saben hacer de todo y ahí es donde trabaja el productor."
**Sound Engineer's Role Mentioned** : Sound engineers are identified as professionals primarily responsible for ensuring the technical correctness of the recording process. While the producer coordinates the overall technical workflow, engineers execute the specific technical aspects of capturing sound. "aunque para ellos están los ingenieros en sonido, pero el productor es el que coordina todo ese trabajo."
