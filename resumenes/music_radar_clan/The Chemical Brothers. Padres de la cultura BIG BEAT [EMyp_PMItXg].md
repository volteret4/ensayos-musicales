## albums
**Exit Planet Dust (1995) - Debut Album** : The Chemical Brothers released their first album, *Exit Planet Dust*, in 1995. By this time, they had already become a recognized entity within British rock music, having befriended many prominent figures and being played in numerous non-electronic bars. Despite initial rejection from both the hardcore rave and rock communities, the album was a resounding success, quickly establishing their sound. Its release served as the official launchpad for the Big Beat culture, inspiring many other UK DJs, including Fatboy Slim, Groove Armada, and The Crystal Method, to transition into producing their own studio albums. "El éxito forrotundo, curiosamente al que al principio tenían el rechazo un poco de esos dos mundos, con ese disco muy rápido se asentaron."

## curiosities
**Kings of Big Beat** : The Chemical Brothers are considered the absolute kings of the Big Beat electronic movement in the United Kingdom, recognized for initiating a significant reinterpretation of electronic music in the 1990s. "Los chemical brothers para ubicarlos son los reyes absolutos de un movimiento electrónico que se conoce en el reino unido como el Big Pit."
**Manchester Roots and Influences** : The Chemical Brothers established their musical foundation in Manchester, spending considerable time there. They frequently credited The Hacienda club as a pivotal location where they learned to define their sound, drawing from the blend of House music (influenced by groups in Manchester like "a Guy Callier" who made electronic music) and the sounds of rock and indie bands such as The Stone Roses and especially New Order. "siempre nombraron a la cienda como uno de los puntos clave en los que los chemicals verdes aprendieron a definir su sonido, gracias a esa mezcla del hausque venía o de grupos que estaban en Manchester como a Guy Callier al que hacían el electrónica y a la vez a gente como los son russes o como sobre todo New Order."
**Hip Hop Sampling Expertise** : A member of The Chemical Brothers was deeply knowledgeable and enthusiastic about American hip hop culture, bringing extensive expertise in "pure sampling" to the band. This contributed to their unique approach to sampling, which differed from the classical Big Beat sampling found in American hip hop. "uno de los miembros de los chemicals verdes muy asentados y muy aficionados al BigPid y a la cultura hip hop americana que traía muchos conocimientos del sampling puro."
**Three Defining Innovations of Big Beat** : The Chemical Brothers introduced three fundamental changes that radically transformed the aggressive rave culture of the time, thereby defining the Big Beat movement that emerged in the mid-90s. First, they significantly lowered the playback tempos, relaxing the "overdriven" speed characteristic of European rave music. Second, they adopted a more classic and simplified sampling process, drawing from American hip hop culture, which made their electronic music less dense and more relaxed. Third, they incorporated sampling and synthesizer techniques that were less synthetic, using sounds derived from real musical instruments such as electric guitars and normal drums, which made electronic music more palatable and introduced elements of rock and indie music. "incorporan como tres puntos que cambian radicalmente esa cultura rey tan agresiva y además estos tres puntos yo creo que son los que vendrían a definir la cultura del BigPid que luego se asentaría a mitad de los 90."
**Initial Rejection from Both Scenes** : Despite their innovative sound, The Chemical Brothers initially faced rejection from both the rave culture, which favored very heavy and hardcore electronic music, and the rock/indie scene in the UK, which was combatively dismissive of electronic and disco-oriented music. This unique position between two opposing musical worlds led to their curious initial rejection from both sides. "generaron curiosamente un rechazo por las dos partes."
**Early Success in Rock/Indie Venues** : Between 1994 and early 1995, The Chemical Brothers began releasing their first singles, which, against expectations, started to gain traction and establish a presence in rock and indie clubs and bars rather than traditional electronic venues. "empiezan a fiancarse curiosamente en las discotecas y bares que no son de electrónica, que son de música rock y de música indie."
**Rapid Integration into British Rock Culture** : Early in their career, The Chemical Brothers quickly forged significant friendships and collaborations within the British rock, indie, and Britpop scenes. They became friends with Noel Gallagher, members of The Charlatans, Richard Ashcroft, Paul Weller, Primal Scream, and Oasis, rapidly immersing themselves in this cultural sphere and beginning to collaborate with these artists. "los chemical brothers ya muy pronto y muy jóvenes empiezan a tener amistades muy interesantes, muy jóvenes los chemical brothers hicieron amigos ya de Noel Galaguer, de los charlatans, hicieron amigos de Richard Ascrow, de Paul Weller de los primal Escrime de Oasis."
**Pioneering Electronic Headliners at Rock Festivals** : The Chemical Brothers achieved an unprecedented feat by becoming headlining acts or significant parts of the headlining lineups at classic rock festivals such as Glasgow Green, Benicàssim International Festival, and Leeds Festival. This marked a historic moment, leading to the novel sight of rock fans (those who listened to Oasis, Blur, or Pulp) dancing to The Chemical Brothers, which was unimaginable in the early 1990s. "por primera vez veías como cabeza de cartel o una parte importante de los cabezas de cartel a un grupo electrónico comerán los chemical brothers."
**Extensive Collaborations with British Rock Icons** : The Chemical Brothers are distinguished as "the kings of collaboration" within British rock music. Their albums feature collaborations with almost every prominent figure in 90s British rock, including Richard Ashcroft, Noel Gallagher, "Bezorton," and Primal Scream, demonstrating their broad appeal and the widespread desire among artists to work with them. "los chemical brothers en sus iscos tienen colaboraciones con Richard Alskroff con Noel Galagher con Bezorton con PrimalScream colaboraron con prácticamente todo el mundo que era alguien dentro del reino nido y todo el mundo quería trabajar con ellos."
**Foundation for New European Electronic Styles** : The Chemical Brothers provided a crucial foundation for new forms of indie electronic and rock electronic music across Europe. Their work was instrumental in establishing the electro sound that emerged in Belgium, influencing artists like Soulwax and 2manydjs. It was also a vital base for defining the modern French House sound, particularly for the entire discography released by Ed Banger Records. Additionally, they were a pillar for the entire Big Beat culture within the UK. "fue una base muy importante para establecer el sonido el electro que se fundaría en Bélgica del que saldrían por ejemplo Soulwars to Menid DJs. Fue una base muy importante para definir el nuevo French House moderno que se fundaría por ejemplo en Ed Banger Records toda la discografía que salede Ed Banger Records tiene una base muy importante en los chemical brothers y luego dentro del reino nido en la fe toda la cultura del Big Beat."
**Limited International Impact Beyond Europe** : While highly influential within the UK and, to a lesser extent, in Belgium, France, Germany, Sweden, and the Nordic countries, The Chemical Brothers' specific electronic sound achieved very little reach outside of Europe. "Salió fuera de Europa, muy poco prácticamente nada, muy muy poco, salió fuera del reino nido, sí pero también muy poco."

# genre - Big Beat

## curiosities
**Definition and Origin** : Big Beat emerged in the UK as an electronic movement aimed at softening the excessively extreme and aggressive rave culture prevalent in Europe during the late 1980s and early 1990s. It incorporated influences from Detroit Techno and Chicago House, blending them with existing European electronic music, American hip hop sampling, and instrumental sounds to create a more relaxed and accessible sound. "el movimiento del BigPid que viene a suavizar mucho esa cultura rey tan, tan extrema."
**Three Defining Characteristics** : The Big Beat movement, largely defined by The Chemical Brothers, incorporated three key characteristics: significantly slower tempos compared to the overdriven European rave music; a more classic, simpler sampling process derived from American hip hop; and the inclusion of samples and synthesizers that emulated real musical instruments like guitars and drums, making the music more palatable to rock and indie audiences. "estos tres puntos yo creo que son los que vendrían a definir la cultura del BigPid que luego se asentaría a mitad de los 90."
**Market Reception and Saturation** : Initially, Big Beat faced rejection from both the hardcore rave scene (for being too slow) and the rock scene (for being too electronic). However, it quickly gained widespread popularity following The Chemical Brothers' debut, becoming the official launchpad for the genre. This immense success led to saturation, with many mediocre groups emerging and producing "bad" albums and concerts, although pioneers like The Chemical Brothers and Fatboy Slim maintained high quality. "Fue un encito tan tremendo que una saturación de grupos como solo pasaran estos casos y una parte muy grande a la cultura Big Beat pues no era muy buena."

# genre - Electronic

## curiosities
**90s UK Context** : Electronic music in the UK during the 1990s underwent a significant reinterpretation, largely spearheaded by The Chemical Brothers. This period was marked by the fusion of incoming Detroit Techno and Chicago House influences with existing local Manchester music at venues like The Hacienda, blending electronic and rock sounds. "una pizza clave dentro de la electrónica de los 90 en el reino unido y fueron el inicio de una reinterpretación electrónica muy interesante."
**Extreme European Ramification** : In Europe, the influx of American House and Techno music, combined with existing European electronic traditions and a tendency for extremization, led to the rapid proliferation of dozens of highly extreme and aggressive electronic styles. This included genres like Acid House, Trance, and Hardcore Techno, which created a profound disconnect between the "beast-like" European rave culture and the more tranquil electronic music found elsewhere. "aquí en Europa comenzó una ramificación en decenas, pero decenas y decenas de estilos electrónicos muy extremos y muy agresivos, reinterpretando todo eso."

# genre - House

## curiosities
**Arrival in Europe and Manchester Fusion** : House music from Chicago, particularly its "músicas House" styles, reached Europe, primarily the UK, with Manchester becoming one of the earliest and most significant hubs. It quickly began to mix with local rock and indie music, especially within clubs like The Hacienda. "llegan las primeras influencias del techno de Detroit y del House de Chicago, sobre todo los músicas House. Y llega a Europa y llega principalmente uno de los primeros sitios a los que llega es el reino unido completamente a Manchester."
**European Extremization** : In Europe, House music, alongside Techno, evolved into a highly extreme and aggressive form compared to its American origins. This radicalization contributed to the emergence of genres such as Acid House, Trance, and Hardcore Techno, diverging sharply from the "classic, more tasteful House" heard in the United States. "se generaba en Europa una versión muy extremada de lo que era la música House y la música technoamericana."

# genre - Rave

## curiosities
**Extreme 90s European Culture** : European rave culture in the late 1980s and early 1990s was characterized by its extreme and aggressive nature, stemming from the radicalization of American House and Techno. This culture was so intense that it "swept away an entire generation" due to pervasive drug use, a situation that the Big Beat movement later sought to soften. "esa cultura electrónica rey de los 90 rasos con una generación entera, o sea si llevó una generación completa por culpa de las drogas."
**Initial Rejection of Slower Tempos** : Within the 90s rave culture, there was a strong preference for very heavy and hardcore electronic music, which initially led to the rejection of slower, more hip hop-influenced sounds like Big Beat. "dentro de la cultura rey blajente estaba muy por la electrónica muy heavy, muy hardcore."
