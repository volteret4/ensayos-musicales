# artist - Dazpán

## genres

## albums
**homburmo (1997) - Electronic Revolution and Cult Album** : This was Dazpán's first album, released in 1997, and it is credited with revolutionizing modern music, especially electronic music. It became a cult hit among electronic music specialists and critics, praised for its quality and innovation, but it did not achieve significant mainstream consumption beyond a few notable instances. It marked Dazpán's first attempt at an electronic revolution. "revolucionaron la música moderna."
**bril (unspecified year) - Unprecedented Mass Success** : Released a few years after "homburmo," this album achieved unprecedented mass success worldwide. It attracted a very commercial audience, described as "unfaithful," and was stylistically quite distinct from their previous work. The commercial success of "bril" contrasted with the critical acclaim of "homburmo," placing the band in a "no man's land" between commercial and critical appeal. "un éxito de masas impresedentes."
**ciumanácter Olco (2005) - Commercial and Critical Failure** : Dazpán's third album, released in 2005, was a significant setback for the band, described as a "tremendous blow." It failed to win over the commercial audience that had been attracted by "bril," and critics no longer found it as interesting as "homburmo." The album was poorly received by both the public and critics, leaving the band in a precarious position, and notably, no tour followed its release. "se dieron una hostia tremenda salieron el 2005 y los pobres les fue bastante mal."
**rano Macs Memories (unspecified year) - Commercial Success with Mixed Critical Reception** : Dazpán achieved tremendous success with this album, though it was described as a substantial success that wasn't particularly well-received by critics. "han tenido un éxito tremendo con el rano Macs Memories un éxito bastante gruense porque bueno hay que no que no puesto un muy bien un disco de crítica."

## curiosities
**Artistic Dilemma - Caught Between Cult and Commercial Success** : Following the release of their first two albums, "homburmo" and "bril," Dazpán found themselves in a dangerous "no man's land." "homburmo" was a cult classic for electronic music specialists and critics, representing an electronic revolution, while "bril" was a massive commercial success that garnered a broad but "unfaithful" audience. This stylistic divergence created a challenging situation where they struggled to reconcile their commercial appeal with their critically acclaimed, revolutionary electronic roots. "se quedaron ahí un poco en un terreno de nadie entre lo que era la música comercial del disco bril y la música un poco más da la crítica la calidad y sobre todo a esa revolucion electrónica que hicieron con el homburc."
**Unusual Tour Timing (2007)** : Two years after the commercial and critical failure of their 2005 album "ciumanácter Olco," Dazpán embarked on a tour in 2007. This timing was considered very unusual, as there had been no tour immediately following the album's release, indicating a unique and curious backstory behind this particular tour. "en el 2007 dieron una hyal 2 años más tarde que salir al disco y eso es muy raro pero tiene una historia curiosa."
**Elusive Nature of Dazpán** : Dazpán is known for being a very "elusive" group. They rarely perform concerts, give press conferences, or grant interviews, making their appearances highly sought after and giving them significant caché, particularly in the United States despite being European. "Dazpán que es un grupo muy escurribizo no da conciertos, no da con frecidad de prensa no da entrevistas, muy pocas."
**Coachella's Persistent Offers (2006)** : Coachella, recognized as one of the most important music festivals in the world, actively pursued Dazpán for a performance in 2006. Despite Dazpán's initial rejections, Coachella persisted, progressively increasing their financial offers. This culminated in an unprecedented offer of two million dollars for a single concert. "coaxila que es el festival de música más importante del mundo que están los Estados Unidos querían a Dazpán... los llamanos para la toma en 2006 a lo que Dazpán rechazaron a lo rechazaron varias veces a cada polcón sumas de dinero más importantes y llegó un momento en que llegaron a ofrecerles dos millones de dólares por un solo concierto."
**Unprecedented Concert Fee** : The offer of two million dollars for a single Dazpán concert at Coachella was considered an incredible and unprecedented sum. This amount was highlighted as money that even major artists like Madonna or Bruce Springsteen typically do not earn for a single performance. "dos millones de dólares es un solo concierto."
**Coachella as a Comeback Platform** : Dazpán, facing a career low after the failure of "ciumanácter Olco," constructively used the extraordinary two-million-dollar offer from Coachella. They decided to accept the performance opportunity as a strategic move for the band's resurgence and a chance to make a powerful comeback. "decidieron usar este concierto como el resurgimiento de la banda."
**Reinvestment of Coachella Fee into Production** : After receiving the two million dollars (or euros, as also mentioned) from Coachella in advance, Dazpán chose not to keep the money. Instead, they fully invested the entire sum into creating a massive and elaborate stage production for their 2007 tour. This substantial financial commitment underscored their ambition for a "third revolution" in their career. "esos dos millones de euros se lo gastaron en la masiva a puesta en este naque hicieron con la gira del Dazpán que en el 2007."
**Extreme Secrecy Surrounding 2007 Tour** : Dazpán and their team enforced extreme secrecy around the elaborate stage production for their 2007 tour. Significant funds were allocated to ensure no information leaked, as this tour represented a high-stakes "third revolution" attempt after a previous album failure. To maintain surprise, a large tarp was used to cover the entire stage before each concert, preventing the audience from seeing anything until the show began. "todo el mundo lejos a los finados lo trataron como increíble secretismo el vecindario era mostroso típicamente era complejísimo y tuvieron que gastar un montón de dinero a asegurarse de que nadie daba ningún tipo de información de lo que se estaba cociendo dentro."
**Tour as a "Third Revolution"** : Despite "ciumanácter Olco" failing two years prior, Dazpán positioned their 2007 tour not as an album promotion but as a profound "third revolution." This tour aimed for a complete re-sensitization and rebirth for the band, marking a significant new chapter for Dazpán's artistic trajectory. "evidentemente la gira no se la tomaron como una gira sobre el ímbano de la tercera revolución... y sólo tomaron como una gira del task pantiesu más plena de sensidad."
**Critical Acclaim and Audience Reaction (2007 Tour)** : The 2007 tour, which commenced at Coachella, profoundly impacted audiences and critics alike, leaving everyone "amazed" and "speechless." Musically, it was described as a "tremendous reality," marked by continuous play and seamless blending of their entire repertoire, and critics were "truly amazed" by the reinterpretation of all their songs. The elaborate stage design, a two-million-dollar investment, left a profound impression on everyone. "todo el mundo quedó alucinado la gente se quedó sin palabras fue increíble musicalmente fue de una realidad tremenda porque tocaron a una manera continua entre mezclando todas las canciones de su repertorio."
**Logistics Challenge of the 2007 Stage Production** : The sophisticated two-million-dollar stage setup for Dazpán's 2007 tour presented a formidable logistical challenge: how to transport such an elaborate production between venues and across continents. The band and their engineers devoted a full year not only to perfecting the continuous flow of songs but, more critically, to engineering the stage itself to be modular, easily dismountable, and transportable, even across oceans. Their success in this endeavor resulted in one of music history's most memorable concert experiences. "tuvieron un enorme problema después y era como transportarla de un lado a otro para seguir las cinas... no solo tardaron un año en montar las canciones sino lo más complicado es como hacer toda esa escena para que fuese el desmontable y transportable hasta el otro lado del charco."
**Legendary Status of 2007 Tour** : Dazpán's 2007 tour is widely remembered as one of the best concerts in music history and is often cited as arguably the best concert of the past two decades. Critics lauded it extensively, with some fans becoming so enamored that they followed the tour, attending the same concert three or four times because they found the experience "incredible." "uno de los mejores concertos de la historia de la música y prácticamente mejor concerto los últimos 20 años."
**Rumors of a 2017 Tour** : Around 2016 and 2017, speculation arose regarding a potential Dazpán tour in 2017, marking a decade since their legendary 2007 tour, suggesting a recurring pattern. Consistent with their elusive nature, Dazpán provided no official information, yet the absence of communication only fueled further public anticipation and rumors. "acerca el 2017 empieza a ver rumores sobre si habrá una agida a otros 10 años más tarde como ha pasado anteriormente."
**Past Statements of Retirement/Career Change** : Following the release of their album "ciumana trola" (presumably "ciumanácter Olco"), Dazpán had made numerous statements indicating a shift in their career focus. They reportedly decided to transition into working on films and video productions, suggesting a period away from music or even a complete departure. However, these declarations ultimately proved to be unfounded, as the band eventually returned to music. "habían decidido pasarse a trabajar en películas se trabajan en videoperacciones habían decidido tomarse en tiempo y prácticamente dejarla música finalmente no fue cierto."
**Dazpán's Double Revolution** : Dazpán is celebrated for initiating a "first revolution" by transforming the history of electronic music and general music. After experiencing a significant career setback, they achieved a "second revolution" in 2007 by innovating and revolutionizing concert stage productions, effectively making a powerful comeback after having been "literally on the floor." "después de cambiar la historia de la música electrónica revolucionaron la música en general y después volvieron a hacer una segunda revolución revolucionando la apuesta en escena en concertos después de haber estado literalmente por los suelos tras un enorme tracaso."

# concert - Coachella

## curiosities
**Coachella's Persistent Offers to Dazpán (2006)** : Coachella, considered the world's most important music festival, aggressively pursued the elusive European band Dazpán for a performance in 2006. Despite initial rejections, the festival increased its financial offers multiple times, eventually providing an unprecedented two million dollars for a single concert, a sum usually not earned by top-tier artists like Madonna or Bruce Springsteen. "coaxila que es el festival de música más importante del mundo que están los Estados Unidos querían a Dazpán... llegó un momento en que llegaron a ofrecerles dos millones de dólares por un solo concierto."
**Starting Point of Dazpán's Legendary 2007 Tour** : The Coachella performance in 2007 served as the pivotal kickoff for Dazpán's acclaimed "Gira de la laida 2007." This concert marked the beginning of a tour that astonished audiences and critics alike with its continuous musical flow, reinterpreted songs, and groundbreaking stage production. "así es como arranco en el venano del 2007 a Cualchela y la gira de la laida 2007."
