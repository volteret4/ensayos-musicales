## curiosities
**Absence from Spotify** : King Crimson's entire discography is currently unavailable on Spotify. Despite years of discussions between Robert Fripp (representing King Crimson) and Spotify, no agreement has been reached yet, although ongoing negotiations suggest a potential future resolution, possibly contingent on financial terms. "King Crimson no está en Spotify."
**Extensive Membership Changes** : Throughout its history, King Crimson has experienced a high turnover of musicians, with between 23 and 25 members having passed through the band. Robert Fripp remains the sole constant member, a factor that significantly complicates the ongoing management of the band's musical rights, as Fripp must share control with various past contributors. "ha pasado entre 23 y 25 miembros."
**Rights Management Disputes** : The band's history is marked by extensive legal battles over music rights, primarily led by Robert Fripp. These disputes have resulted in "enormous enmities" among former members and against record labels, particularly concerning early material initially signed with Island Records, shaping the legal landscape of the music industry. "enormes enemistas de centreyos a lo largo de toda su carrera por este tema de derechos."
**Consent for Re-releases Denied** : Robert Fripp initiated legal action against Universal Music Corporate for unauthorized re-releases. The label had begun publishing compilations, re-editions, and even previously unreleased material found in Island Records' archives without King Crimson's explicit consent for republication, remastering, or the release of new content. "King Crimson no había dado el consentimiento para la repubblicación y la remasterización de todo el material antiguo de King Crimson y tampoco había dado el consentimiento para publicar material inédito de King Crimson."

# artist - Robert Fripp

## member of
King Crimson

## labels

## curiosities
**Pioneering Legal Battles for Rights** : Robert Fripp's complex and lengthy legal battles against the music industry, particularly concerning King Crimson's catalog, have been instrumental in establishing new jurisprudential bases for understanding and managing musical rights. His personal struggle has significantly influenced how rights management is perceived today. "los procesos judiciales de King Crimson o de Robert Flip en concreto. En contra de la industria musical, han sentado muchas bases de cómo entendemos la gestión de derechos."
**Founding Discipline Global Mobile (Late 1980s)** : In the late 1980s, Robert Fripp founded his own record label, Discipline Global Mobile (DGM), after realizing that Island Records held more rights to his material than he did. This decision was a direct response to perceived fraud and a common issue faced by many artists of that era. "decide fundar su propia discográfica Discipline Mobile no Discipline Global Mobile, sí era esa."
**Decades-Long Fight for Rights Recovery (1990s)** : Robert Fripp engaged in a "huge process of legal disputes" throughout almost the entire 1990s. He fought distributors, Island Records, and even former King Crimson members to reclaim the rights to King Crimson's material, a "complex and very expensive process of lawyers" from which he eventually recovered everything. "durante casi toda la década de los 90 es un complejísimo y carísimo proceso de abogados para recuperar su material y lo consigue recuperar todo."
**Co-Ownership and Shared Rights Management** : Despite successfully recovering a significant portion of King Crimson's material rights, Robert Fripp is not the sole owner. He is legally obligated to share the management of these rights with other former members of King Crimson and Island Records, even for material he did not personally create. "está obligado a compartir esta gestión de derechos con otros miembros que han pasado por la carrera de King Crimson y por Islan Records."
**Legal Dispute with Universal Music Corporate (2000s)** : Robert Fripp initiated another "huge process of lawsuits" against Universal Music Corporate after they acquired Island Records. Universal began publishing King Crimson compilations, re-editions, and unreleased material found in Island Records' archives without Fripp's consent, despite Fripp having believed his legal processes were concluded. "empieza a ver un enorme problema cuando ya Robert Flip creía que había acabado todos sus procesos legales porque Universal Music Corporate empieza a publicar una serie de recopilatorios de red ediciones e incluso de cierto material inédito que había quedado en las suficinas de Islan Records sin el consentimiento de Robert Flip."
**Legal Action Against Grooveshark** : Robert Fripp launched a major legal process against Grooveshark, a streaming service described as a "pre-Spotify." This dispute became highly controversial, with Fripp's private emails threatening Grooveshark executives being publicly published. Fripp was recognized as a key figure in the platform's eventual closure. "Robert Flip contra Groofshark."
**Anger Over Kanye West Sampling (2012-2013)** : Robert Fripp expressed immense anger ("very, very angry") after discovering in 2012, through the press and a YouTube video, that Kanye West had sampled the first track from King Crimson's first album in his 2010 song "Power" without prior permission. His fury was heightened by Universal Music (owner of both Island Records and Kanye West's label) effectively granting itself permission without notifying him. "Robert Flip no lo avisara le cabrió muchísimo se cabrió muchísimo pero muchísimo tenemos entrevistas del 2013 y 2012 cabreadísimo."
**Disagreement with Greg Lake on Sampling** : Robert Fripp's frustration regarding the Kanye West sampling was compounded by Greg Lake's positive reaction. Lake, whom Fripp considered the "true composer" and "true genius" of that particular part and album, found the sampling a "fantastic idea" and felt "truly flattered" that a significant artist for younger generations like Kanye West would incorporate his composed material. "lo que más le cabrió fue que Greg Lake... le parece una idea fantástica."
**Continued Legal Battles with Universal Music (Post-2012)** : Following the Kanye West sampling incident, Robert Fripp initiated "another very expensive and very long process" of lawsuits against Universal Music, which commenced in 2012 and continued for years. "empezó otro carísimo y larguísimo proceso desde 2012 con Universal Music."
**Exhaustion and Temporary Retirement Announcement (2015)** : In a 2015 interview with the Financial Times, Robert Fripp publicly stated his intention to leave music, citing fatigue from constantly defending his rights instead of dedicating his time to composing or playing. However, this declaration proved to be temporary, as he remained active and had a tour planned for 2018. "en el Finance Al Time a Robert Flip que dice que él deja la música porque está fatigado y que por desgracia en vez de dedicar esa componería a tocar es la lo único que se dedica a perseguir la defensa de los derechos de su material."
**Significant Legal Victories and Industry Precedents** : Robert Fripp achieved "enormous victories" in his protracted legal battles, successfully recovering rights he had previously lost. His efforts established "important precedents for the industry," significantly smoothing the path for many artists from the 1960s and 1970s, such as AC/DC, who had also been divested of their rights by record labels. "ha conseguido enormes victorias porque no solo recuperó todos los derechos que no tenía de King Crimson pero también esentaba precedentes importantes para la industria se facilitó mucho el camino para muchos artistas que durante los años 60 y 70 habían perdido sus derechos a manos de discográficas como el caso de Isidisi o de muchas otras."
**Current Negotiations with Spotify** : Robert Fripp (representing King Crimson) has been engaged in discussions with Spotify for several years concerning the inclusion of the band's discography on the platform. Despite ongoing negotiations, an agreement has not yet been reached, though the dialogue suggests a potential future resolution, possibly on financial terms. "Robert Flip está en conversaciones con Spotify y Spotify al menos ha admitido que está en conversaciones con King Crimson desde hace años para intentar tener su discográfica pero no están llegando a un acuerdo."
**Complex Rights Management After Greg Lake's Death** : The complexities of managing King Crimson's rights intensified significantly after Greg Lake's death from cancer a few years prior. Lake was regarded as the "true genius" behind the band's first album, the rights for which Robert Fripp had largely managed almost single-handedly. "esta situación se hizo bastante complicado tras la muerte hace unos años de Greg Lake por un cáncer cuando él había sido el verdadero genio que estaba detrás del primer disco de King Crimson."

**Protagonist of King Crimson's First Album** : Greg Lake was considered the "true protagonist" of King Crimson's debut album, serving as both its singer and bassist. He openly expressed feeling "truly flattered" that an artist as influential to young generations as Kanye West would utilize material he composed, and he was surprised by its enduring relevance nearly 50 years later. "Greg Lake que decía que se sentía verdaderamente alagado que alguien tan importante para la gente joven como Kanye West hubiese versionado a King Crimson a un material que le había compuesto."
**Legacy as "True Genius" of First Album** : Greg Lake was highly regarded as the "true genius" behind King Crimson's seminal first album. His death from cancer a few years prior further complicated Robert Fripp's already intricate efforts to manage the band's extensive catalog of rights. "él había sido el verdadero genio que estaba detrás del primer disco de King Crimson."

## songs
**Power (2010)** : Released in 2010, this critically acclaimed song controversially featured a sample from the first track of King Crimson's debut album. Robert Fripp discovered the unauthorized use in 2012 via news reports and YouTube, as no prior permission had been secured. Although Universal Music, which owned both Island Records (co-owner of the King Crimson material) and West's label, eventually sought permission a week to ten days after the song's publication, Fripp's anger stemmed from the lack of notification and prior consent. "sacaba un tema que se llama Power que además tenía unas enormes críticas y usaba un sampling del primer tema de la primer disco de King Crimson."

## curiosities
**Unauthorized Sampling Incident (2010)** : Kanye West's 2010 song "Power" included an unapproved sample from King Crimson's first album. Robert Fripp was not informed prior to its release and learned about the sampling through public media in 2012. Universal Music, which owned West's label and held rights to the King Crimson material, retrospectively requested permission, but this failed to appease Fripp, leading to prolonged legal action. "sacaba un tema que se llama Power... usaba un sampling del primer tema de la primer disco de King Crimson y Robert Flip ni siquiera lo sabía."

# label - Universal Music Corporate

## curiosities
**Acquisition of Island Records** : Universal Music Corporate became the final owner of Island Records, thereby consolidating control over a significant portion of King Crimson's early musical catalog. This acquisition proved pivotal in subsequent legal conflicts with Robert Fripp concerning re-releases and previously unreleased material. "Islan Records... acaba en el monstruo de Universal Music Corporate."
**Unauthorized King Crimson Releases** : After acquiring Island Records, Universal Music Corporate began publishing compilations, re-editions, and certain unreleased King Crimson material without obtaining explicit consent from Robert Fripp. This action prompted a major legal dispute initiated by Fripp, who believed his previous legal battles were concluded. "Universal Music Corporate empieza a publicar una serie de recopilatorios de red ediciones e incluso de cierto material inédito que había quedado en las suficinas de Islan Records sin el consentimiento de Robert Flip."
**Co-Ownership and Lack of Consent Stance** : Universal Music, as the owner of Island Records and a co-proprietor of King Crimson's rights, argued that it was not legally obligated to seek Robert Fripp's permission for re-releases or the publication of unreleased material. This legal stance fueled years of complex and industry-followed lawsuits. "como Universal Music era dueña de Islan Records y era co-propietaria de estos derechos legalmente no está obligada a pedirle permiso y podía hacerlo que le diese la gana con todo ese material."
**Role in Grooveshark's Closure** : Universal Music Corporate, alongside Warner Music, played an active role in a concerted legal and commercial offensive against Grooveshark, spurred by Robert Fripp's complaints. This mass attack was a critical factor in the eventual closure of the streaming platform. "Universal Music guarda el music atacan masivamente a Groofshark y acaban consiguiendo el cierre de la plataforma."
**Kanye West Sampling Dispute Involvement** : Universal Music, by owning both Island Records (a co-owner of the sampled King Crimson material) and Kanye West's record label, was uniquely positioned during the "Power" sampling controversy. They effectively "reached an agreement with themselves" by approving the sample internally but failed to inform Robert Fripp, leading to significant anger and further legal action. "Universal Music era dueña de Islan Records que era propietaria o co-propietaria de ese material, también Universal Music es propietaria de la discográfica de Kanye West entonces se puso el acuerdo consigo misma no tuvo ningún problema pero Robert Flip no lo avisara le cabrió muchísimo."

# curiosity

**Evolution of Music Rights Management** : The extensive legal struggles led by Robert Fripp and King Crimson have profoundly influenced the understanding and practices of music rights management within the industry. These cases have established crucial precedents, significantly easing the path for many artists from the 1960s and 1970s, such as AC/DC, who had previously lost their rights to record labels. "han sentado muchas bases de cómo entendemos la gestión de derechos."
**Grooveshark as a Pre-Spotify Service** : Grooveshark is described as a "pre-Spotify" streaming platform that once hosted a comprehensive music library, including King Crimson's discography. It became a central point for legal disputes concerning peer-to-peer sharing and direct streaming rights, particularly involving artists' intellectual property. "Groofshark era un pre-espotify que tuvimos."
**Impact of Robert Fripp's Legal Actions on Grooveshark** : Robert Fripp's legal battles against Grooveshark were highly contentious, notably including the public release of his private emails threatening the platform's executives. His determined efforts, combined with pressure from Universal Music and Warner Music, were instrumental in Grooveshark's eventual closure. "el fue el protagonista casi absoluto del cierre de Groofshark."
