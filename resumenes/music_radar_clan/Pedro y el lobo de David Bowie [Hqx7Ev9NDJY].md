# artist - David Bowie

## genres
Classical Music

## labels
RCA

## albums
**Peter and the Wolf (1969) - A Prokofiev Narration** : This album, a narration of Sergei Prokofiev's classical work for children, was recorded by David Bowie in 1976 during a turbulent period of his life marked by extreme drug addiction. The speaker claims the work itself was published in 1969 by RCA, who then decided to re-edit and release it with Bowie's narration. Despite featuring Bowie's name, the album did not sell well upon its release, being described as an "unbearable pain" by listeners. It was later re-released in the 1990s but again achieved low sales. This recording is considered a rare curiosity, often absent from Bowie's official or authorized biographies and discographies, existing outside of his conventional musical output. "el disco salió Pedro y el lomo narrado por David Bogud y con la fila de la fila de la fila de la fila no lo compró a mi perri porque después de la mente y sufrible tanto a la fila de la fila de la fila no porque es un coñazo insoportable y no por nadie."

## curiosities
**Turbulent Year of 1976** : The year 1976 was an extremely turbulent period in David Bowie's life, characterized by severe drug addiction that brought him close to death. His intense drug problem escalated significantly after he purchased a multi-million-dollar house in Geneva and moved there in the summer of 1976. "1976 fue el año un poco más turbio de la vida de David Bowie y la cuando estaba extremadamente drogadito y estaba rato y antes muriéndose."
**Mystical and Esthetic Ravings Phase** : During his time in Geneva in 1976, David Bowie entered a profound mystical and esthetic phase, which led him to undertake numerous eccentric and costly endeavors. This included studying classical music, aspiring to be a professional ballet dancer, engaging in prolific abstract painting, and taking drug-induced photographs. "tuvo un enlombra arrabato místico en el cuándo así no para hacer escentricidades."
**Classical Music Studies** : During his esthetic phase in Geneva in 1976, Bowie decided to study classical music, hiring very expensive private teachers to guide him. This costly pursuit was part of a broader period of artistic experimentation and lavish spending, reflecting his erratic behavior at the time. "el decidió estudiar música clásica y se gastó un dinerad de profesores calísimos de música clásica."
**Professional Ballet Aspirations** : While living in Geneva in 1976, Bowie aspired to become a professional ballet dancer. He brought a professional British dancer friend to Geneva, paying him substantial amounts for private dance lessons as he pursued this expensive endeavor. "bailar como venerin profesional y teníamos amigo que era el enlombra profesional británico a la se gastaba un montón de dinero y traerse lo de vio al cinebra y le pagaba un lote en tido dinerad por clase profesionales de danza."
**Prolific Abstract Painting** : During his 1976 esthetic phase, David Bowie became a prolific painter, producing thousands of abstract and "esthetic" pictures, often completing them in as little as five minutes. He painted continuously, creating a vast body of work, though the quality and complexity of these quickly produced pieces were not noted as exceptional. "pintaba de mayor composición miles de cuadros pero miles continuamente cuadros un poco abstractos y esténicos que le pintaban cinco minutos pero no paraba."
**Drug-Induced Photography for Inspiration** : In his drug-addled state in 1976, Bowie took up photography, despite not being particularly skilled at it. He would photograph anything he encountered on the street while completely intoxicated, using these images as raw inspiration for the strange paintings he was creating. "hacia fotografías totalmente drogado todo lo que se encontraba por la calle para lo intentarlo solo de inspiración para esos cuadros extraños que pintaba."
**Recording of Peter and the Wolf Narration (1976)** : In 1976, at the height of his mystical and esthetic ravings, Bowie conceived the idea to record Sergei Prokofiev's classical work *Peter and the Wolf*. He traveled to Philadelphia, where he hired the Philadelphia Symphony Orchestra, described as one of the most expensive orchestras in the world, to record the score while he narrated the children's story in a studio. This project took place during one of the most personally distressing periods of his life. "pensó que es una día de puta madre cogerse a la sinfónica de filadelfia que es una de las sinfónicas más caras del mundo y que grabas en la obra clásica de procofía Pedro y el Loco mientras leí al libro el cuento para niños el Pedro y el Loco."
**Rehabilitation and Recording Low (1976)** : After his tumultuous period in Geneva, David Bowie began rehabilitation in July 1976, marking a shift away from his extreme eccentricities. Following this period, he commenced recording the album *Low*, signifying a new artistic direction. "en enero del 76 es cuando se ha cinebra y en julio a bosto es cuando le digo un yacojizo le llavar el yacoja empieza a rehabilitar si se olvidara de todo esto empieza a grabar la famosa tecnología de la clín."
**Unrecorded Eccentricities (1974-1976)** : The period between 1974 and 1976 saw David Bowie engage in "hundreds" of eccentric and costly endeavors, frequently spending millions on what were considered "truly stupid things." Much of this information remains undocumented in his official biographies because Bowie himself, due to his mental state at the time, could not recall many of these activities or purchases. "quedaron un montón por no decir cientos de estentricidades de David Bogud y esa etapa del 74 a 76 en el cual se gastaba ténticas millonadas en cosas verdaderamente estúpidas."
**Forgotten Purchases and Creations** : During his drug-influenced eccentric phase from 1974 to 1976, Bowie made numerous purchases, such as fashionable paintings, which he later rediscovered in his home without any memory of acquiring them. He also wrote poetry that was subsequently published but that he couldn't recall creating, and painted pictures which he would deny having done, despite being their actual creator. "Él tiene un montón de cuadros de moda que compró en esa época que no recuerdan ni tenerlos siglos descubren su casa él escribió por lo poesía que ni siquiera recuerda y que aparecía publicada él es el tinto cuadros y a veces cuando se han juraba que no sabía hecho pero lo sabía hecho."
**Indifference to Peter and the Wolf Release** : When approached about the release of his *Peter and the Wolf* narration, David Bowie, who was by then in a post-rehabilitation state and embarking on a more "normal" creative phase (like the *Low* era), displayed complete indifference. He reportedly "didn't care in the slightest" about the album's fate. "a David Bogud que ya estaba por dentro de la interabilitario que en pleno ojo de la tecnología de Lorenzo había sido un personaje normal a la llave de guantos de Dios no lo importó lo más mínimo."
**Historical Value of Peter and the Wolf** : Despite its lack of musical merit as a conventional David Bowie release and its poor commercial performance, the *Peter and the Wolf* narration holds significant historical value. It serves as a unique artifact, offering insight into Bowie's character and experiences during a critical, drug-fueled period of his life. "como elemento histórico de la vida de David Bogud y para entender un poco mejor que fue el personaje de David Bogud y está muy bien."
**Unofficial YouTube Availability of Peter and the Wolf** : The *Peter and the Wolf* narration is not available on popular streaming services such as Spotify. However, a dedicated fan has reportedly ripped the album from vinyl, separated it into individual tracks, and uploaded it to YouTube, making this rare historical curiosity accessible to a wider audience. "evidentemente esto no está en el Spotify pero lo que sí he encontrado es un tío muy flipado que se ha cogido el mi pelo se lo ha grabado se lo ha pasado a pistas y lo ha subido a youtube."

# label - RCA

## curiosities
**Involvement with Peter and the Wolf** : RCA is mentioned as the label that "published" *Peter and the Wolf* in 1969. They reportedly decided to re-edit and release the classical work with David Bowie's 1976 narration. It is suggested that RCA and the Philadelphia Symphony Orchestra were motivated by the commercial potential of Bowie's name, as the orchestra's own records struggled with sales, making Bowie's involvement a valuable promotional asset. "se realmente se publicó en 1969 cuando la RCA creó la escorancia de Bogud y pero que también era la filanmonica de la fila a él lo tenía ahí terminado y dijo y pues mira volvamos a editarlo cuando habló con David Bogud."

# curiosity

**Philadelphia Symphony Orchestra Collaboration** : The Philadelphia Symphony Orchestra, described as one of the most expensive in the world, collaborated with David Bowie in 1976 on his narration of Sergei Prokofiev's *Peter and the Wolf*. The orchestra was reportedly enthusiastic about the project, as their own records did not sell well, and Bowie's involvement offered a significant promotional advantage due to his fame. "la sinfónica de filadelfia que es una de las sinfónicas más caras del mundo y que grabas en la obra clásica de procofía Pedro y el Loco."
**Sergei Prokofiev's Peter and the Wolf** : *Peter and the Wolf* is a renowned classical work for children composed by Sergei Prokofiev. David Bowie recorded a narration of this piece in 1976 with the Philadelphia Symphony Orchestra. This undertaking was conceived during a highly eccentric and drug-addled period of Bowie's life, adding a unique, albeit unusual, chapter to the history of the beloved composition. "la obra clásica de procofía Pedro y el Loco."
