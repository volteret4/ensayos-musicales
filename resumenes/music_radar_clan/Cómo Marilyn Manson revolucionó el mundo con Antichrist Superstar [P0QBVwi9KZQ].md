## albums
**Portrait of an American Family (1994)** : This was one of Marilyn Manson's first two controversial and well-received albums prior to "Antichrist Superstar." The band was already critical of American society and consumerism in the 90s, laying the groundwork for future themes. "Mey Manson ya había tenido como dos trabajos anteriores que habían sido como muy polémicos y que habían tenido bastante buena aceptación, teníamos el portreito americano femily."
**Smells Like Children (1995)** : This was one of Marilyn Manson's first two controversial and well-received albums prior to "Antichrist Superstar," known for its critical stance against American society and consumerism in the 90s. This album contributed to Manson's growing reputation outside mainstream circles. "teníamos el It's Mersley Children."
**Antichrist Superstar (1996) - Philosophical Rock Opera** : Released in 1996, this album represents a major artistic leap for Marilyn Manson, with a complex and philosophical argument. It was produced by Trent Reznor of Nine Inch Nails and co-produced by Sean Beavan, involving many collaborators from Nine Inch Nails. The album's themes and inspiration were developed in 1995, followed by a six-month recording process in early 1996. "el anticristo superestar va mucho más allá."
**Antichrist Superstar (1996) - Extreme Production** : The production of "Antichrist Superstar" was an "absolute lack of control," pushing all participants to physical extremes. Marilyn Manson and Trent Reznor worked to their physical and psychological limits for a year, with Manson especially pushed during idea development. Extreme methods included manipulating and destroying recording tapes, and playing partially destroyed instruments to achieve specific sonic deterioration. "fue una absoluta falta de control en la que todas las personas que formaron parte llegaron a los extremos físicos de no poder soportarlo para llegar a trabajar el disco."
**Antichrist Superstar (1996) - Musical Shift** : Upon its release, "Antichrist Superstar" divided some listeners as Marilyn Manson delved deeper into industrial rock sounds, reminiscent of Nine Inch Nails, moving away from the intense hard rock or dark metal of their earlier works, resulting in a sound much more aligned with Trent Reznor's style. "Mey Manson se adentra más en sonidos, pues a lo mejor del rock industrial de Nine Inch Nays, dejando a lo mejor ese rock o ese dar metal tan intenso que tenían las primeras partes aquí por si lo entes ya como más industriales como un sonido como mucho más tren Retnorm."
**Antichrist Superstar (1996) - Commercial and Social Impact** : "Antichrist Superstar" became argumentatively and rock-wise very important, and socially, it almost changed the history of the 90s. The album smashed sales records, received numerous awards, and gained immense worldwide recognition, making the phenomenon of Marilyn Manson unstoppable. "argumentalmente importantísimo, es un disco desde el punto de vista del rock importantísimo pero socialmente fue un disco que casi cambió la historia de los 90."
**Antichrist Superstar (1996) - Censored Visuals** : The music videos associated with "Antichrist Superstar" faced significant censorship attempts, with an important portion being cut. Some of this censored audiovisual material only began to surface years later, around 2005-2007, often released by the video clip creators rather than Marilyn Manson himself. "hubo una parte importante que acabó siendo censurada y que era bastante señores más tarde, sobre todo, ya avanzados en el 2005, 2006, 2007, es como que han ido saliendo, poco a poco no ya por Mey Manson sino por el trabajador del video clip y todo."
**Antichrist Superstar (1996) - Complex Musicality and Legacy** : Musically, "Antichrist Superstar" is a complex piece, incorporating classic rock, goth metal, industrial rock, progressive music, progressive metal, and elements of new wave and synthesizers, making it one of the most complex albums in rock history. It remains a key album for understanding the evolution of rock over the last 30-40 years, despite many people not delving into it due to bad press. "Musicalmente este álbum es la leche, es complejísimo, de los álbumes más complejos que había en la historia del rock."
**Antichrist Superstar (1996) - Mainstream Breakthrough** : This album propelled Marilyn Manson directly into the mainstream, transforming him into an unstoppable force. It garnered numerous awards not only for the album itself but also for its controversial music videos, solidifying his commercial and critical success. "Meli Manzon da el salto directo al mainstream y se convierte en una máquina imparable, recibió muchísimos premios por este disco, incluso por el videoquilip de Anticristos, sobre esta recibió muchísimos premios."
**Antichrist Superstar (1996) - Conceptual Trilogy Part** : "Antichrist Superstar" is explicitly stated to be part of a conceptual trilogy that continued with Marilyn Manson's subsequent two albums, creating a connected narrative across his work. "esparte de una tecnología que continuaría con los dos iscos siguientes."
**Antichrist Superstar (1996) - Aggression and Impact** : The album stands out for its exceptionally high level of aggression, its deeply macabre themes, and the profound impact it had on both the music industry and society at large. "Destinde mucho el nivel de agresividad y sobre todo el nivel de como de macabro es y de impactación."
**Mechanical Animals (1998) - Thematic and Visual Evolution** : The album "Mechanical Animals" maintained a high level of provocation but explored different aspects, featuring strong visual provocation that was less grotesque than its predecessor, "Antichrist Superstar." Its themes delved into more androgenous, spatial, and alien concepts. "El Mechanical Animals tenía muchísima provocación, pero ya en otros aspectos, tenía también mucha provocación visual, pero que no era tan grotesca."
**Mechanical Animals (1998) - Societal Critique** : "Mechanical Animals," along with several later Marilyn Manson albums, included numerous attacks on American society, capitalist systems, consumerism, law, and politics. However, these critiques did not generate the same level of controversy as his earlier direct attacks on religion. "en el mecánico en Álmea, si en varios discos posibles de Marilim Manzon tenemos muchos ataques a la sociedad americana, a la sociedad capitalista, a la sociedad de consumo, a la ley o incluso a la política y a nadie le importó."

## curiosities
**Societal Critique in Early Work** : Marilyn Manson's early albums like "Portrait of an American Family" and "Smells Like Children" were highly critical of American society, consumerism, and 90s culture, a theme that would be intensified in "Antichrist Superstar." "Mey Manson ya era muy crítico con la sociedad americana con el ambiente consumista, con la sociedad actual de los 90."
**Extreme Artistic Approach** : Marilyn Manson pushed artistic boundaries to extreme limits, even compromising his own health, during the creation of "Antichrist Superstar." "lleva el trabajo artístico a unos límites que yo creo que casi no habíamos visto."
**Sleep Deprivation for Art** : During the creation of "Antichrist Superstar," Marilyn Manson intentionally subjected himself to prolonged sleep deprivation, breaking his sleep continuously to induce fatigue and an altered mental state for artistic purposes. "Mey Manson no dormía durante día, su interrompía su sueño de manera continuada para estar fatigado y para tener su mente alterada."
**Self-Inflicted Physical Pain for Art** : To achieve an extreme state and deepen his artistic intensity for "Antichrist Superstar," Marilyn Manson tortured himself, inflicting significant physical pain upon himself. "Mey Manson llegó a torturarse a sí mismo y a infringirse en enorme dolor físico a sí mismo para llegar a profundizar y a sentir dolor."
**Extreme Self-Harm and Drug Use** : During the lyrical composition of "Antichrist Superstar," Marilyn Manson engaged in severe self-harm, including piercing himself with needles and hitting himself, all while in a mentally deteriorated state exacerbated by extensive drug use. "como hacerse daño con agujas, me refiero clavarse cosas, martiliarse, llegó a hacerse verdadero dolor físico en un estado mental verdaderamente deteriorado por el Fue Toda Norgia de Droga, tremenda."
**Verco Weace's Departure** : Verco Weace, a classic member of Marilyn Manson from its early period, abandoned the group during the "Antichrist Superstar" recording process after his father's guitar was broken, leading to his frustrated departure due to the perceived out-of-control and life-threatening studio environment. "Verco Weace, uno de los miembros clásicos de la primera parte de Mey Manson, abandonada el grupo precisamente porque le habían roto una guitarra clavía, roto su padre y acabó hasta dos cojones y se fue despotricando el todo."
**Public Perception as Repugnant** : Marilyn Manson was labeled as perhaps the most repugnant group ever promoted by a major record label, reflecting the extreme public and media reaction to their image and music. "Este es tal vez el grupo más repulnante que haya promocionado una discográfica de las consideradas importantes."
**Christian Protests and Political Opposition** : The release of "Antichrist Superstar" led to magnified controversy, including Christian demonstrations outside Marilyn Manson's concerts and issues with members of the U.S. Senate. "empezaron todas esas manifestaciones cristianas a la puerta de los conciertos de Mey Manson e incluso llóganse a tener problemas con algunos miembros del Senado de los Estados Unidos."
**Social Problem in the United States** : Marilyn Manson became a significant social problem in the United States due to the widespread controversy surrounding "Antichrist Superstar" and its themes. "Mey Manson se había convertido en un problema social para los Estados Unidos."
**Accusations of Promoting Violence and Columbine Behavior** : Marilyn Manson was accused by critics of promoting hate, violence, suicide, death, drug use, and behavior akin to the Columbine massacre, with opponents vowing to fight against his presence. "Si Maril y Manson puede venir a nuestra ciudad y promover el odio, la violencia, el suicidio, la muerte, el consumo de drogas y un comportamiento como el de Columbine, yo puedo decir, no lo conseguirás sin antes pelear."
**Global Breakthrough with Antichrist Superstar** : A significant portion of the world outside the United States discovered Marilyn Manson through "Antichrist Superstar," establishing him as an unstoppable phenomenon. "una parte muy importante del mundo fuera de Estados Unidos, conocí a Mel y Manzo en la parte de Anticristos Superestar y ya el fenomeno era imparable."
**Evolution of Grotesque Spectacle** : The "Antichrist Superstar" tour marked a pivotal point where Marilyn Manson further developed and deepened his commitment to grotesque performance spectacle, building on previous inclinations. "adentro más por un lado a la polémica y por el otro lado a Mel y Manzo a profundizar más sobre el espectáculo grotesco."
**Birth of the "Grotesque Spectacle"** : While Marilyn Manson always had a grotesque aspect to his shows, the "Antichrist Superstar" era is identified as the moment the "grotesque spectacle" truly emerged in capital letters for the band. "es aquí cuando nace el espectáculo grotesco en mayúsculas por parte de Mel y Manzo."
**Breaking Barriers, Influenced by Ozzy Osbourne** : Marilyn Manson broke many barriers during this period, in a way not seen since Ozzy Osbourne, being heavily inspired by Osbourne's absolute and sometimes gratuitous provocation. "rompe muchísimas barreras que yo creo que nadie sabía trevida sobre pasar desde Osi Osborn."
**Misinformation and Exaggeration** : During the "Antichrist Superstar" era, a growing amount of rumors, false information, and exaggerations about Marilyn Manson circulated, amplified by the limited internet connectivity of the 90s. "la cantidad de bullos y de información falsa y de sobreactuaciones que se hizo sobre lo que era Mel y Manzo no paraba de crecer."
**Threats and Entry Bans** : Marilyn Manson faced direct prohibitions from entering venues, fueled by protesting groups who even issued threats, contributing to the cancellation of his concerts. "directamente se le prohibió la entrada y porque había grupos manifestándose, incluso amenazando."
**Death Threats from Conservative and Radical Christian Groups** : Marilyn Manson received death threats from conservative elements and especially from radical Christian movements during the controversial period of "Antichrist Superstar." "Mel y Manzo, un llegó a ser amenazado de muerte para algunos miembros, sus reconcervadores y sobre todo por movimientos cristianos radicales."
**Fans Sue Government Over Concert Bans** : Marilyn Manson's fans took legal action, suing the government for prohibiting his concerts, a testament to their dedication and the legal battles surrounding the band. "fans de Mel y Manzo que llegaron a demás que llegaron a demandar al gobierno por haber prohibido el concierto de Mel y Manzo."
**Ozzy Osbourne's Support for Marilyn Manson** : Ozzy Osbourne personally supported Marilyn Manson by suing the state for prohibiting some of his concerts, a testament to the solidarity among controversial artists. "incluso osivos por mi persona. Llego a demandar al Estado por haber prohibido algunos de los conciertos de Mel y Manzo."
**Mainstream Backlash from Early Fans** : "Antichrist Superstar" represented a significant shift for the band into the mainstream, leading some classic, closed-minded metal fans to hate Marilyn Manson simply for gaining wider visibility, despite the continued quality of the music. "hubo como una parte de esos fanáticos clásicos del metal cerrado, que no les gusta que parte de su música salta al mainstream, con lo cual pasearon a odiarlo solo porque salía el antivillo, creo, porque Meli Manzon siguió manteniendo nivel de calidad muy bueno."
**End of Close Relationship with Trent Reznor** : The "Antichrist Superstar" era marked, in part, the end of the very close working relationship between Marilyn Manson and Trent Reznor, though their mutual influence continued. "fue el final de esa relación tan cerrada que había entre Meli Manzon y el Rennor."
**Shift to Self-Production** : Following "Antichrist Superstar," Marilyn Manson largely transitioned to self-producing his own career, though Sean Beavan (a key collaborator from Nine Inch Nails' ambitious projects) continued to work on "Mechanical Animals." This marked a shift from the close production relationship with Trent Reznor. "a partir de ahí, Meli Manzon casi pasa casi prácticamente a producir esa sin mismo."
**Columbine Massacre Association** : For years, Marilyn Manson was the target of severe criticism and attacks from American society, with conservative movements attempting to link him to the Columbine massacre, especially through claims that the perpetrators listened to his music. "hubo movimientos conservadores que intentaron ligar a Meli Manzon con la macabra matanza de Columbine, porque los chavales que habían hecho la matanza de Columbine en el documental bowling en el recorrer de Meli Manzon."
**Public Scapegoat for Societal Problems** : Marilyn Manson became a public scapegoat, with experts and the public frequently blaming "that rocker, Marilyn Manson" for societal ills and the perceived monstrousness of youth. "Sí, sé roquero, Marilim Manzon."
**Concert Cancellations Following Tragedy** : Marilyn Manson cancelled the last five concerts of his national tour out of respect for the victims of the Littleton tragedy (implied Columbine), demonstrating a sensitivity amid controversy. "Han cancelado los últimos cinco conciertos de su gira nacional por respeto a los caídos en Lita el Ton."
**Denial of Blame for Violence** : Marilyn Manson explicitly stated that artists like him should not be blamed for violent acts such as the Columbine massacre, refuting the accusations made against him. "el cantante afirma que no es artista como él, a quienes hay que culpar."
**Critique of Social Stereotypes, not Religion Itself** : Marilyn Manson's critique was aimed at social stereotypes, including those associated with Christian and Catholic religions, rather than an obsession against religion in general. "Marilim Manzon no tenía una obsesión en contra de la redición cristiana, nicatólica, ni la redición general, sino en contra de muchos esterotipos sociales entre ellos esa."
**Shift in Focus Calms Public Reaction** : When Marilyn Manson shifted his direct attacks away from religion, even while continuing to critique American society, consumerism, and the American way of life, public outrage significantly calmed down. "como dejó de hacerlo directamente contra la religión en la gente se calmó muchísimo."
**Irony of Public Reaction to Thematic Shift** : Ironically, despite the underlying message and critique remaining consistent, public concern about Marilyn Manson diminished significantly once he stopped explicitly naming religion in his criticisms. "como ya no ponía en nombre de religión ya nadie le importaba."
