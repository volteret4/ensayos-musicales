## albums
**Nightclubbing (Post-1979) - Post-Disco Reinvention** : This album is regarded as possibly the most passionate and significant work of Grace Jones's career. Emerging after the anti-disco riots, it marked a profound reinvention of her sound, shifting from her disco roots to an entirely new electronic style. This new direction was heavily influenced by African rhythms and Jamaican sounds, signifying the beginning of what would become the greatest stage of her artistic journey. "tenemos posiblemente la hora más apasionante que tenemos a carrera de grisos que es el Nightclubbing cuando ella todavía conocida como el gran icono de la música disco y de la música disco gay que era se reinventa con una nueva manera totalmente electrónica influenciada por ritmos africanos influenciada por sonidos a jamaycanos y nación a nueva grisos que sería la etapa más grande de su carrera."

## curiosities
**Fashion Icon Status** : Grace Jones was an absolute icon in the fashion industry, working as a model before her musical career. Her background as a model and her status as a fashion icon were crucial to understanding the trajectory of her musical career, illuminating why she became such an important and groundbreaking figure in music. "entendiendo un poco esa parte de la carrera de gresios en la que ella fue modelo y un icono absoluto de la industria de la moda nos ayuda a entender mucho cómo fue acabar a la carrera musical y sobre todo porque fue tan importante y tan reveladora al final."
**Early Modeling Career in US (Late 1960s/Early 1970s)** : Born in Jamaica, Grace Jones moved to the United States at a young age and began modeling while still a student in the late 1960s and early 1970s. However, her distinctive physical appearance did not find much success in the then-conservative American fashion scene. "no tiene mucho éxito en Estados Unidos estamos hablando de finales el los 60 primer año de los 70 donde pues en Estados Unidos aún había una versión bastante conservadora de la moda entonces digamos que el físico de gresios no tuvo mucho éxito."
**Parisian Fashion Icon (1970-1976)** : After being discovered by a European modeling representative, Grace Jones moved to Paris, where her unique physique was embraced by the city's radical and artistic fashion scene. She quickly became an icon, modeling for major haute couture houses of the 1970s, including Yves Saint Laurent, Kenzo, and Balmain. "se la lleva a parís y en prácticamente nada gresios se convierte en todo un icono de la ciudad."
**Androgynous Tuxedo Look (1976)** : In 1976, Grace Jones made history on the runway by appearing in a man's tuxedo for Yves Saint Laurent's collection. While initially regarded by some fashion critics as one of the most "unpleasant" images of the Paris runway, this look rapidly became an iconic moment in fashion history within two to three years. "ella pasaría la historia de la moda cuando en mil novecientos sesventa y seis en la colección de Issa Nuran salía a vestir a con esmoquim de obra de esfilar."
**End of Parisian Modeling Career (Age 28)** : Grace Jones concluded her modeling career in Paris at the age of 28, a relatively advanced age for models in an industry that favored youth and radicalism. Following this, she returned to the United States, specifically New York. "al final pues ya con veinti ocho años ya muy mayor para ser modelo pues termina su etapa como modelo en parís."
**New York Socialite & Studio 54 Figure (Late 1970s)** : Upon her return to New York, Grace Jones quickly established herself within the city's high society, leveraging contacts with photographers from her Paris years. She befriended prominent figures like Andy Warhol and became a regular at Studio 54, known for her provocative and radical personality, which had been shaped by the continuous artistic and fashion provocations prevalent in 1970s Europe. "se hace amiga de gente como Andy Warhol empieza a preguntar el estudio 54 y ya es una persona relativamente conocida en la alta sociedad neoyorkina muy pronto."
**Island Records Contract (Late 1976)** : Grace Jones signed her first record deal with Island Records in late 1976. This contract was initially a speculative venture, as she was a model transitioning into music, seeking to remain active in the entertainment industry. "fue muy poco después de llegar a finales de 1976 cuando ya firmó ese contrato a mislan recos fue realmente un contrato un poco a ver qué pasaba porque ella realmente era una modelo que venía a reconvertirse en algo un poco pues para seguir en activo."
**Impact on New York & Visual Iconography** : Her arrival in New York led to radical success, as she brought important influences from early 1970s Europe, including her uninhibited and provocative personality and significant artistic perspectives. She understood her potential as a visual icon through her physique and unique stage attire, even if not primarily known for her singing. "Nueva York se echa sus pies porque se trajo influencias muy importantes en la Europa de primeros de los 70."
**Richard Bernstein Collaboration** : From the outset of her musical career, Grace Jones collaborated with Richard Bernstein. He was responsible for developing the complete iconography for her album covers and personal styling, crucial elements of her visual identity. "y ya desde un principio ya cuenta con el trabajo de Richard Einstein para trabajar toda la iconografía de sus portadas y su vestimenta y de todo."
**Reinterpretation of Disco Music (Late 1970s)** : As the initial, Afro-American-influenced phase of disco music began to appear exhausted in the late 1970s, Grace Jones emerged as a pivotal figure in its reinterpretation. She introduced a new approach to disco with more electronic and "discotec" elements, incorporating European disco influences that had not deeply penetrated the American scene. "Gray Jones fue uno de los personajes que ayudaron a la reinterpretación de la música disco porque cuando ya esa etapa tan afroamericana de la música disco estaba votada y parecía que ya no daba más de sí reaparece una nueva manera de ver la música disco con otros tintes más electrónicos y más discotequeros."
**"New Gloria Gaynor" of Electronic Disco** : During a period of crisis for the disco movement, Grace Jones quickly established herself as the "new Gloria Gaynor." She championed a more electronic and, at times, "gay" version of disco, which rapidly gained popularity in the late 1970s and early 1980s, coinciding with the rise of groups like Village People. "ella prácticamente en cuanto sale se alza como la nueva Gloria Gaynor de ese nuevo disco más electrónico un tanto a veces gay que se hizo tan popular ya final de los 70 y tirando a los primeros años de los 80."
**European Disco Influence** : Grace Jones drew extensively from the distinct European version of disco, which differed significantly from the pure, Gloria Gaynor-influenced Afro-American disco that struggled to penetrate Europe. European disco incorporated influences from Germany and a less pronounced focus on black music, and she brought this unique perspective to her music. "Gray Jones no se traía solo esa esa versión tan provocadora tan artística y tan visual de su etapa en en parís sino que se traía muchas influencias de la versión europea que tuvimos a la música disco."
**Icon of New York Gay & Disco Culture** : Grace Jones became an absolute icon within both the New York gay culture and the city's vibrant disco scene, where her provocative style and reinterpretation of the genre resonated strongly. "ella se volvió un absoluto icono dentro de la cultura gaynor y orquina y dentro de la cultura disco te quedó a orquina."
**"Queen of Gay Discos" and Anti-Disco Backlash (1979)** : At the peak of her success in 1979, coinciding with the release of a work described as "Queen of Gay Discos," Grace Jones's popularity was negatively impacted by the anti-disco riots. During this period, conservative groups publicly burned disco albums, leading to widespread censorship in many conservative parts of the United States. "cuando saca el mío y de Queen of Gay Discos pues lamentablemente bien el problema porque justo en 1979 se generan los estúrbios antidiscos."
**Reinvention After Anti-Disco Riots (1979)** : While the anti-disco riots of 1979 signaled the end for many disco artists, for Grace Jones, it was merely another step in her career and a crucial moment for reinterpretation, ultimately propelling her to even greater heights. She is characterized as a figure of constant reinvention, relentless provocation, and always pushing boundaries. "para grillos no solo fue un paso más en su carrera sino que fue el momento de una reinterpretación que la gría incluso volver todavía más alto porque ella es un personaje de reinvención constante de provocación constante y de siempre ir más allá."
**Overall Cultural Impact** : Grace Jones is celebrated as not just a musical icon, but also an artistic and expression icon, playing an integral role in the history of fashion over the past 50 years. Her pre-music career as one of the most significant models, especially for 1970s haute couture in Paris, and her iconic 1976 Yves Saint Laurent tuxedo runway appearance, were fundamental to her swift and profound impact on disco music. "Realmente ella no es solo un icono musical sino que esto es un icono artístico y un icono de expresión y es parte de la historia de la moda."

# genre - Disco

## curiosities
**Evolution of Disco (1970s)** : Disco music, which originated in the early 1970s, was initially heavily influenced by soul and Afro-American music. However, by the late 1970s, it began evolving, seeking new directions and incorporating more electronic and "discotec" elements. "la música disco que venía ya de la primera parte de los 70 era todavía muy influenciada del sól y de la música afroamericana y estaba hallándose a otroslares y estaba un poco ya perdiendo."
**European Disco Characteristics** : Unlike the pure, Afro-American-influenced disco popularized by artists like Gloria Gaynor, European disco did not penetrate the continent in the same way. Europe developed a distinct version of disco, featuring artists such as ABBA and Cerrone, with different influences, including a strong German component and less reliance on black music. "la música disco pura tal como la conocemos tan influenciada por Gloria Gaynor la música afroamericana no llegó a penetrar en Europa y tenían en Europa una versión un tanto distinta de lo que era la música disco."
**Anti-Disco Riots (1979)** : In 1979, the anti-disco riots erupted in the United States, marking a significant downturn for the disco movement. Conservative groups publicly burned disco albums, leading to widespread censorship in many conservative regions. These events ultimately signaled the beginning of the end for disco and curtailed the careers of many prominent artists in the genre. "justo en 1979 se generan los estúrbios antidiscos cuando grupos conservadores empiezan a quemar públicamente álbumes de música disco y empieza a ver a una censura enorme en muchas partes son tanto conservadoras de Estados Unidos."
