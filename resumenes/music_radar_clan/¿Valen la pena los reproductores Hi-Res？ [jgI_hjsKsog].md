# artist - Creative

## curiosities
**Nomad Jukebox - First Hard Drive MP3 Player (Late 2000s)** : The Creative Nomad Jukebox was the first portable MP3 player to feature a hard drive, preceding Apple's iPod. It offered 6 gigabytes of storage capacity and used a hard drive rather than flash memory for storage. This device served as the inspiration for Apple's subsequent iPod design, though their underlying philosophies differed. Creative integrated its top-tier Sound Blaster sound card, essentially creating a mini-computer dedicated solely to manipulating and listening to music. It boasted a very powerful sound processing chip and professional audio outputs, including two independent RCA channels. However, it was physically large, heavy, suffered from poor battery life (requiring 6 rechargeable AA batteries), and was very expensive at the time. "Creativ no man jukebox que tenía seis gigas de disco duro y fue el primer reproductor de alta capacidad que usaba disco duro y no usaba memoria flash."
**Sound Blaster Card Integration** : Creative, known for manufacturing the acclaimed Sound Blaster sound cards for computers, incorporated its best PC sound card model into the Nomad Jukebox. This design choice gave the Nomad Jukebox a significant advantage in audio processing quality compared to later devices that prioritized portability over sound fidelity. "Creativ cogió su mejor tarjeta de sonido prácticamente para ordenador es un mejor modelo de son blaster le metió un disco duro externo batería salidas de audio y hizo esto esta máquina realmente era una especie de mini ordenador sólo pensado para manipular y escuchar música."

# artist - Apple

## curiosities
**iPod - Design Philosophy Shift** : The Apple iPod, while bringing fantastic innovations, introduced design flaws and conceptual problems that continue to affect portable devices like smartphones today. Although inspired by the Creative Nomad Jukebox, the iPod was not the first MP3 player with a hard drive. Apple's version prioritized making the device smaller, lighter, with better battery life, and more user-friendly. This came at the cost of audio quality, as its sound processing chip was significantly less powerful than that of the Nomad Jukebox. Apple's focus on portability and affordability over pristine audio fidelity became the dominant philosophy, shaping how sound is processed in most portable gadgets today, including modern iPhones, where even expensive models often feature disappointing audio processing chips. "el iPod de Apple no fue el primer reproductor MP3 con disco duró no fue realmente hubo un reproductor MP3 con disco duró anterior al iPod que fue este el creativ no man jukebox que tenía seis gigas de disco duro y fue el primer reproductor de alta capacidad que usaba disco duro y no usaba memoria flash esta máquina fue en la que se inspiró a Apple para hacer el iPod."
**Compromised Audio Processing in iPhones** : Even high-end iPhones, costing upwards of 1000 euros, are noted for having a "disappointing" sound processing chip. This reflects Apple's historical design philosophy for its portable devices, prioritizing features like screen, processor, and battery over dedicated audio quality. This approach, inherited from the early iPods, has set a standard in the portable electronics market where sound processing is not a primary concern for manufacturers. "aunque tú te cojas un un iPhone de mil euros y tú te mira es el chip de procesado de sonido que tiene es cuanto menos decepcionante."

This power enables it to drive high-demand headphones effectively, which often struggle to perform well when connected to typical smartphones due to insufficient power. # curiosity

**Relevance of Portable Audio Players in 2019** : In 2019, dedicated portable audio players (DAPs), often referred to as hi-res players, have become more relevant than they were 15 years prior. This resurgence is driven by a fundamental problem with digital audio quality that stems not from the files themselves (even lossless FLAC or WAV files can sound disappointing), but from the way they are processed and manipulated by common devices like smartphones and laptops. "posiblemente hoy en pleno 2019 utilizar este cacharrot tengan más sentido que hace 15 años."
**Digital Audio Quality Bottleneck** : The primary challenge to achieving high-quality digital audio playback today lies in the device's audio processing capabilities rather than the audio file's quality. Even with high-fidelity headphones and expensive amplifiers, listening to high-resolution files (e.g., FLAC) on a smartphone or computer often results in a disappointing experience because the internal audio chip is inadequate. "el mayor problema de calidad hoy en día no es tanto el archivo de audiencia sino como reproducimos y manipulamos a esos archivos de audio."
**Smartphone Design Prioritization** : Modern smartphones prioritize features like screen quality, battery life, processor power, and graphics over audio processing. This is evident as manufacturers rarely detail the specifications of their audio processing chips when marketing devices, indicating that audio quality is not a selling point for the majority of consumers. "cuando te vas a comprar un teléfono móvil te ponen mil indicaciones sobre la calidad de la pantalla sobre la batería sobre el procesador sobre la tarjeta gráfica sobre los colores no te dicen nada de su procesado de sonido."
**Laptop Audio Limitations for Professionals** : Similar to smartphones, ultra-thin and flat laptop designs necessitate compromises in audio component quality. Even powerful, expensive laptops like a 2,500-euro MacBook Pro often have internal sound cards that are insufficient for music creators and audio professionals, requiring them to use external sound cards for adequate quality. "los que hacemos música aunque nos gastemos 2 500 euros en un Macbook propotentísimo tenemos que usar tarjetas de sonido externas porque la que tienen los ordenadores no vale."
**Insufficient Output Power for Headphones** : A significant issue with smartphones and laptops is their limited audio output power. This often means that high-end or demanding headphones cannot be driven to their full potential, leading to low volume and a suboptimal listening experience, even if the headphones themselves are capable of excellent sound. "uno de los grandes problemas que tienen los teléfonos es la potencia de salida y os habrá pasado muchas veces que tiene unos abriculares de muy alta gama los conectas a tu teléfono y no se oye."
**The Rise of Hi-Res Audio Players** : Modern hi-res audio players (DAPs) are presented not as an evolution of the mainstream MP3 player but rather as a spiritual successor to devices like the Creative Nomad Jukebox, which prioritized a powerful internal audio processing chip. These devices are designed specifically to deliver superior sound quality, correcting the quality compromises made in general-purpose portable electronics. "reproductores actuales de alta calidad de hi-res que no son una evolución de los MP3 sino que son una evolución del fallido creatif Noman jukebox en los que había un chip de procesado de sonido que era un caño."
**High Cost of Quality Audio Processing** : Dedicated hi-res audio players are expensive, with entry-level models starting around 400 euros and high-end units exceeding 1000 or even 2000 euros. This cost reflects the investment in powerful audio processing chips and components, in stark contrast to the depreciated cost of older MP3 players. "el problema de reproductores hi-res es el mismo que valen una pasta y aunque los últimos modelos de reproductor de MP3 que habían mejorado mucho ya eran muy económicos ... como estos reproductores de audio hi-res tienen chis de procesados sonidos realmente potentes valen una pasta."
**DAC Functionality of Hi-Res Players** : Many hi-res audio players incorporate a Digital-to-Analog Converter (DAC) function, allowing them to be connected via USB to a computer. In this configuration, the computer can use the hi-res player's superior DAC and audio processing chip to convert digital signals to analog, bypassing the computer's often inferior internal audio hardware and significantly enhancing the sound quality. "los puedes usar como DAC lo que significa que tú puedes conectar al reproductor de hi-res a tu ordenador porque por USB y el ordenador puede usar el chip de procesado de sonido puede usarlo como DAC como como fuente externa para manipular el sonido porque es mejor que el de tu ordenador portátil."
**Hi-Res Players for Home Audio Systems** : Beyond portable use, hi-res players offer significant advantages when integrated into a home stereo system. They can serve as a high-quality digital audio source, often outperforming the direct output from smartphones or even laptops (which typically require an external sound card to achieve comparable quality), thus improving the overall listening experience on a dedicated audio setup. "incluso para usar en el equipo de sonido de casa esto te ha dado una calidad que no te da ningún tipo de señal digital que tengas muy seguramente."
