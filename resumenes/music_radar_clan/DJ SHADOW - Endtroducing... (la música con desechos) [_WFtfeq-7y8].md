## albums
**Endtroducing..... (1996) - Historic Debut Album** : This album is widely considered a historical landmark, described as a revolution upon its release, despite not inventing new techniques or methodologies in itself. Its profound significance lies in its contextual value, representing a creative culmination rather than a fresh start. DJ Shadow meticulously crafted the entire album using the Akai MPC60, processing abandoned records found during years of dedicated digging. It is officially cataloged as the first album made 100% through sampling processes, although this claim is subject to controversy due to varying definitions of sampling and the inclusion of some recorded elements like vocals. The album's production style, marked by manual imperfections and rhythmic breaks, solidified processes still in use over two decades later. "Posiblemente el disco de día y sado es el disco que termina todo el proceso de desarrollo musical del hip hop, como que a partir de ese disco conceptualmente el hip hop instrumental llega a un punto climax y a partir de ahí evoluciona muy poco."
**Endtroducing..... (1996) - International Success & US Delay** : DJ Shadow, an American artist and considered one of the pioneers of instrumental hip hop, oddly achieved initial success for *Endtroducing.....* in the United Kingdom, where he recorded it. The album was seen as electronic craftsmanship by the industry but had little impact outside the UK initially. It quickly gained recognition across Europe, but its appreciation in the United States was significantly delayed, taking a considerable amount of time before it was fully understood and recognized as a masterpiece. "A pesar de eso, Diezado sacaría su disco en Londres y no tuvo pradía en ningún éxito en Estados Unidos."
**Endtroducing..... (1996) - Collage of Forgotten Records** : The album is a complex collage of numerous forgotten and abandoned records that had never received attention. DJ Shadow spent years, often eight to ten hours a day, meticulously searching through "musical waste" in record stores to unearth these hidden gems. He resurrected hundreds of such discarded discs, incorporating them into his work. Many of these samples were never credited because their original artists, labels, or even existence were unknown or unregistered, making them impossible to trace or claim. "este disco de DJ se ha dado es un complejo colas de todos estos pequeños discos olvidados y abandonados que nunca le he sido caso."
**Endtroducing..... (1996) - Björk Sample Inclusion** : The album includes fragments of songs by Björk, an artist explicitly mentioned as being credited within *Endtroducing.....*. This highlights that while many samples were from forgotten and uncredited sources, some identifiable and recognizable artists did have their work integrated and acknowledged. "hasta Vior, que está creído en el Entrodius, porque hay fragmentos de alguna canción de Vior."

## curiosities
**King of Diggers** : DJ Shadow is referred to as "the king of diggers" due to his unparalleled dedication and fanaticism in searching for records, possessing a "spider sense" for finding them. He frequented record stores, often spending entire days sifting through vast quantities of records and consistently leaving with large stacks of discoveries. This special access for digging was a privilege not granted to everyone. "Dice Isshado o es el rey de los buscadores. No hay nadie tan fanático. Tiene un sexto sentido. El sentido de las arañas."
**Years of Dedicated Digging** : DJ Shadow dedicated years of his life, often spending eight to ten hours daily, methodically searching through "musical waste" in record stores. His goal was to unearth forgotten, destroyed, or uncataloged records that were never famous or popular, and to resurrect them through his music. A documentary exists showing him explaining and visiting these places where he spent years looking for abandoned records, highlighting the immense effort and dedication involved. "DJ se ha dado paso años enteros de su vida años enteros, rebuscando ocho y diez horas y a las ciendas de discos en los desechos musicales."
**Humility in Digging** : DJ Shadow views the act of digging through countless forgotten records as a humbling experience. He sees the piles of discarded discs as "great piles of broken dreams," recognizing that most of the artists never achieved a career, which instills a sense of respect. This perspective serves as a reminder that any artist creating and releasing music is adding to these potential piles, urging reflection on one's own perceived invincibility. "Solo el estar aquí es una lección de humildad para mí, porque todos estos discos son, en cierto modo, como grandes pilas de sueños rotos, casi ninguno de estos artistas ha hecho carrera, así que eso te impone un respeto."
**Craftsmanship and Manual Effort** : DJ Shadow's work, particularly on his debut album, is characterized as a piece of pure craftsmanship. He achieved complex layering and intricate rhythmic breaks on the Akai MPC60, a machine designed for much simpler sampling processes, by meticulously working layer upon layer. The album's creation involved manual synchronization, quantization, sample chopping, and BPM setting, all done by ear due to the MPC60's technical limitations and lack of a display. "Si hay algo que define ese trabajo y ese lado es la artesanía."
**Pioneer of Instrumental Hip Hop** : DJ Shadow is considered one of the fathers of instrumental hip hop and scratching, with documentary recordings showing him alongside other pioneers like Cut Chemist and Mix Master Mike during the early days of the genre. His foundational use of the Akai MPC60 and his innovative approach to sampling helped solidify the processes used in modern music production, influencing artists like Kanye West. "Tenemos grabaciones de documentales de hip hop, en el que está DJ se ha dado, con Quiebre, con Kat Kimis, con Mixmaster Mike."

# artist - J Dilla

## curiosities
**Manual Sampling Techniques** : J Dilla's work is referenced as an example of 90s instrumental hip hop where samples were triggered manually with fingers, known as "finger drumming." This technique led to characteristic minor desynchronizations and timing issues, similar to those found in DJ Shadow's music, because artists had to adjust sample timings by ear. "esto nos pasa en, por ejemplo, en discos de JDL, o en muchos discos de hip hop instrumental que se hacían con Sample que todo se hacía disparando a mano con los dedos, el finger, graming, todos los samples que iba a disparando a mano entonces."
**Artisanal Approach to Sampling** : J Dilla's music is grouped with that of Cut Chemist and DJ Shadow as exemplifying an artisanal, pure craftsmanship approach to the sampling process. This refers to the manual, ear-driven method of creating tracks with limited technology, which gave their music a distinctive, raw quality marked by subtle imperfections. "te recordaba los discos de Catkemi's, a los de DJ's a Do, a los de DJ's a Do, a los de J.D. La, te recordaba todos estos discos donde todo el proceso de Sample y de Artesania pura."

# genre - Hip Hop

## curiosities
**Early Musical Simplicity** : In its nascent stages in the early 1980s, the instrumental component of hip hop was musically rudimentary and simple. It primarily relied on sampling existing songs, drawing heavily from soul, funk, and disco records. The technique involved looping short fragments (2-10 seconds) and layering 2-4 additional sounds for rappers. "El hip hop, la parte instrumental del hip hop, musicalmente es muy precaria, es muy sencilla."
**Creative Value in Recycling** : The creative essence of early hip hop was not rooted in complex musical technique, but rather in its contextual value of recycling and repurposing existing music. The core creative act was the discovery of fantastic 4-5 second loops from old records that could be repeated and enjoyed by audiences, transforming existing sounds into something new. "El gran elemento creativo que tenía el hip hop era ir a encontrar esos 4 o 5 segundos, tan fantásticos que podías estar repitiendo los en bucle y a la gente le gustaba."
**Evolution of Sampling** : While initially simple, hip hop sampling rapidly evolved to become more complex and interesting, progressing from basic breakbeats created manually with vinyls to more sophisticated machine-based sampling. This evolution, particularly influenced by machines like the Akai MPC60, significantly shaped the musical landscape of the past 30 years across various styles. "Eso es muy rápido, fue evolucionando y se fue haciendo cada vez más complejo y más interesante."

# genre - Instrumental Hip Hop

## curiosities
**Culmination with DJ Shadow's Endtroducing.....** : DJ Shadow's 1996 album, *Endtroducing.....*, is seen as a culminating point in the musical development process of instrumental hip hop. From a conceptual standpoint, the genre reached a climax with this album, with its evolution becoming notably less pronounced afterward, almost marking an end to a particular path rather than a beginning. "Posiblemente el disco de día y sado es el disco que termina todo el proceso de desarrollo musical del hip hop, como que a partir de ese disco conceptualmente el hip hop instrumental llega a un punto climax y a partir de ahí evoluciona muy poco."
**Influence of Manual Desynchronization in the 90s** : The distinct sound of sample-based instrumental hip hop in the 1990s was heavily influenced by the manual timing imperfections, or "desacompasamientos," that resulted from artists manually triggering samples. This characteristic, due to the hands-on nature of the technology and the need to adjust timings by ear, became a defining feature of the genre during that decade, exemplified by artists like J Dilla and DJ Shadow. "Y es una de las cosas que yo creo que más ha marcado el sonido del Sample en los 90, esos pequeños desacompasamientos manuales."

# instrument - Akai MPC60

## curiosities
**Pioneering Sampling Workstation (Late 80s)** : The Akai MPC60, invented by Akai in the late 1980s, was a historically significant machine considered the first practically designed for more complex sampling. Its introduction fundamentally altered musical production forever, although it was initially conceived for relatively simple sampling processes. "acá hay inventó a finales de los 80 la famosísima MPC 60, histórica. Esa máquina cambiaría la producción musical para siempre."
**Technical Limitations and Manual Workflow** : The MPC60 presented significant technical limitations, such as an inability to process musical fragments longer than 13 seconds due to insufficient memory, which would cause the machine to "crash." Its lack of a proper screen (only showing a few letters) meant that critical tasks like synchronization, quantization, sample chopping, and setting BPMs had to be performed entirely manually, relying solely on the producer's ear and dexterity. "No podías poner fragmentos musicales en más de 13 segundos porque petaban no tenía memoria suficiente."
**Foundation of Modern Production** : The Akai MPC60, through the innovative work of artists like DJ Shadow, helped solidify the musical production processes that are still used today. Understanding the evolution of hip hop is intrinsically linked to understanding the development and capabilities of MPC machines, as they represent the core of sound processing and creation for decades. "Con la calle MPC 60 y con Diezado ya se asinta en los procesos, los procesos que usamos hoy en día en la producción musical."

# instrument - Turntables

## curiosities
**Early Sampling Tool** : Before sophisticated sampling machines, the earliest forms of sampling, such as creating breakbeats, were performed manually using turntables and vinyl records. This involved physically manipulating vinyls to loop sections, laying the groundwork for more advanced sampling techniques. "al principio se hacía mano con los vinilos, se hacía el breakbeat."
**Source of Sonic Imperfections** : The use of vinyl records as the source material for samples contributed to characteristic imperfections in 90s sample-based music, notably in DJ Shadow's *Endtroducing.....*. These imperfections included audible vinyl crackle and surface noise, which were a direct consequence of the technology and a part of the era's distinctive sound. "Este disco tiene un montón de ruidos de por ejemplo el rascado de los vinilos que os había diéxado."

# curiosity

**The "Musical Waste" Concept** : The music industry, like any other, generates "waste"—records that are forgotten, unpopular, uncataloged, or even destroyed. These consist of discs that never achieved fame, went unheard, or became obsolete. This concept highlights the vast trove of unappreciated music that artists like DJ Shadow diligently searched for, seeing it as a resource to be resurrected and recycled, bringing new life to abandoned sounds. "la industria musical genera grandísimos discos históricos genera que continuan y discos que se convierten en desechos, discos que van a las tanquetas, que nadie escucha, que acaban destruidos, que acaban descatalogados y olvidados, discos que nunca llegaron a ser famosos ni populares, la industria musical genera desechos, estriste es macabro pero es así"
**Controversy of "First 100% Sample-Based Album"** : DJ Shadow's *Endtroducing.....* is officially credited as the first album made 100% using sampling processes, a claim that sparks significant controversy. The dispute arises from differing interpretations of what constitutes "sampling" and "making an album out of sampling," particularly given that some parts of the album were recorded rather than sampled, such as certain vocals. This highlights the fluidity of definitions in music history. "Según el recorrido, este es el primer disco hecho 100% mediante procesos de sampling de la historia, lo cual genera mucha controversia, porque depende mucho de que entendamos por sampling, depende mucho de que entendamos por hacer un disco sobre sampling."
**The Enduring Standard of MPC Production** : The production methodology solidified by artists like DJ Shadow using machines such as the Akai MPC60 has set a enduring standard. Over 20 years later, this creative culmination in musical production remains highly relevant, with modern Akai MPC models (like the MPC X and MPC Live) continuing to function and operate on the same fundamental principles established decades ago. "Lo que hace interesante este disco no es que sea el primero en nada ni lo que sea el primer en sampling, sino que es un cultme en creativo, en un tipo de producción musical que a día de hoy se sigo utilizando y si están darizada hayan pasado más de 20 años."
**Distinctive Imperfections of 90s Sampling** : A charming and essential quality of 90s sample-based hip hop, evident in albums like *Endtroducing.....*, is the presence of minor flaws, errors, and defects directly stemming from the limitations of the era's sampling technology. These included vinyl crackle and, notably, subtle desynchronizations and timing issues caused by the entirely manual processes of triggering and adjusting samples by ear. These imperfections contributed significantly to the unique sound of the decade. "Este disco tiene un montón de ruidos de por ejemplo el rascado de los vinilos que os había diéxado. Este disco está lleno, por ejemplo, de pequeños desacompasamientos, todo el tiempo."
