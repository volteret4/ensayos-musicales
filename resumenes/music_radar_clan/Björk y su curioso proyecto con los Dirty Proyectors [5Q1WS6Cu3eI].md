# artist - Björk

## genres
Experimental
Alternative

## albums
**Volta (2007)** : This album's extensive and psychologically demanding tour, which lasted a year and a half, led Björk to take a significant artistic hiatus. The production style of *Volta* contrasts sharply with her later, more unrefined works, marking the end of a heavily produced phase. "Después de esa larguísima gira que se lo prolongó pero a día en año y medio ella decidió un poco tomar su respiro."
**Biophilia (2011)** : This album is identified as the beginning of Björk's extremely pure artistic phase, characterized by a deliberate reduction in production work. It represents a significant shift towards a more natural and organic sound compared to her earlier albums. "el biofilia yo creo que ya empiece a saber etapa de viores que es extremadamente pure, y que prácticamente no tiene trabajo de producción."
**Debut (Year Not Specified)** : Mentioned as one of Björk's early solo albums that featured an "enormous amount of production work." This style is contrasted with her later, more minimalist approach seen in projects like the collaborative album with Dirty Projectors and *Biophilia*. "Estos discos que tenía un enorme trabajo de producción."
**Post (Year Not Specified)** : Cited as an early solo album by Björk known for its "enormous amount of production work." This detailed production style later evolved into a purer, more organic sound in her subsequent projects. "Estos discos que tenía un enorme trabajo de producción."

## curiosities
**Artistic Hiatus (2007-2011)** : Björk took a four-year break from her main musical career between the release of *Volta* in 2007 and *Biophilia* in 2011. During this period, she engaged in various non-musical and collaborative projects, using the time to relax and disconnect. "Fue una pausa de unos cuatro años que poco se supone mucho de ella."
**Volta Tour Psychological Impact** : Björk herself described the *Volta* tour as her longest and most psychologically taxing experience, leaving her feeling deeply drained. This intense experience was a primary reason for her subsequent need for an artistic pause. "la esquina más larga y psicologinamente algo tan que tuvo en toda su cara, que la dejó para estar en tu cara psicologicamente y un poco le necesitaba tener una pausa de lo que había sido el éxito del Volta."
**Non-Musical Activism and Art Collaborations** : Beyond music, Björk is widely known for her dedication to environmental defense, animal rights, and ecology. She also frequently engages in collaborations with various art forms, including video creations with non-musical artists. "Sus proyectos no musicales por los que VR que es más conocida es un poco por el trabajo que hace por la defensa de media ambiente, por la recencia de los animales, por la ecología, por todas estas cosas, pero también un poco por todas las colaboraciones que hace con las de la variedía de arte, con proyectos de videocreaciones y de artistas que no son exactamente musicales."
**Evolution in Production Style** : The collaborative project with Dirty Projectors in 2010 serves as a crucial transitional work, bridging the gap between the heavily produced sound of Björk's early solo albums like *Debut* and *Post*, and the raw, natural, and minimally produced approach that would define *Biophilia*. "yo creo que te ayuda mucho a entender un poco el salto que se dio del volta de viores que al 2007 a el salto que empezó en el 2011 con el biofilia."

# artist - Björk & Dirty Projectors (Collaborative Project)

## labels
Domino Records

## albums
**Collaborative Album (2010) - Saving the Ocean** : This album originated from an initial idea for a single charity song, intended to promote protected marine areas and whale conservation at a 2010 event in the National Library of New York. It organically evolved into a full-length album deeply centered on the themes of ocean and whale preservation, created through an intuitive and spontaneous process with minimal conventional preparation. "De hecho el disco bar del océano de las gallenas y de todas estas cosas."
**Collaborative Album Recording Process** : The album was intentionally created with almost no prior composition, rehearsal, or production. The artists gathered and rehearsed for only three days, recording their nascent ideas—not fully formed songs—directly onto cassettes. The primary recording was completed in a single day, with an additional day dedicated to production and minor arrangements. "El proyecto se hace prácticamente sin componer prácticamente sin ensayar y prácticamente sin producir. Y es de este sentido de esta forma de no haber bastante intencionada."
**Collaborative Album Production Style** : Characterized by its raw and simple sound, the album features minimal production and arrangements, reflecting a deliberate choice to move towards a more unrefined aesthetic. This approach is compared to Björk's *Diol* (likely *Voltaic* or a live companion album) which followed *Volta*. "prácticamente no tiene producción, no tiene reglos y es como todo muy curo y muy sencillo."
**Collaborative Album Digital Release (2010)** : The album was initially released with very limited promotion through independent channels, exclusively in a digital format (HVNP3, likely MP3), via a dedicated website. This initial release did not generate significant downloads or substantial funds for its cause. "No no va a tener muchas descargas y tampoco llegado a recordar mucho dinero."
**Collaborative Album Vinyl Re-release (2011)** : Approximately one year after its digital debut, Domino Records (referred to as "dígilo, mi no") issued a small, limited edition vinyl pressing of the project. This re-release was specifically intended to raise additional funds for the marine conservation initiative. "ese proyecto sí se llevó a editar el digilo, un año más tarde. Lo digo todo, mi no, que es la discográfica que moda los transcernina, que moda los kilzas, y nada, muchísimos artistas indiquizos, una pequeña edición de este proyecto un poco para ayudar a recordar más dinero al proyecto."

## curiosities
**Charity Origin** : The project began as a charity initiative at the National Library of New York in 2010, aiming to raise awareness and foster the proliferation of protected marine areas, particularly for whales. This collaborative effort between Björk and Dirty Projectors was initially envisioned as a single song but evolved into a full album for the cause. "Ella se junto con varias artistas y entre ellos uno con el que ya había colaborado puntualmente a algunas ocasiones que llegaron a los irte proyectos."
**Whale Sound Imitation** : Dirty Projectors contributed to the album's unique sound by creating rhythmic foundations using drum machines and incorporating vocal improvisations that imitated whale sounds. This experimental vocal technique drew comparisons to Björk's work on her album *Medúlla*. "Los hip-hop proyectos hacen una especie de primeras bases de fondo con espécie de cajas de ritmos pero sigan así mismo emitando como ruidos de gallenas y jugando con su voz un poco como ya hacía en los hígios de ver vuelta."
**Collector's Vinyl Edition** : The vinyl edition of the collaborative album, released a year after its digital debut, is a highly sought-after item for collectors. Many fans are unaware of the project's existence, and even fewer know about its limited vinyl release. It features a unique 3D cover, was sold exclusively in independent stores, and is strictly numbered, such as edition 1,040. "Este en concreto es la edición, 1.040. Entonces si se es muy fan de viores y es nuestro coleccionismo, esa es una piza que os puede dar mucho que farrar cuando la enseñé."

# curiosity

**Marine Conservation Initiative** : A core purpose of the collaborative musical project between Björk and Dirty Projectors was to advocate for the proliferation of protected marine areas and to raise public awareness regarding the conservation of whales and other marine life. This environmental cause served as the initial inspiration for their joint musical endeavor. "El proyecto en los que ella y varias artistas querían un poco fomentar la proliferación de zonas marinas protegidas, un poco una historia a mis estesanbaras, las gallenas y estas cosas."
