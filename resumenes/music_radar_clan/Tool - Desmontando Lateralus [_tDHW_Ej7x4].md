## albums
**Lateralus (2001)** : This album is widely recognized as a pivotal work in rock history, released with considerable anticipation following the critical and commercial success of its predecessor. The band dedicated years to its development, creating extensive demos at home before a more concentrated three-month studio recording period. Produced by Tool themselves and Biz Botryl, the album is famously laden with intricate secrets, which sparked numerous fan theories and urban legends upon its release, including a rumored halt in production. Notably, the CD and vinyl versions featured different track listings, with the band confirming the vinyl order was a technical compromise due to space limitations and expressing a preference for the CD's sequence. The initial US release contained typographical and spelling errors in the booklet and lyrics, which are now prized collector's items. The album's central themes explore meditation, the astral projection of mind and body, and the concept of dualism, while also weaving in personal narratives such as loss and life stages. Musically, Lateralus is characterized by astonishing rhythmic complexity, frequently featuring instruments playing in different, unsynchronized time signatures and continuous meter changes, a demanding technique the band meticulously developed over years. Although its initial critical reception was not as enthusiastic as Ænima, over time, as its layers and secrets have been uncovered, Lateralus has solidified its place as Tool's most charismatic and influential album, significantly impacting later artists like Muse. "Es un disco completamente clave dentro de la historia del rock."
**Ænima (Year Unspecified)** : This album preceded Lateralus and garnered significant critical acclaim, leading to an extensive and successful tour. Its release, however, was also met with considerable controversy, particularly concerning certain songs like "Die Eier Von Satan," which sparked debates over perceived references to Satanism or Nazism. This album's success and the ensuing discussions built immense anticipation for what Tool would create next, setting a high bar for Lateralus. Critically, Ænima initially received better reviews than its successor. "Había tenido hace unos años el éxito del disco anterior de Lónima que había tenido un montón de críticas, habían hecho esa gira enorme."

## songs
**Die Eier Von Satan (Year Unspecified)** : A track from the Ænima album that generated significant controversy upon its release. The song's lyrics fueled debates among listeners, with some interpreting them as references to Satanism and others to Nazism. This ambiguity was largely attributed to Tool's characteristic use of metaphorical and obscure lyrical styles, which often invite diverse and sometimes conflicting interpretations. "Aquella canción de Diel, Biel Bones, Satan, nunca recuerdo bien el nombre, pero bueno, era esa canción en la que había habido bastante polémicas sobre si hablaban del Satanismo, a la gente que decía que hablaban de los nazis."
**Faaip de Oiad (2001)** : This song serves as the closing track on the Lateralus album and is notable for incorporating a spoken narrative, delivered in a radio-like voice. The text for this narrative is sourced from a real and infamous incident in the United States involving a late-night radio caller. This individual claimed to be a former government employee working at Area 51, detailing secret government plans involving "visitors" from space. The radio host grew increasingly agitated as the caller provided specific, unsettling details, eventually cutting the broadcast. Tool managed to rescue fragments of this intercepted call and integrated them into the song, contributing to its paranoid and mysterious atmosphere. "Dentro de esa canción, con la que cierran el disco, es curioso por gays como una persona hablando, si os fijáis hay como un texto narrado, como medio con voz de radio."
**The Grudge (2001)** : This track from Lateralus is highlighted as a prime example of Tool's intricate rhythmic design. In "The Grudge," the band showcases instruments operating at different, unsynchronized time signatures, illustrating their mastery over complex musical arrangements that defy conventional rhythmic alignment. "El ejemplo más claro es la canción del cheat."
**Lateralus (2001)** : The album's title track, it serves as a quintessential illustration of Tool's thematic interests, specifically referencing the duality between the body and mind. The song explores mystical states achieved through meditation, embodying the band's fascination with these profound and introspective concepts. "La canción de la terá luz es el claro ejemplo de cómo hace referencia a la dualidad, cuerpo y mente, y a todos estos estados místicos en meditación que les gustan."

## curiosities
**Fibonacci Sequence and Golden Ratio in Album Structure** : The rhythmic framework of Tool's Lateralus album is deeply rooted in complex mathematical concepts, specifically the Fibonacci sequence and the Golden Ratio. The band meticulously incorporated these advanced principles into the arrangements, underpinning the album's intricate and layered musicality. "Toda esta historia sobre el complejo isendramado matemático de la serie de Fibonacci y la proporción aurea."
**Ambiguous and Metaphorical Lyrical Style** : Tool's lyrics are consistently characterized by their ambiguity, metaphorical depth, and often bizarre nature. This distinctive approach frequently leads to a wide spectrum of interpretations and has historically ignited controversies, such as the debates surrounding the song "Die Eier Von Satan." Their abstract style actively encourages extensive analysis and discussion among their fanbase. "Las letras de Tull siempre han sido como tan ambigüas y como tan metafóricas y tan extrañas, como que siempre se ha prestado mucho a este tipo de paranoías."
**Band's Self-Production and Creative Autonomy** : Tool is renowned for its strong inclination towards self-production, effectively taking on the primary creative responsibilities for their albums. While Biz Botryl is credited as a producer on Lateralus, his role is primarily viewed as technical, with the band retaining the core creative control and direction. "Tull le pasa como mucho de este tipo de bandas que prácticamente se producen a sí mismos."
**Urban Legends and Fan Theories Surrounding Lateralus Launch** : The release of Lateralus was quickly accompanied by a flurry of urban legends and intricate fan theories. These included rumors of a temporary halt in the album's production. Fans rapidly engaged in deciphering perceived hidden meanings, especially concerning the differing track orders between the CD and vinyl versions, and the mysterious typographical errors found in the US booklet. "El lanzamiento diópia a muchas leyendas urbanas, hay muchas leyendas urbanas de transgenanzamiento."
**Band's Preferred Album Track Order** : Tool openly stated that the intended listening order for Lateralus was not the one ultimately presented on the vinyl release, which had to be adjusted due to technical constraints related to physical space on the record. The band expressed a clear preference for the tracklist found on the compact disc version, referring to the vinyl alteration as a "technical" compromise. "La banda no se había dicho en entrevistas que el orden original para escuchar en la lateral, lo es no era el del disco."
**Collectible Typographical Errors in US Lateralus Booklet** : The initial US pressing of the Lateralus album included typographical and spelling errors within its booklet and song lyrics. These mistakes reportedly led to a temporary halt in production for correction. Copies of this original, flawed edition are now considered highly valuable and sought-after collector's items by fans. "Los que tengáis en la terálus con esas faltas de ortografía, con esos errores tipográficos que sepáis que tenéis una pieza bastante valiosa."
**Complex Thematic Storytelling in Albums** : Tool's albums typically feature a highly intricate narrative structure. This includes a dominant, overarching theme, which is complemented by several "adjacent" or secondary arguments, and further interwoven with smaller, sometimes obscure themes. This creates a "structural chaos" that is challenging to fully grasp and encourages a multitude of interpretations from listeners. "Los discos de tool además siempre tienen, suede tener una estructura argumental como muy padecida, porque tienen como un argumento principal muy dominante, pero no único."
**Interactive and Abstract Lyrical Approach** : Within the primary themes of their songs, Tool often embeds fragments of secondary thematic material or concepts that interact with the main narrative. This is delivered through a highly codified and abstract linguistic style, which deliberately leaves much open to interpretation, compelling listeners to engage deeply and draw their own conclusions. "Dentro de la temática principal, a veces de la canción, como que te meten restos, de otra temática secundaria o de cosas como que interactúan con ellas."
**Extensive Fan Interpretations and Online Discussions** : The profound complexity and inherent ambiguity of Tool's lyrics and themes, particularly evident in Lateralus, have fostered a thriving culture of fan interpretation. Online platforms, including websites dedicated to explaining lyrics and forums like Reddit, continue to host regular and passionate discussions and debates about the band's thematic content, even more than 15 years after the album's release. "Yo creo que hay muchísimas historias de interpretaciones sobre las temáticas que hay dentro de la terá luz."
**Rhythmic Disynchronization as a Compositional Tool** : A defining and extraordinary characteristic of Lateralus is the band's use of rhythmic disynchronization, where different instruments often perform in distinct, unsynchronized time signatures simultaneously. This incredibly complex mathematical approach, developed by Tool over many years, results in music that is notoriously difficult for musicians to play from sheet music, yet achieves a cohesive and powerful sound in recordings and live performances. "Uno de las cosas increíbles que tiene el disco de túl de la terálus es que los instrumentos en muchos momentos trabajan a distintos compases, trabajan rítmicamente desacompasados, van a distintos ritmos."
**Frequent Rhythmic and Meter Changes** : Further enhancing their musical intricacy, Tool's compositions are replete with continuous shifts in rhythm and time signatures. This constant flux contributes to the music's unpredictable and structurally sophisticated nature, distinguishing their work from that of many other bands. "Para hacerlo más complicado, el disco tiene continuos cambios ritmicos y continuos cambios de compas."
**Band's Silence on Interpretations Adds to Mythos** : Despite over 15 years of dedicated fan efforts to decode the perceived secrets of Lateralus, Tool has largely refrained from actively clarifying or corroborating the myriad interpretations. This deliberate ambiguity, while occasionally frustrating for fans seeking definitive answers, is considered a core element contributing to the album's enduring myth and mystique. "Es una pena porque la banda tampoco nos ayuda mucho a corroborar todo ese juego de estifrar el lateralos de túl que hemos tenido durante más de 15 años."
