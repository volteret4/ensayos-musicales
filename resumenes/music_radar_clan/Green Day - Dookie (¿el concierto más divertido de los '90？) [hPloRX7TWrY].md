## albums
**Dookie (1994) - Breakthrough Album** : This album is considered one of the best albums of the 90s and an icon of modern music. It was Grimbey's second or third album, depending on how one counts their previous underground releases and an earlier initial disk. It was co-produced by the band and, for the first time, by an external professional, Rob Cavallo from Reprise Records. Dookie became Grimbey's best-selling album, with over 20 million copies sold, and is their only album to achieve diamond certification. It also received the Grammy for Best Alternative Music Album. "el disco más vendido de la carrera de Grimbey con más de 20 millones de copias y es el único que ha sido el disco de diamante."
**Previous Underground Album(s)** : Prior to signing with a major label, Grimbey had achieved significant success with their completely underground albums, establishing a highly respected presence within the American punk scene. This early success positioned them as a notable and influential band before their mainstream breakthrough. "Grimbey ya había tenido mucho éxito anterior con su álbum completamente underground y se habían hecho un hueco muy importante y respetado dentro de la escena panca americana."

## curiosities
**Signing with Reprise Records - The "Sell-Out" Controversy** : After gaining significant underground success, Grimbey decided to sign with a more professional label, which led to numerous problems within the punk community. All major record labels in the United States vied to sign them, with Warner Brothers even inviting the then-22-year-old, financially struggling band to Disneyland for a day, expenses paid, to impress them. Ultimately, Grimbey rejected the biggest multinationals but signed with Reprise Records, a significant label that was part of Warner Brothers, leading to them being branded as "sell-outs." "acabaron rechazando las más grandes multinacionales, pero firmaron por repris y récordos, no era una grandísima multinacional, pero era una escográfica bastante importante y a la vez pertenecía Warner Brothers, con lo cual acabaron siendo tachados elendidos."
**Expulsion from 1924 Gilman Street Club** : Following their signing with Reprise Records, Grimbey, who had regularly performed at the famous punk club 1924 Gilman Street in California, was expelled from the venue. They were no longer permitted to play there, and numerous graffiti messages against the band appeared on the streets of California, effectively condemning them even before their new album was released. "después de firmar por esta discográfica fueron expulsados del local, no pudieron volver a tocar en él, hubo un montón de pintadas en contra de la banda, en las esténas de gran California."
**"Dookie" Album - Original Back Cover Controversy** : The initial back cover of the album "Dookie" featured Ernie from Sesame Street, a character which the band eventually had to remove. Although they were never formally sued by Sesame Street, as is often rumored, there was a high risk of legal action. Given that "Dookie" contained explicit lyrics and themes addressing sex, drugs, and masturbation, having Ernie on the cover could have generated enormous problems for the band and the label. The original cover was withdrawn and replaced with the current one approximately a year and a half later, after two initial editions. "la contraportada unicional del Duffy tenía a Epi de Sesamun Street... tenían mucho risco de ser demandados...tener a Epi en la contraportada podía generar enormes problemas."
**Recording "Dookie" - Bookmobile Studio** : Despite signing with Reprise Records, a label under Warner Brothers, Grimbey recorded "Dookie" as if they were still a completely underground band. The album was recorded very quickly, in just two weeks, with the band acting as co-producers and having total creative freedom. The budget remained so low that the initial recording of "Dookie" was done in a small bookmobile owned by the father of the drummer, Tre Cool, highlighting the limited economic resources they gained from their major label deal. "el inicio de la ágida del Duffy la acabaron haciendo una especie de pequeño bibliobús a que era propiedad del padre, del batería, del trecúl."
**Woodstock '94 Performance and Riot** : Grimbey's performance at Woodstock '94 became one of the most historic moments in 90s music. Invited to the 25th-anniversary modern edition of the festival, they were still emerging and played a brief 30-minute set on a secondary stage. A heavy downpour turned the festival ground into deep mud. The band began playing hysterically, driving the crowd into a frenzy, leading people to throw mud at each other, and some even rolling in it. As the situation escalated into small fights, the crowd began throwing mud at the band. In response, the singer picked up a piece of mud, put it in his mouth, spat it out, and continued singing, which caused the crowd to completely lose control. "Fue un atentico chaos."
**Chaos and Bassist's Injury at Woodstock '94** : During their infamous Woodstock '94 performance, the crowd completely lost control, throwing massive amounts of mud at the band, covering the stage and instruments. The band reciprocated, stopping periodically to throw mud back at the audience, and actively provoked the crowd to invade the stage, causing security to lose all control. The singer further escalated the chaos by dropping his pants and performing without them. After the concert ended, the band remained on stage, continuing to engage in mud fights with spectators and provoking them, which ultimately led to security forcefully intervening. In the ensuing pandemonium, one security guard mistakenly identified Grimbey's bassist as an attendee, assaulted him, and slammed him against part of the stage decor to quell the fight. As a result, the bassist sustained a massive contusion on his arm and lost two teeth. "uno de los deshubridad confundía al vierte en la jista de Grinde y con uno de los asistencies, al igual lo agredió y lo redujo para detener la pelea, embotrándolo contra parte del decorado del escenario."

# label - Warner Brothers

## curiosities
**Attempt to Sign Grimbey** : Warner Brothers, a prominent record label, actively sought to sign Grimbey. They went as far as inviting the band members, who were young, around 22 years old, and without much money, to Disneyland for a day with all expenses paid, in an attempt to persuade them to sign. "una de esas importantes discográficas que fue Warner Brothers acabó invitándolos a Disneylandia para pasar un día allí para que ficharan por ellos."
**Parent Company of Reprise Records** : Warner Brothers was the parent company of Reprise Records, the label Grimbey eventually signed with. This connection meant that despite rejecting Warner Brothers directly, Grimbey ultimately became associated with the larger corporation through Reprise. "pertenecía Warner Brothers."

# concert - Woodstock '94

## curiosities
**25th Anniversary Festival** : Woodstock '94 was a modern edition of the legendary 1969 festival, held to commemorate its 25th anniversary. The event featured many important bands of the late 80s and 90s, including the Red Hot Chili Peppers. "en 1994 cumplía 25 años y se hizo una nueva edición moderna."
**Grimbey's Performance and Mud Riot** : Grimbey's appearance at Woodstock '94, where they performed a 30-minute set on a secondary stage, became one of the most historical moments in 90s music. Amidst a heavy downpour that turned the grounds into deep mud, the band's hysterical performance ignited a massive mud fight among the audience, escalating into chaos where the band provoked the crowd and even engaged in the mud-slinging. This event led to a security breach, the bassist's injury, and ultimately, massive publicity for Grimbey. "nos dejó uno de los momentos más históricos de la música de los 90."

# curiosity

**Rob Cavallo's Role as Producer** : Rob Cavallo, a highly important figure at Reprise Records, played a pivotal role in the success of Grimbey's album "Dookie." He was the first external professional producer to work with the band, and his contribution was seen as crucial to the album achieving its widespread acclaim and sales. "Rob Cavallo era una persona muy importante del repris y records que fue la discográfica que hizo este álbum y que realmente es una de las personas que ha ido mucho al éxito de este disco."
**"Sesame Street" Character on Original Album Cover** : The initial back cover artwork for Grimbey's album "Dookie" famously featured Ernie from the children's show "Sesame Street." Although not directly sued, the band and label faced a high risk of legal action due to the potential conflict of placing a beloved children's character on an album with explicit lyrical themes, leading to the cover's eventual withdrawal and replacement. "Al principio la contraportada unicional del Duffy tenía a Epi de Sesamun Street, de Barrio Sesamun, como lo conocemos aquí, la cual tuvieron que retirar."
**Bookmobile as an Improvised Recording Studio** : Despite signing with a major record label, Grimbey demonstrated their continued DIY punk ethos by initially recording parts of their album "Dookie" in a small bookmobile. This vehicle was owned by the father of their drummer, Tre Cool, highlighting the band's resourcefulness and the surprisingly low budget for the recording despite their major label deal. "la acabaron haciendo una especie de pequeño bibliobús a que era propiedad del padre, del batería, del trecúl."
**Grimbey's Post-Woodstock Popularity** : The chaotic and widely publicized performance at Woodstock '94, including the infamous mud fight and the bassist's injury, garnered Grimbey enormous popularity. The event was covered by numerous media outlets and significantly boosted their profile, transforming their subsequent tour into one of the most important of the 1990s. "Fue un portal en muchísimos sitios, salieron un montón de regales y les dio una popularidad enorme."
