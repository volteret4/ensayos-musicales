## albums
**Transiro de Plexpress (Year Unspecified)** : Ralph Hütter and Florian Schneider traveled to the United States to handle the production and mastering of this album independently. Upon their return, Karl Bartos discovered the final version of the record only after it had already been manufactured, highlighting the independent and exclusive control maintained by the two founders over significant aspects of the band's output. "cuando salía el Transiro de Plexpress, que Ralph Yfloria se van a Estados Unidos a realizar la producción y masterización vuelven, y el propio Calvarto Siel descubre en la versión final del disco cuando ya está fabricada."

## curiosities
**Hermetic Nature and Public Perception** : Crabwork has consistently maintained a very private stance regarding its internal workings, offering minimal details in interviews and keeping personal lives of its members largely out of the public eye. This secrecy has fueled a considerable amount of speculative and often incorrect information written about the band, making it difficult for the public to access reliable facts about them. "Siempre han sido una banda como muy hermética dentro de lo que pasa dentro del proyecto, nunca nos han dado excesivos datos, nunca han dado entrevistas excesivamente comprometidas."
**Founding as a Personal Project (1970)** : Crabwork originated as the personal endeavor of two individuals, Ralph Hütter and Florian Schneider. Their collaboration began prior to 1970, with existing recordings from that year showing them performing together in a very similar precursor project. They also signed some early Crabwork material as just the two of them. "Crabwork realmente es el proyecto personal de dos personas, de Ralph y de Florian, que empezaron a trabajar juntos, de hecho tenemos ya grabaciones de antes en 1970."
**Definition of Classical Formation** : The band's "classical formation," traditionally recognized as Ralph Hütter, Florian Schneider, Karl Bartos, and Wolfgang Fuhr, is understood to have both started and concluded with the tenure of Wolfgang Fuhr within the group. His arrival and subsequent departure marked the boundaries of this specific lineup's existence. "normalmente consideramos que lo que llamamos la formación clásica de Crabwork, que son Ralph Florian, Carl y Wolfgang, empieza y termina con la llegada y con la marcha de Wolfgang Fuhr."
**Extensive Uncredited Personnel** : Beyond the commonly recognized four main members, Crabwork's operations involved a large, often unacknowledged group of collaborators. This included lyricists, sound engineers, industrial technicians, and experts who were instrumental in constructing the specialized musical machinery used by the band, all of whom were integral to the complex structure of Crabwork. "Tenemos a letristas, tenemos a ingenieros de sonido, tenemos a técnicos industriales, tenemos a gente que los ayudó a construir todas esas máquinas con las que desarrollaban la música."
**Salaried Employment of Members** : All individuals involved in Crabwork, including temporary members like András Hoffman and Flitch Hilbert, and even the core members of the classical formation such as Karl Bartos and Wolfgang Fuhr, were employed as salaried workers by Ralph Hütter and Florian Schneider. They received a fixed wage for their contributions to the project, highlighting the hierarchical structure. "nos cuenta Wolfanfurt que todos ellos, incluidos los otros miembros de Crabwork, incluso de la banda clásica que conocemos a Carvartos y Wolfanfurt, que eran asalariados de Ralph Yfloria."
**"Despotic" Control by Founders** : Ralph Hütter and Florian Schneider were perceived to exert a "despotic" level of control over everyone associated with Crabwork. They managed all aspects of the project, including significant creative and business decisions, often without consulting or even informing other band members. "realmente había una especie de comportamiento de espota entre Ralph Yfloria en todo lo que lo rodeaba, era como los que movían todo el engranaje de una manera un tanto de espota."
**Independent Production and Mastering (Transiro de Plexpress)** : For tasks like mastering and post-production, and even initial production stages, Ralph Hütter and Florian Schneider worked separately from the other band members. This was exemplified with the album "Transiro de Plexpress," where they traveled to the United States for these processes, and Karl Bartos only saw the finished, manufactured version of the album. "Ralph Yfloria se irán separados para hacerla sin contar con el resto de miembros de la banda."
**Exclusive Business Dealings During Tours** : During a lengthy US tour, which lasted nearly a year, Ralph Hütter and Florian Schneider alone handled all commercial agreements and finalized performance arrangements. They often did so without informing the other band members, leaving them unaware of the tour's duration or upcoming locations. "Ralph Yfloria anivan todo el tiempo a cerrar las actuaciones y van todo el tiempo a hacer los acuerdos comerciales sin contar con ellos."
**Founders' Dedication and Obsession** : The lives of Ralph Hütter and Florian Schneider were entirely consumed by and dedicated to the Crabwork project. This extreme commitment manifested in their relentless focus on the Kling Klang Studio, which became the central destination for all of the band's generated income. "la vida de estos dos genios era una vida dedicada por y para el proyecto de Craf Work."
**Austere Living Despite Wealth** : Despite Crabwork generating significant income, the band members, particularly Wolfgang Fuhr, lived under remarkably austere conditions. Funds were predominantly channeled into the Kling Klang Studio, leading to modest accommodations on tour, such as sharing rooms in discreet hotels, and personal financial constraints for individuals like Wolfgang, who at times lacked money for desired purchases. "Craf Work de Aquella ganaba muchísimo dinero y que sin embargo vivían en unas condiciones increíblemente austeras."
**Breakup of Classical Formation Dynamics** : The classical lineup of Crabwork ultimately dissolved due to the dominant personalities of Ralph Hütter and Florian Schneider, and the overwhelming influence of the Kling Klang Studio. Karl Bartos departed out of frustration, feeling creatively stifled because much of the material he developed was not utilized. Wolfgang Fuhr also left the group, feeling dejected and diminished by the band's controlling environment. "la formación clásica de Craf Work se acababa rompiendo."

# artist - Wolfgang Fuhr

## member of
Crabwork

## instruments
Electronic Drum Kit

## curiosities
**Personal Biography - "I was a Robot"** : Wolfgang Fuhr authored a highly reliable and insightful personal biography titled "I was a Robot," which has recently been re-edited. This book offers crucial clarity on Crabwork's inner workings, a topic previously shrouded in mystery and speculation. "es la biografía personal de Wolfgang Fuhr, creo que os lo habían enseñado alguna vez."
**Biography Controversy and Censorship** : The initial release of Wolfgang Fuhr's biography generated significant controversy. The founding members of Crabwork, referred to as "Rancy Florian," were highly displeased with its candid details about the band's internal affairs. They pursued legal action against Fuhr, successfully compelling him to remove certain chapters that contained specific, concrete details they preferred to keep secret. "los braderos fundadores de Crabwork, Rancy Florian, se habían molestado bastante, con la biografía de Fuhr, llegaron incluso a atacarlo judicialmente, llegando a conseguir que se eliminase en algunos de los capítulos."
**Inspiration for "I was a Robot" Title** : The title of Wolfgang Fuhr's biography, "I was a Robot," stems directly from his personal experience within Crabwork. He chose this title not as a reference to any album by Mama Shins, but because he reached a point where he felt like a mere cog or robot within the operational machinery of the band. "El libro de hecho se llama I was a Robot y se llama así, no por el disco de Mamma Shins, sino porque llegó un punto en que el propio Wolfgang Fuhr se sentía un robot dentro del engranaje de Crabwork."
**Salaried Status in Crabwork** : Within Crabwork, Wolfgang Fuhr, like other members of the classical formation including Karl Bartos, was a salaried employee of Ralph Hütter and Florian Schneider, receiving a wage for his contributions. This contractual arrangement underscored the hierarchical structure within the band. "eran asalariados de Ralph Yfloria."
**Departure from Crabwork** : Wolfgang Fuhr ultimately left Crabwork feeling utterly depressed and sorrowful, in a poor state of mind. His departure, alongside Karl Bartos's, marked the definitive end of the band's classical four-member lineup. "Wolfman Thor acababa andorando el grupo totalmente deprimido y triste en un estado un poco malo y ahí es como se rompe la formación clásica."
**Inventor of the Electronic Drum Kit** : As the percussionist for Crabwork, Wolfgang Fuhr personally conceived and developed the very first electronic drum kit with his own hands. This innovative contribution was a significant technical achievement within the band's work. "él era el percusionista dentro del labor de Craf Work, con lo cual él nos cuenta por ejemplo en algún episodio como él inventó y como él desarrolló personalmente con sus manos la primera batería electrónica."
**Ignorance of Tour Details** : Wolfgang Fuhr recounts instances where he would embark on tours without knowing the duration or destinations, as Ralph Hütter and Florian Schneider handled all logistical and commercial agreements without informing the other members. This highlights the lack of transparency and control afforded to him. "me cuenta que él se fue de gira sin saber ni cuánto tiempo ni dónde iba a estar, porque Ralph Yfloria anivan cerrando todos los acuerdos casi sin informables."
**Financial Constraints on Tour** : Despite Crabwork's financial success, Wolfgang Fuhr experienced personal financial limitations, such as not having enough money to purchase a pair of shoes he liked while on tour. This austere living was a direct consequence of all band earnings being reinvested into the Kling Klang Studio. "nos cuenta como él por ejemplo a veces no tenía dinero para compras unos zapatatos que le gustaban cuando estaba de gira, viviendo en una manera enormemente humilde."

# artist - Ralph Hütter

## member of
Crabwork

## curiosities
**Co-Founder of Crabwork** : Ralph Hütter, alongside Florian Schneider, initiated Crabwork as a personal project, with their collaborative work dating back to before 1970. Early recordings from that year feature the duo performing together, establishing their foundational role in the band. "Crabwork realmente es el proyecto personal de dos personas, de Ralph y de Florian, que empezaron a trabajar juntos, de hecho tenemos ya grabaciones de antes en 1970."
**Mortgaging Home for Equipment Development** : Ralph Hütter took the drastic step of mortgaging his personal home to finance the development of the first 36-step sequencer. This critical piece of equipment was built in collaboration with the German sound company Matt Rangweacher, demonstrating his extreme dedication and financial commitment to Crabwork's technological advancement. "el propio Ralph llega y potecar su casa para poder pagar el desarrollo de esta máquina."
**Obsession with Kling Klang Studio** : Ralph Hütter and Florian Schneider became pathologically obsessed with the Kling Klang Studio, turning it into the central hub of their existence. They practically lived in the studio, eating and sleeping there, to the point where it was said the studio had completely "dominated their personality" and taken over their lives. "prácticamente decían que el estudio sabía poderado y dominado la personalidad de Ralph Yfloria, que estaban obsesionados de una manera enfermita, comían allí, dormían allí."

# artist - Karl Bartos

## member of
Crabwork

## albums
**Of the Records (Year Unspecified)** : This solo album by Karl Bartos comprises recordings from the "radioactíbitica" period (likely referencing Kraftwerk's "Radio-Activity" era) that he created but was not permitted to use within Crabwork by Ralph Hütter and Florian Schneider. The material was eventually published years after its creation. "tuvimos la publicación de ese álbum de Carbarto en solitario of the records, que eran todas esas grabaciones del radioactíbitica, había hecho Carbarto y que Ralph Yfloria no le habían dejado utilizar."

## curiosities
**Creative Frustration within Crabwork** : Karl Bartos was considerably more creatively active than Wolfgang Fuhr, and developed a significant amount of material that Ralph Hütter and Florian Schneider prevented him from utilizing within the Crabwork project. This creative suppression ultimately led to his strong dissatisfaction with the band. "Carbarto serán bastante más creativo, que Wolfman Thor y él había desarrollado mucho material que no podía utilizar dentro de Craf Work."
**Departure from Crabwork** : Karl Bartos left Crabwork feeling extremely annoyed and frustrated with Ralph Hütter and Florian Schneider. His departure was a key factor in the dissolution of the band's classical four-member lineup. "Carbarto se acababa andorando la formación muy molesto con Ralph Yfloria porque Carbarto serán bastante más creativo."
**Discovering Final Mixes Post-Production** : For the album "Transiro de Plexpress," Karl Bartos was involved in the recording phase but was completely excluded from the subsequent production and mastering handled independently by Ralph Hütter and Florian Schneider. He only encountered the final, manufactured version of the album after its completion, highlighting the founders' exclusive control over the creative process. "el propio Calvarto Siel descubre en la versión final del disco cuando ya está fabricada, porque ellos trabajan en la grabación, pero todo el resto del trabajo es realizado de los otros dos de manera independiente."

# artist - Matt Rangweacher

## curiosities

This project was so significant that Ralph Hütter mortgaged his house to fund its development. "Matt Rangweacher, la empresa de sonido alemana con la que construyeron el primer secuenciador de 36 pasos que existía."

# concert - US Tour

## curiosities
**Extended Duration and Living Conditions** : Crabwork embarked on a US tour that lasted for nearly a year. During this extensive period, the band members received a salary and were required to share hotel rooms, indicating relatively austere living conditions despite the band's commercial success. "esa enorme gira que tiene por Estados Unidos que droga casi un año, en la que ellos recibían un sueldo, en la que tenían que compartir habitación."
**Founders' Exclusive Business Dealings** : Throughout the US tour, Ralph Hütter and Florian Schneider exclusively managed all negotiations, closing performances and striking commercial agreements. They did this without involving or even informing the other band members, such as Wolfgang Fuhr, who often began parts of the tour without knowing its full duration or destinations. "Ralph Yfloria anivan todo el tiempo a cerrar las actuaciones y van todo el tiempo a hacer los acuerdos comerciales sin contar con ellos."

## curiosities
**First 36-Step Sequencer (Crabwork/Matt Rangweacher)** : Crabwork collaborated with the German sound company Matt Rangweacher to construct the very first 36-step sequencer in existence. The development of this pioneering machine was so financially demanding that Ralph Hütter resorted to mortgaging his own house to cover the costs. "Craf Work desarrolla el primer sintanoma con Matt Rangweacher, la empresa de sonido alemana con la que construyeron el primer secuenciador de 36 pasos que existía."

# curiosity

**Kling Klang Studio as a Financial Sink and Obsession** : All earnings generated by Crabwork were systematically funneled directly into the Kling Klang Studio, making it the central recipient of the band's financial success. This investment was so profound that it became an "enfermitamente" (sickly) obsession for Ralph Hütter and Florian Schneider, who practically lived, ate, and slept in the studio, believing it had dominated their personalities. "todo el dinero que se generaba en Craf Work iba directamente para el clinclan estudio."
**Critique of Wolfgang Fuhr's Biography** : A prominent German music critic and writer, who penned much of the known information about Crabwork, wrote the prologue for the re-edited biography of Wolfgang Fuhr. In it, the critic confessed, "creí que conocía la banda hasta que leí este libro," indicating the book's profound revelations about the band's hidden dynamics. "al rádio es que es un crítico y escritor musical alemán muy importante, que escribió muchísimo de lo que conocemos de Crabwork, hace el prólogo de esta nueva edición y nos dice literalmente creí que conocía la banda hasta que leí este libro."
**Understanding Crabwork Through Biography** : Wolfgang Fuhr's biography is highlighted for shedding light on three critical aspects of Crabwork that were previously misunderstood: the true identities and nature of the band members, the complex and often unclear relationships between them, and the actual goings-on within the Kling Klang Studio during their active period. "Gracias al libro entendemos tres cosas muy importantes de la banda que nunca habíamos terminado de entender."
