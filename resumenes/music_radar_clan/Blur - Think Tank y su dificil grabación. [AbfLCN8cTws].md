## albums
**Think Tank (2003) - Most Difficult Album** : This album, released in 2003 and referred to as '50' in the transcript, is considered the most challenging recording in Blur's career due to internal band tensions and creative disagreements. Initially, the goal was a more commercial and accessible album than their previous work like '13', though not a return to Britpop, but it eventually incorporated strong political themes. "el disco más difícil que habido en la carrera del grupo Blue, que fue el famoso 50 de 2003."
**Think Tank (2003) - Recorded Without Graham Coxon** : Think Tank is notable as the only Blur album recorded predominantly without their main guitarist, Graham Coxon. He left the group during its recording due to personal struggles and creative differences but later briefly rejoined, although his contributions were minimal and largely problematic. "es el único disco que se grabó sin graza en cocción, su principal guita rrista que acababa abandonando el grupo de una grabación de este disco."
**Think Tank (2003) - Evolution from Party Album to Political Statement** : Think Tank, initially conceived as an album reflecting London nightlife and partying, gradually evolved into a politically charged record, creating a diffuse theme balancing hedonism and anti-war activism. The album successfully balanced these seemingly contradictory themes, resulting in a unique and effective artistic statement. "acabó poco a poco convirtiendo en un disco con muchos grites políticos y quedó ahí una temática bastante difusa entre salir de fiesta y pelear contra la guerra."
**Think Tank (2003) - Critical and Commercial Success** : Despite the difficult recording process, Think Tank achieved enormous critical success, won many awards, and was eagerly embraced by fans who were highly anticipating new Blur material. "El disco tuvo un enorme desito de crítica, un montón de premios y la gente estaba tan ambiente por material de blur que se echó al disco y no se tejó y la verdad es que todo tuvo bastante desito."
**Think Tank (2003) - CD vs. Vinyl Release** : The main edition of Think Tank was released on CD because vinyl was not widely popular at the time. The initial 5,000-6,000 units included a special CD edition housed in a cool red-lined box. "la adección cedera, la adección en vinilo pero como esto ya era la época en la que el vinilo estaba muy de capacaira en la gran edición no se hizo en vinilo sino que se hizo en cedera y las primeras se hicieron en fotón 5, 6 mil unidades se hicieron en una edición cedera en caja forrada de rojo bastante chula."
**Think Tank (2003) - Limited Red-Covered Edition** : A particularly rare limited edition, released in the UK, featured a red-lined cover and included unreleased songs and live tracks. This specific CD edition from 2003 is still considered a treasure among Blur fans today. "las primeras unidades se hicieron en portada forrada en rojo que está guay que en algunas canciones inéditas, directos estas cosas y bueno, pelejón en cedera que hoy en día se rediten en 2003 y que está en praten entre todas las simpas edísimas."

## songs
**Out of Time (2003)** : This song from Think Tank is explicitly anti-war and against invasions, reflecting the album's emerging political themes. "hay canciones como el Out of Time que prácticamente van contra las guerras contra las invasiones."
**Ambulance (2003)** : Featured on Think Tank, "Ambulance" (referred to as "Ambelines" in the transcript) represents the album's contrasting theme of partying and going out, balancing the record's political statements. "hay canciones como Ambelines o como Battering orleg que van directamente antes a salir de fiesta."
**Battery in Your Leg (2003)** : The last song on Think Tank, "Battery in Your Leg" (referred to as "bater y yo leer" and "drummer in Giorlès" in the transcript), is significant as Graham Coxon's sole guitar contribution to the album. He recorded some guitar arrangements for it in a single day and is the only credited guitarist on the track. "La única aportación que tuvo graza en cocción al disco y va a grabar tres cuatro cosas en unico día que estuvo presente fue en la última canción, el bater y yo leer que él hizo algunos arreglos de guitarra y es en la única canción en la que él toca la guitarra y en la que son en los títulos."

## curiosities
**Post-'13' Hiatus and Solo Projects** : After recording the album '13', Blur members separated to pursue independent projects before reuniting for 'Think Tank'. "Lord se juntara después de separarse la grabación del 13 para seguir proyectos independientes."
**Tense Reunion in London** : The band's reunion for 'Think Tank' began with a tense meeting in a London studio, as the four members had not seen each other for a long time. This initial gathering was described as quite tense and disjointed. "Se juntan en Londres en la estu grabación y no se habían visto en muchísimo tiempo. Entonces esa primera reunión de ley, los cuatro, fue bastante tensa y bastante coja."
**Manayerti's Role in Calming Tensions** : The tense initial reunion of the band members was somewhat mitigated thanks to Manayerti, who is described as a great friend of the group and helped to soothe the situation. "gracias a Manayerti, siempre ha sido un gran amigo del grupo, se siguió un poco solentada."
**Think Tank (2003) Tour - Absence of Graham Coxon** : The tour supporting Think Tank was described as somewhat melancholic due to the absence of Graham Coxon, who was a significant contributor, especially given the album's overall lack of prominent guitar parts. "La gira fue un poco triste porque no estaba en cocción que siempre aportó mucho y leches un disco planeante no tienen guitarras."
**Think Tank (2003) Singles Artwork** : The singles released from Think Tank (referred to as "los indios" in the transcript) featured artwork with similar themes to the album cover, though these were created by other artists and graphic designers, not Banksy. "Los indios que salieron con el disco todos tienen en sus portadas temáticas muy similaresa a esta pero ya no las hizo más que sino que las hicieron otros artistas y diseñadores gráficos."
**Think Tank (2003) - Listening Insights** : Re-listening to Think Tank provides insight into the album's characteristic lack of guitars due to Graham Coxon's absence, his minimal contribution on "Battery in Your Leg," and the album's overarching dual themes of anti-war pacifism and hedonistic partying. "escucharlo otra vez y ver por un lado porque no hay guitarras porque el garras en coxon no estaba, escucharlo el último drummer in Giorlès que es la queñísima contrapación que llamaba el garras en coxon al disco y escuchar esas dos temáticas entre el disco tequejas, no a la guerra pacifismo, me voy de juerga."

# artist - Damon Albarn

## member of
Blur
Gorillaz
The Good, the Bad & the Queen

## curiosities
**Founding Gorillaz and Solo Work** : During Blur's hiatus, Damon Albarn founded the virtual band Gorillaz, released solo albums, and collaborated with numerous other groups, including The Good, the Bad & the Queen. "fundaría Gorilla, tuvo discos en Solitario, hizo de God de Banan de Queen, químuchos otros grupos."
**Dominance in Think Tank (2003) Production** : Damon Albarn was criticized for taking extensive control over the production of Think Tank, working on the album largely solo and reportedly influencing other band members to argue amongst themselves. "de Imon Alban había prácticamente que he utilizado todo lo que tenía que ver con el disco y le estaba haciendo todo en solitario y lo suisaba a los otros dos para que hayan de como peleares."
**Push Towards Electronic Sound for Think Tank (2003)** : Driven by his increasing interest in electronic music, Damon Albarn steered Think Tank towards a more electronic sound, exerting an influence on the album's direction comparable to Fatboy Slim's production on Syntac. "de Imon Alban, que a veces estaba más interesada en la electrónica, estaba haciendo que el disco poco a poco fue esa caminosa y más era electrónica."
**Pacifist Stance Influencing Think Tank (2003)** : Damon Albarn's long-standing focus on pacifist activities directly influenced the emergence of political themes in Blur's Think Tank album. "de Imon Alban siempre fue una persona muy enfocada en actividades pacifistas."
**Anti-War Activism and Personal Impact** : Damon Albarn publicly demonstrated his pacifist views by wearing an "Against War and Bombs" t-shirt when accepting a Grammy for a Gorillaz album. His strong anti-war sentiments were profoundly influenced by his grandfather's recent death, which occurred while recovering from a hunger strike that protested against "Graza y Graza" (which implies war and injustice). "He presento con la camiseta del Nova La Guerra y contra las bombas cuando recibió el Grammy por el disco de Gorilla y sobre todo él había tenido aún un gran impacto de mucho y es que su abuelo había muerto hace poco recuperándose de una abuela de hambre con la que había protestado encontrar a Graza y Graza."
**Profound Impact of Grandfather's Protest** : The protest and subsequent death of Damon Albarn's grandfather profoundly impacted his worldview, leading to strong pacifist convictions. "eso lo dejo bastante impactado."

# artist - Graham Coxon

## member of
Blur

## instruments
Guitar

## curiosities
**Prolific Solo Career** : During Blur's hiatus, Graham Coxon pursued a quite prolific solo career. "graza en cocción tuvo una carrera bastante proliscina en Solitario."
**Absence from Initial Reunion Meeting** : Graham Coxon's absence from Blur's initial reunion meeting in London significantly contributed to the overall tension surrounding the band's decision to record a new album. "graza en cocción ni siquiera se presentó."
**Controversial Role in Think Tank (2003) Recording** : Despite not being a full public member of Blur during the recording of Think Tank, Graham Coxon significantly complicated the recording process due to his personal issues and disruptive behavior. "el papel que juega graza en cocción dentro de este disco, porque aunque no estaba dentro del grupo de los públicos finalmente, dio bastante por saco."
**Rehabilitation for Alcoholism and Depression** : During the initial recording phases of Think Tank, Graham Coxon was undergoing rehabilitation for severe alcoholism and depression, a fact unknown to the band at the time. "graza en cocción estaba rehabilitándose dentro de una alcoholismo y depresión por los que estaba muy mal, pero el grupo no lo sabía y en un caso lo llegó a decir."
**Unexpected Return to Studio** : After Blur had begun recording Think Tank without him, Graham Coxon unexpectedly appeared at the studio one day and decided to start recording with the band. "un día graza en cocción se presenta allí como quien no tiene la cosa y el tío decide empezar a grabar con ellos."
**Band's Acceptance of Coxon's Return** : Despite his earlier absence, the band accepted Graham Coxon's return to the studio, acknowledging his importance as a key member, referred to as the "second pilálogo" (second pillar). "graza en cocción es el segundo pilálogo y ellos lo afectaron bastante bien."
**Disastrous Return to Recording** : Graham Coxon's return to the Think Tank recording sessions quickly devolved into a disaster due to his ongoing personal struggles and behavior. "El problema es que eso fue un auténtico desastre."
**Antidepressant Treatment and Alcoholism During Recording** : During his brief time in the studio for Think Tank, Graham Coxon was simultaneously on antidepressant treatment and constantly drunk, leading to numerous problems and eccentric behavior that made it practically impossible for the band to work with him. "Él estaba en pleno tratamiento de el antidepresivo y sobre todo él estaba borracho todo el día, entonces tenía un montón de problemas y sobre todo un comportamiento bastante exténtrico, lo cual hizo que prateía el impossible trabajar con él."
**Manager Demands Coxon's Departure** : Due to his disruptive and impossible behavior, the band's manager ultimately instructed Graham Coxon to leave the group during the Think Tank sessions and allow them to work in peace, a decision the rest of the band agreed to as the situation had become untenable. "el manayer acabó diciendo a la grabación cocción que se fuese de el grupo y se marcha si lo dejase trabajar tranquilos, a lo que el resto del grupo accedió porque la situación era rádia en imposibilidad."
**Personal Struggles and Discontent with Album Direction** : Graham Coxon was not only in a very bad personal state but was also highly discontent with the direction the Think Tank album was taking, particularly with Damon Albarn's dominance and the album's electronic shift. "Graza en cocción no solo estará en un punto muy malo de subir a personal, sino que encima estaba muy descontento como unas cosas que estaban pasando por el disco."
**Coxon's Disinterest in Tarle** : Graham Coxon was completely disinterested in 'Tarle', which contributed to his discontent with the album's direction and his minimal creative input. The exact nature of 'Tarle' (song, concept, etc.) is not specified. "Graza en cocción estaba en la gran disto por Tarle y eso no había nada que hacer."
**Deleted Guitar Recordings from Think Tank (2003)** : Graham Coxon recorded guitar parts for several songs on Think Tank, but these were ultimately deleted from the final album, leaving him with only one credited contribution. "él hizo algunas grabaciones de guitarras y otras canciones que finalmente fueron eliminadas."

# artist - Banksy

## curiosities
**Think Tank (2003) Album Cover** : The famous cover art for Blur's album Think Tank was created directly by Banksy, not merely inspired by his style, for which he received a significant payment. "la famosa portada del Cintan la hizo Basky pero el Basky me verdad no está inspirado en Basky sino que es Basky quien hizo esta portada y ganó bastante dinero a cien gra."
**Banksy's Motivation for Think Tank (2003) Artwork** : Banksy reportedly stated that he undertook the Think Tank artwork partly for the money, explaining that while painting walls could make one famous, it did not provide a living. "decía que lo hizo por la cia parte del dinero porque pintaron las calles de cabía pero no te ha de comer."
**Think Tank (2003) Cover and Back Cover Artwork Payment** : Banksy was paid a substantial amount for designing both the front and back covers of Blur's Think Tank, which subsequently became an iconic image in music history. "Le pagaron mucha dinero por hacer lo que fue la portada del Cintan y la contraportada del Cintan y la verdad es que la portaba y el diés un icono de la historia de la música."
