## albums
**Funeral (2004) - Debut Album** : The band's debut album, it was released in a very simple cardboard box, marking a modest beginning for their physical releases. This album is part of what is considered their "sacred trilogy" of first albums, which are now modern classics, having peaked in the early 21st century's alternative music scene. "publicando a que el funeral en una caja de cartón muy sencilla."
**Neon Bible (2007) - Second Studio Album** : This album was released in a cardboard sleeve, indicating a step up in the quality of their physical releases compared to their debut. It is also considered part of their "sacred trilogy" of early albums, contributing to their peak in alternative music in the early 21st century. "el Jaal Neumbeibel que iba en funda de cartón."
**The Suburbs (2010) - Third Studio Album** : This album featured two distinct covers and also included a tape, showcasing a continued evolution in their physical album presentation. It is the final album in their "sacred trilogy" of early works, solidifying their status as modern classics and marking a peak in alternative music at the start of the 21st century. Markus Dravs was one of the producers who worked with the band on this album. "el de Subur que tenías a las dos portadas y cinta."
**Reflektor (2013) - Fourth Studio Album** : This album was presented as a double 180g vinyl set with a beautiful full-silver cover, representing a significant advancement in the band's physical releases. Its production notably included electronic elements, with James Murphy of LCD Soundsystem serving as a co-producer, marking the beginning of the band's trajectory towards more electronic musical territories. "el reflector, ese doble, disco en 180 g con esa portada en full plateado tan bonita."
**Current Album (2017) - Unnamed in Transcript** : This album, released a week prior to the discussion, underwent a complex recording process over eight months in three different countries: the United States, Canada, and France. It features five producers, a new record for the band. The production team includes Markus Dravs, Eric Hegel, an unnamed former member of Pulp, Thomas Bangalter of Daft Punk, and Geoff Barrow, the leader of Portishead, alongside the band members themselves. The album marks a significant shift towards electronic music, incorporating influences from disco, 70s disco-punk, denser electronic bases, and more prominent synthesizers and sounds. Despite these complex influences, it is described as the easiest-listening and most accessible album of their career. Its lyrics are simpler, often repeating verses for minutes, and the musical bases and rhythms are more straightforward. Thematically, it addresses new technologies, internet dominance, and the obsession with social media likes and Instagram, drawing comparisons to themes found in Beyoncé's work. Critical reception has been highly disparate, with Pitchfork awarding it 3/5, NME 5/5, and AllMusic showing an average of 68 with scores ranging from 4-5 to 9-10. The physical release is highly regarded, with a notable feature being that the first edition's cover titles are translated into local languages (e.g., Spanish in Spain, French in France), a practice reminiscent of 70s and early 80s vinyl productions. The CD version includes two covers (Day and Night), and the 7-inch and 12-inch vinyl singles contain unreleased B-sides exclusive to these formats. "Hace una semana salida, el disco con una complejísima grabación, 8 meses de grabación realizada en 3 países distintos, Estados Unidos, Canadá y Francia, y encima con 5 productores, nuevo record para la banda."

## curiosities
**Pre-Release Promotional Campaign (2017)** : For two months leading up to the current album's release, Arcade Fire engaged in an intense and exhausting promotional campaign. This involved creating a vast amount of false information, including fake publications, videos, and even non-existent merchandise websites, all designed to generate anticipation and buzz for the new album. "la banda ha decidido dedicar estos dos meses a crear un montón de información falsa, un montón de publicaciones y videos falsos, incluso webs en merchandising que no existía, para un poco generar expectación."
**Formation of a Promotional Company (2017)** : To manage the promotion for their current album, the band made the unusual decision to create an entirely new company solely for this purpose. This highlights the extensive and dedicated nature of their marketing strategy for the release. "la banda decidió crear una empresa solo para realizar la promoción de este nuevo álbum."
**Unannounced Single Release at Primavera Sound (2017)** : On June 31st (likely a misstated date for late June or early July), a 12-inch vinyl single from the band's current album suddenly appeared for sale at a merchandise stand during the Primavera Sound festival. This occurred without any official announcement or prior notice, surprising festival-goers and signaling the imminent launch of the album. "el 31 de junio, en pleno festival primavera, Saon en el stand en merchandising, aparecía de repente a la venta, el single en vinilo de 12 pulgadas de la banda, sin hacer un lanzamiento oficial."
**Evolution Towards Electronic Sound** : Following their initial "sacred trilogy" of alternative rock albums, Arcade Fire began a deliberate evolution towards a more electronic sound, a path that became evident with their album *Reflektor* and is further pronounced in their current album. This shift is seen as a logical progression, avoiding the band becoming a parody of its earlier work. "prosigue el camino de la banda hacia terrenos más electrónicos o el camino hacia donde crea que les está llevando el camino que empezaron con el reflector."
**Divergent Critical Reception (2017 Album)** : The current album has received widely disparate reviews from major music publications. Pitchfork notably rated it 3 out of 5, while NME gave it a perfect 5 out of 5. AllMusic's average score stands around 68, compiled from individual ratings ranging from 4-5 to 9-10, making individual opinions on the album highly credible due to the industry's divided stance. "Tenemos críticas como pitch fork que le da un 3 sobre 5 e incluso tenemos a NMI que le da un 5 sobre 5."
**Shift in Lyrical and Musical Complexity (2017 Album)** : The band's current album marks a significant departure from the intricate, baroque lyrics and complex instrumentation that defined their earlier work, especially for die-hard fans. The new album features simpler, often repetitive lyrics and more straightforward musical bases and rhythms, which some loyal fans may find disappointing while others might appreciate its accessibility. "los fans de la banda más aferrimos, que se habían enamorado Dark and Fire por esas letras tan rebuscadas, tan barrocas, tan complicadas... eso todo se parece que se ha ido para no volver."
**Localized Album Titles on Physical Releases (2017 Album)** : For the first edition of their current album, Arcade Fire implemented a unique strategy of translating the album titles on the physical cover art into local languages for different countries, such as Spanish in Spain and French in France. This initiative revives a practice common in 1970s and early 1980s vinyl production, which largely disappeared due to globalization in the mid-1980s, and is highly appreciated for its "local instinct." "la banda ha decidido traducir la primera edición a muchos idiomas en muchos países."

# genre - Electronic Music

## curiosities
**Symbiotic Creative Contribution** : A major source of creativity in contemporary music stems from the subtle and smooth symbiosis between alternative music and electronic music. "esta nueva symbiosis entre la música alternativa y la música electrónica, de una manera muy sutila y muy suave, es lo que nos están dando un gran aporte creativo y en día gran parte de la música."

# curiosity

**Historical Practice of Localized Album Titles** : During the 1970s and early 1980s, it was common practice for vinyl productions to feature album titles translated into local languages on their covers, a reflection of localized production. However, this trend largely disappeared from the mid-1980s onwards due to globalization, which led to standardized physical releases across different regions. "en su día durante los 70 y hasta primeras de los 80s hacia mucho porque las producciones de vinilos están locales, pero luego a partir de la mitad de los 80s, un poco la globalización se fue cargando todo esto."
