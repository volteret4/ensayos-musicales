# artist - David Bowie

## genres
World Music

## albums
**Low (1977) - Berlin Trilogy Part 1** : This album was one of the first two discs released by David Bowie as part of his "Berlin Trilogy," preceding the more complicated and controversial *Lodger*. "Según habíamos ido en las dos primeras partes, nos habíamos quedado más o menos a mitad de 1978."
**Heroes (1977) - Berlin Trilogy Part 2** : This album was one of the first two discs released by David Bowie as part of his "Berlin Trilogy." Its critical reception was significantly better than that of *Lodger*. "estuvo muy lejos de las críticas que había tenido con Giro, si que había tenido con Lodger." (Note: The transcript mentions "Giro" and "Lodger" for comparison, but *Heroes* is the intended comparison here as a highly acclaimed album from the trilogy).
**Lodger (1979) - Berlin Trilogy Part 3** : Recorded starting in mid-1978, this album is described as the most complicated, controversial, and doubt-inducing of the Berlin Trilogy. It was not recorded in Berlin but in the United States, Switzerland, and some parts in Paris. Critical reception was mixed; it was often misunderstood, didn't receive excellent reviews, and was seen as the weakest of the trilogy upon release, with some critics not even considering it part of the trilogy. It features significant influences from World Music and Brian Eno, but fewer Krautrock and German music influences than previous albums, lacking their complex instrumental parts. Despite initial reception, *Lodger* gained significant charisma, followers, and fans over the years, becoming a point of inflection in the early 1990s as it contained the "germ" of much of Bowie's sound in the 1980s and 1990s. "Lodger que además, pues, es un disco complicado, es un disco complejo, a veces es un tanto polémico."

## songs
**Boys Keep Swinging (1979)** : This song from the *Lodger* album was later referenced as an important influence by Damon Albarn of Blur, leading to Blur being accused of plagiarism for one of their songs. "acabó invason un playtopor plagio de una de las canciones de Blur que estaba acusada de haber plagado por Skipshing in, que es una de las canciones del Lodger." (Note: "Skipshing in" is inferred to be "Boys Keep Swinging" based on context).
**China Girl (1983) - David Bowie's Version** : This song, originally from Iggy Pop's first solo album *The Idiot*, was covered by David Bowie. Bowie's version provided substantial royalties to Iggy Pop, allowing him to live off the money for an extended period due to his financial difficulties. "prácticamente Iguipop estuvo viviendo de ese dinero durante mucho tiempo porque estaba teniendo problemas económicos."

## curiosities
**Rehabilitation and Return to Confidence (Mid-1978)** : By mid-1978, David Bowie had completely rehabilitated, regaining much self-confidence after overcoming previous struggles. "A mitad de 1978, David Bowie ya estaba completamente rehabilitado, era una persona que había ganado otra vez mucha confianza en sí misma."
**Departure from Berlin (Early 1978)** : From early 1978 onwards, David Bowie no longer spent much time living or visiting Berlin, marking a physical and later inspirational detachment from the city. "Desde casi primeros es el 78 de David Bowie ya no pasaba mucho tiempo en Berlín y va a haber visita pasaba poco tiempo."
**Busy and Touring During Lodger Recordings** : During the recording of *Lodger*, David Bowie was constantly busy and actively touring, making the recording process somewhat diffuse and complex. "Todo un poco difuso, David Bowie ya no paraba, ya estaba haciendo giras."
**Deteriorated Relationship with Brian Eno (Late 1970s)** : The collaborative tandem between David Bowie and Brian Eno was exhausted, and their personal relationship deteriorated during the complex creative and recording process of *Lodger*. This led to them eventually pursuing different paths. "la relación personal entre David Bowie y Brian Hennon queda un tanto deteriorada a nivel personal."
**Unilateral Decisions and Returning Grandeur (Late 1970s)** : During the recording of *Lodger*, David Bowie made certain unilateral decisions, reflecting a returning sense of grandeur and a desire to create a very complex and ambitious project. This contrasted with Brian Eno's typically humble and discreet approach. "ciertas decisiones un y laterales de David Bowie durante los procesos de grabación, también se hace referencia a ciertos aire, es ya un tanto de la grandeza que volvía a tener David Bowie y que quería hacer de lo que era un proyecto muy complejo y muy ambicioso."
**Naming the "Berlin Trilogy"** : It was David Bowie himself who coined the term "Berlin Trilogy" during interviews for the release of *Lodger*, rather than critics or biographers. This was done to create a connection between *Lodger* and his two previous albums, despite significant differences. "es el propio David Bowie, el que llama la trilogía de Berlín a la trilogía de Berlín durante las entrevistas del lanzamiento del Lodger."
**Disconnection from Berlin Inspiration** : Although David Bowie would later buy a house in Berlin, he became completely disconnected from Berlin as a source of inspiration after the *Lodger* period, affecting his creative direction. "quedaría completamente desconectado de Berling. No tanto físicamente, sino también en base a su inspiración, se desconectó totalmente de Berling."
**The 1980s: A Difficult Decade** : The 1980s, beginning officially after *Lodger*, were not an easy or the best decade for David Bowie's career, despite producing major hits. He experienced creative and stylistic struggles and underwent aggressive changes in his image. "Los 80 la verdad es que no fueron una de cada fácil para David Bowie, nos dejó grandes éxitos, nos dejó grandes hits, pero no fue ni la mejor ni la más fácil que etapa de su carrera."
**Continued Support for Iggy Pop** : Despite their paths diverging after the Berlin Trilogy, David Bowie consistently provided support to Iggy Pop, most notably through royalties from his cover of "China Girl," which helped Iggy Pop financially. He also collaborated on Iggy Pop's second album. "el propio David Bowie nunca habría terminado de darle cierto apoyo a Iguipop en ese sentido."

# artist - Brian Eno

## member of
David Bowie (collaborator on Berlin Trilogy)
Talking Heads (producer)

## genres
World Music
Minimal Music

## albums
**Music for Airports (1978)** : Released in 1978, this album is considered one of two masterpieces by Brian Eno that possibly marked the official beginning of minimal music. It comprised recordings made during the Berlin Trilogy period. "salían esas dos obras maestras, que dan inicio oficial a la música minimal posiblemente, que son el Music For Air por si el Music For Films."
**Music for Films (1978)** : Released around 1978-1979, this album is regarded as one of Brian Eno's masterpieces that potentially initiated minimal music. It featured recordings from his time collaborating on the Berlin Trilogy. "el Music For Air por si el Music For Films y luego haría muchísimos proyectos."

## curiosities
**Interest in World Music (Late 1970s / Early 1980s)** : From the late 1970s into the early 1980s, Brian Eno developed a strong interest in World Music, which he also explored in his own productions and which significantly influenced David Bowie's *Lodger* album. "a finales de los 70 primeros, de los 80, una de las cosas en las que se quisieron meter fue en el World Music."
**Personality Contrasting Bowie (Late 1970s)** : Brian Eno's personality was often humble and discreet throughout his career, which sometimes contrasted with David Bowie's returning grandeur and ambitious project ideas during the recording of *Lodger*. "ese aire, pues a veces están humilde y tan discreto que solía tener, ha tenido Brian Hennon toda su carrera."
**Deteriorated Relationship with David Bowie (Late 1970s)** : The personal relationship between Brian Eno and David Bowie deteriorated during the complex creative and recording process of *Lodger*, eventually leading them to pursue separate musical paths. "la relación personal entre David Bowie y Brian Hennon queda un tanto deteriorada a nivel personal."
**Post-Berlin Trilogy Prolificacy (1978-1980s)** : After the Berlin Trilogy, Brian Eno returned to Düsseldorf and became extremely prolific between 1978 and the 1980s, deepening his interest in World Music and producing many important artists, including Talking Heads. "La rafiro Braianno entre el 78 y el 80 y pico hizo un montón de cosas, aparte de esos discos, así interesó también muchísimo por el World Music."

# artist - Iggy Pop

## member of
David Bowie's Second Solo Album (collaborator)

## albums
**The Idiot (1977) - First Solo Album** : Released during the Berlin Trilogy period, this album is Iggy Pop's first solo record and is described as an authentic masterpiece. It features the original version of the song "China Girl." "Iguipop durante la trilogía de Berlingo había sacado el Idiot, su primer disconsolitario que es una auténtica obra maestra."
**Second Solo Album (Post-The Idiot)** : Shortly after *The Idiot*, Iggy Pop released a second solo album. This album utilized some recordings from the Berlin Trilogy period, with a significant portion recorded in Geneva, the United States, and Paris with David Bowie's continued help. It achieved considerable success and received very good reviews. "el segundo disco de Iguipop también tuvo bastante éxito, tuvo muy buenas críticas, fue un gran disco."

## songs
**China Girl (1977)** : This song is an original track from Iggy Pop's first solo album, *The Idiot*. David Bowie later covered it, and the royalties from Bowie's version provided crucial financial support to Iggy Pop during a difficult economic period. "China Girl originariamente es una canción del primer disco de Iguipop en solitario."

## curiosities
**Struggles with Rehabilitation (Late 1970s - Early 1980s)** : Unlike David Bowie, Iggy Pop never fully rehabilitated from drug use during this period, despite significantly reducing his consumption. His full rehabilitation came much later. "Iguipop en contra de lo que le pasó a David Bowie, nunca se llegó a rehabilitar del todo, consiguió descender bastante su consumo de drogas, pero nunca llegó a conseguir rehabilitarse como consiguió rehabilitarse David Bowie."
**Career Decline (Early 1980s)** : After his successful second solo album, Iggy Pop entered a steep decline from approximately 1982 to 1985. This period, affectionately remembered by some fans as "Iggy Pop at rock bottom," was marked by continued drug problems, complex albums that received terrible reviews, and disastrous concerts. "Iguipop empezaría una cuesta bajo sin frenos. ... sus conciertos eran un desastre."
**Financial Difficulties and Bowie's Support (Early 1980s)** : Iggy Pop faced significant economic problems during his early 1980s decline. David Bowie's cover of "China Girl" provided substantial royalties, which effectively sustained Iggy Pop financially for a long time, demonstrating Bowie's continued support. "prácticamente Iguipop estuvo viviendo de ese dinero durante mucho tiempo porque estaba teniendo problemas económicos."
**Eventual Recovery and Respect (Later Years)** : Despite many difficulties and a slow, progressive process, Iggy Pop eventually overcame his struggles, gaining charisma and becoming the respected artist he is today. "Con el tiempo y muy poco a poco, muy progresivamente y con muchas dificultades, Iguipop acababa saliendo adelante, acaba ganando su narizma, acaba convertiendo su rendísimo Iguipop que tenemos hoy en día."

# genre - World Music

## curiosities
**Official Birth in Western Music** : The late 1970s and early 1980s saw a series of artists, including Brian Eno, Peter Gabriel, and David Bowie, become involved in the official birth of World Music within Western music. "teníamos a una serie de artistas que se estaban metiendo en el nacimiento oficial del World Music en la música occidental."
**Influence on David Bowie's Lodger** : David Bowie's album *Lodger* is noted for having many influences from World Music, which would become an important influence for Bowie throughout the 1980s and 1990s. "Lodger tiene muchas influencias de lo que es el World Music que sería una importante influencia para Bowie durante los 80 y 90."

# genre - Britpop

## curiosities
**Lodger's Delayed Influence** : David Bowie's *Lodger* became an important influence for the Britpop movement and associated "match" (Madchester-like) sound from the late 1980s to the mid-1990s, with artists like Noel Gallagher and Brett Anderson referencing it. This influence emerged with a delay of 12-15 years after its release. "este Alban se convirtió en una importante influencia del pop británico, casi desde ese finales de los 80 a mediados de los 90 fue una pieza importante para definir el pop británico."

# curiosity

**The Berlin Trilogy - Poor Documentation** : The period covering David Bowie's Berlin Trilogy (albums *Low*, *Heroes*, *Lodger*) is generally not well-documented in written form, photographs, or videos. The *Lodger* period, in particular, may be the least documented part of this era, leading to strange interpretations and very little available information. "Ya la trilogía de Berlín es una etapa de David Bowie que no está excesivamente documentada, ni en documento escrito, ni en fotografías, ni en vídeos."
