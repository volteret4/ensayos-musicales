# instrument - Turntable

## curiosities
**Types of Turntables - Technical** : These turntables are often designed for DJing, which makes them "incredibly resistant and durable." Their production continued uninterrupted from the mid-90s until approximately ten years ago, a period when many other turntable brands paused manufacturing. They are highly customizable, with parts that can be easily changed or improved, and replacement parts are simple to find due to their standardized design. They typically include essential features such as anti-skating, a ground connection, a counterweight, and a height regulator, even at a price point of around 200 euros. "Son tocadiscos increíblemente resistentes y duraderos."
**Types of Turntables - Desktop (Nice looking)** : While aesthetically pleasing, many desktop turntables lack critical technical features such as a counterweight. Users often encounter significant difficulty in finding compatible replacement needles, as these models are frequently only compatible with a very limited range of non-standardized needles. "te vas a encontrar muchos tocadiscos de 250 euros de sobre mesa que no tengan ni siquiera un contrapeso."
**Types of Turntables - All-in-One Integrated** : These turntables are practical due to their minimal space requirements and low initial cost. However, they are characterized by "precarious" sound quality and are not considered beneficial for the long-term care and preservation of vinyl records. "la calidad de sonido es un tanto precaria y la segunda es que no son especialmente buenos para el cuidado del vinilo."
**Types of Turntables - Vintage/Old** : Purchasing vintage turntables demands considerable expertise, as buyers must meticulously ensure they have been well-restored. These older units commonly present problems related to wear and tear due to their age and previous use. "hay que saber muy bien lo que estás comprando Hay que asegurar si de que están bien restaurados y suelen tener muchos problemas de desgaste, de haberías."
**Market History - Mid-1990s to Mid-2010s** : During this specific period, spanning from the mid-90s to about ten years ago, most turntable manufacturers significantly reduced or halted their production. In contrast, technical turntables continued to be produced without interruption, fueled by a consistent demand from enthusiasts of electronic music, scratching, and DJing, which allowed brands like Technics and Panasonic to continue developing and improving their motors and machines. "desde en mitad de los 90 hasta hace más o menos 10 años A que los tocadiscos prácticamente dejaron de fabricarse por otro lado los tocadiscos técnicos nunca pararon su producción."
**Buying Guide - New Low Budget (under 150 euros)** : It is quite difficult to acquire a decent new turntable for less than 150 euros. The basic model from Audio-Technica is singled out as the best cheap option, performing effectively for its price despite lacking advanced features like a counterweight or anti-skating. "el mejor tocadiscos barato que se puede comprar es el de audio técnica."
**Buying Guide - New Mid Budget (around 250 euros)** : The Audio-Technica LP120 is widely recommended as the best value-for-money turntable available on the market, priced around 250 euros. It is noted for offering "incredible quality" at its adjusted price point. "el audio técnica LP120 Es cuesta unos 250 euros y tiene una relación calidad precio increíble."
**Buying Guide - Second-hand Mid Budget (around 100-120 euros)** : For individuals with a budget of 150 euros, the most advisable approach is to purchase a medium-range second-hand turntable. These models, which typically retail new for around 200 euros, can be found for significantly less, between 100 and 120 euros, on the second-hand market. Brands such as Audio-Technica, Reloop, Gemini, Stanton, America Audio, and "Aquí llama" (e.g., JT 1200 models) are recognized for producing robust and reliable turntables that maintain their functionality over many years. "comprarme un tocadiscos de gama media de segunda mano que tenemos como gama media pues tenemos muchos tocadiscos que están en torno a los 200 euros que de segunda mano pueden costar unos 100 en 110 euros."
**Buying Guide - Second-hand with Upgrade (around 160 euros total)** : A recommended strategy involves purchasing a medium-range second-hand turntable for 100-120 euros and then upgrading its capsule to a superior model, such as one from Ortofon or Audio-Technica, and possibly replacing the needle. This combined investment, totaling approximately 160 euros, results in a high-quality turntable with significantly enhanced sound performance due to the improved capsule. "Cambiarlo una cápsula una un poco mejor una ortofón una audio técnica y se hace falta cambiarle la aguja con todo junto nos estaríamos yendo unos 160 euros y tendríamos un tocadiscos muy bueno pero sobre todo con una cápsula muy buena de sonido."

## curiosities
**Diamond Needles** : The speaker specifies that both of his "Aquí llama JT 1200" turntables are fitted with diamond needles. This indicates a preference for this type of high-quality needle, especially for the demanding practice of scratching. "Los dos tienen sus agujas de diamante."

Users may find themselves embarking on a "horrible process" of searching for suitable needles, as these turntables are frequently compatible with only a very limited number of non-standard options. "Puedes acabarte enfrascado un horrible proceso de búsqueda de agujas porque el tocadiscos es sobre mesa que tiene, sencillamente no es compatible con muchas agujas estandar y a veces solo son compatibles con muy pocas agujas."
