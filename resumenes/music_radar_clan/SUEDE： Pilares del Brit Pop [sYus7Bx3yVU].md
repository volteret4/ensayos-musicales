## albums
**Suede (1993) - Debut Album** : Released in March 1993, this album immediately "collapsed" the United Kingdom music scene, entering the charts directly at number 1 and appearing on the covers of all UK music magazines. It achieved massive success across Europe and the United States with incredible sales, and due to a significant "hunger for musical novelties" in the UK, many music writers consider it one of the most important releases in British history. Unlike Blur's debut, Suede's first album garnered immense critical acclaim, solidifying their reputation as "the darling of British critics in 1993." It also won the Mercury Prize that year. The album's release was preceded by a historic cover on SELECT magazine in April 1993, which some texts claim officially marked the beginning of Britpop. "colapsa al reino unido."
**Dog Man Star (1994) - Second Revolution** : Released rapidly after their debut, this album represented a "second revolution" for the band, featuring more lineup changes. It was described as a "completely revolutionary act" because Suede intentionally defied the formula that brought them initial success, opting for a much darker, deeper, and intimate sound with "very minority and sometimes scandalous lyrics." Despite not achieving the sales peak of its predecessor, it was critically adored, considered "one of the master albums of the 90s." The band aimed to avoid being a "mass consumption object," aspiring to the artistic depth of David Bowie's Berlin Trilogy rather than the mass appeal of Ziggy Stardust. The recording process was complicated by the mysterious departure of Bernard Butler, a key musical figure from their first album, and issues with producer Ed Buller. The band remained very proud of the result, which is now considered a masterpiece. "hacen un salto enormemente arrisgado y se revelan contra todo lo que en principio les iba a dar éxito."
**Coming Up (1996) - Britpop Masterpiece** : Released in September 1996, this album marked a significant shift with a new, younger lineup including guitarist Richard Oakes and keyboardist Neil Codling, the band's first ever keyboardist, bringing fresh and agile ideas. While perhaps less profound than *Dog Man Star*, Brett Anderson had achieved his artistic goals with the previous album and sought something fresher. The album masterfully blended Britpop with classic glam elements reminiscent of David Bowie, featuring hit songs like "Filmstar" and "Beautiful Ones." It achieved immense commercial success, selling "like crazy," and is recognized as "one of the great masterpieces of the Britpop movement," arriving somewhat later in the era. "es una de las grandes obras, maestra es el movimiento Britpop."
**Head Music (1999) - Late Britpop Effort** : Released in 1999, this album attempted to prolong the Britpop era at a time when the genre was considered "dead," as the music world had moved on. Despite being a "very good album," its late release meant it struggled to find its place; it would have been a "great album in 1997, not in 1999." The band found itself "stuck" in the Britpop legacy. During this period, Brett Anderson faced severe heroin addiction, which complicated work and tours, alongside ongoing lineup changes and internal discord, with Anderson having a reputation as a "despot" within the band. "intentaba alargar un poco la estela de Britpop cuando ya el Britpop estaba muerto."
**New Morning (2002)** : This album marked Suede's departure from the Britpop sound, though it still retained "elements of Britpop." However, it failed to impress critics. Following this release, the band eventually dissolved before reuniting years later. "ya no era Britpop, pero seguía teniendo muchos jodetas de Britpop que tampoco convencia la crítica."

## curiosities
**Early Relevance (1991)** : By 1991, two years before their debut album, Suede was already an "important and relevant" band in the UK, amidst a significant "hunger for musical novelties" alongside the emergence of Blur. They gained significant magazine exposure with only a couple of singles of "small repercussion." "Swade ya era una banda importante y relevante dentro del Reino Unido."
**Critical Acclaim Before Debut** : Suede achieved the rare feat of being featured on magazine covers (such as Melody Maker in April 1991, with the headline "The Best New Band in Britain") and being invited to perform at awards ceremonies before they had even released a single album. Esteemed figures like Morrissey and Siouxsie referenced Suede's potential in interviews in 1991. They performed as an "emerging band" at the Express music awards in January 1991. "llegó a ser portadas de revistas musicales en el Reino Unido sin ni siquiera haber tenido un solo disco."
**Justine Frischmann's Departure and Relationships** : The band initially formed with Brett Anderson and Justine Frischmann. Frischmann later left Suede to form Elastica, another significant Britpop band. This was compounded by her becoming romantically involved with Damon Albarn (Blur) after her relationship with Brett Anderson ended, creating considerable tabloid fodder. "Justin Fisherman dejaría Bret Anderson para ser pareja de Damon Alband."
**Label Contract Confusion** : Suede signed a record contract with the independent label Nude Records, remaining with them until the label eventually closed. However, there was often confusion because Sony had a distribution agreement with Nude Records for Suede's material, as the band's potential for global sales was deemed too large for an independent label to handle alone. Sony and Geffen (described as a major player in the grunge movement) both attempted to sign Suede directly but were unsuccessful, as Suede's rights and freedoms lay solely with Nude. "Sony tenía un acuerdo de distribución con New Records para el material de Swade."
**Bernard Butler's Mysterious Exit** : During the recording of *Dog Man Star*, Bernard Butler, a key musical contributor to the success of Suede's first album, abruptly abandoned the band. His departure was unusual, with his belongings reportedly found outside the studio and him being denied entry, indicating a sudden and acrimonious split. "Bernard Butler... acababa abandonando la banda, además de esa manera tan rara."
**Brett Anderson's Reputation and Struggles** : Following *Dog Man Star*, Suede faced a challenging period marked by Brett Anderson's severe heroin addiction, which significantly complicated touring and creative work. The band experienced continued lineup changes and internal strife, with Anderson gaining a reputation as a "despot" within the group. "Bret Anderson siempre tuvo fama de ser un auténtico les potado dentro de la banda."
**Slow Exit from Britpop** : Suede struggled significantly to transition out of the Britpop movement, finding themselves "stuck" as the genre waned. Their later albums, such as *Head Music* (1999), were critically good but released too late to capitalize on the Britpop trend, while *New Morning* (2002), though not Britpop, still contained elements that failed to convince critics, illustrating their slow and difficult adaptation to a post-Britpop landscape. "su salida de Britpop fue muy lenta, muy tardy, ahí les costó muchísimo."

# genre - Britpop

## curiosities
**Official Start (April 1993)** : Some British texts and articles suggest that the Britpop movement officially began with Suede's cover story in SELECT magazine in April 1993, coinciding with the release of their debut album. "el ripop empiezo oficialmente con la portada de abril de 1993 en la revista SELECT."
**"Hunger for Musical Novelties" (Early 90s)** : The early 1990s in the United Kingdom experienced a significant "hunger for musical novelties," creating a fertile ground for new bands like Suede and Blur to emerge and gain rapid relevance even before releasing their first albums. This context contributed to the immense impact of Suede's debut. "había un grandísimo hambre de novedades musicales dentro del Reino Unido."

# label - Nude Records

## curiosities
**Suede's Core Label** : Nude Records was the independent label that signed Suede. All of Suede's record contracts, rights, and creative freedoms were held with Nude Records until the label eventually closed. "Soy firmos contratos discórafico con New Records, y siempre estuvo con New Records hasta que New Records acabó cerrando."
**Distribution Deal with Sony** : Despite Suede being signed to Nude Records, Sony had a distribution agreement for Suede's material. This arrangement was put in place because Suede's potential sales and global reach were considered too substantial for an independent label like Nude to manage entirely on its own. "Sony tenía un acuerdo de distribución con New Records para el material de Swade."

# label - Sony

## curiosities
**Distribution Partner for Suede** : Sony had a distribution agreement with Nude Records for Suede's musical output. This was deemed necessary as Suede's projected impact and sales in the global market were too significant for an independent label to handle single-handedly. "Sony distribuía el material."
**Attempted Suede Signing** : Sony attempted to sign Suede away from Nude Records, but these efforts were unsuccessful, as Suede maintained their contract and rights with Nude. "la propia discórafica Sony intentó que cambiaran de New Records a Sony, que no lo consiguieron."
