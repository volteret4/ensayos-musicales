## albums
**Pablo Honey** : This album was largely a "prefabricated discographic product" created by EMI, with the band lacking significant experience at the time to fully implement their own artistic vision. The band members "no tenían mucha experiencia, entonces como que no pudieron hacer todo lo que le subiese gustado."
**The Bends (1995) - Landmark Album** : The band often considers this their true first album, feeling *Pablo Honey* was a manufactured product. Recording began with advanced demo ideas, but the band disliked the initial work, leading to multiple pauses in production for creative breaks, rethinking sounds, and opening for other bands. EMI pressured the band for a hit, similar to "Creep," and eventually had the final mix remixed in the United States by two different engineers for a "más guitarrero, un pelo y más americano" sound, without the band's involvement. The result was bittersweet for all involved, with chart performance not incredible, UK reviews good but not stellar, and US reviews mixed to mediocre. Despite this, it gained significant sales over many years and saw its "puncha" (impact) grow with the release of its five singles. Its release early in 1995 led to a period of stabilization for the band, reducing commercial pressure and allowing them to gain confidence to pursue different sounds. Over the years, critical re-evaluation has given it much charisma, making it one of Radiohead's best-selling albums (possibly their second-best) and the most referenced by mainstream artists as an influence. It is often recommended as the perfect introductory album to Radiohead's sound, being "experimental, no, lo justo como para poderla adentrarse en lo que será el sonido de Radiohead y es como una versión un poco descafeinada de lo que vendría más adelante."
**OK Computer (1997) - Revolutionary Album** : Recording for this album began in March 1995, approximately one year after the release of *The Bends*. It is considered one of the "grandes revoluciones musicales" of the 20th century, benefiting from the confidence the band gained from *The Bends* to explore sounds beyond the style of "Creep." "empezaría a grabarse lo que sería una de las grandes revoluciones musicales que hemos tenido del siglo XX, en parte con la gran confianza que habían conseguido de ver que no necesariamente tenían que seguir intentando seguir la estilada de creep."
**Kid A (2000)** : This album is recognized as being "realmente influyente" within music. "quiday fue realmente influyente."

## songs
**Creep (1992)** : The immense hype surrounding this song led the band to dislike both the track itself and the *Pablo Honey* album. During the *Pablo Honey* tour, the band was sometimes forced to play "Creep" up to three times per concert, contributing to their exhaustion and frustration. "llegaban a veces a cantar tres veces la canción de Creep en un concerto."
**My Iron Lung (1994)** : This single or EP was released considerably before the final *The Bends* album, as a result of EMI's doubts and pressure for hits. Its initial reception was "no era especialmente buena."

## curiosities
**Band's Discontent with Pablo Honey** : Radiohead views *Pablo Honey* as less of a true Radiohead album due to its nature as a manufactured product by EMI and their own lack of experience during its creation. They were not pleased with the outcome, especially after the immense popularity of "Creep" led them to "odiar el álbum y de recansión."
**Tom Yorke's Annoyance with Pablo Honey Autographs** : Many fans deliberately seek out Tom Yorke to sign copies of *Pablo Honey* during concerts or appearances, specifically because it is known to greatly annoy him. Photos circulating online show "Tom York con cara de muerto cada vez que alguien le enseña un álbum del Pablo Jonei para que se lo firme."
**Exhausting Tours and Creative Depression** : The extensive, long, and frustrating tour for *Pablo Honey*, which involved constantly performing "Creep," led to considerable exhaustion and the first major creative depression for Tom Yorke. This period saw the band caught between a desire for new creative directions and the pressure to maintain the commercial success they had achieved. "estará la primera gran depresión que tenía Tom Jorka a nivel creativo y la banda se junta pues con un sentimiento bastante encontrado entre querer hacer algo nuevo, querer ir más allá y por el otro lado la presión de mantener un poco el hilo que les había dado éxito."
**Reluctance to Be the "Biggest Band"** : The band explicitly stated they never aimed to be the "biggest band in the world," unlike groups such as U2 or R.E.M., even though they wanted to sell records. "we've never wanted to be the biggest band in the world we've obviously wanted to sell records."
**Evolution of Sound During The Bends Recording** : Many characteristic elements that would define Radiohead's sound for years, such as Jonny Greenwood's guitar sound, Tom Yorke's critical lyrics, and his "cat-like" singing style, developed and were refined during the recording process of *The Bends*, rather than being part of the initial album concept. "muchas de las cosas que salen en el álbum de Benz que irían definiendo el sonido de Radiohead por muchos años se fueron gestando no en la idea inicial del disco sino durante la grabación del disco."

## albums
**Blur (1997) - Self-Titled** : The recording of this self-titled album by Blur was influenced by Radiohead's *The Bends*. Damon Albarn cited *The Bends* as a reason for Blur to abandon the classic Britpop style and explore new sounds. "decía que una de las razones que les empujó abandonar la estela clásica del Britpop era precisamente el álbum de Benz de Radiohead que les había hecho un poco darse cuenta de que había que ir a buscar sonidos nuevos."

# genre - Britpop

## curiosities
**The Bends' Influence on Britpop's Evolution** : Radiohead's album *The Bends*, released in the mid-1990s as the golden era of Britpop was ending, played a curious role in pushing the genre towards more alternative or experimental sounds. Damon Albarn of Blur specifically cited *The Bends* as influencing his band to abandon classic Britpop for new sounds on their 1997 self-titled album. "posiblemente fue uno de los álbumes que ayudó al Britpop a empujarlo hacia otros nuevos lugares un poco más alternativos o más experimentales."

# label - EMI Records

## curiosities
**Fabrication of Pablo Honey** : EMI had a very direct commercial vision for Radiohead's debut album *Pablo Honey*, aiming to create an "American sound" within the United Kingdom. The label essentially "manufactured" much of the album, as the band lacked experience at the time. "EMI tenía como una visión comercial muy directa de intentar hacer como una especie de sonida americano dentro del reino unido."
**Pressure and Remixing for The Bends** : EMI exerted constant pressure on Radiohead during the creation of *The Bends* to "fabricar el hit" following the success of "Creep," and was not entirely satisfied with the final result because it failed to generate a second similar hype. This led EMI to take the final mix of *The Bends* to the United States for remixing by two different engineers to achieve a "más guitarrero, un pelo y más americano" sound, notably without the band's involvement in this final stage. "la discográfica los presionó mucho y la discográfica tampoco quedaba contenta del todo porque por un lado conseguían encaminar el disco hacia un sonido más comercial que pero que seguían sin haber podido fabricar un segundo hype con el que había sido creep."

# member - Nigel Godrich

## member of
Radiohead

## curiosities
**Role on The Bends vs. Later Career** : Nigel Godrich worked as a sound engineer on Radiohead's album *The Bends*, but he was not the true producer of the record. He would later become the producer for the majority of Radiohead's subsequent career, defining their sound. "aunque sí trabajan ahí el Godrich no sería el productor, el ingeniero de sonido más adelante si el productor del resto a carrera de Radiohead."
