## albums
**Debut Album - Lack of Production (Year not specified)** : Often considered a masterpiece by critics, though some find it weak. The band's excessive creative freedom, combined with their inexperience, led to a lack of proper production. The album had two "producers" who were actually technical staff from Domino Records, and they merely executed the band's demands. This approach resulted in a fresh sound but introduced small technical production errors, stemming from the band's strong desire to avoid industry influence. "El primer disco de los artichmonks es posiblemente una obra maestra, al margen de que alguna gente lo considera un poco flojo."
**AM - Professional Production (Year not specified)** : This album, described as their latest at the time of discussion, marked the first instance the band worked with a major professional producer, Josh Homme, known for his work with Queens of the Stone Age. This collaboration elevated Arctic Monkeys' music to an unprecedented level of quality, making "AM" a rock masterpiece. The professional production helped to refine many of the subtle production flaws that the band had made in previous albums by deliberately avoiding industry influence. "El elyem es una obra maestra con un nivel de calidad de rock increíble y en parte gracias a que pule muchos de esos pequeños efectos de producción, que la banda lejos de querer influenciarse por la industria había ido cometiendo en sus iscos anteriores."

## curiosities
**Origin Story - Bored Sheffield Students** : The band's story began with four young students from Sheffield who, out of boredom and being poor students, started playing music because there was nothing more interesting to do. They quickly rose to fame through the internet and became a significant reference point in the music industry. "cuatro chavales de Sheffield, que se aburrían en amalos estudiantes, tocaban porque no tenían otra cosa más interesante que hacer y un buen día interno se hace famosos y se convierten en un referente de la industria de la música."
**Demo Distribution Strategy** : Arctic Monkeys adopted a distinctive approach to gaining popularity: instead of selling their home-made CD demos at small concerts, they gave them away for free. They understood that the added value came from people knowing their songs rather than purchasing their material, a strategy that significantly boosted their popularity. "ellos no intentaban vender sus pequeños discos en los conciertos sino que lo regalaban. Sabían que el valor añadido precisamente era que la gente supiese las canciones."
**Fan-Driven Internet Popularity** : The band's audience played a crucial role in their rise to fame by actively sharing the physical copies of the demos they received at concerts. Fans would upload these recordings to the internet, and file sharing greatly contributed to their widespread recognition. "era su público el que compartía en mano esas copias de sus pequeños discos que regalaban en los conciertos, los subía internet, el intercambio de archivos los ayudó en Monton."
**Unbeknownst MySpace Profile** : MySpace was instrumental in popularizing Arctic Monkeys, but intriguingly, the band's official MySpace profile was not created by them. Two fans from Sheffield, who were deeply fond of the band, set up the page without the band's knowledge. This detail was later verified as completely true. "el Mayespace Social de los Artigmonkeys no era de los Artigmonkeys. Era de dos chavales también de Sheffield que eran muy fans de la banda y que hicieron el Mayespace de ellos. Pero ellos no sabían ni que tenían un Mayespace."
**Signing with Domino Records - Creative Freedom Clause** : Arctic Monkeys eventually signed with Domino Records, a label also associated with Franz Ferdinand and significant in the classic rock revival. Despite fan disappointment, this move was inevitable for releasing material in 2005-2006. Notably, their contract included a clause guaranteeing extreme creative freedom, even allowing them the liberty to never record an album, a detail literally written into the agreement. "afirmaron en el contrato que tenían que ser tan libres que podían ser hasta ser libres de no llegar nunca a grabar ningún disco. Y esto físicamente lo llegaron a grabar en el contrato."
**Fan Disappointment and "Selling Out" Accusations** : Many fans felt disappointed by the band's decision to sign with a record label, perceiving it as "selling out" or becoming too involved with the mainstream music industry. They were criticized for attending awards ceremonies like the Brit Awards and participating in the "fanfare" of the music industry. "mucha gente que los ha atachado de vendidos o que han acabado llendo a recoger premios, actuando los brita wards un poco metidos en todo ese fan anduleo de la industria discográfica."
**Intermediate Path in the Music Industry** : Arctic Monkeys are seen as a band stuck on an intermediate path, balancing complete independence from the industry with the necessity of relying on it for their work. This position led to disappointment among hardcore fans who expected total independence, while the industry perhaps anticipated greater compliance. "se han quedado en ese camino intermedio, que a veces es difícil de situar y difícil definir."
**Right Music, Right Time** : A significant factor in Arctic Monkeys' success was their ability to produce the appropriate music at the opportune moment. They offered a style that was experiencing rapidly increasing demand but had limited supply within the rising Rock Revival movement. "hacían la música adecuada en el momento adecuado."

# artist - Kasabian

## curiosities
**Part of New Rock Wave** : Kasabian was among the groups born from the new wave of rock music. They became very famous via the internet and, despite their quality, progressively assimilated into the established norms of the recording industry. "Tenemos casos como casabian que también se hicieron muy famosos gracias a Internet... han ido entrando poco a poco más en los estándares de la industria discográfica."
**Lost Generation / Transition Point** : Kasabian, along with Arctic Monkeys, is considered part of a "lost generation" or transitional phase in the music industry. They represent the shift from an industry dominated by record labels to a more intermediate process where artists exert greater control and primarily use labels for distribution. "deberíamos considerar a gente como a Casabian o como a los artichmonks, ese es la bomb perdido o esa transición, que empieza de una industria discográfica que domina todo el poder de la música a un proceso intermedio donde los artistas deciden todo lo que quieren hacer y usan a las discográficas para distribuir su material."

# curiosity

**Difficulty of Independent Material Release (2005-2017)** : In the early 2000s, it was relatively easy for artists to gain popularity online but challenging to release material without a record label. By 2005-2006, generating and releasing music independently remained very complicated, and as of 2017, it was still nearly impossible for an artist to operate without a label. "a primeros de la década era relativamente sencillo hacerse popular por internet pero no editar material... en el 2005, 2006 era más complicado todavía... y aún a día de hoy es casi imposible trabajar para un artista sin discográfica."
**Impact of Professional Production** : Engaging a good professional producer is crucial for bands, especially those that are self-made and composed of non-professional musicians, as it helps them achieve a higher and more refined level of musical quality. This level of quality is often difficult for inexperienced bands to reach on their own. "un buen productor te ayuda a llegar a un nivel de calidad musical que es difícil llegar a una banda sobre todo, una banda hecha sin misma de músicos que no eran profesionales."
**Music Industry Transition - Artists vs. Labels** : The period around 2005-2006 marked a significant transition in the music industry. It began shifting from a model where record labels held dominant power over music production and distribution to an intermediate process where artists increasingly decide their creative direction and utilize labels primarily for material distribution. This transition is seen as a gradual, not radical, change. "una industria discográfica que domina todo el poder de la música a un proceso intermedio donde los artistas deciden todo lo que quieren hacer y usan a las discográficas para distribuir su material."
**MySpace as a Popularity Platform** : MySpace served as a highly popular platform that enabled bands to gain widespread recognition, even through fan-generated profiles, as was the case with Arctic Monkeys. "el famoso Mayespace que tan popular los hizo."
