# artist - Joy Division

## member of
Post-punk bands of the first generation
Shoegaze (indirect influence)

## curiosities
**Early Indie Sound** : Joy Division is considered part of the first great generation of indie sound, emerging between 1978 and 1982, often categorized as Post-punk or Dark Wave. Their sound marked an initial rupture from previous music. "salía joydivision, salía REM, salía de Kyur, salían los Smiths y que marcan yo creo que el inicio y la ruptura sobre toda la música anterior que llevábamos arrastrando."
**Evolution to Grunge** : Joy Division's Post-punk music evolved, through the influence of mid-80s Shoegaze bands with their heavy, raw, echoed, and distorted guitar work, into the sound that would later define Grunge bands like Nirvana and Soundgarden. "la música Pospang de Joy Division me evolucionando gracias a ese pedaleo, a ese guitarrero tan duro, tan rasgado, con tantos secos, tan distorsionados, que va generando el sonido que después más adelante sería Nirvana, sería Sound Garden y sería ya toda la etapa de Grunge."

# genre - Grunge

## curiosities
**Second Indie Generation Cornerstone** : Grunge emerged in the United States between 1989 and 1993, representing one of the two major movements that defined modern indie music, alongside Brit-pop in the UK. "Por un lado sería la música gruncha en Estados Unidos."
**Official Start and Trilogy** : Grunge officially began between 1981 and 1991, marked by the emergence of the "great Grunge trilogy" featuring bands like Nirvana, Soundgarden, and Pearl Jam. "Justo al mismo momento entre el 81 y el 91 arrancaba oficialmente el gruncho, venía la gran trilogía del gruncho."
**Massive Production** : From its inception, Grunge received immediate and massive production and distribution support from the record industry, distinguishing its rise from the struggles faced by the Shoegaze movement. "que prácticamente meses desde su aparición ya tenían una producción masiva y distribuida."

# genre - Brit-Pop

## curiosities
**Second Indie Generation Cornerstone** : Brit-Pop emerged in the United Kingdom between 1989 and 1993, representing one of the two major movements that defined modern indie music, alongside Grunge in the US. "por el otro lado sería el Brit-Pop en el Reino Unido."
**Early Spark** : The Brit-pop movement likely began around 1989, with Suede often considered its vanguard. "Justo en 1989 casi a la vez arrancaba el movimiento Britop posiblemente con sweat, que era un poco la avanza dilla."
**Revolution and Industry Shift** : By 1991, young bands like Blur and Oasis were revolutionizing British pop, causing the recording industry to reorient its focus towards this new generation, leaving the previously struggling Shoegaze generation behind. "ya en 1991 unos jovencísimos Blood y Basis revolucionaban el pobre tánico, y ya digamos que la industria discográfica se orientaba hacia otro lugar."
**Immediate Label Support** : From its very beginning, Brit-pop benefited from immediate record label support, including massive production and distribution within months of its appearance, a stark contrast to the challenges faced by Shoegaze. "De haber tenido el apoyo discográfico que al mejor tuvieron desde un inicio el Britpop."

# genre - Shoegaze

## curiosities
**Connecting Movement** : Shoegaze emerged in the mid-1980s as a crucial, though often overlooked, movement that effectively connected the first generation of indie music (Post-punk of the early 80s) with the second generation (Grunge/Brit-pop of the early 90s). "justo en el medio, a mitad de los 80, hubo un pequeño movimiento que realmente es la que viene a conectar esas dos grandes generaciones de la música indí."
**Name Origin** : The genre's name, "Shoegaze," originated somewhat ironically from the common behavior of bands in this movement, who were often observed looking down at their shoes while performing due to their use of pedals. "dos cogen ese nombre así un poco ironicamente de ese comportamiento."
**Forgotten Generation** : Despite its importance in musical evolution, Shoegaze is often described as a "forgotten generation" that never gained significant prominence between the two larger indie titans of the early 80s and early 90s. "esa pequeña generación olvidada que nunca ha destacado demasiado entre esos dos grandes titanes de la música moderna."
**Delayed Commercial Recognition** : Although bands like My Bloody Valentine and Pixies formed around 1984-1985, the Shoegaze movement struggled with scarce record industry interest, preventing it from gaining significant momentum until almost the late 1980s, creating a 4-5 year gap of limited commercial impact. "el escaso interés discográfico que generó todo este movimiento hizo que no fuese engarando peso hasta casi finales de los 80 y estuviesen ahí como un gap de tiempo entre 4 y 5 años."
**Grassroots Activity** : During its early, unrecognized phase (1984-1989), the Shoegaze movement was vibrant in independent venues, garages, and through the exchange of pirated cassette tapes among university students. "no salía de los locales independientes, los garajes o las tintas de cassette que se daban en las universidades entre agente."
**Sound Evolution from Post-punk** : The Shoegaze movement was instrumental in evolving the Post-punk sound of bands like Joy Division by incorporating heavy, raw, echoed, and distorted guitar work from pedals, thus laying the groundwork for later Grunge music. "esa música Pospang de Joy Division me evolucionando gracias a ese pedaleo, a ese guitarrero tan duro, tan rasgado, con tantos secos, tan distorsionados."
**Post-90s Continuation** : Although considered "born almost dead" in the late 1980s due to lack of immediate recognition, the Shoegaze movement continued as an independent strand, separate from the major 1990s movements like Grunge and Brit-pop, almost until the mid-1990s, with bands like Slowdive, Yo La Tengo, and Spiritualized. "El movimiento Shugheiz, aunque ya nació prácticamente muerto, a finales de los 80, sí que continuaría como una especie de estela independiente de todos esos grandes movimientos de los 90 hasta casi mitad de los 90."
**Underappreciated Quality** : The limited memory and recognition of Shoegaze bands are not due to a lack of musical quality or evolutionary significance, but rather their misfortune of existing between two massive generations and lacking crucial record label support. "no tienen tanto que ver como su escasa calidad musical, porque era musicalmente muy interesante y muy evolutivo, sino porque tuvieron por un lado la mala suerte de vivir entre esas dos grandísimas generaciones y tuvieron por otro lado la grandísima mala suerte de no contar con el apoyo discográfico."

# curiosity

**Indie Music Generational Blocks** : The history of indie music is marked by two main generational blocks: the first emerging between 1978 and 1982 (often called Post-punk or Dark Wave, featuring bands like Joy Division, R.E.M., The Cure, and The Smiths), and the second in the early 1990s (defined by Grunge in the US and Brit-pop in the UK). "tenemos como dos grandes generaciones que marcan el sonido indital y como lo conocemos. Las dos generaciones fueron la primera que nacería entre 1978 y 82 y la segunda que sería primero de los años 90."
**Mid-80s "Gap" in Indie History** : A noticeable "hole" or disconnect exists in the history of indie music between the two major generational blocks of 1980 and 1990, a period not easily connected and largely filled by the Shoegaze movement. "en tres a dos grandes moles generacionales que tenemos entre 1980 y 1990, muchas veces solemos tener un pequeño agujero. Son dos generaciones que no son fáciles de conectar."
**Bad Luck of Mid-80s Bands** : Bands from the mid-1980s (the Shoegaze era) faced significant disadvantages, not only by being situated between two highly influential and sound-revolutionizing generations (early 80s and early 90s) but also due to persistent lack of interest from the record industry. "Tuvieron muy mal a suerte porque no solo estaban entre esas dos grandes generaciones tan importantes que revolucionaron el sonido, a primero de los 80 y a primero de los 90, sino que luego también tenemos algunos problemas que la condenaron al fracaso."
**Record Industry Indifference to Shoegaze** : The record industry showed little interest in the Shoegaze movement during the mid-1980s, despite important bands forming around 1984-1985, leading to a significant delay in its commercial development until almost 1989-1990. "el escaso interés que siempre tuvo la industria discográfica, porque si dien fue a 1984, 85 cuando Bandage como My Blood y Balantai en los Pixies se fundaban, el escaso interés discográfico que generó todo este movimiento hizo que no fuese engarando peso hasta casi finales de los 80."
**Replacement of Mid-80s Generation** : The mid-80s Shoegaze generation, which struggled to gain traction and establish a visible sound, was ultimately replaced and overshadowed by the subsequent generation of Grunge and Brit-pop, whose movements received immediate and widespread industry support. "cuando empezaba un poco a despuntar y un poco al tener un sonido visible en el mundo eran reemplazados por una generación siguiente."
