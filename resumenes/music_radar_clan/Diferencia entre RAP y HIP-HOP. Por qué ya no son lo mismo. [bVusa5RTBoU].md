# genre - Hip Hop

## curiosities
**Cultural Movement Origin (Late 1970s)** : Hip hop is defined as a comprehensive cultural movement rather than merely a musical style, originating in New York City' primarily in the Bronx, between the late 1970s (specifically 1978-1979) and early 1980s (1981). It emerged from the suburbs inhabited by racial minorities, fostered by cultural promoters like Africa Bambaataa. "el hip hop no es un estilo musical es un movimiento cultural es un movimiento cultural que aparece en la ciudad de Nueva York a finales de los años 70 entre el 78 79 por ciento entre 80 y uno también"
**Four Pillars of Culture** : The foundation of hip hop culture is supported by four main pillars developed in civic centers within New York's suburbs. These are: breakdance (a dance movement), turnabilism (an instrumental movement driven by DJs using vinyl turntables as instruments), rap (a distinct singing style), and graffiti (a pictorial or plastic art movement). "se crearon como los cuatro pilares sobre los que se sustentan la cultura del hip hop que es por un lado un movimiento de baile que se llama el verdader un movimiento instrumental que se llama el turnabilismo o los DJs que son los que empiezan a usar los platos de vinilo como instrumentos y no para poner música de ahí nacería el scratch nacería el breakbeat nacería como varias movimientos de turnabilismo nace una manera de cantar que es el rap y luego nace un movimiento pictórico o un movimiento plástico que se llama el graffiti"
**Diverse Racial Origins** : Despite common misconceptions, hip hop's origins were not exclusively African American but included a mix of racial minorities. For example, Grandmaster Flash, a pioneer of turnabilism, was of Filipino descent, and Puerto Ricans played a significant role in breakdance, demonstrating the multicultural roots of the movement in the US. "la gente asocia erroneamente el hip hop con la cultura afraamericana eso no es correcto porque los nacimientos del hip hop no solo del no solo del rap sino de toda la cultura hip hop está toda la minoría raciales que habían estado sonidos ejemplos que es uno de los padres del turnabilismo de la cultura hip hop era de origen finipino en el break down sobre todo teníamos a muchísimos puertos riqueños"
**Evolution and Fragmentation Theory 1 (Purist)** : One theory maintains a purist view, asserting that all forms of rap, regardless of stylistic differences, are branches of hip hop culture. Publications like "The Hip Hop" channel/magazine subscribe to this idea, even considering "white rap" and instances of "musical intrusiveness" by mainstream artists as part of the overarching hip hop umbrella. "ellos consideran que todo el rap prácticamente son esticiones de la cultura hip hop que se ha ido ramificando mucho pero que forman parte de eso y llega un punto en que por ejemplo en de hip hop o muchas de estas revistas consideran por ejemplo el rap como una estición del rap y lo consideran dentro del hip hop aunque estilísticamente parece que no tenga nada que ver"
**Evolution and Fragmentation Theory 2 (Disconnected Rap)** : An alternative critical perspective suggests that hip hop has fragmented significantly, leading to a segment of rap that remains connected to classical hip hop culture and another, increasingly larger, segment that has disconnected. This view sees rap as a singing method that, while born within hip hop, has evolved to exist independently, making it easier to categorize genres like rap metal or experimental rap. "muchos otros críticos creen que no es que ya ha llegado un punto en que eso se ha fragmentado tanto que que tenemos los partes tenemos una parte del rap que pertenece a la cultura clásica del hip hop y una parte del rap que se ha desconectado del hip hop porque considera que el rap es una manera de cantar que nace vinculada al hip hop pero que poco a poco me en parte se ha ido saliendo"
**Evolution and Fragmentation Theory 3 (Rap Pre-dates Hip Hop)** : A highly debated theory posits that rap, in some form, existed before the formal birth of hip hop culture. This theory traces rap's precursors to "canción narrada" styles used by artists like James Brown and Grace Jones, and even to student protest movements in the late 1960s and early 1970s, where manifestos were read over music, suggesting rap is an independent vocal form that was later integrated into hip hop. "realmente el rap ya existía antes del nacimiento del hip hop esto está muy discutido hay muchísima disputa al respecto todavía hoy porque claro el rap realmente tenemos una evolución que nos lleva a considerar que el rap moderno nace con la cultura hip hop a final de los años 70 pero sí que hay una serie de evolución anterior que desenboca en el nacimiento del rap"

# genre - Rap

## curiosities
**Singing Style within Hip Hop** : Rap is defined as a specific way of singing that emerged as one of the four foundational pillars of hip hop culture. It is distinguished by its characteristic rhythmic cadence, which differentiates it from earlier narrative vocal forms. "nace una manera de cantar que es el rap"
**Modern Birth (Late 1970s)** : Modern rap, as it is broadly understood today, is considered to have originated with the advent of hip hop culture in New York City during the late 1970s, specifically between 1978 and 1981. "el rap moderno nace con la cultura hip hop a final de los años 70"
**Precursors: Narrated Song** : The "canción narrada" (narrated song) style, used by artists such as James Brown and Grace Jones, is identified as a significant precursor to modern rap. The primary distinction is that while narrated song lacked a consistent rhythmic structure, rap incorporates a defined beat and rhythm. "el mayor exponente o el principal es la canción narrada la canción narrada es un recurso de canto que se usaba ya desde muy muy muy atrás de hecho barriwipe usaba la canción narrada gray Jones en los 80 usó muchísimo la canción narrada... James Brown usó muchísimo la canción narrada"
**Precursors: Student Protest Movement (Late 1960s/Early 1970s)** : The origins of rap are also traced by some to African American student protest movements in the late 1960s and early 1970s. During this period, young people in universities would narrate protests or read manifestos with background music, forming another early precursor to rap's vocal style. "había una serie de movimiento de protesta estudiantil muy vinculado la cultura afraamericana también que usaba música de fondo para hacer sus discursos de protesta entonces hay como muchas grabaciones en final de los 60 y 70 de jóvenes en las universidades narrando protesta so leyendo manifestos de protestas con música de fondo"
**Evolving Independence from Hip Hop** : A significant contemporary viewpoint suggests that while rap originated within hip hop culture, it has evolved to a point where many movements now exist independently. Rap is increasingly seen as a versatile, extrapolable method of singing that can be integrated into various genres, such as rap metal or experimental rap, without necessarily adhering to the cultural tenets of hip hop. "creo que ha evolucionado de una manera en la que se ha salido de ahí... hay muchos movimientos de rap y ya hace diez años hace quince años había muchos movimientos de rap que no tienen nada que ver con la cultura hip hop"
**Debate on Trap's Inclusion** : The classification of Trap, particularly "Trap Latino," within hip hop culture is a point of contention among critics. While some sources like "The Hip Hop" publication consider it part of hip hop, others argue that merely rapping does not suffice for inclusion in hip hop culture, which encompasses a broader set of cultural elements. "El trap a ver el trap entra dentro la cultura hip hop muy cogido por pinzas en that en hip hop lo consideran parte del hip hop pero bueno y sobre todo te metes en el trap latino no o sea no necesamente creo que no tiene nada que ver que rapén no significa que tengan que ver con la cultura hip hop"

# genre - Turnabilismo

## curiosities
**Instrumental Pillar of Hip Hop** : Turnabilism is identified as a crucial instrumental movement and one of the four foundational pillars of hip hop culture. It involves DJs innovatively using vinyl turntables as musical instruments for performance, rather than simply for playing records, which led to the development of techniques like scratch and breakbeat. "un movimiento instrumental que se llama el turnabilismo o los DJs que son los que empiezan a usar los platos de vinilo como instrumentos y no para poner música de ahí nacería el scratch nacería el breakbeat"

# genre - Breakdance

## curiosities
**Dance Pillar of Hip Hop** : Breakdance is recognized as one of the four core pillars of hip hop culture, specifically a dynamic dance movement. It emerged in the civic centers of New York's minority suburbs during the late 1970s as an integral part of the nascent hip hop scene. "un movimiento de baile que se llama el verdader"

# genre - Graffiti

## curiosities
**Pictorial Pillar of Hip Hop** : Graffiti is defined as a pictorial or plastic art movement and constitutes one of the four essential pillars of hip hop culture. It developed alongside breakdance, turnabilism, and rap in the cultural hubs of New York's minority neighborhoods during the formative period of hip hop. "un movimiento pictórico o un movimiento plástico que se llama el graffiti"

# genre - Canción Narrada

## curiosities
**Precursor to Rap** : "Canción narrada" (narrated song) is described as a vocal resource or singing technique that significantly predates modern rap and is considered one of its primary precursors. Artists such as James Brown and Grace Jones widely employed this style in their music. "la canción narrada es un recurso de canto que se usaba ya desde muy muy muy atrás"
**Distinction from Rap** : The fundamental difference between "canción narrada" and rap lies in the former's lack of a consistent rhythmic following, which is a defining characteristic of rap. Despite this, the vocal delivery styles are often quite similar, making it challenging to distinguish between them at certain points. "la canción narrada no tiene un seguimiento ritmico y el rap sí tiene un seguimiento ritmico pero son muy muy parecidos es un punto que haces no sabes cuando es canción narrada y cuando es rap"

"los DJs que son los que empiezan a usar los platos de vinilo como instrumentos y no para poner música"
