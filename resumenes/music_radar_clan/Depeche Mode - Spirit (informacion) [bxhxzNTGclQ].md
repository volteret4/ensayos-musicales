## albums
**Spirit (2017) - Latest Album** : This is the band's latest album, released on a Friday, and was considered the most interesting new music release of that day. It follows the trend of recent Depeche Mode albums which are very similar in themes and musical styles, satisfying loyal fans but leaving others feeling it's "more of the same." Despite this, it is still considered a great album. The album features more explicit political and social discourse than previous works, drawing influence from recent political changes. The production changed from Ben Hillier to James Ford for this album, resulting in a sound that is less guitar-driven than its predecessor, *Delta Machine*, and reminiscent of old-school Depeche Mode with a return to more synthesizer and electronic sounds. "el lanzamiento al fin del último disco de de Petchmode de Spirit."
**Delta Machine (2013) - Previous Album** : This album marked Depeche Mode's move to Columbia Records after spending practically their entire career with Mute Records. The shift to a large multinational label, however, went largely unnoticed by fans and music enthusiasts, as it "didn't bring us practically anything." The album was more guitar-driven than its successor, *Spirit*. "en el Delta Machine, en el anterior álbum, de Petchmode fichaba por Colombia después de prácticamente toda su carrera con mi otro record."

## songs
**Where Is The Revolution (2017) - Lead Single** : This single was released a month before the *Spirit* album and generated strong anticipation for the full release. The song is reminiscent of Depeche Mode's old-school style. Lyrically, it features allegories to speeches by Lenin, not as an endorsement of pro-communism or political affiliation, but rather as a call for revolution in response to the contemporary political situation. It was initially released as a CD single, which is now considered rare and likely to become a valuable collector's item, potentially being the last CD single in the band's career due to limited production. A vinyl single release for the song was delayed until April 28th due to high international demand for vinyl production, with Columbia Records prioritizing the manufacturing of the full *Spirit* album. "Where Is The Revolution el tema con el que arrancaba el disco es un tema, yo creo que nos recuerda mucho la vieja escuela de Page Mode."
**Poison Heart (2017)** : This track from the *Spirit* album is highlighted as one of two great songs that are possibly among the best on the album. "nos van a dejar dos grandes temas seguro que son Poison Hard y Cover que es posiblemente de lo mejor que nos va a dejar este álbum."
**Cover Me (2017)** : This track from the *Spirit* album is highlighted as one of two great songs that are possibly among the best on the album. "nos van a dejar dos grandes temas seguro que son Poison Hard y Cover que es posiblemente de lo mejor que nos va a dejar este álbum."

## curiosities
**Consistent Album Style (Past 15-16 Years)** : For the past 15 to 16 years, Depeche Mode's albums have maintained a very similar style in terms of themes and music. While this consistency delights loyal fans, newer or less devoted listeners often find these albums to be "more of the same," even though they are generally considered great albums. "de los últimos quince y seis años de Petchmode he llegado un estado en el que todos sus iscos son muy parecidos de temática, en estilos de música."
**Longevity and New Material** : Depeche Mode is praised for continuing to generate new, high-quality material, distinguishing them from many other groups of their generation who no longer actively release new music. "Tenemos mucha suerte con de Petchmode porque al me encontra de otros grupos de su generación al menos ellos siguen generando material nuevo aunque no muy a menudo y el material es bastante bueno."
**Unnoticed Label Change to Columbia** : The band's transition from Mute Records, their label for most of their career, to the multinational Columbia Records for the *Delta Machine* album, resulted in no noticeable change or benefit for the band's followers or music enthusiasts. "se iban una gran multinacional y la verdad es que no lo hemos notado, ha sido un cambio que a los seguidores de la banda o a los aficinos a música no nos ha portado prácticamente nada."
**Return to Electronic Sound with James Ford** : The change in producer to James Ford for the *Spirit* album marked a subtle shift in Depeche Mode's sound. Ford's contributions helped reintroduce a more synthesizer and electronic feel, making the album "a bit less guitar-driven than Delta Machine" and bringing back a "good old-school Depeche Mode" vibe. "las pequeñas aportaciones que nos ha James Ford es que vuelve un poco ese sonido más sintetizador y electrónico que teníamos antes de Petchmode, es un pelín menos guitarrero que Delta Machine y sí que es un poco más el de Petchmode de la vieja escuela en el buen sentido."
**Explicit Political and Social Themes in *Spirit*** : The *Spirit* album delves into political and social themes, influenced by recent global political changes, which is a continuation of topics from previous works. However, in *Spirit*, this discourse is notably "more evident and explicit" than what fans were previously accustomed to. "Este discurso político nos deja cosas son tanto más evidentes y explícitas de lo que estábamos acostumbrados."
**Martin Gore's Primary Production Role** : Despite working with external producers like James Ford, the members of Depeche Mode, particularly Martin Gore, maintain significant control over their music production. Gore is highlighted as the individual who "does all the production work for the band," suggesting external producers often have a more collaborative or guiding role rather than full creative control. "en el fondo son ellos los que hacen más el trabajo de producción sobre todo Martin Gore, que es el que hace todo el trabajo de producción de la banda."
**"Where Is The Revolution" CD Single as Collector's Item** : The CD single for "Where Is The Revolution," released a month prior to the *Spirit* album, is considered a highly rare item today. Due to its limited production (not many units were manufactured because high demand was not expected), it is likely to become a significant collector's piece, possibly the last CD single released in Depeche Mode's career. "Es posible que sea el último single en disco compacto que vayamos a tener en la carrera de Page Mode, no se han fabricado muchas unidades porque no se espera que vaya a tener una gran demanda."
**Vinyl Single Delay due to High Demand** : The vinyl single release of "Where Is The Revolution" was delayed until April 28th. This delay was a direct consequence of the immense international demand for vinyl production, which currently outstrips the available supply. Columbia Records prioritized the production of the *Spirit* album's vinyl units, deferring the single's release to ensure sufficient album availability. "La enorme demanda de Vinilo soy en día en el mercado internacional, nos está dejando estas situaciones y es que la demanda de producción de Vinilo es más grande que la oferta de producción de Vinilo que tenemos hoy en día en el mundo."

# instrument - CD Single

## curiosities
**Rarity and Collectibility** : CD singles are described as "something very rare" in the current music market and are becoming valuable collector's items. The "Where Is The Revolution" CD single by Depeche Mode is specifically highlighted as potentially being the last of its kind for the band and a future collector's gem due to limited production. This trend is compared to cassette singles from the mid-90s, which are now considered "authentic collector's jewels." "salía hace un mes salían single pero salían single en disco compacto, que hoy en día es algo muy raro y que posiblemente vaya a ser una gran pieza de coleccionismo con los años."
**Decline in Production and Sales** : There is a significant decline in the manufacturing and sales of CD singles, with "almost none" being produced or sold anymore. This decline underscores the collectibility of recent CD singles, particularly those released in limited quantities. "Hoy en día ya casi no se fabrican single en disco compacto, los discos compactos ya casi no se venden."
