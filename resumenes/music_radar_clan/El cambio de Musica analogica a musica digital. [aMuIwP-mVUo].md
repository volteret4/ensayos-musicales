# artist - Dire Straits

## albums
**Brothers in Arms (1985) - First AAD Album (Controversial)** : Released in 1985, *Brothers in Arms* is widely recognized as the first AAD album. The recording process was analog, but the mastering and mixing were done digitally, and the physical format was digital. Its status as the "first" is controversial due to the progressive integration of digital effects into many analog recordings, blurring the exact line of transition. "Llegamos a 1985 cuando sale Brothers In Hals de Dades Trades, que se considera el primer disco a de... se grabó en analógico, pero el proceso de masterización y mezclado ya era digital y se hacían formatos digitales el formato físico."

# instrument - Vinilo

## curiosities
**Classic Analog Format** : Vinyl is presented as the quintessential example of a classic analog physical support. In this format, actual sound waves are literally transferred and reproduced as grooves on the record's surface. "Tenemos un formato clásico analógico que es el vinilo."
**Analog Recording Mechanism** : The analog system fundamentally involves the literal transfer of an audio wave directly onto a physical support, such as vinyl, where the wave's shape is precisely reproduced as a physical groove. "El sistema analógico simplemente es como esa onda de audio se traspasa literalmente a un soporte físico, como puede ser el vinilo reproduciendo esa onda como surco."
**Durability and Degradation Profile** : While considered an "eternal" format in terms of archival storage—meaning it doesn't degrade merely from being stored and can play centuries later—vinyl records do experience degradation through repeated use. "Si yo cojo un vinilo lo guardo y lo mistatará niitos en dos cintos años lo cogen, eso vuelve a sonar perfectamente. Se degrada con el uso."
**Intrinsic Noise Generation** : A small, inherent background noise, known as intrinsic noise, is generated by the needle as it traverses the grooves of a vinyl record during playback. This noise cannot be entirely eliminated. "Por cómo funcionan vinilo la aguja que va recorriendo el surco, genera un pequeño hilo de ruido muy pequeño, que es lo que se conoce como ruido intrínseco."

# instrument - Cinta Electromagnética

## curiosities
**Analog Electromagnetic Support (Cassette Example)** : Cassettes serve as an illustrative example of electromagnetic supports, which are inherently and completely analog. Unlike physical grooves, these systems record a sound wave directly onto a magnetic tape using chemical processes, thereby preserving the wave's continuous form rather than a decomposed coordinate representation. "El caset y todos los sistemas electromagnéticos son completamente analógicos."
**Degradation Over Time** : Electromagnetic tapes are susceptible to degradation over time because the chemical compounds used to magnetize them gradually lose their magnetic properties. This issue is significant enough that many wide-spectrum tapes, even those used for high-quality recordings in the 1990s, have now deteriorated or become largely unusable. "Las cindas electromagnéticas sí se degradan, porque se usan productos químicos para reflejar esa onda de manera electromagnética y el poder magnético de la cinta se va perdiendo."
**Continued Use in 90s for High Definition** : Despite the emergence of digital formats, wide-spectrum electromagnetic tapes continued to be extensively utilized in recording processes throughout the 1990s. These tapes, visually resembling a much larger cassette, were valued for their high quality and exceptional level of definition, making them difficult to abandon and contributing to a long, slow transition away from analog. "Incluso durante toda la década de los 90 se siguió usando muchísima cinta leitromagnética de amplio espectro."
**Partial Intrinsic Noise Reduction** : Portable electromagnetic tape formats, such as home cassettes, were capable of reducing intrinsic background noise by nearly 99%. However, they were unable to eliminate this noise entirely, leaving a small residual presence. "Los oportes de cinta electromagnética, portátiles que tenemos en casa el caset, lo eliminaba al 99% casi, pero no se llegaba a eliminar del todo."

# instrument - Disco Compacto

## curiosities
**Digital Support Example (MP3 Included)** : The compact disc (CD) stands as a prime illustration of a digital support medium. This category also encompasses other digital formats, such as MP3 files, which represent sound through numerical data. "El disco compacto que es el ejemplo de soportes digitales, que incluiría por ejemplo el MP3."
**Initial "Eternal" Claim** : Digital systems, notably compact discs, were initially believed to be intrinsically eternal supports, immune to degradation over time simply by being stored. It was commonly understood that a CD would function perfectly even after 200 years, offering a stark contrast to magnetic tapes. "Los sistemas digitales, nos permitíamos vernos a un sistema que intrinsicamente se entendía que iba a ser eterno, tú podías coger un disco compacto dentro de 200 años y eso volvía a sonar."
**Later Discovery of Degradation** : Approximately 30 years after their initial introduction, it was unexpectedly discovered that compact discs are, in fact, subject to degradation over time, contradicting earlier assumptions about their eternal nature. "Hemos descubierto ahora 30 años después que sí se degradan los discos compactos y no los sabíamos."
**100% Intrinsic Noise Elimination** : The digital format offered a groundbreaking advantage by enabling, for the first time, the complete elimination (100%) of intrinsic noise. This inherent background noise had been an unavoidable characteristic of analog systems like vinyl and magnetic tapes. "El formato digital permitía por primera vez el formato que no tenía nada de ruido intrínseco."

# curiosity

**Recording Process Codes (AAA, AAD, ADD, DDD)** : Every audio recording is characterized by a three-letter code, where 'A' denotes analog and 'D' denotes digital. These three letters specifically indicate the process used for: first, the original music recording; second, the mastering and mixing; and third, the recording of the physical support medium. "Toda grabación se define por tres letras que pueden ser una letra A o una letra D, A viene de analógico y D viene de digital."
**AAA (Triple A) Process** : The "Triple A" code signifies a fully analog process across all stages of audio production. This means the original music recording, the subsequent mastering and mixing, and the final physical support (such as a vinyl record) are all handled using analog technology. "Tenemos desde un primer punto que es el triple A en el que la grabación de la música original se hace analógica, se hace una masterización y mezclado en proceso analógico y se hace un grabado en un soporte analógico, cómo puede ser un vinilo."
**DDD (Triple D) Process** : The "Triple D" code represents a comprehensively digital process throughout audio production. This indicates that the original music recording, the mastering and mixing stages, and the ultimate physical support (such as a digital audio file or a compact disc) are all executed in a digital format. "Luego en el otro extremo tenemos un triple D que significa que se hace ya una grabación digital original de la música, se hace un proceso de masterización y mezclado en formato digital y se hace un grabado en un soporte digital como puede ser un archivo de audio o como puede ser un disco compacto."
**Analog Music Definition** : Music, inherently, is a sound wave, depicted as an oscillating pattern akin to ocean waves or electrical currents. This wave is the physical entity that is either inscribed into the groove of a vinyl record or perceived by our ears to recognize music. "La música como todo sonido es una onda, es como un dibujo estilante, como puede ser la hora del mar o como puede ser la electricidad o cualquier onda."
**Digital Music Definition** : Digital supports convert an audio wave into numerical data through a coordinate-based process. This involves selecting points on the wave's coordinates and recording their values, allowing for the wave to be reconstructed from these discrete points rather than being captured as a continuous, flowing entity. "En un sistema digital no grabamos una onda que fluye sino una serie de puntos que nos permiten reconstruir una onda de audio."
**Gradual Transition to Digital** : The shift from entirely analog (Triple A) to fully digital (Triple D) audio production was not an abrupt change but rather a notably slow, progressive, and often diffuse process, occurring incrementally over time. "Pasamos una manera muy lenta, muy progresiva y muy difusa."
**AAD Transition Phase (80s)** : The initial step in the digital transition involved the AAD format, where the recording and mixing processes remained analog, but the final physical support became digital. This development coincided with the invention of the compact disc format in the 1980s. "Lo primero que se hizo fue un A A D, esto es, si grababa analógico se mezclaba analógico y el soporte era digital."
**The Visitor - First Digital Music Album** : An album titled *The Visitor* is specifically identified as the first music album to be recorded in a digital format. The transcript includes the phrase "se deaba," which provides an additional, albeit phonetically ambiguous, detail about its recording context. "Tenemos el primer disco de música grabado en formato digital que fue de visitor, se deaba."
**Technological Limitations of Early Digital (80s)** : In the early stages of digital technology, despite the advent of digital formats, the rest of the audio production chain (recording, mastering, and mixing) remained analog due to inherent technological limitations. Early digital sound quality, while sufficient for consumer playback, was not precise enough for professional recording and mixing processes, and suitable digital equipment for these stages was simply unavailable. "Esos formatos digitales no llegaban a esos niveles de calidad, luego también teníamos una limitación técnica y es que no había paratos que nos permitiesen hacer todos esos procesos digitalmente."
**Digital Music's Advantage of Progressive Improvement** : A significant advantage of digital music is its capacity for rapid, progressive enhancement. Since its inception, the precision of the coordinate points that define a digital waveform has dramatically improved. This ongoing evolution means that the audio quality offered by today's digital supports, including computer music files, is vastly superior to the cruder coordinate systems of the 1980s, akin to the advancement in digital camera resolution over time. "La calidad de audio que tenemos hoy en día en los soportes digitales... no es la que había en los 80, el proceso de puntos de coordenadas será mucho más dosco."
**Analog Quality Plateau (Late 70s)** : By the late 1970s, analog audio systems had reached a qualitative ceiling, making further advancements in recording and format quality extremely challenging and expensive. This limitation opened the door for digital music, which, despite its initial quality not surpassing analog, provided a new field for significant future development. "En los sistemas analógicos ya durante el final de los 70 habíamos encontrado un pico en el cual no podíamos seguir avanzando."
**Motivation for Digital Transition (Early Advantages)** : The decision to transition to digital supports and embrace digitalization occurred even when digital systems were not inherently superior to analog ones in terms of audio quality. This shift was primarily driven by the significant advantages digital technology offered, which fundamentally altered the understanding and practice of sound recording. "A pesar de que el sistema digital todavía no era mejor que el analógico, nos dio grandísimas ventajas en su día y cambió cómo entendemos la grabación de sonido."
