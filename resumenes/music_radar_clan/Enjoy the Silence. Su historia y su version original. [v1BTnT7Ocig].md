## songs
**Enjoy the Silence (1990) - General Significance** : This song is recognized as one of Depeche Mode's two most important tracks, appearing on the same album as "Personal Jesus." Over the past 30 years, it has inspired numerous cover versions and remixes. "es una de las dos canciones más importantes que tiene el Petchmo y que además las dos y lo que está en el mismo disco, hay personal y sus y en Joy desigence."
**Enjoy the Silence (1990) - True Thematic Meaning** : Contrary to popular belief, which often misinterprets it as a song about love, struggle, or depression, "Enjoy the Silence" is actually dedicated to music itself. This true meaning was confirmed by its composer, Martin Gore. "Tristemente en el cuadro a que piensan muchos, detrás de en Joy desigence no hay ni una canción de amor, ni una canción de lucha, ni una canción de depresión. Es una canción dedicada a la música."
**Enjoy the Silence (1990) - Martin Gore's Solo Origin** : Martin Gore, considered practically the true leader of Depeche Mode, solely composed, recorded, and played this song in solitude. He then presented it to the rest of the group, with the initial intention for it to remain a solo track performed entirely by him. "Martin Gore, que es el verdadero líder, prácticamente de The Petchmo, que compuso, grabó y tocó en solitario esta canción y la presentó al resto del grupo."
**Enjoy the Silence (1990) - Development by Alan Wilder** : Alan Wilder, known as the "fourth member" of Depeche Mode and possessing extensive musical knowledge, recognized the significant potential in Martin Gore's solo composition. He deeply collaborated on the song, transforming it into the collaboratively produced final version known today, which features extensive instrumentation and synthesizer work. "el cuarto miembro de Petchmo, que era el que verdaderamente tenía muchos conocimientos, fue el que trabajó muy profundamente con ella y el que la convirtió en lo que conocemos hoy en día."
**Enjoy the Silence (1990) - Lyrical Inspiration from Silence** : The fundamental lyrical concept for "Enjoy the Silence" originated from Martin Gore's reflections on music. Specifically, he was intrigued by the analogy that the "silences" or gaps between notes are what truly give music rhythm and generate melody. "los herramientos de silencio que hay en la música son los que generan la melodía."
**Enjoy the Silence (1990) - Synthesizer Sound Pre-existence** : The highly characteristic synthesizer sound, resembling female choirs, which became a hallmark of "Enjoy the Silence" and Depeche Mode's overall sound, was not originally created for this song. An almost identical synthesizer arrangement had been previously recorded and used in 1986 on the B-side track "But Not Tonight." "Una versión práctica identica ya la ha reengraado en 1986 en una cara de que se llama Barnaud tonight."
**Enjoy the Silence (1990) - Commercial & Critical Success** : Upon its release, "Enjoy the Silence" achieved resounding success in sales and garnered widespread critical acclaim. "fue un éxito rotundo en venta, si que la crítica se echo a la canción normalmente."
**Enjoy the Silence (1990) - Unique Single Release** : A specific single release of "Enjoy the Silence" features distinct cover art, different from the commonly circulated original single. This particular single is notable for including "Enjoy the Silence (Harmonium version)" as a second track on its A-side. "Vais a ver que la portada es un poco distinta al single que todos habréis visto alguna vez sobre el play de Silence."
**Enjoy the Silence (Harmonium version) (1990)** : This version, found on a particular single release of "Enjoy the Silence," represents the sole existing recording of the song's original concept. It is Martin Gore's initial home recording, featuring his vocals and composition, performed exclusively with a harmonium, and completely devoid of any synthesizer arrangements. This recording offers valuable insight into the song's original genesis and Martin Gore's fundamental role within Depeche Mode. "Esta es la única grabación que tenemos de la versión original que era Joy de Silence cuando se pensó un principio."
**Personal Jesus (1989)** : This song is highlighted as one of Depeche Mode's two most significant tracks, appearing on the same album as "Enjoy the Silence." "es una de las dos canciones más importantes que tiene el Petchmo y que además las dos y lo que está en el mismo disco, hay personal y sus y en Joy desigence."
**But Not Tonight (1986)** : This 1986 B-side track by Depeche Mode is significant because it featured Alan Wilder's distinctive synthesizer sounds. These sounds were later used almost identically in the much more famous song "Enjoy the Silence," indicating their pre-existence. "Una versión práctica identica ya la ha reengraado en 1986 en una cara de que se llama Barnaud tonight."

## curiosities
**Martin Gore's Leadership Role** : Martin Gore is practically considered the true leader of Depeche Mode, taking on significant creative responsibilities for the band. "Martin Gore, que es el verdadero líder, prácticamente de The Petchmo."
**Alan Wilder's Significant Contribution** : Alan Wilder, as the "fourth member" of Depeche Mode, was recognized for his extensive musical knowledge. His deep involvement was crucial in transforming Martin Gore's initial ideas into the sophisticated final versions of the band's songs, particularly his profound work on "Enjoy the Silence." "el cuarto miembro de Petchmo, que era el que verdaderamente tenía muchos conocimientos, fue el que trabajó muy profundamente con ella y el que la convirtió en lo que conocemos hoy en día."
