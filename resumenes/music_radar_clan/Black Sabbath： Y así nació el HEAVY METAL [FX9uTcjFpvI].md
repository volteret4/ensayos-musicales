# artist - Black Sabbath

## genres
Heavy Metal
Punk

## albums
**Black Sabbath (1970) - Debut Album** : This album was a genuine revolution in the music industry and an authentic masterpiece. It was recorded in one day and mixed in another, with a budget of 600 pounds. Technically, the recording is a disaster, showing noise, saturated sounds, and cuts, which is understandable given the limited time and budget. Despite these technical flaws, the public was intelligent enough to appreciate its distinct, fresh, new, and spontaneous sound, turning it into a revolution. This album's raw spontaneity and technical disregard greatly influenced the punk movement. It is officially considered the starting point of heavy metal, not just musically, but aesthetically. "musicalmente son relativamente cuestionables porque su primer disco se grabó en un día con otro día de remetlas con un prosopuesto de 600 libras."
**Paranoid (1971) - Second Album** : This album continued the formula established by their debut, embracing spontaneity, strength, and directness. The band understood that their added value lay in being spontaneous and forceful, a distinctive trait they carried into their second major work. "repetieron la fórmula al final la paranoïla canción concretamente fue grabada compuesta y preparada en 30 minutos lo que nos ha una idea de cómo la banda seguía sabiendo que el valor añadido era ser espontáneo, ser a ser fuerte, ser a ser directos."

## songs

## curiosities
**Official Start of Heavy Metal (1970)** : Black Sabbath's emergence in 1970 is considered the official beginning of heavy metal, primarily because they introduced not only a new way of playing and tuning instruments but also a distinct thematic and aesthetic approach. This comprehensive artistic vision became a fundamental pillar of the heavy metal movement. "no lo podemos llamar heavy metal hasta que no aparece black Sabbath en 1970 porque introduce la otra gran rama del heavy que no solo es esa manera de tocar voy a manera de afinar los instrumentos sino que toda una temática y toda una estética."
**Genuine Social Provocation** : In a late 1960s musical landscape where political criticism (like criticizing Nixon or the Vietnam War) had become politically correct and somewhat boring, Black Sabbath found a new way to provoke. They adopted dark, satanic themes and imagery, creating a visual and lyrical aesthetic of death. This was a true provocation in societies like the UK and US, governed by conservative, traditional, and strongly Christian governments where religion was largely untouchable. "hizo todo ese aire oscuro satánico que en el fondo a ver ellos no eran satánicos claro que no pero sabían que eso provocaba la gente."
**Third Great Musical Provocation** : The band's debut, particularly their use of provocative imagery and themes, is considered the third major provocation in music history. It followed the sexual provocation initiated by Elvis Presley and another significant provocation by Janis Joplin. Black Sabbath's provocation was described as "completely gratuitous" but highly effective in making people react. "la tercera yo creo que sin duda fue la gran provocación social que hizo Black Sabbath que era una provocación completamente gratuita."
**Early Success Despite Sabotage** : Black Sabbath achieved significant success from their inception, despite a massive social conspiracy from 1970 onwards to erase them from the music scene. Musical magazines and media outlets refused to review them or published terrible critiques, often labeling them a disaster. Sales charts sometimes omitted their releases or falsely downgraded their positions. Governments, including the Nixon administration, actively pressured to prevent their records from being released in the United States, although this attempt failed. "llegaron a tener un grandísimo éxito ya desde el inicio a pesar de el enorme sabotaje social que se le hizo Black Sabbath durante los primeros cinco años."
**Influence on Punk and Subsequent Styles** : The band's initial approach, characterized by spontaneity, provocation, and a deliberate disregard for technical perfection (viewing it as secondary), was foundational for the punk movement and many subsequent musical styles. Many contemporary groups still embrace this raw, spontaneous, and free working method, accepting recording errors as part of their artistic value, tracing this lineage back to Black Sabbath. "creaba esa nueva manera completamente basada en la espontanidad en la provocación y casi descuidando esos aspectos técnicos que veían secundarios que luego fue generando el punk."

# genre - Heavy Metal

## curiosities
**Early History Focus** : The discussion focuses significantly on the origins and early history of heavy metal, specifically prior to 1980, with bands like Black Sabbath, Led Zeppelin, Motörhead (briefly mentioned but not elaborated), and Deep Purple being key figures in its development. "heavy metal anterior a 1980 tengo un montón no tengo mucho moderno pero cplim, black Sabbath, motoged y todos en keys todos estos grupos tan importantes en los inicios del heavy metal así que los tengo."
**Debate on Origins** : There is a debate regarding the exact start of heavy metal. Some consider the "heavy sound" of bands like Led Zeppelin or Deep Purple in the late 1960s as its beginning, while others (like the speaker) mark the official start with Black Sabbath in 1970 due to their introduction of a full aesthetic and thematic framework beyond just sound. "hay quien considera el inicio del heavy metal el sonido heavy que empieza ya con lecepeling o con diparpo en las finales de los 60."
**Defining Characteristics** : Heavy metal is defined not only by a specific way of playing and tuning instruments but also by an overarching thematic and aesthetic approach. This comprehensive identity became a foundational characteristic of the movement. "no solo es esa manera de tocar voy a manera de afinar los instrumentos sino que toda una temática y toda una estética."

# genre - Punk

## curiosities
**Roots in Black Sabbath's Early Sound** : The punk movement is directly linked to the early albums of Black Sabbath, particularly their raw, fresh, spontaneous, and technically unpolished sound. This emphasis on immediate and unrefined music, along with a free approach to social provocation, became a core influence for punk. "el panque se basa yo creo que sin duda en los primeros discos de Black Sabbath es el referente no tanto a lo mejor en la temática pero sí en la manera de provocar a la sociedad."
**Prioritizing Freshness Over Technicality** : Punk music prioritized fresh, immediate, and newly created sound over technical correctness. This approach mirrored Black Sabbath's early disregard for polished production in favor of raw energy and directness. "primaba mucho la música fresca y la música recién salida sobre la técnicamente correcta."

# curiosity

**Late 1960s Musical Landscape** : By the late 1960s, music had entered a phase perceived as somewhat boring, characterized by a prevailing "politically correct" tone. Artists extensively criticized figures like Nixon, the Vietnam War, military actions, and conservative parties in the UK and US. This widespread criticism became so common that it lost its provocative edge, with even Nixon reportedly finding it "boring." "a finales de los 60 la música había entrado en una etapa que ahora mejor era un pelín aburrida."
**Need for Genuine Irreverence** : In a context where traditional political criticism no longer generated controversy, there was a clear need for a group that could genuinely "hit the table" and introduce a new, truly irreverent form of provocation. This void was filled by Black Sabbath's radical approach. "pues hacía falta un poco un grupo que dieron golpes sobre la mesa y fue si reverente de verdad citra ese una nueva manera de provocación."
**Nixon Administration's Attempt to Suppress Music** : The Nixon administration exerted pressure to prevent Black Sabbath's records from being released in the United States. This action was part of a larger conspiracy by the adult and "politically correct" world to try and erase the band from the musical landscape. "el gobierno de Nixon llegó a presionar para que no saliesen los discos de Black Sabbath en Estados Unidos cosa que no consiguió."
