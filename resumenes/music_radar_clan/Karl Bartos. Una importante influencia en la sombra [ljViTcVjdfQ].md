# artist - Carl Bartons

## member of
Craguer

## instruments
Synthesizer

## albums
**O of the Record (~2021) - Compilation of Unreleased Material** : This important and interesting album was released approximately three years prior to the discussion. It is not a new recording but a compilation of old tapes Carl Bartons discovered during a house move. These tapes contained numerous recordings he had made for the *Radioactivity* album that were either never finished or were surplus material. Carl Bartons compiled the best of these recordings, which were not heavily promoted, but are of great interest to Craguer fans, often interpreted as the unreleased B-sides of *Radioactivity*. The album transports listeners back to 1975 and illuminates the unique contributions Carl Bartons brought to Craguer's sound that were not present before his involvement. "sacó este famoso O of the Record, que no tuvo mucha promoción, pero para los que se han fan de Craguerk les va a interesar un montón."
**O of the Record (Limited Mini-CD Edition) - 1000 Numbered Units** : A special, limited mini-CD edition of *O of the Record* was released, restricted to 1000 individually numbered units. This edition is considered valuable for collectors and is perfectly cataloged. "hubo una pequeña edición inminido limitada a 1000 unidades de prensada que está bastante bien. A la cual pertenece estos tanto las numeradas perfectamente catalogadas."

## curiosities
**Pillar of Modern Music** : Carl Bartons is described as a very important, unsung artist who has been a pillar of modern music, yet many people are unfamiliar with him or his history. "ha sido un pilar de la música moderna y que hay que conocer."
**Misconception as Founding Member of Craguer** : There is a common, erroneous belief among the few people who know Carl Bartons that he was a founding member of the group Craguerda de Mánia. This is incorrect. "los pocos que lo conocen creen erroneamente que ha sido un niembro fundador del grupo Craguerda de Mánia. Pero eso no es cierto de así."
**Joining Craguer (1975)** : Carl Bartons, already known as a very good musician, joined Craguer in 1975 around the time of the *Autobahn* album. His work on *Autobahn* was described as an "authentic success." "No estuvo al principio, pero sonido al grupo en la Agida del Autobahn en 1975."
**Distinct Musical Contributions to Craguer** : Carl Bartons brought significantly different ideas to Craguer compared to Ralph and Florian, making a substantial contribution. He was a less melodic and less symphonic individual, much more interested in "canned sounds," synthesizers, and especially "looped canned sounds," which later became characteristic of Craguer's sound, particularly in albums like *Computer World*. "Él leó una persona mucho menos melodica, mucho menos sinfónica que le interesaba mucho más los sonidos enlatados, los sintetizadores, y sobre todo los sonidos loops enlatados que fueron después de sacar aterísticos de Craguerda y el mando así o el en Computer World."
**Departure from Craguer (1990)** : After many years together, Carl Bartons left Craguer in the 1990s, specifically in 1990. His departure was "a bit invited to leave" by Ralph and Florian, occurring during a period when Craguer was not in its best shape in the late 1980s and early 1990s. "finalmente en los 90, en 1990, abandonó el grupo un poco invitado a marcharse por Ralph y Florian."
**Extensive Post-Craguer Career** : Following his departure from Craguer, Carl Bartons embarked on countless projects. He formed two solo groups, collaborated with a multitude of artists, and has been a prolific producer for many people. His work over the last 20 years is described as virtually infinite. "Tras un marcha, Carl Bartons hizo infinidad de proyectos. Tuvo dos grupos en solitario, trabajó con un montón de artistas, ha sido productorlo muchísimo asente, y los trabajos que ha hecho en los últimos 20 años son prácticamente incinitos."
**Prolific Demos in the Mid-70s** : In the mid-1970s, Carl Bartons was exceptionally prolific, constantly creating demos and small musical compilations. "Carl Bartons, al mitad de los 70, estaba en una ofregrativa tan importante que no para hacer relaciones y pequeñas recopilaciones."

## albums
**Autobahn (1975)** : Carl Bartons joined the group around the time of this album in 1975, and his work on it was described as an "authentic success." The album represented a more symphonic sound before Carl Bartons' later, more pronounced influence towards "canned sounds" and loops became dominant. "su trabajo en la Agida del Autobahn fue un auténtico éxito."
**Radioactivity (1975)** : This album marked the beginning of Carl Bartons' significant influence on Craguer's sound, featuring many of his contributions. It was practically the first album not recorded solely by founding members Ralph and Florian, showcasing a shift towards more "canned sounds" and loops, and less of the symphonic style heard in *Autobahn*. "Este disco tiene muchísimas influencias de Carl Bartons y fue prácticamente el primer disco que no grabaron el solitario Ralph y Florian."
**Computer World (Unknown Year)** : This album is highlighted as the culmination of the progressive sound change initiated by Carl Bartons, characterized by the increased use of "canned sounds" and loops. These sonic elements became characteristic of Craguer with Carl Bartons' influence. "el mando así o el en Computer World."

## curiosities
**Founding Members and Lineup Changes** : Craguer was founded by Sonral and Florian. The other two members of the band changed relatively often throughout the group's history, with Carl Bartons being the only person to become a true third member for a significant period. "Craguer es un grupo fundado por dos personas de Sonral y Florian y los otros dos miembros han cambiado relativamente a menudo a lo largo de la historia del grupo."
**Difficult Period (Late 1980s-Early 1990s)** : The band was not experiencing its best period during the late 1980s and early 1990s, which contributed to the context of Carl Bartons' departure in 1990. "Craguerda, a finales de los 80 y 30 de la 90 no estaba pasando por su mejor racha."

# instrument - Synthesizer

## curiosities
**Key to "Canned" and Looped Sounds** : Synthesizers were central to Carl Bartons' musical interest, enabling him to introduce and develop "canned sounds" and "looped canned sounds" into Craguer's music. These elements became a defining characteristic of the band's sound, moving away from more melodic and symphonic arrangements. "interesaba mucho más los sonidos enlatados, los sintetizadores, y sobre todo los sonidos loops enlatados."
