## albums
**To Pimp a Butterfly (2015) - Defining Album of the 21st Century** : This album is widely considered by critics to be the most important album of the 21st century. Its gestation began in 2013 during Kenny West's famous Jesus tour, which Kendrick Lamar participated in. The creation of the album involved a complex idea from Kendrick Lamar, who dedicated two years to traveling the world to find the right collaborators. He acted as a "master of ceremonies," bringing together over 100 artists, with 65 officially credited, to contribute to his clear vision for the album. The album transcends traditional rap, incorporating Jazz, Funk, deep Soul, Avant-Garde, and Reggae, synthesizing 50 years of musical genius into just over an hour of music. "considerado por gran parte de la crítica en el álbum más importante del siglo XXI."

## songs
**Mortal Man (2015)** : In this song, Kendrick Lamar delivers clear and forceful messages, including phrases like "juke on some every message," through which he criticizes fans for focusing on him rather than truly relevant historical figures such as Nelson Mandela, whom he regarded as an important reference. The song highlights his reflective and self-critical lyrical style. "nos dice phrases como juke on some every message, en el cual él nos dice o el critica, esos fans que se están fijando tanto en él y no en gente verdaderamente relevante como pudieron ser en su día en el somándela."
**These Walls (2015) - Self-Critique on Success** : (Referred to as "Gues de Ench theory" in the transcript, likely a mishearing or colloquial reference to a concept within "These Walls" or a similar track). In this song, Kendrick Lamar reflects on his youth, when he aspired to change the world and be an influence. He critically examines how his success ultimately distanced him from this goal, leading him to behave like any other famous person who doesn't leverage their fame for social benefit or awareness, instead just living a rich life. "Kendrick Lamar habla de cómo él, de joven quería cambiar el mundo, que quería ser una influencia y el éxito en el fondo lo aleja de su objetivo y en el fondo se comporta como una persona famosa más."
**How Much a Dollar Cost (2015) - Personal Anecdote on Empathy** : In the lyrics of this song, Kendrick Lamar narrates a deeply personal anecdote about encountering a crack-addicted homeless man in a park who asks him for money. He describes his initial negative and somewhat dismissive reaction, and how he subsequently feels remorse, leading him to reflect on his own behavior and how it mirrors broader societal issues, stating that there's a huge struggle within our streets and we only look outward, ignoring internal problems. "el propio Kendrick Lamar nos relata una muy personal anécdota en la que él se encuentra en un parque común, indigente a Dictalcrack, que le pide dinero, lo empieza a un poco a cozar y él empieza a tener una racción un tanto negativa, un poco despectiva y como él se siente mal."
**Institutionalized (2015)** : This track delves into Kendrick Lamar's personal struggles, exploring how he continues to grapple with traumas stemming from his upbringing in American suburbs, despite achieving significant success. It illustrates his deeply personal and self-critical lyrical approach, revealing how past experiences continue to impact him. "él nos habla de cómo tiene esos traumas que hacen que a pesar de que tenga éxitos siga teniendo esos complejos o siga teniendo ese problema de haber pertenecido los suburbios americanos."

## curiosities
**Early Artistic Development and Discovery** : Kendrick Lamar began rapping at 16 years old, quickly demonstrating a mature style influenced by the old school, particularly West Coast sounds, NAS, Eminem, and Tupac. His early work showcased a highly technical mastery of poetry and a humble, direct style, contrasting with the gangsta rap he drew inspiration from. Before turning 18, he was discovered by Snoop Dogg and Dr. Dre, who are described as the "King Midas of modern rap," leading to collaborations with major artists like Snoop Dogg, Reysa, Asad Rocky, and Tutchains while still underage. "un joven de 16 años que empieza ya muy joven a rapear y que ya desde muy joven destaca muchísimo."
**GQ Rapper of the Year and Jesus Tour (2013)** : In 2013, Kendrick Lamar toured with Kenny West on his renowned Jesus tour. During this pivotal year, GQ magazine named him "Rapper of the Year" and featured him on its cover, recognizing him as almost the "person of the year." This period also marked the beginning of the creation of his seminal album, *To Pimp a Butterfly*. "ya la revista Go lo llama rapero del año y sale en la misma portada indicando un poco que es casi persona a gira del año."
**Elaborate Collaborative Process for *To Pimp a Butterfly*** : For his album *To Pimp a Butterfly*, Kendrick Lamar dedicated two years to traveling globally, seeking out specific individuals needed for his complex musical vision. He didn't compose or create everything himself but acted as a "master of ceremonies," guiding a diverse array of artists, including lyricists, trumpeters, and guitarists, with his central idea. These collaborators contributed significantly to the album's immense creativity, making it a collective work under Kendrick Lamar's principal concept rather than solely his own. "Kendrick Lamar lo curioso es que no lo compone o no lo hace todo él, sino que actúa casi como un maestro de ceremonias."
**Prince's Unfulfilled Collaboration on *To Pimp a Butterfly*** : Prince had intended to work on *To Pimp a Butterfly* but was unable to due to scheduling conflicts and time constraints. He later expressed regret over not being able to contribute to the album when he met Kendrick Lamar, indicating the widespread recognition of the project's significance. "el caso de Prince que Iba trabaja en Tutpim a Butterfly, pero al final pues por problemas de tiempo y de agendas no pudo."
**Lyrical Depth and Social Commentary** : Kendrick Lamar's lyrics, which were already notable in his youth for being influenced by West Coast and classic gangsta rap without being unrealistic or solely focused on violence, reached a new level in *To Pimp a Butterfly*. The album's lyrics are described as current, committed, and deeply political, addressing specific racial killings in the United States with names, surnames, and locations, and discussing the new crack epidemic. He also critically examines societal reactions and self-critique. "Las letras son actuales, son comprometidas, son políticas."
**Personal and Self-Critical Lyrical Style** : The lyrical approach in *To Pimp a Butterfly* is extraordinarily personal, akin to reading an artist's private diary. Kendrick Lamar delves into his own thoughts and feelings, including self-criticism, and discusses personal traumas from his American suburban upbringing that persist despite his success. His willingness to make such personal details public is highlighted as a rare and impactful characteristic, drawing comparisons to artists like Bob Dylan and Leonard Cohen. "él se critica enormemente a sí mismo reflexione enormemente sobre sí mismo."
**Comparison to Bob Dylan and Leonard Cohen** : The lyrical depth and personal nature of Kendrick Lamar's work in *To Pimp a Butterfly* are compared to the early careers of Bob Dylan and Leonard Cohen. A New Musical Express writer suggested that if Bob Dylan were young and black today, he would be writing Kendrick Lamar's lyrics, noting similarities in how Dylan described his childhood, heartbreaking facts, and sad characters from his neighborhood. Kendrick Lamar is seen as reinventing this kind of profound, direct storytelling within black and hip hop culture. "posiblemente si Bob Dylan fue ese joven y fue ese negro hoy, estaría haciendo las letras que hay enriquezabar porque salvando las distancias no recuerdo un poco esa fase pues de Bob Dylan o incluso de Leonardo Coyne."
