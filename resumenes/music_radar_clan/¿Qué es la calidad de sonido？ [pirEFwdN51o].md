# curiosity

**Conflicting Research on Audio Quality** : Multiple rigorous scientific studies exist regarding sound quality, yet they arrive at completely contradictory conclusions. Some studies assert that high-quality digital formats or those with minimal compression offer superior sound quality compared to vinyl records, which inherently possess noise. Conversely, other equally stringent studies claim that nothing yet rivals analog sound. This divergence makes it frustrating to find a definitive answer about which format is truly superior. "hay muchísimos estudios que nos dicen después de analizarlo de manera completamente objetiva científica con sus estudios y sus mediciones que llegan a conclusiones completamente distintas."

**Lack of Consensus on Sound Quality Definition** : A fundamental problem hindering the resolution of the audio format debate is the current inability to clearly define "sound quality." Despite widespread discussion, a shared understanding of what constitutes quality sound remains elusive, making it difficult to objectively compare and determine the best formats. This conceptual ambiguity complicates efforts to find definitive answers. "a día de hoy la gran disputa no es encontrar qué formato es mejor o peor sino definir que entendemos por calidad de sonido."

**Format Variability Across Eras and Production Methods** : Audio formats such as vinyl records, compact discs, and cassette tapes should not be viewed as singular, unchanging objects, but rather as a culmination of numerous versions and production qualities throughout history. For instance, vinyl quality can range from modern 180g pressings, to high-quality pressings from the 1960s and 70s, or lower-quality mass-market vinyl from the 70s often made with recycled materials. Similarly, CDs from the early 1980s differ significantly from contemporary CDs due to advancements in metallic layers and music digitalization methods. "no podemos pensar en el disco de vinilo como un objeto y no podemos pensar en el disco compacto como en un objeto y no podemos pensar en la cinta de caset como en un objeto sino que es la suma de un montón de distintos objetos y versiones que hemos tenido a lo largo de la historia."

**Artificial Perception of Overly Pure Sound** : Natural sound waves are inherently imperfect, carrying environmental and transmission noises, which means human hearing is accustomed to non-pure audio. When sound is rendered excessively clear, crisp, or "pure," it can lead to an artificial sensation for many listeners, often described as metallic, robotic, or sharply distorted. This occurs because the human ear and brain do not recognize such an unnaturally pure wave as natural, causing discomfort. "el problema cuando haces un sonido demasiado nítido y demasiado limpio es que tenemos una sensación artificial del sonido."

**Vinyl's "Warm" Sound and Natural Imperfections** : Many individuals express a preference for the "warmer" sound of vinyl, attributing it to the format's intrinsic interferences and noises that are impossible to completely eliminate. These inherent imperfections are identified as more natural than the perfectly pure sound waves that can be artificially achieved through digital cleaning processes. Conversely, digital audio that is made excessively clear and crisp can sometimes feel uncomfortable or artificial to listeners. "mucha gente prefiere el sonido del vinilo y además su sonad definición muy curiosa y es que lo considera más cálido."

**Technical vs. Perceptual Sound Quality** : A distinction is often made between technical sound quality and real sound quality in audio studies. Technical quality considers aspects imperceptible to the human ear, aiming for absolute perfection in sound (e.g., frequency ranges beyond human hearing). Real quality, however, focuses solely on what the human ear can actually perceive. Some researchers dismiss imperceptible technical perfections as irrelevant to actual listening quality, while others argue that even unperceived qualities contribute to a well-produced sound wave. "una calidad técnica de sonido tienen cuenta aspectos que no son perceptibles por el oído humano porque sólo busca la perfección en la calidad del sonido al magente lo que nosotros percibimos."

**Super Audio CD's Blind Test Performance** : The Super Audio CD (SACD) has been a point of discussion, notably for its performance in blind listening tests. It is mentioned that even some of the world's best sound engineers were unable to consistently identify SACD in blind tests. This particular outcome fuels the broader debate on whether audio qualities that are not audibly discernible contribute any real value to the listening experience. "cuando hablábamos el super audio CD que no había pasado las pruebas digas ni siquiera de los mejores ingenieros de sonido del mundo."
