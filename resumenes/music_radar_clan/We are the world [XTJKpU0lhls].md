# artist - USA for Africa

## albums
**We Are The World Single (1985) - Sales and Collectible Value** : The single sold millions of copies, with 80 thousand units sold in the United States during its first year of release. Due to the vast number of copies available worldwide, the physical record does not hold significant collector's value, but it is cherished for its sentimental significance by many. "de ese mirón millones de copias de este single solo en Estados Unidos llevara 80 mil copias al primer año, de vez os podéis imaginar que hay miles de copias de este single por todas partes."

## songs
**We Are The World (1985)** : Composed by Lionel Richie and Michael Jackson and produced by Quincy Jones, this song brought together numerous artists in 1985. Its purpose was to raise funds for campaigns addressing the severe famines that plagued the 1980s. Released in early 1985, the song achieved immediate global success, topping the charts in virtually every country worldwide. On April 5, 1985, it was featured in a monumental event where it was simultaneously broadcast on 5000 radio stations around the globe. For many people over 30, the song evokes strong childhood memories and holds deep sentimental value. "es ridículamente el guía de muerte que cambiaron todos los artistas juntos en 1985."

## curiosities
**Motown Label Misconception** : A common but incorrect belief is that "We Are The World" was edited and released by Motown Records. This misconception likely arose because the song was composed by Lionel Richie and Michael Jackson, both prominent Motown stars, and produced by Quincy Jones, a key figure associated with Motown at the time. Despite the song's initial idea being conceived at the Motown house, it was ultimately released by Columbia Records due to economic and resource-related considerations. "Mucha gente piensa que el guía de bolfue editado por la mota y fue grabado en la estución de la mota porque la compusieron la nera del Richie y Michael Jackson, que eran dos grandes estrellas que tenían la mota."
**Quincy Jones's "Leave Your Ego" Note** : During the invitation process for "We Are The World," producer Quincy Jones personally hand-wrote letters to the major artists he knew, asking them to participate. Each invitation famously included a curious note at the end, instructing the artists to "leave their ego at the door of the recording studio," a clever strategy to manage the collective personalities of so many stars. "les pone al final una nota muy curiosa y es que se les invita a dejar su ego a la puerta de la estudia de la relación."
**Pioneering Charity Initiative** : "We Are The World" spearheaded a groundbreaking "constitutional campaign" (likely referring to a foundational and significant campaign) to provide aid to the Third World. This type of large-scale, celebrity-driven charity initiative, now common, was revolutionary at the time of its release. "nos deja una campaña constitucionaria por ayuda al tercer mundo, como las que hoy en día es realmente común, espero antes nos estamos acostumbrados a ver."
**Simultaneous Global Radio Broadcast (April 5, 1985)** : On April 5, 1985, a remarkable global event took place where "We Are The World" was played simultaneously on 5000 radio stations across the world, marking a significant cultural moment. "el 5 de abril de 1985, se dedito al 5000, en mis horas de radio, sí, muy tanias en todo el mundo que puse en la canción a la vez."

# artist - Quincy Jones

## curiosities
**Producer of We Are The World** : Quincy Jones produced the iconic "We Are The World" song. At the time, he was considered one of Motown's most influential figures, which contributed to the common (though incorrect) belief that Motown released the charity single. "fue producido por Quincy Jones, que era otro de los grandes pilares de la mota donde la época."
**Hand-Written Invitations for We Are The World** : For the "We Are The World" project, Quincy Jones personally hand-wrote invitations to all the major artists with whom he shared a personal connection, urging them to participate in the recording session. "Quincy Jones (...) decidió del personalmente escribir a mano a todos los grandes artistas, prácticamente con los que tenían un cariño personal para que se juntase a grabar el guía de bolfue."

# artist - Madonna

## curiosities
**We Are The World Invitation, No Participation** : Madonna was invited to sing on "We Are The World" but ultimately did not appear on the track. Logistical challenges, such as scheduling conflicts or being geographically distant and unavailable, were cited as reasons for some invited artists' absence. "Madona está invitado en los salidos en el disco. En el caso de estos tres fue porque se podrán elegir y estaba muy lejos y no era aleable."

# artist - Prince

## curiosities
**We Are The World Refusal to Participate** : Prince was invited to contribute to "We Are The World" and even arrived at the recording studio. However, at the last moment, he decided to withdraw, stating that he felt uncomfortable with such a large number of stars gathered in one place. He was reportedly offered a guitar solo to play but still chose to leave without entering the studio. "Prince que llegó a presentarse allí, pero que decidió la gracia en el último momento, diciendo que no se sentía cómodo con tantas estrellas en el mismo sitio."
**Departure from We Are The World Session** : After arriving at the studio for "We Are The World" and reportedly being offered a solo part, Prince ultimately did not feel comfortable and opted to abandon the session, leaving without ever entering the recording studio. "Entonces al final no se sintió cómodo y decidió abandonar sin entrar en presente la estudia de la vación."

# label - Motown

## curiosities
**Influence on Black Music (1960s-1970s)** : Motown Records was one of the most important record labels, known for producing all significant records in black music, including genres like R&B, funk, 1960s disco, and romantic music, particularly between the 1960s and 1970s. "la visina de las discurráficas más importantes y la música que he dicho todos los discos importantes para que antes de los años 60 y 70 sobre todo en lo que fue la música negra, el ratal blar, el funky, la música disco de los años 60 incluso la música más romántica."
**Discovery of Important Black Artists** : Motown was renowned for discovering and launching the careers of numerous important black artists, including Lionel Richie and Tina Turner. "descubrió la nera de la niña del Richie, descubrió la tina, tarné, descubrió a todos los artistas negros importantes."
**Current Status** : Today, Motown is regarded as a mere shadow of its former self, having lost the prominence and influence it once held in the music industry. "Hoy en día ya la mota no les dio una asumbra de lo que era en Valvide como buena gente puede."
**We Are The World Inception Point** : The initial idea to create and record the song "We Are The World," and to gather all the participating artists, was conceived at the Motown house. "la idea de grabar el disco, la idea de la canción, la idea de contra todos los artistas, se gestó en la casa de la mota."

# label - Columbia Records
