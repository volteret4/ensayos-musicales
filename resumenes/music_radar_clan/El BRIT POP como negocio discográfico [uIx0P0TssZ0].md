# artist - Blur

## labels
Food Records
EMI

## albums
**Leisure (1991)** : This debut album was discovered by independent label Food Records in 1990. It is described as a late entry into the Manchester movement, rather than Britpop, but it achieved considerable success within the United Kingdom and garnered significant attention. "Laser no es un disco de Britop, es que hace un disco tardío del movimiento Matchester, que bueno acabó de generando bastante éxito dentro del Reino Unido y llamó bastante la atención."
**Modern Life Is Rubbish (1993)** : Less than a year after EMI purchased Food Records to acquire Blur, this second album was launched with significant promotional backing. "menos un año después de que Emmy compras Food Records, sacan el segundo disco del Blur del Modern Life, Fish Ravi, ya por todo lo alto."
**The Great Escape (1995)** : The album from which the single "Country House" was released, as part of the "Great Battle of Britpop" against Oasis. "Blur lanzaba con el single más potente de Great Escape, que era Countries House"
**Blur (1997) - Self-Titled** : This album marked Blur's departure from the Britpop movement. Released in 1997, it was characterized as a lo-fi record, almost entirely self-produced by the band, and seemingly made almost independently from their label EMI. "en 1997 se acaba el disco blur o monimo que ya es casi prateado en un disco de lofai producido por ellos mismos casi al margen de lo que era Emmy."

## songs
**Country House (1995)** : This single was released from *The Great Escape* album in August 1995 and was considered Blur's most powerful single at the time. It successfully outsold Oasis's "Roll With It" during the "Great Battle of Britpop," contributing to Blur's initial sales victory in the single charts. "Blur lanzaba con el single más potente de Great Escape, que era Countries House, mientras Oasis salía con uno de los singles relativamente flojos del disco."

## curiosities
**Early Career Weariness (1995)** : By 1995, Blur was described as being "burnt out" after four or five years in the market, having released several albums, faced various economic issues, and grown quite disillusioned with the recording industry. "Blur llegaba a 1995, ya relativamente quemado, 4 o 5 años en el mercado había sacado varios iscos, había estado ya con varios temas económicos, había estado bastante desencantado con aiscografica."

# artist - Suede

## labels
Nude Records

## albums
**Suede (1993) - Debut Album** : Released in 1993 on the independent Nude Records label, this album was a pivotal release for the 1990s in the United Kingdom, completely revolutionizing the British recording market. Ed Buller, who was "the fashionable man" in the British music industry at the time, produced this album. "en 1993 salía uno de los discos más importantes para los 90 dentro del Reino Unido, que sería el disco de Suite, cuando sucede a que va a su primer disco revolucionaba totalmente el mercado discográfico británico."
**Coming Up (1996)** : Suede was one of the "great players" of Britpop. This album, which was anticipated as "el combinam", was released shortly after the main wave of Britpop players emerged. "luego también teníamos a Sui que poco más tardé sacaría el combinam"
**Head Music (1999)** : Released in 1999, this album still bore the characteristics of a Britpop record. However, it came out in a market where Britpop had been entirely abandoned, marking the beginning of a crisis for the band that would endure for the remainder of their career. "Su idioma en el 99 es que acaba el hip music que seguía haciendo caso en disco de ripop pero ya en un mercado que el ripop estaba totalmente abandonado y aparte tendría una crisis que le duraría el resto de su carrera."

## curiosities
**Resistance to Britpop's Demise** : Having entered the Britpop scene relatively late and being "fresh" compared to other bands, Suede was among those who resisted the death of Britpop more than others. They continued to release material, facing a complicated career path afterward, including changing producers, but still produced good albums. "fueron los que un poco más se resistieron a la muerte del beat-pop, me refiero así, al final sacó el vigiarnao y a partir de ahí pues bueno entre una lenta y tortura, a veces un tanto agonía acabó cambiando el productor, bueno llevó una carrera un tanto complicada a partir de ahí aunque nos sacó malos discos."

## labels

## albums
**His 'n' Hers (1994)** : Released in 1994, this album was a result of significant financial investment by Polygram and Island Records. Ed Buller, a prominent producer known for Suede's debut, was brought in to produce it, marking a substantial shift from Pulp's previous self-produced, resource-limited efforts. "con ese nuevo desembarco económico que se le daba detrás a Pulp sale en el 94 Gisengers."
**Different Class (1995)** : This album was mentioned as one of the key Britpop releases in 1996, further solidifying Pulp's presence among the movement's main players. "cuando había salido el different class"
**This Is Hardcore (1998)** : Released shortly after Pulp's departure from Britpop, this album was largely produced by Scott Walker (referred to as "el biscocker" in the transcript) and signaled a completely independent musical direction for the band. "justo poco después acaba el disco hardcore ya prácticamente producido por ya el biscocker y seguía un camino completamente independiente."

## curiosities
**Late Entry and Prior Struggles** : Pulp's entry into the Britpop generation was unique, as they had been active for over 10 years, released several albums, and had little success while moving between various independent labels before joining the movement. "Pulp llega a la generación del Brit-Pulp cuando llevaba más de 10 años en el mercado. Había sacado varios discos, no había tenido mucho éxito, había pululado por varias discográficas independientes."
**Polygram and Island Records' Bet** : Polygram and Island Records eventually backed Pulp almost "by elimination" because the band was relatively "burnt out" in the music market. To ensure success for a band with a decade of minimal success, the labels decided to invest heavily financially. "Island Records y Polygram acabaron apostando por Pulp casi un poco por eliminación, porque al final era una banda ya relativamente quemada en el mercado musical."
**Departure from Britpop** : Pulp easily abandoned Britpop, having previously experimented with various stylistic shifts. This allowed them to pursue a completely independent path shortly after the movement's decline. "Pulp que había dado varios tumbos estilísticos, había probado de todo, había hecho de todo pues tampoco le costó demasiado abandonar el ripop"

# genre - Britpop

## curiosities
**Orchestrated Discographic Product** : Contrary to a common idealized perception of independence, Britpop was fundamentally an "enormous discographic product" or a "discographic orgy" that was intentionally engineered by major record labels. "detrás del Brypok nos guste o no hubo un enorme producto discográfico, o sea el Brypok fue una orgía discográfica en contra de lo que muchas veces pensamos."
**Golden Age and Early Gestation** : The golden age of Britpop is generally considered to be between 1994 and 1996, but the movement, as a discographic product, began to take shape much earlier, in the very early 1990s. "la época dorada del Brypok fue entre el 94 y el 96 yo creo, el movimiento discográfico del Brypok se empezó a gestar mucho antes, ya muy primero se los 90."
**Major Label Strategies** : Britpop emerged from crucial discographic strategies and a massive bet by many multinational corporations, which ultimately led to the movement's development. "detrás del Brypok hubo unas estrategias discográficas importantísimas y un enorme apuesta por muchas multinacionales que degeneraron en el movimiento."
**Implosion and Abandonment (1996)** : At its peak in 1996, Britpop abruptly imploded and rapidly disappeared from the media. This demise was largely due to the major record companies abandoning the movement when it was at its highest point, seeking new market trends. "en pleno auge de 1996, cuando estál nos dio un disco de Blurba Oasis, cuando había salido el combi de suéd, cuando había salido el different class, de repente el beat-pop casi imploció una idea desaparece, empiezó una muerte muy rápida en la que desaparece de los medios."

# curiosity

**Major Labels' Crisis in Early 90s UK** : In the decade leading up to the golden age of Britpop, major record labels in the United Kingdom experienced a period of crisis, having missed out on significant musical movements such as Post-Punk, Shoegaze, and the Manchester movement, which were largely dominated by independent labels. "las grandes multinacionales discográficas en el Reino Unido no habían pasado por su mejor momento. Me refiero si nosotros repasamos los 10 años casi antes de la época dorada del Brypok, las grandes multinacionales discográficas habían tenido grandes momentos de la música que se habían perdido."
**Major Labels' Reaction to Crisis (1990)** : In 1990, facing a crisis of missed opportunities, major British multinationals reacted in two ways: an obsessive pursuit of new stars to launch, and an aggressive strategy of buying independent labels to acquire artists they were failing to discover themselves. "reaccionan de dos maneras, una es por un comportamiento obsesivo, por intentar encontrar nuevas estrellas que lanzar y la segunda por una obsesión por comprar discográficas independientes para encontrar los artistas que no estaban encontrando."
**The Great Battle of Britpop (August 1995)** : This highly publicized event in August 1995 saw EMI (with Blur) and Sony (with Oasis) agree to release new material on the same day, intentionally creating a massive battle between the two bands. "llegó al Klimas con la gran batalla del ripop de Great Battle o de Ripop, que fue cuando justo Emisoni, eran los dos grandes combatientes de esta historia. Se ponían de acuerdo para hacer el lanzamiento del nuevo material justo el mismo día, creando una grandísima batalla entre Blur y Oasis."
**True Nature of Blur vs Oasis Rivalry** : The rivalry between Blur and Oasis, often framed as the "Great Battle of Britpop," made far less sense than it appeared. The bands were quite distinct, had different influences, and were at very different stages in their careers, despite sometimes appearing to be peers due to their youth. "la batalla de Blur y Oasis tenía mucho menos sentido el que parecía. Al final eran dos bandas bastante distintas, con influencias son tanto distintas que estaban en dos momentos muy distintos."
**Orchestration of The Great Battle of Britpop** : The "Great Battle of Britpop" was a strategically orchestrated event, heavily promoted by record labels for mutual benefit. It culminated in an NME cover story in August 1995, which compared Blur and Oasis to the Beatles and Rolling Stones, an article widely rumored to have been paid for by one of the multinationals to dominate the music market that year. "discográficamente a las dos les se interesaba mucho esta gran batalla. Justo esta gran batalla culminaba cuando las revista ni música les expresen agosto de 1995, sacabes aportada histórica, ya para la historia de la música que es The Great Battle of the Brit-pop"
**Prolongation of the Britpop Battle** : The "Great Battle of Britpop" extended for over a year, fueled by a dynamic where Blur's single "Country House" initially outsold Oasis's "Roll With It," but Oasis's album (*Morning Glory*) was considerably stronger, leading to a prolonged competition between singles and albums. "la gran batalla de la beat-pop se alargó durante más de un año."
**Britpop's Implosion at its Peak (1996)** : In 1996, at the very height of its popularity with new albums from Blur, Oasis, Suede, and Pulp, Britpop abruptly imploded and rapidly faded from the media. This swift demise was largely a result of major record companies abandoning the movement. "en pleno auge de 1996, cuando estál nos dio un disco de Blurba Oasis, cuando había salido el combi de suéd, cuando había salido el different class, de repente el beat-pop casi imploció una idea desaparece, empiezó una muerte muy rápida en la que desaparece de los medios."
**Major Labels Abandon Britpop** : The reason for Britpop's sudden death in 1996 was primarily the decision of major record labels to abandon the movement, even at its commercial peak. They redirected their focus towards new market trends and artists. "fueron las grandes discográficas casillas que abandonaron el movimiento cuando estaban lo más alto."
**British Market Shift Post-Britpop (1996)** : In 1996, the British music market underwent a significant change. New bands like Placebo emerged, Radiohead's *The Bends* became a critical darling, and the Spice Girls' debut album became one of the most important in UK history. This shift led record labels to concentrate their commercial efforts on these new acts. "en 1996 el mercado británico volvió a cambiar, me refiero en 1996 y salía a mandas como placebo, salía a álbum de pence de radiojet y ya automáticamente se convertía una de las niñas bonitas en el mercado discográfico británico."
