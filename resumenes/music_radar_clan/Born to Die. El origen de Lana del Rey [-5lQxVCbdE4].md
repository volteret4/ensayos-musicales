## albums
**Lizzy Grant (Pre-Polydor Work)** : This refers to her early work under the name Lizzy Grant, which was self-produced and not professionally distributed, featuring an EP that she performed at her concerts. This material was part of the extensive catalog later acquired and removed by Polydor Records. "Hace no es llegar a un disco, es un amado de EP, que da a sus conciertos, pero que no haya tenido una distribución profesional."
**May Jailer (Pre-Polydor EP)** : An EP she created with a "mediocre" first producer, published under the name May Jailer, which she adopted because she was not happy with her self-produced work as Lizzy Grant. The work was described as "chapucero" (botched/sloppy) and its digital release was considered a "disaster." "El trabajo también es bastante chapucero, se pública del IT, no sé si es todo como un desastre, las cosas están bastante mal hechas."
**Born to Die (2012) - Debut Album** : This album achieved significant commercial success, reaching gold status and then platinum, and hitting number 1 in many countries. It is widely included in lists of the best albums of the decade and is noted for having six distinct singles that entered the sales charts, a feat shared only with Lady Gaga in the same century. Critics were divided, with some hailing it as a masterpiece and others in the UK calling it a "completely pre-fabricated and mediated album by a record label." The album's themes were extensively reworked with Justin Parker to be more aesthetic, with Lana Del Rey herself stating it's based on a "trilogy of three places" she lived: New York (adolescence), London (symphonic and baroque music influences), and Miami (retro 50s aesthetic). Its cover art and subsequent album covers (Ultraviolence, Honeymoon) were photographed in or around Miami. "Ella hizo un disco que fue disco de oro, después se cooperquía en disco del platinho, fue número 1, un montón de países, y hoy en día, esta incluiden, prácticamente todas las listas de el mejor disco de la década."
**Born to Die (2012) - Thematic Interpretation** : In an interview, Lana Del Rey claimed that the "Born to Die" album was about the "first great love of her life," which she identified as alcohol. This interpretation highlights a theme she introduced, though the album's themes were heavily influenced and rewritten with Justin Parker. "ella llegó a decir que el morto está y trata del primer gran amor de su vida, que fue la alcohol, fijaros, esa es el mundo, ya llegaba con estas temas."

## songs
**Video Games (2011)** : This was the first single released under the name Lana Del Rey, preceding the "Born to Die" album, though recorded simultaneously with much of the album's material. Initially, its virality on platforms like Facebook was perceived as organic but was later revealed to be heavily engineered through paid promotions and industry-insider retweets. "Video games fue realmente el primer single, lo primero que se dito como el nombre del Ana del Rey."
**Born to Die (2012)** : Released as a single following "Video Games," it was produced with a substantial budget and extensive resources. The album itself, also titled "Born to Die," was withheld until the last moment, leading to explosive sales upon its release. "vamos a postar por esto, y vamos a hacer un nuevo single, esta vez, bien hecho de verdad, con bastante pasta por detrás, y hacemos el porto de la y como single."

## curiosities
**Public Image and Perceived Authenticity** : Lana Del Rey's public persona has been marked by a dichotomy between tragic personal stories and criticisms that she is a "pre-fabricated marketing product." There has been considerable debate regarding where the "myth" of Lana Del Rey ends and the real person, Elizabeth Grant, begins. "por un lado tenemos, esos historias suficientes que son como muy tráficas, y por el otro lado tenemos un montón de gente de la industria musical que dice que hay prácticamente un producto perfabricado del marketing."
**Official vs. Rectified Biography - Early Life** : The official narrative stated Lana Del Rey had a difficult adolescence, becoming an alcoholic at 14 and being sent to a recluse boarding school from 15 to 18 to overcome the problem. This was later rectified; while she was a rebellious and problematic teenager who drank alcohol, there is no medical record or evidence she was treated for alcoholism at any stage, especially not as a teenager. "en contra de lo que nos metieron, no fue al colo con 14 años. Sí es verdad, que fue una persona con muchos problemas de comportamiento, ella era una amiga rebelde, ella se portaba muy mal, se esterraba de casa a menudo, bebía al colo, el social es verdad, bebía bastante al colo, como adolescente."
**Official vs. Rectified Biography - Family Relationships** : The official story claimed her relationships with her parents deteriorated after her time in boarding school, leading her to live with her uncles upon her return to New York. This was later debunked, as it was revealed her father provided significant financial support for her early recordings before she signed with Polydor, indicating a good relationship. "su padre le dio bastante dinero para ayudar a sus primeras grabaciones antes de fechar por polido."
**Official vs. Rectified Biography - Education** : Lana Del Rey's official biography initially stated she studied "professional metaphysics" at New York University, a claim that surprised many because such studies do not exist. This was corrected to "philosophy with a specialization in heterodox mysticism," which was also later found to be untrue. She did enroll in the philosophy faculty at NYU and took some related courses but did not complete her degree. "Ella estudió, pero esto es profesionales de metafísima universidad, porque realmente son estudios que ni siquiera existen."
**Previous Artist Names and Material Deletion** : Before achieving fame as Lana Del Rey, she registered the name Elizabeth Grant (though she never performed under it), performed and released material as Lizzy Grant, and later as May Jailer. After signing with Polydor, a deliberate and active effort was made by Justin Parker to erase all this prior material, buying rights from previous producers and Facebook Records in a multi-million dollar agreement. All content, including published recordings and fan-made videos, was removed from platforms like iTunes and YouTube. "Él fue y recorrió toda la gente que tenía derechos compartidos, con Ana del Rey, sus caceriores trabajos, que casi todo era Facebook Records, que lo van a acuerdo multimillonario para cogerlo todo... y lo moraron todo del marco, se cargaron todo, sacaron todo el iTunes."
**Marketing Strategy Behind "Lana Del Rey" Name** : The official stories Lana Del Rey provided in interviews about the origin of her stage name – either hearing an old radio announcer say "The Lana Del Rey" or her Cuban friends in Miami calling her that – were debunked. The name "Lana Del Rey" was a marketing creation by Polydor Records, chosen for its ease and profitability as an artist brand. "no realmente en la Ana de Rihanna es una colombia de marketing, en el cual Polidor Records llegó a la comisión de que era el nombre más fácil y más rentable para tenerla como artista."
**Justin Parker's Strategic Vision** : Justin Parker, a prominent music executive ("Idol Maker"), was not hesitant about Lana Del Rey. He sought artists to fill the niche of "independent or indie soloists" that Polydor felt was underserved compared to their roster of commercial stars like Rihanna. He saw Lana Del Rey's mystical, "dark" aesthetic as perfect for this role. "no terminaba de ver, es gente que cobría ese bien el hueco de las solistas independientes o del rollo Indy."
**Professional Development and Training** : The official story claimed Lana Del Rey fell in love with London and often visited. In reality, Justin Parker took her to London specifically for intensive professional training in singing and corporal expression, intending to shape her into a fully-fledged artist. "Ya sin parque se llevó a Ana de Rihanna, a Londres, para tener clases profesionales de canto, de expresión corporal, todas estas cosas para ser una artista de hacha y derecha."
**Justin Parker's Personal Investment** : Justin Parker demonstrated his strong belief in Lana Del Rey by making a significant personal investment. He contributed $10,000 from his own pocket on the first day to support her initial improvements and extensively rewrote her existing songs with professional writers to enhance their aesthetic and narrative depth. "Justin Parker ya os dice la muerte, y se casca un atentico de inmigual de su propio bolsillo, y ya le da 10 mil parros con tantas y sonantes en el primer día."
**Extensive Production for Debut Material** : Contrary to a low-budget image, "Video Games" and "Born to Die" (both single and album) were recorded simultaneously with immense resources. The "Born to Die" album, specifically, involved eight different producers, each more expensive and renowned than the last, highlighting a substantial financial and resource investment. "el famoso Borto de la Inenana del Rey, tienen 8 productores distintos, pero no es solo que tengan 8 productores, sino que cada uno de ellos es más caro, más importante y más cotizado del anterior."
**Engineered Virality of "Video Games"** : The viral success of the "Video Games" video on YouTube was not entirely organic. It was a "highly orchestrated viral event," with significant funding from her record label for paid promotions on Facebook and various other pages, and industry insiders actively retweeting and sharing the video to give it momentum. "fue un sitio viral, pero fue un sitio viral muy recombucido, tampoco os creéis que salió de la nada."
**Authenticity vs. Manufacturing - Final Assessment** : While her record label presented an overly dramatic and incredible story, and she wasn't entirely a "pre-fabricated character" like Rihanna, Lana Del Rey is described as a person with a complicated life whose mystical aesthetic, American Dream influences, and "over-the-top" persona largely existed before Polydor Records. Her story is a mix of authentic background and careful industry cultivation. "ella es una persona que tiene la vida bastante complicada, ha sido todo ese rollo místico de influencias de Dios, de sueños americanos, de rollo como un poco luego de ver, ya le venían de serio, de antes de entrar en polido de récords."

## curiosities
**First Record Label** : This was Lana Del Rey's first small record label, which she joined early in her career. She produced an EP with them under the name Lizzy Grant, though it lacked professional distribution. "PaiPo Intraco, que es su primera escografica."

# label - Polydor Records

## curiosities
**Major US Record Label** : Polydor Records is identified as one of the most important major record labels in the United States, known for signing and developing major stars. The executive Justin Parker played a pivotal role in Lana Del Rey's signing and career development at Polydor. "Polido récord es la discurrafial, como es eso es más importante que hay que así en Estados Unidos, a nivel de grandísimas estrellas."

# label - Facebook Records

## curiosities
**Holder of Early Rights** : Facebook Records was a rights holder for some of Lana Del Rey's earlier musical works, prior to her major label signing. Justin Parker, on behalf of Polydor, acquired these rights through a multi-million dollar agreement to consolidate and remove all her previous material. "casi todo era Facebook Records, que lo van a acuerdo multimillonario para cogerlo todo."

# concert - Mando Diao

## curiosities
**Opening Act in Berlin** : Lana Del Rey performed as an opening act for the band Mando Diao in Berlin, in what was described as a one-off concert. This performance was a strategic "test" organized by Justin Parker to gauge her stage presence in a professional setting, separate from a full tour. "la testa como telonera de mando ligado en Berling, de una manera completamente un concierto suelo, ella no hizo gira con mando ligado, y la cosa afora le dán de bien."

# concert - Saturday Night Live (2012)

## curiosities
**Disastrous Performance** : Lana Del Rey performed three songs live on Saturday Night Live in 2012, which is considered the most important television program in American television. Her performance was widely regarded as an "absolute disaster" by critics and audiences alike and was named one of the worst in the show's history. "la actuación que hizo el orden del Rey y el Saturn de Nightlife fue un autentísimo desastre y todo el mundo se ha cachando... y esta actuación fue nombrada como una de las peores de la historia."

# instrument - Guitar

## curiosities
**Uncle's Instruction** : Lana Del Rey was taught to play the guitar by her uncle. She frequently used the instrument during her early performances in bars and "open mic" nights in Brooklyn. "De hecho su tío le enseña a tocar la guitarra."

# curiosity

**The Idol Maker (Justin Parker)** : Justin Parker is a renowned music executive, dubbed "The Idol Maker" due to his track record of developing major artists such as Sia, Rihanna, Katy Perry, Britney Spears, Florence + The Machine, and Major Lazer. He played a crucial role in Lana Del Rey's career, strategically identifying a gap in the market for "intellectual indie" soloists that she could fill, and then personally investing in and meticulously developing her image, sound, and career. "Lo llame el Idol Maker, porque es un fabricante de estrellas."
**Geographical Influences on Aesthetic** : Lana Del Rey's aesthetic and musical themes are heavily influenced by three key locations she has lived: New York, London, and Miami. New York represents her challenging adolescence, London contributed symphonic and baroque musical influences, and Miami inspired her distinct retro 1950s visual aesthetic, evident in album covers for "Born to Die," "Ultraviolence," and "Honeymoon." "él dice que el disco está basado en la trilogía de los tres sitios, una esquella vive, que por un lado es, no mayor que los que se basen, la capa adolescente, la que llega al cóliga, tuvo muchísimos problemas, se basa en Londres, donde ella descubrió música más sinfónica, violines, donde descubrió la música un poco más barroca, que hay tanto en este disco, y en Miami, donde siempre no, es estética, a tan retro y tan arde, con los años 50."
