## curiosities
**Formation at Sex Pistols Concert** : The members of Joy Division met after attending a Sex Pistols concert in Manchester. Their initial steps as a band, under the name Warsaw, were heavily influenced by punk. "los propios joy divisions se conocen en un concerto en match a este de los expuestos y de hecho los primeros pasos de joy divisions son prácticamente de una banda de punk."
**Early Name and Instability (Warsaw)** : Before becoming Joy Division, the band was called Warsaw. During this early period, they were characterized by an unstable lineup, frequent changes in formation, a reliance on first takes, and technical errors that they did not prioritize fixing. Ian Curtis's lyrics were verbally aggressive and intense but lacked the transcendental depth they would later achieve. "Se llamaba Warshow y pues tenían prácticamente la estructura clásica, era una banda inestable, en el que había varios cambios de formación, primábalo, muchas las primeras tomas, muchos errores técnicos que no les preocupaban por subsanar, mucho por dar el instrumento."
**RCA Contract and Disillusionment (1978)** : In 1978, Joy Division signed with RCA, which offered a significant budget and good producers. However, the collaboration was a failure as RCA attempted to mold them into a prefabricated punk product, leading to the band's discomfort with the sound. They ultimately abandoned the label in the summer of 1978. "Durante 1978 los JVS empiezan a trabajar en los estudios de la RCA con bastante presupuesto y además con bastantes buenos productores y acaba siendo un fracaso."
**Ian Curtis's Vision for Sound** : Ian Curtis was identified as the main proponent of Joy Division's new musical direction, steering the band towards a more reflective and less punk-oriented sound after their dissatisfaction with RCA. "el gran protagonista de esa nueva visión fue Ian Cortis, que tenía una visión como mucho más reflexiva y mucho menos pan que era el sonido de la banda."
**Signing with Factory Records (Late 1978)** : After leaving RCA, Joy Division connected with Tony Wilson in Manchester, who signed them to his newly founded Factory Records. Although not technically the label's first release, Joy Division's work became Factory Records' first relevant project, effectively launching the label. "Factory records prácticamente se estrenan, aunque físicamente no es técnicamente el primer trabajo que hace factor y records, el primer trabajo relevante que haces es la yoidivisión."
**Early Local Success and Influences (Summer 1978)** : By the summer of 1978, Joy Division was regularly performing concerts in Manchester, benefiting from Tony Wilson's influence. They had built a loyal local fanbase and were already developing a clear sound distinct from pure punk, incorporating influences from bands like Cabaret Voltaire, Pink Floyd, and Can. "En el verano de 1978 yoidivisión ya daba concertos con bastante regularidad en Manchester, gracias a la influencia de Tony Wilson."
**Self-Taught Musicianship and Technical Development** : Unlike many technically mediocre punk bands, Joy Division's members, despite being self-taught (Ian Curtis, Peter Hook, Stephen Morris), dedicated significant effort to perfecting their technique and discovering new ways of playing their instruments. This commitment to unique approaches defined their distinctive sound. "yo división a un que era gente completamente autodidacta... perfeccionaron su técnica y hicieron mucho esfuerzo en perfeccionar su técnica y en descubrir maneras nuevas de tocar en contra de lo que estaban haciendo muchas bandas y es lo que acabó definiéndolo, lo apasionante del sonido de yoidivisión."
**Peter Hook's Bass Style** : Peter Hook is recognized as a highly influential bassist, not necessarily for virtuosity, but for his distinct playing style. He treated the bass almost like a guitar, using it to mark time and provide a prominent melodic role rather than just a background rhythm. He often used a six-string bass, which was uncommon for bands of their genre. "Peter Huck tocaba el bajo casi como si fuera una guitarra, de manera que no uso el bajo o para hacer sonidos base, de fondo sino que marcaba mucho los tiempos, en muchas canciones de yoidivisión Peter Huck con el bajo es casi el que está marcando los tiempos."
**Stephen Morris's Experimental Drumming** : Stephen Morris developed a highly personal, distinct, and experimental approach to drumming. His "acidic" and fast style, sometimes employing unusual methods of percussion, created a characteristic sound that differed significantly from the "beating" sounds of punk or the diffused background rhythms of bands like The Cure. "Y él tenía una manera muy muy ácil de tocar la batería muy ácida, que incluso a veces usaba como métodos de tocar y métodos de hacer percusión como muy raros y que generabra ese sonido tan característico"
**Bernard Sumner's Role on Guitar and Synthesizers** : Bernard Sumner was the principal guitarist, though his guitar often seemed less prominent than Peter Hook's bass, which frequently served as the main string instrument. Sumner was particularly important for the band's later embrace of synthesizers, especially evident on the album *Closer*. "eso aunque su guitarra no fue tan tan prominente en el sumerio yoidivisión si fue una persona bastante importante para su sonido, sortaban a etapa de sintetizadores"
**Ian Curtis's Lyrical Innovation** : Ian Curtis radically transformed his approach to lyrics, inspiring other bands like Siouxsie and especially The Cure to change their writing styles. His deeply personal narrative and reflective themes helped define the lyrical style of the post-punk genre. "y en Cortis cambia radicalmente la manera de trabajar las letras, la manera de escribir la manera de inspirarse esa manera completamente personal de narrar acontecimientos en sus letras"
**Martin Hannett as Novice Producer** : Martin Hannett, a personal friend of the band, became Joy Division's producer. Despite his later importance in post-punk and UK music of the 80s and 90s, when he started with Joy Division, he was a novice who had never produced an album before. He learned production simultaneously as Joy Division learned to record and Tony Wilson learned to run Factory Records. "el primer LP que produjo Martin Jannett era el de yoidivisión y estaba aprendiendo a producir casi al mismo tiempo al que yoidivisión estaba aprendiendo a grabar y al mismo tiempo al que Toni Wilson estaba aprendiendo a tener una disco gráfica"
**Martin Hannett's Influence on Sound** : Hannett was crucial in defining Joy Division's experimental sound, emphasizing non-instrumental, background, and experimental textures. He was influenced by Krautrock, Can, and Cabaret Voltaire, and his obsession with sound purity led him to innovative recording techniques, such as individually recording drum components, which later became popular. "Martin Jannett si que es verdad que fue una persona muy importante para definir bien lo que sería el sonido de yoidivisión, fue los que más los ayudó a definir ese, toda esa influencia del sonido no instrumental, del sonido de fondo, sonido experimental, muy influenciados por el club rock, muy influenciados por can, muy influenciados por ese tipo de banda, es por cabaret bolter"
**First Album Recording Begins (Early 1979)** : The recording of Joy Division's first album commenced in March or April of 1979. "a primeros en 1979, marzo abril en 1979 comenzaba la grabación del primer álbum de Yoid y Division"

# genre - Post-punk

## curiosities
**Definition and Origins** : Post-punk emerged around the same time as punk (1977-1978), leading to questions about whether it is a genre that truly "comes after" punk or rather a distinct interpretation of punk's fundamental ideas. Joy Division, while not inventing post-punk, became its ultimate expression and a leading exponent. "posiblemente el post-punk no se algo que viene después del punk sino una interpretación distinta de las mismas bases que tenía el punk."
**Characteristics Compared to Punk** : Post-punk differed from punk in several key aspects: it was more introspective and focused on personal experiences and suffering, whereas punk was overtly aggressive and outwardly hateful. Post-punk drew heavily from literature for inspiration, with artists like Ian Curtis, Siouxsie, and The Cure being notable examples. It prioritized sound quality, refined recording techniques, and featured more technically proficient musicians. Its influences were more complex, including Krautrock, Can, Pink Floyd, and The Velvet Underground, making it more intricate than the raw and sometimes musically precarious punk. "el post-punk por otro lado era como otra vertiente muy distinta, no era tan odiada hacia el mundo era como mucho más introspectivo, hablaba mucho más de vivencias personales, ha dado mucho más de sufrimiento personal, tenía mucha influencia en la literatura"

# genre - Punk

## curiosities
**Characteristics and Industry Role (1977-1978)** : Punk was characterized by a raw, fresh sound based on first takes, aggressive lyrics, and a technically incorrect approach to music. Its recording methods were often inherited from bands like Iggy Pop and Black Sabbath. In the UK, punk was strongly linked to anarchist political movements. By 1977-78, with the release of albums like the Sex Pistols' *Never Mind the Bollocks*, punk had become a highly lucrative business for record labels. "El punk tenia esa vertiente que era como muy fresca, se basaban las primeras tomas de grabación, era como muy agresivo, no era técnicamente correcto, tenía una manera grabar como muy heredada de otras bandas pues como podía ser hipólogo, como podía ser incluso Black Sabbath"
**Commercialization by Record Labels** : In 1978, record labels like RCA were actively seeking out any group that resembled the Sex Pistols' punk sound or that played verbally aggressive music, seeing it as a lucrative opportunity for market expansion. "Ya durante 1978 pasaba que cualquiera que hiciste algo parecido por ver los instrumentos, un música verbalmente agresiva era objeto de dinero, entonces era como que tanto la RCA, pues como muchas otras discográficas estaban comentivamente a la caza de cualquier grupo que pudiesen echar un poco más mercado que diez, un poco ese aire pan de los expuestos."

# label - RCA

## curiosities
**Hunting for Punk-like Bands (1978)** : In 1978, RCA and other record labels were aggressively seeking out bands that emulated the Sex Pistols' punk sound or played verbally aggressive music, viewing it as a highly profitable market. "tanto la RCA, pues como muchas otras discográficas estaban comentivamente a la caza de cualquier grupo que pudiesen echar un poco más mercado que diez, un poco ese aire pan de los expuestos."
**Failed Collaboration with Joy Division (1978)** : RCA signed Joy Division in 1978, providing a substantial budget and good producers. However, the label's attempt to create a "prefabricated product" aligning with the punk trend clashed with the band's desire for new sounds, leading to a failed collaboration and Joy Division abandoning the label. "Durante 1978 los JVS empiezan a trabajar en los estudios de la RCA con bastante presupuesto y además con bastantes buenos productores y acaba siendo un fracaso."

# label - Factory Records

## curiosities
**Foundation and Launch with Joy Division** : Founded by music critic Tony Wilson, Factory Records was a new label that had not yet represented any artists when Joy Division contacted Wilson. Joy Division's signing and subsequent work effectively launched Factory Records, making their output the label's first relevant release. "había fundado su primera discográfica que era factor y records, pero efectos prácticos es como si factor y records no se hiciera. Estaba la acabada arrancar, no estaba representando a nadie."
**Early Stages and Learning Curve (1978-1979)** : At its inception, Factory Records was in its learning phase, with Tony Wilson still figuring out how to run a record label. This period was characterized by a collaborative atmosphere where the label, the band, and the producer were all learning and defining their roles simultaneously. "Toni Wilson acaba de estener su disco gráfica, no sabía muy bien cómo hacerlo"

# curiosity

**Early Manager's Ambition** : Joy Division had a manager who, though not an expert, was ambitious and adept at navigating the music industry. This manager was instrumental in securing their initial contact and contract with RCA Records. "un tio bastante ambicioso que ya les había conseguido encontrar con la Recia, con lo cual era un tio que aunque no era un gran experte un manaller, así que se sabía mover muy bien"
**Martin Hannett's Innovative Recording Methods** : Producer Martin Hannett had a unique obsession with sound purity, which led him to employ innovative recording techniques. One such method involved recording individual components of the drum kit separately, a practice that, while not always useful initially, later gained popularity among other producers. "Martin Jannett tenía una obsesión un poco más sana por la pureza de sonido que le llevaba por ejemplo a grabar to más independientes de cada uno de los componentes de la batería"
**Martin Hannett's Later Influence** : Although a novice when he started with Joy Division, Martin Hannett went on to produce many important albums in the post-punk era and beyond. He became a significant producer in defining the sound of 1980s and 1990s music in the United Kingdom. "Martin Jannett grabó muchos álbumes importantes de la era del post-punk e incluso post-teriores y se convertió en un productor realmente importante para definir el sonido de los 80 y de los 90 en el reino unido"
