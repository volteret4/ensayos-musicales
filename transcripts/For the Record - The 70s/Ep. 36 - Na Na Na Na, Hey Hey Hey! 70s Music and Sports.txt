Jay Weiner, a long time sports writer in Minnesota, wrote an article for the Minneapolis Star Tribune in 1981 about the pirates and their iconic throwback baseball caps and how popular they were in Minnesota. Young African Americans were wearing the pirates cap because of the number of black players on the Pittsburgh team. There were 15 black and Hispanic players on the 25 man roster for the 1979 championship team. Weiner pointed out that in Pittsburgh, they were not particularly popular with black people and white people in Pittsburgh referred to them as the homestead grays, the name of the famous Negro league team from Pittsburgh. Weiner interviewed a 22 year old African American named Tracy Thompson who worked at a Minneapolis community center.
