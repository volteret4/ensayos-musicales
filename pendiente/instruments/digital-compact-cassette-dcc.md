**Market Failure and Brief Lifespan** : DCC's high licensing costs deterred widespread adoption, with very few manufacturers joining Philips. Although it offered better sound quality than the MiniDisc due to lower compression, its prohibitive cost and Philips' solitary effort (as Sony championed the MiniDisc) led to its rapid demise. It was commercialized for only six to seven years and manufactured for a mere five, making it one of the most ephemeral formats. **Philips' Solo Venture** : The Digital Compact Cassette (DCC) was launched by Philips alone, just a few years after the DAT. It was conceived as an improved, digital version of the analog electromagnetic cassette.
