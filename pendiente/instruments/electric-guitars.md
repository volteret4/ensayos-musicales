# instrument - Electric guitars

## curiosities
**Emergence as a Musical Instrument (1938)** : Electric guitars only truly established their musical significance around 1938. Many bands, including Benny Goodman's and Bob Wills', began incorporating them at this time, marking a pivotal moment in music history. "Electric guitars only really became a thing in 1938 musically, and a lot of people started using them at the same time." ← Episode 3： ＂Ida Red＂ by Bob Wills and the Texas Playboys | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zu4E9Y1oJA0
**Role in Big Band Decline and Smaller Lineups** : The rise of electric guitars, particularly in Bob Wills' band, contributed to the decline of large big bands between 1942 and 1944. Amplification allowed Wills to significantly reduce his band's size, with electric guitars taking over parts previously played by horn sections, leading to smaller, more compact, and electrified lineups resembling later rock bands. "Will's was able to make his band considerably smaller with the aid of amplification." ← Episode 3： ＂Ida Red＂ by Bob Wills and the Texas Playboys | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zu4E9Y1oJA0
**Western Musicians' Early Adoption** : Western musicians were among the first to widely adopt electric guitars, largely because they were already familiar with and utilizing amplified steel guitars, which paved the way for the acceptance of other amplified string instruments. "Will's band was one of the first to use them, and western musicians generally were more likely to use them, as they were already using amplified steel guitars." ← Episode 3： ＂Ida Red＂ by Bob Wills and the Texas Playboys | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zu4E9Y1oJA0
