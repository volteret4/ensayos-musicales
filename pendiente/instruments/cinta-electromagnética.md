# instrument - Cinta Electromagnética

## curiosities
**Analog Electromagnetic Support (Cassette Example)** : Cassettes serve as an illustrative example of electromagnetic supports, which are inherently and completely analog. Unlike physical grooves, these systems record a sound wave directly onto a magnetic tape using chemical processes, thereby preserving the wave's continuous form rather than a decomposed coordinate representation. **Continued Use in 90s for High Definition** : Despite the emergence of digital formats, wide-spectrum electromagnetic tapes continued to be extensively utilized in recording processes throughout the 1990s. These tapes, visually resembling a much larger cassette, were valued for their high quality and exceptional level of definition, making them difficult to abandon and contributing to a long, slow transition away from analog. **Degradation Over Time** : Electromagnetic tapes are susceptible to degradation over time because the chemical compounds used to magnetize them gradually lose their magnetic properties. This issue is significant enough that many wide-spectrum tapes, even those used for high-quality recordings in the 1990s, have now deteriorated or become largely unusable. **Partial Intrinsic Noise Reduction** : Portable electromagnetic tape formats, such as home cassettes, were capable of reducing intrinsic background noise by nearly 99%. However, they were unable to eliminate this noise entirely, leaving a small residual presence.
