# instrument - ViniCD

## curiosities
**Compact Disc Single Format** : The ViniCD was a very small compact disc format, conceived as a miniature version of a single, much like the 7-inch vinyl record was to the 12-inch LP. It was designed to offer small versions of singles. **Fundamental Flaws and Market Rejection** : The ViniCD made no sense commercially or practically; it cost the same as a standard CD, was uncomfortably small, and offered no tangible benefits to consumers. Due to its limited production runs compared to the mass-produced standard CDs, ViniCDs ironically became more expensive, effectively failing almost before its introduction. "su coste era exactamente lo mismo que el disco compacto normal, era incómodomente pequeño, y no aportaba absolutamente nada ni al consumidor ni a nadie." ← Music Radar Clan > Especial Formatos que fracasaron： Formatos modernos | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1xBZl0vBOXc&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**Limited Commercial Releases and Niche Appeal** : Despite its inherent flaws, some record labels attempted to release singles in the ViniCD format, though very few exist. These rare singles have garnered a cult following among collectors, much like other failed formats.
