# instrument - Honking Tenor Saxophone

## curiosities
**Featured in Bill Doggett's "Honky Tonk"** : Bill Doggett consciously incorporated a honking saxophone player, Clifford Scott, into his group to provide the lead instrumental line and differentiate his sound, leading to the creation of his hit "Honky Tonk." "And so, to vary the style, he decided to take on a hunking saxophone player to be the group's lead instrumentalist, while Dogget would concentrate on providing a rhythmic pad." ← Episode 50： ＂Honky Tonk＂, by Bill Doggett | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H2rYnKmeZF0
**Introduced into R&B by Illinois Jacquet** : The distinctive sound of the honking tenor saxophone was first introduced into R&B music, and subsequently into rock and roll, by Illinois Jacquet's band. "taking the same role again in the band that introduced the honking tennis saxophone into Arwenby and thus into Rock and Roll." ← Episode 50： ＂Honky Tonk＂, by Bill Doggett | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H2rYnKmeZF0
