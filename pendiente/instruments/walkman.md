**Catalyst for Cassette Growth** : The Walkman, a portable cassette player, was instrumental in the explosive growth and popularization of the cassette format. Its impact is used as a benchmark to describe the later, slower, but eventually successful, takeoff of the Compact Disc with the Discman. **Profound Impact on Music Culture** : The Walkman's introduction in 1979 permanently altered music culture by popularizing personal, on-the-go audio consumption. It allowed listeners to enjoy cassettes while walking, playing sports, or driving, creating a musical revolution that prioritized portability despite the cassette's inferior sound quality. **Revolutionary Portable Music Player (1979)** : Introduced by Sony in 1979, the Walkman was a groundbreaking portable device designed for playing cassette tapes. It differentiated itself from earlier portable record players by being significantly smaller (only slightly larger than a cassette case) and, crucially, by enabling users to listen to music while physically in motion, such as walking or exercising.
