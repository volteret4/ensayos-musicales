# instrument - CB Radio

## curiosities
**1976 Craze** : America in 1976 experienced a widespread craze for Citizens Band (CB) radio, which originated from truckers using them during the 1973 oil crisis to communicate about gas, speed traps, and for general conversation to combat monotony. **FCC Regulations and Overload** : CB radios operated on airwaves with strict rules, including not using real names and especially no swearing, which the FCC enforced rigidly. At one point, about half a million people applied for a CB license with the FCC every month, overwhelming the agency to the point where they ceased trying to keep up with applications. **Transition to Mainstream Use** : Initially a vital tool for truck drivers, CB radios rapidly moved into family station wagons, allowing parents to communicate about "smokies" (police), gas, or simply to join in the fun. Their popularity stemmed from the appeal of 24/7 communication, entertainment, and information, similar to modern pocket computers.
