# instrument - 12-string electric guitar

## curiosities
**Influence on Jangle Pop/Folk Rock** : This sound became a foundational element of Jangle Pop and Folk Rock, leading directly to the distinctive sounds of bands like The Byrds, Love, Big Star, Tom Petty, R.E.M., The Smiths, and The Bangles. "But every time you hear a jangly 12-string electric guitar, you're hearing a sound that was originally created by Mike Pender and John McNally playing in unison." ← Episode 113： ＂Needles and Pins＂ by The Searchers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=izC9ZBI0UHM
**Invention of Sound by The Searchers** : The Searchers' "Needles and Pins" achieved a sound akin to an electric 12-string by playing the riff simultaneously on two electric guitars with reverb and in a specific key (A), allowing open strings to ring out. "The result came out sounding like an electric 12 string, and soon both they and the Beatles would be regularly using 12 string rick and backers to get the same sound." ← Episode 113： ＂Needles and Pins＂ by The Searchers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=izC9ZBI0UHM
