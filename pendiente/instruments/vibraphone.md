# instrument - Vibraphone

## curiosities
**Early Novelty Use** : Before 1930, the vibraphone was exclusively used as a novelty instrument, primarily for radio intermission signals, playing only "a couple of chimes." Lionel Hampton's use of it on Louis Armstrong's "Memories of You" in 1930 marked its first recorded appearance in jazz. "Before that, the vibraphone had only ever been used as a novelty instrument. It was most used for radio intermission signals playing a couple of chimes." ← Episode 1： ＂Flying Home＂, Benny Goodman, Charlie Christian, and Lionel Hampton | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RmfeJYl84Qs
**Unsettled Name (1930s)** : The vibraphone was such a new instrument in the 1930s that its name was not yet settled. "Vibraphone" was just one of several trademarks used by different manufacturing companies; for example, Lionel Hampton played an instrument branded "Vibrahap" and called it that for the rest of his life.
