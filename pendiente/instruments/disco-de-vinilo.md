# instrument - Disco de vinilo

## curiosities
**Early Disc Composition (Pre-1931)** : The earliest discs were described as "stone-like" because they were made from a mixture of rubber and mineral, specifically slate. **First Polyvinyl Chloride Disc (1931)** : RCA released the first polyvinyl chloride disc in 1931, using polyvinyl chloride as the material, which became the standard for modern vinyl records. "la RCA sacó el primer disco de cloruro de polivinilo." ← Music Radar Clan > ESPECIAL FORMATOS： Historia del Vinilo. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XPrymLbp3Ag&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**Modern Quality Improvement** : Thanks to extensive research by companies like Technics and Pioneer, driven by the demands of the DJ industry, modern vinyl records now boast superior audio quality, enhanced durability, and significantly reduced noise, elevating vinyl to a high-fidelity format. "a día de hoy tenemos discos de grandísima calidad de audio muy durables y con muchísima reducción de ruido gracias a toda la investigación que hizo sobre todo Technics y Pioneer para la producción industrial para la industria de los DJs." ← Music Radar Clan > ESPECIAL FORMATOS： Historia del Vinilo. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XPrymLbp3Ag&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**Past vs. Present Quality** : Vinyl records from the 1960s, 70s, and 80s, while possessing a fantastic analog sound, were technically outdated and not considered to have the best sound quality. Their production prioritized industrial efficiency and affordability to maximize sales, rather than being conceived as the high-quality cult objects that modern vinyl records have become, a shift largely due to technological development spurred by electronic music. "su calidad de sonido no termina de ser la mejor se hicieron en una época en la que primaba que fue en industrialmente ficientes y que fue en económicos para vender muchos pero no eran ese objeto de culto de calidad que es el que tenemos hoy en día." ← Music Radar Clan > ESPECIAL FORMATOS： Historia del Vinilo. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XPrymLbp3Ag&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**Post-WWII Standardization** : The polyvinyl rubber disc, invented in 1931, achieved widespread adoption and became a standard after World War II. **Pre-Flex Disc Variety and Cost (Pre-1972)** : Before the introduction of the Flex disc in 1972, vinyl records varied significantly in weight (between 120 and 240 grams), playback speeds, and materials, making them very costly to produce.
