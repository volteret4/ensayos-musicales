# instrument - Akai MPC60

## curiosities
**Foundation of Modern Production** : The Akai MPC60, through the innovative work of artists like DJ Shadow, helped solidify the musical production processes that are still used today. Understanding the evolution of hip hop is intrinsically linked to understanding the development and capabilities of MPC machines, as they represent the core of sound processing and creation for decades. **Pioneering Sampling Workstation (Late 80s)** : The Akai MPC60, invented by Akai in the late 1980s, was a historically significant machine considered the first practically designed for more complex sampling. Its introduction fundamentally altered musical production forever, although it was initially conceived for relatively simple sampling processes. **Technical Limitations and Manual Workflow** : The MPC60 presented significant technical limitations, such as an inability to process musical fragments longer than 13 seconds due to insufficient memory, which would cause the machine to "crash." Its lack of a proper screen (only showing a few letters) meant that critical tasks like synchronization, quantization, sample chopping, and setting BPMs had to be performed entirely manually, relying solely on the producer's ear and dexterity.
