# instrument - Aguja de Vinilo (Vinyl Needle)

## curiosities
**Evolution of Needle Design** : Early gramophone needles had to be very hard, large, heavy, and apply significant pressure to the vinyl record. This was essential because the needle needed to directly transfer the maximum possible sound vibration to a membrane, which then mechanically amplified the sound through a funnel, lacking sufficient sound power otherwise. **Impact on Sound Quality** : The design and function of the needle are fundamental to the overall sound quality, potentially accounting for 95-99% of the sound fidelity. The precision of modern induction needles, combined with their ability to facilitate electronic amplification of even minimal vibrations, is a key factor in the superior sound of contemporary vinyl playback. **Modern Induction Needles** : In the modern electronic induction system, the needle incorporates a magnet receptor that generates an induction current. This innovation allows for significantly lighter needles that exert very little pressure on the vinyl, typically between 1 and 3 grams. The electrical amplification makes the strength of the initial vibration less critical, enabling precise and gentle interaction with the record grooves.
