## curiosities
**Impact on Vinyl Quality and Cost (1972)** : The Flex disc, introduced in 1972, standardized vinyl at a lighter weight of 125 grams and 33 revolutions per minute, leading to a significant reduction in overall quality. This cost reduction was achieved by combining polyvinyl with recycled vinyl, a departure from previous 100% virgin vinyl discs which could weigh 200-240 grams and offered superior quality. **Shift from Quality to Efficiency (1972)** : The introduction of the Flex disc in 1972, while making vinyl production industrially efficient and economical, marked a departure from a long-standing technological trend focused on increasing sound quality and reducing noise. This shift resulted in a poorer quality product that was more affordable and easier to reproduce. **Specific Quality Degradations** : The Flex disc introduced a lighter format with much shallower grooves, both contributing to poorer sound quality. Additionally, the inclusion of recycled vinyl as a material component significantly degraded audio fidelity. "se hace un formato ligero que ese peor calidad se hace como un surco mucho más leve que es de peor calidad y sobre todo se hace la introducción del vinilo reciclado como parte del material que destrozó la calidad de sonido." ← Music Radar Clan > ESPECIAL FORMATOS： Historia del Vinilo. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XPrymLbp3Ag&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**Standardization of Vinyl Production (1972)** : Invented by RCA in 1972, the Flex disc standardized vinyl production, establishing the common format from the 1970s through the 1990s and continuing to influence modern discs.
