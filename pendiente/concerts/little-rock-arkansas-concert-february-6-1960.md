# concert - Little Rock, Arkansas Concert (February 6, 1960)

## curiosities
**First Rock and Roll Show of 1960** : This concert, held at the Robinson Auditorium in Little Rock, Arkansas, on February 6, 1960, was notable as the first rock and roll show of the year. Headlined by Jackie Wilson, it featured an all-black lineup, including Jesse Belvin, who was a rising star on the bill, marking a significant event in the early rock and roll calendar. **Potentially First Integrated Audience in Little Rock** : According to some reports, the concert featured an integrated audience, with some sources claiming it was the first ever in Little Rock. This was a profoundly significant social event given the city's recent history of strong opposition to integration, including the National Guard being called in just two years prior to protect black children integrating a school. **Racial Abuse and Car Problems** : The concert faced racial abuse shouted by members of the audience, a common experience for musicians of color at the time. After the show, several musicians, including headliner Jackie Wilson and potentially Jesse Belvin, experienced suspicious car problems en route to Dallas, contributing to later suspicions surrounding the Belvins' fatal crash.
