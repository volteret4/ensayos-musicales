# concert - Soul Train

## curiosities
**Bowie's Historic Appearance (November 1975)** : David Bowie was invited to appear on Soul Train, the televised dance show hosted by Don Cornelius, in November 1975. He was among the first white performers on the show, just months after Elton John's appearance. Bowie, who was interviewed by Cornelius and the audience, performed "Golden Years" during his appearance, marking his early interest in soul music dating back to his teenage years in London where James Brown was popular in French clubs. **Coexistence with American Bandstand (1976-1977)** : Despite their earlier rivalry, Soul Train and American Bandstand demonstrated their capacity for coexistence, as several artists, including The Undisputed Truth, KC and the Sunshine Band, Donna Summer, and The Spinners, appeared on both programs during 1976 and 1977. **Competition from Dick Clark (1973)** : Soul Train faced direct competition in 1973 when Dick Clark launched "Soul Unlimited," a show that deliberately alternated time slots with American Bandstand to mimic Soul Train's appeal. This aggressive move led to significant industry and political intervention. **Don Cornelius's Rift with Dick Clark** : Following the controversy surrounding "Soul Unlimited" and its eventual cancellation to protect Soul Train, Don Cornelius, the show's host, maintained a lifelong estrangement from Dick Clark. Cornelius felt Clark's attempt to launch a competing network-backed show was a direct threat.
