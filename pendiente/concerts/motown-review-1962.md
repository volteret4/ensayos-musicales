# concert - Motown Review (1962)

## curiosities
**All-Star Lineup** : The 1962 Motown Review was The Marvelettes' first major national tour and featured an incredibly impressive roster of Motown acts, including The Miracles, The Temptations, Stevie Wonder, Marvin Gaye, Martha and the Vandellas, and The Supremes, showcasing the depth of Motown's talent. "A tour with a lineup that seems preposterously good these days. All of Motown's major acts and several acts that weren't yet major but soon would be were on the same bill." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Bus Targeted by Gunfire** : In several Southern locations, the Motown Review tour bus, filled with Black artists, was mistaken for a Freedom Riders' bus, leading to people taking actual shots at it. This highlights the dangerous atmosphere of the Civil Rights era. "In several places in the South, the site of a lot of black people on a bus, for the freedom riders to mind, and people actually took part shots at the bus." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Experience with Segregation in the South** : Having grown up in Michigan, The Marvelettes and other Motown artists on the 1962 Motown Review tour were unprepared for the stark realities of segregation in the South, encountering restrictions like being unable to use public toilets or eat in white-only restaurants. "The girls had grown up in Michigan, and while they had an intellectual understanding that the South was different, they were unprepared for the realities of segregation, of not being able to use public toilets or eating the same restaurants that white people did." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Toll on Georgiana Tillman's Health** : The intense and challenging conditions of two years of touring with the Motown Review took an immense toll on Georgiana Tillman's health, exacerbating her pre-existing sickle cell anemia and contributing to her later diagnosis of lupus. "A couple of years living like that took an immense toll on George Anna's health, and she started suffering from unexplained fatigue." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
