# concert - Spirituals to Swing

## curiosities
**1938 Carnegie Hall Concert** : This concert, held at Carnegie Hall in 1938, was organized by John Hammond. It was later released as an album that greatly impressed Bob Dylan and featured performances by Big Bill Broonzy in place of the recently deceased Robert Johnson. **Most Important Concerts in Popular Music History (1938, 1939)** : These concerts, promoted by John Hammond, were held in 1938 and 1939. Hammond aimed to present a history of black music from "primitive folk forms to big band swing" to an integrated audience, a controversial choice at the time. The events were sponsored by the Communist Party USA's magazine, The New Masses, and featured a who's who of black American music, including Big Joe Turner, Pete Johnson, Sister Rosetta Tharpe, and the Count Basie Orchestra. **Revival of Boogie Woogie** : The 1938 "Spirituals to Swing" concert was instrumental in transforming Boogie Woogie from a minority form, largely forgotten, into the biggest thing in music. The performances by artists like Pete Johnson and Albert Ammons introduced the style to a wider audience, leading to its widespread adoption by big bands and its influence on popular music in the early 1940s.
