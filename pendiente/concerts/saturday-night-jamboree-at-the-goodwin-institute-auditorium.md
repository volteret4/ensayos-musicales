# concert - Saturday Night Jamboree at the Goodwin Institute Auditorium

## curiosities
**Crucial Backstage Jam Sessions** : More important than the show itself were the backstage jam sessions where musicians like those in the Rock and Roll Trio would share new riffs and exchange ideas, which proved formative for many early rockabilly acts in the area. "Most of the musicians in Memphis who went on to make important early Ruckabilly records would play at the Jamboree, but more important than the show itself was the backstage area where musicians would Jamb show each other new riffs they'd come up with and pass ideas back and forth." ← Episode 44： ＂Train Kept A-Rollin'＂, by Johnny Burnette and the Rock 'n' Roll Trio | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u3zYAJ2qeR4
**Regular Memphis Country Show** : This was a regular country music show held at the Goodwin Institute Auditorium in Memphis, which was occasionally broadcast on KWEM, and frequently featured the Rock and Roll Trio and other local musicians. "They also regularly played the Saturday night Jamboree at the Goodwin Institute Auditorium and regular country music show that was occasionally broadcast on the same station that Burlesons Old Bands had performed on KWEM." ← Episode 44： ＂Train Kept A-Rollin'＂, by Johnny Burnette and the Rock 'n' Roll Trio | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u3zYAJ2qeR4
