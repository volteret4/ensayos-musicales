# concert - Gilley's Nightclub

## curiosities
**"Disneyland of Honky Tonk Bars"** : Opened in Pasadena, Texas, in 1970 by Mickey Gilley, this establishment was a massive honky-tonk featuring pinball machines, pool tables, and a mechanical bull. It was a rough-and-tumble venue for local oil refinery workers to "blow off steam," with fights considered part of its charm. "Mickey Gilly said he did not like the article because he thought it was mocking country and he thought it was mocking his bar." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 50 - The Travolta Trilogy | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Cultural Escape and Live Music Venue** : Gilley's provided an escape for its patrons, both real and fictional (like Bud Davis in *Urban Cowboy*), through two-step dancing, akin to how 2001 Odyssey offered escape through disco. Beyond Mickey Gilley himself being the house band, legendary artists such as Loretta Lynn, Willie Nelson, and Waylon Jennings also graced its stage. "Gillies offered escape for the characters real and fictional in the form of a place to do the two step." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 50 - The Travolta Trilogy | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Over-saturation and Decline from Urban Cowboy** : While *Urban Cowboy* brought immense popularity and global recognition to Gilley's, introducing "country-ish music" and a glimpse inside the club to millions, this very fame led to its downfall. The influx of tourists caused locals to disengage, ultimately making the establishment lose its original charm and contributing to its decline. "Urban Cowboy brings country-ish music and a glimpse inside Gillies to millions of people who were nowhere near Houston making it so popular that the locals wanted nothing more to do with it at a certain point when it became a haven for tourists like my parents." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 50 - The Travolta Trilogy | https://www.ftr70.com/
