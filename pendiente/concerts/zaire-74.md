# concert - Zaire 74

## curiosities
**Cultural Significance (1974)** : When evaluated beyond its financial outcomes, Zaire 74 is considered a cultural success, serving as a timely and powerful opportunity for African Americans to explore and celebrate their black identity and ancestral roots during a period of such significant self-examination. **Festival Conception (1974)** : The idea for Zaire 74 originated with New Yorker Stewart Levine and exiled South African jazz musician Hugh Masekela, who envisioned it as an opportunity to unite African American and African performers under the theme "a return to the beat." "They saw this as an opportunity to bring together African American and African performers. **Fight Postponement & Free Entry (1974)** : The boxing match's postponement for over a month due to George Foreman's eye injury led to many foreign tourists not attending the music festival. Consequently, on the third night, after performers like James Brown played to largely empty stadiums, everyone was granted free entry, reportedly at the urging of Muhammad Ali and George Foreman. **General Information (1974)** : Zaire 74 was a three-day music festival held in Africa in conjunction with the 1974 boxing match known as the Rumble in the Jungle. The event, which included dozens of musicians from the United States and Africa, drew at least 80,000 people on its third night in Kinshasa and held significant symbolism about black power and African-American ancestral heritage. "dozens of musicians from both the United States and Africa performing for on the third night at least 80,000 people in Kinshasa." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 33 - Zaire '74, Muhammad Ali, and The Rumble in the Jungle | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-33-zaire-74-muhammad-ali-and-the-rumble-in-the-jungle
**Intended Audience & Ticket Pricing (1974)** : The Zaire 74 festival was primarily intended for foreign tourists, not the local Zairian population. Tickets ranged from $3 to $24, a price well beyond the reach of the average person in Zaire, whose per capita income was approximately $100 according to The New York Times. **Music Festival and Boxing Match** : Zaire 74 was a music festival held in conjunction with the historic boxing match between Muhammad Ali and George Foreman, famously known as "The Rumble in the Jungle." **Political Context & Financing (1974)** : Zaire, now the Democratic Republic of the Congo, was led by the dictator President Mobutu, who was supported by the US for his anti-communist stance. Mobutu approved and helped fund the boxing match, providing $5 million each to Ali and Foreman, but did not fund the music festival, although musicians did receive half their pay upfront. **Underrepresentation of African Artists** : Despite the presence of many "great African musicians" at Zaire 74, the festival's financial failure meant that the anticipated exposure for these artists did not materialize. An album dedicated solely to the African musicians' performances was not released until 40 years after the event.
