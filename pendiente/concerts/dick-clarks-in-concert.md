# concert - Dick Clark's In Concert

## curiosities
**Integration into Dick Clark's Empire (1974)** : By 1974, this show became a part of Dick Clark's expanding media empire after he persuaded ABC to transfer its control from Don Kirshner to him. It was broadcast late on Friday nights, featuring concert clips. "in concert becomes part of Dick Clark's Empire." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 47 - Dick Clark's Entertainment Empire | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Kiss's Television Debut (February 1974)** : Dick Clark's In Concert served as the platform for Kiss's very first television appearance in February 1974. Performing at the Aquarius Theater in Los Angeles, they played songs like "Nothing to Lose," "Black Diamond," and "Firehouse" after Redbone's set. "So wouldn't you know who is responsible for kisses first television appearance of course it's Dick Clark and that would be on in concert." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 47 - Dick Clark's Entertainment Empire | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Program Format and Talent Focus** : The show presented concert footage without artist interviews, distinguishing it from American Bandstand. Unlike Bert Sugarman's Midnight Special, which often featured bigger names, "In Concert" frequently showcased "up and comers" and new artists. "This was a late Friday night show with concert clips kind of like midnight special, but it didn't quite get the big names that midnight special got that meant that they would put on like these up and comeers, newcomers." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 47 - Dick Clark's Entertainment Empire | https://www.ftr70.com/
