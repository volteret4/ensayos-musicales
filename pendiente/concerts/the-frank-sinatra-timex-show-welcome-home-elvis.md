# concert - The Frank Sinatra Timex Show, Welcome Home Elvis

## curiosities
**Elvis's Costume and Demeanor (1960)** : Elvis appeared on the special dressed in a dinner jacket, clearly attempting to present himself as a "family friendly entertainer." He initially seemed "deeply uncomfortable" during his performance of "Fame and Fortune." **Frank Sinatra as Host (1960)** : This TV special was hosted by Frank Sinatra to welcome Elvis Presley home from the army, despite Sinatra having been critical and often insulting about Elvis prior to and after his army service. Money was the primary motivator for Sinatra. "The Colonel had arranged for a TV special to be hosted by Frank Sinatra. The Frank Sinatra Timex show, Welcome Home Elvis." ← Episode 82： ＂Are You Lonesome Tonight？＂ by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B0puRvUVwww
**Postmodern Performance Analysis (1960)** : Umberto Eco's definition of postmodernism is applied to Elvis's performance on the show, noting that Elvis was "postmodernist" by knowingly mocking himself even while embracing a cliche, contrasting with Sinatra's "modernist" contempt for the material. "While Elvis is postmodernist, since he is even as he's also knowingly mocking himself." ← Episode 82： ＂Are You Lonesome Tonight？＂ by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B0puRvUVwww
**Rat Pack Involvement (1960)** : Most of the special featured "standard rat packisms," with Frank Sinatra joined by Joey Bishop and Sammy Davis Jr. "Most of that special was the standard rat packisms, with Sinatra joined by Joey Bishop and Sammy Davis Jr." ← Episode 82： ＂Are You Lonesome Tonight？＂ by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B0puRvUVwww
**Sinatra-Elvis Duet (1960)** : The show featured a duet where Frank Sinatra sang "Love Me Tender" while Elvis Presley sang Sinatra's "Witchcraft." By this point, Elvis became fully comfortable on stage and "frankly he wipes the floor with Sinatra." **Strict Socializing Ban (1960)** : During the week of rehearsals for the TV show in Miami, Colonel Parker strictly forbade Elvis Presley's musicians, Scotty Moore, DJ Fontana, and the Jordanaires, from socializing with Elvis.
