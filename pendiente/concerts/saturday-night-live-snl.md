**Elvis Costello's Infamous Stunt (late 1977)** : Elvis Costello and The Attractions were only booked for the SNL gig because The Sex Pistols, the original guests, were "hamstrung by visa issues" and couldn't perform. Although Columbia Records had insisted Costello perform "Less Than Zero," he rebelled against his label's choice live on air by stopping the song after seven seconds and switching to the then-unreleased "Radio, Radio." This infuriated SNL producer Lorne Michaels and NBC brass, leading to Costello's ban from the show for a dozen years, but paradoxically, it generated excellent publicity for Columbia Records. **Misunderstood Rebellion** : Elvis Costello's statement "no reason to do this song here" was misunderstood; by "here," he meant "in America," referring to the obscurity of Oswald Mosley (the subject of "Less Than Zero") to an American audience, not to NBC or *Saturday Night Live*. His rebellion was primarily directed at his record label, Columbia, rather than NBC, despite the urban legend that the switch to "Radio, Radio" (a diatribe against media conglomerates) angered the broadcaster.
