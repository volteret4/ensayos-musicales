# concert - Savoy Ballroom

## curiosities
**Amateur Night and Talent Discovery** : The Savoy Ballroom hosted amateur night contests that served as a platform for discovering new talent. In 1936, Bill Kenny, who would become the defining voice of The Ink Spots, won one such contest there, leading to his recruitment by the band's manager, Mo Gale, who was a co-owner of the venue. "In 1936, Bill Kenny, a 21-year-old high-tener singer, won an amateur night contest at the Savoy Ballroom." ← Episode 6： ＂That's When Your Heartaches Begin＂ by the Ink Spots | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9mYSQaysXRg
**Home of Lindy-Hoppers and Chick Webb** : The Savoy Ballroom was the primary venue where Whitey's Lindy Hoppers danced and where the Lindy-Hop was invented. An entire corner of the ballroom was dedicated to them. It was also the main performance venue for Chick Webb, who was considered "the real King of Swing." "The Savoy was where the Lindy-Huppers invented, and it was the place to dance, because it was where Chick-Web, the real King of Swing, played." ← Episode 4： ＂Choo Choo Ch'Boogie＂ by Louis Jordan | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DFZnwvsXGJM
**Lindy Hop Origin** : The Savoy Ballroom in New York City was the venue where the Lindy Hop dance was performed in the late 1930s. This ballroom played a crucial role in the development and popularization of the dance.
