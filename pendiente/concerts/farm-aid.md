# concert - Farm Aid

## curiosities
**Financial Impact and Organizer Frustration** : The first Farm Aid concert successfully raised nine million dollars. However, this amount was ultimately insufficient to resolve the pervasive agricultural crisis. The organizers—Willie Nelson, Neil Young, and John Mellencamp—remained committed to the cause but expressed significant frustration that Farm Aid's continued existence was necessary, realizing they had underestimated the political complexities of the issue. **First Concert Details** : The inaugural Farm Aid concert was held on September 22, 1985, at the University of Illinois in Champaign. Tickets were priced at $17.50 plus tax, offering fans the chance to see over 50 country, blues, and rock acts. The lineup included prominent artists such as Johnny Cash, George Jones, BB King, Foreigner, Tom Petty and the Heartbreakers, Willie Nelson, Neil Young, and John Mellencamp. **Founding and Persistence** : Farm Aid came into existence two months after Live Aid in 1985 and, unlike Live Aid, continues to operate today. Its formation was sparked by Bob Dylan's comment at Live Aid about helping American farmers, which resonated with Willie Nelson, who then collaborated with John Mellencamp and Neil Young to organize the event.
