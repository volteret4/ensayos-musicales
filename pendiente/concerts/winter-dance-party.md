# concert - Winter Dance Party

## curiosities
**Reduced Lineup and Financial Woes (1959)** : The tour had a surprisingly small lineup of only five acts (Frankie Sado, Dion and The Belmonts, The Big Bopper, Richie Valens, Buddy Holly), and was run on a shoestring budget. After the fatal plane crash, the remaining performers were forced to continue the 13 remaining dates without the headliners and were not paid their full wages due to the bill being unadvertised. "This one had only five. There was an opening act called Frankie Sado, and then Dion and the Belmont, who had had a few minor hits, and had just recorded, but not yet released." ← Episode 74： ＂It Doesn't Matter Any More＂ by Buddy Holly | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6unib5T2mDQ
**Tragic Tour Conditions (1959)** : The "Winter Dance Party" tour, spanning Wisconsin, Iowa, and Minnesota in early 1959, was marked by miserable logistical and environmental conditions, including a zigzagging route with gigs up to 400 miles apart, constant bus breakdowns (eight buses in 11 days), non-functioning heating in sub-zero temperatures (minus 40 Fahrenheit) with three feet of snow, leading to illness (flu for Big Bopper, Valens) and frostbite (Carl Bunch). "The Winter Dance Party toured Wisconsin, Iowa and Minnesota through the end of January and the beginning of February 1959, and the conditions were miserable for everyone concerned." ← Episode 74： ＂It Doesn't Matter Any More＂ by Buddy Holly | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6unib5T2mDQ
