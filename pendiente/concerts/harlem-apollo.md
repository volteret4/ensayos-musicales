# concert - Harlem Apollo

## curiosities
**Challenging Audiences (1957)** : The Harlem Apollo was known for its notoriously hostile audiences, requiring The Crickets to reconfigure their setlists to include more R&B cover versions to win over the crowd, as Holly's original material (except "That'll Be the Day") was not well received. "when it reached the Harlem Apollo, which had notoriously hostile audiences, the group had to reconfigure their sets, as the audiences didn't like any of Holly's original material, except that'll be the day." ← Episode 74： ＂It Doesn't Matter Any More＂ by Buddy Holly | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6unib5T2mDQ
**Early Performances and Later Success** : In their early days in New York, the Isley Brothers frequently "hung around" the Harlem Apollo and occasionally performed at its amateur night. Later, after securing agent Nat Nazarro, they returned to play the Apollo for $950 a week, an astonishing sum for teenagers in the late 1950s. "At first she was unsuccessful and the group just hung around the Harlem Apollo and occasionally performed at their amateur night." ← Episode 81： ＂Shout＂ by the Isley Brothers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=USJDERCABjk
**White Act Performances (Pre-1957)** : While The Crickets performed at the Harlem Apollo, they were not the first white act to do so; Bobby Darin had played there before them, and jazz drummer Buddy Rich possibly as well. "Some have said that the Crickets were the first white act to play the Apollo. That's not the case. Bobby Daring had played there before them, and I think so had the jazz drummer Buddy Rich and maybe one or two others." ← Episode 74： ＂It Doesn't Matter Any More＂ by Buddy Holly | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6unib5T2mDQ
