# concert - Chitlin' Circuit

## curiosities
**Performance Circuit for Black Acts and Audiences** : In 1956, rock and roll acts primarily played the Chitlin' Circuit, which was dedicated to black acts performing for black audiences, highlighting the segregated nature of the music industry at the time. "rock and roll acts played the Chipplin circuit, and that was black acts for black audiences." ← Episode 40： ＂Drugstore Rock 'n' Roll＂ by Janis Martin | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l0i2p9TelN0
**Tour Stops for Black Artists** : The Chitlin' Circuit was a derogatory but commonly used term referring to a network of clubs and theaters that served as regular tour destinations for nearly all major black artists of the era. Lucky Millinder's band, among others, occasionally toured this circuit.
