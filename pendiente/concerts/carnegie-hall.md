## curiosities
**Benny Goodman's Integrated Performance (1938)** : In 1938, Benny Goodman brought an integrated band, featuring prominent black musicians like Count Basie, Leste Jung, Teddy Wilson, and Lionel Hampton alongside white musicians, onto the stage of Carnegie Hall. At the time, this was the most prestigious music venue in the US, making the integrated performance a profoundly significant cultural event. "By 1938, Goodman actually reached the point where he was able to bring an integrated band, featuring Count Basie, Leste Jung, Teddy Wilson and Lionel Hampton. **Spiritual's to Swing Concerts** : Following the success of Goodman's show, John Hammond organized a series of "Spiritual's to Swing" concerts at Carnegie Hall. These concerts are considered some of the most important ever in terms of introducing black American music to white audiences.
