# concert - Louisiana Hayride

## curiosities
**Key Country Music Showcase** : The Louisiana Hayride was a renowned country music show that served as a significant platform for many aspiring artists, including Elvis Presley, to achieve widespread recognition. For white singers in Shreveport like Dale Hawkins, performing on the Hayride was considered the primary pathway to stardom. "The country show where Elvis, among many others, had made his name." ← Episode 63： ＂Susie Q＂, by Dale Hawkins | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4vqj1DcXOfw
**Mid-Tier in Country Music Hierarchy** : The Louisiana Hayride was positioned slightly lower than the Grand Ole Opry in the hierarchy of country and western live variety shows, serving as a platform for artists who were either rising or declining in their careers. "Slightly lower than the Opry were shows like the Louisiana Hayride." ← Episode 40： ＂Drugstore Rock 'n' Roll＂ by Janis Martin | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l0i2p9TelN0
**Platform for Elvis Presley and Johnny Cash** : The Louisiana Hayride was a country music radio show that served as a significant platform for artists to build their reputation. Elvis Presley used it to establish his career, and Johnny Cash subsequently secured a regular slot on the show, following a similar career path. "he was given a regular slot on the Louisiana Hayride, the country music radio show that Elvis had built his reputation on" ← Episode 37： ＂I Walk The Line＂ by Johnny Cash | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1uhw6KjgSME
**Platform for Rising and Falling Stars** : The Hayride booked acts who were not yet well-known enough for the Opry, as well as those who had been dropped by the Opry, such as Elvis Presley and Hank Williams, respectively. "The Hayride was for those who were on their way up or on their way down." ← Episode 40： ＂Drugstore Rock 'n' Roll＂ by Janis Martin | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l0i2p9TelN0
**Prestigious Radio Program** : The Louisiana Hayride was a prestigious radio program that Hank Williams joined in 1947 following the success of his song "Movito Nover." "el cual le permitió unirse al prestigioso programa de radio Luisana High's Ride." ← El genio roto del country： La leyenda de Hank Williams | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qczx7qWg4xM
**Refusal of Elvis's Recommendation (1956)** : Despite Elvis's personal recommendation to Buddy Holly that he would "definitely" be featured on the Louisiana Hayride, the radio station in Shreveport informed Holly and Sonny Curtis that Elvis did not control their show's bookings, leading to their unfulfilled trip. "Elvis had told him that the Louisiana Hayride would definitely have him on at Elvis's recommendation, but when he and Sonny Curtis drove to Shreveport, the radio station told him that it wasn't up to Elvis who got on their show and who didn't." ← Episode Sixty-One： ＂That'll Be the Day＂, by the Crickets | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KVJMKLc54O0
