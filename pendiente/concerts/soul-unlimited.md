# concert - Soul Unlimited

## curiosities
**Dick Clark's Competition to Soul Train (1973)** : This show was created by Dick Clark in 1973 and strategically alternated time slots with American Bandstand as a direct effort to capitalize on the popularity of Soul Train. Hosted by Buster Jones, it featured prominent artists such as Eddie Kendricks, Bill Withers, and Gladys Knight & The Pips. "a show called Soul Unlimited Alternated Time Slots with American Bandstand. And this was all because of Soul Train." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 47 - Dick Clark's Entertainment Empire | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Threat to Soul Train and Subsequent Cancellation** : Despite not being superior to Soul Train in quality, "Soul Unlimited" posed a "real threat" due to its backing by ABC, leveraging the immense power of network television at the time. This situation led to political leaders like Jesse Jackson threatening an ABC boycott. Ultimately, influential music and film producer Clarence Avant intervened, directly urging ABC producers to cancel the show to safeguard Soul Train and its host, Don Cornelius. "Nelson George, who wrote a history of Soul Train, it's called the hippest trip in America. He said that it posed a real threat to Soul Train." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 47 - Dick Clark's Entertainment Empire | https://www.ftr70.com/
