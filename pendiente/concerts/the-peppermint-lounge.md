# concert - The Peppermint Lounge

## curiosities
**Celebrity Hotspot** : The lounge, conveniently located near Broadway, became an unexpected celebrity hotspot. Broadway stars initially started dropping in, followed by other famous figures like Noel Coward, Judy Garland, Marilyn Monroe, Tallulah Bankhead, Norman Mailer (who danced the twist with Lord Beaverbrook's granddaughter), Tennessee Williams, and even Greta Garbo. These celebrities either danced to Joey D and the Starlighters or observed the younger patrons dancing. "The peppermint lounge became a sensation, and the stories all focused on the dance these people were doing." ← Episode 91： ＂The Twist＂ by Chubby Checker | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LzsS8EXdsm8
**Inaccurate Film Portrayal** : The film "Hey Let's Twist," starring Joey D and the Starlighters, inaccurately portrayed The Peppermint Lounge. Instead of its true identity as a mafia-owned bar, the movie depicted it as a family-run Italian restaurant. "in a film which portrayed the peppermint lounge as a family run Italian restaurant rather than a mafia run bar." ← Episode 91： ＂The Twist＂ by Chubby Checker | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LzsS8EXdsm8
**Jackie Kennedy Rumor** : There were persistent rumors, which later proved false, that First Lady Jackie Kennedy had visited The Peppermint Lounge. However, it was noted that she apparently did enjoy dancing the twist herself. "There were even rumours which turned out to be false that Jackie Kennedy had gone to the peppermint lounge, though she did apparently enjoy dancing the twist herself." ← Episode 91： ＂The Twist＂ by Chubby Checker | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LzsS8EXdsm8
**Manhattan Venue Description** : The Peppermint Lounge was described as a "tiny, dingy, bad smelling bar" in Manhattan, which had a capacity of approximately 200 people. It was also noted as being "Working Class Mafia-owned." "They played a three-day weekend at the CD Working Class Mafia-owned bar called The Peppermint Lounge in Manhattan." ← Episode 91： ＂The Twist＂ by Chubby Checker | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LzsS8EXdsm8
**New Jersey Fan Base** : The venue, despite its small size, was consistently packed during Joey D and the Starlighters' residency, primarily with their fans from New Jersey. Many of these patrons were 18 and 19-year-olds who traveled from New Jersey, where the legal drinking age was 21, to New York, where it was 18. "Soon the timey venue, which had a capacity of about 200 people, was packed, largely with the band's fans from New Jersey." ← Episode 91： ＂The Twist＂ by Chubby Checker | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LzsS8EXdsm8
**Publicity Stunt Success** : The owners of The Peppermint Lounge sought publicity for their club, hiring a publicist who enlisted Celebrity Services. This service aimed to attract minor celebrities, hoping for brief mentions in gossip columns. Unexpectedly, this effort generated enough intrigue to draw a wave of major celebrities, transforming the lounge into a sensation.
