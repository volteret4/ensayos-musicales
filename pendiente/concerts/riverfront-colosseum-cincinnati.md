# concert - Riverfront Colosseum (Cincinnati)

## curiosities
**Band's Post-Concert Awareness** : The Who, along with many others, did not become aware of the fatal incident at the entrance until after their concert had concluded. "Even the band did not realize what had happened until after it happened." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 58 - WKRP in Cincinnati | https://www.ftr70.com
**First Mass Concert Death in US** : Tim Reed, who portrayed Venus Flytrap on *WKRP*, described the Riverfront Colosseum tragedy as the first instance of mass death at a concert in American history. "It was the first mass death of the concert in American history like that." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 58 - WKRP in Cincinnati | https://www.ftr70.com
**Inspiration for WKRP Episode** : This tragic event directly influenced an episode of the *WKRP in Cincinnati* television show, as its creator believed it was crucial for a rock and roll station in Cincinnati to acknowledge and address such a significant local tragedy. "Given that this was literally a TV show about our Rock and Roll station in Cincinnati, it seems altogether appropriate that WKRP would address this in some way." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 58 - WKRP in Cincinnati | https://www.ftr70.com
**The Who Concert Tragedy (December 3, 1979)** : On December 3, 1979, 11 fans were fatally crushed while attempting to enter the Riverfront Colosseum for a Who concert in Cincinnati. This general admission event, known then as "Festival Seating," was already subject to safety concerns. "On December 3rd, 1979, 11 fans of the who were killed when they were crushed to death while waiting to enter the Riverfront Colosseum in Cincinnati to see a concert." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 58 - WKRP in Cincinnati | https://www.ftr70.com
