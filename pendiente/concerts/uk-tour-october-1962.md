# concert - UK Tour (October 1962)

## curiosities
**Gene Vincent's Performance Restrictions** : Gene Vincent, also on the tour, faced work permit problems that initially prevented him from performing on stage, relegating him to the role of compere. He later managed to sing "Be-Bop-A-Lula" from off-stage. "This time Vincent was allowed in the country but wasn't allowed to perform on stage, so he appeared only as the compare, at least at the start of the tour." ← Episode 54： Keep A Knockin | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oA4Y_K9E5Y4
**Little Richard's Dramatic Stage Re-Entry** : For his rock and roll comeback, Little Richard initiated his set in complete darkness, with the band vamping for a full minute, before spotlights dramatically illuminated him in an all-white suit, signaling his explosive return to "Long Tall Sally." "When Richard came out on stage he spent a whole minute in pitch darkness with the band vamping before the spotlights suddenly picked him out in an all white suit and he launched into long tall sally." ← Episode 54： Keep A Knockin | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oA4Y_K9E5Y4
**Promoter's Misleading Advertising** : Promoter Don Arden advertised Little Richard's 1962 UK tour as his "first rock and roll tour in five years," despite Richard's prior agreement to perform exclusively gospel music. This discrepancy led to audience disappointment when Richard appeared in white robes singing gospel songs. "Don Arden, the promoter, had been promoting it as Richard's first rock and roll tour in five years and the audience were very far from impressed when Richard came on stage in flowing white robes and started singing peace in the valley and other gospel songs." ← Episode 54： Keep A Knockin | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oA4Y_K9E5Y4
**Sam Cooke's Influence on Richard's Rock and Roll Comeback** : Sam Cooke, the second-billed star, delivered an "incendiary performance" of "Twistin' the Night Away," which, coupled with Little Richard's inherent competitiveness and a desire not to be upstaged, served as the "final straw" that compelled Richard to abandon his gospel set and electrify the audience with rock and roll. "Cook was at the height of his success at this point and he was an astonishing live performer and so when he went out on stage and closed the first half including an insendiary performance of twist in the night away that left the audience applauding through the intermission Richard knew he had to up his game." ← Episode 54： Keep A Knockin | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oA4Y_K9E5Y4
**Wild and Unpredictable Stage Antics** : The tour saw Little Richard becoming increasingly "wilder and more frantic" on stage, a release of five years of pent-up rock and roll energy. His performances included throwing most of his clothes into the audience, jumping on the piano, and even faking his own death mid-song before abruptly reviving to sing "Tutti Frutti," all designed to create maximum audience attention.
