**"Rave" as a Pejorative Term** : Due to the commercialization of the scene by the music industry, which started mass-producing diluted tracks on white labels, and the changing demographics of attendees, the word "Reif" (rave) began to acquire a pejorative connotation. **Dilution of Rave Spirit** : The initial utopian and communal spirit of the rave scene began to dissipate as an increasing number of young people, drawn merely by the party atmosphere, started attending. This influx included dealers and gangs who sought higher BPMs and more aggressive sounds like Break Bichar corei (Breakbeat hardcore), moving away from the minimalist techno of Detroit and the acid house of Chicago. **UK Rave Scene in 1991-1992** : In 1991 and 1992, the rave scene in the United Kingdom transformed from a niche subculture into a widespread mass phenomenon. Illegal parties grew significantly in both size and reach, prompting sensationalist press to publish articles alarming society about the perceived dangers of these events.
