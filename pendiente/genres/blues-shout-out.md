# genre - Blues Shout-out

## curiosities
**Decline with Big Bands** : This style of music largely went out of fashion with the decline of big bands. As technology advanced and amplification improved, the necessity for such raw vocal power diminished, leading to the evolution of vocal performance styles. **Unamplified Vocal Power** : A blues shout-out was a singer, typically male, who possessed the ability to sing loudly and powerfully enough to be heard clearly over a band without any electronic amplification. This skill was crucial in the early 20th century before reliable microphones and powerful amplification became widespread, enabling singers to compete with the increasing volume of larger bands.
