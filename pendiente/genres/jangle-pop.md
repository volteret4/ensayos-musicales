# genre - Jangle Pop

## curiosities
**Influential Sound** : The combination of jangly, reverb-heavy, treble-focused guitars and Everly Brothers-inspired harmonies directly influenced bands like The Byrds, Love, Big Star, Tom Petty, R.E.M., The Smiths, and The Bangles. "That combination of jangling, reverb, heavy trebligh guitars, and definitely brothers inspired harmonies is one that leads directly to the birds, love, big star, Tom Petty, R-E-M, the smiths, and the bangles, among many others." ← Episode 113： ＂Needles and Pins＂ by The Searchers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=izC9ZBI0UHM
**Invention by The Searchers' "Needles and Pins"** : Jangle Pop was inadvertently invented by The Searchers' arrangement of "Needles and Pins," which featured two electric guitars playing a riff simultaneously with reverb, creating an electric 12-string sound. "That record is the root of Django Pop and Folk Rock." ← Episode 113： ＂Needles and Pins＂ by The Searchers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=izC9ZBI0UHM
