# genre - Dungeon Synth

## curiosities
**"Bubble Burst" in the 2000s and Revival in the 2010s** : After expanding across Europe in the 1990s, the Dungeon Synth scene experienced a decline in the 2000s, with very few projects releasing new material due to lack of widespread interest and a clear genre label. This led to a period of obscurity and near-forgetfulness until Andrew Werner's blog in 2011 sparked a renewed interest, causing its popularity to skyrocket. **Evolution and Diversification (2010s onwards)** : Initially following the dark aesthetic of predecessors like Mortiis, Dungeon Synth gradually matured and solidified as its own genre, moving away from its Black Metal roots. It opened up to less dark, more overtly medieval sounds, integrating elements from retro video games and internet culture, even finding common ground with Vaporwave. This led to the emergence of projects offering different visions, from medieval baroque (Sorcerian Myth, Ophif) to enveloping ambient (Old Sorcery, Erang), and synthwave/retro gaming aesthetics (Questmaster). "Poco a poco, el dan Jon Synth comenzó a lejarse de sus raíces en el Black Metal, madurando y consolidándose como un generoso lido, con una estética propia cada vez más definida." ← Dungeon Synth： El género más misterioso de internet | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CBIHO0ihIxQ
**Expansion through Bandcamp and YouTube** : Bandcamp became the primary distribution platform for Dungeon Synth, facilitating its ramification into numerous micro-genres (with over 50 variations documented). Later, YouTube channels like "The Dungeon Synth Archive" further democratized access, making hundreds of previously niche albums available to a much broader audience and allowing the genre to appear directly in users' feeds through algorithms. **Influence from RPG Soundtracks** : The unique aesthetic of early RPG soundtracks, born from the technical necessity of emulating medieval sounds with limited instruments, directly influenced Dungeon Synth. A key example is Burzum's two albums recorded in prison, which, due to a lack of access to instruments, ended up sounding almost like an RPG soundtrack, marking the genre's second wave. **Influences from Fantasy and Role-Playing Games** : The genre is deeply influenced by fantasy novels and role-playing games that its creators enjoyed in their youth, such as "The Lord of the Rings" and "Dungeons & Dragons." Many Dungeon Synth albums are conceived as unofficial soundtracks for stories of dragons, goblins, and abandoned castles, making it a highly narrative genre often favored by role-playing gamers. **Origin and Naming by Andrew Werner (2011)** : The genre officially received its name, Dungeon Synth, in 2011 when enthusiast Andrew Werner collected disparate recordings on a blog. He defined it as "the sound of an ancient crypt, the breath of the tomb, that can only be adequately transmitted with primitive, black, low-fi music, forgotten, dark and ignored by mainstream society." This act created a manifesto and expanded the musical scene. "En su primer post lo define así, el Dungeon Synth es el sonido de una criptantigua, el aliento de la tumba, que solo puede transmitirse adecuadamente con música primitiva, negro, low fey, olvidada, oscura e ignorada por la sociedad dominante." ← Dungeon Synth： El género más misterioso de internet | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CBIHO0ihIxQ
**Reinterpretation of Medieval Fantasy** : Dungeon Synth, which originally aimed to recreate medieval fantasy atmospheres, has acquired a new interpretation on the internet. This reinterpretation moves beyond merely imitating a sound, instead seeking to evoke non-existent times and worlds, thus aligning with the principles of hauntology. "el dungeon synth, que en sus orígenes buscaba recrear atmósferas de fantasía medieval, pero que el internet ha tenido una nueva lectura que va más allá a limitar un sonido, sino que busque a bocar tiempos y mundos que no existieron." ← El Género Musical Más Escalofriante de Internet. **Resurgence and Diversification since Early 90s** : Dungeon Synth, a genre that emerged in the early 1990s, has seen a dramatic increase in popularity in recent years and has begun to branch into various subgenres. Some of these subgenres, such as Medieval Ambient, have developed their own distinct identities, moving beyond the main genre while retaining its core essence. **Roots in Black Metal Instrumentals (Late 1980s / Early 1990s, Norway)** : Dungeon Synth emerged from the extreme and dark Black Metal scene, particularly from the instrumental passages in albums by bands like Mayhem, Darkthrone, and Emperor, which sought to add an epic and atmospheric dimension to their work. These instrumental interludes, notably in Burzum's early records and especially his prison albums, gained more prominence and eventually led to dedicated synthesizer-based projects.
