# genre - Medieval Profane Music

## curiosities
**Characteristics of Profane Music** : Medieval profane music was the music of the street, the tavern, and the courts. It did not adhere to the strict rules of the church, allowing for greater rhythmic and melodic freedom. This secular nature made it more accessible and relatable to the common populace. **Nature and Purpose of Medieval Profane Music** : Alongside religious music, there was a rich tradition of profane music created by troubadours and jongleurs, who sang about themes of love, satire, or war. This tradition, being more free and earthly, profoundly inspired how the modern world and video games later imagined and interpreted medieval sounds. **Preservation of Medieval Music Sources** : Although it is impossible to know with certainty how medieval music truly sounded due to the obvious lack of audio recordings, historical sources like manuscripts, scores, treatises, and even period instruments have been preserved. These artifacts offer invaluable clues for scholars and performers to reconstruct and interpret this ancient music. **Troubadours and Jongleurs as Performers of Profane Music** : Troubadours were primarily composers who wrote songs about courtly love, chivalric deeds, and social satires. Jongleurs, on the other hand, were itinerant musicians who brought this music to the people, performing it with instruments such as lutes, fiddles, wooden flutes, psalteries, or drums. This closer, more rhythmic, and earthly music is what today is associated with popular medieval imagery, evoking banquets, journeys, and adventures. "Los trobadores escribían canciones sobre el amor cortes, a dañas cada balleresca, a sus sátiras sociales.
