# genre - Country-politin

## curiosities
**Emergence and Characteristics** : Country-politin, also known as the Nashville sound, emerged in the 1960s as a style that removed traditional country elements like banjo and fiddle, instead incorporating choirs, orchestras, amplified instruments, and sophisticated arrangements with adult lyrical themes. It was geared towards middle-class adults who found rock and roll too aggressive but disliked "twangy old school hillbilly country-western," seeking relatable stories about love and work. "What do you get when you take the banjo and the fiddle out of country music and you add in a choir and or an orchestra?
