# genre - Ambient House

## curiosities
**Pioneered by KLSE and The Orp** : This genre, along with the concept of "chill-out rooms," was initiated by artists like KLSE (KLF) and The Orp (The Orb), offering more atmospheric and relaxed passages within electronic music. However, these spaces were still largely considered extensions of the dance floor, providing areas to de-stress, recharge, converse, and reconnect between high-energy moments. "incluso los pasajes más atmosféricos del Ambient House o las chill-out rooms que habían iniciado KLSE o The Orp, sigan siendo extensiones de la pista." ← Qué fue el IDM y por qué cambió la música electrónica | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RTwn3hP1XTg
**Popularity and Evolution in the 90s** : Ambient House rapidly gained popularity in the early to mid-1990s, evolving from dance music and giving rise to other subgenres such as Ambient Techno and Ambient Trance. During this period, the genre began to diversify, incorporating external influences beyond rave culture, including instrumental hip-hop, Dub, World, and Downtempo. "El Ambient House se volvió muy popular rápidamente, derivando a otros géneros que también tienen su origen en la música de baile, como el Ambient Techno o el Ambient Trance." ← Chill Out： La pausa que necesitaba la electrónica | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VPOAA3ZaA9s
**Precursor to Chill Out** : The term "Ambient House" was initially used by Jimmy Cauty and Bill Drummond (The KLF) to describe their productions, which were inspired by the relaxing sessions in early rave rest areas like "The White Room." This term quickly gained popularity, eventually deriving into other genres, and served as a direct precursor to the "Chill Out" genre as it became widely known.
