# genre - Muzak

## curiosities
**Association with Technology and Predictability** : With the advent of synthesizers and electronic music, the concept of Muzak became even more popularized as "elevator music." The inherent "cold" quality of computer-generated music and the absence of human interpretation aligned perfectly with Muzak's standardized and predictable nature, further reinforcing its image as artificial and uniform sound in the digital age. "El elemento frío que aporta la música por computadora y el hecho de prescindir del componente humano en interpretación encajaron de manera peculiar con la naturaleza estandalizada y predecible del Muzak." ← Muzak： Música para ser ignorada | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7iN6WiB5Y1g
**Core Element of Utopian Virtual and Mallsoft** : Corporate Muzak is a central focus for the Utopian Virtual subgenre, which seeks to evoke the aesthetic of 80s and 90s commercial electronic music. Mallsoft is explicitly described as "the Muzak of Vaporwave," heavily utilizing background music associated with shopping centers. "centrado en la estética y el sonido de la música electrónica comercial de los años 80 y 90, especialmente al muza corporativo." ← Vaporwave： Mucho más que un Meme | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BhOnKc0SxsA
**Función Empresarial** : Es la famosa "música de extensor" (música de ascensor), utilizada para aumentar la productividad de las empresas. Deriva directamente del Easy Listening. "la famosa música de extensor, la cual se usaba para aumentar la productividad de las empresas." ← Así sonaba el pop de la era atómica | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LqPX20XSa_s
**Influence on Early Vaporwave** : Muzak, along with predefined MIDI digital timbres, contributed to the sound of James Ferraro's influential early album *Far Side Virtual*. "Este álbum usá menos samplers y tiene un sonido más cercano al muzak y a los timbres digitales predifinidos que se suelen asociar con el midi." ← Vaporwave： Mucho más que un Meme | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BhOnKc0SxsA
**Origin as a Corporate Strategy** : The company Muzak, initially Guárez Radio, pivoted from home electrical wiring music distribution to specializing in meticulously programmed background music for commercial spaces. This music, often composed anonymously and devoid of expressive human elements, was designed in laboratories for specific functions, such as influencing productivity or consumer behavior, deliberately avoiding the complexities and demands of "real" music. "Más con una manifestación auténtica de la expresión artística, el Muzak se rige como una copia hyperrealista, una proyección idealizada y neutra de la música, como los son los parques temáticos de los lugares que representan." ← Muzak： Música para ser ignorada | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7iN6WiB5Y1g
**Progressive Stimuli Concept (1950s)** : In the 1950s, as the Muzak company expanded into diverse venues like medical offices, hair salons, shopping centers, and factories, it introduced the concept of "estímulos progresivos." This involved a series of music blocks, typically 15 minutes long, with gradually increasing intensity, followed by 15 minutes of silence, aimed at boosting worker productivity and stimulating consumerism. "Una serie de música diseñada para aumentar la productividad de los trabajadores y el consumismo entre los clientes, que consistían elevar gradualmente la intensidad de la música." ← Muzak： Música para ser ignorada | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7iN6WiB5Y1g
**Ubiquity in Modern Media** : The term "Muzak" has expanded beyond its original corporate context, commonly heard in documentaries, educational videos, corporate presentations, and even video games. The adoption of the CD format for game composers facilitated the use of "imperceptible synthesized sounds," making it an ideal choice for background music in games lacking a strong narrative. "El término Muzak se estendió y ya me da raro o irlo en documentales, vídeos educativos o corporativos." ← Muzak： Música para ser ignorada | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7iN6WiB5Y1g
