# genre - British Rock and Roll

## curiosities
**Clean-Cut and Mainstream from Inception** : Unlike American rock and roll, early British rock and roll started as a clean-cut, mainstream show business product, with little black influence or sexuality, aimed directly at teenagers, rather than emerging from an underclass or minority group. "British rock and roll started out at the point that American rock and roll was only just starting to get to, a clean-cut version of the music, with little black influence or sexuality left in it, designed from the outset to be a part of mainstream show business aimed at teenagers." ← Episode 48： ＂Rock With the Caveman＂ by Tommy Steele | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=scDLm48u2yc
**Cocknified American Style** : Tommy Steele's "overly manored, musical inspired, cocknified impression of an American singing style" is identified as the birth of a particularly English, and specifically London, take on rock and roll, influencing later artists like David Bowie, Anthony Newley, and Ray Davies. "here was the birth of something particularly English and indeed something particularly London in rock and roll music. The overly manored, musical inspired, cocknified impression of an American singing style." ← Episode 48： ＂Rock With the Caveman＂ by Tommy Steele | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=scDLm48u2yc
**Lack of Cultural Roots** : The first wave of British rock and roll was fundamentally an imitation of American music, lacking the deep cultural and social issue ties that characterized its American counterpart. "it was essentially a music that had no roots in the culture." ← Episode 48： ＂Rock With the Caveman＂ by Tommy Steele | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=scDLm48u2yc
**Rapid National Exposure** : Due to Britain's national media structure (unlike America's local media), an artist could become known throughout the country with the right media exposure, sometimes before their second gig. "In Britain, someone could become known throughout the country before they played their second gig, if they got the right media exposure." ← Episode 48： ＂Rock With the Caveman＂ by Tommy Steele | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=scDLm48u2yc
