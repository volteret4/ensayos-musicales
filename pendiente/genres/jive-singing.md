# genre - Jive singing

## curiosities
**Tradition and Characteristics** : Jive singing, a tradition dating back to the 1920s with artists like Cab Calloway, emphasized personality over technical vocal prowess. Jive singers were "hip, and they often used made up words of their own," being "clever, and funny, and sophisticated." Their lyrical themes often included the underworld, drug use, prostitution, or nonsense words. "Jive Singers weren't usually technically great, but they had personality.
