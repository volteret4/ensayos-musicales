# genre - Latin Jazz

## curiosities
**1950s US Boom** : In the 1950s, the United States experienced a significant boom in Latin music. Major record labels adopted traditional elements from Cuba, Colombia, Mexico, and Brazil—such as cumbia, chachacha, tangos, mambo, and samba—and filtered them through the prevailing "Big Band" aesthetic of the era. This unique blend of reimagined and idealized genres and cultures by North Americans ultimately gave rise to the phenomenon known as the Latin Jazz boom.
