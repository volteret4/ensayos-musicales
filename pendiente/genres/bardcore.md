# genre - Bardcore

## curiosities
**Concept and Covered Artists** : Bardcore is a simple concept: it involves covering current songs as if they had been made in the Middle Ages, using medieval instruments and sounds. Artists such as Hildegard von Blingin' and Algal the Bard have created medievalized versions of songs by diverse artists like Eminem, Dolly Parton, or Radiohead. "El barco es muy simple. Se trata de versionar canciones actuales como si hubieran sido hechas en la edad media. El barco es como pide el de barco, quid al garbón, blingingín o algal de barc, han versionado canciones de artistas andispares como minem, dollypartón o radiohead, asonidos instrumentos medievales." ← Por qué todo suena medieval en 2025？ (Dungeon Synth, Bardcore...) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FDNQdBRktSA
**Emergence during the Pandemic** : Bardcore emerged during the pandemic, initially as a musical meme and later evolving into a widely popular phenomenon. It involves re-versioning current songs as if they were created in the Middle Ages, performed with medieval sounds and instruments. **Meme Origin and Pandemic Popularity** : Bardcore began as a joke or a musical meme. The phenomenon became very popular and spread rapidly across social media, possibly because the pandemic era evoked memories of the Black Death. This blend of humor and anachronism deeply connected with millions of people. "El barco empezó como un chiste, como un bebé musical. Y no sé si es que la pandemia que nos tocó vivir recordaba la peste negra, pero lo cierto es que el fenómeno se volvió muy popular y se extendió rápidamente por redes." ← Por qué todo suena medieval en 2025？ (Dungeon Synth, Bardcore...) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FDNQdBRktSA
**Visual Aesthetics** : Each Bardcore cover is often accompanied by visuals, such as illuminated manuscripts or medieval paintings, digitally manipulated with Photoshop. These visuals enhance the illusion, making it seem as though the music could genuinely have originated in the 14th century, further contributing to the shared medievalized aesthetic on the internet.
