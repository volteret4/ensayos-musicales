# genre - Girl Group

## curiosities
**Lyrical Innovation by Gerry Goffin** : With "Will You Love Me Tomorrow," Gerry Goffin found a lyrical formula that would define girl groups from that point on, introducing a kind of "female adolescent emotion" that had previously been absent from pop music. The lyrics from a woman's perspective worrying about a one-night stand resonated with millions. "Goffin's initial dislike of rock and roll music had been because of what he perceived as his lyrical vacuity, and in Will you love me tomorrow, he found a lyrical formula that would define girl groups from that point on." ← Episode 89： ＂Will You Love Me Tomorrow？＂ by the Shirelles | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Kns2HGcuSg
**New York Phenomenon in Early Years** : In their early years, girl groups were largely a phenomenon based in New York. The Shirelles, though from Pasey, New Jersey, were part of this regional trend. "girl groups in the early years were largely phenomenon based in New York and that's more or less the case with the Chirrells who didn't come from New York itself but from Pasey, New Jersey about 16 miles away." ← Episode 89： ＂Will You Love Me Tomorrow？＂ by the Shirelles | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Kns2HGcuSg
**Setting the Template for Success** : The Shirelles are credited with being the most successful of the early girl groups and, more than any other, set the template for all subsequent girl groups, influencing major acts like The Supremes, The Marvelettes, The Crystals, and The Ronettes.
