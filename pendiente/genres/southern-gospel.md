# genre - Southern Gospel

## curiosities
**Anti-Semitic Verse in "Jesus' Work That Loans and Valley"** : The Million Dollar Quartet's take on the Southern Gospel song "Jesus' Work That Loans and Valley" includes a verse about John the Baptist that could be interpreted as mildly anti-Semitic, though not considered hate speech. This detail is flagged as a warning for listeners. "Most interesting is their take on Jesus' work that loans and valley, which unfortunately includes a verse about John the Baptist that could be interpreted as mildly anti-Semitic." ← Episode 51： ＂Matchbox＂ by Carl Perkins | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0Nrjr0AGd3U
**Defined by Demographics and Tradition** : Southern Gospel is described as gospel music performed primarily by white people from the Deep South, distinct from gospel music primarily performed by Black people. It was a significant part of the Million Dollar Quartet session. "The Gospel music performed primarily by white people from the deep south, rather than the Gospel music primarily performed by black people." ← Episode 51： ＂Matchbox＂ by Carl Perkins | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0Nrjr0AGd3U
**Euphemism for White Gospel** : Southern Gospel is noted as a euphemistic term for what is essentially white gospel music. Johnny Cash initially performed this style of music. "This was the music that is euphemistically called Southern Gospel, but which is really white gospel." ← Episode 37： ＂I Walk The Line＂ by Johnny Cash | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1uhw6KjgSME
**Religious Roots of Musicians** : Both Elvis Presley and Jerry Lee Lewis were raised in the Assembly of God, a Pentecostal holy roller church, which heavily influenced their shared religious culture and their performance of Southern Gospel music during the Million Dollar Quartet session. "Both Elvis and Jerry Lee were brought up in the assembly of God, a Pentecostal holy roller church, which we'll talk more about when we get to an episode on Jerry Lee himself." ← Episode 51： ＂Matchbox＂ by Carl Perkins | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0Nrjr0AGd3U
