# genre - EBM

## curiosities
**Defining Sound of "Música Máquina" and Spook Factory** : EBM (Electronic Body Music) was a key genre that helped define the "música máquina" sound of the Ruta, particularly at clubs like Espiral, which was known for its hard-hitting music. At Spook Factory, DJs Fran Lenaers and Tete explicitly moved the musical balance towards sounds closer to techno and EBM, citing Front 242 and Nitzer Ebb as examples. "Su sonido era uno de los más duros de la ruta, e incluía generos como el FM, el Dark Waze, or Noid Diswell, los cuales irían definiendo el sonido de la música máquina." ← Cuando Valencia fue la capital mundial del Clubbing | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ckJgog8P_og
**Emergence in Megamixes (Post-Italo Disco)** : Following the cultural shift brought by "La Ruta y el Sonido Valencia," the Megamix phenomenon started incorporating faster and stronger musical styles. EBM (Electronic Body Music), along with Acid House and New It, were among the first new genres to dominate these compilations, moving beyond the traditional Italo Disco sound. "Asid House, New It y EBM al principio y máquina y OroDens más adelante pasaron a dominar esos recopilatorios." ← Ritmo y Extorsión： Así Dominaron los Megamix los 90s | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-qekjKky5bE
**Pre-existing in Europe** : Electronic Body Music (EBM) was already an established genre in Germany and Belgium prior to the major influx of techno and house music from the United States. This highlights Europe's existing, non-novel relationship with electronic music. "En Europa, la electrónica no era ninguna novedad, en Alemania y Béjica ya existía al EVM..." ← La gran mentira detrás del Eurodance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_F34L8wfrdo
