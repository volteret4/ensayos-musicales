**Definition by Joshua Clover** : Author and UC Davis English professor Joshua Clover described a novelty song as one with "something very exaggerated in its differences or difference from other songs of his genre," usually pop, but sometimes country or rock. This difference, he notes, dominates the song rather than merely distinguishing it. **Humor as an Element** : Humor is considered an important element, with songs being successful if funny or entertaining enough. **Musically Interesting as a Requirement** : Jim Stafford emphasized that for a novelty song to "have legs" and be heard repeatedly, the music itself must be interesting enough to support the lyrics. This implies that while the lyrics are often central to the novelty, the musical quality contributes to its longevity.
