# genre - Hip Hop Español

## curiosities
**Anti-System and Anti-Commercial Identity (90s-2000s)** : In the 90s, Spanish hip-hop developed canons that seemed immovable in the 2000s. Lacking the African-American roots of its US counterpart, it found its identity in an anti-system and anti-commercial stance, emphasizing poetry, social messaging, and boom-bap production. "El gijó Pespañol, al no tener las mismas raíces afraamericanas del estadounidense, buscó su identidad en el antisistema y la anticomercial." ← La salvaje historia del TRAP ESPAÑOL (2013-2026) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4hBhiU5jj6s
**Rejection of Ostentation** : This style of Spanish hip-hop, exemplified by artists like Nach and ToteKing, largely rejected the "swag" and ostentatious attitude often associated with West Coast rap, viewing it as an imported sign of extravagance. "Era visto como un signo de ostentación importado." ← La salvaje historia del TRAP ESPAÑOL (2013-2026) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4hBhiU5jj6s
