# genre - Freeform Radio

## curiosities
**Early Focus on Creativity** : In its initial phase, underground FM stations operating under the freeform model prioritized creativity over ratings, granting disc jockeys the autonomy to program their own music, leading to unique and experimental broadcasts. "Ratings were not really a major concern for those underground FM stations. The focus was much more on creativity." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 58 - WKRP in Cincinnati | https://www.ftr70.com
**Emergence and Purpose** : Freeform radio came into existence in the late 1960s and early 1970s following a U.S. law that prevented stations with both AM and FM frequencies from merely duplicating their AM programming on the FM band, as AM was then dominant. To fill the FM airwaves, disc jockeys often turned to rock music, particularly progressive rock, allowing them to play entire album sides. "Freeform radio came into existence in the late 60s and early 70s when the law was passed in the United States that stations with both AM and FM could not simply use FM to duplicate whatever they were playing on AM because AM was king at this time." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 58 - WKRP in Cincinnati | https://www.ftr70.com
**Shift Towards Commercialism** : Over time, freeform radio evolved as FM stations increasingly focused on ratings, profit, and demographic targeting (age, race, gender) to attract advertisers, leading to greater control over the music selection by DJs. "It would not take long for that to change as FM stations became much more interested in ratings, profit, and then controlling what DJs played. FM radio became very focused on demographics, age, race, gender, so that advertising could be more targeted to them." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 58 - WKRP in Cincinnati | https://www.ftr70.com
