# genre - Eurodance

## curiosities
**"Beauty and the Beast" Vocal Structure** : The distinctive Eurodance vocal structure, pairing a melodic female singer for the choruses with a rapper for the verses, was directly influenced by Hip House. This subgenre from Chicago had already established the practice of blending female soul vocals with rap phrasing. "Esta estructura de la bella en la bestia vocal estaba directamente influenciada por el Hip House, un subgenero de Chicago que ya mezclaba voces femeninas de Soul, con fraseos de rap." ← La gran mentira detrás del Eurodance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_F34L8wfrdo
**Commercial and Peculiar Phenomenon (1990s)** : Eurodance emerged in Europe during the 1990s as one of the most commercial and peculiar musical phenomena. Often labeled "cheesy" and criticized, its creators frequently did not appear in their own music videos. It transitioned from underground club music to widespread radio and television play, profoundly influencing a generation and a continent. "Esta es la historia de LauroDense." ← La gran mentira detrás del Eurodance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_F34L8wfrdo
**Commercialization of Music in the 90s** : In the 1990s, Eurodance became part of a shift towards more commercial music played by DJs on the Ruta. This change reflected a new audience that prioritized pure party atmosphere and hypnotic, trance-like rhythms over the avant-garde experimentation and musical sophistication of the 80s. "Los nuevos DJs empezaron a abrir a la música más comercial, como LeuroDens y Eldens, la experimentación y la inclusión de los 80 había dado paso a la fiesta en la más salvaje de sus expresiones." ← Cuando Valencia fue la capital mundial del Clubbing | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ckJgog8P_og
**Common Musical Structure** : Eurodance projects adhered to a common musical template: a 4x4 house beat, a prominent melody often played on piano or synthesizer, and a pop song structure. The vocal arrangement typically featured a melodic female singer for choruses and a rapper (usually male, often in English) for the verses. Lyrics were generally uncomplicated, with divas singing optimistic themes of love, desire, or party, while rappers delivered messages of self-affirmation or confident attitudes. "Una base 4x4, jauseras. Una melodía, ya sea de piano de sinte. Todo ello con una estructura pop, y lo básico. Una cantante melodica para los estribillos. Y un rapero para las estrofas, el 99% de los casos en inglés." ← La gran mentira detrás del Eurodance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_F34L8wfrdo
**Contribution to Hip Hop Popularization** : Through its widespread commercial success and the strategic incorporation of rap verses, Eurodance inadvertently played a role in popularizing Hip Hop across the European continent. "Y en cierto modo, el Eurodance, ayuda a la popularización del Hip Hop en el viejo continente." ← La gran mentira detrás del Eurodance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_F34L8wfrdo
**Decline and "Death" (New Millennium)** : With the advent of the new millennium, Eurodance effectively came to an end. Its demise was not caused by the feared Y2K bug, but rather by the formula having been so thoroughly exploited that the public simply grew tired of it. Consequently, success charts began to feature other types of music, and channels like MTV abandoned the genre, embracing American hip-hop, nu-metal, and pop artists such as Britney Spears and the Backstreet Boys. "Cuando llegó el nuevo millennio, el temido afectó 2000 no acabó con los ordenadores, pero sí que en Ikilal Eurodance. La fórmula se había exprimido tanto que el público, sencillamente, se acabó cansando." ← La gran mentira detrás del Eurodance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_F34L8wfrdo
**Early Video Aesthetics (Early 1990s)** : In its initial phase (1990-1993), Eurodance music videos shared a very specific aesthetic. They typically featured dark color palettes, with a prevalence of brown tones, sepia, or even stark black and white. This visual style clearly reflected the producers' origins in "cold" electronic scenes like EBM and Newbeat, often incorporating dark and gothic themes, including a noticeable fascination with vampires. "En esta primera etapa, los vídeos compartían una estética muy concreta. Por lo general, tenían una palita de color y soscura, con predominancia de los tonos marrones. El sepia o directamente el blanco en negro." ← La gran mentira detrás del Eurodance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_F34L8wfrdo
**European-Exclusive Code** : Eurodance largely functioned as an exclusively European musical phenomenon. Outside of the continent, its sound and aesthetic proved challenging for international audiences to comprehend or fully embrace. Its distinctive aesthetic became so deeply embedded in European cultural DNA that it significantly influenced the Eurovision Song Contest, which, even today, is seen as a showcase for the genre's "cheesiest" elements. "El Eurodance era un código astrictamente europeo. Cuara de nuestras fronteras, costaba mucho de entender." ← La gran mentira detrás del Eurodance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_F34L8wfrdo
**Evocative Power and Nostalgia** : Despite being formulaic and explicitly designed for commercial success, the genuine magic of Eurodance resides in its profound evocative power. It is capable of transporting listeners back to vivid past memories, such as a childhood school trip party, revealing its deeper impact years after its initial popularity. "Sí, en la música fórmula y cadiseñada para vender. Pero al escucharla tras año sin hacerlo, te das cuenta que su verdadera magia está en su poder evocador." ← La gran mentira detrás del Eurodance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_F34L8wfrdo
**Final Phase and Self-Parody** : In its concluding phase, Eurodance ceased to take itself seriously, if it ever truly had. This period witnessed the emergence of highly exaggerated and kitsch acts across the continent, many of which became "meme fodder." "En su recta final, el Eurodance ya no se tomaba en serio a sí mismo. Si es alguna vez se la había tomado." ← La gran mentira detrás del Eurodance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_F34L8wfrdo
**Late-Decade Visual Aesthetic (Late 1990s)** : As the new millennium approached, Eurodance's visual identity significantly evolved to embrace futuristic themes, computer-generated imagery (CGI), blue and metallic color schemes, "Y2K" hairstyles, and shiny plastic clothing. This aesthetic became deliberately kitsch and grew increasingly exaggerated over time. "El cambio de milenio se acercaba. Y esto se notó especialmente en el apartado visual. Escenario es futurista, CGI, tonos azules y metálicos, peinados 2 mileros, ropas de plástico brillante. Todo se volvió kiche a propósito." ← La gran mentira detrás del Eurodance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_F34L8wfrdo
**Mid-Decade Evolution (Mid-1990s)** : By the mid-1990s, classic Eurodance began to show signs of decline and started incorporating influences from more accelerated European club scenes. It absorbed elements from genres like Happy Hardcore, Trance, Italo Dance, and "Máquina." This led to the creation of new subgenres such as Eurotrance and Bavarian Eurodance, all while retaining the core spirit of catchy melodies, 4x4 beats, and a strong commercial drive. "El Eurodance clásico ya estaba mostrando signos de debilidad. Así que empezó a recibir influencias de otros géneros, como el japi hardcore, pel trance o géneros más del sur, como elitalo dense o la máquina." ← La gran mentira detrás del Eurodance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_F34L8wfrdo
**Music for Mass Dancing** : Unlike other 90s genres such as Britpop, Grunge, or Trip-Hop, which catered to intimate, solitary listening, Eurodance was specifically designed for mass dancing and shared experiences. It became a ubiquitous sound at birthdays, popular festivals, holidays, and was heavily promoted by television channels like MTV, Viva, and VH1. "Esta música estaba hecha para bilar en masa, para ser compartida." ← La gran mentira detrás del Eurodance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_F34L8wfrdo
**Philosophical Legacy** : Eurodance imparted the lesson that music doesn't necessarily need to be life-altering or convey a profound, transcendental message. Despite its formulaic and commercially designed nature, its true magic lies in its evocative power, capable of inspiring people to dance and sing enthusiastically at any party. "El Eurodance nos enseñó que la música no tiene porque salvarte la vida ni tener un mensaje transcendental." ← La gran mentira detrás del Eurodance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_F34L8wfrdo
**Production Quality and BPM Increase (Mid-Late 1990s)** : The market became saturated with Eurodance compilation albums, frequently featuring overproduced music whose quality was increasingly questionable, although some popular tracks still managed to find a place in people's hearts. During this period, tempos accelerated dramatically, reaching 140 to 150 BPM. Synthetic basslines became more aggressive, and songs were filled with climactic buildups and studio effects like flanger or vocoder, often used to "smurf" (pitch-shift) vocals. "Los tiempos se dispararon hasta 140 a 150 BPMs. Los bajos sintéticos se volvieron más agresivos. Y las canciones se llenaron de subidones y efectos de estudio como el flanger o el bocoder para pitufar las voces." ← La gran mentira detrás del Eurodance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_F34L8wfrdo
**Resurrection in the US (A Decade Later)** : Approximately a decade after its apparent "death" in Europe, the spirit of Eurodance unexpectedly resurfaced in the United States. Artists like David Guetta and Calvin Harris, along with those from "black music" backgrounds such as Black Eyed Peas and Rihanna, achieved number-one hits by adopting the same fundamental Eurodance formula. "O no, se avanzamos una década a suspirito y resocitó en el lugar más inesperado. Estados Unidos." ← La gran mentira detrás del Eurodance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_F34L8wfrdo
