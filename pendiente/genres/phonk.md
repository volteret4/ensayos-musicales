# genre - Phonk

## curiosities
**Adaptability and Longevity** : Phonk has shown remarkable longevity for an internet trend, surviving memes, algorithm changes, and its own saturation, earning it the moniker "the cockroach of the music industry." This is attributed to its producers' ability to "adapt to current trends," making it the "perfect genre to mold" for any "shitpost or brain drop." "Es el genero perfecto para moldear al entojo de cualquier shit post o brain drop." ← ¿Qué pasó con el PHONK？ (La historia real) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ehfWyUHFFT8
**Aesthetic Impact of Low Fidelity** : Phonk heavily incorporates the "low fidelity" aesthetic, drawing parallels with low-poly graphics in video games. Just as low-poly visuals create unsettling atmospheres in horror games, Phonk uses the "low fidelity of cassette tapes" to create sonically disturbing and dark environments. "Si el Low Poly usa la baja resolución para inquitar visualmente, el Funk usa la baja fidelidad del caset para inquitar sonoramente y evoca las atmósferas turbias y oscuras." ← ¿Qué pasó con el PHONK？ (La historia real) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ehfWyUHFFT8
**Artistic Style and Imagery** : The visual art associated with Phonk mirrors its low-fidelity sound, featuring elements such as photocopied material, deformed generic typography, harsh shadows, low-poly and cropped images. These visuals are often tinted with a purple hue and heavily draw upon the excessive imagery of Houston and Memphis car culture. "todo ello te he ido del color por porad del lín, y con toda la imagen hería excesiva en arcúutica de Houston y Memphis." ← ¿Qué pasó con el PHONK？ (La historia real) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ehfWyUHFFT8
**Commercialization and Loss of Artistic Sense** : The rapid global growth of Phonk, especially Drift Phonk, led to its commercialization, particularly among teenagers who produced tracks "emptying it practically of any artistic sense." The genre transformed into a quick way to make money, with many new producers openly admitting their sole motivation was profit, leading to the "dehumanization" of its aesthetic. "el fonc se había olvidado de solencia cultural, y se convertió en una vía de ganar dinero rápidamente." ← ¿Qué pasó con el PHONK？ (La historia real) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ehfWyUHFFT8
**Dehumanization of Aesthetic** : As Phonk grew commercially, its artistic identity deteriorated. Album covers became "AI-generated images," losing the original distinctiveness. Only "purple tones, heavy cowbells, and increasingly exaggerated sound saturation" remained, reducing Phonk to "musical nothingness" or "white noise without any personality," akin to music generated by ChatGPT. "De esa identidad original, solo quedaban los tonos por puras, los centeros machacones, y una saturación sonora cada vez más exagerada." ← ¿Qué pasó con el PHONK？ (La historia real) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ehfWyUHFFT8
**Early Spread via SoundCloud** : Following the release of "Filda Funk 1990" in 2011, the "Phonk" label quickly gained traction on SoundCloud. A new global scene emerged, with artists worldwide paying homage to the Memphis and Houston pioneers while adding a new aesthetic filter. "La etiqueta empezó a resonar rápidamente en SoundCloud, y nuevos artistas de diferentes partes del mundo, como Lila Glimain, DJ Sacred o DJ Smokey, empezaron a generar una nueva escena global, venderando a los maestros de Memphis y Houston, pero añadiendo voluntariamente un nuevo filtro estético." ← ¿Qué pasó con el PHONK？ (La historia real) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ehfWyUHFFT8
**Enduring Memphis DNA** : Even in its commercialized and mutated forms, the essential DNA of Memphis rap continues to pulse within phonk. This is evident in its signature cowbells, distorted sub-basses, and ghostly, sampled vocals, which serve as a constant reminder that the genre originated in the neighborhoods with kids recording tapes just to impress friends, unknowingly becoming legends. "Pero incluso en esta vanalización, el ADND Mnfis sigue latiendo. En sus cencerros, en sus sub graves eventados, en sus vocales ampliados hasta sonar fantasmales, esos sonidos recuerdan que todo empezaron los barrios, con chavales que grababan cinta solo para emprisonar a sus colegas, sin saber que se convertirían en leyendas." ← Memphis Rap： Cuando el rap era puro terror | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lWBpNa-xSt4
**Global Homage and Influences** : Producers globally delved into the history of Memphis rap, rediscovering figures like Three 6 Mafia and Tommy Wright III. They paid homage by adopting the horrorcore aesthetic, imitating album covers, resurrecting the raw 808 sound, and mixing it with elements from other Southern rap scenes, such as Atlanta trap and Houston's chop and screw. "A partir de ahí, productores de todo el mundo empezaron a vociar en la historia y a descubrir figuras como Cirys Hym mafia o Tommy Brai III, rendendole su homenaje, adoptaron la estética horror core de Memphis, imitaron sus portadas, rescataron el sonido crudo del 808 y lo mezclaron con elementos de otras escenas del rap soreño, como el trap Dal Lanta o el Chopin Escrib of The Houston." ← Memphis Rap： Cuando el rap era puro terror | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lWBpNa-xSt4
**Initial Internet Distribution** : Like other internet-born genres of its era, such as vaporwave and cloud rap, Phonk initially found its audience and spread on platforms like SoundCloud. "Al igual que otros hijos de internet de su época, como el vapor wave o el cloud rap, el Funk empezamos a dar en plataforma como SoundCloud." ← ¿Qué pasó con el PHONK？ (La historia real) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ehfWyUHFFT8
**Mainstream Explosion & Banalization** : Phonk has experienced a mainstream explosion in countries like Russia and Brazil, with producers like Ghostface Playa achieving millions of streams. Despite this widespread popularity, many believe the genre has lost its original edge, often being mass-produced for car stereos or motivational gym playlists, signaling a "banalization" of its roots. "En Rusia y Brasil, por ejemplo, el funk ha explotado hasta convertirse en un fenómeno mainstream. Productores como Ghostface playa... acumulan millones de reproducciones. Pero para muchos, el generó ha perdido su filo original. Ahora prácticamente se hace en serie, diseñado para sonar en esos curos, buen playlist motivacionales de Jim." ← Memphis Rap： Cuando el rap era puro terror | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lWBpNa-xSt4
**Music for Consumption, Not Listening** : The ubiquitous Phonk heard on social media is characterized as "music to consume, not to listen to." In a world saturated with stimuli and devoid of silence, the genre has found its "ideal place" by providing four-by-four, oversaturated beats designed to grab attention for three seconds in an infinite scroll. "Este fonc que ha indundado las redes no es música para escuchar sino para consumir, y es que en esta vida saturada de estímulos, vivacia de silencios, el género han encontrado su lugar ideal." ← ¿Qué pasó con el PHONK？ (La historia real) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ehfWyUHFFT8
**Origin and Naming (Early 2010s)** : The genre known as Phonk emerged from the revival of 90s Memphis-inspired beats and loops, with its name dating back to the early 2010s. SpaceGhostPurrp and his collective Raider Klan played a key role in popularizing both the term and the sound through songs like "Filled Up Funk 1990." "Asinación el funk, su nombre es arremonta a principios de la década de 2010, cuando el propio Space Ghost Park y su colectivo Reader Clan no por polarizado a un cocanción es como Filled Up Funk 1990." ← Memphis Rap： Cuando el rap era puro terror | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lWBpNa-xSt4
**Original Essence vs. Modern Mutation** : In its early stages, phonk was deeply engaging, almost ritually capturing the essence of Memphis rap. However, a decade later, this original phonk has largely vanished, having mutated into thousands of variants on platforms like SoundCloud and TikTok, each with increasingly flashy sounds and exaggerated aesthetics. "El funk en sus inicios era realmente apasionante, porque capturaba de forma casi ritual la esencia de Memphis. Pero hoy, 10 años después, ese funk original prácticamente ha desaparecido. En planta formas como SoundCloud y, sobre todo, TikTok, el generó a mutar 2.000 variantes, cada cual con un sonido más efectista y una estética más exagerada que la anterior." ← Memphis Rap： Cuando el rap era puro terror | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lWBpNa-xSt4
**Origins and Naming (2011)** : The genre originated in 2011, pioneered by Miami producer and rapper Space Goshpar and his collective Raider Clan. They fused the "dirty and lo-fi techniques" of Memphis rap with the "narcotic slowness" of Houston's Chop 'N' Screw, adding a modern, digital sensibility influenced by West Coast G-Funk. Space Goshpar dubbed this new sound "authentic Miami funk." "Space Goshpar llamó a esta música auténtico funk de Miami y en 2011 lanzó el tema Filda Funk 1990." ← ¿Qué pasó con el PHONK？ (La historia real) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ehfWyUHFFT8
**Spotify's Official Playlist Controversy** : When Spotify finally created an official Phonk playlist, it "ignored pioneers" like Spinsy Gospar, Hollywood, and Lila Glimain, instead filling it with "commercial Russian sound," further cementing the public's misunderstanding of the genre's origins. "Cuando Spotify por fin creó su lista oficial de fonc, obvió a los videonejos como Spinsy Gospar, Hollywood o Lilac Limane llenando la desesonido ruso comercial." ← ¿Qué pasó con el PHONK？ (La historia real) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ehfWyUHFFT8
