# genre - Italo Disco

## curiosities
**Early Influence on Belleville Three** : Italo Disco was among the European electronic music genres that significantly influenced Juan Atkins, Derrick May, and Kevin Saunderson during their formative years in Belleville. **Initial Focus of Early Megamixes** : The second installment of the "Max Mix" series, released six months after the first, was primarily filled with Italo Disco songs and its Spanish counterpart, "Saba de Ishown." This genre was a foundational element for the early Megamix phenomenon in Spain. "un Megamix repleto de canciones de Italo Disco y su versión española, el Saba de Ishown." ← Ritmo y Extorsión： Así Dominaron los Megamix los 90s | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-qekjKky5bE
**Melodic Influence on Eurodance** : Italo Disco exerted a strong melodic influence on the nascent Eurodance sound. Its distinctive melodies contributed to the emergence of "more colorful themes" that successfully transitioned from underground clubs to mainstream sales charts. "Además de la fuerte influencia, melodica delital o disco..." ← La gran mentira detrás del Eurodance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_F34L8wfrdo
**Shift in Megamix Sound** : After its initial dominance, Italo Disco's presence in Megamixes evolved. The genre gave way to faster and more forceful electronic music styles, such as Acid House, New Beat, EBM, and later Máquina and OroDens, especially after the influence of "La Ruta y el Sonido Valencia."
