# genre - Boogaloo

## curiosities
**Barrio Youth Movement** : This genre was primarily played by young, often adolescent musicians in small neighborhood venues in areas like the Bronx, Spanish Harlem, and the Lower East Side of New York, a stark contrast to the glamour and formality of larger orchestras. It was a raw, energetic expression of a burgeoning popular Latin scene. "Música jóvenes, adolescentes en muchos casos, tocaban en pequeños locales del bronze, el Spanish Harlem y en Lower East Side, lejos de glamour y la formalidad de las grandes orquestas." ← LA FANIA. **Fusion Origin** : Boogaloo emerged from a rebellious spirit and a blend of influences, fusing Cuban rhythms such as guaguancó, guajira, and mambo with American rock and soul music. It represented a departure from the more sophisticated Latin sounds of the era. "De esa rebeldida y mezcla de influencias surgió el búgalo, un género que fusionaba ritmos cubanos como el guauanco, la guajida o el mambo, con el rock y el soble estadounidenses." ← LA FANIA.
