## curiosities
**A Cappella Performance and Formalized Structure** : This style of music was typically performed a cappella, though sometimes a single instrument might be added. It developed a relatively formalized structure with specific parts for tenor, soprano, alto, and bass, adhering to strict four-part harmony common in European choral music. The soprano and alto parts were sung in falsetto by men. **Fusion of Musical Elements** : Isekathamia combines elements of traditional Zulu music with two Western influences: religious music heard from church missions, and American minstrel songs, particularly those performed by troupes led by Orfius Macadoo that toured South Africa in the late 19th century. "Those people combined elements of traditional Zulu music with two more Western elements. The first was the religious music that they heard from church missions, and the second was American minstrel songs." ← Episode 92： ＂The Lion Sleeps Tonight＂ by the Tokens | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QMP_DFyktMg
**Origins in Forced Displacement** : This music form was created when rural black people in South Africa were forcibly displaced in the late 19th century and compelled to find work in cities. **Western Chord Progressions and Zulu Language Adaptation** : Isekathamia music usually centered around the common one, four, and five chords found in most Western popular music. The Zulu language lyrics were often distorted to fit Western meters, although the music maintained a more freeform quality compared to much of the Western music of that era.
