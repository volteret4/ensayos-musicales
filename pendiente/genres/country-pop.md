**"Purity of Country Music" Debate** : The argument that country pop artists were "ruining the purity of country music" was a debate that had been ongoing for decades by the time "Urban Cowboy" was released, with roots in the 1960s when radio stations began actively courting pop music fans. **1970s AM Radio Airplay** : In the 1970s, country-pop combinations were highly likely to receive AM radio airplay, contributing to the crossover success of artists like Charlie Pride. While this approach pleased a broader audience, it often "pissed off the purists" of traditional country music.
