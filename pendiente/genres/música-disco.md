This impact was evident in the careers of artists like Madonna (culminating in "Confessions on a Dancefloor"), U2 (in songs like "Lemon"), Kylie Minogue, and Jamiroquai. **Beginning of the End (1978)** : Professionally, the beginning of the end for disco music is marked in 1978 with Gloria Gaynor's release of "I Will Survive," which is considered the last great quality disco hit. After this point, disco began to degenerate into a form that was almost self-parody, becoming somewhat scatological, parodic, and sometimes ridiculous. **Survival in New York's Gay Communities** : Despite the rapid decline of disco after 1978, it did not entirely die out. A small core group, particularly in New York, closely linked to the homosexual and gay communities, kept the music alive, leading to the emergence of acts like Village People.
