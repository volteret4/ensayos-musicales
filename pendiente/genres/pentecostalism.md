# genre - Pentecostalism

## curiosities
**Core Beliefs - Sanctification** : Pentecostals differ from many other Christians by believing that some individuals can become "truly holy" and sinless on earth if they truly believe and are "born again." This state is achieved through "baptism in the Holy Ghost," where the Holy Spirit descends, filling the person with joy and sanctity, often leading to speaking in tongues. "Pentecostals believe that it is possible for some people, if they truly believe, if they're born again... to become truly holy." ← Episode 5： ＂This Train＂ by Sister Rosetta Tharpe | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=II-hDnwUgT8
**Inspiration for Musicians** : The fear of hell and the elation from experiencing the Holy Spirit in Pentecostalism have significantly inspired many important musicians in rock and roll. "This is a very real fear for many Pentecostals, who have a belief in a literal heaven and hell, and it's a fear that has inspired a lot of the most important musicians in rock and roll." ← Episode 5： ＂This Train＂ by Sister Rosetta Tharpe | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=II-hDnwUgT8
**Musical Expression** : Pentecostal churches, particularly in the Southern US, foster a culture of wild, unrestrained, and passionate music and dancing. This musical environment is intended to help people enter a receptive mood for the Holy Spirit. "At many of them, especially in the south of the US, there's a culture of absolutely wild, unrestrained, passionate music and dancing to get people into the mood to have the Spirit enter them." ← Episode 5： ＂This Train＂ by Sister Rosetta Tharpe | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=II-hDnwUgT8
**Outward Holiness** : To maintain holiness after being sanctified by the Holy Spirit, Pentecostals practice "outward holiness," which includes conservative dress and abstaining from swearing, drinking, gambling, smoking, dancing suggestively, or wearing makeup. Failure to adhere to these practices is feared to lead to the devil's influence, reflecting a belief in a literal heaven and hell. "so long as you continue with what's called outward holiness, and so you have to dress conservatively to avoid swearing, to avoid drinking or gambling or smoking or dancing suggestively or wearing makeup." ← Episode 5： ＂This Train＂ by Sister Rosetta Tharpe | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=II-hDnwUgT8
