# genre - Jump band music

## curiosities
**Characteristics and Influences** : Jump band music, pioneered by Louis Jordan and His Tympany Five, was influenced by Lionel Hampton's band and Count Basie. It featured a stripped-back sound compared to big band swing, focusing on simple chord changes (often blues), riffs, solos, and vocals, driven by boogie rhythms and high energy. "The style they were playing in was a style that later became known as jump band music, and it was a style that owed a lot to Lionel Hampton's band and to Count Basie." ← Episode 4： ＂Choo Choo Ch'Boogie＂ by Louis Jordan | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DFZnwvsXGJM
**Evolution from Swing** : Jump band music was a new type of music rooted in swing, but with a greater emphasis on riffs and amplified instruments. It was pioneered by bands like Lionel Hampton's after he left Benny Goodman's group. "That band would be one of the first bands to perform a new type of music, Jump band music, which was rooted in swing but had more of emphasis on riffs and amplified instruments." ← Episode 1： ＂Flying Home＂, Benny Goodman, Charlie Christian, and Lionel Hampton | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RmfeJYl84Qs
**Precursor to Rhythm and Blues** : Jump band music later became known as rhythm and blues. Musicians such as Louis Jordan were explicitly inspired by Lionel Hampton's pioneering jump bands, establishing a clear lineage for the genre. "That Jump band music is the same music that later became known as rhythm and blues, and musicians such as Louis Jordan were clearly inspired by Hampton's band." ← Episode 1： ＂Flying Home＂, Benny Goodman, Charlie Christian, and Lionel Hampton | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RmfeJYl84Qs
**Sound and Arrangement** : Jump band music, despite being performed by smaller ensembles, achieved a full sound, often with a seemingly complete horn section. Arrangements were stripped back, focusing on punchy delivery and a backbeat, emphasizing vocal personality to convey character typically handled by more instruments in larger bands. "The arrangement is stripped back to the basics to what's necessary, this is a punchy track, and it's a track that makes you want to dance." ← Episode 4： ＂Choo Choo Ch'Boogie＂ by Louis Jordan | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DFZnwvsXGJM
