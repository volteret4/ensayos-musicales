# genre - Philly Soul

## curiosities
**"Lush" Sound** : The music associated with Philadelphia International Records and Philly Soul is frequently described as "lush" in various writings, with its characteristic string instruments creating an enveloping, wave-like sonic experience that naturally encourages physical movement. **Blending Motown and Stax** : Mark Neal, a professor of African American Studies at Duke, characterized Gamble and Huff's unique achievement in Philly Soul as successfully melding "the Motown machine with the Stacks grit," resulting in a sound that is simultaneously glossy and smooth yet possesses the raw intensity associated with Stax Records. "Mark Neal, professor of African American Studies at Duke said of Gamble and Huff, quote, they found a way to marry the Motown machine with the Stacks grit." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 37 - Philly Soul and the Sound of the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-37-philly-soul-and-the-sound-of-the-70s
**Distinctive Sound Description** : Journalist Jim Farber (2021) vividly described Philly Soul as "a mixture of creamy strings, punching horns, snaking bass lines and fullsome melodies all combined to create something at once complex and light, a sonic suple fired by soul." Producer and guitarist Bobby Eli famously encapsulated the sound as "funk, dressed in a tuxedo." "He wrote, it is a mixture of creamy strings, punching horns, snaking bass lines and fullsome melodies all combined to create something at once complex and light, a sonic suple fired by soul." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 37 - Philly Soul and the Sound of the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-37-philly-soul-and-the-sound-of-the-70s
**Integration of Social Commentary** : Beyond its appealing and sweet sonic qualities, Gamble and Huff's Philly Soul consciously integrated social commentary, reflecting their attentiveness to contemporary world events and their intention to use music as a medium for conveying deeper, meaningful messages. "There's something more underneath that sweetness, but Gamble and Huff made no pretenses that they didn't pay attention to what was happening in the world." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 37 - Philly Soul and the Sound of the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-37-philly-soul-and-the-sound-of-the-70s
**Producer's Genre** : Philly Soul is characterized as a "producer's genre," underscoring the significant influence of producers like Kenny Gamble and Leon Huff in shaping its signature sound and intricate arrangements, a characteristic that also served as a precursor to the rise of Disco. **Racial Integration ("Gray Eyed")** : In 1978, Billy Paul described Philadelphia soul as "gray eyed," signifying its inherent and enduring racial integration in both its appeal and composition, thereby challenging racial stereotypes often associated with "black music." "Paul said in 1978 that at that all Philadelphia soul is what he called gray eyed meaning racially integrated and it always has been." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 37 - Philly Soul and the Sound of the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-37-philly-soul-and-the-sound-of-the-70s
**Signature Element: Strings** : The prominent and "creamy" use of string arrangements is identified as the hallmark of Philly Soul, providing its distinctive and enveloping sound that sets it apart from other soul genres, such as Motown.
