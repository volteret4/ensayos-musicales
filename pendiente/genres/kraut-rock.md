**Defining Characteristics** : Kraut Rock encompasses experimental rock that emerged in Germany in the late 1960s and early 1970s. It is distinguished by its pioneering use of synthesizers and its adoption of recording and production techniques derived from concrete music. Key features include long improvisations and the consistent use of hypnotic, repetitive rhythms, most notably the "motorik" beat—a 4x4 rhythm that creates a sensation of constant movement, akin to driving on a highway. These elements set it apart from Anglo-Saxon psychedelia and the predominant verse-chorus structures of popular music at the time. "engloba el rock experimental que se desarrolló en la alemania finales de los años 60 y principios de los 70, y destaca por el uso de sintetizadores y técnicas de grabación y producción de la música concreta. **Influence in Early Chill Out Spaces** : Kraut Rock was one of the genres mixed "de forma muy suave" in "The White Room" at London's Club Heaven in 1989. Its "planeador" (gliding/soaring) quality contributed to the desired atmosphere of harmony and tranquility in these early chill-out spaces. "se mezclaban de forma muy suave, música ambiental de brillaneno con el Kraut Rock más planeador, Dap Hamaycano y cualquier tipo de música que encajara con este ambiente de armonía y tranquilidad." ← Chill Out： La pausa que necesitaba la electrónica | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VPOAA3ZaA9s
**Origin of the Term** : The term "Kraut Rock" was initially used in English media as a somewhat humorous or derisive moniker to refer to the diverse German music scene, with "Kraut" meaning "cabbage." Although many artists of the era disliked the term, it is no longer considered controversial by contemporary artists and has become a recognized genre. "El término Kraut Rock se usó por primera vez en los medios ingleses, como siguió morístico para referirse a la diversa escena alemana.
