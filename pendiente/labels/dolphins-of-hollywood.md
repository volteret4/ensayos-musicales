# label - Dolphins of Hollywood

## curiosities
**First Debenares Record** : In 1952, The Debenares recorded their first record for John Dolphin and his Dolphins of Hollywood label. "The Debenares, with a lineup of Barry, Gunter, young Jesse, Thomas Fox and Beverly Thompson visited John Dolphin and made their first record for Dolphins of Hollywood." ← Episode 106：＂Louie Louie＂ by the Kingsmen | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S3agGJsxjpE
**Largest Black-Owned Record Store in LA** : Dolphins of Hollywood was the biggest Black-owned record store in the Los Angeles area, owned by John Dolphin. It played a crucial role in the success of many records through its radio shows broadcast from Dolphin's own station. "Dolphin owned Dolphins of Hollywood, the biggest black owned record store in the LA area, and was responsible for a large part of the success of many of the records we've covered, through getting them played on radio shows broadcast from his station." ← Episode 86： ＂LSD-25＂ by the Gamblers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UNd6UwbL1rA
**Uncredited Release** : John Dolphin released The Debenares' first record under the name The Hollywood Blue Jays, a name he often used for various groups, causing disappointment for the actual band members. "it wasn't credited to the Debenares, but instead to the Hollywood Blue Jays, and named Dolphin had also used for the groups." ← Episode 106：＂Louie Louie＂ by the Kingsmen | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S3agGJsxjpE
