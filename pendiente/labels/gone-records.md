# label - Gone Records

## curiosities
**Family of Labels** : Gone Records was one of a family of labels owned and run by George Goldner. Goldner assigned Richie Barrett as talent scout, producer, and arranger for the Isley Brothers, a role Barrett previously held for acts like Frankie Lymon and The Teenagers, The Chantels, and Goldner's own group, The Valentines. "They chose to sign to Gun Records, one of the family of labels that was owned and run by George Goldner." ← Episode 81： ＂Shout＂ by the Isley Brothers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=USJDERCABjk
**Richie Barrett's "Assembly Line" Production** : Richie Barrett, the producer assigned to the Isley Brothers at Gone Records, was known for his established "assembly line method" of making records. This involved booking a studio for up to 24 hours with backing musicians, and recording dozens of tracks with as many as ten different vocal groups in a row. "He would block Booker Studio and some backing musicians for up to 24 hours, get as many as 10 different vocal groups into the studio, and record dozens of tracks in a row, usually songs written by either group members or by Barrett." ← Episode 81： ＂Shout＂ by the Isley Brothers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=USJDERCABjk
