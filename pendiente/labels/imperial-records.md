# label - Imperial Records

## curiosities
**Album Release Significance** : Imperial Records' decision to release Fats Domino's first album, "Rock and Rollin' with Fat Domino," after six years of exclusively recording singles, was highly significant. It signaled a shift from sales primarily to jukebox manufacturers to direct consumer purchases, particularly by a new white audience willing to buy LPs, a rarity for R&B artists in the 1950s. "Imperial records actually put out an album by him rather than just singles for the first time in the six years he'd been recording for them." ← Episode 45： ＂Blueberry Hill＂, by Fats Domino | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VqIwZGJHgBg
**Dave Bartholomew's A&R Role** : Dave Bartholomew worked as an A&R man for Imperial Records after his military service, a role through which he would eventually suggest signing Fats Domino. **Label for Ricky Nelson** : Imperial Records was the label for teen idol Ricky Nelson, who needed material, leading to Johnny and Dorsey Burnette's successful songwriting partnership for him.
