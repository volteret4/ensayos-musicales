# label - Universal Music Corporate

## curiosities
**Acquisition of Island Records** : Universal Music Corporate became the final owner of Island Records, thereby consolidating control over a significant portion of King Crimson's early musical catalog. This acquisition proved pivotal in subsequent legal conflicts with Robert Fripp concerning re-releases and previously unreleased material. **Co-Ownership and Lack of Consent Stance** : Universal Music, as the owner of Island Records and a co-proprietor of King Crimson's rights, argued that it was not legally obligated to seek Robert Fripp's permission for re-releases or the publication of unreleased material. This legal stance fueled years of complex and industry-followed lawsuits. **Kanye West Sampling Dispute Involvement** : Universal Music, by owning both Island Records (a co-owner of the sampled King Crimson material) and Kanye West's record label, was uniquely positioned during the "Power" sampling controversy. They effectively "reached an agreement with themselves" by approving the sample internally but failed to inform Robert Fripp, leading to significant anger and further legal action. **Role in Grooveshark's Closure** : Universal Music Corporate, alongside Warner Music, played an active role in a concerted legal and commercial offensive against Grooveshark, spurred by Robert Fripp's complaints. This mass attack was a critical factor in the eventual closure of the streaming platform. **Unauthorized King Crimson Releases** : After acquiring Island Records, Universal Music Corporate began publishing compilations, re-editions, and certain unreleased King Crimson material without obtaining explicit consent from Robert Fripp. This action prompted a major legal dispute initiated by Fripp, who believed his previous legal battles were concluded.
