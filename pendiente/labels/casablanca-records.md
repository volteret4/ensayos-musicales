# label - Casablanca Records

## curiosities
**Neil Bogart's "I Feel Love" Insight** : Neil Bogart of Casablanca Records was instrumental in the release of Donna Summer's "I Feel Love" as a single, despite neither Summer nor producer Giorgio Moroder initially believing it should be released that way. "It was never going to be released as a single until Neil Bogart of Casa Blanca Records said that it should be." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 1 - Disco Doesn't Suck (Encore) | https://www.ftr70.com/blog/tag/70s+podcast
**Role in "Love to Love You Baby" Development** : Casablanca Records, founded by Neil Bogart, played a crucial role in the creation of the extended 17-minute version of Donna Summer's "Love to Love You Baby." Bogart's vision for a long-form song conducive to lovemaking led him to request the extended version from Giorgio Moroder, shaping the song's iconic status. "Neil Bogart, the founder of Casa Blanca Records, was given that record by George Eoma Rotor, one of Donna Summer's producers and a Disco genius, by the way." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 4 - Music of the 70s (Sexual) Revolution | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-4-talkin-bout-a-sexual-revolution
