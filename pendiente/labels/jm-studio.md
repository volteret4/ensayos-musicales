# label - J&M Studio

## curiosities
**Cosimo Matassa's Unconventional Path to Studio Owner** : Cosimo Matassa, lacking formal record production training, dropped out of chemistry studies at age 18 to establish J&M Studio, which was remarkably rudimentary compared to modern standards. "He'd been a chemistry student until he dropped out of university, aged 18, and set up the studio, which was laughably rudimentary by today's standards." ← Episode 8： The Fat Man and Fats Domino | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A-1R-DbVdcM
**J&M Studio - Dual Disk Cutter Method** : J&M Studio employed two disk cutters simultaneously: one for creating a safety copy to check audio quality, and the other for cutting the master disk, as direct-to-disk recording didn't allow for post-production changes. "One of them would cut a safety copy, which they could listen to to see if it sounded okay, while the other would be cutting the master." ← Episode 8： The Fat Man and Fats Domino | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A-1R-DbVdcM
**J&M Studio - Modest Origins** : J&M Studio was a tiny, rudimentary space located in the back room of Cosimo Matassa's family appliance store, with a bookmaker's office upstairs and a shoe shine boy working outside. "It was in the back room of his family's appliance store, which also had a bookmaker's upstairs and a shoe shine boy operating outside the studio door." ← Episode 8： The Fat Man and Fats Domino | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A-1R-DbVdcM
**J&M Studio - Seminal Recording Location** : Cosimo Matassa's J&M Studio in Louisiana became a pivotal recording location for the burgeoning rock and roll sound, central to the history of this new music. "Cosimo Matassa's studio, J&M, in Louisiana, which would become one of the most important places in the history of this new music." ← Episode 8： The Fat Man and Fats Domino | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A-1R-DbVdcM
**J&M Studio - Use of Wax Masters** : While most professional studios by this time had transitioned to more robust acetate disks for cutting, J&M Studio notably continued to use wax masters, which were more fragile. "most professional studios, if they were using this cutters at all, were using acetate disks, which are slightly more robust, but apparently J&M were still using wax." ← Episode 8： The Fat Man and Fats Domino | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A-1R-DbVdcM
**J&M Studio - Wax Debris Management** : During recording sessions at J&M Studio, an assistant was tasked with catching the wax debris cut from the disks by the two disk cutters, preventing it from falling onto the floor. "someone's job would be to stand by them and catch the wax they cut out of the disks before it dropped onto the floor." ← Episode 8： The Fat Man and Fats Domino | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A-1R-DbVdcM
**J&M Studio Acoustics - Small and Acoustically Dead** : J&M Studio suffered from poor acoustics, being too small and acoustically dead, often requiring record companies to add echo to masters during post-production. "It was too small and acoustically dead, and the record companies who received the masters and released them, would often end up adding echo after the fact." ← Episode 8： The Fat Man and Fats Domino | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A-1R-DbVdcM
**J&M Studio Recording Equipment - Direct-to-Disk** : J&M Studio utilized a basic three-channel mixer and recorded directly to disk rather than using magnetic tape, reflecting the rudimentary technology of the time. "He had a three channel mixer, and they didn't record to tape, but directly to disk." ← Episode 8： The Fat Man and Fats Domino | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A-1R-DbVdcM
**J&M Studio's Strengths - Matassa and Bartholomew** : Despite its poor acoustics, J&M Studio's success was driven by the exceptional talent of engineer Cosimo Matassa and band leader Dave Bartholomew, whose collaboration with a consistent group of musicians produced influential rock and roll music. "what they did have, was a great engineer in Matassa, and a great band leader, in Dave Bartholomew, and the band he put together for Fat Stomino's first record, would largely work together for the next few years, creating some of the greatest rock and roll music ever made." ← Episode 8： The Fat Man and Fats Domino | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A-1R-DbVdcM
