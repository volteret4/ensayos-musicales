# label - Folkways

## curiosities
**Acquisition of Global Copyright** : Folkways, seeking global rights, secured an agreement with Gallo Records that proved highly disadvantageous to Gallo. Folkways paid a modest one-off fee and allowed Gallo rights in a few African territories, while Folkways gained the copyright everywhere else. "Because they agreed that they would pay Gallow a modest one-off fee, and let Gallow have the rights to the song in a few territories in Africa, and in return Folkways would get the copyright everywhere else." ← Episode 92： ＂The Lion Sleeps Tonight＂ by the Tokens | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QMP_DFyktMg
**Folk Music Promotion and Corporate Affiliation** : Folkways was a record company specifically established to promote folk music, but it operated as part of a much larger conglomerate, the Richmond Organization. "Folkways, a company that was set up to promote Folk Music, but was part of a much bigger conglomerate, the Richmond organisation." ← Episode 92： ＂The Lion Sleeps Tonight＂ by the Tokens | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QMP_DFyktMg
**Initial Copyright of "Wimoweh"** : When The Weavers decided to record "Wimoweh," Folkways contacted Gallo Records and was mistakenly informed that "Ember Bay" was a traditional song. Folkways then copyrighted "Ember Bay" as "Wimoweh" in the USA under the collective pseudonym Paul Campbell, preempting Gallo's later attempt to copyright it under Solomon Linda's name.
