# label - Coral

## curiosities
**Decca Subsidiary for "Insipid White Cover Versions" (1957)** : Coral was one of two labels under the purview of Decca executive Bob Thiele, and it was primarily used for releasing "insipid white cover versions of Black Acts." "The Cuddle, which was being used mostly for insipid white cover versions of Black Acts." ← Episode Sixty-One： ＂That'll Be the Day＂, by the Crickets | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KVJMKLc54O0
**Outlet for Buddy Holly Solo Material (1957)** : Coral also became the designated label for Buddy Holly's solo material, allowing him to record new songs under his own name, with the same musicians who played on The Crickets' records, thereby doubling the amount of material they could release.
