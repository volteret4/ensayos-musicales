# label - Coral Records

## curiosities
**Marketing of Johnny Burnette as Solo Artist** : Coral Records' decision to release recordings under "Johnny Burnette and the Rock and Roll Trio" instead of just the band name, and later solely under "Johnny Burnette," caused significant friction within the band, leading to its eventual breakup, and also prevented The Burnette Brothers from recording as a duo. "Coral Records decided to release their recordings as by Johnny Burnett and the rock and roll trio, and the other two members were understandably furious." ← Episode 44： ＂Train Kept A-Rollin'＂, by Johnny Burnette and the Rock 'n' Roll Trio | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u3zYAJ2qeR4
**Subsidiary of Decca Records** : Coral Records was a subsidiary of Decca Records, which, at the time the Rock and Roll Trio signed in 1956, was one of the biggest major labels in the USA.
