# label - Sun

## curiosities
**Million Dollar Quartet Public Relations (1956)** : Sam Phillips recognized the publicity opportunity of the Million Dollar Quartet session on December 4, 1956. He capitalized on it by calling journalists and photographers, aiming to publicize his stars and, notably, elevating Jerry Lee Lewis to the same status level as Elvis Presley, Carl Perkins, and Johnny Cash. "Sam Phillips took the opportunity to publicise his stars far and wide, and that publicity placed the four of them on the same level." ← Episode 51： ＂Matchbox＂ by Carl Perkins | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0Nrjr0AGd3U
**Sam Phillips' Promotion Decisions** : Carl Perkins and Johnny Cash were both dissatisfied with Sam Phillips' promotion of their work at Sun Records, leading them to consider moving to other labels. This indicates a period of tension between artists and the label's promotional strategies. "Perkins was increasingly dissatisfied with the way Sam Phillips was promoting his work, and like Johnny Cash was strongly considering moving to another label." ← Episode 51： ＂Matchbox＂ by Carl Perkins | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0Nrjr0AGd3U
**Sound Reference for British Engineers** : Junior Engineer Malcolm Addy, who was left to record Cliff Richard's B-side "Move It" due to the main engineer's absence, possessed a clear understanding of what a rock and roll record should sound like. He consequently produced a significantly louder and more resonant sound for the track, which bore a strong resemblance to the records being released by Sun. "Addy was young enough to have some idea himself as to what a Rock and Roll record should sound like, and he came up with a much louder, more resonant sound than anything that had been heard in a British recording session. A record that didn't sound all at this similar to the records that Sun was putting out." ← Episode 70： ＂Move It＂ by Cliff Richard and the Drifters | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uoEWP94f6S4
