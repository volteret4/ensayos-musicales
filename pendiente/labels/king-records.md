# label - King Records

## curiosities
**A&R Man Ralph Bass** : King Records employed Ralph Bass as their A&R (Artists and Repertoire) Man, who was noted as a significant figure in the industry. "King Records, whose A&R Man Ralph Bass will also be hearing more about." ← Episode 7： ＂Good Rockin' Tonight＂ by Wynonie Harris | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IgqSPoYRUCM
**Contract for Bill Doggett** : Henry Glover secured a contract for Bill Doggett with King Records, leading to Doggett's recordings of Hammond organ blues tracks in the style of Wild Bill Davis. "Still, Henry Glover got him a contract with King Records, and he started recording Hammond organ blues tracks in the Davis style." ← Episode 50： ＂Honky Tonk＂, by Bill Doggett | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H2rYnKmeZF0
**Dropped Bill Doggett over Royalty Dispute** : King Records dropped Bill Doggett as a charting artist after 1960 when he requested a higher royalty rate, arguing his recordings had generated millions of dollars for the label. "His career as a charting artist more or less stopped after 1960, when he made the mistake of asking Sid Nathan if he could have a higher royalty rate, given the millions of dollars his recordings had brought in to King Records, and King dropped him." ← Episode 50： ＂Honky Tonk＂, by Bill Doggett | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H2rYnKmeZF0
**Initial Rejection of Jackie Wilson** : Johnny Otis, a talent scout for King Records, attempted to get Jackie Wilson signed to the label, but for unclear reasons, King Records decided only to sign The Royals initially, later signing Little Willie John. "King decided they only wanted to sign the royals, though they would sign Little Willie John a couple of years later." ← Episode Sixty-Four： ＂Reet Petite＂ by Jackie Wilson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BYU-H4NvnmE
**Parent Company of Federal Records** : King Records was the parent company of Federal Records. Sid Nathan, the boss at King, initially opposed the release of "Please Please Please" by The Famous Flames on its subsidiary, Federal. "They were signed to Federal Records, a subsidiary of King, by Ralph Bass, the visionary A&R man we've dealt with in many other episodes." ← Episode 39： ＂Please Please Please＂ by James Brown and the Famous Flames | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DSkNVh93tAs
**Sid Nathan's Dislike of Double-Sided Records** : Sid Nathan, the head of King Records, strongly disliked releasing records where a single song continued onto the B-side, primarily because jukebox operators, his main customers, found them inconvenient. "Nathan hated putting out records where the song continued onto the other side, because the Duke box operators, who were his main customers, didn't like them." ← Episode 50： ＂Honky Tonk＂, by Bill Doggett | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H2rYnKmeZF0
**Signed Billy Ward and His Dominoes** : King Records later signed Billy Ward and his Dominoes, releasing Jackie Wilson's first recording as lead singer with the group, "You Can't Keep a Good Man Down." "Wilson's first recording with the group as lead singer came just after he turned 19. When he went into the studio with them to cut, you can't keep a good man down for King Records." ← Episode Sixty-Four： ＂Reet Petite＂ by Jackie Wilson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BYU-H4NvnmE
