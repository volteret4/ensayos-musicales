# label - Modern Records

## curiosities
**Flair Records Subsidiary** : Flair Records, the label to which The Flares signed, was a subsidiary of Modern Records. "They decided to go with modern records, and ended up signing to Flair Records, one of modern subsidiaries." ← Episode 106：＂Louie Louie＂ by the Kingsmen | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S3agGJsxjpE
**Home for The Cliques and Solo Jesse Belvin** : Modern Records was the label for The Cliques, the duo Jesse Belvin formed with Eugene Church, which released the commercially successful "The Girl in My Dreams." Belvin later signed with Modern Records as a solo artist, recording new material before eventually moving to RCA. "He signed to modern records as a solo artist and went into the studio to record a new song." ← Episode 47： ＂Goodnight My Love＂ by Jesse Belvin | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eoyk3H0M6r8
**Marvin Phillips' New Home for Marvin and Johnny** : After Jesse Belvin was drafted, Marvin Phillips continued their duo as Marvin and Johnny and subsequently moved over to Modern Records. The label became the home for their hits, including "Cherry Pie," and also for Jesse Belvin's later solo work and his duo The Cliques. "Marvin decided to continue the duo without Jesse, renaming it Marvin and Johnny and moved over to modern records." ← Episode 47： ＂Goodnight My Love＂ by Jesse Belvin | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eoyk3H0M6r8
**Owned by Beharry Brothers** : Modern Records was owned by the Beharry Brothers, who also employed Ike Turner as a producer for bands like The Flares.
