# label - Max Music

## curiosities
**End of an Era** : The departure of Ricardo Campoy, who took the Dream Team and other employees to form Vale Music, along with Miguel DeGa's subsequent criminal conviction, ultimately led to the end of Max Music. This marked a significant turning point in the Spanish music industry. "Lo que es cierto es que ese movimiento propició la creación de la nueva discográfica, Vale Music, y el final de Max Music." ← Ritmo y Extorsión： Así Dominaron los Megamix los 90s | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-qekjKky5bE
**Founding and Early Strategy (1964)** : Max Music was founded in 1964 by two salesmen, Miguel DeGa and Ricardo Campoy, who previously sold Italo Disco maxis in clubs. To kickstart their venture, they launched a contest for creative song mixers, offering a 100,000 peseta prize and a record deal, which was won by Mike Platinas. "crean el sillo Max Music en 1964." ← Ritmo y Extorsión： Así Dominaron los Megamix los 90s | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-qekjKky5bE
**Involvement in Criminal Activities** : The intense rivalry between Max Music and newly formed Vale Music (founded by former Max Music co-founder Ricardo Campoy) escalated dramatically. Miguel DeGa, head of Max Music, hired 13 hitmen to kidnap Campoy, an act that mistakenly led to the kidnapping and beating of José María Castells. DeGa was subsequently imprisoned and later escaped. "El 3 de septiembre de 1998, el empresario contrató a 13 carios mexicanos para secuestrar al Ricardo Campoy." ← Ritmo y Extorsión： Así Dominaron los Megamix los 90s | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-qekjKky5bE
**Pioneer of Spanish Megamixes and Name Registration** : Max Music was instrumental in popularizing the Megamix phenomenon in Spain, with its first "Max Mix" album (mixed by Mike Platinas and Javier Usia) being a sales success. The label even registered the name "Megamix" to refer to these types of compilations. "la discográfica registró el nombre de Megamix para referirse a esos trabajos." ← Ritmo y Extorsión： Así Dominaron los Megamix los 90s | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-qekjKky5bE
**Unethical Business Practices** : Max Music was known for its "not entirely ethical" practices. These included forcing contest winners to choose between prize money or a record deal, failing to pay mixers for their work, engaging in corporate espionage, pressuring distributors to favor their products, commissioning cover versions of songs to avoid paying royalties, and flooding the market with compilations to undermine competitors.
