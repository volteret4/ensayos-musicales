# label - Monument Records

## curiosities
**Bankruptcy and Legal Battles (1970s)** : Monument Records went bankrupt in the 1970s. This bankruptcy complicated efforts to reissue Roy Orbison's old songs, as their ownership became tied up in legal battles, making them difficult to acquire during his "wilderness period." **Bob Moore's Secret Ownership** : Bob Moore, a prominent bass player on the Nashville A-team and an uncredited co-producer and arranger for Roy Orbison, secretly owned 37% of Monument Records. This was an example of the backroom deals common in the music industry at the time, unknown to his fellow session players. "For example, none of Bob Moore's fellow session players on the A team knew that he secretly owned 37% of monument records." ← Episode 83： ＂Only the Lonely＂ by Roy Orbison | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uDvkjXa1ALk
**Early Copyright Dispute** : The label's first single, Billy Grammar's "Done Laid Around," caused significant problems due to a copyright issue. Monument believed the folk song was in the public domain, but Fred Foster later discovered it was copyrighted to folk singer Paul Clayton. This required withdrawing the record from sale and reissuing it as "Gotta Travel On" with Clayton credited, costing the new label a substantial amount of money, though the record still became a hit. "But when Clayton wrote the song or not, done later around had to be withdrawn from sale, and reissued under the name Gotta Travel On, with Clayton credited as the composer, something which cost the new label a substantial amount of money." ← Episode 83： ＂Only the Lonely＂ by Roy Orbison | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uDvkjXa1ALk
**Foundation and Ownership (1958)** : Monument Records was started by Fred Foster, who invested his entire life savings into the venture, initially co-owning it with a DJ named Buddy Dean. They were based in Washington at this time. "He then put his entire life savings into starting up his own company, Monument, which he initially co-owned with a DJ named Buddy Dean." ← Episode 83： ＂Only the Lonely＂ by Roy Orbison | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uDvkjXa1ALk
**Label Name and Logo Origin** : As Fred Foster and Buddy Dean were based in Washington, D.C., they used an image of the Washington Monument as the label's logo, which also inspired the company's name. "As Foster and Dean were based in Washington at this time, they used an image of the Washington Monument as the label's logo, and that also inspired the name." ← Episode 83： ＂Only the Lonely＂ by Roy Orbison | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uDvkjXa1ALk
**Move to Nashville and Growth** : After the success of Billy Grammar's first single, Fred Foster bought out his co-owner Buddy Dean and relocated the label to Nashville. This move allowed Foster to start working with the renowned Nashville A-team of session musicians.
