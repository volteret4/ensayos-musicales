# label - Blue Note Records

## curiosities
**Founding and Legacy** : Blue Note Records is described as "one of the first and longest standing institutions of jazz music," founded in 1939. It is celebrating its 85th anniversary in 2024. The label is known for its "cooler branding," attributed to art director Reed Miles and photographer-turned-executive Francis Wolfe. Over its history, it has released albums by numerous prominent artists including Herbie Hancock, Art Blakey and the Jazz Messengers, Robert Glasper, Lee Morgan, John Coltrane, Wayne Shorter, Bobby McFerrin, Greg Porter, Ron Carter, Michelle Andegeo Cello, Charles Lloyd, and Julian Lodge. **Presidency and Vision** : Don Was is the current president of Blue Note Records, leading the label's 85th-anniversary celebrations in 2024. He expressed his intention to speak with artists representing the label's past, present, and future, including Ron Carter, Michelle Andegeo Cello, Charles Lloyd, Julian Lodge, and Nora Jones. **Signing Nora Jones** : Bruce Lentval, the head of Blue Note Records at the time, met Nora Jones after an EMI publishing contact set up a meeting. Nora presented a three-song demo, and despite Lentval remarking that some of it "isn't jazz... This is country," he decided to sign her, stating, "I don't care. Let's do it. Let's make a record." This decision allowed Nora to embark on her entire career with the label. "Bruce listened and he liked it.
