# label - Brunswick

## curiosities
**Decca Subsidiary for Rock and Roll** : Brunswick was a subsidiary label of Decca Records specifically designated for releasing rock and roll records. "Got Wilson's Santa Blunswick, the Decker subsidiary that put out rock and roll records." ← Episode Sixty-Four： ＂Reet Petite＂ by Jackie Wilson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BYU-H4NvnmE
**Decca Subsidiary for Rockabilly (1957)** : Brunswick was one of two labels overseen by Bob Thiele, a Decca executive, and was specifically used for releasing rockabilly tracks, featuring artists such as Johnny Burnette and the Rock and Roll Trio. "Bunswick, which was where he released Rockabilly Tracks by Johnny Benett and the Rock and Roll trio." ← Episode Sixty-One： ＂That'll Be the Day＂, by the Crickets | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KVJMKLc54O0
**Deception Regarding Jackie Wilson's Earnings** : Following Jackie Wilson's collapse, Brunswick Records and Nat Tarnapol provided no financial assistance and actively defrauded Blue Cross Blue Shield by falsely claiming Wilson had no earnings from them in the year prior to his collapse, thus invalidating his insurance coverage for medical bills. "They lied to Blue Cross Blue Shield, claiming that Wilson hadn't had any earnings from them in the year prior to his collapse, when he'd been in the studio and was owed regular union rates for recording sessions." ← Episode Sixty-Four： ＂Reet Petite＂ by Jackie Wilson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BYU-H4NvnmE
**Nat Tarnapol's Acquisition of Co-Ownership** : Nat Tarnapol, Jackie Wilson's manager, strategically used his leverage as Wilson's major manager to gradually secure co-ownership of Brunswick over several years. "Over the next few years, Tarnapoll would manage to get himself made a co-owner of Thunswick, by using the leverage he got as Wilson's major." ← Episode Sixty-Four： ＂Reet Petite＂ by Jackie Wilson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BYU-H4NvnmE
**Royalty Dispute with Songwriters** : Brunswick, under Nat Tarnapol, became embroiled in a dispute with Jackie Wilson's songwriters (Gordy, Davis) when Tarnapol began exclusively placing his own copyrighted songs on the B-sides of Wilson's records to earn royalties. "After they figured out that Tarnopol was putting songs to which he owned the copyright on the b-sides of all Wilson's records, so he could get royalties from the sales." ← Episode Sixty-Four： ＂Reet Petite＂ by Jackie Wilson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BYU-H4NvnmE
