# label - Federal Records

## curiosities
**Dropped The Platters Due to Lack of Success** : Federal Records decided to drop The Platters from their label because the group had not achieved any recording success, leading to their eventual signing with Mercury Records through Book Ram's efforts. "Fed will drop them, as it looked likely they were going to do nothing." ← Episode 31： ＂Only You＂ by the Platters | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ojUNoetEX-c
**Lawsuit Over "Only You" Rush Release** : Federal Records controversially rush-released their "awful" original recording of "Only You" to capitalize on The Platters' success with Mercury. This led to a lawsuit with Book Ram and Mercury. A judge ruled Federal could release their version, but it ultimately failed to chart. "Federal rush released the awful original recording to Cachean and Ram and Mercury took them to court, which eventually ruled in favour of Federal being allowed to put out their version." ← Episode 31： ＂Only You＂ by the Platters | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ojUNoetEX-c
**Produced Linda Hayes and Signed The Flamingos** : Federal Records, where Ralph Bass worked as a producer, was involved in the early careers of several artists mentioned, producing Linda Hayes and signing the group known as The Flamingos (which would later become The Platters). "Ralph Bass, who was currently working for federal records, producing, among other artists, a singer called Linda Hayes." ← Episode 31： ＂Only You＂ by the Platters | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ojUNoetEX-c
**Signing The Famous Flames** : Federal Records, a subsidiary of King, signed The Famous Flames through A&R man Ralph Bass. Bass strongly championed their song "Please Please Please" against the initial opposition of his boss, Sid Nathan, at King. "They were signed to Federal Records, a subsidiary of King, by Ralph Bass, the visionary A&R man we've dealt with in many other episodes." ← Episode 39： ＂Please Please Please＂ by James Brown and the Famous Flames | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DSkNVh93tAs
