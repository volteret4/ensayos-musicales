# label - Satellite Records

## curiosities
**Early Struggles and Location Changes** : Satellite Records initially struggled with distribution and finances. Its first two releases, "Blue Roses" by Fred Byler and "But in High School Baby" by Don Willis, had virtually no sales. The first studio, Jim's wife's uncle's garage, became unavailable when Irwin Ellis moved his tape recorder and the uncle needed the space. "It looked like that would be the end of satellite records, but then three things changed everything for Jim's Stuart and for music history." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Move to Cinema Studio** : Due to disruptive train noise and the inconvenient location of the barber's storage space, Satellite Records moved its studio again, this time to an old abandoned cinema on East Maclamore Avenue, inspired by Hi Records' similar use of a cinema. "So they decided to move studios again, and in doing so they were inspired by another Memphis record label." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Name Change to Stax Records** : Satellite Records was forced to change its name after discovering another label in California already using the "Satellite" name. Jim Stewart, who never liked the name anyway, combined his and Estelle Axton's surnames to create "Stax Records." "Satellite records coming to the attention of another label, also called satellite, in California, who offered to sell the Memphis label the rights to use the name." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Revival and New Studio** : Satellite Records was revived with Estelle Axton's investment and moved to a barber's storage space as a studio. They restarted their numbering, considering a Valtones song their "first real release." "satellite records started up again, restarting their numbering as it from scratch, with what they were now considering their first real release, a song that moment had co-written, sung by a black vocal group, The Valtones." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
