# label - Arista Records

## curiosities
**Clive Davis's Role** : Patti Smith publicly thanked Clive Davis, presumably of Arista Records, at her Rock and Roll Hall of Fame induction, acknowledging his decision to give her a chance when she believed she had no talent. "On that night she thanked Clive Davis of Aresda for giving her a chance." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 57 - Punk and New Wave at New York's CBGB | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Clive Davis's Support** : Clive Davis, the chief of Arista Records, provided Graham Parker with a substantial advance and a generous recording budget for what would become Parker's most celebrated album, 1979's *Squeezing Out Sparks*. "The label's chief Clive Davis gave Parker a big advance and an ample recording budget for what would turn out to be Parker's most celebrated album, 1979's Squeezing Out sparks." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Angry Young Men Edition Part 1 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f780c30a1408dc2baf3
**Graham Parker's New Label** : Arista Records became Graham Parker's new label in the US after his departure from Mercury Records. "in the US by Parker's new label, Arista Records." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Angry Young Men Edition Part 1 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f780c30a1408dc2baf3
**Signed Patti Smith Group** : Arista Records signed the Patti Smith Group after their crucial seven-week residency at CBGB in spring 1975, which helped the band solidify their sound. "when those seven weeks were over they had a deal with eras to records." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 57 - Punk and New Wave at New York's CBGB | https://www.ftr70.com/
