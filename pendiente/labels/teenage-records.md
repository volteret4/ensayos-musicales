# label - Teenage Records

## curiosities
**Lost Isley Brothers Due to Unfiled Contract** : Teenage Records lost the Isley Brothers because, after they signed their contract, it was left on a desk rather than being filed. The brothers then "swiped it" when no one was looking, leaving the label without proof of their contract and allowing the group to freely move to Gone Records. "But then the contract had just been left on a desk after they'd signed it, rather than being filed, and they'd swiped it from the desk when no one was looking." ← Episode 81： ＂Shout＂ by the Isley Brothers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=USJDERCABjk
**Tiny Independent Label** : Teenage Records was a small independent label owned by two former musicians, Bill Bass Gordon and Ben Smith. It was the first label to sign the Isley Brothers and released their debut single, "Angels Crate," in June 1957.
