# label - Blanco y Negro Music

## curiosities
**Creator of the Bolero Mix Series** : Seeing the success of the Max Mix series, Blanco y Negro Music decided to launch its own compilation series called "Bolero Mix." They permanently signed Raúl Orellana as their main Megamixer for this highly successful and long-running series. "en blanco y negro, viendo el éxito de la serie, deciden sacar la soya propia, a la que llaman Bolero Mix, fichando ya definitivamente Raúl Erellana, quien se convirtió en su principal Megamixer." ← Ritmo y Extorsión： Así Dominaron los Megamix los 90s | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-qekjKky5bE
**Early Independent Record Label** : Blanco y Negro Music was an independent record label based in Barcelona that had just started its journey in the industry when it released "Studio 54 Connection." This album, mixed by Raúl Orellana, was the first mix record published in Spain. "El disco le editó Blanco en el Euro Music, una discográfica independiente de la ciudad con Dal que acababa de empezar su andadura en la industria." ← Ritmo y Extorsión： Así Dominaron los Megamix los 90s | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-qekjKky5bE
**Rival to Max Music** : Blanco y Negro Music, despite being an independent label, was one of the two main players (along with Max Music) that dominated sales in the Megamix market, even surpassing major labels like CBS, EMI, and Westpavogs. Its success rivaled that of Max Music, leading to intense competition, including Max Music's controversial hiring of Blanco y Negro's star DJ, Quique Tejada. "Pero blanco y negro, y en especial Max Music, dos discográficas independientes recién creadas estaban arrasando en ventas, superando a gigantes como CBS, Emmy, Westpavogs." ← Ritmo y Extorsión： Así Dominaron los Megamix los 90s | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-qekjKky5bE
