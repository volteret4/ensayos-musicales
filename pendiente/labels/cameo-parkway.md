# label - Cameo Parkway

## curiosities
**Acquisition by Allen Klein and Record Unavailability** : In the late 1960s, Cameo Parkway was acquired by music industry executive Allen Klein, reportedly as a "tax write-off." Consequently, between 1975 and 2005, there was no legal way to obtain any of the recordings released on the label, as they went out of print and were not issued on CD, impacting artist royalties. **Geographic Proximity and Favors for Dick Clark** : Cameo Parkway was based in Philadelphia, the same city from which Dick Clark's television show was broadcast. The label was frequently "willing to do favours for Clark," suggesting a mutually beneficial relationship. "He asked Cameo Parkway, a label based in Philadelphia, the city from which Clark's show was broadcast, and which was often willing to do favours for Clark." ← Episode 91： ＂The Twist＂ by Chubby Checker | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LzsS8EXdsm8
**Guaranteed Hit from Dick Clark** : The label was approached by Dick Clark to remake "The Twist," which represented a "guaranteed hit" due to Clark's unparalleled influence in the music industry at the time. Clark's promotion of an artist virtually assured their success. "This was pretty much a guaranteed hit for the label. Clark was the single most powerful person in the music industry at this point, and if he plugged an artist, they were going to be a success." ← Episode 91： ＂The Twist＂ by Chubby Checker | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LzsS8EXdsm8
**Primary Focus as a Novelty Label** : Cameo Parkway was typically known as a novelty label, and usually did not deal in rock and roll or R&B music. Their involvement with "The Twist" was an exception, driven by the opportunity presented by Dick Clark.
