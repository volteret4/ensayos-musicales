# label - ASCAP

## curiosities
**1941 Boycott** : For ten months in 1941, ASCAP banned all US radio stations from playing any of its songs due to a royalty dispute. This boycott, intended to be catastrophic for radio, instead provided an enormous boost to rival organization BMI and the genres it represented, such as blues, country, and boogie. **Dominant Songwriter/Publisher Organization** : The American Society of Composers and Publishers was, and still is, an organization representing most of the important songwriters and music publishers in the USA. They collected royalties for live performances and radio plays for their members, focusing on "respectable Tin Pan Alley composers." In the early 1940s, they controlled a repertoire of over a quarter-million songs, including most popular songs played by big bands. **Malismal Aversion Among Registered Songwriters** : Songwriters registered with ASCAP in the mid-20th century, particularly those writing pop, Broadway, and crooner repertoire, held a strong view that malisma was "pure laziness," adhering instead to a strict rule of one syllable of lyric for one note of melody.
