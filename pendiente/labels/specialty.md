# label - Specialty

## curiosities
**Release of Backlog After Little Richard's Hiatus** : After Little Richard effectively stopped recording rock and roll due to his religious conversion, Specialty Records capitalized on his undiminished demand by releasing a substantial backlog of his recordings over the next couple of years, including chart-topping hits like "Good Golly Miss Molly" as well as substandard material initially not intended for release. "Whatever the truth, specialty had enough of a backlog of little Richard recordings that they could keep issuing them for the next couple of years." ← Episode 54： Keep A Knockin | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oA4Y_K9E5Y4
**Unfavorable Contract Terms with Little Richard** : Specialty Records' contract with Little Richard paid him only half a cent per record sold, which he considered "far too low," and its terms stipulated that the contract could only be terminated by his death or an "act of God." This led John Mariscalco to suggest Richard's religious conversion was an attempt to legally escape these terms.
