# label - Roulette

## curiosities
**Hugo and Luigi's Co-ownership** : Roulette was a new label set up by Morris Levy, George Goldner, and Alan Freed. Goldner and Freed quickly dropped out, but Hugo and Luigi ended up with a 50% stake in the label, producing numerous hits and their own instrumental tracks. "Morris Levy, who was setting up a new label, Roulette, with George Goldner and Alan Freed." ← Episode 81： ＂Shout＂ by the Isley Brothers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=USJDERCABjk
**Morris Levy's Exploitative Label** : Roulette Records was established by Morris Levy, known for his mafia connections and predatory business practices. Along with his publishing company, Patricia Music, Roulette served as a primary vehicle for Levy to exploit the intellectual property of young, often Black, artists. "Levy then formed a publishing company, Patricia Music, and a record label, Roulette, and started into the business of properly exploiting young black people." ← Episode 35： ＂Why Do Fools Fall In Love？＂ by Frankie Lymon and the Teenagers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AtZf9PJEttM
**Morris Levy's Unfair Practices** : When Hugo and Luigi were recruited by RCA, Morris Levy, the owner of Roulette, released them from their contract only on the condition that they relinquish their shares in Roulette for a price "far less than they were worth."
