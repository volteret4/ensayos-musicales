# label - Richmond Organization

## curiosities
**Conglomerate Owner of Folkways** : The Richmond Organization was a much larger conglomerate that owned Folkways, the company responsible for promoting folk music and signing The Weavers. **Legal Battle Over Copyright Renewal** : In 1989, when George Weiss sought to renew the copyright for "The Lion Sleeps Tonight" and exclude the Richmond Organization from publishing, they took him to court. They argued that the song was based on "Wimoweh," which they owned. Although the court sided with Weiss, it acknowledged a point regarding the song's similarity to "Wimoweh," leading to a small payment to Solomon Linda's family. "As soon as the Richmond Organization heard about this, they took Weiss to court, saying that he couldn't take the publishing rights away from them, because the song was based on Wimaway, which they owned." ← Episode 92： ＂The Lion Sleeps Tonight＂ by the Tokens | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QMP_DFyktMg
**Publisher of "Wimoweh" and "The Lion Sleeps Tonight"** : The Richmond Organization acted as the publisher for "Wimoweh." Later, in an amicable agreement, they received the publishing money for "The Lion Sleeps Tonight" while the credited songwriters (Weiss, Hugo, Luigi) retained songwriting royalties.
