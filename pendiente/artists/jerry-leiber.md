**Belief in Missed Artistic Potential with Elvis (c. 1958)** : Jerry Leiber expressed the conviction that with proper artistic freedom, he and Mike Stoller could have "made history" with Elvis. He believed that Elvis's management team was solely focused on financial gain, leading to a routine of producing generic songs for films that stifled creative ambition. **Complex Artistic Attitude Towards Elvis's Work (c. 1958)** : Jerry Leiber, as half of the songwriting duo Leiber and Stoller, held a nuanced view of his work with Elvis. While he liked Elvis and respected his studio work ethic, he considered much of their output for Elvis, particularly film songs, to be "easy money" rather than "real art" in the way their R&B records were. "But at the same time, they didn't consider the work they were doing with Elvis to be real art, in the way they considered their R and B records to be." ← Episode 72： ＂Trouble＂ by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=psIgGqkQhZI
**Contemptuous Comparison of "Trouble" (1958)** : Jerry Leiber compared "Trouble," a song he co-wrote as a joke, to "Black Denim Trousers and Motorcycle Boots." He expressed a sense of "contempt" for the idea that only "Hell's Angels and Elvis Presley" would take such songs seriously, implying that Elvis's rendition of the bluesy track didn't sound "right" to him. **Pneumonia and Final Break with Elvis (June 1958)** : Jerry Leiber contracted pneumonia, which prevented him from traveling to an Elvis recording session in June 1958. This health issue, combined with Colonel Parker's insistence and Mike Stoller's defiant advice, ultimately led to the permanent separation of Leiber and Stoller from working in the studio with Elvis and writing songs specifically for him.
