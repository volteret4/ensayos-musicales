## curiosities
**Arrest at The Troubadour (October 24, 1962)** : Legendary comedian Lenny Bruce was arrested by the LA County Sheriff's Vice Squad at The Troubadour on October 24, 1962. This was his third arrest in three weeks and was for uttering the "obscene word" "schmuck" in both English and Yiddish. News articles from the time carried headlines like "sick comic Lenny Bruce arrested again." "Legendary comedian Lenny Bruce was arrested by the LA County Sheriff's Vice Squad on October 24th, 1964. **Legal Problems and Overdose Death** : Bruce faced numerous legal problems that severely hampered his ability to secure gigs. Tragically, he died in 1966 from a morphine overdose. His premature death raises questions about what his career might have been had he overcome his addiction, especially with the explosion of comedy in the 1970s. **Trailblazer for Stand-Up Comedy** : Lenny Bruce is considered responsible for the evolution of stand-up comedy into a smarter, more current, and edgier form, paving the way for 1970s comedians. Richard Zoglin noted Steve Martin's comedy was a take on how Bruce made fun of "the old showbiz."
