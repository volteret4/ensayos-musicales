## curiosities
**Forming Three Dots and a Dash** : After gaining experience with Johnny Otis, Big Jay McNeely embarked on a solo career and formed his own vocal group, Three Dots and a Dash. He personally invited Jesse Belvin to join this group as the lead singer, marking one of Belvin's early and formative recording experiences. "As with many of the musicians Otis worked with, McNally soon went on to a solo career of his own, and he formed a vocal group, Three Dots and a Dash." ← Episode 47： ＂Goodnight My Love＂ by Jesse Belvin | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eoyk3H0M6r8
**Saxophone Honker** : McNeely was known as one of the "old-time great saxophone honkers," a style largely inspired by Illinois Jacquet. He became the lead tenor saxophone player for Johnny Otis's band at the Barrel House Club, and his playing featured prominently on records such as Otis's "Barrel House Stomp." "McNally was one of the old-time great saxophone hunkers, inspired mostly by Illinois's Ikeh't, and he had become the lead tenor saxophone player with Johnny Otis's band at the Barrel House Club, and played on records like Otis's Barrel House Stump." ← Episode 47： ＂Goodnight My Love＂ by Jesse Belvin | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eoyk3H0M6r8
**Student of Samuel Brown** : Big Jay McNeely, a prominent rhythm and blues saxophone player, was a student of the influential music teacher Samuel Brown at Jefferson High School. This connection highlights Brown's significant, though often unsung, impact on rock and roll music through the many talented artists he taught.
