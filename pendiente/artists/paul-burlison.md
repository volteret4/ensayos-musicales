## curiosities
**Accidental Amplifier Distortion in Philadelphia** : During a show in Philadelphia on the way to Nashville, Paul Burlison dropped his amplifier, which loosened one of the internal vacuum tubes, producing a distorted sound he had never heard before. "At that show, Paul Burlison dropped his amplifier, loosening one of the vacuum tubes inside." ← Episode 44： ＂Train Kept A-Rollin'＂, by Johnny Burnette and the Rock 'n' Roll Trio | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u3zYAJ2qeR4
**Boxing Colleague of Dorsey** : Paul Burlison was a colleague of Dorsey Burnette at Crown Electric, where he also boxed, and was introduced to Dorsey by Lee Denson. "Another colleague at Crown Electric was a man named Paul Berlison, who also boxed and had been introduced to Dorsey by Lee Denson, who had taught both Dorsey and Elvis their first guitar courts." ← Episode 44： ＂Train Kept A-Rollin'＂, by Johnny Burnette and the Rock 'n' Roll Trio | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u3zYAJ2qeR4

**Death in 2003** : Paul Burlison continued to play rockabilly music until his death in 2003 at the age of 74. "He kept playing Rocky Billy until he died in 2003, aged 74." ← Episode 44： ＂Train Kept A-Rollin'＂, by Johnny Burnette and the Rock 'n' Roll Trio | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u3zYAJ2qeR4
**Deliberate Distortion Technique** : After the accidental amplifier distortion, Burlison began intentionally loosening the vacuum tube every time he wanted to achieve that unique sound, marking a pioneering moment in deliberately recorded distorted guitar. "The distorted sound it gave was like nothing he'd ever heard, and while he replaced the tube, he started loosening it, every time he wanted to get that sound." ← Episode 44： ＂Train Kept A-Rollin'＂, by Johnny Burnette and the Rock 'n' Roll Trio | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u3zYAJ2qeR4
**Early Radio Show and Howlin' Wolf Influence** : In the late 40s and early 50s, he played in a band that had a regular 15-minute show on a local radio station, aired next to a show presented by blues singer Howlin' Wolf, whose electric blues significantly influenced Burlison's guitar playing. "In particular, one of the bands he was in had its own regular 15-minute show on a local radio station and their show was on next to a show presented by the Blues singer Howlin Wolf." ← Episode 44： ＂Train Kept A-Rollin'＂, by Johnny Burnette and the Rock 'n' Roll Trio | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u3zYAJ2qeR4
**Possible Howlin' Wolf Recording Session** : Some sources suggest Paul Burlison played on some of Howlin' Wolf's early recordings at Sun Studios, although most sessionographies for Wolf dispute this claim. "Some sources even say that Berlison played on some of Wolf's early recordings at the Sun Studios, though most of the sessionographies I've seen for Wolf say otherwise." ← Episode 44： ＂Train Kept A-Rollin'＂, by Johnny Burnette and the Rock 'n' Roll Trio | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u3zYAJ2qeR4
**Post-Trio Career as Electrician** : After the Rock and Roll Trio split up, Paul Burlison gave up music and returned to his profession as an electrician. "Paul Burlison, the last member of the trio, gave up music once the trio split up and became an electrician again." ← Episode 44： ＂Train Kept A-Rollin'＂, by Johnny Burnette and the Rock 'n' Roll Trio | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u3zYAJ2qeR4
**Return to Performing in 1980s** : In the 1980s, he re-entered the music scene, forming a new lineup of the Rock and Roll Trio, featuring Johnny Black (who had temporarily replaced Dorsey) and touring drummer Tony Austin. "He then got back into performing and started a new lineup of the rock and roll trio, featuring Johnny Black, who had briefly replaced Dorsey in the group, and Tony Austin, the drummer who had joined with them on many tour dates after they got a recording contract." ← Episode 44： ＂Train Kept A-Rollin'＂, by Johnny Burnette and the Rock 'n' Roll Trio | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u3zYAJ2qeR4
