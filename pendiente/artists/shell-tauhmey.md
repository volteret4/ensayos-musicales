## curiosities
**Disputed Original "You Really Got Me" Production** : Shell Tauhmey produced the initial recording of "You Really Got Me," which Ray and Dave Davis strongly disliked, claiming he "overproduced it, turning it into a full specter sound alike, and drenched the whole thing with echo." Tauhmey, however, defended his version, asserting that the main difference was a slower tempo and that it was a "very different but equally valid take on the song." **Fake Production Credits in UK (1962)** : Shell Tauhmey, an engineer from Los Angeles, came to the UK in 1962 and sought work in the music industry. A friend, Nick Vennett, provided him with a stack of acetates of recent Capitol Records productions (including "Surf and Safari" by The Beach Boys and "Music in the Air" by Lou Rawls) and suggested he "pretend to produce them" to secure work. Tauhmey then presented these to Dick Rowe at Decca, who hired him to produce records, starting with hits like "Charmaine" by The Bachelors.
