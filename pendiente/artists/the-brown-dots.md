## members

## songs
**I Love You for Sentimental Reasons** : This song was written by two members of The Brown Dots and became a hugely influential record. It exemplified a "completely new idea" for vocal groups, echoing The Ink Spots' formula with a guitar intro, a high tenor lead, spoken lyrics, and a high tenor finish, despite Watson's claim of originality. ## curiosities
**Formation by Deke Watson** : After losing the initial legal battle over The Ink Spots' name, Deke Watson formed The Brown Dots, proclaiming it a new vocal group based on a "completely new idea." This idea, ironically, closely mirrored The Ink Spots' signature "Top and Bottom" formula, including the guitar intro, high-tenor lead, spoken lyrics, and tenor finish. "Deke Watson, meanwhile, had been performing with his own ink spots before the lawsuits. But once they were settled and not in his favour, he said he was going to form a new vocal group based on a completely new idea." ← Episode 6： ＂That's When Your Heartaches Begin＂ by the Ink Spots | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9mYSQaysXRg
**Short-Lived Success and Breakup** : While "I Love You for Sentimental Reasons" proved to be a successful and influential record for The Brown Dots, the band's existence was short-lived. The other band members eventually left due to difficulties in getting along with Deke Watson, forming their own successful group, The Four Tunes, without him.
