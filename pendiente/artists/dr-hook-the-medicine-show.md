It became a hit, reaching number six on the Billboard Hot 100, and significantly boosted the Rolling Stone magazine's ethos. The band was featured on the cover of the March 29, 1973, issue, though it was a caricature drawing of three members. ## curiosities
**Anti-Showbiz Ethos** : Lead singer Dennis Locoreer described the band's approach as avoiding flashy poses or showbiz. They developed their performance style by making up songs and voices during long drives between gigs, aiming for a "loose feeling" on stage to entertain themselves and the audience. "We were told if we could get that loose feeling on stage and forget about showbiz then we could have fun and entertain the audience at the same time." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 44 - Streaking, Werewolves, Sharks, & Drinking - 70s Novelty Songs | https://traffic.libsyn.com/seventies/334334714-44100-2-e5ed6adac25ea.m4a
