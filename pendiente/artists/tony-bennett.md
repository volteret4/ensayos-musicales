## curiosities
**Performer of Johnny Mercer Standards** : Tony Bennett was one of the esteemed artists who performed numerous standards composed by Johnny Mercer, the individual who founded Capitol Records. **Target of Coasters' Market Transition** : Tony Bennett was identified as an artist whose records were purchased by the "adult white market," which The Coasters aimed to transition into as their teenage pop fan base declined. **Vocal Style Avoiding Malisma** : Tony Bennett is identified as one of the notable vocalists from the first half of the 20th century who adhered to the practice of singing one note per syllable, hitting each note precisely without excessive ornamentation, consistent with the prevailing definition of good singing at the time. "If you listen to any of the great voices of the first part of the 20th century, Sinatra, Bing Crosby, Tony Bennett, they will almost without exception hit the note dead arm, one note per syllable." ← Episode 32： ＂I Got A Woman＂ by Ray Charles | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0oeI9f2x_fo
