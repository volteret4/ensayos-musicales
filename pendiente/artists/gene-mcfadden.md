# artist - Gene McFadden

## labels
- Philadelphia International Records

## songs
**Ain't No Stoppin' Us Now (1979)** : This song, co-written and performed by Gene McFadden and John Whitehead, became Philadelphia International Records' last major hit in the 1970s and one of the decade's final significant dance records. The writers explicitly viewed it as a declaration of their artistic independence from Kenny Gamble and his creative control at PIR. ## curiosities
**Discontent at PIR** : Gene McFadden, along with John Whitehead, grew increasingly dissatisfied with their roles at Philadelphia International Records, particularly regarding Kenny Gamble's insistence that they remain solely as songwriters despite their aspirations to perform, and Gamble's perceived overemphasis on a "black lens" in the company's output.
