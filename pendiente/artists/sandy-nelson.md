## curiosities
**Accused of Credit Theft** : Richie Polador accused Sandy Nelson, along with Bruce Johnston and Kim Fowley, of having "no scruples whatsoever" and taking credit for songs like "Geronimo" and "Charge" that Polador claimed he wrote and recorded in his own studio. "There are three guys in the business who really have no scruples whatsoever. They are Bruce Johnston, Kim Fowley and Sandy Nelson, and I was Mr Scruples." ← Episode 86： ＂LSD-25＂ by the Gamblers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UNd6UwbL1rA
**Sacked from The Debarrens** : Sandy Nelson was fired from The Debarrens by Jan Berry for playing his drums too loudly. Upon his departure, he famously declared, "you'll see, I'm going to have a hit record that's only drums." This prophecy was later partially fulfilled with his hit "Team Beat." "In Sandy Nelson's case, he was sacked by Berry for playing his drum so loud." ← Episode 86： ＂LSD-25＂ by the Gamblers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UNd6UwbL1rA
**Session Work Success** : Nelson's departure from The Flips coincided with a significant increase in session work, as many people had seen him play live. His drum performance on The Teddy Bears' number one hit "To Know Him Is To Love Him," despite being described as rudimentary, significantly boosted his "cachet" for future gigs, including playing on Gene Vincent's "Crazy Times" LP.
