## curiosities
**"American Bandstand" Host and "Suzy Q" Support** : Dick Clark, the host of "American Bandstand," was a television presenter whose show played a crucial role in breaking Dale Hawkins' "Suzy Q" on the national market. Hawkins later used this connection to launch his own TV program, "The Dale Hawkins Show." **Academy Award and Golden Globe Winner (1978)** : "Last Dance" was chosen as the Best Original Song at the 1978 Academy Awards, triumphing over other notable contenders like Barry Manilow's song from *Foul Play* and Olivia Newton-John's "Hopelessly Devoted to You" from *Grease*. It also secured a Golden Globe for Best Original Song in the same year. "they chose last dance. Last dance. One and Oscar for Best Original Song. **Ambitious Film Project (1973)** : In 1973, at the age of 43, Dick Clark expressed a strong desire to produce a movie documenting the 20-year history of rock music. Although he was an entertainment mogul with various ventures by this time, the movie was never made. "In 1973, Dick Clark, who was 43 at the time, said he really wanted to do a movie about the time, 20 year history of rock." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 47 - Dick Clark's Entertainment Empire | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Booked Cornel Gunter's Coasters** : Dick Clark, who was friends with Cornel Gunter, began booking Gunter's version of The Coasters for tours. This meant that Clark favored Gunter's touring group over the version of The Coasters that was recording in the studio. "Gunter was friends with Dick Clark and so Clark started to book Gunter's version of the group rather than the version that was in the studio." ← Episode 68： ＂Yakety Yak＂ by the Coasters | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dtMHDD0-oOo
**Broadcasting Legacy** : Dick Clark is widely remembered for his significant contributions to entertainment, especially through programs like *American Bandstand* and *New Year's Rockin' Eve*. His work went beyond mere business, as he reportedly wished for his children to understand what was happening during his lifetime, creating a lasting legacy for many. "American bandstand and New Year's Rock and Eve were just that. He so much is said that this is what he wanted his legacy to be. He wanted his kids to know what was happening when he walked the planet." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 47 - Dick Clark's Entertainment Empire | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Involvement in Payola Practices** : Dick Clark was known to participate in payola, the widespread practice where DJs and TV presenters would accept various forms of payment or benefits to promote records. Despite his involvement, he avoided the scapegoat role that befell Alan Fried during the payola scandals. "Dick Clark certainly did, and while no one other than the DJs liked the practice, the small labels that built rock and roll... all saw it as a way that they could equalise things a little bit." ← Episode 67： ＂Johnny B. Goode＂, by Chuck Berry | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sHlA-qihuNw
**Radio Documentary "Rock Roller"** : Dick Clark hosted a radio documentary show titled "Rock Roller." This program may have served to satisfy his interest in documenting rock history, possibly compensating for his unproduced movie project. **Rising Influence in Rock and Roll** : Dick Clark's stature in the rock and roll world grew significantly, becoming more important than figures like Alan Fried. This was evident in Chuck Berry's lyrics for "Sweet Little Sixteen," which updated geographical references to "on Bandstand and in Philadelphia PA," reflecting the prominence of Clark's "American Bandstand" show.
