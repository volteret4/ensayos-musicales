## songs
**LSD25 (Year Unknown)** : This song is the B-side of The Gamblers' single and holds the distinction of being, without any doubt, the first rock and roll record to mention LSD. This makes it a distant ancestor of psychedelic music. Co-written by Derry Weaver and Larry Taylor, both musicians later stated they knew nothing about the drug, claiming they had simply read a magazine article about "acid" and liked the name. Bruce Johnston, a likely member, held a strong anti-drug stance and never indulged. **Moon Dog (Year Unknown)** : Written by Derry Weaver, this song was the A-side of The Gamblers' single. It became a local hit in Southern California, though it did not achieve significant sales elsewhere. Nick Vene (Renee), who owned the rights to the master tape and publishing, later used his position at Capitol Records to include "Moon Dog" on the compilation album "Golden Gasses," which featured major acts like Sam Cooke and The Four Creps, thereby exposing the song to a wider audience. This led to covers by artists like The Ventures, The Surfaris, and The Beach Boys. Larry Taylor's brother, Mel Taylor, who was the drummer for The Ventures, might have helped bring the track to their attention. Nick Vene, The Beach Boys' producer, may also have influenced their cover, and Derry Weaver is credited on some session sheets for The Beach Boys' version, though this is debated as a potential credit inflation by Vene. Historians of the surf music genre generally point to "Moon Dog" as the first surf music record. ## curiosities
**Conflicting Lineup Claims** : The exact lineup of The Gamblers for the "Moon Dog" / "LSD25" single is shrouded in mystery and subject to significant dispute. While the record features two guitarists, bass, drums, and keyboards, as many as 15 people have been credited over the years. Nick Renee claimed the band was originally "Nick and the Gamblers" with himself, Bruce Johnston, Sandy Nelson, Larry Taylor, James Burton, and Richie Polador engineering, with Kim Fowley involved. Kim Fowley, unusually, denied any involvement. Elliot Ingber stated the group consisted of himself, Larry Taylor, Derry Weaver, Howard Hirsch, and Rod Schaefer. Bruce Johnston had no memory of the record. Given Derry Weaver's credit as writer for "Moon Dog" and the existence of his previous band The Moon Dogs, Elliot Ingber's list is considered mostly correct, with Bruce Johnston likely contributing wordless harmonies, leading to a guessed lineup of Derry Weaver, Larry Taylor, Elliot Ingber, Bruce Johnston, and either Rod Schaefer or Sandy Nelson. **Mystery and Unreliable Accounts** : Details about The Gamblers are largely mysterious, pieced together from fragmented and often contradictory recollections from participants. Many individuals involved are known for credit theft, while others were jobbing musicians who, decades later, were unsure if they even played on the record.
