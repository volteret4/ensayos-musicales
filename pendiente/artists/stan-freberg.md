# artist - Stan Freberg

## genres
- Comedy
- Novelty

## songs
**Shaboon (1954)** : A top 20 hit, "Shaboon" showcased Freberg's silly musical deconstructions of popular genres, in this case, Doo-wop. It further solidified his reputation for novelty records that blended satire with catchy melodies. **St. George and the Dragonet (1953)** : This number one smash comedy record was a groundbreaking parody of the popular "Dragnet" radio dramas. It exemplified Freberg's distinctive novelty style, combining spoken word elements with melodic passages and farcical humor. "His singles were of the novelty variety, sometimes spoken word, sometimes melodic, virtually always farcical. ## curiosities
**Comedy Renaissance Man** : Stan Freberg was described as a "comedy renaissance man" due to his successful parallel careers as both a recording artist and an advertising pitchman. He was celebrated for his ability to inject humor and satire into both his musical works and his commercial campaigns. **Normalization of Dual Careers** : Freberg was one of the earliest recording artists to successfully normalize a pop career coexisting alongside an advertising career. He brought his unique absurdist wit and satirical style to both domains, blurring the lines between art and commerce.
