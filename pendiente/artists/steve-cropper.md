## curiosities
**"Last Night" Authorship Theory** : Steve Cropper attributed the success of The Mar-Keys' instrumental "Last Night" to its danceability, specifically its suitability for the twist craze. **Early Band Leadership** : Steve Cropper led The Royal Spades, an all-white group passionate about R&B. He played guitar in the band, which later welcomed Charles Axton and evolved into The Mar-Keys. "The group was led by guitarist Steve Cropper" ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Origins of "Green Onions"** : Cropper remembered a distinct musical lick he and Booker T. Jones had heard on the radio, but couldn't identify the song. This lick became the foundation for "Green Onions," which they improvised into a 12-bar instrumental during an unplanned session. "Cropper remembered a little musical lick he'd heard on the radio one day when he'd been driving with Booker." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Producer Role Post-Moman** : After Chips Moman's departure from Stax, Steve Cropper shared the production duties with Jim Stewart. Cropper had been diligently learning how to make records and was well-equipped to take on this responsibility. "Jim Stewart and Steve Cropper split the production duties that Momen had had between them." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Quitting The Mar-Keys Tour** : Cropper left The Mar-Keys' tour to promote "Last Night" due to resentment over the other teenage band members' unruly behavior and a leadership conflict between himself and Charles Axton. He returned to Memphis to work in the record shop and play on Stax sessions. "Kropper soon got tired of the tour, quit the group, and came back to Memphis." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Reasons for Duck Dunn Replacing Louis Steinberg** : Cropper stated that Louis Steinberg was replaced by Duck Dunn in Booker T and the M.G.'s because Dunn's "hard-eared style" better suited their music. He also mentioned that Dunn was his best friend and he wanted to play more with him, implicitly downplaying Steinberg's claim of racial motivation. "Krupper has always said, he was because Duck Dunn had a hard-eared style that fit their music better than Steinberg's Lucifer Hill, but also that Dunn had been his best friend for years and he wanted to play more with him." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Songwriting Credits** : Steve Cropper co-wrote several influential soul classics, including "In the Midnight Hour," "Dock of the Bay," and "Knock on Wood," showcasing his significant talent as a songwriter for Stax. "even the man who co-wrote in the midnight hour, Doc of the Bay, and Knock on Ward, had his off days." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Studio and Record Shop Contributions** : Cropper assisted in refurbishing the cinema into the Stax recording studio and worked in Estelle Axton's record shop, often helping behind the counter. This dual role made him readily available for studio sessions. "Cropper and Charles Axton helped with the refurbishing of the cinema into a recording studio." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Unsuccessful Country Production** : Steve Cropper produced "The Three Dogwoods" by Nick Charles, a novelty country song told from the perspective of the tree used for Jesus's crucifixion. This record was a commercial failure, demonstrating that even prolific songwriters like Cropper had "off days."
