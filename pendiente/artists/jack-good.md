## curiosities
**Creator of Oh Boy!** : Following creative differences at the BBC, he moved to ABC, an ITV station, where he launched a rival show named "Oh Boy!" This program was entirely focused on music, reflecting Good's strong artistic ideas drawn from diverse sources, including Shakespearean theatrical companies that used stark, all-black backgrounds and a book on the physiology of brainwashing, aiming to create something powerful. "So good moved over to ABC, one of the ITV stations, and started a rival show, Oh Boy." ← Episode 70： ＂Move It＂ by Cliff Richard and the Drifters | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uoEWP94f6S4
**Inspired by Rock Around the Clock** : Originally an actor, his perspective was transformed after watching the film "Rock Around the Clock" in the cinema and observing the audience's reactions. This experience convinced him that audience engagement was a critical component of Rock and Roll and that live performances could achieve a cathartic effect similar to classical Greek drama. "Good had been an actor, until he saw a rock around the clock in the cinema, and saw the way that audiences reacted to the film." ← Episode 70： ＂Move It＂ by Cliff Richard and the Drifters | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uoEWP94f6S4
**Mentoring Cliff Richard** : He insisted that Cliff Richard perform "Move It" on his show and intensively coached Cliff for a full week on his performance. Good provided detailed instructions covering every blink, every instance to clutch his arm as if in pain, and how he should look down rather than directly at the audience. He also selected Cliff's shocking pink outfit (designed to cause a frenzy among the girls, though not visible on black and white TV) and directed the styling of Cliff's hair to be less like Elvis's. "Good work with Cliff for a full week on his performance of the song, instructing him in every blink, every time he should clutch his arm as if in pain, the way he should look down, not straight at the audience, everything." ← Episode 70： ＂Move It＂ by Cliff Richard and the Drifters | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uoEWP94f6S4

**Producer of "Around the Beatles" (1964)** : Jack Good produced "Around the Beatles," a television special that starred The Beatles and also featured an appearance by Millie Small, highlighting the cross-cultural appeal of music at the time. **Shaping Rock and Roll Image** : More than any other individual, Jack Good was instrumental in developing the visual persona of the rock and roll star. His influence was evident in virtually every visual depiction of rock and roll music from the early 1960s onwards. "It was Jack Good, who, more than anyone else, came up with the image of the Rock and Roll star, and his influence could be seen in literally every visual depiction of Rock and Roll music from the early 60s on." ← Episode 70： ＂Move It＂ by Cliff Richard and the Drifters | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uoEWP94f6S4
**Vision for Rock and Roll TV** : He applied his innovative ideas to the BBC's new teenage program, "6-5 Special," making the audience an integral part of the show and positioning performers among the crowd rather than separating them. Despite his vision for a purely rock and roll show, he had to compromise with his co-producer, leading to the inclusion of light music, educational segments, and sports.
