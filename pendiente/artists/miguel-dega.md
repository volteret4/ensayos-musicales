# artist - Miguel DeGa

## curiosities
**Co-founder of Max Music** : Miguel DeGa, a salesman who previously sold Italo Disco maxis in discotheques, decided with Ricardo Campoy to form their own company, Max Music, in 1964. They were central figures in the Spanish Megamix phenomenon and were known for their unscrupulous business practices. "Miguel de Gaid Ricardo Campoy, dos comerciales 20 a.C. que se ganaban la vida vendiendo Maxis de Italo Disco en discotecas, deciden que ha llegado el momento de formar su propia empresa y crean el sillo Max Music en 1964." ← Ritmo y Extorsión： Así Dominaron los Megamix los 90s | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-qekjKky5bE
**Criminal Actions and Imprisonment** : Following Ricardo Campoy's departure from Max Music to found Vale Music, Miguel DeGa's response escalated into criminal activity. On September 3, 1998, DeGa hired 13 Mexican hitmen to kidnap Campoy. Due to a mistaken identity, José María Castells was kidnapped instead. DeGa was subsequently convicted and sentenced to prison, from which he escaped in 2005 during a temporary leave, and his whereabouts remain unknown. "El 3 de septiembre de 1998, el empresario contrató a 13 carios mexicanos para secuestrar al Ricardo Campoy." ← Ritmo y Extorsión： Así Dominaron los Megamix los 90s | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-qekjKky5bE
**Exporting Megamixes to International Markets** : Miguel DeGa was instrumental in expanding the Megamix phenomenon beyond Spain. He opened an office in Miami and successfully exported the concept to countries like Germany, Argentina, and Mexico. "Miguel le agabrió una oficina en Miami y exportó al fenómeno Megamix al extranjero." ← Ritmo y Extorsión： Así Dominaron los Megamix los 90s | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-qekjKky5bE
**Unscrupulous Business Practices** : Miguel DeGa and Ricardo Campoy, the duo behind Max Music, were noted for their lack of scruples in generating profit. Their methods included corporate espionage, pressuring distributors, and commissioning cover versions of songs to avoid paying rights for the originals.
