# artist - PXXR GANG

## members
- Cecilio G (legendary initial member)
- John Biff
- K.D.K.in
- Khaled
- Steve Lean

## genres
- Reggaeton (as La Mafia del Amor)
- Trap

## labels
- Sony Music

## concerts
- Primavera Sound
- Sónar

## albums
**Los Pobres (2015) - Sony Music Debut** : PXXR GANG signed a contract with Sony Music in 2015 to release this single album. According to them, they sent Sony a handful of songs they considered mediocre, intending to mock the label and cash the check. "Según ellos, enviaron a Sony un puñado de canciones que consideraban medio crees para reírse de la discográfica y cobrar el cheque." ← La salvaje historia del TRAP ESPAÑOL (2013-2026) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4hBhiU5jj6s
**Mixtape Gratuito (2015)** : Shortly after the release of "Los Pobres" with Sony Music, PXXR GANG launched a free mixtape. This act was seen as unthinkable if they had signed an exclusivity contract with the label, further underscoring their rebellious approach to the music industry. "A los pocos días lanzaron un mixtape gratuito, algo intensable si hubiese firmado un contrato de exclusividad con ellos." ← La salvaje historia del TRAP ESPAÑOL (2013-2026) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4hBhiU5jj6s

## curiosities
**Confrontation with N.E.N.O. DE CLICCO** : In 2014, PXXR GANG and N.E.N.O. DE CLICCO clashed at a concert, leading to a physical altercation where K.D.K.in slapped Enry-K. The next day, K.D.K.in and John Biff posted a legendary video calmly explaining the incident while eating spaghetti. "Los espaguetis. En 2014, la purga niem de clicco incidieron en un concierto." ← La salvaje historia del TRAP ESPAÑOL (2013-2026) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4hBhiU5jj6s
**Connection to Vaporwave and Street Culture** : PXXR GANG adopted and popularized a specific aesthetic that combined elements of vaporwave (previously popularized by Yung Lean and Sad Boys) with gritty street culture. This blend allowed them to connect with a wider audience in Spanish neighborhoods. "Esa estética con referencias al vape orwave, la habían puesto de moda John Lin su colectivo asadvois, pero la pur gang logró conectar más con la comentitud de los barrios al mezclar, todo esto con lo que a llegero." ← La salvaje historia del TRAP ESPAÑOL (2013-2026) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4hBhiU5jj6s
**Digital Distribution Strategy** : PXXR GANG leveraged the internet, abandoning physical formats and using YouTube to monetize their tracks directly. They understood social media and recognized the music video as a crucial format, producing material often filmed with mobile phones in the street. "Entendían perfectamente a las redes, y sabían que el videoclip era un formato clave." ← La salvaje historia del TRAP ESPAÑOL (2013-2026) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4hBhiU5jj6s
**Early Negative Reception** : Initially, PXXR GANG's videos were met with negative comments online. Viewers criticized the excessive use of Auto-Tune, accused them of misogynistic and violent lyrics, and claimed their music wasn't "real rap." "En un principio se llenaron de comentarios negativos. Haciendo referencia al exceso de Autotune, acusandoles de que tenían letras machistas y violentas, y acusandoles de no ser rap." ← La salvaje historia del TRAP ESPAÑOL (2013-2026) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4hBhiU5jj6s
**Foundation in El Raval** : PXXR GANG was founded in El Raval, Barcelona, by John Biff, Khaled, K.D.K.in, and Steve Lean. The group's formation in this neighborhood, which they called the "true Spanish trap house," was a pivotal moment for the Spanish trap scene. "Y una vez allí, junto a Steve Lin, fundan la banda que lo cambiará todo, la pur gang." ← La salvaje historia del TRAP ESPAÑOL (2013-2026) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4hBhiU5jj6s
**Glorification of Street Hustle** : Their aesthetic glorified "Joseo," which refers to the act of finding ways to make money, whether through music or street hustling. This narrative resonated with their audience. "Esa es la glorificación de Joséo, de buscarte la vida y generar dinero como sea, ya sea con la música o con el trapicheo." ← La salvaje historia del TRAP ESPAÑOL (2013-2026) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4hBhiU5jj6s
**Influence of 90s Aesthetics and Internet Culture** : The group, being digital natives, drew heavily from 90s aesthetics, internet culture, and video games. They blended these influences with street aesthetics, connecting deeply with urban youth. "Se inspiraron mucho en la estética de los años 90, la cultura de internet y los videojuegos." ← La salvaje historia del TRAP ESPAÑOL (2013-2026) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4hBhiU5jj6s
**Legend of Cecilio G's Involvement** : Legend has it that Cecilio G, a chaotic figure, was an initial member of PXXR GANG and gave the group its name as a parody of Rich Gang. The veracity of this story is debated, blurring the lines between fact and myth. "La leyenda cuenta que hubo un miembro más en sus inicios, una figura caótica, la que le expuso el nombre como una parodia de la Rich Gang, el indomable Ececiliogé." ← La salvaje historia del TRAP ESPAÑOL (2013-2026) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4hBhiU5jj6s
**Parallel Reggaeton Project: La Mafia del Amor** : PXXR GANG also formed a parallel reggaeton project called La Mafia del Amor. This demonstrated their willingness to blend genres, mixing trap with Latin music. "la porgang de hecho formó el proyecto paraleno del regetón la mafía del amor." ← La salvaje historia del TRAP ESPAÑOL (2013-2026) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4hBhiU5jj6s
**Performance with Salsa Combo at Primavera Sound** : PXXR GANG had a memorable performance at festivals like Sónar and Primavera Sound, where they played some of their most iconic songs with a salsa combo. "donde tiene un sofamoso concepto, tocando algunas de sus piezas más míticas con un combo de salsa." ← La salvaje historia del TRAP ESPAÑOL (2013-2026) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4hBhiU5jj6s
**Reconciliation with Cecilio G** : Despite a long-standing "war" with their former associate Cecilio G, which played out on Instagram stories and DMs, PXXR GANG and Cecilio G eventually reconciled. "Con el tiempo la purga no y Cecilio G se han reconciliado y me alegro." ← La salvaje historia del TRAP ESPAÑOL (2013-2026) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4hBhiU5jj6s
