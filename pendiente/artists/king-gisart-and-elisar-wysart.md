## albums
**Marderoth de Universe (2017) - Second of Five Albums** : This album, released recently in 2017, is the second installment of a projected five albums the band aimed to release within that year. It has left listeners deeply disconcerted, raising more questions than answers. The album is conceptual, divided into three seemingly independent stories, a format that was popular in the late 1960s but has since declined. Musically, it is described as incredibly complex and well-worked, featuring three distinct styles: epic progressive rock for the first part, epic heavy metal for the central part, and 70s psychedelic rock for the third part, all linked by an interesting musical thread despite the lyrical disconnections. A significant critique is the use of extensive spoken word narration by a man and a woman, which is said to detract from the music. ## songs
**Han Tumía de Márderos de Universe (2017)** : This is the third story on the album, which also lends its name to the album itself. It is entirely unrelated to the previous two narratives. The story features a future robot living in a cyber-digital world who becomes obsessed with human conditions it cannot experience, specifically vomiting and dying. The robot, unable to die more than once, creates a "robot son" with the sole purpose of incessantly vomiting. The son rebels, leading to a robotic symbiosis between the two that mysteriously collapses, magically spreading vomit across the universe before they die. This story is described as surreal and artistic but suffers from a lack of argumentative coherence, leaving many questions about the motivations and events. **Lord of Light y versus Balrog (2017)** : This is the second story on the album "Marderoth de Universe", completely unrelated to the first. It tells of a demigod with lightning powers who accidentally destroys a village he is supposed to protect. The villagers, believing him evil, witness him strike a dead man, who then transforms into a powerful beast named Balrog. A fierce battle ensues between the Lord of Light and Balrog, with the Lord of Light emerging victorious but deciding to leave the village to prevent further harm. The story is likened to epic heavy metal fantasy narratives, but its brevity and lack of coherence are criticized, making it feel more like a simplified clash such as Harry Potter versus Voldemort. **Taylor de Restlet Pist (2017)** : This is the first of three distinct stories featured on the album "Marderoth de Universe". It narrates the tale of a man-beast, where a bestial part of a man gradually possesses his human side, battling societal repression, ultimately leading to the beast consuming the man and the man-beast's demise. The narrative style is compared to the psychological themes explored by Pink Floyd, but the story is perceived as too short and lacking a solid concept, making it difficult to fully understand or enjoy its purpose.
