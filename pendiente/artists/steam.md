## songs
**Na Na Hey Hey Kiss Him Goodbye (1969)** : Credited to the fictional band Steam, this song was originally conceived as a throwaway B-side by its writers, Gary De Carlo, Pauleko, and Dale Frazier. It was released in November 1969, reaching number one by December 6th and holding that position for a couple of weeks. Despite being a breakup song ("He'll never love you the way I love you... go on and kiss him goodbye") with no intended connection to sports, the "Na Na Hey Hey" chant became famously associated with sports, particularly baseball, after Chicago White Sox organist Nancy Faust started playing it in 1977 during games. ## curiosities
**Fictional Band Status** : Steam was a fictional group formed around the recording of "Na Na Hey Hey Kiss Him Goodbye." The song was written by Gary De Carlo, Pauleko, and Dale Frazier, and while legendary session musicians like The Wrecking Crew were thought by some to have been involved, the recording was actually just De Carlo, Leco, and Frazier. **Throwaway B-side Origin** : "Na Na Hey Hey Kiss Him Goodbye" was originally intended as a "throwaway B side" for a Gary De Carlo 45. The idea was that the B-side shouldn't be too good to avoid distracting radio DJs from the A-side. This plan unexpectedly led to the song becoming a major hit for the fictional group Steam, though De Carlo never got a hit under his own name.
