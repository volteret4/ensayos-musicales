## curiosities
**Lull Coxhill's Early Role** : The group featured soprano saxophone player Lull Coxhill, who was described as an "extremely good musician." Coxhill would later become "one of the most respected sax players in Britain" and a significant figure in the 1970s Canterbury scene, highlighting the band's strong musical talent. **Ray Davis's Temporary Project** : Ray Davis joined this group after an audition arranged by Giorgio Gomelsky. While with them, Ray played "Trad along with the blues," and it offered him "more prestigious gigs," temporarily making it seem like his original quartet was "over." Their repertoire included blues standards like "Honky Tonk Train Blues," played in the style of Bob Crosby's Bobcats.
