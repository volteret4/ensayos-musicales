## curiosities
**"Peggy Sue" Title Inspiration** : Jerry Allison believed that a song Buddy Holly intended to name "Cindy Lou" would be better if it was about his own girlfriend, leading to the eventual title "Peggy Sue." **"That'll Be the Day" Contract Recipient (1957)** : To circumvent Buddy Holly's restrictive Decca contract, the Brunswick contract for "That'll Be the Day" was intentionally sent to Jerry Allison, ensuring Holly's name was not overtly linked to the release. "And the contract was sent to Jerry Allison, not Buddy Holly. His name wasn't mentioned at first, in case Thiel decided to mention it to his buses, and the whole thing was blown." ← Episode Sixty-One： ＂That'll Be the Day＂, by the Crickets | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KVJMKLc54O0
**Early Recording Sessions** : Jerry Allison played drums on six songs cut by Sonny Curtis in 1955, backed by Buddy Holly on guitar and Larry Wellborn on bass, marking his early involvement in rock and roll recordings. "Sonny Curtis cut six songs in 1955, backed by Buddy on guitar, Larry Wellborne and bass, and Jerry Allison on drums." ← Episode Sixty-One： ＂That'll Be the Day＂, by the Crickets | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KVJMKLc54O0
**Love for New Orleans R&B** : Jerry Allison, along with Buddy Holly, was a keen admirer of New Orleans R&B, particularly the music of Fat Stomano and artists associated with Dave Bartholomew, and especially liked a song Bartholomew co-wrote for The Spiders. "Buddy and Jerry Allison both really liked New Orleans R&B. They loved Fat Stomano and the other people that Dave Bartholomew worked with, and they particularly liked the song The Bartholomew had co-written for a group called The Spiders." ← Episode Sixty-One： ＂That'll Be the Day＂, by the Crickets | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KVJMKLc54O0
**The Crickets Naming (1957)** : Jerry Allison, along with Buddy Holly, decided on "The Crickets" as the band's name after rejecting "The Beetles" as "too creepy." They were influenced by the name of the R&B group The Spiders. "So they decided that they wanted a name that was something like The Spiders. At first they considered The Beatles, but decided that that was too creepy. People would want to squish them. So they settled on The Crickets." ← Episode Sixty-One： ＂That'll Be the Day＂, by the Crickets | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KVJMKLc54O0
