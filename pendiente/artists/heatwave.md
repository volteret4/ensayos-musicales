## songs
**The Groove Line (1978)** : Released in 1978, this song by Heatwave, a band with roots in Dayton, Ohio, became a significant "skate anthem." Despite not being "pure funk" with its polished horns and disco influences, its beat and lyrics, such as "the wheels are turning. You know we won't stop. We got this boiler burn in. We're keeping it hot," made it seem tailor-made for the roller rink. Rod Temperton, the British keyboardist and writer, was described by Questlove as "the spirit of the band." ## curiosities
**Dayton, Ohio Roots & Disco Boom** : Heatwave, co-founded by brothers Johnny and Keith Wilder from Dayton, Ohio, is recognized as originating from the "birthplace of funk." Keyboardist and writer Rod Temperton, though British, was considered the "spirit of the band" by Questlove and was instrumental in writing major hits for Michael Jackson. Temperton openly stated that Heatwave played a significant role in the European disco boom. **Rod Temperton's Songwriting Success** : Rod Temperton, the British keyboardist and writer for Heatwave, was described by Questlove as "the spirit of the band." Beyond Heatwave, Temperton achieved significant recognition for writing several iconic songs for Michael Jackson, including "Off the Wall," "Rock with You," and "Thriller."
