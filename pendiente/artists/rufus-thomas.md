## songs
**Bearcat (Year Unknown)** : This was the first hit record released on Sun Records, performed by Rufus Thomas. It was notable as an "answer record" to the popular song "Hound Dog." "He'd been the one to record the first hit on son records, Bearcat. The answer record to Hound Dog, we talked about way back in episode 15." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Bearcats** : This track was produced by Sam Phillips for Rufus Thomas as a response to the song "Hound Dog." Its production angered Don Roby. "Don Roby was still annoyed with Sam Phillips over Bearcats, the track that Phillips had produced for Thomas as an answer to Hound Dog." ← Episode 33： ＂Mystery Train＂, by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DSHTmHy3Jo0
**Because I Love You (Year Unknown)** : Rufus Thomas wrote this song and recorded it with his daughter, Carla, for the first session in the newly relocated Stax studio. The track became a local hit and attracted the attention of Jerry Wexler at Atlantic Records. "For the first session in the new studio, they recorded a song Rufus had written, because I love you" ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Tigerman** : Junia Parker and The Blue Flames backed Rufus Thomas on this song. Elvis Presley would later perform "Tigerman" in the 1960s and play it as part of a medley with "Mystery Train" in the 1970s. "Parker and his blue flames also backed Rufus Thomas on Tigerman." ← Episode 33： ＂Mystery Train＂, by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DSHTmHy3Jo0

## curiosities
**Appearance at Memphis Charity Show** : Rufus Thomas was among the performers at a charity Christmas show in Memphis for impoverished black youth, which also featured LC Cook, Ray Charles, BB King, and a brief appearance by Elvis Presley. "The lineup of the show was almost entirely black. People like Ray Charles, BB King, Rufus Thomas and so on." ← Episode Sixty： ＂You Send Me＂ by Sam Cooke | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=92tD5qJR-yY
**First Hit on Sun Records** : Thomas was responsible for recording the first hit on Sun Records, "Bearcat," which was an answer record to "Hound Dog." "He'd been the one to record the first hit on son records, Bearcat. The answer record to Hound Dog, we talked about way back in episode 15." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Godsend for Satellite/Stax** : Thomas, already a hit-making talent, approached Jim Stewart's newly opened studio and suggested recording tracks. This was considered a "godsend" for the struggling label as it guaranteed airplay for his record and others, due to his influence as a DJ. "If you've got a record label and a DJ wants to make a record with you, that's a godsend. Your guaranteed airplay not only for that record, but for a few of your others" ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Memphis Entertainment Figure** : Rufus Thomas was a prominent figure in Memphis's black entertainment scene, serving as a popular DJ, comedian, and MC for nearly every Chitlin circuit show in the area. "Rufus Thomas was the most important man in black entertainment in Memphis. He was a popular DJ and comedian." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
