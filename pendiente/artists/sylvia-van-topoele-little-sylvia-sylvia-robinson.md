## songs
**Drive Daddy Drive** : A "hard R&B number" recorded by Little Sylvia, more in the style of Little Esther, released as the B-side of the "I Went to Your Wedding" single. This showcased a different, less coherent sound in her early career, failing to make her a star. "For every syrupy ballad like I Went to Your Wedding, there was a hard R&B number, more in the Little Esther style, like Drive Daddy Drive." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**I Went to Your Wedding** : An example of a "syrupy ballad" recorded by Little Sylvia in her early career on Savoy or Jubilee Records. This song was part of a mix of styles that lacked coherence and failed to establish her as a star. "A typical example is her version of, I Went to Your Wedding." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Pillow Talk** : A hit song for Sylvia Robinson herself, which she had originally written for Al Green. Green turned it down due to its "blatant sexuality" conflicting with his newfound religion. "Sylvia also had a hit of her own, with a song called Pillow Talk that she'd written for All Green, but which he turned down due to its blatant sexuality, conflicting with his newfound religion." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI

## curiosities
**Acquisition of "Love Is Strange" Share** : According to Bo Diddley, he sold his share in the song "Love Is Strange" to Sylvia sometime in the early 1960s, a move that aligns with her reputation for making "very wise business decisions." "According to Deadly, he also later sold his own share in the song to Sylvia, sometime in the early 60s. This may well be the case, because Sylvia Van Tapool went on to become a very, very successful businessman, who made a lot of very wise business decisions." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Business Acumen and Ambition** : Sylvia possessed a "razor-sharp business mind" and a "huge amount of ambition and a desire for stardom," contrasting sharply with Mickey Baker's more private and studious nature. "Sylvia had a razor-sharp business mind, a huge amount of ambition and a desire for stardom." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Day Job as a Typist** : By the age of 20, Sylvia was working as a typist, struggling to make a living as a singer despite taking guitar lessons from Mickey Baker and becoming "pretty good." "By the time she was 20, Sylvia was holding down a day job as a typist, trying and failing to earn enough money to live on as a singer, but she'd been taking guitar lessons from Mickey Baker and had got pretty good." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Early Career as "Little Sylvia"** : Sylvia began her recording career at the age of 13, using the stage name "Little Sylvia," inspired by Little Esther, who also recorded for Savoy Records. "Sylvia was only 13 when she started her career, using the name Little Sylvia, inspired by Little Esther, who like her was making records for Savoy records." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Founding All Platinum Records** : After the duo with Mickey Baker split, Sylvia took a few years off to have children before establishing her own record label, All Platinum. For this label, she wrote and produced a number of proto-disco hits for other artists. "After a few years off to have kids after the duos split up, she set up her own record label, All Platinum." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Lead Guitar and Backing Vocals for Ike & Tina Turner** : On Ike & Tina Turner's hit "It's Gonna Work Out Fine," Sylvia played the lead guitar part and joined The Ikettes on backing vocals, making the record essentially a Mickey & Sylvia production with Tina Turner as guest vocalist. "Sylvia played the lead guitar part while Mickey did the spoken Ag vocals, which Ag would do live. Sylvia also joined the Ag Kets on backing vocals." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Pivotal Figure in Hip-Hop** : Sylvia Robinson is described as becoming to hip-hop what Sam Phillips was to rock and roll. In 1979, with financing from her husband Joe Robinson's gangster friend Morris Levy, she was more responsible than anyone from the first wave of rock and roll stars for recognizing the commercial potential in hip-hop. "Because Sylvia Robinson, who had been little Sylvia, and the woman calling her lover boy, became to hip-hop, what Sam Phillips was to rock and roll." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Possible Co-writer of "It's Gonna Work Out Fine" (as Sylvia McKinney)** : Sylvia Robinson (then Sylvia Van Topoele) is possibly the same person as Sylvia McKinney, who is credited as a co-writer on "It's Gonna Work Out Fine," a song Mickey & Sylvia suggested for Ike & Tina Turner. "The other credited co-writer is one Sylvia McKinney, who some sources suggest is the same person as Sylvia Van Topool, who had by this point married Joe Robinson and changed her name to Sylvia Robinson." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Relationship with Joe Robinson and Management** : Sylvia started dating Joe Robinson, a man with connections to gangsters, who decided to become her manager to boost her floundering career by forming a duo with Mickey Baker. "But then Sylvia started dating a man named Joe Robinson. Joe Robinson was involved in some way with gangsters." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Unsuccessful Early Recordings** : Her initial recordings on Savoy and Jubilee Records were unsuccessful, characterized by a "strange mix of different styles" that lacked a coherent or distinctive sound necessary to establish her as a star. "She had recorded several unsuccessful tracks on Savoy and Jubilee records." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
