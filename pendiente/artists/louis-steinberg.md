# artist - Louis Steinberg

## member of
- The Triumphs

## instruments
- Bass

## curiosities
**Departure from The M.G.'s** : Louis Steinberg was replaced by Duck Dunn in Booker T and the M.G.'s after the band had achieved several hits. Steinberg publicly claimed his removal was racially motivated due to him being black and Steve Cropper wanting another white man in the band. "Steinberg always claimed that the main reason he was dropped from the MG's was because he was black, and Steve Krupper wanted another white man." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Originator of "Green Onions" Title** : Louis Steinberg is credited with suggesting the title "Green Onions" for the instrumental track by Booker T and the M.G.'s, thinking that onions were "the funkiest thing he could think of." "Louis Steinberg thought that onions were the funkiest thing he could think of, and so the track became green onions." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Recalled for Otis Redding Session** : When Otis Redding was unexpectedly given a chance to record, Steve Cropper immediately ran out to call Louis Steinberg, who was already packing away his bass, back into the studio to play on the impromptu session. "Cropper ran out to get Louis Steinberg, who was packing his bass away, and talent to bring it back into the studio." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Session Player for Early Stax Hits** : Louis Steinberg was a crucial bass player on many early Stax recordings, including Rufus and Carla Thomas's "Because I Love You," The Mar-Keys' "Last Night," The Triumphs' "Burnt Biscuit," and William Bell's "You Don't Miss Your Water."
