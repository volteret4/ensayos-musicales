## songs
**Baby I Love You (1969)** : This song by Andy Kim was on the Billboard Hot 100 in late August 1969, reaching at least number 14, just one spot behind his co-written hit "Sugar Sugar" by The Archies. "And one spot behind Andy Kim's baby I love you. **Rock Me Gently (1974)** : Andy Kim considered this to be the best song of his life. Despite his strong belief in the song, it took a year and a half to get radio play, requiring him to self-finance its release and create his own record label, Ice Records, as he only had enough money for one song (Side B of the 45 was an instrumental of the track). His perseverance paid off when it became a number one hit. Released in June 1974, it remained on the Hot 100 throughout the summer before finally reaching number one. Kim intentionally avoided a guitar or drum solo, opting instead for a "Stevie Wonderish" keyboard solo near the end of the song, performed by his keyboard player. "His perseverance paid off because he not only had recorded the best song of his life he had a number one hit." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 30 - The Sweet Sound of 70s Bubblegum Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/030-bubblegum
**Sugar Sugar (1969)** : Andy Kim co-wrote this song with Jeff Barry, which became a number one hit for The Archies after The Monkees rejected it. ## curiosities
**Appreciation for Pop Songwriting** : Andy Kim deeply loved and appreciated his pop songs, including "Sugar Sugar" and "Rock Me Gently," embracing his role as a pop songwriter rather than apologizing for the "bubblegum" label. He quoted Mark Twain, saying, "the two best days of your life are the day you were born and the day you find out why," in reference to finding his purpose. "It's cool to see him love his pop songs instead of apologizing for them." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 30 - The Sweet Sound of 70s Bubblegum Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/030-bubblegum
