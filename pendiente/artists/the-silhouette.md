# artist - The Silhouette

## members
- Black members
- Couple of singers
- Drummer
- Five saxophones
- Italian members
- Japanese American saxophone player
- Mostly Mexican Americans
- Pianist
- Richie Valens (guitar, vocals)
- Three trumpets
- Vibraphone player

## curiosities
**Band Name Origin** : The band chose their name, The Silhouette, in homage to "The Silhouettes," a popular hit song by The Rays that had recently been released. **Initial Recording by Doug Machia** : In January 1958, Doug Machia recorded The Silhouette during a gig using a portable tape recorder. This recording, which is now lost, was later presented to Bob Keane. Although Keane was not impressed with the band as a whole, Valens' performance on the tape caught his attention enough to invite him for an audition. "Machia recorded the silhouettes at the gig, on a portable tape recorder, and took the recording, which is now lost, to Keane." ← Episode 73： ＂La Bamba＂ by Ritchie Valens | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1v9iSCWnLsc
**Local Following and Valens Family Fundraiser** : The Silhouette garnered a significant local following, leading them to organize and promote their own show in January 1958. This specific event was held in a rented hall with the explicit purpose of raising funds to cover Richie Valens' family's mortgage payment for that month. "The silhouettes built up quite a local following. And in January 1958, they played a show that they promoted themselves." ← Episode 73： ＂La Bamba＂ by Ritchie Valens | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1v9iSCWnLsc
**Pioneering Mixed-Race Lineup** : The Silhouette was an unusually large band for its era, featuring a diverse instrumentation including three trumpets, five saxophones, a vibraphone, piano, drums, and multiple singers, alongside Valens on guitar and vocals. Crucially, the group was groundbreaking for its mixed-race composition, including a majority of Mexican Americans, as well as Black, Italian (at a time when Italians were not universally considered fully white by prevailing racial standards), and a Japanese American saxophone player. "They were very unusual for the time in being a mixed race group." ← Episode 73： ＂La Bamba＂ by Ritchie Valens | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1v9iSCWnLsc
**R&B and Mexican Repertoire** : The band's performance repertoire primarily consisted of R&B songs. However, they would occasionally perform Mexican material, typically when specifically requested for particular events such as weddings. Valens generally did not sing on these Mexican songs due to his limited Spanish proficiency, though he was eventually persuaded to sing "La Bamba." "Their repertoire was apparently largely based around R&B songs, but they would occasionally play Mexican material, usually when requested for a particular event, such as a wedding." ← Episode 73： ＂La Bamba＂ by Ritchie Valens | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1v9iSCWnLsc
**Unrecorded and Disputed History** : The Silhouette never made any professional recordings. After Richie Valens achieved fame, various members of the band were interviewed, but their accounts often diverged significantly regarding who initially invited Valens into the group, who held leadership within the band, who taught Valens his performance skills, which songs they performed, and who contributed to the material that later brought Valens renown.
