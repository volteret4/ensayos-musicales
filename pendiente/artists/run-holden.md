**Second Generation Musician** : Run Holden was a second-generation musician, whose father, Oscar Holden, was known as the "father of Seattle Jazz" and had performed with legendary figures like Louis Armstrong and Jelly Roll Morton. "In fact, he was a second generation musician. His father, Oscar Holdin, was known as the father of Seattle Jazz, and had played with both Louis Armstrong and Jelly Roll Morton." ← Episode 106：＂Louie Louie＂ by the Kingsmen | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S3agGJsxjpE
**Simplified "Louie Louie" Arrangement** : Run Holden's saxophone player, Carlos Ward, created a crucial, guitar-friendly arrangement for "Louie Louie," simplifying Richard Berry's complex second inversion major 7th chord to a simpler minor 5 chord. This arrangement became the standard for nearly all subsequent covers in the Pacific Northwest, as bands learned it from Holden's live performances rather than Berry's original. "Holdin, or rather his saxophone player Carlos Ward, who did the Thunderbirds arrangements, had made a crucial change to Louis Louis, one that made it simpler to play on the guitar, and thus suitable for the guitar heavy music that was starting to predominate in the Pacific Northwest." ← Episode 106：＂Louie Louie＂ by the Kingsmen | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S3agGJsxjpE
