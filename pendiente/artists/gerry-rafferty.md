## songs
**Baker Street (1978)** : Released in 1978, this song features one of the most iconic saxophone riffs, played by Rafael Ravenscroft, although the prevailing view is that Rafferty himself composed the riff after experimenting with guitar and piano. Rafferty chose the saxophone as it evoked the "jazz period of New York City," specifically referencing 1920s jazz. The lyrics were written during a period of personal turmoil, reflecting his struggles with alcoholism, bipolar disorder, depression, and legal issues with his Stealers Wheel contract (1975-1978), portraying a desire for escape and a sober life, but acknowledging a persistent restlessness. The song reached number 2 on the Billboard Hot 100, where it remained for six weeks due to alleged label interference. ## curiosities
**Baker Street's Chart Performance Controversy** : "Baker Street" reportedly remained at number 2 on the Billboard Hot 100 for six consecutive weeks, unable to dislodge Andy Gibb's "Shadow Dancing" from the top spot. A legend suggests that RCA, Andy Gibb's record label, allegedly threatened to pull Gibb from a Billboard show if "Shadow Dancing" was not kept at number 1, leading to "Baker Street" becoming one of the most famous all-time number 2 hits.
