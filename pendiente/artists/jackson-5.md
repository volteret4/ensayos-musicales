# artist - Jackson 5

## albums
**ABC (1970)** : This was the second studio album by the Jackson Five, released in 1970, and sharing its name with one of their hit singles. The song "ABC" from this album famously knocked The Beatles' "Let It Be" out of the number one spot on the Billboard Hot 100. "ABC is from the album of the same name. ## songs
**ABC (1970)** : First heard on television on American Bandstand, this song was released as a single in 1970 and quickly became a number one hit. It gained notoriety for displacing The Beatles' "Let It Be" from the top position on the Billboard Hot 100 chart. "ABC would zoom past, let it be by the Beatles and knock it out of the number one spot on the Billboard Hot 100 in 1970." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 47 - Dick Clark's Entertainment Empire | https://www.ftr70.com/
**I Want You Back (1979)** : A live cover of this song was issued as a single by Graham Parker and The Rumor, and it briefly appeared on the pop chart. "a live cover of the Jackson Fives I Want You Back." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Angry Young Men Edition Part 1 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f780c30a1408dc2baf3
**I Want You Back (On American Bandstand)** : The Jackson 5 performed this song during their appearance on American Bandstand in February 1970. Following the performance, Dick Clark interviewed Michael Jackson. "they had just sung and danced to I want you back." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 47 - Dick Clark's Entertainment Empire | https://www.ftr70.com/
**I'll Be There (1970)** : This hit song by the Jackson 5 prevented The Carpenters' version of "We've Only Just Begun" from reaching the number one spot on the Hot 100 in 1970. "We've Only Just Begun" was consequently held at number 2, getting "stuck behind" "I'll Be There." ## curiosities
**Admiration for The Beatles (1970)** : During their American Bandstand interview, young Michael Jackson and other members of his family expressed their admiration for The Beatles. This sentiment is particularly notable given that the Jackson Five's song "ABC" would later unseat The Beatles' "Let It Be" from the number one spot on the Billboard Hot 100 in 1970. "Seven-year-old Michael Jackson and various members of his family, many of whom said that they admired the Beatles." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 47 - Dick Clark's Entertainment Empire | https://www.ftr70.com/
**First Television Appearances (1969-1970)** : The Jackson 5 made their initial television appearance on the Miss Black America pageant in September 1969. This was followed by a performance on the legendary Ed Sullivan Show in December 1969, reaching a much larger audience, and then their American Bandstand debut in February 1970. "Their first was on the Miss Black America pageant in September 1969 and then they played to a much larger audience on the legendary Ed Sullivan show in December of 69." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 47 - Dick Clark's Entertainment Empire | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Living with Barry Gordy and Diana Ross** : After moving from their hometown of Gary, Indiana, the Jackson family resided in Beverly Hills with Motown president Barry Gordy and Diana Ross, whom Gordy described as the love of his life. This was mentioned during Michael Jackson's interview on American Bandstand. "This is where the Jackson's were living with Motown president Barry Gordy and the woman he described as the love of his life Diana Ross." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 47 - Dick Clark's Entertainment Empire | https://www.ftr70.com/
