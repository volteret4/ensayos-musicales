## songs

**Heartbreak Hotel (1956)** : As a songwriter, she was crucial in finishing "Heartbreak Hotel," conceiving the phrase "heartbreak hotel" itself after Tommy Durden presented initial lyrics. She played the finished song to Elvis, insisting it would be his first million-seller, and later claimed she gave him a third of the songwriting credit to help him buy a house for his parents in Florida. "She thought a lot about the phrase, and eventually came to the conclusion that what you'd find at the end of a lonely street was a heartbreak hotel." ← Episode 38： ＂Heartbreak Hotel＂ by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ES35PYeAwfs

## curiosities
**Collaboration with Colonel Tom Parker** : Mae Axton frequently worked with Colonel Tom Parker to promote shows when they came through Florida, where she resided. **Inspired by Elvis's Family Devotion** : After interviewing Elvis in Florida, Mae Axton was deeply struck by his earnest desire to earn enough money to bring his parents to live there, inspiring her to promise to write him a song. "She said later, that just went through my heart, because I looked down there, and there were all these other kids, different show members for that night, all the guys looking for cute little girls. But his priority was doing something for his mother and daddy." ← Episode 38： ＂Heartbreak Hotel＂ by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ES35PYeAwfs
**Publicist for Hank Snow** : For a period, Mae Axton served as the personal publicist for country singer Hank Snow. "For a time, she was Hank Snow's personal publicist." ← Episode 38： ＂Heartbreak Hotel＂ by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ES35PYeAwfs
**Unexpected Songwriting Debut** : An English teacher and freelance journalist, Mae Axton initially knew nothing about "Hilboly music." Her songwriting career began unexpectedly when Mini Pearl falsely introduced her as a songwriter to Fred Rose of Acuff-Rose Publishing, leading to her being asked to write a novelty song for Dub Dickerson.
