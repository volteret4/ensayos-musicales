## curiosities
**Cotton Club Success and Regular Performer** : The Cotton Club contributed to Cab Calloway's success in the 1920s and early 1930s. He was still playing there regularly when Rosetta Tharp started performing in 1938. "the Cotton Club had been responsible for the success of both Duke Ellington and Cap Calloway, though only Calloway was still playing there regularly by the time Rosetta Tharp started performing there." ← Episode 5： ＂This Train＂ by Sister Rosetta Tharpe | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=II-hDnwUgT8
**Jive Singing Style** : Cab Calloway was recognized for his jive singing style, which influenced Wynonie Harris's ability to perform in a subtler vocal manner. "close to the give singing of a cap-caller way or Louis Jordan." ← Episode 7： ＂Good Rockin' Tonight＂ by Wynonie Harris | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IgqSPoYRUCM
**Pioneer of Jive Singing** : Cab Calloway is identified as a key figure in the tradition of jive singing, which dates back at least to the 1920s. "there was a whole tradition of jive singing, which dates back at least to the 1920s and cab Callaway." ← Episode 4： ＂Choo Choo Ch'Boogie＂ by Louis Jordan | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DFZnwvsXGJM
**Rival in Hip-ness** : Cab Calloway was one of the few contemporary artists who could match Louis Jordan's level of "hip-ness" and performance charisma. "He was someone who could absolutely sell a song, with wit and humour, and a general sense of hip-ness that could possibly be matched at that time, only by Cap Calaway and Slim Gaylord." ← Episode 4： ＂Choo Choo Ch'Boogie＂ by Louis Jordan | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DFZnwvsXGJM
