**"Corruption" and Fascination with Rock** : Schmidt described his New York trip as having "corrupted" him, awakening an immense fascination for the possibilities of rock music. Prior to this, avant-garde music in his academic circle was synonymous with contemporary classical music, but after this experience, he perceived artists like James Brown, The Velvet Underground, and Pink Floyd as embodying a less elitist and more inclusive form of modernity. "Según sus palabras, ese viaje le corrompió, despertando en el una enorme fascinación por las posibilidades del rock. En su círculo, la música de Vanguardia era la música clásica contemporánea. **Explanation of Kraut Rock's Distinct Sound** : As Can's keyboardist, Irmin Schmidt offered a unique explanation for the distinct sound of Kraut Rock, attributing it to the different educational environment Germans grew up in. He suggested that sincere expression from this background inevitably leads to a different "feeling" in their music, devoid of nationalist sentiment. He contrasted this with the melodic focus of American and British music, theorizing that music from lands surrounded by water is more influenced by water and air. "El 10.000 ismiz que se encargará de los tecladores de la banda, explicó el Kraut Rock, en la predilección de los alemanes por ese sonido de esta bonita forma. Los alemanes nos hemos educado en un ambiente completamente diferente, de modo que si nos expresamos de forma sincera, es inevitable que tengamos un feeling diferente, pero sin tener ningún tipo de sentimiento nacionalista. La música americana y británica está más relacionada con las melodías. Si estudias la música de todo el mundo, es que en las tierras rodean de agua, la música está influenciada más por el agua y el aire." ← CAN ｜ La Banda que (posiblemente) Inspiró a tu banda preferida | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4qHiw2wHWAI

**Transformative New York Trip (1966)** : In 1966, Schmidt traveled to New York, where he immersed himself in the experimental music scene, meeting and spending time with prominent figures such as Steve Reich, La Monte Young, Terry Riley, and even interacting with John Cage, feeling a deep affinity for their work. During his stay at the mythical and bohemian Chelsea Hotel, he also encountered Andy Warhol and his circle, including The Velvet Underground. "En 1966, un antiguo alumno alemán de Stochhaus en llamado Irmell Smith hace un viaje en Nueva York. Allí conoce y pasa tiempo con Steve Reitch, la Montellon, Terry Ridley e incluso llegó a tratar con John Cage. Sentía una gran afinidad hacia esos músicos.
