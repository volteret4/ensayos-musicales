## songs
**Strange Fruit (1939)** : Holiday's most famous performance, "Strange Fruit," a highly political and inflammatory song about lynching, was rejected by her label, Columbia, and her producer, John Hammond, due to its controversial nature. Due to Columbia's refusal, Billy Holiday turned to Milt Gabler, a politically left-leaning friend and founder of Commodore, who negotiated a special deal with Columbia to produce and release the song on his Commodore label. It sold over a million copies, became arguably the most important recording in music history, and was named "Song of the Century" by Time magazine in December 1999. ## curiosities
**"Strange Fruit" and Political Activism** : "Strange Fruit," a song about lynching, became Billy Holiday's most famous and politically charged performance. Its controversial nature led to its rejection by her main label, Columbia, but its eventual release by Milt Gabler on Commodore Records made it a historic success and a symbol of protest, evidenced by Rebecca Ferguson's attempt to perform it at Donald Trump's inauguration.
