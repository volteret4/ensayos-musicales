# artist - C. W. McCall

## members
- Bill Freeze (voice of character)
- Chip Davis (music)

## labels
- American Gramophone
- MGM Records

## songs
**Convoy (1975)** : Co-written with Chip Davis, this single was released in November 1975. It became a time capsule of trucker life in the mid-70s, featuring CB slang and telling a story of protest against the 55 mph speed limit, toll roads, and the difficulties faced by the trucking industry. The narrator, Rubberduck, leads a growing group of vehicles. The song hit number one on the Billboard Hot 100 in January 1976 and held the number one spot on the country chart for six weeks. Rolling Stone ranked it number 98 on its list of the top 100 country songs of all time, and it inspired a 1978 movie. **Old Home Filler Up and Keep a Truck in the Cafe (1974)** : This debut single for the C. W. McCall character was a full-length version of the original Old Home Bread jingle, released on Chip Davis's American Gramophone label. It sold 30,000 copies in just a few weeks and achieved a notable peak of number 54 on the Hot 100, which was impressive for a Midwestern regional hit. ## curiosities
**Advertising Commercial for Old Home Bread (1973)** : Bill Freese, using the persona C. W. McCall, created a TV commercial for Old Home Bread. It featured McCall as a truck driver and a waitress named Mavis at the Old Home and Fillarepp Cafe. The commercial won a Clio, the highest award for an advertisement, and was noted for Mavis's seductive portrayal, which would likely not be made today. "That commercial won a Clio, the highest possible award for an advertising commercial or advertisement, and would never be made today as Mavis pretty much seduces C. **Chip Davis's Dislike for Country Music** : Despite "Convoy" becoming a six-week number one hit on Billboard's Hot Country Singles chart, its co-creator Chip Davis paradoxically declared that he didn't even like country music, adding an ironic twist to the song's massive success in the genre. **Fictional Artist Creation (1973)** : C. W. McCall was not a real artist but a fictional recording persona invented by an ad campaign for Old Home Bread in 1973. Bill Freeze was the creative director who conceived and voiced the trucker character, with Chip Davis providing the music, making him a unique commercial-to-music success story. "an artist who wasn't even real. **Film Adaptation (1978)** : The immense popularity of the song "Convoy" led to a movie adaptation two years later, starring Chris Christofferson and Ali McGraw and directed by Sam Peckinpah. This film further solidified the song's cultural impact, grossing nearly $50 million at the box office.
