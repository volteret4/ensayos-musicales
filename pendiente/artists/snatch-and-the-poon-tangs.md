## albums
**For Adults Only (Late 1960s)** : This album was recorded by a trio comprising Johnny Otis, Delmar "Mighty Mouth" Evans, and Sugar (Otis's son), with the cover art designed by Otis in the style of Robert Crumb. Despite its obscene humor and "constant sexual insults," the album had a serious sociological purpose, as Otis intended it to preserve elements of black folklore, including songs like "The Signifying Monkey." ## curiosities
**Lack of Censorship Concern** : Johnny Otis remarked with humor that while his earlier works like "All With Me Henry" and "Willie and the Hand Jive" faced obscenity accusations, the explicit "For Adults Only" album by Snatch and the Poon Tangs, with its "constant sexual insults," surprisingly drew no public concern or bother regarding obscenity. "Otis would in later years laugh that he'd received accusations of obscenity, for all with me Henry and for William the Han Jive, but nobody had seen bothered in the slightest by the records of Snatch and the Poon Tangs with their constant sexual insults." ← REUPLOAD Episode 71： ＂Willie and the Hand Jive＂ by Johnny Otis | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ez6BCUlI6Rg
**Preservation of Black Folklore** : The album "For Adults Only" by Snatch and the Poon Tangs, despite its explicit content, served Johnny Otis's goal of preserving black folklore. Songs like "The Signifying Monkey," which had been performed for years, were included with their original sexual insults intact, unlike censored versions by other artists like Chuck Berry and Willie Dixon. "Otis wanted to preserve bits of black folklore.
