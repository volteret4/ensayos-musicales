# artist - Sylvester

## members
- Tipwerex (composer of initial chords)

## songs
**You Make Me Feel (Mighty Real) (1978)** : This song reached number one on various dance club music charts in 1978. It originated from chords by Tipwerex, with Sylvester adding words and melody spontaneously. Though he initially thought the song wasn't "that hot" due to few words, he found them to perfectly describe the feeling of dancing, sweating, cruising, and carrying on. The song has since been featured in numerous movies, video games, and documentaries. In 2019, the Library of Congress selected it for preservation in the National Recording Registry for its joyous embodiment of pride. ## curiosities
**AIDS Legacy and Philanthropy (1988)** : Sylvester died of AIDS in 1988, leaving the royalties from all his work to HIV and AIDS charities in San Francisco, his adopted hometown. **Initial Dislike of Producer Emphasis** : Sylvester was not a fan of disco, primarily disliking its emphasis on producers over artists. However, with the "Saturday Night Fever" phenomenon making disco an $8 billion industry, he decided to "cash in." "Sylvester was also not a big fan of disco because he didn't like the emphasis on the producer rather than the artist." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 1 - Disco Doesn't Suck (Encore) | https://www.ftr70.com/blog/tag/70s+podcast
**Unambiguous Gay Pride Icon** : Sylvester occupied an unambiguous position on the disco spectrum, proudly and openly gay in the 1970s without costume or parody. Despite growing up with a Pentecostal Church mother who did not accept gayness, he left home at 15 to live with his grandmother due to his mother's non-acceptance, refusing to change who he was. "there is nothing ambiguous about Sylvester. He was not in costume. He was not a parody of a gay man.
