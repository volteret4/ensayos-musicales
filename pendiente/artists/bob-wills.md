**Creative Tension with Milton Brown** : In the original lineup of the Light Crust Doughboys, Wills experienced a creative tension with singer Milton Brown. Brown sought to experiment more with jazz styles, while Wills preferred a traditional hill-billy string band sound, a dynamic that ultimately led to the creation of a new form of music. **Early Blackface Performances** : Bob Wills initially performed as a blackface comedian, a practice that was very common in the US in the 1930s and even in the UK for decades longer. **Mutual Respect with Les Paul and Charlie Christian** : Bob Wills was admired by and admired musicians across racial boundaries. In 1938, jazz guitarist Les Paul traveled from Wisconsin to Tulsa, Oklahoma, specifically to hear Wills' band play, eventually jamming on stage and introducing Charlie Christian to Paul during an interval, in what was likely a rare integrated audience. **Western Swing Pioneer** : Bob Wills was one of the two biggest names in western swing, originating from Texas and later moving to Oklahoma. His style of western swing was influential, and a "paired down version" was performed by Hank Thompson.
