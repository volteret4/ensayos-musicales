# artist - The Carza family

## genres
- Country Music

## songs
**Warwickman Blues (1930)** : This old folk song was recorded by The Carza family in 1930. Its lyrics, including "the train arrived 16 coaches long," became part of the "floating lyrics" commonly used by blues singers, later serving as a basis for Junior Parker's "Mystery Train." ## curiosities
**AP Carza's Songwriting and Repurposing** : AP Carza, the patriarch of the family, was an important songwriter of his generation. He also had a practice of finding old folk songs, repurposing their lyrics, and then crediting himself as the writer. "AP Carza, the family patriarch, was one of the most important songwriters of his generation. But he would also go out and find old folk songs, that he would repurpose, and credit himself with having written." ← Episode 33： ＂Mystery Train＂, by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DSHTmHy3Jo0
**Founding Figures of Country Music** : The Carza family, alongside Jimmy Rogers, were pivotal in defining what country music is.
