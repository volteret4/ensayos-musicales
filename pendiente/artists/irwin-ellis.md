## curiosities
**Music Business Mentor** : Ellis, despite his own limited success, was instrumental in educating Jim Stewart about the economics of the music industry. He explained that the real money came from owning publishing rights rather than just selling records, influencing Stewart's business model for Satellite/Stax. "He told Stuart that you didn't make money from the records themselves. Small labels didn't sell much, but that he was making some good money from their songs." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Tiny Record Label Owner** : Irwin Ellis, Jim Stewart's barber, was also a country fiddle player and owned a minuscule record label called Irwin Records. The label was so small that its first release, by Ellis himself, is believed to be entirely lost. "Irwin Records was a tiny label.
