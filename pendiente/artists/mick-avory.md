## curiosities
**Brief Member of The Rolling Stones** : Mick Avory was a member of The Rolling Stones for "a very brief period." He chose to quit before Charlie Watts joined, as he "didn't want to be a full-time drummer" at that time. Upon the Stones' subsequent success, he "realised his mistake" and sought another band. **Joining The Kinks** : Mick Avory was chosen as the permanent drummer for The Kinks by Ray Davis and the management team, after Dave Davis's preferred choice, Viv Prince of The Pretty Things, was unavailable. Despite Dave Davis taking an "instant dislike to him," Avory was accepted as "the best drummer available." **Not on Early Studio Recordings** : Despite joining The Kinks as their permanent drummer, Mick Avory "wouldn't play on the next few records." Producer Shell Tauhmey preferred to use musicians he already knew, and Avory was considered "a bit of an unknown quantity" in the studio.
