# artist - Lindsey Buckingham

## member of
- Fleetwood Mac

## instruments
- Guitar (criticized as "too muted" on *Rumours* by Robert Hilburn)

## songs
**Go Your Own Way (1977)** : He wrote this song, which was the first single from *Rumours*, reaching the top 20 and securing the #120 spot on *Rolling Stone*'s "500 greatest songs of all time." The lyrics explicitly addressed his turbulent relationship with Stevie Nicks during the band's internal conflicts. ## curiosities
**Condition for Joining Fleetwood Mac (1975)** : Upon being invited to fill the void left by Bob Welch in 1975, Lindsey Buckingham insisted that his girlfriend, Stevie Nicks, also be accepted into Fleetwood Mac as a condition of his own membership. "Lindsey Buckingham insisted that his girlfriend Stevie Nicks be accepted into what remained of Fleetwood Mac at that time when Lindsey Buckingham was invited to fill the void." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 51 - The Softer Side of 70s Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-51-the-softer-side-of-70s-rock
**Criticism of Guitar Work on *Rumours*** : Music critic Robert Hilburn criticized Buckingham's guitar work on the *Rumours* album as being "too muted," suggesting it contributed to the album's studio work being "too undemanding" compared to the band's more dynamic live performances. "Lindsey Buckingham's guitar work is too muted." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 51 - The Softer Side of 70s Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-51-the-softer-side-of-70s-rock
**Involvement in Internal Love Triangle** : He was a central figure in a "messy love triangle" with Stevie Nicks and Mick Fleetwood, which, alongside other internal dramas and drug use, provided significant material for the band's songwriting, particularly on *Rumours*.
