# artist - Genio y Yamashiro Bumi

## curiosities
**Akira Soundtrack - Diegetic Music and Unique Sound** : The music in *Akira*, composed by the collective Genio y Yamashiro Bumi, initially appears as diegetic when Kaneda places a CD into a "yuki box." This music, distinct from typical science fiction scores, is characterized by colorful and textural percussions, mysterious sounds, organic and tribal elements, layers of synthesizers, electronic percussions, and Japanese ritualistic voices. It created a sense of the unknown that fused perfectly with the film's urban, overpopulated setting and narrative. "La música que surge desde el yuki box, originalmente parte de la película, como música diágetica, se convierte en un componente sencial de la banda sonora." ← Las Bandas Sonoras Más Atípicas y Revolucionarias del Cine | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fiSrdUP3YXY
**Musical Influences - Traditional Balinese Music (Gamelan)** : The unique percussive and mysterious sounds featured in *Akira*'s soundtrack were later discovered to be derived from, or heavily influenced by, traditional Balinese music, specifically Gamelan. Genio y Yamashiro Bumi, the collective behind the score for Katsuhiro Tomo's masterpiece, sourced some of their distinctive sounds from this tradition.
