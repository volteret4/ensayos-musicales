## albums
**Gilded Palace of Sin (1969)** : This debut album, though not a commercial success and peaking at #164 on the Billboard Top 200, became a historically significant country rock record. It heavily influenced later musicians like Glenn Frey of the Eagles and is considered a foundational album for artists such as Poco, The Eagles, The Rolling Stones, and Tom Petty & The Heartbreakers. "The first album from the burritos, Gilded Palace of Sin, was not a commercial success. ## curiosities
**Festival Express Participants** : The Flying Burrito Brothers were among the notable musicians and bands participating in the Festival Express traveling rock festival in the summer of 1970. "How about Janice Joplin, the Great Bull Zed, the band, traffic, buddy guy, Delaney and Bonnie, the flying burrito brothers." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 59 - Bands and Booze on the Festival Express of 1970 | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Hay Publicity Stunt** : For the release party of "Gilded Palace of Sin," attendees received packages of hay as a publicity stunt intended to signal the album's country influences. The United States Postal Service even tested the hay to determine if it might be marijuana. This creative marketing generated significant media buzz and established the band's image as "psychedelic cow punks" who were "drug-addled." "If you got an invitation to the release party for the Gilded Palace of Sin, you also got a package of hay." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 35 - Cosmic American Music - 70s Country Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-35-cosmic-american-music-70s-country-rock
**Musical Influence on The Eagles** : Glenn Frey acknowledged that The Eagles borrowed "a bit from the Burrito Brothers," specifically their "toughness," although he noted they "weren't that good a band vocally."
