**We Are Family (1979)** : This song, by four sisters from Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, became the official clubhouse song of the 1979 Pittsburgh Pirates. It was first played during a rain delay at Pittsburgh's Three Rivers Stadium in early June 1979. Pirates legend Willie Stargell embraced it, influencing the team to adopt "The Family" as their identity, which was printed on their dugout and became synonymous with their World Series-winning run. The team even replaced "Take Me Out to the Ball Game" with it during the seventh-inning stretch. Sister Sledge learned of its popularity with the Pirates while on tour in Hamburg, Germany, hearing 50,000 people yelling the words. "We are family by sister sledge played over the stadium PA. And as the song came to an end, pirates legend Willie Stargel called up the press box. **Kathy Sledge's Zaire 74 Experience (1974)** : Kathy Sledge, then 14 or 15 years old, described Zaire 74 as "one of the most exciting points in my life" and "euphoric." **Pittsburgh Pirates Anthem** : "We Are Family" became the unexpected anthem for the 1979 Pittsburgh Pirates. The team, starting in fourth place in the National League East in early June, adopted the song after it was played during a rain delay. Led by Willie Stargell, the team embraced the "family" theme, which was printed on their dugout and became a central part of their identity, propelling them to win the World Series four months later, defeating the Baltimore Orioles. The song was played over the PA, on t-shirts, signs, and even replaced "Take Me Out to the Ball Game" during the seventh-inning stretch. "Willie Stargel said on the night that they won the World Series.
