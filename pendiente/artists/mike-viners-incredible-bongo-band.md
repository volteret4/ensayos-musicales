# artist - Mike Viner's Incredible Bongo Band

## albums
**Bongo Rock (1973)** : This album, featuring the cover of "Apache," became a seminal piece in early hip hop. "Apache" was a 1973 cover of a 1960 instrumental by British composer Jerry Lorton, and it quickly became known for its extended, minute-long percussion break. Music writer Michael Angelo Matos described the band's rendition as "the most crazed piece of orchestral funk ever recorded," maintaining the original's "noble and dramatic" shape while taking the liberty of extending the break with dueling trap drummer and congaist. This particular track is heavily sampled and considered the "national anthem of hip-hop." ## songs
**Apache (1973)** : This 1973 cover of Jerry Lorton's 1960 instrumental became famously known as "The Incredible Bongo Rock" and is often dubbed the "national anthem of hip-hop." The track is characterized by its "crazed piece of orchestral funk" and, most significantly, a minute-long percussion break featuring a trap drummer and congaist dueling. DJ Kool Herk successfully used this break to popularize his "merry go round" technique, leading to its widespread use in early hip-hop. Music writer Michael Angelo Matos highlighted its enduring influence, noting it would be remade and sampled countless times, especially when sampling was less legally regulated.
