# artist - Jesse Stone

## songs
**Money Honey (1953)** : Jesse Stone wrote "Money Honey," a song whose melody is noted as being nearly identical to Gene Vincent's "Be-Bop-A-Lula." Despite this strong similarity, the prevailing culture in R&B at the time was "relaxed enough about borrowings," suggesting Stone likely did not pursue any legal action. "Luckily for Vincent at this time the culture in R&B was relaxed enough about bowings that Jesse Stone seems not to have even considered suing." ← Episode 41： ＂Be-Bop-A-Lula＂ by Gene Vincent and the Bluecaps | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tYBQ5F7O3G0
**Shake, Rattle and Roll (Year not specified)** : Jesse Stone wrote this song, which became Big Joe Turner's most well-known contribution to popular music. He used the pseudonym Charles Calhoun for this BMI song because he was a member of ASCAP under his real name, Jesse Stone, due to the rivalry between the two organizations during that era. "That song was written by the songwriter Jesse Stone, though he was using the name Charles Calhoun, because he was a member of ASCAP under his real name, and this was a BMI song if ever there was one." ← Episode 2： ＂Roll Em Pete＂ by Big Joe Turner | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=thpMb1A_fAs
