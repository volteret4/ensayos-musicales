## curiosities
**Advice to Linda Martell on Heckling** : Shelby Singleton advised Linda Martell not to address the audience in any way when facing hecklers during her performances. Martell admitted this advice was correct, despite the emotional difficulty of remaining silent when subjected to racial slurs. **Handling of Linda Martell's Career (1970)** : Shelby Singleton played a significant role in the trajectory of Linda Martell's career. He reportedly made a lawsuit against her "go away," but then shifted focus to promoting white artist Jeannie C. Riley. Singleton released Martell from her contract but effectively blacklisted her by threatening to sue any subsequent label she might sign with. "Singleton let Martell out of her contract, but then threatened to sue the next label she signed with." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 53 - More than Charley Pride - African Americans in 70s Country | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Plantation Records Name Controversy** : Shelby Singleton was the owner of Plantation Records, a label whose name was controversial due to its racial connotations. Linda Martell directly confronted Singleton about the name, asserting that it implied black people belonged on a plantation. Singleton attempted to dismiss the name as having no racial meaning. "when Singleton tried to brush off the name of his record label, Plantation Records, as having nothing to do with race, she said, oh yes it does. You're saying Black people belong on a plantation." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 53 - More than Charley Pride - African Americans in 70s Country | https://www.ftr70.com/
