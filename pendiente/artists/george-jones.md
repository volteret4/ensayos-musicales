## songs
**White Lightning (1959)** : This song was George Jones's first hit on the country charts. Written by J.P. Richardson (The Big Bopper), it was described as "kind of country" or resembling "early rock and roll," with sounds reminiscent of Jerry Lee Lewis and Little Richard, and a "fair distance" from his later, more traditional country songs like "He Stopped Loving Her Today" or "The Grand Tour." ## curiosities
**Academy of Country Entertainers Co-Founder** : Following Charlie Rich's antics at the 1975 CMA Awards, George Jones and Tammy Wynette hosted a gathering of country musicians at their home to discuss how to address singers perceived as "not country" winning country music awards, which led to the formation of the Academy of Country Entertainers. "George Jones and his wife Tammy Wynette, Country Music royalty, hosted a gathering of other country musicians at their house and discussed what can we do about singers who many decided were not country and they were winning country music awards." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 2 - Countryish Music of the 1970s (Encore) | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Country Music Royalty Status** : George Jones was considered "Country Music royalty" alongside his wife, Tammy Wynette. "George Jones and his wife Tammy Wynette, Country Music royalty..." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 2 - Countryish Music of the 1970s (Encore) | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Irony of Criticizing "Not Country" Artists** : The transcript notes the irony of George Jones being critical of artists for "not being country enough," given that his own very first hit on the country charts, "White Lightning," was described as "kind of country" and sounded a lot like early rock and roll. "There's more irony here in George Jones being critical of artists for not being country enough." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 2 - Countryish Music of the 1970s (Encore) | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Marriage to Tammy Wynette** : George Jones's marriage to Tammy Wynette was tumultuous and his "drunken escapades" were publicly known. Wynette's song "Stand By Your Man" could have "easily turned into a joke" because of their relationship. "The most accepted image was that of faithful wife like Tammy Weinette putting up with George Jones and his drunken escapades." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 23 - Women of Country Music in the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/023-country-women-72820-2-45-pm
**Protest Against Country-Pop Crossover (1974)** : In 1974, George Jones and Tammy Wynette famously left the Country Music Association in protest over Olivia Newton-John's success and her Grammy win, viewing her as not "country enough" due to the blurring lines between country and pop.
