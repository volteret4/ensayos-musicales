## curiosities
**Opposition to "Please Please Please"** : Sid Nathan, Ralph Bass's boss at King Records (parent company of Federal Records), was initially dismissive of "Please Please Please," believing it was just James Brown screaming one word repeatedly and predicting it could not be a hit. He opposed its release, and according to Brown's autobiography, allegedly fired Bass for putting it out before rehiring him when it became successful. **Reluctance to Release "Try Me"** : Sid Nathan initially refused to release James Brown's song "Try Me," even threatening to drop Brown from Federal Records. He only conceded after Brown independently recorded a demo and generated massive radio demand, which resulted in 25,000 orders for the single. Nathan then reluctantly agreed to finance a proper studio recording for the song.
