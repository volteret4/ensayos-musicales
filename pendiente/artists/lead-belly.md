**Central Figure in New York Folk Scene** : Lead Belly was part of a group of left-wing intellectuals and folk singers in New York, centered around Alan Lomax and Pete Seeger, which was pivotal to the Greenwich Village folk scene and the formation of The Armanac Singers and The Weavers. "he hooked up with a group of left-wing intellectuals and folk singers, centering on Alan Lomax, Pete Seeger, and Leab Belly." ← Episode 97： ＂Song to Woody＂ by Bob Dylan | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3DufLVcZyc4
**Friend of Pete Seeger** : Lead Belly was friends with Pete Seeger in the 1940s. "He had been friends with both Woody Guthrie and Lead Belly in the 40s." ← Episode 92： ＂The Lion Sleeps Tonight＂ by the Tokens | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QMP_DFyktMg
**Influence on Dave Van Ronk** : Dave Van Ronk's love for folk blues artists like Lead Belly helped transition him from traditional jazz into the folk scene. "he'd started out as a traditional jazz guitarist and banjo player, but had slowly moved on into the folk side of things, through his love of blues singers like Bessie Smith and folk blues artists like Lead Belly." ← Episode 97： ＂Song to Woody＂ by Bob Dylan | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3DufLVcZyc4
**Repertoire for Dylan** : Lead Belly's songs were part of Odetta's repertoire, which Bob Dylan learned after his epiphany, performing them with Spider-John Körner. "Dylan had soon sold his electric guitar and bought an acoustic, and he immediately learned all of Odetta's repertoire and started performing her songs and lead bellies with a friend Spider-John Körner" ← Episode 97： ＂Song to Woody＂ by Bob Dylan | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3DufLVcZyc4
**Skiffle Repertoire** : His songs were a primary component of the repertoire for skiffle groups, alongside those of Woody Guthrie and Big Bill Broonzy. "a repertoire that was made up primarily of songs by Lead Belly, Woody Guthrie and Big Bill Brunze." ← Episode 70： ＂Move It＂ by Cliff Richard and the Drifters | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uoEWP94f6S4
