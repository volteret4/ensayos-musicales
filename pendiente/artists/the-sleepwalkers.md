## curiosities
**Conflicting Accounts with Kip Tyler** : There is significant confusion regarding Kip Tyler's claimed past with The Sleepwalkers. Some articles state Tyler was the lead singer of "The Sleepwalkers, the toughest rock-and-roll band in the school" at Union High School before forming The Flips, and that he was born in 1929. This creates a chronological paradox, suggesting either two distinct bands named The Sleepwalkers a decade apart, or that Tyler was still in high school at 28, or a deep factual error in these accounts. **Early LSD Exposure** : Henry Vestine, a frequent guitarist for The Sleepwalkers, had already started taking LSD during this period, though the other band members did not indulge. This indicates an early connection between LSD use and musicians within this circle, preceding its wider cultural impact. **Formation and Early Gigs** : Formed by the instrumentalists from The Debarrens—Bruce Johnston (piano), Dave Schostack (saxophone), and Sandy Nelson (drums)—after the vocal group began to dissolve. They frequently had a young blues guitarist named Henry Vestine sit in with them and often played at parties organized by University High student Kim Fowley. **Witnessing John Dolphin's Murder** : On February 1, 1958, Bruce Johnston, Dave Shostack, and Sandy Nelson went to see John Dolphin, owner of Dolphins of Hollywood, hoping to secure a record deal. While waiting, they encountered Percy Ivy, who confronted Dolphin about money owed, leading to a heated argument where Ivy shot and killed Dolphin. Dave Shostack was also hit in the leg. Johnston comforted the dying Dolphin, while Nelson, upon returning after seeking help, found Johnston trying to humor Ivy by negotiating a record deal. "Ivy pulled out a gun and shot Dolphin.
