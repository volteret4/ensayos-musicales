## curiosities
**Background as Street Musician** : Before joining Can, Damo Suzuki was a young, nomadic Japanese troubadour who earned his living by playing music on the streets of various European cities. **Departure from Can to become Jehovah's Witness** : After the release of "Future Days," Damo Suzuki, reportedly unhappy with his role as a vocalist and with the band's direction, decided to leave the group and become a Jehovah's Witness. "damos suquibrillán encantado con su faceta de vocalista y con la banda, abandonó el grupo para convertirse en testigo de Jehová." ← CAN ｜ La Banda que (posiblemente) Inspiró a tu banda preferida | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4qHiw2wHWAI
**Spontaneous Audition and Recruitment by Can** : Suzuki was discovered by Holger Czukay and Jaki Liebezeit playing outside a Munich cafe and was immediately invited to sing with Can that same evening. He accepted without hesitation, and despite knowing only a couple of chords, he improvised lyrics during a performance that reportedly horrified the audience, causing 500 people to leave, yet profoundly captivated the band, leading to his full-time recruitment. "Le proposeron cantar esa misma noche, a lo que Suzuki aceptó sin pensárselo. Sabiendo tan solo un par de acordes, improvisando la letra, hizo una performance que horroriza al público, dicen que mis 500 personas se fueron del show, pero enamoraron a la banda." ← CAN ｜ La Banda que (posiblemente) Inspiró a tu banda preferida | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4qHiw2wHWAI
**Unique Vocal Style** : Damo Suzuki's approach to lyrics was characterized by being original and improvised, especially when he incorporated Japanese into Can's songs. He is recognized as one of the most singular and brilliant vocalists of his generation, with his singing on "Sing Swan Song" specifically noted for influencing Kanye West.
