## curiosities
**Boogie Woogie Piano Discovery** : Young Barry Gordy Jr. quickly figured out how to play the C, F, and G chords, enabling him to start playing Boogie Woogie Piano by ear. "Once young Barry Gordy Jr. figured out how to play the chords C, F and G, he was able to start playing Boogie Woogie Piano by E." ← Episode Sixty-Four： ＂Reet Petite＂ by Jackie Wilson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BYU-H4NvnmE
**Boxing vs. Music Career Decision** : Gordy paralleled Jackie Wilson's early career dilemma, initially unsure whether to become a musician or a boxer like his hero Joe Lewis, until he observed concert posters showing band leaders working every night and looking young, while boxers worked sporadically and looked old, leading him to choose music. "He knew what he was going to do, and it wasn't boxing." ← Episode Sixty-Four： ＂Reet Petite＂ by Jackie Wilson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BYU-H4NvnmE
**Childhood Piano Instruction** : Born the seventh of eight children to a poor Detroit family, Gordy was taught piano rudiments by an uncle who tried to teach him scales, arpeggios, and music reading, but Gordy, easily bored, soon discovered how to play simple triad chords by playing the first three notes of an arpeggio together. "An uncle, who tried to get him to learn to play in the proper manner, learning scales and arpeggios and how to read music." ← Episode Sixty-Four： ＂Reet Petite＂ by Jackie Wilson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BYU-H4NvnmE
**Collaboration with Billy Davis and Sisters** : Gordy began collaborating on songs with Billy Davis and his sister Gwen, who was dating Davis, agreeing to credit any individually written songs to the group and pool their earnings, with their primary goal being to write songs for Jackie Wilson. "Anything any of them wrote on their own would also get credited to them as a group, and they'd pool whatever they got, and they were going to write songs for Jackie Wilson." ← Episode Sixty-Four： ＂Reet Petite＂ by Jackie Wilson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BYU-H4NvnmE
**Conflict with Nat Tarnapol** : Gordy and Billy Davis insisted they should write both sides of Jackie Wilson's singles to get a fair share of royalties, especially as they were effectively producing the sessions without credit or pay, leading to a disagreement with manager Nat Tarnapol, who felt Wilson didn't need them. "Gordy and Davis insisted that they should get to write the songs on both sides of the singles so that they could get a fair share of the money, especially as they were effectively producing the sessions without either a credit or royalties." ← Episode Sixty-Four： ＂Reet Petite＂ by Jackie Wilson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BYU-H4NvnmE
**Creator of a Genre** : Barry Gordy Jr. is one of the very few people in the world who created an entire genre of music, primarily as a record company owner rather than a musician. "There are very few people in the world who can be said to have created an entire genre of music, and even fewer who were primarily record company owners, rather than musicians, but Barry Gordy Jr. was one of them." ← Episode Sixty-Four： ＂Reet Petite＂ by Jackie Wilson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BYU-H4NvnmE
**Early Songwriting Career** : Gordy began his music industry career as a songwriter, focusing on his early songwriting efforts before becoming a record executive. **Founding Anna Records** : After the fallout with Nat Tarnapol, Barry Gordy's sisters, Gwen and Anna Gordy, along with Billy Davis, went on to found their own record label, Anna Records. "Davis and Gwen Gordy went off to found their own record label, along with Gwen and Barry's sister Anna. Anna Records, as it was called, didn't have the most propitious start, with its first single being a Davis and Gwen Gordy song, Hope and Pray, performed by The Voice Masters." ← Episode Sixty-Four： ＂Reet Petite＂ by Jackie Wilson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BYU-H4NvnmE
**Korean War Service** : His nascent music career was put on hold when he was drafted to fight in the Korean War. "His attempt at a music career was soon put on the back burner when he was drafted to fight in the Korean War." ← Episode Sixty-Four： ＂Reet Petite＂ by Jackie Wilson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BYU-H4NvnmE
**Mentoring Smokey Robinson** : After leaving Jackie Wilson's production team, Barry Gordy decided to groom another young artist for stardom, seeing great potential in William "Smokey" Robinson and his group, The Miracles. "Barry, meanwhile, decided to groom another young artist for Stardom. He saw a lot of possibilities in a young man called William Robinson, who everyone referred to as Smokey, and his group The Miracles." ← Episode Sixty-Four： ＂Reet Petite＂ by Jackie Wilson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BYU-H4NvnmE
**Musical Influences** : From an early age, Gordy was a fan of vocal group performances, particularly touching songs about loneliness, loving "Paper Doll" by The Mills Brothers and "We Three" by The Ink Spots, and his favorite Boogie record was Hazel Scott's "Boogie Woogie." "He loved Paper Doll by the mills put this. And we three by the ink spots." ← Episode Sixty-Four： ＂Reet Petite＂ by Jackie Wilson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BYU-H4NvnmE
**Post-Military Struggles** : After military service, he held various short-term jobs and was seen by his family as a "bum" and a dreamer who failed to realize his aspirations of becoming a successful songwriter and millionaire. "He never held down a steady job, and he was a dreamer, who saw himself as becoming a successful songwriter and a millionaire, but had never quite managed to make anything of his dreams." ← Episode Sixty-Four： ＂Reet Petite＂ by Jackie Wilson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BYU-H4NvnmE
**Significance in Music History** : He is considered one of the most important people in 20th-century music, as important to the music of the 1960s as Sam Phillips was to the 1950s, if not more so. "He was as important to the music of the 60s as Sam Phillips was to the 50s, if not more important." ← Episode Sixty-Four： ＂Reet Petite＂ by Jackie Wilson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BYU-H4NvnmE
