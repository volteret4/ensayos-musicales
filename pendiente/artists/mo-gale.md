# artist - Mo Gale

## curiosities
**Manager of Key Black Acts** : Mo Gale was a prominent manager for almost every important Black act based in New York during the late 1930s and early 1940s. His roster included Lucky Millinder, Rosetta Tharpe, The Ink Spots, Ella Fitzgerald, Chick Webb, and Louis Jordan. "he was the manager of almost every important Black Act based in New York in the late 30s and early 40s." ← Episode 50： ＂Honky Tonk＂, by Bill Doggett | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H2rYnKmeZF0
**Manager of Millinder and Webb** : Mo Gale was the manager for both Lucky Millinder and Chick Webb. He claimed it was his idea to bring Sister Rosetta Tharp into Millinder's band, viewing her as a "juggle" to Millinder's "Chick Webb." "Mo Gale, Melinda's manager, was also Chick Webb's manager, and claimed that it was his idea and that he'd seen Tharp as being an elephant's juggle to Melinda's Chick Webb." ← Episode 5： ＂This Train＂ by Sister Rosetta Tharpe | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=II-hDnwUgT8
**Savoy Club Operator** : In addition to managing numerous artists, Mo Gale also ran the famous Savoy Club, a key venue in New York's music scene. "as well as running the Savoy Club, which we talked about in several of the earliest episodes of the podcast." ← Episode 50： ＂Honky Tonk＂, by Bill Doggett | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H2rYnKmeZF0
**Talent Pool Strategy** : Mo Gale maintained an existing pool of talent among his managed acts, often finding musicians for one act from within another. This explains how Bill Doggett moved from Lucky Millinder's band to The Ink Spots, both under Gale's management. "and so whenever one of his acts needed a musician, he would tend to find them from his existing pool of talent." ← Episode 50： ＂Honky Tonk＂, by Bill Doggett | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H2rYnKmeZF0
**Vocal Pairing Suggestion** : Mo Gale suggested pairing Sister Rosetta Tharpe with Trevor Bacon, Lucky Millinder's main singer, for his band, drawing inspiration from the successful collaboration between Louis Jordan and Ella Fitzgerald in Chick Webb's band. "Mo Gale suggested, that Sister Rosetta Tharp would work well, paired up with Melinda's main singer Trevor Bacon." ← Episode 50： ＂Honky Tonk＂, by Bill Doggett | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H2rYnKmeZF0
