# artist - The Teen Kings

## members
- Billy Pat Ellis
- Roy Orbison

## labels
- Columbia Records (auditioned)
- Jewel
- Sun Records

## songs
**Ooby Dooby (Year Unspecified)** : A song written by Orbison's classmates Dick Penner and Wade Moore, which quickly entered The Teen Kings' set and became one of their most successful. They recorded it for Columbia (who turned them down but liked the song), then for Jewel, before Sam Phillips voided the Jewel contract and re-recorded it with Orbison for Sun. "It soon entered the set of the newly named Team Kings and became one of their most successful songs." ← Episode 42： ＂Ooby Dooby＂ by Roy Orbison and the Teen Kings | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1zvzpXqr-k8
**Rock House (Year Unspecified)** : This song was a follow-up single for The Teen Kings, originally written by Harold Jenkins (Conway Twitty). Orbison significantly reworked the demo and earned a co-writing credit. "Orbison took Jenkins's demo and substantially reworked it, earning himself a co-acting credit." ← Episode 42： ＂Ooby Dooby＂ by Roy Orbison and the Teen Kings | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1zvzpXqr-k8
**Your My Baby (Year Unspecified)** : This was the B-side to "Rock House," a song originally written by Johnny Cash and titled "Little Wollipoga," which was renamed to be more radio-friendly for The Teen Kings' version. "That was renamed to the rather more radio-friendly, Your My Baby, for Orbison's version." ← Episode 42： ＂Ooby Dooby＂ by Roy Orbison and the Teen Kings | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1zvzpXqr-k8

## curiosities
**Band's Disgruntlement and Departure** : Following the poor performance of "Rock House," The Teen Kings became increasingly disgruntled, believing Sam Phillips didn't care about them and feeling Roy Orbison monopolized the spotlight. They secretly decided to leave, packing up their equipment and driving away during a scheduled recording session while Orbison and Phillips were on a break. "Rock House didn't do very well, and the band were getting this gruntled. They felt that some Phillips didn't care about any of them, and they were also getting a bit sick of Roy himself, who they thought was taking too much of the spotlight, so they secretly made an agreement." ← Episode 42： ＂Ooby Dooby＂ by Roy Orbison and the Teen Kings | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1zvzpXqr-k8
**Columbia Records Audition and Rejection** : The Teen Kings auditioned for Columbia Records, recording "Ooby Dooby" and a cover of The Clovers' "Hey Miss Fanny." Columbia rejected them but liked "Ooby Dooby" enough to give it to Sid King and the Five Strings. "So instead the Team Kings went into the studio to audition for Columbia Records. They recorded two tracks at that initial session." ← Episode 42： ＂Ooby Dooby＂ by Roy Orbison and the Teen Kings | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1zvzpXqr-k8
**Local TV Series** : The Teen Kings established their own local TV series, in addition to their radio shows, further enhancing their regional presence. "The Team Kings soon got their own local TV series to go with the local radio shows they already had." ← Episode 42： ＂Ooby Dooby＂ by Roy Orbison and the Teen Kings | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1zvzpXqr-k8
