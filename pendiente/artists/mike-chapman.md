# artist - Mike Chapman

## curiosities
**Challenging Studio Sessions** : Chapman described working with Blondie in the studio as "real tough," citing significant animosity among band members (except for Chris Stein and Debbie Harry). He found them "really, really juvenile in their approach to life" and focused on fun rather than hard work. "He said the Blondies, as he called them, were tough in the studio, real tough. None of them liked each other except Chris and Debbie and there was so much animosity." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 57 - Punk and New Wave at New York's CBGB | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Influence on Debbie Harry** : Debbie Harry acknowledged that while Chapman sometimes drove her "crazy" due to his demand for multiple takes, she learned valuable lessons from him, particularly to "listen more carefully," a skill she found useful in acting as well. "I had a hard time doing a lot of takes, but I learned a lot from Mike too. I learned to listen more carefully, which isn't valuable in acting as well, where listening is more important than your own portrayal." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 57 - Punk and New Wave at New York's CBGB | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Producer for Blondie** : Mike Chapman, a producer with credentials from working with Sweet and Suzi Quatro, was enlisted by Chrysalis Records to help Blondie achieve a hit after their first two albums failed to gain traction. He was responsible for adding "some polish" to the band's sound. "They were with Chrysalis records and they wanted Blondie to get a hit so they hooked them up with producer Mike Chapman." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 57 - Punk and New Wave at New York's CBGB | https://www.ftr70.com/
