# artist - Alan Fried

- 1958 Chuck Berry and Jerry Lee Lewis Tour (MC)

## curiosities
**Boston Concert Incident and Downfall** : During the 1958 Chuck Berry and Jerry Lee Lewis tour, Alan Fried's career began its precipitous decline at a Boston concert. After police forced the audience to sit down for dancing, Fried defiantly told the audience, "it looks like the Boston police don't want you to have a good time." This led to police falsely blaming the show for numerous crimes and contributed to Fried's eventual firing. **Career Downturn and Industry Changes** : Fried's career was already in decline as rock and roll evolved, shifting from predominantly black musicians to white artists and being increasingly dominated by major labels producing "pre-packaged teen idols." Fried lacked the connections with major labels and the understanding of this manufactured pop, unlike his rapport with R&B records from smaller labels like Chess. "Rock and roll was moving from being a music made largely by black musicians to one dominated by white people, and to make matters worse, the major labels had finally got a handle on it, and started churning out dozens of pre-packaged teen idols, most of them called Bobby." ← Episode 67： ＂Johnny B. **Forced Resignation from Radio Station** : Following the Boston incident and existing difficulties working with him, Fried's radio station, now profitable, used a "morals clause" in his contract to fire him. He was permitted to frame it as a resignation, but it marked a significant loss of prominence. "Now that this had happened, they took the opportunity to use the Mubbles' clause in Fried's contract to fire him, although he was allowed to present it as a resignation instead of a firing." ← Episode 67： ＂Johnny B. **Payola Scandal Scapegoat** : Within a few years of losing his prominent radio job, Alan Fried became the industry's designated fall guy for the widespread practice of payola. His involvement included receiving writing credits for songs like "Mabeline" and "Sincerely" as a form of payment. "He would, within a couple of years, become the designated industry fall guy for the practice of Paola." ← Episode 67： ＂Johnny B. Goode＂, by Chuck Berry | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sHlA-qihuNw
**Post-Scandal Career and Death** : After the payola scandal broke, Fried lost his job at WABC by the end of 1959 and spent his remaining years moving between progressively smaller radio stations, never regaining his previous prominence.
