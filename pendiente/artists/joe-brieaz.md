**Elektra Records Contract and Promotional Campaign** : After being discovered by Jerry Brande, Joe Brieaz secured a major contract with Elektra Records for half a million dollars, representing a significant commercial opportunity for the United States to join the exploding global glam movement. Elektra Records invested heavily in his promotion, launching an extensive campaign through posters, magazines, and television. "él consigue gracias a Jerry Brande un primer contrato con el extra recorso por medio millón de dólares y la verdad es que para Estados Unidos era una gran oportunidad porque con el movimiento glam y con David Bowie explotando de popularidad en todo el mundo." ← Music Radar Clan > Tras los inicios del Glam. **Failed Tour Attempts** : Due to his explicit homosexuality, eccentric behavior, and appearance, Joe Brieaz gained many enemies, hindering his career from the outset. He was unable to undertake a significant tour, as numerous venues directly refused to host his concerts. He had aspirations to tour Europe, specifically the Opéra Jaws de Paris, where glam music was highly demanded. **Forced Retirement and Later Life** : Following the commercial failure of his first and second albums with Elektra Records and the widespread societal rejection, Joe Brieaz was forced into retirement. He continued to live poorly, at one point staying in a luxury hotel but eventually returning to prostitution. He tragically died of AIDS in the 1980s, completely forgotten and buried from public memory. **Legacy as a Forgotten Genius and Documentary Controversy** : Joe Brieaz's records were uncatalogued, forgotten, and unlisted until later influential artists, such as Morrissey, began referencing him as an important, forgotten genius. **Life in California and Discovery by Jerry Brande (Post-1972)** : After his difficult experience with the band Pigeon and his time in a psychiatric hospital, Joe Brieaz lived in poverty in California, residing in a completely abandoned and ruined apartment. **Societal Resistance and Perceived Lack of Talent** : Joe Brieaz's career faced immense challenges, primarily due to the conservative American society that viewed the glam movement with suspicion, particularly when it came from American artists with such an appearance.
