**Earl Palmer - Legendary Precision Timekeeping** : A studio legend recounts Earl Palmer's incredible ability to precisely adjust song tempos; after a producer requested a take be two seconds shorter than 3:20, Palmer would reportedly "wind up his leg like a clock," count the new tempo, and deliver the next take exactly at 3:18. "Palmer would then pretend to wind up his leg like a clock. Count out the new tempo. **Metronomic Time Sense** : Drummer Earl Palmer possessed an "absolutely metronomic time sense," a skill that was crucial during the recording of Fats Domino's "Blueberry Hill." His precise timing allowed engineer Bunny Robin to seamlessly edit together a coherent version of the song from multiple half-finished takes. "thanks to the absolutely metronomic time sense of Earl Palmer, he managed to do it so well that after more than 30 years of listening to the record, I am still not certain exactly where the join is." ← Episode 45： ＂Blueberry Hill＂, by Fats Domino | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VqIwZGJHgBg
