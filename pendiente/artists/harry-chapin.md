## songs
**Cats in the Cradle (1974)** : This song reached number one on the charts in December 1974 and was inducted into the Grammy Hall of Fame in 2011. Tom Bryan critically described it as a "four-minute musical guilt trip" that oversimplifies the complexities of parenthood to the sole question of a father's physical or mental presence. He further characterized its tear-jerking coda, depicting an adult child having no time for his father, as "pretty dickish," arguing the father shouldn't demand his son's time when the son has a new job and a sick child. The song was inspired by a poem written by Chapin's wife, Sandra, combined with the real-life distant relationship between her ex-husband and his father. Sandra, in turn, was inspired by a country music song she heard on a long drive, which told the story of an old couple reminiscing about their grown children and grandchildren, although she could not recall the song's title or performer. ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 48 - The Worst of the 70s The Music of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/

## curiosities
**Rock Critic Disdain** : Harry Chapin reflected in 1978 that "the cool school of rock critics" consistently disliked his music, often making it clear that liking his songs was "not cool." He felt they would "get vicious" when discussing his work, criticizing his lyrics or storytelling, while at the same time meticulously analyzing single words in other artists' songs. ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 48 - The Worst of the 70s The Music of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/
