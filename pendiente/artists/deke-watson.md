# artist - Deke Watson

## member of
- The Brown Dots

## curiosities
**Claim to Ink Spots Name** : Watson attempted to claim credit for numerous good ideas within The Ink Spots, including the band's name and the "Top and Bottom" formula, though historical consensus often attributes these to others. He also made outrageous claims, such as the original lineup performing at King Edward VIII's coronation. "Watson in his book does seem to take credit for absolutely every good idea anyone involved in the band had, and for other things, which had nothing to do with them." ← Episode 6： ＂That's When Your Heartaches Begin＂ by the Ink Spots | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9mYSQaysXRg
**Early Stage Name (Dice Vastus)** : Before joining The Ink Spots, Deke Watson worked under the stage name Dice Vastus when performing with his "coffee part group," The Percolating Poppies. This name was associated with his early performance style. "When he was in the percolating puppies, Watson used to work under the name for Dice Vastus." ← Episode 6： ＂That's When Your Heartaches Begin＂ by the Ink Spots | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9mYSQaysXRg
**Leadership Conflict with Bill Kenny** : Watson believed himself to be the leader of The Ink Spots because he had originally assembled the band and been its onstage leader before Bill Kenny joined. This belief led to significant personality conflicts and power struggles within the group. "Watson thought of himself as the leader, on the grounds that he was the one who'd put the band together, named it, and been the onstage leader until Kenny came along." ← Episode 6： ＂That's When Your Heartaches Begin＂ by the Ink Spots | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9mYSQaysXRg
**Legal Setbacks and Financial Interest** : Deke Watson was largely considered the "real loser" in the first major legal battle over The Ink Spots' name. While he retained some financial interest in the name, he was explicitly prohibited from touring with The Ink Spots or rejoining the band he had co-founded. "The only real loser was Deke Watson. He continued to get some money for his share of the ink spots name, although I've seen some claims that Bill Kenny bought him out totally." ← Episode 6： ＂That's When Your Heartaches Begin＂ by the Ink Spots | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9mYSQaysXRg
**Quitting The Ink Spots (1944)** : In 1944, Deke Watson quit The Ink Spots, reportedly due to manager Mo Gale giving Bill Kenny a pay raise that Watson and Hoppy Jones did not receive. Following his departure, Watson attempted to form his own version of The Ink Spots, leading to legal disputes.
