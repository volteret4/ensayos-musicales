**Influential British Producer** : Nellee Hooper was one of the most important British music producers of the late 1990s, renowned for his work with artists like Björk, Madonna, and U2. He was also a member of the Wild Bunch movement in Bristol and collaborated with Björk on Trip Hop music which influenced Massive Attack. **Initial Producer for *I Do Not Want What I Haven't Got* (1990)** : Nellee Hooper was initially hired to produce Sinead O'Connor's second album, *I Do Not Want What I Haven't Got*. However, Sinead walked out after just two days, stating that they "couldn't get on together," leading to Chris Birkett taking over production. "Basically, the story is that they started off this album with another producer, Nelly Hooper. **Introduced Björk to Trip Hop and Electronic Sounds to U2 and Madonna** : Hooper was instrumental in introducing Björk to the potent influences of Trip Hop, profoundly shaping her sound. He also introduced electronic sounds to U2 and worked on Madonna's more electronic productions towards the end of the 1990s.
