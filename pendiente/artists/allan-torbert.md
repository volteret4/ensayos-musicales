# artist - Allan Torbert

## curiosities
**KRAK General Manager** : Torbert later bought the country and western station KRAK in Sacramento and served as its general manager, demonstrating his familiarity with "real country music" staples such as Eddie Arnold, Buck Owens, and Porter Wagoner. "But then bought the country in Western station, KRAK in Sacramento, and served as the station's general manager." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 2 - Countryish Music of the 1970s (Encore) | https://www.ftr70.com/
**KSFO Station Manager** : In the 1950s, Allan Torbert was the station manager of KSFO in San Francisco, which featured a middle-of-the-road format including artists like Frank Sinatra, Tony Bennett, and later Barbra Streisand. Under his management, KSFO was the most popular radio station in the Bay Area for about 20 years. "In the 1950s, Alan Torbert was the station manager of KSFO in San Francisco, which had kind of a middle of the road format, you know, Frank Sinatra, Tony Bennett, later on, Barbara Streisand like that." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 2 - Countryish Music of the 1970s (Encore) | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Modern Country Radio Memo** : Torbert's consulting firm issued a memo to country radio stations defining "modern country" as having "no relationship to rural or mountain life," explicitly excluding "screech fiddles, twanging guitars, no mournful nasal twangs." Instead, it advocated for a "sweeping sound full of orchestrations, multi-voiced choruses, amplified instruments, and sophisticated arrangements, and an adult lyric approach." "A memo went out to these stations, and this is some of what the memo said, and I'm quoting. Modern country has no relationship to rural or mountain life. You find no screech fiddles, no twanging guitars, no mournful nasal twangs. Today, you will find the sweeping sound full of orchestrations, multi-voiced choruses, amplified instruments, and sophisticated arrangements, and an adult lyric approach." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 2 - Countryish Music of the 1970s (Encore) | https://www.ftr70.com/
