## curiosities
**US Success and Band Restrictions (Mid-1930s)** : British bandleader Ray Noble had a series of hits in the US during the mid-1930s. Due to the mutual protectionist agreement between musicians' unions, Noble and his vocalist Al Bowley were permitted to travel to the US, but Noble's British band was not. Consequently, Noble had to hire an American musician, Glenn Miller, to assemble a new band for his US performances.
