## songs
**(Untitled Cover of Johnny Otis Oldie) (Late 1959 / Early 1960)** : Emil Ford and the Checkmates released a "sound-like cover version" of an oldie from 1916 that Johnny Otis had previously attempted to revive. While Otis's version failed commercially, Ford's cover became the last British number one hit of the 1950s and the first of the 1960s. This record also achieved two significant milestones: it was the first number one hit by a black British artist and the first record by a black British person to sell over a million copies. Joe Meek was a co-producer on this record.
