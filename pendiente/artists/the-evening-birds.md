## curiosities
**Innovations in Style and Sound** : The group introduced innovations such as dressing in matching straight suits, a departure from the baggy trousers of older groups. They also incorporated extra bass singers, creating a much thicker bass part which in some ways prefigured the sound of doo-wop that emerged in America two decades later. **Most Popular Isekathamia Group (1933-1948)** : The Evening Birds, led by Solomon Linda, quickly became the central group in the Isekathamia scene and were the most popular group between 1933 and their split in 1948. "The evening birds quickly became the centre of this scene, and between 1933 and 1948, when they split, they were the most popular group around." ← Episode 92： ＂The Lion Sleeps Tonight＂ by the Tokens | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QMP_DFyktMg
**Revolving Lineup and Rivalry** : Similar to many doo-wop groups, The Evening Birds experienced a revolving lineup with members joining and leaving to form or join other groups like The Crocodiles and The Dundee Wandering Singers. There was also a competing group named The Evening Birds with a singer who sounded like Linda, leading to a long-running feud. "As with many of the dewwap groups they saw resembled, they had a revolving line up with members coming in going, and joining other groups like the crocodiles and the dundee wandering singers." ← Episode 92： ＂The Lion Sleeps Tonight＂ by the Tokens | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QMP_DFyktMg
**Secured Record Deal through Linda's Job** : The group's recording opportunity with Gallo Records, the only label in South Africa, came about because Solomon Linda had a day job packing records at their factory. This connection enabled him to get the group signed for recording sessions. "But it wasn't this popularity that got the evening birds recorded.
