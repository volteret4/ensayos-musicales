## curiosities
**Band Formation and Name Origin (1956)** : The Blue Caps were formed by Gene Vincent as his backing band before his Nashville recording session. Their name was derived from the blue hats they wore on stage, which were reportedly inspired by the golf caps frequently worn by President Eisenhower. This inspiration was humorously noted as "not the most rebellious" for a group that became a significant influence on youthful rebellion. **Cliff Gallop's Departure and Post-Band Life** : Cliff Gallop, the lead guitarist, left The Blue Caps because he felt he was "too old to be touring in a rock and roll band." Ken Nelson offered him a steady gig as a session guitarist to avoid travel, but Gallop declined, instead taking a job as a high school janitor and maintenance man, only playing occasional gigs for extra money. He became embarrassed when contacted by fans, refused to sign autographs, and his widow ensured his obituaries omitted his time with Gene Vincent after his death in 1989. **Mixed Musical Ability** : The Blue Caps were characterized as a "mixed ability group." While some members were described as "rudimentary musicians at best," the rockabilly genre was generally accommodating of "enthusiastic amateurs." Lead guitarist Cliff Gallop was a notable exception, considered one of the most technically accomplished guitarists in rockabilly. **Unstable Lineup (1956-1958)** : The band was plagued by an unstable lineup, with an astonishing 20 different individuals serving as members within its first two and a half years of existence. Gene Vincent struggled to maintain a consistent group for more than a few weeks or months at a time.
