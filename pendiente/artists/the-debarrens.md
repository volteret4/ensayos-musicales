## curiosities
**"Jenny Lee" Demo and Jan & Arnie Formation** : The Debarrens recorded a demo tape of a song about a local stripper, "Jenny Lee." Following this, Dean Torrance enlisted in the army reserves. While in basic training, he heard a familiar-sounding record on the radio credited to "Jan and Arnie." Jan Berry claimed the original tape had problems and he and Arnie Ginsburg had re-recorded it, leading to its discovery by Joe Lubin of Arwin Records and the formation of Jan and Arnie. **Early Formation and Split** : The Debarrens played several gigs, initially backed by instrumentalists Bruce Johnston (piano), Sandy Nelson (drums), and Dave Shostack (saxophone), all fellow University High students. The group gradually split due to parental pressure for members to focus on school or personal interests. Sandy Nelson was eventually fired by Jan Berry for playing his drums too loudly, leading to Nelson's prophetic statement about having a hit record "only drums." "This group played several gigs together, but slowly split apart as people's mothers wanted them to concentrate on school, or they got cars that they wanted to fix up." ← Episode 86： ＂LSD-25＂ by the Gamblers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UNd6UwbL1rA
**Interracial Vocal Group** : The Debarrens, formed at University High School in Los Angeles in the late 1950s, were one of the few interracial vocal groups of their time. The group's lineup included a Black lead singer (Chuck Steel), a Japanese tenor (Wally Yagi), two Jewish members (Arnie Ginsburg, John Saligman), and two white members (Jan Berry, Dean Torrance).
