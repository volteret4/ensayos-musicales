## songs
**Last Night (1961)** : This instrumental became a big hit for Satellite Records, reaching number three on the pop chart and number two on the R&B chart. Its authorship was debated, with credit given to session sax players Caples and Newman (horn part), Smoochie Smith (keyboard part), Chips Moman (combining ideas), and Charles Axton (family ownership). The first section of the original tape was erased, requiring a meticulous splice from another take. "The record became a big hit, and there were a couple of hypotheses as to why." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0

## curiosities
**Developing a Distinctive Sound** : The Mar-Keys were credited with helping Satellite Records develop the distinctive sound the label was seeking. "It was the marquees, who finally gave satellite the distinctive sound they were looking for" ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Name Change from The Royal Spades** : The group, originally known as The Royal Spades, was renamed The Mar-Keys by Estelle Axton, who found their original name "inappropriate." The new name was a combination of "marquee" (as in the noble title) and "keys" (as in keyboards). "Upon our marquee, as in the noble title, and keys as in keyboards, as Estelle Axton thought, entirely correctly, that their original name was inappropriate." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Recording Second Single Without Them** : While The Mar-Keys were on tour, Stax studio musicians recorded the group's second single without their involvement. The band members only discovered its release when they saw it in the record shop. "The second marquee's single was recorded by the studio musicians while the group were out on tour. The first they even knew about it was when they saw it in the shop." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Touring Resentment** : The Mar-Keys' tour to promote "Last Night" caused significant resentment among the black session players who had contributed to the record. These players felt the instrumental was primarily their work but that "a bunch of white boys were" receiving all the recognition. "which brought resentment from the Black Session players, some of whom claimed that during the session it hadn't even been intended as a marquee's record, and who have annoyed that even though the record was primarily their work, they weren't getting the recognition, and a bunch of white boys were." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
