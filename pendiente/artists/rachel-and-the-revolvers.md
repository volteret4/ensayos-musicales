# artist - Rachel and the Revolvers

## curiosities
**"Low-Commotion" Knockoff (Early 1960s)** : Brian Wilson collaborated with Gary Usher on a side project to create a "low-commotion knockoff" track titled "The Revolution," which was subsequently released under the name Rachel and the Revolvers. There are conflicting accounts regarding the discovery of the singer for this track: some sources claim Usher and Wilson found her by driving to Watts and asking the first black teenage girl they saw if she could sing, while others state a professional session singer, possibly Betty Willis (often confused with Betty Effort), was hired.
