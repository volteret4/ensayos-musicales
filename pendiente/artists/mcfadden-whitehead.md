# artist - McFadden & Whitehead

## labels

## songs
**Ain't No Stoppin' Us Now (1979)** : This song achieved the number one position on the Billboard R&B chart and reached number 13 on the Billboard Hot 100 in 1979. While Kenny Gamble presented it as a message to the Black community, its writers, McFadden and Whitehead, understood it as a declaration of their independence from Gamble and PIR's creative directives, a viewpoint that diverged from the company's perceived political focus. ## curiosities
**Conflict Over Song Interpretation** : While Kenny Gamble framed "Ain't No Stoppin' Us Now" as a message aimed at the Black community, McFadden and Whitehead viewed this interpretation as problematic, believing the company's heightened political focus at the time alienated white audiences and consequently reduced record sales, as their original intention was a universal message rooted in personal experiences. "Gamble and Huff presented this song as a message to the black community, which McFadden and Whitehead thought was part of the problem with the company at that point." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 37 - Philly Soul and the Sound of the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-37-philly-soul-and-the-sound-of-the-70s
**Desire to Sing, Not Just Write** : After successfully writing several songs for Philadelphia International Records, including their contributions to "Backstabbers" with Leon Huff, McFadden & Whitehead expressed a strong desire to perform their own material, a request initially resisted by Kenny Gamble, who preferred them to focus solely on songwriting.
