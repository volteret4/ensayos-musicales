# artist - Belleville Three

## members
- Derrick May
- Juan Atkins
- Kevin Saunderson

## curiosities
**Divergent Label Ventures** : Despite their close relationship, each member of the Belleville Three developed their own distinct vision for the techno genre and its business aspect. This led each of them to found their own independent record labels: Juan Atkins with Metroplex, Derrick May with Transmat, and Kevin Saunderson with KMS Records. "Esta divergencia se reflejo en el hecho de que cada uno de ellos fundó su propio sello discográfico, Derrick and Mike creó Transmat. **European Compilation Genesis** : Towards the end of the decade, the three reunited with Neil Rushton, a talent scout from Virgin Records, to curate a compilation album. This album was intended to introduce their new sound to a European audience, initially titled "The House Sound of Detroit" but ultimately renamed "Techno! The New Dance Sound of Detroit" after Atkins' track "Techno City." "Los tres se reunieron con Raeston para elegir las canciones que irían en el disco que presentase y se nuevos sonido a los europeos." ← Cómo Detroit Reinventó la Música Electrónica ｜ Pioneros del Techno | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H4AtVFOVtUw
**Formation and Early Influences** : In the late 1970s, Jonathan Atkins, Derrick May, and Kevin Saunderson, then students at Belleville school near Detroit, forged a friendship centered around music. They would gather at Atkins' home to intensely listen to artists like Parliament, Funkadelic, Kraftwerk, Gary Numan, and Italo Disco, often dimming the lights and discussing the sounds for hours. "Al salir de colegios se leían ir a casa de Atkins... escuchar música. Musica fan con bajos irrimos electrónicos como Parlameno, FUNCADELIC y sobre todo música europea electrónica, Crafwer, Gary Newman y lo que sería conocido más tarde como Italo Disco." ← Cómo Detroit Reinventó la Música Electrónica ｜ Pioneros del Techno | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H4AtVFOVtUw
**Global Impact and Recognition** : Within a few years of their formative listening sessions, these three individuals profoundly transformed how music was heard and danced to worldwide, earning them the monikers "The Creators, The Innovators, and The Renovators of Techno music," and securing their place in history as the Belleville III. "En unos años, estos tres chicos cambiarían la manera de escuchar e bailar la música en todo el mundo, y serían conocidas como Decreator, Diel Eveitor y Dino Vator de la música Tegno, y pasarían la historia como Belville III." ← Cómo Detroit Reinventó la Música Electrónica ｜ Pioneros del Techno | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H4AtVFOVtUw
**Socio-Economic Context of Detroit** : Their upbringing differed from that of Chicago House artists; they were raised in middle-class families, not the ghettos. This was due to the prosperity of Detroit, where Ford and General Motors factories offered numerous jobs under equal conditions for both Black and white residents, providing a good standard of living. "Atkins la tribuye que por primera vez, en Detroit, tanto blancos como nigos, podían trabajar en las mismas condiciones." ← Cómo Detroit Reinventó la Música Electrónica ｜ Pioneros del Techno | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H4AtVFOVtUw
