## curiosities
**Adapting to Bar Culture** : They developed a dance-oriented musical style rooted in honky-tonk, incorporating elements from bluegrass, western swing, and blues. This versatile approach allowed them to keep intoxicated crowds dancing with their limited instrumentation of two guitars, a double bass, and a drum kit. They began performing in these bars even before they were legally old enough to enter them. "So they very quickly developed a style that was rooted in honky-tongue music, but which was totally oriented around getting people dancing." ← Episode 36： ＂Blue Suede Shoes＂ by Carl Perkins | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TItMbEioi7Y
**Early Bar Performances in Tennessee** : The band refined their skills by playing in rough bars across Tennessee, necessitating the development of dance music. These venues were characterized by patrons who would often buy two bottles of beer at a time – one for drinking and one for smashing over someone else's head. "The Perkins Brothers spent their next several years honing their craft, playing some of the ruffus bars in Tennessee." ← Episode 36： ＂Blue Suede Shoes＂ by Carl Perkins | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TItMbEioi7Y
**Improvised Songwriting** : Carl and Jay Perkins would alternate lead vocals, and when they exhausted their prepared repertoire, Carl would spontaneously compose new songs around standard chord changes. He possessed the ability to improvise both words and music on the spot, often remembering successful choruses or lines, which gradually transformed these improvisations into structured and permanent parts of their setlist.
