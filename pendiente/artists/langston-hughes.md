# artist - Langston Hughes

## curiosities
**"Barry" Story and Dedication (1934)** : In 1934, Langston Hughes wrote a story about racial injustice titled "Barry," featuring a boy named Barry who told stories and sang. Hughes signed the dedication in the book containing this story as "Barry," rather than his own name, which is noted as another possible origin for Chuck Berry's connection to his name in "Johnny B. Good." **Influence on "Johnny B. Good" Title** : The widely circulated newspaper columns by poet Langston Hughes, which featured a character named Jesse B. Simple, are suggested as a possible influence on Chuck Berry's title "Johnny B. Good." Berry would have encountered these columns in his teenage years and early twenties.
