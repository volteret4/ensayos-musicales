# artist - George Goldner

## labels
- G Records
- Rama Records
- Tico Records

## curiosities
**Club Owner and Latin Music Pioneer** : Goldner began his career in the music industry as the owner of popular nightclubs featuring Latin music. The success of these clubs led him to establish Tico Records, a label dedicated to Latin recordings, notably including early works by Tito Puente. "When Goldner had started in the music industry, it had been as the owner of a chain of night clubs which featured Latin music." ← Episode 35： ＂Why Do Fools Fall In Love？＂ by Frankie Lymon and the Teenagers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AtZf9PJEttM
**Gambling Debts and Morris Levy's Acquisition** : Goldner had a business partner, Morris Levy, who owned 50% of his record companies. Goldner accumulated significant gambling debts, which he paid off by selling his record labels. Consequently, Levy eventually owned all of Goldner's companies, including the rights to songs Goldner was credited for. "Levy owned 50% of Goldeners record companies, and would soon vote at own all of them, as Goldener accumulated more gambling debts, and used his record labels to pay them off." ← Episode 35： ＂Why Do Fools Fall In Love？＂ by Frankie Lymon and the Teenagers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AtZf9PJEttM
**Interest in Latin Talent and Frankie Lymon** : Goldner was initially intrigued by The Premiers (The Teenagers) when Richie Barrett presented them, partly because two of the members were Latino, aligning with Goldner's love for Latin music. He quickly identified Frankie Lymon's potential to become a major star. "But he quickly latched on to the potential of Frankie Lyman as a star." ← Episode 35： ＂Why Do Fools Fall In Love？＂ by Frankie Lymon and the Teenagers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AtZf9PJEttM
**Music-Oriented Producer** : Despite his later financial troubles and business dealings, George Goldner was genuinely concerned with the music itself and had a consistent track record of producing hits. For instance, he had just produced "You Baby You" for The Cleftones around the time The Premiers (The Teenagers) signed with him. "And Goldner was someone who was actually concerned with the music, and who had been producing hits consistently for the last few years." ← Episode 35： ＂Why Do Fools Fall In Love？＂ by Frankie Lymon and the Teenagers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AtZf9PJEttM
**Transition to R&B and New Labels** : The Mambo boom attracted many Black teenagers to Goldner's Latin clubs, which in turn piqued his interest in their music. This led him to found Rama Records for R&B singles, followed by G Records, which he named after "G" by The Crows, a successful record released on Rama. "He started first Rama Records, as a label for R&B singles, and then G Records, named after the most successful record that had been put out on Rama, G, by the Cros." ← Episode 35： ＂Why Do Fools Fall In Love？＂ by Frankie Lymon and the Teenagers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AtZf9PJEttM
**Uncredited Co-writer for "Why Do Fools Fall in Love"** : George Goldner was originally credited as a co-writer for "Why Do Fools Fall in Love," although he did not contribute to its actual writing. He was, however, present in the studio during its recording. His credited rights to the song were later acquired by Morris Levy when Levy bought out Goldner's company holdings.
