## members

## curiosities
**Continued Touring** : Mike Pender continued to tour, or at least had tour dates booked, for decades after the split, with his show noted for sticking closer to a "straight guitar-based drum sound." "Pender still tours, or at least has tour dates booked over the course of the next year." ← Episode 113： ＂Needles and Pins＂ by The Searchers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=izC9ZBI0UHM
**Formation from Original Searchers Split** : Mike Pender formed his own touring band, "Mike Pender's Searchers," after disputes over money and his desire to be recognized as the group's star, leading to his departure from the original Searchers lineup in 1985. "Pender decided that he could make more money by touring at his own, but still doing essentially the same show, with hired backing musicians." ← Episode 113： ＂Needles and Pins＂ by The Searchers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=izC9ZBI0UHM
**Legal Battle over Naming Rights** : There were extensive legal battles between Mike Pender and the remaining members of The Searchers (McNally and Allen) over billing and the use of the band name, which nearly led to Pender's imprisonment. "There was a serious danger of Pender going to prison over their contractual disputes." ← Episode 113： ＂Needles and Pins＂ by The Searchers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=izC9ZBI0UHM
