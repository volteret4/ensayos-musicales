# artist - reconio Morricone

## curiosities
**A Fistful of Dollars Score - Resourceful Instrumentation** : Faced with a minimal budget for *Por un poñado de dólares*, reconio Morricone innovatively composed the score using an electric guitar, whistles, shouts, and percussions, as a full orchestra was not an option. This creative constraint resulted in a distinctive and genre-defining sound for the Western film. "Para la primera de ellas, por un poñado de dólares, apenas tenían presupuesto para la música y al no poder permitirse un arque está Morricone compuso una partitura para guitarra eléctrica, silbidos, gritos y percusiones." ← Las Bandas Sonoras Más Atípicas y Revolucionarias del Cine | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fiSrdUP3YXY
**Dollar Trilogy Scores - Iconic Western Sound and Budget Innovation** : reconio Morricone (Ennio Morricone) composed the unforgettable soundtracks for Sergio Leone's Dollar Trilogy films. For the first film, *Por un poñado de dólares* (*A Fistful of Dollars*), a limited budget meant an orchestra couldn't be afforded, prompting Morricone to compose a score for electric guitar, whistles, shouts, and percussions. While he refined this approach in *En la muerte tenía un precio* (*For a Few Dollars More*), it was for *El bueno soyol y el malo* (*The Good, the Bad and the Ugly*) that he wrote one of the most iconic scores in cinematic history. "Otras bandas sonoras que han dejado una huella imorrable en la historia del cine y han indefinido el sonido de lo este son las de la trilogía del dólar, compuestas por en el riconio Morricone dirigida a Sporzey, Leone." ← Las Bandas Sonoras Más Atípicas y Revolucionarias del Cine | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fiSrdUP3YXY
**The Good, the Bad and the Ugly Score - Landmark Achievement** : The score composed by reconio Morricone for *El bueno soyol y el malo* is recognized as one of the most iconic film scores in the history of cinema. This work cemented his innovative approach to scoring Westerns, building on his previous successes in the Dollar Trilogy. **The Hateful Eight - Redesigned Discarded Music** : Quentin Tarantino specifically requested "descartes" (discarded pieces) from reconio Morricone for the soundtrack of his film *Los odiosos 8* (*The Hateful Eight*). These "forgotten notes" found redemption and a new place within the narrative, highlighting how musical compositions can find their appropriate moment even after being set aside. "cuenten Tarantino le pidió esos descartes a Eniomorricone para la banda sonora de la película de los odiosos 8." ← Las Bandas Sonoras Más Atípicas y Revolucionarias del Cine | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fiSrdUP3YXY
**The Thing (1982) - Partial Usage and Director's Preference** : For the film *The Thing* (1982), reconio Morricone was hired with a larger budget than his previous works with John Carpenter. However, the director was not entirely satisfied with the outcome, and only a minimal portion of Morricone's extensive composition was ultimately used, primarily what became the main theme. This theme was noted for its close resemblance to Carpenter's own synthesized music. "De toda la música que compuso el italiano, apenas usaron el que sería el tema principal de la película, el cual poco difiere de la música sentitizada de Carpenter." ← Las Bandas Sonoras Más Atípicas y Revolucionarias del Cine | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fiSrdUP3YXY
