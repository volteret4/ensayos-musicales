## curiosities
**"Real King of Swing" Title** : Chick Webb was considered by many to be the person who most deserved the title "King of Swing" more than anyone else, even before Benny Goodman. "But Chick-Web was the person who deserved the title more than anyone else." ← Episode 4： ＂Choo Choo Ch'Boogie＂ by Louis Jordan | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DFZnwvsXGJM
**1937 Savoy Cutting Contest Victory** : In 1937, Chick Webb's Savoy Orchestra won a famous cutting contest against Benny Goodman's band at the Savoy Ballroom. This event attracted 4,000 dancers inside and 5,000 people outside, with Chick Webb's band emerging as the undisputed winner. "Chick Webb's band set up on one stage, Goodman's on another. Four thousand dancers crowded the inside of the ballroom, and despite a police cord on outside to keep trouble down, I know the five thousand people outside tried to hear what was happening, and Chick Webb's band won. Absolutely." ← Episode 4： ＂Choo Choo Ch'Boogie＂ by Louis Jordan | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DFZnwvsXGJM
**Arrangement Sales to Benny Goodman** : Benny Goodman, admiring Webb's band, purchased many of their arrangements and used them for his own band, including most of Goodman's biggest crowd-pleasers not written by Fletcher Henderson. The arrangements were bought from Edgar Sampson, Webb's saxophone player and main arranger. "Benny Goodman admired Webb's band enough, that he bought the bands' arrangements and used them himself." ← Episode 4： ＂Choo Choo Ch'Boogie＂ by Louis Jordan | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DFZnwvsXGJM
**Childhood Illness and Musical Start** : Chick Webb contracted tuberculosis of the spine as a child, which led him to take up the drums as a form of therapy. He began playing professionally at age eleven. "He was a small man, who contracted tuberculosis of the spine as a child, and he'd taken up the drums as a kind of therapy." ← Episode 4： ＂Choo Choo Ch'Boogie＂ by Louis Jordan | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DFZnwvsXGJM
**Declining Health and Death** : Chick Webb's disability significantly worsened, leading him to continue performing out of obligation to his musicians. He often passed out after shows and tragically died at age 34 in June 1939. "Chick Webb's disability had been affecting him more, and he was only continuing to perform, because he felt he owed it to his musicians. He died aged 34 in June 1939." ← Episode 4： ＂Choo Choo Ch'Boogie＂ by Louis Jordan | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DFZnwvsXGJM
**Leader of Premier Swing Band** : By the age of thirty, Chick Webb was leading what was considered "bar none, the best swing band in New York for dancing." His band was known for its incredible energy in inspiring Lindy-Hop dancing. "by the time he was thirty, he was leading what was, bar non, the best swing band in New York for dancing." ← Episode 4： ＂Choo Choo Ch'Boogie＂ by Louis Jordan | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DFZnwvsXGJM
**Lindy Hoppers Strike and Misunderstanding** : Chick Webb's band appeared less exciting than Basie's in terms of getting people dancing due to a strike by Whitey's Lindy Hoppers. Webb had mistakenly believed the dancers were disparaging his band, leading him to insult them, prompting their leader, Whitey, to instruct them to dance only to Basie's music. "Chick Webb had misheard a discussion between a couple of the dancers about how good the Basie band was going to be that night. He assumed they were saying that Basie was going to be better than him, and he got into a huff." ← Episode 4： ＂Choo Choo Ch'Boogie＂ by Louis Jordan | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DFZnwvsXGJM
**Managed by Mo Gale** : Chick Webb was a bandleader also managed by Mo Gale, who tried to draw a parallel between Webb and Lucky Millinder's band with the addition of Rosetta Tharp. "Mo Gale, Melinda's manager, was also Chick Webb's manager." ← Episode 5： ＂This Train＂ by Sister Rosetta Tharpe | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=II-hDnwUgT8
**Nearly Unbeatable in Cutting Contests** : Chick Webb's band won numerous cutting contests, only definitively losing once, to Duke Ellington, a loss considered without shame due to Ellington's stature and different musical approach. "The only time Chick Webb ever definitely lost a cutting contest was against Duke Allington, but everyone knew that Chick Webb and Duke Allington weren't really trying to do the same kind of thing." ← Episode 4： ＂Choo Choo Ch'Boogie＂ by Louis Jordan | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DFZnwvsXGJM
**Rivalry with Benny Goodman** : Chick Webb's band had a significant rivalry with Benny Goodman's band. While Goodman's band was larger, Webb's band was considered more popular among true connoisseurs. "Goodman's band was bigger, in every sense, but Webb's band was more popular with those who knew the real deal when they heard it." ← Episode 4： ＂Choo Choo Ch'Boogie＂ by Louis Jordan | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DFZnwvsXGJM
**Talent of Key Members** : The enduring quality of Chick Webb's band was attributed to the talent of its "two big stars," Ella Fitzgerald and Louis Jordan, an alto sax player and male lead vocalist. "That's how good Webb's band were, and it's also how good his two big stars were." ← Episode 4： ＂Choo Choo Ch'Boogie＂ by Louis Jordan | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DFZnwvsXGJM
