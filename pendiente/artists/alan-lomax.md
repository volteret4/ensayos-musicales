# artist - Alan Lomax

## genres
- Folk (as folklorist/collector)

## curiosities
**Carla Rotolo's Employer** : Carla Rotolo, instrumental in Bob Dylan's breakthrough, worked as an assistant to Alan Lomax. "Carla, who was the assistant to Alan Lomax" ← Episode 97： ＂Song to Woody＂ by Bob Dylan | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3DufLVcZyc4
**Central Figure in New York Folk Scene** : Alan Lomax was a key figure in a group of left-wing intellectuals and folk singers in New York, which included Pete Seeger and Lead Belly, centered at Armanac House in Greenwich Village. "he hooked up with a group of left-wing intellectuals and folk singers, centering on Alan Lomax, Pete Seeger, and Leab Belly." ← Episode 97： ＂Song to Woody＂ by Bob Dylan | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3DufLVcZyc4
**Contract for Ramblin' Jack Elliott** : While in the UK, Lomax facilitated Ramblin' Jack Elliott's contract with Topic Records, a folk label affiliated with the Communist Party of Great Britain through the Workers Music Association. "Alan Lomax, who had moved to the UK temporarily to escape the anti-communist witch hunt, had got Elliott to contract with Topic Records" ← Episode 97： ＂Song to Woody＂ by Bob Dylan | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3DufLVcZyc4
**Interest in Robert Johnson** : Lomax was a fan of Robert Johnson and traveled to Mississippi to try and record him, unaware that Johnson had already died. "Hammond's friend Alan Lohmachs had also been a fan of Johnson. He'd gone to Mississippi later to try to record Johnson, also without having realised that Johnson had died." ← Episode 97： ＂Song to Woody＂ by Bob Dylan | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3DufLVcZyc4
