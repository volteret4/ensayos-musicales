## curiosities
**Influence on The Coasters' Later Style** : Lieber and Stoller's earlier work with The Robins served as a stylistic model for The Coasters' later songs. After becoming bored with writing for teenagers, The Coasters' music shifted to an "older style" that featured social comments and more adult topics, reminiscent of The Robins' material. "From that point on, most of the group's songs would be in the older style that they'd used with the robins, songs making social comments and talking about Adolf topics." ← Episode 68： ＂Yakety Yak＂ by the Coasters | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dtMHDD0-oOo
**Model for "Shopping for Clothes"** : Early songs written by Lieber and Stoller for The Robins were among the influences for The Coasters' comedy blues song "Shopping for Clothes," originally by Kent Harris.
