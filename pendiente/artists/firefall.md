# artist - Firefall

## members
- David Muse (saxes, flute, keyboards)
- Jock Bartley (lead guitar)
- Michael Clarke (drummer, founding member)
- Rick Roberts (lead singer, founding member)
- Timothy B. Schmit (background vocals on "Just Remember I Love You")

## genres
- Country Rock
- Yacht Rock

## curiosities
**Austin City Limits Season 2 Debut (1977)** : Firefall appeared on the opening episode of Season 2 of Austin City Limits, which aired just a few weeks after the release of their album "Luna Sea" in July 1977. Their inclusion indicated Austin City Limits' expanding musical horizons beyond traditional country. "Firefall was on the opening episode of season two of Austin City Limits just a few weeks after the release of the album Luna C in July 1977." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 49 - Austin City Limits and the 70s Country Music Revolution | http://ftr70.com
**Formation and Byrds/Burrito Brothers Connection (1974)** : Firefall formed in Boulder, Colorado, in 1974, with founding members including Rick Roberts (who had replaced Gram Parsons in The Flying Burrito Brothers) and Michael Clarke (drummer for The Byrds and The Flying Burrito Brothers). This lineage links Firefall directly to country rock pioneers. "Their DNA is in the music of the Eagles and their DNA is in the band that formed in Boulder, Colorado in 1974, Firefall." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 49 - Austin City Limits and the 70s Country Music Revolution | http://ftr70.com
**Sound Description (Rick Roberts)** : Rick Roberts, Firefall's lead singer, described the band's sound as "swish," characterizing it as a smooth beat that just flows. "Rick Roberts was asked to describe Firefall's sound and he said, swish, a smooth beat that just flows." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 49 - Austin City Limits and the 70s Country Music Revolution | http://ftr70.com
**Sound Philosophy (Jock Bartley)** : Jock Bartley, Firefall's lead guitar player, emphasized a philosophy of "what you didn't play" – the spaces left in the music – as crucial to their sound, drawing a comparison to Miles Davis. He stated that every part played by the band, including David Muse on saxes, flute, and keyboards, was to further the song, which contributed to their first three records sounding great even today. "It wasn't really what you played, it's what you didn't play, the spaces you leave." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 49 - Austin City Limits and the 70s Country Music Revolution | http://ftr70.com
