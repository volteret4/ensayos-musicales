**Impeached the President (June 1973)** : Released on June 1st, 1973, by The Honeydrippers (essentially Roy Hammond with recruited high school students), this political funk song emerged amidst the growing calls for Richard Nixon's impeachment and resignation due to the Watergate scandal. It is one of the most sampled songs in history, though Hammond received no financial compensation for its extensive use until its re-release on iTunes in 2017. ## curiosities
**Formation of Independent Label** : After the president of Mercury Records expressed that Roy Hammond was "holding himself back" by recording political songs, Hammond decided to form his own record label. This enabled him to release politically charged music, such as "Impeached the President," without label interference. **Sampling of "Impeached the President"** : "Impeached the President" is one of the most sampled songs in music history. Roy Hammond was aware that other artists were "borrowing parts of his song" for years, but he only confirmed it when he heard Janet Jackson's "That's The Way Love Goes." Despite its widespread sampling, Hammond received no financial compensation until the song was re-released on iTunes in 2017, largely due to its frequent unauthorized use. Hammond passed away in 2020.
