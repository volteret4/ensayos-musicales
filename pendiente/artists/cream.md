**Sunshine of Your Love (1968)** : This hit single from "Disraeli Gears" achieved success by entering the Top 40 in early 1968, and later re-charting that summer to reach number five in August 1968. The song is notable for Jack Bruce's high tenor vocals, Eric Clapton's distinctive guitar riffs, and Ginger Baker's double bass drum work, offering "another glimpse of what is to come for heavy metal." Its structure, built around a repeated main riff, foreshadowed a common technique in heavy metal. Jack Bruce's lead vocal style on this track is also suggested as an influence on Robert Plant. ## curiosities
**Forerunner of Heavy Metal** : Cream is identified as one of the most influential bands and a significant forerunner of heavy metal. While not a heavy metal band itself, it contributed crucial "heavy metal pieces" that helped shape the genre's development. "So while not the only band that is the forerunner of heavy metal, one of the most influential bands was Cream." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 24 - Loud, Louder, Loudest - 70s Heavy Metal | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-24-loud-louder-loudest-70s-heavy-metal
**Inspiration for Tin Machine** : Cream was cited as an inspiration for David Bowie's band Tin Machine. Bowie aimed to create a four-piece rock band with himself solely as the lead singer, harkening back to the dynamic and influential styles of bands like Cream. "Inspiration was taken from Jimmy Hendrix, Cream and the Pixies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2FyAZGUI_mk ← cream
