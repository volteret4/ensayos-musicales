## curiosities
**Ability to Cover Other Artists' Hits** : Campbell possessed a rare talent for covering other singers' hits without drawing criticism or alienating the original artists' fans. He could seamlessly transition between songs like Jerry Reed's "Today Is Mine," The Beatles' "Yesterday," and Andy Williams' "Moon River," demonstrating his versatility and appeal. Campbell would join John Hartford (composer of "Gentle On My Mind") and Nelson Riddle's Orchestra for a "middle of the road accompaniment" segment, offering a free mini-concert that captivated home audiences. **The Glenn Campbell Goodtime Hour (1969-1971)** : Debuting on January 29, 1969, this show replaced the canceled Smothers Brothers Show. While it featured some comedy that hasn't aged well, its music was its distinguishing factor. The show, with Nelson Riddle as music director, showcased Campbell's country-pop blend and hosted a wide array of artists from various genres. It was eventually canceled in 1971 after four seasons, despite its success, surviving the "rural purge" for about a year.
