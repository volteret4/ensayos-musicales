# artist - The Tennessee 3

## members
- Johnny Cash
- Luther Perkins
- Marshall Grant
- Red Carnotle (initially)

## instruments
- Acoustic guitar
- Double bass
- Electric guitar
- Steel guitar

## curiosities
**Audition for Sam Phillips and Reduction to The Tennessee 3** : Johnny Cash brought Luther Perkins, Marshall Grant, and Red Carnotle to meet Sam Phillips. When it came time to audition, the steel player, Red Carnotle, became so nervous he couldn't tune his guitar and decided to leave, feeling he was holding the others back. The remaining trio became "The Tennessee 3," a name they chose because, though they all lived in Tennessee, none were originally from there. "Eventually he decided that he was holding the other three back, and left the studio, and the audition continued with just the group who had now become the Tennessee 3." ← Episode 37： ＂I Walk The Line＂ by Johnny Cash | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1uhw6KjgSME
**Formation and Early Sound** : The group initially formed as Johnny Cash, Luther Perkins, and Marshall Grant, sometimes including steel player Red Carnotle. They were unskilled but possessed a willingness to experiment and Cash's distinctive voice. They primarily played gospel songs, aiming to sound like The Louvin Brothers. "Cash, Perkins and Grant all played acoustic guitar at first, and none of them were particularly good." ← Episode 37： ＂I Walk The Line＂ by Johnny Cash | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1uhw6KjgSME
**Musical Limitations and Innovative Sound** : Their limitations as musicians forced them to develop a unique sound. Luther Perkins played electric guitar lead lines that were essentially boogie-woogie bass lines transposed up an octave. Marshall Grant, who had never played double bass before, taped markers on his instrument and could only play single notes on every other beat, creating big gaps. These limitations paradoxically led to their signature sound, focusing on rhythm rather than virtuosity.
