**Big Bill Broonzy Influence & Working-Class Identity** : A 16-minute documentary about Big Bill Broonzy's tour of Belgium, "Low Light and Blue Smoke," deeply affected Ray. His father's approval of Broonzy, describing him as a "real man" unlike "high-voiced girly-sounding pop singers," resonated with Ray's respect for older generations' musical tastes. Crucially, Ray felt Broonzy's performance sounded "authentic" and "working class," which aligned with Ray's own working-class background, distinguishing him from "comparatively privileged" contemporaries like John Lennon or Mick Jagger. **Hornsey Art School Experience** : After leaving school, Ray attended Hornsey Art School, where he felt completely "in his element." Despite his working-class background, Ray harbored a desire to "climb the social ladder" and thrived among people interested in art and music. His particular passion at the time was cinema, regularly attending college film society showings of works by directors such as Bergman, Kurosawa, Truffaut, Eisenstein, and Griffith, although he would complain about the one-shilling entry fee. "Ray, on the other hand, was in his element.
