**Untitled Sam Cork Song (Year Not Specified, year before "Stand By Me")** : This song, written by Sam Cork for his old group, was directly inspired by Reverend Charles Albert Tindley's 1905 spiritual. It featured Johnny Taylor on lead vocal and served as the proximate influence for Benny King's "Stand By Me." "The proximity influence for the song was a song that Sam Cork had written for his old group The Soul Sturvers, the year before, which had in turn been inspired by Tindley's song." ← Episode 94： ＂Stand By Me＂, by Ben E. ## curiosities
**Financial Performance as a Gospel Group** : In 1956, the group's record sales were in the "low tens of thousands," which was considered a reasonable amount for a gospel group but insufficient to generate significant wealth for its members. "The soul-sturvers were only selling in the low tens of thousands. A reasonable amount for a gospel group but hardly the kinds of numbers that would make anyone rich." ← Episode Sixty： ＂You Send Me＂ by Sam Cooke | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=92tD5qJR-yY
**Founding and Early Style (1926)** : The Soul Stirrers originated in 1926, performing in the "Jubilee Style," a manner of singing spiritual music common among black singers before Thomas Dorsey's revolution in gospel. "The Soul Sturlers had started out in 1926 as a group in what was called The Jubilee Style." ← Episode Sixty： ＂You Send Me＂ by Sam Cooke | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=92tD5qJR-yY
**Live Performance Popularity (1955)** : The Soul Stirrers were notably more popular as a live act than as a recording act, a characteristic evident in a 1955 live recording produced by Bumps Blackwell. "The soul-sturvers were more popular as a live-act than as a recording act, and hearing the live recording of them that Bumps Blackwell produced in 1955, it's easy to see why." ← Episode Sixty： ＂You Send Me＂ by Sam Cooke | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=92tD5qJR-yY
**Transition to New Gospel Style** : Following Thomas Dorsey's innovation in gospel music, The Soul Stirrers successfully adapted their style, becoming one of the first and most acclaimed gospel quartets in this new format. "The Soul Sturlers switched styles and became one of the first and best gospel quartets in the new style." ← Episode Sixty： ＂You Send Me＂ by Sam Cooke | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=92tD5qJR-yY
