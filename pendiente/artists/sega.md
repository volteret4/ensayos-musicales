## curiosities
**Dreamcast Startup Sound by Ryuichi Sakamoto (1998)** : For the Dreamcast console, Sega commissioned renowned electronic musician and Oscar winner Ryuichi Sakamoto to compose its startup sound. Sakamoto created a "crystalline, aquatic, and full of light" sound that aimed to transmit immense hope for the future. Paradoxically, the Dreamcast became Sega's "swan song" in hardware, eventually being outcompeted by PlayStation 2, GameCube, and Xbox. "Para Dreamcast, se gaga contra todo nada más, y nada menos que a la llorado Richie saca moto. Y en el ánimo de la música electrónica con Jeloma y Corquestra y ganador de un Oscar para componer su sonido de arranque." ← Por Qué Tu Cerebro Nunca Olvidará Este Sonido | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=86tqZ33gn_4
**Megadrive Startup Sound (Early 90s)** : The famous "Sega!" intro sound, which became synonymous with the Megadrive console, was not integrated into the console's hardware but rather embedded within the first Sonic game. This strategic decision was effective for branding, as nearly every Megadrive owner possessed the game, making the two-second audio, consisting of a digitalized and compressed chorus of voices, a de facto console boot sound that consumed a significant portion of the game's memory. "Esta famosísima intro de Megadrive. ¡Sica!
