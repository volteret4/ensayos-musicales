**Unnamed First Song** : Bart's first sold song, purchased by Bill Cutton for 25 guineas, was a lament about old-style cafes being replaced by upscale coffee bars, with lyrics quoted as: "Oh, for a cup of tea, instead of a cup of chini, what would it mean to me, just one little cup so teeny? You ask for some tea and they reckon your barmy, ask for a banger they'll give you some army, oh for the liquid they served in the army, just a cup of tea." "No recording of that song seems to exist, but the lyrics to the song are lament about the way that old-style cafes were being replaced by upscale coffee bars." ← Episode 48： ＂Rock With the Caveman＂ by Tommy Steele | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=scDLm48u2yc

## curiosities
**Bohemian Soho Figure** : Lionel Bart was at the center of a bohemian crowd in Soho, living in a squat that also housed other notable artists like Jim Antrobus, Shirley Eaton, and David Graham. "Bart was at the centre of a bohemian crowd in Soho, and the party was held at a squat where Bart, a card-carrying member of the Communist party, spent most of his time." ← Episode 48： ＂Rock With the Caveman＂ by Tommy Steele | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=scDLm48u2yc
**Brief Member of The Cavemen** : Bart briefly performed with Thomas Hicks and Mike Pratt as The Cavemen, playing washboard, but soon tired of performing and focused on songwriting.
