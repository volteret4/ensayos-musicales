# artist - John Williams

## members
- London Symphony Orchestra (as conductor)

## albums
**Star Wars (1977) - Original Motion Picture Soundtrack** : Composed and conducted by John Williams and performed by the London Symphony Orchestra, this soundtrack was a huge seller in 1977, competing with Fleetwood Mac's "Rumours." Despite being a double record set of instrumental orchestral music, its popularity stemmed from its quality and its function as a souvenir of the beloved movie. It won an Academy Award. ## songs
**Theme from Star Wars (1977)** : This majestic and exciting orchestral theme is instantly recognizable. A disco version of the theme, favored by radio in the 1970s, spent two weeks at the top of the Billboard Hot 100 chart in 1977. The original score, part of the Academy Award-winning soundtrack, helped reclaim space for orchestral music in film. "The theme from Star Wars is majestic and it's exciting like the movie. **Inspiration to Musicians** : The "Star Wars" soundtrack was so impactful that, according to critic Alex Ross in 2016, it inspired some members of the London Symphony to learn to play their instruments. Composer Andrew Norman decided to learn to write music after watching "Star Wars" on video.
