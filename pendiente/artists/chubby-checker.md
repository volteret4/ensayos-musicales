**The Twist (1960)** : This version of "The Twist" went to number one on the Billboard Pop Chart as a result of Dick Clark's constant plugging on his TV shows. It was so similar to Hank Ballard's original version that Ballard initially believed it was his own record. Key differences included Checker's drummer playing more on the cymbals and his saxophone player playing all the way through the song, rather than just a solo. The record re-entered the charts 18 months after its initial release, returning to number one, making it the only non-Christmas themed record to achieve this feat in American history. As recently as 2018, Billboard listed it as the all-time number one record on the Hot 100. "It was so close to Ballard's version that when Ballard first heard it on the radio, he was convinced it was his own record." ← Episode 91： ＂The Twist＂ by Chubby Checker | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LzsS8EXdsm8

## curiosities
**Bitterness and Perceived Erasure from History** : In recent decades, Chubby Checker has expressed significant bitterness and felt overlooked regarding his place in rock and roll history, despite his massive commercial success. He is convinced there is an attempt to erase him from the genre's narrative and believes he deserves the same prominence as figures like Elvis Presley and The Beatles. "Recent interviews show that Checker is convinced he is the victim of an attempt to erase him from rock and roll history, and believes he deserves equal prominence with Elvis and the Beatles." ← Episode 91： ＂The Twist＂ by Chubby Checker | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LzsS8EXdsm8
**Claims of Racism and Hall of Fame Omission** : Checker attributes his lack of recognition, particularly his non-induction into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, to racism. He specifically points to his marriage to a white woman as a contributing factor. He has publicly protested outside the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame regarding this perceived oversight. "He believes his lack of recognition is down to racism, as he married a white woman and has protested outside the rock and roll whole of fame at his lack of induction." ← Episode 91： ＂The Twist＂ by Chubby Checker | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LzsS8EXdsm8
**Dick Clark's Christmas Card Recording** : Before his success with "The Twist," Ernest Evans recorded an audio Christmas card for Dick Clark's friends. This private recording featured Evans singing jingle bells while performing impressions of various singers, including Fats Domino, Elvis Presley, and The Chipmunks. A later public re-recording as a duet with Bobby Rydell included these same impressions. "Evan said told the people at Cameo Parkway he could do impressions of different singers, and so they'd ask him to record it." ← Episode 91： ＂The Twist＂ by Chubby Checker | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LzsS8EXdsm8
**Dick Clark's Promotion for "The Twist"** : Dick Clark deliberately chose Chubby Checker to remake "The Twist" instead of Hank Ballard. Clark sought a performer who was known to him, "unthreatening" (which is generally considered to have meant "less obviously black"), younger, not associated with an "adult act," and with whom Clark might have financial connections. Checker's ability to do impressions and perform in the style of Ballard made him an easy choice. Clark then ensured Checker performed a toned-down, television-appropriate version of the dance on his shows. "So when Clark wanted a record that sounded exactly like Hank Ballard in the Midnighters singing The Twist, it was easy enough for Chacker to do a Ballard in Fashion." ← Episode 91： ＂The Twist＂ by Chubby Checker | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LzsS8EXdsm8

**Marriage to Catherine Allodders** : In 1964, Chubby Checker married Catherine Allodders, who had been crowned Miss World 1962. Their marriage has proven remarkably enduring, lasting for nearly 60 years to the present day, which is considered unusual for a union between a rock star and a beauty queen. **Stage Name Origin** : Ernest Evans was given his stage name, Chubby Checa, by Dick Clark's wife, Barbara. This name was a playful reference to Evans' impression of Fats Domino, combining "Chubby" (like "Fats") with "Checa" (a twist on "Domino"). "Dick Clark's wife Barbara was there when he was doing the recording, and she called him Chubby Checa as a play on Fat Stomano." ← Episode 91： ＂The Twist＂ by Chubby Checker | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LzsS8EXdsm8
**Unavailable Recordings and Lost Royalties** : For a prolonged period, Chubby Checker's most successful records were legally unavailable. After Cameo Parkway was acquired by music industry executive Allen Klein in the late 1960s (reportedly as a tax write-off), the label's recordings went out of print and were not reissued on CD between 1975 and 2005. This meant Checker did not receive royalties he would have otherwise earned from 30 years of nostalgia compilation albums.
