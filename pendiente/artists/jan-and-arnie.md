# artist - Jan and Arnie

## members
- Arnie Ginsburg
- Jan Berry

## labels
- Arwin Records

## songs
**Jenny Lee (Year Unknown)** : This song was originally recorded as a demo by The Debarrens about a local stripper. According to Jan Berry, he and Arnie Ginsburg re-recorded it due to issues with the original tape. Joe Lubin, Vice President of Arwin Records, heard an acetate of this re-recording and decided to release it, overdubbing extra instruments. The single became a significant hit, reaching number three on the charts, placing them just behind The Elderly Brothers at number one and Shab Wully (with "The Purple People Eater") at number two. Dean Torrance, in army basic training, was surprised to hear the song credited to Jan and Arnie and believed he could still hear his own voice on the track. "At one point, the elderly brothers were at number one in the charts. ## curiosities
**Dean Torrance's Army Enlistment** : Dean Torrance, originally part of The Debarrens, enlisted in the army reserves for six months of basic training to avoid a longer draft. While in basic training, he heard "Jenny Lee" on the radio, credited to Jan and Arnie, and was convinced he could hear his own voice, leading him to regret his military choice. "And Dean Torrance was off in the army, regretting his choices." ← Episode 86： ＂LSD-25＂ by the Gamblers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UNd6UwbL1rA
**Discovery by Joe Lubin** : After Jan Berry and Arnie Ginsburg re-recorded "Jenny Lee," Jan took an acetate copy to a friend's party. Joe Lubin, Vice President of Arwin Records (owned by Marty Melcher, Doris Day's husband), happened to hear it from another room while Jan was having the acetate cut. Lubin was impressed and promised to make Jan and Arnie "bigger than the Elderly Brothers," eventually overdubbing instruments and releasing the track.
