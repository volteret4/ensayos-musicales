# artist - Macintosh Plus

## albums
**Floral Shoppe (2011)** : Released just a year after Daniel Lopatin's pioneering work, Floral Shoppe became arguably the most popular release within the vaporwave genre. It transcended its musical origins to become a significant internet meme and defined the recognizable aesthetic of vaporwave for a wide audience. **Floral Shoppe (2011) - Diana Ross Transformation** : The opening track of Floral Shoppe is a slowed and partially manipulated version of Diana Ross's "It's Your Move." By slowing down Ross's originally optimistic and danceable song, Macintosh Plus transformed it into something nostalgic, melancholic, and even ghostly, demonstrating how tempo and pitch changes profoundly alter a piece's emotional impact. **Floral Shoppe (2011) - Influence and Example** : Compared to Lopatin's more heavily fragmented work, Floral Shoppe featured less extensively chopped and manipulated sounds. The album's famous second track serves as a prime example of how manipulating speed can imbue an existing song with entirely new meaning and emotional resonance.
