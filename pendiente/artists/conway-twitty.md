## curiosities
**Later Identity of Harold Jenkins** : Conway Twitty is the stage name adopted by Harold Jenkins, under which he achieved success as a country star and his earlier Sun recordings were eventually released. "That song was originally written by a young singer called Harold Jenkins, who was making recordings for sonat at the time, though the recordings didn't get released until after Jenkins became a country star under the name Conway Twitty." ← Episode 42： ＂Ooby Dooby＂ by Roy Orbison and the Teen Kings | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1zvzpXqr-k8
**Unreleased Sun Recordings** : Conway Twitty had numerous unreleased songs stored in Sun Records' vaults. Shelby Singleton, upon acquiring Sun, ensured that pretty much every scrap of material from these vaults, including Twitty's, saw release. "In their four-year Rocket Billy Hayday, Sam Phillips and Jack Clements had recorded literally thousands of unreleased songs by Jerry Lee Lewis, Johnny Cash, Roy Orbison, Karl Perkins, Conway Twitty, Charlie Rich, Billy Lee Riley, and more." ← Episode 66： ＂Great Balls of Fire＂ by Jerry Lee Lewis | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CVp5e4EWCqI
