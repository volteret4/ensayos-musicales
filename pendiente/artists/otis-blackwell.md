## albums
**These Are My Songs (1970s)** : Otis Blackwell recorded this album in the 1970s, featuring many of the hit songs he had written for other artists. The version of "Great Balls of Fire" on this album is considered a potential indicator of what his impressive demo for Sam Phillips sounded like. "We don't have access to his demo of Great Balls of Fire, but in the 70s he recorded an album called These Are My Songs, featuring many of the hits he'd written for other people, and it's possible that the version of Great Balls of Fire on that album gives some idea of what the demo that so impressed Philips sounded like." ← Episode 66： ＂Great Balls of Fire＂ by Jerry Lee Lewis | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CVp5e4EWCqI

## curiosities
**Co-Credit for Elvis** : Otis Blackwell was credited as co-writer for "Don't Be Cruel" alongside Elvis Presley, at the insistence of Colonel Tom Parker, who wanted Elvis to "get a cut for making it a hit." "Don't be cruel, a song written by Otis Blackwell, but credited to Blackwell and Presley, as the Colonel insisted that his boy get a cut for making it a hit." ← Episode 38： ＂Heartbreak Hotel＂ by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ES35PYeAwfs
**Professional Songwriter** : Otis Blackwell was a New York-based songwriter, one of a relatively small number of black people who successfully secured work as a professional songwriter for major publishing companies. "He was a New York-based songwriter, one of a relatively small number of black people, who managed to get work as a professional songwriter for one of the big publishing companies." ← Episode 66： ＂Great Balls of Fire＂ by Jerry Lee Lewis | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CVp5e4EWCqI
**Superstition about Meeting Elvis** : Otis Blackwell intentionally never met Elvis Presley, even declining an opportunity, because he developed a superstition about meeting the artist who had given him his biggest hits. "The one time he did have a chance to meet him, he declined, as he developed a superstition about meeting the man who'd given him his biggest hits." ← Episode 38： ＂Heartbreak Hotel＂ by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ES35PYeAwfs
