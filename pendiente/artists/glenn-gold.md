## albums
**Goldberg Variations (1955)** : Glenn Gold's inaugural recording for CBS Masterworks, chosen against the label's initial hesitations due to Baroque music typically being overlooked by the public. Despite these concerns, the album broke records and surpassed Elizabeth Tron, relegating her to second place, which marked a significant shift in classical music. Gold's interpretation was noted for its unique precision and energy, meticulously dissecting every detail to reveal its internal structure. He delivered a careful and clear rendition, ensuring each variation sounded independent while highlighting multiple voices and rhythms. His unconventional rhythms and speeds imbued the piece with a fresh and vibrant character. "Contra todo pronóstico, el disco batió recorz y relegó a Elizabeth Tron al segundo lugar." ← El Pianista que Llevó a Bach al Cosmos ｜ Glenn Gould | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WpwjSQr8Qco
**Goldberg Variations (1981)** : A later rendition of the Goldberg Variations recorded by Glenn Gold, this time employing digital technology and with Gold exerting precise control over every technical aspect of the recording process. This interpretation was recognized as more introspective and mature compared to his initial 1955 recording, serving as a profound testament to his musical vision and relentless pursuit of perfection. This album was released one year before his passing in 1982. "Fue una interpretación más introspectiva y madura en comparación con su primera grabación de 1955." ← El Pianista que Llevó a Bach al Cosmos ｜ Glenn Gould | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WpwjSQr8Qco

## songs
**El Clave bien Temperado (Johann Sebastian Bach)** : An interpretation by Glenn Gold that was selected for inclusion on the "Golden Record," an interstellar message carried aboard the Voyager One space probe. This specific recording, alongside a collection of sounds and images from Earth, was designed to convey the cultural richness of our planet to any potential extraterrestrial civilization. The Voyager One probe, still carrying this recording, passed the heliopause in 2012, becoming the first human-made object to venture into interstellar space, thereby realizing Gold's "cosmic vision of music" with the vastness of space and its stars as its only audience. "Abordo, sigue llevando consigo su interpretación del clave bien temperado de Bach, haciendo si de realidad su visión cósmica de la música y sin más público que las estrellas." ← El Pianista que Llevó a Bach al Cosmos ｜ Glenn Gould | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WpwjSQr8Qco
**Goldberg Variations (Johann Sebastian Bach)** : This monumental work became central to Glenn Gold's career, notably launching him to fame following his 1955 recording. The piece held a deep fascination for Gold, instilled by his teacher Alberto Guerrero. Gold viewed Bach as embodying the fundamental criteria of music, representing a perfect balance between emotion and logic, and considered him the ultimate starting point and source for all subsequent music. His interpretations were renowned for their unique precision, clarity, and ability to expose the internal structure of the composition, making each variation distinct. "La obra que años después le lanzaría a la fama." ← El Pianista que Llevó a Bach al Cosmos ｜ Glenn Gould | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WpwjSQr8Qco

## curiosities
**Anxiety and Self-Medication During Concert Career** : Following 1955, Glenn Gold's career evolved into a "marathon of concerts," which induced severe anxiety. He occasionally canceled recitals at the last moment due to feeling incapable of achieving the perfection he sought and perceiving himself as enslaved by time. As a consequence of this anxiety, he resorted to self-medication, consuming anxiolytics before recitals and a substance called "Brutal" at night while listening to the radio until sleep overcame him. "Su vida se convirtió en un amaratón de conciertos, cosa que lo hacía pasar por terribles ansiedades, en ocasiones anolabra recitales en el último momento por verse incapacitado para la perfección, ya que no podía dejar de ser sublimeto del tiempo, debido a la ansiedad empezó a tomedicarse." ← El Pianista que Llevó a Bach al Cosmos ｜ Glenn Gould | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WpwjSQr8Qco
**Cosmic Vision of Music** : For Glenn Gold, music represented a universal language capable of transcending time, space, and cultures, leading him to develop an almost cosmic vision of its power and reach. This vision was symbolically actualized when his interpretation of Bach's "El Clave bien Temperado" was included on the Golden Record aboard the Voyager One probe, which ventured into interstellar space with "no more audience than the stars." "Para que le engol, la música era una especie de lenguaje universal que trascendía al tiempo, el espacio y las culturas. Tenía una visión casi cósmica de la música." ← El Pianista que Llevó a Bach al Cosmos ｜ Glenn Gould | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WpwjSQr8Qco
**Early Life and Musical Education** : Born in Toronto in 1932, Glenn Gold began playing the piano at the tender age of three, receiving his initial instruction from his mother, who was also a musician. He acquired the ability to read music even before learning to read words. At the age of ten, he enrolled in the conservatory, where he studied for ten years under the tutelage of the esteemed Chilean pianist Alberto Guerrero, who ignited in Gold a profound fascination for the Goldberg Variations—an opus that would subsequently catapult him to international renown. "Glenn Gold nací de un toronto en 1932, empezó a tocar el piano con tres años. Su madre, quien se dedicaba a la música, le enseñó todo lo que sabía." ← El Pianista que Llevó a Bach al Cosmos ｜ Glenn Gould | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WpwjSQr8Qco
**Increasing Isolation and Pursuit of Perfection** : Over time, Glenn Gold became increasingly isolated, experiencing both physical and mental withdrawal. He actively avoided interviews and largely abstained from social engagements, choosing instead to deeply immerse himself in his inner world and his artistic pursuits. He spent extensive hours at home or in the recording studio, environments where he found the absolute control he intensely desired. He evolved into an enigmatic, solitary genius, relentlessly seeking musical perfection from a distance, shielded from the cacophony and distractions of the external world. "Con el tiempo se izló cada vez más, su aislamiento no era solo físico, sino también mental. Evitaba las entrevistas y rechazaba la mayoría de las relaciones sociales, somergiéndose profundamente en su mundo interior y en su arte." ← El Pianista que Llevó a Bach al Cosmos ｜ Glenn Gould | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WpwjSQr8Qco
**Obsession with Detail and Inadvertent Vocalizations** : Gold demonstrated an extreme obsession with detail, which manifested in every note, pause, and movement of his performances. His constant humming, singing, or murmuring during playing often inadvertently permeated his piano recordings, eventually becoming a distinctive personal hallmark recognized by his audience. "Mostraba una obsesión extrema por el detalle, que se transmitían cada nota, cada pausa y cada movimiento. En sus grabaciones, en el micrófono de los pianos se colaba su canto río constante." ← El Pianista que Llevó a Bach al Cosmos ｜ Glenn Gould | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WpwjSQr8Qco
**Posthumous Diagnosis of Asperger's Syndrome** : Two decades after his death, scientific examinations indicated that Glenn Gold likely suffered from Asperger's Syndrome. This theory is widely supported by many individuals who themselves live with the disorder, providing a potential explanation for his distinctive behaviors and intense focus. "20 años después de su muerte, exámenes científicos de agnosticaron que, probablemente, padeciar siendo meras perger, una teoría que muchos que comparten este trastorno también apoyan." ← El Pianista que Llevó a Bach al Cosmos ｜ Glenn Gould | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WpwjSQr8Qco
**Retirement from Live Performance** : At the age of 34, having already achieved international recognition, Glenn Gold announced his retirement from live performing stages to dedicate himself exclusively to recording. He found the recording studio to be a more comfortable environment, where he could control every minute detail and deliver unique interpretations at his own pace, free from the pressures of live audiences and the fear of errors. "A los 34 años, cuando era una figura internacional, anunció que se retiraba de los escenarios para centrarse en la grabación, donde podía controlar cada detalle y ofrecieron interpretación única, allí es donde se sentía más cómodo, ya que podía trabajar en la interpretación con calma, sin preocuparse tanto por errores o por el estrés del público." ← El Pianista que Llevó a Bach al Cosmos ｜ Glenn Gould | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WpwjSQr8Qco
**School Experiences and Departure from Formal Education** : During his time in school, Glenn Gold was subjected to bullying by other children who would strike him, yet he never retaliated, a behavior that only seemed to encourage further torment. At 19, he discontinued his formal studies, having concluded that no teacher could impart any further knowledge to him. "En el colegio los niños le golpeaban y él nunca devolvía a los golpes, cosa que hacía que se ensañase en más con él." ← El Pianista que Llevó a Bach al Cosmos ｜ Glenn Gould | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WpwjSQr8Qco
**Signature Posture and Custom-Built Chair** : Glenn Gold habitually traveled with his small, well-worn chair, which necessitated his characteristic hunched posture, with his nose nearly grazing the piano keys. He was inseparable from this chair, bringing it to every performance and recording session; it had been constructed by his father when Gold was just a child. Audiences were captivated not only by his unique appearance but, more significantly, by his revolutionary approach to playing. "Siempre viajaba con su pequeña y deteriorada silla, que lo hacía tocar con su característica postura encorbada, con las teclas a la altura de la nariz, nunca tocaba sin ella, él hallaba a cada actuación y grabación, te ha construyendo su padre cuando era tan solo un niño." ← El Pianista que Llevó a Bach al Cosmos ｜ Glenn Gould | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WpwjSQr8Qco
**Unique Pre-Performance Rituals and Distinctive Attire** : Glenn Gold was frequently characterized as an "eccentric artist" by the press, a label he personally disavowed. Irrespective of the season, Gold consistently wore an overcoat, one or two scarves, and gloves. Prior to each concert, he would immerse his hands in hot water for 20 minutes, a ritual akin to that of surgeons, reflecting the almost surgical precision he applied to his playing. "Independientemente de si fuese verano o invierno, Gold siempre iba a vestido con su abrigo, una o dos bofandas y guantes. Su mergía durante 20 minutos las manos en agua caliente antes de cada concierto del mismo modo que hacían los cirujanos." ← El Pianista que Llevó a Bach al Cosmos ｜ Glenn Gould | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WpwjSQr8Qco
