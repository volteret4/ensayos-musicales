## curiosities
**Origin of Name** : Formed by Harry Webb, Terry Smart, and Norman Midham, the group initially considered calling themselves "The Planet" after the launch of Sputnik. However, after researching the etymology of "planet" and discovering it came from the Greek for "wanderer" or "Drifter," they adopted the name "The Drifters," unaware of the existence of an American group with the identical name. "Inspired by the recent launch of Sputnik, they thought of calling themselves the planet, but they decided that wasn't quite right, and looked up the etymology of planet and found it came from the Greek for wanderer or Drifter, and so they became the Drifters, unaware there was an American group of the same name." ← Episode 70： ＂Move It＂ by Cliff Richard and the Drifters | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uoEWP94f6S4
**Original Lineup Evolution** : The initial formation included Harry Webb, Terry Smart, and Norman Midham. Ian Sanwell later joined as the lead guitarist. The lineup then evolved to include Ian Sanwell on bass, Terry Smart, Hank Marvin, and Bruce Welch. Eventually, only the name "The Drifters" remained from the original configuration before the band was rebranded as "The Shadows."
