**Influence on Young Singers** : To the younger generation of singers in Chicago, such as Lou Rawls, the Highway QC's were seen as "the group to beat" and a significant aspiration. "On the other hand, to all the younger kids around Chicago, the highway QCs were clearly the group to beat, and people like a young singer named Lou Rolls looked up to them as something to aspire to." ← Episode Sixty： ＂You Send Me＂ by Sam Cooke | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=92tD5qJR-yY
**Mentorship and Betrayal** : R.B. Robinson of The Soul Stirrers mentored the Highway QC's, helping them secure better gigs and convincing them they were "headed for the big time." However, his true intention was to train Sam Cooke to replace R.H. Harris in The Soul Stirrers, leaving the remaining QC's heartbroken when Cooke departed. "But it turned out that behind the scenes that had been trouble in the soul-stirvers." ← Episode Sixty： ＂You Send Me＂ by Sam Cooke | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=92tD5qJR-yY
**Rivalry with The Soul Stirrers** : The Highway QC's considered themselves disciples of The Soul Stirrers but also believed the older group was jealous of them. When the QC's attended Soul Stirrers shows, they were often ignored and only allowed to perform one song if the audience strongly insisted. "When the highway QCs turned up at soul-stirvers shows, though, the soul-stirvers would act as if they didn't know them, and would only invite them onto the stage if the audience absolutely insisted, and would then limit their performance to a single song." ← Episode Sixty： ＂You Send Me＂ by Sam Cooke | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=92tD5qJR-yY
**Sam Cooke's Stardom within the Group** : Even though the other members were decent singers, Sam Cooke was "absolutely, unquestionably, the star" of the Highway QC's, with member Kredel Copeland noting that Sam's captivating voice was their primary claim to fame.
