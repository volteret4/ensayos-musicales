# artist - Booker T and the M.G.'s

## members
- Al Jackson (drums - original)
- Booker T. Jones (organ)
- Duck Dunn (bass - replacement)
- Isaac Hayes (keyboards - interim)
- Louis Steinberg (bass - original)
- Steve Cropper (guitar)

## genres
- R&B
- Soul

## labels
- Bolt Records
- Stax Records

## songs
**Green Onions (1962)** : Born from an impromptu jam session, this instrumental became the biggest hit Stax had ever recorded, reaching number one on the R&B charts and number three on the pop charts. Its title was suggested by bassist Louis Steinberg. "Green onions went to number one on the R&B charts, and number three on the pop charts, and became the biggest thing stacks it ever recorded." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0

## curiosities
**Accidental Formation and "Green Onions"** : The group formed by accident during a cancelled Billy Lee Riley session at Stax. The four musicians—Steve Cropper, Booker T. Jones, Louis Steinberg, and Al Jackson—were "noodling on some blues" when Jim Stewart heard them and told them to record it, leading to the creation of "Green Onions." "The record that would prove stacks to be capable of doing great things without Chip's Momen came about by accident." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Bass Player Change (Duck Dunn)** : Louis Steinberg was replaced by Duck Dunn as the bass player for Booker T and the M.G.'s after the band had achieved several hits. While Steinberg claimed racial motivation, Steve Cropper stated it was due to Dunn's "hard-eared style" better fitting their music and his desire to play more with his best friend. "The other change came a couple of years later, when after the MG's had had a few hits. Louis Steinberg was replaced by Duck Dunn." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Becoming the Stax House Band** : After the massive success of "Green Onions," Booker T and the M.G.'s became the official Stax house band, playing on virtually every session. They recorded their own instrumentals as Booker T and the M.G.'s, backed horn players as The Mar-Keys, and provided backing for all the singers who came through the Stax doors. "That Curg group became the stacks house band, playing on every session from that point on." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Foundation of Stax Records** : Regardless of the specific lineup changes, Booker T and the M.G.'s were the fundamental "foundation of Stax's records for the rest of the 60's," not only producing instrumental hits but also providing backing for numerous legendary vocalists. "But whether with Jones or Hayes, Steinberg or Dunn, the MG's would be the foundation of Stax's records for the rest of the 60's" ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Name Origin and Trademark Avoidance** : The name Booker T and the M.G.'s was chosen, with M.G.'s originally referring to the MG sports car, similar to The Triumphs (Chips Moman's car). They later provided alternative explanations, "Memphis Group" or "Mixed Group" (referencing their black and white members), to avoid a trademark lawsuit. "They later said that MG's stood either from Memphis group, or for mixed group because they had both black and white members, but the original idea was definitely the car." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Temporary Keyboardist (Isaac Hayes)** : When Booker T. Jones went to university after "Green Onions," Isaac Hayes filled in as the weekday keyboardist for a couple of years, ensuring continuity for the Stax house band.
