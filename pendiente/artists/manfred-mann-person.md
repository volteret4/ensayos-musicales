**Early London Life and Pseudonym** : After moving to England, he started playing jazz keyboards and giving piano lessons. He also earned money by writing record reviews, for which he adopted the pseudonym "Manfred Man" (initially spelled with a 't' on the end, which he later dropped), taken from jazz drummer Shelley Man. **Emigration to the UK (June 1961)** : Desperate to leave South Africa, Lubawitz seized an opportunity in June 1961 when he was still a Commonwealth citizen, just before South Africa lost its Commonwealth membership and its citizens' automatic right to immigrate to the UK. He was part of a year's grace period for this change.
