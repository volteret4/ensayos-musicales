## curiosities
**Early Collaboration with Hawkins** : DJ Fontana, a cousin of Stan Lewis and a musician who played on the Louisiana Hayride, played with Dale Hawkins for a period before accepting a more lucrative position with Elvis Presley. "Lewis took an interest in his young employees' music career, and introduced Hawkins to his cousin, DJ Fontana, another musician who played on the Louisiana Hayride." ← Episode 63： ＂Susie Q＂, by Dale Hawkins | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4vqj1DcXOfw
**Excluded from Film Soundtrack** : DJ Fontana was not allowed to play on the soundtrack for Elvis Presley's first film, "Doreen O'Brothers" (later "Love Me Tender"), as the film company replaced Elvis's regular band with Hollywood session musicians. "he wasn't allowed to use Scotty Bill and DJ on the soundtrack, because the film company didn't think they could sound hillbilly enough." ← Episode 38： ＂Heartbreak Hotel＂ by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ES35PYeAwfs
**First Chance to Shine on "My Baby Left Me" (1956)** : The B-side track "My Baby Left Me" from the "I Want You, I Need You, I Love You" single gave DJ Fontana his "first chance to shine" and showcase his drumming. "this one also gave DJ Fontana his first chance to shine." ← Episode 38： ＂Heartbreak Hotel＂ by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ES35PYeAwfs
**First Recording Session with Elvis (1956)** : DJ Fontana, who joined Elvis Presley's band on drums in 1955, made his first recording appearance on the "Heartbreak Hotel" session for RCA Records. "DJ Fontana, who had joined the band on drums in 1955, and was recording for the first time." ← Episode 38： ＂Heartbreak Hotel＂ by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ES35PYeAwfs
**Played on Classic Rock and Roll Records** : DJ Fontana was one of the musicians who had played on classic rock and roll records and later became a member of The Sun Session Band. "A band made up of many of the musicians who had played on classic rock and roll records, including Stan Kessler, Jimmy Van Eaten, Sonny Burgess and DJ Fantamer." ← Episode 44： ＂Train Kept A-Rollin'＂, by Johnny Burnette and the Rock 'n' Roll Trio | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u3zYAJ2qeR4
**Refusal to Resign from Elvis's Band (1957)** : Unlike Scotty Moore and Bill Black, DJ Fontana chose not to sign the resignation letter from Elvis's band in September 1957. Having joined the group later, he had not experienced the same perceived loss of standing or financial grievances that prompted the others' departure. "DJ Fontana wouldn't sign the letter." ← Episode 72： ＂Trouble＂ by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=psIgGqkQhZI
