## curiosities
**"I'll Be Home for Christmas" Copyright Controversy** : Ram independently wrote "I'll Be Home for Christmas" in memory and registered its copyright in December 1942. He was shocked when Walter Kent and Kim Gannon released a strongly similar song, recorded by Bing Crosby, a few months later. His publishers, Mills Music, successfully sued to get Ram credited on future releases. "The story of Al Behon for Christmas was a rather controversial one." ← Episode 31： ＂Only You＂ by the Platters | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ojUNoetEX-c
**Control of Five Platters Inc.** : Book Ram was the controlling force behind "Five Platters Inc.," the corporation he established shortly after The Platters signed to Mercury, which held the rights to the band's name and was instrumental in the subsequent legal battles.
