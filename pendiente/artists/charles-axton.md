# artist - Charles Axton

## instruments
- Tenor sax

## curiosities
**Controversial Nickname** : Charles Axton, Estelle Axton's son and Jim Stewart's nephew, was known by a nickname that, while harmless in 1950s US, later became an offensive racist slur in the UK against people of Pakistani descent. For historical accuracy, the transcript refers to him as "Charles." "Normally, I refer to people by the name they chose to go by, but in his case he was known by a nickname which was harmless in that time and place, but later became an extremely offensive racist slur in the UK, used against people of Pakistani descent." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Joining The Royal Spades** : Charles Axton, despite only having played tenor saxophone for a couple of weeks, managed to join The Royal Spades by leveraging his family's ownership of a record label. He also suggested expanding the guitar-based drum band with a horn section. "They'd laughed when he told them that he'd only been playing a couple of weeks, but once he explained that his mother and uncle owned a record label, he was in the group" ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Leadership Conflict and Tour Behavior** : Charles Axton believed himself to be a co-leader of The Mar-Keys, leading to conflicts with Steve Cropper. During the tour to promote "Last Night," Charles and the other teenage band members, many away from home for the first time, behaved like "wild animals," contributing to Cropper's decision to quit the tour. "Kropper and Charles Axton both believed themselves to be the band's leader, and that the other should obey them." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Songwriting Credit for "Last Night"** : Charles Axton received songwriting credit for The Mar-Keys' hit "Last Night" primarily because his family owned the record label, even though his actual contribution to the song was limited to playing on the record. "And Charles Axton didn't contribute anything to the song other than playing on the record, but because his family owned the record label, he got credit as well." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Studio Refurbishment** : Charles Axton contributed to the conversion of the old cinema on East Maclamore Avenue into the new Stax recording studio. He, along with Steve Cropper, assisted with the refurbishment efforts.
