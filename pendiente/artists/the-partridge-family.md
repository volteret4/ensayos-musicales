## songs
**I Think I Love You (1970)** : This song, despite David Cassidy's initial resistance to bubblegum, shot to number one, largely fueled by the popularity of *The Partridge Family* television show. The show debuted on September 25, 1970, and the song hit number one on November 21, 1970, famously knocking The Jackson 5's "I'll Be There" from the top spot. "Pretty sure the TV show the Partridge family had a lot to do with that. The show debuted on September 25th 1970 and then I think I love you. Hit number one November 21st..." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 30 - The Sweet Sound of 70s Bubblegum Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/030-bubblegum

## curiosities
**Marketing of Teen Idols** : The show was a vehicle for marketing teen idols, with its music and stars, particularly David Cassidy, becoming a significant part of the bubblegum pop phenomenon and its associated merchandise sales. "I mean, after all, you can't put the arches on tour and think of the merchandise sales too. So that was David Cassidy's fate as well as the fate of other teen idols who made music that fits into the bubble gum category." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 30 - The Sweet Sound of 70s Bubblegum Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/030-bubblegum
**Television Sitcom Origins** : *The Partridge Family* was a TV sitcom that debuted on September 25, 1970, and featured David Cassidy playing 16-year-old Keith Partridge alongside his real stepmother, Shirley Jones. The show's producers had previously worked on The Monkees and initially intended to dub in all the music until they discovered David Cassidy could sing.
