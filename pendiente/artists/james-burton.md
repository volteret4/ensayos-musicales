## curiosities
**"Suzy Q" Guitar Riff Architect** : James Burton was the guitarist who created the distinctive, biting guitar riff for Dale Hawkins' "Suzy Q," playing on both the demo and the final released version. Despite his crucial contribution, he controversially received no songwriting credit. **Post-Hawkins Session Career** : After leaving Dale Hawkins' band, James Burton went on to work with Louisiana musician Bob Luman, then Ricky Nelson, and became a highly sought-after session player, contributing to recordings for artists ranging from The Monkees to Elvis Presley. **Reunion with Hawkins** : Burton reunited with Dale Hawkins in the late 1960s to play guitar on Hawkins' Swamp Rock-style album. This comeback was prompted by Creedence Clearwater Revival's successful cover of "Suzy Q."
