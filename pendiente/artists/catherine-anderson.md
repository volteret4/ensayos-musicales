## curiosities
**Attempt to Regain Group Name and Death** : Catherine Anderson (Jean Caffin) was actively pursuing a lawsuit under the Truth in Music Act to regain ownership of The Marvelettes' name, which Motown had sold off, when she died of a stroke in 2011. This legal action highlighted the struggle of original members to control their legacy. **Exclusion from Motown Anniversaries** : Despite her contributions, Catherine Anderson was not invited to any of Motown's major anniversary celebrations, a consistent oversight for the original members of the label's first number one group. **Group Role and Financial Management** : Within The Marvelettes, Catherine Anderson served as the group's spokesperson for interviews and was responsible for managing their money, indicating a practical and articulate role within the ensemble. **Refusal for Album Cover Appearance** : When Motown sought to feature her on the cover photograph for Wonder Young's 1970 solo album "The Return of The Marvelettes," Catherine Anderson refused. She stated that if she wasn't considered good enough to sing on the record, she wouldn't appear on its cover.
