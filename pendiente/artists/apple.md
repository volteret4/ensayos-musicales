**Compromised Audio Processing in iPhones** : Even high-end iPhones, costing upwards of 1000 euros, are noted for having a "disappointing" sound processing chip. This reflects Apple's historical design philosophy for its portable devices, prioritizing features like screen, processor, and battery over dedicated audio quality. This approach, inherited from the early iPods, has set a standard in the portable electronics market where sound processing is not a primary concern for manufacturers. **iPod - Design Philosophy Shift** : The Apple iPod, while bringing fantastic innovations, introduced design flaws and conceptual problems that continue to affect portable devices like smartphones today. Although inspired by the Creative Nomad Jukebox, the iPod was not the first MP3 player with a hard drive. Apple's version prioritized making the device smaller, lighter, with better battery life, and more user-friendly. This came at the cost of audio quality, as its sound processing chip was significantly less powerful than that of the Nomad Jukebox. Apple's focus on portability and affordability over pristine audio fidelity became the dominant philosophy, shaping how sound is processed in most portable gadgets today, including modern iPhones, where even expensive models often feature disappointing audio processing chips.
