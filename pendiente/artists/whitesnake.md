## songs
**Here I Go Again (1982/1987)** : This song had two distinct versions, reflecting the band's evolving sound and image. The 1982 version from *Saints & Sinners* featured a blues influence, with David Coverdale sporting long brown hair and the band dressed in jeans and leather jackets. By 1987, the version on the *Whitesnake* album showcased Coverdale with blonde hair and the band in leather pants, with almost all traces of the blues "erased," replaced by a heavier sound influenced by Def Leppard. The 1987 version reached number one on the Billboard Top 40 in October 1987, significantly boosted by its MTV video featuring Tony Catane. "Here I go again, that is from White Snake's seventh album, simply titled White Snake. The sound is heavier, definitely reflects more of a death leopard influence than a muddy waters influence. ## curiosities
**Blues Influences and Departure** : Even at the peak of Whitesnake's hair metal success, David Coverdale maintained that his primary influence was the blues, identifying as a "true British rocker" who came of age in the 1970s. However, as the band adapted to the hair metal trend, their sound evolved, and by the 1987 version of "Here I Go Again," "almost any trace of the blues is erased," with a sound that reflected Def Leppard more than Muddy Waters. **Image Adaptation for Hair Metal Trend** : David Coverdale, at 36, acknowledged the importance of image, stating, "I've come to grips with the fact that image is an important thing." He consciously adapted his look for the hair metal wave, transforming his "pretty bland" long brown hair to blonde and using mousse, while the band switched from leather jackets to leather pants. This visual transformation paralleled a musical shift, where almost all traces of their blues influence were "erased" in favor of a heavier, more pop-oriented sound.
