# artist - Billy Ward and his Dominoes

## members
- Billy Ward (leader, vocal coach)
- Clyde McPhatter (lead singer)
- Jackie Wilson (lead singer)

## genres
- Pop
- R&B

## labels
- Decca Records
- King Records

## concerts
- Las Vegas residency (supporting Louis Prima in Keely Smith)

## curiosities

**Discipline from Ward** : Billy Ward taught Jackie Wilson breath control and provided him with much-needed discipline, becoming a father figure. **Focus on Live Performances** : The group's strategy was not solely to succeed as recording artists but also as live performers, securing a lucrative two-year residency in Las Vegas. "The group weren't just looking to have success as recording artists, but as live performers." ← Episode Sixty-Four： ＂Reet Petite＂ by Jackie Wilson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BYU-H4NvnmE
**Jackie Wilson's Departure** : Jackie Wilson decided to leave the group in 1957 because it became apparent that Billy Ward's ambitions extended no further than continuing to play Las Vegas indefinitely, earning vast amounts of money without hard work or travel, while Wilson desired more from his career.
