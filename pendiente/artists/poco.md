## songs
**Crazy Love (1978)** : Released in November 1978 from the album "Legend," this song became Poco's first major hit, reaching number one on the Billboard Adult Contemporary charts for seven weeks and number seventeen on the Billboard Hot 100, marking their first top forty hit. It was released during a period when new singles were typically avoided due to Christmas music programming, which provided an open lane for its success on the Adult Contemporary chart. "It was crazy love that saved polko in nineteen seventy eight and finally gave them the hit that they never had." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 35 - Cosmic American Music - 70s Country Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-35-cosmic-american-music-70s-country-rock
**Heart of the Night (1979)** : Poco's next hit after "Crazy Love," which leaned more into the soft rock genre, reaching number twenty on the Billboard Hot 100 a year after "Crazy Love." **Country Rock Influence** : Poco's influence on country rock is noted as similar to that of Gram Parsons, with their musical DNA present in the genre, even if they did not achieve the same level of fame as other bands. Pure Prairie League specifically stated they wanted to emulate Poco's sound after listening to them.
