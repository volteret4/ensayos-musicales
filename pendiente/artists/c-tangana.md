## curiosities
**Advocate for Business and Industry Integration** : C. Tangana represented a faction of trap artists who advocated for integrating with the music industry, securing funding, and changing the game from within, contrasting with the street-oriented, anti-establishment stance of other artists. "Otro, defendiendo la postura del negocio, la de coger el dinero y cambiar el juego desde dentro." ← La salvaje historia del TRAP ESPAÑOL (2013-2026) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4hBhiU5jj6s
**Artistic and Commercial Differences at Primavera Sound 2018** : At the Primavera Sound festival in 2018, C. Tangana participated in a discussion with John Biff and Bad Gyal. This debate highlighted significant artistic, commercial, and ideological differences between C. Tangana (representing Madrid's more business-minded approach) and John Biff (representing Granada's street-oriented trap). "se sentaron en una charla John Bif, se tangana y batial. Y se dejaron patentes las diferencias de criterio artístico, comercial y diológico ante el madrileño y el gran adino." ← La salvaje historia del TRAP ESPAÑOL (2013-2026) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4hBhiU5jj6s
**Beef with PXXR GANG** : C. Tangana initiated a beef with PXXR GANG through his song "No te pegarás," where he subtly criticized their perceived repetitiveness. This prompted a swift video response from K.D.K.in. "C tangana ubs caíde caín. **Founder of Agorazein (as Crema)** : C. Tangana, originally known as Crema, founded the collective Agorazein. He initially aimed to dismantle the promises of rap but later pivoted to experiment with new sounds, Auto-Tune, and a more pop and commercial aesthetic. "su fundador, Zetangana, que venía a deserrar a las promesas del rap bajo el nombre de crema, dio un volantazo y empezó a coquetear con estos nuevos sonidos, con el autotune y con una estética más pop y comercial." ← La salvaje historia del TRAP ESPAÑOL (2013-2026) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4hBhiU5jj6s
**Shift to Pop and Commercial Aesthetic** : Around 2015, C. Tangana, as the founder of Agorazein, made a significant shift in his musical direction. He began to flirt with new sounds, incorporating Auto-Tune and adopting a more pop and commercial aesthetic, moving away from his earlier rap promises.
