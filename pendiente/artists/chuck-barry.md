# artist - Chuck Barry

## songs
**Havana Moon** : This song, the B-side to "You Can't Catch Me," served as a direct model for Richard Berry's "Louie Louie." Richard Berry borrowed its vocal melody, its political theme of waiting for a ship and missing a girl, and its imitation of Caribbean speech patterns like "Mi Stand and Wait for Boat to Come." "Havana Moon by Chuck Barry was the best side to you can't catch me, and Richard Barry took its vocal melody, his political theme of someone drinking while waiting for a ship to arrive, and missing a girl who the narrator will see at the end of the boat journey, and its attempt at imitating Caribbean speech patterns by saying things like, Mi Stand and Wait for Boat to Come." ← Episode 106：＂Louie Louie＂ by the Kingsmen | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S3agGJsxjpE
