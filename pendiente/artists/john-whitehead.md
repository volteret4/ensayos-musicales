# artist - John Whitehead

## labels
- Philadelphia International Records

## songs
**Ain't No Stoppin' Us Now (1979)** : Co-written and performed by John Whitehead and Gene McFadden, this song marked Philadelphia International Records' last major hit of the 1970s and a notable dance record. Whitehead explicitly stated that the song served as a "declaration of our independence from Gamble," challenging his views on their artistic roles and the company's direction. ## curiosities
**Discontent at PIR** : John Whitehead and Gene McFadden became increasingly unhappy with their situation at Philadelphia International Records, specifically with Kenny Gamble's creative control and his insistence that they primarily serve as writers, ultimately leading to their desire for artistic independence. "Some writers became more discontent, more unhappy with the way they were used." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 37 - Philly Soul and the Sound of the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-37-philly-soul-and-the-sound-of-the-70s
**Universal Message Philosophy** : Whitehead asserted that "Ain't No Stoppin' Us Now" carried a universal message rather than being exclusively aimed at "black people," arguing that their songs, derived from personal experiences, resonated broadly and that Gamble's political focus alienated white audiences, thereby limiting record sales. "We didn't mean the song to say, get off your butts black people.
