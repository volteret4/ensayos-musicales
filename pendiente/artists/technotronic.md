## songs
**Pump Up The Jam (1989)** : This song, released by the Belgian group Technotronic, became an unprecedented success. It was instrumental in establishing and popularizing the core formula of what would become Eurodance, prompting many other projects to emulate its style. ## curiosities
**Formation and Early Success (Late 1980s)** : The Belgian band Technotronic was formed by Newbeat producer Jo Bogaert, who collaborated with Belgian vocalist Manuela Kamosi, known as Jacket K, for the singing parts. Their debut single "Pump Up The Jam" achieved unprecedented success, establishing a foundational formula for the emerging Eurodance genre. "El productor belga de Newbeet, Jobo Gart, formó la banda con la vocalista belga con Golanja, Jacket K, que encantaba los temas." ← La gran mentira detrás del Eurodance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_F34L8wfrdo
**Vocalist vs. Model Controversy** : Initially, Technotronic's record label decided that vocalist Manuela Kamosi (Jacket K) did not possess a "marketable image" and opted to hire a model to appear in the music videos and on the album cover instead. Following the song's initial success and a scandal reminiscent of Milli Vanilli, Jacket K was quickly integrated into the videos to restore the band's credibility. "La discográfica dijo que la chica tenía una imagen de más allá de un drogina para vender, así que ficharon a una modelo para ilustrar los videoclips y la portada del disco." ← La gran mentira detrás del Eurodance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_F34L8wfrdo
