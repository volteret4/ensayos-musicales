**Black Superman, Muhammad Ali (1974)** : Recorded and released in 1974 by Johnny Wakelin and The Kinshasa Band, this reggae-influenced song became a number one hit on the Billboard Pop Charts in 1975, maintaining its position for six months. It echoed Muhammad Ali's iconic phrases like "floats like a butterfly, stings like a bee" and, as noted by Mitchell Morris, reinforced ideals of black masculinity. The song was originally a tribute to a Hungarian boxer, later reworked by Wakelin.
