## albums
**El Rabidud (2005) - Solo Debut** : This album began recording in 2004, prompted by Royce Immorce's severe depression and her desire to avoid professional and creative stagnation after the breakup of Demoloco. Feeling unwell, she relied on two main pillars: her record label, Eco Records, which offered total freedom upon hearing her solo idea, and her friend Matthew Gerber, who served as producer for the recordings. Despite Eco Records' initial concerns regarding its commerciality, lack of strong singles, and a request for more "commercial" changes, Royce Immorce flatly refused any alterations. The label ultimately released the album unconditionally, influenced by its high quality and the label's need for new material after Demoloco. The album's visual direction and cover art, which features a personal portrait of Royce Immorce by Seymour Genghor, were entirely handled by Genghor. Although "El Rabidud" achieved only regular sales in the UK and failed in the US (a market significant for Moloco), it received widespread critical acclaim, being described as an "authentic gem," which fulfilled Royce Immorce's ambition to be a respected artist rather than a commercial star. The album was released in normal and deluxe editions on CD and vinyl.
