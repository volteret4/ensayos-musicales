# artist - Björk & Dirty Projectors (Collaborative Project)

## labels
- Domino Records

## instruments
- Cassettes
- Drum machine

## albums
**Collaborative Album (2010) - Saving the Ocean** : This album originated from an initial idea for a single charity song, intended to promote protected marine areas and whale conservation at a 2010 event in the National Library of New York. It organically evolved into a full-length album deeply centered on the themes of ocean and whale preservation, created through an intuitive and spontaneous process with minimal conventional preparation. **Collaborative Album Production Style** : Characterized by its raw and simple sound, the album features minimal production and arrangements, reflecting a deliberate choice to move towards a more unrefined aesthetic. This approach is compared to Björk's *Diol* (likely *Voltaic* or a live companion album) which followed *Volta*. "prácticamente no tiene producción, no tiene reglos y es como todo muy curo y muy sencillo." ← Music Radar Clan > Björk y su curioso proyecto con los Dirty Proyectors | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Q1WS6Cu3eI&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**Collaborative Album Recording Process** : The album was intentionally created with almost no prior composition, rehearsal, or production. The artists gathered and rehearsed for only three days, recording their nascent ideas—not fully formed songs—directly onto cassettes. The primary recording was completed in a single day, with an additional day dedicated to production and minor arrangements. "El proyecto se hace prácticamente sin componer prácticamente sin ensayar y prácticamente sin producir. Y es de este sentido de esta forma de no haber bastante intencionada." ← Music Radar Clan > Björk y su curioso proyecto con los Dirty Proyectors | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Q1WS6Cu3eI&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc

## curiosities
**Charity Origin** : The project began as a charity initiative at the National Library of New York in 2010, aiming to raise awareness and foster the proliferation of protected marine areas, particularly for whales. This collaborative effort between Björk and Dirty Projectors was initially envisioned as a single song but evolved into a full album for the cause. "Ella se junto con varias artistas y entre ellos uno con el que ya había colaborado puntualmente a algunas ocasiones que llegaron a los irte proyectos." ← Music Radar Clan > Björk y su curioso proyecto con los Dirty Proyectors | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Q1WS6Cu3eI&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**Collector's Vinyl Edition** : The vinyl edition of the collaborative album, released a year after its digital debut, is a highly sought-after item for collectors. Many fans are unaware of the project's existence, and even fewer know about its limited vinyl release. It features a unique 3D cover, was sold exclusively in independent stores, and is strictly numbered, such as edition 1,040. "Este en concreto es la edición, 1.040. Entonces si se es muy fan de viores y es nuestro coleccionismo, esa es una piza que os puede dar mucho que farrar cuando la enseñé." ← Music Radar Clan > Björk y su curioso proyecto con los Dirty Proyectors | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Q1WS6Cu3eI&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**Whale Sound Imitation** : Dirty Projectors contributed to the album's unique sound by creating rhythmic foundations using drum machines and incorporating vocal improvisations that imitated whale sounds. This experimental vocal technique drew comparisons to Björk's work on her album *Medúlla*.
