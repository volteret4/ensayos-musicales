## curiosities
**More Into Rock** : Craig Fuller eventually rejoined Pure Prairie League after his legal issues but did not remain with the band for long because he was "more into rock" than the collective direction the band wanted to pursue. This musical difference ultimately led to his second departure. **Vietnam War Conscientious Objector Status** : Craig Fuller created significant issues for Pure Prairie League by claiming conscientious objector status for the Vietnam War draft. In 1973, he refused to report for the draft and was sentenced to six months in prison in Springfield, Missouri, followed by two years of service in an "alternative mental hospital." These legal problems were a direct cause for RCA dropping the band after their 1972 album "Bustin' Out."
