## curiosities
**Early British Rock and Roll Record (Pre-Steele)** : Tony Crumbi, a serious jazz drummer in his 30s, was inspired by the film "Rock Around the Clock" to form Tony Crumbi and the Rocket and record the Bill Haley sound-alike "Teach You To Rock." This record was made before Tommy Steele began performing, meaning Steele was not the very first British rock and roller as sometimes claimed. "by the time Hicks started performing, there had already been at least one British rock and roll record made." ← Episode 48： ＂Rock With the Caveman＂ by Tommy Steele | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=scDLm48u2yc
**Not Teen Idol Material** : Due to his age (30s) and serious jazz background, Tony Crumbi was not considered "teen idol material." He quickly returned to playing bebop and has largely been omitted from British rock history in favor of Tommy Steele.
