# artist - Jerry Jeff Walker

## genres
- Country Rock
- Folk
- Outlaw Country
- Progressive Country

## songs
**Mr. Bojangles (1968)** : Walker wrote this song in 1968, which was later covered by the Nitty Gritty Dirt Band. He met the inspiration for "Bojangles" in a New Orleans jail after being arrested for public disturbance while drunk; the actual man's name was not Bojangles, but the moniker derived from the African American dancer Bill Bojangles Robinson. "Walter Bojangles was a real guy who Walker met in a jail in New Orleans after he was arrested for making a public disturbance while drunk." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 46 - The Chaos and Music of the Ozark Music Festival of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/

## curiosities
**Country Rock Originator** : Jerry Jeff Walker is recognized as one of the originators of the country rock sound, which was heavily influenced by folk music and evolved into the Outlaw Country or Progressive Country movement of the 1970s. "Walker is one of the originators of the country rock sound, which is very much influenced by folk and that kind of evolves into this." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 46 - The Chaos and Music of the Ozark Music Festival of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/
