## songs
**Fairy Tale (1974)** : Written by sisters Anita and Bonnie Pointer, "Fairy Tale" was inspired by Anita's heartbreak over a married DJ. The song is described as "100% in the country vein" with "beautiful harmonies" and prominent pedal steel guitar. Their own band refused to record it, leading the sisters to record with Nashville session musicians. Released as a single in the summer of 1974, it reached number 13 on the Billboard Hot Country Chart and also received Top 40 radio airplay. "Fairy Tale" earned the Pointer Sisters a Grammy Award for Best Country Performance by a Group or Duo in 1975, making them the only African American women to win a country Grammy until Beyoncé in 2024. ## curiosities
**Arkansas Roots and Country Upbringing** : The Pointer Sisters, hailing from Arkansas, grew up immersed in country music. They listened to it at home, on the radio, and in church, considering themselves "country people." "We were country people. **Band's Refusal to Play Country** : The Pointer Sisters' own band refused to record "Fairy Tale," with their drummer stating, "I didn't join this group to play country music." This led the sisters to record the song with Nashville session musicians. **Experience with Racism in Nashville** : Upon arriving in Nashville for an event, the Pointer Sisters were mistakenly taken to the back of the venue, with staff assuming they were there to work in the kitchen. **Grammy Award for Country Performance (1975)** : The Pointer Sisters won a Grammy Award for Best Country Performance by a Group or Duo in 1975 for their song "Fairy Tale." They held the distinction of being the only African American women to win a country Grammy for 50 years until Beyoncé's win in 2024.
