# artist - Larry Parns

## curiosities
**Cut Out Original Managers** : Parns, along with John Kennedy, cut out Tommy Steele's initial managers, Bill Vali and Roy Tuvey, to the extent that they are not even mentioned in Steele's autobiography. "Kennedy, Parns and Hicks cut Vali and Tuvey out altogether to the extent that neither of them are even mentioned in the version of this story in Tommy Steele's autobiography." ← Episode 48： ＂Rock With the Caveman＂ by Tommy Steele | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=scDLm48u2yc
**Important British Rock and Roll Manager** : Larry Parns became the most important manager in British rock and roll in the 1950s and early 1960s, discovering and renaming many stars. "Larry Parns, who would go on to become the most important manager in British rock and roll in the 50s and early 60s." ← Episode 48： ＂Rock With the Caveman＂ by Tommy Steele | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=scDLm48u2yc
**Renamer of Rock Stars** : Parns was instrumental in renaming Thomas Hicks to Tommy Steele and continued this practice with a "whole host of further rock stars" including Duffy Power, Johnny Gentle, Billy Fury, and Marty Wilde. "Parns would go on to name a whole host of further rock stars in a similar manner." ← Episode 48： ＂Rock With the Caveman＂ by Tommy Steele | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=scDLm48u2yc
**Teen Idol Manager** : Managed a "whole host" of teen idols who emerged in the wake of Tommy Steele's initial success. These artists included Adam Faith, Marty Wilde, Terry Dean, Vince Taylor, Johnny Gentle, Billy Fury, Duffy Power, and Dickie Pride. Cliff Richard was notably one of the few contemporary stars not under Parns' management. "most of them managed by Larry Parns, Adam Faith, Marty Wilde, Terry Dean, Vince Taylor, Johnny Gentle, Billy Fury, Duffy Power, Dickie Pride and many more." ← Episode 70： ＂Move It＂ by Cliff Richard and the Drifters | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uoEWP94f6S4
