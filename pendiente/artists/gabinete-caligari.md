## albums
**Split with Parálisis Permanente (1981)** : Gabinete Caligari's debut work was a split single with Parálisis Permanente, before they explored a more "cañí" (traditional Spanish/flamenco-infused) side of Spanish pop rock. The split, featuring their songs "Golpes" and "Sombras Negras," became an underground gem and boosted both bands' visibility. ## curiosities
**Early Influences and Evolution** : Before venturing into the more "cañí" (traditional, rougher) side of Spanish pop rock, Gabinete Caligari was influenced by bands like The Cure and the charisma of Eduardo Benavente. This early phase connected them to the burgeoning "onda siniestra."
