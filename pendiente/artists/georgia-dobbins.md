# artist - Georgia Dobbins

## member of
- The Marvelettes

## songs
**Please Mr. Postman (1961) - Lyrical Contributor** : Georgia Dobbins played a crucial role in shaping "Please Mr. Postman" from an initial blues idea by William Garrett. She wrote most of the lyrics and recast the song into a less bluesy style, contributing significantly to its eventual chart-topping success. ## curiosities
**Discovery by Gladys Horton** : Georgia Dobbins initially caught Gladys Horton's eye through her performances in high school talent contests in Inkster, Michigan. Despite having graduated, Gladys persuaded Georgia to join her group for a talent contest entry, laying the groundwork for The Marvelettes. **Forced Departure from The Marvelettes** : Georgia Dobbins was forced to quit The Marvelettes before they signed their record contract because her father, who believed the entertainment industry was sinful, refused to counter-sign the contract as she was under 21. Her mother was too ill to sign, leading Georgia to a deep depression where she gave up singing entirely until 1978. "Her father believed the entertainment industry to be sinful, and wouldn't sign.
