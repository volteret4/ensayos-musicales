## curiosities
**Concert Hysteria** : Shaun Cassidy's concerts generated an "incredible" level of excitement and hysteria among fans, as observed by Al Wallace, the chief of security at The Plaza in Detroit, who likened it to the frenzy for Diana Ross or Tony Orlando. **Critical Reception** : Critics like Scott Kane of the *Atlanta Constitution* severely criticized Shaun Cassidy, describing his voice as "bland and colorless" and his music, specifically "Da Doo Ron Ron," as "excruciating." Kane's negative reviews provoked a flood of outraged letters from Cassidy's fans. "Critics like Scott Kane of the Atlanta Constitution raked Sean Cassidy over the coals He said that Sean Cassidy had a voice that was bland and colorless And he said that he was the one who made a lot of money to hold other slew of letters to the letters to the editor From fans who were outraged at the mistreatment of their teen idol." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 30 - The Sweet Sound of 70s Bubblegum Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/030-bubblegum
**TV Star and Pop Music Star Image** : Shaun Cassidy successfully combined a TV star image with a pop music star image, following in the footsteps of Ricky Nelson and his brother David Cassidy. He starred alongside Parker Stevenson and Pamela Sue Anderson in the ABC series *The Hardy Boys and Nancy Drew Mysteries* from 1977 to 1979.
