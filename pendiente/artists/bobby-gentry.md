## songs
**Fancy (1969)** : Written and recorded by Bobby Gentry in 1969, "Fancy" was a modest country and pop crossover hit in 1970. Gentry considered it her strongest statement for women's liberation, addressing themes of survival and using one's circumstances to rise above them. The song tells the story of a poor teenage girl pushed into prostitution who ultimately uses the situation to her advantage. "Fancy was a modest country and pop crossover hit in 1970. It was released in 1969 and made into the charts in 1970 for Bobby Gentry." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 23 - Women of Country Music in the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/023-country-women-72820-2-45-pm
**Ode to Billie Joe (1967)** : This song, released in 1967, tells the mysterious story of Billy Joe McAllister who dropped something into the water before jumping off the Tallahatchee Bridge. Its popularity was immense, but Gentry proved to be more than a one-hit-wonder. ## curiosities
**Advocacy for Women's Liberation** : Bobby Gentry explicitly stated that her song "Fancy" was her "strongest statement for women's lib," expressing her agreement with the movement and its core issues such as equality, equal pay, daycare centers, and abortion rights. This was a powerful stance for a woman in 1970. "Gentry said, Fancy is my strongest statement for women's lib if you really listen to it." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 23 - Women of Country Music in the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/023-country-women-72820-2-45-pm
**Diverse Talents** : Beyond her songwriting and singing, Bobby Gentry was a DJ for Armed Forces Radio and is believed to have painted the portraits for her album covers, including "Fancy" and "Patchwork." "According to Tara Murtha who wrote an excellent book on Gentry in Ode de Billy Joe for the 33 in a third book series, Gentry was a DJ for Armed Forces Radio and she probably painted the portraits for the album covers for fancy and patchwork." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 23 - Women of Country Music in the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/023-country-women-72820-2-45-pm
**Early Life and Musical Education** : Bobby Gentry hailed from Mississippi but was not the "hillbilly" stereotype the industry tried to impose on her. She studied music in California from the age of 13. "And even though she was from Mississippi, she was not the hillbilly that the industry tried to make her into. She had studied music in California starting at the age of 13." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 23 - Women of Country Music in the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/023-country-women-72820-2-45-pm
**Las Vegas Residency (1960s-1970s)** : For ten years, Bobby Gentry starred in critically acclaimed shows in Las Vegas. These performances were known for their over-the-top set designs, outrageous costumes often designed by Gentry herself, and stellar choreography, including a "gender-bending tribute to Elvis Presley" where she performed in a skin-tight, glittering pantsuit. "In for 10 years, she starred in shows in Las Vegas that were... critically acclaimed for over-the-top set design, outrageous costumes she often designed herself in stellar choreography, including a gender-bending tribute to Elvis Presley performed in a skin-tight, glittering pantsuit." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 23 - Women of Country Music in the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/023-country-women-72820-2-45-pm
**Retirement from Music (Late 1970s)** : Bobby Gentry abruptly walked away from her music career by the end of the 1970s. Her last known public appearance was on the Christmas episode of Johnny Carson's show in 1978, after which she ceased performing, giving interviews, or participating in retrospectives. "She was on the Christmas episode of Johnny Carson's show in 1978 and that was it. No more performing, no more interviews, no retrospectives, nothing.
