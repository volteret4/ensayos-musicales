**Will You Love Me Tomorrow (Year unknown, early 1960s)** : As co-writer with Carole King, Goffin penned the lyrics for this number one hit for The Shirelles. His lyrics brought unprecedented depth to pop music, focusing on female adolescent emotion and a woman's worry about a one-night stand. This lyrical formula, in Goffin's view, addressed the vacuity he perceived in earlier rock and roll lyrics and became a defining characteristic for girl groups thereafter. **Initial Dislike of Rock and Roll** : Goffin initially disliked rock and roll music due to what he perceived as its "lyrical vacuity." His work on "Will You Love Me Tomorrow" allowed him to develop a lyrical formula for girl groups that introduced greater emotional depth to pop music.
