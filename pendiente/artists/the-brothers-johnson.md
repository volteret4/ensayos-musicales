## albums
**Look Out for Number One (1976)** : The debut LP for The Brothers Johnson, produced by Quincy Jones, who challenged them to write songs around titles he provided, such as "Get The Funk Out My Face." The album delivered a "funky but polished sound" and reached an impressive number nine on the album chart, ultimately going platinum. **Mellow Madness (1975)** : The Quincy Jones album on which George and Lewis Johnson were hired to play prior to their own debut. The Brothers Johnson contributed four songs to this LP, including "Is It Love That We're Missing," which became a number 18 hit on the R&B chart, marking Quincy's highest charting hit under his own name at that point. ## curiosities
**Session Performers to Recording Artists** : Having spent the early 1970s backing up artists like Bobby Womack, The Supremes, and Billy Preston, The Brothers Johnson were ace session performers but neophyte recording artists when Quincy Jones hired them to play on his 1975 album *Mellow Madness*. Their contribution led Jones to agree to produce their entire debut album.
