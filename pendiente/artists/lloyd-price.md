## curiosities
**Commercial Success at Specialty** : Lloyd Price was one of several Specialty Records artists who were experiencing significant sales success, described as "selling hand-over-fist," which contrasted with The Soul Stirrers' more modest gospel sales. "other specialty acts, like Lloyd Price, Jesse Belvin, and guitar slim, were all selling hand-over-fist." ← Episode Sixty： ＂You Send Me＂ by Sam Cooke | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=92tD5qJR-yY
**Early Sound System Favorite** : Lloyd Price was among the artists frequently mentioned in the histories of early Jamaican sound systems, known for his New Orleans R&B music which was popular with local audiences. "The other artists who get repeatedly named in the histories of the early sound systems, along with Jordan and Harris, have fat stomino, Lloyd Price, Professor Longher." ← Episode 114： ＂My Boy Lollipop＂ by Millie | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kf-DtqoOsRI
**Musical Style** : Lloyd Price was known for producing music in a similar style to Fats Domino, performing sessions with the same musicians as Domino. "All of these people were veterans of sessions either for Domino or for artists who had worked in Domino's style, like Lloyd Price or Smiley Lewis." ← Episode 34： ＂Tutti Frutti＂ by Little Richard | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7mk9SW46Li4
**Recommendation to Specialty Records** : Lloyd Price suggested to Little Richard that he send a demo tape to Specialty Records, which was Price's own label. This recommendation ultimately led to Richard's breakthrough with the label. "Richard had met Lloyd Price, and Price had suggested that Richard send a demo tape in to specialty records, Price's label." ← Episode 34： ＂Tutti Frutti＂ by Little Richard | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7mk9SW46Li4
