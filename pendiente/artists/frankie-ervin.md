# artist - Frankie Ervin

## songs
**Dragnet Blues** : A novelty "cash-in" record made by Frankie Ervin, which resulted in a lawsuit from the creators of the popular TV show *Dragnet*. This highlights Ervin's reputation for making such opportunistic recordings during his time with Charles Brown's group. **Johnny Ace's Last Letter** : A version of this song by Frankie Ervin, which was rush-released by Johnny Moore's Three Blazers after Johnny Ace's death. This was another instance of Ervin's involvement in novelty or tribute cash-in records, leveraging timely events for commercial release. **Nature Boy** : The follow-up single by The Shields, for which original members, including Frankie Ervin, reunited. They were joined by members of The Turks. Although Ervin claimed it sold a million copies, it was less successful than "You Cheated," and the record label, Dot Records, reportedly grew tired of Ervin's demands for credit and royalties. **You Cheated** : Although Frankie Ervin reportedly "hated the song" and disliked doo-wop in general, he recorded the lead vocals for this track, which was ultimately credited to The Shields. The record became a massive hit, reaching number 12 on the pop charts, and famously outsold the original white group's version. "Irving hated the song. ## curiosities
**Dislike for Doo-wop and "You Cheated"** : Despite his involvement with the hit doo-wop track "You Cheated," Frankie Ervin generally disliked the doo-wop genre and found "You Cheated" to be a particularly bad example of it. He only agreed to record it for career reasons, as he was actively seeking a solo career at the time. "Irving hated the song. He didn't like Doop generally, and he thought the song was a particularly bad example of the genre." ← Episode 47： ＂Goodnight My Love＂ by Jesse Belvin | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eoyk3H0M6r8
**Frustration Over Lack of Credit for The Shields** : Frankie Ervin was annoyed when "You Cheated" was released under the group name The Shields rather than as a solo record by him, despite him performing the lead vocals. The label's decision was reportedly a strategic move to avoid promoting a solo singer who might later demand money and royalties. "Frankie Irving was naturally annoyed, that he wasn't given the label credit for the record." ← Episode 47： ＂Goodnight My Love＂ by Jesse Belvin | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eoyk3H0M6r8
**Reputation for Novelty Cash-in Records** : While a member of Charles Brown's group, Frankie Ervin developed a reputation for performing novelty "cash-in" records. This included tracks like "Dragnet Blues," which led to a lawsuit, and his version of "Johnny Ace's Last Letter," demonstrating his knack for capitalizing on popular trends and events.
