# artist - Sonny Curtis

## instruments
- Guitar
- Martin acoustic

## curiosities
**Collaboration with Scotty Turner** : Curtis was an aspiring songwriter and collaborated with Scotty Turner on "My Baby's Coming Home," an early example of his songwriting, though it wasn't recorded at the time, a demo version was later found by Harry Nelson. "A typical early example of his songwriting was one he wrote in collaboration with his friend Scotty Turner, my baby's coming home." ← Episode Sixty-One： ＂That'll Be the Day＂, by the Crickets | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KVJMKLc54O0
**Departure from Buddy Holly (1956)** : Sonny Curtis stopped working with Buddy Holly towards the end of 1956 because Owen Bradley, Holly's producer at Decca, disliked Curtis's guitar playing and preferred Holly to record with session musicians. Simultaneously, Curtis received an offer to play guitar for Slim Whitman, then the biggest star in country music. "Around this time, Sonny Curtis stopped working with Holly. Owen Bradley didn't like his guitar playing, and wanted Holly to record with the session musicians he used with everyone else, while Curtis got an offer to play guitar for Slim Whitman, who at the time was about the biggest star in country music." ← Episode Sixty-One： ＂That'll Be the Day＂, by the Crickets | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KVJMKLc54O0
**Early Songwriting Success (Teenager)** : As a teenager, Sonny Curtis made an early impression in the music world by writing the song "Someday" when he was only 16, which was subsequently recorded by country star Webb Pierce. "When he was only 16, he had written a song, Someday, that was recorded by the country star, by PS." ← Episode Sixty-One： ＂That'll Be the Day＂, by the Crickets | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KVJMKLc54O0
**Musical Style Shift Post-Elvis** : Immediately after Elvis's emergence, Sonny Curtis and Buddy Holly switched their musical styles, with Curtis borrowing Buddy's Stratocaster to play Scotty Moore's guitar licks.
