## songs
**Behind Closed Doors (1973)** : The title track from his 1973 album, it was his first number one country hit and garnered significant awards, including CMA Song of the Year, CMA Single of the Year, a Grammy for Kenny O'Dell as songwriter, and a Grammy for Rich for Country Vocal Performance. The song also crossed over to the Billboard Hot 100, reaching number 15. "It won the CMA Song of the Year, the CMA single of the year, a songwriter Kenny O'Dell won a Grammy for a country song of the year, Rich won a Grammy for country vocal performance, all by the way Behind Close Doors also crossed over to the Billboard Hot 100 and made it to number 15." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 2 - Countryish Music of the 1970s (Encore) | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Good Time Charlie's Got the Blues** : Charlie Rich covered Danny O'Keefe's song "Good Time Charlie's Got the Blues." It is firmly placed in the "country-politin" column of country music, featuring an orchestral arrangement with steel guitar and strings. ## curiosities
**1974 CMA Entertainer of the Year Win** : Charlie Rich won the CMA Entertainer of the Year award in 1974, reaping rewards from his successful 1973 work. "As the 1974 winner, he was reaping the rewards of his work that he had done in 1973." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 2 - Countryish Music of the 1970s (Encore) | https://www.ftr70.com/
**1975 CMA Awards Incident - Card Burning** : As the reigning CMA entertainer of the year, Charlie Rich staggered onto the stage at Nashville's Grand Ole Opry in 1975, fueled by gin and tonics, and announced John Denver as the winner. He then pulled out a lighter and set the card with Denver's name on it on fire, an act widely interpreted as a statement that John Denver was not welcome in country music. Rich later claimed it was an accident caused by pain killers. "Rich reaches into his pocket, takes out a lighter and sets the card with Denver's name on it on fire." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 2 - Countryish Music of the 1970s (Encore) | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Drinking Habits** : It was well-known that Charlie Rich frequently drank heavily, which was cited as a factor in his behavior at the 1975 CMA Awards.
