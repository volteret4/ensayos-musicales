# artist - Robert Wace

## members
- The Bull Weevils (briefly as lead singer)

## curiosities
**Dilettante Pop Star Ambitions** : Robert Wace, described as a "deletante wannabe pop star," initially sought The Bull Weevils as his backing band, arranging lucrative gigs at "society functions for his rich friends" in exchange for performing a couple of songs during their shows. However, his pop star aspirations proved unsuccessful, as he was "booed off stage" during a performance for an audience not composed of his friends, making it clear there was "no future" for him as a pop star. **Funded "You Really Got Me" Re-recording** : Robert Wace demonstrated faith in The Kinks' vision for "You Really Got Me" when Ray Davis vehemently disliked Shell Tauhmey's initial overproduced recording. He personally "paid for a new recording session out of his own pocket" after Larry Page insisted the original couldn't be released due to publishing rights, enabling the creation of the hit version. **Manager of The Kinks (Early)** : After his own pop star career failed, Robert Wace, alongside his best friend Grenville Collins, agreed to manage The Bull Weevils (later The Kinks). Their management deal stipulated that Collins would invest half his stockbroker wages, and Wace and Collins would receive "50% of the group's earnings." "Ways and Collins would manage the group.
