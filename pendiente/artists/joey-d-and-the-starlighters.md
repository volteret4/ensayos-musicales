## curiosities
**"Hey Let's Twist" Film** : Joey D and the Starlighters starred in the film "Hey let's twist." The movie inaccurately depicted The Peppermint Lounge, where the band had their famous residency, as a family-run Italian restaurant rather than its true identity as a mafia-owned bar. "Hey let's twist, starred Joey D in the Starlighters, in a film which portrayed the peppermint lounge as a family run Italian restaurant rather than a mafia run bar." ← Episode 91： ＂The Twist＂ by Chubby Checker | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LzsS8EXdsm8
**Early Singles on Scepter Records** : The group released a couple of singles on Scepter Records, with much of their material produced and written by Luther Dixon, who was a significant figure for the label. However, neither of these initial singles achieved particular success. **Origin of The Young Rascals** : The instrumentalists from the 1964 lineup of Joey D and the Starlighters eventually broke away to form their own band, The Young Rascals. This new group went on to have a series of hits between 1965 and 1970, marking a significant legacy from the Starlighters' lineup. "The young rascals, a group who had a series of hits from 1965 to 1970, started out as the instrumentalists in the 1964 lineup of Joey Dean the Starlighters, before breaking out to become their own band." ← Episode 91： ＂The Twist＂ by Chubby Checker | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LzsS8EXdsm8
**The Peppermint Lounge Residency** : The band secured a three-day weekend gig at The Peppermint Lounge in Manhattan, which proved so successful that they were offered a residency and became the house band. The small venue, with a capacity of about 200, became packed, largely with their 18-19 year old fans from New Jersey who were able to drink legally in New York (where the age was 18) but not in New Jersey (where it was 21). "Their shows there were so successful that they got a residency there, and became the house band." ← Episode 91： ＂The Twist＂ by Chubby Checker | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LzsS8EXdsm8
**The Shirelles Connection** : Joey D'Nicola, the band's leader, was a classmate of The Shirelles. When The Shirelles achieved their first hit, they advised D'Nicola to connect with Florence Greenberg, presumably to further his musical career.
