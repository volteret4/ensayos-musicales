## curiosities
**Criticism of "That'll Be the Day" (1956)** : Owen Bradley famously described the first, unreleased Decca recording of "That'll Be the Day" as "the worst song I've ever heard," primarily due to Buddy Holly singing out of his normal range following Webb Pierce's advice. "One of the songs that he recorded during that time, but which wasn't released, was one that Owen Bradley described as the worst song I've ever heard." ← Episode Sixty-One： ＂That'll Be the Day＂, by the Crickets | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KVJMKLc54O0
**Decca Recording Studio (1956)** : Owen Bradley's studio was the location for Buddy Holly's first Decca recording session in January 1956, and Holly continued to record there for the remainder of the year. "That first Decker recording session took place in OM Bradley's studio" ← Episode Sixty-One： ＂That'll Be the Day＂, by the Crickets | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KVJMKLc54O0
**Disapproval of Sonny Curtis's Playing (1956)** : Owen Bradley disliked Sonny Curtis's guitar playing and preferred Buddy Holly to record with his own studio session musicians, contributing to Curtis's departure from Holly's band. "Owen Bradley didn't like his guitar playing, and wanted Holly to record with the session musicians he used with everyone else." ← Episode Sixty-One： ＂That'll Be the Day＂, by the Crickets | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KVJMKLc54O0
**Regular Session Musicians** : Bradley often used regular session musicians for his recordings, including Grady Martin on rhythm guitar and Doug Kirkham on drums, who rounded out Buddy Holly's first Decca session. "The session was rounded out by two of the regular musicians that Bradley used on his sessions. Grady Martin on rhythm guitar, so Buddy didn't have to sing and play at the same time, and Doug Kirkham on drums." ← Episode Sixty-One： ＂That'll Be the Day＂, by the Crickets | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KVJMKLc54O0
