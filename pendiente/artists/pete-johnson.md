## albums
**Roll and Pete (1938)** : Recorded in December 1938 in New York with Big Joe Turner, this song is considered by some to be the first rock and roll record ever, and has been widely credited as the first Boogie song to use a backbeat rather than a shuffle beat. His piano part contains all the musical elements of early rock and roll, particularly the melodic phrasing in his right hand, which, if transferred to guitar, resembles Chuck Berry's entire guitar style and features elements also heard in Jerry Lee Lewis's playing. ## songs
**It's All Right Baby (1938)** : This is the title given to a live recording of Pete Johnson and Big Joe Turner performing "Roll and Pete" at Carnegie Hall, just a week before its studio recording. This live version features a much clearer backbeat, with Turner audibly clapping along on the second and fourth beats, providing evidence of the rhythm's early use in performance. ## curiosities
**Boogie Woogie Piano Virtuoso** : Pete Johnson was considered one of the greatest exponents of the Boogie Woogie style. His playing, alongside Big Joe Turner's vocals, was pivotal in the "Spirituals to Swing" concerts, helping to revive Boogie Woogie and change it from a minority form into a major music trend in 1938. He and Turner could stretch "Roll and Pete" out to an hour in live performance by improvising new lyrics.
