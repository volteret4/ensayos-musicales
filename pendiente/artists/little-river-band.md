## albums
**Sleeper Catcher (1978)** : Released in June 1978, this was the band's fourth studio album. The Australian pressing of the album featured a "much more prog rock type of opening," described as "creative and complex," which differed significantly from the US issue. Despite containing future hits like "Reminiscing," the US record label initially received it with skepticism, asking "where are the singles." ## songs
**Reminiscing (1978)** : Written by founding member Graham Goble, inspired by a C9 chord shown to him by David Briggs, this song was released in June 1978 on the *Sleeper Catcher* album. It peaked at number three on the Billboard Hot 100 in the US and garnered high praise from John Lennon, who listened to it "on repeat," and Frank Sinatra. Goble attributed its greater popularity in the US compared to Australia to its depiction of "the world that was my internal world," a fantasy of 1940s and 1950s American pop culture that resonated strongly with Americans raised in that era. The record label initially overlooked its potential as a single.
