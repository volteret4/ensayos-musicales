## albums
**Anthology of American Folk Music (1952) - Groundbreaking Compilation** : Released in 1952 by Harry Smith and Folkways Records owner Moses Asch, this six-album set was considered "the most important bootleg ever." Smith, a record collector, experimental filmmaker, and occultist, compiled it from his vast collection of old 78 records, mostly commercial issues from the late 1920s and early 1930s. The compilation spanned genres from hard blues to country and gospel, suggesting a unique American Roots music tradition. It became the "core repertoire for every American folk musician of the 50s" and crystallized the folk revival movement. The labels from which the original 78s were sourced did not protest the release, as there was no market for back catalog at the time. ## curiosities
**Record Collector and Occultist** : Harry Smith was a record collector and experimental filmmaker, known for his vast collection of old 78 records. He was also a follower of the occultist Alistair Crowley, and his "Anthology of American Folk Music" was at least partly conceived as a magical work, with esoteric symbolism in its album covers.
