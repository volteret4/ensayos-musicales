**Mystery Train (1953)** : This song was the B-side of Junia Parker and The Blue Flames' second single for Sun Records. It originates from the old folk song "Warwickman Blues," recorded in 1930 by The Carza family, with lyrics like "the train arrived 16 coaches long" becoming part of floating blues lyrics. The composition was credited to Parker and Sam Phillips, with Phillips claiming three major changes: giving the song its title, insisting on "16 coaches long" instead of Parker's "50 coaches long," and suggesting the band start slowly and build tempo. "But it was the B side that made an impression. The B side was a song called Mystery Train." ← Episode 33： ＂Mystery Train＂, by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DSHTmHy3Jo0
