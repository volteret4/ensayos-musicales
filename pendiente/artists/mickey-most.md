# artist - Mickey Most

## curiosities
**Independent Producer Pioneer** : Mickey Most emerged as one of London's new wave of independent producers, following in the footsteps of figures like Joe Meek and Andrew Oldham. **Parting Ways with The Animals** : The Animals ultimately ended their working relationship with Mickey Most because they became "sick of most attempts to make them more poppy." "But that was the point at which the animals are most with part company. **Persuading on Single Length** : Despite the general rule against singles exceeding three minutes for radio play, Mickey Most was eventually convinced by Charles Chandler to release The Animals' "House of the Rising Sun" at its full 4 minutes and 20 seconds without edits. "but most was eventually persuaded by Chas Chandler that the track needed to go out as it was, with no edits." ← Episode 115： ＂House of the Rising Sun＂ by the Animals | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=34aIGCnb4X4
**South African Success** : After starting his career in the duo The Most Brothers, Most moved to South Africa, where he achieved 11 number one hits by producing cover versions of American rock singles, backed by a band called The Playboys. "most had moved to South Africa, where he'd had 11 number one hits, with cover versions of American mock singles, backspare band called The Playboys." ← Episode 115： ＂House of the Rising Sun＂ by the Animals | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=34aIGCnb4X4
**Transition to Production** : Upon returning to the UK in 1963 with less success as a performer, Most transitioned into production, making The Animals his very first signing.
