# artist - Robert Altman

## albums
**Nashville Soundtrack** : Robert Altman's 1975 film "Nashville" had a soundtrack that uniquely featured no track list and included a significant amount of in-movie dialogue. Altman's approach for the film allowed actors to sing their own songs, often ones they had already written, with the screenplay then being adapted to accommodate these musical pieces. ## curiosities
**"MASH" Film (1970)** : Robert Altman directed the 1970 film "MASH," which was set in Korea but served as a clear statement about the Vietnam War. Altman had previously worked with Timothy Brown, who played Spear-chucker Jones in "MASH," before casting him as Tommy Brown in "Nashville." "Altman did something similar with the 1970 movie Mash which was set in Korea but was very clearly a statement about Vietnam." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 53 - More than Charley Pride - African Americans in 70s Country | https://www.ftr70.com/
**"Nashville" Film (1975)** : Robert Altman directed the 1975 film "Nashville," a satirical look at the 1970s country music industry and American politics. The film included the character Tommy Brown, widely believed to be based on Charlie Pride, although Pride denied this. Altman's methodology for the film involved actors singing their own songs, which were then integrated into the screenplay. "Robert Altman, made a movie about the country music industry which came out in 1975." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 53 - More than Charley Pride - African Americans in 70s Country | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Actor-Driven Music in "Nashville"** : For his film "Nashville," Robert Altman gave actors the opportunity to sing their own songs, often compositions they had already written outside the film's production. The screenplay was then specifically written to incorporate these songs, allowing for a unique integration of character and music.
