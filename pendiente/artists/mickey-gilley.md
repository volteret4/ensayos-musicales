**Stand By Me (1980)** : Gilley's slowed-down, country-fied cover of the classic Ben E. King song, written by Lieber and Stolar, was a significant feature in the film *Urban Cowboy*. It reached number one on the country charts and number 22 on the Billboard Hot 100 in 1980. The song plays in the movie during a scene where Bud and Sissy attempt to make each other jealous by dancing with other partners. "Mickey Gillie was already well known but this slowed down, country-wide version of the song made him a star." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 50 - The Travolta Trilogy | https://www.ftr70.com/

## curiosities
**Cousin and Fellow Pianist** : Mickey Gilley was a cousin of Jerry Lee Lewis and Jimmy Swaggart, and they would all play piano together, though Jerry Lee was considered the most talented and dedicated. "He and Swaggot and their other cousin Mickey Gilly would all play piano together as well as separately." ← Episode 59： ＂Whole Lotta Shakin' Goin' On＂ by Jerry Lee Lewis | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wiSA3J9iJ7M
**Criticism and Financial Benefit from Urban Cowboy** : Gilley initially disliked Aaron Latham's article that inspired *Urban Cowboy*, believing it mocked country music and his bar. However, he "sure enough appreciated" all the money and publicity the movie generated for his establishment. "Mickey Gilly said he did not like the article because he thought it was mocking country and he thought it was mocking his bar." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 50 - The Travolta Trilogy | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Impact of Urban Cowboy on Country Music** : Mickey Gilley, whose nightclub Gilley's was central to the movie "Urban Cowboy," believed that the film and its soundtrack revitalized country music when it was "at a standstill." He credited the movie with introducing country music to a wider audience, with John Travolta's star power providing an additional boost. "Mickey Gilly said that at that point country music was at a standstill and then it exploded on the market." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 28 - Soundtracks of the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/028-soundtracks-of-the-70s-12221-5-45-pm
**Sudden Surge in Popularity** : Gilley experienced an immediate and overwhelming increase in personal popularity shortly after *Urban Cowboy* premiered. A week or two after the film's release, he and his crew were greeted by 25,000 screaming fans at a Six Flags theme park in Arlington, Texas, receiving a standing ovation, and his performance of "Stand By Me" was so loud he couldn't hear his own drums. "When Terry the road manager walked on stage carrying guitar they stood up and applauded him and when I did stand by me they screamed so loud I couldn't hear the drums." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 50 - The Travolta Trilogy | https://www.ftr70.com/
