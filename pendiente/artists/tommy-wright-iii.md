## albums
**Memphis Massacre (1992)** : Recorded at the tender age of 15 after breaking his piggy bank to pay the producer, this cassette featured eight songs recorded in a single day. Tommy Wright III personally sold these tapes at his school. "Las ocho canciones que formaron su cassette me infisma sacré y se grabaron en tan solo un día, y al mismo las vendía después en la escuela." ← Memphis Rap： Cuando el rap era puro terror | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lWBpNa-xSt4
**On The Run (1994)** : Self-produced and released two years after his debut, this album is considered one of the most influential records in the entire Memphis scene. It was particularly noted for Tommy Wright III's distinctive rapping style and his innovative use of the 808, featuring frantic hi-hats and an iconic cowbell that helped define a new sound. "Su siguiente álbum, H.S. Tu H.S. Das Tudas, ya producido por él, salió dos años después en 1994 y es en uno de los discos más influyentes de toda la escena, sobre todo por su forma de rapear y por ese uso de la 8-08, con esos hi-hats frenéticos y en mi toco acencerroca habría creando un estilo." ← Memphis Rap： Cuando el rap era puro terror | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lWBpNa-xSt4
**On The Run Album Cover (1994)** : The cover of his influential 1994 album depicted Tommy Wright III in a cemetery, holding a shovel. "en la portada aparecía en un cementerio con palan mano." ← Memphis Rap： Cuando el rap era puro terror | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lWBpNa-xSt4
**Untitled Album Cover (Post-1994)** : The cover of his subsequent album directly featured his police mugshot, a stark reflection of his adolescence spent in and out of jail. ## curiosities
**DIY Debut at 15** : At just 15 years old, he literally broke his piggy bank to fund the production of his first album, "Memphis Massacre," which he then personally sold at school, embodying the raw DIY spirit of the scene. "No hago su premed disco con tan solo 15 años, rompiendo literalmente su hucha para apagarle al productor." ← Memphis Rap： Cuando el rap era puro terror | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lWBpNa-xSt4
**Pioneer of Fast Rhythm** : Tommy Wright III was a pioneer of the fast-paced rhythm that significantly influenced the development of both the Crunk and Trap genres. "fue pionero en el ritmo rápido que luego influirá tanto en el cron como en el trap." ← Memphis Rap： Cuando el rap era puro terror | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lWBpNa-xSt4
**Post-Incarceration & Internet Legend** : His prolific run concluded in 2001 when he served a 4-year jail sentence. Upon his release, he lost his studio but was soon to become an internet legend, indicating a later rediscovery and influence online. "Su racha terminó en 2001 cuando cumplió 4 años de carcel, cuando salió perdiós estudio, pero pronto se iba a convertir en una leyenda de internet." ← Memphis Rap： Cuando el rap era puro terror | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lWBpNa-xSt4
**Rediscovery by Phonk Producers** : Global producers, particularly those working in phonk, delved into the history of Memphis rap and rediscovered figures like Tommy Wright III, paying homage by adopting the horrorcore aesthetic and resurrecting the raw 808 sound. "A partir de ahí, productores de todo el mundo empezaron a vociar en la historia y a descubrir figuras como Cirys Hym mafia o Tommy Brai III, rendendole su homenaje." ← Memphis Rap： Cuando el rap era puro terror | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lWBpNa-xSt4
**Troubled Adolescence & Prolific Output** : He spent his adolescence frequently entering and exiting jail, a reality visually conveyed through his album covers, one of which directly featured his mugshot. Despite this, he released 5 albums in 8 years, solidifying his status as a city legend. "se pasó la adolescencia entrando y saliendo de la carcel, lanzó 5 discos en 8 años y se convertió en una leyenda de la ciudad." ← Memphis Rap： Cuando el rap era puro terror | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lWBpNa-xSt4
**Unintended Internet Influence** : In a recent interview, Tommy Wright III revealed that he produced and distributed his tapes primarily to impress his friends, never imagining that he was shaping a scene and a sound that would leave an enormous and enduring mark on the internet. "Tomi Wright III lo resumió hace poco en una entrevista, daba a esas cintas solo para impresionar a los colegas, nunca pensaron que estaban dando forma a una escena y a un sonido que acabaría dejando una huella dormen internet." ← Memphis Rap： Cuando el rap era puro terror | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lWBpNa-xSt4
