## curiosities
**Blue Note Artist** : Brian Blade is a recognized Blue Note artist who has released several acclaimed records on the label. He is also featured on Charles Lloyd's new album. **Collaborator on Norah Jones' Early Work** : Brian Blade played drums on Norah Jones' initial recording sessions with Craig Street for her first album. He later played with her again at the Blue Note anniversary concert and on her recording of Horace Silver's "Peace" with Wayne Shorter. "I mean, he was on those first sessions with Craig." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Introducing Broken Record - Norah Jones Begins Again | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f1264fe6d21276e0cbc
**Exceptional Drumming Style** : Norah Jones describes Brian Blade's drumming as heavenly; he is an incredibly attentive listener who reacts in the moment, making the music feel alive without ever being "wrong," "distracting," or "busy." His playing is never for self-assertion, characterized by a "super deep" pocket and incredible dynamics, ranging from "real quiet" to explosive. "He doesn't play anything just to play it. He's always listening and he listens more than anyone I've ever played with." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Introducing Broken Record - Norah Jones Begins Again | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f1264fe6d21276e0cbc
**Patience in Performance** : Brian Blade is noted for his discipline of only playing drums when he has "something to say." He might sit out half a tune, only to enter perfectly at the right moment, a quality also observed in Wayne Shorter. "They don't really play until they have something to say, you know, there's times where Brian is sat out on half a tune and I'm like, I guess he's not going to play it." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Introducing Broken Record - Norah Jones Begins Again | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f1264fe6d21276e0cbc
**Reunion at Blue Note Anniversary** : Norah Jones hadn't played with Brian Blade in "a long, long time, like years" due to his busy schedule, but they reconnected and performed together at the Blue Note anniversary concert, an experience she found "so special." "It was the first time I played with Brian in a long time. I hadn't actually seen him in a while. He played in my first records, but we sort of drifted and he's hard to pin down.
