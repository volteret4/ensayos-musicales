**Credit Theft and Deception** : Nick Renee, generally credited as the producer of "Moon Dog" / "LSD25" by The Gamblers, for years falsely claimed that Derry Weaver (credited co-composer and lead guitarist) was a pseudonym for himself. When confronted with evidence, Renee admitted Weaver was real but still maintained the pseudonym claim. He further alleged Weaver had died in a car crash, allowing Renee to use Weaver's Social Security number to fraudulently claim extra money as a staff producer. This story was disproven by Renee's death in 1998 and Weaver's actual death in 2013, but Renee's lies went unchallenged for years due to Weaver only giving one interview in 2001. **Exploitation of Moon Dog Rights** : After taking a job at Capitol Records, Nick Vene (Renee) used his position and ownership of the master tape and publishing rights for The Gamblers' "Moon Dog" to include the minor hit on the Capitol compilation album "Golden Gasses." This move significantly exposed the song to a much wider audience and led to covers by other major artists.
