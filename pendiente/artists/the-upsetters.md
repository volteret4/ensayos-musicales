## curiosities
**"World Famous Upsetters" Single (1962)** : The band released the single "I'm in Love Again" in December 1962, credited to "the world famous Upsetters," but it featured Little Richard's highly recognizable lead vocal, indicating his covert return to rock and roll recording before his official comeback. **Little Richard's Backing Band** : The Upsetters were Little Richard's trusted backing band, for whom he strongly advocated to be used in the studio, convinced they were superior to session musicians. "He was convinced that his own backing band, the upsets, were at least as good as the studio musicians and he was pushing for specialty to let him use them in the studio." ← Episode 54： Keep A Knockin | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oA4Y_K9E5Y4
**Studio Recording with Little Richard** : After Specialty Records finally allowed Little Richard to use his own band, The Upsetters, in the studio, they recorded the song "Keep a-Knockin'" together in early 1957.
