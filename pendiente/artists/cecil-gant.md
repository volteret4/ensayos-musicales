## curiosities
**Dual Musical Style** : While Gant's A-side releases were typically ballads, his B-sides consistently featured boogie rockers, a style that perfectly aligned with Roy Brown's "Good Rockin' Tonight." "But while Gant A-sides were ballad, his B-sides were Boogie Rockers, and very much in the style of Brown's song." ← Episode 7： ＂Good Rockin' Tonight＂ by Wynonie Harris | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IgqSPoYRUCM
**Highly Successful with "I Wonder"** : Cecil Gant was enjoying considerable success with "I Wonder," his biggest hit, which was a ballad, when Roy Brown approached him seeking to have "Good Rockin' Tonight" recorded. "Gant was writing high off his biggest hit, I Wonder, which was a ballad." ← Episode 7： ＂Good Rockin' Tonight＂ by Wynonie Harris | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IgqSPoYRUCM
**Improvisational Recording Style** : According to Jim Ballet, who produced Gant's records, everything Gant recorded was improvised in a single take. He reportedly struggled to remember or repeat his performances, making him an unlikely artist to record others' pre-written songs. "Everything Gant recorded was improvised in one take, and he could never remember what it was he'd just done and could never repeat a song." ← Episode 7： ＂Good Rockin' Tonight＂ by Wynonie Harris | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IgqSPoYRUCM
**Securing Roy Brown's Deal** : Despite his improvisational nature, Gant was so impressed by Roy Brown's singing and his song "Good Rockin' Tonight" that he called the head of his record company at 2:30 AM. After hearing Brown sing the song twice over the phone, the executive instructed Gant to give Brown $50 and ensure he remained in sight, leading to Brown's recording deal. "But he was so impressed by Brown singing, as well as his song, that he phoned the head of his record company at 2.30am and got Brown to sing down the phone." ← Episode 7： ＂Good Rockin' Tonight＂ by Wynonie Harris | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IgqSPoYRUCM
