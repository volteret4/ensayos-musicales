**1963 New Year's Resolution** : Despite their popularity on stage and the backing of DJ Murray the K, The Ronettes' records on Colpix were not selling. Believing the label was the problem, they resolved in 1963 to seek out Phil Spector to produce them, given his growing reputation as a hitmaker. **1964 UK Tour and Relationships** : During a brief tour of the UK in early 1964, The Ronettes developed friendships with both The Beatles and their support act, The Rolling Stones. Ronnie experienced a "brief chaste flirtation" with John Lennon, while Estelle had a "little more" with George Harrison. Phil Spector even sent a telegram attempting to forbid The Rolling Stones from spending time with the group. **1966 Beatles Tour Invitation and Substitute** : In 1966, The Ronettes were invited to be the support act for The Beatles' last-ever tour. Phil Spector once again prevented Ronnie from participating, insisting she needed to be in the studio, leading to Elaine substituting for her, much to The Beatles' disappointment. No material from these studio sessions was ever released. **Accidental Peppermint Lounge Gig** : To circumvent age restrictions at the Peppermint Lounge during the height of the Twist craze, the underage girls dressed alike to appear older. The club manager mistook them for booked dancers who hadn't arrived, instructing them to perform, which inadvertently secured them a regular gig dancing behind Joey Dee and the Starliters. **Royalty Lawsuit (1998-2002)** : In 1998, The Ronettes initiated a lawsuit against Phil Spector for unpaid royalties, having collectively received only $14,482.30 from all their hit records, an amount equivalent to a single 1964 royalty payment. Spector counter-argued he was owed money due to recording costs. After multiple appeals, each group member eventually received approximately $500,000 in 2002, which was still considered a small fraction of their true earnings. "By 1998, when the Vanette finally sued Specter for unpaid royalties, they had earned between them a total of $14,482.30 in royalties from all their hit records, the amount that came from a single 1964 royalty payment." ← Episode 110： ＂Be My Baby＂ by the Ronettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4BmEQRvAHAY
