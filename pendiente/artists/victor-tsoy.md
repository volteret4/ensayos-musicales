**Avoiding Military Service (1983)** : In 1983, Victor Tsoy was recruited by the Soviet army, as the USSR was embroiled in conflicts in Afghanistan and needed soldiers. To avoid going to war, he feigned a mental illness. He spent a month and a half institutionalized, receiving treatment he did not need, but which ultimately led to him being declared unfit for the army. Those close to him noted that something changed in him during this period, and he was never the same again. "En 1983 fue reculotado por el ejército En esa época, LaURS estaban plenoconflictos de Afganistán, necesitaba soldados." ← Viktor Tsoi. **Birth and Early Life (1962-1979)** : Victor Tsoy was born in 1962 in Leningrad. His mother was a physical education teacher, and his father was a Russian-Korean engineer with ancestry tracing back to present-day North Korea. He studied at art school but quickly lost interest, being expelled in 1979 at the age of 17 for low performance. In the Soviet Union of that era, stopping studies meant having to work, as unemployment could lead to arrest and imprisonment for "social parasitism." "Victor Tsoy nació en 1962 en el engrado. Su madre era profesora de educación física y su padre, un ingeniero ruso coreano cuya sentencia se remota al actual corear del norte." ← Viktor Tsoi. **Cameo in "Asa" (1987)** : Victor Tsoy had a brief but very powerful cameo in Sergei Solovyov's cult film "Asa" in 1987, portraying himself. In a memorable scene, as a restaurant manager reads a list of rules he must follow before performing, Tsoy rises mid-sentence, removes his coat, and takes to the stage, singing "Khochu Peremen!" **Death (August 15, 1990)** : On August 15, 1990, at 12:28 PM, on the Slocatalsim road in Latvia (then part of the USSR), Victor Tsoy's car crossed into the opposing lane while taking a curve and collided head-on with a bus. The musician died instantly. An autopsy revealed no traces of drugs or alcohol, and the official conclusion was that he simply fell asleep at the wheel due to exhaustion. "15 de agosto de 1990, 12 y 28 del medio día. **Final Retreat and Demos (Summer 1990)** : After playing to tens of thousands at the Luzhniki Stadium, Victor Tsoy felt the need to disappear for a time. He retreated to a quiet country house in Tonya to spend part of the summer, where he fished and prepared demos (voice and guitar tracks) for Kino's next album. It was here that he was recording just before his death. "Después de tocar el cielo, soy necesitada desaparecer un tiempo, así que se retiró a una tranquila casa de campón de Tonya para pasar parte del verano." ← Viktor Tsoi. **Kamchatka Boiler Room - Artistic Refuge** : While his music began to circulate, Victor Tsoy worked in a boiler room of a residential block, where he did night shifts but also had a lot of free time. He named this place "Kamchatka," and it eventually became a significant refuge for Russian bohemians. Many musicians worked there, and it has since been converted into a museum dedicated to Tsoy. "Mientras son músicas en vez de circular, Victor trabajaba en una sala de calderas de un bloque residencial, donde hacía las rosturnos, pero también tenía mucho tiempo libre." ← Viktor Tsoi. **Physical Appearance and Bruce Lee Influence** : Tsoy possessed peculiar Asian features that distinguished him from the majority of Soviet society. He greatly admired Bruce Lee and began to emulate his image and practice martial arts, skills he would later showcase in films. "Físicamente, Tsoy tenía unos rasgos asiáticos muy pecoleares que le hacían esta carta de la mojenia sociedad soviética. **Post-Mortem Impact and Enduring Myth** : The news of Victor Tsoy's death opened national news and had such a profound impact on Soviet society that there were reportedly "dozens of registrations of young people who had taken their own lives" across the country, though this is likely an exaggeration. Shortly after his death, a Moscow street wall began to be covered with photos of Tsoy and lyrics from his songs. Today, this wall continues to accumulate tributes, and every August 15th, people gather to sing his songs and write "Tsoy está vivo" (Tsoy is alive), illustrating the enduring reach of his figure. "El hígado, en muchos sitios, que al conocer la historia de su muerte, decenas de jóvenes se quitaron la vida. Todo pareciindicar que es una exageración." ← Viktor Tsoi.
