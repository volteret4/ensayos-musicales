## songs
**Burnt Biscuit (Year Unknown)** : Moman wrote this instrumental track, which was the first single released on Stax's subsidiary label, Bolt. Released under the name The Triumphs, after Moman's sports car, he believed it had the potential to be as big as "Last Night," though it wasn't a hit. "Chips Momen wrote, burnt biscuit, by a group consisting of Momen, Rufus Thomas's son Mavelle, Louis Steinberg, and Howard Grames." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Last Night (1961)** : Chips Moman received songwriting credit for this instrumental hit by The Mar-Keys because he suggested combining the separately written keyboard and horn lines. He also produced the several sessions where the song was recorded, often with different lineups. ## curiosities
**California Studio Experience** : Moman moved to California with Johnny and Dorsie Bennett, working as a session player at Gold Star Studios. This experience provided him with valuable studio techniques that he later applied back in Memphis. "He went with the Bennett to California, where he was a session player for a time, though I've never been able to find a list of any of the records he played on, just people saying he played at Gold Star Studios." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Departure from Stax** : Chips Moman left Stax Records following disagreements with Estelle Axton and claims that Jim Stewart reneged on a promise to give him a third of the company. After leaving, he moved to Nashville briefly before founding American Sound Studio in another part of Memphis. "Everyone has a different story about what happened, but the upshot was that Momen left the company, went to Nashville for a while, and then founded his own studio, America, in another part of Memphis." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Early Career and Mentorship** : As a teenage guitarist, Chips Moman was discovered by Sun Records artist Warren Smith while playing at a Memphis drugstore. Smith offered him a job, kickstarting Moman's career in music. "Chip's was a teenage guitarist who had been playing a friend's guitar at a drugstore in Memphis, just hanging around after work, when Warren Smith worked him." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Musical Contributions After Stax** : Post-Stax, Moman became a prolific songwriter and producer, creating a string of soul, country, and pop classics. "Momen would become responsible for writing and producing a whole string of Soul, Country, and Mark Classics" ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Return to Memphis and Studio Expertise** : An accident led Moman to return to Memphis, where he applied his Californian studio techniques to produce Don Willis's "But in High School Baby." His eagerness and knowledge made him a key figure in the revitalized Satellite Records.
