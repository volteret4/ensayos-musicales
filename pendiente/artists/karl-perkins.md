## songs
**Blue Suede Shoes (1956)** : This was a hit for Karl Perkins. He may have missed an opportunity to have a hit follow-up to this song at Sun Records when he reportedly turned down "Great Balls of Fire." The stop-start intro of "Great Balls of Fire" was reminiscent of the intro to "Blue Suede Shoes," suggesting Perkins could have had a hit with it, though perhaps not as big as Jerry Lee Lewis's. **Glad All Over (1957)** : Karl Perkins performed this song in the film "Jamboree." Unlike most of his recordings, this track was not written by him but by Aaron Schroeder. Perkins reportedly found the song "dreck" and considered it only slightly less awful than another option he was given, choosing it over "Great Balls of Fire." He did not believe it was a suitable follow-up to "Whole Lotta Shakin'." "That song was one of the few that Perkins recorded, which wasn't written by him. ## curiosities
**Departure from Sun Records** : Karl Perkins became increasingly frustrated with Sam Phillips' favoritism towards Jerry Lee Lewis, feeling his own career was being neglected. He was particularly irked by the credit "Jerry Lee Lewis and his pumping piano" while his own records never mentioned "Karl's guitar." Despite Phillips' belated promise to credit him as "the Rocking guitar man," it was too late. Perkins, along with Johnny Cash, made an agreement with Columbia Records on November 1, 1957, to join the new label once their Sun contracts expired. "Patrons and Johnny Cash, both made an agreement with Columbia Records, on November 1, 1957, that when their current contracts with Sun expired, they'd start recording for the new label." ← Episode 66： ＂Great Balls of Fire＂ by Jerry Lee Lewis | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CVp5e4EWCqI
**Post-Sun Career** : Unlike Johnny Cash, Karl Perkins did not achieve greater success after leaving Sun Records for Columbia. **Turning Down "Great Balls of Fire"** : Karl Perkins claimed he was offered "Great Balls of Fire" to perform in the film "Jamboree" but turned it down, believing it was "dreck" along with another song provided by Hill & Range. Other people involved in the situation remember it differently.
