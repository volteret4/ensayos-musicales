## songs
**Seasons in the Sun (1974)** : This song was ranked #5 on a 2006 CNN poll of the worst songs of all time. According to Tom Harrison, music critic for The Province (Vancouver newspaper), in June 1983, with 11 million records sold, it "may be the fifth best selling single of all time." Tom Bryan of StereoGum described it as "sentimental mush." Terry Jacks' paper boy loved the song, even when Jacks' friends thought it "sucks," and the paper boy returned with friends to hear it again, which Jacks credited with launching its pop music history success. An uncredited review of the album in the Hamilton Ohio Journal News (March 10, 1974) attributed the single's success not to the performance, but to "the McEwen magical touch of schlock poetry and a hummelbo melody." The song went to number one on March 2, 1974, and sold 11 million copies. Rolling Stone magazine's "worst song of the 70s" poll ranked it at #7. Jacks explicitly stated that he liked Top 40 music, and to make the original composition a hit, he added a beat and tried to make the arrangement as interesting as possible. The lyrics were originally written by Belgian songwriter Jacques Brel, then adapted into English by Rod McKuen. Jacks heard Brel's original (about a man heartbroken over his wife's cheating) and then McKuen's version, making tweaks to it. He opted to remove the lyrics about the cheating wife, which McKuen had kept, and instead wrote about a friend who died of leukemia. ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 48 - The Worst of the 70s The Music of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/

## curiosities
**Beach Boys Collaboration Attempt** : Terry Jacks made some tweaks to "Seasons in the Sun" with the intention of giving it to The Beach Boys, who had asked him to write a song for them. However, "things got weird" with The Beach Boys, as Jacks believed Brian Wilson was attempting to steal the song.
