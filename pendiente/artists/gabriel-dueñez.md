# artist - Gabriel Dueñez

## curiosities
**Accidental Origin of Cumbia Rebajada (Early 1990s)** : In Monterrey, Mexico, around the same time DJ Screw was developing his sound, Gabriel Dueñez, a "solidero" (sound system operator), inadvertently created a new subgenre. During a party where he had been playing Colombian cumbia and vallenato records for over six hours, his sound equipment failed, causing the music to play slower and heavier. "Más o menos al mismo tiempo que DJ Escriu creaba sus primeros mixtaifs de Chopin Escriut, el Monterrey, México, el solidero Gabriel Duñez tras estar reproduciendo discos de convia colombiana a sí ballenatos." ← ¿Por qué el Slowed & Reverb y los Speed Up están rompiendo YT y TikTok？ | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0WWra85DsCo
**Audience Reception and Continuation** : Despite repairing his equipment for subsequent parties, Dueñez was surprised when the audience began requesting the slowed-down music once again. Far from perceiving it as an error, listeners were delighted with the heavy and "calencioso" (warm/passionate) sound, finding it offered an almost psychedelic and more danceable quality for the local public. **Birth of Cumbia Rebajada** : This accidental discovery and its positive reception led to the birth of "cumbia rebajada" (slowed cumbia), a crucial subgenre for understanding Colombian and "cholo" culture. Similar to Houston, a distinctive scene and aesthetic developed around these parties in Monterrey, all without any direct physical or cultural connection to the movements happening elsewhere.
