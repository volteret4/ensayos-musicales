## curiosities
**Autobiography Title** : The autobiography of Brian and Eddie Holland is named after "Come and Get These Memories," despite the team having co-written many bigger and more obvious hits. This highlights the significance of that song as the beginning of the Motown sound. "Brian and Eddie Holland's autobiography is named after this, rather than after any of the bigger and more obvious hits they would like to co-write." ← Episode 111： ＂Heat Wave＂ by Martha and the Vandellas | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AAiUJhSKjzs
**Division of Labor** : Within the Holland, Dozier and Holland team, Brian Holland and Lamont Dozier typically took charge of the musical aspects, including riffs, hooks, melodies, chord changes, and studio work with drummers, keyboardists, and bass players. Eddie Holland primarily concentrated on crafting lyrics, focusing on meaning and "repeat formation." "Holland Dozier and Holland, really Brian Holland and the Montdozier, who took charge of the musical side of things with Eddie concentrating on the lyrics, would make their own." ← Episode 111： ＂Heat Wave＂ by Martha and the Vandellas | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AAiUJhSKjzs
**Motown Domination** : The team of Holland, Dozier and Holland were pivotal in Motown's domination of the American charts throughout the rest of the decade, following the release of their defining "Motown sound" record for Martha and the Van Dellers. They transitioned from minor players to the most important team for the label's commercial success.
