**Longevity of Career** : Despite a decline in popularity after WWII, The Mills Brothers continued to have occasional hits into the late 1960s and performed together into the late 1980s. The last surviving Mills Brother, performing with one of his sons, carried on the family band until his death in 1999, showcasing remarkable career longevity. "Their career was far from over. **Massive Early Success (1930-1939)** : From 1930 through 1939, The Mills Brothers were by far the biggest Black act in the United States. They made multiple appearances on Bing Crosby's radio show, starred in films, and toured the world, establishing themselves as a premier entertainment act. "From 1930 through 1939, they were far and away the biggest black act in the US." ← Episode 6： ＂That's When Your Heartaches Begin＂ by the Ink Spots | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9mYSQaysXRg
**Origin in a Talent Contest** : The Mills Brothers began their unique vocal journey after a talent contest where John Mills lost his kazoo. Improvising, he cupped his hands and imitated a trumpet, leading the brothers to decide to specialize in imitating brass instruments with their voices. "John Mills had lost the kazoo he was going to play. He cupped his hands in front of his mouth and imitated a trumpet." ← Episode 6： ＂That's When Your Heartaches Begin＂ by the Ink Spots | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9mYSQaysXRg
**Pioneers of Vocal Instrumentation** : The Mills Brothers became renowned for their ability to perfectly imitate brass instruments like trumpet, tuba, and trombone using only their voices, often listening to instrumental musicians to copy their voicings. This technique set a standard for vocal groups and influenced many who followed. "They're imitating trumpet, a tuba, and a trombone with their voices, and they'd listen to instrumental musicians and copy their voicings." ← Episode 6： ＂That's When Your Heartaches Begin＂ by the Ink Spots | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9mYSQaysXRg
**World War II Disruption and Decline** : Their international touring schedule led to their eventual downfall when, during a 1939 UK tour, the outbreak of World War II trapped them. Massive transport disruptions and the war itself meant it took them two years to return to the US, by which time their widespread popularity had faded. "It was the touring the world that caused their eventual downfall. They went to play the UK in 1939, and discovered that with World War II eminent, the only ship away from the UK they could get at the end of their tour was one that went to Australia." ← Episode 6： ＂That's When Your Heartaches Begin＂ by the Ink Spots | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9mYSQaysXRg
