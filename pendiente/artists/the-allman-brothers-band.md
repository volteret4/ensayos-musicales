The studio version ran for five minutes, while the live version extended to 22 minutes. The new FM stations of the early 1970s, particularly late-night DJs, would play the entire live version. ## curiosities
**Avoidance of Overt Political Messages** : The Allman Brothers Band generally avoided overtly political messages in their music, setting them apart from some other Southern rock groups. While individual members were politically active, such as Greg Allman's campaign efforts for Jimmy Carter in 1976, their songs maintained a non-political stance. **Duane Allman's Guitar Prowess** : Duane Allman is lauded as "the greatest rock and roll guitar player that ever lived." **Early Label and Radio Struggles** : The band was not signed by a major label and insisted on staying in Georgia, which likely contributed to their difficulty in getting radio airplay, a crucial factor for selling records in the 1970s.
