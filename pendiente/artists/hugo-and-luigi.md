## curiosities
**Children's Records to Pop Producers** : Hugo Peretti and Luigi Criatore, known as Hugo and Luigi, began their careers making children's records. They were later hired by Mercury Records, marking their entry into popular music production. "had started out making children's records, before being hired by Mercury Records" ← Episode 81： ＂Shout＂ by the Isley Brothers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=USJDERCABjk
**Ethically and Musically Appalling Covers** : At Mercury Records, Hugo and Luigi were responsible for producing Georgia Gibbs' cover versions of "Black Records," which were criticized as being "both ethically and musically appalling" for their appropriation and often inferior quality. "The cover versions by Georgia Gibbs of Black Records that we've talked about previously, and which were both ethically and musically appalling." ← Episode 81： ＂Shout＂ by the Isley Brothers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=USJDERCABjk
**Founders and Stake in Roulette Records** : Hugo and Luigi were instrumental in the founding of Roulette Records alongside Morris Levy, George Goldner, and Alan Freed. While Goldner and Freed quickly exited, Hugo and Luigi maintained a significant 50% stake in the new label. "Morris Levy, who was setting up a new label, Roulette, with George Goldner and Alan Freed." ← Episode 81： ＂Shout＂ by the Isley Brothers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=USJDERCABjk
**Lack of Understanding for Rock and Roll** : Despite their success, Hugo and Luigi were perceived as not truly understanding rock and roll music. They frequently produced follow-up singles for acts who had previous hits before working with them, only for those acts to stop having hits once the duo took over production. "While they were there, they showed they didn't really get rock and roll music at all." ← Episode 81： ＂Shout＂ by the Isley Brothers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=USJDERCABjk
**Success with "Poppy Background and Soulful Vocals"** : Although their production style didn't immediately work for The Isley Brothers with "I'm Gonna Knock on Your Door," their combination of a "poppy background and soulful vocals" would later prove very fruitful for another artist they would produce. "That combination of a poppy background and soulful vocals would soon bear a lot of fruit for another artist Hugo and Luigi were going to start working with, but it didn't quite work for the Ayersley's yet." ← Episode 81： ＂Shout＂ by the Isley Brothers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=USJDERCABjk
**Unfair Departure from Roulette** : After approximately a year with Roulette, Hugo and Luigi were poached by RCA. Morris Levy, the label's owner, permitted their departure only on the condition that they surrendered their 50% shares in Roulette for "far less than they were worth," demonstrating an unfair business practice. "Morris Levy let them go, so long as they gave up their shares in Roulette, for far less than they were worth." ← Episode 81： ＂Shout＂ by the Isley Brothers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=USJDERCABjk
