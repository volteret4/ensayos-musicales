# artist - The Diamonds

## albums
**Words of Love - Cover Version** : The Diamonds, a group known for covering tracks by black artists, released a cover version of Buddy Holly's "Words of Love" which reached number 13 on the charts, even before Holly's original version was released. ## songs
**Why Do Fools Fall in Love (Year Unspecified) - Cover Version** : The Diamonds, a Canadian group, released a white cover version of "Why Do Fools Fall in Love" almost immediately after The Teenagers' original. Their version only reached number 12 on the charts, significantly less successful than The Teenagers' original at number 6, signaling a shift in audience preference. ## curiosities
**Adult Performers and Sound** : The Diamonds were an all-adult group, which likely hindered their ability to compete with Frankie Lymon's "novelty sound" of a very young boy singing in falsetto. They followed the trend of white groups, like The Crew Cuts, covering doo-wop songs for the mainstream market.
