## songs
**Love Machine (1975)** : This song was listed among the popular disco songs appearing on the Billboard Hot 100 chart on January 3, 1976. "love machine by the miracles." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 1 - Disco Doesn't Suck (Encore) | https://www.ftr70.com/blog/tag/70s+podcast
**Shop Around (1960)** : This song, written by Smokey Robinson, became Motown's first major hit, reaching number two on the pop charts and selling a million copies. Its success was achieved after Barry Gordy insisted on a new, faster arrangement for a third version of the song, recorded in the middle of the night. "They did, and the second version of Shop Around was pulled, and replaced with the new third version, which went to number two on the pop charts, and sold a million copies." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM

## curiosities
**Backing Track Reusability** : Motown's multitracking system allowed for the reuse of backing tracks. If a song was a hit for The Miracles, The Marvelettes could then use the same backing track for a cover version to fill out an album. "if a song was a hit for, say, the miracles, the marvellettes could then use the same backing track for a cover version of it to fill out an album." ← Episode 111： ＂Heat Wave＂ by Martha and the Vandellas | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AAiUJhSKjzs

**Marvin Tarplan's Role** : Marvin Tarplan, The Primettes' guitarist, was borrowed by Smokey Robinson for a Miracles tour, initiating a 50-year collaboration that saw him contribute to The Miracles' recordings and tours, and co-write many of Robinson's biggest hits.
