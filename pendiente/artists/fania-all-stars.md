## curiosities
**Cheetah Club Performance (1971)** : The Fania All-Stars performed at the Cheetah Club in Manhattan in 1971 before an audience of 4,000 people. This event, despite some reported criticism from old guard musicians towards Willie Colón and Héctor Lavoe's songs, was a success, leading to the release of two albums and a documentary film. "Adalberto Santiago, Chio Feliciano, doctor Laboe, Willy Colon, Ray Barretto, Bobby Valentin y muchos más tocaron en el chita club de Manhattan ante 4000 personas... el evento fue un éxito y se lanzaron dos discos y una película del evento." ← LA FANIA. **Extensive Latin American and Global Touring** : After the success of the Cheetah Club concert and its accompanying film, the Fania All-Stars began extensive tours throughout Latin America. They later expanded their reach globally, performing in Europe, Africa, and even Japan, where they were a sensation, solidifying their status as international stars. "La fanía oflestar comenzó a girar por toda la Tenoamerica... Quidaron por todo el mundo. Además de recorrer casi toda Latinoamérica, llevaron su música a Europa, África y hasta Japón, donde fueron una sensación." ← LA FANIA. El imperio que creó la salsa | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UXx3AvorG-k
**Formation (1971)** : The Fania All-Stars was created in 1971 by Johnny Pacheco as a supergroup, bringing together the leading stars of the Fania Records label. This collective aimed to showcase the best of the label's talent. "En 1971, Johnny Pachico creó un super grupo con las estrellas del sillo al que llamó fanía Oles Tars." ← LA FANIA. El imperio que creó la salsa | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UXx3AvorG-k
**Inclusion of Latin Music Legends** : The Fania All-Stars project expanded to include legendary figures from the "old guard" of Latin music, who held almost mythological status. Notable additions included Celia Cruz and the renowned Tito Puente. "Al proyecto, se sumaron también figuras legendarias de la vieja goberna de la música latina, con un estatus casi mitológico, como Celia Cruz, o el más reticente y topuente." ← LA FANIA. El imperio que creó la salsa | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UXx3AvorG-k
**Proclaimed Birthplace of Salsa** : The Fania All-Stars' performance at the Cheetah Club in 1971 is often cited as the symbolic birthplace of salsa music in the USA, rather than in specific Latin American countries like Panama, Puerto Rico, the Dominican Republic, or Cuba. "La salsa nació esa noche, no en Panamá, Puerto Rico, República Dominicana o Cuba, sino en Estados Unidos." ← LA FANIA. El imperio que creó la salsa | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UXx3AvorG-k
**Unknown in US Mainstream** : Despite their global fame and success, the Fania All-Stars and their individual stars remained largely unknown in their own country, the United States, outside of Latin barrios. Their albums received minimal reviews beyond the Latin music circuit and were not played on generalist radio stations. "Sus estrellas, sigan siendo prácticamente desconocidas en su propia tierra, Estados Unidos... Sus discos apenas recibían reseñas fuera del circuito latino y no son abañen radios generalistas." ← LA FANIA. El imperio que creó la salsa | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UXx3AvorG-k
