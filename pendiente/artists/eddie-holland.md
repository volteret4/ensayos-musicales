# artist - Eddie Holland

## labels

## songs
**Jamie (Year unknown)** : A song co-written by Barrett Strong and Mickey Stevenson, originally recorded by Strong. When Strong left Motown, the label reused the backing track, and Eddie Holland recorded new vocals over it, resulting in a minor hit that reached the top 30. Despite its chart success, Eddie received no royalties because he hadn't yet earned out the production costs from previous flop singles. ## curiosities
**Advocacy for Brian Holland** : In his early time with Barry Gordy, Eddie frequently tried to convince Gordy to take his younger brother Brian seriously, despite Brian being in his early teens and Gordy's skepticism about his talent. "Eddie spent much of his early time working with Gordy, trying to get Gordy to take his little brother seriously." ← Episode 111： ＂Heat Wave＂ by Martha and the Vandellas | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AAiUJhSKjzs
**Dislike of Live Performance** : While Eddie Holland loved singing, he strongly disliked the experience of performing on stage. Despite this, he continued performing, believing it was important not to quit a job until a better opportunity arose. "Holland quickly found that he didn't enjoy performing on stage. He loved singing, but he didn't like the actual experience of being on stage." ← Episode 111： ＂Heat Wave＂ by Martha and the Vandellas | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AAiUJhSKjzs
**Early Singing Career & Jackie Wilson Comparison** : Years before Motown's start, Eddie Holland worked with Barry Gordy as a singer. He was known for a voice similar to Jackie Wilson's, and Gordy initially hired him as a demo singer for songs intended for Wilson, later attempting to mold him into a "Wilson clone" with his first single, "You." "He was a singer who was known for having a similar sounding voice to that of Jackie Wilson, and Gordy had taken him on, first as a sound-like demo singer, recording songs written for Wilson, so Wilson could hear how they would sound in his voice, and later trying to mold him into a Wilson clone, starting with Holland's first single, You." ← Episode 111： ＂Heat Wave＂ by Martha and the Vandellas | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AAiUJhSKjzs
**Motown Tour Guide** : Eddie Holland, a member of the renowned songwriting trio Holland-Dozier-Holland, personally guided Kenny Gamble and Leon Huff through Motown's facilities on Gamble's first plane journey, providing them with insights into the music industry, including how contracts functioned. "Eddie Holland, who was part of the legendary songwriting trio, Holland Dozier Holland with his brother Brian and Lamont Dozier, showed them around." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 37 - Philly Soul and the Sound of the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-37-philly-soul-and-the-sound-of-the-70s
**Part of Holland-Dozier-Holland** : Eddie Holland, Brian Holland's brother, became a new writing partner for Brian, along with Lamont Dozier, effectively forming the renowned Holland-Dozier-Holland songwriting and production team. "Freddie kept finding that when he came round to hit the film after work, if Brian Holland had had an idea for a song, he'd already finished it, usually with the help of his brother ready and their new writing partner, Lamont Dozier." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Songwriting Technique - Repeat Formation** : Eddie Holland developed a songwriting technique he called "repeat formation" after studying Smokey Robinson's writing. He aimed for lyrics where every word mattered, without meaningless filler. To ensure the song's idea was conveyed even if a line was misheard, he would paraphrase the same piece of information two or three times within the lyrics. "So he came up with a technique he referred to as repeat formation, where he would give the same piece of information two or three times, paraphrasing it." ← Episode 111： ＂Heat Wave＂ by Martha and the Vandellas | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AAiUJhSKjzs
**Switch to Songwriting for Royalties** : After his minor hit "Jamie" failed to earn him royalties (due to unearned production costs), but his brother Brian showed a royalty statement for thousands of dollars from songwriting, Eddie realized he was in the wrong job. He disliked performing and was earning nothing, so he took nine months off to teach himself songwriting, only performing the bare contractual minimum. "Eddie decided that he was in the wrong job. He didn't like performing anyway, and his brother was making serious money while he was working away earning nothing." ← Episode 111： ＂Heat Wave＂ by Martha and the Vandellas | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AAiUJhSKjzs
