## songs
**Sweet City Woman (1971)** : The song is not about a woman, but rather about the band's move from Calgary to Toronto, capturing the energy of a new place with city lights. It features a banjo, which is seen as a clever nod to their Calgary origins, a city with a western identity. The song also includes a French phrase, "bonsei bong," as a shout-out to their fans in Montreal. It was a number one hit in Canada for a month in 1971, reaching number one on three different Canadian charts, and peaked at number eight on the Billboard Top 40 in the United States. When the song hit number one, the band was performing in high schools, as there were not many other venues available to them at the time. "Sweet Sweet City Woman. ## curiosities
**Canadian Success vs. American Perception** : From an American perspective, The Stampeders are considered one-hit wonders as they never had another hit in the United States. However, from a Canadian perspective, they are not, having achieved seven more top ten hits in Canada before disbanding in 1980. They were a very popular touring band and toured internationally more than any other Canadian band of their era. While they opened for acts like The Beach Boys and Steely Dan in the US, they were headliners in Canada.
