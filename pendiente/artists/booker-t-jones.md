## curiosities
**Attending University Post-Hit** : Soon after recording "Green Onions," Booker T. Jones went to university. For a couple of years, he could only play on weekends and during holidays, with Isaac Hayes filling in as the studio's weekday keyboard player. "Booker T actually went off to university soon after recording Green onions, so for a couple of years he could only play on weekends and drawing holidays." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Keyboardist of Choice** : Booker T. Jones became frequently called upon to play keyboards at Stax, recommended by Floyd Newman, who hoped that if Jones was on keyboards, he wouldn't be playing baritone sax, thus securing more sax gigs for Newman. "By this point, Bucket-E was being called on a lot to play keyboards. As Floyd Newman recommended him as a reliable piano player, in the hopes that if Jones was on keyboards, he wouldn't be playing Bavito and Sax, so Newman would get more of those gigs." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Multi-Instrumental Prodigy** : Booker T. Jones, a classmate of David Porter at Booker T. Washington High School, was exceptionally talented, capable of playing numerous instruments—including oboe, sax, trombone, double bass, guitar, and keyboards—all to a professional standard. "This classmate was also called Bucketee, Bucketee Jones, and he could play everything. He played Obo, sax, trombone, double bass, guitar, and keyboards, and played them all to a professional standard." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Origins of "Green Onions"** : Booker T. Jones and Steve Cropper were fascinated by a musical lick they heard on the radio while driving, though they could never recall the song it came from. This lick, developed during an impromptu session, became the basis for the instrumental "Green Onions." "Cropper remembered a little musical lick he'd heard on the radio one day when he'd been driving with Booker." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Recruited for a Session from School** : While in high school, Booker T. Jones was dramatically pulled out of class by David Porter to play baritone saxophone on Rufus and Carla Thomas's "Because I Love You," after borrowing a sax from the school's music room. "Porter popped over to the school, walked into the classroom Jones was in, told the teacher that another teacher wanted to see Jones, pulled him out of the class, and told him he was going to make a record." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
