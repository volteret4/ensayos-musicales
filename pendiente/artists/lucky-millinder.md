## curiosities
**Band Acquisition Strategy** : Lucky Millinder had a unique method of forming his orchestra: he would buy out and assume leadership of existing bands, which would then become the Lucky Millinder Orchestra. "The way Melinda worked was that he bought out and took over the leadership of, existing bands, which then became the Lucky Melinda Orchestra." ← Episode 50： ＂Honky Tonk＂, by Bill Doggett | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H2rYnKmeZF0

**Forgotten Figure in Black Music** : Lucky Millinder, though largely overlooked today, was one of the most significant figures in black music during the 1940s, known for his compelling personality and influential role. **Leader of Mills Blue Rhythm Band** : Millinder started his career as the leader of the Mills Blue Rhythm Band, which served as the house band at the Cotton Club, before moving on to lead his own ensemble. "Later on, he'd started out as the leader of the mill's Blue Rhythm band, the house band in the cotton club." ← Episode 7： ＂Good Rockin' Tonight＂ by Wynonie Harris | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IgqSPoYRUCM
**Non-Instrumentalist Leader** : Lucky Millinder served as a frontman and occasional singer for his orchestra but could not play any instrument himself. He relied heavily on his musicians, particularly Bill Doggett, to work out arrangements. "Melinda was a frontman and occasional singer, but he couldn't play an instrument, and was reliant on the musicians in his band to work the arrangements out for him." ← Episode 50： ＂Honky Tonk＂, by Bill Doggett | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H2rYnKmeZF0
**Non-Musician Talent Recognizer** : Millinder did not play instruments, read music, or sing frequently, but he possessed an exceptional ability to identify and cultivate musical talent in others. "What Melinda could definitely do was recognize talent." ← Episode 7： ＂Good Rockin' Tonight＂ by Wynonie Harris | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IgqSPoYRUCM
**Origin of "Lucky" Nickname** : A popular story suggests that Al Capone, after losing $10,000 in dice, was offered good luck by Millinder rubbing the dice. Capone subsequently won $50,000 and called him "Lucky" ever since. However, it's more probable that "Lucky" was a shortening of his birth name, Lucius, with the anecdote reflecting the kind of people Millinder associated with. "One story about how he got his name is that Al Capone was down $10,000 playing dice. Melinda offered to rub the dice for luck, and Capone ended the night $50,000 up and called him lucky from then on." ← Episode 7： ＂Good Rockin' Tonight＂ by Wynonie Harris | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IgqSPoYRUCM
**Pioneer of Rhythm and Blues** : Millinder's band is widely considered a strong candidate for being the first rhythm and blues band, as they uniquely combined boogie, jump, and jive sounds with a powerful blues feeling, specifically designed to make people dance. "And if you want to point to one band that you can call the first rhythm in Blue's band, you probably want to look at Melinda's band, who more than any other band of the era were able to combine all the boogie, jump, and give sounds with a strong blues feeling and get people dancing." ← Episode 7： ＂Good Rockin' Tonight＂ by Wynonie Harris | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IgqSPoYRUCM
