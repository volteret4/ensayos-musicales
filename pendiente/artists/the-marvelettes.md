## songs
**Destination Anywhere (1968)** : This record was released during a period when The Marvelettes continued to have R&B hits but were struggling to break into the Top 100 on the pop charts. The group was facing internal challenges, including Wonder Young's substance abuse and mental health issues. "The group continued having R&B hits, but while they kept releasing great records like Destination anywhere, they were by now barely scraping the heart 100 on the pop charts." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Don't Mess With Bill (1966)** : This single, their first of 1966, marked a resurgence in the group's pop chart success, becoming their first top ten pop hit since "Playboy" in early 1962. Smokey Robinson took over their production and began using Wonder Young as the primary lead vocalist for this and subsequent tracks. "Don't mess with Bill, their first single of 1966, became their first top ten pop hit since Playboy in early 1962." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Forever (Year unknown)** : This song, originating from a collaboration between Brian Holland and Lamont Dozier, was described as a "marvel at Salmon Track" (likely "Smash Track"). It was later released as a B-side and reached the top 30 in its own right. "That song became a marvel at Salmon Track, forever, which a few years later would be put out as a B-side and make the top 30 in its own right." ← Episode 111： ＂Heat Wave＂ by Martha and the Vandellas | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AAiUJhSKjzs
**Locking Up My Heart (Year unknown)** : This was the first single credited to Holland-Dozier-Holland, written and produced for The Marvelettes. It was a "comparative flop" for the group and is associated with the beginning of their "downward slump." "That was a comparative flop for the Marvel Let's, and the beginning of the Downwood Slump we talked about for them in the episode on Please Mr. Postman" ← Episode 111： ＂Heat Wave＂ by Martha and the Vandellas | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AAiUJhSKjzs
**Playboy (1962)** : Originally conceived by Gladys Horton, this song was rewritten by Brian Holland, Robert Bateman, and Mickey Stephenson, propelling the group back into the top 10 on the pop chart (number 7) and number 4 on the R&B chart. It marked a significant return to chart success after "Twistin' Postman" didn't perform as well. "This time it was Gladys, who came up with a song called Playboy, which Brian Holland, Robert Baigman and Mickey Stephenson rewrote, and which made number 7 on the pop chart and number 4 on the R&B chart." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Please Mr. Postman (1961)** : This song became Motown's first number one on the pop charts and its second million-seller, crucially saving the label from potential bankruptcy after earlier follow-ups to "Shop Around" had sold poorly. It was the first record from Motown to distinctly feature "the Motown Sound" and established the label as "the sound of Young America." "Please Mr. Postman became the second million seller for Motown, and its first number one on the pop charts." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM

**The Hunter Gets Captured by the Game (1967)** : Written by Smokey Robinson specifically for Wonder Young, this track continued the trend of Wonder serving as the sole lead vocalist, with Motown's in-house backing vocal group, The Andantes, providing harmonies. This production method was attributed to the group's constant touring schedule. "Robinson also wrote the marvelous The Hunter Gets captured by the game for the group. **Twistin' Postman (1962)** : Released as The Marvelettes' second single, this song was designed to capitalize on the success of "Please Mr. Postman" and the widespread popularity of the "twist craze" at the time. It reached the top 40 on the charts. "Twist in Postman went top 40, but it didn't do anything like as well as please Mr Postman." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM

## curiosities
**Auditioning for Motown** : After placing fourth in a talent contest offering a Motown audition as a prize, The Cassin Yet impressed the label but were told they needed to present original material to be signed. This led to the creation of "Please Mr. Postman," which later secured their contract. "The group came forth, but one of their teachers, Shirley Sharply, knew the person from Motown who was arranging the auditions, and persuaded them to offer auditions to the top five, rather than just to the winners." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Dual Lead Vocal Attempt as The Darnells** : Motown experimented with changing the group's dynamic by releasing a single under the name "The Darnells," featuring Gladys Horton and Wonder Young on dual lead vocals, backed by Motown's in-house group, The Andantes. This endeavor, however, proved unsuccessful. "The record was put out under the name The Darnells, but was unsuccessful." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Early Success Over The Supremes** : The Marvelettes were signed by Motown Records and achieved significant success as "major stars," effectively "leapfrogging" over The Supremes in terms of chart performance and commercial impact during their early careers. "Motown had signed the Maratholettes, and they'd leapfogged over the Supremes to become major stars." ← Episode 116： ＂Where Did Our Love Go？＂ by The Supremes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g2PetxEXQC4
**Erosion of Group Cohesion** : The departure of Juanita and Georgiana significantly impacted the group's internal dynamics, as they had been the "glue" that held the initial members together. Catherine Anderson later noted that the remaining members, who hadn't known each other prior, transitioned from being friends to merely "acquaintances and co-workers." "Juanita and Georgiana had been the glue holding the group together. They'd been the ones who had been friends with all the others." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Followers of The Shirelles' Template** : The Marvelettes are listed as one of the prominent girl groups that followed in the footsteps of The Shirelles, who set the template for the booming girl group sound of the 1960s. "The Supremes, the Marvelettes, the crystals, the Ronettes, and the rest, all were following in the Chirrells footsteps." ← Episode 89： ＂Will You Love Me Tomorrow？＂ by the Shirelles | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Kns2HGcuSg
**Formation as The Cassin Yet** : Gladys Horton formed a new group for a high school talent contest, initially jokingly naming themselves The Cassin Yet. The name, a self-deprecating reference to their perceived vocal abilities ("because they can't sing yet"), was used for their performance, which placed fourth but secured them a Motown audition. "The group she formed originally jokingly named themselves The Cassin Yet, because they can't sing yet, and that was the name under which they performed at the talent contest." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Georgia Dobbins' Father's Refusal to Sign Contract** : Georgia Dobbins, a key contributor to "Please Mr. Postman," was forced to quit the group when her father refused to counter-sign her record contract because she was under 21 and he believed the entertainment industry was "sinful." Her mother was too ill to sign, and this led to Georgia giving up singing entirely until 1978. "Her father hadn't known she was singing, until she brought the record contract home for him to counter sign." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Georgiana Tillman's Illness and Early Death** : Georgiana Tillman, who had been diagnosed with sickle cell anemia as a child, became increasingly ill from the strain of constant touring. She was later diagnosed with lupus, an autoimmune disease that was often a death sentence at the time, leading her to retire from music and work as a secretary for Motown. She died in 1980 at the age of 36. "Eventually it was realised that she had lupus, an autoimmune disease which is now largely treatable if not curable, but at the time was often a death sentence." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Gladys Horton's Departure and Group Priority** : Gladys Horton's decision to leave the group in 1967 lowered The Marvelettes' priority within Motown. Although Wonder Young was the lead singer by then, the group was still informally considered "Gladys' group" by Motown staff due to her initial leadership role. "This made the group an even lower priority for Motown. While Wander was by now the undisputed lead singer, within Motown there were thought of as Gladys' group, as she'd been the leader in the beginning." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Impact of Touring on Recording Practices** : By the mid-1960s, while records were still released as by The Marvelettes, Smokey Robinson often only utilized Wonder Young for lead vocals, with The Andantes providing backing. This practice was justified by the group's constant touring schedule, making it easier to record without the full lineup. "By this point, while the records were getting released as by The Marvellettes, Robinson was only using Wander for lead vocals, and having the Andante's single or backing vocals." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Juanita's Departure Due to American Bandstand Mockery** : Juanita (Winnie Cowatt) left the group after a humiliating experience on "American Bandstand." She mistakenly said Detroit was a suburb of Inkster instead of the other way around, leading to mockery that caused her to have a breakdown. "She felt so bad about this slip-up and the way she was mocked for it, so she had a breakdown and ended up leaving the group." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Lack of Recognition at Motown Anniversaries** : Despite being the first group to achieve a number one hit for Motown, none of the original Marvelettes members were invited to participate in any of the label's 25th, 40th, 50th, or 60th-anniversary celebrations or associated TV specials, which honored the label's history. "None of the members of the first group to hit number one on the label were invited to be part of any of them." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Motor City Records Reunion Album** : In the late 1980s, British DJ Ian Levine, who owned Motor City Records, persuaded Gladys Horton and Wonder Young to come out of retirement to record a new Marvelettes album. This reunion effort featured two new backing vocalists, Echo Johnson and Jean McLean, and included remakes of old hits and new songs. "He managed to persuade Gladys and Wanda out of retirement to make a new Marvillette's album with two new backing vocalists, Echo Johnson and Jean McLean." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Motown's Policy on Group Members and Touring** : After "Please Mr. Postman" became a hit, Motown initially suggested sending "any five girls" on the road as The Marvelettes, a proposition the actual members rejected. The label later replaced a pregnant Wonder Young on tour with Florence Ballard from The Supremes, ensuring that touring contracts for the group specified "five Marvelettes." "Not only that, but at one point when Wonder had been pregnant, Motown had replaced him on the road with Florence Ballard from the Supremes." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Name Change to The Marvelettes** : The group's name, The Cassin Yet, was deemed unsuitable, and Barry Gordy supposedly renamed them The Marvelettes after hearing them sing and exclaiming, "those girls are marvels." This new name marked their official birth as a Motown act. "The name they were eventually given supposedly came after Barry Gordy heard them sing it and said, those girls are marvels." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Origin as The Delvid Matt** : Gladys Horton, at just 14 years old, formed an earlier group named The Delvid Matt in Inkster, Michigan. This group released one single before disbanding, but Gladys remained determined to pursue music. "When Horton was only 14, she had formed a group called The Delvid Matt, who made one single, and the Delvid Matt had split up." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Outperforming The Supremes** : The Marvelettes consistently achieved greater chart success than The Supremes for a two-year period, securing hits while The Supremes struggled to break into the Top 100. "the Supremes now being passed in chart success, not only by the Marvelettes." ← Episode 116： ＂Where Did Our Love Go？＂ by The Supremes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g2PetxEXQC4
**Rejection of "Where Did Our Love Go"** : The Marvelettes, including member Catherine Anderson, were offered the backing track for "Where Did Our Love Go" by Holland-Dozier-Holland for a potential album, but they chose to reject the "dreadful song," opting for other material instead. They subsequently advised The Supremes to avoid it if possible. "The Marvelettes were played a couple of backing tracks, which have been proposed as their next recording, and they chose to dump the 8 bar one." ← Episode 116： ＂Where Did Our Love Go？＂ by The Supremes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g2PetxEXQC4
**Shift to Wonder Young as Sole Lead Vocalist** : After a period of less successful follow-ups, Smokey Robinson took over the group's production and decided to make Wonder Young the primary lead vocalist, replacing Gladys Horton, who had sung lead on their earlier hits. This change coincided with a second commercial wind for the group as a trio. "Smokey Robinson took over their production and decided to start using Wander as the lead vocalist, rather than Gladys, who had sung lead on their hits up to that point." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Unannounced Group Split** : The Marvelettes informally dissolved without any official announcement; they simply stopped receiving bookings for tours and went their separate ways. Motown, owning the group name, subsequently sold it off, allowing other groups to tour under The Marvelettes' name without any original members. "The groups split up though nothing was announced, they just didn't get booked and anymore tours, and went their separate ways." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Wanda Young's Personal Tragedies and Mental Health Decline** : Wonder Young experienced a series of personal crises, including cocaine and alcohol abuse, the shooting death of her younger sister, and having her drink spiked during a European tour. These events severely impacted her mental health, leading to lasting problems for over 50 years and rendering her unable to perform regularly for decades. "She was never the same again, and has had mental health problems for the last 50 years." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
