## curiosities
**Acquisition of George Goldner's Companies** : Levy initially owned 50% of George Goldner's record companies. As Goldner accumulated significant gambling debts, he used his record labels to pay them off, leading Levy to eventually acquire full ownership of all Goldner's companies, including any associated song rights. **Attempt to Trademark "Rock and Roll"** : Driven by his desire to monetize intellectual property, Levy collaborated with his friend Alan Freed in an attempt to trademark the phrase "rock and roll." Their aim was to force any record labeled as such to pay them for its usage, but the term spread too rapidly to be successfully enforced. **Founding Birdland and Music Publishing** : In the 1940s, Levy founded Birdland, the world-famous jazz club. When ASCAP sought performance royalties for songwriters, Levy immediately recognized the profit potential in music publishing. He subsequently established his own publishing company, Patricia Music, and record label, Roulette, specifically to exploit artists' intellectual property for long-term gain. "Levy had been the founder of Birdland, the world famous jazz club, in the 1940s, but when ASCAP came to him...
