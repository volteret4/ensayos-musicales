## albums
**Time for another (1975)** : This album was the follow-up to Ace's hit single "How Long" and was generally considered a disappointment. According to Paul King, the album lacked energy, likely because the band went straight into the studio after an exhausting tour with Yes. In 1977, King expressed a desire to forget they ever recorded the album and wished they could re-record the songs. ## songs
**How Long (1975)** : Lyrically, "How Long" appears to be about infidelity, but it was actually written by Paul Carrack about internal band turmoil. The inspiration came from an incident where bassist Tex Comer was considering joining two other bands at a time when Ace was already facing problems due to their drummer having quit. Carrack described the band's state in 1975 to Rolling Stone magazine, saying, "we were really at the bottom, quite down with various problems." He sketched out the lyrics on the back of a bus ticket while traveling to see his future mother-in-law. The song's laid-back groove made it a popular soft rock track on radio stations in the mid-70s, drawing comparisons to bands like Little River Band and Steely Dan. Ellen King, who sang vocals and played guitar for Ace, noted that the song had an "American feel to it." It made its chart debut in March 1975 and reached number three on the Billboard Top 40 on May 31, 1975. "How long has this been going on? ## curiosities
**Differing Views on Success** : Paul King, a member of Ace, did not believe the success of "How Long" came "too much too soon," stating that the band had been "slogging through clubs for two years" and were ready for success. However, Paul Carrack, the songwriter, later reflected in the early 1980s that success with Ace "was too much too soon" and that the band "took a lot of hard knocks on the way down."
