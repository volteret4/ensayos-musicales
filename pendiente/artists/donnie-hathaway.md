## albums
**Donnie Hathaway Live (1971)** : Considered one of the greatest live solo recordings of the 1970s, Side One of this album was recorded at The Troubadour in 1971. Although recorded in 1971, the complete cut was not released until after his death in 1979. **Gospel vs. Pop Dilemma** : Throughout his career, Hathaway struggled to reconcile his deeply gospel-oriented background, as noted by his high school band director, with the demands of making pop music. This internal conflict led him to consistently include at least one gospel song on his records. "He had a really hard time reconciling making pop music instead of gospel. So he always included a gospel song on his records." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 41 - The Impact of The Troubadour on 1970s Popular Culture | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-41-the-impact-of-the-troubadour-on-1970s-popular-culture
**Howard University Scholarship** : In the fall of 1963, Hathaway enrolled in classes at Howard University, having received a fine art scholarship primarily due to his exceptional piano skills. Initially, he considered paths like teaching or preaching, but music was ingrained in his DNA. "In the fall of 1963, he enrolled in classes at Howard University, thanks in part to a fine art scholarship because of his piano skills." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 41 - The Impact of The Troubadour on 1970s Popular Culture | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-41-the-impact-of-the-troubadour-on-1970s-popular-culture
**Live Performance Style** : Like a true jazz musician, Donnie Hathaway possessed a seemingly innate ability to make each live performance a unique experience. David Ritz, author of a short book about "Donnie Hathaway Live," emphasized that the live Donnie was the most moving, unadorned, and direct representation of his artistry.
