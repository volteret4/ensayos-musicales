## curiosities
**Creation of Chopped and Screwed Sound** : DJ Screw's experimentation involved repeating long phrases with pauses and stretching beats until the songs seemed to "melt," giving the music a new, dreamlike and narcotic meaning. This unique manipulation of tempo and pitch became known as "Chopped and Screwed," with "Chop" referring to the cuts, repetitions, and jumps in phrases, and "Screw" to the dragged, deep tempo. **Early Experiments with Hip Hop (Early 1990s)** : DJ Screw, a pivotal figure in the Houston, Texas music scene, began experimenting with his hip hop records in the early 1990s. He developed a technique of mixing two copies of the same record simultaneously at a significantly slower tempo, typically around 60-70 BPMs, resulting in a unique, languid sound. **Legendary Mixtapes and Local Scene Growth** : His now-legendary mixtapes on cassette began circulating, with people sharing and re-recording them.
