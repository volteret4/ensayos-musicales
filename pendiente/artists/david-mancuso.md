# artist - David Mancuso

## curiosities
**Atmosphere Creation** : Mancuso created atmosphere in his loft using music, lights, and even changes in temperature. He was unable to sell alcohol or food, and drugs were less common than at later clubs like Studio 54. The core vibe came from the music, an amalgamation of different styles united by a common beat. "Mankuso used music and lights and even changes in temperature to create atmosphere." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 1 - Disco Doesn't Suck (Encore) | https://www.ftr70.com/blog/tag/70s+podcast
**Loft as Pre-Disco Birthplace (1970)** : David Mancuso's loft at 647 Broadway in New York is regarded as the birthplace of the pre-disco era. Mancuso, an introverted man, opened his home to all, regardless of sexual orientation or race, inviting them to dance. "David Mankuso's loft in New York is probably the place where predisco is born." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 1 - Disco Doesn't Suck (Encore) | https://www.ftr70.com/blog/tag/70s+podcast
**Sanctuary for Marginalized Communities** : The Loft was the beginning of "members-only sanctuaries" that particularly attracted gay men, but also welcomed people of all races and genders, legitimizing the presence of the queer community. These spaces offered refuge where individuals could dance freely without fear. "The loft was the beginning of the members only sanctuaries that attracted gay men in particular, because it was places like the loft, the sanctuary, paradise garage that welcomed even legitimized the presence of the queer community." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 1 - Disco Doesn't Suck (Encore) | https://www.ftr70.com/blog/tag/70s+podcast
**Valentine's Day 1970 Party** : On Valentine's Day, 1970, David Mancuso hosted a party at his loft. Invitations carried the message "love saves the day," aiming to create a safe space for people to enjoy each other's company and dance. "On Valentine's Day, 1970, David Mankuso opened up his loft at 647 Broadway, and he had a party.
