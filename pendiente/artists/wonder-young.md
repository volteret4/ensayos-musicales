## curiosities
**Ascension to Lead Vocalist** : After a period of declining pop chart success, Smokey Robinson took over the production of The Marvelettes and made the strategic decision to utilize Wonder Young as the primary lead vocalist, replacing Gladys Horton. This shift led to a "second commercial wind" for the group. "Smokey Robinson took over their production and decided to start using Wander as the lead vocalist, rather than Gladys, who had sung lead on their hits up to that point." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Enduring Mental Health Problems** : The series of personal tragedies and abuses left Wonder Young with profound and lasting mental health problems that persisted for 50 years. This significantly impacted her ability to perform regularly for decades. "She was never the same again, and has had mental health problems for the last 50 years." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Exclusion from Motown Anniversaries** : Despite her significant role, Wonder Young was not invited to any of Motown's milestone anniversary celebrations. "None of the members of the first group to hit number one on the label were invited to be part of any of them." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Pregnancy and Touring Replacement** : During her time with The Marvelettes, Wonder Young became pregnant, leading Motown to temporarily replace her on tour with Florence Ballard of The Supremes. This ensured the group could fulfill their contracts requiring five members on the road. "At one point when Wonder had been pregnant, Motown had replaced him on the road with Florence Ballard from the Supremes." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Replacement of Georgia Dobbins** : Wonder Young joined The Marvelettes as the replacement for Georgia Dobbins, who had to leave the group due to her father's refusal to sign her contract. She became an integral part of the lineup from this point forward. "By the time they came back to Motown with the beginnings of a song, Georgia had been replaced by Wonder Young" ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Reunion Recording and Later Performance** : In the late 1980s, Ian Levine convinced a still-unwell Wonder Young to participate in a Marvelettes reunion album for Motor City Records alongside Gladys Horton. While too ill for regular touring at the time, her mental health has reportedly improved in recent years, allowing her to perform again. "Wanda was still too well to perform regularly, but Gladys went out on tour on the oldies' circuit, singing her old hit as Gladys Horton of the Marvillette, as none of the group owned the original name." ← Episode 93： ＂Please Mr. Postman＂ by the Marvelettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPg8Wx30-uM
**Substance Abuse and Personal Tragedies** : Wonder Young faced severe personal struggles including cocaine and alcohol abuse, which caused her to start acting strangely. Further trauma included her younger sister being shot dead (mistakenly for her older sister) and her own drink being spiked during a European tour. "Anne Wanda was having problems.
