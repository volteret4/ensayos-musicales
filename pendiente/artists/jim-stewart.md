## curiosities
**"What'd I Say" Damascene Conversion** : Jim Stewart experienced a profound shift in his musical direction after hearing Ray Charles's "What'd I Say," produced by Jerry Wexler. This record solidified his commitment to an R&B style for his company. "He'd recently had a sort of damacine conversion after hearing that record, and was now fully committed to his company's new R&B style." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Barber's Business Advice** : A tip from his barber, Irwin Ellis, about the lucrative nature of owning song publishing rights, rather than just selling records, significantly influenced Stewart's approach to the music business. This financial insight appealed to him given his background in banking. "Alice explained to Stuart that he was still getting checks every few months, because he owned the publishing for this song that someone else had written and wrote to him." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Conflict with Chips Moman** : Jim Stewart was involved in a dispute with Chips Moman, who claimed he had been promised a third of the company but Stewart refused. This disagreement led to Moman's departure from Stax Records. "he also claims that he had been promised a third of the company, but Jim Stewart changed his mind and refused to cut him in." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Early Life and Influences** : Jim Stewart was a part-time fiddle player deeply influenced by Bob Wills' western swing music. Despite his musical passion, he worked a day job at a bank as music wasn't providing a stable income. "Stewart was in a western swing band and was hugely influenced by Bbock Will's, but he wasn't making any real money from music." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Founding Satellite Records** : Stewart pooled savings with a singing DJ and a rhythm guitarist, raising $1,000 to release a record. They named their label Satellite Records, a name nobody liked but was chosen due to Sputnik's recent launch. "They came up with the name satellite records for their label. Nobody liked it, but they couldn't think of anything better, and satellites were in the news with the recent launch of Sputnik." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Reluctant Release of Otis Redding's Debut** : Jim Stewart was initially unimpressed by Otis Redding's first recording session, describing the A-side as "a fun but unoriginal little Richard sound alike." He only agreed to release the record as a favor to promoter Joe Galkin, not anticipating the B-side would become a hit. "Jim Stewart wasn't particularly impressed with the results, but he owed Galkin a favour, so he released the record." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Restarting Satellite Records** : After initial failures and setbacks, Stewart convinced his sister Estelle Axton to buy out his business partners. With Axton's financial backing from remortgaging her house, and Chips Moman's studio expertise, Satellite Records was re-launched in a barber's storage space. "Stuart Mice persuaded his sister, Estelle Axton, to buy out his business partners." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Stax Records Name Origin** : When Satellite Records faced a trademark conflict with another label of the same name, Jim Stewart, who disliked the original name anyway, decided to rename the label. The new name, Stax Records, was created using the first two letters of his surname (Stewart) and his sister Estelle Axton's surname. "Jim Stewart had never liked satellite as a name anyway, and so they quickly reissued the record with a new label, named after the first letters of Jim Stewart and Asterl Axton's surnames. Stax Records was born." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Wife's Uncle's Garage Studio** : The initial recordings for Satellite Records, including Fred Byler's "Blue Roses," took place in Jim's wife's uncle's garage using Irwin Ellis's tape recorder. This makeshift studio ceased to be available when Ellis moved and the uncle needed his garage back. "they hired Alice in his tape recorder to record it in Jim's wife's uncle's garage." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Witnessing Elvis's Rise** : Stewart had been part of a house band at a venue where Elvis Presley played in his early career. He observed Elvis's rapid ascent from an obscure local artist to a global superstar, which fueled his attraction to the music industry. "One of the gigs he'd had was in the house band at a venue where Elvis sometimes played in his early years, and he'd seen how Elvis had gone from an obscure local boy all the way to the biggest star in the world." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
