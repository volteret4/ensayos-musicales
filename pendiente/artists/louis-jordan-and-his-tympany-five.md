## curiosities
**Influence on Decline of Big Bands** : The success of Louis Jordan and His Tympany Five, and their ability to produce a full sound with a smaller, cheaper lineup, contributed significantly to the decline of the big band era, as noted by Clarence "Gatemouth" Brown. **Pioneering "Jump Band Music" Sound** : Louis Jordan's Tympany Five developed a unique sound, distinct from other contemporary bands, which became known as "jump band music." Their original lineup of Jordan plus five instruments (trumpet, sax, piano, bass, drums) managed to sound almost as full as larger big bands.
