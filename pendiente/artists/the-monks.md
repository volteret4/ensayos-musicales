# artist - The Monks

## members
- 5 ex-US soldiers

## genres
- Garage Rock

## albums
**Black Monk Time (1966)** : Released in 1966, this was the Monks' only album, and it presented a unique blend of garage rock, obsessive distortion, and lyrics that bordered on political Dadaism. The music industry and the public struggled to understand their atypical sound and imagery, leading to them being largely ignored, discarded, and forgotten for a period. ## curiosities
**Anti-Psychedelic Image and Message** : Formed in Germany during the 1960s by five former American soldiers, The Monks adopted a completely atypical aesthetic. They shaved their crowns like medieval monks, wore black habits, and positioned themselves as explicitly anti-psychedelic during the peak of the Flower Power era, singing about war, human idiocy, and modern meaninglessness while others focused on love and spiritual expansion.
