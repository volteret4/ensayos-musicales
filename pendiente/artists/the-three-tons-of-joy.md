## curiosities
**Disputed Membership** : The exact identity of the other two members of The Three Tons of Joy, besides Marie Adams, remains a subject of debate among reliable sources. Some refer to them as sisters Sadie and Francine McKinley, while others identify them as Adams's two sisters, Doris and Francine. "There seems to be some debate about the identity of the other two in the three tons of joy. I have seen reliable sources refer to them as two sisters, Sadie and Francine McKinley, and, as Adams' two sisters, Doris and Francine, and have no way of determining which of these is correct." ← REUPLOAD Episode 71： ＂Willie and the Hand Jive＂ by Johnny Otis | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ez6BCUlI6Rg
**Formation and Naming** : The Three Tons of Joy were a vocal trio formed by Johnny Otis, with Marie Adams as the lead singer, after Adams was dropped by P-Cock Records. They were given their name because all members were "extremely fat." They were part of Otis's show from 1953 until 1960. "kept her in his band, as the lead singer of a vocal trio, the three tons of joy, who were so called because they were all extremely fat." ← REUPLOAD Episode 71： ＂Willie and the Hand Jive＂ by Johnny Otis | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ez6BCUlI6Rg
**Synchronized Dancing** : The trio was known for their synchronized dancing routines, which they performed even when they were not singing as part of Johnny Otis's show. They remained with his show until 1960. "The three of them would do synchronised dancing, even when they weren't singing, and they remained with Otis' show until 1960." ← REUPLOAD Episode 71： ＂Willie and the Hand Jive＂ by Johnny Otis | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ez6BCUlI6Rg
**UK TV Appearance and Hand Jive Demonstration** : The Three Tons of Joy traveled to the UK to appear on the "6-5 Special" TV show, backed by British musicians. During their time there, they also scouted potential tour venues with Johnny Otis's manager. Crucially, they demonstrated the "Hand Jive" dance to Otis, which inspired him to write the song "Willie and the Hand Jive." They also demonstrated the dance during Otis's TV shows and stage performances.
