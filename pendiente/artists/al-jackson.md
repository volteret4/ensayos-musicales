**Experienced Jazz and R&B Drummer** : Al Jackson was an older black musician who had honed his skills playing jazz and R&B throughout the 1950s. He had previously played with Booker T. Jones in Willie Mitchell's band. **First Full-Time Musician at Stax** : Stax eventually agreed to pay Al Jackson a salary instead of one-off session fees, making him the first musician employed by the label as a full-time player. This marked a significant commitment from the label to its house band. **Reluctance to Play Sessions** : Jackson initially resisted playing sessions at Stax, believing they would be less lucrative than his regular gigging with Willie Mitchell's band. He wanted to avoid wasting his time on potentially unrewarding work.
