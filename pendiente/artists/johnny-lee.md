# artist - Johnny Lee

## songs
**Looking for Love (1980)** : Despite being hated by critics and rejected by 20 other singers, Johnny Lee's recording of "Looking for Love" became the song most identified with "Urban Cowboy." It was a top five pop hit and spent three weeks at number one on the country charts in 1980. Its release was deliberately delayed until after the movie to ensure audiences understood its context. The lyrics reflect a search for genuine connection: "Well I spent a lot of time looking for you single bars and good time lovers never true. Playin' a fool's game open to win and tellin' those sweet lies and losing again. I was lookin' for love in all the wrong places lookin' for love in too many faces searching for rights lookin' for traces but I'm dreaming of open to find a friend that I love her." ## curiosities
**Urban Cowboy Music Impact** : Johnny Lee, singer of "Looking for Love," stated that "Urban Cowboy" brought out a lot of great music and "put country back on the map again." He observed that it attracted listeners of all ages and continues to resonate with new generations who discover the movie.
