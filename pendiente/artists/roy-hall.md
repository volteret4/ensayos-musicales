## curiosities
**Co-Author of "Whole Lotta Shakin' Going On"** : Roy Hall, a white pianist, claimed co-authorship of "Whole Lotta Shakin' Going On" with Dave Curly Williams, both knowing each other. They were supposedly inspired while drunk and milking rattlesnakes on holiday in Pahokie, Florida, with the opening line originating from someone saying "we got 21 drums, we got an old bass horn, and they even keep in time on a ding dong" when a bell rang for dinner. "two people both claimed to have written the song, a black singer called Dave Curly Williams, and a white pianist called Roy Hall, both of whom knew each other, and both of whom are now credited as the song's writers." ← Episode 59： ＂Whole Lotta Shakin' Goin' On＂ by Jerry Lee Lewis | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wiSA3J9iJ7M
**Loss of Royalties** : After "Whole Lotta Shakin' Going On" became a hit, Roy Hall's ex-wife sued him and was awarded all of his share of the song's royalties.
