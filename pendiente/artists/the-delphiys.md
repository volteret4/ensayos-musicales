## curiosities
**Formation and Early Dynamics** : Martha Reeves joined The Delphiys with three other singers: Gloria Williams (or Williamson), Annette Beard, and Rosalind Ashford. Despite personality clashes, their voices blended well, and they worked effectively on stage. Gloria Williams was initially the leader and lead singer. "The group found out early on that they didn't particularly get on with each other as people, their personalities were all too different, but their voices blended well and they worked well on stage." ← Episode 111： ＂Heat Wave＂ by Martha and the Vandellas | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AAiUJhSKjzs
**Lineup Fluctuations and Local Success** : The Delphiys experienced lineup changes with members coming and going, though the accounts of who was in the group when are often disputed based on credit claims rather than strict truth. The group never achieved more than local success. "No matter who was in the group though, they never had more than local success." ← Episode 111： ＂Heat Wave＂ by Martha and the Vandellas | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AAiUJhSKjzs
**Motown Audition** : When Motown's usual female backing group, The Andantes, were unavailable, Martha Reeves called the rest of The Delphiys (who may have been split up) to fill in. They auditioned for Barry Gordy, who was happy with them and signed them to Motown, though he wanted a new name due to potential contract issues with Checkmate Records. "They had to audition for Barry Gordy, but Gordy was perfectly happy with them and signed them to Motown." ← Episode 111： ＂Heat Wave＂ by Martha and the Vandellas | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AAiUJhSKjzs
**Talent Contest Rivalries** : The group performed at amateur nights and talent contests, developing rivalries with The Primes (who became The Temptations) and The Primettes (who became The Supremes). These three groups often took turns winning the contests. "developed a rivalry with two other groups.
