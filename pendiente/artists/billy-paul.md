# artist - Billy Paul

## labels
- Philadelphia International Records

## songs
**Am I Black Enough for You (1973)** : Released as the follow-up to his international hit "Me and Mrs. Jones," this funky song, characterized by a driving baseline and direct lyrics advocating for change and against poverty, proved to be a commercial flop. Billy Paul attributed its failure to "limited appeal" among white audiences, though it did find popularity among his African American fanbase. **Me and Mrs. Jones (1972)** : Presented as a slow jam by Gamble and Huff, this song initially puzzled jazz singer Billy Paul, who questioned why he was being offered a pop song about an affair. However, he agreed to record it due to his personal marital issues, which allowed him to relate to the theme, and it subsequently became a number one hit for three consecutive weeks in 1972. ## curiosities
**"Gray Eyed" Philly Soul** : In 1978, Billy Paul characterized Philadelphia soul as "gray eyed," implying that it was, and always had been, a racially integrated musical form, appealing across diverse audiences despite some songs specifically targeting Black communities. "Paul said in 1978 that at that all Philadelphia soul is what he called gray eyed meaning racially integrated and it always has been." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 37 - Philly Soul and the Sound of the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-37-philly-soul-and-the-sound-of-the-70s
**Criticism of "Am I Black Enough for You" Release** : Billy Paul openly blamed Kenny Gamble and Clive Davis for releasing "Am I Black Enough for You" as the successor to his international hit "Me and Mrs. Jones," contending that its explicitly targeted appeal to Black audiences limited its commercial reach and consequently hindered his subsequent career.
