## songs
**Louie Louie (Cover)** : The Whalers' version of "Louie Louie" became the definitive template for almost every subsequent cover. Although released as a Rockin' Robin solo record due to contractual reasons, it featured the full band. Rockin' Robin uniquely tracked down Richard Berry's original record to learn the lyrics, an ironic detail given the later controversy over unintelligible lyrics. "But it was their version of Louis Louis that became the template for almost every version that ever followed." ← Episode 106：＂Louie Louie＂ by the Kingsmen | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S3agGJsxjpE
**Tall Cool One (1959)** : The Whalers recorded this self-penned instrumental in 1959, which became a top 40 hit. This was their only chart success. ## curiosities
**"Fabulous Whalers" Distinction** : The group is often referred to as "The Fabulous Whalers" to differentiate them from Bob Marley's later, more renowned group of the same name. **Rockin' Robin's Post-Music Career and Death** : After his music career, Rockin' Robin became an associate professor of biochemistry. He tragically died young in a car crash in 1967. "Rockin Robbins went on to become an associate professor of biochemistry, before dying far too young in a car crash in 1967." ← Episode 106：＂Louie Louie＂ by the Kingsmen | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S3agGJsxjpE
**Spanish Castle Residency and Jimi Hendrix Influence** : The Whalers held a residency at the local venue, The Spanish Castle. Their exceptional live shows there were later commemorated by regular audience member Jimi Hendrix in his song "Spanish Castle Magic." "The shows at that venue were so good that several years later, one of the regular audience members, Jimmy Hendrix, would commemorate them in the song Spanish Castle Map Jic." ← Episode 106：＂Louie Louie＂ by the Kingsmen | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S3agGJsxjpE
