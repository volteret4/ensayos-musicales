## genres

## curiosities
**Described as Funk's "Id"** : Parliament Funkadelic was seen as the "id" of funk, in contrast to Earth Wind & Fire's "super-ego." *The Rolling Stone Encyclopedia of Rock and Roll* characterized them as EWF's "ideological rivals," noting their "down and dirty, but equally eclectic" style. **Focus on Deep Funk over Pop Crossover** : George Clinton's dual bands, Parliament and Funkadelic, never achieved the major pop crossover success of Earth Wind & Fire, as neither group landed a Top 10 hit on the Hot 100. However, away from the pop spotlight, Clinton led an elaborate and "crazy funk revolution" with equally spacey LP covers, making P-Funk the prototype for deep 70s funk fans.
