# artist - Anita Ward

## genres
- Disco

## songs
**Ring My Bell (1979)** : This song was originally written by Frederick Knight for Stacey Lattisaw, who was 11 years old at the time (and 14 when she later covered "Love on a Two Way Street" in 1981). Anita Ward, then 23 and a teacher, was initially hesitant about the song. It was rewritten with more adult lyrics, having originally been conceived about teenagers talking on the phone, and then given the "disco treatment." The use of a synthetic drum machine and Ward's unique, high soprano voice are credited with significantly contributing to its success. The lyrics include: "I'm glad you're home. Now did you really miss me? I guess you did by the look in your eye." The song was tested in dance clubs and "caught fire right away," with a Memphis newspaper comparing its impact to how Elvis caught on. When the song was climbing the charts, Ward was asked to perform on Midnight Special but initially hesitated due to her teaching commitments. It won a Grammy in 1980 for Best R&B Female Vocal Performance, but Ward had no further hits. "I'm glad you're home. Now did you really miss me? ## curiosities
**Teacher's Dilemma** : When her song "Ring My Bell" was climbing the charts, Anita Ward, who was a teacher, was asked to get on a plane to perform on *Midnight Special*. She expressed hesitation due to it being the school year.
