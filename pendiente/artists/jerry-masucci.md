# artist - Jerry Masucci

## curiosities
**Co-Founder of Fania Records (1964)** : Jerry Masucci, an Italian-American ex-policeman and lawyer, partnered with Johnny Pacheco to found Fania Records in 1964. Pacheco, lacking capital, proposed the business venture to Masucci. "Por eso, le propuso su abogado Jerry Maswichi, un expolicía ítolo americano que se asociaran para montar el negocio. Y así, en 1964, Nace, Paniar Records." ← LA FANIA. El imperio que creó la salsa | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UXx3AvorG-k
**Commissioned "El Cantante"** : Faced with Héctor Lavoe's severe addiction problems in 1978, Masucci commissioned Rubén Blades, then a rising star, to compose a special song for the Puerto Rican singer. This resulted in "El Cantante," a song that revitalized Lavoe's career. "Masuchi, encargó a su nueva estrella Rubén Blades que compusiera una canción especial para Puerto Riqueño." ← LA FANIA. **Initial Reluctance to Siembra's Sound** : Masucci was initially hesitant about the innovative sound presented on Willie Colón and Rubén Blades' album *Siembra*. However, his reservations proved unfounded as the album became the label's best-selling record. "Masuichi... fue en un principio reticente al sonido de siembra, pero se equivocaba." ← LA FANIA. **Organizer of Yankee Stadium Concert** : To celebrate the second anniversary of the Fania All-Stars' Cheetah Club performance, Masucci rented Yankee Stadium to organize a concert intended to definitively consolidate the Fania phenomenon. However, the event was plagued by overcrowding and disorder. "Jerry Masuichi quisiera un más lejos, alquilo el Yankee Stadium para organizar el concierto que debía consolidar definitivamente el fenómeno fanía." ← LA FANIA. **Sole Owner of Fania Records** : Masucci eventually became the sole owner of Fania Records, acquiring Johnny Pacheco's share as Pacheco wished to dedicate himself solely to artistic direction. This gave Masucci complete control over the label's operations. "Masuichi, quien para entonces, llega el único propietario de la fans y a transcomprarles su parte a Johnny Pacheco, que prefería dedicarse exclusivamente a la dirección artística." ← LA FANIA. El imperio que creó la salsa | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UXx3AvorG-k
**Sued by Artists and End of New Releases** : In the 1980s, Masucci faced lawsuits from numerous musicians, including Rubén Blades, over unpaid royalties. Overwhelmed by litigation and dealing with the "egos of 35 groups," he decided to cease releasing new music at the beginning of that decade, effectively ending Fania's active era in salsa. "Los músicos, entre ellos Rubén Blades, presentaron demandas contra Masuchi por impagos de regalías... Masuchi, cansado de lidiar con los hegos de 35 grupos y completamente desbordado por los litigios, decidió dejar deditar música nueva a principios de esa década, marcando así el fin de la heradora de la salsa." ← LA FANIA.
