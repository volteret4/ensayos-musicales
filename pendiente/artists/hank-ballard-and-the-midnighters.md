**The Twist (1959)** : Initially released in early 1959 as the B-side of the ballad "Teardrops on Your Letter," this song reached number 16 on the R&B chart. It was recorded in late 1958 and stemmed from a 15-bar blues song by the Sensational Nightingales, which Ballard and guitarist Cal Green adapted to a pre-existing 12-bar melody previously used on their song "Is Your Love For Real." This melody had a long ancestry, inspired by Clyde McPhatter and The Drifters and showing influence from The Radio 4. Initially, it featured a Jimmy Reed-style blues beat, which was changed for the studio recording in November 1958 with a new intro. An earlier demo for VJ Records reportedly contained sexually suggestive lyrics from the Nightingales' original. Ballard was credited as the sole writer, omitting the Nightingales, Green, and other contributing songwriters. Years later, King Records overdubbed an identical saxophone part onto Ballard's track and reissued it to make it sound more like Chubby Checker's successful version, which led to Ballard's version reaching number 28 on the pop charts. "It was released with Ballard as the sole credited writer as the base side of a ballad called Teardrops on Your Letter." ← Episode 91： ＂The Twist＂ by Chubby Checker | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LzsS8EXdsm8

## curiosities
**Decline by 1958** : By 1958, Hank Ballard and the Midnighters were largely a forgotten group. Their R&B style, rooted in the LA sound pioneered by Johnny Otis, had been superseded by newer sounds from Memphis, Chicago, and New York. They had not had a hit in three years and were concerned about being dropped by their record label. "By 1958 though, they were largely a forgotten group." ← Episode 91： ＂The Twist＂ by Chubby Checker | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LzsS8EXdsm8
**Dick Clark's Reasons for Exclusion** : Dick Clark deliberately did not invite Hank Ballard and the Midnighters onto his shows to perform "The Twist," even as it began to chart. Various reasons are cited: they might have been too busy touring, Clark preferred someone "less threatening" (understood to mean "less obviously Black"), someone younger, concerns about the "adult nature" of Ballard's act, or Clark's preference for performers with whom he had a financial link. "Now, there were several stories about why Clark didn't ask Hank Ballard and the Midnighters onto the show." ← Episode 91： ＂The Twist＂ by Chubby Checker | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LzsS8EXdsm8
**Discovery of "The Twist"** : Despite their decline in chart success, the group remained a popular live act and toured regularly. While performing in Florida (either Tampa or Miami), they played on the same bill as the gospel group the Sensational Nightingales, who offered them the secular song "The Twist" as they could not perform it themselves. **Guitarist Cal Green's Imprisonment** : Cal Green, the Midnighters' guitarist who helped adapt "The Twist" melody, ended up in prison for two years around this time due to marijuana possession. This meant he missed out on almost the entire story of "The Twist" craze and its subsequent success. **Influence on The Famous Flames' Style** : Hank Ballard and the Midnighters were one of two groups that The Famous Flames increasingly patterned their style after. Their work, particularly with its "blatant sexuality" in "Work With Me Annie," contributed to the Famous Flames' focus on energy and raw, visceral emotion.
