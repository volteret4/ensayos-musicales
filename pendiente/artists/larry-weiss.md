# artist - Larry Weiss

## genres
- Pop

## songs
**Bend Me, Shape Me (1968)** : Larry Weiss wrote this song, which was recorded by The American Breed in 1968. The American Breed later evolved into Rufus, which became Rufus and Chaka Khan. "He wrote Bend Me, Shape Me. That was recorded by the American breed in 1968, which became Rufus, which then, yes, becomes Rufus of Rufus and Chaka Khan." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 2 - Countryish Music of the 1970s (Encore) | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Rhinestone Cowboy (1974)** : Larry Weiss wrote and recorded his own version of this song in 1974, which was described as sounding "a little bit like Neil Diamond." He wrote it with the explicit goal of achieving a big, memorable hit. "Larry Weiss, the songwriter of Rhyne Stone cowboy, tapped into that with this song. He wrote it and recorded his own self in 1974." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 2 - Countryish Music of the 1970s (Encore) | https://www.ftr70.com/

## curiosities
**CMA Song of the Year Winner (1976)** : Larry Weiss won the CMA Song of the Year award for "Rhinestone Cowboy" in 1976. When accepting the award, he sounded "almost apologetic," seeming to confirm he was not from Nashville and expressed regret for winning despite being from New York. "Larry Weiss won the CMAs song of the year for Rhyne Stone cowboy in 1976. When he almost sounded apologetic when he accepted the award, it was seemingly like he needed to confirm that he was not from Nashville, and I'm so sorry that I'm from New York, and I won this award." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 2 - Countryish Music of the 1970s (Encore) | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Diverse Songwriting Credits** : Before writing "Rhinestone Cowboy," Larry Weiss wrote many songs in the 1960s for artists such as The Charelles, The Animals, and Jeff Beck. "He wrote many songs before he wrote Rhyne Stone cowboy in the 1960s. He wrote for the charelles. He wrote for the animals. Jeff Beck." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 2 - Countryish Music of the 1970s (Encore) | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Queens, New York City Origin** : Larry Weiss is from Queens, New York City. He wrote "Rhinestone Cowboy" after moving to LA, reflecting on his life and paying homage to cowboy movie heroes of his youth like Gene Autry. "Larry Weiss from?
