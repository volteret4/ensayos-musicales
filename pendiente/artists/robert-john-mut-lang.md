## curiosities
**Birth and Early Life** : Born Robert John Lang in Rhodesia, which is now Zimbabwe, and raised in Durban, South Africa. His parents were German expatriates, with his father being a mining engineer and his mother from a high society German family. **Breaking UK Chart Record** : In 1991, Mut Lang produced Brian Adams' hit, which spent 16 weeks at number one on the official UK singles chart, breaking Slim Whitman's 1955 record of 11 weeks at number one with "Rose Marie." "That chart record would not be beaten for 36 years until a hit by Brian Adams in 1991 spent 16 weeks official UK chart and that single was produced by you guessed it, Mut Lang." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Pour Some Sugar on Me Edition Part 1 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4ef5d1ba84fb8f04493d
**Brief Attempt as Frontline Artist** : Mut briefly tried being a frontman artist but later gave it up, choosing to focus on production. He intentionally kept his name off album titles, unlike other producer-artists. "He briefly tried being a front line artist then basically gave it up for life." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Pour Some Sugar on Me Edition Part 1 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4ef5d1ba84fb8f04493d
**Career Start - Commercials** : After high school, Mut moved to the Johannesburg area and took a job producing commercials, which was his entry into recording before forming his first bands. **Collaborative Reputation** : He earned a reputation for meticulousness in the studio, with artists like Graham Parker finding his instrument-by-instrument recording approach stiff. **Long Gap in Album Production (1984-1987)** : Mut went almost four years between 1984 and 1987 without producing a full released album, not due to a change in his chart fortunes, but because of his total focus on a new LP for one of his previous clients, which would become one of the biggest albums in glam metal history and the most painstaking project of his career. **Musical Influence - Slim Whitman** : As a young boy, Mut loved country music, particularly the keening, yodeling vocals of American country and western star Slim Whitman. This influence would later echo in the high-pitched hard rock vocals featured in his production work. **Pivotal Role in ACDC's Success** : Lang was pivotal in ACDC's massive success, particularly with the "Back in Black" album, where he honed their sound to a diamond-hard core and guided them through the catastrophe of Bon Scott's death. **Signature Sound - Anthemic Global Pop** : Lang established himself as the author of an anthemic brand of global pop, characterized by a crunching, pumping sound he mined for hits from the 1980s deep into the 21st century. His productions often made genres sound distinctively "Mut-like" regardless of the artist.
