# artist - Daniel Lopatin

## albums
**Chuck Person's Eccojams Vol. 1 (2010)** : Daniel Lopatin, operating under the alias Chuck Person, released this collection of his earlier works in 2010. The album built upon his initial YouTube experiments, further developing the Eccojams style by presenting popular song extracts reduced in tempo and pitch, and assembled in loops. This release was foundational for the early vaporwave scene. "Al año siguiente, el mismo Lopatín publicó una colección de esos trabajos a los cañadio otros, bajolálias de Chuck Person en Chuck Person Seco-Jumps volumen 1." ← Vaporwave： Mucho más que un Meme | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BhOnKc0SxsA
**Chuck Person's Eccojams Vol. 1 (2010) - Creative Manipulation** : Through this album, Daniel Lopatin skillfully manipulated songs by artists like Michael Jackson, Kate Bush, and Tupac. By altering their fragments, he managed to imbue them with a different, more evocative, and nostalgic sentiment and tone, laying the groundwork for the vaporwave aesthetic. ## curiosities
**First Vaporwave Recordings (2009)** : In 2009, Daniel Lopatin, then known by his YouTube handle SunsetCorp and later also as Oneohtrix Point Never, uploaded the earliest recordings of what would become vaporwave to his channel. These videos featured extracts of popular songs, slowed down, pitch-shifted, and looped, accompanied by unsettling and nostalgic images related to technology. "Las primeras grabaciones del género las podemos escuchar en el año 2009, cuando los veros de YouTube, San SetCorp, quien más tarde se descubrió que se trataba de Daniel Dopatín, más conocido por su proyecto, One-Etricks, Ponnevar, subió los vídeos en su canal." ← Vaporwave： Mucho más que un Meme | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BhOnKc0SxsA
**Pivotal Figure in Vaporwave's Origins** : Daniel Lopatin is considered, alongside James Ferraro and Vektroid, one of the most important individuals in the development of vaporwave, having been responsible for some of its foundational musical experiments and releases.
