## albums
**Soul Makossa (1973)** : Recorded in Paris, this album was praised by Vince Aletti as "one of the best to emerge from the new discotech scene," far exceeding its title single. Dibango and his band of expatriates from formerly French West Africa crafted a "heady Afro jazz blend using horns and electric guitars, but in an utterly African way," creating a sound perfectly suited for "a subtropical evening in the jungle of the cities." ## songs
**Soul Makossa (1972)** : This 45-single became a pivotal early disco hit in 1972, initially popularized by DJ David Mancuso at his Loft house parties and later by legendary WBLS Radio DJ Frankie Crocker, who gave it heavy radio rotation. Cited by music journalist Vince Aletti as the perfect example of the emerging disco sound, it demonstrated the power of DJs in breaking hits and highlighted the African roots of disco.
