## curiosities
**Acrimonious Split with Parker** : Hank Snow's relationship with Tom Parker ended "very acrimoniously" after two years. Snow later referred to Parker as the "most egotistical, obnoxious human being" he had ever dealt with among all his managers. "His association with Hank Snow would only last two years, and would break up very acrimoniously, with Snow later saying, I have worked with several managers over the years and have had respect for them all except one. Tom Parker was the most egotistical, obnoxious human being I've ever had dealings with." ← Episode 33： ＂Mystery Train＂, by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DSHTmHy3Jo0
**Exclusive Management by Tom Parker (1953-1955)** : After Eddie Arnold fired Tom Parker in 1953, Parker quickly became Hank Snow's exclusive manager, an association that lasted two years. "Arnold fired the Kernel, and the Kernel quickly instead became the exclusive manager of Hank Snow." ← Episode 33： ＂Mystery Train＂, by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DSHTmHy3Jo0
**Grand Ole Opry Refusal to Rock and Roll Trio** : Hank Snow refused to help the Rock and Roll Trio get on the Grand Ole Opry, explaining that it had taken him 20 years to work his way up to perform there and he couldn't simply get three strangers onto the show. "Snow explained to them that it had taken him 20 years in the business to work his way up to being on the Grand Ole Opry and he couldn't just get three random people he'd never met before onto the show." ← Episode 44： ＂Train Kept A-Rollin'＂, by Johnny Burnette and the Rock 'n' Roll Trio | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u3zYAJ2qeR4
**Headliner for Key Show (1955)** : Hank Snow was the headliner at a country music show in September 1955, which Gene Vincent attended. While Snow was the main attraction, it was the performance of a younger Elvis Presley lower down the bill that ultimately inspired Vincent's musical direction. "The headliner was Hank Snow, and the Louvin brothers were also on the bell, but the act that changed Jean's life was lower down the bell. A young singer named Elvis Presley." ← Episode 41： ＂Be-Bop-A-Lula＂ by Gene Vincent and the Bluecaps | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tYBQ5F7O3G0
**Jimmie Rodgers' Advocate** : Snow frequently covered songs by Jimmie Rodgers and admired him so much that he named his son Jimmie Rodgers Snow. Through Snow, young Robert Zimmerman (Bob Dylan) became aware of Jimmie Rodgers' songs. **Parker's Deception Regarding Elvis Management** : The reason for Snow's animosity was that Tom Parker tricked him out of a major business opportunity. Snow and Parker had formed a management company for other artists, and Snow assumed they were partners in managing Elvis until he discovered Parker had cut him out entirely. "The two of them had formed a management company to manage other artists, and when Parker found another artist he wanted to manage, Snow naturally assumed that they were partners, right up until he discovered they weren't." ← Episode 33： ＂Mystery Train＂, by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DSHTmHy3Jo0
**Persuading Elvis's Parents** : Hank Snow, being a major idol to Elvis Presley's parents, was used by Tom Parker to convince them of the benefits of Parker managing their son. "He got Hank Snow to work on Elvis's family. Elvis's parents were big Hank snow fans and after being told by their idol how much the Colonel had helped him they were practically salivating to get to Elvis signed with him." ← Episode 33： ＂Mystery Train＂, by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DSHTmHy3Jo0
