## songs
**Spirit in the Sky (1970)** : Greenbaum, who is Jewish, was inspired to write this song after hearing country star Porter Wagoner sing "Pastor Absent on Vacation" on television. He felt that the "love generation fizzled out into revolution, but I think some people really wanted to believe in something." The song was notable for its distinctive fuzz tone, produced by a fuzz box built directly into his Fender guitar. Background vocals were provided by the Stovall Sisters, a gospel trio from Oakland, California. It reached number three on the Billboard Hot 100 in April 1970. This shows the cross-genre influence of religious themes. **Reception and Intent of "Spirit in the Sky"** : Some listeners were unhappy with the lyric "never been a sinner, never sinned," perceiving it as making light of religious beliefs. Greenbaum clarified 50 years later that he "met no harm" and didn't intend it as a joke or against anyone, simply that "It wasn't my religion, I just did it." He noted that many churches later incorporated the song into their services. "It did upset some people.
