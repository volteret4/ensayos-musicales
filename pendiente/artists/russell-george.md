# artist - Russell George

## instruments
- Electric Bass

## curiosities
**Creating the Groove for "Afternoon Delight"** : As a session musician on "Afternoon Delight," George noticed the "folkies" were struggling with a groove and offered to kick it off. He set up the entire groove with a "one a two a one two three four" count-off, following his motto "when it's right, it's right." "I said to Bill do you mind if I kick it off my count off a one a two a one two three four set up the whole groove my motto's always been when it's right it's right." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 31 - The Spirit of '76 - Pop Music on America's Bicentennial | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-31-the-spirit-of-76-pop-music-on-americas-bicentennial
**Session Musician** : Russell George was a session musician for 20 years, teaching himself electric bass after hearing The Beatles' "Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band" in 1967, which he said changed his career. He played on James Brown and LeBelle albums.
