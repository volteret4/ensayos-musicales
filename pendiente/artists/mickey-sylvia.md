## songs
**Dearest** : A "boldly ballad" very much in the same vein as "Love Is Strange," but only reached number 85 on the Hot 100, performing worse than their previous record. Despite its lack of commercial success, it was a favorite of Buddy Holly, who recorded home demos of it. It was not considered "especially good" and had a sound similar to Jean and Junis, with minimal distinctive guitar work, just "stabbing punctuation" and a "perfunctory solo." "Originally, they signed with the label called Rainbow Records, a medium-sized indie label in New York, where they put out their first single, I'm so glad." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Love Is Strange (1956)** : A major hit that reached number 11 on the pop chart and number 1 on the R&B chart. It was co-written by Bo Diddley (lyrics, credited to Ethel Smith) and Jody Williams (music), with Mickey and Sylvia credited as arrangers, presumably for their changes including the famous spoken word section. Bo Diddley later sold his share to Sylvia. The song became a classic, covered by many artists. "Lovey's Strange was a big hit. It went to number 11 in the pop chart, and number 1 on the R&B chart." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Love Is Strange (Year unknown)** : Carole King and Gerry Goffin worked on a song in the style of "Love Is Strange" for Mickey & Sylvia, at a time when Atlantic Records was looking for new material for the duo. "King mentioned that she knew that Atlantic were looking for a new song for Mickey and Sylvia, and the two of them worked on a song based on the style of Love Is Strange, which they completed very quickly and talked to Atlantic." ← Episode 89： ＂Will You Love Me Tomorrow？＂ by the Shirelles | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Kns2HGcuSg
**Next Record After Love Is Strange** : Their follow-up record to "Love Is Strange" achieved Top 10 R&B status but only reached number 47 on the pop charts, indicating diminishing returns for their career. **Se De Boom Run Dun** : The B-side to their first single, "I'm So Glad," released on Rainbow Records. While also "far from a wonderful song," it contained "the semi-collipse" and "the belights" that would later make them famous. ## curiosities
**"Love Is Strange" Origin Story** : The song originated from Bo Diddley, who had written and recorded it in May 1956 but not released it. Jody Williams, Bo Diddley's guitarist, played the song for Sylvia, who liked it, leading Mickey and Sylvia to record it and make it a hit. "But at that point, fate intervened in the form of Bo Deadly." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Diminishing Commercial Returns** : While they released many more records after "Love Is Strange," their sales and chart performance steadily declined. Their next record went Top 10 R&B but only Pop #47, and the one after that, "Dearest," only reached #85 on the Hot 100. "Mickey and Sylvia would release many more records, but they would have diminishing returns." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**End of Duo and Mickey's Departure** : Their own recordings continued to be unsuccessful, and Mickey faced personal challenges due to his white wife amidst virulent racism in the US, alongside his increasing interest in academic music. These factors led him to move to Europe and pursue jazz and composition, effectively ending the duo's active career. "However, Mickey and Sylvia's recordings under their own name were still having no success, and Mickey was also having problems because his then wife was white, and with the particularly virulent form of racism the US was suffering through at the time, he didn't want to be in the country anymore." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Formation as a Duo** : The duo was formed when Sylvia started taking guitar lessons from Mickey Baker. Different sources attribute the idea to either Joe Robinson (inspired by Jean and Junis) or Mickey Baker (who wanted to emulate Les Paul and Mary Ford). The gimmick was a male-female duo who could both sing and play guitar, focusing on Mickey's string-bending style. "Eventually it was decided that Sylvia should join with her guitar teacher and former duo." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Independent Venture: Willow Records** : After being dropped from Vic Records, Mickey and Sylvia temporarily split but later reunited to form their own company, Willow Records, to release their material independently. "After Mickey and Sylvia were dropped along with the rest of the Vic artists. This bled up temporarily, but then got back together to start their own company, Willow Records, to release their own material." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Move to Groove Records (RCA Subsidiary)** : Their airplay in New York attracted the attention of Bob Rollant at Groove Records, a subsidiary of RCA established in 1953 as a "fake indie label" to access the R&B market. "But the records did get some airplay in New York, if nowhere else, and that brought them to the attention of Bob Rullant's at Groove Records." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Onstage Chemistry, Offstage Discord** : Mickey and Sylvia had musical chemistry on stage and recordings, but offstage, they soon began to "great on each other" due to their contrasting personalities and ambitions. "But they worked well as a musical team, even if they were never going to be the best of friends." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Post-Hit Decline and RCA Label Closure** : After "Love Is Strange" became a breakthrough hit, RCA controversially closed Groove Records and moved its artists and producer Valence to another subsidiary, Vic Records. This loss of momentum severely affected Mickey and Sylvia's career, leading to diminishing returns on subsequent releases. "But after groove records that had this breakthrough big hit, RCA decided to close the label down and move the acts on the label and their producer Valence to another subsidiary. Vic." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Production for Ike & Tina Turner** : To return a favor for Ike Turner playing on some of their Willow Records, Mickey and Sylvia agreed to produce a record for Ike & Tina Turner. They suggested "It's Gonna Work Out Fine," a song they had previously recorded for Vic (unreleased), and handled its production and arrangement, with Sylvia playing lead guitar and Mickey providing spoken vocals. "Ag Turner played on some of their records, and to return the favour they agreed to produce a record for Ag and Tina Turner." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Rainbow Records Signing** : Their first label was Rainbow Records, a medium-sized indie label in New York. Their initial singles, "I'm So Glad" and "Se De Boom Run Dun," did not sell well, but gained some airplay in New York, attracting attention from larger labels. "Originally, they signed with the label called Rainbow Records, a medium-sized indie label in New York, where they put out their first single, I'm so glad." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Recording Team at Groove Records** : At Groove Records, Mickey and Sylvia were paired with saxophonist and arranger King Curtis, whom Mickey already knew from Doc Pomus. Curtis assembled a team of top R&B musicians in New York, many of whom also played on Atlantic sessions. "Valence put them together with the sex-offenist and arranger King Curtis, who Mickey already knew from his work with Doc Pommas, and Curtis put together a team of the best R&B musicians in New York, many of them the same people who would play on most of Atlantic sessions." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
