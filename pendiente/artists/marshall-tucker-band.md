# artist - Marshall Tucker Band

## members
- Jerry Eubanks (flute)
- Tommy Caldwell (bass)
- Toy Caldwell (lead guitar)

## genres
- Country
- Southern Rock

## labels
- Capricorn

## concerts
- Milwaukee Performing Arts Center (1974)
- Ozark Music Festival (1974)

## albums
**The Long Hard Ride** : This album was the subject of an article by rock critic Richard Regal, who praised the band's distinctive sound. "Richard Regal made an interesting point about this in his article about the Marshall Tucker band album The Long Hard Ride." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 46 - The Chaos and Music of the Ozark Music Festival of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Where We All Belong (1974)** : A double LP released in November 1974, four months after the Ozark Music Festival. This album included a live set recorded two weeks after the festival at a show in Milwaukee. The live performance was described by Toy Caldwell in 1975 as "edgier," "more rock than country," and possessing "a whole lot more energy" compared to their older records, truly showcasing their live style. "where we all belong is the first album that really shows our style." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 46 - The Chaos and Music of the Ozark Music Festival of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/

## curiosities
**Band Name Origin** : The band was not named after an actual member; "Marshall Tucker" was the name found on the key that opened their rehearsal hall, which was possibly a former piano repair shop or warehouse. "There was no Marshall Tucker in the band. That was the name on the key that opened their rehearsal hall with more of a warehouse really." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 46 - The Chaos and Music of the Ozark Music Festival of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Discovery and Early Career** : The band was discovered by the manager of The Allman Brothers Band, who heard them in Macon, Georgia. They subsequently signed with Capricorn, the same label as The Allman Brothers Band, and opened for them on tour. "It was the manager of the Alman Brothers band that discovered the Marshall Tucker band when he heard them in make in Georgia." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 46 - The Chaos and Music of the Ozark Music Festival of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Distinctive Southern Rock Sound** : Rock critic Richard Regal characterized their sound as featuring "warm, honey laden guitar meshes" and "lyrical celebrations of simple virtues," which he felt resonated authentically because the band "have always been back on their land," rather than adopting a "back to the land" escapist trend. The band's diverse instrumentation, including piano, horn, flute, sax, and fiddle alongside guitars, contributes to a sound that exemplifies the broad umbrella of Southern rock. "the warm, honey laden guitar meshes, the lyrical celebrations of simple virtues which ring true because these guys aren't going back to the land as escapists from the urban willies." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 46 - The Chaos and Music of the Ozark Music Festival of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/
