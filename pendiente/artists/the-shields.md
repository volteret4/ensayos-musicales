# artist - The Shields

## members
- Alex Hodge (on Nature Boy)
- Frankie Ervin (lead vocals, toured as The Shields)
- Gaynell Hodge (on Nature Boy)
- Jesse Belvin (falsetto vocals, assembled group)
- Johnny "Guitar" Watson (backing vocals)
- Mel Williams (backing vocals)
- Members of The Turks (on Nature Boy)
- Tommy "Buster" Williams (backing vocals)

## genres
- Doo-wop

## labels
- Dot Records (leased production)

## songs
**Nature Boy** : The follow-up single to "You Cheated," for which the original Shields members, including Frankie Ervin, reunited. They were joined by members of The Turks, including both Alex and Gaynell Hodge. According to Ervin, this song also sold a million copies, although it was not as commercially successful as its predecessor, indicating diminishing returns for the group. "But The Original Shields members reunited for the follow-up single Nature Boy, where they were joined by the members of The Turks, who were yet another group The Belvin was recording with, and who included both Hodgpluthers." ← Episode 47： ＂Goodnight My Love＂ by Jesse Belvin | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eoyk3H0M6r8
**You Cheated (1958)** : A massive hit that reached number 12 on the pop charts, famously outselling the original version by a white group, The Slades. The record featured Frankie Ervin on lead vocals and Jesse Belvin singing the falsetto part, with a scratch vocal group assembled by Belvin for the session. It was an independent production leased to Dot Records, who opted for a generic group name instead of crediting Ervin as a solo artist. "You cheated, ended up making number 12 on the pop charts." ← Episode 47： ＂Goodnight My Love＂ by Jesse Belvin | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eoyk3H0M6r8

## curiosities
**Accidental Million-Selling Group** : The Shields originated as a scratch vocal group assembled by Jesse Belvin for Frankie Ervin's recording of "You Cheated." The song unexpectedly became a massive hit, credited to The Shields by Dot Records, effectively creating another million-selling group that Belvin was inadvertently involved with. "But while Belvin had accidentally put together yet another million-selling group, he had also moved on to bigger things." ← Episode 47： ＂Goodnight My Love＂ by Jesse Belvin | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eoyk3H0M6r8
**Changing Lineups and Belvin's Departure** : For The Shields' third record, only Jesse Belvin remained from the original recording lineup, indicating the transient nature of the group. Subsequent recordings by The Shields did not feature Belvin at all, as he had moved on to bigger career opportunities with RCA, signaling the end of his direct involvement. "So The Third Shields record only featured Belvin out of The Original lineup, and subsequent recordings didn't even feature him." ← Episode 47： ＂Goodnight My Love＂ by Jesse Belvin | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eoyk3H0M6r8
**Label's Avoidance of Solo Artist Demands** : The recording of "You Cheated" was an independent production leased to Dot Records. The label deliberately credited the song to a generic group name, The Shields, rather than Frankie Ervin as a solo artist. This decision was reportedly made to avoid promoting a solo singer who might later demand credits, royalties, or more money, especially given that Ervin was Black. "Somewhere along the line someone decided that it was better to have a generic group name, rather than promote it as by a solo singer, who might get ideas about wanting money." ← Episode 47： ＂Goodnight My Love＂ by Jesse Belvin | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eoyk3H0M6r8
**Outselling the Original by a Black Group** : In an unusual turn for the music industry at the time, The Shields' sound-alike version of "You Cheated," performed by a black group, managed to outsell the original recording by the white group, The Slades. This was a notable reversal of the typical industry trend, where covers by black artists rarely surpassed white originals commercially. "This time, a sound-like record by a black group managed to outsell the original by a white group." ← Episode 47： ＂Goodnight My Love＂ by Jesse Belvin | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eoyk3H0M6r8
**Touring Lineup vs. Recording Lineup** : Due to original recording members (Jesse Belvin, Johnny "Guitar" Watson, Tommy "Buster" Williams, Mel Williams) being under contract to other labels, Frankie Ervin toured and made TV appearances as "The Shields" backed by random singers assembled by the record label. Only for the follow-up, "Nature Boy," did some original members reunite, highlighting the ad-hoc nature of the group's performance lineup.
