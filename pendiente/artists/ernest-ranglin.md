# artist - Ernest Ranglin

## instruments
- Guitar

## songs
**Easy Snapping** : Ernest Ranglin served as the arranger for Coxsone Dodd's "Easy Snapping," widely considered the first real Ska record. His role involved stressing the off-beats as per Dodd's directive, which some believe led to the term "Ska." "Ernest Wranglin, who was the arranger for the record, to stress the off beats more, saying, play it, Scar, Scar, Scar." ← Episode 114： ＂My Boy Lollipop＂ by Millie | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kf-DtqoOsRI
**My Boy Lollipop (1964)** : Ernest Ranglin arranged Millie Small's hit version of "My Boy Lollipop." He hired a local band, Jimmy Powell and the Five Dimensions, for the instrumental backing and made the decision to replace the original saxophone solo with a harmonica solo. "Blackwell got Ernest Wranglin... to come up with an arrangement, and Wranglin hired a local band to perform the instrumental backing." ← Episode 114： ＂My Boy Lollipop＂ by Millie | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kf-DtqoOsRI
