## albums
**Black Moses (1971)** : An album from 1971 that was a significant influence on later music. Melodic parts and rhythms from this album have been sampled and used by many hip-hop artists, and even modern artists like Portishead. This album predates Barry White's 1974 recordings by several years, making it a crucial precursor for both rap and much subsequent music. **Hot Buttered Soul (1969)** : This album, recorded by Isaac Hayes, featured only four epic songs. It was a pivotal release for Stax Records, which had recently split from Atlantic Records and needed to quickly build a new catalog. Stax released 27 albums simultaneously, offering many artists a chance, but Hayes insisted on doing it his way. Despite his personal indifference to its commercial performance, stating he didn't care if it sold, the album went on to redefine soul music. **Walk On By (1969)** : Isaac Hayes's 12-minute rendition of Dionne Warwick's 1963 song "Walk On By" exemplifies his groundbreaking approach to soul music. His extended, soulful reinterpretation transformed the "very pleasant" original into an immersive and "cool" experience, showcasing his unique artistic vision and disregard for conventional song structures. ## curiosities
**Assassination of Dr. King's Influence** : While *Hot Buttered Soul* was not an overtly political album, it was profoundly inspired by the pain and anger Isaac Hayes felt following the assassination of Dr. Martin Luther King Jr., whom he considered a friend. Initially, Hayes was so overcome with anger that he couldn't create music, but he channeled this emotion into his art and a determination to succeed. He resolved to become successful and powerful enough to use his voice to effect change. "Even though this album was not an overt political statement, it was inspired by the pain of Dr. King's assassination. Hayes considered Dr. King a friend and said that after King's death he was so angry he couldn't make music." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 22 - Power to the People - The Music of the Black Power Movement | https://seventies.libsyn.com/022-power-to-the-people-62520-11-01-am
**Bald Head and Gold Chains** : The album cover for *Hot Buttered Soul* famously featured only the top of Isaac Hayes's bald head, a bold visual statement. His live performances were equally striking, as he often wore a suit made out of gold chain, which he humorously referred to as his "natural air conditioning." This powerful stage presence contributed to his aura, with drummer Willie Hall describing him as "cool as shit." "It's just the top of Isaac's bald head. When he was on stage, he wore a suit made out of a gold chain. **Black Moses LP Jacket - Seizure of Musical Space** : The LP jacket for his album *Black Moses* unfolded to reveal a full-length portrait of Hayes in a robe, arms outstretched. Emily Lordi, a historian of black popular music, highlighted that this "seizure of musical space," both visually and sonically, made a profound political statement during a time when black people were feeling acutely unwelcome in public spheres, facing policing and violence. Hayes's deliberate claiming of time and space in his art resonated deeply with listeners. "His seizure of musical space, literalized in the LP jacket for black Moses, which unfolded to reveal a full-length portrait of Hayes in a robe, his arms outstretched, made a political statement at a time when black people were being made to feel acutely unwelcome in the public sphere." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 22 - Power to the People - The Music of the Black Power Movement | https://seventies.libsyn.com/022-power-to-the-people-62520-11-01-am
