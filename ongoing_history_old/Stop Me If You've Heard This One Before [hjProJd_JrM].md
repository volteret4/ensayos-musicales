# artist - William Shatner

## labels
Decca

## albums
**The Transformed Man (1968)** : William Shatner's most famous record, released by Decca. He conceived it as a project to link classic literature (Shakespeare, Edmond Rostand) with contemporary song lyrics by performing them over original musical backgrounds, aiming to showcase contrasting philosophies. Each combined piece was intended to be six minutes long.

## songs
**Lucy in the Sky with Diamonds (1967) - William Shatner Version** : William Shatner's interpretation on "The Transformed Man" album, where he stated he was "performing LSD" by portraying a "guy looped" experiencing a vision of "tangerine trees and marmalade skies." He believed this performance was often taken out of context by listeners.

## curiosities
**How Time Flies Stage Production** : William Shatner developed a stage production titled "How Time Flies," based on reminiscences of his 60-year career in show business. The show was structured as a celebrity interview in front of an audience, incorporating audio and video clips over 90 minutes with a 15-minute intermission.
**How Time Flies Tour Locations** : The stage production "How Time Flies" toured various cities including Vancouver, Regina, Edmonton, Winnipeg, Toronto, and Montreal.
**Attempt to Sing Stompin' Tom Connors' "The Hockey Song"** : William Shatner attempted to perform "The Hockey Song" by Stompin' Tom Connors as a sing-along for his stage show but struggled with its execution in rehearsals, leading Alan Cross to try and coach him.
**The "You Will Drink Beer" Incident in Regina** : At a brew pub in Regina, William Shatner, while enjoying a flight of various beers, commanded Alan Cross in a "Wrath of Khan" voice to drink beer, despite Cross's stated preference for not being a beer drinker, resulting in a severe hangover for Cross.
**"How Time Flies" on Broadway** : William Shatner's stage production "How Time Flies" successfully made it to Broadway, albeit in a rejigged format where Alan Cross's role was replaced by an empty chair prop. Alan Cross conceded the production was "much better" without his participation.
**Recording Philosophy for The Transformed Man (1968)** : William Shatner explained his intention for "The Transformed Man" was to perform classic literary speeches alongside contemporary song lyrics, using original musical backdrops to compare or contrast philosophies within a six-minute format for each track.

## curiosities
**Reunion Tour (Post-Madchester Era)** : The Happy Mondays reunited for a series of shows across the UK following their 90s Madchester era, which was characterized by significant drug and alcohol abuse. The band members demonstrated a new "ultra-clean living" lifestyle.

# venue - Abbey Road Studios

## instruments
Grand piano
Upright piano (Lady Madonna Piano)

## curiosities
**Private Tour (Early Morning)** : Alan Cross secured a private tour of Abbey Road Studios through EMI connections, attending at 8 AM before normal work hours began.
**Historical Client Roster** : Numerous renowned artists and bands have recorded albums at Abbey Road Studios, including The Beatles, Pink Floyd, Oasis, Blur, Muse, Kate Bush, Depeche Mode, Iron Maiden, Queen, U2, Michael Jackson, and Glenn Miller.
**Studio 3 Features and Activity** : Studio 3 is described as being the size of a couple of basketball courts with a very high ceiling. During Alan Cross's visit, a 65-piece orchestra was recording the soundtrack for "Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows Part 2."
**Studio 2 Historical Significance** : Studio 2 is historically significant as the primary recording location for The Beatles (with George Martin) and where Pink Floyd's "Dark Side of the Moon" was created. Its control room overlooks the studio space from one floor up.
**"Lady Madonna Piano" in Studio 2** : A beat-up upright piano in Studio 2, famously used by The Beatles, bears a "Lady Madonna Piano. Do not touch." sign. Alan Cross admitted to touching it despite the warning.
**Studio 1 Features and Activity** : Studio 1 is described as the smallest of the studios and was "jammed with gear." Florence + The Machine were "midway through recording their 'Ceremonials' album" there during Alan Cross's visit.
**Daily Operations** : Abbey Road Studios have been run in a structured manner since the 1930s, with a bell ringing at 9 AM to signal the start of the workday.
**Equipment Diversity** : The studios house a wide array of equipment, ranging from vintage gear from the 1940s and 1950s to brand new installations.

# curiosity

**Alan Cross's Fake Death and Return** : Alan Cross recounts faking his death and being away from his podcast for three years due to unspecified troubles in Asia involving "max dark credit cards," a casino pit boss in Macau, and "tigers," leading him to respect "Seek for you to Roy now." He explains he is now fine, aside from certain parts of Asia being unwelcoming and developing a phobia of large orange cats.
**Alan Cross's Personal Adventures (General)** : Alan Cross's three-year absence from his podcast involved extensive global travels and escapades, including avoiding triads in Macau after a bad evening at a baccarat table in Beijing.
**"Who the F Is Arthur Fogel?" Documentary** : A documentary film about Arthur Fogel's career, titled "Who the F Is Arthur Fogel?", was produced over approximately three years. Alan Cross served as one of the script editors, and the film premiered in Hollywood before airing on HBO Canada.
**Arthur Fogel's Role in Music Industry** : Arthur Fogel, originally from Hamilton, Canada, rose to become the head of global touring for Live Nation in Los Angeles, widely recognized as "the most powerful man in the concert industry." He is credited with reuniting The Police, is Madonna's only trusted promoter, and orchestrated U2's record-breaking 360 Tour. At the time of the documentary's inception, he notably lacked a Wikipedia page.
**"A Journal of Musical Things" Website** : Alan Cross maintains a website, ajournalofmusicalthings.com, which is updated daily with music stories, news, and recommendations, and offers a free, spam-free newsletter.
**The Ongoing History of New Music Podcast** : Hosted by Alan Cross, with technical productions by Rob Johnston, this podcast is available on platforms including iTunes, Google Play, Stitcher, and Spotify.
