**Learjet Plane Crash (September 19, 2008)** : After performing with a side project in Columbia, South Carolina, he boarded a Learjet 60 for a flight to Van Nuys, California. The plane failed to lift off, hit runway lights, crashed through a fence, crossed a highway, and ended against an embankment. His wife was supposed to join him but had a "freaky feeling" and stayed behind.
**Survival and Severe Injuries (September 2008)** : One of two survivors (out of six people aboard, including both pilots who died), he sustained very severe second and third-degree burns. He spent nearly three months in hospitals and burn units, undergoing 16 surgeries, and at one point, faced potential amputation of his foot. He also suffered severe PTSD.
**Crash Investigation Findings**: The investigation determined the crash was caused by badly underinflated jet tires bursting during takeoff. The pilot then aborted the takeoff at too high a speed, leading to a loss of control.
**Legal Actions and Settlements**: He filed lawsuits against the plane's owners, Goodyear (the tire manufacturer), and the aircraft maintenance company responsible for the improperly inflated tires. All lawsuits were eventually settled.
**Autobiography**: Wrote a first-person account of his ordeal in his book, "Can I Say: Living Large, Cheating Death, and Drums, Drums, Drums."

# curiosity

**Air Rage Incidents** : A phenomenon where passengers become unruly during flights, often due to factors like cramped seating, reclining seats, excessive alcohol consumption, drug issues, sexual escapades, or simply disruptive behavior. The text provides numerous examples involving musicians.
**Pan Am Flight 103 Lockerbie Bombing (December 21, 1988)** : A devastating act of airline terrorism where a bomb, hidden inside a cassette player and placed in the forward luggage compartment of a Boeing 747, exploded at 31,000 feet over Lockerbie, Scotland. The plane, en route from London to New York, disintegrated, killing all 243 passengers, 16 crew members, and 11 people on the ground.
**"The Day the Music Died" (February 3, 1959)** : A historical event referring to the tragic small plane crash in a cornfield outside Mason City, Iowa, that claimed the lives of musicians Buddy Holly, Ritchie Valens, and The Big Bopper.
**Non-Insane Automatism (Legal Term)** : A legal concept describing certain types of non-voluntary behavior. This term was central to Peter Buck's acquittal in his 2002 air rage trial, attributed to an unusual reaction between his Ambien medication and alcohol.
**Hypomania (Medical Condition)** : Described as a potentially very frightening condition in which an individual can be irrational, aggressive, and difficult. It can be triggered by stress and sleep deprivation, and was diagnosed in Dolores O'Riordan after her 2014 air rage incident, being part of her broader bipolar disorder.
