# artist - I'm Mother Earth

## members
Jag Tana
Christian Tana
Edwin
Brian Bern

## genres
Can Rock
Alt Rock

## albums
**Dig (1993) – Debut Album** : Released in the summer of 1993, this debut record was described as a "shock" due to the band's accomplished, tight, and together sound. It was recorded in Los Angeles and produced by Mike Kling. The album went platinum in Canada.
**Scenery and Fish (Year not specified) – Second Album** : This album achieved double platinum sales status in Canada.
**Blue Green Orange (1999) – Third Album** : Released in 1999.
**Quick Silver Meat Dream (2003) – Final Album** : This was the band's last record, released in 2003 before their breakup.

## songs
**Not Quite Sonic (1993)** : Featured on their debut record "Dig", released in 1993. The intro of this song is noted to have a possible inspiration from Slash's opening guitar bits in Guns N' Roses' "Welcome to the Jungle".

## curiosities
**Formation (1990)** : The band was formed in 1990 when brothers Jag and Christian Tana met vocalist Edwin at a shared rehearsal space.
**Early Success & Bidding War (Early 1990s)** : After recording demos and playing gigs, the band became the subject of a bidding war before releasing their debut album.
**Vocalist Change (Post-Scenery and Fish)** : Original vocalist Edwin decided to leave the band after the release of "Scenery and Fish" and was replaced by Brian Bern.
**Breakup (Post-2003)** : The band broke up after the release of their final record "Quick Silver Meat Dream" in 2003, with members pursuing other projects.
**Edwin's Solo Career and New Band (Post-2003)** : After leaving the band, Edwin pursued a solo career, releasing three records. In 2008, he joined Mike Turner (ex-Our Lady Peace) and Jeff Burrows (The Tea Party) to form the band Crash Karma.
**Brian Bern's Post-Band Career (Post-2003)** : Brian Bern became a radio personality in Halifax and unsuccessfully auditioned to be the new singer for Stone Temple Pilots. He is also currently involved in the world of cryptocurrency.
**Jag Tana's Post-Band Career (Post-2003)** : Jag Tana started a record label, produced several acts, and worked on TV projects.
**Christian Tana's Post-Band Career (Post-2003)** : Christian Tana continued in the music industry, working with his brother's record label, serving as general manager of an event venue in Peterborough, and working as an event planner for a theater in Coburg, Ontario.
**Reunions (2012, 2016)** : The band reunited in 2012 with Brian Bern as singer, a reunion that lasted for approximately three years. A series of reunion dates across the country occurred in early 2016.

# curiosity

**Canadian Content Laws (January 1971)** : Introduced in January 1971, these laws mandated that Canadian radio stations devote 30% of their playlists to Canadian artists. This artificial demand spurred the growth of a modern domestic music industry, including more labels, studios, promoters, and agents, fostering a Canadian star system.
**Growth of Canadian Music Industry (1971-1990s)** : Before 1971, Canada lacked a "first world" music industry, serving as a backwater for major labels. The Canadian content laws initiated a 20-year maturation period, leading to a truly world-class domestic music scene by the 1990s, with Canadian artists able to make a living by staying in Canada.

**Music BC Executive Director (Year not specified)** : Bob Death, former keyboardist for Rhymes with Orange, served as the Executive Director of Music BC, an organization involved in the music industry.
