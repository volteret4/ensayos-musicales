Adam Clayton

## curiosities
**Adam Clayton Drug Bust (August 6, 1989)** : Bassist Adam Clayton was arrested in the parking lot of the Blue Light pub in Glen Allen, Ireland, for possession of 19 grams of pot. He was charged with intent to traffic under Irish law.
**Adam Clayton Trial and Fine (September 1, 1989)** : Adam Clayton pled guilty at his trial, held on September 1, 1989, and was fined 25,000 pounds, which was donated to the Women's Aid Refugee Center.
**Adam Clayton No Criminal Record (September 1989)** : As a result of the Probation of Offenders Act coming into effect, no conviction was officially recorded for Adam Clayton, preventing him from having a criminal record. This allowed U2's scheduled tour to begin on September 15.

# artist - R.E.M.

## venues
Trafalgar Square

## curiosities
**British Airways Flight Incident (April 21, 2001)** : Guitarist Peter Buck experienced a severe adverse reaction to Ambien (a prescription sleeping pill) and approximately 15 glasses of red wine during a British Airways flight from Seattle to London. He was en route to play a concert for Nelson Mandela at Trafalgar Square.
**On-Board Disruptive Behavior (April 21, 2001)** : Peter Buck's actions included attempting to insert a CD into a food trolley, ripping up a formal warning card, grabbing a cutlery knife, smearing yogurt on himself and the cabin, and attacking flight crew members. The flight was nearly diverted due to his belligerence.
**Trial and Acquittal (April 5, 2002)** : Peter Buck was charged with being drunk on a plane, two counts of common assault, and one count of damaging British Airways' property. After his first trial collapsed, a second three-week trial featured Bono of U2 testifying as a character witness. He was acquitted on April 5, 2002, when the jury accepted doctor's testimony that his actions were a result of "non-insane automatism" caused by an unusual reaction between Ambien and alcohol. Peter Buck flew home to Seattle on British Airways.

# artist - David Bowie

## albums
**Station to Station (1976)** : A platinum-selling album that David Bowie claims to have no memory of recording, writing, releasing, or performing due to severe drug use.

## curiosities
**Los Angeles Drug Haze (1970s)** : In the 1970s, particularly during his time in Los Angeles, Bowie subsisted on a diet of milk, red peppers, and cocaine, leading to alarming weight loss, reportedly to less than 100 pounds.
**Alien Overlords and Egyptian Artifacts (1970s)** : Convinced he was being monitored and targeted for kidnapping by invisible alien overlords, Bowie filled his house with Egyptian artifacts for protection.
**Paranoia and Occult Fears (1970s)** : Traveled with a telescope to scan hotel rooftops for alien spacecraft and employed an assistant to buy UFO books. He also feared witches planning to steal his semen and kept his urine and fingernail clippings in the fridge to prevent them from being used for spells.
**Secret Messages and Backward Records (1970s)** : Believed he was receiving secret, satanic messages from The Rolling Stones and from himself via the future, leading him to have a special turntable made to play his own albums backward in search of these hidden communications.
**Confrontation with Cocaine Dealer (1970s)** : Attempted to run down a cocaine dealer in a parking garage because he believed he was being ripped off.
**Amnesia Regarding "Station to Station" (1976)** : Despite its platinum success, David Bowie has no recollection of recording, writing, releasing, or performing the "Station to Station" album due to his severe cocaine addiction during that period.

## curiosities
**Pancreas Explosion (1994)** : In 1994, after 13 years with Guns N' Roses and chronic excessive drinking and cocaine use, bassist Duff McKagan's pancreas exploded.
**Internal Acid Spill and Third-Degree Burns (1994)** : The explosion of his pancreas caused digestive enzymes to spill over his stomach, seep through his intestines, and reach his thigh muscles, creating an internal "acid spill" that burned and ate away at organs and muscles, resulting in third-degree burns inside his abdomen.
**Life-Saving Surgery and Permanent Sobriety (1994)** : Duff McKagan survived the life-threatening incident after emergency surgery, during which doctors noted steam, smoke, acidic residue, and the smell of disintegrating organs. He subsequently quit drinking entirely, as even one shot of whiskey could be fatal.

# artist - Red Hot Chili Peppers

## members
John Frusciante

## albums
**Californication (1999)** : The first album released by the Red Hot Chili Peppers after John Frusciante rejoined the band clean and sober, following his severe heroin addiction.

## songs
**Scar Tissue (1999)** : A song from the "Californication" album, which implicitly references John Frusciante's past struggles with addiction and the physical scars it left.

## curiosities
**Severe Heroin Addiction (1992-1997)** : John Frusciante developed a deep heroin addiction, starting after joining the Red Hot Chili Peppers, which led him to quit the band mid-tour in Japan in 1992. He spent the mid-1990s in Los Angeles in a profound junkie state.
**Physical Deterioration and Health Crises (Mid-1990s)** : During his addiction, Frusciante became emaciated (described as a "skeleton covered in thin skin"), suffered from arms covered in track marks and scars, skin abscesses, a near-fatal blood infection, fears of gangrene and amputation, and burn marks from free-basing. His skin began to slough off.
**Dental Decay and Costly Replacement (Mid-1990s)** : His teeth rotted out completely, reduced to "smelly, crumbly, discolored stumps" riddled with infection. After getting clean, he had them all pulled and replaced with fake ones at a cost exceeding $70,000.
**Impact on Creativity and Johnny Depp Documentary (Mid-1990s)** : Felt unable to write music or play guitar, only finding happiness when high. His squalid living conditions were documented in an unreleased film titled "Stuff" by Johnny Depp.
**Loss of Home and Guitars (Mid-1990s)** : His house in the Hollywood Hills eventually burned down, destroying his remaining vintage guitar collection (many others had been pawned for drugs).
**Rejoining the Band and Rehabilitation (January 1998)** : The Red Hot Chili Peppers decided they would only continue if John Frusciante returned. He checked into a treatment facility in January 1998, with Anthony Kiedis buying him a new guitar as a gesture of support. By April, he was well enough to rehearse.
**Post-Addiction Lifestyle (Since 1998)** : Clean since 1998, John Frusciante exercises daily, eats unprocessed food, abstains from smoking and most sexual activity, and practices Vipassana Buddhist meditation, immersing himself in music as a clean person. He also underwent numerous skin grafts for his arm scars.

# artist - Alice in Chains

## members

## venues

## curiosities
**Last Performance and Hermit-Like Existence (July 3, 1996 - April 2002)** : Layne Staley gave his last performance with Alice in Chains on July 3, 1996, after which he withdrew from everyone, living a hermit-like existence in a condo on the 4500 block of 8th Avenue Northeast in Seattle's University District.
**Extreme Physical Deterioration (Post-1996)** : His body became an emaciated shell, dropping to about 80 pounds. He suffered from rotted teeth, liver failure, constant vomiting, and loss of bowel control, using drugs primarily to alleviate dope sickness rather than to get high.
**Isolation and Public Sightings (Post-1996)** : He was rarely seen, with drugs delivered to his door. His only public sightings were occasionally at a neighborhood bar called The Rainbow, where he would sit alone, eyes closed, and nod off without ordering or drinking anything.
**Discovery of Body and Estimated Death Date (April 19, 2002)** : An anonymous 911 call on Friday, April 19, 2002, led police to his condo. At 5:41 pm, police kicked in the door and found his body on the couch, surrounded by drug paraphernalia including syringes and crack pipes.
**Unrecognizable Remains and Cause of Death (April 2002)** : Staley's body was in such a state that it was unrecognizable, requiring 24 hours for positive identification by the King County Medical Office. The coroner estimated his time of death as April 5, 2002, which tragically coincided with the date Kurt Cobain had killed himself. The autopsy determined the cause of death was an overdose from a speedball (a mixture of cocaine and heroin).

# label - Factory Records

## curiosities
**Financial Collapse (Early 1990s)** : By the early 1990s, Factory Records was under severe financial stress due to a miserable recession, falling property values, and high interest rates, causing its asset value to plummet from 3.5 million pounds to 900,000 pounds, with 2.5 million pounds borrowed.
**Failed London Records Acquisition** : An attempt to sell assets to the more financially solid London Records for 4 million pounds collapsed when it was discovered that Factory Records had no legally binding contracts with its artists, as no signatures were ever obtained.
**Role of Happy Mondays in Demise** : The extreme drug-related expenses, property damage, and destructive behavior of the Happy Mondays were cited as a primary factor in the label's ultimate downfall.

# curiosity

**Human Brain and Dopamine Reward System** : The amygdala, cerebellum, and nucleus accumbens are three parts of the brain involved in the creation and regulation of dopamine, the "feel-good hormone." Dopamine is released when positive stimuli like orgasm, cocaine, or a great song are detected, signaling the body for more of the experience, thus intrinsically linking sex, drugs, and rock and roll.
**Probation of Offenders Act (Ireland) - Legal Impact** : This act allowed for Adam Clayton of U2 to avoid a criminal conviction despite pleading guilty to a drug charge in 1989. He received a fine that was donated to charity, and no criminal record was recorded, which allowed the band's upcoming tour to proceed as scheduled.
**Internet Entertainment Group (IEG) Operations** : IEG was an early internet company known for obtaining and distributing celebrity sex tapes, including the infamous Pam Anderson Tommy Lee tape. The head of IEG, Seth Warshavsky, fled to Thailand after a judge ruled against him in a separate $1.4 million sex tape issue, impacting the attempted release of Perry Farrell's tape.
**Non-Insane Automatism - Legal Defense** : This legal term describes non-voluntary behavior, successfully used in 2002 to acquit R.E.M.'s Peter Buck for his disruptive actions on a British Airways flight. It was argued that his behavior was caused by an unusual and non-voluntary reaction between the prescription sleeping pill Ambien and red wine.
