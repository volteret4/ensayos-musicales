## albums
**Inxs (1980)** : First self-titled album, released in October 1980, featured the Australian hit "Just Keep Walking."
**Underneath the Colours (1981)** : Second Australia-only album, released in November 1981.
**Shabooh Shoobah (1982)** : First international album, released October 13, 1982, introduced the band to an international audience.
**The Swing (1984)** : Fourth album and second international release, produced by Nile Rodgers in New York; became a huge hit at home in Australia (one of the five biggest selling domestic albums), Canada, Argentina, and France, selling a million copies in the US.
**Listen Like Thieves (1985)** : Fifth album, released October 14, 1985, marked their international breakthrough.
**Kick (1987)** : Released October 12, 1987, became a number one album in Australia, Canada, and New Zealand; number three in the US and France; and a top 10 record in the UK. Estimated total sales were north of 20 million copies, including a million in Canada.
**X (1990)** : Released in 1990, sold extremely well.
**Welcome to Wherever You Are (1992)** : Released in 1992, performed below expectations, attributed to the public's growing fascination with grunge and alternative music.
**Full Moon, Dirty Hearts (1993)** : Released November 1993, fared no better than its predecessor.
**Greatest Hits (1994)** : Released in 1994, to fulfill a contract with an American company, as mentioned by Michael Hutchence in a TV interview.
**Elegantly Wasted (1997)** : Released seven months before Michael Hutchence's death.

## songs
**Simple Simon (1980)** : First single, released May 1980.
**Just Keep Walking (1980)** : An Australian hit from their self-titled debut album.
**The One Thing (1982)** : The big single from the album Shabooh Shoobah.
**What You Need (1985)** : The song that ignited their international breakthrough from the album Listen Like Thieves, originally with the working title "Funk Song No. 13."
**Need You Tonight (1987)** : A major single from the Kick album.
**Never Tear Us Apart (1987)** : A prominent song from the Kick album, heavily featured in Michael Hutchence's funeral service.
**Suicide Blonde (1990)** : Allegedly inspired by Kylie Minogue after she announced her intention to dye her hair during their relationship.
**Elegantly Wasted (1997)** : The title track of their 1997 album.

## curiosities
**Early Band Name (1979)** : The group changed their name from Ferris Brothers to In Excess in 1979.
**Grueling Australian Pub Circuit (1970s-1980s)** : Developed their uniquely funky swagger by frequently playing original material on the challenging Australian pub circuit, often performing at least 300 shows a year.
**First American Performance Audience (March 1983)** : Their debut performance in America, a show in San Diego, was attended by only 24 people, contrasting with audiences of 14,000 in Australia by mid-1983.
**Record Label Rejection of Kick Album (1987)** : The band's manager bypassed the New York record company, which had rejected the Kick album as "too funky, too dancing," by releasing it through college and alternative radio, leading to immediate success.
**Massive Concert Grosses (1987-1991)** : During their peak, a single show in Mexico City grossed over a million dollars, and a show at Wembley Stadium in London grossed over three million, amounts considered huge for the era.
**Record-breaking Attendance in Rio (Late 1980s)** : More than 100,000 fans attended an InXS concert in Rio de Janeiro.
**Second Highest Selling Australian Band (Pre-1997)** : The band sold nearly 80 million albums, placing them second only to AC/DC in terms of Australian success stories.
**Post-Hutchence Continuation (1998 onwards)** : After a year-long hiatus following Michael Hutchence's death, the band continued with guest singers, including JD Fortune, who was selected via the reality TV series Rockstar, fired after one album, and later rehired.
**Documentaries (2017, 2019)** : Featured in "Michael Hutchence, the last rock and roll star" (2017) and "Mystified Michael Hutchence" (2019).
**Hypothetical Heritage Status (Contemporary)** : The band would likely be a significant heritage act today, touring to baby boomer fans and their children for substantial earnings if Hutchence were alive.

## curiosities
**Early Life and Accent** : Grew up across Sydney, Brisbane, Hong Kong, Los Angeles, and the Bronx, which contributed to his distinct, somewhat "odd" accent.
**Swimming Accident** : Ended a promising swimming career after severely breaking his arm.
**Early Poetry and Singing Job (1976)** : Developed an interest in poetry after his injury, leading to a singing job for a local toy store in Hong Kong at age 16.
**Quote on "Pop Eats Its Young" (1994)** : In 1994, reflecting on Kurt Cobain's suicide, Hutchence remarked, "People killing themselves over stuff they shouldn't. Pop eats its young, that's for sure."
**International Rock Superstar Status (1980s)** : Was one of Australia's biggest international rock superstars, leading a band that sold nearly 80 million albums.
**Acting Role in Dogs in Space (1986)** : Appeared in the film "Dogs in Space."
**Max Q Side Project (Late 1980s)** : Maintained a side project called Max Q.
**Party Drugs and "Wild Man of Rock" Persona (Late 1980s - 1990s)** : Known for extensive use of cocaine, ecstasy, methadone, and alcohol, frequently seen openly using drugs at fashionable nightclubs, and involved in drunken brawls, earning him the British tabloid moniker "the Wild Man of Rock."
**High-Profile Relationships (1980s - 1990s)** : Dated prominent women including Virginia Hey (Bond girl), Belinda Carlisle (The Go-Go's singer), Kylie Minogue (pop singer), Helena Christensen (supermodel), and Paula Yates (TV host).
**Early Promiscuity (Early Career)** : In the band's formative years, he was known to have sex with up to three women in a single night in the back of the band's panel van.
**"Corrupting" Kylie Minogue (Late 1980s)** : Admitted his "hobby" was "corrupting" Kylie Minogue, transforming her image from a "mild-mannered pop star to this supervixen dude," with tabloids reporting discoveries of handcuffs in her luggage and tales of all-night ecstasy binges.
**Copenhagen Head Injury (August 1992)** : Suffered a fractured skull after being assaulted by a cab driver in Copenhagen, hitting his head on the curb, resulting in the permanent loss of his senses of smell and taste.
**Personality Changes Post-Injury (1992 onwards)** : Became depressed and aggressive following his head injury, leading to physical altercations with band members, including one instance where he brandished a knife.
**Sexually Charged TV Interview with Paula Yates (1994)** : Conducted a highly publicized, seven-minute interview with Paula Yates on her show "The Big Breakfast," which was described as the "most sexually charged TV interview of all time," featuring them entwined on a bed and viewed by two million people.
**Hotel Liaison with Paula Yates (March 1995)** : His relationship with Helena Christensen ended after he was discovered with Paula Yates in a hotel, becoming a major tabloid story in England with sensational headlines like "six illegal things Michael did to me on our first night."
**Drug Raid at Paula Yates's House (September 1996)** : A police raid at Paula Yates's Chelsea home uncovered a significant quantity of opium and smoking pipes, which Hutchence and Yates claimed were planted by employees.
**Fatherhood and Custody Battle Strain (1996-1997)** : Became the father of Heavenly Hiraani Tiger Lily with Paula Yates, and the intense custody battle between Yates and Bob Geldof exacerbated his addictions and emotional distress.
**Paranoia and Depression (1997)** : Exhibited paranoia in early 1997, believing his phones and homes were bugged, and sought treatment for "minor depression" from psychiatrist Mark Collins on October 17, 1997, who noted no suicidal thoughts at that time.
**Appearance and Erratic Behavior on Final Tour (Spring/Summer 1997)** : During his final tour, he appeared visibly tired and older than his 37 years, with reports of erratic behavior during performances.
**Death in Sydney (November 22, 1997)** : His body was discovered in Room 524 of the Ritz Carlton Hotel in Sydney on November 22, 1997.
**Last Dinner with Father (November 21, 1997)** : The night before his death, he had dinner with his father and stepmother at Flavor of India, appearing jovial, though his father perceived something amiss.
**Final Binge (November 21-22, 1997)** : After dinner, he returned to his hotel suite and continued a binge of daiquiris, champagne, and beer with Australian soap opera star and ex-girlfriend Kim Wilson and her boyfriend, who left around 4 AM.
**Loud Argument with Bob Geldof (Early Morning, November 22, 1997)** : Made two phone calls to Bob Geldof between 4-5 AM, engaging in a loud, 20-minute argument during which Hutchence tried to persuade Geldof to drop a court injunction, screaming phrases like "Your children hate you. I'm their father, little man."
**Distressed Call from Paula Yates (5:38 AM, November 22, 1997)** : Paula Yates called him at 5:38 AM, later stating that he sounded "really desperate."
**Mailed Letters (Early Morning, November 22, 1997)** : Visited the hotel front desk, appearing "really rough," to mail two letters—one to Paula Yates and another to a friend in Hong Kong—marking the last time he was seen alive.
**Final Phone Calls (Morning, November 22, 1997)** : Made multiple phone calls, leaving voicemails for his personal manager Martha Troup at 9:38 AM and 9:50 AM, and speaking with ex-girlfriend Michelle Bennett at 9:54 AM, during which he sounded "crying and depressed and drunk."
**Violent Rage and Self-Harm in Hotel Room (Between 9:50-10:30 AM, November 22, 1997)** : Experienced a violent rage in his hotel room, scattering five types of prescription pills, breaking his hand by punching the wall, throwing furniture, and cutting his face.
**Method of Death (Between 9:50-10:30 AM, November 22, 1997)** : Hanged himself using a snakeskin leather belt fastened to a door.
**Body Discovery (10:50 AM, November 22, 1997)** : His naked body was found in a kneeling position facing the door, with his head deeply strained into the belt loop, causing the buckle to break; no suicide note was present.
**Funeral Broadcast (November 27, 1997)** : His funeral was held on November 27, 1997, at St. Andrew's Cathedral, and was broadcast live on Australian television.
**Paula Yates's Funeral Actions (November 27, 1997)** : Paula Yates attended the funeral in a low-cut dress and, according to one account, slipped a gram of heroin into his pocket before the coffin was closed.
**Autopsy Findings and Official Cause of Death (Late 1997)** : An autopsy revealed his blood contained alcohol, cocaine, Prozac, and other prescription drugs; the coroner officially ruled his death a suicide.
**Autoerotic Asphyxiation Theory (1999)** : Paula Yates suggested on "60 Minutes Australia" in 1999 that his death might have resulted from solo sex play (autoerotic asphyxiation) gone wrong, a theory the coroner explicitly rejected, stating, "there is no forensic or other evidence to substantiate this suggestion."
**History of Sexual Kinks (Pre-1997)** : The autoerotic asphyxiation theory was fueled by his reported history of sexual kinks, including his mother's statement that he had been involved with sadomasochists months prior.
**Pursuit of Ultimate Sexual High (Pre-1997)** : In one of his final interviews, he spoke about his desire to achieve the ultimate sexual high.
**Brain Tumor Suicide Theory (Pre-1997)** : Some believed he was convinced he had a brain tumor and intended to commit suicide before the tumor killed him, despite CAT scans confirming no tumor; a friend claimed he said he "would do a Kurt before the cancer got to him."
**Paula Yates Blames Bob Geldof (Post-1997)** : Paula Yates hysterically blamed Bob Geldof for Michael's death, stating, "He killed Michael," though she later vacillated between this accusation and the autoerotic asphyxiation theory.
**Dispute Over Ashes (Post-1997)** : Following his cremation, a significant dispute arose over his ashes among his family, Paula Yates, and the band.
**Financial Problems and Estate (Post-1997)** : Despite the millions of albums sold by InXS and his investment in 'Crocodile Dundee', he had been experiencing financial difficulties for years and was practically bankrupt; a complex network of trusts and holding companies held at least eight million pounds, but legal fees consumed virtually all remaining assets.

# curiosity

**Pre-Internet Music Information Flow (Before 1990s)** : Music news traveled slowly and through multiple filters before the internet, often leading to information being stripped or drastically altered, especially concerning sensitive events like celebrity deaths.
**Kurt Cobain's Death Comparison (1994)** : Michael Hutchence's death was widely compared to Kurt Cobain's, both involving suicide and sparking considerable confusion, speculation, and conspiracy theories.
**Bill Clinton-Monica Lewinsky Scandal (1997)** : The widespread focus on the Bill Clinton-Monica Lewinsky scandal in North America at the time contributed to less comprehensive coverage of Michael Hutchence's death in the region.
**Paula Yates and Bob Geldof's Marriage (1977-1995)** : Paula Yates and Bob Geldof were married for 18 years before their separation in February 1995.
**Paula Yates's Breast Implants Tabloid Coverage (Mid-1990s)** : Paula Yates's new breast implants received extensive tabloid coverage during her high-profile relationship with Michael Hutchence.
**Bob Geldof's Custody Battle (1996)** : Bob Geldof initiated a custody battle for his three daughters with Paula Yates—Fifi, Trixabel, Peaches, and Pixie—a story heavily exploited by tabloids.
**Hysterical Fan at Hutchence's Funeral (November 27, 1997)** : A "weirdo" fan, known for disrupting public events, appeared at Michael Hutchence's funeral, screaming obscenities and threatening to hang himself from the church balcony.
**Post-Mortem Photos of Hutchence (Post-1997)** : A magazine published post-mortem photographs of Michael Hutchence, reportedly in connection with the autoerotic asphyxiation theory.
**Paula Yates's Later Life and Scandals (1997-2000)** : Following Michael Hutchence's death, Paula Yates endured multiple rehab stints, at least one suicide attempt, and sold her story to a news organization for nearly a million dollars.
**Paula Yates's Paternity Scandal (Post-1997)** : A scandal erupted when DNA tests revealed that her biological father was British TV personality Huey Green, contradicting her mother's claim that it was Jesse Yates.
**Paula Yates's Death (September 17, 2000)** : Paula Yates died on September 17, 2000, less than three years after Michael Hutchence, from a heroin overdose on her daughter Pixie's 10th birthday, while with her four-year-old daughter Tiger Lily.
**Bob Geldof Adopts Tiger Lily (2000)** : Bob Geldof took custody of all his and Paula Yates's children and formally adopted Tiger Lily, Michael Hutchence's daughter.
**Peaches Geldof's Death (2014)** : Peaches Geldof, one of Bob and Paula's daughters, died of a heroin overdose in 2014 at the age of 25.
**Modern Disinformation Landscape (Contemporary)** : The narrative highlights how the internet dramatically accelerates the spread of conspiracy theories and disinformation, contrasting it with the slower news dissemination during Hutchence's era.
