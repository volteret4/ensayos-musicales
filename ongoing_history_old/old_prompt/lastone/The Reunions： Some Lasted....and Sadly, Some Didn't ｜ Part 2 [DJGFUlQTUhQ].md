## albums
**Fourth – Reunion Album** : Released following the band's reunion announcement on June 26, 2007, preceded by a recorded single.

## songs
**Love Is Noise (2008)** : The comeback single, released August 10, 2008, 14 months after the band announced their reunion.

## curiosities
**Hiatus (September 1995 – 1996)** : A period where the band "ceased to exist" before their major breakup, later described as a hiatus rather than a formal breakup.
**Internal Conflict in Dusseldorf (June 7, 1998)** : Singer Richard Ashcroft and guitarist Nick McCabe had a physical altercation after a show in Dusseldorf, Germany, resulting in Ashcroft's face being "pounded" and McCabe's broken hand.
**Nick McCabe's Initial Departure (June 7, 1998)** : Following the fight in Dusseldorf, Nick McCabe decided he was done with the band and was replaced by session guitarist BJ Cole to complete remaining commitments.
**Last Show Before Breakup (August 29, 1998)** : The band played their final show together in Ireland on August 29, 1998, though the official breakup announcement came later.
**Official Breakup Announcement (April 28, 1999)** : The band made the official announcement of their breakup on April 28, 1999.
**Reunion Announcement (June 26, 2007)** : After eight years, one month, and 30 days apart, the band announced their reunion.
**Simon Tongue Excluded from Reunion** : Guitarist Simon Tongue was available but was deliberately kept out of the reunion.
**Second Breakup (August 10, 2008)** : The band broke up again, allegedly because Nick McCabe and Simon Jones believed Richard Ashcroft was using the reunion to further his solo career, leading to a "big fight."
**Serial Breaker-Upper Status** : Following their second breakup, the band was declared a "serial breaker, upper" and banned from future reunion programs by the podcast host.

# artist - Orchestral Manoeuvres in the Dark

## genres
Technopop

## albums
**Architectures & Morality (1981) – Classic Technopop Album** : A classic Technopop album, the original lineup received serious offers to perform it in full on a tour, which became the primary reason for their reunion.

## songs
**Joan of Arc (1981)** : A single from their 1981 album, Architectures & Morality.

## curiosities
**Band Formation and Era (1978)** : Began their solid run from the very beginnings of the Technopop era in 1978.
**Initial Dissolution (October 1996)** : The group gracefully retired in October 1996, sensing that a synthesizer band from the 80s no longer suited the prevailing Britpop era.
**Reunion (January 1, 2006)** : The original lineup reunited on January 1, 2006, after being apart for nine years, three months, and one day.
**Motivation for Reunion (2006)** : The reunion was prompted by "serious offers" to perform their 1981 album, Architectures & Morality, in its entirety on a concert tour, described as "too good to resist" and involving "lots of cash."
**Continued Togetherness** : The band has remained together since their 2006 reunion.

# artist - Crowded House

## members
Neil Finn
Paul Hester

## venues
Sydney Opera House

## songs
**Don't Dream It's Over** : A song by Crowded House.

## curiosities
**Origin from Split Enz** : The band grew out of Split Enz, which was one of the most successful New Zealand bands of all time.
**Active Period (1985 – 1996)** : Led by singer Neil Finn, the band had a series of best-selling albums between 1985 and 1996, gaining fans worldwide including Eddie Vedder of Pearl Jam.
**Farewell Gig at Sydney Opera House (November 24, 1996)** : The band performed a giant farewell gig on the steps of the famous Sydney Opera House to raise money for the Sydney Children's Hospital, which was experiencing a cash crunch.
**Paul Hester's Post-Band Career** : Paul Hester became a TV star in Australia, notably playing "Paul the cook" for The Wiggles.
**Paul Hester's Death (March 26, 2005)** : Paul Hester, suffering from personal problems and deep depression, died by hanging himself on March 26, 2005.
**Reunion Announcement (January 22, 2007)** : The band officially announced their reunion on January 22, 2007, after 10 years, one month, and 28 days apart.
**Motivation for Reunion (2007)** : The reunion was spurred by Paul Hester's funeral and the 10-year anniversary of the Sydney Opera House performance (coinciding with a DVD release), which led to a "reconnection" among the members.
**Continued Togetherness** : The band is still together after spending over 10 years apart following their reunion.

# artist - Faith No More

## members
Mike Patton
Billy Gould
Mike Bordin
Jim Martin

## genres
Rock
Heavy guitars mixed with rap style vocals

## albums
**The Real Thing** : An album by Faith No More, which includes the song "Epic."

## songs
**Epic** : A song from Faith No More's album "The Real Thing."

## curiosities
**Active Period (1981 – April 19, 1998)** : The band was active from 1981 until their official breakup on April 19, 1998.
**Breakup Reason (1998)** : No specific reason was given for the breakup; singer Mike Patton had reportedly grown bored and wanted to explore other musical ideas.
**Official Breakup Statement (April 19, 1998)** : Bass player Billy Gould sent out a fax and email stating the band was breaking up mutually, enabling members to pursue individual projects, and jokingly blamed "Puffy" for starting it.
**Post-Breakup Activities** : Members engaged in other bands like Imperial Teen and Mr. Bungle, made guest appearances, produced records for others, formed record labels, and drummer Mike Bordin worked with Ozzy Osbourne for a while.
**Jim Martin's Post-Band Career** : Guitarist Jim Martin became a professional pumpkin grower after the breakup, known for trying to grow 1,300-pound pumpkins.
**Reunion Announcement (February 24, 2009)** : The band announced their reunion on February 24, 2009, after 10 years, nine months, and 28 days apart.
**Reunion Lineup (2009)** : The reunion proceeded without original guitarist Jim Martin.
**Motivation for Reunion (2009)** : The reunion was speculated to be driven by money, with the observation that "there's no pension plan in rock," and was followed by an amazingly successful summer tour of Europe.
**New Generation of Fans (2009)** : A new generation of fans discovered the band's music during their time apart, contributing to the success of their reunion.

# artist - The Pixies

## members
Kim Deal
Black Francis
David Lovering
Joey Santiago
Kelly Deal

## venues
Vancouver
Minneapolis

## songs
**This One (2004)** : Performed live at the Fine Line Music Cafe in Minneapolis on April 13, 2004, which was the first show of their reunion tour.

## curiosities
**Breakup Tensions (Early 1990s)** : Tensions built throughout the early 90s due to creative differences, power struggles, and an endless cycle of recording and touring, making the band feel they weren't getting ahead.
**Opening for U2 (1992)** : The band toured opening for U2 during the Achtung Baby tour in 1992, playing to less than a quarter-full arenas and stadiums, as their musical style was a "bad fit" for the audience, despite U2's respect for the band.
**Jim Greer's Spin Magazine Article (1992)** : Bass player Kim Deal's boyfriend, Jim Greer, a writer for Spin Magazine, followed the Pixies on the U2 tour and documented their experiences with U2, management, and road crew, further increasing band tensions.
**Last Show Before Breakup (April 25, 1992)** : The band was "fried" after their last show with U2 in Vancouver on April 25, 1992, leading to a decision for a one-year break.
**Black Francis's Breakup Announcement (January 15, 1993)** : Singer Black Francis decided to leave for good, sending a fax to the management company on January 15, 1993, to announce his departure and the band's official breakup, a decision he later regretted.
**Black Francis's Solo Career** : After the breakup, Black Francis renamed himself Frank Black and released a string of solo albums.
**Kim Deal's Post-Breakup Project** : Kim Deal turned her Pixies side project, The Breeders, into a full-time band with her sister Kelly Deal.
**Kelly Deal's Legal Troubles** : Kelly Deal was busted when she accepted a FedEx package full of heroin, which took a couple of years to resolve.
**David Lovering's Post-Band Career** : Drummer David Lovering became a magician called "The Scientific Phenomenalist," applying his engineering knowledge to magic, though it was not financially lucrative and led to a "financial mess."
**Joey Santiago's Post-Band Career** : Guitarist Joey Santiago stayed with music, becoming a composer for TV and film, including writing a couple of songs for the series "Weeds."
**Increased Fame While Apart** : While the band was apart, their fame grew significantly, helped by bands like The Smashing Pumpkins and Nirvana publicly praising them, building their myth and legend.
**Reunion Motivation (2004)** : The reunion was motivated by "time healing wounds," the fact that no one was making much money separately, and significant financial offers for a reunion tour due to their increased fame.
**First Reunion Show (April 13, 2004)** : The band played together for the first time since their 1992 U2 show at a club in Minneapolis on April 13, 2004, after a gap of 11 years, two months, and 30 days.

# artist - My Bloody Valentine

## members
Kevin Shields
Debbie Gooch

## genres
Noise pop
Shoegazer

## labels
Island Records

## albums
**Loveless (1991) – Groundbreaking Noise Pop Album** : A groundbreaking noise pop album released on November 4, 1991, which was the epitome of the shoegazer sound and influenced subsequent music. Its high production cost practically bankrupted their original label.

## curiosities
**Bankruptcy of Original Label (1991)** : The high cost of making the "Loveless" album in 1991 almost bankrupted their original record label.
**Island Records Advance and Studio Troubles** : The band was signed to Island Records and received a half-million dollar advance, all of which was spent constructing a new studio that proved to be "awful" and plagued with technical problems.
**Band "Evaporation" (End of 1993 / July 1, 1993)** : By the end of 1993, the band had "just sort of evaporated" due to financial and technical issues, with July 1, 1993, being the best estimated date of their effective non-existence.
**Debbie Gooch's Post-Band Career** : Bass player Debbie Gooch was seen driving a cab in London, symbolizing the band's dire situation during their period of inactivity.
**Reunion Rumors (Summer 2007)** : Rumors of a reunion began circulating in the summer of 2007.
**Reunion Confirmation (November 15, 2007)** : Confirmation of the reunion came on November 15, 2007, with the announcement of three gigs for the following summer.
**Slow Pace of Reunion** : My Bloody Valentine is known for never rushing, reflected in their long period of inactivity and the deliberate pace of their reunion, which saw them apart for 14 years, four months, and 14 days.

# artist - Public Image Limited

## members
Johnny Lydon
Michael Balsari (Flea)

## genres
Experimental rock
Reggae
Dub
German rock (70s)
Funk
Industrial
Punk funk

## labels
Virgin Records

## albums
**Metal Box – Influential Re-release** : A very influential record in many circles, re-released in a 30th-anniversary edition around the time of the band's reunion in 2009.

## songs
**Death Disco** : A song inspired by the death of Johnny Lydon's mother.
**Memories** : A song from the "Metal Box" album.

## curiosities
**Formation (Christmas Day, 1978)** : Formed by Johnny Lydon (aka Johnny Rotten of the Sex Pistols) on Christmas Day, 1978, as an experimental post-Pistols project to explore new approaches to rock.
**Musical Influences** : Johnny Lydon explored reggae, dub, 1970s German rock, and hung out with funk musicians, developing a unique and experimental sound.
**Impact on Genres** : Had a profound effect on several genres of music, including industrial and punk funk.
**Influenced Artists** : Influenced Nine Inch Nails, Massive Attack, Manic Street Preachers, Ministry, and Primal Scream.
**Flea's Audition (1984)** : Michael Balsari, later known as Flea, auditioned for bass in the band in 1984 and was offered the gig, but decided to stick with his new band forming with Anthony (Red Hot Chili Peppers).
**Last Gig and Breakup (September 18, 1992)** : Their final gig was on September 18, 1992, becoming their last show when their label, Virgin Records, refused to pay for any more tour support.
**Johnny Lydon's Post-Band Activities** : Johnny Lydon ended up reuniting with the Sex Pistols and participated in a variety of other musical projects.
**Motivation for Reunion (Summer 2009)** : Johnny Lydon decided to resurrect Public Image Limited due to the 30th-anniversary re-release of their influential "Metal Box" album.
**Emotional Motivation for Reunion (2009)** : Further motivated by personal losses: his father died of cancer in 2008, and his brother's cancer, which had been in remission, recurred, reminding him of his mother's death, which inspired the song "Death Disco."
**Reunion Announcement (September 7, 2009)** : The actual reunion announcement was made on September 7, 2009, following 16 years, 11 months, and 20 days apart.
**Reunion Lineup (2009)** : While Johnny Lydon was "out front," the rest of the band members were different from the original lineup, but it was still considered "Johnny's band."

# curiosity

**Financial Motivation for Reunions** : A common thread in many reunion stories is money, with promoters paying "big bucks" for old bands to tour again, serving as an "excellent way to top up the retirement fund very, very quickly," especially as "there's no pension plan in rock." The longer a band has been apart, the more lucrative the reunion can be.
