# artist - Alan Fried

## curiosities

**Playing "Race Records" (1951)** : A white radio DJ who started his career in Cleveland and became famous for playing "race records" (songs by Black artists) in 1951. These records were considered "forbidden fruit" for many young white teenagers at the time.
**Coining the Term "Rock and Roll" (1951)** : Fried is credited with coining the term "Rock and Roll" for the music he played, drawing on African-American slang for sex that had been used on records since at least the 1920s. He defined the genre as "swing music with a modern name" and likely used the phrase as a "secret code" that teenagers understood while their parents remained unaware of its sexual connotations
**Moondog Infringement Lawsuit (1956)** : Fried originally called his radio show "The Moondog House" and referred to himself as the "King of the Moon Doggers" after co-opting the title from Moondog’s "Moondog Symphony". In 1956, Moondog successfully sued Fried and the radio station WINS; Fried was ordered to pay $6,000 in damages and permanently cease using the name.
**Unsuccessful Trademark Attempt** : Following the success of the term, Fried attempted to trademark "Rock and Roll" to maintain exclusive use of the phrase, though this effort ultimately failed.

# artist - Bo Diddley

## curiosities

**The "Bo Diddley Beat"** : Diddley introduced a specific rhythm to rock and roll that is now universally known as the "Bo Diddley beat". While he did not invent the rhythm—as its history traces back at least 50 years—he was the one who effectively integrated it into the genre, influencing artists ranging from Iggy Pop to Jett.

# artist - Charlie Christian

## instruments

Electric guitar

## curiosities

**The First Guitar Hero** : A Black musician from Oklahoma, Christian is recognized as the world's first guitar hero for transforming the electric guitar from a rhythm-only instrument into a lead solo instrument. He began innovating with guitar solos during jam sessions as early as 1931, eventually acquiring a new electric model in 1936.
**Joining Benny Goodman (1939)** : Christian’s impact was solidified when he joined Benny Goodman's Big Band in 1939, where he was given the platform to improvise and solo. His work created the foundations for the role of the modern lead guitarist.
**Death (1941)** : His groundbreaking career was cut short when he died of tuberculosis on March 3rd, 1941, at the age of 25.

# artist - Chuck Berry

## instruments

Electric guitar

## curiosities

**The Father of Rock and Roll** : Berry is credited more than anyone else with taking rhythm and blues and "rocking it up" to create rock as a separate, distinct musical thing. He established the foundation of the genre through riff-heavy guitar playing, swagger, and lyrics aimed squarely at the troubles and lives of young people.
**The Voyager Golden Disk (1977)** : Berry's music was the only rock and roll song included on the Voyager Golden Disk, a gold phonograph record launched on Voyager I and II. This ensures that if an alien civilization ever finds the record, Berry's work will be their first exposure to the genre.

# artist - Death

## members

## curiosities

**First Black Punk Band** : Composed of three brothers from Detroit, Death is cited by some as the first true punk band, potentially recording punk-like music before The Ramones' 1974 debut. They were inspired to form after seeing The Beatles perform on Ed Sullivan in 1964.
**Name Change and Breakup** : Originally named Rockfire Funk Express, they changed their name to Death after their father died in a car accident. The band broke up in 1977 after releasing several overlooked records in small runs of roughly 500 copies.
**Rediscovery (2009)** : Their early 1970s recordings were rediscovered in 2009, gaining a new generation of fans for their pioneering "punky" sound.

# artist - George W. Johnson

## venues

Vaudeville

## instruments

Thomas Edison's wax cylinders

## songs

**The Laughing Song (1890)** : Originally recorded in 1890, this became Johnson's most popular track and was the best-selling song in the world by 1894. It reached number one on the brand-new music charts of the day and is said to have sold up to 50,000 copies.

## curiosities

**The World's First Pop Star** : A former slave freed around 1853, Johnson became the first person to have a hit record and the first Black performer to make the music charts. He was recognized as the world's biggest pop star in the late 1890s after starting his career singing on the streets of New York in the 1870s.
**Cylinder Recording Labor** : Because early wax cylinders were master recordings, Johnson had to perform his songs individually for each unit sold. To meet sales of 50,000 copies, he sang "The Laughing Song" between 12,000 and 15,000 times, often repeating the song up to 50 times a day into five recorders at once.

# artist - Jackie Brenston and His Delta Cats

## members

Willie Kizart

## venues

Riverside Hotel

## songs

**Rocket 88 (1951)** : Released in late March 1951, this track is frequently nominated as the first true rock and roll record. It featured upfront vocals, heavy piano and saxophone power, and a good-time backbeat.

## curiosities

**Busted Bass Amp Innovation** : During a 75-mile drive to the recording session on March 2nd, 1951, bass player Willie Kizart's amp was dropped, causing the speaker cone to loosen. Producer Sam Phillips liked the resulting distorted "buzz" sound and recorded it as is, inadvertently creating a foundational rock and roll sonic element.

# artist - John Lydon

## curiosities

**Childhood Meningitis (1963)** : At age seven, Lydon contracted spinal meningitis, likely from playing in rat-infested puddles in North London. He fell into a coma for seven months and spent a full year in the hospital, which resulted in the loss of four years of his early memories.
**Formation of the Rotten Persona** : The illness left Lydon with a permanent spinal curvature and a piercing stare, and it changed his personality into that of a fierce contrarian. These traits directly informed his "Johnny Rotten" identity and his role as the frontman for the Sex Pistols.
**Sex Pistols Audition** : Lydon secured his position in the Sex Pistols at the "Sex" clothing shop on King's Road by singing Alice Cooper's "I'm 18" on the shop's jukebox. Malcolm McLaren was captivated by Lydon's DIY "I Hate Pink Floyd" T-shirt and his distinctive look.

# artist - Little Richard

## songs

**Tutti Frutti** : One of his most famous songs, the original and uncensored lyrics of which contained explicit hints at his sexuality.

## curiosities

**Rock's First Openly Gay Star** : Richard identified as gay or bisexual and used flamboyant, effeminate mannerisms and makeup to define his stage persona. He famously challenged Elvis Presley's "King" title by proclaiming himself "the queen" of rock and roll.
**Breaking the Color Barrier** : Richard was the first Black artist to truly cross over to white audiences, attracting integrated crowds to his high-energy shows and helping to break down racial barriers in the culture. His influence was massive, affecting artists from The Beatles and David Bowie to Lemmy of Motorhead.

# artist - Richard Hell

## songs

**Blank Generation** : A track featuring Hell, who is noted for "accidentally" making the safety pin a fashion staple through his personal style.

## curiosities

**Poverty and the Safety Pin** : Living as a struggling poet and musician in New York, Hell used safety pins to hold his falling-apart clothes together because he was extremely poor. This look was observed by Malcolm McLaren, who brought the "fashion statement" back to London and shared it with Vivienne Westwood.
**The Neon Boys and CBGB** : In 1972, Hell played in The Neon Boys, which evolved into the band Television. The band secured a residency at CBGB in 1973, which would become known as "ground zero" for the birth of punk rock.

# genre - Rock and Roll

## curiosities

**Birth and Evolution** : Rock and roll formed in the early 1950s as a "stew" of R&B, Blues, Country Western, Hillbilly, and traditional Black musical styles. Its birth was a gradual process rather than the result of a single song, facilitated by radio stations playing a hodgepodge of different materials.
**The Term's Origins** : The phrase "rock and roll" originally described the movement of a ship on rough seas and the animated singing in Black churches. It later became a euphemism for sex before being popularized as a musical term by DJ Alan Fried in 1951.

# genre - Gothic Rock

## curiosities

**Volcanic Roots** : The 1815 eruption of Mount Tambora in Indonesia led to "the year without a summer" in 1816, which inspired Mary Shelley to write _Frankenstein_. This book became a foundational text for gothic literature, which later influenced the dark themes and "ultra-noir" look of gothic rock in the late 1970s.
**Post-Punk Development** : As UK punk rock gained momentum in 1976, artists facing dire economic conditions gravitating toward "dark thoughts" about their life prospects. This led to a theatrical style featuring Victorian-era fashion and dark musical themes, eventually categorized as "gothic" by music writers.

# instrument - Transistor Radio

## curiosities

**The Social Media of Its Day** : Developed in 1948 by Bell Labs to replace vacuum tubes, the transistor enabled the creation of portable radios. This allowed teenagers to take their music with them and listen away from parental oversight, serving as the crucial delivery mechanism for the rock and roll revolution.

# curiosity

**The Supernova Theory of Music** : A theory posits that all human music can be traced back to mutations caused by a supernova event 41,000 years ago. The resulting cosmic radiation is theorized to have caused a beneficial change in the microcephaline gene, leading to larger human brains capable of complex thought and artistic expression.
**The Cotton Gin's Connection to Rock** : Eli Whitney's 1794 invention of the cotton gin significantly increased manual efficiency, which paradoxically led to plantation owners buying out or expelling former slaves from their land. This displacement contributed to the Great Migration, where millions of Black Americans moved to urban centers, fostering the development of jazz, blues, and R&B which eventually converged into rock and roll.
**The First Musical Instruments** : The earliest known musical instruments discovered by archaeologists are flutes crafted from vulture bones, dating back at least 30,000 years.
**The Safety Pin's Punk History** : Walter Hunt invented the safety pin in 1849 but sold the patent for $400 to settle a debt. Over a century later, the item became a prominent punk rock fashion staple after being adopted by Richard Hell and later the London punk scene.
