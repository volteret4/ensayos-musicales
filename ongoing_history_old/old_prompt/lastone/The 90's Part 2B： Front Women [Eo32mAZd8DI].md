# artist - The Ace of Cups

## curiosities
**Early All-Female Rock Band** : Formed in San Francisco by 1967, notable for being an all-female group who wrote their own songs, played instruments proficiently, and could "shred like a man," which was considered radical for the time.

**Breakup** : The band broke up by 1972, facing persistent sexist attitudes in the music industry that deemed female-fronted rock bands "silly."

# artist - Sonic Youth

## members

## genres

## albums
**Goo (1990) – First Major Label Album** : Co-produced by Butch Vig. Featured the song "Cool Thing," written by Kim Gordon.

## songs
**Cool Thing (1990)** : Featured on the album "Goo" (1990). Written by Kim Gordon after an encounter with LL Cool J, whose attitude towards women she disliked. Featured guest vocals from Chuck D of Public Enemy. Kim Gordon sang lead vocals and played bass on the track.

## curiosities
**No Wave Origins** : Emerged from the "weird New York City-based scene" known as No Wave.
**Guitar Sound Refinement** : Over ten years, the band refined their guitar sound, becoming ranked as one of the most important American indie rock bands of the 1980s.
**Democratic Structure** : Operated as a "democracy," with Kim Gordon not solely serving as the front person but having her turn as a songwriter and lead singer.
**Kim Gordon's Activism** : Kim Gordon wrote "Cool Thing" expressing a desire for liberation of "girls from male, white, corporate oppression."
**Kim Gordon's Early Life** : Kim Gordon was born in Rochester, New York, raised in Los Angeles, but formed her first band while a student at York University in Toronto.
**Kim Gordon as an Icon** : Became an icon for a "certain group of women" for her role in a cool indie band, influencing many young women, including Courtney Love.
**Kim Gordon's Other Creative Work** : Kim Gordon is also a visual artist (painting), former owner of a clothing line, has done acting, written books, directed music videos, and produced albums.
**Video Co-production** : Kim Gordon co-produced the music video for The Breeders' song "Cannonball" (1993).

## albums

**Reactivation** : The band was reactivated after The Pixies broke up, allowing Kim Deal to write new songs and return to the studio for their second album.
**Music Video Co-production** : The music video for "Cannonball" (1993) was co-produced by Kim Gordon of Sonic Youth.

**Shirley Manson's Audition** : Shirley Manson initially failed her audition but was given another chance due to her shared musical taste with the guys. She "nailed it" on the second attempt.
**Record Deal Acquisition** : Secured a record deal by sending samples to labels with minimal information, primarily leveraging Butch Vig's involvement (due to his "solid run of producing hit albums").

# curiosity

**"Summer of Love" Context (1967)** : A period of optimism where civil rights issues, the Vietnam War, drugs, free love, and women's liberation were prevalent. It was also a time when the idea of an all-female rock band like The Ace of Cups was still considered radical.
**Sexism in Early Rock Music** : Prevailing sexist attitudes in the late 1960s and early 1970s perpetuated the idea that female-fronted rock bands were "silly" and "could not rock," contributing to the breakup of The Ace of Cups by 1972. This changed significantly with the emergence of punk rock.
**Rise of Female-Fronted Bands in Punk** : Punk rock brought "new egalitarian values," leading fans to embrace groups fronted by, or entirely consisting of, women, including Blondie, The Pretenders, The Slits, Patti Smith, The Raincoats, Siouxsie and the Banshees, and The Plasmatics.
**Evolution of Women's Role in Rock (1990s)** : By "Generation X and the 90s," the idea of bands with female leaders or all-female members was no longer considered "weird at all," reflecting significant progress in women's roles in rock.
**"Smells Like Teen Spirit" Title Origin** : The title for Nirvana's iconic song originated from a remark made by Kathleen Hanna to Kurt Cobain in 1991, noting he "smelled sweet, almost like a particular type of deodorant marketed to teens." Cobain used this phrase, unaware it was a brand name.
**Butch Vig's Production Reputation** : Butch Vig was a highly regarded record producer known for a "solid run of producing hit albums," which helped bands like Garbage secure record deals even with little other information. He also worked on Sonic Youth's "Goo" and Nirvana's "Nevermind."
**90s Alt-Rock Female Influence List** : Key female fronted/dominated bands of the alt-rock 90s mentioned include Kim Gordon (Sonic Youth), Courtney Love (Hole), Bikini Kill, L7, The Breeders, Dolores O'Riordan (The Cranberries), Beth Gibbons (Portishead), Veruca Salt, Elastica, and Shirley Manson (Garbage).

**Podcast Series on Alt-Rock 90s** : This episode, "part two of chapter two" on women in Alt Rock bands, is part of a multi-part series on the alternative 90s. A previous episode covered solo female performers, and chapter three will focus on Grunge.
