# artist - Oasis

## members
Noel Gallagher
Liam Gallagher
Alan White (drummer in 2000, suffered from tendonitis)
Matt Dytin (replacement guitarist in 2000)

## albums
**Standing on the Shoulder of Giants (2000)** : Released on February 28, 2000. Noel Gallagher retrospectively stated the band should have taken a year off before making this record, believing it "should have never been made."
**Heathen Chemistry (2002)** : Album supported by a tour in late 2002, during which the Munich bar brawl occurred on December 1, 2002.
**Don't Believe The Truth (2005) – Sixth Studio Album** : Referred to in the source text as "What believed the hype," released on May 30, 2005. Achieved a number one debut in the UK and its best chart performance in the US since *Be Here Now*. Spawned a series of singles, two of which reached number one in the UK, and was supported by a very successful tour and numerous awards.
**Dig Out Your Soul (2008)** : Released on October 6, 2008. Reached number one in the UK and number five in the US. An 18-month tour was planned to support the album but was interrupted by Noel Gallagher's injury.

## songs
**Stop Crying Your Heart Out (2002)** : Featured on the album *Heathen Chemistry*.

## curiosities
**Barcelona 2000 Incident** : On May 20, 2000, Liam Gallagher made a derogatory comment about Noel Gallagher's then-wife, Meg Matthews, questioning the paternity of their daughter Anase. Noel responded by leaping on Liam, punching him in the face, splitting his lip, and giving him a serious headbutt. Noel then stated he quit the band.
**Noel's Temporary Departure (2000)** : Following the Barcelona incident, Noel Gallagher left the band for a period. Oasis played 18 shows without him, with Matt Dytin serving as a replacement guitarist.
**Noel's Return and Modified Touring (2000)** : Noel returned to Oasis on July 15, 2000, for large outdoor festivals across the UK. A press release stated he would no longer tour with Oasis overseas.
**Noel's Unforgiveness (2005)** : Noel Gallagher broke a five-year silence on the Barcelona incident in 2005, stating, "I've never forgiven him because he's never apologized. He's my brother, but he's at arm's length until he apologizes for what he's done."
**Liam's Vocal Issues and Mid-Set Departures (2002)** : In August 2002, Liam Gallagher walked off stage four songs into a set in Fort Lauderdale, Florida, citing a "bad voice" due to the flu, which had also caused a show cancellation in Spain ten days earlier. By the fall, he had bailed on three shows mid-set.
**Six Weeks of Silence on Tour (2002)** : During a tour in 2002, Liam and Noel Gallagher did not speak to each other for a period of six weeks.
**Munich Bar Brawl (December 1, 2002)** : Liam Gallagher, Alan White, and two other band members got drunk at the Bayer Sherhoff hotel bar in Munich, Germany, while on tour supporting *Heathen Chemistry*. They threw peanuts at five Italians, jumped on stage for "unwanted karaoke" when an Oasis song was played, and caused a table to collapse. A fight ensued, moving from the bar to the hotel lobby, resulting in broken furniture and smashed windows.
**Munich Brawl Aftermath (December 1, 2002)** : Police responded with ten cars. Alan White sustained a bleeding head injury from an ash tray, and both security personnel were injured, with one knocked unconscious. Liam Gallagher lost several teeth, was bruised, and kicked a police officer in the ribs during his arrest. Toxicology results showed he was very drunk with a substantial quantity of cocaine in his system.
**Munich Brawl Financial and Legal Consequences (December 2002)** : Liam Gallagher required $30,000 worth of emergency dental surgery and spent 12 hours in jail with two other band members. The remainder of the European tour was cancelled, costing the band millions in revenue. Management had to pay a fine of over $300,000 on Liam's behalf for the band to leave Germany. Liam faced two years in jail. Noel Gallagher, who was in his room during the incident, was furious.
**Noel's Comments on Munich Brawl (2002)** : Noel Gallagher's only comment on the incident was, "all I'm bothered about is that he can still sing." The "Italian guys" were later identified as four real estate agents and a computer nurse, not "mafia dudes" as initially thought.
**Noel's Media Sniping at Liam (2004)** : Noel Gallagher made several public comments about Liam, including: "He's actually frightened to death of me. I can read him, and I can play him like a slightly disused arcade game. I can make him make decisions that he thinks are his, but really their mine." And "I don't know who the guy is who's in these interviews. He seems really cool because the guy I've been in a band with for the last 18 years is a knobhead." And "He's rude, arrogant, intimidating, and lazy. He's the angriest man you'll ever meet. He's like a man with a fork in a world of soup."
**Noel Gallagher Stage Attack (September 8, 2008)** : Noel Gallagher was attacked by a fan on stage at the Virgin Festival in Toronto, breaking three ribs and leading to the cancellation of several shows.
**New Three-Album Deal (2009)** : Oasis secured a new three-album record deal.
**Manchester Hometown Shows (June 4, 2009)** : The band played three massive hometown shows in Manchester, attracting over 70,000 people per night.
**V Festival Cancellation and Lawsuit (August 23, 2009)** : Liam Gallagher cancelled a scheduled performance at the V Festival due to "laryngitis." Two years later, Noel Gallagher claimed Liam was "massively hungover," leading Liam to sue his brother for slander, a suit that was later dropped.
**Paris Dressing Room Incident (August 28, 2009)** : Five days after the V Festival cancellation, backstage in Paris, Liam Gallagher, enraged by bad press he attributed to Noel, threw a plum at a wall. He then re-entered the dressing room wielding one of Noel's guitars "like an axe," nearly hitting Noel in the head before the guitar was trashed.
**Noel Quits Oasis (August 28, 2009)** : Following the Paris incident, Noel Gallagher left the dressing room and, five minutes before showtime, declared he couldn't continue. Two hours later, a statement appeared on the Oasis website: "it's with some sadness and great relief to tell you that I quit Oasis tonight. People will write and say what they like, but I simply could not go on working with Liam a day longer." This marked the end of Oasis after 18 years.
**Noel's Post-Breakup Press Conference (2011)** : At a press conference two years after the breakup, Noel Gallagher described Liam's violence in the Paris incident as "WWE wrestling," likening him to "the much-o-man Randy Savage." He recounted Liam throwing a plum and then wielding a guitar "like an axe," calling it an "unnecessary violent act" that nearly took off his face.
**Liam Sues Noel for Slander (c. 2011)** : Liam Gallagher sued Noel for slander following Noel's comments at the 2011 press conference, but the suit was later dropped.
**Super Sonic Documentary (October 2016)** : The Oasis documentary *Super Sonic* premiered in London. Liam Gallagher attended, but Noel Gallagher did not, with Liam quipping about Noel being in one of his "really, really, really, really, really, really, really, really, really big houses."
**Liam's Shifting Stance on Reunion (October 2016)** : While continuing to snipe at Noel (e.g., tweeting "Potato" with Noel's picture, "F Oasis," criticizing Noel for associating with Bono and Damon Albarn), Liam also expressed openness to an Oasis reunion around the time of the *Super Sonic* premiere.
**Predicted Reunion Tour** : The podcast states that Oasis is "back together in touring next summer," with shows planned in the UK, Ireland, Toronto (at Danforth Music Hall in August), several US cities, Mexico City, Asia, Australia, New Zealand, and South America.
