**Far Out (2003)** : This is a nine-note instrumental composition, loosely based on the Fibonacci sequence, that Blur composed as a call sign for the Martian probe Beagle II in 2003. It was intended to be broadcast back to Earth upon a safe landing. ## curiosities
**Space Exploration Call Sign** : In 2003, the band Blur composed a unique nine-note instrumental titled "Far Out," loosely based on the Fibonacci sequence, to serve as a call sign for the Martian probe Beagle II. The intention was for the probe to broadcast this instrumental back to Earth upon a successful landing to signal its operational status. Unfortunately, the probe was lost. However, it was later speculated that Beagle II did survive the landing and played the song, but due to antenna damage, the signal was not received on Earth.
