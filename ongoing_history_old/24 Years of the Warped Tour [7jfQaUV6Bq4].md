# artist - Green Day

## albums

**Dookie (1994)** : Album released before 2000, establishing the band but preceding a new generation of punk rock fans who were too young to have experienced it upon its initial release.
**Greatest Hits Album (Post-2000 Warped Tour)** : A greatest hits album was issued by their label following their re-energizing 2000 Warped Tour appearance, turning into a surprise success.
**American Idiot** : The band's re-energization from the 2000 Warped Tour and subsequent touring with Blink-182 put Billy Joe Armstrong on the road to running this album.

## curiosities

**Security Guard Fireworks Incident** : A Green Day security guard accidentally blew his fingers off with fireworks during the tour, leading to a subsequent ban on fireworks.
**Career Crisis (Late 1990s)** : By the end of the 1990s, the band was musically, physically, and creatively "out of gas," and close to breaking up.
**Warped Tour Invitation (2000)** : Organizer Kevin Lyman offered them a slot on the 2000 Warped Tour, encouraging them to play without pressure, hang with new bands, absorb energy, and embrace an "elder statesman" role.
**Initial Refusal to Play Warped Tour (Pre-2000)** : The band initially stated they would "never, ever play the Warped Tour," alongside never watching the movie Titanic.
**Warped Tour Performance (2000)** : They agreed to sign on for the 2000 Warped Tour, performing live on July 30, 2000.
**Re-energization and New Fanbase (2000)** : Playing the Warped Tour re-energized the band and introduced them to a new generation of punk rock fans who were too young for their 1994 album, Dookie.
**Tour with Blink-182 (Post-2000)** : After the Warped Tour, Green Day went on the road with Blink-182, who by then were selling tens of millions of records, confirming Green Day's "elder statesman" role.
**Catalyst for Second Act (2000)** : Their participation in the 2000 Warped Tour is seen as a very strong argument for why the band had a "second act" to their career.

# curiosity

**Ongoing History of New Music Podcast** : Hosted by Alan Cross, it explores the stories and characters that shaped modern music. Available early and ad-free on Amazon Music for Prime members.
**Early Rock Package Tours (1950s-Late 1960s)** : The most efficient and cost-effective way to tour acts, involving bundling artists together, often with a common backing band and on-site PA equipment, known as caravan tours.
**Alan Fried** : Pioneering disc jockey who took caravan tours on the road in the 1950s.
**Dick Clark** : Well-known figure who took caravan tours on the road in the 1950s.
**Lollapalooza (1991)** : A multi-act bill created by Perry Farrell of Jane's Addiction. The 1991 tour was successful enough to expand in 1992 across multiple stages and attractions.
**Lollapalooza as Alternative Festival (1990s)** : For a couple of years in the 1990s, it was the touring music festival for the alternative generation.
**Lollapalooza Modern Format** : Now a static festival held in Chicago every August.
**Lollapalooza Imitators (1990s)** : Spawned imitators such as Edgefest, Lilith Fair, Summersalt, and Another Roadside Attraction.
**Defunct Imitator Festivals** : Edgefest, Lilith Fair, Summersalt, and Another Roadside Attraction are all defunct.
**Vans Warped Tour (1995-2018)** : The longest-running touring music festival in history, founded by Kevin Lyman, running for 24 years from 1995 until 2018.
**Kevin Lyman's Background** : Born in 1961, American. Booked bands like Bad Religion and The Bangles in university (California State Polytechnic University in Pomona, majored in recreation administration). Worked at Goldenvoice as a production manager for groups like Metallica and Jane's Addiction.
**Warped Tour Naming Origin** : Kevin Lyman initially planned to call his tour "The Bomb" in 1995, but changed it to "Warped" (the name of a magazine he'd worked on) after the Oklahoma City bombing on April 19, 1995.
**Oklahoma City Bombing (April 19, 1995)** : Worst terrorist attack on American soil until September 11, 2001.
**First Warped Tour (1995)** : Began August 4 in Salt Lake City, with 25 dates including Toronto and Vancouver. Featured two stages for up-and-coming alternative bands.
**Financial Struggles of First Warped Tour (1995)** : Kevin Lyman lost money and couldn't pay everyone, indicating a need for sponsorship.
**Calvin Klein Sponsorship Attempt (Pre-1996)** : A meeting with Calvin Klein for title sponsorship was canceled due to a blizzard, which trapped their representative for 24 hours.
**Vans Sponsorship Acquisition (Pre-1996)** : During the 24-hour delay of the Calvin Klein meeting, Vans, the skateboard and shoe company, stepped in and signed a $300,000 deal, making it officially the "Vans Warped Tour" from 1996 onward.
**Airwalks Sponsorship Attempt (1997)** : Another shoe company, Airwalks, attempted to scoop the sponsorship for 1997, but Kevin Lyman declined due to his preference for the Vans team.
**Warped Tour Ethos - "Punk Rock Summer Boot Camp"** : Kevin Lyman often referred to the Warped Tour this way, emphasizing the hard work, low pay, grueling pace, tough crowds, and lack of luxuries.
**Warped Tour Stages Expansion (1996)** : Expanded to include a main stage, a side stage, and a "locals only" stage.
**Warped Tour Flexible Lineup (1996 onwards)** : Bands were allowed to participate for only a couple of shows before dropping off the tour.
**Warped Tour Performer Count (1997)** : A total of 97 different acts participated in the 1997 tour.
**Warped Tour Performer Count (General)** : No one knows exactly how many bands have performed since 1995. In any given year, more than 250 names appeared on the bill. Wikipedia attempts to track them but struggles.
**Warped Tour Stages (Later Years)** : The festival sometimes had as many as nine stages, plus an acoustic tent and a comedy tent.
**Average Daily Bands on Warped Tour** : On average, there were at least 90 bands playing per day at each stop.
**Warped Tour Parent Entry Policy (Later Years)** : Allowed parents of young children to enter the show for free, averaging 800 parents per show.
**Warped Tour Parent Policy Financial Impact** : This policy resulted in an estimated $1.4 million in lost ticket revenue (at $40 a ticket) but was more than offset by the increase in children attending due to free parent entry.
**Warped Tour Ticket Sales Vulnerability** : The financial breakpoint for the tour often came down to the last 10 days, making it vulnerable to bad weather (rain, heat).
**Tadah Catering** : A company owned by Shelley Lynn, which was with the Warped Tour from the very beginning.
**Warped Tour Catering Scale (Typical Year)** : Tadah Catering served over 1,300 meals a day, totaling more than 82,000 in a typical year, including 6,000 pounds of potatoes, 18,000 cups of coffee, and 3,500 cases of beer.
**Warped Barbecue Offerings (Typical Night)** : Served 300 hot dogs, 300 hamburgers, and 100 veggie burgers.
**Artist/Crew Meal Quality (Warped Tour)** : 90% of artists and their crews ate better on the Warped Tour than they did at home.
**Warped Tour Lineup Changes** : The lineup changes from city to city, preventing fans from planning their day around a fixed performance schedule.
**Attendee Arrival Time (Warped Tour)** : On average, 90% of attendees showed up as soon as the gates opened.
**Warped Stage Set Simplicity** : Stage sets tended to be very bare bones, with Avenged Sevenfold being noted as the first band to use a prop (a smoke machine).
**Skatin' for Satan (1998)** : The Warped Tour merged with Ozzfest in 1998 for an event called "Skatin' for Satan."
**Barbecue Band Program (Warped Tour)** : A program where smaller bands prepare the post-show dinner for other performers and crew in exchange for being able to play on the tour. This program was instrumental for bands like Blink-182, Katy Perry, and My Chemical Romance.
**End of Vans Warped Tour (2018)** : After 24 years, founder Kevin Lyman decided to end the tour due to the changes in the economics of the music business.
**Music Industry Context at Warped Tour's Start (1995)** : In 1995, hardly anyone used the internet, MP3s were unknown, streaming was years away, and compact discs ruled the market.
**Philanthropic Efforts (Warped Tour)** : The tour consistently supported numerous charities, including Feed Our Children Now, Music Cares, Hope for the Day, You Hear Me, Headcount, FEND, A Voice for the Innocent, breast cancer awareness groups, and environmental groups.
**Food Collection (Warped Tour)** : It was not unusual for over 300,000 pounds of food to be collected during a single tour.
**Music Cares Funding (Warped Tour)** : The tour raised hundreds of thousands of dollars for Music Cares, an organization that helps musicians and roadies with health insurance.
**Hope for the Day (Warped Tour)** : A suicide prevention outreach program supported by the tour.
**You Hear Me (Warped Tour)** : A mental health organization that also addresses bullying and peer pressure, supported by the tour.
**Headcount (Warped Tour)** : An organization focused on voter registration, supported by the tour.
**FEND (Warped Tour)** : An anti-opioid abuse group, supported by the tour.
**A Voice for the Innocent (Warped Tour)** : An organization that supports victims of rape and sexual abuse, supported by the tour.
**Warped Tour Blood Drive** : Was named "Independent Blood Drive of the Year" for six consecutive years.
**Volunteer Participation (Warped Tour)** : Thousands of volunteers and interns helped with the tour; in 2012, 16,400 volunteers assisted.
