## albums

**21 Pilots (2009) – Self-Titled Debut Album** : A self-produced indie record released on December 29, 2009, featuring the original lineup of Tyler Joseph, Nick Thomas, and Chris Salih. Despite being recorded at home with low-key promotion, it managed to sell approximately 115,000 copies.

**Regional at Best (2011) – Second Indie Album** : Released on July 8, 2011, this project was primarily a solo effort by Tyler Joseph, who described it as a "glorified mixtape". As a condition of their later record deal, the album was "completely discontinued" and wiped from digital platforms. Today, physical copies are highly valuable, selling for between $35 and $900.

**Vessel (2013) – Third Studio Album** : Released on January 8, 2013, under Fueled by Ramen, this album expanded the band's fanbase significantly and led to major festival slots at Lollapalooza and Bonnaroo. It received moderate radio play, which helped transition the band into the mainstream.

**Blurryface (2015) – Fourth Album & Breakthrough** : This 2015 release served as the band's major commercial breakthrough, selling nearly 2 million copies—a significant feat in the streaming era. The album introduced a complex narrative centered on a character named "Blurryface," who personified the frontman's insecurities.

**Trench (2018) – Fifth Studio Album** : Released in 2018, this album expanded the band's lore into a fictional world involving a city called "Dema" and a resistance group known as the "Banditos". Its themes delve into doubt, anxiety, faith, and suicide.

## songs

**Blasphemy (2008)** : A track from Tyler Joseph's high school solo album, _No Fun Intended_.
**Oh Ms Believer (2009)** : A song featured on the band's self-titled indie debut.
**Forest (2011)** : A track from the discontinued _Regional at Best_, recorded while Tyler was the band's sole member.
**Guns for Hands (2012)** : Originally on _Regional at Best_, it was one of five tracks re-recorded for the _Vessel_ album.
**Car Radio (2013)** : A successful single inspired by a real-life incident where Tyler’s car was broken into; the theft of his radio forced him to drive in silence and reflect.
**Stressed Out (2015)** : A prominent lead single that contributed to the massive success of the _Blurryface_ era.
**Chlorine (2018)** : A single from _Trench_ featuring "Ned," a "weird little alien animal". The release was teased with chemical codes (atomic number 17 and weight 35.427) corresponding to the element chlorine.
**Jumpsuit (2018)** : The lead single from _Trench_, announced via a cryptic email featuring a closing eye image.

## curiosities

**Origin of Band Name** : The name is derived from Arthur Miller’s 1947 play _All My Sons_, which details a true story of 21 pilots who died due to faulty aircraft engines approved by a corrupt businessman.
**Stylized Branding** : The omission of a hyphen in "21 pilots" was a specific design choice by Tyler Joseph for visual adaptability in logos.
**The Skeleton Clique** : The band's dedicated fanbase is known as the "Skeleton Clique". Members often refer to each other as "frens" and use the phrase "Stay Street".
**dmaorg.info Mystery** : In 2018, fans discovered a hidden website hidden in a digital image on the band's store. The site contained cryptic "404 error" warnings and journal entries from a character named Clancy, leading to the _Trench_ album reveal.
**Blurryface Identity** : In the _Trench_ lore, the character Blurryface is revealed to be "Niko" (Nicolas Bourbaki), the chief bishop of the fictional city Dema.
**The Banditos' Color** : The resistance group in the _Trench_ narrative uses a specific shade of yellow because the oppressive "bishops" are unable to perceive that color.

# curiosity

**The "Fourth Album" Breakthrough** : Several major rock acts followed a trajectory where mainstream success arrived only after multiple releases:

- **Red Hot Chili Peppers**: Career began to blossom with their fourth album.
- **Muse**: Were playing clubs in North America while already playing arenas in Europe by their fourth album.
- **R.E.M.**: Achieved mainstream consciousness with their fifth album after maintaining a cult following for four records.
- **The Black Keys**: Experienced their major breakthrough on their sixth album.

**Tower of Silence** : In Zoroastrianism, this is a structure where the dead are placed to be consumed by vultures; the band adapted this concept for the city of "Dema" in their fictional mythology.
