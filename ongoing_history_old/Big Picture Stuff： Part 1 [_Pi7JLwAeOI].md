# artist - Enheduanna

## curiosities

**First Named Composer in History (Sumer, c. 2300 BC)** : A royal Sumerian high priestess in the city-state of Ur who is identified as the earliest known composer by name. She was a poet and singer-songwriter whose hymns to the gods were set to music; while her work was religious, some compositions included "dirty and sexy" themes.

# artist - Pythagoras

## curiosities

**Architect of Mathematical Music Theory (c. 570 BC)** : A Greek mathematician who used math and science to organize and define musical sounds, effectively distinguishing melody from noise. He is considered foundational to music history for creating a system to define how notes relate to one another.
**Pythagorean Tuning and Ratios** : Pythagoras developed a tuning system based on mathematical ratios, such as 3:2, derived from experiments with strings. He discovered that a string half the length of another produces a pitch exactly double (an octave), a discovery that dictated Western scales, instrument tuning, and composition until the early 1500s.

# artist - Sappho

## curiosities

**Lyric Poet and LGBTQ+ Symbol** : An early lyric poet from the Greek island of Lesbos who composed poetry specifically intended to be sung accompanied by a lyre. She is recognized as a symbol of love and desire between women, and her legacy is the origin of the words "sapphic" and "lesbian."
**Expanding Musical Subject Matter** : During a time when music was strictly restricted to religious rituals, storytelling, or praising great men, Sappho broke convention by writing love songs, which were previously considered "unwise."

# artist - Dr. Peter Goldmark

## curiosities

**Inventor of Polyvinyl Chloride (Vinyl) Records** : The chief engineer at Columbia Records who was tasked with finding a substitute for shellac during the 1940s supply crisis. His team successfully adapted polyvinyl chloride—previously used for sewer pipes—to create more durable records that could hold five times more music than 78 RPM discs.

# genre - Blues

## curiosities

**Origins and "Bent Notes" (c. 1870s)** : Emerging from the US South, the blues introduced "blue notes" that existed between the accepted tones of the Pythagorean scale. These "natural imperfections" were intentionally excluded by traditional Western tuning but became the soul of the genre.
**Juke Joints and the Chitlin' Circuit** : Early blues was performed in informal Southern venues called Juke Joints. These were connected by the "Chitlin' Circuit," a touring network that eventually linked rural Florida to urban hubs like the Apollo Theater and Cotton Club in New York.
**Cultural Fusion** : The blues mixed with country, Western, and hillbilly music in the South, creating the specific musical DNA that would eventually evolve into rock and roll.

---

# genre - Jazz

## curiosities

**Birth and Dominance (1910-1950s)** : Jazz emerged as an offshoot of Ragtime in New Orleans and spread North during the Great Migration. It reigned as the dominant form of American popular culture from the early 1900s until the mid-1950s.
**Evolution of Time Signatures** : Jazz played a central role in the "Great Metric Shift," moving from the 2/4 time of Ragtime into the 4/4 time that now dominates 90% of all popular music.

---

# genre - Ragtime

## curiosities

**The "Malarial" Scandal of 1900** : In 1900, a music magazine condemned Ragtime as "virulent poison" and a "malarial epidemic" that threatened the sanity of the youth. This reflected the intense cultural and racist anxieties triggered by its "ragged" or syncopated rhythms.

---

# genre - Rhythm and Blues (R&B)

## curiosities

**Origins from Big Band Downsizing** : R&B emerged in the late 1940s when big bands were forced to downsize due to post-war economic pressures and the 1942-1944 recording strike. These smaller groups emphasized a heavier rhythm and beat, leading directly to the birth of rock and roll.

---

# instrument - Polyvinyl Chloride (Vinyl)

## curiosities

**From Sewer Pipes to Superstars** : Originally used for industrial sewer pipes in the 1920s, vinyl became the savior of the music industry during the Shellac Crisis. It allowed for "microgrooves," which meant records could be smaller, more durable, and hold significantly more audio than previous formats.

# instrument - Shellac

## curiosities

**The Lac Bug Secretion** : Prior to the 1940s, 78 RPM records were made of shellac, a waxy resin secreted by the female lac bug. The material was mixed with minerals, cotton fibers, and carbon to create brittle phonograph discs.

# instrument - Drums

## curiosities

**Ancient Women Drummers** : Historical evidence from 3,000 years ago in Egypt, Mesopotamia, and Ancient Greece shows that women were humanity's first formalized drummers. They used drums to induce trance states during rituals.
**Masculinization for Warfare** : Over centuries, the role of drumming shifted from women to men as the instrument was adopted for military purposes to lead armies into battle.

# curiosity

**The Great Migration (1910-1940) and Rock's Foundation** : Following the 13th Amendment (1865), approximately 2 million African Americans fled the Jim Crow laws and poverty of the South for Northern cities like Chicago and Detroit. This movement facilitated the blending of work songs, spirituals, and gospel with other genres, serving as the foundational event for the birth of rock and roll.
**James Burke's Connections Model** : The study of music history can be viewed through the lens of science historian James Burke, who illustrated how unexpected interconnections between technology, science, and historical events (like the Shellac Crisis or the Great Migration) trigger musical revolutions.
**The Biological Basis of Music** : The human brain has a specialized center for music that stores musical memories distinctly from other data. This center triggers "feel-good hormones" similar to an orgasm, a biological phenomenon that even confused Charles Darwin.
**Lullabies as a Survival Mechanism** : Anthropologists theorize that lullabies are a universal survival mechanism used by all known societies to quiet infants and prevent their cries from alerting predators to the tribe's location.
**Music as Ancient Story Preservation** : Before written language, early humans used music to preserve and transmit cultural myths and folk tales across thousands of generations, beginning with the first migrations out of Africa.
**The Great Metric Shift (Post-1910)** : Before 1900, Western music was dominated by 3/4 (waltz) time, found in national anthems and traditional songs. After 1910, influence from Ragtime and Jazz shifted the global preference to 4/4 time, which aligns with the human heart's preference for a 120 BPM tempo.
**The Shellac Crisis of 1941** : On December 8, 1941, Japan invaded the Mele Peninsula, cutting off the world's supply of shellac. This forced the recording industry to stop production; for a time, consumers could only buy a new record if they traded in an old one to be melted down.
**US Musician Strike (1942-1944)** : A massive strike that brought the recording industry to a standstill, effectively killing off expensive "Big Bands" (which could have 20+ members) and forcing the industry to move toward smaller, more economical ensembles like those found in early R&B and Bebop.
