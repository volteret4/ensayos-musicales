## albums

## songs
**Boredom (1977)** : A track from their landmark self-released EP "Spiral Scratch".

## curiosities
**Formation and Early Influences (1976)** : Formed in Manchester in 1976 by Pete Shelley and Howard Devoto, who were significantly influenced by the Velvet Underground and the burgeoning punk scene.
**Sex Pistols Concert Promotion (June 4, 1976)** : Arranged for the then-unknown band The Sex Pistols to perform at the Lesser Free Trade Hall in Manchester on June 4, 1976. This gig became a mythical event in rock history.
**Self-Release Revelation (1977)** : Energized by the nascent punk scene, and with no labels interested in their music, they established their own label, New Hormones. Their release of "Spiral Scratch" on January 29, 1977, served as the first self-released punk record, proving that anyone, even inexperienced individuals from a "musical backwater" like Manchester, could independently release a record.
**Spiral Scratch Recording Cost (1977)** : The entire recording and release of the "Spiral Scratch" EP cost 500 pounds.

**Major Labels Dominance** : The music industry is largely controlled by three major record labels—Universal, Sony, and Warner—which are multinational entities that command the majority of global music trade and its associated revenue.
**Independent Labels as Outliers** : Beyond the major players, hundreds to thousands of independent labels operate, considered "indie" due to their lack of affiliation with the "big three." These labels are characterized as representing the underground, non-mainstream, experimental, daring, outlier, unusual, and non-conformist segments of the music world.
**Indie Market Share Growth (Early/Mid-80s)** : In the early and mid-1980s, despite the continued control of the music market by major labels, the market share of indie music began a steady annual increase, building significant momentum that originated during the punk years.
**900+ UK Indie Records (1977-1981)** : In the four years following the Buzzcocks' self-release of "Spiral Scratch" in 1977, more than 900 independent records were released in Britain through similar DIY methods.
**Ronald Reagan's Influence on Hardcore Punk** : President Ronald Reagan's deeply conservative and hawkish political policies were met with strong disapproval by many young people, leading to the emergence of hardcore punk as a form of rebellion. This political climate fostered the need, desire, and demand for a new generation of independent record labels in the early 1980s, which quickly expanded beyond the hardcore genre itself.
**NME C86 Cassette Collection (1986)** : Released by the British music paper NME in 1986 (as a circulation booster), this cassette featured 22 power pop and jangly guitar-based songs from various indie labels across the UK. It was representative of the current state of new music in Britain, becoming a shorthand term for a particular sound and attitude. The collection is regarded by some as the beginning of the modern era of indie music in the UK, fostering a sense of community and validating the efforts of independent musicians.
