# genre - Punk Rock

## curiosities
**Origin and Mission (1970s)** : Born in the 1970s, it was designed to be the "enemy, the destroyer of pop music," aiming to obliterate the "schmaltzy, wimpy, bland metal crap" heard on AM Top 40 radio.
**Brewing Period (1974-1976)** : Began brewing between 1974 and 1976, partly as a response to the music prevalent on radio at the time.
**Core Characteristics** : Typically characterized by angry lyrics, two chords played very fast, loud performances, and often a political and socially aware stance, professing to hate much of the previous generation's music.
**Subgenres and Varieties** : Encompasses a wide range of varieties including hardcore punk, sky punk, cyber punk, synth punk, anarcho punk, cow punk, gypsy punk, Christian punk, Celtic punk, art punk, garage punk, glam punk, crust punk, horror punk, street punk, melodic punk, afro punk, skate punk, Chicano punk, folk punk, troll punk, punk blues, punk pathetic, punk metal, riot grrrl, queer core, rap core, straight edge, emo, and oi.

# genre - Pop Punk

## curiosities
**Paradoxical Juxtaposition** : Described as a paradoxical juxtaposition or oxymoron, similar to "jumbo shrimp," given punk's original anti-pop ethos.
**Evolution from 1970s Punk** : Represents a mutation from the original 1970s punk rock, integrating pop elements into the hard, fast, and loud sound.
**Worldwide Phenomenon** : Became one of the most popular forms of punk rock of all time, evolving into a worldwide phenomenon, particularly from the 1990s onward.
**Defining Pop Elements** : Characterized by pop sensibilities like melody, clear song structure, sing-along choruses, short and punchy songs (typically less than four, often less than three minutes), and a verse-chorus-verse 32-bar structure, while maintaining a subculture image and appeal.
**Term Coining** : The term "pop punk" began to be circulated by the San Francisco-based publication Maximum Rock and Roll when discussing melodic punk bands such as Social Distortion and The Vandals.
**1992 Turning Point** : The year 1992 is noted as a turning point when this emerging genre started to gain wider attention, notably through a Spin Magazine article titled "California Dreaming."

# genre - Hardcore Punk

## curiosities
**American Invention** : Described as an "American invention" that directly descended from the hard, fast, and loud punk of the 1970s.
**Intensified Punk** : Aimed to amplify the existing punk sound, making the music even harder, faster, and louder.
**Subgenres** : Includes bandage, death core, pornogrind, screamo, power violence, positive hardcore, nard core, and Nintendo core.
**Incorporation of Pop Sensibilities** : Despite its intensity, bands like Black Flag, Bad Religion, and The Descendents began to incorporate melodic elements and "pop sensibilities."
**Rigid Dogma Challenge** : Had a "rigid, immovable hardcore dogma" that bands like Hüsker Dü broke by introducing nuanced concept albums and strong melodic elements.

# curiosity

**AM Top 40 Radio Dominance (1974-1976)** : During the mid-1970s, the vast majority of people obtained almost all their music from AM Top 40 radio, playing music that punk rock actively opposed.
**FM Rock Radio (1970s)** : While FM stations played rock and album cuts in the 1970s, they were "nowhere near as popular" as AM stations for general music consumption.
**Definition of a Pop Song** : A song with pop elements is characterized by melody, strong song structure, sing-along choruses, a short and punchy duration (often less than four, sometimes less than three minutes), and a typical verse-chorus-verse 32-bar structure, though its specific musical arrangements, lyrics, and image may be "left of center" for mainstream audiences.
**"Pop Sensibilities" as Description** : Describing an artist as using "pop sensibilities" is not an insult; it is a way to describe their song's sound and construction, not an implication of commercial motivation or desire for popularity.
**Concept Albums in Punk/Hardcore (1980s)** : Concept albums were generally considered "extremely uncool" in the early 1980s punk and hardcore scene, viewed as something best left to bands like Pink Floyd, a dogma Hüsker Dü notably defied with Zen Arcade.
**Maximum Rock and Roll Magazine's Influence** : This San Francisco-based publication started using the term "pop punk" to describe melodic punk bands like Social Distortion and The Vandals, helping to define the emerging genre.
**Spin Magazine "California Dreaming" (1992)** : An article published in the summer of 1992 that highlighted various melodic punk bands, including those on Lookout Records like Green Day, signaling a turning point for pop punk's wider recognition.
**Pop Punk Popularity Explosion (1990s and Beyond)** : The genre experienced a significant explosion in popularity throughout the 1990s and has remained one of the most successful and popular forms of music in the world of rock today.
