# artist - George W. Johnson

## venues

Vaudeville

## instruments

Thomas Edison's wax cylinders

## songs

**The Laughing Song (1890)** : Originally recorded in 1890, this became Johnson's most popular track and was the best-selling song ever by 1894. It reached number one on the music charts of the day and sold approximately 50,000 copies, a feat that required Johnson to perform the song between 12,000 and 15,000 times because every cylinder was a master recording.

## curiosities

**First Recording Star** : Recognized as the world's first and biggest pop star in the late 1890s, Johnson was a former slave who was freed around 1853 and began singing on the streets of New York in the 1870s. He became the first person to have a hit record and the first Black performer to appear on music charts.
**Mastering the Wax Cylinder** : Because early Thomas Edison phonographs required individual master recordings for each unit sold, Johnson often repeated his songs up to 50 times a day. His powerful voice allowed up to five recorders to capture his performance simultaneously.
**Marketing and Legacy** : His label marketed him specifically as a Black man during a period of intense segregation and active memory of slavery, which was highly unusual for the time. Despite his early stardom, his popularity eventually faded; he died in 1914 while working as an office doorman and was buried in an unmarked grave in Queens.

# artist - Charlie Christian

## curiosities

**The First Guitar Hero** : A Black musician from Oklahoma, Christian is recognized as the world's first guitar hero for transforming the amplified guitar from a rhythm instrument into a lead instrument. He began innovating with solos during jam sessions as early as 1931, eventually acquiring a new electric guitar in 1936.
**Joining Benny Goodman (1939)** : Upon joining Benny Goodman's Big Band in 1939, Christian was given the platform to improvise and perform solos, creating the foundations for the modern role of the lead guitarist. His career was tragically cut short when he died of tuberculosis on March 3, 1941, at the age of 25.

# artist - Jack L. Cooper

## venues

WBSC Chicago

## curiosities

**The First Professional Radio DJ** : A former boxer and semi-pro baseball player, Cooper became the host of _The All-Negro Hour_ on November 3, 1929. He is considered the first professional radio DJ because he brought his own phonograph and collection of jazz and gospel records to play on air during his variety show.

## venues

Riverside Hotel

## songs

**Rocket 88 (1951)** : Released in late March 1951, this song is frequently nominated as the first true rock and roll record. It featured upfront vocals, heavy piano and saxophone arrangements, and a distinctive "good time" backbeat.

## curiosities

**The Busted Bass Amp Innovation** : During a drive from Clarkstale, Mississippi, to Memphis, bass player Willie Kizart's amp was dropped, causing the speaker cone to loosen. Producer Sam Phillips liked the resulting distorted, vibrating sound and decided to record it as is, inadvertently creating a foundational rock sound.

# artist - Sister Rosetta Tharpe

## genres

Gospel blues
Swing

## curiosities

**The Godmother of Rock and Roll** : A queer Black woman from Arkansas, Tharpe was a pioneering electric guitarist who bridged the gap between gospel and swing. She is widely considered the first female guitar hero and was revered by legends like Elvis Presley, Little Richard, and Chuck Berry.
**1964 European Tour Impact** : During a 1964 tour that included a stop in Manchester, her performance style and technique directly inspired a generation of British rockers. Musicians such as Eric Clapton, Jeff Beck, Keith Richards, and Brian Jones became her "disciples" following these shows.

# artist - Little Richard

## songs

**Tutti Frutti (1955)** : This is one of his most famous songs, though its original, uncensored lyrics were much more explicit and hinted at his sexuality.

## curiosities

**Rock's First Openly Gay Star** : Little Richard identified as gay or bisexual and used flamboyant, effeminate mannerisms and makeup to define his stage persona. He famously challenged Elvis Presley's title, stating that while Elvis was the king, he was "the queen".
**Breaking the Color Barrier** : He was the first Black artist to truly cross over to white audiences, attracting integrated crowds to his high-energy shows and helping to break down racial barriers in culture. His style had a massive influence on diverse artists ranging from The Beatles to Lemmy of Motorhead.

# artist - Chuck Berry

## curiosities

**The Father of Rock and Roll** : Berry is credited more than anyone else with taking R&B and "rocking it up" to create rock as a distinct genre. He established the foundation of the genre through his riff-heavy electric guitar style and lyrics that focused on the concerns and troubles of young people.
**The Voyager Golden Disk (1977)** : Berry's work was chosen as the only rock and roll song included on the Voyager Golden Disk launched into space. This ensures that if an alien civilization ever finds the record, their first exposure to the genre will be through his music.

# artist - Peggy Jones

## curiosities

**The Mother of Rock and Roll** : Jones began working as a guitarist in Bo Diddley's band in 1957, making her one of the first female rock guitarists in a famous band. She was an early experimenter with guitar synthesizers, a technology that was virtually unused in R&B at that time.

# artist - Death

## members

David Hackney
Bobby Hackney
Dennis Hackney

## curiosities

**Punk Pioneers** : Composed of three brothers from Detroit, this band is argued by some to be the first punk band, potentially making punk music before The Ramones' 1974 debut. They were originally called Rockfire Funk Express but changed their name to Death following the loss of their father in a car accident.
**Rediscovery (2009)** : After breaking up in 1977, their 1970s recordings—which had originally been released in very small runs of 500 copies—were rediscovered in 2009 and gained widespread recognition for their "punky" sound.

# artist - Tina Bell / Bam Bam

## members

Tina Bell
Tommy Martin
Matt Cameron

## venues

Reciprocal Recording

## albums

**Villains (1984) – EP** : This EP was released at least a year before other prominent early Grunge groups began releasing records.

## curiosities

**Godmother of Grunge** : Tina Bell helped invent the Grunge sound that defined 1990s rock and was the genre's first female front person. Her band, Bam Bam, was the first Grunge group to record at Reciprocal Recording, a studio later used by Nirvana and Soundgarden.
**Challenges and Legacy** : Bell faced significant racial abuse as a Black female singer in the punk scene, and despite opening for future stars like Soundgarden, her band struggled to gain traction. After quitting music and moving to Europe, she returned to the U.S. and eventually died in Las Vegas in 2012; her body was undiscovered for weeks, and her creative possessions were subsequently thrown out.

# genre - Rock and Roll

## curiosities

**Origins and Ingredients** : Rock and roll formed in the early 1950s as a "stew" of R&B, Blues, Country Western, Hillbilly, and traditional Black musical traditions. Its birth was a gradual process rather than the result of a single song, aided by local radio stations playing a hodgepodge of different materials.
**The Term "Rock and Roll"** : The phrase originally described the movement of ships on rough seas and was also used to describe the animated singing in Black churches. It later became a euphemism for sex before being popularized as a musical term by DJ Alan Fried in 1951.

---

# genre - Blues

## curiosities

**Structural Foundation** : The Blues originated in the South as an African-American invention characterized by songs about hard lives and difficult relationships. Its three-line stanza structure traces back to Mississippi Delta field songs and forms the structural foundation for rock, R&B, funk, and hip-hop.

---

# instrument - Wah-Wah Pedal

## curiosities

**Origins and Popularization** : While the wah-wah effect was mechanically reproduced by brass players as far back as the 1920s, it was incorporated into rock sound in 1967. Jimi Hendrix and Eric Clapton both released tracks featuring the pedal within days of each other in the spring of 1967.

# curiosity

**The 1619 Arrival** : The roots of rock music trace back to 1619, when approximately 20 African captives arrived in Virginia, marking the beginning of the forced migration that brought African rhythms and song traditions to North America.
**The Transistor Radio Revolution** : The rise of transistor radio technology allowed young people to take their music away from their parents, facilitating the spread of the "forbidden fruit" of "race records" and early rock and roll.
**The Bo Diddley Beat** : Although Bo Diddley did not invent this specific rhythm (which has a 50-year history), he is credited with introducing it to rock and roll, where it has since been used by artists ranging from Iggy Pop to Jett.
