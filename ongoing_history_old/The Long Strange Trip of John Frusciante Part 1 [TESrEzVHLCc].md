## albums
**The Uplift Mofo Party Plan (1987) – Hillel Slovak's Last Full Album** : This was the last full album to feature Hillel Slovak on guitar before his death.
**Mother's Milk (1989) – Debut of Classic Lineup** : This album featured John Frusciante on guitar for 9 songs and Chad Smith on drums, marking the beginning of what would become the classic Red Hot Chili Peppers lineup. Produced by Michael Beinhorn, who convinced Frusciante to play in a heavier style.
**Blood Sugar Sex Magic (1991) – Breakthrough Album** : Released on September 24, 1991, the same day as Nirvana's *Nevermind*. This was the first album to feature John Frusciante as guitarist and led the band from a mid-level group to one of the biggest acts of the alternative era, selling over 15 million copies. The album was produced by Rick Rubin and recorded in a historic mansion at 351 Laurel Canyon Boulevard in April 1991. Six singles were released from this record.

## songs
**Stone Cold Bush (1991)** : Performed on Saturday Night Live, Anthony Kiedis was visibly angry with John Frusciante during this performance, appearing to kick him.
**Under the Bridge (1991)** : Performed on Saturday Night Live, John Frusciante's guitar and vocals were described as "woozy and weird," "dissonant," and "out of tune," which Anthony Kiedis believed was due to Frusciante being under the influence of heroin. This performance was cited as "The Final Straw" before Frusciante's first departure.
**Californication (1998 Demo)** : An early demo of this song was recorded in September 1998 in Flea's garage, shortly after John Frusciante rejoined the band following six years of absence and drug addiction.

## curiosities
**John Frusciante's Early Audition (1988)** : John Frusciante auditioned for the band at age 17 after Hillel Slovak's death, connected by his frequent attendance at their shows and friendship with drummer D.H. Peligro. Despite his own admission of lacking "the funk," a jam session with Flea led to his recruitment in October 1988.
**Lineup Changes and "Complete Refurbishment" (1988-1989)** : Following Hillel Slovak's death on June 25, 1988, drummer Jack Irons quit. Dwayne "Blackbird" Knight (formerly of Funkadelic) was briefly considered as guitarist. D.H. Peligro (from Dead Kennedys) was briefly hired as drummer but didn't work out. John Frusciante joined in October 1988, and Chad Smith joined in December 1988, leading to a "complete refurbishment" of the band by early 1989.
**Producer Change (1991)** : For *Blood Sugar Sex Magic*, the band switched producers from Michael Beinhorn (who produced *Mother's Milk*) to Rick Rubin.
**Recording at a Haunted Mansion (1991)** : *Blood Sugar Sex Magic* was recorded in April 1991 at a historic mansion built in 1918 at 351 Laurel Canyon Boulevard, which had a reputation for being haunted. The mansion was isolated and lacked a working phone. A Canadian company converted the basement into a recording studio. Drummer Chad Smith was too freaked out by the vibes to stay there, while John Frusciante found it inspirational. Two psychics and a medium were called to check the place out.
**John Frusciante's Discomfort with Success (1991-1992)** : Following the immense success of *Blood Sugar Sex Magic*, John Frusciante became increasingly uncomfortable with the band's fame, preferring to be in a "cool underground band" and play small clubs rather than arenas.
**Tension and Alienation with Anthony Kiedis (1991-1992)** : Initially close, Anthony Kiedis had to distance himself from John Frusciante due to Frusciante's partying and Kiedis's struggles with sobriety. This led to tension and "weird grudges."
**Bonding with Kurt Cobain (1992)** : John Frusciante found a "soulmate" in Kurt Cobain when Nirvana opened shows for the Chili Peppers, as both shared a desire for their bands to remain "cool and underground," which further strained Frusciante's relationship with his bandmates.
**"Freaky Frusciante Stories" (1992)** : During this period, John Frusciante developed eccentric habits, such as bringing his guitar to meals, chain-smoking, and spontaneously shaving his head.
**Breaching Band Rules on Tour (1992)** : For the European leg of the *Blood Sugar Sex Magic* tour, John Frusciante broke a band rule by bringing his girlfriend, further angering Anthony Kiedis.
**Saturday Night Live Performance Incident (1992)** : During a performance on Saturday Night Live, Anthony Kiedis was "livid" and felt "sabotaged" by John Frusciante's "dissonant" and "out of tune" guitar and vocals, leading to a heated confrontation after the show. Kiedis felt "stabbed in the back" and "hung up to dry in front of all of America."
**John Frusciante's First Departure (May 7, 1992)** : John Frusciante refused to go on stage at Sonic City in Japan just minutes before a show, stating, "I want to go home right now...I quit...I can't give you what it takes to be in this band anymore." He claimed "voices inside his head" told him to quit. He was convinced to play one final, "bad" 15-song set, marking his last show with the band for six years. The band explained his departure as an inability to deal with mental stress.
**Tour Cancellations (1992)** : Following John Frusciante's departure, attempts to bring in guitarist Van Der Schloss were unsuccessful due to time constraints, leading to the cancellation of remaining tour dates, including major festival dates.
**Post-Frusciante Guitarists (1992-1997)** : After John Frusciante's initial departure, Eric Marshall briefly filled in, performing at Lollapalooza in 1992. Dave Navarro (from Jane's Addiction) was then brought in for one album but was fired in April 1997.
**Flea's Evolving Bass Style (Early 1990s)** : John Frusciante's influence led Flea to develop a more melodic bass playing style, moving away from his predominant finger picking, popping, and slapping techniques.
**Anthony Kiedis's Evolving Vocal Style (Early 1990s)** : Anthony Kiedis became more comfortable and confident with John Frusciante's melodic guitar style, which in turn made Kiedis's own approach to singing more melodic.
**Support Acts for Blood Sugar Sex Magic Tour** : The band was joined by two new bands, The Smashing Pumpkins and Pearl Jam, as openers during the tour supporting *Blood Sugar Sex Magic*.
**John Frusciante's Return to the Band (January 1998)** : Following his recovery from severe drug addiction, John Frusciante was encouraged by Flea to rejoin the band in January 1998. Anthony Kiedis bought him a 62 Stratocaster to replace his lost guitars, and they began jamming and writing in Flea's garage.

## albums
**Niandra LaDes and Usually Just a T-Shirt (1994) – First Solo Album** : This album was Frusciante's debut solo release, characterized by its low-fi, stream-of-consciousness style, recorded with basic gear. He stated that all songs were recorded in a single take. Frusciante admitted recording it while a heroin addict, and it is considered inseparable from his addiction during that period. It sold approximately 15,000 copies.
**Smile from the Streets You Hold (1997) – Drug Money Album** : Frusciante admitted this album was released solely to make money for drugs. During its recording, he claimed to be having "verbal communications with the spirits" and stated his greater concern for his "fame in their world" than in this one. The album features his late friend River Phoenix on the song "Higher Down" and includes a bong hit sound on "Four Air." The opening track, "Enter Uh Uh," starkly illustrates the depth of his addiction at the time.

## curiosities
**Early Musical Prodigy (Childhood)** : Born in Queens, New York, Frusciante grew up in a musical family. He moved to Tucson, then Florida, and eventually Santa Monica and Los Angeles with his mother after his parents' divorce. His stepfather encouraged his musical interests.
**Early Guitar Influences (By Age 11)** : By the age of 11, Frusciante was studying the styles of guitarists such as David Gilmour of Pink Floyd, Jimi Hendrix, Jimmy Page, Jeff Beck, and Frank Zappa, in addition to his early love for punk rock.
**Dropping Out of High School (Teenage Years)** : Frusciante convinced his parents to let him drop out of high school to study music full time.
**Admiration for Hillel Slovak (Mid-1980s)** : From age 15 (around 1984), Frusciante was a devoted fan of the Red Hot Chili Peppers, attending their shows and learning all their songs, hoping for a chance to speak with guitarist Hillel Slovak.
**Declining Frank Zappa's Offer (Age 17)** : At 17, Frusciante was called by Frank Zappa to join his band, but he declined because Zappa did not condone illegal drug use, which Frusciante felt he could not avoid.
**Brief Stint with Thelonius Monster (1988)** : Prior to joining the Red Hot Chili Peppers, Frusciante had accepted a gig with the L.A. punk band Thelonius Monster and was reportedly "three hours into his membership" when the Chili Peppers called with their offer.
**Struggles with Hillel Slovak's Legacy (Early Stint with RHCP)** : Frusciante felt immense pressure to step into the shoes of the deceased Hillel Slovak, which contributed to his later problems.
**Post-RHCP Audition for Meat Puppets (1992)** : After quitting the Red Hot Chili Peppers in 1992, Frusciante auditioned for the band Meat Puppets in Phoenix, but it did not work out.
**Deep Heroin Addiction and Depression (1992-1996)** : Following his first departure from the Chili Peppers, Frusciante fell into a deep depression, became a "full-fledged junkie," and barely left his Hollywood home for three years. He believed heroin was essential for creativity, stating, "I was very sad and I was always happy when I was on drugs. Therefore, I should be on drugs all the time." He remained a heroin addict almost constantly for five years until late 1996.
**Uncashed Royalty Checks (Mid-1990s)** : During his addiction, Frusciante neglected his finances, leading to uncashed royalty checks, including one for $600,000 found by Flea's daughter.
**Creative Pursuits During Addiction (Mid-1990s)** : While addicted, Frusciante focused on painting, attempting to write screenplays, and making demos with a four-track recorder.
**Cocaine Psychosis (Mid-1990s)** : Frusciante experienced what he later identified as cocaine psychosis, brought on by prolonged use of coke and crack. This involved paranoid delusions, such as covering his body with goggles and a ski mask to "wage war against the ghosts" on his roof, and calling Perry Farrell asking how to "get the snakes out of your eyes."
**House Fire (1996)** : His house burned down in 1996, starting in his painting room, likely due to the presence of paint cans and turpentine. He lost all his paintings, including two by poet William Burroughs. While his guitars initially survived, they were later stolen after he escaped to New York. The only musical items saved were his four-track demo tapes.
**Severe Physical Decline (1996-1998)** : By 1996, Frusciante weighed only 88 pounds and was described as "a skeleton covered in skin." His arms were severely scarred from self-harm and needle marks, he suffered from abscesses, his teeth had rotted out, and he contracted a near-fatal blood infection, even experiencing clinical death for a few minutes.
**Cold Turkey Quitting Heroin (Late 1996)** : After almost five years of constant heroin use, Frusciante quit cold turkey in late 1996, though he continued using crack for a couple more years.
**Entry into Rehab (January 1998)** : A lethal infection growing in his mouth (due to rotted teeth) convinced him to enter rehab in January 1998, as he faced sepsis and death without intervention. This led to a $75,000 procedure for tooth extraction and implants, as well as skin grafts for his abscesses.
**Painting Exhibition (1998)** : Following his rehab and recovery, Frusciante displayed some of his paintings at a gallery event, which is where Anthony Kiedis reconnected with him.
**Gifted Guitar (1998)** : Upon rejoining the Red Hot Chili Peppers, Frusciante did not own a guitar. Anthony Kiedis took him to a music shop and bought him a '62 Stratocaster.

# curiosity

**Homo Blatodia Classification for Musicians** : A proposed scientific classification for resilient musicians, comparing them to the nearly indestructible cockroach genus *Blatodia*. Two lineages are recognized: *periplaneta philogenosa* (the smokey brown cockroach) is used to classify Keith Richards, and *periplaneta americana* (the American cockroach) is used to classify John Frusciante, due to his survival despite a hard life including drugs, health problems, and dabbling in the occult.
**"Ongoing History of New Music" Podcast** : Hosted by Alun Croix, this podcast delves into various topics in music history, including "The Long Strange Trip of John Frusciante."
**Alun Croix's Website** : "ajournalofmusicalthings.com" serves as a source for daily music news and information, offering a free daily newsletter.
**Historical Mansion at 351 Laurel Canyon Boulevard** : Built in 1918, this mansion, used for recording *Blood Sugar Sex Magic*, was allegedly home to Harry Houdini (though his main estate was nearby at 2400 Laurel Canyon, with rumored connecting tunnels). It was also reputed to be haunted by the ghost of a furniture store owner's son who died after being pushed from a balcony, or gangsters shot during a Prohibition-era raid.
