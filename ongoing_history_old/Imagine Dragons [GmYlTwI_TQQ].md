# artist - Imagine Dragons

## members

Daniel Platzman (drummer) : joined as drummer after Andrew Tollman left, Berkeley School of Music graduate, award-winning musician, current member.

## albums
**Speak To Me (2008) – Debut EP** : self-released indie EP, featured the title track "Speak To Me," released shortly after the band's formation.
**Second EP (2009) – Five-Song Indie Release** : self-released indie EP, recorded at Battleborn Studios (owned by The Killers), released in early September 2009, featured the song "I Need A Moonlight."
**Third EP (2010) – Five-Track Indie Release** : self-released indie EP, recorded at Battleborn Studios (owned by The Killers), released on March 12, 2010, after tracks were posted on MySpace, featured the song "All Eyes."
**Fourth Indie EP (2011) – Eight-Song Release** : self-released indie EP, recorded in a studio inside the Palms Hotel, released almost one year to the day from the previous EP, included several videos, featured the local Las Vegas hit "It's Time."
**Continued Silence (2012) – Breakthrough Interscope EP** : first release under the Interscope deal in February 2012, six tracks, featured the major hit single "Radioactive."
**Night Visions (2012) – Debut Studio Album** : appeared eight months after the *Continued Silence* EP, debuted at number two on the American album chart, sold more copies in its first week than any other rock album since 2006, stayed in the top 10 through 2012, 2013, and 2014, went top five in Canada, UK, Ireland, Mexico, Australia, Norway, Portugal, the Netherlands, and New Zealand, sold seven million copies worldwide, was the most streamed song on Spotify in 2013.
**Smoke + Mirrors (2015) – Second Studio Album** : released in February 2015, debuted at number one in the US, Canada, and the UK, featured a massive promotional push including flying fans to tiny clubs, Hard Rock Cafe screen takeover, and an album artwork scavenger hunt.
**Evolve (2017) – Third Studio Album** : released in June 2017, explored a unique sound combining rock, pop, hip-hop, and electronic music.
**Origins (2018) – Fourth Studio Album** : released on November 9, 2018, featured "Natural" as its first single, the final album included in the majority catalog sale to Concord Music Publishing.
**Mercury Act One (2021) – Fifth Studio Album** : released in 2021, fell outside the catalog deal, featured "Wrecked" as one of its singles.

## songs
**Speak To Me (2008)** : title track of their debut self-released EP, represents the band's very early sound.
**I Need A Moonlight (2009)** : song from the band's second self-released EP, recorded at Battleborn Studios.
**All Eyes (2010)** : song from the band's third self-released EP, released after tracks were posted on MySpace, showcased the band's sound starting to come together.
**It's Time (2011)** : title track of the band's fourth self-released EP, written by Dan Reynolds while suffering from depression, became a local hit on Las Vegas radio.
**Radioactive (2012)** : first track from the *Continued Silence* EP, inspired by Dan Reynolds' loss of faith in Mormonism, a major hit that stayed on the singles charts for 87 weeks (a record at the time), sold 3 million copies making it one of the biggest selling rock songs in history according to SoundScan, won a Grammy for Best Rock Performance.
**Demons (2012)** : single from the *Night Visions* album, stayed on the Top 100 for 61 weeks, reportedly the eighth most downloaded song in rock history.
**I Bet My Life (2014)** : first single from the *Smoke + Mirrors* album.
**Believer (2017)** : single from the *Evolve* album, inspired by Dan Reynolds' experience with Ankylosing Spondylitis (a type of arthritis affecting the spine, hips, and shoulders), serving as a personal pep talk to push through pain.
**Natural (2018)** : first single from the *Origins* album.
**Wrecked (2021)** : single from the *Mercury Act One* album, inspired by the sudden death of Dan Reynolds' sister-in-law due to cancer.

## curiosities
**Formation in Provo, Utah (2008)** : Imagine Dragons was formed in Provo, Utah, in 2008 while singer Dan Reynolds was attending Brigham Young University.
**Relocation to Las Vegas** : after establishing a large local following in Provo, the band moved to Las Vegas to pursue broader opportunities, performing in lounges, bars, and clubs and often playing covers by The Cure and The Rolling Stones.
**Band Name Anagram Legend** : the name "Imagine Dragons" is, according to band legend, an anagram of a secret word or phrase that only the band knows and has vowed never to reveal, even to their families; online anagram generators have failed to produce a definitive 13-letter solution.
**CBGB "Gorman Dysers" Speculation** : a 12-letter anagram, "Gorman Dysing," was suggested as a possible solution to the band name, potentially linking to the full name of the punk bar CBGB ("Country, Bluegrass, Blues, and Other Music for Uplifting Gorman Dysers"), though the band has not confirmed this.
**The Killers' Battleborn Studios** : Imagine Dragons recorded their second and third self-released EPs at Battleborn Studios, which is owned by the Las Vegas-based band The Killers.
**Bite of Las Vegas Festival Breakthrough (October 17, 2009)** : the band received a pivotal opportunity when they were drafted at the last minute to fill a slot for Train (whose singer Pat Monahan fell ill) at the Bite of Las Vegas festival, performing for 26,000 people and impressing the audience.
**Dan Reynolds' Mormon Background** : born seventh of nine children in a Mormon family in Las Vegas, Dan Reynolds was an Eagle Scout, high school quarterback, and served as a full-time missionary for two years with highly restricted communication (two phone calls home per year).
**Dan Reynolds' Driving Musical Ambition** : despite being an excellent student at Brigham Young University with potential for a business career, Dan Reynolds realized his essential need to be a professional musician and committed all his discipline and focus to this path.
**Extensive Early Work Ethic** : prior to their major breakthrough, Imagine Dragons self-released four EPs, worked social media extensively, posted videos online, played numerous gigs, and networked with other bands.
**Significant Lineup Changes** : the band experienced several lineup changes in its early years, including original members Andrew Beck, Aurora Florence, and Dave Lemke leaving, and the Tolmans (Andrew and Brittany) departing to start a family, before stabilizing with the current core.
**Massive Streaming Success** : Imagine Dragons are streaming monsters with over 74 billion global career streams, four of their songs streamed more than a billion times each, and were the most streamed rock band on Spotify.
**Catalog Sale to Concord Music Publishing (August 2020)** : Imagine Dragons sold a majority stake in their music catalog (everything released up to *Origins* album) to Concord Music Publishing for an assumed sum north of $100 million US dollars, a rare move for a band at mid-career rather than in their twilight years.
**Activism for LGBTQ+ Rights** : Dan Reynolds has become a prominent activist for LGBTQ+ rights, heading the Love Loud foundation and music festival which has raised over a million dollars for LGBTQ+ organizations, and frequently brings a rainbow flag out at Imagine Dragons shows as a unifying moment.
**Confrontation with the Mormon Church** : Dan Reynolds' LGBTQ+ activism has often targeted the Mormon Church (The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints) due to its stance on gay people, compelling the Church to issue statements regarding his criticisms, making him arguably the most famous Mormon in music.
**Personal Inspiration for Songwriting** : Dan Reynolds has drawn heavily on personal experiences for song lyrics, including his depression for "It's Time," his struggle with Ankylosing Spondylitis for "Believer," and the death of his sister-in-law for "Wrecked."
**Philanthropic Engagements** : the band is deeply engaged in philanthropy, supporting cancer causes, anti-violence groups, Amnesty International, school music programs, refugee support through the United Nations, and scholarships.
**Financial Prudence of Catalog Sale** : taking money upfront from the catalog sale allowed the band to save a huge bundle on taxes and provided funds for further investments and philanthropic endeavors.

# curiosity

**Challenges for Musicians in the Music Business** : historically difficult to "make it" in the music business, requiring greatness, luck, being in the right place at the right time, with the right sound, image, and attitude.
**Internet Disruption of the Music Industry (circa 2000)** : the internet, free-flowing digital files, streaming, social media, and YouTube have made music more accessible but also exponentially increased competition, making it harder for new acts to be heard.
**Pre-Internet vs. Post-Internet Artist Divide** : a split exists between artists who established their reputation before the internet hit the music industry around 2000 and those who emerged afterward, with the latter facing significantly higher competition.
**Scarcity of Post-2000 Arena Headliners** : a notable observation is the difficulty in naming many rock bands that emerged after 2000 who are currently capable of filling an arena as a headliner, highlighting the impact of the internet era.
**Catalog Sales by Older Artists** : the trend of artists selling their music catalogs for large sums is typically seen with artists in the twilight of their careers, such as Bob Dylan, Bruce Springsteen, and Tina Turner.
**Diverse Revenue Streams for Music Catalogs** : beyond album sales, streaming, and radio airplay, music catalogs generate revenue from TV commercials, movie soundtracks, video games, TikTok, licensing to platforms like Peloton, and other uses requiring proper music.
**Alan Cross's "The Ongoing History of New Music" Podcast** : a podcast hosted by Alan Cross that provides an ongoing history of new music, available early and ad-free on Amazon Music for Prime subscribers.
**"The Ongoing History of New Music Unlimited" on Apple Podcasts** : a subscription service ($3.49/month) offered by Alan Cross's podcast on Apple Podcasts, granting ad-free access to the full archive, new episodes two days early, and special bonus episodes.
**Alan Cross's Online Presence** : Alan Cross maintains a presence on Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, and TikTok, and offers a website (ajournalofmusicalthings.com) with a free newsletter for daily music news.
