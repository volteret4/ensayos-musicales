# venue - Lollapalooza

## curiosities
**1991 Lineup** : The inaugural Lollapalooza lineup featured Jane's Addiction, Siouxsie and the Banshees, Living Colour, Nine Inch Nails, Butthole Surfers, Rollins Band, Ice-T & Body Count, and Fishbone. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**1992 Main Stage Lineup** : The 1992 main stage featured Lush, Pearl Jam, The Jesus and Mary Chain, Soundgarden, Ice Cube (fresh from N.W.A.), Ministry, and closed with the Red Hot Chili Peppers, who were riding high from their *Blood Sugar Sex Magik* album. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**1992 Resurgence** : The second Lollapalooza tour, starting on July 18, 1992, experienced a dramatic change in landscape, becoming a sensation with sold-out shows and immense excitement. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**1996 Headliner Controversy** : The 1996 festival was "okay," but its headliner, Metallica, "kind of hijacked the whole thing" as their inclusion "didn't feel right" for the alternative vibe, despite a lineup that still included Soundgarden, The Ramones, Rancid, and Rage Against the Machine. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Conception as Traveling Festival** : Unlike previous US festivals like Monterey, Woodstock, Altamont, Us Festivals, or Gathering of the Tribes, which were rooted in one place, Lollapalooza was designed as a traveling festival, moving from city to city over several months. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Decline and Hiatus (1997-2003)** : Enthusiasm for the traveling festival waned by its last tour in 1997. An attempt to revive it in 2003 went largely unremembered. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Early Criticisms** : The festival faced significant criticism initially, with skeptics doubting its financial viability, predicting disaster for taking "a bunch of no name, so-called alternative bands" on a two-month road tour. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**First Show (1991)** : The very first Lollapalooza show began outside Phoenix on July 18, 1991. The initial box office performance was modest, making some money but not a lot. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Glory Years (1992-1995)** : The period from 1992 through 1995 is considered Lollapalooza's "glory years," during which it profoundly represented the state of the alternative nation. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Initial Purpose as Farewell Tour** : Lollapalooza was originally conceived as a farewell tour for Jane's Addiction, masquerading as a music festival to help it get off the ground. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Introduction of Second Stage (1992)** : 1992 also marked the first year that a second stage was introduced on the tour, featuring bands like Stone Temple Pilots, Cypress Hill, Rage Against the Machine, and Tool at various stops. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Major Participants (1992-1995)** : During its peak, the festival featured The Smashing Pumpkins, Beastie Boys, Green Day, Sonic Youth, Beck, Tool, Nick Cave, The Verve, Patti Smith, and Moby. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Name Origin** : The name "Lollapalooza" was chosen from old Three Stooges clips, derived from a late 19th-century slang word meaning an "extraordinary or unusual thing or event." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Origin and Inspiration** : The idea for Lollapalooza originated from Perry Farrell of Jane's Addiction, who was inspired by the intense crowd reaction to the Pixies' set at Reading Festival on August 26, 1990. Farrell questioned why such a powerful musical gathering couldn't exist in America. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Permanent Home and Revival (2005)** : Lollapalooza reestablished itself as a major music festival when it found a permanent annual home in Chicago in 2005. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Pop Punk Headliner Decline (2010s)** : pop punk bands no longer headlined this festival in the 2010s. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Timing of First Tour** : The first Lollapalooza tour wrapped up on August 28, 1991, coincidentally the day before Nirvana released "Smells Like Teen Spirit" and Pearl Jam released their debut album, marking it as "a few months too early" for the impending alt-rock explosion. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
