# venue - Manny's Music

## curiosities
**Appearances in Pop Culture** : Manny's Music was featured in popular culture, including shots in Guns N' Roses' "Paradise City" video, highlighting its iconic status within the music industry. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
**Closure and Legacy of Henry Goldrich** : Manny's Music ceased operations in 2009. Henry Goldrich, who inherited and managed the store, died at the age of 88 in early 2021. Both the store and its proprietor left an indelible mark on music history through their influence on countless musicians and the gear they sold. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
**Historic Music Retailer** : Manny's Music was a renowned music store located on West 58th Street, between 6th and 7th Avenues, in New York City. Established in 1935 by Manny Goldrich, and later managed by his son Henry Goldrich, it became a legendary destination for musicians seeking high-quality instruments and accessories. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
**Meeting Point for Renowned Musicians** : Manny's Music was a compulsory stop for countless touring and local musicians in New York City. Its customer base included The Beatles, Pete Townshend, Bob Dylan, and thousands of other famous and aspiring artists. The store's reputation and clientele gave it a significant impact on the trajectory of rock music. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
**Purchase of David Gilmour's "Black Strat"** : David Gilmour's legendary "Black Strat," a Fender Stratocaster famously used on numerous iconic Pink Floyd recordings, was purchased at Manny's Music in 1969. This acquisition further cemented the store's legacy as a source of legendary musical instruments. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
