# venue - Roskilde Festival

## curiosities
**2000 Tragedy and Safety Protocols** : The festival's worst year was 2000, when nine people died and 26 were injured during a Pearl Jam set, in front of approximately 60,000 attendees at the Orange Stage. This tragic accident led to the implementation of new safety protocols at festivals worldwide. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Founding and History (1971)** : Roskilde Festival, held in a Danish town, was started by two high school students in 1971 and has been running annually ever since. It typically occurs right after Glastonbury Festival. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Notable Performers** : Roskilde's lineups rival Glastonbury's, featuring artists such as U2, Bob Marley, Nirvana, Bob Dylan, Sex Pistols, Radiohead, Red Hot Chili Peppers, Black Sabbath, Prince, and Rage Against the Machine. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Scale and Diversity** : In modern times, Roskilde can host up to 130,000 people who come to see 180 different acts, representing genres like rock, folk, electronic, and hip hop. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**The Naked Run** : A unique tradition at Roskilde is the "Naked Run," a race around the campsite where participants wear no clothes. The top male and female runners receive free tickets to the following year's festival, a tradition so popular that qualifier events are now held. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
