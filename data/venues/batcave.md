# venue - Batcave

## curiosities
**Aesthetic and Policy** : The club featured black walls decorated with leather, lace, and design cues from 1930s and 1940s black-and-white horror films. It had an unofficial dress code and a strict music policy: "Nothing funky, nothing poppy." The musical and aesthetic focus was on reinventing glam-era David Bowie (e.g., *Ziggy Stardust* or *Aladdin Sane*) crossed with Tim Curry's Dr. Frank-N-Furter from *The Rocky Horror Picture Show*, fostering an atmosphere of sexual inclusivity (straight, gay, and everything in between). ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
**Headquarters of Early Goth (July 1982)** : The Batcave was a club that opened in July 1982 in London's Soho district, becoming the essential "headquarters" and "ground zero for Goth proper." It was founded by members of the band Specimen. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
**Influence and Expansion** : The Batcave became incredibly popular, leading to franchising. "Batcave nights" were staged at other clubs around the UK, maintaining the same look, feel, and sound. It even established a residency in New York at a place called Danceteria. The Batcave played a crucial role, arguably more than any other entity, in spreading the "gospel of Goth" in its early days. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
