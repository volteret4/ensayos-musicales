# venue - Woodstock '94

## curiosities
**Attendance and Conditions** : The festival attracted approximately 550,000 people. It was plagued by "terrible organization," overpriced food, and adverse weather conditions including rain, mud, and thunderstorms. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Career Boost for Alt Acts** : Despite its organizational issues, Woodstock '94 received significant media coverage and provided substantial career boosts for many alternative acts who performed. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Location and Anniversary** : Woodstock '94 was held in Saugerties, New York, on the 25th anniversary of the original 1969 event, at a site resembling Max Yasgur's farm. Notably, none of the Woodstock festivals have ever actually taken place in Woodstock, New York. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Mike Dirnt Incident** : During Green Day's set, fans, "having had enough of being abused," began tearing up and throwing turf. In the resulting confusion, Green Day bassist Mike Dirnt was mistakenly tackled by a security guard, smashing his face into a stage monitor, breaking several teeth, and requiring emergency dental surgery. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Notable Performers** : Performers included Nine Inch Nails (who performed covered in mud), Red Hot Chili Peppers (with new guitarist Dave Navarro), Violent Femmes, Collective Soul, James, Blind Melon, and The Cranberries. Green Day also performed, on a Sunday, after their album *Dookie* had become a huge hit. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
