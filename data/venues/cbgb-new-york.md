# venue - CBGB, New York

## curiosities
**Closure Due to Gentrification (2006)** : Survived for decades but faced increasing financial pressure due to rising rent ($19,000, then $35,000 a month), a $90,000 lawsuit from a local residence committee, and neighborhood gentrification. An attempt to declare it a historical landmark failed.
**Final Weeks and Last Gig (2006)** : Its final weeks in October 2006 featured performances by bands like Bad Brains, The Dictators, and Blondie, culminating in Patti Smith performing the last ever gig on October 15, 2006.
**Ground Zero for Punk Rock (1970s)** : Became a crucial venue for the birth and development of punk rock in the 1970s, hosting bands like the Ramones, Talking Heads, Blondie, and Television.
**Opened by Hilly Crystal (1973)** : An ex-Marine named Hilly Crystal opened the club in 1973 in the Bowery, a rundown part of New York City, where rent was cheap and starving artists could live and rehearse.
