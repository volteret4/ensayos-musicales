# concert - Railway Tavern

## curiosities
**Origin of The Who's Instrument Destruction Trademark** : In 1964, The Who was playing at the Railway Tavern in London, an intimate venue with a low ceiling. Pete Townsend accidentally broke his new Rickenbacker guitar by hitting the ceiling, leading him to deliberately destroy it and his amp in frustration. This act was well-received by the audience and became a regular part of The Who's live shows, establishing their trademark instrument destruction. "The who were playing a small pup in London called the Railway Tavern. The ceiling was quite low. And at some point, Pete Townsend raised his brand new Rikin' Backer guitar too high and smashed the headstock into the ceiling." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yPok3MemDmE
