# concert - Project Revolution Tour

## curiosities
**Chris Cornell and Chester Bennington Performance (2008)** : During the 2008 Project Revolution Tour, Chris Cornell was on the bill, and he was joined onstage by Chester Bennington to perform Temple of the Dog's song "Hunger Strike." This surprise duet was later released twice to Linkin Park's fan club. "If we go back to the 2008 project revolution tour, Chris was on the bill. And when he stepped forward to sing the Temple of Dog Song, Hunger Strike, a song written for and dedicated to Chris's late roommate Andrew Wood of Mother Lovevont, Chester surprised everybody by coming in to sing with him." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qQm7u3W13eM
**Innovative Merch Sales** : During the Project Revolution tour, Linkin Park implemented an innovative approach to merchandise by selling blank CDs for $11 at their booths. Each CD came with a download code, allowing fans to legally download and burn that specific Linkin Park show for their personal enjoyment. "What was cool about that one is that Lincoln Park sold blank CDs for $11 at their merch booths. Each came with a download code that allowed any fan to download and burn that particular Lincoln Park show for their personal use." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIlCaKCvylI
**Mobile Festival Initiative** : The Project Revolution tour, which ran in 2007 and 2008, was a mobile two-stage festival that visited 29 cities across four continents. It notably featured Linkin Park alongside other bands such as My Chemical Romance, Taking Back Sunday, and Placebo. "Also tucked in there was the Project Revolution tour, which was a mobile two-stage festival that traveled to 29 cities and also featured bands like Mike Hemmacher-Romance, Taking Back Sunday and Placebo." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIlCaKCvylI
