# concert - Winterland Ballroom

## curiosities
**Location of Sex Pistols' Final Performance** : The Sex Pistols' US tour, which was "running on fumes," concluded at the Winterland Ballroom in San Francisco on January 14, 1978. Johnny Rotten's closing remarks, "Have I yet to feel that you've been cheated? Good night," marked the end of the band. "By the time the tour limped into the Winterland Ballroom in San Francisco in January the 14th, the Sex Pistols were running on fumes. And then, for the final song, they came to an end." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LE4YenwmeXM
**Sex Pistols Final Performance** : This venue was the location of the Sex Pistols' final concert on January 14, 1978. "The last show was at the Winterland Ballroom on January 14, 1978." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gKrAXMG8sTc
**Sex Pistols' Final Performance (1978)** : The Winterland Ballroom in San Francisco hosted the Sex Pistols' last performance on January 14, 1978, effectively ending their tumultuous American tour and marking the final time the original lineup, with Sid Vicious, would play together for almost 18 years. The concert, described as not a "rock and roll party" but rather like "a dying horse," was a chaotic culmination of internal strife, hostile audiences, and personal excesses, captured in "The Great Rock and Roll Swindle." "That night in Winterland in San Francisco, the sex pistols played to the whole world. It wasn't a rock and roll party. It was more like a dying horse that needed putting out of its misery." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1y2UuU-me_s
