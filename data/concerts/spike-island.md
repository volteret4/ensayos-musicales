# concert - Spike Island

## curiosities
**Massive Stone Roses Performance (1990)** : In 1990, The Stone Roses performed a landmark outdoor concert at Spike Island, attracting an enormous crowd of 30,000 people to what was described as a "horrible plot of land." This gig was a monumental event for the Madchester scene, showcasing its massive appeal and momentum. "The Spike Island gig, which attracts 30,000 people to this horrible plot of land." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dZStbEe4Gkk
**The Stone Roses Headliner (1990)** : On May 27, 1990, Spike Island hosted The Stone Roses as headliners for an outdoor festival on once heavily polluted land. This event was remarkable for drawing 30,000 attendees, a significant increase from the 30 people they attracted to bar gigs just a year prior. "A year later, May 27, 1990, the Roses had lined an outdoor festival on a strip of once heavily polluted land called Spike Island. Twelve months earlier, they were struggling to get 30 people to show up to a bar gig. This time, though, 30,000 people turned up, paying about 14 quid to get in." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q4dEV1yn3OY
**The Stone Roses' Landmark Gig (May 27, 1990)** : On May 27, 1990, Spike Island, a site on the Mersey River near Liverpool, hosted the largest gig of The Stone Roses' career, attracting 30,000 "ecstasy field fans." Despite being a somewhat toxic location due to a nearby chemical factory (causing chemical burns and residue), the event, which featured an indie band headlining to such a massive crowd for the first time, was hailed as a cultural watershed and the height of Madchester and ecstasy mania. "The Stone Roses, the most important rock band to come out of Britain in a long, long time, played the biggest gig of the career in front of 30,000 ecstasy field fans in a toxic dump known as Spike Island." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AymMybE33zU
**The Stone Roses' Legendary Outdoor Gig (May 27, 1990)** : Spike Island, a peninsula on the Mersey River surrounded by chemical factories and power plants and a reclaimed chemical dump, was the site of a legendary outdoor gig by The Stone Roses on May 27, 1990. Although the band's performance was considered "average or even below average," it attracted their biggest crowd (28,000-30,000 people). The event became iconic as a "meeting of the minds" for the "Madchester kids" and "rave kids," and was heavily hyped by the British music press, who declared it the start of the 90s. "This is when The Stone Roses played a legendary outdoor gig at a place called Spike Island." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=huAvyInzz3Q
