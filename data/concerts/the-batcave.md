# concert - The Batcave

## curiosities
**Expansion and Influence** : The Batcave's popularity quickly grew beyond its original London location, leading to "Batcave nights" in other UK clubs, franchised versions, and even a showcase at the Danceterian club in New York, significantly contributing to the spread of Goth culture. "The Batcave took off. In fact, it couldn't be confined to just that one location." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gd9sO7c3N1s
**Founding as Goth Headquarters (July 1982)** : Opened in July 1982 in London's Soho district by the theatrical group Speseman, The Batcave became the central "headquarters" and "Ground Zero for the Goth explosion." "That all changed in July of 1982 when a new club opened in the Soho district of London called the Batcave." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gd9sO7c3N1s
**Franchising and Global Reach** : Due to its popularity, The Batcave franchised, staging themed nights at other clubs across the UK and even establishing a residence in New York at a place called The Danceteria, effectively spreading the goth "gospel." "The Batcave became so popular that it had to franchise. They staged Batcave nights at other clubs around the UK, all with the same look and feel and sound. They even made it to New York, where they had a residence at a place called the Dancer Tereo." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
**Horror-Inspired Decor** : The club's interior was decorated with black, leather, and lace, incorporating design elements from 1930s horror movies. "They decorated the place in black and leather and lace with other design cues taken from horror movies from the 1930s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gd9sO7c3N1s
**Sexual Inclusivity** : The Batcave fostered an inclusive atmosphere regarding sexuality, welcoming straight, gay, and everything in between without judgment. "That means there was also a sex vibe to everything. Straight gay and everything in between. No judgment whatsoever." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
**Soho London Club (July 1982)** : Opened in July 1982 in London's Soho district by members of the band Specimen, The Batcave became the essential headquarters and central hub for the emerging goth scene. "In July 1982, a new club opened in the Soho district of London. It was called the Batcave." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
**Strict Music Policy** : The Batcave enforced a strict music policy, explicitly stating "absolutely no funk." Its musical aim was to fuse "glam era David Bowie" with Dr. Frankenforder from The Rocky Horror Picture Show, promoting an environment of diverse sexuality. "And there was a definite music policy in this place, absolutely no funk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gd9sO7c3N1s
**Theatrical Decor and Policy** : Decorated with black walls, leather, lace, and design cues from 1930s-40s black and white horror films, the club enforced an unofficial dress code and a strict music policy that excluded funky or poppy sounds, focusing on a reinvention of glam-era David Bowie crossed with Tim Curry's Dr. Frank N. Furter from The Rocky Horror Picture Show. "Specimen's club had black walls and was decorated with leather and lace and other design cues from black and white horror films from the 30s and 40s. There was an unofficial dress code, and there was a hard and fast music policy. Nothing funky, nothing poppy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
