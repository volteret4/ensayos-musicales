# concert - U2 360 tour

## curiosities
**Bono's Back Injury and Tour Postponement (2010)** : The tour faced a significant postponement in 2010 when Bono suffered a severe back injury. Despite the high financial stakes of the tour, German doctors aided his recovery, and all postponed shows were successfully rescheduled. "There was a long postponement in 2010 when Bono badly hurt his back." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=19Z_AAEy8Bc
**Costly Venue Modifications (2009-2011)** : Due to the enormous size of its stages, the tour necessitated expensive modifications at some venues. U2 paid $2 million to reposition an HD screen at Cowboy Stadium in Dallas and $3 million to expand the Hipodrome in Montreal to accommodate the "claw" structures. "U2 had to pay $2 million to hoist the HD screen out of the way at Cowboy Stadium and Dallas. They also had to expand the hipodrome in Montreal just so they could get inside, and that cost $3 million." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=19Z_AAEy8Bc
**Extensive Logistics and Crew Requirements (2009-2011)** : The 360 tour's logistical complexity demanded 120 trucks per stage, 137 permanent roadies, and 120 locally hired crew. The stages were continuously leapfrogged across the globe, with one in use, one being dismantled, and one in transit, requiring shipping for ocean crossings. "And each stage required 120 trucks to transport it. Plus, 137 permanent roadies and 120 locally hired crew." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=19Z_AAEy8Bc
**Massive "Claw" Stages (2009-2011)** : The tour featured three enormous, four-legged "claw" stages, each over 50 meters tall, costing up to $30 million, and capable of holding 200 tons of gear. This innovative design provided a 360-degree view for audiences, increasing venue capacity by 10-20%. "Three massive, four-legged claw stages were constructed by companies in Belgium and the United States. Each was over 50 meters tall and cost up to $30 million each." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=19Z_AAEy8Bc
**Most Ambitious Touring Construct (2009-2011)** : The U2 360 tour, which ran from June 30, 2009, to July 30, 2011, is recognized as one of the most, if not the most, ambitious touring constructs of all time, utilizing unprecedented stage technology and logistics. "This remains one of the most, if not the most, ambitious touring constructs of all time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=19Z_AAEy8Bc
**One Claw Stage Repurposed (Post-Tour)** : One of the unique "claw" stages from the U2 360 tour has been repurposed and is now located at the Loveland Living Planet Aquarium in Salt Lake City, where it serves as a multi-functional space for concerts, movies, community events, and a farmer's market. "I can't find two of them, but one can be seen at the Loveland Living Planet Aquarium in Salt Lake City." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=19Z_AAEy8Bc
**Record-Breaking Gross Revenue (Pre-2019)** : The U2 360 tour, with 111 shows and 7.3 million tickets sold, recorded an official box office take of $736 million US dollars, making it the highest-grossing concert tour of all time for several years. Adjusted for inflation, its gross nears $1 billion, securing its place as the third highest-grossing tour historically. "Officially, the box-oppest take was $736 million US dollars." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=19Z_AAEy8Bc
