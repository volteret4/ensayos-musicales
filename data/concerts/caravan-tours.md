# concert - Caravan Tours

## curiosities
**Common Backing Band** : A notable feature of early caravan tours was the use of a common backing band, where all the artists on the bill would share the same musicians for their performances. "In some cases, there would be a common backing band. All the artists would use the same musicians." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Early Rock Package Tours (1950s)** : In the 1950s, these tours were the most efficient and cost-effective method for sending rock acts on the road, bundling multiple artists together. They were often organized by pioneering disc jockeys like Alan Freed and Dick Clark, playing at venues such as theaters and county fairs. "These became known as caravan tours, guys like Alan Fried, the pioneering disc jockey, the Dick Clark, you know him, right? Took all these acts on the road playing places like theaters and county fairs and wherever else they could find a booking." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**On-site PA Equipment** : During the era of caravan tours in the 1950s, PA (public address) equipment was often supplied directly at the performance venues, rather than being carried by the touring acts. "PA equipment, such as it was in those days, was often supplied on site." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
