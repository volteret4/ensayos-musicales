# concert - International Pop Underground Convention

## curiosities
**"Love Rock Revolution Girl Style" Night** : The first night of the International Pop Underground Convention was specifically titled "Love Rock Revolution Girl Style" and featured an all-women lineup, including pioneering Riot Grrrl bands like Bikini Kill and Bratmobile. This dedicated night underscored the emerging female-centric movement. "The first night was called Love Rock Revolution Girl Style, and it featured in all women lineup." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Qss5kC2FUx0
**Manifesto-like Poster Description** : The poster for the International Pop Underground Convention declared a call for "international rockers of the world to convene in celebration of our grand independence," inviting "hangman hipsters, new mod rockers, side street walkers, scooter-mounted dream girls, punks, teds, the instigators of the Love Rock explosion, the editors of every angry girl's in, the plotters of youth, repellion in every form" to gather from August 20th to 25th, 1991. This detailed description encapsulated the DIY, rebellious, and diverse spirit of the indie underground. "Let me read from the poster. As the corporate ogre expands its creeping influence on the minds of industrialized youth, the time has come for the international rockers of the world to convene in celebration of our grand independence." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Qss5kC2FUx0
**Six-Day Olympia Festival (August 1991)** : The International Pop Underground Convention was a significant six-day music festival held in Olympia in late August 1991. It was a major formative event for the Riot Grrrl movement, emphasizing independent artists and a do-it-yourself ethos, featuring a diverse lineup of male and female indie bands. "A major formative event was the six-day international pop underground convention. This was a music festival in Olympia in late August 1991." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Qss5kC2FUx0
