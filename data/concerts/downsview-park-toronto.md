# concert - Downsview Park, Toronto

## curiosities
**Catastrophic Stage Collapse (June 16, 2012)** : On June 16, 2012, at Downsview Park, approximately one hour before gates were scheduled to open for a Radiohead concert, a temporary stage structure weighing 7,000 kilos tragically collapsed. This incident occurred on a sunny, nearly windless day. "a part of the stage constructed for that night's gig, a structured laden with 7,000 kilos of gear suddenly gave way, plummeting to the ground" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vt6DVj7S6nk
**Extensive Equipment Damage** : The collapse destroyed Radiohead's entire light show and the majority of their musical gear, leading to the immediate cancellation of the concert. "The band's entire light show and most of their musical gear was destroyed." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vt6DVj7S6nk
**Fatalities and Injuries** : The stage collapse resulted in the death of 33-year-old drum tech Scott Johnson, who was tragically killed by a video monitor weighing 2,270 kilos. Three other members of the crew sustained injuries during the incident. "killing 33-year-old drum tech Scott Johnson, a guy who'd work with drummers Phil Selway for years. He was killed by a video monitor that weighed 2,270 kilos." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vt6DVj7S6nk
**Involved Parties and Not Guilty Pleas** : The defendants in the legal proceedings included Live Nation (the promoter), Optics Staging and Services (the company responsible for stage construction), and Dominic Caligari (the engineer overseeing the build). All three defendants entered pleas of not guilty. "Parties included live nation, the promoter, optics staging and services, the company responsible for building the stage, Dominic Caligari, the engineer at charge of supervising the build, and all three defendants pleaded not guilty." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vt6DVj7S6nk
**Lack of Accountability** : As of the time of the program's production (late 2018), no one had been held accountable for the stage collapse, and a promised inquest had not occurred, leaving the matter entirely unresolved. "Still no promised inquest, and no one has been held accountable." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vt6DVj7S6nk
**Protracted Legal Aftermath** : The tragedy instigated a "long, farcical train" of unresolved legal proceedings, including investigations, lawsuits, counter-suits, charges, changes in judges, legal bureaucracies, two criminal trials, and ultimately a mistrial. "And then came the long, farcical train of investigations, lawsuits, counter-suits, charges, switch judges, legal bureaucracies, two criminal trials, and in the end, a mistrial that left everything unresolved." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vt6DVj7S6nk
**Proximity of Band to Collapse** : Due to unforeseen delays in the production schedule, Radiohead narrowly avoided being on stage during the collapse; had things been running on time, the band would have been sound-checking when the structure failed. "Had things been on time, Radiohead would have been sound checking at the time of the collapse." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vt6DVj7S6nk
**Victim's Father's Expertise** : Ken Johnson, the father of the deceased drum tech Scott Johnson, works for Britain's National Access and Scaffolding Confederation and serves on its health and safety and technical committees. Given his professional background, he "has no doubt why the accident happened." "Ken Johnson, Scott's father, works for Britain's national access and scaffolding confederation, and sits on their health and safety and technical committees. He has no doubt why the accident happened." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vt6DVj7S6nk
