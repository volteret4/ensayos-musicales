# concert - Coachella

## curiosities
**1990s Origin** : While often considered a 21st-century event, Coachella actually started in the 1990s, specifically on October 9th and 10th, 1999. Its lineup was reflective of the decade's end. "But while most people consider Coachella to be a 21st century thing, it actually started in the 90s, October 9th and 10th 1999." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Blink 182 Surprise Headliner (2023)** : On April 14, 2023, Blink 182 was announced as a surprise replacement headliner for R&B singer Frank Ocean, who had to cancel with only two days' notice. This performance marked the first time the original lineup (Mark, Travis, Tom) had played together in nine years, drawing 150,000 attendees for an hour-long set that was described as "one big sing along." "And with just two days to go, Blink 182 was announced as his surprise replacement." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EaGUC4av1R4
**Booking Indie Rock Bands (Early 2000s)** : Coachella, a major American festival, began actively booking indie rock bands during the early 2000s, reflecting the growing mainstream acceptance and popularity of the genre. This inclusion helped to elevate the status of the indie rock revival, moving it from underground scenes to larger, more established platforms. "Festivals like Coachella in America and Glastonbury in the UK started booking these bands." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xid8CZf4oYo
**Disastrous First Event (1999)** : The first Coachella festival in 1999 was poorly attended, with only 10,000 people showing up due to the long drive from LA into the desert, the recent disaster of Woodstock 99, no permitted camping, and insanely hot weather. The promoter lost nearly a million dollars and had to delay payments to some bands. "The lineup was fitting as we moved into the last 100 days of the decade. There was Beck and Morrissey, Underworld, Raging Ends the Machine, Pavement, Modest Mouse, Sounded great, right? But only 10,000 people showed up." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Evolution from Alt-Rock to Pop/Hip-Hop (Post-2008)** : Coachella, which began in the desert in 1999, initially focused on "Lollapalooza-type bands" such as Tool, Rage Against The Machine, Jane's Addiction, Oasis, Red Hot Chili Peppers, The Cure, Radiohead, Nine Inch Nails, Depeche Mode, Coldplay, and The Killers. However, starting around 2008, the festival's focus shifted towards pop and hip-hop, mirroring a cultural change in America where these genres became dominant among festival-goers. While still featuring some rock acts, its overall complexion is now vastly different from its early years. "When it began in the desert in 1999, its focus was on La La Palosa type bands, Tool, Rage, Jain's Addiction Oasis, Chili Peppers, The Cure, Radiohead, Ninish Nails, The Pesh Mode, Coldplay, Killers, and so on." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZzIqIydZTlA

**Growing Alt Rock Influence** : Coachella steadily increased in size and prominence each year in the mid-2000s, with its lineup featuring an almost exclusive focus on alt rock acts. "Coachella was getting bigger and bigger every year with a line up that was almost exclusively alt rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7dXtgTkdv9w
**Hiatus and Re-emergence** : The initial 1999 festival was so rough that it almost killed the entire concept, leading to no event in 2000. However, it reappeared in 2001 and has been a growing concern ever since. "That first Coachella was so rough it almost killed the entire concept. There was no event in 2000, but it reappeared in 2001 and has been a going and growing concern ever since." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Mid-Aughts Headliners** : During the middle of the aughts (2000s), Coachella featured a strong lineup of established and reformed alt-rock acts as headliners, including Weezer, Tool, Depeche Mode, Radiohead, The Cure, and The Smashing Pumpkins. "Headliners in the middle arts included Weezer, Tool, Depeche Mode, Radiohead, The Cure, are reform smashing pumpkins." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7dXtgTkdv9w
**Modern Glastonbury Equivalent** : Coachella is described as a "monster" festival spread over two weekends at the Empire Polo Grounds in Indio, California, every April, attracting people globally. It is considered the North American equivalent to Glastonbury. "If there is an North American equivalent to Glastonbury, this is certainly it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Nirvana Reunion Rumors (around 2000)** : Rumors circulated that there would be a full Nirvana reunion, with Kurt Cobain and Rivers Cuomo rehearsing for it at Coachella sometime around the year 2000. "rumors from sprouting that Rivers was in rehearsal with Nirvana for a reunion, a full Nirvana reunion with Kurt, I guess, at Coachella sometime around 2000." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pmKjZzypzvg
**Pop Punk Headliner Absence (2010s)** : Pop punk bands no longer headlined major festivals like Coachella in the 2010s, indicative of the genre's decline in mainstream popularity. "Pop punk bands no longer headline festivals like Coachella or La Paloza." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Tupac Shakur Hologram Performance (2012)** : Coachella hosted a pivotal moment in hologram technology in 2012 when a virtual Tupac Shakur performed alongside Dr. Dre and Snoop Dogg. Although technically a sophisticated projection using Pepper's Ghost, its remarkably lifelike appearance was a "turning point" in the development and acceptance of such performances. "Then in 2012, a very dead two-pock Shakur showed up at Coachella during a set featuring Dr. Dre and Snoop Dog." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GYUuTbOvyak
