**Booking Indie Rock Bands (Early 2000s)** : Coachella, a major American festival, began actively booking indie rock bands during the early 2000s, reflecting the growing mainstream acceptance and popularity of the genre. This inclusion helped to elevate the status of the indie rock revival, moving it from underground scenes to larger, more established platforms. **Evolution from Alt-Rock to Pop/Hip-Hop (Post-2008)** : Coachella, which began in the desert in 1999, initially focused on "Lollapalooza-type bands" such as Tool, Rage Against The Machine, Jane's Addiction, Oasis, Red Hot Chili Peppers, The Cure, Radiohead, Nine Inch Nails, Depeche Mode, Coldplay, and The Killers. However, starting around 2008, the festival's focus shifted towards pop and hip-hop, mirroring a cultural change in America where these genres became dominant among festival-goers. While still featuring some rock acts, its overall complexion is now vastly different from its early years.
