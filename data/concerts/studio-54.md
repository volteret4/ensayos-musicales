## curiosities
**Elitist Policy (1977)** : When Studio 54 opened in New York in 1977, it maintained a "very elitist policy" regarding who was permitted entry, a stark contrast to the inclusive nature of earlier disco venues like The Loft. **Site of Mick Jagger's Apology to Liza Minnelli** : In 1979, Mick Jagger publicly apologized to Liza Minnelli at Studio 54. This apology was for the controversial inclusion of Liza's mother's image on the original "Some Girls" album cover, which had caused significant offense. **Symbol of 1970s Mainstream Music Scene** : Studio 54 in New York City was a prominent symbol of the mainstream music scene in the mid-1970s, particularly the disco era. Record executives were "too busy going to big stadium shows and getting down at Studio 54" to pay attention to the emerging punk scene. **Symbol of Disco's Shift to Exclusivity** : Emerging in the late 1970s, Studio 54 exemplified the transformation of disco from an inclusive environment focused on self-expression to an exclusive one. Its emphasis on how people looked rather than their ability to dance fundamentally altered the communal spirit of earlier disco clubs. "Disco was an inclusive environment until clubs like Studio 54 came along late in the 70s and ruined all that and made it instead an exclusive environment that was solely focused on how you looked." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 50 - The Travolta Trilogy | https://www.ftr70.com/
