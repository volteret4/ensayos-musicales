# concert - MTV Unplugged

## curiosities
**Coincident Performances by STP and Nirvana** : Stone Temple Pilots' MTV Unplugged episode was broadcast on November 17, 1993. Notably, Nirvana set up in the same studio to record their famous unplugged performance just one day after STP's broadcast. Both bands chose to cover "semi obscure songs by David Bowie" as part of their sets. "On Temple Pilots, performing for MTV with an unplugged episode broadcast on November 17, 1993, that by the way was one day before Nirvana set up in the same studio to record their famous unplugged performance." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r6vVw66-gmI
**Kurt's Reluctance to Include Dave Grohl** : Prior to the taping, Kurt Cobain considered banning Dave Grohl from participating because he didn't think Dave was up to playing for the acoustic set. "And until the band actually took the stage for the taping of the MTV and Plug broadcast, Kurt had been thinking of banning Dave from the gig because he didn't think he was up to it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cS8k3PYU_Eo
**Nirvana Performance (November 1993)** : Nirvana's performance on MTV Unplugged in November 1993 was likely an attempt to re-excite fans about the band. While it did achieve this, its significant impact was mainly realized after Kurt Cobain's death. "I think part of the reason Nirvana did the MTV Unplug performance in November of 1993 was to try to get people excited about the band again." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cS8k3PYU_Eo
