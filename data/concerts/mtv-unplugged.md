# concert - MTV Unplugged

## curiosities
**Artist Dissatisfaction with Commercial Hype** : Many artists who participated ended up complaining to MTV because the network always generated a commercial "hype" and expectation that did not align with the artists' artistic intentions. "muchos artistas que han hecho antivilant plags se han acabado que jando de la antivil porque siempre esperaba una especie de hype comercial." ← Music Radar Clan > Por qué el MTV Unplugged de Nirvana NO es un mal álbum | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U4CmYfakHkQ&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**Coincident Performances by STP and Nirvana** : Stone Temple Pilots' MTV Unplugged episode was broadcast on November 17, 1993. Notably, Nirvana set up in the same studio to record their famous unplugged performance just one day after STP's broadcast. Both bands chose to cover "semi obscure songs by David Bowie" as part of their sets. "On Temple Pilots, performing for MTV with an unplugged episode broadcast on November 17, 1993, that by the way was one day before Nirvana set up in the same studio to record their famous unplugged performance." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r6vVw66-gmI ← mtv-unplugged
**Forced Collaborations** : MTV often pressured artists into collaborations and duets to generate spectacle, which sometimes lacked artistic meaning or seemed forced, such as Mariah Carey covering The Jackson 5. "la antivision represionó a montar a los artistas para hacer esos duetos y colaboraciones que generaba mucho espectáculo pero que a veces no tenían mucho sentido." ← Music Radar Clan > Por qué el MTV Unplugged de Nirvana NO es un mal álbum | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U4CmYfakHkQ&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**Inconsistent Production and Authenticity** : The format was questioned for various reasons, including concerts that weren't truly unplugged, sessions recorded over multiple takes resulting in a mix of 4 or 5 different recordings, and instances like Annie Lennox's performance being a prior jazz festival concert rather than a dedicated Unplugged. "siempre ha habido un extraño problema de posicionamiento del antivilant plag desde conciertos que no eran conciertos a antivilant plag que se grabaron durante varias tomas." ← Music Radar Clan > Por qué el MTV Unplugged de Nirvana NO es un mal álbum | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U4CmYfakHkQ&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**Kurt's Reluctance to Include Dave Grohl** : Prior to the taping, Kurt Cobain considered banning Dave Grohl from participating because he didn't think Dave was up to playing for the acoustic set. "And until the band actually took the stage for the taping of the MTV and Plug broadcast, Kurt had been thinking of banning Dave from the gig because he didn't think he was up to it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cS8k3PYU_Eo ← mtv-unplugged ← mtv-unplugged
**Nirvana Performance (November 1993)** : Nirvana's performance on MTV Unplugged in November 1993 was likely an attempt to re-excite fans about the band. While it did achieve this, its significant impact was mainly realized after Kurt Cobain's death. "I think part of the reason Nirvana did the MTV Unplug performance in November of 1993 was to try to get people excited about the band again." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cS8k3PYU_Eo ← mtv-unplugged ← mtv-unplugged
**Positional Problematic** : From its inception, the MTV Unplugged series suffered from a "serious positioning problem." It was always an "odd mix" that never fully defined itself, blending "great glories" like Eric Clapton, Bob Dylan, or Annie Lennox (who delivered historical Unplugged performances) with "strange" ones from artists like Shakira or Ricky Martin.
