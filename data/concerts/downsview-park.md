# concert - Downsview Park

## curiosities
**Fatal Stage Collapse (June 16, 2012)** : On June 16, 2012, a stage being built at Downsview Park in Toronto for a Radiohead headlining performance collapsed. The tragic incident, occurring before the band's soundcheck, killed drum tech Scott Johnson, who was crushed by a 5,000-pound video monitor, and seriously injured others. **Radiohead Stage Collapse (June 16, 2012)** : At Downsview Park in Toronto on June 16, 2012, a temporary stage constructed for a Radiohead concert collapsed an hour before gates opened and minutes before the band's soundcheck. Despite having approved plans, permits, and inspections, the stage roof fell, causing the death of Radiohead's drum tech, Scott Johnson, who was crushed by a 2,300-kilogram video monitor, and injuring three other crew members. The concert was canceled, leading to a prolonged period of inquests, lawsuits, and court cases that lasted until late 2020. The tragic event prompted the implementation of new, stricter safety standards for temporary concert stages globally, though many observers felt that justice was not adequately served in the aftermath. "This happened on Saturday, June 16, 2012." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GlcOoINI7EQ
