## curiosities
**Birthplace of New Romanticism** : The Blitz Club, along with Billies, was a pivotal British club that cultivated the New Romantic genre. It provided a dedicated space for new music, leading to the formation of influential synth-based groups such as Spandau Ballet, Visage, and Culture Club. "One such offshoot was known as New Romantic, which had its roots in a couple of British clubs dedicated to new music. One was called Billies, and the other was the Blitz Club." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY ← the-blitz-club
**Birthplace of New Romantics and Synth-pop** : The Blitz Club is recognized as the cradle of the New Romantics movement and, by extension, the seedbed for the synth-pop genre. The endless nights spent dancing to Kraftwerk deeply influenced attendees and nascent bands like Ultravox, Visage, and Spandau Ballet. "El blitz fue la cuna de las tenaníos románticas y de rebote, el semillero del synth pop." ← Por qué KRAFTWERK es la banda más influyente de la historia？ | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wWOBez9Cqhs
**London's New Music Incubator** : Opened in a small London basement in the late 1970s by Steve Strange and Rusty Egan, who didn't fit with the punk scene, The Blitz Club was a venue where music spoke a new language. Kraftwerk's music was a constant presence there, making it an influential hub for emerging electronic sounds.
