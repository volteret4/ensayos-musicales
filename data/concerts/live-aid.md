**Concert Details** : Live Aid was held in Philadelphia on July 13, 1985. It was a major benefit concert intended to raise money to fight famine in Ethiopia. Bob Dylan, Keith Richards, and Ron Wood performed, with Dylan making a controversial remark about also helping American farmers with their mortgages. This comment was a catalyst for the creation of Farm Aid. "It was at Live Aid in Philadelphia on July 13 1985 that Bob Dylan who with Keith Richards and Ron Wood of the Rolling Stones arguably gave one of the worst performances of his life said also though in a somewhat offhanded way that it would be nice if some of the money raised to fight famine in Ethiopia that day could go to helping American farmers pay their mortgages." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 55 - Heartland Rock is about More than the Heartland | https://www.ftr70.com/

**Fundraising Success (July 13, 1985)** : Over the course of 16 hours on July 13, 1985, Live Aid, with performances from London's Wembley Stadium and Philadelphia, engaged more than two billion people and successfully raised $70 million US to alleviate famine in Ethiopia. "By the time things wrapped up in Philadelphia 16 hours later, more than two billion people had helped raise $70 million US for famine relief in Ethiopia." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2RKchFW42Jc ← live-aid
**Global Charity Event (July 13, 1985)** : Live Aid was a massive charity event held on July 13, 1985, simultaneously at Wembley Stadium in London and in Philadelphia. Kicking off at 12:01 PM GMT in London, the 16-hour concert series raised $70 million US for famine relief in Ethiopia, reaching over two billion people worldwide. "It was the start of Live Aid, one of the most spectacular charity events ever conceived." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2RKchFW42Jc ← live-aid
