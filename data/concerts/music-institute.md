# concert - Music Institute

## curiosities
**First Major Techno Club (1988)** : The Music Institute opened in Detroit in 1988, marking the establishment of the first major techno nightclub in a city where techno had previously only been played in abandoned buildings and venues. **Hub for Second Generation Artists** : Key artists of the second techno generation, such as Carl Craig, Richie Hawtin, and Jeff Mills, frequented the Music Institute. They would attend to enter trance-like states through the techno music, often dancing continuously for 8 to 15 hours. **Unique Operating Model** : The club, where Derrick May was the resident DJ, operated for over a year. It was notable for not selling alcohol or beer (only water and juice), having no entry fee, and attracting a clientele who often couldn't afford to buy anything due to their lack of resources. "The Rikmai sería el DJ residente, estaría abierto un año y pico, y donde ni siquiera se vendía alcohol ni cerveza solo podías ver agua y zumo y donde no se pagaba por entrar y donde la mayoría de la gente no consumía nada porque no tenía dinero." ← Music Radar Clan > La historia del TECHNO en DETROIT.
