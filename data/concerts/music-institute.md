# concert - Music Institute

## curiosities
**Detroit Techno Hub** : Towards the end of the 1980s, Juan Atkins and partners opened their own nightclub, the Music Institute, inspired by the Chicago scene. This club quickly became the central hub for Detroit techno, fostering the emergence of the "second scene of techno" with artists like Jeff Mills, Robert Hood, and Carl Craig. **First Major Techno Club (1988)** : The Music Institute opened in Detroit in 1988, marking the establishment of the first major techno nightclub in a city where techno had previously only been played in abandoned buildings and venues. **Hub for Second Generation Artists** : Key artists of the second techno generation, such as Carl Craig, Richie Hawtin, and Jeff Mills, frequented the Music Institute. They would attend to enter trance-like states through the techno music, often dancing continuously for 8 to 15 hours. **Short-Lived but Influential** : Despite its profound influence on the second wave of techno artists, the Music Institute club was only open for a year and a half. Its closure was partly due to young people in Detroit at the time preferring gangsta rap over the new techno sounds. **Unique Operating Model** : The club, where Derrick May was the resident DJ, operated for over a year. It was notable for not selling alcohol or beer (only water and juice), having no entry fee, and attracting a clientele who often couldn't afford to buy anything due to their lack of resources.
