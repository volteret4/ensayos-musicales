**New York Punk Scene Hub – Early Gathering Place** : CBGB was one of the central venues for the original punk scene in New York. It served as a gathering place for the "few dozen weirdos" who comprised the early community in the "uglier end of town." "The original punk scene in New York consisted of a few dozen weirdos who hung out at places like CBGB, the Mud Club, and Maxx's Kansas City, in the uglier end of town." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lip0bZX7hBE
**Origin and Rise as Punk Mecca (1973-1974)** : CBGB, originally named CBGBOMFUG (Country, Bluegrass, Blues, and Other Music for Uplifting Gormandizers), was discovered by Richard Hell, Richard Lloyd, and Tom Verlaine in December 1973 while seeking a venue for their band Television. They persuaded owner Hilly Crystal to let them play on Sunday nights. After a "rocky start," word spread among former Mercer Art Center regulars about the "cool happening" at CBGB, leading Crystal to abandon his original music policy and embrace the "freaky people." Throughout 1974 and the rest of the decade, CBGB became "ground zero" and New York's "punk mecca" for the nascent punk rock movement. "And throughout 1974, and for pretty much the rest of the decade, CBGB became ground zero for this new thing called punk rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MTDY6qT11Dc
**Ramones' Home Venue** : CBGB became the Ramones' primary venue, their "home," where they played an impressive 74 times by the end of 1974. In 1975, they performed there 33 times, sometimes alone, other times on bills with bands like Talking Heads, Blondie, or Tuftarts. "CBGBC was the Ramones home. They played elsewhere, but in 1975, they played CB's 33 times." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=j8uk7BnbXwU
**Setting of Ramones' Debut** : On Friday, August 16, 1974, CBGB was a "dive bar" located at 315 Bowery at Bleeker in a rather uncivilized, slum-like part of New York City, surrounded by garbage, abandoned buildings, drug addicts, and homeless people. The owner, Hilly Crystal, an ex-Marine, had started allowing young rock bands to play there since spring 1974, transitioning from its original focus as a "country blues and bluegrass bar." "This part of the city was, to be honest, rather uncivilized. OK, it was a slum." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=j8uk7BnbXwU
**The Ramones' Revolutionary Debut (August 14, 1974)** : On August 14, 1974, this New York club hosted The Ramones' very first public performance, attended by fewer than a dozen people. Yet, by the end of that year, The Ramones had played at CBGB 74 times, consistently growing their audience. The venue eventually became a packed hub for a diverse crowd, including Andy Warhol's associates, intellectuals, wealthy uptown youths seeking to "slum it," and music fans desperately looking for something new and exciting, effectively marking the beginning of the New York punk rock revolution. "The Ramones played their first ever show at a New York club called CBGB." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8UIBWyUt38g
