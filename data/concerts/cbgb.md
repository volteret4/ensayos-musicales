**Birthplace of New York Punk** : In the mid-1970s, CBGB, a small club in New York, became the crucial venue where bands that couldn't find a place in the city's more sophisticated establishments began to play. These bands, though not necessarily virtuosos, shared a common identity: a rejection of stadium rock rules and a desire for a new, raw sound, making the club the epicenter of the emerging New York punk scene. **Club History and Punk Ground Zero** : Founded in 1969 by Hilly Crystal as "Hilly's on the Bowery" and re-opened on December 10, 1973, as CBGB and OMFUG at 315 Bowery in Lower Manhattan, the club initially aimed to feature Country, Bluegrass, and Blues. However, it swiftly transformed into the "ground zero" for punk music after the collapse of the Mercer Art Center in August 1973, which prompted avant-garde artists, including early punk bands like Television, The Ramones, Patti Smith, Suicide, Blondie, Talking Heads, and The Heartbreakers, to seek a new performance space. The venue subsequently gained global fame and became an enduring punk icon. "CBGB became ground zero for an emerging form of music called Punk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uEe_HLUay5M ← cbgb ← cbgb

**Origin and Rise as Punk Mecca (1973-1974)** : CBGB, originally named CBGBOMFUG (Country, Bluegrass, Blues, and Other Music for Uplifting Gormandizers), was discovered by Richard Hell, Richard Lloyd, and Tom Verlaine in December 1973 while seeking a venue for their band Television. They persuaded owner Hilly Crystal to let them play on Sunday nights. After a "rocky start," word spread among former Mercer Art Center regulars about the "cool happening" at CBGB, leading Crystal to abandon his original music policy and embrace the "freaky people." Throughout 1974 and the rest of the decade, CBGB became "ground zero" and New York's "punk mecca" for the nascent punk rock movement. "And throughout 1974, and for pretty much the rest of the decade, CBGB became ground zero for this new thing called punk rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MTDY6qT11Dc ← cbgb
