# concert - Theater of Dionysus

## curiosities
**Early Ticketing and Scalping** : Not all Athenians could attend shows at the Theater of Dionysus, requiring permission which soon evolved into hard tickets, possibly with a price. This early system also saw the emergence of scalpers, who would sell tickets at inflated prices for sold-out events. "You needed permission to get in. And that soon meant some kind of hard ticket. And chances are if an event was sold out, there was some guy outside the theater offering to sell you one at an inflated price, of course." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OCkov6FTliE
**First Major Outdoor Theater (325 BCE)** : Built into the hillside of the Acropolis in Athens around 325 BCE, the Theater of Dionysus was the first major outdoor theater. It featured semi-circular rows and could accommodate up to 17,000 people, necessitating a system of permission and hard tickets for entry.
