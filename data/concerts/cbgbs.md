# concert - CBGBs

## curiosities
**Epicenter of New York Punk Scene** : The scene surrounding CBGBs in New York became a rapidly growing hub for emerging punk artists and a focal point for the developing punk culture. Legs McNeill's "Punk" fanzine extensively documented the groups and individuals associated with this specific scene, significantly contributing to the popularization of the term "punk artist" for those within it. "And as the scene surrounding CBGBs grew bigger and bigger, circulation of punk increased." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z8BTWo66SDI
**Malcolm McLaren's Punk Fashion Epiphany (1975)** : In 1975, while on a trip to New York for a fashion fair, Malcolm McLaren visited CBGBs to immerse himself in the local music scene. It was there he encountered Richard Hell, whose poverty-driven habit of securing ripped clothing with safety pins mistakenly inspired McLaren, leading him to adopt this look and introduce it to his London store, "Sex," where it became a foundational element of punk fashion. "Malcolm took a side trip to CBGBs to see what all the fuss was about. And that's where he ran into Richard Hell." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1y2UuU-me_s
