**Free Festival Decision (1969)** : The festival's organization, having lacked time to properly fence the grounds, found it impossible to control the entry of people. Many of the 50,000 early arrivals did not have tickets, and the original $18 entry fee was largely unpaid. Faced with uncontrollable crowds and the potential for riots if they tried to enforce payment, the organizers opted to surrender and allow everyone to enter freely. **Permitting and Venue Struggles (1969)** : In the early months of 1969, the four young organizers faced immense difficulties, lacking a lineup, venue, and permits due to government and local opposition. They struggled to find a farmer willing to host, encountering significant community resistance and last-minute laws to prevent the festival. After months of frustration, they finally secured a farm in Bethel, nearly 70 kilometers from the originally planned Woodstock site, despite it lacking water, electricity, sanitation, and direct road access. **Traffic Chaos and Mass Migration (1969)** : By August 13, 1969, tens of thousands of cars were stuck on surrounding roads, unable to advance, as people attempted to access a venue without direct road entry. Frustrated and paralyzed, thousands abandoned their vehicles mid-road and chose to walk, in some cases up to 20 kilometers, to reach the festival. An estimated 180,000 of the nearly half a million attendees left their cars behind.
