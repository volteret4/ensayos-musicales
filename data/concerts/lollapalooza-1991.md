# concert - Lollapalooza (1991)

## curiosities
**Conception by Perry Farrell** : Lollapalooza was conceived by Perry Farrell, who, after being inspired by the energy of The Pixies at the 1990 Reading Festival, sought to replicate the UK festival experience in America. He gathered management and investors to create an "old-style traveling caravan" of bands. "He thought to himself, hey, why can't we replicate the UK festival experience in America? So when he got home, he got some management types and moneymen together and created an old-style traveling caravan just like they used to have in the 1960s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xdn31jMOOA4
**Inaugural Tour and Lineup** : The first Lollapalooza tour commenced on July 18, 1991, and concluded on August 28 of the same year. It was headlined by Jane's Addiction and featured a diverse lineup of up-and-coming alternative acts including Nine Inch Nails, the Rollins Band, Ice Tea, Body Counts, the Butthole Surfers, and Suzy and the Banshees. "The first La La Paloza tour started on July 18, 1991 and ended on August 28. It featured Jane's Addiction as the headliners along with up and coming bands like Nine Inch Nails and the Rollins Band along with Ice Tea and Body Counts and the Butthole Surfers and Suzy and the Banshees and more." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xdn31jMOOA4
**Initial Skepticism and Unexpected Success** : Initially, there was widespread skepticism about Lollapalooza's viability, with promoters believing there weren't enough alternative music fans to support such an event. However, within a year, everything changed, and the festival played a crucial role in the birth of the "alternative nation." "It seemed that no one believed that Perry Farrell's idea was a good one. ...Honestly, that was the buzz in the summer of 1991, but within a year, everything was different." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xdn31jMOOA4
