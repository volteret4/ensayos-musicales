# concert - Jamaican sound system parties

## curiosities
**Birthplace of Modern Remixes** : In the late 1960s and early 1970s, these parties were the genesis of modern remixes, where sound system operators played instrumental "versions" of songs. The goal was to keep everyone dancing amidst intense competition to host the biggest and loudest gigs with the best "toasters" performing live rhymes over the music. "And soon the island was filled with guys who ran sound systems, these giant portable DJ rigs, that were always ready for a party. The goal was to keep everybody dancing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=08i9lVd6T8c
**Real-Time Remixing** : At these parties, DJs used two turntables and a microphone to perform real-time remixes, surprising and delighting the crowds with on-the-fly creations. The most skilled mixers, coupled with the biggest sound systems and best "toasters," became significant stars. "Using two turn tables in a microphone, songs were remixed in real time, offering surprise and delight to the crowd." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=08i9lVd6T8c
