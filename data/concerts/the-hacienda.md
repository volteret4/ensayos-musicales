## curiosities
**Decline and Closure (1997)** : The Hacienda eventually succumbed to problems with drugs, gangs, and violence. It lost its liquor license in the summer of 1997 and was forced to close permanently on June 23 of that year. The building was torn down in 2002 and replaced by condominiums, but its operation had significantly driven a boom in dance music throughout the UK. "It lost its liquor license in the summer of 1997, which forced it to close forever on June 23rd of that year." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0FYq5MBdHw4
**Ground Zero for Alt Dance** : The Hacienda was considered "ground zero for all dance" in the UK, not only hosting performances but, more importantly, elevating the role of the DJ as a star. Its influence was integral to the explosion of dance culture across the UK, contributing to movements like Madchester and influencing artists such as The Prodigy and The Chemical Brothers. "But the Hacienda was ground zero for all dance." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0FYq5MBdHw4
**Infamous Manchester Club** : The Hacienda was an infamous club in Manchester, co-owned by New Order and Factory Records. Initially a "miserable failure," it transformed into "the center of the universe" for UK club culture once the rave scene took off, though it later succumbed to issues with drugs, gangs, and deaths. "He was a DJ from Manchester, who often man the turntables at the Haseyenda, the infamous club owned by New Order and Factory Records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**New Order's Dance Club (Manchester 1982)** : The Hacienda was a dance club opened by New Order on Whitworth Street West in Manchester on May 21, 1982. Despite being a major financial drain for the band, who lost millions of pounds due to its high costs and poor management, it became a pivotal site for alt dance.
