# concert - Spike Island (May 27th, 1990)

## curiosities
**Legendary Despite Performance Flaws** : This outdoor gig by The Stone Roses, held on a reclaimed chemical dump on a peninsula by the River Mersey (where attendees claimed to get "chemical burn"), attracted their largest crowd of 28,000 to 30,000 people. Despite being dubbed "the gig of the year," the band's performance was widely considered average or below average due to nervousness, tentativeness, and a suspect sound system. Its legendary status is largely attributed to its immense attendance, its significance as a cultural "meeting of the minds" between the Madchester and rave cultures (facilitated by ecstasy), and the extensive hype generated by the British music press, which proclaimed "the 90s begin now."
