## curiosities
**Government Attempt to Ban Raves (1994)** : By 1994, the UK's rave scene had grown so large and prominent that the government attempted to ban raves through a law called the Criminal Justice and Public Order Act. This act reflected official concern over the burgeoning dance culture. "The UK's scene got so big that by 1994, the government tried to ban raves with a law called the Criminal Justice and Public Order Act." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**Rise of UK Rave Culture (1988)** : Raves emerged as a new dance culture in the UK in 1988, coinciding with the "Summer of Love" and the widespread use of the drug Ecstasy. This culture shifted the focus from bands and songs to the DJ, who often remained anonymous, mixing and making people dance, becoming the performer and star. "This was called The Summer of Love. The beginnings of a new dance culture in the UK. It was rave culture." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
