**Censorship of The Rolling Stones (1967)** : During The Rolling Stones' 1967 performance, Ed Sullivan compelled the band to alter the lyrics of "Let's Spend the Night Together" to "Let's spend some time together," demonstrating mainstream television's censorship of explicit content. Mick Jagger's eye-rolling during the performance became a silent protest against this restriction. "Ed Sullivan made them change the words to let spend some time together, he wouldn't allow them to perform on his show unless they'd change the record." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 4 - Music of the 70s (Sexual) Revolution | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-4-talkin-bout-a-sexual-revolution

**Premier Television Variety Program (1956)** : In 1956, "The Ed Sullivan Show" on CBS was the most popular variety program on television, regularly drawing tens of millions of viewers. Ed Sullivan was renowned for his astute ability to identify and book popular acts, famously securing Elvis Presley for three appearances. "In 1956, the biggest variety program on television was the Ed Sullivan show on CBS.
