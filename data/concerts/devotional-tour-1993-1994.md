# concert - Devotional Tour (1993-1994)

## curiosities
**Extreme Decadence and Excess** : The Devotional Tour, a world tour spanning 1993 and 1994, was characterized by "unbelievably decadent" excesses. It featured backstage VIP areas and hotel suites designed like porn movie sets, where parties fueled by liquid ecstasy (preferred over pills) could last up to three days, though the widespread intoxication made reliable accounts of events difficult. "This was the unbelievably decadent devotional tour. Item 13. Each night up to 20 of the most beautiful women in the audience were invited into special backstage VIP areas and hotel suites that were outfitted like the set of a porn movie." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3oF7iPRONnQ
**Multiple Incidents and Arrests** : The tour was fraught with incidents, including the arrests of Dave Gahan and touring keyboardist Darabomonte in Montreal after Gahan punched a hotel concierge (September 8, 1993), and Martin Gore's arrest in Denver for refusing to turn down his stereo (November 4, 1993). Dave Gahan also suffered a mid-show heart attack in New Orleans (October 8, 1993) and fell 12 feet off stage in Indianapolis, breaking ribs (July 1994). "Dave Cajon and Touring Keyboard as Darabomonte are arrested and jailed after Dave punched the concierge of a Montreal hotel in the mouth." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3oF7iPRONnQ
**Staffing and Health Issues** : The tour's 120-member staff included both a psychiatrist and, controversially, a drug dealer, reflecting the band's struggles with depression and addiction. The tour's grueling nature and lifestyle led to significant health crises among the band members, including Alan Wilder's kidney and gall stones, Andrew Fletcher's nervous breakdown, Martin Gore's grand mal seizure, and Dave Gahan's severe drug abuse, which caused extreme weight loss and violent outbursts. "This shrink was part of a 120 member staff that accompanied the band on the tour for the album Songs of Faith and Devotion." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3oF7iPRONnQ
