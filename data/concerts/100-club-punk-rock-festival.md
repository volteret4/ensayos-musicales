# concert - 100 Club Punk Rock Festival

## curiosities
**Britain's First Official Punk Festival (September 20-21, 1976)** : Located on Oxford Street in London, the 100 Club, previously known as a jazz venue, hosted Britain's first official punk rock festival over two nights. Featuring bands like the Sex Pistols, The Clash, and The Damned, the event drew lines around the block, though only about 600 people could enter. Despite mixed performance quality, it solidified a community and attracted record company and music press interest, making punk rock "no longer a secret" in the UK. "This was the site of Britain's first official punk rock festival." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=huAvyInzz3Q
**First British Punk Festival (1976)** : This festival, held over two nights on September 20th and 21st, 1976, at the 100 Club (a former jazz hangout at 100 Oxford Street), is considered the first ever British punk rock festival. Hundreds of people crammed into the basement venue, while many more were left outside. The lineup included The Clash, Susie in the Band Sheets (making their debut), The Buzzcocks, and headliners The Sex Pistols. "And so it came to pass that an event was held at a tiny basement venue at 100 Oxford Street called, appropriately, the 100 Club." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wYuNolSDLFM
**Sid Vicious Incidents (1976)** : The festival was infamous for incidents involving Sid Vicious, who was then the drummer for Susie in the Band Sheets. On the first night, he "savagely beat up" a well-known music journalist, allegedly involving a bicycle chain. On the second night, he was arrested for throwing a beer glass that hit a girl in the face and blinded her in one eye. "He hit a girl in the face and blinded her in one eye." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wYuNolSDLFM
