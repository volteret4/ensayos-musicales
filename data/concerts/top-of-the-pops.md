# concert - Top of the Pops

## curiosities
**Blur's "Country House" Performance** : After winning the "Battle of Britpop" singles chart war, Blur appeared on "Top of the Pops" to perform their number one single "Country House." During this appearance, Blur's bassist Alex James notably wore a torn-up Oasis t-shirt. "They claimed the number one spot and got to appear on top of the pops. Base player Alex James, worn a torn up Oasis t-shirt for the event." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zUr9sjWgUcw
**David Bowie and The Spiders from Mars Performance (July 6, 1972)** : This BBC broadcast on July 6, 1972, featuring David Bowie and The Spiders from Mars, was a pivotal moment for glam rock and sexual identity. Following Dr. Hook and the Medicine Show, Bowie's theatrical appearance (quilted jumpsuit, red boxing boots, red-orange hair, anisocoria) and public affection with Mick Ronson (an arm draped around his shoulders) shocked and inspired viewers, leading to the immediate emergence of "Ziggy Clones" and offering a sense of affirmation for young people questioning their sexuality. "One especially interesting performance was on top of the pops on the BBC, July 6th, 1972." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw
**Glam Rock Breakthrough (1971)** : This hugely important British TV show featured T-Rex in March 1971, where Marc Bolan appeared in satin clothes and covered with glitter dust. This performance was observed by David Bowie, who considered it "brilliant" and a catalyst for his own embrace of glam rock. "In March 1971, T-Rex appeared on top of the pops, the hugely important British TV show." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sPCrATqBdeA
**Mark Bolan's Glitter Appearance (March 1971)** : In March 1971, Mark Bolan of T-Rex appeared on the hugely important British TV show, Top of the Pops. His performance, where he wore satin clothes covered with glitter dust, profoundly inspired David Bowie and is credited as the beginning of Glam Rock. "In March 1971, T-Rex appeared on top of the pops, the hugely important British TV show, and he was wearing satin clothes covered with glitter dust." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P3xjW0A57Wk
**Purpose of Early Music Videos** : Before the advent of MTV, music videos were primarily produced to be sent to TV shows like "Top of the Pops," allowing artists to promote their music without having to appear in person. This also offered artists greater control over their public image than live television appearances. "The main purpose was to send them to TV shows like Top of the Pops so that the artist didn't have to go in person." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2FyAZGUI_mk
**Street Band's Performance of "Toast"** : Street Band performed their hit song "Toast" live on the weekly British chart show "Top of the Pops." "They even performed the song live on the weekly British chart show Top of the Pops." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zUQ2vZzmRiI
**The Four Tops Miss Flight (1988)** : The R&B group The Four Tops had seats booked on Pan Am Flight 103 on December 21, 1988, but missed the fateful flight due to oversleeping. They had performed on BBC's Top of the Pops the night before, leading to them missing their flight by minutes and inadvertently escaping the Lockerbie bombing. "The four tops, the R&B group, had seats but they overslept after performing on BBC's top of the pops the night before." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hmnMG6f80Sg
