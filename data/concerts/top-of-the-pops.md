**David Bowie Performance (1972)** : David Bowie's performance of "Starman" on Top of the Pops in 1972, following its success as his first hit since "Space Oddity," featured him with short-cropped hair (resembling an 80s mullet) and a multicolored jumpsuit. Bowie's flirtatious interaction with guitarist Mick Ronson and his stage presence shocked parents while thrilling children. **David Bowie and The Spiders from Mars Performance (July 6, 1972)** : This BBC broadcast on July 6, 1972, featuring David Bowie and The Spiders from Mars, was a pivotal moment for glam rock and sexual identity. Following Dr. Hook and the Medicine Show, Bowie's theatrical appearance (quilted jumpsuit, red boxing boots, red-orange hair, anisocoria) and public affection with Mick Ronson (an arm draped around his shoulders) shocked and inspired viewers, leading to the immediate emergence of "Ziggy Clones" and offering a sense of affirmation for young people questioning their sexuality. "One especially interesting performance was on top of the pops on the BBC, July 6th, 1972." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw ← top-of-the-pops ← top-of-the-pops
**Glam Rock Breakthrough (1971)** : This hugely important British TV show featured T-Rex in March 1971, where Marc Bolan appeared in satin clothes and covered with glitter dust. This performance was observed by David Bowie, who considered it "brilliant" and a catalyst for his own embrace of glam rock. **Madonna's Performance in London (1985)** : About three months after her scandalous 1984 MTV Music Awards performance, Madonna was specifically invited to London to recreate the exact same act on "Top of the Pops." This invitation highlighted the differing cultural attitudes between the US and Europe in the 1980s, where such provocations were received not as scandal but as "fresh air" and a symbol of freedom. **Mark Bolan's Glitter Appearance (March 1971)** : In March 1971, Mark Bolan of T-Rex appeared on the hugely important British TV show, Top of the Pops. His performance, where he wore satin clothes covered with glitter dust, profoundly inspired David Bowie and is credited as the beginning of Glam Rock. **Purpose of Early Music Videos** : Before the advent of MTV, music videos were primarily produced to be sent to TV shows like "Top of the Pops," allowing artists to promote their music without having to appear in person. This also offered artists greater control over their public image than live television appearances. "The main purpose was to send them to TV shows like Top of the Pops so that the artist didn't have to go in person." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2FyAZGUI_mk ← top-of-the-pops ← top-of-the-pops

**The Four Tops Miss Flight (1988)** : The R&B group The Four Tops had seats booked on Pan Am Flight 103 on December 21, 1988, but missed the fateful flight due to oversleeping. They had performed on BBC's Top of the Pops the night before, leading to them missing their flight by minutes and inadvertently escaping the Lockerbie bombing. "The four tops, the R&B group, had seats but they overslept after performing on BBC's top of the pops the night before." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hmnMG6f80Sg ← top-of-the-pops
