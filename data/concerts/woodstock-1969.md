# concert - Woodstock 1969

## curiosities
**Bill Hanley's Sound System (1969)** : Bill Hanley, often called the "Father of Festival Sound," was responsible for designing, building, and operating the entire sound system for the massive Woodstock concert in August 1969. Drawing on his experience from political rallies, he ensured that attendees could hear the performances and also engineered the audio recording for the Woodstock movie soundtrack, for which he received an Academy Award nomination, despite earning "almost no money." "When it was announced that there was going to be a massive concert in Woodstock, New York in August 1969, organizers called Hanley." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qrrwqdpCjpQ
**Film and Soundtrack for Promoter Salvation** : The movie based on Woodstock from 1969, along with its subsequent soundtrack releases, was instrumental in saving the promoters of the festival from financial ruin. The commercial success of the film and albums helped offset the significant costs and potential losses from the event itself. "For example, the movie based on Woodstock from 1969 and its subsequent soundtrack releases are the things that saved the promoters from losing their shirts." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM
