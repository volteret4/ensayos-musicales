# concert - Lesser Free Trade Hall

## curiosities
**Sex Pistols' Influential Gig (1976)** : On Friday, June 4, 1976, after 7:30 PM, the Sex Pistols performed a seminal, albeit poorly attended (fewer than 50 people at 50 pence a ticket), concert at the Lesser Free Trade Hall in Manchester. This seemingly minor, money-losing event became a crucial turning point for English rock, inspiring many in the audience, including future members of the Buzzcocks, Pet Shop Boys, Joy Division, and New Order, to embrace punk rock. "Just after 730 on the night of Friday, June 4, 1976, even though the tickets were just 50 pence, less than 50 people showed up, but those who were there were inspired to do whatever was the sex pistols were doing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1y2UuU-me_s
