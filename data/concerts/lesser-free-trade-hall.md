# concert - Lesser Free Trade Hall

## curiosities
**Seminal Sex Pistols Gig (Manchester)** : This venue hosted the Sex Pistols' first gig in Manchester, an event described as "so important to British punk rock culture." It was promoted by Howard Devoto and Pete Shelley, the principals of The Buzzcocks. "If you haven't seen the movie, rent it now. You will see why this concert was so important to British punk rock culture." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wYuNolSDLFM
**Sex Pistols' Influential Gig (1976)** : On Friday, June 4, 1976, after 7:30 PM, the Sex Pistols performed a seminal, albeit poorly attended (fewer than 50 people at 50 pence a ticket), concert at the Lesser Free Trade Hall in Manchester. This seemingly minor, money-losing event became a crucial turning point for English rock, inspiring many in the audience, including future members of the Buzzcocks, Pet Shop Boys, Joy Division, and New Order, to embrace punk rock. "Just after 730 on the night of Friday, June 4, 1976, even though the tickets were just 50 pence, less than 50 people showed up, but those who were there were inspired to do whatever was the sex pistols were doing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1y2UuU-me_s
**Sex Pistols' Influential Gig (Mid-1970s)** : In the mid-1970s, the Sex Pistols played a famously small yet immensely influential show at the Lesser Free Trade Hall in Manchester. This event is widely credited with inspiring many attendees to form their own bands, directly contributing to the birth of the Manchester punk and post-punk scenes and the founding of Factory Records. "The Sex Pistols play the lesser free trade hall, that famous show." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dZStbEe4Gkk
