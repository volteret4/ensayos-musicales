## curiosities
**Toronto Recovery Concert (2003)** : SARS-Stock was a massive outdoor concert held in Toronto on July 30, 2003, at Downsview Park, an airport site. The event was conceived to revive tourism and morale in Toronto, which had been severely impacted by the SARS outbreak—the hardest-hit city outside of Asia, with travel advisories and concert cancellations. Headlined by The Rolling Stones, with Dan Aykroyd hosting, and featuring acts like The Tea Party, Flaming Lips, Rush, and AC/DC, the concert sold 150,000 Canadian tickets in one day at $21.50 each. It drew approximately 500,000 attendees and was broadcast live by CBC and MuchMoreMusic, successfully projecting a message that Toronto was recovering.
