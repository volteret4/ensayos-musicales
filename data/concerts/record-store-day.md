# concert - Record Store Day

## curiosities
**Expansion and Impact** : The success of Record Store Day led to major retailers like Best Buy, Target, Walmart, and Urban Outfitters beginning to stock vinyl as a defensive strategy against plummeting CD sales caused by piracy. Since its inception, vinyl sales have seen double-digit growth year after year. The event is now supported by around 1500 stores globally, with backing from prominent artists such as the Foo Fighters, Jack White, Paul McCartney, Billie Eilish, and Taylor Swift. A Black Friday edition was later introduced, and during the pandemic, multiple Record Store Days were organized to help sustain independent record stores. "In fact, it was so encouraging that other retailers like Best Buy, Target, Walmart, and Urban Outfitters started stocking vinyl as a hedge against declining CD sales caused by music piracy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nX8uAFDzDYM
**Flexi-Disc Availability** : Flexi-discs are periodically made available or released during Record Store Day events, serving as a special attraction for collectors of unique physical music formats. "They sometimes show up on record store day." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B2g4QSREzpw
**Metallica's Endorsement (2008)** : A significant endorsement for the inaugural Record Store Day came from Metallica, who hosted an autograph session at Rasputin Music in San Francisco on April 19, 2008. Fans waited up to three days to meet the band, demonstrating the event's immediate appeal and legitimacy. "The biggest endorsement happened at a San Francisco record store called Respeutian Music, when Metallica showed up for an autograph session." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nX8uAFDzDYM
**Origin and Purpose (2008)** : Record Store Day was conceived in 2007 by a group of independent record store owners in Baltimore, who were struggling with declining sales due to digital music. Inspired by "Free Comic Book Day," they sought an initiative to draw people back to stores to buy records. The first event was held on April 19, 2008, with approximately 200 participating stores, and its positive reception was highly encouraging. "Thus was born record store day, an annual celebration of vinyl and the stores that sell it. It's held on the third Saturday in April. The first record store day was on April 19th, 2008, and about 200 stores participated." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nX8uAFDzDYM
