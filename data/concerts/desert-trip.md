**"Coachella for Old People" (2016)** : Desert Trip, also known as "Coachella for Old People," was a major festival held over two weekends in October 2016, where hundreds of thousands of attendees paid up to $1,500 US dollars each. The lineup featured legendary artists including The Rolling Stones, Neil Young, Paul McCartney, Roger Waters, Bob Dylan, and The Who. The event grossed $160 million, nearly double that year's Coachella, highlighting the significant financial power and interest in heritage acts.
