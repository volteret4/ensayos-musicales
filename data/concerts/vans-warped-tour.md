# concert - Vans Warped Tour

## curiosities
**"Punk Rock Summer Boot Camp"** : Founder Kevin Lyman frequently referred to the Warped Tour as his "punk rock summer boot camp," due to the demanding nature, low pay, grueling pace, tough crowds, and lack of luxuries for participants. "The Wonder Kevin Lyman often called Warped his punk rock summer boot camp." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Average Daily Bands** : On average, regardless of the location, each Warped Tour stop would feature at least 90 bands performing per day. "And on average, no matter where the show stops, there are at least 90 bands a day." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Award-Winning Blood Drive** : The Warped Tour's blood drive was recognized as the "independent blood drive of the year" for six consecutive years. "And there were six years in a row that Warped Tour's blood drive was named independent blood drive of the year." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Bare Bones Stage Sets** : Warped Tour stage sets were generally very basic, with Avenged Sevenfold being notable as the first band to introduce any kind of prop, a smoke machine. "Warped stage sets tend to be very bare bones. The first band to use any kind of prop on stage was a Venge 7 Fold. And the prop they brought with them? Smoke Machine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Content Pivot to Punk and Skate Culture (1996)** : With the new Vans sponsorship, the festival's content shifted from broad-range alternative music to a focus on punk and other genres that resonated with skate culture. "His new sponsorship also meant a content pivot. Kevin moved the festival from broad range alternative to focusing on punk and other music that went well with skate culture." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Cultural Institution and Rite of Passage** : Over its 24-year run, the Warped Tour evolved into a significant cultural institution and a rite of passage for both the fans who attended and the bands that performed, with some finding their careers launched on the tour. "In the process, this thing has become a cultural institution and a right of passage, not only for fans who attended, but the bands who participated." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Early Arrival of Attendees** : Kevin Lyman noted that, on average, 90% of Warped Tour attendees would arrive as soon as the gates opened. "Kevin Lyman says that on average, 90% of the attendees show up as soon as the gates open." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**End of Touring (2018)** : The Vans Warped Tour concluded its 24-year run in 2018. Founder Kevin Lyman decided to end it due to the personal sacrifice of dedicating every summer to the tour (allowing only two weeks off a year) and the changing economics of the music business since its 1995 inception. "2018 was the last year for the band's Warped Tour. After 24 years, founder Kevin Lyman had had enough sacrificing all those summers to a single cause." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Expansion to Multiple Stages (1997)** : The Warped Tour expanded its setup, featuring a main stage, a side stage, and a "locals only" stage, starting around the third tour. It also allowed bands to join for a few shows and then depart. "For the third tour in 1996, Warp expanded to a main stage, a side stage, and a stage marked locals only. And also, bands were allowed to come in, play for a couple of shows, and then drop off the tour." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Extensive Volunteer Support** : The tour heavily relied on thousands of volunteers and interns for its operations and philanthropic initiatives. In 2012 alone, 16,400 volunteers contributed to the Warped Tour. "None of that would be possible. And many of the other things Warped has done at all the stops along the tour without the help of thousands of volunteers and interns. I found this number for 2012. That year, 16,400 volunteers helped with warped." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**First Tour Details (1995)** : The inaugural Warped Tour took place in 1995, with the first date on August 4 in Salt Lake City. It comprised 25 dates, including stops in Toronto and Vancouver, Canada, featuring about 20 up-and-coming alternative bands on two stages. "And in 1995, the first Warped Tour went ahead. The first date was August 4 in Salt Lake City. There were 25 dates, including two in Canada, Toronto and Vancouver." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Founder Kevin Lyman** : The Warped Tour was founded by Kevin Lyman, who conceived the idea while working as a stage manager for Lollapalooza in 1991, aiming to combine music with extreme sports like skateboarding. "The founder of Warped is Kevin Lyman and for a guy who has brought so much music to so many people, we really don't know that much about him." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Free Entry for Parents (Late Tour Life)** : In its later years, the Warped Tour implemented a policy allowing parents of young attendees to enter for free. Despite an estimated loss of $1.4 million in ticket revenue (800 parents per show at $40 a ticket), this strategy led to an increased number of kids attending, ultimately balancing out the revenue. "Late in the tour's life, Warp started allowing parents of young kids to come to the show for free. And on average, Warped welcomed 800 parents per show." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Initial Financial Losses (1995)** : The first Warped Tour in 1995 (then just "Warped Tour") resulted in financial losses for Kevin Lyman, who couldn't pay everyone, leading him to seek a title sponsor for future iterations. "Kevin realized that if the festival were to continue, he would need a sponsor. He lost money and couldn't pay everyone that first time out, and things were pretty grim." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Initial Name "The Bomb" (1995)** : Kevin Lyman initially intended to name his new tour "The Bomb." However, on the very day of its announcement, April 19, 1995, the Oklahoma City bombing occurred, leading him to change the name to "Warped." "His would be called the bomb. Great name. However, on the very day, Kevin was going to announce his new venture, his very first tour. This would be April the 19th, 1995. Timothy McVey blew up the federal building in Oklahoma City." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Longest Running Touring Music Festival (1995-2018)** : The Vans Warped Tour began in 1995 and continued annually until the summer of 2018, making it the longest-running touring music festival. "The Vans Warp Tour began in 1995 and ran every year until the summer of 2018." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Massive Catering Operation** : Feeding the Warped Tour staff and artists was compared to feeding an army. Tadah Catering, led by Shelley Lynn, served over 1,300 meals daily (more than 82,000 annually), including vast quantities of potatoes, coffee, and beer. The nightly Warped Barbecue served 300 hot dogs, 300 hamburgers, and 100 veggie burgers, with 90% of artists and crews reportedly eating better on tour than at home. "feeding every Warped Tour is like feeding an army. A woman by the name of Shelley Lynn owns a company called Tadah Catering. She was with the tour from the very beginning. Tadah served more than 1,300 meals a day or more than 82,000 in a typical year." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Merger with Ozzfest (1998)** : In 1998, the Warped Tour temporarily merged with Ozzfest for a joint event titled "Skaten for Satan," attracting a unique crowd. "In 1998, the Warped Tour emerged with Oz Fest for something that was called Skaten for Satan." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Multiple Stages (Up to Nine)** : In some years, the Warped Tour featured as many as nine stages, in addition to specialized tents like an acoustic tent and a comedy tent. "Some years, the festival has had as many as nine stages, plus an acoustic tint and a comedy tint." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Number of Acts (1997)** : The 1997 Warped Tour featured a total of 97 different acts. "As far as the record show, a total of 97 different acts participated in the 97 tour." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Philanthropic Efforts** : The Warped Tour was known for its significant philanthropic component, supporting various charities and causes. It collected over 300,000 pounds of food per tour for organizations like Feed Our Children Now, raised hundreds of thousands of dollars for MusiCares (assisting musicians with health insurance), and supported mental health (Hope For The Day, You Hear Me), voter registration (HeadCount), anti-opioid abuse (FEND), victims of sexual abuse (A Voice for the Innocent), breast cancer awareness, and environmental groups. "There has always been a big philanthropic part of Warped. The tour has supported a lot of charities over the year, like feed our children now, and it wasn't unusual for more than 300,000 pounds of food to be collected over the course of one tour." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Rotating Lineup** : The Warped Tour's lineup changed from city to city, a deliberate strategy to prevent fans from planning their day around a fixed performance schedule. "The lineup changes from city to city. That way fans can't plan their day around a set schedule for performances." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**The Barbecue Band Concept** : The "Barbecue Band" was a unique Warped Tour initiative where smaller, breakthrough-seeking bands would agree to prepare the post-show dinner for other performers and the crew. In exchange, they earned a slot to play on the tour. Past "Barbecue Bands" included Blink-182, Katy Perry, and My Chemical Romance. "There's something called the Barbecue Band. This is the group that agrees to prepare the post-show dinner for other performers and the crew in exchange for being able to play on the tour themselves." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Ticket Sales Breakpoint** : Kevin Lyman observed that the critical financial breakpoint for ticket sales on any given Warped Tour often came down to the last 10 days of the tour, making it vulnerable to unpredictable factors like bad weather. "Kevin hasn't figured out that when it comes to ticket sales, the maker breakpoint of any band's Warped Tour comes down to the last 10 days of the tour." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Unknown Total Number of Bands** : No one knows the exact total number of bands that have performed on the Warped Tour since its inception in 1995, as groups varied in their participation, from full tours to single gigs. In any given year, over 250 names would appear on the bill. "As far as anybody can tell, no one knows exactly how many bands have performed as part of the Warped Tour since it started in 1995." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Vans Sponsorship (1996)** : In 1996, Vans, a skateboard and shoe company, signed a $300,000 deal to become the title sponsor of the tour, officially renaming it the "Vans Warped Tour" from that year forward. This happened after a blizzard prevented a potential deal with Calvin Klein. "And before anything could be rescheduled, Vans, the skateboard company, the shoe company, stepped in during that 24 hour delay and signed a deal for $300,000. So from 1996 forward, it was officially the Vans Warped Tour." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
