# concert - Mundoq Coronation Ball

## curiosities
**First Major Rock Concert and Ticketing Disaster (1952)** : The Mundoq Coronation Ball, held on Friday, March 21, 1952, at the Cleveland Arena, is recognized as the first major rock concert. Produced by local radio DJ Alan Freed, tickets were priced at $1.15 in advance or $1.75 at the door (equivalent to $17-20 today), with no service charges. "The first major rock concert happened on Friday, March 21, 1952 at the Cleveland Arena." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OCkov6FTliE
**Overselling, Corruption, and Riot** : The concert was a catastrophic sellout, with 20,000 people attempting to enter a venue designed for 10,000, leaving 6,000 outside. Problems included more tickets being printed than capacity allowed, counterfeit tickets, and box office staff taking bribes to supply scalpers. The event culminated in teenagers storming the doors, leading to police intervention and the show being shut down before completion. "Twenty thousand people in a building designed for ten thousand and about six thousand kids were left outside screaming that they couldn't buy tickets at the door." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OCkov6FTliE
