# concert - Tibetan Freedom Festival

## curiosities
**Adam Yauch's Vision** : The concept for the Tibetan Freedom Festival originated from Adam Yauch's desire for the Beastie Boys to do more to raise awareness about Chinese oppression in Tibet, envisioning a large-scale event featuring major musical acts. "Adam Yock was the guy who really started it. He felt that the Beasties could somehow do more to raise awareness about what was going on. And that's when he came up with the idea of a Tibetan Freedom Festival, a large gig featuring some major names." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9_BTe15NooI
**Inaugural Event (1996)** : The first annual Tibetan Freedom Show was held on July 15, 1996, at Golden Gate Park in San Francisco. It attracted over 100,000 attendees, each paying $30, with all profits directed to the pro-Tibet Milarepa Fund. The lineup featured the Beastie Boys, Smashing Pumpkins, Bjork, Sonic Youth, and the Chili Peppers, among others. "More than 100,000 people paid 30 bucks a pop to the first annual Tibetan Freedom Show at Golden Gate Park in San Francisco on July 15, 1996." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9_BTe15NooI
**Inaugural Festival Organized by Beastie Boys** : Rage Against the Machine supported and performed at the "first Tibetan Freedom Festival," an event organized by the Beastie Boys. This festival was one of many causes Rage championed as part of their political activism. "included the first Tibetan Freedom Festival, which was organized by the Beastie Boys." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t2uXHP9cBdM
**Subsequent Festivals (1997, 1998)** : Following the success of the first event, a two-day festival was held in New York in 1997, which was also a big success. Another two-day event took place in Washington, D.C., in 1998, continuing the mission of raising awareness for Tibet. "A year later, a two-day event was held in New York, another big success. And after that in 1998, there was another two-day event in Washington, D.C." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9_BTe15NooI
