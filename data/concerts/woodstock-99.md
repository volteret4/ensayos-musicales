**Escalation to Violence and Arson (1999)** : The extreme frustration among the 200,000 attendees at Woodstock 99 rapidly escalated into hatred and then widespread violence, especially as the weekend progressed. The mob mentality grew, culminating in fires started with candles distributed by a peace group and exacerbated by the Red Hot Chili Peppers' performance. Vendor booths were looted and burned, bonfires made from water bottles, plywood perimeter walls torched, ATMs savaged, merchandise areas raided and burned, and even a speaker tower was set alight and toppled. **Site Selection and Conditions (1999)** : The 30th-anniversary Woodstock concert, held on July 23-25, 1999, in Rome, New York, took place at an old air force base that had been controversially declared a hazardous waste site. The location featured miles of hot concrete and asphalt under high 30s temperatures, with a distinct lack of trees or shade. Its selection was primarily due to its "defensible perimeter" to prevent gate crashers, despite the unsuitable environmental conditions. "Why was the show held there in the first place?
