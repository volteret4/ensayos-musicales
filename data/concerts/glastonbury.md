# concert - Glastonbury

## curiosities
**1990s Gold Standard** : By the 1990s, Glastonbury was considered the "gold standard" by which all other music festivals were judged. "By the 90s, that festival was the gold standard by which all others were judged." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Booking Indie Rock Bands (Early 2000s)** : Glastonbury, a prominent UK festival, played a significant role in the indie rock revival of the early 2000s by featuring bands from this movement. Its booking decisions helped propel these artists from underground notoriety to wider public recognition, demonstrating the genre's expanding influence. "Festivals like Coachella in America and Glastonbury in the UK started booking these bands." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xid8CZf4oYo
**Cultural Significance and Attendance** : Glastonbury has become a "right of passage" for music fans, attracting around 175,000 people regardless of weather conditions. Tickets sell out in less than an hour, months before any acts are announced, and performances there can make or break careers. "Glastonbury has become a right of passage for generations of music fans. Each event attracts somewhere in the neighborhood of 175,000 people, regardless of the heat or the cold or the rain or the mud." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Founding and History** : Glastonbury, founded by Michael Evis after seeing Led Zeppelin play outdoors, was first staged in the fall of 1970. It drew on an older Glastonbury tradition of classical music and arts events (1914-1926). Evis initially called it the Piltdown Festival, but that name lasted only one year. "Glastonbury's founder is Michael Evis, who decided to get into the festival business after seeing Led Zeppelin play outdoors." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Largest Greenfield Festival** : Glastonbury is known as the largest annual Greenfield Festival in the world, meaning people gather in a field, specifically Worthy Farm (a working dairy farm), for five days almost every June. It has been held annually since 1981, with "fallow years" every half-decade to allow the land and local residents to recover from the crowds. "It is known as the largest annual Greenfield Festival in the world, and this simply means that people come to a field, and in this case it's worthy farm, which is a working dairy farm, for five days just about every June." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Planned Return in 2021** : The Glastonbury festival announced its intention to return in June 2021, provided vaccine distribution and efficacy are successful. "Glastonbury says it'll be back in June 2021." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nYPGQiDL5bw
