# concert - Edge Fest

## curiosities
**All-Canadian Origins (1987)** : Edge Fest began in 1987 as an all-Canadian event, despite concerns that no one would attend a show consisting almost entirely of Canadian rock bands. The first year surprisingly drew 25,000 people, marking a significant turning point in public acceptance of homegrown music. "Instead, that first year, 25,000 people were there." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6PGgNCaW4xo
**Canadian Traveling Festival** : Edge Fest initially started as a Toronto-only event but expanded to become a national tour in Canada during 1997, 1998, and 1999. It emerged during a period of financial growth in the music industry, inspired by Lollapalooza's success. "There was Edge Fest, which moved from being a Toronto only thing to a national tour in 1997, 1998, and 1999." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Catalyst for USS's Publishing Deal** : USS's performance at Edge Fest caught the attention of a representative from the publishing company Sony ATV, who subsequently signed the band and connected them with producer Tog Salter, initiating a major creative shift. "When we played Edge Fest, there was someone from the publishing company, Sony ATV, was at that show." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**Growth and Internationalization (1990s)** : Throughout the 1990s, Edge Fest's attendance routinely surpassed 35,000 at its biggest shows, with virtually every gig selling out. International acts like Foo Fighters, Blur, and Green Day were added, but the event remained primarily Canadian. In one summer, there were three separate Edge Fests, and it expanded into a national tour with nine stops in 1997, and eight stops in both 1998 and 1999. "From then on, attendance was routinely over 35,000 of the biggest shows. Virtually every one of these gigs sold out, and one summer, there were three Edge Fest." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6PGgNCaW4xo
**Hip Participation** : The Tragically Hip was noted for their participation in festivals like Edge Fest during their "golden ears" period of success. "festivals like Edge Fest." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Showcase for Canadian Talent** : Edge Fest provided a crucial platform for numerous Canadian acts, including Our Lady Peace, The Tea Party, Blue Rodeo, Crash Test Dummies, Sons of Freedom, Real Statics, The Tragically Hip, Headstones, and Big Sugar, among many others. It became a clear indicator of the robust domestic support for Canadian rock music.
