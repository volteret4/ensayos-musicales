# concert - Lollapalooza

## curiosities
**Cultural Phenomenon (Early 1990s)** : In January 1993, Lollapalooza was recognized as a significant cultural "thing," alongside the rising grunge movement. Its popularity influenced radio station management to pursue an alternative format. "Lola Palusa was a thing and thought, yeah, this is maybe something that we can work with." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bI6TTA-fn7c
**Current Static Format** : In contemporary times, Lollapalooza has transformed from a touring festival into a static event held annually in Chicago every August. "Lala Palusa is a static festival held in Chicago every August." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Evolution from Alt-Rock to Pop/Hip-Hop** : Originally conceived in 1990 as a touring festival showcasing "left-of-center and alternative bands" like Red Hot Chili Peppers, Soundgarden, Rage Against The Machine, Pearl Jam, Tool, Green Day, Metallica, Hole, Smashing Pumpkin, Speedy Boy, Sonic Youth, and Remones, Lollapalooza was an essential part of 90s music culture. After its original concept "ran out of steam" in the late 90s, it was reborn as a single weekend event in Chicago. However, its original alt-rock focus is now largely gone, with the festival primarily featuring pop and hip-hop acts, reflecting a cultural shift in America. "When La La Palosa was first conceived in 1990, it was a full-blown touring festival of Weirdos, featuring the best in left-of-center and alternative bands." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZzIqIydZTlA
**Expanded Format (1992)** : For its second year in 1992, Lollapalooza expanded significantly, incorporating multiple stages and a wider array of attractions. "And this time things were expanded across multiple stages and multiple attractions." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Festival for the Alternative Generation** : For several years in the 1990s, Lollapalooza was recognized as the premier touring music festival catering to the alternative generation. "And for the next couple of years, Lala Palusa was the touring music festival for the alternative generation." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Founded by Perry Farrell (1991)** : Lollapalooza was founded in 1991 by Perry Farrell, the singer for Jane's Addiction, as a multi-act bill to support what was intended to be Jane's Addiction's final tour. Its format was reminiscent of the old caravan tours from the 1950s. "Very fair, the singer for Jane's edition put together a multi-act bill to support what would be the last ever tour for Jane's edition." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Generation-Defining Event (1991-1995)** : Lollapalooza was a defining event for a generation during the 1991-1995 music era, which was characterized by a rapid influx of new sounds and scenes, including grunge, American indie, and gangster rap. "the entire Lola Paloose generation was gangster rap." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bI6TTA-fn7c
**Inspiration for Kevin Lyman** : Kevin Lyman, future founder of the Warped Tour, worked as a stage manager on the first Lollapalooza road trip in 1991. He was inspired by Jane's Addiction's performance of "Mountain Song" and observed both the strengths and weaknesses of Lollapalooza, particularly its booking of artists that were "too mainstream and not eclectic enough," before starting his own tour. "And it was while working with Jane's addiction as a stage manager on that first Lola Blues a road trip in 1991 that he started thinking of the new possibilities of package tours." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Moshing Goes Mainstream (1992)** : Footage from the 1992 Lollapalooza tour clearly demonstrated that moshing and crowd-surfing had become pervasive, with crowds consistently in motion and engaging in these behaviors. This festival tour marked moshing's widespread adoption into the mainstream alternative rock scene. "Just look at any footage of that year's Lullapalooza tour." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KMx4bmq8o2Y
**Origin in Arizona** : The first Lollapalooza show was staged in Arizona less than a year after the 1990 Reading Festival, directly inspired by Jane's Addiction's management team witnessing the crowd's energy during The Pixies' performance at Reading. "And less than a year later, the first Lollapaloo's show was staged in Arizona. And it all started with a live version of this song." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI

**Spawned Imitators** : The success of Lollapalooza inspired a wave of similar traveling music festivals in the 1990s, including Edgefest, Summersault, and Another Roadside Attraction.
