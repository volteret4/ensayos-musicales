## curiosities
**Challenges as a Woman in 1970s Rock** : Linda Ronstadt found it difficult to establish her place in rock music during the 1970s as a woman. She struggled with a lack of confidence, resorting to cocaine and speed, and sensed resentment from fans while opening for Neil Young. She also felt societal pressure to act sexy and utilized a male crew member as a "pseudo interpreter" when communicating with other men. "It was not easy for her to find her place in rock music as a woman in the 70s." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 47 - Dick Clark's Entertainment Empire | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Collaboration Attempt with Dolly Parton and Emmylou Harris (Mid-1970s)** : Following an impressive performance on Dolly Parton's TV show with Dolly and Emmylou Harris, the trio attempted to record an album together shortly after. However, it "didn't sound right" and faced contractual issues, delaying their successful "Trio" album until 1987. "No Dolly and Emmy Lou and Linda were as impressed as anyone with how they sounded and tried to make a record not long after that.
