## curiosities
**Gen Y Pop Preference** : The Backstreet Boys were cited as an example of the pop artists that Generation Y was into, reflecting the broader "pop phase" that characterized Gen Y's musical preferences in the late 1990s. "It was all spice girls and backstreet boys and puff daddy and on vogue." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Glamour in Pop Music Videos** : The Backstreet Boys were part of a trend in the mid to late 1990s where glamour returned to pop music, characterized by expensive videos. This marked a contrast to the anti-fashion ethos that emerged from the alt-rock revolution. "Glamour returned to pop music with expensive videos from Britney Spears, Backstreet Boys, and InSync." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7IDbqdIw3WU
**Pop Music Decline** : The Backstreet Boys were part of a group of pop artists from the mid-90s whose audience, by the mid-2000s, had grown older and began seeking angrier, more expressive music. This shift contributed to the rise of bands like Alexis on Fire. "But during that time, we were seeing the end of the spike in pop music, you know, the back street boys and instincts and Christine Aguilar is in Britney Spears and that group of people who had been into pop since the middle 90s with spice girls and whatever, they were now getting older and they were getting angry and they were looking for something that spoke to that anger and their fears and their frustrations." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nEPT8ZVZ0so
**Rise of Pop Music** : The Backstreet Boys were among the pop artists that defined the musical tastes of Generation Y towards the end of the 1990s, reflecting a shift away from brooding grunge and post-punk alt-rock. "This cheerful generation led us to an era of spice girls and backstreet boys in sync, Britney Spears." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Symbol of 90s Pop Dominance** : The Backstreet Boys were mentioned by Hugh Dillon as part of the pop music landscape in the 1990s that contributed to his sense of a "hell" world during his addiction, as he felt this music was eclipsing the rock music he valued. "I'm watching the backstreet boys and boys and it's like what f**king happened and I have no control." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zBd13KtYlAA
