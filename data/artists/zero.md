## songs
**Rhyne Stone (1997)** : This track was featured on Zero's four-track demo EP released in 1997. A portion of the song was later incorporated into "Forgotten" on Linkin Park's *Hybrid Theory* album. "The track is called Rhyne Stone." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YcdidjWaIqE
**Rine Stone (1997)** : This track is from Zero's 1997 4-track cassette, which was recorded in singer Mark Wakefield's bedroom. Zero eventually morphed into Linkin Park. "The track is called Rine Stone." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM

## curiosities
**Diverse Musical Influences** : The members of Zero had a wide array of musical tastes that influenced their sound, including bands like Alice in Chains, The Roots, and The Prodigy. "Everyone in the band was into different things. Allison Chains, the Roots, the Prodigy, you mix it all together and it all came out like this." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
**Founding and Early Recordings** : Zero was founded in LA by three high school friends: Mike Shinoda, Brad Delson, and Rob Borden, who, along with other members, began playing gigs and recording. They released a 4-track cassette in 1997, available in two versions with different covers, which are now highly sought after by collectors due to their rarity. These early recordings, including the track "Rine Stone," were made in singer Mark Wakefield's bedroom using a basic computer setup. "This was a group founded by three high school friends, Mike Shinoda, Brad Delson and Rob Borden." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
**Four-Track Demo EP (1997)** : Zero released a four-track demo EP in 1997 featuring original singer Mark Wakefield. Despite initially being unsellable, copies of this demo later appeared on eBay, fetching hundreds of dollars. "There was one known demo tape, a four track EP released in 1997, which featured a singer named Mark Wakefield." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YcdidjWaIqE
**Lack of Initial Record Label Interest** : Despite being a promising band and creating "okay stuff," Zero struggled to garner interest from record companies during their early years. "Some okay stuff, but it wasn't finding any love at the record company level." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
**Numerous Rejections** : Zero faced significant challenges in securing a record deal, reportedly receiving as many as 50 rejections for their original demos. This lack of success eventually led to the departure of their initial singer, Mark Wakefield. "There are lots of rejections of those original demos. Some say as many as 50 rejections, and in the end, Wakefield got the message or got bored or something and left." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YcdidjWaIqE
