## albums
**Amarok (1990) - Anti-Commercial Culmination** : An incredible and complex uninterrupted 60-minute piece, released in 1990, that encapsulates and culminates all the influences from Michael Field's initial creative period. This album features a blend of progressive rock, Celtic music, and romantic elements, presenting a monstrous fusion of influences, styles, and instruments that nonetheless sounds remarkably cohesive and solid. It was explicitly designed to be anti-commercial, outside of prevailing musical trends, and structured to prevent the extraction of any single, a deliberate move made in defiance of Virgin Records' pressure for a "Tubular Bells 2." **Ommadawn (1975) - Defining Soundscape** : Released in 1975, this album is identified as another major work by Michael Field and forms part of his "great trilogy" from the early stages of his career. It was during the creation of this album and the preceding works that Michael Field further developed his distinctive sound, exploring new instruments and incorporating diverse influences. **Tubular Bells (1973) - Revolutionary Debut** : This album, released in 1973, revolutionized the music industry. Its immense success was pivotal for the foundation and growth of Virgin Records, contributing significantly to Richard Branson's fortune as it was the breakthrough hit for his nascent record label, which had started in the early 1970s. ## curiosities
**"Great Trilogy" - Early Career Definition** : The most recognized and legendary period of Michael Field's career is considered to be his beginning, between the ages of 17 and 20, when he released a "great trilogy" of albums. This era, encompassing "Tubular Bells" (1973) and "Ommadawn" (1975), was crucial for him to progressively define his unique musical sound. "la parte más conocida o la parte más mítica de Michael Field es precisamente el inicio de su carrera cuando apenas tenía 17, 20 años, cuando saca esa gran trilogía." ← Music Radar Clan > Mike Oldfield - Amarok. ¿Es su mejor disco？ | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pOxKggnrgUw&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**Anti-Electronic and Digital Stance** : In the era of 1990, when "Amarok" was released, Michael Field was a staunch opponent of electronic instruments, electronic music, and digital production methods. This aversion dictated the album's production, which entirely utilized "old school" systems, a choice considered truly admirable given the technological trends of the time. "Michael Field en esa época era un grandísimo atacante de todos los instrumentos electrónicos y de la música electrónica, incluso era un gran atacante de la producción digital." ← Music Radar Clan > Mike Oldfield - Amarok. ¿Es su mejor disco？ | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pOxKggnrgUw&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**Defiance of Virgin Records with Amarok** : Michael Field created "Amarok" partly as an expression of his frustration and anger with Virgin Records, who had spent years pressuring him to produce a "Tubular Bells 2." Although he never entirely dismissed the idea of a sequel, he was profoundly dissatisfied with his relationship with Virgin Records, openly criticizing both Richard Branson and the label in various interviews, and believing Virgin's actions in the 1980s negatively impacted his discographic quality. "fue una hora hecha por Michael Field, y para la mala leche o el cabrero que tenía con Virging." ← Music Radar Clan > Mike Oldfield - Amarok. ¿Es su mejor disco？ | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pOxKggnrgUw&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**Early Start & Autodidacticism** : Michael Field began his musical career at the age of 15, entirely self-taught, forging his own path as a musician. **Influences in Early Career** : Across his first three albums, including "Tubular Bells" and "Ommadawn," Michael Field gradually shaped his sound by exploring and incorporating a wide array of new instruments and sonic textures. His influences during this period included distinct elements such as Celtic music and the rhythmic patterns of African drums. **Relationship with Richard Branson & Virgin Records** : Michael Field's relationship with Richard Branson and Virgin Records was notably contentious, described as being almost "at loggerheads." Despite this difficult dynamic, Virgin Records and Richard Branson owe a significant portion of their success and fortune to the immense commercial impact of Michael Field's "Tubular Bells." **Solo Recording and Virtuosity** : "Amarok" was recorded almost entirely by Michael Field alone, who played dozens of instruments masterfully and largely self-produced the album, with only minor punctual assistance from a producer. The resulting sound is astonishingly rich, giving the impression of an enormous band or even an orchestra of 30-50 musicians who have rigorously rehearsed for years, serving as a testament to his exceptional musical virtuosity.
