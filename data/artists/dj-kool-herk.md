**Apache and the Perfect Beat** : In his 2005 interview with Terry Gross, DJ Kool Herk recounted how his "merry go round" experiment took off with the song "Apache," often referred to as "The Incredible Bongo Rock." He noticed that people would specifically wait for this part of his set. The success of using "Apache" in this manner led other DJs to search for the "perfect beat," though, according to Herk, no one has ever truly beaten "that record" (Apache). **The Merry Go Round Technique** : DJ Kool Herk pioneered the "merry go round" technique, which involved using two turntables to extend the "break" sections of funk and soul records. He observed that dancers (B-boys) would wait for specific parts of records to perform their special moves. By extending these instrumental breaks, he allowed dancers more time to "do their thing" and showcase their best moves, which became known as breakdancing in the 1980s. He discussed this experiment in a 2005 interview with Terry Gross on NPR's Fresh Air.
