# artist - Sonic Youth

## members
- Kim Gordon (bass, guitar, vocals, songwriter)

## genres
- Alt-Rock
- Indie Rock
- No Wave

## albums
**Goo (1990) – First Major Label Album** : Co-produced by Butch Vig. Featured the song "Cool Thing," written by Kim Gordon.

## songs
**Cool Thing (1990)** : Featured on the album "Goo" (1990). Written by Kim Gordon after an encounter with LL Cool J, whose attitude towards women she disliked. Featured guest vocals from Chuck D of Public Enemy. Kim Gordon sang lead vocals and played bass on the track.

## curiosities
**Beneficiary of Alt-Rock Appetite** : Benefited from record labels looking for alt-rock beyond grunge after the "Grunge Gold Rush."
**Butch Vig Remix Production** : Butch Vig produced a remix record for the group.
**Collaboration on Mike Watt's Album (1995)** : Members of Sonic Youth contributed to Mike Watt's solo album "Ball-Hog or Tugboat," which also featured Dave Grohl.
**Democratic Structure** : Operated as a "democracy," with Kim Gordon not solely serving as the front person but having her turn as a songwriter and lead singer.
**Guitar Sound Refinement** : Over ten years, the band refined their guitar sound, becoming ranked as one of the most important American indie rock bands of the 1980s.
**Kim Gordon as an Icon** : Became an icon for a "certain group of women" for her role in a cool indie band, influencing many young women, including Courtney Love.
**Kim Gordon's Activism** : Kim Gordon wrote "Cool Thing" expressing a desire for liberation of "girls from male, white, corporate oppression."
**Kim Gordon's Early Life** : Kim Gordon was born in Rochester, New York, raised in Los Angeles, but formed her first band while a student at York University in Toronto.
**Kim Gordon's Other Creative Work** : Kim Gordon is also a visual artist (painting), former owner of a clothing line, has done acting, written books, directed music videos, and produced albums.
**No Wave Origins** : Emerged from the "weird New York City-based scene" known as No Wave.
**Video Co-production** : Kim Gordon co-produced the music video for The Breeders' song "Cannonball" (1993).
