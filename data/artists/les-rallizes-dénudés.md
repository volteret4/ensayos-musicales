## curiosities
**Band Formation and Longevity** : The name "Les Rallizes Dénudés" translates roughly to "The Naked Suitcases." This experimental rock band formed in Kyoto, Japan, in 1967 and continued through various lineups until 1996. "They were an experimental rock band formed in Kyoto, Japan in 1967 and survived through a number of combinations until 1996." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5vKSjpvVQa8
**Bassist's Airliner Hijacking (March 31, 1970)** : On March 31, 1970, bassist Moriyaki Wakabayashi was part of a crew that hijacked Japan Airlines Flight 351, a Boeing 727 on a domestic flight. Initially, they demanded to be diverted to Cuba, but settled for Pyongyang, North Korea, after realizing the plane lacked the range. "On March 31, 1970, bass player Moriyaki Wakabayashi was part of a crew that hijacked an airliner." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5vKSjpvVQa8
**Founder's Affiliation with Terrorist Group** : Takashi Mizutani, the band's founder and lead singer, was known to have an affiliation with the Japanese Red Army, a terrorist group. "The band's founder and lead singer was Takashi Musutani, who is known to have had an affiliation with a terrorist group called the Japanese Red Army." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5vKSjpvVQa8
**Hijacking Deception and Exile** : Authorities deceived the hijackers by flying them to Seoul, South Korea, and faking the airport to look like Pyongyang. The hijackers realized the trick when they heard American jazz music in the terminal but eventually negotiated passage to North Korea. Wakabayashi is believed to still reside in North Korea, though he has expressed remorse and a desire to return to Japan, even if it means jail. "But when the hijackers heard American jazz music playing in the terminal, they realized they've been tricked." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5vKSjpvVQa8
**Stigmatization and Secrecy** : Due to their bassist's crimes, the remaining members of Les Rallizes Dénudés were stigmatized. They lived under constant suspicion and maintained a secretive existence throughout their careers.
