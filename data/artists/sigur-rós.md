## albums
**( ) (2002) - Untitled Album** : Released in 2002, this album is considered a masterpiece and a significant musical reference. It consists of eight untitled songs, with the first four being more cheerful and the subsequent four sadder, separated by a 36-second silence meant to simulate the time required to flip a vinyl record. The physical edition is distinctive, featuring a blank onion paper booklet with all information presented in white, making it conceptually more complex than The Beatles' "White Album." The album incorporates Jónsi's invented language, "Bonnesca," and unique guitar sounds produced using cello bows. The Icelandic experimental string quartet Amina collaborated on its recording. It has been used extensively as a soundtrack for films, commercials, and awareness campaigns. **Babatikatiddo (Unknown Year) - Dance Soundtrack** : This is a 20-minute symphonic piece, considered a continuation or "second part" of the "( )" album. It was created in collaboration with Radiohead for a dance work by Merce Cunningham. Sigur Rós utilized many discarded sounds from the recording sessions of their untitled album for this project, releasing three songs for the soundtrack on a separate disc. This disc had very limited units and was not intended for commercial sale. The vinyl version is particularly interesting for featuring symbols resembling cave paintings or stick figures on its unrecorded side. **36-Second Silence on ( ) Album** : The "( )" album notably includes a 36-second silence that separates its two halves: four more cheerful songs followed by four sadder ones. This specific duration was chosen to represent the theoretical time it takes to pick up a vinyl record, flip it over, and place it back on the turntable. "están separadas por 36 segundos de silencio, que es el silencio que en teoría se tarda en coger el vinilo, dar la vuelta y volnerla a dar la alplay." ← Music Radar Clan > La musica en Islandia -  Sigur Ros () | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FPOVseXjEEo&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc

**Jónsi's Invented Language - Bonnesca** : Jónsi, the band's vocalist, created his own language called "Bonnesca" for many of their songs. This language is an alteration of Icelandic, where a consistent sound is inserted between each syllable, aiming to make the language more harmonious and musical, a technique that has yielded successful results and serves as a significant reference in the industry.
