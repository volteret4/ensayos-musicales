## curiosities
**Benchmark for Innovation** : His significant impact on guitar technique and technology in the late 1970s and early 1980s is used as a point of comparison for Matt Bellamy's influence in the 2000s. "What Eddie Van Halen was to the late 1970s and early 1980s, you could say that Matt is the equivalent to the 2000s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nVIswnZYFkU
**Constantly Modified Frankenstrat** : Eddie Van Halen's famous Frankenstrat guitar was subject to continuous modifications. He persistently altered the instrument in his relentless pursuit of the specific guitar tones he envisioned in his mind. "Think of Eddie Van Halen's Frankenstrat, which was always being modified as Eddie chased the guitar tones he heard in his head." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Frankenstein Guitar** : Eddie Van Halen's "Frankenstein" guitar is essentially a heavily modified Fender Stratocaster, showcasing his innovative approach to instrument customization. "and Eddie Van Halen's Frankenstein guitar, that's basically a heavily modified Stratocaster." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=E6cXyaHALBQ
**Left-Hand Fretboard Mastery (N/A)** : Eddie Van Halen is highlighted as a guitar hero who uses his left hand to execute intricate solos on the fretboard, demonstrating significant skill. **Pioneering Electric Guitar Techniques** : Eddie Van Halen is recognized for being among the first to perform innovative actions with an electric guitar, pushing the boundaries of what was thought possible with the instrument. His contributions were revolutionary and influential in the development of guitar playing. "Jimmy Hendrix and Eddie Van Halen were the first to do things with an electric guitar that no one else had." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m7MbV-eCLyE
