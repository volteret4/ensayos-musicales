## songs
**Gimme Shelter (1969)** : Mary Clayton performed a legendary guest vocal on this Rolling Stones track, recorded on November 2, 1969, at Sunset Sound in LA. She was called late at night by producer Jack Nitzsche while almost five months pregnant, initially refusing but persuaded by her husband. She arrived in her pajamas and rollers, was greeted by Keith Richards, and completed three takes. Her voice cracked powerfully on the word "murder," eliciting an impressed "woo" from Mick Jagger, creating one of the great guest vocals in history. "That has gone down in history as one of the great guest vocals on any record." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE ← mary-clayton

**Sweet Home Alabama (1974)** : Mary Clayton, an African-American woman from New Orleans and renowned backup singer, was asked to provide backup vocals for Lynyrd Skynyrd's "Sweet Home Alabama." Initially, she refused, associating Alabama with events like the 1963 16th Street Baptist Church bombing in Birmingham, the use of bullhorns and firehoses against Civil Rights activists, and George Wallace's segregationist inaugural address in 1963. However, at her husband's urging, she agreed to contribute, making it clear that her participation was to "claim her own experience as an African-American woman in the South." "She did it to claim her own experience as an African-American woman in the South.
