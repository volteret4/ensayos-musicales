## curiosities
**Black Flag's "Damaged" as a Revelation** : Nate Mendel's understanding of hardcore punk solidified upon listening to Black Flag's "Damaged" album. He initially didn't grasp it, but halfway through, "it clicked," revealing to him the energy, aggression, and "weirdness" of the genre that he came to love. "Well, the one that, that, where I understood what hardcore we call it, hardcore at the time was about, was, uh, black flags damaged." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mh-qg1izM98
**Kennewick, Washington Background** : Nate Mendel grew up in Kennewick, a small town in Eastern Washington State, where there was "really no music happening." This lack of a local scene likely contributed to his and his friends' gravitating towards punk rock, seeing themselves as "misfits" drawn to something different. "Eastern Washington State. Okay. It's tiny little town out in the desert. What would you call? Kenwick." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mh-qg1izM98
**Limited Musical Knowledge Focused on Hardcore** : Nate Mendel admits to having the "least depth of musical knowledge" among his bandmates, primarily caring about hardcore music for a formative period of about ten years. He often relies on Taylor Hawkins to introduce him to other bands, such as Boston. "I only cared about hardcore for about 10 years. Like those formative 10 years." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mh-qg1izM98
