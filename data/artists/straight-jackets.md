# artist - Straight Jackets

## members
- Jason Larson (bass)

## labels

## albums
**Unspecified Vinyl Record (1989)** : The Straight Jackets, a band from Bedford (associated with a New Brunswick experience), released a vinyl record on DTK Records around 1989. They were admired as "superstars" by future Sloan members and were the headline act that Carney Lake Road supported on tour. "They had a vinyl record on DTK records. And that came out in about 1989 or so." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE

## curiosities
**Co-Manager and Scene Figure** : Peter Rowan served as a co-manager for Sloan and was also involved in managing other bands from the Halifax scene, such as Eric's Trip and Hardship Post. His connections facilitated Murder Records' expansion to release records for these other local groups. "And also our co-manager, the time Peter Rowan was also involved with managing like Eric's trip and hardship post." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
**Pre-Sloan Influencers and Tourmates** : The Straight Jackets were a Bedford-based band (part of a "New Brunswick experience") that headlined tours with Carney Lake Road. Their bass player, Jason Larson, had the nickname "Sloan," which was later adopted by the band Sloan, who even featured his face on their debut EP cover. "Straight Jackets was what they were called. They had a record out. It was very exciting." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
