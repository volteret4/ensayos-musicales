# artist - Nash the Slash

## curiosities
**Nash the Slash (Jeff Plunman, Toronto, 1948–2014) – Surgical Bandages Adopted After Three Mile Island Accident (1979); David Gilmour Offered Guitar Part but Nash Refused All Guitars** : Nash the Slash's real name was Jeff Plunman, born in Toronto on March 26, 1948. The stage name came from a character in a 1927 Laurel and Hardy silent film. He co-founded the prog band FM in 1975 and audiences could see his face briefly, but after the nuclear accident at Three Mile Island near Harrisburg, Pennsylvania in March 1979, Nash adopted surgical bandages that completely obscured his face as a way of raising awareness about what could happen if a nuclear plant melted down. He left FM around 1980 to resume his solo career, toured with Gary Numan and the Tubes, hung out with Iggy Pop and Daniel Lanois, and Brian Eno collaborated on his records. David Gilmour of Pink Floyd was such a fan that he offered to lay down a guitar part on one of Nash's songs — but Nash refused because he never used guitars on any of his songs. He made records until 2012, was especially popular in Germany and Poland, and died of an apparent heart attack at his Toronto home on May 10, 2014, at age 66. His music and costumes are managed by the Nash the Slash Legacy organization and much was donated to the National Music Center in Calgary. His best-known record was 1984's American Bandages.
