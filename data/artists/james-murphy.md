**Regret Over Black Star Production** : James Murphy, unaware of David Bowie's terminal illness at the time of Black Star's recording, later expressed profound regret for not fully committing to the album's production. He has stated in interviews that it was the "biggest mistake of his life" not to have paid more attention to David Bowie's request. "es algo que hoy en día en sus entrevistas dice que se revendirá toda su vida, dice que había sido el mayor error de su vida no haber hecho más caso a David Bowie." ← Music Radar Clan > David Bowie - BlackStar (lo que sabemos) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7gjhR5zK148&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc ← james-murphy
**Rejected Producer Role for Black Star** : James Murphy was initially slated to be a co-producer for David Bowie's Black Star alongside Bowie and Tony Visconti. However, due to his intense involvement in numerous other projects—including his own record label, collaborations, productions, and event organization—he was unable to dedicate sufficient time, often being late or absent from sessions and struggling to understand Bowie's experimental material. He ultimately declined full producer credit, contributing only specific rhythmic and sound elements. "Finalmente, James Murphy terminó rechazando el trabajo como productor colaborador para el Black Star, él sí que trabajo en el álbum, haciendo algunas bases de sonidos de ritmos, poniendo algunas ideas, ayudando a producir alguna cosa pero su trabajo no dejo de ser un montón de cosas puntuales que se entremezclan en el disco." ← Music Radar Clan > David Bowie - BlackStar (lo que sabemos) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7gjhR5zK148&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc ← james-murphy
