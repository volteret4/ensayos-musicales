# artist - Scott Rainer

## member of
- Blink-182

## curiosities
**Alcohol Abuse and Band Tensions** : By 1998, after three straight years of intense touring with little time off, Scott's drinking problems became severe, leading to escalating tensions, fights, and a breakdown in communication among band members. "But his problems with drinking continued, something had to give." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eljIIqCu5gU
**Bedroom Recording Studio** : His bedroom served as the primary recording venue for Blink's early demo tapes, including "Fly Swatter" and "Demo Number Two," where a mix of original songs and covers were produced. "It was a mix of original songs and covers recorded in Scott's bedroom that was distributed on cassette." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eljIIqCu5gU
**Disagreement over Major Label Deal** : He strongly believed that signing with MCA, a major label, constituted a "very unpunk move," creating significant friction and disagreement within the band, particularly with Mark Hoppas and Tom DeLong who were supportive of the deal. "Scott? Not so much. He thought that signing with MCA was a very unpunk move." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eljIIqCu5gU
**Family Relocation and Living Arrangements** : His family moved to Reno, Nevada, which created a logistical challenge for him to participate in recording "Cheshire Cat" with the band. To overcome this, he moved in with his sister in San Diego temporarily. "To make matters even more complicated, Scott's family had just moved to Reno, Nevada. So he moved in with his sister in San Diego while the band made the record." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eljIIqCu5gU
**Fired from Blink-182** : In May 1998, despite agreeing to enter rehab, Tom DeLong and Mark Hoppas felt that Scott's behavior was negatively impacting the band's performances, leading to his dismissal. While some accounts suggest he was fired over the phone, he was ultimately replaced by Travis Barker. "So Scott was fired. Some story said he was fired over the phone, but whatever the case is, he was out and Travis was in." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eljIIqCu5gU
**Initial Meeting with Tom DeLong** : Scott Rainer, a drummer whose current band was "falling apart," was drawn to Tom DeLong's solo acoustic performance at a local battle of the bands. He later met Tom at a party, which eventually led to their musical collaboration. "And this attracted the attention of a drummer named Scott Rainer who was in a band that was in the process of falling apart." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eljIIqCu5gU
**Temporary Replacement by Mark Crawl** : Due to ongoing issues with his living arrangements, Scott was temporarily replaced by his high school friend, Mark Crawl. During this period, the band pooled money to facilitate Scott's travel back and forth between Reno and San Diego. "For a while, the band pulled money so we could fly back and forth to Reno. And for a time, he was replaced by a dude named Mark Crawl, who was Scott's high school buddy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eljIIqCu5gU
**Underage for Contract** : When Blink signed with Cargo Records, Scott was under the age of 18, making him legally ineligible to sign the contract himself. "The only member of the band who could sign the contract was Mark. He was old enough. Tom wasn't around because he was work and Scott was under 18, so he was ineligible." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eljIIqCu5gU
**Unfinished High School and College Thoughts** : Due to his commitments with the band, Scott never completed high school. Around the time of his departure from Blink-182, he began to consider attending college, indicating a period of personal conflict and reflection. "Even though because of his work with a man, he never even finished high school." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eljIIqCu5gU
