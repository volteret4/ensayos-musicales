## albums
**Ramones (1976) – Debut Album** : The band released their debut album in April 1976. This release preceded their highly influential gig at the Roundhouse in London on July 4th, 1976, where they introduced their sound to the nascent UK punk community. "The Ramones, who had released their debut album back in April, were part of a triple bill with the stranglers and the flaming grueves at a venue called the Roundhouse." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MTDY6qT11Dc
**Ramones (1976) – Self-Titled Debut** : Released on April 23, 1976, this album features a simple black and white photograph of the four band members in ripped jeans and leather jackets, posed against a graffiti-covered brick wall. This aesthetic was intended to represent the band as a unified "gang." The photo was taken by Roberta Bailey for *Punk Magazine* and was chosen after the band's initial preference for a style resembling The Beatles' *Meet the Beatles* (1964) was rejected by Sire Records art director Tony Wadler. The image was taken in Albert's Garden, a private community garden in New York City, and manager Danny Fields approved of its perfection. The cover helped solidify a distinctive punk-fashion look and has since become one of the most imitated album poses, even becoming part of the permanent collection of the Museum of Modern Art in New York. "This is a perfect representation of the band as a gang aesthetic." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sn5TWBFd0XM

## curiosities
**Backstage Rider – Wholesome Demands** : The Ramones' rider was notably modest for a punk band, requesting only towels, freshly squeezed orange juice, a quart of fresh milk, two six-packs of either Coca-Cola or Pepsi, chocolate chip cookies, a fruit tray, two large cheese pizzas delivered precisely 90 minutes before showtime, two bars of soap, and a case of beer. "Pretty wholesome for a punk band, right?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z2hvUDtBxho
**Iconic Cover Photography Fee** : Photographer Roberta Bailey was paid a total of $125 for capturing the image used on the Ramones' self-titled debut album, which went on to become one of the most iconic images in rock and roll history. "Roberta Bailey was paid a grand total of $125." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sn5TWBFd0XM
**Inspiration for British Punk (1976)** : The Ramones played a pivotal gig at the Roundhouse in London on Sunday, July 4th, 1976, as part of America's Bicentennial. Around 2,000 members of the "infant UK punk community" packed the venue, eager to hear the New York sound. This performance, along with another at Dingwalls, was immensely influential; key figures like Johnny Rotten and Joe Strummer attended soundchecks and shows, stating that the Ramones "turned them on" and "inspired them to form their own bands." The band felt like "royalty" upon arrival, noting the shift from selling out CBGB's to playing for 3,000 kids already "tuned in." "No one could have known it at the time, but this was one of the most influential gigs in the history of rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MTDY6qT11Dc
**Merchandise Sales Outpacing Album Sales** : The Ramones have achieved remarkably higher sales figures for their t-shirts worldwide compared to their album sales, illustrating the significant impact and power of band merchandise. "The Ramones have sold far more t-shirts worldwide than they ever sold albums. That's the power of merch kits." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GO0K_N7Ys7I
