## albums
**I Hear A New World (1960) - Conceptual Space Album** : Conceived and produced by Joe Meek in 1960, this album is possibly the first conceptual rock album in history. It presents a musical fantasy about outer space, heavily employing echoes, reverbs, and pitch modulation of voices to simulate alien sounds. It also uniquely uses modified household sounds to evoke the vastness of space, and this album significantly predated the later success of Space Music. "Es el mismo año mi concebió y produjo a Hira New World, posiblemente el primer disco conceptual de la historia del rock, una fantasía musical sobre el espacio exterior, repleta de ecos, reverbs, modulación del pitch de las voces para simular la voz extraterrestre o el uso de sonidos caseros modificados para simular el espacio." ← El productor que escuchó el nuevo mundo. **Innovative Recording Techniques** : Meek was renowned for his unorthodox production methods, which included the exaggerated use of self-made compressors, direct bass input without an amplifier, sampling, and extensive overdubbing on one or two-track recorders to craft new and complex textures. He also utilized a distinctive technique of recording songs at slower speeds and then accelerating them during production to achieve his unique sound. "donde destacó por sus técnicas poportodoxas, como el uso exagerado de compresores que el mismo fabricaba, la entrada directa de bajo sin amplificador, el sampling, o el sobredoblaje en grabadoras de una o dos pistas para crear nuevas texturas." ← El productor que escuchó el nuevo mundo.
