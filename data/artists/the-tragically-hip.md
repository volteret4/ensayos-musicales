**Fully Completely (1992) – Third Studio Album** : Released on October 6, 1992, this album was recorded at Battery Studios in Willesden Green, North London, in July 1992, produced by Chris Sangerides. It marked a departure from the band's usual live-recording style, with Sangerides building tracks from the ground up, starting with bass and drums for the first three weeks. This method contributed to a "bigger sound," more instrument isolation, and enhanced guitar sounds. The album immediately sold 140,000 copies in Canada upon release, reaching 200,000 within three months, and by January 2007, it was certified for over 1 million domestic sales (a diamond award), placing it in company with albums like U2's *Joshua Tree* and Nirvana's *Nevermind*. It was the only Hip album to be released in Japan, on April 23, 1993. A 2014 Deluxe Reissue included a remastered album, two bonus tracks, and a complete live recording from an invitation-only record release show at the Horseshoe Tavern on September 13, 1992. "they had no idea that they had created something extraordinary." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Vs1YyXgLpTE

**50 Mission Cap (Year Not Given) – Second Single from Fully Completely** : This song, the second single from the album, delves into a specific piece of Canadian hockey history. It recounts the story of Bill Barilko, a Toronto Maple Leafs defense player who won four Stanley Cups before disappearing in a float plane crash on August 26, 1951, whose wreckage was found on June 6, 1962. The title refers to a World War II US Army Air Force cloth officer's cap, often reshaped by pilots inserting a stiff object, like a hockey card, after being crushed by headphones. Despite initial concerns about a hockey-themed song's universal appeal, its strong lyrics made it work globally, with Gord Downie reportedly improvising the lyrics while playing a "caveman riff" and holding a hockey card. "But we all knew exactly what he was singing about. We'd been on that trip to the Smithsonian in Washington and seen the 50-mission cap and the whole concept." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Vs1YyXgLpTE

**Magnetic Tape Degradation and Baking Process** : Magnetic tapes degrade over time, a process often indicated by an "acidic" or "vinegary" smell.
