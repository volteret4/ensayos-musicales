# artist - Gene Krupa

## instruments
- Bass drum (24-inch)
- Cymbals (two, mounted on stands)
- Hi-hats (13-inch, on stand)
- Ludwig style pedal
- Snare drum (14-inch)
- Tom-tom (13-inch, mounted)
- Tom-tom (16-inch, floor with legs)

## curiosities
**1948 Slingerland Kit Configuration** : Gene Krupa's 1948 drum kit, manufactured by Slingerland (a company that started making drums in 1913 and was his favorite at the time), featured a specific setup. This included a 24-inch bass drum played with a Ludwig-style pedal, a 14-inch snare, a 13-inch tom-tom mounted on a stand, a 16-inch tom-tom with legs for floor placement, 13-inch hi-hats on a hi-hat stand, and two additional cymbals mounted on their own stands. "Crouppa's 1948 kit featured a 24-inch bass drum, played with a Ludwig style pedal, a 14-inch snare, a 13-inch tom tom mounted on a stand, and a 16-inch tom with legs that stood on the floor." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WL3zEWJJNuw
**Drum Kit Standardization** : Alongside Buddy Rich, Gene Krupa made substantial contributions to standardizing the construction and setup of drum kits. By 1948, Krupa's personal drum kit had evolved to a configuration that would be immediately recognizable and playable by any modern drummer. "Also, Crouppa and Rich did a lot to standardize how drum kits were constructed and set up." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WL3zEWJJNuw
**First Drum Superstar** : Gene Krupa is widely considered to be the very first drum superstar, gaining significant attention and respect during the mid-1930s swing era when drummers began to receive more prominence due to the emphasis on rhythm for dancing. "The very first drum superstar was probably Jean Crouppa." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WL3zEWJJNuw
**Legendary Drum Battles with Buddy Rich** : One of Gene Krupa's most popular attractions was the "drum battle," where he would challenge another drummer, most frequently his friend Buddy Rich, to a competition to determine who was superior. These battles were held on stage, televised, and recorded, and played a significant role in making drumming a "really really cool thing." "His most frequent competitor was his friend Buddy Rich." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WL3zEWJJNuw
**Orchestra Leader and Stage Presence** : Krupa led his own orchestra, and his drum kit was distinctively set up on a large riser, positioning him high above all the other musicians. He played with immense energy and flamboyance, captivating audiences who found his performance primal and powerful. "He had his own orchestra, and his kit was set up on a big riser high above all the other musicians, and he played with a lot of energy and flamboyance." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WL3zEWJJNuw
