## albums
**45 (1982) - Debut Album** : Kino's debut album, recorded in 1982 with the assistance of musicians from the band Aquarium. The title, "45," referred to the 45-minute duration of the tape. Lacking a dedicated drummer, the band utilized an electronic drum machine, which, combined with acoustic guitars, contributed to a curious and primitive sound. The lyrics were a novel element for Soviet society, filled with contained poetry that elevated everyday life to the heroic, offering a "radiography of their time" by singing about existential emptiness rather than direct political statements. "El nombre era porque la cinta duraba con 25 minutos." ← Viktor Tsoi. El padre del Postpunk Ruso | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UpqLty1-X4Q
**46 (Unofficial Album)** : This album followed "45" and was distributed unofficially. Despite its informal release, many fans consider it the band's second work, indicating its importance within Kino's discography even without official backing. "45 vino una semana en el año que fue el año para su siguiente trabajo, pero se distribuyó uno oficialmente y muchos fans lo consideran el segundo trabajo de la banda." ← Viktor Tsoi. El padre del Postpunk Ruso | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UpqLty1-X4Q
**Chyorny Albom (The Black Album) (1991) - Posthumous Release** : Following Victor Tsoy's death, police recovered a miraculously intact cassette from the car wreck. This cassette contained the voice and guitar tracks that Tsoy and guitarist Yuri Kasparyan had recorded in Tonya. Tsoy's bandmates composed the remaining music around these foundational tracks, releasing a posthumous album with a completely black cover, which subsequently became known as "The Black Album." "Sus compañeros de banda, composieron la música sobre el alrededor, y lanzaron un disco post-tumor que no la portaba completamente negra." ← Viktor Tsoi. El padre del Postpunk Ruso | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UpqLty1-X4Q
**Gruppa Krovi (Blood Group) (1988) - Breakthrough Album** : Released in 1988, this album marked the peak of Kino's popularity. It was massively distributed across the entire USSR through "magnetizdat," a system of pirated cassette copies made by fans for each other in their homes. This grassroots distribution allowed Kino to become the country's biggest band despite lacking official support. The album was recorded concurrently with the filming of Sergei Solovyov's movie "Asa." "El disco se movió masivamente por toda la urs a través de la magnetistad, copias piratas en caset que los fans grababan de unos a otros en sus casas." ← Viktor Tsoi. El padre del Postpunk Ruso | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UpqLty1-X4Q

## songs
**Change (1989)** : Kino, with their singer Soi, released the song "Change" (translates from Russian) around 1989 during the last years of the Soviet period. This song quickly became a political rallying cry amidst the significant social and political changes occurring in 1989, 1990, and 1991. The band, like others, was allegedly part of a CIA operation to import subversive rock into the USSR, with claims that Soi was given songs written by Hollywood songwriters. "Kino and their singer Soi from 1989. The song is called Change and yes it did become something of a political rally and cry in those fast changing days of 1989, 1990 and 1991." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YeNWDtN_sHE ← kino
**Gruppa Krovi (1988)** : This song, the single from the album of the same name, became widely recognized and was later notably used in the video game Grand Theft Auto 4. Its inclusion in the game accompanied the narrative of an Eastern European immigrant arriving in Liberty City, pursuing the American dream only to find a corrupt and heartless urban environment. "el single de Grupa Crovy, que es un tema, si usó para el videojuego Grandes Fauto 4." ← Viktor Tsoi. El padre del Postpunk Ruso | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UpqLty1-X4Q
**Khochu Peremen! (I Want Changes) (circa 1987)** : An iconic Kino song whose lyrics explored themes of existential void rather than direct politics. It gained immense popularity when it was featured in the final scene of the cult film "Asa" in 1987. Subsequently, the masses appropriated the song, transforming it into a political anthem during the Perestroika era, as it resonated with calls for changes and reforms. Victor Tsoy, however, consistently rejected this political label for his lyrics. "Cuando la canción son en la escena final de la película de Culto Asa de 1987, las masas apropiaron de ella y la convirtieron en un digno político en plena perestroica, viviendo cambios y reformas." ← Viktor Tsoi. ## curiosities
**Founding (1981)** : Victor Tsoy and guitarist Alex S. Rybin founded their first band, Garin i Giperboloidy, in June 1981, before eventually changing its name to Kino. "Punto, en vez de a uno de 1961, fundaron su propia banda, Gary Nighy pervolo Di." ← Viktor Tsoi. El padre del Postpunk Ruso | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UpqLty1-X4Q
**Internal Conflict and Lineup Change (Post-45)** : Following their debut album, Kino experienced a challenging period marked by creative differences between Victor Tsoy and Alex S. Rybin. Their clashing ideas regarding the group's direction and leadership ultimately led to Rybin abandoning Kino, leaving Tsoy to continue the band alone. "Soy Ribin Chocaban y tenía ni ideas contraria sobre el rumbo y el liderazgo del grupo, por lo que Ribin acabó abandonando Quinoa." ← Viktor Tsoi. El padre del Postpunk Ruso | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UpqLty1-X4Q
**Musical Evolution and Influences** : For their subsequent albums, Kino expanded their lineup by recruiting more musicians and increasingly incorporated electronic instruments into their sound. Musically, they were considered close to bands like The Cure and Joy Division, and Victor Tsoy particularly admired The Smiths. Their distinctive dark and danceable sound was notably filtered by the brutalist architecture, coldness, and crudeness of Leningrad, creating a unique sonic identity that has since been embraced by the new wave of post-punk in Eastern Europe. "Posicalmente, que no estaba cerca de bandas como de Kyu, Royo y División, una de las bandas que más amigaba Victor Toy eran desmits." ← Viktor Tsoi. El padre del Postpunk Ruso | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UpqLty1-X4Q
**Name Change (1982)** : The band Garin i Giperboloidy changed its name to Kino in 1982, establishing the identity under which they would become renowned. "Y ya, en 1982, se cambiaron el nombre áquino." ← Viktor Tsoi. El padre del Postpunk Ruso | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UpqLty1-X4Q
**Rebirth on the Internet** : Decades after their initial success, Kino has experienced a significant rebirth on the internet. Hundreds of their songs have been translated into Spanish and numerous other languages by young people who, despite the time difference, feel a deep connection with the band's music, demonstrating its timeless appeal. "seguramente sea por eso que Quinoa ha vivido un renacimiento en internet. De escenas de canciones suyas han sido traducidas al español y muchos otros idiomas por jóvenes que deca las despues, sin tener una profunda conexión con la banda." ← Viktor Tsoi.
