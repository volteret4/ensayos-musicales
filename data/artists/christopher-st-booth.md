## songs
**Alter Your Motives (1986)** : This song was originally known as "Everyone Knows That" during a prolonged online search, but its correct title was eventually identified. It was specifically written for an obscure 1986 porn film titled "Angels of Passion," appearing at the 1-hour, 7-minute, and 31-second mark, and was never available anywhere else commercially. "And it turns out that the song is called Alter Your Motives." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UsPoPUByjYw

## curiosities
**Bafflement at Rediscovery** : When Chris Booth learned about the extensive online search for his song, he was completely baffled by the attention. He considered officially releasing it but faced challenges, as he no longer possessed all the constituent parts of the song (like guitar and synth pieces) and the master recording was with a deceased friend. "When news of the search and mystery reached Chris, he was completely baffled." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UsPoPUByjYw
**Composing for Porn Films** : Christopher St. Booth, after his band Sweeny Todd broke up, moved to Encino, California, and took on odd jobs. When offered an opportunity by a friend who made porn films, he started writing music for them due to the good money, carefully registering the copyrights with Sokan, the Canadian Performing Arts Organization. "Then, a friend who made porn said, I need music for one of my new films. Can you do it? Sure they can." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UsPoPUByjYw
**Lost Song Discovered in Porn Film** : The song "Alter Your Motives" (known as "Everyone Knows That" during its search) was found within an obscure 1986 porn film titled "Angels of Passion" by online music detectives. The "carnal noises" from the film were removed by fans, not the original creators, to isolate the music. "The searchers then watched every porn film featuring Chris Booth's music until they found the song in an obscure movie from 1986 called Angels of Passion." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UsPoPUByjYw
**Post-Porn Film Career** : After 1986, Christopher St. Booth and his twin brother Philip Booth shifted their focus from porn film music to specializing in movies about ghosts and exorcisms.
