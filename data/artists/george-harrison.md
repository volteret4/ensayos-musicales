**My Sweet Lord (1970)** : George Harrison was inspired to write this song by the gospel hit "Oh Happy Day" by the Edwin Hawkins Singers. He took great thought in writing it, anticipating that "a lot of people might get weird about it" due to fear of the words "Lord" and "God." Lyrically, the song is a risk as it pays homage to both Christianity (with "Hallelujah" in the background) and Hinduism (with "Hare Krishna" in the background), reflecting Harrison's affection for both. Musically, it was determined by a judge to have unintentionally plagiarized "He's So Fine" by The Chiffons. The song featured a prominent tambourine and went to number one in December 1970, holding the top spot for about a month. "Harrison, who's considered the third beetle, always in the shadow of John Lennon and Paul McCartney, was writing the song something? Was that just a fluke?" ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 32 - Take Me to Church - Religion in 70s Popular Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-32-take-me-to-church-religion-in-70s-popular-music ← george-harrison
**My Sweet Lord (1971)** : George Harrison's song was involved in a long-running plagiarism case against The Chiffons' "He's So Fine" (1963). The judge ultimately ruled that Harrison was guilty of "subconscious plagiarism" or cryptomnesia, meaning he unconsciously replicated the melody, believing it to be an original composition, despite differences in key, arrangement, and instrumentation. The case, which started in 1971, was resolved in 1993, primarily over the ownership of the central melody and back royalties. "George Harrison got caught with this. Because it sounded way too much like this chiffon song from 1963." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fS8V3kYXT6w ← george-harrison ← george-harrison
