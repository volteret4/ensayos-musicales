## curiosities
**Early Drumming Education – Joe Bonny and Lessons** : Larry Mullen started playing drums at the early age of nine. His first teacher was Joe Bonny, who was recognized at the time as Ireland's best-known drummer. Larry's mother supported his interest by purchasing a secondhand drum set for 17 pounds. Despite extensive formal lessons, he grew bored with the strict formality, rudiment exercises, sight-reading charts, and playing along to others' music. "his first teacher was a guy named Joe Bonny who at the time was Ireland's best known drummer and he taught Larry well." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo
**Father's Advice – Irish Army Band** : Larry Mullen's father, Larry Mullen Senior, was very supportive of his son's drumming ambitions. However, he once advised Larry to join the Irish army for seven years, suggesting that this path would allow him to play in the army band and learn to play all percussion instruments. "Larry Mullen's senior was very supportive of his son's drumming ambitions but his advice was uh Well, he almost had his son convince to join the Irish army for seven years so he could play in the band And learn to play all the percussion instruments." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo
**Forming U2 – The Bulletin Board Note** : At age 15, frustrated with formal lessons, Larry Mullen pinned a note to the bulletin board at Mount Temple High School in Dublin. The note sought out other musicians who had "wasted money" on guitars. This initiative led him to approach Adam Clayton, and later attracted Dave and Dick Evans (The Edge) and Paul Husson (Bono), leading to the band's first rehearsal in Larry's kitchen in the autumn of 1976. "one day when he was 15 Larry pinned a note to the bulldozen board at Mount Temple High School in Dublin The note said money wasted on a drum kit Were there others out there who had wasted money on guitars." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo
**Pre-U2 Performances – Local and National Gigs** : Before he even entered high school, Larry Mullen was actively performing. He played in a band associated with the local post office that traveled around the country. He also had the honor of playing in the significant St. Patrick's Day parade in Dublin. "Before Larry even made it to high school. He was playing at a band at the local post office Which actually traveled around the country He also played in the big St. Patrick's Day parade in Dublin, which is a huge honor." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo
