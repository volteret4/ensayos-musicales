## songs
**Black Sabbath (1970)** : Featured on the 1970 album of the same name, this song clearly displays Tony Iomi's tri tone, played with a trill and distortion. Iomi's inspiration for this sound came from Gustav Holst's "Mars, the Bringer of War," which he adapted by slowing it down and adding distortion, marking it as a pivotal moment for the birth of heavy metal. "And you can clearly hear Iomi's tri tone played with a little bit of a trill. For some, this is the birth of heavy metal." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=41eKVp8JIhU

**Tony Iomi's Tri Tone Inspiration from Gustav Holst** : Tony Iomi drew significant inspiration from the composer Gustav Holst's symphonic work, "The Planets," which premiered in London in 1918. He was particularly captivated by the "Mars, the Bringer of War" section, which featured a flattened fifth that he found sounded "warlike." Iomi applied this concept to his electric guitar, slowing down the interval and adding distortion to create a signature sound. "He was a fan of the composer Gustav Holtz, especially his symphonic creation called The Planets, which first premiered in London in 1918. There's a section of the planets called Mars, the Bringer of War. He liked that a lot. It featured a flattened fifth that sounded, well, very warlike." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=41eKVp8JIhU
