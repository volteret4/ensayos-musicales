# artist - De La Soul

## albums
**3 Feet High and Rising (1989)** : De La Soul's 1989 album, *3 Feet High and Rising*, featured the track "Transmitting Why From Mars," which contained a sampled portion of The Turtles' 1969 song "You Showed Me." This unauthorized sample led to a $1.7 million lawsuit against the band and their record company. ## curiosities
**Collaborations with Gorillaz** : De La Soul contributed to the Gorillaz's album "Demon Days" on the song "Feel Good Inc.," which won a Grammy Award. They also contributed to the "Plastic Beach" and "Humans" albums. "additional help on that track comes from De La Soul." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iVdTCqpd798
**Lawsuit by The Turtles (1989)** : In 1989, De La Soul faced a $1.7 million lawsuit from The Turtles for "stealing" a portion of their 1969 song "You Showed Me" for the track "Transmitting Why From Mars" on the album *3 Feet High and Rising*. The lawsuit was settled out of court, establishing a precedent against unauthorized sampling. "That resulted in a $1.7 million lawsuit against Dela Sol and their record company. Settled off court, but the message was clear. Do not sample without permission." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ak6OuOUkRrQ
**Streaming Legalities Sorted (Until 2023)** : De La Soul's early music was so "sample-heavy" that it took until 2023 for all the legalities surrounding the samples to be completely sorted out. This extensive legal process prevented their songs from being made available on streaming music services for an extended period. "Dela Sol's early music was so sample-heavy that it took until 2023 for all the legalities to be sorted out and their songs could finally be made available on streaming music services." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ak6OuOUkRrQ
