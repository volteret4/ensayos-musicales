**Superstar (1971)** : Karen Carpenter famously played the drums while singing this song on "The Carol Burnett Show," a mesmerizing performance for many. Originally titled "Groupie" and written in 1969 by Bonnie Bramlett and Leon Russell, the song is about the loneliness of groupies, an experience Karen stated she hadn't had but understood. Richard Carpenter later revised the lyrics for the Carpenters' version, changing "I can't wait to sleep with you again" to "I can't wait to be with you again." It reached number two on the Billboard Hot 100 in October 1971, selling a million records in eight weeks, held from the top spot by Rod Stewart's "Maggie May."
