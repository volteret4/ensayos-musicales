## concerts
- **Berlin Concert** : During a concert in Berlin, Bono's performance of "Beautiful Day" was largely "on point," although he may have been holding back slightly. He relied on The Edge's background vocals to add "oomph" to the performance. "On this particular night in Berlin, things were pretty much on point, although maybe he was holding back just a little."

They were allotted time for three songs, starting well with "Sunday Bloody Sunday." **Where the Streets Have No Name (1987) – Engineer Physically Blocked Brian Eno from Erasing Tape** : The Edge brought demos to the *Joshua Tree* sessions declaring he had written "the most amazing guitar part he'd ever written in his life" and did a little dance in his home studio. The band then spent enormous amounts of rehearsal time and recording tape unable to nail the song — the timing changes and chord changes proved too complex. Co-producer Brian Eno became so frustrated that he proposed erasing the tape and starting from scratch. Engineer Pat McCarthy disagreed and physically blocked access to the tape machine with his body, resulting in a physical confrontation. The band eventually built the song from three separate basic takes, making it a Frankenstein composite. Even then, Eno and co-producer Daniel Lanois found it too much; Steve Lillywhite was brought in to finish the track, to Eno's annoyance. The result opens the album. "There's the intro, the first two verses, and the third verse. This is a Frankenstein monster of a song." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mmI2oN9zolQ
