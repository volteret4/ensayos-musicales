## concerts
- **Berlin Concert** : During a concert in Berlin, Bono's performance of "Beautiful Day" was largely "on point," although he may have been holding back slightly. He relied on The Edge's background vocals to add "oomph" to the performance. "On this particular night in Berlin, things were pretty much on point, although maybe he was holding back just a little."
- **Live Aid Performance and Fallout (July 13, 1985)** : U2 performed at Live Aid at Wembley Stadium in London, introduced by Jack Nicholson at 5:20 PM. They were allotted time for three songs, starting well with "Sunday Bloody Sunday." However, the set went "pear-shaped" when Bono's lengthy on-stage interaction with an audience member during "Bad" consumed too much time, causing them to miss playing their planned third song, "Pride (In the Name of Love)." "The band, like everybody else, had only enough time to play three songs, and things started well enough with a version of Sunday Bloody Sunday."
