## albums
**Achtung Baby (1991)** : The experiences of the band in Berlin in October 1990, including their stay in a reclaimed villa, served as the strange start to the journey that eventually resulted in this album.
**Mission: Impossible Theme (1996) – Larry Mullen and Adam Clayton Project** : Larry Mullen and Adam Clayton updated Lalo Schifrin's original theme for the first *Mission: Impossible* film, starring Tom Cruise. It was released as a single in 1996.

## curiosities
**Adam Clayton's Default Bass Role** : Adam Clayton became a bass player by default in a small band, which was the only other group he had played in.
**Adam Clayton's Response to Ad** : Adam Clayton, the bassist, was the first person to respond to Larry Mullen's ad on the bulletin board.
**Adam Clayton's School Reputation** : Adam Clayton had a reputation as a lazy troublemaker in school, with the bass guitar being the only thing he truly cared about.
**Bono's Humanitarian Flights** : Bono frequently flies private for various humanitarian missions.
**Bono's Villa Encounter (1990)** : Bono was given a villa that had previously housed Soviet leader Leonid Brezhnev. Naked, he later encountered the original German family who had been dispossessed in 1961 and had reclaimed their home after reunification, leading U2 to quickly move to new rental quarters.
**Chartering Private Planes for Tours** : The band charters private planes for themselves and their crew when touring the world.
**Encountering German Reunification Protests (1990)** : While in Berlin for reunification celebrations, U2 mistakenly ended up at a protest meeting to put the Berlin Wall back up.
**First Rehearsal** : Their first rehearsal took place in the kitchen of Larry Mullen Jr.'s house in autumn 1976, marking the first time the members played together.
**Formation Origin** : Formed after Larry Mullen Jr., at age 15, pinned a note on the bulletin board at Mount Temple High School in Dublin, seeking other musicians.
**Hansa Studios Booking (1990)** : After arriving in Berlin on October 2, 1990, U2 had booked Hansa Studios to begin working on ideas for their next album.
**Larry Mullen's Band Foundation (1976)** : Larry Mullen, the drummer, initiated the formation of U2 by posting a note on a school bulletin board in 1976.
**Larry Mullen's Drumming Instructor** : Larry Mullen received lessons from Joe Bonnie, who was known as Ireland's most famous drummer for a time.
**Larry Mullen's Early Touring** : Larry Mullen spent a significant portion of his younger days touring the country in a marching band.
**Larry Mullen's Military Band Training** : Larry Mullen was a military band drummer by training.
**Larry Mullen's Pivotal Role** : Drummer Larry Mullen Jr. is credited as the person responsible for forming the band in the first place.
**Last Commercial Flight into East Germany (1990)** : On October 2, 1990, U2 boarded the last commercial British Airways flight into Tegal Airport in Berlin, East Germany, just before East and West Germany ceased to exist at midnight. The pilot circled the Brandenburg Gate.
**Learjet Cargo Door Detachment (2014)** : On Thursday, November 13, 2014, Bono was aboard a private Learjet 60 on a two-hour flight from Dublin to Berlin's Schönefeld Airport. The rear cargo hatch became detached over the German coast, and then blew off entirely 11 minutes before touchdown. Two suitcases were lost, and a piece of the door was found in the Brandenburg area.
**Plane Shot At in Jamaica (1996)** : On January 16, 1996, Bono, his family, and friends (including Jimmy Buffett and Island Records head Chris Blackwell) were on Jimmy Buffett's Grumman HU-16C plane, "Hemisphere Dancer," taxing at Negril Airport, Jamaica. Jamaican police mistook it for a drug runner's plane and opened fire, making Bono feel like he was in a "James Bond movie" and terrifying his family.
**Private Plane for Tours** : When U2 tours, they typically charter private planes for the band and crew.
