## songs
**Planet Claire (1979)** : As Side One Track One of their 1979 debut album, "Planet Claire" features mysterious Morse code. This code is identified as a call tape or ZKR tape, a Canadian Forces message from Milkov and Hubbard's, Nova Scotia, informing ships about optimal ship-to-shore communication frequencies. "It's what's known as a call tape or a ZKR tape. It's a recording of a message sent out by the Canadian forces-based Milkov and Hubbard's Nova Scotia." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M

## curiosities
**Athens, Georgia Music Scene Pioneers** : The B-52s are mentioned as a group that began the Athens, Georgia music scene in the mid-1970s. Their success encouraged Peter Buck and Michael Stipe to form R.E.M. "The Athens, Georgia music scene, began in the mid 1970s with the B-52s and more obscure groups like the fans and pylon and the tone tones and love tractor and method actors." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Distinctive Style and Rehearsal Space** : The band cultivated a unique visual style, incorporating 1950s thrift store clothing and bouffant hairdos. For their sound, their guitarist would replace the lower two strings with bass strings. They rehearsed in the blood-letting room of an abandoned mortuary, emphasizing their unconventional approach. "And just for fun, we'll rehearse in the blood-litting room of an abandoned mortuary." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**Formation and Early Ethos** : The B-52s formed in Athens, Georgia, after a cocktail gathering in a Chinese restaurant, with the intention of injecting silliness and fun into rock music, which they felt was too serious. "They were formed over too many cocktails in a Chinese restaurant in the unassuming college town of Athens, Georgia. Why did rock have to be so serious?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**Initial "Novelty Band" Perception** : Due to their overtly goofy and silly presentation, The B-52s were initially dismissed by some as merely a novelty band. "They were so goofy that some wrote them off as a novelty band, at least at first." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**Inspiration for New Musicians** : Despite early skepticism, The B-52s, a mixed-gender and multi-sexual band, became a significant inspiration for a new generation of musicians, with Kurt Cobain notably transfixed by their performance on Saturday Night Live in 1980. "The truth is that this mixed gender and multi-sexual band became an inspiration for a new generation of musicians. Kurt Cobain saw the one Saturday Night Live in 1980. It was absolutely transfixed by them." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
