## curiosities
**Early Recording Approach (Early 1980s)** : REM's first single, recorded at Drive-In Studio in Winston-Salem, North Carolina, was a quick affair primarily intended as a demo due to the band's limited financial resources. The resulting sound was characterized as murky, muddy, and tinny in places, clearly lacking high fidelity. However, despite these sonic imperfections, the recording possessed an undeniable quality that resonated with audiences. "The result was kind of murky, kind of muddy, kind of tinny in places, definitely not a high fidelity. But it had something." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jq-e-fZxXOs
**Label Patience for Breakthrough** : REM's label exhibited patience, waiting five albums before the band achieved a breakthrough. This contrasts sharply with the accelerated pace of the modern music industry, where new acts are rarely given such an extended period to develop and gain traction. "REM's label were patient enough to wait five albums before I break through." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Y6nHw5xx88
**Perfect Timing for College Rock (Early 1980s)** : The release of REM's first single coincided perfectly with the rise of campus radio stations, which were beginning to gain significant influence and power in the world of new rock. This era, often referred to as "college rock" (a term interchangeable with "alternative rock"), provided a fertile ground for independent labels operating outside the major label system to embrace and promote the loose, rough, raw, back-to-basics, and cheap approach of bands like REM. "REM's timing turned out to be perfect. That original hip-toen single came out when campus radio stations started to gain influence and power when it came to New Rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jq-e-fZxXOs
