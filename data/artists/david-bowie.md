**Bowie Bonds (1997) – Financial Innovation** : In 1997, Bowie, with banker David Pullman, pioneered "Bowie Bonds," a financial instrument where he securitized his future music royalties as collateral to issue bonds. He sold these bonds, raising $55 million, which he then used to repurchase the rights to his music that he had lost through previous bad management deals with Tony DeFries. In 2007, the bonds were liquidated, and all music rights reverted to Bowie, meaning by his death in 2016, he owned almost everything he had produced.
