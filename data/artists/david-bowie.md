**Bowie Bonds (1997) – Financial Innovation** : In 1997, Bowie, with banker David Pullman, pioneered "Bowie Bonds," a financial instrument where he securitized his future music royalties as collateral to issue bonds. He sold these bonds, raising $55 million, which he then used to repurchase the rights to his music that he had lost through previous bad management deals with Tony DeFries. This marked the first time a performer used their cash flow potential in this manner. Issued a few years before widespread music piracy, the bonds initially had a high A3 rating and paid 7.9% interest over 10 years, attracting Prudential Insurance. However, within 18 months, the music industry tanked due to Napster and declining physical sales, causing the bonds' rating to drop to BAA3 (near junk status). In 2007, the bonds were liquidated, and all music rights reverted to Bowie, meaning by his death in 2016, he owned almost everything he had produced. "This was the first time in financial history that a performer had used their cash flow potential to make money." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Yq2A_o9-ulM
