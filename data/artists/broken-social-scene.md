## curiosities
**Beneficiary of Radio Star Maker** : Broken Social Scene's career was furthered by the funding available from the new Radio Star Maker program, introduced in 2000. This program, along with Factor, was instrumental in supporting emerging and established Canadian talent. "Soon this cash was instrumental in helping further the careers of metric, broken social scene, Alexis on fire, city and color, and many more." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6PGgNCaW4xo
**Contribution to Al Purdy Tribute** : Jason Collette of Broken Social Scene was among the artists who contributed to the soundtrack of the 2015 documentary on Al Purdy, which featured songs and poems inspired by the poet. "Bruce Cobra and Greg Kieler of Blue Rodeo, Sarah Harmer, Jason Collette of Broken Social Scene, even Margaret Atwood appeared on that album." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dnz_Ycd_q7s
**Polaris Music Prize Nomination (2006)** : Broken Social Scene was among the artists nominated for the inaugural Polaris Music Prize in 2006, but their album ultimately lost to Final Fantasy's "He Poos Clouds." "The winning album was... By Final Fantasy... which won out over records by Broken Social Scene, The New Prenogpers, and Metric." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZzIqIydZTlA
**Post-Rock Adjacency** : While primarily categorized as indie rock, some of their material can be classified as "post-indie rock" or post-rock adjacent, due to their complex textures, softer edges, more swing, and a sense of humor and joy. "Yes, they are primarily categorized as indie rock, but I might even classify some of their material as post- indie rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
**Toronto Collective** : Broken Social Scene is described as a globally famous Toronto collective known for producing complex material with interesting textures. "A globally famous Toronto collective broken social scene, maybe not pure post-rock, but definitely post-rock adjacent, if that makes any sense." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
