## curiosities
**Departure from R.E.M.** : After rejoining R.E.M. later in 1995 following his aneurysm, Berry found that "things just weren't the same." He announced his departure in November 1997, stating a desire for a life change after 17 years with the band and "pounding the tub since I was 9 years old." "I'm ready to do something else." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jMfH9xAOpI
**On-Stage Brain Aneurysm** : On March 1, 1995, Bill Berry suffered a brain aneurysm on stage while performing falsetto background vocals for the song "Tongue." This led to emergency brain surgery and months of recovery. "Then came a transformative moment on March 1st, 1995, when he suffered a brain aneurism on stage while he was in the middle of singing some falsetto background vocals to a song called Tongue." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jMfH9xAOpI
**Pre-Law Background and Multi-Instrumentalist** : Bill Berry was a founding member of R.E.M. and was studying pre-law at the University of Georgia in Athens. He played a variety of instruments and co-wrote several of the band's hits, including "Everybody Hurts" and "Man on the Moon." "He was a founding member of the band, while in pre-law at the University of Georgia in Athens." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jMfH9xAOpI
**Retirement as Gentleman Farmer** : Bill Berry retired to a 60-acre hay farm in Watkinsville, Georgia, a property his girlfriend purchased in the late 1980s as an investment. He embraced rural life, driving a tractor and caring for sheep, although now a caretaker manages most farm duties, and Berry occasionally tends a garden. "Gentleman Farmer. Bill retired to a hay farm in Watkinsville, Georgia at the end of a long-laid off-old Farmington Road." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jMfH9xAOpI
