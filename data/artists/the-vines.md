# artist - The Vines

## members
- Craig Nichols
- Craig Nichols (singer, guitarist)
- Patrick Matthews (bass player)

## genres
- Garage Rock
- Nirvana-influenced (stripped-down rock)
- Punk

## albums
**Highly Evolved (2002)** : The Vines released their debut album on July 14, 2002, after having accumulated around three dozen songs by 2001 and recording in Los Angeles. Its release coincided with albums from The Strokes, The White Stripes, The Von Bondis, The Ye-Eyes, and The Hives, contributing to the "critical mass" of the Garage Rock revival. "And later that year, they were in LA recording their debut album and that record came out on July 14th, 2002." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UpWz3X33vSY

## curiosities
**Australian Origins and McDonald's Meeting** : The Vines were formed in Sydney, Australia, in 1994. Singer and guitarist Craig Nichols and bass player Patrick Matthews met while working together at their local McDonald's. "They were formed in Sydney, Australia in 1994, when singer and guitarist Craig Nichols and bass player Patrick Matthews met while working at their local McDonald's." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UpWz3X33vSY
**Debut Recording in Los Angeles** : The significant hype from their 2001 single led to an offer for the band to fly to Los Angeles to record their debut record. "That was enough for The Vines to get an offer to fly to Los Angeles to record a debut record." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=raRCZ0Y5up8
**Eccentric Frontman Craig Nichols** : Lead singer Craig Nichols was described as an eccentric frontman whose erratic behavior became part of the band's story. He reportedly smoked a lot of dope, insisted on eating at McDonald's at least once a day, and was volatile both in interviews and on stage. His behavior was later explained by a diagnosis of Asperger syndrome, a form of autism. "Their story was the help of the fact that they had an eccentric frontman in the form of Craig Nichols." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=raRCZ0Y5up8
**Meeting Revival Criteria** : The Vines met the emerging criteria for indie rock bands at the time, possessing a name that began with "The," playing "shouty, riffy, punky songs," and having an appealing look and attitude that music journalists were eager to promote to resurrect rock. "They met the criteria. They had a name that began with The, which was the thing at the time. They played shouty, riffy, punky songs. And they had a look at attitude that music journalists, desperate to resurrect Rock, could get behind." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=raRCZ0Y5up8
**Nirvana Influence** : Both Craig Nichols and Patrick Matthews were huge fans of Nirvana, specifically drawing inspiration from the parts of Nirvana's catalog where Kurt Cobain stripped everything down to the basics. This foundational influence shaped The Vines' own musical approach. "Both were huge fans of Nirvana and wanted to do something like that. Specifically, the parts of the Nirvana canon were Kurt stripped everything down to the basics." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UpWz3X33vSY
**Origin and Early Invisibility (1994-1999)** : Formed in Sydney, Australia, in 1994, The Vines remained almost completely invisible throughout the entire 1990s, with little public or industry interest. "They were born in Sydney, Australia in 1994. And for all of the 90s, they remained almost completely invisible." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=raRCZ0Y5up8
**Part of Indy Rock Revival** : The Vines were explicitly named as a band that, alongside The Strokes, The White Stripes, the Yeah Yeah Yeahs, Interpol, The Hives, and Arcade Fire, was a firm part of the Indy Rock Revival in the late 2003/early 2004 period. "The strokes were firmly part of the Indy Rock Revival that also included the white stripes, the yay-yayas, interpole, the vines, the hives, and this new band called Arcade Fire." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jEfofyobGiI
**Quality of Music** : Despite their frontman's erratic nature, the band's music was consistently described as good. "In the meantime though, the music was good. It was good." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=raRCZ0Y5up8
**Rolling Stone Cover (2002)** : In October 2002, the band was featured on the cover of Rolling Stone magazine, under the headline "Rock is back. Meet the Vines," symbolizing the perceived return of rock music. "In October 2002, they appeared on the cover of Rolling Stone. The headline was, Rock is back. Meet the Vines." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=raRCZ0Y5up8
**Single Release and Hype (2001)** : A single released in November 2001 generated incredible hype, marking a turning point in their career. "But then a single in November 2001. The hype was incredible." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=raRCZ0Y5up8
