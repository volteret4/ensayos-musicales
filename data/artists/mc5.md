## curiosities
**Detroit Origins and Radical Politics (Mid-Late 1960s)** : The MC5 was a Detroit-area group that emerged in the mid-to-late 1960s. They were known for being loud, profane, and highly political, embracing a "left-wing, super radical kind of way." "Another Detroit area group that made waves in the mid- and light 60s was the MC5." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U
**Dropped by Record Label Due to Vulgar Letter** : After releasing only one album under their new deal, the MC5 were dropped by their record company. This occurred because they wrote a vulgar letter on company letterhead and delivered it to record stores that had refused to stock their album. "Then they were dropped after they wrote a vulgar letter on company letterhead and delivered it to record stores that refused to stock their album." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U
**Enhanced Street Cred from Controversies** : Despite being dropped by their label for sending a vulgar letter, such stunts only served to enhance the MC5's "street cred" and rebellious image. "But of course, stunts like that only enhanced their street cred." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U
**Inclusion on First Modern Picture Disc (1969)** : MC5 was featured on what is considered the first modern-day picture disc, a compilation released by the German label Metronome in 1969, alongside The Doors. "a compilation of bands like the Doors and the MC5 released by a German label called Metronome in 1969." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B2g4QSREzpw
**Record Deal and Democratic National Convention (1968)** : The MC5 secured a "big record deal" in 1968. Around the same time, they performed at the "ill-fated" Democratic National Convention in Chicago, appearing as guests of the White Panther Party. "Somehow they were signed to a big record deal in 1968.
