## songs
**Jessie's Girl (1981)** : This song was the "upset" winner of the 1981 Billboard Song of the Summer. It is now regarded as a "new wave light pop perennial" that receives approximately 85,000 radio spins annually nationwide. Its "punchy guitars and synths" are said to feel most appropriate in the summer, and it notably reached number one on the Hot 100 the very week MTV launched in August 1981, a fitting detail given Springfield's concurrent career as a part-time soap opera actor. **Early Categorization in the 1980s** : Rick Springfield, like Tom Petty and Greg Kihn, was occasionally grouped into the early alternative, new wave, or Powerpop categories in the early 1980s. This classification was a result of the broad tendency to label any succinct, energetic rock song as Powerpop during a period of genre fluidity.
