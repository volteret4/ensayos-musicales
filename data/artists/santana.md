## curiosities
**2000 Sales Performance** : The band produced one of the biggest selling rock records of the year 2000, amidst a dominant pop and hip-hop music scene, indicating a challenging period for rock. "The biggest selling rock records of the year were from, well, they were from Creed and Santana..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc ← santana
**Demand for Quality Live Sound** : Santana was one of several prominent musicians, including Frank Zappa and The Eagles, who became "picky" about their live sound quality. Their demand that "they sound good on stage" contributed to pushing sound companies to develop better equipment and techniques. "A lot of this could be attributed to the pickiness of the musicians, artists like Santana and Frank Zappa and the Eagles demanded that they sound good on stage." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qrrwqdpCjpQ ← santana
**Early Life and Influences** : Carlos Santana was born in Mexico in 1947 and moved to San Francisco in 1960. He was influenced by his father, who played the violin in a mariachi band, and later by American music genres like blues, R&B, jazz, and Richie Valens's rock and roll. "His father played the violin and Carlos played with his dad's mariachi band." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 25 - The Hispanic Influence on 70s Popular Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-25-the-hispanic-influence-on-70s-popular-music
**Jazz Journey in the 1970s** : Santana embarked on a journey into jazz during the 1970s, incorporating more jazz than rock elements into their music, which consequently kept their songs largely off the Billboard Hot 100. "Santana made this journey into jazz in the 70s. In fact, a bit more jazz than rock and it kept Santana's music largely off the Billboard Hot 100." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 38 - The Influence of Jazz on 70s Popular Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-38-the-influence-of-jazz-on-70s-popular-music ← santana
**Musical Style and Percussion** : Carlos Santana felt somewhat restricted by the "blues band" label, as his sound incorporated diverse influences from blues legends like B.B. King and Howlin' Wolf, along with unique touches like the conga. The percussion, particularly the conga, was crucial to their sound, getting people dancing and being as important as Carlos's guitar. "Still, the percussion in Santana is an important part of their recipe is important, I think, as Carlos's guitar." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 25 - The Hispanic Influence on 70s Popular Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-25-the-hispanic-influence-on-70s-popular-music
**Planned Woodstock 50 Performance** : Santana was among the 80 artists originally confirmed to perform at the Woodstock 50 festival, which was planned for August 2019 to commemorate the original event. However, the festival was ultimately canceled due to numerous logistical and financial issues. "80 artists had originally signed up, including Santana, Dead & Company, The Killers, Jay-Z, Imagine Dragons, Cage the Elf and Miley Cyrus, Vampire Weekend, and more." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uEe_HLUay5M ← santana ← santana
**Post-Woodstock Impact** : After their electrifying eight-song set at Woodstock, Santana's career dramatically changed. A couple of months later, they performed at the Fillmore West to raise money for farm workers involved in the Delano Grape Strike, demonstrating their commitment to social causes. "It was all about to change after that eight song set at Woodstock." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 25 - The Hispanic Influence on 70s Popular Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-25-the-hispanic-influence-on-70s-popular-music
**Pre-Woodstock Recognition** : Before their iconic performance at Woodstock in 1969, Santana was well known in San Francisco, playing at venues like Bill Graham's Fillmore West and touring with bands such as Chicago and Jefferson Airplane. However, they had no records released at that time, limiting their national recognition. "They were well known in San Francisco where they played at Bill Graham's Filmor West and they had been touring with bands like Chicago and Jefferson Airplane, but they didn't have any records out." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 25 - The Hispanic Influence on 70s Popular Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-25-the-hispanic-influence-on-70s-popular-music
