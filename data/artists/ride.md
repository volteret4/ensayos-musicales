## albums
**Nowhere (1990) - Shoegaze Landmark** : Released in the fall of 1990, *Nowhere* is recognized as "one of the greatest albums of the original shoegaze era." Much of its material, including the song "Vapor Trail," was written by Andy Bell, who would later become the bass player for Oasis and BDI. "In the fall of 1990, they released No Where, one of the greatest albums of the original shoegase era." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vEHdoUlGurY

## songs
**Leave Them All Behind (1992)** : This song from 1992 by the Oxford-based band Ride was presented as a prime example that "ticks all the boxes" for the Shoegaze genre, embodying its characteristic loud, fuzzy, buzzy guitar rock sound with distortion and feedback. "If leave them all behind from 1992, and that's what you call Shoegaze, and had Grunge not come along and pushed Shoegaze out of the way, remember that Grunge threatened to swamp much of the domestic rock scene in the UK in the early 90s. Shoegaze might have survived." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Vapor Trail (1990)** : This song, released in late 1990, was written by Andy Bell, who later went on to play bass for Oasis and BDI. The track is representative of the band's sound during an era where they aimed to be "the Smiths, only a lot louder and fuzzier," likened to "How Soon Is Now on steroids." "This is called Vapor Trail. From late 1990, that's Ride with Vapor Trail, a song written by Andy Bell, the future bass player for both Oasis and BDI." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vEHdoUlGurY

## curiosities
**Discovery by Creation Records** : A demo tape from Ride reached Jim Reed of The Jesus and Mary Chain, who, impressed by the material, passed it along to Alan McGee, the head of Creation Records. This led to Ride being signed by the influential label. "One of their demo tapes reached Jim Reed of the Jesus and Mary Chain, who liked it enough to pass it along to Alan McGee, the head of creation records, the chain's label." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vEHdoUlGurY
**Formation and Sound Aspiration** : Formed in Oxford in 1988, the band aimed to emulate The Smiths but with a significantly louder and fuzzier sound, described as "How Soon Is Now on steroids." This blend of melodic sensibility with amplified noise positioned them firmly within the nascent shoegaze movement. "Ride came together in 1988, and they wanted to be the Smiths, only a lot louder and fuzzier." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vEHdoUlGurY
**Influence on "Smeared"** : Ride, a "noisy pop" band on Creation Records, was a significant influence on the sound of Sloan's album "Smeared," contributing to its loud guitar aesthetic. "like my bloody Valentine and ride and all those sort of noisy pop bands that were on creation records and everything." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
**Influence on Sigur Rós** : Sigur Rós comes from a tradition of shoegaze groups, listing Ride as an important influence alongside My Bloody Valentine. "Siger-Rus, which means victory rose, by the way, comes from the tradition of groups into Shugays, such as My Bloody Valentine and Ride." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
**Meteoric Rise and Decline** : The band experienced a rapid ascent in popularity but ultimately "lost their way after a couple of years" and eventually broke up. Their trajectory mirrored the relatively short-lived initial shoegaze era. "Ride had a meteoric ride up, but lost their way after a couple of years, and broke up." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vEHdoUlGurY
**Outskirts of Britpop** : Ride was identified as one of the "shoe-gazer bands" circling the outskirts of the Britpop universe, indicating their concurrent but distinct musical style during the era. "Circling the outskirts of this universe were shoe-gazer bands like My Bloody Valentine and Ride and Lush and Swerve Driver and Slow Dive." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DSvoppf1dGY
**Oxford Music Scene** : Ride was one of the "big-ish Oxford bands" that the Jericho Tavern, a local venue where "On a Friday" performed a pivotal gig in October 1991, was "semi-famous" for hosting. "the Jericho Tavern, which by the way was semi-famous at the time for hosting other big-ish Oxford bands like Super Grass and Ride and Slow Dive." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t-G22K0vLnM
