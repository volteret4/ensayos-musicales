## curiosities
**Cessna 206 Crash (2008)** : Ed Robertson, who obtained his pilot's license in 2005 and was featured in the TV show "Ed's Up" while working on a floatplane license, was involved in a plane crash on August 24, 2008. At around 12:30 pm, his Cessna 206 amphibious plane lost speed after taking off from Baptiste Lake near Bancroft, Ontario, and crashed into a grove of trees about 300 meters from shore. Despite the crash, Ed and his three passengers were all able to walk away. "On the afternoon of August 24, 2008, Ed took off an Ascena 206, one of these amphibious planes, from Baptiste Lake near Bankroft, Ontario." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hmnMG6f80Sg ← ed-robertson
**Crash Investigation and Continued Flying** : The Transportation Safety Board investigated Ed Robertson's 2008 Cessna crash but could not find a concrete cause, ruling out pilot error, mechanical malfunction, or weather. The only explanation suggested was some kind of "air current anomaly" that led to a loss of lift, airspeed, and altitude. Ed was praised for his cooperation in the investigation and, as far as is known, continues to fly whenever he can. "The transportation safety board launched an investigation but couldn't find a concrete cause. Not pilot error, not mechanical malfunction, not weather." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hmnMG6f80Sg ← ed-robertson
**Pilot's License and Plane Crash (2005, 2008)** : Ed Robertson obtained his pilot's license in 2005 and was featured in the TV show "Ed's Up," documenting his journey to secure a float plane license. On August 24, 2008, his Cessna 206 amphibious plane lost speed and crashed into a grove of trees about 300 meters from shore on Baptiste Lake near Bancroft, Ontario. Ed and his three passengers miraculously walked away unharmed. A Transportation Safety Board investigation could not find a concrete cause, ruling out pilot error, mechanical malfunction, or weather, suggesting an air current anomaly as the likely reason for the loss of lift and airspeed. "Ed and his three passengers were all able to walk away. The transportation safety board launched an investigation but couldn't find a concrete cause." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3eGBnwPWRGo ← ed-robertson
