# artist - Richard Ashcroft

## member of
- The Verve

## songs
**Bittersweet Symphony (1997)** : As the lead member of The Verve, Richard Ashcroft negotiated the initial sample use for "Bittersweet Symphony." However, due to Allan Klein's lawsuit regarding the sample, Ashcroft was forced to sign over all songwriting credits and rights to the entire song, receiving only $1,000 and no royalties for over 20 years until the rights were returned to him in May 2019. ## curiosities
**Discomfort with Legal Process** : Richard Ashcroft, along with Mick Jagger and Keith Richards, publicly expressed their discomfort and lack of intention to be involved in the complex and controversial legal processes surrounding "Bitter Sweet Symphony," indicating a desire to distance themselves from the disputes. "Tato Mink Jaer como Chris Riches en el pasado han hecho varias declaraciones respecto a todo este proceso legal tan complejo del que se han visto en versos y ellos no tenían ninguna intención de ver si en estos rollos si no lo consideran correcto." ← Music Radar Clan > Bitter Sweet Symphony： Quien fue su verdadero compositor？ | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CsBQFChCsuM&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc ← richard-ashcroft
**Grammy Nomination as Composer** : Despite the intricate legal situation that saw Allen Klein acquire 100% of "Bitter Sweet Symphony"'s rights, Richard Ashcroft, alongside Mick Jagger and Keith Richards, was nominated for a Grammy for "Best Song of the Year" as a composer of the track. This situation, where he was nominated for a song he didn't entirely compose and no longer owned, is a notable curiosity.
