## albums
**Generation Terrorists (1992) – Debut Album** : This 18-track, 73-minute album was released on February 10, 1992. Richie Edwards, despite being a prominent member, did not play a single note on the record, fulfilling other roles within the band. The band signed with Columbia Records just six days after Richie's infamous "for real" self-mutilation incident. "It was released on February 10th, 1992, and Richie didn't play a single note on the record, but that was okay because he had other roles to perform." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCwgvHg86Oc
**Gold Against the Soul (1993) – Second Album** : Released in the summer of 1993, the artwork for this album was inspired by a painting by Yukio Mishima, the leader of a radical Japanese political group who committed ritual suicide. The album itself explored themes of loneliness and despair. "A second album entitled Gold Against the Soul came out in the summer of 1993. The artwork for that one was based on a painting by Yukio Mishima, the leader of a radical Japanese political group." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCwgvHg86Oc
**The Holy Bible (1994) – Third Record** : This album, released in August 1994, delved into dark and heavy topics such as the death penalty, prostitution, political revolution, and suicide. The lyrics and themes extensively hinted at Richie Edwards' deteriorating mental state, including his depression, drug use, alcoholism, self-harm, and anorexia. "Gold Against the Soul was followed by a third record called the Holy Bible in August 1994, which covered topics like the death penalty, prostitution, political revolution, and suicide." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCwgvHg86Oc

## curiosities
**"For Real" Self-Mutilation Incident (May 15, 1991)** : After a gig at the Norwich Art Center, NME reviewer Steve LeMack questioned the band's sincerity. During a private conversation, Richie Edwards dramatically carved the words "for real" into his forearm with a razor blade, resulting in 17 stitches and significant blood loss. A photographer captured the graphic event, leading to an internal debate at the NME offices about publishing the pictures, which were eventually printed and generated considerable notoriety for the band. "I mean, who carries a razor blade with him? And he carved the words for real into his forearm. Blood gushed everywhere, but Richie insisted on talking." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCwgvHg86Oc
**Acknowledged Influence of Public Image Limited** : Manic Street Preachers are among the bands that have publicly acknowledged the profound influence of Public Image Limited on music. "Nine-inch nails, massive attack, manic street preachers, ministry, primal scream, they all acknowledged the importance of public image limited." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJGFUlQTUhQ
**Apparent Suicide Attempt (July 18, 1994)** : Richie Edwards' mental state culminated in an apparent suicide attempt when his parents found him in a bathtub, bleeding from his arms, intoxicated and drugged. He was rushed to the hospital, where he was diagnosed with severe depression and voluntarily admitted to a psychiatric hospital the following day. "He phoned his parents, who raced over to his apartment to find him lying in the bathtub with blood seeping out of his arms. He was stoned and he was drunk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCwgvHg86Oc

**Disappearance from Embassy Hotel (January 31 - February 1, 1995)** : After a final rehearsal with the band on January 31, 1995, Richie gave small presents to his bandmates, possibly as an apology. He then checked into the Embassy Hotel in London with James Dean Bradfield. Richie was last seen by James Dean Bradfield at approximately 8:30 pm on January 31, 1995, in his room (516), taking a bath and stating he would spend a quiet night alone, before he vanished completely by February 1, 1995. "James Dean Bradfield may have been the last person to see Richie alive. This brings us to February 1, 1995. What happened? **Final Days Before Disappearance** : Following the holiday season, Richie ceased writing in his diary on January 17, 1995, just three days after his beloved cocker spaniel, Snoopy, died at age 17, leaving him inconsolable. Coupled with band tensions and a relationship breakdown, he seemed to be on the verge of a crisis. He made four trips to a Cardiff bank machine, withdrawing £200 each time, spending money on mundane items like comic books, books, VHS movies, and flowers for his mother, whom he visited one last time. "By the end of January, Richie was teetering on the edge of something really, really bad. He also seemed to be up to something." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCwgvHg86Oc
**Growing Rift and Paranoia** : Following the Bangkok trip, a rift began to form between Richie and the rest of the band, marked by his increasing paranoia, as evidenced by his insistence on sitting alone at the back of the plane. He also began to identify with Pink, the burned-out rock star character from Pink Floyd's *The Wall*. "The trip to Thailand also seemed to mark the beginning of a rift with the rest of the band. Flying to and from Bangkok, Richie insisted on sitting at the back of the plane by himself." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCwgvHg86Oc
**Hamburg Incident (December 2, 1994)** : After a show in Hamburg, Richie was found outside the band's hotel in his pajamas, repeatedly banging his head against a brick wall until he was bloody, moaning, "I want to go home." This incident led to the immediate termination of the European leg of the tour due to intense band tension. "On December 2, 1994, after a show in Hamburg, Richie was found outside the band's hotel in his pajamas, bashing his head against a brick wall until he was bloodied." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCwgvHg86Oc
**Last Appearance (December 21, 1994)** : Richie Edwards' final public appearance with the Manic Street Preachers was at the Astoria in London, concluding a three-night stand. **Origin and Early Life in Blackwood** : All members of the Manic Street Preachers originated from Blackwood, a "south and bleak coal town" in South Wales, which had been abandoned by industry, leaving behind only betting shops and pubs. Richie Edwards described the environment as one of "unemployment, violence, and defeatism." "Like all members of the Manic Street Preachers, Richie was from South Wales, a south and bleak coal town called Blackwood, abandoned by industry, and leaving nothing but betting shops and pubs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCwgvHg86Oc
**Post-Rehab Fragility and Relapse** : Upon discharge from Priory Hospital on September 8, 1994, Richie was back on the road with the band two weeks later, but remained extremely fragile, cycling between hope and hopelessness. He maintained an unhealthy regimen of smoking 65 cigarettes a day, consuming 30 cups of coffee, and drinking half a bottle of vodka daily to cope. "He was better, but still very emotionally fragile, veering from hopefulness to hopelessness. He was smoking 65 cigarettes a day and powering himself with 30 cups of coffee to counteract the half bottle of vodka he habitually drank." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCwgvHg86Oc
**Priory Hospital Stay and Diagnosis (July 28 - Sept 8, 1994)** : Richie was transferred to the renowned Priory Hospital in London, where he was diagnosed with Borderline Personality Disorder, a condition that explained his difficulties with identity, relationships, and detachment from reality. He was prescribed a six-week stay, a 12-step program, and the maximum dose of Prozac, while the band continued to play gigs to cover his mounting medical bills. "On July 28, 1994, he was moved to primary hospital, a renowned rehab facility in London popular with the celebrity set." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCwgvHg86Oc
**Richie Edwards' Role as "Minister of Propaganda"** : In the spring of 1989, Richie Edwards was adopted as the fourth member and spokesman for the band, referred to as their "Minister of Propaganda." Despite having "zero musical ability" and mostly miming on stage with his amp turned down, his primary role was to attract attention, write lyrics, and contribute intellectually to the band's image and sound. "In the spring of 1989, the Manic Street Preachers adopted Richie as a fourth member of the band and spokesman. He was referred to as their Minister of Propaganda." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCwgvHg86Oc
**Richie's Declining Mental and Physical Health** : As the band's career took off, Richie's personal struggles intensified, marked by insomnia, alcohol abuse leading to rehab, and increasing isolation. His weight plummeted, and his self-harm escalated, including slashing his chest in Amsterdam and using a meat cleaver (allegedly to avoid performing) and cutting himself on stage in Bangkok. "Even as the manic's career started to take off, things were not good for Richie. He had always suffered from insomnia and used alcohol to get to sleep. That resulted in a trip to rehab." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCwgvHg86Oc
**Unique Rock and Roll Philosophy** : In the early 1990s, the Manics distinguished themselves by professing to have more in common with American hard rock bands like Guns N' Roses and Motley Crue than with bands from Manchester or the emerging Britpop scene. They openly bragged about wanting to sell millions of records, a desire uncommon among indie bands of that era, and readily signed with a major label. "The Manics were different for the early 1990s. They professed to have more in common with guns and roses in Motley Crew than anything from Manchester or early Britpop, saying that real rock and roll came from America." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCwgvHg86Oc
