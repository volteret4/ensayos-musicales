## curiosities
**80s-Style Keyboards** : Along with The Killers, The Bravery were instrumental in reintroducing the sound of 80s-style keyboards into rock music in the mid-2000s. This marked a shift towards greater sonic variety beyond the grunge and punk influences that had dominated the preceding era. "The bravery and the killers brought the sound of 80-style keyboards back." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-CT7yYXonIk
**Musical Influences** : The Bravery incorporated elements from Depeche Mode, The Cure, Echo & the Bunnymen, The Cars, and Duran Duran to create a sound that felt both new and familiar. Their music, released in 2005, prompted rhetorical questions about whether it was from 2005 or 1980. "They took bits of Depeche Mode, the cure, echo in the bunnyman, the cars, and Durand-Dorand to come up with something new yet very familiar." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
**Singer's Songwriting Career** : Sam Indicott, the singer for The Bravery, has gone on to write songs for notable artists such as Christina Aguilera and Shakira. "singer Sam Indicott has gone on to write songs for Christina Aguilera and Shakira." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
