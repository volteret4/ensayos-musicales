## songs
**Beer Can (1994) – CD Single** : Released as a single from the *Mellow Gold* album, the "Beer Can" CD single officially featured four previously unreleased tracks. However, it also contained a fifth, unlisted track buried at the very end, which was a raw demo version of "Loser." "One of the other singles from that record was Beer Can. And it was released as a CD single featuring four other previously unreleased tracks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zT6pRGYLWhw
**Loser (1993)** : This breakthrough song was developed with Carl Stevenson, whom Beck's indie label, Bungload, connected him with. Stevenson initially found Beck "a little weird" but became interested after hearing Beck's "doodlings with a guitar and a sampler and a drum machine." Stevenson encouraged Beck to rap like Public Enemy's Chuck D, but when this failed, Beck became self-deprecating, singing "I'm a Loser baby. So why don't you kill me?" This led to the song's iconic chorus. It was issued as a single in late 1993. "Anyway, the song was finished and issued as a single in late 93 and resulted in some pretty good things for Bex." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zT6pRGYLWhw
**Loser (1994) – Raw Demo Version** : A very raw and unlisted demo of "Loser" was discovered buried at the end of the "Beer Can" CD single released in 1994. This track provides insight into the creative process between Beck and Carl Stevenson as they "trying to sort things out," offering a unique glimpse into the development of one of the 90s' most enduring songs. "A very raw, raw demo found buried and unlisted at the end of his Beer Can CD single of 1994." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zT6pRGYLWhw
**Two Turntables and a Microphone (1996)** : A lyric from Beck's song "Two Turntables and a Microphone" was quoted to illustrate the importance of vinyl and turntables in DJ culture and the club scene. "As Beck sang, two turntables and a microphone." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GES0Ms_kaCI

## curiosities
**Backstage Rider – Modest Requirements** : Beck had a reputation for being similarly undemanding in his rider requests. He typically asked for two packs of Rice Krisps, a container of hummus, and some bottled water and soft drinks, reflecting a very straightforward approach to backstage hospitality. "Two packs of rice crisps, a container of hummus and some bottled water and soft drinks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z2hvUDtBxho
**Collector of Vintage Synths** : He collects old Moog, Oberheim, and Roland synthesizers, demonstrating an appreciation for vintage electronic instruments. "I had this conversation with Beck, and he collects old mugs and overhimes and rollins." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KF5MwJVYng
**Declined Mad Men Score Offer** : Beck was approached multiple times by the producers of the TV series *Mad Men* with an offer to compose the score for the show. Despite repeated invitations, he declined all of them. "When producers were pulling together the TV series that would be known as Mad Men, they offered back the chance to write the score. They offered in the gig multiple times, but he turned them all down." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aSk1XQHNkd8
**Lofi Classification (Indie Work)** : Beck, particularly his work on indie labels, was considered to be Lofi, aligning with the genre's preference for raw, less polished recordings, which gained prominence in the 1990s. "We had Dinosaur Jr., Cebado, even Beck, especially as indie label work, were all considered to be Lofi." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Turned Onto Synth Sounds** : Mentioned as an artist who has become interested in the resurgence of synthesizer sounds in music. "Beck has been turned onto these sounds." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KF5MwJVYng
**Twilight Soundtracks Contribution** : Beck contributed to the various *Twilight* soundtracks that accompanied the film series. These soundtracks featured a nice selection of tunes, mostly album cuts and outtakes, from a range of artists spanning different genres. "The Paramore, Lincoln Park, Collective Soul, Death Cab for Cutie, Tom York, the Killers, Editors, Metric, Block Keys, Dead Weather, Beck, Vampire Weekend, Florence in the Machine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM
