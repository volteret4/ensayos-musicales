## curiosities
**Anti-Nirvana Vibe and Organic Radio Success** : Hootie & the Blowfish were perceived as having an "anti-Nirvana" sound or vibe during their rise in the mid-1990s. This appeal contributed to the organic success of their "I Go Blind" cover on radio, as DJs in cities like Boulder or Denver picked it up and fans requested it widely. "It was sort of the anti-Nurvana and people started requesting it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9gdKha0PLg
**Cover of "I Go Blind" by 5440** : Hootie & the Blowfish covered 5440's song "I Go Blind." Although they had recorded it, it was cut from their first album. It later appeared as a B-side on a cassette single and was licensed for the "Friends" soundtrack. They specifically requested that it not be released as a single from the soundtrack. "They had recorded I Go Blind. It didn't make their first record. It was cut. But they said, you can have this song, but just promise us you won't release it as a single for the friend soundtrack." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9gdKha0PLg
**Early Fandom of 5440 and R.E.M.** : Before their rise to fame, Hootie & the Blowfish were avid fans of 5440 and R.E.M. They regularly attended 5440's shows at the 930 Club in Washington, DC, and, as a cover band, performed almost every song from 5440's "Green Record" and every R.E.M. song. "They're two favorite bands were REM and 5440 oddly." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9gdKha0PLg
**No US Airplay Royalties for Cover Songs** : As an American artist playing a cover on American radio, Hootie & the Blowfish did not receive radio airplay royalties for "I Go Blind." These royalties are typically paid only to the composers and publishers of the original song. Their only benefit came from mechanical royalties (sales) of the "Friends" soundtrack or the cassette single. "if you are an American artist on American radio and you're playing a cover, you don't reap any radio airplay royalties from that because it only goes to the composers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9gdKha0PLg
**Positive Relationship with 5440** : The band maintained a very positive relationship with 5440 following the success of "I Go Blind." They were genuinely happy for 5440's financial gain, toured together, and their drummer, Sony, made efforts to ensure 5440 received their due royalties. "The band were very, very happy for us. We did some tour with them... Sony was, came in with the billboard... he goes, you guys are going to get some money." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9gdKha0PLg
**Record Label's Failed Attempt to Stop "I Go Blind" Airplay** : Hootie & the Blowfish's record label attempted to halt the widespread radio play of "I Go Blind" in favor of their newly released official single. However, the new single "pretty well died" on radio, with stations opting to continue playing the popular 5440 cover. "They tried to stop it. I mean, they're new record just been released and they basically told Radio, look, shut that song down. Here's our new single. They released that single and it pretty well died." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9gdKha0PLg
