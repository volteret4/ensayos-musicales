## albums
**Coke Machine Glow (2001)** : This was Gord Downie's debut solo album, released in 2001. It was described as "pretty quirky stuff" compared to The Tragically Hip's music, featured "The Goddamn Band" (which included members from Dinny Resruund, Eric's Trip, and The Skyteegers), and was compared to the work of Tom Waits due to its spoken word elements, low-fi recording, and difficult-to-categorize music. The album reached number 26 on the Canadian charts. "Koch Machine Glow has been compared to the work of Tom Waits. Lots of spoken word. Lots of low-fi recording and music that's really really hard to categorize in Pigeon Hole." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Introduce Yourself (2017)** : This double album was a surprise announcement on September 28, 2017, with a release date of October 27, 2017. Although the sessions for the album had been completed in February of that year, its unexpected announcement led to speculation that Gord's cancer treatments might be working. "There would be a new solo album entitled Introduce Yourself on October 27th. Surely this meant that the chemo and the radiation and all the other treatments were working. Gord's well enough to make a double album." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Secret Path (2016)** : Released on October 18, 2016, this project encompassed an album, a film, and a graphic novel. Its genesis stemmed from Gord's brother Mike reading the story of Chanie Wenjack in McLean's magazine, leading to Gord's deep involvement in Indigenous issues and reconciliation efforts. "This became Gord's secret path, the album, the film, and the graphic novel." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**The Battle of the Nudes (2003)** : Released in 2003 during an off-year for The Tragically Hip, this was Downie's second solo album. The backing band was again his ensemble, now known as "Country of Miracles." The music on this record was characterized as "less conceptual and heavier" than his previous solo effort. "The music on this record was less conceptual and heavier." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**The Grand Bounce (2010)** : This album, Gord Downie's third solo effort, was released in the summer of 2010, seven years after "The Battle of the Nudes." It featured the "Country of Miracles" as his backing band and was produced by Chris Walla of Death Cab for Cutie. "The Country of Miracles was back again for the grand bounts and the producer was Chris Walla of Death Cab for Cutie." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Unspecified Final Solo Album (2017)** : This album, mentioned after Gord's death, featured 23 songs, each dedicated to a specific person. One song was directed at his brother Pat, a fellow Boston Bruins fan, reflecting Gord's lifelong passion for hockey. "A final solo album. 23 songs, each dedicated to a specific person." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
