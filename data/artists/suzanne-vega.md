# artist - Suzanne Vega

## albums
**Solitude Standing (1987)** : This album featured an acapella track that German engineers later used to perfect an algorithm. This algorithm was crucial in developing the MP3 technology, which could shrink a song from a CD to one-tenth its original size, making it suitable for internet distribution. "That was the song German engineers used to perfect an algorithm that could shrink a song from a CD down to a computer file one tenth its original size." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-CT7yYXonIk

## songs
**Tom's Diner (1981) – MP3 Reference Track** : This song was penned by Suzanne Vega in 1981 while at Tom's Restaurant, located at 2880 Broadway in New York City, a location also famously featured on the television show "Seinfeld." Its unique characteristics—being a pure a cappella recording with almost no reverb—made it an ideal reference track for the Fraunhofer Institute in Germany during the development of the MP3 compression algorithm. Engineers on the project meticulously listened to the song "tens of thousands of times" as they worked to compress the digital audio file to one-tenth its original size without introducing audible glitches, ultimately achieving a very good result. "Now I played that song because it was used as a reference track when the Fraunhofer Institute in Germany was working on creating the MP3." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Zniri9uSlc
**Tom's Diner (N/A)** : This song was the first ever to be successfully encoded into an MP3 format. Carl Hein's Brendan Berg and his team at the Froenhofer Institute chose it due to its delicate passages and big beats, which allowed them to easily detect flaws and glitches during the compression process. Its transformation into an MP3 marked a pivotal moment in music history, tracing back to the origins of the digital music industry. "Since it had so many delicate passages and big beats, Brendan Berg and his team could hear any flaws and glitches in the compression process." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MTDY6qT11Dc
**Tom's Diner (Unspecified Year)** : This song holds significant historical importance as the very first track to be ripped into an MP3 file. It was utilized in Carl Heinz Brandenburg's laboratory at the Fraunhofer Institute specifically to assess the accuracy and effectiveness of the MP3 compression algorithm during its developmental stages. "The first ever song ripped to MP3 by chief inventor of the MP3 Carl Heinz Brennenberg." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
