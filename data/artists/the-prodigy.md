## albums
**Always Outnumbered, Never Outgunned (2004) - Controversial Evolution** : Released in 2004 after a long period of silence and extensive touring, this album was primarily made by Liam Howlett alone. It received very mixed and polarizing reviews from fans and critics, with opinions ranging from enthusiastic approval to strong dislike. It was considered a controversial album for many Prodigy fans. The album represented a "sad but necessary evolution," as the hardcore techno culture in which The Prodigy originated had died out, and electronic music had moved in very different directions by the new millennium. Despite the initial controversy, the album's sound is considered "really current" even today, as recreating an album like "The Fat of the Land" in 2004 would not have made sense in the changed musical landscape. **Experience (1992) - Debut Album** : This was the band's first album, made exclusively by Liam Howlett, and was deeply rooted in hardcore techno. It was one of the very few, possibly the only, fast hardcore techno albums to achieve mainstream success and album sales, as most hardcore techno was single-driven. It sold millions of copies and was highly successful in European capitals where hardcore techno was established, such as the Netherlands, Berlin, and London, and to some extent Paris. It offered a slightly softened, more sample-heavy, and less aggressive version of fast hardcore techno, but was largely ignored or dismissed by electronic music critics who preferred more "intellectual" IDM, considering it sacrilegious. "It sold millions of copies and gave us an idea of what such fast hardcore techno could become if it wasn't so aggressive and was more focused on sampling." ← Music Radar Clan > The Prodigy： Electrónica y rabia que hizo historia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WWj6hkbXHGY&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc ← the-prodigy ← the-prodigy

**Music for the Jilted Generation (1994) - Breakthrough Album** : This second album, released two years after their debut, achieved immense success, sweeping across all of Europe, including Spain, and selling millions of copies. It emerged as hardcore techno was fading into niche circles and the Big Beat generation was rising, acting as a perfect blend of hardcore techno remnants and Big Beat culture. Unlike their instrumental debut, it featured more samples, less speed, and more spoken parts, making it a surprisingly complex album. Critics who had been resistant to their previous work could not ignore its relevance and were compelled to review it. The album incorporated a wide range of influences, including Rave, Dark Techno, German Industrial Music, Rock, and Hip Hop, and produced true anthems of 90s electronic music. **The Fat of the Land (1997) - Masterpiece Album** : Released in 1997, this album is considered The Prodigy's masterpiece, one of the best electronic music albums ever, and one of the most relevant albums of the 1990s. With this record, The Prodigy took a significant leap forward, creating much more complex music. A crucial factor was Keith Flint's creative involvement; previously a mere figurehead, he contributed to the album's creative process, introducing a much more violent, punk and rock-influenced sound, with heavily distorted guitars and even some ska elements. This brought a very interesting renewal to The Prodigy's sound and a step forward in the iconography of their music videos. It became the quintessential "electronic punk album" and the album of an entire angry generation, selling millions worldwide despite many radio stations refusing to play it and its music videos being censored.
