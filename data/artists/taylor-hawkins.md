## curiosities
**First Paid Gig Experience** : Taylor Hawkins recounted his first experience getting paid for playing music in a cover band at a wedding. When the band wasn't paid, his mother went to the event organizer's workplace and demanded their money, successfully securing their payment. This marked his first financial gain from music, making him realize the potential for a career. "my mom went down to the people. The guy that worked, where the guy worked and spreeb. I want to be on a money. Amazing. Oh yeah. Mom got your money? Yeah, my mom got her money for us." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mh-qg1izM98
**Jane's Addiction as Personal "Punk Rock"** : Taylor Hawkins was disillusioned with the "blah music" of hair metal (like Warrant) during his high school years. Hearing Jane's Addiction was a transformative experience, as he found them "disgusting," "awesome," and "scary," providing a raw energy that resonated with him much like punk rock did for others. "And then I heard Jane's addiction and it was like, dude, I could play like the dudes in the poster. But they were a mess and they were disgusting and they were awesome and they were scary." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mh-qg1izM98
**Neil Peart's Drumming Influence** : Taylor Hawkins credits Neil Peart's drumming from Rush records for influencing "any crazy fill" he performs on the drums. This highlights Peart's lasting impact on Hawkins' percussive style within the Foo Fighters. "A lot of like anything weird that I do on the drums is usually ripped off from a rush record, like any crazy fill." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mh-qg1izM98
**Orange County Music Scene** : Growing up in Orange County, Laguna Beach, California, Taylor Hawkins observed a predominant reggae music scene, where hard rock was "frowned upon." He felt compelled to hide his Rush and Queen records from his brother and his friends to avoid being labeled a "hesher." "everybody played reggae music. Which was where? Orange County, Laguna Beach, California, and everybody liked reggae." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mh-qg1izM98
