**Having My Baby (1974) - Controversial #1 Hit** : This song was ranked #1 on a 2006 CNN poll of the worst songs of all time, winning "in a landslide." It reached number one on the Billboard Hot 100 in the summer of 1974. The song immediately faced significant backlash, particularly from the women's liberation movement, which criticized lyrics implying male ownership ("my baby" instead of "our baby") and presenting childbearing as a demonstration of a woman's love. Paul Anka, who wrote the song when his first wife Ann was pregnant, responded to the criticism in a San Francisco Examiner interview, stating, "To hell with what they think there are people out there who like to go to bed and have babies." Peter Riley, writing for Stereo Review in 1974, called it "the worst," criticizing Anka's "grunts out the unforgettable lyrics" and "Elvis-like roar," yet admitted he found himself "vacantly humming it" exactly as he did with "Diana" years prior, suggesting Anka possessed a "powerful natural gift of communication." Odia Coates sang along with Anka on the track. As an adult, the song appears to contain a subtle message regarding abortion, acknowledging it as a legal option with lyrics such as "you didn't have to do it wouldn't put you through it I swept it from your life but you wouldn't do it." The song also topped "more than one... worst of the 1970s poll and maybe the worst of all time." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 48 - The Worst of the 70s The Music of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/

## curiosities
**Backlash for "Having My Baby"** : Paul Anka faced significant backlash at the time of "Having My Baby"'s release in 1974, particularly from the women's liberation movement. Critics objected to the lyrics, which they felt implied male ownership (using "my baby" instead of "our baby") and presented a woman's pregnancy as a demonstration of her love for a man. Anka himself wrote the song when his first wife, Ann, was pregnant and remained unapologetic about the controversy. ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 48 - The Worst of the 70s The Music of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Early Teen Idol Success** : Paul Anka achieved teen idol status in the 1950s, notably securing a hit with "Diana" when he was just 16 years old. ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 48 - The Worst of the 70s The Music of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Flow to America** : Paul Anka was among the ambitious and talented Canadian musicians who flowed south to seek their fortune in America. This was a common trend when domestic acceptance and popularity for Canadian artists often only came after achieving success in the US. "ambitious, talented Canadian musicians flowed south to seek their fortune in America." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6PGgNCaW4xo ← paul-anka
**Frank Sinatra Collaboration** : Paul Anka enjoyed a unique relationship with Frank Sinatra, who would occasionally ask him to write songs. One notable result of this collaboration was the song "My Way." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 48 - The Worst of the 70s The Music of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Pre-1970 Exodus to US** : Paul Anka, a promising Canadian artist before 1970, moved to the United States because the nascent Canadian music industry offered limited opportunities to make a living. "Neil Young, Jody Mitchell, Paul Anke, Leonard Cohen, all of our biggest and most promising artists beat a path for the United States." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U2kYleeMnl8 ← paul-anka ← paul-anka
