## albums
**I'm Your Man** : This album was being promoted in 1988 when IRS Records decided to relaunch Leonard Cohen's career as an alternative artist, leading to an ill-fated live radio interview. "And he had this album called I'm Your Man." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4xxgPX-aR90
**I'm Your Man (1988) - Breakthrough Album** : This album, released in 1988, marked a significant breakthrough for Leonard Cohen into the alternative music world, becoming a hit. It features the song "First We Take Manhattan," which showcases his characteristically low baritone voice. "Leonard made a strange breakthrough into the alternative world in 1988 when his album, I'm Your Man, became a hit." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7_ZmZxnSOnE

## songs
**First We Take Manhattan (1988)** : Featured on Leonard Cohen's 1988 breakthrough album *I'm Your Man*, this song demonstrates his extremely low vocal range, notably including a low B flat. Cohen's vocal style, described as more of a drone or "method acting" than traditional singing, conveys deep emotion. "And that record is this song, first we take Manhattan." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7_ZmZxnSOnE
**Hallelujah** : Leonard Cohen's iconic song "Hallelujah" has been famously covered by other artists, including Katie Lang and Jeff Buckley, who brought new interpretations to its rich lyrical content, transcending Cohen's own distinctive vocal delivery. "Just listen to what Katie Lang and Jeff Buckley did for Howelooia." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7_ZmZxnSOnE
**Sisters of Mercy (Year Unspecified)** : This song inspired the name of the prominent goth band The Sisters of Mercy and is cited as an example of Cohen's dark material that appealed to goth fans. "And then there's his song Sisters of Mercy, from which the band took their name." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI

**Disastrous Live Interview (1988)** : In 1988, while on air, the speaker interviewed Leonard Cohen, who was 55 years old and being relaunched by IRS Records as an alternative artist. The speaker, unaware of Cohen's stature as a respected writer, poet, and songwriter whose work was studied at PhD level, was unprepared. Cohen, dressed in all black and smoking "terrible, detained cigarettes," responded poorly when the speaker tried to call him "Lenny," blowing smoke in his face and sternly replying "Don't." The interview was short and unsuccessful, attributed by the speaker to his own lack of knowledge and propriety. "So he comes in and he's all dressed in black hair, black sunglasses, black clothes, black shoes. And he's smoking these terrible, detained cigarettes, these French cigarettes that's stunk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4xxgPX-aR90
**Influence on Goth Music** : Leonard Cohen is recognized for his contributions to goth, particularly the darkness present in some of his material, which resonated with goths, similar to Nico's influence. His song "Sisters of Mercy" also directly inspired the band of the same name. "And here's where we can give a nod to Leonard Cohen for his contributions to Goth. **Publishing Rights Sale to Hypnosis Song Fund** : Leonard Cohen's catalog was acquired by the Hypnosis Song Fund, making him one of "the great Canadians of old" whose legacy is being managed. "Hypnosis is bought publishing from... Leonard Cohen." "We have the great Canadians of old, whether that's Neil Young or whether it's Leonard Cohen." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pe40SqDrpZ0
**Vocal Style and Evolution** : Leonard Cohen is described as having a unique vocal style rather than being a traditional singer who hits melodic notes. His voice is a baritone that became progressively lower and more gruff with age, often described as less musical and more of a drone or "method acting" to convey emotion. He was known to reach a low G. In the 1960s, he was initially promoted as a more musical Bob Dylan. "It's a baritone that got lower and lower and lower the older he got." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7_ZmZxnSOnE
