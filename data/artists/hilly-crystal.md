# artist - Hilly Crystal

## curiosities
**Closure of CBGB (2006)** : Hilly Crystal was forced to close CBGB due to escalating financial pressures. Monthly rent had risen from $19,000 to $35,000, and the club faced a $90,000 lawsuit from a local residence committee. Despite court cases and an attempt to declare the club a historical landmark, the ongoing gentrification of the neighborhood and rising costs made it impossible to continue. "In the end, rent went up to $35,000 a month and Hilly had no choice but to close the place down." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Epz5Sn151bk
**Founder of CBGB (1973)** : Hilly Crystal, an ex-marine, opened the CBGB club in 1973 in the Bowery neighborhood of New York. Despite the area being an economic and social disaster zone at the time, the cheap rent allowed starving artists to live, rehearse, and create. Crystal established the club's musical policy, which fostered an environment where artists, "freaks, and weirdos" could do their own thing, leading to the birth of punk rock. "When an ex-marine named Hilly Crystal opened the place in 1973, it was in a part of town where nobody wanted to go." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Epz5Sn151bk
