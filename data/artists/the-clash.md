## curiosities
**Attendance at Ramones London Gigs (July 1976)** : Members of the newly formed Clash, including Joe Strummer and Simo Jones, were transformed by seeing The Ramones perform in London on July 4th and 6th, 1976.
**Backstage Entry Anecdote (July 4, 1976)** : Joe Strummer and Simo Jones, along with some Sex Pistols members, gained backstage entry at the Ramones' Roundhouse gig by throwing a rock at Johnny Ramone's window and forming a human chain to be pulled inside.
**Influence on "New Wave of New Wave"** : Groups in the early 1990s "new wave of new wave" movement mined their material for inspiration.
**Musical Influence** : Hugh Dillon's love for The Clash was a factor in his decision to travel to London when he left Kingston.
**Performance at 100 Club Punk Rock Festival (September 20 & 21, 1976)** : The Clash performed at Britain's first official punk rock festival at the 100 Club on Oxford Street, London.
**Record Label Hesitation in America (Late 1970s)** : Their record label was afraid to release their records in America due to punk's controversial image.
