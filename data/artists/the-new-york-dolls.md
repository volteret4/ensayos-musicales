## curiosities
**Commercial Failure and Impact** : Despite being a commercial failure and eventually breaking up due to drugs and alcohol after only two albums, The New York Dolls served a crucial purpose. Their outrageous image, bad attitude, and amateurish playing acted as a catalyst, initiating a unification of "musical weirdos" in New York. "Although the New York dolls were a commercial failure and were eventually torn apart by drugs and alcohol after just two albums, they served a very real purpose." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4
**Cult Following and Influences** : Despite being dismissed by many for their unconventional style, the New York Dolls developed a significant following among those who appreciated "weird things." They were seen by some as embodying the true spirit of rock and roll, where attitude and energy surpassed virtuosity. The band regularly played alongside other influential artists like Iggy Pop and KISS. "Soon, the dolls were playing regular gigs alongside Iggy Pop, and a band who took the makeup thing even further." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
**Demise of the Band** : After a couple of tours under Malcolm McLaren's management, the band began to fall apart. By March 1975, while on the road in Florida, the Dolls were nearing their end, as evidenced by surviving live recordings from that period. Shortly after this, the band was "done." "Short time later, they were done." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
**Early "New Wave" Designation** : In the early 1970s, some writers began to refer to The New York Dolls as "new wave" or "a new wave in rock," applying the term to artists who rejected the traditional norms of blues-based rock and roll. "some writers began referring to groups like the Velvet Underground and the New York dolls as new wave, or at least a new wave in rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
**Early Influence** : The New York Dolls were identified as one of the "ultra-fringy players" in the early 1970s whose music was outside the mainstream and only for "weirdos" at the time, but retrospectively recognized as incredibly important to the history of alternative rock. "People like Iggy Pop and Lou Reed and The New York Dolls... in retrospect, these are incredibly important people to our story." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4
**Featured in Punk Magazine** : The fanzine "Punk," which appeared at the end of 1975, covered The New York Dolls, among other raw, primitive garage bands from the 60s and artists in the burgeoning CBGB scene. "It also covered Iggy Pop and the Stooges, The New York Dolls, and even some of their raw, primitive garage bands from the 60s like Question Mark and Mysterians." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4
**Formation and Naming** : The band formed in the fall of 1971, comprising four former teenage gang members from Brooklyn who found playing in a band more appealing than petty burglaries. They took their name from the New York Doll Hospital, a real store on Jamaica Avenue in Queens specializing in doll repairs. "So taking their name from the New York Doll Hospital, which was a real store on Jamaica Avenue in Queens that specialized in fixing rare dolls, the New York Dolls began scary people in Manhattan in the spring of 1972." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
**London Show and Drummer's Death** : In November 1972, Rod Stewart invited the New York Dolls to London to open a show for him. During this trip, drummer Billy Mercier became intoxicated, and after being taken back to an apartment by two groupies, he was dunked in cold water in a bathtub and had coffee poured down his throat, leading to his suffocation and drowning. "But then drummer Billy Mercier got drunk. Two groupies hauled them back to their apartment, dunked them in cold water in the bathtub, and poured coffee down his throat. He suffocated and he drowned." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
**Malcolm McLaren's Association** : Malcolm McLaren, who was at one time their manager, started using the term "new wave" to describe cool new bands that weren't quite punk. "Malcolm McLaren, a one-time manager of the New York dolls, started throwing at the term when he was trying to describe cool new bands that weren't quite punk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
**Malcolm McLaren's Management** : After being dropped from their record deal, the band hired Malcolm McLaren, the inexperienced but ambitious owner of a small, unusual clothing shop in London, as their new manager. McLaren, who discovered the Dolls during a New York clothing show, envisioned a complete image makeover, replacing their spandex, makeup, and big hair with red leather. He also proposed using a big Soviet flag as a stage backdrop for live performances, a move deemed not particularly smart at the time and less engaging than their previous sexual weirdness. "He thought the dolls needed a complete image makeover. That would fix everything." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
**Malcolm McLaren's Prior Management** : Malcolm McLaren, who later formed the Sex Pistols, was the failed manager of the New York band The New York Dolls prior to his venture with the Sex Pistols in 1975. "In 1975, Malcolm McLaren was the failed manager of a New York band called The New York Dolls." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AymMybE33zU
**Mercer Arts Center Performance** : The New York Dolls played at the Mercer Arts Center, which was part of the Broadway Central Hotel and served as a hangout for "weirdos" before its collapse in August 1974. "The New York Dolls played there, and it had become something of a hangout for these weirdos." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4
**Musical Philosophy** : The band's music was characterized by short and raunchy songs, deliberately avoiding solos and complex arrangements. They prioritized attitude and energy over musical virtuosity, believing these qualities were more important than technical accomplishment. This approach was a reaction against the increasing sophistication of mainstream rock. "And as for the music, all the songs were short and raunchy. No solos, no complex arrangements." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
**Provocative Image and Early Gigs** : The New York Dolls started performing in Manhattan in the spring of 1972, with their first gig at a sleazy hotel in Times Square. They created a shocking image by wearing makeup, spandex, and fishnets, and professing to be gay or bisexual. This look pushed the emerging glam rock aesthetic from London "miles further," distinguishing them significantly. "They showed up wearing makeup, and dressed in spandex, and fishnets, and professing to be gay." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
**Unreleased Song "Teenaged News"** : The fanzine "Punk" was initially intended to be named "Teenaged News," after an unreleased song by The New York Dolls, before they found inspiration for their final title. "Now at first, this fanzine was supposed to be called Teenaged News, after an unreleased song by The New York Dolls." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4
