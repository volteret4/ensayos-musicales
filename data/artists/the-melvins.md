## albums
**Deep Six (1985)** : The Melvins contributed songs to the "Deep Six" compilation, which was released in the fall of 1985. This record, although it took over two years to sell out its 2,000 copies, was important for allowing the Pacific Northwest community to hear the emerging regional sound. "One of the groups on the album was led by one of the people responsible for distributing those legendary homemade punk cassettes by Zawsbourne and his band The Melvins." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xdn31jMOOA4

## curiosities
**Connecting Dave Grohl to Nirvana (1990)** : Buzz Osborne, a member of the Seattle band The Melvins, played a crucial role in Dave Grohl joining Nirvana. When Grohl, a friend, called him desperate for a drumming gig, Osborne, aware of Nirvana's drummer issues, provided him with the phone number of Nirvana's bassist, directly leading to Grohl's audition and recruitment. "Now Buzz was in a Seattle band called The Melvins, and Dave was hoping that he knew of any openings for a drummer. Now it just so happens that one of Buzz's friends was having drummer issues. He gave Dave the bass player's phone number." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1y2UuU-me_s
**Early Grunge Pioneers** : The Melvins were identified as one of the bands featured in the early years of grunge, specifically in the mid-1980s, contributing to the scene's foundational development. "In the early years, this would be the middle 1980s. It featured bands like the Melvin's..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tFBSrKMEZxI
**Early Grunge Sound** : The Melvins, led by Buzz Osborne from Aberdeen, Washington, were featured on the 1985 "Deep Six" compilation, contributing some of the earliest sounds of grunge, arguably the most important alternative sound of the 1990s. "That's some of the earliest sounds of grunge, arguably the most important alternative sound of the 1990s. And it started in the local Seattle Indy scene." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Influencing Kurt Cobain** : Buzz Osborne and The Melvins were highly influential in the burgeoning Seattle scene, notably to Kurt Cobain of Nirvana, who was such a devoted fan that he would sometimes roadie for them without pay. "Nirvana, featuring of course Kurt Cobain, a stoner and former janitor who was such a big fan of the melvins that he's sometimes rowdy for them for free." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xdn31jMOOA4
**Limited Influence on Alexis on Fire** : While a heavy band, The Melvins were explicitly mentioned as a band that none of the Alexis on Fire members, except potentially Dallas Green, were listening to as an influence, highlighting specific sonic preferences even within heavy music. "None of us were listening to the Melvents. Right. You were listening to the Melvents, right?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nEPT8ZVZ0so
**Potential Collaboration with Kurt Cobain** : Buzz Osborne of The Melvins was mentioned as a potential musician Kurt Cobain might have worked with on a solo project if he had lived. "Maybe people like, I don't know, Buzz A's, One of the Melvin's" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cS8k3PYU_Eo
