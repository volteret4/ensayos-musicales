## curiosities
**Decision to Break Up (2000)** : In October 2000, Billy Corgan made the decision to disband the Smashing Pumpkins. Despite their status as one of the most important, influential, and bestselling bands of the 1990s alternative nation, the band faced too many problems with their record label, management, and reportedly with guitarist James Iha, whom Corgan seemed to hold responsible for the ultimate breakup. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9T9wi0vEvc
**Final Concert at The Metro (2000)** : The Smashing Pumpkins' final show was announced on October 17, 2000, and took place on December 2nd at The Metro in Chicago. This club held significant history for the band, as it was also the venue where they played their first proper gig on October 5, 1988. The farewell performance was an emotional night that lasted four long hours, serving as a "grand finale" to their career. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9T9wi0vEvc

**Involvement in Professional Wrestling** : Billy Corgan's engagement with professional wrestling began in 2001 when he attended an ECW card and, after being insulted, had the opportunity to hit one of the wrestlers with a guitar. A decade later, in 2011, he established his own professional league named Pro Resistance Wrestling. This evolved into work with Non-Stop Action Wrestling in 2015, where he became president, effectively serving as their Vince McMahon in 2016. In 2017, he left that league to purchase the National Wrestling Alliance (NWA), one of the oldest pro wrestling leagues globally, founded in 1948, where he also serves as president, having successfully revived the organization. "In 2017 where he also serves as president. That's one of the oldest pro wrestling leagues in the world, having been around since 1948." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xc85sLj0Cnk
