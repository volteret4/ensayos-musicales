## albums
**Adore (1998)** : This album, released in 1997, features the song "Ava Adore" and is seen by many fans as the "dividing line between good pumpkins and everything that came later pumpkins." In September 2014, a super deluxe version was released, comprising "Six CD's One DVD for a total of 107 tracks." "A record that seems to be the dividing line between good pumpkins and everything that came later pumpkins for a startling number of fans." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UH3n1oQSOc0
**Adore (1998) - Commercial Flop** : Released in 1998, *Adore* was the album preceding *Glass and the Machines of God*. It was considered "something of a commercial flop," which made the record company reluctant to support another ambitious, potentially double album project. "especially since the last album, Adore, which came out in 1998, was something of a commercial flop." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_lSbUUfBNNI
**Gish (1991)** : One of the first three albums that established the "defining smashing pumpkin sound" characterized by "big guitars, loud soft dynamics, great drumming, and Billy Corgan's whisper to a wine to his scream vocals." "Those first three albums, Gish, Simea's Dream, and Melon Cully and the Infinite Sadness, found what became the defining smashing pumpkin sound." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UH3n1oQSOc0
**Glass and the Machines of God (1999) - Unfinished Rock Opera** : Intended as the Smashing Pumpkins' fifth and final studio record in 1999, this project was envisioned as a giant rock opera song cycle. It revolved around a rock star named Zero (renamed Glass by God), his true love June, and their collective rise, fall, and redemption. Band members were to assume these characters, with live performances presented as theater. The album was abandoned before completion due to bassist D'arcy Wretzky's departure (or firing), Chris James Eha's disengagement, and the record company's reluctance to release another sprawling album, especially after *Adore* was a commercial flop. "Its proposed title was Glass and the Machines of God. Never heard of it? Not surprised... The best guess is that glass and the Machines of God would have been divided into two halves of 14 songs each." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_lSbUUfBNNI
**Machina II/The Friends & Enemies of Modern Music (2000) - Free Online Release** : Released in the fall of 2000, this record featured much of the remaining tracks from the *Glass and the Machines of God* project. It was notably given away for free online, a pioneering move at the time, and had an extremely limited physical release of only 25 copies, whose whereabouts are unknown. "In the fall of 2000, the Pumpkins released a record called Machina 2, the Friends and Enemies of Modern Music. It featured much of the rest of the tracks from glass and the Machines of God, and was given away for free online. Again, nobody was doing this kind of thing back then, so it was really cool." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_lSbUUfBNNI
**Machina, the Machines of God (2000) - Salvaged Album** : This album was released in 2000, collecting the "best material" from the abandoned *Glass and the Machines of God* project in an attempt to salvage something from the effort. It won a Grammy for best artwork and was promoted with an early viral online marketing campaign encouraging fans to decipher its themes and storylines, though the campaign was abandoned unfinished. Despite critical re-evaluation, it became the second lowest-selling album in Pumpkins history. "In the end, Glass and the Machines of God was abandoned. The best material was then collected into a record called Machina, the Machines of God, in an attempt to salvage something, anything from the effort... Want a Grammy? For best artwork." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_lSbUUfBNNI
