## albums
**12 minutes since February 32nd (1987)** : This recording is cited as an example of Jandec's mysterious, spooky, and disturbing yet honestly made music, characterized by an atonal folk and blues style with an East Texas twang. It encapsulates the challenging nature of his experimental sound. "This is a 1987 recording entitled, 12 minutes since February 32nd." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
**Prolific Output** : Jandec is noted for his extreme prolificacy, having released at least 85 albums since 1978, all on his self-owned label, Corwood Industries. This consistent output, coupled with his reclusive nature, cements his status as an outsider music superstar. "I know that there are at least 85 Jandec albums that have been released since 1978 and they have all been released on his own Corwood Industries label." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8

## curiosities
**Dedicated Guitar Tuning** : Despite the often challenging and seemingly unstructured nature of his music, Jandec reportedly maintains that he carefully tunes his guitar for each and every composition. This detail adds another layer to his unique and enigmatic artistic process, suggesting deliberate intent behind his sound. "And by the way, Jandec maintains that he carefully tunes his guitar for each and every composition." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
**First Live Performances** : The mystery surrounding Jandec began to unravel in 2004 when he performed live for the first time at a festival in Glasgow, though uncertainty persisted about the performer's true identity. Since this debut, he (or someone appearing to be him) has made several live appearances annually. "The mystery began to unravel in 2004 when he performed live for the first time in a festival in Glasgow." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
**Mysterious Background and Finances** : Little is definitively known about Jandec's background; he is thought to be from Houston, Texas, with indications he was a failed author or a machinist. The source of his funding for recordings and his Corwood Industries label remains unclear, though hints suggest the "Sterling Smith Corporation," sharing a phone number with Corwood, is a stocks and securities company. "Outside of the fact that he was in Texas, no one knew where he was, where he got his money to finance his recordings and his record label and how he supported himself." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
**Reclusive Nature** : Jandec, whose real name is thought to be Sterling Richard Smith, is known for his extreme reclusiveness, often likened to J.D. Salinger. He has never given interviews, answered correspondence, or provided insight into his identity or personal life, maintaining an almost complete aura of mystery around his public persona. "The guy is like JD Salinger. He is unbelievably reclusive." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
