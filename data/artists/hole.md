## albums
**Live Through This (1994)** : The album cover features a model named Lailani Bishop, portrayed as a beauty queen holding a bouquet of flowers. The concept, which Courtney Love conveyed to photographer Ellen Von Unwerth, was "miscongeniality" (as intended by the band). The band's logo on the album uses a font remarkably similar to that used by Mattel for Barbie dolls. "On the cover, we see what seems to be a beauty queen with a bouquet of flowers. The model's name is Lailani Bishop. The concept Courtney Lovegate to the photographer, whose name was Ellen Vaughan Unworth, was to come up with a misconjuniality theme." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pwebM3TfSdg
**Live Through This (1994) – Established Hole as a Serious Force, Released Just After Kurt's Death** : Courtney Love and Hole had an earlier album and a high profile through her relationship with Kurt Cobain, but *Live Through This* (1994) established Hole as a musically serious entity in their own right. The album arrived just after Kurt's death, adding unavoidable biographical context to its already charged content. "There's nothing like success to spur imitation." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4Jvy4q40tuo
**Live Through This (1994) – Rumored Kurt Cobain Collaboration** : Upon its release in April 1994, *Live Through This* became subject to widespread suspicions and accusations that Courtney Love received substantial songwriting assistance from her husband, Kurt Cobain. While it is considered reasonable for a musician couple to bounce ideas off each other, the extent of Cobain's alleged involvement became a contentious point for those skeptical of Love's authorship. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CkSIErb0hxY
**My Body the Hand Grenade (1997) – "Miss World" Demo with Alleged Cobain Involvement** : This 1997 compilation of outtakes and demos features a demo version of "Miss World," a song from *Live Through This*. The liner notes state the recording was made in Rio between January 19th and 22nd, 1993, but do not list contributing musicians. It is rumored that Kurt Cobain played guitar and/or bass on this particular recording, given that Hole's bass player was not present during the sessions. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CkSIErb0hxY
**New Hole Album (1994)** : In early April 1994, Courtney Love was in Los Angeles engaged in promotional activities for a new album by her band, Hole, when Kurt Cobain disappeared from rehab. "Courtney, who was in LA doing promotion for the new whole album, was freaked out by Kurt's disappearance." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sKWgbxk46eM
**Pretty on the Inside (1991)** : This was the debut record for Hole, released in September 1991. It was notably co-produced by Kim Gordon of Sonic Youth and Don Fleming, who was known for his work with Dinosaur Jr. and other indie groups. Though not an immediate hit, it eventually garnered cult favorite status. "the result was a 1991 record entitled Pretty on the Inside, co-produced by Kim and Don Fleming, a guy known for his work with dinosaur Jr. and a ton of other indie groups." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI

## songs
**Asking For It (Undetermined Vintage) – Unreleased Version with Kurt Cobain** : An unreleased recording of "Asking For It" features both Courtney Love and Kurt Cobain. This track is presented as additional circumstantial evidence supporting the theory that Kurt Cobain may have helped Courtney Love write songs for Hole's breakthrough album, *Live Through This*. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CkSIErb0hxY
**Asking for It – Unofficial Version with Kurt Cobain on Vocals (1993, Rio de Janeiro)** : An unofficial version of this song exists featuring what is widely identified as Kurt Cobain on background vocals. It is believed to have been recorded at Ariola Studios in Rio de Janeiro between January 19–22, 1993, when Nirvana was in Rio for four days and Courtney was traveling with the band. This version did not appear on *Live Through This*; the only official outtake from those sessions known to exist is a demo of "Miss World" (on the rarities CD *My Body the Hand Grenade*). "It's really not that big a leap to assume that the version of asking for it that we just heard was also recorded during the Rio sessions." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hFo4S32xH3U
**Gold Dust Woman (1996) – Fleetwood Mac Cover** : Hole contributed a cover of Fleetwood Mac's "Gold Dust Woman" to *The Crow: City of Angels* soundtrack in 1996, which became a hit from the collection. The soundtrack sold approximately 3 million copies. "The hit was a cover of the Fleetwood Maxx on Gold Dust Woman by Whole." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM
**Miss World (1993/1997) – Demo with Rumored Kurt Cobain Involvement** : A demo version of "Miss World," from the 1997 compilation *My Body the Hand Grenade*, was recorded in Rio de Janeiro between January 19th and 22nd, 1993. During these sessions, Hole's bass player was absent, leading to rumors that Kurt Cobain contributed guitar and/or bass parts to the recording. This is cited as potential evidence of professional collaboration between Love and Cobain. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CkSIErb0hxY
**Teenage Horde (1991)** : A single released in 1991 from Hole's debut album, "Pretty on the Inside." This song achieved considerable success by reaching number one on the UK Indie Chart. "This song actually made it to number one on the UK Indy Chart." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI

**Courtney Love's First Husband – Punk Transvestite Singer Falling James Moreland (1989)** : Before marrying Kurt Cobain, Courtney Love had a drunken impulse-wedding in Las Vegas in 1989 with punk transvestite singer Falling James Moreland, the lead vocalist of the Leaving Trains (who had released albums on Black Flag's SST label). Moreland was the boyfriend of Jennifer Finch, who was about to join LA band L7. After sobering up, Courtney realized the mistake and they divorced within months. Moreland produced the first single for Courtney's new band, which was supposed to be called "Sweet Baby Crystal Powered by God" but was released as Hole — and the single was called "Retard Girl."
