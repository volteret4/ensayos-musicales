**Gary Moffet on Canadian Inferiority Complex (1981)** : In 1981, Gary Moffet of April Wine articulated the ongoing "inferiority complex" in Canada regarding its own bands, criticizing the perception that Canadian artists needed to succeed in the US before being respected at home. He questioned why Canadian judgment wasn't trusted independently of American approval. "Gary Moffet of the band April Wine was still talking about the perception that Canadian bands needed to make it in the US even in 1981. He said Canada has an inferiority complex because of it. They don't trust their own bands with enough respect. They don't perceive you as being really big unless you go away, improve yourself in America. Why should you have to do well away from home before your own people consider you a top band? That's silly. Canadians should trust their own judgment. They shouldn't need America's approval about something before they accept it." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 45 - Oh, Canada! Canada's Rock Music Industry in the 70s | https://www.ftr70.com/
