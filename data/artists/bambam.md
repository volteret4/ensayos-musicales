## curiosities
**Decision to Forego CZ Records Offer** : Despite interest from CZ Records, a small label instrumental in releasing early grunge records by bands like Soundgarden, BamBam made the decision to continue independently, which may have limited their broader exposure. "CZ Records, a tiny label that was instrumental in putting out indie records by early grunge bands, including Soundgarden, was interested in BamBam, but they made the decision to continue on their own." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Early Cessation of Activity** : Tina Bell fronted BamBam until 1990, just one year before the grunge movement exploded into mainstream popularity. "Tina fronted BamBam until 1990, one year before Grunge exploded." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_1NNvxgx8uA
**Early Seattle Grunge Pioneers** : Formed in Seattle in 1983 by Tina Bell and Tommy Martin, BamBam had an "unmistakably grungy sound" even before the genre was recognized, making them early architects of the grunge aesthetic. "BamBam, led by Tina, was unmistakably grungy in their sound. They had a decent local following, but for some reason, few outside Seattle took them seriously." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Exclusion from Grunge History** : Despite their pioneering role, Tina Bell and BamBam were never given the credit they deserved and are almost completely expunged from the history of Grunge. Information about them online is often incorrect, with some mentions even omitting Tina entirely. "Tina and BamBam were Grunge before anyone even started using that word, but Tina and the band were never given the credit they deserve. In fact, they're almost completely expunged from the history of Grunge." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_1NNvxgx8uA
**Failed Move to London and Deportation** : The band attempted to move to London, hoping for a more receptive audience, but due to not filling out the proper paperwork, BamBam was "immediately deported back to the States," ending their international aspirations. "Tina and the group made the decision to move to London, thinking that their sound would get a friendlier reception. It did not. In fact, because they didn't fill out the proper paperwork, BamBam was immediately deported back to the States." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Kurt Cobain as Roadie** : The group also employed a young Kurt Cobain as a roadie for a period, connecting the future Nirvana frontman directly to one of grunge's earliest acts. "It's important to point out that the group also employed a young kid as a rote for a while, and that kid's name was Kurt Cobain." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Kurt Cobain's Early Involvement** : At one point, the band BamBam featured a "rowdy" named Kurt Cobain, indicating an early connection to the future Nirvana frontman. "And get this, BamBam once featured a rowdy named Kurt Cobain." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_1NNvxgx8uA
**Matt Cameron's Early Involvement** : Drummer Matt Cameron, who would later achieve fame with Soundgarden and Pearl Jam, was an early member of BamBam at the very beginning of the grunge movement, predating its widespread recognition. "And yes, the same Matt Cameron who would later play with Soundgarden and Pearl Jam. He would soon leave the band, but it's interesting that he was part of BamBam at the very beginning of the whole grunge thing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Opened for Major Grunge Bands** : BamBam managed to open gigs for significant bands in the burgeoning grunge scene, including Pearl Jam, Soundgarden, and Alice in Chains, underscoring their presence and talent within the Seattle music community. "But still they managed to open gigs for Pearl Jam. Soundgarden, Alice and Chains." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Pioneers of Grunge** : BamBam, fronted by Tina Bell, was a Seattle-based band performing their "grunge thing" as early as 1983, predating the widespread recognition of the sound by a couple of years. Tina Bell is rightly considered one of the inventors of Grunge, as their sound existed before the term was even commonly used. "Tina was a Black woman who fronted a very grungy sounding band from Seattle called BamBam, and they were doing their Grunge thing in 1983, a couple of years before that sound really began to catch on." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_1NNvxgx8uA
**Posthumous Recognition as Godmother of Grunge** : It wasn't until the late 2010s that Tina Bell and BamBam began to receive the credit they deserved for creating the grunge aesthetic in Seattle, with Tina Bell now referred to as "the godmother of grunge" or even "the queen of grunge." "It wasn't until the late 2010s that Tina and BamBam started getting the credit they deserved in creating the whole grunge aesthetic in Seattle. If Francois Hardy could be called the grandmother of grunge, it's no stretch at all to call Tina Bell the godmother of grunge. Some even refer to her as the queen of grunge." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Reasons for Lack of Recognition** : Several factors may have contributed to BamBam's historical oversight, including Tina Bell being a Black woman fronting a punk-rock band in a male-dominated world, their sound being five years too early, issues with bad management, a record deal that went very wrong, or the band stopping at an inopportune moment. "Maybe because this was a Black woman, fronting this kind of punky rock band in a man's world, or it could be that BamBam sound was five years too early. Bad management might be another problem, a record deal then went very wrong, or maybe they just stopped at the wrong time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_1NNvxgx8uA
**Significant Early Career Highlights** : Despite their obscurity, BamBam played important gigs and festivals, recorded at the same studio where Nirvana made their first records, won local music awards, and even had The Melvins open for them. "It's very strange considering that they played some important gigs in festivals recorded at the same studio where Nirvana made their first records, won some local music awards, and had the Melvins open for them." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_1NNvxgx8uA
**Tina Bell's Biography** : Tina Bell passed away in 2012. Her son, an Academy Award-winning documentary director, authored a biography about her titled "Conversations with the Grunge Queen." "If you want to know more about Tina who died in 2012, by the way, there's a biography written by her son called Conversations with the Grunge Queen." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_1NNvxgx8uA
**Tina Bell's Departure and Tragic Death** : Tina Bell left BamBam in 1990 and quit music entirely, falling on hard times by the 2010s, struggling with depression and alcohol. She tragically died of cirrhosis of the liver in an empty Las Vegas apartment in 2012, with her body not found for a couple of weeks. "Tina left BamBam in 1990 and was not replaced. They continued as an instrumental band. Tina quit music entirely. By the 2010, she'd fallen on hard times, often finding it hard to find a decent place to live. She volunteered at a local church, but still struggled with depression, which she sought to treat with alcohol. She ended up dying of cirrhosis of the liver in an empty Las Vegas apartment in 2012." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Tina Bell's Experience with Racial Abuse** : Tina Bell, a barely five-foot-black woman fronting a heavy rock band, endured a "sickening" amount of racial abuse, highlighting the significant challenges and prejudices she faced as a pioneer in the music scene. "Tina had an especially rough time. She was a barely five-foot-block woman, fronting a heavy rock band, and the amount of racial abuse she endured was sickening." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
