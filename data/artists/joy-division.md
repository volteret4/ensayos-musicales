## curiosities
**"Unknown Pleasures" T-Shirt Political Controversy** : The band's "Unknown Pleasures" t-shirt became the center of a political controversy when Australian Prime Minister Anthony Albanese wore it in public. The opposition criticized it as undignified and attempted to label Joy Division as a "Nazi-loving anti-Semitic band," which the text describes as an attempt to score cheap political points. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0DFmS6MPbFI
**Band Formation Inspiration** : After seeing the Sex Pistols play in Manchester, Peter Hook and his friend Barney decided they could make similar noise. Barney got a guitar, and Peter found a bass. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=95clNVd0hYg
**Band's "Death Throes" (Spring 1980)** : By spring 1980, the band was described as being in its "death throes," amidst personal struggles of singer Ian Curtis. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qC0Ee4-dpnI
**Categorization in New Wave (Early 1980s)** : Categorized under New Wave acts who preferred "darker guitars" alongside Public Image Limited and The Cure. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
**Cult Goth Band** : While not a big mainstream act, their records were a huge part of the cult of Goth in the latter half of the 1980s. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Dark Sound and Tragic Legacy** : Emerging from Manchester, Joy Division was defined by a dark sound and the deep, dramatic delivery of singer Ian Curtis. The band's association with gloom and drama was amplified by Curtis’s suicide in May 1980. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f2KA97T4u08
**Developing a Signature Sound** : The band rehearsed in a room of a local pub where the drums and guitars were too loud, preventing Hookie from hearing himself through his underpowered amp. To cut through the din, he started playing his parts far higher on the fretboard than normal. This combination of "mistakes" ultimately advanced the cause of rock and roll. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=95clNVd0hYg
**Discovery by Rob Gretton** : The band, originally named Warsaw, was discovered by Rob Gretton when they performed as Joy Division at a talent showcase around 2 AM. Gretton was so impressed by their 20-minute set that he launched a campaign to become their manager and successfully secured the position. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**Evolution to New Order** : Joy Division later evolved into the band New Order. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oPgeSCy93bo
**Final Gig Performance (May 2, 1980)** : The final Joy Division concert was held on May 2nd, 1980, at Birmingham University and was recorded. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qC0Ee4-dpnI
**Formation (Spring 1976)** : The band was formed by Bernie and his friend Peter after they witnessed the Sex Pistols play in Manchester in the spring of 1976. Despite describing the Sex Pistols' performance as "terrible," they were inspired by it, wanting "to get up there and be terrible too." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oPgeSCy93bo
**Franz Ferdinand's Fandom** : Loved by the band Franz Ferdinand. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
**Ian Curtis's Last Appearance (May 2, 1980)** : Despite a seizure during the show, Ian Curtis recovered backstage and returned to perform an encore, which marked his last appearance on stage. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qC0Ee4-dpnI
**Ian Curtis's On-Stage Seizure (May 2, 1980)** : During the final gig at Birmingham University on May 2nd, 1980, Ian Curtis fainted on stage during the song "Decades" due to an epileptic seizure; the audience initially mistook it for part of his unique "herky jerky" dancing. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qC0Ee4-dpnI
**Ian Curtis's Struggles (Spring 1980)** : Singer Ian Curtis was battling drugs, alcohol, an affair with a Belgian woman, severe and worsening epilepsy (with frequent seizures and ineffective medication), and deep depression, leading him to consider quitting the band, moving to the continent to open a bookshop, and divorcing his wife. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qC0Ee4-dpnI
**Ian Curtis's Suicide (May 1980)** : Ian Curtis died by suicide less than two weeks after the final Joy Division concert on May 2nd, 1980. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qC0Ee4-dpnI
**Improvised Equipment** : Lacking money for an amplifier, Peter Hook and Barney figured out how to wire their gear through the tone arm of the turntable in a console stereo owned by one of their families. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=95clNVd0hYg

**Rob Gretton's Management (1977-1980)** : Rob Gretton managed Joy Division throughout their career, overseeing their two studio albums. He was with the band up until the suicide of singer Ian Curtis in May 1980, after which the surviving members reformed as New Order, with Gretton continuing as their manager. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**Signed to Factory Records** : Through the efforts of manager Rob Gretton, Joy Division signed with Factory Records, an indie label in Manchester that Gretton also worked with. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**Tragic Amplification of Darkness (Post-Punk Era)** : Joy Division was already known for its dark music. The tragic suicide of their lead singer, Ian Curtis, further intensified the perception of their darkness, adding a significant layer to their legacy within the emerging gloomy music scene. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
**Unconventional Playing Style Origin** : When Joy Division formed, Peter Hook, like other kids inspired by punk, made it up as he went along. He didn't know what he was doing or that his technique was "wrong" — his bass was slung far too low, his stance was incorrect, and his amp was severely underpowered. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=95clNVd0hYg
