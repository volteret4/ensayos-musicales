**Love Will Tear Us Apart (1980) – Posthumous Release & "Suicide Note"** : This most famous Joy Division track was one of their last, recorded in March 1980 by producer Martin Hannett at Strawberry studio outside Manchester. It was released on June 27, 1980, approximately five weeks after lead singer Ian Curtis took his own life. The song originated from an August 1979 jam session, with Curtis's lyrics reflecting the intense turmoil of his life, including worsening epilepsy exacerbated by various lifestyle factors, a failing marriage to Deborah, and an affair with Anaconda Rae. Despite its happy-sounding, major key arrangement that initially puzzled the band, its profound lyrical depth and Hannett's meticulous production make it a poignant reflection of Curtis's final weeks, leading it to be called Joy Division's "Suicide Note Song." "It wouldn't be released until June 27, 1980, about five weeks after singer Ian Curtis took his own life." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk

## curiosities
**Band Pact and Dissolution** : The other three members were devastated by Ian Curtis's death and had made a pact that if any member left for any reason, the band would automatically self-destruct. This meant Joy Division was officially over, but the surviving members chose to create something new together. "And they had all made a pact. Should any member leave for any reason, the band would automatically self-destruct." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vLZla5h4xKQ
**Band's Initial Puzzlement over Song Tone** : The other members of Joy Division were initially puzzled by the sound of "Love Will Tear Us Apart," noting its unusually happy tone for a band known for its dark and glum reputation. It was strange to them that a Joy Division song was written in a major key, contrasting sharply with their established style. "It was an awfully happy sounding song for a band with a reputation of being dark and glum." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk
**Dead Wax Messages** : Joy Division was known for etching cryptic messages into the dead wax area of their records. Examples mentioned include "old blue" and "this is the way," the meaning of which remains puzzling. "Joy Division was quite fond of etching something into their dead wax, most of which are pretty puzzling messages." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IIJySSo276k
**Early Career and Influence** : In the late 1970s, Joy Division was a highly regarded English indie band with no money, described as "skint" in the British way. Years later, they were recognized for being a groundbreaking band, becoming legendary and one of the most influential bands in the entire history of Alt Rock. "The Division is now legendary, one of the most influential bands in the entire history of Alt Rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vLZla5h4xKQ
**Ian Curtis's Personal Turmoil** : The lyrics for "Love Will Tear Us Apart" were deeply personal, drawn from the considerable turmoil in Ian Curtis's life. This included his epilepsy, which was growing worse due to drugs, alcohol, late nights, smoking bad food, and the stress of holding down a day job while being in a struggling band. His failing marriage to Deborah and an affair with a Belgian woman named Anaconda Rae also contributed significantly to his emotional state reflected in the song. "Curtis's lyrics were drawn from the turmoil in his life." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk
**Ian Curtis's Suicide (1980)** : Singer Ian Curtis killed himself on May 18, 1980, at the age of 23. He had been suffering from severe depression, epilepsy, and substance abuse problems related to his illnesses, and had attempted suicide at least once before. His deteriorating condition was compounded by marital problems. The day before the band's first North American tour, Ian stayed up all night watching TV, listening to music, drinking whiskey and coffee, then hanged himself in his kitchen with a clothesline. His wife, Deborah, found him the next morning. "Ian had been suffering from severe depression, epilepsy, and all manner of substance abuse problems related to his illnesses." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vLZla5h4xKQ
**Ian Curtis's Suicide and Legacy** : Ian Curtis's death by suicide cut short Joy Division's potential, but it ultimately cemented their legend. His untimely passing contributed to the band's status as one of the most influential British indie bands of the 20th century and beyond. "His death, though, spawned a legend, and Joy Division has become one of the great influential British indie bands of the 20th century." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4
**Influence on Mogwai** : Mogwai cites Joy Division as a significant influence, particularly among post-punk bands. "The guys in Mug-Y are highly influenced by post-punk bands, like Jordy Vision, Sonic Youth, and my Bloody Valentine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
**Lyrics Reflecting Relationships** : Specific lines in "Love Will Tear Us Apart," such as "why is the bedroom so cold and turned away on your side," are understood to be direct expressions from Ian Curtis, speaking to either his wife Deborah or his girlfriend Anaconda Rae, or both. This reveals the deeply personal and autobiographical nature of the song's emotional core. "Lines like why is the bedroom so cold and turned away on your side aren't just lines plucked from somewhere, he's speaking to one or both women in his life." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk
**Martin Hannett's Production Obsession** : During the recording sessions for "Love Will Tear Us Apart," producer Martin Hannett became intensely obsessed with the song. He meticulously recorded each instrument separately and spent a significant amount of time perfecting Ian Curtis's voice in the mix. His dedication was so extreme that he once called drummer Steven Morris at 4 AM, demanding he come to the studio immediately because he couldn't get the snare drum sound right, and frequently called the band in the middle of the night to hear the latest mix. "Hannet mixed and remix the song again and again and again." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk
**Strawberry Studio Recording Location** : Joy Division recorded their famous track "Love Will Tear Us Apart" at Strawberry studio, which is located just outside Manchester, in March of 1980. This studio served as the site for one of the band's final and most impactful recording sessions. "It was recorded by producer Martin Hannet in a studio called Strawberry, just outside Manchester in March of 1980." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk
**Tony Wilson's Vocal Suggestion** : Tony Wilson of Factory Records suggested to Ian Curtis that he try using his baritone range when singing "Love Will Tear Us Apart," in a manner similar to Frank Sinatra. Curtis adopted this suggestion, adding a "very old-school crooning" element to his vocal performance on the track. "Tony Wilson of the band's factory records made a suggestion. Ian tried using the baritone range in the same way Frank Sinatra would when you sing this song." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk
**Uncategorizable Sound and Style** : Joy Division, an archetypical British indie band from Manchester, developed a unique "strange spooky paranoid sound" in the late 70s. Their music was minimalist with Ian Curtis's distinct vocals, and live performances featured his "weird, strange, jerky" dance moves, influencing fans to adopt a dark 3-quarter length raincoat as an unofficial uniform. They defied easy categorization, being too dour for New Wave and not thrashing enough for punk, highlighting their distinctiveness within the post-punk movement. "By the late 70s, Joy Division had developed a strange spooky paranoid sound." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4
