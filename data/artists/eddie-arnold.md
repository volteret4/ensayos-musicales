**Texarkana Baby (1949)** : Released in March 1949, "Texarkana Baby" by Eddie Arnold was among the very first 45 RPM singles ever issued. This 7-inch single format was RCA Records' direct response to Columbia Records' introduction of the LP. "And amongst the first 45's ever released was this from Eddie Arnold called Texarkana Baby." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gP3oQeYEvso ← eddie-arnold
**Texarkana Baby (1949) – First Official 7-inch Single** : This country and western song, released by RCA on March 31, 1949, holds the distinction of being the very first official 7-inch single. Notably, its initial pressing was on green vinyl, following RCA's brief attempt at color-coding records by genre. "The first official release was this, Texar Cana Baby by a country in Western singer named Eddie Arnold." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VlIErdSSVOM ← eddie-arnold

**KRAK Radio Station Staple** : Eddie Arnold was considered a staple artist on KRAK, a country and western radio station managed by Allan Torbert. He represented the type of performer that would satisfy country music purists. "Eddie Arnold, Buck Owens, Porter Wagner. **Kickbacks and Financial Arrangements** : Both Steve Scholls and Eddie Arnold received money from Hill and Range for Arnold recording their published songs. Parker exploited Scholls's involvement in kickbacks to control him and took 25% of a $20,000 bribe Hill and Range paid Arnold. "Both Scholls and Arnold were given money by Hill and Range, for Arnold recording the publisher's songs. Parker had Scholls in his pocket, because he knew that Scholls was taking kickbacks, and could inform Scholls as buses at RCA, and Parker in turn took 25% of the $20,000 bribe the Till and Range paid Arnold." ← Episode 33： ＂Mystery Train＂, by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DSHTmHy3Jo0
**Parker's Management Team for Arnold** : Tom Parker managed Arnold, Steve Scholls (head of country and R&B at RCA) handled record production, Hill and Range music publishers managed song performance and publication (with kickbacks), and the William Morris Agency handled bookings. "Parker would handle the management. Steve Scholls, the head of country and Arvin B at RCA, would handle the record production." ← Episode 33： ＂Mystery Train＂, by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DSHTmHy3Jo0
**Rise to Stardom with Parker's Team** : Arnold became one of the biggest stars in country music, a success largely attributed to the management team Tom Parker assembled around him.
