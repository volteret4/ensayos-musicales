# artist - The Shags

## members
- Austin Wiggins (manager/producer)
- Dot Wiggins
- Wiggins sisters (implied two others besides Dot)

## genres
- Garage Rock
- Outsider Music (previously covered)

## labels
- RCA Records

## albums
**Philosophy of the World (1969)** : This album was recorded in less than a day, featuring 12 songs by the trio of sisters from New Hampshire. Despite being considered "not that good" by conventional standards, it earned legendary status and was praised for the sincerity and authenticity of its heartfelt performances. "The shags with the title track of their 1969 album Philosophy of the World, and yes I know it's not that good." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
**Philosophy of the World (1999 Reissue)** : The album was reissued by RCA Records in 1999 and has remained in print ever since, attesting to its enduring cult following. Its initial reception for being "so bad" paradoxically made it legendary, leading to its re-evaluation and widespread availability. "The cult of the shags is now so big that RCA records reissued Philosophy of the World in 1999, and have kept it in print ever since." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8

## songs

This personal subject matter is characteristic of their unique approach. "Dot Wiggins was the chief songwriter, and her creations had song titles like My Pal Footfoot, which was about her cat, and that little sports car." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8

## curiosities
**Comparison to Nobody** : The Christian music group "Nobody" is humorously described as "The Shags of Soul," drawing a parallel to their unique style within outsider music. "These are the Shags of Soul." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HcK4M7-02ik
**Critical Acclaim and Influence** : The Shags' album *Philosophy of the World* gained legendary status, with notable figures like Frank Zappa praising their work. In 1996, Rolling Stone magazine included the record in its lists of the 100 most influential recordings of the 20th century, one of the greatest garage rock recordings of all time, and one of the 50 most significant indie releases. "Guys like Frank Zappa started singing the praises of the shags." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
**Mention in Outsider Music History** : The Shags are listed among artists previously covered as examples of outsider music, alongside Wesley Willis, The Legendary Stardust Cowboy, and others. "On those programs, I covered Wesley Willis, the Shags, the legendary Stardust cowboy, Jandic, Captain B. Fart, Daniel Johnson, Florence Foster Jenkins, and even Charles Manson." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HcK4M7-02ik
**Parental Management and Recording Drive** : The band was managed and produced by their father, Austin Wiggins, who, despite being "dirt poor," purchased instruments and arranged lessons for his daughters. He then drove them to Massachusetts, believing them to be at their peak, to record 12 songs in less than a day. "By being dirt poor, he bought his children instruments and paid for lessons. And then he drove everyone down to Massachusetts in order to get his girls on tape while they were at their peak." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
**Post-Austin Wiggins Era** : Following Austin Wiggins' death in 1975, the Wiggins daughters pursued other endeavors, as their father's constant encouragement ceased. However, their album *Philosophy of the World* subsequently achieved legendary, almost mythical status, prompting its rediscovery and critical re-evaluation. "Austin Wiggins died in 1975, and without his constant pushing, the daughters went on to other things." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
**Worst Group in Recording Studio** : The Shags, a trio of sisters from the Woods of New Hampshire, were frequently described as possibly the worst group to ever enter a recording studio. Despite this perception, their material is regarded as the "garageiest" within garage rock and they were name-checked by musicians as far back as the 1970s. "The shags were the three Wiggins sisters. They were managed and produced by their father Austin Wiggins, who for many accounts was just a delusional jerk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
