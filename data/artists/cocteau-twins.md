## songs
**Lorelei (1984)** : This song is from the 1984 album *Treasure*. The band's sound was characterized by guitarist Robin Guthrie's use of effects and exploration of new sounds, combined with Elizabeth Fraser's gorgeous soprano voice. Fraser's vocals were often indistinct, seeming to consist of made-up sounds or "speaking in tongues," contributing to the dreamy and atmospheric quality of their music. "The cocktail twins with Laura Lai from a 1984 album on the 4AD label called Treasure." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vEHdoUlGurY
**Pearly-Dewdrops' Drops (1984)** : This song is from the 1984 3-track EP "The Spangled Maker." It achieved success as a top 30 mainstream hit on the singles charts and a number one indie hit. Elizabeth Fraser's vocals, delivered in her unique made-up language, prioritize atmospheric and sonic textures over lyrical meaning. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFLMkvJAaUY

## curiosities
**Artwork Designer** : Vaughn Oliver created artwork for the Cocteau Twins. "Vaughn Oliver created some great stuff for the pixies, the cocktail twins, and director David Lynch." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pwebM3TfSdg
**Elizabeth Fraser's Vocal Style** : Fraser was known for singing in a self-invented language, a style described as almost operatic or "angelic" by listeners. Her vocals emphasized atmospheric and sonic textures, with the lyrical content being less important. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFLMkvJAaUY
**Example of Quiet Dreampop** : The Cocteau Twins are presented as an example of Dreampop that demonstrates the genre's capacity for quiet, atmospheric, and textured sounds, characterized by abundant reverb and echo. This highlights the softer end of the Dreampop spectrum, contrasting with its potentially more serious or louder manifestations that might touch upon shoegaze. "Yes, Dreampop can be quiet like the cocktail twins and Mazzy Star, but it can also leak into the Shougay's world and touch on groups like Slow Dive and My Bloody Valentine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jhgZrwtYE2g
**Failed Coachella Reunion (2005)** : A reunion for the Coachella festival in 2005 was nearly organized, but the lingering emotional pain from Fraser and Guthrie's past relationship prevented it from happening. A planned 55-day world tour following Coachella was consequently canceled. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFLMkvJAaUY
**Formation and Lineup Changes (1979)** : The Cocteau Twins formed in Central Scotland in 1979. After a challenging start, they stabilized as a duo comprising vocalist Elizabeth Fraser and multi-instrumentalist Robin Guthrie. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFLMkvJAaUY
**Heyday and Decline (1984-1997)** : The Cocteau Twins experienced their most successful period from 1984 to 1994. The band's dynamic changed after the collapse of Elizabeth Fraser and Robin Guthrie's romantic relationship, though they released a few more albums. The group officially disbanded in 1997. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFLMkvJAaUY
**Immersive Dreampop** : The Cocteau Twins are categorized as dreampop, creating immersive music with heavy atmospherics, texture, and extensive use of effects and processing, while largely departing from traditional song structures. "Dreampop, like the cocktail twins, works. This is very immersive stuff, heavy on the atmospherics and texture with lots of effects and processing, and with only a passing nod to traditional song structures." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pLf3kUONewg
**Influence on Sigur Rós** : The Cocteau Twins are cited as very important in the dream pop influences for Sigur Rós. "Dreampop, the Cocktoe twins are very important here, along with the Verve and anything Radiohead did with OK Computer and Beyond." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
**Influences** : The band incorporated elements from Bauhaus and Siouxsie and the Banshees (referred to as "Suzy in the band she's in") and Joy Division, adding their distinctive style. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFLMkvJAaUY
**Musical Intent - Room to Think** : The band's core idea when writing and recording music was to create an experience that would allow listeners "room to think." This involved an enigmatic vocal style from Elizabeth Fraser and experimental guitar sounds from Robin Guthrie, leading to a strange yet deep and immersive listening experience. "When the band set out to write and record music, their idea was to give you room to think as you listened." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vEHdoUlGurY
**Robin Guthrie's Guitar Technique** : Robin Guthrie focused on achieving specific guitar tones, sounds, and textures rather than technical precision. He openly admitted to technical limitations but layered guitar tracks with reverb and echo to create a rich sound, effectively covering up his perceived deficiencies. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFLMkvJAaUY
