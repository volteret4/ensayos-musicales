She broke through due to the early 90s alt-rock explosion dismantling prejudices against female musicians, her embrace by old-school alt-rock fans as a spiritual successor to Kate Bush, and her music's quiet vulnerability resonating with the underlying fear and confusion also present in grunge, but from a female perspective.
