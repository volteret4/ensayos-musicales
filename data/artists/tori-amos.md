**Chromesthesia and Unique Playing Style** : Tori Amos experiences chromesthesia, a neurological condition where music appears to her as "structures of light" or colors. This synesthetic perception may have provided a neurological advantage, allowing her to follow colors in addition to sounds as she played. "Tori may have had something of a neurological advantage because for as long as she can remember, music appeared to her as structures of light, which is a condition known as chromatizia. This helped her play because in addition to the sounds of the notes, she could just follow the colors." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dJiSZ4X0F2g

**Expulsion from Peabody Institute** : Tori Amos studied classical music at the Peabody Institute until age eleven, at which point her discovery of rock music, including artists like Led Zeppelin and Jimi Hendrix, led to insubordination. Consequently, her scholarship was revoked, and she was asked to leave the institution. "Tori studied classical music at the Peabody until she was eleven, but then a problem. Tori discovered rock, zeppelin, jimmy Hendrix, and so on. And she became subordinate. Her scholarship was revoked, and she was asked to leave." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dJiSZ4X0F2g
**Prodigious Early Musical Talent** : Tori Amos began her musical journey at an exceptionally young age, starting to plunk notes on the piano at two and composing her own songs by three. Her remarkable talent earned her a full scholarship to the Peabody Institute at John Hopkins University in Baltimore at just five years old, making her the youngest ever recipient. "When she was two, she started plunking notes in the piano, even forming melodies that she'd heard once. By age three, she was writing her own songs. By age five, she was given a full scholarship to the Peabody Institute at John Hopkins University in Baltimore." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dJiSZ4X0F2g
**Rock Band Detour and Solo Transition** : Tori Amos had a brief detour into a rock band called Why Can't Tori Read, which secured a deal with Atlantic Records, though the project ultimately "didn't work." Her solo career was then launched due to a contractual obligation to deliver six more albums under that existing deal, requiring extensive polishing for her first solo release. "There was a detour into a rock band called Why Can't Tori Read, which had a deal with Atlantic Records. You can find their songs online, but that really didn't work. But because she had a contractual obligation to deliver six more albums under that deal, she went solo." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dJiSZ4X0F2g
