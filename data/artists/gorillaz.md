## albums
**Demon Days (2005)** : The second Gorillaz album, released on May 11, 2005, following the reopening of their website in December 2004. Produced by Danger Mouse, it featured a dark theme about the dangers of ego and a world descending into darkness, partly inspired by Damon Albarn's train trip from Beijing to Mongolia. The album explored hip-hop influences and followed a musical arc from calm to progressively darker, selling at least 8 million copies worldwide. "As successful as the first album was, this one was even bigger, moving at least 8 million copies around the world." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iVdTCqpd798
**Demon Days (2005) – Second Album Sales** : This album, released in 2005, sold more than 6 million copies worldwide. "That was followed by demon days in 2005. That one sold more than 6 million around the world." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z7glrAoXtkA
**Gorillaz (2001)** : The debut album, recorded in both London and Jamaica, featuring an experimental sound blending trip-hop, alt-rock, Brit-pop, dub, reggae, and hip-hop. Its release was heralded by the single "Tomorrow Comes Today" and launched Phase 1: Celebrity Take Down. The album sold approximately 1 million copies in the UK, over 2 million in North America, and more than 7 million worldwide. "That single propelled the album to sales of about a million at home in the UK, over two million in North America, and beyond seven million worldwide, which is not a bad start for a fake band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iVdTCqpd798
**Gorillaz (2001) – Debut Album Sales** : This debut album, released in 2001, sold over 10 million copies worldwide. "But their debut album didn't come out until 2001. And it sold, wait for it, more than 10 million copies worldwide." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z7glrAoXtkA
**Humans (2017)** : The fifth Gorillaz album, released on April 28, 2017, after a seven-year hiatus and a prolonged social media buildup. It featured numerous collaborators, with some notable artists declining to participate or having their contributions unused. "After seven long years, the fifth album was finally ready. Humans was released on April 28th, 2017 after a long, long buildup on social media." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iVdTCqpd798
**Plastic Beach (2010)** : The third album, initially titled "Carousel," was released on March 3, 2010. Its final title and concept were inspired by Damon Albarn's depression over plastic trash on a beach, tying into the environmental issue of the Great Pacific Garbage Patch. The album's music was designed to be much more pop-oriented than its predecessors and featured an extensive list of collaborators. "As for the music, it's much more pop than the first two records. That was by design from the outset." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iVdTCqpd798
**Subsequent Albums – Declining Sales** : Albums released after *Demon Days* have experienced substantially lower sales, a trend observed with almost every other rock band globally. "Since then though, subsequent albums have sold substantially less, just like we've seen with just about every other rock band on the planet." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z7glrAoXtkA
**The Fall (2010)** : A fan club album recorded entirely by Damon Albarn on his iPad during the 2010 World Tour, released during the "Phase 4: Humans After All" era. "This era included a fan club album entitled The Fall, which was recorded entirely by Damon on his iPad during that 2010 World Tour." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iVdTCqpd798

**Creation and Concept** : Formed by Damon Albarn and Jamie Hewlett in 1997, the band originated from their observations of MTV artists' perceived vacuity and lack of substance. The idea was to create a "fake band" with fictional characters to comment on contemporary music. "Bloody hell, somebody said, we should just form a fake band. You make some music, all draw the characters." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iVdTCqpd798
**Damon Albarn and Jamie Hewlett Dispute (Post-2010)** : After "The Fall" album, Damon Albarn and Jamie Hewlett had a falling out and did not work together for almost four years. Hewlett reportedly felt his comic creations were taking a backseat to the music's increasing scale. "Jamie, the music was getting bigger while his comic creations were taking more of a back seat. Didn't like that very much." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iVdTCqpd798
