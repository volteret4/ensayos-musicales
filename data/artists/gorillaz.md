## albums
**Demon Days (2005)** : The second Gorillaz album, released on May 11, 2005, following the reopening of their website in December 2004. Produced by Danger Mouse, it featured a dark theme about the dangers of ego and a world descending into darkness, partly inspired by Damon Albarn's train trip from Beijing to Mongolia. The album explored hip-hop influences and followed a musical arc from calm to progressively darker, selling at least 8 million copies worldwide. **Gorillaz (2001)** : The debut album, recorded in both London and Jamaica, featuring an experimental sound blending trip-hop, alt-rock, Brit-pop, dub, reggae, and hip-hop. Its release was heralded by the single "Tomorrow Comes Today" and launched Phase 1: Celebrity Take Down. The album sold approximately 1 million copies in the UK, over 2 million in North America, and more than 7 million worldwide.
