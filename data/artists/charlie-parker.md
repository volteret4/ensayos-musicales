## curiosities
**Bebop Co-Inventor** : Charlie Parker was one of the musicians, alongside Dizzy Gillespie, who were inventing a new form of jazz, later known as bebop or bop, in New York between 1942 and 1944. This form, which occurred during the Musicians' Union Strike, is considered some of the most important music of the 20th century, though its birth was not recorded. **Defining Figure of Bebop** : Charlie Parker is cited as a key musician whose work defined bebop jazz. This genre originated in New York in the mid-1940s and was characterized by "harmonic complexity, instrumental virtuosity, and individual self-expression." **Pioneer of Bebop Jazz** : Charlie Parker was a key figure in the development of bebop, a splintering of the traditional jazz sound that emerged as ambitious musicians sought more improvisational and complex forms. Bebop was characterized by experimentalism and virtuosity, created by musicians for musicians, and was not typically suitable for dancing or singing along. **Use of Devil's Triad in Jazz (1950s)** : Charlie Parker, a pioneering figure in jazz, is identified as one of the players who extensively used the "devil's tri tone" (flattened fifth) in his music, particularly during the 1950s. This highlights jazz's embrace of dissonance and complex harmonies for its expressive and innovative qualities.
