## curiosities
**Pioneer of Bebop Jazz** : Charlie Parker was a key figure in the development of bebop, a splintering of the traditional jazz sound that emerged as ambitious musicians sought more improvisational and complex forms. Bebop was characterized by experimentalism and virtuosity, created by musicians for musicians, and was not typically suitable for dancing or singing along. "Charlie Parker, Sonny Rollins, Dexter Gordon, Miles Davis, Dizzy Gillespie, Thelonius Monk. **Use of Devil's Triad in Jazz (1950s)** : Charlie Parker, a pioneering figure in jazz, is identified as one of the players who extensively used the "devil's tri tone" (flattened fifth) in his music, particularly during the 1950s. This highlights jazz's embrace of dissonance and complex harmonies for its expressive and innovative qualities.
