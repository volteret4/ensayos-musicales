## curiosities
**"Terrible" Pop Music of Late 1980s** : This group was named as an example of the "absolutely terrible" pop music prevalent at the end of the 1980s, which contributed to Generation X's dire experience as rock fans. "New kids on the block, Debbie Gibson, Tiffany, if you were part of Generation X, the children of the baby boomers, it was a pretty dire time to be a rock fan." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tFBSrKMEZxI
**Aging Out of Pop Phase (1990)** : The generation of kids who were fans of New Kids on the Block had aged out of their pop phase by 1990, contributing to the decline of pop music. "The generation of kids who were into new kids in the block and Tiffany and Debbie Gibson had aged out of their pop phase." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Rf1tFWQg51Y
**Mainstream Pop (Late 1980s)** : New Kids on the Block represented the kind of mainstream pop music that fans of alternative and non-mainstream rock actively avoided at the end of the 1980s. Their music was commonly found on Top 40 radio, which was antithetical to the alternative music scene's sensibilities. "You want nothing to do with hair metal or classic rock or new kids on the block or anything on top 40 radio." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**Pop Dominance (1988)** : In 1988, the future of music appeared to belong to pop acts like New Kids on the Block, signaling a low point for rock music and prompting "rock is dead" cries. "It looked especially bad through 1988 when it looked as if the future belonged to new kids on the block..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Pop Dominance in Early 1990s** : New Kids on the Block were among the leading pop acts that characterized the music scene at the very beginning of the 1990s, before the cultural shift away from "Happy-Go-Lucky Pop" began in 1990-1991. "At the very beginning of the decade, pop music ruled Michael Jackson, Janet Jackson, Maddon, Millie Vanilley, and new kids on the block." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Y6nHw5xx88
