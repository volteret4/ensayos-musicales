## songs
**Hey Man Nice Shot (1995)** : This song by Filter, released in July 1995, is about the public suicide of R. Budd Dwyer, the Pennsylvania state treasurer, on January 22, 1987. Dwyer, convicted of bribery, shot himself in the mouth during a press conference while declaring his innocence. Richard Patrick's lyrics address this event, clarifying that the song is not, as some believed, about Kurt Cobain's suicide. "Now you know what Richard Patrick is talking about. It's not the suicide of Kurt Cobain, which is what some people said. It's about the death of our Bud Dwyer." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cM3-tINKdvo
**Hey Man, Nice Shot (1995)** : This song is inspired by the suicide of R. Budd Dwyer, the State Treasurer of Pennsylvania. Dwyer shot himself in the mouth with a .357 magnum during a televised press conference on January 23, 1987, the morning before his sentencing for bribery. His conviction stemmed from accepting a $300,000 bribe from a company called CTA, which had won a contract to sort out a state tax problem. The suicide was caught on video by at least five cameras and widely broadcast, sparking a debate about appropriate news coverage. Richard Patrick's song is a direct reference to this event and not, as some speculated, about Kurt Cobain's suicide. "Now you know what Richard Patrick is talking about. It's not the suicide of Kurt Cobain, which is what some people said. It's about the death of our But Dwyer." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4PlieAEthSg

## curiosities
**Inspired by Skinny Puppy** : Filter is explicitly listed as an artist who was inspired and influenced by the Canadian industrial band Skinny Puppy. "Here's a short list of artists who say they were inspired and influenced by Skinny Puppy, Ministry, Nine-inch Nails, Maryland Manson, the Prodigy, Ramstein, Filter, Corn, Lincoln Park, Evan Essence." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE
**The Crow: City of Angels Soundtrack Contribution (1996)** : Filter was featured on the soundtrack for *The Crow: City of Angels*, the 1996 sequel to *The Crow*. This collection was considered "pretty much as good as the first one" and included other prominent bands of the era. "Bush, Corn, Deftones, E-Pop, White Zombie, Filter." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM
**The Crow: Salvation Soundtrack Contribution (2000)** : Filter contributed to *The Crow: Salvation* soundtrack, released in the spring of 2000. This album was associated with the sequel to *The Crow: City of Angels*, although the movie itself did not appear until 2004 and went straight to DVD. "More Hole, More Filter, Monster Magnet, Stabbing Westward, Kid Rock, Crystal Method." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM
