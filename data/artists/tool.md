## albums
**10,000 Days (2006)** : This album was released by Tool in 2006. The 13-year, five-month, and three-day gap between "10,000 Days" and "Fear Inoculum" meant that when the former was released, the iPhone had not yet been released, Google had not yet bought YouTube, and MySpace was still a prominent social media platform. "When Tool released the 10,000 days album back in 2006, the iPhone hadn't been released yet." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HSvgRyGiOpE
**Fear Inoculum (2019)** : Released on August 20, 2019, this album marked Tool's highly anticipated comeback after a 13-year, five-month, and three-day gap between studio albums. It featured seven songs running over 79 minutes and became a number one album, cementing Tool's status as a significant pre-internet era rock act. "Eventually, though, we did get the Fear Inoculum album on August 20, 2019." "Seven songs running over 79 minutes, and it was all very good." "And yes, it was a number one album." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HSvgRyGiOpE

This was the biggest and most anticipated comeback of the 2010s, a period marked by legal issues, distractions with other projects, and significant "second guessing within the band." "there was no bigger and no more anticipated comeback than what we saw with Tool, who ended a gap between albums that lasted 13 years, five months and three days." "Why the delay? **Lachrymology – Tool's Invented Pseudo-Science of Crying** : Tool promotes a fake philosophy called "lachrymology" (the study of crying), supposedly derived from a 1940s book called *A Joyful Guide to Lachrymology* by one Ronald P. Vincent — a former crop duster turned philosopher after his wife was "dismembered in a bizarre snowplow accident." The only source for the existence of this book and its author is Tool themselves, who have never confirmed or denied the hoax. The core thesis — that humans cannot evolve emotionally, spiritually, or mentally without experiencing pain, and that releasing repressed trauma (possibly through crying) improves mental, spiritual, and physical condition — has been elaborated by fans into something resembling Freudian psychoanalysis. "Tool maintains that they discovered this science through the writings of Ronald P. Vincent, who wrote a book back in the 1940s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BrQQHqbQG0Y
**Math Rocky Approach to Progressive Rock** : Tool's music is noted for its "math rocky approach" within the broader context of progressive rock. This aligns with the 2010s resurgence of complex, hard-to-play rock, often involving multi-string guitars and intricate arrangements. "We've already talked about tool and the math rocky approach." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HSvgRyGiOpE
**Pre-Internet Era Rock Act** : Tool is characterized as one of the more interesting rock acts from the "pre-internet era," having established their career and many big hits before the internet disrupted the music industry. They, like other major rock bands of that era, maintained the ability to fill arenas and amphitheaters. "They continue to be one of the more interesting rock acts from the pre-internet era." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HSvgRyGiOpE
