## albums
**Come Away With Me (2002) - Debut Album** : Released on Blue Note Records, this was Nora Jones' "juggernaut debut album" that came out in February 2002. Before its official release, actress Sandra Bullock had an advanced copy and sought to include music from it in her movie "Two Weeks Notice." The album's rise to popularity was described as a "slow study rise." Nora wrote only two or three songs on the album, with the rest contributed by Jesse Harris, Lee Alexander, and various covers. The initial recording session for the album with producer Craig Street involved recording 21 songs in a week and mixing them immediately, but the album was rejected due to perceived issues with the mixes. Ultimately, three "amazing takes" from this session were used on the final record. **Artistic Evolution and Avoiding Pigeonholing** : Nora Jones expressed excitement about continuously "finding stuff" and getting "into songwriting" after her debut album, where she only wrote a few songs. She was eager to avoid being "pigeonholed" into a specific sound like a piano trio and deliberately explored new genres, such as bluegrass, which influenced her second album to be "a little more country." Don Was compared her artistic evolution to Miles Davis, noting how she, like Miles, always sounded like herself but kept it interesting by "changing the textures." "Did the success of come away with me? Were you trying to avoid getting pigeonholed into piano, trio? **Blue Note Record Deal Negotiation** : Nora Jones secured a meeting with Bruce Lentval, the head of Blue Note Records, after an EMI publishing contact heard her play at her 21st birthday jazz brunch gig at The Garage on 7th Avenue. **Early Gigs and Songwriting Inspiration in NYC** : After moving to New York City and dropping out of college, Nora took restaurant gigs, which paid less than her previous job in Texas. She was inspired by songwriter friends performing at The Living Room, which prompted her to start writing songs again after a long hiatus since a high school synthesizer class. Her mother sent her an old guitar, on which she learned simple chords and wrote her breakthrough song, "Come Away With Me." **Mimicking and Finding Her Authentic Voice** : Growing up, Nora Jones learned by mimicking her favorite singers, including Aretha Franklin (especially harmonies for "A Natural Woman") and Billie Holiday (whom she portrayed in a high school program, singing "Strange Fruit" and actively trying to emulate her sound). **Pianist Skills and Live Performance Preference** : Don Was praised Nora Jones as an "incredible pianist" with "incredibly sophisticated" voicings and a deep understanding of theory, scales, and modes, though she admits to not having a lot of "chops" or playing very fast. She feels she has gotten "a lot better in the last 10 years" due to playing more piano live, often in a trio format. She finds it challenging to sing without accompanying herself, stating she's "not great at it" and "don't know what to do with my hands," feeling more connected to her performance when playing and singing simultaneously. When recording, she gets a better vocal when singing and playing live together, as overdubbing tends to lead to overthinking and overplaying.
