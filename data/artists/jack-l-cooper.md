## curiosities
**First Professional Radio DJ** : Jack L. Cooper, a Black former boxer and semi-pro baseball player, became the host of "The All Negro Hour" on WBSC in Chicago, first broadcast on November 3rd, 1929. As his show grew in popularity, expanding to nine and a half hours a week by mid-1936, Cooper potentially became the first ever professional radio DJ by playing his personal collection of jazz and gospel records on air using his own phonograph. "He brought in his phonograph and collection of jazz and gospel records, which Cooper played on the air, and this would make Cooper the first ever professional radio DJ." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
