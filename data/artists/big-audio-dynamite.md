# artist - Big Audio Dynamite

## members

## songs
**E = MC² (1985)** : This song marked a significant commercial breakthrough for sampling in rock and roll. While the music itself was new, Mick Jones dubbed in dialogue snippets from the 1970 film *Performance* for added effect. The song became a massive hit single in 1985 and 1986, drawing attention primarily for its innovative use of samples rather than just its melody. "And E. Ecos MC square became the first worldwide rock and roll hit to feature Sampled." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0

## curiosities
**"Sample Crazy" Approach (Year not specified)** : After Mick Jones was "kicked out of the clash," he formed Big Audio Dynamite and became intensely focused on using sampling technology. The band's sound was characterized by extensive use of samples, reflecting Jones's innovative approach to music creation with this new technology. "After he was kicked out of the clash, he formed a new band called Big Audio Dynamite, and he went sample crazy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ak6OuOUkRrQ
**Boost from Levi's Commercial (1991)** : Big Audio Dynamite, Mick Jones's post-Clash project, received a significant boost in promotion due to its association with The Clash's "Should I Stay or Should I Go" being licensed for a 1991 Levi's commercial. This tie-in contributed to renewed interest in Jones's current work. "Big Audio Dynamite got a big push." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DYtsWfguqGo
