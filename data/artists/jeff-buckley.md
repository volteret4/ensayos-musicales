**Tragic Accidental Drowning** : On the evening of May 29, 1997, while in Memphis to record new material, Jeff Buckley went for a swim in Wolf River Harbor, which connects to the Mississippi River. He had swum there before, but this time he entered the water fully clothed and wearing heavy boots. While floating on his back and singing the chorus to Led Zeppelin's "Whole Lotta Love," a boat passed by, creating a wake that caused him to disappear. His body was found five days later downstream. The death was ruled an accidental drowning, with no drugs or alcohol involved and no indication of suicide. He was 30 years old. "Everything was going absolutely according to plan until the evening of May 29, 1997. Jeff was in Memphis to record some new material, and that night he and Keith Fulti, a rodean is banned, sat on the bank of Wolf River Harbor, which is connected to the Mississippi River, listening to music. Jeff had gone swimming there a few times before, and decided to do it again. So he went into the water. This time he was fully clothed and wearing big, heavy boots. He floated out on his back and began seeing the chorus to Led Zeppelin's whole lot of love. But then a boat came by, stirring up a big wake, and with that, Jeff was gone. His body was found five days later down River. No drugs, no alcohol, nothing to indicate suicide. It was a straight, accidental drowning.
