## labels

## instruments

## albums
**Grace (1994) – Debut Album** : A critically acclaimed debut album that initially had slow sales but gained momentum over time, eventually becoming a classic.

## curiosities
**Accidental Drowning** : On May 29, 1997, while in Memphis to record new material, Jeff went swimming fully clothed and wearing heavy boots in the Wolf River Harbor (connected to the Mississippi River). A boat's wake caused him to disappear, and his body was found five days later.
**Demo and Record Deal** : In 1990, his father's former manager helped him make a demo. After initial rejections, he performed as a solo artist in New York, was discovered by Columbia Records, and signed a deal.
**Discovery of Prog Rock** : He discovered Rush and Kiss and various prog rock bands, leading to his first electric guitar at age 12.
**Early Career** : Jeff played gigs while supporting himself working in a hotel.
**Early Musical Development** : He picked up a guitar at age five.
**Mother's Musical Background** : His mother, Mary Guibert, was a classically trained musician who played piano and cello.
**No Drugs or Alcohol Indication** : No drugs or alcohol were found, and there was no indication of suicide; his death was ruled an accidental drowning.
**Posthumous Classic Status of Grace** : His sole proper studio album, "Grace," became a classic after his early death at age 30.
**Son of Tim Buckley and Mary Guibert** : Jeff Buckley was the son of Tim Buckley and Mary Guibert. His parents divorced before he was born, and he only met his father once at age eight.
**Stepfather's Musical Influences** : His stepfather was a fan of Pink Floyd, Jimi Hendrix, The Who, Led Zeppelin, and Queen.
