# artist - Earl Scruggs

## member of
- The Earl Scruggs Review

## instruments
- **Scruggs Style Banjo** : Earl Scruggs revolutionized banjo playing in bluegrass with his unique "Scruggs style," involving picking the five-string banjo with two fingers and a thumb. Although he did not claim to invent it, this was the prevalent style from his home in North Carolina. "The Scrugg's style is picking at the five string banjo with three fingers, two fingers and a thumb actually."

## curiosities
**Anti-Vietnam War Protest (1969)** : In 1969, Scruggs upset many in country music and their fans by appearing at a peace rally (an anti-Vietnam war rally) in Washington, D.C. He stated that while he would not protest if he believed there was a valid reason for American troops to be there, he could not see a reason to support the war at that time. "Earl upset a lot of people in country music and their fans when he appeared at a peace rally in Washington, DC, which is also, of course, a way of saying an anti-Vietnam war rally." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 46 - The Chaos and Music of the Ozark Music Festival of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Boredom with Traditional Bluegrass (1960)** : By 1960, Scruggs grew bored with traditional bluegrass and desired to play more contemporary music, stating he would get "depressed" if he didn't. "By 1960 though, Scrugg's was getting a bit bored with traditional bluegrass." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 46 - The Chaos and Music of the Ozark Music Festival of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Breakup with Lester Flatt (1969)** : His desire to play more contemporary music contrasted with Lester Flatt's commitment to traditional bluegrass, leading to their breakup in 1969, which shocked fans and the music industry. "Lester Platt though wanted to stay true to bluegrass and their breakup in 1969 was a shock to their fans and to many people in the entire music industry." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 46 - The Chaos and Music of the Ozark Music Festival of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Collaboration with Nitty Gritty Dirt Band** : Scruggs admired the Nitty Gritty Dirt Band's album "Uncle Charlie and his dog Teddy," which led to their collaboration on the bluegrass triple LP "Will the Circle Be Unbroken." "Earl Scrugg's really liked the record. **Playing for Rock Fans** : Scruggs enjoyed performing for rock fans, as evidenced by his appearance at the 1968 Miami Pop Festival, which featured artists like Janis Joplin, Joni Mitchell, Marvin Gaye, and The Grateful Dead.
