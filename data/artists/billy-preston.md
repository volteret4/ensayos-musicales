**The "Fifth Beatle"** : Billy Preston was one of the few individuals referred to as the "Fifth Beatle," alongside producer George Martin, drummer Pete Best, and manager Brian Epstein. He earned this distinction by being the only other musician to be credited on a record label alongside The Beatles, playing keyboards during their "Let It Be" sessions in 1969 and on "Abbey Road." His manager, Joyce Moore, described his contribution as "sprinkl[ing] the glue and the glitter" that made everything "just felt good" when he joined the band. "The nickname fifth beetle was given to a few people, producer George Martin, the guy who was the drummer before Ringo, Pete Best, the manager, Brian Epstein, but the only other musician that got credited on a record label with the Beatles was Billy Preston." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 39 - American Top 40 - Keeping Our Feet on the Ground and Reaching for the Stars (Part 1) | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-39-american-top-40-keeping-our-feet-on-the-ground-and-reaching-for-the-stars-part-1
**Versatile Session and Touring Musician** : Billy Preston was a highly accomplished musician who taught himself to play keyboards. Beyond his work with The Beatles, he played with Ray Charles, Little Richard, and Sly and the Family Stone, and toured with the Rolling Stones in the mid-1970s. His solo career also benefited significantly from his performance alongside George Harrison, Eric Clapton, and Bob Dylan at the Concert for Bangladesh in 1971, which helped increase his name recognition among record buyers.
