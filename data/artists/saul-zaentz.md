## labels

## curiosities
**Antagonistic Relationship with John Fogarty** : There was a lot of loathing between Saul Zaentz and John Fogarty, stemming from Fogarty's decision to give up ownership of his CCR songs to Zaentz to escape his contract with Fantasy Records. "There was a lot of loathing on both sides." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wYD_dIvYY5o
**Plagiarism Lawsuit Against John Fogarty (1985)** : Saul Zaentz, who owned the copyrights to Credence Clearwater Revival's songs (including "Run Through the Jungle") through Fantasy Records, sued John Fogarty for plagiarism. Zaentz alleged that Fogarty's solo song "Old Man Down the Road" sounded too similar to "Run Through the Jungle," a song Zaentz owned the copyright to, despite Fogarty having written both. "While in Saul freaked out, got all bent out of shape, and then he sued John for plagiarism, alleging that a song on Center Field called Old Man Down the Road, which John owned, sounded just like an old CCR song called Run Through the Jungle, which Saul owned as the result of the contract dispute." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wYD_dIvYY5o
