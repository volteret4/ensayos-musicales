**Can You Believe (Year Unknown)** : This single was a collaboration between Norah Jones and Leon Michels, who also played horn and saxophone on some of her albums. Making this song was one of the "funnest times" she had playing, especially as it was after a year and a half of not playing with anyone due to the pandemic. **Come Away With Me (Year Unknown)** : Norah Jones wrote this song in high school, after her college days. She composed it in her tiny bedroom after seeing her friends play, using the five chords she knew. Being a "theory nerd" from second grade piano lessons, she notated the chord changes (e.g., "one, two, three, one, six, five, three, one") in her notebook, but without a voice recorder or cell phone, she forgot the original version by the next morning. "And I didn't have a voice recorder or anything. We didn't have cell phones yet. So I wrote it in my notebook and I wrote the chord changes above it." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Introducing Broken Record - Norah Jones Begins Again | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f1264fe6d21276e0cbc

**Collaborative Recording Pace** : The recording of Norah Jones' new album with Leon Michels involved short, frequent sessions (11 AM to 2 PM), pieced together whenever possible due to their children's school schedules. This contrasted with her previous album made with "the Angel Mouse," which involved two months in the studio. Norah found this new, flexible approach "so fun." "This was just here and there and dribs and drabs and always short days because our kids were in school and just 11 to 2 and all right, cool." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Introducing Broken Record - Norah Jones Begins Again | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f1264fe6d21276e0cbc
**Discovery of "Heart Ake"** : Norah Jones discovered the song "Heart Ake" by Lowell George around the time she was making her second record. Kevin Bright, who played guitar on her first album, introduced her to Lowell George's work, specifically the album "Thanks, I'll Eat It Here." She subsequently found and admired a bootleg demo of "Heart Ake" featuring Lowell George and Linda Ronstadt. "So and the next day my picture was on the cover of the New York Post, Nexus Saddam Hussein's picture. **Resilience and Boundaries** : Following the intense fame of her first album, Norah Jones learned to establish strong boundaries, becoming "stubborn" and comfortable with saying "no" to demands she felt were not artistically driven, such as a label-requested remix for pop radio. She attributes some of this strength to her mother, a "strong woman." However, she believes the version she later recalled and perfected was "a little different" from her original creation, a detail lost because she couldn't record it. "I think it's a little different. It was a little different than what I had originally come up with and I will never know. Because you didn't remember that.
