## curiosities
**Blues Icon at Manchester Special** : Muddy Waters performed at the Manchester 1964 TV special alongside Sister Rosetta Tharp and other blues greats. "Along with Tharp, there was muddy waters, otis span, Reverend Gary Davis and Sonny Terri and Varnie McGee, all performing in the open area in Manchester, in front of an extremely earnest audience of young British white people." ← Episode 5： ＂This Train＂ by Sister Rosetta Tharpe | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=II-hDnwUgT8
**O.B. McClinton Impression** : O.B. McClinton used Muddy Waters as an example in his live shows to illustrate the difference between rhythm and blues and country. He would sing the same song first in the style of Johnny Cash and then in the style of Muddy Waters. "I illustrate the difference between country and rhythm and blues by singing the same song as if I were Johnny Cash and then Muddy Waters." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 53 - More than Charley Pride - African Americans in 70s Country | https://www.ftr70.com/
