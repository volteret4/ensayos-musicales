# artist - Rick Nelson

## albums
**Garden Party (1972)** : This album featured the eponymous song, which became a Top 10 hit on the Billboard Hot 100 in 1972. The song was directly inspired by Nelson's negative experience at a Madison Square Garden concert where he was booed for playing his new country rock material. ## songs
**Garden Party (1972)** : Written by Nelson after he was booed off stage at Madison Square Garden in October 1971 for performing country rock instead of his earlier rock and roll hits. The lyrics reflect on the audience's expectation for his 1950s image and music, and his decision to prioritize his own artistic path over pleasing everyone. The song includes lines about playing old songs, not looking the same, and the adage "you can't please everyone so you got to please yourself," as well as a reference to Johnny B. Goode. "So Nelson wrote about that night and he turned it into a song. Played them all the old songs. Thought that's why they came. No one heard the music. ## curiosities
**Booed at Madison Square Garden** : In October 1971, Rick Nelson and his Stone Canyon Band were booed by fans at Madison Square Garden during a "Rock and Roll Spectacular" (renamed from "Rock and Roll Revival"). Fans, who expected his 1950s hits and image from "Ozzie and Harriet," rejected his country rock sound, long hair, boots, and bell-bottom jeans. Nelson believed the booing was directed at him for his changed musical direction and appearance. "So they booed him and his long hair to the point that Randy Meisner, who would leave the band and go help create something else called the Eagles, left." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 35 - Cosmic American Music - 70s Country Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-35-cosmic-american-music-70s-country-rock
**Changing Image and Musical Direction** : Nelson's appearance, featuring boots, bell-bottom jeans, and shaggy hair, clashed with the 1950s Ricky Nelson image that fans from "Ozzie and Harriet" expected at the 1971 Madison Square Garden concert. He had been making country-tinged music since the mid-1960s, so his new material shouldn't have been a complete surprise, yet it was met with strong resistance. "They wanted Ricky Nelson from the Ozzy and Harriet show of the 50s, not Rick Nelson with boots and bell-bottom jeans and shaggy hair." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 35 - Cosmic American Music - 70s Country Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-35-cosmic-american-music-70s-country-rock
**Image Reinvention Effort** : Rick Nelson adopted this name as part of an effort to transition from his earlier identity as a teenage idol, Ricky Nelson, to broaden his audience. This name change was part of a difficult process for him to alter his public image. **Later Reliance on Old Hits** : Ironically, a decade after being booed for his new country rock sound, Rick Nelson began putting on shows that relied on his old hits and their more familiar arrangements. This pivot suggests an eventual capitulation to audience expectations for nostalgia.
