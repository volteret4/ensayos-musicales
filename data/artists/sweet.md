## curiosities
**Image Management and US Touring** : Sweet was displeased that their hit "Little Willie" was released in the US a year later than in Europe (1973 vs. 1972), by which time they were actively trying to change their image away from bubblegum. Guitarist Andy Scott stated in a 1978 interview that the band intentionally did not tour the United States to capitalize on "Little Willie"'s success because it was 18 months "after the fact." They also disliked being considered "opening act material" in the US and having audiences only want to hear their older bubblegum hits. "Andy Scott who played guitar for sweet said in a 1978 interview that that was intentional and that there was quote no way the band was going to come in tour because of Little Willie being a hit 18 months after the fact." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 30 - The Sweet Sound of 70s Bubblegum Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/030-bubblegum ← sweet
**Name Changes** : In the late 1960s, the band was initially known as The Sweet Shop, a clear reference to the prevailing popularity of bubblegum music. The name was later shortened to The Sweet. "In the late 60s the Sweet was known as the Sweet Shop and incredibly obvious nod to the popularity of bubble gum music.
