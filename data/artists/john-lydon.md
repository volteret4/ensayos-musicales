**Decision to End Johnny Rotten Persona (January 14, 1978)** : John Lydon, previously known as Johnny Rotten and the primary face of punk, decided to "kill his character" at the Sex Pistols' last concert of their American tour, which also became their final show together. He was reportedly tired of playing the same accelerated three-chord Chuck Berry style and felt music could be more. **Eclectic Musical Tastes Revealed (1977)** : In 1977, shortly after the Sex Pistols released "God Save the Queen," John Lydon was interviewed by a London radio station to play his favorite songs on a special program called "The Punk and His Music." While everyone expected the wildest punk, he, the supposed "Antichrist of punk," played reggae, Krautrock bands like Can, Tim Buckley ballads, and Captain Beefheart's wild hip-hop experimentation. This music, despised by the punk scene at the time, later became a significant reference for post-punk. "Todo el mundo esperaba escuchar el punk más albaje, pero en su lugar, el supuesto anticristo del punk, un setem más de Rigi, de bandas de Krauro como Cannes, Valadas de Tim Buckley, o la loquísima experimentación hip-hid de Captain Vithhart." ← La traición que salvó al punk. **Outrage over "Aggressive Bubblegum" Comment** : John Lydon, also known as Johnny Rotten, the lead singer of the Sex Pistols, expressed extreme anger at Tom Verlaine's comment that punk music was merely "slightly aggressive Bubblegum." Lydon interpreted this as a jealous and superficial insult, accusing Verlaine and his "New York trendy expletive" peers of being "posers" from wealthy families who couldn't comprehend the "ferocious and frontline" content of the Sex Pistols. "No, no, no, no, no, no, no, no, again I've always hated the label punk But if he calls the Sex Pistols slightly aggressive Bubblegum, then he really is showing what a jealous, superficial, expletive, I always thought he was, okay I didn't say expletive, the newspaper said that Johnny goes on to say, and he is part and parcel now he's talking about Tom Verlaine Him and all of them New York trendy expletives that had the liberty to call themselves punk They weren't really, they were posers." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 30 - The Sweet Sound of 70s Bubblegum Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/030-bubblegum ← john-lydon

**Post-Sex Pistols Escape to Jamaica** : After the Sex Pistols, Rotten reclaimed his birth name, John Lydon, and his first decision was to escape. He packed his bags and spent time in Jamaica, where he mingled with prominent dub artists. This Jamaican genre, based on echo, empty space, and heavy bass, would become one of the many genres defining post-punk. "Su primera decisión fue escapar, hizo las malitas y pasó una temporada en Jamaica, donde se codeó con los grandes totes del lab." ← La traición que salvó al punk.
