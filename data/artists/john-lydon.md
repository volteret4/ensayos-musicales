## songs
**Death Disco (1988)** : This Public Image Limited song is highlighted due to John Lydon's near-death experience in the Lockerbie bombing. He and his wife, Nora, were supposed to be on Pan Am Flight 103 on December 21, 1988, but missed it by minutes after his wife took too long to pack, leading to a fight and traffic delays to Heathrow. **Near-Miss Lockerbie Bombing (1988)** : John Lydon, of the Sex Pistols and Public Image Limited, came very close to dying in the bombing of Pan Am Flight 103 over Lockerbie, Scotland, on December 21, 1988. He and his wife, Nora, had seats on the flight but missed it by minutes due to his wife taking too long to pack, which caused a big fight, and then getting stuck in traffic on the way to Heathrow Airport. **Outrage over "Aggressive Bubblegum" Comment** : John Lydon, also known as Johnny Rotten, the lead singer of the Sex Pistols, expressed extreme anger at Tom Verlaine's comment that punk music was merely "slightly aggressive Bubblegum." Lydon interpreted this as a jealous and superficial insult, accusing Verlaine and his "New York trendy expletive" peers of being "posers" from wealthy families who couldn't comprehend the "ferocious and frontline" content of the Sex Pistols.
