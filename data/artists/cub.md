## albums
**Betty Cola (1993)** : CUB's debut album, released in the fall of 1993, comprised a collection of their previously released material along with several covers. The album's distinctive artwork was created by a cartoonist for Archie comics, reflecting the band's deliberately sweet and childlike aesthetic, which they termed "cuddlecore." The album includes the song "My Chinchilla." "The record was called Betty Cola, and from that is a song called My Chinchilla." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jhgZrwtYE2g

## curiosities
**Childlike Stage Persona and Fan Interaction** : CUB was renowned for their exceptionally sweet and childlike persona, which they consistently embraced. They frequently performed live shows in their pajamas and were known to bring gifts for their fans, meticulously working to present as childlike an image as possible in their music and stage presence. "They sometimes played shows in their pajamas." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jhgZrwtYE2g
**Cuddlecore Genre Creation** : CUB, a band from Vancouver signed to Mint Records, developed and named their own genre, "cuddlecore," to describe their music. This genre encapsulated their melodic sound, jangly guitars, and a distinct childlike quality that they intentionally cultivated through their music and performances, often playing shows in pajamas and bringing gifts for fans. "Their music was melodic, it had the jangly guitars, and it had such a childlike quality to it that the band created a new genre for themselves." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jhgZrwtYE2g
