# artist - Portishead

## members
- Adrian Utley
- Beth Gibbons (singer)
- Geoff Barrow

## genres
- Electronic
- Funk
- Hip Hop
- Jazz
- Soul
- Trip Hop

## albums
**Dummy (1994) – Debut Record** : Released in the summer of 1994. It won the Mercury Prize the following year (1995).

## songs
**Sour Times (1994)** : One of three singles from their 1994 debut record "Dummy." Features the "unmistakable voice" of Beth Gibbons.

## curiosities
**Beth Gibbons' Enigmatic Persona** : Beth Gibbons was one of the most enigmatic frontwomen of the decade, giving "zero interviews" and possessing a "fragile" singing voice.
**Bristol Origins** : From Bristol, about 12 kilometers away from a town also named Portishead.
**Trip Hop Innovators** : Helped define the sound known as Trip Hop in 1994.
**UK Dominance** : Helped turn Trip Hop into one of the "most dominant sounds in the UK in the middle 90s," even competing with Britpop bands like Blur and Oasis.
