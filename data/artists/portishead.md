**Dummy** : The stripped-down sound of Portishead's album "Dummy" fascinated Noel Gallagher and served as an inspiration for the vibe he aimed to create with Oasis' "Wonderwall," though without venturing into trip-hop territory. "Noll had become fascinated by the stripped-down sound of the Portushead album, Dummy, and was looking to create that vibe without going into trip-hop territory." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IhG-WJH9om8 ← portishead ← portishead
**Dummy (1994) - Definitive Trip Hop Soundtrack** : Released in 1994, this album predated Massive Attack's *Protection* and became widely regarded as the definitive Trip Hop record, especially in the United States. Geoff Barrow infused it with "cinematic anguish," creating an album characterized by dark, unhealthy sounds, all filtered through a dragging, melancholic urban atmosphere that starkly contrasted with Beth Gibbons' spectral vocals. Many critics described it as "gothic Hip Hop" or the soundtrack to an unreleased dark film. "Así que en 1994 por Tisket se les aderantaron con Dami." ← El género más OSCURO de los 90 (...que acabó en anuncios de coches) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9gAEaRBEw9c
**Dummy (1994) – Debut Record** : Portishead's debut album was released in the summer of 1994. The following year, it earned the prestigious Mercury Prize, solidifying its critical acclaim. "Portus Head's debut record came out on the summer of 1994, and it won the Mercury Prize the following year, and this was one of the singles." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI ← portishead

## curiosities
**Beth Gibbons' Enigmatic Persona** : Beth Gibbons was considered one of the most enigmatic frontwomen of the 1990s. She rarely gave interviews, and her singing voice was often described as fragile, contributing to her mysterious public image. "Beth was one of the most enigmatic front women of the decade. We knew so little about her. She gave zero interviews, and she sounded so fragile when she sang." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI ← portishead
**Founded by Geoff Barrow** : Portishead was founded by Geoff Barrow after he was deeply impressed and "blown away" by the sounds he heard emerging from the studio during the recording of Massive Attack's *Blue Lines*. This pivotal moment led him to establish the group a little later. **Origin and Defining Trip Hop** : Hailing from Bristol, UK, Portishead helped to define the "Trip Hop" sound in 1994. This genre is characterized as a form of down-tempo music subtly integrating elements of electronic and hip hop, along with dashes of funk, soul, jazz, samples, and various sonic experimentation. "In 1994 they helped define a sound that became known as Trip Hop." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI ← portishead
**Pillar of Trip Hop** : Portishead is considered one of the three great pillars of Trip Hop, alongside Massive Attack and Tricky. These three entities converged in the same studio during the *Blue Lines* recording, marking the simultaneous beginning of the Trip Hop movement. "como los tres grandes pilares del trip hop, que fueron la desmasiva atac por el otro lado Strixi y por el otro lado Sportishead, coincidieron y empezaron a gestar lo que sería el movimiento trip hop dentro del mismo estudio al mismo tiempo." ← Music Radar Clan > Massive Attack y El Origen del Trip Hop en Bristol. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bpOr4KKm5rM&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc ← portishead ← portishead
