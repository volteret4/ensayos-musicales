## curiosities
**Brought to Stax by Joe Galkin** : Johnny Jenkins, a local guitarist, was brought to Stax by record promoter Joe Galkin, who hoped Stax could help him achieve an instrumental hit, given the label's success with such tracks. "Joe Galkin, a record promoter to whom Jim Stewart Oda Favour was merging a local guitarist, Johnny Jenkins, and brought him into the studio to see if Stax could get him an instrumental hit" ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Otis Redding's Driver** : Jenkins could not drive, so his band's singer, Otis Redding, drove him to the Stax studio for his session. This circumstance serendipitously led to Redding's own recording opportunity. "Galkin had made a deal with the singer in Jenkins's band. If he'd drive Jenkins to the studio, since Jenkins couldn't drive, he'd try to get a record cut with him as well." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Unsuccessful Stax Single** : Jenkins did release a single on Stax, but it was not particularly special and failed to achieve any success. His first session at Stax was considered a flop. "Jenkins did eventually release a single on Stax, but it wasn't particularly special and didn't have any success.
