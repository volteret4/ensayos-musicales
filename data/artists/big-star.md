## curiosities
**Commercial Flop, Critical Success** : Big Star, with frontman Alex Chilton, aimed to follow in the footsteps of The Beatles and The Kinks while also admiring The Beach Boys' melodies. Despite being major critical darlings with a strong following among other musicians, they struggled with a bad record deal, failed to achieve hit singles, and sold few records, leading to their breakup after three years. "Unfortunately for Big Star, they couldn't manage any hit singles and never sold many records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hstJ8M2laho
**Influence on Modern Bands** : Big Star's innovative sound influenced a wide range of bands, from The Cars and Blondie to Sloan and Blink-182, solidifying their legacy as foundational figures in Powerpop. "Someone from the cars in Blondie to Sloan and Blink 1.2 were influenced by Big Star." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Musical Style - Melodic Hooks and Catchy Melodies** : Big Star's genius lay in combining "very melodic hooks with very catchy melodies and big guitars," creating a sound that often appeared happy but frequently concealed lyrics that were "on the depressed and melancholy side." "But their genius was to combine very melodic hooks with very catchy melodies and big guitars. And while the music tended to sound happy, that often covered up lyrics that were on the depressed and melancholy side." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Pioneers of Powerpop** : From their formation in Memphis in 1971, Big Star is credited with essentially inventing the genre known as Powerpop, blending influences from The Beatles, The Who, and The Beach Boys with their own distinctive style. "They essentially invented the genre that we now call Powerpop." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Posthumous Rediscovery and Cult Following** : After breaking up in 1975 due to commercial disappointment, Big Star was rediscovered in the 1990s by a new generation of alternative rock musicians and fans, leading to the development of a strong cult following. "But in the years that followed, especially in the 1990s, they were rediscovered by new generation of alt-wrong musicians and fans. A cult following built." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Precursors to Other Powerpop Bands** : Big Star predated other successful Powerpop bands of the 1970s, such as Badfinger, Todd Rundgren, The Raspberries, Cheap Trick, and The Knack, establishing the genre's foundation before its wider recognition. "There were other Powerpop bands in the 70s who had hits. There was Bad Finger and Todd Rungren. We had the raspberries, Cheap Trick, the Nack, but Big Star was there before all of them." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Reunion and Alex Chilton's Death** : The band reunited in 1993 and remained active until singer Alex Chilton passed away from a heart attack, marking the end of their second phase. "A cult following built, and in 1993, the group reunited and stayed together until singer Alex Chilton died of a heart attack." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Undiscovered Cult Favorite** : After their breakup due to commercial struggles, Big Star remained an undiscovered cult favorite for approximately two decades. They were later "discovered" by a number of 90s-era alt-rock bands, who recognized that Big Star's sound from 1971 was significantly ahead of its time. "Big Star remained an undiscovered cult favorite for a couple of decades. But then they were discovered by a number of 90s era alt rock bands who realized that whatever Big Star was doing back in 1971, they were way, way ahead of their time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hstJ8M2laho
