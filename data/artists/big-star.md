## curiosities
**Commercial Flop, Critical Success** : Big Star, with frontman Alex Chilton, aimed to follow in the footsteps of The Beatles and The Kinks while also admiring The Beach Boys' melodies. Despite being major critical darlings with a strong following among other musicians, they struggled with a bad record deal, failed to achieve hit singles, and sold few records, leading to their breakup after three years. "Unfortunately for Big Star, they couldn't manage any hit singles and never sold many records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hstJ8M2laho
**Undiscovered Cult Favorite** : After their breakup due to commercial struggles, Big Star remained an undiscovered cult favorite for approximately two decades. They were later "discovered" by a number of 90s-era alt-rock bands, who recognized that Big Star's sound from 1971 was significantly ahead of its time. "Big Star remained an undiscovered cult favorite for a couple of decades. But then they were discovered by a number of 90s era alt rock bands who realized that whatever Big Star was doing back in 1971, they were way, way ahead of their time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hstJ8M2laho
