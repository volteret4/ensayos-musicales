## curiosities
**Attacks by Monarchists (1977)** : In response to "God Save the Queen," members of the Sex Pistols and their associates were victims of violent attacks by monarchist supporters. On the evening of June 18th, 1977, Johnny Rotten, producer Chris Thomas, and engineer Bill Price were assaulted by a gang armed with razors in the parking lot of London's Pegasus Hotel. Johnny Rotten stated that his thick leather pants saved him from bleeding to death, though some tendons in his arm were severed, making it permanently difficult for him to play guitar. The following night, June 19th, drummer Paul Cook was attacked outside a pub by six individuals wielding pipes, resulting in him needing 15 stitches in his head. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8UIBWyUt38g
**Attendance at Ramones London Gigs (July 1976)** : Johnny Rotten and Sid Vicious were among the key figures transformed by seeing The Ramones perform in London on July 4th and 6th, 1976. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qC0Ee4-dpnI
**Bernie Rhodes' Involvement** : Bernie Rhodes, a figure in the early punk rock scene, initially worked with Malcolm McLaren on the Sex Pistols. However, his offer to co-manage the band was rebuffed, which greatly annoyed him. Rhodes later claimed to have "invented punk rock" by recruiting Johnny Rotten for the band. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**Conflict with Malcolm McLaren** : Despite becoming a sensation and challenging the British establishment, the band, particularly singer Johnny Rotten, grew distrustful of Malcolm McLaren. McLaren was accused of widespread mismanagement, and band members frequently reported that funds were unavailable when they sought payment. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**Critic's Harsh View of Band and Genre** : An unnamed journalist expressed an extremely negative opinion of punk rock, describing it as "nauseating, disgusting, degrading, ghastly, sleazy, pruriant, biogristic and generally nauseating." This critic believed that most punk groups "would be vastly improved by sudden death" and singled out the Sex Pistols as "unbelievably nauseating" and "the antithesis of humankind," wishing for their "total and other non-existence." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8UIBWyUt38g
**Early Label Troubles** : The Sex Pistols' initial management, Glitter Best (Malcolm McLaren's company), secured a deal with EMI, but the band was quickly dropped due to their controversial nature and paid to leave. A subsequent contract with A&M Records lasted only five days before they were again paid to depart. Their third deal with Virgin Records ultimately "stuck." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**First Live Gig (November 6, 1975)** : The band's inaugural live performance took place in London at the St. Martins art school, in a small upstairs room. They were the opening act for Bizzouca Joe, featuring singer Adam Ant. The school's social programmer pulled the plug after only two songs, but despite this initial reception, their appearance was pivotal for music history. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oPgeSCy93bo
**Formation and Naming by Malcolm McLaren** : Malcolm McLaren created the Sex Pistols as a "living, breathing advertisement" for his Kings Road shop (then renamed Sex) and an outlet for his anti-establishment political beliefs. The group's name, "Sex Pistols," was derived from the shop's name and the perceived danger of "pistols." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**Implosion and Litigation (1978-1987)** : The band's relationship with Malcolm McLaren rapidly deteriorated, culminating in their implosion at the end of a "disastrously executed American tour" in 1978. This was followed by years of litigation between band members and Glitter Best, with the band finally regaining control of their music in 1987. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**Inspiration for Joe Strummer** : When the Sex Pistols opened for The 101ers at a gig at the Nashville club, their "attitude and energy" profoundly impressed Joe Strummer, the guitar player for The 101ers, despite their musicianship being described as "awful." This led Joe Strummer to leave his band and form The Clash. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oPgeSCy93bo
**Inspiration for Joy Division** : In the spring of 1976, Bernie and his friend Peter saw the Sex Pistols play in Manchester. Though Bernie initially found their performance "terrible," he also thought it was "great" and was inspired to "get up there and be terrible too," leading them to form their first group, Warsaw, which later became Joy Division. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oPgeSCy93bo
**Legendary Manchester Performance (June 4, 1976)** : Played a pivotal and mythologized gig at the Lesser Free Trade Hall in Manchester on June 4, 1976, a show arranged by the Buzzcocks. Despite an audience of only around 40 people, this event inspired attendees to form influential bands like Georgia Vision, Pet Shop Boys, Simply Red, and The Smiths, and to establish Factory Records. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Mocking Non-Punks** : Legend suggests Sid Vicious started Pogoing as a way of mocking non-punks who came to see the Sex Pistols, as he felt they didn't really belong. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KMx4bmq8o2Y
**Performance at 100 Club Punk Rock Festival (September 20, 1976)** : The Sex Pistols performed on the first night of Britain's first official punk rock festival at the 100 Club on Oxford Street, London. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qC0Ee4-dpnI
**TV Show Scandal (Late 1976)** : Prior to December 2, 1976, the band appeared on a popular TV show where they used "bad words," causing a national scandal. All of Britain was discussing it. This event greatly inspired Declan McManus, who later became Elvis Costello, and Siouxsie Sioux was noted as being in the audience. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oPgeSCy93bo
**The Bromley Contingent (Mid-1970s)** : As the Sex Pistols rose to prominence, they were followed everywhere by a group of dedicated fans known as the Bromley Contingent. This group attended significant events with the band, including the Bill Grundy TV show in December 1976. Susan Ballion (Siouxsie Sioux) was a part of this contingent and was inspired by the Pistols to enter the punk scene. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
