## curiosities
**Attendance at Ramones London Gigs (July 1976)** : Johnny Rotten and Sid Vicious were among the key figures transformed by seeing The Ramones perform in London on July 4th and 6th, 1976.
**Legendary Manchester Performance (June 4, 1976)** : Played a pivotal and mythologized gig at the Lesser Free Trade Hall in Manchester on June 4, 1976, a show arranged by the Buzzcocks. Despite an audience of only around 40 people, this event inspired attendees to form influential bands like Georgia Vision, Pet Shop Boys, Simply Red, and The Smiths, and to establish Factory Records.
**Mocking Non-Punks** : Legend suggests Sid Vicious started Pogoing as a way of mocking non-punks who came to see the Sex Pistols, as he felt they didn't really belong.
**Performance at 100 Club Punk Rock Festival (September 20, 1976)** : The Sex Pistols performed on the first night of Britain's first official punk rock festival at the 100 Club on Oxford Street, London.
