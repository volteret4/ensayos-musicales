## curiosities
**Favorite of Ice-T** : Slayer was a favorite band of Ice-T, particularly for their thrash metal speed, which influenced his musical direction with Body Count. "He also appreciated the speed of thrash metal, and Slayer is a favorite." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Judgment Night Collaboration with Ice-T (1993)** : Slayer collaborated with rapper Ice-T for the 1993 *Judgment Night* soundtrack. This pairing was considered radical at the time due to the limited crossover between rock and hip-hop. The project was a major catalyst for the creation of rap rock and nu metal, and helped bridge the divide between rock and hip-hop fans. "The pairings included Pearl Jam and Cypress Hill, Slayer working with Ice Tea, Faith No More and Buella Tribe, Dinosaur Jr., and Del the Funky Homo sapien, 11 collaborations at all." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM
**Kerry King's Contribution to *Licensed to Ill*** : Kerry King, the guitarist for Slayer, played guitar on the Beastie Boys' album *Licensed to Ill*, specifically on the track "No Sleep Till Brooklyn." This occurred while Slayer was in the process of recording their own album, *Reign in Blood*. "I mean, Kerry King of Slayer played guitar on it, Slayer." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l1jPP1FhGo8
**Marshall Stacks Setup** : Kerry King of Slayer is noted for his specific Marshall stacks setup, utilizing the JCM 800 model.
