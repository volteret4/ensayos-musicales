## curiosities
**Adam Yock Joins** : Adam Yock, who knew how to play "two notes" on the bass, started hanging out at the band's rehearsals and was asked to join when their regular bass player left the city for the summer. "Yock started hanging out at Young Aborigines' rehearsals before he was asked to join when the regular bass player left the city for the summer." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l1jPP1FhGo8
**Brief Existence and Regrouping** : The group played only two gigs, both on the same night, before packing it in. However, within "just days," they regrouped to form The Beastie Boys. "The group played two gigs, both in the same night, and they packed it in. But within just days, the Young Aborigines regrouped as The Beastie Boys." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l1jPP1FhGo8
**Formation (1981)** : The Young Aborigines were formed in 1981 by a teenage drummer named Michael Diamond, the son of a successful art dealer from the Upper West Side of New York. "That's when a spiky-haired kid, a teenage drummer named Michael Diamond, the son of a successful art dealer from the Upper West Side, formed a group called The Young Aborigines." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l1jPP1FhGo8
