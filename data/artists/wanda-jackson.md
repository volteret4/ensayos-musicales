**Hot Dog! That Made Him Mad (1956)** : This song had been Wanda Jackson's favorite in her stage act for years, and she would perform it with a tremendous growl on the title line, which consistently excited audiences. However, during the studio recording, she horrified producer Ken Nelson by drinking a large amount of milk, unaware of its detrimental effect on a singer's throat. As a result, she couldn't achieve the signature growl, and while the record was still good, it failed to become the massive success it otherwise would have been. ← Episode 43： ＂I Gotta Know＂ by Wanda Jackson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gEInec4z65Q
**I Gotta Know (1956)** : This song was written by Thelma Blackmon, a friend's mother who had previously written songs for Jackson. Wanda decided it would be perfect to restart her career after Elvis Presley convinced her to move into Rockabilly. Recorded at Capitol Tower with session musicians like Jelly Sanders (fiddle), Joe Mephys (guitar), and Ralph Mooney (steel guitar), it became a big country hit, reaching number 15 on the country charts. The song is notable for its genre mixing, with the choruses being distinctly country and the verses falling into the rock and roll genre, a common technique for female performers transitioning into rock and roll. It featured Elvis-style hiccups on the title line, which came close to parody but contributed to its success. ← Episode 43： ＂I Gotta Know＂ by Wanda Jackson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gEInec4z65Q
**Without Your Love (Songwriter, 1950s)** : Wanda Jackson wrote this song for Bobby Lord, a singer with whom she appeared on the radio show Ozark Jubilee. **Childhood Musical Aspirations** : Before even starting school, Wanda Jackson was deeply influenced by Rose Maddox's stage presence and decided she wanted to be a "girl singer just like Rose Maddox." Her father supported this ambition, buying her a guitar from the Sears Robuck catalogue when she was six and teaching her chords. They would play together nightly, with her mother writing down chords for songs they learned from radio and records. ← Episode 43： ＂I Gotta Know＂ by Wanda Jackson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gEInec4z65Q
**Discovery of Elvis Presley and Influence** : After leaving school and becoming a full-time musician, Jackson's father, her manager, secured a booking agent named Bob Neal in Memphis. Neal arranged for her to tour with a new singer he managed, Elvis Presley, and Johnny Cash. Backstage at the first show, she heard the "terrible screaming" from Elvis's fans and immediately recognized he was not an ordinary country singer. They started dating, and Elvis gave her his ring, which she still possesses. He had a profound influence on her, teaching her to strum an acoustic guitar with a single strum, a more rock and roll style, and convincing her to pursue rockabilly music. ← Episode 43： ＂I Gotta Know＂ by Wanda Jackson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gEInec4z65Q
**Early Life and Migration** : Born in Oklahoma, Wanda Jackson and her family migrated to California during the tail end of the Great Depression, as "stragglers in the Great Migration" of "Oak Ease." They lived in Greenfield, a few miles from Bakersfield, where her father worked. This put her in the same musical and social environment as future Bakersfield Sound musicians, before they moved back to Oklahoma a few years later. ← Episode 43： ＂I Gotta Know＂ by Wanda Jackson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gEInec4z65Q
**Early TV Show and Mentorship (14 years old)** : At 14, after auditioning for a local TV show, Hank Thompson discovered Wanda Jackson. Initially unaware of her age due to her mature voice, he invited her to perform with his band. Although she had never played with a band and felt she wasn't good initially, Thompson mentored her, teaching her stage performance and eventually inviting her to his local TV show, which led to her getting her own show in Oklahoma. ← Episode 43： ＂I Gotta Know＂ by Wanda Jackson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gEInec4z65Q

**Grand Ole Opry Scandal (Uncovered Shoulders)** : During her first performance at the Grand Ole Opry, Wanda Jackson was told she could not go on stage in her chosen dress because her shoulders were uncovered, which was deemed "obscene" by the establishment. At the time, she was one of the only country singers trying to present a glamorous image rather than a "farm girl" look. When she complied by wearing a jacket, she was further humiliated by comedy acts who mocked her throughout her set, confirming the country establishment's resistance to her style. ← Episode 43： ＂I Gotta Know＂ by Wanda Jackson | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gEInec4z65Q
