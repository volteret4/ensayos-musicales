# artist - The Saints

## genres
- Garage Band

## songs
**(I'm) Stranded (1976)** : This song was initially released as a rough, raw garage band-like indie single in September 1976, with 500 copies. It quickly made its way to England, causing a "major freak out" in the music media due to its new and shocking sound compared to the dull pop of the era. Its impact, alongside that of the Sex Pistols and The Clash, was credited with changing music in the UK forever. ## curiosities
**Brisbane Origin and Style (Late 1973/1974)** : Originating from Brisbane, Australia, The Saints began as Kid Galahad and the Eternals around late 1973 or 1974. Influenced by The Stooges and The Velvet Underground, they developed a distinctive fast-paced style, as they found they "couldn't play slow." By late 1975, they were performing their songs "really, really fast." "A band called The Saints released a rough, raw garage band like single that rocked in an entirely new way. They had obviously got their hands on some stooge's and velvet underground records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4
**Impact on UK Music Scene** : The Saints, along with the Sex Pistols and The Clash, were identified as one of three bands that "changed the music forever in the UK," particularly after their single "(I'm) Stranded" caused a media sensation there in 1976. "Because of that media freak out, three bands changed the music forever in the UK, the sex pistols, the clash, and the Saints." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4
