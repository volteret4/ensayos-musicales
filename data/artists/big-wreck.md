## albums

It was nominated for a Juno Award and included the single "Wolves." "The Albatross album was nominated for a Juno." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**But for the Sun (2019)** : This album was released in 2019, following "Grace Street," and saw continued touring. "...after which came But for the Sun in 2019." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**Ghosts (2014)** : This album followed "Albatross" in 2014 and also received a Juno nomination. "It was followed by Ghosts in 2014. And it too was nominated for a Juno." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**Grace Street (2017)** : Released in 2017, this album came after Ian Thorneley's solo effort "Secrets," marking a return to Big Wreck. "And then it was back to Big Wreck for the Grey Street album in 2017..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**In Loving Memory Of... (1997) - Debut Album** : Big Wreck's first album, released in 1997, consisted mostly of demos that Atlantic Records purchased. It marked the first time Ian Thornley heard his voice through speakers. The album was praised for sounding polished and fully formed, defying typical debut record expectations, and sold a quarter million copies in Canada. "that first record was a result of searching for a particular..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z9QChV543aQ
**The Pleasure and the Greed (2001)** : This was Big Wreck's second album, released in the summer of 2001. It included the song "Ladylike." "that's Ladylike from the second album, The Pleasure and the Greed, released in the summer of 2001." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**The Pleasure and the Greed (2001) - Second Album** : Released in June 2001, this was Big Wreck's second album, coming four years after their debut. The label, Atlantic Records, reportedly pressured the band to sound like Nickelback. The band, however, wanted to move in the opposite direction, leading to creative clashes and contributing to the eventual breakup of the original lineup. "The second album, the difficult second record comes out. The pleasure of the Greed in 2001." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z9QChV543aQ

## curiosities
**Albatross Album Naming** : The album "Albatross" was named after its titular song, which itself was named when the word "Albatross" simply "fell into" Ian Thorneley's lap during writing, rather than a conscious reference to the Rime of the Ancient Mariner or John Cleese's sketch. The general practice for naming albums is to choose a song title, with "In Loving Memory" and "But for the Sun" being exceptions. "Dude, I just named the album after the song." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**Contrast between Industry 1.0 and 2.0** : Ian Thorneley noted a significant difference in the music industry between Big Wreck's first and second versions. The initial era involved experiences like being wined and dined by label executives such as Ahmet Ertegun, while the later period with SRO Anthem offered more creative freedom without the commercial pressures of chasing hits. "The music industry of Big Rec version 1.0. It was very different from what you had to deal with Big Rec version 2.0." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**Creative Conflict with Atlantic Records** : For their second album, Atlantic Records pressured Big Wreck to adopt a sound similar to Nickelback, aiming for a more "packaged" and commercially viable product. The band, however, desired to move in a more experimental direction, creating significant tension and contributing to the eventual dissolution of the original lineup. "They needed to nickel back. They wanted, that's totally what they wanted." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z9QChV543aQ
**First Version Disbandment (2001-2002)** : The first iteration of Big Wreck concluded around 2001 or 2002, with Ian Thorneley subsequently pursuing other musical projects. "But that first version of the band was done by 2002." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**Formation in Boston** : Big Wreck was formed in Boston, Massachusetts, out of collaborations between Ian Thornley, Brian Doherty, Dave Henning, and Forest Williams, who had met while attending Berklee School of Music. "It was formed in Boston." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z9QChV543aQ
**Management Trust Affiliation** : Big Wreck was managed by The Management Trust, a Canadian management company known for also representing The Tragically Hip, The Watchmen, and Andrew Cash at the time. The Management Trust played a crucial role in securing studio time and facilitating the band's early career development. "We had, we had management. We had the management trust." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z9QChV543aQ
**Recording at The Bath House** : Big Wreck recorded at The Bath House, The Tragically Hip's residential studio. "Blue rodeo, Ronnie Hawkins, Sam Roberts, Big Rec, and dozens and dozens of others have worked there." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QEkGSpxeuMk
**Reformation as Big Wreck Version 2.0** : The band reunited around 2010-2011, initiated by Ian Thorneley missing the connection with former members. A Thorneley tour stop near Sarnia, where Brian Dorody lived, led to Dorody filling in for a show when the existing guitarist was unavailable, sparking the idea for a new Big Wreck era. "And then all of a sudden, there's Big Rec version 2." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**Ruben Huzenga's Collaboration** : Guitarist Ruben Huzenga, formerly of Glüleg, collaborated with Big Wreck in his personal studio. This illustrates his continued involvement in Canadian music production and inter-band relationships. "Ruben Huzenga has his own studio where he's worked with Edwin and also with Big Rec." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=22SSwvfRMVg
**Search for a Singer** : Initially, Big Wreck struggled to find a suitable singer, leading to Ian Thornley reluctantly taking on the vocal duties himself. They even tried out one singer who turned out to be associated with a "gang" and was suspected of stealing from the band's van, leading to his swift dismissal. "How did you end up singing? Because we couldn't find one." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z9QChV543aQ
**Version 1.0 Dissolution** : The original lineup of Big Wreck (version 1.0) lasted for exactly two albums, with its breakup influenced by factors including record label interference, extremely long touring periods (up to 18 months straight), and internal conflicts within the band. "So big rec version 1.0 lasts exactly two albums." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z9QChV543aQ
