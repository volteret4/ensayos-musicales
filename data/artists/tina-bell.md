## curiosities
**Facing Racial Abuse as a Punk Singer** : As a Black female punk singer in the early 1980s, Tina Bell faced significant challenges and endured a lot of racial abuse. Her toughness was a necessity in navigating this hostile environment within the music scene. "She had to be because a Black female punk singer? A lot of racial abuse was hurled at her." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
**Godmother of Grunge and First Female Grunge Frontperson** : Tina Bell, a Black woman, fronted the group Bam Bam, which formed in 1983, placing her at the very earliest stages of Grunge in the early 1980s. She is recognized as the genre's first ever female front person and is considered a founder of the sound that defined 1990s rock, earning her the title "Godmother of Grunge." "If we look at things in context of the history of Grunge, she was the genre's first ever female front person." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
**Kurt Cobain as a Roadie** : Bam Bam attracted fans, and at one point, a young Kurt Cobain worked as a roadie for the band, highlighting their early presence and connection within the nascent Seattle music scene. "But Bam Bam also had their fans, some kid named Kurt Cobain once worked as a roadie for Bam Bam." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
**Pioneering Recording at Reciprocal Recording** : Bam Bam made their first record, the "Villains" EP, in 1984 at Reciprocal Recording. This made them the first Grunge band to record at that studio, which later became famous for hosting bands like Nirvana, Alice in Chains, Soundgarden, The Melvins, and Mudhoney. "They used reciprocal recording, making them the first Grunge band to make music there." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
**Struggles with Recognition and Later Life** : Despite their early innovation and connections within the Seattle scene, Bam Bam struggled to gain traction and were largely ignored while other local bands achieved success. Tina left in 1990, moved to England and then the Netherlands, but was deported back to the US. She quit music, divorced, battled depression, alcoholism, and cirrhosis, eventually dying in Las Vegas on October 10, 2012, at age 55, with her possessions tragically lost. "But Bam Bam had a hard time gaining any kind of traction." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
