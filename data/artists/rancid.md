## albums
**...And Out Come the Wolves (1995)** : Released in the summer of 1995, this third album by Rancid was a platinum seller that effectively bridged the grunge scene and punk, while also bringing back the dependable Ska feel. The band's rise was part of the punk revival, alongside groups like Green Day and The Offspring, and it played a significant role in the third wave of Ska. "In the summer of 1995, they released their third album and Outcome the Wolves. And it bridged everything from the grunge scene and punk, as well as bringing back the old dependable SCAT feel. And the result was a platinum album." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WX2GlpMNg_8
**Let's Go (1994)** : This was Rancid's second album, released on June 21, 1994. The band maintained their punk rock ethics by staying with Epitaph but agreed to a strategic compromise where Warner Brothers, a major label, would distribute and "stealthily promote" their records. "Ransett met the industry halfway. They stayed with epitaph, which pleased bad religion and preserved their punk rock ethics, but they agreed to have their epitaph records distributed and stealthily promoted by Warner Brothers, one of the majors." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gkLMwrthZ9s
**Let's Go (1994) - Second Album** : Released five months after Green Day's *Dookie*, *Let's Go* benefited from the burgeoning appetite for resurrected American punk rock. While not reaching the massive sales of *Dookie*, it peaked at number 97 on the album charts and sold over half a million copies, a significant achievement for an indie band. "Ranset from Let's Go, their second album which came out five months after Duckey. Good timing because by then there was this growing appetite for this new resurrected form of American punk rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0QtMI00T5jw
**Life Won't Wait (1998)** : The 1998 album _Life Won't Wait_ includes the song "Turn Table." "It's from Rancid's 1998 album, Life Won't Wait." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IIJySSo276k

## curiosities
**Accusations of Selling Out** : As Rancid gained popularity and commercial recognition, they, alongside other successful pop punk bands like Green Day and The Offspring, were accused of "selling out" by punk purists. "purists became annoyed, accusing groups like Green Dan, the offspring and Rancidov selling out." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Band Origins and Label Strategy** : Rancid evolved from the revered band Operation Ivy and were label-mates with Bad Religion on Epitaph. Their songs were known for being short, tight, and melodic, perfectly complementing the sound kids sought after Green Day's success. They balanced punk ethics with commercial reach by remaining on Epitaph while securing distribution and quiet promotion from Warner Brothers. "Not only was Ransett label-mates with bad religion, but they devolved out of a band called Operation Ivy, a group that was revered by a young Billy Joe Armstrong, who of course was in Green Day" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gkLMwrthZ9s
**Clash-like Sound** : This band was noted for sometimes sounding more like The Clash than The Clash themselves. "Ransett, who sometimes sounded more clash than the clash." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Commitment to Indie Label** : Unlike Green Day, Rancid opted to remain an independent band, recording their music for Epitaph Records, the label founded by members of Bad Religion. "But unlike Green Day, Ranset chose to stay indie, recording for Bad Religion's Epitaph Records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0QtMI00T5jw
**Connecting Fans to Original Punk** : Through Rancid's influence, many younger fans were introduced to the original British punk bands of the 1970s, such as The Clash. This connection also led to a resurgence of previously out-of-fashion styles, like big spiky mohawks, at Rancid's shows. "And through Ranset, a lot of kids were led back to the original British punks of the 1970s, like the clash. Big spiky mohawks were seen at a lot of Ranset shows, something that had gone out of fashion for a while." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0QtMI00T5jw
**Dual Revival Role** : Rancid descended from the hardcore punk band Operation Ivy, which had started incorporating Ska as early as 1987. Rancid figured prominently in both the punk revival and the third wave of Ska, benefiting from the growing popularity of punk revivalists like Green Day and The Offspring. "Then came Ranset, who figured in both the punk revival and third wave SCAT. And as punk revivalists like Green Day and the offspring got bigger and bigger, Ranset was basically sucked up in their wake." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WX2GlpMNg_8
**Elder Statesmen of the Scene** : With some members approaching 30, Rancid was perceived as the "older brothers" or "elder statesmen" of the mid-90s punk scene, which garnered them a certain level of respect among younger fans. "Ranset were a little older too, with a couple of members pushing 30. They were seen as the older brothers, the elder statesmen of the scene, and that brought them a certain amount of respect." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0QtMI00T5jw
**Evolution from Operation Ivy** : Rancid formed and evolved from Operation Ivy, a Bay Area band that preceded them in the punk scene. "They evolved out of a Bay Area band called Operation Ivy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Fourth Wave Continuation** : Rancid is mentioned as a third-wave group that is finding new fans, indicating their continued relevance and contribution to the emergence of the fourth wave of Ska. "Third wave groups like Ranset and Wheel Big Fish are finding new fans." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WX2GlpMNg_8
**Hellcat Records Association** : The label Hellcat Records, which records The Interrupters, is associated with Rancid, highlighting the band's ongoing influence in the Ska scene. "They're from Los Angeles and record for Hellcat Records, a label associated with, and here's that name again, Ranset." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WX2GlpMNg_8
**Mainstream Exposure via MTV** : Despite being an indie band, Rancid was propelled into the mainstream "stratosphere" due to the immense popularity created by Green Day. MTV, searching for more songs similar to Green Day's hits, discovered Rancid and their song "Salvation," giving them significant exposure. "But because of the huge vortex formed around Green Day, Ranset was sucked up into the stratosphere anyway, thanks largely to MTV. They went looking for more songs like Basket Case and Longview, and that's when they found Ranset and Salvation." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0QtMI00T5jw
**Melodic Enough for Prime Time** : Rancid was identified as a "pure punk band" whose melodic qualities made them suitable for broader mainstream exposure. This characteristic helped them appeal to a wider audience beyond traditional punk circles. "Another pure punk band that was deemed melodic enough for prime time was Rancid." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Multiple Warped Tour Appearances** : Rancid has agreed to participate in the Warped Tour at least three times. "Ransid has agreed to participate at least three times." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Origins and Green Day's Support** : Rancid, a Bay Area band, formed in 1991 following the disbandment of Operation Ivy, which Green Day members Billy Joe Armstrong and Mike Dirnt had been big fans of. As Green Day gained prominence, they actively promoted Rancid, drawing them into the mainstream spotlight. "Ranset came together in 1991 once Op Ivy disbanded after a couple of years, and as Green Day was blowing up, they chatted up Ranset whenever they could." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0QtMI00T5jw
**Tim Armstrong's Support for Famous Stars and Straps** : Tim Armstrong of Rancid is noted for publicly wearing gear from Travis Barker's clothing company, Famous Stars and Straps, helping to promote the brand. This shows support among musicians for each other's entrepreneurial ventures. "If you look carefully, you can see Travis's gear on you guys like Tim Armstrong of Ransid". ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WIFxAaReLn0
