## albums
**Elastica (1995)** : This debut album, released during the Britpop era, faced multiple claims of musical similarities. The song "Connection" was admitted by Justine Frischmann to have "nicked some of Three Girl Rumba" from Wire, leading to a surrender of a "certain amount of the royalties from that first album to Wire." Additionally, "Waking Up" was found to be similar to The Stranglers' "No More Heroes," resulting in "a piece of that first Elastica album" being awarded to The Stranglers. "The ruling was that Elastica had to surrender a certain amount of the royalties from that first album to Wire, which, you know, given her admission of guilt seems kind of fair." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rfObVld9rIE
**Elastica (1995) – Debut Record** : Released in 1995, this album quickly became the fastest-selling debut record since the first Oasis album released the previous year. "When that first record came out, it became the fastest-selling debut record since the first oasis album the year before." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI

This admission led to a settlement without a court case. "In fact, she admitted to being such a big fan that she deliberately nicked some of Three Girl Rumbaw for one of her songs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L09R2evjyMc
**Admission of Influence and Theft** : Justine Frischmann, the leader of Elastica, openly "admitted to being such a big fan" of Wire that she "deliberately nicks some of Three Girl Rumba for one of her songs," leading to royalties from Elastica's first album being awarded to Wire. Another song, "Waking Up," also led to a portion of the album's royalties being awarded to The Stranglers for similarity to their song "No More Heroes," categorizing it as "well-intentioned theft." "In fact, she admitted to being such a big fan that she deliberately nicks some of Three Girl Rumba for one of her songs, you know, as sort of a tribute or something." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rfObVld9rIE
**Britpop Era Band** : Elastica was a prominent band during the Britpop era of the mid-1990s, an era characterized by celebrating Britishness and acknowledging predecessors in British rock. "Elastica was one of the great bands of the Britpop era of the middle 1990s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L09R2evjyMc
**Britpop's "First Couple"** : Frontwoman Justine Frischmann's partnership with Damon Albarn of Blur positioned them as "the first couple of a moment" within the burgeoning Britpop scene, drawing significant media attention. "Justine was partnered out with Damon Albarn of Blur and together they essentially served as the first couple of a moment." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI
**Heroin's Impact and Band Decline** : The band's potential was severely undermined by a major heroin addiction issue that "invaded the band." This led to a five-year delay in recording their follow-up album, by which time Britpop had faded, and the album was poorly received, ultimately resulting in the band's breakup. "The sad part of their story is that heroin invaded the band in a major, major way." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI
**Justine Frischmann's Post-Elastica Career** : After Elastica disbanded, Justine Frischmann collaborated with MIA, co-writing songs and achieving a hit. She also worked in television, married a professor of atmospheric science at the University of California Davis, and now works as a visual artist in San Francisco. "Justine wrote some songs with MIA and had a hit with her.
