# artist - Elastica

## members
- Annie Holland
- Donna Matthews
- Justin Welch
- Justine Frischmann (frontwoman, singer, songwriter, guitarist)

## genres
- Brit Pop
- Britpop
- New Wave of New Wave

## albums
**Elastica (1995) – Debut Album** : Released in 1995. Became the fastest-selling debut record since the first Oasis album the year before.
**Second Album (Undated)** : Took five years to record due to heroin issues within the band. Released when Britpop was over and was deemed "not very good at all."

## songs
**Stutter (1993)** : Released in late 1993, this song noted similarities to original 1970s New Wave.
**Stutter (1995)** : A song from their 1995 debut record, characterized as "short, sharp, and fun."

## curiosities
**"New Wave of New Wave" Affiliation** : Part of the "new wave of new wave" movement, which was short-lived and later subsumed into Brit Pop.
**Britpop's "First Couple"** : Justine Frischmann's partnership with Damon Albarn of Blur led them to be considered "the first couple of a moment" in Britpop.
**Heroin Issues** : Heroin "invaded the band in a major, major way," causing a five-year delay for their follow-up album and contributing to the band's eventual breakup.
**Justine Frischmann's Background** : Justine Frischmann is the daughter of a "well-to-do architect" and a former member of the band Suede.
**Plagiarism Lawsuits** : Sued twice, once by Wire and another time by The Stranglers, for "nicking bits" of their old songs due to their strong influence from 70s New Wave.
**Post-Breakup Careers (Justine Frischmann)** : After Elastica broke up, Justine Frischmann wrote songs with M.I.A. (having a hit), did some TV work, married an atmospheric science professor at the University of California Davis, and now works as a visual artist in the San Francisco area.
**Rapid Fame** : The band became "super famous, super fast" even before releasing their debut album.
