## albums
**Elastica (1995) – Debut Album** : Released in 1995. Became the fastest-selling debut record since the first Oasis album the year before. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI
**Elastica (1995) – Self-Titled Debut** : The band's debut album, released in March (following a November 1994 single), was described as "fresh, very fun, and very good," and also "familiar sounding." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg
**Second Album (Undated)** : Took five years to record due to heroin issues within the band. Released when Britpop was over and was deemed "not very good at all." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI

## songs
**Connection (1994)** : This track "nicked" elements from Wire’s "Three Girl Rumba". Leader Justine Frishman admitted to the deliberate borrowing as a tribute, which resulted in the band surrendering a portion of their album royalties to Wire. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rfObVld9rIE
**Stutter (1993)** : Released in late 1993, this song noted similarities to original 1970s New Wave. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
**Stutter (1994)** : A single from their self-titled debut album, released in November 1994. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg
**Stutter (1995)** : A song from their 1995 debut record, characterized as "short, sharp, and fun." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI
**Waking Up (1995)** : A song found to be "awfully like" The Stranglers' "No More Heroes," leading to another royalty settlement for the band's debut album. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rfObVld9rIE

## curiosities
**"New Wave of New Wave" Affiliation** : Part of the "new wave of new wave" movement, which was short-lived and later subsumed into Brit Pop. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
**Britpop's "First Couple"** : Justine Frischmann's partnership with Damon Albarn of Blur led them to be considered "the first couple of a moment" in Britpop. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI
**Britpop's First Couple (Mid-1990s)** : Justin Frischmann was Damon Albarn's (Blur) girlfriend, making them "Britpop's first couple." This relationship later broke up "mostly over drug problems." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg
**Former Suede Member** : Frontwoman Justin Frischmann was a former member of Suede, a connection seen as "perfect" for Britpop coverage. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg
**Heroin Habits and Creative Stagnation (Late Britpop)** : Most of the band, including leader Justin Frischmann, acquired "heavy heroin habits," rendering them unable to "get it together long enough to write a second album." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg
**Heroin Issues** : Heroin "invaded the band in a major, major way," causing a five-year delay for their follow-up album and contributing to the band's eventual breakup. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI
**Justine Frischmann's Background** : Justine Frischmann is the daughter of a "well-to-do architect" and a former member of the band Suede. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI
**Plagiarism Lawsuits** : Sued twice, once by Wire and another time by The Stranglers, for "nicking bits" of their old songs due to their strong influence from 70s New Wave. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
**Post-Breakup Careers (Justine Frischmann)** : After Elastica broke up, Justine Frischmann wrote songs with M.I.A. (having a hit), did some TV work, married an atmospheric science professor at the University of California Davis, and now works as a visual artist in the San Francisco area. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI
**Post-Britpop Status (Late 1997)** : As Britpop ended, the band "evaporated." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg
**Rapid Fame** : The band became "super famous, super fast" even before releasing their debut album. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI
