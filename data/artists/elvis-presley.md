## songs
**My Happiness (2015) – Acetate Recording** : This unique acetate recording was originally made by Elvis Presley as a birthday present for his mother. This specific acetate played a pivotal role in his discovery by Sam Phillips and is considered a foundational record in the history of rock and roll. The sole copy was purchased by Jack White in 2015 for $300,000. "This acetate, which is another name for it, is My Happiness by Elvis Presley." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IIJySSo276k

## curiosities
**"I Hate Elvis" Buttons** : Colonel Tom Parker, Elvis Presley's manager, was known for his astute business sense and constant pursuit of profit. When he and Elvis ventured into "hostile territory," Parker capitalized on the negative sentiment by selling "I hate Elvis" buttons to those who disliked the star, reportedly making a significant fortune. "When he and Elvis went into hostile territory, he sold I hate Elvis buttons to haters. Made a fortune." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GO0K_N7Ys7I
**Army Enlistment and Rock's Decline (1958)** : On March 2, 1958, Elvis enlisted in the army, a pivotal moment that led to Rock and Roll losing its "king" and subsequently entering a period of decline that lasted well into the 1960s. "That's the day Elvis enlisted in the army and Rock and Roll lost its king." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Becoming Mainstream** : As Elvis's career progressed, he increasingly became a mainstream star, which led to other artists seeking to further subvert musical norms. "But as Elvis became more of a mainstream star, there were those who were already looking to subvert things even further." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U
**Elvis's Wart and Toenail Clipping Collectibles** : A dedicated Elvis collector named Joni Mabe from Athens, Georgia, possesses unique memorabilia: the preserved remains of a wart Elvis had removed in 1957 before joining the army, kept in a tube of formaldehyde. Mabe claims to have been offered $1 million for the wart on two separate occasions and also owns one of Elvis's toenail clippings. "She has the remains of a wart. Elvis had removed in 1957 before he went into the army." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-N6IM5Nm5iw
**Featured in Apple Commercials** : Elvis Presley's music has been utilized in Apple's high-profile TV commercial campaigns, particularly for the iPod and iPhone, demonstrating the tech company's use of iconic musical figures to capture audience attention. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sfie4YYncxk
**First True Rock and Roll Star** : Elvis Presley, a young white truck driver who was said to sound like he was black, became the first true rock and roll star. In the context of the 1950s, if rock and roll was considered alternative to the mainstream, then Elvis could be seen as the first alternative music star. "Elvis Presley was the first true rocket roll star, and if rocket roll was the alternative to the mainstream in the 1950s, we could also say that Elvis was our first alternative music star too." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U
