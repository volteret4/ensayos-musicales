## songs
**Pump Up The Volume (1987) – Groundbreaking Sample-Based Single** : Created by five London-based producers and musicians (Martin, Alex, Rudy, Ivo Watts Russell, and Stephen, whose initials formed the band name M/A/R/R/S), this single was a groundbreaking musical collage. It extensively used samples from diverse sources including Israeli singer Ofra Haza, James Brown, the Iranian Revolutionary Army chorus, and rap duo Eric B. & Rakim, with the latter's sample providing the song's title. Initially distributed anonymously on 500 white labels to club DJs in the fall of 1987, its sounds and ideas rapidly spread through dance clubs and eventually into mainstream pop and rock, making a long-lasting impact despite not being the first sampled hit. ## curiosities
**White Label Distribution of "Pump Up The Volume"** : To introduce their innovative sample-heavy single "Pump Up The Volume," M/A/R/R/S distributed 500 anonymous white label copies to club DJs. This strategy allowed the track's sounds and ideas to spread organically through dance clubs before breaking into mainstream pop and rock, contributing to its significant and lasting impact.
