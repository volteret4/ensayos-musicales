# artist - Thorneley

## members
- Brian Dorody (initially excluded, later joined for shows)
- Ian Thorneley
- Jeff Burles (drummer for some shows)
- Paula (guitar player)

## labels
- 604 Records

## albums
**Come Again (2004) – Debut Album** : Released on May 11, 2004, this was the debut album for Ian Thorneley's new band "Thorneley" under 604 Records. It produced a huge single on Canadian radio and earned two Juno nominations, with at least four singles making it to radio. "The debut from this new group was called Come Again and it was released on May 11th, 2004." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**Tiny Pictures (2009) – Second Album** : Released in February 2009, five years after "Come Again," much of the material for this album had been written years prior but delayed due to Ian Thorneley's touring commitments with other bands. Ian played most of the instruments, with songwriting collaborations including David Gen. "This was the second album released under the name Thorneley, and came out five years after the first one, February 2009." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM

## curiosities
**Creative Differences with Management** : Brian Dorody, a holdover from Big Wreck, was initially excluded from the Thorneley lineup because management felt his presence would make the new band too similar to Big Wreck, aiming for Thorneley to be a "separate sort of thing." This decision added strain to relationships. "Brian was initially going to be the other guitar player in Thorneley. And then the sort of what's called the grown-ups, the people on the other side of the desk, where like, it's too much like Big Rec of Brian's around." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**Formation of the Band** : After the first Big Wreck disbanded, Ian Thorneley moved to Toronto, got married, and began writing, leading to the formation of the band "Thorneley." The lineup, except for Brian Dorody, was new. "His relationship with Chad Kruger of Nickelback led him deciding with 604 Records out of Vancouver. And this was the beginning of a brand new band simply called Thorneley..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**Touring and Lineup Changes** : The band Thorneley engaged in tours, including one where guitarist Paula announced she would be absent for a gig in Edmonton due to her honeymoon. This unexpected vacancy led to Brian Dorody filling in, which subsequently contributed to the reformation of Big Wreck. "Paula was like, oh, by the way, that gig in Edmonton, I'm not going to be there. As I'm going on my honeymoon in Portugal..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
