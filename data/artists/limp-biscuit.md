## curiosities
**Breakup Date (November 8, 2005)** : This date, following the release of their greatest hits album and subsequent silence, is used as the band's official breakup date. "So what we're going to do is use that date as our breakup date. November 8th 2005." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yR3xc3ra5tE
**Drummer John Otto's Issues** : Drummer John Otto also experienced issues that limited his participation. "And drummer John Otto had issues too. So he wasn't able to do much." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yR3xc3ra5tE
**First Reunion Gig (May 2009) – Riga, Latvia** : Three months after the reunion announcement, the original lineup, which had not been seen together in eight years, played their first gig in Riga, Latvia. "Three months later, the original lineup, something that we hadn't seen in eight years played their first gig together." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yR3xc3ra5tE
**Fred Durst as Executive** : Executives at the band's record company thought highly enough of Fred Durst to appoint him an executive. "executives at their record company thought enough of Fred Durst to make him an executive." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yR3xc3ra5tE
**Guitarist Wes Borland's Departures and Return (2001-2004)** : Guitarist Wes Borland initially quit the band in 2001. He was replaced by Mike Smith, but Smith departed by 2004, leading to Borland's return. "First guitarist Wes Borland had quit 2001. He was replaced by Mike Smith, but by 2004 he had gone. Wes was back." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yR3xc3ra5tE
**New Album Work Begins (August 24, 2009)** : By August 24, 2009, all members of the reunited band had begun working on a new album. "And in August the 24th of 2009, everyone started working on a new album." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yR3xc3ra5tE
**Public Disfavor & Internal Issues (Mid-2005)** : By mid-2005, Limp Biscuit, and Fred Durst in particular, had "worn out their welcome with the public" as their sound and approach had gone "out of style." This period was also marked by internal problems. "But by the middle of 2005, Limp Biscuit in general and Fred Durst in particular had worn out their welcome with the public." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yR3xc3ra5tE
**Reunion Announcement (February 11, 2009)** : On this date, Fred Durst posted a message on Twitter – "1am Pacific time. Limp Biscuit.com tonight" – signaling the band's official return. "And on February 11th of 2009, those who were following Fred on Twitter... got this message. 1am Pacific time. Limp Biscuit.com tonight." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yR3xc3ra5tE
**Reunion Rumors (Fall 2008)** : By the fall of 2008, rumors began circulating that the band's internal differences had been resolved and they were reuniting. "There were rumors that differences had been fixed and that Limp Biscuit was back together." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yR3xc3ra5tE
**Time Apart (3 years, 3 months, 3 days)** : The band was apart for this duration, from their designated breakup date of November 8, 2005, to their reunion announcement on February 11, 2009. "Let Biscuit and Nookie, apart for three years, three months and three days." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yR3xc3ra5tE
