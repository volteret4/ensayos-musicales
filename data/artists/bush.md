## albums
**16 Stone (1994) – Breakthrough Album** : Released on December 6, 1994, this album, once discovered by the public, sold 50,000 copies a week for months on end. Its success was a significant factor in the band selling approximately 20 million records worldwide during the 1990s. "That's Bush and Machine Head from 16 Stone, an album released on December 6, 1994." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lGNrJgwQjgg
**Man On the Run (2014) – Personal Label Release** : This album was released on Gavin Rossdale's personal label, Zoomer Rock Records. Initially self-funded, a distribution deal was later secured with Red after Rossdale's manager, Lenny Beer, initiated talks and Red expressed interest beyond simple distribution, eventually taking over further aspects of the release. "I worked really hard on Man On the Run, this record that we have now." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lGNrJgwQjgg
**Razorblade Suitcase (1996)** : This album also achieved multi-million sales, indicating its commercial success. Gavin Rossdale referenced doing a lot of "research and swallowing" from this record, suggesting a deep immersion in its creation or impact. "And so I was doing a lot of research and swallowing from 1996's Razorblade suitcase." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lGNrJgwQjgg
**Sea of Memories (2011) – Self-Funded Comeback** : This record was largely recorded while Gavin Rossdale was still signed to Interscope, with support from Jimmy Iovine. Nearing completion, Rossdale left Interscope and personally funded the album's setup and distribution. It successfully reached number one in America, marking the first time a self-released record achieved this feat, and also performed well in Canada, eventually repaying his initial investment through touring. "First self-released ever to do that here in America." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lGNrJgwQjgg
**The Science of Things (1999)** : Released in October 1999, this album hit the market about four months after Napster launched, placing it in a period of significant industry change due to digital file sharing. "The chemicals between us, from the 1999 album, The Science of Things." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lGNrJgwQjgg

## curiosities
**Active Rock Radio Crossover** : This band saw crossover airplay on active rock radio stations, helping the format become less of a niche. "But there was also some crossover with alternative rock stations, offspring, food fighters, bush and so on." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Artist Philosophy on Performance and Creation** : Rossdale's artistic philosophy centers on pouring maximum effort into making the best possible record, free from commercial expectations or statistical pressures. He performs every live show with the intensity of it being his last, maintaining the same energy and intention for audiences of 400 or 40,000, believing this commitment is vital for an artist's longevity and to earn audience support. "The main thing my only job and my only concern is to pour over that record and make it the best that I can so that if God forbid, somebody takes this time and doesn't do the whole thing on speakers with somebody else, they can enjoy it together." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lGNrJgwQjgg
**Career Transition from 90s Rock Star to Indie Musician** : Gavin Rossdale's journey with Bush exemplifies the transition from multi-platinum success in the 1990s to adapting to the contemporary, post-CD music business. He consciously embraced the new realities, including digital distribution and self-funding, as a means to continue making music. "How can a musician who enjoyed multi-platinum glory days in the 1990s adjust to the new realities of the music business today?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lGNrJgwQjgg
**Departure from Interscope and Self-Funding** : Rossdale made a proactive decision to leave Interscope after a conversation with Jimmy Iovine, opting to fund his album "Sea of Memories" himself rather than wait to be "tapped on the shoulder." Interscope was generous in providing previous recordings, but the bulk of the album's setup and execution was a personal investment. "I had a conversation with Jimmy and I wanted to leave before I was tapped on the shoulder into whatever." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lGNrJgwQjgg
**Early Career Gamble and Indispensability Avoidance** : In his early career, Rossdale deliberately chose menial labor jobs that paid no more than $50 a day and where he could not become indispensable. This strategic choice was made to prevent any alternative career path from becoming too enticing and diverting him from his primary goal of pursuing music, a significant gamble that ultimately paid off. "Every job I did was only mean your labor and it was only in places where I knew that they would never, I could not become indispensable." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lGNrJgwQjgg
**Embracing Digital Consumption and Streaming** : Rossdale actively uses Spotify Premium and purchases music from Beatport for harder-to-find tracks, acknowledging the collective effort required in the new music ecosystem. He views streaming as "absolutely incredible" for fostering interest and providing instant access to music. "So I fully embraced the way that music is now, and I have my Spotify premium." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lGNrJgwQjgg
**Entrepreneurial Approach and Personal Label** : Rossdale has adopted an entrepreneurial mindset, effectively running his own music business with the assistance of managers and distribution deals. The release of his 2014 album "Man On the Run" on his personal label, Zoomer Rock Records, signifies a return to the independent artist model, reminiscent of his early career. "So you've turned into a real entrepreneur. You're doing things, you're running your own business essentially right now with help from a couple of managers and you've got a distribution deal and that's the thing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lGNrJgwQjgg
