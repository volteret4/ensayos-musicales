# artist - Pere Ubu

## members
- Dave Thomas (lead singer)

## albums
**Cloudland (1989)** : This album, released in 1989, includes the song "Waiting for Mary," which brought Pere Ubu close to achieving a genuine alternative radio hit. Despite their consistent cult status, this album and song represent one of their most significant bids for wider recognition. "It's from an album called Cloudland." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7_ZmZxnSOnE

## songs
**Final Solution (1976)** : Recorded in early 1976, this was the third song ever recorded by Pere Ubu and became an "indie rock classic." Its unique and chilling sound was unlike anything else being made in 1976, helping it reach listeners across North America and the UK. "Recorded in early 1976, the third Pair Ubu song ever to be recorded, this is called Final Solution." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4
**Waiting for Mary (1989)** : This song from the 1989 album *Cloudland* brought Pere Ubu close to having a genuine alternative radio hit. It features Dave Thomas's distinctive high-pitched voice. The band, including Thomas, continues to be a cult favorite. "It's called Waiting for Mary." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7_ZmZxnSOnE

## curiosities
**Cleveland Origin and Sound (1975)** : Pere Ubu emerged from Cleveland, having reorganized from the band Rocket from the Tombs in 1975. They were part of a scene of "extreme groups" that were artsy, anti-hippie, anti-establishment, and experimental. They began releasing self-produced indie singles in 1975. "Eventually we organized itself as Pair Ubu. In 1975, they began to release a series of self-produced indie singles that reached a few selecteers around North America. And even as far away as the UK." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4
**Cult Status** : Pere Ubu has never achieved widespread mainstream success, maintaining a consistent cult status throughout their career. They came close to a genuine alternative radio hit in 1989 with their song "Waiting for Mary" from the album *Cloudland*. "pair Ubu never ascended above cult status, but they came close to having a genuine alternative radio hit in 1989 with this song." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7_ZmZxnSOnE
**Embraced by New York Avant-garde** : When Pere Ubu brought their distinctive Cleveland sound to New York, they were "embraced" by the artsy community, including "soulmates" like Television and Patti Smith. "When Ubu brought this music to New York, home of artsy soulmates like television and Patsy Smith, they were embraced." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4
**Formation and Dave Thomas's Voice** : Pere Ubu formed in Cleveland in 1975 with Dave Thomas as their lead singer. Thomas is described as a large man with a surprisingly high-pitched voice, which can be initially disarming. Despite his unusual vocal style, the band maintains a cult following. "He's a big bear of a dude, but with a very high pitched voice." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7_ZmZxnSOnE
