## albums

The album title lent its name to the entire subgenre of punk, "hardcore," after the band extensively toured in support of it. "On April 22, 1981, the group released their second album. ## curiosities
**Documented Moshing in Film** : DOA was one of the groups highlighted in "The Decline of Western Civilization," a film that captured and disseminated images of moshing within the punk scenes of D.C. and Southern California. "The bands ranging from the Ramones to the dead canadies to DOA to minor threat." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KMx4bmq8o2Y
**Iconic Vancouver Hardcore Band** : DOA is recognized as a prominent band from Vancouver's 70s and 80s hardcore scene, which was a defining musical characteristic of the city during that era. Matthew Good referenced them while discussing the loss of Vancouver's influential musical "edge." "I can't see no means no or DOA or adversity or anyone doing that." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=--g0gugy3ZE
**Independent Label Founding** : Like many other early hardcore bands, DOA had to establish their own indie label, Sudden Death Records, to release their music. "By the way, DOA also had to form their own indie label to get their music out there and they called it Sudden Death Records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PEHkKfmmDiI
**Naming the Hardcore Genre** : DOA is credited with giving the subgenre of punk its name. Their second album, released on April 22, 1981, was titled "Hardcore 81," and the term caught on and has been used ever since to refer to this harder, faster, louder, and more aggressive form of punk. "Second, it was DOA that gave this new, harder, faster, louder and more aggressive form of punk its name." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PEHkKfmmDiI
**West Coast Touring Network ("The List")** : DOA was instrumental in compiling and trading "the list," a growing network of punk-friendly venues and places to stay across North America. This list significantly expanded the reach of punk and hardcore bands throughout the continent. "DOA compiled something called the list. This was a growing list of punk-friendly venues and places to stay across North America that was made available and then traded among dozens, maybe hundreds of other like-minded bands." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PEHkKfmmDiI
