## albums
**Hardcore '81 (1981) – Album Title Credited with Naming the Genre "Hardcore Punk"** : D.O.A. evolved from a Vancouver band called The Skulls and played their first gig as D.O.A. on February 20, 1978 at the Japanese Hall. Their debut "Disco Sucks" EP in summer 1978 was funded by the band's pooled unemployment checks. Unlike Toronto bands (who could drive to New York), Vancouver's geography forced them south to LA and east from there; their constant touring led them to compile a list of punk-friendly venues and crash spaces across North America — "The List" — which they shared freely with other bands and which helped establish a coast-to-coast touring infrastructure for punk. Their 1981 album *Hardcore '81* and the tour supporting it are generally credited with giving the fast, heavy, aggressive variant of punk its name: hardcore. "DOA helped create an international punk community by compiling a list of punk-friendly places to play and places to stay." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0X7TQMGgqbY
**Hardcore 81 (1981)** : This album is widely credited with popularizing, if not outright inventing, the term "hardcore" for the genre. Its release was pivotal in defining and disseminating the new sound. "Vancouver's D.O.A released an album entitled Hardcore 81, which if it didn't invent the term, sure made it popular." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0ARYt4sYT90
**Hardcore 81 (1981) – Naming the Genre** : Released on April 22, 1981, this was D.O.A.'s second album. The band toured extensively to support it, and the album's title, "Hardcore 81," is credited with giving the entire subgenre of punk its name. "On April 22, 1981, the group released their second album. They called it Hardcore 81 and then they toured the hell out of it. The term Hardcore caught on and ever since, that's been the name for this subgenre of punk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0qigzi1j81U

## curiosities
**"The List" of Venues** : D.O.A. compiled and traded "the list," which was a growing catalog of punk-friendly venues and places to stay across North America. This list was shared among dozens, if not hundreds, of like-minded bands, significantly expanding the reach of punk and hardcore across the continent. "DOA compiled something called the list. This was a growing list of punk-friendly venues and places to stay across North America that was made available and then traded among dozens, maybe hundreds of other like-minded bands." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0qigzi1j81U
**Coined "Hardcore" Term** : D.O.A. is credited with giving the new, harder, faster, louder, and more aggressive form of punk its name. They achieved this with the release of their second album, "Hardcore 81," on April 22, 1981, after which the term caught on and became the official name for the subgenre. "Second, it was DOA that gave this new, harder, faster, louder and more aggressive form of punk its name. On April 22, 1981, the group released their second album. They called it Hardcore 81 and then they toured the hell out of it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0qigzi1j81U
**Godfathers of Hardcore** : Hailing from Vancouver, D.O.A. are considered the godfathers of the hardcore genre. Their album "Hardcore 81" was instrumental in popularizing the term and sound. The band's music exemplified early hardcore's strong anti-establishment stance. "And now they're considered to be the godfathers of the genre." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0ARYt4sYT90
**West Coast Touring Strategy** : D.O.A., based in Vancouver, primarily toured north-south along the West Coast from San Diego to Vancouver, finding it easier and more affordable than east-west travel due to the Rocky Mountains. They would then head east once they got south. "Vancouver's DOA was very much part of this and while they did tour through Canada, it was often easier to go up and down the west coast playing the same gigs as their hardcore brother." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0qigzi1j81U
