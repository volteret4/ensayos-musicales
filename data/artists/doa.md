**Hardcore '81 (1981) – Album Title Credited with Naming the Genre "Hardcore Punk"** : D.O.A. evolved from a Vancouver band called The Skulls and played their first gig as D.O.A. on February 20, 1978 at the Japanese Hall. Their debut "Disco Sucks" EP in summer 1978 was funded by the band's pooled unemployment checks. Unlike Toronto bands (who could drive to New York), Vancouver's geography forced them south to LA and east from there; their constant touring led them to compile a list of punk-friendly venues and crash spaces across North America — "The List" — which they shared freely with other bands and which helped establish a coast-to-coast touring infrastructure for punk. Their 1981 album *Hardcore '81* and the tour supporting it are generally credited with giving the fast, heavy, aggressive variant of punk its name: hardcore. "DOA helped create an international punk community by compiling a list of punk-friendly places to play and places to stay." ← Ongoing History of New Music > 14 Important Canadian Punk Bands | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0X7TQMGgqbY&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Hardcore 81 (1981) – Naming the Genre** : Released on April 22, 1981, this was D.O.A.'s second album. The band toured extensively to support it, and the album's title, "Hardcore 81," is credited with giving the entire subgenre of punk its name. "On April 22, 1981, the group released their second album. They called it Hardcore 81 and then they toured the hell out of it. **West Coast Touring Strategy** : D.O.A., based in Vancouver, primarily toured north-south along the West Coast from San Diego to Vancouver, finding it easier and more affordable than east-west travel due to the Rocky Mountains. They would then head east once they got south.
