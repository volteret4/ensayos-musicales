## curiosities
**Adoption of Magnetic Tape – Catalyst for Industry Shift** : Bing Crosby was convinced by Major Jack Mullin to adopt magnetic tape recording for his radio shows. This endorsement played a critical role in normalizing tape recording technology in professional audio production. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OrHtPVBjXw4
**Desire for Golf Time** : As a major radio star, Crosby hated performing two live shows daily to cover both East and West Coast time zones. He realized the magnetophone would allow him to record a show once and then go play golf while technicians broadcast the tape later. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
**Intimate Vocal Delivery** : Like Sinatra, Crosby utilized microphone technology to move away from the "big lungs" requirement of the acoustic era. He sang intimately and close to the microphone, creating a sense of romance and emotion previously impossible to capture. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
**Investment in Ampex** : Crosby hired Jack Mullen as his chief engineer and invested $50,000 (valued at over half a million dollars today) into Ampex. This investment helped Ampex set the global standard for audio and video tape recording. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
**Involvement with the Magnetophone (1947)** : Crosby’s musical director, Merton McKenzie, introduced him to Jack Mullen’s demonstration of the Nazi-invented magnetophone in 1947. Crosby was immediately interested in the technology's potential for radio. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
