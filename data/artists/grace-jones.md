## curiosities
**Dynamic Personality and Image** : Grace Jones, a Jamaican model, singer, record producer, songwriter, and actress, possessed a personality and image so versatile that she felt at home in any club across the world, regardless of the dance music genre played. Her unique presence allowed her to transcend musical boundaries. "Her personality and image was such that she was at home at any kind of club anywhere in the world that played any kind of dance music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0FYq5MBdHw4
**Early 1980s Dance Club Music** : In the early 1980s, Grace Jones produced music that was described as cool, slinky, and sufficiently outside the mainstream to appeal to the alternative crowd in their non-disco dance clubs, particularly in the UK. Her sound helped bridge the gap between various dance genres. "And here's the kind of stuff she was making for dance clubs in the early 1980s. Cool, slinky, and just outside of the mainstream enough to work with the alternative crowd in their non-disco dance clubs, especially in the UK." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0FYq5MBdHw4
**Evolution of Musical Style** : Initially a disco artist, Grace Jones evolved her style to incorporate heavy influences from New Wave, the Post Punk scene, funk, technopop, and reggae. This eclectic blend made her music distinct and ahead of its time, particularly for dance clubs in the early 1980s. "Jones started with disco, but then moved toward the style that was heavily influenced by New Wave, the Post Punk scene, funk, techno pop, and reggae." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0FYq5MBdHw4
