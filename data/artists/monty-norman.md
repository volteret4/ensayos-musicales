# artist - Monty Norman

## songs
**James Bond Theme (1962) – Original Composition** : Monty Norman composed this theme, drawing inspiration from his earlier song, "Good Sign, Bad Sign," which featured Indian characters on the island of Trinidad in a musical based on the book "A House for Mr. Bizwaz" (also released around 1962). Norman adapted the melody by moving it to a 20-guitar. Although the film's producers initially disliked his version, composer John Barry later arranged it for orchestra, creating the classic rendition used in all Bond movies. Norman has continued to collect royalties for its use throughout his life, reportedly still cashing checks at 94 years old. "His inspiration was a song called Good Sign, Bad Sign, which is sung by Indian characters on the island of Trinidad in a musical based on a book called A House for Mr. Bizwaz." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K0pgJ7XAlpo

## curiosities
**James Bond Theme (1962)** : In 1962, composer Monty Norman adapted an earlier song he had written, "Good Sign, Bad Sign," by adding a twangy guitar to create the theme song for the new James Bond movie. "In 1962 a composer named Monty Norman was asked to come up with a theme song for a new movie. He looked at a song he'd written called Good Sign, Bad Sign. Then he added a 20 guitar and submitted it to the producers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pOeChe_D1Ag
