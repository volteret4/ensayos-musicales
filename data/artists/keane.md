## curiosities
**Battle, England Origins - Population 6,048** : Keane originates from Battle, England, a town with a population of 6,048. The town is historically significant as the site where William the Conqueror defeated King Harold II in 1066. "Barely 6,000 people live in battle, which is in the southeast of England, and it's called battle because back in 1066, this is where William the conqueror kicked the crap out of King Harold II, the history of England as we know it begins here. It's also in the hometown of Keene." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CyTI00SIrLE
**Cherry Keane Inspiration and Tribute** : The band switched their name to Cherry Keane, a real person and a friend of Tom Chaplin's mother. After Cherry Keane died of cancer, she left some money to Tom's family, part of which Tom invested in his musical aspirations. As a tribute, they renamed the band after her, later shortening it to just Keane. "so they switched to Cherry Keene, and Cherry Keene was a real person, a friend of Tom Chaplin's mum. Then, Cherry Keene died of cancer, and left some money to Tom's family, some of which went to Tom, which he then invested in his musical aspirations. As a tribute to her, they renamed the band after her. After a while, though, they shortened the name from Cherry Keene to just Keene." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CyTI00SIrLE
**Childhood Connection and Shared School** : Tom Chaplin eventually met Tim Rice-Oxley's brother, Tim. They later attended the same private school, which was owned by Tom Chaplin's family, fostering their early connection. "Now, eventually, Tom Chaplin met up with Tom Rice Oxley's brother Tim. They later ended up at the same private school, which was owned by Tom's family." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CyTI00SIrLE
**Community-Oriented Small Town Roots** : The band's origins and name story highlight a strong sense of community typical of a small town. "Sounds all pretty. Community oriented in small town, doesn't it? Because it was." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CyTI00SIrLE
**Debut Live Performance** : Keane made their live debut on July 13, 1998, in London, marking a significant step in their career. "Anyway, Keene made their live debut on July 13, 1998 in London." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CyTI00SIrLE
**Defining Sound with Yamaha CP70BM** : Following their guitarist's departure, Tim Rice-Oxley acquired a Yamaha CP70BM, a hybrid acoustic electric grand piano, influenced by Beatles producer George Martin's preference for the instrument. Plugging it in produced a "massive sound" that instantly became the signature sound of Keane, distinguishing them by centering their music around the piano rather than the guitar, leading to alt rock hits. "as soon as we plugged it in, it made this massive sound, and we immediately knew it was the sound of Keene. It opened up everything for the band, which started having all rock hits like this." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dJiSZ4X0F2g
**Formation Post-University** : Keane as a band didn't fully come together until 1997, after all the members had spent some time at university. "Keene, as a band, didn't really come together until 1997 after everybody had a chance to go to university for a bit, and it almost didn't happen." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CyTI00SIrLE
**Formation and Early Lineup Change** : Keane was formed in 1995 and initially included a guitar player for approximately the first five years. The departure of this guitarist created a "big hole" in the band's sound, prompting a significant shift in their instrumental lineup and ultimately their musical direction. "Keene was formed in 1995 and had a guitar player for the first five years or so, but when that guy left, there was, as Tim put it, a big hole in our sound." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dJiSZ4X0F2g
**Original Band Name - The Lotus Eaters** : The group was initially called The Lotus Eaters, but they had to change their name because another band from the 1980s already used it. "Where did the name come from? Well, the group used to be called the Lotus Edirs, but there was already a band with that name from the 1980s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CyTI00SIrLE
**Record Deal in 1999** : Following their live debut, Keane secured a record deal in 1999, which led to their albums and eventual breakthrough. "a record deal followed in 1999." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CyTI00SIrLE
**Shared Birthday and Parental Friendship** : Singer Tom Chaplin and pianist Tim Rice-Oxley were both born on March 8, 1979. Their mothers bonded over having newborns with the same birthday and subsequently became friends. "This was the day that singer Tom Chaplin was born, and it was also the same birthday as a guy named Tom Rice Oxley, the two mothers bonded over newborns, and they became friends." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CyTI00SIrLE
**Tim Rice-Oxley Declined Coldplay Offer** : Tim Rice-Oxley, a skilled piano player, was asked by Chris Martin to join his band, Coldplay, but Tim declined the offer. He believed that Keane had a better chance of success. "By this time, Tim Rice Oxley was a pretty good piano player, and some dude named Chris Martin was so impressed. He had asked him to join his band, which was called Coldplay. Tim thought that Keene was a better bat, so he said no to Chris." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CyTI00SIrLE
