## albums
**Spiral Scratch (1977) – Debut EP** : Released on January 29, 1977, on their self-established label New Hormones. This EP was the first punk record to be self-released and cost 500 pounds to record and distribute.

## songs
**Boredom (1977)** : A track from their landmark self-released EP "Spiral Scratch".

## curiosities
**Formation and Early Influences (1976)** : Formed in Manchester in 1976 by Pete Shelley and Howard Devoto, who were significantly influenced by the Velvet Underground and the burgeoning punk scene.
**Self-Release Revelation (1977)** : Energized by the nascent punk scene, and with no labels interested in their music, they established their own label, New Hormones. Their release of "Spiral Scratch" on January 29, 1977, served as the first self-released punk record, proving that anyone, even inexperienced individuals from a "musical backwater" like Manchester, could independently release a record.
**Sex Pistols Concert Promotion (June 4, 1976)** : Arranged for the then-unknown band The Sex Pistols to perform at the Lesser Free Trade Hall in Manchester on June 4, 1976. This gig became a mythical event in rock history.
**Spiral Scratch Recording Cost (1977)** : The entire recording and release of the "Spiral Scratch" EP cost 500 pounds.
