## albums
**Spiral Scratch** : This album is noted as a pioneering self-produced, independently released record by a Manchester punk band. "probably the first to release a self-produced album independently, and that would be the spiral scratch album." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4
**Spiral Scratch (1977)** : This EP was the first punk record to be self-released, entirely by the Buzzcocks themselves, without any outside help. Released on January 29, 1977, it cost them 500 pounds to record and release it on their self-established label, New Hormones. It was a revelation to young musicians, proving that anyone could release a record on their own. "On January 29, 1977, they released an EP called Spiral Scratch. This was the first punk record to be self-released that did everything by themselves with no outside help." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Spiral Scratch (1977) - First Independent British Punk Record** : Released on January 29, 1977, this four-track EP was the first "fully independent British punk rock record." Howard Devoto borrowed money from his father to finance it, and the band financed and distributed it themselves on a label they formed. This radical "do it yourself" approach proved to everyone that independent recording was possible. "So for Trackey P called Spiral Scratch and it came out on January 29th, 1977." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wYuNolSDLFM

## curiosities
**Establishing New Hormones Label** : Energized by the Sex Pistols show and facing a music scene where no labels were interested in releasing their kind of music, the Buzzcocks established their own label, New Hormones. This act demonstrated that inexperienced musicians from a "musical backwater" like Manchester could successfully release their own records. "But with the scene being so new, there were no labels interested in releasing this kind of music, so they established their own label." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Formation and Early Influences** : The band was formed in Manchester in 1976 by Pete Shelley and Howard Devoto, who were both fans of The Velvet Underground and becoming interested in punk music. "The Buzzcocks were formed in Manchester in 1976 by two guys, Pete Shelley and Howard Devoto. Both were big velvet underground fans and had started paying more attention to this thing that people were calling punk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Formation and Early Promotion (1975)** : The Buzzcocks were formed sometime in 1975. Principals Howard Devoto and Pete Shelley promoted the Sex Pistols' first gig in Manchester at the Lesser Free Trade Hall, an event central to British punk rock culture and depicted in the movie "24 Hour Party People." "Anyway, that show was promoted by Howard Devoto and Pete Shelley, the two principals in The Buzzcocks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wYuNolSDLFM
**Influence on Manchester Music Scene (Early 1980s)** : The Buzzcocks were one of the groups, along with The Fall and Joy Division, that began to "shake things up" in Manchester during the original punk era, contributing significantly to the city's cultural transformation from an industrial hub to a vibrant music scene. "Groups like the bus cocks in the fall and joy division had begun to shake things after in the original punk era." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2RKchFW42Jc
**Inspired by Sex Pistols Gig (1976)** : Future members of the Buzzcocks were among the small audience of fewer than 50 people who attended the Sex Pistols' pivotal, though financially unsuccessful, gig at the Lesser Free Trade Hall in Manchester on June 4, 1976. This event served as a significant inspiration for them to pursue punk rock. "In the audience that night were future members of the buscocks and the pet shop boys and joy division and new order." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1y2UuU-me_s
**Manchester Origin** : The Buzzcocks are included in a list of numerous bands with roots in Manchester. "Buzcocso." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=q_Scyr2czh8
**Manchester Origins** : The Buzzcocks are listed among the numerous bands from Manchester, emphasizing the city's prolific musical output. "loads of loads of Aces, Buzcocs of Fall, James, Godly and Cream, Badly Drone Boy, Elbow, you know, John Mayl, Star Sailor-Verb, Phil Linnett, group of Manchester, Smith and you are a simply red, all his, you know, he goes on and on and this, this is bands and bands and bands, you know." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KF_adyXf86Q
**Peele Sessions Recordings (70s, 80s, 90s)** : The Buzzcocks (referred to as "the Bus Cox") were part of the hundreds of bands that recorded "Peele Sessions" live at the BBC throughout the 70s, 80s, and 90s. These sessions, curated by DJ John Peel, played a vital role in showcasing new music while navigating the BBC's "Needle Time" regulations. "The Smiths, R.E.M., New Order, the Fall, Equivalent of the Buntyman, Susie the Banshee, the Bus Cox, the list is almost endless." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ak6OuOUkRrQ
**Pete Shelley's Death in 2018** : Pete Shelley of the Buzzcocks was among the musicians lost in 2018. "Pete Shelley of the Buzzcocks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sdPeii-8Ysw
**Sex Pistols Manchester Show** : The Buzzcocks arranged for a still-unknown band, The Sex Pistols, to play the Lesser Free Trade Hall in Manchester on June 4th, 1976. This gig became mythical and legendary, inspiring about 40 attendees to form bands like Joy Division, Pet Shop Boys, Simply Red, and The Smiths, and to establish Factory Records. "They arranged for a still unknown band called The Sex Pistols to play the lesser free trade hall in Manchester on June 4th, 1976, a show that has gone down in myth and legend as one of the most important gigs in the history of rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Soulful Groove in Punk** : Even as a punk band from Manchester, the Buzzcocks were recognized for having a noticeable "groove" and elements of "soul music" in their sound, highlighting Manchester's pervasive connection to soul influences across genres. "Even Manchester punk bands, like listen to some buscox stuff they've got, you know, groove. They've got soul music, and soul music, but I mean, it's been really important in Manchester, and I don't know why, it has nothing in Liverpool." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dZStbEe4Gkk
**Unique Backstage Demand** : The Buzzcocks were the only band in Warped Tour history known to have made a specific backstage demand, requesting a bottle of champagne. "The buzz cocks, who were the only band to ever demand something backstage, they wanted a bottle of champagne." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
