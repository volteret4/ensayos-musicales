## albums
**Fat Domino Rock and Rollin (April 1956)** : Released only three months after his first album, this follow-up consisted mainly of outtakes and unreleased tracks dating back to 1953. Despite its content, it achieved remarkable commercial success, selling 20,000 copies in its first week upon release in April 1956, an extraordinary accomplishment for an album by a black artist aimed at a teenage audience at that time, especially one featuring only a single hit. **Rock and Rollin' with Fat Domino (Early 1956)** : This was Fats Domino's inaugural album released by Imperial Records, marking a significant milestone as it was his first album after six years of exclusively recording singles for the label. The release was notable because albums were rarely issued for R&B and blues artists in the 1950s, whose sales were typically directed at jukebox manufacturers. This album was composed almost entirely of his previously released material, primarily hit singles from the years preceding the rock and roll boom. "Dave Bartholomew heard the show, and realized that he could capitalize on the part of the film.
