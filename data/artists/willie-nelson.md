**Bloody Mary Morning (1974)** : This song was the third in Willie Nelson's set list for the Austin City Limits pilot in 1974 and was crucial for his desired creative control. Originally written by Nelson for his 1970 album "Both Sides Now" (then titled "Bloody Merry"), it was heard by Atlantic Records VP Jerry Wexler in Nashville in 1972, leading to Nelson's signing with Atlantic for their new country division. He rewrote it for "Phases and Stages," changing the spelling to "M-A-R-Y" and the narrative to a man migrating to Texas from Los Angeles to forget a former lover. It reached number 17 on the Billboard Country Chart in 1974 and became a staple of Nelson's live shows. It was produced by R&B legend Jerry Wexler and inspired by Nelson's desire to be a better parent. "Bloody Mary Morning, that is actually from the pilot episode of Austin City Limits that was written by Willie Nelson produced by R&B Legend, Jerry Wexler and forever after that a staple of Willie's live shows." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 49 - Austin City Limits and the 70s Country Music Revolution | http://ftr70.com

## curiosities
**American Legend Status** : Willie Nelson is regarded as a "genuine American legend" for his life and art. "a genuine American legend." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Introducing Broken Record - Norah Jones Begins Again | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f1264fe6d21276e0cbc
**Austin Refuge for Progressive Artists** : Willie Nelson, favoring long hair and denim over "sparkling nudie suits," found Austin, Texas, to be a refuge in the early to mid-1970s. This was due to Austin's liberal-minded college town atmosphere, which accepted his radical appearance and provided artistic freedom away from Nashville's polished country sound. "Nelson preferred to do that. Did that preferred the denim over the sparkling nudie suits that were so popular too?" ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 49 - Austin City Limits and the 70s Country Music Revolution | http://ftr70.com
**Collaboration with Daniel Lanois** : Daniel Lanois worked with Willie Nelson on projects following his breakthrough with U2. "He went on to work with Peter Gabriel and his so album and there was Bob Dylan and Willie Nelson and Luscious Jackson and of course lots more U2." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QkSKxm_U9Yc ← willie-nelson
**Commitment to Farm Aid** : As a co-organizer of Farm Aid, Willie Nelson, along with John Mellencamp and Neil Young, remained deeply committed to the cause of supporting American farmers. Despite successfully raising nine million dollars at the first concert, they were frustrated that the event needed to continue to exist, indicating the ongoing nature of the agricultural crisis and their dedication to addressing it. "Rain on the scarecrow made it number two on the Billboard Hot 100 by the way farm a raised nine million dollars which was not ever going to be enough money and melanchamp and Nelson and young would remain committed to the cause but they were very frustrated that farm a needed to continue to exist." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 55 - Heartland Rock is about More than the Heartland | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Creative Control at Atlantic Records (1972)** : Jerry Wexler, VP of Atlantic Records, offered Willie Nelson a "deal he could not refuse" after hearing "Bloody Mary Morning" at a Nashville party in 1972. This deal was significant not just for the money, but for the creative control it granted Nelson, allowing him to make critically acclaimed records like "Shotgun Willie" and "Phases and Stages." "He offers Nelson a deal he could not refuse, which was as much about creative control as it was about the money." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 49 - Austin City Limits and the 70s Country Music Revolution | http://ftr70.com
**Iconic Austin City Limits Debut (1974)** : Willie Nelson's performance on October 17, 1974, served as the official pilot for Austin City Limits. A concerted effort was made to ensure a large audience, which resulted in people being "crammed everywhere," with fans even sitting on the stage. He played a 20-song set, opening with "Whiskey River." "Scott Newton who was a photographer for Austin City Limits said, there would be no Austin City Limits if there was no Willie Nelson." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 49 - Austin City Limits and the 70s Country Music Revolution | http://ftr70.com
**Influenced by Hank Williams** : Willie Nelson (referred to as "William Nelson" in the transcript) is listed among the musicians who were inspired and followed the musical trajectory set by Hank Williams. "trazando un camino que seguirán músicos como Johnny Cash, Dolip Arton o William Nelson." ← El genio roto del country： La leyenda de Hank Williams | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qczx7qWg4xM
**Initiating Farm Aid** : A Texan deeply aware of the struggles of American farmers, Willie Nelson had been contemplating organizing a benefit for them for several years. Bob Dylan's controversial remark at Live Aid in July 1985 about helping farmers' mortgages finally spurred him into action. Shortly after Live Aid, while playing golf in Indiana, Nelson discussed the idea with an acquaintance of John Mellencamp, leading to the creation of Farm Aid. "Not long after Live Aid Nelson was in Indiana playing golf with the mutual acquaintance of Indiana native John melanchamp the topic of a benefit for farmers came up melanchamp's friends said well you know John just made a record about that very topic and the rest is as they say history." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 55 - Heartland Rock is about More than the Heartland | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Labeled as "Hippies"** : Willie Nelson, along with Waylon Jennings, faced criticism and skepticism for injecting rock elements into their country sound, with some questioning their authenticity by labeling them as "hippies." "Why were they trying to be hippies?" ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 35 - Cosmic American Music - 70s Country Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-35-cosmic-american-music-70s-country-rock ← willie-nelson
**Nashville House Fire (1969)** : Willie Nelson's house in Nashville burned down right before Christmas in 1969. This event, combined with his feeling constrained by the type of music country purists expected, led him to return to his home state of Texas. "Willie Nelson's Nashville house burned down right before Christmas in 1969." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 49 - Austin City Limits and the 70s Country Music Revolution | http://ftr70.com
**Singing and Guitar as One Expression** : Willie Nelson viewed his singing and guitar playing as a single, inseparable artistic expression. In a recording session, he became angry when an engineer turned his guitar down in the mix, unable to comprehend why anyone would separate his vocal from his instrument, expecting them to be at an equal level. "He just couldn't understand why anyone would separate his singing from his guitar. Why, they wouldn't be at an equal level." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Introducing Broken Record - Norah Jones Begins Again | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f1264fe6d21276e0cbc
