# artist - Andrew Loog Oldham

## curiosities
**Composer of Key Sample, No Remuneration** : Despite being the original composer of the "cinco famos anotas" (five famous notes) that formed the core instrumental sample in "Bitter Sweet Symphony," Andrew Loog Oldham ultimately did not receive any financial compensation for their use after the complex legal battles concluded. This situation left him feeling deeply frustrated regarding the outcome. **Loss of Rights to Instrumental Versions** : Andrew Loog Oldham had previously been sued by Allen Klein over the rights to the instrumental versions of The Rolling Stones' songs he had created in the late 1960s. As a result of this earlier legal dispute, Oldham lost all rights he held over these instrumental tracks, which subsequently fell under Allen Klein's ownership. "Alinclain ya en su día habían mandado a Alinclain por los derechos de estas versiones instrumentales que había hecho a finales de los 60 y por el cual había acabado perdiendo todos los derechos que tenía suelas canciones." ← Music Radar Clan > Bitter Sweet Symphony： Quien fue su verdadero compositor？ | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CsBQFChCsuM&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc ← andrew-loog-oldham
**Producer and First Manager of The Rolling Stones** : Andrew Loog Oldham held significant roles with The Rolling Stones, serving as their first professional manager and also as their producer. This professional relationship places him in a key position regarding the origins of the music later sampled by The Verve. "Alan Luke, aparte de grabar estas versiones sinfónicas, fue el primer banal de que tuvieron los Rolling Stones profesional. **Rolling Stones Manager** : Andrew Loog Oldham was the manager of The Rolling Stones during their early career, releasing "The Rolling Stones Songbook" in 1966 which contained the orchestral arrangement of "The Last Time" later sampled by The Verve. He was later succeeded by Allan Klein. "Andrew Lou Geoldem, the manager of the Rolling Stones back in the day." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fS8V3kYXT6w ← andrew-loog-oldham
**Symphonic Versions of Rolling Stones Songs** : Andrew Loog Oldham was responsible for creating symphonic versions of Rolling Stones songs in the late 1960s. One of these instrumental versions, specifically featuring five repetitive notes from "The Last Time," was later sampled by The Verve for their song "Bitter Sweet Symphony." Oldham initially granted permission for its use, but he subsequently filed a lawsuit, feeling he deserved more prominence than agreed upon as the song became hugely successful, although his initial demand did not succeed.
