## curiosities
**Cassette Club for Distribution (1982)** : From 1982 onwards, R. Stevie Moore founded his "R. Stevie Moore cassette club" as a primary method for distributing his music. Members of the club would receive a list of his available material, with each song meticulously annotated with a "listenability quotient" that scored precisely how low-fi the recording actually was, reflecting a deliberate embrace and categorization of the lo-fi aesthetic. **Early Home Recording Pioneer (1950s-1970s)** : Coming from a musical background, with his father Bob Moore playing on recordings for Elvis and Roy Orbison, R. Stevie Moore was inspired to make his own recordings at home. He utilized whatever gear he could assemble with his dad, including cheap cassette recorders or secondhand reel-to-reel machines, and began releasing records through his own label by the late 1950s. His work demonstrated an early embrace of home recording as a means of artistic expression. "R. Steve Moore was also in love with the cassette. The idea of being able to quickly record something on his reel to reel machine and then dub off copies onto cassettes for distribution was very appealing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jq-e-fZxXOs
