## songs
**Spinning Wheel (1969)** : This song was written by David Clayton Thomas two years before he joined Blood, Sweat & Tears, and was initially rejected in his native Canada for being "too jazzy" and hard to sell. It became a perfect fit for the band, noted for its "horns forward jazz" style, and was one of the biggest hits of 1969. The lyrics discuss letting go of troubles, not conforming, and allude to the 1960s psychedelic movement, without being overly intellectual or vacuous. **Band's Musical Identity** : Al Kooper originally created the band with a vision of it being a rock band, but other members perceived it as a jazz band. David Clayton Thomas stated that without his R&B and soul influences, the band would have been "just a jazz band," asserting his role in making it more of a rock band. **Controversial Cold War Tour (1970)** : In the summer of 1970, during the height of the Cold War, the band embarked on a tour of communist countries including Poland, Romania, and Yugoslavia, after the US State Department offered Canadian David Clayton Thomas permanent residency in the United States in exchange for their participation. The band was surprised by censorship and directives to play more jazz and less "rhythm" because it was deemed to be "inciting rioting." **David Clayton Thomas' Departure (1972)** : David Clayton Thomas left the band for three years in 1972, citing the overwhelming touring schedule as the reason. While he received songwriting royalties, most other band members relied solely on touring for their income, which perpetuated the continuous touring.
