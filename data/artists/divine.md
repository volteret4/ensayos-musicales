**Impact as a Grotesque Drag Queen and Cultural Icon** : Known as Divine, Harris Glenn Milstead was an important figure for 20th-century culture, described as the "drag queen par excellence." He challenged stereotypes with his enormous overweight physique and grotesque character, yet possessed incredible artistic capabilities. He collaborated extensively with John Waters on films like "Pink Flamingo" and "Harry's play," and achieved such respect that Disney recognized him as the direct inspiration for Ursula, the villain in "The Little Mermaid."
