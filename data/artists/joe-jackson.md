## curiosities
**"Angry Young Men" Tag** : Upon the release of *Look Sharp*, Jackson was immediately compared to Elvis Costello, as music magazines labeled both snide but melodic rockers as "angry young men." This term, initially coined for British playwrights and novelists, also soon included Graham Parker. He was perceived as possessing a "gimmelied, distinctly British take on rock." "Jackson was immediately compared to Costello, as magazines tagged both snide but melodic rockers as angry young men." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Angry Young Men Edition Part 1 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f780c30a1408dc2baf3
**"Least Likely Punk" Background** : Jackson was described as the "least likely punk of all" due to his profound love of jazz and classical music. He earned a scholarship to London's Royal Academy of Music and developed his skills as a multi-instrumentalist on the cabaret circuit rather than as a pub rocker. "Joe Jackson was, in a way, the least likely punk of all, a lover of jazz and classical music who'd earned a scholarship to London's Royal Academy of Music." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Angry Young Men Edition Part 1 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f780c30a1408dc2baf3
**Admiration for Punk and New Wave** : Despite his classical education and diverse musical background from playing in clubs, Jackson admired the punk and New Wave movements, specifically appreciating their energy, simplicity, and outspoken lyrical tone. He found no difficulty in incorporating these elements into his own music. "despite his classical education and background playing many types of music in clubs, Jackson admired the punk and New Wave movements, especially the energy and simplicity of the music and the outspoken tone of the lyrics." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Angry Young Men Edition Part 1 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f780c30a1408dc2baf3
**Musical Evolution in the 80s** : In the 1980s, Jackson would transition to become the "jazz bow's favorite rocker," indicating his move beyond the confines of punk, post punk, or power pop. **Name Change Inspired by TV** : He did not adopt the name Joe Jackson until the mid-1970s, choosing it because of his resemblance to the title character of a British TV puppet show called Joe 90.
