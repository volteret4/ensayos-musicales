## songs
**Drip City (1955)** : Le Caine created this track by taking the sound of a single water drip and processing it through his synthesizer. It serves as a pioneering example of keyboard-generated electronic music. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8

## curiosities
**Influence on Modern Electronic Music** : The foundational work Le Caine did in keyboard synthesis is cited as the reason artists like Trent Reznor and Nine Inch Nails are able to create electronic music today. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
**The Electronic Sackbut (1945-1948)** : Unveiled in 1948, this device is considered the world's first keyboard synthesizer, predating the work of Bob Moog by nearly twenty years. It featured force-sensitive keys played with one hand while the other adjusted dials to control voltage circuits and waveforms. Le Caine was a Canadian scientist from Port Arthur who worked on radar and atomic physics before focusing on electronically generated music. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
