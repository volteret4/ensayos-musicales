## albums
**Gerbrieth (1973) – Self-Titled Debut** : This self-titled debut album was recorded with contributions from Peter Frampton and John Paul Jones of Led Zeppelin. To promote it, Electric Records spent $200,000 on a nearly nude billboard of Gerbrieth in Times Square, as well as full-page ads in prominent publications such as The New York Times, Rolling Stone, Vogue, and Penthouse. Despite significant investment and its groundbreaking nature, the album's promotion was cut short due to the collapse of his career. ## curiosities
**$500,000 Deal with Electric Records** : His manager, Jerry Brandt, secured a deal with Electric Records for $500,000, which was an enormous sum of money at the time, underscoring the label's significant investment and belief in his potential. "That didn't stop a manager by the name of Jerry Brandt from striking a deal with the electric records for $500,000, a huge amount of money at the time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hswtuenFwvM
**AIDS Diagnosis and Death (1980s)** : By the early 1980s, Gerbrieth's "bathhouse habits" led him to contract AIDS. He tragically died on August 3, 1987, notably just one week after his restrictive 10-year contract with Jerry Brandt expired. "By the early 80s, his bathhouse habits had caught up with him and Gerbrieth contracted AIDS." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hswtuenFwvM
**Ambitious Stage Production for Paris Opera House** : An additional $200,000 was allocated for a lavish stage production intended to open at the Paris Opera House. This production included elaborate props, such as a 40-foot model of the Empire State Building, which was designed to represent a phallic symbol. "Another $200,000 was spent on a stage production that was supposed to open at the Paris Opera House." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hswtuenFwvM
**Background as Part-time Drug Addict and Rent Boy** : Before his major record deal, Gerbrieth had a background that included being a part-time drug addict and an occasional rent boy. This detail highlights the unconventional and challenging circumstances of his early life. "He was also a part-time drug addict and occasional rent boy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hswtuenFwvM
**Career Collapse and Contractual Issues** : His career ultimately collapsed; the Paris shows never happened, and after two albums, Gerbrieth disappeared. He struggled to make music due to an "iron clad 10-year contract" with Jerry Brandt, with whom he was no longer communicating. "The Paris shows never happened and after two albums, Gerbrieth disappeared." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hswtuenFwvM
**First Openly and Genuinely Gay Rock Star** : Gerbrieth (real name Bruce Campbell) is identified as the first rock singer to admit being 100% gay from the outset, making him the world's first openly and genuinely gay rock star. He was also the first gay singer to be signed to a major record label, Electric Records. "Gerbrieth, the world's first openly gay and genuinely gay rock star." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hswtuenFwvM
