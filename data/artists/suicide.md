**"Punk Masses" Gigs** : Inspired by Lester Bangs' December 1970 reference to Iggy Pop as a "stooge punk" in Cream Magazine, Alan Vega and his band Suicide began calling their gigs "punk masses," an early adoption of the term "punk" in a performance context. **Anti-Commercial Philosophy** : Suicide's philosophy was reactive against consumer music and large progressive rock concerts, which meant that achieving commercial success was not aligned with the No Wave movement's ethos. **CBGB Scene Band** : Suicide was one of the bands featured in the burgeoning CBGB scene, alongside Blondie, The Talking Heads, and The Heartbreakers, as the club became a central hub for new music. "CBGB was becoming the center of a new scene featuring bands with names like Blondie and Suicide and The Talking Heds and a television spin-off band called The Heartbreakers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4 ← suicide
**Early Performances at CBGB** : Suicide was one of the significant bands playing at CBGB by the summer of 1974, contributing to the vibrant scene that established the club as a pivotal location for new and emerging forms of music, including punk. "By the summer of 1974, Hilly's bar, now known just as CBGB to everyone, was hosting not just television, but the Ramones, Patty Smith, Suicide, Blondie, Talking Heads, the Heartbreakers, and a bunch of other groups." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uEe_HLUay5M ← suicide ← suicide
**Early Two-Piece Band (Early 1970s)** : Suicide was one of the earliest two-piece bands remembered, performing around New York City in the early 1970s. Their approach of having only two members perform all the music on stage was considered "really, really rare" at the time. "The first two piece band I remember encountering was Suicide." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GlcOoINI7EQ ← suicide ← suicide
