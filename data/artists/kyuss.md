# artist - Kyuss

## members
- Brett Bjork (drums - 1987 demo)
- Fredo Hernandez (drums - 1995 breakup)
- John Garcia (singer)
- Josh Homme (guitar)
- Nick Oliveri (bass - 1987 demo, later rejoined)
- Scott Reeder (bass - 1995 breakup)

## genres
- Desert Rock
- Dirt Rock
- Hardcore punk (early influence)
- Sludge rock
- Stoner Rock (term Josh Homme hated)

## labels
- Dali
- Elektra

## curiosities
**Anti-Radio Philosophy** : Kyuss intentionally avoided mainstream radio appeal, actively working to write songs that would "make radio cringe." They would even cut out parts that sounded too much like a catchy chorus, focusing instead on building a cult following. "In fact, they worked hard at writing songs that would make radio cringe, except interestingly, a station in Minneapolis who became a big supporter. If something sounded too much like a catchy chorus, they'd actually cut that from the song." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mG1TQuhcf1o
**Breakup in 1995** : Kyuss broke up in October 1995, with the lineup consisting of Josh Homme, Scott Reeder on bass, Fredo Hernandez on drums, and John Garcia on vocals. The breakup appeared amicable, largely due to boredom and a desire by members like Josh Homme (who was 21 at the time, having started when he was 17) to explore other musical avenues. "And then suddenly, out of nowhere, in October of 1995, caes broke up. At the time, the group consisted of Josh, Scott Reader on bass, drummer Fredo Hernandez, and singer John Garcia. The breakup seemed to be fairly friendly. Everybody just seemed to be bored. It was time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mG1TQuhcf1o
**Career Highlight** : A notable achievement for Kyuss was opening for Metallica during their tour in Australia. "Their career highlight was opening for Metallica in Australia." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mG1TQuhcf1o
**Distinctive Guitar Sound** : Kyuss developed a unique sound by tuning guitars down as low as possible, to the point where the strings were floppy. Josh Homme further enhanced this by running his guitar through both guitar and bass amps and cabinets, making the low end particularly prominent. "The guitars were tuned down as low as possible, right to the point where the strings were so floppy that they couldn't be played. The sounds that came out seemed to appeal to the stoners in the crowd, so they went with that." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mG1TQuhcf1o
**Lineup Instability and Label Issues** : The band experienced numerous lineup changes, including Nick Oliveri leaving and returning, and two different drummers and bass players over time. They also faced challenges when their record label went under and they were absorbed into Elektra, a major label part of the Warner Empire, which they disliked. "And the low light was probably all the lineup changes. Nick all of a very left and came back. The band went through two drummers and two bass players. And there were other problems. Their record label went under and they got absorbed into Elektra, part of the major label Warner Empire. That didn't sit well with them." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mG1TQuhcf1o
**Manager Firing** : Kyuss made the decision to fire their manager at some point in their career. "And oh, they also fired their manager." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mG1TQuhcf1o
**Musical Influence and Genre Pioneering** : Kyuss was highly influential, considered "ground zero" for a new form of fuzzy guitar music. They were credited with pioneering genres known as Desert Rock, Dirt Rock, and particularly Stoner Rock, a term Josh Homme personally disliked. "They were ground zero of a new form of fuzzy guitar music. Some called it Desert Rock. You can see why. And others used the term Dirt Rock. But the one that really stuck was Stoner Rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mG1TQuhcf1o
**Name Origin and Change** : Initially known as Sons of Kyuss, the name was derived from the Dungeons and Dragons game, where "Caes" (Kyuss) is an evil high priest with hideous offspring. The band shortened their name to just "Kyuss" in 1990 at the insistence of their record label, Dali. "Now that makes no sense unless you play Dungeons and Dragons. The game features an evil high priest called caes, and he had some hideous offspring, hence sons of caes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mG1TQuhcf1o
**Sales and Success Metrics** : For Kyuss, selling 40,000 copies of an album was considered a significant victory, reflecting their focus on a cult following rather than mass appeal. "And if an album sold 40,000 copies, that was fine. That was the big victory for them." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mG1TQuhcf1o
