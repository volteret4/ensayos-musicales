## curiosities
**Million-Selling Debut** : The Ye-Eyes' 2003 debut album was both a critical hit and a commercial success, selling around a million copies worldwide, highlighting the commercial potential of the Garage Rock revival. "And then in 2003, they released a debut album which was not only a critical hit, but also sold around a million copies worldwide." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UpWz3X33vSY
**New York Formation and Midwest Influences** : Singer Karen O. and drummer Brian Chase met at college in Ohio in the late 1990s. They later moved to New York, where they met photographer and guitarist Nick Zinner. Their initial band, Unitarred, evolved its sound to incorporate the "trashy, punky Garage Rock" Karen O. heard in Ohio, drawing influences from Detroit (140 miles up I-90) and Cleveland (35 miles east on Highway 10), near Akron (home of The Pretenders and The Black Keys). "Singer Karen O. met drummer Brian Chase at College in Ohio in the late 1990s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UpWz3X33vSY
**Toured with White Stripes and Strokes** : After renaming themselves from Unitarred to The Ye-Eyes, the band quickly began touring with other prominent Garage Rock revival acts, The White Stripes and The Strokes.
