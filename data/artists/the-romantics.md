## songs
**What I Like About You (1980)** : First released in January 1980, this song is considered an all-time classic of Powerpop, despite initially failing to make the Top 40 in the US. Its popularity soared a year later when its video gained significant airplay on the newly emerging MTV, leading to vast licensing arrangements with various entities. ## curiosities
**Delayed Commercial Success and Licensing** : "What I Like About You" was an initial commercial disappointment, not breaking the Top 40 upon its January 1980 release. Its massive success came a year later, driven by heavy rotation of its video on MTV. This led to the song being extensively licensed for various uses, from the Los Angeles Dodgers to Barbie dolls, SeaWorld, and numerous movies and TV shows, generating substantial revenue for the band. "It wasn't until a video started getting played on this new thing called MTV a year later that it began to have any real impact." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hstJ8M2laho
**Image and Genre Perception** : The Romantics, a Detroit-based band, were known for dressing in red leather. Initially, they perceived themselves as more aligned with punk rock or new wave. However, their music ultimately leaned heavily into the Powerpop sound, demonstrating the genre's pervasive influence at the turn of the decade. "This month after Nick Lowe and the NAC were having hits with their brand of Powerpop came these four guys from Detroit, all dressed in red leather." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hstJ8M2laho
