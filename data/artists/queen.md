**Jazz (1978) - Bicycle Race Nudism Controversy** : The iconography surrounding the first single "Bicycle Race" was highly controversial. Freddie Mercury, inspired by a photograph of nude women in a race, wanted to base the single and album's visual theme on this. Due to censorship, the single cover could not show full nudity, so a small cropped image of a nude woman on a bicycle had panties drawn onto her. The original "Jazz" album versions included a small poster of the nude women's race, which was censored in countries like the United States, forcing Queen to remove it. To circumvent this, a coupon was included, allowing fans to mail away for the poster. Queen subsequently created extensive iconography around the nude cyclists, including posters, concert visuals, and merchandise. This visual theme was significant in the 1970s, an era when nudism was a form of protest against the connection between nudity and sexuality.
