## songs
**Africa (1982)** : Released as a single from their fourth album in 1982, "Africa" became a huge hit despite the band not fully believing in it, thinking it was "okay" but not destined for the success it achieved. Composed of some of the best session players in the world, Toto was considered a "corporate rock band"—a project intentionally designed for mainstream appeal and high sales, which made them "highly uncool" to some rock audiences, especially the alt-rock crowd. Musically, however, the song is described as a "pretty fine piece of music" with solid performance, arrangement, and production. In 2018, it experienced a resurgence in popularity, becoming a meme and being featured in the Netflix show "Stranger Things." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw
**Rosanna (1982)** : This was a major single from Toto's fourth album, released in 1982. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw

## curiosities
**Band's Opinion on "Africa" (1982)** : Despite "Africa" becoming a huge hit, the band Toto "didn't really believe in the song all that much," considering it merely "okay" and not expecting it to achieve the success it did. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw
**Guitar Work on Michael Jackson's Beat It (1982)** : Steve Lukather, Toto's guitarist, played all the guitar parts on Michael Jackson's "Beat It," except for the solo, which was performed by Eddie Van Halen. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw
**Session Player Lineup** : Toto was renowned for its lineup of "some of the best session players in the world." For example, guitarist Steve Lukather played on Michael Jackson's "Beat It," and Jeff Porcaro was the drummer for the band. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw
