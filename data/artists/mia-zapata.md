**DNA Match to Jesus Mesquia** : In 2003, a breakthrough occurred in Mia Zapata's cold case when a DNA sample from a bite mark on her breast, preserved in cold storage by the Seattle PD, matched Jesus Mesquia. Mesquia, a Cuban immigrant and fisherman with a history of violence against women, had been in Seattle at the time of the murder. "The saliva from that bite mark belonged to Jesus Mesquia." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q6dAX6HCjgw
**DNA Technology Advances and Match** : By 2002, advancements in DNA extraction and the establishment of an international database allowed for comparing cold case samples. A routine check matched the preserved saliva sample from Mia Zapata to Jesus Mezquia, who had given a DNA sample after a domestic abuse charge in Florida. "The problem was that in 1993 the technology for extracting the necessary information from DNA wasn't available yet. By this time all kinds of important information could be extracted from any kind of body fluid or residue. There was also an international database that allowed law enforcement to follow up on cold cases by comparing DNA samples.
