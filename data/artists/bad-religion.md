## albums
**Bad Religion (1981)** : This self-titled EP was the first release from the Epitaph label in 1981, a company established by the band themselves because no other label was interested in releasing their music. Initially, Epitaph served as a vehicle solely for Bad Religion's records. "The first release from the label was their self-titled EP in 1981." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Stranger Than Fiction (1994)** : This album was released on September 6, 1994. The band had been persuaded to sign a major record deal, transitioning from their independent label, Epitaph Records. Stranger Than Fiction quickly achieved commercial success, selling more than half a million copies. "And on September 6, 1994, they released an album entitled Stranger Than Fiction, and it quickly sold more than half a million copies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Suffer (1988)** : Released in 1988, this album is identified as a potential "inflection point" for skate punk, marking a shift towards more melodic music with actual singable melodies, while retaining speed and intensity. Following this record, most bands in the genre adopted a direction with more "pop sensibilities." "The inflection point might have been Bad Religions 1988 album Suffer." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ

## songs
**21st Century Digital Boy** : This song is presented as a prime example of Bad Religion's musical style, characterized by its blend of punk rock energy with accessible pop songwriting sensibilities. "Bad Religion with 21st Century Digital Boy. Again, punk rock, but with pop songwriting sensibilities." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Bad Religion (1981)** : This track was Side One, Track One from Bad Religion's self-titled EP, which marked the inaugural release on their own Epitaph label in February 1981. It was the first song anyone heard from the band when their EP debuted. "Bad Religion with a song called, Bad Religion. The first thing anyone ever heard when they released their first self-titled EP on their own Epitaph label in February of 1981." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**We're Only Gonna Die (1982)** : This 1982 song is a hardcore track that starts and finishes with typical intensity, but uniquely features a breakdown in the middle where pop elements are "sandwiched in," illustrating the band's blend of genres. "Bad Religion, with we're only gonna die, a hardcore song with a little pop sandwiched in the middle." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tZV1lz-obQE

## curiosities
**Accused of Blasphemy and Sacrilege** : When Bad Religion left their indie imprint, Epitaph, for a major record label, punk purists condemned the decision as "blasphemy and sacrilege." This reaction highlighted the ideological clash between punk's DIY ethos and mainstream commercialism. "And bad religion, with a major record label leaving their own indie imprint, epitaph, behind, blasphemy and sacrilege." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Early Booking by Kevin Lyman** : Kevin Lyman, the future founder of the Warped Tour, booked Bad Religion for performances at California State Polytechnic University in Pomona when they were just starting out in the early 1980s. "What he would basically do is hire bands like Bad Religion and the Bangles when those groups were just starting out in the book at the university." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Founding Epitaph Label** : Bad Religion, like Black Flag, faced the challenge of no one wanting to release their music. This compelled them to establish their own label, Epitaph, which initially served as a platform solely for their own records but later grew significantly beyond hardcore punk. "Another band that found themselves in the same boat as Black Flag was Bad Religion. No one wanted to release their stuff either, so they had to establish their own label, and they called theirs Epitaph." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Hardcore with Pop Sensibilities** : Bad Religion possessed impeccable hardcore credentials, known for their strong political, socially aware, and rage-fueled lyrical content. However, they distinguished themselves by integrating a "smooth edge" through melodic vocals and harmonies, effectively applying "pop sensibilities" within their hardcore sound. "impeccable hardcore credentials, lots of politics, social awareness, and rage. But at the same time, they gave everything a smooth edge with melodic vocals and even harmonies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tZV1lz-obQE
**Tony Hawk Video Game Feature** : Music from Bad Religion was featured in Tony Hawk's popular skateboarding video games. "Tony Hawk's video games sold by the millions, and each one featured appropriate music from groups like The Vandals and Laguewagon, Suicidal tendencies, Millen-Call and Bad Religion, and so many more." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Transition to Major Label** : Bad Religion, initially working on their independent label Epitaph Records, was eventually convinced to sign a major record deal. This move sparked considerable controversy among punk purists. "They had been working on their own independent epitaph records, and they were convinced to sign a major record deal." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Warped Tour Feature** : Bad Religion was among the bands that performed during the early years of the Warped Tour, a significant platform for pop punk and ska punk acts. "In just the first few years, the tour featured bad religion." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
