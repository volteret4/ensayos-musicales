## albums
**Bad Religion (1981) – Self-titled EP** : The first release from their self-established label Epitaph, issued in February 1981.
**Stranger Than Fiction (1994)** : Released September 6, 1994, after signing a major record deal; quickly sold more than half a million copies; exemplifies punk rock with pop songwriting sensibilities.

## songs
**21st Century (Digital Boy)** : Featured on the album Stranger Than Fiction; noted for its blend of punk rock with pop songwriting sensibilities.
**Bad Religion (1981)** : The Side One, Track One from their self-titled EP, which was the inaugural song heard when the EP was released in February 1981 on their Epitaph label.
**We're Only Gonna Die (1982)** : A hardcore song from 1982 that starts and finishes hardcore but includes a "pop sandwiched" breakdown in the center.

## curiosities
**"Selling Out" Accusations** : Sparked "blasphemy" and "sacrilege" accusations from purists for leaving their indie imprint, Epitaph, for a major label.
**Epitaph's Initial Focus** : Epitaph was initially solely a vehicle for Bad Religion's records.
**Featured on Early Warped Tour** : Was one of the dozens of acts featured in the first few years of the Vans Warped Tour.
**Independent Career on Epitaph Records** : Had been working on their own independent Epitaph Records before being convinced to sign a major record deal.
**Label Formation** : Established their own label, Epitaph, because no existing record labels were willing to release their music.
**Melodic Hardcore Approach** : Maintained impeccable hardcore credentials, lots of politics, social awareness, and rage, but simultaneously incorporated melodic vocals and harmonies for a smooth edge.
