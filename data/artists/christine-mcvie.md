## curiosities
**Publishing Rights Sale to Hypnosis Song Fund** : Christine McVie, from Fleetwood Mac, sold her publishing rights to the Hypnosis Song Fund. "Hypnosis is bought publishing from... Christine McVeef, Lee Wood Mac" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pe40SqDrpZ0
**Acceptance of Another Woman in the Band** : When Stevie Nicks joined Fleetwood Mac, she was concerned about how Christine McVie would react to having another woman in the band, but was grateful to find out that Christine was "okay with that." "She also wondered if Christine McVee would be okay with having another woman in the band and was grateful to find out that she was okay with that." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 7 - Women Who Rocked the 70s | http://www.ftr70.com
**Divorce from John McVie (Mid-Late 1970s)** : She was "on their way to divorce" from her husband and bandmate, John McVie, during the tumultuous period of internal relationship drama that provided much of the lyrical inspiration for Fleetwood Mac's *Rumours* album. "the same time the McVees were on their way to divorce." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 51 - The Softer Side of 70s Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-51-the-softer-side-of-70s-rock
**Pledge with Stevie Nicks** : On the day Stevie Nicks joined Fleetwood Mac, Christine McVie and Nicks made a mutual vow never to be treated as "less than" or "second class citizens" within the male-dominated rock environment. They promised to fight for their place, ensure their songs and music were equally as good as the men's, and be included in creative discussions. "She said that they said to each other, we will never be treated like second class citizens. We will never be not allowed to hang out in a room full of intelligent, crazy rock and roll stars, because we are just as crazy and just as intelligent as they are." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 7 - Women Who Rocked the 70s | http://www.ftr70.com

**Over My Head (1975)** : Christine McVie wrote and sang the lead vocal on this song, which was the very first single released by the revamped Fleetwood Mac from their 1975 album (known as "The White Album"). It became their first Top 20 hit in the United States and is about a relationship in trouble. "Christine McBeat wrote and sang the lead vocal on the very first single that the revamped Fleetwood Mac released from the 1975 album that's come to be called the White Album.
