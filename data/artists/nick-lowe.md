## curiosities
**British Pub Rock Roots** : Nick Lowe emerged from the British pub rock scene in the early 1970s, which served as a foundational stage for his distinctive Powerpop sound. His background in this scene informed his songwriting, leading to both his own hit singles and tracks covered by other prominent artists. "One of the British Godfathers of this sound was Nick Lowe, a guy who came out of the British pubroxene of the early 70s..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hstJ8M2laho ← nick-lowe ← nick-lowe
**Critical Acclaim vs. Commercial Success** : In a 1986 interview with Karen Schlossberg for Cream magazine, Nick Lowe reflected on the emotional toll of his career, stating that he had released three albums he considered "really good" with "great critical reviews by and large," but they "disappeared without a trace." This pattern led him to "wonder about your own sanity." He expressed a dislike for the "sound which all records have got to have nowadays in order to get on the radio," preferring spontaneity and "a little element of the human being at work" over perfect, error-free recordings. "I listen to the radio nowadays and I keep on dying to hear a record with a mistake on it. They're also bloody perfect and they really get on my nerves." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 60 - One-Hit Wonders of the 70s | https://www.ftr70.com
