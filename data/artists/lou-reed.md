## albums
**Metal Machine Music (1975)** : Released in the summer of 1975, this double album was created by Lou Reed as a deliberate act of defiance against RCA, from whom he wanted to exit his contract. Contractually obligated to deliver more albums, Reed produced "Metal Machine Music," a work consisting of over four sides of continuous noise and feedback. Reed claimed to be channeling avant-garde composers like La Monte Young and Iannis Xenakis, and even Beethoven, though the album is primarily recognized for its weaponized, sustained distortion and locked groove on side four, designed to play infinitely. RCA released it as an "in-your-face" move back at Lou, and its sounds even inspired the Breene language in "Star Trek: The Next Generation." "Lou recorded a double album. Now to hear Lou explain what he did, he was merely channeling the experimental spirit of an avant-garde composer from the 1960s named Le Mans Young." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w
**Metal Machine Music (1975) – 64 Minutes of Guitar Feedback as Contract Retaliation; Later Reissued and Covered** : Lou Reed was contractually obligated to deliver one more album to RCA and wanted out. He set up guitars with unusual tunings in front of amplifiers in his New York City apartment, let them feed back on themselves, and recorded everything on a small 4-track. The resulting double album ran 64 minutes; side four of the vinyl ended in a locked groove that played forever. RCA released it as a counterattack hoping to end his career — it worked for nearly a decade. However, the record was later re-evaluated as an important forerunner of industrial, ambient, drone, and doom music. It has been reissued on CD (2000, Buddha Records) and has sold more than 100,000 copies total. A 10-person German ensemble called Zeichkratzer began performing it live around 2000 and released their own recording of it in 2014, arranged for strings, wind instruments, piano, and accordion. Sonic Youth used samples from it in their own work. "Since its release in 1975, Metal Machine Music has sold more than 100,000 copies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Dt7mb4yyuWM
**Metal Machine Music (1975) – Double Album of Pure Guitar Feedback Delivered as Contract FU** : Unhappy with RCA Records and contractually obligated to deliver one more album, Lou Reed recorded a double album of nothing but guitar feedback in his New York apartment: two guitars given weird tunings, placed in front of amplifiers and left to feed back on themselves for 64 minutes, recorded on a small 4-track machine. Side four of the vinyl featured a locked groove that played infinitely. RCA retaliated by actually releasing it, hoping to destroy his career — and it did, sending his career into a decade-long decline. The record was later reissued on CD in 2000, re-evaluated as an important milestone in industrial, ambient, drone, and doom music. A German ensemble called Zeichkratzer performed and recorded it live around the turn of the millennium, arranged for strings, wind instruments, piano, and accordion. "What began as a really dumb career move turned into revered art." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oljjY7jO-kM

**Electroshock Therapy in Youth (Late 1950s)** : As a freshman at New York University, Lou Reed's parents were deeply alarmed by what they perceived as his antisocial tendencies. This traumatic experience caused Reed to resent his parents for the rest of his life.
