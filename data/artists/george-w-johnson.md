# artist - George W. Johnson

## venues
- Vaudeville

## instruments
- Thomas Edison's wax cylinders

## songs
**The Laughing Song (1890)** : Originally recorded in 1890, this became Johnson's most popular track and was the best-selling song in the world by 1894. It reached number one on the brand-new music charts of the day and is said to have sold up to 50,000 copies. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f2KA97T4u08

## curiosities
**Cylinder Recording Labor** : Because early wax cylinders were master recordings, Johnson had to perform his songs individually for each unit sold. To meet sales of 50,000 copies, he sang "The Laughing Song" between 12,000 and 15,000 times, often repeating the song up to 50 times a day into five recorders at once. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f2KA97T4u08
**First Recording Star** : Recognized as the world's first and biggest pop star in the late 1890s, Johnson was a former slave who was freed around 1853 and began singing on the streets of New York in the 1870s. He became the first person to have a hit record and the first Black performer to appear on music charts. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
**Marketing and Legacy** : His label marketed him specifically as a Black man during a period of intense segregation and active memory of slavery, which was highly unusual for the time. Despite his early stardom, his popularity eventually faded; he died in 1914 while working as an office doorman and was buried in an unmarked grave in Queens. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
**Mastering the Wax Cylinder** : Because early Thomas Edison phonographs required individual master recordings for each unit sold, Johnson often repeated his songs up to 50 times a day. His powerful voice allowed up to five recorders to capture his performance simultaneously. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
**The World's First Pop Star** : A former slave freed around 1853, Johnson became the first person to have a hit record and the first Black performer to make the music charts. He was recognized as the world's biggest pop star in the late 1890s after starting his career singing on the streets of New York in the 1870s. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f2KA97T4u08
