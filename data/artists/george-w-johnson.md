## songs
**The Laughing Song (1890)** : This was George W. Johnson's most popular track, recorded in 1890 using Thomas Edison's wax cylinders. By 1894, it became the best-selling song ever, reaching number one on the nascent music charts and making Johnson the first Black performer to top those charts. "Johnson's most popular track was The Laughing Song, which was recorded in 1890." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ ← george-w-johnson
**The Laughing Song (1897 Rerecording)** : A later recording of "The Laughing Song" was made in 1897. Due to the inability to reproduce wax cylinders, Johnson had to record each cylinder individually, sometimes singing the song 50 times a day, leading to estimates of 12,000-15,000 performances to meet orders for 50,000 copies. "All I can tell you is that it was a later recording from 1897." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ ← george-w-johnson

## curiosities
**Career Decline and Death** : After achieving immense popularity as the world's first pop star, George W. Johnson's career faded. He eventually worked as an office doorman and died in 1914 at the age of 67. He was buried in an unmarked grave in Queens, New York. "But like so many careers, Johnson's popularity faded and he ended up working as an office door man." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ ← george-w-johnson
**Early Marketing of a Black Artist** : Johnson's record label explicitly marketed him as a Black man, which was an unusual and bold move during a time when slavery was a recent memory for many and segregation was still prevalent. "What was interesting is that his record label marketed him as a black man, something that just wasn't done when slavery was still fresh in the minds of many and segregation was strong." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ ← george-w-johnson
**First Black Performer to Reach Number One** : Johnson achieved a historic milestone when his song "The Laughing Song" reached number one on the brand new music charts of the day by 1894, making him the first Black performer to top those charts. His recordings also led to him becoming a major Vaudeville star. "In fact, he was the first black performer to bake those charts." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ ← george-w-johnson
**Unique Recording Process** : Due to the technical limitations of Thomas Edison's wax cylinders, which were impossible to reproduce, every cylinder was a master recording. Johnson, with his strong voice, could be recorded by up to five machines simultaneously, and he would often repeat a song 50 times a day to produce enough copies. "So in other words, every single cylinder had to be recorded individually." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ ← george-w-johnson
**World's First Recording Star** : George W. Johnson, a former slave freed around 1853, became the world's first recording star and biggest pop star in the late 1890s. He sang on the streets of New York City before being recruited in 1890 by Charles Marshall to make recordings for coin-operated phonographs. "George W. Johnson was the world's first and biggest pop star in the late 1890s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ ← george-w-johnson
