## songs
**Get Up and Boogie (1976)** : This disco song by Silver Convention from 1976 was one of the samples incorporated into Kon Kan's "I Beg Your Pardon." "Get up and boogie, a 1976 disco song by Silver Convention." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_qxdftwrX3E
**I Beg Your Pardon (1989)** : Conceived while Barry Harris was DJing in Portugal and recorded in Los Angeles, this song became an alt-rock hit in Canada and a Top 40 hit across Canada, the US, Belgium, Ireland, the Netherlands, and the UK. It features extensive sampling from various sources, including "I Never Promised You a Rose Garden" (1971, by Loretta Lynn – likely Lynn Anderson), "Get Up and Boogie" (1976 by Silver Convention), a Marble Royal Singer-Retts TV commercial, and "Go" by Tones on Tail. It has garnered 24.4 million plays and views on Spotify and YouTube, with a one-hit wonder score of 2.76 out of 10. "The song was conceived while Barry was DJing in Portugal. It was recorded in Los Angeles and released on an album called Move to Move in 1989." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_qxdftwrX3E

## curiosities
**Canadian Identity and Name Origin** : Kon Kan, a synth-pop group from Toronto founded by Barry Harris, intentionally chose its name as a play on "CanCon" (Canadian content radio regulations) to proudly signify their Canadian origins on an international stage. "The name Concan is a play on CanCon, the radio regulations in Canada that require radio stations to play a specific amount of Canadian content. Barry was proud to be Canadian, so he wanted everyone to know that internationally." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_qxdftwrX3E
**Extensive Sampling** : The song "I Beg Your Pardon" is notable for its liberal use of samples from diverse musical and commercial sources, which contributes to its unique sound. "There are a lot of samples here, including I never promised you a Rose Garden, a 1971 Hit Roller-Retta-Lin. Get up and boogie, a 1976 disco song by Silver Convention, a Marble Royal Singer-Retts TV commercial and a bunch of others, including by the way, Go from Tone's on Tale, which we heard earlier." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_qxdftwrX3E
**Groups with Technopop DNA** : Kon Kan was identified as a group whose sound incorporated substantial Technopop DNA, illustrating how the genre's influence persisted and diversified even as its initial popularity waned. "And there were other groups with plenty of Technopop DNA, camouflage, went in Rome, the information society, Khan Khan." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
