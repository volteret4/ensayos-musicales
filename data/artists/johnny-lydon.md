## curiosities
**Aggressive Dance Punk with Public Image Limited** : After his time with the Sex Pistols, Johnny Lydon led Public Image Limited (PIL) in exploring a more aggressive form of dance punk. While still danceable, PIL's music had a distinct edge, described as "scary," "agitated," and "twitchy." "There was also a more aggressive version of this approach, led by Johnny Lydon's post-sex pistols band Public Image Limited." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4
**Auditioning Michael Balzary (Flea) for PiL** : In 1984, Johnny Lydon auditioned a young bass player from Los Angeles named Michael Balzary (later known as Flea) for Public Image Limited. Flea got the gig but chose instead to stick with his new band, the Red Hot Chili Peppers, which he was forming with his friend Anthony. "One of the people, Johnny auditioned for the band in 1984, was a young bass player from Los Angeles named Michael Ballzary." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJGFUlQTUhQ
**Caregiver for Wife with Alzheimer's** : Johnny Lydon is a dedicated caregiver for his wife, Nora, whom he affectionately calls "babby," as she battles Alzheimer's disease. His personal experience with childhood memory loss from meningitis allows him to deeply empathize with her condition and the challenges she faces, providing her with round-the-clock care. "He will also tell you about his wife Nora who he's caring for as she deals with Alzheimer's." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u5NLt6mkmrk
**Childhood Meningitis** : As a child, Johnny Lydon contracted a severe case of meningitis that left him in a coma for nearly a year. The lasting effects of this illness are credited with causing his distinctive stare. "Johnny Liden, who came down with a terrible case of meningitis as a kid, which left him comatose for nearly a year. The after effects give him that distinctive stare." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NQgn0_mUh74
**Dark Music Contribution** : Johnny Lydon's work with Public Image Limited was noted for its dark sound, contributing to the development of the Goth scene. "Johnny Lydon's public image limited was pretty dark, same with Killing Joke." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gd9sO7c3N1s
**Missed Pan Am Flight 103 (1988)** : Johnny Lydon and his wife, Nora, narrowly missed Pan Am Flight 103 on December 21, 1988, which was later bombed over Lockerbie, Scotland. Delays caused by Nora taking too long to pack, a subsequent argument, and getting stuck in traffic on the way to Heathrow Airport meant they missed the flight by minutes, thus saving their lives. "Nora, his wife, took too long to pack. They had a big fight, which delayed them even more, and then they got stuck in traffic on the way to Heathrow. They missed their flight by minutes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3eGBnwPWRGo
**Post-Sex Pistols Musical Freedom** : After leaving the Sex Pistols, Johnny Lydon gained the freedom to pursue his personal musical interests, which included a strong admiration for the German avant-garde group Can. This freedom allowed him to indulge in new musical directions with Public Image Limited. "And once he was out of the sex pistols, he was free to indulge in his musical whims." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE
**Potential Eyesight Deterioration** : In addition to the permanent curvature of his spine and his distinctive stare, Johnny Lydon lives with the possibility that his eyesight will progressively deteriorate into nothingness due to the long-term effects of his childhood spinal meningitis. "He was also left with a permanent curvature of the spine and that stare and the possibility that his eyesight will deteriorate into nothingness." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u5NLt6mkmrk
**Profound Memory Loss and Identity Crisis** : After being discharged from the hospital following his meningitis battle, Johnny Lydon suffered from severe memory loss that persisted for about four years, leaving him unable to recall his own name, his parents' names, or virtually anything from his past. He later reflected that this ordeal forced him to completely rebuild his identity, calling the meningitis "God's greatest gift" because it compelled him to discover who he truly was. "He couldn't remember his name or the name of his mom and dad." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u5NLt6mkmrk
**Spinal Meningitis and Lasting Effects** : Johnny Lydon contracted a severe case of spinal meningitis at the age of seven, an illness that left him with a permanent, intimidating stare and a lasting curvature of the spine. He spent an entire year at St. Anne's Hospital in London, enduring nausea, headaches, intermittent comas, hallucinations, and painful spinal taps, missing over a year of school. "It's the result of a terrible case of spinal men and gytus that he contracted when he was seven." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u5NLt6mkmrk
