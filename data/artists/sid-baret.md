## albums
**Solo Albums (Date Unspecified)** : Sid Baret released two solo albums, described as "a bit strange," particularly due to their production. The recording process involved Sid Baret playing only fragments, never a complete song, and he couldn't wait for other artists or create song introductions or endings. This necessitated a complex production process of overdubbing, cutting, and pasting different pieces, and sometimes having others create intros. The albums are seen more as a showcase of his improvisational talent than his compositional skills, as they were largely the result of improvisation. **Financial Struggles and Pin Floyd's Support (1978)** : By 1978, Sid Baret, despite having earned substantial money from Pin Floyd and ongoing royalties from their first two albums, ran out of money while living in London. He moved back to Cambridge to live with his family. This financial support allowed him and his family to live comfortably without money worries for the rest of his life. **The Man, The Myth, The Genius** : Sid Baret is described as a "man myth" and the "great genius" behind the early success of Pin Floyd, yet he suffered from severe mental health issues.
