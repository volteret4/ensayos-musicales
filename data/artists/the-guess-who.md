**Shake It All Over (1965)** : This very solid garage rock single was originally released in 1965 by Chad Allen and the Expressions from Winnipeg. Initially, radio stations ignored it because it was Canadian. Quality Records cleverly sent out a 7-inch single with "Guess Who?" on the label instead of the band's name, fooling DJs into believing it was a new British invasion band. The song, a cover of Johnny Kid in the Pirates, hit number one in Canada, number 22 in the US, and number 27 in the UK, leading the band to officially change their name to The Guess Who. "The DJs did not get the joke. They told their listeners that they were listening to shake it all over by this cool new band called the Guess Who?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6PGgNCaW4xo

**"Heavy Metal Robots" Description (1970)** : In the February 7, 1970, edition of Cream Magazine, music critic Lester Bangs described The Guess Who as "heavy metal robots" following their hit single "Undun." While this marked an early reference to music using the term "heavy metal," it was intended as an insult rather than a genre classification. "They're quite refreshing in the wake of all the heavy metal robots of the year past." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8GU8ZpSgY8
**Evolution from Chad Allen and the Expressions** : The Guess Who is identified as the band that Chad Allen and the Expressions later became, showcasing a lineage from the original Canadian Garage Rock scene. **Increased FM Airplay** : Due to new FM radio rules in Canada that mandated playing more album tracks and Canadian content, The Guess Who's music, alongside others like Bachman-Turner Overdrive and Neil Young, saw significantly more airplay. This was in part because there weren't enough new Canadian bands of a certain caliber yet. "a lot more guest who, back then turn or overdrive the band and Neil Young got played." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6PGgNCaW4xo
**Name Change Due to Stunt** : The band, originally known as Chad Allen and the Expressions, was forced to change their name to The Guess Who after a publicity stunt by Quality Records. The label sent out a single with "Guess Who?" on it, leading DJs to mistakenly promote them as a hot new British invasion band when their cover of "Shake It All Over" became a hit. "the group had no choice but to change their name from Chad Allen and the expressions to the Guess Who." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6PGgNCaW4xo
