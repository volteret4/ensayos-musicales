## curiosities
**Futurist Philosophy** : OMD, along with other similar artists, aimed to look toward a "brave new future," with their music sometimes labeled "futurist," in contrast to a perceived undesirable present. "But for OMD, and others like them, the present sucked. Let's look to a brave new future. In fact, some people call music like this futurist." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
**Live Performance Setup** : During live performances, McCluskey and Humphreys surrounded themselves with synthesizers, signal generators, oscilloscopes, and a reel-to-reel tape recorder named Winston, creating an unusual sight even for the open-minded punk era. "And when they played live McCluskey and Humphrey's surround of themselves with not only synthesizers, but signal generators and oscilloscopes and a real-to-real tape recorder named Winston." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
**Origin and Name Changes** : The band, formed in 1977 by Andy McCluskey and Paul Humphreys, initially went by the name "The Id." After becoming "firmly hooked on the joys of electronics," they renamed themselves "VCLXI," after a transistor part, before adopting their "deliberately pretentious name" in the fall of 1978. "To begin as the id, and were formed by Andy McCluskey and Paul Humphrey's 1977. They started playing in and around Liverpool, especially at a hot club called Erics." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
**Upbeat Electronic Pop** : Their first single, released in May 1979, showcased a unique style of electronic pop music that, unlike Kraftwerk's sound, was upbeat, happy, and described as "holds full." "Not only was their music completely electronic, but unlike craftwork, OMD's band of pop music was upbeat, happy, and there we say it. Holds full." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
