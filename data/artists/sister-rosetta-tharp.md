## songs
**Down by the Riverside (1944)** : Recorded in 1944, this song exemplifies Sister Rosetta Tharpe's musical style, notably featuring a significant "swing" in her performance. It is historically recognized as a proto-rock and roll track, demonstrating her early contributions to the genre's development. "This song is considered to be proto-rock and roll again from 1944." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjQiZqpmQlE

## curiosities
**Accolades and Honors** : Sister Rosetta Tharpe is a member of both the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame and the Blues Hall of Fame, and was honored with her own US postage stamp, recognizing her immense contributions to music. "Sister Rosetta Tharp from 1944 and a member of the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, the Blues Hall of Fame and a woman who had her own US-posted stamp." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjQiZqpmQlE
**Godmother of Rock and Roll** : She is considered by some to be the "godmother of rock and roll" due to her significant contributions and influence across multiple genres. "if somebody deserves to be called the godmother of rock and roll, it's her, Sister Rosetta Tharp." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oLC49nCf0ZM
**Pioneering Black Queer Woman** : Sister Rosetta Tharp was a queer black woman active in the 1940s, whose music spanned and influenced blues, jazz, gospel, and early rock genres. "She was a queer black woman in the 1940s who burned through blues, jazz, gospel, and early rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oLC49nCf0ZM
**Pioneering Electric Guitar Distortion** : She was one of the very first musicians to use distortion on the electric guitar, a technique that significantly impacted later guitarists like Eric Clapton and Keith Richards. "She was one of the very first people to use distortion on the electric guitar." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjQiZqpmQlE
