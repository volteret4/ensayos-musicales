## songs
**Mad World (1982)** : This single was released in Fall 1982 (September) and became an "across the board hit," popular in new wave, alternative rock, and pop genres. Roland Orzabal wrote the song, with Curt Smith, the bass player, performing the vocals. "A single called Mad World was released the previous fall. This would be September 1982. And it became an across the board hit." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw
**Pale Shelter (1982)** : This 1982 song from Tears for Fears is underpinned by the sound of the Roland CR-78 programmable drum machine. "It underpins this song from Tears for Fears in 1982." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dablAKDOOV0

## curiosities
**Influence of Arthur Janov's Primal Scream Therapy** : Roland Orzabal was deeply interested in psychology and psychiatry at the time, particularly fascinated by the teachings of Arthur Janov, the developer of primal scream therapy. This therapeutic approach encourages patients to delve deep into their psyches to uncover the root of their issues, and was also embraced by figures like John Lennon and Yoko Ono. "Roland was deep into psychology and psychiatry at the time. He was particularly fascinated by the teachings of Arthur Janov, the guy behind primal screen therapy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw
**Saluted on Teal Album** : Tears for Fears was among the artists whose songs were saluted (covered) on Weezer's Teal Album. "So Tears for Fierce." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LYRPxtP61JM
