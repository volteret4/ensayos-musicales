**Admirers of 60s Garage Rock** : The Ramones were great admirers of the 60s era "Nuggets bands." Their musical style combined elements of Phil Spector-type girl groups with garage rock, ultimately becoming an inspiration for generations of people who wanted to rock out. "The Ramones were great admirers of these Nuggets bands, these 60s era bands." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UpWz3X33vSY

**Backstage Window Encounter (July 4, 1976)** : Joe Strummer of The Clash recounted how he, Johnny Rotten, and members of the Sex Pistols met The Ramones backstage at the Roundhouse by throwing a rock at a window. Johnny Ramone stuck his head out, and the British punks introduced themselves, being subsequently pulled up through the window in a "human chain" to get in. "And so they kind of formed a sort of human chain and pulled us up through this window. And that was the first time we met him and that was just a really great punk rock moment." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=huAvyInzz3Q
**Band Name Significance** : The name "Ramones" was chosen to signify unity, family, brother, gang, and brotherhood. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9T9wi0vEvc
**CBGB Debut (August 16, 1974)** : On August 16, 1974, The Ramones played their first gig at CBGB, opening for Television or possibly two other bands. The audience was minimal, with only six people (plus Hilly and his dog) present. They played 20 songs in 17 minutes, characterized by a primitive, ragged, and goofy appearance. "On August 16th, 1974, the Ramones opened for television, or they might have been opening for two other bands. No one really remembers because there were just six people in the bar." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4
**CBGB Residency** : The Ramones were considered the "home band" of the iconic New York club CBGB, where they played more frequently than almost any other group. "This was the home of the Ramones." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gKrAXMG8sTc
**Conquest of England (July 4 & 6, 1976)** : The Ramones played two pivotal shows in London, first at the Roundhouse (capacity 2,000) on America's bicentennial, and then at Dingwalls two nights later. Despite their European record company trying to cancel gigs, the band delivered explosive performances. These concerts were England's "first proper exposure" to US-style punk rock and are credited with transforming nearly every major player in the burgeoning British punk rock scene, including members of the Sex Pistols, The Clash, The Pretenders, The Damned, and Generation X. "And over the course of those two nights, July 4th and July 6th, 1976, just about every single person who would become a major player in the British punk rock explosion were taught a lesson by the Ramones." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=huAvyInzz3Q
**Cuckoo's Nest Performances** : The Ramones were among the punk bands that frequently played at The Cuckoo's Nest in Costa Mesa, a club often called "The Birthplace of Slam Dancing" in Southern California music lore. "The place hosted a lot of punk shows, from the Ramones to the Dam to Social Distortion." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KMx4bmq8o2Y
**Debut Performance at CBGB (August 14, 1974)** : On Wednesday, August 14, 1974, The Ramones played their first-ever show at the iconic New York club CBGB. The initial audience was sparse, consisting of fewer than a dozen people, including the club owner's dog. However, by the end of 1974, the band had performed at CBGB 74 times, with each gig progressively increasing their buzz. The club soon became packed with a diverse crowd, attracting Andy Warhol's circle, intellectuals, wealthy uptown youths seeking to "slum it," and music fans bored with existing sounds and looking for novelty. The Ramones' distinctive style — four "brothers" dressed in torn jeans and leather jackets, playing 1960s-style "bubble gum pop songs" at "a million miles an hour," without solos, stage interaction, or audience acknowledgment, pausing only when Johnny counted off the next song — marked the genesis of the New York punk rock revolution. "The Ramones played their first ever show at a New York club called CBGB." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8UIBWyUt38g
**Disdain for CBGB** : The Ramones themselves didn't think highly of CBGB initially, finding the stage too small, Hilly Crystal unhygienic, and the club plagued by rats and mice. They also found the clientele unpleasant, noting patrons would relieve themselves in corners due to the awful bathrooms. "Now to be fair, the Ramones didn't think too much of CBGB either. This stage was too small. They thought Hilly needed to bathe more and they hated how his dog, all the rats and all the mice, cracked all over the place." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4

**Formation (1974)** : The Ramones formed in 1974 when John Cummings (Johnny Ramone) and Douglas Colvin (Dee Dee Ramone) decided to start a band after getting drunk together. They spent $250 on a guitar, bass, and amp, holding their first rehearsal four days later. "They went from beers in a strip club, they got drunk, and they decided that they should form a band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4
**Formation Facilitated by Manie's Music** : The Ramones were formed a year after Douglas Colvin (D.D.) and John Cummings (Johnny) visited Manie's Music in 1973 to purchase a guitar, a bass, and a small amp, inspired to form a band. Along with Joey and Tommy, they solidified the lineup. "The Ramones, another band made possible by Henry Goldrich, the proprietor of Manie's music in New York." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
**Formation and Lineup Change – Drummer to Frontman** : The Ramones were formed in early 1974, with Jeff Hyman (later Joey Ramone) on drums, John Cummings (later Johnny Ramone) on guitar, and Douglas Colvin (later D.D. Ramone) on bass. Their first gig was on March 30, 1974. Due to Jeff's powerful, fast drumming style and his superior singing ability, he transitioned to lead vocalist, with a friend named Tommy taking over on drums using a basic $100 kit. **Music Philosophy and Band Name Meaning** : A journalist astutely characterized the Ramones' music as serving for "the head, the heart, and for the feet." **Name Origin (Paul McCartney Connection)** : The band's chosen surname, "Ramone," was inspired by Dee Dee Ramone's admiration for Paul McCartney. During his time with The Silver Beatles, McCartney would use the alias "Paul Ramone" to check into hotel rooms. Dee Dee, already calling himself Dee Dee Ramone, suggested "The Ramones" for the band to signify unity and family. "DD was a big fan of Paul McCartney and the Beatles, and the days of the Silver Beatles, Paul McCartney used to check on two hotel rooms using the alias Paul Ramone." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4
**Openness to Pop Influences** : Unlike some early punk bands that professed disdain for previous generations, The Ramones were open about their pop sensibilities and expressed no issues with iconic popular acts like The Beatles, The Beach Boys, Motown, or 60s girl groups. They frequently covered 60s pop songs, overtly demonstrating their appreciation for the genre. "The Ramones definitely a punk band, but under the surface, they were also very much a pop band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tZV1lz-obQE
**Original Lineup and Roles** : The initial lineup included Jeff Hyman (Joey Ramone) on drums, a "6'6-inch skinny guy" and "OCD-suffering refugee from a failed band called Sniper." Their first gig was in March. By July, Jeff Hyman moved to lead vocals because he was a better singer than drummer, and their manager, Tommy (Tommy Ramone), a recording engineer who had worked with Jimi Hendrix, took over on drums. "Their drummer was a 6'6-inch skinny guy named Jeff Heimann, an OCD-suffering refugee from a failed band called Sniper." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4
**Performance Length Controversy** : In their early days, The Ramones faced arguments backstage because audience members would complain they didn't play long enough, often only 45 minutes instead of the contracted hour. The band's defense was that they delivered an hour's worth of material in 45 minutes due to their rapid-fire style and lack of stage patter between songs. "There were always arguments backstage in the early days where the Ramones, when people would complain that the Ramones didn't play long enough.
