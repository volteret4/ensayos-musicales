**Conquest of England (July 4 & 6, 1976)** : The Ramones played two pivotal shows in London, first at the Roundhouse (capacity 2,000) on America's bicentennial, and then at Dingwalls two nights later. Despite their European record company trying to cancel gigs, the band delivered explosive performances. **Debut Performance at CBGB (August 14, 1974)** : On Wednesday, August 14, 1974, The Ramones played their first-ever show at the iconic New York club CBGB. The initial audience was sparse, consisting of fewer than a dozen people, including the club owner's dog. However, by the end of 1974, the band had performed at CBGB 74 times, with each gig progressively increasing their buzz. The club soon became packed with a diverse crowd, attracting Andy Warhol's circle, intellectuals, wealthy uptown youths seeking to "slum it," and music fans bored with existing sounds and looking for novelty. The Ramones' distinctive style — four "brothers" dressed in torn jeans and leather jackets, playing 1960s-style "bubble gum pop songs" at "a million miles an hour," without solos, stage interaction, or audience acknowledgment, pausing only when Johnny counted off the next song — marked the genesis of the New York punk rock revolution. "The Ramones played their first ever show at a New York club called CBGB." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8UIBWyUt38g
