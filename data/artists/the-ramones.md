**Band Name Meaning** : The name "Ramones" signifies unity, family, brother, gang, and brotherhood. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8UIBWyUt38g
**CBGB Residency (1974)** : By the end of 1974, The Ramones had played CBGB 74 times. With each performance, the buzz grew, and the club became crammed with diverse audiences, including Andy Warhol's entourage, intellectual types, wealthy uptown kids looking to "slum it," and music fans seeking something new and exciting. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8UIBWyUt38g
**Debut Album Release (Early 1976)** : Their debut album was released a few months prior to their pivotal London gigs in July 1976. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qC0Ee4-dpnI
**Debut Performance (1974)** : The band played their first ever show on Wednesday, August 14th, 1974, at the CBGB club in New York. There were fewer than a dozen people present, including the owner's dog. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8UIBWyUt38g
**Desired Sound Reference** : Green Day explicitly stated that they like The Ramones and are carrying on their musical legacy. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nyfdGYWelic
**Distinctive Image and Sound** : In the fall of 1974, The Ramones were distinct from other bands. They dressed in torn jeans and leather jackets, playing what amounted to 1960s bubblegum pop songs but at an extremely fast pace ("a million miles an hour"). Their performances featured no solos, no specific stage patterns, no acknowledgment of the audience, and no "letting out." The only breaks occurred when Johnny counted off the next song. This marked the beginning of the New York punk rock revolution. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8UIBWyUt38g
**Drummer Change and Name Adoption** : The original drummer Jeff Hyman (Joey Ramone) moved to lead vocals due to his loud and fast drumming style, and a friend named Tommy (Tommy Ramone) was "forced to keep time" on a $100 drum set. The original members adopted the common surname Ramone. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo
**Embrace of 60s Pop** : Never made a secret of their pop influences, openly covering various 1960s pop songs. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tZV1lz-obQE
**European Tour Cancellation Scare (July 1976)** : Their European record company connection had canceled almost all their tour gigs and was going to pull them from the Roundhouse show, but the band proceeded. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qC0Ee4-dpnI
**Formation and First Gear Purchase (1973)** : Douglas Colvin (who would become Dee Dee Ramone) and John Cummings (Johnny Ramone) decided to form a band in 1973 after an afternoon spent under the influence of marijuana. Immediately after, they went to Manny's Music in New York City and purchased a guitar, a bass, and a small amplifier, thus acquiring the foundational gear for their future band. A year later, with Joey and Tommy, they officially formed The Ramones. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
**Impact on British Punk Scene (July 1976)** : Their two London gigs in July 1976 were instrumental in exposing British musicians, including Johnny Rotten, Sid Vicious, members of The Clash, Chrissie Hynde, The Damned, and Billy Idol, to US punk rock, inspiring the British punk explosion. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qC0Ee4-dpnI
**Initial Lineup and First Gig** : Formed in early 1974 when Jeff Hyman (Joey Ramone) on drums, John Cummings (Johnny Ramone) on guitar, and Douglas Colvin (D.D. Ramone) on bass began jamming. Their first gig together was on March 30, 1974. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo
**Lollapalooza Performance (1996)** : The Ramones were featured in the 1996 Lollapalooza lineup. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**London Gigs (July 4 & 6, 1976)** : The Ramones played at the Roundhouse on July 4th and Dingwalls on July 6th, 1976, gigs that introduced US-style punk rock to England and transformed many future major players in the British punk rock scene. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qC0Ee4-dpnI
