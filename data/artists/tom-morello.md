## curiosities
**Academic Achievement** : In 1982, Morello enrolled at Harvard University, from which he graduated with an honors Bachelor of Arts degree in political science. He managed to achieve decent academic marks despite dedicating up to eight hours at a time to guitar practice. "In 1982, Tom enrolled at Harvard and graduated with an honors BA in political science." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Xo5DRSW95QQ
**Academic Achievements and Political Leanings** : Morello's political leanings were evident from high school, where he was sometimes considered an anarchist. He graduated with honors and went on to Harvard University, where he studied political science and earned a bachelor's degree in social studies. "Tom's politics have always leaned left, going as far as being a little anarchist at times even in high school. He graduated with honors and enrolled in Harvard as a political science student. You played in more bands there too, including one called Board of Education, which also featured a future Nobel Prize winner in chemistry." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_1NNvxgx8uA
**African American Contribution to Alt-Rock** : Tom Morello, guitarist for Rage Against the Machine and Audioslave, is identified as one of the many African American artists who have made important contributions to alternative rock. "Ben Harper, rage against the machine in AudioSlave guitars Tom Morello, there's Slash." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Contemporary Wah Pedal Usage** : Tom Morello of Rage Against the Machine is cited as a contemporary guitarist of African descent who prominently uses the wah-wah pedal, demonstrating the lasting legacy of this effect, pioneered by artists like Jimi Hendrix. "He loves his wall." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
**Disillusionment with Politics** : Morello briefly worked in the office of a U.S. Senator, an experience that greatly disillusioned him and led him to abandon any aspirations of a political career, choosing instead to focus on music. "For a while, he worked in the office of a US Senator, but what he saw really disillusioned him. He was never, after this, going to have a career in politics. Music was his thing, starting with a band called Lockup." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_1NNvxgx8uA
**Diverse Ancestry and Political Roots** : Tom Morello was born in Harlem. His mother is of Irish and Italian descent, while his father, Niggethi Najorroj, was from Kenya. His father participated in the Mau Mau uprising, an eight-year revolt against British colonialism, and later became Kenya's first ambassador to the United Nations in 1960. Additionally, a great-uncle on his father's side was the first president of independent Kenya, and another uncle became a cabinet minister. "Tom was born in Harlem. His mom was both Irish and Italian while his dad was from Kenya. Dad's name was Niggethi Najorroj. He participated in the Maumau uprising, an eight-year-long revolt against British colonialism." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_1NNvxgx8uA
**Early Connection to Adam Jones** : While attending school in Illinois, Tom Morello was in the same class as Adam Jones, who would later become the guitarist for Tool. They also played together in early bands like Electric Sheep. "In the same class was this kid named Adam Jones. He would later grow up to be the guitarist and tool. After picking up the guitar at age 13, he played in a bunch of bands with names like Nebula and Electric Sheep, which, by the way, also featured Adam Jones." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_1NNvxgx8uA
**Early Life and Family** : Born in Harlem, Tom Morello's father was from Kenya and became the first Kenyan ambassador to the UN after Kenya achieved independence from Britain in 1963. His mother met his father while teaching on a military base in Kenya. Following his parents' divorce, Tom and his mother relocated to the suburbs of Chicago. "He was born in Harlem. His dad was from Kenya." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Xo5DRSW95QQ
**Family Connection to Adam Jones** : Tom Morello, guitarist for Rage Against the Machine, Audioslave, and Prophets of Rage, has a family connection to Adam Jones of Tool. Morello's mother was Adam Jones's home-room teacher. "The mother of Tom Morello, guitarist for rage against the machine, audio slave and profits of rage, was the home-room teacher of Adam Jones, guitarist with tool." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=72Lei7uug8c
**Hip-Hop Influence on Guitar Style** : Despite playing in bands that covered rock acts like Led Zeppelin, Bachman-Turner Overdrive, and Black Sabbath, Tom Morello was profoundly influenced by Run DMC and Public Enemy. This led him to develop a unique guitar style that involved scratching the strings, mimicking a DJ's turntable technique. "Although these bands covered songs like Led Zeppelin, Bachminton, Overdrive, and Black Sabbath, Tom was profoundly affected by run DMC in public enemy. That's how his guitar style developed, scratching the strings, like a DJ would scratch on a turntable." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_1NNvxgx8uA
**Initiated Audioslave Formation** : After Zack de la Rocha's departure from Rage Against The Machine, Tom Morello contacted Chris Cornell to see if he would be interested in joining the remaining members of the band (Timmy and Brad). "On the other end was guitarist Tom Morello from Rage Against The Machine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-f9jhqAHkQo
**Innate Political Interest** : His family history, particularly his parents' and paternal relatives' involvement in significant political movements and leadership roles, explains Tom Morello's lifelong interest in politics, as it "literally runs in his blood." "So if you've ever wondered where Tom gets his interest in politics, it literally runs in his blood." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_1NNvxgx8uA
**Instrumental in Forming Tool** : Tom Morello played a crucial role in the formation of the band Tool by introducing his former classmate Adam Jones and Jones's new friend, singer Maynard James Keenan, to drummer Danny Carey. "Meanwhile, Adam Jones, who had also moved west, was looking to form his own band. Tom introduced Adam and his new friend, singer, Maynard James Keenan, to a drummer named Daddy Kerry. So, yeah, Tom basically put two all together." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_1NNvxgx8uA
**Musical Journey** : Morello's first band, at age 13, was a Led Zeppelin tribute band where he served as the singer. He initially struggled with the guitar and did not begin to study it seriously until he was about 16 years old, indicating he was a "late bloomer" on the instrument. "He really didn't start studying the guitar seriously until he was about 16 years old. He's a real late bloomer." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Xo5DRSW95QQ
**Parents' Political Activism** : Tom Morello's parents met while attending a pro-democracy protest in Nairobi, highlighting the deep-seated political influence in his family background. His mother returned to New York with Niggethi when she became pregnant, and Tom was subsequently born there. "And his parents met while both were at a pro-democracy protest in Nairobi. She soon found herself pregnant and returned to New York with Niggethi where Tom was born." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_1NNvxgx8uA
**Political Activism** : Morello was politically engaged from a young age, identifying himself as a "hardcore anarchist." Growing up in an entirely white suburb, he was acutely aware of and deeply affected by the racism he observed around him. "Tom was very political from the beginning. Extremely left wing. In fact, he categorized himself as hardcore anarchist." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Xo5DRSW95QQ
**Post-Graduation Career** : After completing his studies at Harvard, Morello moved to California, where he worked as an aide for Alan Cranston, a Democratic Senator. "Once he graduated, he moved to California to work as an aide to Alan Cranston, a Democratic Senator." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Xo5DRSW95QQ
