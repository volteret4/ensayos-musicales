## albums
**Songs from an American Movie Volume One, Learning How to Smile (2000)** : Released in July 2000, this album has a somewhat loose construction but is based on frontman Art Alexakis's second divorce. It focuses on the happy aspects of marriage, including falling in love, courting, good times, and nostalgia. This record was generally better received than its follow-up. "This album was mostly about the happy part of marriage, the falling in love, the courting, the good times, and the nostalgia." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KyNY3zv6uc
**Songs from an American Movie Volume Two, A Good Time for a Bad Attitude (2000)** : Released in November 2000, this record serves as the second part of a two-volume divorce experience. In contrast to the first volume, this album centers on the anger associated with a breakup. **Boston Mosh Pit Lawsuit (1997)** : During Everclear's performance at The Paradise club in Boston on November 13, 1997, three members of the New England Patriots – quarterback Drew Bledsoe, backup quarterback Scott Zolak, and offensive lineman Max Lane (who weighed 305 pounds) – were in the mosh pit. Max Lane arced off the stage and landed on 23-year-old Tamika Messier, crushing her and causing severe neck, shoulder, and arm injuries, including two herniated discs and three fused vertebrae. Messier filed a $1.2 million lawsuit on December 10, 1997, against the club, Lane, Bledsoe, and Everclear, which was settled in March 1999, with Everclear contributing to the settlement and subsequently facing higher liability insurance premiums. **MS Diagnosis (April 2017)** : Art Alexakis began noticing symptoms in 2015, including stumbling and forgetting lyrics, which some mistook for drinking or drug use. He dismissed it as tiredness. After a car crash in April 2017 and a subsequent arm twinge, an MRI, arranged by his daughter, revealed lacerations on his brain indicative of multiple sclerosis. He kept the diagnosis quiet for four years. "Then in April 2017, he was on his way to pick up his daughter at school. It was raining, and he crashed into a jeep. Now nobody was hurt, but then two weeks later, he had a twinge in his arm that he thought was left over from a snowboarding crash or maybe the jeep crash. His daughter set him up with an MRI, and that's when he learned the news. They found lacerations on Art's brain indicative of multiple sclerosis." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uzIMWVbi-GM
**Managing Multiple Sclerosis** : Initially viewing his MS diagnosis as a death sentence, Art Alexakis's prognosis improved after working with doctors who prescribed Copazone. He takes three injections a week, avoids inflammatory foods like sugar, and has been told by his doctor he could have another 30 years if his condition remains stable. While he can no longer run and has taken up swimming, his guitar playing remains unaffected, and he doesn't expect to be confined to a wheelchair.
