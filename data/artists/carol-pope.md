## songs
**High School Confidential (1980)** : This song appeared on Top 40 AM radio in 1980, creating a scandal and fascination due to its extremely upfront and suggestive themes concerning sexuality. Its success was particularly notable given that it was achieved in what was perceived as "dull, boring, conservative Canada." ## curiosities
**Extremely Upfront and Suggestive About Sex** : Carol Pope was "extremely up front and extremely suggestive when it came to sex," challenging societal norms and making bold statements through her music and persona. She was a woman openly declaring her lesbian identity in the early 1980s rock scene, which was almost unprecedented at the time. "Here was a woman being totally up front about being a lesbian. Who was doing that in the early 80s in the world of rock? Pretty much no one." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hswtuenFwvM
**First Openly Gay Alt-Rock Role Model (Canadian)** : Carol Pope is identified as one of the first openly gay alt-rock role models in the world, and notably, she was Canadian. She fronted the Toronto group Rough Trade. "One of the first openly gay, alt-rock role models in the world was a woman and a Canadian." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hswtuenFwvM
**Rough Trade Band Name Origin** : The name of her band, Rough Trade, was itself a term derived from gay subculture, directly signaling their connection to the community. "Carol Pope fronted rough trade, a Toronto group whose very name was a term from gay subculture." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hswtuenFwvM
