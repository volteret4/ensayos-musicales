# artist - Travis Barker

## curiosities
**Autobiographical Reflection** : In his autobiography, Travis Barker reflects on how life-altering events, such as his near-fatal plane crash, clarify one's understanding of personal relationships. He articulates a realization about who truly matters in life. "He writes in his autobiography that comes to point in life when you realize who really matters, who never did, and who always will." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Collaboration with DJ AM** : Travis Barker formed a collaborative project with DJ AM, aiming to merge live drumming with DJ mixing. DJ AM would spin classic songs on his decks, while Travis would augment the grooves with live drums, creating a unique hybrid performance style that they released online. "Their idea was to combine a DJ's mixing with live drums. DJ A.M. would spin classic songs and his decks and Travis would augment the groove." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**DJ AM's Death Impact** : When Travis Barker learned of DJ AM's death from a drug overdose, it caused a significant setback in his recovery and mental state, impacting him for months. This grief further complicated the already challenging process of recording the "Neighborhoods" album with Blink-182. "when Travis found out that DJA am died of a drug overdose, that set him back months." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Fashion Company** : Travis Barker ventured into the fashion industry by establishing his own company, Famous Stars and Strapz. **Five-Year Silence with Tom DeLonge** : By mid-2008, a significant communication breakdown had occurred between Travis Barker and Tom DeLonge, with Travis noting that he hadn't spoken a word to Tom in five years. This extended silence highlighted the deep rifts within Blink-182, making the prospect of a reunion seem unlikely. "Travis hadn't said a word to Tom in five years or would be the point of getting back together of nobody's talking." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE

This expanded his musical repertoire beyond rock drumming. "And musically he got deeper into beats, working with a series of hip-hop artists on recording and remixes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Plane Crash Injuries and Recovery (2008)** : Travis Barker sustained severe injuries in the September 2008 Learjet crash, suffering 30 re-burns to 65% of his body and developing blood clots. He spent four months in a Georgia hospital, undergoing 27 different surgeries. Amputation was considered, but he underwent tangential excision (scraping away dead skin). The agony was so intense that he offered friends a million dollars to end his suffering. He also developed PTSD and eventually divorced his wife after the ordeal. "Travis had 30 re-burns to 65% of his body. He had blood clots all over. He was in a Georgia hospital for four months and had 27 different surgeries." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Pre-Crash Premonition and Message** : On the way to the airport for a chartered Learjet 60 flight with DJ AM, Travis Barker had a strong premonition that something was amiss. He consumed several joints, three Vicodin, and his Xanax. Before boarding, he sent a picture of the small plane to a friend with a message expressing his unease and detailing his wishes for his children if anything were to happen to him. "Travis had a bad feeling on the way to the airport... I'm just letting you know, something doesn't feel right. The plane is really small. If anything happens to me, make sure the kids get the house and they're taken care of." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Reality TV Show** : Travis Barker starred in a reality television series called "Meet the Barkers" on MTV. The show documented his life with his wife, Shannon Mochler, even extending to filming inside the Blink-182 rehearsal space, which was a point of contention for Tom DeLonge. "Remember, meet the Barkers? That was an MTV show that followed Travis and his wife Shannon Mochler everywhere, including into the Blink rehearsal space." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
