## curiosities
**"Sellout" Image and Strategy** : Sigue Sigue Sputnik deliberately built their entire image around being "sellouts," proclaiming their slogan "Fleece the world" and describing themselves as "high-tech sex, designer violence, and the fifth generation of rock and roll," with a goal to elevate selling out to a new level where hype and packaging superseded musical ability. "And their whole thing, their whole image was built on being cellouts." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7kH_AfT3i4k
**Auctioned Album Advertising Space** : For their 1986 debut album, "Flaunt It," Sigue Sigue Sputnik controversially auctioned off advertising space between the tracks, and also sold space on the external album artwork to companies like Studio Line (L'Oreal) and ID Magazine, effectively embedding commercials directly into the album. "Not only did studio live from L'Oreal and Id Magazine by space on the external album artwork, but commercials were embedded in the album itself." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7kH_AfT3i4k
**Band's Longevity** : Despite their initial "crass commercialism" and breaking up after their second album, Sigue Sigue Sputnik managed to survive "in fits and starts" over a period of 20 years, finally ending in 2004. "The band survived and fits and starts over 20 years, finally ending it all in 2004." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7kH_AfT3i4k
**Lack of Musical Ability** : Several members of Sigue Sigue Sputnik openly had "almost zero musical ability," being included in the band primarily for their visual appeal and looks. "Oh, and did I mention that several members of the band had almost zero musical ability? They looked good, though, which was the only reason they were there." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7kH_AfT3i4k
**Music Secondary to Hype** : For Sigue Sigue Sputnik, the music itself was secondary to the band's focus on hype, packaging, image, costumes, hair, slogans, and making money, a strategy considered shocking in 1986. "Music was secondary to hype, packaging, image, costumes, hair, slogans, and making money." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7kH_AfT3i4k
**Record Deal Based on Sci-Fi Video** : The band secured their record deal with EMI Records without even submitting a demo tape; instead, they presented executives with a video composed of clips from science fiction movies, listing characters from *A Clockwork Orange*, *Terminator*, and *Mad Max* as their inspirations. "SIGSIG Sputnik didn't even bother putting together a demo tape to get their record deal. All they did was show a bunch of executives a video consisting of a bunch of clips from a bunch of science fiction movies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7kH_AfT3i4k
