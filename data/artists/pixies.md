## songs
**Debaser (Year not specified in transcript)** : The Pixies performed "Debaser" at Reading on August 26, 1990, as the day's headliners. The immense energy and crowd reaction to this song inspired Perry Farrell of Jane's Addiction to create Lollapalooza. "The image that stuck with both of them was when the pixies played this song." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Gigantic (1987)** : This song, from the *Surfer Rosa* album, was co-written by Black Francis and bassist Kim Deal, with Deal unexpectedly singing lead vocals. It became a major fan favorite, frequently included in the Pixies' encores, and has been covered by other indie bands. In spring 2014, Apple used the track in a TV commercial for iPhones, iPads, and iPods, despite its lyrics describing a woman observing an attractive black man making love to another woman. The song's success contributed to the band's first breakup, as Kim Deal's desire to sing lead on more Pixies songs was opposed by Black Francis. "The most obvious was the song gigantic from Serfa Rosa the band's first album Kim co-wrote it with black Francis and she ended up singing lead to everybody's surprise" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M1JTdUxOEMU
**Monkey Gone to Heaven (1989)** : A song from the Pixies' 1989 album "Doolittle," it embodies the band's distinctive style. The Pixies, while not explicitly labeled as nerd rock, exhibited nerdy and geeky qualities that became influential on subsequent bands in the 1990s. "This is Monkey Gone to Heaven from their greatest album, 1989's Doodle." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8lQv8eMJ868
**Wave of Mutilation (UK Surf Mix) (1989) – Not a Remix but a Completely Different Recording; 800 Promo Copies** : The Pixies' standard version of "Wave of Mutilation" appears on Doolittle (1989) — loud, fast, screaming. The UK Surf Mix is a slower, more languid, almost tranquil completely different recording, mislabeled as a remix. It first appeared on a one-sided 12-inch promotional record of only 800 copies, then on the B-side of the "Here Comes Your Man" single, then on the soundtrack to the Christian Slater film Pump Up the Volume. For some fans, it is the superior version. A comprehensive collection of Pixies B-sides is available on The Complete B-Sides (released March 2001). "Depending on your view of the Pixies, you may consider this to be the superior version of Wave of Mutilation." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-7KiHetQxMQ
