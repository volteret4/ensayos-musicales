## albums
**Licensed to Ill (1986)** : This monster album became the first rap record to reach number one on the Billboard charts, setting high expectations for the Beastie Boys' follow-up. "a monster album that became the first rap record to reach number one on the Billboard charts." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sn5TWBFd0XM
**Paul's Boutique (1989)** : This album, released in 1989, was the highly anticipated follow-up to *Licensed to Ill*. It was initially a commercial flop because it diverged significantly from expectations, being ahead of its time. The packaging is noteworthy, best viewed in its vinyl gatefold form, which unfolds from 12x12 inches to 12 inches by three feet, revealing a panoramic photo of Ludlow Street on Manhattan's Lower East Side, specifically from the perspective of 99 Ludlow Street. While MCA's alias Nathaniel Hornblower was credited, the real photographer was Jeremy Chattan, the Beastie Boys' original bass player from their punk era, who took about 30 rolls of film, including some lost bird's-eye shots from 101 Park Avenue. "Instead, they released something so ahead of its time that the record was initially a commercial flop." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sn5TWBFd0XM

## curiosities
**"Paul's Boutique" Store Fictionality** : The titular "Paul's Boutique" store depicted on the album cover never actually existed. The name was conjured up by Mike D, and the actual store photographed at 99 Ludlow Street was Lee's Sportswear, where the band hung a temporary sign. Today, neither Lee's Sportswear nor the adjacent Ben's Shoes remains at the location, with Google Street View showing a bank in its place. "There was no such store as Paul's boutique. That was the name Mike D. conjured up." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sn5TWBFd0XM
**2005 Renaissance** : By spring 2005, the Beastie Boys were "once hot again" and experiencing a "big-time renaissance" as rock music was on the ascendant. "The offspring and the Beastie boys were once hot again." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Classification as Alternative** : The Beastie Boys were mentioned as one of the bands in the early 1990s whose unique sound defied easy classification, leading to their categorization as "alternative" music. "there were the beastie boys... Alternative came to mean difference. It was a basket in which to put bands that defied description." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t2uXHP9cBdM
**Organizers of Tibetan Freedom Festival** : The Beastie Boys were responsible for organizing the first Tibetan Freedom Festival, an event that Rage Against the Machine supported and participated in as part of their broader activism. "included the first Tibetan Freedom Festival, which was organized by the Beastie Boys." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t2uXHP9cBdM
**Rhyme and Reason Tour Cancellation (2000)** : The Beastie Boys were scheduled to co-headline the 30-date "Rhyme and Reason Tour" with Rage Against the Machine in the summer of 2000, a tour intended to carry activist messages and donate to charity. The entire tour was canceled after member Mike D suffered a dislocated shoulder in a mountain bike accident. "In the summer of 2000, Rage announced that they were going to do a 30-date tour with the Beastie Boys called the Rhyme and Reason Tour... But then Mike D drove his mountain bike into a pothole and dislocated his shoulder." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t2uXHP9cBdM
