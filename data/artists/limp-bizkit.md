## curiosities
**Fred Durst's Path to Success (Early 1995)** : Fred Durst, initially a tattoo artist and a huge fan of Korn in Jacksonville, Florida, cleverly used an offer of free tattoos to meet the band. When Korn's guitarist, Head, accepted his offer in early 1995, Durst was able to give a Limp Bizkit demo to Korn's bassist, Fieldy. Fieldy, impressed by the demo, passed it along to Korn's management and then-budding "new metal" producer Ross Robinson. This chain of events led to Limp Bizkit securing a major label deal and becoming one of the most successful bands in the "new metal" era. Durst himself later became a major executive at Interscope Records, illustrating the far-reaching consequences of this initial interaction. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Somq2MoRi5Q
**Jessica Malachik Tragedy and Lawsuits (2001)** : At the Big Day Out Festival in Australia in 2001, 16-year-old Jessica Malachik was crushed in the pit and died five days later from a heart attack related to asphyxiation. The coroner cited singer Fred Durst for "alarming and inflammatory comments" when EMS people tried to rescue Jessica. Her parents filed a civil suit against Limp Bizkit, and the band was subsequently sued by their own insurance company, which stated such lawsuits were not covered by their policy. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KMx4bmq8o2Y
