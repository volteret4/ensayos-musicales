## curiosities
**Extensive Session Work** : Jim Gordon was a highly prolific drummer, contributing to literally hundreds of songs for a wide array of prominent artists including George Harrison, Joe Cocker, Dave Mason, Gordon Lightfoot, Harry Nilsson, Frank Zappa, Art Garfunkel, Traffic, and Steely Dan. He even contributed to the first Muppet movie. "Gordon could be heard on literally hundreds of songs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5vKSjpvVQa8
**Jim Gordon Death (March 13, 2023) – Died of Natural Causes in Psychiatric Prison at 77; Killed Mother in 1983** : Jim Gordon was a highly respected session drummer in the 1960s who played on dozens of recordings and performed with Eric Clapton in Derek and the Dominoes, including on the classic "Layla." He then developed severe schizophrenia — voices told him to starve himself, he couldn't sleep, was alcoholic, and received the wrong treatment. On June 3, 1983, a voice told him to attack his mother with a hammer and stab her to death with a butcher knife. He did. He was sentenced to prison and denied parole at least 10 times due to the severity of his mental illness; he remained in a psychiatric facility until he died of natural causes on March 13, 2023, at age 77. "A voice told him to attack his mother with a hammer and stab her to death with a butcher knife. So he did." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-JPLLJyOid8
**Matricide and Incarceration (June 3, 1983)** : On June 3, 1983, under the delusion that his mother was Satan, a voice instructed Gordon to kill her. He fatally attacked her with a hammer and a butcher knife. He was sentenced to 16 years in jail, as California law prevented the use of insanity as a defense, though his schizophrenia was acknowledged by the judge. "On June 3, 1983, a voice told him to kill his mother because she was Satan in disguise." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5vKSjpvVQa8
**Onset of Schizophrenia (Late 1970s)** : In the latter part of the 1970s, Gordon began experiencing severe mental health issues, including hearing voices, stopping eating, and ceasing to play drums. Initially misdiagnosed as alcohol abuse, these symptoms were later correctly identified as schizophrenia. "At some point in the latter part of the 1970s, he started hearing voices." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5vKSjpvVQa8
**Refusal of Parole and Death in Prison** : Jim Gordon remained incarcerated far beyond his initial 16-year sentence due to his persistent psychological incapacitation. He never attended any of his ten scheduled parole hearings, indicating no desire for release. He died of natural causes at age 77 on March 13, 2023, at the California Medical Facility in Vacaville, California. "He had no desire to be released." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5vKSjpvVQa8
