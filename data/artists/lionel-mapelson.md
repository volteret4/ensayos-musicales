# artist - Lionel Mapelson

## instruments
- Batini Phonograph

## albums
**Mapelson Cylinders (1900–1903) – Early Opera Bootleg Archive** : A collection of approximately 100 recordings made by Lionel Mapelson at the Metropolitan Opera, capturing live performances between roughly 1900 and 1903. These fragile wax cylinder recordings represent some of the earliest known live performance recordings and were later transferred to 10-inch 78 RPM records beginning in 1939. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OrHtPVBjXw4

## songs
**Tosca (1903) – Live Opera Recording** : Mapelson recorded a performance of _Tosca_ in 1903, requiring constant manual intervention due to technological limits. To capture the full opera, he had to swap wax cylinders every two minutes, demonstrating the extreme labor involved in early audio recording. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OrHtPVBjXw4

## curiosities
**52-Year Tenure and Death (1937)** : Mapelson served as librarian of the Metropolitan Opera for 52 years and died of a heart attack on December 21, 1937. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OrHtPVBjXw4
**Catwalk Recording Technique – 40 Feet Above Stage** : He recorded performances from a catwalk approximately 40 feet above the stage, using a portable phonograph to capture live opera acoustics from above the orchestra and singers. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OrHtPVBjXw4
**Cylinder Erasure Method – Reusable Recording Medium** : Due to the cost and fragility of wax cylinders, Mapelson would shave off grooves from unsatisfactory recordings, effectively erasing them for reuse. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OrHtPVBjXw4
**Legal Ambiguity – Unauthorized or Permitted?** : There is historical disagreement about whether Mapelson had permission to make and possibly distribute these recordings, placing his work at the boundary between archival preservation and piracy. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OrHtPVBjXw4
**Limited Recording Window – Activity Ceased After Three Years** : He produced around 120 recordings over approximately three years before stopping, likely because the Metropolitan Opera became aware of his activities and sought to control recordings themselves. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OrHtPVBjXw4
**Phonograph Obsession (March 22, 1900) – Self-Described “Maniac”** : In his diary, Mapelson described himself as “a phonograph maniac always making or buying records,” revealing his deep personal fixation on recording technology. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OrHtPVBjXw4
**“Godfather of Bootlegging” – Metropolitan Opera Librarian** : Lionel Mapelson, the official librarian of the Metropolitan Opera in New York, is widely considered the first major figure in music bootlegging. His position gave him unique access to performances, which he recorded independently. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OrHtPVBjXw4
