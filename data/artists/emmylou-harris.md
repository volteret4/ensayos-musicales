## songs
**Boulder to Birmingham** : Written by Bill Danoff, this song appeared on Emmylou Harris's 1975 album "Pieces of Sky." Harris used it to process her profound grief and feelings of loss following Gram Parsons' death, finding power in its words to express what she otherwise could not. The lyrics speak of finding solace, enduring loss, and the desire to see a loved one's face again. "She put her feelings about losing Graham, and she worked through her grief over losing him into a song that she called Bolder to Birmingham." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 35 - Cosmic American Music - 70s Country Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-35-cosmic-american-music-70s-country-rock ← emmylou-harris
**I Can't Help It (If I'm Still In Love With You)** : Emmylou Harris sang background vocals on Linda Ronstadt's cover of this Hank Williams song, featured on Ronstadt's "Heart Like A Wheel" album. **Grief Over Gram Parsons' Death** : Harris was profoundly distraught by Gram Parsons' death, having believed she would sing with him for the rest of her life. She channeled her feelings of loss and grief into the song "Boulder to Birmingham," which became a very important outlet for her. **Living Gram Parsons' Vision** : Emmylou Harris is seen as embodying Gram Parsons' vision of "cosmic American music" through her ability to authentically sing across multiple genres like gospel, bluegrass, country, and country rock. She describes her approach as "bleeding outside the lines sometimes," delivering diverse music with one of the most beautiful voices.
