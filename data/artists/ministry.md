## curiosities
**Al Jorgensen's Backstage Antics (Lollapalooza 1992)** : At a stop on the 1992 Lollapalooza tour, Ministry's leader, Al Jorgensen, was observed throwing furniture (table, lamps, couch, chair, carpet) out of his backstage trailer. He then carried a broken bottle of Bushmills whiskey, taking long swigs despite its jagged neck. Jorgensen then enlisted help to re-arrange the discarded furniture on the concrete outside his trailer, creating an impromptu "living room," and attempted to share his broken bottle of whiskey. "The last thing to come out of the trailer was Al Jorgensen, the leader of ministry. And he was carrying this broken bottle of Bush Mills whiskey." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=huAvyInzz3Q
**Al Jourgensen's Alias (Hypo Luxa)** : Al Jourgensen used the alias Hypo Luxa when producing Nine Inch Nails' cover of "Get Down Make Love." "If you look at the credits, you'll see that it was produced by someone named Hypo Luxa, which is actually El Jorgonson of Ministry." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DKdF8NgF5Bw
**Al Jourgensen's Backstage Antics at Lollapalooza '92** : At the Canadian stop of the 1992 Lollapalooza Festival in Barrie, north of Toronto, Ministry's leader Al Jourgensen engaged in notorious backstage behavior. This included throwing various pieces of furniture out of his trailer, drinking from a broken bottle of Bushmills whiskey, and then coercing an observer to help him arrange the discarded furniture into a makeshift "living room" on the concrete. "The last thing to come out of the trailer was Al Jorgensen, the leader of ministry and he was carrying this broken bottle of Bush Mills whiskey." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qC0Ee4-dpnI
**Associated with Wax Trax** : Ministry, fronted by Al Jourgensen, was one of the bands associated with Wax Trax of Chicago, a specialist indie label for heavy music that appeared around 1990. "This was the home of Ministry Front II IV II, Front Line Assembly and Dozens More." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Deepening into Alt-Rock** : Exploring the 1990s alternative music landscape often led listeners to discover heavier or more experimental acts like Ministry, signifying a move into more intense and diverse sounds within the genre. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m7MbV-eCLyE
**Drum Samples Used by ZZ Top in Late 1980s** : Ministry's drum sounds were sampled and utilized by ZZ Top on some of their late 1980s records. These samples were triggered and credited to ZZ Top's drummer, Frank Beard, indicating an early instance of inter-band sample usage. "Not physical drums, but samples taken from Ministry Records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-N6IM5Nm5iw
**Give It Away Remix (1991) – Ministry's Al Jourgensen Used a Stoned Chicken on the Mixing Console** : Al Jourgensen was invited by the Red Hot Chili Peppers to produce a remix of "Give It Away" for their *Blood Sugar Sex Magik* album. His reported method: he brought a live chicken into the studio, blew marijuana smoke in its face until the bird was intoxicated, then let the stoned chicken stagger across the mixing desk. Wherever the chicken left a dropping, Jourgensen made an adjustment to the mix. "He brought a chicken into the studio and blew pot smoke in the chicken's face... Where the chicken pooped, Al made an adjustment to the mix." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mmI2oN9zolQ
**Goth Aesthetic Retention** : Even after crossing the line into industrial music, Ministry maintained Goth characteristics, particularly in their fashion and stage props.
