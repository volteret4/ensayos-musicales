## songs
**We Are The World (1985)** : Composed by Lionel Richie and Michael Jackson and produced by Quincy Jones, this song brought together numerous artists in 1985. Its purpose was to raise funds for campaigns addressing the severe famines that plagued the 1980s. Released in early 1985, the song achieved immediate global success, topping the charts in virtually every country worldwide. On April 5, 1985, it was featured in a monumental event where it was simultaneously broadcast on 5000 radio stations around the globe. For many people over 30, the song evokes strong childhood memories and holds deep sentimental value. ## curiosities
**Motown Label Misconception** : A common but incorrect belief is that "We Are The World" was edited and released by Motown Records. This misconception likely arose because the song was composed by Lionel Richie and Michael Jackson, both prominent Motown stars, and produced by Quincy Jones, a key figure associated with Motown at the time. Despite the song's initial idea being conceived at the Motown house, it was ultimately released by Columbia Records due to economic and resource-related considerations. **Quincy Jones's "Leave Your Ego" Note** : During the invitation process for "We Are The World," producer Quincy Jones personally hand-wrote letters to the major artists he knew, asking them to participate.
