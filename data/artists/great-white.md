**The Station Nightclub Fire (February 20, 2003)** : On February 20, 2003, during a Great White concert at The Station Nightclub in West Warwick, Rhode Island (mistakenly referred to as Long Island in the transcript), a pyrotechnic display at the start of their set ignited flammable foam insulation on the club's walls and ceiling. The entire building was engulfed in flames within six minutes, resulting in 230 injuries and 100 deaths, including Great White guitarist Ty Longley and a local radio DJ who was MCing the event. The tragedy led to criminal trials, civil lawsuits totaling approximately $176 million in settlements, and the imprisonment of one of the club's owners. Crucially, the disaster prompted significant increases in insurance requirements and the implementation of new, stringent safety protocols for indoor shows worldwide, making venues generally safer after this date.
