## albums
**Donnie Darko (2001) – Original Soundtrack** : San Diego musician Michael Andrews was contacted by director Richard Kelly in 2001 to work on the soundtrack for the film "Donnie Darko." Andrews thought "Mad World" would be perfect for the soundtrack and recorded a stripped-down, slowed-tempo version for it. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw

## songs
**Mad World (2003) – Tears for Fears Cover** : Michael Andrews, who loved "Mad World" as a kid, believed it was perfect for the "Donnie Darko" soundtrack but needed a different form. He stripped the song down to its basics, using only piano, a mellotron, and a few other flourishes, partly for artistic reasons and partly due to budgetary constraints. He also slowed the tempo by approximately 50%. Andrews recruited his favorite singer, Gary Jules, with whom he had previously been in a band, for vocals. This version was included on the official movie soundtrack and DVD, and was later released as a single on December 15, 2003, soaring to the top of the British charts and becoming the Christmas number one that year. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw
