## albums
**Isn't Anything (1988) – Shoegaze Foundation** : This 1988 debut album set the foundational sound for the Shoegaze genre. Shoegaze itself is described as the extreme, louder end of Dreampop. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFLMkvJAaUY
**Loveless (1991)** : This album took two years to make and literally bankrupted the Creation label due to its intensive production. Despite the financial cost, "Loveless" is still widely cited as a sonic blueprint that influenced hundreds, if not thousands, of bands throughout the 1990s and up to the present day. "My Bloody Valentine spent two years making an album called Lovelace, that literally bankrupted Creation." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Loveless (1991) – Defining Shoegaze Album; Inspired Chapterhouse, Ride, Lush** : My Bloody Valentine's Loveless is considered the definitive shoegaze record, characterized by waves of distorted, floaty guitar noise designed to wash over the listener at high volume. It directly inspired a generation of British bands including Chapterhouse, Ride, and Lush. "My Bloody Valentine was a leader in this area and inspired a ton of followers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B2ZbaaYx0MI

**Informal Dissolution (1993)** : My Bloody Valentine never officially broke up; instead, leader Kevin Shields became distracted. After the release of their expensive and groundbreaking "Loveless" album in 1991, they signed with Island Records and received a half-million-dollar advance, which was spent building a new studio. This studio proved to be "awful" with numerous technical problems. The band "just sort of evaporated" by the end of 1993, with their existence effectively ceasing around July 1, 1993. "And by the end of 1993, the band had just sort of evaporated." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJGFUlQTUhQ

**The Apocalypse Live Shows** : My Bloody Valentine has a reputation for performing brutally loud live shows. These concerts often culminate in a segment affectionately termed "the Apocalypse" by fans, which consists of up to 30 minutes of uninterrupted distortion and feedback. "My bloody Valentine has a reputation of playing brutally loud shows, culminating in something fans called the Apocalypse, which is up to 30 minutes of nothing but distortion and feedback." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w
**Wall of Fuzz and Feedback** : My Bloody Valentine is renowned for taking the use of fuzz, feedback, and distortion to new levels, which laid the foundational groundwork for the shoegaze genre. This genre, characterized by its immersive soundscapes, would be impossible to create without these extreme sonic elements. "Their wall of fuzz and feedback and distortion took things to new levels, creating the foundations of shoegase, a genre that could not exist without these sounds." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w
