## albums
**Isn't Anything (1988) - Shoegaze Precursor** : Released in 1988, this record is considered to have arrived "at the very beginning of the whole shoegaze thing." It was characterized by "guitar drones and layers and layers of feedback" that generated a "near-trance-like soundscape," an "impenetrable atmosphere of sound," and made extensive use of effects pedals. "In 1988, they released a record called, Isn't Anything, which was filled with guitar drones and layers and layers of feedback that created near-translikes soundscape." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vEHdoUlGurY
**Isn't Anything (1988) – Shoegaze Foundation** : This 1988 debut album set the foundational sound for the Shoegaze genre. Shoegaze itself is described as the extreme, louder end of Dreampop. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFLMkvJAaUY
**Loveless (1991)** : This album took two years to make and literally bankrupted the Creation label due to its intensive production. Despite the financial cost, "Loveless" is still widely cited as a sonic blueprint that influenced hundreds, if not thousands, of bands throughout the 1990s and up to the present day. "My Bloody Valentine spent two years making an album called Lovelace, that literally bankrupted Creation." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Loveless (1991) - Influential Masterpiece** : Released in 1991, *Loveless* is considered the band's "biggest contribution" to music. It was recorded over two years in 19 different studios, a process so extensive and costly that it "nearly bankrupted their record label." Despite not selling in "appreciable numbers," its influence significantly "far outweighed its commercial performance," deeply impacting artists like Trent Reznor, Billy Corgan, Robert Smith, Courtney Love, and The Edge of U2. "It was an album called Loveless that was recorded over two years in 19 different studios. And it was so expensive that it nearly bankrupted their record label." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vEHdoUlGurY
**Loveless (1991) - Tremendously Influential** : My Bloody Valentine issued the "tremendously influential loveless album" in 1991. "my bloody Valentine issues the tremendously influential loveless album" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Rf1tFWQg51Y
**Loveless (1991) – Defining Shoegaze Album; Inspired Chapterhouse, Ride, Lush** : My Bloody Valentine's Loveless is considered the definitive shoegaze record, characterized by waves of distorted, floaty guitar noise designed to wash over the listener at high volume. It directly inspired a generation of British bands including Chapterhouse, Ride, and Lush. "My Bloody Valentine was a leader in this area and inspired a ton of followers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B2ZbaaYx0MI

**Pioneering Noise Soundscape** : My Bloody Valentine from Dublin were central to developing the shoegaze sound, focusing on "layers of noise, psychotic amounts of noise." They created an "impenetrable atmosphere of sound" with guitars, often making vocals difficult to discern. Their sound featured "weird harmonics" and "near-trance-like soundscapes" from drones and feedback, achieved through extensive use of effects pedals. "Rarely had so many people use so many effects pedals in the guitars to create such an impenetrable atmosphere of sound." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vEHdoUlGurY
