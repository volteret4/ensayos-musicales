## curiosities

"When a car line crush was seriously reactivated and started getting back to work, Trevor had to quit his nurse gig." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jMfH9xAOpI
**Documentary Subject** : Trevor Hurst's experience working in the Kenna Poaca indigenous community is documented in a film called "Flatlander." "A documentary called Flatlander covers his experience." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jMfH9xAOpI
**Return to Education for Psychiatric Nursing** : After Econoline Crush's initial breakup and sporadic returns, Trevor Hurst, at age 45, enrolled in a psychiatric nursing program at Brandon University in 2011. This decision was driven by financial needs (having four kids), an interest in psychology, and his personal history with alcohol and addiction demons. "So in 2011, Trevor went back to school at the age of 45." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jMfH9xAOpI
**Work in Indigenous Community** : After graduating, Hurst found a job at Kenna Poaca, a Dakota community in southwest Manitoba, which had sought help for two years. He became deeply involved in the community's indigenous culture for several years, also serving as an instructor for nursing fundamentals. "Trevor became deeply involved in the community's indigenous culture, spending several years there." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jMfH9xAOpI
