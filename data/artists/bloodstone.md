## curiosities
**Disco's Impact** : Like many R&B groups, Bloodstone's career was significantly affected by disco, until they later re-emerged on the legacy circuit. "Like a lot of R&B groups, Disco kind of did them in until they re-emerged on the legacy circuit." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 46 - The Chaos and Music of the Ozark Music Festival of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/ ← bloodstone
**Move to London (1971)** : The founding members, originally from the "ghetto of Kansas City," moved to London in 1971 because there was too much competition for recording contracts in Los Angeles. "they went there in 1971 because there was just too much competition for recording contracts in Los Angeles." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 46 - The Chaos and Music of the Ozark Music Festival of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/ ← bloodstone
**Ozark Festival Reception (1974)** : At the Ozark Music Festival, MC Wolfman Jack announced Bloodstone as a "band from London," which led the audience to expect a white rock band, perhaps similar to The Rolling Stones. As an African American R&B band, their music did not fit the audience's expectation of rock or bluegrass, resulting in fans throwing objects and shouting for them to leave the stage. "They did not like what they heard and they let the band know it by throwing things at them and shouting encouragement for them to depart the stage." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 46 - The Chaos and Music of the Ozark Music Festival of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/ ← bloodstone
