# artist - Bachman-Turner Overdrive

## curiosities
**Early Success and Domestic Acceptance** : Bachman-Turner Overdrive (BTO) had success as far back as the 1960s, but like many Canadian groups, domestic acceptance and popularity only came after they had a hit in America. This pattern highlights the historical bias where external validation was crucial for Canadian artists at home. "The guests who BTO Rush, Triumph, April Wine and others had success as far back as the 1960s. But in most cases, domestic acceptance and popularity only came after these groups had a hit in America." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6PGgNCaW4xo
**Increased FM Airplay** : Due to new FM radio rules in Canada that mandated playing more album tracks and Canadian content, Bachman-Turner Overdrive's music, alongside others like The Guess Who and Neil Young, saw significantly more airplay. This was in part because there weren't enough new Canadian bands of a certain caliber yet. "a lot more guest who, back then turn or overdrive the band and Neil Young got played." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6PGgNCaW4xo
**"Car Music" Description** : Randy Bachman described Bachman-Turner Overdrive's music as "car music," meaning the kind of music specifically designed for listening to while driving. **Band Name Origin** : The band's name, Bachman-Turner Overdrive (BTO), originated from a trucking magazine called "Overdrive" that they encountered at a steakhouse in Windsor, Ontario. "the name came from a trucking magazine that they saw at a steakhouse in Windsor, Ontario. The name of the magazine was Overdrive." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 45 - Oh, Canada! **Formation (1970)** : Following Randy Bachman's departure from The Guess Who in 1970, he co-founded a new band, Bachman-Turner Overdrive, with former Guess Who member Chad Allan, his brother Robbie Bachman, and bassist Fred Turner. "He left the guest who in 1970 and with another former band member Chad Allen and Backman's brother Robbie and bassist red turner, they formed a new band." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 45 - Oh, Canada! **Fred Turner's Role in Naming** : Fred Turner proposed the band name by writing "BTO" on a napkin, which the members found appealing because "Overdrive" aptly described their musical style. "Fred Turner, he took a napkin, he wrote BTO on it. He's like, what do you think about this? They liked it because Overdrive seemed like a good descriptor of their style of music." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 45 - Oh, Canada! **Strict Band Conduct and Tim Bachman's Firing** : Randy Bachman enforced strict behavioral rules for his new bandmates, including prohibitions on drinking, drugs, and pre-marital sex. This commitment to "straight-laced behavior" was so strong that his brother, Tim Bachman, was fired from the band, with strong belief that it was for not adhering to these regulations. "He was serious, though, about the type of straight lace behavior that he expected from his new bandmates so much so that he fired his brother Tim and it's strongly believed that Tim was fired for not adhering to these rules." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 45 - Oh, Canada!
