## songs
**It's Your Move** : This optimistic and danceable song by Diana Ross was famously used by Macintosh Plus for the opening track of their seminal vaporwave album, "Floral Shoppe." When slowed down and partially manipulated, the song's original cheerful essence was transformed into something nostalgic, melancholic, and even ghostly, showcasing the profound emotional shift that tempo and pitch manipulation can create. "Al ralentizar la canción de Ross, la cual es optimista y bailable, se transforma en algo nostálgico, melancólico e incluso fantasmal." ← ¿Por qué el Slowed & Reverb y los Speed Up están rompiendo YT y TikTok？ | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0WWra85DsCo
**Love Hangover (1976)** : This song became Diana Ross's fourth number one solo career hit on May 29, 1976, making her the female solo artist with the most number ones at that time. Vince Aletti listed it as an essential disco record of 1976. The club version was an extended play, longer than what was typically heard on the radio. It was nominated for a Grammy for Best Female R&B Performance but lost to Aretha Franklin's "Master of Eyes." **Grammy Nominations Without Wins** : Diana Ross had been nominated for 12 Grammys but had zero wins, a point highlighted when "Love Hangover" lost the Grammy for Best Female R&B Performance. "Diana Ross has been nominated for 12 Grammys and has zero wins." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 31 - The Spirit of '76 - Pop Music on America's Bicentennial | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-31-the-spirit-of-76-pop-music-on-americas-bicentennial
**Recording "Love Hangover"** : When producer Hal Davis presented Diana Ross with "Love Hangover," she initially disliked the music, though she liked the lyrics. Davis then transformed the studio into a club atmosphere with red and strobe lights to help Ross feel the mood, resulting in her genuine laughter being captured and left in the final recording. "she had fun with this record when you hear her laughing in the background on this record that's genuine and Davis left it in." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 31 - The Spirit of '76 - Pop Music on America's Bicentennial | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-31-the-spirit-of-76-pop-music-on-americas-bicentennial
