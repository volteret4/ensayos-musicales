## albums
**First Issue (1978) - 'Versyshu' (Debut Album)** : Public Image Ltd launched their first album in 1978, referred to in the transcript as "Versyshu," generating high expectations as it was Lydon's new band and he was still considered the face of punk by many. However, its design was far from punk, with members posing like a Vogue photoshoot. Its sound also surprised many, as it didn't completely break with punk rock tradition but included tracks with strong dub and dance music influences. **Flowers of Romance (1981) - More Radical** : Following *Metal Box*, Public Image Ltd released *Flowers of Romance*, an album described as even more radical than its predecessor. Subsequent albums by the band became somewhat more conventional. "Luego habían flowed esos romance, un disco así que ve más radical que Metalbox." ← La traición que salvó al punk. PiL | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YzQ6o-FgxSY
**Metal Box (1979) - Musical Masterpiece** : *Metal Box* is considered a masterpiece and one of the biggest ruptures in music history. It featured incredible bass lines from Jah Wobble, acting as both rhythmic pulse and melodic motif, creating physical and heavy lows described as "the human heartbeat." Keith Levene, freed from traditional rhythm and melody duties, played dislocatedly, producing crystalline and kaleidoscopic sounds. "Y ahora sí, hablemos de esta obra maestra, Metalbox." ← La traición que salvó al punk. PiL | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YzQ6o-FgxSY
**Metal Box (1979) - Studio as Instrument** : For *Metal Box*, Public Image Ltd fully embraced the methodologies of disco and Jamaican dub, using the recording studio as an instrument in itself. They radically processed sounds, employed echo chambers, and incorporated immense empty spaces, treating silence as equally important as noise. "Abrazaron del todo la metodología de la música disco y del tapjamaicano, o sea que usaban el estudio de grabación como si fueran instrumentos más." ← La traición que salvó al punk. PiL | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YzQ6o-FgxSY
**Metal Box (1979) - Symbol of Rupture** : The metallic box packaging of *Metal Box* served as a symbol of a radical break from the punk scene that had dominated two years prior. Its release marked the birth of something new, rejecting punk's rules, limits, and dogmas, and forging a new path for music. "esta caja metálica no era solo una lecdota, sino que era un símbolo, el símbolo de una ruptura con una escena que había arrasado dos años antes, el punk." ← La traición que salvó al punk. PiL | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YzQ6o-FgxSY
**Metal Box (1979) - Unconventional Packaging** : Released in the winter of 1979, *Metal Box* was presented not as a conventional record but as a metallic 16mm film canister, devoid of band photos. This weighty, hard-to-store, and more expensive object was intentionally "hostile to the consumer," a deliberate choice by John Lydon. "Se encontraron con esto, una caja metálica de película de 16 milímetros, sin fotos de la banda, que pesaban montón, que era incómoda de almacenar en las estanterías y para colmo era mucho más caro que un disco normal." ← La traición que salvó al punk. ## songs
**"Fodderstompf" (1978) - The 'Ponderstown' Joke Track** : This rhythmic and repetitive song, referred to by the transcript as "Ponderstown," was created as a joke to extend the album and fulfill contractual obligations with Virgin. It was inspired by Donna Summer's "I Feel Love" and is notably one of their most "rock tourist" tracks, showcasing potent dub and dance music influences. Despite being a joke, its hypnotic dub-funk bassline and characteristic drum sound laid the rhythmic foundations for their subsequent album, *Metal Box*. "Esta especie de broma estaba inspirada en el afil love de Don Asamer, ya en corrigenariamente a eso, una broma, es con diferencia su tema más rock turista, es la que tiene la influencia del dub y la música de baile más latente." ← La traición que salvó al punk. PiL | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YzQ6o-FgxSY

**Formation (1978)** : Public Image Ltd (PIL) was formed in 1978, following the separation of the Sex Pistols. The band was seen as a continuation of the tradition established by the Sex Pistols.
