## curiosities
**Breakthrough at 37** : Nathaniel Rateliff found widespread recognition at age 37 following the viral success of a song from his 2015 self-titled album with The Night Sweats, after many years of struggling in the music industry. "And suddenly, everybody was talking about Nathaniel at age 37." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P45lJTdneoI
**Early Career Challenges** : After his initial band Born in the Flood, and subsequent solo work, Nathaniel Rateliff released multiple records and played hundreds of gigs, receiving good reviews but struggling to achieve significant financial success, making a "hard living." "Reviews were generally good, but that really didn't translate into any big paydays. Yes, it was a living, but it was a hard one." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P45lJTdneoI
**Rejected Record Deal** : During his time with Born in the Flood, Nathaniel Rateliff turned down an indie record deal, despite his band being a "local sensation" and receiving praise from The New York Times. "There was an indie album, but for some reason Nathaniel turned down a record deal." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P45lJTdneoI
**Relocation to Denver (Age 18)** : Nathaniel Rateliff moved from Missouri to Denver at the age of 18, where he would later form his first band, Born in the Flood. "He relocated from Missouri to Denver when he was 18." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P45lJTdneoI
**Sound Shift to Soul (2013)** : In 2013, after a decade and a half in Denver and feeling discouraged with his career trajectory, Nathaniel Rateliff made a "Hail Mary Pass" by changing his sound from dark and somber to more soulful. This led to the formation of The Night Sweats and a deal with Stax Records. "Then in 2013, a decade and a half after moving to Denver, Nathaniel changed up his sound. And frankly, this was a Hail Mary Pass." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P45lJTdneoI
