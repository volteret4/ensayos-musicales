## songs
**Shook Me All Night Long (1980)** : This song was part of the playlists used to subject Iraqi prisoners of war to long stretches of metal music during the First Gulf War, which began in 1990. "ACDC shook me all night long, paranoid from Black Sabbath, and no more Mr. Nice Guy from Alice Cooper were some of the songs on those playlists." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=R1wdsGt07Gg

## curiosities
**Artwork Design Studio** : Hipgnosis, a London-based design studio, created artwork for ACDC. "They did work for Pink Floyd, Led Zeppelin, ACDC, Peter Gabriel, and many others." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pwebM3TfSdg
**Awful Concert Sound (1978)** : ACDC opened for Aerosmith at the Winnipeg Arena on August 30, 1978, with Bon Scott on lead vocals. Despite the potential for an "awesome" show, the sound was described as a "blare of distortion," making the entire experience terrible. "That should have been awesome, but it wasn't." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qrrwqdpCjpQ
**Interactive Packaging** : ACDC has released albums and box sets with elaborate art, including packaging that features embedded LED screens for moving pictures instead of static images. "Tool, ACDC, Pink Floyd, and others have released albums and box sets loaded with art, even to the point of including LED screens embedded in the packaging." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pwebM3TfSdg
**Operation Just Cause Playlist** : ACDC's music was part of the playlist deployed by the US Army during Operation Just Cause in December 1989, when they bombarded the Vatican Embassy in Panama City with loud music to compel Manuel Noriega to surrender. "There was Motley Crew, there was Led Zeppelin, there was ACDC, it was actually quite the playlist." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=R1wdsGt07Gg
**Prohibition on Sampling (Multiple Years)** : Sampling from ACDC's music was considered extremely risky and legally problematic. The transcript warns that "woe to anyone who dared to use a sample from the Beatles or ACDC," indicating a strong stance against unauthorized use of their material. "Meanwhile, woe to anyone who dared to use a sample from the Beatles or ACDC." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ak6OuOUkRrQ
**Soviet Blacklist - Neo-Fascism and Violence** : ACDC was featured on the 1985 "not recommended music" list by Soviet authorities, who cited "Neo-Fascism and Violence" as reasons for their prohibition. This strong ideological opposition positioned the group as a significant cultural threat in the eyes of the Soviet Union. "ACDC, Neo-Fascism and Violence." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NyKvAhO7MHE
