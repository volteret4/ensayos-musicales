**German Origins and Early Career** : Klaus Nomi, a German artist, had a strong affinity for opera and began his career singing opera in a gay disco in West Berlin. After moving to New York, his performances grew increasingly otherworldly, incorporating wild costumes, makeup, and elaborate hair. His live shows were known for dramatic elements like smoke, strobes, and explosions. "Clouse was German, and he loved opera. **New York Underground and David Bowie Collaboration** : Nomi became a prominent figure in the New York Underground scene, gaining recognition from artists such as Keith Haring and Jean-Michel Basquiat. David Bowie, upon hearing about Nomi, rushed to see one of his live shows. This led to Nomi's appearance with Bowie on Saturday Night Live on December 15, 1979, where Nomi sang backup on three songs.
