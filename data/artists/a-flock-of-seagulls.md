## curiosities
**Band Members as Hairdressers** : Two members of A Flock of Seagulls had prior experience as hairdressers, a detail often cited in connection with the band's distinctive and often elaborate hairstyles. "Now there were two hairdressers in the band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q3zkJX8S4Xc
**First British Technopop Billboard Top Ten Hit** : A Flock of Seagulls achieved a significant milestone by being the first British Technopop band to break into the top ten of the Billboard singles chart, likely during the winter of 1981-82. This success was attributed not only to the song itself but also heavily to the extensive MTV play of its video and singer Mike Score's distinctive swoopy haircut. "As far as anyone can tell, this song was the first hit by a British Technopop band to reach the top ten of the Billboard singles chart. The song is fine, but it was MTV play of the video and singer Mike Scors' swoopy haircut that really made that happen." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
**Iconic New Wave Hairstyle** : The singer of A Flock of Seagulls is frequently the first individual recalled when discussing 1980s New Wave hairstyles, underscoring the profound impact of his visual presentation on the genre's aesthetic. "In fact, when you talk about new wave hairstyles from the 1980s, the first guy who inevitably comes to mind is the dude from a flock of seagulls." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q3zkJX8S4Xc
**Mike Score's Signature Haircut** : Mike Score, the singer of A Flock of Seagulls, was recognized for his unique "goofy swoopy in your face haircut," which became a defining image for the band and a memorable new wave style.
