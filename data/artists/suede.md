## songs
**Animal Nitrate (1993)** : The third single from their 1992 self-titled album, released on February 22, 1993. The song's sharp, slashing guitars and glaminess made it sound distinct to English ears, feeling fresh yet familiar and evoking classic British rock from the early 1970s, which singer Brett Anderson intended. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg

## curiosities
**Britpop Status (Late 1995)** : By the end of 1995, the band had "crept back into the scene" amidst the dominance of Oasis and Blur. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg
**Celebration of English Eccentricity (Early 1990s)** : While most of the UK was embracing grunge, Suede rejected it, instead celebrating the "great eccentric English rock of the 70s and 80s." This included influences from David Bowie, Roxy Music, T-Rex, The Smiths, Morrissey, British Psych, Jagger Swagger, and Glam guitars. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg
**Dandy English Pop Persona** : Singer Brett Anderson cultivated the persona of a "dandy English pop," proudly singing in a London accent, explicitly contrasting with the grunge aesthetic. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg
**Early Britpop Pioneer (1992)** : The band was among the very first signs of what would become Britpop, appearing in 1992. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg
