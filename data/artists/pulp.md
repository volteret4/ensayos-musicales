## curiosities
**Artsy Style and Observational Lyrics** : Pulp was noted for being "more artsy than most," with lead singer Jarvis Cocker's lyrics frequently characterized as "observational." This lyrical approach was a key influence on the Arctic Monkeys. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YvcnHKI4oIw
**Long History and Mid-90s Success** : Pulp had been active since the late 1970s but did not achieve significant success until the mid-1990s, coinciding with when the Arctic Monkeys were in junior high. This timing meant Pulp was an unavoidable presence for young, aspiring musicians in Sheffield. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YvcnHKI4oIw
**Steve Mackey Death (March 2, 2023) – Undisclosed Illness, Age 56; Produced Florence and the Machine's Lungs; Appeared in Harry Potter** : Steve Mackey joined Pulp in 1989 and was their bassist through the glory years of the 1990s Britpop era before leaving around 2002. He became a producer and remix artist, working with Cornershop, The Kills, and The Horrors, and co-produced Florence and the Machine's debut album *Lungs*. He also worked as a music director and sound designer for institutions including the Louvre, the Museum of Modern Art, and a Shanghai museum. Harry Potter fans may know him as one of the Weird Sisters in *Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire* — a group that also featured Jarvis Cocker of Pulp and Johnny Greenwood and Phil Selway of Radiohead. When Pulp announced a reunion in early 2023, Mackey did not want to participate — in retrospect, probably because he was already ill. His death was announced March 2, 2023, having been in hospital since Christmas 2022 with an undisclosed condition. He was 56.
