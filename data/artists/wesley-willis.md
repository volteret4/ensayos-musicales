## curiosities
**Background and Mental Health** : Wesley Willis was a 6'5", 350 lb street artist from Chicago who began showing signs of schizophrenia in 1989, leading to institutionalization. He had distinctive behaviors, including head-butting as a greeting (resulting in a perpetual forehead bruise), and struggled with hygiene and unexpected vomiting, yet found solace in music. "Wesley was a 6'5, 350 lb street artist from Chicago. His thing was selling landscape drawings to people who passed by on the street." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
**Comparison to Jimmy Mitchell** : Jimmy Mitchell's style is noted to have similarities to that of Wesley Willis, suggesting a shared approach within outsider music. "If you're a student of outsider music, you might hear some of the similarities between his style and that of Wesley Willis, a performer covered on a previous episode on this subject." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HcK4M7-02ik
**Death and Legacy** : Wesley Willis's schizophrenia worsened over time, and he was later diagnosed with leukemia, which led to his death in August 2003. A large memorial service was held for him, and he continues to be regarded as a true outsider music superstar due to his prolific output and unbridled passion. "Sadly, Wesley schizophrenia became worse and then he was diagnosed with leukemia and he died in August of 2003." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
**Major Label Distribution** : Friends helped Wesley form The Wesley Willis Fiasco, which toured with various indie bands. His recognition grew to the extent that he secured a record deal with Rick Rubin's American Recordings, providing major label distribution for some of his material, a rare achievement for an outsider artist. "He even picked up a record deal with Rick Rubin's American recordings, which means he actually had major label distribution for some of his material." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
**Mention in Outsider Music History** : Wesley Willis is listed among artists previously covered as examples of outsider music, alongside The Shags, The Legendary Stardust Cowboy, and others. "On those programs, I covered Wesley Willis, the Shags, the legendary Stardust cowboy, Jandic, Captain B. Fart, Daniel Johnson, Florence Foster Jenkins, and even Charles Manson." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HcK4M7-02ik
**Music as Liberation** : Music, particularly rock n' roll which he affectionately called his "joy ride music," served as a significant source of liberation for Wesley Willis from what he referred to as his "mental hell rides." He composed and sang passionately, typically using a cheap Technics electronic keyboard. "But one of the few things that liberated him from what he called his mental hell rides was music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
**Support from Indie Musicians** : Indie musicians in Chicago and beyond, including producer Steve Albini and a former bandmate of Billy Corgan from a pre-Smashing Pumpkins band, took notice and supported Wesley. The Canadian industrial metal band Monster Voodoo Machine recorded with him, and Eddie Vedder was charmed by his work. "The indie musicians of Chicago and beyond took note, and they took a liking to him." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
