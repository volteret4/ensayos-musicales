**Louis Louis (1963)** : This track, considered an all-time classic and one of the most famous Garage Rock anthems, was recorded on April 6, 1963, in Portland, Oregon. It cost only $50 to produce and was a cover of an obscure 1957 R&B song by Louisiana singer Richard Berry. The session took place at Northwestern Motion Pictures and Recording, produced by a local DJ. The band blasted it out in a single take, meaning the released version is their only studio performance that day. The arrangement changed the original beat, and singer Joe Eli, wearing heavy braces, sang directly into a single boom microphone, contributing to the unintelligible vocals. The song is famous for a mistake: after the guitar solo, Eli comes in early, stops, and drummer Lynn Easton plays an extra drum fill to cover the error; this mistake is now intentionally replicated in modern performances. It became a regional, then national, hit, and rumors of pornographic lyrics led to its ban from radio stations and entirely in the state of Indiana. "On April 6, 1963, a bunch of dudes from Portland, Oregon spent 50 bucks to record a cover of an obscure 1957 R&B song called Louis Louis, originally done by a Louisiana singer named Richard Berry." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UpWz3X33vSY ← the-kingsmen
