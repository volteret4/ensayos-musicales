## curiosities
**Launch of American Garage Band Era** : The Kingsmen are credited with launching the era of the American garage band. These groups were typically rough, ragged, and occasionally incorporated risqué material into their music. "The Kingsman launched the era of the American garage band, groups that were often rough and ragged, and who got risque from time to time within material." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U
**Singer's Enunciation Issues** : The lead singer of The Kingsmen, Jack Ely, wore heavy braces on his teeth and was forced to sing upwards into a single overhead microphone, which contributed to his inability to enunciate the lyrics clearly. "The singer wore these heavy braces on his teeth, and he was forced to sing up into a single overhead microphone so don't wonder he couldn't enunciate." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U
**Unintelligible Lyrics Controversy** : The unclear lyrics of The Kingsmen's "Louie Louie" led to significant controversy. Some politicians believed the song contained very dirty and dangerous messages, leading the governor of Indiana to ban it. The FBI even launched an 18-month investigation, shadowing the group on tour, but ultimately concluded that the lyrics were "unintelligible at any speed." "Some politicians believed he was singing something very, very dirty and therefore dangerous." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5vKSjpvVQa8
