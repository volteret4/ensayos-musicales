**Heart of Gold (1972) - Critically Controversial Album** : Don McLeese, in a 1992 essay for *Rolling Stone's Illustrated History of Rock and Roll*, referred to this album as "the closest he's been in his career to Schmahl." While popular and beloved in 1972, it has also been considered his "worst album" due to his "experimentation with softer sounds" that "offend[ed] rock critics." **Ohio (1970) – Kent State Protest Song** : Written in May 1970, this song was a direct response to the outrage felt after four protesting students were tragically killed by National Guard troops at Kent State University. The speed of its creation and release was remarkable, as it was on sale and on the radio within a month of the incident. Lyrics include: "Ten soldiers and Nixon coming, we're finally on our own. This summer I hear the drumming for dead in Ohio. Gotta get down to the rain." "Neil Young was so outraged that he wrote the song Ohio, and it was on sale and on the radio within a month." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0ARYt4sYT90 ← neil-young
**Only Love Can Break Your Heart** : This song was included in a Spotify playlist created by Queen Mary University in London, which listed 700 songs most likely to induce chills or goosebumps in people. The research indicated that sadder, slower, less intense, and more instrumental songs generally proved most effective in eliciting this physical response. "This playlist included Only Love Can Break Your Heart by Neil Young." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mTbagQE7akQ ← neil-young

**Godfather of Grunge** : Neil Young is recognized as the "godfather of Grunge," particularly in Seattle where he was a big favorite. His career phase in the 1970s, characterized by "obnoxiously overdriven guitar grinding under a simple melody" and a "calculated sloppiness," held a specific appeal that deeply resonated with and influenced the burgeoning Seattle music scene.
