## curiosities
**Accidental Feedback Discovery** : On October 18, 1964, while at Abbey Road Studios, John Lennon accidentally discovered the controlled feedback sound used on "I Feel Fine." After Paul McCartney plucked a bass string, John leaned his semi-acoustic Gibson guitar against Paul's bass amp and began to walk away, inadvertently causing the guitar to pick up the bass note and create feedback. Recognizing the unique sound, both Lennon and McCartney asked producer George Martin to capture it for their new song. "But then the guitar picked up the sound of that bass note and started feeding back, and the sound was really cool, but a total accident." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w
