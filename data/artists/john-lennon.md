## albums
**Double Fantasy (1980) – Autographed by Lennon on Assassination Day** : John and Yoko's recently released album, which John Lennon autographed for Mark David Chapman around 5 PM on December 8, 1980, before heading to a recording studio. This is believed to be Lennon's last autograph and bears both Chapman's and Lennon's fingerprints. In the chaos following Lennon's assassination five hours later, Chapman left the record in a planter at The Dakota, Lennon's New York apartment. It was found by an unknown individual who kept it under their bed for 18 years. The cover features Lennon's signature in black marker and police evidence markings. It first sold in 1998 for $150,000, then for $520,000, and again in November 2020 for $900,000, making it the most expensive piece of vinyl in the world. "John Lennon was assassinated on December 8, 1980. The shooter, of course, was Mark David Chapman. Before he killed Lennon, Chapman hung around the Dakota, Lennon's New York apartment, with a copy of John and Yoko's just released album, Double Fantasy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tbmssCZ05ro

**"More Popular Than Jesus" Comment (1966)** : In 1966, John Lennon made a controversial remark claiming that The Beatles were "more popular than Jesus." This comment was considered blasphemous by broadcasters Tommy Charles and Doug Layton in Birmingham, Alabama, who subsequently launched a campaign to ban The Beatles, resulting in public record destruction. "And when John Lennon made a flippin' comment in 1966 about the Beatles being more popular than Jesus, two broadcasters, Tommy Charles and Doug Layton from Birmingham, Alabama, started a campaign to ban the Beatles over this blasphemy, and many records were broken and burned." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=41eKVp8JIhU
**Accidental Feedback Discovery** : On October 18, 1964, while at Abbey Road Studios, John Lennon accidentally discovered the controlled feedback sound used on "I Feel Fine." After Paul McCartney plucked a bass string, John leaned his semi-acoustic Gibson guitar against Paul's bass amp and began to walk away, inadvertently causing the guitar to pick up the bass note and create feedback. Recognizing the unique sound, both Lennon and McCartney asked producer George Martin to capture it for their new song. "But then the guitar picked up the sound of that bass note and started feeding back, and the sound was really cool, but a total accident." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w
