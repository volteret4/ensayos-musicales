## songs
**I Shot the Sheriff (1973) – Justice vs. Contraception Debate** : Written around 1972 and released as a single in February 1973, this song appears on the surface to be about a person on the run from the law after shooting a sheriff. Bob Marley himself stated it was a metaphor for justice, and he initially wanted to say "I shot the police" but changed it to "sheriff" to avoid issues with the Jamaican government. However, his former girlfriend, Esther Anderson, maintains it's a protest against contraception, arguing that Marley viewed birth control pills—which she was seeking at the time—as an abomination to his Rastafari beliefs, and that "Sheriff Brown" is a veiled reference to a doctor who "kills a seed before it grows." "Bob sings Sheriff Brown always hated me for what I don't know. Every time I plant a seed, he said, kill it before it grow." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk

**Les Paul Jr. and Reggae Sound** : Bob Marley was a notable fan of Gibson's Les Paul guitars, particularly the Les Paul Jr., which he modified. The unique sound produced by this instrument, tailored to his specifications, played a crucial role in bringing reggae music to a global audience. "Bob Marley was a fan of the Les Paul guitars Gibson eventually started making. His main instrument was a Gibson Les Paul Jr. He modified it a bit, and the sound it produced brought reggae to the world." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=E6cXyaHALBQ
