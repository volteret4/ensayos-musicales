**Security (1982)** : This fourth solo album by Peter Gabriel, released in 1982, was recorded digitally. Initially, when only vinyl was available, it was an AAD (Analog-Analog-Digital) recording due to analog mastering for vinyl. However, with the advent of CDs in 1984, it was re-released as a full DDD (Digital-Digital-Digital) recording, making it possibly the first album to be entirely digital from recording to mastering on CD. "I do know that Peter Gabriel recorded Security, his fourth solo album, digitally. **Sledgehammer (1986)** : This song is associated with a brilliant claymation video from 1986, which was composed of over 7,000 still shots over its four-minute runtime. The video was inspired by the work of Czech surrealist filmmaker Jan Svankmayer and directed by Stephen Johnson in collaboration with Aardman Animations (known for Wallace and Gromit). The production was grueling, requiring Gabriel to lie motionless under a sheet of glass for 16 hours, but it resulted in nine MTV Music Video Awards and is considered one of the greatest videos ever made.
