## albums
**Piss Factory / Hey Joe (1974) – Debut Single** : On June 5, 1974, Patti Smith and her group (consisting of a guitarist and a piano player) recorded a seven-inch single using three hours of studio time. Side one featured a cover of a Jimi Hendrix song, and side two contained an original composition by Smith, which historians believe to be "Piss Factory," depicting her experience working in a toy factory in Pittman, New Jersey. A total of 1600 copies were pressed and distributed to music and bookstores throughout Manhattan, where they quickly sold out. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9T9wi0vEvc

## curiosities
**"Godmother of Punk" Title** : Within a few years of initiating her musical endeavors, Patti Smith earned the esteemed title "the godmother of punk," highlighting her influential role in the nascent punk rock scene. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9T9wi0vEvc
**Claim for First True Punk Rock Record (1974)** : Many music historians credit Patti Smith's 1974 debut single, particularly its original B-side (believed to be "Piss Factory"), as the "first true punk rock record." This significant independent recording directly stemmed from her profound graveside vision in Paris two years prior. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9T9wi0vEvc
**Early Punk Priestess Gig (1976)** : Patti Smith, described as a "punk priestess," played an influential show in New York City a couple of months before the Ramones' pivotal appearance in England. Her performance was an early, significant manifestation of the burgeoning punk rock phenomenon in the US. "Outside of a show, punk priestess Patty Smith had played a couple of months earlier, the Ramones appearance would be England's first proper exposure to US style punk rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qC0Ee4-dpnI
**Last Performer at CBGB (October 15, 2006)** : Patti Smith performed the very last gig at the historic CBGB club in New York on October 15, 2006, marking the venue's final night of operation. "On this final night, October 15th, 2006, Patty Smith performed one last time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Epz5Sn151bk
**Paris Cemetery Epiphany (1972)** : In the summer of 1972, Patti Smith, then an aspiring poet from New York, visited the Père Lachaise Cemetery in Paris. While at the graveside of The Doors singer Jim Morrison, she experienced an epiphany that led her to believe she could be more than just a poet. Jim Morrison "apparently appeared to her" during this transformative moment. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9T9wi0vEvc
**Transition to Musical Career** : Following her pivotal experience in Paris, Patti Smith returned to the United States and began incorporating a guitar player and a piano player into her poetry readings. This shift marked the beginning of her musical journey, leading to an increasing number of gigs and attracting the attention of record companies within a few years. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9T9wi0vEvc
