# artist - Grady Martin

## instruments
- **Fuzz Pedal Design (Maestro FZ-1 Fuzz-Tone)** : Grady Martin, with the help of studio engineer Glenn Snottie and WSMTV engineer Revis Hobbs, designed the circuit for the first-ever guitar fuzz pedal. This prototype, in the form of a foot pedal, was presented to Gibson guitars, who then manufactured it as the Maestro FZ-1 Fuzz-Tone.
- **Six-String Baritone Guitar** : Martin used this instrument when plugging directly into the recording console at Quonset Hut studio in Nashville, leading to the accidental discovery of the fuzz sound on Marty Robbins' "Don't Worry."
- Fuzz Box

## songs
**Don't Worry About Me (1960)** : Recorded with country star Marty Robbins, this song features the first recorded instance of intentional guitar distortion. During the session, an unknown malfunction in Martin's amplifier produced a "fuzzy" sound that he liked and sought to replicate. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
**The Fuzz** : After the success of Marty Robbins' "Don't Worry" and the widespread interest in its unique distorted guitar sound, Grady Martin himself recorded a song titled "The Fuzz." This demonstrated his early recognition and embrace of the sound he accidentally helped create. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg

## curiosities
**Accidental Distortion Discovery** : In late 1960, while playing guitar on Marty Robbins' "Don't Worry" at Quonset Hut studio in Nashville, Grady Martin plugged his six-string baritone guitar directly into the recording console. A faulty transformer in one of the preamps of the mixing desk halfway failed, causing a "fuzzy" or distorted sound. This console had faulty transistors and transformers due to its manufacturer moving operations. Although Robbins disliked the "vulgar" sound, Martin, producer Owen Bradley, and engineer Glenn Snottie found it "cool" and kept it in the song. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
**Collaboration with Gibson Guitars** : Following the hit success of "Don't Worry" and the public's demand to know how its unique sound was produced, Gibson Guitars approached Grady Martin. They requested his assistance in designing a device that could reliably reproduce this fuzzy sound. Martin, alongside Glenn Snottie and Revis Hobbs, developed a circuit that became the prototype for the Maestro FZ-1 Fuzz-Tone, the first commercial distortion pedal. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
**Invention of the Fuzz Box** : Following the accidental discovery during the 1960 Marty Robbins session, Martin and his collaborators figured out how to reproduce the distorted sound on command. They created an electronic circuit activated by a foot pedal, which they called a "fuzz box". This invention led to the commercial release of the Maestro Fuzz Tone FZ1 and fundamentally changed the powerful, "menacing" nature of the electric guitar. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
