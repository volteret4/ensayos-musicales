## curiosities
**Bridge to Geffen Records** : Rick Arboit, working at Network Records, received Sloan's tape from Cam Carpenter and liked it, but his hands were tied regarding signing them to MCA. He then connected them to Todd Sullivan at Geffen, indirectly facilitating Sloan's signing to a major label. "He told his friend Rick Arboit who worked at Network to come, hey, check out this band. He liked our tape. And his hands were tied. I don't think he could do anything in Canada at MCA for us." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
**Early Career in Thrush Hermit** : Joel Plaskett (referred to as "Joe Plasket") was a young member of the Halifax band Thrush Hermit, around 16 years old, when they were supported by Sloan through record releases on Murder Records and touring opportunities. "Thrusher mitt. Who gives you Joe Plasket now. Those guys were kids. They were 16 or whatever." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
**Geffen A&R Who Signed Sloan** : Todd Sullivan, an A&R at Geffen Records, was introduced to Sloan's music by Rick Arboit. He particularly loved their song "Underwhelmed" and, after seeing them perform, offered them a record deal with Geffen. He later became a mentee of Tom Zutat, founder of The Enclave. "He set it to a friend of his Todd Sullivanic Geffen who loved the song underwhelmed and then came to see his play and then he was like, do you guys want to sign to Geffen?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
**Key A&R in Sloan's Discovery** : Cam Carpenter, an A&R representative at MCA Canada (before it became Universal), was an initial point of contact for Sloan. He received their demo tape, saw them play, and recommended them to Rick Arboit, thereby initiating the sequence of events that led to Sloan's major label deal. "I found the name of this guy, Cam Carpenter, who used to do ANR at MCA here in Canada back before it was universal." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
**Young Halifax Talent Supported by Sloan** : Thrush Hermit was a band from the Halifax scene, featuring members like a young Joel Plaskett (around 16 years old). Sloan provided them with an opportunity to release a record through Murder Records and brought them on tour, offering support to emerging talent. "Thrusher mitt. Who gives you Joe Plasket now. Those guys were kids. They were 16 or whatever. And we, whatever, I don't know how much we had to do with their thing. But they were just young kids and we gave them an opportunity maybe to put out a record that they might not otherwise had." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
