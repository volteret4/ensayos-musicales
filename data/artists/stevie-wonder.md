# artist - Stevie Wonder

## songs
**Uptight (Everything's All Right) (1966)** : This 1966 hit was the basis for Stevie Wonder's complaint against Oasis's song "Step Out" from the *What's the Story Morning Glory* album, as it sounded "way too much like" his track. Wonder "won" the dispute, resulting in "Step Out" being removed from all subsequent editions of the Oasis album and Noel Gallagher's royalties being reduced. "This was also taken by Stevie Wonder over a song called Step Out from What's the Story Morning Glory album which sounded way too much life as 1966 hit Uptight Everything's All Right." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rfObVld9rIE
**Uptight Everything's All Right (1966)** : This song was a top three hit in 1966 and was co-written by Sylvia Moe and Henry Cosby, a staff producer at Motown. Its chorus later became the subject of a lawsuit against Oasis for its similarity to their song "Step Out." "In 1966, Stevie had a top three hit with a song called Uptight Everything's All Right." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L09R2evjyMc

## curiosities
**Credit for Oasis's "Step Out"** : Following a court case over the similarities between his 1966 hit "Uptight Everything's All Right" and Oasis's "Step Out," Stevie Wonder, along with Sylvia Moe and Henry Cosby, received co-writing credits for "Step Out" and was awarded at least a quarter of the proceeds from any version of the song by Oasis.
