**Reflektor (2013) – Fourth Album** : Released on October 28th, 2013, this album was profoundly influenced by the band's extensive experiences in Haiti, where they absorbed Rara music, and by their studies of the Brazilian movie "Black Orpheus" and essays by Danish philosopher Soren Kierkegaard. The album's cover artwork features Rodin's famous sculpture of Orpheus and Eurydice, tying into its thematic undercurrents. **The Suburbs (2010) – Third Album** : Released on August 2nd, 2010, this album is a semi-autobiographical and semi-apocalyptic exploration of the gated, planned community north of Houston, Woodlands, Texas, where Win and William Butler grew up. Recording took place in three locations: Win and Regine Butler's house in Montreal, a church they had purchased in Farnham, Quebec, and in New York. The album debuted at number one in Canada, the US, the UK, Ireland, Norway, Portugal, and Belgium, and reached the top 10 in Australia, Denmark, Germany, Mexico, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, the Netherlands, Finland, and New Zealand. It achieved platinum status in Canada and the UK, and gold in the US. This album made history by winning Album of the Year at the Grammy Awards, Juno Awards, Brit Awards (Best International Album), and the Polaris Music Prize, making it the first album to achieve such a comprehensive sweep of major awards. "The Suburbs probably sold a little more than that." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ir2FfLQJNtY

**Recording Process for "The Suburbs" Album** : To infuse their digital releases with the distinctive sound of vinyl for "The Suburbs," Arcade Fire recorded each song onto old-school 24-track magnetic tape through a 1940s console, which relied on vacuum tubes rather than transistors or computer chips. After mixing, each song was pressed onto a 12-inch lacquer or acetate, and then this physical recording was digitized. This technique, while aiming for an "archive of a physical thing," sometimes led to a loss of low-end frequencies, resulting in a slightly shrill or mid-rangey sound. "Win says the digital is the archive of this physical thing that exists in the world." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ir2FfLQJNtY
