**Funeral (2004) - Breakthrough Debut Album** : Recorded quickly in about a week in the summer of 2003 at Hotel to Tango studio in Montreal and finished by early 2004, this album was released on September 14, 2004. It was titled "Funeral" due to the deaths of several band members' relatives (grandfathers, grandmothers, aunts) during its creation. The album received immediate critical acclaim and attention, leading to club shows, festival appearances globally, a Time Magazine cover, and friendships with figures like David Bowie. It became the best-selling album ever for Merge Records at the time, garnering three Grammy and three Brit Award nominations, and a Juno for Songwriters of the Year, making it the most significant debut album of the decade. "The band called it funeral, because so many of the band's relatives died during its making." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q_c-m_NDznw

**Neon Bible (2007) - Critically Acclaimed Sophomore Album** : Recorded mostly in the band's self-purchased church headquarters in Farnham, Quebec, with some sessions in New York, this album was released on March 7, 2007. It explored themes of dissonance and dread, influenced by Win Butler's outsider perspective on the US while living in Canada. *Neon Bible* debuted at No. 1 in Canada and Ireland, No. 2 in the US and UK, No. 7 in Australia, and No. 9 in France. It garnered "unanimously ecstatic" reviews (except for Rolling Stone, which felt "a little cold"), became Merge Records' best-selling album, was a finalist for the 2007 Polaris Music Prize, received a Grammy nomination for Best Alternative Album (losing to The White Stripes' *Icky Thump*), and won a Juno in the same category. "They called this album Neon Bible." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q_c-m_NDznw
**Neon Bible (2007) – Second Album** : This album was a finalist for the 2007 Polaris Music Prize and received a nomination for Best Alternative Album at the Grammys, where it lost to "Icky Thump" by The White Stripes. **Reflektor (2013) – Fourth Album** : Released on October 28th, 2013, this album was profoundly influenced by the band's extensive experiences in Haiti, where they absorbed Rara music, and by their studies of the Brazilian movie "Black Orpheus" and essays by Danish philosopher Soren Kierkegaard. The album's cover artwork features Rodin's famous sculpture of Orpheus and Eurydice, tying into its thematic undercurrents. **The Suburbs (2010) – Third Album** : Released on August 2nd, 2010, this album is a semi-autobiographical and semi-apocalyptic exploration of the gated, planned community north of Houston, Woodlands, Texas, where Win and William Butler grew up. Recording took place in three locations: Win and Regine Butler's house in Montreal, a church they had purchased in Farnham, Quebec, and in New York. The album debuted at number one in Canada, the US, the UK, Ireland, Norway, Portugal, and Belgium, and reached the top 10 in Australia, Denmark, Germany, Mexico, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, the Netherlands, Finland, and New Zealand. It achieved platinum status in Canada and the UK, and gold in the US. This album made history by winning Album of the Year at the Grammy Awards, Juno Awards, Brit Awards (Best International Album), and the Polaris Music Prize, making it the first album to achieve such a comprehensive sweep of major awards. "The Suburbs probably sold a little more than that." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ir2FfLQJNtY
