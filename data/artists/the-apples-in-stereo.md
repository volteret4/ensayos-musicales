## curiosities
**Band Decision on Licensing** : Despite their punk rock purist ethos, the $18,000 licensing offer for "Strawberry Fire" was more than the band could make in a year. When Robert and Hillary consulted the rest of the band, there was no hesitation, and the unanimous decision was to accept the money. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sfie4YYncxk
**Early Financial Struggles** : As an indie band from Denver active since 1992, The Apples in Stereo were highly regarded within the indie community but struggled with album sales, typically moving only about 20,000 copies per record. To make ends meet, they toured frequently, playing around 100 shows a year, with gigs earning between $250 and $2,500. When not on the road, members took regular jobs, and t-shirt sales were minimal, bringing in around $100 per show. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sfie4YYncxk
**Ethical Dilemma over Licensing** : The $18,000 offer for "Strawberry Fire" created an "existential crisis" for The Apples in Stereo, who had always adhered to the purist ethos of punk rock. They worried about compromising their artistic principles and disappointing fans who expected them to maintain that stance. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sfie4YYncxk
**Financial Crisis of 1999** : In the summer of 1999, the band's principals, Robert Schneider and Hillary Sydney (both 30), faced a severe financial bind. Both were working telemarketing jobs, and Hillary was eight months pregnant with their first child, which would prevent them from touring—a vital income source. They were concerned about affording their $825 monthly rent. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sfie4YYncxk
**Financial Rescue via Licensing** : Accepting the $18,000 deal to license "Strawberry Fire" for a Sony TV commercial ultimately rescued The Apples in Stereo from a very difficult financial situation, proving to be a pivotal moment for the indie band. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sfie4YYncxk
**Sony TV Ad Offer** : During their financial crisis in 1999, Robert Schneider and Hillary Sydney received a call from a friend at the ad agency Young and Rubikam in New York. The agency was working on a campaign for Sony televisions and wanted to use their song "Strawberry Fire," offering $18,000 for the license. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sfie4YYncxk
