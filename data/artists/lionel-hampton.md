**Flying Home (1939)** : Hampton co-wrote this signature song of the Benny Goodman Sextet after humming the riff on a flight from LA to Atlantic City. After leaving Goodman's band a couple of years later, "Flying Home" became the signature song for his own big band, and he recorded it multiple times, significantly influencing the nascent Jump band style. "Goodman asked, what's that you're singing? And Hampton said, I don't know, we can call it Flying Home I guess." ← Episode 1： ＂Flying Home＂, Benny Goodman, Charlie Christian, and Lionel Hampton | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RmfeJYl84Qs
**Flying Home (1942) - Decker Version** : Lionel Hampton recorded his most famous version of "Flying Home" for Decker in 1942, featuring Illinois Jacket on saxophone, labeled as an instrumental "Fox Strott." This recording introduced a whole new sound to listeners, particularly Jacket's distinctive "hunk and scrunk" tenor sax playing, which defined the emerging genre of rhythm and blues. "but the most famous example is the version he recorded in 1942 for Decker, with instrumental Fox Strott on the label." ← Episode 1： ＂Flying Home＂, Benny Goodman, Charlie Christian, and Lionel Hampton | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RmfeJYl84Qs
**Hey Baba Reba** : Lionel Hampton recorded "Hey Baba Reba," which was a re-recording of Helen Humes' song "Be-Baba-Leba." This version became a massive R&B hit and is also widely considered one of the inspirations behind the term "bebop" being applied to a style of music. "The song has been re-recorded by Lionel Hampton as Hey Baba Viba, which had been a massive R&B hit, and the song is also generally considered one of the inspirations behind the term Bbapp, being applied to the style of music." ← Episode 41： ＂Be-Bop-A-Lula＂ by Gene Vincent and the Bluecaps | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tYBQ5F7O3G0
**Memories of You (1930)** : Lionel Hampton is believed to have made the first recorded jazz appearance of the vibraphone on this Louis Armstrong recording. Prior to this, the vibraphone was only used as a novelty instrument, often for radio intermission signals. "He appears to have been the first person to use the vibraphone on Air Jazz record, on a recording by Louis Armstrong of the Song Memories of You from 1930." ← Episode 1： ＂Flying Home＂, Benny Goodman, Charlie Christian, and Lionel Hampton | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RmfeJYl84Qs

## curiosities
**Early Adoption of Precision Bass** : Lionel Hampton's band is believed to be the first to incorporate the Fender Precision Bass into their lineup, with Monk Montgomery as their bassist. This marked a significant early adoption of the electric bass in a professional setting. "As far as we can tell, Lionel Hampton's band was the first to incorporate the P-Base. It appears that his guy was named Monk Montgomery." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=E6cXyaHALBQ ← lionel-hampton
**Early Afterbeat Drumming Style** : In his autobiography, Lionel Hampton described his drumming style before switching to the vibraphone, stating he was already playing with a "heavy afterbeat," which he identified as the "rock and roll beat" that wouldn't become popular until the 1950s. He aimed for people to dance, have a good time, and clap their hands to his drumming, but this style was never recorded. "I was already playing with a heavy afterbeat, getting that rock and roll beat that wouldn't even get popular until the 1950s." ← Episode 2： ＂Roll Em Pete＂ by Big Joe Turner | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=thpMb1A_fAs
**Formed Own Big Band** : A couple of years after the 1939 "Flying Home" recording, Lionel Hampton left Benny Goodman's band to form his own big band. This band became a pioneer in "Jump band music," which was rooted in swing but emphasized riffs and amplified instruments, eventually evolving into rhythm and blues. "A couple of years later, Lionel Hampton left Goodman's band to form his own big band and flying home became their signature song." ← Episode 1： ＂Flying Home＂, Benny Goodman, Charlie Christian, and Lionel Hampton | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RmfeJYl84Qs
**Influence on Early R&B** : Lionel Hampton's band was one of the significant influences on early R&B, alongside Count Basie's, contributing to the blend of jump band and classy jazz that characterized the genre. "The Count Basie band was, like Lionel Hamptons, one of the bands that had most influenced early R&B..." ← Episode 32： ＂I Got A Woman＂ by Ray Charles | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0oeI9f2x_fo
**Influence on James Brown's Funk** : Lionel Hampton's style of jazz and swing, along with Count Basie's, heavily influenced James Brown's much later funk recordings. Brown's music is seen as the logical progression of the style these musicians developed. "That style of music wouldn't show up in his earlier records, which were mostly fairly standard vocal group R&B, but if you listen to his much later funk recordings, they owe a lot to Basie and Lionel Hampton." ← Episode 39： ＂Please Please Please＂ by James Brown and the Famous Flames | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DSkNVh93tAs
**Influence on Jump Band Music** : Lionel Hampton's band was a significant influence on the development of jump band music, the style pioneered by Louis Jordan and His Tympany Five. "it was a style that owed a lot to Lionel Hampton's band" ← Episode 4： ＂Choo Choo Ch'Boogie＂ by Louis Jordan | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DFZnwvsXGJM

**Rock Music Pioneer Recognition** : In the late 1940s and early 1950s, as big bands lost popularity, Hampton became a great B-bop musician, but he was also recognized by rock musicians as a pioneer. He appeared in the 1957 Alan Fried film "Mr. Rock and Roll" as the only jazz musician alongside rock and roll stars like Little Richard and Clyde McFatter. "But he was also recognised by the rock musicians as a pioneer." ← Episode 1： ＂Flying Home＂, Benny Goodman, Charlie Christian, and Lionel Hampton | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RmfeJYl84Qs
**Trained as Drummer** : Before becoming a vibraphone player, Hampton trained as a drummer and was often billed as "the fastest drummer in the world." His unique melodic sensibility allowed him to become the premier soloist on the new instrument, the vibraphone. "Hampton had trained us at rumour before becoming a vibraphone player and was often billed as the fastest drummer in the world." ← Episode 1： ＂Flying Home＂, Benny Goodman, Charlie Christian, and Lionel Hampton | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RmfeJYl84Qs
**Vibraphone Pioneer** : Lionel Hampton was a virtuoso vibraphone player who "more or less defined how that instrument was incorporated into jazz," and appears to be the first person to use it on a recorded jazz track (Louis Armstrong's "Memories of You" in 1930). At the time, the vibraphone was so new its name wasn't settled, and Hampton played an instrument branded "Vibrahap," which he called it for life. "He was a virtuoso vibraphoneist who more or less defined how that instrument was incorporated into jazz." ← Episode 1： ＂Flying Home＂, Benny Goodman, Charlie Christian, and Lionel Hampton | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RmfeJYl84Qs
