## curiosities
**Contribution to New Wave** : The Talking Heads, with former Modern Lovers keyboardist Jerry Harrison, became a major component of the American New Wave era. This demonstrates the later success of musicians who emerged from the less-known scene. "And both the talking heads and the cars would eventually become major components of the American New Wave era." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4 ← the-talking-heads
**Early CBGB Scene Involvement** : The Talking Heads were one of the bands, along with Blondie, Suicide, and The Heartbreakers, that contributed to CBGB becoming the central hub for the new music scene that was developing. Tina Weymouth, their bass player, was an early observer of The Ramones' initial performances. "CBGB was becoming the center of a new scene featuring bands with names like Blondie and Suicide and The Talking Heds and a television spin-off band called The Heartbreakers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4 ← the-talking-heads
**Early Sire Records Signing** : The Talking Heads were among the new artists signed by Seymour Stein to Sire Records, who loved the emerging post-punk music. Sire Records used the term "New Wave" to market these bands in America, intentionally avoiding the "punk" label which carried too much baggage in North America. "He signed the Talking Hands." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U ← the-talking-heads
**Example of American New Wave (1979)** : Their music from 1979 is presented as a prime example of American New Wave, distinguishing it slightly from its British counterpart. "Talking Hits from 1979 and an example of American New Wave." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U ← the-talking-heads
**Witness to Ramones' Early Days** : Tina Weymouth, the bass player for The Talking Heads, recalled the early arguments backstage at CBGB regarding The Ramones' short performance lengths, where they would play an hour's worth of material in 45 minutes. "One person who remembers those early days was Tina Waymof, the bass player for a future CBGB band called The Talking Heds." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4 ← the-talking-heads
