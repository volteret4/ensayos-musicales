## albums
**Brother to Brother (1978)** : Released in August 1978, the album "Brother to Brother" reached number one in Canada and number four on the Billboard Hot 100 in the US. The album featured the hit single "I Just Wanna Stop," written by Gino's brother, Ross Vannelli, and was nominated for a Grammy for Pop Male Vocal Performance of the Year. "That is from the album Brother to Brother written by Gina's brother Ross. It was released in August 1978. It went to number one in Canada. Number four on the Billboard Hot 100 in the US. **I Just Wanna Stop (1978) - Montreal Reference** : In 1978, Gino Vannelli's inclusion of a reference to Montreal in the lyrics of "I Just Wanna Stop" was considered a notable and somewhat unusual act, as it was common wisdom in the industry to avoid Canadian geographical references in songs aimed at international markets. "it was kind of a big deal in 1978 that Vannelly references Montreal. He just said, whatever, it's where I'm from, but references to Canadian cities. That was hardly common." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 45 - Oh, Canada! Canada's Rock Music Industry in the 70s | https://www.ftr70.com/ ← gino-vannelli
**I Just Wanna Stop (1978) - Production Quality** : Gino Vannelli's first hit single, "I Just Wanna Stop," released in 1978, was noted for its very careful and professional production, drawing comparisons to the sophisticated sound of Steely Dan. "Gina Vannelly's first hit single, I just want to stop, is very carefully and professionally produced.
