## songs
**I Think I Love You (1970)** : David Cassidy initially resisted singing this song, preferring to pursue acting or rock music. However, producer West Farrell insisted on bubblegum records. Radio stations were reluctant to play it until a station in Iowa gave it a chance, after which it quickly became a number one hit. The song reached number one on November 21, 1970, shortly after *The Partridge Family* TV show debuted, and notably knocked "I'll Be There" by The Jackson 5 off the top spot. It was David Cassidy's first and only number one hit, and it was also the last song he performed live in his youth. "I think I love you. **Rolling Stone Interview and Image Control (1972)** : In a 1972 interview with Robin Green for *Rolling Stone*, Cassidy was initially hostile, fearing ridicule. He discussed his image and the expectations placed upon him two years into playing Keith Partridge, admitting his resistance to cutting bubblegum records. To challenge his bubblegum image and assert his desire to be a rock star, he posed nude for an Annie Leibovitz photo shoot for the magazine's cover. "He talked about his image and what was expected of him by that time two years into playing Keith Partridge. He told the interviewer Robin Green.
