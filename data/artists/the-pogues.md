## albums
**If I Should Fall From Grace With God (1988)** : The sessions for this album, produced by Steve Lillywhite, included early attempts to record "Fairy Tale of New York" with Shane McGowan singing both male and female parts. Lillywhite later brought the rough cut to his wife, Kirsty MacColl, for her to record the female vocals. "Those sessions were part of what became the Pogs' third album, which was called If I Should Fall From Grace With God." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k9ok8M31-38

## songs
**Fairy Tale of New York (1987)** : Despite becoming a Christmas favorite, the song is not traditionally Christmassy, focusing instead on a fight between a drunken couple on Christmas Eve. It took over two years to write and record, starting as a piece by banjo player Jim Finer in 1985. "Not very Christmassy in the traditional sense, but still one of the biggest alt rock Christmas songs ever, if not of all time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k9ok8M31-38
**Fairy Tale of New York - Inspiration and Concept Shift** : Returning to England in March 1986, the song's concept changed from a sailor to a drunken couple's Christmas Eve fight. Part of the inspiration came from a book Shane McGowan read on tour, "A Fairy Tale of New York," about an Irish man returning to New York after studying in Ireland. "Part of the inspiration came from a book that Shane had read on tour called A Fairy Tale of New York, which dealt with an Irish dude returning to New York after going to school in Ireland." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k9ok8M31-38
**Fairy Tale of New York - Kirsty MacColl's Contribution (1987)** : In March 1987, Shane McGowan recorded both male and female parts for "Fairy Tale of New York" as a demo during sessions for "If I Should Fall From Grace With God." Producer Steve Lillywhite, who had worked with U2, took this rough cut to his wife, singer Kirsty MacColl, in August 1987. She recorded her parts in the studio alone, surprising the band with the results, and Shane then re-recorded his parts to complement hers. "Steve brought her into the studio one night to do her bits, and then she went home to look after the kids." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k9ok8M31-38
**Fairy Tale of New York - Long Development** : Jim Finer first started developing the song in 1985, showing it to Shane McGowan. For the next two years, they struggled with the lyrics, which initially centered on a lonely sailor, a concept deemed "crap." The band blew off recording it for Christmas 1985 and went on tour in America. "It first started to come together as a piece sometime in 1985 by Jim Feiner, the Pogs Banjo player." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k9ok8M31-38
**Fairy Tale of New York - Separate Vocal Recordings** : A notable curiosity of "Fairy Tale of New York" is that, despite being a duet, Shane McGowan and Kirsty MacColl never recorded their parts together in the same studio. MacColl recorded her vocals separately based on Shane's demo, and then Shane adjusted his vocals. "As you listen to this, remember that although the song was written as a duet and sung as a duet, Shane and Kirsty were never ever in the same studio together." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k9ok8M31-38
**Fairy Tale of New York - Vocalist Challenges** : The Pogues faced difficulty finding a suitable female vocalist for the duet. It was initially written for bass player Cait O'Riordan, but she left the band after running off with Elvis Costello, shelving the song for Christmas 1986. "Besides, the Pogs really couldn't find anybody they liked to sing the female part." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k9ok8M31-38
**Fairytale of New York (1987)** : Kirsty MacColl provided the female vocals for this song, which is frequently heard around Christmas time. "She's the female voice that she heard on the Pogues song Fairy Tailed New York, the one that you hear around Christmas all the time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZcYKZ5ovoy4
**Fairytale of New York (Year Unspecified)** : Kirsty MacColl duets with Shane MacGowan on this famous song. "She's the woman who duets with Shane McGowan on the Pog's Fairy Tale of New York." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZhzjZixCcK8

## curiosities
**Cait O'Riordan's Departure** : Cait O'Riordan, the Pogues' bass player for whom the female part of "Fairy Tale of New York" was originally written, left the band after she "had run off with Elvis Castell." This departure forced the song to be shelved for Christmas 1986. "It had been written for Cater Reardon, a Pogs bass player, but she had run off with Elvis Castell, so that shelled the song for Christmas 1986." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k9ok8M31-38
**Chris Berkett's Work** : Chris Berkett worked on records for The Pogues, which sold tens of millions of copies. "talking heads, the poges, Shunato Connor, Bob Geldoff, Quincy Jones, Buffy St. Marie, Sting Peter Gabriel and many others." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mjQBaX_3oL4
**Collaboration with Steve Lillywhite** : The Pogues have worked with Steve Lillywhite, a very famous record producer. "He's also worked with Morrissey and The Pogues." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZcYKZ5ovoy4
**Difficult Live Introduction** : The speaker recounted a challenging experience trying to introduce The Pogues, specifically Shane MacGowan. He couldn't be found initially because he was out drinking. When located, he was hustled onto the stage to sing before he passed out, preventing the speaker from making the introduction. The speaker could smell MacGowan from 15 feet away, but noted that he still "knew all the words" once he got to the microphone. "I never got to introduce the band because they wanted to get Shane in front of a mic as quickly as they possibly could. I got to within 15 feet of him and I could smell him. I really could." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4xxgPX-aR90
**Future Episode Plans** : The speaker plans to create an episode on Shane MacGowan in the new year for 2024, highlighting The Pogues' significant role in integrating Irish Celtic folk tradition into punk rock. "I'm going to have to do something on Shane in the new year because the Pogs were so important when it comes to bringing that Irish Celtic folk tradition into punk rock and there's a great story there, a really great story." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4xxgPX-aR90
**Steve Lillywhite's Role** : Producer Steve Lillywhite, known for his work on U2's first three albums, played a crucial role in the development of "Fairy Tale of New York." He produced The Pogues' third album, "If I Should Fall From Grace With God," and, being married to Kirsty MacColl, arranged for her to record the female vocal parts. "The producer was a guy named Steve Lillie White. He had helped you too with their first three albums." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k9ok8M31-38
