## albums
**Buckingham Nicks (1973) - Duo Album** : This album was recorded in Sound City Studios the year before Mick Fleetwood discovered Lindsey Buckingham and Stevie Nicks. Despite being recorded with "the cream of LA session players," the album "went nowhere," failing to chart or generate radio hits, though it contained sturdy songs like Nicks-penned "Crying in the Night." ## songs
**Frozen Love (1973)** : This track from the *Buckingham Nicks* album was recorded by the duo and played for Mick Fleetwood by Sound City house engineer Keith Olson. Fleetwood was so impressed by "Frozen Love" that it led to him offering Lindsey Buckingham a position in Fleetwood Mac. "Keith Olson played him a track he had recorded with the duo called Frozen Love." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Thinking About Tomorrow Edition Part 1 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4ef18e6dd12efb02c429

The lyrics explicitly addressed his turbulent relationship with Stevie Nicks during the band's internal conflicts. **Package Deal Requirement - Joining Fleetwood Mac** : When Mick Fleetwood offered Lindsey Buckingham the lead guitarist job for Fleetwood Mac in late 1974, Buckingham insisted that he and Stevie Nicks were a "package deal," meaning both had to be hired. **Pre-Fleetwood Mac Performance - Fritz** : Lindsey Buckingham had been performing with Stevie Nicks since the late 1960s, with Nicks singing lead in his psychedelic rock band called Fritz. This early collaboration established their musical partnership before they formed Buckingham Nicks and later joined Fleetwood Mac. **Romantic and Musical Partners - Buckingham Nicks Duo** : Before joining Fleetwood Mac, Lindsey Buckingham and Stevie Nicks were both romantic and musical partners, performing together since the late 60s and releasing the album *Buckingham Nicks* in 1973. Their relationship dynamics would later become a significant part of Fleetwood Mac's drama.
