## albums
**Definitely Maybe (1994) – Debut Album** : The artwork for Oasis's debut album, released in May 1994, was designed by Brian Cannon with photography by Michael Spencer Jones. It features the five band members in the front room of rhythm guitarist Paul "Bonehead" Arthurs' home in Didsbury, Manchester, an idea inspired by a Beatles compilation. The meticulously staged shot includes personal items, carefully chosen props, and features the album title in Cannon's own handwriting. "Artwork was important to Oasis from the very beginning." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tbKuJR0cPs

## curiosities
**"Definitely Maybe" Album Art Inspiration** : The photoshoot for Oasis's "Definitely Maybe" album drew inspiration from the back cover of The Beatles' 1966 compilation, "A Collection of Beatles Oldies." This choice was a compromise with Noel Gallagher's initial idea, which was a shot of The Beatles in Japan all sitting around a table, a concept deemed uninteresting by photographer Michael Spencer Jones. "The inspiration was another album, specifically the back cover of a 1966 Beatles compilation entitled A Collection of Beatles Oldies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tbKuJR0cPs
**"Definitely Maybe" Drink Prop** : During the "Definitely Maybe" photoshoot, empty cans of Red Stripe beer were initially present but were replaced by glasses of a diluted British soft drink called Rabena. This substitution was made because real red wine tends to photograph as black, and the diluted Rabena was specifically prepared to achieve the visual effect of red wine. "It's a British soft drink called Rabina that was diluted just enough to look like red wine because if you photograph real wine, it tends to look black in the photographs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tbKuJR0cPs
**"Definitely Maybe" Photoshoot Location** : The front cover photograph for Oasis's 1994 debut album, "Definitely Maybe," was taken in the living room of rhythm guitarist Paul "Bonehead" Arthurs' home in Didsbury, Manchester. The room was much smaller and narrower than it appears in the final image, necessitating photographer Michael Spencer Jones to use a wide-angle lens to include all band members. "The setting was the front room of the home of rhythm guitars Paul Bohnhead Arthur's." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tbKuJR0cPs
**"Definitely Maybe" Title Lettering** : The lettering for the album title "Definitely Maybe" on the cover is a unique detail, as it is not a commercially available font. Instead, it features the actual handwriting of the album's art director, Brian Cannon. "That's not a special font or anything. That's Brian Cannon's actual handwriting." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tbKuJR0cPs
**Album Artwork Collaboration (1993-1998)** : Noel Gallagher first met designer Brian Cannon about a year before Oasis's debut album and was impressed by his work for The Verve, bonding over Adidas running shoe designs. Noel specifically requested Cannon to design Oasis's artwork once they were signed, a collaboration that continued for the next four years, covering their early albums and singles. "Telling Brian, when we get signed, I want you to do our artwork." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tbKuJR0cPs
**Liam Gallagher's Mummy Pose** : On the cover of Oasis's "Definitely Maybe" album, Liam Gallagher is seen lying on the floor in a pose reminiscent of an Egyptian mummy. This specific pose was inspired by photographer Michael Spencer Jones's visit to the Egyptology collection at the Manchester Science Museum. Additionally, the pose helped to conceal the rough, recently stripped floors of Bonehead's living room. "If you want to know why Liam is lying on the floor in the position of an Egyptian mummy, that's why." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tbKuJR0cPs
**Longevity Beyond the Seven-Year Cycle** : Oasis is mentioned as an example of a band that has successfully navigated and survived beyond the common seven-year lifecycle, suggesting an exceptional ability to overcome internal conflicts and maintain relevance. "Groups that have survived multiple seven-year cycles. You too, the stones, the oasis, green day, food fighters." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Props and Symbolism on "Definitely Maybe" Cover** : The "Definitely Maybe" album cover is rich with personal objects and symbolic elements chosen by the band members, at the request of photographer Michael Spencer Jones. These include a pink flamingo and an Epiphone Riviera guitar belonging to Bonehead, a pack of Benson and Hedges cigarettes, and portraits of Manchester City football star Rodney Marsh and Manchester United's George Best (Bonehead's favorite). Burt Bacharach's picture was Noel's contribution, and a TV plays "The Good, the Bad and the Ugly," Noel's favorite movie, while a blow-up globe brought by sound guy Mark Coyle and roadie Phil Smith signaled aspirations for "world domination." "Spencer asked everyone to bring personal objects with them, things that meant something to them." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tbKuJR0cPs
