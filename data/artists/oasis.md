**Acquiesce (1995) - B-Side with a Breakthrough Story** : Originally a B-side to "Some Might Say," this song, like "Supersonic," came together at the very last minute. In February 1995, Noel Gallagher was traveling by train to Loco studio in Wales when the train broke down in the Severant Tunnel, leaving him stuck for four hours. He overheard a passenger use the word "Aquies" during a phone call, liked the sound, and began strumming the opening chords, working out 90% of the arrangement despite a fellow passenger's protest to stop playing. Liam Gallagher sang the verses, and Noel sang the chorus because Liam, recovering from a throat infection after dates in Japan, couldn't hit the high notes. Alan McGee was so impressed he wanted it as the single, but Noel insisted on "Some Might Say." It eventually gained recognition, promoted as the single for the 1998 compilation "The Masterplan" and also appearing on "Stop the Clocks." **Don't Look Back in Anger (1996) - A Second British National Anthem** : The fifth single from "Morning Glory," this was the first Oasis single sung exclusively by Noel Gallagher. He wrote it on Tuesday, April 18, 1995, on a rainy night in Paris after returning from a strip club, while Oasis was opening for The Verve. Inspiration came from David Bowie's "All the Young Dudes" (performed by Mott the Hoople) and a bootlegged tape of John Lennon's memoirs (stolen after his murder on December 8, 1980), particularly Lennon's quote about "trying to start a revolution from me bed." The piano sounds also share similarities with Lennon's "Imagine" and "Watching the Wheels." Six days later, Liam Gallagher misheard Noel singing "Sally can wait" during a soundcheck for their first arena headlining gig in Sheffield; Noel, not having specific lyrics, then finished the song in the dressing room with the iconic "don't look back and anger." Noel performed it live acoustically hours later. "Sally" is ambiguous, possibly a nod to The Stone Roses' "Sally Cinnamon."
