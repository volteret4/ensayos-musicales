**Career as Record Producer (Post-1958)** : While Elvis was serving in the army, Scotty Moore transitioned into record production. He produced "Tragedy" for Thomas Wayne (Thomas Wayne Perkins), which became a pop hit, reaching number five on the charts. "Scotty became a record producer, producing the record tragedy for Thomas Wayne." ← Episode 72： ＂Trouble＂ by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=psIgGqkQhZI
**Conditions for Rejoining Elvis (1958)** : After initially resigning, Scotty Moore was offered his job back by Elvis, who missed their stage presence. Moore's new terms included a per diem, ensuring payment regardless of performances. However, his role was diminished in the studio, where he became a regular rhythm guitarist while Hank Garland took over as the lead player on Elvis's records. "They'd be getting paid a per DM whether or not they were performing, which was something." ← Episode 72： ＂Trouble＂ by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=psIgGqkQhZI

**Resignation from Elvis's Band (September 1957)** : Scotty Moore, alongside Bill Black, resigned from Elvis's band in September 1957 due to financial grievances and frustration with Colonel Parker's control. They were placed on a salary rather than a split of earnings, suffered from a drastically cut concert schedule (only 14 shows that year), and felt unable to communicate directly with Elvis due to Parker's interference. "In September, Scotty Moore and Bill Black had written to Elvis, resigning from his band." ← Episode 72： ＂Trouble＂ by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=psIgGqkQhZI
**Use of Echo and Reverb** : Scotty Moore, a guitarist in Elvis Presley's band, was known for using echo and reverb in his sound, an element admired by East Bay Ray of the Dead Kennedys. "Another hero was Scotty Moore, who played an Elvis Presley's band, and Scotty liked to use echo and reverb in his sound, something that Ray really liked." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pOeChe_D1Ag ← scotty-moore
**Work for Sam Phillips (Post-1958)** : After his success as a producer, Scotty Moore took a job working for Sam Phillips. He continued this employment for several years, even after rejoining Elvis's studio band following Elvis's return from military service. "Scotty took a job working for Sam Phillips, and when Elvis got out of the army and Scotty rejoined him, he continued working for Phillips for a number of years." ← Episode 72： ＂Trouble＂ by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=psIgGqkQhZI
