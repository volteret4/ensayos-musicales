## curiosities
**ABBA Voyage Residency** : In London, ABBA launched "ABBA Voyage," a residency in a purpose-built theater that transports audiences back to the band's peak in the late 1970s and early 1980s. Uniquely, all members of ABBA are still alive but have no interest in performing live. The show utilizes motion capture suits and sophisticated projection technology to create lifelike "Abbatars" on stage. "All the members of the band are still alive but have no interest in performing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GYUuTbOvyak
**Outrageous Stage Costumes Designed as Tax Deductions Under Swedish Law** : ABBA's deliberately extravagant and unwearable stage outfits from the 1970s were intentionally designed to be so unusual that they could not be used in everyday life, thereby qualifying as a tax deduction under Swedish law. "Their unusual costumes were designed to be so extravagant that they couldn't be used in daily life, allowing a tax deduction under Swedish law." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0DFmS6MPbFI
**Ownership of Polar Studios** : The legendary Polar Studios in Stockholm was owned by the Swedish pop group ABBA. This studio gained further fame as the recording location for Led Zeppelin's "In Through the Out Door" and served as the architectural and functional template for Dave Grohl's 606 Studios. "The template is a place called Polar Studios in Stockholm, which is owned by ABBA." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PHLwjD-L724
