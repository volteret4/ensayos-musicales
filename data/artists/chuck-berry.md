## curiosities
**Touring Practice – Cash Payment Requirement** : In the days before he encountered tax troubles with the IRS and adopted more conventional bookkeeping, Chuck Berry famously demanded his performance fee to be delivered in cash, in a paper bag, before the show. If the bag full of money was not present, the gig would not happen. "His performance fee had to be delivered in cash in a paper bag before the show, and if there was no bag full of money, there was no gig." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z2hvUDtBxho
**Touring Practice – Local Backing Band Requirements** : For his performances, Chuck Berry required the local backup band to be familiar with all his songs and to consist of a drummer, a pianist, and one bass player. They needed to be available for at least three hours prior to showtime for rehearsal, even though Chuck himself often showed up only five minutes before the lights went down. "The local band had to be familiar with all of Chuck's songs, and must include a drummer, a pianist, and one bass player." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z2hvUDtBxho
**Touring Practice – No Permanent Band** : Chuck Berry, known as the father of rock and roll, did not tour with a fixed backing band due to the expense. Instead, he expected promoters to easily find a local backup band for him, as "everybody in the world knew his songs." "he did not tour with a band, it's too expensive." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z2hvUDtBxho
**Touring Practice – Specific Equipment and Luxury Transport** : Chuck Berry traveled only with his guitar and demanded a very specific Fender amp to be supplied. Additionally, he required a brand new rented Mercedes S-Class vehicle, and only an S-Class, for the duration of his visit to the city, with all gas, insurance, and mileage paid. "Chuck traveled only with his guitar. He also needed to be supplied with a very specific fender amp." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z2hvUDtBxho
