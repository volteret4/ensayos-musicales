# artist - John Hughes

## curiosities
**"Pretty in Pink" Ending Change (1986)** : Hughes made a last-minute decision to change the ending of his film "Pretty in Pink" without informing OMD. This rendered the song they had initially written for the film ("God Is the Love") unusable and forced them to write "If You Leave" under extreme pressure in a single day. "John Hughes changed the end of the film without us knowing. And then we, we, we, we, we had a couple of days off in LA before a massive tour started." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0fQQubt9UTI
**Curator of 1980s Soundtracks (1980s)** : John Hughes was known for creating soundtracks for his "brat pack" movies that featured alternative music, often British and electronic. He believed these sounds suited the "outsider" characters in his films, leading him to collaborate with bands like OMD, New Order, Simple Minds, and Kim Wilde. "I suspect that the soundtracks that he put together, which were very often British and quite often electronic, would be the sort of alternative music that those kids in the films would listen to." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0fQQubt9UTI
