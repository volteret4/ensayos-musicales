- Matthew Followill
- Nathan Followill

## instruments
- Bass
- Drums

## albums
**Aha Shake Heartbreak (2005) – UK-First Release and Chart Success** : Released first in the UK, where it reached number 3 on the British album charts, this album did not appear in North America until nearly five months later in February 2005. It continued their five-syllable album title rule and solidified their growing international fanbase. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Because of the Times (2007) – Third Album Continuing Naming Pattern** : Their third album maintained the band’s internal rule of five-syllable titles. It represented a continuation of their evolution before achieving mainstream global success. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Holy Roller Novocaine EP (2003) – Debut EP Recorded in Memphis** : Recorded approximately one month after signing their record deal in 2001, this was the band’s first official release. It included early foundational tracks such as “Wicker Chair” and “Molly’s Chambers,” capturing the raw sound developed during their earliest writing sessions in their mother’s garage. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Only by the Night (2008) – Mainstream Breakthrough Album** : Their fourth album marked the point where the band “really pushed them over the top,” bringing major commercial success and enabling them to transition to large-scale venues after years of gradual growth. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Youth and Young Manhood – International Breakthrough in UK and Europe** : This album achieved unexpected early success in the UK and Europe, establishing the band internationally before they were widely accepted in their native American South. It laid the groundwork for their later global rise. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Youth and Young Manhood – Title Origin from Father’s Bible** : The title of their debut full-length album was taken from a diagram on the back of their father’s Bible describing the “tree of Moses’ life.” This album incorporated material from their debut EP, with some tracks re-recorded, and marked their first significant exposure to a wider audience. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4

## curiosities
**Early UK Breakthrough – Overseas Before Home Success** : The band achieved recognition in the UK and Europe before being embraced in the American South, an unusual trajectory for a Tennessee-based group. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Family-Based Lineup – Brothers and Cousin Formation** : The band consists of three brothers—Caleb, Nathan, and Jared Followill—and their cousin Matthew Followill. This tight familial structure defined both their chemistry and early formation. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Father’s Secret Rock Listening – Nighttime Radio Trick** : Their father would play sports radio to lull their mother to sleep, then switch to rock stations like The Rolling Stones and Neil Young broadcasts. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**First Gig Curtain Mishap – Visible Celebration** : After their first performance, the band celebrated behind what they thought was a solid curtain, only to realize it was transparent and the audience could see everything. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**First Gig Opening for Billy Joe Shaver – Mismatched Audience** : Their debut performance was opening for a country artist to an audience expecting “Old Western music,” highlighting their early disconnect with typical audiences. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Five-Syllable Album Title Rule – Internal Band Tradition** : The band established a rule that all full-length album titles must contain exactly five syllables, a pattern maintained across multiple releases. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Garage Songwriting Sessions – First Five Songs in Two Weeks** : After receiving a Led Zeppelin box set from their label, the band wrote their first five songs in just two weeks in their mother’s garage (“shed”), effectively jumpstarting their career. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Lack of Technical Knowledge – Reliance on Crew** : Band members admitted to limited understanding of their instruments, sometimes needing “cheat sheets” from technicians during interviews or magazine features. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Limited Early Exposure to Rock Music – Late Discovery** : Due to their father’s anti–rock and roll stance, they did not hear Led Zeppelin’s first album until 2001. Their early exposure came secretly through their father listening to AM radio at night. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**No Soundcheck Habit – Trust in Crew** : Unlike highly meticulous bands like U2, Kings of Leon often skip soundchecks, relying entirely on their crew’s preparation. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Nomadic Childhood – Traveling American South** : The family traveled extensively in an old Chrysler across the southern United States, often sleeping in campsites due to lack of money for hotels, shaping their worldview and experiences. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Preacher Family Upbringing – Strict Pentecostal Environment** : Raised in a deeply religious household, their father was a traveling preacher, and their early lives were centered around church, gospel music, and conservative values. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Real Names vs Middle Names – Identity Choice** : All members use their middle names professionally: Caleb (Anthony), Nathan (Ivan), Jared (Michael Jared), and Matthew (Cameron Matthew), creating a unified naming convention within the band. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Record Deal Before Full Band Formation (2001)** : Caleb and Nathan secured a record deal before the full lineup existed, then recruited Jared (who had never played bass) and Matthew (with minimal guitar experience) to complete the band. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**San Diego Fire Incident (September 11, 2001) – Accidental Balcony Blaze** : During a performance for radio and TV programmers, Nathan accidentally started a fire by dropping a cigarette onto Astroturf on a penthouse balcony, causing significant damage. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Self-Taught Musicianship – Minimal Formal Training** : Caleb learned guitar by watching a friend play basic chords (C and F), while other members had little to no formal training, relying heavily on instinct rather than technical knowledge. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Touring with Bob Dylan – Association with Iconic Artist** : They also toured with Bob Dylan, further cementing their credibility within the music industry. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Touring with The Strokes – Early Industry Validation** : Their music impressed The Strokes, leading to touring opportunities during the completion of their debut album. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Touring with U2 – Learning Large-Scale Production** : Touring with U2 taught them about staging, lighting, and the business of large-scale performances, influencing their later shows. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**“Wicker Chair” Origin – Drug-Fueled Writing Session** : The song was inspired by Jared sitting in a white wicker chair while the band was “stoned,” with lyrics subtly referencing the moment and its “helpless situations.” ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
