# artist - Blackpool

## members
- Chris Murphy (bass)

## genres
- Roots Rock

## albums
**We The Living (Pre-Sloan Recording)** : This was a record Chris Murphy played on with Blackpool. Despite his personal preference for hardcore at the time, he later regarded the roots rock album as "pretty cool." It was recorded live off the floor with producer Terry Brown, during which Murphy, playing bass, struggled with technique, prompting Brown to instruct him to stick to root notes ("footballs"). "Looking back now, I think actually the record that I played on was just called We The Living. It was actually pretty cool." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE

## curiosities
**Pre-Sloan Roots Rock Ensemble** : Blackpool was a pre-Sloan "Roots Rock band" in which Chris Murphy played bass. He joined primarily to record with renowned producer Terry Brown, who was holding open recording sessions. The band practiced equal money splitting, a tradition later adopted by Sloan. "It was more kind of a Roots Rock band, but at the time I was still into hardcore basically." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
**Renowned Producer and Studio Mentor** : Terry Brown is a notable producer known for working on "some of their [Rush's] biggest records." He was also present during the recording of The Who's "Substitute" and The Trogs' "Wild Thing." During a session with Blackpool, he strictly instructed Chris Murphy to play only root notes on the bass, a technique Murphy jokingly still refers to as "footballs." "Terry Brown, a guy who worked with Russian, some of their biggest records, ended up working with them." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
