## curiosities
**Absence of a Bass Player** : In the band's early days, The Cramps did not have a bass player, leaving it entirely up to guitarist Poison Ivy to handle the low-end duties and anchor the music. "Early on, the band didn't have a bass player. It was entirely up to Ivy to hold things down." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oLC49nCf0ZM ← the-cramps
**Adoption of Stage Names (Poison Ivy Rorschach and Lux Interior)** : Cristiuala adopted the stage name Poison Ivy Rorschach, inspired by her passion for comics and psychology. Eric Gilsher chose Lux Interior, a name he found in a car advertisement. These names were part of their effort to shape a 1950s aesthetic filtered through the emerging punk style of New York. "Cristi se bautizó como Poison Ivy Roshach, inspirada por su pasión por los cómics y la psicología. Mientras que Eric se rebautizó como lux interior, un nombre tomado de un anuncio de coches." ← Amor, Terror y Psychobilly： La Historia de The Cramps | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tBVTzVQDQeQ
**Band's End with Lux Interior's Death** : The Cramps disbanded after the death of singer Lux Interior in 2009 from an undiagnosed cardiovascular condition. Lux and Poison Ivy had been together for 37 years. "until Lux died of an undiagnosed cardiovascular condition in 2009. Now is the end of the band. **Bryan Gregory's Mid-Tour Departure (1980)** : Shortly after the release of *Songs the Lord Taught Us* in 1980, guitarist Bryan Gregory abandoned the band mid-tour, reportedly taking the band's van and equipment with him. Rumors circulated that he had joined a satanic cult, but the truth was he was experiencing a crisis exacerbated by heroin use. "Poco después de sacar el disco, Reyangre goria abandonó la banda amplia en la gira, llevándose consigo a la forgoneta y el equipo. Corrió el rumor entre las cena y los fans de que se había unido en la secta satanica. **Coining the Term "Psychobilly"** : The Cramps coined the term "Psychobilly" and used it to promote their concerts on posters and flyers. The term quickly gained traction and became a recognized musical genre. They also employed other descriptive terms for their music, such as "Voodoo Rockabilly" and "Gothabilly."
