## songs
**I Think I Love You (1970)** : This song, despite David Cassidy's initial resistance to bubblegum, shot to number one, largely fueled by the popularity of *The Partridge Family* television show. The show debuted on September 25, 1970, and the song hit number one on November 21, 1970, famously knocking The Jackson 5's "I'll Be There" from the top spot. "Pretty sure the TV show the Partridge family had a lot to do with that. The show debuted on September 25th 1970 and then I think I love you. ## curiosities
**Marketing of Teen Idols** : The show was a vehicle for marketing teen idols, with its music and stars, particularly David Cassidy, becoming a significant part of the bubblegum pop phenomenon and its associated merchandise sales. "I mean, after all, you can't put the arches on tour and think of the merchandise sales too. So that was David Cassidy's fate as well as the fate of other teen idols who made music that fits into the bubble gum category." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 30 - The Sweet Sound of 70s Bubblegum Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/030-bubblegum ← the-partridge-family
**Pop Group Comparison** : Critic Charlie Gillette suggested that Dr. Hook, if they were to "relax and become a straight pop group," could "wipe the Osmonds and the Partridge Family off the board," indicating The Partridge Family's status as a popular, clean-cut pop group at the time. "They would wipe the osmonds and the partridge family off the board." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 44 - Streaking, Werewolves, Sharks, & Drinking - 70s Novelty Songs | https://traffic.libsyn.com/seventies/334334714-44100-2-e5ed6adac25ea.m4a
**Television Sitcom Origins** : *The Partridge Family* was a TV sitcom that debuted on September 25, 1970, and featured David Cassidy playing 16-year-old Keith Partridge alongside his real stepmother, Shirley Jones. The show's producers had previously worked on The Monkees and initially intended to dub in all the music until they discovered David Cassidy could sing.
