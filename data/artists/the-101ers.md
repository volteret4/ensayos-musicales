## curiosities
**Disbandment After Sex Pistols Show (April 3, 1976)** : Joe Strummer realized The 101ers were "going nowhere" after attending a Sex Pistols concert at The Nashville Rooms in Kensington, London, on April 3, 1976. He recognized that the Sex Pistols' raw energy represented "the future," prompting him to seek a new musical direction which led to the formation of The Clash. ← Ongoing History of New Music > Joe Strummer： A Remembrance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4m2Kx3ffJUQ&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG ← the-101ers
**Formation and Name Origin** : Sometime in 1974, after busking in London, Joe Strummer (then John Miller) rounded up friends to form a group called The 101ers. The band's name comes from "the number of the house in West London where all the band members lived." ← Ongoing History of New Music > Joe Strummer： A Remembrance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4m2Kx3ffJUQ&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG ← the-101ers
**London Flat** : The members of The 101ers resided together in a flat located at 101 Walter Tinteres in London. "Bunch of guys lived in a flat and 101, Walter Tinteres in London. They played in a group which they called appropriately enough the 101ers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oPgeSCy93bo ← the-101ers
**Nashville Club Gig (Pre-Clash)** : The band had a gig at a club called Nashville where the Sex Pistols performed as the opening act. This event proved pivotal as their guitar player, Joe, was so impressed by the Sex Pistols' attitude and energy that he subsequently left The 101ers to form The Clash. "One night they had a gig at a club called Nashville." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oPgeSCy93bo ← the-101ers
**Pub Circuit Success and Horn Section** : The 101ers achieved considerable success on the London pub circuit, playing covers of American R&B songs.
