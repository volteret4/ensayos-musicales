## albums
**60.000 (1993)** : This electronic, instrumental album was released two years before Air was founded. Many of its titles were in French. It was initially released in Germany in very limited quantities, less than 1,000 units. A British independent label later re-released it in the UK, but it largely went unnoticed. This album was at the center of a "sangrante" (bloody/painful) conflict with Air due to its stylistic similarities and French titles preceding Air's debut. The album is not available on Spotify or iTunes today, and physical copies are extremely rare. ## curiosities
**A Farts Record Label** : In the 1990s, Peter Culman founded his own record label in Germany called "A Farts." The label produced hundreds of works, none of which sold more than 1,000 units. The style of music produced by the label was described as being "ese rollo en el ronque de Gustav Rael." **Collaborations and Posthumous Recognition** : Peter Culman was more widely known for his collaborations with other artists like Brian Eno and Richie Hawtin than for his own musical pieces, partly due to his extensive use of different names. After his death, some of his collaborative works and pieces from alternative projects, previously unreleased or obscure, were made available on Spotify. **Death and Legacy** : Peter Culman passed away in 2002 from a heart attack. After his death, his record label went bankrupt. Due to the extremely small print runs of his works (often less than 1,000 units), much of his discography became exceptionally rare, described as "dead on video." While his primary album "60.000" remains elusive, some of his other productions and collaborations have since appeared on streaming platforms. **Early Career and Pseudonyms** : Peter Culman was an artist and producer who also owned his own graphic label in the 90s. Although he didn't achieve significant mainstream success outside Germany, his work was considered very interesting. Following a conflict related to his 1993 album and Air's debut, he continued his career under various pseudonyms, with "Pit Nanduke" being the most widely recognized.
