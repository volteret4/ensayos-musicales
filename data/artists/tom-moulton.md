# artist - Tom Moulton

## curiosities
**First 12-inch Record** : In 1974, Tom Moulton produced the first 12-inch record, which was Al Downing's "I'll Be Holding On." This innovation, initially called "disco discs," offered extended song versions with additional dance beats, catering to DJs and dancers who desired longer grooves and easier beat mixing. "The name of this first 12-inch record, the first record done this way, was all be holding on by Al Dowling." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0FYq5MBdHw4
**Inventor of the 12-inch Single** : Tom Moulton is credited as the inventor of the 12-inch single, an invention he stumbled upon by mistake. Not a DJ or musician himself, Moulton was skilled at extending songs with additional beats. When he ran out of 7-inch blanks while on a tight deadline, he used 12-inch blanks, stretching the grooves to fill the space, resulting in the first 12-inch record. "The inventor of the 12-inch was Tom Molten, and his invention was a mistake." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0FYq5MBdHw4
