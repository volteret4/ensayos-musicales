**Why Can't We Be Friends (1975)** : Released in April 1975, this song reached number six on the Billboard Hot 100. It is a "wonderful stew" of reggae, funk, R&B, and pop, with an upbeat and positive message. The drummer, Harold Brown, conceived the idea while traveling in Japan, reflecting on shared human connections. The song gained international recognition when it was played in outer space during the first manned international space mission in July 1975, where the American Apollo and Russian Soyuz spacecraft docked, symbolizing global cooperation. Percussionist Papa Dee Allen described it as an attempt to make a "universal anthem" for at least friendship, if not love, in a changing world.
