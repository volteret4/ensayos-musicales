## curiosities
**Disbandment by Mid-1980s** : Throbbing Gristle had broken up by the mid-1980s, marking the end of their initial phase and contributing to a shift in the definition of industrial music. "Throbbing Crystal had broken up, Noe Bowden had discovered to use chord changes, and a new synthesis of styles was developing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE
**Early Industrial Group** : Throbbing Gristle was mentioned as an example of an early industrial music group, a genre with roots in the late 1970s that lived on the fringes of the Alt Rock universe due to its intense, abrasive nature. "Groups said names like Throbbing Gristle, Cabery Voltaire, Knights of Ev in Front II IV II." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Early Industrial Pioneers** : This British group's early recordings are highlighted as "wild stuff" within early industrial music, demonstrating the goal to show that the electric guitar was not necessary, or at least could be used as something other than a conventional rock instrument. "Go back and listen to some of their first recordings by groups such as Britain's Throbbing Gristle." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pLf3kUONewg
**Fascination with Taboos** : The band was fascinated by taboo subjects such as torture, cults, war, the occult, pornography, and societal taboos in general, which translated into their confrontational and often deliberately unpleasant music. "Throbbing Gristle was fascinated by torture, cults, war, the occult, pornography, and societal taboos in general." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE
**Formation and Early Style** : Formed in Hull, northeast of England, a bleak economic area in the late 1970s, Throbbing Gristle began as a hybrid of punk and performance art. By the end of the 1970s, they had developed a distinctive and immersive musical world of their own. "Throbbing Gristle came out of hull in the northeast of England. Not a really happy place in the late 70s in terms of the economy. It was actually pretty bleak. In the beginning, Throbbing Gristle was half punk, half performance art group, but by the end of the 1970s, they were heavily into a world of their own." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE
**Harsh Performance Art** : Throbbing Gristle was a performance art band known for being "harsh" and aiming to shock, laying the early foundations for what would later be known as industrial music. "This is a taste of throbbing gristle, a harsh performance art band who was out to shock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
**Industrial Music Scene Influence** : This band was very important to the industrial music scene and a favorite of Trent Reznor. Peter Christopherson, a member, collaborated on the original video art project for Nine Inch Nails' "Broken" EP. "Two bands very important to the industrial music scene and two favorites of Trent." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DKdF8NgF5Bw
**Pioneering "Industrial" Terminology** : Throbbing Gristle is likely the first group to use the term "industrial" to describe their music, adopting the idea from San Francisco artist Monte Cazazza, who coined the phrase "industrial music for industrial people." They actively gave sound to this statement, reflecting the industrial image in their music, which often sounded like it was recorded in a factory, and through their record label, Industrial Records. "Oh, and another thing. Throbbing Gristle was probably the first group to use the term industrial to describe their music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE
