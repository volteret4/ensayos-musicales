## curiosities
**Defining Figure of Bebop** : Dexter Gordon is mentioned as one of the artists whose music was central to defining bebop. Originating in New York in the mid-1940s, bebop jazz was noted for its "harmonic complexity, instrumental virtuosity, and individual self-expression." "The music made by people like Charlie Parker, Oscar Peterson, Dexter Gordon, Dizzy Gillespie, and so on, and which pretty much defined what was thought of as jazz in the post-war era." ← Episode 41： ＂Be-Bop-A-Lula＂ by Gene Vincent and the Bluecaps | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tYBQ5F7O3G0
**Pioneer of Bebop Jazz** : Dexter Gordon contributed to the rise of bebop jazz, an experimental and virtuosic style that emerged as a reaction against the constraints of big band music. Bebop featured complex improvisations and often lacked discernible beats or melodies for dancing. "Charlie Parker, Sonny Rollins, Dexter Gordon, Miles Davis, Dizzy Gillespie, Thelonius Monk. Their work helped put the nail in the coffin of the big bands." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V7WjNR1ujhk ← dexter-gordon
