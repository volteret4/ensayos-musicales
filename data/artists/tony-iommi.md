## curiosities
**DIY Prosthetic Thimbles** : To manage the pain and difficulty of playing guitar after his injury, Iommi fashioned plastic thimbles from a dishwashing soap bottle and wrapped them in leather from an old jacket. These thimbles allowed him to play but made it difficult to feel the strings, requiring him to press down very hard. "he fashioned a couple of plastic thimbles from a bottle of dishwashing soap and wrapped them in leather from an old leather jacket." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V7WjNR1ujhk
**Development of Signature Sound** : Due to his finger injury and the use of thimbles, Iommi found soloing tough and concentrated on playing heavy power chords. He couldn't bend standard guitar strings well, so he used banjo strings and tuned his guitar low to C sharp (drop C sharp) to make them looser and easier to play. This combination of techniques—heavy power chords, banjo strings, and low tuning—created the distinctive heavy, sinister, and powerful guitar sound that made Black Sabbath famous and significantly contributed to the sound of heavy metal. "Put this all together, and you have the distinctive guitar sound that made his band, Black Sabbath, famous." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V7WjNR1ujhk
**Factory Accident and Finger Injury (1966)** : At age 17, while working in a Birmingham factory that manufactured metal rings, Iommi suffered an industrial accident on his last day of work. Reassigned to a huge press that operated like a guillotine due to a colleague's absence, his right hand was caught, severing the ends of his middle and ring fingers. Doctors informed him he might as well forget about playing the guitar. "bang, it came down. It just took the ends off my fingers. I actually pulled them off." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V7WjNR1ujhk
**Influence on Grunge** : More than a decade after Black Sabbath's rise, a new generation of metal-adjacent bands from the Pacific Northwest, influenced by Tony Iommi, adopted his "no fingers tuning," specifically drop D or drop C sharp. This tuning became a foundational element of the grunge genre. "That dropped D or in Tony's case, drop C sharp became the foundation of grunge." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V7WjNR1ujhk
**Inspiration from Django Reinhart** : After his factory accident, Iommi's foreman, feeling sympathetic, played him a recording by guitarist Django Reinhart. Reinhart, despite terrible burns to his hand leaving him with only two workable fingers, became a master of the guitar, inspiring Iommi to continue playing despite his own injury. "That inspired Tony to keep going, but it was painful." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V7WjNR1ujhk
