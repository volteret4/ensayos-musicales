## albums
**Parklife (April 25, 1994) – Starting Point for Britpop; Food Records Called It a Mistake; 90 Weeks on UK Charts** : Blur had released two albums without setting the world alight. By 1993, frontman Damon Albarn was increasingly angry that American alt-rock was smothering British indie music; Manchester was over, and raves and dance clubs dominated. *Parklife* — a loose concept album capturing the essence of British life, initially to be titled "London" — was called a mistake by their own label Food Records. It proved anything but: an immediate hit with audiences young and old, it stayed on British album charts for 90 weeks and is now considered one of the best British albums of the 1990s. It was released three weeks after the news of Kurt Cobain's death. Along with Oasis's *Definitely Maybe* later that year, it is credited as the starting point of Britpop. "If we're looking for a starting point for Britpop, this is probably it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4Jvy4q40tuo

## songs
**Beagle II (2002) – Instrumental Recorded for British Mars Probe, Lost on Christmas Day 2003** : Blur bassist Alex James and drummer Dave Rowntree, both astronomy enthusiasts, approached the British Space Agency when they heard Britain was launching a Mars probe and proposed providing music to be broadcast upon a successful landing. The agency agreed, and on January 30, 2002, Blur recorded a specially commissioned instrumental called "Beagle II," based loosely on a mathematical sequence. The probe — named Beagle II — was launched on June 2, 2003, designed to land on the Martian surface in the equatorial region known as Isidis Planitia on Christmas Day 2003. After successfully separating from the parent spacecraft Mars Express in Martian orbit on December 19, Beagle II began its descent at 2:47 am London time on December 25 and was never heard from again, joining the long list of failed Martian probes. Blur's debut performance on Mars was never heard. "Essentially, we are going to play the first gig on Mars." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7lpjI8F2Apk
**Song 2 (1997)** : Released in 1997, this song has been extensively licensed for numerous non-musical projects, including movie soundtracks, TV commercials, TV shows, video games, and even as sports anthems for events like the 2012 Summer Olympics in London and the World Cup. It continues to generate millions of dollars for the band annually, despite reputedly being "whipped together in the studio in about 15 minutes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sfie4YYncxk

## curiosities
**Alex James – Became Celebrated British Cheesemaker After Blur's 2004 Hiatus** : When Blur's extended hiatus began in 2004, bassist Alex James moved to the Cotswolds and immersed himself in artisan cheesemaking. His farm features more than 400 sheep, valued for their milk. His specialty is a soft goat's cheese wrapped in a vine leaf called "Little Wallop," and he also makes varieties called "Geronimo" (named after his eldest son), "Churchill Lady," "Rotten Bastard," and "Mad Bitch," plus a triangular variety he calls "Wedgie." He produced a video series called "The Cheese Diaries" for *The Guardian*. The origin of his cheese obsession traces to an early Blur Japan tour when, asked what fans should bring him as gifts, he drunkenly said "cheese" — and was deluged with Japanese cheese at every subsequent visit. "Alex became, in the words of some, the poster boy for the renaissance of British cheesemaking." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BrQQHqbQG0Y
**Alex James's Award-Winning Cheese Production** : After Blur achieved significant financial success, bassist Alex James moved to the countryside, acquiring a farmhouse on 200 acres populated by hundreds of sheep and cows, which he transformed into a cheese-making operation. His cheeses have earned numerous awards and are widely available in British supermarkets. Notable varieties include "Blue Monday" (named after the New Order song), a creamy Shropshire blue cheese, as well as "Queen Maud," "Rotten Bastard," and "Mad Bitch." Alex James is highly regarded in the cheese industry, having served as a judge in competitions and authored books on the subject, including "All Cheeses Great and Small." Even the Royal Family is said to enjoy products from his farm. "Alex is so good at cheese that he has been a judge in cheese competitions." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xc85sLj0Cnk
