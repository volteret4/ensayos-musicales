## albums

## songs
**Beagle 2 Theme (2003)** : A special song written by Blur, instigated by Alex James, intended to be broadcast from Mars by the Beagle 2 probe upon its landing on Christmas Day 2003; however, the probe was lost. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QfiRRtsZoYU
**Country House (1995)** : This single was released on August 14th, 1995, in a highly publicized chart battle against Oasis's "Roll With It." The release date was deliberately moved by Damon Albarn to coincide with Oasis's single, intensifying their rivalry. "Country House" sold 270,000 copies in its first week, winning the sales battle against Oasis's 220,000 copies for "Roll With It." Blur achieved this sales advantage by releasing two versions of the single, each with a CD containing different bonus tracks, thereby incentivizing fans to buy both copies and inflate sales figures. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8UIBWyUt38g
**Far Out (1994)** : A track from Blur's 'Park Life' album, written by Alex James, featuring themes of moons and stars. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QfiRRtsZoYU
**Far Out (2003)** : This is a nine-note instrumental composition, loosely based on the Fibonacci sequence, that Blur composed as a call sign for the Martian probe Beagle II in 2003. It was intended to be broadcast back to Earth upon a safe landing. Although Beagle II was lost, it was later speculated that the probe did survive the landing and played the song, but its damaged antenna pointed in the wrong direction, preventing Earth from hearing it. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xc85sLj0Cnk
**Song 2 (1997)** : This song was covered playfully by Weezer during their tours. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw

**The Brit Pop "Record War" with Oasis (1995)** : A major rivalry, escalating into a "war" between two prominent Brit Pop acts, Blur and Oasis. Early tensions included a near-fight at a San Francisco radio station and a near-brawl between Damon Albarn and Liam Gallagher backstage at an awards ceremony. The conflict culminated on August 14th, 1995, when Damon Albarn deliberately synchronized the release date of Blur's new single with Oasis's, creating a direct chart confrontation. Blur's "Country House" competed against Oasis's "Roll With It," generating widespread media attention and public excitement across Britain. The combined sales of these two singles made up almost 30% of all UK singles sold that week. Blur emerged victorious in the first week's sales, with "Country House" selling 270,000 copies compared to Oasis's 220,000 for "Roll With It." This victory was partly due to Blur's tactic of releasing two versions of "Country House," each featuring different bonus tracks on the CD, prompting dedicated fans to buy both copies. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8UIBWyUt38g
