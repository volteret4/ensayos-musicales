**Beagle 2 Theme (2003)** : This song was composed by Blur, instigated by Alex James, with the unique intention of being broadcast from Mars by the Beagle 2 space probe upon its planned landing on Christmas Day 2003. Although the probe was lost, the song remains a testament to the project. "It's Blur's Beagle 2 theme instigated by bass player Alex James." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QfiRRtsZoYU
**Beagle II (2002) – Instrumental Recorded for British Mars Probe, Lost on Christmas Day 2003** : Blur bassist Alex James and drummer Dave Rowntree, both astronomy enthusiasts, approached the British Space Agency when they heard Britain was launching a Mars probe and proposed providing music to be broadcast upon a successful landing. The agency agreed, and on January 30, 2002, Blur recorded a specially commissioned instrumental called "Beagle II," based loosely on a mathematical sequence. The probe — named Beagle II — was launched on June 2, 2003, designed to land on the Martian surface in the equatorial region known as Isidis Planitia on Christmas Day 2003. After successfully separating from the parent spacecraft Mars Express in Martian orbit on December 19, Beagle II began its descent at 2:47 am London time on December 25 and was never heard from again, joining the long list of failed Martian probes. Blur's debut performance on Mars was never heard. "Essentially, we are going to play the first gig on Mars." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7lpjI8F2Apk
