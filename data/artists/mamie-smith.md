# artist - Mamie Smith

## genres
- Blues
- R&B
- Rock Music

## songs
**Crazy Blues (1920)** : Recorded in a New York studio on August 10, 1920, this song addressed the mistreatment of black people, with a hidden meaning relating to racial injustice rather than just domestic violence. It achieved immediate success, selling 75,000 copies in its first month and over two million copies overall, and was one of the first songs to establish the blues as a popular recorded art form, laying foundations for future blues, R&B, and ultimately rock music. "It was also one of the first songs to establish the blues as a popular art form, at least as in a recorded sense." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjQiZqpmQlE

## curiosities
**Pioneering Black Female Singer** : Mamie Smith was among the first in a line of black female singers who challenged conventional expectations for female vocalists, helping to sell millions of blues records throughout the 1920s and 1930s and significantly contributing to the evolution of R&B and rock music. "Mamie Smith was one of the first of a long line of black female singers who went beyond the bounds of what female singers were supposed to do and supposed to sound like." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjQiZqpmQlE
