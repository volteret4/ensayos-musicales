## curiosities
**Mixed-Race and Mixed-Gender Lineup** : The Selecter was one of the original two-tone ska bands from Coventry, England. Similar to their contemporaries The Specials, they featured a mixed-race lineup, but further distinguished themselves by also being a mixed-gender band. "Like their mates, the specials, the selector had a mixed race lineup. But they went further by being mixed gender." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_1NNvxgx8uA
**Pauline Black's Diverse Career** : Beyond her more than 40 years in music, Pauline Black has also hosted a TV quiz show for children, acted on both stage and television, and contributed to radio programming for the BBC. "Pauline has been active in music for more than 40 years, but she also hosted a TV quiz show for kids, acted on both stage and television, and has even done some work on the radio for the BBC." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_1NNvxgx8uA
**Pauline Black's Identity and Stage Name** : Frontwoman Pauline Black was born to an Anglo-Jewish mother and a Nigerian father, and was raised as Pauline Vickers by a white middle-class couple. She adopted the stage name Pauline Black both to conceal her musical activities from her boss, who disapproved of such pursuits, and to acknowledge her African roots. "She was brought up as Pauline Vickers, but adopted the stage name Pauline Black for a couple of reasons. First of all, she didn't want her boss to know that she was playing in a band. He apparently frowned on things like that. And to acknowledge her African roots." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_1NNvxgx8uA
