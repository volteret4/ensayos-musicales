# artist - David Lynch

## genres
- Dreampop (influence)

## curiosities
**"Twin Peaks" Soundtrack Collaboration** : Lynch's collaboration with composer Angelo Badalamenti and singer Julee Cruise, which began with "Blue Velvet," extended to the TV series "Twin Peaks." He had a specific dreamy and spooky atmosphere in mind for the show, which they helped bring to life through its soundtrack. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFLMkvJAaUY
**Death (January 2025) – Cardiac Arrest at 78, Aggravated by Emphysema and California Wildfires** : Filmmaker and musician David Lynch died at age 78 of cardiac arrest, a condition aggravated by emphysema and the California wildfires. As a musician, he produced the influential album *Floating Into the Night* by Julee Cruise. "Died at 78 of a cardiac arrest aggravated by emphysema and the California wildfires." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZPlsR7_WiZQ
**Failed Song Licensing for "Blue Velvet"** : For his 1986 film "Blue Velvet," Lynch attempted to license "Song to the Siren" by This Mortal Coil from 4AD, but the label refused. This led him to collaborate with Angelo Badalamenti and Julee Cruise for the film's soundtrack. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFLMkvJAaUY
**Floating Into the Night – Julee Cruise Production** : Lynch produced this album for singer Julee Cruise, which is described as influential. "He produced the influential album 'Floating Into the Night' by Julee Cruise." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZPlsR7_WiZQ
**Interest in British Dreampop** : By the time his film "Blue Velvet" was released in 1986, David Lynch was already a fan of British Dreampop. This interest informed the "dreamy, spooky atmosphere" he sought for his film and later for his TV series "Twin Peaks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFLMkvJAaUY
