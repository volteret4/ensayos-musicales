## albums
**Chasing Shadows (EP - 2016)** : This four-track EP, released in 2016, was designed as a companion piece to another novel. The novel itself delves into a vast government cover-up concerning the existence of aliens, titled "Secret Machines," and is also planned to be accompanied by a documentary series. "In 2016, there was Chasing Shadows, another four-track EP designed to be a companion piece to another novel, one about a vast government cover-up of the existence of aliens." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**I Am Pire (Album - 2007)** : This was the second album by Angels & Airwaves, released 18 months after their debut. It performed reasonably well commercially, selling approximately 500,000 copies worldwide. Alongside the album, Tom DeLonge had promised a "mind blowing full length movie," though this film never materialized. "a second album entitled I Am Pire came out 18 months after the first and it's all reasonably well, moving about 500,000 copies worldwide." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Poet Anderson of Nightmares (EP)** : This four-track EP was released as a companion piece to a novel co-written by Tom DeLonge and Suzanne Young, titled "Poet Anderson of Nightmares." It represents Tom's expanded creative endeavors into literature alongside music. "a four-track EP that accompanied a novel Tom wrote with Suzanne Young called Poet Anderson of Nightmares." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**The Dreamwalker (Album)** : This album was part of a larger project that included a short animated film. The film associated with "The Dreamwalker" received an award at the Toronto International Film Festival, showcasing Tom DeLonge's multimedia ambitions. "An album called The Dreamwalker came with a short animated film that won an award at the Toronto International Film Festival." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**We Don't Need to Whistper (Album - 2006)** : This debut album was rushed into release after five of its demos were hacked from Tom DeLonge's email and leaked online. Tom ambitiously described the project as "the greatest rock and roll revolution for this generation," later attributing this hyperbolic statement to the painkillers he was taking at the time due to a bad back. The album showcased influences from The Cure, which Tom was listening to extensively. "Here's one of the singles from the album, which carried the title, We Don't Need to Whistper." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE

## curiosities
**Artistic Re-invention** : The formation of Angels & Airwaves represented Tom DeLonge's desire to evolve as an artist, moving away from the "sophomore goofy teenager" persona he had embodied for many years with Blink-182. This new project allowed him to explore different artistic avenues and express a more mature perspective. "it gave Machins to be another type of artist, something away from that sophomore goofy teenager rule that he'd played for so many years." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Demos Leak and Rushed Release** : The project was initially developed in secret, but its cover was blown when a fan hacked into Tom DeLonge's email, stole five demo tracks, and leaked them online. This forced the band to rush the official release of their debut album. "then some fan hacked into his email and made off with five demos and leaked them online. So with the secret out, the decision was made to rush the release." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Formation and Personal Struggles** : Angels & Airwaves was formed by Tom DeLonge during Blink-182's hiatus. This period was marked by personal struggles for Tom, including a nervous breakdown attributed to the Blink-182 breakup, a severe back condition, and addiction to painkillers, which affected his clarity of thought. He dedicated months to working on new material in his home studio, teaching himself piano and audio engineering, finding the process therapeutic. "Tom was completely invisible the entire time, Plus 44 was in the news. The breakup of Blink caused what he'd later call a nervous breakdown." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Political and Utopian Philosophy** : Angels & Airwaves' philosophy was deeply influenced by Tom DeLonge's engagement with politics, sparked by his endorsement of John Kerry for president and his opposition to the Iraq War (where his brother was deployed), alongside his father's leukemia diagnosis. The band adopted a strong streak of utopianism and positivity, with their motto being "Friends and Family First, Band Seconds," reflecting Tom's personal priorities. "Tom became committed to the idea of reform, and the work it would take to make this a better world. And this became baked into the philosophy of this band, Angels, and Airwaves." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Prolific Multimedia Output** : Since his departure from Blink-182, Tom DeLonge has been highly productive, releasing seven Angels & Airwaves projects, many of which are integral parts of larger multimedia endeavors. These include albums accompanied by animated films, EPs linked to novels co-authored by him, and concepts that extend into documentary series, demonstrating his broad creative scope. "There are now seven angels and airwaves releases." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Unfulfilled Movie Promise** : Following the release of their debut album, Tom DeLonge expressed significant satisfaction with Angels & Airwaves' initial output and promised fans a "mind blowing full length movie" in addition to new music. However, this full-length film project never materialized. "he promised that in addition to more new music there would be some kind of mind blowing full length movie. We never saw the movie." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
