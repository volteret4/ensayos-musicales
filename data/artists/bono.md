**360 Tour Postponement and Financial Impact (2010)** : Due to Bono's back injury, 16 scheduled North American dates on U2's massive 360 world tour had to be postponed for a year. This resulted in an estimated loss of $100 million in ticket sales, marking the largest insurance claim in the history of canceled concerts. "The 16 canceled dates represented a loss of about $100 million in ticket sales. It was the biggest insurance claim in the history of canceled concerts." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NQgn0_mUh74

**Back Injury and Emergency Surgery (May 10, 2010)** : On his 50th birthday, Bono experienced severe back pain, followed by leg numbness and partial paralysis. He was rushed for emergency surgery, where he was diagnosed with a herniated disc, severe compression of the sciatic nerve, an 8-millimeter tear in the disc's ligaments, and disc fragments in his spinal canal. Delicate surgery was required to prevent permanent paralysis. "He was rushed to the hospital for emergency surgery. The problem was discovered to be a herniated disc and a severe compression of the sciatic nerve." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NQgn0_mUh74
**Bicycle Crash and Multiple Fractures (November 2014)** : While preparing for the Innocence and Experience tour, Bono crashed his bike in Central Park, sustaining severe injuries. These included a left facial fracture (affecting his eye orbit), a left scapula (shoulder blade) fractured in three places, a left compound distal humerus fracture (where the bone broke in six pieces and pierced his skin), and a fracture of his fifth metacarpal in his left hand. "He was riding his bike through Central Park and he crashed. Hard. And the injuries were ugly." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NQgn0_mUh74

**Glaucoma Diagnosis and Sunglasses (Early 1990s)** : Bono was diagnosed with glaucoma, a chronic eye condition that causes pressure buildup on the eyeball, in the early 1990s. One of its symptoms is sensitivity to light, even indoors, which is the medical reason he started wearing sunglasses consistently around that time. He has undergone various treatments like eye drops and lasers to prevent optic nerve damage. "Bono was first diagnosed with Glacoma in the early 1990s, which, as you might remember, is exactly when he started wearing sunglasses all the time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NQgn0_mUh74
**Grandstanding at Live Aid (July 13, 1985)** : During U2's performance of "Bad" at Live Aid, Bono engaged in an extended moment trying to pull a dance partner from the audience, consuming many minutes of their allotted time. This "grandstanding" caused the band to miss playing their biggest hit, "Pride (In the Name of Love)," and led to a major backstage confrontation. "Donald decided he needed a dance partner and spent many long minutes trying to pull somebody out of the audience." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2RKchFW42Jc
**Inspiration from Greg Carroll for "The Joshua Tree"** : Following the death of U2 roadie Greg Carroll, Bono, while working on lyrics for "The Joshua Tree" album, recalled a tour of Auckland given to him by Carroll. This experience, particularly a visit to the sacred Maori site of Maungakiekie (One Tree Hill), became a direct lyrical inspiration for the album and the song "One Tree Hill," both dedicated to Carroll. "When everyone returned to Ireland, Bono, who was working on lyrics for songs for what would become the Joshua Tree album, remembered that tour of Auckland that he got from Carroll." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZVGnSVfed9s
**Jamaican Shooting Incident (1996)** : On January 16, 1996, Bono, his family, and friends including Jimmy Buffett and Island Records head Chris Blackwell, were taxing on the tarmac at Negril airport in Jamaica aboard Buffett's Grumman HU-16C plane, "Hemisphere Dancer," with Buffett at the controls. Jamaican police mistakenly believed the aircraft was a drug runner's plane and opened fire on it. Bono described the terrifying event as being "in the middle of a James Bond movie," and while everyone was safe, he and his family cut their holiday short, spending the rest of their time in Miami, with the Jamaican government issuing a full apology. "Those boys were shooting all over the place. I felt as if we were in the middle of a James Bond movie. Only this was real." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hmnMG6f80Sg
**Learjet Cargo Hatch Detachment (2014)** : On November 13, 2014, while Bono was on a two-hour flight from Dublin to Berlin's Schönefeld Airport aboard a private Learjet 60, the rear cargo hatch of the plane became detached as it approached the German coast. Passengers heard a "big thud," and at 12:15 AM, 11 minutes before touchdown, the door blew off entirely. Upon landing, two suitcases were found missing, and a piece of the door was later discovered in the Brandenburg area. "As the plane approached the German coast, the rear cargo hatch of the plane became detached." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hmnMG6f80Sg
