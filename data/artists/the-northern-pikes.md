## curiosities
**Brian Potvin's Talent Scouting and Songwriting** : Upon the Pikes' retirement, guitarist Brian Potvin transitioned into talent scouting for Polygram and Universal Records, working with artists such as Finger Eleven and Tegan and Sara. He also freelances as a songwriter, contributing material to other bands and to the Discovery Channel, including the series "Survivor Man." "When the Pikes retired, he went to work scouting talent for Polygram, and later Universal Records. This had him work with everyone from finger 11 to Tegan and Sarah. He also freelances as a songwriter. Some material goes to other bands, while other stuff has been for the Discovery Channel, including the series Survivor Man." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EE8U4yyKtd0
**Five-Year Retirement** : Their retirement lasted for a period of five years before their record label initiated their reformation. "That retirement lasted for five years." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EE8U4yyKtd0
**Formation and Initial Run (1983-1993)** : The Northern Pikes formed in 1983 and maintained an active career until 1993, when they chose to retire rather than formally break up. "They were formed in 1983, and they had a run until 1993, when they retired. Not broke up, but retired." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EE8U4yyKtd0
**Jay Semko's Multi-Faceted Career** : Bass player and singer Jay Semko has pursued a diverse career, composing music for numerous TV series (including "Due South," "Trailer Park Boys" movie, and shows on CBC, Global History Channel, Discovery Channel, APTN, Showcase), performing voiceover work, writing music for museum installations, producing other artists, and releasing at least 11 solo albums. "Space Player and singer J. Senko wrote the music for the TV series Do South. He wrote music for the first trailer park boys movie. He wrote music for the series scene on the CBC, Global History Channel, Discovery Channel, APTN, Showcase, and a whole bunch more. He does voiceover work for radio and TV commercials. He wrote music for museum installations. And he's produced records for other artists, and has at least 11 solo albums." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EE8U4yyKtd0
**Juno Nomination and Charting Singles** : In addition to their albums and EPs, the band received a Juno nomination and had multiple singles that charted in both Canada and the United States. "a Juno nomination, and a bunch of singles that charted in both Canada and the US." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EE8U4yyKtd0
**Reformation and Sporadic Activity (1999-Present)** : The band reformed in 1999 to tour behind a greatest hits album, and their reunion has continued sporadically since then, involving occasional new recordings and performances. "And that reunion, such that it is, being a sporadic sort of thing, has officially held since then." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EE8U4yyKtd0
**Saskatoon Origins and National Breakthrough** : The band originated from Saskatoon and achieved national success in the mid to late 1980s, an uncommon feat for a band from that city at the time, positioning them as pioneers of alt-rock from the region. "Back then, and I mean the mid to late 80s, it was really rare for a band from Saskatoon to break out nationally. As far as I remember, the Pikes were the first alt rock band to do that." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EE8U4yyKtd0
