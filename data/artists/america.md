## curiosities
**Considered for Soviet Tour** : The soft rock band America was among the groups considered to be the first American band to tour the Soviet Union in 1977. However, the Nitty Gritty Dirt Band was ultimately selected for this cultural mission, which aimed to introduce American rock music into Soviet society as a form of soft power. "In 1977, the nitty-gritty dirt band was chosen over the Dubie brothers, and the soft rock band America, must have been their name, to be the first American band to tour through the Soviet Union." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NyKvAhO7MHE ← america
**Non-Political Stance of Members (1972)** : In a spring 1972 interview, Dewey Bunnell stated that the band members, all between 18 and 20 years old, were "not political" and that "all that they were into as music," distinguishing them from more politically engaged youth of earlier eras. "Dewey Bennell said that nobody in the band ages 18 19 and 20 was political all that they were into as music." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 51 - The Softer Side of 70s Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-51-the-softer-side-of-70s-rock
**Pretending to Be a British Band (Early 1970s)** : The band initially pretended to be British, believing that "unknown bands were better received if they were British rather than American" in the early 1970s. "Why they called themselves British if they were not because they thought that unknown bands were better received if they were British rather than American which may have there may have been something to that in 1971 or 72." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 51 - The Softer Side of 70s Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-51-the-softer-side-of-70s-rock
**Shift from Self-Production to George Martin** : After self-producing their first three albums proved to be "a lot of work," America sought a producer. They successfully enlisted George Martin, renowned for his work with The Beatles, who produced their 1974 album *Holiday* and later conducted a symphony orchestra for their 1975 live recording.
