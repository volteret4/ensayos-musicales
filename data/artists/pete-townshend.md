## curiosities
**"I Want My MTV" Campaign** : Pete Townshend was one of the prominent artists who appeared in promos for the "I Want My MTV" campaign. This highly effective campaign encouraged people to call their local cable companies to demand that they carry the new channel, significantly expanding MTV's reach and power across the US. **Commissioning Marshall Amps (1962)** : In 1962, Pete Townshend, a regular customer of Jim Marshall, requested a custom-built amplifier that would be "bigger and louder than the American Fender amps that he had been using." Townshend's explicit instruction was, "I don't want to hear the crowd," leading Marshall to develop more powerful amplification technology that was crucial for the evolving sound of rock. **First Rock Opera Credit** : Pete Townshend is credited with creating the first rock opera, "A Quick One While He's Away," a 10-minute song with six movements recorded for The Who's 1966 album *A Quick One*. This ambitious composition established a precedent for the rock opera format.
