## songs
**Hotel California (1976)** : This song is cited as a prime example of an "FM fade," characterized by its long and slow fade-out. The extended fade reinforces the song's impact and hook in the listener's mind. "Think what's a good example. Hotel California by the Eagles. That fade goes on forever." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yPok3MemDmE ← eagles
**Life in the Fast Lane (1976)** : This song was featured on the "FM" movie soundtrack, which was curated to reflect the FM radio hits of the late 1970s. "Life in the Fast Lane by the Eagles..." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 28 - Soundtracks of the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/028-soundtracks-of-the-70s-12221-5-45-pm
**One of These Nights (1975)** : This song was the winner of the 1975 Billboard Song of the Summer. It features a "steamy groove" that "objectively sounds like a hot August night," which was the month it topped the Hot 100. "this 1975 song of the summer by Yes, The Eagles has a steamy groove that objectively sounds like a hot August night, appropriately that was the month when it topped the hot 100." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Song(s) of the Summer Edition Part 2 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4ef50c30a1408dc292e4
**Take It to the Limit (1975)** : This song by The Eagles was cited as a contemporary radio hit of the era, providing a point of comparison for the sound of ABBA's "Mamma Mia." "radio hits of the era like, take it to the limit by the Eagles." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 56 - Talk Shows of the 1970s | https://www.ftr70.com/ ← eagles
