## curiosities
**Anticipation of Grunge** : It has been argued that Tin Machine's sound notably pre-saged the Grunge movement that emerged a couple of years later, making them a "visionary" group in retrospect for their influence on 90s rock and alternative music. "But I've argued that Tin Machine pre-saged Grunge a couple of years later." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rw0uXf5UJvo
**Bowie's 90s Reinvention** : Tin Machine was formed by David Bowie as part of his 90s reinvention, envisioned as a four-piece rock band with Bowie as the lead singer, drawing inspiration from artists like Jimi Hendrix, Cream, and The Pixies. Their debut record had a distinct sound, not easily mistaken for a typical David Bowie release. "His idea was to form a four-piece rock band with him as the lead singer. Inspiration was taken from Jimmy Hendrix, Cream, and the Pixies. They called themselves Tin Machine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rw0uXf5UJvo
**Initial Muted Reception, Later Re-evaluation** : Upon their first record's release, Tin Machine received a "muted" reaction from critics and fans. However, the project has since been favorably re-evaluated, recognized as "visionary" for its prescient sound that pre-saged the grunge, rock, and alternative music movements of the 1990s. "Reaction was... muted." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rw0uXf5UJvo
