## curiosities
**Ahead of Its Time** : Bowie had correctly anticipated that music would become heavier and angrier, but Tin Machine's fundamental problem was that they were "doing that two or three years before the world was ready for it," suggesting a re-evaluation of their historical significance is needed. "Bowie had correctly ascertained that music was going to get heavier and angrier. Tin Machine's problem is that they were doing that two or three years before the world was ready for it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Anticipation of Grunge** : It has been argued that Tin Machine's sound notably pre-saged the Grunge movement that emerged a couple of years later, making them a "visionary" group in retrospect for their influence on 90s rock and alternative music. "But I've argued that Tin Machine pre-saged Grunge a couple of years later." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rw0uXf5UJvo
**Bowie's 90s Reinvention** : Tin Machine was formed by David Bowie as part of his 90s reinvention, envisioned as a four-piece rock band with Bowie as the lead singer, drawing inspiration from artists like Jimi Hendrix, Cream, and The Pixies. Their debut record had a distinct sound, not easily mistaken for a typical David Bowie release. "His idea was to form a four-piece rock band with him as the lead singer. Inspiration was taken from Jimmy Hendrix, Cream, and the Pixies. They called themselves Tin Machine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rw0uXf5UJvo
**Bowie's Return to Band Format (1988)** : Formed in 1988, Tin Machine marked David Bowie's first time being in a band since the early 1960s, representing a deliberate shift towards a more collaborative and hard-edged sound driven by his personal musical preferences. "It was the first time that Bowie had been in a band since sometime in the early 1960s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Fan and Critic Backlash** : Despite initial success, the band "soon ran out of steam" as fans and critics felt that Bowie had "stopped being Bowie," leading to a lack of fond remembrance for the project among many of his followers. "Fans and critics felt that Bowie had stopped being Bowie. There was a second album and a live record, and then the band broke up in 1992. Tin Machine is frankly not remembered fondly by many Bowie fans, if at all." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Formation as a Four-Piece Rock Band** : David Bowie formed Tin Machine with the explicit idea of creating a four-piece rock band where he would serve solely as the lead singer. This project was a conscious effort to flush away the baggage of his past hits and artistic personas, drawing inspiration from the raw aesthetics of bands like Jimi Hendrix, Cream, and the Pixies. "His idea was to form a four piece rock band with him just as the lead singer." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2FyAZGUI_mk
**Harsh Critical Reception** : The two albums released by Tin Machine between 1989 and 1991 received very harsh reviews. Critics often judged the band not only against David Bowie's celebrated past works but also against the rising tide of alternative music at the time, perceiving Bowie as an "old man trying to be hip again" with his rock band. "There would be two Tin Machine albums before the project was retired and during this time which would be between 1989 and 1991, they received some pretty harsh reviews." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2FyAZGUI_mk
**Hypothetical Success in Later Years** : It is proposed that if Tin Machine had emerged later, "in, let's say 1992 or 1993," their reception and impact "would have been very, very different," implying that their timing was their primary impediment to greater success. "I think Tin Machine requires a re-evaluation. If they had arrived in, let’s say 1992 or 1993, I maintained that things would have been very, very different." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Initial Muted Reception, Later Re-evaluation** : Upon their first record's release, Tin Machine received a "muted" reaction from critics and fans. However, the project has since been favorably re-evaluated, recognized as "visionary" for its prescient sound that pre-saged the grunge, rock, and alternative music movements of the 1990s. "Reaction was... muted." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rw0uXf5UJvo
**Predating 1990s Hard Rock** : Tin Machine's experimental and very hard sound significantly predated much of the grunge, punk, and hardcore music that would become prevalent in the 1990s, showcasing Bowie's accurate foresight into the future direction of rock music. "Tin Machine was experimental and very hard, predating a lot of the music that we would hear with Grunge and Punk and Hardcore in the 1990s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
