**Controversial but Beneficial Departure** : Alan Wilder's departure sparked significant controversy among Depeche Mode fans, particularly because he held a very clear and defined vision for the band's sound, especially concerning production. While his presence undeniably embodied a crucial part of the band's essence, his leaving ultimately proved beneficial in the long run. It allowed the remaining members to explore new musical horizons, incorporate diverse influences like Trip Hop and Krautrock, and adopt more complex, computerized electronic structures not previously possible under his more synthesiser-focused approach. **Departure from Depeche Mode (1995)** : Alan Wilder, considered by some to be the "great talent" of Depeche Mode, left the band in 1995 due to what he perceived as an "unsustainable situation." This departure occurred amidst Dave Gahan's severe heroin addiction and Martin Gore's personal struggles, contributing to widespread media speculation about the band's imminent demise. **Near-Death Experience (September 1, 1994)** : On September 1, 1994, approximately two months after the Devotional Tour concluded, Alan Wilder was driving through the Scottish countryside when an RAF jet fighter trainer almost crashed onto his car, missing him by just over 200 meters. Had his girlfriend not seen the plane approaching and alerted him to pull over into a farm road, his car would have been hit. The two pilots onboard the jet were killed. **Resignation from Depeche Mode (June 1, 1995)** : Alan Wilder officially resigned from Depeche Mode on June 1, 1995, after 13 years with the group. His official statement cited increasing dissatisfaction with internal relations, working practices, and a consistent imbalance in the distribution of workload, stating that his input "never received the respect and acknowledgement that it warrants."
