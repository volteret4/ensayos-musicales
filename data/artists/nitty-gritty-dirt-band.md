# artist - Nitty Gritty Dirt Band

## genres
- Appalachian Mountain Surf music
- Country Rock

## concerts
- **1977 Soviet Union Tour** : The Nitty Gritty Dirt Band was selected over the Doobie Brothers and the soft rock band America to become the first American band to tour the Soviet Union in 1977. This tour, championed by President Jimmy Carter who believed rock and roll could undermine communism, was a wild success among young people in Russia and various Soviet republics, with demand for tickets overwhelming the KGB. "In 1977, the nitty-gritty dirt band was chosen over the Dubie brothers, and the soft rock band America, must have been their name, to be the first American band to tour through the Soviet Union."

## songs
**Mr. Bojangles** : This song was part of the repertoire performed by the Nitty Gritty Dirt Band during their historic 1977 tour of the Soviet Union. Although described as "not very rock and roll," the performance of such songs served the purpose of injecting American culture into the Soviet populace as part of a US soft power initiative. "Mr. Bold Jangoos. Mr. Bold Jangoos. Mr. Bold Jangoos. Dance. **Mr. Bojangles (1970)** : This cover of Jerry Jeff Walker's 1968 song was featured on their album "Uncle Charlie and His Dog Teddy." Producer Bill McEwan enhanced the studio recording by adding tape of an interview with his wife's great Uncle Charlie, whose dog, Teddy, howled in sync with Charlie's harmonica. The song reached number 7 on the Billboard Hot 100 in 1970, remaining on the pop charts for 36 weeks, and was inducted into the Grammy Hall of Fame in 2010. ## curiosities
**Fiddle Accompanied Spoken Word (1974)** : The Nitty Gritty Dirt Band, fitting into the country rock genre, recorded a spoken word version of Stephen Vincent Benét's poem "The Mountain Whippoorwill" with fiddle accompaniment on their 1974 album "Stars and Stripes Forever." This recording contains the "seeds" of "The Devil Went Down to Georgia" by The Charlie Daniels Band. "The nitty gritty dirt band who also would fit into this kind of country rock genre. **First American Band to Tour Soviet Union (1977)** : In 1977, the Nitty Gritty Dirt Band was chosen over more conventional acts like the Doobie Brothers and America to be the first American band to tour the Soviet Union. This tour, endorsed by President Jimmy Carter, aimed to undermine communism through rock and roll. The tour proved immensely successful among young people in Russia and other Soviet republics, generating such high demand for tickets that it overwhelmed the KGB. "To be the first American band to tour through the Soviet Union, President Jimmy Carter was all for this, thinking that rock and roll could undermine communism." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YeNWDtN_sHE ← nitty-gritty-dirt-band
**Kansas City Heroes** : Rock journalism legend Greg Shaw regarded the Nitty Gritty Dirt Band as heroes in Kansas City, a city he noted was not a major rock music center, yet fans there were devoted and willing to travel for their music. Shaw also identified Kansas City as the "spiritual home of country rock."
