## curiosities
**John Frusciante's Uncredited but Pivotal Role** : John Frusciante was not an official member of The Mars Volta, but he played a very significant and important role throughout their creative development, recordings, and overall musical process. Despite his crucial contributions, he was often uncredited on their albums, appearing only for specific guitar solos. His involvement is confirmed by interviews with band members like Omar Rodríguez-López and Frusciante himself, with some of his guitar solos from this era considered career-defining. **Part of 2010s Progressive Rock Resurgence** : The Mars Volta was included in the broad category of bands participating in the 2010s comeback of progressive rock. This resurgence was characterized by an "attitude and an approach to instruments and arrangements," encompassing various experimental and complex styles like math rock and djent. "This was a big tent, including everyone from Biffy Cliro, to Dance Gavan Dance to the Mars Volta." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HSvgRyGiOpE ← the-mars-volta
**Regional Reception Disparity (Americas vs. Europe)** : The Mars Volta's albums are widely considered masterpieces in the United States and across Latin America. However, in Europe, the band and their albums never fully resonated with audiences. A notable Spanish music magazine, upon the release of their second album, controversially labeled it "the most pretentious album they had ever heard in their entire life," highlighting the stark difference in critical reception across continents.
