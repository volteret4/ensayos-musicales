## curiosities
**Considered "Too Light" for Punk** : In the mid-2000s bifurcation of pop punk, Good Charlotte was identified as being "too light" or "too poppy for punk." This led to some alt-rock radio stations refusing to play their music, claiming they appealed to the "wrong kind of people" (i.e., fans who were too young). "groups like Headley and Simple Plan and Good Charlotte were maybe a little too light." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o

However, they were eventually "damned by the audience," who began to dismiss their music with terms like "kitty punk."
