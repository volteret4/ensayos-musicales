## songs
**Bo Diddley (1955)** : This song is associated with the distinctive "Bo Diddley beat," a rhythm that Bo Diddley popularized and introduced to rock and roll, even though its history dates back at least 50 years before him. The beat has since been widely adopted by artists like Iggy Pop and Joan Jett. "the man who used a particular rhythm so effectively that we actually now call it the bow-diddly beat." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ ← bo-diddley
**Bo Diddley (Undated)** : The rhythm from Bo Diddley's song "Bo Diddley" was explicitly referenced as an inspiration for Johnny Otis's "Willie and the Hand Jive." Otis claimed to have heard the "Bo Diddley rhythm" from convicts on a chain gang before Diddley ever made a record, suggesting an independent or parallel discovery of the rhythmic pattern. "Otis coupled their expanse Han Jiving to the Bo Deadly rhythm, though he would always claim for the rest of his life, that he'd heard that rhythm from convicts on a chain gang, before Deadly ever made a record." ← REUPLOAD Episode 71： ＂Willie and the Hand Jive＂ by Johnny Otis | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ez6BCUlI6Rg
**Love Is Strange (1956 - Unreleased Original)** : Bo Diddley wrote and recorded this song in May 1956 but did not release it. Jody Williams played the lead guitar, reusing lyrics he had used for "Billy's Blues." Diddley claimed he told publishers Jody Williams wrote the music and he wrote the lyrics, requesting his wife Ethel Smith be credited for the lyrics. "In May 1956, Deadly had written and recorded a song called Love Is Strange and not got round to releasing it." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI

## curiosities
**Bo Diddley Beat** : The "Bo Diddley beat" is cited as an example of a rhythm and beat that is "not protected" by copyright, illustrating that specific rhythmic patterns can be used by multiple artists without legal consequence. "That's why you can have bow diddly. And Iggy Pop. And you too. And Jet." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rfObVld9rIE ← bo-diddley
**Co-Writer of "Love Is Strange"** : Bo Diddley co-wrote "Love Is Strange," a song that was a favorite of Buddy Holly's and served as the musical basis for Holly's first solo single, "Words of Love." "who had co-written Love Is Strange as a cricket side." ← Episode Sixty-One： ＂That'll Be the Day＂, by the Crickets | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KVJMKLc54O0
**Co-writer of "Love Is Strange"** : Bo Diddley is credited as a co-writer for "Love Is Strange," claiming to have written the lyrics (credited to his wife Ethel Smith), while Jody Williams wrote the music. "Now, Deadly claimed that what he told the song's publishers was that Jody Williams wrote the music while he wrote the lyrics, but he asked that the credit for the lyrics bepotting the name of his wife, Ethel Smith." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Dispute over Writing Credits for "Love Is Strange"** : Jody Williams, who played lead guitar on Diddley's unreleased version of "Love Is Strange," was not credited for the music and blamed Diddley. Diddley argued that most people he signed contracts with were "liars and thieves" and he wasn't surprised Williams's name was omitted, leading to a decades-long estrangement between them. "Williams as Deadly didn't, and Williams blamed Deadly for the emission while Deadly just said with some evidence that most of the people he signed contracts with were liars and thieves, and that it didn't surprise him that they'd missed Williams's name off." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Divorce and Destroyed Papers** : Before his divorce from Ethel Smith, she reportedly burned all his papers, which Diddley claimed was to prevent him from proving he had given her money, making it impossible to ascertain the exact terms of his contracts, including those related to "Love Is Strange." "We'll never know for sure what was actually in Deadly's contract, because, again, according to Deadly, just before he and Smith divorced, she burned all his papers, so she could claim that he never gave her any money, and he couldn't prove otherwise." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Giant of Rock Music** : Bo Diddley is recognized as one of the significant figures in the broader landscape of rock music. "Where would we be with a bow diddly and little Richard and Jimmy Hendrix?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc ← bo-diddley
**Influence on The Crickets' Music** : The Crickets recorded the song "Not Fade Away," which they described as being "patented after Bo Didley," indicating a direct influence from his distinctive musical style. "they went into the studio and caught another song they had patented after Bo Didley, who had co-written Love Is Strange as a cricket side." ← Episode Sixty-One： ＂That'll Be the Day＂, by the Crickets | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KVJMKLc54O0
**Influenced by Gene Autry** : Bo Diddley was one of the artists whose musical development was shaped by the influence of Gene Autry, a leading Western music star during the late 1930s and early 1940s. "influenced everyone from Les Paul to Bowie Didley." ← Episode 45： ＂Blueberry Hill＂, by Fats Domino | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VqIwZGJHgBg
**Popularizer of the "Bo Diddley Beat"** : Bo Diddley is renowned for using a particular rhythm so effectively that it became known as the "Bo Diddley beat." Although the beat itself originated at least 50 years before his time, he was instrumental in introducing it to rock and roll, influencing numerous artists from Iggy Pop to Joan Jett. "Bow didn't invent that beat. It's history goes back at least 50 years, but he was the one who introduced it to rock and roll." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ ← bo-diddley
**Sale of "Love Is Strange" Share** : According to Bo Diddley, he later sold his share in the song "Love Is Strange" to Sylvia (Van Topoele/Robinson) sometime in the early 1960s, a decision that proved astute given Sylvia's subsequent business acumen. "According to Deadly, he also later sold his own share in the song to Sylvia, sometime in the early 60s." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Session Guitarist for Other Artists** : Diddley was friendly with Mickey Baker and used Baker as a session guitarist on outside recordings he produced for other artists, including Billy Stewart and The Marquees (which featured Marvin Gaye). "At the time, Deadly was friendly with Mickey Baker and was using Baker as a session guitarist on outside recordings he was producing for other artists, including recordings with Billy Stuart and with the marquees, a vocal group which featured a young singer named Marvin Gaye." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
