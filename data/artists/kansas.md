## songs
**Carry On Wayward Son (1976)** : This song was a hit for Kansas, reaching number 11, and is credited with saving the band from being dropped by Don Kirshner's record label. Songwriter Kerry Livgren, who later became a born-again Christian and Christian rock artist, stated in a 2004 interview that the song was autobiographical, representing his "own spiritual journey" and "a song of self-encouragement" in his search for truth. **Dust in the Wind (1978)** : This song was Kansas's only Top 10 hit, reaching number six in April 1978. Written by Kerry Livgren, it draws references from the Bible, specifically Ecclesiastes ("everything he has accomplished is futile, like chasing the wind") and Genesis ("for dust thou art, and unto dust thou shalt return"). Some clergy members rejected the song's message due to the line "all we are is dust in the wind," deeming it potentially offensive. The song's universal theme of the impermanence of life resonated widely.
