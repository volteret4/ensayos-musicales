## albums
**2112 (1976)** : This album is a complex concept record featuring a narrative that includes the Red Star, the Solar Federation, and priests controlling existence in the temples of Syrikes. The story also involves a rebel guitar player who rises up and commits suicide. The entire work was inspired by the objectiveist philosopher Ayn Rand, particularly her books like *Atlas Shrugged*. "We've got the Red Star, the Solar Federation, and the priests who run the temples of Syrikes in their command over existence. And then we have the rebel guitar player who rises up and then commits suicide." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KyNY3zv6uc

## curiosities
**1975 Concert at Travis Strait Electric Company** : In 1975, Rush performed at the Travis Strait Electric Company in Dallas. Admission for this show was $3, and attendees were offered free beer between 8:30 PM and 10:00 PM, creating a lively atmosphere with liquid refreshments and game rooms. "This is from 1975. And admission to this show was $3. And get this. There was free beer from 830 to 10." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8q2bRZyHnHE
**2112 Cultural Impact** : Dave Grohl received the "2112" album from a cousin in third grade, which broadened his musical horizons beyond AM radio. He was initially hesitant to show the album to friends due to the band's image, including their "kimonos" and Alex Lifeson's "camel toe." "I remember taking it home, but I didn't, I was afraid to show my friends this new record because of those kimonos they were wearing and the like crazy camel toe that Alex Lifeson had." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mh-qg1izM98
**Baseball Memorabilia Collector** : Geddy Lee is an avid baseball fanatic. For a period, he collected autographed baseballs from the old Negro League, amassing over 200 before donating them to the Negro Baseball League Museum in Kansas City. "He had more than 200 before he donated them to the Negro baseball league musician in Kansas City." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xc85sLj0Cnk
**Concert Ticket Price Example** : At some point, a Rush concert ticket was priced at $12, which highlights the increase in concert ticket costs over several decades, as such an amount today would barely cover service charges for some tickets. **Geddy Lee's Bass Parts** : Tim Commerford of Rage Against the Machine spent hours studying and learning the intricate bass parts played by Geddy Lee on Rush albums, indicating a significant influence on his playing style. "After that Tim spent hours trying to learn giddy leaves parts in all the rush albums he could buy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CVPPymc_qrk
**Influence on Math Rock** : Rush was cited as a master of complex rock that inspired Math Rock, a genre described as complicated, hard to play, and "impossible to dance to." This influence contributed to the 2010s comeback of progressive rock, including the rise of Djent and other complex styles utilizing multi-string guitars. "This was complicated, complex, hard to play, and impossible to dance to rock, inspired by masters like Russian, King Crimson, and yes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HSvgRyGiOpE
**Rock and Roll Hall of Fame Induction** : Rush was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, with the Foo Fighters members, specifically Dave Grohl and Taylor Hawkins, expressing their admiration for the band's significance.
