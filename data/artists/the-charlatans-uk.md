## curiosities
**Martin Duffy's Death** : Martin Duffy, who was most recently the keyboardist for The Charlatans UK, passed away just before Christmas at the age of 55. "And most recently the Charlottesons UK, passed away just before Christmas at the age of 55." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sdPeii-8Ysw
**Rob Collins Armed Robbery Conviction (1992–1993) – Friend Robbed Liquor Store "To Impress His Rock Star Friend"** : On December 3, 1992, keyboardist Rob Collins drove an old school friend named Michael Whitehouse to a liquor store and waited in the car. Michael robbed the store — reportedly to impress his rock star friend — and ran back to the car; Collins drove away without knowing the full extent of what had just happened. Witnesses got his license plate number. Three hours later, police arrested Collins; he spent five days in custody while Whitehouse confessed to armed robbery. Original charges of armed robbery and possession of a firearm were reduced to assisting an offender after the crime. At trial on September 21, 1993, Whitehouse was sentenced to four years; Collins received eight months and served time as prisoner RD1533 at Redditch Open Prison, where a fellow inmate hummed the melody of a Charlatans song at him in the showers. Collins died in a car crash on July 22, 1996. "His buddy Michael confessed to armed robbery and then Rob began to talk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fMVGuHjJp9U
**Rob Collins's Armed Robbery Conviction** : On December 3, 1992, after returning from a successful tour of Japan, keyboardist Rob Collins decompressed with friends. He drove a friend, Michael Whitehouse, to a liquor store, waiting in the car while Whitehouse robbed it at gunpoint. Collins, initially unaware, sped away when Whitehouse ran out. The car's license plate was recorded, leading to Collins's arrest a few hours later, and he was held for five days. Whitehouse confessed, stating he wanted to impress Rob. Collins was sentenced on September 21, 1993, to four years in prison for "assisting an offender after a crime" and served eight months at Redditch Open Prison, where he was known as Prisoner RD1533. "When Rob's trial finished on September 21, 1993, he was sentenced to four years in prison on a charge of assisting an offender after a crime." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6xygY-npCr4
