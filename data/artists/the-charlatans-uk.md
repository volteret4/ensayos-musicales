## curiosities
**Rob Collins Armed Robbery Conviction (1992–1993) – Friend Robbed Liquor Store "To Impress His Rock Star Friend"** : On December 3, 1992, keyboardist Rob Collins drove an old school friend named Michael Whitehouse to a liquor store and waited in the car. Michael robbed the store — reportedly to impress his rock star friend — and ran back to the car; Collins drove away without knowing the full extent of what had just happened. Witnesses got his license plate number. Three hours later, police arrested Collins; he spent five days in custody while Whitehouse confessed to armed robbery. Original charges of armed robbery and possession of a firearm were reduced to assisting an offender after the crime. At trial on September 21, 1993, Whitehouse was sentenced to four years; Collins received eight months and served time as prisoner RD1533 at Redditch Open Prison, where a fellow inmate hummed the melody of a Charlatans song at him in the showers. Collins died in a car crash on July 22, 1996. "His buddy Michael confessed to armed robbery and then Rob began to talk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fMVGuHjJp9U
