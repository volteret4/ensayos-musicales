## albums
**When Your Heart Stop's Beating (2006)** : This was the debut and only album by Plus 44, released on November 13, 2006. The band initially aimed for a more electronic sound, but after various recording attempts, a different style emerged. The album was considered a modest success, receiving some airplay and selling around 300,000 copies worldwide. ## curiosities
**Electronic Sound Ambition** : The original artistic vision for Plus 44 was to explore a more electronic sound. However, after a period of trial and error in recording, the band's sonic direction evolved into something different from this initial concept. "The original idea was for Plus 44 to be more electronic in its approach, but after a series of false starts and a lot of recording and secrecy, a different sound emerged." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Formation During Blink 182 Hiatus (Post-2005)** : This band was formed by Mark Hoppus and Travis Barker during Blink 182's indefinite hiatus, a period when Mark Hoppus and Tom DeLonge were not speaking. It was one of several solo/side projects that emerged during that time. "Bands like Tom's Angels and Airwaves and Mark and Travis's Plus 44." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EaGUC4av1R4
**Formation and Origin of Name** : Plus 44 was formed by Mark Hoppus and Travis Barker during Blink-182's hiatus, initially conceived as a more electronic project. The band's name, "Plus 44," was inspired by the international dialing code for the United Kingdom, where the idea for the band first germinated among its members. "Mark Hoppiss and Travis Barker formed a band that they called Plus 44. That name comes from the code you have to use to call the UK." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Formation by Mark and Travis (Post-Blink 182)** : Mark and Travis formed this new band after Blink 182 split, and it "did okay." "Well, Mark and Travis stuck together in a new band they called Plus 44, which also did okay." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yR3xc3ra5tE
**Plus 44 Tour Experience** : The tour supporting the album "When Your Heart Stop's Beating" was a highlight for Mark Hoppus and Travis Barker (members of Plus 44), providing them the opportunity to play small clubs again after a long time, which was a re-energizing experience. During this tour, Travis Barker broke his arm during a video shoot, causing him immense pain and forcing him to miss several gigs. "The best part about it was the tour where Mark and Tom got to play small clubs for the first time in a long time. That was really energizing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
