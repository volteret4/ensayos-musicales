## songs
**Casual Viewing (2000s)** : This song is taken from a line inspired by Genesis' "Lamb Lies Down on Broadway" and Marshall McLuhan's concept of casual viewing. Its theme explores how communication is changing the world, referencing the emerging internet era. The music video for the song was filmed globally using high-end Sony digital cameras in locations like Casablanca, Bangkok, and Essaouira, Morocco. "the song casual viewing is taken from a line, er, er, from Brad and I were huge Genesis fans from Lamb Lice down on Broadway. You know, Marshall McClue and casual viewing. So the theme of the song is how communication is changing the world." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9gdKha0PLg
**Easy Love (2005)** : Featured on the "Yes to Everything" album, this song was co-written by Dave Genn and was the band's last top 10 hit on Canadian FM radio. "Easy love is on Yes to Everything... that was the last sort of FM radio, top 10 we had in Canada. That was a Dave Riff, by the way." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9gdKha0PLg
**Food Loser** : This song directly relates to the common experience of dining out with a group where one person's order is incorrect, delayed, or cold. "We have a song called food loser which everybody can relate to this is when you're out dining with a group of people and somebody's order It gets wrong or it doesn't come where it's cold." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9gdKha0PLg
**Hey Brad** : This song is inspired by Dave Genn's initial impression of Brad Merritt when he first joined the band. Dave, not understanding Brad's personality at the time, asked, "where did you find that guy?", which became the basis for the song. "Dave first joined the band We did we're on the road and he didn't quite understand Brad's vibe and personality And he turned to me and go where did you find that guy? And so there's a song about Brad. It's called hey Brad." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9gdKha0PLg
**I Go Blind** : This older 5440 song gained significant popularity after being covered by Hootie & the Blowfish. The American band, who were fans from the 930 Club in Washington, DC, used it as a B-side on a cassette single and later it was included on the "Friends" soundtrack. Hootie had asked that it not be released as a single for the soundtrack, but radio stations in Boulder/Denver started playing it organically due to Hootie's popularity, defying their record label's attempt to push Hootie's new single. "An American band had blown up with their first album. And they decided to cover an old 5440 song. So this would be a good time to revisit I Go Blind." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9gdKha0PLg
**Love You All (1996)** : This song is featured on the 1996 album "Trusted by Millions," which achieved platinum status. "54 40 and love you all from their 1996 album, Trusted by Millions." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9gdKha0PLg
**Moving On** : This song is noted as the band's highest charting single. It was mentioned in conjunction with the title track of "Since When." "the band's highest charting single, Moving On." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9gdKha0PLg
**Options** : This song recounts a story from a show in Regina, where Blue Rodeo was also playing. Greg Keelor of Blue Rodeo visited 5440's dressing room, and upon hearing Neil Osborne express that he couldn't imagine doing anything else if he stopped making music, Keelor wisely responded, "nice to know your options are behind you," acknowledging a common dilemma for career musicians. "We have another song called options which is a story of When you're playing in Regina at the same time as Blue Rodeo and Keylor came over to check out our set... he goes nice to know your options are behind you." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9gdKha0PLg
**Same Guy Different Body** : This song depicts the common touring experience of a drunk individual who infiltrates the dressing room, initially confident and friendly, but then becomes aggressive and confrontational when perceived as being unwanted. "We have another song called same guy different body Which is that drunk person that somehow worms their way into the dressing room and is all brave And you can't get rid of them and then they get all dark and want to fight you because they there's not that drunk that they know you don't like them." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9gdKha0PLg

## curiosities
**"I Go Blind" on the Friends Soundtrack** : The 5440 song "I Go Blind" was included on the soundtrack for the TV show "Friends." This came about because the A&R executive assembling the soundtrack was the same person who had signed Hootie & the Blowfish to their label. Hootie had recorded "I Go Blind" but cut it from their first album, allowing its use on the soundtrack with the stipulation that it not be released as a single. "It ends up with a friend soundtrack, right?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9gdKha0PLg
**Ambivalence Towards Publishing Deals** : The band describes publishing deals as a "double-edged sword." While they provide upfront money (e.g., down payments for houses), musicians later realize they are giving away rights to half their future income after the initial recoupment. "you sell the rights to half of your songs... Wait a minute. I'm giving you half of all my money now. So it's double edged sword there." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9gdKha0PLg
**Band Longevity and Lineup Stability (1980-Present)** : 5440 has been active since 1980. Despite a few lineup changes (three by one account), the core members have remained with the group, demonstrating remarkable stability and dedication over four decades. "This band has been a going concern since 1980. And well, there have been a couple of lineup changes over the years, three by my account. The core of the group is still there." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9gdKha0PLg
**COVID-19 as an Impetus for New Music** : During the COVID-19 pandemic, Brad Merritt initiated Zoom calls with the band members, who live in different locations (three on islands, Dave in Vancouver). These calls, initially for reminiscing and telling stories, became the creative spark for their upcoming album, "West Coast Band," where stories from their history are transformed into songs. "Brad came up with the idea of, you know, doing a Zoom call. So we got them and this is this became the impetus, which is for the record that's going to come out next, because we just started telling stories." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9gdKha0PLg

**Decision for Artistic Autonomy** : Following a negative experience with their "Show Me" record, which they felt was too influenced by the record company's path of least resistance, the band resolved to "stick together as a band" and pursue their "own thing," rather than solely listening to external advice. "So I think, well, we're just going to stick together as a band and not listen to these people anymore." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9gdKha0PLg
**Digital Revolution and Industry Warnings (Early 2000s)** : During the production of the "Casual Viewing" album, Sony Music Canada president Rick Kamillary warned Neil Osborne that the traditional music industry was "over" due to digital changes like Napster, with CDs "giving away masters" and record companies seeking an "exit plan." This foreshadowed the dramatic shift in music consumption. "He goes, it's over, Neil, it's all over... We're giving away masters, CDs is giving away masters and then Napster and all that started to happen. He goes, I'm looking for my ex a plan." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9gdKha0PLg
**Dual Genre Identity: Classic vs. Modern Rock (Pre-COVID)** : Just before the COVID-19 pandemic, 5440 experienced a weekend where they played two distinct festivals. On one day, they were the "youngest band on the bill" playing alongside classic rock acts like Boston and REO Speedwagon, effectively "graduating to classic rock." The very next day, they occupied a middle slot with younger, "super cool bands" like Wintersleep, realizing they were simultaneously embraced by "both camps." "We played two different festivals... So when we were we were a band new band in a classic rock we played with Boston and our EO speed wagon... and then the next day we were sort of in the middle slot of With winter sleep and all these I don't even know the names of these bands the super cool bands." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9gdKha0PLg
**Early Anti-Commercial Stance (Pre-1998)** : Initially, 5440 was strongly against commercial endorsements, embodying a "nearly young don't sing for Miller don't sing for Bud kind of vibe." They turned down offers from cigarette companies and had contractual control over stage visibility, once refusing to play a festival opening for Pearl Jam until a Labatt's balloon was deflated, leading to a standoff. "we were totally against that... cigarette companies... nearly young don't sing for Miller don't sing for Bud kind of vibe." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9gdKha0PLg

"I'm 30 now shoot myself... I'm 40 shoot me... I'm 50. Amen. This is amazing. And now I'm 60. They decided to leverage their existing body of work from the "old system" while learning to navigate and adapt to the emerging "digital world." "because that was another point where we were saying, yeah, we could just pack it in now and find something else to do. **Loss of Control Over Venue Branding** : The band eventually lost the battle against corporate branding in venues. Molson, Bud, Labatt's, and cigarette companies began directly contracting with venues and promoters, making it impossible for bands to play without encountering corporate logos. This meant bands no longer had a choice in performing on a "Coca-Cola stage" or similar, losing the fight without realizing it. "the Moulson and the cigarette companies went directly to the venues, went directly to the promoters where if you wanted to play at all, you had to see Moulson or Bud or LaBaz or, you know, players light or whatever it was, or Coke, you didn't have a choice anymore." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9gdKha0PLg
