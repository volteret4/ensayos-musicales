## songs
**Africa (2018) – Toto Cover** : Weezer's cover of Toto's "Africa" was prompted by a Twitter campaign started in September 2017 by 14-year-old Mary Klim, who became obsessed with the idea after hearing the song on "Stranger Things." Despite no initial response from Rivers Cuomo and only a dismissive "I laughed" tweet from drummer Patrick Wilson, Weezer released their version about a week after a "troll" cover of Toto's "Rosanna." The "Africa" cover immediately "blew up," soared to the top of iTunes charts, and received worldwide radio play, becoming Weezer's biggest hit in over a decade. Toto, the original band, was "absolutely thrilled" by the cover, with guitarist Steve Lukather sending Mary Klim a video message of thanks, reportedly with Ringo Starr "cooking around in the background." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw
**Aneurysm (Nirvana Cover) (1998)** : Performed by Weezer under the alias "Goat Punishment" at a secret show in Hollywood in November 1998. This cover of Nirvana's B-side to "Snows like Teen Spirit" (likely "Smells Like Teen Spirit") is cited as evidence in the "Kurt is not dead" conspiracy theory. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pmKjZzypzvg
**Breed (Nirvana Cover) (1998)** : Weezer performed this Nirvana song under the name Goat Punishment in November 1998. The existence of a recording of this cover performance is presented by some as sufficient evidence to believe that Rivers Cuomo and Kurt Cobain are actually the same person, implying Kurt is not dead. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pmKjZzypzvg

## curiosities
**Easing Back into Music (2000)** : The Warped Tour was their way of easing themselves back into performing after Rivers Cuomo took time to attend Harvard. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Goat Punishment Shows (November 1998)** : In November 1998, Weezer played a secret show in Hollywood under the name "Goat Punishment," where they performed six Nirvana songs, including "Swapmate" and "Aneurysm." A few days later, they held a second Goat Punishment show, exclusively performing Oasis covers. These Nirvana cover performances are a central piece of "evidence" for the Kurt Cobain/Rivers Cuomo identity conspiracy. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pmKjZzypzvg
**Hiatus Between Albums (1996-2001)** : Their 2000 Warped Tour appearance occurred near the end of a hiatus between the Pinkerton album (1996) and The Green Album (2001). ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**History of Live Covers** : Weezer has a reputation for playfully covering other artists' material during their tours, including songs by Lady Gaga, Blur, Nirvana, Pink Floyd, Black Sabbath, The Clash, Green Day, Beach Boys, and Coldplay. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw
**Mary Klim's Twitter Campaign for Africa (2017-2018)** : In September 2017, 14-year-old Mary Klim from Cleveland initiated a Twitter campaign under the handle @WeezerCoverAfrica. Obsessed with Toto's "Africa" after hearing it on "Stranger Things," she tweeted Rivers Cuomo things like "it's about time you bless the rains down in Africa." This campaign slowly built a following, and while Rivers Cuomo never responded, drummer Patrick Wilson famously tweeted "I laughed." Weezer eventually responded in May 2018 by first releasing a cover of Toto's "Rosanna" as a "troll" before finally posting their version of "Africa" a week later, which became a massive hit. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw
**Rivers Cuomo / Kurt Cobain Identity Conspiracy** : This theory, which originated on Reddit, claims that Rivers Cuomo and Kurt Cobain are the same person, implying that Kurt Cobain is still alive. Proponents point to several "facts": Weezer's "Goat Punishment" show in November 1998 where they covered six Nirvana songs; rumors of Rivers Cuomo rehearsing for a Nirvana reunion with Kurt at Coachella around 2000; the fact that both Nirvana and Weezer shared a biographer (John D. Lurison); their shared record label; Weezer's tour with the Foo Fighters; and the claim that there were never any conflicting recording studio bookings or tour dates between the two bands. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pmKjZzypzvg
**Toto's Reaction to Africa Cover (2018)** : The original band Toto was "absolutely thrilled" by Weezer's cover of "Africa." Steve Lukather, Toto's guitarist, sent a video message thanking Mary Klim, the fan who started the Twitter campaign, for making it happen, with Ringo Starr "cooking around in the background." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw
