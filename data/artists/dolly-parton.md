## songs
**Coat of Many Colors** : This song tells the poignant story of Dolly Parton's mother sewing her a coat out of rags because that's all she could afford. Parton sang it with "zero shame," celebrating her humble roots. "When Dolly Parton sang about her coat of many colors, which tells us the story of her mother sewing her a coat out of rags because that is all she could afford to do, there is zero shame in it." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 23 - Women of Country Music in the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/023-country-women-72820-2-45-pm
**Great Balls of Fire (Cover)** : Dolly Parton is one of the artists mentioned who has covered Jerry Lee Lewis's classic hit. "It sold over a million copies in the first ten days it was out, and it became a classic that has been covered by everyone from Dolly Parton to Erasmith." ← Episode 66： ＂Great Balls of Fire＂ by Jerry Lee Lewis | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CVp5e4EWCqI
**Here You Come Again (1977)** : This song was a number one country hit and a number three pop hit, featuring a pedal steel guitar. Despite Porter Wagoner's hope that she would not lean too much towards pop, Parton explicitly aimed to broaden her fan base with this song, aligning with country radio's goal to attract a wider audience. "It was a number one country hit. It was a number three pop hit and that's exactly right. It is both." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 2 - Countryish Music of the 1970s (Encore) | https://www.ftr70.com/
**I Will Always Love You (1974)** : Dolly Parton wrote this song for Porter Wagoner when she sought to leave their performing partnership in 1974. She famously played it for him, causing him to cry and agree to release her from her contract, on the condition that he produce the record. "Now Dolly has told many times how she wrote, I will always love you for him and played it for him and he cried and he finally agreed to let her out of her contract if she would let him produce the record." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 23 - Women of Country Music in the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/023-country-women-72820-2-45-pm
**Jolene (1973)** : Released in Fall 1973, this song features a narrator pleading with another woman not to take her man. It was inspired by Dolly Parton's real-life husband flirting with a local bank clerk, while the visual image of Jolene came from a red-headed young fan with green eyes who asked for an autograph. It became a big hit for Parton, won a Grammy, and has been covered many times. "It was inspired when her real-life husband started flirting with a local bank clerk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw ← dolly-parton
**Nine to Five** : This song, while not considered purely country, exemplifies Dolly Parton's "country girl in the city vibe" and her successful crossover into pop. "Even though her music like nine to five wasn't really country she always has been and so she kind of has this country girl in the city vibe like we are viewing it all through a country girl's eyes." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 23 - Women of Country Music in the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/023-country-women-72820-2-45-pm

## curiosities
**"Iron Butterfly" Nickname and $1 Million Settlement** : Known as the "Iron Butterfly" for her business acumen and grace, Dolly Parton settled out of court with Porter Wagoner for $1 million in their contractual dispute. She later stated it took her a while to pay it off, noting that "he got the first million dollars that I ever made." "Now Dolly who is as tough as nails as a businesswoman they call her the iron butterfly but also is the epitome of grace. She settled out of court with him and she settled with him for $1 million." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 23 - Women of Country Music in the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/023-country-women-72820-2-45-pm
**Appearance vs. Talent** : While much attention was often given to Dolly Parton's appearance, especially in her younger years, she acknowledged that her looks alone could only take her so far. She emphasized that her voice and, particularly, her songwriting were the true essence of her career. "Her voice and especially her songwriting. That's the essence of her career." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 23 - Women of Country Music in the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/023-country-women-72820-2-45-pm
**Career Launch from Porter Wagoner's TV Show** : Dolly Parton's career was notably launched via country star Porter Wagoner's television show. "Wagner, whose TV show is notable for being the career launching pad for Dolly Parton." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 32 - Take Me to Church - Religion in 70s Popular Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-32-take-me-to-church-religion-in-70s-popular-music ← dolly-parton

**Collaboration Attempt with Emmylou Harris and Linda Ronstadt (Mid-1970s)** : Following an impressive performance on Dolly Parton's TV show with Emmylou Harris and Linda Ronstadt, the trio attempted to record an album together shortly after. However, it "didn't sound right" and faced contractual issues, delaying their successful "Trio" album until 1987. "No Dolly and Emmy Lou and Linda were as impressed as anyone with how they sounded and tried to make a record not long after that. It didn't sound right and I think there were some contractual issues." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 23 - Women of Country Music in the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/023-country-women-72820-2-45-pm
**Contractual Dispute and Lawsuit with Porter Wagoner** : In 1974, Dolly Parton sought to leave her contract with Porter Wagoner after seven years, despite having initially promised him five. Wagoner initially refused to release her, leading to a lawsuit in 1979 where he sued her for $3 million, seeking 15% of her net income and record royalties from 1974 to 1979, accusing her of being "selfish" and stabbing him in the back. "Wagner sued Dolly for $3 million in 1979 saying that she broke their contract and he wanted 15% of her net income between June 1974 and June 1979 and he wanted 15% of her record royalties." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 23 - Women of Country Music in the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/023-country-women-72820-2-45-pm
**Departure from Porter Wagoner (1974)** : In February 1974, Dolly Parton left the Porter Wagoner show, a move that was her launching pad but which she had outgrown, indicating her bigger plans for her career. She was nominated for CMA Female Vocalist of the Year in 1974. "Dolly Parton left the Porter Wagner show in February, which was no doubt her launching pad, but she'd outgrown him and she had bigger plans for her career." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 40 - Honestly Loving Olivia Newton-John | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-40-honestly-loving-olivia-newton-john
**Early Appearances on Porter Wagoner's TV Show** : Dolly Parton made her first appearances on Porter Wagoner's TV show, an early stage in her career. "She wanted to broaden her fan base, which is again exactly what country radio had been trying to do back when she was making her first appearances on Porter Wagner's TV show." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 2 - Countryish Music of the 1970s (Encore) | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Highly Honored Female Songwriter** : Dolly Parton is recognized as one of the greatest songwriters of all time and is the most honored woman in country music. She has received at least one Grammy nomination and some wins from each of the Academy Awards, the Grammy Awards, the Tony Awards, and the Golden Globes. "No woman has been honored more in the realm of country music than her." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw ← dolly-parton
**Imagination Library Foundation** : Dolly Parton founded an organization called the Imagination Library, dedicated to promoting childhood literacy. Since its inception, the organization has successfully donated one hundred million books to children. "Dolly Parton founded an organization called the Imagination Library since it began she's donated one hundred million books to children." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GO0K_N7Ys7I ← dolly-parton
**Influenced by Hank Williams** : Dolly Parton (referred to as "Dolip Arton" in the transcript) is cited as one of the musicians who followed the influential musical path established by Hank Williams. "trazando un camino que seguirán músicos como Johnny Cash, Dolip Arton o William Nelson." ← El genio roto del country： La leyenda de Hank Williams | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qczx7qWg4xM
**Intentional Move Towards Pop (Late 1970s)** : As the 1970s progressed, Dolly Parton intentionally shifted her music towards pop, a move that displeased many in country music, including Porter Wagoner. She hired Gary Klein, a producer who had worked with Barbra Streisand, signaling her deliberate effort to change her image. She famously countered critics by saying, "I'm not leaving country music I am taking it with me." "Dolly moved more toward pop as the 70s progressed which was again upsetting to many people in country music and Porter Wagner weighed in on it said he did not approve." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 23 - Women of Country Music in the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/023-country-women-72820-2-45-pm
**O.B. McClinton Impression** : O.B. McClinton included an impression of Dolly Parton in his live shows to demonstrate his versatility and to avoid being perceived as merely a "carbon copy" of Charlie Pride. "I go right into an impression of Deli Parton just so they wouldn't say that I'm a carbon copy of her either." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 53 - More than Charley Pride - African Americans in 70s Country | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Partnership with Porter Wagoner (1967-1974)** : Dolly Parton's career began closely linked with Porter Wagoner starting in 1967. She performed on his TV show, was part of his touring company, and recorded approximately 12 albums with him. Their collaboration was formidable, with Wagoner reportedly relying on her to sell records. "Starting in 1967 Dolly's career was linked with that of Porter Wagner. She had been a performer on the Porter Wagner TV show. She was part of his touring company and she made like 12 albums with him when she asked that he let her go in 1974." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 23 - Women of Country Music in the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/023-country-women-72820-2-45-pm
**Proposed Statue Replacement for KKK Leader** : In Tennessee, there is a desire to replace a bust of an old KKK leader with a statue of Dolly Parton, reflecting a similar sentiment seen in proposals to replace Confederate statues. "just like people in Tennessee, you want to take down a bust of an old KKK leader and replace it with one of Dolly Parton." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HoBx6s3JmVQ ← dolly-parton
**Rural Southern Origins** : Dolly Parton, like Loretta Lynn, was born poor in the rural South, specifically in Tennessee, about 14 years after Lynn. This shared background contributed to her ability to connect with fans through relatable storytelling in her songs. "Dolly Parton like Loretta Lynn was born poor in the rural south. Dolly is from Tennessee in about 14 years younger than Loretta." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 23 - Women of Country Music in the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/023-country-women-72820-2-45-pm
**Solo TV Show (1976)** : Dolly Parton launched her first solo TV show in 1976, which was a mix of country and pop, mirroring the broader industry trend. This show was an early indicator of her future success, including her eventual move into movies. "Dolly got her first solo TV show in 1976 but you know everybody had a variety show in the 70s so that's not a huge surprise but it is a sign of things to come for her." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 23 - Women of Country Music in the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/023-country-women-72820-2-45-pm
