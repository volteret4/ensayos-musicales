## curiosities
**Knife Purchase with Sid Vicious** : Steve Lucky, a member of the Canadian band The Viletones, accompanied Sid Vicious when he purchased the 007 hunting knife on 42nd Street that would later be used in Nancy Spungen's murder. "Sid had bought it with a couple of friends, including Steve Lucky of the Canadian band The Vile Tones at a place on 42nd Street." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6xygY-npCr4
**Screaming Fist (May 1977) – Possible First Canadian Punk Vinyl; Song Named William Gibson's Operation in Neuromancer** : The Viletones formed in Toronto in December 1976 and were at the Ramones' first Toronto show on September 26, 1976. They released a three-track, seven-inch single possibly as early as May 1977, making them contenders for the first Canadian band on vinyl. The key track is called "Screaming Fist." Science fiction writer William Gibson later named a fictional covert operation in his landmark 1984 novel *Neuromancer* — in which hackers attack Soviet infrastructure — "Operation Screaming Fist," directly after the Viletones song. Gibson also coined the term "cyberspace" two years prior. "Gibson was also the person who first coined the term cyberspace." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0X7TQMGgqbY
