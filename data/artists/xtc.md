## albums
**Drums and Wires (1978)** : This 1978 album features the song "Making Plans for Nigel," and is presented as an example of British New Wave. XTC, from Swindon west of London, embodied the happy, fun, sharp-suited, skinny-tied, choppy, sharp-sounding pop with clever and geeky lyrics that characterized some British New Wave bands. However, after the B-side release gained popularity, subsequent pressings of "Skylarking" were modified to include "Dear God" by deleting the track "Mermaid Smile." ## songs
**Dear God (1986)** : This song was initially an orphaned, non-album track recorded for the "Skylarking" album but excluded due to its anti-religious message. It was first released as the B-side to the single "Grass," where it gained popularity among college DJs. Its unexpected success led to its inclusion on later pressings of the "Skylarking" album, replacing "Mermaid Smile," and eventually becoming one of the band's biggest hits. "Dear God from XTC once an outtake B side, but later one of the biggest hits the band ever had." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QBCEmZ4Jheo
**Dear God (1986) – Dropped from Album; Nearly Destroyed XTC; Then Saved Skylarking Sales** : "Dear God" was written by Andy Partridge for the Skylarking album (produced by Todd Rundgren). Everyone involved — Partridge, Rundgren, and Virgin Records — agreed it should not be picked as the single, with Partridge calling it "an arrogant attempt to sum up man's relationship with God in just 3.5 minutes," and Virgin disliking the whiny child at the opening. It was dropped from the album entirely and relegated to B-side on the 7-inch and 12-inch of "Grass" (August 26, 1986). "Grass" bombed completely. Radio stations flipped the single and played "Dear God" instead; listener response was massive. XTC's American label called England in panic. The album was repressed with the track restored; it sold a quarter million copies. Religious groups protested; a Florida radio station received a bomb threat; an 18-year-old student in Birmingham, New York named Gary Pulles held a knife to a school secretary demanding the song be played over the PA repeatedly. "As a result, the record started to sell." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-7KiHetQxMQ
**Dear God (1987) – Bomb Threats, Hostage Incident at School** : The song "Dear God," considered blasphemous, generated bomb threats at a radio station and a hostage-taking incident at a school in which a student demanded that the song be played over the public address system. "Generated bomb threats at a radio station and a school hostage incident where a student demanded the song be played on the intercom." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f4TOWLYRnGs
**Grass (1986)** : "Grass" was an official single from XTC's "Skylarking" album. While the A-side "Grass" did not achieve significant popularity, its B-side, "Dear God," became a surprise hit after college DJs began playing it. "The only place that you could find it was on the B side of an official single from the album called Grass. Grass however went nowhere." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QBCEmZ4Jheo

## curiosities
**Featured on MTV** : XTC was one of the New Wave bands whose music videos were regularly broadcast on MTV, helping to shape the channel's early identity and popularize the genre. "They played all the groups that we've talked about in many more. Eurythmics, XTC, Soft Sale, Adamance, Human League, Bowah Wow, and The Go Go's..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**New Wave Powerpop** : XTC is listed as one of the bands whose musical output can be categorized under the new wave umbrella of Powerpop. Their style contributed to the genre's presence within the emerging alternative rock scene, characterized by clever songwriting and melodic hooks. "We can probably include the jam, the smiths, XTC, squeeze, and the vapors." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hstJ8M2laho
**Prolific Output and Creative Shift** : XTC was a prolific band, often releasing at least one album per year. In 1985, they sought a creative departure from their usual post-punk quirkiness, embracing 60s psychedelia and aiming for a more fun, less serious approach to songwriting, which led to the creation of their pseudonymous project, The Dukes of Stratosphear. "XTC was a pretty prolific band in their day, releasing at least an album a year, but in 1985 they decided to try something different." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=itK5fEqnA5E
**Times Square Soundtrack Contribution (1980)** : XTC was included on the soundtrack for the 1980 movie *Time Square*. This soundtrack was noteworthy for being one of the initial collections to feature the nascent alternative music movement of the time, such as punk and new wave. "The soundtrack featured the pretenders, Roxy music, Gary Newman, XTC, Joe Jackson." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM
