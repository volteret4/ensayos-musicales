# artist - Yoko Ono

## genres
- Outsider Music (comparison)

## songs
**Give Peace a Chance (1969)** : This anthem was recorded on June 1, 1969, in room 1742 of the Queen Elizabeth Hotel in Montreal, during the week-long bed-in for peace she co-hosted with John Lennon. André Perry facilitated the recording by setting up a simple four-microphone, four-track recorder system. "on June 1st, 1969, in room 1742, the Anthem give peace a chance was recorded." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2IDbX5rNT1E

**Contribution to Flaming Lips Blood-Infused Vinyl** : Yoko Ono was among the collaborators, including Chris Martin and Kesha, who were asked to contribute their "precious bodily fluid" for ten special editions of The Flaming Lips' 2012 Record Store Day vinyl, "Hetty Fwins," which featured traces of blood in its clear vinyl. "Yoko Ono?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sQrbNUDZuOQ
**Montreal Bed-In for Peace (1969)** : Yoko Ono and her partner John Lennon held a week-long non-violent "bed-in for peace" protest in Montreal from May 26 to June 1, 1969, as a novel form of activism against the Vietnam War. This event was a successor to their Amsterdam bed-in and was relocated to Montreal after Lennon was denied entry to the US and a brief, uncomfortable stay in the Bahamas. "On May 26th, June 1st, 1969, he held a bed in for peace protest in Montreal." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2IDbX5rNT1E
**Mother of Sean Lennon** : Yoko Ono is the mother of Sean Lennon. "Sean Lennon is the son of John and Yoko." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MmXDbXI6Sqk
**Notable Visitors During Bed-In (1969)** : During their Montreal bed-in, Yoko Ono and John Lennon were visited by prominent figures such as Timothy Leary, Tommy Smothers, Dick Gregory, Murray the K, Al Capp, Alan Ginsburg, and then-Prime Minister Pierre Trudeau. "During the week in Montreal, the couple was visited by Timothy Leary, Tommy Smothers, Dick Gregory, Murray the Kay, Al Cap, Alan Ginsburg, and of course, Prime Minister Pierre Trudeau." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2IDbX5rNT1E
**Permission for Hotel Suite (1991)** : In 1991, Yoko Ono granted permission to the Queen Elizabeth Hotel to begin renting room 1742 in John Lennon's name. Subsequently, the rooms they stayed in were combined and renovated into a large suite, now known as the "John Lennon and Yoko Ono suite." "Years later, in 1991, the Hotel received permission from Yoko Ono to begin renting room 1742 in Lenin's name." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2IDbX5rNT1E
**Queen Elizabeth Hotel Stay (1969)** : Yoko Ono and John Lennon arrived in Montreal on May 26, 1969, and resided in rooms 1738, 1740, 1742, and 1744 at the Queen Elizabeth Hotel for their bed-in. This location was chosen for its proximity to New York, aiding American press coverage. "John and Yoko flew to Montreal on May 26th and stayed in the room 1738, 1740, 1742, and 1744 at the Queen Elizabeth Hotel." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2IDbX5rNT1E
