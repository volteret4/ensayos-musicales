**Lo-Fi Distribution and Cult Status (1980s)** : Daniel Johnston, who suffered from bipolar disorder and frequently visited psychiatric institutions, recorded his songs at home using a $59 Sanyo tape machine propped on his parents' piano. Lacking a way to copy tapes, he would re-perform songs for each copy. He distributed these cassettes while working at McDonald's in Austin, Texas, by dropping them into Big Mac bags, or by giving/selling them to people on the street, which helped him become a local cult favorite due to the honest, childlike, and deeply moving nature of his music. "Tape by tape. McDonald's bag by McDonald's bag, Daniel became a local cult favorite." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jq-e-fZxXOs
**Mental Illness and Early Career** : Daniel Johnston has openly dealt with manic depression and schizophrenia, experiencing "buds of delusion" that raised genuine fears for his safety and that of others. He began his recording career by making cassettes on a $59 Stenial Boombox and gained attention by distributing them to everyone he met under his self-operated label, Stress. "Like Wesley Willis, Daniel has said to deal with mental illness. Manic depression and schizophrenia." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
**Multifaceted Artistic Endeavors** : In addition to his music, Daniel Johnston is also an accomplished painter, has helped develop an iPhone game titled "Hi, How Are You?", and has been the subject of multiple documentaries. These diverse artistic contributions showcase his expansive creative talents beyond songwriting. "And not only does he make music, but Daniel also paints. **Outsider Music Figure and Mainstream Recognition (Early 1990s)** : Throughout the 1980s, Daniel Johnston released records that cemented his status as a low-fi hero and an important figure in "outsider music," a genre characterized by technically imperfect but passionately delivered performances. His unique approach and growing underground reputation eventually led to an MTV report on local music scenes. His fame significantly expanded in the early 1990s when Kurt Cobain was seen publicly wearing a Daniel Johnston T-shirt, elevating him to a major cult figure. "And in the early 1990s, when Kurt Cobain was spotted wearing a Daniel Johnston T-shirt, he became a major cult figure." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jq-e-fZxXOs
**Professional Recording and Worsening Health** : By 1988, Daniel Johnston was well-regarded enough to record professionally in New York, but concurrently, his mental health deteriorated significantly. A notable incident involved him becoming convinced that "Casper, the friendly ghost," had stolen a plane key during a flight with his pilot father, leading to a crash landing and his subsequent commitment. "By 1988, he was well regarded enough to be given a shot of recording professionally in New York. At the same time though, his mental health got worse." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
**Refusal of Electra Deal** : Daniel Johnston famously refused a major record deal from Electra, explaining his decision by stating that Metallica, who were signed to Electra, were "obviously possessed by Satan." He also fired his manager who brought the deal, believing the manager was "obviously possessed too," demonstrating his unique worldview impacting his career choices. "Electra came calling with a major deal, but Daniel refused to sign. Why? Well, because Metallica was on Electra and they were obviously possessed by Satan." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
