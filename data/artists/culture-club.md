## curiosities
**Early Alternative Classification** : Culture Club was initially considered an alternative band, despite their new wave sound having stripped away much of the punk DNA. "Culture Club and WAM, both of which, by the way, were considered to be alternative bands in the very beginning." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
**MTV Prominence and Style** : Culture Club, fronted by Boy George and his unique look, was a British band that gained significant airtime on MTV, becoming a key part of the "second British invasion" that characterized the channel's early programming. "Culture Club with Boyd George's unique look..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**Pioneers of New Romanticism** : Culture Club was identified as a New Romantic group that rose from the British club circuit, specifically Billies and The Blitz Club, which were instrumental in the development of synth-based new music and associated subcultures. "One such offshoot was known as New Romantic, which had its roots in a couple of British clubs dedicated to new music. One was called Billies, and the other was the Blitz Club. This was the breeding site of groups like Spandovalet, Visage, and Culture Club." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
**Prominence in New Pop** : Culture Club, fronted by Boy George, was a prime example of a New Pop band. Their unique visual presentation, amplified by music videos, helped them become cultural phenomena in the early 1980s. "Think Boy George and Culture Club, ABC in their Sharp Tuxedos, Orange Juice, a band of Scotland featuring Edwin Collins, Asda Camer was another, Heaven 17 was still another." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4
