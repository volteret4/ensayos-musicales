**In Utero (1993)** : The cover artwork for Nirvana's 1993 album *In Utero*, designed by Art Director Robert Fisher, features a winged figure on a pale, cracked background. This angelic figure is depicted as ripped open, exposing bare bones and organs, reflecting Kurt Cobain's fascination with human anatomy as art, creating an image that is both peaceful and disturbing. The figure is actually a "transparent anatomical mannequin," a life-sized, anatomically correct female model used for teaching anatomy, which was found at a medical supply store in the Mall of America in Minneapolis. Nirvana used several such mannequins as stage props for the *In Utero* tour. The back cover, photographed by Charles Peterson, displays various items from the medical store and Kurt's personal collection, including plastic fetuses, body parts, a turtle shell, orchids, and lilies, all laid out on his living room floor. These items incorporate symbols from *The Woman's Dictionary of Symbols and Sacred Objects*, aligning with Kurt's interest in artwork representing themes of sex, women, birth, and death, which also hints at the raw music within the album. "The angelic figure is ripped open. We see bare bones and exposed organs, but not to Kurt s fascination with human anatomy as art. She was taken to Frant's trailer and tortured with whips, razors, and a blowtorch while tied to a pulley apparatus, but managed to escape from his van during a stop for gas. Frant was arrested the next day and is serving two consecutive 75-year sentences. The song was initially titled "Hitchhiker" and first recorded during the Blue EP sessions, then refined during the Nevermind sessions, with the album version being a remixed demo. During a 1991 Nevermind tour performance of "Polly," two audience members sexually assaulted a girl, an incident Kurt Cobain condemned in the liner notes of the Incestosite album.
