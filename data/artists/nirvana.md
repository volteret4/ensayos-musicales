**In Utero (1993)** : The cover artwork for Nirvana's 1993 album *In Utero*, designed by Art Director Robert Fisher, features a winged figure on a pale, cracked background. This angelic figure is depicted as ripped open, exposing bare bones and organs, reflecting Kurt Cobain's fascination with human anatomy as art, creating an image that is both peaceful and disturbing. The figure is actually a "transparent anatomical mannequin," a life-sized, anatomically correct female model used for teaching anatomy, which was found at a medical supply store in the Mall of America in Minneapolis. Nirvana used several such mannequins as stage props for the *In Utero* tour. The back cover, photographed by Charles Peterson, displays various items from the medical store and Kurt's personal collection, including plastic fetuses, body parts, a turtle shell, orchids, and lilies, all laid out on his living room floor. These items incorporate symbols from *The Woman's Dictionary of Symbols and Sacred Objects*, aligning with Kurt's interest in artwork representing themes of sex, women, birth, and death, which also hints at the raw music within the album. "The angelic figure is ripped open. We see bare bones and exposed organs, but not to Kurt s fascination with human anatomy as art. The status of a potential lawsuit is unclear, with some sources claiming it was dropped after Kurt Cobain's suicide, and others suggesting Killing Joke opted not to sue for personal and financial reasons. Despite past issues, Dave Grohl has since performed and recorded with Killing Joke, signaling a resolution. **"In Utero" Title Evolution** : Kurt Cobain initially wanted to title Nirvana's 1993 album "I Hate Myself and Want to Die," claiming it was a joke. **Church of Kurt Cobain (1996) – Media Hoax Won a Contest to Fool The Oregonian** : On May 29, 1996, a man calling himself "Reverend Jim Dillon" announced the founding of the Church of Kurt Cobain in Portland, Oregon, using Nirvana song lyrics as liturgy. The story received front-page coverage in *The Oregonian*, was picked up by MTV, and led the ABC Evening News with Peter Jennings. In reality it was a hoax — a local radio station had run a contest to see if listeners could trick *The Oregonian* into printing a fake story, and the winner was a man named Jerry Catel, who became "the Reverend Jim Dillon." His secondary intent was to make a statement about media shallowness and celebrity idolatry. "It was a hoax. The truth is that all this originated with a local radio station which staged a contest to see if listeners could get the Oregonian to print a fake story." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BrQQHqbQG0Y

**Dave Grohl Joined Nirvana Because His Band's Bassist Broke Up with His Girlfriend (1990)** : Dave dropped out of high school to tour with DC punk band Scream for about three years. Dave flew to Seattle on September 21, 1990; four days later he auditioned and passed. Dave later wrote a song about Skeater Thompson called "Just Another Story About Skeater Thompson," available only on a cassette-only release made under the name "Late" in late 1990, titled *Pocket Watch*. **Dave Grohl's Father Covered the Kent State Massacre (May 1970)** : In 1970, the Grohl family lived in Niles, Ohio (near Columbus). James Grohl won several awards for his on-the-spot reporting, earning a major promotion and reassignment to Washington D.C., where Dave grew up and discovered music.
