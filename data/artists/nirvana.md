## albums
**In Utero** : An album for which another version of Dave Grohl's song "Marigold" was recorded.
**In Utero (1993)** : Album recorded by Steve Albini.
**In Utero (1993) – Recording Sessions (February 1992)** : Recording sessions for this album took place around February 1992, during which Dave Grohl recorded his version of "Mera Gold."
**Last Ever Completed Song** : Recorded at Robert Lang Studios north of Seattle.
**Nevermind** : Their major label debut album. Kurt Cobain decided to relocate the band to Butch Vig's studios in Wisconsin for demos, and then asked Vig to produce the entire album.
**Nevermind (1991)** : In the fall of 1991, this album was certified to be selling 300,000 copies a week for months, a sales figure that shocked the record industry after SoundScan was implemented.
**Nevermind (1991) – Chart-topping Album** : Released in September 1991, this album rapidly became "the biggest thing in the world."

## songs
**Come As You Are (1994)** : Played at their last show in Munich on March 1, 1994, with the power going out during the song.
**Get Together (1967)** : Originally performed by The Youngbloods and written by Chad Powers. Lyrics from this song were incorporated into Nirvana's "While Pissing."
**Heart Shaped Box (1993) – CD Single** : The CD single version of this song included "Mera Gold" as its last track.
**Last Song Played Live (1994)** : The final, unnamed song of their 23-song setlist at Terminal 1, Munich, on March 1, 1994.
**Love Buzz (1988) – First Ever Single** : This song was performed at Dave Grohl's first official gig with Nirvana on October 11, 1990, at the North Shore Surf Club in Olympia, Washington.
**Marigold (1990)** : The only original song in Nirvana's discography not written or sung by Kurt Cobain. Dave Grohl wrote and recorded it in 1990, initially releasing it on an indie set under the name Late. Another version was recorded for *In Utero*.
**My Best Friend's Girl (The Cars cover) (1994)** : Opened Nirvana's last show at Terminal 1 in Munich on March 1, 1994.
**Smells Like Teen Spirit** : The title of this song originated from Kathleen Hanna's comment to Kurt Cobain in 1991 that he smelled "sweet, almost like a particular type of deodorant marketed to teens." Cobain used this remark as the song's title.
**Smells Like Teen Spirit (1994)** : Skipped from the setlist at their last show in Munich, possibly due to Kurt Cobain's vocal issues.
**While Pissing (1991)** : A song from the "Nevermind" album, where Kurt Cobain shares songwriting credit with Chad Powers of Quicksilver Messenger Service. This is due to the inclusion of yelping at the beginning of the song, which consists of lyrics from "Get Together" by The Youngbloods, a song written by Powers.

## curiosities
**"Mera Gold" – Only Non-Cobain Song** : "Mera Gold," recorded by Dave Grohl during the "In Utero" sessions, is the only song in the entire Nirvana canon that was neither written nor sung by Kurt Cobain. It did not make the album but was released as the last track on the "Heart Shaped Box" CD single and later on the "With The Lights Out" box set.
**Alt Rock "With Training Wheels"** : made great music and lured millions into the alternative nation with a "different yet familiar approach to traditional guitar rock."
**Continuing Popularity in 21st Century** : remains extremely popular with fans despite ceasing to exist, making it harder for new artists to break through.
**Dave Grohl's First Gig (October 11, 1990)** : Dave Grohl's inaugural official performance with Nirvana took place at the North Shore Surf Club in Olympia, Washington. The 300-person venue sold out, Dave broke his snare drum due to intense playing, and the band caused the venue's power to blow out three times.
**Dave Grohl's Joining** : Dave Grohl joined the band after its bassist Krist Novoselic received his phone number from Buzz Osborne of The Melvins. Grohl packed his drums in a cardboard box and traveled up the coast to join the "weird little band."
**Dave Grohl's Sole Songwriting Credit** : Dave Grohl's "Marigold" is the single original song in their entire discography that was not written or sung by Kurt Cobain.
**Impact of Death (1994)** : Found dead on April 8, 1994, which coincided with the release of The Offspring's Smash; his death contributed to a shift in public taste away from the "heaviness and bleakness" of grunge, making way for pop punk.
**Impact on Canadian Music (early 1990s)** : Their breakthrough ("the Nirvana thing happened") in the early nineties directly led to a "rush to sign Canadian bands" by labels like Sub Pop, creating opportunities for bands like Headstones due to their "raw angst."
**Influenced by My Bloody Valentine** : Counted among the bands significantly influenced by My Bloody Valentine's innovative use of guitar noise and walls of sound.
**Kurt Cobain's Admiration for Kathleen Hanna** : Kurt Cobain idolized Kathleen Hanna of Bikini Kill.
**Kurt Cobain's Anti-Moshing Stance** : Initially stage-dived himself, but later grew to hate the "jocks" who moshed whenever Nirvana played, which helped sour his view of being in a famous band.
**Kurt Cobain's Death** : Died on April 8, 1994. The news of his death put the formation of the band Garbage on hold, as their producer Butch Vig had also produced "Nevermind."
**Last Show at Terminal 1 (1994)** : Nirvana's final concert was at Terminal 1 in Munich, Germany, on March 1, 1994, during a European tour. Kurt Cobain was sick with bronchitis. Tickets cost 35 Deutsch marks. The power went out during "Come As You Are." They played a 23-song set, skipping "Smells Like Teen Spirit."
**Limited Songwriting Credits (Nevermind)** : Kurt Cobain shares songwriting credit on only two tracks on the "Nevermind" album.
**Major Label Signing Lawsuit (1991)** : Upon signing their major label deal with DGC Records, Dave Grohl faced a lawsuit for $40,000 from an entity associated with his former band Scream, alleging breach of contract.
**Meshed Rhythm Section** : Dave Grohl on drums and Krist Novoselic on bass are highlighted for how well they "meshed together."
**Nevermind Royalty Dispute (Spring 1992)** : After "Nevermind" achieved massive global success, Kurt Cobain demanded a renegotiation of royalty arrangements in the spring of 1992. He initially sought 90% of songwriting royalties, backdated to the album's September 1991 release, controversially calling Dave Grohl and Chris Novoselic "greedy." After significant contention, Kurt was eventually bargained down to 75%.
**Pat Smear's Tenure** : Pat Smear played guitar with Nirvana.
**Pioneered Grunge Gold Rush** : Its massive sales after SoundScan led record labels to pivot and initiate the "Grunge Gold Rush."
**Pivotal Grunge Band** : especially instrumental in Alt Rock's shift towards hard guitar rock.
**Support for L7** : Along with Pearl Jam, Rage Against The Machine, and Red Hot Chili Peppers, they became fans of L7 after playing pro-choice events and offered support to the all-female band.
**Tour Cancellation and Kurt Cobain's Death (1994)** : The rest of Nirvana's European tour was cancelled after the Munich show. Two days later, Kurt Cobain overdosed in a Rome hotel room in an apparent suicide attempt, and he died by shotgun a month after that.
**Vigorous Mosh Pits (1990s)** : Saw vigorous mosh pits at their shows during the 1990s Alt Rock era.
