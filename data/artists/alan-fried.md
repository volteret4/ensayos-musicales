# artist - Alan Fried

## curiosities
**Coining the Term "Rock and Roll" (1951)** : Fried is credited with coining the term "Rock and Roll" for the music he played, drawing on African-American slang for sex that had been used on records since at least the 1920s. He defined the genre as "swing music with a modern name" and likely used the phrase as a "secret code" that teenagers understood while their parents remained unaware of its sexual connotations ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f2KA97T4u08
**Moondog Infringement Lawsuit (1956)** : Fried originally called his radio show "The Moondog House" and referred to himself as the "King of the Moon Doggers" after co-opting the title from Moondog’s "Moondog Symphony". In 1956, Moondog successfully sued Fried and the radio station WINS; Fried was ordered to pay $6,000 in damages and permanently cease using the name. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f2KA97T4u08
**Playing "Race Records" (1951)** : A white radio DJ who started his career in Cleveland and became famous for playing "race records" (songs by Black artists) in 1951. These records were considered "forbidden fruit" for many young white teenagers at the time. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f2KA97T4u08
**Unsuccessful Trademark Attempt** : Following the success of the term, Fried attempted to trademark "Rock and Roll" to maintain exclusive use of the phrase, though this effort ultimately failed. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f2KA97T4u08
