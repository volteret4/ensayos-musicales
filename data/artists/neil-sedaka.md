## curiosities
**Early Songwriting and Performing Career** : Neil Sedaka briefly dated Carole King and, with Howard Greenfield, co-wrote "While I Dream," which his group, The Tokens, recorded after securing a deal. Sedaka and Greenfield later became successful staff songwriters, and Sedaka also became a successful performer, with his first hit, "Oh! Carol," being written about Carole King. "Siddak had briefly dated her and had co-written that song himself with Howard Greenfield, and his group got a record deal under the name The Tokens." ← Episode 89： ＂Will You Love Me Tomorrow？＂ by the Shirelles | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Kns2HGcuSg
**Introducing Carole King to Aldon Music** : King bumped into Sedaka at 1650 Broadway, and he told her she should meet Al Nevins and Don Kirshner, the owners of Aldon Music, leading to Goffin and King's significant songwriting contract. **Scopatone Involvement** : Neil Sedaka was among the artists who produced films for the Scopatone machines. These machines were a form of video jukebox popular in the mid-1960s, offering short, often color, musical films in public venues.
