**Post-Beatles Collaboration with McCartney (1973)** : George Martin, who built and ran Air Studios in London, agreed to produce the recording sessions for Paul McCartney's "Live and Let Die" theme. This marked a significant reunion, as it was the first time Martin had worked with a Beatle since the conclusion of the "Abbey Road" sessions in 1969. Martin later compelled the Bond producers to use McCartney's version as the title sequence. "Martin agreed to produce the sessions marking this as the first time since the end of the Abbey Road sessions that he got to work with a beetle." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K0pgJ7XAlpo
**Producer's Ultimatum for "Live and Let Die" (1973)** : George Martin delivered an ultimatum to the Bond film producers regarding Paul McCartney's "Live and Let Die" theme. He informed them that if they wanted the "very good theme song," they would have to use Paul's version for the title sequence, otherwise they would need to find someone else for their movie. Faced with time constraints, the producers, either Harry Saltzman or Albert Broccoli, conceded. "When it was done George Martin went to the producers and said look we've got a very good theme song for you but the only way you're going to get it is if you use Paul's version as the title sequence." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K0pgJ7XAlpo
