# artist - Shirley Bassey

## genres
- Theater singer
- Welsh singer

## songs
**Diamonds Are Forever (1971) – Title Theme** : Shirley Bassey was called back to sing the theme for the 1971 James Bond film "Diamonds Are Forever." This marked her second time performing a Bond theme, solidifying her association with the franchise after her earlier success with "Goldfinger." "Shirley Bassie was called back." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K0pgJ7XAlpo
**Goldfinger (1964) – Title Theme** : Shirley Bassey, a Welsh singer with a background in theater, performed the theme song for the 1964 James Bond film "Goldfinger." The song became a top 40 hit despite initial strong hatred from producer Harry Saltzman, who called it "the worst blanking song I've ever heard in my blanking life." Its success led to Bassey becoming the first singer to perform multiple Bond themes. "The singer is Shirley Bassie, a Welsh singer who started in the theater." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K0pgJ7XAlpo
**Moonraker (1979) – Title Theme** : Shirley Bassey returned for her third James Bond theme, singing the title song for "Moonraker" in 1979. Her performance continued the trend of female vocalists for the franchise's themes during the Roger Moore era. "Shirley Bassi again from Winrecker in 1979." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K0pgJ7XAlpo

## curiosities
**First Multi-Bond Theme Singer** : Shirley Bassey was the first artist to sing multiple James Bond themes, starting with "Goldfinger" in 1964. She later returned for "Diamonds Are Forever" in 1971 and "Moonraker" in 1979, establishing a recurring presence in the franchise's musical history. "It would also be the first of three Shirley Bassie Bond themes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K0pgJ7XAlpo
