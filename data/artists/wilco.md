## albums
**A Ghost is Born (2003)** : This album was recorded during a period when Jeff Tweedy was severely struggling with crippling migraines, panic attacks, and a painkiller addiction, leading him to believe he was going to die. The album includes a track that audibly represents the onset of a migraine. "When Wilco was recording their 2003 album, A Ghost is Born, things were really, really bad. Vicodin attacks, migraines, benches on painkillers and at one point Jeff was convinced he was going to die." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uzIMWVbi-GM
**Sky Blue Sky (2007)** : This album, released after Jeff Tweedy sought rehab for his painkiller addiction and migraines, is described as reflecting his improved state of health and recovery, indicating a more positive and stable period in his life. "This can be heard on the album that came after A Ghost is Born, which was Sky Blue Sky in 2007." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uzIMWVbi-GM

## songs
**Either Way (2007)** : This song from the album *Sky Blue Sky* is presented as an example of Jeff Tweedy's music after he sought rehab for his migraines and painkiller addiction, reflecting his improved state. "This is a song called Either Way." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uzIMWVbi-GM
**Less Than You Think (2003)** : Featured on the album *A Ghost is Born*, this song starts as a normal ballad. Three minutes in, it fades out, and for the next 12 minutes, it provides an audible representation of what Jeff Tweedy felt when one of his severe migraines began. "There's a track on this album entitled Less Than You Think. It starts like a normal ballad but three minutes in it fades out and for the next 12 minutes we hear something like this. It's supposed to be an audible representation of what Jeff felt when one of these awful migraines started." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uzIMWVbi-GM
**Record Labels (2011) – Nick Lowe Cover** : This song is a cover of a Nick Lowe track from 1991. Wilco released their version in 2011 on DBPM Records, their own independent company. They received marketing assistance from Anti-Records, which is a sister label to Epitaph Records, and Anti-Records has a distribution deal with Republic Records, a sublabel of the Universal Music Group. "It came out in 2011 on DBPM Records, which is another indie company run by Wilco themselves." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AcADOuQUiAQ

**Migraines and Painkiller Addiction** : Jeff Tweedy has suffered from debilitating migraines since childhood, which led to an addiction to Vicodin, Oxycontin, and later stolen morphine. His Vicodin use began in the late 90s, where painkillers became necessary for him to feel normal and also suppressed his panic attacks. "Jeff's condition, which has been with him since he was a kid, led to an addiction to Vicodin and Oxycontin and eventually stolen morphine. The Vicodin started in the late 90s and eventually he only felt normal when he was on painkillers. It also kind of helped that they suppressed his panic attacks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uzIMWVbi-GM
**Rehab and Recovery** : Jeff Tweedy eventually sought rehab to address both his crippling migraines and his addiction to painkillers. While panic attacks still occur, he is now in a much better place overall, with his recovery evident in subsequent musical works. "The good news is that Jeff finally sought rehab for both problems, the migraines and the painkiller addiction. The panic attack still come but overall he's in a lot better place." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uzIMWVbi-GM
