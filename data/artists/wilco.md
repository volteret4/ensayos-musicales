**Less Than You Think (2003)** : Featured on the album *A Ghost is Born*, this song starts as a normal ballad. Three minutes in, it fades out, and for the next 12 minutes, it provides an audible representation of what Jeff Tweedy felt when one of his severe migraines began. "There's a track on this album entitled Less Than You Think. It starts like a normal ballad but three minutes in it fades out and for the next 12 minutes we hear something like this. It's supposed to be an audible representation of what Jeff felt when one of these awful migraines started." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uzIMWVbi-GM ← wilco ← wilco

**Migraines and Painkiller Addiction** : Jeff Tweedy has suffered from debilitating migraines since childhood, which led to an addiction to Vicodin, Oxycontin, and later stolen morphine. His Vicodin use began in the late 90s, where painkillers became necessary for him to feel normal and also suppressed his panic attacks. "Jeff's condition, which has been with him since he was a kid, led to an addiction to Vicodin and Oxycontin and eventually stolen morphine. The Vicodin started in the late 90s and eventually he only felt normal when he was on painkillers. It also kind of helped that they suppressed his panic attacks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uzIMWVbi-GM ← wilco ← wilco
**Rehab and Recovery** : Jeff Tweedy eventually sought rehab to address both his crippling migraines and his addiction to painkillers. While panic attacks still occur, he is now in a much better place overall, with his recovery evident in subsequent musical works. "The good news is that Jeff finally sought rehab for both problems, the migraines and the painkiller addiction.
