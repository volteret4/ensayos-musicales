**Superfly (1972) - Original Motion Picture Soundtrack** : Curtis Mayfield eagerly wrote this soundtrack to comment on life in the ghetto and provide perspective on both drug users and pushers. It sold five million copies and can stand alone from the movie. Music critic Vince Aletti noted that Mayfield moved away from typical soundtrack conventions, with songs commenting on the film's action rather than merely illustrating it, and lyrics not restricted to the title track. The album blends influences from funk, blues, jazz, and rock, reflecting the diverse nature of American cities. "This Mayfield said that he was eager to write for the Superfly soundtrack because he wanted to add to the commentary on life in the ghetto and offer a glimpse of what the victims and the drug pushers both may have felt." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 28 - Soundtracks of the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/028-soundtracks-of-the-70s-12221-5-45-pm

## curiosities
**Black Music for Black People** : Curtis Mayfield articulated a clear philosophy regarding his music's intended audience and purpose. He stated that black musicians were primarily creating "black music for black people," implying that commercial crossover to white audiences, while sometimes occurring, was not the main objective. This perspective reflected the Black Power movement's emphasis on self-determination and creating art that served the black community first. "Curtis Mayfield said that black musicians were making black music for black people and if it crossed over it did. But that was not the point." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 22 - Power to the People - The Music of the Black Power Movement | https://seventies.libsyn.com/022-power-to-the-people-62520-11-01-am ← curtis-mayfield
**Death** : Curtis Mayfield died at age 57 in 1999, just months after his solo induction into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
