# artist - The New Seekers

## songs
**Coca-Cola Commercial Jingle (Adapted)** : The group achieved a massive worldwide hit single by adapting a jingle from a Coca-Cola commercial. This song later became the subject of a lawsuit against Oasis for similarities to their song "Shaker Maker." "A group who took a jingle for a Coke commercial... and they turned it into a massive worldwide hit single." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L09R2evjyMc
**I'd Like to Teach the World to Sing (1971)** : This song, released in 1970, was a "massive worldwide hit single," with a cover version also popular around the same time. It was "derived from one of the most popular and most famous Coca-Cola commercials of all time." The melody was later found to be similar to Oasis's "Shaker Maker," leading to a lawsuit against Oasis. "Not only was this a massive worldwide hit single, a cover version was also a big hit around the same time. And it was all derived from one of the most popular and most famous Coca-Cola commercials of all time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rfObVld9rIE

## curiosities
**Lawsuit Against Oasis** : The New Seekers successfully sued Oasis due to the song "Shaker Maker" having a melody eerily similar to their worldwide hit, which originated from a Coca-Cola commercial jingle. The court case resulted in Oasis being ordered to make changes to their song. "And yes, there were similarities and yes, there was a lawsuit." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L09R2evjyMc
