# artist - The New Seekers

## members
- British and Australian singers (quintet)

## songs
**Coca-Cola Commercial Jingle (Adapted)** : The group achieved a massive worldwide hit single by adapting a jingle from a Coca-Cola commercial. This song later became the subject of a lawsuit against Oasis for similarities to their song "Shaker Maker." "A group who took a jingle for a Coke commercial... and they turned it into a massive worldwide hit single." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L09R2evjyMc ← the-new-seekers
**I'd Like to Teach the World to Sing (1970)** : A "massive worldwide hit single" from 1970, which was itself a cover version and directly derived from "one of the most popular and most famous Coca-Cola commercials of all time." Its melody was found to be similar to Oasis's "Shaker Maker," leading to a successful lawsuit against Oasis. "Not only was this a massive worldwide hit single, a cover version was also a big hit around the same time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4cZfegexwwk ← the-new-seekers
**I'd Like to Teach the World to Sing (1971)** : This song, released in 1970, was a "massive worldwide hit single," with a cover version also popular around the same time. It was "derived from one of the most popular and most famous Coca-Cola commercials of all time." The melody was later found to be similar to Oasis's "Shaker Maker," leading to a lawsuit against Oasis. **I'd Like to Teach the World to Sing (in Perfect Harmony) (1971)** : Originally recorded by the band as a radio jingle with Coca-Cola lyrics for McCann Erickson, it was later released as a single without product references due to popular demand. Their version, sounding more like the original commercial, became the bigger hit between competing releases, peaking at number 7 on the Hot 100 in January 1972 and notably reaching number 1 in the UK. "Since the New Seekers naturally sounded more like the commercial, after all, they'd recorded the original version. **Look What They Done to My Song Ma (1970)** : This cover version became a number 14 hit for the quintet in 1970, preceding their success with "I'd like to Teach the World to Sing." The song helped establish their presence on the charts before their more iconic jingle-turned-hit. ## curiosities
**Lawsuit Against Oasis** : The New Seekers successfully sued Oasis due to the song "Shaker Maker" having a melody eerily similar to their worldwide hit, which originated from a Coca-Cola commercial jingle. The court case resulted in Oasis being ordered to make changes to their song. "And yes, there were similarities and yes, there was a lawsuit." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L09R2evjyMc ← the-new-seekers
**Original Coca-Cola Jingle Recording (1971)** : The New Seekers recorded the original radio jingle for the famous "Hilltop" Coca-Cola commercial with lyrics explicitly mentioning Coca-Cola, prior to its adaptation and release as a general pop single without product references. This initial recording established their sound as synonymous with the iconic ad. "The New Seekers recorded the original radio jingle with the lyrics about Coca-Cola." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > I’d Like to Teach the World to Buy Edition Part 1 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f1264fe6d21276e0cc5
**US Touring Schedule (1971)** : While touring America toward the end of 1971, the band managed to find time to enter a studio and record their own full-length version of "I'd Like to Teach the World to Sing," which was then released to compete directly on the charts with The Hillside Singers' version. "Meanwhile, the New Seekers, who were touring America toward the end of 1971, found time to get into a studio and record their own version." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > I’d Like to Teach the World to Buy Edition Part 1 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f1264fe6d21276e0cc5
