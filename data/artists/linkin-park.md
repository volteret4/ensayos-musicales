## albums
**Hybrid Theory (2000) – Debut Album** : Released on October 20, 2000, this album was a monumental success, selling five million copies in its first year and over 30 million worldwide. It topped charts in New Zealand (number one), and reached number two in the US, Germany, and Australia, also charting in the top ten in Canada, Austria, Belgium, Denmark, Finland, Hungary, Norway, Scotland, Sweden, and Switzerland. The album earned three Grammy nominations, winning one for "Crawling" in the Best Hard Rock Performance category. Its success was attributed to the band's strong music, Mike Shinoda's rapping, and their firm stance against various "stupid suggestions" from label representatives regarding their image and sound, such as removing rap elements or altering their logo. "That record was released on October 20th of 2000." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
**Hybrid Theory (2001)** : This album sold over 4.8 million copies in 2001, at a time when more than 26 million people were actively using Napster for file-sharing. The album's strong sales occurred despite the significant impact of peer-to-peer sharing on the music industry. "As Lincoln Park was on their way to selling more than 4.8 million copies of their hybrid theory album in 2001, more than 26 million people were using Napster." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-CT7yYXonIk
**Meteora (2003)** : This was Linkin Park's second studio album, released in the spring of 2003. "Lincoln Park from their second album, Mediaora." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
**Reanimation (2002) – Remix Album** : Released in 2002, this was a 20-track remix album co-produced by Mike Shinoda. Creating a remix album so early in their career was unusual, but Warner Brothers Records approved it due to the strong sales of their debut, Hybrid Theory. The album went on to sell two million copies and is recognized as the third best-selling remix album of all time. "Reanimation is officially the third best-selling remix album of all time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM

## songs
**Crawling (2000)** : The second single from Linkin Park's debut album, Hybrid Theory. Chester Bennington composed the lyrics drawing directly from his experiences with drugs and alcohol. The track features minimal rapping from Mike Shinoda, a decision made by the label to test the waters regarding the presence of a rapper in the band. The song's musical style was influenced by the band's collective appreciation for Depeche Mode, and it went on to win a Grammy for Best Hard Rock Performance. "Crawling, the second single from Lincoln Park's debut album, Hybrid Theory." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
**In the End (2000)** : Featured on their debut album Hybrid Theory, this song became a significant hit for Linkin Park. It reached number one on the singles charts in the US and climbed to number two in Canada, solidifying the band's mainstream success. "In the end became a genuine top 40 hit reaching all the way to number one on the singles charts in the US and all the way to number two in Canada." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
**One Step Closer (2000)** : A single from Hybrid Theory, this song originated from the band's frustration with producer Don Gilmore, who was intensely pushing them in the studio. Chester Bennington's lyrics, such as "I cannot take this anymore. I'm saying everything I've ever done before, all these words make no sense" and the chorus "Everything you say takes me one step closer to the edge and I'm going to break," were explicitly directed at Gilmore for dismissing his lyrical submissions. The "shut up" part was also 100% aimed at him. Despite the band's initial reluctance to include it on the album, it was released as a single, became a hit, and effectively launched Linkin Park's career. "One step closer, which is basically Chester Bennington screaming at hybrid theory producer Don Gilmore because Chester was tired of being pushed around in the studio." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
**Papercut (2000)** : One of the four singles released from the debut album Hybrid Theory. A live recording of "Papercut" was made at the Docklands Arena in London for the BBC, and this version was subsequently included on a bonus disc accompanying the special two-disc Japanese edition of the *Hybrid Theory* album. "It's a version of PaperCut recorded of the Docklands Arena in London for the BBC." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
**Points of Authority (2002 Remix)** : A reinterpreted mix of this song is featured on Linkin Park's 2002 remix album, Reanimation. The new vocal contribution on this particular track comes from Jay Gordon, who is the lead singer of the San Francisco-based band Orgy. "This is the reinterpreted mix of points of authority from Lincoln Park's Reanimation album from 2002." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
**Somewhere I Belong (2003)** : This song is featured on Linkin Park's second album, Meteora. The lyrics express a desire for authenticity and belonging, including lines like "I want to feel like I'm close to something real. I want to find something I want it all along. Somewhere I belong." "Lincoln Park from their second album, Mediaora." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
**Untitled (Demo Version) (pre-2000)** : This demo track, which would later become the hit song "In the End," was part of a nine-song demo created by Hybrid Theory before they officially became Linkin Park. This particular demo version was circulated and rejected by record labels at least 42 times. The lyrics, even in this early form, included the memorable line, "But in the end, it doesn't even matter." "In the end, the demo version, before it even had a title by Hybrid Theory just before they changed their name to Lincoln Park." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM

## curiosities
**Album Sales (2007)** : In 2007, Linkin Park's biggest selling rock record achieved sales of 2.1 million copies. This figure reflects their commercial success within the rock genre during a period of overall decline in recorded music sales. "2007, Lincoln Parking, 2.1 million." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-CT7yYXonIk
**Band's Stand Against Label Interference** : In an unusual move for a new band, Linkin Park directly met with Warner Brothers executives and firmly rejected various label suggestions, including demands to remove rapping, change Mike Shinoda's rap style to mimic Fred Durst, bring in a reggae vocalist named Matt Lyons, or introduce gimmicks for DJ Joe Hahn like wearing a cowboy hat and lab coat. They also refused to change their logo, which some at the label found "too Asian." "And the meeting was risky because new bands just don't show up in the boardrooms of major record labels and start dictating terms for how they were to be marketed and how their fan club should be treated." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
**Biggest Selling Modern Rock Act (2000s)** : By spring 2005, the band was "on their way to becoming the biggest selling modern rock act of the decade." "Lincoln Park was on their way to becoming the biggest selling modern rock act of the decade." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Chester Bennington's Return** : Chester Bennington left Stone Temple Pilots on November 9, 2015, to return to Linkin Park. The plan was for him to rejoin STP after Linkin Park's next project was complete. "Chester left the group amicably on November 9th, 2015 to go back to Lincoln Park." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vLZla5h4xKQ
**Chester Bennington's Suicide (2017)** : Chester Bennington committed suicide on July 20, 2017, after returning to Linkin Park from his temporary role in Stone Temple Pilots. "But then we all know what happened next, Chester committed suicide on July 20th of 2017." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vLZla5h4xKQ
**Extended Hiatus After Chester Bennington's Death** : Following the death of lead singer Chester Bennington in July 2017, Linkin Park entered a prolonged period of inactivity as the remaining members grappled with the loss and adjusted to the tragedy. The band was absent from the music scene for a considerable time, with their eventual return in late 2024 accompanied by lineup changes. "Lincoln Park was absent from the scene for a long, long time after Chester's death as everybody tried to adjust." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xid8CZf4oYo
