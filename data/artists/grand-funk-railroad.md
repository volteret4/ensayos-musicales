## curiosities
**Associated with "Electronic Noise"** : In 1970, Los Angeles Times critic Robert Hilburn described the rock scene as having "a lot of million sellers and a lot of electronic noise from Led Zeppelin to Grand Funk Railroad," contrasting it with the artistic substance he found in Elton John's debut. **Major International Rock Act (Early 1970s)** : Grand Funk Railroad was surprisingly one of the biggest international rock acts in the early 1970s, despite their perceived obscurity today, at a time when much of rock music was considered tired. "The biggest international rock acts in the early 1970s were Led Zeppelin, Pink Floyd, The Rolling Stones, Elton John, and as unbelievable as it sounds today, Grand Funk Railroad." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4 ← grand-funk-railroad
**Member Honored by Gibby Haynes' Dog** : Mark Farner, a member of Grand Funk Railroad, was the namesake for a pit bull owned by Gibby Haynes of the Butthole Surfers, reflecting the cross-band appreciation.
