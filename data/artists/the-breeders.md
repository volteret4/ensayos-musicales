## songs
**Cannonball (1993)** : This "big single" was the primary catalyst for the widespread success of The Breeders' album "Last Splash." It contains a distinctive "mistake" where Kim Deal's bass was tuned flat during recording; she corrected herself mid-song, but the initial "bum notes" were kept because they created cool, false expectations. The music video for "Cannonball" was co-produced by Kim Gordon of Sonic Youth. "The catalyst for all this was a song called Cannonball, Big Single, and there was a big mistake in the song." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI ← the-breeders
**Cannonball (Year not mentioned, associated with 1993 album)** : The breakthrough single from the album "Last Splash," which significantly contributed to the album's success. It famously features an intentional mistake in the intro where bass player Josephine Wiggs hit wrong notes during rehearsals, which the band found "so cool" they decided to keep it in the final recording. "This thing exploded, thanks mainly to a single called Cannonball. ... In the rehearsals for the recording sessions, bass player Josephine Wiggs kept hitting the wrong notes during the intro... they kept it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oLC49nCf0ZM ← the-breeders
