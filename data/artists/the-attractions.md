# artist - The Attractions

## members
- Pete Thomas (drummer)

## curiosities
**Formation for Second Album** : The Attractions were formed by Elvis Costello for his second album because Clover (his backing band for "My Aim Is True") was unavailable and "lacked a certain intensity." The band consisted of "music industry vets." "That's when Elvis Gustell finally formed his own group. The attractions were made up of some music industry vets and they would join Elvis off and on over the next couple of decades." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vKRKu7O_5HA
**Long-term Collaboration** : The Attractions collaborated with Elvis Costello "off and on over the next couple of decades," providing a consistent musical partnership throughout much of his career. "The attractions were made up of some music industry vets and they would join Elvis off and on over the next couple of decades." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vKRKu7O_5HA
**Reunion for "Blood and Chocolate"** : After a period where Costello primarily used The Attractions for live gigs and worked solo in the studio, the band reunited for the 1986 album "Blood and Chocolate," which was described as a return to his "old school" sound. "But then in 1986, there was a reunion. The album was called Blood and Chocolate." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vKRKu7O_5HA
**Second Album Backup Band** : The Attractions became Elvis Costello's crack backup band, but they did not form until his second album. His debut album, My Aim Is True, was recorded with a different, uncredited American band called Clover (performing as The Sham Rocks). "But that band didn't come together until the second album." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=itK5fEqnA5E
