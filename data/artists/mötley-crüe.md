## curiosities
**"New Rockers" (Late 1980s)** : Identified as one of the "new rockers" of the late 1980s, characterized by "more flash than substance," during a period when pop music was dominant. "The new rockers, Poison, Motley Crew, Quiet Rhyth, were really more flash than substance." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Adoption of the "Rock Dot" Umlaut** : Mötley Crüe is among the many bands that adopted the umlaut (or "rock dot") in their name, following the trend initiated by Blue Oyster Cult. This was done primarily for its aesthetic appeal, making the name look foreign, gothic, exotic, or menacing. "There's Motley Crew, Queen's Reich, Spinal Tap, Green Jelly, Motorhead, Maximal Park, Moxie Frouze, and tons of others." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yPok3MemDmE
**Collaboration with Bob Rock** : Mötley Crüe is listed among Bob Rock's clients. "We got the cult, Veruch assault, Erasmith, Motley crew, Ardady Pease, the Tea Party, Eric and High Fy, Afghan Wigs, David Lee Roth, the Conorline Crush, Cher." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QkSKxm_U9Yc
**Collaboration with Garth Richardson** : Garth Richardson worked on projects with Mötley Crüe, among many other bands, after producing Rage Against the Machine's debut. "That led to more work with everyone from Motley Crew to 5440 and Kitty and Mudvane and Chavelle and the Catherine Wheel and Trapped." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QkSKxm_U9Yc
**Glam Rock Lineage** : Mötley Crüe in 1985 is presented as being "cut from the same cloth" as 1973 Slade, illustrating the direct lineage from glam rock to hair metal, characterized by big hair, makeup, spandex, and party anthems. "But 1985 Motley Crew, next to 1973 Slade, and you'll see that they're cut from the same cloth." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw
**Las Vegas Residency Trend** : Mötley Crüe joined a growing number of rock bands who began accepting offers for Las Vegas residencies, following the path of pop stars who had successfully redefined Vegas entertainment. This trend allowed artists to have fans come to them, rather than enduring the exhaustive nature of traditional touring. "David Lee Roth, Error Smith, Motley Crude, Journey, The Eagles, and Billy Idle among them." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GYUuTbOvyak
**Legal Consequences and Remorse** : Neil was charged with vehicular manslaughter and drunk driving. He served 20 days in jail, completed 200 hours of community service, and was ordered to pay $2.6 million to the crash's other victims. Even Neil himself later admitted he should have received a much longer jail sentence than 20 days. "Neil was charged with vehicular manslaughter and drunk driving." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5vKSjpvVQa8
**Rehab at Impact Drug and Alcohol Treatment Center** : Every member of Mötley Crüe reportedly attended the Impact Drug and Alcohol Treatment Center in Pasadena for rehabilitation. "This is a place that is hosted every member of Motley Crew and also Brent Gerowicz of Bad Religion." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EyagC0T7mR4
**Tattoo Glorification in Hair Metal** : Mötley Crüe, along with other bands of the hair metal era like Guns N' Roses and LA Guns, openly glorified tattoos. This was a significant part of the visual shock and appeal of the time, alongside exaggerated hair, makeup, spandex, and shoes. "Molly crew also, they also glorified tattoos. I believe they have a record called new tattoo. Guns and roses saying about tattoos. Molly crew is saying about getting tattooed. So in the 80s, bands like Molly crew, Guns and roses, LA guns, they glorify tattoos. Right up front." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jZeMmPekKMQ
**Vehicular Manslaughter of Razzle (1984)** : In 1984, Vince Neil and Razzle, the drummer for Hanoi Rocks, left a party in Neil's powerful 1972 Ford Pantera to get more alcohol. While driving 65 mph in a 25 mph zone, Neil hit a wet patch, crossed into oncoming traffic, and crashed into two cars. Razzle died in the hospital, and Neil suffered cuts and cracked ribs. "While going 65 in a 25 miles per hour zone, the Pantera hit a wet patch of pavement, it crossed over into the opposite lane and crashed into two cars traveling the other way." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5vKSjpvVQa8

This detail is noted as part of Lindemulder's extensive and varied career history.
