## songs
**Brand New Cadillac (1959)** : Released in April 1959 as a B-side, this song is the original version later covered by The Clash. Vince Taylor, the American singer, also served as part of the inspiration for David Bowie's Ziggy Stardust character. "The original was by Vince Taylor, an American singer who provided part of the inspiration for David Bowie's Ziggy Stardust character." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_KJ60im4KAc
**Pledge in My Love (1959)** : This single, released in 1959 by British rock-abilly singer Vince Taylor, had a B-side that was significantly more popular and was covered by numerous bands over the years, including The Downliner's Sect, The Renegades, Hepstars, Shamrocks, The Sikky Boys (1976), Kim Fowley (1977), and The Clash (1979). "In 1959, a British rock-abilly singer named Vince Taylor released a single called Pledge in My Love. ## curiosities
**Alien/God Persona** : Following a mental crisis, English-American singer Vince Taylor believed he existed somewhere "between a god and an alien," a unique self-perception that served as inspiration for David Bowie's Ziggy Stardust character. "an English American singer, with some kind of mental crisis and now believed that he existed somewhere between a god and an alien." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw
**Covered by The Clash** : American singer Vince Taylor's old blues song was used as a warm-up piece by The Clash during the recording sessions for their 1979 album "London Calling." Producer Guy Stevens secretly recorded the band's take on it, which he then accepted despite the band's protests about its sloppiness and tempo. "The clash went into the studio in the summer of 1979 to record what would become the London Calling album, producer Guy Stevens had all four guys warm up with a cover of an old blues song by American singer Vince Taylor." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Zniri9uSlc
**Fabulous Style** : Vince Taylor, as part of the Playboys, was among the artists who followed Little Richard's flamboyant path, exhibiting a distinctive and fabulous style. "Vince Taylor in the playboys... They all sounded different, but they all had a specific sort of fabulous style about them." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw
