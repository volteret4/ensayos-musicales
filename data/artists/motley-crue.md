## songs
**Home Sweet Home (1985)** : This power ballad, featured on the 1985 album *Theater of Pain*, was crafted to be sung in auditoriums and arenas, demonstrating a mass appeal that extended beyond a niche audience. Nikki Sixx noted its immense popularity, stating that "Home Sweet Home" was the number one song on MTV's request line for five months, an achievement he credited entirely to the fans. "This is from the August nineteen eighty seven Alan town Pennsylvania newspaper we have great admiration for our fans niki six says and we don't care about MTV or radio or whether critics like us or don't like us it just doesn't matter to us what matters is when the kids make home sweet home the number one song on the request line for five months on MTV that's something that no one but the kids do and that floors me." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 54 - Into the 80s! **Hair Metal Aesthetic and Lifestyle** : Motley Crue fully embraced the hair metal aesthetic, characterized by "sky high teased hair, terror tight leather pants, lots of metal studs, carefully done makeup," drawing influence from bands like Kiss and the New York Dolls, with David Bowie cited as a "babysitter." Beyond their look, the band famously "lived the part of the drug and alcohol fueled rock stars," embodying the chaotic image associated with the genre.
