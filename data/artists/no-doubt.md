**No Doubt (Anaheim, 1986) – Started as Applecore; Original Singer John Spence Committed Suicide Age 18; Tragic Kingdom (1995) 17 Million Copies; Eric Stefani Left for The Simpsons** : No Doubt was formed in 1986 as a band called Applecore by Eric Stefani and his sister Gwen, later joined by John Spence (who said "no doubt" when he agreed with you — that became the band's name). Spence was the main singer with Gwen on backup. Just before a big showcase for major labels, Spence committed suicide at age 18. After a brief breakup, the band reformed with a new singer named Alan Mead, who didn't last, and then Gwen took over vocals. A self-titled 1992 album flopped (maybe 30,000 copies). A second album called The Beacon Street Collection sold only a few more. Keyboardist Eric Stefani then left to become an animator for The Simpsons, preferring animation to music. Interscope paired the remaining band with producer Matthew Wilder; the resulting Tragic Kingdom came out October 10, 1995 and eventually sold north of 17 million copies worldwide, doing more for ska punk than any other CD. "At last count worldwide sales were somewhere north of 17 million copies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Yqo-YDJR0nM

**Reunion for Touring** : No Doubt was one of many bands from past decades that reunited in the 21st century, a trend largely motivated by financial incentives. With declining CD sales impacting artist revenues, returning to the touring circuit offered a lucrative way to appeal to nostalgic fan bases and fill large venues. "The list of reunions is long." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GYUuTbOvyak
**Third Wave Ska Leader** : No Doubt was a prominent band in the "third wave" of Ska, which arrived in the 1990s. This wave saw significant popularity with bands like The Mighty Mighty Bosstones, Sublime, Reel Big Fish, Goldfinger, and Dance Hall Crashers. The Interrupters were noted as the first female-fronted Ska band to have a major hit since No Doubt. "Third wave Scott arrived in the 1990s, no doubt, muddy, muddy boss tones, sublime, real big fish, gold finger, dancehall crashes and so many more." "They became the first female fronted Skaban to have a major hit since no doubt." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HSvgRyGiOpE
