## curiosities
**Anonymous Image and Sound** : Members of Underground Resistance were known for performing camouflaged, maintaining anonymity. They developed a very minimalist, pure, and dense techno sound, distinct from the earlier, more house-like techno, focusing heavily on rhythmic bases. **Formation and Philosophy (1989)** : Underground Resistance emerged in 1989 as part of the second wave of techno. This collective of DJs and producers formed with the explicit goal of reclaiming the essential, underground spirit of Detroit techno, reacting against its commercialization by multinational corporations. **Influence on Modern Techno** : Along with Richie Hawtin, Underground Resistance is largely responsible for creating the minimalist, rhythm-heavy, monotonous, and constant techno sound that is known today, often without melody. This sound is considered more characteristic of the second wave of techno than the initial creations of the genre's founders. "la segunda gran generación del techno, creo el techno que conocemos hoy en día, el techno minimalista, cargadísimo de base rímpica, monótono, constante, donde la melodía a veces ya ni siquiera existe." ← Music Radar Clan > La historia del TECHNO en DETROIT. **Social and Political Activism** : Underground Resistance was deeply committed to its underground philosophy, linking with African-American liberation movements and anti-capitalist urban guerrilla movements. This commitment reflected their desire to return to the primal philosophy of techno, where people without resources united in trance states through rhythmic bases.
