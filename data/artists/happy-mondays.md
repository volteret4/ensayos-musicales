## albums
**Madchester Rave On EP (Year not given)** : This EP was created "just for a bit of fun" and was central to the coining of the term "Madchester." The title "Rave On" was reportedly chosen spontaneously by someone named Balsaw when looking for a name to give to the press for tracks that were about "All Night Where-outs parties." The press then mistakenly associated this with the emerging "rave" scene. "It was in the studio doing the EP, the Mad Chester Ae Von EP." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bI6TTA-fn7c
**Yes Please (1992) – Most Expensive Indie Record Never Properly Made** : The Happy Mondays traveled to Barbados to record at Eddie Grant's Blue Wave Studios with Tina Weymouth and Chris Frantz (Talking Heads) producing. Shaun Ryder's methadone supply (four weeks' worth in 500ml vials) was dropped and shattered at the airport before departure. In Barbados he found crack cocaine as a substitute, eventually consuming up to 30 rocks a day (at 40 cents each vs. $50 back home). Bez broke his arm in a rented Jeep accident; Ryder drove another rental car through the front of a bar; keyboardist Paul Davis wrecked three rental cars; total vehicles destroyed numbered more than ten; band members sold Eddie Grant's furniture to buy more drugs. Tony Wilson flew in to find Shaun and Bez pushing a couch down the road to trade it for crack. Only one song was fully completed; Ryder forgot to write lyrics. The project cost £400,000. The one released single, "Stinking Thinking," peaked at number 31 in the UK. Factory Records filed for bankruptcy in November 1992, six weeks after the album's release. "Four hundred thousand pounds down the drain. The most expensive indie record never made." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oljjY7jO-kM

## songs
**C30, C60, C90 Go (1980)** : This single was released by British group Bow Wow Wow as an encouragement for home taping during a period when the record industry was fighting against it. The song's title refers to common blank cassette lengths, and one version of the single even came on a cassette with side two left blank for taping. ## curiosities
**Barbados Recording Session Disaster – Collapse of Factory Records** : The Happy Mondays' drug binge in Barbados during the recording of their album proved catastrophic for Factory Records in the early 1990s. Band members consumed crack at a staggering rate, destroyed cars, a member broke his arm, and they sold their own possessions to fund drug purchases. They delivered an album devoid of lyrics and vocals. The incident directly accelerated the financial ruin of Factory Records. "Destroyed cars, a member broke his arm, and sold their belongings to buy drugs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RKvFJMkZdBE
**Early Recognition** : The speaker recalled reading about the Happy Mondays and immediately recognizing their potential to become a significant band. The radio station's staff enthusiastically supported their music, believing they were leading the culture in Toronto, Ontario, Western New York, and possibly beyond, particularly for British music. "I remember reading about the Happy Mondays and thinking, okay, this band is going to be something along with the stone roses and the charlatans." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4xxgPX-aR90
**Factory Records Collapse – No Legal Contracts with Artists** : Factory Records' attempt to sell its assets to London Records was derailed when it was discovered that the label held no legally binding contracts with any of its artists. This fundamental structural flaw made the label impossible to sell and hastened its complete collapse. "They had no legally binding contracts with their artists." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RKvFJMkZdBE
**Food Health Discussions on Tour Bus** : During their reunion tour, members like Gaz and Rovetta would engage in discussions on the tour bus about healthy eating, railing against "the evils of eating pork and processed foods and having too much meat," which was a stark contrast to their past image. "The rest of us sat in the dining area, listening to gas and rovetta, rail above the evils of eating pork and processed foods and having too much meat." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
**Gaz Whelan's Relocation to Burlington, Ontario** : The band's drummer, Gaz Whelan, relocated from Manchester to Burlington, Ontario, a decision prompted by his wife's interest in Canada after seeing it featured on a real estate television show. **Madchester Holy Trinity** : The Happy Mondays are noted as being part of the "holy trinity of Madchester," a significant musical movement. "The happy Mondays, part of the holy trinity of Madchester..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Madchester Sound Figure** : The Happy Mondays were identified as a prominent band within the "Madchester Sound" scene at the beginning of the 1990s, alongside The Stone Roses, The Charlatans, and Inspiral Carpets. "The Mad Chester Sound, featuring bands like The Roses and The Happy Mondays and The Charlotons and The Inspiral Carpets, was all the rage." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=huAvyInzz3Q
**Origin of "Madchester" Term** : The term "Madchester" was coined by "two lots of new castles" who had heard the Happy Mondays' music and videos. It became strongly associated with their "Madchester Rave On EP," particularly stemming from the EP's title, which was hastily chosen as "Rave On" from a comment about "All Night Where-outs parties." This led the press to link the music with "raves," creating the "Madchester" moniker. "That was coin by two lots of new castles who'd heard us." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bI6TTA-fn7c
**Shift to "Ultra-Cleaned Living Ways"** : The band, famously associated with drugs, alcohol, and debauchery in the 90s (pot, coke, heroin, PCP, crack), underwent a drastic lifestyle change by the time of their reunion tour. Their tour bus was clean, smoke-free, drug-free, and alcohol-free, with only coffee and chocolate available, and wives and children often on board. "The happy Mondays from their baggy, drug-y days, it is not that way anymore. I am witness to their current ultra-cleaned living ways." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
**UK Reunion Tour (2010s)** : Against initial odds, the Happy Mondays reunited for a series of shows across the UK, signaling a significant return for the band known for its Madchester era excesses. The tour included a stop at Brixton Academy where Inspiral Carpets opened. "But, against all odds, the Mondays came back together for a series of shows across the UK." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
