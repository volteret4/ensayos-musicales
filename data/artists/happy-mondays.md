## curiosities
**"Ultra-Clean Living" Tour Bus (Post-Reunion)** : During a UK reunion tour, the Happy Mondays' tour bus was notably clean, smoke-free, drug-free, and alcohol-free, stocking only coffee and chocolate. Wives and children were present, and band members, including Gaz Whelan and Rowetta, discussed healthy eating habits, a stark contrast to their previous reputation. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
**Bez's Role and New Passion** : Bez, a former member, was known as a drug supplier for the band and Manchester. He has since become a public advocate for bees and an obsessed beekeeper. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0DFmS6MPbFI
**Crack Consumption in Barbados (Early 1990s)** : The band was sent to Barbados to record "Yes Please" to avoid heroin temptations, but instead found and heavily consumed crack cocaine. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RKvFJMkZdBE
**Drug-Induced Financial Ruin of Factory Records (Early 1990s)** : The band's excessive drug consumption and associated costs were directly blamed for driving their record label, Factory Records, out of business. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RKvFJMkZdBE
**End of Madchester Scene (Early 1990s)** : The band was a feature of the Manchester scene, which was "pretty much over" by the early 1990s, contributing to a void in British music. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg
**Gaz Whelan's Relocation to Burlington, Ontario** : Gaz Whelan, the band's drummer, relocated from Manchester, UK, to Burlington, Ontario, Canada, after his wife was inspired by a real estate show featuring Canada. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
**Madchester Trinity Member** : Recognized as part of the "holy trinity of Madchester," and a key band on Factory Records. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Property Damage and Theft for Drugs (Early 1990s)** : While in Barbados, the band destroyed two rental cars and one member's arm was broken in five places in a crash. They sold their clothes to island drug dealers and stole couches from the recording studio to trade for crack. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RKvFJMkZdBE
**Reunion Tour (Post-Madchester Era)** : The Happy Mondays reunited for a series of shows across the UK following their 90s Madchester era, which was characterized by significant drug and alcohol abuse. The band members demonstrated a new "ultra-clean living" lifestyle. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
**Signed to Factory Records** : Happy Mondays were signed to Factory Records, an indie label that manager Rob Gretton worked for and brought clients to. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**Unfinished Album Recording (Early 1990s)** : Upon returning to Manchester from Barbados, it was discovered that while the band had written music and recorded some songs for "Yes Please," they had failed to record any lyrics or vocals. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RKvFJMkZdBE
