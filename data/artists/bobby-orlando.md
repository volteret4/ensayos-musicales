# artist - Bobby Orlando

## curiosities
**Mentor to Pechos Boys** : In 1982, Bobby Orlando met the young Pechos Boys in New York and introduced them to the city's suburban disco and gay community culture that had survived underground. He went on to record their first important tracks, including "West End Girls," "Opportunism," and "It's a Sin," which became their foundational hits. **New York Producer & Disco Revival** : Known as "Bob-O" in the industry, Bobby Orlando was a New York producer heavily involved with both the decline of disco and the subsequent resurgence of a new "gay disco" scene. This scene notably included groups like Village People, Divine, and The Flirts.
