## curiosities
**Pioneer of Lyric Poetry and Love Songs (Ancient Greece)** : Sappho, a poet from the Greek island of Lesbos, challenged ancient conventions which restricted music to religious rituals, lamentations, or praising men. She wrote lyric poetry specifically intended to be sung, typically accompanied by a lyre, and became known as a symbol of love and desire between women, giving rise to the terms "sapphic" and "lesbian." While much of her work has been lost, it is unknown if she performed her own compositions.
