## curiosities
**Anecdotes with Ahmet Ertegun** : During Big Wreck's first iteration, Ian Thorneley had experiences with Ahmet Ertegun, co-founder of Atlantic Records, including a dinner at his office where Ertegun heard Big Wreck's music and shared stories about bands like the Rolling Stones and Led Zeppelin. Ertegun was described as a small Turkish man with a perfectly manicured mustache, often accompanied by "Amazonian gorgeous women." "Did you actually go for dinner with Ahmed Erdogan? Yeah, I mean, I was brought up to his office by Craig Calman." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**Connection with Chad Kroeger** : Ian Thorneley has worked extensively with Chad Kroeger of Nickelback, considering him a close friend and family member. However, he acknowledges that Kroeger's successful working methods differ significantly from his own, leading to external pressures from those trying to replicate Kroeger's success. "But having said that, he, he, I, that I've worked a lot with the guy and he's like, he's like a family member and he's a very close guy and very close friend." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**Early Business Exploitation Experience** : During the period with the band Thorneley, Ian encountered individuals in business who attempted to take advantage of him, leading to a significant phone conversation in 2005 or 2006 where his artistic and personal needs were dismissed. This experience taught him about the harsh realities of the music industry. "And the quote was, I don't give a fuck about your child." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**Financial Worries and Motivation** : Despite his success, Ian Thorneley consistently experiences financial worries, especially with family responsibilities. This serves as a significant self-motivating mechanism, extending beyond purely artistic concerns to the practical needs of supporting his family. "I have, I have 11 month old at home, you know, and he's, he eats a lot of food. He eats more than I do." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**First Velvet Revolver Consideration (Pre-Weiland)** : Before Scott Weiland joined Velvet Revolver, Ian Thorneley was considered as a potential singer. He received a CD with three instrumental tracks to write over, but he didn't pay much attention to it as his first Thorneley record was about to be released. "I remember getting a CD right before the first Thorneley record came out. I got a CD with three cuts on it, just instrumental tracks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**Live Wardrobe Malfunction** : During a Thorneley tour show in Edmonton or Calgary, possibly on Halloween, the power repeatedly went out due to running too many amps. While Ian was engaging the audience with jumps and antics, his pants split open in the crotch, revealing his anatomy due to not wearing underwear. His guitar tech, Shayne, attempted to tape him up, and a crew member named Timmy retrieved new pants, allowing him to finish the show. "And during one of these jumps, my pants split open in the crotchal area. And these days I didn't wear underwear." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**Post-Big Wreck Version 1.0 Career Transition** : After Big Wreck's initial disbandment around 2001-2002, Ian Thorneley moved back to Toronto and found work as a session player. He contributed to albums by artists such as Nickelback, Sarah Harmer, and Stephen Fearing. "After big rec version 1.0 broke up, Ian moved back to Toronto where he found work as a session player." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**Protecting Artistic Vision** : Ian Thorneley demonstrated a strong conviction in protecting his artistic output, which he likened to protecting his child. This conviction allowed him to resist manipulation and commercial pressures, distinguishing him from many musicians who might compromise for a living. "Now, what's, what I'm finding interesting is that this is your child, your baby. Yeah. And the conviction that you have in protecting your baby is not something that you see in a lot of musicians..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**Reason for Rejecting Velvet Revolver Frontman Role** : Despite the musical chemistry and positive vibe during rehearsals, Ian Thorneley ultimately did not join Velvet Revolver. The band sought a "peacock" frontman in the vein of David Lee Roth or Scott Weiland, a role that didn't align with Ian's preference to play guitar on stage. "I'm not that comfortable doing the whole, you know, front guy tambourine thing. It's just not for me." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**Second Velvet Revolver Consideration and Rehearsals (Post-Weiland)** : After Scott Weiland's departure, Ian was again approached by Velvet Revolver. Slash himself called Ian to send over material, and Ian subsequently rehearsed with the band in Los Angeles, experiencing Slash's full touring rig and observing the band members as "real dudes." "The phone call came from slash. I was driving back from a gig or something. Oh, the phone rings and it's slash." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**Shared "Guitarist First" Affliction with Miles Kennedy** : Ian Thorneley notes a long-standing connection with Miles Kennedy, lead singer for Slash's solo band and Alter Bridge. They share the characteristic of being "guitar players first and singers second," although Kennedy has since embraced the frontman role without a guitar. "Oddly enough, like Miles, who sings with slash all the time, the, the mild and I go way back and, and we suffer from the same affliction where guitar players first and singers second." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
