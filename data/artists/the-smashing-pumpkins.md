**Mellon Collie and the Infinite Sadness (1995) – Cycle of Life** : Released in 1995, this double album was conceived as a concept record despite the mid-90s stigma against such ambitious projects, which were considered egotistical and "dumb" by the Alternative Nation. Billy Corgan insisted on creating what he called "The Wall for Generation X," referencing Pink Floyd's iconic album. With producers Alan Molder and Flood, the band created a thematically unified album (though without a linear narrative) exploring the cycle of life and death. Corgan intended it as a message and an exploration of his own experiences, advice, and observations for Generation X, specifically those between entering high school and graduating college. The CD version was divided into "Dawn to Dusk" and "Twilight to Starlight," while the vinyl offered more granular sections: "Dawn," "Tea Time," "Dusk," "Twilight," "Midnight," and "Starlight." "Billy Corgan says that it was definitely conceived as a concept record." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Dm5N6yDRxXQ
