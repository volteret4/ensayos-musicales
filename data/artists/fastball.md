## members

## songs
**The Way (1998) – Romanticized Tragedy** : This one-hit wonder from 1998 was written by Austin-based songwriter Tony Scalzo after he discovered the tragic story of Leila and Ray Howard. Scalzo characterized the song as a romanticized account of what might have happened to the elderly Texas couple, who suffered from cognitive decline and went missing during a short drive, only to be found dead two weeks later almost 450 miles from home after driving off a cliff. "He wrote this song for his band Fastball, and he characterizes the song as a romanticized account of what might have happened." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk

## curiosities
**Discovery of Neglect at Howard Home** : On July 3rd, a welfare check at the Howard's home revealed clear signs of cognitive decline, including their hearing aids being left behind and their cat, Happy, found abandoned. This discovery prompted a sick bulletin, a reward offer for their safe return, and extensive media coverage, though it ultimately led to no immediate success in finding the couple. "Given the state of the place, there were obvious signs that the couple were suffering from cognitive decline." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk
**Multiple Police Stops in Arkansas** : After going missing from Texas, Leila and Ray Howard were pulled over twice by different cops in rural Arkansas on July 2nd. The first stop was for Leila driving with her headlights off, where the trooper noted they were polite but heading in the wrong direction, and Leila seemed to remember their home address. Half an hour later, they were stopped again for driving with high beams on. In both instances, they were let go with a warning, as neither officer realized they were dealing with missing persons. "Neither cop had any idea that they were dealing with a couple of missing persons." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk
**The Disappearance of Leila and Ray Howard** : In 1997, 83-year-old Leila Howard, suffering from early-stage Alzheimer's, and 88-year-old Ray Howard, recovering from brain surgery and a stroke, decided to drive 17 miles from Salado, Texas, to Temple for Pioneer Days. Their son offered to drive, but they declined, asserting they knew the way. They left on Sunday, June 29th, and disappeared, later spotted at a Walmart. This tragic journey inspired Fastball's song "The Way." "The previous year, an elderly couple named Leila and Ray Howard of Solado, Texas decided to go for a drive." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk
**Tragic Death in Arkansas Ravine** : Two weeks after leaving their Salado, Texas home, on July 12, 1997, Leila and Ray Howard were found dead in their car at the bottom of a ravine near Hot Springs, Arkansas, almost 450 miles from their house. It is believed Leila may have been trying to find a former vacation spot, and with no skid marks, it appears she drove straight over the 25-foot cliff edge. Ray was found in the passenger seat, while Leila's body was discovered about 20 feet away, still holding her purse and car keys, with the duration their bodies lay there unknown. "Leila and Howard were found dead in their car at the bottom of a ravine near Hot Springs, Arkansas, almost 450 miles from their house." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk
