## curiosities
**Influence on Seattle Scene** : Tracks from Bad Brains were featured on homemade punk cassettes distributed by Buzz Osborne in Aberdeen, Washington, influencing the developing Seattle rock scene. "Buzz Osborne began giving out cassettes featuring his favorite punk songs, All Indie bands, TBI from the stuages, tracks from bad brains and black flag, and Buzz gave these tapes only to people he considered cool." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Origin of "Mosh" Term (Jamaican Accent)** : Bad Brains, a D.C.-area band known for blending hardcore with dub and reggae, played a key role in the term "mosh" becoming prevalent. Their singer, HR, frequently encouraged audiences to "mash it up" from the stage. Due to his Jamaican accent, "mash" often came out sounding like "mosh," leading to the pronunciation shift. "HR had a Jamaican accent. So whenever he said and sang mash, it kind of came out as Mosh." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KMx4bmq8o2Y
**Performance During CBGB's Final Weeks** : Bad Brains was one of the bands that performed at CBGB during its final weeks leading up to its closure in October 2006. "The final weeks featured bands like Bad Brains, The Dictators, and even Blondie." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Epz5Sn151bk
