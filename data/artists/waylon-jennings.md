**Good Hearted Woman** : Waylon Jennings wrote "Good Hearted Woman" after seeing an advertisement that mentioned many of Tina Turner's songs depicted "Good-hearted women loving two-timing men." This indirect inspiration led to the creation of the song. "Whalen Jennings did see an ad that inspired him. It said that a lot of Tina's songs were about Good-hearted women loving two-timing men and he wrote Good-hearted women based on that." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 53 - More than Charley Pride - African Americans in 70s Country | https://www.ftr70.com/

This version blended Cajun music with R&B, featuring Holly on guitar and King Curtis on saxophone. **Plane Seat Incident (1959)** : On the "Winter Dance Party" tour, Waylon Jennings gave up his seat on Buddy Holly's chartered plane to The Big Bopper, who was ill. Jennings jokingly told Holly, "I hope your old boss freezes," to which Holly retorted, "yeah well I hope you are all playing crashes." This exchange tragically preceded the plane crash that killed Holly.
