## albums
**Attack & Release (2008)** : The band's fifth studio album, released in 2008, marked their first collaboration with an outside producer, Danger Mouse. Danger Mouse initially had a prior commitment to produce an album for Ike Turner, but that project fell through, making him available. The album was recorded at Summa studio in Painsville, Ohio. Danger Mouse contributed as a musician, primarily playing keyboards and bass, with additional musicians providing guitars, vocals, and clarinets. Patrick Carney's drum sound on this album, influenced by a "John Bonham phase" in the summer of 2007, featured his kit set up like Bonzo's, resulting in a notably "bigger, deeper, tighter sound." "From Attack and Release, that's the album, this is called I Got Mine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
**Black Rock (2009)** : A collaborative album recorded in 2009 with Damon Dash, featuring numerous R&B and hip hop guest artists. "In 2009, they recorded a record called Black Rock, which was a collaboration with a guy named Damon Dash." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
**Brothers (2010)** : Released on May 18, 2010, this sixth album was a significant commercial breakthrough, earning five Grammy nominations and winning for Best Alternative Album and Best Artwork. Its cover, properly titled *Brothers* despite a longer, nonsensical version, is a tribute to a 1969 Howlin' Wolf album. The recording, which included one song produced by Danger Mouse, took place primarily at the Muscle Shoals Sound Studio in Alabama, a legendary location that presented significant challenges; its in-house gear was unusable after 30 years, and new digital equipment was fried by stray voltage. Despite this, the studio's acoustics and natural echo chambers were exceptional, emphasizing the bass on the record. The album's creation was marked by personal and band tensions, including Patrick Carney's contentious divorce. The physical CD was designed to change color when played: black to white in North America, white to black in Australia, and silver to black in Europe. "This record, which came out on May 18, 2010, was called Brothers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
**Chulahoma** : A tribute record dedicated to blues singer Junior Kimbrough, a significant influence on Dan Auerbach, who was a big fan of Kimbrough in high school. "and a tribute record called Chula Homa, which is dedicated to a blue singer named Junior Kimbrough." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
**El Camino (2011)** : Released just 19 months after *Brothers* in 2011, following multiple tours. Danger Mouse served as the full-time producer, joining the band in Nashville in the spring of 2011. Recording occurred at Dan Auerbach's newly acquired studio, Easy Eye Sound, providing a relaxed environment with functional gear. The band commenced recording without any pre-written material, composing songs around short hooks inspired by favorite bands such as The Beatles, The Clash, The Cramps, Elvis, and The Ramones, with Danger Mouse co-writing all 11 tracks. The album's cover features a simple image of an old Chrysler van, reminiscent of "The Grey Ghost," their former touring vehicle. *El Camino* achieved massive success, with platinum sales worldwide and triple-platinum status in Canada, a key market for the band. It won a Grammy for Best Rock Album, and its single "Lonely Boy" earned two additional awards, while Dan Auerbach also secured Producer of the Year. "Love that video with Derek T. Tuggle's dancing. That's Lonely Boy from the Black Keys seventh studio album called El Camino." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
**Live in Austin (2003)** : An official bootleg album, despite being recorded in a bootleg style, it was released with the band's approval. It captures a show played on October 24, 2003, at Emo's in Austin, Texas, during their tour for the *Thickfreakness* album. Notably, its initial release was as a podcast. "It's simply called Live in Austin, and dates all the way back to a show they played in October 24, 2003, at a place called Emo's when the band was touring the Thick Freakness album." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
**Magic Potion (2006)** : This fourth studio album, released in 2006, saw the band switch labels to Nonesuch and return to Patrick Carney's basement studio, outfitted with approximately $5,000 worth of "crappy gear." They opted for this familiar setting due to its distinctive vibe and "weird sounds." Despite its commercial success, reaching the top 100 on the American album charts and peaking at number 12 in Australia, the band was not entirely satisfied with the album, specifically noting issues with cheap mastering that resulted in a lack of low-end. "The fourth studio album was a 2006 release entitled Magic Potion." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
**Rubber Factory (2000) – Tire Warehouse Recording** : Released in 2000, this album was recorded in an abandoned tire warehouse they rented for $500 a month, using only used gear and recycled recording tape. The album's title reflects its unconventional recording location. "Their 2000 album was entitled, Surprise, Rubber Factory, makes sense." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=raRCZ0Y5up8
**Rubber Factory (2004)** : The third album was recorded over five months in an abandoned General Tire factory in East Akron, Ohio, which ceased operations in 1982. The band rented the second floor for $500 a month, acquired a recording console on eBay that was previously owned by a sound engineer for Loverboy (though it proved to be "crap" and was abandoned post-recording), and utilized recycled recording tape. Despite technical glitches, the album garnered positive reviews, led to a slot at Lollapalooza, and an appearance on David Letterman. It was their first album to break into the top 200 album chart, peaking at number 143. "The third album was recorded in a different spot, an abandoned tire factory in East Akron that was once run by General Tyre, but they bailed out in 1982." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
**The Big Come Up (2002)** : The debut album, recorded in Patrick Carney's parents' unfinished basement in Akron, Ohio, using an 8-track digi recorder between January and March of 2002. It was released on May 14, 2002, on CD and in ten different colors of vinyl, with none exceeding a thousand copies, making them collector's items. It was released on the LA-based label Alive, their only release with that label. "It was called The Big Come Up and was recorded in Patrick's parents' unfinished basement in Akron using an 8-track digi recorder between January and March of 2002." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
**The Moan (2004)** : One of at least two EPs released by the band, this one dating from 2004. "There are at least two EPs, one of which is called The Mone from 2004..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
**Thickfreakness (2003)** : This album was funded by Dan Auerbach and Patrick Carney through lawn mowing and was recorded in a single 14-hour sprint in Patrick Carney's basement. Critically acclaimed like their debut, this album had a Japanese release titled *Inezuma Rock and Blues* because "Thickfreakness" did not translate well, with "Inezuma" meaning lightning in Japanese. "This one was called Thick Freakness, and like the first record was loved by critics." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
**Turn Blue (2014)** : The band's eighth album, recorded intermittently over a period exceeding a year and a half before its release on May 12, 2014. Similar to how *Brothers* was influenced by Patrick Carney's divorce, *Turn Blue* was informed by Dan Auerbach's divorce, with lyrical themes reflecting this personal experience. The album's existence was announced by Mike Tyson via a tweet. Its title is an homage to Gullardi, a Cleveland late-night horror movie host from the 1960s, whose catchphrase inspired it. The cover art also references Gullardi's interest in films exploring mind control, brainwashing, and hypnotism. Five singles were released from the album. "Turn blue, the eighth album, was recorded in fits and starts over more than a year and a half before being released on May 12, 2014." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q

## curiosities
**"Grey Ghost" Van** : In their early touring days, The Black Keys used a 1994 Chrysler van, which they affectionately named "The Grey Ghost," to travel between gigs. A similar old Chrysler van later featured prominently on the album cover of *El Camino*. "they were just scraping by and chugging from gig to gig in a 1994 Chrysler van that they called the Grey Ghost..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
**Band Tension and Patrick Carney's Divorce (2010)** : During the period surrounding the recording of *Brothers*, significant internal tension arose between Dan Auerbach and Patrick Carney. Patrick acknowledged they were "sick of each other" and maintained separate lives outside of band work. This was compounded by Dan's solo album (made without Patrick's knowledge, which upset him), and Patrick's tumultuous divorce from his wife, Denise. Dan believed Denise was "bad news," and she was accused of cheating, playing mind games, and taking money. Patrick consequently suffered from depression and began drinking, leading the band to the brink of collapse, until he divorced his wife over the phone while the band was in Europe, after which their relationship and the band's dynamic began to improve. "Then there was the drama between Dan and Patrick's wife Denise." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
**Contemporaries in the Mid-2000s** : During their early and mid-2000s period, The Black Keys shared the scene with other notable indie bands such as Vampire Weekend, The National, Interpol, The Hold Steady, and Gaslight Anthem. "Their contemporaries at the time included Vampire Weekend, We Had The National, Interpol, The Hold Steady, and Gas Line Anthem." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=raRCZ0Y5up8
**DIY Recording Environment** : The band recorded their album "Rubber Factory" in an abandoned tire warehouse they rented for $500 a month, utilizing only used gear and recycled recording tape, demonstrating a strong DIY ethic. "They were working out of an abandoned tire warehouse that they rated for $500 a month, and they were recording on used gear using recycled recording tape." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=raRCZ0Y5up8
**Dan Auerbach's Divorce (2014)** : The creation of the *Turn Blue* album was heavily influenced by Dan Auerbach's divorce. A widely circulated anecdote claimed a custody battle over "a lock of Bob Dylan's hair," but this was a misunderstanding; the actual dispute was over a poster of Bob Dylan with large hair, which was colloquially referred to as "Bob Dylan's hair." "And just like brothers, was Patrick Kernie's divorce album, this one was informed by Dan's divorce." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q

**Early Demo (Holy Grail)** : The Black Keys' very first demo, recorded by Dan Auerbach and Patrick Carney with Gary Fumovar on keyboards, was submitted to numerous indie labels, but only Alive Records showed interest. This demo is now considered a "holy grail" by hardcore fans, though Dan and Patrick themselves deem it "very substandard" and prefer it not to be heard. "That demo, the one they sent out to all these indie labels, is like the holy grail to hardcore block keys fans." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
**Formation Accident** : Dan Auerbach and Patrick Carney, who met around ages 8 or 9 and became friends in high school in Akron, Ohio (both briefly attending the University of Akron), initially planned to record a demo for Dan's solo singer-songwriter pursuits. They invited musician friends to Patrick's basement, but on the appointed day in 2001, only Dan and Patrick appeared, leading them to spontaneously form a duo. "On the appointed day, no one except Dan and Patrick showed up. So that left the two of them to form a band on their own." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
**Formation and Early Independent Success (2001)** : Dan Auerbach and Patrick Carney formed the band in 2001 in Accord, Ohio. They released a series of independent albums that, while generally well-reviewed, received limited attention beyond a dedicated fan base prior to their mainstream breakthrough. "Dan Auerbach and Patrick Carney first got together in 2001 in Accord Ohio, and they released a series of independent albums that were generally well-reviewed, but didn't receive much attention outside of a tight circle of fans." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=raRCZ0Y5up8
**Gullardi Influence on Album Title and Art** : The title *Turn Blue* was inspired by a catchphrase of Gullardi, a Cleveland late-night horror movie show host from the 1960s, serving as an homage. The album's cover art also reflects Gullardi's interest in films centered on themes of mind control, brainwashing, and hypnotism. "The title was co-opted from a catchphrase by a dude named Gullardy, who hosted a late night horror movie show in Cleveland back in the 1960s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
**Junior Kimbrough's Influence on Dan Auerbach** : Junior Kimbrough, a blues singer and the subject of The Black Keys' *Chulahoma* EP, was a profound influence on Dan Auerbach. Dan was a devoted fan in high school, and listening to Kimbrough, particularly while smoking marijuana, proved to be a pivotal experience that guided his decision to pursue a full-time career as a guitarist. "In fact, listening to Kimbrough, while smoking a lot of dope, helped him decide to pursue the guitar full time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
**Later Song Licensing** : After an initial refusal of a significant commercial licensing deal, the band later reversed its stance, licensing many of its songs for use in movies, TV shows, video games, and products from companies such as Sony, Nissan, American Express, and Victoria's Secret. This change of heart occurred when financial needs, such as paying rent and sustaining touring, became more pressing than maintaining an anti-sell-out image. "Later, they'd have a change of heart, and plenty of black keys songs would be licensed for movies and TV and products by Sony, video games, jeans, Nissan, American Express, and even Victoria's Secret." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
**Mayonnaise Commercial Offer** : Early in their career, a UK-based mayonnaise manufacturer offered The Black Keys 200,000 pounds for the use of one of their songs in a TV commercial. Despite struggling financially and touring in their 1994 Chrysler van known as "The Grey Ghost," the band declined the offer, believing it would harm their artistic integrity, a decision that infuriated their parents. "The manufacturer of a brand of mayonnaise based on the UK offered the keys 200,000 pounds for the use of one of their songs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
**Mike Tyson Announces Album** : The release of The Black Keys' *Turn Blue* album was publicly announced through a tweet by boxer Mike Tyson. "Its existence was announced by Mike Tyson, yes, him, in a tweet." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
**Patrick Carney's Side Projects and Production** : Patrick Carney operates his own indie label, Audio Eagle Records, which signed the band Drummer. He also joined Drummer in the studio, playing bass, despite the band's members all being drummers from other groups. Carney's production work includes contributing to The Sheepdogs' 2012 debut record, as well as albums for Tennis and Tobias Gesso Jr. "Patrick Carney was watching an indie band called Drummer, who was signed to his audio-eagle records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
**The White Stripes Comparison** : The Black Keys experienced an increase in interest and profile due to the rising popularity of The White Stripes, another garage-punk two-piece band. The greater success achieved by The White Stripes inadvertently helped to draw more attention to The Black Keys. "The bigger the white stripes got, the more interesting to grow in the black keys." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
