**SNL Pope John Paul II Photo Tear (October 3, 1992) – Career-Ending Television Moment** : After a series of controversies (refusing to appear on SNL while Andrew Dice Clay was host in 1990; refusing the national anthem before a New Jersey show in August 1990; skipping the 1991 Grammys despite four nominations; a perceived pro-Saddam comment in March 1991), Sinead O'Connor appeared on *Saturday Night Live* on October 3, 1992, singing Bob Marley's "War." In rehearsal she held a picture of a refugee child; live, she substituted a photo of Pope John Paul II — taken from her mother's bedroom wall — and tore it up at the word "evil," saying "fight the real enemy." There was complete silence from the studio audience. NBC received nearly 5,000 complaints and the resulting media blowback permanently ended her mainstream career. The inspiration was Bob Geldof tearing up a picture of John Travolta on *Top of the Pops* in the 1970s. "NBC received almost 5,000 complaints. The blowback in the media was intense and her career never, ever recovered." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oljjY7jO-kM
