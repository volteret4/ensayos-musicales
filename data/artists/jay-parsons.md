## curiosities
**Early Life and Entertainment Drive** : Jay grew up in Stouffville, Ontario, and was an avid basketball player, drawn to its "show off" quality as an individual within a team setting. This inherent desire to entertain extended into his high school years, where he DJ'd school events, performed announcements, and served as student council president at Markham High. His immersion in 90s hip hop and DJ culture, embracing the "four elements" of hip hop, further fueled his natural knack for captivating audiences. "I played basketball my entire young life and for me, I feel looking back on it. Um I think I like basketball because as Ash pointed out, it has that show off quality to it where it's, it's you on a team, but it's your chance to really show who you are." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ayPJfDAF_to
**Post-Graduation Struggles and Meeting Ash** : After earning a business degree, Jay faced the typical "university graduate quagmire" of being unable to secure a corporate job in downtown Toronto due to a lack of experience. He returned to his previous employment at the Angus Glen golf course in Markham. It was there, in the summer of 2004, that he serendipitously met Ash while loading beer, a meeting that led to Ash asking him to DJ his sister's wedding and ignited their future musical partnership. "the doors kept getting slammed in my face everywhere. I went because I didn't have any experience...I went back to my old job at a golf course in Markham called Angus Glen." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ayPJfDAF_to
**University Punk Band Experience** : While attending Trent University in Peterborough in the early 2000s, Jay was invited to join a punk band as a turntablist. This reflected a contemporary trend where DJs scratched records for rock bands. This experience was transformative, revealing to him that he could actively participate in a band without playing traditional instruments like guitar, bass, or drums, thereby deepening his engagement with band life. "I got asked to join a punk band because uh back in the 2000, the early 2000s DJs were scratching records for uh rock bands. So um I was one of the few turntable lists on Trent University campus. And so I joined this punk band to scratch." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ayPJfDAF_to
**Working in the Oil Fields (2007-2008)** : After Ash briefly pursued a solo path as "Ubiquitous Synergy Seeker," Jay was working in the oil fields of Northern Alberta, including Fort McMurray, when Ash invited him to rejoin for a show in April 2007. Following the release of the *Welding the C-Drive* EP, Jay returned to the oil fields, this time in Cold Lake, Alberta, to help construct a pipeline on an air weapons range, seeking income due to Toronto's high cost of living. It was during his time there, in January 2008, that he received the pivotal call about their EP gaining significant radio attention. "I was working in the oil fields in Northern Alberta I just returned from Fort McMurray...I went back to the oil fields this time I went up to cold Lake Alberta and to help build a pipeline on air weapons range." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ayPJfDAF_to
