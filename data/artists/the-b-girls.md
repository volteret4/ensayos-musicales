# artist - The B-Girls

## curiosities
**Friends with Blondie and Ramones; Almost Signed to IRS; Influenced B-52s; Background Vocals on Blondie's Autoamerican** : The B-Girls formed in Toronto in 1977 with a sound closer to British Invasion mid-1960s than hard punk, though their look and attitude were fully punk. They toured with and opened for Teenage Head, the Viletones, and eventually The Clash, then relocated to New York where they became close with Blondie, the Ramones, the Cramps, and the Dead Boys. Members sang background vocals on Blondie's *Autoamerican* (1980), had demos produced by Debbie Harry and Mick Jones of The Clash, and nearly signed with IRS Records — which chose a Los Angeles band called the Go-Go's instead. The Go-Go's were fans of the B-Girls. The B-Girls' existence and sound also influenced a band from Georgia called the B-52s. No album was released before the original lineup fell apart by 1981.
