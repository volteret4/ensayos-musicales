# artist - Ike Turner

## member of
- Jackie Brenston and his Delta Cats

## members
- Willie Kitsert (guitar)

## songs
**Rocket 88 (1951)** : Recorded around March 1, 1951, this song features accidental guitar distortion after Ike Turner's band's guitar amp, belonging to Willie Kitsert, was damaged during a drive to Memphis, Tennessee. One account suggests it fell off the car, another that it was dropped from the trunk. The speaker cone burst, and newspaper was wadded and jammed into the enclosure as a makeshift repair. The resulting sound from this damaged amp was used for the recording, making it potentially the first rock and roll song if guitar distortion is the guideline. "And the sound that came out of this amp was pretty cool." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w ← ike-turner

## curiosities
**"Spoken Ike Vocals" on "It's Gonna Work Out Fine"** : For Ike & Tina Turner's hit "It's Gonna Work Out Fine," Mickey Baker performed the "spoken Ike vocals" on the record, which Ike Turner would then perform live. **Collaboration with Mickey & Sylvia (Willow Records)** : Ike Turner played on some of Mickey & Sylvia's records released on their independent label, Willow Records. "Ag Turner played on some of their records." ← Episode 49： ＂Love is Strange＂ by Mickey and Sylvia | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkyLPVvPbcI
**Control over Tina Turner's Career** : Ike Turner, Tina Turner's abusive, controlling, and drug-addicted husband, held complete decision-making power over her musical career until she left him in 1975. This included the decision to record her first solo album, "Tina Turns the Country On!," as a country record to expand her audience. "Ike he made the decisions about her musical career." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 53 - More than Charley Pride - African Americans in 70s Country | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Danger Mouse Production Commitment** : Danger Mouse was initially unavailable to produce The Black Keys' *Attack & Release* album because he had committed to producing an album for Ike Turner. However, the project ultimately fell through, making Danger Mouse available for The Black Keys. "Unfortunately, he was unavailable because he was committed to producing an album for Ike Turner." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q ← ike-turner

**Talent Scout Role** : Ike Turner's work as a talent scout for Chess and Modern Records is referenced as a comparison to Richie Barrett's role for George Goldner, illustrating the industry practice of identifying, arranging for, and developing new musical talent. **Vocals on Rocket 88** : Ike Turner provided vocals on Jackie Brenston's "Rocket 88" in the early 1950s. This song was considered an early, "frightening" example of rock and roll, blending rhythm and blues with hillbilly and country music. "This is Jackie Brinston's Rocket 88 with Ike Turnway vocals." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U ← ike-turner
