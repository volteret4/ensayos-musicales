## curiosities
**Creation for Television** : The Monkees were a band created specifically for a television show, rather than forming organically, although its members Michael Nesmith, Peter Tork, Davy Jones, and Micky Dolenz were real actors and musicians hired to portray fictional musicians inspired by The Beatles. "The monkeys not a real band per se in that they were created for a television show." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 30 - The Sweet Sound of 70s Bubblegum Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/030-bubblegum ← the-monkees
**Davy Jones's Galactic Fame** : By 1966, the other Davy Jones, the British pop singer who prompted David Jones to change his name to Bowie, became "galactically famous" as one of the four members of this "prefabricated TV band." This occurred well after our David Jones had already made his name change. "But by 1966, this Davy Jones would become galactically famous, as one of the four members of the prefabricated TV band The Monkeys." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Starman to Blackstar Edition Part 1 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/696aa42a4796fcbb571adba7
**Early Influence on Derek Whibley** : Around 1986, at the age of six, Derek Whibley became deeply obsessed with The Monkees Television Show, an early experience that, along with The Beatles' music, inspired his desire to become a musician. "I was really obsessed with the Monkeys Television Show." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6L4xKBFEYkE ← the-monkees
**First Made-for-TV Pop Band (1966)** : The Monkees were assembled in late 1965 by ABC executives aiming to capitalize on The Beatles' success, making them the first made-for-TV pop band. Each half-hour sitcom-style show featured at least one music video-type sequence, leading to the band's rapid rise and a long string of hit singles. "released by Mike Nessmith, formerly of the monkeys." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oMBmdfrA47Q ← the-monkees
**Manufactured TV Band** : The Monkees were described as a "manufactured TV band from the 1960s."
