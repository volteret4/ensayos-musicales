## songs
**Not Your Steppin' Stone (1967)** : This song was the B-side to The Monkees' single "I'm a Believer." It gained an unexpected punk rock association when the Sex Pistols, surprisingly fans of the manufactured 1960s TV band, frequently performed roaring live versions of it. "When the pistols played live, they often roared through this B side to the monkey single, I'm a believer. This is a not your step-in-stone." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gkLMwrthZ9s
**Star Collector (1967)** : This song is noted as one of the very first instances, if not the first, of a synthesizer being used on a record. "The monkeys were among the first, if not the very first, to use a synth on a record. This appeared in a 1967 song called Star Collector." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0fQQubt9UTI

## curiosities
**Former Band of Name Originator** : Mike Nesmith, the individual whose VHS tape provided the phrase for The Tragically Hip's name, was formerly a member of the popular band The Monkees. "released by Mike Nessmith, formerly of the monkeys." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oMBmdfrA47Q
**Manufactured TV Band** : The Monkees were described as a "manufactured TV band from the 1960s." Surprisingly, they were admired by the Sex Pistols, who often covered their B-side "Not Your Steppin' Stone" live. "Yeah, that manufactured TV band from the 1960s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gkLMwrthZ9s
**US Concert Tour with The Jimi Hendrix Experience** : The Monkees embarked on a concert tour of the U.S., which was notably joined by The Jimi Hendrix Experience starting July 29. The tour was promoted by Kina. "Who grew up with the monkeys is called the Jimmy Hendrix experience and they joined the monkeys concert tour of the U.S." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8q2bRZyHnHE
