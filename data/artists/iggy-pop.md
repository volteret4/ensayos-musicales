## albums
**Lust for Life (1977) – Title Track** : The title track features one of the "coolest rock beats ever recorded." The drum beat was inspired by the theme song of a German news program that Iggy Pop and David Bowie watched while trying to "straighten out their lives in Berlin." Bowie picked up on the theme and gave Iggy the idea for the beat. "Still one of the coolest rock beats ever recorded." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo
**Raw Power (1973)** : This album was created with the patronage of David Bowie, who convinced his manager to invest cash into The Stooges project. The album was intentionally designed to be extremely intense, aiming to "actually hurt people as it came out of the speakers." It features the song "Search and Destroy." "The result was an injection of cash that was enough to make an album that was designed to be so intense that it would actually hurt people as it came out of the speakers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4

## curiosities
**Accidental Guitar Incident and Stage Persona Birth (October 30-31, 1967)** : The day before The Stooges' first show, guitarist Ron Ashton painted Iggy's Hawaiian guitar, including the pickups, rendering it useless. Iggy took the guitar on stage anyway, then abandoned it, dancing "like a nut" as his pants began to fall down. When the audience reacted positively, believing it was part of the show, Iggy amplified his erratic behavior, which was the foundational moment for his punk rock stage persona. "And it was this event that set Iggy on the road to becoming the Godfather of Punk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2RKchFW42Jc
**Bowie's Admiration and Support** : David Bowie considered Iggy Pop one of his musical heroes, captivated by Iggy's craziness, mental instability, and intelligence that Bowie saw beyond the drug use and wild stage persona. Bowie became Iggy's patron, securing funding for The Stooges' *Raw Power* album. When Iggy eventually ended up in a mental hospital after the band's collapse, Bowie was his only visitor. "Bowie saw that Iggy was actually very, very intelligent." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
**Drug Use and Mental Instability** : Around the same time Jonathan Richmond was recording "Roadrunner" (circa 1971), Iggy Pop and The Stooges were dropped from their record label and were increasingly involved with hard drugs. Iggy was known for being in an almost constant state of mental instability, characterized by crazy, animalistic, demonic, and self-destructive stage antics. "They were also getting deeper into hard, hard drugs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
**Early 1970s Psychotic Break – Voluntary Psychiatric Commitment** : In the early 1970s, Iggy Pop spent a large record-label advance on drugs and suffered a psychotic episode. Given the choice between jail and a Los Angeles psychiatric institute, he voluntarily chose hospitalization. His only visitor during his stay was David Bowie, who was himself deeply immersed in his own drug haze at the time. "His only visitor was David Bowie, also immersed in his own drug fog." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RKvFJMkZdBE
**Early Career as a Drummer** : Before gaining fame as Iggy Pop, James Jewel Osterberg was a drummer in Detroit in the mid-1960s. He backed Motown groups like The Shangri-La's and The Temptations when they played in Michigan, and also played in a local band called The Iguanas. "James Jewel Osterberg was a drummer from Detroit who had spent much of the middle 60s backing up Motown groups like the Shangri-La's and the temptations whenever they played around Michigan." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2RKchFW42Jc
**Early Drumming Career – Motown Gigs** : In the mid-sixties, Iggy Pop began his musical career as a drummer for the Detroit band The Iguanas, playing gigs around Michigan and recording several songs. He was also hired locally by Motown acts like The Shangri-La's, The Temptations, and The Four Tops for their live gigs, as they toured with minimal musicians and hired bassists and drummers on location. He was known for being able to pick up almost any song within seconds. "Iggy was one of the guys These Motown acts would hire to play drums for them." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo
**Early Music Career Start (1965)** : Iggy Pop began making music in 1965, marking an early start in the business, though not the longest-running as identified by the record. **Elton John Gorilla Suit Incident – Richards, Atlanta, October 1973** : In October 1973, Iggy Pop and the Stooges were playing at Richards in Atlanta at the same time Elton John was touring America. Elton — engaged in a semi-friendly rivalry with David Bowie, who was a known Stooges supporter — decided to crash the show dressed in a full gorilla suit to surprise Iggy on stage. A photographer from Detroit music magazine *Creem* was tipped off. No one warned Iggy that this was planned, and no one warned Elton that Iggy was heavily intoxicated on speed that night. When Elton bounded onto the stage in the gorilla suit, Iggy genuinely believed it was a real gorilla, was terrified, and fled the stage. The incident was captured on film. After Elton removed the gorilla head and multiple people calmed Iggy down, the show resumed and the two performed several songs together — Iggy bare-chested, Elton still in the suit. "Iggy thought it was an actual gorilla. And this freak out is captured on film." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7lpjI8F2Apk

**Origin of Name "Iggy Pop"** : After shaving off his eyebrows during a period of heavy drinking, someone remarked that Jimmy Osterberg looked like a local drug addict named Jim Pop. This observation led to him adopting the stage name Iggy Pop. "And after he shaved off his eyebrows after drinking too much, someone remarked that he looked an awful lot like a local drugie called Jim Pop.
