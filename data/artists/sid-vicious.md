## curiosities
**Fatal Overdose (1979)** : The night before his trial, on February 1, 1979, Sid attended a party in his honor at his new girlfriend Michelle Robinson's home, with his mother and friends present. His mother supplied him with heroin, and later he took more from her purse. He overdosed and was put to bed around 3 AM. No doctor was called to avoid violating his bail conditions. The next morning, he was found dead, naked, on top of the covers. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9T9wi0vEvc
**Heroin Addiction** : Sid Vicious developed a severe heroin addiction while he was with the Sex Pistols. After the band broke up, his addiction continued, impacting his ability to maintain a responsible or coherent life with his American girlfriend, Nancy Spungen. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9T9wi0vEvc
**Murder Charge and Bail (1979)** : Sid Vicious was charged with Nancy Spungen's murder, with his trial scheduled for February 2, 1979. He was released from jail on $50,000 bail prior to the trial. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9T9wi0vEvc
**Nancy Spungen's Death (1978)** : On October 12, 1978, Sid Vicious awoke from a heroin haze to discover his girlfriend, Nancy Spungen, dead from stab wounds in the bathroom of their room at the Chelsea Hotel in New York, with his knife found in her stomach. The exact circumstances of her death—whether it was murder, suicide, or a drug deal gone wrong—remain unknown. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9T9wi0vEvc
**Posthumous Icon Status** : Sid Vicious died just over three months before his 22nd birthday. His death solidified his status as a punk icon, inspiring numerous books and movies about him and Nancy, and cementing his legend within the punk movement. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9T9wi0vEvc
**Real Name and Nickname Origin** : Sid Vicious's birth name was John Simon Richie. His friend Johnny Lydon (Johnny Rotten) gave him the nickname "Sid" after Lydon's pet hamster. The "Vicious" part of his name was inspired by a Lou Reed song. Despite being initially "quite a gentle guy," Richie felt compelled to live up to the "Vicious" title. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9T9wi0vEvc
**Solo Career Attempt** : Prior to his death, Sid Vicious attempted to launch a solo career, which included his cover of The Heartbreakers' song "Born to Lose." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9T9wi0vEvc
**Suicide Note and Death Pact Theory** : The purity of the heroin he consumed was known to Sid, having been informed by his mother. A note later found in his passport supported the theory of suicide, stating a "death pact" with Nancy and requesting burial next to her in his leather jacket, jeans, and motorcycle boots. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9T9wi0vEvc
**Violent Persona and Behavior** : Sid Vicious cultivated a violent persona, often using a length of bicycle chain or a broken bottle as weapons. During his time with the Sex Pistols, he was known to inflict "serious damage" on fans, journalists, and other musicians. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9T9wi0vEvc
