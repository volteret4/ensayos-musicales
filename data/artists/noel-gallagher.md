It was described as more experimental and marked the first time Noel Gallagher did not produce himself, instead collaborating with David Holmes, a "very old school British electronic artist." The album successfully innovates Noel Gallagher's sound while still appealing to fans of his classic style, evolving his music without losing his loyal audience and attracting new listeners. It incorporates experimental elements not as electronic music itself, but through the use of noises, loops, and samples from various sources, such as a woman with scissors, a telephone ringing, or car sounds. These unexpected elements are integrated based on a solid understanding of his core sound. The album's structure begins with a long instrumental piece, filled with saturated sounds and noises, designed to prepare the listener for the blend of mixed influences and his classic sound. While the instrumental aspects and production are highly praised, the lyrics are noted as weaker and more "banal" compared to his previous work, suggesting a focus on sound over lyrical complexity.
