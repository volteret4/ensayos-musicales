## curiosities
**Ahead of Their Time and Breakup (1977)** : Death was considered too far ahead of their time, leading to their breakup in 1977 and subsequent obscurity for many years. "Unfortunately though, they were too far ahead of their time, and they broke up in 1977, and were pretty much forgotten." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Band Name Change Origin** : The band originally formed in 1971 under the name Rockfire Funk Express. After their father died in a car accident, the brothers changed their band's name to Death, reflecting a profound personal impact. "But then their father died in a car accident and that's when rockfire Funk Express changed their name to death." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
**Epiphany from The Who and Alice Cooper** : The band, originally playing funk, experienced a musical epiphany after seeing performances by The Who and Alice Cooper, leading them towards a harder sound. "They began as a funk band, but after seeing the who and Alice Cooper, they had an epiphany." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Formation in Detroit (1971)** : Death was formed in Detroit in 1971 by two brothers and a friend, initially starting as a funk band. "They were called Death, two brothers and a friend who formed the band in Detroit in 1971." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Limited Early Release and Rediscovery** : Death's early records, produced in runs of only about 500 copies, largely went unnoticed, and the band broke up in 1977. However, they experienced a rediscovery in 2009 when their early 1970s recordings began to circulate, leading to reconsideration of their place in punk history. "They started making records that sadly went largely unnoticed, partly because they only came out and runs of maybe 500 copies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
**Possible First Punk Band** : Death, a band formed by three Black brothers from Detroit (David, Bobby, and Dennis Hackney) in 1971 after their father's death, is argued to be the first punk band. Inspired by The Beatles' Ed Sullivan performance in 1964, their early 1970s recordings, rediscovered in 2009, sounded like convincing punk rock possibly predating The Ramones' 1974 debut. "It is possible that death was making music like this before the Ramones played their first gig in 1974." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
**Pre-Punk Pioneers** : Retroactively, Death, composed of three black men from Detroit, is now considered one of the most important pre-punk bands in America, having been significantly overlooked in their original era. "three black men from Detroit, who, albeit retroactively, are considered to be one of the most important pre-punk bands in America." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Revival by Sons' Band Rough Frances** : The music of Death was rediscovered and brought back to public attention when the three sons of one of the original members formed a band called Rough Frances and began covering their father's songs after finding out about his past online. "But then, the three sons of one of the members started covering their dad's songs in a band called Rough Frances after discovering their dad's previous life online." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
