## albums
**For All the World to See (Released by Drag City Records)** : This album, released by Drag City Records decades after its initial recording, compiled the seven songs commissioned by Columbia in 1975. Its release led to the belated recognition of Death as pioneers of punk rock, introducing their sound to a world finally ready for it. "One thing led to another and those old master tapes from 1975 made their way to a label called Drag City Records, who released those seven songs commissioned by Columbia on an album entitled For All the World to See." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**For the Whole World To See (2009) – Reissue of 1975 Material** : This album, released in 2009, featured the original tapes from 1975, showcasing music that was ahead of its time. ## songs
**Early 1970s Recordings (Rediscovered 2009)** : Death's early 1970s recordings, rediscovered in 2009, contained music that sounded remarkably "punky." The band, composed of three Black brothers from Detroit, may have been creating this music before the Ramones' first gig in 1974, suggesting they could be the first punk band. "Recording from the early 1970s started making the rounds and damn these songs, sure, sounded pretty punky." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
**Keep On Knockin (1975/2009)** : This track, by three black men from Detroit, is from their material originally recorded in 1975 and reissued in 2009, highlighting their retroactive recognition as one of America's most important pre-punk bands. "From Detroit, that's Death with Keep On Knockin, three black men from Detroit, who, albeit retroactively, are considered to be one of the most important pre-punk bands in America." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Politicians in My Eyes (1975)** : This song, released as a seven-inch single in 1975, became a highly sought-after collector's item, exchanging hands for up to a thousand dollars. It was later digitized and shared as MP3s, eventually leading to the band's rediscovery, and is now recognized as an important example of proto-punk music from 1975. "Meanwhile though, a seven inch single for a song called Politicians in My Eyes became a collector's item, exchanging hands for up to a thousand dollars." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY

## curiosities
**Ahead of Their Time and Breakup (1977)** : Death was considered too far ahead of their time, leading to their breakup in 1977 and subsequent obscurity for many years. "Unfortunately though, they were too far ahead of their time, and they broke up in 1977, and were pretty much forgotten." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Band Name Change Origin** : The band originally formed in 1971 under the name Rockfire Funk Express. After their father died in a car accident, the brothers changed their band's name to Death, reflecting a profound personal impact. "But then their father died in a car accident and that's when rockfire Funk Express changed their name to death." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
**Belated Recognition as Punk Pioneers** : Upon the release of their album "For All the World to See," the music world reacted with surprise, recognizing it as "punk" and belatedly crediting "three black guys from Detroit making punk rock before the music even had a name" with helping to create the modern punk rock sound. "When people heard this, the reaction was, wow, this is punk. Three black guys from Detroit making punk rock before the music even had a name." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Columbia Records Dispute Over Name** : In 1975, Columbia Records expressed interest in the band and paid for the recording of seven songs, but demanded they change their name from "Death" due to concerns about commercial viability. When the band refused, Columbia withdrew its offer, claiming a group with that name would have "zero commercial success." "In 1975, Columbia Records expressed interest in the band and paid for the recording of seven songs. And while the music was good, the label did not like the band's name and demanded that they find something else. When death refused, Columbia bailed, saying that a group with that name would have zero commercial success." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**David Hackney's Death and Brothers' Continued Music** : David Hackney passed away from lung cancer in 1982, years before the band's rediscovery. His brothers, Bobby and Julian, continue to play with various groups in Vermont and have participated in a few reunions of Death. "Whereas death now, well, David Hackney died of lung cancer in 1982. The other two brothers are still in Vermont and still playing with various groups, and there have been a few reunions along the way." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY

**Limited Early Release and Rediscovery** : Death's early records, produced in runs of only about 500 copies, largely went unnoticed, and the band broke up in 1977. However, they experienced a rediscovery in 2009 when their early 1970s recordings began to circulate, leading to reconsideration of their place in punk history. "They started making records that sadly went largely unnoticed, partly because they only came out and runs of maybe 500 copies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
**Possible First Punk Band** : Death, a band formed by three Black brothers from Detroit (David, Bobby, and Dennis Hackney) in 1971 after their father's death, is argued to be the first punk band. Inspired by The Beatles' Ed Sullivan performance in 1964, their early 1970s recordings, rediscovered in 2009, sounded like convincing punk rock possibly predating The Ramones' 1974 debut. "It is possible that death was making music like this before the Ramones played their first gig in 1974." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
**Post-Breakup - The Fourth Movement** : After Death broke up two years later (around 1977), the Hackney brothers moved to Vermont and began singing and recording gospel songs under the name The Fourth Movement, and the original band was "almost completely forgotten." "Two years later, the band was done. Everybody moved to Vermont and switched to singing and recording gospel songs under the name The Fourth Movement." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Pre-Punk Pioneers** : Retroactively, Death, composed of three black men from Detroit, is now considered one of the most important pre-punk bands in America, having been significantly overlooked in their original era. "three black men from Detroit, who, albeit retroactively, are considered to be one of the most important pre-punk bands in America." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Rediscovery Through MP3s** : Decades later, Julian Hackney's son discovered his college roommate playing one of Death's digitized MP3s, leading him to exclaim, "That's my dad!" This chance encounter ultimately led to the release of their 1975 master tapes by Drag City Records. "That music inevitably ended up being digitized and began training hands as MP3s through the early 21st century. In one day, Julian Hackney, the son of Bobby, heard his college roommate playing one of those MP3s. That's my dad, he said." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Reunion Performances** : Following their rediscovery, the band was enticed to reunite and even performed at one of Joey Ramone's Memorial birthday parties, a testament to their newly recognized status in punk history. "The band was enticed to reunite and even performed at one of Joey Ramones Memorial birthday parties." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
