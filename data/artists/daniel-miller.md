## curiosities
**Creator of The Normal and "Warm Leatherette" (1978)** : Daniel Miller was the driving force behind the group The Normal, and he crafted the song "Warm Leatherette" (November 1978) by drawing inspiration from Kraftwerk, Lou Reed, and Giorgio Moroder. This composition contributed to the emerging "cold wave" sound. "Daniel Miller, who's the guy behind the normal, took some elements of craft work, Lou Reed and George Eomeroder, to create this." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE ← daniel-miller ← daniel-miller
**Founder of Mute Records** : Daniel Miller founded Mute Records using the funds he earned from his unexpected hit single, "Warm Leatherette." His vision for Mute was to create an "all synthesizer label," a focus that proved highly successful as keyboard-driven music gained traction on British charts after 1981. "Daniel Miller, who took the money he made from a surprise hit single of his called Warm Leatherat and founded an independent record company called Mut." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zB5zxycrbnY ← daniel-miller
