## curiosities
**Artistic Sensibility** : The Sex Pistols are mentioned among a group of bands that possess an inherent artistic sensibility without being "over the top," often attributed to members having attended art school, studied art, or collected art. "You too, the cure, Marilyn Manson, Roxy Music, the Sex Pistols, Blur, they all get it without going over the top, mainly because each one of them either A went to art school, or B studied art, or C collect art." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RWbKqqW78yg
**Covered Eddie Cochran Songs** : The Sex Pistols covered songs by Eddie Cochran, who wrote about teenage boredom, demonstrating Cochran's influence on later generations of punk artists. "Eddie Cochrane, who songs about teenage boredom were later covered by sedentions of the sex pistols." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U
**Early Fan Base** : Siouxsie Sioux was noted as one of the Sex Pistols' first fans and was present in the audience during their scandalous TV appearance where they swore at the host. "Susie Su is another person with connections to the sex pistols. She was one of their first fans." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oPgeSCy93bo
**First Gig (November 1975)** : The Sex Pistols played their first gig in November 1975, the same month Patti Smith released her influential debut album "Horses." They were contemporaries of The Slits and a foundational band of the punk movement. "That was the same month the sex pistols played their first gig" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Qss5kC2FUx0
**First Live Gig – St. Martins (November 6, 1975)** : The band's debut performance took place at an art school in London, St. Martins, as an unexpected opening act for Bizzouca Joe. They reportedly lasted only about two songs before the school's social programmer halted their performance by pulling the plug. This event, despite its unceremonious ending, was considered a pivotal moment that changed music. "music was never to be quite the same." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oPgeSCy93bo
**Impact on UK Music Scene (1975)** : The Sex Pistols were identified as one of three bands, alongside The Clash and The Saints, that "changed the music forever in the UK," following the media frenzy over The Saints' 1976 single. Their emergence in England coincided with the germination of punk rock in New York by 1975. "Because of that media freak out, three bands changed the music forever in the UK, the sex pistols, the clash, and the Saints." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4
**Manchester Gig Inspiration (Spring 1976)** : A performance by the Sex Pistols in Manchester, though described as "terrible" by attendees Bernie and Peter, was nonetheless found to be "great" in its raw energy. This concert directly inspired Bernie and Peter to form their own band, which would eventually become Joy Division. "He says, they were terrible. I thought they were great. I wanted to get up there and be terrible too." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oPgeSCy93bo
**Opening Act for The 101ers – Nashville Club** : The band opened for The 101ers at a club called Nashville. Despite their "awful" musicianship, their attitude and energy profoundly impressed Joe, the guitarist for The 101ers, motivating him to leave his band and form The Clash. "It was their attitude and their energy that grabbed his attention." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oPgeSCy93bo
**Rolling Stone Magazine Cover (1977)** : The Sex Pistols were featured on the cover of the October 20th, 1977, edition of Rolling Stone magazine, which carried the headline, "Rock is sick and living in London." This highlights punk's impact and controversy in the late 70s. "The October 20th, 1977 edition of Rolling Stone magazine had the sex pistols on the cover, but the headline, Rock is sick and living in London." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
**TV Show Scandal (December 1, 1976)** : The Sex Pistols appeared on a popular television program where they used "bad words," leading to a significant scandal across Britain. This incident was widely discussed and served as a catalyst for future musicians, inspiring Declan McManus (later Elvis Costello) to pursue a career in music. "All of Britain was talking about it. Terrible, terrible. These young men on TV." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oPgeSCy93bo
