## albums
**Tina Turns the Country On! (1974)** : Released in 1974, this was Tina Turner's first solo album and was a country record. The decision to make a country album was influenced by her then-husband Ike Turner, who aimed to expose her talents to a wider audience. The album largely consisted of covers and received mixed critical reviews, yet earned her a Grammy nomination in an R&B category, not country. "her first solo album released in 1974 was a country record Tina turns the country on." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 53 - More than Charley Pride - African Americans in 70s Country | https://www.ftr70.com/

## songs
**GoldenEye (1995) – Title Theme** : Tina Turner performed the title theme for the 1995 James Bond film "GoldenEye," Pierce Brosnan's first movie as Bond. The song was written by Bono and The Edge of U2, making it essentially a U2 song "disguised as a Tina Turner performance." It became a decent hit in various parts of the world, including the UK Top 10 and Top 5 in several European countries. "The producers approached Tina Turner to do the theme and then she was connected with Bondo in the edge of U2 to write the song so they did." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K0pgJ7XAlpo ← tina-turner
**Good Hearted Woman (Late 1970s)** : Tina Turner covered the song "Good Hearted Woman" in the late 1970s. The song's origin was indirectly inspired by Tina Turner; Waylon Jennings saw an advertisement that referenced many of Tina's songs being about "Good-hearted women loving two-timing men," which prompted him to write the song. "She also, by the way, did a cover of that song in the late 1970s." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 53 - More than Charley Pride - African Americans in 70s Country | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Help Me Make It Through the Night (1974)** : Tina Turner covered "Help Me Make It Through the Night" on her 1974 album "Tina Turns the Country On!" The song was written and originally recorded by Kris Kristofferson in 1970, with Sammi Smith having the biggest commercially successful cover in 1971. Turner's rendition, while "wonderful," was noted for being "still bluesy," which may have influenced its Grammy nomination in an R&B rather than a country category. "This is Tina Turner with her cover of Help Me Make It Through The Night." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 53 - More than Charley Pride - African Americans in 70s Country | https://www.ftr70.com/

**Ike Turner's Control and Career Decisions** : Prior to Tina Turner running away from him in Dallas, Texas, in 1975, her abusive, controlling, and drug-addicted husband Ike Turner made all decisions regarding her musical career. The decision to record her first solo album as a country record, "Tina Turns the Country On!," was his, made with the intent of broadening her audience.
