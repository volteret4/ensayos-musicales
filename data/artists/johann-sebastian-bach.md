**El Clave bien Temperado** : A composition by Bach, notably interpreted by Glenn Gold, that was included on the "Golden Record" aboard the Voyager One space probe. This recording forms part of an interstellar message intended for potential extraterrestrial civilizations, designed to showcase Earth's rich cultural heritage. **Goldberg Variations** : This piece gained significant recognition and launched Glenn Gold to fame through his interpretations. Gold selected it for his first major recording in 1955 and later recorded a second, more introspective version in 1981. His interpretations were widely praised for their unique precision, energy, and his ability to unveil the intricate internal structure of the music. "La obra que años después le lanzaría a la fama." ← El Pianista que Llevó a Bach al Cosmos ｜ Glenn Gould | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WpwjSQr8Qco

## curiosities
**Glenn Gold's Philosophical View of Bach** : Glenn Gold deeply revered Johann Sebastian Bach, regarding him as the embodiment of music's fundamental criteria—a perfect and harmonious balance between emotion and logic. Gold considered Bach to be the ultimate genesis point of all music, the foundational source from which musicians across subsequent generations would draw inspiration and influence. "Para Gold Bach encarnaba los criterios fundamentales de la música, el equilibrio perfecto entre emoción y lógica." ← El Pianista que Llevó a Bach al Cosmos ｜ Glenn Gould | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WpwjSQr8Qco
**Post-Mortem Revival** : Johann Sebastian Bach was one of the most important and influential composers of all time during his life, but after his death in March 1685, he was almost forgotten until a Bach revival in the 19th century made him a retro hero in classical music. "The only reason we talk about Bach today is because there was a Bach revival in the 19th century." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1VWJ0uCQx8w ← johann-sebastian-bach ← johann-sebastian-bach
