# artist - The Sam Willows

## songs
**Glass House** : This song by the Singaporean band The Sam Willows was produced by Steve Lillywhite. The band spent four 12-hour days in a studio in Singapore working on it, and Lillywhite then took the tracks to his studio in Los Angeles for mixing. The original version was described as a "Mumford and Son's Fokie thing without a proper drummer." "From Singapore, that's the Sam Willows, with the Steve Lindley White produced version of a song called Glass House." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM

## curiosities
**Band Without a Drummer** : The Sam Willows are noted for not having a proper drummer. Their song "Glass House" was originally described as a "Mumford and Son's Fokie thing" before Steve Lillywhite's production transformed it. "The song originally was a nice, well, Mumford and Son's Fokie thing without a proper drummer. In fact, the band doesn't have a drummer." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
**Steve Lillywhite Production Week in Singapore** : The Sam Willows, a band from Singapore, worked with British producer Steve Lillywhite for a week in a local studio as part of a project initiated by the local Music Industry Association. They put in "four 12-hour days" recording one of their songs, with Lillywhite later mixing the tracks in his LA studio. "The group is called the Sam Willows, and they put in four 12-hour days in a studio, and then Steve took the tracks back with him to LA to mix it in his own studio." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
