# artist - Talk Show

## members
- David Coots
- David Coutts
- Dean DeLeo
- Eric Kretz
- Robert DeLeo
- Three members of Stone Temple Pilots (excluding Scott Weiland)

## albums
**Talk Show (1998) – Solo Album** : Released by the other three members of Stone Temple Pilots (Dean DeLeo, Robert DeLeo, Eric Kretz) with singer David Coots while Scott Wyland worked on his solo material. The album did not perform well, selling only around 60,000 copies, and did not receive the positive critical reception that Wyland's "12 Bar Blues" did. "It also didn't do very well, selling maybe 60,000 copies, and it did not get the good reviews that 12 bar blues got either." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TDwagoyMvck

## curiosities
**Artistic Underperformance** : The band was considered a "disaster" and demonstrated that its members were "merely competent rock band" without Scott Weiland, especially when compared to Weiland's solo artistic output. "While Talk Show demonstrated that they were a merely competent rock band, Wyland's record showed that he had something that made STP special." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-f9jhqAHkQo
**Post-STP Project** : Formed by the instrumental members of Stone Temple Pilots after their split with Scott Weiland in 1997. "The other three guys formed a group called Talk Show". ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-f9jhqAHkQo
**STP Hiatus Project** : During a hiatus of Stone Temple Pilots in 1998, which was intended to help Scott Weiland with his issues, the other three members (Dean DeLeo, Robert DeLeo, and Eric Kretz) formed a new project called Talk Show with singer David Coutts. This project "did not work out very well," leading to the original STP lineup reuniting. "At that point, the other three quarters of STP formed a new project called Talk Show with the singer named David Coots, which did not work out very well and STP was tuned back together in its original form." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vLZla5h4xKQ
**Therapeutic for Band Members (1998)** : The other Stone Temple Pilots members found the process of making the "Talk Show" record to be "therapeutic" for them, much like Scott Wyland found his solo album therapeutic. This experience taught them valuable lessons, particularly about their chemistry and mutual importance. "I think after the talk show thing, I think the talk show record was really therapeutic for all of us to make." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TDwagoyMvck
