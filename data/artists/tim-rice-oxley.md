## curiosities
**Early Piano Lessons and Beatles Influence** : Tim Rice-Oxley took piano lessons as a teenager but became disengaged by the classical curriculum. He subsequently ceased formal lessons and developed his unique style primarily through extensive listening to The Beatles' records, which became a significant influence on his musical approach. "One took piano lessons as a teenager, but grew tired of all the classical material he had to play. So he stopped with the lessons and started listening to a lot of Beatles records, and that's where most of his style developed." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dJiSZ4X0F2g
**Shift from Bass to Piano in Keane** : Initially the bass player for Keane, Tim Rice-Oxley transitioned to piano after the band's guitarist departed around five years into their formation, leaving a "big hole" in their sound. This change allowed him to fulfill his desire to play piano, specifically favoring non-digital instruments. "He'd been the bass player until that point, and although he wanted to play the piano, he really didn't want to use anything digital." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dJiSZ4X0F2g
**Signature Piano Sound with Yamaha CP70BM** : Tim Rice-Oxley acquired a Yamaha CP70BM, a hybrid acoustic electric grand piano, after learning that Beatles producer George Martin highly regarded it. Upon tracking down a used one and plugging it in, the instrument produced a "massive sound" that immediately became Keane's defining characteristic and opened up new musical possibilities, leading to alt rock hits. "He acquired a Yamaha CP70BM, a hybrid acoustic electric grand piano, after reading that the Beatles producer George Martin really liked this thing. He eventually tracked down a used one, and here's a quote, as soon as we plugged it in, it made this massive sound, and we immediately knew it was the sound of Keene." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dJiSZ4X0F2g
