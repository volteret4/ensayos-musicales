## curiosities
**Fifth Symphony as Torture** : The music of Beethoven, specifically a couple of notes from his Fifth Symphony, was used as a trigger for psychological conditioning in the film "Clockwork Orange." The protagonist, Alex, was made to get very sick when exposed to sex and violence, with Beethoven's music inducing violent retching. "A couple of notes from the fifth symphony, and he was wretching on the floor." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=R1wdsGt07Gg
**Use of Devil's Triad** : Beethoven, a seminal classical composer, is cited among numerous others who incorporated the "devil's tri tone" (flattened fifth) into his compositions. This demonstrates that dissonant chords, often later associated with "evil" or "devil's music," were historically employed by revered classical musicians for expressive purposes. "But then it did show up in a lot of classical compositions from people like Beethoven, Wagner, Debussy, Lyst, Sabelius, Bartok, dozens of others." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=41eKVp8JIhU
