**Band's Struggles in Early 90s** : In the early 1990s, Depeche Mode faced significant internal challenges, largely due to extensive drug and alcohol abuse. Martin Gore experienced severe seizures that prompted doctors to suspect a brain tumor, while Andy Fletcher suffered debilitating panic attacks. Keyboardist Alan Wilder eventually quit the band, unable to cope with the circumstances. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pmKjZzypzvg

**Dave Gahan Vampire Conspiracy** : During the early 1990s, singer Dave Gahan's struggles with heroin and cocaine contributed to a conspiracy theory that he was an actual vampire. Fueling this theory was his rock-god persona and the commissioning of a coffin-shaped bed for touring, which he used for pre-show naps. Combined with a rumored incident where he bit a journalist on the neck backstage and bizarre behavior (such as painting the walls, ceilings, and floors of his house black), the vampire theory gained traction. Even as the public internet was nascent, "vampiric looking" pictures of Gahan circulated, and "People lost the fanguing in sizeurs too." In a 2001 interview, Gahan himself admitted, "I definitely could have been a vampire in my own head. Even the bed I slept in in Los Angeles was in the shape of a coffin, a huge double bed shaped like a coffin. My whole life was spinal tap at that time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pmKjZzypzvg

**Martin Gore Assumes Songwriting Role (Post-1981)** : Following Vince Clarke's departure, Martin Gore, an ex-bank clerk, stepped up to fill the void as the band's chief songwriter. Since Clarke left, Gore has been responsible for writing "99.9%" of Depeche Mode's material. His prolific songwriting has been instrumental in the band's success, helping Depeche Mode become one of the biggest "new rock" bands of all time. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Somq2MoRi5Q
**On-Stage Tape Machines** : Early Depeche Mode videos show them using prominently displayed reel-to-reel tape machines on stage for pre-recorded rhythm tracks. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**Riot for Album Release** : in March 1990, 25,000 people rioted outside a record store in Los Angeles during an autograph session for their *Violator* album, an unusual occurrence for an alternative band at the time. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Transition to Synth Band** : Depeche Mode switched from being a guitar band primarily for two reasons. Firstly, they felt the sound of guitars was "very limited" and found synthesizers "very interesting" due to the sounds they could produce. Secondly, synthesizers offered significantly easier transport in the early days, as they could be carried "under our arms on a train" and plugged directly into a PA system, unlike the logistical problems associated with transporting guitars and amplifiers. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oPgeSCy93bo
**Vince Clarke's Departure (December 1, 1981)** : Vince Clarke, who was the band's sole songwriter at the time, announced he was quitting Depeche Mode on December 1, 1981. His reason for leaving was a desire to be in a band that would stay in the studio and avoid touring. This departure left Depeche Mode without a primary songwriter, creating a significant challenge for the band's future. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Somq2MoRi5Q
