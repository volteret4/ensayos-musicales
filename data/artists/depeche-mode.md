**Music for the Masses (1987)** : Depeche Mode's 1987 album cover features a beige background with the band's logo and album title in stark block letters. **Vince Clark's Departure (December 1, 1981)** : On December 1, 1981, chief songwriter Vince Clark left Depeche Mode, believing the group had little future and that he could achieve greater success on his own. While his subsequent projects like Yazoo and Eurasia had hits, Depeche Mode's overall album sales (over 50 million) ultimately surpassed his post-band career. "The date was December 1, 1981. The first member and chief son writer Vince Clark decided that he wanted out. Why? Well, he didn't think the group had much of a future." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3oF7iPRONnQ
**Violator Autograph Session Riot (March 20, 1990)** : On March 20, 1990, an autograph session for Depeche Mode's album *Violator* at The Warehouse music store in Los Angeles escalated into an infamous riot. Thousands of fans overwhelmed private security, prompting the LAPD to call a tactical alert and dispatch 20 police units, with additional ambulances later required. The event resulted in multiple arrests, injuries including one person being crushed, and damage to a Fox News microwave van, forcing the band to leave early.
