# artist - Chester Bennington

## curiosities
**Audition for Zero** : Chester was contacted by a VP of A&R at Zomba Music and asked to audition for a promising band in LA called Zero, which needed a frontman. He received two tapes from Zero—one with existing vocals by Mark Wakefield and another with instrumentals. He recorded his vocal interpretations over the instrumental tape and sent it back, successfully securing the position. "Chester was asked to provide some vocal interpretations for the music, record them over the instrumentals and then send the tape back." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
**Contribution to Linkin Park's Name** : Chester Bennington was credited with suggesting the name "Linkin Park" after driving past a real Lincoln Park in Santa Monica. The band also appreciated the national appeal of a name associated with Abraham Lincoln. "But it was Chester who came up with the name that stuck. He was driving past a real Lincoln Park in Santa Monica." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
**Date of Death and Cause** : Chester Bennington, the vocalist for Linkin Park, was confirmed dead by his own hand on Thursday, July 20, 2017. His death came as a shock and was widely considered unexpected, even among a series of other musician deaths around that time. "Chester Bennington, vocalist with Lincoln Park, was not only dead, but dead by his own hand." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
**Death by Suicide (2017)** : Chester Bennington died on July 20, 2017, by hanging himself from a door in his bedroom, discovered by a housekeeper. The date of his death eerily coincided with what would have been Chris Cornell's 53rd birthday, suggesting a potential connection to Cornell's passing two months prior, which was described as breaking "something in Chester." Despite a vacation with his family in Arizona, Bennington returned to LA for an alleged Linkin Park video shoot, but "something went very wrong" that evening. No drugs or significant alcohol were found, and his autopsy revealed nothing of concern in his blood, leaving his motivations largely unknown. "The only possible clue is that July 20 would have been Chris Cornell's 53rd birthday." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xid8CZf4oYo
**Early Life and Birthplace** : Chester Bennington was born on March 20, 1976, in Phoenix. His mother was a nurse, and his father was a police detective whose work primarily focused on child sex abuse cases. "Chester was born on March 20th, 1976 in Phoenix." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
**Family Life** : At the time of his death, Chester Bennington had a loving family which included six children. "He had a loving family with six, six kids." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
**High School Bullying and Abuse** : Chester's high school experience was difficult, marked by bullying and physical altercations. More profoundly, he was sexually abused by an older male friend from the age of seven until he was 13, an experience he kept secret due to shame and fear of not being believed. "High school was hell where he was bullied and beaten up. But that paled in comparison to the sexual abuse that was subjected upon him by an older male friend." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
**Marriage and Career Frustration** : By 1998, a young Chester Bennington was married, despite both he and his wife being barely 20. Feeling completely frustrated with his lack of progress in the music industry, he quit his band Grey Daze and took a job as an assistant with a digital services company. "But by then, he was completely frustrated with his lack of progress in the music biz." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
**Music as an Escape** : Music provided a crucial escape for Chester Bennington during his difficult teenage years, becoming a significant and positive outlet. "Fortunately, music became a bigger and better escape." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
**Parents' Divorce and Custody** : When Chester was 11 years old, his parents divorced, and his father was granted custody. This event significantly impacted him, leading him to delve deeply into drug use. "Mom and dad divorced when Chester was 11, dad got custody." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
**Personal Struggles and Fragile Nature** : Chester Bennington battled significant personal issues throughout his life, including childhood abuse, addiction to cocaine and methamphetamine, a drinking problem, persistent depression, multiple failed attempts at rehabilitation, and previous suicide attempts. He was described as having a "certain fragile nature," finding solace in his friendship with Chris Cornell, with whom he often shared his struggles. "He had all sorts of issues, abused as a child, addiction to coke and meth, a drinking problem, lingering depression, failed attempts at rehab, a couple of suicide attempts." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xid8CZf4oYo
**Restrictions and Early Job** : After moving in with his mother at 17 due to his drug use, Chester was essentially permanently grounded, with his only approved outings being for his shifts at Burger King. "he was basically permanently grounded, except for his shifts in Burger King." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
**Teenage Drug Abuse** : As a teenager, Chester Bennington experimented with a wide range of drugs, including pot, coke, various opioids, LSD, and meth. His father, a police officer, was unable to manage his drug abuse, leading Chester to move in with his mother at the age of 17. "As a teenager, he tried everything. Pot, Coke, various opioids, LSD, meth, basically the whole buffet." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
