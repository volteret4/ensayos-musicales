**American Girl (Year Unspecified)** : During the final tour of Tom Petty's life, "American Girl" was performed as the concluding song of his sets. As Petty and the band played, a video screen displayed a diverse array of images of women, starting with a white woman and expanding to include African Americans, a Latina woman in a US military uniform, older women, middle-aged women, and transgender women. This performance, showcasing "actual American women," was the last significant act Petty presented to his fans before his passing, emphasizing an idea of evolution and broader thinking. **Even the Losers (1980)** : This song appeared on the 1979 album "Damn the Torpedoes" and was subsequently released as a single in the summer of 1980. Its release marked a significant breakthrough for Tom Petty and the Heartbreakers. The track is also recognized as part of a larger collection of music that champions the optimism often found among the downtrodden, a key theme within the Heartland Rock genre. ## curiosities
**Breakthrough with Even the Losers** : The release of "Even the Losers" as a single in the summer of 1980 is considered the breakthrough moment for Tom Petty and the Heartbreakers, establishing their presence and impact in the music scene. "I think it's fair to say it was the breakthrough for Tom Petty and the Heartbreakers." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 55 - Heartland Rock is about More than the Heartland | https://www.ftr70.com/
**California Band Identity** : Despite Tom Petty's southern roots, he made it clear that he considered the Heartbreakers to be a "California band," aligning them with the tradition of other influential California groups like Buffalo Springfield, The Flying Burrito Brothers, and The Beach Boys. This perspective influenced their musical identity and where Petty chose to reside for most of his life. "he made it clear that he considered the heartbreakers to be a california band in the tradition of buffalo springfield the flying burrito brothers and even the beach boys and that's why." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 55 - Heartland Rock is about More than the Heartland | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Final Performance and Message with American Girl** : On the final tour of Tom Petty's life, the song "American Girl" served as the closing number, which also amounted to being the last song he ever performed publicly. During this performance, a video screen showcased a montage of diverse women, including white, African American, Latina in military uniform, older, middle-aged, and transgender women, symbolizing "actual American women." This poignant act, delivered just before his death, communicated a powerful message about evolution and broadening perspectives, making it potentially his most significant final public gesture to fans. "This is the last thing that Petty did for his fans before his death And maybe the most significant thing he did Is this idea of evolution and how we can think And maybe even broaden how we think." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 55 - Heartland Rock is about More than the Heartland | https://www.ftr70.com/

**Musical Style** : The band specialized in short, sharp, choppy songs that heavily featured rhythm guitar and conveyed a vaguely anti-corporate message. This style aligned them with the burgeoning New Wave movement. "Tom Petty specialized in short, sharp choppy songs with lots of rhythm guitar and vaguely anti-corporate stents." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iL9KpQFqH4o ← tom-petty-and-the-heartbreakers ← tom-petty-and-the-heartbreakers
**New Wave Branding** : Tom Petty and the Heartbreakers, a relatively new band from Florida, were categorized as New Wave. This classification was based on their shared musical characteristics with other New Wave acts like Talking Heads, Blondie, and Elvis Costello.
