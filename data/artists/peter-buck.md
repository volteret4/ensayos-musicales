## curiosities
**Acquittal and Legal Defense (2002)** : After his first trial collapsed, Peter Buck's second trial lasted three weeks, with Bono of U2 testifying as a character witness. He was acquitted on April 5, 2002, based on a doctor's testimony. The jury believed his actions were caused by "non-insane automatism," an unusual reaction between Ambien and alcohol, leading to non-voluntary behavior.
**Acquittal on "Non-Insane Automatism" (April 5, 2002)** : His first trial collapsed. At the second trial, which lasted three weeks and featured Bono of U2 as a character witness, Peter Buck was acquitted. The jury accepted a doctor's testimony that the combination of Ambien and red wine resulted in "non-insane automatism," a legal term for non-voluntary behavior caused by an unusual drug-alcohol reaction.
**Arrest and Charges (April 2001)** : Woke up confused in a police cell at Heathrow Airport, believing he was having a heart attack. He was charged with various counts of being drunk on a plane, two counts of common assault, and one count of damaging British Airways' property.
**Arrest at Heathrow (2001)** : Following the incident on British Airways Flight 48 on April 21, 2001, Peter Buck was arrested upon arrival at Heathrow Airport and held in a police cell.
**British Airways Air Rage Incident (2001)** : On April 21, 2001, prior to a scheduled R.E.M. concert for Nelson Mandela, Peter Buck took a prescription sleeping pill (Ambien) and consumed a large amount of red wine (reportedly 15 top-ups). His actions on British Airways Flight 48 included attempting to insert a CD into a food trolley, flipping it and spilling contents in first class, ripping up a formal warning card, grabbing a cutlery knife, smearing yogurt on himself and the cabin, and attacking flight crew members.
