**Acquittal and "Non-Insane Automatism" Defense (2002)** : Peter Buck's first trial collapsed, but at his second trial, which lasted three weeks and featured Bono of U2 as a character witness, he was acquitted on April 5, 2002. The jury believed doctor's testimony that the combination of Ambien and red wine resulted in "non-insane automatism," a legal term for non-voluntary behavior caused by an unusual drug-alcohol reaction, thus excusing his actions. "The combination of ambient and red wine resulted in something known as non-insane automatism." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hmnMG6f80Sg
**Arrest and Acquittal (April 21, 2001 - April 5, 2002)** : Peter Buck was arrested upon landing at Heathrow following his air rage incident. His first trial collapsed, but at the second, three-week trial, he was acquitted on April 5, 2002. The jury accepted his doctor's testimony of "non-insane automatism," a condition where he "went nuts" instead of entering R.E.M. sleep, with Bono of U2 testifying as a character witness. "Peter was acquitted mainly because the jury bought the doctor's testimony of non-insane automatism." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2RKchFW42Jc
**Arrest and Charges (2001)** : Following the in-flight incident, Peter Buck woke up in a police cell at Heathrow Airport, initially believing he was having a heart attack. He was subsequently charged with being drunk on a plane, two counts of common assault, and one count of damaging British Airways' property. "He thought he was having a heart attack and could understand why he was in this weird hospital Disneyland." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hmnMG6f80Sg
**British Airways Air Rage Incident (April 21, 2001)** : On April 21, 2001, R.E.M. guitarist Peter Buck boarded British Airways flight 48 to London, consuming a sleeping pill (M.B.N.) with up to 15 glasses of red wine. Instead of sleeping, he experienced "non-insane automatism," leading him to act wildly in the first-class cabin, ransacking it, flinging yogurt, and attacking crew members. "Peter pulled out a sleeping pill called M.B.N. and proceeded to wash it down with up to 15 glasses of red wine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2RKchFW42Jc
**British Airways Flight 48 Incident (2001)** : On April 21, 2001, prior to a scheduled concert for Nelson Mandela in London, Peter Buck boarded British Airways Flight 48. He combined a prescription sleeping pill, Ambien, with a large amount of red wine (reportedly 15 top-ups). This combination led to bizarre and disruptive behavior where he attempted to insert a CD into a food trolley, ripped up a formal warning card, grabbed a cutlery knife, smeared yogurt on himself and the cabin, and attacked flight crew members, almost causing the flight to be diverted for safety reasons. "Peter attempted to insert a CD into a food trolley, thinking it was actually a very big CD player." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hmnMG6f80Sg
**Return Flight with British Airways (2002)** : Despite the dramatic incident and subsequent legal proceedings, Peter Buck flew home to Seattle on British Airways after his acquittal.
