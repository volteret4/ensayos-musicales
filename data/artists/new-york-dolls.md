**Arbitrary Breakup Date (1977)** : While their last shows were in 1976 and they were definitively done by October 1977, an arbitrary breakup date of October 1, 1977, is assigned due to the difficulty in pinpointing the exact end. **Arthur Kane's Difficult Post-Breakup Life** : Bassist Arthur Kane faced severe challenges after the breakup, moving to Los Angeles where he struggled with alcoholism, experienced a near-fatal fall from a window, and was beaten senseless with a baseball bat by a homeless man, leaving him in a coma; he later became a Mormon in 1989 and worked as a librarian in the church's genealogy center. "Bassist Arthur Kane had a rough time. He moved to Los Angeles where he drank himself into an alcoholic stupor. He was so drunk one time that he fell out of a window, and nearly died." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0hnBC0y59QA
**Arthur Kane's Sudden Death Post-Reunion (2004)** : Just 22 days after returning to Los Angeles from the reunion gig in England, Arthur Kane felt ill, was diagnosed with leukemia in an emergency room, and died within two hours. "But just 22 days after returning to LA from England, Arthur felt like he had the flu. He went to an emergency room of an LA hospital where he was diagnosed with leukemia. And within two hours, two hours, he was dead, just like that." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0hnBC0y59QA
**Carrying On as a Duo** : Following Arthur Kane's death, the remaining two Dolls, David Johansen and Sylvain Sylvain, decided to continue as a duo, recording a "pretty good record" titled "One Day, it will please us to remember even this," which included guest appearances by Iggy Pop and Michael Stipe.
