**Acute Anxiety Diagnosis** : Meg White suffered from acute anxiety, which was officially cited as the reason for The White Stripes' unexpected cancellation of their planned tour dates in the United States and the UK in 2007. This condition had a significant impact on the band's ability to tour and ultimately contributed to their separation. **Brother and Sister Myth** : A persistent myth claimed that Jack and Meg White were brother and sister, which is entirely false. Jack Gillis (his birth name) was the tenth of ten children from a Catholic family in Detroit, with no sister named Meg, while Meg White was from Gross Point Farms with one sister, Heather, and no brothers. This false narrative was deliberately cultivated by Jack White in early interviews, where he referred to Meg as his "little sister," even after their marriage certificate was posted online in 2001. "When you see a band that's two pieces, husband and wife, boyfriend and girlfriend, you think, oh, I see. When their brother and sister, you go, oh, that's interesting. **Reluctance to Fame** : Meg White possessed a timid personality and was generally reluctant towards media attention, which became increasingly challenging as The White Stripes achieved massive fame. She did not enjoy touring or interviews, and with the band's growing popularity, she began to withdraw more significantly from the public eye.
