## curiosities
**Digital A/B Side Releases** : MGMT is among the artists noted for releasing two digital tracks concurrently, implicitly designating one as more significant than the other, thereby carrying on the metaphorical concept of A-sides and B-sides into the digital realm. This practice reflects a lingering momentum from traditional single formats despite the absence of physical sides. "For example, acts like the Smash and Pumpkins and MGMT and others have been on to release two digital tracks at the same time, with some kind of subtle hint that one of them is slightly more important than the other." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QBCEmZ4Jheo
**Eclectic Influences and Modern Sound** : MGMT's music incorporated influences from artists like David Bowie, The Beatles, The Beach Boys, and Pink Floyd, yet it managed to avoid sounding retro due to its alignment with contemporary indie rock trends. This blend resulted in a sound that was both new and comforting. "There were bits of bowie and the beetles in what they did. You could also hear elements of the beach boys and pink Floyd." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_yslM5oanRo
**Formation and Sound** : Formed in 2002 in Middletown, Connecticut, by Andrew VanWingarten and Ben Goldwasser, MGMT cultivated an indie pop sound infused with psychedelic elements. Their music was perceived as experimental, echoing the attitude of some alt-rock bands from two decades prior. "They were formed in 2002 in Middletown, Connecticut by Andrew VanWingarten and Ben Goldwasser." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_yslM5oanRo
**Insistence on Vinyl Releases** : MGMT was noted as one of the bands, alongside the Foo Fighters, Gaslight Anthem, Radiohead, Nine-inch Nails, and U2, that started requiring their new music to be released on vinyl. "Bands from the Foo Fighters to MGMT, to Gaslight Anthem, to Radiohead, to Nine-inch Nails, to YouTube began insisting that their new releases be made available on vinyl." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GES0Ms_kaCI
**Synth-Heavy Band Example** : Listed as one of the new synth-heavy bands that emerged, indicating a contemporary interest in synthesizer-driven music. "There's MGMT, there's Owl City, there's Empire of the Sun, Caribou, Phoenix, Beck has been turned onto these sounds, and Moby, check it out." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KF5MwJVYng
