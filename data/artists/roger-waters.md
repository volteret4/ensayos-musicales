**Inspiration for "Another Brick in the Wall"** : Roger Waters' lyrics for "Another Brick in the Wall" were inspired by his experiences in 1950s grammar schools in Britain. He described these institutions as being "more about controlling boys than educating them," a critical reflection that resonated widely with students and sparked controversy. "Waters was writing about a different era though. **Leader with Business Vision and Label Disputes** : Roger Waters was the leader of Pink Floyd and was known not only as a great musician but also for his enormous business vision. He had very clear ideas about how the group's discography should be commercially developed, which led him to get involved in many matters that the record label considered its own, such as album launches and distribution, generating significant controversy. This entrepreneurial approach contributed to his long-standing poor relationship with the record company, especially evidenced by the challenges surrounding *The Dark Side of the Moon*. "Roger Waters, aparte de un gran músico, también tiene una enorme visión empresarial." ← Music Radar Clan > Pink Floyd - The Dark Side of The Moon (historia) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FkXnDYvqkAQ&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**Stadium Frustration and "The Wall" Concept** : Roger Waters began conceiving *The Wall* in 1977, driven by his profound frustration with performing in large stadiums. He found these shows "an alienating experience" because, while some attendees were loyal fans, "most of whom were only there for the beer." This sentiment led him to propose the concept of "The Wall" to the rest of Pink Floyd in 1978. "The wall was born out of the frustration that Roger Waters felt about performing in large stadiums.
