## curiosities
**Battle with TVT Records** : A multi-year battle ensued between TVT Records and Nine Inch Nails due to the label's dissatisfaction with the band's harder sound. This conflict was eventually resolved when a major label stepped in to purchase the rights to Trent Reznor's work. "And so it began a multi-year battle between TVT and Nine-Inch Stales that was only solved when a major label stepped in to buy the rights to Trenton's work." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Direct Fan Engagement** : Nine Inch Nails, along with Radiohead, pioneered "grand experiments" in music distribution by leveraging their large fan bases directly. They bypassed traditional record deals and labels, selling products directly to fans and managing their audience without intermediaries. "And then we have the grand experiments by bands such as 9-inch nails and radio head." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-CT7yYXonIk
**Early Label Misunderstanding** : In 1987, Steve Gottlieb of TVT Records signed Trent Reznor from Cleveland, expecting a Depeche Mode-style band based on his demos. Gottlieb was annoyed when the music turned out to be substantially harder. "TVThead Steve Gottlieb signed this kid from Cleveland named Trent Rezner. Based on the demos he had heard, he thought he was getting a Depeche Mode style band. And he got was actually substantially harder. And he was extremely annoyed." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Reinvention and Success (Pre-2005)** : The band was successfully reinvented by Trent Reznor by spring 2005, achieving renewed success and hit albums during rock's resurgence. "Trent Resner had successfully reinvented nine-inch nails." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Return of Bigger Older Acts Post 9/11** : Nine Inch Nails was among the established acts whose popularity saw a return after 9/11 and the subsequent wars, as the public mood shifted away from happy music and rock began to experience a resurgence. "And a return of some bigger older acts, U2, 9-H nails, Green Day, Weezer." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Y6nHw5xx88
**Rider Request – Linen Napkins and "Bowels"** : Trent Reznor once had a rider that asked for linen napkins, china plates, and "bowels" (an apparent typo for bowls), indicating a desire for specific upscale dining items backstage. "one of his riders wants to ask for linen napkins, china plates, and bowels." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z2hvUDtBxho
**TVT's Role in Success** : Despite the label's initial annoyance, Nine Inch Nails' songs would not have been released without TVT Records, highlighting the indie label's crucial role in the band's early career.
