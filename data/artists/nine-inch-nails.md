## curiosities
**Extensive 12-inch Remixes (90s-Early 2000s)** : Nine-inch Nails frequently had their singles remixed in multiple ways for nightclubs during the 90s and early 2000s, catering to the alt-rock dance club scene. This extensive remix culture diminished significantly by the 2010s. **Insistence on Vinyl Releases** : Nine-inch Nails was cited among the bands, including the Foo Fighters, MGMT, Gaslight Anthem, Radiohead, and U2, that insisted on vinyl availability for their new releases. "Bands from the Foo Fighters to MGMT, to Gaslight Anthem, to Radiohead, to Nine-inch Nails, to YouTube began insisting that their new releases be made available on vinyl." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The History of Vinyl： Part 2 | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GES0Ms_kaCI&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Lollapalooza 1991 Lineup** : Nine-inch Nails were part of the lineup for the first Lollapalooza festival in the summer of 1991, at a time when they were considered a "weirdo group" with little mainstream potential. ← Ongoing History of New Music > Theories, Thoughts, and Half-Baked Ideas | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Nothing Records – Vanity Boutique Label** : Trent Reznor's Nothing Records was created by Interscope, a subsidiary of Universal, as a special vanity boutique label. This arrangement gave Trent Reznor extra creative control, allowing him to pursue, sign, develop, and produce talent independently. It exemplifies how major labels can establish indie-looking imprints for significant artists.
