## curiosities
**Insistence on Vinyl Releases** : Nine-inch Nails was cited among the bands, including the Foo Fighters, MGMT, Gaslight Anthem, Radiohead, and U2, that insisted on vinyl availability for their new releases. "Bands from the Foo Fighters to MGMT, to Gaslight Anthem, to Radiohead, to Nine-inch Nails, to YouTube began insisting that their new releases be made available on vinyl." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GES0Ms_kaCI
**Lollapalooza 1991 Lineup** : Nine-inch Nails were part of the lineup for the first Lollapalooza festival in the summer of 1991, at a time when they were considered a "weirdo group" with little mainstream potential. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Nothing Records – Vanity Boutique Label** : Trent Reznor's Nothing Records was created by Interscope, a subsidiary of Universal, as a special vanity boutique label. This arrangement gave Trent Reznor extra creative control, allowing him to pursue, sign, develop, and produce talent independently. It exemplifies how major labels can establish indie-looking imprints for significant artists. "This gave Trent some extra creative control and allowed him to pursue, sign, develop and produce talent on his own." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ft8nruhWCas
