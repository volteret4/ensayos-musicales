**Seven Nation Army** : The riff for this song, featuring many different colors of distortion, was created by running an early 1960s K Hollow Body guitar, equipped with a single pickup, into a Digitech Whammy pedal, which shifted the notes Jack White played down an entire octave. Jack White later played the same seven notes on the same guitar with a slide, without pitch shifting, but run through a Big Muff Pi effects pedal with the sustain turned almost all the way up. The signal then went to a Sears Silver Tone amplifier from the mid-1960s, set with a little extra bass and treble, resulting in awesome distorted tones. "The riff was created by running a K Hollow Body guitar from the early 1960s, equipped with a single pickup into a Digitech whammy pedal, which shifted the notes Jack White played down an entire octave." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w

**Magic Stick Assault (December 13, 2003) – Jack White Beat Von Bondies Singer Jason Stollsteimer** : On a Saturday night at the Magic Stick club in Detroit during the CD release party for a local band called Blanche, Jack White approached Von Bondies singer Jason Stollsteimer, spat in his face, and began punching him. When Stollsteimer fell to the floor, White continued hitting him until bystanders pulled him away. White filed a counter-complaint claiming self-defense. He initially pleaded not guilty, then changed his plea to guilty of misdemeanor assault and battery on March 9, 2004. Sentence: $500 fine, $200 court costs, one year of anger management. White maintained that Stollsteimer had grabbed him and pulled out his hair, and that his retaliation lasted less than 10 seconds. He also alleged that Stollsteimer refused to wash off the blood before posing for press photos sent to NME the next morning, and that the permanently damaged eye he claimed was a pre-existing injury from a contact lens incident as a teenager. White and Stollsteimer had not spoken for two years prior to the incident. **Relationship of Jack and Meg White** : Contrary to a widely circulated myth, Jack (John Anthony Gillis) and Meg White are not brother and sister; they are ex-husband and wife. They met in Detroit in 1994, where Meghan Martha White was a bartender at Memphis Smoke, and John Anthony Gillis was an ex-furniture upholsterer. They married on September 12, 1996, with Jack taking Meg's last name in a reversal of tradition. They started their band, released albums, and then divorced on March 24, 2000, but remained friends and bandmates. The "brother and sister" claim began as a joke when the band started, and despite the truth being revealed when they gained fame, some people still refused to believe it. "For once and for all, they are not brother and sister. They are actually ex-husband and wife. John Anthony Gillis met Meghan Martha White in Detroit in 1994. She was a bartender at a place called Memphis Smoke, and he was an ex-furniture upholsterer." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zUQ2vZzmRiI
