## curiosities
**Hamilton Punk Provocateurs – "Surfin' on Heroin," Nazi Allegations, Cassette "Burn the Flag" (1978)** : The Forgotten Rebels formed in Hamilton in 1977, about 18 months after Teenage Head. Led by Mickey DeSadist, they were sarcastic, sometimes profane, and featured songs like "Surfin' on Heroin," "Bomb the Boats and Feed the Fish" (about Vietnamese refugees), "No Beatles Reunion," and a song called "Reich 'n' Roll" that drew allegations of anti-Semitism and homophobia; the band maintained it was all satire. Their first release was a six-song cassette called *Burn the Flag* in 1978, followed by *Tomorrow Belongs to Us*. They occasionally attracted crowds of several thousand.
