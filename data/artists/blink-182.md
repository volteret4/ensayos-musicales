## albums
**California (2016)** : This album was recorded with Matt Skiba as guitarist, replacing Tom Delonge. The band developed 28 songs quickly and completed the recording session within three months, releasing the album on July 1, 2016. The rapid and "old-school Blink" approach proved successful, with the album reaching the top 10 in 13 countries. "They came up with 28 songs in very short order, burned through the recording session in three months and released it on July 1, 2016." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Enema of the State (1999)** : The cover of Blink-182's 1999 album, "Enema of the State," features porn star Jeanine Lindemulder provocatively dressed as a "sexy nurse" putting on a rubber glove, while the back cover shows the band naked, preparing for what appears to be a medical "probing." The original album title was "Turn Your Head and Cough," but photographer Dave Goldman's idea for the glove inspired the band to change the title to its final version. "That's a pretty sexy nurse in the cover of Blink 182's 1999 album, Enema of the State." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tbKuJR0cPs
**Neighborhoods (2011)** : This album, released on September 27, 2011, marked the band's first record together in nine years and nearly three years after Travis Barker's plane crash. Its creation was a long and emotionally challenging process, exacerbated by the members' busy individual schedules, Tom DeLonge's continued work with Angels & Airwaves, Mark Hoppus's TV show commitments, Travis Barker's grief over DJ AM's death, and the absence of their longtime producer Jerry Finn. The album was one of the most anticipated releases of 2011 but sold approximately 500,000 units worldwide, which was considered less than stellar. "They called it neighborhoods, and this was the first single." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE

## curiosities
**"Enema of the State" Album Cover Model** : The "sexy nurse" on the cover of Blink-182's 1999 album "Enema of the State" is Jeanine Lindemulder, a porn star, though the band was unaware of her profession when they selected her photo from options provided by their record label. Photographer Dave Goldman, who conducted the shoot on March 12, 1999, was also oblivious to both Lindemulder's background and the band's identity. "Well, it's porn star, Jeanine Lindemolder." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tbKuJR0cPs
**Backstage Rider – Entertainment and Reading Material** : Blink-182's rider specified a need for various entertainment items, including DVDs to watch, a selection of current magazines covering fashion, home decor, cars, and science. Additionally, it explicitly requested at least one "X-rated lesbian theme magazine." "at least one had to be, and I quote, an X-rated lesbian theme magazine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z2hvUDtBxho
**Band Bureaucracy Challenges** : During the recording of their "Neighborhoods" album, the band faced significant bureaucratic challenges. While they were apart, each member had acquired their own manager, lawyers, accountants, and publicists. At one point, the three-piece band collectively had four managers, making decision-making and project coordination incredibly difficult. "At one point during the recording of neighborhoods, this three-piece band had four managers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Departure from Interscope Records (2012)** : In October 2012, Blink-182 split from their major label, Interscope Records. This move allowed them to pursue independent projects, such as their five-song EP "Dogs Eating Dogs," giving them complete creative control without external interference. "They left Interscope Records in October 2012." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Dislike of Reality TV Presence** : Tom Delonge expressed his disapproval of Travis Barker's involvement in reality television, specifically the MTV show "Meet the Barkers," which extensively documented Travis and his wife Shannon Mochler. The presence of TV cameras, even in the Blink-182 rehearsal space, was a source of discomfort for Tom. "Tom also wasn't a fan of Travis getting into the reality TV show game." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Hiatus and Internal Conflicts (2005)** : In February 2005, Blink-182 announced an official hiatus, which was effectively a breakup due to widespread fatigue and interpersonal friction among the members after nearly a decade of intense work. The band had transitioned from being teenagers to adults, leading to personal changes and a desire to pursue individual paths. The record label, despite the internal issues, strictly instructed the band not to publicly state that it was the end of the band. "Part two of the Blink 182 story ended with the announcement of the hiatus in February 2005." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Hospital Reunion (2008)** : Following Travis Barker's severe injuries from the plane crash, Mark Hoppus was among the first to visit him in the hospital, and Tom Delonge also made the trip. The three members of Blink-182 met in a "sobering reunion" at Travis's bedside, prompting a realization for all three about what truly mattered in their lives and relationships. "The three guys met up in the hospital in a pretty sobering reunion." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Influence on Pop Punk** : Blink-182 is considered one of the most influential pop punk bands of the 21st century. Their style and career trajectory have left a significant mark on numerous artists across the genre, inspiring bands like Sum 41, Good Charlotte, Simple Plan, Panic! At The Disco, Fall Out Boy, Hedley, Billy Talent, You Me At Six, and Paramore. "Blink has to be considered one of the most influential pop punk bands of the 21st century." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Jerry Finn's Push for Other Projects** : Longtime producer Jerry Finn, along with Mark Hoppus and Travis Barker, made it clear they wanted to work on another project during Blink-182's hiatus. This desire led to the formation of Plus 44 by Mark and Travis, further solidifying the band's fragmentation. "It also didn't help the situation that the other two thirds of the band and their long time producer Jerry Finn made it very clear that they wanted to work together on another project." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Max Schieber Joins (2015)** : In March 2015, following Tom Delonge's second departure, Blink-182 played two club shows with Max Schieber (likely Matt Skiba) of Alkaline Trio filling in as guitarist. Lawyers worked to formalize Tom's exit and Max's integration into the band. "In March 2015, Blink played two club shows without Tom. Max Schieber, the guitarist for a band called Alkaline Trio filled in." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Multiple Versions of "Enema of the State" Cover** : Blink-182's "Enema of the State" album had four different cover versions due to various issues and complaints. The initial version featured a Red Cross on Jeanine Lindemulder's hat and a capital "B" for the band's logo, which was corrected to a lowercase "b" in the second version. Following a complaint from the American Red Cross regarding logo infringement, a third version was released with a plain white nurse's hat. A fourth, censored version, exclusive to Malaysia, removed Lindemulder's cleavage and edited the semi-nude band shot on the back, but oddly brought back the Red Cross symbol. "Anyway, there were actually four different versions of the cover." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tbKuJR0cPs
**Origin of "Enema of the State" Title** : Blink-182's 1999 album was initially intended to be titled "Turn Your Head and Cough." However, photographer Dave Goldman's spontaneous suggestion to include a rubber glove in the cover art, due to its humorous and suggestive implications, inspired the band to change the album's title to "Enema of the State." "The album was originally going to be called Turn Your Head and Cough, but Goldman suddenly came up with the idea of a glove because he thought that the visual and its implications was pretty funny." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tbKuJR0cPs
**Popularity and Green Day Support (Late 1990s)** : The band was "really hot at the time" and provided an opening tour slot for Green Day, which helped to save Green Day from breaking up. "Blink was really hot at the time..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Reunion Announcement (2009)** : The band officially announced their reunion at the 2009 Grammy Awards. This was followed by a reunion tour during the summer, signaling their return to active music creation and performance. "Their first appearance together was at the 2009 Grammy Awards, where they announced that they were getting back together." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Surviving Multiple Lifespans** : Despite the general observation that bands have an average lifespan of seven years, Blink-182 has defied this trend, navigating through several such cycles of career highs, lows, and lineup changes. "Blink 182 has gone through several of those lifetimes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Tom Delonge's Desire for Break** : Tom Delonge's decision to take a break stemmed from feeling the intense grind of the band's schedule and needing at least six months to decompress. He was disappointed that Mark Hoppus and Travis Barker didn't fully understand his need for this extended time away. "The story seems to be that Tom just wanted a break from the grind and he was disappointed that the other two guys didn't understand why he needed at least six months to decompress." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Tom Delonge's Second Departure (2014-2015)** : Tom Delonge quit the band again around 2014, although he briefly returned proposing group therapy led by motivational speaker Tony Robbins, which did not resolve underlying issues. Communication ceased once more, leading to indefinite delays for a planned 2013 album. His manager eventually sent an email, one week before a scheduled studio session, stating Tom's indefinite unwillingness to participate in Blink-182 activities. The band issued a statement announcing his departure, to which Tom countered that the band demanded he put all other projects on hold for an album and tour within six months. He refused these terms and officially left. "Tom's manager sent an email... saying that Tom did not want to participate in any Blink activities indefinitely." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Travis Barker's Plane Crash (2008)** : On September 19, 2008, less than a month after Jerry Finn's death, Travis Barker and DJ AM were involved in a Learjet 60 plane crash in Columbia, South Carolina, after a show. The chartered plane, which Travis was persuaded to take by DJ AM, suffered underinflated tires that burst during takeoff, causing the landing gear to collapse and the plane to crash through lights, a fence, across a highway, and into an embankment. Travis and DJ AM were the only two survivors from the six people on board. "Then came the plane crash. This was September 19th, 2008." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
