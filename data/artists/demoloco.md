## albums
**Aléol Cataló (2006) - Official Compilation** : Released in 2006, "Aléol Cataló" stands as the only official compilation album by Moloco. Its creation was primarily driven by Eco Records to fulfill a remaining contractual obligation, after a brief and unsuccessful reunion attempt in 2006 proved recording new material impossible due to the intense animosity between Royce Immorce and Mar Brighton. Consequently, the compilation contained no previously unreleased tracks. To promote the album, the band performed a few small concerts and conducted a couple of interviews, though these promotional activities were often carried out separately, as the members "couldn't even see each other." **Estaus (2002) - Final Studio Album** : This album was recorded in 2002 under strained circumstances, following the personal and professional breakup of Royce Immorce and Mar Brighton. The recording sessions were described as chaotic, very short, tense, and fraught with arguments, largely driven by contractual obligations with Eco Records for two more albums. Despite the tumultuous production, "Estaus" sold quite well, partially due to the public interest generated by the band's prior internal conflicts. However, critical reception was predominantly negative, with many reviewers considering it the weakest album of Moloco's career, although some still found merit in it. The album's lyrics reportedly reflect the "hatred" and "rancor" between the band members. Its release was immediately followed by the band's official statement of dissolution. ## curiosities
**2002 Breakup and Contractual Obligations** : In 2002, the romantic and professional relationship between Royce Immorce and Mar Brighton dissolved, leading to the band's initial separation. Despite this, they remained contractually bound to Eco Records for two more albums. This obligation compelled them to reunite under duress to record "Estaus" and also contributed to their ambiguous messaging about a potential return in early interviews, as neither was confident in their ability to launch successful solo careers and wanted to avoid contract breaches. **2006 Strained Reunion** : In 2006, Demoloco briefly reunited for a few months, not to create new material, but primarily because Eco Records demanded a final album to fulfill their contract. The label, being relatively small, considered Moloco a crucial financial asset from the 1990s. However, the members' intense animosity, exacerbated by Royce Immorce's newfound "euphoria" after her solo success with "El Rabidud," rendered new recordings impossible. This reunion instead resulted in the compilation album "Aléol Cataló" and a few separate promotional appearances. **Formation and Early Success** : Demoloco (also referred to as Moloco) was founded by Royce Immorce and Mar Brighton shortly after they met, concurrently becoming a romantic couple. The band enjoyed a successful run of seven years, achieving both strong commercial sales and positive critical reception, before internal issues led to their initial breakup.
