## instruments
- **Hyle Talk Box** : For the song "Rocky Mountain Way," Walsh initially used a small, quiet talk box by Pete Drake in the studio, which distorted the sound to create a unique, almost robotic effect. Bob Hyle, whom Walsh met through ham radios, then created a high-powered talk box specifically for Walsh to replicate this sound live. This custom talk box, with a tube attached to the mic stand, became an "indelible sound of 70s rock" and was later notably used by Peter Frampton. "This is Joe Walsh on the Hyle talk box in Rocky Mountain Way."
- Guitar

## songs
**Life of Illusion (Cover)** : A song by Joe Walsh that the Foo Fighters covered, with Taylor Hawkins performing lead vocals. This cover is another example of Hawkins showcasing his vocal talents within the band. **Rocky Mountain Way (1973)** : This song, featuring the distinct sound of the Hyle talk box (not a synthesizer), was written by Walsh with the music coming before the lyrics. The lyrics were inspired as he looked up at the Rocky Mountains while mowing his lawn in Colorado, reflecting on accepting his departure from the James Gang and appreciating his life there. A bootleg copy of his live performance at the Ozark Festival indicates it "sounded great live."
