## curiosities
**B-Side Hit** : Otis Redding's first release on Stax was initially unimpressive to Jim Stewart, who considered the A-side "a fun but unoriginal little Richard sound alike." However, DJs soon flipped the record, and the B-side became a hit, launching Redding's legendary career. "But soon DJs flipped the record, and it was the B side that became the hit. The Otis Redding would never again be thought of as just Johnny Jenkins's driver" ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Influence on Early Tragically Hip** : The Tragically Hip's early setlists largely consisted of blues rock standards, including material from Otis Redding. "Early stuff from the stones, material from the doors, the yard birds, Marvin Gaye, Otis Redding, Ben Morrison, that kind of thing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dnz_Ycd_q7s ← otis-redding
**Plane Crash Death (1967)** : Otis Redding died on December 10, 1967, when his small plane crashed into Lake Monoma near Madison, Wisconsin. "Otis Redding died on December 10th, 1967, when his small plane crashed into Lake Monoma near Madison, Wisconsin." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3eGBnwPWRGo ← otis-redding
**The "Driver" Who Became a Star** : Otis Redding initially arrived at Stax as Johnny Jenkins's driver, not as a scheduled artist. Due to a favor owed by Jim Stewart to promoter Joe Galkin, Redding was given an impromptu recording opportunity. "Nobody was interested, but Galkin wore Jim Stewart down, and he agreed to listen to this person who he just thought of as Johnny Jenkins's driver." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
