## curiosities
**Early Childhood & Hometown** : Born in Dundas, Ontario, a small town west of Hamilton, Botrell grew up in a suburban environment. Dundas was described as a tougher town during his youth, but later became a "pretty tweed town" with retirees and young families. "I was born in Dundas, Ontario, which is kind of a small town tacked onto the very west end of Hamilton." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vWvYe1a-2Aw ← david-botrell ← david-botrell
**Entry into Recording at Grant Avenue Studios** : Botrell's career began when his girlfriend's uncle, Bob Lannwaw, owner of Grant Avenue Studios, offered him a chance to hang out after he expressed interest in a job. Botrell learned about recording at night, assisting on sessions, cleaning, and making tea for nine months before securing an assistant position in 1983 or 1984. "I walked in the door of that studio and I looked to the left and there was that MCI console and all those lights. And music was going on in the other room and I was like, man, I want something to do with this." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vWvYe1a-2Aw ← david-botrell ← david-botrell
**Formative Influence of Ambient Music** : His early experiences as an assistant in 1983-84 at Grant Avenue Studios were heavily influenced by ambient sessions, which provided an unusual, creative environment without traditional pop structure rules. This experience shaped his initial approach to making music, emphasizing feeling over rigid song forms. "my beginnings in recording and making music stemmed from those early ambient sessions." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vWvYe1a-2Aw ← david-botrell ← david-botrell
**Genre-Agnostic Production Philosophy** : Botrell asserts that genre is "not that important" to him; he values "integrity in the writing and the conviction of the message" above musical style. His diverse catalog includes heavy music, world music, softer bands, Scottish country, and atmospheric singers like LCL, challenging his North American reputation as "the Tool guy." "I don't really care what the style of the music is. I look for the integrity in the writing and the conviction of the message that they're writing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vWvYe1a-2Aw ← david-botrell ← david-botrell
**Hands-on vs. Delegating Engineering** : While proficient with Pro Tools and mic placement, Botrell prefers to delegate engineering tasks to skilled engineers like Tom Tapley, allowing him to focus on coaching and optimizing musical performances. He finds this approach, also favored by Steve Lilywhite, more efficient for band tracks, though he may handle vocals personally. "I'm often to the point of like you operate that and I'm going to work with the musician and I'm going to coach him." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vWvYe1a-2Aw ← david-botrell ← david-botrell
**Indispensable Role in Peter Gabriel's Organization** : Botrell made himself indispensable by handling various tasks from setting up keyboards, organizing tape storage, to preparing multi-track tapes for musicians during rehearsals for Peter Gabriel's tours. This led to him becoming a crucial part of the touring crew, requiring expedited visas to join the US leg. "I essentially did my best to make myself indispensable and if you do that then people like to have you around." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vWvYe1a-2Aw ← david-botrell ← david-botrell
**Involvement in Songwriting and Arrangement** : Botrell actively contributes to songwriting and arrangement, offering suggestions for chords and transitions to enhance the emotional flow of songs, especially in moving between distinct verse, bridge, and chorus blocks. He focuses on creating "transition moments" for greater impact. "I work really well in transitions." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vWvYe1a-2Aw ← david-botrell ← david-botrell
**Move to England with Peter Gabriel** : David Botrell followed Bob Lannwaw to England, where Lannwaw was working with Peter Gabriel, initially assisting in setup and technical roles before becoming an indispensable part of Peter Gabriel's touring and studio operations. He stayed in the UK for 20 years, making his way from Bath to London. "when Bob went over to work at Peter Gabriel, he said I might need an assistant over there. Do you want to come over? I said yes, of course." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vWvYe1a-2Aw ← david-botrell ← david-botrell
**Move to New York City** : Around 1997, while living in Bethnal Green, London, Botrell moved to New York City to work on projects with Universal Republic. He lived in the West Village and worked at Soundtrack Studios near New Square, fulfilling a long-held fascination with the city. "I was always fascinated with New York City. And I'd always wanted to live there." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vWvYe1a-2Aw ← david-botrell ← david-botrell
**Musical Skill Assessment** : David Botrell openly states that while he plays instruments, he does not play them "very well." He considers his primary talent to be helping "great musicians play better or write better," rather than his own instrumental prowess. "My talent is helping great musicians play better or write better." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vWvYe1a-2Aw ← david-botrell ← david-botrell
**Pre-Production Importance** : He emphasizes the importance of pre-production, where much collaborative writing and idea-testing takes place in less expensive environments like rehearsal rooms or home studios, before moving to a proper recording studio for final tracking. "I like to get involved in pre-production first." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vWvYe1a-2Aw ← david-botrell ← david-botrell
**Producer's Perspective on Listening to Past Work** : Like many producers, Botrell rarely listens to his past work, citing the tendency to focus on perceived flaws or changes he would make, similar to Steve Lilywhite's reluctance to revisit his U2 albums. "Once I'm done with most of them, it's not that often." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vWvYe1a-2Aw ← david-botrell ← david-botrell
**Production Philosophy - Collaborative Approach** : Botrell defines a record producer's responsibility as getting the "best out of the artist and musician and the songs," preferring a collaborative approach over autocratic styles like Todd Rundgren or Bob Ezrin. He encourages artists to try all ideas without argument, quickly discerning what works. "I'm a collaborative producer. So I like to work with the artist." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vWvYe1a-2Aw ← david-botrell ← david-botrell
**Return to Toronto** : After living in New York City for about a year and a half, Botrell met his future ex-wife, a Toronto native, which led to him commuting between the two cities before making Toronto his permanent home in 2006. "I met my now ex-wife who is from Toronto. And so I sort of commuted back and forth from New York to Toronto and then ended up just moving back to Toronto." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vWvYe1a-2Aw ← david-botrell ← david-botrell

**UK Studio Work (1986-1997)** : For the first 11 years in the UK, David Botrell worked primarily at Real World Studios, helping to build the facility in a mill building in Box (Barks). After moving to London, he also worked at Eden, Ten House, and Trident Studios. "I did most of my work at, for the first 11 years at Real World Studios." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vWvYe1a-2Aw ← david-botrell ← david-botrell
**Unfulfilled Dream of Being a Record Producer** : In high school, David Botrell aspired to be a record producer, driven by his love for music. However, he faced rejection from schools due to his lack of a portfolio at 18 and insufficient musical adeptness beyond being a "pretty good drummer" and having "seven years of accordion lessons." "I had the notion that I was going to be a record producer." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vWvYe1a-2Aw ← david-botrell ← david-botrell
