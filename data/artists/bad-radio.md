## songs
**Homework (1989 Demo)** : This song by Bad Radio features Eddie Vedder on vocals and dates from sometime in 1989, approximately a year before he moved to Seattle to join Pearl Jam. "Let's give a listen to what Bad Radio sounded like. This is called Homework. That's Eddie Better." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r4QJMx1LES8
**I'm Alive (Unreleased)** : This song was part of a collection of demos that Bad Radio had recorded and was ready to release as an album. The album, however, never saw the light of day because lead singer Eddie Vedder left the band in February 1990 to join an unnamed band in Seattle, which turned out to be Pearl Jam. "Eddie Vedder, in the days before Pearl Jam, that's Bad Radio, his San Diego band, and I'm Alive. I had demos recorded, and we're ready to release an album, but it was never to be because Eddie moved up the coast to a gig that turned out to be a little bit more lucrative." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gdWLtMy4TBU

## curiosities
**Disintegration After Eddie Vedder's Departure** : Bad Radio, a funk-rock band fronted by Eddie Vedder from 1986 to 1990, continued for a short period after Vedder left in February 1990 to join what would become Pearl Jam. The band eventually disintegrated following his departure, with their planned album remaining unreleased. "Bad Radio continued for a while, but eventually disintegrated." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gdWLtMy4TBU
**Duran Duran Inspiration** : Before Eddie Vedder joined, Bad Radio's primary musical inspiration was Duran Duran, a sound that the band found wasn't working for them. "Before Eddie joined, the band's main inspiration was Durand Durand." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r4QJMx1LES8
**Four-Song Cassette (1989)** : Bad Radio released a four-song cassette in 1989, which hinted at more music to come. This release occurred during Eddie Vedder's tenure as their singer, shortly before he departed to join Pearl Jam. "There was a four-song cassette released in 1989. That hinted of more to come." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gdWLtMy4TBU
**Pearl Jam Fan Community Bootlegs** : Despite their unreleased album, the Pearl Jam fan community has actively kept Bad Radio's music alive by circulating bootlegs of their material. This effort by fans ensures that the music from Eddie Vedder's pre-Pearl Jam band remains accessible. "Meanwhile, the Pearl Jam fan community has kept Bad Radio alive by circulating boot legs of Bad Radio stuff." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gdWLtMy4TBU
**Red Hot Chili Peppers Sound Shift** : After Eddie Vedder became their singer, Bad Radio's sound evolved to become increasingly similar to that of the Red Hot Chili Peppers. "After Eddie came on board, Bad Radio began to sound more and more like the Red Hot Chili Peppers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r4QJMx1LES8
