## curiosities
**Addiction Parallels** : Hugh Dillon drew a comparison between his own extreme pain and the struggles of Elvis or Jim Morrison, indicating that his addiction had driven him to a point where his next step was "not living." "It's like Elvis or Jim Morrison. It was, you know, my next step was not living." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zBd13KtYlAA
**Influence on The Black Keys** : Elvis was one of the favorite artists that inspired the short hooks used in the composition of songs for The Black Keys' *El Camino* album, indicating a classic rock and roll influence. "and Elvis". ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PmwfC4HR3_I
**Physical Appearance During Addiction** : Hugh Dillon reflected on feeling and looking "bloated" and "250 pounds" during his addiction, a state that he implicitly linked to the physical decline often associated with Elvis. "I'm on, I'm trying to go to rehabs. I meet nice came out with 250 pounds." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zBd13KtYlAA
**Soviet X-Ray Records (Ribs)** : During the period when Western records were prohibited in the Soviet Union, songs by Elvis were among the most popular choices for black market X-ray records, or "ribs," due to high demand. "Today, people collect these ribs, especially of songs by the Beatles, the Rolling Stones, Elvis, and the Beach Boys, as well as other Western artists." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B2g4QSREzpw
