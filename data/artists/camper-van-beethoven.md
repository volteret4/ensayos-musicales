## albums
**Telephone Free Landslide (1985) - Debut Album** : This album was Camper Van Beethoven's 1985 debut and featured a nonsensical title. It showcased the band's blend of witty, droll, smart-assy, and completely nonsensical lyrics. ## curiosities
**David Lowery's Academic and Writing Career** : Singer David Lowery is a lecturer in the music business program at the University of Georgia and maintains a website called `The Trichordist`, where he writes on music. "And singer David Lowry works as a lecturer in the music business program at the University of Georgia, plus he has a website where he writes on music called The Trichortist." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9_3EbwKPnU
**David Lowery's Diverse Background** : Frontman David Lowery was trained as a mathematician and later worked as a financial analyst and a trader of derivatives, a profession described as largely misunderstood. His varied background informed his witty, droll, smart-assy, and often nonsensical lyrics. "Out front was David Lowry, a mathematician by training who would later work as a financial analyst and a traitor of derivatives, something that almost no one really understands." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9_3EbwKPnU
**San Francisco Area Origin (1983)** : Camper Van Beethoven originated from the San Francisco area, with a history dating back to at least 1983, a period considered the earliest days of what would become known as alternative music. "They're from the San Francisco area. I'm going to have a history that goes back to at least 1983." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9_3EbwKPnU
**Spin-off Group Cracker** : Camper Van Beethoven still exists today, as does their spin-off group, Cracker, which achieved significant alt-rock hits in the 1990s. "Campers still exist today as does their spin-off group Cracker. They had some big all-rock hits in the 90s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9_3EbwKPnU
**Surrealist, Absurdist Folk Style** : From their inception, Camper Van Beethoven embraced a style they termed "surrealist, absurdist folk." Their music incorporated elements of punk alongside folk music, and their lineup occasionally featured a violinist and ska beats. "From the beginning, the band was into what they called surrealist, absurdist folk. They had elements of punk to what they were doing. But they also seemed to like folk music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9_3EbwKPnU
