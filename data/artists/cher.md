**Gypsys, Tramps & Thieves (1971)** : This song, the first of Cher's trilogy of number-one hits, is about a 16-year-old girl from an immigrant family traveling through the Southern US with a medicine show in the late 19th century. The lyrics depict the family as outcasts doing what was necessary to survive, including prostitution, and allude to the narrator's pregnancy, suggesting a continuing cycle of poverty. Rob Tannenbaum, a music journalist, called it one of the greatest songs of the 20th century in 2017, praising its voyeuristic, exploitative nature redeemed by Cher's "punkish defiance" in addressing prejudice, poverty, and consequences of pregnancy for working-class women.
