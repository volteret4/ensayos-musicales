**Formation and Influence (1970s)** : Formed by Susan Ballion (who became Siouxsie Sioux) and bass player Steve Severin, Siouxsie and the Banshees emerged as one of the first generation goth bands, with their music being played as early as 1978. Siouxsie's distinctive appearance, featuring black hair, heavy mascara and eyeshadow, and big soulful eyes, along with the band's name conjuring horror, established her as an "architect for Goth's Worldwide." The band continued to grow in popularity, and Siouxsie's fashion choices became increasingly influential. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
**Goth Purity Debate** : There is a significant debate among Goth fans regarding their "Goth purity." The band has, at times, distanced themselves from the Goth label, but their dark music, themes of introspection, and fashion have inextricably linked them to Goth. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
