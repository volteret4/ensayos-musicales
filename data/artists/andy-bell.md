**Initial Criticism as Erasure Singer** : In the early days of Erasure, Andy Bell faced significant criticism from audiences, who sometimes interrupted concerts to accuse him of not contributing much or being a "bad copy" of Alison Moyet. This was particularly evident during performances in small venues following the commercial failure of *Wonderland*. **Powerful and Versatile Vocal Range** : Andy Bell possessed two key qualities Vince Clarke sought: a potent voice with a broad range, capable of performing excellent falsettos and singing almost like a tenor. He was also noted for his easygoing personality. **Transformative Impact on Vince Clarke's Music** : While Andy Bell's contribution to Erasure's first album, *Wonderland*, was limited due to already completed material, his incredible personality and interpretive style later brought a new, romantic, dreamy, and sometimes melancholic touch to Vince Clarke's compositions. This unique interpretation gave Erasure a much more complete sound.
