**Breakthrough at 33** : Debbie Harry became a big rock star in her early 30s. **Early Career Diversity** : Before her breakthrough with Blondie, Debbie Harry held various jobs in New York, including secretary at the American office of the BBC, waitress, go-go dancer, and a Playboy bunny at the New York Playboy Club. "She worked in New York. And for a while she was a secretary at the American office of the BBC. After that she was a waitress. She was a go-go dancer. And for a while a playboy bunny slinging cocktails in the New York location of the Playboy Club." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P45lJTdneoI
**Joining Blondie at 31** : By 1974, Debbie Harry was in The Stilettos, which led to Angel in the Snake, and then Blondie. She was 31 years old when she started playing for the nascent punk scene at CBGB in 1975, which was considered "ancient" for the time. "By the time she started playing for the nascent punks at CBGB she was an ancient 31 years old." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P45lJTdneoI
**Singing Career Start (1968)** : Debbie Harry's singing career began in 1968 as a backing singer for the folk band The Wind in the Willows, who released one album.
