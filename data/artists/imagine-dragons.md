## albums
**Continued Silence (2012) – Major Label Debut EP** : A six-track EP released in February 2012, marking the band's first release under their deal with Interscope. It included the major hit "Radioactive" as its first track, which propelled the band into mainstream success. "The first track on this new 6-track EP, which was called Continued Silence, and came out in February of 2012, was this one, and you probably know it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Evolve (2017) – Third Studio Album** : Released in June 2017, this album featured a unique sound combining elements of rock, pop, hip hop, and electronic music that appealed to a broad audience. "The third Imagine Dragons album was called Evolve. It appeared in June 2017." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Hell and Silence (2010) – Third EP** : Released only seven months after their second EP, on March 12, 2010. It also featured five tracks and was recorded at The Killers' studio, with some tracks posted on MySpace before its release. "It also featured five tracks, and was released on March 12, 2010." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Imagine Dragons (2009) – Second EP** : A five-song indie EP released at the beginning of September 2009, recorded at Battleborn Studios, which is owned by The Killers. "It was a five song indie thing that was released at the beginning of September 2009." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**It's Time (2011) – Fourth Indie EP** : This eight-song EP was released almost one year to the day from the previous one, recorded in a studio inside the Palm's Hotel, and included a bunch of videos. The title track became a local hit on Las Vegas radio. "Eight songs this time, all recorded in a recording studio inside the Palm's Hotel." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Mercury Act One (2021) – Fifth Studio Album** : Released in 2021, this album falls outside the Concord Music Publishing catalog deal, allowing the band to retain earnings from it. It features the song "Follow You," inspired by singer Dan Reynolds' reunion with his wife after a seven-month separation. "Imagine dragons from their 2021 album Mercury Act One." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Night Visions (2012) – Debut Studio Album** : Released eight months after the `Continued Silence` EP, with six of its eleven songs taken from previous EPs. It debuted at number two on the American album chart, selling more copies in its first week than any other rock album since 2006, and remained in the top 10 through 2012, 2013, and 2014. It also reached the top five in Canada, the UK, Ireland, Mexico, Australia, Norway, Portugal, the Netherlands, and New Zealand, selling seven million copies worldwide, a significant number for the age of streaming, and was the most streamed song on all of Spotify in 2013 (likely referring to the album or artist being most streamed). "It debuted on number two on the American album chart, selling more copies in its first week than any other rock album since 2006." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Origins (2018) – Fourth Studio Album** : This album showed up on November 9, 2018, with "Natural" as its first single. It was the last album included in the band's catalog sale to Concord Music Publishing. "Let's get a song in here from the fourth Imagine Dragons album. It's called Origins, and it showed up on November 9, 2018." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Smoke + Mirrors (2015) – Second Studio Album** : Appeared in February 2015, almost three years after their debut album, following 170 gigs supporting `Night Visions`. It was trimmed from over 50 demos to 13 final tracks and debuted at number one in the US, Canada, and the UK, supported by a massive promotional push including flying fans to tiny clubs, Hard Rock Cafe screen takeovers, and a scavenger hunt in the album artwork. "Smoke and Mirrors appeared in February 2015 and debuted at number one in the US, also number one in Canada and the UK." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Speak To Me (2008) – Debut EP** : The band's first EP, released in 2008. The title track was highlighted as an instructive example of their early sound. "I think I listened to a song from this record would be instructive." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ

## curiosities
**Ankylosing Spondylitis and Songwriting** : Singer Dan Reynolds suffers from Ankylosing Spondylitis, a type of arthritis that affects the joints of the spine, hips, and shoulders, causing increasing stiffness and pain. He channeled this personal struggle into the song "Believer," writing it as a self-motivational pep talk. "Dan Reynolds suffers from a type of arthritis called Enkelosing Spondylitis." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Battleborn Studios Recordings** : Two of Imagine Dragons' early independent EPs, `Imagine Dragons` (2009) and `Hell and Silence` (2010), were recorded at Battleborn Studios, which is owned by the Las Vegas-based band The Killers. "The next Imagine Dragons EP was recorded at Battleborn Studios. If that name sounds vaguely familiar, it's because it's owned by the killers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Berkeley School of Music Influence** : Three out of the four current members of Imagine Dragons (Wayne Sermon, Ben McKee, and Daniel Platzman) are graduates or students of the Berkeley School of Music, indicating a strong academic musical background within the band. "That leaves us with today's Imagine Dragons lineup. Dan Reynolds, Wayne Sermon, Ben McKee, and Daniel Platsman, three of them, Berkeley School of Music students." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Breakthrough Performance at Bite of Las Vegas** : On October 17, 2009, Imagine Dragons got an unexpected break at the "Bite of Las Vegas" festival when they were drafted last-minute to fill in for Train, whose singer Pat Monahan fell ill. They performed for 26,000 people at a skate park, which launched them onto a new trajectory, leading to local awards and headlining gigs. "Imagine Dragons were drafted to fill trained spots. They had 26,000 people to themselves, and they made the most of that chance." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Catalog Sale (2020s)** : Imagine Dragons made $100 million by selling their music catalog and publishing rights, illustrating a major trend in the music business of well-capitalized companies acquiring artist assets. "Imagine Dragons made $100 million this way." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Yq2A_o9-ulM
**Catalog Sale to Concord Music Publishing** : In August 2020, Imagine Dragons sold a majority stake in their music catalog, encompassing everything released up to and including the `Origins` album, to Concord Music Publishing. The sale price is widely assumed to be north of 100 million US dollars. This deal allows the band to receive years of royalties upfront and has significant tax benefits, while Concord believes the band has a long lifespan ahead with steady revenue from various platforms. "In August of 2020, Imagine Dragons made the decision to sell a majority stake in everything they had released up to and including the Origins album." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Contemporary Catalog Sale** : Imagine Dragons are included in a list of contemporary acts who have sold some or all of their back catalog, indicating that this financial strategy is attractive to artists across different career stages. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sfie4YYncxk
**Dan Reynolds' Professional Drive** : Despite being a great student with discipline who could have pursued a business career, Dan Reynolds realized he had to be a professional musician and dedicated all his energy and focus to this path. "He realized that he had to be a professional musician. So he put all his energies, all his focus, and all his discipline into that." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Early Life of Dan Reynolds** : Singer Dan Reynolds was born seventh in a nine-child Mormon family in Las Vegas. He was an Eagle Scout, quarterback of his high school team, and served as a full-time missionary for two years in his late teens, during which he was allowed only two phone calls home per year (Mother's Day and Christmas). "He was born seventh in a nine child Mormon family in Las Vegas." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ

**LGBTQ+ Activism** : Dan Reynolds became an outspoken activist for LGBTQ+ rights, despite being a straight, married former Mormon missionary with three children. He heads the LoveLoud foundation and music festival, which has raised over a million dollars for LGBTQ+-related organizations. He openly displays a rainbow flag at shows, even in risky regions like Turkey and Utah, where the Mormon church has differing views, and has prompted statements from the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. "Shortly after the evolve album came out, we started hearing about Dan Reynolds' activism for LGBTQ rights." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ

"According to band legend, that is an anagram for something." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ

**Relocation to Las Vegas** : After forming in Provo, Utah, and building a large local following, the band moved to Las Vegas down I-15 to expand their opportunities beyond Utah, performing in various lounges, bars, and clubs, and playing many covers, including songs by The Cure and The Rolling Stones. "So the band moved down I-15 to Las Vegas where they started performing in various lounges and bars and clubs, lots of covers by the cure in the stones." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Streaming Success with Pop-Leaning Rock Sound** : Imagine Dragons were one of the rock bands that found significant streaming success in the 2010s, with their songs garnering hundreds of millions, and occasionally billions, of listens. Their sound was described as leaning towards the pop end of rock, aligning with the preferences of the early streaming generation. "There was the luminaires, Lorde, Imagine Dragons, Coldplay, Mumford and Sons. **Tolman Couple Departure** : Founding drummer Andrew Tolman and his wife, keyboardist Brittany Tolman, left the band after its breakthrough performance. **Unicorn Status in Modern Rock** : Imagine Dragons is described as a "unicorn" in the rock world because they emerged after streaming became dominant (formed in 2008) yet still manage to sell millions of records and fill arenas. This success is attributed to good songs, hard work, discipline, commitment, social media outreach, and luck. "Imagine Dragons are a unicorn in the world of rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
