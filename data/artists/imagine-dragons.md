# artist - Imagine Dragons

## members
- Andrew Beck (guitar) : original member, left the band shortly after the first EP release.
- Andrew Tollman (drummer) : original member, fellow Brigham Young University student, left the band with his wife Brittany Tollman to settle down and have a family.
- Aurora Florence (pianist) : original member, left the band shortly after the first EP release.
- Ben McKee (bass) : joined as bassist after Dave Lemke left, Berkeley School of Music student, previously played upright bass in a jazz trio, current member.
- Brittany Tollman (keyboards, backup vocals) : joined as keyboardist after Aurora Florence left, Andrew Tollman's wife, left the band with her husband to settle down and have a family.
- Dan Reynolds (singer) : co-founder, born in Las Vegas, seventh of nine children, Mormon background, Eagle Scout, high school quarterback, full-time missionary for two years, attended Brigham Young University, driving need to be a professional musician.
- Daniel Platzman (drummer) : joined as drummer after Andrew Tollman left, Berkeley School of Music graduate, award-winning musician, current member.
- Dave Lemke (bass) : original member, left the band after some initial gigs.
- Teresa Flamino (keyboards) : filled Brittany Tollman's spot, stayed for a few months, left by early 2012.
- Wayne Sermon (guitar) : joined as guitarist after Andrew Beck left, graduate of Berkeley School of Music, current member.

## labels
- Concord Music Publishing : purchased the majority stake in Imagine Dragons' music catalog released up to and including the *Origins* album in August 2020 for over $100 million US dollars.
- Interscope : signed Imagine Dragons in November 2011, released their breakthrough EP and debut album.

- Clubs (Las Vegas) : performed in various clubs after moving to Las Vegas from Provo, Utah.
- Hard Rock Cafe : used as part of a massive promotional push for the *Smoke + Mirrors* album, with screens taken over worldwide.
- Lounges (Las Vegas) : performed in various lounges after moving to Las Vegas from Provo, Utah.

## albums

**Evolve (2017) – Third Studio Album** : released in June 2017, explored a unique sound combining rock, pop, hip-hop, and electronic music. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ

**Origins (2018) – Fourth Studio Album** : released on November 9, 2018, featured "Natural" as its first single, the final album included in the majority catalog sale to Concord Music Publishing. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Second EP (2009) – Five-Song Indie Release** : self-released indie EP, recorded at Battleborn Studios (owned by The Killers), released in early September 2009, featured the song "I Need A Moonlight." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Smoke + Mirrors (2015) – Second Studio Album** : released in February 2015, debuted at number one in the US, Canada, and the UK, featured a massive promotional push including flying fans to tiny clubs, Hard Rock Cafe screen takeover, and an album artwork scavenger hunt. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Speak To Me (2008) – Debut EP** : self-released indie EP, featured the title track "Speak To Me," released shortly after the band's formation. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Third EP (2010) – Five-Track Indie Release** : self-released indie EP, recorded at Battleborn Studios (owned by The Killers), released on March 12, 2010, after tracks were posted on MySpace, featured the song "All Eyes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ

## songs

**Believer (2017)** : single from the *Evolve* album, inspired by Dan Reynolds' experience with Ankylosing Spondylitis (a type of arthritis affecting the spine, hips, and shoulders), serving as a personal pep talk to push through pain. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Demons (2012)** : single from the *Night Visions* album, stayed on the Top 100 for 61 weeks, reportedly the eighth most downloaded song in rock history. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**I Bet My Life (2014)** : first single from the *Smoke + Mirrors* album. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**I Need A Moonlight (2009)** : song from the band's second self-released EP, recorded at Battleborn Studios. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**It's Time (2011)** : title track of the band's fourth self-released EP, written by Dan Reynolds while suffering from depression, became a local hit on Las Vegas radio. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Natural (2018)** : first single from the *Origins* album. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Radioactive (2012)** : first track from the *Continued Silence* EP, inspired by Dan Reynolds' loss of faith in Mormonism, a major hit that stayed on the singles charts for 87 weeks (a record at the time), sold 3 million copies making it one of the biggest selling rock songs in history according to SoundScan, won a Grammy for Best Rock Performance. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Speak To Me (2008)** : title track of their debut self-released EP, represents the band's very early sound. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Wrecked (2021)** : single from the *Mercury Act One* album, inspired by the sudden death of Dan Reynolds' sister-in-law due to cancer. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ

## curiosities
**Activism for LGBTQ+ Rights** : Dan Reynolds has become a prominent activist for LGBTQ+ rights, heading the Love Loud foundation and music festival which has raised over a million dollars for LGBTQ+ organizations, and frequently brings a rainbow flag out at Imagine Dragons shows as a unifying moment. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Band Name Anagram Legend** : the name "Imagine Dragons" is, according to band legend, an anagram of a secret word or phrase that only the band knows and has vowed never to reveal, even to their families; online anagram generators have failed to produce a definitive 13-letter solution. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Bite of Las Vegas Festival Breakthrough (October 17, 2009)** : the band received a pivotal opportunity when they were drafted at the last minute to fill a slot for Train (whose singer Pat Monahan fell ill) at the Bite of Las Vegas festival, performing for 26,000 people and impressing the audience. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**CBGB "Gorman Dysers" Speculation** : a 12-letter anagram, "Gorman Dysing," was suggested as a possible solution to the band name, potentially linking to the full name of the punk bar CBGB ("Country, Bluegrass, Blues, and Other Music for Uplifting Gorman Dysers"), though the band has not confirmed this. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Catalog Sale to Concord Music Publishing (August 2020)** : Imagine Dragons sold a majority stake in their music catalog (everything released up to *Origins* album) to Concord Music Publishing for an assumed sum north of $100 million US dollars, a rare move for a band at mid-career rather than in their twilight years. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Confrontation with the Mormon Church** : Dan Reynolds' LGBTQ+ activism has often targeted the Mormon Church (The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints) due to its stance on gay people, compelling the Church to issue statements regarding his criticisms, making him arguably the most famous Mormon in music. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Dan Reynolds' Driving Musical Ambition** : despite being an excellent student at Brigham Young University with potential for a business career, Dan Reynolds realized his essential need to be a professional musician and committed all his discipline and focus to this path. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Dan Reynolds' Mormon Background** : born seventh of nine children in a Mormon family in Las Vegas, Dan Reynolds was an Eagle Scout, high school quarterback, and served as a full-time missionary for two years with highly restricted communication (two phone calls home per year). ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Extensive Early Work Ethic** : prior to their major breakthrough, Imagine Dragons self-released four EPs, worked social media extensively, posted videos online, played numerous gigs, and networked with other bands. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Financial Prudence of Catalog Sale** : taking money upfront from the catalog sale allowed the band to save a huge bundle on taxes and provided funds for further investments and philanthropic endeavors. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Formation in Provo, Utah (2008)** : Imagine Dragons was formed in Provo, Utah, in 2008 while singer Dan Reynolds was attending Brigham Young University. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Massive Streaming Success** : Imagine Dragons are streaming monsters with over 74 billion global career streams, four of their songs streamed more than a billion times each, and were the most streamed rock band on Spotify. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Personal Inspiration for Songwriting** : Dan Reynolds has drawn heavily on personal experiences for song lyrics, including his depression for "It's Time," his struggle with Ankylosing Spondylitis for "Believer," and the death of his sister-in-law for "Wrecked." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Philanthropic Engagements** : the band is deeply engaged in philanthropy, supporting cancer causes, anti-violence groups, Amnesty International, school music programs, refugee support through the United Nations, and scholarships. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Relocation to Las Vegas** : after establishing a large local following in Provo, the band moved to Las Vegas to pursue broader opportunities, performing in lounges, bars, and clubs and often playing covers by The Cure and The Rolling Stones. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**Significant Lineup Changes** : the band experienced several lineup changes in its early years, including original members Andrew Beck, Aurora Florence, and Dave Lemke leaving, and the Tolmans (Andrew and Brittany) departing to start a family, before stabilizing with the current core. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
**The Killers' Battleborn Studios** : Imagine Dragons recorded their second and third self-released EPs at Battleborn Studios, which is owned by the Las Vegas-based band The Killers. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GmYlTwI_TQQ
