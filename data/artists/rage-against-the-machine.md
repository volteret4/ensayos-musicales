## albums
**Rage Against The Machine (1992) - Debut Album** : Released on November 3rd, 1992, this album features a powerful photograph of Thich Kwan Duk, a burning Buddhist monk, on its cover. The liner notes clearly state, "no samples, keyboards or synthesizers were used in the making of this record." The album includes guest vocals from Maynard James Keenan of Tool and Stephen Perkins of Jane's Addiction. Notably, the version of "Bullet in the Head" on the album was recorded before the band had ever performed live. Renowned for its audio quality, "Track 3, take the power back" is specifically recommended for testing speakers and amplifiers. "on November 3rd, 1992, the album with a dying, Fitchquan dock on the cover appeared in stores." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Xo5DRSW95QQ
**The Battle of Los Angeles** : This album achieved the number one position on the charts, demonstrating the band's continued commercial and critical success. "We had Rage Against The Machine with the number one album with the Battle of Los Angeles." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hBQE4t72i3o

## songs
**Bombtrack (1992)** : This song is the opening track of Rage Against The Machine's 1992 debut record. The instrumental intro for "Bombtrack" was a piece Tom Morello had leftover from his previous band, Lock Up. The main riff of the song was derived from "Alice in My Fantacies" by Funkadelic, a track from their 1974 album "Standing on the Verge of Getting It On." "Bomb track, the first song, and they're amazing, 1992 first record." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Xo5DRSW95QQ
**Bullet in the Head (1992)** : A demo version of this song was created during the band's initial rehearsals in the San Fernando Valley, and it was noted for being remarkably true to its final recorded form. The album version of "Bullet in the Head" was recorded before the band had ever performed a single live show. "that's a demo of Bulletin the Head, which of course would end up on Rage's debut record." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Xo5DRSW95QQ
**Freedom (1992)** : This track is featured on Rage Against The Machine's self-titled debut album, which was released on November 3rd, 1992. "Rage against the machine with freedom from their self-titled debut record released on November 3rd, 1992." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Xo5DRSW95QQ
**Killing In The Name Of (1992)** : This song is given as an example of a rock song that directly tackles the theme of murder. "killing in the name of by rage." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZcYKZ5ovoy4
**Killing in the Name (1992)** : Recognized as one of the greatest and angriest rock songs of the 1990s from their debut album. Due to its lyrical content, specifically containing 17 instances of the "F-Bomb," the Canadian Broadcast Standards Council's regulations often necessitate radio stations to play a sanitized or truncated version of the song. "Rage with the truncated version of one of the greatest and angriest rock songs of the 1990s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Xo5DRSW95QQ
**Know Your Enemy (1992)** : Appearing on Rage Against The Machine's debut album, "Know Your Enemy" features prominent guest vocals from Maynard James Keenan, the singer of Tool. Additionally, Stephen Perkins, the drummer for Jane's Addiction, also contributed vocals to this song. "Maynard James Keenan of Tool, sings on a song called Know Your Enemy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Xo5DRSW95QQ

## curiosities
**2000 Breakup** : Rage Against The Machine broke up around the year 2000, contributing to a sense of disappointment and decline within the rock and alt-rock scenes at the turn of the millennium. "There were other disappointments too, rage against the machine broke up." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hBQE4t72i3o
**2022 Reunion Tour Cut Short** : The band embarked on a reunion tour in 2022, which lasted only 36 days, from July 9, 2022, in Troy, Wisconsin, to August 14, 2022, at Madison Square Garden. The tour was cut short after vocalist Zach D. LaRocca tore his Achilles tendon on stage. "Although their reunion lasted all of 36 days before it fell apart, the first show July 9, 2022 in Troy, Wisconsin, last show, August 14, 2022 at Madison Square Garden. It might have gone on longer and might have been much bigger. Ted Singer, Zach D. LaRocca, not torn his Achilles on stage." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZzIqIydZTlA
**21st Century Reunion** : Rage Against The Machine was mentioned as one of the prominent alt-rock bands that reunited in the 21st century, helping to inject temporary vitality back into the alt-rock scene and bring lapsed fans back. "In addition, the Pixies, smashing pumpkins, the lemon heads, rage against the machine...These reunions injected some life into the scene, albeit temporary life." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_yslM5oanRo
**Band Naming** : The band adopted the name "Rage Against The Machine" from the title of a planned second album by Zach Dela Roka's previous band, Inside Out, which never materialized. The name was ultimately deemed more fitting and powerful for the new group. "the band had decided that Rage against the machine would be a great name." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Xo5DRSW95QQ
**Breakup Date (October 18, 2000)** : The band officially "fell apart" on this date. "They fell apart on October the 18th, 2000." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yR3xc3ra5tE
**Creative/Political Dispute Leading to Singer's Departure** : Singer Zack de la Rocha left the band due to a "creative dispute, maybe a political dispute," as he sought to take the band in a direction that the other members did not agree with. "Zach, Rage's singer, had decided to leave." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-f9jhqAHkQo
**Decision to Continue as a Unit** : Following Zack de la Rocha's departure, the remaining three members—Tom Morello, Timmy, and Brad—decided they wanted to stay together and continue making music. "However, the other three guys, Tom, Timmy and Brad, decided that they really wanted to stay together." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-f9jhqAHkQo
**Initial Performances and Demo Tape** : After forming, the band began by playing at parties and small clubs throughout the Los Angeles area. They self-funded the recording of an indie cassette demo, which they then sold directly at their shows to build a following. "So the new groups started playing parties and small clubs in and around the LA area." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Xo5DRSW95QQ
**Liner Notes Thank Yous** : The debut album's liner notes feature an extensive list of acknowledgments, including Beto (Zach Dela Roka's father, an artist who destroyed his paintings during a nervous breakdown) and revolutionary musicians such as Chuck D and Joe Strummer. "Beto is Zack's dad, the artist who went crazy in Burdala's paintings." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Xo5DRSW95QQ
**Major Label Deal (Epic Records)** : Despite previous negative experiences with major labels by three-quarters of the band members, Rage Against The Machine signed with Epic Records. This decision was driven by the label's agreement to grant the band complete creative control over their material and recognizing that a major label's global distribution and marketing power was essential for effectively disseminating their political message. "Epic was the major that agreed to all of Rage's demands." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Xo5DRSW95QQ
**Reunion Decision Date (January 22, 2007)** : The band decided to play together again on this date. "and didn't decide to play together until January 22nd, 2007." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yR3xc3ra5tE
**Support from Tool** : Rage Against The Machine gained significant early exposure by opening for Tool at some of their concerts. Adam Jones of Tool, a friend of Tom Morello, invited Rage to perform, which helped their demo tape attract the attention of talent scouts and led to a bidding war. "Adam invited Rage to open for some of their gigs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Xo5DRSW95QQ
**Time Apart (6 years, 3 months, 5 days)** : The band was apart for this duration, calculated from their breakup to their decision to reunite. "So that works out to about six years, three months and five days." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yR3xc3ra5tE
**Transition to Audioslave** : After Chris Cornell joined the remaining members of Rage Against The Machine, they collectively decided to "jettison the Rage name" and reform as Audioslave. "And I'm not really sure why there was a collective decision to jettison the Rage name in favor of Audio Slive, which is where we are today." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-f9jhqAHkQo
