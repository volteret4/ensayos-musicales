## albums
**Paradise and Me (1995)** : K's Choice's second album, released in 1995, which included the song "Not An Addict." The album did not rise above number 121 on the charts in North America. "The album didn't rise above number 121 on the charts." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZcF5JMhBj2M
**Paradise in Me (Undated)** : This album featured the song "Not An Addict," which became the band's breakout hit, reaching the top 40 in multiple countries and becoming an alt-rock hit in North America. The success of this song led to K's Choice touring with Alanis Morissette during her *Jagged Little Pill* peak. "Then came Paradise and Me, another record, which featured a song called Not An Addict." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SOAL0GODN3M
**The Great Subconscious Club (1994)** : This was K's Choice's debut album, released in 1994. The band was initially known as "Just The Choice" but had to change their name to "K's Choice" at the last minute due to an American band already holding dibs on the original name. The band initially claimed the "K" stood for Joseph K. from Kafka's *The Trial*, but later admitted it simply sounded good. "The debut album was called The Great Subconscious Club. That was released in 1994." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SOAL0GODN3M

## songs
**Not An Addict (1995)** : A song that started as a local hit in Belgium, then became regional, and spread internationally through 1996 after being picked up by American label 550 Music (part of the Sony Empire). It caught fire in North America at the end of the Grunge era, though five of the album's six singles went nowhere in North America. The lyrics were written by Sarah Bettens, better known as Sam in the 90s, and are primarily about addiction, specifically cigarettes, though Bettens was also experimenting with acid and mushrooms at the time. As of February 2026, it had approximately 27 million Spotify plays and 7.5 million YouTube views, totaling 38.4 million, with a One Hit Wonder Power Ranking of 3.36 out of 10. "Yes, it's about addiction, primarily cigarettes, actually." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZcF5JMhBj2M
**Not An Addict (1996)** : Released in 1996, "Not An Addict" became K's Choice's only major hit, achieving Top 40 status in numerous countries and becoming an alt-rock hit in North America. The song's success led to the band touring with Alanis Morissette, who was at the height of her popularity with the *Jagged Little Pill* album. The band has never been able to replicate this song's success. "And this one blew up, reaching the top 40 in a bunch of countries." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SOAL0GODN3M

## curiosities
**Breakup and Reunion** : K's Choice experienced a breakup in 2003, but later reunited in 2009. They have released more albums since their reunion, but none have achieved the same level of success as their hit song "Not An Addict." "There were more albums, followed by a breakup in 2003." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SOAL0GODN3M
**Continued Presence in Benelux** : K's Choice maintains a significant presence in Belgium, the Netherlands, and Luxembourg, contrasting with their limited recognition in the rest of the world. "They continue to have a significant presence in Belgium, the Netherlands, and Luxembourg, but for the rest of the world, not so much." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZcF5JMhBj2M
**Origin of Band Name** : K's Choice was originally called "Just The Choice" but was forced to change their name to "K's Choice" at the last minute before their debut album's release due to an American band having prior claim to the name. They initially offered a cover story that "K" stood for Joseph K., the main character in Franz Kafka's novel *The Trial*, but later confessed the name change was simply because "K's Choice just sounded good." "It had to be changed to Case Choice at the very last second because an American band had dibs on the name." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SOAL0GODN3M
**Sam Bettens' Transition** : The band's singer, Sarah Bettens, amassed a large lesbian following during K's Choice's heyday. After moving to Tennessee and working as a firefighter, Sarah transitioned to a man in 2019 and now goes by the name Sam Bettens. He currently resides in Palm Desert, California, with his partner, two stepchildren, and two adopted children. "Then in 2019, Sarah transitioned to a man. And now goes by the name Sam Betons." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SOAL0GODN3M
