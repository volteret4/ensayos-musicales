## albums
**Beyoncé (2013)** : A self-titled album released by Beyoncé on Friday, June 10, 2013, as a surprise. This unconventional Friday release date, before the establishment of the global release day, gave her a strategic head start over other artists for chart sales, as it allowed her first chart week to include Friday, Saturday, Sunday, and Monday, thereby artificially inflating her initial sales figures. "She decided to mess things up with a surprise release of a self titled album on June 10, 2013, which was a Friday." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GlcOoINI7EQ ← beyoncé ← beyoncé
**Cowboy Carter (2024)** : Beyoncé won the Grammy Award for Best Country Album and Album of the Year for "Cowboy Carter" in 2024. This achievement made her the first African American woman to win a country Grammy since The Pointer Sisters in 1975, 50 years prior. "Beyonce won best country album and album of the year for that matter for Country Carter." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 53 - More than Charley Pride - African Americans in 70s Country | https://www.ftr70.com/

## songs
**Crazy in Love (2003)** : This song, featuring Jay-Z, was the "incontestable" winner of the 2003 Billboard Song of the Summer and formally launched Beyoncé's solo career. It was built from a Shy Lights horn sample, with every aspect of its production evoking summer: a "sweaty beat" that dominated July and August, lyrics about a "love like a fever," and Jay-Z's rap about feeling "crazy and ranged." The accompanying video notably featured dancers "jiggling and twerking in tank tops and short shorts," as Beyoncé asserted her role as "pop's new queen." "Everything about this production evoked summer, the sweaty beat that dominated July and August of 2003, the lyrics about a love like a fever, J. Z's rap about feeling quote crazy and ranged." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Song(s) of the Summer Edition Part 2 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4ef50c30a1408dc292e4
