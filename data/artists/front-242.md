## albums
**Front by Front (1988)** : This album, released in 1988, marked Front 242's turn in the spotlight and contained the influential song "Headhunter." It solidified their position as pioneers of electronic body music. "Front II-IV II's turn in the spotlight came in 1988 when they released a album called Front by Front, which contained this song." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE

## songs
**Headhunter (1988)** : Released in 1988, this industrial music track extensively features the Yamaha DX7 synthesizer. With the possible exception of the drum track, the song is almost entirely composed using the DX7, showcasing the instrument's maximum advantage and its role in shaping the sound of the 1980s. "With the exception of the drum track, and frankly, I'm not even sure that's not included, this song by Front242 is pretty much entirely DX7." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TsiYLGGSJ2U

## curiosities
**Associated with Wax Trax** : Front 242 was one of the bands associated with Wax Trax of Chicago, a specialist indie label for heavy music that appeared around 1990. "This was the home of Ministry Front II IV II, Front Line Assembly and Dozens More." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Collaboration with Ministry** : Several members of Front 242 contributed directly to Ministry's "groundbreaking records" during the mid-80s, assisting in Ministry's stylistic transition and highlighting a collaborative spirit within the emerging industrial scene. "Several members of Front II-IV II actually helped in Ministries' transition by appearing on those groundbreaking records of the mid-80s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE
**Influence on Ministry's Al Jourgensen** : A Front 242 show in Chicago is famously cited as the location where Al Jourgensen of Ministry had an epiphany, causing him to re-evaluate his "meek, techno pop ways" and leading to Ministry's transition into a harder industrial sound. "But legend has it that it was at a Front II-IV II show in Chicago that Alan Durgensen had as epiphany, of sorts, the one that showed him the error of his meek, techno pop ways." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE
**Influence on Trent Reznor** : Front 242 was a key influence on Trent Reznor, specifically their "rage and fury." This inspiration helped guide Trent's evolving sound towards the more aggressive, industrial style adopted by Nine Inch Nails. "He found that he really liked the rage and fury of bands like Ministry and Front242 and Canada's Skinny Puppy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M3nUQtja0qc
**Pioneering Electronic Body Music (EBM) (1981)** : As early as 1981, Belgium's Front 242 was creating what they termed "electronic body music" (EBM), defining a new subgenre with their distinct sound and approach. "They had been creating what they called electronic body music as early as 1981." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE
