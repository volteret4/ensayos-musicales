## curiosities
**Blinding and Vow (1932)** : Born Lewis Harden, he was permanently blinded at age 12 when a dynamite cap exploded in his face in rural Kansas. Following this accident, he vowed to become a composer, attending schools for the blind to learn music theory through Braille books. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f2KA97T4u08
**Identity and Street Performance** : Moondog was Louis Harden Jr., a homeless, six-foot-tall, blind New York street performer who dressed in a Viking costume. He was often found silently banging a drum outside the stage door of Carnegie Hall, where he also composed music and made records. His distinctive appearance and musical talent drew attention from prominent classical musicians. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
**Lawsuit Against Alan Freed** : Moondog sued disc jockey Alan Freed for using his moniker "Moon Dog" on air and for his "Moon Dog Balls" concerts, as well as using Moondog's Symphony as a radio show theme. With the assistance of big band leader Benny Goodman, Moondog won the lawsuit, securing $6,000 in damages and a promise from Freed to cease using the name "Moon Dog." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
**New York Career and Name** : After moving to New York in 1943, he performed on the street and became well-known to legends like Benny Goodman, Charlie Parker, and Leonard Bernstein. He adopted the moniker "Moondog" around 1947, inspired by a dog he knew that howled at the moon. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f2KA97T4u08
**Post-Lawsuit Career and Legacy** : After the lawsuit, Moondog continued to be recognized as a serious performer and composer, releasing several albums under a recording contract. He later married, started a family, and returned to Carnegie Hall in 1989 to conduct a performance of his own compositions. He eventually moved to Europe and died of heart failure in 1999 at the age of 83. His life and work were influential to a wide range of artists, from "Itenorid hippies" to bands cited as important in the creation of Trip Hop music. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
