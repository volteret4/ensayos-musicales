## curiosities
**Band Name from Gay Subculture** : The Toronto group's very name, "Rough Trade," was a term originating from gay subculture, indicating a direct connection to the community and setting a tone for their provocative content. "a Toronto group whose very name was a term from gay subculture." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hswtuenFwvM
**Emergence as Serious Stars** : Rough Trade emerged as one of the serious stars through the late 1970s and into the 1980s, benefiting from the new industry infrastructure and increased radio support for Canadian talent. This period marked a shift towards greater national and international success for homegrown artists. "Love her boy, Chilla Wack, Bruce Coburn, rough trade, the pay-all was Toronto, the headpins, the spoons, bride-adams, parachute club, men without hats, Cory Hart. They all emerged as serious stars through the late 1970s and into the 1980s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6PGgNCaW4xo
**Kevin Staples Death (2025)** : Kevin Staples, keyboardist of Rough Trade, died in 2025. "The keyboardist of the Canadian new wave/post-punk band known for 'High School Confidential.'" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZPlsR7_WiZQ
**True North Records Success** : Rough Trade was among the artists on Bernie Finkelstein's True North Records who achieved "legitimate hits" due to the support provided by Cancon quotas. "True North was unabashedly Canadian, and thanks to the Cancon quotas, a few of their acts broke through and had legitimate hits, artists like Bruce Cobard in Rough Trade." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U2kYleeMnl8
