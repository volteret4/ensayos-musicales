## albums
**Rose Garden (1971)** : Lynn Anderson's album "Rose Garden" was a massive success, reaching number one on the country album chart for 14 consecutive weeks. This achievement was unmatched by another female artist until Shania Twain's "The Woman in Me" 25 years later. "The album was number one on the country album chart for 14 weeks in a row and the next woman to do better than that was Shenaya Twain with the woman in me and that was 25 years later." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 23 - Women of Country Music in the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/023-country-women-72820-2-45-pm

## curiosities
**Appearance on Dina (1976)** : Lynn Anderson, known for her song "Rose Garden," was featured on Dina Shore's talk show on November 26, 1976, alongside Wild Cherry, George Kirby, Kathy Jordan, and ABBA. "The November 26 1976 episode of Dina featured the comedian George Kirby the band wild cherry Miss Teenage America Kathy Jordan singer Lynn Anderson who sang Rose Garden." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 56 - Talk Shows of the 1970s | https://www.ftr70.com/ ← lynn-anderson
**Appearance-focused Criticism (1973)** : A 1973 newspaper article from Tyler, Texas, titled "Women Country Singers far removed from their counterparts," focused heavily on Lynn Anderson's attractiveness on her album covers, illustrating how women in country music were often judged more for their looks than their musical talent. "The author goes on to say how attractive Connie Smith and Lynn Anderson are on their album covers." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 23 - Women of Country Music in the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/023-country-women-72820-2-45-pm
**Producer/Husband's Control over Material** : Lynn Anderson's producer, who was also her husband, initially had to be persuaded that "Rose Garden" was an "appropriate song for a woman to sing." This highlights the significant limitations placed on female country artists in choosing their own material until the late 1970s. "Lynn Anderson wanted to record it too but her producer, who also happened to be her husband, had to be convinced that it was an appropriate song for a woman to sing." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 23 - Women of Country Music in the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/023-country-women-72820-2-45-pm
