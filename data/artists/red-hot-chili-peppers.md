**Californication (1999) – Decades Best Comeback** : Released on June 8, 1999, after John Frusciante's return, this album marked "one of the greatest comebacks of the decade." It became the band's biggest selling album to that point, selling more than 15 million copies and winning a Grammy. "The resulting album was one of the greatest comebacks of the decade. It was the Pepper's biggest selling album to that point, sold more than 15 million copies, won the McGrammy and it was called California Cation." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Californication (1999) – Loudness Wars Example** : Released in 1999, this album is frequently cited as one of the worst-sounding records affected by the "loudness wars" due to excessive compression during mastering. The heavy compression resulted in audible clipping, distortion, and a near-zero dynamic range, leading to a comparatively unsatisfactory listening experience. An unauthorized "Unmastered Edition" circulates online, offering a superior sonic experience with enhanced clarity in Chad Smith's cymbals, subtle keyboard lines, improved background vocals by Flea, greater separation in Flea's bass notes, and more openness in John Frusciante's chords. "There is so much compression used in the mastering that you can hear all kinds of clipping and distortion." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Zniri9uSlc
**Mother's Milk (1989)** : This album is identified as a "little breakthrough" for the Red Hot Chili Peppers, following a period where the band "discovered themselves" over their preceding three albums. "They discovered themselves through 3 albums before they could deliver a little breakthrough with Mother's Milk..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
**One Hot Minute (1995)** : This album, featuring Dave Navarro on guitar, was released in 1995 and was considered "inferior" to their previous work with John Frusciante, notably `Blood Sugar Sex Magik`. "Their 1995 album, One Hot Minute with Dave Navarro, X of Jane's addiction, of course, was considered to be inferior to what they had accomplished with John Fruciante and Blood Sugar Sex Magic four years earlier." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Stadium Arcadium (2006)** : This double-disc album was released on May 5, 2006. It spawned the hit single "Dani California" and went on to sell approximately 6.5 million copies by the end of the decade, making it one of the few commercial bright spots for the recording industry during a dismal period of declining sales. "The Red Hot Chili Peppers with Danny California, a single from their double disc Stadium Arcadia, which came out on May 5, 2006, and eventually sold about 6.5 million copies by the end of the decade." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-CT7yYXonIk

## curiosities
**"Mother's Milk" Nudity Lawsuit** : The cover of *Mother's Milk* sparked controversy due to its depiction of Don Lane, a topless woman cradling the band members. Lane claimed the band did not ask for her permission to use her image. Several record store chains, including Walmart, refused to stock or display the record due to perceived female nudity, despite the minimal exposure. Walmart specifically opted for a censored version where band members were enlarged to cover more of Lane. Compounding the issue, limited promotional posters were distributed with Lane's breasts completely exposed, again without her knowledge. Lane subsequently sued the band and was awarded a $50,000 settlement. "She sued the band and won a settlement of $50,000." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sn5TWBFd0XM
**Anthony Kiedis's Initial Reunion Concerns** : Anthony Kiedis was initially apprehensive about John Frusciante rejoining the band, worrying that unresolved past resentments could get in the way of their future success. He felt the need to understand if John's return stemmed from a place of love and creation rather than bitterness. "Anthony was initially worried that past resentments would get in the way of their success." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Nr-3Y3UWQcI
**Anthony Kiedis's Pre-Reunion Sensation** : Anthony Kiedis observed that Flea was not "on fire" or feeling his "best self" in the band's previous incarnation, which led Anthony to independently wonder about John Frusciante's potential involvement again, initially thinking of him writing or producing rather than fully rejoining. This sensation grew despite no communication with John for some time. "I just got the sensation like, it would be really nice if John would get involved." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Nr-3Y3UWQcI
**Chad Smith's Reunion Catalyst** : The idea for John Frusciante's return was sparked by Chad Smith, who told John at Flea's wedding that he missed playing with him and suggested they, along with Flea, should jam sometime. This casual comment initiated the chain of events leading to John's rejoining the band. "Chad had said something to me when I saw him at Flea's wedding. And he was like, he misses playing with me and him and Flea should jam sometime." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Nr-3Y3UWQcI
**Classification as Alternative** : Red Hot Chili Peppers were among the bands identified in the early 1990s as being uniquely difficult to categorize, resulting in their classification under the broad "alternative" music label. "there were the red hot chili peppers... Alternative came to mean difference. It was a basket in which to put bands that defied description." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t2uXHP9cBdM
**Collaborative Songwriting Process** : The creative process for new material involved a natural flow of ideas among the members. John Frusciante would bring "gems" (ideas, chord progressions, arrangements, vocal melodies) to practice, which would inspire Flea to bring his own contributions. Anthony Kiedis would then go for long drives in his Chevy to generate his parts and add them to the collective "stew." "It's like every time John would bring a gem into practice, you know, flee would feel like, okay, I'll bring a gem." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Nr-3Y3UWQcI
**Early 1990s Peak and Guitarist Issues** : The band peaked "too soon" in the 1990s and faced "guitarist problems" by the time the Alt Nation movement was in full swing, impacting their album quality. "They had peaked too soon in the 1990s and by the time the whole Alt Nation thing was in full swing, they were back to having guitarist problems." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Early Touring Rider – New Underwear and Socks** : Dating back to their early 1980s history, the Red Hot Chili Peppers were among the first bands to request new underwear (specifically button-fly boxers) and new socks in their rider. This practical demand addressed the lack of laundry opportunities during extensive tours, especially in their formative years. "One of the first things they learned to ask for was new underwear." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z2hvUDtBxho
**Fans of Gang of Four** : Coming out of Hollywood, the Red Hot Chili Peppers were noted as big fans of Gang of Four, indicating a lineage of funk-infused punk. "The Red Hot Chili Peppers, big fans of the Gang of Four, came out of Hollywood." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4
