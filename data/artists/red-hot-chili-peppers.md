## curiosities
**Beneficiary of Alt-Rock Appetite** : Benefited from record labels looking for alt-rock beyond grunge after the "Grunge Gold Rush."
**Chad Smith's Audition (1989)** : Chad Smith, a hard-hitting drummer from Detroit, was the last of 30 people to audition for the gig in 1989. He almost didn't make it, but a "click" happened when he jammed with Flea, and he has been a Chili Pepper ever since.
**Chad Smith's Musical Influences** : Chad Smith grew up listening to the relaxed rhythms of Motown.
**Continued Presence in Alt Rock Post-Grunge** : were among the acts still present in Alt Rock playlists after grunge became dominant.
**Continuing Popularity in 21st Century** : remains popular decades after its emergence, making it harder for new artists to break through.
**Flea as Studio Musician** : Flea has been hired as a studio musician by artists including Alanis Morissette, Mick Jagger, and Johnny Cash.
**Flea's Collaboration on Mike Watt's Album (1995)** : Flea contributed to Mike Watt's solo album "Ball-Hog or Tugboat," which also featured Dave Grohl.
**Flea's Early Musical Background** : Flea grew up in a household where jazz was the main music. His first instrument was the trumpet, and he didn't start playing bass until high school.
**Flea's Real Name** : Flea's real name is Michael Balzary.
**Flea's Unique Bass Style** : Due to his hardcore jazz background and fascination with funk, Flea quickly developed a unique style that has been copied by millions worldwide.
**Hyde Park Concert (June 2004)** : The band performed a concert in Hyde Park in London in June 2004.
**Lollapalooza Festival Performance (1992)** : The Red Hot Chili Peppers were a main stage act at the 1992 Lollapalooza Festival, which featured an "awesome" energy and an arguably best-ever lineup.
**Support for L7** : Along with Nirvana, Pearl Jam, and Rage Against The Machine, they became fans of L7 after playing pro-choice events and offered support to the all-female band.
