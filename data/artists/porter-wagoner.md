# artist - Porter Wagoner

## curiosities
**Career Launchpad for Dolly Parton** : Porter Wagoner's television show is noted for being the platform that launched Dolly Parton's career. He was also a country star known for many country gospel songs, including "Pastor Absent on Vacation," which inspired Norman Greenbaum. **Contract Dispute and $3 Million Lawsuit Against Dolly Parton (1979)** : When Dolly Parton sought to leave their partnership in 1974, Wagoner initially refused to release her. He later sued her for $3 million in 1979, claiming breach of contract and demanding 15% of her net income and record royalties from 1974-1979. He accused her of being "selfish" and believed she owed him for her start in the industry. "Wagner sued Dolly for $3 million in 1979 saying that she broke their contract and he wanted 15% of her net income between June 1974 and June 1979 and he wanted 15% of her record royalties." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 23 - Women of Country Music in the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/023-country-women-72820-2-45-pm
**Disapproval of Dolly Parton's Pop Crossover** : Porter Wagoner publicly expressed his disapproval as Dolly Parton moved increasingly towards pop music in the late 1970s, reflecting a common sentiment among country purists at the time. "Dolly moved more toward pop as the 70s progressed which was again upsetting to many people in country music and Porter Wagner weighed in on it said he did not approve." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 23 - Women of Country Music in the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/023-country-women-72820-2-45-pm
**Dolly Parton's Early Career Mentor** : Dolly Parton made her first appearances on Porter Wagoner's TV show. Wagoner expressed hope that Dolly would not move too far toward pop music, a wish she ultimately did not adhere to. **KRAK Radio Station Staple** : Porter Wagoner was listed as a staple artist on KRAK, a country and western radio station managed by Allan Torbert, and represented the type of performer appreciated by country music purists. **Mentorship and Collaboration with Dolly Parton (1967-1974)** : Porter Wagoner gave Dolly Parton her start in the music industry, and her career was linked with his from 1967. She performed on his TV show and was part of his touring company, recording approximately 12 albums with him. Wagoner reportedly relied heavily on Parton to sell records.
