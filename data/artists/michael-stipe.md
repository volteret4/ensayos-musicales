**Guarded Sexuality and Self-Identification (2011 Revelations)** : For many years, Michael Stipe maintained strict privacy regarding his sexuality, leading to public assumptions that he might be gay. **Nervous Breakdown and HIV/AIDS Anxiety (Leading up to 1985-1987)** : Michael Stipe suffered a "complete nervous breakdown" leading up to 1985, which his bandmates misconstrued as him being "impossible." During this period, he was consumed by terror, believing his past behaviors as a gay man would lead to his death from AIDS/HIV. This paranoia manifested as dread with every cold until he underwent an anonymous HIV test in 1987. This emotional turmoil significantly impacted the recording of R.E.M.'s third album, "Fables of the Reconstruction," contributing to it being the band's least favorite and a commercial disappointment.
