**Stadium Rock Era** : By 1973, Led Zeppelin, alongside bands like The Rolling Stones and The Who, had "graduated" to playing stadiums, becoming multi-millionaires living in mansions. This perceived detachment from the everyday fan was one of the reasons a "reboot" for rock and roll was sought, leading to the rise of punk. "Second, rock had graduated to playing stadiums, Led Zeppelin, the Rolling Stones, the Who." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gkLMwrthZ9s
**Swan Song Records – Vanity Label** : In the 1970s, at the height of their global fame, Led Zeppelin's label, Atlantic, created a special subsidiary called Swan Song specifically for them. This served as an early example of a vanity label, an indie-looking imprint established by a major label for a prominent artist. "When Led Zeppelin ruled the world in the 1970s, their label Atlantic created a special subsidiary for them called Swan Song." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ft8nruhWCas
**Ticketron's Challenges with Major Shows** : Led Zeppelin was cited as an example of a band for whom Ticketron's computerized systems failed to cope with high demand by the late 1980s. The company's inability to efficiently process ticket requests for such popular acts led them to abandon computerized sales and return to selling traditional hard tickets. "It got so bad that for big shows, Rolling Stone, Led Zeppelin II examples, the Ticketron had to become a seller of hard tickets just like the old days." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OCkov6FTliE
