**Vs. (1993)** : Pearl Jam's highly anticipated album, released in stages in October 1993, features an Angora goat on its cover, not a sheep. The photograph was taken by bassist Jeff Ament at Lifeline Farm in Victor, Montana. The goat, described as friendly and curious, was unaware it was destined for slaughter, a metaphor Jeff Ament used to express the band's feeling of being "slaves to the star making machinery of the popular song," which also explains the original working title, "Five Against One," and the final title, "Vs." Other panels of the album feature symbolic images of human feet walking in mud, representing the band. "The goat, by the way, wasn't angry at all. ## songs
**Jeremy (1991) – School Shooting Narratives** : One of Pearl Jam's biggest songs from their *Ten* album, "Jeremy" intricately weaves together two distinct school shooting stories. The first involves a troubled seventh grader named Brian from Eddie Vedder's junior high in San Diego, who brought a sawed-off shotgun to school in the 1970s and shot up a fish tank, an event that deeply affected Vedder. The second, more central narrative, details the life and tragic suicide of 15-year-old Jeremy Dell of Richardson, Texas, who, after a history of psychiatric issues, suicidal attempts, and school disciplinary problems, ultimately shot himself in front of his English class on January 8, 1991. The song was inspired after Eddie Vedder read a newspaper story about Jeremy Dell. "It tells two stories that are intertwined into one and both involve school shootings." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk

## curiosities
**"Vs." Anti-System Release Strategy** : Pearl Jam deliberately released *Vs.* in stages, with the vinyl edition first appearing in the UK on October 11, 1993, followed by North America on October 12. The CD and cassette versions were delayed until October 18 and 19, respectively. This staggered release was a conscious effort by the band to "mess with the system" and "stick it to the label," Epic Records, which they felt was trying to kill off vinyl in favor of more expensive and profitable CDs. In 1993, none of the band members even owned a CD player, and Jeff Ament preferred a cassette version as his primary listening format. Further challenging the label, the vinyl edition was released without the album title or band name printed on it, forcing Epic to add a sticker to the shrink wrap. "Why? Phil Jim just wanted to mess with the system. So they did. In their mind, it was another way to stick it to the label." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sn5TWBFd0XM

**Brian's Junior High Shooting Incident** : One of the inspirations for "Jeremy" came from Eddie Vedder's own junior high experience in San Diego during the 1970s. A troubled seventh-grader named Brian, with whom Vedder had some confrontations, brought a sawed-off shotgun to school, hid it in bushes until lunchtime, and then shot up a 1,000-gallon fish tank. Although no one was injured or died, the event deeply shook Vedder, prompting him to consider what if he had been in the fish tank room at that moment. "No one died, no one was injured. **The Day of Jeremy Dell's Suicide (January 8, 1991)** : On the morning of Tuesday, January 8, 1991, Jeremy Dell woke at 7:10 AM, called a friend, Nancy, and mentioned he was already high from smoking weed, appearing happy. He arrived at Richardson High at 8:20 AM and gave other students a letter to be mailed, stating he wouldn't be around after school. After missing his 9 AM class, he arrived late to his second-period English class at 9:35 AM. When teacher Fay Barnette followed procedure to send him to the principal's office for a late slip, Jeremy instead went to his locker (number 540), retrieved the stolen .357 Magnum, returned to the classroom, placed his hall pass on the teacher's desk, and, turning to her, stated, "Miss, I got what I really went for." He then put the gun in his mouth and pulled the trigger in front of approximately 30 classmates, with the bang heard throughout the school. "He then got out the gun, put it in his mouth, and pulled the trigger. There were about 30 other kids in the room at the time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk

**Wanda Richardson's Public Statement (2018)** : Jeremy Dell's mother, Wanda Richardson, gave her first public interview about her son's death to a local ABC affiliate, WFAA, in 2018. She stated that "That day that he died did not define his life," emphasizing his identity as a son, brother, nephew, cousin, grandson, friend, and talented individual. Initially in shock and disbelief, she later reflected on how she thinks of other mothers and sisters affected by school shootings, wanting to comfort them, although she still isn't sure of the exact "why" behind her son's actions.
