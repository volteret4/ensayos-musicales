## curiosities
**Discovery of Daniel Johnston (Early 1990s)** : After attending a Sonic Youth show in Buffalo, New York, Lou Barlow had a revelation when he heard Daniel Johnston's music playing on a boom box. He immediately recognized it as the kind of music he was already making using a couple of tape decks he had purchased from Radio Shack, solidifying his understanding and appreciation for the lo-fi aesthetic. "Lou Barlow went to a Sonic Youth Show in Buffalo, New York, and after the show, he heard some of Daniel Johnston's music playing on a boom box." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jq-e-fZxXOs
**Sebadoh Lo-Fi Project (Early 1990s)** : While working with Jay Mascus in the band Dinosaurs Jr. in the Boston area, Lou Barlow maintained a separate low-fi project that he eventually named Sebadoh. This project exclusively released music on Shrimper, a cassette-only label, further cementing Barlow's dedication to the raw, unpolished, and accessible nature of cassette-based lo-fi recording and distribution. "When he wasn't working with them, Lou had his own low-fi project he eventually called Cebado, which released music on a cassette-only label called Shrimper." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jq-e-fZxXOs
