## albums
**Maybe It's Me (1997)** : This album, released in 1997, featured the re-recorded version of "Red" and helped push the record to Gold status. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P0pOgz3ZobE
**Treble Charger (1995) – Self-Titled Debut** : Originally released as an indie record in 1995, it led to a major label deal and two re-releases. One re-release was a groundbreaking CD-ROM edition featuring a digital zine with other Canadian rock bands. It earned a Juno nomination for Best Alternative Album. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P0pOgz3ZobE

## songs

## curiosities

**Bill Priddle's Post-Band Career (Post-2006)** : Guitarist Bill Priddle dedicated more time to working with Broken Social Scene after Treble Charger's disbandment. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P0pOgz3ZobE
**CD-ROM Release (1995)** : One re-release of their self-titled 1995 album was a groundbreaking CD-ROM that included a digital zine featuring information about other Canadian rock bands. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P0pOgz3ZobE
**Gregg Nori's Post-Band Career (Post-2006)** : Singer Gregg Nori managed and produced Sum 41, a band he discovered. He also managed other bands, worked with beer companies, and secured TV gigs, among other ventures. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P0pOgz3ZobE
**Morris Palter's Post-Band Career (Post-2006)** : Another former drummer, Morris Palter, earned a degree in contemporary percussion performance and is now sought after by composers worldwide. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P0pOgz3ZobE
**Name Change (Pre-1995)** : The band changed their name to Treble Charger after discovering an American band already held the rights to "NC17" and threatened legal action. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P0pOgz3ZobE
**Original Name (1992)** : The band originally formed in Sault Ste. Marie in 1992 under the name NC17, referencing the American movie rating. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P0pOgz3ZobE
**Trevor McGregor's Post-Band Career (Post-2006)** : Former drummer Trevor McGregor became a composer for TV and film. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P0pOgz3ZobE
