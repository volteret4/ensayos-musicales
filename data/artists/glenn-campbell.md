**Temporary Replacement for Brian Wilson (December 1964 - Five Months)** : Glenn Campbell, an accomplished session guitarist who had contributed to several Beach Boys recordings and had a nascent singing career, swiftly stepped in to replace Brian Wilson on stage for five months following Wilson's mental breakdown in December 1964. Campbell performed most of the group's live shows during this period. "Campbell, who was a session guitarist who had played on a number of The Beach Boys recordings, had had a minor career as a singer at this point, had flown out at almost no notice, and for the next five months he replaced Brian on stage for most of their shows, before the group got a permanent replacement in." ← Episode 117： ＂Don't Worry Baby＂ by the Beach Boys | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hPv7iS8FsbA
**The Glenn Campbell Goodtime Hour (1969-1971)** : Debuting on January 29, 1969, this show replaced the canceled Smothers Brothers Show. While it featured some comedy that hasn't aged well, its music was its distinguishing factor. The show, with Nelson Riddle as music director, showcased Campbell's country-pop blend and hosted a wide array of artists from various genres. It was eventually canceled in 1971 after four seasons, despite its success, surviving the "rural purge" for about a year.
