## albums
**Dookie (1994) - Breakthrough Album** : This album is considered one of the best albums of the 90s and an icon of modern music. It was Grimbey's second or third album, depending on how one counts their previous underground releases and an earlier initial disk. It was co-produced by the band and, for the first time, by an external professional, Rob Cavallo from Reprise Records. Dookie became Grimbey's best-selling album, with over 20 million copies sold, and is their only album to achieve diamond certification. It also received the Grammy for Best Alternative Music Album. ## curiosities
**"Dookie" Album - Original Back Cover Controversy** : The initial back cover of the album "Dookie" featured Ernie from Sesame Street, a character which the band eventually had to remove. Although they were never formally sued by Sesame Street, as is often rumored, there was a high risk of legal action. Given that "Dookie" contained explicit lyrics and themes addressing sex, drugs, and masturbation, having Ernie on the cover could have generated enormous problems for the band and the label. The original cover was withdrawn and replaced with the current one approximately a year and a half later, after two initial editions. **Chaos and Bassist's Injury at Woodstock '94** : During their infamous Woodstock '94 performance, the crowd completely lost control, throwing massive amounts of mud at the band, covering the stage and instruments. The band reciprocated, stopping periodically to throw mud back at the audience, and actively provoked the crowd to invade the stage, causing security to lose all control. The singer further escalated the chaos by dropping his pants and performing without them. After the concert ended, the band remained on stage, continuing to engage in mud fights with spectators and provoking them, which ultimately led to security forcefully intervening. In the ensuing pandemonium, one security guard mistakenly identified Grimbey's bassist as an attendee, assaulted him, and slammed him against part of the stage decor to quell the fight. As a result, the bassist sustained a massive contusion on his arm and lost two teeth. **Expulsion from 1924 Gilman Street Club** : Following their signing with Reprise Records, Grimbey, who had regularly performed at the famous punk club 1924 Gilman Street in California, was expelled from the venue. They were no longer permitted to play there, and numerous graffiti messages against the band appeared on the streets of California, effectively condemning them even before their new album was released. **Recording "Dookie" - Bookmobile Studio** : Despite signing with Reprise Records, a label under Warner Brothers, Grimbey recorded "Dookie" as if they were still a completely underground band. The album was recorded very quickly, in just two weeks, with the band acting as co-producers and having total creative freedom. The budget remained so low that the initial recording of "Dookie" was done in a small bookmobile owned by the father of the drummer, Tre Cool, highlighting the limited economic resources they gained from their major label deal. **Signing with Reprise Records - The "Sell-Out" Controversy** : After gaining significant underground success, Grimbey decided to sign with a more professional label, which led to numerous problems within the punk community. All major record labels in the United States vied to sign them, with Warner Brothers even inviting the then-22-year-old, financially struggling band to Disneyland for a day, expenses paid, to impress them. Ultimately, Grimbey rejected the biggest multinationals but signed with Reprise Records, a significant label that was part of Warner Brothers, leading to them being branded as "sell-outs." **Woodstock '94 Performance and Riot** : Grimbey's performance at Woodstock '94 became one of the most historic moments in 90s music. Invited to the 25th-anniversary modern edition of the festival, they were still emerging and played a brief 30-minute set on a secondary stage. A heavy downpour turned the festival ground into deep mud. The band began playing hysterically, driving the crowd into a frenzy, leading people to throw mud at each other, and some even rolling in it. As the situation escalated into small fights, the crowd began throwing mud at the band. In response, the singer picked up a piece of mud, put it in his mouth, spat it out, and continued singing, which caused the crowd to completely lose control.
