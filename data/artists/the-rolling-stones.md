## songs
**(I Can't Get No) Satisfaction (1965)** : This iconic song, released on May 12, 1965, four weeks after its final session at RCA Studios in Hollywood, featured a distinctive distorted guitar riff that became a cornerstone of rock music. The sound was achieved using a Maestro Fuzz-Tone pedal. While writing and recording, Keith Richards intended the riff to be a placeholder for a horn section, even setting the pedal to mimic a saxophone. Despite his personal dislike for the guitar sound and his preference for real horns, the rest of the band loved it, and Richards was overruled, leading to the guitar part's inclusion and the song's groundbreaking impact. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg

**Keith Richards' Initial Dislike of Fuzz Sound** : When recording "(I Can't Get No) Satisfaction," Keith Richards reportedly "hated" the fuzzy guitar sound produced by the Maestro Fuzz-Tone pedal. As a blues guitarist, he envisioned a horn section playing the riff and intended the distorted guitar to be a temporary element.
