## concerts
- **1981 American Tour - Jovan Musk Sponsorship** : This tour marked an early instance of major corporate sponsorship in rock music, with Jovan Musk, a men's cologne company, paying $3 million for exclusive rights. The Jovan name appeared on over two million concert tickets and was mentioned whenever the tour was, such as "Jovan presents the Rolling Stones 1981 American tour." The sponsorship also included promotional giveaways like tickets for radio contests and merchandise featuring both the band and Jovan logos. The deal was designed to market Jovan as a "hip product" to rock fans aged 16 to 34, successfully boosting both the band's earnings (grossing $50 million) and Jovan's sales, despite initial public criticism as "crass and corporate." "For $3 million, Jovan Musk, a men's cologne and toiletries company bought the exclusive rights to the tour."
