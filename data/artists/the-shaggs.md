# artist - The Shaggs

## members
- Austin Wiggins
- Dot Wiggins
- Wiggins sister

## albums
**Philosophy of the World (1969) – Legendary Cult Record** : Released in 1969, this album by The Shaggs is considered one of the great cult records of the last 50 years. It is described as "weird" and "hard to listen to," often guaranteed to clear a party. Despite its unusual qualities, Kurt Cobain ranked it as number five on his top 50 albums of all time. "The result was a record called Philosophy of the World... That is one of the great cult records from the last 50 years. It is weird, it is hard to listen to, and guaranteed to chase everybody out of a party at the end of the night." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gxbuajGKbko

## songs
**My Pell Footfoot (1969) – Dot Wiggins Composition** : This song is featured on The Shaggs' 1969 album, *Philosophy of the World*. Written by Dot Wiggins, the band's chief songwriter, it is about a "butter cat" and includes distinctive lyrics such as "My Pell's name is footfoot, your way is high, it's too far. My Pell's name is footfoot, I never find him home. I go to his house, not at his door." "Dot Wiggins was the chief songwriter, and her creations had titles like My Pell Footfoot, which was a butter cat, and that little sports car." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gxbuajGKbko

## curiosities
**Cult Status and Musician Endorsements** : Despite or because of their unconventional sound, The Shaggs are frequently name-checked by famous musicians who cite their recordings as favorite garage rock albums. They are paradoxically also described as "quite possibly the worst group to ever walk into a recording studio." "The Shags are often named checked by famous musicians, asked to name their favorite garage rock recordings of all time. They are also paradoxically quite possibly the worst group to ever walk into a recording studio." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gxbuajGKbko
**Father's Managerial Vision** : Their father, Austin Wiggins, served as their manager and producer. Despite being financially poor, he purchased instruments for his daughters and paid for their lessons. He drove them to Massachusetts to record, believing he needed to capture their sound "while they were still at their peak," despite what others might perceive as their lack of musical talent. "They were managed and produced by their father Austin Wiggins, and I'm putting air quotes around managed and produced. Despite being dirt poor, he bought his children instruments and paid for lessons. And then he drove everybody down to Massachusetts in order to get his girls on tape while they were still at their peak." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gxbuajGKbko
**Kurt Cobain's High Regard** : Despite the album's peculiar nature, Kurt Cobain notably ranked *Philosophy of the World* as number five on his personal list of the top 50 albums of all time. "Kurt Cobain ranked this album as number five on his top 50 albums of all time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gxbuajGKbko
**Pioneers of Accidental Lo-Fi** : In 1969, long before lo-fi music became a deliberate genre, The Shaggs, a group of sisters from Fremont, New Hampshire, released what is considered by some to be the definitive lo-fi record of all time. "In 1969, long before people started making music that was deliberately low-fi, there was a group of sisters from Fremont, New Hampshire, who released what could be the definitive low-fi record of all time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gxbuajGKbko
**Rapid Recording Process** : The Shaggs recorded an entire dozen songs in less than a single day. "They recorded a dozen songs and less than a day." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gxbuajGKbko
