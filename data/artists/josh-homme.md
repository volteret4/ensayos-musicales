## curiosities
**Attempt to Revive Gamma Ray** : After his work with Screaming Trees and the first Desert Sessions, Josh Homme considered resurrecting his Gamma Ray project for a new long-term endeavor. However, a German power metal band already held the name and threatened legal action, forcing him to choose a different name. "His first thought was to resurrect gamma ray, but there was a problem. A German power metal band had dibs on that name and started making noises about legal action." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mG1TQuhcf1o
**Desert Session Projects – Produced by Eleven Members** : Josh Homme's Desert Session projects received production assistance from Elaine Johannes and Natasha Schneider of the band Eleven, following their work on Chris Cornell's solo album. This collaboration brought Johannes and Schneider into contact with members from various other prominent bands, including A Perfect Circle, Nine Inch Nails, Danzig, Marilyn Manson's band, and PJ Harvey. "got producing gigs for no doubt in Josh Hommie in the early days of his desert session projects, which brought them into contact with members of a perfect circle, nine-inch nails, Danzig, Wade, Maryland Manson's band, and PJ Harvey." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lip0bZX7hBE
**Early Band Practice Locations** : During his time with Cats and Jammer, Josh Homme recalls practicing in garages in the high desert that were later converted into meth labs at night. "Josh remembers practicing in garages that were later converted into meth labs at night." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mG1TQuhcf1o
**Early Life and Musical Beginnings** : Born into a well-to-do family in the high desert east of Palm Springs, California, where his father worked as a contractor in the Coachella Valley. Josh initially wanted to play drums, but his parents encouraged him to learn a "real instrument," leading him to acquire his first $50 acoustic guitar at age 10. "Josh Ami, who is the only continuous member of Queens of the Stone Age, was a kid from a well to do family. Dad was a contractor who built houses in the Coachella Valley. Josh first wanted to play drums, but Mum and Dad insisted that he learn a real instrument." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mG1TQuhcf1o
**Early Music Lessons** : His initial formal music training involved learning to play polkas on the guitar, a venture that lasted only about two weeks before he discovered the raw energy of hardcore punk. "The first thing Josh learned how to play were polkas. That lasted about two weeks before he discovered hardcore punk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mG1TQuhcf1o
**Early Polka Guitar Lessons** : At the age of nine, when Josh Homme first began learning to play the guitar, he took polka lessons. This unexpected musical foundation precedes his later fame as a guitarist known for his distinct rock style. "When Josh Hommie of Queens of the Stone Age first wanted to learn how to play the guitar at the age of nine, he took polka lessons." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aSk1XQHNkd8
**Guitar Sound Secrecy** : Josh Homme is famously protective of the specifics of how he achieves his guitar sound, carefully guarding his techniques. "And by the way, do not ask Josh about how he gets his guitar sound. He guards it very, very carefully." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mG1TQuhcf1o
**Near-Death Experience** : It is mentioned that Josh Homme once nearly died, a significant event in his history. "Oh, and then there was that time Josh nearly died." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mG1TQuhcf1o
**Post-Kyuss Musical Influences** : Following the breakup of Kyuss, Homme's musical direction was significantly influenced by his discovery of albums like Iggy Pop's "Lust for Life" and "The Idiot," steering him towards a new sound distinct from Kyuss's. "It was around this time that Josh discovered albums like Lust for Life and the Idiot. That was the kind of music he wanted to make now, not whatever Kaya's was doing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mG1TQuhcf1o
**Screaming Trees Tour as Guitarist** : Josh Homme spent two years touring as the rhythm guitarist for the Seattle band Screaming Trees after meeting their singer Mark Lanegan, a Kyuss fan. He was described as a six-foot-five red-haired rhythm guitarist during their Lollapalooza 1996 set. "Mark was a fan of Kaya's and needed someone to tour with the trees and ask Josh if he would like to be his guitarist. So he did that for two years. If you were at Lollapalooza in 1996 and saw the tree set, that six foot five red-haired rhythm guitarist was Josh Hommie." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mG1TQuhcf1o
**Seattle Hiatus and Education** : After his Gamma Ray project, Homme sought a change of scenery from the desert, moving to Seattle in 1996 and 1997. During this period, he took a break from music to study business at the University of Washington. "He needed a change of scenery from the desert. So he moved to Seattle, a very wet place to do nothing. The idea was to take a break from music and go back to school. And he did that for 1996 and 1997. He studied business at the University of Washington." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mG1TQuhcf1o
**Unique Guitar Strumming Style** : Homme's distinctive guitar playing style is attributed to a combination of the straight up and down strumming techniques found in hardcore punk bands like GBH and Discharge, integrated with the strumming required for playing polkas. "You combine that with the strumming required to play polkas, and you suddenly begin to understand why Josh plays guitar the way he does, and why Queens of the Stone Age sounds just a little bit different." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mG1TQuhcf1o
