# artist - Luther Vandross

## albums
**Young Americans (1975) - Vocal Arranger & Backup Singer** : A then-unknown Luther Vandross collaborated on David Bowie's 1975 album 'Young Americans'. He not only sang backup vocals but also arranged the vocals on the title track, leading his chorus to sing a lyrical reference to The Beatles' "A Day in the Life" in the middle of the song. "Among his new collaborators were guitarist Carlos Alamar, saxophonist David Sanborn, and a then unknown singer named Luther Vandros, who both sang backup and arranged the vocals on the song Young Americans." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Starman to Blackstar Edition Part 1 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/696aa42a4796fcbb571adba7

## curiosities
**Early Jingle Career** : Before achieving his big break as a renowned recording artist, Luther Vandross sustained his career and paid his bills by singing numerous jingles. His vocal talents were featured in advertisements for a diverse range of brands, including Coca-Cola, Kentucky Fried Chicken, and Juicy Fruit gum.
