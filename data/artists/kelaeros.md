# artist - Kelaero's

## concerts

## curiosities
**Band's Shared Responsibility in Fire** : In the initial 2009 trial following the Cromañón fire, members of the band Kelaero's did not face jail time. However, in a subsequent trial in 2011, they were ruled to have shared responsibility for the tragedy and received jail sentences of up to 20 years, based on witness testimony that they encouraged the crowd to fire off flares, which started the blaze. "The members of the band, Kelaero's, didn't face any jail time in the first trial in 2009." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pgchw2h87aw
**Catastrophic Safety Failures at Cromañón** : The Cromañón fire disaster was compounded by severe safety deficiencies: four of the six exit doors were locked, two emergency exits were blocked, there were no fire safety systems or alarms, and 10 of the 15 fire extinguishers on site were non-functional. These failures contributed significantly to the tragic outcome of 194 deaths and 1,432 injuries. "Four of the six doors leading out were locked. Two emergency exits were blocked. There were no fire safety systems, including no alarm." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pgchw2h87aw
**Cromañón Fire (December 30, 2004)** : On December 30, 2004, the rock band Kelaero's was performing for 4,000 people at the Cromañón venue in Buenos Aires, which had a legal capacity of only 1,500. Similar to the Station Nightclub fire, pyrotechnics ignited insulating foam on the roof and a blasting net filled with decorations, causing "fire to literally start raining" on the crowd within seconds. "A rock band called Kelaero's was playing for 4,000 people. This was December 30th, 2004." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pgchw2h87aw
