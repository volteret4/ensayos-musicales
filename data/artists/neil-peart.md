## curiosities
**Attempt to Break into UK Music Scene** : At 18 years old in 1970, Neil Peart moved to England with the ambition of entering its vibrant music scene. Although this attempt did not result in a musical breakthrough, he gained valuable experience and learned a lot. By 1972, approximately 18 months later, he returned home to St. Catharines, where he worked at his father's farm equipment dealership while continuing to play in local bands. "Neil moved to England to see if you could get involved in the music scene there. That didn't work out, but he learned a lot." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B0CZ1deEgic
**Diverse Interests** : Beyond his musical contributions, Neil Peart harbored a wide range of intellectual and personal interests. He had a profound love for motorcycles and a passion for big band music. He was also a hockey fan, enjoyed reading books extensively, and was dedicated to studying philosophy. "He loved to read books and study philosophy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B0CZ1deEgic
**Drumming Style Evolution and Lessons** : In 1994, Neil Peart sought further drumming instruction from teacher Freddie Gruber, an experience that significantly refined his technique. He altered his stick-holding method, adopted different movements, and evolved his playing style, often discovering that "less was more." This refinement contributed to his highly musical approach to drumming, which extended beyond mere timekeeping to include a wide array of percussion instruments and fluid movements. "In 1994, he went back to taking lessons from a teacher named Freddie Groober." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B0CZ1deEgic
**Early Drumming Beginnings** : Neil Peart was born in Hamilton on September 12, 1952, and his first home was on the family dairy farm near Hager'sville. He started drumming at an early age, reportedly using chopsticks on his sister's playpen. The family later moved to St. Catharines, where at age 13, Neil began taking formal drum lessons using practice pads. After a year of committed practice, his parents bought him his first proper red drum kit. "Neil apparently liked to drum chopsticks on his sister's playpin." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B0CZ1deEgic
**Early Influences** : While growing up and playing in local bands, Neil Peart was deeply influenced by prominent drummers across different genres. He admired Keith Moon of The Who, Ginger Baker of Cream, John Bonham of Led Zeppelin, and Mitch Mitchell of The Jimi Hendrix Experience. Additionally, he was fascinated by big band drummers such as Gene Krupa and Buddy Rich. "He loved Keith Moon of the Who, ginger baker of cream, John Bonham of Zeppelin, and Mitch Mitchell of Jimmy Hendrix's groove." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B0CZ1deEgic
**Health Struggles and Retirement from Touring** : Neil Peart faced significant health challenges late in his career, including arthritis, carpal tunnel syndrome, chronic tendonitis, shoulder problems, and lower back issues—common ailments for hard-hitting drummers. A severe foot infection made playing Rush's R-42R tour very painful. He initially resisted embarking on this final tour, agreeing only after being persuaded, stating, "one more time and then I'm out." His last show was in Los Angeles on August 1st, 2015, after which he announced his retirement from Rush and from drumming entirely. "Neil hadn't been well, but it was all about arthritis and carpal tunnel, two things that hard-hitting drummers often suffer after years behind the kit." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B0CZ1deEgic
**Joining Rush** : Neil Peart formally became a member of Rush on July 29th, 1974, a date which coincided with Geddy Lee's 21st birthday. He secured the position after auditioning, replacing founding drummer John Rutsey. His first gig with the band was shortly after, on August 14th, 1974, in Pittsburgh, where Rush opened for Uriah Heep and Manfred Mann. "He got the job and formally became a member of Rush on July 29th, 1974, which just happened to be Gettie Lee's 21st birthday." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B0CZ1deEgic
**Personal Tragedies and Temporary Retirement from Rush** : The late 1990s were an exceptionally difficult period for Neil Peart, leading to a temporary retirement from Rush. On August 10, 1997, his 19-year-old daughter, Selina, died in a single-car accident while driving to university. Just five months later, his common-law wife of 23 years, Jackie, passed away from cancer. This double trauma prompted him to temporarily step away from the band to cope, embarking on a motorcycle tour across North America to help process his grief. "An August 10th, 1997, Neil's 19 year old daughter, Selina, died in a single car accident." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B0CZ1deEgic
**Personality and Public Persona** : Despite his powerful and loud drumming style, Neil Peart was known as the "quiet one" within Rush, often described as an introvert and a "reluctant drumming institution." He maintained a very private life, with interviews being rare, and fan meet-and-greets typically handled by Alex Lifeson and Geddy Lee. He was highly articulate, extremely well-read, a deep thinker, and possessed a wicked sense of humor, earning unending respect for his abilities as a drummer, lyricist, and writer. "For someone who played so loud and so hard, Neil was the quiet one." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B0CZ1deEgic
**Post-Trauma Writing Career and Remarriage** : Following his temporary retirement from Rush due to personal tragedies, Neil Peart entered a new phase of his writing career. He produced memoirs, novels, book reviews, essays, and later, blogs, with books being a favorite subject on his website under the heading "Bubba's Book Club." He also became widely known as an avid motorcyclist. This hiatus proved beneficial, as he remarried to Kerry in 2000 and subsequently returned to Rush to record the *Vapor Trails* album in 2001. "The result was a new phase of his writing career, with memoirs, novels, book reviews, essays, and soon after blogs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B0CZ1deEgic
**Private Battle with Brain Cancer** : Sometime in 2016, Neil Peart was diagnosed with glioblastoma, a form of brain cancer that he battled for over three years. His illness was kept entirely private, with family, friends, and management closing ranks to respect his privacy. He passed away quietly on January 7, 2020, at the age of 67. The announcement of his death was delayed until January 10, maintaining a low-key and dignified farewell, true to his introverted nature. "sometime in 2016, Neil was diagnosed as having brain cancer. He battled it for more than three years." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B0CZ1deEgic
**Reflections on Drumming as an Athlete** : In December 2015, Neil Peart authored an article for *Drumhead* magazine titled "Neil Peart Reflects on 50 Years of Hitting Things with Sticks," where he discussed the physically demanding nature of his craft. He acknowledged drumming as largely an "athletic undertaking" and recognized that, like all athletes, there comes a time to step away from the game. He preferred to retire rather than face the decline described in Rush's song "Losing It" from the 1982 *Signals* album. "The reality is that my style of drumming is largely an athletic undertaking." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B0CZ1deEgic
**Rush Audition and Unique Appearance** : In 1974, Neil Peart auditioned for Rush as one of five drummers, replacing founding member John Rutsey who was leaving due to health issues and a musical mismatch. At the audition, Alex Lifeson and Geddy Lee initially found Neil's appearance "goofy" because he had short hair during a time when long hair was fashionable. He also brought an unusual Slingerland double bass drum kit, notable for its unusually small bass drums. "Alex and Gettie thought Neil looked goofy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B0CZ1deEgic
