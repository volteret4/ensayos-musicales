## curiosities
**Co-Writer of "I Want Elvis for Christmas"** : Don Kirshner co-wrote the novelty song "I Want Elvis for Christmas." He would later become one of the most important music publishing executives globally. **Creation of The Archies** : After The Monkees refused to record "Sugar Sugar," Kirshner, determined to make the song a hit, created the fictional cartoon band The Archies who would "not talk back" and "just do what I tell them to do." "So Kirschner said you know what I'm going to do? I'm going to create a fictional band that will not talk back to me. They will just do what I tell them to do. **Creator of Midnight Special** : Don Kirshner was the creator of *The Midnight Special*, a late-night music program that provided viewing pleasure for rock and pop fans in the 1970s. **Perseverance with "Sugar Sugar"** : Kirshner faced initial resistance from radio DJs to play "Sugar Sugar" by The Archies, but his persistence led him to a DJ in Texas who eventually played the song, causing it to become a massive hit. "It did take some convincing by Kirschner to get any radio DJ to play it. It was not that this was a bad song. In fact anyone who has ever heard pop music can hear this is a hit. It was just that it was by the Archies. The way Kirschner tells it he finally got a DJ in Texas to play it one time and he did and then all the calls came in requesting the song and the rest as they say was history." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 30 - The Sweet Sound of 70s Bubblegum Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/030-bubblegum ← don-kirshner
