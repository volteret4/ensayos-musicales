**Sketches of Spain (1960) - Flamenco Fascination** : Released the year after *Kind of Blue*, this album showcases Davis's profound fascination with flamenco. It features interpretations, including a version of the second movement of Joaquín Rodrigo's *Concierto de Aranjuez* and Manuel de Falla's "Canción del fuego fatuo del amor brujo." "Quedó tan fascinado con el género que al año siguiente le viví con disco titulado Esqueches Off Spain, donde hacen una versión del segundo movimiento del concierto a Duranjuev, dejó aquí un rodrigo y la canción del fuego fatua del amor grujo de Manuel de Falla." ← Cómo el Flamenco Influyó a Músicos Extranjeros (Y Quizá No Lo Notaste) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9ReRvRfbHec

## curiosities
**Artistic Consistency and Evolution** : Don Was used Miles Davis as an example of an artist who maintained his core identity while constantly evolving his sound. He noted that whether Miles played with an electric band, on "Kind of Blue," or with Herbie, Wayne, Tony, and Ron, "he always sounded like Miles." This approach of changing textures to keep things interesting for himself was compared to Nora Jones' own artistic development. "You know, if you listen to Miles, whether it's with an electric band or whether it's on kind of blue or in the Herbie Wayne Tony Ron band, you know, he always sounded like Miles." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Introducing Broken Record - Norah Jones Begins Again | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f1264fe6d21276e0cbc
**Deep Fascination with Flamenco** : Miles Davis was so captivated by the flamenco genre that, following the release of *Kind of Blue*, he dedicated an entire album, *Sketches of Spain*, to exploring its influences the very next year. "Quedó tan fascinado con el género que al año siguiente le viví con disco titulado Esqueches Off Spain." ← Cómo el Flamenco Influyó a Músicos Extranjeros (Y Quizá No Lo Notaste) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9ReRvRfbHec
**Drum Machine Experimentation (1970s)** : Miles Davis, similar to Pink Floyd, experimented with pre-programmed rhythm sounds from drum machines during the 1970s, exploring their rhythmic potential despite the limitations of preset programs. "Pre-programmed rhythm sounds could be heard all through the 1970s, everyone from Pink Floyd to Miles Davis experimented with them." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dablAKDOOV0 ← miles-davis ← miles-davis

**Pioneer of Bebop Jazz** : Miles Davis was a significant figure in the bebop movement, a challenging and highly virtuosic form of jazz that emphasized improvisation among musicians. Bebop's complex nature moved jazz in a new direction, away from the more structured, dance-oriented big band sound. "Charlie Parker, Sonny Rollins, Dexter Gordon, Miles Davis, Dizzy Gillespie, Thelonius Monk. Their work helped put the nail in the coffin of the big bands." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V7WjNR1ujhk ← miles-davis
