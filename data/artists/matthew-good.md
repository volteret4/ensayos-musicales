# artist - Matthew Good

## member of

## albums
**Avalanche (2003) - First Solo Album** : Released in March 2003, this album was written a month after the Matthew Good Band's breakup. It marked a new era where Matthew Good had complete creative control, allowing him to arrange instruments and command the overall sonic context without external conflicts. This freedom led to a "balanced" album where "everything wasn't competing against one another." "Finally got to do what I wanted to do." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=--g0gugy3ZE
**Hospital Music (2007) - Cathartic Album** : Released in 2007, this album featured the song `Born Losers`. Its creation was described by Matthew as a "very cathartic experience," following a period of intense personal crisis, including his divorce, a diagnosis of bipolarity, and a period of hospitalization. "obviously the creation of that record as being a very cathartic experience." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=--g0gugy3ZE
**Hospital Music (No Year Specified)** : This album was a direct result of Matthew Good's experiences with his bipolar disorder, his manic episode, and subsequent hospitalization. The creation of the record was a deeply cathartic experience for him, marking a significant personal and creative period in his life. "The result of Matt's experience was an album entitled Hospital Music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=trMzjWWmbZo
**In a Coma (Implied Post-2004) - Greatest Hits Collection** : A comprehensive collection comprising two CDs and a DVD. The DVD is notable for featuring Matthew Good's audio commentary, where he describes the videos as they play. While Matthew would have preferred a greatest hits album to feature more album cuts for fans, he acknowledged that labels typically prioritize singles for such releases, often timing them for Christmas sales as "flash cash." "That's actually a very solid collection. Two CDs and a DVD loaded with stuff." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=--g0gugy3ZE
**Live at Massey Hall (2008) - Live Album** : A live record released in 2008, which includes a recording of the song `Born Losers`. "That recording was from a 2008 live record entitled Live at Massie Hall." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=--g0gugy3ZE
**Vancouver (2009) - City Observation Album** : Released in 2009, this album's title is not a tribute but rather an artistic reflection on the profound changes Matthew Good observed in Vancouver over 16 years of living downtown. He noted the dramatic diminishment of the city's arts community, particularly its grassroots live venues and overall musical "edge." "the reason that I did it was that I lived downtown for a better part of 16 years. And I saw one of what I think... one of the most dramatic changes that I've ever seen a city undergo." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=--g0gugy3ZE
**Vent Coover (2009) - Album** : Released in 2009, this album features the song "Last Parade." "Matthew Good from his 2009 album, Vent Coover, that's called Last Parade." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-s0gwXY_CZQ
**White Light Rock and Roll Review (2004) - Experimental Record** : This album was ready by mid-June 2004, just 15 months after `Avalanche`. Matthew described it as an "experiment," influenced by his extensive listening to Buddy Holly and repeatedly watching *Hail! Hail! Rock 'n' Roll*. The recording process was intentionally primitive, recorded "live off the floor" with all musicians playing simultaneously in one room, a technique Matthew admired for its organic quality. "That record for me was more of an experiment." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=--g0gugy3ZE

**Hospitalization and Enlightenment** : During a manic episode while in Bristol, England, Matthew Good returned home and, in an accidental overdose of sedatives (referred to as "Ativan" or "out of Ann" in the transcript) combined with alcohol, collapsed. He woke up in the hospital, where he willfully committed himself for about a week. This period, facilitated by cooperative hospital staff and a familiar psychiatrist, led to a "mass enlightenment" about his condition, marking it as a "cathartic experience." "I woke up in the hospital and I had to willfully commit myself." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=--g0gugy3ZE

Matthew uses an inhaler but has avoided steroid treatments like prednisone due to their severe side effects. "Sarcoid osus. It's basically a disease that can affect different parts of your body. For me, it affects my lungs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=--g0gugy3ZE
