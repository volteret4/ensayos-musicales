## curiosities
**Midnight Express Theme: Chase (1978)** : Moroder composed "Chase," the instrumental theme for the 1978 movie *Midnight Express*. This track, released on the film's soundtrack, was highly regarded and won the Academy Award for Best Original Score in 1979, further showcasing Moroder's groundbreaking electronic sound. **Production of "I Feel Love" (1977)** : Giorgio Moroder produced Donna Summer's 1977 track "I Feel Love," which became known as the first truly computerized and synthesized dance single. His work on this track incorporated elements that later influenced artists like Daniel Miller of The Normal. "That was the first truly computerized, slashed synthesized dance single, which she created with the help of producer George Eomeroder." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE
**Synth Experimentation and Production** : Giorgio Moroder, an Italian musician, was a pioneer in experimenting with new synthesizers as they emerged. His work, particularly his production of Donna Summer's "I Feel Love" in 1977, was highly influential, shaping the sound of disco with robotic, futuristic elements and inspiring countless electronic and technopop bands. Moroder's innovations drew a direct line to groups like Depeche Mode, OMD, and the Human League, and even inspired David Bowie, serving as a gateway to electronic music and Kraftwerk for many fans. "He had an extraordinarily big hit in 1977 with this song." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM
