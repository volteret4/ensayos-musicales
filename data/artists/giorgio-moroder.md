## curiosities
**"I Feel Love" Production** : He produced Donna Summer's "I Feel Love," creating a sound that was a "completely different way of imagining pop music" through his heavy use of the Moog synthesizer, influencing future electronic music genres. **Dance Music Trailblazer** : Giorgio Moroder is widely recognized as a trailblazer in dance music for his distinctive "very tight productions," characterized by a heavy reliance on the Moog synthesizer. He reportedly drew inspiration from the German electronic music band Kraftwerk. "The producer of I Feel Love, Georgio Marauder, is now widely considered as the trailblazer and dance music for his very tight productions." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 1 - Disco Doesn't Suck (Encore) | https://www.ftr70.com/blog/tag/70s+podcast

**Midnight Express Theme: Chase (1978)** : Moroder composed "Chase," the instrumental theme for the 1978 movie *Midnight Express*. This track, released on the film's soundtrack, was highly regarded and won the Academy Award for Best Original Score in 1979, further showcasing Moroder's groundbreaking electronic sound. **Production of "I Feel Love" (1977)** : Giorgio Moroder produced Donna Summer's 1977 track "I Feel Love," which became known as the first truly computerized and synthesized dance single. His work on this track incorporated elements that later influenced artists like Daniel Miller of The Normal. "That was the first truly computerized, slashed synthesized dance single, which she created with the help of producer George Eomeroder." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE ← giorgio-moroder ← giorgio-moroder
**Synth Experimentation and Production** : Giorgio Moroder, an Italian musician, was a pioneer in experimenting with new synthesizers as they emerged. His work, particularly his production of Donna Summer's "I Feel Love" in 1977, was highly influential, shaping the sound of disco with robotic, futuristic elements and inspiring countless electronic and technopop bands. Moroder's innovations drew a direct line to groups like Depeche Mode, OMD, and the Human League, and even inspired David Bowie, serving as a gateway to electronic music and Kraftwerk for many fans. "He had an extraordinarily big hit in 1977 with this song." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM ← giorgio-moroder
