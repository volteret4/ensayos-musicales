**Formation and Marriage (1967)** : Delaney Bramlett, a guitar player for the `Shindig` TV show house band, met Bonnie O'Farrell at a bowling alley in Los Angeles in 1967. Bonnie, from East St. Louis, Illinois, dropped out of school at 14 to pursue a music career and was the first white singer for Ike & Tina Turner. They had instant chemistry and married seven days after meeting. "Delinean Bramlett was a guitar player for the house band for the TV show Shindig when he met Bonnie O'Farrell at a bowling alley in Los Angeles in 1967." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 59 - Bands and Booze on the Festival Express of 1970 | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Influence on American Roots Rock** : Their collaborative approach to music, borrowing songs and band members, and their eclectic style blending Southern rock, gospel, and soul, were instrumental in pushing rock music back toward American roots traditions. This direction became central to 1970s music through artists like The Allman Brothers, The Band, and Lynyrd Skynyrd. "This song and the entire on tour album is going to help push rock back toward American roots traditions." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 59 - Bands and Booze on the Festival Express of 1970 | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Loose Band Lineup** : Delaney & Bonnie were known for their flexible band lineup, often functioning as a jam band with live jam sessions, where members could include anyone who was around. "And to say band is kind of a loose term because in addition to Delinean and Bonnie Bramlett, the band could have been anyone who was around." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 59 - Bands and Booze on the Festival Express of 1970 | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Stax Records Signing (1968)** : In 1968, Delaney & Bonnie signed with the legendary Stax Records in Memphis, an experience that infused their music with a strong soul influence even after they left the label. "In 1968 Delinean Bonnie signed with the legendary stacks records and Memphis and you can hear that soul influence in their music even after they left stacks." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 59 - Bands and Booze on the Festival Express of 1970 | https://www.ftr70.com/

## albums
**On Tour with Eric Clapton (1970)** : Recorded live in England, this album reached #29 on the Billboard album chart in December 1970. Released in April 1970, two months before the Festival Express, it was a significant hit and prompted Eric Clapton to say in his autobiography that following Delaney & Bonnie on stage with Blind Faith was difficult because he believed they were "miles better" live. "Delinean Bonnie did not have that one big hit but they did have one hit album on tour that was by Delinean Bonnie and friends with Eric Clapton." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 59 - Bands and Booze on the Festival Express of 1970 | https://www.ftr70.com/

**Groupie (1969)** : Written by Bonnie Bramlett with Leon Russell and inspired by Rita Coolidge, this song was the B-side to their single "Coming Home." It later became a huge hit for The Carpenters under the title "Superstar." "In 1969 with electric records they recorded a song that was written by Bonnie with Leon Russell and it was definitely inspired by Rita Coolidge. It was called Groopy." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 59 - Bands and Booze on the Festival Express of 1970 | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Only You Know and I Know (1971)** : Originally written by Dave Mason of Traffic, Delaney & Bonnie released their version as a single in 1971, contributing to the song's collaborative history. This song, and the `On Tour` album from which it emerged, helped push rock music toward American roots traditions, combining country, gospel harmonies, and blues. "Dave Mason had a hit with that song in 1970 and then Delaney and Bonnie released it as a single in 1971." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 59 - Bands and Booze on the Festival Express of 1970 | https://www.ftr70.com/
