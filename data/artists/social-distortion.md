## albums
**Mommy's Little Monster (1983) – Debut Proto Pop Punk** : Released in 1983, this debut album by Social Distortion is recognized as a "proto pop punk" record. It contained musical elements that would later become evident in the sounds of influential bands like Green Day and The Offspring. "In 1983, they released a debut album called Mummy's Little Monster." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tZV1lz-obQE
**White Light, White Heat, White Trash (1996)** : This 1996 album featured alt-rock radio hits like "I Was Wrong" after the band, under savvy management, signed with a major label. "Social distortion and I was wrong from a 1996 album called White Light, White Heat, White and Trash." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0QtMI00T5jw

**Long-running Career and Struggles** : Social Distortion had been actively performing since 1978, often without significant mainstream success. Their leader, Mike Ness, was known for being unpredictable and prone to selling band equipment to fund drug use. "Social distortion had been plugging away without a whole lot of success since 1978. Leader Mike Ness was unpredictable and prone to hawking the band equipment for drugs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0QtMI00T5jw
**Major Label Success with Savvy Management** : After acquiring astute management, Social Distortion stabilized, signed with a major label, and began producing alt-rock radio hits. Their management team was the same as The Offspring's, which allowed Social Distortion to benefit from The Offspring's surge in popularity. "But after finally acquiring some savvy management, social defund their footing, signed to a major label and started putting out alt-rock radio hits like this. By the way, they were managed by the same people as the offspring, so when that group took off, Social D was cut and they're up-trapped, much to their benefit of course." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0QtMI00T5jw
