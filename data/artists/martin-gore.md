**Influenced by Industrial Bands** : As the songwriter for Depeche Mode, Martin Gore privately listened to aggressive industrial acts such as Fad Gadget (known for using power drills), Test Department (known for performances in quarries and car factories), and Einstürzende Neubauten. This exposure led him to incorporate industrial elements into Depeche Mode's music as he gained more control. **Panic Attacks and Seizures (1993)** : Martin Gore experienced severe panic attacks, leading him to believe he was dying, and he really should have been taking it easy. In November 1993, ahead of five nights at the Great Western Forum in Los Angeles, he suffered a grand mal seizure during a business meeting at the Sunset Marquis Hotel. This was his second such attack, attributed to stress, alcohol, and drugs, in addition to numerous minor panic attacks. **Sobriety and Continued Music Career (Late 1990s-Present)** : Martin Gore achieved sobriety midway through the recording of the "Ultra" album, which eased the rest of the recording process, particularly the vocal tracks. He continues to record and tour with Dave Gahan, maintaining a sober lifestyle. **Songwriting Dominance and Personal Challenges (Early 1990s)** : Martin Gore became the sole songwriter for Depeche Mode, which created tension with Alan Wilder. He claimed to be autistic and indulged heavily in partying and drinking, often withdrawing into video games like "Sonic the Hedgehog." Becoming a father for the first time in June 1991 added further stress. "He wanted to contribute more as a songwriter, but that had pretty much become the sole domain of Martin Gore." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jvh8SP2QKXQ ← martin-gore
**Struggles with Alcoholism and Personal Problems (Mid-1990s)** : In the mid-1990s, concurrent with the band's lowest point, Martin Gore faced significant personal challenges, including problems with alcoholism and intense sentimental issues. These struggles put him in a very difficult period of his life and contributed to the overall tension within Depeche Mode. His emotional state, marked by sadness, aggression, and exhaustion from sleeplessness and relationship conflicts, is palpably present in the Ultra album.
