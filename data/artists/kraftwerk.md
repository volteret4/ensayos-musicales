"Side one was built entirely around the joys of driving the band's Volkswagen on the super highways of West Germany." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Dm5N6yDRxXQ
**Autobahn (1974) – Totally Electronic Album** : This album was entirely electronic, and a drastically edited version of its 23-minute title track became a worldwide pop hit. The song itself is a simple ode to the joy of driving through the German countryside in a Volkswagen, with the band members recording the actual car sounds used on the record in their "People's Car." "In 1974, they released a totally electronic album entitled Autobahn, and a drastically edited version of the 23-minute title track became a worldwide pop hit." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**Computer Love (1981)** : This 1981 track by Kraftwerk served as an inspiration for Coldplay's 2005 hit. **Computer World (1981)** : This album was released in 1981, a period when artificial intelligence was still considered science fiction. **The Man-Machine (1978)** : By 1978, when Kraftwerk released "The Model" (from The Man-Machine album), the band had been experimenting with synthesizer technology for approximately five years, leading to best-selling albums and hit songs on international charts. **Trans Europe Express (1977)** : Released in 1977, this album by Kraftwerk is described as massively influential. Over more than 30 years since its release, it has impacted a wide range of genres, including dance music, industrial noise, rap, and hip hop. Its sounds were considered "weird" and "alien," inspiring numerous other artists. **Trans-Europe Express (1977)** : Released in 1977, this album is noted for its influential beats, which were foundational in creating hip hop music. Founding members Ralf Hütter and Florian Schneider, originally classical music students, developed a keen interest in synthesizers and began experimenting with synth and drum machine music, leading to their unique all-electronic sound. "Organization, remember that band we talked about? It became Kraftwerk, which is German for power plant." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ePZDkqLM_gw

**German Identity in Music (1974)** : Hailing from Dusseldorf, Kraftwerk aimed to create a sound that would distinguish Germany musically from the rest of the world. They drew upon Germany's long history of electronic music experimentation, referencing an electronic studio opened in Cologne in 1951. "They were from Dusseldorf in what was then West Germany. And they were part of a generation of musicians who were looking for a sound that would set Germany apart from the rest of the world." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0fQQubt9UTI
**Influence of German Identity** : Growing up in post-World War II Germany, with a complex and "confused sense of identity" exacerbated by the denazification period, young musicians like Kraftwerk aimed to forge a new musical identity. Their goal was to create music not beholden to American or UK influences, leading them to embrace the modern electronic music of their homeland, often abandoning traditional rock instrumentation. "Their goal came to be the creation of a new form of music that wasn't beholden to what was happening in America or the UK." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iL9KpQFqH4o
