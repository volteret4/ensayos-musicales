## albums
**70s Concept Record** : Kraftwerk released a concept record in the 1970s, contributing to the alt rock component of concept albums during that decade. "We went back to the 1970s for records from Bowley and Kraftwerk before we moved on to a breakthrough hardcore album from Husker Doe in the 1980s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KyNY3zv6uc
**Autobahn (1974)** : The title track of this 1974 album consumed the entire first side of the vinyl record, running for 22 minutes and 43 seconds. It exemplified the maximum length a single song could achieve on one side of a vinyl album during that era. "In 1974, Kraftwerk released an album called Autobahn, and the title track took up all of side one, 22 minutes and 43 seconds." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T1XSJIm0Nh4
**Autobahn (1974) – Driving Experience** : Side one of this 1974 album was built entirely around the joys of driving the band's Volkswagen on the super highways of West Germany. This theme was carried through a single 23-minute track that occupied the entire side. "Side one was built entirely around the joys of driving the band's Volkswagen on the super highways of West Germany." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Dm5N6yDRxXQ
**Autobahn (1974) – Totally Electronic Album** : This album was entirely electronic, and a drastically edited version of its 23-minute title track became a worldwide pop hit. The song itself is a simple ode to the joy of driving through the German countryside in a Volkswagen, with the band members recording the actual car sounds used on the record in their "People's Car." "In 1974, they released a totally electronic album entitled Autobahn, and a drastically edited version of the 23-minute title track became a worldwide pop hit." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**Computer World (1981)** : This album was released in 1981, a period when artificial intelligence was still considered science fiction. As one of the most technically minded groups of all time, their work often explored themes related to technology and machines, making this album a notable reference point for the early intersection of computers and music. "That's computer world from 1981. A time when artificial intelligence was nothing but science fiction." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QFtS8fl553Y
**Computer World (1981) – Rise of Computers** : Released in 1981, this record explored the rise of the computer within society, making amazingly prescient observations. Notably, the album came out the same year IBM introduced the very first personal computer. "The record explored the rise of the computer within society, and was amazingly prescient in its observations." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Dm5N6yDRxXQ
**Radio-Activity (1975)** : This album was out around the time Andy McCluskey saw Kraftwerk play live in 1975, an experience that greatly influenced OMD. "It was the radio activity album, but out of barn had just been a hit." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0fQQubt9UTI
**Radio-Activity (1975) – Radio Themes** : Released in the fall of 1975, this album explored the theme of activity on and with the radio. "The next album, Radio Activity from the fall of 1975, was about just that, Activity on and with the radio." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Dm5N6yDRxXQ
**The Man-Machine (1978)** : By 1978, when Kraftwerk released "The Model" (from The Man-Machine album), the band had been experimenting with synthesizer technology for approximately five years, leading to best-selling albums and hit songs on international charts. Their distinctive sound, characterized by a robotic, cold, artificial, emotionally sterile, and intense vibe, fascinated artists like David Bowie and significantly influenced the emergence of Technopop. "At the time, craft work released the model in 1978. They'd been experimenting with synthesizer technology for about five years." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
**The Man-Machine (1978) – Automation and Space** : Released a year after *Trans-Europe Express*, this album explored themes of robots, automation, and space. "And then a year later, it was The Man Machine, which explored robots, automation, and space." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Dm5N6yDRxXQ
**Trans-Europe Express (1977) – Train Travel** : This album, released in the spring of 1977, was inspired by the experience of riding a train across the European continent. "It was followed by Trans-Europe Express in the spring of 1977. That album was inspired by, yes, riding a train across the continent." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Dm5N6yDRxXQ

## songs
**Autobahn (1974)** : The original version of this song was almost 23 minutes long, but its radio edit became a massive global hit. It was distinctive as an all-electronic song, marking a turning point where such music was no longer solely a curiosity, partly due to falling synth prices and the concurrent punk rock explosion. "The original version is almost 23 minutes long, but that radio edit turned into a huge hit all over the world." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0fQQubt9UTI
**Computer Love (1981)** : This track is from Kraftwerk's 1981 concept album *Computer World*. Coldplay later intentionally borrowed its melody line for their song "Talk" from the *X&Y* album, with Kraftwerk's approval. "Kraftwerk and Computer Love from their 1981 concept album Computer World." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Dm5N6yDRxXQ
**The Model (1978)** : Released in 1978, this song was part of Kraftwerk's work that garnered international attention, helping to popularize electronic sounds. Its release followed about five years of the band's experimentation with synthesizer technology, contributing to their reputation and influencing subsequent artists with its distinct, somewhat cold and artificial aesthetic. "This is from 1978. She's a modern and she's looking good. I'd like to take a home that's on the stove." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
**The Model (1978) – Inspiring Electronic Pop** : This song was a major hit single in many parts of the world and served as inspiring material for others to create pop music using machines and robots. "The pioneering all electronic German band Kraftwerk from 1978. That was a major hit single in many corners of the world. It's called the model. Again, really inspiring stuff." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KF5MwJVYng
**Trans-Europe Express (1977)** : A sample from this song was notably used by Afrika Bambaataa in his highly influential 1982 track "Planet Rock." This use of sampling demonstrated Kraftwerk's lasting impact on genres like hip hop and electro. "and note the sample from craft works trans-Europe Express." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0

## curiosities
**All-Electronic German Band** : Described as a pioneering all-electronic German band from 1978. "The pioneering all electronic German band Kraftwerk from 1978." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KF5MwJVYng
**Antithesis of Punk Fashion** : The band adopted a sophisticated look with suits, short hair, and sometimes ties, which stood in stark contrast to the scruffy aesthetic of punk fashion. "Crawford, for example, wore suits, had short hair, sometimes wore ties." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KF5MwJVYng
**Clean Lines as Art Statement** : The band Kraftwerk was celebrated for its minimalist aesthetic and clean lines, which transcended mere fashion to become a profound art statement in itself. This visual approach served as an inspiration for later generations of musicians. "They look at the clean lines of a band like Kraftwerk, which became an art statement in itself." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mhQ36Y-c9fM
**Coldplay's "Talk" Melody Source** : Coldplay intentionally borrowed the melody line for their song "Talk" from the *X&Y* album from Kraftwerk's "Computer Love," and Kraftwerk was agreeable to this. "And yes, Coldplay does know they left the melody line for Talk from the X and Y album. **Consistently Excellent Live Sound (2004, 2014)** : Kraftwerk is recognized for consistently delivering "awesome, great concert sound," with notable performances in 2004 and 2014. Their live audio quality is so refined that it is compared to "listening to an extremely good stereo system," reflecting their meticulous approach to sound. "They've always taken amazing care when it comes to their live sound." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qrrwqdpCjpQ
**Electronic Music Pioneers and Influences** : Kraftwerk was incredibly important and influential in the development of electronic music, with many genres and ideas flowing from their work, including Technopop, Industrial Music, and elements of Hip Hop. They were instrumental in establishing the idea that electronics were as viable as guitars for making rock and roll, and their clean, thematic approach to albums made them worthy of deep study. "Kraftwerk was incredibly important and incredibly influential when it came to the whole idea of electronic music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Dm5N6yDRxXQ
**Founding Fathers of Rap Influence** : Kraftwerk is recognized for their foundational influence on rap music, particularly through Afrika Bambaataa's sampling of their work in "Planet Rock." Their pioneering electronic sounds provided a template for future hip hop and dance genres. "And by extension, we must give respect to craft work." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**Gateway via Moroder** : For those who became interested in electronic music through the work of Giorgio Moroder, it was a relatively short step to discover and appreciate Kraftwerk. Moroder's innovative use of synthesizers served as an important entry point, leading listeners to other pioneering electronic artists like Kraftwerk. "And if you got into electronic music through Moroder, it wasn't that far a hop to craftwork." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM
**German Identity in Music (1974)** : Hailing from Dusseldorf, Kraftwerk aimed to create a sound that would distinguish Germany musically from the rest of the world. They drew upon Germany's long history of electronic music experimentation, referencing an electronic studio opened in Cologne in 1951. "They were from Dusseldorf in what was then West Germany. And they were part of a generation of musicians who were looking for a sound that would set Germany apart from the rest of the world." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0fQQubt9UTI
**Influence on Mogwai** : Mogwai cites Kraftwerk as an influence. "They also like to cop ideas from Fugazi, and Kraftwerk, and Nirvana, and Electronic Offits like the Orb and the Apex Twin." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
**Influential Robotic Sound** : Kraftwerk's music from the late 1970s, exemplified by "The Model," was characterized by a robotic, cold, deliberately artificial, emotionally sterile, and intense vibe. This sound was a significant part of early Technopop and greatly intrigued and influenced artists like David Bowie. "And note the vibe of that song. Much of early technopop was robotic, cold, deliberately artificial, emotionally sterile, and just generally a little intense. That description can also be applied to craft work, the normal and some of Bowie's electronic experiments." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY

**Pioneering German Electronic Music** : In the 1960s, West German artists like Kraftwerk sought to develop a unique musical style that diverged from American and British sounds, leading many to embrace electronic sounds. Kraftwerk emerged as the most influential among them, achieving best-selling albums and international chart success that significantly drew attention to electronic music. "But special props must go to craft work. In the 1960s, Germans, or more correctly West Germans, we're looking for a style of music that they could call their own." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
**Pioneering Synthesizer Use** : Kraftwerk demonstrated how synthesizers could be utilized for purposes beyond experimental music or sound effects for planetarium shows, influencing subsequent electronic music artists. "Meanwhile, groups such as Kraftworth were demonstrating how synthesizer sounds could be used for something other than experimental music and sound effects for planetarium shows." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
**Synthesizer Application to Pop Music** : Kraftwerk demonstrated how synthesizers could be effectively integrated into pop music in meaningful ways. Their influence was significant for musicians inspired by punk but seeking alternatives to guitar-driven sounds, especially by the early 1980s when synthesizers became widespread. "They showed how synthesizers could be applied to pop music in meaningful ways." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**Technically Minded Group** : Kraftwerk is noted as one of the most technically minded groups of all time, with their album "Computer World" (1981) predating the reality of artificial intelligence. The group is still active and potentially experimenting with new technologies in their "sonic lab," continuing their legacy of incorporating advanced tech into their music. "Craftwork, one of the most technically minded groups of all time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QFtS8fl553Y
**Transformative Live Performance (September 11, 1975)** : Andy McCluskey of OMD saw Kraftwerk perform at the Liverpool Empire on this date, an event he credits with transforming his life and inspiring OMD's musical direction.
