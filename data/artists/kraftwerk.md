# artist - Kraftwerk

## genres
- Electronic
- Pop

## albums
**Autobahn (1974)** : This was a "totally electronic album." A drastically edited version of its 23-minute title track became a worldwide pop hit. The sounds on the record were actually recorded by the band members in their Volkswagen, and the song is described as a simple ode to the joys of driving through the German countryside. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0

## songs
**The Model (1978)** : A song released by the group in 1978 that showcased their electronic experimentation and helped establish their sound on international charts. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY

## curiosities
**Influence on Hip-Hop** : Kraftwerk is acknowledged for its significant influence on the development of hip-hop, especially through its "Trans-Europe Express" track which was sampled by Afrika Bambaataa. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**Influence on Pop Music and Synth Adoption** : The influence of Kraftwerk cannot be underestimated; they demonstrated how synthesizers could be applied to pop music in meaningful ways. Groups inspired by punk but bored with guitars took many lessons from Kraftwerk. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**Pioneering German Electronic Music (Early 1970s)** : Kraftwerk was one of the most important German groups that turned to electronics in the early 1970s to create a musical identity distinct from the British and American rock and roll. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**West German Electronic Pioneers** : Kraftwerk was the most important of several West German groups in the 1960s seeking a unique electronic sound. By 1978, they had been experimenting with synthesizer technology for approximately five years, eventually achieving best-selling albums and hit songs globally. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
