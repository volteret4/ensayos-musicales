## songs
**Flying Home (1939)** : Christian's solo on the Benny Goodman Sextet's "Flying Home" was astonishingly advanced for its time, both in technique and sound. His short bursts of single-note guitar lines are considered early rockabilly, anticipating the playing of Scotty Moore 16 years later, and were revolutionary given the guitar's previous role as a rhythm instrument. **Electric Guitar Pioneer** : Charlie Christian was a pioneer of the electric guitar, significantly elevating its prominence as a solo instrument. While he was not the first electric guitarist, nor the first to record it, his innovative playing fundamentally changed the approach to guitar in popular music by allowing it to play loud, single melody lines on par with horns. "Christian is sometimes erroneously called the first electric guitarist, or the first person to play electric guitar on record, or even the inventor of the electric guitar. He was none of those things, but he was a pioneer in the instrument, and the first person to really bring it to prominence as a solo instrument." ← Episode 1： ＂Flying Home＂, Benny Goodman, Charlie Christian, and Lionel Hampton | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RmfeJYl84Qs
**First Guitar Hero** : Charlie Christian, a Black man from Oklahoma, is recognized as the world's first guitar hero for his pioneering use of the electric guitar. He began soloing during jam sessions in 1931, acquired an electric guitar in 1936, and by 1939 with Benny Goodman's big band, was allowed to improvise and solo during performances, laying the foundations for the lead guitarist role. "So by every measure you want to use, Charlie Christian was the world's first guitar hero." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ ← charlie-christian
