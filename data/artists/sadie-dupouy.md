# artist - Sadie DuPouy

## labels

## curiosities
**First Female Record Label Owner** : In 1958, Sadie DuPouy, whose real name was Johnny May Matthews, borrowed $85 to establish the Northern Recording Company. This venture made her, as far as can be determined, the first woman ever to own her own record label, based out of Detroit, marking a significant milestone for women in the industry. "In 1958, she borrowed $85 to form a label called Northern Recording Company. And as far as I can tell, that made her the first woman to ever own her own record label." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
**Mentoring Berry Gordy** : Sadie DuPouy played a crucial role in the early development of Motown Records by teaching Berry Gordy how to negotiate distribution deals for his artists. Her expertise was vital in laying the groundwork for Motown's future success and widespread reach. "And it was she who taught Barry Gordy about how to make distribution deals for artists on his own Motown Records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
