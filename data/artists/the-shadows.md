**Name Change** : The band known as "Cliff Richard and The Drifters" eventually changed its name to "Cliff Richard and The Shadows." This change occurred by Cliff Richard's 19th birthday, at which point none of the original members of The Drifters remained in the group. "By Cliff's 19th birthday, the only thing left of the original drifters was the name, and soon that would change too, as Cliff Richard and the drifters became Cliff Richard and the shadows." ← Episode 70： ＂Move It＂ by Cliff Richard and the Drifters | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uoEWP94f6S4
**Origin of "The Shadows' Walk"** : While performing Cliff Richard's cover of "Willie and the Hand Jive" live in 1959, The Shadows developed a synchronized dance. They started moving in unison to visually enliven the song. This performance was so well-received by audiences that they began applying these dance steps to every song, which ultimately evolved into their famous, or infamous, signature stage movement known as "The Shadows' Walk."
