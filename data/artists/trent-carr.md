## curiosities
**Art School Background** : Attended art school before joining Sean Penn Atenshry as the guitar player, a decision that led him to quit his studies. This background is credited by Hugh Dillon for influencing his unique and less "stereotypical" guitar style. "I was going to art school at the time. I do, I was a slightly different background than you." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=y7kmkWJ54ww
**Childhood Friend and Musical Influence on Tim White** : Carr has known Tim White since childhood and played a pivotal role in White's musical journey. He suggested White buy a bass instead of a guitar, and later provided White's "musical rock and roll education" by exposing him to bands like Led Zeppelin and The Who, fostering White's development as a bassist. "Trent said, no, you should get a bass. I'm like, I'm like, I'm like, okay. Yeah." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=y7kmkWJ54ww
**Distinctive Guitar Style** : Hugh Dillon highlights Carr's guitar playing as non-"stereotypical dumb guitar rock," attributing its artistic quality to his art school background. Dillon finds inspiration in Carr's riffs, which he describes as subconscious musical pieces. "I think because he was an artist, it wasn't stereotypical dumb guitar rock. There was something in it that I recognized." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=y7kmkWJ54ww
**Early Jamming Leading to The Headstones** : As friends with Mark Gibson, Carr began jamming with Randy Kwan at a house on Carlson. Hugh Dillon later joined these sessions, creating an "electric moment" where the musical chemistry felt like a "real band" forming, which eventually became The Headstones. "Mark and Randy and I were jamming and he came in and it was just like, that was cool. Like one of those electric moments." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=y7kmkWJ54ww
