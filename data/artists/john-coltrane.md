**Use of Devil's Triad in Jazz (1950s)** : John Coltrane, a highly influential jazz musician, is mentioned alongside Charlie Parker as a key player who frequently utilized the "devil's tri tone" (flattened fifth) in his jazz compositions during the 1950s. His use of this chord underscores its importance in shaping the sophisticated and often challenging sound of modern jazz.
