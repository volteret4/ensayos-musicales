## curiosities
**"Cop Killer" Controversy** : Their song "Cop Killer" sparked significant controversy, being widely interpreted as an attack on police forces. This ignited a major First Amendment debate in the U.S., which shared many elements with the later Black Lives Matter movement. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**African American Roots of Rock** : Ice-T often emphasized that rock music originated with African American artists like Chuck Berry and Little Richard. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Audience Bridge** : This approach served to introduce hip-hop to white audiences and metal to black audiences. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Band Ethos** : Ice-T described Body Count as "a rock band with a rap mentality," focusing on social and political issues such as racism, police brutality, and inner-city crime. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Controversy from Black Audiences** : Ice-T was accused by some of his black audiences of "selling out" by venturing into rock music. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Controversy from White Audiences** : The presence of an all-black metal band was not always appreciated by white audiences. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Corporate Pressure** : The controversy became so intense that shareholders threatened to withdraw all their money from Warner Brothers Records if the label did not address the issues surrounding Body Count. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Enduring Impact** : Despite the controversies, the band continued and was a significant force in the emergence of nu metal in the mid to late 1990s. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Formation** : Ice-T discussed forming a metal band on the side with his friend, guitarist Ernie C., around 1989. They gathered a group of friends they had gone to high school with in Los Angeles. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Ice-T's Acting Career** : Ice-T is also known for his extensive acting career, including his role on "Law & Order" and dozens of other acting gigs. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Ice-T's Transition** : Formed by Ice-T, who was primarily known as a gangster rapper before this project. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Live Performance Approach** : Typically, their live sets were split, with half devoted to Body Count material and the other half to hip-hop. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Musical Lineage** : A direct line can be drawn from Body Count to bands such as Korn, Slipknot, and Limp Bizkit. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Rock Influences** : Ice-T's personal rock influences included Led Zeppelin, Black Sabbath, and Judas Priest, and he appreciated the speed of thrash metal, with Slayer being a favorite. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
