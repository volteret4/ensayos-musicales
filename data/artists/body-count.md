"And there were provocations like the song Cop Killer, which was taken as an an attack on police forces everywhere. **"Rock Band with a Rap Mentality"** : Ice-T described Body Count as "a rock band with a rap mentality," reflecting their blend of heavy metal sound with lyrical themes typical of hip-hop. "Hey, Ice Tea would say, we're a rock band with a rap mentality." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**"Selling Out" Accusations from Black Audiences** : Ice-T faced accusations of "selling out" from his black gangster rap audience for moving into the rock genre with Body Count. "First of all, Ice Tea's black audiences accused him of selling out by going in a rock direction." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc

"Meanwhile, groups like House of Pain and Cypress Hill and Body Count were heard more and more in alternative dance clubs and on new rock radio stations." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5bQz8R0gbXc
**Bridging Audiences** : Body Count was credited with introducing hip-hop to white audiences and metal to black audiences, expanding the reach of both genres. "This brought hip-hop to white audiences and metal to black ones." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Contribution to Nu-Metal** : Despite controversies, Body Count persevered and played a significant role in the emergence of the nu-metal movement during the mid to late 1990s, influencing bands like KoRn, Slipknot, and Limp Bizkit. "But the band continued, and they were a big part of the move into new metal in the middle in late 90s. So basically, you can draw a line from Body Count, right through corn, slipknot, and limp biscuit." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Ice-T's Metal Band (1989)** : Body Count was formed around 1989 by Ice-T, primarily known as a gangster rapper, and guitarist Ernie C. They gathered high school friends in Los Angeles to create a metal band, inspired by Living Color. "They also inspired this next band, Body Count. This is Ice Tea's band. Around 1989, he got to talking to his buddy, guitarist Ernie C., and they thought it would be really cool if they formed a metal band on the side." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Live Performance Blend** : Typically, during their live performances, half of the set featured Body Count's material, and the other half was dedicated to hip-hop, bridging these genres for diverse audiences. "Typically, when they played, half of the set was devoted to Body Count material, and the other was hip-hop." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
