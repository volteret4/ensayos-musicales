## albums
**Hospice (2009)** : Released in August 2009, this independent concept album is described as quiet and poignant, diverging from large, complex theatrical projects. The narrative unfolds as an analogy for a relationship, involving a terminally ill patient suffering from bone cancer in hospice care. The patient is angry and abusive, yet a male hospice worker becomes attached to her, falls in love despite the abuse, and doesn't seem to care that she is dying. The entire story is detailed in the album's liner notes. The events appear to be drawn from the life of frontman Peter Silberman, though he prefers not to discuss them. "The story is about a relationship that unfolds through an analogy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KyNY3zv6uc ← the-antlers

## songs
**Kettering** : This track from the album *Hospice* is named after the Sloan Kettering Cancer Center in New York. The song is part of a "beautiful but gut-wrenching" concept album centered around a dying cancer patient and her male nurse.
