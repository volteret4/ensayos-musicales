## curiosities
**Band Formation Year** : The Pretenders finally solidified in 1978 after several earlier attempts by Chrissie Hynde to establish a band. "There were several before the pretenders finally took hold of 1978." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oLC49nCf0ZM
**Bridge Between Punk and New Wave** : The Pretenders were considered a crucial bridge, linking the raw energy of old-school punk with the evolving sounds of the New Wave era. "The pretenders were a crucial bridge between old school punk and the era of New Wave." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**Chrissie Hynde's Punk Credentials** : Lead singer Chrissie Hynde, originally from Akron, Ohio, moved to the UK and became deeply involved in the early punk scene, working at Malcolm McLaren's shop "Sex" and playing guitar in bands that morphed into The Clash and The Damned, even considering a marriage of convenience with Sid Vicious. "So yeah, a lot of bonafide punk credentials." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**Chrissy Hynde Witnesses The Ramones (July 4, 1976)** : Chrissy Hynde, who would later form The Pretenders (which was still unformed at the time), was among the British punk figures transformed by The Ramones' influential gig at the Roundhouse in London. "Chrissy Hind of the still unformed pretenders... They were all at these gigs and they were all transformed by what they saw and heard." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=huAvyInzz3Q
**Chrissy Hynde's Advice to Chick Rockers (1984)** : In 1984, Chrissie Hynde published a piece titled "Chrissy Hines Advice to Chick Rockers," which is noted as making for "interesting reading." "In 1984, she published something called Chrissy Hines Advice to Chick Rockers. Google it. It certainly makes for some interesting reading." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oLC49nCf0ZM
**Continued Activity** : Despite being nearly 70 years old, Chrissie Hynde remains active in music. "even though she's nearly 70, she is still going." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oLC49nCf0ZM
**Early Influences** : From childhood, Chrissie Hynde loved the guitar and, due to a scarcity of female role models, drew inspiration from James Brown's guitarist. She also admired artists like Alice Cooper, The Stooges, and Jeff Beck. "She loved the guitar from the time she was a kid. With few female role models, she looked to the guitarist employed by James Brown. She loved Alice Cooper and the stooages and Jeff Beck." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oLC49nCf0ZM
**Fighting Sexism and Becoming a Role Model** : Despite the "tremendous bias against female players" and "awful" sexism prevalent in the 1970s, Chrissie Hynde persevered, using the "Free For All" attitude of punk to prove her guitar-playing abilities were on par with men, eventually becoming a role model herself. "The sexism was awful. But Chrissy fought through it and in the process became a role model herself." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oLC49nCf0ZM
**Move to London and Punk Scene Immersion** : In her early 20s, Hynde moved from Akron, Ohio, to London, where she deeply engaged with the burgeoning early punk scene. She contributed writing to the music magazine The NME and frequently associated with figures from bands like the Sex Pistols and The Clash while trying to form her own band. "When she was in her early 20s, she moved from Akrona, Ohio to London and immersed herself in the early punk scene. She wrote for the Music Magazine, The NME, and she hung out with people like the sex pistols in the clash, while trying to get a band off the ground." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oLC49nCf0ZM
**North American Importation by Seymour Stein** : The Pretenders were among the "unknown English bands" that legendary record executive Seymour Stein, founder of Sire Records, imported into North America, helping them gain exposure and a following. "He's the guy that imported unknown English bands like The Smiths and The Pretenders and The English Beat into North America." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
**Radio Success** : The Pretenders were among the New Wave bands that found success on pop and rock radio, indicating their ability to cross over to mainstream audiences. "While some New Wave bands thrived on pop and rock radio, the pretenders, blondie, the cars, the police, among them, others struggled to find attention outside of their original circle of fans." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**Sire Records Endorsement** : Seymour Stein, head of Sire Records, was drawn to The Pretenders, appreciating their edgy, punky sound, songwriting talent, and the image of a strong American woman (Chrissie Hynde) fronting a band that was 75% English, which he saw as the epitome of New Wave. "To him, it didn't get any more new wave than that." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**Sire Records Signing** : The Pretenders were among the post-punk bands from the UK and Ireland signed by Seymour Stein to Sire Records, indicating his commitment to this emerging sound. Sire Records used the term "New Wave" as a marketing tool for these acts in North America. "he was very, very into this new post-punk music coming out of the UK in Ireland, the pretenders." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
