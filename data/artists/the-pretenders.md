**American New Wave Marketing** : Sire Records used them in their marketing campaign for American New Wave. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
**Attendance at Ramones London Gigs (July 1976)** : Chrissie Hynde, of the then-unformed Pretenders, was among the key figures transformed by seeing The Ramones perform in London on July 4th and 6th, 1976. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qC0Ee4-dpnI
**Bridge Between Eras** : Formed in 1978 with three English musicians, the band was viewed as a crucial bridge between old-school punk and New Wave. They were described as an "edgy, punky band with a gift for songwriting," epitomizing the New Wave sound through Hynde's strong understanding of melody. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**Chrissie Hynde's Punk Roots** : Originally from Akron, Ohio, Hynde moved to the UK and became deeply embedded in the early punk scene, working at Malcolm McLaren's "Sex" shop and writing for British music magazines. She played guitar in early iterations of bands that would become The Clash and The Damned, and even considered a marriage of convenience with Sid Vicious to stay in the country. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**Imported to North America by Seymour Stein** : Seymour Stein was instrumental in importing "unknown English bands" like The Pretenders into North America. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
**MTV Exposure (Early 1980s)** : Mentioned as a New Wave band that was successful due to MTV exposure. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
