## songs

The similarities led to an ambiguous situation regarding a potential lawsuit from Killing Joke against Nirvana. **Eighties (1985)** : Released in 1985, this song's opening guitar bit bore a strong resemblance to Nirvana's "Come As You Are." Jazz Coleman, Killing Joke's principal songwriter, "notice[d] the similarities." Legal action might have been initiated but was either terminated after Kurt Cobain's 1994 suicide or never pursued due to respect for Nirvana. A legal case would also be difficult as guitar riffs cannot be copyrighted. "The band is killing joke and the year is 1985." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rfObVld9rIE

## curiosities
**Collaboration with Dave Grohl** : Despite the past controversy surrounding "Come As You Are," all appears to be forgiven between the bands, as Dave Grohl (formerly of Nirvana) has since performed and recorded with Killing Joke. "Kurt is gone and Dave Grohl has performed and recorded with killing joke." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L09R2evjyMc
**Plagiarism Claim Against Nirvana** : Killing Joke's song "80s" (1985), co-written by Jazz Coleman, was observed to have a guitar riff strikingly similar to Nirvana's "Come As You Are" (1991). The situation regarding a lawsuit remains unclear, with conflicting reports on whether action was taken or dropped after Kurt Cobain's death, or simply considered but not pursued for financial/personal reasons. "Some sources say that there was a lawsuit, but that it was dropped after Kurt Cobain commuted suicide. Others say that even though they considered suing, killing joke decided not to take any action on copyright infringement for personal and financial reasons." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L09R2evjyMc
