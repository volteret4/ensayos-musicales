## songs
**80s (1985)** : This song from 1985, fronted and co-written by Jazz Coleman, features a guitar bit that drew significant comparisons to Nirvana's "Come As You Are" (1991). The similarities led to an ambiguous situation regarding a potential lawsuit from Killing Joke against Nirvana. "The song is from 1985 and it's called 80s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L09R2evjyMc
**Eighties (1985)** : Released in 1985, this song's opening guitar bit bore a strong resemblance to Nirvana's "Come As You Are." Jazz Coleman, Killing Joke's principal songwriter, "notice[d] the similarities." Legal action might have been initiated but was either terminated after Kurt Cobain's 1994 suicide or never pursued due to respect for Nirvana. A legal case would also be difficult as guitar riffs cannot be copyrighted. "The band is killing joke and the year is 1985." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rfObVld9rIE

## curiosities
**12-inch Remix Business** : Killing Joke participated in the 12-inch remix trend, a popular format in the 1980s for bands seeking to expand their presence in dance clubs, following the lead of New Order's "Blue Monday." "And so was Depeche Mode and The Cure and The Human League and Simple Minds and The Cult and Killing Joke and Erasure and Psychedelic Furs and Madness. They all got into the 12-inch business." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**Collaboration with Dave Grohl** : Despite the past controversy surrounding "Come As You Are," all appears to be forgiven between the bands, as Dave Grohl (formerly of Nirvana) has since performed and recorded with Killing Joke. "Kurt is gone and Dave Grohl has performed and recorded with killing joke." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L09R2evjyMc
**Dark Music Contribution** : Killing Joke was identified as a band with a "pretty dark" sound that contributed to the coalescing of the Goth scene. "Johnny Lydon's public image limited was pretty dark, same with Killing Joke." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gd9sO7c3N1s
**Dark Sound Contribution** : Killing Joke was identified as another band with a "pretty dark" sound, aligning them with the post-punk artists that were shaping the emerging goth genre. "same thing with killing joke." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
**Plagiarism Claim Against Nirvana** : Killing Joke's song "80s" (1985), co-written by Jazz Coleman, was observed to have a guitar riff strikingly similar to Nirvana's "Come As You Are" (1991). The situation regarding a lawsuit remains unclear, with conflicting reports on whether action was taken or dropped after Kurt Cobain's death, or simply considered but not pursued for financial/personal reasons. "Some sources say that there was a lawsuit, but that it was dropped after Kurt Cobain commuted suicide. Others say that even though they considered suing, killing joke decided not to take any action on copyright infringement for personal and financial reasons." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L09R2evjyMc
