**Costly and Unhappy Album Release (1990)** : After 12 studio sessions, 10 band members, 7 producers, and over £1 million in studio costs, the band was forced to release their record on October 1st, 1990, despite Mavers' profound dissatisfaction with the outcome. He was quoted saying, "I hate it. There was not one good thing I could find to say about it. It's like a snake with a broken back." "Eventually, after 12 studio sessions, 10 band members, seven producers, and over 1 million pounds in studio costs, he was forced to release the record on October 1st, 1990, even though he still wasn't happy with what had been recorded. In fact, he was quoted as saying, I hate it. There was not one good thing I could find to say about it. It's like a snake with a broken back." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oGscVtrdER0
**Current Life and Income** : As far as is known, Mavers resides in a Liverpool suburb as a family man, stubbornly refusing to speak with most journalists or engage with the music industry. He financially sustains his family through the licensing and repeated coverage of the band's single hit, which earns him a regular six-figure income year after year. "As far as we know, Mavers is living in a Liverpool suburb as a family man, stubbornly unwilling to talk to most every journalist or engage in anything to do with the vile music industry. He is pretty much completely unknowable. So how does he and his family make ends meet? With this one hit Wonder Hit, which has been licensed and covered over and over again through the last three plus decades, burning him a regular six-figure income year after year." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oGscVtrdER0
**Lee Mavers' Disappearance and Rumors** : Following the album's release, Lee Mavers disappeared, leading to various rumors: that he became a painter, went into home decor, became a hopeless heroin addict (though he later recovered), was ceaselessly re-recording old songs, or was living in a monastery where monks praised his musical genius. "And with that, he disappeared. Oh, there were rumors and stories. He'd become a painter, he'd got into home decor, he'd become a hopeless heroin addict. He was re-recording and re-recording songs from that album in a Sisyphean attempt to get them right, that he was living in a monastery, where the resident monks had declared him to be the best thing to happen in music since Mozart." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oGscVtrdER0
