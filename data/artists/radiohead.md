**In Rainbows (2007)** : The album was completed by July 2007, amidst the band's dispute with EMI. Radiohead decided to retain full ownership, including publishing, masters, and copyright. It was secretly announced on October 1, 2007, by guitarist Jonny Greenwood on their website Dead Air Space, stating it would be released in 10 days. The album became available on October 10, 2007, at inrainbows.com, featuring a unique "pay-what-you-want" model where fans determined the price, only paying a mandatory credit card processing fee of 45 pence (about 90 cents USD). The songs were 160 kbps, DRM-free, and distributed via a private network to handle anticipated heavy traffic. While many downloaded it for free, only two out of five visitors paid more than the minimum fee, with an average payment of four pounds (about eight dollars). Despite this, the band netted millions in pure profits, and in December 2007, a premium disc box was released for 40 pounds, including the CD, a bonus CD, digital photos, artwork, and 180-gram vinyl. The album's release was tied to a "Theory of Ten" with various numerical coincidences. It generated approximately £3 million in pure profits and sold 3 million copies across all formats, becoming the biggest-selling vinyl record of 2008. "The new album is finished, and it's coming out in 10 days. We called it In Rainbows." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vt6DVj7S6nk
**In Rainbows (2007) / OK Computer (1997) – The "10-Spiracy"** : Radiohead fans identified an elaborate numerical pattern connecting the two albums over a decade apart. *In Rainbows* has 10 tracks, contains 10 letters in its title, was released on October 10 (the 10th day of the 10th month), was made available on an alleged 10 download servers, was announced on October 1 (10 days before release) via 10 cryptic fan messages each using the letter X (Roman numeral 10). Some fans believe the albums are meant to be listened to together by interleaving tracks — starting with "Airbag" from *OK Computer*, then "15 Step" from *In Rainbows*, alternating, with each song cross-faded by 10 seconds. A listener named Kevin applied the golden mean (1.618/divine proportion) to the total length of *In Rainbows* in seconds, producing a timestamp landing at 2 minutes 49 seconds into "Reckoner" — a major crescendo where Tom's vocals shift "from U to In Rainbows." A mole inside Radiohead reportedly confirmed Thom Yorke was "most amused" watching the theorists at work. "It sent chills down my spine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BrQQHqbQG0Y

**Kid A (2000)** : Radiohead's fourth album, released on October 3, 2000. **OK Computer (1997)** : The album artwork was designed by Stanley Donwood, who has worked with Radiohead for decades. **The King of Limbs (2011)** : This album, the band's eighth, was conceived primarily in a studio setting, contrasting with the road-tested approach of its predecessor, "In Rainbows." Recording spanned almost two years, largely taking place in Los Angeles, with rumors of a temporary studio set up at actress Drew Barrymore's home, which was cryptically acknowledged in the liner notes. The album featured extensive use of samples, loops, ambient sounds, and complex edits, centering its sound around the rhythm section of bassist Colin Greenwood and drummer Phil Selway, and even incorporated turntables with vinyl emulation software. Announced on February 14, 2011, on the band's website, it was made available digitally five days later, with MP3s priced at $9 US and WAV files at $14 US; physical versions were released in May.
