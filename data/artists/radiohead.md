## albums
**A Moon-Shaped Pool (2016)** : Recording for this album began in September 2014, two years after the tragic stage collapse in Toronto. The songs are notably presented in alphabetical order. Its release followed a straightforward, coordinated digital strategy through the band's website, iTunes, and, despite Thom Yorke's earlier reservations, streaming music sites, with a special edition physical version also made available. There were no interviews or advance road-testing of the songs; promotional hints came primarily through the band's website and social media, including commissioned short vignettes on Instagram. A notable controversy occurred on June 17, 2016, the day of the physical release, when a listening session at an indie record store in Istanbul, Turkey, was attacked by a gang who objected to music and beer being present during Ramadan, though fortunately no one was hurt. "Not a lot of razzle d'azza with this one, unless you count the fact that the songs are presented in alphabetical order." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vt6DVj7S6nk

**In Rainbows (2007)** : The album was completed by July 2007, amidst the band's dispute with EMI. Radiohead decided to retain full ownership, including publishing, masters, and copyright. It was secretly announced on October 1, 2007, by guitarist Jonny Greenwood on their website Dead Air Space, stating it would be released in 10 days. The album became available on October 10, 2007, at inrainbows.com, featuring a unique "pay-what-you-want" model where fans determined the price, only paying a mandatory credit card processing fee of 45 pence (about 90 cents USD). The songs were 160 kbps, DRM-free, and distributed via a private network to handle anticipated heavy traffic. While many downloaded it for free, only two out of five visitors paid more than the minimum fee, with an average payment of four pounds (about eight dollars). Despite this, the band netted millions in pure profits, and in December 2007, a premium disc box was released for 40 pounds, including the CD, a bonus CD, digital photos, artwork, and 180-gram vinyl. The album's release was tied to a "Theory of Ten" with various numerical coincidences. It generated approximately £3 million in pure profits and sold 3 million copies across all formats, becoming the biggest-selling vinyl record of 2008. "The new album is finished, and it's coming out in 10 days. We called it In Rainbows." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vt6DVj7S6nk

**The King of Limbs (2011)** : This album, the band's eighth, was conceived primarily in a studio setting, contrasting with the road-tested approach of its predecessor, "In Rainbows." Recording spanned almost two years, largely taking place in Los Angeles, with rumors of a temporary studio set up at actress Drew Barrymore's home, which was cryptically acknowledged in the liner notes. The album featured extensive use of samples, loops, ambient sounds, and complex edits, centering its sound around the rhythm section of bassist Colin Greenwood and drummer Phil Selway, and even incorporated turntables with vinyl emulation software. Announced on February 14, 2011, on the band's website, it was made available digitally five days later, with MP3s priced at $9 US and WAV files at $14 US; physical versions were released in May. At 37 minutes, it was their shortest album. Its release was accompanied by the "Newspaper edition," a collaboration with Stanley Donwood, featuring a newspaper-like insert designed to fade in sunlight as a metaphor for natural decay, and distributed as a free paper called "Universal Sigh" at indie record stores globally on March 28, 2011, containing poetry, lyrics, artwork, and short stories. "At 37 minutes, this was the shortest radiohead album to date." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vt6DVj7S6nk
**The King of Limbs (2011) – Digital and Physical Release Strategy** : Radiohead uniquely released "The King of Limbs" exclusively on their website in 2011. Despite this initial digital-only approach, the album still sold well when physical copies were released two months later, reaching number three on the Billboard album charts. "In 2011, they made their King of Limbs album available exclusively on their website. And even when they released physical copies two months later, it's still sold. And reached as high as number three on the Billboard album charts." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Y6nHw5xx88
