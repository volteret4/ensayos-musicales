**In Rainbows (2007)** : The album was completed by July 2007, amidst the band's dispute with EMI. Radiohead decided to retain full ownership, including publishing, masters, and copyright. It was secretly announced on October 1, 2007, by guitarist Jonny Greenwood on their website Dead Air Space, stating it would be released in 10 days. The album became available on October 10, 2007, at inrainbows.com, featuring a unique "pay-what-you-want" model where fans determined the price, only paying a mandatory credit card processing fee of 45 pence (about 90 cents USD). The songs were 160 kbps, DRM-free, and distributed via a private network to handle anticipated heavy traffic. While many downloaded it for free, only two out of five visitors paid more than the minimum fee, with an average payment of four pounds (about eight dollars). Despite this, the band netted millions in pure profits, and in December 2007, a premium disc box was released for 40 pounds, including the CD, a bonus CD, digital photos, artwork, and 180-gram vinyl. The album's release was tied to a "Theory of Ten" with various numerical coincidences. It generated approximately £3 million in pure profits and sold 3 million copies across all formats, becoming the biggest-selling vinyl record of 2008. **Okay Computer (1997)** : Radiohead's third album, released on July 1, 1997. It became a massively successful and influential album, with its first single "Paranoid Android" reaching number three on the UK singles charts and the album itself hitting number one.
