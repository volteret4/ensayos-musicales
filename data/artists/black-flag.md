## albums
**Damaged (1981)** : This album was a pivotal record for Nate Mendel, who initially struggled to understand hardcore music but experienced a click halfway through listening to "Damaged." He was drawn to its energy, aggression, and "weirdness." "Well, the one that, that, where I understood what hardcore we call it, hardcore at the time was about, was, uh, black flags damaged." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mh-qg1izM98
**Nervous Breakdown (1979)** : This EP was the very first release on the SST label in 1979, which was established by Greg Ginn specifically to distribute material from Black Flag because no other labels would work with them. "The first release on the label was a Black Flag EP entitled Nervous Breakdown in 1979." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8

## songs
**Nervous Breakdown (1978)** : Released in 1978, this song by Black Flag, a band from Southern California, was characterized by hard, fast, and intense music. While not classified as actual skate punk, it is considered a significant part of its ancestry. "That's Black Flag from 1978 with nervous breakdown, not actual skate punk, but definitely part of its ancestry." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**TV Party (1982)** : Released in 1982, with Henry Rollins on vocals, this song is characterized as a "fun dumb song," showcasing a less serious side of the otherwise intense hardcore band. "It's Black Flag from 1982, Henry Rollins on vocals by this time, with a fun dumb song called TV Party." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tZV1lz-obQE
**Wasted (1979)** : This track from the "Nervous Breakdown" EP, released in 1979, succinctly captured the typical feelings of a hardcore kid at the time with its short, sharp, and fast delivery. "From that record, this is Wasted. And it pretty much describes how a typical hardcore kid might have felt back then." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8

**Henry Rollins – Former 7-Eleven Clerk from DC Replaced Co-Founder, Took Band to New Level** : Black Flag was co-founded in 1976 with Keith Morris as singer, but Morris was gone by 1979 (creative differences, cocaine and speed). After brief stints by Ron Reyes and Dez Cadena, the band discovered Henry Rollins — a Washington, D.C. hardcore fan and former 7-Eleven clerk — who had attended their shows. Rollins took Black Flag to an entirely new level and remained their singer until they broke up in summer 1986. "His replacement was a former 7-11 clerk from Washington, D.C., named Henry Rollins." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l0F22NTnQzQ
**Influence on Seattle Scene** : Black Flag's hardcore punk tracks were included on cassettes distributed by Buzz Osborne in Aberdeen, Washington, influencing the emerging Seattle sound. In Seattle, it was not considered strange to be a fan of both Black Flag and Aerosmith simultaneously, leading to a unique hybrid sound. "Hardcore punk mixed easily with hard rock and Seattle. No one considered it strange to be both into black flag and air smith at the same time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Intense Hardcore with Fun Songs** : Black Flag stood out as one of the most intense hardcore bands of the early 1980s. Despite their aggressive reputation, they also demonstrated a capacity for "fun dumb" songs, such as "TV Party." "Black Flag was one of the most intense hardcore bands of the early 80s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tZV1lz-obQE
**Most Important American Indie Act** : Black Flag became one of the most important American indie acts of all time, especially after Henry Rollins from Washington D.C. joined as their vocalist. The band's inability to find an existing record label willing to release their music led their guitarist, Greg Ginn, to transform his electronics company into SST Records to distribute their work. "Flag became one of the most important American index of all time, especially after a kid from Washington D.C. named Henry Rollins joined in his vocalist." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
