## songs
**Before the Next Teardrop Falls** : "Before the Next Teardrop Falls" was a country pop crossover hit for Freddie Fender. This song is used as a benchmark for catchy songwriting, compared to Ben Peters' work on Charlie Pride's "Kiss An Angel Good Morning." "another country pop crossover that Freddie Fender made a hit before the next cheer drop falls." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 53 - More than Charley Pride - African Americans in 70s Country | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Before the Next Teardrop Falls (1967/1974)** : Freddie Fender wrote this song in 1967, and various singers, including Linda Martell, recorded it. His English and Spanish recording in 1974 garnered significant attention from country radio, leading it to number one on both the Billboard Country charts and the Billboard Pop Chart in March 1975. Fender initially didn't want to record it, believing it wasn't "rock and roll enough." "But if he ever breaks your heart, if the tear drop ever starts, I'll be there before the next cheer drop falls." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 25 - The Hispanic Influence on 70s Popular Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-25-the-hispanic-influence-on-70s-popular-music
**Wasted Days and Wasted Nights (1959/1975)** : Freddie Fender originally recorded this rockabilly-influenced song in 1959. After his career stall due to prison, it was re-released in 1975 and became a major crossover hit, reaching number one on the country charts and top 10 on the Billboard Pop Chart in August 1975. Fender considered it his favorite crossover hit. ## curiosities
**"Born Loser Records" Identity** : Freddie Fender expressed a preference for singing what he called "Born Loser Records," as he could identify best with their themes, reflecting his own interesting life experiences. "You know, Freddie Fender said that he liked to sing what he called the Born Loser Records because he could identify best with them." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 25 - The Hispanic Influence on 70s Popular Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-25-the-hispanic-influence-on-70s-popular-music
**Bilingual Recordings** : Following his release from prison, Freddie Fender recorded several bilingual records in an effort to revive his career. This approach ultimately led to his breakthrough success with "Before the Next Teardrop Falls." "He then recorded several bilingual records trying to get his career back on track." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 25 - The Hispanic Influence on 70s Popular Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-25-the-hispanic-influence-on-70s-popular-music
**CMA Awards (1975)** : In 1975, Freddie Fender achieved significant recognition in country music, winning CMA Single of the Year, Male Vocalist of the Year, and Album of the Year, primarily for his work related to "Before the Next Teardrop Falls." **Marijuana Arrest and Prison** : After recording "Wasted Days and Wasted Nights" in 1959, Fender was arrested for marijuana possession, resulting in a three-year prison sentence. Despite being in Angola, he was still permitted to make records, suggesting he wasn't considered a major threat. "It was starting to get some traction when he was arrested for marijuana possession and that got him three years in prison." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 25 - The Hispanic Influence on 70s Popular Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-25-the-hispanic-influence-on-70s-popular-music
**Name Change from Baldemar Huerta** : In 1958, Baldemar Huerta changed his name to Freddie Fender at the suggestion of his manager. The manager believed an "American name" would make it easier for "gringos" to read on jukeboxes. He chose "Fender" from an amplifier and "Freddie" was a spontaneous choice, noting the 'y' made it look good on promotional materials. "Well, I changed it in 1958. I had been recording under my name of Baldemar Wertha with a Hispanic label out of Macaulan, Texas, and I had a manager." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 25 - The Hispanic Influence on 70s Popular Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-25-the-hispanic-influence-on-70s-popular-music
