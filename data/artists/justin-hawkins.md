## curiosities
**Initial Public Reaction and Intent** : Upon the release of The Darkness's debut album *Permission to Land* in 2003, there was an initial public reaction questioning if the band, and Justin Hawkins's vocal style, was a joke or a parody of Freddie Mercury and Queen. However, Hawkins maintained that his approach was, and remains, entirely serious. His use of high-pitched vocals was noteworthy because it had been a long time since such a style was prevalent in Alt Rock. "This guy's making fun of Freddie Mercury or Queen. No, Justin was and remains dead serious." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7_ZmZxnSOnE ← justin-hawkins
**Vocal Technique and Range** : Justin Hawkins is known for his distinctive vocal style, often mistaken by journalists for falsetto. However, Hawkins clarifies that he primarily uses "head voice" for softer high singing and "high belt" for most of his powerful high notes, feeling the tone resonate in his head rather than throat or chest. He possesses an impressive vocal range of over four octaves, capable of reaching a low A (A2) and as high as an E6. "Justin says he doesn't use a falsetto. I quote, I use head voice for softer high singing, but it's mostly high belt." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7_ZmZxnSOnE ← justin-hawkins
