## curiosities
**Audience Resistance to Electronic Music** : Despite their pioneering use of synthesizers, drum machines, and tape backing vocals, The Human League sometimes faced strong resistance from audiences who booed them offstage, demanding "real musicians." "The humanly, with their arsenal of synthesizers and drum machines and tape backing vocals, were sometimes booed offstage by audiences who demanded real musicians." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
**Commercial Peak of Technopop** : The Human League achieved a massive global hit in the winter of 1981-82, a period recognized as the commercial peak for Technopop music. Their success exemplified the genre's accessibility and danceability, bringing electronic sounds to a wide international audience. "There's the human leg with a massive global hit from the winter of 1981-82, which is the period when Technopop really reached its commercial peak." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
**Featured on MTV** : The Human League was one of the New Wave groups frequently showcased on MTV, demonstrating the channel's commitment to playing a wide array of bands within the genre. "They played all the groups that we've talked about in many more. Eurythmics, XTC, Soft Sale, Adamance, Human League, Bowah Wow, and The Go Go's..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**Sheffield New Pop Band** : The Human League, originating from Sheffield, was categorized as a New Pop band, largely due to their new wavy approach, the prominent use of synthesizers, and the impact of music videos on their presentation. "And so was the human leg who came out of Sheffield." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4
