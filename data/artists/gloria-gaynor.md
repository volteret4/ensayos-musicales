# artist - Gloria Gaynor

## genres
- Disco

## songs
**I Will Survive (1978)** : Released in the fall of 1978, this "glorious breakup disco song" became a dance hit and an anthem for female empowerment, also embraced by gay culture. It was originally released as a B-side to "Substitute," a cover of a Righteous Brothers single that "stiffed." DJs discovered "I Will Survive" on the flip side and began playing it in clubs, launching its success. In 2016, it was selected for preservation in the National Recording Registry of the United States. The song has been covered "dozens of times by artists around the world." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw

**Opinion on Cake's Cover (1996)** : Gloria Gaynor "hates" Cake's 1996 cover of "I Will Survive" because it includes a swear word in the first verse that was not present in her original recording. Radio versions of Cake's cover have since been edited to remove this word. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw
**Original B-side Status** : "I Will Survive" was initially released as a B-side in 1978. The intended A-side, a cover of The Righteous Brothers' "Substitute," failed to gain traction, but DJs discovered and began playing "I Will Survive," leading to its immense popularity. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw
