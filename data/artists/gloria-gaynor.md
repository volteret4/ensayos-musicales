## albums
**Never Can Say Goodbye (1975) - Discomix Pioneer** : This Gloria Gaynor album, released in 1975, was the first record to be produced in the "discomix" format. Its producer was directly influenced by the seamless track transitions found on *Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band*. ## songs
**I Will Survive (1978)** : This "glorious breakup disco song" became a dance hit, an anthem for female empowerment, and was widely embraced by gay culture. Despite its eventual fame, it was originally released as a B-side in 1978, with the A-side being a cover of the Righteous Brothers' "Substitute," which failed. DJs, however, discovered "I Will Survive" and began playing it in clubs, launching its success. "What people forget is that I Will Survive was a B side." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw ← gloria-gaynor
**I Will Survive (unknown year) - First Disco Mix Recording** : This track is noted as the first song to be recorded in the disco mix format, a 12-inch Maxi-single with extended instrumental parts designed to facilitate smooth transitions between songs for DJs.
