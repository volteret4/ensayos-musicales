**I Will Survive (1978)** : This "glorious breakup disco song" became a dance hit, an anthem for female empowerment, and was widely embraced by gay culture. Despite its eventual fame, it was originally released as a B-side in 1978, with the A-side being a cover of the Righteous Brothers' "Substitute," which failed. DJs, however, discovered "I Will Survive" and began playing it in clubs, launching its success. "What people forget is that I Will Survive was a B side." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw ← gloria-gaynor

**Never Can Say Goodbye (1974)** : Originally recorded by The Jackson Five in 1971, Gloria Gaynor's version was the very first song to reach number one on Billboard's new Disco Dance Chart in late October 1974. Music journalist Vince Letty's "Disco File" column reported it as a dance club essential for approximately a month. It also peaked at number nine on the Billboard Hot 100. ## curiosities
**Crowned Queen of Disco Texts (1975)** : In February 1975, Gloria Gaynor was officially crowned the "Queen of Disco Texts" by the newly formed National Association of Disco Tech Disc Jockeys at a disco in New York. She was presented with a crown, a 30-pound velvet cape, and a trophy, expressing her delight at the acknowledgment.
