**Renowned Session Musician** : Carol Kaye was one of the most acclaimed bass players and session guitarists of the 1960s, contributing to hundreds of classic records for artists including The Beach Boys, Phil Spector, and Frank Zappa. She was notably the only woman consistently engaged in session work on a rock instrument in Los Angeles during that era. "Ms. Kay was one of the great bass players of the 60s, and also one of the better session guitarists. She played on hundreds of records in the 60s, including many, many classics from The Beach Boys, Spectre, Frank Zapper and others, and she was the only woman getting regular session work in LA on a rock instrument." ← Episode 110： ＂Be My Baby＂ by the Ronettes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4BmEQRvAHAY
**Switch from Guitar to Bass in 1963** : Kaye began working in studios in 1957 as a guitarist but switched to bass in 1963. This was a bold move at a time when bass was not considered "a lady's instrument," yet she embraced the opportunity for gigs, defying gender norms. "She started working in studios in 1957, switching from guitar to bass in 1963. And let's say no woman played bass in 1963. She was just not a lady's instrument, but she didn't care if there were gigs to be had as a session bass player." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE ← carol-kaye
**Unreliable Credits and False Claims** : Carol Kaye is described as an unreliable and dishonest source regarding her session credits. While some memory lapses over five decades are acknowledged (e.g., "Good Vibrations"), she has made numerous unsubstantiated claims, such as playing on almost the entire recorded works of Motown's James Jamerson (claiming secret tape shipments from Detroit to LA, denied by Motown staff) or The Monkees' "I'm a Believer" (recorded in New York by New York musicians). When confronted with falsehoods, she responds with abuse and libel on her website rather than admitting error.
