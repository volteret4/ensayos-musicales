**Early Life and Musical Influences – Childhood to Age 11** : John Frusciante was born in Queens, New York, into a very musical family. His family moved multiple times, including to Tucson, Florida (where his dad was a judge), Santa Monica, and finally Los Angeles after his parents' divorce. His stepfather greatly encouraged his musical interests. His initial passion was for punk rock band The Jirms, which used unusual punk rock tuning. By age 11, he was studying the styles of David Gilmour, Jimi Hendrix, Jimmy Page, Jeff Beck, and Frank Zappa, eventually convincing his parents to let him drop out of high school to study music full time. "He was so into guitar that he convinced his parents that he should drop out of high school and let him study music full time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc
**Early Red Hot Chili Peppers Dynamics and Drug Use (1989-1991)** : Upon joining the Red Hot Chili Peppers, Frusciante initially bonded closely with Anthony Kiedis, but their relationship became problematic as Kiedis sought sobriety while Frusciante, a teenager, embraced a wild lifestyle of drinking and drugs, believing it was part of being a "Chili Pepper." He also felt immense pressure to fill Hillel Slovak's shoes. Despite his desire to get high, Frusciante's unique, melodic, "sinewy, slinky, slippery" style of playing was pivotal in transforming the band's sound, making Anthony more comfortable than he had been with Slovak, and influencing Flea's bass playing to become more melodic. "John was this teenager who wanted to drink and do drugs and have sex 24-7. He thought this was how you were supposed to be a Chili Pepper, wild and wacky, in his words." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc
