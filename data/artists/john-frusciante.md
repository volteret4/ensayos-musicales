# artist - John Frusciante

## genres
- Experimental
- Low-fi

## instruments
- Four-track recorder
- Guitar

## albums
**Niandra LaDes and Usually Just a T-Shirt (1994) – First Solo Album** : This album was Frusciante's debut solo release, characterized by its low-fi, stream-of-consciousness style, recorded with basic gear. He stated that all songs were recorded in a single take. Frusciante admitted recording it while a heroin addict, and it is considered inseparable from his addiction during that period. It sold approximately 15,000 copies. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc
**Smile from the Streets You Hold (1997) – Drug Money Album** : Frusciante admitted this album was released solely to make money for drugs. During its recording, he claimed to be having "verbal communications with the spirits" and stated his greater concern for his "fame in their world" than in this one. The album features his late friend River Phoenix on the song "Higher Down" and includes a bong hit sound on "Four Air." The opening track, "Enter Uh Uh," starkly illustrates the depth of his addiction at the time. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc

## curiosities
**Admiration for Hillel Slovak (Mid-1980s)** : From age 15 (around 1984), Frusciante was a devoted fan of the Red Hot Chili Peppers, attending their shows and learning all their songs, hoping for a chance to speak with guitarist Hillel Slovak. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc
**Brief Stint with Thelonius Monster (1988)** : Prior to joining the Red Hot Chili Peppers, Frusciante had accepted a gig with the L.A. punk band Thelonius Monster and was reportedly "three hours into his membership" when the Chili Peppers called with their offer. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc
**Cocaine Psychosis (Mid-1990s)** : Frusciante experienced what he later identified as cocaine psychosis, brought on by prolonged use of coke and crack. This involved paranoid delusions, such as covering his body with goggles and a ski mask to "wage war against the ghosts" on his roof, and calling Perry Farrell asking how to "get the snakes out of your eyes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc
**Cold Turkey Quitting Heroin (Late 1996)** : After almost five years of constant heroin use, Frusciante quit cold turkey in late 1996, though he continued using crack for a couple more years. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc
**Creative Pursuits During Addiction (Mid-1990s)** : While addicted, Frusciante focused on painting, attempting to write screenplays, and making demos with a four-track recorder. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc
**Declining Frank Zappa's Offer (Age 17)** : At 17, Frusciante was called by Frank Zappa to join his band, but he declined because Zappa did not condone illegal drug use, which Frusciante felt he could not avoid. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc
**Deep Heroin Addiction and Depression (1992-1996)** : Following his first departure from the Chili Peppers, Frusciante fell into a deep depression, became a "full-fledged junkie," and barely left his Hollywood home for three years. He believed heroin was essential for creativity, stating, "I was very sad and I was always happy when I was on drugs. Therefore, I should be on drugs all the time." He remained a heroin addict almost constantly for five years until late 1996. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc
**Dropping Out of High School (Teenage Years)** : Frusciante convinced his parents to let him drop out of high school to study music full time. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc
**Early Guitar Influences (By Age 11)** : By the age of 11, Frusciante was studying the styles of guitarists such as David Gilmour of Pink Floyd, Jimi Hendrix, Jimmy Page, Jeff Beck, and Frank Zappa, in addition to his early love for punk rock. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc
**Early Musical Prodigy (Childhood)** : Born in Queens, New York, Frusciante grew up in a musical family. He moved to Tucson, then Florida, and eventually Santa Monica and Los Angeles with his mother after his parents' divorce. His stepfather encouraged his musical interests. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc
**Entry into Rehab (January 1998)** : A lethal infection growing in his mouth (due to rotted teeth) convinced him to enter rehab in January 1998, as he faced sepsis and death without intervention. This led to a $75,000 procedure for tooth extraction and implants, as well as skin grafts for his abscesses. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc
**Gifted Guitar (1998)** : Upon rejoining the Red Hot Chili Peppers, Frusciante did not own a guitar. Anthony Kiedis took him to a music shop and bought him a '62 Stratocaster. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc
**House Fire (1996)** : His house burned down in 1996, starting in his painting room, likely due to the presence of paint cans and turpentine. He lost all his paintings, including two by poet William Burroughs. While his guitars initially survived, they were later stolen after he escaped to New York. The only musical items saved were his four-track demo tapes. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc
**Painting Exhibition (1998)** : Following his rehab and recovery, Frusciante displayed some of his paintings at a gallery event, which is where Anthony Kiedis reconnected with him. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc
**Post-RHCP Audition for Meat Puppets (1992)** : After quitting the Red Hot Chili Peppers in 1992, Frusciante auditioned for the band Meat Puppets in Phoenix, but it did not work out. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc
**Severe Physical Decline (1996-1998)** : By 1996, Frusciante weighed only 88 pounds and was described as "a skeleton covered in skin." His arms were severely scarred from self-harm and needle marks, he suffered from abscesses, his teeth had rotted out, and he contracted a near-fatal blood infection, even experiencing clinical death for a few minutes. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc
**Struggles with Hillel Slovak's Legacy (Early Stint with RHCP)** : Frusciante felt immense pressure to step into the shoes of the deceased Hillel Slovak, which contributed to his later problems. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc
**Uncashed Royalty Checks (Mid-1990s)** : During his addiction, Frusciante neglected his finances, leading to uncashed royalty checks, including one for $600,000 found by Flea's daughter. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc
