## curiosities
**Alleged Theft of Master Tapes** : The finished album from Ian McCulloch's collaboration with Johnny Marr and Will Sargent was never released, with legend stating that the master tapes were stolen from a courier truck in a hold-up, and no backups existed. This led to the album being lost, although its authenticity and the theft story itself have been questioned. "According to legend, the master tapes were sent to the record company after everything had been completed. But somewhere along the line, they were stolen from the career truck in a hold-up. There were no backups, and the album was lost." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gdWLtMy4TBU
**Collaboration with Johnny Marr (1993)** : In 1993, while estranged from Echo and the Bunnyman, Ian McCulloch collaborated with ex-Smiths guitarist Johnny Marr, writing at least an album's worth of material. This project generated significant excitement in the British music press, especially when Echo guitarist Will Sargent joined them. "In 1993, Ian McCulloch was still estranged from Echo and the Bunnyman, and he was looking to get back into the game. At the same time, ex-Smith's guitarist, Johnny Marr, wanted to do something new. They wrote at least an album's worth of material together, and the British music press went nuts, speculating over the possibilities." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gdWLtMy4TBU
**Theories Behind Unreleased Album** : Two main theories exist regarding the unreleased Ian McCulloch/Johnny Marr album. One suggests the theft story was fabricated by Ian to facilitate his return to Echo and the Bunnyman without interference from the collaboration. Another theory proposes the music was of poor quality due to frequent arguments between Ian and Johnny, indicating the album might never have been truly completed. "The theory is that Ian and Johnny made up the story because Ian wanted to get back with the Bunnieman, and this project would have ruined everything. Another theory is that the music was crap because Ian and Johnny were arguing so much that an album never really was completed." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gdWLtMy4TBU
