# artist - JFA (Jody Foster's Army)

## albums
**Blatant Localism (1981)** : This EP, released in 1981, contained six songs and had a total running time of seven minutes and nine seconds. It was notable for being loaded with skateboarding references, including its cover artwork, and is considered probably the first obvious record directly connected to skateboarding. "In fact, this was probably the first obvious record of skateboarding." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ

## songs
**Skateboard Themes** : Many of JFA's songs featured themes directly related to skateboarding, reflecting the band's origins and connection to the culture. "So many of their songs had skateboard themes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ

## curiosities
**Signature Skateboards** : At least three custom JFA signature skateboards were produced, further cementing their iconic status within the skateboarding community. "And there are at least three custom JFA signature skateboards." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Skateboard Compilation Presence** : JFA appeared on nearly every skateboard-related punk rock compilation throughout the 1980s. These compilations bore titles such as "Blazing Wheels and Marking Trucks," "Born to Skate," "Concrete Waves," and "Explicit Skate Rock." "JFA appeared on just about every skateboard related punk rock compilation in the 1980s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Skateboard Film Soundtracks** : At least a dozen soundtracks for skateboard films featured material from JFA. "I know of at least a dozen soundtracks to skateboard films that feature JFA material." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Tony Hawk's Recognition** : Pro-skater Tony Hawk was familiar with JFA, leading to the inclusion of their music in his popular skateboard video games. "Tony Hawk knew all about JFA, which is why you'll find JFA music in his skateboard video games." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
