## albums
**The Eraser (2006) - Kid A Outtakes Album** : This solo album by Tom Yorke is composed almost entirely, approximately 90%, of material derived directly from the discarded recordings and creative surplus generated during Radiohead's "Kid A" sessions. It serves as a testament to the immense volume of quality content produced during that period, much of which was later repurposed. ## curiosities
**Bear Logo Acceptance and Evolution** : Tom Yorke confirmed in an interview that he initially admired the macabre image of the bear and used it exclusively for "Kid A" (QD) advertising. The bear's widespread acceptance by the public ultimately led to its adoption as one of Radiohead's standard logos, despite not being initially conceived as such. "Tom Yorke hacía referencia de que le había gustado un montón, esa imagen macabra de losos que la usó solo como publicidad para el QD. Pero luego le acabó gustando un montón, tuvo un montón de aceptación y que finalmente se quedó como uno de los logán se está en der de la banda." ← Music Radar Clan > El logo de Radiohead. **Cyclical Creative Process** : Tom Yorke is characterized by a distinctive creative pattern, marked by alternating phases of intense creative explosion and profound creative crisis or depression. The "Kid A" sessions, for example, represent the zenith of his creative output during such an explosive phase. **Dr. Shock Alias** : Tom Yorke used the pseudonym Dr. Shock for artistic expression projects that were not musical in nature. **Interest in Electronic Music and Technology** : Tom Yorke developed a keen interest in electronics, exploring various new music systems. He learned from other electronic artists and became proficient in using sampling machines and recording canned sounds. These new interests and acquired skills became fundamental to the future direction and sound of Radiohead. "Él se ha interesado mucho por la electrónica, conocía muchos otras artistas electrónicos que le habían enseñado muchos sistemas de música nuevos, él aprendió a utilizar muchísimas máquinas desampleados, agravar sonidos en latados." ← Music Radar Clan > OK Computer vs  Kid A： ¿Qué disco fue más importante？ | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=idvxszNeeqo&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**NME Interview on Kid A Material** : In an interview with NME, Tom Yorke expressed the overwhelming volume of material generated during the "Kid A" sessions. He famously stated that he wished he could have released ten albums from the content produced during that period, indicating the difficulty he faced in selecting what to include. **Pre-Kid A Creative Explosion Assessment** : Tom Yorke characterized the period immediately preceding the release of "Kid A" as the band's most significant explosion of artistic creativity. During this time, the band produced a wealth of music alongside numerous other projects, such as posters, short films, videos, animations, and comics.
