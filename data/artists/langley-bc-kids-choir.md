# artist - Langley B.C. Kids Choir

## members
- 60-member children's choir (grades 4-7)
- Hans Finger (music teacher/guitarist)

## concerts
- Glenwood School recording (1975)
- Wicksbrown Elementary recording (1977)

## songs
**Space Oddity (David Bowie Cover)** : The children's choir from Langley, British Columbia, recorded an "innocent, guileless version" of David Bowie's "Space Oddity" in 1975. This rendition was later highly praised by David Bowie himself, who declared it one of the best interpretations he had ever heard of his song. "The kids of Langley, British Columbia, with their innocent, dialless version of David Bowie's face-audity." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8

## curiosities
**David Bowie's Praise** : David Bowie openly stated that the Langley kids' version of "Space Oddity" was one of the best interpretations of his songs he had ever heard. This endorsement highlighted the unique charm and sincerity with which the choir performed, despite any technical shortcomings. "And David Bowie went on record as saying that this is one of the best interpretations that he has ever heard of one of his songs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
**Origin of the Project** : In the 1970s, it became popular for school choirs to perform rock songs. Hans Finger, a music teacher and guitarist in Langley, B.C., led a 60-member children's choir drawn from three rural schools (Glenwood, Lockheel, South Calvav Elementary), teaching them his favorite songs as they had limited prior exposure to rock culture. "A music teacher and sometimes guitarist and Langley B.C. named Hans Finger was in charge of a 60-member children's choir." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
**Recording Process and Initial Release (1975)** : At the end of 1975, Finger recorded the choir's performances in the Glenwood gym using two microphones and a tape machine. They recorded nine songs, each captured in a single take. 300 vinyl copies were pressed and sold to the children, their parents, and faculty, initially as a local keepsake. "At the end of 1975, he recorded the results in the gym and Glenwood using two microphones in a tape machine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
**Recording Process and Initial Release (1977)** : In 1977, Hans Finger repeated the recording project at Wicksbrown Elementary in Langley with another 90 children. Although these subsequent recordings disappeared for decades, they eventually resurfaced on CD, contributing to their broader rediscovery and appreciation. "In 1977, Finger did it again at Wicksbrown Elementary and Langley with another 90 kids." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
