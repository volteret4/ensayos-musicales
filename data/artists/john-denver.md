## concerts
- **The Midnight Special Pilot (1972)** : John Denver hosted the 90-minute pilot of *The Midnight Special* in August 1972, which focused on encouraging young people to vote. During the show, he discussed voter registration with Cass Elliott and performed "Leaving on a Jet Plane" with her. "The host was John Denver who was writing this wave of success from Take Me Home Country Roads and was about to have even more success with Rocky Mountain High."

## albums
**Back Home Again (1974)** : This album was released in June 1974. Its title track won CMA Song of the Year in 1975, and two other cuts from the album, "Annie's Song" and "Thank God I'm a Country Boy," became two of Denver's biggest hits. **Popular Favorites** : John Denver is mentioned as a "popular favorite" among singer-songwriters sampled by critic Robert Hillburn in 1973. **Songwriting Credits** : John Denver is noted for writing well-known songs such as "Rocky Mountain High" and "Take Me Home Country Road," in addition to "Leaving on a Jet Plane." **Successful Plagiarism Lawsuit Against New Order (1990)** : In 1990, John Denver successfully sued New Order for plagiarism, winning the case against them for similarities between his song "Leaving on a Jet Plane" (a 1969 number one hit for Peter, Paul and Mary) and New Order's 1990 song "Run." "In 1990, John Denver, this cute little folk singer who looked like a muffet, nailed New Order with a plagiarism suit and won." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wYD_dIvYY5o ← john-denver
