## albums
**Enter the Wu-Tang (36 Chambers) (1993)** : This was the debut album of the group, which emerged in 1993 from Staten Island, New York. Its production, handled without significant resources or budget, resulted in a characteristic raw, underground sound, akin to an "underground demo tape from a cassette" rather than a super-production. The album was a musical revolution, particularly in its distinct sampling technique, utilizing deconstructed and broken samples from 40s, 50s, and 60s soul, funk, jazz, and classic soul, setting a new standard for hip hop sampling that influenced later artists like J.C. and Kanye West. It established the group's unique lyrical style, where each of the eight members brought their own distinct attitude, from Ghostface Killah's "swaggering attitude" to Inspectah Deck's "reflective and quiet parts," using metaphors inspired by martial arts, Shaolin, chess, and warfare. The group had to resort to hustling drugs to finance the recording of this first album, as they received no money from RCA despite signing a landmark contract. "El disco se hizo, siempre he supuesto sin medios, sin recursos, sin ayuda y de hecho es lo que dio ese sonido tan característico de su mejor butanclan que trae todo ese sonido del Andernal New York inodil Hip Hop y lo trae a un disco de verdad." ← Music Radar Clan > Wu Tang Clan. **Wu-Tang Forever (1997)** : Released four or five years after their debut album, this album is considered by some to be even better than the first, and is recognized as one of the best albums in hip hop history and music overall. It was one of the best-selling hip hop albums in history and received critical acclaim as a masterpiece, earning some of the best ratings in rap history despite immense public expectation. Unlike their debut, this was a super-production with abundant resources, yet it successfully maintained the characteristic raw, underground essence of the Wu Tang Clan. While RZA continued to control the production, the album showcased a more cohesive and uniform group effort, with all members contributing significantly. The lyrics are considered the best the group ever wrote, deeply influenced by important Black liberation movements in the United States, such as the Black Panthers and FIPS, resulting in profound, committed, and explicit yet incredibly well-crafted verses. ## curiosities
**Early Funding through Drug Dealing** : During their initial period, the Wu Tang Clan financed their first album by dealing drugs, which is one of the many legends surrounding the group's origins. This contrasts with the often caricatured "gangsta rap" image of other artists who, despite rapping about street life, were already wealthy and operating from a position of privilege. "Y tenemos también historias de cómo pues drapicheando con drogas, conseguiron pagarse el primer disco." ← Music Radar Clan > Wu Tang Clan. **Formation and Early Struggles - Staten Island** : The group originated in the late 1980s and early 1990s in Staten Island, New York, a somewhat remote and socio-economically disadvantaged area where its members resided. All members were already involved in the New York underground hip hop scene, having individually produced some works but struggling to achieve prominence against established acts like Public Enemy, N.W.A., Ice Cube, and Dr. Dre. The idea for the group came from RZA and GZA, who decided to unite the eight members to create a unique project distinct from the prevailing market trends. "nos tenemos que ir a final de los 80 primeros de los 90 a Stater Island. Esa isla que está en Nueva York con poco alejada que también es una de las zonas bastante malas de Nueva York, donde vivían los miembros del Wu Tang Clan." ← Music Radar Clan > Wu Tang Clan. **Metaphorical Lyrical Approach** : The group's lyrics were highly innovative for 1990s hip hop, which was typically direct and explicit. **RZA's Five-Year Economic and Business Plan** : Between their first and second albums, RZA orchestrated what is known in hip hop history as his "five-year plan." As the group's leader, he meticulously developed an economic and business strategy for the Wu Tang Clan. The plan involved releasing the group's debut album, followed by successful solo albums from each of the eight members, before reconvening for another Wu Tang Clan album. This strategy led to the release of critically acclaimed solo works from members like RZA (under the name Gaysjar, as stated in transcript), Method Man, and Old Dirty Bastard (with *Return to the 36 Chambers*), allowing the Wu Tang Clan to dominate the mid-90s hip hop market through both group and individual projects and collaborations. "reisar que era el líder, no solo creativo sino que era el líder casi empresarial del Guttankland estableció todo un plan para hacer un desarrollo económico empresarial de lo que sería el Guttankland." ← Music Radar Clan > Wu Tang Clan. **RZA's Innovative Record Deal Strategy** : RZA, as the creative and business leader of the Wu Tang Clan, devised a highly intelligent and specific record contract strategy in the 1990s. Recognizing that generic contracts often trapped individual members after a band's breakup, RZA insisted on a deal that bound the Wu Tang Clan as a group but left its members free to pursue independent solo careers and sign separate record deals. This concept, common today, was revolutionary and difficult to secure in the 90s, but RZA ultimately achieved it with RCA. "Entonces Reisa quería hacer un contrato discográfico que afectase al butanclan como conjunto, pero no a sus miembros como entidades individuales." ← Music Radar Clan > Wu Tang Clan. **Revolutionary Sampling Techniques** : The Wu Tang Clan revolutionized hip hop sampling with their debut album. Lacking a budget, they sourced samples from classic soul, funk, and jazz records of the 1940s, 50s, and 60s. RZA would take fragments, deconstruct and "break" them to make them unidentifiable, creating slow, sophisticated, and distinct rhythmic bases that radically differed from the fast, agile beats prevalent in 80s hip hop. This approach profoundly influenced subsequent artists like J.C. and Kanye West. "Y generó una manera de hacer bases muy distintas, radicalmente distinta a lo que se estaba haciendo." ← Music Radar Clan > Wu Tang Clan. **Unique Lyrical Perspective** : The Wu Tang Clan offered a more realistic portrayal of "gangsta rap" in their lyrics, focusing on drug dealing, insecurity, and police brutality from the perspective of the citizen who suffers it, rather than from the detached, super-produced viewpoint sometimes presented by groups like Public Enemy. Each of the eight members had a unique lyrical style, contributing to a rich and varied sound that could be sarcastic, ironic, or even humorous. "Los Wu Tang Clan en sus letras no sabían del rapicheo, no sabían de la inseguridad, no sabían de la brota de las policial, pero desde el punto de vista del ciudadano que lo sufre." ← Music Radar Clan > Wu Tang Clan.
