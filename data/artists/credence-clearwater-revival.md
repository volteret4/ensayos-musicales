# artist - Credence Clearwater Revival

## members
- John Fogarty (founder, lead singer, chief songwriter)

## songs
**Fortunate Son (1969) – Anti-Vietnam War Anthem** : Released in the fall of 1969, this powerful song served as an anti-Vietnam War anthem. It specifically targets the "fortunate sons," who were the offspring of the rich and powerful, capable of escaping the draft for military service. The song's core message highlighted the hypocrisy of supporting a war without having to bear its personal costs. "The message was, hey, it's easy to support the war if you never have to pay any of the costs for it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0ARYt4sYT90
**Run Through the Jungle (Year Unspecified)** : This CCR song, the copyright of which was owned by Saul Zaentz (Fantasy Records), was central to a plagiarism lawsuit against John Fogarty. Zaentz alleged that Fogarty's solo song "Old Man Down the Road" sounded too similar. "alleging that a song on Center Field called Old Man Down the Road, which John owned, sounded just like an old CCR song called Run Through the Jungle, which Saul owned as the result of the contract dispute." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wYD_dIvYY5o

## curiosities
**Copyright Ownership Dispute** : The copyrights to songs written by John Fogarty for Credence Clearwater Revival were transferred to Saul Zaentz, who ran the band's record label, Fantasy Records, as part of a contract dispute settlement. This led to a bizarre lawsuit where Fogarty was sued for plagiarizing himself. "Although John had written the songs for Credence, the copyrights, the ownership of the songs he had written, had been given over to a guy named Saul Zance." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wYD_dIvYY5o
