## albums
**King Cole Trio (1945)** : This album, issued by Capitol Records in 1945, was an "old school" album containing four 78 RPM records tucked into sleeves. It was one of the biggest early successes demonstrating the value of album artwork, spending most of its year at number one on the charts. Its commercial performance convinced the industry of the power of decorated packaging. "One of the biggest early successes was the King Cole trio. It was an album from that King Cole, issued by Capital Records in 1945. It was an old school album with 478 RPM records tucked into the sleeves. ## curiosities
**Pioneering West Coast Smooth Blues** : In the late 1940s and early 1950s, the Nat King Cole Trio was one of the biggest groups in the R&B field, popularizing a smooth blues style that incorporated elements of crooning alongside blues and jazz. Their style was highly influential on the burgeoning rock music field, with groups like the Chuck Berry Trio attempting to emulate it. "Two of the biggest groups in the Iron B field were the Nat King Colds' Rio and Johnny Moore's three blazers featuring Charles Brown." ← Episode 32： ＂I Got A Woman＂ by Ray Charles | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0oeI9f2x_fo
**Pre-Pop Ballad Influence** : Before Nat King Cole achieved massive pop success with ballads like "Unforgettable," the music he made with his trio, while perhaps not as commercially successful, was incredibly influential on the developing rock music scene. "But it's still hard for modern listeners to remember that before his massive pop success with ballads like unforgettable, Cold was making music, which may not have been quite as successful commercially, but which was incredibly influential on the burgeoning rock music field." ← Episode 32： ＂I Got A Woman＂ by Ray Charles | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0oeI9f2x_fo
