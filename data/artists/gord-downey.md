# artist - Gord Downey

## members
- Gord Downey (vocals, drums)

## genres
- Conceptual
- Heavier
- Low-fi
- Quirky
- Spoken Word

## instruments
- Drums (on "Pasco Submarine")

## albums
**Introduce Yourself (2017)** : A surprise double album released on October 27, 2017. Its recording sessions were completed in February 2017. The album features 23 songs, each dedicated to a specific person, including "The Bruins Kept Us Tight." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Koch Machine Glow (2001)** : His debut solo record, characterized as quirky, with spoken word elements and a low-fi recording style, drawing comparisons to Tom Waits. It reached number 26 on the Canadian charts. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Secret Path (2016)** : Released on October 18, 2016, this album was part of a larger multimedia project that included a film and a graphic novel, all dedicated to telling the story of Chanie Wenjack. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**The Battle of the Nudes (2003)** : His second solo album, which featured a less conceptual and heavier musical approach than his first. It includes the fan-favorite song "Pasco Submarine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**The Grand Bounce (2010)** : His third solo album, produced by Chris Walla of Death Cab for Cutie. The album features the song "The East Wind." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4

## songs
**Nothing but Heartache in Your Social Life (2001)** : A track from "Koch Machine Glow," co-written with Canadian Film Director Adam Goyin. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Pasco Submarine (2003)** : A fan-favorite song from "The Battle of the Nudes," notable for featuring Gord Downey himself playing the drums. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Son (2016)** : A song from the "Secret Path" album, released October 18, 2016. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**The Bruins Kept Us Tight (2017)** : A track from the "Introduce Yourself" album, specifically directed at his brother Pat, highlighting their shared fandom for the Boston Bruins hockey team. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**The East Wind (2010)** : Featured on "The Grand Bounce," this song includes astute meteorological observations attributed to a farmer, and incorporates quotes from literary figures such as Al Purdy and Walter von Tilburg Clark (specifically, a line from "The Oxbow Incident"). ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4

## curiosities
**"Cactus Relationship" Philosophy** : Gord Downey applied his "cactus relationship" philosophy – characterized by low maintenance and reduced expectations – to his relationships with bandmates and family. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Anonymous Goaltending** : To maintain anonymity while playing hockey at a very good outdoor rink in a Toronto East End park, he would dress in full goalie gear, including his mask, at home before walking to the rink. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Assembly of First Nations Appearance (December 2016)** : Appeared at a gathering in Gatineau, where he was presented with an eagle feather and given the Lakota spirit name A’sokomanii ("the man who walks among the stars"). ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Brain Surgery (Early 2016)** : Underwent a full temporal lobectomy performed by Dr. Douglas DJ Cook at Kingston General Hospital, involving the removal of his left temporal lobe, hippocampus, and surrounding tissue. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Chanie Wenjack Fund** : A project dedicated to supporting reconciliation between Canada's First Nations and other Canadians, inspired by the tragic story of Chanie Wenjack. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Cognitive Impairments** : Experienced significant memory loss, including forgetting his children's names, confusion, and difficulty forming sentences, often resorting to writing reminders on his hands. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Collaborations with Other Artists** : Collaborated with various artists including Dallas Green, Buck 65, Blue Rodeo, The Sadies, The Rheostatics, and F.D. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Creative Drive Beyond The Hip** : Felt a need to pursue creative projects outside of The Tragically Hip. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Final Days and Death (October 18, 2017)** : Passed away at age 53 on October 18, 2017, the night before, surrounded by family, friends, and bandmates, following rumors of declining health. His death prompted widespread national mourning. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Glioblastoma Diagnosis (December 2015)** : Suffered his first seizure in Kingston in December 2015, leading to a diagnosis of incurable primary glioblastoma, an aggressive brain cancer in his left temporal lobe. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Hockey Fanatic** : A lifelong hockey fan and player, with Boston Bruins coach and GM Harry Sinden as his godfather. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Home Sold, Relocation to Kingston** : His home in the Beaches area of Toronto was sold, and he spent most of his final time residing in Kingston. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Indigenous Reconciliation Advocacy** : Became deeply involved in advocating for Canada's Indigenous people, leading to the establishment of the Chanie Wenjack Fund. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Lake Ontario Waterkeeper Board Member** : Served as a board member for Lake Ontario Waterkeeper, an organization focused on protecting Lake Ontario and its watershed. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Last Public Singing Performance (February 2, 2017)** : Joined Blue Rodeo and The Sadies on stage at Massey Hall in Toronto to sing "Lost Together," marking his final public vocal performance. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Order of Canada Investiture (June 2017)** : Attended an investiture ceremony in Ottawa to receive the Order of Canada for his leadership in Indigenous issues. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Plan for Final Days** : He and his brother Mike had planned to build a log cabin next door to Chanie Wenjack's family as a place for him to live out his final days when he became very sick. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Post-Surgery Treatment (Early 2016)** : Received 30 rounds of radiation (twice daily, five days a week for six weeks), supplemented by the oral chemotherapy drug Tolozolamide. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Posthumous Album Context** : His double album "Introduce Yourself" was released posthumously on October 27, 2017, but its recording sessions had been completed in February 2017. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Public Announcement of Incurable Cancer (May 24, 2016)** : Dr. James Perry of Sunnybrook Hospital in Toronto publicly announced Gord Downey's incurable brain tumor, news that profoundly affected Canada. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Treatment Choice** : Opted for a treatment plan that prioritized a better quality of life for a shorter duration, enabling him to accomplish more in his remaining time. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**We Day Address (July 2, 2017)** : Appeared in Ottawa with Pearl and Daisy Wenjack to deliver a powerful address on the necessity of reconciliation and the historical impact of residential schools. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
