## curiosities
**2006 Blood Disorder and Tour Cancellation** : In the spring of 2006, at the peak of Korn's success, Jonathan Davis began experiencing strange bruises and extreme weakness after gigs. He was diagnosed with Immune Thrombocytopenic Purpura (ITP), a blood infection caused by an allergic reaction to medication, which drastically lowered his blood platelet count to five (normal range is 140-400). While on tour in the UK, all further shows were canceled due to the risk of a brain hemorrhage from his constant head banging. "It turns out he had something called immune frombocytopetic perpera. This is a type of blood infection brought on, in Jonathan's case, by an allergic reaction to some medication he'd been taking. IMP results in low blood platelet count, meaning that Jonathan's blood had difficulty clotting. That explains the bruises. A normal count is between 140 and 400. Jonathan's count was five. Had he continued, Jonathan would have been in danger of having a brain hemorrhage because he was head banging all the time." ← Ongoing History of New Music > People Who (Almost) Died | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7rXwaxlEkbQ&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
