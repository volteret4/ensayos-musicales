**Korn (1994) – Debut Album** : Korn's self-titled debut album, released in 1994, features unsettling artwork depicting an eight-year-old girl, Justine Ferrarra, on a swing in a playground. A menacing shadow with lobster-like claws, cast by co-art director Dante Ariola, looms over her. The back cover maintains the sinister theme by showing the same playground but with the girl conspicuously missing. ## curiosities
**Controversial Album Art** : The cover art for Korn's debut album was considered so creepy and sinister that Justine Ferrarra, the eight-year-old model, was not shown the final picture until she reached the eighth grade. Furthermore, the family of Paul Pontius, her uncle and an Immortal Records employee, expressed significant displeasure with the unsettling results of the album's artwork. "Little Justine wasn't shown the picture until she was in the eighth grade, simply because it's so creepy and sinister." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tbKuJR0cPs
**Model on Debut Album Cover** : The young girl featured on Korn's 1994 self-titled debut album cover is Justine Ferrarra, who was eight years old at the time. She is the niece of Paul Pontius, an employee at Korn's then-label, Immortal Records, and was paid $300 for her participation in the photoshoot. "The little girl's name is Justine Ferrarra." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tbKuJR0cPs
