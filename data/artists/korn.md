**Korn (1994) – Debut Album** : Korn's self-titled debut album, released in 1994, features unsettling artwork depicting an eight-year-old girl, Justine Ferrarra, on a swing in a playground. A menacing shadow with lobster-like claws, cast by co-art director Dante Ariola, looms over her. The back cover maintains the sinister theme by showing the same playground but with the girl conspicuously missing. "It features a little girl dressed in a school uniform on a swing in a playground with an unseen figure casting a menacing shadow with lobster-like claws." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tbKuJR0cPs

Without Head's acceptance and Durst's belief in his own band, Limp Bizkit's career might not have materialized as it did. "None of this would have happened had head from Corn not taken up Fred on his offer for free tattoo and had Fred not had a little faith in his new band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Somq2MoRi5Q
