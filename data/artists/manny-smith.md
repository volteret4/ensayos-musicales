# artist - Manny Smith

## songs
**Crazy Blues (1920) - Hidden Meaning and Impact** : Recorded in a New York studio on August 10, 1920, this song by Manny Smith openly addressed the mistreatment of black people. While it appeared to be about domestic violence, substituting "my man" for "the white man or white people" revealed its intended, hidden meaning, making it a powerful social commentary. The song immediately became a sensation, selling 75,000 copies in its first month and over two million copies overall, and was pivotal in establishing the blues as a popular recorded art form, laying foundational elements for future blues, R&B, and rock music. ## curiosities
**First African-American Woman to Record Blues** : On August 10, 1920, an African-American woman from Cincinnati named Manny Smith traveled to a recording studio in New York, becoming one of the first of a long line of black female singers who went beyond the traditional bounds for female vocalists. Her work, alongside other women, laid foundations for blues, R&B, and ultimately rock music.
