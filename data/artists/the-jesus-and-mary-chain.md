## curiosities
**British Music Press Attention (March 1985)** : The riot at North London Polytechnic College received significant attention from the British music press due to their widespread presence at the event. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qC0Ee4-dpnI
**Creation Records Breakthrough (November 1984)** : Their single, released in November 1984, sold sufficiently well on Creation Records to bring significant attention to the label. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Lollapalooza Performance (1992)** : The Jesus and Mary Chain performed on the main stage lineup of Lollapalooza in 1992. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Non-Traditional Concert Style (Pre-1985)** : The band was known for not staging traditional concerts, with sets lasting only 10 to 20 minutes, featuring squalling feedback, and often playing with their backs to the audience, acting more as a "provocation." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qC0Ee4-dpnI
**Notorious Noisy Band** : Went on to become one of the most notorious "noisy bands" of the 1980s. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**On-Stage Fighting** : John and William Reed were known to fight on stage in the middle of a show. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5bB7GAUM63Y
**The Great Jesus and Mary Chain Riot (March 15, 1985)** : A concert at North London Polytechnic College on March 15, 1985, descended into a riot due to over-sold tickets, a hot and cramped venue, police departure, an opening band (Meat Whiplash) throwing a bottle into the crowd, and J&MC making the crowd wait an hour before playing a 20-minute set and leaving. The frustrated crowd then threw cans, invaded the stage, and smashed all the gear. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qC0Ee4-dpnI
