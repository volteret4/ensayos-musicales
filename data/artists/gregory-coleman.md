# artist - Gregory Coleman

## curiosities
**Lack of Compensation and Death (Died 2006)** : Despite his drum bit, the "Amen Break," being the "most sampled piece of music in the universe," Gregory Coleman never received any compensation for its widespread use. He tragically died homeless and destitute in 2006, highlighting a significant issue regarding artist royalties and sampling in the music industry. "According to the website, whosample.com, that bit has been used in nearly 6,200 songs, and drummer Gregory Coleman never received a penny. He died homeless and destitute in 2006." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ak6OuOUkRrQ
**The "Amen Break" (1969)** : Gregory Coleman, the drummer for The Winstons, played the seven-second drum bit in their 1969 song "Amen Brother," which became famously known as the "Amen Break." This specific drum segment is recognized as the "most sampled piece of music in the universe," appearing in nearly 6,200 songs. "It features a seven-second drum bit by Gregory Coleman. This is the most sampled piece of music in the universe." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ak6OuOUkRrQ
