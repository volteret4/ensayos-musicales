**Priesthood Calling Abandoned for Guitar Amplifier – Jack White's Near-Seminary Life** : Jack White (born John Anthony Gillis) is the youngest of ten children of Gorman and Theresa Gillis, a devout Catholic couple in Detroit. Both parents worked for the Catholic Archdiocese of Detroit — his mother as a secretary, his father in maintenance. Jack served as an altar boy at the Church of the Holy Redeemer and, following in the footsteps of his six brothers, developed a sense of religious calling. He applied to and was accepted at a seminary in Wisconsin, but had a last-second change of heart when he realized he would not be permitted to bring his new guitar amplifier. He enrolled in public school instead. Had he gone, there would have been no White Stripes, no marriage and divorce to Meg White, and no subsequent marriage to model Karen Elson. "So talk about a decision that changes your life, huh?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e6S5CTX7kkE
