**Brother and Sister Myth** : A persistent myth claimed that Jack and Meg White were brother and sister, which is entirely false. Jack Gillis (his birth name) was the tenth of ten children from a Catholic family in Detroit, with no sister named Meg, while Meg White was from Gross Point Farms with one sister, Heather, and no brothers. This false narrative was deliberately cultivated by Jack White in early interviews, where he referred to Meg as his "little sister," even after their marriage certificate was posted online in 2001. "When you see a band that's two pieces, husband and wife, boyfriend and girlfriend, you think, oh, I see. When their brother and sister, you go, oh, that's interesting. **Priesthood Calling Abandoned for Guitar Amplifier – Jack White's Near-Seminary Life** : Jack White (born John Anthony Gillis) is the youngest of ten children of Gorman and Theresa Gillis, a devout Catholic couple in Detroit. Both parents worked for the Catholic Archdiocese of Detroit — his mother as a secretary, his father in maintenance. Jack served as an altar boy at the Church of the Holy Redeemer and, following in the footsteps of his six brothers, developed a sense of religious calling. He applied to and was accepted at a seminary in Wisconsin, but had a last-second change of heart when he realized he would not be permitted to bring his new guitar amplifier. He enrolled in public school instead. Had he gone, there would have been no White Stripes, no marriage and divorce to Meg White, and no subsequent marriage to model Karen Elson. "So talk about a decision that changes your life, huh?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e6S5CTX7kkE
