**Another Way to Die (2008) – First Bond Duet** : Jack White co-performed "Another Way to Die" with Alicia Keys for the 2008 James Bond film "Quantum of Solace," marking the first duet in Bond theme history. White wrote the song, played all the instruments, and produced the track. The video was shot in Toronto while he was promoting his guitar documentary, "It Might Get Loud." The song was a massive success in the UK and Europe and charted top 15 in Canada, though it was not a significant hit in the US. **Priesthood Calling Abandoned for Guitar Amplifier – Jack White's Near-Seminary Life** : Jack White (born John Anthony Gillis) is the youngest of ten children of Gorman and Theresa Gillis, a devout Catholic couple in Detroit. Both parents worked for the Catholic Archdiocese of Detroit — his mother as a secretary, his father in maintenance. Jack served as an altar boy at the Church of the Holy Redeemer and, following in the footsteps of his six brothers, developed a sense of religious calling. He applied to and was accepted at a seminary in Wisconsin, but had a last-second change of heart when he realized he would not be permitted to bring his new guitar amplifier. He enrolled in public school instead. Had he gone, there would have been no White Stripes, no marriage and divorce to Meg White, and no subsequent marriage to model Karen Elson. "So talk about a decision that changes your life, huh?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e6S5CTX7kkE
