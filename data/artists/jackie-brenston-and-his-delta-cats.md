## songs
**Rocket 88 (1951)** : Recorded on March 3, 1951, this song about an Oldsmobile, performed by Ike Turner and his Kings of Rhythm, is considered by many musicologists to be the first true Rock and Roll record, signifying the "Ground Zero for Rock and Roll." "The song was called Rocket 88, and for many musicologists, that was the first true Rock and Roll record." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc

## curiosities
**Accidental Bass Distortion on "Rocket 88"** : During the recording session for "Rocket 88" on March 2nd, 1951, bass player Willie Kizart's amplifier was accidentally dropped, jarring loose the speaker cone. This resulted in a distinctive buzz and vibration. Producer Sam Phillips decided to embrace this "cool" distorted sound, which became a new and defining characteristic of the track, potentially making it the first rock and roll record. "But then producer Sam Phillips, a white guy, said, hold on, sounds kind of cool. Let's use it as is." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
**Claim to First Rock and Roll Record** : While the exact "first rock and roll record" is debated, "Rocket 88" by Jackie Brenston and his Delta Cats is strongly nominated, largely because producer Sam Phillips consistently touted it as such. Phillips' endorsement, coming from the discoverer of Elvis Presley, helped solidify this legend into a widely accepted fact. "It was because Sam Phillips said it was." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
**Recording Journey to Sun Studios** : On March 2nd, 1951, the Delta Cats packed into a station wagon with their instruments and drove 75 miles from Clarksdale, Mississippi, where they had been rehearsing at the Riverside Hotel, to Sun Studios in Memphis along Highway 61 to record. "On March 2nd 1951, all the Delta Cats packed up an old station wagon in front of the Riverside Hotel in Clarkstale, Mississippi where they'd been rehearsing and were ready to make a record." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
