## songs
**Stranglehold (1975)** : Released in 1975 on his debut solo album, "Stranglehold" is celebrated for containing "one of the best guitar solos in rock history." Initially sung by Derek St. Holmes, Nugent later assumed vocal duties due to jealousy over the attention directed away from him. Nugent clarified that the song was a "song of defiance with the music industry," not, as some critics speculated, about a woman (which had led to accusations of misogyny). He used this song to counter critics who dismissed his music as "noise," asserting its melody and diverse musical influences. Its raw energy and powerful appeal to fans were presented as evidence of its artistic merit, despite early industry skepticism. **Defense Against "Noise" Criticism** : In a 1977 newspaper article, Ted Nugent defended his music against critics who dismissed it as "simply noise." He argued that he would be a "pretty bad musician after all these years if he'd only learned three chords and how to make noise with them," citing "Stranglehold" as an example of a melodically and influentially rich composition. **Defiance of Music Industry** : Nugent's early solo career faced considerable resistance from the music industry, with "every record label passed" on him. They found his feedback-laden, guitar-jamming style and song themes, like "Stranglehold," unappealing. Despite this, his success, fueled by dedicated fans and unparalleled concert energy, served as a direct challenge to the industry's initial rejection.
