**Stranglehold (1975)** : Released in 1975 on his debut solo album, "Stranglehold" is celebrated for containing "one of the best guitar solos in rock history." Initially sung by Derek St. Holmes, Nugent later assumed vocal duties due to jealousy over the attention directed away from him. Nugent clarified that the song was a "song of defiance with the music industry," not, as some critics speculated, about a woman (which had led to accusations of misogyny). He used this song to counter critics who dismissed his music as "noise," asserting its melody and diverse musical influences. Its raw energy and powerful appeal to fans were presented as evidence of its artistic merit, despite early industry skepticism.
