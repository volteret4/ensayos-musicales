# artist - The Beatles

## albums
**Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band (1967) – Pioneering Concept Album** : released on June 1, 1967, explicitly designed to be listened to as a whole and in one sitting, marking a shift towards albums as artistic statements.

## songs
**I Me Mine (1970)** : Last song the band finished recording before they broke up, session on April 1, 1970.

## curiosities
**Chris Shiflett's Early Listening** : Chris Shiflett listened to The Beatles as a kid, following his brothers' tastes.
**Dave Grohl's Early Listening** : Dave Grohl listened to The Beatles until he was about 13 years old.
**Early Career Album Strategy** : released albums early in their careers, but they were mostly collections of previously released singles with some B-sides and filler.
**Inspiration for Joey Ramone** : Jeff Hyman (Joey Ramone) was a big fan of The Beatles, prompting him to acquire drums to play along with their records.
**Musical Influence** : Hugh Dillon's love for The Beatles was a factor in his decision to travel to London when he left Kingston.
**Pioneers of Album-Oriented Rock** : around 1965, they looked at the album format and began to write longer, more complex songs and collections of songs released in a carefully sequenced order, shifting rock/pop away from singles.
**Taylor Hawkins' Early Listening** : Taylor Hawkins listened to The Beatles.
**Working with Iconic Directors** : Taj Khrichlo dreams of hearing stories from directors who worked with The Stones and The Beatles (implied by context of "the best of the best" and "working with Madonna and working with the stones").
