## albums
**Abbey Road (1969) – Early Synthesizer Experimentation** : The band began to experiment with early synthesizers during this period, incorporating the new technology into their studio recordings. **Revolver (1966)** : Cited as one of the most significant rock releases of all time, commonly found in rock fans' collections. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VR5Fncdc2kQ
**Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band (1967)** : Released on June 1, 1967; meant to be enjoyed as a whole, in sequence, over 39 minutes and 36 seconds; a turning point that established the album as the primary currency of the recorded music business. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Sc9HApnyBLM
**Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band (1967) – Industry Game Changer** : This album fundamentally shifted the music industry's focus from low-margin singles to high-margin albums, making the full-length record the basis for everything in the music business. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z7glrAoXtkA
**Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band (1967) – Pioneering Concept Album** : released on June 1, 1967, explicitly designed to be listened to as a whole and in one sitting, marking a shift towards albums as artistic statements. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**The White Album (1968) – Self-Titled Double Album** : This 1968 release contains the song "Ob-La-Di, Ob-La-Da," a track noted for its extreme simplicity. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rfObVld9rIE

## songs
**Here Comes the Sun (1969)** : A track from the _Abbey Road_ album that features the use of an early synthesizer. It serves as an example of mainstream rock artists beginning to dabble in electronic sounds long before the Technopop era. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
**I Me Mine (1970)** : Last song the band finished recording before they broke up, session on April 1, 1970. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Epz5Sn151bk
**Let It Be (1970)** : Sung by Bono at the traditional Māori funeral of Greg Carroll in New Zealand. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VR5Fncdc2kQ
**Ob-La-Di, Ob-La-Da (1968)** : A ridiculously simple song recorded in 1968 and released on _The White Album_. The composition is set in the key of B-flat major, though it is easily transposable to the key of C. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rfObVld9rIE
