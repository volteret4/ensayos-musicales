## concerts
- **Shakespeare Stadium (August 1965)** : The Beatles performed at Shakespeare Stadium in August 1965, during which their amplification system proved insufficient for the crowd's noise. They used four All-Tech amplifiers, each generating 175 watts, through the stadium's baseball PA system. Despite this being considered "crazy, crazy loud" at the time, the crowd generated 135 decibels of noise, which was twice the volume the amps could produce, highlighting the critical need for improved concert amplification. "When The Beatles played Shakespeare Stadium in August 1965, all they had behind them was four all-tech amplifiers, each generating 175 watts of power, through the same PA system that the announcer used for baseball games."

**I Feel Fine (1964)** : On October 18, 1964, while recording at Abbey Road Studios, the Beatles accidentally discovered the controlled feedback sound used at the beginning of this song. Paul McCartney plucked a bass string, and John Lennon laid his semi-acoustic Gibson guitar against Paul's bass amp, causing the guitar to pick up the bass note and feed back. Recognizing the cool sound, Paul and John asked producer George Martin to capture it, and it was subsequently edited into the track. This is considered one of the first uses of controlled or semi-controlled feedback on a rock record. "As far as anyone can tell, this was one of the first uses of control, semi-controlled, feedback on a rock record." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w
