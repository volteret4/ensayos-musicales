# artist - Madness

## genres
- Ska

## concerts
- **Madstock Festival (August 18, 1992)** : Madness, described as "one of the great British Ska bands," played at the Madstock festival in Finsbury Park, London, on August 18, 1992. This event marked the band's first appearance in eight years and attracted exactly 36,000 and seven people. "Played an event in Finsbury Park in London called Madstock."

## songs
**One Step Beyond (1979)** : During Madness's performance of "One Step Beyond" at the Madstock festival on August 18, 1992, the collective dancing ("skanking") of the 36,000+ attendees created vibrations so intense they simulated an earthquake for blocks around. These vibrations were strong enough to register on the Richter scale. "So many people were dancing in skankinger in the song, one step beyond, that they created vibrations that simulated an earthquake for blocks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2RKchFW42Jc

## curiosities
**Madness – Named After Old Prince Buster Song; North London Group; "Nutty Sound"** : Madness was a North London group with what was described as a "nutty sound" — a loose blend of punk and traditional Jamaican ska. Two-Tone signed them and issued their first singles. Their name came from an old Prince Buster song called "Madness." "Two-tone also issued the first singles from a group that featured Prince Buster's old saxophone player, and they were called Mbiet." [Madness] ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VxWp7HrT8oE
**Madstock "Earthquake" (August 18, 1992)** : During Madness's performance at the Madstock festival in Finsbury Park, London, on August 18, 1992, the vigorous dancing of over 36,000 attendees caused vibrations that were felt by residents in nearby apartment blocks, shaking buildings and rattling windows. Scientists later investigated and determined that the collective "skanking" during the song "One Step Beyond" created vibrations that registered on the Richter scale, simulating an actual earthquake. "So many people were dancing in skankinger in the song, one step beyond, that they created vibrations that simulated an earthquake for blocks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2RKchFW42Jc
