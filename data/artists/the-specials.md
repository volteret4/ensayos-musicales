## songs
**A Message to You, Rudy (1979)** : A cover of the 1967 Jamaican song "Rudy, A Message to You" by Dandy Livingstone. This track is from their debut album. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Gangsters (1979)** : This debut single famously opens with singer Terry Hall exclaiming, "Bernie Rhodes don't argue." This shout-out refers to an incident at a Paris hotel where the band's gear was confiscated due to non-payment, and their manager, Bernie Rhodes, was involved in the situation. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc

## curiosities
**Anti-Racism Inspiration** : Dammers hated the racism he observed in his multi-racial city and was inspired by the Rock Against Racism movement that began in 1978. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Band Logo and Iconography** : The band's logo featured a stylized Jamaican rude boy named Walt Japsco, depicted with a black suit, white shirt, black tie, pork pie hat, white socks, and black shoes. Walt Japsco was based on a photograph of Jamaican musician Peter Tosh, formerly of Bob Marley's Wailers. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Chart Success for Indie Single (May 1979)** : Their very first record released on Two-tone in May 1979, with 5,000 copies distributed, included a song that reached number six on the British charts, a rare and significant achievement for an indie record at the time. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Formation Context** : Formed amidst racial tensions and the rise of the hard-right National Front in the UK during the 1970s, which led to street demonstrations, rioting, and injuries in British cities. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Foundational Influence** : Their efforts created the foundations for the ska-punk genre that continues to thrive today. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Founding Vision** : Formed by white keyboardist Jerry Dammers, who was captivated by the music West Indian immigrants brought to Coventry, especially reggae, dub, rock steady, and ska. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Integrated Lineup** : From its inception, the band was designed to be integrated, always featuring a mix of black and white musicians, including some Caribbean immigrants. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Management by Bernie Rhodes** : The Specials were managed by Bernie Rhodes, a detail famously acknowledged in their debut single, "Gangsters," where singer Terry Hall shouts "Bernie Rhodes don't argue" at the beginning, referencing a dispute where Rhodes was implicated after the band's gear was confiscated in a Paris hotel due to non-payment. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**Message of Harmony** : The Specials consistently preached racial and social harmony during their performances. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Ska Revival** : They were instrumental in introducing ska into the post-punk world. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Ska Revival Flag Carriers** : Became one of the most important flag carriers in the revival of Ska, specifically the "Third Wave Ska" movement, driven by Jerry Dammers' commitment to bringing Ska to a new generation. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
