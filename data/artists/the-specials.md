## albums
**Gangsters (1979) – First Two-Tone Single; Band Borrowed £700 from Family; B-Side Created The Selector** : In March 1979, the Specials borrowed 700 pounds from family and friends to record "Gangsters," the first Two-Tone single, based on a sample from a Prince Buster song. Having only enough money to record the A-side, Jerry Dammers offered the B-side to a friend named Noel Davis, who contributed an instrumental he had recorded with his brother-in-law in his living room in 1977. Dammers remixed the track and included it as the B-side; the song was called "The Selector" — and that became the name of the (then non-existent) band. This shared 7-inch was the beginning of the post-punk ska revival, which was faster and tighter than traditional ska with a greater reliance on horn sections. "Gangsters was the first two-tone single and a fine example of cooperation." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VxWp7HrT8oE

## songs
**"Song from 1949" (covered, late 1970s)** : The Specials covered a song from 1949, originally written by Carl Sigmund and Herb Madison. This cover was part of a new wave of ska that emerged in central Britain at the end of the 1970s, as Jamaican immigrants introduced and evolved the sound. "those songs helped establish a new wave of ska, which included another cover by the specials, which we just heard." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zNIVqKqAlnk
**Concrete Jungle (1979)** : This song from 1979 represents a new twist on Ska music. White kids from Coventry, influenced by the original Jamaican working-class sound, blended it with working-class punk from England, creating a new wave of "skanking" music. "The specials with concrete jungle from 1979." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V7WjNR1ujhk
**Gangsters (1979)** : The debut single by The Specials from 1979 notably features singer Terry Hall saying, "Bernie Rhodes don't argue" at the beginning. This shout-out is a direct reference to Bernie Rhodes, who was the band's manager. The line refers to an incident at a Paris hotel where management confiscated the Specials' gear due to non-payment, and Bernie was entangled in the situation. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**Ghost Town** : This song explicitly laid blame on the government, particularly Margaret Thatcher's policies, for failing the youth of the nation. It reflects the widespread discontent with austerity measures and their impact on young people in 1980s Britain. "The specials Ghost Town blamed the government for letting down the youth of the nation." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0ARYt4sYT90

## curiosities
**Adopting Ska for Energy** : Finding reggae too slow for the energetic sound he aimed for, Dammers turned to ska, reggae's ancestor, which had a choppier beat akin to punk but suitable for dancing, and could convey both anger and happiness. "Now Reggae was too slow for the energy that Jerry wanted to produce, so he defaulted to Reggae's ancestor. Scott." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
**Advocates for Racial Harmony** : In their performances, The Specials consistently promoted messages of racial and social harmony. "Whenever the specials played, they preached racial social harmony." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Anti-Racism Stance and Rock Against Racism** : Dammers, growing up in a multi-racial city and witnessing racism, was inspired by the Rock Against Racism movement that started in 1978, leading him to form an inclusive band. "Growing up in a multi-racial city, he hated the racism that he saw around him and was inspired by the rock against racism movement that began in 1978." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**British Music Press Championing** : The Specials were a prominent band within the post-punk Ska movement and were widely championed by the British music press. "SCA was one of the big post-punk sounds thanks to bands like the Specials and the Selector and Madness." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zB5zxycrbnY
**Formation and Influences** : Formed by white keyboardist Jerry Dammers in Coventry, The Specials were deeply influenced by the music brought by West Indian immigrants to his hometown, specifically reggae, dub, rock steady, and especially ska. "They were formed by keyboardist Jerry Damors, a white kid who was entranced by all the music new West Indian immigrants had brought with them to his hometown of Coventry." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Founding Two Tone Records** : In March 1979, The Specials borrowed £700 from family and friends to establish their independent record label, Two Tone, named after the black and white suits worn by mods and skinheads in the 1960s and reflecting the band's peaceful, multi-racial lineup. "In March 1979, the Specials borrowed 700 pounds from family and friends to form an independent record label that Jerry Damors called Two Tone." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
**Integrated Band Design** : From its inception, The Specials were designed to be an integrated band, consistently featuring a mix of black and white musicians, including Caribbean immigrants, over the years. "From the very beginning, the specials were designed to be an integrated band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Inventing the Uniform** : Jerry Dammers created a distinctive uniform for the band, a blend of West Indian rude boy, British skinhead, and British mod styles, featuring suits, pork pie hats, dark glasses, crisp white shirts, and neat ties. "One of the things he did was invent a uniform. It was a cross between a West Indian rude boy, a British skinhead, and a British mod. This meant suits, pork pie hats, dark glasses, and crisp white shirts and neat ties." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
**Jerry Dammers' Background and Motivation** : Founder Jerry Dammers, born in India and the ex-hippie son of a missionary minister, was deeply influenced by the diverse music heard on the streets of Coventry. He used music to rebel against his father and to push back against the bleak working-class existence of his city, embracing punk's freedom of expression. "Jerry Damers was born in India and the ex-Hippie son of a missionary minister. He was a huge fan of all this music that he was hearing in the street. He was also very much into the freedom of expression that came with the punk movement of the 1970s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
**John Hughes Film Exposure (1980s)** : The Specials were one of many bands whose music was heavily mined for the soundtracks of John Hughes' 1980s films. These carefully curated soundtracks played a significant role in introducing a wide audience in North America to bands from the alternative rock scene, particularly British performers. "So many people learned of bands like The Specials and Spandovalet and Billy Idle and General Public and Wang Chung and Yellow and New Order and the Psychedelic Furs and the Smiths and Echo and the Bunyman, Susanne Vagan in excess the Jesus and Mary chain, Flesh for Luluo and so many others from those soundtrack albums." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM

**Two-Tone Records Founded 1979 – Black-and-White Logo Based on Peter Tosh Photo; Anti-Racist Mission** : Jerry Dammers founded Two-Tone Records, naming it after the black-and-white suits worn by mods and skinheads of the 1960s and after the peaceful multiracial makeup of the band. The label's logo — a man in a black and white suit, white shirt, black tie, sunglasses, pork pie hat, white socks, and black loafers — was drawn by Dammers based on a photo of Peter Tosh when he was a member of the Wailers. The character was named Walt Jabsco (Walt after Walt Disney). Two-Tone had an explicit anti-racism message at a time when racial tensions in the UK were getting out of control. "Two-tone became the core of the second wave of Scott." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VxWp7HrT8oE
