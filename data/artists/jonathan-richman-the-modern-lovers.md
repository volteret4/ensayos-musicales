## songs
**Roadrunner (1972)** : Recorded in 1972, this song is described as embodying modern powerpop, comparable to works by bands like The Strokes and Sloan. It serves as an ode to the state of Massachusetts, a muscle car, and the experience of listening to AM radio late at night. "It's an ode to the state of Massachusetts. Also, it's an ode to a muscle car, and it's an ode to listening to AM radio late at night." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY

## curiosities
**Anti-Hippie Image** : Richman deliberately bucked the trends of his era by wearing his hair short when most male rockers sported long hair, openly reveling in his anti-hippie stance and embracing a distinct individuality. "He was also someone who at a time when every male rocker and every male rock fan wore their hair long, but this guy, nope, he wanted to wear his hair short. He was vehemently anti-hyppy and he reveled in being different." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Contemporary Powerpop Sound** : Recorded in 1972, their song "Roadrunner" is noted for sounding "every bit as modern powerpop as anything by the strokes or slone, the new pranographers, the Danny Warhol's, Jimmy Eat World, and even the Go-Ghosts, and the knack and blondie and the Romantics," demonstrating its ahead-of-its-time quality. "It was recorded in 1972, and it is every bit as modern powerpop as anything by the strokes or slone, the new pranographers, the Danny Warhol's, Jimmy Eat World, and even the Go-Ghosts, and the knack and blondie and the Romantics." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Groundwork for Punk Rock** : The stripped-down songs of Jonathan Richman and The Modern Lovers were instrumental in laying the groundwork for punk rock, with their music frequently classified as proto-punk, meaning it predated and helped define the genre. "His stripped down songs help set the groundwork for punk rock. In fact, we classify his music as proto-punk, the stuff that had to happen first before punk rock could be born." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Movie Appearance - There's Something About Mary** : Jonathan Richman made notable appearances in the movie "There's Something About Mary," where he was featured as the guitarist who pops up in various strange places throughout the film. "And if you remember the movie, there's something about Mary. Jonathan is the guy with a guitar who shows up in strain places during the film." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Velvet Underground Fanatic** : Jonathan Richman was a "hardcore velvet underground fan," directly inspired by their music to pursue a career in music himself.
