## songs
**Blue Monday (1983) – Band Agreed to Dissolve if Anyone Left; Reconstituted as New Order After Ian Curtis's Death** : Joy Division had a pact: if anyone left for any reason, the band would break up. When singer Ian Curtis hanged himself from his kitchen doorframe on May 18, 1980 — the night before a North American tour that could have broken the band internationally — the three surviving members honored the dissolution but immediately reconstituted under the name New Order. Curtis was 23. After everyone took turns at vocals, guitarist Bernard Sumner was designated singer in late July 1980; Stephen Morris's girlfriend Gillian Gilbert was added on keyboards that fall, completing the classic lineup. "Blue Monday" — their biggest single — was built by accident from a drum machine left running while the band tried to leave the stage early; a missed programmed note became a happy accident. The most successful 12-inch in history, it lost money on every copy sold due to the expensive die-cut packaging. The name Blue Monday refers to the Monday after Ian Curtis died by suicide the previous Sunday.
