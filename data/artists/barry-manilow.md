**Catalyst for Musicians Union Backlash Against Synths** : Barry Manilow became the "last straw" for the UK Musicians Union on May 23, 1982, prompting their public grievances against synthesizers. Previously touring with a string section, he replaced his orchestra with just a couple of synth players in 1981, leading to job losses for traditional musicians and escalating calls for a ban on synthesizers. "The last straw wasn't craftwork, or OMD, or Durand Durand, but very Manolo, one of the biggest mainstream artists at that time. In the past, Manolo had gone on tour with a string section, providing musicians with a solid living. But in 1981, he dumped the orchestra and toured with just a couple of synth players. That had to stop." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
**Impact on UK Musicians Union (1982)** : Barry Manilow's decision to replace his orchestra with synthesizers during his tours sparked outrage within the UK Musicians Union in 1982. This move was a primary catalyst for the union to pass a motion demanding that synthesizers be banned, viewing them as a threat to musicians' livelihoods. "The problem was this guy. In 1982, Barry Manolo was a huge star." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ak6OuOUkRrQ

**Touring Orchestra Replacement (Early 1980s)** : In the early 1980s, during a severe recession, Barry Manilow, a huge star, faced financial pressure to cut touring expenses. Consequently, he replaced his full orchestra—including violinists, cellists, and brass sections—with "a couple of guys with synthesizers," whose keyboards could emulate all the necessary orchestral sounds. "Manolo couldn't afford to take a full orchestra with him on tour anymore.
