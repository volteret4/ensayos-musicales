## albums
**Fun House (1970) – Second Album** : Named after their living quarters in Detroit, this album was recorded in Los Angeles. The recording process was characterized by extensive drug use, including the band's first experience with cocaine. The album was recorded largely live off the floor with minimal overdubs to capture their raw live sound, with its seven songs recorded in sequence like a live gig. Released in July 1970, *Fun House* is retrospectively considered the best representation of The Stooges' sound, though at the time it was savaged by critics and commercially unsuccessful. "That whole record was recorded live off the floor." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Raw Power (1973)** : This album was created with the patronage of David Bowie, who convinced his manager to invest cash into The Stooges project. The album was intentionally designed to be extremely intense, aiming to "actually hurt people as it came out of the speakers." It features the song "Search and Destroy." "The result was an injection of cash that was enough to make an album that was designed to be so intense that it would actually hurt people as it came out of the speakers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
**Raw Power (1973) – David Bowie Collaboration** : This album resulted from David Bowie convincing Iggy Pop, the Ashton brothers, and James Williamson to come to England to make a record for Columbia Records. Despite Bowie's connection and influence, the album struggled commercially, peaking at 182 on the Top 200 charts. However, it deeply inspired many artists, including a 16-year-old Johnny Lydon and Mick Jones (The Clash) who were at Iggy's first UK show, and Kurt Cobain who later called it his favorite album. "But the people who bought this record were inspired." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Skull Ring (2003) – Iggy Pop Solo Album featuring The Stooges** : This album was released under Iggy Pop's name after Virgin Records suggested he incorporate guest musicians, specifically hinting at the "Ashton Boys," to generate buzz for his next record and avoid being dropped. Ron and Scott Ashton appeared on four songs, marking a close, but not official, reunion of the band members. "Ron and Scott appeared on four songs in a record that was eventually released under the title Skull Ring." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**The Stooges (1969) – Self-titled debut** : This album was produced by John Cale of The Velvet Underground. Due to the band's inability to rehearse for more than 20 minutes, half the material was allegedly written the night before their first day in the studio, with old avant-garde ideas reworked into more coherent songs. The initial mix was rejected by Elektra president Jack Holtzman, who then re-mixed it himself, but despite this, the album did not sell well as it was considered too raw and primitive for most people at the time. "Nobody was making music that sounded like this." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**The Weirdness (2007) – Reunion Album** : This album was released on March 6, 2007, marking the first album of new material from The Stooges since *Raw Power* in 1973, a gap of 34 years. It featured Iggy Pop, Ron Ashton, and Scott Ashton, with punk stalwart Mike Watt on bass. The album was followed by extensive touring throughout the mid-2000s. "It was the first album of new stuages material since raw power in 1973, 34 years." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8

## curiosities
**Band Formation (Late 1967)** : The Stooges were formed by James Osterberg (later Iggy Pop) and a group of "local degenerates" one night in Detroit, inspired by a bag of dope and a Three Stooges movie marathon on television. "One night, over a bag of dope and a three-stooge's movie marathon on television, he and a bunch of local degenerates decided to form a band that they called the Stooge's." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2RKchFW42Jc
**Band Name Origin ("Psychedelic Stooges")** : The name originated during a drug-fueled session at the Ashton house in 1967 where the band members were watching "The Three Stooges" on TV while high on acid. Initially, they suggested "The Psychedelic Stooges" but it was later shortened to just "The Stooges" as no one could remember how to spell "psychedelic." "When they were very, very high, they were watching the three stuages on TV and somebody suggested I know we'll just call ourselves the psychedelic stuages." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Bowie's Intervention and *Raw Power* Production** : David Bowie, then on a hot streak with significant industry power, bonded with Iggy Pop. He convinced Iggy, Ron and Scott Ashton, and James Williamson to move to England to record *Raw Power* for Columbia Records, effectively resurrecting the band. "Bowie was on a hot streak and thus he had power. And after bonding with Iggy, he convinced him, the Ashton brothers in James Williamson, to come to England where they'd all make a record for Columbia Records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Bowie's Support During Hospitalization** : During Iggy Pop's hospitalization in 1974, he had only two visitors: David Bowie and Bowie's costume designer, Ola Hudson (mother of Saul Hudson, later known as Slash). Bowie, despite his own struggles with drugs, played a crucial role as Iggy's and ultimately The Stooges' savior. "He had two visitors. One was David Bowie and the other was Bowie's costume designer, a woman named Ola Hudson." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Collapse and Iggy's Hospitalization** : Following their struggles, The Stooges couldn't maintain cohesion, leading to their eventual collapse. This period saw Iggy Pop's personal struggles escalate, resulting in him ending up in a mental hospital. David Bowie, a devoted admirer and patron, was Iggy's sole visitor during this time. "Of course, the Stooges couldn't hold it together and everything collapsed. Iggy eventually ended up at a mental hospital, where his only visitor was, you guessed it, David Bowie." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
**Commercial Failure and Punk Legacy** : The Stooges' albums, though critically acclaimed in retrospect, were largely commercial failures during their initial release due to their raw and primitive sound, which was ahead of its time. Despite this, their debut album is considered one of the most important pre-punk records, contributing to Iggy's legend as the "godfather of punk." "It has gone down on history as being one of the most important pre-punk records of all time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Confrontational Stage Presence & Stage Diving** : The Stooges developed a divisive live reputation, attracting audiences who either loved or hated them. Iggy Pop's performances were characterized by self-destructive, confrontational, and contortionist antics, often involving him being half-naked, and frequently diving into the audience without concern for his or their safety. He is considered a possible inventor of stage diving. "It's quite possible that Iggy was the inventor of stage diving." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Covered by The Sisters of Mercy** : The Stooges were among the artists whose songs were covered by The Sisters of Mercy in their early days, indicating an influence or appreciation within the nascent goth scene. "But in the early days, the Sisters were known for their covers, stooges, bob Dylan, not chocolate if I'm a band, velvet underground, and a killer version of Louis Louis." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
**Discovery and Signing by Danny Fields** : The Stooges came to the attention of Danny Fields, a talent scout for Elektra Records known for signing hippie bands like The Doors. Fields discovered them while in Detroit to check out MC5, and despite his bosses being "stupified" by The Stooges, he convinced Elektra to sign them. "However, the stuages eventually came to the attention of Danny Fields, a talent scout for electro-records who was hired for his ability to find the right hippie bands." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Dropped by Elektra and Financial Ruin** : Due to their escalating drug problems, chaotic gigs (where Iggy was sometimes too stoned to stand), Elektra Records dropped The Stooges. The label also demanded an $11,000 refund on an advance given to the band. The Stooges then entered a nine-month period of inactivity, save for drug use, with Ron Ashton becoming estranged from the group, calling it a "death trip." "So Electra dropped them. Actually, they more than dropped them. They demanded a refund of $11,000 on an advance they had given the band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Dropped by Label and Drug Use** : Around 1971, The Stooges were dropped from their record label and were deeply entrenched in hard drug use. This period coincided with Jonathan Richmond recording "Roadrunner," highlighting a stark contrast in musical approaches and lifestyles within the alternative scene. "Well, at about the same time Jonathan Richmond was recording Roadrunner, the Stooges were being dropped from their record label." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
**Drug Addiction and Lineup Changes (Late 1970)** : By late 1970, Iggy and Scott Ashton were severely addicted to heroin. Bass player Dave Alexander was fired for being too drunk to perform at a gig. New bass players cycled through, and a second guitarist, James Williamson, was added, but the band struggled with stability. "By the end of 1970, Iggy and Scott Ashton were serious hairwind junkies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Early Influences & Recognition** : The Stooges are frequently cited as a major influence by contemporary bands like The White Stripes and The Hives, and by 90s bands such as Jane's Addiction and Nirvana. They also shaped the sound of 80s bands like Sonic Youth and inspired 70s artists like the Sex Pistols, Ramones, and David Bowie to make music. "So the stooges seem to be on everybody's list even though they hardly sold any records in their day and were so messed up that their singer ended up in an asylum." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Early Professional Gigs and "Osterizer" (1968)** : The band's first professional gig was opening for the Amboy Dukes (featuring Ted Nugent) on January 20, 1968, followed by an opening slot for Blood Sweat and Tears on March 3, 1968. During these shows, Iggy notably played a homemade instrument called the "Osterizer," which was a vacuum cleaner attached to a blender, although he soon abandoned it due to amplification difficulties. "And was the singer playing a vacuum cleaner? Yes, yes it was. And was it attached to a blender? And yeah, it was." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Early Stage Diving (1969)** : Iggy Pop is noted for performing stage diving with The Stooges as early as 1969. This makes him one of the earliest documented figures engaging in this behavior, which later became common at punk and rock shows. "Iggy Pop was doing this with the Stooges as far back as 1969." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KMx4bmq8o2Y
**Extreme Performances** : The Stooges, featuring Iggy Pop, were known for their "extreme things" performed in America, contributing to the outrageous aesthetic that inspired David Bowie's Ziggy Stardust persona. "who was doing all kinds of extreme things in America with his band, The Stooges." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw
**Extreme Stage Antics (1970)** : By the spring of 1970, The Stooges' live performances became even more extreme and out of control. Iggy would often get naked, cut himself with broken glass, and smear himself with substances like raw meat and peanut butter. Audiences attended primarily for the spectacle, rather than the music. "Iggy was just out of control, often getting naked, cutting himself with broken glass, and smearing things like raw meat and peanut butter all over himself." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Financial Troubles and Truck Incident** : The Stooges were plagued by debts, including an IRS investigation for unpaid taxes, and bills resulting from an incident where Scott Ashton attempted to drive the band's 14-foot truck under a 13-foot bridge. "They were under investigation by the IRS for non-payment of taxes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**First Breakup and Iggy's Hiatus** : Eventually, the band dissolved. Iggy Pop spent the next six months in Florida cutting lawns for retirees. "Eventually, the stuages were done. Iggy spent the next six months in Florida cutting lawns for retirees." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**First Gig (Halloween 1967)** : The Stooges' debut performance was on Halloween 1967 at their headquarters on State Street in Ann Arbor, described as "weird." Iggy wore an aluminum foil wig and played a Hawaiian guitar tuned only to E, Scott played oil drums with ball-peen hammers, and Dave created reverb effects by banging on Ron's amplifier. Despite the unconventional and "awful" performance, both band and audience, being high, considered it a success. "Iggy wore a wig made of strands of aluminum foil and played a Hawaiian guitar with every string tune to E." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**First Show Guitar Malfunction (October 31, 1967)** : On the day of their inaugural performance, Iggy Pop's Hawaiian guitar was rendered useless because guitarist Ron Ashton had painted it, pickups included. Iggy's subsequent abandonment of the guitar and spontaneous, wild dancing, combined with his pants falling down, led to the birth of his legendary, audience-loved punk rock stage antics. "So when the Stooge's hit the stage, Iggy's guitar was completely useless because of the paint." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2RKchFW42Jc
**Formation and Influence – Post-Drumming for Iggy Pop** : After his initial drumming experiences, Iggy Pop moved on from behind the drum kit to start writing songs and formed The Stooges. This band, along with Iggy's subsequent involvement with David Bowie and other experiences, contributed significantly to laying the foundations for modern punk rock. "He started writing songs moved on to the stooges got involved with David Bowie took a lot of LSD and pretty much laid the foundations for modern punk rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo
**Formation and Members (1967)** : The band was formed in 1967 by James Newell Osterberg (Iggy), brothers Ron and Scott Ashton, and Dave Alexander. Iggy, who was a known drummer in Ann Arbor, befriended Ron Ashton, leading to Scott joining, and then Dave Alexander, a high school dropout who had traveled the UK in search of the meaning of The Beatles. "These four guys decided to form a band with Iggy out front and on keyboards." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Iggy Pop's Name Origin** : James Newell Osterberg adopted the stage name "Iggy" from his previous band, The Iguanas. The "Pop" part was added after he shaved off his eyebrows in a "weird stoned gesture," leading the other band members to comment that he resembled a local drug user named Jim Pop. "The pop part came after Iggy shaved off his eyebrows in some weird stone to gesture." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Iggy's Arrest and Hospitalization (October 1974)** : In October 1974, Iggy Pop was arrested for being "aggressively strung out" at an LA burger joint. He was subsequently admitted to the UCLA Neuropsychiatric Hospital, where he detoxed and underwent psychiatric evaluation in an 8x4 room for several weeks. "Iggy was arrested for being aggressively strung out in an LA burger joint. He ended up at the UCLA Neurosyche Atric Hospital." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Iggy's Survival and Drug Dealing (Early 1970s)** : During the band's inactive period after being dropped by Elektra, Iggy Pop sustained himself by becoming a middleman for a heroin dealer. However, he often used the drugs himself, exacerbating his addiction. "Iggy paid the bills by becoming a middleman for a hairwind dealer, but sometimes he forgot his role and ended up using all the drugs himself." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Influence on Kid Rock's Vision** : The Stooges were cited by Kid Rock as an influence on his musical vision, which he described as "very white trash Public Enemy style rap, plus The Stooges, plus Kiss, plus Hank Williams." "A vision he calls very white trash public enemy style rap, plus the stooge's plus kiss plus Hank Williams." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5bQz8R0gbXc
**Influence on Punk Rock** : Despite remaining fringe figures, the first three albums by The Stooges, particularly through David Bowie's influence and acceptance of their unconventional style, became fundamental templates for the development of punk rock. Their raw energy and disregard for mainstream rock conventions inspired a new generation of musicians. "And this is how the first three Stooges albums became some of the most important templates for what would be eventually called punk rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
**Influence on Seattle Scene** : Tracks from The Stooges were featured on homemade punk cassettes distributed by Buzz Osborne in Aberdeen, Washington, influencing the developing Seattle rock scene. "Buzz Osborne began giving out cassettes featuring his favorite punk songs, All Indie bands, TBI from the stuages, tracks from bad brains and black flag, and Buzz gave these tapes only to people he considered cool." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**James Williamson's Career Shift** : Following the initial breakup, guitarist James Williamson had a brief reunion with Iggy Pop in 1979 but then retired from music to become an electronics engineer. He earned an electrical engineering degree and eventually became the Vice President of Technology Standards for Sony. "He became an electronics engineer, although there was a brief reunion with Iggy in 1979. But then he retired from music, got an electrical engineering degree, and became the vice president of technology standards for Sony." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Jim Morrison's Influence (1967)** : On October 20, 1967, Iggy attended a Doors show at the University of Michigan and was greatly inspired by Jim Morrison's drunken behavior and provocative interactions with the audience, which influenced Iggy's own stage persona. "Iggy was there and he was inspired by Jim Morrison's drunkenness in the way he provoked the audience." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Last Original Show (February 9, 1974)** : The final show played by The Stooges before their first long hiatus was on February 9, 1974, at The Rock'n'Roll Farm, a biker bar in Wayne, Michigan. Iggy, wearing a pink leotard, provoked the biker audience with insults during a 45-minute rendition of "Louis Louis," leading to eggs and bottles being thrown. Iggy then confronted a large biker, was punched in the face, and the band left the stage, effectively ending their run. "The last show the studios played together was at a club called The Rock'n'Roll Farm in Wayne, Michigan." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Meeting David Bowie (September 7, 1971)** : While in New York "sponging off a friend," Iggy Pop met David Bowie on September 7, 1971. Bowie, who was on the verge of launching his Ziggy Stardust phase, was influenced by Iggy, choosing the name Ziggy partly due to Iggy's persona. "The date was September 7th of 1971. Bowie was about to launch into his Ziggy stardust phase. That character was to be in part inspired by Iggy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Official Reunion at Coachella (2003)** : The Stooges officially regrouped and performed at the Coachella Festival in California on April 27, 2003. This reunion occurred 10,670 days, or 29 years, two months, and 19 days after their last gig at the biker bar on February 9, 1974. "And so it came to pass that Iggy Pop and the stuages regrouped and performed at the Coachella Festival in California on April 27, 2003." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Reunion Overtures and Iggy's Reluctance** : For decades after their initial breakup, various attempts were made to reunite Iggy Pop and the Ashton brothers, but Iggy, whose solo career was flourishing and increasingly drug-free, felt revisiting the past would not be in his best interests. "But things were going so well for Iggy that he just felt that it wouldn't be in his best interests to revisit the past." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Reunion for Touring** : The Stooges were one of many bands from past decades that reunited in the 21st century, a trend largely motivated by financial incentives. With declining CD sales impacting artist revenues, returning to the touring circuit offered a lucrative way to appeal to nostalgic fan bases and fill large venues. "The list of reunions is long." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GYUuTbOvyak
**Rock and Roll Hall of Fame Induction (2010)** : The Stooges were finally inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2010 after six previous unsuccessful attempts. This recognition came despite their historical lack of significant record sales. "And in 2010, they finally made it into the rock and roll hall of fame after six unsuccessful attempts." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Ron Ashton's Death (2009)** : Ron Ashton died of a heart attack at the age of 60, approximately two years after the release of the reunion album *The Weirdness*. The band subsequently continued with James Williamson returning on guitar. "And then Ron Ashton died of a heart attack. He made it to 60 years old." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Ron Ashton's Estrangement and Side Projects** : After the initial breakup of The Stooges, Ron Ashton was estranged from the group due to the "death trip" drug use. He later played in several bands that didn't achieve significant success, though one group featured Thurston Moore of Sonic Youth, Mark Arm of Mudhoney, and J Mascis of Dinosaur Jr. He also pursued acting, appearing in the original *Texas Chainsaw Massacre* (1974). "Ron Ashton played in a series of bands, none of which did much, although there was the group that featured Thurston Moore of Sonic Youth, also Mark Arm of Mudhoney and Jay Mascus of Dinosaur Jr." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Steve McCay's Post-Stooges Work** : Saxophonist Steve McCay, who performed on the *Fun House* album, continued his career working with a variety of avant-garde performers. "Steve McCay, he was the saxophoneist who turned on the funhouse album, while he worked with a series of on-guard performers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
