**Play (1999) – Pioneering Licensing Strategy** : Moby's fifth studio album, released in 1999, became a landmark by offering all 18 of its songs to advertising agencies, film studios, and TV producers. This unprecedented gamble by his management team aimed to expose his music to a wider audience than traditional touring and radio airplay could provide. The strategy proved immensely successful, with companies like Nordstrom, Volkswagen, and Levi's licensing every single track, leading to widespread recognition for Moby and ultimately selling 12 million copies worldwide. It served as a significant "wake-up call" for the artist community, especially as music piracy surged and physical music sales plummeted in the subsequent years. "Every single track was licensed. Everyone heard about Mobie as a result. And play ended up selling 12 million copies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xid8CZf4oYo ← moby

**Teany – Vegan Cafe and Online Tea Store** : Moby used to own and operate a vegan cafe and online store in New York City called Teany, located at 90 Rivington in Lower Manhattan. Opened in 2002 with his then-girlfriend and partner Kelly Tisdale, the establishment sold 98 different types of loose tea. Despite neither Moby nor Kelly having prior restaurant experience, Moby's financial resources allowed for mistakes, although the business underwent a redesign in 2007. Moby is no longer involved with the restaurant, but Teany Ice Tea is now sold globally, and a book listing Teany tea recipes exists.
