## albums
**Play** : This album became a household name and sold 10 million copies due to an unconventional licensing strategy. Moby and his manager licensed all 18 songs from the record for various commercial uses after the album initially failed to gain traction through traditional outlets like radio. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sfie4YYncxk
**Play (1999) - Licensing Strategy** : Moby and his manager devised a groundbreaking strategy to license all 18 tracks from the "Play" album for TV commercials and shows, as traditional radio and video channels showed little interest. This "licensed everything strategy" proved incredibly successful, leading to weekly sales of 150,000 copies, nine hit singles, all tracks being licensed, and global sales exceeding 10 million copies. The album transformed Moby from a cult DJ into a worldwide star, though some fans criticized the extensive commercialization, exemplified by an attempt to sell his "soul" on eBay. "The album itself sold somewhere north of 10 million copies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DYtsWfguqGo
**Play (1999) – Extensive Commercial Licensing** : All 17 tracks from this album were licensed for a wide array of commercial endorsements, including usage in TV shows, movie soundtracks, and television commercials. This unprecedented level of commercial integration made Moby a target for numerous critics who labeled him a "gigantic sellout" for allegedly prostituting his art for financial gain. "Every single song in the play album, and there were 17 tracks in the CD, have been licensed for use in everything from TV shows and movie soundtracks to TV commercials." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dMsEj62KFEw
**Play (1999) – Pioneering Licensing Strategy** : Moby's fifth studio album, released in 1999, became a landmark by offering all 18 of its songs to advertising agencies, film studios, and TV producers. This unprecedented gamble by his management team aimed to expose his music to a wider audience than traditional touring and radio airplay could provide. The strategy proved immensely successful, with companies like Nordstrom, Volkswagen, and Levi's licensing every single track, leading to widespread recognition for Moby and ultimately selling 12 million copies worldwide. It served as a significant "wake-up call" for the artist community, especially as music piracy surged and physical music sales plummeted in the subsequent years. "Every single track was licensed. Everyone heard about Mobie as a result. And play ended up selling 12 million copies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xid8CZf4oYo

**Redefining "Selling Out"** : Moby is credited with being perhaps most responsible for changing the perception of artists licensing their music for commercial purposes, transforming it from a "giant sin" and career-ending insult into an "essential part of any act's income stream." His success with "Play" demonstrated the financial viability and audience reach possible through sync deals, especially as physical sales dropped by 90% and streaming offered little meaningful income for most musicians. This shift made licensing music an aspirational component of an artist's business plan. "After he showed how there was money to be made from licensing music in an industry where physical media was dying, Mobie changed the concept of selling out." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xid8CZf4oYo
