## curiosities
**Anger at Bono Post-Live Aid (July 13, 1985)** : After U2's Live Aid performance, The Edge was "furious" with Bono for his on-stage "grandstanding" and failure to adhere to the band's plan, which resulted in them not playing their hit "Pride (In the Name of Love)." "The Edge, Larry and Adam were furious with Bono's grandstanding and his inability to stick with the plan." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2RKchFW42Jc
**Captive (1986) - Soundtrack Collaboration** : The Edge composed the soundtrack for the movie *Captive* and collaborated with Sinead O'Connor, getting her to sing the movie's theme, "Heroine," and also giving her a songwriting credit. "And suddenly, she found herself in the studio with the edge who was composing the soundtrack for a movie called Captive." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=afuqjWl1JyI
**Guitar Parts for "One Tree Hill"** : The Edge contributed his distinctive guitar parts to the U2 song "One Tree Hill." These parts, combined with Bono's lyrics and the efforts of Toronto musicians, helped finalize the track dedicated to roadie Greg Carroll. "With Bono's lyrics, the edges guitar parts and contributions from three Toronto musicians at Grant Avenue Studios in Hamilton, the song was finished." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZVGnSVfed9s
