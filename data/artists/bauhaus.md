**Bela Lugosi's Dead (1979) - Debut Single** : Issued in August 1979 by indie label Small Wonder, this atmospheric piece served as Bauhaus's one and only single. It featured a Bassenova beat, a distinctive three-note bass line by David J, a reggae feel from Daniel Ash, and an erotic, vampiric vocal approach by Peter Murphy, inspired by the actor who played Dracula. Clocking in at over nine and a half minutes, its unusual length for a debut single was a risk taken by the label. "It was an atmospheric piece, featuring a Bassenova beat, a three note bass line by David J, a reggae feel from Daniel, and a rather erotic vampiric approach by Peter that was inspired by the actor who used to play Dracula." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A89ipsMeMAg

## curiosities
**Band Breakup - Hammersmith Palais (1983)** : Bauhaus concluded their initial run with a final performance on July 5, 1983, at the Hammersmith Palais in London. The breakup was attributed to rising egos, creative differences, the pressures of touring, and Peter Murphy's severe bout of pneumonia, which forced the band to record an album without him, leading to internal divisions. "As the gig at the Hammersmith Pele came to an end, David J grabbed the mic and said, rest in peace. And that was that. Bajaos was dead." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A89ipsMeMAg
