## songs
**Bela Lugosi's Dead (1979)** : This nine-and-a-half-minute debut single was dedicated to Hungarian actor Bela Lugosi, famous for his portrayal of Dracula, and was instrumental in earning Bauhaus the "Goth" label. The song was released on Beggars Banquet, one of the many nimble indie labels emerging in post-punk England. "Bauhaus and Bella Logosi's dead, at least on beggars banquet, another one of those small nimble indie labels that popped up almost daily in post-punk England." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
**Bela Lugosi's Dead – Foundational Text of Gothic Rock** : This Bauhaus track is cited as one of the defining moments in the crystallization of gothic rock as a distinct genre, adopting the dark aesthetic sensibility that had filtered through decades of Gothic literature and horror cinema. "Bauhaus, with 'Bela Lugosi's Dead,' adopted dark aesthetics to help shape gothic rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f2KA97T4u08
**Bella Lugosi's Dead** : This song was performed by Bauhaus in a nightclub scene in the 1983 movie *The Hunger*, a film popular with Goth audiences. "it included a nightclub scene with Bauhaus performing Bella Logosi's dead." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ

## curiosities
**Birthplace and Early Support** : Formed in the English Midlands in 1978, Bauhaus greatly benefited from the rise of small independent record labels across Britain, which provided a platform for their unique sound. "Bauhaus were born in the English Midlands in 1978 and were yet another group to benefit from the emergence of small independent record labels across Britain." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
**Emergence of Goth Music (Late 1970s)** : Bauhaus is identified as a band that appeared in the late 1970s, marking the emergence of what is now known as goth music. This is significant when considering the theory that elements of proto-goth appeared earlier in the 1974 film "Phantom of the Paradise." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Influence of Gothic Approach** : Their gothic musical style, as showcased on their debut single released on Beggars Banquet, proved to be highly popular and influential in subsequent years. "Bauhaus' Gothic approach would prove to be very popular in the years ahead." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
**Pioneering Goth Tag** : The band was among the first to be labeled "Goth," largely due to their debut single, a nine-and-a-half-minute track dedicated to Bella Lugosi, the Hungarian actor famous for portraying Dracula. "Bauhaus was one of the first bands to be tagged with the Goth label, thanks mainly to their very first single, a nine and a half minute single dedicated to Bella Logosi, the Hungarian actor best known for his role as Dracula." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
**Relevance to Death Goths** : Bauhaus is a band that is "big with" the "death Goths," who are purists faithful to the original Goth aesthetics of the late 1970s. "Baja's and Georgia Vision are big with these people." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Second Generation Goth Influence** : Along with The Cure, The Sisters of Mercy, Siouxsie and the Banshees, and Joy Division, Bauhaus records were a significant part of "second generation Goth" in the latter half of the 1980s. "Along with the cure and the sister of mercy, choosing the banshees and some immortal Bauhaus and joy division records, there were a series of other bands that, well not exactly big in the mainstream, were huge parts of the cult of the Goth." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
