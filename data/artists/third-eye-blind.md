## songs
**Crystal Meth Addiction Hit** : This "big hit" by Third Eye Blind, while sounding like a fun pop song, is actually about crystal meth addiction. The uncensored version explicitly details the drug's effects and repeated use with lyrics such as, "Doing crystal meth will lift you up until you break. It won't stop. I won't come down. I keep stock with the TikTok rhythm. I bump for the drop. And then I bumped up. I took the hit that I was given. Then I bumped up again. Then bumped again." ## curiosities
**First-Person Lyrical Approach** : Third Eye Blind was cited as an example of a Post-Grunge band whose lyrical style leaned heavily on the "first person approach," illustrating a key distinction from the more indirect lyrical themes of Grunge. "Now think about the lyrical writing style of collective soul or bush or third eye blind and matchbox 20. You see what I mean?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU ← third-eye-blind
**Song Origin from Speeding Friends** : The song about crystal meth addiction was written during a period when a group of Stephen Jenkins's friends in San Francisco were constantly "speeding." "It was written at about the time when a bunch of San Francisco friends were constantly speeding." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=i5lR8zz7_ZE ← third-eye-blind ← third-eye-blind
**Tour Bus Bunk Description** : Arion Salazar describes the bunks on a tour bus as small, coffin-like compartments. These bunks have curtains that can be pulled and buttoned shut, creating a "little cocoon" that serves as a private space for band members, likened to the "hellbop" concept. "We're in these little things that are like coffins, the curtains you pull them shut and button them shut and you're like securing your little cocoon." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DgN0vBIugLc ← third-eye-blind
