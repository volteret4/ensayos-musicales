## songs
**Radio Ban for "Glorified Juvenile Delinquency"** : "Rumble" was banned by radio stations in New York and Boston because its gritty, street-fight-like sound was perceived as "glorified juvenile delinquency," despite being an instrumental track. Wray himself retitled the composition "Rumble" because he thought it sounded like a street fight. "He retied the composition rumble because he thought it sounded like a street fight. And it did because it was banned by radio stations in both New York and Boston because it sounded like, and this is a, quote, glorified juvenile delinquency." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YFv6Ln00XjA
**Rumble (1958)** : This instrumental track by Link Wray, released in 1958, was created by intentionally poking holes in the speaker cones of his amplifier to achieve a raspy, distorted sound. The song was so controversial for its time that it was banned by some radio stations, who feared it encouraged juvenile delinquency and gang fights. Its unique sound proved hugely influential, inspiring guitarists globally to seek ways to duplicate it. "This song was so sinister for its day that it was banned by some radio stations believing that it encouraged not just juvenile delinquency, but gangfights." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w

## curiosities
**Influence on Guitar Legends** : His innovative guitar playing style, particularly his use of power chords, directly influenced iconic guitarists such as Jimmy Page and Pete Townshend, who picked up the guitar in part because of his sound. "Jimmy Page, Pete Townsend, guys like that picked up the guitar in part because of Link Ray." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YFv6Ln00XjA
**Inventor of the Power Chord** : Link Wray is credited with popularizing the idea of playing power chords on an electric guitar, a technique that would profoundly influence rock music. He is often called the "inventor of the power cord." "He has been called the inventor of the power cord, Jimmy Page, Pete Townsend, guys like that picked up the guitar in part because of Link Ray." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YFv6Ln00XjA
**Shawnee Heritage Obscured** : Link Wray was born in North Carolina to parents from the Shawnee tribe, making him a Native American. However, census documents of the time identified him as white, obscuring his true ethnicity. "His name was Link Ray, and he was born in North Carolina to parents from the Shawnee tribe. But that ethnicity was obscured by census documents that identified him as white, but he was definitely Native American." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YFv6Ln00XjA
**Speaker Cone Alteration** : In 1958, Link Wray, inspired by messing around with a damaged amplifier, started intentionally poking holes in the speaker cones of his own amp. This technique was used to achieve a raspy, loved sound for his instrumental track "Rumble." "He started poking holes in the speaker cones of his amp to make them sound all raspy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w
