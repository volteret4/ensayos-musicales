# artist - Link Wray

## songs
**Rumble (1958)** : This instrumental track by Link Wray, released in 1958, was created by intentionally poking holes in the speaker cones of his amplifier to achieve a raspy, distorted sound. The song was so controversial for its time that it was banned by some radio stations, who feared it encouraged juvenile delinquency and gang fights. Its unique sound proved hugely influential, inspiring guitarists globally to seek ways to duplicate it. ## curiosities
**Speaker Cone Alteration** : In 1958, Link Wray, inspired by messing around with a damaged amplifier, started intentionally poking holes in the speaker cones of his own amp. This technique was used to achieve a raspy, loved sound for his instrumental track "Rumble."
