## curiosities
**Bebop Co-Inventor** : Dizzy Gillespie was one of the musicians, alongside Charlie Parker, who were inventing a new form of jazz, later known as bebop or bop, in New York between 1942 and 1944. This form, which occurred during the Musicians' Union Strike, is considered some of the most important music of the 20th century, though its birth was not recorded. **Defining Figure of Bebop** : Dizzy Gillespie is listed as one of the pivotal figures whose musical contributions defined bebop. This distinct jazz style, which developed in New York during the mid-1940s, was characterized by its "harmonic complexity, instrumental virtuosity, and individual self-expression." **Pioneer of Bebop Jazz** : Dizzy Gillespie was instrumental in the creation of bebop, an experimental and complex jazz style that prioritized musical virtuosity and improvisation. This new direction in jazz was made by musicians for musicians and marked a clear break from the popular big band format.
