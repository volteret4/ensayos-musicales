## albums
**Audio of Being (Implied ~2001) - Challenging Recording Period** : The album following `Beautiful Midnight`, its creation was fraught with difficulty due to intensifying internal band politics, Matthew Good's severe sarcoidosis and anxiety, and a contentious recording process. Matthew worked 14 hours a day writing in Whistler, but rehearsals and recording were plagued by band members seeking publishing credits, leading to Matthew being physically ill during vocal takes. "we're into the audio of being, which was awkward. Well, I threw up that entire year, every day." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=--g0gugy3ZE
**Beautiful Midnight (1999) - Highly Successful Record** : Released on September 14, 1999, this album contained major hits like `Load Me Up`, `Hello Time Bomb`, and `Strange Days`. Despite its high success, its potential for even greater achievement was hampered by significant internal band dysfunction and Matthew Good's principled refusal to engage in what he perceived as "games" within the American music industry, which affected its U.S. release. "Beautiful midnight came out on September 14, 1999, and it was loaded with it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=--g0gugy3ZE
**Underdogs (Implied Pre-1999) - Predecessor Album** : This album is mentioned as the creative starting point that `Beautiful Midnight` built upon, suggesting it was an earlier successful release. "beautiful midnight certainly picks up where underdogs take off" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=--g0gugy3ZE

## curiosities
**Breakup in 2001** : The Matthew Good Band dissolved around 2001, an outcome of pervasive internal dysfunction, Matthew's illness, and an unwillingness to tour the `Audio of Being` record. The band members had reached a point where communication broke down, and individuals asserted their indispensability, leading Matthew, who usually tried to "placate" others, to finally end the group. "And that was the end of the Matthew Good Band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=--g0gugy3ZE
**Dave's Disregard for Band Work** : During the writing phase for `Audio of Being` in Whistler, Dave (likely Dave Genn) arrived late because he was busy working with "Hollywood" (presumably Holly McNarland) and in "Ireland." Matthew Good expressed frustration, believing Dave should have prioritized band commitments given their success (a near Triple Platinum album). "Dave came up for a little bit. But unfortunately, he was a little late because he was busy with Hollywood and Ireland working with Hollywood." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=--g0gugy3ZE
**Dysfunctional Tour and Matthew's 30th Birthday** : The tour supporting `Audio of Being` was marked by extreme dysfunction, with band members refusing to speak to one another. Matthew's 30th birthday occurred on this tour in "Gogory," where he avoided his bandmates by leaving a celebratory cake in his hotel room and going to the Corral Center. He also sustained a back injury in a car accident during the tour. "The whole tour was ridiculous." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=--g0gugy3ZE
**Featured in "In Their Own Words" Series** : Matthew Good Band was featured in the "In Their Own Words" series, a program that brings band members into the studio to share their personal history. "Matthew Good, Finger Leaven..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bI6TTA-fn7c
**Internal Dysfunction and Political Dynamics** : The Matthew Good Band suffered from significant internal dysfunction, described as "very political and weird," particularly during the creation of `Audio of Being`. This included members vying for publishing rights by injecting ideas and a general lack of communication during tours, ultimately contributing to the band's dissolution. "It might have been even more successful had the Matthew Good band been functioning properly. There was a lot of internal dysfunction." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=--g0gugy3ZE
**Publishing Rights Conflicts** : During rehearsals for `Audio of Being`, band members deliberately tried to inject their ideas into songs, not purely for creative reasons, but "because they know that if they do, then they can make a case for publishing." This self-serving behavior significantly "hampered" the entire creative and recording process. "People are trying to inject ideas because they know that if they do, then they can make a case for publishing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=--g0gugy3ZE
**Top Billing Misperception** : The band was publicly perceived as primarily "Matthew Good," giving him top billing despite his personal preference against it. This public misperception was a factor in the band's identity and internal dynamics. "The Matthew Good Band was a group that gave Matt top billing, something that he never wanted. This all came as the result of a public misperception." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=--g0gugy3ZE
