## curiosities
**"Bill Martin" Alias and "Piano Man" Origin Story** : Following the problematic release of *Cold Spring Harbor*, he departed New York for Los Angeles, where he spent six months performing as a lounge singer under the alias "Bill Martin." During this period, he gathered observations and wrote songs about the people he encountered, directly inspiring his signature song "Piano Man." "He left New York for Los Angeles where he spent six months as a piano man. He was a lounge singer yes but a songwriter too. **Early Struggles and Unwavering Musical Dedication** : Before achieving fame, Billy Joel endured significant hardship, including "slept on the floor of a laundromat," which underscored his deep dedication to music. **Perspective on Popular Music (1980)** : In 1980, Billy Joel observed that the fundamental appeal of popular music, including rock and roll, pop, funk, lies initially in its "beat" and "melody," with lyrics being something listeners dig into later if they truly enjoy the song. He applied this to "My Sharona," noting its "pubescent dumbbo words" fit the song's overall appeal. "Rock and roll pop funk. They can all be so many things, both reflective and reactive. But let's remember the essence of popular music. A song comes on. What do you hear first? Words? Nah, you hear a beat. **Resistance to Commercialism and Industry Pressures (1975)** : At 26 years old in 1975, Billy Joel vocalized a strong aversion to being "commercial" and "play[ing] the game" of the music industry. He explicitly refused to host shows like *Midnight Special* or engage with personalities like Wolfman Jack, seeing them as "operating in a bygone era." He vowed to make music "my own way or not at all," prioritizing his artistic integrity over "stupid flashing lights" or contrived appearances. "I get people saying to me all the time Billy you could be a giant a real superstar if you'd only play the game hype yourself be a little more commercial don't be so cynical like everyone really got upset when I told the people from the midnight special. That I wouldn't think of hosting a show like theirs are sitting down and talking to a jerk like Wolfman Jack." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 51 - The Softer Side of 70s Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-51-the-softer-side-of-70s-rock ← billy-joel
