**52nd Street** : When the first 50 CD titles were released in Japan on October 1, 1982, Billy Joel's `52nd Street` was at the very top of that list, determined by the sequence of catalog numbers. This indicates its prominence in the initial rollout of the compact disk format. `"And if we go by the sequence of catalog numbers, the CD at the top of that list was Billy Joel's 52nd Street."` ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OuFvfZJKcpc
**52nd Street (1982) - Possible First Commercially Available CD (Japan)** : Billy Joel's album "52nd Street" went on sale in Japan in October 1982, and it might have been one of the very first commercially available Compact Discs to hit the market. However, there is ongoing dispute regarding the definitive first commercially released CD. "Maybe it's Billy Joel's 52nd Street." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gP3oQeYEvso
**52nd Street (1982) – First of Sony Japan's Initial 50 CD Titles** : According to legend, "52nd Street" was the first of 50 titles simultaneously issued by Sony Japan on October 1, 1982, marking a significant early release in the format's history. "On October 1, 1982, Sony Japan issued 50 titles all at once. And according to legend, the first of those 50 was 52nd Street from Billy Joel." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BHkJFA0LMro
**52nd Street (Year not specified)** : Billy Joel's album *52nd Street* was the first album to be released on CD as a reissue. This historic release signaled the beginning of artists' existing catalogs being transferred to the new digital format. ## curiosities
**Massive Career Based on Piano Songs** : Billy Joel achieved a massive career predominantly through songs composed for and performed on the piano. He is recognized alongside other artists for building a successful career heavily reliant on the piano as his main instrument. "We do have Elton John and Billy Joel and Carol King. **Possible Shared Recording with Iggy Pop** : Legend suggests that Billy Joel may have played piano on the Shangri-La's song "Leader of the Pack" on the same record that Iggy Pop allegedly played drums. However, they were never in the studio together; Iggy was paid and sent home before Billy Joel recorded his part. "It's very possible that Iggy Pop played on the same record as Billy Joel." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OxctiPr0l-8
