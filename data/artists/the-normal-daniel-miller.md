## songs
**Warm Leatherette (1978)** : Released in November 1978 under the name The Normal, this song was recorded by Daniel Miller in his bedroom using a Korg 700S synthesizer and a 4-track reel-to-reel machine, despite his lack of traditional musical experience. It became a hit, selling 30,000 copies after Rough Trade record shop in Notting Hill agreed to stock a test pressing, intriguing many young musicians with its minimalist, futuristic, and attitude-filled sound. "After it came out in November 1978, the record became a hit." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY

## curiosities
**Bedroom Punk Aesthetic with Synthesizers** : Daniel Miller, aspiring to be a punk but unable to play guitar, bought a Korg 700S and a 4-track reel-to-reel machine from his film editor savings. He recorded "Warm Leatherette" in his bedroom, creating a minimalist, futuristic sound with attitude, which sold 30,000 copies. This demonstrated that musical experience wasn't necessary for creating impactful electronic music, embodying a punk sensibility of DIY attitude over traditional ability. "He started experimenting with it in his bedroom. Recorded two songs, found some money to make a test pressing and convinced the rough-trade record shop in Notting Hill to stock it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
**Minimalist Sound as a Contrast** : The minimalist sound of Daniel Miller's "Warm Leatherette" served as a benchmark against which the later, more sonically advanced and textured Technopop songs from 1980-81 were compared, highlighting the rapid technological progress in a short period. "Remember what warm leatherettes sounded like? Now compare that minimalism to something like this, which came out less than two years ago." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
