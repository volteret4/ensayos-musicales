## curiosities
**"No Hugging, No Learning" Philosophy** : David would often say on the Seinfeld set, "there was no hugging, no learning," a mantra that defined the characters' static nature, contributing to their humor. "As Larry David would often say on set, there was no hugging, no learning." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6O5TCTO-go4
**Seinfeld Reunion on Curb Your Enthusiasm** : David had previously refused to do a Seinfeld reunion, but he found it funny to integrate one into *Curb Your Enthusiasm*, seeing it as the "perfect way to do it, but not really do it." The entire season revolved around a fictionalized reboot of the original series, utilizing reconstructed original sets like Jerry's apartment and Monk's Cafe, with Jerry's apartment updated to reflect the 11 years that had passed since the finale. This storyline finally gave fans the Seinfeld ending they had hoped for. "David previously refused to make a Seinfeld reunion, but he thought it would be funny to do it on Curb, because it would be the perfect way to do it, but not really do it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6O5TCTO-go4
**Show's Origin as a Late-Night Special** : In 1989, NBC approached Larry David and Jerry Seinfeld not for a sitcom, but to write and produce a 90-minute late-night special. Instead, they wrote a 30-minute script for a pilot, "The Seinfeld Chronicles." "When NBC approached Jerry Seinfeld and Larry David in 1989, the network wasn't actually looking for a sitcom. They asked the writing duo to write and produce a 90-minute late-night special." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6O5TCTO-go4
