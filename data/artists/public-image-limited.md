## curiosities
**Categorization in New Wave (Early 1980s)** : Categorized under New Wave acts who preferred "darker guitars" alongside Joy Division and The Cure. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
**Drummer Anecdote** : There is a story that Jah Wobble hated a particular drummer so much that he "set him on fire." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=95clNVd0hYg
**Dub Influence** : Jah Wobble's playing was heavily influenced by dub, a Jamaican style characterized by slow, low rhythms with a deep, deep groove. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=95clNVd0hYg
**Emotional Motivation for Reunion (2009)** : Further motivated by personal losses: his father died of cancer in 2008, and his brother's cancer, which had been in remission, recurred, reminding him of his mother's death, which inspired the song "Death Disco." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJGFUlQTUhQ
**Flea's Audition (1984)** : Michael Balsari, later known as Flea, auditioned for bass in the band in 1984 and was offered the gig, but decided to stick with his new band forming with Anthony (Red Hot Chili Peppers). ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJGFUlQTUhQ
**Formation (Christmas Day, 1978)** : Formed by Johnny Lydon (aka Johnny Rotten of the Sex Pistols) on Christmas Day, 1978, as an experimental post-Pistols project to explore new approaches to rock. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJGFUlQTUhQ
**Impact on Genres** : Had a profound effect on several genres of music, including industrial and punk funk. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJGFUlQTUhQ
**Influenced Artists** : Influenced Nine Inch Nails, Massive Attack, Manic Street Preachers, Ministry, and Primal Scream. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJGFUlQTUhQ
**Johnny Lydon's Post-Band Activities** : Johnny Lydon ended up reuniting with the Sex Pistols and participated in a variety of other musical projects. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJGFUlQTUhQ
**Joining Public Image Limited** : After the Sex Pistols broke up, Johnny Lydon chose Jah Wobble to be the bass player in his new group, Public Image Limited. This was considered a risky choice because Wobble tended to be "a little prickly." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=95clNVd0hYg
**Last Gig and Breakup (September 18, 1992)** : Their final gig was on September 18, 1992, becoming their last show when their label, Virgin Records, refused to pay for any more tour support. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJGFUlQTUhQ
**Motivation for Reunion (Summer 2009)** : Johnny Lydon decided to resurrect Public Image Limited due to the 30th-anniversary re-release of their influential "Metal Box" album. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJGFUlQTUhQ
**Musical Influences** : Johnny Lydon explored reggae, dub, 1970s German rock, and hung out with funk musicians, developing a unique and experimental sound. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJGFUlQTUhQ
**Pioneering World Music** : After leaving Public Image Limited, he delved deeply into world music long before many others, working with musicians from as far away as China. This made him very important in this area, and people have sought him out for collaborations and to play on their recordings due to his skilled and versatile approach. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=95clNVd0hYg
**Real Name and Nickname Origin** : Jah Wobble's real name is John Wardle. He was one of "four Johns" who hung out in the punk days, alongside John Lydon (Johnny Rotten) and John Ritchie (Sid Vicious). His nickname, "Jawwabel," came early, possibly due to his "wobbly" playing style, or supposedly given by Sid Vicious when drunk. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=95clNVd0hYg
**Reunion Announcement (September 7, 2009)** : The actual reunion announcement was made on September 7, 2009, following 16 years, 11 months, and 20 days apart. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJGFUlQTUhQ
**Reunion Lineup (2009)** : While Johnny Lydon was "out front," the rest of the band members were different from the original lineup, but it was still considered "Johnny's band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJGFUlQTUhQ
