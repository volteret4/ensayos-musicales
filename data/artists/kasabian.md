## curiosities
**Lost Generation / Transition Point** : Kasabian, along with Arctic Monkeys, is considered part of a "lost generation" or transitional phase in the music industry. They represent the shift from an industry dominated by record labels to a more intermediate process where artists exert greater control and primarily use labels for distribution. "deberíamos considerar a gente como a Casabian o como a los artichmonks, ese es la bomb perdido o esa transición, que empieza de una industria discográfica que domina todo el poder de la música a un proceso intermedio donde los artistas deciden todo lo que quieren hacer y usan a las discográficas para distribuir su material." ← Music Radar Clan > Arctic Monkeys： Cómo nació un mito. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t7SqB6i2xzE&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc ← kasabian
**Part of New Rock Wave** : Kasabian was among the groups born from the new wave of rock music. They became very famous via the internet and, despite their quality, progressively assimilated into the established norms of the recording industry. "Tenemos casos como casabian que también se hicieron muy famosos gracias a Internet... han ido entrando poco a poco más en los estándares de la industria discográfica." ← Music Radar Clan > Arctic Monkeys： Cómo nació un mito. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t7SqB6i2xzE&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc ← kasabian
**Tom Meighan Dismissed July 2020 After Assault Conviction; Serge Pizzorno New Frontman from October 2021** : Kasabian formed in 1997 and carved out a successful career in the UK over more than a decade. In July 2020, founding singer and member Tom Meighan announced he was leaving — a mutual decision following his admission in court that he had assaulted his ex-fiancée in front of their child, which was a clear red line for the rest of the band. He was permanently dismissed and severed from all band associations. Guitarist Serge Pizzorno took over as singer, with this new version of Kasabian making their public debut in October 2021.
