## curiosities
**Collaboration with Fender on Stratocaster for Surf Music** : Richard Monsour, known by his stage name Dick Dale, was a prominent figure in the emerging genre of Surf Music. Fender hired him as a consultant to broaden their customer base beyond country music. With Dale's input, the Fender Stratocaster became an indispensable element of the surf music sound. "Monsour, who used the stage name Dick Dale, was big into this budding thing called Surf Music. And with his help, the Fender Stratocaster became an essential part of that surf music sound." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=E6cXyaHALBQ
**First 100-Watt Guitar Amp (1962)** : In 1962, surf guitar king Dick Dale collaborated with Fender to create and produce the first ever 100-watt guitar amplifier. This was a significant achievement, allowing for a powerful, overdriven sound when set to its limits. "In 1962, surf guitar king Dick Dale worked with Fender to produce the first ever 100 watt guitar amp, a very big deal, setting everything to overdrive created a sound like this." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w
**Lack of Top 40 Hits but Powerful Sound** : Despite never achieving a Top 40 hit, Dick Dale's powerful guitar sound left a lasting impression on anyone who heard him play. "Now Dick Dale never had a top 40 hit, but anyone who heard him play the guitar never forgot that sound of power." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U
**Left-Handed, Upside-Down Guitar Playing** : Dick Dale played his guitar left-handed and upside down, developing new sounds that led him to become a consultant for the Fender Company. In this role, he tested amplifiers and portable reverb units, contributing to their development. "Dick played his guitar left handed and upside down." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U
**Legendary Surf Guitarist Participant** : Dick Dale, recognized as a legendary surf guitarist from the 1960s, was among the seasoned musicians who played the Warped Tour. "Dick Dale, the legendary surf guitarist from the 1960s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Surf Music as a Precursor to Heavy Rock** : Surf Music, exemplified by recordings from 1962, is identified as containing elements that could be seen as precursors to the future sound of heavy rock.
