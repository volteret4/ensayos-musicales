## songs
**Let's Go Trippin' (1961)** : Released in 1961, this instrumental marked a significant step in the formation of the surf sound, characterized by three chords and a twangy guitar. It also incorporated Middle Eastern influences, which Dale learned from his Lebanese mother. The song was first played in Newport Beach, California, where surfing was popular, became a regional hit, and then spread across North America, solidifying Dale's role in coining the term "Surf Music." "Dale learned some of those middle eastern licks from his Lebanese sample." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pOeChe_D1Ag
**Misirlou (1963)** : This instrumental, released in 1963, is a cover of a 1920s piece of music. Dick Dale's version prominently features his signature Middle Eastern influences and twangy guitar style. It later gained widespread recognition from its inclusion in the soundtrack of Quentin Tarantino's film, *Pulp Fiction*. "You'll recognize it from the soundtrack of Pulp Fiction." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pOeChe_D1Ag

## curiosities
**Coined "Surf Music"** : Dick Dale is credited with coining the term "Surf Music," marking the beginning of the original surf music craze and its association with California culture. "It was Dick Dale who coined the term Surf Music, and this was the beginning of the original surf music craze and part of the obsession with California." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pOeChe_D1Ag
**Collaboration with Fender on Stratocaster for Surf Music** : Richard Monsour, known by his stage name Dick Dale, was a prominent figure in the emerging genre of Surf Music. Fender hired him as a consultant to broaden their customer base beyond country music. With Dale's input, the Fender Stratocaster became an indispensable element of the surf music sound. "Monsour, who used the stage name Dick Dale, was big into this budding thing called Surf Music. And with his help, the Fender Stratocaster became an essential part of that surf music sound." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=E6cXyaHALBQ
**First 100-Watt Guitar Amp (1962)** : In 1962, surf guitar king Dick Dale collaborated with Fender to create and produce the first ever 100-watt guitar amplifier. This was a significant achievement, allowing for a powerful, overdriven sound when set to its limits. "In 1962, surf guitar king Dick Dale worked with Fender to produce the first ever 100 watt guitar amp, a very big deal, setting everything to overdrive created a sound like this." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w
**Lack of Top 40 Hits but Powerful Sound** : Despite never achieving a Top 40 hit, Dick Dale's powerful guitar sound left a lasting impression on anyone who heard him play. "Now Dick Dale never had a top 40 hit, but anyone who heard him play the guitar never forgot that sound of power." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U
**Left-Handed, Upside-Down Guitar Playing** : Dick Dale played his guitar left-handed and upside down, developing new sounds that led him to become a consultant for the Fender Company. In this role, he tested amplifiers and portable reverb units, contributing to their development. "Dick played his guitar left handed and upside down." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U
**Legendary Surf Guitarist Participant** : Dick Dale, recognized as a legendary surf guitarist from the 1960s, was among the seasoned musicians who played the Warped Tour. "Dick Dale, the legendary surf guitarist from the 1960s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Surf Music as a Precursor to Heavy Rock** : Surf Music, exemplified by recordings from 1962, is identified as containing elements that could be seen as precursors to the future sound of heavy rock. "In fact, if you listen to songs like this, you can maybe hear the future of Heavy Rock. This however, is from 1962." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=E6cXyaHALBQ
**Unique Picking Style and Gear** : Dale developed a distinctive picking style influenced by jazz drummer Gene Krupa. He used heavy gauge strings and played through incredibly loud custom-made Fender amplifiers to achieve his sound. "He had a picking style influenced by jazz drummer Jean Croupeau. He used heavy strings and super loud custom made amps by Fender." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pOeChe_D1Ag
