## curiosities
**Burned One Million Pounds in Cash – Jura, Scotland, August 23, 1994** : After a series of deliberately provocative stunts (including dumping a dead sheep and eight gallons of blood at an awards show after-party) and announcing their retirement from music until world peace was achieved, Bill Drummond and Jimmy Cauty of the KLF deleted their entire back catalogue and made what is believed to be the single largest cash withdrawal in UK banking history. They nailed one million pounds in £50 notes to a board, paraded it around England as an artwork called *Nailed to the Wall*, then on the evening of August 23, 1994, traveled to the island of Jura in Scotland with one journalist and one cameraman. In an abandoned boathouse, they set fire to the entire million pounds. A documentary called *Watch the KLF Foundation Burn a Million Quid* exists on YouTube. The pair have never fully explained their motivation beyond vague references to satire of materialism and contempt for the music industry.
