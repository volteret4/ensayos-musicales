## songs
**Strange Fruit (1939) – Anti-Lynching Song; Banned by Racists; Sent to Southern Senators Who Refused to Outlaw Lynching** : "Strange Fruit" was a landmark 1939 anti-lynching song that was banned in some places by racists who considered it too incendiary. Activists sent copies to Southern senators who refused to outlaw lynching. It created the template for anti-racism and civil rights protest songs that followed. ## curiosities
**Babysitter for Billy Crystal** : Legendary singer Billie Holiday used to babysit actor Billy Crystal. This connection arose because Holiday recorded for Commodore Records, a label co-owned by Crystal's father. **Discovery by Youth** : A 21-year-old Humber College student recently discovered the music of Billie Holiday, despite the artist having died in 1959. This anecdote highlights how streaming gives modern youth access to the entire history of recorded music, leading to ecumenical tastes and the discovery of artists from various eras.
