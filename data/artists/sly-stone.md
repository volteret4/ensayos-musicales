## curiosities
**Burden of African American Stardom** : The immense expectations placed on Sly Stone, not only as a superstar but specifically as an African American superstar, were perceived to be a significant burden on him. Questlove's documentary explores this aspect in greater depth. "ultimately the expectations on Sly to be not just a superstar but an African American one I think proved to be too much of a burden." ← For the Record - The 70s > FTR70 Sound Bite - Remembering Sly Stone | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Clyde Davis's Influence** : Following the commercial failure of Sly and the Family Stone's first album, "A Whole New Thing," Sly Stone was encouraged by CBS Records executive Clyde Davis to write music more suited for radio. This directive led to the creation of songs like "Dance to the Music." "after that Sly wrote more for the radio because well that's what CBS records executive Clyde Davis told him to do giving us dance to the music." ← For the Record - The 70s > FTR70 Sound Bite - Remembering Sly Stone | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Death and Legacy** : Sly Stone passed away on Monday, June 9th, 2025, at the age of 82, a lifespan many considered longer than expected given his personal struggles. His notorious drug use, subsequent erratic behavior, and frequent missed shows are cited as reasons why he has not been collectively remembered as the musical genius he clearly was. "No doubt 82 was longer than many people ever expected him to live." ← For the Record - The 70s > FTR70 Sound Bite - Remembering Sly Stone | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Influence on Bee Gees' Bass Sound** : Sly Stone's bass sound was openly emulated by the Bee Gees on their 1975 number one single "Jive Talkin'," indicating his foundational impact on popular music. **Musical Inactivity Period** : Sly Stone effectively dropped off the musical radar in 1982, remaining largely inactive until the publication of his memoir and Questlove's documentary brought renewed attention to his work. "When musically he dropped off the radar in 1982 that was really kind of it until he was he published his memoir and then Quest Love did the documentary." ← For the Record - The 70s > FTR70 Sound Bite - Remembering Sly Stone | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Questlove's Documentary** : A documentary about Sly Stone, titled "Sly Lives," directed by Questlove, began streaming on Hulu approximately six months prior to Stone's death. This film delves into the "demons" and personal burdens faced by Sly Stone, including the significant pressure of being not just a superstar, but an African American one. "You could go watch Quest Love's documentary on Sly Sly Lives, which began streaming on Hulu about six months ago." ← For the Record - The 70s > FTR70 Sound Bite - Remembering Sly Stone | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Sly Stone Death (2025) – COPD After Living in a Van and $5M Settlement** : Sly Stone, pioneer of funk, soul, and rock, died in 2025 of COPD. After years of personal struggles that included living in a camper van, he won a $5 million copyright lawsuit that allowed a brief return to public life before his death. "After years of struggles and living in a camper van, won a $5 million copyright lawsuit." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZPlsR7_WiZQ ← sly-stone
**Template for 70s Music** : Sly Stone is recognized for providing a foundational template for the music of the 1970s, particularly through his innovative use of instrumentation and sound. His work showed what was musically possible and influenced subsequent acts.
