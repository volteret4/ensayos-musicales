## curiosities
**1987 Lineage** : The band is cited as a modern example of the gothic genre whose compositional roots can be traced directly back to the 12th-century innovations of Hildegard von Bingen and the acoustics of medieval cathedrals. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
**Band Name Origin** : The band took their name from Leonard Cohen's song "Sisters of Mercy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
**Dr. Avalanche's Evolution** : Dr. Avalanche, the band's drum machine, was named because it frequently crashed. There have been multiple iterations of Dr. Avalanche, starting with a Boss DR-55, followed by Roland TR-606 and TR-808 models, and eventually a special custom MacBook partly designed by the British military. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
**Early Covers** : In their early days, the band was known for covering various artists, including The Stooges, Bob Dylan, A Chocolate-A-Fun Band (likely a transcription error for a different band name), The Velvet Underground, and a "killer version" of "Louie Louie." They also released a 12-inch version of a Rolling Stones classic, transforming it for goth fans. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
**Formation and Contribution (1980)** : Formed in Leeds in 1980, during the earliest days of goth, The Sisters of Mercy became one of the most important bands in the genre. They were known for making "doom and gloom rock in an interesting way." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
**Jim Steinman Collaboration (Late 1980s)** : In the late 1980s, The Sisters of Mercy teamed up with songwriter Jim Steinman, known for his work on Meat Loaf's *Bat Out of Hell* and Bonnie Tyler's "Total Eclipse of the Heart," showcasing an unexpected musical cross-pollination. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
**Troublesome Drum Machine** : The band toured with a drum machine they nicknamed "Dr. Abelash" because it frequently crashed during performances. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**Volatile Lineup** : Beyond frontman Andrew Eldritch and the drum machine Dr. Avalanche, the band has had close to 20 ex-members. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
