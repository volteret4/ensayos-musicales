## albums
**21 Pilots (2009) – Self-Titled Debut Album** : A self-produced indie record released on December 29, 2009, featuring the original lineup of Tyler Joseph, Nick Thomas, and Chris Salih. Despite being recorded at home with low-key promotion, it managed to sell approximately 115,000 copies. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8sAxFvktx-A
**Blurryface (2015) – Fourth Album & Breakthrough** : This 2015 release served as the band's major commercial breakthrough, selling nearly 2 million copies—a significant feat in the streaming era. The album introduced a complex narrative centered on a character named "Blurryface," who personified the frontman's insecurities. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8sAxFvktx-A
**Regional at Best (2011) – Discontinued Second Album** : Released on July 8, 2011, this project was primarily a solo effort by Tyler Joseph, who described it as a "glorified mixtape". As a condition of their signing with Fueled by Ramen, the album was completely discontinued and wiped from digital platforms. Physical copies have become highly collectible, trading for between $35 and $900. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zUQ2vZzmRiI
**Regional at Best (2011) – Second Indie Album** : Released on July 8, 2011, this project was primarily a solo effort by Tyler Joseph, who described it as a "glorified mixtape". As a condition of their later record deal, the album was "completely discontinued" and wiped from digital platforms. Today, physical copies are highly valuable, selling for between $35 and $900. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8sAxFvktx-A
**Trench (2018) – Fifth Studio Album** : Released in 2018, this album expanded the band's lore into a fictional world involving a city called "Dema" and a resistance group known as the "Banditos". Its themes delve into doubt, anxiety, faith, and suicide. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8sAxFvktx-A
**Vessel (2013) – Third Studio Album** : Released on January 8, 2013, under Fueled by Ramen, this album expanded the band's fanbase significantly and led to major festival slots at Lollapalooza and Bonnaroo. It received moderate radio play, which helped transition the band into the mainstream. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8sAxFvktx-A

## curiosities
**Banditos' Color** : In the _Trench_ mythology, the resistance group known as the Banditos uses a specific shade of yellow because the oppressive "bishops" of Dema are unable to perceive it. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zUQ2vZzmRiI
**Blurryface Identity** : In the _Trench_ lore, the character Blurryface is revealed to be "Niko" (Nicolas Bourbaki), the chief bishop of the fictional city Dema. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8sAxFvktx-A
**Origin of Band Name** : The name is derived from Arthur Miller’s 1947 play _All My Sons_, which details a true story of 21 pilots who died due to faulty aircraft engines approved by a corrupt businessman. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8sAxFvktx-A
**Stylized Branding** : The omission of a hyphen in "21 pilots" was a specific design choice by Tyler Joseph for visual adaptability in logos. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8sAxFvktx-A
**The Banditos' Color** : The resistance group in the _Trench_ narrative uses a specific shade of yellow because the oppressive "bishops" are unable to perceive that color. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8sAxFvktx-A
**The Skeleton Clique** : The band's dedicated fanbase is known as the "Skeleton Clique". Members often refer to each other as "frens" and use the phrase "Stay Street". ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8sAxFvktx-A
**dmaorg.info Mystery** : In 2018, fans discovered a hidden website hidden in a digital image on the band's store. The site contained cryptic "404 error" warnings and journal entries from a character named Clancy, leading to the _Trench_ album reveal. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8sAxFvktx-A
