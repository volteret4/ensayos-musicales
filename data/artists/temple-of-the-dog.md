## albums
**Temple of the Dog (1991) – Self-Titled Tribute Album** : This album was recorded in just 15 days and released on April 16, 1991, as a tribute to Andrew Wood, the deceased singer of Mother Love Bone. It featured Chris Cornell, Matt Cameron, Jeff Ament, Stone Gossard, Mike McCready, and an unproven Eddie Vedder. Although it initially received great reviews but little attention, it later went multi-platinum worldwide after Soundgarden and Pearl Jam achieved massive success. "The Temple of the Dog album was recorded in just 15 days and released on April 16, 1991." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lip0bZX7hBE
**Temple of the Dog (Undated) – Self-Titled Tribute Album** : A tribute album recorded by friends of Andrew Wood after his death. The project featured Chris Cornell and Matt Cameron from Soundgarden, former Mother Love Bone members Jeff Ament and Stone Gossard, Jeff McCready, and Eddie Vedder, retrospectively forming a Seattle Grunge Supergroup with members of Soundgarden and future Pearl Jam. "They call this project Temple of the Dog." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tFBSrKMEZxI

## songs
**Hunger Strike (1990) – Unprompted Eddie Vedder Duet** : During the recording of this song, while Chris Cornell was laying down vocals, Eddie Vedder spontaneously picked up a microphone and began singing the low harmonies without being asked. This impromptu contribution solidified the legendary duet between the two future rock giants, recorded at a time when neither Soundgarden nor the nascent Pearl Jam were widely known. "But in the midst of Chris recording the vocals for the song that we're about to hear, Eddie just picked up a mic and started singing the low parts. Nobody really asked him to do it, he just kind of did it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lip0bZX7hBE

## curiosities
**Initial Lack of Attention, Later Multi-Platinum Success – Post-Hype Phenomenon** : Despite receiving great reviews upon its release in April 1991, the Temple of the Dog album was largely ignored by the public. However, once Mookie Blaylock changed its name to Pearl Jam and both Soundgarden and Pearl Jam achieved massive global success, interest in the album exploded, leading it to sell multi-platinum worldwide. "It got great reviews, but nobody paid much attention until Mookie Blaylock changed their name to Pearl Jam and, well, you know, that story." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lip0bZX7hBE
**Seattle Grunge Supergroup** : The project is considered, in retrospect, a Seattle Grunge Supergroup, combining members who were already established in Soundgarden with others who would go on to form Pearl Jam. "Temple of the Dog, what in retrospect is a Seattle Grunge Supergroup featuring members of Soundgarden and future members of Pearl Jam." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tFBSrKMEZxI
**Tribute to Andrew Wood** : The band formed to record a tribute album for Andrew Wood, the deceased singer of Mother Love Bone, an event crucial to the coalescence of the Seattle grunge scene. "Some of his friends came together to record a tribute album." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tFBSrKMEZxI
**Tribute to Andrew Wood – Origin of the Project** : Temple of the Dog was formed as a quick tribute to Andrew Wood, Chris Cornell's former roommate and the singer of Mother Love Bone, who tragically died in March 1990. Cornell wrote several songs in his friend's honor, which he then shared with the surviving members of Mother Love Bone, leading to the formation of this supergroup. "When he got back to Seattle, he showed the surviving members of Mother Love Bone, the songs. When other materials started flowing, they decided to record a quick tribute album under the name Temple of the Dog." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lip0bZX7hBE
