## curiosities
**Formation and Early Rehearsals** : Formed in 1983 in Minneapolis, this three-piece band was named after a Swedish board game. Their first rehearsals involved stumbling through Ramones covers in bassist Greg Norton's basement. "At about the same time, Husker Dew was stumbling through Ramones covers during their first rehearsals and bassist Greg Norton's basement, another important Minneapolis band was coming together in a basement on Bryant Street South." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Influence on Hardcore Punk** : Husker Dew had a huge influence on hardcore punk across America. They appeared on stage in street clothes, eschewing typical punk attire like leather jackets or mohawk haircuts. "Husker Dew would ultimately have a huge influence on hardcore punk across America." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Influence on Major Label Strategy** : The band was part of a group of artists (including Sonic Youth and Dinosaur Jr.) that major labels, staffed by former college radio enthusiasts, were interested in signing in the late 1980s as an experiment to see if these indie bands could be commercially viable. "They were influenced of wanting to bring bands like us or Husker Doe or Dinosaur Jr. or whatever." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xdn31jMOOA4
**Musical Approach** : Although their music was loud and fast, Husker Dew's songs incorporated elements of a basic pop song, giving melody and song structure equal standing with power and volume. This approach pushed the parameters of punk forward. "And although their music was loud and fast, the songs incorporated elements of a basic pop song. Melody and song structure was given equal standing with power and volume." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Musical Influence on Grunge** : Nirvana's "Nevermind" album was described as a refinement of the music first created by bands like Husker Dew and Sonic Youth, indicating their foundational role in the development of the alternative sound. "Never mind was a refinement of the music first made by bands like Husker Doe and Sonic Youth." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xdn31jMOOA4
**Pioneering Indie-to-Major Transition** : Husker Dew was among the first indie bands to accept an offer to jump to a major label, a move that initially generated whispers of a "sell-out." However, by sticking to their creative vision, they demonstrated that it was possible to record for a major label while still maintaining an indie attitude. "Husker do had recently done the same thing. At first, there were whispers of sell-out, but both bands stuck to their creative guns." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Pioneering Major Label Transition** : Husker Dew was one of the early indie bands to make the jump from an independent label (SST) to a major label (Warner Brothers). This move was seen as a precedent for other alternative bands, like R.E.M. and Sonic Youth, seeking stability beyond the indie circuit. "Now, Husker Dew had done it, jumping from SST to Warner Brothers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xdn31jMOOA4
**Spreading Influence and Major Label Transition** : Thanks to endless touring and strong support from SST Records, Husker Dew's influence spread throughout North America. They became one of the first bands to rise through the indie ranks and sign with a major label, yet they maintained their uncompromising do-it-yourself ethic until the very end. "Thanks to endless touring and strong support from SST, Husker Dew was able to spread their influence to all corners of North America." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
