## curiosities
**Attack Progression and Hostage Taking** : The shooting inside the Bataclan lasted at least 10 minutes, followed by the gunmen reloading and resuming random firing, even shooting four survivors they found playing dead. Two gunmen ascended to the balconies, taking approximately 20 hostages, forcing some to act as lookouts, while the third remained downstairs. Police arrived at 10:15 PM and locked down the building. Negotiations over a cell phone for almost an hour focused on France's involvement in Syria. "The shooting lasted for at least 10 minutes before the gunman reloaded and then they started to shoot again at random." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pgchw2h87aw
**Band Name Origin (1998)** : Co-founded in 1998 by Josh Homme and Jesse Hughes, the band's name arose from a night spent listening to the Polish metal band Vader, leading someone to suggest playing "Death Metal, but like the Eagles." An alternative account states Josh Homme coined the phrase after someone in a bar mistakenly referred to Scorpions' "Winds of Change" as "Death Metal." "The name came from a night listening to a Polish metal band called Vader. And someone, no one is sure who, said, we should play Death Metal, but like the Eagles." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BvhjsTUIS2o
**Bataclan Attack (November 13, 2015)** : Josh Homme was not present at the Bataclan theater in Paris on November 13, 2015, when terrorists attacked an Eagles of Death Metal concert, resulting in the deaths of 89 people, due to his flexible touring schedule with the band. "That's why he wasn't at the Bataclan theater in Paris when terrorists took over the place on November 13th, 2015 and killed 89 people who were all at an Eagles of Deathmale show." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BvhjsTUIS2o
**Bataclan Massacre Eyewitness Account** : Jesse Hughes, the singer for Eagles of Death Metal, provided a powerful account of what he witnessed from the stage during the Bataclan Massacre on November 13, 2015. He recounted seeing acts of immense bravery, with people shielding loved ones from bullets, rather than any cowardice. This quote underlines the tragic heroism amidst the terror. "I didn't just see people getting murdered. I saw people jump up and jump in front of bullets for the ones they love and give their life. I never saw a single coward that night. I only saw bravery." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xid8CZf4oYo
**Bataclan Terrorist Attack (November 13, 2015)** : On November 13, 2015, a sold-out Eagles of Death Metal concert at the Bataclan in Paris, attended by about 1500 people, became the target of a terrorist attack. Three gunmen, dressed in black and wearing suicide vests, entered the venue at 9:49 PM, after shooting people outside, and opened fire with AK-47s into the general admission area during the guitar solo of "I Love the Devil," plunging the hall into chaos with muzzle flashes as the only light. "One of the most shocking terrorist attacks to ever hit Europe happened at a concert on November 13, 2015 at a Paris venue called the Bataclan." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pgchw2h87aw
**End of Siege and Casualties** : At 12:18 AM, the order was given to storm the gunmen, an operation that lasted three minutes. One gunman detonated a suicide belt, and the second was shot before he could detonate his. In total, 89 people died, including Eagles of Death Metal's tour manager. The trial for 20 men charged with plotting the various attacks, including at the Bataclan, concluded in 2022. "In the end, 89 people died, including the Eagles of Death Metal tour manager." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pgchw2h87aw
**Escape and Hiding Tactics During Attack** : During brief lulls in gunfire at the Bataclan, people desperately sought escape or refuge. Those in the balconies found safety in toilets or on the roof by forcing open a trap door, while others broke false ceilings to hide in metal rafters. Some barricaded themselves in dressing rooms with the band using a fridge and furniture, and others managed to escape through emergency exits behind the stage curtain, scrambling over deceased bodies. "Meanwhile, during that brief break in the gunfire when everybody was reloading, people tried to escape." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pgchw2h87aw
**Jesse Hughes' Testimony and Resilience** : Eagles of Death Metal singer Jesse Hughes, alongside other band members and 300 survivors, returned to Paris to testify in the trial following the Bataclan attack. He defiantly stated, "Evil did not win. You can't kill rock and roll," and three months after the shootings, the band returned to Paris to complete the interrupted show. "'Evil did not win,' he said. "'You can't kill rock and roll.'" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pgchw2h87aw
