## albums
**Déjà Vu (1970)** : Released on March 11, 1970, this was the band's second album and their first with Neil Young, who only played on half of its tracks. Critics considered it "too perfect" and expressed doubts about replicating its "beautiful harmonies" live, sometimes commenting that it "just wasn't messy enough." *Rolling Stone* magazine has listed *Déjà Vu* multiple times in its "top 500 albums of all time," most recently at number 220. ## curiosities
**"Godfathers of Soft Rock" Influence** : Crosby, Stills, Nash & Young are considered strong candidates for the "godfathers of soft rock" due to their significant influence on many mid-to-late 1970s bands that achieved hits with soft rock sounds, particularly those emphasizing vocal harmonies. "if there is such a thing as godfathers of soft rock I think you could make a very strong case for crossbeast is nation young so many of the bands of the mid to late 70s who had hits with the soft rock sound especially those who leaned into harmony credited CS and why with being a major influence." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 51 - The Softer Side of 70s Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-51-the-softer-side-of-70s-rock
**David Crosby's Harmonic Genius** : David Crosby, despite his "famously difficult" reputation, was a "musical genius" responsible for arranging most, if not all, of the harmonies on *Déjà Vu*. His work in this area "plants the seeds for the soft rock boom to come." "he arranged most if not all of the harmonies on this record and in doing so he plants the seeds for the soft rock boom to come." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 51 - The Softer Side of 70s Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-51-the-softer-side-of-70s-rock
**Influence on America and Misattribution** : The band America was frequently compared to CSNY, or even accused of being "CSNY knockoffs." When America's song "A Horse with No Name" was released in 1971, its sound was so similar that many listeners mistakenly believed it was a Neil Young track.
