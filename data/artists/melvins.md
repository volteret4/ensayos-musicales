**Gluey Porch Treatments (1987) - Early Grunge Defining Album** : Recorded after Melvins moved to Seattle and extensively mingled with local bands, this album is identified as potentially the first grunge album because it includes core elements defining grunge. It marked an evolution from their earlier thrash/hardcore style, incorporating a noisy, "macarra" (thuggish) sound with elements of classic rock and introspective, furious lyrics that prefigured Nirvana, Pearl Jam, and Soundgarden. The album's release on a local Seattle label, likely Sub Pop, solidified its place in the nascent grunge scene. ## curiosities
**Buzz Osborne's Recommendation to Nirvana (1990)** : Buzz Osborne, a member of The Melvins and a figure "idolized by Kurt Cobain," played a pivotal role in Dave Grohl joining Nirvana. Osborne recommended Dave for the open drummer slot after Scream broke up, directly leading to Dave's audition and subsequent entry into Nirvana. "But then Buzz Osborne of the Melvins, a guy idolized by Kurt Cobain, recommended him for the open drummer slot with Nirvana." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_AY5lS-FmlU ← melvins

**Evolution of Style** : Melvins evolved their style significantly around 1987, moving away from a thrash/hardcore sound. This evolution was influenced by the "rabioso" (furious) and introspective nature of Seattle bands, contrasting with the more "macarra" (thuggish) rock from places like Washington and California which were laying the groundwork for new punk bands like Blink-182 and Limp Bizkit.
