## curiosities
**Encounter with Johnny Rotten (1975)** : In 1975, Malcolm McLaren's clothing shop served as a significant location in music history. It was here that he encountered Johnny Rotten, who was wearing an "I Hate Pink Floyd" t-shirt, leading to Rotten's consideration for the lead singer role in the Sex Pistols. "Johnny Rotten walked into Malcolm McLaren's clothing shop in 1975. He was immediately considered for the job of lead singer of the sex pistols, based on the fact that he was wearing an eye-hate pink Floyd t-shirt." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-CT7yYXonIk
**New York Dolls Management** : Malcolm McLaren, owner of a "small, weird clothing shop in London," ventured into band management after encountering the New York Dolls during a clothing show in New York. When the Dolls were dropped from their record deal and sought new management, McLaren, despite his inexperience, stepped in with ambitious plans. He envisioned a radical image makeover for the band, proposing red leather attire instead of their previous spandex and makeup, and using a large Soviet flag as a live backdrop, a move that proved to be less appealing to their original fanbase. "His name was Malcolm McLaren, and he chanced upon the dolls when he was in New York for a clothing show." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
**Post-Dolls Plans** : After the New York Dolls ultimately fell apart following a couple of tours under his management, Malcolm McLaren returned to England. He carried with him "definite ideas on what he'd do next time," suggesting his experience with the Dolls significantly shaped his future endeavors in the music industry. "But all was not lost. Because Malcolm McLaren went back to his shop in England with some definite ideas on what he'd do next time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
