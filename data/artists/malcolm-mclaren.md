# artist - Malcolm McLaren

## member of
- Sex Pistols

## albums
**Duck Rock (1983)** : This album, released in 1983, featured Malcolm McLaren's exploration of rap and hip hop, sounds he discovered from New York in the early 80s. It included the World's Famous Supreme Team, a DJ duo credited with introducing the idea of scratching. *Duck Rock* was massively influential, particularly in the UK, and helped McLaren perpetrate hip hop similar to how he did with punk, but with fewer legal issues. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc

## songs
**Buffalo Gals (1983)** : This song from Malcolm McLaren's 1983 album *Duck Rock* is a notable example of his integration of rap and hip hop elements into his music. It became well-known for its distinctive vocal hook. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc

## curiosities
**Advocacy for Sid Vicious after Nancy's Death** : After Nancy Spungin's murder and Sid Vicious's arrest, Malcolm McLaren and Sid's mother immediately sought a high-profile lawyer for his defense. McLaren strongly believed Sid was innocent, theorizing that an unknown guest had robbed the couple and then stabbed Nancy, noting that $20,000 in royalty money was missing from their room. "McLaren thought that Sid was innocent. He had a theory. He thought that the couple was robbed by an unknown guest who then stabbed Nancy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LE4YenwmeXM
**British Airways Commercial Collaboration (1980s)** : Malcolm McLaren worked on TV advertisements for British Airways, famously arranging a piece called "The Flower Duet" with Greek composer Yanni. This piece was cribbed from the French opera *Lakmé* and became widely recognized as part of the "world's favorite airline" campaign. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**Collaboration with Vivienne Westwood** : Malcolm McLaren co-owned a clothing store in Chelsea, London, with his partner Vivienne Westwood. Initially named "Let It Rock," the store specialized in Teddyboy fashion, bondage gear, and sex toys. Following McLaren's inspiration from Richard Hell, the store was later renamed "Sex" and began selling creations that incorporated safety pins, which became an iconic element of early punk fashion. "Before Malcolm McLaren hooked up with the pistols, he and his partner Vivian Westwood owned a clothing store in the Chelsea area of London called Let It Rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1y2UuU-me_s
**Early Life and Fashion Career** : Born in Yemen and raised by his grandmother in London, Malcolm McLaren attended various art schools before deciding on a career in fashion. He was always a schemer and, along with his girlfriend Vivienne Westwood, opened a shop at 430 Kings Road in London, initially called Paradise Garage in 1971. The shop evolved through names like Too Fast to Live, Too Young to Die and Sex, becoming a hub for unusual clothing, including fetish and bondage gear. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**Embracing Hip Hop and Electronic Music (1980s)** : In the early 1980s, Malcolm McLaren discovered rap and hip hop from New York and became a major evangelist for the new sound, incorporating these elements into his own records. He later ventured into electronic and club music, releasing a series of singles and albums that achieved success on the dance charts. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**Encounter with Johnny Rotten (1975)** : In 1975, Malcolm McLaren's clothing shop served as a significant location in music history. It was here that he encountered Johnny Rotten, who was wearing an "I Hate Pink Floyd" t-shirt, leading to Rotten's consideration for the lead singer role in the Sex Pistols. "Johnny Rotten walked into Malcolm McLaren's clothing shop in 1975. He was immediately considered for the job of lead singer of the sex pistols, based on the fact that he was wearing an eye-hate pink Floyd t-shirt." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-CT7yYXonIk
**Film, TV, and Art Ventures (1980s-1990s)** : McLaren diversified his career into film and TV projects, serving as both a director and producer, and occasionally as a performer. He was also a painter and, in 1985, he took a job with CBS Films where he oversaw various productions, including a movie titled "Heavy Metal Serving Nazis." He was also involved in a biopic about Led Zeppelin manager Peter Grant. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**Final Illness and Legacy (2009-2010)** : In October 2009, Malcolm McLaren was diagnosed with mesothelioma, a form of lung cancer often linked to asbestos exposure. He died on April 8, 2010, at a hospital in Switzerland. His burial featured a coffin spray-painted with "Too Fast to Live, Too Young to Die," and Sid Vicious's version of "My Way" was played. His headstone encapsulates his philosophy with the epitaph: "Better a Spectacular Failure than a benign success." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**New York Dolls Management** : Malcolm McLaren, owner of a "small, weird clothing shop in London," ventured into band management after encountering the New York Dolls during a clothing show in New York. When the Dolls were dropped from their record deal and sought new management, McLaren, despite his inexperience, stepped in with ambitious plans. He envisioned a radical image makeover for the band, proposing red leather attire instead of their previous spandex and makeup, and using a large Soviet flag as a live backdrop, a move that proved to be less appealing to their original fanbase. "His name was Malcolm McLaren, and he chanced upon the dolls when he was in New York for a clothing show." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
**New York Dolls and Band Management** : After a trip to a New York fashion fair in 1973, Malcolm McLaren became deeply interested in the New York Dolls and decided to try his hand at band management. This initial venture was a disaster, and the Dolls soon broke up, but McLaren remained undeterred. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**Owner of "Sex" Clothing Shop** : Malcolm McLaren owned the clothing shop named "Sex" on Kings Road in Chelsea, London. This shop served as a hub for the early punk scene and was where he recruited John Lighten for the Sex Pistols. "All four hung around a clothing shop called Sex on the Kings Road in Chelsea, which is where owner Malcolm McLaren recruited John Lighten to be the singer of a group." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LE4YenwmeXM
**Payment Strategy for Sid Vicious** : During the Sex Pistols' chaotic US tour, McLaren put Sid Vicious on a weekly stipend of $14, hoping that a lack of readily available cash might help him "keep him straight" from drug use. "McLaren had him on a weekly stipend of $14, thinking that maybe a lock of cash would keep him straight." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LE4YenwmeXM
**Political Ambition and Reality TV (1990s-2000s)** : In 1999, Malcolm McLaren launched a campaign to be mayor of London, famously promising to allow public libraries to serve alcohol, though he never made it to Election Day. In the 2000s, he became a regular on reality TV shows like *Big Brother*. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**Post-Dolls Plans** : After the New York Dolls ultimately fell apart following a couple of tours under his management, Malcolm McLaren returned to England. He carried with him "definite ideas on what he'd do next time," suggesting his experience with the Dolls significantly shaped his future endeavors in the music industry. "But all was not lost. Because Malcolm McLaren went back to his shop in England with some definite ideas on what he'd do next time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
**Punk Fashion Inspiration (1975)** : During a 1975 trip to New York for a fashion fair, Malcolm McLaren took a detour to CBGBs, where he encountered Richard Hell, then bassist for The Heartbreakers. Hell, due to his poverty, wore ripped clothes held together with safety pins, a look McLaren misinterpreted as a "grand fashion statement," inspiring him to integrate safety pins into his own clothing designs back in England. "What drew Malcolm to Richard was a sense of style or what Malcolm thought to be Richard's sense of style because, well, you see, Richard was actually so poor that he'd wears clothing until they literally fell apart." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1y2UuU-me_s
**Reaction to Sid Vicious's Violence** : After Sid Vicious assaulted journalist Nick Kent with a bicycle chain in June 1976, McLaren expressed dismay, calling Sid a "psychopath" and promising that he would never be allowed at their concerts again. "Malcolm McLaren though was mortified. He told Kent, oh God, that guy's a psychopath. He'll never be one of our concerts again. I promise that." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LE4YenwmeXM
**Strategy for Sex Pistols US Tour** : For the Sex Pistols' 1977 US tour, McLaren conceived a "subversive" strategy to tour the punk-unfriendly Southern US, believing it would be "very punk." "McLaren thought it would be very subversive, very punk, if the pistols made a big swing into the punk, unfriendly, southern US." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LE4YenwmeXM
**Vision for Sex Pistols** : McLaren recruited John Lighten to be the singer of a group that would serve as a "living breathing advertisement" for his clothing store, "Sex," which he then named the Sex Pistols. "Malcolm McLaren recruited John Lighten to be the singer of a group that would be a living breathing advertisement for his store. He called them the Sex Pistols." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LE4YenwmeXM
