**Autopsy and Cause of Death** : The autopsy determined Lane Staley had been dead for two weeks, likely dying on Friday, April 5, 2002—the day after Mike Starr's visit and coincidentally the same day Kurt Cobain died in 1994. The cause of death was ruled an accidental overdose of a speedball, a mixture of cocaine and heroin. His body was severely decomposed, weighing only 39 kilograms (80 pounds), and dental records were required for identification, which was challenging due to the loss of most of his teeth. He was 34 years old. "Pause of death was ruled as an accidental overdose of a speedball, a mix of coke and heroin." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hhii2_BVE7A

**Demri Parrott's Influence and Death (1996)** : Lane Staley's former fiancée, Demri Parrott, was also addicted to drugs, and it appeared her use began after meeting him. She made repeated attempts to get clean, and after each release from hospitalization, she would spend weeks trying to help Lane, but neither of them succeeded. Her death from a drug overdose on October 29, 1996, deeply affected Staley, even though they had broken up earlier, leading to him being placed on a 24-hour suicide watch, and he reportedly never recovered from her passing. "It didn't help that his former fiance, Demory Parade, was also a junkie, and when she died of a drug overdose on October 29, 1996, Lane fell apart, even though they'd broken up sometime earlier." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hhii2_BVE7A
**Deteriorating Health and Isolation** : During his final years, Lane Staley's health worsened significantly; he suffered from unhealing open sores, and rumors circulated about him losing fingers or an arm due to abscesses. He became increasingly isolated from Alice in Chains and by 1999, rarely left his condo in Seattle's University District, where he primarily painted, played video games, watched TV, and used drugs delivered by dealers, only leaving to withdraw money from a bank machine for payments. "His drug use got much, much heavier. He did start seeing, or at least hanging out with, a woman named Michelle Crane. She spent as much time as she could with him, but his health kept getting worse." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hhii2_BVE7A
**Discovery of Death (April 19, 2002)** : On April 17, 2002, Lane Staley's mother visited his apartment to inform him of Demri's brother's death, finding unopened mail and no answer at the door. Two days later, his accountants alerted Alice in Chains' former manager, Susan Silver, that no money had been withdrawn for two weeks, a significant red flag. Silver contacted Staley's mother, who returned to the condo, found his usually quiet cat, Sadie, meowing excessively, and then called 911. Police forced entry and discovered Staley's body amidst drug paraphernalia. "On April 19, the accountants who looked after Lane's money contacted Alison Chains former manager Susan Silver, saying that no money had been withdrawn from his bank account for two weeks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hhii2_BVE7A
