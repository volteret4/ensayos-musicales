## albums
**AM (2013)** : AM is the band's 2013 album, from which the song "Why'd You Only Call Me When You're High?" was one of six singles. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YvcnHKI4oIw
**Favorite Worst Nightmare (Second Studio Record)** : This was the band's second studio record, featuring the international hit "Fluorescent Adolescent." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YvcnHKI4oIw
**Whatever People Say I Am, That's What I'm Not (2006) – Debut Album** : Released on January 23rd, 2006, this album became the fastest-selling record in British history, moving 360,000 copies in its first seven days. It has since sold around 2 million copies in the UK and several million more worldwide. The album garnered significant critical acclaim, with two songs reaching number one on the UK charts, five music magazines naming it the year's Best Album, and winning the Mercury Prize for Best Album of 2006, Best International Album of the Year in Ireland, and two Brit Awards for Best Album and Best Group. Many of its tracks were re-recorded demos that had previously been shared online. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YvcnHKI4oIw
**Whatever People Say I Am, That's What I'm Not – Fastest-Selling British Debut** : This debut album set a record for the fastest-selling debut album in the history of British music, surpassing even Oasis, despite the band having started by giving away their music for free. "Their debut album, whatever people say I am, that's what I'm not, set a record for the fastest-selling debut album in the history of British music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=raRCZ0Y5up8

## curiosities
**Alex Turner's Lyrical Talent** : Alex Turner is recognized as one of the best lyricists of his generation in non-hip hop music, possibly one of the best alt-rock lyricists since Morrissey. He developed a habit of writing song lyrics in the back of his school books when bored in high school. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YvcnHKI4oIw
**Band's Initial Unawareness of Online Sharing** : Initially, the Arctic Monkeys were unaware that their music was being posted online. They only began to notice the impact when crowds at their gigs grew larger and a significant number of fans were singing along to songs the band hadn't officially released. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YvcnHKI4oIw
**Defying Music Industry Fears** : At a time when the music industry was "freaking out" over file sharing, the Arctic Monkeys leveraged it to become famous. Despite industry warnings that giving away music for free would lead to financial ruin, the band continued to embrace online sharing. This approach proved that sharing music online could broaden and deepen a band's appeal, setting a new standard for rapidly building a passionate fanbase. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YvcnHKI4oIw
**Domino Records Signing and Debut Single Success** : After indie labels began to take notice of their online popularity, the group signed with Domino. Their very first single released through the label debuted at number one on the British singles charts in October 2005. "The Arctic Monkeys, their debut album, whatever people say I am, that's what I'm not, set a record for the fastest-selling debut album in the history of British music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=raRCZ0Y5up8
**Early Lyrical Prowess** : The Arctic Monkeys' songs are constructed in a very distinctive fashion, and their lyrics are considered "above and beyond so many bands." The band achieved fame while still teenagers. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YvcnHKI4oIw
**Embracing File Sharing for Fame** : The Arctic Monkeys are sometimes credited as the first band to effectively use the internet to break through to a mass audience, achieving this when they were in their late teens and early twenties. In 2003, they began recording demos at Sheffield's Two Fly studio, accumulating 22 tracks by the following year. These demos were burned onto CDs and given away at gigs for two years, leading fans to rip them to MP3s and share them online. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YvcnHKI4oIw
**High Green Isolation** : All four members of the band originated from High Green, an isolated northern suburb of Sheffield. During their formative years, the lack of a direct transit connection to downtown meant young residents often stayed within their neighborhood, fostering local activities like forming bands. This close proximity, with everyone living within a ten-minute walk and attending the same school, facilitated the band's formation. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YvcnHKI4oIw
**Internet-Savvy Origins in Sheffield** : A very young, erudite, and internet-conscious band from Sheffield, the Arctic Monkeys gained early traction by giving away their music at gigs and embracing fans sharing their songs for free on MySpace. "The Arctic Monkeys started by giving away their music at gigs and didn't care when fans put their songs up for free on MySpace." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=raRCZ0Y5up8
**Matt Helders' Drummer Origin** : Matt Helders became the band's drummer by necessity, as all other instruments were already claimed, making him the "Ringo" of the group. His background included being "very much into beats" and listening to Motown due to his parents' influence. By his teens, he was deeply immersed in hip hop, with Wu Tang, Outcast, and Eminem as favorites, and he harbored dreams of becoming a DJ by age 14. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YvcnHKI4oIw
**Matt Helders' Hip Hop and Dance Scene Disillusionment** : By the late 1990s, Matt Helders' interest in the dance scene, encompassing genres like garage and jungle, waned as it became more commercial ("big money territory"). This shift was a "huge turnoff" for those who preferred the underground nature of these scenes. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YvcnHKI4oIw
**Media Frenzy for Internet Breakthrough** : When the media recognized that the Arctic Monkeys were poised to be the first band to achieve mainstream success through the internet, a "frenzy" ensued, intensifying their rapid rise to fame. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YvcnHKI4oIw
**Sheffield Origins** : The Arctic Monkeys hail from Sheffield, England, a city with a rich musical history, particularly noted for synth pop in the 1980s and the subsequent era of Acid House. Growing up in Sheffield in the 90s meant being aware of its diverse music scene. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YvcnHKI4oIw
**The Sheriff's Crucial Role** : A fan named Mark Bull, known as "The Sheriff" in Arctic Monkeys lore, was instrumental in their online breakthrough. He is believed to be the first person to post the band's demo tracks online for public access. As the band distributed more songs and played more shows, The Sheriff continued to upload their material, collecting it under the title "Beneath the Boardwalk," a reference to one of the band's regular venues. His website eventually hosted 18 Arctic Monkeys songs. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YvcnHKI4oIw
**Unusual Path to Fame** : The way the Arctic Monkeys became famous was completely antithetical to the conventional methods of the music business. Their approach to gaining an audience made them unusual but earned admiration. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YvcnHKI4oIw
**Virtuous Circle of Internet Growth** : The online sharing of demos, facilitated by The Sheriff, created a "virtuous circle" of increasing popularity. Fans downloaded, shared, burned, and created MySpace sites dedicated to the songs. This led to widespread word-of-mouth, more downloads, and larger attendance at gigs, establishing a self-generating community that grew rapidly. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YvcnHKI4oIw
