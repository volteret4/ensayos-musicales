## albums
**Inlet (2020)** : This album marked Hum's reunion and first new release in 22 years, coming out in 2020. It followed a long period of working as an indie band after being dropped by RCA. "And in 2020, they got back together again and released a record called Inlet. This album in 22 years." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SOAL0GODN3M
**You'd Prefer an Astronaut (1995)** : Released in 1995 on RCA, this was Hum's third album overall but their major label debut. The album sold close to half a million records and has been cited as extremely influential by other bands, including the Deftones, who noted its significant impact on their sound development. "But by 1995, they had a major label deal with RCA, which resulted in you'd prefer an astronaut." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SOAL0GODN3M

## songs
**Stars (1995)** : "Stars" was the first single from Hum's 1995 album, *You'd Prefer an Astronaut*. It became a big alt-rock radio hit, generating significant exposure and contributing to the album selling close to half a million records. Despite its success, subsequent singles from the album did not perform well, and the band was later dropped by RCA. "The first single was called Stars. Big, alt-rock radio hit. Lots of exposure, close to half a million records sold." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SOAL0GODN3M
**Stars in Cadillac Commercial (Undated)** : The song "Stars" generated significant income for Hum when it was featured in a commercial for Cadillac. This placement helped the band earn good money from their one major hit. "Still, they made some good money from the song, especially when it showed up in a commercial for Cadillac." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SOAL0GODN3M

## curiosities
**2020 Reunion** : Hum reunited in 2020 to release *Inlet*, their first album in 22 years. This marked a significant return for the band after years of independent work. "And in 2020, they got back together again and released a record called Inlet. This album in 22 years." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SOAL0GODN3M
**Dropped by RCA** : After the success of "Stars" and *You'd Prefer an Astronaut*, subsequent singles from the album, specifically "Stift," failed to perform well. Following a second record for RCA, the band was dropped by the label and reverted to working as an indie band. "A couple of other singles from the album Stift, and after a second record for RCA, they were dropped and have been working as an indie band ever since." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SOAL0GODN3M
**Formation and Early Indie Work** : Hum has existed in various forms since 1989, releasing music on an indie label starting in 1991 before securing a major label deal with RCA in 1995. They are from Champagne, Illinois. "They've been around in one form or another, not counting various breaks since 1989." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SOAL0GODN3M
**Influence on Other Bands** : Hum's album *You'd Prefer an Astronaut* and its lead single "Stars" have been cited as highly influential by other bands. For instance, the Deftones stated that the album was crucial to the development of their sound. Other bands like The Smashing Pumpkins and Sonic Youth are also mentioned as part of the "heavy fuzzy bands" influenced by Hum. "The Deftones, for example, say that you'd prefer an astronaut was a big deal to them and how they developed their sound." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SOAL0GODN3M
