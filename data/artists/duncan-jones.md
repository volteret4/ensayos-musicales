# artist - Duncan Jones

## curiosities
**Birth and Childhood** : Born Zawi on May 30, 1971, to David and Angela Bowie, he was known as Zawi Bowie. He grew up primarily with his mother after his parents' divorce in 1980, living in cities like Berlin, London, Switzerland, and Scotland, where he attended an expensive private boarding school. "Although when he was born on May 30, 1971, his name was Zawi. He was the son of David and Angela Bowie." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U40hOYy_94E
**Education** : Duncan Jones was expelled from high school before graduation, but later earned a degree in philosophy. His star research paper was titled "How to Kill Your Computer Friend, an investigation of the mind-body problem and how it relates to the hypothetical creation of a thinking machine." He pursued a PhD in philosophy at Vanderbilt University in Tennessee before leaving to attend film school in London. "School life was a little rough. He was expelled before graduation from high school." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U40hOYy_94E
**Filmmaking Career** : Duncan Jones is a director who wrote and directed a sci-fi short film called "Whistle" in 2002. His major breakthrough came with a TV commercial campaign for French Connection UK stores, and he was working on a sci-fi movie titled "Moon," starring Sam Rockwell and Kevin Spacey, which tells the story of an astronaut stranded on a moon mining colony. "And now he's working as a director. He wrote and directed a sci-fi short called Whistle in 2002." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U40hOYy_94E
**Name Changes** : At age 12, Zawi changed his name to Joe Jones because he was tired of jokes; Jones is his father's actual surname. This name stuck for about six years before he decided to go by Duncan, which is his actual first name. He has been known as Duncan Zawi Haywood Jones ever since.
