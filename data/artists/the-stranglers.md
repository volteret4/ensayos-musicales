## curiosities
**Aggressive Bass Sound** : Jean-Jacques Burnel achieved a unique, growly and aggressive sound by using a pick, playing very close to the guitar's bridge, and driving his amplifier into distortion territory. This approach redefined the role of a bass player within a band. "And to get that growly song, he played very close to the guitar's bridge and drove his amplifier into distortion territory." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kbFfT0pKchI
**Band Formation and Royalty Settlement** : Formed in 1974, this British band later noticed a strong similarity between their 1977 song "No More Heroes" and Elastica's song "Waking Up." This similarity resulted in The Stranglers being awarded a piece of the royalties from Elastica's first album, which was described as an actual case of "theft." "British band that was formed in 1974, noticed that the Elastica song Waking Up sounded awfully like one of their songs from 1977 called No More Heroes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L09R2evjyMc

**Influence on "New Wave of New Wave"** : The Stranglers were a significant inspiration for groups associated with the "new wave of new wave" movement in the early 1990s, who drew inspiration from the 1977-1980 period. "They were wild about groups like [...] the Stranglers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
**Jean-Jacques Burnel – Classical Guitar Training Applied to Aggressive, Melodic Bass** : Jean-Jacques Burnel came to the bass with a classical guitar background and developed melodic yet aggressive bass lines, using a pick and distortion effects.
