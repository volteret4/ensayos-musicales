## curiosities
**Career Launched by Ted Mack Amateur Hour** : Pat Boone's career was successfully launched by his appearance on the Ted Mack Amateur Hour, a popular 1950s TV talent show. "The show launched the careers of Pat Boone and Margaret in Gladys Knight among others." ← Episode 44： ＂Train Kept A-Rollin'＂, by Johnny Burnette and the Rock 'n' Roll Trio | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u3zYAJ2qeR4
**Clean-Cut Cover Artist** : Pat Boone was known for his "good clean-cut wholesome Christian white boy" image. He often covered Little Richard's songs, changing the lyrics to remove any perceived immorality. "A good Christian boy like Pat Boone couldn't be heard thinking about such immorality." ← Episode 34： ＂Tutti Frutti＂ by Little Richard | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7mk9SW46Li4
**Exploitative Cover Versions** : Pat Boone gained notoriety for recording "sound-alike versions of black musicians' hits," which were specifically marketed to white audiences who were unwilling to purchase music by Black artists. This practice was ethically questionable, as it acted as a "spoiler" for the original Black artists' records. "Pat Boone a few years later, who would record sound-alike versions of black musicians hits, specifically to sell to people who wouldn't buy music by black people and act as a spoiler for their records." ← Episode 3： ＂Ida Red＂ by Bob Wills and the Texas Playboys | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zu4E9Y1oJA0
