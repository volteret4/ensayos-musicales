**Musical Evolution (1982)** : Initially, New Order sounded similar to Joy Division. However, a trip to New York, where they were exposed to post-disco, early house, and electro music, changed their direction. They became fascinated with synthesizers, drum machines, and sequencers, shifting by 1982 from Joy Division's guitar-based style to a more electronic and dance-oriented sound with Bernard Sumner fronting the band. "And by 1982, New Order had moved away from the guitar-based material that Joy Division had become famous for into something more electronic and dancing, all with Bernard out front." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vLZla5h4xKQ
