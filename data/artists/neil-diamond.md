**I'm Glad You're Here With Me Tonight (1977)** : This was Neil Diamond's eleventh album and the second consecutive one to be accompanied by a TV special, reflecting his status as a great showman. The album featured songs like "Desiree" and a solo version of "You Don't Bring Me Flowers." **Neil Diamond Gold (1970) - Live Album at The Troubadour** : Recorded live at The Troubadour in July 1970, this album features ten songs. Neil Diamond wrote every single track on the album, with the sole exception of "Both Sides Now," which was penned by Joni Mitchell. The crowd's reaction to "Sweet Caroline" on this recording was notably polite clapping, decades before it became a popular anthem at sporting events and weddings. **Strategic Hiatus (1972-1976)** : Neil Diamond deliberately stayed off the stage for four years between 1972 and 1976, aiming to learn from the experiences of artists like Elvis Presley and The Beatles. He believed that constant public presence could lead to an artist becoming "public property" and wished to maintain his independence. "He actually said he tried to learn a little something from Elvis and the Beatles. He said, I think if you're out in front of the public too much, if you're too recognized, too well known, you do in a sense become public property. I always wanted to be my own man." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 39 - American Top 40 - Keeping Our Feet on the Ground and Reaching for the Stars (Part 1) | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-39-american-top-40-keeping-our-feet-on-the-ground-and-reaching-for-the-stars-part-1
**Struggling Songwriter to Hitmaker** : For about eight years, Diamond made only around $20 a year in royalties as a songwriter, forcing him to sell vitamin pills door-to-door to support himself. His financial situation changed significantly after Jay and the Americans recorded "Sunday and Me" in 1966, providing him with enough money to enter a recording studio and record his own three hit singles. "He made about 20 bucks a year in royalties.
