# artist - Andy Warhol

## curiosities
**Circle of Friends and "Walk on the Wild Side"** : Lou Reed's hit song "Walk on the Wild Side" tells the true stories of several figures within Andy Warhol's social circle. These individuals included Holly Woodlawn, a transsexual from Miami; Candy Darling, another transsexual; and Joe Campbell, who was referred to as the "Sugar Plum Fairy." "This song told the true story of a number of people in Andy Warhol's circle." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
**Musical Commissions** : Andy Warhol commissioned three songs for a Broadway musical he was attempting to stage. These songs later appeared on Lou Reed's 1972 album *Transformer*, indicating Warhol's influence on Reed's creative output. "Three of the songs were commissioned by Andy Warhol for a Broadway musical he was trying to stage." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
**Portrayed in Film** : Andy Warhol was portrayed by Weezer guitarist Brian Bell in the film "Factory Girl." "Brian played Andy Warhol." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LYRPxtP61JM
**Album Cover Design & Peelable Banana** : Andy Warhol designed the iconic album cover for "The Velvet Underground & Nico," famously insisting on a peelable banana sticker. This creative decision led to significant production challenges and delays, making the album costly and complex to manufacture, but Warhol refused to allow its release without this specific artistic element. **Discovery of The Velvet Underground (Late 1965/Early 1966)** : Andy Warhol discovered The Velvet Underground between late 1965 and early 1966. There are contradictory accounts regarding who precisely introduced the band to Warhol, with cinematic collaborators Querantón Morricay (Gerard Malanga) and Barbara Rabin (Barbara Rubin) mentioned as possibilities. **Influence on Lou Reed's "Transformer"** : Lou Reed's experiences at Andy Warhol's Factory heavily influenced the thematic content of his "Transformer" album. The record delves into "macabre stories" from the Factory, and his song "Walk on the Wild Side" specifically references and lists many notable characters from that environment. "Y realmente la temática del Transformer viene a ser no solo esas grabaciones desechadas de la parte de la Belbet Underground sino las experiencias que el propio Luric había tenido en la factoria de Andy Warhol." ← Music Radar Clan > Tras los pasos de Lou Reed | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aLzyz4Ec5cU&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc ← andy-warhol
**Insistence on Nico Joining The Velvet Underground** : Andy Warhol introduced Nico, a German actress and model from The Factory, to The Velvet Underground. He felt the band needed a "beautiful woman" and was obsessed with Nico at the time, thereby forcing her into the band against the opinions of John Cale and Lou Reed. "Andy Warhol le parecía que hacía falta una mujer guapa dentro de la velvita underground. Entonces para él la idea perfecta era Nico en ese tiempo estaba sesionado con ella y entonces pues la introduce un poco con calzador en contra de la opinión de John Kale y de Lou Reed." ← Music Radar Clan > The Velvet Underground： Una vision más humana que musical | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_TMMVFTuyC4&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc ← andy-warhol
**Lou Reed's Hatred After Velvet Underground** : After the dissolution of the Velvet Underground, Lou Reed developed a "true hatred" for Andy Warhol. This animosity might have contributed to the somewhat negative portrayal of Factory characters and their "bad influence" or "bad lives" in Lou Reed's work, such as the song "Walk on the Wild Side." "Aquí de hecho también puede que tengamos un poco de rencor hacia la mala relación que quedó del Luric con el propio Andy Warhol que quedaron como muy enemistados con Luric, sintiendo un verdadero odio hacia Andy Warhol, justo tras la disolución de la Belbet Underground." ← Music Radar Clan > Tras los pasos de Lou Reed | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aLzyz4Ec5cU&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc ← andy-warhol
**MGM Executive's Reaction to Album** : After hearing "The Velvet Underground & Nico," a Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer executive famously told Andy Warhol, "Andy, esto es una mierda" ("Andy, this is shit"). Ironically, this same individual was responsible for the Verve Records division and ultimately oversaw the album's release. **Promotion of The Velvet Underground (Early 1966)** : Andy Warhol secured numerous small concerts and art showcases for The Velvet Underground, actively promoting them within the New York art world.
