## curiosities
**Architectural Influence on Composition** : Von Bingen’s slow, somber, and unadorned chanting style was a direct response to the acoustics of large stone Gothic cathedrals. Rapid chord changes or beats would become an "unlistenable mess" due to the heavy reverb, so her music was designed to let the echo enhance the sound. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
**Invention of Sacred Monophony** : She essentially invented the specific sound of liturgical chanting that became the standard in Catholic churches and monasteries from the 12th century onward. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
**Mother of Science** : Born in 1098 in Germany, von Bingen was a nun, mystic, and visionary often referred to as the "mother of science" in her home country. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
