## albums
**Ordo Virtutum (c. 1156)** : This is Hildegard von Bingen's most famous work, composed around 1156. This "beautiful haunting stuff" was ideally suited for the acoustics of the large, echoey stone cathedrals being built across Germany at the time. Its slow, somber style, without fast tempos, quick lyrics, rapid chord changes, or strong beats, prevented the natural echo and reverb of these gothic structures from turning the music into an "unlistenable mess." ## curiosities
**Inventor of Liturgical Chanting** : Hildegard von Bingen basically invented the sound of standard liturgical chanting heard in Catholic churches, cathedrals, and monasteries starting around the 12th century. Her style, known officially as sacred monophony, was characterized by slow, somber melodies perfectly suited to the long reverberation times of large gothic cathedrals, influencing composers for centuries on how to use architectural acoustics to enhance music. "Hilogart basically invented the sound of standard liturgical chanting that began to be heard in Catholic churches, cathedrals, and monasteries, starting in about the 12th century." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw ← hildegard-von-bingen
**Mother of Science in Germany** : Hildegard von Bingen, born in 1098 in what is now modern Germany, was a nun, mystic, and visionary, with some considering her to be the "mother of science" in Germany. Her wide-ranging influence extended beyond music to various intellectual fields.
