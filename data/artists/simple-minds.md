## songs
**Don't You (Forget About Me)** : This song is noted for being loaded with vocables, particularly at its conclusion, featuring many "la la la" sounds. The use of these singing placeholders is considered perfect for the song. "Simple minds taking us out of don't you forget about me, with many la la la, the singing placeholder known as a vocable." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_YjwRApoJhA
**Don't You (Forget About Me) (1985)** : This song, written by Keith Forsey for *The Breakfast Club* soundtrack, was famously rejected by Brian Ferry, Billy Idol, and considered for Corey Hart and Cy Curnin before Virgin Records suggested Simple Minds. The band was initially reluctant, preferring to record their own material and focusing on breaking into America, believing the song wouldn't help. However, under pressure from the label and an appeal from Chrissie Hynde (married to singer Jim Kerr), they completely rearranged and recorded the song in just three hours. Despite their initial dismissal of it as a "throwaway soundtrack song," it became their biggest hit. "Simple minds with their song from the Breakfast Club soundtrack, a track they did not write, a track that was rejected by a bunch of other people, and a track they did not want to record." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM

## curiosities
**12-inch Remix Business** : Simple Minds embraced the 12-inch remix format, joining many other bands in the 1980s who saw its potential for club play and extending their musical presence beyond traditional album releases. This trend followed the success of New Order's "Blue Monday." "And so was Depeche Mode and The Cure and The Human League and Simple Minds and The Cult and Killing Joke and Erasure and Psychedelic Furs and Madness. They all got into the 12-inch business." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**Collaborated with Anton Corbijn** : Photographer Anton Corbijn, known for designing The Killers' "Sam's Town" cover, is a favored collaborator of Simple Minds, contributing to their visual identity. "The photographer was Anton Corbin, the Dutch guy who was a favorite of you two, Depeche Mode, Coldplay, Bruce Springsteen, Kate Bush, Bjork Simple Minds..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tbKuJR0cPs
