## songs
**The Bells (Year TBD)** : Jeff Mills played this track in his sets for two years before its official release, building legendary anticipation. Considered "the most important track in the history of techno," it's a "miracle of techno" where every element—kicks, hi-hats, bass, and the bell loop—is clearly perceived as a sonic X-ray of the genre. Its sequence of calls between high and low bells has become one of the most iconic and revered passages in techno history, capable of causing a collective explosion on the dance floor with a single hit. "Su secuencia de llamada entre campanas altas y bajas se ha convertido en uno de los pasajes más icónicos y reverenciados de toda la historia del techno." ← Cómo Jeff Mills transformó el techno para siempre | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=omE3zOiZAXs

## curiosities
**Comparison to Minimalist Composers** : Jeff Mills brought techno to spaces comparable to the minimalism of Steve Reich (transcribed as Steve Ray) and Philip Glass (transcribed as Joe Filiglass), with the key difference being that while Reich and Glass performed in theaters and museums, Mills did so in clubs and on dance floors. "llevo el techno a unos espacios comparable con el minimalismo de Steve Ray, Joe Filiglass, lo único que estos dos interpretaban en teatro son museos, mientras que Jeff Mills, en clubs y pistas de baile." ← Cómo Jeff Mills transformó el techno para siempre | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=omE3zOiZAXs
**DJ Set as Non-Verbal Narrative** : For Jeff Mills, a DJ set was much more than a succession of tracks; it was a non-verbal narrative about the fantastic and the astonishing, an exercise in sonic abstraction where each rhythmic layer and abrupt cut told part of the story, as "nobody DJed like him." "Para él, un set podía ser mucho más con su excesión de temas. Era una narración no verbal sobre lo fantástico y la sombroso." ← Cómo Jeff Mills transformó el techno para siempre | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=omE3zOiZAXs
**DJing Style: 3-4 Decks and TR-909** : Jeff Mills is historically known as one of the most visionary and radical DJs of his time, famous for his sessions using three, and sometimes up to four, turntables, often accompanied by the Roland TR-909 drum machine. In these sets, Mills could mix up to 70 vinyls in just one hour, achieving a hypnotic speed and overflowing intensity that broke the boundaries of dance music. "Si que no famosa, asocesiones a tres y en ocasiones hasta cuatro platos, a las que en muchos casos se sumaba la caja de Rindmoss TR-909." ← Cómo Jeff Mills transformó el techno para siempre | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=omE3zOiZAXs
**Departure from Underground Resistance** : Jeff Mills unofficially left Underground Resistance in 1991. While neither party has confirmed it, rumors suggest he disagreed with the increasingly politicized direction the collective was taking. **Early Career as "The Wizard"** : Jeff Mills began his career DJing in Detroit clubs when he was underage, earning the nickname "The Wizard" due to his exceptional dexterity with turntables. His early sets were influenced by virtuoso DJs like Grandmaster Flash and featured numerous DJ tricks, mixing everything new like Funk, House, Electro, and Industrial. **Tresor Club Residency and Detroit-Berlin Axis** : A turning point in Mills' career came in 1992 when Dimitri Hegemann (transcribed as Dimitrija Garman) signed him for the newly opened Tresor in Berlin, where he acted as a resident for several months. In this cavernous, brick-walled, industrial space, Mills honed his brutalist and minimalist styles, testing early Axis Records releases live and forging his reputation among an audience eager for hard, futuristic sounds, solidifying the Detroit-Berlin connection. "En ese espacio cavernoso, de paredes del adrillo y ambiente industrial, Mills afinó sus estilos más minimalistas y brutal, poniendo a prueba en directo a las primeras referencias de Axis Records, y forjando su reputación entre una adiencia a vidas de sonidos duros y futuristas." ← Cómo Jeff Mills transformó el techno para siempre | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=omE3zOiZAXs
