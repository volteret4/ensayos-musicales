**Exemption from National Service (1957)** : Ringo Starr, born on July 7, 1940, was exempt from compulsory national service in Britain. This exemption, due to the end of the obligation in 1957 for those born after October 1, 1939, allowed him to focus on music. "And future Beatles Ringo Starr was born on July 7, 1940." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V7WjNR1ujhk ← ringo-starr
**Fatherly Advice to Zach Starkey** : Ringo Starr gave his son, Zach Starkey, only one drum lesson and tried to dissuade him from a music career, suggesting he pursue a profession like a doctor or lawyer instead. "Dad gave him exactly one lesson before he tried to dissuade his son about getting into show business. Be a doctor, be a lawyer, don't be a musician. **Japanese Applesauce Commercial (1996)** : In 1996, Ringo Starr traveled to Japan to participate in an advertisement for a brand of applesauce. The anecdote suggests that "Ringo" reportedly means "applesauce" in Japanese. "In 1996, Ringo Star went to Japan to take part in an ad for a brand of applesauce. By applesauce, apparently that's what Ringo means in Japanese." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aSk1XQHNkd8 ← ringo-starr
