# artist - Bill Monroe's Blue Grass Boys

## members
- Earl Scruggs (1940s)
- Lester Flatt

## curiosities
**Origin of "Bluegrass"** : The genre of music became known as "bluegrass" after Bill Monroe's band, the Blue Grass Boys, in which Earl Scruggs played in the 1940s. "bluegrass became the name for their genre of music." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 46 - The Chaos and Music of the Ozark Music Festival of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Scruggs' Banjo Playing** : Bill Monroe was profoundly impressed by Earl Scruggs' fast and lightning-quick banjo playing. "Monroe was completely blown away by the way Scrugg's played the banjo." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 46 - The Chaos and Music of the Ozark Music Festival of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/
