# artist - Twenty One Pilots

## genres

## albums
**Bluriface (2015) – Biggest Rock Album of the 2010s** : This album was identified as the biggest rock album of the 2010s, based on various metrics including sales and streams. It spent 234 weeks on the charts, held the number one position for nine weeks, and all its songs combined have been streamed 3.5 billion times. "The biggest rock album of the 2010s was Bluriface from 21 Pilots." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=72Lei7uug8c
**Blurryface (2016)** : This album contributed significantly to Twenty One Pilots becoming one of the top streaming artists in 2016, the year streaming generated more revenue for the American recorded music business than all other forms of music distribution combined. "If we go back to that year, we'll see that one of the top streaming artists of 2016 was these guys, thanks to their blurry face album." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZzIqIydZTlA

## curiosities
**Backmasking in Digital Era** : Twenty One Pilots is listed among the artists who have utilized backward messages in their recordings, a technique that made a comeback with the widespread availability of digital sound editing software from the late 1990s and early 2000s. "Back, cake, cold play, M&M, Fransford Nand, Lincoln Park, Marilyn Manson, Moby Soundgarden Tool, 21 Pilots, and a ton more." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_YjwRApoJhA
**Metadata Spelling of Band Name and Song Titles** : Twenty One Pilots serves as an example for metadata challenges, specifically the directive not to hyphenate their band name ("Twenty-One Pilots"), and the compounding issue of song titles often being spelled as single words. "Now remember, you do not split their name with a hyphen. If you do put a hyphen between 20 and one, then that's a metadata issue." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Sc9HApnyBLM
**Pop-Leaning Rock Sound** : Twenty One Pilots are mentioned as an example of an artist whose sound leaned more towards the pop end of rock, a characteristic shared by other successful rock acts in the streaming-dominated 2010s. "But these artists and songs all had one thing in mind. Their sounds leaned more towards the pop end of rock, like 21 Pilots." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Y6nHw5xx88
**USS Precursor Sound** : USS's first EP, "From Welding the Sea Drive," released years before, contained sounds and styles that were similar to what Twenty One Pilots would later achieve, implying USS was ahead of the curve.
