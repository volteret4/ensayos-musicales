# artist - USS

## members
- Ash Boo Shultz
- Jay Parsons

## genres
- Drum and Bass
- Dubstep
- Rock

## albums
**Advanced Basics (2014) – Philosophical Recovery Album** : Released in two versions (regular and international), this album was created during Ash's recovery from a severe drug bender; its title reflects the philosophy that "the simplest things are the hardest things". ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**Approved (2011) – EP** : A pivotal release produced by Tog Salter that marked a shift toward more traditional song structures and choruses. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**New World Alphabet (2017) – Long EP** : A seven-song collection whose mood was split by geography; the first four songs were written in California, while the final three became "really dark" after the band returned to the Canadian winter. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**Questamation (2009) – Debut Full-Length Album** : Released on March 9, 2009, this album featured the first acapella demo Ash sent to Jay, "Cloudboy". ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**Welling the Sea Drive (EP) – First EP** : The band's debut extended play, which members later noted featured a sound "ahead of the curve," drawing comparisons to the later success of Twenty One Pilots. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g

## songs
**Cloudboy (2009)** : Originally an acapella demo that so impressed Jay Parsons he compared Ash's talent to "the second coming of Kurt Cobain". ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**NA OK (2011)** : The first collaborative track written with producer Tog Salter, created only after Ash overcame intense initial resistance to outside creative input. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**This Is The Best (2012) – Breakthrough Single** : Developed in Toronto over a drum and bass beat, this song integrated dubstep elements and expanded the band's reach to rock radio in Detroit and Western New York; it was recorded at the end of Ash's eight-month drug bender. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**Who’s With Me (2017) – Successful Single** : An "earworm" track written in John Mayer’s former Calabasas mansion with a former member of OneRepublic; the lyrics were inspired by a friend's bowling catchphrase. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**Work Shoes (2017)** : A track from _New World Alphabet_ inspired by the band members' past blue-collar lives in factories and oil fields. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g

## curiosities
**"Word Jams"** : The band creates unique song titles by combining words that they feel "have never sat on the bus beside each other in the history of language," such as "Neurochemical Warfare Gas Masquerade". ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**Chlorophyll Harmonic** : In early solo shows, Ash performed with ten plants on stage, using microphones to capture what he called "plant solos". ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**Creative "Direct Download"** : Ash describes the band's evolved songwriting process using an _Amadeus_ analogy, moving from a fragmented experience to a "receptive" state similar to Mozart's effortless composition. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**Neuroplasticity Experiments** : Ash’s early unconventional performances involved playing two cassette tapes simultaneously—one self-help and one heavy metal—while making prolonged eye contact to "disrupt the brain" of the audience. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**The "Human Kabob" Origin** : Jay Parsons' nickname came from a university thesis he wrote on Vlad the Impaler (Vlad Drakul), whom he likened to a "human kabob". ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
