## albums
**Advanced Basics (2014)** : This album was released in 2014, two years after the initial European tour with Walk Off the Earth. It had two versions: a regular version and an international album. The creation process was challenging for Ash, who was recovering from an eight-month drug bender, making it a "one foot in front of the other" effort. "So our song shipwreck which is one of the most important songs to our band came out of that album came out of facing again another round of like you know the PhD dissertation with the demons." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**Approved EP (2011)** : This record was considered a "long EP" or "short album" following "Welding the Sea Drive" and "Questamation." It marked a significant shift in Ash's creative process, born out of confronting his control issues and learning to collaborate with producer Tog Salter and Jay. This shift was critical for the band's growth. "NA OK from the approved EP, a record born out of a lot of angst and fear and growth and acceptance." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**Customization (2009) – Only Full-Length Record** : At the end of 2008, Ash dedicated himself to completing the band's only full-length album, *Customization*, by working intensely in a friend's studio in Etobicoke. The record was produced and engineered by Matthew von Wagner in collaboration with Ash. "ash buried himself from a friend studio in a tow bico to finish what would become our only full length record customization." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ayPJfDAF_to
**New World Alphabet (2017)** : Referred to as a "long EP" or "seven sevens," this album was written after a period of touring. The first four songs were written in California, reflecting a desire to shake up the process by being in the desert, mountains, and by the ocean. The album takes a darker turn from song five onwards, as the band returned to Canada in winter. "And again another album that does extremely well for you." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**Questamation (2009)** : This was the band's first full album, released on March 9, 2009. It included their first song "Cloudboy" and the single "Three Purple Butterflies." "USS from their first full inked album, Questamation, which was released on March 9, 2009." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**Welding the C-Drive (2007) – First Record/EP** : Ash compiled content for this first record, which was released in the summer of 2007. The band celebrated its release with a sold-out CD release party at The Gladstone Hotel. In January 2008, 102.1 The Edge began discussing the album, leading to "Hollowpoint in Hyperbole" being added to radio rotation as a single. The EP was notably brought to the station by Barry Taylor, who praised its maturity for an independent debut. "Ash started compiling all the content that would make our first record well in the C drive and that summer we put out that record and we played the Gladstone hotel sold out CD release party." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ayPJfDAF_to

## curiosities
**Ash's Control Freak Tendencies** : Ash struggled with perfectionism and the need to control every aspect of the creative process, stemming from childhood trauma related to his father leaving. This manifested as an inability to collaborate, even with Jay, and caused a hysterical reaction during initial sessions with producer Tog Salter near Lake Erie, almost leading to the abandonment of the "Approved EP." "You start to learn a lot about yourself and situations like that. You go, oh my, and then all of a sudden, you just, you go, boom, boom, boom, boom, boom, you get right to the heart of it and you just go, oh my goodness. I'm like, I don't trust anybody else to do anything because if other people do something, it might not be perfect." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**Ash's Drug Bender and Album Completion Challenge (2013)** : After writing "This Is The Best" at the end of an eight-month "alphabet drug" bender, Ash went "lights out" for a while in November-December 2013, making the completion of the "Advanced Basics" album extremely difficult. He described being at "1% power" and unable to reach an "outlet." "Well, what was amazing Alan is that when we wrote and recorded this is the best, it was right at the end of like an eight month bender that I was on and I was on like every alphabet drug when we were in the studio and we recorded the song and just put it out." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**Ash's Stage Presence Transformation** : Ash revealed that until recently, he stopped wearing a hat and sunglasses on stage and had kept his eyes closed the entire time during performances, due to being "completely incapable of connection with other humans." The success of his "public experiment" in personal growth allowed him to shed these defenses. "I just stopped wearing a hat and sunglasses on stage because I would have my eyes closed the whole time really." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**Bowling as a Post-Addiction Hobby** : After stopping drugs, Ash found he had no hobbies outside of music and drug use. To discover what "human beings did," he started bowling with friends every Tuesday night for $8.88 at World Bowl. This new social activity indirectly sparked the creation of the song "Who's With Me." "When I stopped doing drugs I couldn't I didn't know what human beings did I didn't understand because I didn't have any hobbies it was just in music and drugs and so I was like what do you do like what do what do people do and so I was like bowling." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**Canadian Two-Piece Band** : USS is identified as a Canadian two-piece band that contributed to the flourishing of the rock duo format in the 21st century. **Chlorophyll Harmonic Stage Setup** : In his earlier abstract performance life, Ash would have ten plants on stage, set up microphones in front of them, and include "plant solos," incorporating the concept of the "chlorophyll harmonic." "I would have like 10 plants on stage with me, which I called the chlorophyll harmonic. And I have put set microphones up in front of them and there'd be like plants, solos and stuff." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**Early EP Similarities to Twenty One Pilots** : The band noted that their first EP, "From Welding the Sea Drive," released years prior, contained sounds and styles that were later adopted by Twenty One Pilots, suggesting USS was ahead of its time. "You can go back to our first EP from welding the C drive and you can hear what 21 pilots is doing in the last couple of years." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**Ecstasy-Spiked Smoothie Incident at The Casbah** : After a show at The Casbah in Hamilton, Ash, who was deeply immersed in meditation at the time, was encouraged by audience members in the front row to finish his on-stage smoothie. After chugging it, he experienced what he initially perceived as a "flash of enlightenment" or "satori," characterized by intense joy, peace, and a feeling of unity. He later realized, while in the band van, that his smoothie had been secretly spiked with ecstasy, making it a "very fascinating experience" of unknowingly being under the influence of drugs. "And all of a sudden I went, oh I'm like it's happening. I'm like I'm here. I'm like I did it. I'm like this is enlightenment...And all of a sudden I realized in the van that someone had spiked my smoothie with ecstasy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ayPJfDAF_to
**Evolution of USS's Creative Process** : The band's songwriting process dramatically changed after Ash's "armor was cracked" in 2011. It transformed from a "fragmented creative experience" of piecing together disparate ideas (like 10,000 puzzle pieces from 700 different puzzles) to a more receptive, direct "download of information," comparable to Mozart's seemingly effortless composition as described by Salieri in "Amadeus." "We've arrived at a point where enough of these layers, enough of the rust-gunked goop, has been polished from the antenna to allow this absolute receptivity that is allowing for these experiences with creativity that are all very similar to what Solary was describing with Mozart, where it's just a direct download of information." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g

"I just sang our conversation. **Jay Parsons' Radio Career** : Jay began his radio career in 2012 when Al Ford from Sonic in Edmonton asked him to host a show for an hour every weekend, where he played remixes from the rock world with a modern electronic take. In 2017, he joined 102.1 The Edge to host "The Military Mixtape" on Saturday nights, considering it one of the greatest experiences of his life outside USS, fulfilling a dream on a station he grew up listening to. "And so for an hour every weekend I would do remixes from all over the rock world and it was like a modern electronic take on what was happening at all rock fast forward to 2017 um 102.1 the edge uh came on board and uh gave me the military mixtape and that's on Saturday nights and it's been one of the greatest experiences of my life outside of USS." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**Jay's "Human Kabab" Nickname Origin** : Jay's nickname originated from his first-year university History 120 thesis paper about Vlad Drakuhl (Vlad the Impaler) from 15th-century Transylvania/Romania. Vlad's brutal punishment of impaling enemies' heads on stakes outside his castle reminded Jay of a "human kabab scenario," which he later adopted as his DJ handle at Trent University after a local promoter asked him for one. "And I thought I would, I thought it was funny because to me it was like a human kabab scenario." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**Jay's Patience and Band Cohesion** : Jay's extraordinary patience and unwavering commitment were crucial in keeping USS together, especially during Ash's personal struggles like his drug bender. Ash explicitly states he "could not believe that Jay stuck around" and credits him with the "patience of a saint," acknowledging that few others would have tolerated the difficulties. "I am a lucky I'm I'm I've like won the lottery of life like meeting him because there really isn't anyone. I don't know if there's another human that could have tolerated everything that's that's gone on." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**Jay's Role as A&R and Creative Partner** : When Ash first approached Jay with demos like "Cloudboy" and "Antivenom," Jay felt like he was acting as an A&R (Artists and Repertoire) person, recognizing Ash's talent akin to "Kirk Cobain in Canada." Jay simultaneously developed his own creativity by learning from Ash and incorporating his influences from 80s pop and 90s hip hop into the band's sound. "I also felt like I was A and R in him." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**Neuroplasticity and Brain Disruption Experiment** : Ash was deeply interested in neuroplasticity and disrupting the brain. He would perform on stage with a stereo playing two different cassette tapes simultaneously—one with "how to heal your inner child" and another with Helmet and House of Pain's "You're Just Another Victim" from the "Judgment Night" soundtrack—while holding the stereo to his head and staring at the audience to create discomfort. "I was just so interested in the whole idea of neuroplasticity and the whole idea of of rewind, like just disrupting, you know, just rupting the brain." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
**Singapore Electronic Gear Malfunction (2000s)** : During a performance at the Music Matters festival in Clark Quay, Singapore, USS encountered severe technical difficulties due to underestimating the local power situation. They blew fuses in their main electronic instruments and tripped breakers at two different clubs because of the 220-volt supply, contrasting with their 120-volt North American gear. This forced them to perform an unplanned acoustic set, with Ash playing acoustic guitar, Jay attempting to scratch on a non-spinning record player, and a drummer providing beats normally produced by a laptop. "Not only did we blow the fuses in our main electronic instruments, we also blew the breaker at two different clubs and so we had to DIY it and go back to our roots." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ayPJfDAF_to
**The Smoothie Era (c. 2008 - Singapore Incident)** : Beginning around 2008, Ash, then a full-time musician, established a ritual of making and sharing protein and electrolyte smoothies on stage during USS shows. This unique practice became a signature element of their performances, often involving pouring smoothies for the audience. The "smoothie era" dramatically concluded in Singapore when the blender, due to voltage incompatibility, caught fire, sparked, smoked, and mildly electrocuted Ash on stage, marking its "grand finale." "Starting in about 2008 I was a full time rougher musician by night...it just became this thing that we did at every show and so yeah when we got to Singapore the blender actually caught on fire and started sparking and smoking and I got mildly electrocuted on stage and that was like the grand finale of the smoothie era." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ayPJfDAF_to
**Therapeutic Songwriting Process for "Approved EP"** : The writing sessions for the "Approved EP" with Tog Salter became a deep psychological experience for Ash. Salter, acting like a "songwriter shaman," helped Ash confront his control issues, past traumas, and "learned helplessness," enabling him to embrace collaboration and "go big" in his music. "That I learned a lot, that learned helplessness, that scared little boy, talk solter, like, extracted, like a friggin singer, singer, singer, songwriter, shaman, like, extracted this big voice out of me." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N-3eddy4J7g
