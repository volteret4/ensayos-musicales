## songs
**Brimful of Asha (1997)** : Released in 1997, "Brimful of Asha" became Cornershop's only major alt-rock radio hit, both in North America and globally. The song, which took two months and "several pounds of pot" to complete, features a reference to Asha Bhosle, Bollywood's most famous playback singer. An initial version of the song was deemed "a touch too slow" by the record label. "Corner Shop's head guy is to Ginger Sing. And the story is that this song took two months and several pounds of pot to complete." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SOAL0GODN3M
**Brimful of Asha - Fatboy Slim Remix (1997)** : To fix the perceived slowness of "Brimful of Asha," the record label hired Fat Boy Slim. His remix involved a simple but effective technique: increasing the speed of the master tape. This subtle pitch shift, from the key of A to somewhere between B flat and B, gave the song the "right amount of energy" and transformed it into a hit. "The label hired Fat Boy Slim. And his remix consisted of nothing more than increasing the speed of the master tape." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SOAL0GODN3M
