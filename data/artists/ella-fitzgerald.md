# artist - Ella Fitzgerald

## albums
**Rhythm Is My Business (1962)** : An album where Ella Fitzgerald reunited with Bill Doggett, seeking to create music with more rhythm compared to her recent ballad-focused albums. Doggett's arrangements and Hammond organ prominently featured on the record. The song was originally written for her to sing in an Abbott and Costello film but was cut from her appearance. ## curiosities
**Collaborations with The Ink Spots** : Towards the end of Bill Doggett's tenure with The Ink Spots, Ella Fitzgerald recorded several hit singles with the group, which notably featured Doggett on piano. "And indeed, during the end of Doggert's time with the ink spot, Fitzgerald recorded a number of hit singles with the group, which of course featured Doggert on the piano." ← Episode 50： ＂Honky Tonk＂, by Bill Doggett | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H2rYnKmeZF0
**Inspiration and Imitation** : Ella Fitzgerald was one of the jazz singers Nora Jones admired but eventually decided she didn't have to imitate. Nora realized the importance of "being myself" rather than trying to sound like other renowned vocalists. "I don't have to imitate Ella, you know, or Saravan." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Introducing Broken Record - Norah Jones Begins Again | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f1264fe6d21276e0cbc
**Managed by Mo Gale** : Ella Fitzgerald was among the notable Black acts managed by Mo Gale, who also managed Lucky Millinder, Sister Rosetta Tharpe, The Ink Spots, Chick Webb, and Louis Jordan. "Doggert would also perform with Ella Fitzgerald, who was also managed by Gale." ← Episode 50： ＂Honky Tonk＂, by Bill Doggett | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H2rYnKmeZF0
**Outlier in Malismal Use** : Ella Fitzgerald was noted as one of the few exceptions among prominent early 20th-century vocalists who occasionally employed a small amount of malisma to ornament her singing, though to a lesser extent than Billie Holiday. "There were a few outliers. Billy Holiday and Ella Fitzgerald, for example, would both use a little Malisma, Holiday more than Ella, to ornament their sound." ← Episode 32： ＂I Got A Woman＂ by Ray Charles | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0oeI9f2x_fo
**Personal Friend of Sy Kravitz** : Ella Fitzgerald was listed among the personal friends of Sy Kravitz, Lenny Kravitz's father, who was a jazz promoter. "And a sometimes jazz promoter who listed Elefits Gerald, Miles Davis, and Duke Ellington as personal friends." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc ← ella-fitzgerald
**Reunion with Bill Doggett for Rhythmic Music** : In 1962, Ella Fitzgerald sought to return to making music with more rhythm, deviating from her recent albums of ballads, and specifically teamed up with Bill Doggett to achieve this goal. "In 1962, he teamed up again with LFHT's Gerald, who wanted to go back to making music with a bit more rhythm than her recent albums of balance." ← Episode 50： ＂Honky Tonk＂, by Bill Doggett | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H2rYnKmeZF0
**Successor to Chick Webb's Band Leadership** : After Chick Webb's death in June 1939 at age 34, Ella Fitzgerald became the leader of his band, though it ultimately disbanded in the mid-1940s like many big bands. "He died aged 34 in June 1939, and Ella Fitzgerald became the leader of his band." ← Episode 4： ＂Choo Choo Ch'Boogie＂ by Louis Jordan | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DFZnwvsXGJM
**Teenage Star with Chick Webb** : As a new teenage singer for Chick Webb's band, Ella Fitzgerald was already recognized as one of the great singers of her time.
