## songs
**Roadrunner (1971)** : This song was part of a series of demos recorded by Jonathan Richman in 1971. It featured a stripped-down sound, characterized by droning fuzz and distortion, combined with sharp and pointed lyrics. Richman famously forbade guitar and drum solos, creating a sound very different from the mainstream rock of the era. The song is noted for sounding like something an indie band might release today and was considered a "harbinger for the punk to come," though the timing wasn't quite right for its widespread acceptance. **Roadrunner (1972)** : This song features Jonathan Richman singing about blasting the radio in a Plymouth Roadrunner while driving on Route 128 in Massachusetts. There is an ongoing effort to designate it as the state's official rock song. ## curiosities
**Contrarian Persona** : Jonathan Richman, a Boston native, presented himself as the antithesis of the prevailing big names of his era. He kept his hair short, actively opposed drug use, and was frequently heard yelling "I hate hippies" around Boston. Unlike many contemporaries who favored The Beatles, The Rolling Stones, or the growing prog rock scene, Richman preferred the raw, unconventional sound of The Velvet Underground and The Stooges, leading him to be perceived as "weird" by mainstream audiences. "Jonathan Richmond was the complete opposite of the big names of the day. He wore his hair short and he was against using drugs. He could often be heard yelling, I hate hippies around Boston." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
**Early Obscurity and Band Breakup** : Jonathan Richman and his band, The Modern Lovers, worked in obscurity for a couple of years after recording demos like "Roadrunner" in 1971. Despite the forward-thinking nature of their music, the timing was not right for their style to gain widespread recognition. The band eventually broke up in 1974. "Jonathan and his band, the modern lovers, toiled in obscurity for a couple of years before breaking up in 1974." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
**Prototypical Alternative Artist (1970s)** : Jonathan Richman is highlighted as a "prototypical alternative artist" from the 1970s, primarily because he recorded for Beserkley Records. This label was identified as an "alternative label," signifying its independence from the major record companies of the era.
