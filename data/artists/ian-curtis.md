## curiosities
**Death by Suicide (May 18, 1980)** : Ian Curtis died by suicide on May 18, 1980, at the age of 23. He had been in very poor health, suffering from epileptic seizures exacerbated by a "crazy cocktail" of prescriptions, drinking, illegal drug use, poor diet, and late nights. He also faced an impending divorce, an affair with a Belgian woman, money worries, and pressure to continue performing with Joy Division despite his problems. The final Joy Division show on May 2, 1980, saw Ian faint during the last song, experiencing another grand mal seizure, leading to the cancellation of their upcoming North American tour. On the evening of May 17, Ian returned to his home at 77 Barton Street in Macclesfield, Manchester. His wife Deborah was out. He watched the 1977 film "Strozek," drank strong coffee and whiskey, and then wrote a long note to Deborah all night. As the sun rose on May 18, he put on his favorite album, "The Idiot" by Iggy Pop, went to the garden for a length of rope from the clothesline, and hanged himself. Deborah found him a few hours later. "He'd been in very poor health, suffering from epileptic seizures that were exacerbated by a crazy cocktail of prescriptions, drinking illegal drug use, poor diet late nights, an impending divorce, and a ferry was having with a woman from Belgium worries about money and pressures to keep on performing with the band despite all these problems." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sP75_gbZzIU
**Final Joy Division Performance (May 2, 1980)** : During what would be Joy Division's final show, Ian Curtis fainted and went into another grand mal seizure during the last song. This incident led to the decision to cancel all upcoming shows before the band was scheduled to leave for their first-ever North American tour, which was planned for two weeks later. "The final joint evision show was held on May 2nd, 1980. During the final song, Ian fainted and went into another grandmaw seizure." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sP75_gbZzIU
**Pre-Suicide Activities (May 17-18, 1980)** : On the evening of May 17, 1980, Ian Curtis returned to his home at 77 Barton Street in Macclesfield, Manchester, while his wife Deborah was out. He watched the 1977 film "Strozek," which depicted a musician unable to decide between two women in his life, and consumed several cups of strong coffee and a shot of whiskey. With pictures of his wife and baby daughters in front of him, he spent the entire night writing a long note to Deborah. As the sun came up on May 18, he put on Iggy Pop's album "The Idiot," retrieved a rope from the garden clothesline, and hanged himself. "Once she was gone, Ian sat down to watch his movie, which was a 1977 film entitled Strozek, which follows a musician and formmental patient who travels to the US and is unable to decide between the two women in his life." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sP75_gbZzIU
