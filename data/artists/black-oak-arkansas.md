## curiosities
**Confederate Flag Use** : The band prominently used the Confederate flag in their imagery. Their lead guitar player, Stanley Knight, had a huge Confederate flag displayed on his guitar, and the drum risers during their performances were covered with a bunch of these flags. **Prominent Use of Confederate Flag** : Black Oak Arkansas was one of many Southern rock bands that prominently displayed the Confederate flag. Their lead guitar player, Stanley Knight, had a large Confederate flag on his guitar, and the band would cover the drum risers with "four or five flags." "black oak Arkansas's lead guitar player Stanley Knight had a large Confederate flag on his guitar and the band covered the drum risers with four or five flags." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 3 - Nostalgia, Race, and Rebels in 70s Southern Rock (Encore) | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-3-nostalgia-race-and-rebels-in-70s-southern-rock-encore
**Rebellious Image and Criminal Activity** : The band not only glorified the image of the rebellious "hell raiser" but actively lived it. Two of their founding members stole public address equipment from their high school for their band, and subsequently engaged in a "little crime spree." They would have faced prison time if they had not been minors at the time. "Black Oak Arkansas not only glorified the image of the rebellious hell razor that band lived it." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 3 - Nostalgia, Race, and Rebels in 70s Southern Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/03-bittersweet-home-alabama
