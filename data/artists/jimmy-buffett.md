## curiosities
**"Parrothead" Alcohol Sales Influence** : Jimmy Buffett is an artist known for his dedicated fanbase, often referred to as "Parrotheads," who reliably consume significant amounts of alcohol at his concerts. This strong fan reputation allows Buffett to demand and receive a share of alcohol sales, sometimes leading to him earning 105% or more of ticket sales. "Jimmy Buffett is one of those acts because his fans, the parrots, will reliably drink a lot at every show. **Billionaire Status** : Jimmy Buffett achieved billionaire status, confirmed by Forbes, largely through the strength of his songs about the tropical lifestyle, but also significantly through his ventures into best-selling novels, restaurants, hotels, resorts, and cruise ships. **Critical Reception & Personal Philosophy** : Critics frequently dismissed Buffett's music as "lightweight" or "novelty." In a 1996 interview with music journalist Mark Kemp, Buffett shared that such reviews initially hurt his feelings, particularly comments that praised the crowd but disparaged the music. He eventually stopped reading reviews, asserting that critics lacked understanding of the challenge involved in commanding the attention of 25,000 people and concluded that it was acceptable if some didn't "get" his music, as enough people did. "He said, it used to hurt my feelings because people would review me and say the crowd was amazing to watch but the music sucked And I'd go, how can you say that? Unless you walk on a stage and test your own ability to command the attention of 25,000 people You don't have any clue as to what it feels like So I quit reading everything, if they don't get it, it's fine Because enough people do." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 44 - Streaking, Werewolves, Sharks, & Drinking - 70s Novelty Songs | https://traffic.libsyn.com/seventies/334334714-44100-2-e5ed6adac25ea.m4a
