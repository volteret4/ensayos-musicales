## albums
**Avalon (1982)** : This album was recorded under analog conditions at Compass Point Studios in the Bahamas, utilizing two-inch tape likely running at 30 inches per second, before being transferred to vinyl. The vinyl version is described as exquisite and is considered an excellent record for testing audio equipment. "a vinyl version of Roxy Music's Avalon album sounds absolutely exquisite." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B2g4QSREzpw
**Roxy Music (1972)** : This self-titled debut album was released on June 16, 1972. It was characterized by its fashionable, glamorous, and artsy approach, incorporating film and cultural references, and venturing into the avant-garde with futuristic and retro, sexy and standoffish, silly and serious elements. Its experimental nature, particularly its heavy reliance on synthesizer on Side Two as noted by a Cream Magazine review in 1973, divided critics but appealed to a segment of British music fans, including future punks. "Their cell titled debut album was released on June 16th, 1972." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw

## curiosities
**Artistic Sensibility** : Roxy Music is mentioned among a group of bands that possess an inherent artistic sensibility without being "over the top," often attributed to members having attended art school, studied art, or collected art. "You too, the cure, Marilyn Manson, Roxy Music, the Sex Pistols, Blur, they all get it without going over the top, mainly because each one of them either A went to art school, or B studied art, or C collect art." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RWbKqqW78yg
**Artwork Designer** : Peter Saville, chief art design guy at Factory Records, did design work for Roxy Music. "He did everything from gig posters to album design for Joy Division New Order, Roxas music, Swade, pulp, Martha Nemuffins, and Noam D." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pwebM3TfSdg
**Cream Magazine Review (1973)** : A 1973 review in Detroit's Cream Magazine criticized Roxy Music's debut album, describing its artifice as potentially "unhealthy as the sheen on a piece of rotten meat" and noting Side Two's excessive reliance on Brian Eno's synthesizer, which "leans a little too heavily on the synthesizer... without the saving grace of drums and bass line." Despite the harsh critique, this divisiveness made them appealing to a niche audience. "From the drag queen on the corner, to the fop finery in the centerfold, to the polished deformity of the music on the record, this celebrates the kind of artifice that could come to seem as unhealthy as the sheen on a piece of rotten meat." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw
**Early Influence from Noel Coward** : The young man who would later form Roxy Music was influenced by Noel Coward's work, which was still popular on the West End and Broadway in the mid-1960s. "and the young man who would later form Roxy Music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw
**Formation and Early Struggles** : Roxy Music was formed in 1970 as an experimental art rock outfit, with Brian Ferry fronting the band after being fired from his job teaching ceramics. After a slow start, they secured a record deal in 1971. "They were formed as an experimental art rock outfit in 1970, an out front was Brian Ferry, who had just been fired from his job teaching ceramics at a girl's school." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw
**Highly Influential Art Rock Band** : Roxy Music is described as a highly influential art rock band, of which Brian Eno was a one-time member before embarking on his solo work and production career. "Produced by Brian Eno, a one-time member of the highly influential art rock band Roxy music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pLf3kUONewg
**Highly Influential British Band** : Roxy Music is considered one of the most influential British bands of all time, with a profound impact on British punks, goth kids, New Romantics, and avant-garde artists. Their influence is evident in bands like Sex Pistols, Siouxsie and the Banshees, Duran Duran, The Smiths, Talking Heads, Radiohead, and Human League. "it's not an exaggeration to say that Roxy is one of the most influential British bands of all time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw
**Influence on British Punks** : Similar to David Bowie, Roxy Music garnered the attention of young people who would later become British punks, partly because their artifice and experimental sound, especially the synthesizer-heavy Side Two, were disliked by mainstream critics. "Like Bowie, Roxy Music attracted the attention of kids who would soon become Britain's punks. They especially liked that the critics weren't crazy about Roxy Music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw
**Influence on New Romanticism** : The band's style provided inspiration for the New Romanticism movement, alongside David Bowie and Brian Eno. "Taking cues from David Bowie and Roxy Music and Brian Eno." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KF5MwJVYng
**Influence on Suede** : Roxy Music was cited as an influence on Suede, which celebrated "the great eccentric English rock of the 70s and 80s" and aimed to evoke classic British rock sounds. **New Romantic Influence** : Roxy Music, known for their white dinner jackets and often outrageous costumes, served as an inspirational reference for the Blitz kids. These young individuals were seeking a more glamorous and fun aesthetic than the angry nihilism of punk, contributing to the emergence of the New Romantic movement. "Roxy music, for example, were their white dinner jackets and sometimes outrageous costumes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mAlc-N2wn5s

This soundtrack was significant for being one of the first to highlight the burgeoning alternative music scene, including punk and new wave, to a broader audience.
