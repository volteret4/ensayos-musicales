## albums
**Avalon (1982)** : This album was recorded under analog conditions at Compass Point Studios in the Bahamas, utilizing two-inch tape likely running at 30 inches per second, before being transferred to vinyl. The vinyl version is described as exquisite and is considered an excellent record for testing audio equipment. "a vinyl version of Roxy Music's Avalon album sounds absolutely exquisite." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B2g4QSREzpw

## curiosities
**Artistic Sensibility** : Roxy Music is mentioned among a group of bands that possess an inherent artistic sensibility without being "over the top," often attributed to members having attended art school, studied art, or collected art. "You too, the cure, Marilyn Manson, Roxy Music, the Sex Pistols, Blur, they all get it without going over the top, mainly because each one of them either A went to art school, or B studied art, or C collect art." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RWbKqqW78yg
**Artwork Designer** : Peter Saville, chief art design guy at Factory Records, did design work for Roxy Music. "He did everything from gig posters to album design for Joy Division New Order, Roxas music, Swade, pulp, Martha Nemuffins, and Noam D." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pwebM3TfSdg
**Highly Influential Art Rock Band** : Roxy Music is described as a highly influential art rock band, of which Brian Eno was a one-time member before embarking on his solo work and production career. "Produced by Brian Eno, a one-time member of the highly influential art rock band Roxy music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pLf3kUONewg
**Influence on New Romanticism** : The band's style provided inspiration for the New Romanticism movement, alongside David Bowie and Brian Eno. "Taking cues from David Bowie and Roxy Music and Brian Eno." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KF5MwJVYng
**Influence on Suede** : Roxy Music was cited as an influence on Suede, which celebrated "the great eccentric English rock of the 70s and 80s" and aimed to evoke classic British rock sounds. "Instead, Swade celebrated the great eccentric English rock of the 70s and 80s. Bowie, Roxy music, T-Rex, the Smiths, Morrissey, British Psych, Jagger Swagger, Glam guitars." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg
**New Romantic Influence** : Roxy Music, known for their white dinner jackets and often outrageous costumes, served as an inspirational reference for the Blitz kids. These young individuals were seeking a more glamorous and fun aesthetic than the angry nihilism of punk, contributing to the emergence of the New Romantic movement. "Roxy music, for example, were their white dinner jackets and sometimes outrageous costumes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mAlc-N2wn5s
**Suede Influence** : Roxy Music was celebrated by Suede as an influence, representing the great eccentric English rock of the 1970s and 1980s that Suede sought to revive. "Instead, Swade celebrated the great eccentric English rock of the 70s and 80s. David Bowie, Roxy Music, T-Rex, Smiths, Morrissey, British Psych, Jagger Swagger, Glam guitars..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5q5EnZj1FwE
**Times Square Soundtrack Contribution (1980)** : Roxy Music was among the artists featured on the soundtrack for the 1980 movie *Time Square*. This soundtrack was significant for being one of the first to highlight the burgeoning alternative music scene, including punk and new wave, to a broader audience. "The soundtrack featured the pretenders, Roxy music, Gary Newman, XTC, Joe Jackson." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM
