# artist - Wendy Carlos

## albums
**Switched-On Bach (1968)** : This was an all-electronic album that classical musician Walter Carlos recorded without using a symphony orchestra, which was considered "heresy of heresies" at the time. Carlos worked with Dr. Robert Moog to perfect the synthesizer sounds and controls, and the album went on to sell more than a million copies. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0

## curiosities
**A Clockwork Orange Soundtrack (1972)** : Carlos created the all-synth soundtrack for Stanley Kubrick's futuristic film, showcasing the potential of electronic music in cinema. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
**Collaboration with Bob Moog** : Carlos worked closely with engineer Bob Moog on the development and application of his namesake synthesizer. This collaboration helped refine the instrument for professional musical use. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
**Score for "A Clockwork Orange" (Early 1970s)** : In the early 1970s, Walter Carlos composed the all-electronic score for Stanley Kubrick's movie "A Clockwork Orange." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**Sex Change and Relocation** : The profits generated from Carlos's bold synthesizer experiments, including the "A Clockwork Orange" score, funded a sex change operation. Wendy Carlos now lives and works in New York City. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**Synthesizer Collaboration with Dr. Moog** : As Walter Carlos, she collaborated with Dr. Robert Moog to perfect the synthesizer, specifically working on the sounds it produced and how those sounds could be controlled and manipulated. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
