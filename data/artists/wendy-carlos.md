**Impact of "Switched-On Bach"** : The album was a massive commercial success and played a crucial role in legitimizing the synthesizer. It demonstrated that synthesizers could be used for sophisticated musical compositions beyond simple sound effects, opening new possibilities for electronic music and garnering critical acclaim. **Switched-On Bach (1968)** : Released in 1968, this album featured 10 pieces by Johann Sebastian Bach, entirely performed on a giant modular Moog synthesizer unit. The recording process was extensive, taking five months and over a thousand studio hours on a custom-built eight-track recorder due to the synth's monophonic nature and complex sound programming. "The result was an album entitled Switched On Bach, 10 pieces by Johann Sebastian Bach, and everything was played on a giant modular synthesizer unit supplied by Bob Mogue." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE ← wendy-carlos
**Switched-On Bach (1968) - Interpretación de Bach con Sintetizador** : Este álbum, lanzado en 1968, consistió en la reinterpretación de obras de Johann Sebastian Bach utilizando un sintetizador Moog. Se convirtió en un hito tanto comercial como artístico, logrando ganar tres premios Grammy y siendo el primer disco de música electrónica en alcanzar el Top 10 del Billboard 200. "Su álbum Switcheton Back, de 1958, desde el pretaobra de Johann Sebastian Bach en sintetizador, fue unito tanto comercial como artístico, ganando tres premios Grammy siendo el primer disco de música electrónica en estar en el Top 10 del Billboard 200." ← Ellas vieron el futuro： Pioneras de la música electrónica | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CZ5cQRm-haU

**A Clockwork Orange (1972) Score** : Wendy Carlos, a classical musician who had been collaborating with Bob Moog on the development and application of his new modular synthesizers, was hired by director Stanley Kubrick to compose the score for his 1971 futuristic dystopian film, *A Clockwork Orange*. The entirely electronic score, released as a soundtrack in 1972 (with Carlos identifying as Walter Carlos at the time), introduced sounds no one had heard before and became a landmark in the widespread development of electronic music, influencing many rock musicians seeking novel sounds. "No one had ever heard such sounds. It was completely electronic." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM ← wendy-carlos
