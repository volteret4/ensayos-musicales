**Paradise by the Dashboard Light (1977)** : This 7-minute and 56-second song faced significant challenges when released as a 7-inch single in 1977, due to the need to make it "pop" through AM radio and fit its considerable length onto one side of the record. To achieve this, the grooves on the 7-inch record had to be cut incredibly close together and narrower, which regrettably sacrificed the amount of bass that could be stored. "One of the absolute worst offenders was the 1977 Meat Love hit Paradise by the dashboard light." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=08i9lVd6T8c ← meat-loaf ← meat-loaf
**Paradise by the Dashboard Light (1978)** : Released in 1978, several months after the *Bat Out of Hell* album, this song is a theatrical performance blending drama and rock. Its theme of teenage lust depicts a boy attempting to persuade a girl to have sex in a car. The duet features Ellen Foley and Meat Loaf, with a narrative intro ("I remember every little thing...") and a unique baseball play-by-play by Yankee Legend Phil Rizzuto during the climax of the seduction attempt. Foley's character then interjects, demanding to know if he will love her forever before she goes further. After some evasion ("Let me sleep on it"), he agrees to everlasting love once she assents to "go all the way tonight," followed by a section of regret from the male character. The song "snuck into the top 40 at number 39" on Billboard, a surprising but understandable chart position given the disco era's radio landscape. "Paradise by the Dashboard Light is every bit a theatrical performance, and it's as much drama as it is rock." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 42 - Rock Operas of the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-42-rock-operas-of-the-70s ← meat-loaf

## curiosities
**Acting Background** : Meat Loaf was an actor, serving as an understudy to John Belushi on the National Lampoon Roadshow. His love for acting was evident in his musical performances, particularly in "Paradise by the Dashboard Light," which bassist Kasim Sulton noted was "indelibly linked to his whole life." "And Meatloaf was an actor. He was the understudy to John Belushi on the National Lampoon Roadshow, and he met Steinman in the early 70s when he auditioned for Steinman's 1973 musical More Than You Deserve." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 42 - Rock Operas of the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-42-rock-operas-of-the-70s ← meat-loaf
**COVID-19 as Cause of Death** : Meat Loaf died on January 20th at age 74, and despite a history of various health problems, it was ultimately COVID-19 that claimed his life. "He'd had many health problems over the years, but it appears that it was COVID that finally did him in at age 74." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sdPeii-8Ysw ← meat-loaf
**Declined to Perform for Donald Trump** : Meat Loaf turned down various requests to perform for Donald Trump, joining a list of artists who chose not to show musical support. "The killers, Celine Dion, Meat Lough and Erasmith all turned him down to play various things." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HoBx6s3JmVQ ← meat-loaf
**Guantanamo Bay Torture** : Meat Loaf's music was among the tracks used for musical torture at Camp Delta, the military prison at Guantanamo Bay. Prisoners were sometimes forced to listen to it, even through headphones, for extended periods during interrogation. "If it wasn't Eminem, the music came from Dr. Dre and Meatloaf, sometimes while being forced to wear headphones." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=R1wdsGt07Gg ← meat-loaf ← meat-loaf
**Performance of Paradise by the Dashboard Light** : According to bassist Kasim Sulton, Meat Loaf deeply enjoyed performing "Paradise by the Dashboard Light," which allowed him to fully embody an acting role. Sulton observed that Meat Loaf brought this theatricality to every song, but especially to "Paradise," feeling a profound personal connection to the material. "Meat got to act in that song, and he loved acting. He did that with every song, but especially in Paradise because it was indelibly linked to his whole life.
