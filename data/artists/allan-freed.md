# artist - Allan Freed

## curiosities
**Forced Renaming and Coining "Rock and Roll"** : After losing a lawsuit to the original Moondog (Louis Hardin Jr.), Allan Freed was forced to stop using the "Moon Dog" moniker. Needing to revamp his image and find a new catchphrase, he decided to use "rock and roll," an African-American slang phrase for sex. This term was understood by the youth but not by parents, thus becoming the widely adopted descriptor for the new type of music emerging in the 1950s. "This was a problem because Fried had to revamp his image. He needed a new catchphrase, and that's when he decided to use an African-American slang phrase for sex." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
**Pioneering Black R&B for White Audiences** : In the 1950s, Allan Freed was a disc jockey in Cleveland who played Black R&B records for white teenagers, a radical practice for the time. He referred to himself as "the Moon Dog" on air and hosted immensely successful "Moon Dog Balls" concerts. "Back in the 1950s, Alan Fried was a disc jockey and cleveland, playing black R&B records for white kids. Really radical thing to do at the time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
