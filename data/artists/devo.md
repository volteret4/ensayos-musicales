## curiosities

**Akron, Ohio Origin (1972)** : Devo formed in 1972 in Akron, Ohio, a city known for tire manufacturing. They emerged two years before The Ramones started playing at CBGB and several years before other major punk bands like The Sex Pistols and The Clash. "They came out of the tire manufacturing city of Aquino, Ohio in 1972." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9_3EbwKPnU
**Brother's Passing and Unique Urn** : Bob Casale died of heart failure in 2014. His remains were cremated and subsequently placed in a 3D-printed urn that was designed to look exactly like the distinctive "flower pot things" or Devo Energy Domes the band used to wear on their heads. "And that would be the lineup today if Bob Cassali hadn't died of heart failure in 2014. And just so you know, he was cremated and his remains were placed in a 3D printed urn that looks exactly like one of those weird flower pot things Devo used to wear in their heads." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gxbuajGKbko
**David Bowie's Advocacy (1977)** : David Bowie became aware of Devo in 1977 after a friend's wife sent him a demo tape. Bowie was highly impressed and immediately campaigned to secure them a record deal with Morecord. "In 1977, David Bowie heard about Devo when the wife of a friend sent him a demo tape. Bowie loved it and immediately campaigned to get the Morecord deal, which he managed to do." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h6snk0m4200
**David Bowie's Appreciation (1975)** : By 1975, Devo had attracted the attention of David Bowie, who recognized and appreciated their artistic message. Bowie's support was instrumental in helping Devo secure a major label record deal. "By 1975, they'd attracted the attention of David Bowie, who really appreciated what they were trying to say with their art, and he helped Divo get a major label record deal." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9_3EbwKPnU
**Devolution Concept** : The central theme of Devo's art was "devolution," the idea that mankind had begun to regress. This notion was popular in the early 1970s and evolved out of a satirical art project, maintaining a serious underlying message despite its jokey presentation. "Their thing was devolution. The idea that mankind had begun to regress, a very popular notion in the early 1970s, and they evolved out of a satirical art project." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9_3EbwKPnU
**Distinctive Image** : The band was known for its distinctive visual style, which included wearing what appeared to be radiation suits and using flower pots as hats. This look contributed to their telegenic quality in early 1980s music videos. "it was kind of cool how they all wore what looked like radiation suits and flower pots as hats." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9_3EbwKPnU
**Distinctive Image and Personas** : The band adopted a unique uniform, consisting of yellow radiation suits and hats resembling plastic flower pots, to visually represent their theory of devolution. They also sometimes appeared on stage under personas such as Buji Boy and the Chinaman. "They began to dress in a weird uniform, yellow radiation suits with hats that looked like plastic flower pots." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h6snk0m4200
**Dystopian View Popularity** : Devo became very popular with a subset of punk and post-punk bands who gravitated towards their weird, dystopian views of the world. Their unique aesthetic and conceptual approach resonated with these left-of-center artists. "Divo became very popular with a subset of punk and post punk bands who liked weird dystopian views of the world." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9_3EbwKPnU
**Electronic Evolution and Influence** : As Devo delved deeper into electronic music, their sound became even more quirky and strange, inspiring a wide array of artists across new wave, post-punk, industrial, and alt-rock genres, including the B-52s, Oingo Boingo, Nine Inch Nails, Primus, and Weezer. Even Soundgarden covered one of their songs. "As time went on, Devo got deeper into electronics, which only served to make them more quirky and strange." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h6snk0m4200
**Formation and Devolution Theory (1973)** : Devo formed in Akron, Ohio, in 1973, developing a theory of devolution, which posited that humankind was regressing or evolving backward. This intellectual concept informed their quirky, experimental music. "They were formed in Akron, Ohio in 1973 and came up with this theory of devolution." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h6snk0m4200
**Kent State University Origin** : Devo initially formed in 1973 as a "joke band" at Kent State University. "Devo first came together in 1973 as a joke band at Kent State University." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gxbuajGKbko

**Music Video Pioneers (Early 1980s)** : Devo, along with bands like the B-52s, were among the "left of center types" whose unique visual styles translated well into music videos, which became prominent in the early 1980s. Their distinct aesthetic made them highly telegenic. "Divo was joined by the B-52s and a few other left of center types, all of whom made for great music videos when that became a thing in the early 1980s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9_3EbwKPnU
