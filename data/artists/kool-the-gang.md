## curiosities
**Career Imperative to Adapt to Disco** : By the mid-1970s, Kool & the Gang faced a career imperative to adapt to the rising disco trend, prompting band leader Robert "Kool" Bell to steer the group towards a new sound. "For Cool and the Gang, however, the choice was motivated by a career imperative." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Give Up the Funk Edition Part 2 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f2ad1ba84fb8f045799
**Continued Funk Elements** : Despite their shift towards pop and R&B crossover, Kool & the Gang did not entirely abandon funk, retaining funk baselines and syncopated horns in their smoothed-out sound. "And they didn't have to give up funk entirely." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Give Up the Funk Edition Part 2 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f2ad1ba84fb8f045799
**Initial Failed Disco Attempts** : Kool & the Gang's initial attempts to embrace disco, such as the *Open Sesame* LP and the "Everybody's Dancing" single and album, were largely unsuccessful, with the album bubbling under the chart at number 207 and the single missing both the R&B Top 40 and the Hot 100 entirely. This period led to the perception that "disco had seemingly made Cool and the Gang obsolete." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Give Up the Funk Edition Part 2 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f2ad1ba84fb8f045799
**Kings of Pop and R&B Crossover in the 80s** : Though only medium-level hitmakers in the 70s, Kool & the Gang became "kings of pop and R&B crossover" in the 1980s, scoring 7 more Top 10 hits (10 total out of 13 Top 40 hits in the 80s alone) by mining their smoothed-out, gently funky pop sound. "Cool and the Gang became kings of pop and B crossover." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Give Up the Funk Edition Part 2 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f2ad1ba84fb8f045799
**Rising Generation of 70s Funk** : Kool & the Gang was part of a rising generation of syncopated bands and soulful singers in the first half of the 1970s that took up the innovations of 60s funk. "These acts from war to cool in the gang to the Ohio players, saw their funky recordings rise not just on the singles charts, but also the album chart." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Give Up the Funk Edition Part 2 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f2ad1ba84fb8f045799
**Total Overhaul and 80s Pop Sound** : Facing obsolescence after failed disco attempts, band leader Robert Bell initiated a total overhaul, bringing in a new producer, new instrumentalists, and permanent lead singer James "JT" Taylor. This resulted in a shift towards slick balladry and gently funky pop, anticipating the sound of 80s pop and R&B, which proved to be a very wise move. "They would need a total overhaul." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Give Up the Funk Edition Part 2 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f2ad1ba84fb8f045799
