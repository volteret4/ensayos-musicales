## albums
**Get The Knack (1979) - Debut Record** : The Knack's debut record, released shortly after "My Sharona" became a hit, rapidly climbed to number one in both Canada and the US. It achieved the distinction of being Capitol Records' fastest-selling album since The Beatles' "Meet the Beatles" in 1964, having been recorded for an economical $18,000. "The Nack's debut record, which hit number one in both Canada and the US, was the fastest-selling album Capital Records released since Meet the Beatles in 1964." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hstJ8M2laho
**Get The Knack (1979) – Rapid Success Story** : Released on June 11, 1979, after being recorded in two weeks for $18,000, "Get The Knack" sold 1 million copies by June 24, becoming Capitol's fastest-selling debut album since "Meet the Beatles" in 1964 and generating astounding profits. "Get The NAC was released on June 11, 1979. By June 24, just 13 days later, the album had sold 1 million copies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M

## curiosities
**Backlash from Rapid Success** : Despite their phenomenal commercial success, The Knack experienced severe backlash due to how quickly they became popular. However, their success demonstrated to other record labels that signing New Wave bands could be a profitable strategy during an economic downturn. "And even though the NAC experienced some severe backlash because they became so successful so fast, other record labels realized that their way out of the economic doldrums might just be to sign more new wave bands." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**Doug Feiger's Family and Demise** : Doug Feiger's brother, Jeffrey Feiger, was a lawyer known for representing Dr. Jack Kevorkian. Doug himself tragically died of brain and lung cancer in 2010. "You may have heard of his brother, a lawyer named Jeffrey Feiger, who later represented Dr. Jack Kavorkian in all those cases of assisted suicide." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hstJ8M2laho
**Early Failures and Breakthrough** : The Knack, originating in Detroit with singer Doug Feiger, initially faced total failure, with all their demos being rejected by record companies. Their breakthrough came when guitarist Bertene Vaire presented a guitar riff and drum part, which combined with Feiger's lyrics inspired by Sharona Elperin, led to their hit song "My Sharona." "At first, Doug and his band were total failures. All their demos had been rejected by all the record companies they approached." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hstJ8M2laho
**Reality Bites Soundtrack Contribution (1994)** : The Knack contributed to the soundtrack for *Reality Bites*, a key film in the "slacker culture" movement released in 1994. The soundtrack also featured other notable artists like U2 and Lisa Loeb. "The NAC, you two, and the Massive Hit for Lisa Loeb stay, I missed you." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM
**Sharona Elperin as Inspiration** : The song "My Sharona" was written by Doug Feiger in about 15 minutes, inspired by his infatuation with 17-year-old Sharona Elperin, whom he had recently met. Sharona Elperin is currently a real estate agent in California. "It was shortly after that, though, that Doug ran into a 17-year-old girl named Sharona Elperin." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hstJ8M2laho
