**Antichrist Superstar - Philosophical Masterpiece** : This album is considered one of the most important and captivating works in the history of rock and possibly music in general. It is a profoundly philosophical album, drawing significant inspiration from the philosopher Friedrich Nietzsche, particularly his works *The Antichrist*, *Ecce Homo*, and *Thus Spoke Zarathustra*. The album's title itself is inspired by Nietzsche's *The Antichrist* and the film *Jesus Christ Superstar*. Manson uses religion, especially Christianity, to exemplify societal problems and philosophical dilemmas, viewing religion as a means of morally constraining individuals. The album is structured into three acts, mirroring Nietzsche's three phases of human liberation, but reinterpreted by Manson in a darker and more macabre way. It features continuous references to Christianity and Judaism, with inspirations from the Jewish Kabbalah appearing in its booklet. Despite often being simplistically viewed as an attack on religion, it is a complex philosophical piece that also critiques law, police, state, and government structures.
