## curiosities
**Phantom of the Paradise – Actor and Composer (1974)** : In 1974, Paul Williams was hired by director Brian De Palma to star in and write the music for "Phantom of the Paradise," a rock musical horror comedy loosely based on "The Phantom of the Opera." He played the villain, Swan, whose music venue is terrorized by the disfigured Winslow Leech. The film was a box office flop globally, except for its bizarre and enduring popularity in Winnipeg. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Potential Proto-Goth/Death Metal Inventor (1974)** : Paul Williams' work in "Phantom of the Paradise" in 1974, particularly the "Rock Opera" within the film and the fictional band The Undead (whose singer, Peter Ebling, wore corpse paint similar to modern death/black metal), leads to a theory that he accidentally invented proto-goth or proto-death metal music. Goth music didn't widely emerge until bands like Bauhaus in the late 1970s, and death metal in the mid-1980s. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
