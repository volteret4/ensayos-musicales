## curiosities
**"We've Only Just Begun" Origin (1970)** : As a journeyman songwriter, Paul Williams co-wrote "We've Only Just Begun" and sang the jingle for a Crocker Citizens Bank commercial. This exposure caught the ear of Richard Carpenter, leading to The Carpenters' hugely successful rendition and the song's transformation into a pop classic. "The Jingle was sung by the man who co-wrote it, Journeyman Songwriter Paul Williams." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > I’d Like to Teach the World to Buy Edition Part 1 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f1264fe6d21276e0cc5
**Additional Verses for Freddie Allen** : Williams and his songwriting partner had penned a bridge and a third verse for a version of "We've Only Just Begun" that was recorded by an artist named Freddie Allen. However, Allen's version of the song ultimately did not achieve hit status. "As it turned out, Williams and his songwriting partner had penned a bridge and a third verse for a version of the song recorded by one Freddie Allen." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > I’d Like to Teach the World to Buy Edition Part 1 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f1264fe6d21276e0cc5
**Career-Breaking Song** : Paul Williams personally credits "We've Only Just Begun" with being the pivotal song that broke his career, bringing him significant recognition and opening new doors in the music industry due to its widespread success. **Phantom of the Paradise – Actor and Composer (1974)** : In 1974, Paul Williams was hired by director Brian De Palma to star in and write the music for "Phantom of the Paradise," a rock musical horror comedy loosely based on "The Phantom of the Opera." He played the villain, Swan, whose music venue is terrorized by the disfigured Winslow Leech. The film was a box office flop globally, except for its bizarre and enduring popularity in Winnipeg. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU ← paul-williams
**Potential Proto-Goth/Death Metal Inventor (1974)** : Paul Williams' work in "Phantom of the Paradise" in 1974, particularly the "Rock Opera" within the film and the fictional band The Undead (whose singer, Peter Ebling, wore corpse paint similar to modern death/black metal), leads to a theory that he accidentally invented proto-goth or proto-death metal music. Goth music didn't widely emerge until bands like Bauhaus in the late 1970s, and death metal in the mid-1980s. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU ← paul-williams
**Proto-Goth/Death Metal Theory** : A theory suggests that Paul Williams, through his work on the 1974 film 'Phantom of the Paradise,' may have accidentally invented goth or death metal music, preceding their recognized emergence in the late 1970s and mid-1980s, respectively. **Swan Character in Phantom of the Paradise** : Paul Williams played the villainous character Swan in Brian De Palma's 1974 film 'Phantom of the Paradise,' an evil figure who steals Winslow Leech's music and operates "The Paradise" music venue.
