# artist - Claire Torry

## songs
**The Great Gig in the Sky (1973)** : Claire Torry's extraordinary improvised vocal performance on Pink Floyd's "The Great Gig in the Sky" was recorded in 1973 at Abbey Road Studios. Engineer Alan Parsons directed her to listen to the music, feel it, and "let her rip" without words, pretending to be a musical instrument, resulting in a compilation of about half a dozen takes that became legendary. "That was the sum total of the direction she was given, and what came out was extraordinary." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE ← claire-torry

## curiosities
**Acquired Songwriting Credit** : Following her 2004 lawsuit against Pink Floyd, the case was settled out of court for an undisclosed sum. As a result of this settlement, all editions of "Dark Side of the Moon" issued after 2005 include the credit "vocal composition by Claire Torry" in the liner notes, acknowledging her creative contribution to the song. "In 2004, Claire sued Pink Floyd on the basis that her performance, something that she created, was such an integral part of the song that she was do some cash." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE ← claire-torry
**Compensation and Royalties Dispute** : For her iconic performance on "The Great Gig in the Sky," Claire Torry was paid a standard flat fee of 30 pounds, which is equivalent to approximately $400 today, with no royalties. In 2004, she sued Pink Floyd, arguing that her performance was so integral to the song that she deserved a songwriting credit and additional compensation, highlighting issues of artist compensation. "For that performance, which, by the way, is a compilation of maybe half a dozen takes, Claire was paid a standard flat fee of 30 pounds with no royalties." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE ← claire-torry
