# artist - Danger Mouse

## instruments
- Bass (on *Attack and Release*)
- Keyboards (on *Attack and Release*)

## curiosities
**Demon Days Production** : Danger Mouse was enlisted by Damon Albarn to produce the Gorillaz album "Demon Days," helping to manage the project's complexities. "To help sort out all the complexities, Damon called up Danger Mouse to produce the project." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iVdTCqpd798
**Ike Turner Production Commitment** : Danger Mouse's prior commitment to producing an album for Ike Turner initially prevented him from working with The Black Keys on *Attack and Release*. However, the eventual collapse of the Ike Turner project freed him up to collaborate with the band. "Unfortunately, he was unavailable because he was committed to producing an album for Ike Turner." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PmwfC4HR3_I
**Producer Collaboration with Black Keys** : Danger Mouse became The Black Keys' first external producer for their fifth album, *Attack and Release*, playing keyboards and bass on the record. He was initially unavailable due to a commitment to produce for Ike Turner, but that project's cancellation made him accessible. He later produced one song on *Brothers* and then fully produced and co-wrote all 11 tracks on *El Camino*. "Danger Mouse played a bunch of different instruments on the album, keyboards and bass mainly, and a few other musicians were called into filling with guitars and vocals and even clarinets." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PmwfC4HR3_I
