## songs
**Vacant / Passive (2004)** : "Vacant" was a Tapeworm song written by Danny Loner, arranged by Charlie Clouzer, with background vocals by Trent Reznor and lyrics/melody by Maynard James Keenan. When Tapeworm dissolved, Maynard repurposed the song for A Perfect Circle's 2004 cover album *Emotive*, where it appeared under the title "Passive." "I'm going to play you something called vacant. It was written by Danny Loner, arranged by Charlie Clouzer, featured background vocals by Trent's and lyrics in melody by Maynard. ## curiosities
**Project History (1995-2004)** : Tapeworm was a Nine Inch Nails-related side project that existed in various forms from 1995 to sometime in 2004, featuring Trent Reznor and his live band members Danny Loner and Charlie Clouzer. Numerous other artists, including Maynard James Keenan (Tool), producer Alan Molder, Atticus Ross, Paige Hamilton (Helmet), Tony Halliday (Curve), Phil Anselmo (Pantera), and Tommy Victor (Prong, Ministry), were involved. Despite promises of release dates and reports in 2003 that the album was ready to be mixed, the project was ultimately killed by Reznor due to creative differences, Maynard's obligations with A Perfect Circle, and Reznor's decision to resurrect Nine Inch Nails, stating the material "just wasn't good enough." "This was a 9-inch nails-related side project that existed in various forms from 1995 to sometime in 2004... Trent decided to resurrect 9-inch nails. Maynard had obligations with his side projects a perfect circle. There were fights over creative direction, and then Trent killed everything, saying that the material just wasn't good enough." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_lSbUUfBNNI
**Recording Sessions and Near Completion (2001-2003)** : The Tapeworm project saw significant recording activity, with producer Alan Molder assisting in recording at least an album's worth of material by 2001. Further sessions were held in New Orleans and Atlanta in 2003, with reports indicating that an album was ready to be mixed, suggesting it was close to completion before various obstacles arose. "And then in 2001, Alan Molder apparently helped record at least an album's worth of stuff. In 2003, there were more recording sessions of both New Orleans and Atlanta. And then when we were told that an album was ready to mix." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gdWLtMy4TBU
**Supergroup Lineup and Origins (1994-2001)** : Tapeworm was conceived as a side project during Nine Inch Nails' "The Downward Spiral" sessions in 1994, intended as a collaborative effort for Danny Lohner and Charlie Clouser. It evolved to include a star-studded lineup: Trent Reznor, Maynard James Keenan (Tool), producer Alan Molder, Atticus Ross, Phil Anselmo and Rex Brown (Pantera), Page Hamilton (Helmet), Toni Halliday (Curve), Tommy Victor (Ministry), and drummer Josh Freese, with at least an album's worth of material recorded by 2001. "If you're Trent Rezner, Danny Loaner and Charlie Closer from his live band, Maynard James Keenan from Tool, producer Alan Molder and Atticus Ross. I mean, wow, awesome, right? The idea for tapeworm went as far back as the sessions for the downward spiral in 1994. Tapeworm was designed as something to which Danny and Charlie could contribute as equals. This idea evolved into something pretty cool. Maynard was brought in, Phil and Salmo from Pantera, Paige Hamilton from Helmut, Tony Haliday from a cool band called Curve, Tommy Victor from Ministry, drummer Josh Freeze. And then in 2001, Alan Molder apparently helped record at least an album's worth of stuff." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gdWLtMy4TBU
**Survival of Tracks as "Tapeworm DNA"** : Despite the Tapeworm album never being officially released, two tracks are noted to carry its "DNA": "Passive" on A Perfect Circle's 2004 album "Emotive" (a descendant of a song called "Vacant"), and "Potions" which appeared on a Puscifer album. These releases offer glimpses into the material created during the project. "Two release tracks do have tapeworm DNA, though. One is called passive. It's on a perfect circle's 2004 album, Emotive. It's a descendant from a song called Vacant. Another is Potions, which appeared on a pusifer album." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gdWLtMy4TBU
