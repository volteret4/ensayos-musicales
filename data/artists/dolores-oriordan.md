**Aer Lingus Air Rage Arrest and Bipolar Diagnosis (2014-2016)** : On November 10, 2014, Dolores O'Riordan was arrested after a disturbance on Aer Lingus flight 110 from New York to Shannon, about 20 minutes before landing. She was described as irrational and aggressive, injuring a flight attendant, headbutting a police officer, and resisting arrest while proclaiming, "I'm an icon. I'm the queen of Limerick." Transferred to St. Patrick's Psychiatric Hospital, she was diagnosed with bipolar disorder, paranoid and delusional, and stayed for three weeks. She pleaded guilty in December 2015 and was fined $6,600 Canadian on February 24, 2016, with the judge noting her mental illness. The episode was attributed to stress, sleep deprivation, recent separation from her husband of 21 years, and the second anniversary of her father's death. **Guilty Plea and Bipolar Disorder Diagnosis (2015-2016)** : In December 2015, Dolores O'Riordan pleaded guilty to charges of air rage, stating she had no memory of the events but did not deny her involvement. She was subsequently diagnosed with bipolar disorder, with the episode attributed to hypomania triggered by stress and sleep deprivation, exacerbated by her recent separation from her husband of 21 years, the second anniversary of her father's death, and weeks away from her children while living out of a suitcase. On February 24, 2016, she was fined $6,600 Canadian, with the judge noting it would be unfair to be harsher given her mental illness. "She was diagnosed as being genuinely mentally ill, paranoid and delusional." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hmnMG6f80Sg
