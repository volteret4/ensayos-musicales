## curiosities
**Compared to P-Funk Stage Shows** : Earth Wind & Fire's stage shows were acknowledged as elaborate but were sometimes considered less extravagant than P-Funk's Mother Ship Tour, which featured a full-scale spaceship. **Contrast with Parliament Funkadelic** : Earth Wind & Fire represented the "heightened super-ego" of funk, contrasted with Parliament Funkadelic, which was described as the "id." *The Rolling Stone Encyclopedia of Rock and Roll* positioned P-Funk as EWF's "ideological rivals," characterized as "down and dirty, but equally eclectic." "If Earth, Wind, and Fire represented funk's heightened super-eagong, you might say Parliament Funkadelic was the id." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Give Up the Funk Edition Part 2 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f2ad1ba84fb8f045799
**Early Black Exploitation Soundtrack** : Earth Wind & Fire served as the uncredited backing band for Melvin Van Peables' seminal 1971 film *Sweet Sweet Back's Badass Song*, a foundational black exploitation movie. Their contribution to the soundtrack, which All Music describes as "serviceable period Funk Soul," is barely discernible, with the main telltale sign being Maurice White's kalimba playing. "Frankly, you can barely tell Earth Wind and Fire are playing on that soundtrack, which all music calls, quote, serviceable period Funk Soul." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Give Up the Funk Edition Part 2 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f2ad1ba84fb8f045799
**Early Record Deal and Dissolution** : After signing with Warner Brothers Records, Earth Wind & Fire released two albums and contributed to the *Sweet Back* soundtrack, but these achieved only modest success. Consequently, Maurice White disbanded the initial group, retaining only the band name and his brother, bassist Verdean White, before signing a new deal with Columbia Records in 1973. "White disbanded the group, retaining only the Earth Wind and Fire name, and his brother, bassist Verdean White, signed to a new deal with Columbia Records in 1973..." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Give Up the Funk Edition Part 2 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f2ad1ba84fb8f045799
**Evolution of Live Shows** : By 2022, EWF's live shows no longer featured onstage pyrotechnics or magic tricks by Doug Henning. Pyramid images and laser lights were projected onto screens, and the band no longer wore elaborate wide collars, sequins, or moon boots, though the distinctive groove and undeniable funk remained. "There are no onstage pyrotechnics anymore at an EWF show, no magic tricks, the pyramid images and laser lights are now just projected onto a screen." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Give Up the Funk Edition Part 2 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f2ad1ba84fb8f045799

Maurice White sometimes incorporated stunts designed by magician Doug Henning, and the band's stage presence was often augmented by their regular brass combo, the Phoenix Horns. **Sophisticated Album Art and Iconography** : Earth Wind & Fire's albums were known for their elaborate gatefold sleeves, intricate Afrocentric imagery, and slick, artfully arranged band photos. This visual presentation elevated R&B and funk iconography beyond even groups like the Ohio Players, making EWF albums comparable to progressive or hard rock albums and appealing to a broader audience who collected LPs alongside acts like Led Zeppelin. "EWF albums, even more than the sexy covers from the Ohio players, upped the ante for R&B and funk iconography." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Give Up the Funk Edition Part 2 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f2ad1ba84fb8f045799
