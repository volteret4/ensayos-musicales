## curiosities
**Discovery by Andy Warhol (1965)** : In December 1965, The Velvet Underground secured their first regular gig at Cafe Bizarre in Greenwich Village, New York City, where they were contracted to play six sets a night, six nights a week, for five dollars per set. However, their unique and "weird" music frequently drove away patrons, leading to them being fired at least once. Just before a second dismissal, Andy Warhol and his entourage (including groupies and "weirdos") visited the club. Warhol was profoundly impressed by the band's "avant-garde approach to rock" and immediately signed them to be his official house band at his performance art headquarters, The Factory, on East 47th Street. Warhol assumed the roles of their patron, producer, and promoter, providing the band with a crucial break that allowed them to embark on their "long, strange road to becoming legends" and eventually be hailed as the first alternative rock band in history. Without Warhol's intervention, it is speculated their career trajectory would have been vastly different. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8UIBWyUt38g
