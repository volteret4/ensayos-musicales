# artist - The Velvet Underground

## members
- Maureen Tucker

## genres
- Alternative

## songs
**Heroin** : A song by the band, used to exemplify Maureen Tucker's unique drumming style, noting the "lack of snare or symbols or hi hats or Anything remotely rock and roll."

## curiosities
**Early Application of "New Wave" Term (Early 1970s)** : Referred to as "New Wave" or "a New Wave in rock" by some writers in the early 1970s, due to their rejection of traditional blues-based rock and roll norms.
**First Female Rock Drummer (1965)** : May have been the first rock band of any kind to feature a female drummer, Maureen Tucker, who joined in 1965.
**First True Alternative Band** : Considered by many to be the first true alternative band.
**Previous Drummer's Fate** : Their first drummer quit the band and moved to Nepal, where he eventually died of malnutrition.
**Unconventional Drumming** : Their drum parts, specifically by Maureen Tucker, were noted for a lack of snare, cymbals, or hi-hats, contributing to their distinctive sound.
