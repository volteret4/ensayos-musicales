## albums
**Debut Album (1966) – Scepter Studios Sessions** : An acetate pressing from the Scepter Studios Sessions, which predated and contained material for the band's debut album, was discovered and later sold as the most valuable alt-rock collectible. This brittle aluminum acetate could only be played a limited number of times before being destroyed. "These were known as the Scepter Studios Sessions." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zWNzlSxrj6s
**The Velvet Underground & Nico (1967) – Debut Album** : This debut album, credited as being produced by Andy Warhol (though he primarily provided inspiration rather than technical input), was scandalous, shocking, noisy, and angry. Its themes openly addressed sex, drugs, life on the street, S&M, and getting wasted, which was revolutionary for 1967. Despite its explicit content and raw sound, it only reached number 199 on the Billboard Top 200 Chart before disappearing, but it served as a crucial alternative to sugary pop and psychedelic rock for a discerning audience. "The album was just too raw and too real to gain any kind of mainstream attention." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U
**Unreleased Acetate (1966)** : In April 1966, an acetate was secretly made after hours at Scepter Studios in New York City, containing material that would later appear on The Velvet Underground's debut record but with different recordings. This unique item was lost for years until Montreal record collector Warren Hill discovered it at a New York flea market in Chelsea, purchasing it for 75 cents. After an initial high bid of $155,000 on eBay fell through, it was successfully sold to a high-end dealer in 2014 for $25,200. Recordings from this "crackly and rough" acetate have since been made publicly available. "As for the most valuable and collectible alternative record, the answer seems to be a velvet underground acetate made in secret after hours at Sector Studios in New York City in April of 1966." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IIJySSo276k
**White Light/White Heat (1968)** : The second album by The Velvet Underground, released in 1968, exemplifies their raw and distorted sound. The track "Sister Ray" from this album was recorded in one take with no overdubs. Ron Kale contributed to the distorted sound by tuning an electric keyboard as low as it could go and then turning the volume as high as possible. Live performances of this track could extend for up to three-quarters of an hour, pushing every amp and instrument to its limits to create distortion and feedback. "Ron Kale uses an electric keyboard tuned down as low as it will go. And then, he turned up the volume as high as possible, which is how he got that distorted sound." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w

**Sister Ray (1968)** : Featured on The Velvet Underground's 1968 album "White Light/White Heat," this song was recorded in a single take without overdubs. Its heavily distorted sound was achieved by Ron Kale tuning an electric keyboard to its lowest possible setting and then maximizing its volume. When performed live, the track could extend for up to three-quarters of an hour, with the band deliberately overdriving every amp and instrument to generate intense distortion and feedback. "The Velvet Underground and Sister Ray from 1968 creating distortion and feedback the old fashion way, over driving every amp and every instrument they had." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w

## curiosities
**Andy Warhol's Producer Credit** : Andy Warhol was credited as the producer for the band's 1967 debut record. However, he admitted to not knowing much about making records, and his role was primarily to inspire and encourage the band, not to handle any technical aspects of production. "Okay, he was credited as being the producer but he didn't really know much about making records. He was just there to inspire and encourage them. He didn't touch anything." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U
**Brittle Aluminum Acetate** : The most valuable Velvet Underground collectible was a brittle, aluminum acetate pressing that contained an alternate take of "Heroin" from their Scepter Studios Sessions. Such a record could only be played about two dozen times before wearing out and being destroyed, highlighting its extreme fragility and rarity, though it was eventually ripped to MP3 for preservation. "Now a brittle, aluminum acetate, such as this, cannot be played more than two dozen times because it'll just wear out and be destroyed." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zWNzlSxrj6s
**Cafe Bizarre Residency (Spring 1966)** : In the spring of 1966, the band began a residency at The Cafe Bizarre in Greenwich Village, New York, where their raw and gritty style attracted attention. "In the spring of 1966, they began a residency at a club called The Cafe Bazaar. This was in Greenwich Village." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U
**Contrast with Mainstream and Psychedelic Rock** : The Velvet Underground offered something new and important for listeners who were disillusioned with "sugary pop" on AM radio or the "acid wash" of psychedelic rock. Their raw and real sound resonated with those seeking something different. "But for those who were looking for something other than the sugary pop that was being played on AM radio and who didn't like the acid wash of psychedelic rock, The Velvet Underground represented something new and important." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U
**Early "New Wave" Designation** : In the early 1970s, some writers began to refer to The Velvet Underground as "new wave" or "a new wave in rock," applying the term to artists who rejected the traditional norms of blues-based rock and roll, similar to its use in French cinema. "some writers began referring to groups like the Velvet Underground and the New York dolls as new wave, or at least a new wave in rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
**Early Alternative Sound** : The Velvet Underground is frequently cited as the first alternative band, known for their raw, rough sound, which heavily featured distortion, feedback, and noise. They pioneered an approach where instruments were often overdriven to their limits, creating a distinct and influential sound. "Louis previous band, the Velvet Underground, has often been called the first alternative band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w
**First Paying Gig (December 11, 1965)** : The Velvet Underground played their first paying gig on December 11, 1965, at a high school in New Jersey. They were paid $75 for the performance. "They played their first paying gig on December 11, 1965. It was a high school in New Jersey and they were paid $75." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U

**First True Alternative Band** : The Velvet Underground is widely considered perhaps the first true alternative band, pioneering a sound and approach that laid groundwork for the alt-rock movement. Their early work and influence are highly regarded in music history. "perhaps the first true alternative band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zWNzlSxrj6s
**Formation as the First Alternative Rock Band (Spring 1965)** : The Velvet Underground began to form in the spring of 1965, with rehearsals starting that summer. By December 1965, their lineup was complete, and they are widely considered to be the first ever alternative rock band. "A few months later, Lew and his bandmate, the Welsh-born John Kale, would be part of what is considered to be the first ever alternative rock band, The Velvet Underground." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U
**Fringe Status and Bowie's Admiration** : The Velvet Underground, featuring Lou Reed, occupied a position on the extreme fringes of the music scene, with little recognition outside of "Uber hipsters." Despite their obscurity, David Bowie was a great admirer of the band, particularly Lou Reed, recognizing their unique significance. "No one except the Uber hipsters gave a damn or even knew about Lou Reed in the velvet underground." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
**Mentorship by Andy Warhol** : Andy Warhol, the pop artist, heard The Velvet Underground at The Cafe Bizarre and immediately brought them into his circle. He hired them to perform at the "freaky parties" held at The Factory, his New York headquarters, becoming their mentor and chief patron. "And one guy who heard about their raw and gritty approach was Andy Warhol." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U
**Most Valuable Alt-Rock Collectible (2006)** : A one-of-a-kind, undiscovered acetate pressing of the Scepter Studios Sessions for The Velvet Underground's debut album became the world's most valuable collectible alt-rock record. Discovered in September 2002 by Warren Hill for 75 cents at a street sale in New York City, it was eventually sold in December 2006 for $25,200 after an initial eBay bid of over $155,000 fell through. "making it, for the time being, the world's most valuable, collectible, alt-rock record." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zWNzlSxrj6s
