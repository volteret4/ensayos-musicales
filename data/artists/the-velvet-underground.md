**The Velvet Underground & Nico (1967)** : The cover artwork for this album, designed by Andy Warhol, features a banana with the instructions "peel slowly and see." When peeled, the banana skin reveals a flesh-colored banana underneath, inviting interpretation. This type of packaging was very expensive to produce; subsequent editions were unpeelable. Original releases in good condition are highly valuable on the collector's market. A 2008 edition included a sticker to replicate the original effect. "The cover featured a banana with the instructions peel slowly and see. If you peel back the banana skin, you've got a flesh-colored banana. So read into that what you want." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pwebM3TfSdg
**The Velvet Underground & Nico (1967) – Debut Album** : This debut album, credited as being produced by Andy Warhol (though he primarily provided inspiration rather than technical input), was scandalous, shocking, noisy, and angry. Its themes openly addressed sex, drugs, life on the street, S&M, and getting wasted, which was revolutionary for 1967. Despite its explicit content and raw sound, it only reached number 199 on the Billboard Top 200 Chart before disappearing, but it served as a crucial alternative to sugary pop and psychedelic rock for a discerning audience. "The album was just too raw and too real to gain any kind of mainstream attention." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U
**The Velvet Underground (1969) - Third Album Influence** : The third album by The Velvet Underground was a key influence on Sloan's "Twice Removed," inspiring the band's deliberate move towards a quieter and more introspective musical direction. "I think some of the records that we're listening to more were like Fleetwood Mac or the third Velvet Underground album, which is sort of quieter records, you know." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
**The Velvet Underground and Nico (1965) - Debut Album** : Released in 1965, this debut album was starkly different from mainstream rock and roll which focused on cars and girls; instead, it explored the gritty realities of New York City, including crime, weird sex, strange people, and drugs, which was considered too extreme at the time. Despite selling only about 30,000 copies between 1965 and 1970 and peaking at number 199 on the Billboard Top 200 album charts, its influence was immense, with producer Brian Eno famously stating that everyone who bought a copy started a band. "When their debut album arrived in 1965, rock and roll was largely about cars and girls and various escapists and fun topics. Not the velvet underground. They were from the gritty streets of New York City where there was crime, weird sex, strange people and drugs, lots and lots of drugs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cy5SCQV0HAY
**Unreleased Acetate (1966)** : In April 1966, an acetate was secretly made after hours at Scepter Studios in New York City, containing material that would later appear on The Velvet Underground's debut record but with different recordings. This unique item was lost for years until Montreal record collector Warren Hill discovered it at a New York flea market in Chelsea, purchasing it for 75 cents. After an initial high bid of $155,000 on eBay fell through, it was successfully sold to a high-end dealer in 2014 for $25,200. Recordings from this "crackly and rough" acetate have since been made publicly available. "As for the most valuable and collectible alternative record, the answer seems to be a velvet underground acetate made in secret after hours at Sector Studios in New York City in April of 1966." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IIJySSo276k
**White Light/White Heat (1967)** : This album provides a notable example of extreme channel separation in early stereo mixing, where instruments and vocals were panned hard left and hard right, with little to no sound in the center. This mixing approach, exemplified by the track "The Gift," was an experimental and often distracting use of early stereo technology. "In 1967, the Velvet Underground released the white light white heat album." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cbSmBrhNACg
**White Light/White Heat (1968)** : The second album by The Velvet Underground, released in 1968, exemplifies their raw and distorted sound. The track "Sister Ray" from this album was recorded in one take with no overdubs. Ron Kale contributed to the distorted sound by tuning an electric keyboard as low as it could go and then turning the volume as high as possible. Live performances of this track could extend for up to three-quarters of an hour, pushing every amp and instrument to its limits to create distortion and feedback. "Ron Kale uses an electric keyboard tuned down as low as it will go. And then, he turned up the volume as high as possible, which is how he got that distorted sound." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w

**Scepter Studios Demos (1966) – Discovered by Warren Hill** : On April 25, 1966, The Velvet Underground recorded demos at Scepter Studios in New York, resulting in two acetate test pressings that were circulated to record companies, all of whom declined to sign the band. The band later signed with Verve, re-recording their material. In 2002, Warren Hill found one of these acetates at a flea market in Chelsea, New York, for 75 cents. After researching its rarity, he auctioned it on eBay for $25,200, and it has since been digitized for wider listening. "What he had bought was one of only two copies of this test pressing. It featured the velvet underground recording some demos at Scepter Studios in New York on April 25th 1966." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tbmssCZ05ro
