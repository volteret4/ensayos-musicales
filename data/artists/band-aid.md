## songs
**Do They Know It's Christmas? (1984)** : The creation of this charity single was spearheaded by Bob Geldof, inspired by a BBC documentary on the Ethiopian famine he watched on November 11, 1984. Initially, Geldof thought of a benefit record for The Boomtown Rats, but realizing it wouldn't sell well, he enlisted Midge Ure of Ultravox, who took one of Geldof's half-finished songs ("It's My World") and composed the music. With lyrics written in a taxi by Geldof, 25 of Britain's most famous musicians, including Sting, Simon Le Bon, Bono, David Bowie, and Paul Weller, gathered at Sarm Studios in Notting Hill, London, on November 25, 1984, to record the track. The single was released on December 7, entered the charts at number one on December 15, and held the top position for five weeks, selling over three million copies in the UK (making it one of the biggest-selling UK singles of all time) and an additional one million in the US. This project marked the beginning of Band Aid, raising significant funds and inspiring subsequent charity efforts like Live Aid and other charity singles, ultimately fostering a belief that "Rock and Roll did have a conscience and that music could make a difference." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Somq2MoRi5Q
