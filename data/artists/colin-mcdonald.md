# artist - Colin McDonald

## member of
- The Trews

## curiosities
**Early Bands (High School)** : Before forming The Trews, Colin McDonald played in "not serious" punk and grunge bands with friends in high school while living in Newfoundland and Nova Scotia. His first band with a buddy from grade nine was called "One-eyed Trouser Snake," inspired by Monty Python's "Meaning of Life." "I started playing in like not serious like kind of punk and grunge bands in high school with my friends." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Emotional Reaction to "Highway of Heroes"** : Colin McDonald was deeply moved when he first played "Highway of Heroes," a song he co-wrote about Nikola Goddard. He "started to cry," an unusual reaction for him to his own work, recognizing the song's profound power. "I started to cry. And I never done that with anything." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Illness on Ace Frehley Tour** : Colin McDonald suffered a severe bronchial infection before and during the first three weeks of a six-week US tour opening for Ace Frehley. This left him unable to sing his usual high, hard vocal parts, which was particularly challenging given the audience was primarily "die-hard Kiss fans." He eventually recovered his voice after getting antibiotics in the US. "for the first like three weeks at Ace Freyly tour, I couldn't sing. It was a nightmare." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
