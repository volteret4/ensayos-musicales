## curiosities
**"The Drifting Cowboys" Band Name Origin** : Hank Williams named his band "The Drifting Cowboys" out of his deep admiration for Western movies and the figure of the cowboy. The band members adopted full Western attire, and Williams himself always wore his characteristic cream suit and large cowboy hat. "Hank Williams amaba las películas de lo este y la figura del vaquero, por eso le puso a su nombre a su banda, los cuales vestían con toda la indumentaria de lo este, y él empezó a vestir siempre con su característico, traje crema y su gran sombrero de vaquero." ← El genio roto del country： La leyenda de Hank Williams | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qczx7qWg4xM
**Band Instability Due to Alcoholism** : Following the departure of his original band members for WWII, substitute musicians who joined The Drifting Cowboys rarely lasted more than a day, primarily due to Hank Williams' severe alcoholism. This addiction also led to his dismissal from radio station WSFA. "Los miembros institutos que entraban en la banda, no aguantaban más de un día debido al alcoholismo de Williams. Esa abicción le llevó a que la misora WSFA lo despidiera." ← El genio roto del country： La leyenda de Hank Williams | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qczx7qWg4xM
**Birth and Early Life** : Born Hiram King Williams on September 17, 1923, in Montolive, Alabama. His father, a veteran of the First World War, worked on the railways, leading the family to move frequently. When Hank was seven, his father suffered facial paralysis from a war accident and was admitted to a veterans' hospital for the rest of his life, leaving Hank to be raised by his mother. "en Montolive, a la Bama, el 17 de septiembre de 1923, nace Jirán King Williams, que pasara la historia como Hanck Williams." ← El genio roto del country： La leyenda de Hank Williams | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qczx7qWg4xM
**Brutal Rawness and Self-Destruction** : Hank Williams sang with a brutal rawness and lived completely on the margins of society, leading a self-destructive lifestyle. His coherent embrace of this path, which culminated in his early death, mirrored the spirit of many later punk artists, even preceding them in his rebellious ethos. "Hank Williams cantaba con una crudeza brutal, vivía al margen de todo y se autodestruyó con más coherencia que muchos panks que vinieron después." ← El punk existía antes de Sex Pistols (y era algo salvaje) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ilXpBujkWZE

**Final Journey and Death (December 31, 1952)** : On New Year's Eve 1952, Hank Williams canceled a show due to an ice storm and hired Charles Carr, a university student, to drive him in his Cadillac Series 62 to a concert in Canton, Ohio. During the journey, he complained of pain and was given an injection of morphine and analgesics, which he mixed with alcohol. He collapsed in the backseat after writing a song. Four hours later, in Oak Hill, West Virginia, Carr discovered Williams' hand was cold, revealing his death at age 29. "En la vispera del año nuevo de 1952, Hanke Williams canceló un espectáculo por una tormenta de hielo, y encargo a Charles Carr, un estudiante universitario, llevarle en su Cadillac series 62 a nuevo concierto en Canton, o hallo." ← El genio roto del country： La leyenda de Hank Williams | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qczx7qWg4xM
**First Guitar and Mentorship** : At the age of eight, Hank Williams' mother gave him his first guitar. He spent most of his days playing it on the street, where he met Rufus "Tee Tot" Payne, an African-American Blues musician. Payne showed Williams that music extended beyond sheet music, focusing on lived experience and feeling, and taught him the importance of honesty and an unshakeable rhythm, which became foundational to his style. "se crió con su madre, que el de regaló su primera guitarra cuando tenía 8 años." ← El genio roto del country： La leyenda de Hank Williams | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qczx7qWg4xM
**Honky Tonk Performances and Alcohol Consumption** : During his tours through the Southern United States, Hank Williams frequently performed in honky tonks, which were rustic bars typical of the region. According to his biographers, he spent a significant portion of his show earnings on alcohol consumed in these venues, marking the beginning of his severe drinking problem. "Cuando esos conciertos donde Hank empezó a consumir grandes cantidades de alcohol, según relató sus biografías, gastaba a gran parte de los ingresos del espectáculo en beber en esos locales." ← El genio roto del country： La leyenda de Hank Williams | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qczx7qWg4xM

**Life of Pain and Instability (1952)** : The year 1952, his last year of life, was marked by constant pain and pressure despite his external popularity.
