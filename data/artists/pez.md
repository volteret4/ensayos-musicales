# artist - Pez

## members
- Johnny (owned a 4-track)

## albums
**Demoluca (1994) - Four-Song Demo** : This was a four-song demo recorded in 1994 in Johnny's parents' basement using a Tascam 4-track. Only about 100 copies were made, and it was instrumental in helping the band secure early performances in downtown Toronto. "We recorded our first four-song demo in my parents' basement, and we called it Demoluca. That was done in 1994, and we used that to try to get shows downtown." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r4QJMx1LES8

## songs
**Happy (1993 Demo)** : This home demo recording of the song "Happy" is from 1993, when the band was known as Pez, prior to becoming Billy Talent. "That's Billy Talent from 1993, when they were still trading under the name Pez. It's a home demo recording of a song called Happy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r4QJMx1LES8

## curiosities
**Limited Release Demo** : Only around 100 copies of the "Demoluca" demo tape were produced, making it a highly sought-after item that occasionally appears on sites like eBay. "There was only a hundred copies made. Well, look at this. From Demoluca, here's Pez, and Happy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r4QJMx1LES8
**Recording in Parents' Basement** : Pez recorded their first four-song demo, "Demoluca," in 1994 in "Johnny's" parents' basement, utilizing a Tascam 4-track recorder. "There was a couple of tapes we recorded first in my parents' basement on Johnny's. He used to have a four track and back then it was a hard thing to find and he had a task in four tracks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r4QJMx1LES8
**Securing First Toronto Gigs** : The band gained their first major opportunity in downtown Toronto by providing their "Demoluca" tape to William New, who organized "Elvis Mondays" at the Old Elmucongo. A last-minute cancellation led to their initial booking, which subsequently led to more gigs and networking with other local bands like The Salads and A Flatulite. "Our first big break downtown was we gave this tape to a guy named William New, who had the oldest Mondays at the Old Elmucongo." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r4QJMx1LES8
