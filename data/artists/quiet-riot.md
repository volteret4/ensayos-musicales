## albums
**Metal Health (1983)** : This album, released in 1983, was the first by a heavy metal band to reach number one on the charts, marking a significant milestone for the genre. The band's popularity was immensely boosted by its accompanying video, which featured lead singer Kevin Dubrow in a face mask and red straightjacket, escaping a padded cell to join the band on stage. The crowd depicted in the video consisted of students from the California Institute of the Arts. The video for "Metal Health" cost $10,000 to make. **Early Formation and Industry Frustration** : Quiet Riot formed eight years before 1984, playing in Los Angeles clubs like the Whisky a Go Go since the mid-1970s. The band members expressed significant frustration that fellow LA hard rockers Van Halen secured a major record deal before them, despite believing Quiet Riot was more popular. This perceived injustice contributed to Randy Rhoads' decision to leave the band and join Ozzy Osbourne. **MTV's Role in Early Band Exposure** : Bassist Randy Sarzo explained that in MTV's early days, the channel lacked sufficient video content from bands, as music videos were not typically part of band budgets focused on radio airplay for publishing residuals. Record companies initially hesitated to fund expensive videos due to a perceived lack of return, but eventually saw increased record sales, which included publishing and mechanicals, as justification. Quiet Riot's first video for "Metal Health" cost $10,000, and the subsequent video for "Cum On Feel the Noize" cost $40,000, significantly boosting their exposure through constant rotation on MTV, even before MTV had widespread subscriptions, as stores and bars began playing the channel.
