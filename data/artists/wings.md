## songs
**Live and Let Die (1973) – Worldwide Hit** : Paul McCartney and Wings performed "Live and Let Die" for the 1973 James Bond film. This track marked a significant shift for the Bond franchise by introducing a rock song as a title theme, becoming a major worldwide hit and is considered by some to be the best Bond theme ever. The song's success was global and set a new standard for future Bond themes. "This became a major hit worldwide and if you ask me this is still the best Bond theme ever." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K0pgJ7XAlpo ← wings
**Silly Love Songs (1976)** : This song, from the album "Wings at the Speed of Sound," was number one in America on July 4, 1976, and remained at the top for five weeks. It served as Paul McCartney's response to critics, including John Lennon, who disparaged his "light pop songs" and was seen as a statement celebrating pop music. Andrew Grant Jackson noted it marked the end of an era, aligning with the generation's desire for escapism. ## curiosities
**"Silly Love Songs" Accusation** : As they prepared to record Paul McCartney's fifth album following The Beatles' breakup, Paul McCartney's new band, Wings, was accused by critics of producing "nothing more than silly love songs." This criticism contributed to the perception that McCartney was struggling to redefine his artistic direction post-Beatles. "His new band Wings was accused of creating nothing more than silly love songs as they were getting ready to record McCartney's fifth album since the breakup of his other band." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 42 - Rock Operas of the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-42-rock-operas-of-the-70s ← wings
**Album Art Designed by Storm Thorgerson** : Wings' album covers were among the many iconic designs created by the renowned Storm Thorgerson and his partner Aubrey Powell. Their joint efforts contributed to the visual representation of this post-Beatles project.
