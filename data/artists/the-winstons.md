## genres

## songs
**Amen Brother (1969)** : This 1969 song is notable because its drum break makes it the second most sampled song, after James Brown's extensive catalog. The "Amen break" from this track is a fundamental building block in countless hip hop, jungle, drum and bass, and other electronic music productions. "The next closest competition is the Winston's, largely because of their 1969 song, Amen Brother." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 6 - The 70s Roots of Hip Hop | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-6-the-70s-roots-of-hip-hop
**Amen, Brother** : The drum solo from this song is one of the most sampled and recognizable beats in hip hop history. Dr. Dre notably sampled and slowed this particular section for the N.W.A. song "Straight Outta Compton," and in the early 1990s in the UK, various musicians accelerated and chopped this same solo to create the Drum & Bass genre. **Color Him Father (1969)** : The Winstons, an interracial group, originally recorded "Color Him Father" in their R&B style. Despite winning a Grammy for Richard Lewis Spencer (the songwriter) for R&B Song of the Year in 1970, the band's label refused to promote them or use their photo because of their interracial lineup.
