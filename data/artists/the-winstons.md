# artist - The Winston's

## members
- Gregory Coleman (drummer)
- Richard Spencer (leader)

## genres
- Funk
- Soul

## songs
**Amen Brother (1969)** : An otherwise unremarkable B-side to their single "Color Him Father," this song features a pivotal four-bar, seven-second syncopated drum break played by drummer Gregory Coleman at 1 minute and 16 seconds into the track. This specific drum break later became known as the "Amen Break" and was sampled extensively in other genres. "That's part of an otherwise unremarkable B side from 1969." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VnXBJqKwkrw

## curiosities
**Lack of Royalties for Amen Break** : Despite the Amen Break from "Amen Brother" being sampled in over 5,309 songs and becoming a major part of numerous hits across hip-hop, electronic, and EDM, neither Gregory Coleman nor The Winston's received any royalties. Gregory Coleman died homeless and broke in 2006, unaware of his profound impact on music, and band leader Richard Spencer died in 2020. "Not as the case with so many samples, neither Coleman nor the Winston's have seen any royalties from this, despite the fact that this piece of music has become part of so many other songs, some of which, many of which have become major hits." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VnXBJqKwkrw
