**TVT Records Contract Dispute** : After signing with TVT Records, the label's owner, Steve Gottlieb, disliked Trent's debut album "Pretty Hate Machine" for being too heavy and dark. When Steve demanded a more commercially palatable follow-up, Trent refused and sought release from his contract. TVT denied his request, putting him in "limbo for a couple of years" and forbidding him from recording or releasing new music, even allowing "Pretty Hate Machine" to go out of print. This forced Trent to record secretly under fake names. "But when Steve demanded that the follow up record be more commercially palatable, Trent not so respectfully declined and demanded that he be led out of his contract." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DKdF8NgF5Bw
**Taming Noise** : Since the 1980s, Trent Reznor has been actively experimenting with how to harness and control noise within his music. His approach to sound design, heavily reliant on distortion and feedback, was fundamental to the creation of albums like "The Downward Spiral." "Trent Rezner has been experimenting with taming noise since Nineage Nails began in the 1980s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w
**Unreleased Projects (Tapeworm, Zach de la Rocha solo album)** : Trent Reznor has several unreleased projects. The Tapeworm project, formed in 1995 with various collaborators, continued off and on for nine years but never released anything. He also produced around 20 tracks for Zach de la Rocha's solo album after Zach left Rage Against the Machine, which Trent considered "excellent," but Zach felt uncomfortable with the results, and it was never released. "I mean, the tapeworm thing didn't turn out the way it would be to hoped. **Woodstock '94 Performance and Legacy (August 1994)** : Trent Reznor, as the frontman of Nine Inch Nails, led a performance at Woodstock '94 that solidified his position as one of the most important new rock artists of the 1990s. Despite pre-show chaos involving a power line striking their bus, he and the band deliberately rolled in the mud on stage in solidarity with the audience, creating a memorable and impactful set. "The performance that night solidified Trent's position as one of the most important new rock artists of the 1990s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2RKchFW42Jc
**Zero DB Initiative Signatory** : Trent Reznor is among the artists who signed the Zero DB initiative, formed in 2008 as part of the charity Reprieve.
