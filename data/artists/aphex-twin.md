## albums
**Costick Window (1996) – Self-Titled Test Pressing** : An album released under the pseudonym "Costick Window," which was actually Aphex Twin. As few as five test pressings were made before the album's release was canceled. Over the years, these test pressings have appeared for sale: one sold for $13,000 US in the late 1990s, and another for $13,500 US in 2014. A Kickstarter campaign by electronic musicians raised $47,000 to purchase the album and release it digitally. Ultimately, Marcus Pearson, the creator of Minecraft, bought the physical copy in an eBay auction for $46,300, with the proceeds distributed among Aphex Twin, Kickstarter contributors, and Doctors Without Borders. "In 1996, a record by someone going by the name of Costick Window finished a self-titled album. This was actually Electrodance Guy, the Athex Twin, as few as five test pressings were made before its release was cancelled." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tbmssCZ05ro
**Castig Windu Unreleased Album (1994) - The Stolen Discogs/eBay Saga** : In 1994, Aphex Twin, operating under aliases such as Castig Windu, was developing material for a potential album and proceeded to press approximately 5,000 vinyl copies as a test. Ultimately, he decided against a formal release, and the project was abandoned. Years later, after the record label where he had worked changed locations, one of these vinyls was acquired by an individual through unstated means (implied theft from the "único que había en la discográfica"). This unique copy surfaced for sale on Discogs in January 2014, advertised as the "only existing copy" of his unreleased work, sparking significant collector interest. "Lo curioso es que 1994, 20 años atrás Apex Twin estaba trabajando en material y hizo una prueba para convertir eso en un álbum. **Castig Windu Unreleased Album - Contents and Live Usage** : The unreleased Castig Windu album contained a variety of tracks. Some of these songs had previously appeared in various Aphex Twin compilations throughout the 1990s, surprising those who acquired the album. A significant portion consisted of practically unfinished songs or tracks in a "precarious" state, along with numerous rhythmic bases and conceptual ideas intended for future albums. Aphex Twin had often used material from these vinyls, particularly other samples he kept, in his live DJ sets ("pinchadas") over the years, with many of these performances existing as bootlegs. "Las que ya habían sido editadas nos llevamos la sorpresa porque habían sido editadas en distintos recobulatorios de Apex Twin durante los 90. **Archetypal IDM Artist** : Aphex Twin is considered a prime example of IDM, creating music driven purely by experimentation, rather than overt emotional communication. In interviews, he often states his primary interest is experimenting with sound, creating and working with sound as a physical element, without an underlying emotional message. "era música que nacia por y para la experimentación... no hay una comunicación emotiva por detrás sino una experimentación pura del sonido como un elemento físico." ← Music Radar Clan > BOARDS OF CANADA. **Genius and Alias Use** : Described as a prodigious genius and an entire style unto himself, Aphex Twin has been a "shadow genius" in electronic music for the past two decades. He is renowned for constantly changing his name, utilizing over 15 recognized aliases, including Castig Windu, and releasing material under more than six of them. His propensity for not confirming his own material or identity often leads to rumors, making him a "very complicated" and elusive figure in music. **Unconventional Live Performance** : Aphex Twin famously performed at Sónar many years ago by placing mannequins on stage, without the artists themselves being visible. The audience could not tell if the performance was live or recorded, or if the artists were even present, echoing MF Doom's philosophical approach to physical presence in live music.
