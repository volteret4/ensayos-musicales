## albums
**Artificial Intelligence (1992)** : Featured as the opening artist on the seminal Warp Records compilation, which played a crucial role in redefining electronic music listening. His presence on this album helped shift the genre from club-focused dance music to a more contemplative, home-listening experience and was instrumental in shaping the emerging IDM sound. **Castig Windu Unreleased Album (1994) - The Stolen Discogs/eBay Saga** : In 1994, Aphex Twin, operating under aliases such as Castig Windu, was developing material for a potential album and proceeded to press approximately 5,000 vinyl copies as a test. Ultimately, he decided against a formal release, and the project was abandoned. Years later, after the record label where he had worked changed locations, one of these vinyls was acquired by an individual through unstated means (implied theft from the "único que había en la discográfica"). This unique copy surfaced for sale on Discogs in January 2014, advertised as the "only existing copy" of his unreleased work, sparking significant collector interest. "Lo curioso es que 1994, 20 años atrás Apex Twin estaba trabajando en material y hizo una prueba para convertir eso en un álbum. Llego a trabajar a juntar muchos materiales, algunas bases, muchas cosas y lo llegó a prensar en 5.000." ← Music Radar Clan > El disco secreto de Aphex Twin que apareció en 2014 | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qCiMVOddDyE&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc ← aphex-twin ← aphex-twin
**Castig Windu Unreleased Album - Contents and Live Usage** : The unreleased Castig Windu album contained a variety of tracks. Some of these songs had previously appeared in various Aphex Twin compilations throughout the 1990s, surprising those who acquired the album. A significant portion consisted of practically unfinished songs or tracks in a "precarious" state, along with numerous rhythmic bases and conceptual ideas intended for future albums. Aphex Twin had often used material from these vinyls, particularly other samples he kept, in his live DJ sets ("pinchadas") over the years, with many of these performances existing as bootlegs. "Las que ya habían sido editadas nos llevamos la sorpresa porque habían sido editadas en distintos recobulatorios de Apex Twin durante los 90. Muchas de ellas son canciones prácticamente no terminadas o en un estado un tanto precario y luego hay muchos sonidos de bases rítmicas y de ideas que iban a ser discos." ← Music Radar Clan > El disco secreto de Aphex Twin que apareció en 2014 | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qCiMVOddDyE&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc ← aphex-twin ← aphex-twin
**Costick Window (1996) – Self-Titled Test Pressing** : An album released under the pseudonym "Costick Window," which was actually Aphex Twin. As few as five test pressings were made before the album's release was canceled. Over the years, these test pressings have appeared for sale: one sold for $13,000 US in the late 1990s, and another for $13,500 US in 2014. A Kickstarter campaign by electronic musicians raised $47,000 to purchase the album and release it digitally. Ultimately, Marcus Pearson, the creator of Minecraft, bought the physical copy in an eBay auction for $46,300, with the proceeds distributed among Aphex Twin, Kickstarter contributors, and Doctors Without Borders. "In 1996, a record by someone going by the name of Costick Window finished a self-titled album. This was actually Electrodance Guy, the Athex Twin, as few as five test pressings were made before its release was cancelled." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tbmssCZ05ro ← aphex-twin
**Selected Ambient Works 85-92 (1992)** : Released a few months after the Warp compilation on Apollo, the ambient division of the influential Belgian label R&S Records. This album is considered by some to be the fundamental record for EDM, presenting a series of ambient tracks that are more like a soft, bubbly techno, allegedly composed between 1965 and 1992 (with some tracks dating back to his early teenage years). Its release paved new pathways for EDM in subsequent years and served as a direct influence for a second wave of artists. "El disco salió apenas unos meses después de la recopelación de Warp en el Sello Apollo, la división ambiental del super influyente Sello Belga, Ryes y Records." ← Qué fue el IDM y por qué cambió la música electrónica | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RTwn3hP1XTg

**Synthesizer Deconstruction** : Dissatisfied with the sounds of 1980s synthesizers, he would frequently dismantle, tinker with, and reassemble them. This hands-on approach allowed him to learn electronics and develop his unique sound, leading to his first musical compositions. **Unconventional Live Performance** : Aphex Twin famously performed at Sónar many years ago by placing mannequins on stage, without the artists themselves being visible. The audience could not tell if the performance was live or recorded, or if the artists were even present, echoing MF Doom's philosophical approach to physical presence in live music. "cuando actuaron aquí de nadie se en el sonar hace un montón de años pusieron unos manicis en el escenario y ellos no estaban el escenario no sabías ni si estaban no sabías ni si era grabado el directo no sabías nada había uno manici esquitos ahí en los instrumentos." ← Music Radar Clan > MF DOOM.
