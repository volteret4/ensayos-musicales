# artist - David Whitaker

## curiosities
**Arranger of "The Last Time" String Figure** : David Whitaker was the orchestrator hired to arrange the string figure in the 1966 Andrew Loog Oldham Orchestra's version of The Rolling Stones' "The Last Time." This six-second string figure, which he composed and arranged, became central to The Verve's "Bittersweet Symphony" lawsuit. Despite his pivotal role in creating the sampled material, he received no additional compensation beyond his initial fee in 1966. "They were written and arranged by an orchestrator for higher guy named David Whitaker." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fS8V3kYXT6w
**Originator of "Bittersweet Symphony" Snippet** : David Whitaker was the orchestral arranger who originally wrote the distinctive five-note progression in Andrew Loog Oldham's version of "The Last Time." This specific musical phrase later served as the inspiration and sampled basis for The Verve's "Bittersweet Symphony." "He wrote that five note bit." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AcADOuQUiAQ
