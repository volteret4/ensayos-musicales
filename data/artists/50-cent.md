## curiosities
**"Old School" Classification** : Even today, artists like 50 Cent are categorized as "old school" hip hop, contrasting with the "new school" movement initiated by Kanye West. "Incluso hoy en día artistas que tenemos en activo como J.S.E.T. como 50 Cent, los seguimos llamando lo old school, la vieja escuela." ← Music Radar Clan > Poniendo a Kanye West en su lugar | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ih5F70jcm6M&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc ← 50-cent
**Album Sales (2003)** : In 2003, 50 Cent's album sales reached 7 million copies, making it one of the top-selling records of that year. This occurred during a period when overall music industry sales were beginning to show signs of decline due to digital piracy. "In 2003, it was 50 cent with 7 million." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-CT7yYXonIk ← 50-cent
**Late 90s/Early 2000s Gangster Rap Figure** : 50 Cent is mentioned as an important artist from the late 1990s, representing a somewhat later evolution of gangster rap. He embodied the characteristic image of "almost delinquents" with weapons, legal troubles, and lyrics focused on delinquent subculture. "Cuando ya nos vamos hacia finales de los 90 que ya tenemos a 50 cent, tenemos a J7, tenemos a muchos otros artistas muy importantes, siguen siendo una evolución un poco tardía de lo que viene a ser esos restos en el gangster rap." ← Music Radar Clan > Poniendo a Kanye West en su lugar | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ih5F70jcm6M&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc ← 50-cent
