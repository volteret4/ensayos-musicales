## curiosities
**Direct Lyrical References to Springsteen** : Throughout their career, The Gaslight Anthem has directly referenced Springsteen lyrics in their own songs, sometimes incorporating full lines from tracks such as "I'm on Fire," "Atlantic City," and "Sandy." "Through the Gaslight Anthem's career, some of their lyrics have been direct references to Springsteen lyrics, sometimes dropping in full lines from songs like I'm on fire in Atlantic City and Sandy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2EerEOuP52I
**European Popularity** : The Gaslight Anthem's music resonated significantly in Europe, indicating a broader appeal beyond North America. "There was something about what the Gaslight Anthem did that caught on in Europe." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2EerEOuP52I
**New Jersey Roots** : Leader Brian Fallon hails from New Brunswick, New Jersey, a background noted for making Springsteen's influence almost unavoidable, as humorously put by Jon Bon Jovi. "First of all, he is also from New Jersey, New Brunswick, New Jersey to be specific." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2EerEOuP52I
**Positive Messages in Lyrics** : Their work is noted for being "peppered" with plenty of positive messages, a characteristic identified as very "Springsteen-ish." "peppered their stuff with plenty of positive messages. Again, all very springsteenish." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2EerEOuP52I
**Springsteen Endorsement at Glastonbury (2009)** : Bruce Springsteen personally recognized The Gaslight Anthem's work by joining them on stage during their performance at the Glastonbury Festival in 2009, an event considered a major endorsement. "In 2009, when the band was performing at Glastonbury, Springsteen joined them on stage. You cannot get a bigger endorsement than that." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2EerEOuP52I
**Themes of Yearning and Dreams** : The band's music, infused with soulful punk rock, frequently explores themes of yearning and dreams for individuals navigating big city life or those in the heartland aspiring to reach urban centers, mirroring Springsteen's common lyrical narratives. "they're also singing about yearning and dreams from someone caught on the big city or someone who lives in the heartland in hopes to make it to the big city." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2EerEOuP52I
