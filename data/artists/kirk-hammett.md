## curiosities
**"It's Alive" Exhibition at ROM** : Hammett's extensive, comprehensive, and well-preserved collection of classic horror movie posters and artwork is deemed significant enough to be featured in major museum exhibitions, including "It's Alive: Classic Horror and Sci-Fi Art from the Kirk Hammett Collection" at the Royal Ontario Museum (ROM) in Toronto. This exhibition ran from July 12, 2019, until January 5, 2020, with plans to tour other museums globally thereafter. "This collection is so big and so comprehensive and so well preserved that it's the subject of exhibitions at major museums, including the Royal Ontario Museum in Toronto." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CgDCaUxbQDU
**Bunk Swap with Cliff Burton (September 27, 1986)** : On the night of the fatal bus crash that killed Cliff Burton, Kirk Hammett's usual bunk was chosen by Burton after a card game. Hammett, indifferent, opted for another bunk, inadvertently avoiding the fatal spot. "Cliff drew the Ace of Spades which was the high card so he got to choose his bunk first. And he chose the bunk that Kirk Hammett had been using." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dVJuITJyNTU
**Childhood Interest in Horror** : Hammett's fascination with horror began around age five or six, in the late 1960s or early 1970s. He was initially drawn to the genre because it offered a distinctly different feel and atmosphere compared to the prevalent Disney movies and cartoons that defined much of the culture he experienced as a child. "It was something that I was attracted to initially because it was so different and had a different sort of feel and atmosphere to a lot of the culture I was getting at the time as a 5 or 6 year old, you know, that would have entailed a lot of Disney movies and cartoons." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CgDCaUxbQDU
**Collection Focus on Universal Monsters** : While he enjoys most horror movies from the turn of the last century to modern times, Hammett primarily focuses his collection on classic Universal horror monsters and movies from the early 1930s. His key interest lies in characters such as Frankenstein, Dracula, The Mummy, Wolfman, Phantom of the Opera, Creature from the Black Lagoon, and The Hunchback. "But I try to keep it to what I really love and what means the most to me, which is the classic universal horror monsters and those movies that came out in the early 30s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CgDCaUxbQDU
**Discovery of Rare Posters** : When Hammett started collecting over three decades ago, the existence of a "Frankenstein 3 sheet" or a "Mummy 3 sheet" poster was entirely unknown. He subsequently found both, and these pieces currently represent the only known copies of their kind in the world. "when I first started 30 years ago, no one knew that there was a Frankenstein three sheet out there or a mummy three sheet. I mean, I found both." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CgDCaUxbQDU
**Diverse Horror Memorabilia** : Beyond movie posters, Hammett's collection encompasses a wide array of horror memorabilia, including props (items used in movies), toys (both vintage and continuously produced modern ones), masks, artwork, paintings, comics, original art, and sculptures. He differentiates his collecting from genres like Star Wars or Star Trek, strictly adhering to horror. "I, I tried to keep it to a horror movie posters, props, which are, you know, items that were used in certain movies. I love, I love the toys." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CgDCaUxbQDU
**Early Entry into Collecting** : Hammett began collecting movie posters over 30 years ago, when the hobby was still underground and had relatively few participants. This allowed him to acquire available pieces at "sensible" prices because there was less competition among collectors. "I got into this hobby when it was so underground, over 30 years ago, there weren't a lot of movie poster collectors." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CgDCaUxbQDU
**Exhibition Content** : The "It's Alive" exhibition at the Royal Ontario Museum features his "core collection," specifically "my hundred best pieces," which totals over a hundred items. This selection broadly represents the horror genre from the turn of the last century to modern times, designed to illustrate the evolution of horror movie posters and the cultural trends reflected in them. "The collection that, of things that are going to, to the Toronto Museum is my core collection or in other words, my hundred best pieces." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CgDCaUxbQDU
**Exhibition Goals** : Hammett hopes that visitors to his exhibition will first and foremost recognize the beauty and grandeur of the collected works and be "blown away" by seeing such a concentration of great pieces together. Beyond aesthetic appreciation, he aims for the exhibition to educate the public about the art of movie posters, their historical contribution to culture, and their evolution from the turn of the last century to the present day. "Well, first and foremost, I hope that people will see these things and just get, we'll recognize the beauty of it and the grand tour of it and just get blown away by seeing all this stuff in one spot because when you have all these really great pieces and they're all together, you get the synergy going and it's for me, it's a really cool energy and it feels really good and it's overwhelmingly just cool just to be surround by cool horror pieces if you're a fan." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CgDCaUxbQDU
**Favorite Piece: The Mummy 3 Sheet** : Hammett's absolute favorite piece in his collection, irrespective of its monetary value, is "The Mummy 3 sheet" poster, an image so significant to him that it is also depicted on his guitar. He finds himself completely engrossed by its colors, composition, and overall richness, describing it as having an "amazing effect" that allows his mind to dive in totally and get lost. "You know, it probably would be the Mummy 3 sheet. I love that image. It's on my guitar. You know, when I look at it, I just get lost in the colors of it, the composition, just the luscious of it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CgDCaUxbQDU
**First Horror Movie Experience** : His first horror movie experience was watching "Day of the Triffids," likely from the late 1950s or early 1960s. This viewing made a strong impression on him, prompting him to actively seek out more horror-related stories across various media such as comic books, magazines, and books. "Yes, a movie called Day of the Triffids." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CgDCaUxbQDU
**Holy Grail Items** : Hammett's "holy grail" items, pieces he would love to find but which have not yet been discovered, include a "Bride of Frankenstein 3 sheet" and a "Dracula 3 sheet." These are billboard-sized posters from the 1920s that, if found, would be incredible additions to his collection. "Well, there's a lot of pieces out there that I would love to have that have not just been found at all, you know, like a bright of Frankenstein, three sheet, you know, Dracula, three sheet." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CgDCaUxbQDU
**Horror Movie and Memorabilia Passion** : Kirk Hammett has maintained a lifelong passion for horror movies and horror memorabilia, with his primary obsession being posters from old sci-fi and horror films. This interest began in his childhood, around the age of five or six. "Kirk has always been a fan of horror movies. And Kirk is a fan of horror memorabilia. But his biggest passion are posters from old sci-fi and horror movies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CgDCaUxbQDU
**Preference for Thematic Horror** : Hammett prefers horror movies with supernatural, monster, outer space, or paranormal themes, tending to avoid overtly violent horror. He sees "torture porn" as a "cheap thrill" that often lacks the plot and intrigue he values, making it difficult for him to recognize any artistry in it. "I tend to shy away from horror movies that are overtly violent. You know, I like horror but I don't like horror for the sake of it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CgDCaUxbQDU
