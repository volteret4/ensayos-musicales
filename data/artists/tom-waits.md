**Early Career and Discovery** : Before his breakthrough, Tom Waits worked as a doorman at a nightclub in San Diego that featured "idiosyncratic performers." He drew the attention of Asylum Records due to his early folk and jazzy style, reminiscent of Joni Mitchell. The label's founders, David Geffen and Elliot Roberts, who were fans of Bob Dylan, saw Waits as a potential "Dylan" for their roster. "Tom caught the attention of Asylum because like the other artists on the label, he was sort of foky at the beginning." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sEZ13v1LNYQ

**Vocal Evolution and Legendary Self-Harm** : Tom Waits's vocal stylings underwent a significant transformation over the years. Initially gruff and soulful, he is said to have deliberately damaged his vocal chords with excessive whiskey and cigarettes, a legendary act intended to cultivate an older, more world-weary sound. This unique approach to his voice often leads to him being listed among singers who are perceived as "unable to sing." "according to legend, he deliberately tortured his vocal chords with lots of whiskey and cigarettes to make him sound older and more world-weary." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7_ZmZxnSOnE
