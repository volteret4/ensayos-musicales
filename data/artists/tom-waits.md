**Musical Style and Categorization** : Described as difficult to categorize, Tom Waits was seen as a misfit, a "refugee from the beat era of the 50s" but writing about the 70s. His phrasing, a hybrid spoken/sung scat, owed a strong debt to jazz and blues, with musical accompaniment blending influences from Thelonious Monk, Oscar Brown Jr., and Oscar Peterson. He developed a unique brand of gravelly storytelling. **The Troubadour Hootnanny Experience** : On Mondays, The Troubadour hosted an open mic night called "The Hoot." Tom Waits would arrive when the club opened, around 9 AM, to get his name on the list for a chance to perform a three or four-song set, often waiting all day for his 15 minutes on stage. His first performance there left him "scared shitless." "I used to take the bus to the troubadour and stand out front at 9 o'clock in the morning on a Monday and wait all day to get up and do 15 minutes on stage because you know you never had confidence." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 41 - The Impact of The Troubadour on 1970s Popular Culture | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-41-the-impact-of-the-troubadour-on-1970s-popular-culture
**Vocal Evolution and Legendary Self-Harm** : Tom Waits's vocal stylings underwent a significant transformation over the years. Initially gruff and soulful, he is said to have deliberately damaged his vocal chords with excessive whiskey and cigarettes, a legendary act intended to cultivate an older, more world-weary sound. This unique approach to his voice often leads to him being listed among singers who are perceived as "unable to sing."
