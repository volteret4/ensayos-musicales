## curiosities
**Frustration with Sam Phillips** : Billy Lee Riley, a guitarist, was part of the normal Sun Studio session players but expressed fury at Sam Phillips' focus on Jerry Lee Lewis's career at the expense of others. Despite his anger, Riley continued to work on sessions for Phillips, including playing guitar on Jerry Lee Lewis's "Breathless." "Riley was also furious with Sam Phillips, for the way he was concentrating on Lewis's career at the expense of everyone else's, but he was still working on sessions for Phillips." ← Episode 66： ＂Great Balls of Fire＂ by Jerry Lee Lewis | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CVp5e4EWCqI
**Recording Antics at Sun Label** : Billy Lee Riley was a rockabilly singer who recorded for the Sun label in Memphis in the 1950s, much like Elvis. He pushed rock and roll to extremes, with people recalling how he would literally swing upside down from the rafters, screaming into the microphone during recording sessions. "People who sat in on some of Billy Lee's recording sessions remember how he sometimes literally swung upside down from the rafters screaming into the microphone." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U ← billy-lee-riley
**Session at Stax Studio** : Billy Lee Riley, a rockabilly artist associated with Sun Records, was booked for a session at Stax in early 1962. The session ended prematurely, with differing accounts suggesting he was either cutting a jingle, produced awful tracks, or arrived too drunk to record. "No one's quite sure what he was using the studio for in early 1962. Some say he was cutting a jingle, some say he cut a few actual tracks, but that they were awful. And others that he turned up too drunk to record." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Unreleased Sun Recordings** : Billy Lee Riley had numerous unreleased songs stored in Sun Records' vaults. Shelby Singleton, upon acquiring Sun, ensured that pretty much every scrap of material from these vaults, including Riley's, saw release. "In their four-year Rocket Billy Hayday, Sam Phillips and Jack Clements had recorded literally thousands of unreleased songs by Jerry Lee Lewis, Johnny Cash, Roy Orbison, Karl Perkins, Conway Twitty, Charlie Rich, Billy Lee Riley, and more." ← Episode 66： ＂Great Balls of Fire＂ by Jerry Lee Lewis | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CVp5e4EWCqI
