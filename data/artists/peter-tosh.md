## songs
**Legalize It (1976)** : The title track of his 1976 solo album, this song explicitly advocates for the legalization of marijuana and stood in direct contrast to the musical direction Bob Marley was pursuing, leading the Jamaican government to attempt to ban the record. ## curiosities
**Bitterness Over Marley's Skin Tone** : Part of Peter Tosh's resentment stemmed from his belief that Bob Marley garnered more attention due to his lighter skin, leading Tosh to fundamentally align himself with the "black camp" amidst Jamaica's societal dynamics, as described by author Colin Grant. "Some of that bitterness was from Tosh's point of view due to Marley being lighter skinned than he was." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 29 - Every Little Thing is Gonna Be All Right - The Influence of 70s Reggae | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-29-every-little-thing-is-gonna-be-all-right-the-influence-of-70s-reggae
**Departure from The Wailers (1973)** : Peter Tosh left The Wailers in 1973, citing unhappiness with Chris Blackwell's refusal to support his solo work and a perception that Bob Marley, being lighter-skinned, received preferential attention within the band and from the label. "Tosh left the wailers in 1973. He was unhappy that Chris Blackwell would not let him put out a solo album and there were rumblings about the attention going to Marley." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 29 - Every Little Thing is Gonna Be All Right - The Influence of 70s Reggae | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-29-every-little-thing-is-gonna-be-all-right-the-influence-of-70s-reggae
**Inspiration for 2 Tone Mascot** : An old photograph of reggae star Peter Tosh served as the direct inspiration for the black and white rude boy image created by Jerry Dammers. This image later became known as Walt Japsco, the unofficial mascot of Dammers' 2 Tone record label and the broader Ska movement. "In fact, it was based on a photo, a very old picture of reggae star Peter Tosh." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mhQ36Y-c9fM ← peter-tosh
**Inspiration for The Specials' Logo** : Peter Tosh, a Jamaican musician and former member of Bob Marley's Wailers, was the real-life individual whose photograph inspired the stylized Jamaican rude boy character, Walt Japsco, featured in The Specials' band logo. "Walt was actually based on a photograph of Jamaican musician Peter Tosh, who was a former member of Bob Arley's Whalers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc ← peter-tosh
**Marijuana Dealer Funding** : After Chris Blackwell declined to fund his solo album, Peter Tosh secured the necessary money for "Legalize It" from a marijuana dealer, who initially expressed displeasure with the album's controversial title. "Since Chris Blackwell would not fund his solo album, Tosh got a marijuana dealer to give him the money for legalize it, although the dealer initially was not too happy about the name of the album." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 29 - Every Little Thing is Gonna Be All Right - The Influence of 70s Reggae | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-29-every-little-thing-is-gonna-be-all-right-the-influence-of-70s-reggae
**Newspaper Ad for "Legalize It"** : In response to the Jamaican government's attempt to ban his record, Peter Tosh took a defiant stance by purchasing an advertisement in a Jamaican newspaper to print the complete lyrics of "Legalize It," ensuring his message reached the public. "Tosh also took out an ad and a Jamaican newspaper and printed the lyrics within the ad." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 29 - Every Little Thing is Gonna Be All Right - The Influence of 70s Reggae | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-29-every-little-thing-is-gonna-be-all-right-the-influence-of-70s-reggae
**Political Activism** : Peter Tosh was a fervent political activist who personally endured police brutality, and his protests were profoundly influenced by the works of American civil rights leaders such as Dr. Martin Luther King, Stokely Carmichael, and Malcolm X, even though their writings were banned in Jamaica. "He did not just protest police brutality because of articles he read.
