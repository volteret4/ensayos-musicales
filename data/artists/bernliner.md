## curiosities
**Early Flat Disc Commercial Failure** : Despite Bernliner's innovations, his early flat discs, composed of shellac and mineral, suffered a significant commercial failure. They were prohibitively expensive, extremely heavy, and highly susceptible to breaking when dropped, eventually being superseded by plastic vinyl discs as advancements in plastic material knowledge and casting techniques emerged in the early 20th century. **Shellac Disc Invention (Late 19th Century)** : Bernliner revived Thomas Edison's original idea of a flat disc, inventing the first "vinyl" disc made from shellac and mineral, often pizarra (slate), meaning early records were literally stone. These discs were remarkably resistant to the erosion from rudimentary steel needles and recording methods, but the nascent understanding of plastic materials made them costly and heavy precursors to modern vinyl. **Sound Recording Innovations (Late 19th Century)** : Towards the end of the 19th century, around 1895, Bernliner significantly advanced sound recording technology by discovering a superior method for engraving sound into grooves. He is recognized as the inventor of sound formats, the process of sound recording, and the first gramophones, effectively laying the groundwork for subsequent physical music formats.
