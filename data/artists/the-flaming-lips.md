# artist - The Flaming Lips

## members
- Wayne Coyne (singer)

## albums
**Hetty Fwins (2012)** : This special Record Store Day vinyl release was notable for its clear vinyl that vividly displayed traces of the Flaming Lips' blood. The band also required collaborators like Chris Martin, Kesha, and Yoko Ono to contribute their own bodily fluids for ten special editions, which initially retailed for $2,500 in April 2010 (with proceeds benefiting the Oklahoma Humane Society and the Academy of Contemporary Music at the University of Central Oklahoma) and now sell for over $6,000. "The album is Hetty Fwins and yes, there is that blood-filled vinyl edition that goes for more than $6,000 these days." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sQrbNUDZuOQ
**In the Court of the Crimson King (1969) – King Crimson Cover** : The Flaming Lips, along with Stardeath and White Dwarfs, released a song-by-song cover of King Crimson's 1969 album *In the Court of the Crimson King*. "They also released a song by song cover of a King Crimson album called In the Court of the Crimson King from 1969." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw
**The Dark Side of the Moon (2009) – Pink Floyd Cover** : In 2009, The Flaming Lips, in collaboration with Stardeath and White Dwarfs, covered Pink Floyd's *The Dark Side of the Moon* album in its entirety. "Back in 2009, they helped the lips cover Pink Floyd's Dark Side of the Moon album in its entirety." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw

## songs
**Borderline (2009)** : This cover version by The Flaming Lips, in collaboration with Stardeath and White Dwarfs, was released in Spring 2009 as part of a split 7-inch single with The Black Keys for Record Store Day, pressed on green vinyl. The band intentionally slowed the tempo down to make the song more interesting, contrasting their avant-garde style with Madonna's mainstream pop original. "The Flaming Lips along with Star Death and White Dwarfs covering Madonna's Borderline that was released in 2009." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw
**Is David Bowie Dying? (2012)** : This song, featuring Neon Indian, was included on The Flaming Lips' album "Hetty Fwins," a special Record Store Day vinyl release from 2012. "The flaming lips featuring Neon Indian with the song called Is David Bowie Dying from 2012." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sQrbNUDZuOQ

## curiosities
**Blood Art Creation** : The Flaming Lips are known for incorporating their blood into their art, a practice similar to KISS. In 2010, Wayne Coyne extracted a substantial amount of his own blood to create a poster for the band, documented in a YouTube video. "In 2010, Wayne Coyne extracted some blood from his body, a fairly substantial amount, too, and created a lips poster with it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sQrbNUDZuOQ
**Blood-Infused Vinyl Edition** : For their 2012 Record Store Day release, "Hetty Fwins," The Flaming Lips created a special clear vinyl edition that visibly contained traces of the band's blood. They also extended this practice to their collaborators, demanding that artists like Chris Martin of Coldplay, Kesha, and Yoko Ono contribute their own blood for ten ultra-rare special editions of the record. "Not only did the clear vinyl vividly show traces of flaming lips blood, but the band also demanded that any of the collaborators on this record also contribute their precious bodily fluid for 10 special editions of the record." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sQrbNUDZuOQ
**Collaborator with Sean Lennon** : The Flaming Lips have collaborated with Sean Lennon on records. "The Flaming Lips." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MmXDbXI6Sqk
**Covered by Scott Weiland (Year Unknown)** : The Flaming Lips were among the artists whose songs were covered by Scott Weiland on his album "A Compilation of Scott Weiland Cover Songs," released via his website. "covering everything from the Smiths and David Bowie to Nirvana, the Flaming Lips, and even the Stone Roses." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WfTq9d2YadY
**Special Edition Vinyl Pricing** : The blood-filled special editions of "Hetty Fwins," released in April 2010, had an original MSRP of $2,500. Proceeds from these sales were donated to the Oklahoma Humane Society and the Academy of Contemporary Music at the University of Central Oklahoma. These records have since been seen on the market selling for over $6,000. "Well, the original MSRP, when they went on sale in April of 2010, was $2,500, with proceeds going to the Oklahoma Humane Society and the Academy of Contemporary Music at the University of Central Oklahoma." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sQrbNUDZuOQ
