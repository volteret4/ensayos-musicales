**Saturday Night Live Stunt (1977)** : Costello and The Attractions secured their appearance on NBC TV's *Saturday Night Live* only because the original guests, The Sex Pistols, were unable to perform due to visa issues. Columbia Records insisted Costello perform his single "Less Than Zero," but believing Americans wouldn't understand its subject matter (British fascist Oswald Mosley), Costello abruptly stopped the song after just seven seconds live on the air and switched to the then-unreleased "Radio, Radio." This act enraged SNL producer Lorne Michaels and NBC brass, leading to Costello being banned from the show for a dozen years.
