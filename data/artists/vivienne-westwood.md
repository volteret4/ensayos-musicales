# artist - Vivienne Westwood

## curiosities
**Co-owner of SEX (later 6) Shop** : Vivienne Westwood co-owned a shop, initially named SEX and later known as "6," located in central London. This shop became a crucial hub for the development of the punk aesthetic. **Collaboration with Malcolm McLaren** : Vivienne Westwood co-owned a clothing store in Chelsea, London, with her partner Malcolm McLaren. The store, initially called "Let It Rock," focused on fashion for Teddyboys, bondage gear, and sex toys. After McLaren's pivotal trip to New York, the store was rebranded as "Sex" and became instrumental in popularizing punk fashion by selling garments held together with safety pins, a style inspired by Richard Hell. **Early Shop Style Evolution** : Her shop initially sold rockabilly clothing. Over time, she began incorporating sadomasochistic elements and blending them with the aesthetic ideas Malcolm McLaren brought back from Richard Hell. This fusion led to the creation of the first punk aesthetic garments. **Expensive and Copied Clothing** : The clothes sold at her shop, SEX (6), were very expensive. Consequently, the Sex Pistols often stole or were given clothes, and many fans resorted to copying her designs at home because they couldn't afford them. **False Claim to Authorship** : Vivienne Westwood claimed to be the originator of the punk aesthetic movement. However, this claim was later disproven, as she had imported and adapted ideas initially conceived by Richard Hell.
