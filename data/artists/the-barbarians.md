## curiosities
**Mid-60s Garage Band** : The Barbarians were part of a series of rough, ragged, and raw-sounding bands in the 1960s that came from North American garages and basements. Their attire, which often included leather jackets, contributed to a rebellious image likened to Marlon Brando or James Dean, causing some to describe these musicians as looking like "punks." "There was the count five, the barbarians, the standels, the trogs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z8BTWo66SDI
**Original Garage Rock Band with Punk Style** : The Barbarians were an original Garage Rock band from Massachusetts. They embraced an early "punk" aesthetic by dressing in black leather jackets, reminiscent of the motorcycle gang punk portrayed by Marlon Brando in the 1953 film "The Wild Ones." Their drummer, Dermammolton, notably played with a hook in place of his missing left hand.
