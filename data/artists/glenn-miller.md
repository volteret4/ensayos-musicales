## curiosities
**Competent Trombone Player** : Glenn Miller was described as a competent trombone player, but his band's widespread acclaim was not attributed to his individual musical virtuosity or fantastic trombone playing. "Glenn Miller was a competent enough trombone player, but no one listens to the Glenn Miller band, and thinks, wow, one of those four trombone players is fantastic." ← Episode 7： ＂Good Rockin' Tonight＂ by Wynonie Harris | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IgqSPoYRUCM
**Disappearance During World War II (December 1942)** : Big Band leader Glenn Miller went missing in December 1942 when his military plane reportedly went down over the English Channel during a mission in World War II. Despite extensive efforts, no wreckage or remains were ever found, though wartime conditions and severe weather were cited as contributing factors. "No one has ever heard from Big Band leader Glenn Miller after his military plane evidently went down somewhere over the English Channel while on a mission during World War II." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCwgvHg86Oc ← glenn-miller
**First Big Band Experience (Mid-1930s)** : Glenn Miller gained his first experience in putting together a big band when he was hired by British bandleader Ray Noble. Due to musicians' union restrictions, Noble's own band could not travel to the US, so he employed Miller to assemble a new American band. "So, for example, Glenn Miller got his first experience of putting together a big band, because Ray Noble, a British band leader, had had hits in the US in the mid-30s." ← REUPLOAD Episode 71： ＂Willie and the Hand Jive＂ by Johnny Otis | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ez6BCUlI6Rg
