## songs
**Mr. Brightside (2003) – Breakthrough Betrayal Anthem** : Released in the fall of 2003, "Mr. Brightside" is one of the 21st century's biggest songs, demonstrating incredible staying power, especially in the UK where it's charted for over 450 weeks. It has been streamed nearly 2.5 billion times on Spotify, won numerous awards, and is a staple at sporting events, generating millions for The Killers. The song's origin traces back to Brandon Flowers' heartbreak as a 19-year-old, after he saw his ex-girlfriend with another man at The Crown and Anchor bar in Las Vegas, leading him to furiously write down his spiraling imaginations of their actions later that night, attempting to put on a brave face by calling himself "Mr. Brightside." The music was already written by guitarist Dave Keuning, and their crude demo with a drum machine helped them secure a record deal. "As Brandon has said, who knows that betrayal could sound so good." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk

## curiosities
**"Mr. Brightside" as a Coping Mechanism** : After seeing his ex-girlfriend with another man, Brandon Flowers immediately left the bar but couldn't shake the image from his mind. He then spiraled into imagining what they would be doing later that night, writing everything down on a pad of paper in an attempt to convince himself to put on a brave face, hence calling himself "Mr. Brightside." "He wrote everything out on a pad of paper hoping to convince himself to put on a brave face with the situation." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk
**"Sam's Town" Album Title Origin** : The Killers' album "Sam's Town" derives its name from the Sam's Town Hotel and Gambling Hall, a casino located in their hometown of Las Vegas. Bassist Mark Stoermer held a personal connection to this landmark, as he could see the casino's sign from his bedroom when he was young. "It's the title is taken from a casino in the band's hometown of Las Vegas." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tbKuJR0cPs
**80s-Style Keyboards** : The Killers, alongside The Bravery, were credited with bringing 80s-style keyboards back into vogue in rock music during the mid-2000s. Their sound contributed to a broader palette of styles emerging in the alt-rock universe. "The bravery and the killers brought the sound of 80-style keyboards back." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-CT7yYXonIk
**Anton Corbijn's Vision for "Sam's Town" Cover** : Photographer Anton Corbijn, a renowned artist who has collaborated with U2, Depeche Mode, and many others, rejected The Killers' initial concept for their "Sam's Town" cover, which they described as a "chic gypsy look." Corbijn instead proposed an aesthetic of "tired and faded glory," bringing to life this vision with elements such as a beat-up camper trailer, a stuffed big-horned desert sheep, and the model Felice LaZae. "When presented with the project, he overruled the band's desire for something they described as a chic gypsy look." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tbKuJR0cPs
**Brandon Flowers' Personal Inspiration** : The genesis of "Mr. Brightside" came from a deeply personal experience for singer Brandon Flowers. As a 19-year-old, he was living in Las Vegas with his sister, renting a room for $200 a month, and was heartbroken after walking into The Crown and Anchor bar and seeing his ex-girlfriend, his first serious relationship, with another man. This betrayal inspired the song's narrative. "Somewhere at the root of all this, at all these achievements and profits is a real woman." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk
**Crude Demo's Role in Record Deal** : The initial composition of "Mr. Brightside" involved Brandon Flowers writing the lyrics over music already composed by guitarist Dave Keuning. They then created a crude demo using a drum machine, and this early version of the song was instrumental in helping the full Killers lineup secure their first record deal. "When the full killer's lineup came together, this was one of the songs that landed them a record deal." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk
