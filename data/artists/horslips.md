# artist - Horslips

## genres
- Celtic Folk
- Celtic Rock
- Traditional Irish Music

## albums
**Happy to Meet, Sorry to Part (1972) - Debut Celtic Rock Album** : This album is considered the first Celtic rock album. Its title is derived from a traditional Irish jig. It was recorded using the Rolling Stones' mobile truck, which had just been used by The Rolling Stones for "Exile on Main Street" in France and was later used by Deep Purple for "Smoke on the Water" in Switzerland. "The first album was called Happy to Meet, Sorry to Part, from 1972. This title comes from that of a traditional Irish jig. It was recorded with a Rolling Stones mobile truck, just after the Stones used it to make exile on Main Street in France, and not long after D-Purple used it to record smoke on the water in Switzerland." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cuUF4MByxyo

## curiosities
**Connection to U2 Management** : Barry Devlin, the bassist and vocalist for Horslips, recommended a young band named U2 to his friend Paul McGinnis, who was a big Horslips fan. This recommendation led to Paul McGinnis becoming U2's manager. "One thing that you should know about horse-lips is that bassist and vocalist Barry Devlin recommended a young band to his friend, who was a very big horse-lips fan, and that friend's name was Paul McGinnis. And that's how Paul McGinnis became the manager of U2." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cuUF4MByxyo
**First Major Irish International Success** : This band achieved significant success outside of Ireland without needing to leave their home country first, a notable accomplishment for an Irish band at the time. "They were also the first major Irish band to have success outside of Ireland without having to leave the country first." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cuUF4MByxyo
**Formation and Mission** : Horslips was formed in Dublin in 1970 with the specific goal of updating traditional Irish and Celtic folk music. They are recognized as the first group to be labeled "Celtic rock" and are considered the founding fathers of the genre. "They were formed in Dublin in 1970 and were on a mission to update traditional Irish and Celtic folk music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cuUF4MByxyo
**Impact on Irish Music Revival** : Along with Thin Lizzy, Horslips faced criticism from traditionalists in Ireland but played a crucial role in revitalizing interest in traditional Irish music. "Both Thin Lizzy and Horse-lips took a lot of abuse from the musical traditionalists of Ireland, but both contributed greatly to a revival of interest in traditional Irish music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cuUF4MByxyo
**Managerial Learning for Paul McGuinness** : Irish folk rock band Horslips were quite successful in Ireland, and their manager was a friend of Paul McGuinness. By "hanging around with them," McGuinness gained valuable insights and learned several aspects of being a manager, which later informed his own career. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
