## curiosities
**Creative Process of Contribution** : As part of the band's collaborative songwriting, Anthony Kiedis developed his contributions after John Frusciante and Flea had brought their musical "gems" into practice. His method involved going for "a very long drive in my Chevy" to figure out what he could add to the emerging songs. "I better go for a very long drive in my Chevy and figure out something to add to the stew, which is part of our creative process." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Nr-3Y3UWQcI
**Developing Melodic Singing Style with Frusciante** : Anthony Kiedis's singing approach became "more melodic" after working with John Frusciante, finding more comfort and confidence with the young guitarist's style than he had with Hillel Slovak. He personally coached the 18-year-old Frusciante, whom the band called "Greeney," one-on-one. "Anthony became more comfortable with John's style. Actually, more comfortable than he had been writing and singing with Hillel." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc
**Early Life and Exposure to Drugs and Sex (Childhood)** : Anthony Kiedis moved to California to live with his father, Blackie Dammit, a bit-part actor and Hollywood drug dealer, at the age of 12. He recounted in his memoir, "Scar Tissue," an incident at age four where his father blew pot smoke in his face on Sunset Strip. By the time he was 12, Kiedis was clubbing with his father, taking quaaludes provided by his father, and even proposed a sexual encounter with one of his father's girlfriends, Kimberly, which his father facilitated. Keith Moon was an adult friend and role model to Kiedis during his pre-teen years. "When I was about four, my dad and I were on one of our outings walking down sunset strip. When he suddenly stopped and gently blew some pot smoke in my face." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DA2iJsu3GRY
**Impact of John's Declaration** : A single statement from John Frusciante, "I was born to be in this band," profoundly impacted Anthony Kiedis. This declaration made Anthony feel that the reunion was a destined outcome and that he "had no choice" but to fully support it, regardless of the potential consequences. "And he said, I was born to be in this band. And I was just, I was like, he is telling the truth. And there's no fucking winner that I'm going to stop that from happening or do anything other than get out of the way and just let it let it flow." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Nr-3Y3UWQcI
**Independent Premonition of John's Involvement** : Even before Flea mentioned it, Anthony Kiedis had a strong sensation that "it would be really nice if John would get involved" with the band's music again. He initially considered possibilities like John writing or producing, not necessarily a full rejoining. "I just got the sensation like, it would be really nice if John would get involved." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Nr-3Y3UWQcI
**Initial Worries about John's Return** : Anthony Kiedis confessed to being initially worried about John Frusciante rejoining the Red Hot Chili Peppers. His concern was that any past resentments could potentially impede the band's success. "Anthony was initially worried that past resentments would get in the way of their success." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Nr-3Y3UWQcI
**Livid Over Saturday Night Live Performance (1992)** : Anthony Kiedis was "livid" and believed John Frusciante "sabotaged everything" during the Red Hot Chili Peppers' "Saturday Night Live" performance. He felt betrayed by Frusciante's "dissonant out of tune experiment" during "Under the Bridge," especially given the live television audience. He confronted Frusciante after the show, suspecting heroin use. "I felt like I was getting stabbed in the back and hung up to drive in front of all of America. While this guy was off in a corner in the shadow, playing some dissonant out of tune experiment." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc

**Observation of Flea's Discontent** : Anthony Kiedis noticed that Flea was not "feeling his best self" in the band's prior incarnation, describing him as not being "on fire" for whatever reason. This observation contributed to Anthony's independent thoughts about John Frusciante's potential return. "I knew that Flea was in a mood in a disposition where he wasn't feeling his, his, his best self in the previous incarnation. For whatever reason, it just like he wasn't, he wasn't on fire." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Nr-3Y3UWQcI
**Observing the Changing Musical Guard (1991)** : In his autobiography, "Scar Tissue," Anthony Kiedis noted that the "Blood Sugar Sex Magik" tour coincided with a major shift in the musical landscape, observing the decline of late 80s pop metal bands and family sitcoms, and a new energy in music, exemplified by Nirvana's "out of this world" songs. "The Blood Sugar Sex Magic Tour seemed to auger a changing of the musical guard." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Rf1tFWQg51Y
**Reaching Out to Frusciante for Reunion (1998)** : Following Dave Navarro's firing in April 1997, Anthony Kiedis made the initial move to reconnect with John Frusciante, visiting him at a gallery event where Frusciante's paintings were displayed. Recognizing Frusciante's recovery and lack of an instrument, Kiedis personally bought him a '62 Stratocaster to help him restart his musical journey and rejoin the Red Hot Chili Peppers. "Anthony was the first to reach out by going to a gallery event where John was displaying some of his paintings." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc
**Reconciliation Meeting with John Frusciante** : Anthony Kiedis visited John Frusciante at his home to ascertain John's true feelings and intentions regarding a reunion. He sought to determine if John's desire to return stemmed from "love and creation" or lingering "bitterness." Anthony found "a great deal of resolution," concluding that animosity was resolved. "And walking in and sitting on the couch with him and just trying to get a real sense of, you know, is this coming from a place of love and creation? Or does he still have any kind of bitterness towards me or us?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Nr-3Y3UWQcI
**Simultaneous Proposal of John's Return** : Anthony Kiedis and Flea remarkably approached each other at the same time, independently, to discuss John Frusciante's potential return to the band. Anthony was planning to suggest John's participation, while Flea was preparing to announce that he had already jammed with John and considered his full rejoining. "And basically, we were both saying the exact same thing at the exact same time, which was what do you think about John?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Nr-3Y3UWQcI
**Struggles with Sobriety (1988-1992)** : Following Hillel Slovak's death in 1988, Anthony Kiedis was working hard to maintain his sobriety after a "drugs and alcohol bender." His efforts to stay away from heroin, coke, and alcohol led him to distance himself from John Frusciante, whose teenage partying lifestyle was at odds with Kiedis's recovery. He continued to face "continuing sobriety struggles" during the band's turmoil in 1992. "Anthony was working hard to be sober after going on a drugs and alcohol bender after Hillel died." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc
**Vulnerability and Confidence in Reunion** : Anthony Kiedis admitted to feeling a sense of vulnerability about whether "the old magic" with John Frusciante would still be present upon their reunion, questioning if they would merely be "weird guys in a room." However, he ultimately felt more confident than concerned about the outcome, especially once they started playing. "A little bit, it was kind of a vulnerable feeling because like John said, is the old magic still there? Or are we just going to be some weird guys in a room trying to make it happen?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Nr-3Y3UWQcI
