## curiosities
**"Twangy Guitar" Influence** : The distinct "twangy guitar" sound heard in Tom Petty's music is attributed as one of the elements inherited from Credence Clearwater Revival, contributing to the development of Heartland Rock. "the twangy guitar that we hear in Tom Petty's music." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 55 - Heartland Rock is about More than the Heartland | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Confederate Flag Controversy** : During the "Southern Accents" tour, Tom Petty controversially featured a Confederate flag as a stage prop, which unintentionally attracted neo-Confederates to his shows who believed he was aligning with their views. Many years later, in 2015, Petty expressed profound regret for this decision in a Rolling Stone interview, calling himself "stupid" for not foreseeing the negative connotations and comparing the flag's offensive imagery to how a swastika looks to a Jewish person. "if you think a bit longer there's bad connotations to this they might have it at the football game or whatever but they also have it at clan rallies if that's part of it in any way it doesn't belong in any way representing the United States of America." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 55 - Heartland Rock is about More than the Heartland | https://www.ftr70.com/

**Early Categorization in the 1980s** : In the early 1980s, Tom Petty was sometimes erroneously categorized into the Powerpop, New Wave, or Alternative artist mix. This happened due to a tendency at the time to label any short, sharp rock songs as Powerpop, illustrating the confusion around genre definitions. **Geographic Background** : Tom Petty's inclusion in the Heartland Rock canon is often seen as mystifying because he was not from the American Heartland, but rather from Gainesville, Florida. Despite this, he spent the majority of his life in Los Angeles and considered his band, The Heartbreakers, a California band in the tradition of groups like Buffalo Springfield, The Flying Burrito Brothers, and The Beach Boys. "petty also is not from the heartland he is from gains will Florida although he and his southern draw spent the vast majority of his life in Los Angeles he made it clear that he considered the heartbreakers to be a california band in the tradition of buffalo springfield the flying burrito brothers and even the beach boys and that's why." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 55 - Heartland Rock is about More than the Heartland | https://www.ftr70.com/

**Musical Themes and Sound** : Tom Petty's music, and that of The Heartbreakers, embodies the emotional aspect of Heartland Rock, focusing on themes of love, longing, and heartbreak rather than industrial or agrarian struggles. Their sound is characterized as edgy rock and roll, with some punk influences, though Petty himself disliked that comparison. "Their music is not about factories or farms or labor strikes it is kind of edgy rock and roll it does sound a bit punk even if petty didn't really like that comparison the songs are often about love wanting it having it losing it." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 55 - Heartland Rock is about More than the Heartland | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Opposition to Trump Campaign Music Use** : Tom Petty was among a long list of artists who expressed strong opposition to Donald Trump's campaign using their music at political rallies. Despite venues often holding blanket performing rights licenses, artists did not want their work associated with the campaign and many pursued cease and desist orders or lawsuits.
