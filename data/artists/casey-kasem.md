**Early Career and Voice Persona** : Casey Kasem was known as a DJ and a B-grade actor, notably providing the voice for Shaggy in Scooby Doo. Initially, he adopted an FM underground radio station sound to avoid appearing "bubblegum," as pop music was not considered "cool" in 1970 when rock and roll was dominant. Over time, his persona evolved to a slightly hip-dad vibe, especially during the disco era, where he was upbeat and sincere. "He admits that he was trying to not sound quote to bubblegum because a show that is highly dependent on pop music was just not considered cool in 1970." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 39 - American Top 40 - Keeping Our Feet on the Ground and Reaching for the Stars (Part 1) | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-39-american-top-40-keeping-our-feet-on-the-ground-and-reaching-for-the-stars-part-1
**Elvis Presley Tribute (1977)** : Following Elvis Presley's death on August 16, 1977, Casey Kasem delivered a heartfelt tribute on American Top 40, acknowledging Elvis's monumental impact as "the most important individual of the rock era." He highlighted Elvis's journey from a polite country boy driving a delivery truck to one of the century's most salable entertainment talents, crediting Colonel Tom Parker with his discovery (though the transcript corrects this to Sam Phillips's secretary). "This is Kasey Kasey on American Top 40, AT 40 originates in Hollywood and our countdown continues. **Inspiration for Trivia Segments** : The idea for Casey Kasem's popular trivia bits, which offered teasers before commercial breaks and kept listeners engaged week after week, came from a book he found in the trash at a San Francisco radio station. The book, titled "Who's Who in Pop Music 1962," became a fortuitous discovery after his station manager asked him to incorporate more information about musicians and less comedy into his broadcasts.
