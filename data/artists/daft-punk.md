**Homework (1997) - Breakthrough Album** : This album, released approximately 20 years before the transcript's context, is considered a major turning point in 90s electronic music and music in general. It is a cult object and revolutionary because it went against the prevailing trend of complex, fast, and genre-blending electronic music of the mid-90s, returning to the slow, simple tempos characteristic of 80s techno. The album drastically reduced electronic music to its minimal expression, utilizing fewer than 30 sounds in its creation. It was initially not conceived for mass consumption, leading Virgin Records to print only 50,000 CD copies, as it was thought to be a niche album for professional rave culture, with the vinyl edition being considered more important. However, it quickly sold 650,000 CDs, exhausting the initial run immediately and prompting massive production. The album lacks a thematic or argumentative thread, being a compilation of material produced by two artists from the rave culture, eventually gathered into two vinyl discs. While its immediate influence in the late 90s and early 2000s was not substantial, as electronic music continued its complex path, its impact became evident around 2007 with the "second wave" of modern electronic music, showing significant influences on subsequent groups. ← Music Radar Clan > Daft Punk： Como el Homework revolucionó la electrónica | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=myhlWJ6DQpw&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc ← daft-punk ← daft-punk

**The Robot Helmets – 1974 Phantom of the Paradise; Air Conditioning; Communication Links; Spider-Man Effects Company** : The robot mythology was inspired partly by the 1974 Brian De Palma film Phantom of the Paradise, in which disfigured character Winslow Leech wore a helmet to hide his injuries, and partly by a 1970s band called Space, whose members wore helmets and spacesuits for anonymity. One set of helmets was made by the same special effects company that worked on one of the Spider-Man movies. The anonymity was so effective they could walk right through crowds at their own shows unrecognized. One impostor ran up a huge bar tab at a club in Ibiza by claiming to be Thomas Bangalter.
