## songs
**Afternoon Delight (1976)** : Bill Danoff wrote "Afternoon Delight," drawing inspiration from a cafe menu and pulling lines and metaphors together over approximately a year. He described his songwriting process as non-linear, like solving a Rubik's Cube, and the music was partly inspired by Rod Stewart's "Maggie May." "afternoon delight was written by Bill Danop who with his wife a tabby neighbor co-wrote take me home country roads with John Denver." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 31 - The Spirit of '76 - Pop Music on America's Bicentennial | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-31-the-spirit-of-76-pop-music-on-americas-bicentennial
**Boulder to Birmingham** : Bill Danoff composed this song, which Emmylou Harris later performed on her 1975 album "Pieces of Sky." Harris used the song to articulate her profound grief over the death of Gram Parsons.
