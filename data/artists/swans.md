**Filth (1983-84)** : Released early in the band's career, this album, along with *Cop*, is today considered a true gem and a masterpiece of guitar sound experimentation. At the time of its release, Michael Gira was not a professional musician and had another job. It was released on independent labels and received little to no repercussion, selling very few copies and failing to gain recognition, making it difficult for the band to secure concerts. Critics gave it very bad reviews, finding it radically different from the mass-consumed rock of the early 80s by bands like Metallica and Iron Maiden. Its sound was completely atmospheric, contrasting with the sharp, virtuoso, and fast guitar playing common in 80s rock. The lyrics, penned by Michael Gira, were disturbing, addressing themes such as sex, religion, cruelty, submission (sometimes voluntary), love leading to submission, vile behaviors, and aggression. These lyrics were so powerful that, had the band been famous, they likely would have been censored. **Soundtracks for the Blind (1996)** : Released as the band's final album before their separation, this double album is paradoxically considered by many to be the best of Swans' career, despite coming out of a period of immense creative, audience, and musical focus crisis. Michael Gira, believing Swans was "dead," compiled and reworked a multitude of ideas accumulated over the previous 12-13 years of the band's existence. The album masterfully collects all of Swans' diverse stages: from their early hard noise rock to the softer, more melodic period with Jarboe, and even the more banal melodic rock that followed the "Love Will Tear Us Apart" cover. It functions as a "farewell compilation," packaging the best of these often disjointed stages into a surprisingly cohesive and brilliant work. It received fantastic reviews in both Europe and the United States, leading many publications like Pitchfork and AllMusic to re-evaluate and update their initially horrible reviews of Swans' early albums. "En el 96 sale el que para muchos es el mejor disco de su carrera, curiosamente después de todo ese problema creativo." ← Music Radar Clan > SWANS. **The Burning World (1989)** : This album is widely considered an "absolute disaster" and the "worst album in Swans' entire career." It was the result of the band's unexpected popularity from their "Love Will Tear Us Apart" cover, which led them to sign with a major label and work with significant economic resources and a professional producer for the first time. The album sweetened and relaxed Swans' sound, introducing much more banal lyrics, including classic love songs that were completely out of character for the band. It featured much more melodic music with influences from country, Americana, and folk, which clashed with Swans' established discourse. The album failed to convince anyone: classic fans were horrified (even by the cover art), and the new audience gained from the single was neither loyal nor knew what to expect. Critically panned, disliked by the band, and a commercial failure, it is seen as the beginning of the end for the first iteration of Swans, which dissolved in the mid-90s. "El resultado, pues, Borning Wall de 1989, un absoluto desastre, el peor desastre y el peor disco de toda la carrera de suans." ← Music Radar Clan > SWANS. ## songs
**Love Will Tear Us Apart (1988) - Joy Division Cover** : Released in 1988, this cover of Joy Division's iconic song is ironically considered by Michael Gira to be the "worst mistake of his entire career." Despite Gira's personal view, the single brought immense popularity to Swans, especially in the United States, and also significantly in Europe. It became, by far, their best-selling release until their later material in the late 90s. However, this popularity attracted a new audience that was largely disconnected from the aggressive, artistic, and performance-driven ethos Swans had always championed. The success of the single ultimately led the band to leave their independent labels and sign with a major label, a move that would subsequently lead to the critically panned and commercially disastrous album, *The Burning World*. **Legendary and Visceral Live Performances** : Swans' concerts, especially in their early period (before 1985), were legendary and described as "insane" experiences, bordering between a musical concert and an artistic performance or "videoperformance." Michael Gira and the band sought to create a full sensory experience for attendees, often engaging in provocative actions such as insulting the audience, touching them, or performing non-physical but impactful aggressions like pulling hair. They also manipulated the environment by, for example, firing fire alarms, or plunging the room into darkness. With Jarboe's arrival in the mid-80s, these performances became even more exaggerated, sometimes leading to protests and local newspaper articles that decried them as "not music, but something much more grotesque." "los conciertos esuans estaban casi en el límite entre el concierto y la performance artística." ← Music Radar Clan > SWANS.
