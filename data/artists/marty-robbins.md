# artist - Marty Robbins

## members
- Grady Martin (guitarist)

## songs
**Don't Worry (1961)** : Recorded in late 1960, this song featured Grady Martin's baritone guitar part which was plugged directly into a recording console with a faulty preamplifier, resulting in an unintended fuzzy sound due to faulty transistors and transformers. Marty Robbins initially disliked the sound, but producer Owen Bradley and studio engineer Glen Snottie insisted on keeping it. When the song became a hit in the spring of 1961, other artists inquired about how the unique guitar sound was achieved, though no one immediately knew. "Marty Robbins hated the sound, but Grady thought it was cool and so did producer Owen Bradley." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w
**Don't Worry About Me (1960)** : This country song was being recorded in Nashville in 1960 when session musician Grady Martin's amplifier and guitar unexpectedly produced a cool, fuzzy, and distorted sound, an event that led to the invention of the fuzz box. "Grady was working as a session musician in Nashville and he was booked in to work with a country star Marty Robbins on a song called Don't Worry About Me." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
