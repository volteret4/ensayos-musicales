# artist - Marty Robbins

## songs
**Don't Worry (1961)** : This song, recorded in late 1960 at Quonset Hut studio in Nashville, became a big hit in the spring of 1961. It featured an accidental distorted guitar sound created by Grady Martin when a faulty transformer in the recording console halfway failed. Despite Marty Robbins initially disliking the "vulgar" sound, producer Owen Bradley and engineer Glenn Snottie insisted on keeping it in the song, leading to widespread inquiry about how the unique sound was achieved. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
**Don't Worry About Me (1960)** : This song is the first recorded instance of intentional guitar distortion, resulting from a malfunction in session player Grady Martin's amplifier. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
