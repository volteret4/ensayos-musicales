**Approval of Kanye West Sampling** : In stark contrast to Robert Fripp's strong disapproval, Greg Lake viewed Kanye West's sampling of King Crimson's first album's first track for the song "Power" as a "fantastic idea." He openly expressed feeling "truly flattered" that an artist as influential to young generations as Kanye West would utilize material he composed, and he was surprised by its enduring relevance nearly 50 years later. **Protagonist of King Crimson's First Album** : Greg Lake was considered the "true protagonist" of King Crimson's debut album, serving as both its singer and bassist. He was also credited as the author, or a primary author, of its most pivotal musical parts, which generated legal ambiguities regarding authorship with Robert Fripp. "Greg Lake... es el verdadero protagonista del primer risco de la banda.
