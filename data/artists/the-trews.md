## albums
**A Den of Thieves (2005)** : This album, released in 2005, also achieved gold status. It was created quickly after extensive touring from September 2003 to December 2004, with song ideas developed in basements between tours. The band recorded four or five songs in Austin, Texas, with Gordy Johnson, and demoed the rest in their St. Catharines, Ontario basement. Jack Douglas, renowned for his work with Aerosmith, The Who, and John Lennon's "Double Fantasy," produced the album in Toronto in Winter 2005, having agreed after hearing 20 demos. "By the winter of 2005, we were in the studio in Toronto making it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Civilianaires (2018)** : This album was produced by Derek Hoffman, a young producer based in Toronto, and represented a sonic departure for the band. It was their first record not made "live off the floor," instead building tracks and singing over them in Hoffman's basement studio on the East End of Toronto. The album was well-received and nominated for an award that year. "That was the first time we made a record kind of not live off the floor." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Friends & Total Strangers (2009)** : This acoustic album was recorded live over two nights at the Glenn Gould Theatre, located at the CBC headquarters in downtown Toronto. The concept for an acoustic set stemmed from a trip to Japan in 2008, where the band played an acoustic show at the Oscar Peterson Theatre in Tokyo due to heavy equipment constraints. The positive reception of this acoustic performance led to the decision to record the album in January 2009, with John Angus McDonald mixing and producing. "somebody came up with the idea of playing acoustic. And so we did this acoustic set that went over like gangbusters and somebody thought we should maybe get this down." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Hope and Ruin (2011)** : This was the band's fourth album, recorded at The Bathouse, The Tragically Hip's studio in Bath, Kingston. The recording process began in January 2010 after Gord Sinclair offered the band days to write, record, jam, and hang out. The vision for the record was to unite the band's acoustic sound with their high-octane energy on one record, resulting in a diverse collection of songs. "Yeah, that sort of vision for that record was to like I'm talking to Gord about like, can we bring what we do acoustically and what we do on our most high octane energy nights, like under the same umbrella on one record?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**House of Ill Fame (2003)** : The band's debut album, released in 2003, achieved gold status in Canada. Production began in 2002 with Gordy Johnson at Phase One Studio in Scarborough, initially for a four-song EP, three of which made it onto the album. Recording was done piecemeal due to budget constraints, with additional sessions at Metalworks in Summer 2002 (funded by a Rock Search win) and more at Phase One, completing by January 1, 2003. "By January 1st, 2003, it was in the can." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**No Time for Later (2008)** : Released in 2008, this album received Juno nominations and marked the band's first proper US release, promoted by Merovingian and EMI. To produce it, the band specifically chose Gus, known for his work on Pre-Set's "Hello Master" album, as they felt he was making the "best active rock." The band rented a house in Brooklyn and spent two months in Summer 2007 recording the album there. "we moved to Brooklyn for the summer of 07, rented a house in Brooklyn for two months and made, it made that record." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**One-eyed Trouser EP (1997)** : This was the band's first piece of recorded material, though barely remembered and not officially released, except for 50 boxes sold from the back of their mother's van. It was engineered and recorded by Lawrence Curry, known for producing Sloan's "One Chord to Another." Despite the band not being "overly proud of it," copies are still sought after by fans, with some distributed to Patreon subscribers during the pandemic. "it was our first foray into recording." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**The Trews (2015) - Self-Titled** : This self-titled album, the band's fifth, garnered significant support from Classic Rock magazine in the UK. The magazine, known for promoting new music, had previously nominated the band for a "best new band" award for their second album. For this album, Classic Rock magazine packaged the record with every issue, giving away approximately 90,000 copies in the UK. "they actually packaged the record with every issue and gave away like 90,000 copies of it in the UK with Classic Rock magazine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**The Wanderer (2021)** : This album was released late in 2021, featuring songs written during the COVID-19 pandemic. It includes the "COVID era song" titled "I Want to Play." "We did manage to get a whole other record out, which came out late in 21 finally." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM

## songs
**Antigonish Song (1997)** : This song is a sample from the band's rarely remembered 1997 "One-eyed Trouser EP." It carries personal significance as it directly references the band's hometown. "This is called Antigonish Song." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Godspeed Rebel (Unreleased/2020)** : This song, previously unreleased, was put out as a single during the COVID-19 pandemic. The accompanying video was produced entirely using Zoom, as band members were unable to be in the same room together due to restrictions. "we had a song called Godspeed Rebel that we never released and we decided to release a single for it and a video using only Zoom because that's all we could use." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Highway of Heroes (2009)** : This powerful song was written by Colin McDonald, John Angus McDonald, and Gordy Johnson over the phone in about 15 minutes, just two nights before their first acoustic tour in October 2009. It was inspired by the tragic story of Nikola Goddard, a high school friend of John Angus and Jack, who was the first Canadian female killed in combat in Afghanistan in 2006. The song deeply moved Colin McDonald when first played, and it quickly became a special part of their acoustic live sets, recorded in January 2010. "And it happened in like 15 minutes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Highway of Heroes (Pre-2010)** : This song was "in the can," meaning it was recorded or ready for release, around January 2010, prior to the recording of their album "Hope and Ruin." "We had highway of heroes in the can. Or we were about to have highways that are heroes in the can, sorry." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Hold Me In Your Arms (2008)** : A single from "No Time for Later," this song received significant promotion on active rock radio in the US as part of the band's first major American push. It garnered some traction, being played on Sirius Satellite Radio's Alternative Nation. "We spent a boatload of money promoting homey near arms at active rock radio down there." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**I Want to Play (2021)** : This track, described as a "COVID era song," is featured on the band's 2021 album, "The Wanderer." It encapsulates the band's and musicians' desire to return to live performances during the pandemic. "The truth in their COVID era song, I want to play and the track that's turned up on their 2021 album, The Wanderer." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Not Ready To Go (2003)** : Featured on "House of Ill Fame," this song became a major hit, reaching number one on Canadian Rock Radio and being the most played song of the year. Sony's head of radio promo, Velo Mazek, championed the song, relentlessly pushing it to radio. Its success was pivotal, opening doors for the band to play arenas and embark on extensive tours, performing hundreds of shows a year. "It was the most played song of the year." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Paranoid Freak (2008)** : This song, from "No Time for Later," also received airplay on Sirius Satellite Radio's Alternative Nation in the US, contributing to the album's modest traction south of the border. "two songs got played there, hold me in your arms and paranoid freak, but it was definitely, it wasn't like a, you know, home run, but it definitely got a little bit, a little bit of traction." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**So She's Leaving (2005)** : Released as a single from "A Den of Thieves," this song became a "pretty big hit at radio." Its success contributed to the high energy and visibility surrounding the band during the promotion of their second album. "we put out a single which was so she's leaving and that became like a pretty big hit at radio." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Tired of Waiting (2003)** : This track, also from "House of Ill Fame," was a significant song for the band alongside "Not Ready To Go." It contributed to the album's success and the band's rising profile in the Canadian music scene. "And it had Not Ready To Go, and Tired of Waiting, which were two really big songs for us." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Twist and Shout (1963)** : The Trews performed a 12-minute version of this song as an encore with Bruce Springsteen. The impromptu collaboration happened after a band member was asked by Springsteen if he knew the song. The performance is available on YouTube under "Bruce Springsteen with the Trews." "we got up and did like a 12-minute version of twist and shout at the end of his set. And it's on YouTube." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM

## curiosities
**"Civilianaires" Recording Departure** : The recording of the 2018 album "Civilianaires" marked a change in the band's approach. It was their first record that was not recorded "live off the floor," instead being built up track-by-track in producer Derek Hoffman's basement studio in Toronto. "That was the first time we made a record kind of not live off the floor." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**25 Years in Career** : The band noted they are 25 years into their career, expressing a desire to continue creating music, touring the world, and inspiring each other. They hope their best songs and shows are still ahead, aiming to keep growing and refining their craft for their supportive fanbase. "This is 25 years into our career." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Bruce Springsteen Encore Performance** : The Trews performed a 12-minute version of "Twist and Shout" with Bruce Springsteen as an encore after a member was invited by Springsteen during a show. The band ensured they stayed sober enough to perform decently. "we better stay sober enough over the next three hours of his show so we can play twist and shout in the encore decently." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**COVID-19 Pandemic Response** : During the COVID-19 pandemic, The Trews adopted various strategies to stay active and connected with fans, including performing online concerts from a couch in Toronto every Friday night, launching a Patreon, songwriting, home demoing, and engaging fans on Instagram and Facebook. They also produced a Zoom-only video for their single "Godspeed Rebel." "we immediately started doing like a lot of online concerts, like a lot of like, I would do sessions from my couch in Toronto, just playing songs every Friday night." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Classic Rock Magazine Support** : Classic Rock magazine in the UK has been consistently supportive of The Trews. They nominated the band for a "best new band" award for their second album at the UK Classic Rock Awards, where band members attended alongside figures like Jimmy Page and Steven Tyler. The magazine also reviewed their records and interviewed them regularly. "they nominated us for an award on our second album, like a best new band award at the UK Classic Rock Awards." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Colin McDonald's Illness on Ace Frehley Tour** : Before a six-week US tour opening for Ace Frehley at House of Blues venues, Colin McDonald contracted a severe bronchial infection, losing his singing voice for the first three weeks. The audience, largely "die hard Kiss fans," were not typically receptive to new music. He eventually recovered his voice after obtaining antibiotics in America, leading Ace Frehley's famous sound engineer, Nightbob, to jokingly comment that he was "learning how to sing on this tour." "for the first like three weeks at Ace Freyly tour, I couldn't sing. It was a nightmare." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Dave Taylor Becomes Manager** : After their second appearance at The Horseshoe, Buffalo, New York promoter Dave Taylor, who promoted several major venues like The Trowel, saw them perform. Impressed, he offered to manage them, helping them secure better shows in Buffalo. "He saw us. He's like, you guys need a manager. I've got clubs. I can get you on some good shows. Let me represent you." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Extensive Touring After Debut Album** : The success of "Not Ready To Go" transformed the band's career, leading to relentless touring with "a couple hundred shows a year" in the first few years. They opened for various bands, including Three Days Grace, Evanescence, and Big Sugar, playing in arenas. "suddenly we were getting our first offers to play in arenas opening for bands. And we were on the road all the time, like couple hundred shows a year, easy for those first couple of years." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Fan Engagement and Creative Leash** : The Trews expressed gratitude for their "really great fans" who allow them a "long leash creatively," following them on diverse musical journeys. The band aims to continue providing exciting, feel-good entertainment that leaves people smiling and singing. "they allow us a long leash creatively. Like they kind of follow us on a lot of our different journeys musically." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Featured in "In Their Own Words" Series** : The Trews was featured in the "In Their Own Words" series, which invites multiple band members to share their personal histories. "the Trues, and 5440." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bI6TTA-fn7c
**First Arena Show Inspiration** : A band member's first meaningful arena show was watching The Tragically Hip perform on their "Phantom Power" tour in the late 90s in Halifax. The experience of seeing Gord Downie "own the stage" and the band's delivery left a lasting impact. "watching Downey own the stage the way he did and watched the band deliver on the level that they did. That never left me." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Forced Name Change to The Trews (2002)** : Right before the release of their first EP in 2002, which Gordy Johnson produced, the band received a cease and desist letter from an acid jazz band from Mississauga also named "Trouser." Faced with an overnight deadline to rename themselves for the record, Jack Siparek's mother, Jean, suggested "The Trews," explaining it meant "Scottish pants," similar to trousers but distinct from kilts. "Jack's mother, Jean called us and said, you guys should call yourselves the trouser because the trues mean the same thing as trousers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Formation and Early Cover Gigs (1996)** : The band started in 1996 in Colin McDonald's grandmother's basement, playing cover songs, including tracks by Tom Petty, The Beatles, The Tragically Hip, James Brown, and The Black Crowes. They gigged frequently in Antigonish high schools and local bars like Chuggles, Piper's Pub, and Pats Place, sometimes needing permission slips to play underage. "We started in 1996 and my grandmother's basement playing cover songs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Horseshoe Tavern Breakthrough** : During their self-booked national tour, the band secured a "legit gig" at The Horseshoe in Toronto on a new music night, booked by Craig Lasky. Their performance led Lasky to immediately call them back for another booking the next day, prompting the band to frequently return to Toronto. "Craig called us the next day... he's like, when can I book you again?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Japan Trip with Canadian Artists (2008)** : In 2008, the band took a trip to Japan to play at the Canadian consulate, sharing a versatile bill at the Oscar Peterson Theatre in Tokyo with artists like Bedouin Soundclash, The Rural Alberta Advantage (then known as Wooden Hands), The Russian Futurists, and Alex Cuba. The venue's inability to accommodate their usual "heavy equipment, loud rock show" prompted their first significant acoustic performance. "we went and played at the consulate with like Bedouin Sound Clash and the band that ended to be the rural Alberta advantage." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Jeff Healey's Bar Residency (2001)** : Following an opening gig for Jeff Healey in Halifax in Summer 2001, Healey invited the band to be the house band at his new Toronto bar, Healey's, on Bathurst and Queen. The Trews accepted, playing every Thursday throughout Summer and Fall 2001. "He said, look, I'm starting a bar called Healey's. You guys give me my house band every Thursday." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Larry Wanigas Management** : During their residency at Jeff Healey's bar, they caught the attention of legendary manager Larry Wanigas, known for managing k.d. lang and Big Sugar. Wanigas liked the band and introduced them to producer Gordy Johnson, leading to their first album. "Larry Wanigas who is the, you know, legendary manager, he managed Katie Lang and at the time he was managing big sugar." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Lifelong Band Connection** : Three members, Jack, Johnny, and Collins, describe their connection as lifelong and inspirational, driving them to continually seek new songs and enjoy making music and performing for audiences globally. "the three of us, me and Johnny and Collins formed a connection like years ago. And it feels like that it's a lifelong connection." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Name Change to Trouser (2000)** : In 2000, after years of playing cover gigs as One-eyed Trouser, the band's manager in Buffalo, New York, suggested shortening their name to "Trouser," believing "eyed" bands were passé. This change coincided with their move from Nova Scotia to Ontario. "So we call our band trouser in 2000." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Niagara Falls Residency** : The band lived for two years in a rented house at 6289 Main Street in Niagara Falls, Ontario, which they described as a "rundown former crack den." This location became crucial to their career, as they established their base there after living across the river in Buffalo at their manager's house, and launched their career from this residence. "We lived there for like two years and we literally got our career off the ground from that house." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Opening for The Rolling Stones** : While working on "A Den of Thieves" in Toronto in Summer 2005, rumors surfaced about The Rolling Stones rehearsing for a secret show. Larry Wanigas, unable to get tickets, instead secured The Trews an opening slot at The Phoenix with just 24 hours' notice, contingent on Charlie Watts' health. The band was sworn to secrecy and, upon arriving for soundcheck, found The Rolling Stones already on stage. They earned a photo with the legendary band after their set. "Larry calls... I can't get you tickets, but you can open for them." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Original Name: One-eyed Trouser Snake** : Before becoming The Trews, Colin McDonald had a punk/grunge band in high school in Newfoundland and Nova Scotia with a friend who shared a love for Monty Python. This earlier band was named One-eyed Trouser Snake, after a song from Monty Python's "Meaning of Life." When the core members (Colin, John Angus, Jack) formed, they adopted a shortened version, "One-eyed Trouser." "me and my buddy from grade nine really liked Monty Python and we called our first band One-eyed trouser snake from that song from Meaning of Life." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Patreon Launch (Pandemic)** : At the beginning of the pandemic, The Trews launched a Patreon account. As part of this initiative, they distributed leftover copies of their rare 1997 "One-eyed Trouser EP," which had been stored at Colin McDonald's mother's house, to their "diehard fans." "We launched a Patreon at the beginning of the pandemic and one of the, and we had a bunch of these leftover trouser EPs from my mom's house and we gave them to a bunch of our diehard fans." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Rock Search Win (2002)** : After opening for Big Sugar, a connection facilitated by Larry Wanigas, the band was seen by representatives from 97.7 Hits FM. They were encouraged to enter and subsequently won Rock Search in 2002, which provided them with funds to record at Metalworks studio. "we went and won it in 2002." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Self-Booked National Tour** : Driven by ambition to break out of Nova Scotia, the band embarked on an audacious self-booked national tour from Antigonish to Vancouver and back. They cold-called numerous venues, often facing rejection, and slept in the back of a van with a bed built over their amps, relying on friends and family for support. "we booked ourselves from anti-genitio to Vancouver and back, sleeping in the back of the van." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Self-Titled Album Magazine Giveaway** : For their 2015 self-titled album, Classic Rock magazine packaged the record with every issue, distributing approximately 90,000 copies of it in the UK. This initiative provided significant exposure for the band. "they actually packaged the record with every issue and gave away like 90,000 copies of it in the UK with Classic Rock magazine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**The "Bohemian Rhapsody" Incident** : During their tour with Robert Plant, a fan dared The Trews on live radio to perform their old cover of Queen's "Bohemian Rhapsody" at a sold-out show in Rexall Place, Edmonton. The band delivered a "punky version" that garnered a standing ovation. This angered Robert Plant, who, having known Freddie Mercury, didn't want the past relived. He "accosted" their drummer, Sean, the next day in Calgary, but later issued a public apology by praising Canadian songwriters, including The Trews, at the Saddledome. "we got word that we really ticked Robert Plant off." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**The Bathouse Recording Experience** : The band recorded their "Hope and Ruin" album at The Bathouse, The Tragically Hip's studio in Bath, Kingston. This studio is described as an old coach house with a swimming pool, basketball court, grand piano, and living/sleeping quarters upstairs, functioning as a live-work recording space. "It's one of these old-style live work recording studios." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**The Tragically Hip Connection** : The Trews opened for The Tragically Hip in 2004 and maintained contact with them, leading to Gord Sinclair producing "Hope and Ruin" at The Bathouse. The Bathouse also served as a "Hip museum," featuring artifacts like the console from "Phantom Power," a painting from "In Between Evolution," and original artwork from "Fully Completely." "getting to work in this like house that's technically a hip museum." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Touring with Robert Plant (2005)** : The Trews embarked on a full North American tour, "a dozen dates" from Toronto to Victoria, opening for Robert Plant, who was promoting his "Mighty ReArranger" album. Plant was noted for his friendliness, checking out their guitars at their first soundcheck and performing Led Zeppelin songs like "Going to California" and "That's The Way." "we were getting on a bus to tour with the Golden God himself." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
