# artist - Andrew Loog Oldham Orchestra

## albums
**The Rolling Stones Songbook (1965)** : This album, released in 1965, was a collection of orchestral covers of Rolling Stones songs, including an interpretation of "The Last Time." These arrangements were often "watered down" versions, designed for easy listening fans who might be offended by rock and roll music. A five-note progression from their version of "The Last Time" later became the basis for The Verve's "Bittersweet Symphony." ## curiosities
**Lawsuit for Royalties** : Andrew Loog Oldham, who was the original manager of The Rolling Stones, sued The Verve, claiming they owed him $1.7 million in royalties for "Bittersweet Symphony" due to the use of a five-note progression from his orchestra's version of "The Last Time." **Orchestral Interpretations for Easy Listening** : The Andrew Loog Oldham Orchestra created orchestral versions of The Rolling Stones' songs, released in 1965. These arrangements were designed to be "watered down" for fans of easy listening music, catering to those who might be offended by the original rock and roll sound.
