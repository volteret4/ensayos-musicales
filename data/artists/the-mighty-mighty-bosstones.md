## curiosities
**Booked for Content Pivot (1996)** : The Mighty Mighty Bosstones were booked for the 1996 Warped Tour as the festival shifted its focus to punk and skate culture-compatible music after securing Vans as a sponsor. "This meant booking fishbone, the muddy, muddy boss tones, no effect, 3-11 penny wise, and this weird new group from San Diego called Blink 182." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Nate Albert's Membership** : Nate Albert, who later became a prominent A&R executive, played guitar for The Mighty Mighty Bosstones. His involvement in the band is mentioned in the context of his A&R career. "Nate is a former member of the mighty, mighty boss tones. He played guitar on songs like this." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5kPmqmbFfkY
**Returned for 1997 Tour** : The band was among the acts that returned to participate in the Warped Tour in 1997. "Like 1 and 2 was back, as were the boss tones." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Third Wave Ska Band** : The Mighty Mighty Bosstones were identified as a band from the "third wave" of Ska, which arrived in the 1990s. This wave saw significant popularity with bands like No Doubt, Sublime, Reel Big Fish, Goldfinger, and Dance Hall Crashers. "Third wave Scott arrived in the 1990s, no doubt, muddy, muddy boss tones, sublime, real big fish, gold finger, dancehall crashes and so many more." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HSvgRyGiOpE
