# artist - They Might Be Giants

## members
- John Flansburgh
- John Linnell

## genres

- Nerd Rock

## albums
**Flood (1990)** : Released in 1990, this album featured several genuine alt-rock radio hits, including "Particle Man." The album's success came after the band's unconventional methods, like "dial the song," gained them a cult following and eventually a major record label deal. "And in 1990 they released a record called Flood." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h6snk0m4200

## songs
**Particle Man (1990)** : This song became an alt-rock radio hit in 1990, known for its existentialist themes that defy straightforward interpretation. It showcased They Might Be Giants' quirky and intellectual songwriting, making it a prominent example of their nerdy appeal. "It's called Particle Man. And don't try to make sense of this unless you bend your head around existentialist things. Otherwise, you're just going to hurt yourself." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h6snk0m4200
**The Guitar (N/A)** : This song by They Might Be Giants was played to highlight the significance of the electric guitar as "new rock moment number three," underscoring its foundational role in modern music. "Hey, the guitar. They might be giants with a song called the guitar." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MTDY6qT11Dc

## curiosities
**"Nerd Rock Kings" Designation** : Billboard magazine eventually referred to They Might Be Giants as the "Nerd Rock Kings," a designation that may have been instrumental in bringing the term "nerd rock" into mainstream usage. "Billboard would eventually refer to them as the Nerd Rock Kings." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h6snk0m4200
**Dial-A-Song Service** : They developed an innovative service called "dial the song," where fans could call a specific phone number (716-387-6962) to hear a new song daily or weekly. Although disconnected for a while, the service occasionally resurfaces. "They ran something called dial the song where fans could dial a number and get a new song every day, every week, whenever." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h6snk0m4200
**Formation and Early Innovation (1982)** : The two-man group, consisting of John Flansburgh and John Linnell, formed in Brooklyn in 1982. They were known for their unusual projects, such as writing songs for their answering machine. "They've been around since 1982 doing weird things like writing songs for their answering machine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h6snk0m4200
