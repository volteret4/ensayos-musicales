# artist - Michael Nesmith

## curiosities
**Connection to The Tragically Hip Name** : Michael Nesmith, a video pioneer and former member of The Monkees, produced the 1981 VHS tape "Elephant Parts," which contained a skit from which The Tragically Hip took their name. "This was produced by video pioneer and former member of the monkeys Michael Nessmith." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dnz_Ycd_q7s
**Dislike for Music Categorization** : Michael Nesmith believed that attempting to categorize music, including the "country rock" label, was a "waste of time." He expressed a general aversion to such classifications. "Nessmith said though that he thinks it's a waste of time to try to categorize music at all and he also did not like the country rock label." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 35 - Cosmic American Music - 70s Country Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-35-cosmic-american-music-70s-country-rock
**Founding The First National Band** : After achieving fame with The Monkees in the 1960s, Michael Nesmith was a member of The First National Band, which is recognized as one of the earliest country rock groups. Despite this, he personally did not appreciate the "country rock" label. "Michael Nessmith, who became very famous as a member of the monkeys in the 1960s, was part of one of the first country rock bands after that." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 35 - Cosmic American Music - 70s Country Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-35-cosmic-american-music-70s-country-rock
**Real Musician in Fictional Band** : Michael Nesmith was a real person, an actor and musician, who was hired to play a fictional musician within The Monkees, a band created for a television show. "But Michael Naysmith... these were real people. They were actors and musicians hired to pay fictional musicians seemingly inspired by the Beatles." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 30 - The Sweet Sound of 70s Bubblegum Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/030-bubblegum
