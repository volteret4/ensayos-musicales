## songs
**Diamonds and Guns (2002)** : The first single from The Transplants' debut album. Its piano lines were somewhat inspired by The Clash's "Rock the Casbah." The song found commercial success by being used as the soundtrack for a shampoo commercial. "The first single from the album, inspired somewhat by the piano lines of the Clashes rock the Casbah, did okay." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tkLRJiOBu0

## curiosities
**Formation and Sound** : This group originated with Tim Armstrong, who was experimenting with computer-generated hip-hop beats. Travis Barker joined as the final piece of the band, allowing him to explore more demanding drumming parts than he typically played with Blink 182. It's described as an indie punk supergroup. "Travis had his own muses to follow. He and Tim Armstrong from Ranset got together to explore hip-hop beats in a group called the Transplants." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tkLRJiOBu0
**Travis Barker's Artistic Stretch** : Travis Barker appreciated the opportunity to stretch himself musically with The Transplants, learning to play drum parts that were significantly more demanding than those required in Blink 182. "Travis Barker from Blink 182, who really love the idea of being able to stretch himself by learning to play parts that were much more demanding than what he was called on to do with Blink 182." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tkLRJiOBu0
