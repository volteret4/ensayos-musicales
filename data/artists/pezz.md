## albums
**Demoluca (1994) - First 4-Song Demo** : This was Pezz's first recording, a four-song demo tape recorded in Ian D'Sa's parents' basement on John Gallant's Tascam 4-track. Only 100 copies were made, and it was used to secure downtown Toronto shows, leading to their first gig at Elvis Mondays. "So we recorded our first four-song demo in my parents' basement and we called it Demoluca. That was done in 94 and we used that to try to get shows downtown." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cZlLH9J_lJw
**Dudebox (Year not specified in transcript)** : Pezz's second demo, a six-song EP, was recorded at Signal to Noise studio in Toronto with engineer Dave Tedesco. It represented an improvement in recording quality over their initial home demos. "And then we recorded the second tape that we did was that a little bit of better studio was an on-four track. It was a signal to noise. A signal to noise, yeah. And Toronto with Dave Tedesco. And that was another, that was a six-song EP, Dudebox, yeah." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cZlLH9J_lJw
**The Billy Talent EP (2001) - Green Album 4-Song Demo** : This demo, informally known as "the green album," contained four songs including "Try Honesty," "Cut the Curtains," "This Is How It Goes," and "Beach Balls." It was recorded in Burlington at The Music Gym with Justin Cooper, and only 200 to 250 copies were made. This EP was significant as the band intentionally focused their sound, departing from the eclectic nature of "Wattouche," and is credited with saving the band. "There's one more demo that's, it's the green album and it's like a four song demo. And we only made like 200 or 250. ... And we literally just kind of focused our sound into one thing. And that was, that ended up being the four songs on the screen EP. And it was just called the Billy Talent EP." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cZlLH9J_lJw
**Wattouche (1999) - Full Length Album** : Released on July 23rd, 1999, this full-length album was recorded with producer Brad Nelson (known for working with The Headstones) and Darryl Smith at Tattoo Music, a jingle studio where they recorded for free at night. The band self-produced 500 copies using Aaron Solowaniak's credit card and promoted it independently, earning positive reviews at Canadian Music Week despite having no label or official management. "Oh, yeah, we did a full length, yeah, with Darryl Smith, a chemical sound, and that came at Brad Nelson. That was 99. Yeah, Darryl Smith, Dan Brad Nelson at... Yeah, that was Wattouche. That was Wattouche. And that one's still available." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cZlLH9J_lJw

## curiosities
**Alanis Morissette - Wattouche Studio Encounter** : During the recording of "Wattouche" at Tattoo Music, Alanis Morissette was present in the kitchen, as she was friends with the studio owners, Tim and Tom Thorney, and was working with them. At the time, she was a superstar, having sold 40 million records. She listened to their music, offered advice, and even made them spaghetti. "When we were making Wattouche, we walk in and I remember I was going to go get a cup of tea and a lanness more set was standing in this kitchen. ... And then she ended up coming in and like listening to our tunes and hanging out and was so cool and gave us good advice and... Made us spaghetti." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cZlLH9J_lJw
**Band Name - Pezz (Origin and Spelling)** : The band settled on the name "Pezz" because Ben Kowalewicz had many Pez dispensers in his room, which served as a band meeting spot. They added "X-RZ" to avoid potential legal issues with the Pez candy company. "And I think we settled on Pes because in my room I had a bunch of Pes dispensers all the time. We'd always have like band meetings up there. And it was just like, we couldn't think of a name. So we're like, why don't you just call it Pes and add an X-RZ so we don't get sued." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cZlLH9J_lJw
**Band Name - The Other One** : The band's initial name was "The Other One" for a handful of shows, as they struggled to decide on a permanent name. "Well, originally it was the other one. Yeah. **Band Name Challenge - Pez vs. Pezz** : In 1996, Pezz received an email from a representative of another punk band called "Pes" on BIO Records in the United States. This band's records were available in Canadian stores, leading to a request for Pezz to change their name. They continued to use the name "Pezz" until 1999 as they didn't have a record deal at the time. "And technically, yeah, we ended up getting an email when email first came about in 1996. I got an email from a guy representing a band called Pes on BIO records in the United States. And he just basically said we have records in Canadian stores so you have to change your name." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cZlLH9J_lJw

**The Billy Talent EP - "Saved the Band"** : The 2001 "Billy Talent EP" (often referred to as the green album demo) is credited by the band members as having "saved the band" during a particularly difficult period that included Aaron Solowaniak's MS diagnosis and John Gallant's temporary departure.
