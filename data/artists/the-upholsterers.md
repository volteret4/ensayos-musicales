# artist - The Upholsterers

## members
- Brian Moldun
- Jack Gillis (Jack White)

## albums
**Makeers of High Grade Suites (Mid-1990s)** : This album was recorded by Jack Gillis (Jack White) and Brian Moldun, who called themselves The Upholsterers. It is estimated to be from the mid-1990s. "That record was called Makeers of High Grade Suites, and they build themselves, perhaps not surprisingly, as the Apulsterers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r4QJMx1LES8
**Your Furniture was always dead, I was just afraid to tell you (Mid-1990s) - 7-inch Single** : An extremely rare 3-track, 7-inch vinyl single by The Upholsterers, with approximately 100 copies produced in the mid-1990s. Notably, these singles were concealed within couches and chairs that Jack and Brian were reupholstering, making them highly sought after by collectors with only two known copies found. "There were about 100 vinyl copies out there of a 3-track 7-inch called Your Furniture was always dead, I was just afraid to tell you." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r4QJMx1LES8

## curiosities
**Extreme Rarity of Single** : The 7-inch single, "Your Furniture was always dead, I was just afraid to tell you," is incredibly rare, with only an estimated 100 copies produced and a mere two discovered. Its scarcity is such that major record collecting websites do not even attempt to price it, and audio recordings are nearly impossible to find online. "Only two of those 100 or so copies have been found so far. It is so rare that most record collecting sites won't even affix a price to it. I can't even find audio of it posted anywhere." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r4QJMx1LES8
**Unique Single Distribution** : The Upholsterers famously hid about 100 vinyl copies of their 3-track 7-inch single, "Your Furniture was always dead, I was just afraid to tell you," inside furniture they were reupholstering. This unique distribution method contributed to the single's extreme rarity, with only two copies having been found. "Jack and Brian hid some singles inside couches and chairs that they were fixing up." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r4QJMx1LES8
