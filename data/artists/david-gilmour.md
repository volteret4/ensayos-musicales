## curiosities
**Auction Sale of the "Black Strat"** : David Gilmour's legendary "Black Strat" was eventually sold at auction for nearly $4 million US dollars. The purchaser was Jim Irsay, who is the owner of the Indianapolis Colts. "It sold at auction for nearly $4 million US dollars to Jim Isray, the owner of the Indianapolis Colts." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw ← david-gilmour
**Purchase of the "Black Strat" (1969)** : David Gilmour's renowned "Black Strat," the legendary guitar used on many iconic Pink Floyd recordings, was purchased at Manie's Music in 1969. This acquisition highlights the store's importance as a source for significant musical instruments. "David Gilmore's famous Black Strat, the legendary guitar, used on so many iconic Pink Floyd recordings, was purchased at Manie's in 1969." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg ← david-gilmour
**Roadie for Pink Floyd, then Member** : In the mid-1960s, David Gilmour initially worked as a roadie for Pink Floyd. He was later invited to join the band as a guitarist by drummer Nick Mason, marking his transition from support staff to a full-fledged member. "Way back in the mid 1960s David Gilmore roadied for a young band called Pink Floyd. He was drummer Nick Mason who invited him into the band as a guitarist." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZVGnSVfed9s ← david-gilmour ← david-gilmour
**The "Black Strat"** : David Gilmour's iconic "Black Strat" was purchased at Mani's Music in New York in 1970. This specific guitar became a cornerstone of his sound, being utilized for every Pink Floyd album and tour following its acquisition. "David Gilmore's beautiful solo on Pink Floyd's comfortably numb was played on his famous Black Strat, a guitar he bought at Mani's Music in New York in 1970." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw ← david-gilmour
**Use of Wah-Wah Pedal** : David Gilmour frequently used the wah-wah pedal throughout Pink Floyd's material, showcasing the influence of the pedal, which was introduced to prominent guitarists like him at Manie's Music.
