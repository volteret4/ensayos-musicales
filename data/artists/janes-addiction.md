## curiosities
**Beneficiary of Alt-Rock Appetite** : Benefited from record labels looking for alt-rock beyond grunge after the "Grunge Gold Rush." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Collaboration on Mike Watt's Album (1995)** : Members of Jane's Addiction contributed to Mike Watt's solo album "Ball-Hog or Tugboat," which also featured Dave Grohl. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_AY5lS-FmlU
**Eric Avery's Joining** : Eric Avery, the bassist, was the first to join, being a friend of "Jane," the junkie hooker after whom the band was named. He and Perry Farrell spent time jamming together. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CVPPymc_qrk
**Founding Band of Lollapalooza** : was a founding band of the first Lollapalooza festival in 1991. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Introduction of Navarro and Perkins** : Eric Avery introduced Perry Farrell to two school friends, guitarist Dave Navarro and drummer Stephen Perkins, who were both members of a metal band called Disaster. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CVPPymc_qrk
**John Anson Ford Theatre Performances** : Taylor Hawkins saw Jane's Addiction perform seven nights at the John Anson Ford Theatre in LA. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mh-qg1izM98
**Kevin Lyman Stage Manager (1991)** : Kevin Lyman worked as a stage manager for Jane's Addiction on the first Lollapalooza road trip in 1991, which inspired his idea for package tours. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Lollapalooza Founding (1991)** : Singer Perry Farrell organized the first Lollapalooza festival in 1991 to support what would be the band's last-ever tour at the time. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Lollapalooza Inspiration (1990)** : On August 26, 1990, Perry Farrell, frontman of Jane's Addiction, witnessed the Pixies' headline performance at Reading Festival, where 30,000 people reacted ecstatically to their song "Debaser." This energetic crowd response made Farrell question why such an event couldn't exist in America, leading to the conception of Lollapalooza. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Lollapalooza Performance (1991)** : Jane's Addiction headlined the very first Lollapalooza show, which began outside Phoenix on July 18, 1991. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Lollapalooza's Original Purpose** : Lollapalooza was initially conceived as a farewell tour for Jane's Addiction, cleverly disguised as a music festival to help it get off the ground. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Mention in Musical Evolution** : Jane's Addiction is mentioned in the context of tracing the evolution of music, specifically asking about its connection to The Jam. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oPgeSCy93bo
**Mountain Song Inspiration** : Perry Farrell's performance of "Mountain Song" on the 1991 Lollapalooza tour was identified by Kevin Lyman as the inspirational moment that led him to conceive of his own tour. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Perry Farrell's Myth about Stephen Perkins** : Perry Farrell concocted a myth that the band's first drummer was a guy from Tahiti who smuggled canaries but died of an overdose, and that Stephen Perkins was his twin brother and a former boyfriend of actress Jodie Foster, all of which is presented as untrue. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CVPPymc_qrk
**Psychic Rhythmic Connection** : Eric Avery and Stephen Perkins were described as having a "psychic rhythmic connection" that was evident in their parts in songs. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CVPPymc_qrk
**Taylor Hawkins' "Punk Rock"** : Taylor Hawkins considered Jane's Addiction his "punk rock." He became a "complete disciple" after hearing them in high school, seeing them as messy, disgusting, awesome, and scary, a stark contrast to the "crappy hair metal" of the era. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mh-qg1izM98
**Usage of Marshall Amps** : Guitarist Dave Navarro is a known user of Marshall amplifiers, contributing to the brand's association with powerful rock sounds. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
