**"Hey, all right. Forget about it. It's mine. Mine all mine. **Jane Says (1988) – Breakthrough for an LA Band** : Recorded over six months in 1987 and 1988 and released on August 23, 1988, "Jane Says" became Jane's Addiction's big breakthrough song. Despite its meandering lyrics, simple two-chord structure, and lack of a traditional chorus (featuring only three verses and a bridge), the song successfully established the band as part of a new wave of LA rock bands in the late 1980s. The song is a direct reflection of the life and struggles of Jane Bainter, Perry Farrell's ex-housemate and muse. "Jane says, have you seen my wig around? I can make it without it. She knows they all want it to go." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk
**Mountain Song** : Kevin Lyman, the founder of the Warped Tour, was inspired to create his own touring festival after watching Perry Farrell's performance of "Mountain Song" during the first Lollapalooza road trip in 1991, which he was stage managing. "Jane's performance of this song on that tour. There was just something about Perry Farrell building up Mountain Song that had Kevin Lyman thinking about his own tour." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Superhero** : This song was licensed for use as the theme for the TV series "Entourage," providing ongoing exposure and revenue for the band through its repeated play at the beginning of each episode. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sfie4YYncxk
**Three Days (Unspecified Year)** : This song was mentioned in the context of Dave Navarro, a guitarist known for utilizing the influential Marshall amplifier, thereby associating the band with Jim Marshall's significant invention. **Band Naming Origin** : The band's name, Jane's Addiction, was derived from "Jane," a friend of Eric Avery described as a "junkie hooker." "Jane being the junkie hooker after which the band was eventually named." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CVPPymc_qrk

"Jane, Sergio, Perry, and about half a dozen other people shared a Victorian house on Wilton Street in East Hollywood back in the 1980s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk

**Impact on Taylor Hawkins** : Jane's Addiction profoundly influenced Taylor Hawkins in high school, offering a raw, unpolished alternative to the "blah music" of hair metal. Hawkins viewed them as both "dudes in the poster" and "a mess," "disgusting," "awesome," and "scary," making them his personal equivalent of punk rock. "And then I heard Jane's addiction and it was like, dude, I could play like the dudes in the poster. But they were a mess and they were disgusting and they were awesome and they were scary." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mh-qg1izM98
**Jane Bainter's Drug Addiction** : Jane Bainter, the band's namesake and muse, was a junkie, a fact explicitly referenced in the band's name ("Jane's addiction"). The song "Jane Says" directly details her struggles with drug use. "First, she was a junkie. Farrell and was Jane's addiction." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk
**Jane Bainter's Later Life and Estrangement** : By the end of the century, Jane Bainter had achieved sobriety, being clean for eight years, and successfully made her desired move to Spain. However, she eventually fell out with Perry Farrell and the band, resulting in her being snubbed and denied entry to their shows. Furthermore, she has received no financial compensation from anything related to the band or its music. "The good news is that by the end of the century, she had been clean for eight years and did end up going to Spain." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk
**Jane Bainter: Muse and Namesake** : Jane Bainter was not only Perry Farrell's ex-housemate and a muse for his lyrics, but she is also the namesake for the band, "Jane's Addiction." She was described as having a posh, aristocratic air and being very intellectual, with her picture featured in the artwork of the band's indie debut album. "Many fans don't know that there really is a Jane who said a bunch of these things. Her name was Jane Bainter." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk
**Jane's Daily Life and Aspirations** : Jane Bainter held a day job at an office, but her nights were consumed by shooting up. Despite this, she expressed a strong desire to escape her situation, telling anyone who would listen that she was saving money to run away to Spain, where her divorced mother and new boyfriend had a house in the south of the country. However, her drug addiction made it impossible for her to get herself together enough to make the trip at the time. "She had a day job at an office, but at night she'd come home and shoot up." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk
