# artist - Jane's Addiction

## members
- Dave Navarro
- Eric Avery
- Perry Farrell
- Stephen Perkins

## genres
- Alt-Rock
- Metal

## curiosities
**Beneficiary of Alt-Rock Appetite** : Benefited from record labels looking for alt-rock beyond grunge after the "Grunge Gold Rush."
**Collaboration on Mike Watt's Album (1995)** : Members of Jane's Addiction contributed to Mike Watt's solo album "Ball-Hog or Tugboat," which also featured Dave Grohl.
**Eric Avery's Joining** : Eric Avery, the bassist, was the first to join, being a friend of "Jane," the junkie hooker after whom the band was named. He and Perry Farrell spent time jamming together.
**Founding Band of Lollapalooza** : was a founding band of the first Lollapalooza festival in 1991.
**Introduction of Navarro and Perkins** : Eric Avery introduced Perry Farrell to two school friends, guitarist Dave Navarro and drummer Stephen Perkins, who were both members of a metal band called Disaster.
**John Anson Ford Theatre Performances** : Taylor Hawkins saw Jane's Addiction perform seven nights at the John Anson Ford Theatre in LA.
**Perry Farrell's Myth about Stephen Perkins** : Perry Farrell concocted a myth that the band's first drummer was a guy from Tahiti who smuggled canaries but died of an overdose, and that Stephen Perkins was his twin brother and a former boyfriend of actress Jodie Foster, all of which is presented as untrue.
**Psychic Rhythmic Connection** : Eric Avery and Stephen Perkins were described as having a "psychic rhythmic connection" that was evident in their parts in songs.
**Taylor Hawkins' "Punk Rock"** : Taylor Hawkins considered Jane's Addiction his "punk rock." He became a "complete disciple" after hearing them in high school, seeing them as messy, disgusting, awesome, and scary, a stark contrast to the "crappy hair metal" of the era.
