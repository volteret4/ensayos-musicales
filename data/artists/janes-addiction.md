## songs
**Jane Says (1988) – Breakthrough for an LA Band** : Recorded over six months in 1987 and 1988 and released on August 23, 1988, "Jane Says" became Jane's Addiction's big breakthrough song. Despite its meandering lyrics, simple two-chord structure, and lack of a traditional chorus (featuring only three verses and a bridge), the song successfully established the band as part of a new wave of LA rock bands in the late 1980s. The song is a direct reflection of the life and struggles of Jane Bainter, Perry Farrell's ex-housemate and muse. "Jane says, have you seen my wig around? I can make it without it. She knows they all want it to go." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk

**Jane Bainter's Later Life and Estrangement** : By the end of the century, Jane Bainter had achieved sobriety, being clean for eight years, and successfully made her desired move to Spain. **Jane Bainter: Muse and Namesake** : Jane Bainter was not only Perry Farrell's ex-housemate and a muse for his lyrics, but she is also the namesake for the band, "Jane's Addiction." She was described as having a posh, aristocratic air and being very intellectual, with her picture featured in the artwork of the band's indie debut album. "Many fans don't know that there really is a Jane who said a bunch of these things. Her name was Jane Bainter." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk
**Jane's Daily Life and Aspirations** : Jane Bainter held a day job at an office, but her nights were consumed by shooting up. However, her drug addiction made it impossible for her to get herself together enough to make the trip at the time.
