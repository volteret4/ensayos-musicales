## curiosities
**Extensive Collaborations** : Beyond her work with The Wrecking Crew, Carol K collaborated with an almost endless list of artists, including Glenn Campbell, Neil Young, Frank Zappa, Ray Charles, Tina Turner, and later with Frank Black of the Pixies on his 2006 record *Fast Man Raider*. "Glenn Campbell, Neil Young, Frank Zappa, Ray Charles, Tina Turner. The list is nearly endless." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjQiZqpmQlE
**In-Demand Session Musician** : Her studio career began in 1957 as a guitarist, and she transitioned to bass in 1963. At a time when no women typically played bass, she embraced the instrument for available gigs, quickly becoming a key member of The Wrecking Crew, an elite group of studio musicians known for their versatility and high demand. "She started working in studios in 1957, switching from guitar to bass in 1963. And listen, no woman played bass in 1963. It just wasn't a ladies instrument." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjQiZqpmQlE
**Most Heard Bass Player** : Carol K is considered one of the most frequently heard bass players in music history, with a conservative estimate placing her involvement on 10,000 different recordings. "She is one of the most heard bass players in the history of music. A modest guess is that she appears on 10,000 different recordings." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjQiZqpmQlE
