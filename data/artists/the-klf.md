# artist - The KLF

## genres
- Acid House
- Experimental

## songs
**Doctorin' the Tardis (1988)** : Released in 1988 under the alias The Timelords, this acid house anthem famously sampled Delia Derbyshire's "Doctor Who Theme" and Gary Glitter's "Rock and Roll Part 2." The track's availability is limited, partly due to Gary Glitter's disgraced status and the KLF's anti-copyright philosophy, expressed in their name "Copyright Liberation Front." "The KLF working as the Timewords in 1988 and Dr. In The Tartus, which takes Delia Derbyshire's version of the Dr. Who theme and melds it with Gary Glitter's Rock and Roll Part 2." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjQiZqpmQlE

## curiosities
**Building a Pyramid from Human Ashes** : The KLF, a distinctive music and art collective, is engaged in a project to construct a pyramid in Liverpool using the cremated remains of 34,592 deceased individuals. People can have their ashes formed into a brick to be added to this structure, with a reservation cost of £99. "The KLF, the weird music and art collective, is building a pyramid out of thirty four thousand five hundred ninety two dead people." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HoBx6s3JmVQ
**Copyright Liberation Front Philosophy** : The KLF, also known as "The Timelords" for specific projects, adopted the moniker "Copyright Liberation Front," reflecting their stance on sampling and copyright, which sometimes led to issues with the availability of their music. "After all, KLF stands for Copyright Liberation Front." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjQiZqpmQlE
**People's Pyramid Project (2018)** : The KLF conceived of building a pyramid constructed from the remains of 34,592 deceased individuals. Construction on this "People's Pyramid" began on November 23rd, 2018, in the Liverpool area, with individuals able to reserve a spot for 99 pounds. "came up with the idea of building a pyramid made of the remains of 34,592 dead people." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=72Lei7uug8c
**Public Money Burning (1994)** : The KLF is known as one of the most subversive experimental bands in British music history, notably for publicly burning one million pounds in cash in 1994. "Remember that this is a band that burned a million pounds in cash in public" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=72Lei7uug8c
