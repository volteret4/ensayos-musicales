## curiosities
**Admiration for Mark Linkous** : Metric was noted as an admirer of Mark Linkous, the songwriter and frontman for Sparklehorse, highlighting his respected status among his peers in the music industry. "He had fans of the flaming lips, and metric of the Silver Sun pickups, and Patty Smith and many others." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SOAL0GODN3M
**Beneficiary of Radio Star Maker** : Metric's career was furthered by the funding available from the new Radio Star Maker program, introduced in 2000. This program, along with Factor, was instrumental in supporting emerging and established Canadian talent. "Soon this cash was instrumental in helping further the careers of metric, broken social scene, Alexis on fire, city and color, and many more." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6PGgNCaW4xo
**Emily Haines's Entrepreneurial Ventures** : Emily Haines has collaborated with a clothing company to create bespoke designs and has also launched her own fragrance called Siren. "She's worked with a clothing company to come up with bespoke designs, and she has her own fragrance called Siren." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=11UsTJQyQds
**Emily Haines's Philanthropic Work** : Emily Haines is actively involved with "Fleet for Hear Me," an organization that supports children's music education programs in schools. She is also part of the Global Citizen Group, specifically its "She Decides" division, which focuses on women's issues. "She works with an organization called Fleet for Hear Me, which works with children's music education programs and schools. She's part of the Global Citizen Group, specifically with a woman's issues division called She Decides." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=11UsTJQyQds
**Founding Members' Independence** : Emily Haines and James Shaw have consistently prioritized Metric's independence, choosing to operate outside the major label system and manage their affairs internally. This approach has proven highly successful and made them influential in the 21st century for maintaining creative and financial control. "They've insisted that Metric be as independent as possible, and they've done very well working outside the major label system and running things themselves." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=11UsTJQyQds
**Indie Rock Revival (2000s)** : Metric is mentioned as one of the "fresh sounding bands" that emerged during the Indie Rock Revival in the early 2000s. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFLMkvJAaUY
**Polaris Music Prize Nomination (2006)** : Metric was one of the bands nominated for the 2006 Polaris Music Prize, an award aimed at recognizing artistic merit over commercial success, though the prize was ultimately awarded to Final Fantasy. "The winning album was... By Final Fantasy... which won out over records by Broken Social Scene, The New Prenogpers, and Metric." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZzIqIydZTlA
**Support for Other Female Artists** : Emily Haines and James Shaw have extended their influence by supporting other female artists. In 2017, they collectively produced the debut album for the band Beaches, a collaboration that helped Beaches establish themselves as a significant force in Canadian rock. "I should point out that Emily and James Shaw have paid it forward when it comes to helping out other female artists. They collectively produce the debut album for Beaches in 2017, which helped them become a real force in Canadian rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=11UsTJQyQds
