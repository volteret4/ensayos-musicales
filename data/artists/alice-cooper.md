## curiosities
**"Alice" Persona Description (1974)** : In a 1974 interview, Alice Cooper clarified that "Alice" is a dangerous persona he cannot embody constantly. He described the band as "all American kids" who reflect America's current attitude, acting as a social mirror that can be frightening to parents. He emphasized not taking a serious standpoint or preaching, opting instead for entertainment that incorporates "hard rock" with theater, asserting that the old "jam" style of rock groups from the 60s was "dead." "Alice is a, you know, I can't be Alice all the time because he's a little bit too dangerous." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 26 - Starmen - Glam Rock of the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/026-glam-11820-6-34-pm
**American Glam Rock Acceptability** : Alice Cooper's brand of glam rock made the genre acceptable for American audiences, distinguished from British glam by its "butch manly" presentation. David Bowie noted that Cooper's use of guillotines, fireworks, muscle, and metal offered a less ambiguous sexual bent that was more readily accepted in places like Ohio, contrasting with the "snippily" British approach. "Before an apt, we were bookended by Alice and Kiss butch manly glam with lots of guillotines and fireworks, muscle and metal." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 26 - Starmen - Glam Rock of the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/026-glam-11820-6-34-pm

The chicken was torn to pieces, leading to sensationalized rumors of animal sacrifice and the singer drinking its blood, which the band never denied on management's advice. "Vincent Fernier, the singer, believed that as God was his witness, chickens could fly. So he grabbed the chicken and threw it into the crowd.
