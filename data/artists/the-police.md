**Sting's Background and Nickname** : The singer and bass player, whose real name is Gordon Sumner, was a former school teacher. He earned the nickname "Sting" because he used to wear a striped shirt that made him look like a long, skinny bumblebee. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=95clNVd0hYg
**Sting's Musical Precision** : Sting knew what he was doing musically because he had already played in a string of jazz bands, which allowed him to translate that precision into the Police's sound. His collaboration with drummer Stewart Copeland, a fan of early 70s prog rock bands who had worked with Curved Air, also contributed to their sound. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=95clNVd0hYg
**Sting's Solo Career** : Sting went solo and established himself as someone capable of playing the most difficult sorts of music and even unusual instruments like the loot. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=95clNVd0hYg
**The Perfect New Wave Package** : Comprised of two Englishmen and an American, the band crafted short, tight pop songs infused with reggae and ska elements. Their sound was distinctly "not punk" but remained edgy enough to be classified as New Wave. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**Unique Sound (1977)** : When The Police first emerged in 1977, they sounded distinct from their contemporaries, characterized by a punky style tempered with pop and an unusual reggae influence for the time. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=95clNVd0hYg
