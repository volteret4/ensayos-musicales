## curiosities
**Album Art Designed by Storm Thorgerson** : The Police's album covers were among the many iconic designs created by the renowned Storm Thorgerson and his partner Aubrey Powell. Their work helped define the visual aesthetics of numerous influential artists. "That includes Pink Floyd, Black Sabbath, Led Zeppelin, ACDC, the police..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tbKuJR0cPs
**New Wave Package** : The Police, comprising two Englishmen and an American, were seen as a perfect New Wave package due to their short, tight pop songs infused with reggae and ska, their edgy yet non-punk sound, and their distinctive visual appeal, especially after bleaching their hair blonde for a Wrigley's Gum commercial. "They were another perfect new wave package." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**Radio Success** : The Police were one of the New Wave bands that successfully gained traction on pop and rock radio, contributing to the genre's broader appeal. "While some New Wave bands thrived on pop and rock radio, the pretenders, blondie, the cars, the police, among them, others struggled to find attention outside of their original circle of fans." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**Reunited by Arthur Fogel** : The Police were famously put back together by Arthur Fogel, the head of global touring for Live Nation. This achievement highlights Fogel's significant influence and power within the concert industry. "The man who put the police back together? That was Arthur Fogel of Hamilton." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM

**Sting – Former Jazz-Trained Teacher Who Fused Punk, Pop, and Reggae** : Sting, a former schoolteacher with a jazz background, fused punk, pop, and reggae in an innovative way that made him a pillar of the new wave movement and a key figure in the era of the music video. **Visual Branding for Wrigley's Gum** : To enhance their visual appeal for a TV commercial for Wrigley's Gum, all members of The Police bleached their hair blonde, showcasing the growing importance of image in the New Wave era.
