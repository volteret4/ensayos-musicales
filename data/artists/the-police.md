## songs
**Driven to Tears** : This song is cited as an example of a track featuring a drum kit recorded using the "Close Miking" technique, which was popular in the 1950s, 60s, and most of the 70s. This technique aimed to capture the pure sound of the stick striking the drum, resulting in a tight, sharp crack from the snare. "The police and driven to tears, that was a close mic drum kit." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TsiYLGGSJ2U
**Roxanne (1978)** : Released in 1978, "Roxanne" by The Police is highlighted as a New Wave classic, combining short, tight pop structures with elements of reggae and ska, distinguishing it from pure punk while maintaining an edgy feel. "Roxanne from the police, a new wave classic from 1978." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M

## curiosities
**Album Art Designed by Storm Thorgerson** : The Police's album covers were among the many iconic designs created by the renowned Storm Thorgerson and his partner Aubrey Powell. Their work helped define the visual aesthetics of numerous influential artists. **Career-Long Indie Status (Commercial Success)** : The Police were considered an indie band for their entire career because all their albums and singles were released on A&M Records, which was an independent label until its acquisition in 1989. They sold 75 million records for A&M, demonstrating that "indie" status is about label relationship, not commercial success. "Actually, since every single one of their albums and singles was on ANM Records, we can actually say that they were an indie band for their entire career." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
**Effective Solo Transition** : The group dynamic of The Police effectively transitioned into a solo singer situation at one point in their career. "a group might become a solo singer, the police or the pretenders effectively did that." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AcADOuQUiAQ
**Major Reunion (2007)** : The Police undertook a significant reunion tour in 2007, an event cited as a benchmark for comparison when assessing the scale and impact of other major band reunions, such as the upcoming Oasis tour. **New Wave Package** : The Police, comprising two Englishmen and an American, were seen as a perfect New Wave package due to their short, tight pop songs infused with reggae and ska, their edgy yet non-punk sound, and their distinctive visual appeal, especially after bleaching their hair blonde for a Wrigley's Gum commercial. "They were another perfect new wave package." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**Radio Success** : The Police were one of the New Wave bands that successfully gained traction on pop and rock radio, contributing to the genre's broader appeal. "While some New Wave bands thrived on pop and rock radio, the pretenders, blondie, the cars, the police, among them, others struggled to find attention outside of their original circle of fans." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**Reunion Tour for Record-Breaking Profit (2007-2008)** : The Police, a group historically known for internal disagreements, reunited for a tour in 2007 and 2008 due to an unprecedented financial offer from Live Nation's head of global touring, Arthur Fogel. The tour grossed $359 million, equivalent to $508 million in today's currency, showcasing the immense profitability of reunions driven by economic factors and fan nostalgia. "The police reunion tour of 2007 and 2008 grossed $359 million. That's $508 million in today's cash." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GYUuTbOvyak
**Reunited by Arthur Fogel** : The Police were famously put back together by Arthur Fogel, the head of global touring for Live Nation.
