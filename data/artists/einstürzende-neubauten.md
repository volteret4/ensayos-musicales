## curiosities
**Early Industrial Pioneers** : This German group's initial recordings are described as "wild stuff" within early industrial music, with the aim to showcase that the electric guitar could be used unconventionally or even rendered unnecessary. "Go back and listen to some of their first recordings by groups such as... Germany's Einstein-Dé Noybaden." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pLf3kUONewg
**Extreme Sound Experimentation** : The group was known for extreme sound experimentation. For example, their singer, Blixa, once had his chest wired with microphones while someone gave him a bearhug until his ribs cracked, and this sound was incorporated into one of their compositions. These extremes became a hallmark of their classroom music. "For example, Blixer, the singer, once wired his chest with microphones and had someone give him a barehug until his ribs cracked, and NoiButton used the sound in one of their compositions." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE
**Influence on Martin Gore (Depeche Mode)** : Martin Gore, the songwriter for Depeche Mode, listed Einstürzende Neubauten among the industrial bands he listened to in his spare time, indicating their significant influence on his musical development and later work with Depeche Mode. "And of course, Einstein's Andy Neubaden. This aggressive approach to music really appealed to Martin." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE
**Non-Traditional Instrumentation** : In their early days, Einstürzende Neubauten deliberately eschewed conventional instruments, melodies, or chord changes. Instead, they created music using objects like anvils, sledgehammers, broken glass, banging lead pipes, sheet metal, and power tools, aiming to sound like a "15-car pile-up on the floor of a steel plant." "In the beginning, NoiButton didn't even bother with instruments. These guys weren't into chord changes or melodies or anything like that. They made their music with anivals, sledgehammers, by breaking glass and banging-led pipes and sheet metal, and of course, power tools." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE
**Shift to Chord Changes by Mid-1980s** : By the mid-1980s, Einstürzende Neubauten had evolved their approach to music, notably beginning to incorporate chord changes into their compositions, marking a departure from their earlier, purely noise-based methodology. "Noe Bowden had discovered to use chord changes, and a new synthesis of styles was developing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE
