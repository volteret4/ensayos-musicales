# artist - The Offspring

## members
- Dexter Holland

## genres
- Pop Punk

## labels
- Columbia
- Epitaph Records
- Sony

## albums
**Americana (1998)** : Released November 1998; described as "full of pop punk fun"; includes songs like "Why Don't You Get A Job" and a cover of "Feelings"; sold over 10 million copies.
**Smash (1994)** : Released April 8, 1994; its release date was overshadowed by the news of Kurt Cobain's death; sold over 18 million copies, making it the biggest selling indie record of all time at that point.

## songs
**Feelings (1975) (Cover)** : An ironic cover of a 1975 ballad by Morris Albert, featured on Americana.
**Hidden Track on Americana** : The CD version of the Americana album includes a hidden track at the end.
**Pretty Fly (for a White Guy) (1998)** : The big single from the Americana album; tells the story of a hapless white kid trying to be cool, featuring a "zombie baby" lyric.
**Why Don't You Get A Job** : A song from the Americana album.

## curiosities
**"Selling Out" Accusations** : Accused by purists of "selling out" due to mainstream success.
**Contemporary Act** : Mentioned as a still-relevant pop punk band in the 2010s.
**Label Change** : Jumped from indie label Epitaph to Columbia, an imprint of Sony, one of the major labels.
**Mainstream Breakthrough** : Contributed to the "big 12 month period" in 1994 when pop punk broke through to the mainstream.
**Mastery of Pop Punk Attitude** : Particularly good at figuring out the fun, almost carefree attitude that defined pop punk.
**Publishing Catalog Sale (2016)** : In early 2016, The Offspring sold a chunk of their publishing catalog for $50 million, which helped resolve Dexter Holland's plane-related financial problems.
**Publishing Catalog Sale (Early 2016)** : The band sold a chunk of their publishing catalog for $50 million, which helped Dexter Holland resolve his outstanding debts related to his private aircraft.
