**Smash (1994) – Indie Breakthrough** : Released on April 8, 1994, coincidentally the same day Kurt Cobain was found dead, this was a fully independent album issued through Bad Religion's Epitaph label. Its timing was critical as grunge was waning and alt-rock audiences sought new sounds, with Cobain's death creating a creative vacuum that the album helped fill. Influenced by a revival in old school punk and Nirvana's melodic yet intense sound, "Smash" became part of the mid-90s "holy trinity of punk rock" alongside Green Day and Rancid. It is potentially the biggest-selling indie record of all time, achieving global sales of approximately 20 million copies. "Today it could be the biggest selling indie record of all time, with global sales somewhere around 20 million copies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Smash (April 11, 1994) – Released 3 Days After Kurt's Death; $20,000 Budget; 18 Million Copies; Biggest-Selling Indie Record Ever** : The Offspring released *Smash* just three days after the world learned of Kurt Cobain's death, on April 11, 1994. Recorded on a $20,000 budget in North Hollywood for Epitaph — the Orange County indie label co-founded by Bad Religion — it was expected to sell perhaps 15,000 copies if lucky. The timing was fortunate: Green Day's *Dookie* had already established that pop punk had an audience. *Smash* broke out via radio in Southern California and spread nationally and internationally, leaving Epitaph dazed as sales hit 500,000, then a million, two million, five million, ten million, and ultimately approximately 18 million worldwide — the biggest-selling independent rock record in history. "Would Green Day and the Offspring have become as big as they did had Nirvana continued?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4Jvy4q40tuo

**Anarchy Airlines – Dexter Holland's Private Jet Fleet, Solo Round-the-World Flight 2004** : *Smash* (1994) and subsequent Offspring albums generating more than 32 million in worldwide sales gave singer Dexter Holland the means to pursue his passion for aviation. He earned his first pilot's license in 1996, subsequently gaining more advanced certifications. He owns a Citation II twin-engine jet (approximately $2.5 million used), painted with the anarchy symbol on the tailfin and furnished with leopard-print interior — his own "Anarchy Airlines." He also flies an Aero Vodochody L-39 Czech/Russian jet fighter. He has enough simulator hours to pilot a 747. On November 25, 2004, Holland completed a secret solo circumnavigation: Los Angeles, New York, Iceland, Egypt, Dubai, Bangkok, Siberia, Russia, Alaska, and home — 11 days sustained almost entirely on beef jerky and Doritos, during which he encountered a volcanic eruption in Iceland, locusts in Egypt, and uncooperative customs officials in Russia. "Some people are into golf. Some people are into shooting deer. I'm into flying." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SQtCQX5O1PE
**Aviation Achievements and Financial Challenges** : Dexter Holland is rated to fly executive jets, having obtained his pilot's license in 1996. Although he once owned three planes, two were repossessed, and he faced a lawsuit over payment arrears for another. He resolved these debts by selling off some of The Offspring's publishing money, allowing him to fly freely again. "So in late 2004, he did it.
