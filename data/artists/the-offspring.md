**Smash (1994) – Biggest Selling Independent Record** : Released on April 15, 1994, on the independent Epitaph label, this album initially received little fanfare, gaining its first audience among skateboarders and snowboarders. Its trajectory changed significantly when a fluke led an LA radio station to play its first single, an uncommon occurrence for Epitaph bands, and the song then spread to other stations. Epitaph invested $5,000 for a video, which MTV subsequently aired, causing an explosion in sales. An album initially expected to sell 60,000 copies over its lifetime suddenly sold that amount every couple of days, ultimately selling over 12 million copies and becoming the biggest selling independent record of all time. **Smash (1994) – Indie Breakthrough** : Released on April 8, 1994, coincidentally the same day Kurt Cobain was found dead, this was a fully independent album issued through Bad Religion's Epitaph label. Its timing was critical as grunge was waning and alt-rock audiences sought new sounds, with Cobain's death creating a creative vacuum that the album helped fill. Influenced by a revival in old school punk and Nirvana's melodic yet intense sound, "Smash" became part of the mid-90s "holy trinity of punk rock" alongside Green Day and Rancid. It is potentially the biggest-selling indie record of all time, achieving global sales of approximately 20 million copies. "Today it could be the biggest selling indie record of all time, with global sales somewhere around 20 million copies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Smash (April 11, 1994) – Released 3 Days After Kurt's Death; $20,000 Budget; 18 Million Copies; Biggest-Selling Indie Record Ever** : The Offspring released *Smash* just three days after the world learned of Kurt Cobain's death, on April 11, 1994. Recorded on a $20,000 budget in North Hollywood for Epitaph — the Orange County indie label co-founded by Bad Religion — it was expected to sell perhaps 15,000 copies if lucky. The timing was fortunate: Green Day's *Dookie* had already established that pop punk had an audience. *Smash* broke out via radio in Southern California and spread nationally and internationally, leaving Epitaph dazed as sales hit 500,000, then a million, two million, five million, ten million, and ultimately approximately 18 million worldwide — the biggest-selling independent rock record in history. "Would Green Day and the Offspring have become as big as they did had Nirvana continued?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4Jvy4q40tuo

The song was also subject to a plagiarism case due to an Arabic-sounding passage, but a musicologist ruled it was not a copy of Agent Orange's "Bloodstains," despite sharing an Arabian scale basis.
