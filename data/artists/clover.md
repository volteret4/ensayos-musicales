# artist - Clover

## curiosities
**Backed Elvis Costello** : Members of this California band provided the backing for Elvis Costello on his 1977 debut album, *My Aim Is True*, as he had not yet formed his own backing band. **Backing Elvis Costello's Debut** : Clover, an American band residing in London, served as the backing band for Elvis Costello's debut album, "My Aim Is True." They were specifically hired by Stiff Records for the sessions, with their singer (Huey Lewis) being the only member not to participate, merely observing. "The real name of the Sham Rocks was actually Clover, and they were a bunch of Americans living in London who had some extra time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vKRKu7O_5HA ← clover ← clover
**Evolution into Huey Lewis and the News** : After recording with Elvis Costello, Clover returned to the United States and subsequently changed their name, becoming the well-known band Huey Lewis and the News. This means Costello's first backing band was essentially Huey Lewis and the News, without Huey himself on vocals for those sessions. **Post-Breakup Member Destinies** : After Clover broke up in 1978, its members pursued various paths. One individual joined The Doobie Brothers before eventually quitting music to become a chiropractor. Another member achieved recognition by writing the song "867-5309/Jenny" for Tommy Tutone. Additionally, two members, along with Clover's singer (who had merely hung around during the Elvis Costello sessions), went on to form the successful band Huey Lewis and the News. **Uncredited Backing Band for Elvis Costello** : Clover was an American band that found itself broke while touring the UK. They were hired by Jake Riviera of Stiff Records to back Elvis Costello on his debut album, My Aim Is True, recording through late 1976 and early 1977 in a tiny studio in Islington, London. Due to contractual issues (another British band was already named Clover), they used the pseudonym The Sham Rocks and were never credited on the original album release.
