# artist - Clover

## curiosities
**Post-Breakup Member Destinies** : After Clover broke up in 1978, its members pursued various paths. One individual joined The Doobie Brothers before eventually quitting music to become a chiropractor. Another member achieved recognition by writing the song "867-5309/Jenny" for Tommy Tutone. Additionally, two members, along with Clover's singer (who had merely hung around during the Elvis Costello sessions), went on to form the successful band Huey Lewis and the News. "Clover broke up in 1978. **Uncredited Backing Band for Elvis Costello** : Clover was an American band that found itself broke while touring the UK. They were hired by Jake Riviera of Stiff Records to back Elvis Costello on his debut album, My Aim Is True, recording through late 1976 and early 1977 in a tiny studio in Islington, London. Due to contractual issues (another British band was already named Clover), they used the pseudonym The Sham Rocks and were never credited on the original album release. "So Riviera found a band called Clover, who had come over from America, and were pretty much broke as they tried to or the UK." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=itK5fEqnA5E
