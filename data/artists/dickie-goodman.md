**Mr. Jaws (1975)** : Released on November 6, 1975, this song was Goodman's response to the popularity of the movie *Jaws*. He acted as a roving reporter interviewing Mr. Jaws and other characters from the movie, sampling 13 or 14 contemporary songs. It became a hit by the end of November 1975, reaching number four on the Billboard Hot 100, and was the number one novelty song of the year. The song samples the ominous *Jaws* theme, which was at number 44 on the charts when "Mr. Jaws" was released. ## curiosities
**Commercially Driven** : Goodman saw opportunities in popular cultural phenomena, like the movie *Jaws*, and quickly capitalized on them by creating novelty records. He was reportedly laughing and writing material for a new record while *Jaws* was terrifying people. **Parody and Copyright Precedent (1956)** : Dickie Goodman was taken to court for copyright infringement for his 1956 novelty song "Flying Saucer," but a judge ruled it was parody and therefore a brand new composition, allowing him to continue having hits using this method until 1975. "Goodman was taken to court for copyright infringement, but he was left off the hook when a judge declared that this was parody and therefore a brand new composition." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dnQ1pNbIUjw ← dickie-goodman ← dickie-goodman
**Pioneer of the Break-in Record** : Dickie Goodman is considered a pioneer of the "break-in" record format, which involves mixing snippets of current songs and news reports into a narrative. This technique, though technically challenging in the 1950s and 70s, defined his novelty song style.
