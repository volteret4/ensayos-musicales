## songs
**Somewhere There's An Angel (2007)** : This song, released in 2007 by Vancouver-based performer Dan O'Donnell under his professional name The Thirst in Revival, was issued as 100 vinyl copies with a retail price of $200 each. The song was inspired by Arthur Miller's play "The Crucible" and took two years to write. ## curiosities
**Art Project Single Release** : Dan O'Donnell conceptualized the release of "Somewhere There's An Angel" as an art project, aiming to make a statement on the commodification and perceived devaluation of music. The project involved ten acclaimed British artists, each designing ten unique sleeves for the 100 vinyl copies, and the entire endeavor has been exhibited in galleries. "Dan looks at this as an art project. A statement on the commodification of music and how this kind of art has been devalued in the minds of the public." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zWNzlSxrj6s ← the-thirst-in-revival
**Highest-Priced Debut Single** : The Thirst in Revival, the professional name for Dan O'Donnell, released 100 vinyl copies of his debut single "Somewhere There's An Angel" in 2007, each priced at $200. This is considered, as far as anyone can tell, the highest price ever set for a debut single. "this is the highest price ever put on a debut single." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zWNzlSxrj6s ← the-thirst-in-revival
**Inspiration from Arthur Miller** : The song "Somewhere There's An Angel" by The Thirst in Revival was directly inspired by the classic Arthur Miller play "The Crucible." The composition and writing process for this track took Dan O'Donnell two years to complete, reflecting a deep engagement with its thematic origins. "Dan says that this song was inspired by the classic Arthur Miller play The Crucible and it took him two years to write." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zWNzlSxrj6s ← the-thirst-in-revival
