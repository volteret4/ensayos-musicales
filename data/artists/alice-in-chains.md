**Dirt (1992)** : Alice in Chains' second album, *Dirt*, released in 1992, represented a full pivot to their grunge sound and image, having evolved from their earlier hair metal style. Given the album's dark themes, photographer Rocky Schenk aimed for a "hellish" and disturbing cover. The artwork features a nude woman, Mariah O'Brien, half-buried in what appears to be a cracked, dry desert floor, leaving her fate ambiguous. Layne Staley revealed his input for the cover was inspired by "a certain person who basically buried my ass so the woman on the cover is kind of the portrayal of that person getting sucked down into the dirt instead of me." The "desert" was actually a set built in Schenk's Hollywood studio using miniatures for mountains and foam core/clay for the ground. "So what we have is a cover featuring a nude woman half-barried in what looks like a cracked and dry desert floor." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sn5TWBFd0XM

## curiosities
**Band in Limbo (Post-Staley's Death)** : After Staley's death, Alice in Chains never formally broke up but entered a "land of limbo," essentially ceasing to exist due to the loss of their singer and the profound impact of his death on the members. "Alison Chains never really broke up, but with no singer and so many scars affecting so many members of the band, they really didn't exist either. So they were in this land of limbo." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yR3xc3ra5tE

"The original Allison Chains was more Van Halen and Motley Crude than Grunge. **Formation and Name Origin** : Layne Staley had been living with a homeless Jerry Cantrell at their rehearsal space when Sleeze, Staley's band, was eventually renamed Alice in Chains. After Staley's other band (a funk outfit) and Cantrell's separate project both broke up, Cantrell and Sean Kinney asked Staley to be the vocalist for their new group, adopting the name of Staley's former band, Alice in Chains, for the new project. "Sleeze was eventually renamed Alice in Chains, while Lane was living with a homeless Jerry Cantrell at their rehearsal space." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vLZla5h4xKQ
**Founding Members and Early Lineup** : Guitarist Jerry Cantrell and drummer Sean Kinney were the founders of Alice in Chains. Layne Staley was not a founder; he was drafted in early from a glam band called Sleeze, for whom he played drums. "That was guitarist Jerry Cantrell and drummer Sean Kinney. **Layne Staley's Decline and Isolation (Late Original Existence)** : In the band's final years of original existence, singer Layne Staley became a "super hard core junkie," largely confined to his condo, painting, playing video games, and injecting smack. He rarely ventured out, had drugs delivered, and lost teeth due to malnutrition. "And this is because senior Lane Staley had turned into such a super hard court junkie, and he wasn't able to do much of anything except staying his condo and paint and play video games and shoot smack into his veins." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yR3xc3ra5tE
**Layne Staley's Drug Addiction and Decline** : Around 1995, Layne Staley's drug habit spiraled out of control, leading to his virtual disappearance from 1996 onward. His last performance with the band was on July 3, 1996, in Kansas City, during a tour with KISS. He became a severe addict, barricading himself in his Seattle condo, where he painted, played video games, and had drugs delivered, leaving the band paralyzed. "Lane turned into an irreparable junkie, barricading himself in his Seattle condo where he painted, played video games, and had all his drugs delivered to the door." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vLZla5h4xKQ
**Layne Staley's Vocal Style** : Layne Staley's voice and his harmonies with Jerry Cantrell were central to the band's sound, considered the best hard rock harmonies since David Lee Roth and Michael Anthony in Van Halen's early days. Their vocal arrangements often featured dual lead vocals, strategically overlapped, with the two voices typically separated by a major third. Staley, with the more powerful delivery, could transition from a menacing monotone to sharp, cutting explosions, and also had a great scream, appealing to both metal and alternative fans. "Technically, the two voices were separated by a major third with Lane having the more powerful of the two deliveries." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vLZla5h4xKQ
**Mariah O'Brien's Cover Shoot** : Mariah O'Brien, known for appearing on Spinal Tap's "Bitch School" single, posed for the *Dirt* album cover on June 14, 1992. She lay in the body-shaped hole in the studio's "desert" set for eight hours while photographer Rocky Schenk took hundreds of shots and adjusted lighting. O'Brien later recalled having to hold her urge to urinate during the lengthy session. It was clarified that O'Brien, not singer Layne Staley's then-girlfriend Demri Parrott, is the woman on the cover. Today, Mariah O'Brien works as an interior designer in Los Angeles. "Mariah had to lie in that hole for eight hours as Schenk took hundreds of shots, adjusting the lighting countless times." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sn5TWBFd0XM

**William DuVall Joins as Lead Vocalist** : Following a successful show in March 2006, where William DuVall (singer for Comes with the Fall) performed as a guest vocalist, the band considered him for a permanent role. They had known and respected DuVall for five or six years. Other candidates, like Vinny Dombrowski of Sponge, were considered but didn't fit, and Scott Weiland was deemed unsuitable due to his drug issues. DuVall was chosen because he sounded close to Layne Staley without being a clone. His integration was gradual, leading to a full commitment by late 2008. "One of the guest vocalists that evening was a guy named William Duval, the singer with a group called Comes with the Fall." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vLZla5h4xKQ
**William DuVall Joins as Singer** : William DuVall, then singer for the Atlanta band Come With The Fall, had met Jerry Cantrell around 2000, and his band later opened for and functioned as Cantrell's backing band on solo tours around 2002. Their developing friendship led to DuVall becoming the new permanent singer for Alice in Chains. "Everybody became friends, talked to him to having DuVall be a permanent new singer for Alison Chains. And it happened." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yR3xc3ra5tE
