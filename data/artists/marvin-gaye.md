## albums
**Let's Get It On (1973) - Erotic and Socially Aware** : Released in 1973, this album featured liner notes written by Marvin Gaye himself, which included a quote from T.S. Eliot: "birth and copulation and death." While undoubtedly about sex, the album is also described as being as "socially and politically aware" as his earlier "What's Going On." Sociology professor Michael Eric Dyson praised it as a brave album for a black man to create such erotic content, directly countering the black exploitation films of the era by celebrating black sexuality, eroticism, and love. It reflects a part of the sexual revolution that viewed sexual freedom as beautiful, natural, and potentially a "gift from God." **Killed by His Father** : Marvin Gaye was shot and killed by his father, Marvin Gaye Senior, on April 1, 1984. The incident occurred when Marvin Gaye attempted to intervene in a fight his father was having with his mother. His father, who may have been mentally impaired due to a brain tumor, was responsible for the fatal shooting. "About a year later, April 1, 1984, Marvin Gaye was shot by his father when he tried to intervene in a fight his father, Marvin Gaye's senior, was having with his wife, Marvin's mother." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q6dAX6HCjgw ← marvin-gaye
**Minister's Son and Spirituality** : Marvin Gaye was the son of a minister, a background that influenced the spiritual undertones often present in his music, even in his most sensual work. This spiritual dimension, for instance in lyrics like "I've been sanctified" from "Let's Get It On," provides depth to his exploration of sexuality.
