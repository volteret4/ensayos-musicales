## albums
**...And No One Else Wanted to Play (1985) – Edmonton Hardcore Pioneers, Credited with Influencing Skate Punk** : SNFU formed in Edmonton in 1981 as one of the first bands to influence what would become known as Skate Punk. Vocalist Mr. Chi Pig (Ken Chin), the second youngest of 12 children from one of the last families in Edmonton to get running water, was known as one of the most energetic and athletic front men in punk anywhere. Like D.O.A., they toured relentlessly across North America, building an audience through constant gigging. Billy Joe Armstrong of Green Day cited them as an influence. They were signed to Epitaph (the label that also launched Bad Religion and The Offspring) and produced eight studio albums, plus live albums, EPs, and compilations. Mr. Chi Pig was the only constant member through nearly three dozen different musicians over the band's history. He was diagnosed with a fatal medical condition in 2018, spent his last months recording solo material, and died on July 16, 2020 — after which plans for a mural and statue were immediately floated by Edmonton's punk community.
