**American Idiot (2004)** : This album, released in the fall, was conceived as a full-blown rock opera. The band had fallen out of favor with fans and was perceived as "old and tired," with talk of their potential demise; this rock opera was considered a last-ditch effort to revive their career. Instead of re-recording, they started from scratch, with each member contributing short fragments of music. **Cigarettes and Valentines (2002–2003) – Nearly Complete Album Stolen; Band Started Over, Created American Idiot** : By 2002, Green Day's career had stalled; their last proper album was years behind them. They recorded a follow-up called *Cigarettes and Valentines* that was almost finished when the master tapes disappeared — apparently stolen by persons unknown. Singer Billie Joe Armstrong insisted the album contained strong material; bassist Mike Dirnt acknowledged backup tapes existed but said they were inferior. The band started over entirely, creating *American Idiot*, which sold in the hundreds of millions and relaunched their career. The stolen album has never surfaced online or in bootleg form. **Dookie (1994) – Breakthrough Album** : Released on February 1, 1994, through Reprise Records, a label originally created by Frank Sinatra. The album initially sold slowly, moving only 9,000 copies in its first week. However, its sales trajectory changed significantly after Kurt Cobain's death two months later, as the music scene shifted away from the bleakness of grunge. By the summer of 1994, Dookie peaked at number 2 on the Billboard charts and achieved number one status in Canada, Australia, and New Zealand, also reaching the top 10 in several other countries. It has since sold at least 20 million units. "By the summer of 1994, that Duckey album peaked at number 2 in the Billboard charts, and was the number one hit in Canada, Australia, and New Zealand." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Dookie (1994) – Breakthrough Major Label Album** : Released on February 1, 1994, this album achieved immense success, staying on the charts for two years and selling more than 10 million copies. It served as the major entry point for most of the world to Green Day and was instrumental in paving the way for numerous pop-punk bands like Sum 41, Blink-182, Good Charlotte, and Simple Plan. Some observers referred to it as "the punk rock version of The Joshua Tree," while others derisively called it "Flukey." "On February 1, 1994, Green Day released a record called Duckey. They knew it was pretty good, but no one expected it to stay on the charts for two years and sell more than 10 million copies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nyfdGYWelic
**Dookie (February 1, 1994) – Only 9,000 Copies Shipped Initially; Exploded After Kurt Cobain's Death; 20 Million Worldwide** : Warner Bros. expected so little from Green Day's major label debut that only 9,000 copies were sent to stores on release day. The label's ceiling for success was 200,000 copies. The first single "Longview" had its MTV video debut on February 22; by the end of March the album was rising on the charts. When Kurt Cobain died on April 8, 1994, the shock created a vacuum in the alt-rock world — and *Dookie*'s punk energy helped fill it. By year's end it had gone multi-platinum; it was later certified Diamond (10 million in the US alone); worldwide sales reached approximately 20 million. It set off the mid-1990s punk pop revival, influencing the Offspring, Rancid, Sum 41, Blink-182, Fall Out Boy, and others. *Dookie* was also the first album Lady Gaga ever bought with her own money. Time Magazine ranked its influence alongside *Nevermind*. "This record acted as something of a bomb to the hearts broken by the end of Nirvana." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4Jvy4q40tuo
