## albums
**American Idiot** : The CD edition of this album was identified as an "audio atrocity" due to the negative effects of the "loudness wars," a mastering trend that involved heavy compression and diminished the music's dynamic range. "A lot of the Foo Fighters catalog, and American Idiot by Green Day." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZzIqIydZTlA
**American Idiot (2004) – Rock Opera Success** : Released on September 21, 2004, this album, built from song snippets, became a highly successful rock opera. It sold 14 million copies, won Grammys, produced a DVD and live album, and a spin-off musical, cementing the band's renaissance and riding its success for over two years. "Green Day, a band whose time had come again thanks to the revival of rock that began in 2003, and they would ride the success of American Idiot for more than two years after its release on September 21, 2004." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**American Idiot (2005)** : This album sold 3.4 million copies in 2005, making it the biggest selling rock record of that year. Its success coincided with what was considered the peak year for rock music according to the rock versus pop cycle theory. "That was Green Day's American Idiot sold 3.4 million copies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-CT7yYXonIk
**Cigarettes and Valentines (Unreleased)** : An album the band started working on, but it was "either stolen or wiped out in a hard drive crash," forcing them to start over with new material. "Now legend has it that they started on a record called cigarettes and valentines that was either stolen or wiped out in a hard drive crash..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Dookie (Year Unspecified)** : An earlier album from the band's career, remembered by their older audience, but not by the younger crowd at Blink-182 concerts. ## curiosities
**Admonishing Cell Phone Usage at Gigs** : Green Day is noted for actively confronting cell phone-obsessed fans from the stage, demanding that they remain "in the moment" and engage directly with the live performance rather than through their phone screens. This represents one of the stricter reactions artists have taken against the prevalence of mobile devices at concerts. "Groups like Green Day are always admonishing cell phone obsessed fans from the stage demanding that they be in the moment." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xid8CZf4oYo
**Greatest Hits Album (Late 1990s)** : Their label released a greatest hits album around the same time they opened for Blink-182, which, along with the tour reception, "gave the band the will to give it another good shot." "The success of a greatest to tell when put up by their label at the same time gave the band the will to give it another good shot." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Longevity Beyond the Seven-Year Cycle** : Green Day stands out as an exception to the general rule of a seven-year band lifespan, having sustained their career through multiple such periods, showcasing their enduring appeal and stability. "Groups that have survived multiple seven-year cycles. You too, the stones, the oasis, green day, food fighters." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Miscategorization as Emo** : Green Day, despite not being an Emo band, was sometimes wrongly categorized into the Emo scene during its peak. This highlights the broad and sometimes inaccurate labeling of bands within the alt-rock universe. "Even bands that weren't Emo, like Green Day and Weezer, believe it or not, were wrongly lumped into this scene." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-CT7yYXonIk
**Near Breakup & Blink-182 Tour (Late 1990s)** : By the end of the 1990s, the band had "run out of gas and were actually on the verge of breaking up." They were saved by an opening slot on a Blink-182 tour and the simultaneous release of a greatest hits album. "The only thing that saved them was an opening slot on a Blink 182 tour." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Return of Bigger Older Acts Post 9/11** : Green Day was one of the bigger and older acts that experienced a return to heightened popularity in the period following 9/11 and the American invasions of Iraq and Afghanistan, contributing to the resurgence of rock music. "And a return of some bigger older acts, U2, 9-H nails, Green Day, Weezer." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Y6nHw5xx88
