**American Idiot (2004)** : Released in the fall of 2004, this album was designed as a full-blown rock opera. Its creation was accidental, occurring after the band mysteriously lost the master tapes for a planned record titled "Cigarettes and Valentines." They decided to start from scratch, with each band member contributing short musical fragments. These fragments were then strung together, and the band drew inspiration from The Who's rock operas and Andrew Lloyd Webber's Jesus Christ Superstar. The album tells the story of Jesus of Suburbia, a powerless anti-hero who despises his existence and the world, eventually leaving his town for the city. There, he encounters St. Jimmy, a punk rock kid, and "what's her name," a riot girl type. The libretto's interpretation suggests St. Jimmy commits suicide and that Jesus of Suburbia and St. Jimmy are ultimately the same person. The album sold approximately 10 million copies (CDs and DVDs), becoming the most successful alt-rock concept album ever and one of the most successful concept records of all time. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KyNY3zv6uc
