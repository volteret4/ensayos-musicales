## curiosities
**Breaking Gender Barriers – First Female Rock Drummer (Retrospectively)** : When Maureen Tucker joined The Velvet Underground in 1965, there were no known women drummers in rock music; playing drums was widely considered a "man's job." She therefore broke significant gender barriers, though this pioneering role was largely unnoticed at the time, possibly due to her unconventional drumming setup. Only in retrospect has she received due recognition as a musical pioneer. "Mo broke down some serious gender barriers But oddly enough no one really took any notice at the time probably because she wasn't setting upper drums properly anyway So only in retrospect that Mo got her due as a musical pioneer." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo ← maureen-tucker
**Inspiration for Style – Nigerian Drum Virtuoso** : Maureen Tucker stated that her distinctive drumming style and setup, which included playing while standing and using felt mallets, were inspired by a Nigerian drum virtuoso. "Mo says her style and setup was inspired by a Nigerian drum virtuoso." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo ← maureen-tucker
**Joining The Velvet Underground – Unique Circumstances** : Maureen Tucker was the last member to join The Velvet Underground. She was drafted into the band after their initial drummer quit and relocated to Nepal, where he later died of malnutrition. "She was drafted after the velvet's first drummer quit and moved to Nepal Where he would eventually die of male nutrition, but that's another story." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo ← maureen-tucker
**Unorthodox Drumming Style – Standing and Mallets** : Maureen Tucker, often known as Mo, had an "unorthodox" drumming style and setup. Instead of sitting behind a conventional drum kit, she stood up to play. She used felt mallets instead of traditional sticks and laid her bass drum on its side to play it with mallets, similar to a marching bass drum. She also "hardly used symbols." "Most style was On orthodox instead of sitting down behind a conventional kit she stood up and played everything with felt mallets instead of sticks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo ← maureen-tucker
