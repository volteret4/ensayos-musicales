**Death in Plane Crash (1959)** : The Big Bopper died in a plane crash on February 3, 1959, along with Buddy Holly and Richie Valens, during the "Winter Dance Party" tour. He had been suffering from the flu due to the miserable tour conditions and took Buddy Holly's chartered plane for comfort. "You're likely also to know that Richie Valens and the Big Bopper died in the same crash, even if you don't know any of the songs that either of those two artists recorded." ← Episode 74： ＂It Doesn't Matter Any More＂ by Buddy Holly | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6unib5T2mDQ
**Ill-fated Tour** : The Big Bopper performed on the penultimate date of an ill-fated tour, which was attended by a young Robert Zimmerman (Bob Dylan), alongside Buddy Holly and Richie Valens. "He was enough of a fan of Rock and Roll music that he went to see Buddy Holly, Richie Valens, and the Big Bopper, on the penultimate date of their ill-fated tour." ← Episode 97： ＂Song to Woody＂ by Bob Dylan | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3DufLVcZyc4
**Last-Minute Plane Passenger (1959)** : The Big Bopper was offered a seat on Buddy Holly's chartered plane to Fargo, North Dakota, given the harsh tour conditions and his illness, replacing Tommy Allsup or Waylon Jennings. **Plane Crash Death (1957)** : On February 3, 1957, a day famously known as "the day the music died," The Big Bopper was killed when the small plane he was a passenger in went down in a cornfield outside of Mason City, Iowa. He died alongside fellow musicians Buddy Holly and Richie Valens.
