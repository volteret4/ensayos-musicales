**Jump (1983)** : Released in December 1983 as the first single from the album *1984*, this song controversially featured Eddie Van Halen playing a keyboard synthesizer. Despite the departure from traditional heavy metal, he played the synthesizer with electric guitar power chords, which helped win over fans. Eddie Van Halen reportedly borrowed the synth riff from Daryl Hall of Hall & Oates, who gave his blessing. "Jump" was nominated for a Grammy for Best Rock Performance by a Duo or Group with Vocal but lost to Prince and The Revolution's "Purple Rain." It was included in the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame's list of 500 songs that shaped Rock and Roll, was ubiquitous in 1984-1985, and opened the door for synthesizers in rock bands, exposing Van Halen and metal to a wider audience.
