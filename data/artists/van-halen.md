## curiosities
**"Dinosaurs" of Late 80s Rock** : This band was emblematic of the "one long parade of classic rock bands and the second acts of their careers" that Generation X actively disliked at the beginning of the 90s, considering them "dinosaurs." "You know, lots of Van Halen and Erasmith and Leonard Skinner didn't know what John was. They were just dinosaurs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tFBSrKMEZxI
**Active Rock Radio Airplay** : This classic rock band was played on active rock radio stations in the 2010s as an homage to traditional rock values, alongside new material. "They also played the homage to guns and rouses, NACDC and Led Zeppelin and Van Halen and Ozzy and Sabbath and Death Lepred and Jimmy Hendrix." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Catalyst for In-Ear Monitor Invention** : Van Halen played a crucial role in the development of in-ear monitors. Their sound engineer, Jerry Harvey, invented the technology after drummer Alex Van Halen complained that the "constant on-stage volume was killing his hearing." "The inventor was a guy named Jerry Harvey, a sound engineer who worked with Van Halen." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qrrwqdpCjpQ
**Collaboration with Bruce Fairbairn** : Bruce Fairbairn produced mega-platinum albums for Van Halen throughout the 1980s and 1990s. "Throughout the 80s and 90s, he produced mega platinum albums for Erasmith and ACDC and Bon Jovi and Inaccess and Kiss and Leverboy and Poison, The Scorpions and Van Halen and a ton of others." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QkSKxm_U9Yc
**Contrast to No Wave** : Van Halen is mentioned as a contemporary contrast to the difficult-to-listen-to music of the No Wave scene in the late 1970s. "This was not Van Halen or even the clash." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pLf3kUONewg
**Covered by Aztec Camera** : The band's music was covered by Aztec Camera, who created their own "impression" of Van Halen's work. "They're doing their impression of Van Halen." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oPgeSCy93bo
**Hide Your Sheep Tour Rider (1982-1983) – Brown M&M's Clause** : The band's rider for their 97-show, 10-month tour across six countries in North and South America, included a legendary clause on page 36 (specifically page 40, under "munchies") that stated "absolutely no brown ones" among the M&Ms. This was not a serious demand but a test to ensure that promoters had thoroughly read the entire 50-plus page contract, as failure to do so often led to "fractious relations" due to overlooked logistical and technical requirements. "absolutely no brown ones." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z2hvUDtBxho
**Influence on Living Color** : Van Halen was one of the key inspirations for Living Color, influencing their unique funk metal sound. "They were inspired equally by James Brown, Funkadelic, Run DMC, Slion the Family Stone, and Led Zeppelin Van Halen, and the Clash." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Major Label Mainstream Artist** : Van Halen was presented as an example of a "big, famous band" on "big major labels." These artists embodied the mainstream rock paradigm, selling millions of records, concert tickets, and merchandise, and attaining global fame through widespread media exposure. "They signed bands like Led Zeppelin and the Stones and the Beatles and ACDC and Van Halen." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z8BTWo66SDI
**Soviet Blacklist - Anti-Soviet Propaganda** : Van Halen was included on the USSR's 1985 "not recommended music" list, with the explicit justification of "anti-Soviet propaganda." This classification underscored the Soviet authorities' view of their music as a direct ideological threat to the communist state. "Van Halen, anti-Soviet propaganda." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NyKvAhO7MHE

**Three Lead Singers – David Lee Roth, Sammy Hagar, Gary Cherone; Eddie Van Halen Died October 6, 2020** : Van Halen has had three lead singers: David Lee Roth (1973–1984), Sammy Hagar (1985–1996), Roth briefly in late 1996, Gary Cherone of Extreme (1996–1999), Hagar again (2003–2005), and Roth from 2006 onward. A reunion tour with Roth was being planned when Eddie Van Halen was diagnosed with cancer; he died on October 6, 2020. "And then Eddie Van Halen got sick with cancer and eventually died on October 6, 2020." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l0F22NTnQzQ
