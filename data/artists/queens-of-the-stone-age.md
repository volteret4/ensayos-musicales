**Songs for the Deaf (2002)** : Released in the summer of 2002, this album was conceived as a concept record, though its conceptual nature is subtle and often missed. It is intended to be a soundtrack for a drive from Los Angeles through the desert to Joshua Tree National Park, with the listener flipping through small-town radio stations along the way. The artwork on some versions of the album depicts the dashboard of a Fiat 124 spider, visually representing this journey. "This is supposed to be taken as a soundtrack of a drive from Los Angeles through to Joshua Tree National Park out in the desert, while flipping through small town radio stations along the way, hence the dashboard of a Fiat 124 spider shown in the artwork of some versions of the album." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KyNY3zv6uc
**Songs for the Death** : This album was recorded with Josh Homme, Nic Oliveri, and Mark Lanegan, and featured Dave Grohl as a full-time drummer who joined halfway through the sessions. The record incorporated interstitial bits with fake radio DJs to simulate listening to a car radio driving in the desert at night, where every station change would play a Queens of the Stone Age song. Despite the concept, the record label reportedly disliked the album from the demo stage. The title was inspired by the band's sound engineer, who played music at dance parties for deaf people, where attendees enjoyed feeling the bass by holding large balloons. "The audience loved it when he pumped up the bass, which they could really feel by holding big balloons." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BvhjsTUIS2o

**Band Naming Origin** : The name "Queens of the Stone Age" originated from producer Chris Goss, a member of Masters of Reality, who would jokingly refer to Kyuss as "Queens of the Stone Age" during studio sessions, suggesting they sounded like royal women of the Stone Age. Josh Homme found the phrase cool and funny, especially imagining it on a marquee or concert ticket, leading him to adopt it for his new project. "Whenever he was in the studio with the band, he would yell at Kias, calling them Queens of the Stone Age.
