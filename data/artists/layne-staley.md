## curiosities
**Band Breakup and Sean Kinney's Efforts (November 1997 - 2002)** : Alice in Chains officially broke up in November 1997 due to Layne Staley's inactivity as their frontman. Despite the breakup, drummer Sean Kinney regularly checked in on Staley, visiting his condo at least three times a week to ensure he was "okay," or as okay as circumstances allowed. Staley, however, was highly withdrawn, often ignoring calls and visitors at his door. "With nothing happening with their frontman, Allison Chains broke up in November 1997. Over the years that followed Lane deteriorated." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sP75_gbZzIU
**Condo Purchase and Isolation (April 1997)** : In April 1997, Layne Staley moved into a three-bedroom, 1500 square foot condo in Seattle's University District, purchased for $262,000 by the LaRusta Trust, part of the Alice in Chains Management Company. Staley used "John LaRusta" as an alias to keep his real name out of public records. He spent his time painting, watching TV, playing video games, and doing "lots and lots of drugs." Alice in Chains guitarist Jerry Cantrell installed a home recording outfit for him, which Staley used only occasionally, further highlighting his increasing isolation. "The purchaser was the LaRusta Trust, which was part of the Allison Chains Management Company. John LaRusta was an alias used by Lane. He did one his real name used in any public records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sP75_gbZzIU
**Death (April 5, 2002)** : Layne Staley died on or about April 5, 2002, a date notably eight years after Kurt Cobain's death. His death was discovered on April 19, 2002, after his accountants noticed no bank withdrawals for weeks. Seattle police found him on his couch with a heroin cooker and syringe nearby after forcing entry into his dead-bolted condo. "The best guess the corner could come up with is that Lane died on or about April 5. The same day on which Kurt Cobain had died eight years earlier." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2RKchFW42Jc
**Death by Overdose (April 5, 2002)** : Layne Staley's body was found on April 19, 2002, approximately two weeks after his death, which the medical examiner estimated occurred around April 5, 2002 – the anniversary of Kurt Cobain's death. The official cause of death was an overdose of a mix of heroin and cocaine. He weighed only 86 pounds at the time of discovery, with a syringe sticking out of his leg and another fully loaded syringe in his hand. The condo was littered with drugs, crackpipes, and a stash of coke. His death was not a surprise, as he had been a "drugged up mess" for years, with a Seattle music paper reportedly having an obituary ready for him in the mid-90s. "The official cause of death was an overdose of a mix of heroin and cocaine, but it was just a matter of time because his body was in such terrible, terrible shape." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sP75_gbZzIU

**Discovery of Death (April 19, 2002)** : Layne Staley's death was discovered on April 19, 2002, by Seattle police after his accountants alerted his mother and stepfather that there had been no withdrawals from his bank accounts for weeks. Police found him on his couch with drug paraphernalia. "Just after 5.30 in the afternoon of April 19, 2002, Seattle police kicked in the dead-bolted door of Lane's condo." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2RKchFW42Jc
**Final Days and Discovery (April 2002)** : In his final years, Layne Staley lived as a recluse, rarely leaving his condo except for occasional visits to a local bar called The Rainbow, where he would sit in a back corner, drink, and pass out. His extreme withdrawal meant that his death went unnoticed for approximately two weeks. Accountants managing his financial affairs noticed his bank account hadn't been touched, prompting concern. On April 19, his mother, accompanied by Seattle Police, broke down his door and found him dead on the couch. "He was so withdrawn and so much of a hermit that it wasn't until the accountants in charge of Lane's financial affairs noticed that his bank account had been touched for two weeks that they suspected that something might be wrong." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sP75_gbZzIU
