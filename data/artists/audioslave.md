## albums
**Audioslave (2002) – Debut Album** : Audioslave's 2002 debut album cover, designed by legendary artist Storm Thorgerson, depicts a golden, flame-like sculpture situated in a desolate landscape, accompanied by a solitary figure in a red shirt and shorts. Thorgerson described the music as "brooding and sultry" and explained the flame symbolizes "the eternal flame, or the flame of remembrance," embodying the band's previous incarnations, Soundgarden and Rage Against the Machine. The image is a real, posed photograph taken on the volcanic island of Lanzarote. "The cover was designed by Storm Thorgerson, probably the most famous album art creator of all time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tbKuJR0cPs
**Audioslave (2002) – Debut Album Release and Label Deal** : Audioslave's self-titled debut album was released in 2002. A complex label deal was worked out: the first album was released jointly by Epic (Rage Against the Machine's label) and Interscope (Chris Cornell's label). The second album was solely on Interscope, and the third was Interscope for the US and Epic for the rest of the world. "The first album would be released by both Epic, Rage Against the Machine's label, and Interscope, Chris's label." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lip0bZX7hBE

**"Civilian" Demos – Early, Unofficial Name** : Before Audioslave officially formed, 13 demos featuring Chris Cornell with the Rage Against the Machine instrumentalists surfaced online under the name "Civilian." This name was considered for the band but was ultimately found to be already taken. "It was then that 13 demos showed up online under the name civilian." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lip0bZX7hBE
**Authenticity of "Audioslave" Album Cover** : The cover artwork for Audioslave's 2002 debut album is notable for its authentic production, being a genuine, posed photograph captured in a real-world location on the volcanic island of Lanzarote, one of the Canary Islands. The design deliberately eschewed modern digital manipulation, meaning "there's no photoshop. There's no AI" involved in its creation. "Now what I love about covers like this is that there's no photoshop. There's no AI. This is a posed photograph in a real location." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tbKuJR0cPs
**Black Calivados – Chris Cornell's Parisian Restaurant** : Chris Cornell invested in and owned a restaurant in Paris named Black Calivados, which he opened around the time he fell in love with publicist Vicki Cariannis, daughter of a New York restaurant executive. The restaurant is described as "very hip, very expensive," with decor designed by the individual responsible for Victoria's Secret fashion shows. The menu offers "New American" cuisine, featuring dishes like Caramelized Quail, Truffled Mac and Cheese, and Peanut Butter, Soul, and Popcorn Fois Gras, with a burger priced at approximately 40 euros. "But modeling is a fickle thing, so Chris decided to sink money into a restaurant in Paris called Black Calivados." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WIFxAaReLn0

Producer Rick Rubin suggested Chris Cornell, leading to an instant creative connection and the rapid composition of 21 songs. "Rage Against the Machine had imploded... **Formation of the Band** : After Zach de la Rocha left Rage Against the Machine, the remaining instrumentalists (Tom Morello, Brad Wilk, Timmy C) considered finding a new singer for Rage but were ultimately connected with Chris Cornell by producer Rick Rubin. During their first rehearsals, the immediate chemistry led to the writing of 21 songs in 19 days, including "Exploder," "Light My Way," and "Bring 'Em Back Alive." The members were committed to forming a full band, not just a one-off project, aiming for their new work to be "the best thing" they had ever done. "The first time we got together, we just jammed together and we decided, well, let's just try writing some songs. And I think the first three days of rehearsal with Chris, we wrote, exploder, light my way, and bring them back alive." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t2uXHP9cBdM
