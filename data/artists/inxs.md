**Death (November 22–23, 1997) – Ritz Carlton Sydney, Belt, No Suicide Note; Coroner: Consistent with Auto-Erotic Asphyxiation** : The night before Michael Hutchence's death began normally: a dinner with his father and stepmother at a restaurant called the Flapper of India, where he seemed fine. He returned to room 524 at the Ritz Carlton Double Bay, Sydney. Ex-girlfriend Kim Wilson (inspiration for "Suicide Blonde") came to the hotel with her boyfriend; they drank daiquiris, champagne, and beer in the bar before going to Hutchence's room. They left around 4am. Over the following hours, Hutchence made multiple calls but reached only answering machines. Early that morning he went downstairs looking disheveled to mail some letters — the last time anyone saw him alive. A maid found his body around noon on Saturday, November 23. He appeared to have gone into a violent rage — prescription pills scattered across the room, a broken hand from hitting the wall, facial cuts, furniture thrown — before fastening a leather belt to the bathroom door and hanging himself. There was no suicide note. The official coroner's verdict was death consistent with auto-erotic asphyxiation, a practice that kills an estimated 500 to 1,000 people per year worldwide. Hutchence had acknowledged in a late interview trying to achieve "the ultimate sexual high," and his mother confirmed he had spent time with sadomasochists in the preceding months.
