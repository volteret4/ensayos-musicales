**Full Moon, Dirty Hearts (1993)** : Released in November 1993, this album experienced similar commercial struggles to *Welcome to Wherever You Are*. "Full moon dirty hearts which came out in November 1993 fared no better." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=23OgreyK66o
**Greatest Hits (1994)** : Michael Hutchence mentioned this album in a 1994 TV interview, explaining its release was due to the completion of a contract with an American record company and the band's desire to release it "properly" worldwide. "Now, Michael, you've got your greatest hits album out of them." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=23OgreyK66o
**INXS (1980)** : Their self-titled debut album, released in October 1980, featured the Australian hit "Just Keep Walking." "In October 1980, they released their first album, a self-titled record that featured an Australian hit called Just Keep Walking." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=23OgreyK66o
**Kick (1987)** : Released on October 12, 1987, this album was a monumental international success, reaching #1 in Australia, Canada, and New Zealand, #3 in the US and France, and a top 10 record in the UK. It sold an estimated "north of 20 million" copies, including a million in Canada (twice its sales in Australia). Five of its twelve songs became major singles. "Number one album in Australia, in Canada, and New Zealand. Number three in the US and France. And finally, a top 10 record in the UK. The best estimate of total sales is somewhere north of 20 million." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=23OgreyK66o
**Listen Like Thieves (1985)** : Released on October 14, 1985, this fifth album marked their true international breakthrough, fueled by a key song that had the working title "Funk Song No. 13." "It was called Listen Like Thebes, and it appeared on October 14, 1985. This was the international breakthrough they were looking for." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=23OgreyK66o
**Shabooh Shoobah (1982)** : Released on October 13, 1982, this album served as their international introduction, though it only did "okay" in that regard. "It came out on October 13th, 1982, and this was the big single." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=23OgreyK66o
**The Swing (1984)** : Their fourth album and second international release, produced by Nile Rodgers in New York. It was a massive success in Australia (one of the five biggest selling domestic albums at the time), Canada, Argentina, and France, and sold a million copies in the US. The UK remained "immune to InXS's charms." "The swing was a huge hit at home, where it became one of the five biggest selling domestic albums to that point, and was also hit in Canada and Argentina and France in a few other countries." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=23OgreyK66o
**Underneath the Colours (1981)** : Released in November 1981, this was their second album and was initially an Australia-only release. "That release was followed in November 1981 by a second Australia-only album and entitled Underneath the Colors." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=23OgreyK66o
**Welcome to Wherever You Are (1992)** : This album underperformed commercially, falling victim to the public's increasing interest in grunge and alternative music. "their 1992 album, Welcome to Wherever You Are, did not do as well as expected. A victim of the public's growing fascination with grunge and all things alternative." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=23OgreyK66o
**X (1990)** : Released after *Kick*, this album also sold "extremely well," but marked the beginning of the band's peak decline. "After kick came X in 1990. It also sold extremely well." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=23OgreyK66o

## curiosities
**20th Anniversary TV Special (1997)** : In November and December 1997, InXS was in Sydney filming a TV special for Australian television to commemorate their 20th anniversary, coinciding with local performance dates. "In excess was back home in Sydney, filming a TV special for Australian TV. Everybody was pumped up for this documentary commemorating the 20th anniversary of In Excess, which coincided with some dates at home in November and December." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=23OgreyK66o
**Album Sales** : The band sold close to 80 million albums, establishing them as the second biggest Australian success story, after AC/DC. "His band had sold somewhere near 80 million albums, putting them second only to, who, ACDC I guess, in terms of Australian success stories." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=23OgreyK66o
**Australian Pub Circuit Development** : The band honed their skills by playing at least 300 shows a year on the demanding Australian pub circuit, including mining towns and sheep stations, developing a distinctive "funky swagger." "It excess learned their chops the hard way on the grueling Australian pub circuit." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=23OgreyK66o
**Early International vs. Domestic Success** : By summer 1983, InXS was drawing crowds of 14,000 people in Australia, showcasing their domestic popularity. However, their first American performance in San Diego in March 1983, was attended by only 24 people, highlighting the disparity in their early international reach. "Their first American performance, which was the show in San Diego in March 1983, was in front of 24 people." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=23OgreyK66o
**First International Deal (1982)** : In 1982, InXS secured their first international record deal, marking a significant step toward global recognition. "The Big Break came in 1982 when in excess signed their first international deal." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=23OgreyK66o
**Kick Album Initial Rejection** : After recording *Kick*, InXS's record company in New York rejected it, deeming it "too funky, too dancing," and offered a million dollars for the band to start over. However, their manager bypassed the label by releasing it through college and alternative radio, leading to immediate success. "They took one listen and rejected it. Ah, too funky, too dancing, they said, look, here's a million dollars. Go back to Australia, start again." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=23OgreyK66o
**Name Change (1979)** : The group transitioned from "The Farriss Brothers" to "InXS" in 1979. "In 1979, the group changed their name. They became known as in excess." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=23OgreyK66o
**New Record Deal and Album (1997)** : At the start of 1997, InXS secured a new record deal and had an album in production, signaling their intent for a professional "fresh start." "By the beginning of 1997, in excess had a new record deal and a new album on the way. Professionally, the group was looking for a fresh start." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=23OgreyK66o
**Opening for Major Acts** : InXS toured extensively, frequently serving as an opening act for prominent bands such as The Go-Go's, The Kinks, and Hall & Oates. "The band was on the road almost constantly, usually opening for someone like the Go-Go's or the Kinks or Holland Oats." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=23OgreyK66o
**Peak Popularity (1987-1991)** : Between 1987 and 1991, InXS achieved massive global success, with millions in album sales, widespread music and video presence, and hugely profitable concert tours. A show in Mexico City grossed over $1 million, a Wembley Stadium concert in London grossed over $3 million, and over 100,000 fans attended a show in Rio de Janeiro. Some critics even considered U2 their only rivals worldwide. "Between 1987 and 1991, Inexess was everywhere." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=23OgreyK66o
**Post-Hutchence Continuation** : After Michael Hutchence's death, the band took a year-long hiatus before continuing with guest singers. They later participated in the reality TV series *Rock Star*, where Canadian singer JD Fortune was chosen as their new frontman. Fortune sang on one album, was fired, but then rehired a few years later. "In excess, tried to continue after taking a year off after Michael died. There was a period when they used guest singers before signing on to do a reality TV series called Rockstar that ultimately resulted in JD Fortune, a Canadian singer, getting the gig." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=23OgreyK66o
