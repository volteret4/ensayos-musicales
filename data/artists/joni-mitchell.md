## curiosities
**Astounding Player** : Joni Mitchell is described as an "astounding player in her day," indicating her high level of skill and impact as a musician. "an astounding player in her day." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oLC49nCf0ZM
**Canadian Songwriting Legacy** : Joni Mitchell was cited by Robert Plant as a key figure in Canada's "rich history of songwriting," alongside Neil Young and Leonard Cohen, during a moment that served as an indirect apology to The Trews. "Canada has always had such a rich history of songwriting and with Neil Young and Joni Mitchell and Leonard Cohen and the trues." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Flow to America** : Joni Mitchell was among the ambitious and talented Canadian musicians who flowed south to seek their fortune in America. This movement was characteristic of an era when Canadian artists often needed external validation, particularly from the United States, to gain full acceptance in their home country. "ambitious, talented Canadian musicians flowed south to seek their fortune in America." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6PGgNCaW4xo
**Mentioned as a Poet (not strictly rock)** : Joni Mitchell is considered a poet, though not strictly within the rock genre, similar to Leonard Cohen. "So, Joni Mitchell, yeah, but not exactly rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dnz_Ycd_q7s
**Musical Origin – Piano to Guitar at 20** : Joni Mitchell initially played the piano and did not acquire her first guitar until she was 20 years old. Many of her early songs were composed on a dulcimer. "Joni was a piano player, didn't buy her first guitar until she was 20." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hbNJQ6OYmd0
**Polio's Influence on Guitar Technique and Tunings** : A childhood bout with polio resulted in a weakened left hand, compelling Joni Mitchell to invent creative chord voicings. This led to her extensive use of unusual guitar tunings, reportedly employing at least 51 different tunings throughout her career. "And because about of polio left her with a weakened left hand, she had to be creative with how she formed chords and that led to all kinds of exotic tunings." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hbNJQ6OYmd0
**Pre-1970 Exodus to US** : Joni Mitchell was one of the significant Canadian artists who, prior to 1970, relocated to the United States to pursue a viable career in music, due to the underdeveloped Canadian music infrastructure. "Neil Young, Jody Mitchell, Paul Anke, Leonard Cohen, all of our biggest and most promising artists beat a path for the United States." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U2kYleeMnl8
**Unique Guitar Style – Melodic and Percussive** : Joni Mitchell's signature guitar style was characterized by a right-hand technique that combined melodic and percussive elements. Her playing incorporated unique tunings, unusual chords, harmonics, and percussive sounds, making her a distinctive voice among guitarists. "She also had a special way of stroking the strings with her right hand that could be both melodic and percussive at the same time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hbNJQ6OYmd0
