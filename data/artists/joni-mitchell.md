**Childhood Polio Infection** : Joni Mitchell contracted polio in 1951 when she was nine years old. The disease caused her spine to become twisted, and she was unable to walk. She spent weeks in the hospital and was bedridden for months afterward, but she pushed herself to walk again, motivated by not wanting to miss Christmas. "Joni Mitchell caught polio in 1951 when she was nine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u5NLt6mkmrk

**Impact of Polio on Music** : The polio infection left Joni Mitchell with some mobility problems in her left hand, which unexpectedly proved to be a creative advantage. This limitation compelled her to form chords in innovative and interesting ways, leading to her unique guitar technique. There are now entire websites dedicated to her distinct playing style, directly influenced by her polio. "The polio left her with some mobility problems in her left hand." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u5NLt6mkmrk

**Musical Origin – Piano to Guitar at 20** : Joni Mitchell initially played the piano and did not acquire her first guitar until she was 20 years old. **Polio's Influence on Guitar Technique and Tunings** : A childhood bout with polio resulted in a weakened left hand, compelling Joni Mitchell to invent creative chord voicings. This led to her extensive use of unusual guitar tunings, reportedly employing at least 51 different tunings throughout her career. "And because about of polio left her with a weakened left hand, she had to be creative with how she formed chords and that led to all kinds of exotic tunings." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hbNJQ6OYmd0
**Post-Polio Syndrome and Later Afflictions** : At age 51, decades after her initial recovery, Joni Mitchell experienced the resurgence of her polio symptoms in the form of post-polio syndrome, a condition affecting up to 40% of patients. She has also battled Morgellons disease, a rare condition characterized by fibers appearing under the skin, which nearly halted her recording and performing career multiple times. "At age 51, it all came roaring back as post polio syndrome, which effects up to 40% of patients' decades after they thought they'd recovered." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u5NLt6mkmrk
**Pre-1970 Exodus to US** : Joni Mitchell was one of the significant Canadian artists who, prior to 1970, relocated to the United States to pursue a viable career in music, due to the underdeveloped Canadian music infrastructure. "Neil Young, Jody Mitchell, Paul Anke, Leonard Cohen, all of our biggest and most promising artists beat a path for the United States." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U2kYleeMnl8
**Unique Guitar Style – Melodic and Percussive** : Joni Mitchell's signature guitar style was characterized by a right-hand technique that combined melodic and percussive elements. Her playing incorporated unique tunings, unusual chords, harmonics, and percussive sounds, making her a distinctive voice among guitarists. "She also had a special way of stroking the strings with her right hand that could be both melodic and percussive at the same time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hbNJQ6OYmd0
