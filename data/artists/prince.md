# artist - Prince

## albums
**Graffiti Bridge (1990)** : The cover of Prince's 1990 album "Graffiti Bridge" features a scientific photograph of subatomic particles in a bubble chamber. This identical image was also later used for The Strokes' North American "Is This It" cover and appeared on the cover of theoretical physicist Freeman Dyson's book, "The Scientist as Rebel." "It's been used in other places too, including on Princess 1990 graffiti bridge and on the cover of a book called The Scientist as Rebel by theoretical physicist Freeman Dyson." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tbKuJR0cPs

## songs
**Purple Rain** : This song was featured in a Spotify playlist curated by Queen Mary University in London, comprising 700 songs identified as most likely to generate chills or goosebumps in listeners. Studies found that sadder, slower, less intense, and more instrumental songs were particularly effective in producing this physiological reaction. "Prince's Purple Rain." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mTbagQE7akQ

## curiosities
**Backmasking Use (70s/80s)** : Prince was among the artists who incorporated backmasking effects into his music during the 1970s and 1980s, a time when the practice was most prevalent. "Tons of artists used back masking effects in the 70s and 80s. That seems to be when things reached their peak. Iron Maiden, Pink Floyd, Sticks, Prince B-52s, Weird Al, they were all into it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_YjwRApoJhA
**Backstage Conduct Policy – No Eye Contact or Conversation** : Prince reportedly had a strict backstage policy where no crew members were allowed to talk to him or even look at him. There were instances of people being fired for violating this rule, although contrasting anecdotal evidence suggested he could also be a nice, normal guy. "no crew members were allowed to talk to him or even look at him." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z2hvUDtBxho
**Booked Creed for Private Party** : Prince once booked the band Creed to perform at a private party. This arrangement was made with the intention of evoking high school memories for those in attendance. "Prince booked Creed for a private party. The city wanted to bring back memories of high school." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HoBx6s3JmVQ
**Exceptional Ping Pong Player** : Prince was notably enthusiastic about ping pong and was highly skilled, capable of defeating almost anyone he played against. "Prince was really into ping pong. He could destroy just about anyone." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xc85sLj0Cnk
**Fidget Spinners as Merch (2017)** : In 2017, merchandise featuring branded fidget spinners was sold under Prince's name or estate. This was part of a broader trend where many artists offered fidget spinners as a popular merch item. "Everybody sold branded fidget spinners, arcade fire, David Bowie, Prince Black Flag, the misfits, Nirvana, Queens of the Stone Age, followed boy, weird." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aSk1XQHNkd8
**Last Public Sighting (Record Store Day 2016)** : One of Prince's final public appearances occurred on Record Store Day in 2016, when he was spotted at a store called The Electric Fetus. Tragically, he passed away five days later. "One of the final public settings of prints happened on record store day 2016 when he was spotted at a place called the Electric Fetus." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
**Prince Studio Outtakes – Especially Valued by Fans for Creative Insight** : Among the most prized bootleg material for Prince's fanbase are his studio outtakes, which provide glimpses of the extensive unreleased recordings he was known to keep in his vault. "Studio outtakes are among the most valued by fans, offering an intimate view of the creative process." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Uly2cT2vc28
**Support for Clyde Stubblefield** : Prince was a huge fan of drummer Clyde Stubblefield and provided significant financial support for Stubblefield's healthcare costs, paying "tens of thousands of dollars" when Stubblefield suffered from cancer and kidney disease. This act of generosity highlights the esteem in which Stubblefield was held by other musicians. "When he came down with cancer and kidney disease, Prince was a huge fan, paid for tens of thousands of dollars of Clyde's health care costs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VnXBJqKwkrw
