## songs
**The Night They Drove Old Dixie Down (1969)** : This song was released in September 1969 by the mostly Canadian band. The lead vocals were handled by Levon Helm, the only American member of the band, who was from Arkansas and possessed a "wonderfully gritty and gravely voice." The lyrics are offered from the point of view of a defeated southerner named Virgil Caine, reflecting the pain of the Civil War coming to an end in 1865, and surveying the destruction around him as a total loss. The song contains a play on words with "raise a cane." "His voice reflects the pain in the lyrics which are offered from the point of view of a defeated southerner named Virgil Cain." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 3 - Nostalgia, Race, and Rebels in 70s Southern Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/03-bittersweet-home-alabama
**The Night They Drove Old Dixie Down (September 1969)** : This song was released in September 1969, featuring lead vocals by Leavon Helm, the band's only American member from Arkansas. Helm's gritty, gravelly voice perfectly conveys the pain of the lyrics, which are sung from the perspective of a defeated white southerner reflecting on the end of the Civil War in 1865. The narrator, Virgil Cane, laments the destruction around him but resolves to continue working the land. The song reflects a sense of pain and loss, mirroring the feelings of many white southerners in the early 1970s who found their changing landscape unrecognizable. "The night they drove Old Dixie down, you know, that's a song of pain." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 3 - Nostalgia, Race, and Rebels in 70s Southern Rock (Encore) | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-3-nostalgia-race-and-rebels-in-70s-southern-rock-encore
**The Weight (1968)** : Written by Robbie Robertson for the album `Music From Big Pink`, this song features cascading lines of melody combined with "mock serious lyrics" that bring an Old Testament character face-to-face with a 1970 rock musician. It was notably heard in the movie `Easy Rider` and performed live during the 1970 Festival Express, with Arkansas native Levon Helm on lead vocals.
