## curiosities
**90s Alt-Rock Discovery** : Lush is listed as one of many bands from the 1990s that older listeners (e.g., in their mid-40s) who loved grunge as teenagers might discover as "new to them" music today. This suggests they were part of the broader alternative rock scene that went unnoticed by some at the time. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m7MbV-eCLyE
**Female-Led Band Dynamics** : Lush, a London-based band composed of two men and two women, was notable for its "sexual equality," with the women, Miki Berenyi and Emma Anderson, largely "in charge" and responsible for writing "pretty much all the songs." This balance of female vocals with male guitar aggression was a notable aspect of the shoegaze scene. "Mickey Burrani and Emma Anderson, they were out front and wrote pretty much all the songs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vEHdoUlGurY
**Lollapalooza 1992 Appearance** : Lush, described as an "up and coming shoot-ass band from the UK," was the first band on the main stage lineup for the second Lollapalooza tour, which began on July 18, 1992. "The first band was lush, an up and coming shoot-ass band from the UK." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Outskirts of Britpop** : Lush was identified as one of the "shoe-gazer bands" circling the outskirts of the Britpop universe, indicating their concurrent but distinct musical style during the era. "Circling the outskirts of this universe were shoe-gazer bands like My Bloody Valentine and Ride and Lush and Swerve Driver and Slow Dive." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DSvoppf1dGY
**Post-Band Careers** : After Lush disbanded, singer Miki Berenyi became a production editor for British music magazines. Co-singer Emma Anderson transitioned into music business management, while bassist Phil King, after a stint playing bass in The Jesus and Mary Chain, began working in music media as a picture researcher. "Singer Mickey Burrani ended up as a production editor to a couple of British music magazines, and co-singer Emma Anderson works in music business management. And after a stint playing bass in the Jesus and Marychain, bassist Phil King works in music media as a picture researcher." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vEHdoUlGurY
**Shoegaze Band Example** : Lush was mentioned as an example of a Shoegaze band, a genre that despite being pushed aside by Grunge in the early 90s, never truly went away and whose influences can still be felt in later bands. "There were bands like Slow Dive, Chapter House, Curve, Lush, My Bloody Valentine, Adorable, The Catherine Wheel." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Tragic End and Aftermath** : The band continued until 1996, at which point drummer Chris Acland committed suicide. Following this tragedy, the remaining members attempted to carry on but ultimately felt they "just couldn't," leading to the band's dissolution. "They lasted until 1996 when drummer Chris Acklin committed suicide. The band tried to carry on, but they felt they just couldn't." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vEHdoUlGurY
