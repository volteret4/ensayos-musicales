## albums
**Go Girl Crazy (1975)** : This album features a famous picture inside its CD booklet that shows the band at a White Castle hamburger stand, wearing black leather jackets. This image, reminiscent of 1950s troublemakers like Marlon Brando and James Dean, inspired the authors of "Punk" fanzine to adopt the title, thereby linking the band to the very origin of the term "punk rock" for a new form of music. "This was on the inside of their 1975 album Go Girl Crazy. It's a shot of them at a white castle hamburger stand wearing black leather jackets." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4
**Go Girl Crazy (N/A) - Album Artwork Inspiration** : The artwork for this album, featuring the band and their friends inside a White Castle hamburger stand, all wearing old-style leather motorcycle jackets, inspired Legs McNeil and his buddy when they were deciding on a name for their fanzine. The band's appearance, described as looking like "wise guy punks from the 1950s" akin to Fonzy or Marlon Brando in "The Wild One," led directly to the fanzine being called "Punk." "And the artwork contains a shot of the band and their buddies inside a white castle hamburger stand." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1y2UuU-me_s
**The Dictators Go Girl Crazy (1975)** : Released in 1975, this album featured artwork depicting all four band members in leather jackets at a hamburger stand. This visual proved pivotal as it inspired fan Legs McNeill to rename his fanzine "Punk," thereby directly linking the band's image to the emerging genre. "The album that featured a picture of all four members in leather jackets, which inspired fan Legs McNeil to call his fan scene about this music, punk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z8BTWo66SDI

## curiosities
**Inspiration for "Punk" Fanzine Name** : The Dictators were a band from Manhattan's "scusy scene" whose album art for "The Dictators Go Girl Crazy" (1975) showed all four members in leather jackets at a hamburger stand. This image led fan Legs McNeill to exclaim that they looked like a "bunch of... punks," prompting him to rename his fanzine to "Punk," thus solidifying the term for the genre. "Inside was a shot of all four members of the band wearing leather jackets at a hamburger stand. And legs thought they all look like a bunch of... punks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z8BTWo66SDI
**Inspiration for "Punk" Magazine Name** : The Dictators' 1975 album "Go Girl Crazy" featured an inside picture of the band at a White Castle, dressed in black leather jackets, evoking the image of 1950s "punks" like Marlon Brando and James Dean. This image directly inspired the fanzine's authors to name their publication "Punk," thus making The Dictators historically significant as the band who inspired the word used to describe a new form of stripped-down rock and roll. "Instead, they got inspired by a picture of another New York band called The Dictators... And the Dictators? Well, they've gone down in history as the band who inspired the word, to describe a brand new form of stripped down rock and roll." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4
**Inspiration for Punk Magazine** : The Dictators were the favorite band of Legs McNeil in the mid-1970s New York underground music scene. His desire to find an excuse to spend time with them led him to conceive of starting a fanzine, which he hoped would provide a legitimate reason to meet and interview the band, or perhaps gain free entry to shows and drinks. "His favorite band of them all was the dictators, and he wanted to come up with an excuse to hang out with them." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1y2UuU-me_s
**Performance During CBGB's Final Weeks** : The Dictators were among the bands that performed at CBGB during its final weeks before the club closed down in October 2006. "The final weeks featured bands like Bad Brains, The Dictators, and even Blondie." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Epz5Sn151bk
