## albums
**Hey Now Hey (1973)** : An album produced by Quincy Jones for the "Queen of Soul," Aretha Franklin. This album spawned her number one R&B and Top 20 Pop hit "Angel." "And produced the 1973 album Hey Now Hey, for Queen of Soul, Aritha Franklin, which spawned her number 1 R&B Top 20 Pop Hit Angel." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > I Wanna Rock with Q. **Sparkle (Soundtrack) (1976)** : Released in 1976, this soundtrack was considered one of Aretha Franklin's best albums and marked a comeback, earning her one of only two gold records in the 1970s. She sang the songs for the soundtrack, despite the movie's star, Irene Cara, having wanted to record it herself. The album is praised for showcasing Franklin's impressive vocal range. "The Sparkle soundtrack, I think, is one of her best and it got her one of her only two gold records in the 1970s." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 47 - Dick Clark's Entertainment Empire | https://www.ftr70.com/

## curiosities
**Backed by The Sweet Inspirations** : The Sweet Inspirations, who later provided backing vocals for Elvis Presley, had previously backed Aretha Franklin. "the sweet inspirations, the girl group who had backed Aretha Franklin." ← Episode 38： ＂Heartbreak Hotel＂ by Elvis Presley | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ES35PYeAwfs
**Backstage Rider – Pre-Show Cash Demand** : In addition to canapés and ginger ale, Aretha Franklin's rider included a demand for $25,000 in cash, in addition to her usual performance fee, which had to be provided before she went on stage. She often kept this purse, containing the cash, on top of her piano in full view to keep an eye on it. "She demanded $25,000 in cash. This is in addition to her usual performance fee. And she had to have it before she went on stage." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z2hvUDtBxho ← aretha-franklin
**Commercial Peak and 1970s Comeback** : Aretha Franklin reached her commercial peak in the 1960s through her collaboration with Jerry Wexler, which helped her cultivate her deep southern soulful sound. While she remained relevant, the "Sparkle" soundtrack in 1976 was seen as a significant comeback in the 1970s, preceding a further lean into pop music in the 1980s that constituted another major comeback. "maybe she had maybe reached her commercial peak in the 1960s when she teamed up with Jerry Wexler and kind of found that really deep southern soulful sound." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 47 - Dick Clark's Entertainment Empire | https://www.ftr70.com/

**Impact on American Music and Dick Clark's Legacy** : Aretha Franklin proclaimed that a singer had not truly achieved success until they had appeared on American Bandstand, emphasizing the show's pivotal role in the music industry. She spoke of Dick Clark as a "very warm and classy and just an ageless person," praising his ease to work with and wide appeal among artists, industry professionals, and even parents. "If you didn't go on American bandstand, you just hadn't made it yet." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 47 - Dick Clark's Entertainment Empire | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Influence on The Spinners' Label Choice (Early 1970s)** : Aretha Franklin's success at Atlantic Records served as a key inspiration for The Spinners' decision to leave Motown and sign with Atlantic, as they believed that "if Atlantic records is good enough for Arita Franklin, it's good enough for us." "if Atlantic records is good enough for Arita Franklin, it's good enough for us." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 33 - Zaire '74, Muhammad Ali, and The Rumble in the Jungle | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-33-zaire-74-muhammad-ali-and-the-rumble-in-the-jungle
**Inspiration to Lamont Dozier** : Lamont Dozier was a junior high classmate of Aretha Franklin. Hearing her, at age 14, sing and play piano at church profoundly inspired him to pursue his own musical aspirations. "That 14-year-old Aretha Franklin, and as you can imagine, being classmates with someone who could perform like that caused the Motown Dozier to radically revise his ideas of what it was possible for him to do." ← Episode 111： ＂Heat Wave＂ by Martha and the Vandellas | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AAiUJhSKjzs
**Near Signing to Blue Note Records** : Aretha Franklin was close to signing with Blue Note Records. Don Was traveled to Detroit multiple times to work out a plan for a basic recording project centered around her piano playing, with Jerry Jemmott on bass and Bernard Purdie on drums. However, the project was derailed when Aretha fell ill. "We came this close to having her sign to Blu-Nob Really? Yeah, and I went to Detroit a couple times I'd known her And I said she got to play piano We were just going to do a thing like maybe have Jerry Jamat playing bass and Naruto playing drums And maybe someone playing organ or guitar So I'm keeping it real basic And everything would stem from her piano playing We're going to do it in Detroit so she didn't have to travel And she got sick Oh, so she was into it Yeah, she was totally into it." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Introducing Broken Record - Norah Jones Begins Again | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f1264fe6d21276e0cbc
**Norah Jones' Favorite Piano Player** : Norah Jones deeply admires Aretha Franklin's piano playing, considering her one of her all-time favorites for her distinctive "feel." She loved Aretha's piano contributions on recordings even before knowing it was Aretha playing. "It really reminds me of a wretha When she played piano Well, she's one of my favorite piano players." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Introducing Broken Record - Norah Jones Begins Again | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4f1264fe6d21276e0cbc
**Opportunity to Work With Tom Bell** : Upon joining Atlantic Records, Tom Bell had the chance to collaborate with Aretha Franklin but chose not to, believing that she, along with Donny Hathaway, were self-sufficient artists who did not require his production expertise. "He had the opportunity to work with Aretha Franklin or Donnie Hathaway when he joined Atlantic, but he figured that they did not need him." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 37 - Philly Soul and the Sound of the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-37-philly-soul-and-the-sound-of-the-70s
