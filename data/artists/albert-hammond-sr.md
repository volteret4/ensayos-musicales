# artist - Albert Hammond Sr.

## curiosities
**Involvement in Copyright Case** : Albert Hammond Sr. was one of the litigants in the copyright infringement trial against Radiohead concerning their song "Creep," which was found to be similar to his 1972 song "The Air That I Breathe" by The Hollies. Consequently, he now receives 25% of the writing credit for Radiohead's "Creep." "One of the litigants in this trial was Albert Hammond, the father of Albert Hammond Jr. of the Strokes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t-G22K0vLnM
**Prolific Songwriter** : Albert Hammond Sr. is a successful songwriter who wrote numerous songs for a wide array of prominent artists. His credits include songs for Celine Dion, Aretha Franklin, Whitney Houston, Willie Nelson, Chicago, and The Carpenters. He also received a writing credit on Radiohead's song "Creep" due to its melodic resemblance to "The Air That I Breathe," a song he wrote for The Hollies in the 1970s, as determined by a court case.
