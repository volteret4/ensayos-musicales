## songs
**Jesus Is Just Alright (1972)** : The Doobie Brothers covered this song in 1972, drawing inspiration from The Byrds' 1969 version rather than the 1966 original by Art Reynolds. Tom Johnston, a member of the band, stated that they had no intention of recording a gospel song, viewing themselves simply as "musicians outplaying a gig." The song entered the Top 40, peaking at number 35 in 1973. Live performances often attracted "one-wayers" who would point fingers skyward and throw scriptures onto the stage. "Jesus is just alright. **Unintentional Religious Following** : The Doobie Brothers' cover of "Jesus Is Just Alright" was not recorded with religious intent; band member Tom Johnston stated they were "just musicians outplaying a gig." However, the song's popularity attracted religious fans, known as "one-wayers," who would attend their shows, point their fingers skyward, and even throw scriptures onto the stage, much to the band's initial confusion.
