**Live Performance Concerns (1973)** : The Doobie Brothers, like other rock bands of the era, expressed concern about performing live on *The Midnight Special* without lip-syncing, worried about how their sound would translate. However, their performance "didn't sound so bad" and was part of the show's appeal in offering authentic live music. **Unintentional Religious Following** : The Doobie Brothers' cover of "Jesus Is Just Alright" was not recorded with religious intent; band member Tom Johnston stated they were "just musicians outplaying a gig." However, the song's popularity attracted religious fans, known as "one-wayers," who would attend their shows, point their fingers skyward, and even throw scriptures onto the stage, much to the band's initial confusion.
