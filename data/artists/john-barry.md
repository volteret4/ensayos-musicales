# artist - John Barry

## songs
**A View to a Kill (1985) – Orchestral Arrangement** : John Barry took the bones of the song created by Duran Duran for "A View to a Kill" and arranged it, using a 60-piece orchestra. His contribution helped shape the track into a classic Bond theme and one of Duran Duran's biggest hits, reaching number one on the American singles charts. "Durand Durand came up with the bones of the song and John Berry took it from there using a 60 piece orchestra." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K0pgJ7XAlpo ← john-barry
**From Russia with Love (1963) – Instrumental Title Theme** : John Barry created the instrumental title theme for the 1963 James Bond film "From Russia with Love." While an instrumental, it served as the opening music for the movie. Barry contributed significantly to the film's score. "From Russia with Love in 1963 had an instrumental as a title theme done by Barry." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K0pgJ7XAlpo ← john-barry
**Goldfinger (1964) – Co-writer** : John Barry had a hand in writing the theme song for the 1964 film "Goldfinger," collaborating with Anthony Newley. Sung by Shirley Bassey, this song became a top 40 hit, despite initial strong disapproval from producer Harry Saltzman. "John Barry had a hand in this as did Anthony Newley, a British song and danceman who was admired greatly by a young David Bowie." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K0pgJ7XAlpo ← john-barry
**James Bond Theme (1962) – Orchestral Arrangement** : After Monty Norman's initial composition was rejected by producers, John Barry was brought in to arrange the melody for an orchestra. His arrangement transformed the theme into the iconic classic that has been featured in every James Bond film. This version set the standard for the franchise's musical identity. "So they gave it to composer John Barry, who arranged it for orchestra and boom, there's your classic." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K0pgJ7XAlpo ← john-barry
**On Her Majesty's Secret Service (1969) – Instrumental Opening** : John Barry wrote a wordless composition for the opening sequence of the 1969 James Bond film "On Her Majesty's Secret Service." This instrumental piece set the tone for the film starring George Lazenby, who only played Bond for this one movie. "John Barry wrote something wordless for the opening sequence." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K0pgJ7XAlpo ← john-barry
**The Living Daylights (1987) – Co-writer** : John Barry collaborated with A-Ha guitarist Paul Waaktaar-Savoy on the theme song for "The Living Daylights" in 1987. This partnership helped create a sizable hit, especially in Norway, aligning with the producers' decision to embrace the new wave sound for Timothy Dalton's first Bond film. "I guess guitarist Paul Wackertar and John Berry worked on it together." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K0pgJ7XAlpo ← john-barry
**Thunderball (1965) – Composer** : John Barry composed the title theme for the 1965 James Bond film "Thunderball." He specifically went to Wales to recruit Tom Jones to sing the song, marking another collaboration that continued the tradition of grand orchestral Bond themes. "Barry went to Wales again for 1965's Thunderball getting Tom Jones to sing the title theme." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K0pgJ7XAlpo ← john-barry
**You Only Live Twice (1967) – Composer** : John Barry was the composer for the title theme of the 1967 James Bond movie "You Only Live Twice," which was sung by Nancy Sinatra. This version was heard by moviegoers, although a different version became a hit single. "Nancy Sinatra, daughter of Frank got the gig. A different version ended up being something of a hit single but this is what movie goers heard." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K0pgJ7XAlpo ← john-barry

## curiosities
**Departure from Bond Films** : John Barry had moved on from scoring Bond films by 1989, making "Licensed to Kill" the first title song not to involve him. His extensive contributions to the franchise had defined much of its musical character for decades. "He had moved on from scoring Bond films." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K0pgJ7XAlpo ← john-barry
