## albums
**Unsold Demos on Bongload** : Beck put together some demos with friend Carl Stevenson, which he planned to release on the small LA label Bongload. Bongload pressed a thousand copies, which remained unsold on local record store shelves for almost two years. "Beck put together some demos that he planned to release on a small LA label called Bongload. Bongload liked it and pressed up a thousand copies, which just kind of sat there, unsolved, on the shelves of the local record stores for almost two years." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5bQz8R0gbXc

## songs
**Loser (1994)** : This single began gaining traction in 1994 after being discovered by "radio types," and it sounded "insanely different and fresh." It significantly boosted Beck's career, helping him sell records "by the truckload" and increasing the trend towards a rock-rap sound. "Loser only increased the growing tilt towards a new sound that was part rock and part rap." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5bQz8R0gbXc
**MTV Makes Me Want to Smoke Crack (Early Experiment)** : This was an "obscure song" that resulted from Beck's first experiment with a newly acquired drum machine, prompting him to try another shot at recording. "That was an obscure song called MTV makes me want to smoke crack, so he decided to give it another shot." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5bQz8R0gbXc

## curiosities
**Early Career as Folk Singer** : Beck started his career as a "weirdo, slacker, folk singer," touring from coffee house to coffee house, often traveling by Greyhound bus. "After starting out as a weirdo, slacker, folk singer, touring from coffee house to coffee house on a Greyhound bus." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5bQz8R0gbXc
**Self-Described "Loser" Persona** : Beck described himself as a "loser" at the time his hit song "Loser" gained popularity, reinforcing his unconventional image. "soon everybody was singing and wrapping along with this Beck guy, a self-described loser." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5bQz8R0gbXc
**Transition to Drum Machine Experimentation** : After returning to LA, Beck began experimenting with a newly acquired drum machine, which led to his breakthrough sound. "Beck Hanson returned to LA and started playing with a newly acquired drum machine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5bQz8R0gbXc
**Unique Genre Blending** : His music was noted for defying easy categorization, being "not folk, that's not rap, it's not hip-hop, it's not rock, it's just Beck." "So it's not folk that's not rap, it's not hip-hop, it's not rock, it's just Beck." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5bQz8R0gbXc
