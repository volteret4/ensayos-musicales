## songs
**Killing an Arab (1979) – Banned Despite Anti-Racist Intent, Based on Camus Novel** : "Killing an Arab" caused riots and bans due to its title, despite the fact that the song was directly based on Albert Camus's novel *The Stranger* and carried an explicitly anti-racist message. The band received almost no financial compensation from the song due to contractual problems, meaning they suffered the controversy without any economic benefit. "Based on Camus's 'The Stranger' and conveying an anti-racist message, the band received almost no economic benefit due to contractual issues." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f4TOWLYRnGs
**Lament** : This song was among those tested by the Arnold World Wide advertising agency for the 2000 Volkswagen "Milky Way" commercial. **Fan Self-Stabbing on Stage – Los Angeles, July 27, 1986** : During a Cure concert in Los Angeles on July 27, 1986, attended by approximately 18,000 people, an audience member named John Morland — recently broken up with his girlfriend and severely depressed — jumped onto the stage, pulled out a knife, and began stabbing himself in what appeared to be some kind of final romantic gesture intended for his ex. Much of the crowd assumed the incident was part of the show and cheered him on. Security grabbed Morland before he could do serious damage; he survived. The incident profoundly disturbed the band. "Our crowd thought it was part of the show, so they cheered him on." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lyYsTon_NSc
