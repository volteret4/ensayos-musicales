**Strike-Proof Vocalist** : During the Musicians' Union Strike of 1942-44, which prohibited instrumentalists from recording for major labels, singers like Frank Sinatra were unaffected. As Tommy Dorsey's old vocalist, he was able to continue recording, backed by vocal groups instead of instrumentalists. This period contributed to the rise of singers as stars in American music, shifting away from instrumentalists. "Dorsi's old vocalist Frank Sinatra could, backed by a vocal group instead of instrumentalists." ← Episode 2： ＂Roll Em Pete＂ by Big Joe Turner | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=thpMb1A_fAs

**Transition from Big Band Singer to Solo Star (1942)** : Frank Sinatra, like other big band singers, benefited from the loophole in the 1942 American Federation of Musicians strike. Not being categorized as "musicians" by the union, these singers were free to record and embark on solo careers, becoming stars in their own right, independent of band leaders.
