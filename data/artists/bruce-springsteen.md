## albums
**Born in the U.S.A. (1984)** : Released on June 4, 1984, this album features a cover photo of Bruce's backside in well-worn jeans, with a cap sticking out of his pocket and the American flag in the background, signaling a different, less solemn sound than its predecessor, "Nebraska." The song's verses tell a poignant story of a young man from a hometown jam being sent to war, killing "the yellow man," and returning home to unemployment and a dismissive VA, detailing a betrayal of American veterans rather than a patriotic anthem. It reached number nine on the Billboard Hot 100 in the fall of 1984 and was named number 59 out of the top 365 songs of the 20th century by the Recording Industry Association of America.
