## albums
**Born in the USA (1984) - First CD Pressed in North America** : Bruce Springsteen's "Born in the USA" was reportedly the first Compact Disc to be pressed in North America, occurring in 1984. This landmark event took place as more CD manufacturing plants began operating outside of Germany and Japan, which were the exclusive production sites initially. "And apparently the first CD pressed in North America was Bruce Springsteen's Born in the USA." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gP3oQeYEvso
**Born in the USA (1984) – First US-Produced CD** : This album was the first compact disc produced at the new Sony manufacturing plant in Terre Haute, Indiana, which began operations in October 1984. "A Sony facility went online in Tarot Hote, Indiana, in October 1984. And the first CD it produced was Bruce Springsteen's Born in the USA." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BHkJFA0LMro
**Born to Run (1975)** : The album cover, described as simple and easily visualized, was created by John Berg. "If I ask you to visualize Springsteen's Born to Run album, it's simple. That was created by a guy named John Berg." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pwebM3TfSdg

"I bought darkness on the edge of town. The day that it came out as a 14 year old." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pe40SqDrpZ0

**Nebraska (1982)** : Bruce Springsteen recorded the entirety of this album between 1981 and 1982 using only a portable Fostex cassette unit. This minimalist approach showcased an early example of a major artist creating a full-length, impactful record outside of expensive, professional studio environments. "Bruce Springsteen recorded his entire Nebraska album throughout 1981 and 1982 on a portable Fortrack cassette unit." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uEe_HLUay5M
**Nebraska (1982) - Home Recorded Demos Released as Album** : Recorded between December 17, 1981, and January 3, 1982, in his home using an early Portastudio, this album began as a collection of demos intended for the E Street Band to learn and re-record in a professional studio. However, Springsteen found that he and the band could not replicate the original feeling and raw intimacy of these home recordings. Consequently, he made the decision to release the album exactly as it was made on cassette at home, which went on to sell almost 2 million copies and was an inspiration for other artists. "So Springsteen made the decision to release this album as he originally made it on cassette at home." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jq-e-fZxXOs
**The Ghost of Tom Joad (1995)** : This album features the title track, originally a quiet folk song that narrates the story of the main character from John Steinbeck's novel "The Grapes of Wrath." It later inspired a loud cover by Rage Against the Machine and was re-recorded for a subsequent album. "In its original form, it's a quiet folk song that told the story of the main character from the John Steinbeck novel The Grapes of Wrath." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2EerEOuP52I
