# artist - Alexander Graham Bell

## instruments
- Microphone
- Speaker

## curiosities
**Invention of the Telephone (1876)** : Completed on March 10, 1876, Bell's device transformed sound vibrations into electrical signals and back again. This became the foundation for the first practical working telephone and the modern microphone/speaker relationship. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
**Mohawk Language Translation** : Bell translated the unwritten Mohawk language of the Six Nations Reserve into his visible speech symbols. For this work, he was named an honorary chief, and the experience deepened his understanding of how sound could be visually represented and converted into electrical signals. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
**Mother's Deteriorating Hearing** : A personal tragedy that served as the primary motivation for Bell to develop "visible speech." This was intended as a method for the deaf to communicate verbally. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
**Primitive Robot Contraption (c. 1859)** : At age 12, Bell built a device featuring a head, lips, and mouth operated by bellows. This early experiment was capable of "speaking" and demonstrated his foundational interest in synthesizing human speech. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
**Telephone System Flaws** : Bell's initial telephone design was impractical for widespread networks because it required two phones per person (one for outgoing and one for incoming calls) and dedicated wires between every desired caller. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
**Truve the Speaking Terrier** : Bell experimented with a stray sky terrier named Truve, teaching the dog to "speak" human-like sounds such as "Mama." This research into sound transmission was a precursor to his methods for teaching deaf children. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
