# artist - Brenda Lee

## songs
**I'm Sorry (1960)** : This song was the winner of the 1960 Billboard Song of the Summer. It is characterized as an "awfully weepy" ballad, but one that is "at least filled with heartache." "And in seventh, let's have Brenda Lee's 1960 winner. I'm sorry, which is awfully weepy, but at least filled with heartache." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Song(s) of the Summer Edition Part 2 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4ef50c30a1408dc292e4
**Rockin' Around the Christmas Tree** : This song was recorded onto the world's smallest "vinyl" record, which measured only 15 micrometers across. Created by the Nano Made section at the Technical University of Denmark, this tiny record played for 25 seconds in stereo. "Record runs just 25 seconds and what's on it? Brenda Lee's rocking around the Christmas tree." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B2g4QSREzpw ← brenda-lee

## curiosities
**Recording with Nashville A-team** : Brenda Lee recorded with the same Nashville A-team of session musicians that also worked with Elvis Presley and later Roy Orbison. "recording for RCA with the same Nashville A team that were recording with Elvis and Brenda Lee." ← Episode 83： ＂Only the Lonely＂ by Roy Orbison | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uDvkjXa1ALk
**Voice Polish Comparison (1975)** : Critic Robert Hull, in his assessment of Olivia Newton-John's singing, included Brenda Lee as an example of a vocalist with more "polish." "True, her voice ain't got the polish of Karen Carpenter, Leslie Gore or Brenda Lee." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 40 - Honestly Loving Olivia Newton-John | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-40-honestly-loving-olivia-newton-john
