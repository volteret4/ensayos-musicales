**Feedback Loops for David Bowie (1977)** : In 1977, Robert Fripp was brought in to collaborate with David Bowie for his "Heroes" album in Berlin, with the sole instruction to "do something weird" for the title track. Fripp created three distinct feedback loops: an A note fed back four feet from the amplifier, a G note fed back three feet from the amp, and a similar third take. All these guitar feedback tracks were then processed through an EMS synthesizer, a keyboard-less synth about the size of a briefcase, contributing a unique distorted and feedback-laden texture to Bowie's song. "Three takes, all based on feedback loops." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w ← robert-fripp
