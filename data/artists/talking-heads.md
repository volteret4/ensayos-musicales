"Talking heads from 1979 and life during wartime put that song into the context of the big mainstream rock releases of the day." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h6snk0m4200

## curiosities
**Art School Background and Convention Challenge** : The Talking Heads, with their solid art school background, aimed to challenge musical conventions, contributing to their "bent" musical outlook. "With a solid art school background, the group was all about challenging conventions when it came to music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h6snk0m4200
**Art School Origins** : The band originated from an actual English-style art school, the Rhode Island School of Design, which highlights the influence of art education on their formation. "This band was from an actual English style art school. They were formed at a place called the Rhode Island School of Design." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gKrAXMG8sTc
**Collaboration with Steve Lillywhite** : Talking Heads were among the many notable bands and artists with whom British producer Steve Lillywhite worked on albums. "Steve Lilley White, the British producer who worked on albums from You Too, The Psychedelic Furs, Peter Gabriel Simple Minds, Talking Heads, Morrissey, Rolling Stones, Dave Matthews, BDI, The Killer, Suzing the Banshees, and dozens more." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
**David Byrne's Social Awkwardness** : Frontman David Byrne was known for being socially awkward in person, later speculating that he might be on the Asperger spectrum. This awkwardness often translated into his stage presence, making him appear quirky, experimental, and occasionally paranoid, yet he was celebrated in the punk era for his authenticity. "In person, he was socially awkward. He later speculated that he's somewhere on the Asperger spectrum and was almost as awkward on stage." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h6snk0m4200
**Embrace of Individuality in Punk** : The Talking Heads thrived in the late 1970s punk rock scene, which allowed artists to express themselves regardless of their identity or musical ability, as long as they had something meaningful to say. This environment was perfect for their unique, nerdy music. "He's a great example of how punk allowed everyone to be who they were. As long as you had something to say, you should be able to say it regardless of who you were or your musical ability." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h6snk0m4200
**Evolution of Rhythmic Style** : The band's early material primarily utilized basic 4/4 time. However, by the 1980s, their rhythmic approach became significantly more intricate and experimental. "Early talking head stuff was in basic 4-4 time, but by the time they got to the 1980s, things had just listened for yourself." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CVPPymc_qrk
**Featured in Apple Commercials** : Talking Heads' music has been part of Apple's TV commercials, contributing to the company's distinctive use of renowned artists' songs in their advertisements for the iPod and iPhone. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sfie4YYncxk
**Formation and Early Days** : David Byrne, Chris, and Martino lived together in Rhode Island while attending art school. They eventually formed a group and relocated to New York City, where they spent considerable time performing gigs at a specific "scusy little club" frequented by "cool people." "Eventually, they formed a group and they moved to New York where they spent a lot of time playing gigs at a scusy little club where all the cool people hung out." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oPgeSCy93bo
**Founding Members' Background** : Drummer Chris Frantz and bassist Tina Weymouth, a husband-and-wife team, met while attending art school in Rhode Island. They later moved to New York with singer David Byrne in the mid-1970s to form the band. "They met while attending art school in Rhode Island and decided to move to New York with singer David Byrne in the middle 1970s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CVPPymc_qrk
**Influence on Arcade Fire's Artistry** : David Byrne, the frontman of Talking Heads, identified similarities between his band's former artsy, semi-world music approach and Arcade Fire's songcraft, indicating a potential stylistic resonance or influence. "David Byrne of The Talking Hints wrote at length about how he admired their song craft and how he heard and saw bits of what his old band used to do in their artsy semi-world music sort of way back in the day." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ir2FfLQJNtY
**Influence on Modest Mouse** : Modest Mouse was described as sounding like a "reincarnation" of Talking Heads in the 2006-2007 period. This indicates the enduring influence of Talking Heads' distinctive sound on subsequent generations of bands. "Modest mouse sounded like a reincarnation of the talking heads." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-CT7yYXonIk
**New York Punk and Apolitical Stance** : The Talking Heads were part of the artsy outsider punk movement in New York. Along with bands like the Ramones and Blondie, they were generally not characterized by political songwriting, setting them apart from their British punk counterparts. "if you look at the major players, like the Ramones and Blondie and the Talking Heds, none of them were very political." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0ARYt4sYT90
**Post-Breakup Projects** : Following their time with Talking Heads, the members pursued various projects: David Byrne embarked on a solo career, Jerry Harrison also engaged in solo work, and Chris Frantz and Tina Weymouth formed the group Tom Tom Club. "David Byrne has a solo career, Jerry Harrison does stuff on his own and Chris and Tina are the Tom Tom Fub." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oPgeSCy93bo
**Presence in CBGB Scene** : The Talking Heads were among the significant bands that emerged from and helped define the scene at CBGB in the 1970s. "The Ramones, the talking heads, blondie, television, and so many hundreds of other groups turned this dive bar into a scene." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Epz5Sn151bk
**Produced by Brian Eno** : Brian Eno produced records for the band Talking Heads. "He's produced records for David Bowie, you two, the Talking Hits and Coldplay" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QfiRRtsZoYU
**Rhythmically Complex Sound** : Emerging from the punk and new wave scene of the late 1970s and early 1980s, the Talking Heads were noted for their rhythmic complexity. Their sound was characterized by "slinky and funky grooves" which elevated post-punk musicianship, partly due to producer Brian Eno's involvement. "Out of all the bands to emerge from the punk and new wave scene of the late 70s in the early 1980s, the talking heads were easily the most rhythmically complex." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CVPPymc_qrk
**Shared Bills with Ramones** : In 1975, Talking Heads played on bills with the Ramones at CBGB. Their bassist, Tina Weymouth, later recounted "arguments backstage" concerning the Ramones' famously short set times and the exceptional devotion of their fanbase. "Other times, they'd be on a bill with a talking heads or blondey or a band called Tuftarts." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=j8uk7BnbXwU
**Signed by Seymour Stein** : The Talking Heads were one of the influential bands signed by Seymour Stein, the founder of Sire Records. Stein played a crucial role in their early career. "He's the guy who signed the Ramones and the Talking Heds." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
**Sire Records Branding** : The Talking Heads were among the inaugural bands branded as "New Wave" by Sire Records, contributing to the establishment of the term in North America by the end of 1977. "The Talking Hits, one of the first bands branded by Sire Records as New Wave." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**Times Square Soundtrack Contribution (1980)** : Talking Heads were contributors to the 1980 *Time Square* soundtrack. This double LP, despite the movie and album performing dismally in sales, provided an instant snapshot of the cutting-edge music of the day, helping to introduce audiences to punk and new wave. "Talking heads, one of the contributors to the Time Square soundtrack from 1980." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM
**Tina Weymouth – Broke Barriers as a Female Bassist in the 1970s** : Tina Weymouth became a pioneering figure as a woman playing bass in a major rock band in the 1970s, at a time when it was considered unusual or "unfeminine." Her presence directly inspired future female bassists including Kim Gordon and Kim Deal. "Broke barriers as a woman playing bass in the 1970s, in a time when it was 'unusual' or 'unfeminine.'" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=95clNVd0hYg
**Unique Sound and Humor** : Initially perceived as an "odd band out" or a "weird choice" when touring with The Ramones, the Talking Heads distinguished themselves with a minimalist musical approach and a wry sense of humor. "But if you took the time to listen, you would see the same sense of minimalism at work, and also that same rye sense of humor." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gKrAXMG8sTc
