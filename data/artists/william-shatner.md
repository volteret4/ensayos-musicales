# artist - William Shatner

## labels
- Decca

## albums
**The Transformed Man (1968)** : William Shatner's most famous record, released by Decca. He conceived it as a project to link classic literature (Shakespeare, Edmond Rostand) with contemporary song lyrics by performing them over original musical backgrounds, aiming to showcase contrasting philosophies. Each combined piece was intended to be six minutes long. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM

## songs
**Lucy in the Sky with Diamonds (1967) - William Shatner Version** : William Shatner's interpretation on "The Transformed Man" album, where he stated he was "performing LSD" by portraying a "guy looped" experiencing a vision of "tangerine trees and marmalade skies." He believed this performance was often taken out of context by listeners. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM

## curiosities
**"How Time Flies" on Broadway** : William Shatner's stage production "How Time Flies" successfully made it to Broadway, albeit in a rejigged format where Alan Cross's role was replaced by an empty chair prop. Alan Cross conceded the production was "much better" without his participation. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
**Attempt to Sing Stompin' Tom Connors' "The Hockey Song"** : William Shatner attempted to perform "The Hockey Song" by Stompin' Tom Connors as a sing-along for his stage show but struggled with its execution in rehearsals, leading Alan Cross to try and coach him. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
**How Time Flies Stage Production** : William Shatner developed a stage production titled "How Time Flies," based on reminiscences of his 60-year career in show business. The show was structured as a celebrity interview in front of an audience, incorporating audio and video clips over 90 minutes with a 15-minute intermission. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
**How Time Flies Tour Locations** : The stage production "How Time Flies" toured various cities including Vancouver, Regina, Edmonton, Winnipeg, Toronto, and Montreal. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
**Recording Philosophy for The Transformed Man (1968)** : William Shatner explained his intention for "The Transformed Man" was to perform classic literary speeches alongside contemporary song lyrics, using original musical backdrops to compare or contrast philosophies within a six-minute format for each track. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
**The "You Will Drink Beer" Incident in Regina** : At a brew pub in Regina, William Shatner, while enjoying a flight of various beers, commanded Alan Cross in a "Wrath of Khan" voice to drink beer, despite Cross's stated preference for not being a beer drinker, resulting in a severe hangover for Cross. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
