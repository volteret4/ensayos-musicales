## albums
**The Transformed Man (1968)** : This album was recorded after Decca Records asked Shatner to make a record while he was a young actor having success with Star Trek. He conceived it as a philosophical project linking classic literature (Shakespeare, Edmond Rostand) with modern song lyrics, aiming to show comparative or opposing philosophies. Each track was designed as a six-minute pairing (three minutes literature, three minutes music) meant to be heard together for context. "The most famous being the transformed man from 1968." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
**The Transformed Man (1968) - Concept** : Shatner's vision for the album was to perform great speeches from literature, such as Shakespeare and Edmond Rostand, alongside contemporary song lyrics with melodic musical backgrounds. His aim was to illustrate either a comparative or an opposing philosophy between the literary and musical components of each track. "I'll do a literature, a piece of literature, and link it to music. Because I love literature and the great speeches that Shakespeare and Edmund Rostan wrote and all those people." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
**The Transformed Man (1968) - Recording** : The album was recorded in Los Angeles at a Decca studio. Shatner emphasized that for the album's concept to be understood, both the literary and musical parts of each six-minute cut had to be heard together, as taking one out of context would obscure his artistic intention. "Made in LA, if I remember correctly, to take a studio, some kind." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8

## songs
**Lucy in the Sky with Diamonds (1968)** : Featured on "The Transformed Man" album, Shatner performed this song out of its original context, which led to some confusion among listeners. He explained his interpretation of it as "LSD," a vision of tangerine trees and marmalade skies, performed as a "guy looped," contrasting with literary figures who achieve great heights without drugs. "People took things like Lucy and the Sky with Diamonds out of context. They were, what's he doing? Well, what I'm doing is performing LSD, a guy who thinks of tangering trees and marmalade skies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
**Lucy in the Sky with Diamonds (The Beatles Cover)** : Shatner performed this song on *The Transformed Man*, aiming to convey the vision of LSD by portraying "a guy looped," experiencing "change of injuries and marmalade skies." He intended this performance to contrast with the non-drug-influenced philosophical speech from Edmond Rostand's Cyrano. "Things like Lucy the Sky with Diamonds, out of context. Well, what's he doing? Well, what I'm doing is performing LSD, a guy who thinks of a change of, a change of injuries and marmalade skies. And that's LSD to me. That's a vision. And I try to perform it as a guy looped." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
**The Hockey Song (Stompin' Tom Connors Cover)** : William Shatner insisted on performing this famous Stompin' Tom Connors track at the conclusion of each show during a stage production he was involved in. Despite initial difficulties in rehearsals, he worked to deliver a performance of the song. "At the conclusion of each show, he insisted on singing a song. So we sat down to try to decide what song he should do. And in the end, we came up with the hockey song, the famous Stomp and Tom Connors track." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8

## curiosities
**"How Time Flies" Broadway Adaptation** : Following the successful tour of "How Time Flies," Shatner was so pleased that he decided to take the production to Broadway. After negotiations, the show did make it to Broadway, though with a rejigged format. "When the two are wrapped up, the shot was so pleased that he declared we're taking this thing to Broadway." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
**"How Time Flies" Stage Production (2000s)** : Shatner conceived a stage production based on reminiscences from his 60-year career in show business. The show was structured as a celebrity interview in front of an audience, incorporating audio and video clips over 90 minutes with a 15-minute intermission, and toured through Vancouver, Regina, Edmonton, Winnipeg, Toronto, and Montreal. "The tour for the production, which was called How Time Flies, by the way, started in Vancouver and made its way through Regina Edmonton Winnipeg, Toronto Montreal." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
**"The Hockey Song" Sing-along Attempt** : Shatner wanted to end each "How Time Flies" show with a sing-along and considered performing "The Hockey Song" by Stompin' Tom Connors. **Musical Style and Philosophy** : William Shatner's singing style is described as eccentric, yet he expresses a deep love for music, stating "music is in his soul." He acknowledges that his skills lie in words rather than music, viewing music itself as a "mysterious place to go" even for seasoned musicians. "I am a dearth of that and totally recognize it. So my skills are in words and not music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
**Philosophy of Experimentation and Failure** : Shatner advocates for continuous experimentation in all aspects of life, asserting that "every time should be a time of experimentation," rather than limiting it to specific periods. He firmly believes that failure is a necessary prerequisite for success, as one learns what doesn't work before discovering what does, and that people should be given allowance for this process. "Well, every time should be a time of experimentation. Okay. I should take one year to experiment, not another year. Everything should be filled with experimentation." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
**Private Jet Travel** : Shatner travelled between gigs on his private jet during the "How Time Flies" tour, offering a ride from Toronto to Montreal on one occasion.
