## songs
**That'll Be the Day (Year Unknown) – Buddy Holly Cover** : As the precursor to The Beatles, The Quarrymen recorded a cover of Buddy Holly's "That'll Be the Day." Paul McCartney owns the original, single copy of this recording, which is estimated to be valued at approximately $250,000 US. McCartney reissued this recording in 1981, producing 25 copies each on 10-inch 78s and 7-inch 45s, with these reissues now worth nearly $20,000 each. ## curiosities
**1981 Reissue of "That'll Be the Day"** : In 1981, Paul McCartney reissued The Quarrymen's recording of "That'll Be the Day." These reissues were made in limited runs of 25 copies for both 10-inch 78s and 7-inch 45s, and are now highly valuable, fetching close to $20,000 each. "McCartney reassured that recording in 1981 on both 10-inch 78s and 7-inch 45s in lots of 25 each." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IIJySSo276k
**Exemption from National Service (1957, 1960)** : The end of compulsory national service in Britain in 1957 (for those born after Oct 1, 1939) and its complete abolition in November 1960 meant that young men in bands like The Quarrymen were exempt from military service. This freedom allowed members like John Lennon (born October 1940) and Ringo Starr (born July 1940), and by implication, all four future Beatles, to continue pursuing music rather than being drafted. "And that meant that the young men in a new group called the Corrie men wouldn't have to worry about going into the army, so they kept at it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V7WjNR1ujhk
**Paul McCartney's Original Copy of "That'll Be the Day"** : Paul McCartney possesses the singular original recording of Buddy Holly's "That'll Be the Day" by his pre-Beatles band, The Quarrymen, with its estimated value being around $250,000 US. "Paul McCartney owns the one and only original copy, and its estimated value is in the vicinity of 250,000 US." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IIJySSo276k
