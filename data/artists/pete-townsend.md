## curiosities
**Accidental Instrument Destruction as a Trademark** : In 1964, while playing at the Railway Tavern in London, Pete Townsend accidentally broke the headstock of his new Rickenbacker guitar on a low ceiling. His subsequent frustration led him to intentionally smash the guitar and his amplifier, an act that was popular with the audience and became a regular part of The Who's performances, eventually defining their stage persona for a time. "Pete Townsend raised his brand new Rikin' Backer guitar too high and smashed the headstock into the ceiling. He was annoyed and decided to make a spectacle of his frustration and hammered the guitar into the stage in his amp until it was nothing but splinters." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yPok3MemDmE ← pete-townsend
