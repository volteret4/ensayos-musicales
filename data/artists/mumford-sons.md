## curiosities
**Appearance and Impact (2009)** : This band appeared around 2009, sounding fresh and different. They achieved significant commercial success and had both cross-genre and cross-generational appeal, helping to usher in a new generation of softer and poppier alt rock, and sparking a folk scene growth in Britain. "When they appeared, they sounded fresh and different." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Comparison to Fairground Attraction** : Fairground Attraction is described as "the Mumford and sons of their day," suggesting a similar folk-pop style and level of popularity in their era. "They were, in a sense, the Mumford and sons of their day." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZcF5JMhBj2M
**Era Influence** : Mumford & Sons characterized an era of indie rock that was prevalent around 2012-2013, known for its use of banjos and mandolins, and a "melodic, woe-as-me introspective" sound. This era made it challenging for traditional rock bands to gain traction. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ocMHRLQMQY0
**Streaming Success with Pop-Leaning Rock Sound** : Mumford and Sons were among the rock bands that experienced successful streams in the 2010s, with their songs reaching hundreds of millions or even billions of listens. Their music was described as leaning towards the pop end of rock, which helped them thrive amidst the shift to streaming consumption. "There was the luminaires, Lorde, Imagine Dragons, Coldplay, Mumford and Sons. They all had songs that streamed hundreds of millions, and in some cases billions of times through the decade." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Y6nHw5xx88
