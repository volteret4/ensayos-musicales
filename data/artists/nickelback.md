## curiosities
**Album Sales (2006)** : Nickelback's album was the biggest selling rock record in 2006, achieving sales of 2.7 million copies. This success occurred as rock music was starting its slow decline after peaking, according to industry observations. "2006, the biggest selling rock record was Nickelback with 2.7 million." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-CT7yYXonIk
**Hate to Love (2023) – Documentary** : The band directly addressed the "insane everyone hates Nickelback meme" in their 2023 documentary, "Hate to Love," offering their perspective on the decade-long period of intense public ridicule. "The band finally addressed things head on in their 2023 documentary Hate to Love." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZzIqIydZTlA
**Impact on Music Industry Ecosystem** : Despite widespread criticism and "irrational global hatred," Nickelback is recognized as a vital component of the music industry's ecosystem. Their commercial success and high album sales meant they were a "hit band" that generated substantial profits. This revenue, particularly during their tenure with Roadrunner Records, was instrumental in subsidizing releases for numerous metal bands such as Opeth, Killswitch Engage, and Mastodon, and also provided crucial "seed money" for Billy Talent's entry into the US market. They exemplify the "rule of 30" in music, where a few highly successful acts financially support many others. "They are an important part of the music industry ecosystem." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Zniri9uSlc
**Subject of "Irrational Hate"** : Nickelback became the target of an "inexplicable and irrational amount of hate and ridicule," beginning in the early 2000s, possibly after comedian Colin Quinn's insult on Comedy Central which turned into a channel promo. Despite selling millions of albums, being extensively played on American radio (more than The Beatles), and selling hundreds of thousands of concert tickets, the band became a meme, a punchline, and shorthand for "bad music." This level of abuse was considered unwarranted. "As for number 64, I'm going to go with what I think was the inexplicable and irrational amount of hate and ridicule directed towards Nickelback." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZzIqIydZTlA
