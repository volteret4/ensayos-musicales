This theory suggests that their widespread presence and constant exposure led to "familiarity bred contempt," contributing to the intense and disproportionate hatred they received. "And Nickelback only got bigger and bigger and bigger over the coming years. And apparently familiarity bred contempt." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TsiYLGGSJ2U

**Authenticity Study and Perceived Genericism (University of Eastern Finland)** : A study from the University of Eastern Finland, titled "Hipper Critical BS performed through Gritted Teeth, Authenticity Dis courses in Nickelback's album reviews and Finnish media," investigated the origins of the band's widespread hate. It concluded that critics perceived Nickelback as "not genuine enough," "too generic in their sound and their image," and simultaneously following genre expectations "too well" (seen as empty imitation) and "not well enough" (interpreted as commercial tactics and a lack of sincere identity). "According to critics, Nickelback isn't genuine enough. Plus they're too generic in their sound and their image. I quote, Nickelback is too much of everything to be enough of something. They follow genre expectations too well, which is seen as empty imitation. But also not well enough, which is read as commercial tactics and as a lack of a stable and sincere identity." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TsiYLGGSJ2U
**Band Name Origin** : The name Nickelback originated from Mike Kruger's time working at Starbucks, where he frequently told customers, "Here's your Nickelback." "Mike Kruger used to work at Starbucks and found himself saying to everyone, here's your Nickelback." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gxbuajGKbko

**Comedy Central Promo Origin of Hate (2003)** : One theory for the widespread hatred attributes its origin to a Comedy Central promo for the show "Tough Crowd," featuring Colin Quinn, which began airing on May 5, 2003. In the promo, Quinn humorously linked violent music to violent behavior, quipping that "bad music makes bad music," and specifically, "listening to Nickelback makes me want to kill Nickelback." This coincided with Nickelback's explosion in popularity with their "Silver Side Up" album and hit single "How You Remind Me." "For the answer, we probably have to go back to Comedy Central, the American TV channel, back in 2003. The channel ran a promo for a show called Tough Crowd featuring Colin Quinn. In this promo, he referenced a study that linked violent music to violent behavior. I quote, no one talks about the studies that show that bad music makes bad music. But listening to Nickelback makes me want to kill Nickelback." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TsiYLGGSJ2U
