"The band finally addressed things head on in their 2023 documentary Hate to Love." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZzIqIydZTlA
**Impact on Music Industry Ecosystem** : Despite widespread criticism and "irrational global hatred," Nickelback is recognized as a vital component of the music industry's ecosystem. Their commercial success and high album sales meant they were a "hit band" that generated substantial profits. This revenue, particularly during their tenure with Roadrunner Records, was instrumental in subsidizing releases for numerous metal bands such as Opeth, Killswitch Engage, and Mastodon, and also provided crucial "seed money" for Billy Talent's entry into the US market. They exemplify the "rule of 30" in music, where a few highly successful acts financially support many others. "They are an important part of the music industry ecosystem." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Zniri9uSlc
**Inclusion as Superstar Act** : Despite any strong opinions, Nickelback must be included as one of the few superstar rock acts to emerge in the 21st century due to their impressive sales and touring figures. "You got them include Nickelback as one of the few superstar rock acts to emerge in the 21st century." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z7glrAoXtkA
**Origins as Village Idiot** : The band started as a cover band called Village Idiot, featuring brothers Chad and Mike Kruger, their cousin Brandon Kruger, and friend Ryan Peek. "They started as a cover band called Village Idiot." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gxbuajGKbko

**Subject of "Irrational Hate"** : Nickelback became the target of an "inexplicable and irrational amount of hate and ridicule," beginning in the early 2000s, possibly after comedian Colin Quinn's insult on Comedy Central which turned into a channel promo. This level of abuse was considered unwarranted.
