## songs
**Small Town Boy (1984)** : This first single from Bronski Beat was a powerful track with a captivating melody and beat that drew listeners in before they realized the profound message within. It told the poignant story of a young gay man who leaves his family and hometown because he doesn't fit in or feel wanted. The accompanying video was also very impactful, with singer Jimmy Somerville portraying the character, drawing on his own experiences of bullying, family estrangement, and rejection. Despite its controversial themes, "Small Town Boy" became a top 10 hit in Canada, the UK, Australia, France, Italy, and the Netherlands, though it only reached number 48 in the US. "It told the story of a young gay man leaving his family and his hometown because he just didn't fit in or wasn't wanted." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hswtuenFwvM

## curiosities
**Groundbreaking Gay Positive Band** : Bronski Beat was recognized as a groundbreaking "gay positive band" in the early 1980s, featuring Richard Coles on keyboards and Jimmy Somerville as the singer. "That eventually led to a job playing keyboards for the groundbreaking gay positive band Bronsky beat in the early 80s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jMfH9xAOpI
**Jimmy Somerville's Personal Connection to "Small Town Boy"** : The video for "Small Town Boy" featured singer Jimmy Somerville, who personally experienced the same kinds of bullying, estrangement from family, and rejection depicted in the song and its visual narrative. This personal connection added authenticity and emotional weight to the song's message. "You couldn't help but feel terrible for the character played by singer Jimmy Somerville, who by the way experienced the same sort of things that we see in the video, the bullying, the estrangement from family, and the rejection." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hswtuenFwvM
**Motivation for Honest Gay Songs** : The band started by playing local arts festivals and became increasingly frustrated with how "sanitized and safe" other gay performers were. This frustration fueled their mission to write honest songs directly addressing the realities of being gay, including the politics, social challenges, and abuse faced by the LGBTQ community. "Everything was to write honest songs about being gay, the politics involved, the social challenges, and the abuse the LGBTQ community faced." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hswtuenFwvM
**Openly Gay Trio from Glasgow** : Bronski Beat was a synth rock trio from Glasgow in the early to mid-1980s, a period and place described as bleak and challenging for openly gay individuals. All three members of the band were openly gay, making their public presence particularly brave. "They were a synth rock trio from Glasgow, a pretty bleak place in the early 80s and mid-80s, and a tough place to be openly gay, as all three members of the band were." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hswtuenFwvM
**Post-Peak Technopop Persistence** : As the popularity of pure Technopop waned in the mid-1980s, Bronski Beat was noted as a group that, along with their spin-off The Communards, continued to stand their ground and maintain a presence with their electronic sound. "Bronski beat and their spin off the Comming Arts stood their ground." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
**Quick Record Deal with London Records** : Bronski Beat secured a record deal with London Records after performing only nine gigs, indicating their immediate impact and appeal to the industry. "It took them all of nine gigs to get a record deal with London Records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hswtuenFwvM
**Technopop Descendant** : Listed as one of the artists who descended from the original Technopop scene. "There was Bronsky beat, aha, yellow, Elfaville, Frankie Ghost of Hollywood. They all descended from the original Tecno Pop scene." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KF5MwJVYng
