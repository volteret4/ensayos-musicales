**Shout Sister Shout** : Co-composed by Bill Doggett and Lucky Millinder, this song later became the title for a biography of Sister Rosetta Tharpe. She was paired with Trevor Bacon, Millinder's main singer, during her time with the band, with Bill Doggett as the pianist and likely arranger for her big band recordings. "he co-composed with Melinda, the song that later gave its title to a biography of Tharp, Shout Sister Shout." ← Episode 50： ＂Honky Tonk＂, by Bill Doggett | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H2rYnKmeZF0
**Strange Things Happening Every Day (1945)** : Recorded in 1945, this song features Sister Rosetta Tharpe playing a resonator guitar with noticeable ferocity. It was released before rock and roll was officially invented, leading some to consider it one of the very first rock and roll songs. ## curiosities
**"Godmother of Rock and Roll" Title** : Due to her significant influence on early rock and roll artists and her innovative use of the electric guitar, Sister Rosetta Tharpe has been affectionately christened the "Godmother of Rock and Roll" by some. "So no wonder some people have christened her the godmother of rock and roll." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hbNJQ6OYmd0 ← sister-rosetta-tharpe
**Chicago Presence (1940s)** : Sister Rosetta Tharpe was living and performing in Chicago during Sam Cooke's formative years in the 1940s, alongside Thomas Dorsey and Mahalia Jackson. "Dorsey, Mahalia Jackson and Sister Rosetta Tharp were all living and performing in the city during Young Sam's formative years" ← Episode Sixty： ＂You Send Me＂ by Sam Cooke | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=92tD5qJR-yY
**Closeted Queer Identity** : While Sister Rosetta Tharpe was queer, she maintained privacy about her sexuality. This detail is contrasted with Little Richard's more open, albeit conflicted, expression of his sexuality. "I mentioned that sister Rosetta was queer, but that was something that she kept quiet." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ ← sister-rosetta-tharpe
**Early Influence on Stand By Me** : Sister Rosetta Tharpe was known for performing a gospel song from 1905 by Reverend Charles Albert Tindley, which served as one of the deep roots and prehistories for Benny King's "Stand By Me." "A gospel song that had been performed by Sister Rosetta Tharp." ← Episode 94： ＂Stand By Me＂, by Ben E. King | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OgNsNmzW2po
**Godmother of Rock and Roll** : Sister Rosetta Tharpe, a queer Black woman from Cotton Plant, Arkansas, is widely called the "Godmother of rock and roll." She was a pioneering guitarist who transitioned to the electric guitar, revered by figures like Johnny Cash, Jerry Lee Lewis, Elvis Presley, Little Richard, and Chuck Berry for her influential bridge between gospel blues and swing music. "A lot of people will point to sister Rosetta Thar, a guitarist who graduated to the electric guitar and has been called the Godmother of rock and roll." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ ← sister-rosetta-tharpe
**Influence on British Rock Legends** : During her European tour in the spring of 1964, Sister Rosetta Tharpe performed in Manchester, where her inspiring performance deeply affected audience members Eric Clapton, Jeff Beck, Keith Richards, and Brian Jones of The Rolling Stones. These future rock icons became her "disciples" after witnessing her groundbreaking show. "Her performance was so inspiring that it changed the lives of four notable members of the audience." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ ← sister-rosetta-tharpe
**Pioneering Electric Guitarist – First Woman Transition** : Starting in the 1930s, Sister Rosetta Tharpe began making gospel records, initially on acoustic guitar, then transitioning to the electric guitar. She is noted as potentially the first woman to make this shift and was using distortion to her advantage even before distortion pedals were invented. "As far as I can tell, she was the first woman to make that transition from acoustic to electric." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hbNJQ6OYmd0 ← sister-rosetta-tharpe
**Profound Influence on Blues and Rock Pioneers** : Sister Rosetta Tharpe had a deep impact on the trajectory of the blues and on the pioneers of rock and roll, including major figures like Little Richard, Chuck Berry, Jerry Lee Lewis, and Johnny Cash, all of whom were ardent fans. Her work also captivated British musicians such as Keith Richards, Eric Clapton, and Jeff Beck. "What is not in dispute is that sister Rosetta Tharp had a profound effect on the course of the blues, and then with the pioneers of rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hbNJQ6OYmd0 ← sister-rosetta-tharpe
