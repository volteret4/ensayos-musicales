## curiosities
**Closeted Queer Identity** : While Sister Rosetta Tharpe was queer, she maintained privacy about her sexuality. This detail is contrasted with Little Richard's more open, albeit conflicted, expression of his sexuality. "I mentioned that sister Rosetta was queer, but that was something that she kept quiet." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
**Godmother of Rock and Roll** : Sister Rosetta Tharpe, a queer Black woman from Cotton Plant, Arkansas, is widely called the "Godmother of rock and roll." She was a pioneering guitarist who transitioned to the electric guitar, revered by figures like Johnny Cash, Jerry Lee Lewis, Elvis Presley, Little Richard, and Chuck Berry for her influential bridge between gospel blues and swing music. "A lot of people will point to sister Rosetta Thar, a guitarist who graduated to the electric guitar and has been called the Godmother of rock and roll." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
**Influence on British Rock Legends** : During her European tour in the spring of 1964, Sister Rosetta Tharpe performed in Manchester, where her inspiring performance deeply affected audience members Eric Clapton, Jeff Beck, Keith Richards, and Brian Jones of The Rolling Stones. These future rock icons became her "disciples" after witnessing her groundbreaking show. "Her performance was so inspiring that it changed the lives of four notable members of the audience." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
