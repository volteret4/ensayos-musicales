## curiosities
**Andy Rourke's Bass Style** : Andy Rourke, the bass player, was known for funk and subtlety, and he taught Mike Joyce how the bass and bass drum should work together.
**Andy Rourke's Post-Smiths Career** : Andy Rourke worked as a hired gun for a while before reuniting with Mike Joyce in a group called Vinnie Peculiar.
**Critical Acclaim** : Considered the most important British band of the 1980s and the most important indie band that the UK and Alt-Rock have ever produced.
**Defining Indie Sound (Mid-1980s)** : Became the biggest indie band of their era in the mid-1980s, whose influences continue to be discussed. Their sound was characterized by a simple arrangement of guitar, bass, drums, and vocals.
**Featured on Alt-Rock Playlists** : their music was found on radio playlists for late 1986 alt-rock stations.
**Foundational Influence** : Their work laid the foundations for two important future indie eras: Madchester in the late 1980s and early 1990s, and Britpop, which dominated the remainder of the 1990s.
**Image and Style** : Their aesthetic drew from both 1960s rock and the post-punk era.
**Influence on 2000s Revival** : Was a band rediscovered and mined for inspiration by early 2000s New Wave revivalists.
**Influence on The Killers** : Their sound contributed to the "DNA" of The Killers.
**Inspired by Sex Pistols (1976)** : Formed after attending the Sex Pistols' legendary concert at the Lesser Free Trade Hall in Manchester on June 4, 1976.
**Mike Joyce's Drumming Style** : Mike Joyce, the drummer, admitted to being a "ham-fisted" player, focused on power and volume.
**Mike Joyce's Post-Smiths Career** : Mike Joyce went on to play with a variety of bands, including Badly Drawn Boy and Suede.
**Musical Pushback** : Represented a deliberate pushback against the dominant use of synthesizers and drum machines prevalent in the early 1980s.
**Royalty Lawsuit (1996)** : In 1996, both Mike Joyce and Andy Rourke sued Morrissey and Johnny Marr for non-payment of royalties. Andy settled for £80,000, while Mike was awarded £1.2 million. Morrissey has reportedly continued to appeal the judgment.
**The Killers' Fandom** : The members of The Killers are big fans of The Smiths and Morrissey.
**Unique Sound** : The combination of Mike Joyce and Andy Rourke created a unique sound for its time, described by Mike as a "weird kind of communion."
