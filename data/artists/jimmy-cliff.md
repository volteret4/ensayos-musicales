## songs
**King of Kings** : This song was originally written by Jimmy Cliff. It gained chart recognition in Britain when Ezrako and the Launchers released a cover version, becoming the first Ska record to hit the British charts. "The first SCAR record to hit the charts in Britain was a cover of a Jimmy Cliff song, King of Kings, performed by Ezrako and the launchers." ← Episode 114： ＂My Boy Lollipop＂ by Millie | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kf-DtqoOsRI
**The Harder They Come (1972)** : The title track from the movie and soundtrack, this song never charted on the Billboard charts but was recognized as number 122 on Rolling Stone's list of the top 500 singles. Its lyrics speak of resilience against oppression: "Well, the oppressors are trying to keep me down, trying to drive me underground. And they think that they have got the battle won. I say forgive them, Lord. They know not what they've done. Because as sure as the sun will shine, I'm going to get my share now, what's mine. And then the harder they come, the harder they fall, one and all." "The harder they come, it never made it onto the Billboard charts. **Vietnam (1969)** : Released in 1969, this song was highly praised by both Paul Simon and Bob Dylan, who considered it one of the best protest songs ever created. Jimmy Cliff later performed it alongside Paul Simon at "The Concert at Hyde Park" in 2012. ## curiosities
**Authenticity of The Harder They Come** : Jimmy Cliff, reflecting 50 years later, stated that "The Harder They Come" was a landmark movie due to its authentic portrayal of life, with non-professional actors and a story that resonated with anyone from the country looking to make a life in the city. "it was such a landmark movie because it was very real. None of us were professional actors. Clip says. The story is the story of any one of us who was born in the country or coming from the country." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 28 - Soundtracks of the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/028-soundtracks-of-the-70s-12221-5-45-pm

**Jimmy Cliff Death (2025) – Seizure and Pneumonia at 81, Predecessor to Bob Marley** : Jimmy Cliff, the pioneering reggae artist and Jamaican national hero who predated Bob Marley in bringing reggae to global audiences, died at age 81 of a seizure and pneumonia. **Reggae's Natural Fit (1976)** : In 1976, Jimmy Cliff articulated his view that reggae was the inherent music of the 70s, perceiving it as the most complete contemporary genre that resonated with the prevailing lifestyle aspirations of the era. "Jimmy Cliff said in 1976 that reggae is the natural music of the 70s. It is the most complete contemporary form of music that fits with the lifestyle that people are trying to live." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 29 - Every Little Thing is Gonna Be All Right - The Influence of 70s Reggae | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-29-every-little-thing-is-gonna-be-all-right-the-influence-of-70s-reggae
**Role in The Harder They Come (1972)** : Jimmy Cliff starred as Ivan, a musician who is exploited by the music industry, receiving only $20 for a song he wrote. This exploitation leads him to a life of crime in Kingston, Jamaica. He described the film as a "landmark movie" because of its realism, with none of the actors being professionals.
