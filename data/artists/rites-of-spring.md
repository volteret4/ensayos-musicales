## albums
**Rites of Spring (1985)** : This self-titled record, released in 1985 and produced by Ian MacKaye, is widely regarded by many emo scholars as the first true emo record. The band played loud but not always as fast as their peers, choosing instead to focus on melodic guitars and deeply personal lyrical themes such as depression, bitter romanticism, and nostalgia, departing from typical political and social hardcore themes. "It's a self-titled record, which is considered by many emo scholars to be the first true emo record." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v_am7ifh7Pk

## curiosities
**D.C. Scene Origins of Emo** : Rites of Spring emerged from the Washington D.C. scene in the mid-1980s, which is credited as a crucial birthplace for emo. The band's approach contrasted with the violence prevalent at some hardcore shows, as they chose to focus on personal lyrical themes and more melodic guitar work. "we need to give all kinds of credit to a scene in Washington, D.C. that emerged in the middle 1980s. And this is where we encounter a hardcore band called Rites of Spring." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v_am7ifh7Pk
**Early Emo Band** : Rites of Spring is cited as one of the early original emo bands, stemming from the Washington, D.C. hardcore punk scene and noted for its emphasis on emotional expression. "Early original emo bands included minor threat and rights of spring." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mAlc-N2wn5s
**Personal Lyrical Themes** : Diverging from common political and social themes in hardcore, Rites of Spring focused their lyrics on deeply personal subjects such as depression, bitter romanticism, and nostalgia for better times.
