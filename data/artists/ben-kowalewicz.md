# artist - Ben Kowalewicz

## curiosities
**Dropped Out, Multiple Jobs, Independent at 17** : Ben dropped out of school and held numerous part-time jobs, including at Second Cup, Deli's, music shops (Music World), and unloading airplanes. By age 17, he was living independently with his brothers, working three or four jobs to pay rent. "I dropped out. ... I was in the King of part-time jobs. Like I worked at second cups. I worked at Deli's. I worked at a music shop. I worked at music world. I unloaded airplanes for a long time. ... I was doing three, four jobs, you know. Because by the time I was 17, I was kind of living by myself and living with my brothers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cZlLH9J_lJw
**Drummer Booted** : Ben was "booted" from his drumming role in To Each His Own when Aaron Solowaniak, a more proficient drummer, was discovered practicing in a friend's basement. "And it was Aaron. And so I got the boot out of the band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cZlLH9J_lJw
**First Singing Role** : After being removed as the drummer, Ben was called back to To Each His Own to sing backups, a role he accepted despite never having sung before. "And I got a call about a month or two later asking if I wanted to sing backups in this band. And I was like, what? So I just wanted to be in a band. So I said, sure, I never had sung before." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cZlLH9J_lJw
**Hired at The Edge** : Following George Stroumboulopoulos's advice, Ben emailed Phil Evans at The Edge radio station for a job interview. Despite having no high school or radio/television education, he convinced Phil Evans to hire him by promising to work harder than anyone else. "And he said, you know, when did you graduate from radio television? I'm like, I didn't. He's like, well, when did you graduate from high school? Like, I didn't. And he said, well, why should I give you the job? And I was like, because I'll work harder than anybody else. I'll come here early and I'll stay late every day. I promise. ... And as I was walking home, he called me and said, you start Monday." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cZlLH9J_lJw
**Mod Robes Job, George Stroumboulopoulos, Ted's Wrecking Yard** : Ben worked at Mod Robes on Queen Street, selling pants. During this time, George Stroumboulopoulos would attend Pezz shows at Ted's Wrecking Yard, where Pezz became known as "the cancel band" due to frequently filling last-minute slots. "I was working at a mode robes on Queen Street selling pants. And I remember a guy named by the name of George Straumelopoulos would come and see us play because we were playing like Ted's wrecking our knowledge. We kind of became like the cancel band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cZlLH9J_lJw
**Quit Mod Robes, Sought Radio Job** : After watching the movie "Office Space," Ben abruptly quit his job at Mod Robes. He then contacted George Stroumboulopoulos, seeking advice on how to get a job in radio. "And I remember watching the movie Office Space and then the next day I went into mod robes and I quit. And I called George and I said, hey, I want to get a job at radio. How do I do it? How, where would I start?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cZlLH9J_lJw
**To Each His Own Drummer** : Ben Kowalewicz initially joined a band called To Each His Own as a drummer, having just moved to Meadowvale and meeting Trevor Bowman. "And long story short, I was the drummer in this band called To Each His Own for a minute." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cZlLH9J_lJw
