**Removed from Tour Over Dog** : Sublime was asked to leave the Warped Tour because they insisted on bringing the band's dog, and the tour had a strict "no dog zone" policy. "sublime was asked to leave the tour because they insisted on bringing the band's dog and there was no dog zone." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Sublime (Long Beach, 1988) – Co-Headlined First Vans Warped Tour 1995; Singer Brad Nowell Died of Heroin Overdose Two Months Before Major Label Album Came Out** : Sublime came together in 1988 and had their own label (Skunk Records) by 1992. They became wildly popular by playing anywhere and everywhere, co-headlining the first Vans Warped Tour in 1995 and the first Sno-Core tour. After landing a major label deal, their first major label CD sold millions. Unfortunately, singer Brad Nowell died of a heroin overdose just two months before it came out. "Shortly after they also snagged a major label record deal and that first big label CD sold in the millions." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Yqo-YDJR0nM
**Third Wave Ska Band** : Sublime was identified as a band from the "third wave" of Ska, which arrived in the 1990s. This wave saw significant popularity with bands like No Doubt, The Mighty Mighty Bosstones, Reel Big Fish, Goldfinger, and Dance Hall Crashers. "Third wave Scott arrived in the 1990s, no doubt, muddy, muddy boss tones, sublime, real big fish, gold finger, dancehall crashes and so many more." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HSvgRyGiOpE
