## curiosities
**Britpop Party Attendee** : Ash was listed among the bands that were part of the Britpop scene and received coverage during its peak. "Here's a list of some of the people at the Britpop party. ...Ash..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5q5EnZj1FwE
**Bus Accident on I-5 (2002)** : On August 16, 2002, while Ash was part of Moby's Area Two Tour and traveling to Detroit, their bus tipped over on Interstate 5 in Oregon. The incident occurred after the driver swerved to avoid large tire shreds in the middle of the road. All band and crew members, who were sleeping in their bunks at the time, were thrown from their positions, with drummer Rick McMurray sustaining broken ribs, which was thankfully the worst of the injuries. "Ash was a part of Moby's area two tour and they were on their way to Detroit on the night of August 16th, 2002 when their bus tipped over on interstate five in Oregon." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DgN0vBIugLc
**Childhood Sickness and Transformation** : Ash grew up in a prosperous suburban environment, excelling as a hockey player. At nine years old, he became severely ill, leading to six years of hospitalizations, parental divorce, and his father's financial collapse. He remained physically stunted at 4'6" and 65 lbs for approximately eight years, unable to play hockey. This period of intense pain, isolation, and medical challenges, including mood-altering prednisone, profoundly shaped his appreciation for physical health and inspired his later lifestyle choices. "I got really, really sick. My parents got divorced. Empire crumbled...I got stuck at like four foot six, like 65 pounds." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ayPJfDAF_to
**Early Musical Path and Mentor** : Unable to pursue hockey due to his illness, Ash's mother encouraged him to join a play, recognizing his love for making people laugh and being a show-off. He joined a musical, where he began singing for the first time. Later, while playing bass in his high school band, he was critically encouraged by Aubrey Mints, the singer of his brother's band, Church Bus, to become a lead vocalist after performing Beck's "Nightmare Hippie Girl." Ash considers this conversation a pivotal moment in his life, a realization he reflected on twenty years later during a reunion with Aubrey in Los Angeles. "Aubrey comes up to me after the show and he goes, you need to kick back out of the band. You need to be the singer." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ayPJfDAF_to
**Post-EP Work and Meditation (2007-2008)** : Following the release of the *Welding the C-Drive* EP in summer 2007, Ash traveled to Texas to meditate, while Jay returned to the oil fields to earn money. This period of external work and personal introspection was a necessary hiatus for both members before their music began to gain significant radio attention in early 2008. "Ash went down to Texas to meditate and to just basically you know we both like needed to do you know we needed to work to make money because Toronto rent is high." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ayPJfDAF_to
**Solo Venture and Reunion with Jay (2007)** : After an initial period with Jay, Ash pursued a solo path as "Ubiquitous Synergy Seeker." In April 2007, he invited Jay to rejoin him for a show at Saltz Pub in The Junction, Toronto. Jay, who was working in the Northern Alberta oil fields at the time, returned to play, and they performed old songs from their pre-USS band, Team of Captains. This reunion performance was so enjoyable that it solidified their decision to formally continue their musical partnership as USS. "when Ash went solo and he became ubiquitous synergy seeker he asked me to rejoin forces with him in April 2007." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ayPJfDAF_to
**The "Ubiquitous Synergy Seeker" Manifesto** : During a period of personal stagnation working in a factory, Ash reflected on his "learned helplessness" stemming from childhood trauma and the inhibiting effects of being a "pothead." He decided to write a "script" for the person he aspired to become. While working, he discovered the word "ubiquitous" ("everywhere simultaneously") in a dictionary, which, combined with his studies of Eastern philosophy, led him to coin "Ubiquitous Synergy Seeker." This name encapsulates a paradox: the journey of seeking something that is inherently ever-present, serving as his personal manifesto and the foundational philosophy for his band. "So the name ubiquitous synergy seeker is actually a paradox because how can you seek something that's ever present but that's the journey." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ayPJfDAF_to
