## songs
**I'll Take You There (1972)** : Released in 1972, this song reached number one on June 3rd, 1972. While it doesn't explicitly mention God, its hopeful and inspirational lyrics ("I know a place, ain't nobody crying, ain't nobody worried, ain't no smiling faces, no no lying to the races") embody a gospel spirit. Mavis Staples mostly ad-libbed the lyrics during recording at Muscle Shoals in Alabama. The music itself is described as "straight up gospel." Teresa Davis of The Emotions predicted it would be a hit. ## curiosities
**Dual Hall of Fame Inductees** : The Staples Singers are unique in being inducted into both the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame and the Gospel Hall of Fame, reflecting their significant contributions across both secular and sacred music genres. "That the Staples singers are in both the rock and roll Hall of Fame and the gospel Hall of Fame tells you something about this group." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 32 - Take Me to Church - Religion in 70s Popular Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-32-take-me-to-church-religion-in-70s-popular-music ← the-staples-singers
**Songwriting Credit Dispute for "I'll Take You There"** : During the recording of "I'll Take You There" at Muscle Shoals, Mavis Staples ad-libbed most of the lyrics. She was "not too pleased" that Al Bell took the soul songwriting credits for the track. **Transition to Mainstream Sound** : Purvis Staples, son of Pop and brother of Mavis, played a key role in the group's transition from a purely gospel sound to a more mainstream style that incorporated R&B, folk, and pop elements. However, he was not part of the group's lineup when they achieved their major 1970s hits.
