**Formation and Unique Sound (1982)** : Formed in Vancouver in 1982, Skinny Puppy quickly developed a distinctive sound incorporating keyboards, samplers that used clips from horror movies, drum machines, and loud guitars. The band is considered crucial to the development of the modern industrial sound, perhaps more so than any other early 80s group. "They were formed at Vancouver in 1982 and almost immediately carved out their own special sound with keyboards and samplers that took clips from horror movies. They had drum machines and they had loud guitars." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE

**Influence on Trent Reznor** : Skinny Puppy was a profound influence on Trent Reznor, particularly their sound's "rage and fury." They were instrumental in his shift away from techno-pop and towards the more aggressive, industrial direction that characterized early Nine Inch Nails material, directly inspiring his first song in this new style, "Down In It." "He found that he really liked the rage and fury of bands like Ministry and Front242 and Canada's Skinny Puppy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M3nUQtja0qc
**Influence on Trent Reznor and Nine Inch Nails** : Skinny Puppy's intense and heavy sound, particularly from songs like "Dig It," directly inspired Trent Reznor to re-evaluate his work in techno-pop and subsequently form Nine Inch Nails to emulate their style. "It was songs like that, which made Trent Rezner rethink what he was doing in his Tecno Pop bands, and ultimately he formed 9-inch-dales to emulate Skinny Puppy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EE8U4yyKtd0
**Lawsuit Against US Military (2014)** : Canadian industrial pioneer Skinny Puppy sued the US military in 2014, demanding $666,000 in compensation. The lawsuit was for the unauthorized use of their music on prisoners, a fact the band only discovered after a guard who had served at Guantanamo Bay informed them their songs were being used for "enhanced interrogation." "In 2014, Canadian industrial pioneer Skinny Puppy sued the US military for using their music on prisoners." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=R1wdsGt07Gg

**Use of Found Sounds (1983)** : As early as 1983, the Canadian group Skinny Puppy incorporated "found sound" into their music by using bits of dialogue lifted directly from movies to augment their sound. This practice was an early example of using external audio sources to create unique textures and atmospheres. "For example, Canada's skinny puppy used bits of dialogue lifted directly from movies to augment their sound as early as 1983." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
