## songs
**Dig It (unspecified year)** : This song directly influenced Trent Reznor's first "new style" Nine Inch Nails track, "Down In It." Trent openly admitted that "Down In It" was "a total ripoff" of "Dig It," highlighting Skinny Puppy's significant impact on his transition to a harder, more sinister sound. "The original version I did was about half speed of the one in the record. And it was a total ripoff of Dig It, my Skinny Puppy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M3nUQtja0qc

## curiosities
**Found by Network Records** : Skinny Puppy, a highly non-commercial industrial band that pushed the boundaries of electronic music and the use of samples, was discovered and signed by Network Records. "Network found Skinny Puppy, a highly non-commercial industrial band who pushed the boundaries of electronic music in the use of samples." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Goth and Industrial Blending** : Skinny Puppy is noted as a band that straddles the line between Goth and industrial music. "Some bands straddle that line between Goth and industrial just by doing what they do, nine-inch nails and skinny puppy are just two examples." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Heroin Decimation** : Heroin tragically decimated and brought death to the band members of Skinny Puppy. "Heroin decimated and brought death to the New York dolls. The pretenders sublime, smashing pumpkins, allyson chains, skinny puppy holes, Scott Wylin, D.D. Ramone, Billy Eidle, a chunk of ever-clear, a vast swath of the Britpop scene of the 1990s, and pretty much everyone who's ever been in the Red Hot Chili Peppers past in present." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=i5lR8zz7_ZE
**Influence on Marilyn Manson** : Marilyn Manson admired industrial bands like Skinny Puppy. "Cross that with a love for industrial bands like nine-inch nails and skinny puppy" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Influence on Trent Reznor** : Skinny Puppy was a profound influence on Trent Reznor, particularly their sound's "rage and fury." They were instrumental in his shift away from techno-pop and towards the more aggressive, industrial direction that characterized early Nine Inch Nails material, directly inspiring his first song in this new style, "Down In It." "He found that he really liked the rage and fury of bands like Ministry and Front242 and Canada's Skinny Puppy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M3nUQtja0qc
**Use of Found Sounds (1983)** : As early as 1983, the Canadian group Skinny Puppy incorporated "found sound" into their music by using bits of dialogue lifted directly from movies to augment their sound. This practice was an early example of using external audio sources to create unique textures and atmospheres. "For example, Canada's skinny puppy used bits of dialogue lifted directly from movies to augment their sound as early as 1983." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
