## albums
**Buckingham Nicks (1973) - Duo Album** : This album was recorded in Sound City Studios the year before Mick Fleetwood discovered Lindsey Buckingham and Stevie Nicks. Despite being recorded with "the cream of LA session players," the album "went nowhere," failing to chart or generate radio hits, though it contained sturdy songs like Nicks-penned "Crying in the Night." ## songs
**Crying in the Night (1973)** : This song was penned by Stevie Nicks and featured on the *Buckingham Nicks* album, recorded in 1973. Although the album largely flopped, "Crying in the Night" was noted for its sturdiness, hinting at the songwriting talent Nicks would bring to Fleetwood Mac. ## curiosities
**Abundance of Songs - Flowing Lacy Bell Sleeves** : When Stevie Nicks joined Fleetwood Mac, she had an abundance of unreleased songs, humorously described as being "up her flowing lacy bell sleeves." This rich repertoire included "Landslide," which she contributed to the band's 1975 self-titled album. **Aspiration for "Straight Up Rock and Roll"** : Upon becoming a solo artist, Stevie Nicks expressed a desire to make a "Tom Petty album" and pursue "straight up rock and roll," indicating a preference for a harder sound than Fleetwood Mac's 70s "softer rock." "She wanted to make a Tom Petty album. At least that's what she said when she became a solo artist. **Double Induction into Rock and Roll Hall of Fame** : Stevie Nicks was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame twice: first with Fleetwood Mac in 1998, and then as a solo artist 21 years later, making her the first woman to achieve this honor. Twenty-two men had received this honor previously. "Stevie Nicks was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame for the second time. She was inducted with Fleetwood Mac in 1998, and then 21 years later as a solo artist." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 7 - Women Who Rocked the 70s | http://www.ftr70.com ← stevie-nicks
**Early Career Sexism Experience** : Stevie Nicks recalled a single instance early in her career where a man in the crowd made "lute remarks," and legendary concert promoter Bill Graham immediately intervened, telling him to leave. She considered her overall experience with sexism in the industry to be relatively easy. "She can remember one time, very early in her career, when a guy in the crowd made some lute remarks to her, and the legendary concert promoter Bill Graham told him to essentially get the fuck out, and that was that." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 7 - Women Who Rocked the 70s | http://www.ftr70.com ← stevie-nicks
**Entry into Fleetwood Mac (1975)** : She joined Fleetwood Mac in 1975, as her boyfriend Lindsey Buckingham made her inclusion a non-negotiable condition for his own acceptance into the band. **Involvement in Internal Love Triangle** : She was part of a "messy love triangle" with Lindsey Buckingham and Mick Fleetwood, a significant source of the intense internal drama and lyrical material for the band during the *Rumours* era. "relationship drama Stevie and Mick and Stevie and Lindsey form a messy love triangle." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 51 - The Softer Side of 70s Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-51-the-softer-side-of-70s-rock ← stevie-nicks
**Outsized Personality - Joining Fleetwood Mac** : Stevie Nicks, along with Lindsey Buckingham, possessed an "outsized personality" that contributed to Fleetwood Mac's transformation into multi-platinum superstars when they joined in the mid-70s. This contrasted with Christine McVie's more self-effacing nature. "Vocalist Stevie Nicks, and guitarist and studio whiz Lindsey Buckingham." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Thinking About Tomorrow Edition Part 1 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4ef18e6dd12efb02c429
**Pledge with Christine McVie** : On the day Stevie Nicks joined Fleetwood Mac, she and Christine McVie made a mutual vow never to be treated as "less than" or "second class citizens" within the male-dominated rock environment. They promised to fight for their place, ensure their songs and music were equally as good as the men's, and be included in creative discussions. "She said that they said to each other, we will never be treated like second class citizens. We will never be not allowed to hang out in a room full of intelligent, crazy rock and roll stars, because we are just as crazy and just as intelligent as they are." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 7 - Women Who Rocked the 70s | http://www.ftr70.com ← stevie-nicks
**Pre-Fleetwood Mac Performance - Fritz** : Stevie Nicks had been performing with Lindsey Buckingham since the late 1960s, initially singing lead with his psychedelic rock band called Fritz. This early collaboration established their musical partnership before they formed Buckingham Nicks and later joined Fleetwood Mac. "Buckingham and Nix have been performing with each other since the late 60s when Lindsay got Stevie to sing lead with his psychedelic rock band Fritz." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Thinking About Tomorrow Edition Part 1 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4ef18e6dd12efb02c429
**Reveal of Christine McVie's Illness - Post-Mortem** : A few days after Christine McVie's death on November 30, 2022, bandmate Stevie Nicks revealed that the rest of Fleetwood Mac hadn't known McVie was ill until immediately before her passing. This detail highlighted McVie's private nature, not wanting to trouble the world with her drama. **Romantic and Musical Partners - Buckingham Nicks Duo** : Before joining Fleetwood Mac, Stevie Nicks and Lindsey Buckingham were both romantic and musical partners, performing together since the late 60s and releasing the album *Buckingham Nicks* in 1973. Their relationship dynamics would later become a significant part of Fleetwood Mac's drama.
