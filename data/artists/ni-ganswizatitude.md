## albums
**Straight, Abra Kampton (1988) - Defining Rap Album** : This album, released in 1988, marked a pivotal moment in the timeline of rap, leaving American society quite disoriented. Formed out of necessity in the mid-80s due to the extensive Afro-American hip hop scene, the band broke a generational gap and established themselves as absolute leaders of the "gangsta movement." The album is characterized by its rage, aggression, and violence, serving as a therapeutic reflection of the harsh realities lived in Afro-American suburbs. It explicitly discusses racist police behavior, indiscriminate arrests, and police violence, with some band members having personally suffered arbitrary detentions. It also explores the crude and direct relationship of Afro-American suburbs with drugs, detailing both those who profited and those who were devastated and victimized. The album created a "convulsion" in the United States, resonating deeply with many Afro-American youths who identified more with its discourse than with the more restrained messages of older generations. Its release caused significant discomfort, even for the police, due to its direct attacks on law enforcement and calls for popular uprising. It is considered an important, politically complex album and a faithful representation of Afro-American society in the late 80s. **nigas por life (1991) - Controversial Second Album** : Released in 1991, this album was a commercial success and had significant repercussions, but it was also highly questionable. It escalated its lyrical content beyond their first album, moving past police brutality and drug dealing to include highly problematic discourses such as sexual violence, mistreatment of women, homophobic attitudes, and even racist sentiments among the band members themselves. The band actively contributed to the banalization of the words "nigga" and "bitch" on this album. Following its release, the band eventually dissolved due to numerous internal conflicts and significant questioning from within Afro-American culture regarding the controversial nature of its content.
