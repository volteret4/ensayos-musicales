**Merry Christmas Darling (1970)** : The lyrics for this Christmas song were originally written in 1944 by an 18-year-old Richard Puler for a girl he loved, intended as a Christmas present, but they broke up before he could give it to her. He later made a recording on a 78-rpm record but couldn't recall the singer. In the late 1960s, Puler, who was a director of the Cal State Long Beach choir and had been hired by Karen Carpenter's parents to give her voice lessons, collaborated with the Carpenters on the song. Richard Carpenter kept the lyrics and re-arranged the music, turning it into a Christmas classic. Critics, like John Wasserman and Kevin Henry, dismissed it as "overwhelmingly feeble-minded" and "overly sentimental," suggesting the band was merely "chasing money." Initially, it was released as a single in 1970 with "Santa Claus Is Comin' to Town" as the flip side, available only by mail order through the Carpenters' fan club for $1.25. It later appeared on a Goodyear Tire Company Christmas album in 1972 before finally being included on a Carpenters album, "Christmas Portrait," in 1978. Karen Carpenter's vocal performance on the track, longing for a distant loved one, was described by Melinda Newman of Billboard as "pure sublime perfection," conveying a mix of yearning, sadness, and hope, and it remains a "soundtrack for any college couples separated during the holiday break." A remastered version features on the "Christmas Portrait" album. "Her vocal on Merry Christmas darling, as she longs for her loved one, who is a way for Christmas a New Year's Day, is pure sublime perfection. She conveys the perfect mix of yearning, sadness and hope, since she knows even though it's unspoken, they will be joyfully reunited after the holidays." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 52 - 70s Christmas Music | https://www.ftr70.com/

**We've Only Just Begun (1970) – Former Bank Jingle** : This song, released in late 1970, started as a failed jingle for the Crocker National Bank of California, part of a campaign designed by Hal Reiner and Partners to appeal to young couples. Songwriter Paul Williams, struggling at the time, was tasked with writing the jingle, which initially had only two verses and a bridge. Richard Carpenter saw the TV commercial, got excited, and tracked Williams down, leading to a finished version of the song. It sold a million copies, spent seven weeks at number one, ten weeks in the top ten, and earned the Carpenters two Grammy Awards. "This song, this giant hit, this multi-million dollar success started as a failed jingle in a bank commercial." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk ← the-carpenters
