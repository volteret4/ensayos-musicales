# artist - The Carpenters

## members
- Paul Williams
- Richard Carpenter

## songs
**We've Only Just Begun (1970) – Former Bank Jingle** : This song, released in late 1970, started as a failed jingle for the Crocker National Bank of California, part of a campaign designed by Hal Reiner and Partners to appeal to young couples. Songwriter Paul Williams, struggling at the time, was tasked with writing the jingle, which initially had only two verses and a bridge. Richard Carpenter saw the TV commercial, got excited, and tracked Williams down, leading to a finished version of the song. It sold a million copies, spent seven weeks at number one, ten weeks in the top ten, and earned the Carpenters two Grammy Awards. "This song, this giant hit, this multi-million dollar success started as a failed jingle in a bank commercial." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk

## curiosities
**Crocker National Bank Campaign Failure** : The "We've Only Just Begun" jingle was created for a Crocker National Bank of California campaign aimed at young couples seeking loans. While the TV commercial featuring the jingle was popular, it did not achieve its desired effect as the many young people interested in loans lacked the necessary collateral. Consequently, Crocker suspended the campaign. "So the whole thing was a flop. And Crocker suspended the campaign." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk
**Jingle Licensing to Recoup Costs** : After Crocker National Bank suspended its campaign, the agency Hal Reiner and Partners, rather than taking a financial hit for the work done, attempted to recoup costs by licensing the "We've Only Just Begun" jingle to other banks. This move inadvertently led to Richard Carpenter discovering the song. "Rather than take the financial hit for all the work, writing and partners had done, they tried to recoup as much as they could by licensing the jingle to other banks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk
