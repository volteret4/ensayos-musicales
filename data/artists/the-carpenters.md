**We've Only Just Begun (1970) – Former Bank Jingle** : This song, released in late 1970, started as a failed jingle for the Crocker National Bank of California, part of a campaign designed by Hal Reiner and Partners to appeal to young couples. Songwriter Paul Williams, struggling at the time, was tasked with writing the jingle, which initially had only two verses and a bridge. Richard Carpenter saw the TV commercial, got excited, and tracked Williams down, leading to a finished version of the song. It sold a million copies, spent seven weeks at number one, ten weeks in the top ten, and earned the Carpenters two Grammy Awards. "This song, this giant hit, this multi-million dollar success started as a failed jingle in a bank commercial." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk ← the-carpenters

## curiosities
**"Ice Cream" Body of Work** : Lester Bangs, in his 1971 review, described the Carpenters' body of work as akin to "ice cream," characterizing it as "pleasant and mildly bracing." "And he described the body of work by Karen and Richard Carpenter as being like ice cream, quote pleasant and mildly bracing." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 34 - A 70s Pop Manifesto - Finding Meaning in 70s Pop | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-34-a-70s-pop-manifesto-finding-meaning-in-70s-pop
**"White Middle Class American Kids" Identity** : Richard Carpenter articulated the band's identity by stating, "we were white middle class American kids. We wrote like that, we sang like that, we dressed like that, we lived like that," in response to criticism that their music was boring. He emphasized that while they were pop, they were not "bland, unimaginative pop music." "Richard said of the Carpenter's, we were white middle class American kids. We wrote like that, we sang like that, we dressed like that, we lived like that." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 34 - A 70s Pop Manifesto - Finding Meaning in 70s Pop | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-34-a-70s-pop-manifesto-finding-meaning-in-70s-pop
**Complaint Against Sex Pistols** : As artists on A&M Records, The Carpenters complained about the label's decision to sign the Sex Pistols, contributing to the latter's rapid termination from the label. "And when other A&M artists like the Carpenters complained, the pistols were dropped." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wYuNolSDLFM ← the-carpenters
**Crocker National Bank Campaign Failure** : The "We've Only Just Begun" jingle was created for a Crocker National Bank of California campaign aimed at young couples seeking loans. While the TV commercial featuring the jingle was popular, it did not achieve its desired effect as the many young people interested in loans lacked the necessary collateral. Consequently, Crocker suspended the campaign. "So the whole thing was a flop. And Crocker suspended the campaign." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk ← the-carpenters
**Jingle Licensing to Recoup Costs** : After Crocker National Bank suspended its campaign, the agency Hal Reiner and Partners, rather than taking a financial hit for the work done, attempted to recoup costs by licensing the "We've Only Just Begun" jingle to other banks. This move inadvertently led to Richard Carpenter discovering the song. "Rather than take the financial hit for all the work, writing and partners had done, they tried to recoup as much as they could by licensing the jingle to other banks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk ← the-carpenters
**Karen Carpenter's Drumming Prowess** : Karen Carpenter displayed her drumming skills in a 1976 TV special, performing a drum solo across multiple kits with various percussion instruments. Despite the band's adult contemporary sound, she was an "awesome drummer" and considered herself primarily a drummer who happened to sing, rather than a singer who played drums. Her performance challenged perceptions of women playing drums at the time. "Karen believed that she was a drummer who just happened to sing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9GHpC0kCgHY ← the-carpenters
**Late Scopatone Films** : The Carpenters were among the artists who continued to make films for Scopatone machines even as their popularity declined after 1967. The last Scopatone film was made in 1978, highlighting the machine's prolonged but tapering existence. "The Carpenters and Abba made films for the machines." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The Rise and Fall and Future of the Music Video - Part 1 | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lqd6IbUJ7tg&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG ← the-carpenters
**Lester Bangs Concert Review (1971)** : Rock critic Lester Bangs reviewed a Carpenters concert in 1971 and, predictably, did not enjoy the show, primarily due to the band's lack of stage presence and the laid-back attitude of the concert-goers, rather than the music itself. He did, however, appreciate their song "We've Only Just Begun." "The rock critic Lester Banges was given the task of reviewing a concert by the Carpenter's in 1971.
