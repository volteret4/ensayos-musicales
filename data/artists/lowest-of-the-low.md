**Shakespeare My Box (Box Set) - Career Retrospective** : This massive box set comprehensively collects all of Lowest of the Low's albums on vinyl, accompanied by posters, stickers, and other memorabilia. It serves as a celebration of their entire discography, with a strong focus on their iconic debut. "Plus, there's a massive box set called Shakespeare My Box, which collects together all lowest of the low albums on vinyl, together with posters and stickers and other things that you find in a box set." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=22SSwvfRMVg
**Shakespeare My Butt (1991) - Influential Indie Debut** : Released in late 1991, this debut album was critically acclaimed, frequently appearing in polls as one of the finest rock albums in Canadian history. It achieved remarkable sales of over 50,000 copies for an indie record and significantly influenced many emerging Canadian bands of the 1990s. "The result was a record called Shakespeare My But, which was released in late 1991." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=22SSwvfRMVg
**Sorted Fiction (2004) - Studio Album** : This studio album was released in 2004, indicating the band's continued output and commitment to creating new material following their reunion. It further cemented their policy of not categorizing periods of low activity as "breakups." "There was a new studio album called Sorted Fiction in 2004." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=22SSwvfRMVg

## curiosities
**Early 1990s Prime Time Success** : Lowest of the Low was among the Canadian bands that got "a shot at prime time" in the early 1990s. "The early 90s were pretty amazing with a long list of Canadian bands getting a shot at prime time. We had moist, the watchman, lowest of the low, sons of freedom, grapes of wrath, rhymes with orange, pure Gandarva's limb lifter." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U2kYleeMnl8
**Evolution from Popular Front** : Lowest of the Low originated from an 80s band called Popular Front. They attempted to secure a major record deal as Popular Front, and again in 1991 as Lowest of the Low, but were consistently rejected by major labels. "They evolved out of an 80s band called Popular Front and tried to get a major record deal, but were rejected by used to everybody." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=22SSwvfRMVg
**Post-Can Rock Revolution Breakup and Reform** : Lowest of the Low were among the Canadian bands that broke up after the 1990s but later reformed due to public demand, demonstrating the lasting impact and fan loyalty cultivated during the Can Rock Revolution. "Others broke up, like I'm on the earth, and the real statics and lowest of the low, only to reform later due to public demand." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U2kYleeMnl8
**Post-Flop Breakup and Hiatus** : Following the commercial and critical failure of their 1994 album *Halusagini*, the band broke up, and its members maintained no contact for approximately six years. This period represented a significant hiatus in their collective career. "So the band broke up, and nobody spoke to each other for about six years." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=22SSwvfRMVg
**Reunion and "Never Break Up" Policy** : In 2000, offers for a reunion emerged, prompting the band to reform. They subsequently adopted a strategy to "never call any low in activity a breakup," opting instead to reconvene periodically for performances and new recordings. "But then in 2000, some offers for a reunion started coming in. And that was enough to kick off a period where the band decided to never call any low in activity a breakup." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=22SSwvfRMVg
**Rise in 1992** : Lowest of the Low began their rise in 1992, becoming part of a significant wave of new Canadian artists. Their emergence contributed to the growing diversity and popularity of the Canadian music scene in the early 90s. "1992, Sons of Freedom, Boot Sauce, Lowest of the Low, More Hip, More Barodicad Ladies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6PGgNCaW4xo
**Risky Indie Path in Early 90s** : Lowest of the Low chose to persist as an indie group in the early 1990s, a decision considered risky when major label signings were the prevailing benchmark of a band's quality. They consciously embarked on a complete do-it-yourself path. "Nevertheless, they soldered on as an indie group, which back in the early 90s was a really risky thing to do." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=22SSwvfRMVg
**Self-Sabotage of Major Label Deals** : Despite receiving major label offers after the success of *Shakespeare My Butt*, the band candidly admitted to "self-sabotaging" these opportunities because they disapproved of the plans presented to them. This decision led them to remain an indie group. "Although the band does admit that there was interest, but they kind of self-sabotage that whole approach." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=22SSwvfRMVg
