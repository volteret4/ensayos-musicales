## albums
**The Earth Is Not a Cold Dead Place (2003)** : This is considered their biggest album, a concept album featuring what they describe as love songs. "The biggest album, and probably have to go with The Earth is not a cold dead place from 2003." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
**Those Who Tell the Truth Shall Die, Those Who Tell the Truth Will Live Forever (2001)** : Released on September 4, 2001, this album's liner notes featured a picture of an airplane with the text "this plane will crash tomorrow," an idea they had been developing for about a year. After 9/11, false rumors spread about its release date and content, associating it with the tragedy. "On September 4, 2001, they released an album entitled Those Who Telled The Truth Shall Die, Those Who Telled The Truth Will Live Forever." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY

## curiosities
**9/11 Conspiracy Theory Involvement** : The band became entangled in a 9/11 conspiracy due to their album *Those Who Tell the Truth Shall Die, Those Who Tell the Truth Will Live Forever*, released on September 4, 2001. Its liner notes featured an airplane image with "this plane will crash tomorrow." Post-9/11, false rumors circulated about a September 10 release date and a song titled "This Plane Will Crash Tomorrow." "One more thing about explosions in the sky. They were caught in a 9-11 conspiracy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
**Airport Security Incident** : Following the 9/11 events, bassist Michael James was stopped by airport security when they discovered one of his guitars was inscribed with "This Plane Will Crash Tomorrow," a reference to the controversial album art. "But that didn't stop bass player Michael James from being stopped by airport security when they discovered one of his guitars was inscribed with This Plane Will Crash Tomorrow." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
**Extensive Media Contributions** : The band has contributed music to numerous media projects, including the 2004 film *Friday Night Lights*, various BBC programs, documentaries, TV shows like *CSI*, and video games such as *Street Fighter* and *Major League Baseball 2K12*. "You may know them from the music from the 2004 film Friday Night Lights, along with a bunch of other movies. They've also contributed music to some BBC programs, a series of documentaries, TV shows like CSI, and video games such as Street Fighter, and Major League Baseball 2K12." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
**Formation and Instrumentation (1999)** : Formed in Texas in 1999, the band originally consisted of four members: three guitars and drums, with bass and keyboards occasionally integrated into their sound. "There are four members who first got together in 1999, three guitars and drums, but bass and keyboards creep in from time to time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
**Musical Style** : Explosions in the Sky is known for its entirely instrumental compositions, characterized by significant dynamics, masterful crescendos, and the inclusion of melodic hooks. "Everything is instrumental with lots of dynamics. They are masters of the crescendo, yet they don't forget to include hooks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
