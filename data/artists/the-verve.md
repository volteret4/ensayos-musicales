## songs
**Bittersweet Symphony (1997)** : Written by Richard Ashcroft, this song was inspired by a five-note progression from Andrew Loog Oldham Orchestra's 1965 cover of The Rolling Stones' "The Last Time." The Verve initially licensed the snippet from Abkco Music (controlled by Allen Klein) for 50% of the song's earnings. However, Abkco later sued, claiming more of the sample was used than agreed, and Andrew Loog Oldham also sued for $1.7 million in royalties. Under duress, The Verve surrendered 100% of the publishing to Abkco and Oldham, leading to the songwriting credits being changed to "Ashcroft Jagger Richards." Abkco subsequently licensed the recording to Nike for a TV commercial, which was seen as "selling out" and severely damaged the band's artistic integrity with their indie fanbase, despite it not being their decision. The song was nominated for a Grammy, with the songwriters listed as "Jagger Richards," and the original five-note bit was actually written by orchestral arranger David Whitaker. "Richard Ashcroft of the Verve came up with a song called Bittersweet Symphony." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AcADOuQUiAQ
**Love Is Noise (2008)** : This was the comeback single released on August 10, 2008, approximately 14 months after the band initially announced their reunion. "This is the comeback single. It's the verve and love is noise." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJGFUlQTUhQ

## curiosities
**Brian Cannon Design Work** : Brian Cannon, the art director who later designed Oasis's debut album "Definitely Maybe," had previously created artwork for The Verve. His designs for The Verve impressed Noel Gallagher, leading to their subsequent collaboration. "Noal liked what Brian had done with the verb around that time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tbKuJR0cPs
**Copyright Dispute and Loss of Publishing Rights** : The Verve faced a significant legal battle over their song "Bittersweet Symphony," stemming from a licensed sample. Despite an initial agreement for 50% of earnings, Abkco Music (controlled by Allen Klein) and Andrew Loog Oldham sued, leading to The Verve being forced to surrender 100% of the song's publishing rights, resulting in Mick Jagger and Keith Richards being credited as co-writers and receiving all royalties. "Alright, give us 100% of the money made from this song or we will force you to recall every single copy of the song an album from every store and radio station and whatever worldwide." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AcADOuQUiAQ
**Damage to Artistic Integrity** : After losing control of "Bittersweet Symphony," the song was licensed by Abkco to Nike for a TV commercial in 1997. This move, perceived as the "height of selling out" at the time, severely damaged The Verve's artistic integrity in the eyes of their indie fanbase, even though the band had no control over the decision. "This destroyed the Verve's artistic integrity in the eyes of their indie fan base." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AcADOuQUiAQ
**Eight-Year Separation and Reunion (2007)** : For eight years following their 1999 breakup, all members were adamant that a reunion would not happen. However, Nick McCabe began working with drummer Pete Salisbury, which prompted Richard Ashcroft to reconsider. Bass player Simon Jones was available, but guitarist Simon Tongue was deliberately excluded. The reunion was announced on June 26, 2007, after a period of eight years, one month, and 30 days apart. "For the next eight years, everyone was adamant that the verve would not reunite. The time tends to mellow people out." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJGFUlQTUhQ
**First Hiatus (1995-1996)** : From September 1995 to some time in 1996, the band experienced a period that appeared to be a dissolution but was later considered a hiatus rather than a formal breakup. "There was a time from September 1995 to some time in 1996 where it looked like they were done, but they weren't." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJGFUlQTUhQ
**First Official Breakup (1999)** : The band's proper breakup was officially announced on April 28, 1999, though they had effectively ceased to exist since their final show in Ireland on August 29, 1998, which was performed with only most of the band members. "The proper breakup of the verve came on April 28th of 1999. That's when the official announcement was made." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJGFUlQTUhQ
**Grammy Credit Discrepancy** : "Bittersweet Symphony" was nominated for a Grammy Award for Best Song, but the ballot controversially listed the songwriters as "Jagger Richards," reflecting the outcome of the publishing dispute rather than Richard Ashcroft's original composition. "And the ballot listed the songwriters of Bitter Sweet Symphony as Jagger Richards." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AcADOuQUiAQ
**Guitarist's Exit Before First Breakup (1998)** : On June 7, 1998, after a show in Rysho, Dusseldorf, Germany, singer Richard Ashcroft had a physical altercation with guitarist Nick McCabe, resulting in McCabe suffering a broken hand. McCabe subsequently decided to leave the band and was replaced by session guitarist B.J. Cole for the remaining tour commitments. "Which face took a pounding and Nick ended up with a broken hand. And with that, Nick decided he was done." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJGFUlQTUhQ
