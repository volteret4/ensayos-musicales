**Death and Legacy** : Ben Dorsi passed away on September 17th, 2017, at the age of 92. At the time of his death, he held the distinction of not only being considered the world's first roadie but also the oldest working roadie in the industry. "Ben Dorsi died on September 17th, 2017 at the age of 92. Not only was he the first Rody, but he was also the oldest working one." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZVGnSVfed9s
**Early Life and Career** : Hailing from San Antonio, Texas, Ben Dorsi began his career in show business by working for the Ice Capades. After a stint in the Navy, he moved to California, where he held various jobs including a gardener and John Wayne's chauffeur. "Dorsi was from San Antonio, Texas, and he began his career in show business by working for the ice capades." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZVGnSVfed9s
**Extensive Work History** : Throughout his six-decade career, Ben Dorsi worked for numerous prominent artists, starting with Hank Thompson, then a young Elvis Presley, followed by Johnny Cash, Patsy Cline, Kris Kristofferson, Merle Haggard, and Willie Nelson. **Pioneer Roadie** : Ben Dorsi is widely considered by those who study the history of road crews to be the very first roadie of the modern era. He was known for his big heart and his habit of calling people he liked "Lovie." "Those who study the history of roadcrues tend to point to Ben Dorsi, who also went by the name Lovie because he had a really big heart and tended to call people he liked Lovie." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZVGnSVfed9s
**Start with Hank Thompson** : On Halloween night in 1950, Ben Dorsi began a new job traveling with honky-tonk western performer Hank Thompson to help set up his gear. This role marked the start of his dedicated career as a roadie, a gig he would continue for the next six decades.
