## curiosities
**Childhood Drumming Ambition** : As a big fan of The Beatles, he acquired a snare drum and hi-hats by having his mom trade supermarket stamps, allowing him to play along to Beatles records. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo
**Expanded Drum Kit** : At age 13, his grandmother gave him a full drum set, which he eventually expanded to be as large as the one played by his drumming hero, Keith Moon of The Who. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo
**Pivotal Figure in Punk Rock** : Became one of the most influential and important figures in the history of punk rock after transitioning from drums to lead vocals for The Ramones. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo
**Posthumous Impact on Alan Cross** : His death "really, really bothered" Alan Cross, indicating a significant personal connection to his work. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TDwagoyMvck
**Punk Rock Afterlife for Christmas Baby Please Come Home** : Gave the 1963 classic by Darlene Love a "punk rock after life" approximately 20 years prior to 2020. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nYPGQiDL5bw
**Renaming and Band Formation** : Originally Jeff Hyman, he adopted the name Joey Ramone when the band members (himself, John Cummings, and Douglas Colvin) adopted the surname Ramone for the newly formed group. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo
**Transition to Lead Vocalist** : Initially the drummer and singer for his band Sniper, he moved to lead vocals in early 1974 after forming a new group with John Cummings and Douglas Colvin, as his drumming was very loud and fast, and he was a better singer than the other two. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo
