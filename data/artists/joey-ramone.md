## songs
**Judy Is a Punk (Early 1974)** : Written by Joey Ramone when he was still playing drums for The Ramones in early 1974.

## curiosities
**Childhood Drumming Ambition** : As a big fan of The Beatles, he acquired a snare drum and hi-hats by having his mom trade supermarket stamps, allowing him to play along to Beatles records.
**Expanded Drum Kit** : At age 13, his grandmother gave him a full drum set, which he eventually expanded to be as large as the one played by his drumming hero, Keith Moon of The Who.
**Pivotal Figure in Punk Rock** : Became one of the most influential and important figures in the history of punk rock after transitioning from drums to lead vocals for The Ramones.
**Posthumous Impact on Alan Cross** : His death "really, really bothered" Alan Cross, indicating a significant personal connection to his work.
**Renaming and Band Formation** : Originally Jeff Hyman, he adopted the name Joey Ramone when the band members (himself, John Cummings, and Douglas Colvin) adopted the surname Ramone for the newly formed group.
**Transition to Lead Vocalist** : Initially the drummer and singer for his band Sniper, he moved to lead vocals in early 1974 after forming a new group with John Cummings and Douglas Colvin, as his drumming was very loud and fast, and he was a better singer than the other two.
