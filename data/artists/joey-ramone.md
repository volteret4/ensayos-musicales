## curiosities
**Death from Lymphoma (2001)** : Joey Ramone, considered by some to be perhaps the most influential punk rocker ever, died after a long battle with lymphoma. His illness had been an "open secret" for years, and he had seemed stable until a critical incident in late 2000. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9T9wi0vEvc
**Early Drumming & Influences – Beatles and Keith Moon** : Growing up, Jeff Hyman (later Joey Ramone) was a big fan of The Beatles. He acquired a snare drum and high hats by trading supermarket stamps his mom collected, allowing him to play along with Beatles records. At 13, his grandmother gave him a full drum set, which he expanded to match the size of Keith Moon's kit, who was his drumming hero. "Jeff got his mom to trade her stamps for a snare drum and a set of high hats so he could play along with his Beatles records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo
**Emotional Impact of Death** : Joey Ramone's death was deeply felt by many people, underscoring the profound and often unacknowledged importance of The Ramones in the history of rock and roll. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9T9wi0vEvc

**Final Decline and Death (2001)** : Following a brief release in February 2001, Joey Ramone returned to Presbyterian Hospital in New York. As his hip healed, the cancer aggressively returned, systematically destroying his immune system and leading to severe infection. He never left the hospital again, and his family was called to his room on Sunday, April 15, 2001, where he passed away at 2:40 PM. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9T9wi0vEvc
**Hip Break Incident (2000)** : On December 30, 2000, while walking down a street in New York City, Joey Ramone fell and broke his hip. This severe injury necessitated taking him off his chemotherapy treatment so he could undergo surgery. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9T9wi0vEvc
**Influential Punk Figure – Original Drummer** : Joey Ramone is recognized as one of the most influential and important figures in the history of punk rock. His foundational role in The Ramones began as the band's original drummer, before he transitioned to lead vocals. "Joey Ramon one of the most influential and important figures in the history of punk rock Was actually a drummer." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo
**Transition from Drummer to Singer – Founding The Ramones** : In early 1974, Jeff Hyman was playing drums and singing in a band called Sniper. He then connected with John Cummings (guitar) and Douglas Colvin (bass), forming a new group that played its first gig on March 30, 1974. However, Hyman's drumming was too loud and fast for the band, and he proved to be a better singer than his bandmates. He moved to frontman, adopting the name Joey Ramone, while a friend named Tommy took over on a $100 drum set. "Jeff moved down front and became the singer and a friend named Tommy was forced to keep time using a hundred dollar drum set." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo
