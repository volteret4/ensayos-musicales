## curiosities
**Backstage Epiphany** : At the Alan Freed show, after trying to get autographs backstage, King and her date were accidentally let in through the stage door. Seeing the performers casually hanging out backstage solidified her ambition. It wasn't to be a performer herself, but to be "part of that world, someone that those performers knew and respected." "She knew then, not that she wanted to be a performer herself, but that she wanted to be part of that world, someone that those performers knew and respected." ← Episode 89： ＂Will You Love Me Tomorrow？＂ by the Shirelles | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Kns2HGcuSg
**Child Prodigy and Relative Pitch** : From a very early age, Carole King displayed exceptional musical talent. As a child, her father would use her talent as a party trick: she would turn to the wall while he played notes on the piano, and she would call out which ones he was playing. While her father claimed she had perfect pitch, she actually possessed relative pitch, meaning she could identify all other notes once given a starting note, which her father always provided as middle C. "In her autobiography, she talks about how, when she was a child, her dad would have her, as a party trick, turn to the wall while he played notes on the piano, and she called out which one he was playing." ← Episode 89： ＂Will You Love Me Tomorrow？＂ by the Shirelles | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Kns2HGcuSg
**College and Meeting Jerry Landis (Paul Simon)** : At 16, King, who had skipped multiple school years, enrolled in college while attempting to build her music career. There, she met Jerry Landis, who had a minor hit with "Hey School Girl" as one half of the duo Tom and Jerry. King and Landis collaborated on recording demos for other songwriters, sometimes reusing The Co-Sounds name, though they never wrote songs together. "So while she was trying to have a music career, she also went off to college aged 16. She had skipped multiple years in school, where she met someone else, who had had a minor hit." ← Episode 89： ＂Will You Love Me Tomorrow？＂ by the Shirelles | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Kns2HGcuSg
**Danny Carey Collaboration** : Drummer Danny Carey played with Carole King during his career. "And drummer Danny Carey was playing with everyone from a weirdo band called Green Jelly to Carol King." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JJGJXOoT2PI ← carole-king ← carole-king
**Discovery by Aldon Music** : King met Neil Sedaka at 1650 Broadway while trying to sell songs. Sedaka introduced her to Al Nevins and Don Kirshner, the owners of Aldon Music. After hearing songs she co-wrote with Goffin, Aldon signed Goffin and King to a three-year contract, offering advances of $1,000, $2,000, and $3,000 for consecutive years, offset against royalties. This was a significant sum for the young couple, transitioning them from soul-crushing day jobs to cubicle work at Aldon. "But then, when she was on one of her visits to 1650 Broadway to sell songs, King bumped into Sedaka, who told her she should come and meet Al Nevin's and Don Kirschner, the owners of Aldon Music." ← Episode 89： ＂Will You Love Me Tomorrow？＂ by the Shirelles | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Kns2HGcuSg
**Early Performances** : By the age of eight, King had appeared in a few shows, including the nationally broadcast "Ted Mack Amateur Hour," where she performed in a duo with a friend. "By the age of 8 she had appeared in a couple of shows, including Ted Max Amateur Hour, which was a nationally broadcast show, performing in a duo with a friend." ← Episode 89： ＂Will You Love Me Tomorrow？＂ by the Shirelles | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Kns2HGcuSg
**First Meetings with Record Labels** : King first visited Atlantic Records, where Jerry Wexler and Ahmet Ertegun listened to her and encouraged her to return with more songs, which was not a rejection but also not the instant acceptance she hoped for. Her second stop was ABC-Paramount, where she met Ennio Costa. "The record label he mentioned to her specifically was Atlantic Records, so she tried that one first." ← Episode 89： ＂Will You Love Me Tomorrow？＂ by the Shirelles | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Kns2HGcuSg
**Inspiration at Alan Freed Show (1955)** : At 13, a date with Jules Zwick took King to an Alan Freed show at the Paramount in Brooklyn, where she saw R&B stars like LaVern Baker, B.B. King, Mickey Baker, and The Moonglows. This show was her introduction to a world of music far removed from the "white bread pop" of her upbringing. It was not primarily seeing the performers on stage that inspired her, but rather an accidental backstage experience. "Zwick took her to an Alan Fried show at the Paramount in Brooklyn, where she saw Le Verne Baker, BB King, Mickey Baker, The Moon Glows, and several other R&B stars of the period." ← Episode 89： ＂Will You Love Me Tomorrow？＂ by the Shirelles | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Kns2HGcuSg
**Massive Career Based on Piano Songs** : Carole King built a massive career, with much of her success stemming from songs composed on the piano. She is highlighted as an artist who utilized the piano as her central instrument for both songwriting and performance. "We do have Elton John and Billy Joel and Carol King. **Meeting Alan Freed for Advice** : King approached Alan Freed to play him her songs. Freed listened politely and advised her on how to become a professional songwriter for the R&B market, suggesting she find record label addresses in the phone book and explaining publishing contracts. He specifically mentioned Atlantic Records. "She decided that she was going to go and see Alan Friede and play him some of her songs. Friede listened to her politely, and explained to her how, at the time, one went about becoming a professional songwriter for the R&B market." ← Episode 89： ＂Will You Love Me Tomorrow？＂ by the Shirelles | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Kns2HGcuSg
**Meeting and Marriage to Gerry Goffin** : While in college, King met Jerry Goffin, who would become her first husband. She was initially attracted to his appearance, as he resembled an ideal boyfriend drawing she had clipped from a magazine. Goffin initially disliked rock and roll, viewing its lyrics as vacuous, but became eager to write with King when he learned she sold songs. By age 17, they were married and expecting their first child, supplementing Goffin's day job income with occasional $25 songwriting advances. "Someone else she met while at college was the man who was to rick on her first husband, another Jerry, Jerry Goughin." ← Episode 89： ＂Will You Love Me Tomorrow？＂ by the Shirelles | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Kns2HGcuSg
**Self-Taught Orchestration** : Despite having no prior experience writing for an orchestra, Carole King decided to write the string arrangement for The Shirelles' "Will You Love Me Tomorrow." She borrowed a book on arrangement from the library, quickly studied it, and completed the entire string arrangement overnight. "She'd never written for an orchestra before, so she got a book and arrangement out of the library, and looked through it quickly before writing the string arrangement overnight." ← Episode 89： ＂Will You Love Me Tomorrow？＂ by the Shirelles | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Kns2HGcuSg
**Signing a Recording Contract with ABC-Paramount** : At ABC-Paramount, Ennio Costa not only offered King a publishing contract but also, to her surprise, a recording contract as a solo artist. She was disappointed he wasn't interested in signing her group, The Co-Sounds, but still signed immediately. Her initial solo singles, primarily her own compositions, did not achieve significant sales, partly due to her perceived weakness as a lyricist. "She was flabbergasted to find that rather than just sign her to a publishing contract, he asked her to sign a recording contract as well." ← Episode 89： ＂Will You Love Me Tomorrow？＂ by the Shirelles | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Kns2HGcuSg
**Stage School and Al Pacino** : King briefly attended a stage school where one of her classmates was Al Pacino. She soon left, however, deciding she wasn't suited for the non-musical aspects of the curriculum, and returned to a regular high school. "She started attending a stage school, where one of her classmates was Al Pacino, but after a short while she left, deciding that she wasn't cut out for the non-musical aspects of the school, and went back to a normal high school where she formed her first group, The Co-Sounds, along with Swick." ← Episode 89： ＂Will You Love Me Tomorrow？＂ by the Shirelles | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Kns2HGcuSg
**Start in Songwriting** : King started writing songs after hearing Neil Sedaka, then with The Linktones, sing "While I Dream," a song he co-wrote with Howard Greenfield. Sedaka's group, The Tokens, got a record deal, prompting King to believe she could do the same. She discovered a talent for melodies but felt less confident in her lyrical abilities. "King figured that if he could do that, so could she. She started writing songs, and found she was good at melodies, but not particularly great at lyrics, but she still thought she was good enough to do something." ← Episode 89： ＂Will You Love Me Tomorrow？＂ by the Shirelles | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Kns2HGcuSg
