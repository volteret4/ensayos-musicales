**The Air That I Breathe (1972)** : This 1972 song was the subject of an infringement claim against Radiohead's "Creep." The co-writers of "The Air That I Breathe," Mike Hazelwood and Albert Hammond, were subsequently added to the songwriting credits for "Creep." **The Air that I Breathe (1974)** : The composers of "The Air that I Breathe" successfully sued Radiohead, claiming melodic similarities with Radiohead's 1993 song "Creep," leading to them receiving songwriting credits and a share of the royalties from "Creep." One of the composers is the father of Albert Hammond Jr. of The Strokes. ## curiosities
**First Kinks Tour Companions** : The Kinks' "first big tour" saw them playing on the bottom of the bill alongside The Hollies and The Dave Clark Five, with the latter two bands higher up in the lineup. "playing on the bottom of the bill with the Dave Clark Faye from the Hollies further up." ← Episode 119： ＂You Really Got Me＂ by the Kinks | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_XNJ6Qwmh_c
**Graham Nash's Intervention on Kinks' Stage Act** : When promoter Arthur Howes attempted to refine The Kinks' stage act for a more "showbiz shape," Graham Nash of The Hollies intervened. Nash "ordered him to leave the Kinks alone," stating that they were "okay as they are," thus preventing further changes to their raw performance style.
