## curiosities
**Early Influences** : Pat Smear's early listening habits, before punk rock, included progressive and mainstream rock artists like Yes, Queen, Alice Cooper, and David Bowie, alongside The Runaways. He later embraced punk rock when the scene became open to bands like The Germs who "didn't know how to play." "I was listening to yes. I was listening to Queen and a Alaskupper and David Bowie." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mh-qg1izM98
**The Germs' Sound Self-Perception** : Pat Smear recalls that when The Germs recorded their first proper show, known as "Germicide," in the summer of 1977, the band genuinely believed they sounded like Alice Cooper, despite their raw, emerging punk sound. He challenges listeners to identify an Alice Cooper element in their early work. "we think we sound like Alaskupper. I challenge you to go listen to it and tell me what you think." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mh-qg1izM98
