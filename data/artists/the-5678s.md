# artist - The 5678's

## genres
- Garage Rock
- Punk
- Rockabilly

## songs
**Woo Hoo (1996) (cover)** : The 5678's' biggest hit was a cover of "Woo Hoo," originally released by the rockabilly group The Rock-A-Teens in 1959. The 5678's covered it in 1996, and it gained significant international exposure when Quentin Tarantino picked it for the soundtrack of "Kill Bill Volume 1" in 2003, serving as an example of the minor Garage Rock revival of the mid-1990s. ## curiosities
**All-Female Tokyo Trio** : Formed in Tokyo in 1986, The 5678's are an all-female trio. They incorporated elements of Garage Rock, Surf Rock, straight-ahead Punk, and Rockabilly into their music, releasing multiple albums and singles. Their biggest hit, a cover of "Woo Hoo," gained significant traction when it was featured in Quentin Tarantino's "Kill Bill Volume 1." "This is an all-female trio that emerged out of Tokyo in 1986, taking elements of garage rock, surf rock, straight ahead punk, and rockabilly, and releasing a number of albums and singles." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UpWz3X33vSY
