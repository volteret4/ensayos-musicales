## albums
**Vivadixie Submarine Transmission Plot (1996)** : This record, released in 1996, was Sparklehorse's debut album and featured their only alt-rock hit, "Sunday I Will Treat You Good." The album's success led to Sparklehorse being invited on a European tour with Radiohead, an event that unfortunately marked a turning point for frontman Mark Linkous. ## curiosities
**Drug Overdose and Its Aftermath** : During a European tour with Radiohead following the release of their debut album, Mark Linkous suffered a severe overdose of antidepressants, Valium, and heroin, exacerbated by alcohol consumption. He was found 14 hours later in his London hotel bathroom, having passed out with his legs pinned, which led to a build-up of potassium that caused his heart to stop. Although revived and undergoing surgery, he lost the use of both legs for six months, requiring a wheelchair, and developed kidney failure, necessitating dialysis for the rest of his life. "Here Mark Linkhouse O.D. and he depressants, valium, and heroin all washed down with a lot of alcohol." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SOAL0GODN3M
**Frontman's Reputation** : Sparklehorse was led by Mark Linkous, a highly respected songwriter from Richmond, Virginia. He garnered admiration from various notable artists and bands, including The Flaming Lips, Metric, The Silver Sun Pickups, and Patti Smith. "The group was Sparkle Horse. And they were led by a very well-regarded songwriter from Richmond, Virginia, named Mark Linkhouse." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SOAL0GODN3M
**Impact on Songwriting** : Mark Linkous's near-fatal overdose and subsequent health issues profoundly impacted his perspective and songwriting, which became noticeably "darker and more somber." Despite his struggles, he continued to create music for Sparklehorse and contributed to various TV and film projects. "This affected his outlook in a songwriting, which became darker and more somber." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SOAL0GODN3M
**Tragic Suicide** : Mark Linkous's battle with depression persisted despite his continued musical work. While in the process of divorcing his wife of 19 years, he took his own life in Knoxville, Tennessee. He used a rifle, aiming it at his heart, and at the time of his death, he had a blood alcohol level of .43, with various antidepressants and anti-anxiety medications found in his system. "Then, while on the process of a divorce from his wife of 19 years, he took a rifle, went for a walk, sat down on an alley in Knoxville, Tennessee, aimed the gun at his heart and pulled the trigger." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SOAL0GODN3M
