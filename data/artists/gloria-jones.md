# artist - Gloria Jones

## genres
- Mid 60s Soul Music
- Northern Soul

## songs
**Tainted Love (1964)** : The original version of the song from the Cincinnati-born singer, released in 1964, later covered by Soft Cell. "This is the original version from 1964 by the Cincinnati-born singer Gloria Jones." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dnQ1pNbIUjw
**Tainted Love (1965)** : Written by Ed Cobb, Gloria Jones released this song in May 1965 as the B-side to "My Bad Boys Come In Home," which was intended to be a major single. Both tracks flopped and the record disappeared until it was rediscovered years later by a British club DJ. "She released it in May 1965 as a B side to what was supposed to be a big single called My Bad Boys Come In Home." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw
**Tainted Love (Mid 1960s)** : This song by Gloria Jones was a significant Northern Soul hit in the mid-1960s, a type of music that found a dedicated audience and "a home" in cities like Manchester and Liverpool. "Gloria Jones with the kind of mid 60s soul music that found a home in places like Manchester and Liverpool." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dZStbEe4Gkk

## curiosities
**Involved in Marc Bolan's Death (September 16, 1977)** : Gloria Jones was Mark Bolan's girlfriend and was driving the Mini Cooper when it crashed into a tree in southwest London on September 16, 1977, resulting in Bolan's death. "his girlfriend Gloria Jones drove their mini-cooper into a tree in southwest London." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw
**Involvement in Mark Bolan's Death (1977)** : On September 16, 1977, Gloria Jones was driving Mark Bolan's Mini home from a night out when she lost control of the car over a small bridge, hitting a fence post and a tree, which instantly killed Bolan. "This was all fine until September 16, 1977, when she was driving Bowlin's mini home from a night out and something went wrong over a small bridge and the car went out of control, hit a fence post and then a tree." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw
**Re-recording after Northern Soul Popularity (1973)** : After British club DJ Richard Seerling began playing her original B-side "Tainted Love" to great success in Northern Soul clubs, Gloria Jones re-recorded the song in 1973, but this new version also failed to gain traction. "Word got back to Gloria Jones who re-recorded the song and it failed again." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw
