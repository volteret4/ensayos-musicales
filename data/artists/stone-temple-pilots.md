## albums
**Core (1993) – Debut Record** : The band's debut album, released in 1993, which featured the hit song "Plush." The album's success marked the period when the band signed their major label record deal and began touring. "It's apparently a song about death, inspired by a newspaper story about a girl who'd been found dead somewhere in San Diego." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TDwagoyMvck
**Number 4 (1999) – Fourth Album** : This album was released on October 26, 1999, following the band's reunion and their entry into the studio with producer Brendan O'Brien in February 1999. The first song they recorded for the album was "Down." "It was released on October 26, 1999." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TDwagoyMvck
**Purple (1994) – Chart Performance** : "Purple" debuted at number one on the charts in the US, also reaching number one in Australia, number two in Canada, and number ten in the UK, indicating significant international success. "Hype was such that the album debuted at number one on the charts in the US, and made it as high as number one in Australia, number two in Canada, number ten in the UK." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TDwagoyMvck
**Purple (1994) – Second Album** : Recorded in Atlanta during a break in the band's touring schedule, this album was completed rather quickly in four weeks, partly due to Scott Wyland's drug use allowing him to avoid withdrawal symptoms. Released on June 7, 1994, it showcased a sound that was "less grunge and more straight ahead all rock" compared to their debut. "Purple was released on June 7th, 1994, and I remember when it came out thinking, wow, this sounds a lot different than the first album." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TDwagoyMvck
**Stone Temple Pilots (2018) – Self-Titled Album** : This self-titled album was released on March 16, 2018, featuring Jeff Gutt as the band's new lead singer. This marked a new chapter for the band, showcasing their first single with Gutt. "And on March 16th, 2018, Stone Temple Pod's version, what is this? Three, four? Release date, self-titled album." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vLZla5h4xKQ
**Tiny Music Songs from the Vatican Gift Shop (1996) – Sales Performance** : While "Tiny Music" produced big radio hits, its store sales were less impressive, selling about a third of what "Purple" achieved and a quarter of "Core," totaling approximately 2 million copies. "It sold about a third of what purple did and a quarter of the total they saw with core." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TDwagoyMvck
**Tiny Music Songs from the Vatican Gift Shop (1996) – Third Album** : Released on March 26, 1996, this album represented a further evolution in the band's sound, described as even more distinct from "Purple." The band aimed for a stripped-back, "glam-esque" feel, with Scott Wyland consciously attempting to emulate David Bowie in his melodies and lyrics. "Tiny Music was released on March 26, 1996." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TDwagoyMvck
