## songs
**Bad Moon Rising** : A song by John Fogarty that is often misheard, with the lyric "There's a bathroom on the right" instead of the correct line. "John Fogarty does not sing. There's a bathroom on the right in bad moon rising." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_YjwRApoJhA
**Old Man Down the Road (1985)** : This song from John Fogarty's 1985 solo album "Center Field" was the subject of a plagiarism lawsuit brought by Saul Zaentz, who alleged it sounded too similar to the CCR song "Run Through the Jungle," the copyright of which Zaentz owned. Fogarty owned "Old Man Down the Road." "While in Saul freaked out, got all bent out of shape, and then he sued John for plagiarism, alleging that a song on Center Field called Old Man Down the Road, which John owned, sounded just like an old CCR song called Run Through the Jungle, which Saul owned as the result of the contract dispute." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wYD_dIvYY5o
**Zanz Can't Dance (1985)** : This song from the 1985 "Center Field" album was clearly directed at John Fogarty's former boss, Saul Zaentz, the owner of Fantasy Records and the copyrights to Fogarty's CCR songs. "It featured a song called Zance Can't Dance. It was clearly directed at John's old boss." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wYD_dIvYY5o

## curiosities
**Contract Dispute and Copyright Transfer** : John Fogarty had given ownership of his Credence Clearwater Revival songs to Saul Zaentz, who ran CCR's record label, Fantasy Records, in order to get out of his contract, indicating a strong animosity between them. "Although John had written the songs for Credence, the copyrights, the ownership of the songs he had written, had been given over to a guy named Saul Zance." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wYD_dIvYY5o
**Courtroom Guitar Demonstration** : In his self-plagiarism lawsuit, John Fogarty brought his guitar to court and demonstrated to the judge and jury the differences between his solo song "Old Man Down the Road" and the CCR song "Run Through the Jungle." This demonstration was a key factor in the judge's decision to dismiss the case. "Fortunately, the judge in the case decided not to set a precedent and threw things out, largely because John brought his guitar to court and sat on the stand showing the judge and the jury the differences between the songs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wYD_dIvYY5o
**Plagiarism Lawsuit for Self-Plagiarism (1985)** : John Fogarty, founder and chief songwriter for Credence Clearwater Revival, was sued for plagiarism in 1985 after releasing his solo album "Center Field." The lawsuit alleged that songs on his solo album, specifically "Old Man Down the Road," stole melodies from old CCR songs like "Run Through the Jungle," the copyrights of which were owned by Saul Zaentz of Fantasy Records, not Fogarty himself. "Then, in 1985, John released a solo record called Center Field and it too did very, very well. But then John was served with a lawsuit alleging plagiarism." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wYD_dIvYY5o
