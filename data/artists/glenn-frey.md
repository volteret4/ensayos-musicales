## curiosities
**Death (January 18, 2016)** : Glenn Frey died on January 18, 2016, due to complications from intestinal surgery. "January 18th, Glenn Fry of the Eagles, complications from intestinal surgery." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k ← glenn-frey
**Early Supporter of "Beautiful Loser"** : Glenn Frey of Eagles was the first person to hear Bob Seger's song "Beautiful Loser" and encouraged him to record it. This endorsement was significant in the song's journey to becoming a track on Seger's 1975 album of the same name. "The first person to hear it who said you should record that was Glenn Fry of Eagles." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 55 - Heartland Rock is about More than the Heartland | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Formation of The Eagles** : Glenn Frey and Don Henley eventually formed The Eagles, a band that achieved significant success. "Of course, Glen Frye and Don Henley go on to form the Eagles." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 41 - The Impact of The Troubadour on 1970s Popular Culture | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-41-the-impact-of-the-troubadour-on-1970s-popular-culture
**Influenced by The Flying Burrito Brothers** : Glenn Frey, a founding member of The Eagles, was a regular attendee at The Flying Burrito Brothers' performances at The Troubadour in Los Angeles. Their music, particularly the album "Gilded Palace of Sin," had a significant influence on him and subsequently on the Eagles. "Glenn Fry, who Hillman said was a regular in the crowd at the Trubidor in Los Angeles where the burritos played." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 35 - Cosmic American Music - 70s Country Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-35-cosmic-american-music-70s-country-rock ← glenn-frey
**Meeting at The Troubadour** : Glenn Frey met Linda Ronstadt and Don Henley at The Troubadour bar, a significant encounter that subsequently led to the formation of The Eagles. "I met Bernie Leiden and G. Glenn Fry and Don Henley.
