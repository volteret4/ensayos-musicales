Themes include deep ecology and the empathy gap. "Both origin of symmetry from 2001 and black holes and revelations from 2006 were about astronomy and cosmology and quantum physics." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NbtZ_nfxb3I
**Showbiz (1999) – First Album Featuring Material from 1997 Demo** : The band's debut album included a more polished version of "Do We Need This?", which first appeared on a 1997 demo tape. **The Resistance (2009) – Album Source for Uprising** : This album contained the track "Uprising" which was the number one alt-rock track in the world at the beginning of the 2010s, having held that position since mid-October 2009. The album's success highlighted the band's continued relevance into the new decade. "From the resistance, this is muse and uprising." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Y6nHw5xx88
**The Resistance (2009) – Recorded Almost Entirely at Matt's Lake Como Villa** : The album, including "Uprising," was recorded largely at what Muse called Studio Bellini — Matt Bellamy's villa in the village of Moltrasio on Lake Como, Italy, once owned by Italian composer Vincenzo Bellini. George Clooney, Sting, and Richard Branson also have properties in the area. Winston Churchill once lived there and used the villa for painting. The location was also used in *Star Wars: Attack of the Clones* as the planet Naboo. Bellamy later put the two Lake Como apartments on the market for €2.5 million after splitting with his Italian fiancée. "Recorded almost entirely in Matt's villa on Lake Como in Italy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NbtZ_nfxb3I
**The Second Law (2012) – Themed After the Second Law of Thermodynamics** : The album takes the Second Law of Thermodynamics — involving the increasing entropy of the universe — as its conceptual framework. ## curiosities
**Conspiracy Theories and Scientific Obsessions – Bilderbergs, 9/11 Truthers, Alien Reptile Royals** : Bellamy at one time believed 9/11 was an inside job (he later recanted), is concerned about the Bilderberg Group and Trilateral Commission, and based "Unnatural Selection" on the Babylonian Brotherhood theory — the idea that the British Royal Family are descendants of giant alien lizards, as proposed by British writer David Icke. Right-wing conspiracy theorists began using Muse music in their internet videos, which greatly annoyed Bellamy — not because of the conspiracy usage but because he felt the right wing had "hijacked all the best conspiracies." A Muse fan-compiled reading list includes both fringe (Zecharia Sitchin's ancient alien theories) and legitimate scientific works (Brian Greene's *The Elegant Universe*, Orwell's *1984*). "He felt that the right wing had hijacked all the best conspiracies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NbtZ_nfxb3I
**Cryptic Fan Codes and Hidden Messages – Bicycles Hidden Across America (2005 Tour)** : On a 2005 tour, Muse printed coded set lists each night using fake song titles that were actually anagrams and cipher clues. Solving these led to email addresses, then to four autographed bicycles — the band's tour fitness bikes — hidden under abandoned railway bridges across America, with combination locks whose codes were embedded in the puzzles. One clue required fans to write "Christ" on their body, photograph it, and email the image to unlock the next clue; only four people worldwide solved that one. A harder puzzle directed the solver to a specific recording studio in England to claim a signed Muse guitar. For the launch of "United States of Eurasia," USB sticks were hidden in Berlin, Tokyo, Hong Kong, Moscow, Paris, and Dubai, each containing a code to enter on a website; once all codes were entered, parts of the new song were revealed. "Only four people worldwide succeeded in breaking that one." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NbtZ_nfxb3I
**Featured in Apple Commercials** : Muse's music has been part of Apple's commercial campaigns, illustrating the company's practice of integrating well-known bands into its advertisements for products like the iPod and iPhone. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sfie4YYncxk
**First Demo Tape (May 1995) – Cassette Worth ~$1,000 Today** : In May 1995, the band recorded four songs possibly under the guidance of Matt's father George ("Copyright Geo Bell Music" appears on the cassette label). The tape, titled *This Is a Muse Demo*, was distributed to promoters and venues across the northwest of England by their first manager Phil Corthals, securing more gigs and their first overseas show (in France). Very few copies were made; the cassette now sells for close to $1,000. "If you can find one, the asking price is close to $1,000." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NbtZ_nfxb3I
**Formation – Won Battle of the Bands as Rocket Baby Dolls (1994, Teignmouth)** : In 1994, at the Broadmeadow Sports Centre in Teignmouth, Devon (population ~15,000), 16-year-olds Matt Bellamy, Christopher Wolstenholme, and Dominic Howard competed in a battle of the bands as "Rocket Baby Dolls" (evolved from "Fixed Penalty," itself evolved from "Gothic Plague"). They entered halfheartedly, thinking the concept idiotic, but the violence of their performance drew such a strong crowd response that they won — surprising themselves enough to take music seriously. About a week later they renamed themselves Muse. "They played with such violence and because the crowd responded so well to this violence, they ended up winning." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NbtZ_nfxb3I
**George Bellamy (Matt's Father) – Played on "Telstar" (1962), Second British Song to Hit #1 in America** : In 1961, Matt Bellamy's father George Bellamy, then a 20-year-old country singer, answered an ad in *Melody Maker* placed by legendary UK producer Joe Meek, who was assembling a studio group called the Tornados. George played rhythm guitar on "Telstar," an instrumental single released summer 1962, capitalizing on the excitement around the launch of the Telstar communications satellite. It became just the second British song to reach number one on the American charts and the second instrumental to top charts on both sides of the Atlantic, selling over 5 million copies worldwide. Joe Meek subsequently "went extra crazy" and the Tornados disintegrated; George ended up working as a plumber. "Tell Star was a massive hit, becoming just the second British song to reach number one on the American charts." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NbtZ_nfxb3I
**Matt Bellamy's Car Theft Ring – Stole a Ford Escort from the Wrong Guy** : At 18, having moved from Teignmouth to Exeter to live above a porn store with a painter/drug dealer friend, Bellamy got involved in a small-time car theft ring, stealing cheap vehicles for modest profit. The scheme fell apart when he stole a Ford Escort belonging to someone dangerous, who showed up demanding £500 or he would harm Bellamy and his family. Lacking the money, Bellamy handed over the band's touring van. "So much for a career in Grand Theft Auto." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NbtZ_nfxb3I
**Name Origin – Occult Witchcraft Sessions with Three Girls** : The name "Muse" may have come from a teacher introducing Bellamy to the word, but the preferred explanation involves three girls from school who practiced witchcraft in abandoned houses, casting spells. Matt — who was attracted to one of them — tagged along and played guitar during the sessions. "He thought one of them was kind of hot, so he went with her and the other two friends to abandoned houses where they cast spells." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NbtZ_nfxb3I
**North American Breakthrough Delay** : Despite performing in arenas across Europe, Muse continued to play clubs in North America until the release of their fourth album, indicating a slower path to prominence on that continent. "They might have been playing arenas in Europe, but when it came to their fourth album, they were still playing clubs on this side of the Atlantic." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8sAxFvktx-A
