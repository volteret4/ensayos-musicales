**First Show October 17, 1975 – Pre-dates Sex Pistols and The Clash; 1980 Ontario Place Riot** : Teenage Head formed in Hamilton, Ontario, and played their first show on October 17, 1975 at Westdale High School — three weeks before the Sex Pistols played their first gig, and nine months before The Clash formed. They were a major force in Canadian punk, combining elements of the Ramones, New York Dolls, and 1950s rock and roll. Their profile peaked with a 1980 show at Ontario Place in Toronto where thousands tried to get in; what followed became an infamous riot. American radio programmers loved the music but balked at the band name (for obvious reasons), prompting a temporary rename to "Teenage Heads" for US purposes. Just before a crucial US showcase in September 1980, guitarist Gord Lewis was in a serious car accident, canceling the shows and closing the window of opportunity. Original singer Frankie Venom died of throat cancer in 2008; the band continues with Dave Rave on vocals more than 40 years later. "Long after most of their original punk rock contemporaries called it quits, or died off, Teenage Head still with us." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0X7TQMGgqbY
