**Controversial Legal Outcome of Death** : The "Percolito" was owned and captained by Mexican millionaire Guillermo Gonzales Nova, who initially admitted to being at the helm and was seen in handcuffs, but the story later changed to an employee, Jose Sen Yam, being the pilot. Despite eyewitnesses disputing this, Sen Yam, a deckhand without proper documents, was arrested and found guilty of "culpable homicide" under Mexican law, receiving a sentence of two years ten months. However, he avoided jail by paying a $90 USD fine and was ordered to pay just over $2,100 in restitution based on his salary, leading to widespread suspicion of a payoff for taking the blame. "Although he first apparently admitted to being at the helm with the time of the accident, he was seen being let away from the scene in handcuffs. The story later changed. The news story was that a 26-year-old employee named Jose Sen Yam was actually piloting the boat at the time of the accident, although there are a number of eyewitnesses who dispute that, including Diaz, the dive master." "Jose was arrested and found guilty of something known under Mexican law as culpable homicide, the sentence two years ten months. **Fatal Boating Accident in Cozumel (December 2000)** : On December 18, 2000, while scuba diving with her sons and dive master Ivan Diaz off Cozumel's Chunkanab reef, a national marine park restricted to power boats, Kirsty MacColl was struck and killed instantly by the propellers of a 31-foot motorboat named the "Percolito." The boat, powered by 315 horsepower engines, roared through the restricted area as MacColl surfaced, and she managed to push her 15-year-old son Jamie out of the way just before impact, sustaining severe head and chest injuries that virtually severed her body. "Just after 2pm, just as Kirstie came to the surface after the first of two planned dives, a 31-foot motorboat called the Percolito came roaring through this restricted area with its 315 horsepower engines. Kirstie was right in the way. She pushed her 15-year-old son Jamie out of the way just in time, but the boat hit her in the head and caused severe chest injuries. She was killed instantly by the props." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZhzjZixCcK8
**Justice for Kirsty Campaign** : Kirsty MacColl's family launched the "Justice for Kirsty" campaign, lobbying Mexican authorities, making applications to human rights councils, and securing support from U2 to pressure the government after a judge was found to have mishandled the case. Despite their efforts, the campaign closed in December 2009, having exhausted all possible avenues without achieving further legal recourse.
