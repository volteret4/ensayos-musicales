## songs
**She Sells Sanctuary (1985)** : This enduring alt-rock song from the 80s was the big single from The Cult's "Love" album. It was recorded in March 1985, and despite its success, the song was initially buried deep on the record, appearing as Track 9 on the CD (Side 2 on the album). "You didn't get to She Sells Sanctuary until Track 9 on the CD." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k9ok8M31-38
**She Sells Sanctuary - Drunk Drumming Incident** : During the recording of "She Sells Sanctuary," drummer Nigel Preston, who had a severe drinking problem, showed up intoxicated and struggled to play. Singer Ian Astbury and guitarist Billy Duffy gradually removed pieces of his drum set, leaving him with just a snare, bass drum, and hi-hats. This limitation forced a much more rhythmic, straight-ahead 4/4 beat with minimal snare fills, inadvertently creating the song's distinctive, effective drumming. "The result was a song that was much more rhythmic, much more straight ahead than anything to call it ever done." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k9ok8M31-38

## curiosities
**12-inch Remix Business** : The Cult was among the bands that engaged in the 12-inch remix trend, following the influence of New Order's "Blue Monday." This format became a key element of club culture and alternative music in the 1980s. "And so was Depeche Mode and The Cure and The Human League and Simple Minds and The Cult and Killing Joke and Erasure and Psychedelic Furs and Madness. They all got into the 12-inch business." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**Band Name Evolution** : Before settling on "The Cult," the band went by two other names: Southern Death Cult (for one album) and Death Cult (for another). By the end of 1983, they were simply "The Cult." "Up until that record came out, The Cult went by two other names." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k9ok8M31-38
**Collaboration with Bob Rock** : The Cult is listed among Bob Rock's clients. "We got the cult, Veruch assault, Erasmith, Motley crew, Ardady Pease, the Tea Party, Eric and High Fy, Afghan Wigs, David Lee Roth, the Conorline Crush, Cher." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QkSKxm_U9Yc
**Formed by Billy Duffy** : Guitarist Billy Duffy formed The Cult with Ian Astbury a couple of years after his brief tenure in The Nosebleeds with Morrissey. "Billy going on to form the cult with Ian Aspery a couple of years later." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vev8lzfW6Xo
**Later Commercial Success** : After their evolution from Southern Death Cult and Death Cult, The Cult achieved significant commercial success, selling millions of records in the late 1980s and early 1990s. "And yes, it is the cult that went on to sell millions of records in the late 80s and early 1990s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gd9sO7c3N1s
**Mark Brachecki as Video Drummer** : The drummer seen in the music video for "She Sells Sanctuary" was Mark Brachecki, who was on loan from the band Big Country, as Nigel Preston had already been fired. "The drummer we see in the video for Sanctuary is a dude named Mark Brachecki, who was on loan from the band Big Country." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k9ok8M31-38
**Nigel Preston's Drinking Problem** : Drummer Nigel Preston had a serious drinking problem that made him increasingly unreliable during recording sessions, often showing up late or too drunk to play. This unreliability directly led to the minimalist drumming on "She Sells Sanctuary." "Nigel however had a serious drinking problem and was becoming more and more unreliable." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k9ok8M31-38
**Nigel Preston's Firing and Death** : Although "Sanctuary" and the "Love" album were major hits, Nigel Preston was fired shortly after his parts were recorded due to his unreliability. He later died in 1992, but his drumming on "She Sells Sanctuary" remains a part of The Cult's legacy. "Nigel wasn't invited to stick around. He was fired soon after all his parts were recorded." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k9ok8M31-38
