## albums
**Greatest Hits (1988) – Last 8-Track Release** : This album, released in November of 1988, is considered the last album released by a record company on the 8-track format in the US and Canada. "The best answer seems to be Fleetwood Max Greatest Hits, which came out in November of 1988." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Epz5Sn151bk
**Greatest Hits (1997) – Last Major Label 8-Track Release** : Fleetwood Mac's "Greatest Hits" was the final major label album to be released on the 8-track format in 1997, marking the end of an era for that audio medium. "The last major label release on eight track was Fleetwood Max Greatest Hits in 1997." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BHkJFA0LMro

## songs

## curiosities
**Early Digital Recording Efforts** : Fleetwood Mac is cited as one of the groups who utilized early digital recording for their albums, reflecting the nascent stages of digital audio production where full end-to-end digital processing was not yet common. "There were plenty of other albums like that, Stevie Wonder, Christopher Cross, Fleetwood Mac, and a bunch of others." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yPok3MemDmE
**Partial Catalog Sale** : Four-fifths of Fleetwood Mac members have sold portions of their song catalogs, joining numerous other established artists in monetizing their musical assets for large sums. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sfie4YYncxk
**Past Royalty Income as Retirement Annuity** : In the "old days," groups like Fleetwood Mac could effectively retire because they reliably received large royalty checks every six months from sales of records they had released decades earlier. "And groups like the Eagles and Fleetwood Mac could retire because every six months or so. A big fat royalty check would arrive in the mail as the result of sales of records that they released decades earlier." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z7glrAoXtkA

**Publishing Share Sold by Stevie Nicks** : Stevie Nicks' share of Fleetwood Mac's publishing catalog was sold for about $100 million. "Stevie Nicks, her share of Fleetwood Max Publishing, was worth about $100 million" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pe40SqDrpZ0
**Reunion for Touring** : Fleetwood Mac was one of many bands from past decades that reunited in the 21st century, a trend largely motivated by financial incentives. With declining CD sales impacting artist revenues, returning to the touring circuit offered a lucrative way to appeal to nostalgic fan bases and fill large venues. "The list of reunions is long." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GYUuTbOvyak
