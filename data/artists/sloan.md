**Smeared (1992) - Geffen Debut** : The band's first record on Geffen/DGC, initially recorded in a house in Halifax for approximately $1,200 with modest aspirations of pressing 500-1000 CDs. After signing, it was remixed by Geffen and its sound was heavily influenced by Nirvana and noisy pop bands from England like My Bloody Valentine and Ride, characterized by loud guitars. In 1996, Sloan regained the rights to this album in Canada, bringing it under their Murder Records label. "I think a lot of the records that really influenced us for smeared was, you know, the best parts of Nirvana but also the best parts of what was going on in England at the time, like my bloody Valentine and ride and all those sort of noisy pop bands that were on creation records and everything." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE

"The original version of Underwhelmed from Sloan's debut release, which is called The Peppermint-eaky." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
**Twice Removed (1994) - Quieter Departure** : Sloan's second album, released in 1994, which marked a deliberate stylistic shift away from the fuzzy, loud guitar sound of "Smeared" towards a more poppy and quieter aesthetic. Influenced by Fleetwood Mac, The Velvet Underground's third album, and The Beatles, this move was seen by the band as "career suicide" and initially met with skepticism from Geffen Records, who suggested redoing it. Recorded in Lenny Kravitz's studio using his analog board and Beatles gear, it cost $120,000, a significant increase from their debut. Despite early commercial struggles, it became one of their most beloved albums, eventually selling as many copies as "Smeared," and was named the number one Canadian album of all time by Chart Magazine in 1996. Sloan later regained the rights to this album in Canada. "Sloan from their second record, twice removed. Which sounded substantially less fuzzy and more poppy than the first couple of records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE

## curiosities
**All Members Contribute Songs and Vocals** : Sloan is noted for its unique collaborative structure where every member of the band not only gets a turn at the microphone for lead vocals but also contributes songs to their albums. "not only does everyone get a turn at the mic, but everyone in the band also gets to contribute songs" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M1JTdUxOEMU
**American Market Attempts** : Sloan made multiple efforts to break into the lucrative American market. Their first attempt was in 1992 with the "Smeared" album on Geffen, Nirvana's label, but it fell apart due to label politics. A mid-1990s deal with The Enclave also failed when the label went out of business. Even in 2003, they tried again, managing only about two appearances on MTV's "120 Minutes." "Yes, we never had any kind of, you know, we were on 120 minutes on MTV in the States." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QB3nyFH_tRU
**Andrew Scott - Athletic and Charismatic** : Andrew Scott was a provincial track and field record holder in Nova Scotia before joining Sloan as the drummer. He was also voted one of Canada's 20 sexiest musicians. "And drummer Andrew Scott was the former holder of several provincial track and field records in Nova Scotia. He was also voted one of Canada's 20 sexiest musicians." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
**Aspiration for International Recognition and Box Sets** : Despite feeling they hadn't "gotten any bigger" in 1999, Sloan continues to record with the long-term goal of accumulating an "enormous body of work." They hope to eventually gain recognition from international publications like Mojo Magazine or Record Collector Magazine, which could lead to a retrospective box set and coffee table book, although they acknowledge the audience might currently be too small without a "huge hit." "Just to have an enormous body of work so that Mojo Magazine finally goes, we are sorry, we never wrote about you guys and you guys are unbelievable and you have all this stuff and nobody knows." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
**Band Name Origin** : The band's name, "Sloan," was derived from the nickname of Jason Larson, the bass player of the New Brunswick/Bedford band The Straight Jackets. Larson's French-speaking boss in a Montreal warehouse mispronounced "Slow One" as "Sloan," and the band adopted this nickname, featuring Larson's face on their debut EP, "The Peppermint-eaky." "Their bass player, his nickname, he worked... And his French boss used to call... The bass player, Jason Larson used to call him Slow One. And with the French accent, it just turned into Sloan." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
**Canada's Answer to Nirvana** : Sloan, from Halifax, was initially perceived as "Canada's answer to Nirvana," leading to Halifax being briefly considered "Seattle East" due to the widespread frenzy and saturation of grunge. "Sloan originally pegged as Canada's answer to Nirvana. And for a short while, Halifax was considered to be Seattle East." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tFBSrKMEZxI
**Canadian Band Challenges** : Operating as a rock band in Canada, the world's second-largest country, presents unique challenges due to its vast geography (nearly 10 million square kilometers spread over six time zones with just over three people per square kilometer). Touring bands become intimately familiar with the 7,604 kilometers of the Trans-Canada Highway. Despite 77% of the population living in cities, bands must work extremely hard to reach fans, especially while living next door to the largest net exporter of popular culture in the universe, the United States, which has "chewed up the domestic careers of countless bands." "Geography has chewed up the domestic careers of countless bands." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QB3nyFH_tRU

**Collaborative and Individualistic Songwriting** : Sloan operates with an egalitarian approach where all four members sing, write, and contribute creatively, splitting money equally. Andrew Scott's move to Toronto in 1993 fostered a more individualistic recording process, with members often playing all instruments on their own songs. This "parallel play" style was particularly evident on their album of the same name. "So we've all kind of evolved into this band that writes sort of individually." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
**Contractual Standoff with Geffen** : Facing a six-album contract with Geffen after their informal breakup, Sloan initially withheld the news from the label to avoid disadvantageous "broken up band" contractual clauses. Geffen eventually released them from their contract in an artist-friendly move, allowing Sloan to pursue other options. "As a band that was broken up, we were on Geffen and we were signed something like six records or something like that, which is not really in our favor." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
**Cult Phenomenon in the States** : Despite limited mainstream success in the United States, Sloan has achieved a "cult phenomenon" status there. American fans typically discover them from their college days when "Smeared" was on Geffen, or through independent research online, appreciating their pop and rock music. This underground following becomes "more and more underground" the further south one travels. "So we are a cult phenomenon in the States, which is fun. **Democratic Band Structure** : Sloan operates with an "uber democratic structure" where every member writes songs and sings lead vocals. All money is split equally among the four members, which has been crucial in keeping the band together and avoiding the conflicts over financial distribution that have plagued other bands, such as The Smiths. "Every single member of Sloan writes songs and every single member gets a chance to sing lead." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QB3nyFH_tRU

"Just to have an enormous body of work so that Mojo Magazine finally goes, we are sorry. We never wrote about you guys." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QB3nyFH_tRU

**Formation of Murder Records (Early 1992)** : Sloan established their own record label, Murder Records, in early 1992, to release their debut EP "The Peppermint-eaky" and bypass the extended timelines of major labels like Geffen/DGC, which projected a much later release date for their first album. The label allowed them to quickly put out music, facilitate touring, and later expanded to release records for other Halifax bands, fostering a communal scene. "So that's why we started murder records was just to put out that EP. And then there was other bands that we knew of that we were friends with." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
**Founding Members' Diverse Backgrounds** : The band's members brought diverse backgrounds to its formation: Jay Ferguson was a record store clerk from Halifax; Chris Murphy was a child model for Winnie the Pooh sleeping bags and Snyder's Bacon commercials; Andrew Scott was a provincial high school high jump champion and track and field record holder, later voted one of Canada's 20 sexiest musicians; and Patrick Pentland was born in Northern Ireland, lived in Jamaica, and settled in Sackville, Nova Scotia. "And this is a show on how a record store clerk, a saxophone player born in Northern Ireland, a former provincial high school high jump champion, and a model for Winnie the Pooh sleeping bags in the Eaton's catalog came together to become one of this country's most enduring rock bands." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
**Halifax Music Community Support** : After gaining major label attention, Sloan made a conscious effort to support the Halifax music scene, where they felt they "knew everybody who was into cool music." "But we really tried to be gracious about it and really try part of the expanding murder records and putting out other records was an effort to be a part of the community where we were all of a sudden our aspirations changed to being national or international." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE

**Recording Philosophy** : Chris Murphy prefers making records "on the cheap" to ensure they are seen as a success, prioritizing low cost over high sales figures. He contrasts this with their second album which cost $120,000 due to major label funding, whereas their first record, remixed by Geffen, cost only $1,200. "I like to make everything on the cheap. So that it can be seen as a success." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QB3nyFH_tRU
**Regaining Music Rights** : Following the collapse of The Enclave and their departure from Geffen, Sloan successfully fought to remain independent and regained the rights to their music. They eventually acquired the Canadian rights to "Smeared" and "Twice Removed," consolidating their entire catalog under their own Murder Records label, a point of pride for the band. "The good news is that Sloan successfully fought to stay independent. They also got the rights to their music back, which, as you heard, is something the band is pretty proud of." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
**Self-Sufficiency in Recording** : After expensive major label experiences (e.g., $120,000 for "Twice Removed"), Sloan embraced self-sufficiency, recording albums like "Never Hear The End of It" and "Parallel Play" in their own rehearsal studio. **Signing to Geffen/DGC Records** : Sloan, an indie band from Nova Scotia, signed to Geffen/DGC, Nirvana's record label, after a rapid series of events. A demo tape sent to Cam Carpenter (A&R at MCA Canada) led to a recommendation to Rick Arboit (Network Records), who then passed it to Todd Sullivan (Geffen). Sullivan, impressed by "Underwhelmed," signed them, a rare and exciting occurrence for a Halifax band. "He set it to a friend of his Todd Sullivanic Geffen who loved the song underwhelmed and then came to see his play and then he was like, do you guys want to sign to Geffen?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE

Zutat aimed to make Sloan a major hit, but the label went out of business within six months, leaving Sloan without a US label by 1997.
