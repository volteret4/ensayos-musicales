## songs
**Sugar Sugar (1969)** : This song, co-written by Jeff Barry and Andy Kim, was rejected by The Monkees, leading Don Kirshner to create The Archies as a fictional band to record it. Tony Dante sang all vocal parts, with his voice multi-tracked to create the illusion of a full band. Released around Memorial Day 1969, it initially struggled for radio play until Kirshner convinced a DJ in Texas to spin it, leading to a surge of requests. The song rapidly climbed the Billboard Hot 100, reaching number 41 in early August, then 24, 14, and finally number 3 by the end of August 1969, where it was behind Johnny Cash's "A Boy Named Sue" and The Rolling Stones' "Honky Tonk Women." "Sugar Sugar" then unseated "Honky Tonk Women" from the number one spot and remained there for a month, famously preventing Creedence Clearwater Revival's "Green River" from reaching number one. This track is described as having a good hook, simple lyrics, and a syncopated rhythm, and is noted for molding the taste of a generation of preteens. ## curiosities
**Impact on Public Taste** : The Archies, through their continuous presence on TV every Saturday morning from 1968 to 1978, profoundly influenced the musical tastes of a whole generation of preteens, teaching them to presume instant gratification as their birthright. **Origins as a Fictional Band** : The Archies were a cartoon band, created by Don Kirshner specifically to record "Sugar Sugar" after The Monkees refused the song. The band was based on the Archie comics and had its own Saturday morning cartoon, ensuring they "could not say no" to Kirshner. "And that is partially how the Archies were born.
