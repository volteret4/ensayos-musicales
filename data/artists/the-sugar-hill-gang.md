## songs
**Rapper's Delight (1979)** : This song became the first mainstream hip hop hit, marking the beginning of hip hop's dominance in popular music. Kenny Arnold, an Iowa Hawkeyes basketball player, was known to play the song on repeat for hours in 1980. The track was essentially rapping over the bassline of Chic's "Good Times." While the quick wordplay was known in live rap performances, this was unprecedented in mainstream music. The rapping is described as tame compared to later hip hop. The three members, initially naive to the music industry, were recruited by Sylvia Robinson, who found Big Hank at Krispy Krust Pizza and the others by driving around asking rappers to record over "Good Times." The song was recorded in 14 minutes. Sylvia Robinson mailed records to black radio stations and pushed for airplay, which led to listener demand and the birth of the hip hop industry. Music writer Eric Reese stated that "Rapper's Delight" was a "tear in the veil" and a "standard" for hip hop's success when companies were hesitant to finance rap artists or labels. It broke financial barriers and inspired rappers to develop their own styles. "Now what you hear is not a chest on the rapid to the beat. ## curiosities
**Legal Troubles Over Names** : The members of The Sugar Hill Gang encountered significant legal troubles over their own names and who had the right to be "The Sugar Hill Gang." This was attributed to their naiveté regarding the music industry's practices, such as the conflict of interest when the record label and band management are the same entity. "These guys encountered a whole lot of legal troubles over their own names and who really had the right to be the Sugar Hill gang.
