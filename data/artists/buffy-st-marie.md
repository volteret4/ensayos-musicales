## albums
**Coincidence and Likely Stories (1991)** : This album, produced by Chris Berkett, marked Buffy St. Marie's comeback after 15 years out of the business due to being blacklisted by the Nixon administration. It was notable as the first album to be "born on the web" by exchanging MIDI files via Compuserve between Hawaii and London. "I made her comeback album, Coincidence and Likely Stories." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mjQBaX_3oL4 ← buffy-st-marie
**Medicine Songs** : This is Buffy St. Marie's latest album at the time of the discussion, released on True North Records, with Chris Berkett involved in its production. "We did the last one was Medicine Songs, which just came out in True North Records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mjQBaX_3oL4 ← buffy-st-marie

## curiosities
**Blacklisted by Nixon Administration** : Buffy St. Marie was blacklisted by the Nixon administration for 15 years due to writing "songs about truth," leading to her absence from the music business. "She was blacklisted by the Nixon administration for writing songs about truth." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mjQBaX_3oL4 ← buffy-st-marie
**Early Online Collaboration Pioneer** : For her album "Coincidence and Likely Stories," Buffy St. Marie, living in Hawaii, collaborated with Chris Berkett in London by sending MIDI files of her keyboard parts via Compuserve (a dial-in modem service before the World Wide Web). This pioneering method allowed the album to be "born on the web." "So Roger managed to get her MIDI files onto the Compuserve and I managed to work out and download them via Compuserve into my system in London." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mjQBaX_3oL4 ← buffy-st-marie
**Sesame Street Work** : During her 15-year hiatus from the music business, Buffy St. Marie worked on "Sesame Street." "She'd been out of the business. She'd been doing Sesame Street for 15 years." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mjQBaX_3oL4 ← buffy-st-marie
**Vocal Recording at Walter Becker's Studio** : Since Buffy St. Marie did not have a 24-track machine in her home studio, Chris Berkett had to transport 15 heavy 24-track tapes in a flight case to Walter Becker's studio in Maui to record her vocals and other parts. "I had to take a flight case full of like 1524 tracks. Really heavy. Over to Walter Becker studio, Stevie Dan studio in Maui because Buffy didn't have a 24 track machine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mjQBaX_3oL4 ← buffy-st-marie
