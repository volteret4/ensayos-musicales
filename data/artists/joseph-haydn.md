**Symphony No. 45 "Farewell Symphony" (1772) - Early Example of Gradual Exit** : In 1772, Joseph Haydn composed Symphony No. 45, famously nicknamed the "Farewell Symphony," which concluded with musicians leaving the stage one by one until only two violins remained. While not an electronic fade-out, it conceptually mirrored a gradual disappearance of sound in performance.
