## curiosities
**Hit Their Stride (Pre-2005)** : By spring 2005, the band "hit their stride" and was capable of "selling out just about any place they played," becoming a major force in the rock revival. "The food fighters hit their stride, and they were now capable of selling out just about any place they played." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Live Performance Dominance** : The Foo Fighters were recognized as one of the "big boys" in the alt-rock scene during the latter half of the 2000s. While their music sales were respectable, their primary source of income came from live performances, indicative of a shift in industry economics. "Sales of their music were respectable, but the big paydays came from playing live." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-CT7yYXonIk
**Longevity Beyond the Seven-Year Cycle** : The Foo Fighters are recognized as a band that has surpassed the typical seven-year lifespan, successfully navigating several cycles of formation, growth, and creative evolution. "Groups that have survived multiple seven-year cycles. You too, the stones, the oasis, green day, food fighters." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Streaming Performance Compared to Pop Superstars** : Despite being one of the biggest rock acts of the last 30 years, the Foo Fighters were not considered "superstars" in terms of streaming. Their most streamed song, "Learn to Fly," had approximately 350 million listens by the end of the 2010s, a figure dwarfed by Ed Sheeran's "Shape of You" which garnered 2.4 billion streams. "But even for a band like The Food Fighters, one of the biggest rock acts of the last 30 years. It weren't superstars it's streaming." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Y6nHw5xx88
