## songs
**Fortunate Son (1969)** : This song, written by John Fogerty, was inspired by the disparity where children of wealthy and connected parents received draft deferments for the Vietnam War. It was controversially played during Donald Trump's presidential campaign, despite Trump himself receiving five draft deferments and at the same time being reported to have called soldiers and war dead "losers and suckers." This renewed attention to the song was significant enough to draw Dale Hawkins back into the studio for a second attempt at a recording career. ## curiosities
**Absence in Nixon's Collection** : Despite being a music fan who would show off his record collection, Richard Nixon's tastes leaned classical, and his collection did not include popular rock bands like Creedence Clearwater Revival. This absence was noted when discussing Nixon's musical preferences and his disdain for the pop and rock music of the 60s. "there were no records by the Rolling Stones or the Beatles or Credence Clearwater Revival in his collection." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 5 - Anti-Nixon Songs of the 70s | http://www.ftr70.com
