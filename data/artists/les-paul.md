## curiosities
**Consultant for Gibson** : Paul had been actively "pestering" Gibson since the mid-1940s about developing a new type of electric guitar. The company eventually brought him in-house to work as a consultant alongside Gibson designer Tim McCarty. "Paul had been pestering Gibson about such things since the mid-1940s. The company finally said, OK, all right, fine. Let's see what you can do." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Experimentation with Music Technology** : Les Paul was a jazz and pop artist known for his pioneering experiments in creating his own electric guitars and his work with early multi-track tape machine technology. His innovations were fundamental to the development of modern recording. "He was a jazz and pop artist who experimented with making his own electric guitars, as well as messing around with this new multi-track tape machine technology." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Full Endorsement of His Signature Guitar** : After working with Gibson on various shapes, designs, and materials for approximately a year, Les Paul was so pleased with the final product that he gave it his full, enthusiastic endorsement. In recognition, Gibson named the iconic guitar after him. "Les Paul loved the final product so much that he gave it a full-throated endorsement. And Gibson returned the favor by naming the guitar after him." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Unveiling of the Gibson Les Paul Guitar** : The first Gibson Les Paul guitar was officially unveiled on June 24, 1952, during a special event hosted for musicians at the Waldorf Astoria Hotel in New York. This landmark guitar was crafted from mahogany and featured a distinctive bright gold finish. "The first Gibson Les Paul was unveiled on June 24, 1952, at a special event for musicians at the Waldorf Astoria Hotel in New York." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
