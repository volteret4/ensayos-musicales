# artist - Psychedelic Furs

## curiosities
**12-inch Remix Business** : The Psychedelic Furs also got into the 12-inch remix business, aligning with a broader trend in the 1980s driven by the success of tracks like New Order's "Blue Monday." This format was crucial for bridging indie music with dance culture. "And so was Depeche Mode and The Cure and The Human League and Simple Minds and The Cult and Killing Joke and Erasure and Psychedelic Furs and Madness. They all got into the 12-inch business." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**Influence on "On a Friday" (1986)** : Psychedelic Furs was noted as one of the 80s influences audible on "On a Friday's" 1986 self-issued five-track cassette. "You too, water boys, psychedelic furs, the Smiths." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t-G22K0vLnM
**Unrealized Album Project** : David Botrell and Bob Lannwaw were slated to work on the next Psychedelic Furs record. However, the project did not proceed because the band had no songs written for rehearsal, and Lannwaw, having just spent a year and a half with an artist who wrote as they went, declined to continue. "Dan was slated to do the next psychedelic furs record and I went down with him to rehearse at John Giblin's rehearsal studio in Surrey" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vWvYe1a-2Aw
