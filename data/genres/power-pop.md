**British and American Origins** : While British bands such as The Beatles and their descendants Badfinger provided an initial "spark" for Power Pop, the first prominent wave of Power Pop bands were predominantly American, including groups like The Raspberries (fronted by Eric Carmen), Big Star (led by Alex Chilton), and the solo artist Todd Rundgren. **Core Characteristics** : Power Pop emerged as a "back-to-basics rock movement" that took the catchy 1960s pop sounds of bands like The Who or The Beach Boys and "turbo-charged it" with increased energy and volume. **Early New Wave Characteristic** : The first period of the New Wave era (roughly 1978-1984) was characterized by a jerky, twitchy, fun, and sometimes goofy style of power pop, primarily based on traditional instruments like guitars, bass, and drums. "The first featured the jerky, twitchy, fun sort of power pop that could get a little goofy from time to time. That's your standard new wave.
