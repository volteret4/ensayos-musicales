# genre - Power Pop

## curiosities
**Early New Wave Characteristic** : The first period of the New Wave era (roughly 1978-1984) was characterized by a jerky, twitchy, fun, and sometimes goofy style of power pop, primarily based on traditional instruments like guitars, bass, and drums. "The first featured the jerky, twitchy, fun sort of power pop that could get a little goofy from time to time. That's your standard new wave. It was based largely on guitars, bass, and drums." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
**End of 70s / Early 80s Appeal** : Power pop, exemplified by The Pretenders, was highly sought after at the close of the 1970s and the beginning of the 1980s, alongside the quirky sounds of The B-52s and the genre-bending of The Police, catering to a strong appetite for change. "The goofy stuff from the B-52s and the power pop of the pretenders and the genre bending of the police were just what a lot of people were looking for at the end of the 70s in the beginning of the 80s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**First Wave New Wave Characteristic** : The first wave of New Wave was characterized as an energetic, guitar-based, slightly jittery brand of power pop. "The energetic guitar based, slightly jittery brand of power pop featuring people in suit jackets and skinny ties." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
