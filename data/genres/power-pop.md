# genre - Power Pop

## curiosities
**Big Star's Influence and Liner Note Grammy** : Big Star, a cult-favorite Power Pop act from the 1970s, became a major influence on Power Pop bands globally. Despite their limited record sales, they notably received a Grammy Award for Best Liner Notes in 2013 for their box set "Keep an Eye on the Sky." "Alex Chilton and Big Star major influence on Power Pop bands everywhere." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KaIp6KVn-co
**Blue Album's Contrast to Pinkerton** : The "Pinkerton" album was noted for not being a "grungey power pop album like the first one," indicating that Weezer's debut, "The Blue Album," was characterized by a power pop sound. "This was not a grungy power pop album like the first one." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DoJa8jcrlUA
**Early New Wave Characteristic** : The first period of the New Wave era (roughly 1978-1984) was characterized by a jerky, twitchy, fun, and sometimes goofy style of power pop, primarily based on traditional instruments like guitars, bass, and drums. "The first featured the jerky, twitchy, fun sort of power pop that could get a little goofy from time to time. That's your standard new wave. It was based largely on guitars, bass, and drums." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
**End of 70s / Early 80s Appeal** : Power pop, exemplified by The Pretenders, was highly sought after at the close of the 1970s and the beginning of the 1980s, alongside the quirky sounds of The B-52s and the genre-bending of The Police, catering to a strong appetite for change. "The goofy stuff from the B-52s and the power pop of the pretenders and the genre bending of the police were just what a lot of people were looking for at the end of the 70s in the beginning of the 80s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**First Wave New Wave Characteristic** : The first wave of New Wave was characterized as an energetic, guitar-based, slightly jittery brand of power pop. "The energetic guitar based, slightly jittery brand of power pop featuring people in suit jackets and skinny ties." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
**Presence in Mid-90s America** : The middle 1990s in America saw the rise of notable power pop bands, such as Veruca Salt, featuring strong female artists. "Veruchas Salt, bookended by Nina Gordon and Louise Post. They were one of the great American power pop bands of the middle 1990s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zZM9ajwYr9E
**Subgenre of New Wave** : Power Pop was identified as a distinct subgenre nested within the broader "New Wave" umbrella. Artists such as Elvis Costello and Tom Petty were cited as prime examples of this style, characterized by its catchy melodies and energetic delivery. "There was Power Pop, which could include everyone from Elvis Costello to Tom Petty, a new Scott Seen, a specials English beat and so on, and all of the flavors of Technopop, all those groups who threw away their guitars and favor of synthesizers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z8BTWo66SDI
**The Raspberries as Pioneers** : The Raspberries are widely cited as a groundbreaking band and one of the first examples of the Power Pop sound, particularly in the context of early alt-rock. "Ask anyone about Power Pop, and they will talk about the raspberries." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HBS24NwhrE8
