# genre - Techno

## curiosities
**Darker Cousin of House** : Described as the "darker heavier cousin of house," with its core DNA characterized as "Technopop on Crystal Meth." "And let's not forget about Tecno, the darker heavier cousin of house. **Definition and Characteristics** : Techno is fundamentally percussion-based electronic dance music, typically stripped down to emphasize bass lines and drum beats. Its origins are diverse, stemming from early synthesizer pioneers, avant-garde electronic composers, Kraftwerk, David Bowie, the English techno pop scene, and Afrika Bambaataa. "Generally speaking, techno is percussion-based electronic dance music, stripped down to the point where bass lines and drum beats rule." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The History of Alt-Rock： Chapter 10 | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Detroit Origins** : The techno genre solidified in Detroit, where three friends – Kevin Saunderson, Juan Atkins, and Derrick May – combined influences from Motown, disco, dance music, and the aforementioned electronic pioneers. Their goal was to create funky dance music using affordable synthesizers, which eventually moved to Chicago and then internationally. "But it took this small scene in Detroit to take all those elements and put them together in a new and highly influential way." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The History of Alt-Rock： Chapter 10 | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Foundation Laid by Kraftwerk** : All techno music, including artists like Underworld and Daft Punk, and the entire Detroit techno scene, can be traced back in some fashion to the profound influence of Kraftwerk, highlighting their foundational role in electronic dance music. "Today's EDM and all techno from groups like Underworld and anything that came out of Detroit to Daff Punk, Ambient and Chillout music, Craftwork influence." ← Ongoing History of New Music > Explaining Krautrock | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ePZDkqLM_gw&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Global Cultural Centers** : Toronto became a "massive center of techno culture," alongside other major hubs like the UK, the Spanish Isles of Ibiza, Italy, New York, Chicago, and LA. This global spread demonstrated the genre's broad appeal, primarily within the underground. "Toronto became a massive center of techno culture." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The Post-Punk Explosion Part 4： Alt-Dance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0FYq5MBdHw4&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Post-Hacienda Fracturing** : Following the boom in dance music driven by venues like The Hacienda in the UK, dance music fractured into numerous subgenres, including techno. While its origins can also be traced to Detroit, this evolution highlighted the diversification of electronic dance music. "We'd soon see things fracture into techno, trance, jungle, big beat, breakbeat, hardcore, drums and bass, trip hop, electronic body music and a million different types of house music, each with its own production aesthetics." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The Post-Punk Explosion Part 4： Alt-Dance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0FYq5MBdHw4&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
