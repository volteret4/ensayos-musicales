# genre - Southern Rock

## curiosities
**Diverse Umbrella** : Southern rock is a broad and diverse genre, encompassing a wide array of sounds and styles. The Marshall Tucker Band serves as a key example, demonstrating that "Southern rock is not all the same." "Southern rock is not all the same and the Marshall Tucker band kind of exemplifies that." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 46 - The Chaos and Music of the Ozark Music Festival of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Early Skateboarding Soundtrack** : In the 1970s, kids at the Oxnard skate park initially skated to southern rock, before punk rock became their preferred soundtrack. "And they all skated to either metal or southern rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ ← southern-rock
**Influence of Delaney & Bonnie** : Delaney & Bonnie's eclectic style, which included Southern rock elements, helped to push rock back toward American roots traditions, a direction that became central to 70s music through artists like The Allman Brothers, The Band, and Lynyrd Skynyrd. "Their style it was really quite eclectic. You can hear the Southern rock." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 59 - Bands and Booze on the Festival Express of 1970 | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Ozark Festival Showcase** : Despite the festival's chaos and bluegrass being an afterthought, the Ozark Music Festival of 1974 provided a significant showcase for the diversity of not just rock, but specifically the broad spectrum of Southern rock. "it was good as showcase as any as you would find in the in the 70s on the diversity, not just of rock, but this broad umbrella of Southern rock." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 46 - The Chaos and Music of the Ozark Music Festival of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Peak Period (1977)** : Southern Rock experienced its peak popularity in 1977, with Leonard Skinnerd at the forefront, leading the genre. "Southern Rock was at its peak and Skinnerd was leading the pack." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dgzF_KBSQWM ← southern-rock
