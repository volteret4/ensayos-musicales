**Definition and Imagery** : Southern Rock is defined as the rock and roll bands of the 1970s who used either literal or figurative images of some version of the Southern United States in their music and performances. Mark Kemp, author of "Dixie Lullaby," described it as an "otherworldly musical stew," while Greg Allman humorously stated that saying "Southern Rock" is like saying "rock rock" because rock and roll originated in the South. **Evolution Post-1977** : Following the traditional heyday, bands like 38 Special evolved their sound to be more commercially viable, seeking radio play and MTV exposure. Donnie Van Zant even declared the term "Southern Rock" passé in 1986, indicating a deliberate move away from the genre's stereotypical imagery towards "arena rock." "I think that hold on loosely sounds a lot more like a Rainer Rock than Southern Rock, which seems to be what 38 Special thought they needed to do to sell more records." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 3 - Nostalgia, Race, and Rebels in 70s Southern Rock (Encore) | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-3-nostalgia-race-and-rebels-in-70s-southern-rock-encore
**Glorification of Rebellion and Stereotypes** : Southern rock bands often "played into the stereotypes of the Southern white male as this impoverished and misunderstood rebel." They glorified themes of poverty, drinking, and fighting, aligning with the "rebel factor" associated with the Confederacy. **Heritage vs. Racism Debate** : While some Southern Rock bands and their fans claimed to be simply honoring their heritage rather than being racist, the use of loaded symbols like the Confederate flag on stage or guitars made it impossible to separate their heritage from the history of racism and slavery for many. This created a dilemma where people asked questions or made assumptions about their intentions. "you cannot separate your heritage from racism and slavery if you wave the confederate flag around the stage or even attach it to your guitar you can't have it both ways." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 3 - Nostalgia, Race, and Rebels in 70s Southern Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/03-bittersweet-home-alabama
**Heyday and Decline (1970-1977)** : The heyday of Southern Rock is generally considered to be from roughly 1970 to 1977. Many fans believe that the genre "lost its edge" after the 1977 plane crash that killed Ronny Van Zant and two other members of Lynyrd Skynyrd, effectively marking the end of the movement.
