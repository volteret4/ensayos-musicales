# genre - Heavy Metal

## curiosities
**Early Print Musical Reference (1970)** : The earliest verified print reference to "heavy metal" in a musical context appeared on February 7, 1970, in Cream Magazine. Music critic Lester Bangs described The Guess Who as "heavy metal robots," though this was intended as an insult rather than a definitive genre label. "They're quite refreshing in the wake of all the heavy metal robots of the year past." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8GU8ZpSgY8
**Emergence as Genre Descriptor (Mid-1970s)** : The term "heavy metal" entered the music vernacular in the mid-1970s, gaining widespread use to describe a new wave of "loud, heavy guitar rock." This sound was pioneered by bands like Led Zeppelin, Black Sabbath, and Deep Purple, solidifying "heavy metal" as one of rock's most significant subgenres. "By the mid-1970s, everybody was using it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8GU8ZpSgY8
**Etymological Research (2013)** : The origin of the term "heavy metal" when applied to music was extensively researched by Dina Weinstein, a professor of sociology at DePaul University in Chicago. In 2013, she published a paper titled "Just So Stories, How Heavy Metal Got Its Name, A Cautionary Tale," attempting to solve this etymological puzzle by examining various sources. "She wrote a paper entitled Just So Stories, How Heavy Metal Got Its Name, A Cautionary Tale." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8GU8ZpSgY8
**Fans Most Likely to Run Sub-7 Minute Mile** : A study commissioned by the website Test Fitness Equipment found that heavy metal fans are the demographic most likely to be able to run a mile in under seven minutes. "heavy metal fans are most likely to be able to run a sub seven minute mile." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HoBx6s3JmVQ
**Heavy Metal Knitting Championship (Finland)** : The annual Heavy Metal Knitting Championship, held in Finland, requires participants to knit while performing interpretively to metal music. This unique competition continued even amidst the pandemic, with the 2021 winner, "the Knitting Witch," hailing from the Netherlands. "This requires entrance to actually knit something while performing interpretively against a backdrop of metal music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-N6IM5Nm5iw
**Motivational Music for Bodybuilding** : Heavy metal music is a popular choice for bodybuilders, with studies indicating its effectiveness in keeping them "pumped up" between sets. The loud, aggressive nature of the music is believed to contribute to sustained motivation during weight training. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BHN6db7m3DM
**Scientific Origin** : The term "heavy metals" originates from chemistry and metallurgy, referring to actual metals with high densities such as iron, copper, tin, silver, gold, platinum, gallium, thallium, and hafnium. This scientific usage predates its musical application. "Heavy metals are actual metals with high densities." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8GU8ZpSgY8
**William S. Burrows' Literary Usage (1961-1965)** : Writer and poet William S. Burrows used the character "Uranian Willie, the Heavy Metal Kid" in his 1961 novel "The Soft Machine" and a 1965 spoken word performance. While the term appeared, Burrows was discussing aliens and addictive drugs, not music, and thus his work is not considered the origin of the musical genre's name. "Burrows isn't talking about music at all. He's actually talking about aliens and addictive drugs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8GU8ZpSgY8
