# genre - Indie Rock

## curiosities
**Booking Indie Rock Bands (Early 2000s)** : Coachella, a major American festival, began actively booking indie rock bands during the early 2000s, reflecting the growing mainstream acceptance and popularity of the genre. This inclusion helped to elevate the status of the indie rock revival, moving it from underground scenes to larger, more established platforms. "Festivals like Coachella in America and Glastonbury in the UK started booking these bands." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xid8CZf4oYo
**Definition and Origins** : "Indie" music is defined by artists who operate independently from major record labels, often self-producing or even launching their own labels. This movement saw early examples like the Buzzcocks and was fostered by small independent labels working outside the major system. "Now indie, as far as we're concerned, means artists who have done everything on their own with no help, that is independent from one of the big established record labels." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4
**Digital World's Influence** : The Indie Rock Revival of the early 2000s served as one of the earliest and most prominent examples of how the nascent "new digital world" could effectively generate and sustain a novel musical movement. The ease of illegal file-sharing platforms like Napster enabled listeners to effortlessly discover and access new bands, circumventing traditional record store searches and contributing to the genre's organic rise and widespread appeal. "The indie rock revival was one of the very first examples of how the new digital world could produce and sustain a brand new musical movement and trend." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xid8CZf4oYo
**Early 2000s Revival** : The early 2000s witnessed a significant "Indie Rock Revival," a broad cultural movement spanning North America and parts of Europe that brought about a resurgence of "stripped down guitar-based rock." This movement was crucial because, by the end of the 1990s, guitar rock was widely considered "dead," with electronica predicted to dominate music. Bands, many originating from New York (The Strokes, Yeah Yeah Yeahs, Interpol, TV on the Radio), Detroit (The White Stripes), Sweden (The Hives), England (The Libertines), and Scotland (Franz Ferdinand), spearheaded this revival, initially operating underground before their music gained traction on radio, TV, films, and commercials, and at festivals like Coachella and Glastonbury. "This was important because at the end of the 1990s, it did look like guitar rock was dead. It was all about electronica." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xid8CZf4oYo
**Foundations in Early 2000s** : The Strokes' debut album `Is This It` became one of the foundations for the indie rock movement that emerged later in the 2000s. "This is it album became one of the foundations for the indie rock movement that we'd see later in the decade." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Hamas Terrorist Attack (2023)** : On October 7, 2023, the Supernova Festival, an EDM event in Southern Israel coordinated for Jewish holidays and promoted as a celebration of "friends, love, and infinite freedom," came under attack by Hamas fighters. Just after sunrise, as part of a larger push into Israel, Hamas fighters on foot, motorcycles, trucks, and powered paragliders, armed with AK-47s, assault rifles, hand grenades, and rocket-propelled grenades, surrounded the festival grounds. They opened fire on attendees, executing many who tried to escape. The attack killed at least 364 people, including 17 police officers, injured hundreds, and resulted in 40 or more festival-goers being taken hostage to the Gaza Strip, along with numerous reports of sexual assaults. "It was the largest terror attack in the history of Israel, and the worst massacre of Israeli citizens ever. It was the worst killing of Jews since the Holocaust." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xid8CZf4oYo
**Impact on Festival Security Protocols** : The tragic events at the Route 91 Harvest Festival in Las Vegas in 2017 led to a significant overhaul of security protocols across festivals globally, particularly in the United States. This horrific incident forced event organizers to implement new measures, fundamentally changing the security landscape and attendee experience at US festivals. "But this awful event set a lot of security protocols in place that are now used by festivals around the world, especially in America." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xid8CZf4oYo
**Increased Power and Credibility** : Indie culture emerged as a powerful global force in the late 2000s, with an "indie" label becoming crucial for a musician's brand, appeal, and credibility. This was facilitated by the ability of small independent labels to rapidly capitalize on trends and bypass traditional gatekeepers. "Indie culture is now in fact a powerful force just about everywhere in the world." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-CT7yYXonIk
**Massacre During Eagles of Death Metal Concert (2015)** : On Friday, November 13, 2015, the Bataclan theater in Paris was the site of a horrific massacre, part of a series of coordinated attacks across the city. Three heavily armed men, French natives of Algerian descent (Foued Muhammad Agad, Sami Amimour, and Ismaël Omar Mostefaï), stormed the venue at 9:40 PM, just as Eagles of Death Metal began their set in front of approximately 1500 people, following opening act White Miles. The attackers killed three people outside before entering and opening fire, leading to 90 deaths, mostly within the first 20 minutes of the attack, and hundreds wounded. Hostages were taken, and the event concluded by 1 AM, with bodies removed over 10 hours and identification made difficult by IDs left in checked coats. "A series of coordinated attacks across the city culminated with a massacre at the Bataclan theater where Eagles of Death Metal were performing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xid8CZf4oYo

**Revival Post 9/11** : Indie rock experienced a revival in popularity after the 9/11 attacks and the American invasions of Iraq and Afghanistan. As happy music seemed inappropriate for the times, bands like The Strokes, The White Stripes, and The Killers contributed to a renewed interest in rock music. "This popularity began to rise again, driven by an indie rock revival." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Y6nHw5xx88
**Rise in the 21st Century** : Indie rock experienced a significant rise in the first decade of the 21st century, becoming a powerful force globally. Its growth was fueled by new technologies that leveled the playing field, allowing small independent companies to quickly distribute music worldwide without major label support. **Site of Deadliest Mass Shooting in US History (2017)** : On October 1, 2017, the Route 91 Harvest Festival in Las Vegas, held at the Las Vegas Village across from the Mandalay Bay Hotel, became the scene of the deadliest mass shooting by a lone gunman in American history. While Jason Aldean was on stage around 10:00 PM, 64-year-old Stephen Paddock opened fire from room 134 on the 32nd floor of the Mandalay Bay Hotel, continuing for 10 awful minutes starting at 10:05 PM. The attack resulted in 58 deaths (with two more later), 413 gunshot wounds, and at least 867 injuries. Paddock was found dead of a self-inflicted gunshot wound, and a large arsenal of 24 firearms and thousands of rounds of ammunition were found in his room. Despite extensive investigation, his motivations remain unknown. "This remains the deadliest mass shooting by a lone gunman in American history." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xid8CZf4oYo
