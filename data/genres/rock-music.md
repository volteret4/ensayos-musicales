**Indie Revolution of the 2000s** : The early 21st century saw an "indie revolution" where a new generation of musicians began releasing stripped-down rock through independent labels on both sides of the Atlantic. This movement was propelled by the financial crisis of major labels, the rise of illegal file sharing, the drop in CD sales, and millennials' desire for more authentic music, along with their internet-savvy ability to discover it. "Add all this together and you end up with the indie revolution of the Outs, which continues even today." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=raRCZ0Y5up8
**Late 90s Doldrums** : By the end of the 1990s, rock music was in a severe decline, with fans expressing genuine concern for its future. The scene appeared bleak, especially when contrasted with the vibrant earlier part of the decade, facing challenges from rap, hip hop, and electronica. "By the end of the 90s, rock music was deep in the doldrums. **Mainstream Decay and New Generation Revival** : A recurring truth in rock's history is that the mainstream often deteriorates to such an extent that a new generation takes it upon themselves to "fix it." This involves returning to basics, stripping down the music, and starting anew, a phenomenon observed at the close of the 1990s and the dawn of the 2000s. "Sometimes the mainstream has to get so bad that a new generation decides, right, if it's going to be fixed, I'm going to have to do it myself." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=raRCZ0Y5up8
