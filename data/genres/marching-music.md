# genre - Marching Music

## curiosities
**Cost-Driven Consolidation of Drummers** : The expense of hiring and maintaining marching bands, primarily due to the large number of drummers required, prompted a push to downsize and consolidate, leading to innovations allowing a single drummer to play multiple percussion parts. "But it was expensive to hire and maintain a marching band because of all those drummers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WL3zEWJJNuw
**Dominance in 1890s US Popular Music** : Marching music was the most popular form of music in the United States during the 1890s, with figures like John Philip Sousa being prominent. These bands, an offshoot of military music, demanded precise time-keeping. "In fact, the biggest form of popular music in the US in the 1890s was marching music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WL3zEWJJNuw
**Snare Drum's Military Utility** : The snare drum, with its distinct staccato crack, was highly valued for military applications due to its effectiveness in keeping precise time. "This makes the snare very good for military use." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WL3zEWJJNuw
