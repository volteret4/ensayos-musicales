**British Invention and Rapid Spread** : Industrial music was a British invention that emerged quickly following the punk movement. Alongside Goth, Technopop, and Modern Skah, it experienced rapid dissemination and often a brief existence, influenced by the intense cultural churn within the British Isles. "There was Goth and Tecdopop and Industrial, Modern Skah, which were all basically British inventions." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zB5zxycrbnY

**Industrial Music – Throbbing Gristle Founded It; "Industrial Music for Industrial People"; Ministry and NIN Biggest Sellers** : Industrial music's roots go back to the 1970s with Throbbing Gristle, who founded a label called Industrial Records with the motto "Industrial Music for Industrial People." The sound moved to Europe first, then was imported into the US in the early 1980s via a Chicago label named Wax Tracks, where it caught on with the corner of the alternative crowd seeking something powerful, angry, and brutal. Industrial fragmented into body music, power electronics, Japanoise (some of the most extreme music ever made), power noise, industrial metal, martial industrial, witch house, dark ambient, industrial hip-hop, and industrial techno. The two best-selling industrial bands are Ministry (which started as a synth-pop band) and Nine Inch Nails, whose Trent Reznor has done more than anyone to bring industrial music to the masses. "The two best-selling industrial bands of all time are Ministry, which started out as its fashion mode like synth band, and Nineteen Snails, headed up by Trent Resner." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9cAeeExXKwU
**Influence of Public Image Limited** : Public Image Limited had a profound effect on the industrial music genre, with artists like Nine Inch Nails acknowledging their importance. "He hung out with some funk musicians, and eventually pulled together a band that ended up having a profound effect on several genres of music, including industrial and punk funk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJGFUlQTUhQ
**Origins and Influence** : Industrial music, particularly the "rage and fury" found in bands like Ministry, Front 242, and Skinny Puppy, significantly influenced Trent Reznor's shift from techno-pop. This genre's aesthetic was pivotal in shaping the harder, more sinister sound that defined Nine Inch Nails. "He found that he really liked the rage and fury of bands like Ministry and Front242 and Canada's Skinny Puppy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M3nUQtja0qc
**Prolific Album Output** : British industrial bands, notably Psychic TV, have demonstrated periods of extremely high productivity, releasing a remarkable number of albums in very short timeframes, even if some of the recordings were live and of varying quality. "The British industrial outfit called Psychic TV with Stranger Affair from their live and Paris album from 1986. That's part of their record-breaking run of releasing the most albums in the shortest period of time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T1XSJIm0Nh4
**Simultaneous Evolution with Goth** : Goth and Industrial music have evolved simultaneously for years, with an "unspoken division of labor in terms of intensity." "Goth and Industrial have been evolving simultaneously for years now." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Sound Characteristics** : Industrial music is characterized as "hard and fast and heavy and distorted and angry." It relies on guitars and "sharp keyboard sounds of techno over ultra heavy beats." "While industrial is hard and fast and heavy and distorted and angry, Goth can be softer and slower and here's that word again, introspective." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
