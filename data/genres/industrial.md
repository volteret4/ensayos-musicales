**Industrial Music – Throbbing Gristle Founded It; "Industrial Music for Industrial People"; Ministry and NIN Biggest Sellers** : Industrial music's roots go back to the 1970s with Throbbing Gristle, who founded a label called Industrial Records with the motto "Industrial Music for Industrial People." The sound moved to Europe first, then was imported into the US in the early 1980s via a Chicago label named Wax Tracks, where it caught on with the corner of the alternative crowd seeking something powerful, angry, and brutal. Industrial fragmented into body music, power electronics, Japanoise (some of the most extreme music ever made), power noise, industrial metal, martial industrial, witch house, dark ambient, industrial hip-hop, and industrial techno. The two best-selling industrial bands are Ministry (which started as a synth-pop band) and Nine Inch Nails, whose Trent Reznor has done more than anyone to bring industrial music to the masses. "The two best-selling industrial bands of all time are Ministry, which started out as its fashion mode like synth band, and Nineteen Snails, headed up by Trent Resner." ← Ongoing History of New Music > A Guide to Genres： Part 2 | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9cAeeExXKwU&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
