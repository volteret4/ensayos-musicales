# genre - Reggae

## curiosities
**Application in Dog-Friendly Music** : The 2020 Christmas song for dogs, "Raise the Woof," was created with a reggae beat to leverage its calming effect on canines. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nYPGQiDL5bw
**Bob Marley as "King"** : Bob Marley is referred to as "The King of All Things reggae." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MmXDbXI6Sqk
**Canine Preference** : Research, particularly from Scotland, indicates that dogs love reggae music, experiencing reduced stress levels. This effect is attributed to reggae rhythms being similar to a dog's resting heartbeat. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nYPGQiDL5bw
**Dogs' Preferred Music** : Scientists have reportedly proven that reggae is the genre of music that dogs like the most. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qtdetc5-MQo
**Dominant Scene in Laguna Beach** : Reggae music was the dominant genre in Laguna Beach, California, where Taylor Hawkins grew up, with hard rock being "frowned upon" and associated with "heshers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mh-qg1izM98
**Influence in UK Ska** : Jerry Dammers of The Specials was entranced by reggae brought by West Indian immigrants. Bad Brains also veered into reggae. Grace Jones was known for her reggae sound in the 1980s. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Rob Gretton's DJ Setlist** : Before punk records were widely available, Rob Gretton, later manager of Joy Division and New Order, frequently played soul and reggae music during his DJ sets at the Rafters club in Manchester. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**West Indian Influence in UK** : Its sounds, along with Ska, were introduced to industrial cities like Coventry by West Indian immigrants. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
