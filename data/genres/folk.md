"Of all the scenes in Britain during this decade, folk probably saw the largest growth." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k ← folk

"An English folk band who played accordians and banders in ukuleles, fighting against a sea of more normal alt-rock bands, along with pop stars like Beyonce and Katy Perry and Justin Bieber, where the hell did a band like Mumford & Sons fit within this equation?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P1RyOchn7pk ← folk ← folk
**Medieval Revival in 1960s Folk Music** : In the 20th century, specifically during the 1960s, folk music played a crucial role by rescuing many medieval forms and instruments. This period experienced a surge of choral groups and composers who rediscovered medieval repertoire, thereby preparing the ground for its later transformation into a massive cultural phenomenon with the arrival of video games, cinema, and the internet. "Durante los años 60, el folk rescató muchas de sus formas instrumentos y se vivió una oje de grupos corales y compositores que rescataron el repertor y medieval." ← Por qué todo suena medieval en 2025？ (Dungeon Synth, Bardcore...) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FDNQdBRktSA
**New Influence in Alt Rock (Late 2000s)** : In the late 2000s, folk music, particularly its acoustic aspects, was explored as a potential new influence for alt rock, questioning if the industry could successfully market folk and bluegrass-influenced music to the alt-rock demographic. This innovation arose during a "down part of a cycle" for rock.
