## curiosities
**Avoidance in Medical Settings** : Country music is identified as one of the genres that should be avoided in doctor's or dentist's waiting rooms for anxious patients, as it does not contribute to relaxation. **Influence of Blues Slide Players** : The slide guitar techniques developed by early 20th-century Mississippi Delta blues players, who improvised with knives or beer bottle necks, influenced countless country musicians. "Like that, interned influence not only a billion country players, but rockers like Rye Kooter, Brian Jones and Keith Richards of the Rolling Stones and Duane Almond." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8GU8ZpSgY8
**Personal Dislike Despite Familiarity** : Despite efforts to increase cognitive familiarity with country music, including spending time in Nashville, interacting with the country community, hanging out with hardcore fans, interviewing musicians, and attending shows, the speaker still finds they "can't stand it." **Prevalence of Alcohol References** : A notable characteristic of country music is its frequent mention of alcohol; statistics show that one in five country music songs contains some reference to alcohol. "One in five country music songs have some reference to alcohol." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GO0K_N7Ys7I
**Rise of Steel Guitar** : The steel guitar, an evolution of the Hawaiian lap-style guitar where a metal slide (the "steel") is used, became a significant instrument and sound in country music. "Follow that forward a number of decades and you end up with the steel guitar, which became a big thing in country music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8GU8ZpSgY8
**Yeehaw Challenge Meme (2018-2019)** : The "Yeehaw Challenge" was a cultural meme circulating within the country music world around 2018-2019. It played a significant role in the initial viral spread and chart success of Lil Nas X's "Old Town Road." "It just so happened that there was a meme in the country music world at the time called the Yeehaw Challenge. And Montero's song got swept up in it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YhdsufCUC2w
