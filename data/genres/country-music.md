# genre - Country music

## curiosities
**Authenticity and Struggle** : For country music, the concept of authenticity is deeply tied to the "music of struggle," almost reveling in hardship, which contrasts with rock's focus on escaping struggle. This emphasis on lived experience often led to skepticism towards privileged artists or those perceived as insincere. "Some genres do not permit veering too far away from this elusive idea of authenticity. Which for country is music of struggle, almost reveling in it, and for rock is escaping from it." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 35 - Cosmic American Music - 70s Country Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-35-cosmic-american-music-70s-country-rock
**Drum Kit Ban** : For a long time, drum kits were banned from country music shows, including prestigious venues like The Grand Ole Opry. When Western Swing bands were eventually allowed to perform there, drummers were often forced to hide behind a curtain. **Genre Segregation in Charts (1956)** : Despite Fats Domino's "Blueberry Hill" being listed among Billboard's top 30 country sellers for the year, it never appeared on the top 100 country charts in 1956, illustrating strict genre segregation. This incident marked the sole appearance by a black artist on any Billboard country charts in the 1950s, highlighting a disparity between audience purchasing habits and industry gatekeepers' recognition. "Blueberry Hill made Billbud's top 30 records of the year for country sales in his annual round-up, but it never appeared even on the top 100 country charts during 1956 itself." ← Episode 45： ＂Blueberry Hill＂, by Fats Domino | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VqIwZGJHgBg
**Geographic and Demographic Origins** : Country music originated in the rural areas of the Eastern US, particularly Appalachia, Kentucky, Tennessee, and Nashville. It was primarily created by white people living in these regions. "Country music was the music made by white people who lived in the rural areas of the eastern US, basically." ← Episode 3： ＂Ida Red＂ by Bob Wills and the Texas Playboys | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zu4E9Y1oJA0
**Skepticism Towards Rock Singers** : Some country singers and their fans were concerned that rock singers entering the country genre were insincere in their appreciation or were making fun of country music. The differing approaches to music-making, such as a rock artist's desire to "play what you feel" versus a country session player's preference for specific instructions, fueled this suspicion. "They were worried that the rock singers were not sincere about their appreciation for country.
