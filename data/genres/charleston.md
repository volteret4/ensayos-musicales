**Sexual and Vulgar Criticism (1920s)** : The Charleston faced the harshest criticism during the dancing mania of the 1920s. It was seen as encouraging young women to "rye" (writhe) on the dance floor in front of strangers, leading to its description as too sexual and vulgar. Consequently, the Charleston was banned in some jurisdictions. "The harshest comments were reserved for the Charleston.
