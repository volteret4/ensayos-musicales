**Foundations and Characteristics** : Bluegrass has its roots in mountain music and is a mixture of gospel, blues, and country. It is distinctly known for its use of the banjo and fiddle, as well as the beautiful close harmonies typically sung in trios or quartets. The fiddle music of Appalachia and the Ozarks forms part of its very foundation. "Bluegrass has its roots in mountain music. Like rock it's really a mixture of many other genres too." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 46 - The Chaos and Music of the Ozark Music Festival of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/ ← bluegrass
**New Influence in Alt Rock (Late 2000s)** : Bluegrass was considered as a potential new musical influence for the alt-rock crowd in the late 2000s, alongside folk. This experimentation emerged as rock entered a phase of innovation during its cyclical decline. "Could the industry maybe sell a form of folk and bluegrass influence music to the alt-rock crowd?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_yslM5oanRo ← bluegrass
**Roots Music Category** : Bluegrass is recognized as a type of roots music that needs space for expression and appreciation, alongside other traditional forms like hillbilly music.
