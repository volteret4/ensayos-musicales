# genre - K-Pop

## curiosities
**Global Reach via YouTube** : K-pop, or Korean Pop, has achieved widespread global visibility thanks to YouTube's borderless platform. The ability for content to be legally monetized and accessible worldwide has allowed genres like K-pop to be seen "everywhere." "K-pop can be seen everywhere." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7IDbqdIw3WU
**DMZ Propaganda Countermeasure** : In South Korea, K-pop songs were blasted to the north from speakers set up along the south side of the demilitarized zone (DMZ). This was done to drown out propaganda announcements coming southward from North Korea, whose speakers were too weak to compete. The practice stopped in 2018 when it created too much tension and threatened hostilities. "In South Korea, speakers were set up along the south side of the demilitarized zone, and K-pop songs were blasted to the north." ← Ongoing History of New Music > Using Music as a Weapon | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=R1wdsGt07Gg&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Preference of Younger Generations** : As emo's popularity waned during its decline (around 2008-2010), younger fans entering their coming-of-age years increasingly gravitated towards K-pop as a preferred musical genre. "Younger fans now in their coming of age years wanted something different, and for them that was pop, K-pop specifically, and hip-hop, so interest-wained." ← Ongoing History of New Music > Alt Rock Revivals Part 3： Emo | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v_am7ifh7Pk&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Rise to Global Phenomenon (2010s)** : The 2010s marked the rise of K-pop into a global phenomenon, representing the "next step in the globalization of music." Its rapid expansion and international success are expected to continue for decades. "The rise of K-pop into a global phenomenon. That's going to continue for decades to come.
