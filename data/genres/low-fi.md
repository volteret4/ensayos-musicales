# genre - Low-fi

## curiosities
**Description in Solo Work** : The term "low-fi" was used to describe aspects of Gord Downie's first solo album, "Coke Machine Glow," alongside spoken word elements, indicating a specific recording and musical aesthetic that was difficult to categorize. "Lots of spoken word. Lots of low-fi recording and music that's really really hard to categorize in Pigeon Hole." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Um_KEEcCeT4
**Growth Due to Home Recording Gear** : The proliferation of cheap and powerful home recording equipment significantly spurred the growth and resurgence of low-fi music. This technology allowed independent artists to create music on limited budgets, fostering a sound that embraced imperfections and a lack of slickness, which became defining characteristics of the genre. "More low-fi, something called Bedroom Pop, a resurgence in sounds like shoegays, new flavors of hip hop." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uEe_HLUay5M
