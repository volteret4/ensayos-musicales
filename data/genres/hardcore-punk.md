# genre - Hardcore Punk

## curiosities
**American Invention** : Described as an "American invention" that directly descended from the hard, fast, and loud punk of the 1970s.
**Core Ideology** : Characterized by being anti-establishment, anti-hippie, deliberately anti-commercial, and positioned against what its proponents viewed as the corrupt music industry.
**Driver of Indie Label Creation** : Due to its anti-commercial nature and rejection by existing record labels, hardcore acts were compelled to establish their own labels, such as SST, Epitaph, and Alternative Tentacles.
**Establishing Indie Music Universe** : The hardcore scene was crucially important in establishing the entire universe of indie music in the early 1980s.
**Growth with Reagan's Presidency** : The genre's popularity and widespread appeal significantly increased during Ronald Reagan's time in office, as young people reacted to his conservative political policies.
**Incorporation of Pop Sensibilities** : Despite its intensity, bands like Black Flag, Bad Religion, and The Descendents began to incorporate melodic elements and "pop sensibilities."
**Intensified Punk** : Aimed to amplify the existing punk sound, making the music even harder, faster, and louder.
**Musical Characteristics** : Known for being short, sharp, and fast, tailored to deliver its message efficiently.
**Origins (Late 1970s, California)** : Its roots are traced to the late 1970s in California, emerging as the fast, hard, radical, and angry extreme of the punk rock spectrum.
**Rigid Dogma Challenge** : Had a "rigid, immovable hardcore dogma" that bands like Hüsker Dü broke by introducing nuanced concept albums and strong melodic elements.
**Subgenres** : Includes bandage, death core, pornogrind, screamo, power violence, positive hardcore, nard core, and Nintendo core.
