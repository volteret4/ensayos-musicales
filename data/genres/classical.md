**EMI's Composition of Bach-Style Music (1997)** : At a 1997 University of Oregon competition, the computer program EMI (Experiments in Musical Intelligence), developed by David Cope, composed pieces in the style of classical composer J.S. **Early CD Technology Showcases (1979-1982)** : Classical music played a pivotal role in the early demonstration and commercialization of compact disc technology. The first test pressing in 1979 featured the Berlin Philharmonic performing Richard Strauss's "An Alpine Symphony," selected for its clarity and dynamic range. Later, Chopin's waltzes, performed by Claudia Arrau, became the first CD produced for public consumption on August 17, 1982. "The first test pressing, a CD used to demonstrate the new technology, was a recording of the Berlin Philharmonic, conducted by Herbert von Karrihan, performing Richard Strauss' and Elpine Symphony. This appeared in 1979, and this recording was chosen for its clarity and wide range of dynamics, something that the compact is promised." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BHkJFA0LMro
