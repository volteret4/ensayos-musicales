# genre - Trag Jazz

## curiosities
**Adaptability of Arrangements** : Trag Jazz arrangements were highly adaptable, encompassing a wide range of source material. This included everything from popular songs of the era to traditional nursery rhymes. "Starting from pop songs to nursery rhymes were fair game for Trag Jazz arrangements." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zNIVqKqAlnk
**Emergence in 1950s Britain** : Trag Jazz appeared in mid to late 1950s Britain, as a rejection of contemporary popular music by some young people. It emerged amidst imported American rock and roll, the skiffle craze, and various folk music forms. "some young people rejected contemporary sounds, in favor of something known as Trag Jazz." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zNIVqKqAlnk
**Emphasis on Authentic Sounds** : New acts playing Trag Jazz actively sought out and mined the "more pure, more authentic sounds of the past." Their goal was to find renewed inspiration through these older musical traditions. "The new acts mined the more pure, more authentic sounds of the past, hoping to be, you know, inspired again." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zNIVqKqAlnk
**Popularity in UK until 1960s** : Trag Jazz was a popular musical form in the UK, remaining "the thing" until sometime in the 1960s. For a period, it successfully captured the interest of the British music scene. "Trag Jazz was the thing in the UK until some time in the 1960s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zNIVqKqAlnk
**Presence in the US in 1940s** : Trag Jazz was also a musical phenomenon occurring in the United States during the 1940s. A significant song from 1949, later covered by various artists, was written during this period when Trag Jazz was active in the US. "at a time when Trag Jazz was happening in the US." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zNIVqKqAlnk
**Revival of Dixieland Jazz** : Trag Jazz represented a revival of Dixieland Jazz, a style originating from New Orleans that first emerged around the time of World War I. This revival involved mining more "pure" and "authentic" sounds from the past. "This was a revival of something close to Dixieland Jazz from New Orleans, which emerged around the same time as World War I." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zNIVqKqAlnk
