# genre - Pop Punk

## curiosities
**1992 Turning Point** : The year 1992 is noted as a turning point when this emerging genre started to gain wider attention, notably through a Spin Magazine article titled "California Dreaming." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tZV1lz-obQE
**Artist Aging** : big pop punk bands from the early 90s and 2000s had grown up, finding it hard to maintain youthful angst when approaching 50, and their fans also outgrew the music. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Attitude Shift** : Characterized by an attitude shift away from politics, social messages, and class struggles, embracing fun, almost carefree themes, often written in major keys to sound inherently happy, and encouraging goofiness and juvenile humor. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Bifurcation in Mid-2000s** : Experienced a "bifurcation" where some bands were deemed "too poppy for punk" by alt-rock radio stations and audiences, who considered their music "kiddie punk" appealing to the "wrong kind of people" (i.e., too young). ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Decline in Early 2010s** : saw a decline in the early part of the 2010s, with overall popularity less than in the early 2000s. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Decline in Late 2000s** : Declined as video channels stopped showing music videos, supporting magazines (Alternative Press, Spin) shrunk to online publications, commercial radio shifted focus, and bands moved towards pure pop. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Defining Pop Elements** : Characterized by pop sensibilities like melody, clear song structure, sing-along choruses, short and punchy songs (typically less than four, often less than three minutes), and a verse-chorus-verse 32-bar structure, while maintaining a subculture image and appeal. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tZV1lz-obQE
**Dookie's Paving Role (1994)** : Green Day's album *Dookie* is widely credited with paving the way for a generation of pop punk bands. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nyfdGYWelic
**Economic & Cultural Context (Mid-1990s)** : Flourished during a period of economic improvement in the mid-1990s, serving as an "antidote" and "release" from the gloom of grunge and the early 90s recession. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Emergence of "New Punk Pop" (Post-2010)** : A new generation of bands emerged after 2010, often with a "darker" sound compared to 1990s pop punk, possibly influenced by the 2008 financial crisis or the election of Donald Trump. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Emo's Decline** : Emo, which had been a driving force of pop punk a decade earlier, largely went out of fashion, contributing to pop punk's decline. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Enduring Nature** : Proved to be pretty enduring, with its measure of "snotiness" ensuring it will likely never completely disappear, despite not reaching its mid-90s peak due to competition from hip hop and rap. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Evolution from 1970s Punk** : Represents a mutation from the original 1970s punk rock, integrating pop elements into the hard, fast, and loud sound. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tZV1lz-obQE
**Festival and Venue Shift** : pop punk bands no longer headlined major festivals like Coachella or Lollapalooza, and venues once known for booking them either closed or moved to booking different acts. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Folk-Pop Competition** : the pop side of Alt Rock became dominated by folk, acoustic guitars, and banjo bands, which seemed to consume all available attention, contributing to pop punk's decline. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Magazine Decline** : magazines championing this subgenre either shrank (e.g., Alternative Press) or disappeared entirely (e.g., AMP). ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Origin as Hybrid** : A hybrid genre formed by the merger of punk and pop over decades, responsible for selling hundreds of millions of records and concert tickets. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Paradoxical Juxtaposition** : Described as a paradoxical juxtaposition or oxymoron, similar to "jumbo shrimp," given punk's original anti-pop ethos. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tZV1lz-obQE
**Pop Radio Exclusion** : pop radio stations, which previously incorporated pop punk, squeezed this music out in favor of more dance-oriented tunes. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Purity Controversy** : Faced accusations of "selling out" from purists who felt the music was "softer in intensity" and too commercially successful, disqualifying it from being true punk and labeling bands as "illegitimate pretenders." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Term Coining** : The term "pop punk" began to be circulated by the San Francisco-based publication Maximum Rock and Roll when discussing melodic punk bands such as Social Distortion and The Vandals. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tZV1lz-obQE
**Warped Tour Disappearance** : the disappearance of the Warped Tour negatively impacted the pop punk scene. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Worldwide Phenomenon** : Became one of the most popular forms of punk rock of all time, evolving into a worldwide phenomenon, particularly from the 1990s onward. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tZV1lz-obQE
