# genre - Pop Music

## curiosities
**Cyclical Battle with Rock** : Since the 1950s, Pop and Rock have been "locked in a battle where each combatant is 180 degrees out of phase with each other," meaning one rises as the other declines in creativity and popularity, typically in 13-year cycles. "See, since the birth of Rock in the 1950s, Rock and Pop have been locked in a battle where each combatant is 180 degrees out of phase with each other." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Dominance and Decline (2000-2001)** : Happy, dancing pop music was dominant through 2000 and 2001. However, following the 9/11 attacks and the initiation of wars, this genre became "completely out of step with the Zeitgeist" and was perceived as inappropriate, leading to a decline in its momentum. "happy, dancing pop music was dominant through 2000 and 2001. But after the attacks in New York and Washington and the subsequent opening of the wars in Afghanistan and Iraq, happy, dancing pop music was suddenly completely out of step with the Zeitkist." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=raRCZ0Y5up8
**Dominance in 2000** : By the end of 2000, "vacuous pop music had taken over the universe," with CDs selling by the hundreds of millions, overshadowing rock and other genres. "When the dust cleared at the end of 2000, it was very clear that vacuous pop music had taken over the universe." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Mid-1980s Ascendancy** : By mid-1984, pop music, propelled by artists like Huey Lewis and Whitney Houston, reached the top of its cycle, pushing rock into decline and creating a demand for classic rock. "Huey Lewis and Whitney Houston propelled pop to the top of the cycle." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Mid-2000s Resurgence** : By 2006, the "appetite for rock was beginning to wane, and pop music was once again in the Ascendant," marking the decline of rock's fifth cycle. "By the time we got into 2006, the appetite for rock was beginning to wane, and pop music was once again in the Ascendant." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Pop Sensibilities Definition** : Writing with "pop sensibilities" is understood as a descriptive term, not an insult, referring to songs that incorporate clear elements such as melody, defined song structure, and sing-along choruses. Such songs are often short and punchy, typically under four or even three minutes, and commonly constructed in the typical verse-chorus-verse pattern using a 32-bar structure, similar to mainstream pop hits. However, the distinctiveness from mainstream pop lies in the musical arrangements, lyrics, and image, which may be "well left of center for mainstream audiences." "You can write songs with pop elements, melody, song structure, sing along choruses, but still keep your subculture image and appeal." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tZV1lz-obQE
**Resurgence in Late 1990s** : After the peak of alternative rock, pop charts, boy bands, and artists like Antiphany, Debbie Gibson, the Backstreet Boys, and Britney Spears ruled the music landscape, indicating the end of rock's fourth cycle. "Within two years after that, boy bands and pop charts ruled. The Alt Nation had grown up and moved on and were supplanted by Generation Wise Taste for the Backstreet Boys and Brittany and so on." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
