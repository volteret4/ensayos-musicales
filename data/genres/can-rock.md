**Definition and Origin** : "Can Rock" is a term that emerged with the book *Have Not Been The Same: The Can Rock Renaissance* (1985-1995) to describe Canada's significant contribution to Alt Rock. It represents a "huge burst of new acts" in the 1990s that never ceased, encompassing a broad range of Canadian music from The Tragically Hip to Sarah McLachlan and Sloan to the Cowboy Junkies. "Can Rock is a term that came into use with a book called Have Not Been The Same, subtitled The Can Rock Renaissance, which detailed for the first time what happened to music in this country between 1985 and 1995." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The 90s Part 8： The CanRock Revolution | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U2kYleeMnl8&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
