# genre - Northern Soul

## curiosities
**British Dance Movement** : Northern Soul was a tight dance movement centered in Northern England that developed a passionate following for American black soul music and Motown. "There was a tight scene back in England called Northern Soul. This was a dance movement centered in Northern England that worshipped American black soul music in Motown." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw
**Cultural Impact on Record Collecting (1970s)** : Around 1975, Frank Wilson's "Do I Love You Indeed I Do?" became a hit with fans of Northern Soul in England. This scene was comprised of people who loved American R&B, and the more rare a record was, the better, making Frank Wilson's obscure track highly desirable. "These were people who loved American R&B and the more rare, the better." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tbmssCZ05ro
**Influence on Madchester** : Northern Soul is identified as one of the original foundations of the Madchester music scene. "Madchester's foundations are originally Northern Seoul and stuff." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KF_adyXf86Q
**Manchester Musical Roots** : Northern Soul is identified as one of the original foundations of Manchester's musical heritage. "Manchester's foundations are originally Northern Seoul and stuff." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=q_Scyr2czh8
**Mod Culture Offshoot** : Following the decline of mod culture in the late 1960s due to the rise of hippie culture, some former mods transitioned their musical interests to a specific form of music known as Northern Soul. "Some mods became skinheads or swayed heads, and others got into a form of music known as Northern Soul." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mAlc-N2wn5s
