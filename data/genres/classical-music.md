# genre - Classical Music

## curiosities
**19th-Century Revival** : After Johann Sebastian Bach's death in March 1685, he was nearly forgotten until a significant revival in the 19th century re-established him as a retro hero and one of the most important and influential composers of all time. "The only reason we talk about Bach today is because there was a Bach revival in the 19th century." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1VWJ0uCQx8w
**Continuous Use of Mutes** : Mutes for brass and other instruments have been used for centuries and continue to be a standard practice in classical music performances. "They're still being used in classical music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8GU8ZpSgY8
**Crowd-Surfing Incident (2014)** : In 2014, an audience member was ejected from a classical music concert for crowd-surfing during a performance of Handel's Messiah. This unusual incident highlighted how deeply ingrained and widespread crowd-surfing had become, even in genres traditionally associated with formal audience behavior. "In 2014, an audience member was ejected for crowd-serving during a classical music concert." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KMx4bmq8o2Y
**Disliked by Psychopathic-Trending Individuals** : A study conducted at New York University found that classical music was generally disliked by students who scored high on psychopathy scales. These individuals tended to rate classical pieces very low in preference. "same thing with classical music they didn't like it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aSk1XQHNkd8
**Early Liner Notes Context (Early 20th Century)** : The tradition of adding explanatory text to recorded music packaging emerged in the first half of the 20th century, following the practice of printed music programs handed out at symphonies and operas. With the introduction of the 12-inch LP in 1948, which was initially reserved for genres like classical music, extensive essays were commissioned to educate listeners. "These were then sold together in a package that looked like a photo album, except that this was called a record album, and yes, that is the origin of the modern term." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KaIp6KVn-co
**Faster Performance and Recording Tempo (2018 Study)** : A 2018 study indicated that both live performances and studio recordings of classical music are now up to 30% faster than they were sixty years prior, suggesting a general acceleration in the genre's delivery. "A 2018 study says the classical music performances and recordings are up to 30% faster than they were 60 years ago." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GO0K_N7Ys7I
**Grammy Award for Liner Notes (1964)** : The Grammy Award for Best Liner Notes, established in 1964, predominantly recognized classical recordings, along with collections, anthologies, or greatest hits packages. This trend continued for many years, highlighting the academic and informative nature of liner notes in the classical genre. "Pretty much every award has been given out to someone who has written liner notes for classical recordings, or a collection, or an anthology, or a great-a-titz package" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KaIp6KVn-co
**Influence on Wine Purchasing Behavior (1993)** : A 1993 experiment demonstrated that playing classical music in a wine store influenced customer behavior. Shoppers would spend more time examining wine labels and were more likely to purchase more expensive bottles compared to when other genres of music were played. "A 1993 experiment showed that when classical music was played, people stopped and examined the labels more often and more carefully when compared to times when other genres of music were playing. They also ended up buying more expensive bottles of wine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aSk1XQHNkd8
**Long Hair as a Fashionable Choice** : In earlier periods, it was entirely fashionable for eminent classical musicians like Beethoven and Mozart, as well as their contemporaries and successors, to wear long hair. This indicates a cyclical nature of fashion trends in men's hairstyles, where long hair was once a mark of distinction in classical music. "Now, that's a bit weird because not that much earlier, it was completely fashionable for the greatest classical musicians in the world to wear their hair long." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bferkEWvJg0
**The Mozart Effect Controversy** : Classical music became central to the "Mozart effect" theory, which proposed it could enhance infant intellect. This led to state-funded initiatives like Georgia providing classical music CDs to newborns. However, later research challenged the exclusivity of classical music's benefits, suggesting other forms of stimulation yielded similar results. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BHN6db7m3DM
