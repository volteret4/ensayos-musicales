**Early Adoption of Compact Disc (1981-1985)** : Classical music aficionados and orchestra conductors, notably Herbert von Karajan, quickly embraced the Compact Disc in the early 1980s. This rapid adoption was due to the CD's ability to overcome inherent limitations of vinyl records, such as channel contamination and intrinsic noise, which significantly affected the clarity and fidelity of classical recordings. With the introduction of the 12-inch LP in 1948, which was initially reserved for genres like classical music, extensive essays were commissioned to educate listeners.
