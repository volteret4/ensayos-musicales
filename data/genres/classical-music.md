**Influence on Wine Purchasing Behavior (1993)** : A 1993 experiment demonstrated that playing classical music in a wine store influenced customer behavior. Shoppers would spend more time examining wine labels and were more likely to purchase more expensive bottles compared to when other genres of music were played. "A 1993 experiment showed that when classical music was played, people stopped and examined the labels more often and more carefully when compared to times when other genres of music were playing. **The Mozart Effect Controversy** : Classical music became central to the "Mozart effect" theory, which proposed it could enhance infant intellect. This led to state-funded initiatives like Georgia providing classical music CDs to newborns. However, later research challenged the exclusivity of classical music's benefits, suggesting other forms of stimulation yielded similar results. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BHN6db7m3DM
