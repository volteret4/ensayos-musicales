# genre - Indie

## curiosities
**"Selling Out" Perception** : Within certain segments of the indie world, profiting from music is often viewed as "wrong" and a betrayal of artistic ideals and principles. This perspective suggests that money compromises the purity of the relationship between the artist and their music, as well as with their fans. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sfie4YYncxk
**Band T-Shirt Concert Etiquette** : In the Indy Rock genre, it is generally considered "uncool" to wear a band's t-shirt to their own concert, possibly because it might suggest being "too much of a fan" or not "cool enough." "However, if you want to look at Indy Rock, it seems that it is generally considered uncool to wear shirt to show." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KtdIMNoHqA8
**Basic Enduring Sound** : Indie music is listed as one of the basic and enduring sounds that will never go away, but is continuously challenged by new genres. "The basic sounds are still with us. Rock, alternative, indie, Goth, industrial, metal, synth-based music, ska, reggae, and so on. They're never going to go away." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tdTs-4Irv8c
**British Equivalent of Alternative** : Originally, "indie" in the UK was the equivalent of the North American term "alternative," referring to music from small record labels not owned by major multinational companies. The UK maintained this usage even as North America embraced "alternative nation" in the 1990s. "See originally indie was the British equivalent of the North American term alternative." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=__7iEl3lH8g
**Composer Style for Johnny Greenwood** : Johnny Greenwood works in indie styles as a composer for movie, TV, and documentary soundtracks. "This guy can work in a variety of styles. Rock, indie, jazz, chamber orchestra, and full-on classical music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nVIswnZYFkU
**Growth of Independent Labels** : The independent label scene became crucial for bands like Bauhaus in post-punk England, with "small nimble indie labels" appearing almost daily, capable of quickly signing and promoting new sounds and trends. "Bauhaus and Bella Logosi's dead, at least on beggars banquet, another one of those small nimble indie labels that popped up almost daily in post-punk England." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
**Later Terminology** : The word "indie" emerged as a term slightly later than "post punk" to describe the music evolving from the original punk scene. "The word that came a little later was indie." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
**Modern North American Meaning** : Today in North America, "indie" essentially refers to music from artists working within an expanding network of labels not associated with the four major labels. However, it has also evolved to describe a certain aesthetic, sensibility, and even a sound, regardless of label origin, sometimes even originating from a major label, especially given distribution deals between independents and majors. "Well essentially it means any music from artists who work within an expanding network of record labels that aren't associated with the four remaining major labels." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=__7iEl3lH8g
**MySpace Artist Style** : Indie was identified as one of the distinctive styles associated with "MySpace Artists." The platform's direct artist-to-fan interaction fostered communities around genres like indie, contributing to their popularization and the launch of new acts. "Before long, we had something called MySpace Artists, a distinctive set of styles that involved pop punk, emo, and indie acts." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GYUuTbOvyak
**Studying Aid for High Achievers** : Indie music was identified as the third genre listened to by students who achieved the highest grades, suggesting it is effective for studying. "and number three, indie." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KtdIMNoHqA8
**UK Record Charts Influence** : The UK music scene favored the term "indie" partly due to their love for record charts, with an official independent record chart existing since at least 1980, publicizing the progress of indie artists weekly. "An official independent record chart had been part of the music culture since at least 1980." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=__7iEl3lH8g
**Underground Cred and Self-Promotion** : Some artists aim to maintain "original underground cred" by avoiding traditional label structures, instead using platforms like blogs, Myspace, Facebook, YouTube, SoundCloud, and Bandcamp for promotion and distribution. "They want to keep their original underground cred and find alternate ways of promoting themselves and distributing their music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=__7iEl3lH8g
