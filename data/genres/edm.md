# genre - EDM

## curiosities
**Club Consumption through Speakers** : EDM is identified as a modern music form primarily experienced communally through speakers in nightclubs. This emphasizes the social and physical impact of sound delivered via large speaker systems. "When they're out in nightclubs to EDM, this apparently new form of music, this has begun. It's coming through speakers, by the way." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lGNrJgwQjgg
**Co-opting of 12-inch Remixes (2010s)** : By the 2010s, any 12-inch singles that were still being produced, including those for alt-rock songs, were more likely to be co-opted and popularized by EDM culture rather than serving an independent alt-rock dance scene. This shows EDM's growing influence on dance music remixing. "But by the 2010s, any 12-inch singles that were being made were most likely to be co-opted by and popularized by EDM culture." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mNqqLDpUuZY
**Evolution from Electronica** : EDM (Electronic Dance Music) is the modern term for what was previously known as Electronica, signifying the genre's branching off and blooming into a massive force in contemporary music. "What we used to call electronic is what we call EDM today. And we don't need to talk about how big that has become, do we?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Extensive Use of Amen Break** : EDM (Electronic Dance Music) has extensively employed the "Amen Break" sample, which is integrated into a vast number of tracks. This highlights the break's widespread influence across different forms of electronic music production. "It's all over hip-hop. It's all over electronic and EDM." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VnXBJqKwkrw
**Foundation Laid by Kraftwerk** : Kraftwerk's innovative electronic music laid the foundation for modern EDM and all forms of techno, influencing artists and groups like Underworld and Daft Punk, as well as the entire Detroit techno scene. "Today's EDM and all techno from groups like Underworld and anything that came out of Detroit to Daff Punk, Ambient and Chillout music, Craftwork influence." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ePZDkqLM_gw
**Foundations in Early Electronic Music** : Electronic Dance Music (EDM) owes its roots to pioneers like Daphne Oram and Delia Derbyshire. Their early work in composing with synthetic sounds and experimental audio equipment, particularly at the BBC Radiofonc Workshop, established critical techniques and sounds that would eventually influence the development of modern EDM. "If you're into electronic music and EDM, you should know that these next two women are essential to what you'd listen to." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
**Integration into Hyperpop** : EDM elements, specifically keyboards, are a key component of Hyperpop, contributing to its exaggerated and dance-oriented sound. "So that means a lot of earwormy hooks, lots of dance beats, EDM keyboards, plenty of auto tune, and even a cartoonish feel" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tdTs-4Irv8c
**Merchandise Spending Habits** : EDM fans, alongside country music people, were found to spend the least amount of money on merchandise at shows. "EDM fans and country music people spend the least." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KtdIMNoHqA8
**Preference for High-Energy Dance Music (2010s)** : By the 2010s, if listeners were seeking high-energy dance music, they were most likely drawn to EDM, often presented by "superstar DJs." This indicated a shift in dance music preference away from alt-rock towards electronic dance music. "If you were looking for high-energy dance music, you were most likely into EDM presented by superstar DJs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mNqqLDpUuZY
**Sexual Encounters Playlists Ranking** : EDM was ranked as the sixth most common genre on playlists created to soundtrack sexual encounters, behind pop, hip hop, R&B, Indy Rock, and alternative. "and EDM sixth." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KtdIMNoHqA8
