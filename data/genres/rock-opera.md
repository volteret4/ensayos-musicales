# genre - Rock Opera

## curiosities
**Definition and Distinction** : A rock opera, like traditional opera, is fundamentally a story told entirely through singing. This distinguishes it from rock musical theater, which typically incorporates spoken dialogue alongside songs. **Format Definition** : A rock opera is a type of concept album designed with the structure and narrative ambition of a traditional opera, but performed using rock music. **Origins as Serious Art Form** : The rock opera genre emerged in the 1960s, driven by a desire for rock music to be recognized and taken seriously as an art form. This ambition was notably demonstrated by The Who, who daringly sought to produce work on a scale comparable to Wagnerian opera, a venture that proved successful with their fans. "Like so many things in the 70s, the rock opera was born in the 60s and it's part of this desire for the genre of rock music to be taken seriously as an art form.
