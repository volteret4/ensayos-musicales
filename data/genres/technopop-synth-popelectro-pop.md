# genre - Technopop (Synth Pop/Electro Pop)

## curiosities
**Commercial Peak (1981-1982)** : The genre reached its commercial zenith in the winter of 1981-82. This period was marked by massive global hits from bands like The Human League, Duran Duran, Soft Cell, and ABC that were both accessible and danceable. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
**Emergence and Definition** : Technopop emerged in earnest around 1979 as the synthesizer-driven second half of the New Wave era. It is defined as post-punk music where electronic keyboards and drum machines are the dominant instruments, often with real guitars and drums being completely absent. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
**Evolution and Decline** : By the mid-1980s, the genre's popularity as a distinct movement declined as synthesizer technology became common. Synths transitioned from being "new and futuristic" to the "common present," making it harder to distinguish between real and synthesized sounds. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
**Vibe and Lyrical Themes** : Early Technopop often had a robotic, cold, and deliberately artificial feel, sometimes described as "Blade Runner-ish". Lyrically, the genre focused on futurism, alienation, isolation, and dystopian views of life, sharing a sense of nihilism with guitar-driven punk. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
