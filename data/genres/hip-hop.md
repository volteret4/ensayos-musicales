"Hip-hop and rap could not have been invented if it were not for the SL-1200 and the machines it inspired." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IIJySSo276k

**Origin of Drum Break Sampling** : Hip-hop's rise in the 1970s and 1980s led DJs to seek out and loop drum breaks from funk and soul records on their turntables, paving the way for sampling technology. The Amen Break and Funky Drummer Break became foundational elements of the genre. "Now let's fast forward about a decade to when hip-hop was on the rise. DJs were looking for drum breaks that they could loop on their turntables, the funkier or the break, the better." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VnXBJqKwkrw

**Target of New York City Cabaret Law (1980s)** : In the 1980s, New York City's cabaret law, originally enacted in 1926 to control speakeasies and racial mixing, was utilized by Rudy Giuliani to crack down on hip-hop venues. This enforcement forced hip-hop dance parties into dangerous, unlicensed underground warehouses and basements. "In the 1980s Rudy Giuliani used this law to crack down on hip-hop venues and raves, forcing dance parties into dangerous underground unlicensed warehouse and basements." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aSk1XQHNkd8
