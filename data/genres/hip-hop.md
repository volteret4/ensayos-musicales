**Origin of Drum Break Sampling** : Hip-hop's rise in the 1970s and 1980s led DJs to seek out and loop drum breaks from funk and soul records on their turntables, paving the way for sampling technology. The Amen Break and Funky Drummer Break became foundational elements of the genre. "Now let's fast forward about a decade to when hip-hop was on the rise. DJs were looking for drum breaks that they could loop on their turntables, the funkier or the break, the better." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The Unsung Heroes of Music： Part 2 | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VnXBJqKwkrw&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Origins in Sampling (1970s)** : Hip-hop as a genre was fundamentally born out of sampling techniques in the 1970s. This involved DJs manipulating beats and grooves with fast hands on turntables and mixers, laying the groundwork for electronic sampling in subsequent decades. **Target of New York City Cabaret Law (1980s)** : In the 1980s, New York City's cabaret law, originally enacted in 1926 to control speakeasies and racial mixing, was utilized by Rudy Giuliani to crack down on hip-hop venues. This enforcement forced hip-hop dance parties into dangerous, unlicensed underground warehouses and basements. "In the 1980s Rudy Giuliani used this law to crack down on hip-hop venues and raves, forcing dance parties into dangerous underground unlicensed warehouse and basements." ← Ongoing History of New Music > 60 Mind-Blowing Facts About Music in 60 Minutes： 2017 Edition | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aSk1XQHNkd8&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Underestimated Sales Before SoundScan** : Before the advent of SoundScan technology, rap and hip-hop genres were significantly underestimated and downplayed by the traditional sales tracking system. SoundScan's accurate data revealed that these genres were actually "selling by the ton," exposing a previous disconnect between perceived and actual popularity.
