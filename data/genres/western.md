# genre - Western

## curiosities
**Growth During Musicians' Strike (1942)** : Western performers, like country musicians, were not subject to the American Federation of Musicians strike in 1942 because ACAPE did not classify them as "real musicians." This exemption allowed them to continue recording and disseminating their music rapidly at a time when Big Band recordings were restricted. "Acape also did not consider country musicians, western performers, and R&B acts to be real musicians. As a result, they were not covered by any prohibitions." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V7WjNR1ujhk
**Increased Radio Exposure (WWII Era)** : As radio programming shifted to more cost-effective record playbacks and local stations gained more evening autonomy, Western records found increased airtime. This new exposure introduced the genre to wider audiences alongside country, hillbilly, and R&B. "But it also made room for country, western, hillbilly, and R&B records. People started to hear other genres on the radio for the very first time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V7WjNR1ujhk
