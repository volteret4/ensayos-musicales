# genre - Geek Rock

## curiosities
**Cousin of Lofi** : Geek Rock, also known as Nerd Rock or Dork Rock, was described as a "cousin" to Lofi, emerging alongside the rise of personal computers and their associated culture. "Lofi's cousin, I think we can say that, is Geek Rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Emergence as a Term** : "Geek Rock" emerged as a new name for nerd rock towards the end of the 1990s, indicating a slight evolution or sub-categorization within the broader genre. "It was towards the end of the decade that it acquired a new name, Geek Rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h6snk0m4200
**Identifiable Sound** : Like Lofi, Geek Rock is a genre that listeners recognize distinctively when they hear it, indicating its unique and consistent sonic and thematic identity. "Like Low Fie, you know Geek Rock when you hear it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Lyrical Themes** : The most important aspect of Geek Rock was its subject matter, with lyrics celebrating social and emotional awkwardness, and covering themes like comic book characters, video games, science fiction, and technology, often delivered with a "real sense of humor," much of it self-deprecating and clever. "Most important was the subject matter of their lyrics. In addition to celebrating their social and emotional awkwardness, they liked to sing about comic book characters, video games, science fiction, technology." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Musical Characteristics** : Geek Rockers often favored "weird instruments and strange electronic effects" in addition to standard guitar, bass, and drums, contributing to their distinctive sound. "Geeks liked weird instruments and strange electronic effects to go with their standard guitar bass and drums." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Outsider Status** : Geek Rockers were seen as "outsiders" even within the outlier universe of Alt Rock; being labeled "weird" by Goth kids, Lofi practitioners, and Shoegazers was considered a "badge of honor." "Geek Rockers were outsiders even in the outlier universe of Alt Rock. In the Goth Kids and Low Fires and Shoegays's branded You as Weird, that was a badge of honor." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
