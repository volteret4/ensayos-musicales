# genre - Mod

## curiosities

**Origin and Culture** : Mod, short for "modernist," emerged from a group who preferred modern jazz over trad jazz. By the mid-60s, their primary musical interests shifted to soul and R&B. It was a working-class subculture, concentrated in southeast England, especially London, fueled by newfound comparative affluence in the early 60s, which provided young working-class people with disposable income for fashion, music, and drugs. "Mod stood for modernist, and the mods emerged from the group of people who liked modern jazz rather than trot, but by this point their primary musical interests were in soul and R&B." ← Episode 118： ＂Do-Wah-Diddy-Diddy＂ by Manfred Mann | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QAP7abAbwwM
**R&B Music and Scooter Culture** : Mods were fervent enthusiasts of rhythm and blues (R&B) music, drawing inspiration from artists like James Brown from the US and local bands such as The Who, The Kinks, and Small Faces. Their signature mode of transportation was a heavily accessorized and highly customized Vespa T-S or Lambretta GT200 motor scooter. "The mods loved rhythm and blues, R&B music, people like James Brown from the States and locally the Who and the Kinks in the Small Faces. Their mode of transportation was a heavily accessorized and highly customized Vespat-TS or Lambreta GT200 motor scooter." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mhQ36Y-c9fM ← mod

**Regulated Uniform and Attention to Detail** : The Mod uniform was meticulously regulated and policed, emphasizing a sharp appearance and rigorous attention to detail. This included ensuring pants were hemmed to the exact right length, with a cuff allowing for pant swing, maintaining no more than a 1-inch gap between the pant leg and the top of the shoe. "And they wore a uniform which was highly regulated and policed. See the duty of the mod was to look sharp and pay attention to detail. For example, it was very important to have your pants hemmed at exactly the right length. And the cuff had to allow for the swing of your pants so there was never more than a 1-inch gap between the end of your pant leg and the top of your shoe." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mhQ36Y-c9fM ← mod
**Revival and Enduring Influence** : Mod fashion experienced a significant revival in the 1970s, spearheaded by The Jam, and despite the band's eventual demise, Mod culture and fashion re-emerged, albeit in an altered form, during the Britpop era of the mid-1990s. In the 21st century, pockets of Mods continue to thrive globally, proudly carrying on the fashion attitudes of their predecessors, including driving updated versions of their iconic scooters. "There was a mod revival in the 1970s and was led by the jam. ...Mod culture and fashion survived the demise of the jam and rose again in a somewhat altered form during the Brit pop era of the middle 1990s. And here in the 21st century pockets of mods are thriving around the world with all of them carrying on the proud fashion attitudes of their forefathers. Which includes driving updated version of those cool little scooters." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mhQ36Y-c9fM ← mod
**Specific Clothing Items** : Mod fashion favored slim-fitting suits, Ben Sherman shirts featuring narrow button-down collars, three-button mohair jackets, and parkas often adorned with distinctive Union Jack flags or target symbols. "Mods favored slim-fitting suits featuring Ben Sherman shirts with narrow button-down collars. Jackets were three button mohairs and there were lots of parkas, often featuring union jacks or targets." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mhQ36Y-c9fM ← mod
**Style and Music** : Mods had a distinct style featuring sharp Italian suits, pop art, op art, and Black American music or British imitations. Music was primarily functional for dancing, and many mods would take amphetamines to dance through entire weekends at clubs to soul and R&B music.
