**Definition and Origin** : Big Beat emerged in the UK as an electronic movement aimed at softening the excessively extreme and aggressive rave culture prevalent in Europe during the late 1980s and early 1990s. It incorporated influences from Detroit Techno and Chicago House, blending them with existing European electronic music, American hip hop sampling, and instrumental sounds to create a more relaxed and accessible sound. **Rhythmic Characteristics** : The rhythms of Big Beat are similar to breakbeat but are more forceful and intense, typically consisting of sampled breaks from classic funk, soul, and hip hop records. These rhythms are generally played at a tempo between 110 and 135 BPM, which is faster than most hip hop and trip hop tracks but slower than the dominant techno and drum and bass of the era. "Las rímpicas del Big Beat son muy similares a las del Breque Beat, pero más contundentes y intensas, suelen ser brex ampliados de discos clásicos de funk, soul y hip hop, y están en un tempo en 310 y 135 BPMs." ← Por qué el cine de los 2000 sonaba así？ Big Beat | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UOFTiKXruFQ
