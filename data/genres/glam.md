**Early Origins (Late 1960s - Early 1970s)** : The origins of glam music are more complex than commonly perceived, extending beyond David Bowie's work in the early 1970s with `Hunky Dory` and `Ziggy Stardust`. Key predecessors like Marc Bolan (from 1969-1970) and Joe Brieaz (from 1968-1969) were already developing the aesthetic and theatrical components of glam years prior. "cuando nosotros pensamos en lo que son los inicios del glam yo creo que todos nos remontamos a primeros de los años 70 con David Bowie con el Han Quidori y con el Ciguit Star Dust pero realmente los inicios del glam fueron bastante más complejos." ← Music Radar Clan > Tras los inicios del Glam. **Joey Ramone's Involvement** : Joey Ramone embraced the glam style around his 21st birthday, idolizing David Bowie and Alice Cooper. He would wear satin jumpsuits with rhinestones and knee-high platform boots, which made him appear six feet eight inches tall. "When Joey turned 21, he got big into glam." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=j8uk7BnbXwU ← glam
**Sexual Revolution and Androgyny** : Glam music represented a new sexual revolution, characterized by a highly theatrical and androgyne aesthetic, as exemplified by artists like Marc Bolan with his elaborate attire. This visual and performative aspect was central to the movement. **US vs. European Reception** : In the United States, glam music faced significant resistance due to a deeply conservative society that viewed the movement with suspicion, particularly when it came from American artists with a glam appearance. Conversely, Europe, especially places like Paris, showed a "great demand" for glam music. "Por un lado teníamos una sociedad muy conservadora todavía en Estados Unidos que miraba con recelo todo este movimiento glam que venía del otro lado del charco... es en Europa donde había un gran grandísima demanda de música glam." ← Music Radar Clan > Tras los inicios del Glam.
