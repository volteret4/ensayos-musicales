# genre - Digital Music

## curiosities
**Early Digital Glitches in MP3 Compression** : Initial efforts to develop MP3 compression were plagued by digital glitches that severely compromised audio quality, making early compressed tracks sound poor. Engineers dedicated substantial time and effort to resolve these technical issues and refine the algorithms. "Early attempts were plagued by digital glitches that made things sound awful." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fhq9WVeiE_U
**High-Resolution Audio Devices** : There have been and continue to be attempts to persuade the public to buy dedicated music devices capable of playing high-resolution audio, which offers sound quality superior to that of a compact disc. However, convincing consumers to carry an additional device alongside their mobile phones has proven challenging, as demonstrated by the limited success of products like the Pono music player. "And at the other end, there have been and continue to be attempts at getting the public to purchase dedicated music devices that play high-resolution audio, which is digital music recorded at better than CD quality." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fhq9WVeiE_U
**MP3 as a Music Sharing Medium** : In the mid-1990s, as internet usage increased, MP3s became an effective format for sharing music, even over slow dial-up modems. This ease of distribution prompted the recorded music industry's first major concerns regarding digital piracy. "In the middle 90s, with the internet starting to catch on, MP3's proved great for sharing music, even through clunky old dial-up modems." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fhq9WVeiE_U
