# genre - Classic Rock

## curiosities
**Defined by Older Acts in Early 1990s** : The term "classic rock" began to be defined in the early 1990s by established acts like the Rolling Stones, Pink Floyd, and Elton John, as hair metal was losing its appeal. This categorization emerged as a way to describe older, enduring rock artists. "It was also dominated by older acts that were defining a new term, classic rock, Rolling Stones, Pink Floyd, Elton John, and so on." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Y6nHw5xx88
**Industry Figures at Awards** : The UK Classic Rock Awards featured prominent figures from the classic rock world, such as Jimmy Page and Steven Tyler, indicating the genre's significant industry presence and influence. "Jimmy Page was there and Stephen Tyler was there and all these insane giants from the Classic Rock world were there." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Insatiable Market (Mid-1980s)** : As rock declined and pop ascended by mid-1984, many rock fans turned to the music of the 1960s and 1970s, creating an "insatiable market for what was now being called classic rock." "Many rock fans turned to the 60s and 70s, creating an insatiable market for what was now being called classic rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Support for New Music** : Classic Rock magazine in the UK is noted for its supportive stance towards new music, particularly for bands that fit its promotional mold. This support led to The Trews being nominated for awards and having their albums reviewed and distributed by the magazine. "They, that's a very supportive magazine of like new music just generally." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3zbKOwkpkAM
**Wayne's World Soundtrack (1992) Dominance** : The first *Wayne's World* CD from 1992 was noted for being "mostly classic rock." While it also featured contemporary alternative bands like the Red Hot Chili Peppers and Soundgarden, its overall composition leaned heavily towards established rock sounds. "The first Wayne's World CD from 92 is pretty good, although it's mostly classic rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM
