# genre - Classic Rock

## curiosities
**Defined by Older Acts in Early 1990s** : The term "classic rock" began to be defined in the early 1990s by established acts like the Rolling Stones, Pink Floyd, and Elton John, as hair metal was losing its appeal. This categorization emerged as a way to describe older, enduring rock artists. "It was also dominated by older acts that were defining a new term, classic rock, Rolling Stones, Pink Floyd, Elton John, and so on." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Y6nHw5xx88
**Insatiable Market (Mid-1980s)** : As rock declined and pop ascended by mid-1984, many rock fans turned to the music of the 1960s and 1970s, creating an "insatiable market for what was now being called classic rock." "Many rock fans turned to the 60s and 70s, creating an insatiable market for what was now being called classic rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Wayne's World Soundtrack (1992) Dominance** : The first *Wayne's World* CD from 1992 was noted for being "mostly classic rock." While it also featured contemporary alternative bands like the Red Hot Chili Peppers and Soundgarden, its overall composition leaned heavily towards established rock sounds. "The first Wayne's World CD from 92 is pretty good, although it's mostly classic rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM
**Definition and Lineage** : "Classic rock" is a term used to describe bands such as Van Halen, Aerosmith, The Rolling Stones, and Led Zeppelin. Their musical traditions are deeply rooted in the blues, with spiritual ancestors identified as figures like Robert Johnson and Howlin' Wolf, distinguishing them from genres grounded in punk. "At the other end of the spectrum, Van Halen and Eros Smith and the Rolling Stones and Led Zeppelin are now described as classic rock. Their sonic traditions are grounded in the blues, not punk." ← Ongoing History of New Music > Definition (from the vault) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ft8nruhWCas&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Mainstream Rejection (Late 1980s)** : By the late 1980s, classic rock was among the mainstream genres that fans of alternative and non-mainstream music deliberately avoided. This audience sought out unique sounds in indie clubs and record stores, contrasting with the music widely available on Top 40 radio. "You want nothing to do with hair metal or classic rock or new kids on the block or anything on top 40 radio." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The History of Alt-Rock： Chapter 10 | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
