# genre - Gothic Fiction

## curiosities
**Foundation in 1816** : Gothic fiction was founded in 1816, known as "the year without a summer." During this period, four artsy individuals, including John William Polidori and Mary Shelley, were holed up in Villa Diodati near Geneva, Switzerland. To pass the time amidst dark, cold, wet, rainy days, they drank, had sex, took opium, and competed to create the best horror story. Polidori conceived "The Vampyre," about undead bloodsuckers, 80 years before Bram Stoker's *Dracula*. Mary Shelley, then 22, envisioned *Frankenstein*, about a mad scientist creating a being from dead body parts.
