# genre - Folk Rock

## curiosities
**Grateful Dead's Shift to Folk Rock** : As the 1970s approached, The Grateful Dead transitioned from their 1960s psychedelic sound to a folk rock approach, characterized by concise songwriting, storytelling, stripped-down arrangements, and drawing from Americana traditions. "But as the 70s approached, they were taking more of a folk rock approach." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 59 - Bands and Booze on the Festival Express of 1970 | https://www.ftr70.com/ ← folk-rock
**Influential Sound** : The combination of jangly, reverb-heavy, treble-focused guitars and Everly Brothers-inspired harmonies directly influenced bands like The Byrds, Love, Big Star, Tom Petty, R.E.M., The Smiths, and The Bangles. "That combination of jangling, reverb, heavy trebligh guitars, and definitely brothers inspired harmonies is one that leads directly to the birds, love, big star, Tom Petty, R-E-M, the smiths, and the bangles, among many others." ← Episode 113： ＂Needles and Pins＂ by The Searchers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=izC9ZBI0UHM
**Invention by The Searchers' "Needles and Pins"** : Folk Rock was inadvertently invented by The Searchers' arrangement of "Needles and Pins," which featured two electric guitars playing a riff simultaneously with reverb, creating an electric 12-string sound. "That record is the root of Django Pop and Folk Rock." ← Episode 113： ＂Needles and Pins＂ by The Searchers | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=izC9ZBI0UHM
**Post-1978 Rock Diversification** : Folk rock was identified as a specific sub-genre within mainstream rock discussions after 1978, contributing to the increasingly complex categorization of music. "People were now talking about metal and Prague and folk rock, and soft rock, and country rock, and blues rock, and of course, punk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M ← folk-rock
