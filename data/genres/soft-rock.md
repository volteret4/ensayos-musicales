**Defining Characteristics and Evolution from Rock** : Soft rock, also known as "mellow rock" or "safe rock," developed from 1960s and early 1970s rock music by adopting a "less aggressive sound," an "emphasis on the acoustic guitar rather than electric," and a distinct "emphasis on vocal harmonies." It expanded rock and roll by blending it with other genres such as country, R&B, and jazz. "That genre that we call rock music in the 60s and into the early 70s did take on a less aggressive sound. There also might be more of an emphasis on the acoustic guitar rather than electric, definitely an emphasis on vocal harmonies." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 51 - The Softer Side of 70s Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-51-the-softer-side-of-70s-rock
**Expansion, Not Just Restraint** : Kalefa Sanneh, in his book *Major Labels*, posited that "the term soft rock could denote not merely restraint but also expansion as musicians mixed rock and roll not only with country as the Eagles did, but also with R&B and jazz." This view highlights the genre's role in broadening musical boundaries.
