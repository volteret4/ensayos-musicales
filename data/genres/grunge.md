# genre - Grunge

## curiosities
**"Alt Rock with Training Wheels"** : described as a genre that made great music and lured millions into the alternative nation with a "different yet familiar approach to traditional guitar rock."
**"Gold Rush" Phenomenon** : Its sudden popularity, particularly after Nirvana's Nevermind sales figures were revealed by SoundScan, sparked a "Grunge Gold Rush" where record labels sought out similar acts.
**"Well Ran Dry" in Late 90s** : the original grunge bands died off, and for a while in the late 90s, there was "nothing concrete to replace it," leading to "Dreck-like New Metal."
**Alt-Rock's Addiction to 90s Music** : the popularity of grunge and other 90s music led to audiences continually demanding it, and many acts from that era remained popular, making it harder for new artists to break through in the 21st century.
**Decline by 1994** : Began "slowly losing momentum" by 1994, with its "heaviness and bleakness" seeming "a little too much" after Kurt Cobain's death, and remaining bands sounding "more and more derivative."
**Dominant Era (Early 1990s)** : Defined the early 1990s era, featuring bands like Nirvana, Pearl Jam, and Soundgarden, and Alice in Chains.
**Evolution in STP's Sound** : Stone Temple Pilots' debut album *Core* was characterized as grunge, while their second album *Purple* was described as less grunge and more "straight ahead alt rock."
**Foundation of Commercial Alt-Rock Radio** : became the foundation of alt-rock radio's sound for many years, with stations playing grunge or grunge-ish (guitar-based, often drop-tuned) music as a "safer from a ratings point of view" option.
**Impact on Alt-Rock Homogenization** : its popularity led alt-rock to coalesce around hard guitar rock, reducing the variety of sounds on alt-rock radio and leading to an "unstoppable march towards a homogeneity in sound."
**Musical Style** : Characterized by music that was "slow and low," sometimes employing a quiet-loud dynamic.
**Rise in Early 1990s** : became a dominant force in alternative music around 1991-1992, supplanting the mainstream.
**Shaped by Butch Vig** : The entire grunge revolution of the early 1990s was significantly shaped by producer and drummer Butch Vig.
