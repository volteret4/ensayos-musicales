# genre - Grunge

## curiosities
**"Alt-Rock with Training Wheels"** : Grunge bands not only created great music but also served as "Alt Rock with training wheels," successfully luring people into the alternative music scene. Their "different yet familiar" approach to traditional guitar rock drew millions into the "alternative nation," inspiring them to explore deeper into alt-rock and beyond. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Catalyst for Record Label Pivots** : The unexpected sales success of grunge, particularly Nirvana's Nevermind, prompted record labels to drastically "pivot" their strategies. This shift initiated the "Grunge Gold Rush," as labels aggressively sought to sign more alternative rock acts. "So this caused the record labels to pivot... And so began the Grunge Gold Rush." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Decline and "Well Going Dry" (Late 1990s)** : The original grunge bands eventually "died off," and by the late 1990s, the "Grunge well basically went dry," leaving no concrete genre to replace it. This period was followed by what is described as "Dreck-like New Metal." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Decline by 2000** : By the turn of the millennium, "the grunge heroes had either broken up, checked out, moved on, or died," contributing to Alt Rock's perceived dead end. "The grunge heroes had either broken up, checked out, moved on, or died." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Decline in Mid-1990s** : Grunge's dominance began to decline around mid-1996 as Generation X aged and moved on, the artists defining the era lost momentum or disappeared, and an improving economy made the "dour sounds" of grunge less resonant. This decline paved the way for the resurgence of happy pop music. "Gen X grew up and moved on, the artists that defined the early nineties either disappeared or lost their momentum, the economy improved so the dour sounds of grunge no longer resonated as much." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Y6nHw5xx88
**Defining Era of the Early 1990s** : The early 1990s were famously "the era of grunge," dominated by influential bands such as Nirvana, Pearl Jam, Soundgarden, Alice in Chains, The Smashing Pumpkins, and Stone Temple Pilots. These bands were known for their distinctive "slow and low" sound, often employing a "quiet loud dynamic." "Now, this of course was the era of grunge. We had Nirvana, Pearl Jam and Sound Garden, Analysis and Chains and so on." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Drop D Tuning** : Drop D tuning became nearly synonymous with the grunge genre, notably employed by bands such as Nirvana, Soundgarden, Pearl Jam, and Alice in Chains. This tuning, where the lowest string is dropped from E to D, makes guitar chords sound grittier, heavier, and more powerful, especially when combined with foot pedals and overdriven amps, thus becoming a defining characteristic of the genre's sound. "Drop D not only became synonymous with grunge, Nirvana, Sound Garden, Pearl Jam, Allison Chains, but it can be heard in a lot of other songs by the Foo Fighters, Muse, Tool, Green Day, Rage Against the Machine, and dozens of others." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w
**Early 1990s Dominance** : Grunge was a dominant and "awesome" form of alternative rock in the early 1990s, alongside industrial, Manchester, and Britpop. However, its appeal faded away by the late 1990s, contributing to a sense of disillusionment in the rock scene, particularly in the wake of Kurt Cobain's suicide. "The awesomeness of the alternation of the early 1990s, with grunge and industrial and Manchester and Britpop, had faded away." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-CT7yYXonIk
**Eddie Vedder as Grunge Icon** : Eddie Vedder, frontman for Pearl Jam, is recognized as a grunge icon, known for his serious social, political, and environmental views, a stark contrast to his early childhood modeling career. "Now, of course, Eddie is a grunge icon with serious social, political and environmental views." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dVJuITJyNTU
**Emergence in Early 1990s** : In January 1993, grunge was identified as a significant and growing music phenomenon, alongside the rise of Lollapalooza. This burgeoning popularity led radio station management to embrace an alternative format rather than switching to country music. "Grunge was happening. Lola Palusa was a thing..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bI6TTA-fn7c
**Fender Twin Reverb in Grunge** : The Fender Twin Reverb amplifier, known for its clean sound, surprisingly found use in the grunge era, which was largely characterized by distortion. Kurt Cobain notably used a Twin Reverb on Nirvana's "Bleach" album, demonstrating its unexpected versatility. "The amp even found use in the Grunge era, which seems a little weird given that Grunge was all about distortion." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Foundation of Commercial Alt-Rock Radio** : For many years, grunge became the "foundation" of commercial alt-rock radio's sound. From a ratings perspective, it was safer to play grunge or grunge-ish, guitar-based music, often with drop-tuning, rather than risk chances on truly new and different sounds. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Gone Trend** : Grunge is noted as a musical trend and subculture that has "gone," though its echoes persist. "They even grunge as gone, although its echoes remain." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Heaviness and Bleakness** : The characteristic "heaviness and bleakness of Grunge seemed a little too much" for audiences following Kurt Cobain's death. This shift in public sentiment led many to seek out lighter, more optimistic musical alternatives. "And suddenly the heaviness and bleakness of Grunge seemed a little too much." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Homogenization of Alt-Rock Radio** : Grunge's massive popularity effectively turned it into the definition of alt-rock, leading to a homogenization of sound across alternative radio stations. The varied sounds of the 80s were largely squeezed out, replaced by a focus on big guitars and screaming vocalists, as stations looked to national charts for programming cues. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Impact on INXS Sales** : The public's growing fascination with grunge, alongside alternative music, contributed to the underperformance of INXS albums like *Welcome to Wherever You Are* (1992) and *Full Moon, Dirty Hearts* (1993). "A victim of the public's growing fascination with grunge and all things alternative." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=23OgreyK66o
**Impact on Music Landscape** : The explosion of grunge in the early 1990s led to the complete crumbling of "the walls between mainstream rock and the alternative universe," thereby paving the way for other underground genres like Goth, industrial, various forms of electronica, and punk rock to gain mainstream recognition. "After Grunge blew up in the early 1990s, the walls between mainstream rock and the alternative universe crumbled completely." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eljIIqCu5gU
**Late 1991 Emergence and Teenage Engagement** : Grunge "hit in late 1991" and was a defining genre for teenagers, who "sucked up" music from bands like Nirvana, Pearl Jam, Alice in Chains, and Soundgarden during their formative years. This period was crucial for their musical identity formation. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m7MbV-eCLyE
**Leading 90s Music** : Grunge music was at the forefront of the "alternative revolution" in the early 1990s, defining a period from 1991 to 1995 with its new and impactful sound. "It's led by Grunge, but supported by all manner of alternative music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**Leading Alt-Rock Movement** : Grunge was the leading force at the dawn of the 1990s, serving as an "entry drug" that paved the way for the broader alternative rock movement. "Grunge, let the way. But that was just the entry drug." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Mid-90s Loss of Momentum** : By the mid-1990s, there was a widespread "feeling that Grunge was slowly losing momentum." Factors contributing to this included Nirvana's In Utero being a sales disappointment and other prominent grunge bands either evolving their sound or appearing derivative, creating an opportune opening for pop punk. "And besides, there was a feeling that Grunge was slowly losing momentum." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Moshing Goes Mainstream with Grunge (Early 1990s)** : Due to the Pacific Northwest's strong punk connections with California, moshing was introduced to the grunge scene. When grunge achieved mainstream success, moshing also became widespread, becoming a standard practice at rock gigs by about 1992. "When Grunge went mainstream, so did moshing. And by about 1992, everybody was doing it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KMx4bmq8o2Y
**Origins in Seattle** : Grunge originated entirely within the local Seattle indie scene, seeded by other indie music. Its earliest sounds were captured on compilations like "Deep Six," featuring bands such as The Melvins and Soundgarden. "That's some of the earliest sounds of grunge, arguably the most important alternative sound of the 1990s. And it started in the local Seattle Indy scene. All of it. And it was seeded by other indie music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Overlooked Pioneers** : The history of grunge has unfortunately overlooked important and influential figures like Tina Bell and her band Bam Bam, who were "doing the grunge thing" in Seattle as early as 1983. This highlights a gap in the recognized narrative of the genre's origins. "Now this is a sad example of important and influential people being left out of music history." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VnXBJqKwkrw
**Pacific Northwest Birthplace** : Grunge music originated in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States. In the late 1980s and early 1990s, this area was geographically isolated from major national tour routes, leading to the development of north-south musical trade routes. "Remember that the Pacific Northwest was where Grunge was born." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KMx4bmq8o2Y
**Parental Connections to Era** : A number of the musical offspring discussed have direct parental connections to the original Grunge era. "This last bunch all have parental connections to the original Grunge era." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dY7uB_QQWBc
**Prominence in Early 1990s** : Grunge was identified as "the big thing" in new music during the early 1990s, dominating the musical landscape of the era. "The big thing of course was Grunge." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t2uXHP9cBdM
**Rise and Dominance in Early 1990s** : Triggered by a terrible recession and the First Gulf War in 1990, and the coming of age of Generation X, grunge and alternative music culture rapidly rose to dominance in 1991, clearing out the old guard of pop and hair metal. This music resonated with Gen X's feelings and anger, enjoying widespread popularity until about mid-1996. "In about nine months, the old guard and the pop cohort were cleared out by the rise of grunge and alternative music culture." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Y6nHw5xx88
**Rise in US (1990)** : The beginnings of Grunge in the US in the spring of 1990 contributed to rock's quick resurgence, alongside Madchester in the UK. "There was the rise of Madchester in the UK and the beginnings of Grunge in the US." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Rise to Mainstream Dominance (Early 1990s)** : Grunge emerged as the dominant force in alternative rock by 1992, supplanting the mainstream. Riff-ready songs from bands like Pearl Jam, Soundgarden, Alice in Chains, and especially Nirvana, caused alt-rock to coalesce around hard guitar rock. Its immense popularity led to record sales and concert tickets selling in ever-increasing amounts. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Seattle Origins and Drug Culture (Early 1990s)** : The Grunge "explosion" in the early 1990s saw bands like Alice in Chains emerge from Seattle, characterized by their dark themes and the significant role of drugs, particularly heroin, in their music and the lives of their members. "Allison Chains had been one of the first and one of the biggest bands to come out of Seattle during the Grunge explosion in the early 1990s, but they were also one of the darkest drugs, especially heroin, played a huge part not only the band's music, but in the day-to-day lives the guys in the group." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2RKchFW42Jc
**Shaping the Grunge Revolution – Butch Vig's Production** : The grunge revolution of the early 1990s was significantly shaped by Butch Vig, primarily through his work as a producer rather than a performer. His production of Nirvana's "Nevermind" was a pivotal moment, following his work on Killdozer's "For Ladies Only," which first garnered widespread attention. "Butch Vig a guy who helped shape the whole grunge revolution of the early 1990s Not necessarily as a performer But as a producer." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo
