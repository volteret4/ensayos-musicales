**Influence on Post-New Wave Techno Pop (N/A)** : Infected a number of post-new wave techno pop bands, turning them from "happy, bouncy, and lightweight" to "dark and powerful and even sinister." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RvzvcACkYqk

**Least Understood (N/A)** : One of the least understood post-punk genres by the general public and mass media. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RvzvcACkYqk

**Misunderstanding (N/A)** : Can be very misunderstood, often perceived as scary and intimidating to the uninitiated due to its look and sound. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RvzvcACkYqk

**Rise to Prominence (Early 1980s)** : Became "quite the rage" by the early 1980s. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RvzvcACkYqk

**Softer and Slower Sound (N/A)** : Compared to industrial music, Goth can be "softer and slower" and "introspective." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RvzvcACkYqk
**Sub-Types and Terminology (N/A)** : Includes traditional Goths, neo-Goths, mall Goths, mini-Goths, and baby bats (teenagers too young for Goth clubs). Other regional terms include "death rock" (Los Angeles), "Gogan" (Australia), "Cucuracha" (Spain), and "dark" (Italy). "Spooky kid" is used for followers of Marilyn Manson. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RvzvcACkYqk
**Worldwide Presence (N/A)** : One of the strongest, most solid, and most widespread rock sub-cultures, found globally in places like Los Angeles, Australia, Spain, Italy, France, Belgium, Scandinavia, Russia, Japan, and New Zealand. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RvzvcACkYqk
