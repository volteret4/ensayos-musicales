# genre - Goth

## curiosities
**Cross-Pollination with Industrial** : A significant next age in Goth's development came from cross-pollination with its close cousin, industrial music. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Cultural Spread** : Its influence extended beyond just music, creeping into film, fashion, television, literature, and "every corner of pop culture." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Death Goths (Purists)** : A group of performers and fans who are faithful to the original musical, fashion, and lifestyle aesthetics first created at the end of the 1970s. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Emergence in Late 1970s** : what is now called goth music did not emerge until bands like Bauhaus appeared in the late 1970s. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Enduring Fashion Influence** : Goth fashion has spread throughout pop culture, including movies, TV, mainstream fashion, literature, and art, and has been around for decades, showing no signs of going away. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Expansion in 1990s** : Its influence continued to expand through the latter half of the 1990s, leading to the creation of more specific niches, with many bands remaining deep underground where they and their fans prefer to stay. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Fan Subtypes** : Includes traditional Goths, neo-Goths, Mall Goths, Mini-Goths, and "Baby Bats" (teenagers too young to enter Goth clubs). ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**First Generation Goth (Late 1970s - ~1985)** : The initial period of development for Goth music and culture. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Formalization of Culture** : Aspects of Goth culture began to be formalized through the spread of Goth-themed magazines, such as Propaganda. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Goth-tinged Genres** : Includes industrial, some emo-ish bands (e.g., 30 Seconds to Mars, My Chemical Romance), and various metal scenes (death metal, doom metal, black metal). ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Internal Stratification** : Goth itself began to cleave and stratify as it adapted to different musical, cultural, and fashion environments. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Longevity as a Sub-culture** : Unlike many rock-based sub-cultures, it did not wither and die; instead, it spread and grew stronger, becoming one of the "strongest, most solid, most widespread of all the sub-cultures in rock" and "one of the longest surviving alternative rock sub-cultures." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Misunderstanding** : Can be very misunderstood, with its look and sound often appearing "scary and intimidating to the uninitiated," and extremists bringing a bad name to the community. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Musical Characteristics** : Can be "softer and slower" and "introspective" compared to industrial music. It typically has a keyboard-based approach more in common with early 1980s technopop and smoother modern electronic music, making it "a little more... danceable." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Origin Story** : Gothrock began as a spin-off from the British punk of the 1970s. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Part of Diverse 80s Alt-Rock** : was a genre played on alt-rock radio stations in the late 80s, contributing to their breadth of music. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Possible Proto-Origins in 1974** : Alan Cross theorizes that *Phantom of the Paradise* (1974) and its fictional band The Undead, with corpse paint and macabre themes, could be considered a proto-goth group. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Public Misconceptions** : One of the least understood post-punk genres by the general public and mass media, often receiving widespread attention only when linked to violent acts or "gun-wielding nutcases." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Reality of Goth Community** : The "vast, vast majority of people associated with Goth culture are gentle, literate people who just like to hang out with like-minded folks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Regional Terminology** : In Los Angeles, "Death Rock" was used; in Australia, "Gogan"; in Spain, "Ku Khoracha"; in Italy, "Dark." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Second Generation Goth (Latter Half of 1980s)** : A period where Goth spread far beyond the UK, carried by bands like Sisters of Mercy, Siouxsie and the Banshees, and The Cure. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Worldwide Presence** : Described as "worldwide," with Goths scenes in France, Belgium, Scandinavia, Russia, Japan, and New Zealand, essentially "pretty much everywhere you find rock music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
