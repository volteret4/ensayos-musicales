**Early Influences and Evolution (Late 90s - 2000s)** : By the late 1990s, influences from various Southern rap scenes (Memphis' dark sound, Houston's chopped & screwed) converged in Atlanta under the name "Dirty South." In the 2000s, this dirty mix distilled into trap, a genre that eliminated funk and soul, focusing on the hardest, most synthetic, and street-oriented elements. "A finales de los 90, todas estas influencias y la actitud chula es cada enlanta, empezaron a la chocaria mezclarse bajo un mismo nombre, el Disney House." ← La salvaje historia del TRAP ESPAÑOL (2013-2026) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4hBhiU5jj6s
**Emergence in the Southern US (80s-90s)** : Trap originated in various rap scenes in the peripheries of different cities in the Southern United States during the 1980s and 1990s. These scenes had a distinct philosophy and sound, independent of the dominant East and West Coast styles. "Durante los años 80 y 90, en la perifería de diferentes ciudades del sur de los Estados Unidos, surgen varias escenas de rap, con una filosofía y un sonido muy distinto al dominante de las costas este y yo este." ← La salvaje historia del TRAP ESPAÑOL (2013-2026) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4hBhiU5jj6s
**Etymology: "Trap Houses" of Atlanta** : The genre's name comes from the "trap houses" in Atlanta—abandoned houses where crack cocaine was cooked and sold 24/7. This origin reflects the genre's lyrical content, which often chronicled street dealings. "El género tomó el nombre de las trap houses dan lanta, casas abandonadas donde se cocinaba y se vendía crack las 24 horas." ← La salvaje historia del TRAP ESPAÑOL (2013-2026) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4hBhiU5jj6s
**Foundational Sound Elements** : The non-glamorous aesthetic, raw and direct sound of Memphis rap, with its dirty drums, heavy sub-basses, and machine-gun-like hi-hats, established the crucial groundwork for what would later explode as trap music in Atlanta and globally. "La estética y la actitud sin glamour, el sonido, créidirecto, con esas cajas sucias, su graves pesados y hi-hats que sonaban como una metralleta, todo eso terminó de sentar las bases para lo que después explotarían la lanta y arrasto del mundo." ← Memphis Rap： Cuando el rap era puro terror | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lWBpNa-xSt4

**Lasting Impact on Music Production** : While pure trap as a genre largely disappeared, its aesthetic changes and rhythms conquered the music scene. Auto-Tune, triplet hi-hats, and trap slang became ubiquitous in commercial pop and indie music. **Lyrical Themes** : Trap lyrics, popularized by artists like T.I., Jeezy, and Gucci Mane, were direct chronicles of street life and hustling. They did not seek moral or political lessons, but rather depicted the day-to-day realities of the street. "marcaron al género con letras que no buscaban de elecciones morales ni políticas, sino que eran crónicas del trapicheo y el día a día en la calle." ← La salvaje historia del TRAP ESPAÑOL (2013-2026) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4hBhiU5jj6s
**Memphis Rap as Foundation** : The raw and dark sound of Memphis rap, characterized by cowbells, syncopated rhythms, and heavy sub-basses, ultimately laid the groundwork for genres such as trap. **Role of Autotune** : While Auto-Tune was designed for pitch correction, trap artists exaggerated its effect, deforming the human voice in a peculiar way to create an abrasive, narcotic-like sound. It was seen as anti-rap—a cold, synthetic music for a similarly cold world. "El autotune se había creado para correcir tonos a la hora de cantar, pero si los exageras, deforma la voz humana de una forma muy peculiar y así lo soltrab para que sonar al estridente y sin arcóticas." ← La salvaje historia del TRAP ESPAÑOL (2013-2026) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4hBhiU5jj6s
**Sonic Characteristics** : Trap sound was "plastic," distinct from the organic boom-bap of dominant hip-hop. It used Roland TR-808 drum machines, creating frantic triplet hi-hats and deep sub-bass instead of samples and real drums. Rhythms typically ranged from 60 to 90 BPM, often doubled for more hi-hats, accompanied by dark synth melodies and heavy Auto-Tune. "El trap directamente sonaba plástico. En vez de sample si baterías reales usaba la rola ante R08 y similares, que en lugar de marcar el ritmo lo ametrellaban con tresillos freníticos y sub graves profundos." ← La salvaje historia del TRAP ESPAÑOL (2013-2026) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4hBhiU5jj6s
**Spanish Trap's Beginnings (Prototrap)** : In Spain, the "prototrap" scene emerged after El director Sao introduced Southern rap in 2002. Spanish rappers and producers began experimenting with these sounds and referencing trap. "Y poco a poco, diferentes raperos y productores, empezan a coquetear con este rap soleño y a referenciar al trap, generándose así una escena que podríamos llamar Prototrap." ← La salvaje historia del TRAP ESPAÑOL (2013-2026) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4hBhiU5jj6s
