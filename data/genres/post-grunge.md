# genre - Post-Grunge

## curiosities
**Lyrical Approach** : A key distinction of Post-Grunge was its lyrical approach, where lyricists "used the first person approach a lot more," in contrast to the more metaphorical framing often found in Grunge lyrics. "And there was a slightly different lyrical approach, where as a lot of Grunge lyrics were framed in metaphors, Post Grunge lyricists used the first person approach. A lot more." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Musical Characteristics** : Post-Grunge featured "slicker" production, "more pronounced" pop sensibilities in songwriting and arrangements, and guitars that were "not too nearly as low" as those in Grunge. "The production, a little slicker. The pop sensibilities in the songwriting and arrangements, a little more pronounced. The guitars are too nearly as low." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Original Insult and Later Redefinition** : The term "Post-Grunge" was initially invented to describe bands that followed Grunge but were seen as derivative, often considered an insult meaning "unoriginal or bad clones." Later, the definition was toned down to mean a hybrid sound merging alt-rock (specifically the Grunge part) with more mainstream aesthetics. "This term was invented to describe bands that came after Grunge, but really weren't into that sound at all. But by listening to them, you could tell that Grunge must have happened." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
