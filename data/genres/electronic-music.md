# genre - Electronic music

## curiosities
**Early 1980s Experimentation** : By the early 1980s, significant experimentation with electricity and new machines like computers and samplers led to an abundance of new and interesting music. This era saw music being created by machines, orchestrated by computers, and programmed by individuals with a punk ethos. "And by the time the decade ended, there was plenty of new and interesting music to go around." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**Early Development in West Germany** : The first place where new electronic music truly took hold was in West Germany, fostering an avant-garde scene near Cologne by 1953, where experimentation with oscillators, wave generators, and tape loops flourished. "The place where this new electronic music really first caught on was in West Germany." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE
**Impact of TONTO Synthesizer** : The TONTO synthesizer, built by Malcolm Cecil and Robert Margouleff, had a massive influence on the world of electronic music by allowing for sounds and multi-track possibilities never before heard. Its capabilities led synthesizer manufacturers to significantly advance technology in the 1970s, producing smaller, faster, and cheaper electronic keyboards that in turn fueled the rise of Techno Pop. "Tonto influenced the shape of rock music and the use of the recording studio. And Tonto was a big reason synthesizing manufacturers worked so hard to advance the technology in the 1970s, creating these smaller, faster, cheaper electronic keyboards." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
**Importance of Daphne Oram and Delia Derbyshire** : The work of Daphne Oram and Delia Derbyshire at the BBC Radiophonic Workshop and in their independent careers is considered very important in the history of electronic music. They pioneered the use of early electronic equipment like sine wave oscillators, magnetic tape recorders, and tape manipulation to compose with synthetic sounds for radio, TV, and plays, shaping the foundations of the genre. "This is very important stuff in the history of electronic music, and she continued with her experimental compositions until 1975." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vV1APhjBt9M
**John Frusciante's Work During Hiatus** : During his time away from the Red Hot Chili Peppers, John Frusciante was actively involved in making electronic music, though he continued to practice guitar. This period marked a different creative direction for him before his return to rock music. "Practicing guitar is something I was always doing even though I'm making electronic music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Nr-3Y3UWQcI
**Pioneering Composers and Equipment** : The foundational work of Daphne Oram and Delia Derbyshire in the BBC Radiofonc Workshop, utilizing sine wave oscillators and magnetic tape machines before the advent of synthesizers, was crucial for the development of electronic music and EDM. Their experiments in composing with synthetic sounds paved the way for future innovations in the genre. "If you're into electronic music and EDM, you should know that these next two women are essential to what you'd listen to." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
**Surge in Interest in West Germany (Post-Mini Moog)** : Following the 1971 invention of the Mini Moog, there was a huge surge in interest in electronic music within West Germany, making it a hotbed for experimentation and the emergence of leading electronic groups like Kraftwerk. "Again, people went nuts in West Germany. There was a huge surge in interest in electronic music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE
**Vaporwave Micro-Genre** : Vaporwave is described as a subgenre, or more accurately a micro-genre, of electronic music, indicating its specific sonic characteristics and targeted audience within the broader electronic sphere. "This is a subgenre of electronic music. No, wait, it's more like a micro-genre, which means that it's very, very specific in the way it sounds, and it's aimed at a small and specific audience." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tdTs-4Irv8c
