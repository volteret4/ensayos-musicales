# genre - Jazz

## curiosities
**20th Century Dominance** : Jazz dominated the first half of the 20th century and was the primary force pushing Western culture forward before being displaced by rock and roll in the 1950s. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z7glrAoXtkA
**Birth and Dominance (1910-1950s)** : Jazz emerged as an offshoot of Ragtime in New Orleans and spread North during the Great Migration. It reigned as the dominant form of American popular culture from the early 1900s until the mid-1950s. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_Pi7JLwAeOI
**Early Use of Long-Playing Albums (June 1948)** : The long-playing album (LP) first appeared in June 1948, initially used for jazz recordings due to the format's expense. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Sc9HApnyBLM
**Evolution of Time Signatures** : Jazz played a central role in the "Great Metric Shift," moving from the 2/4 time of Ragtime into the 4/4 time that now dominates 90% of all popular music. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_Pi7JLwAeOI
**Lenny Kravitz's Background** : Lenny Kravitz was exposed to jazz early in life and his father was a jazz promoter. Vernon Reid of Living Colour could play jazz. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Tim Buckley's Exploration** : Tim Buckley explored elements of jazz in his music during his career. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MmXDbXI6Sqk
**Traditional Album Format** : prior to 1965, albums were primarily reserved for "proper music" like classical recordings, jazz, soundtracks, and Broadway cast recordings. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
