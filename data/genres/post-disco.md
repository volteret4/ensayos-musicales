# genre - Post-Disco

## curiosities
**Definition** : Post-disco refers to dance music that emerged from the disco boom of the late 1970s. "Post-disco obviously dance music born out of the disco boom of the late 70s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
**Emergence from Post Punk** : Post Disco began to emerge from the "post punk folks" as the stigma of dancing in clubs dissipated after the decline of mainstream disco. It incorporated significant pop and R&B elements, reflecting a new direction for dance music. "Some of this new dance momentum peeled off into something called Post Disco." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The Post-Punk Explosion Part 4： Alt-Dance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0FYq5MBdHw4&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Pop and R&B Elements** : This genre, a direct successor to disco in the post-punk era, was characterized by its incorporation of pop and R&B elements. Artists like Madonna, Prince, and Michael Jackson (with "Thriller") were associated with this sound, which was distinct from the earlier, more monotonous disco.
