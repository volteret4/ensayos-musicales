# genre - Rhythm and Blues

## curiosities
**"Race Music" Label and Condemnation** : In its early days, Rhythm and Blues, particularly when performed by Black artists, was referred to as "Race music" and heavily criticized by religious authorities. It was condemned as "heathen" and characterized by "unnatural carnal rhythms." "Race music, which is what they called rhythm and blues, made by black performers back then, also took a lot of the heat for being heathen." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g-tBn8dsSqw
**Coalescence and Foundation for Rock and Roll** : Rhythm and Blues coalesced as a distinct musical form, drawing players from musicals, saloons, bars, juke joints, and brothels. These combos were crucial in creating the musical combinations that would ultimately give rise to rock and roll, serving as a vital precursor to the genre. "Meanwhile, another form of music dubbed rhythm and blue is coalesced, featuring players from the musicals and saloons and bars and juke joints and brothels. And it was these combos who helped create the combinations of what would become rock and roll." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dJiSZ4X0F2g
**Emergence of Rhythm and Blues (Late 1940s)** : Rhythm and Blues (R&B) emerged in the late 1940s as big jazz bands downsized due to economic pressures, wartime, and musician strikes. Smaller groups began to emphasize rhythm and beat, contrasting with the less danceable bebop. This new focus, combined with influences from country, western, hillbilly, and traditional blues, created the fertile ground for the birth of rock in the 1950s. "And by the late 1940s, this became known as rhythm and blues." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_Pi7JLwAeOI
**Foundation of Rock and Roll** : Rhythm and blues is one of the primary Black Music genres from which rock and roll, and subsequently alt rock, substantially descended. "It's only right, given that rock and roll is descended substantially from Black Music, the blues, rhythm and blues, jump blues, jazz, gospel, and so many other genres." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_1NNvxgx8uA
**R&B's Origins in the Great Migration – Cotton Gin, Slavery, and Urban Demographics** : The origins of rhythm and blues trace back to the invention of the cotton gin in 1794, which entrenched slavery, and the subsequent dispossession of African-American land, which drove the Great Migration from the rural South to northern cities from the late nineteenth century through the early twentieth. This demographic shift was the crucible in which jazz and blues evolved into rhythm and blues. "The cotton gin (1794) entrenched slavery, dispossession drove the Great Migration, and the resulting demographic shift was the crucible in which jazz and blues evolved into R&B." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f2KA97T4u08
**RCA's Saris (Not Red) Vinyl Coding** : For its 7-inch singles, RCA used "saris" colored vinyl for Rhythm and Blues releases, specifically clarifying that it was "not red." This was part of a short-lived attempt to color-code genres by vinyl pressing color. "Rhythm and blues was saris. Not red, saris." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VlIErdSSVOM
