# genre - New Rock

## curiosities
**Current Broad Term** : "New Rock" is presently the most common phrase used to describe contemporary rock music. This term gained currency as a simpler and more fitting descriptor compared to older terms like "alternative," which had lost its original meaning, or more academic terms like "post-modern" and "modern rock." "And currently, though, the most common phrase is just New Rock. It's new, it's rock, so it fits." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z8BTWo66SDI
**Definition in the 21st Century** : In the first decade of the 21st century, "New Rock" was adopted as a generic term for any new rock and roll music. Its authenticity and approval are enhanced if its DNA can be traced back to the punk rock and new wave movements of the 1970s, or its sonic lineage linked to artists like David Bowie, Kraftwerk, Iggy Pop, and The Velvet Underground. "If it's got any strand of rock and roll in its DNA and it's new, then it stands to reason that it is new rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ft8nruhWCas
**Evolution of "Punk" Terminology** : The popularity of "Punk" magazine led to bands featured within its pages being referred to as "punk bands." This shorthand quickly evolved into common usage, where people would simply say they liked "punk music" or "punk rock," illustrating how a seemingly small cultural artifact can trigger a cascading series of changes, akin to the butterfly effect. "Pretty soon anyone who was featured in the magazine was being referred to as a punk band. They were shorthand when someone asked what kind of music you like, you could reply, I like groups that they write about in punk magazine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1y2UuU-me_s
**Greatest Rhythm Sections** : The genre of New Rock is host to some of the greatest rhythm sections, emphasizing the crucial role of bassists and drummers in establishing a band's foundation and groove. "These are New Rock's greatest rhythm sections." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CVPPymc_qrk
**Iggy Pop's Historical Importance** : Iggy Pop is recognized as one of the most important historical figures in New Rock, underscoring his foundational contribution to the genre. "It's Iggy Pop, one of the most important historical figures in New Rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OxctiPr0l-8
**Origin of the Term "Punk" (1976)** : The modern usage of the word "punk" as an adjective for hard, fast, and primitive rock emerged from a fanzine created by Legs McNeil in 1976. The magazine, named "Punk" after The Dictators' album art, featured bands like The Dictators, Lou Reed, and The Ramones, leading to the term becoming synonymous with their musical style. "The group that inspired the modern usage of the word punk when it came to rock and roll." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1y2UuU-me_s
**The Stooges' Foundational Impact** : The Stooges were considered "absolutely bloody essential" in laying the groundwork for the New Rock genre, alongside punk and alternative rock, in the decades that followed. "absolutely bloody essential in setting the stage for all the punk, alternative, and New Rock that was to come in the decades ahead." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0hnBC0y59QA
**Top 100 Moments Broadcast (2004)** : The "Top 100 Moments in New Rock" series originally aired in the spring of 2004, providing a historical perspective on pivotal events in the genre's development up to that point. The series aimed to revisit where music had been and how much it had changed. "Now bear in mind that these shows originally aired in the spring of 2004." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MTDY6qT11Dc
