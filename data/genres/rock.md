# genre - Rock

## curiosities
**12-Year Pop and Rock Cycle** : For over 50 years, dating back to the early 1950s (birth date estimated around 1951), rock and pop popularity fluctuated in an inverse 12-year cycle. Rock ascended from 1951-1957, then declined until 1964 (Beatles). It surged again until 1970, then faded for pop. It returned with punk in 1976, faded 1982-83 (new pop stars). Another comeback in 1990 with alternative/grunge until 1996, then pop returned, and rock was declared "dead" until 2000-2001. Remained dominant until 2008, then fell off. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nX8uAFDzDYM
**African American Roots** : Ice-T often pointed out that rock originated with African American artists like Chuck Berry and Little Richard. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Aging Demographic** : Most of the world's biggest rock acts are now middle-aged or older, with few new "superstars" emerging to replace them. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PmwfC4HR3_I
**Death of 12-Year Cycle** : This cycle, which should have seen a rock revival after 2014, died. This is attributed to streaming and the decline of traditional gatekeepers (radio, record stores, labels, music magazines, cable music video channels), leading to a fragmented, non-monocultural music landscape. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nX8uAFDzDYM
**Decline as Mainstream Force** : Receives less label time and money for promotion compared to hip hop, rap, R&B, and pop, as it has demonstrated less streaming success in the "musical moneyball" era. This explains why rock has not been a big mainstream force as it once was. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Sc9HApnyBLM
**Dominance of White Artists** : Rock, alt-rock, and indie rock have been predominantly white, leading to questions about cultural identity, exclusion, and racial barriers for people of color. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Early Package Tours (1950s)** : In the early days of rock in the 1950s, package tours were the most efficient and cost-effective way to send acts on tour. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
