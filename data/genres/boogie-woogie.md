# genre - Boogie Woogie

## curiosities
**Fats Domino's Signature Style** : Fats Domino was celebrated for his unique Boogie Woogie piano style, which was characterized by its reliance on triplets rather than the more conventional, straightforward rhythms often used by other pianists. "He was known as one of the best Boogie Boogie players around, with a unique style based on triplets, rather than the more straightforward rhythms many Boogie pianists used." ← Episode 8： The Fat Man and Fats Domino | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A-1R-DbVdcM
**Piano Playing Style** : Boogie Woogie is a distinct style of piano playing that became popular in the 1930s. It is characterized by the left hand playing a strong, repeating bass line, often referred to as "eight to the bar," while the right hand plays decorative melodic passages over it. This bass line and melody combination became the cornerstone of rock piano playing, as well as influencing country music. **Revival and Mainstream Popularity (1938)** : By 1938, Boogie Woogie had somewhat fallen out of fashion after a brief popularity a decade earlier. However, the "Spirituals to Swing" concerts organized by John Hammond, featuring artists like Pete Johnson and Albert Ammons, dramatically changed this. Suddenly, Boogie Woogie became "the biggest thing in music," leading every big band to adapt it to their style, and its influence appeared in numerous popular songs of the early 1940s.
