# genre - Alt-rock

## curiosities
**"Golden Age" of Commercial Alt-Rock Radio** : The pre-grunge era of commercial alt-rock radio in the 1980s is retrospectively considered by some to be a "golden age." This period was characterized by hardcore music fans staffing stations, who were on a mission to deliver good music, despite low salaries and the constant threat of format changes. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**1980s Culture** : In the 1980s, the alt-rock scene, including bands, managers, labels, DJs, record stores, and fans, embraced an "outsider-ness," desiring no connection with the mainstream, and fostered a "very tribal" environment. "People involved in the scene, the bands and the managers, the labels and the DJs, the record stores and fans, reveled in their outsider-ness." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2wZydttn4Cs
**1980s Radio Diversity** : Commercial alt-rock radio stations in the 1980s exhibited staggering breadth in their playlists, featuring techno pop, industrial, various forms of punk, early rap and hip hop, goth, British indie, remixes, ethereal dream pop, and guitar rock, with each station having a distinct local flavor due to a lack of centralized charts. "This kind of variety was typical for alt rock stations all across North America. No two stations were exactly the same, each had its own local flavor." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2wZydttn4Cs
**1990s Dominance** : Alt-rock was the dominant music and culture for about 10 years, particularly throughout the 1990s, during which it fueled the rise of the North American festival scene. "After 10 years of being the dominant sort of music and culture, Alt Rock itself was on the wane." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**80s "Outsider-ness"** : In the 1980s, alt-rock was characterized by its "outsider-ness," with participants in the scene, including bands, managers, labels, DJs, record stores, and fans, reveling in their detachment from the mainstream. There was a mutual disinterest between the alt-rock scene and the mainstream. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**90s Scene for Discovery** : The 1990s alt-rock scene is highlighted as an area ripe for rediscovery for those in their mid-40s who loved grunge as teenagers. The scene contained numerous acts, beyond the most popular, that were "adjacent" and went unnoticed at the time, offering "new to you" material with the power and energy of that era. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m7MbV-eCLyE
**Alt-Rock Shrines** : Alt-rock has many sites that have become sacred to its fans, serving as pilgrimage destinations. These locations range from pubs where bands formed to album cover locations and artists' former homes, drawing visitors from around the world. These modern shrines are akin to historical rock and roll landmarks like Buddy Holly's plane crash site or Elvis's Graceland. "There are many dozens of alt rock shrines, but I've picked out my favorites." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sKHdTSfDd6E
**Amorphous Definition** : Alternative music was defined by being non-mainstream, ignored by most radio stations, aesthetically "a little left of center," and considered "weird by the majority," qualifying it as alternative by default as there was no other way to categorize it. "If it was a non-mainstream and ignored by most radio stations, b, a little left of center in terms of aesthetics, and c, considered weird by the majority, well then it qualified as alternative by default, simply because there was no other way to categorize it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Billboard Chart Coverage** : Billboard magazine features a specialty chart dedicated to alt-rock music, among its couple of dozen specific genre charts. "Billboard magazine. This is where we find the hot 100 singles, the top 200 album charts, and a couple of dozen specialty charts for dance, alt-rock, hip-hop, and so on." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TsiYLGGSJ2U
**Current State (21st Century)** : In the 21st century, alt-rock radio has "loosened up" and "weaned itself at least a little bit from Grunge guitars," experiencing an explosion of indie rock and a greater variety of sounds, though some argue there are now "too few big guitar bands." "Happily, things have loosened up and Indy Rock has exploded. Alt Rock Radio has weaned itself at least a little bit from Grunge guitars." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2wZydttn4Cs
**Diversity of the 1990s Revolution** : The Alt-rock revolution of the 1990s was notable for its immense diversity, with sounds being incredibly varied due to the "amorphous" nature of alternative music itself. "One of the great things about the alt-rock revolution of the 1990s was its diversity." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Foundational Conditions for Rise in 1990s** : The emergence of alt-rock in the 1990s was underpinned by several foundational changes. These included the demographic influence of Generation X, vast financial flows in the music industry, the pervasive power of music videos, the accurate sales tracking provided by SoundScan, the adoption of digital recording technology, the widespread use of sampling, the proliferation of personal computers, and the mass adoption of the internet. "Let's review these foundational things that were required for Alt Rock to take off in the 1990s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r8AazBVoS7g
**Future Resurgence** : Despite bottoming out at the end of the 1990s, the seeds for alt-rock's next cycle were already being planted, with a new crop of indie bands like The Arctic Monkeys and Arcade Fire poised to bring it back into the spotlight. "But even as the 90s died out, in alt-rock bottomed out, the seeds for the next cycle were already being planted." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Golden Age (1980s)** : In retrospect, some consider the 1980s to be the "golden age of commercial alt-rock radio" due to the immense musical variety and the passionate, mission-driven staff dedicated to delivering "good music to the masses." "But in retrospect, some consider this to be the golden age of commercial alt-rock radio." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2wZydttn4Cs
**Gorillaz Debut Album Component** : Alt-rock was one of the genres that contributed to the experimental sound of the Gorillaz's debut album, alongside trip-hop, Brit-pop, dub, reggae, and hip-hop. "The music was rather experimental, more trip-hop, some alt-rock, bits of Brit-pop, little dub, little reggae, and a bunch of hip-hop." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iVdTCqpd798
**Grunge as "Training Wheels"** : Grunge served as "Alt Rock with training wheels," successfully luring millions of people into the alternative music scene with its accessible yet distinct approach to traditional guitar rock. This exposure encouraged listeners to explore deeper into alt-rock and other genres, enriching their musical lives. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Grunge's Catalyst for Mainstream Influx** : The arrival of Grunge "ripped a hole in the music space-time continuum," creating a "tear into a parallel universe" that allowed a flood of previously evolving alternative sounds and scenes to invade the mainstream. "Then along came Grunge, the biggest sound of the decade. It ripped a hole in the music space-time continuum." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Grunge's Impact (1990s)** : With the rise of grunge in the early 1990s, particularly bands like Pearl Jam, Soundgarden, Alice in Chains, and Nirvana, alt-rock began to "coalesce around hard guitar rock," becoming less diverse and eventually supplanting the mainstream. "Alt Rock began to coalesce around hard guitar rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2wZydttn4Cs
**Homogenization of Sound (1990s)** : Grunge's immense popularity led to the homogenization of alt-rock radio playlists, as program directors across North America began to follow the trends of larger stations like KROQ in Los Angeles, largely squeezing out the varied sounds of the 1980s in favor of big guitars and screaming vocalists. "The result was an unstoppable march towards a homogeneity in sound." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2wZydttn4Cs
**Hope for Electronica as Savior** : For a few weeks in the spring of 1997, there were predictions that Alt Rock would be "saved" by Electronica, especially after The Prodigy's album *Fat of the Land* topped charts worldwide, though this ultimately did not materialize. "And for a few weeks in the spring of 1997, there were predictions that All Rock would be saved for this amorphous thing called electronica, especially after the prodigy had a number one album all over the world with Fat of the Land." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Impact of Generational Shift** : The decline of alt-rock was partly due to a new generation, Generation Y, entering their years of musical awareness, who preferred pop-oriented tastes over brooding grunge or intense post-punk alt-rock. "Generation Y just wasn't into brooding grunge or any of the kind of intense post punk all rock that their forebearers were." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Impact of Grunge on Alt-Rock** : Grunge effectively "ruined" the diverse nature of 80s alt-rock by causing it to coalesce around hard guitar rock, leading to a homogenization of sound. As record labels and sales surged, the focus shifted predominantly to guitar-based music, marginalizing other varied sounds that were once prominent. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Late 1990s Decline** : By the end of the 1990s, alt-rock began to wane as artists either grew up, moved on, or broke up, and the music fragmented with many one-hit wonders. New artists often seemed derivative, leading to a "Creed Syndrome" where the music felt watered down. "After 10 years of being the dominant sort of music and culture, Alt Rock itself was on the wane." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Late 90s Decline and Splintering** : By 1997, Alt Rock was struggling to maintain public attention as Gen X aged, the economy improved, and Gen Y ushered in a pop phase. The genre had "splintered into a million different subgenres," resulting in a proliferation of one-hit wonders and a general downturn. "All Rock was definitely struggling to keep the public's attention." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Post-Grunge Homogeneity on Radio** : The immense popularity of grunge led to alt-rock radio stations across North America adopting a more homogeneous sound. Program directors began to mimic playlists from larger stations like K-Rock in Los Angeles, rather than developing local flavors, resulting in a focus on big guitars and screaming vocalists. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Post-Grunge Playlisting Conservatism** : After the grunge era, commercial alt-rock radio became conservative, preferring to play "grunge or grunge-ish" (guitar-based, often with drop-tuning) music rather than taking chances on genuinely new and different sounds. This approach, driven by a desire for stable ratings and revenues, led to tightened playlists and a diminished variety. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Post-Grunge Slump (Late 1990s)** : After the initial grunge bands "died off" and the "grunge well basically went dry," the late 1990s experienced a period where "nothing concrete to replace it" emerged, leading to "Dreck-like New Metal" and a grim outlook for the genre. "The original Grunge bands died off and we were left with Dreck-like New Metal." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2wZydttn4Cs
**Pre-90s Choice Overload** : Before the 1990s, those into alternative music were "spoiled for choice" due to the thriving multiple genres, sub-genres, and even sub-sub-sub-sub-sub-sub-genres within the alt-rock universe, ensuring there was something for everyone. "This means that if you were into alternative music before the 1990s, you were spoiled for choice." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Pre-Grunge Diversity in 80s Radio** : In the 1980s, commercial alt-rock radio stations were not connected by industry charts or tracked airplay, leading to a "staggering breadth of music" on playlists. This included techno pop, industrial, various punk styles, early rap/hip pop, Goth, British indie, dance remixes, ethereal dream pop, and a smattering of guitar rock, with no two stations sounding exactly alike. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Resurgence of Variety Post-2000s** : In recent times, alt-rock radio has "loosened up" and "weaned itself" from its over-reliance on grunge guitars, leading to an explosion of indie rock. The variety of sounds on alt-rock radio playlists is now much greater than it has been in quite some time, although some argue there are too few big guitar bands today. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Shift from Alternative to Mainstream** : The flood of alt-rock into the mainstream, triggered by Grunge, was so strong that the term "alternative music" eventually lost its original meaning, as "Alternative became mainstream," making it no longer an alternative to anything except the artists it displaced. "This flood was so strong that it wasn't long before the term alternative music lost any meaning. Alternative became mainstream, meaning that it wasn't an alternative to anything really, except the pushed out artists that would now form the basis of a new radio format called Classic Rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**The Alt Rock Gold Rush (1990s)** : The introduction of SoundScan technology in 1991 sparked an "Alt Rock Gold Rush" within the music industry. Once labels had accurate data showing that "weirdo music" like alt-rock was selling massive units without significant marketing, they began to invest heavily in promoting it and signing more talent. This direct link between sales data and label investment fueled the genre's explosive growth. "The Alt Rock Gold Rush was on." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r8AazBVoS7g
**Vinyl Adherence (Post-CD Era)** : Many alt-rock bands, alongside punk, never fully embraced digital formats, continuing to release 7-inch singles and vinyl albums to independent record stores. "Punk and other alt-rock bands. There were many groups who never bought into digital ever." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GES0Ms_kaCI
