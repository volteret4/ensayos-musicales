# genre - Alt-Rock

## curiosities
**"Golden Age" of Commercial Alt-Rock Radio** : some retrospectively consider the 80s as the golden age, despite low salaries for staff who were "hardcore music fans" on a mission to deliver good music, prioritizing music over ratings initially.
**"Music for Weirdos and Outsiders" (Pre-1990s)** : Up until the early 1990s, this genre was largely considered music for "weirdos and outsiders."
**1980s Outsider Culture** : the scene in the 80s reveled in its "outsider-ness," wanting nothing to do with the mainstream, characterized by a tribal feel.
**Early Radio Career (1986)** : Alan Cross's career in alt-rock radio began in 1986, encountering a diverse range of music.
**Grunge's Impact (1990s)** : the rise of grunge (Pearl Jam, Soundgarden, Alice in Chains, Nirvana) caused alt-rock to coalesce around hard guitar rock, leading to a homogenization of sound and the squeezing out of earlier diverse sounds.
**Increased Accessibility (Mid-1990s)** : The pop punk form of alt-rock became even more accessible to more people than grunge.
**Modern State of Alt-Rock** : the variety of sounds is much greater, with less reliance on grunge guitars, to the point that some argue there are "too few big guitar bands" today.
**Musical Evolution (2010s)** : Overall, alt-rock in the 2010s moved towards slower tempos, away from guitar-heavy sounds, incorporating more keyboards, samples, and banjos, and went through a profound pop phase.
**Post-Grunge Appetite** : After the Grunge Gold Rush, record labels realized there was an appetite for alt-rock beyond grunge, leading them to sign bands like Jane's Addiction, Red Hot Chili Peppers, Sonic Youth, and R.E.M.
**Post-Grunge Evolution** : after the original grunge bands died off and New Metal emerged, things "loosened up" with the explosion of Indie Rock, leading to a greater variety of sounds than in the late 90s.
**Radio Station Diversity in the 80s** : commercial alt-rock radio stations in the 80s were not connected by charts or airplay tracking; each station curated its own playlist, leading to a "staggering breadth of music."
**Shift in Radio Demands** : modern alt-rock radio is unlikely to return to its pre-grunge diversity due to irrevocable changes in public demands and listening habits over 40 years.
