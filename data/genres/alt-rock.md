# genre - Alt-Rock

## curiosities
**"Golden Age" of Commercial Alt-Rock Radio** : some retrospectively consider the 80s as the golden age, despite low salaries for staff who were "hardcore music fans" on a mission to deliver good music, prioritizing music over ratings initially. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**"Music for Weirdos and Outsiders" (Pre-1990s)** : Up until the early 1990s, this genre was largely considered music for "weirdos and outsiders." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**1980s Outsider Culture** : the scene in the 80s reveled in its "outsider-ness," wanting nothing to do with the mainstream, characterized by a tribal feel. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**1990s Dominance and Foundation** : The alt-rock nation of the 1990s was instrumental in kickstarting and building the North American festival scene, creating traditions and an industry that previously did not exist culturally. It provided the atmosphere without which festivals like Coachella would not have existed. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Christmas Show Focus** : The annual Ongoing History Christmas show typically features a selection of "weirdo alt-rock Christmas songs" from over the decades, chosen to fight back against mainstream holiday music. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nYPGQiDL5bw
**Dominance of White Artists** : Rock, alt-rock, and indie rock have been predominantly white, leading to questions about cultural identity, exclusion, and racial barriers for people of color. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Early Radio Career (1986)** : Alan Cross's career in alt-rock radio began in 1986, encountering a diverse range of music. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Evolution through Diversity** : The evolution of alt-rock has been a highly collaborative process, incorporating contributions from countless artists, with people of color playing a vital, incalculable role. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Future Resurgence** : Despite bottoming out at the end of the 90s, alt-rock's next cycle was already being planted, with new indie artists like The Arctic Monkeys, Arcade Fire, The White Stripes, The Killers, and The Strokes emerging, many of whom bypassed the standard music industry model. The genre was expected to return "for another stretch in the sun" within a few years. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Grunge's Impact (1990s)** : the rise of grunge (Pearl Jam, Soundgarden, Alice in Chains, Nirvana) caused alt-rock to coalesce around hard guitar rock, leading to a homogenization of sound and the squeezing out of earlier diverse sounds. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Increased Accessibility (Mid-1990s)** : The pop punk form of alt-rock became even more accessible to more people than grunge. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Modern State of Alt-Rock** : the variety of sounds is much greater, with less reliance on grunge guitars, to the point that some argue there are "too few big guitar bands" today. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Musical Evolution (2010s)** : Overall, alt-rock in the 2010s moved towards slower tempos, away from guitar-heavy sounds, incorporating more keyboards, samples, and banjos, and went through a profound pop phase. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Post-1990s Decline** : By the close of the 1990s, the genre, after 10 years of being the dominant sort of music and culture, was on the wane, with the idea of traveling alternative music festivals burning out. Many artists who pioneered the genre had "grown up, moved on or broken up." The music became fragmented with many one-hit wonders, and new artists often seemed "derivative, too safe, too watered down," leading to a phase described as "the Creed Syndrome." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Post-Grunge Appetite** : After the Grunge Gold Rush, record labels realized there was an appetite for alt-rock beyond grunge, leading them to sign bands like Jane's Addiction, Red Hot Chili Peppers, Sonic Youth, and R.E.M. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Post-Grunge Evolution** : after the original grunge bands died off and New Metal emerged, things "loosened up" with the explosion of Indie Rock, leading to a greater variety of sounds than in the late 90s. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Radio Station Diversity in the 80s** : commercial alt-rock radio stations in the 80s were not connected by charts or airplay tracking; each station curated its own playlist, leading to a "staggering breadth of music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Shift in Audience Taste** : Generation X, which had fueled the alt-rock boom, had grown up and moved on. The incoming Generation Y was not interested in "brooding grunge or any of the kind of intense post-punk alt-rock," preferring pop-leaning tastes. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Shift in Radio Demands** : modern alt-rock radio is unlikely to return to its pre-grunge diversity due to irrevocable changes in public demands and listening habits over 40 years. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
