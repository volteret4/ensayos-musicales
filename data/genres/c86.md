# genre - C86

## curiosities
**C86 – NME Cassette, May 1986; Young Raw Indie UK Bands; "C" from Cassette Capacity Designations** : In May 1986, NME included a special cassette with one of their issues featuring young, recently signed bands from various independent British labels. Because blank cassettes had numerical designations (C60 = 60 min, C90 = 90 min), the NME called their 1986 cassette C86. All bands on the tape were young, the music was raw and sometimes primitive, and nothing came from a major label. C86 became shorthand for young, raw, primitive indie rock in the UK. "If you know your cassette culture, you'll know that blank cassettes had special numerical designations." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9cAeeExXKwU
**Compilation Cassette as Genre Origin** : The *C86* compilation cassette, a promotional offer by the NME in May 1986, became a phenomenon and was described as "a big bang for the British indie scene," eventually spawning its own genre. To say a band was "so C86" meant they adhered to a specific style characterized by indie roots, confessional lyrics, simple arrangements, and jangly guitars, solidifying the tape's immense impact on indie music. "The tape even spawned its own genre." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jhgZrwtYE2g
**Impact on British Indie Scene** : The *C86* cassette, a collection of guitar-based songs by indie artists, often primitively recorded with self-conscious lyrics, became so popular and widely discussed that it was given a commercial release in November 1986. This compilation was highly inspirational to a new generation of British kids, leading to the formation of numerous new bands and becoming known as the "C86 movement," a crucial part of Tweetpop's history. "It's not much of a stretch to say that this one tape was a big bang for the British indie scene." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jhgZrwtYE2g
