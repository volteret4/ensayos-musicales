# genre - Post-Rock

## curiosities
**Core Characteristics** : It is rock music focused on experimentation, unusual uses of traditional rock instruments, an emphasis on texture and atmospherics, wide dynamic range, attention to repetition of musical motifs, often completely instrumental, and cinematic in scope and nature. "To review, it's rock music that focuses on experimentation, unusual uses of traditional rock instruments, a focus on texture and atmospherics, lots of dynamic range, attention to repetition of musical motifs, and often, but not always, completely instrumental and very cinematic in scope and nature." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
**Definition and Philosophy** : Post-rock implies music that comes "after rock," but since rock is still present, it is better understood not as a genre or a sound, but as a philosophy or aesthetic. It explores "what happens if rock instruments stop behaving like rock instruments," focusing on experimentation, unusual uses of traditional rock instruments, texture, atmospherics, dynamic range, repetition of musical motifs, and often instrumental or cinematic scope. "This term implies that there is already something that comes after rock. But rock is still very much with us, so what's so post about it? This requires a substantial amount of deconstruction, and this is part two of our attempt to define post-rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
**Enduring Nature** : Post-rock shows no signs of abating, continuously exploring new areas and sounds, with its experiments often trickling down to influence the broader rock world. "One thing is for sure though, post-rock shows no sign of a baiting. There are always areas and sounds to explore, and some of those experiments will continue to trickle down to the rest of the rock world." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
**Hybridization Potential** : The genre lends itself well to hybridization with other forms of rock because it is largely instrumental and thus free of language barriers. "It does lend itself to much hybridization because it's largely instrumental free of language barriers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
**Lyrical and Riff Emphasis** : In post-rock, riffs are often secondary to texture, and while lyrics can be nice, they are not necessary, with the focus often on instrumentation to guide the listener on a wordless or nearly wordless musical journey. "Riffs, not smart about texture. Lyrics, nice but not necessary. Much of the time, the focus is on the instrumentation, and the goal is to take the listener on a wordless or nearly wordless musical trip." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
**Network and Global Reach** : Post-rock functions as a network, spreading through word-of-mouth, touring, and platforms like Bandcamp, YouTube, and Discord. It is a global phenomenon, with artists from the US, Canada, UK, Japan, Scandinavia, Eastern Europe, China, Latin America, and Israel. "It's also a network. This music travels by word of mouth. It travels by touring and sites like Bandcamp and YouTube and Discord all play a big part." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
**Non-Traditional Outcomes** : Despite using traditional rock instruments like guitars, bass, drums, keyboards, and outboard gear, post-rock produces non-rock outcomes, avoiding typical verse-chorus structures in favor of atmospheric, experimental, and cinematic compositions. "What we're doing is producing non-rock outcomes. That means compositions without the usual verse chorus, verse structure, and more atmospheric, some more experimentation, and more of a cinematic approach to audio." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY

Black Emperor, Sigur Rós, Explosions in the Sky, Mono, This Will Destroy You, God Is An Astronaut, and Russian Circles. Singer Rousse, Explosions in the Sky, Mono, this will destroy you, God is an astronaut and Russian circles." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
**Regional Approaches - Japan** : The Japanese post-rock scene is largely characterized by precision in its musical execution. "We could also look to Japanese Post Rock. That scene is largely about precision." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
**Regional Approaches - Montreal/Chicago** : Post-rock from Montreal and, to a certain extent, Chicago, is often more political and intellectual in its expression. "Montreal Post Rock, and to a certain extent, Chicago, is more political and intellectual." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
**Regional Approaches - Texas** : Texas post-rock bands are distinguished as among the most cinematic in their musical approach. "And Texas Post Rock bands are among the most cinematic." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
**Regional Approaches - UK** : Post-rock bands from the UK tend to be spacey while also exhibiting restraint in their music-making. "Bands from the UK tend to be rather spacey while at the same time exhibiting some restraint in their music making." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
**Structure and Dynamics** : Post-rock often features long tracks that slowly and leisurely evolve, incorporating a wide dynamic range from whisper-quiet to overwhelmingly loud, frequently creating an emotional arc or oral narrative. "Long tracks that slowly and leisurely evolve with plenty of dynamics ranging from whisper-quiet to overwhelming loudness. Many pieces are wrapped in a sort of oral narrative that can be best described as an emotional arc." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
