# genre - Punk Rock

## curiosities
**Birth at CBGB (1970s)** : CBGB in New York is described as the "Ground Zero" for much of the punk rock movement of the 1970s, where bands like the Ramones, Talking Heads, Blondie, and Television cultivated the genre's sensibilities.
**Brewing Period (1974-1976)** : Began brewing between 1974 and 1976, partly as a response to the music prevalent on radio at the time.
**British Punk Rock Explosion (Post-September 1976)** : Following Britain's first official punk rock festival at the 100 Club in September 1976, punk rock was no longer a secret and "things just kind of went crazy."
**Core Characteristics** : Typically characterized by angry lyrics, two chords played very fast, loud performances, and often a political and socially aware stance, professing to hate much of the previous generation's music.
**Dave Grohl's Life-Changing Experience** : Seeing Naked Raygun perform live in a small, dingy club (The Cubby Bear in Chicago) at age 13 completely changed Dave Grohl's life and inspired him to pursue this style of music.
**Early Characteristics (1970s/Early 1980s)** : Characterized by being loud, fast, angry, nihilistic, and strident, often considered "too intense" for mass consumption.
**Egalitarian Values** : Brought new egalitarian values, leading fans to embrace groups fronted by, led by, or entirely consisting of women, contrasting with earlier sexist attitudes in rock.
**Focus on Local Issues** : Punk rock acts like folk music by having kids write about things important to them, such as neighborhood or home life, rather than abstract or distant topics.
**Foundations Laid by Iggy Pop** : Iggy Pop is credited with laying the foundations for modern punk rock after his drumming career.
**Fracturing into Segments (End of 1970s)** : By the end of the 1970s, original punk rock sounds had fractured and segmented, with one of these segments evolving into hardcore.
**Influential Figures from Drumming Backgrounds** : Several key figures in punk rock history, such as Joey Ramone, originated as drummers before moving into other roles.
**Initial Reaction (Mid-late 1970s)** : Musicians loved the free expression but were less keen on the associated anger, aggression, violence, and politics.
**Jacob Dylan's Preference** : Jacob Dylan, son of Bob Dylan, preferred punk bands like The Clash, The Buzzcocks, and The Jam in his youth.
**North American Resistance (Late 1970s)** : In North America, punk was considered problematic due to its perceived political, class-oriented, crass, vulgar, and violent nature, leading radio stations and record labels to be wary of bands like The Ramones and The Clash.
**Origin and Mission (1970s)** : Born in the 1970s, it was designed to be the "enemy, the destroyer of pop music," aiming to obliterate the "schmaltzy, wimpy, bland metal crap" heard on AM Top 40 radio.
**Original Purpose** : Originally created as an "attack on pop" music.
**Pat Smear's Entry into Music** : Pat Smear started his musical journey at the same time punk rock was emerging, finding it an open scene for bands like The Germs who initially "didn't know how to play."
**Pogo Dance Origin (1970s UK)** : The Pogo dance became a significant activity during the original punk rock days in the UK in the 1970s, partly due to the necessity of jumping to see bands performing on floor-level stages in many venues.
**Precursor to New Wave** : New Wave music could not have existed without punk having happened first, indicating an inextricable relationship.
**Rejection of Concept Albums (1970s)** : With its focus on "two-minute two-chord songs of burning hate," Punk Rock contributed to the concept album falling out of favor for a significant period in the 1970s.
**Revival in Mid-1990s** : Experienced a revival, largely powered by bands later categorized as pop punk, which was more accessible and easier to digest than old school punk or early 1980s hardcore.
**Rolling Stone Coverage (October 20, 1977)** : The October 20, 1977, edition of Rolling Stone magazine featured the Sex Pistols on its cover with the headline "Rock is sick and living in London," while other critics dismissed punk as a fleeting fad.
**Simplicity and Accessibility** : Dave Grohl describes punk rock as being like folk music in its simplicity (often three chords), making it accessible for aspiring musicians who don't feel they can emulate virtuosos like Jimmy Page or Peter Frampton.
**Spiritual Descendants in Alt Rock** : Alt Rock bands of the 21st century, which revitalized the concept album, are described as "spiritual descendants" of the punk movement that initially led to the concept album's decline.
**Subgenres and Varieties** : Encompasses a wide range of varieties including hardcore punk, sky punk, cyber punk, synth punk, anarcho punk, cow punk, gypsy punk, Christian punk, Celtic punk, art punk, garage punk, glam punk, crust punk, horror punk, street punk, melodic punk, afro punk, skate punk, Chicano punk, folk punk, troll punk, punk blues, punk pathetic, punk metal, riot grrrl, queer core, rap core, straight edge, emo, and oi.
**US Style Introduction to UK (July 1976)** : The Ramones' gigs in London on July 4th and 6th, 1976, provided England's first proper exposure to US-style punk rock.
