# genre - Glam Rock

## curiosities
**1970s British Movement** : Glam rock was a prominent music movement in Britain during the 1970s, featuring artists such as Bowie, T-Rex, The Sweet, and Gary Glitter. Its flashy fashion spurred a "Ted revival" around 1974, as a counter-reaction to its style. "This was in large part a response to the glam rock of the era, Boi, T-Rex, the sweet, Gary Glitter, and so on." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mAlc-N2wn5s
**Brief but Global Impact** : Glam rock's "moment in the sun" was short-lived, primarily from 1971 to 1975, and largely a UK phenomenon with only David Bowie and Roxy Music gaining significant traction in North America. However, its DNA of "extremism, flamboyance, and theatricality" has spread globally across rock and pop, continually reappearing in fashion and music trends. "Glam was not around for very long. We can terribly put its moment in the sun from 1971 to 1975." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw
**Decline and Enduring Influence** : Glam and glitter rock largely became "extinct" by 1977, but their influence persisted throughout the 1980s, 1990s, and continues to be seen today. "And while glam and glitter were pretty much extinct by 1977, its influence continued into the 80s, 90s and even today." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw
**Decline from Charts (1976-1977)** : Glam rock largely disappeared from the music charts by 1976-1977, coinciding with the rise of punk rock and disco. Despite its decline, the theatricality and exaggerated costuming it introduced became a permanent part of the rock canon. "Glam was all but gone from the charts by the time punk rock and disco came along in 1976 and 1977." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw
**Definition and Characteristics** : Glam rock was a theatrical form of rock, a distinct musical sub-genre, and a significant movement in fashion, culture, and non-binary sexuality. It served as a bridge between 1960s rock and the developments of the 1970s and beyond. Predominantly a British phenomenon in the early 1970s, its influence (1971-1975) is still pervasive in rock worldwide, contributing to the existence of many important and long-lasting genres today. "Glam was rock at its most theatrical. Not only was it a sound, a proper musical sub-shin, but it was also a movement in fashion, the clothes, the hair, the makeup." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw
**Geographical and Temporal Scope** : Primarily a British phenomenon that emerged in the early 1970s, with its core influential period spanning from about 1971 to 1975. Despite its relatively short duration and regional focus, its influence spread globally. "Yes, this was a thing in the early 70s and yes, it was mostly a British thing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw
**Glittery, Flamboyant Look** : Glam rock, as exemplified by artists like David Bowie (during his Ziggy Stardust years) and the New York Dolls, was a genre that prioritized a glittery, flamboyant aesthetic in the early 1970s. For these artists, the specific clothes and makeup chosen were as crucial to their identity and impact as the music itself. "over in England, glam rock, the glittery, flamboyant look favored by David Bowie and Ziggy Stardust years was as much about the right clothes and the right makeup as it was about music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mhQ36Y-c9fM
**Introduction and Influence** : Glam rock emerged in the late 1960s and early 1970s as a highly shocking and outrageous style, fundamentally altering rock music despite its relatively short lifespan of four to five years. Its influence is still observable and felt today. "This is the surprising history of glam rock. It still lives and can still make things very interesting." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw
**London Scene and Public Perception** : Glam rock was a prominent musical and fashion movement in London. While it was immensely popular among "teeny-boppers," many perceived it as having the "trappings of disposable bubblegum pop," indicating a divide in its cultural reception. "While glam rock was all the rage among teeny-boppers and had the trappings of disposable bubblegum popped to many people, Bowie was ready to take things to the next level." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
**New Romantics' Nostalgia** : The New Romantic movement, which emerged after the angry nihilism of punk, expressed a longing for the earlier days of British glam rock in the early 1970s, when dressing up and flamboyant styles were not only permitted but encouraged. "They longed for the days of British glam rock in the early 1970s when it was okay to dress up." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mAlc-N2wn5s
**New York Dolls' Extension of Style** : The New York Dolls significantly extended the glam rock aesthetic, pushing the look "miles further" with their makeup, spandex, and fishnets, creating a highly shocking and provocative image in Manhattan. "And yeah, people had heard about this glam rock thing that was happening in London, but it was nothing like this. The dolls took that look miles further." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
**Origin and Aesthetics** : Glam Rock began with Mark Bolan of T-Rex appearing on "Top of the Pops" in March 1971, wearing satin clothes covered with glitter dust, which David Bowie found brilliant. Bowie, inspired by Bolan, Iggy Pop, Lou Reed, and The Legendary Stardust Cowboy, fully embraced the genre by using his acting and mime lessons, experimenting with makeup and costuming, and adopting a revolutionary haircut. "This was the beginning of Glam Rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P3xjW0A57Wk
**Peak and Widespread Influence** : Glam rock reached its peak around 1974, extending its influence beyond punk and post-punk into various rock acts. Artists like Elton John, Queen (with Freddie Mercury's flamboyance), and Kiss (with their makeup) visibly adopted glam aesthetics. Richard O'Brien, author of *The Rocky Horror Picture Show*, reportedly listened exclusively to glam rock during its creation. Even The Rolling Stones and Rod Stewart embraced glam trappings in the mid-1970s. "Glam peaks sometime around 1974... Glam seeped into other areas of rock beyond just punk and all the other post-punk genres." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw
**Swadeheds' Musical Interest** : Swadeheds, a British subculture, also appreciated British glam rock, surprisingly aligning with the musical preferences of their Teddyboy rivals. "As for music, Swades like British glam, just like their Teddyboy rivals, but they also shared a skinhead interest in soul, reggae, and sky." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mAlc-N2wn5s
