# genre - Glam rock

Its flashy fashion spurred a "Ted revival" around 1974, as a counter-reaction to its style. **Definition and Characteristics** : Glam rock was a theatrical form of rock, a distinct musical sub-genre, and a significant movement in fashion, culture, and non-binary sexuality. It served as a bridge between 1960s rock and the developments of the 1970s and beyond. Predominantly a British phenomenon in the early 1970s, its influence (1971-1975) is still pervasive in rock worldwide, contributing to the existence of many important and long-lasting genres today. "Glam was rock at its most theatrical. Not only was it a sound, a proper musical sub-shin, but it was also a movement in fashion, the clothes, the hair, the makeup." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The Surprising History of Glam Rock | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Geographical and Temporal Scope** : Primarily a British phenomenon that emerged in the early 1970s, with its core influential period spanning from about 1971 to 1975. Despite its relatively short duration and regional focus, its influence spread globally. For these artists, the specific clothes and makeup chosen were as crucial to their identity and impact as the music itself. **Musical Characteristics** : Glam rock was musically simpler and more spontaneous than the elaborate psychedelic and progressive rock of the late 1960s. Recordings were less polished, prioritizing spontaneity. Musically, it incorporated remnants of psychedelic guitar (distorted, eccentric), folk music influences (melancholic ballads), and the love for simple, catchy melodies from early pop-rock like The Beatles. "era musicalmente más sencillo, era muy espontáneo, las grabaciones no eran lo más pulido del mundo, primaba muchísimo lo que era la espontanidad." ← Music Radar Clan > EL PIONERO DEL GLAM： MARC BOLAN Y T REX | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mw_HxfX8iBA&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc

**Origin and Aesthetics** : Glam Rock began with Mark Bolan of T-Rex appearing on "Top of the Pops" in March 1971, wearing satin clothes covered with glitter dust, which David Bowie found brilliant. Bowie, inspired by Bolan, Iggy Pop, Lou Reed, and The Legendary Stardust Cowboy, fully embraced the genre by using his acting and mime lessons, experimenting with makeup and costuming, and adopting a revolutionary haircut. "This was the beginning of Glam Rock." ← Ongoing History of New Music > Why Bowie Still Matters - Part 1 | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P3xjW0A57Wk&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Origin and Pioneers** : Glam music was not pioneered by David Bowie, who was merely its greatest exponent in the 1970s white movement. Instead, Marc Bolan of T-Rex is identified as the person who perfectly connected the late 1960s psychedelic era with the 1970s glam movement, making him arguably the genre's true originator. "David Bowie no fue el pionero de la música glam, él no fue esa primera persona, no fue la persona que conecta la música del reino nido que había en el reino nido a finales de los años 60, con lo que fue el movimiento de la música glam de los años 70." ← Music Radar Clan > EL PIONERO DEL GLAM： MARC BOLAN Y T REX | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mw_HxfX8iBA&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**Origins and Bowie's Embrace** : Glam rock emerged in March 1971, notably influenced by Marc Bolan's appearance on "Top of the Pops" wearing satin clothes and glitter dust. David Bowie, inspired by this and other artists like Iggy Pop, Lou Reed, and The Legendary Stardust Cowboy, decided to "go all in" on the genre, creating the Ziggy Stardust persona. "It was the beginning of glam rock and he wanted a piece of it." ← Ongoing History of New Music > Remembering David Bowie： Part 1 | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sPCrATqBdeA&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Peak and Widespread Influence** : Glam rock reached its peak around 1974, extending its influence beyond punk and post-punk into various rock acts. Artists like Elton John, Queen (with Freddie Mercury's flamboyance), and Kiss (with their makeup) visibly adopted glam aesthetics. Richard O'Brien, author of *The Rocky Horror Picture Show*, reportedly listened exclusively to glam rock during its creation. Even The Rolling Stones and Rod Stewart embraced glam trappings in the mid-1970s. "Glam peaks sometime around 1974... Glam seeped into other areas of rock beyond just punk and all the other post-punk genres." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The Surprising History of Glam Rock | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Performance and Aesthetic** : A defining characteristic of glam was the artists' stage behavior, which constituted "almost 90% of the show." This included eccentric, irreverent, explicit, and provocative conduct, coupled with an androgynous, colorful, often leopard-print aesthetic. Lyrics typically shifted from intellectual themes to more loving, sexually explicit, romantic, and melancholic ballads. "el comportamiento de los artistas en el escenario era casi el 90% del espectáculo." ← Music Radar Clan > EL PIONERO DEL GLAM： MARC BOLAN Y T REX | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mw_HxfX8iBA&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
