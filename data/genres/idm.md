**Intellectual but Un-danceable** : IDM (Intelligent Dance Music) emerged as an intellectual counterpart to more intense electronic music, featuring artists like Autechre (Textuín), Tosca, and Boards of Canada (The Tumbles). While intellectually stimulating, much of IDM was not designed for dancing. **Releasing All Material** : A notable characteristic of many IDM artists is their tendency to release almost all of their creative output, including unfinished material, often through various platforms or compilations, contrasting significantly with the highly selective release strategy of Boards of Canada. **Technological Approach** : Many IDM artists associated with Warp Records tended to use the latest technologies or computers for their electronic music production, a practice that contrasted with Boards of Canada's use of vintage equipment.
