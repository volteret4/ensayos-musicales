# genre - Top 40 Radio

## curiosities
**Origin of the Format (early 1950s)** : The format of Top 40 radio originated in the early 1950s when Todd Stortz, a radio station owner in Omaha, Nebraska, observed a waitress repeatedly playing the same popular songs on a jukebox. This led him to implement a high-repetition playlist of only hit songs at his station, which quickly became top of the market. "The theory of repetition leading to more listeners goes back to the early 1950s when a guy named Todd Stortz implemented a higher repetition playlist on a radio station in Omaha, Nebraska." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_YjwRApoJhA
**Repetition-Based Programming** : Top 40 radio, now often referred to as Contemporary Hit Radio, is fundamentally built on repetition. The audience for these stations generally enjoys hearing the same popular songs multiple times, leading to programming strategies that heavily feature hit songs in frequent rotation. "And this was the beginning of top 40 radio, a format built on repetition." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_YjwRApoJhA
