**Emergence as Enduring Post-Punk Subset** : Industrial music emerged as a unique strand that survived the post-punk explosion of the late 1970s and early 1980s, becoming one of the most enduring subsets of alternative music. **Evolution from 1988 to Present** : The period from 1988 onwards has been a significant time for industrial music fans, with the genre evolving from its primitive clanging and banging into a sophisticated amalgam of metal, distortion, percussion, samplers, and synthesizers. While its widespread popularity from the 1990s has diminished, it remains a distinct and hard-edged musical form. "It's evolved from the primitive clang and banging into something very sophisticated. **Increased Guitar Use (By 1987)** : By 1987, guitars became much more commonly used in industrial music, especially by North American groups such as Skinny Puppy and Ministry, indicating a shift in the genre's sonic palette. "By 1987, it was very clear that things were changing rapidly on the industrial scene. **Mid-1980s Redefinition** : By the mid-1980s, industrial music underwent a redefinition. With Throbbing Gristle broken up and Einstürzende Neubauten incorporating chord changes, a new synthesis emerged. This new style combined the "factory sounds" of the original movement with aggressive Technopop elements, resulting in music that was loud, harsh, angry, dark, distorted, but also danceable. "But by the mid-1980s, there had been a change. Throbbing Crystal had broken up, Noe Bowden had discovered to use chord changes, and a new synthesis of styles was developing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE
**Yamaha DX7 Prominence in 1980s** : The Yamaha DX7 synthesizer and its FM synthesis technology significantly shaped the sound of industrial music in the 1980s. The instrument's ubiquity meant its sounds were integrated into tracks across various genres, including industrial music, becoming a defining sonic characteristic of the era. "The DX7 and its FM synthesis technology really did become the sound of the 80s, no matter what the genre, including industrial music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TsiYLGGSJ2U
