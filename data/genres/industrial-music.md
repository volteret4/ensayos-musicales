# genre - Industrial Music

## curiosities
**Creation by Cabaret Voltaire** : Industrial Music, described as a heavy form of electronic music, was developed with contributions from bands like Cabaret Voltaire, who were part of the Factory Records roster. "Cabaret Voltaire, who helped create the heavy form of electronic music called Industrial Music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Foundations from Performance Art** : The ideas and tenets demonstrated by harsh performance art bands like Throbbing Gristle began to lay the groundwork for what would later be recognized as industrial music. "These tenants and ideas began to lay the foundations of what we later call industrial music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
**Key Bands** : Throbbing Gristle and Coil are cited as two bands "very important to the industrial music scene" and were favorites of Trent Reznor. "Two bands very important to the industrial music scene and two favorites of Trent." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DKdF8NgF5Bw
**Niche Sound and Indie Label Role** : Industrial music was considered too heavy for most people, making it a niche sound that major labels typically avoided. This demand was subsequently filled by specialist indie labels such as Network Records of Vancouver and Wax Trax of Chicago. "This stuff is way too heavy for most people, making it, let's face in a real niche sound. Or at least it was for a while anyway. So that meant that major label stayed away." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Post-Punk Subgenre** : Industrial music is recognized as one of the major post-punk genres from the late 1970s and early 1980s, and is noted as a genre that is "still with us today." "There was new wave, techno pop, and all its subsets, industrial music, goth, and a revival of scot." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4
**Production Spike in Early 1990s** : The early 1990s experienced a "spike in the production of industrial music," suggesting an increase in its creation and popularity during that time. "there was a spike in the production of industrial music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t2uXHP9cBdM
