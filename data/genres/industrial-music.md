# genre - Industrial Music

## curiosities
**Breakthrough in Early 90s** : A "strange thing happened at the beginning of the 90s" when a number of industrial-minded groups broke through into the mainstream, some even before Grunge, possibly due to "anxiety brought on by the first Gulf War," the "intense recession," or "never-ending Republican rule in the US." "But then a strange thing happened at the beginning of the 90s. Maybe it was the anxiety brought on by the first Gulf War. Maybe it was the intense recession. Maybe it was the never-ending Republican rule in the US." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Brutal and Heavy Programming** : The inherent ability of keyboards and drum machines to be programmed to create brutal and heavy sounds inevitably led some musicians to explore this direction, cementing its roots in electronic music. "Because keyboards and drum machines could be programmed to sound so brutal and heavy, it was inevitable that some musicians would take this root." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KF5MwJVYng
**Challenging Guitar's Role** : A key goal for these artists was to demonstrate that the electric guitar was not necessary for music, or at the very least, could be utilized in ways other than a conventional rock instrument. "The goal by these artists and others was to show that the electric guitar wasn't necessary, or at the very least, could be something other than a conventional rock instrument." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pLf3kUONewg
**Creation by Cabaret Voltaire** : Industrial Music, described as a heavy form of electronic music, was developed with contributions from bands like Cabaret Voltaire, who were part of the Factory Records roster. "Cabaret Voltaire, who helped create the heavy form of electronic music called Industrial Music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Cultural Context** : Industrial music thrived in "dark clubs" with loud music and sometimes violent dancing, and its practitioners and fans collectively conveyed a message to "wake up, sheeple, question authority, think for yourselves, and be honest about the pain you feel." "It thrived in dark clubs, where the music was loud and the dancing was sometimes violent. Its practitioners and fans were collectively shouting, wake up, sheeple, question authority, think for yourselves, and be honest about the pain you feel." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**David Bowie's Importance to Industrial Fans** : David Bowie was very important to fans of industrial music, particularly those who admired Nine Inch Nails, reflecting his broad influence across genres. "And if you loved industrial music, Bowie was very important to you." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rw0uXf5UJvo
**Early Industrial as Pre-Post Rock** : Early industrial music, exemplified by groups like Britain's Throbbing Gristle and Germany's Einstürzende Neubauten, is considered to align with post-rock criteria through its experimental and unconventional use of instruments, particularly the electric guitar. "We could include early industrial music. Go back and listen to some of their first recordings by groups such as Britain's Throbbing Gristle and Germany's Einstein-Dé Noybaden." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pLf3kUONewg
**Foundation in Technopop's First Wave** : The cold, robotic sound of Technopop's first wave (1977-mid 1980) formed the fundamental basis for what would later be recognized as industrial music. "Songs like this formed the very basic foundations of what we would later call industrial music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KF5MwJVYng
**Foundations from Performance Art** : The ideas and tenets demonstrated by harsh performance art bands like Throbbing Gristle began to lay the groundwork for what would later be recognized as industrial music. "These tenants and ideas began to lay the foundations of what we later call industrial music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
**Fringe Existence** : For a long time, industrial music "lived on the fringes of the Alt Rock universe," deemed "too heavy, too angry, too aggressive, too harsh, too distorted, too abrasive and just generally too intense for prime time." "For the longest time, industrial lived on the fringes of the Alt Rock universe. It was just too heavy, too angry, too aggressive, too harsh, too distorted, too abrasive and just generally too intense for prime time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Industrial Music Overview (Early 1990s)** : This genre was embraced by the "Lala Pulosa generation" in the US during the early 1990s, coexisting with grunge and hip hop. "They got into industrial music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg
**Influence from Joy Division** : The sound and style of Joy Division are credited with contributing significantly to the development of industrial music. "Their sound and style contributed to everything from the development of Goth to industrial music to Emo." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1VWJ0uCQx8w
**Key Bands** : Throbbing Gristle and Coil are cited as two bands "very important to the industrial music scene" and were favorites of Trent Reznor. "Two bands very important to the industrial music scene and two favorites of Trent." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DKdF8NgF5Bw
**Niche Sound and Indie Label Role** : Industrial music was considered too heavy for most people, making it a niche sound that major labels typically avoided. This demand was subsequently filled by specialist indie labels such as Network Records of Vancouver and Wax Trax of Chicago. "This stuff is way too heavy for most people, making it, let's face in a real niche sound. Or at least it was for a while anyway. So that meant that major label stayed away." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Possible Influence of Glam Rock** : It is suggested that industrial music may also owe its existence to the influence of glam rock. "You might even want to say the same thing about industrial music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw
**Post-Punk Invention** : Industrial music was another post-punk invention with roots in the "late 1970s," characterized by its reliance on electronics, often incorporating big guitars and live drums. "Take industrial music, for example. This was another post-punk invention with roots in the late 1970s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Post-Punk Subgenre** : Industrial music, in a form of alternative dance, branched out from Technopop as one of the subgenres emerging from the post-punk era. "From that butted industrial music in a form of alternative dance" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
**Production Spike in Early 1990s** : The early 1990s experienced a "spike in the production of industrial music," suggesting an increase in its creation and popularity during that time. "there was a spike in the production of industrial music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t2uXHP9cBdM
**Retreat to Margins** : Industrial music is still present today, but it has "again retreated to the margins," awaiting a future time when society might once more require "really, really angry music." "Industrial is still a thing. It's out there. But it's again retreated to the margins, awaiting a time when we once again need really, really angry music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
