**Descent from French Music and African Slave Culture** : Calypso music descended from a form of French music that was "co-opted by African slaves working on the plantations." This historical lineage highlights its complex cultural synthesis rooted in colonial history and slave experiences. **Origin in Trinidad and Tobago** : Calypso is a Caribbean rhythm that originated in Trinidad and Tobago. It serves as one of the key Caribbean influences in the hybrid sound of ska. "Calypso, which came out of Trinidad and Tobago, and descended from, believe it or not, a form of French music that was co-opted by African slaves working on the plantations." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zNIVqKqAlnk ← calypso
**Similarity to Mento** : Many musicians and listeners, including the speaker, found it difficult to discern a clear difference between Calypso and Mento music, despite experts asserting a distinction. This led Mento groups to often market their music as Calypso. **Trinidadian and Tobagonian Origins** : Calypso originated in Trinidad and Tobago and was a twist on a type of French music that slaves co-opted while working on sugar cane plantations. This indigenous Caribbean music mixed with foreign sounds to influence the creation of Ska.
