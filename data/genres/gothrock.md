# genre - Gothrock

## curiosities
**British Punk Spin-off** : Gothrock originated as a spin-off from British punk in the 1970s and gained significant popularity by the early 1980s. "Gothrock began as a spin-off from the British punk of the 1970s and became quite the rage by the early 1980s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Enduring Subculture** : Unlike many rock-based subcultures, Gothrock did not wither and die but instead spread, grew stronger, and extended its influence beyond music into film, fashion, television, literature, and pop culture. "But unlike a lot of rock-based sub-cultures, it did not wither and die. Instead it spread and got stronger." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Evolution with Industrial** : The development of Goth entered a new age through cross-pollination with its close cousin, industrial music. "The next age in Goth's development came at the result of more cross-pollination. In this time, it was with its close cousin industrial music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Longest Surviving Post-Punk Genre** : Goth Rock is considered one of the longest surviving post-punk genres but remains one of the least understood by the general public and mass media. "Goth Rock may be one of the longest surviving of all the post punk genres." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Misunderstood Nature** : Goth can be very misunderstood; its look and sound can appear "scary and intimidating to the uninitiated," and extremists can unfairly tarnish its reputation. "But Goth can also be very misunderstood." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Sound Characteristics** : Goth is characterized as being "softer and slower" and "introspective," in contrast to industrial music. It often employs a keyboard-based approach similar to early 1980s technopop and smoother modern electronic sounds, making it "a little more danceable." "On the other hand, Goth has a keyboard based approach that is more in common with the techno popper of the early 1980s and the smoother flavors of modern electronic than it does with any hard techno." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Worldwide Presence** : Goth is described as one of the strongest, most solid, and most widespread subcultures in rock, with a worldwide presence including scenes in France, Belgium, Scandinavia, Russia, Japan, and New Zealand. "Goth is one of the strongest, most solid, most widespread of all the sub-cultures in rock and it's worldwide." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
