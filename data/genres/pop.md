**12-Year Rock and Pop Cycle** : Historically, popular music followed a 12-year cycle where pop's popularity and influence would descend while rock ascended, and vice versa. This pattern can be traced back to the early 1950s, with pop surging after rock's decline around 1957. The cycle reversed in 1964 with The Beatles' arrival in America, sustaining until about 1970 when pop again took over. Pop stars in 1982-83 led to a fade in rock, and after 1996, pop made a comeback, with rock being "declared dead." Public sentiment embraced pop again around 2008 after rock's dominance, but the traditional 12-year cycle, which would have predicted a rock revival, did not re-establish itself due to factors like streaming and the decline of traditional music gatekeepers. "Rock then goes into a decline while pop music surges." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nX8uAFDzDYM ← pop
