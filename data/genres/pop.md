**12-Year Pop and Rock Cycle** : For over 50 years, dating back to the early 1950s (birth date estimated around 1951), rock and pop popularity fluctuated in an inverse 12-year cycle. Pop ascended from 1957-1964, then declined until 1970. It surged again for 5-6 years, then faded for rock. It returned 1982-83 with new pop stars, faded for alternative/grunge. Pop came back after 1996, and rock was declared "dead" until 2000-2001. After 2008, public sentiment again embraced pop. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nX8uAFDzDYM
**Death of 12-Year Cycle** : This cycle, which should have seen a rock revival after 2014, died. This is attributed to streaming and the decline of traditional gatekeepers (radio, record stores, labels, music magazines, cable music video channels), leading to a fragmented, non-monocultural music landscape. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nX8uAFDzDYM
**Dominance after 1983 Recession** : after the gloom of the 1980 recession began to lift around 1983-84, pop music dominated. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Late 1990s Resurgence** : As alt-rock waned, pop music ascended, reflecting a shift in musical taste towards the "pop side" among Generation Y. This period saw the rise of artists like Spice Girls, Backstreet Boys, NSYNC, and Britney Spears, marking a return to the pop dominance seen in the mid-to-late 1980s. This shift coincided with an improved economy and a more optimistic cultural mood. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ

**Trend Towards Sadness (1965-2015)** : Analysis of 150,000 top 100 hit songs released between 1965 and 2015 revealed a trend of music getting sadder. The use of the word "love" in lyrics fell by half, while negative words like "hate" rose sharply. Approximately 50% of top 40 songs are now in minor keys, compared to only 15% in the 1960s (85% were in major keys). The Centers for Disease Control (CDC) has noted the increased demand for sad songs, particularly by teenage girls, on their mental health radar. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qtdetc5-MQo
