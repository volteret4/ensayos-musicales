**Mainstream-Oriented Band Type** : "Corporate rock band" was a term used to describe a project specifically put together with the explicit goal of being mainstream and maximizing record sales. Bands fitting this description, like Toto, were often considered "uncool" by certain segments of the rock audience, particularly the alt-rock crowd. "Toto was what we used to call a corporate rock band. The kind of project put together with the express purpose of being mainstream and selling as many records as possible." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw ← corporate-rock
**Music Played by 1970s FM Radio** : Corporate rock was the prevalent music played on most FM radio stations in the 1970s, featuring bands like Styx, Journey, and Boston. This style contrasted with the "alternative" programming that some stations began to introduce by occasionally playing punk or New Wave tracks. "So instead of just playing sticks and journey in Boston and what was called corporate rock, they added the occasional punk or new wave track." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjTWcy8RHEs ← corporate-rock ← corporate-rock
