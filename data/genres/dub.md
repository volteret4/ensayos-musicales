# genre - Dub

## curiosities
**Experimentation by Early Punk Bands** : Early punk bands like The Clash, initially known for their straightforward sound, later experimented with dub, demonstrating punk's license to incorporate diverse musical styles. "I mean, the clash started out as two chord wonders, but then started experimenting with dub." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0QtMI00T5jw
**Gorillaz Debut Album Component** : Dub was included as one of the diverse musical influences that shaped the sound of the Gorillaz's debut album. "The music was rather experimental, more trip-hop, some alt-rock, bits of Brit-pop, little dub, little reggae, and a bunch of hip-hop." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iVdTCqpd798
**Influence on Bass Playing Style** : Dub, a Jamaican style of music, heavily influenced bass players such as Jah Wobble, who adopted its slow, low characteristics and deep, deep grooves into his playing. "Jaw's playing was heavily influenced by dub that Jamaican style of playing that could be slow and low with a deep, deep groove." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kbFfT0pKchI
**Influence on Public Image Limited** : Dub music was a significant influence on Johnny Lydon's creative direction for Public Image Limited, contributing to their experimental sound. "He got deep into reggae and dub." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJGFUlQTUhQ
**Introduced to Punks by Don Letts** : In the 1970s, Don Letts, a DJ, played a crucial role in introducing dub music to a new generation of punk kids in clubs. With few punk records available at the time, Jamaican music, including dub, served as the initial soundtrack for the burgeoning punk scene. "He was the DJ who introduced reggae and dub to a new generation of these punk kids in the 1970s in clubs where they hung out. See, there were no punk records yet, so that music, Jamaican music, dub music, black music became theirs until they had some punk records to listen to." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_1NNvxgx8uA
**Stuart Copeland's Distinct Feel** : Stuart Copeland of The Police was known for incorporating a distinctly reggae and dub feel into his drumming. This characteristic style was often highlighted through his liberal use of rim shots. "Second, he had a distinctly reggae and dub feel to his playing, something often expressed with liberal use of rim shots." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WL3zEWJJNuw
