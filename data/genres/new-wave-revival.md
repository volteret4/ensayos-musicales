# genre - New Wave Revival

## curiosities
**Demographic Shift and Millennial Music** : This movement marked a demographic shift, with artists who were "children of the 80s and 90s" (Millennials) creating music primarily for other Millennials, indicating a generational handover in shaping the music agenda from the mid-2000s onward. "The artists setting the agenda for the middle odds and forward were children of the 80s and 90s. Millennials making music for other millennials." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_yslM5oanRo
**Gathering Attention in Mid-2000s** : The New Wave Revival gained considerable attention throughout the mid-2000s, with a host of bands including The Rapture, The Bravery, Bloc Party, Kaiser Chiefs, The Killers, Phoenix, Passion Pit, and The Ting Tings embodying this sound. "The New Wave Revival, the New New Wave, gathered quite a bit of attention throughout the middle of the odds." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_yslM5oanRo
**Resurgence of 80s/90s Influences** : The New Wave Revival (sometimes called "New New Wave") saw artists actively drawing influences from 80s and 90s bands like Duran Duran, Joy Division, The Cure, and New Order. These younger artists put their own contemporary spin on these classic sounds. "Others were willing to reach back into the 80s or even the 70s for influences. And a few of these acts became wrapped up in something called the New Wave Revival. Or even, and this is a bit silly, the New Wave." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_yslM5oanRo
