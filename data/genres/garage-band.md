# genre - Garage Band

## curiosities
**Mid-60s North American Phenomenon** : Throughout the mid-1960s, garage bands proliferated in garages across North America. These groups were often characterized by their rough and ragged sound, sometimes incorporating risqué material. "Throughout the mid-60s, bands popped up in garages all across North America." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U
**Rebellious Image and "Punks" Label** : Some garage bands adopted a rebellious image, wearing leather jackets reminiscent of James Dean. Some observers at the time used the term "punks" to describe this look, an early association of the word with a musical subculture. "Some observers said these jackets made them look like punks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U
**Underground Popularity** : While a few garage bands achieved hit singles, most remained underground. They found popularity with a specific type of music fan who preferred their music "rough and raw and real." "They remained underground, popular with a certain type of music band, the kind that liked their music rough and raw and real." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U
