# genre - Post-Progressive

## curiosities
**Alternative Term for Complex Rock** : Post-Progressive was offered as an alternative term for the complex and stretching styles of rock that emerged during the 2010s progressive rock comeback. This genre, which included elements of math rock and Djent, was defined more by an "attitude and an approach to instruments and arrangements" than a fixed sound. "Another term that we could use for this might be post-progressive." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HSvgRyGiOpE
**Deceptively Simple Yet Complex Sound** : This genre is characterized by deceptively simple melodies layered over an "A-level" complexity, featuring interesting textures, timbres, and harmonies, resulting in a wide range of sounds. "Now generally speaking, this music has deceptively simple melodies, but below that is some A-level complexity with some interesting textures, timbres, and harmonies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tdTs-4Irv8c
**Evolution from 2000s Radiohead** : Post-Progressive describes music that builds upon the innovations of bands like Radiohead in the 2000s, taking their experimental approach to new levels of complexity. "This describes the music of a bunch of bands who looked at what groups like Radiohead were doing in the 2000s and then took it to the next level." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tdTs-4Irv8c
