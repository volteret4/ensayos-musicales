**Birth, Decline, and 2010s Comeback** : Progressive Rock was born in the late 1960s and early 1970s, flourishing as rock musicians became more virtuosic and recording technology advanced, allowing for complex sounds. It fell out of favor when punk and new wave movements criticized it as "too bloated, too inaccessible, too elitist, too complicated." However, it saw a significant comeback in the 2010s, evolving to include styles like Math Rock and Djent, and attracting bands from Biffy Clyro to The Mars Volta and Radiohead. "Prague rock was born in the late 1960s and early 70s, and was a big deal until punk came along and tried to wipe it out." **Characteristics (Early 1970s)** : The early 1970s was identified as the "age of Prague Rock," characterized by ultra-complex musical constructions created by "virtual musicians" in sophisticated recording studios. It fused rock and classical music, often being escapist, fantastical, bombastic, and "completely over the top."
