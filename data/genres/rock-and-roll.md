# genre - Rock and Roll

## curiosities
**"Rock is Dead" Cries (1960, 1972, 1988, 2000)** : The sentiment that "rock is dead" has been a recurring cry during periods of its decline, specifically heard in 1960, 1972, 1988, and 2000, only for the genre to consistently resurrect itself. "The same rock as dead cries that we heard in 1960 and 1972 were trotted out once again." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Cultural Association with Dopamine** : Rock and roll, alongside sex and drugs, is associated with the brain's natural feel-good hormone, dopamine. Evolution and biology have seemingly wired human brains for these experiences, as they all stimulate dopamine production, leading to feelings of pleasure. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BHN6db7m3DM
**Cyclical Battle with Pop** : Since its birth in the 1950s, Rock and Pop have been "locked in a battle where each combatant is 180 degrees out of phase with each other," with each cycle lasting about 13 years and rock consistently proving "very good at resurrection." "Since the birth of Rock in the 1950s, Rock and Pop have been locked in a battle where each combatant is 180 degrees out of phase with each other." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Father of the Genre – Chuck Berry** : Chuck Berry is referred to as the "father of rock and roll," acknowledging his foundational influence on the genre. "Chuck Berry, the father of rock and roll, was very particular in his requirements." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z2hvUDtBxho
**Fifth Cycle Ascendancy (2004-2005)** : By early 2004, rock was "once again on the ascendant and pop was in decline," with new artists influenced by alt-rock, underground rock, and punk gaining attention. The summer of 2005 was an "embarrassment of riches," with many rock bands having hit albums. "By the beginning of 2004, it was clear that rock was once again on the ascendant and pop was in decline. It was the fifth cycle in the rock pop death match." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Fifth Cycle Decline (2006)** : By 2006, the "appetite for rock was beginning to wane," with pop music once again ascending, signaling the end of rock's fifth cycle, with technology emerging as a new driver. "By the time we got into 2006, the appetite for rock was beginning to wane, and pop music was once again in the Ascendant." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Fifth Cycle Signs (Early 2000s)** : Despite grim predictions, U2's `All That You Can't Leave Behind` in late 2000 and the buzz around indie bands like The Strokes post-9/11 suggested that rock "wasn't dead after all" and hinted at a new cycle driven by young people. "A few people were beginning to wonder if rock wasn't dead after all. That U2 record was actually pretty good." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Fifth Cycle Start (2003)** : The White Stripes' breakthrough album `Elephant` on April 1, 2003, was "right on schedule" according to the 13-year cycle theory, marking the beginning of a surge for alt-rock to a new peak. "So here we are with the white stripes. They have their breakthrough album right on schedule. April 1st, 2003, 13 years after Depeche Mode and violator." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**First Cycle Peak and Decline (1956-1960s)** : The first cycle peaked with Elvis in 1956 but began its decline on March 2, 1958, with Elvis's army enlistment. This period, lasting into the 1960s, saw rock declared a "fad" by record executives. "It peaked with Elvis in 1956. The day new Ma began on March 2nd of 1958." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Foundational Learning for Reunion** : John Frusciante proposed that the Red Hot Chili Peppers relearn "basic rock and roll kind of building blocks" by studying old blues songs and early Red Hot Chili Peppers songs. This approach helped the band re-establish their core musical connection before writing new material. "Let's go all the way back and just learn how to play some really beautiful, somewhat challenging, but basic rock and roll kind of building blocks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Nr-3Y3UWQcI
**Fourth Cycle (1990-1994)** : The first indication of this cycle was the Depeche Mode riot on March 20, 1990, leading to a quick pickup in spring 1990 with the rise of Madchester and Grunge. By fall 1991, rock was dominant, peaking before Kurt Cobain's suicide in 1994. "The first indication that a fourth cycle was starting was at a riot at a de Pesh mode autograph session in Los Angeles on March 20th of 1990." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Fourth Cycle Decline (1996-2000)** : This cycle went "to hell" when Metallica hijacked the 1996 Lollapalooza headliner spot, leading to the dominance of boy bands and pop charts within two years, with the Alt Nation having "grown up and moved on." "We knew it was all going to hell when Metallica hijacked the headliner spot on the 1996 Lollipalooza tour." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Ground Zero (1951)** : March 3, 1951, the day "Rocket 88" was recorded, is considered "Ground Zero for Rock and Roll" by many who study music. "For many people who study this stuff, Ground Zero for Rock and Roll was March the 3rd of 1951." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**John Frusciante's Return to Rock Songwriting** : During his hiatus from the Red Hot Chili Peppers, John Frusciante had not written a lot of rock music songs. His return to the band led to a creative outpouring, as he had accumulated a "reservoir of ideas" that flowed naturally into the new rock compositions. "I think because John hadn't written a lot of rock music songs for quite a while, he had in him a reservoir of ideas and chord progressions and arrangements and vocal melodies and all these things that he must have been kind of quietly keeping in his back pocket that started to flow." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Nr-3Y3UWQcI
**Second Cycle (1964-1969)** : Initiated by The Beatles' Ed Sullivan appearance on February 9, 1964, this cycle "rescued Rock" and lasted through the decade, peaking at Woodstock in August 1969. "Cycle 2 starts 13 years later with the American TV debut The Beatles on the Ed Sullivan Show, February 9th, 1964." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Second Cycle Decline (1969-Early 1970s)** : The decline began after the "bad vibes and death at Eltonmont" in December 1969, leading to creative exhaustion, disillusionment with hippie ideals, and a period of nostalgia for 1950s music. "This second cycle peaked at Woodstock six years later, August of 1969, and began its slide following the bad vibes and death at Eltonmont, another music festival, that December." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Struggles in 2000** : By the end of 2000, rock was declared "very, very ill" and possibly "dead" due to the overwhelming dominance of pop and rap/hip hop, with only compilation albums and a few New Metal bands seeing significant sales. "If rock, all rock wasn't dead, it was at least very, very ill." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Third Cycle (1977-Mid-1984)** : A reaction to the "mediocrities of the early 1970s," this cycle began with the rise of punk rock, specifically highlighted by the Sex Pistols' contract signing on March 10, 1977. Punk and New Wave helped pave the way for the music video generation before rock "blown itself out" by mid-1984. "The third cycle was a reaction to the mediocrities of the early 1970s, fed up with the state of music, a new generation embraced punk rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
