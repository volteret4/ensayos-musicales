# genre - Rock and Roll

## curiosities
**Brother's Obsession** : Hugh Dillon's brother, Jude, was obsessed with rock and roll, an undercurrent in their Catholic family.
**Demand for Louder Amplification** : The emergence of rock and roll led customers to seek louder and more rugged amplifiers than existing American models like Fender, prompting Jim Marshall to invent his own.
**Early Single-Driven Era** : prior to 1965, rock and roll lived primarily as 7-inch 45 RPM singles, with albums being secondary collections of singles and B-sides.
**Evolution Through Drummers** : Throughout its history, drummers have made significant contributions, shaping its course and enabling what listeners enjoy today, even if glory often goes to singers and guitar players.
**Hugh Dillon's Passion** : Hugh Dillon was drawn to rock and roll music from a young age, loving it obsessively and seeing it as an escape from a life of crime.
**Shift to Album Format (Post-1965)** : The Beatles, Bob Dylan, and others began to use the album as an artistic statement around 1965, shifting rock and pop away from singles.
