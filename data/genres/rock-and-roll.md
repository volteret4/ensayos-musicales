# genre - Rock and Roll

## curiosities
**Birth and Evolution** : Rock and roll formed in the early 1950s as a "stew" of R&B, Blues, Country Western, Hillbilly, and traditional Black musical styles. Its birth was a gradual process rather than the result of a single song, facilitated by radio stations playing a hodgepodge of different materials. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f2KA97T4u08
**Brother's Obsession** : Hugh Dillon's brother, Jude, was obsessed with rock and roll, an undercurrent in their Catholic family. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=y7kmkWJ54ww
**Demand for Louder Amplification** : The emergence of rock and roll led customers to seek louder and more rugged amplifiers than existing American models like Fender, prompting Jim Marshall to invent his own. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo
**Early Single-Driven Era** : prior to 1965, rock and roll lived primarily as 7-inch 45 RPM singles, with albums being secondary collections of singles and B-sides. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Evolution Through Drummers** : Throughout its history, drummers have made significant contributions, shaping its course and enabling what listeners enjoy today, even if glory often goes to singers and guitar players. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo
**Hugh Dillon's Passion** : Hugh Dillon was drawn to rock and roll music from a young age, loving it obsessively and seeing it as an escape from a life of crime. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=y7kmkWJ54ww
**Origin of the Term** : The term "rock and roll" was popularized in the 1950s by disc jockey Alan Freed. After a lawsuit prevented him from using his previous moniker "Moon Dog," Freed adopted "rock and roll" as a new catchphrase for the black R&B music he played for white audiences. This term was an African-American slang phrase for sex, which resonated with younger audiences while remaining unapparent to adults, thus becoming the defining descriptor for the new music genre. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
**Origins and Ingredients** : Rock and roll formed in the early 1950s as a "stew" of R&B, Blues, Country Western, Hillbilly, and traditional Black musical traditions. Its birth was a gradual process rather than the result of a single song, aided by local radio stations playing a hodgepodge of different materials. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
**Seven-Inch Single as Core (1950s)** : When rock and roll emerged in the 1950s, the seven-inch single became its "heart and soul." Albums were typically released only after a series of successful singles, often repackaging singles with extra tracks. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Sc9HApnyBLM
**Shift to Album Format (Post-1965)** : The Beatles, Bob Dylan, and others began to use the album as an artistic statement around 1965, shifting rock and pop away from singles. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**The Term "Rock and Roll"** : The phrase originally described the movement of ships on rough seas and was also used to describe the animated singing in Black churches. It later became a euphemism for sex before being popularized as a musical term by DJ Alan Fried in 1951. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
