# genre - Blues

## curiosities
**Cultural Fusion** : The blues mixed with country, Western, and hillbilly music in the South, creating the specific musical DNA that would eventually evolve into rock and roll. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_Pi7JLwAeOI
**Fanny May (1960)** : Buster Brown's song "Fanny May" (1960) is identified as a blues song. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Epz5Sn151bk
**Foundation of Rock** : Rock music was born out of a collision between blues, R&B, country, and hillbilly music in the 1950s. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Juke Joints and the Chitlin' Circuit** : Early blues was performed in informal Southern venues called Juke Joints. These were connected by the "Chitlin' Circuit," a touring network that eventually linked rural Florida to urban hubs like the Apollo Theater and Cotton Club in New York. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_Pi7JLwAeOI
**Kingston Scene** : Hugh Dillon and Gord Downie would frequent downtown Kingston venues like the Prince George to watch old blues acts like Luther "Guitar Jr." Johnson. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=y7kmkWJ54ww
**Origins and "Bent Notes" (c. 1870s)** : Emerging from the US South, the blues introduced "blue notes" that existed between the accepted tones of the Pythagorean scale. These "natural imperfections" were intentionally excluded by traditional Western tuning but became the soul of the genre. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_Pi7JLwAeOI
**Robert Johnson Crossroads Legend** : The legendary tale of blues artist Robert Johnson selling his soul to the devil at a rural Mississippi crossroads for guitar virtuosity is a significant curiosity within the genre. The purported location at highways 49 and 61 near Clarksdale, Mississippi, remains a site of interest for those who believe the legend. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pmKjZzypzvg
**Single-Driven Era (Pre-1965)** : Popular music, including blues, was primarily released as single songs before the mid-1960s. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Sc9HApnyBLM
**Structural Foundation** : The Blues originated in the South as an African-American invention characterized by songs about hard lives and difficult relationships. Its three-line stanza structure traces back to Mississippi Delta field songs and forms the structural foundation for rock, R&B, funk, and hip-hop. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
