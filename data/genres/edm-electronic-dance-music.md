# genre - EDM (Electronic Dance Music)

## curiosities
**Emergence as a Major Genre** : In the 2010s, EDM emerged as a fourth genre competing for attention alongside rock, pop, and hip hop, characterized by superstar DJs and massive public events. For many, electronic dance music was considered "the new rock and roll," with "people with laptops" replacing guitarists as the big heroes. **Generational Revival** : EDM experienced two significant revivals. The first was spearheaded by an older generation, including artists like David Guetta and Tiësto, who slowly and gradually brought the genre back to prominence. The second, a "new flourishing," was led by artists like Avicii, Calvin Harris, and Swedish House Mafia, who gave it a renewed impulse and brought people back to enjoy dance music. **Shift in Artist Treatment Between Generations** : The older generation of EDM artists like David Guetta and Tiësto built their careers gradually, gaining significant experience ("bagaje muy importante") before reaching stadium-filling status, which allowed some, like Guetta, to gain control over their own promotion. In contrast, the new generation, including Avicii, Calvin Harris, Swedish House Mafia, and The Chainsmokers, were instantly turned into "objects of mass consumption," subjected to immediate and overwhelming pressure from music and spectacle promotion, without the same options to control their careers.
