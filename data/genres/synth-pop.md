# genre - Synth Pop

## curiosities
**Alternative Term for Technopop** : "Synth pop" is an alternative term used by some people to describe the synthesizer-driven second half of the New Wave era, which is also referred to as Technopop or Electro Pop. "And we're going to use the term Technopop, although some people prefer calling it synth pop or electro pop." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
**Birth in Early 1980s** : Synth pop was born as a form of New Wave in the early 1980s, spurred by the accessibility of synthesizers. This subgenre emphasized synths, sequencers, and drum machines over guitars, appealing to musicians who enjoyed experimenting with new sounds, regardless of formal training. "And so was born synth pop, a form of new wave that issued guitars for synths, sequencers, and drum machines." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
**Early 1980s Hits** : Thomas Dolby's songs "She Blinded Me with Science" and "Hyperactive!" are highlighted as big alt-rock hits that defined the synth pop years of the early 1980s. "Those were all big, alt-rock hits during the synth pop years of the early 1980s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QfiRRtsZoYU
**Sheffield's 1980s Prominence** : Sheffield first gained fame for its synth pop scene in the 1980s, producing influential bands such as Heaven's 17, Cabaret Voltaire, The Human League, and ABC. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YvcnHKI4oIw
**Subgenre of New Wave** : As the New Wave genre expanded and diversified, synthpop became one of its defined subgenres. "Synthpop had its own section." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
**Trent Reznor's Early Aspiration** : Trent Reznor was profoundly inspired by synth-pop bands like The Human League, OMD, and Depeche Mode, finding their synthesizers and drum machines "really cool," to the extent that he once considered a job working for a company that made them. His band Option 30 also worked to avoid being "too polisish" or "too heavily towards synth pop," despite its influence. "But the synthesizers and drum machines of the human league and OMD and Depeche mode. Now that was really cool." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M3nUQtja0qc
