# genre - Synth Pop

## curiosities
**Alternative Term for Technopop** : "Synth pop" is an alternative term used by some people to describe the synthesizer-driven second half of the New Wave era, which is also referred to as Technopop or Electro Pop. "And we're going to use the term Technopop, although some people prefer calling it synth pop or electro pop." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The Post-Punk Explosion Part 2： Techno-Pop | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Birth in Early 1980s** : Synth pop was born as a form of New Wave in the early 1980s, spurred by the accessibility of synthesizers. This subgenre emphasized synths, sequencers, and drum machines over guitars, appealing to musicians who enjoyed experimenting with new sounds, regardless of formal training. "And so was born synth pop, a form of new wave that issued guitars for synths, sequencers, and drum machines." ← Ongoing History of New Music > Alt Rock Revivals Part 5： New Wave | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Disco's Pop Evolution** : In the 1980s, the pop-oriented branch of disco music evolved into Synth Pop, with groups like Simple Minds following the path set by Devo. "sobretodo también al Sine Pop que luego ya vendrían los simple minds y o en D y todos estos grupos." ← Music Radar Clan > Pet Shop Boys. **Early 1980s Hits** : Thomas Dolby's songs "She Blinded Me with Science" and "Hyperactive!" are highlighted as big alt-rock hits that defined the synth pop years of the early 1980s. "Those were all big, alt-rock hits during the synth pop years of the early 1980s." ← Ongoing History of New Music > Eggheads and Geeks | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QfiRRtsZoYU&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Embrace of Simmons Electronic Drums** : Synth pop and new wave bands enthusiastically adopted Simmons electronic drums upon their release, leveraging them to create futuristic sounds that were often described as resembling "lightsabers." "They were embraced by a bunch of new wave and synth pop bands who immediately began to set them to sound like, well, lightsabers." ← Ongoing History of New Music > History of the Modern Drum Kit | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WL3zEWJJNuw&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Later Naming of Existing Sound** : Although artists like Ultravox were making Synth Pop music in the early 1980s, the specific name "Synth Pop" for the genre only truly emerged around the mid-1980s, specifically around 1983. Before then, it was often simply categorized under New Wave. "En esa In Pop casi está de 80 y de los 83 no le empezamos a llamar Xi In Pop aunque ya existía." ← Music Radar Clan > Que es el New Wave | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jxaTsoSRXZE&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**New Wave's Final Definition** : As other sub-genres took on their own names, New Wave increasingly became associated with Synth Pop, particularly the first generation of the genre. Today, New Wave is often understood as synonymous with early Synth Pop, including electro-pop and techno-pop, covering the period from roughly 1978 to 1984/85. "el New Wave se iba quedando en la parte que en la última parte que es la que más tarde cuentardó, en coger nombre que fue Xi In Pop." ← Music Radar Clan > Que es el New Wave | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jxaTsoSRXZE&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**Sheffield's 1980s Prominence** : Sheffield first gained fame for its synth pop scene in the 1980s, producing influential bands such as Heaven's 17, Cabaret Voltaire, The Human League, and ABC. ← Ongoing History of New Music > Deconstructing the Arctic Monkeys | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YvcnHKI4oIw&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Subgenre of New Wave** : As the New Wave genre expanded and diversified, synthpop became one of its defined subgenres. "Synthpop had its own section." ← Ongoing History of New Music > Alt Rock Revivals Part 5： New Wave | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Trent Reznor's Early Aspiration** : Trent Reznor was profoundly inspired by synth-pop bands like The Human League, OMD, and Depeche Mode, finding their synthesizers and drum machines "really cool," to the extent that he once considered a job working for a company that made them. His band Option 30 also worked to avoid being "too polisish" or "too heavily towards synth pop," despite its influence. "But the synthesizers and drum machines of the human league and OMD and Depeche mode. Now that was really cool." ← Ongoing History of New Music > Deep Dark Secrets of Nine Inch Nails | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M3nUQtja0qc&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
