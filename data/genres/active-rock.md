# genre - Active Rock

## curiosities
**Crossover and Appeal** : incorporated alternative rock artists like Offspring, Foo Fighters, and Bush to become less of a niche, attracting listeners beyond its target demographic of men aged 18-34. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Historical Homage** : while focusing on new material, active rock radio stations also paid homage to classic artists like Guns N' Roses, AC/DC, Led Zeppelin, Van Halen, Ozzy Osbourne, Black Sabbath, Def Leppard, and Jimi Hendrix, plus a few metal bands from the 80s and 2000s. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**New Radio Format (2010s)** : a new radio format that rose in the 2010s, focusing on the harder end of alt rock, born in the US and largely remaining an American phenomenon (with half a dozen substations in Canada and a couple in Australia). ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Polarization and Values** : considered by some as polarizing and too driven by testosterone, but by others as upholding the traditional rock values of alternative music. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Programming Focus** : played hard and heavy stuff, with a bit of classic rock but mostly new material; notably, "zero bands playing banjos and dulcimers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Ratings Growth and Industry Impact (2010s)** : many active rock stations saw ratings growth in the 2010s as people looked for something louder and different, and for some, "angry music in the era of Donald Trump," attracting attention from independent record labels who saw it as a viable third promotional option behind alternative stations and mainstream rock outlets. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
