# genre - Baggy / Madchester

## curiosities
**Baggy / Madchester – Born from Phil Sacks' Cheap Bell-Bottoms; Happy Mondays, Stone Roses, Inspiral Carpets** : The Baggy/Madchester sound began when a Manchester clothing stall owner named Phil Sacks received a cheap shipment of bell-bottom trousers, which local musicians bought as rebellion against tight jeans. Sacks began managing his customers, who became the Happy Mondays. By the late 1980s, a new generation of groups were mixing loose psych-inspired music with house and acid house from clubs like the Hacienda, plus ecstasy and acid. A holy trinity emerged: the Happy Mondays (the Rolling Stones), the Stone Roses (the Beatles), and the Inspiral Carpets (the Kinks or the Who). The term "Madchester" was coined by Philip Shotten, a video director for Factory Records.
