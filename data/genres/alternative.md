# genre - Alternative

## curiosities
**"College Rock" as Predecessor** : For a period during the 1980s and 1990s, non-mainstream rock was sometimes referred to as "college rock," largely because it was the type of music frequently broadcast on campus radio stations. However, this term was considered somewhat limiting, especially for non-college audiences, leading to the eventual adoption of more inclusive terms like "alternative" and "Indie." "For a while, some called it college rock because it seemed to be the kind of music that you heard on campus radio stations." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z8BTWo66SDI
**Alternative to Mainstream** : The term "alternative" arose because signing to independent labels provided an alternative to the mainstream music industry for radical new music like punk. These labels and the music they promoted became known as "alternative." "Signing to these independent labels was an alternative to the mainstream. They became known as alternative labels, and the music they promoted was alternative music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
**Basic Enduring Sound** : Alternative music is listed as one of the basic and enduring sounds that will never go away, but is continuously challenged by new genres. "The basic sounds are still with us. Rock, alternative, indie, Goth, industrial, metal, synth-based music, ska, reggae, and so on. They're never going to go away." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tdTs-4Irv8c
**Birth of the "Alternative Nation"** : The proliferation and acceptance of this music in the 1990s led to the coining of the term "alternative nation," signifying the widespread cultural impact of this genre. "It was the birth of what we call in the 90s the alternative nation." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjTWcy8RHEs
**Campus Radio's Role in Popularization** : Campus radio stations played a critical role in the growth and networking of the alternative scene. They specialized in playing music from independent labels and, as they became more influential and connected through magazines and conventions, they began consistently referring to this music as "alternative." "Campus stations specialized in this stuff." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjTWcy8RHEs
**Catch-All Umbrella Term** : Similar to New Wave, "alternative" functioned as a broad umbrella term encompassing all music that emerged following punk and consciously rejected mainstream commercialization. It covered a vast stylistic spectrum, from low-fi fuzzy guitar rock (Sonic Youth) to electronic pop (Depeche Mode), heavy industrial music (Skinny Puppy, Ministry), and even delicate music from major-label artists (Kate Bush, Tori Amos). "Like New Wave, this was a catch-all umbrella term that described all the music that came in the wake of punk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z8BTWo66SDI
**Challenges in Discovery Today** : While good alternative music still exists, the sheer volume of available music makes it much harder to discover new artists that create the same level of excitement experienced in earlier days. "So it's the good music is there. It's just there's so much of it. It takes a lot longer to find stuff that's going to make you excited like you were back in the day." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4xxgPX-aR90
**Crossover with Indie** : There is significant crossover between the terms "Indie" and "alternative," though it eventually became possible for an "alternative" act to sign with a major label. "But bottom line here is that there's plenty of crossover between the terms Indy and alternative, although it later became possible to be an alternative act on a major label, but we'll get to that." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
**Defining Attitude of the 1980s** : In the late 1980s, the term "Alternative" began to describe a certain attitude in rock music, encompassing anything that couldn't fit into regular categories, characterized by being new, weird, and hated by the mainstream. "If you couldn't categorize a song or an artist by tossing it into any of the regular buckets, then there was only one other bucket you could use, and it quickly filled up." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tFBSrKMEZxI
**Distinct from Mainstream** : Regardless of its precise origin, the music labeled "alternative" was fundamentally seen as an alternative to the mainstream. It represented sounds and artists that were not part of the commercial establishment. "And yes, this music was an alternative to the mainstream." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjTWcy8RHEs

"The word that fans and critics gravitated to was alternative." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjTWcy8RHEs

"Alternative music has become again a nichey, more of a niche thing than it used to be. It was niche that it became the mainstream. **First True Alternative Band – The Velvet Underground** : The Velvet Underground is widely regarded by many as the first true alternative band. Their innovative sound and unconventional approach, including Maureen Tucker's unique drumming style, set them apart and influenced subsequent alternative music movements. "our group considered by many to be the first true alternative band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo
**Foundational Band** : The Velvet Underground is frequently identified as the first alternative band, largely due to their signature rough, raw sound which was heavily imbued with distortion, feedback, and noise. Their experimental approach set a precedent for later alternative music. "Louis previous band, the Velvet Underground, has often been called the first alternative band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w

This proliferation made the term increasingly confusing, especially after the mainstream success of Nirvana and grunge effectively made the "alternative world mainstream," causing the word to lose its original intent of signifying an *alternative* to established norms. "As the 80s turned into the 90s, alternative music began to fragment, into dozens and dozens of different streams." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z8BTWo66SDI

**Lineage Outside Mainstream Rock** : The word "alternative" eventually became a broad umbrella term for rock music that was distinct from the lineage of bands like The Beatles, The Rolling Stones, and Led Zeppelin. Instead, its roots were traced to artists such as David Bowie, Iggy Pop, The Velvet Underground, and The Ramones. "the word alternative became more widely used as this umbrella term for rock music that was different that did not descend from the Beatles and the Stones and Led Zeppelin, but from Bowie and Iggy Pop and the Velvet Underground and the Ramones." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjTWcy8RHEs

**Meaning by Grunge Era (Early 90s)** : By the early 1990s and the grunge era, "alternative" described a new generation of music descended from punk and new wave, rather than from bands like Led Zeppelin or The Beatles. It signified an aesthetic and sensibility, not merely a record label origin, and largely lost its countercultural significance. "by the time we got to the grunge era of the early 90s, alternative was being used to describe a new generation of music that had descended from punk and new wave and not from Led Zeppelin and the Beatles." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=__7iEl3lH8g
**Modern Usage and Diminished Relevance** : While some "artsy types" favored "post-modern" (which was sometimes simplified to "modern rock"), the most common phrase for contemporary rock music is now simply "New Rock." Consequently, the word "alternative" has largely lost its original meaning and intended purpose, as what was once considered alternative frequently now occupies the mainstream. "So as a result, the word alternative has lost its meaning, or at least the intent of its original meaning." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z8BTWo66SDI

**Nebulous Umbrella Term of the 1990s** : "Alternative" emerged in the early 1990s as a "nebulous umbrella term" used to classify bands that were so unique they "defied description." It came to broadly signify "difference" and served as a category for artists like Tool, Red Hot Chili Peppers, Beastie Boys, and Rage Against the Machine, causing confusion due to its broadness. "Alternative came to mean difference. It was a basket in which to put bands that defied description." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t2uXHP9cBdM
**Non-Mainstream Counterpart to Major Labels** : For many years, rock music was bifurcated into the mainstream, populated by famous bands on major labels (e.g., Led Zeppelin, The Stones, The Beatles, ACDC, Van Halen), and a category of "all the music that didn't get mainstream attention." This latter category comprised music deemed too weird, different, or innovative for general media and the public, though it cultivated dedicated fan bases often experienced in smaller club settings rather than stadiums. "On the other side of this equation, there was all the music that didn't get mainstream attention. This was the stuff that was just too weird or too different or too innovative for the general media and the general public." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z8BTWo66SDI
**North American Term Origin (1980)** : The term "alternative" in North America dates back to at least 1980, emerging as rock began to fragment. It originally described music from artists rejected by major labels due to smaller audiences, finding homes with small regional labels like Berserkli and Rounder. These labels and their music were seen as an "alternative" to the mainstream. "Now alternative dates back to at least 1980, a time when rock was really starting to fragment." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=__7iEl3lH8g
**Origin on Independent Labels (Late 1970s/Early 1980s)** : The "alternative" designation originated with small, independent, niche record companies such as Epitaph, SST, Beserkley, Rounder, and Sire, which emerged in the late 1970s and early 1980s. These "alternative labels" were unaffiliated with major multinational corporations and provided a vital platform for groups influenced by the punk movement, often signing bands that major labels overlooked due to perceived lack of mass commercial appeal. "The only way these acts could get their music out there was to create alliances with small, independent, niche record companies who weren't affiliated with a major label." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z8BTWo66SDI
