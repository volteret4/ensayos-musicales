# genre - Britpop

## curiosities
**Bloc Party's Context** : Bloc Party emerged as part of the post-Brit Pop world, alongside bands like Coldplay and the Kaiser Chiefs. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Cultural Storm (1991-1997)** : An era that acted as a "perfect cultural storm," running from approximately 1991 to 1997. During this time, "London was swaying again," and "British acts were selling tens of millions of albums the world over." It was characterized as "fresh and fun and cool." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg
**Decline** : The scene was considered "over" by the time Elastica released its second album, five years after its debut. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI
**Decline and Collapse (Late 1995-1997)** : The "Cool Britannia" era collapsed under the weight of "overexposure, boredom, and drugs." Factors contributing to its decline included a loss of focus with many inferior bands, overexposure of major acts like Oasis, the challenge for major players to release successful follow-up albums, and rampant heroin and cocaine use within the industry. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg
**Emergence from a Musical Vacuum (Early 1990s)** : The movement arose to fill a void in British music. The 1980s club and rave culture had waned, the Manchester scene (featuring The Stone Roses and Happy Mondays) was "pretty much over," and Shoegaze had "failed to ignite the masses." This coincided with the rise of Nirvana's *Nevermind*, which allowed grunge and American alt-rock to enter the British Isles. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg
**Female-Fronted Bands** : A scene that included many female-fronted groups such as Lush, Catatonia, Echobelly, Sleeper, Saint Etienne, and Elastica. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI
**Foundations from The Smiths** : The Smiths laid the foundations for Britpop, a genre that would dominate the remainder of the 1990s. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Mid-90s Musical Era** : A musical movement in the mid-1990s that celebrated "British-ness" and frequently acknowledged or borrowed from British rock predecessors. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rfObVld9rIE
**Official End (Late 1997)** : By the end of 1997, "almost everyone was distancing themselves from anything to do with Britpop." The "party was officially over," characterized by a "bad hangover," the toll of drugs, and public and media boredom. It was subsequently replaced by phenomena like the Spice Girls, and bands like Radiohead and Coldplay. The "six-year wave" had concluded. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg
**Origin of the Term (1994-1995)** : The word "Britpop" first appeared in the May 1994 issue of *The Face* magazine, an abbreviation riffing on Blur's view for a "renewed emphasis on British pop." A few months later, *The Guardian* newspaper used the term. The "real tipping point" came in *NME* in January 1995, after which the term became widely adopted. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg
**Peak Hysteria (Summer 1995)** : The movement reached its peak in the summer of 1995, primarily fueled by the intense rivalry, or "war," between Blur and Oasis. Media coverage was "non-stop," and the public engagement was so high that bookies even took bets on the outcome of their chart battle. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg
**Political Co-option (Late Britpop)** : Prime Minister Tony Blair, a self-professed fan, invited Noel Gallagher of Oasis to 10 Downing Street. This event was perceived as the "music of the people" being "co-opted by the establishment and the government," which was seen as damaging to the movement's image. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg
**Post-1990s Decline** : By the end of the 1990s, Britpop was struggling; its artists were described as "hung over from their party" or "messed up on drugs." Oasis had "flatlined," and Blur had "turned their back on Britpop." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**UK's Defense Against Grunge (Early 1990s)** : From its inception, Britpop's primary objective was to "defend the empire from the grunge marauders from the colonies," which had invaded and were conquering British lands, supplanting the "proud tradition of British rock and wall." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg
**Wine Brand** : The term "Britpop" has been used for a brand of sparkling wine, a dry Chardonnay bubbly, introduced by Blur bassist and professional cheesemaker Alex James. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qtdetc5-MQo
