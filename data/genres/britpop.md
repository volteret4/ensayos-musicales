**Britpop – Direct Descendant of Madchester; British Musical Nationalism; Beatles, Stones, Kinks, Who, Glam** : Britpop was the major UK genre of the 1990s, the direct descendant of the Madchester/Baggy sound, adding British musical nationalism — embracing the influences of the Beatles, the Stones, the Kinks, the Who, Glam, and the Mod era. Key bands included Blur, Suede, Supergrass, Pulp, Menswear, and — biggest of all — Oasis. "It took what madchester was all about and added in some British musical nationalism, embracing the influences of the Beatles and the Stones and the Kinks and the Who and the Glam era and the Mod era." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9cAeeExXKwU
