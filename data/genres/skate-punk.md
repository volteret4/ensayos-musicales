# genre - Skate Punk

## curiosities
**Coined by Thrasher Magazine** : The term "Skate Punk" itself appears to have been coined by an employee of Thrasher Magazine. "The term Skate Punk also seems to have been coined by an employee of Thrasher Magazine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Definition and Origins** : Skate punk is a subgenre of Alt Rock built on skateboarding culture, with its roots tracing back to the shared music of surfers and skaters, and later evolving with punk rock in the late 1970s. Its "ground zero" is likely Oxnard, California. "My main interest with skateboarding is the music that evolved along with it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Early 2010s Resurgence** : The early 2010s saw another surge in skateboarding's popularity, which in turn inspired a new generation of bands drawing from older skate punk groups. "In the early 2010s, there was another spike in popularity of skateboarding. And that resulted in a new crop of bands all inspired by older skate punk groups." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Early Sound and Issues** : The early days of skate punk were characterized by sharp, fast, angry songs filled with adrenaline. While cathartic, this sound was also associated with problems like violence, including LA gang violence, which made playing gigs difficult for the skate crowd for a time. "As you heard, the early days of the genre were dominated by sharp, fast, angry songs, containing the maximum amount of adrenaline." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Glory Years (Late 1994-2004)** : The "glory years" of skate punk began in late 1994 and continued for approximately ten years, with The Offspring's success leading to wider discovery of bands like Goldfinger, Lagwagon, Face to Face, MXPX, and Unwritten Law. "Skate Punk's glory years began in late 1994 and continued for a good 10 years." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Mainstream Breakthrough (1990s)** : Skate punk experienced a significant mainstream breakthrough in the 1990s, especially after the rise of grunge and alt-rock. This was exemplified by bands like Pennywise with "Unknown Road" (1993) and No FX with "Punk in Drublic" (early 90s), culminating in The Offspring's "Smash" (1994) which brought the genre widespread recognition. "Skate Punk did have to wait for its turn for a mainstream breakthrough, but when it happened, it happened big." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Mutation with Heavy Metal (1985)** : By 1985, the original skate punk sound began incorporating heavy metal elements, particularly guitar solos. This evolution fractured the community; some bands transitioned to metal genres, while others remained dedicated to hardcore punk. "By 1985, the original Skate Punk sound had acquired a new thing, elements of heavy metal, including guitar solos." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Nardcore Scene** : The Nardcore scene, named after Oxnard, California, emerged when local skaters formed bands like Aggression and Ill Repute, specializing in music for the skate crowd. "And the scene even had a name, Nardcore, after Oxnard." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**New Millennium Growth** : By the new millennium, skate punk had become massive, with established bands like Blink-182, The Offspring, MXPX, Pennywise, and No FX thriving, and newer bands like Sum 41 emerging. "By the new millennium, skate punk was massive." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Original Name: Skate Thrash** : The genre's original name was "Skate Thrash," which is related to the name of Thrasher Magazine. "The magazine's name is also related to the genre's original name, Skate Thrash." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Shift to Melodic Sound** : Skate punk bands began to integrate more melodic elements, retaining their fast and intense qualities but adding "singable melodies." Bad Religion's 1988 album "Suffer" is seen as a key inflection point for this shift towards "pop sensibilities." "Some Skate Punk bands started to get more melodic." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
