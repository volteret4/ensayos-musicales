# genre - Arena Rock

## curiosities
**"Heroes" Too Arty for the Era** : It is suggested that David Bowie's 1977 album and song "Heroes" might have been "too arty" for its time, given the prevalent music genres of the era, which included arena rock, disco, and punk. This may explain why the song initially underperformed on the charts despite its eventual acclaim. **Corporate Perception** : At the end of the 1970s, much of the arena rock scene was perceived as overly corporate, leading audiences to seek out more authentic alternatives like New Wave. "The Roxanne featured a lot of mostly arena rock bands that some felt were too corporate." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M ← arena-rock
**Late 1970s / 1980s Explosion** : Arena rock began to gain momentum in the late 1970s and "totally blew up" in the 1980s. This genre, characterized by power ballads, attracted a new breed of rock fans, predominantly younger women and their mothers, filling large venues. "Arena Rock and the power ballad that filled up those arenas with a brand new breed of rock fans, the younger women and probably their moms too, took hold of rock." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 7 - Women Who Rocked the 70s | http://www.ftr70.com ← arena-rock
**Mut Lang's Perfection of the Genre** : Although the term and subgenre of arena rock predated Mut Lang's heyday, he arguably perfected it, taking a "crunching, pumping sound" from ACDC's breakthrough right through his 21st-century work with bands like Maroon 5 and Nickelback, making his power ballads designed for stadiums. "Indeed Mut helped define what came to be known as arena rock, that term and the subgenre arena rock predated his heyday, but he arguably perfected arena rock." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > Pour Some Sugar on Me Edition Part 1 | https://shows.acast.com/hit-parade/episodes/695d4ef5d1ba84fb8f04493d
