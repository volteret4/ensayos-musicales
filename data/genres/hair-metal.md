# genre - Hair Metal

## curiosities
**Abrupt End of the Era** : The hair metal era came to an abrupt end around 1991, primarily ushered out by the rise of grunge and alternative rock. Kip Winger of the band Winger famously articulated this shift after watching Nirvana's "Smells Like Teen Spirit" video, declaring, "We are finished. We all knew it. It was obvious." **Defining Aesthetic and Origins** : Hair metal, the most common term for 1980s metal, was defined by the "sky high hair styles" of its members, heavy makeup, tight leather pants, and metal studs. Musically, it originated in the clubs of Los Angeles in the late 1970s and achieved mainstream status through the power of MTV. Its roots are traced back to 1970s glam rock, drawing influences from artists like David Bowie, T. Rex, the New York Dolls, and Kiss, which emphasized visual performance as much as the music. "the metal of the eighties has come to me most commonly known as hair metal because of the sky high.
