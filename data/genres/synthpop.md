# genre - Synthpop

## curiosities
**Alternative Name for Technopop** : This term was also an acceptable alternative name for the genre known as Technopop during its original emergence. "although synthpop was also acceptable." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KF5MwJVYng
**Chicago House Music Influence** : Synthpop influences later migrated to Chicago, becoming an integral part of the development and rise of house music in the city. "In North America, synth pop influences settled in Chicago, where the sounds became integral to the rise of house music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KF5MwJVYng
**MTV Programming Staple** : Its stylish nature made it perfectly suited for MTV upon its August 1981 launch. British synthpop acts provided a significant source of programming for the new channel due to their extensive backlog of performance videos. "And they were all very stylish which was perfect because there was this new thing called MTV. And it needed to be fit. See when MTV first went on the air in August of 1981, they had a grand total of 250 videos, most of which were from Britain. So the UK had this long history of bands performing on TV, so they had a big backlog of stuff to choose from. So until North American record labels in bands started investing in making videos, a big source of programming for MTV were clips from these stylish synth pop bands from the UK." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KF5MwJVYng
**Prevalence of Two-Person Model** : Many bands in this genre, especially from the UK, commonly featured a two-person lineup: a singer or front person and a composer/programmer/operator. "Many of them had this two-person model. You had the singer or front person and at the back, the composer, programmer, operator." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KF5MwJVYng
**Synthpop / Technopop – Guitars Out, Electronics In; Filled the Post-Disco Dance Void** : Synthpop (also called Technopop) emerged as one of the biggest offshoots of New Wave, based on the idea that guitars were out and electronics were the future. It was also extremely danceable, filling the post-disco dance void that had been created when disco was deemed uncool. Devo (from Akron, Ohio, supported by David Bowie) were among the original synthpop pioneers, and keyboard-focused UK acts like Depeche Mode, Orchestral Manoeuvres in the Dark, and Soft Cell pushed the sound further. "For practitioners and fans, guitars were out. The future was all about electronics." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M11CC71FSWE
