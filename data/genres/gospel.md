# genre - Gospel

## curiosities
**Component of Indie Folk** : Gospel music is identified as one of the genres that came together in Indie Folk, contributing to its rootsy and acoustic characteristics. **Definition in Secular Context** : Tom Bryan, author of "The Number Ones" column for StereoGum, posed questions about whether a religious song requires explicit mention of deities or denominations, or if it can simply be about imagining a better future. This highlights the fluidity of the "gospel" classification in mainstream music. "Does a religious song have to be about a specific system of beliefs? Does it have to invoke deities or denominations or holy words?" ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 32 - Take Me to Church - Religion in 70s Popular Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-32-take-me-to-church-religion-in-70s-popular-music
**Dissemination via Radio (WWII Era)** : Gospel music, along with other styles like Boogie-Woogie, Jump Blues, Swing, and Spear Trolls, became part of a "whole new melting pot of sounds" that spread rapidly via radio during World War II. This shift was attributed to the decline of Big Bands and the American Federation of Musicians strike, creating new programming opportunities for radio stations. "Boogie-Wooogie, Jump Blues, Swing, Gospel, Spear Trolls, a whole new melting pot of sounds was created and disseminated through the radio as a result of the big band problems and the musician's strike." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V7WjNR1ujhk ← gospel
**Early Fusion with Electric Guitar** : Sister Rosetta Tharp pioneered the combination of gospel music with electric guitar in the 1930s and 1940s, paving the way for future rock and roll artists. "Sister Rosetta Tharp, who was combining electric guitar and gospel in the 30s and 40s." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 32 - Take Me to Church - Religion in 70s Popular Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-32-take-me-to-church-religion-in-70s-popular-music
**Foundation of Rock and Roll** : Gospel music is one of the primary Black Music genres from which rock and roll, and subsequently alt rock, substantially descended. "It's only right, given that rock and roll is descended substantially from Black Music, the blues, rhythm and blues, jump blues, jazz, gospel, and so many other genres." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_1NNvxgx8uA ← gospel
**Mainstream Artists Topping Charts** : The gospel charts can be topped by unexpected artists, as demonstrated by Snoop Dogg's "Bible of Love" album reaching number one in 2018. **Mainstream Pop Crossover and Criticism** : In 1968, the Edwin Hawkins Singers' "Oh Happy Day" became one of the first gospel songs to achieve pop hit status. This led to criticism from churches that disapproved of their singers profiting from hymns. **More Granular Classification** : Gospel music was identified as one of the more granular genre classifications in 1955, showing an early inclination to define specific musical styles beyond the broader categories of popular music. "You could slice certain genres into thinner slices to include big band, Dixie Land, Scott, Hillbilly, and Jazz, gospel, Broadway show music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hstJ8M2laho ← gospel
