# genre - Bubblegum

## curiosities
**Critical Reception** : Bubblegum music faced significant resistance from rock critics who often published scathing reviews, primarily due to concerns about a lack of "authenticity," especially when record labels intentionally targeted preteens. They were also bothered by "fake bands" scoring mega hits. **Definition and Target Audience** : Bubblegum music refers both to its sound, typically uptempo dance music with uncomplicated lyrics, and the age group it is marketed to, generally 12 to 16-year-olds, with slight variations. "The term refers as much to the age group that the music is marketed to as it does to the sound which is generally uptempo dance music with uncomplicated lyrics. The age group for marketing was roughly 12 to 16." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 30 - The Sweet Sound of 70s Bubblegum Music | https://seventies.libsyn.com/030-bubblegum ← bubblegum
**Demand for Teen Idols** : Ron Tepper, a marketer for a Los Angeles company, observed that demand for teen idols could not be manufactured; they either fit what teenagers wanted or they didn't. If they fit, merchandise and records sold; if not, "you could not give those records away." "Ron Tepper was a marketer for the law for a company in Los Angeles and he said that you could not generate demand for teen idols. **Evolution into Teen Idol Market** : In the early 1970s in the United States, bubblegum music morphed into a phenomenon closely associated with teen idols, who also sold merchandise like *Tiger Beat* and *16 Magazine* posters and lunchboxes. While teen idols were "real" people unlike cartoon bands, from a musical standpoint, there was little to differentiate them from bubblegum acts.
