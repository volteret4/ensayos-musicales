# genre - New Romantics

## curiosities
**Bowie's Ashes to Ashes and the New Romantics Scene** : David Bowie's song "Ashes to Ashes" and its expensive music video tapped directly into the burgeoning New Romantics scene in Britain, a stylish and futuristic electronic pop movement originating from London clubs like the Blitz Club. Several members of this scene even appeared in the video. "Not only was the song sonically brilliant... but it tapped into a new scene coming out of Britain, the new Romantics, a stylish and futuristic former post-punk electronic pop coming out of London clubs, especially a place called the Blitz Club." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rw0uXf5UJvo
**Emergence from London Clubs** : The New Romantics was a stylish and futuristic form of post-punk electronic pop that emerged from London clubs in the early 1980s. David Bowie's song "Ashes to Ashes" and its accompanying music video perfectly tapped into this new scene, even featuring several kids from the movement in the video. "But it tapped into this new scene coming out of Britain, the new Romantics, a stylish and futuristic form of post-punk electronic pop coming out of the London clubs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2FyAZGUI_mk
**Fashion-First Movement** : New Romantics originated as a fashion-first movement, serving as a backlash against the "austere anti-fashion attitude" of punk. Adherents adopted "way over the top dress," makeup, wild hairstyles, and engaged in gender bending, drawing inspiration from glam rock and late 18th/early 19th-century Dandy styles. "It's important to understand that this sound started as a fashion first, and as a backlash against the austere anti-fashion attitude of the punks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0FYq5MBdHw4
**Musical Origins** : Although initially a fashion movement, some New Romantics adherents eventually ventured into music, leading to the formation of influential groups like Duran Duran, Culture Club, Spandau Ballet, and A Flock of Seagulls. The sound was greatly influenced by David Bowie and Roxy Music. "Just so happens that some of the adherents decided to get into music, and this was the origin of such groups as Durand Ran, Culture Club, Spandovalet, and a flock of Seagulls." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0FYq5MBdHw4
**Rapid Mutation and Decline (Early 1980s)** : The New Romantics scene mutated quickly, with fashion evolving from outrageous to sharp suits. Despite peripheral associations with groups like Ultravox, Japan, and Adam Ant, the rapid changes led to the scene largely burning out by 1982. "The New Romantic scene mutated rapidly. Fashion moved from the outrageous to sharp suits." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0FYq5MBdHw4
**Subgenre of Synthpop** : Within the broader category of synthpop, the New Romantics emerged as a distinct subgenre. "And within that, there were the new romantics." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
**UK Scene and Clubs (Late 1970s)** : The New Romantics scene emerged in the UK in the late 1970s, centered around clubs in London, Birmingham, and Liverpool, such as the Blitz Club, Billies, and Erics. This movement was marked by its distinctive fashion and musical styles. "a new scene called New Romantics took hold in a few clubs in London, Birmingham, and Liverpool in the late 1970s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0FYq5MBdHw4
