# genre - Alternative Music

## curiosities
**"Big Bang" Origin** : Alternative music emerged as a "big bang" from the two core principles of 1970s punk rock: a musical reset and a DIY attitude. This led to chain reactions of new ideas, sounds, attitudes, fashion, belief systems, and general approaches, breaking rules and exploring new concepts. "Taken together, these two principles resulted in what can be described as the big bang for what would later be called alternative music." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The Post-Punk Explosion Part 7： All the rest | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**"Dookie" Grammy Award** : Grimbey's album "Dookie" was recognized for its excellence in the genre, receiving the Grammy Award for Best Alternative Music Album. This award solidified its status and influence within the alternative music landscape of the 1990s. "También te hubo el Grammy al mejor álbum de música alternativa." ← Music Radar Clan > Green Day - Dookie (¿el concierto más divertido de los '90？) | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hPloRX7TWrY&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**1990s Keyboard Dominance** : In the early 1990s, before the massive success of "Smells Like Teen Spirit," alternative rock radio was largely dominated by keyboard-based music from bands like Depeche Mode, INXS, New Order, and The Cure, with a generally "left-leaning" sound. "alt rock radio at the time was about Depeche Mode and in excess, New Order, The Curie Ratio and Left Leaning Alternative Music that was more often than not keyboard based." ← Ongoing History of New Music > Alt Rock Revivals Part 1： Punk | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gkLMwrthZ9s&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**90s Revolution** : The early 1990s witnessed an "alternative revolution" in music, characterized by an abundance of new and cool sounds led by Grunge, marking a special time where new songs, artists, sounds, and scenes emerged daily. "It's led by Grunge, but supported by all manner of alternative music." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The Post-Punk Explosion Part 1： New Wave | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Adrift Period (Early 2000s)** : At the start of the new century, after the decline of Electronica and Nu Metal, the Alt Rock scene was largely "adrift," with many bands sounding similar and few new stars emerging, apart from occasional "indie brilliance." "for a while, the whole alt-roxying was, well, let's be honest, pretty adrift. Outside the occasional burst of indie brilliance...things all really did sound the same for a few years and no real stars emerged." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The 500th Show! | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ua93Ui1u6UI&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Björk's Debut Album as Collector's Item** : Björk's 1977 debut album became a "holy grail" for collectors in independent and alternative music. "este disco se ha convertido uno de los santos griales del coleccionismo de la música independiente y alternativa." ← Music Radar Clan > Björk - Su verdadero primer álbum | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=s4LSBQ-QXsg&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**British "Indie" vs. American "Alternative"** : In Britain, the term "alternative" did not gain widespread traction; instead, "indie" served as the preferred word for this type of music, reflecting a different linguistic and cultural adoption of similar musical phenomena. "In Britain, the A word alternative never really cut out. Instead the word indie was their word for alternative." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The Post-Punk Explosion Part 7： All the rest | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Cycles of Sounds and Trends** : Alternative music, having existed for over 45 years, has seen its sounds and trends cycle through multiple times. This occurs as new generations discover and adopt musical styles that appeal to them, leading to periodic revivals. "Alternative music has been around long enough, 45 years in counting, that certain sounds and trends have cycled through maybe a couple of times, as new generations pick up the ball and run with whatever music they seem to like." ← Ongoing History of New Music > Alt Rock Revivals Part 3： Emo | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v_am7ifh7Pk&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Definition in the 1950s** : In the 1950s, if rock and roll was considered the alternative to the mainstream, then its early stars, such as Elvis Presley, could be viewed as the first alternative music stars. "if rocket roll was the alternative to the mainstream in the 1950s, we could also say that Elvis was our first alternative music star too." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The History of Alt-Rock： Chapter 1 | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Dependence on Pioneers** : Without performers like Iggy Pop, today's New Rock and Alternative Music "wouldn't be possible," emphasizing the foundational role of early icons in shaping contemporary genres. "We need to care because without these performers, today's New Rock and Alternative Music wouldn't be possible." ← Ongoing History of New Music > What's The Big Deal About Iggy Pop | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OxctiPr0l-8&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Dominant Force (Mid-90s)** : By the mid-1990s, Alternative Music and Modern Rock were described as the "dominant musical force in the universe." "Alternative music and Modern Rock was the dominant musical force in the universe." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The 500th Show! | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ua93Ui1u6UI&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Evolution (Since 1993)** : Since February 1993, alternative music has undergone numerous changes and cycles, transitioning from the decline of the Manchester Sound to the rise of Grunge, Industrial, Punk comebacks, and then fragmentation and resurrections of other styles. "alternative music has gone through many changes in cycles." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The 500th Show! | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ua93Ui1u6UI&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Lack of Historical Documentation (Early 1990s)** : In late 1992, there was almost no existing historical writing on alternative music, with sources limited to old magazine articles, record company materials, and year-end charts. "At the time there was almost zero historical writing on alternative music. The only sources were articles in old magazines and boxes of stuff dropped off by record companies and some year-end best of charts." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The 500th Show! | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ua93Ui1u6UI&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Resurgence (2004)** : By late 2002 and definitively by 2004, Alt Rock experienced a resurgence with the emergence of many new and exciting bands. "By late 2002, there was a sense that maybe something was starting to bubble up from the underground again...By 2004, Alt Rock was back with all kinds of new exciting bands." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The 500th Show! | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ua93Ui1u6UI&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Rise in Early 90s** : In the early 1990s, Alternative Music was rising, leading to Iggy Pop being recognized once again as a legendary alt-rock pioneer, alongside other influential acts like The Velvet Underground and The Sex Pistols. "The early 90s were pretty good to Iggy. **Rise of the Term** : The term "alternative music" arose as a categorization for artists who sounded different and recorded for smaller independent labels, providing an "alternative to whatever the mainstream was offering at the time." This followed the evolution from "college rock" to "indie groups." "hence the rise of the term alternative music." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The Post-Punk Explosion Part 7： All the rest | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Transformation in the 80s and 90s** : New Order, formed by the remaining members of Joy Division, played a crucial role in transforming alternative music throughout the 1980s and into the 1990s, building upon the foundations laid by their previous band. "And of course, there's the work of New Order, who helped transform alternative music in the 80s and into the 90s." ← Ongoing History of New Music > What's the Big Deal About Joy Division？ | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1VWJ0uCQx8w&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
