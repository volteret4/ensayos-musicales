# genre - Alternative Music

## curiosities
**"Big Bang" Origin** : Alternative music emerged as a "big bang" from the two core principles of 1970s punk rock: a musical reset and a DIY attitude. This led to chain reactions of new ideas, sounds, attitudes, fashion, belief systems, and general approaches, breaking rules and exploring new concepts. "Taken together, these two principles resulted in what can be described as the big bang for what would later be called alternative music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4
**1990s Keyboard Dominance** : In the early 1990s, before the massive success of "Smells Like Teen Spirit," alternative rock radio was largely dominated by keyboard-based music from bands like Depeche Mode, INXS, New Order, and The Cure, with a generally "left-leaning" sound. "alt rock radio at the time was about Depeche Mode and in excess, New Order, The Curie Ratio and Left Leaning Alternative Music that was more often than not keyboard based." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gkLMwrthZ9s
**90s Revolution** : The early 1990s witnessed an "alternative revolution" in music, characterized by an abundance of new and cool sounds led by Grunge, marking a special time where new songs, artists, sounds, and scenes emerged daily. "It's led by Grunge, but supported by all manner of alternative music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**British "Indie" vs. American "Alternative"** : In Britain, the term "alternative" did not gain widespread traction; instead, "indie" served as the preferred word for this type of music, reflecting a different linguistic and cultural adoption of similar musical phenomena. "In Britain, the A word alternative never really cut out. Instead the word indie was their word for alternative." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4
**Cycles of Sounds and Trends** : Alternative music, having existed for over 45 years, has seen its sounds and trends cycle through multiple times. This occurs as new generations discover and adopt musical styles that appeal to them, leading to periodic revivals. "Alternative music has been around long enough, 45 years in counting, that certain sounds and trends have cycled through maybe a couple of times, as new generations pick up the ball and run with whatever music they seem to like." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v_am7ifh7Pk
**Definition in the 1950s** : In the 1950s, if rock and roll was considered the alternative to the mainstream, then its early stars, such as Elvis Presley, could be viewed as the first alternative music stars. "if rocket roll was the alternative to the mainstream in the 1950s, we could also say that Elvis was our first alternative music star too." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U
**Rise of the Term** : The term "alternative music" arose as a categorization for artists who sounded different and recorded for smaller independent labels, providing an "alternative to whatever the mainstream was offering at the time." This followed the evolution from "college rock" to "indie groups." "hence the rise of the term alternative music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4
**Transformation in the 80s and 90s** : New Order, formed by the remaining members of Joy Division, played a crucial role in transforming alternative music throughout the 1980s and into the 1990s, building upon the foundations laid by their previous band. "And of course, there's the work of New Order, who helped transform alternative music in the 80s and into the 90s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1VWJ0uCQx8w
