# genre - No Wave

## curiosities
**Attitude Towards Experimentation in New York** : The no wave scene in New York was part of the experimental lineage, contributing to the development of an attitude that characterized post-rock. "Then we trace this attitude towards experimentation through things like crowd rock, David Bowie, public image limited, and the no wave seen in New York." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
**Avant-Garde New York Movement** : No Wave was a short-lived, highly avant-garde post-punk style that appeared in New York City in the late 1970s and early 1980s. Its practitioners explicitly rebelled against all musical conventions, prioritizing noise, confrontation, and dissonance to create "groundbreaking art" and avoid rock clichés. "No Wave practitioners rebelled against all musical conventions. The worst thing, they believed, was to recycle the same old rock clichés over and over and over again." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4
**Characteristics: Dissonance and Noise** : The genre featured lots of dissonance, noise, and guitar chords and sounds that "just did not make sense." It occasionally incorporated elements of free jazz, funk, and disco. "Lots of dissonance, lots of noise, and a lot of guitar chords and sounds that just did not make sense. Elements of free jazz and funk and disco were sometimes incorporated." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pLf3kUONewg
**Ephemeral Nature of Bands** : With the exception of Sonic Youth, every single No Wave band was short-lived and "flamed out," indicating the intensely experimental and often commercially challenging nature of the movement. "Every single no-wave band flamed out." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjTWcy8RHEs
**Fusion of Punk and Avant-Garde** : The No Wave genre was characterized by an equal blend of punk's raw energy and avant-garde art sensibilities. Adherents believed that rock and roll, including punk, had become too conservative and institutionalized. "This was equal parts punk and avant-garde art." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjTWcy8RHEs
**Late 1970s New York Scene** : No Wave was a short-lived, very avant-garde live scene in New York in the late 1970s, characterized by its deliberate non-commercialism and intent to be difficult to listen to. "Before we go any further in this history of post-rock, we have to loop around to New York for a very short live scene called No Wave. This happened in the late 1970s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pLf3kUONewg
**Negative, Harsh, Formless Sound** : Predictably, No Wave music was often described as negative, harsh, formless, noisy, and frequently completely atonal. This extreme sound was a direct response to the perceived conservatism of other contemporary genres. "And predictably, the music was negative and harsh, and formless, and noisy, and often completely atonal." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjTWcy8RHEs
**New York City Origins and Nihilistic Style** : No Wave emerged from New York City as a "cousin" to cold wave, distinguished by its very nihilistic, dissonant, and difficult-to-listen-to yet artsy aesthetic. It incorporated both synthesizers and traditional instruments. "Cold wave at a cousin called No Wave, which was very nihilistic and dissonant and hard to listen to, but artsy. And it came out of New York City." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE
**Origins and Philosophy** : Emerging in the late 1970s from New York City's East Village, No Wave was a short-lived movement that combined punk and avant-garde rock. Adherents believed rock and roll had become too conservative and even punk was institutionalized, leading them to create music often lacking musical training, characterized by negativity, harshness, formlessness, noise, and atonality. "A short-lived movement centered in the East Village known as No Wave was born in the late 1970s. Now No Wave was equal parts punk and avant-garde rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Rejection of Conventions** : No Wave artists rejected all rock conventions, including those expressed in punk and new wave, aiming to create an effect and elicit a response, even anger. It was nihilistic, pessimistic, and confrontational, yet wrapped in an artsy guise that included cinema, fashion, and visual art. "All No Wave artists professed to reject all conventions of rock, even as it was expressed in punk and new wave. It was nihilistic, it was pessimistic, it was confrontational, but it was also wrapped in an artsy guise that included cinema, fashion, and all manner of visual art." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pLf3kUONewg
**Rejection of Musical Training** : A notable characteristic of many No Wave bands was their deliberate lack of formal musical training. This contributed to their raw, experimental, and often atonal sound, embracing a rejection of traditional musical norms. "This is why many no-wave bands often had no musical training whatsoever." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjTWcy8RHEs
**Short-Lived East Village Movement (Late 1970s)** : No Wave was a brief but intense musical movement centered in New York City's East Village during the late 1970s. It emerged as many underground fans felt bored with both punk and New Wave. "And this resulted in a short-lived movement centered in the East Village known as No Wave." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjTWcy8RHEs
