# genre - Alt Dance

## curiosities
**Emergence from Post Punk** : Alt Dance is a style of music that emerged in the years immediately following the punk rock era of the 1970s, specifically from the "post-punk explosion." It signifies a period when dancing became cool again for "cool kids" after the stigma associated with disco had subsided. "It's part four of the post-punk explosion, and it's all about what we'll call alt dance." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0FYq5MBdHw4
**Revival of Dance Culture** : Following the "death of disco" by 1980, alt dance emerged as a "cool underground dance music" that welcomed outsiders and "weirdos," contrasting sharply with the exclusionary glitzy disco clubs like Studio 54. This movement helped restore dancing's status as a socially acceptable and cool activity. "This was cool underground dance music that welcomed outsiders and weirdos." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0FYq5MBdHw4
**Underground for the Underground** : While alt dance music grew substantially, especially in major cultural centers like the UK, Ibiza, Italy, New York, Chicago, LA, and Toronto, very little of it made it to mainstream radio. This underground nature was precisely how its fans preferred it, cultivating a unique subculture. "But very little of this music made it to mainstream radio, which is just how fans liked it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0FYq5MBdHw4
