# genre - Chillwave

## curiosities
**Chillwave – "Summer of 2009"; Coined Ironically by Hipster Runoff Blog; Washed Out "Feel It All Around"** : Chillwave is more atmospheric than Dreampop, with lots of reverb, very mellow, using vintage synthesizers for a retro-ish sound seemingly rooted (but not quite) in the 1980s. The word appears to come from the blog Hipster Runoff, and the intent was ironic — calling something "chillwave" was not a compliment. The internet turned it into a real thing. The summer of 2009 is still referred to as the summer of chillwave. It was one of the first alt-rock sub-sub-genres to bubble up almost exclusively from the internet. Artists include Ariel Pink, Neon Indian, and Washed Out (Ernest Green, Athens, Georgia). "The intent was ironic. Calling something chill wave was not meant as a compliment, but then the internet took all as it does and turned it into a thing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9cAeeExXKwU
**Influence on Vaporwave** : Chillwave from the 2000s is identified as one of the source genres from which Vaporwave drew elements, contributing to its unique sound. "Vaporware took bits from Psych, Lounge Music, R&B, and the Chill Wave of the Outs, and then slowed everything down." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tdTs-4Irv8c
**Lo-Fi Offshoot Subgenre (Post-1995)** : Identified as an offshoot subgenre of low-fidelity music that emerged after 1995, Chillwave represents the broadening impact and stylistic diversification of the lo-fi aesthetic. Its development illustrates how the foundational principles of lo-fi continued to inspire new musical movements and sounds. "Low five became such an established mindset and process that it began to spawn offshoot subgenres, such as hip-negogic pop and chill wave and glow five." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jq-e-fZxXOs
