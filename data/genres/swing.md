# genre - Swing

## curiosities
**Decline and Transformation (Late 1944)** : By the end of 1944, Swing was effectively dead as the dominant popular music form, largely due to a confluence of events including WWII, the ASCAP Boycott, and the Musicians' Union Strike. Swing musicians either transitioned into bebop or began playing new forms of music like Rhythm and Blues, which were inspired by swing but also by boogie and blues. "The end result of all this was that, at the end of 1944, Swing was effectively dead, as was the tradition of instrumentless being the stars in American music." ← Episode 2： ＂Roll Em Pete＂ by Big Joe Turner | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=thpMb1A_fAs
**Dissemination via Radio (WWII Era)** : Swing music, as part of a diverse "melting pot" of sounds, was disseminated through radio during World War II. This increased exposure was a consequence of the difficulties Big Bands faced and the American Federation of Musicians strike, which allowed new genres to fill the airwaves. "Boogie-Wooogie, Jump Blues, Swing, Gospel, Spear Trolls, a whole new melting pot of sounds was created and disseminated through the radio as a result of the big band problems and the musician's strike." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V7WjNR1ujhk ← swing

**Most Popular Music in Mid-1930s North America** : By the mid-1930s, swing had become the most popular form of music across North America. The strong emphasis on rhythm, vital for dancing, led to drummers receiving increased attention and respect within the music industry. "By the middle 30s, swing was the most popular form of music in North America, and because such an emphasis was placed on rhythm for dancing, drummers started getting more attention and more respect." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WL3zEWJJNuw ← swing ← swing
**Polite Society Reaction** : The reaction to Dixieland jazz, from which swing evolved, by polite society in the early 1920s was one of moral panic, similar to later reactions to rock and roll and hip-hop. It was denounced as "evil, jungle music" that incited "ludeness and intoxication" and violence. **Popularity in the 1930s** : Swing music was a dominant and popular genre in the 1930s, serving as the starting point from which Lucky Millinder's band transitioned into the emerging jump band style during the 1940s. "Slowly, during the 1940s, Melinda transitioned his band from the kind of swing music that had been popular in the 30s to the jump band style that was becoming more popular." ← Episode 7： ＂Good Rockin' Tonight＂ by Wynonie Harris | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IgqSPoYRUCM
**Roots in 1920s Jazz** : Swing music had its origins in 1920s jazz, but emerged as a distinct form when musicians like Paul Whiteman "cleaned up" the rough edges of earlier jazz, and later solidified with big bands led by figures like Benny Goodman. "Swing was a form of music that had its roots in 1920s jazz." ← Episode 1： ＂Flying Home＂, Benny Goodman, Charlie Christian, and Lionel Hampton | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RmfeJYl84Qs
**The Shuffle Rhythm** : Swing music is primarily characterized by the shuffle rhythm, where the first beat in a bar is stressed (one and two and three and four). This rhythm was the basis of almost all popular music before the backbeat. While strong and popular, it is described as a "little bit polite, a little bit tame" compared to the excitement of a backbeat.
