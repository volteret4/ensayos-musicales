# genre - Swing

## curiosities
**Dissemination via Radio (WWII Era)** : Swing music, as part of a diverse "melting pot" of sounds, was disseminated through radio during World War II. This increased exposure was a consequence of the difficulties Big Bands faced and the American Federation of Musicians strike, which allowed new genres to fill the airwaves. "Boogie-Wooogie, Jump Blues, Swing, Gospel, Spear Trolls, a whole new melting pot of sounds was created and disseminated through the radio as a result of the big band problems and the musician's strike." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V7WjNR1ujhk
**Hi-hat Importance in the 1930s** : The sound of hi-hats became a crucial component of the swing and big band era of the 1930s, playing a very important role in the musical style. "The sound of hi-hats became a very important part of the swing and big band-era of the 1930s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WL3zEWJJNuw
**Most Popular Music in Mid-1930s North America** : By the mid-1930s, swing had become the most popular form of music across North America. The strong emphasis on rhythm, vital for dancing, led to drummers receiving increased attention and respect within the music industry. "By the middle 30s, swing was the most popular form of music in North America, and because such an emphasis was placed on rhythm for dancing, drummers started getting more attention and more respect." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WL3zEWJJNuw
