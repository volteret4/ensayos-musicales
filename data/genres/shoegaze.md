# genre - Shoegaze

**British Origins** : Shoegaze, like early Dreampop, originated in Britain. It emerged as a related genre, essentially described as Dreampop with significantly amplified guitars. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFLMkvJAaUY
**Early 90s Prominence and 2010s Semi-Revival** : Shoegaze, a "loud fuzzy sound," had its peak in the early 1990s and seemed to be largely done by the end of that decade. However, by the 2010s, a thriving underground scene emerged, leading to a "minor revival" or "semi-revival." This scene featured club nights, social media activity, and new record labels (mostly British), and saw shoegaze techniques absorbed into other genres like Black Metal (creating Blackgaze) and Emo, and a variant known as Art School Shoegaze from Japan. **Lingering Presence and 21st Century Revival** : Shoegaze never truly vanished, remaining in the margins and awaiting a significant revival. **Loud, Immersive, Textured Sound** : Characterized by floaty, atmospheric, and highly textured sound, Shoegaze is not necessarily relaxing due to its extreme volume. **Musical Style** : The sound of shoegaze built upon Dreampop, taking its focus on layered sound to an extreme. It featured dreamy, loud, droning, distorted guitars, extensive use of effects pedals, languid rhythms, and vocals mixed very low, almost "barely there." "It was sort of dreamy, but also very, very, very, very loud, droning, distorted guitars, running through all kinds of effects pedals with languid rhythms and almost barely their vocals low down in the mix." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4
**Naming Convention** : The genre was named Shoegaze because of the common habit of many performers to "avoid contact with the audience" by staring down at the floor instead of engaging with them directly. "It was called Shoegaze because of the habit of many performers to avoid contact with the audience." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Origin and Characteristics** : Shoegaze was a "loud, fuzzy, buzzy form of guitar rock" that originated from the Thames River Valley outside of London in the very late 80s and early 90s, characterized by heavy distortion, feedback, vocals buried in the mix, and a "disarming amount of melody," making it immersive, semi-psychedelic, and dreamy. "Let's start with Shoegaze. This was a loud, fuzzy, buzzy form of guitar rock that came out of the Thames River Valley outside of London in the very late 80s and early 90s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Origin of the Term** : Shoegaze was a genre characterized by its very loud, droning, distorted guitars, extensive use of effects pedals, languid rhythms, and barely audible vocals. The name "shoegaze" originated from British music journalists, particularly from _Sounds_ magazine after a review of a Moose show, who observed bands staring at their shoes (and pedals) instead of interacting with the audience. "This sound became known as shoegas, because the British music press noted that these bands didn't really connect with the audience much well performing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4
**Overlap with Post-Rock** : Shoegaze bands have explored post-rock elements, both genres offering immersive sound experiences where listeners can get lost in effects and distortion. However, some post-rock can be clean and clear, contrasting with shoegaze's typical wall of sound. "Shoegaze bands have played with post-rock. Both are immersive sound experiences. You get lost in the effects in the distortion, but some post-rock is clean and clear." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
**Reliance on Fuzz and Feedback** : The shoegaze genre is fundamentally built upon and cannot exist without extensive use of fuzz, feedback, and distortion. Bands like My Bloody Valentine pushed these sonic elements to new extremes, creating the "wall of fuzz and feedback" that became a hallmark of the genre. "Their wall of fuzz and feedback and distortion took things to new levels, creating the foundations of shoegase, a genre that could not exist without these sounds." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w

**Etymology of the Term** : The term "shoegaze" originated from the observed stage behavior of these new bands. Unlike mainstream frontmen who performed frenetically, shoegaze musicians often looked down at their feet while playing. This was partly due to a desire for the "music to do all the talking" and, more practically, because they had to "keep their eyes on the foot pedals they were using" for their heavily treated guitar parts. "To the audience they appeared to be looking at their shoes all the time, hence the label shoegasing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vEHdoUlGurY ← shoegaze
