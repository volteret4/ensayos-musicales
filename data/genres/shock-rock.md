# genre - Shock Rock

## curiosities
**Early Rock and Roll as Shock Rock** : In its genesis, all rock and roll was considered "shock rock" by many, with figures like Elvis, Jerry Lee Lewis, and Little Richard viewed as heathenish and sent by Satan to corrupt youth due to their music, swiveling hips, and sneering lips. "In the beginning, all rock and roll was shock rock. These kids and their music inspired by black musicians, their swiveling hips and sneering lips. It was the stuff of heathens, music of the devil. Elvis, Jerry Lee Lewis, little Richard, shocking awful stuff, sent by Satan to corrupt the youth." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SIK2YFSmf9U
**Evolution to Deliberate Shock** : As early rock and roll became mainstream and less shocking, the concept of "shock rock" emerged as a deliberate strategy to create outlandish music and performances guaranteed to make people recoil in fear, disgust, and horror, while simultaneously attracting fans who found it "cool." "Eventually, though, early rock and roll became acceptable mainstream music and not nearly as shocking as it once was. This gave birth to the idea of using rock music to shock, period." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SIK2YFSmf9U
**Pushing Boundaries in the 1960s** : Throughout the 1960s, numerous performers sought to test societal limits with their music and behavior, pushing the boundaries of what was acceptable in rock culture. "As the rock scene grew throughout the 1960s, there were plenty of performers who wanted to see just how far society would let them take things" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SIK2YFSmf9U
**Shock for Shock's Sake (Early 1970s)** : By the early 1970s, a new dimension of shock rock emerged where the primary motivation was "shocking for shock's sake," emphasizing provocation as an end in itself. "By the early 1970s, there was this idea of shocking for shock's sake." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SIK2YFSmf9U
