**Origins of Ska – Jamaica 1950s; African Rhythms + Jazz/R&B + Calypso + Mento = Shuffle, Then Ska** : Ska was born in Jamaica in the years following World War II. Big Band, Dixieland, Jazz, Blues, and R&B were heard over shortwave radio from places like New Orleans and from American military personnel in the Caribbean. This musical stew became known as Shuffle and was heard on Jamaican national radio through the late 1950s and early 1960s. In 1962, Cecil Bustamante Campbell (Prince Buster) changed the beat emphasis to the 2 and 4, and ska was born. "So we have Dixieland, we have Jazz, Blues, R&B, Calypso and Minto. **Why "Ska"? – Murky Origin: Guitar Sound, Clue J. Johnson's Made-Up Word, Byron Lee, or The Skatalites** : The origin of the word "ska" is murky. Theories include: it was taken from the sound of the choppy guitar click (ska-ska-ska); a Jamaican musician named Clue J. Johnson liked to say "ska-vuvie" all the time; Byron Lee, leader of a band called the Dragonaires, coined it; or The Skatalites invented it when they changed their name from The Satellites.
