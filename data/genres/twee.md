# genre - Twee

## curiosities
**Twee – Innocence, Sentimentality, Naivety; Belle and Sebastian "Boy with the Arab Strap" (1998) as Defining Example** : Twee is a minor subsidiary division of indie pop that also has attributes of chamber pop (strings, horn sections, piano). It projects innocence, sentimentality, vulnerability, and naivety cut with harmless playfulness — almost like the thoughts of 12-year-olds brought to life. It is post-punk but definitely not punk. Glasgow's Belle and Sebastian, led by singer-songwriter Stuart Murdoch, is considered the defining Twee band. Their 1998 track "The Boy with the Arab Strap" — despite being about a certain type of sexual device — exemplifies the sound.
