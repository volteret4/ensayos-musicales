# genre - Shuffle

## curiosities
**Emergence and Radio Play** : The "musical stew" of Dixieland, Jazz, Blues, R&B, Calypso, and Minto became known as Shuffle. This new sound was frequently broadcast on Jamaican National Radio throughout the late 1950s and into the early 1960s. "This musical stew became known as Shuffle and was often heard on Jamaican National Radio throughout the late 1950s and into the early 1960s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zNIVqKqAlnk
**Popularity** : Shuffle proved to be a popular new sound, particularly among American soldiers returning home. Their desire to take shuffle records back with them spurred entrepreneurial activity in recording and selling this music. "This new sound proof would be pretty popular, especially with people like soldiers returning to the US." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zNIVqKqAlnk
**Precursor to Ska** : Shuffle was a musical sound that emerged from the combination of foreign sounds (Dixieland, jazz, blues, R&B) and indigenous Caribbean music (mental, calypso) in Jamaica after World War II. It became very popular throughout Jamaica in the 1950s and into the 1960s, frequently heard on national radio, and was a direct precursor to Ska. "These sounds all together ended up with something called shuffle. And shuffle became a big deal throughout Jamaica through the 50s and into the 1960s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WX2GlpMNg_8
