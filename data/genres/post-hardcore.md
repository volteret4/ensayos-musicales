# genre - Post-hardcore

## curiosities
**Definition** : "Post-hardcore" refers to a band with a sound that could not have existed had it not been preceded and influenced by hardcore punk. The DNA of the sound is similar but has evolved. "Same thing in the case of post hardcore. It's a band with a sound that could have not existed, had it not been preceded, and influenced by hardcore punk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=__7iEl3lH8g
**Early 90s Mention** : Post-hardcore was mentioned as one of the many forms of Alt Rock that enjoyed moments in the sun during the 90s, showcasing the heavier and more aggressive end of the alternative spectrum. "We could have just as well mentioned, early emo, dream pop, math rock, post-hardcore, alt metal, alt country, Tweet Rock, contemporary Christian alternative music, and so many more." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Hardcore Influence** : Post-hardcore is listed as one of the genres that has been significantly influenced by hardcore music since the 1980s. "we've seen the influence this music has had on metal and in creating post-hardcore music as well as skate punk and emo." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0qigzi1j81U
**Origins in Hardcore/Metal Fusion** : Post-hardcore emerged from the blend of hardcore and metal, as exemplified by bands like NoMeansNo. The sound of NoMeansNo is noted for containing elements that would later define the post-hardcore genre, influencing a generation of bands. "If you listen carefully to what they did, you can hear elements of what would become known as post-hardcore, a sound that would later birth groups like at the drive-in, Rollins Band, Alexis on Fire, Billy Talent, The Death Tones and Silverstein." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0qigzi1j81U
