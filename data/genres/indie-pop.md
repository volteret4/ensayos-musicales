# genre - Indie Pop

## curiosities
**1990s "Indie Sheen" and Wider Popularity** : In the 1990s, the term "Indie" became synonymous with "cool," a perception that was amplified as grunge and its associated styles swept away older genres like heritage bands and hair metal. Indie Pop and Indie Rock, including Tweetpop, though generally less heavy than grunge or mainstream alt-rock, benefited significantly from this "indie sheen," opening doors to wider popularity. They offered a desirable alternative for fans seeking new, cool music but were turned off by the aggression of grunge, industrial, punk funk, and ska punk. "In the 90s, Indy meant cool, grunge and its brother-in-swebbed away the old guard, including the heritage bands of the 60s, 70s, 90s, along with whatever hair metal bands were left." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jhgZrwtYE2g
**Emotional Focus and The Smiths' Influence** : The Smiths played a pivotal role in popularizing "ultra-emotional" indie pop; their breakup inspired numerous imitators who continued to craft emotionally charged songs. This emphasis on confessional lyrics and deeply felt sentiments, combined with subtle musical innovations over time, incrementally led to the solidification of indie pop as a distinct and standalone genre within the broader music landscape. "By the time The Smiths broke up a few years later, they'd spawn dozens of imitators or at least groups that were making these ultra-emotional indie pop songs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jhgZrwtYE2g
