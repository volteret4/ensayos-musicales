## curiosities
**Record Label Preference for Early Rush** : Following the release of Rush's *Fly By Night* album, their record label expressed dissatisfaction with the band's new musical direction. The label preferred more songs in the heavy blues rock style, similar to tracks like "In the Mood" and "Working Man" from Rush's first album, drawing comparisons to bands like Led Zeppelin. "Initially, the record label wasn't happy with the new direction on Fly by night. They wanted more songs like In the Mood and Working Man from the first album, you know, more heavy blues rock along the lines of Led Zeppelin." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B0CZ1deEgic
