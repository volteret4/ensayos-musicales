# genre - Concept Album

## curiosities
**21st Century Resurgence** : The concept album was slowly resurrected and experienced a significant surge in popularity by the early 21st century, with many coming from alt rock bands. "The concept album was slowly and carefully resurrected. And by the time we got to the first years of the 21st century, bam! We got tons of them." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KyNY3zv6uc
**Decline with Punk Rock** : The advent of punk rock, characterized by its two-minute, two-chord songs, led to concept albums falling out of favor for an extended period. "When punk rock came along with its two-minute two-chord songs of burning hate, the concept album fell out of favor for a very long time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KyNY3zv6uc
**Definition and Structure** : A concept album is a record where a story or a series of themes link everything together, front to back. It transforms a four-minute musical short story into an hour-long musical novel, breaking a complex narrative into constituent parts spread across multiple songs. "A record where the story or a series of themes link everything together back to front and front to back." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KyNY3zv6uc
**Historical Popularity (1960s-1970s)** : Concept albums were a "big business" in the late 1960s and 1970s, particularly embraced by progressive rock bands, with greater complexity often correlating to higher regard. "Concept albums were big business in the late 1960s and 1970s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KyNY3zv6uc
**Market Strategy in Digital Age** : Record labels favored concept albums in the 21st century as a strategy to combat the trend of fans buying individual songs via iTunes and downloading services. The format inherently encourages consumers to purchase entire albums for the narrative to make sense, thereby helping to keep the idea of the album alive. "A concept record didn't make sense unless you bought the whole thing, right?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KyNY3zv6uc
**Nature of Conception** : Concept albums do not always have to be grand, complex, or highly theatrical projects; they can sometimes be quiet and poignant, as exemplified by The Antlers' album *Hospice*. "Concept albums don't have to be big, complex, highly theatrical projects. Sometimes they can be just quiet and poignant." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KyNY3zv6uc
