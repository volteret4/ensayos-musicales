# genre - Ska-punk

## curiosities
**Fashion** : Ska-punk fashion encompasses a range of haircuts, from mohawks to buzzcuts. Trilbies or fedoras are popular hats, often contributing to a "corny" aesthetic that is integral to the look. Button-down shirts can be worn for a corny style, but the standard uniform includes band t-shirts, often tucked into pants. Unlike the black and white motif of Two Tone ska, ska-punk welcomes vibrant colors, such as bright red suspenders. Skinny or slim-fit jeans are commonly worn with rolled-up cuffs to display shoes like Doc Martens, Chuck Taylors, or Vans. "Dress includes virtually any kind of haircut from Mohawk's to Buzzcuts, hats are good, trilobies or fedoras are best. It's got corny, but corny is a big part of the look." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mAlc-N2wn5s
**Third Wave Ska Evolution (1990s US)** : Ska-punk represents the "third wave" of ska, originating in the United States in the 1990s. This genre uniquely blends the traditional beats and rhythms of first and second-wave ska with the angsty, energetic elements of punk rock. "Third wave Skop is rooted in the US, starting in the 1990s. It takes the original beats and rhythms of first and second wave Skop and adds some angsty punk rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mAlc-N2wn5s
