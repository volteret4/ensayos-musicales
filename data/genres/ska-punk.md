# genre - Ska Punk

## curiosities
**Flavor of Third Wave Ska** : Ska Punk was a descriptor for the third wave of Ska, which had "several flavors," with bands incorporating more punk or metal elements than previous iterations of Ska. "SCAT Punk, as it became known, had several flavors." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WX2GlpMNg_8
**Revival of Ska and Reggae Influences** : The mid-1990s witnessed a new generation of ska punks who integrated reggae and ska into their punk vision, echoing how original 1970s punk bands like The Clash (with dub experiments) and The Slits (with reggae channeling) had done the same. "There was a long list of groups that incorporated reggae and sky into their vision of what punk rock could be. If you recall your music history, this is exactly what happened with the original punk in the 1970s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0QtMI00T5jw
**Subset of Pop Punk** : Ska punk is described as a "subset of pop punk." This categorization highlights its close stylistic relationship and shared audience with pop punk, particularly evident on platforms like the Warped Tour. "That and Scott punk, which can be viewed as a subset of pop punk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
