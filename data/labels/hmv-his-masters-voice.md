# label - HMV (His Master's Voice)

## curiosities
**Acquisition by Sunrise Records and Revitalization (2017 onwards)** : Following HMV Canada's closure, Canadian chain Sunrise Records acquired the leases for 70 HMV locations across Canada. Subsequently, Sunrise also purchased Hillcoe's interest in HMV UK and Ireland, thus keeping the HMV brand alive. While some stores were closed, others reopened, including the new HMV Vault flagship store in Birmingham in 2019. "However, Canada's Sunrise Records, a chain formerly with just a handful of locations, moved in and snapped at the leases for 70 HMV locations across Canada." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
**Canadian Operations and Customer Loyalty (Pre-2010)** : HMV Canada was notable for its customer-centric policies, including a no-hassle returns policy and a popular loyalty program. This program allowed customers to receive an 11th CD for free after purchasing ten, which was particularly appealing given that CDs retailed for $17-20 each at the time. "First, they had a no-hassle returns policy. And secondly, they had a loyalty program by 10 CDs, Get the 11th Free, which was really good when you consider that CDs at the time were running 17, 18, 19, 20 dollars each." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
**Canadian Subsidiary Closure (2017)** : HMV Canada, operating as a subsidiary of the parent company, also encountered significant financial difficulties, ultimately leading to bankruptcy. All HMV stores in Canada were permanently closed by April 30, 2017. "Bankruptcy Beckons and all the stores were closed by April 30, 2017." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
**Financial Decline and Bankruptcies (2010s)** : The entire HMV group began facing severe financial problems by 2010. While vinyl sales saw a rise, they were insufficient to offset the steep decline in CD sales. Concurrently, operational costs such as rents increased while customer foot traffic dwindled. A strategic pivot towards selling more music merchandise also failed to reverse the trend. Following a particularly difficult Christmas season compounded by adverse weather, HMV initiated store closures in January 2011. Exactly two years later, in January 2013, HMV UK declared bankruptcy, and subsequent revival attempts by Hillcoe also failed, leading to more closures and another bankruptcy. "By 2010, the entire HMV group was running into financial problems." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
**Global Expansion and Flagship Store (1990s)** : By the 1990s, HMV had evolved into a global franchise, operating 320 stores at its peak. Its flagship location at 383 Oxford Street in London was recognized as the world's largest record store, known for hosting regular, decent-sized in-store performances. The chain extended its reach across continents, with stores in Australia, Ireland, Singapore, Hong Kong, India, and Italy. "At its peak in the 1990s, HMV owned 320 stores." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
**Merger Leading to EMI (1931)** : A decade after its initial store opening, the Gramophone Company, HMV's parent entity, merged with another recorded music manufacturer. This union resulted in the formation of Electrical and Musical Industries, which subsequently became known as EMI. "Ten years later, there was a merger between the gramophone company and another recorded music manufacturer, resulting in electrical and musical industries, henceforth known as EMI." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
**Origins and First Store (1921)** : HMV, an acronym for "His Master's Voice," originated in the UK as a record label owned by the Gramophone Company. The very first HMV store opened in 1921 on Oxford Street in London, capitalizing on the surging demand for records that followed the gramophone's popularity during and after World War I. "And the first store opened on Oxford Street in London in 1921." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
**US Market Challenges (Closed by 2004)** : HMV maintained a limited presence in the United States, primarily establishing stores in Manhattan. However, these American ventures did not perform well and all US locations were closed by the end of 2004. "The American store is closed by the end of 2004." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
