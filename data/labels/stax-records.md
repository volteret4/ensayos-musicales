# label - Stax Records

## curiosities
**Early Post-Moman Struggles** : After Chips Moman left, Stax "flounded a little bit for a few months." Jim Stewart and Steve Cropper shared production duties, releasing a mix of good but unsuccessful soul records and "truly die a novelty country songs" that failed to hit the charts. "After Momen left, the label flounded a little bit for a few months." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Market Research Advantage** : The Stax studio, located in a former cinema, had a record shop in its lobby run by Estelle Axton. This allowed the label to conduct real-time market research by observing what black teenagers were buying and listening to, and even testing new recordings on the crowd. "By having a record shop in the record studio itself, they knew what was selling and could make more music that sounded like that." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Not Exclusively a Soul Label Initially** : In its early days, Stax Records was not exclusively a soul label. It continued to cut country and rockabilly records, such as the session booked for Billy Lee Riley that accidentally led to the formation of Booker T and the M.G.'s. "Stacks were still not exclusively a soul label, and it was cutting the art country unrocketably record" ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Origin from Satellite Records** : Stax Records was born from Satellite Records after a trademark conflict. The name is an acronym formed from the first two letters of founders Jim Stewart and Estelle Axton's surnames. "Stax Records was born." ← Episode 105： ＂Green Onions＂ by Booker T.and the MGs | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cw6xJG8E_w0
**Rapid Catalog Expansion** : To quickly establish a new catalog after its split from Atlantic Records, Stax Records released an unprecedented 27 albums all at once. This strategy provided numerous artists, including Isaac Hayes, with the opportunity to create and release music, with Hayes notably insisting on doing it "his way." "Stacks records had split from Atlantic records and needed a catalog so they released 27 albums all at once to give them this quick catalog of music." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 22 - Power to the People - The Music of the Black Power Movement | https://seventies.libsyn.com/022-power-to-the-people-62520-11-01-am ← stax-records
**Signing of Delaney & Bonnie (1968)** : In 1968, Delaney & Bonnie signed with the legendary Stax Records in Memphis, which influenced their music with a distinct soul sound. "In 1968 Delinean Bonnie signed with the legendary stacks records and Memphis and you can hear that soul influence in their music." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 59 - Bands and Booze on the Festival Express of 1970 | https://www.ftr70.com/ ← stax-records
**Split from Atlantic Records** : Stax Records underwent a significant transition when it split from Atlantic Records. This separation necessitated a rapid strategy to rebuild its music catalog independently. **The "Mixed Group" House Band** : The success of "Green Onions" led to Booker T and the M.G.'s becoming the Stax house band, a significant development for the label. This group, with its "mixed" black and white members, played on virtually every session, providing a consistent and distinctive sound.
