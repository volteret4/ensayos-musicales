# label - ATV Music

## curiosities
**Acquisition of Dick James' Northern Songs Share** : ATV Music acquired Dick James's share of Northern Songs, which included The Beatles' publishing rights, without informing John Lennon and Paul McCartney. "Dick James wanted out of Northern Songs. That's when he sold his share to a company called ATV Music without giving any notice to John and Paul." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pe40SqDrpZ0
**Acquisition of The Beatles' Remaining Share (By 1970)** : ATV Music eventually acquired The Beatles' remaining stake in Northern Songs, owning 99% of their publishing by January 1970, a move that fueled the band's dissolution. "the Beatles sold their piece of Northern Songs to ATV. By January 1970, ATV owned 99% of the Beatles' publishing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pe40SqDrpZ0
**Partial Sale to Sony Music (1995)** : Michael Jackson, facing financial issues, sold half of the ATV Music catalog to Sony Music for approximately $100 million. "Jackson was having financial issues, so he sold half of this catalog to Sony Music for about $100 million." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pe40SqDrpZ0
**Remaining Shares Sold to Sony by Jackson Estate (2016)** : In 2016, Michael Jackson's estate sold the remaining shares in ATV Music to Sony for $750 million, consolidating Sony's ownership. "And in 2016, the rest of his shares in ATV Music are sold to Sony for $750 million." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pe40SqDrpZ0
**Sale of Catalog to Michael Jackson (1985)** : In 1985, ATV Music, holding 4,000 songs including 250 by The Beatles and tracks by The Rolling Stones, Elvis, and Bruce Springsteen, was sold to Michael Jackson for $47.5 million. "In 1985, ATV Music, that company with the Beatles publishing, went up for sale. Four thousand songs, including 250 from the Beatles, which is pretty much everything they ever wrote, as well as tracks by the Rolling Stones, Elvis and Bruce Springsteen. Jackson then outbid McCartney, paying $47.5 million for this catalog." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pe40SqDrpZ0
**Sony Acquires Larger Share Option (2006)** : Sony retained an option to buy an additional 50% of Jackson's remaining share in ATV Music in 2006, which would have resulted in Sony owning 75% of The Beatles' publishing, with the catalog valued at $1 billion at that time. "In 2006, Jacko was having more financial issues, so Sony retained an option to buy 50% of Jackson's remaining share. So that means Sony would own 75% of the Beatles publishing. And at that point, the catalog was valued at about $1 billion." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pe40SqDrpZ0
