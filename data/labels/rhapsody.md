# label - Rhapsody

## curiosities
**Evolution of Listen.com** : Rhapsody was the name adopted by Listen.com in 2001, after the company initially launched in 1999 as the first modern streaming service. It subsequently evolved into the legitimate Napster. "It changed its name to Rhapsody in 2001 and is now known as Napster, but this is a completely legitimate and legal Napster unlike the original." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Y6nHw5xx88
**First Legitimate Streaming Service (2001)** : Rhapsody is identified as the first legitimate streaming music service, launched in 2001. It pioneered the legal streaming model, paving the way for the eventual dominance of streaming in the music industry, which by the end of the 2000s saw dozens of services emerge, led by Spotify. "The first legitimate streamer was Rhapsody in 2001." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
**Launch as First Streaming Platform (2001)** : Rhapsody debuted in December 2001 as the pioneering platform for music streaming. It offered subscribers unlimited access to its online music library for a monthly fee, a concept deemed radical by both record labels and music fans at a time when music ownership was the norm. "First, out of the gate was a platform called Rhapsody in December 2001. For a monthly fee, subscribers could stream as much of the company's online library as they wanted." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fhq9WVeiE_U
**Ownership vs. Access Challenge** : Rhapsody initially faced resistance from music fans and the industry due to its model of paying for access rather than ownership, meaning music access ceased upon subscription cancellation. This challenged the deeply ingrained "ownership mindset" that had characterized the music industry for its first century, where physical ownership was the only guarantee of on-demand listening. "This was way too radical for most record labels. And it was weird for music fans too. Wait, we're just accessing the music and we're not owning it? And when we stop paying our monthly fee, all the music goes away? Yeah, that's the way it was. Remember, though, that we were still locked into the ownership mindset back then. For the first hundred years of the music industry, the only way we could guarantee unlimited on-demand listening to music was to own it on a piece of plastic. Streaming was entirely virtual, and you didn't own it. You rent it. It took a long time for people to come around to this idea." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fhq9WVeiE_U
**Rebranding as Napster** : Rhapsody eventually acquired the intellectual property rights of the file-sharing service Napster and subsequently rebranded itself under the Napster name, becoming a legal streaming version of the formerly controversial platform. "Even Rhapsody, the original streaming music service, is still with us. Although after they bought the intellectual property rights of Napster, they rebranded themselves with that name. Rhapsody is now known as Napster, a legal version." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fhq9WVeiE_U
