# label - Simple Machines

## curiosities
**Cassette-Only Experiment** : Simple Machines was a "cheapo label" run out of a house in Arlington, Virginia, by two members of the band Sudami. The company was an experiment with the idea of exclusively releasing material on cassette. Instead of expensive CDs and vinyl, they would "dub off new cassettes as the market demanded, usually five at a time," ensuring their releases would "never be out of print." "The company was an experiment. The idea was to only release material on cassette." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_AY5lS-FmlU
**Dave Grohl's Crush on Jenny Toomey** : One of the people running Simple Machines was Jenny Toomey. Dave Grohl had "a huge crush on her," which was suggested as potentially the "only reason he agreed to let her label put out" his solo album *Pocketwatch* under the alias "Late." "One of the people running Simple Machines was Jenny Toomey. Dave had a huge crush on here, which might be the only reason he agreed to let her label put out that cassette." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_AY5lS-FmlU
**No-Budget Label for "Pocket Watch"** : Simple Machines was a no-budget label run by Jenny Toomey, which took on the task of distributing Dave Grohl's cassette-only album "Pocket Watch" under the name Late. Utilizing volunteers and multiple cassette decks, the label produced as many dubs as possible until the master tapes degraded. "Jenny ran a no-budget label called Simple Machines, and using volunteers and a bunch of cassette decks, she ran off as many dubs of Pocket Watch as she could until the master cassettes wore out." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gdWLtMy4TBU
