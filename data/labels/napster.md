# label - Napster

## curiosities
**Illegal File Sharing Revolution (June 1, 1999)** : The original version of Napster launched on June 1, 1999, initiating an "illegal file sharing revolution" that severely damaged the legitimate music industry. By allowing users to easily exchange MP3 files online, Napster caused CD sales to "crater," forcing record labels to confront a new digital reality and eventually leading to the rise of legal digital downloads and streaming services. "But then on June 1st, 1999, the original version of Napster arrived, setting off the illegal file sharing revolution that nearly killed the legitimate established music industry." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
**Legitimate Streaming Service** : The legitimate and legal Napster is the current iteration of the streaming service that began as Listen.com in 1999 and was later known as Rhapsody starting in 2001. This version is distinct from the original file-sharing platform. "It changed its name to Rhapsody in 2001 and is now known as Napster, but this is a completely legitimate and legal Napster unlike the original." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Y6nHw5xx88
**Target of Metallica Lawsuit – Music Downloading Controversy** : Napster was the central platform in a major music downloading controversy in the early 2000s, becoming the target of a lawsuit from Metallica. The band discovered unreleased files on the service and initiated legal action in April 2000, presenting evidence of approximately 350,000 users trading 95 Metallica songs, amounting to 1.4 million copyright violations, and aggressively campaigning against free downloading.
