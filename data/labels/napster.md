# label - Napster

## curiosities
**Massive Copyright Violations** : Lars Ulrich's legal team identified 1.4 million copyright violations involving 95 Metallica songs by approximately 350,000 users. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo
**Peer-to-Peer File Sharing (1999)** : Launched in June 1999, Napster was a program that allowed people to trade music in the new MP3 format. It enabled users to download songs for free from unknown sources online, posing a significant threat to the traditional record industry. By the end of 1999, its use, and that of other peer-to-peer file sharing software, had spiked dramatically, permanently altering the music industry landscape. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Publicity Stunt** : A list of ~350,000 users trading Metallica files, spanning 60,000 pages, was publicly displayed in front of TV cameras as part of Metallica's lawsuit against the service. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo
**Target of Metallica Lawsuit (2000)** : Sued by Metallica in April 2000 after unreleased Metallica files were discovered on the platform. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo
