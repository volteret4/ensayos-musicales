# label - 2 Tone

## curiosities
**Founder and Racial Harmony Symbolism** : Founded by Jerry Dammers, the leader of The Specials, 2 Tone was a Ska-based record label whose name was inspired by the two-toned suits worn by skinheads and Mods in the 1960s. The black and white color scheme was intentionally chosen to symbolize racial harmony. "Jerry Damors, the founder and leader of the specials, one of the great SCA bands of all time, and the founder of a SCA bass record label called Tuton. In the name of the label was fashion based. Damors dubbed the company after the two toned toned suits worn by skinheads and mods back in the 1960s. The black and white color scheme was supposed to symbolize racial harmony." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mhQ36Y-c9fM
**Walt Japsco Mascot** : Jerry Dammers, drawing from his art school days, created a checkerboard pattern featuring a black and white rendering of a rude boy, which resembled a photo negative. This image, based on an old photo of reggae star Peter Tosh, became Walt Japsco, the unofficial mascot for both the 2 Tone label and the broader Ska movement. "Damors was fascinated by this look. He had sketchpads full of designs, one of which was this checkerboard pattern that Jerry had come up with in his art school days. He made that checkerboard with a black and white rendering of a rude boy that kind of looked like a negative of a photo. In fact, it was based on a photo, a very old picture of reggae star Peter Tosh. This guy became the unofficial mascot of Tuton and the SCA movement in general. Even at a name, he was called Walt Japsco." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mhQ36Y-c9fM
