# label - Creation Records

## curiosities
**Alan McGee's Direct Discovery and Signing** : Alan McGee, the head of Creation Records, famously signed Oasis "more or less on the spot" after witnessing their impromptu performance at King Tuts Wawa Hut. His initial step to promote the band involved releasing a simple 12-inch single with a plain white label, which he "slipped it to a variety of people within the industry, hoping to drum up some hype," a strategy that successfully launched Oasis's career. "he was so impressed with the boys from Manchester, the Oasis boys, that he decided more or less on the spot to sign him up." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8UIBWyUt38g
**Bankrupted by My Bloody Valentine's Loveless** : My Bloody Valentine's album "Loveless" took two years to produce and was so costly that it literally bankrupted Creation Records, despite becoming a seminal work that influenced countless bands. "My Bloody Valentine spent two years making an album called Lovelace, that literally bankrupted Creation." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Discovery of Oasis** : Creation Records, through its representative Alan McGee, allegedly discovered the band Oasis at King Tuts Wah Wah Hut in Glasgow, Scotland, on May 31, 1993. This discovery supposedly occurred after Oasis threatened the venue owner with arson if they were not permitted to play. "Oasis was discovered by Alan McGee of creation records after they allegedly threatened the owner with arson if he didn't let them play that night." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Yl2poVWguu0
**Financial Challenges and Sale** : Creation Records experienced financial difficulties despite its successes. Notably, one of My Bloody Valentine's records was so expensive to produce that it "nearly bankrupted the label." Alan McGee eventually sold Creation Records to Sony. "Despite its successes, Creation did have its money issues like a lot of these other labels, especially after one of McGee's proudest signings, my bloody Valentine, turned in a record that was so expensive to make that it nearly bankrupted the label." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zB5zxycrbnY
**Founded by Alan McGee** : Creation Records was founded in 1983 by Scotsman Alan McGee, who was tired of the synth-based music prevalent at the time and desired to release material that resonated more with the raw promise of the Sex Pistols. "Another company who was right there was Creation, founded by Scotsman Alan McGee in 1983. He grown bored with the synth-based music of the time and decided to form a label that would release stuff that he liked, material that sounded more like the promise of the sex pistols." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Founding and Indie Niche** : Creation Records was a "fascinating British indie label" founded by Alan McGee in 1983. It successfully carved out an important niche within the early 1980s indie movement, similar to labels like Mute, Rough Trade, Factory, and Two-Tone. "Another fascinating British indie label of the 80s was Creation. It was founded by a Scotsman named Alan McGee in 1983." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zB5zxycrbnY
**Prominent on C86 Cassette** : Creation Records was one of the indie labels whose artists were featured on the influential C86 cassette, a compilation that reflected the state of new music in Britain in 1986. "They tapped labels like Creation and Rough Tray and a bunch of others." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Sony Bankrolling (1993)** : In 1993, Sony was reportedly "bankroll[ing] Creation" Records, and the label's executives wanted to observe Oasis perform before officially signing the band. This led to Oasis performing at a Sony convention that year. "Because I think at the time Sony were a bankroll in creation and I think they wanted to have a look at us." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dMsEj62KFEw
