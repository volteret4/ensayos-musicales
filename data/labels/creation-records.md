# label - Creation Records

## curiosities
**C86 Cassette Contributor (1986)** : One of the indie labels from which bands were selected to contribute to the NME's C86 cassette collection in 1986. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Discovery of Oasis (1993)** : Label head Alan McGee was present at King Tut's Wah Wah Hut in Glasgow, Scotland, on May 31st, 1993, when Oasis, through aggressive means, secured an unscheduled performance. Impressed by their set, McGee signed them "more or less on the spot," which led to their rapid rise to becoming one of the biggest British and global bands of the 1990s. McGee's initial action was to release a white-label 12-inch single to generate industry hype. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8UIBWyUt38g
**Early Breakthroughs (1983-1984)** : Its first release was by The Legend in the summer of 1983, with a significant breakthrough occurring in November 1984 with a single by The Jesus and Mary Chain, which brought considerable attention to the label. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Financial Ruin from "Loveless"** : The extensive two-year production process for My Bloody Valentine's album "Loveless" literally bankrupted the label. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Founding Vision (1983)** : Founded by Scotsman Alan McGee in 1983, driven by his disillusionment with the prevalent synth-based music of the time and a desire to release material that captured the "promise of the Sex Pistols." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
