# label - Warner Brothers

## curiosities
**Acquisition of Sire Records (1978)** : Warner Brothers acquired Sire Records in 1978, incorporating the formerly independent label into a major label operation. This acquisition included artists signed to Sire at the time, such as the Talking Heads. "But when Sire was acquired by Warner Brothers in 1978, they became part of a major label operation." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
**Attracting Indie Bands** : Warner Brothers successfully signed prominent indie bands like R.E.M., Husker Dü, Sonic Youth, and Nirvana by offering them significant contracts and the promise of wider distribution, stable income, and more sustainable touring conditions that independent labels could no longer provide. "So it came to pass on April 8, 1988, R.E.M. signed with Warner Brothers and took home a $6 million check." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DYtsWfguqGo
**Instruction to Create Videos for MTV** : Due to MTV's initial struggle to acquire enough video content, Warner Brothers, which owned numerous record labels, instructed its subsidiary labels to actively produce music videos. This directive aimed to ensure a steady supply of new material for the fledgling network. "And these labels were instructed to get on the video bandwagon so that MTV would have a steady supply of new products." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjTWcy8RHEs
**Largest Record Company in 1988** : In 1988, Warner Brothers was considered the largest record company in the world, and it was to this major label that R.E.M. transitioned from their indie label IRS. "Then in 1988, the group accepted an offer to jump from IRS. Their indie label, the one they'd been with from the beginning, to Warner Brothers, the largest record company in the world at the time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Major Label Signing of R.E.M. (1988)** : Warner Brothers signed R.E.M. in 1988 for an initial sum of $6 million after a bidding war, which was an exceptionally large amount for an indie band at that time. Eight years later, they renewed the deal for $80 million, signifying R.E.M.'s commercial success and influence in the music industry. "Warner signed them in 1988 for $6 million, an insanely huge number for an indie band at that time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vJszsJQpINQ
**Parent Company of MTV** : Warner Brothers was the parent company of MTV, giving them significant influence over the early direction and content of the music television channel. "The good news is that the parent company of MTV, Warner Brothers, owned a lot of record labels." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjTWcy8RHEs
**President Tom Wally's Meeting Style** : Tom Wally, the president of Warner Brothers, demonstrated an unconventional approach to meeting Billy Talent. He insisted the band come to his New Jersey beach house rather than him coming to the city. During a "s***ly little session" in a rented airplane hanger, he sat and looked at the floor, preoccupied with his house. Despite the odd circumstances, he still wanted to sign them and later hosted them for dinner on his patio during a family birthday, providing fried chicken and beer. "Tom Wally had, he wanted to see us. He was the president of Warner Brothers. And he didn't want to leave his beach house in New Jersey to come to the city." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GT_u0eFDlk8
**R.E.M. Signing (April 8, 1988)** : Warner Brothers, a major record label, successfully signed R.E.M. on April 8, 1988, offering a significant $6 million check and a promise of complete creative control. This move was pivotal as R.E.M. became the largest college rock indie band to transition to a multi-million dollar major label, seeking expanded distribution and an end to their constant touring cycle. "So when Warner's came with that $6 million check on April the 8th, 1988, they signed." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AymMybE33zU
**R.E.M.'s Contractual Obligations Fulfilled (2010)** : R.E.M. fulfilled their contractual obligations to Warner Brothers by 2010, after which they became free agents. This event marked a significant turning point in the band's career, leading to their eventual breakup when independent recording attempts stalled. "by 2010 they'd fulfilled their contractual obligations to Warner Brothers and became free agents." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vJszsJQpINQ
**Rob Cavallo's Pivotal Role in Signing Green Day** : Rob Cavallo, a representative for Warner Brothers, played a crucial role in signing Green Day to the label. Cavallo had a strong history of success, including helping to launch The Goo Goo Dolls, and his father had managed prominent groups such as The Lovin' Spoonful and Earth Wind & Fire. He famously signed Green Day for $300,000 without ever having seen them perform live. "But then a Warner Brothers rep named Rob Covello started some hard lobbying. He also had a pretty good track record." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nyfdGYWelic
**Signing of Linkin Park** : Warner Brothers eventually signed Linkin Park on their fourth attempt, despite the band having been rejected about four dozen times by various labels. Most within the label, apart from one persistent advocate, held low expectations for the band's potential success. "They were finally signed to Warner Brothers on their fourth try. They had been rejected three times, fourth try, they got in. No one at the label, with the exception of the guy who successfully begged his bosses to give the band a contract, thought that Lincoln Park would amount to anything." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7dXtgTkdv9w
**Strategic Major Label Distribution for Indie Bands** : Warner Brothers, a major label, engaged in a unique partnership with Rancid, an Epitaph band, by agreeing to distribute and "stealthily promote" their records. This allowed Rancid to maintain their punk rock ethics by staying with an indie label while benefiting from a major's reach. "but they agreed to have their epitaph records distributed and stealthily promoted by Warner Brothers, one of the majors." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gkLMwrthZ9s
