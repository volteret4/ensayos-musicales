**Color-Coded Vinyl Records (1949)** : Upon the launch of the 7-inch single, RCA introduced a system of color-coding records by genre. **Early Vinyl Experimentation and Patent Lapse (1930s)** : In 1931, RCA experimented with polyvinyl chloride as a superior replacement for the materials used in 78 RPM records. They even produced 12-inch vinyl records, ready for public sale, but the Great Depression prevented their release. Consequently, RCA allowed many of their crucial patents related to this vinyl technology to expire, a decision later regarded as "dumb" as Columbia Records would later use similar principles for the LP. "Then in 1931, RCA was experimenting with vinyl as a replacement for the crappy materials that went into making 78s. RCA even started making vinyl records that were 12 inches in diameter, and were ready to sell them to the general public. But it was the depression. And no one was in the mood for introducing a new format into what seemed at the time to be a dying industry... So not only did RCA give up, but they also led a bunch of their patents expire." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VlIErdSSVOM ← rca

**Introduction of 7-inch Single (1949)** : In March 1949, RCA introduced the 7-inch single, which was initially designed to be played exclusively on special turntables manufactured by RCA. These record players were relatively small, described as being the size of a squat blender, offering a degree of portability, though they still required an electrical outlet. The prominent large hole in the center was a distinctive design feature of these records. `"When RCA introduced the 7-inch single in March of 1949, the original idea was to make them playable only on special turn tables made by RCA."` ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OuFvfZJKcpc ← rca
