# label - Atlantic Records

## curiosities
**A&R Criticism of Billy Talent 2** : Mary Gormley, an A&R representative at Atlantic Records, expressed dissatisfaction with "Billy Talent 2," telling the band, "I don't hear a Green Day single, guys," despite the album later producing some of their biggest hits and becoming a fan favorite. "And guess what our in our rap Mary Gormley said to us when she heard the record for the first time. I don't hear a Green Day single, guys." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GT_u0eFDlk8
**Deal with Why Can't Tori Read** : Atlantic Records had a recording deal with the rock band Why Can't Tori Read, which featured Tori Amos. Although the band's venture "didn't work," the contractual obligation for six more albums under this deal directly led to Tori Amos launching her solo career. "There was a detour into a rock band called Why Can't Tori Read, which had a deal with Atlantic Records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dJiSZ4X0F2g
**Eugenius Record Deal** : Atlantic Records signed the Scottish band Eugenius to a major label deal. This deal was secured following Kurt Cobain's public endorsement of the band after they opened for Nirvana on their 1991 European tour, leading to the release of two well-reviewed albums. "Kurt's endorsement of the band got them a major label deal with Atlantic Records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m7MbV-eCLyE
**Hip-Pocket Records Releases (1966)** : Atlantic Records partnered with Filco and Ford in 1966 to release singles on Hip-Pocket Records. These thin, bendable plastic Flexi Discs were sold for $0.69 at Ford dealerships and Woolworth stores, featuring artists like Chuck Berry and Sonny and Cher. `"They got Atlantic, Mercury, and Roulette records to release singles this way, selling them for $0.69 at four dealerships and through Woolworth stores."` ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OuFvfZJKcpc
**Initial Discovery and Bidding War** : Atlantic Records A&R representatives from New York were called to Billy Talent's rehearsal space late one cold night by their manager Steve Hoffman. Blown away by their performance, they flew back to New York and expressed strong interest to their president, initiating a bidding war involving Warner Canada, Elektra, and Warner Brothers. "Atlantic, these two guys were blown away. And we're literally smoking cigarettes and drinking beer and our little, you know, five by five. Three saws in front of them and then they're like, whoa, what's this?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GT_u0eFDlk8
**Lack of Follow-up Support in US** : After the initial success of "Try Honesty" from Billy Talent's debut album, Atlantic Records chose not to release a second single in the US. The band believed this was because the label prioritized promoting "bigger artists" nearing Christmas, marking the beginning of their ongoing "open battle with America." "And in the States, we released Tri-Honesty and it did quite well and then the label had a choice to release another single and they didn't. And that was kind of the beginning of our open battle with America." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GT_u0eFDlk8
**Legendary Status and Signing** : Billy Talent signed an 80/20 split deal with Atlantic for the world and America, with Warner Canada handling Canada. The legendary status of Atlantic, known for artists like Led Zeppelin, was a significant factor in their decision. The band met co-founder Ahmet Ertegun in his office during the signing, noting Jimmy Page's bow from "The Song Remains the Same" on the wall as a powerful symbol of the label's history. "that's a pretty legendary record label. I mean, that's that's Led Zeppelin. That was part of the selling power." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GT_u0eFDlk8
**P.O.D. Signing** : Atlantic Records signed P.O.D., a move that marked one of the first major crossovers of a band from the "Christian Underground" into the mainstream world of "Heathen Rock." "Eventually and thanks to the network of fans POD had amassed in the Christian friendly clubs across the US they were discovered and signed by Atlantic Records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6cbL0zLQeIs
**Signing of Mighty Joe Young** : An agent who saw Mighty Joe Young perform tipped off Atlantic Records, who then observed the group multiple times. Following a show at The Shamrock in Silver Lake, Atlantic Records made an offer, and the band signed their major label deal on April 1st, 1992. "The agent who saw the band play, tipped off Atlantic Records who came down to see the group play a bunch of times." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r6vVw66-gmI
**Signing of P.O.D.** : Atlantic Records signed P.O.D. after the band gained a substantial fanbase through Christian-friendly clubs across the US, marking P.O.D.'s transition from the Christian Underground to the mainstream. "POD became one of the first major crossovers from the Christian Underground into the mainstream world of Heathen Rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g-tBn8dsSqw
**Suggests New STP Album (2009)** : After the successful 2008-2009 reunion tour, Atlantic Records, Stone Temple Pilots' old record label, suggested it was time for the band to record another album, nine years after "Shangri-La Dee Da," to capitalize on their regained momentum. "The 2008-2009 STP reunion tour went well enough for Atlantic STP's old record label to suggest that it was time for another album." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WfTq9d2YadY
