# label - The Enclave

## curiosities
**Failed Deal with Sloan** : In the mid-1990s, Sloan secured a deal with The Enclave label as part of their efforts to penetrate the American market. However, before any significant developments could occur, the label "went out of business," halting Sloan's plans. "But then came a deal with a label called The Enclave in the middle 1990s. But before anything could happen, that label went out of business." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QB3nyFH_tRU
**Short-Lived Boutique Label (1996)** : The Enclave was a short-lived boutique record label founded by Tom Zutat, the mentor of Todd Sullivan (who signed Sloan to Geffen). Sloan signed with The Enclave in 1996 after Geffen released them from their contract, with Zutat having a mandate to make them a big hit. However, the label went out of business within approximately six months, leaving Sloan without a US label by 1997. "And we signed to this other label called the OnClave, which was short lived, which just set up to be really good at head, like balance of bastion, like the water boys." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
