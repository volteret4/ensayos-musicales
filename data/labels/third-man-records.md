# label - Third Man Records

## curiosities
**Analog Production Advocacy** : Third Man Records, owned by Jack White, offers vinyl versions of albums like The White Stripes' "Elephant" that are believed to be fully analog (AAA: Analog recording, Analog mixing, Analog mastering), demonstrating a commitment to traditional analog production. "If you go to Jack's third man record store in Nashville or Detroit, you can buy a vial version of the elephant album that I'm pretty sure is old school AAA." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yPok3MemDmE
**Early Dismissal of Seven Nation Army Riff** : Ben Swank, an employee at Third Man Records, initially reacted skeptically to Jack White's newly created seven-note riff for "Seven Nation Army," telling him "you can do better" and criticizing its rhythm. This anecdote illustrates the subjective nature of creative evaluation, especially considering the riff's eventual iconic status. "He later played it to Ben Swank, who worked at third-man records, the stripes label. Nah, he said, you can do better." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uEe_HLUay5M
**Elvis Presley's First Recording Reissue (Undated)** : Jack White, after purchasing Elvis Presley's first-ever recording from July 18, 1953, digitized it and re-released it through his label, Third Man Records, so that the entire world could hear it. "He's digitized it and re-released it through third-man records, so the entire world can hear it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tbmssCZ05ro
**Esoteric Roster** : The label boasts a diverse and "esoteric roster" of artists, encompassing Jack White's projects (The White Stripes, The Raconteurs, The Dead Weather) as well as figures such as Conan O'Brien and Johnny Cash. "Third Man has a very esoteric roster, ranging from Jack White and the white stripes and the rack on tours and the dead weather, which are all Jack's projects, of course, to Conan O'Brien and Johnny Cash." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q4dEV1yn3OY
**First Proper Release (March 11, 2009)** : The first official release from Third Man Records was a seven-inch single by The Dead Weather, "Hang You From The Heavens," released on March 11, 2009. "here is the first ever proper Third Man release. It's a seven-year single from the Dead Weather released on March 11, 2009. It's Hang You From The Heavens." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q4dEV1yn3OY
**Founding and Mission (2001)** : Founded by Jack White in 2001, initially to release early White Stripes 45s, Third Man Records has expanded into a vehicle for White's various projects and numerous other indie artists. Based in Nashville, it is noted for its significant contribution to the vinyl resurrection with the motto "your turntables not dead." "He's got his own independent venture, Third Man Records, which is based out of Nashville. Third Man's history goes back to 2001, when Jack and Meg still had the white stripes going into Detroit. The original idea was to release some early white stripes, 45s, but then it just kind of grew from there." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q4dEV1yn3OY
**Innovative Vinyl Pressings** : This record label is renowned for its unique and unconventional special edition vinyl releases. These often feature records with two halves containing wild colored oil inside, and similar pressings have incorporated materials such as blood, hair, urine, marijuana leaves, razor blades, gunpowder, and even cremated remains. "Jack White's Third Man Records loves to release special editions that feature two halves with some kind of wild colored oil inside." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B2g4QSREzpw
**Nashville Headquarters (2009)** : In 2009, Third Man Records debuted its Nashville store, which serves as the label's headquarters, a main store, a live venue, a recording studio, a warehouse, and a photo studio. Its unique location in an industrial area, near an auto repair shop, a methadone clinic, and a homeless shelter, is noted for serving a diverse community. "In 2009, Third Man debuted a store in Nashville, which is the headquarters for the record label. Its main store, a live venue, a recording studio, a warehouse, a photo studio. Not in the greatest location. It's in an industrial area next to an Ottawa pair place, also near a methadone clinic, and not too far from a major homeless shelter, but it serves everybody very well." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q4dEV1yn3OY
**Rolling Record Store** : The company operates a unique "rolling record store," which is a truck that travels to music festivals across the continent, providing a mobile retail experience for fans. "The company has its own rolling record store, which is a truck that shows up in music festivals across the continent." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q4dEV1yn3OY
**Source for Unique 7-inch Vinyl** : Third Man Records, Jack White's record store in Nashville, was the source for a specific 7-inch record with catalog number TMR001, featuring The Dead Weather and a Gary Numan cover. "I pull out this 45 RPM, seven-inch record that I bought at Jack White's third-man record store in Nashville, it actually has the catalog number TMR001." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VlIErdSSVOM
**Vinyl Resurrection Efforts** : Third Man Records is credited with doing more for the "vinyl resurrection" than any other indie label, actively promoting the format with its company motto: "your turntables not dead." "Third Man has also done more for the vinyl resurrection than any other indie label I can think of. I mean, the company's motto is, your turntables not dead." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q4dEV1yn3OY
**Vinyl-Centric Philosophy** : Third Man Records, founded by Jack White, has the motto "your turntable's not dead" and is entirely dedicated to vinyl, with a unique setup in Nashville allowing for recording, mixing, and pressing of records locally. "The motto of Jack White's third man records is, your turntable's not dead." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GES0Ms_kaCI
