# label - Def Jam

## curiosities
**Co-Founders** : Def Jam was a new record label co-founded by Rick Rubin and Russell Simmons. "By November 1984, the Beasties had signed to Def Jam, a new record label co-founded by Rick Rubin and Russell Simmons." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cYwk-B3qwwk
**Dispute Over New Album and Royalties** : After the *Licensed to Ill* tour, Def Jam demanded a new album from the Beastie Boys immediately, but the band felt burned out and refused. In response, Def Jam began withholding royalty payments, leading to a protracted dispute that ultimately resulted in the Beastie Boys leaving the label and signing with Capitol Records. "Then the relationship with deaf jam blew up. They wanted a new album right away, but the Beastie said, no, forget it. We're burned out. We need to recharge. So deaf jam started holding back royalties." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l1jPP1FhGo8
**Founding and Early Beastie Boys Signing (1984)** : Def Jam was a new record label co-founded by Rick Rubin and Russell Simmons. The Beastie Boys signed with Def Jam in November 1984, and the label's second single released was their track "Rock Hard." "By November 1984, the Beasties had signed to Def Jam, a new record label co-founded by Rick Rubin and Russell Simmons." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l1jPP1FhGo8
**Founding by Rick Rubin (1986)** : In May of 1986, Rick Rubin, a metalhead who also loved rap, was the head of Def Jam, a new label. He made a pivotal move by orchestrating the collaboration between Run DMC and Aerosmith, which helped to break down genre barriers. "Rick Rubin, the head of a new label called Def Jam, had an inspired idea." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PqOJ3YiFgYE
