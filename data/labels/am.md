# label - A&M

## curiosities
**Destruction of "God Save the Queen" Single** : Following the Sex Pistols' short-lived and controversial contract, A&M Records ordered the destruction of all copies of the band's first single for the label, "God Save the Queen." This decision was part of the label's efforts to sever ties and mitigate the fallout from their brief and tumultuous association with the punk band, making the few surviving copies highly valuable to collectors. "Meanwhile, all copies of the first ANM single, God Save the Queen, were ordered to destroy it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RN3JIDtPayY
**Sex Pistols Contract and Termination (March 1977)** : A&M Records signed the Sex Pistols to a 150,000-pound deal on March 10, 1977, in front of Buckingham Palace in London, only to drop them six days later. The termination was prompted by the band's extensive trashing of the A&M offices after the signing and negative feedback from other artists on the label, such as Captain & Tennille. Despite the termination, the Sex Pistols still walked away with 75,000 pounds. "Six days later, ANM announced that they were dropping the band in part due to the flak they were getting from other acts on the label, acts like the captain and tenel." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RN3JIDtPayY
**Skeptical A&R Executive (Paul A. Graham)** : At the time of the "Underdogs" album's release, the head A&R executive at A&M (or Universal), Paul A. Graham, made an office bet that the album would not sell more than 30,000 records. Despite this low expectation, the album's sales significantly surpassed this figure. "There was an office bed at Universal, or at A&M, at the time, Paul A. Graham, who bloody knows what it was then." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-s0gwXY_CZQ
