## curiosities
**Canceled Joint Tour (2000)** : The Rhyme and Reason Tour was a planned 30-date tour in the summer of 2000 featuring Rage Against the Machine and the Beastie Boys. It was intended to carry activist messages and donate a dollar from each ticket to charity. However, the tour was entirely canceled due to Beastie Boys member Mike D dislocating his shoulder in a mountain bike accident. "Rage announced that they were going to do a 30-date tour with the Beastie Boys called the Rhyme and Reason Tour... the whole tour had to be canceled." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t2uXHP9cBdM
**Censorship Allegations by Rage Against the Machine** : NBC, as the broadcaster of Saturday Night Live, was accused by Rage Against the Machine of censorship. This occurred after the band's performance on the show was cut short following their protest against guest host Steve Forbes and rumored plans to denounce NBC's parent company, General Electric, for its role in weapons manufacturing and war crimes. "Rage immediately complained of being censored by GE and NBC." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t2uXHP9cBdM
**Inaugural Festival Organized by Beastie Boys** : Rage Against the Machine supported and performed at the "first Tibetan Freedom Festival," an event organized by the Beastie Boys. This festival was one of many causes Rage championed as part of their political activism. "included the first Tibetan Freedom Festival, which was organized by the Beastie Boys." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t2uXHP9cBdM
**Rage Against the Machine Reunion Performance** : Rage Against the Machine reunited for a performance at the Coachella Music Festival in California in April 2007. This reunion came after significant rumors following Christmas 2006, overcoming past animosity, and was reportedly motivated by the band's desire to oppose the political climate under President George Bush. "rumors began to take hold that Rage would reunite for the Coachella Music Festival in California in April of 2007. And against what everybody thought would happen, it did happen." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t2uXHP9cBdM
**Rage Against the Machine's Early Main Stage Performance** : Rage Against the Machine performed at the 1993 Lollapalooza Festival as one of the first bands on the main stage. Their performance, particularly of "Killing in the Name Of," caused the crowd to "lose its collective mind," establishing them as a powerful new force. "I remember seeing them play the 1993 Lala Palluza Festival. They were one of the first bands on the main stage." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t2uXHP9cBdM
**Rage Against the Machine's Last Show for a Long Time** : Rage Against the Machine recorded a live album at the Olympic Grand Auditorium in Los Angeles on September 13, 2000. This concert proved to be the band's final performance for an extended period, as Zach de la Rocha announced his departure a month later, leading to the band's initial breakup. "The date was September 13th, 2000, and this turned out to be the last ever show Rage would ever do for a long, long time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t2uXHP9cBdM
