**Founding and Global Rollout** : Spotify was founded in 2006 and began its online rollout country by country in 2008. It launched in the US in July 2011 and in Canada in 2014, becoming a major player in the global streaming music market. "Spotify was founded in 2006 and began going online country by country in 2008." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Y6nHw5xx88
**Influence on Song Composition** : The platform's payment structure, which only compensates artists after a song runs for at least 30 seconds, has influenced how music is composed. Artists now prioritize holding a listener's attention during this initial half-minute, leading to shorter intros, front-loaded choruses, and an abundance of "sugary hooks" in the first 30 seconds. "no one gets paid until a song runs for at least 30 seconds." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nX8uAFDzDYM
**Listener Behavior and "Lean Back Listening"** : Streaming has altered music consumption habits, with many using music as background "soundtrack" to fill silence, rather than actively listening. This phenomenon, termed "lean back listening," involves passively letting music come to the listener by simply clicking play, contrasting with "lean forward music," which involves actively seeking out, absorbing, and appreciating music. This shift has impacted how algorithms work, the kind of music composers create, and general attitudes toward the abundant music supply. "We've created a situation where a lot of us are hearing music, but not listening." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nX8uAFDzDYM
**Low Payouts and Artist Compensation** : Spotify, like other streaming platforms, offers significantly lower payouts to artists compared to traditional physical sales. While it provides global reach for a song, the revenue per stream is often equivalent to radio play. This economic model has led to a "thin layer of very successful artists on top" and millions of struggling artists at the bottom, forcing many to rely on alternative income streams like merchandise, touring, and licensing. "Streaming pays out much less than the sales of physical formats." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nX8uAFDzDYM
**Skipping Behavior Data** : Spotify usage data indicates a significant tendency for users to skip songs: 25% of users skip an unfamiliar new song within the first five seconds, another 29% skip within 10 seconds, and 35% are gone by the 30-second mark. This behavior contributes to the devaluation of music as both an art form and a commodity. "According to some Spotify numbers I saw while back, 25% of users will skip an unfamiliar new song in the first five seconds." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nX8uAFDzDYM
