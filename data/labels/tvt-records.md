# label - TVT Records

## curiosities
**Clientele** : TVT Records has since expanded its reach into publishing, with notable clients including Beyonce and Justin Timberlake. "And just so you know, Beyonce and Justin Timberlake are TVT clients." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Conflict with Trent Reznor** : TVT Records, a Chicago-based label, signed Trent Reznor with the expectation of an electronic band similar to Depeche Mode. However, the resulting music from Nine Inch Nails, particularly the album Pretty Hate Machine, was deemed "way too heavy" by label head Steve Gottlieb, who infamously called the album "an abortion," leading to a protracted legal battle that restricted Reznor's recording activities. "Labelhead Steve Gottlieb thought he was getting a depeche mode like electronic band. What he ended up with was Nine Inch Nails." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=itK5fEqnA5E
**Contract Dispute with Trent Reznor** : After the success of "Pretty Hate Machine," TVT demanded a more commercially palatable follow-up. Trent Reznor refused and sought to be released from his contract, but TVT declined, putting him "in limbo for a couple of years." During this time, TVT allowed "Pretty Hate Machine" to go out of print and forbade Trent from recording or releasing new music, threatening to confiscate any new material for its own use. "But when Steve demanded that the follow up record be more commercially palatable, Trent not so respectfully declined and demanded that he be led out of his contract. TVT said, nope. And put Trent in limbo for a couple of years." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DKdF8NgF5Bw
**Expectations vs. Reality with Nine Inch Nails** : Steve Gottlieb signed Trent Reznor, expecting "the next Depeche Mode," a dancing technopop band. However, he "kind of hated" Nine Inch Nails' debut album "Pretty Hate Machine," calling it an "abortion" due to its heavy, dark, loud, and angry music. Despite his dislike, the album was issued and sold half a million copies. "Steve heard Trent's demos and thought he had the next depeche moan on his hands, a dancing technopop band capable of turning out hit singles. But when Trent turned in his record he called it pretty hate machine. He kind of hated it. Actually he called it an abortion quote unquote." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DKdF8NgF5Bw
**Founder and Origin** : Founded by Steve Gottlieb, TVT originally stood for "TVTunes" and initially released compilations of old TV theme songs from shows like "The Jetsons," "The Monsters," and "The Beverly Hillbillies." These compilations sold well enough for Gottlieb to sign "real bands." "Demos were sent out to a bunch of labels with TVT emerging the winner. And this was pretty unusual because TVT stood for TVTunes. This was a label founded by Steve Gottlieb." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DKdF8NgF5Bw
**Origin as TV Theme Label** : TVT Records, which originally stood for "TVTunes," began as a label that focused solely on releasing collections of old TV theme songs. "TVT stood for TVTunes. But in the late 80s, it got into signing artists for original new recordings." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Shift to Original Recordings and Expansion** : In the late 1980s, TVT Records diversified by signing artists for original new recordings. In 1992, the company acquired Wax Trax, and subsequently expanded further through deals with other labels and entering the publishing business. "But in the late 80s, it got into signing artists for original new recordings. Then in 1992, it bought wax tracks. After that, the company expanded by doing deals with other labels and getting into publishing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
