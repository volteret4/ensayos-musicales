# label - Apple

## curiosities
**Acquisition of Sound Jam and iTunes Launch** : In 2000, Apple acquired a product called Sound Jam, which was software designed for CD-ripping and MP3 playback, fulfilling Steve Jobs's ambition for the company to deeply engage with the music sector. This acquisition led to the software being renamed iTunes, which was then officially introduced on January 9, 2001. "So in 2000, the company bought a product called Sound Jam, which was CD-ripping and MP3 playback software." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Zniri9uSlc
**Graphical User Interface Pioneer** : Apple was recognized in the early 1990s for having a graphical user interface (GUI) for its machines, which other companies like Microsoft and IBM sought to surpass. Apple's pioneering work in GUIs set a benchmark for user-friendly computing that was aspired to by competitors. "That meant a graphical user interface that was hopefully better than what Apple had for their machines." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
**Stock Price Surge (2001-2007)** : Before the iTunes music player was revealed on January 1, 2001, Apple's stock price was 39 cents. It remained around 39 cents in 2002 and dropped to 32 cents in 2003 when the iTunes music store went online. However, by 2004, it rose to 116 cents, then 254 cents in 2005, 297 cents by the end of 2006, and 597 cents a year later. The iPod and iTunes ecosystem saved both the music industry and Apple itself, which at one point controlled 70% of all digital music sales worldwide. Today, Apple boasts a market cap of approximately $3.5 trillion. "A January 1st, 2001, about 10 days before the iTunes music player was revealed, Apple stock price was just 39 cents." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nX8uAFDzDYM
**iTunes Music Store Dominance** : Recognizing the music industry's struggle with illegal digital file sharing, Steve Jobs introduced the iTunes Music Store as a legitimate digital download option. It quickly became the dominant platform, controlling 70% of the worldwide market for legitimate digital music downloads, providing a crucial revenue stream for the struggling record labels in the face of cratering CD sales. "His iTunes music store, and it wasn't long before iTunes controlled 70% of the worldwide market for legitimate digital downloads." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
**iTunes Music Store and iPod Launch (2003)** : Apple, under Steve Jobs, emerged as the savior of the recorded music industry by introducing the iTunes music store in April 2003 (initially Mac-only, then Windows in October). This followed the earlier release of the iTunes media player and the iPod, which was marketed as holding "a thousand songs in your pocket." Jobs convinced the five major record labels in 2002 to license their music, selling songs for 99 cents with Apple taking 30%. This move changed everything, leading to the iPod becoming the fastest-selling consumer electronic product and providing the first effective solution to music piracy by offering legal, clean digital tracks. "the iTunes music store was introduced in April 2003." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nX8uAFDzDYM
