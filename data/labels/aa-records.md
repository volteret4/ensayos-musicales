# label - A&A Records

## curiosities
**Bankruptcy (1993)** : The company's strategy of rapid expansion ultimately proved unsustainable, leading to its bankruptcy in 1993. "But rapid expansion hurts and in 1993 the company went bankrupt." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
**Canadian Dominance and Flagship (Peak)** : At its zenith, A&A Records was the largest record chain in Canada, operating 260 shops. For many years, its flagship store was strategically located on Yonge Street, adjacent to Sam the Record Man. "At their peak there were 260 ANA records shops and for years their flagship was right next door to Sam's on Young Street." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
**Founding and Naming (Post World War II)** : A&A Records was founded at the conclusion of World War II by Alice and Aaron Kenner, whose initials formed the company's name, along with Alice's husband, Mac. "ANA Records was founded at the end of World War II by Alice and Aaron Kenner, they're the ANA in the name, along with Alice's husband Mac." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
