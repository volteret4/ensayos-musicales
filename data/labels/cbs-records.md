# label - CBS Records

## curiosities
**CBS Columbia System** : CBS Records developed and offered its own distinct stereo system, known as the Columbia system. This proprietary solution was one of several attempts by record companies to establish a viable method for stereo record production. "CBS records came up with their Columbia system." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cbSmBrhNACg
**The Clash Signing** : CBS Records signed The Clash to a record deal in 1977. "In 1977, the clash managed to sign a deal with CBS records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gKrAXMG8sTc
**The Clash Signing Controversy (1977)** : CBS Records signed The Clash to a worldwide recording contract for 100,000 pounds on January 25, 1977, amidst a fierce bidding war. This deal was widely condemned by the punk community as a betrayal of the genre's anti-establishment ethos, with one fanzine even declaring "punk died the day the clash signed to CBS." "And on January 25, 1977, at the very height of punk rock in the UK, they signed a worldwide recording contract with CBS records for 100,000 pounds." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DYtsWfguqGo
**Worldwide CD Launch (March 1983)** : CBS Records participated in the big worldwide launch of the compact disc format in March 1983, releasing 16 albums on the new format. This included major titles like Michael Jackson's "Thriller," helping to establish the CD in the market. "Then came the big worldwide launch in March of 1983, when CBS records released 16 albums on the new format, including Thriller from Michael Jackson." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BHkJFA0LMro
