# label - Record Labels

## curiosities
**Control of the Music Industry - 1990s** : In the 1990s, a handful of record labels controlled the entire music industry, which was described as a "crystal palace." Unlike today's easy access to music online, music then was a physical object, primarily CDs. These labels held the "keys to the kingdom" and were unwilling to relinquish their control. **Crisis due to Piracy - 2000s** : In parallel with the ringtone boom, the record industry faced its biggest crisis due to widespread piracy via platforms like Napster, Emule, and Kazaa. Instead of adapting to the changing landscape, labels responded with lawsuits, restrictions, and moralistic campaigns claiming "piracy killed music," clinging to an outdated model with abusive pricing.
