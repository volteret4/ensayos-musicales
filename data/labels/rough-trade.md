# label - Rough Trade

## curiosities
**Early Supporter of DIY Electronic Music** : Rough Trade record shop in Notting Hill played a crucial role in the early Technopop scene by agreeing to stock Daniel Miller's self-produced "Warm Leatherette" single, which was recorded with rudimentary gear in his bedroom. This support helped the minimalist, futuristic record sell 30,000 copies, inspiring many aspiring musicians. "He started experimenting with it in his bedroom. Recorded two songs, found some money to make a test pressing and convinced the rough-trade record shop in Notting Hill to stock it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
**Home to The Smiths** : Rough Trade served as the UK home label for The Smiths, widely considered the biggest indie band of their era and one whose influences continue to be discussed. "As this tape was getting people excited about the possibilities of new music, rough trade was home to the biggest indie band of the era, whose influences are still being talked about today." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Pioneering Independent Label** : Rough Trade was among the small independent labels that emerged in the post-punk era, specializing in operating outside the major label system and supporting independent artists. "that included rough trade for a D, beggars banquet, epitaph, SST, and many others." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4
**Prominent on C86 Cassette** : Rough Trade was one of the indie labels whose artists were tapped to be featured on the influential C86 cassette, a compilation that reflected the state of new music in Britain in 1986. "They tapped labels like Creation and Rough Tray and a bunch of others." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Recommended Record Store in London** : Rough Trade is recommended as a must-visit record store in London, with specific locations mentioned in Notting Hill and the East End store in Brick Lane. "I never go to London without visiting Rough Trade and Notting Hill or the East End store in Brick Lane." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
