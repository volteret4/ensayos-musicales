# label - Grundig

## curiosities
**Cassette Format Competition (Early 1960s)** : Grundig was one of the companies that pushed their own versions of cassette technology in the early 1960s, competing with Philips' compact cassette design. This initial competition made the compact cassette's success less immediate. `"But Ottens' cassette wasn't a slam dunk success, as other companies like Grundig and Telefunken pushed their versions of the technology."` ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OuFvfZJKcpc
**Rejection of Personal Music Player (1972)** : In 1972, Grundig, a major manufacturer of consumer electronics, was among several companies that rejected Andreas Pavel's invention, the Stereo Belt. They believed that no one would buy a portable machine designed for listening to music with headphones. "He took his invention to a number of people. Gamahaw, Phillips, Grundig, all big manufacturers of consumer electronics." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
