# label - BMG

## curiosities
**Acquisition by Sony** : In 2008, BMG itself was acquired by Sony, leading to the formation of a new entity initially called Sony BMG, and later Sony Music Entertainment. "But then in 2008, BMG was bought by Sony, creating a new company called Sony BMG." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JJGJXOoT2PI
**Acquisition of Zamba** : In 2002, BMG acquired Zamba, which meant that Volcano (and all its artists, including Tool) effectively returned to BMG's ownership, placing Tool back under the company that had owned their initial label, Zoo Entertainment. "In 2002, Zamba was bought by BMG, which returned Volcano, and all the other artists on the roster, back to the company that had owned it when it was still called Zoo Entertainment." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JJGJXOoT2PI
**Intervention in Zoo's Financial Troubles** : In 1995, BMG, as the parent label of Zoo Entertainment, intervened due to Zoo's significant financial issues, leading to a layoff of many staff members. "In 1995, parent label BMG swooped in and laid off a bunch of people." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JJGJXOoT2PI
**MusicNet (Failed Online Music Store)** : BMG was one of the labels involved with "MusicNet," an early online music store created by major labels in an attempt to sell digital files. Similar to its competitor, this service was difficult to use, featured restrictions, and was loaded with complicated digital rights management (DRM) safeguards. It hung on longer than its competitor but was eventually sold off for parts in 2005. "MusicNet, which handled BMG, EMI and Warner." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nX8uAFDzDYM
**Parent Company of Private Music** : BMG was the parent company of Private Music, a Los Angeles-based subsidiary with whom The Matthew Good Band signed a record deal for "Underdogs." "Well, Underdogs, I signed a record deal with Private Music, which was a BMG subsidiary in Los Angeles." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-s0gwXY_CZQ
**Sloan's Canadian Distributor (2001)** : In 2001, BMG served as Sloan's distributor in Canada. The band reported "doing alright" during this period, which coincided with the release of their album "Pretty Together" and two successful music videos. "2001, we're on BMG in Canada, and we're doing alright." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QB3nyFH_tRU
**Standalone Major Label** : At the time Tool first signed with Zoo Entertainment, BMG existed as a standalone major label, with Zoo affiliated with it. "They were affiliated with BMG, which still existed at the time as a standalone major label." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JJGJXOoT2PI
