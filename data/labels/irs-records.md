## curiosities

**Challenges for Indie Labels and Bands** : Most indie labels often faced difficulties in consistently getting music into stores, and even when they did, bands had trouble getting paid. This financial instability and limited distribution often necessitated constant touring for bands like R.E.M. to gain exposure and make ends meet. "Most indie labels have a very hard time getting music into the stores on a consistent and regular basis." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AymMybE33zU
**Dread Zeppelin Signing** : IRS Records signed Dread Zeppelin, and was noted as R.E.M.'s old label. "They were then signed to IRS Records, REM's old label." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9_3EbwKPnU
**Indie Label and R.E.M.'s Growth** : IRS Records was a larger indie label run by Miles Copeland, brother of Police drummer Stuart Copeland. R.E.M. released five albums on IRS between 1982 and 1985, growing to be one of North America's most respected alternative bands and pioneers of the college rock scene. However, by 1987, R.E.M. outgrew IRS's distribution and support capabilities, leading to their departure for a major label. "IRS distribution, their ability to get R.E.M. records into record stores, had gone as far as it could, and it was time to move on to someone who could get the records out there worldwide." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AymMybE33zU
**Interest in Hassel Adkins** : Miles Copeland, the head of IRS Records and brother of Stuart Copeland of The Police, expressed interest in producing an album with Hassel Adkins, although the project ultimately did not materialize. "Miles Copeland, the head of IRS records, brother of Stuart Copeland of the police, wanted to do an album with him, but it never worked out." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HcK4M7-02ik
**Leonard Cohen's Relaunch** : In 1988, IRS Records made the decision to launch or relaunch Leonard Cohen's career by marketing him as an alternative artist, coinciding with the release of his album *I'm Your Man*. "For some reason, IRS records had decided to launch or relaunch Leonard Cohen's career as an alternative artist." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4xxgPX-aR90
**R.E.M. Distribution Limitations** : R.E.M. released five albums through IRS Records between 1982 and 1985, growing into respected indie leaders. However, by 1987, the band felt IRS's distribution capabilities had reached their limit, prompting R.E.M. to seek a major label deal with Warner Brothers to achieve worldwide reach for their music.
