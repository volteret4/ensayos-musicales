# label - Moog

## curiosities
**Accidental Success of "Wonky Oscillators"** : The Mini-Moog was not initially perfect, struggling with tuning stability and holding settings due to its three oscillators failing to sync properly. Paradoxically, these "wonky oscillators" allowed the Mini-Moog to produce wildly unconventional and desirable sounds. "It wasn't very reliable when it came to staying in tune and holding settings because engineers couldn't get the things three oscillators, the devices that controlled voltage in the sound to sync up properly." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Creation of the Mini-Moog** : Faced with the potential demise of the company due to the high cost and impracticality of modular synthesizers, Moog engineers developed the Mini-Moog, a tiny, self-contained synth introduced in 1970. This was a crucial move to create an affordable and portable analog synthesizer. "So the engineers created a tiny self-contained synth." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Integration of the Pitch Wheel** : The Mini-Moog was equipped with a pitch wheel, a novel feature that enabled players to bend notes in a manner similar to how a guitarist bends strings. This innovation greatly expanded the expressive capabilities of the synthesizer. "It also came with a thing called a pitch wheel, which allowed players to bend notes like a guitarist bend strings." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Unexpected Commercial Success** : Bob Moog had low expectations for the Mini-Moog, predicting sales of only about 100 units. However, once musicians discovered its unique, "otherworldly sounds," sales soared into the thousands, with at least 12,000 units sold between 1970 and 1981, making it the first classic synthesizer. "Mog had no hope for the Mini-Mog, thinking that the company might sell 100 of them. But once they were discovered by musicians looking for new, otherworldly sounds, Mini-Mogs started selling by the thousands." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
