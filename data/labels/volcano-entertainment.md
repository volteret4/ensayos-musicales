# label - Volcano Entertainment

## curiosities
**Acquisition by Zamba** : In the spring of 1998, Volcano was bought by Zamba. This was part of a broader corporate restructuring that eventually led to Q Prime acquiring a stake in the new Volcano, intending to provide a stable home for Tool. "In the spring of 1998, Zoo was bought by Zamba, whereupon Zoo's name was changed to Volcano." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JJGJXOoT2PI
**Acquisition of Zoo & Sony Patronage** : Volcano Entertainment acquired Zoo Entertainment in 1996, becoming the new home for Tool. Volcano's major label patron was Sony Records. For a period, Zoo and Volcano co-existed as a partnership, with Zoo retaining custody of Tool's second album, *Anima*. "Volcano Entertainment in 1996." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JJGJXOoT2PI
**Confusing Name Changes** : Volcano underwent a series of confusing name changes, first to Free World, then changing back to Zoo Entertainment, and then simply Zoo, contributing to significant confusion and legal complications for Tool. "Volcano had changed its name to Free World." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JJGJXOoT2PI
**Contract & Royalty Disputes with Tool** : Volcano, or Zoo (which became more or less the same entity), was central to Tool's legal troubles. They were criticized for being slow with royalty checks for the successful *Anima* album and, critically, "forgot to pick up the option" on Tool's contract, leading to a lawsuit against the band for breach of contract, and a counter-suit from Tool. "Tool's label Volcano, or Zoo, which more or less the same thing, is suing the band for bailing on their contract." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JJGJXOoT2PI
**Formation of Volcano 2 & Dissectional** : After Q Prime acquired a 50% stake in the new Volcano (from Zamba) to provide Tool with a stable label environment and resolve ongoing lawsuits, a new company called Volcano 2 was formed. This structure included giving Tool its own sub-label, Dissectional, which was set up as a joint company to ensure Tool had a direct say in its operations and prevent future disputes. "This new company, now called Volcano 2, was structured to give Tool its own sub-label, which they called dissectional." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JJGJXOoT2PI
**Return to BMG Ownership** : In 2002, Volcano's parent company, Zamba, was acquired by BMG. This meant that Volcano, along with all its artists (including Tool), effectively returned to the ownership of BMG, the company that had previously owned Zoo Entertainment, Tool's original label. "In 2002, Zamba was bought by BMG, which returned Volcano, and all the other artists on the roster, back to the company that had owned it when it was still called Zoo Entertainment." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JJGJXOoT2PI
