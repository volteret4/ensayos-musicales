**Catalog Acquisition Company** : Hypnosis Song Fund was mentioned as one of the companies involved in the "gold rush" for artists' music catalogs in the late 2010s. These companies acquired music rights for long-term lucrative propositions, offering artists upfront financial security and tax advantages. "They had names like Primary Wave, Hypnosis Song Fund, Roundheal, Concord, and Half a Does Another's." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GYUuTbOvyak
**Founder and Mission** : The Hypnosis Song Fund, founded by Quebec-born former record plugger Merck Mercuriatis, is one of the largest and best-capitalized companies acquiring song catalogs. Its mission is to establish songs as an asset class for institutional investors and the stock market, emphasizing their predictable and reliable income stream, which makes them investible like gold or oil. "One of the biggest and best capitalized companies is the Hypnosis song fund, which was started by a Quebec-born former record plugger named Merck Mercuriatis." "what I wanted to do was to establish songs as an asset class and for institutional investors and for the stock market to understand that when these songs become successful, you know, they become a part of the fabric of people's lives, they become a part of the fabric of our society, and therefore they have very predictable and reliable income, and that predictable reliable income is investible." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pe40SqDrpZ0
**Investment Horizon (Copyright Law)** : Hypnosis calculates its income stream based on copyright law, which in Canada, is 70 years after the death of the last co-composer, resulting in an average income stream duration of about 101 years for their acquisitions. "It's 70 years after the death of the last co-composer. So we've paid an average 15 times multiple for our songs. And we have an income stream that's going to last for about 101 years." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pe40SqDrpZ0
**Song Management Approach** : Hypnosis aims to "unlock and exploit" the value in acquired songs through active "song management," contrasting with older publishing models where large companies had too many songs to effectively promote. This involves licensing for streams, sales, radio airplay, sync placements (movies, TV, commercials, video games), samples, and interpolations, and celebrating the artists' legacies. "My job has always been to be an artist manager. I can't play the guitar. I can't sing a song. What I bring to the table is responsibility. And now I'm putting the same responsibility that I used to put into managing people like Alton John or Guns n' Roses into managing great songs like Sweet Dreams were made of this." "You're going to be your whole thing now is to unlock and exploit the value in these songs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pe40SqDrpZ0
**Total Investment and Songs Acquired (As of Interview)** : As of the interview, Hypnosis had spent $3 billion US dollars to acquire the rights to 60,000 songs. **Valuation Methodologies** : Hypnosis evaluates catalogs based on a multiple of annual earnings (average 15 times multiple paid) and return on investment (ROI) or internal rate of return (IRR). Crucially, performance in the streaming world, specifically monthly listeners on platforms like Spotify, significantly impacts a catalog's value and predictability. "Sometimes people look at it as a multiple of annual earnings. When we look at it from the point of view of what's the ROI, what's the return on investment going to be, or the IRR, depending on which terminology you'd like to use." "The most important value factor besides the predictability and reliability is how is your song, how is your set of songs performing in a streaming world relative to the rest of the market. If you've got 70 million monthly listeners on Spotify, your catalog is going to be worth a lot more than if you've got 10 million monthly listeners on Spotify." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pe40SqDrpZ0
