# label - Vevo

## curiosities
**Destruction of MySpace Music** : Vevo proved highly successful, significantly impacting the online music landscape by "destroying MySpace Music," which until 2009 had been the most visited music site in the US. This highlights Vevo's effectiveness in consolidating major label content and attracting users. "Vivo destroyed my space music, which up until 2009 was the most visited music site in the US." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7IDbqdIw3WU
**Major Label Joint Venture (2009)** : Vevo was launched on December 8, 2009, as a joint venture created by the four major record labels at the time: Universal, Sony, Warner, and EMI. Its concept was to provide a platform dedicated exclusively to music videos, controlled entirely by these labels, with the goal of offering premium content and attracting high-value advertisers. "Vivo, a joint venture between the four major record labels at the time, Universal, Sony, Warner and EMI." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7IDbqdIw3WU
**Partnerships and Dual Role** : Vevo established partnerships with MTV Networks and YouTube, including its own secure channel on YouTube. It functioned both as a destination for online videos and a supplier of branded music videos to its partners, meaning fans could watch Vevo-branded videos on YouTube, MTV, or other platforms. "Vivo was both a place to go to watch online videos and a supplier of videos to partners." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7IDbqdIw3WU
**Valuable Audience Metrics** : Both labels and artists highly valued Vevo because of the detailed audience metrics it provided. This data allowed for sophisticated marketing strategies, turning into a competitive battle to see which artist could achieve the most views on Vevo within a 24-hour period. "And both labels and artists loved it because of the audience metrics that could be studied." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7IDbqdIw3WU
**Vevo TV Launch (2013)** : In 2013, Vevo TV was launched as a 24-hour internet channel. It featured continuous blocks of music videos and various music specials, aiming to provide a curated, linear viewing experience similar to traditional music video channels, but online. "And then in 2013, Vivo TV was launched. This was a 24 hour internet channel running blocks of music videos and various music specials." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7IDbqdIw3WU
