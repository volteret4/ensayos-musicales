**Rio PMP 300 Launch and Impact (1998)** : Diamond Multimedia introduced the Rio PMP 300, a portable MP3 player, around the same time Iger Labs began importing the MP3 man into the US. The Rio PMP 300 was a significant sales success during Christmas 1998, prompting considerable alarm from the American recorded music industry, which argued that the device promoted illegal copying and digital theft. "This happened at about the same time a company called Iger Labs started importing the MP3 man and rebranding it for sale in the US. This happened at about the same time a company called Diamond Multimedia started selling the Rio PMP 300. It was a big hit over Christmas 1998, which scared the hell out of the American recorded music industry. **Rio PMP 300 Legal Victory** : Diamond Multimedia faced legal challenges from the music industry but ultimately prevailed in court. Its victory was anchored in a 1976 US Supreme Court ruling that had legalized the sale of Sony VCRs, establishing that devices enabling private copying of material for personal use were lawful. "And the whole thing went to court. But Diamond Multimedia won the case based on a 1976 US Supreme Court ruling that made it okay for Sony to sell VCRs. That decision said that such devices were legal because they allowed for private copying of material, copying of material for private use." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fhq9WVeiE_U
**Rio PMP 300 Sales Figures** : Despite its legal triumph and initial popularity, the Diamond Rio PMP 300 sold approximately 200,000 units. However, the legal precedent established by its court case proved vital, paving the way for the broader development and sale of other portable MP3 players.
