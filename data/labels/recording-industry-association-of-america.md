## curiosities
**Limewire Copyright Lawsuit (2006-2011)** : The Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA) initiated legal action against the file-sharing site Limewire and its creator, Martin Gordon, in 2006. In 2010, a judge ruled that Limewire and Gordon had infringed upon millions of copyrights, leading to an immediate shutdown order for the service. In 2011, the RIAA, alongside 13 record companies, filed a staggering lawsuit for $75 trillion US dollars, a figure based on infringements of just 11,000 songs, which at the time exceeded the entire global GDP. Though no one possesses such an amount, Gordon ultimately settled for $105 million. This lawsuit, along with others against file-sharing programs like Grokster ($50 million settlement) and another unnamed service ($100 million settlement), marked a significant effort by the music industry to combat piracy.
