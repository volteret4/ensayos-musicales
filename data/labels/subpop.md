# label - Subpop

## curiosities
**Cohesion for Seattle Underground** : Despite being "terminally broke," Subpop provided a crucial sense of cohesion for the Seattle underground music scene, offering local acts a legitimate way to release material without compromising their punk credentials. "Although Subpop was terminally broke, the label nevertheless offered the Seattle Underground some kind of cohesion. Local acts now had a way of releasing material without jeopardizing their hard-earned punk credentials." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xdn31jMOOA4
**Cool Label Status (1988)** : By mid-1988, Subpop was recognized as a "pretty cool little label," boasting a roster that included local groups like Soundgarden, Screaming Trees, and Mudhoney. "The middle of 1988, Subpop was considered to be a pretty cool little label. The roster featured local groups like Soundgarden, Screaming Trees and Mud Honey." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xdn31jMOOA4
**Early Strategy and Studio Deal** : Subpop established a deal with a local recording studio, arranging for any "cool or interesting" bands passing through to be referred to the label. This helped them identify and sign emerging talent in the Seattle scene. "So he called up his dad and he said, Dad, can I have $20,000 to start an indie record label? He called his label Subpop and worked out a deal with the local recording studio. If anyone cool or interesting pass through the studio, they were to be referred to Subpop." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xdn31jMOOA4
**Financially Strapped Status** : Subpop was financially strapped before Nirvana's eventual success helped to save the label. "Not only would Nirvana eventually be the savior of the financially strapped Subpop label, but they would also become the focus of a worldwide interest in grunge." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**First Release: Subpop 100 (1986)** : The label's inaugural release was a compilation titled "Subpop 100," issued in July 1986. "The first thing the label did was release a collection called the Subpop 100 in July of 1986." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xdn31jMOOA4
**Formation and Early Strategy** : Bruce Pabbitt, inspired by the "Deep Six" compilation, borrowed $20,000 from his father to form Subpop. The company worked out a deal with Reciprocal Recordings, a local studio, to refer any cool or interesting bands to Subpop. "The borrowing $20,000 from his father, Pabbitt formed a company he called Subpop and worked out a deal with reciprocal recordings, a local recording studio." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Founding by Bruce Pavitt** : Subpop was founded by Bruce Pavitt, a Musak employee and local music enthusiast. Inspired by the "Deep Six" compilation, he secured $20,000 from his father to start the label. "So he called up his dad and he said, Dad, can I have $20,000 to start an indie record label? He called his label Subpop." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xdn31jMOOA4
**Home to The Thermals** : Subpop is mentioned as the record label for the band The Thermals. "It's a band on Subpop called The Thermals." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vJszsJQpINQ
**Hype Strategy for Grunge Bands - Early 1989** : In early 1989, co-owners Jonathan Poe and Bruce Pabbitt, desperate to hype their acts like Nirvana, decided to emulate Jimi Hendrix's path to success by breaking bands in the UK first. Despite having no money, they risked flying a Melody Maker writer to Seattle to generate British press. "the two owners of the Subpop record label Jonathan Poe and Men and Bruce Pabbitt were desperate to hype their acts." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DSvoppf1dGY
**Indie Distribution Challenges** : Subpop faced significant challenges with distribution and collecting money from sales, a common issue that has led to the downfall of hundreds of indie bands. Nirvana experienced these difficulties firsthand while recording for Subpop. "Nirvana went through it when they were recording for Subpop." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ft8nruhWCas
**Nirvana's Departure** : Although Subpop had high hopes for Nirvana, the label was too financially constrained to meet the band's needs. This financial limitation ultimately led to Nirvana signing with DGC Records on January 4, 1991. "Now of course, Subpop had high hopes for Nirvana, but they were too broke to keep the band happy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xdn31jMOOA4
**Partnership with Jonathan Poneman** : Bruce Pavitt entered into a partnership with Jonathan Poneman, a concert promoter and DJ at college radio station KAOS, solidifying Subpop's operational structure. "After that, Pavitt entered into a partnership with Jonathan Pohnman, a concert promoter and sometime DJed, a nearby college radio station called K-A-O-S or Chaos." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xdn31jMOOA4
**Singles Club Innovation** : By mid-1988, Subpop was a "pretty cool little label." To enhance its profile, the company launched the Subpop Singles Club, offering subscribers a new, limited-edition indie single by mail every month for an annual fee. The very first single, released in November 1988, was from Nirvana. "And as an added touch, the company started the Subpop Singles Club. For an annual fee, subscribers received a brand new limited edition indie single in the mail every single month." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Subpop 100 Compilation (July 1986)** : In July 1986, Subpop released a collection titled "Subpop 100," subtitled "To K-Tell with Love." Although it only featured two local acts, it marked a significant beginning for the label. "Enough bands eventually submitted tapes by July of 1986 for the company to release a collection called the Subpop 100, subtitled To K-Tell with Love. And although the album only featured two local acts, it was at least a start." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Subpop Singles Club (1988)** : To enhance its appeal, Subpop launched the "Subpop Singles Club," which, for an annual fee, provided subscribers with a new limited edition seven-inch single every month. Nirvana's very first single was the inaugural release in November 1988. "And as an added touch, the company started the Subpop singles club for an annual fee. Subscribers received a brand new limited edition seven inch single in the mail every single month." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xdn31jMOOA4
