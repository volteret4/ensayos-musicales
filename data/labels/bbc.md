# label - BBC

## curiosities
**"Needle Time" Restriction (Introduced Year not specified, removed 1988)** : The BBC implemented "Needle Time," a deal with the musician's union that restricted the amount of commercially recorded music it could play during any 24-hour period. This policy, which ensured the BBC would play either live music or recordings made in its own studios, was in effect until 1988, and as recently as 1967, only allowed five hours a day of commercial records. "For example, this marked the beginning of something called Needle Time with the BBC, which was a deal with the musician's union. It restricted the BBC to playing only a certain amount of recorded music during any 24-hour period, thereby ensuring that the BBC would play either live music or recordings the BBC made themselves in their studios or elsewhere." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ak6OuOUkRrQ
**"Space Oddity" as Moon Landing Theme** : The BBC, along with other broadcasters, picked up David Bowie's "Space Oddity" as an unofficial theme for the Apollo 11 moon landing on July 20th, 1969. This helped the song gain widespread attention and contributed to its status as Bowie's breakthrough hit. "Broadcasters everywhere, including the BBC, picked it up as an unofficial theme to the moon landing on July 20th." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
**"Tomorrow's World" CD Demonstration (Year not specified)** : The BBC show *Tomorrow's World* hosted the first public demonstration of a compact disc, featuring the Bee Gees' album *Living Eyes*. This event introduced the new digital format to a public audience. "The first CD to receive a public demonstration was living eyes by the Bee Gees on a BBC show called Tomorrow's World." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ak6OuOUkRrQ
**Amazing Archive of Recordings (Decades long)** : As a direct consequence of "Needle Time" restrictions, the BBC developed an "amazing archive of recordings" that spans decades. To get around these restrictions, the BBC invited thousands of artists, including The Beatles, Bob Marley, Joy Division, and Tears for Fears, to make records in their studios or elsewhere. "This is why the BBC has such an amazing archive of recordings that go back decades." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ak6OuOUkRrQ
**Extensive Vinyl Collection** : The BBC possesses an extremely large vinyl collection, with its record shelves extending for an impressive 66 miles, which is equivalent to 100 kilometers. "Someone measured the shelving the BBC has for its records. And those shelves extend for sixty six miles, a hundred kilometers of shelves filled with records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HoBx6s3JmVQ
**Nine Inch Nails Miniseries Partnership** : The BBC is collaborating with HBO to develop a sci-fi miniseries based on Nine Inch Nails' concept album *Year Zero*. This project involves significant financial investment and major industry players. "Year Zero is so compelling that HBO and the BBC are working together to create a sci-fi miniseries based on the record." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KyNY3zv6uc
