# label - Mercury Records

## curiosities
**Hip-Pocket Records Releases (1966)** : In 1966, Mercury Records participated in the Hip-Pocket Records initiative with Filco and Ford, releasing singles on the bendable plastic Flexi Disc format. These releases, featuring artists like Tommy James and Neil Diamond, were sold for $0.69 through Ford dealerships and Woolworth stores. `"They got Atlantic, Mercury, and Roulette records to release singles this way, selling them for $0.69 at four dealerships and through Woolworth stores."` ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OuFvfZJKcpc
**Major Label Signing of Mother Love Bone** : Mercury Records was the major label that signed Mother Love Bone, signaling early industry interest in bands emerging from the Pacific Northwest grunge scene. "Mother Love Bone nailed a deal with Mercury records..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tFBSrKMEZxI
