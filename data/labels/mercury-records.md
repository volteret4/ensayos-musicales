# label - Mercury Records

## curiosities
**David Bowie's Introduction to Norm O'Dam** : In 1970, upon signing with Mercury Records, David Bowie received a collection of 7-inch singles as a welcome gift, which included Norm O'Dam's "Paralyzed." This introduction sparked Bowie's obsession with the record and artist, ultimately inspiring his "Stardust" persona. "When Bowie signed to Mercury in 1970, he was given a stack of seven-inch singles as kind of a welcome to the label gift." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
**Hip-Pocket Records Releases (1966)** : In 1966, Mercury Records participated in the Hip-Pocket Records initiative with Filco and Ford, releasing singles on the bendable plastic Flexi Disc format. These releases, featuring artists like Tommy James and Neil Diamond, were sold for $0.69 through Ford dealerships and Woolworth stores. `"They got Atlantic, Mercury, and Roulette records to release singles this way, selling them for $0.69 at four dealerships and through Woolworth stores."` ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OuFvfZJKcpc
**Major Label Signing of Mother Love Bone** : Mercury Records was the major label that signed Mother Love Bone, signaling early industry interest in bands emerging from the Pacific Northwest grunge scene. "Mother Love Bone nailed a deal with Mercury records..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tFBSrKMEZxI
**Norm O'Dam's Single Release** : Mercury Records released Norm O'Dam's single "Paralyzed" in 1968. This unusual track, whose meaning was unclear to many at the time, later gained significant historical importance when it profoundly influenced David Bowie. "He did, however, manage to release a single for Mercury Records in 1968 called Paralyzed." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
