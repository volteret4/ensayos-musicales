# label - IBM

## curiosities
**Early Operating System Rivalry (OS/2)** : IBM's OS/2 operating system was a competitor to Microsoft Windows 3.1 in the early 1990s, engaged in a "real dogfight" to develop an easier graphical user interface for personal computers. This competition, while not ultimately leading to OS/2's dominance, was part of the technological landscape that pushed for more user-friendly computing, which indirectly facilitated future music technology developments. "IBM had an operating system called OS2." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
**Employment of Dieter Sitzer** : Professor Dieter Sitzer, a German electrical engineer whose research focused on data reproduction for sound and images, worked for IBM in Switzerland. His foundational work, particularly in seeking better telephone transmission, contributed indirectly to the concepts of audio compression later developed by his PhD student, Carl Heinz Brandenburg.
