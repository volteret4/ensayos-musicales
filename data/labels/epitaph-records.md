# label - Epitaph Records

## curiosities
**Formation by Bad Religion** : Epitaph Records was formed by the members of Bad Religion in 1980 to release their debut EP after no one else would take a chance on their music. This demonstrates the prevailing DIY ethic in early hardcore. "When no one would release their debut EP, they formed their own indie label, and they called it Epitaph." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0qigzi1j81U
**Founded by Bad Religion** : Epitaph Records is an independent label that was founded by members of Bad Religion, playing a crucial role in the indie punk scene. "That led to a second deal with Epitaph, the indie label final by bad religion." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0QtMI00T5jw
**Founding (1980)** : Epitaph Records was founded in 1980 by a band (implied to be Bad Religion), becoming a significant independent label in the punk rock scene. "the band that founded their own label, Epitaph Records, in 1980." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q4dEV1yn3OY
**Funding for Offspring Video** : Epitaph Records invested $5,000 into producing a quick music video for The Offspring's "Come Out and Play" after the song started receiving massive radio attention. "Then MTV called, where's the video? So Epitaph sunk $5,000 into making a quickie clip." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0QtMI00T5jw
**Global Importance and Major Success** : Epitaph later grew to become "one of the most important indie labels in the entire world." Its significance was cemented when it successfully "took a chance on a band called The Offspring," leading to massive commercial success. "Epitaph, of course, would later become one of the most important indie labels in the entire world. They were the ones who took a chance on a band called The Offspring, and I think we all know how that story turned out." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0qigzi1j81U
**Home of the Biggest Selling Indie Record** : The Offspring's album "Smash," released on Epitaph, sold over 18 million copies, earning it the title of "the biggest selling indie record of all time" at that particular juncture. "making it the biggest selling indie record of all time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Important Indie Label of the Era** : Epitaph Records was recognized as one of the most important independent labels during the era, notably releasing records for bands like Bad Religion, Rancid, and NOFX. "Their label, Epitaph, was one of the most important indie labels of the era." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0QtMI00T5jw
**Independent Label for Punk Bands** : Epitaph Records served as an independent label where bands such as Bad Religion and Rancid recorded their music. The Offspring also began their career on this label. "their own independent epitaph records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Purist Backlash Against Label Departure** : Punk purists considered it "blasphemy and sacrilege" when bands like Bad Religion chose to leave their "own indie imprint, epitaph," to sign with major record labels. This highlighted the conflict between indie punk ideals and mainstream commercialism. "And bad religion, with a major record label leaving their own indie imprint, epitaph, behind, blasphemy and sacrilege." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Relationship with Anti-Records** : Epitaph Records is described as a "sister label" to Anti-Records. "anti-records, which is a sister label to epitaph records..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AcADOuQUiAQ
**Released Hurley Album** : Weezer's "Hurley" album was the only Weezer album to be released on Epitaph Records. "The only Weezer album to be released on Epitaph records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LYRPxtP61JM
**Roll the Tanks Album Release** : Epitaph Records is the label that released Roll the Tanks' 2014 album, "Broke Till Midnight." "That's from a 2014 album entitled, Broke Till Midnight, band on the epitaph label called, Roll the Tanks, and they're singing about record players." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TsiYLGGSJ2U
**The Offspring's Breakthrough Releases** : The Offspring released their third, explosively successful, indie record on Epitaph Records on April 8, 1994, selling 11 million copies globally and solidifying the label's crucial role in the mid-90s punk revival. "After two indie albums, they released a third indie record on April 8, 1994. It exploded, selling no fewer than 11 million copies around the world. Off-Spring, back when they were a pure indie band recording for Epitaph Records, they were a major, major part of the mid-90s punk rock revival." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q4dEV1yn3OY
