# label - 4AD

## curiosities
**Adventurous UK Indie Label** : 4AD is described as an adventurous indie label based out of the UK. It was the label that first picked up The Pixies when they couldn't find an American label interested in their experimental noise. "As they couldn't find an American label interested in their brand of experimental noise, the group was first picked up by 4AD, that adventurous indie label out of the UK." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Avant-Garde Focus** : 4AD was initially established as a platform to test out more avant-garde acts, with the intention that successful ones would "graduate" to Beggars Banquet, the larger parent label. However, this model didn't fully materialize. "4AD was established as a place where the more avant-garde acts could be tested out, if they did well, while then they would graduate up to Bigger's, the Big Dog in the Office." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Cult Following and Reputation** : Instead of being a stepping stone, 4AD developed a substantial following of fans who valued the label's distinct "weirdness." Its reputation grew to such an extent that people would purchase records simply because they were on 4AD, often without prior knowledge of the band. This fostered a cult-like community around the label. "Instead, 4AD found a sizeable number of fans who appreciated the label's weirdness. Its reputation got to the point where people bought records because they were on 4AD, and not necessarily because they'd heard of the band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Greatest Contribution: MARRS** : 4AD's most significant contribution to the evolution of music is considered to be the studio project MARRS and its influential single "Pump Up The Volume." "But 4AD's greatest contribution to the evolution of music may have been a studio project called Mars." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Key Artists** : The label's roster included influential groups such as Throwing Muses, This Mortal Coil, Dead Can Dance, The Birthday Party, Modern English, and Da-Da, along with the Pixies. "There were groups like Throwing Muses and This Mortal Coil, Dead Condense in the Birthday Party, Modern English, and Da-Da." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Pioneering Independent Label** : 4AD was a small independent label that specialized in working outside the major label system. It notably signed the Scottish Dreampop band Cocteau Twins in 1979. "One of the earliest examples of Dreampop was Scotland's Cocktoe twins, who arrived from the scene in 1979, signing to the indie label 4AD." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4
