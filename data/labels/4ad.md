# label - 4AD

## curiosities
**Adventurous British Indie Label** : 4AD is described as an adventurous British indie label that played a significant role in creating MARRS' "Pump Up the Volume." Ivo Watts Russell, who ran the company, assembled musicians and DJs to create the groundbreaking track, demonstrating the label's innovative approach. "Remember the last show when we talked about 4AD, the adventurous British Indie label?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**Adventurous UK Indie Label** : 4AD is described as an adventurous indie label based out of the UK. It was the label that first picked up The Pixies when they couldn't find an American label interested in their experimental noise. "As they couldn't find an American label interested in their brand of experimental noise, the group was first picked up by 4AD, that adventurous indie label out of the UK." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Avant-Garde Focus** : 4AD was initially established as a platform to test out more avant-garde acts, with the intention that successful ones would "graduate" to Beggars Banquet, the larger parent label. However, this model didn't fully materialize. "4AD was established as a place where the more avant-garde acts could be tested out, if they did well, while then they would graduate up to Bigger's, the Big Dog in the Office." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Collaborative Project Home** : The 4AD label was instrumental in the creation of M|A|R|R|S, a one-time project that brought together two of its signed groups, AR Kane and Colorbox, to produce the influential single "Pump Up the Volume." "This was a one-time only project by a couple of groups on Britain's 4AD label, AR Kane and Colorbox." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_qxdftwrX3E
**Cult Following and Reputation** : Instead of being a stepping stone, 4AD developed a substantial following of fans who valued the label's distinct "weirdness." Its reputation grew to such an extent that people would purchase records simply because they were on 4AD, often without prior knowledge of the band. This fostered a cult-like community around the label. "Instead, 4AD found a sizeable number of fans who appreciated the label's weirdness. Its reputation got to the point where people bought records because they were on 4AD, and not necessarily because they'd heard of the band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Experimental Alt-Rock Label** : 4AD is recognized as a wonderfully experimental alt-rock label based in the UK. It has a history of taking chances on unique and innovative bands, including The Pixies, Bon Iver, and The National, and expanded this tradition by signing Tune-Yards after her early recordings made with a handheld voice recorder. "4AD, the wonderfully experimental alt-rock label out of the UK, that also took a chance with bands like the Pixies, Bonnie Verre, the National, and so many others." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rEawe_1Fqf0
**Fan Trust and Reputation** : The label developed such a strong reputation that fans would often purchase records from unknown bands simply because they were released on 4AD, trusting in the label's consistent artistic parameters. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFLMkvJAaUY
**Founding (1980)** : England's 4AD label, an abbreviation of "forward," was founded in 1980. It became highly influential and specialized in the emerging Goth and Dreampop styles. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFLMkvJAaUY
**Greatest Contribution: MARRS** : 4AD's most significant contribution to the evolution of music is considered to be the studio project MARRS and its influential single "Pump Up The Volume." "But 4AD's greatest contribution to the evolution of music may have been a studio project called Mars." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Key Artists** : The label's roster included influential groups such as Throwing Muses, This Mortal Coil, Dead Can Dance, The Birthday Party, Modern English, and Da-Da, along with the Pixies. "There were groups like Throwing Muses and This Mortal Coil, Dead Condense in the Birthday Party, Modern English, and Da-Da." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Pioneering Independent Label** : 4AD was a small independent label that specialized in working outside the major label system. It notably signed the Scottish Dreampop band Cocteau Twins in 1979. "One of the earliest examples of Dreampop was Scotland's Cocktoe twins, who arrived from the scene in 1979, signing to the indie label 4AD." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4
**Pixies' International Release** : 4AD, a British label specializing in experimental and avant-garde post-punk, was responsible for releasing The Pixies' 1989 album "Do Little." This occurred because The Pixies were unable to find an American label willing to release the album at the time. "Back then, they couldn't find anyone in America to release it so they had to go through 4AD, the British label which specialized in experimental and avant-garde post-punk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xdn31jMOOA4
**Specialization in Emerging Styles** : 4AD was crucial in establishing the emerging Dreampop and Goth-adjacent styles, signing acts like Bauhaus, Cocteau Twins, This Mortal Coil, and AR Kane that had a distinctive sound. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFLMkvJAaUY
**Strong Visual Identity** : Beyond its sound, 4AD was known for its powerful visual identity, characterized by its distinctive logo and album artwork. This cohesive aesthetic contributed to its unique reputation. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFLMkvJAaUY
