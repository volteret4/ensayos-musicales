# label - Murder Records

## curiosities
**Band-Founded Independent Label (Early 1992)** : Sloan founded Murder Records in early 1992 as a means to release their debut EP, "The Peppermint-eaky," and bypass the slow pace of major labels like Geffen/DGC, which projected a much later release date for their first album. The label quickly became a vehicle for releasing their own music and supporting other Halifax bands like Eric's Trip and Hardship Post, fostering a communal scene. "So that's why we started murder records was just to put out that EP." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
**Canadian Catalog Home** : After their major label experiences, Sloan consolidated their entire Canadian catalog, including "Smeared" and "Twice Removed" (whose rights they regained), under Murder Records. The label served as their primary Canadian outlet after their initial two Geffen records and became the label for "One Chord to Another" in Canada. "In Canada, we're on murder records the whole time in Canada after the two Geffen records. One court to another, sorry, is on murder records in Canada. And then we get the rights back in Canada to smeared in twice from him. So we have the whole catalog on murder records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
**Sloan's Own Label** : Murder Records is Sloan's own record label, established from the band's inception. It serves as the fundamental entity behind every single Sloan release, demonstrating the band's commitment to self-management and creative control. "From the beginning, Sloan has had their own record label, Murder Records. That's at the root of every single Sloan release." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QB3nyFH_tRU
