## curiosities
**Beatles Sell Their Share to ATV Music (By 1970)** : After disputes over their publishing share, The Beatles eventually sold their remaining stake in Northern Songs to ATV Music, leading to ATV owning 99% of The Beatles' publishing by January 1970 and contributing to the band's breakup. "the Beatles sold their piece of Northern Songs to ATV. By January 1970, ATV owned 99% of the Beatles' publishing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pe40SqDrpZ0
**Becomes Publicly Traded (1965)** : Two years after its formation, Northern Songs became a publicly traded company, with Ringo Starr and George Harrison acquiring small stakes. "Two years later, Northern Songs became a publicly traded company. At this point, Ringo and George also got small stakes in the business." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pe40SqDrpZ0
**Dick James Sells Share to ATV Music** : Following soured relations after Brian Epstein's death, Dick James sold his share of Northern Songs to ATV Music without informing John Lennon and Paul McCartney, leading to significant discord. "Dick James wanted out of Northern Songs. That's when he sold his share to a company called ATV Music without giving any notice to John and Paul." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pe40SqDrpZ0
**Original Beatles Publisher (1963)** : Northern Songs was the initial company responsible for publishing The Beatles' music, formed in February 1963 with ownership by Dick James, Brian Epstein, John Lennon, and Paul McCartney. "In the beginning, this would be February 1963, the publishing for the Beatles music was handled by a company called Northern Songs.
