# label - Sony BMG

## curiosities
**Major Label Status** : Sony BMG is recognized as one of the four major multinational record labels, playing a dominant role in the global distribution of recorded music. Like other major labels, it maintains a network of subsidiary imprints. "Universal, EMI, Sony BMG, and Warner." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ft8nruhWCas
**One of the Biggest Major Labels (2005)** : In 2005, Sony BMG was recognized as one of the biggest major record labels, holding significant power and influence in the music industry during a period of rampant piracy. "Sony BMG was one of the biggest major labels at the time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vJszsJQpINQ
**Rootkit Controversy (2005)** : Sony BMG was at the center of a major controversy in 2005 for secretly installing "Rootkit" malware (XCP and MediaMax) on users' computers via compact discs, including albums from artists like Neil Diamond, Frank Sinatra, Celine Dion, and Cyndi Lauper. This malicious code was designed to prevent CD ripping but compromised personal computer security and was installed without user consent or notification, leading to widespread outrage, lawsuits, and millions paid in damages. "The Sony BMG Root Kid concept was born." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vJszsJQpINQ
