# label - Gibson

## curiosities
**Acquisition of Epiphone** : In 1958, Epiphone, a company initially formed in Izmir, Turkey, in 1873, was acquired by Gibson's parent company, leading to the merger of the two guitar lines. This meant Epiphone models, though produced under their own name, were owned by Gibson. "Epiphone was a standalone company formed in Izmir, Turkey in 1873, and their business was largely devoted to making banjos. But then in 1958, it was bought by Gibson's parent company and the two lines were merged, so it's an epiphone but it's owned by Gibson." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Financial Struggles in 2010s** : Gibson, a major manufacturer of electric guitars, found itself deep in debt during the 2010s, reflecting an alarming drop in electric guitar sales across the industry during that period. "Two major manufacturers Gibson and Fender were deep in debt." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Y6nHw5xx88
**Founding and Early Products** : Gibson was founded in Kalamazoo, Michigan, in 1894 by Orville Gibson, initially specializing in the manufacture of mandolins. Orville Gibson later invented the Archtop guitar, drawing inspiration from violin construction. "Orville Gibson founded his company in Kalamazoo, Michigan in 1894, the first factory manufactured Mandalins." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Introduction of the Les Paul Guitar** : Following new ownership in 1950, Gibson introduced its iconic Les Paul guitar in 1952. Developed in collaboration with jazz and pop artist Les Paul and designer Tim McCarty, this solid-body instrument addressed the feedback issues prevalent in hollow-body guitars of the era. "By 1950, there was new ownership. In 1952, the company introduced their iconic guitar, the Les Paul." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Leadership During Lean Times** : Guy Hart managed Gibson from 1924 to 1948, navigating the company through the challenging periods of the Depression and World War II. During these lean times, he diversified production to include high-quality wooden toys and, later, metal parts for military use. "Between 1924 and 1948, the company was run by Guy Hart. During lean times, he had Gibson manufacture high quality wooden toys." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Post-1969 Production Shifts** : After 1969, Gibson relocated production of some Epiphone models to Korea, then China, and subsequently Indonesia. This move resulted in a complex production landscape where some Epiphone ES-335s were produced by Gibson, while others were made by various licensed companies. "Things get a little murky after 1969 when Gibson moved production to Korea and then China and then Indonesia." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**The ES-335 Innovation** : In 1958, Gibson introduced the ES-335, a semi-acoustic, semi-hollow body guitar designed by Tim McCarty. This model resolved the issue of dark and warm tones, offering a lighter body and double cutaways for easy access to the 22nd fret, blending solid-body amplification with acoustic warmth. "The solution came in 1958 with the Gibson ES-335, a semi-acoustic semi-holobody guitar." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Wartime Production and Workforce** : During World War II, Gibson's factories shifted to producing metal parts for the military. Despite this, they continued to make guitars, employing women in the factories to compensate for the significant number of men who had been drafted into service. "During the war, factories started producing metal parts for use by the military. There were still making guitars, but so many men had been drafted that women were employed in the factories." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
