**Extravagant Album Promotion (1990s)** : In 1998, Warner Bros. Records demonstrated the lavish budgets available for promotion in the 1990s by hosting a global listening party for R.E.M.'s *Up* album in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. The label flew in journalists and radio and music channel personnel from around the world to listen to the record, charging all associated costs back to the band. **MTV Parent Company (1981)** : Warner Bros., as the parent company of MTV, played a crucial role in the channel's early success. They owned numerous record labels and instructed them to actively participate in the video bandwagon, ensuring MTV had a consistent supply of new promotional content.
