**Rejection of The Beatles (Early 1960s)** : Decca Records famously rejected The Beatles in the early 1960s. The label's talent scouts expressed the belief that "guitar groups were on their way out," making this a notable historical misjudgment in music industry history. **Copyright Holder for "The Rolling Stones Songbook"** : Decca Records held the copyright for "The Rolling Stones Songbook," an album released in 1966 by The Andrew Loog Oldham Orchestra. The Verve initially negotiated with Decca to license a six-second string figure from this album for their song "Bittersweet Symphony," but Decca did not hold all the necessary rights to The Rolling Stones' catalog, leading to further legal complications. "He then had his people at the record label negotiate with Decker Records, the holder of the copyright of the Rolling Stones songbook, to use those six seconds as a sample." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fS8V3kYXT6w ← decca-records
**Gender Discrimination Policy** : Decca Records, a prominent record label of its era, maintained a discriminatory company policy that barred women from being hired in engineering or sound production roles. Women were only considered for positions such as cleaners or assistants, illustrating significant gender bias in the music industry at the time.
