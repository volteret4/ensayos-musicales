# label - Decca Records

## curiosities
**Copyright Holder for "The Rolling Stones Songbook"** : Decca Records held the copyright for "The Rolling Stones Songbook," an album released in 1966 by The Andrew Loog Oldham Orchestra. The Verve initially negotiated with Decca to license a six-second string figure from this album for their song "Bittersweet Symphony," but Decca did not hold all the necessary rights to The Rolling Stones' catalog, leading to further legal complications. "He then had his people at the record label negotiate with Decker Records, the holder of the copyright of the Rolling Stones songbook, to use those six seconds as a sample." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fS8V3kYXT6w
**Rejection of The Beatles (Early 1960s)** : Decca Records famously rejected The Beatles in the early 1960s. The label's talent scouts expressed the belief that "guitar groups were on their way out," making this a notable historical misjudgment in music industry history. "At around the same time, the Beatles were rejected by Decker Records because their talent scouts believe that guitar groups were on their way out." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L0j9iDuHQVA
**Rejection of The Beatles (Pre-1964)** : Decca famously rejected The Beatles, believing "guitar groups were on the way out" and that the band had "no future in show business."
