# label - Zoo Entertainment

## curiosities
**Acquisition by Zamba** : In the spring of 1998, Zoo was acquired by Zamba, at which point Zoo's name was changed to Volcano. This acquisition and subsequent renaming marked another shift in the complex corporate ownership Tool faced. "In the spring of 1998, Zoo was bought by Zamba, whereupon Zoo's name was changed to Volcano." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JJGJXOoT2PI
**Confusing Name Changes** : After being absorbed into Volcano, the entity underwent confusing name changes, including Volcano changing to Free World, then Free World Entertainment changing back to Zoo Entertainment, and then simply Zoo. These changes caused significant confusion and paralysis for Tool. "And then just for kicks, Free World Entertainment changed its name back to Zoo Entertainment, and then just back to Zoo." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JJGJXOoT2PI
**Early Affiliation and Artist Roster** : Zoo Entertainment was the label Tool initially chose, affiliated at the time with BMG, which was a standalone major label. It also served as the label for Green Jelly, providing a level of comfort for Tool's drummer Danny Carey, who had played with Green Jelly. "Tool decided to go with a label called Zoo Entertainment." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JJGJXOoT2PI
**Financial Troubles & Acquisitions** : By April 1993, when Tool's debut album *Undertow* was released, Zoo was already experiencing financial trouble. In 1995, its parent label BMG laid off numerous staff, leading to Zoo's sale to Volcano Entertainment in 1996. For a period, Zoo and Volcano co-existed as a partnership, with Zoo retaining custody of Tool's second album, *Anima*. "Zoo was in financial trouble." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JJGJXOoT2PI
**Royalty & Contract Disputes** : Zoo was criticized for being "really slow with the royalty checks" for the briskly selling *Anima* album, and the initial payment was significantly smaller than expected. Critically, Zoo (then absorbed into Volcano) "forgot to pick up the option" for Tool's contract renewal. This oversight, attributed to the formality falling through the cracks, led to Tool deciding they no longer wished to deal with the company, which in turn prompted Zoo to sue Tool for breach of contract. "Then, Tool found out that Zoo, Volcano, forgot, forgot to pick up the option for Tools Contract." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JJGJXOoT2PI
