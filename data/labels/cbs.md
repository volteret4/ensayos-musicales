## curiosities
**Signing The Clash (1977)** : CBS signed The Clash to a record deal on January 25, 1977, after their emergence on the British punk scene. "As far as British punk went, the clash finally signed a record deal with CBS on the 25th." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wYuNolSDLFM
**The Clash's Controversial Signing (1977)** : On January 26, 1977, CBS Records signed The Clash to a major label deal valued at 100,000 pounds, a figure significantly higher than other competing offers. This decision sparked widespread condemnation within the British punk community, who viewed it as a "sell out" for a band previously seen as ideologically pure and anti-establishment, leading to protests throughout the UK. "The date was January 26, 1977. That's when the clash signed a major record deal with CBS." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1y2UuU-me_s
**The Clash's Unfavorable Record Deal** : The Clash's manager, Bernie Rhodes, negotiated a deal with CBS that committed the band to 13 albums at a significantly low royalty rate, a deal they believed was for only five albums. This unfavorable contract contributed to the band falling deeply into debt and was a major factor in Rhodes's initial firing. "The clash thought Bernie had negotiated a deal with CBS for five albums, one per year for five years. What actually happened is that Rose signed them up to a total of 13 albums at a really low royalty rate." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pAOFzQZPjSM
