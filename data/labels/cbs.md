This quality was observed in shows like The Smothers Brothers Show, The Carol Burnett Show, and The Sonny & Cher Show. **The Clash's Controversial Signing (1977)** : On January 26, 1977, CBS Records signed The Clash to a major label deal valued at 100,000 pounds, a figure significantly higher than other competing offers. This decision sparked widespread condemnation within the British punk community, who viewed it as a "sell out" for a band previously seen as ideologically pure and anti-establishment, leading to protests throughout the UK. **The Rural Purge (1970-1971)** : Under the leadership of Fred Silverman, who took over CBS in 1970-1971, the network underwent a significant programming shift known as the "rural purge." This initiative systematically canceled any show perceived as "remotely country," regardless of its ratings, including popular programs like The Johnny Cash Show, Petticoat Junction, The Beverly Hillbillies, The Red Skelton Show, Green Acres, and even The Ed Sullivan Show. The move was replaced by "urban friendly programming" such as All in the Family, MASH, and The Mary Tyler Moore Show, driven by a desire to target more heavily populated areas for higher ratings.
