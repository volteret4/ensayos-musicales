# label - Electroharmonics

## curiosities
**Big Muff Development (1969)** : Founded by Mike Matthews, Electroharmonics was already selling the Muff Fuzz in 1969 when Matthews sought to create a pedal with greater sustain. He hired Bell Labs engineer Bob Meyer to design a foot pedal that could provide both on-demand fuzz and a prolonged ringing sound. The resulting unit, the Big Muff, was the first to use the word "distortion" in addition to "fuzz" in its description, and it was a massive hit, influencing the sound of the 1970s and 1990s alt-rock. "Electroharmonics actually used the word distortion in addition to fuzz to describe what they were doing. They were the first." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w
**Big Muff Variations and Impact** : Electroharmonics produced numerous versions of the Big Muff pedal, which experienced fluctuating popularity over musical trends. It became an almost indispensable tool for 90s alt-rock bands, with artists like Korn, Bush, Dinosaur Jr., and Sonic Youth being fans. Mudhoney even named an EP "Superfuzz Big Muff" in its honor, and a specific model was crucial for The Smashing Pumpkins' sound. A special orange version was sold, famously described as "all the guitars on Simee's Dream in a box." "Electroharmonics sold many different versions of the Big Muff, which has gone in and out of style with trends and music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w
