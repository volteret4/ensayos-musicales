# label - Sony

## curiosities
**Animated Shorts for The Smashing Pumpkins** : Sony had plans to produce a series of animated shorts for the web based on The Smashing Pumpkins' *Machina, The Machines of God* concept. However, these plans were ultimately unrealized, likely due to the impact of the dotcom bubble. "And at one point, Sony was even going to create a series of animated shorts for the web." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KyNY3zv6uc
**Artist Autonomy** : Sony was David Bowie's record company during his secret comeback period leading up to *The Next Day* in 2013, but they understood better than to pressure him, recognizing his unique approach to releasing music. "He was signed to Sony, but they knew better than to push him." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Yq2A_o9-ulM
**Introduction of the Walkman (1979)** : In 1979, Sony began selling the Walkman, a portable cassette player designed to be worn on a belt and used with headphones. This product quickly became a commercial success but also ignited a patent dispute with Andreas Pavel, who had conceived a similar device years earlier. "It was 1979. Sony started selling something called the Walkman." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
**Licensing of CD Technology (After 1970)** : A few years after Jim Russell secured the first patent for his optical recording and playback device in 1970 (which essentially was the first compact disk), Sony, in partnership with Phillips, acquired licenses to further develop this nascent technology into a marketable consumer product. "A few years later, Sony and Phillips purchased the licenses to develop the technology into consumer product." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
**Major Record Label** : Sony is identified as one of the three major record labels that dominate the music industry, operating as a multinational entity controlling a significant portion of the world's music trade and revenue. "The music industry is dominated by three major record labels, Universal, Sony, and Warner." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Patent Dispute and Settlement with Pavel (1986, 2003)** : Sony engaged in negotiations with Andreas Pavel over alleged violations of his patent related to the personal music player concept. An initial agreement in 1986 addressed Walkman sales only in Germany, while attempts at a similar settlement in the UK were unsuccessful and costly for Pavel. However, in 2003, Sony and Pavel reached an out-of-court settlement, rumored to include $10 million in cash and a share of royalties from certain Walkman models. "He began to negotiate with Sony over what he considered to be violations of his patent." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
**Press Play (Failed Online Music Store)** : Sony was a joint venture partner in "Press Play," one of two early online music stores created by major labels in an attempt to sell digital files. This service was difficult to use, came with restrictions, and included complicated digital rights management (DRM) safeguards. It was a disaster, lasting only about two years before being shuttered in March 2003. "There was Press Play, which was a joint venture between Universal and Sony." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nX8uAFDzDYM
