**Cassette Standardization (1965)** : Sony played a crucial role in the global standardization of the compact cassette. **Walkman (1979) - Portable Cassette Player** : Sony launched the Walkman on July 1, 1979, as a modification of their "Pressman" audio recorder for journalists. Requested by Sony head Masaru Ibuka for long flights, the original TPS-LP2 model was small, light, and came with high-quality headphones. It uniquely featured two headphone jacks, reflecting an initial belief that users wouldn't want complete isolation. Initially branded as Soundabout, Stowaway, or Freestyle in various regions, it was eventually standardized globally as the Walkman. Priced at $150 in 1979 (nearly $600 today), Sony projected monthly sales of 5,000 units but sold 30,000 in its first 60 days, fundamentally changing portable music and boosting cassette sales. `"So imagine his surprise. When on July 1st, 1979, Sony started selling something called the Walkman."` ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OuFvfZJKcpc
