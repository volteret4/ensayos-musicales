# label - Warner Brothers Records

## curiosities
**Dispute with Linkin Park (2005)** : Warner Brothers was involved in a public and contentious dispute with Linkin Park in 2005, occurring during a period of financial instability for the label. The label accused the band's management of "extortion" and "making baseless charges" in their negotiation tactics, while Linkin Park sought a $60 million advance. "executives were brawling with Lincoln Park, one of the label's biggest assets, and one of the label's biggest sources of revenue." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YcdidjWaIqE
**Financial Struggles (Mid-2000s)** : In mid-2005, Warner Brothers faced significant financial difficulties, including new management, cost-cutting measures, layoffs of over 1,000 employees and 30% of its artist roster in 2004, and a negative credit rating due to debt. The label's attempt to raise $750 million through a stock offering was met with a hesitant market, achieving only $16 per share instead of the targeted $24. "Warner Brothers was under new management and was trying to cut both costs and raise money." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YcdidjWaIqE
**Initial Rejection of Linkin Park** : Warner Brothers Records initially turned down Linkin Park (then known as Hybrid Theory) "many, many times" before eventually signing them, indicating their initial skepticism despite later success. "The label that finally took a chance in the band was Warner Brothers, even though they had told this band to go away many, many times." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YcdidjWaIqE
**Initial Rejections of Linkin Park** : Warner Brothers Records initially turned down Hybrid Theory (later Linkin Park) at least three times before finally signing them in late 1999. This signing was largely due to a former Zomba A&R executive, Jeff, who had become a VP at Warner. "even though Warner had turned down Hybrid Theory slash Lincoln Park three times in the past, with this guy in charge, got named Jeff, they got a meeting, and they were signed in late 1999." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
**Jeff Blue's Role** : Jeff Blue, who had initially championed the band at Zomba, later moved to Warner Brothers and became their internal advocate, ultimately securing Linkin Park's record deal. "their champion was, again, this dude Jeff Blue, who had quit Zamba and had moved over to Warner Brothers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YcdidjWaIqE
**Label's Misjudgment of Linkin Park's Sound** : Despite Linkin Park's eventual success with over 100 million records sold, Warner Brothers Records was initially unsure how to market the band, with some, including the label president, reportedly disliking their sound and debating whether to release their debut album at all due to "screaming and rapping." They made various suggestions, such as cutting the rap, Mike Shinoda rapping like Fred Durst, bringing in a reggae vocalist, or adding gimmicks to Joe Hahn. "Warner did not know what to do with Lincoln Park and some, including apparently the president of the label just hated their sound." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
**Shareholder Threat Over Body Count's "Cop Killer"** : During the controversy surrounding Body Count's song "Cop Killer," shareholders of Warner Brothers Records threatened to withdraw all their investments if the label did not address the band and the song. "It got so crazy that shareholders threatened to pull all their money for Warner Brothers records if the label didn't do something about Body Count." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
