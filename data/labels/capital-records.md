**Extensive Promotion of Smile** : Capital Records initially provided strong backing for Brian Wilson and the Smile project, even hiring Derek Taylor, former press chief for The Beatles, to promote Wilson as a musical genius. The label actively advertised Smile, predicting over a million sales by January 1967 and printing more than 400,000 album covers in anticipation. **Major Label Status and Johnny Otis Signing (1957)** : Capital Records was recognized as one of the biggest record labels in the United States. Johnny Otis secured a contract with Capital in 1957, ensuring him major label distribution for the first time in many years, largely due to the advocacy of comedian Mickey Katz. **Signing and Dropping of Virtual Rapper FM Mecha (2022)** : Capital Records signed virtual rapper FM Mecha in August 2022 and released the single "Florida Water." However, the label subsequently dropped FM Mecha due to significant backlash and criticism from the public, who viewed the character as a caricature of Black culture created by non-Black individuals. **Smile Album Release Agreement** : Capital Records reached an agreement with The Beach Boys for the Smile album to be released on January 15, 1967. This provided a tight deadline of merely three months to capitalize on the significant hype generated by the "Good Vibrations" single.
