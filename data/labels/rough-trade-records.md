# label - Rough Trade Records

## curiosities
**British Indie Label Influence** : A London-based independent record label known for its significant role in breaking new music, particularly since the punk era in the 1970s. They provided an early platform for bands like The Strokes. "The strokes started by sending a demo to Rough Trade Records in London, and they agreed to release an EP called The Modern Age." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=raRCZ0Y5up8
**London Store Experience** : The speaker purchased their first White Stripes record, *White Blood Cells*, at the original Rough Trade store in Notting Hill, London, highlighting the label's physical presence and influence in the indie music scene. "I bought my first white stripes record at the original Rough Trade store in Notting Hill, London." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q4dEV1yn3OY
**Maintaining Physical Locations** : Rough Trade Records was highlighted as one of the few music retailers that continued to operate multiple physical locations, with two in London, one in Bristol, one in Nottingham, one in Liverpool, and two in New York, contrasting with the general mass extinction of chain stores. "They have two locations in London, one in Bristol, another in Nottingham, one in Liverpool, and a couple of New York locations." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZzIqIydZTlA
**Resurrection for The Strokes** : Rough Trade Records (UK) won a fierce bidding war for The Strokes after the success of their "The Modern Age" EP. At the time, the company had ceased operating as a label and was only managing bands. However, company head Geoff Travis was so impressed with The Strokes that he resurrected the label specifically to sign and release their music. "The winner in the bidding war was UK's Rough Trade Records, even though they'd stopped being a label at the time and were only managing bands." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jEfofyobGiI
**The Strokes' "The Modern Age" EP (2001)** : Rough Trade Records in the UK agreed to release The Strokes' *The Modern Age* EP on January 29, 2001, after US labels showed no interest. The EP's immense demand led to the label struggling to maintain supply to shops. "they sent a demo to rough trade records in the UK, who agreed to release an EP called The Modern Age. When it came out on January 29, 2001, rough trade struggled to maintain a supply to the shops." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q4dEV1yn3OY
**The Sundays' Deal and Financial Difficulties** : The Sundays secured a record deal with Rough Trade Records. The label later encountered financial difficulties, which led The Sundays to take over management of their own affairs. "they got a deal with rough trade records. When rough trade ran into financial difficulties, they started managing everything themselves" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Dhh5_PDhd0
**UK Distribution Deal for Funeral** : Rough Trade Records secured a distribution deal for Arcade Fire's *Funeral* album in the UK, playing a crucial role in the band's initial recognition and success across the Atlantic. "thanks to a distribution deal with rough trade records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q_c-m_NDznw
