**"Coachella for Old People" (2016)** : Desert Trip, also known as "Coachella for Old People," was a major festival held over two weekends in October 2016, where hundreds of thousands of attendees paid up to $1,500 US dollars each. The lineup featured legendary artists including The Rolling Stones, Neil Young, Paul McCartney, Roger Waters, Bob Dylan, and The Who. The event grossed $160 million, nearly double that year's Coachella, highlighting the significant financial power and interest in heritage acts. "The best example might be Desert Trip, the so-called Coachella for Old People in 2016. **Establishment and Purpose (2006)** : The Polaris Music Prize was established in Canada in 2006, mirroring international awards like Scandinavia's Polar Music Prize and the UK's Mercury Prize. Its goal is to identify the "best album of the year" without considering genre or commercial success, aiming for a true meritocracy focused solely on musical merit. A jury of music professionals outside the label system nominates hundreds of albums, which are then narrowed down to a long list of 40, then a short list of 10. In September, a sequestered jury determines the winner, who receives $50,000, while short-listed artists get $3,000. It intentionally generates controversy and encourages listeners to discover new music. "Since 2006, we've had the Polaris music prize in Canada. The goal of each of these music prizes is to determine the best album of the year without any regard to genre or commercial success." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZzIqIydZTlA
**Evolution from Alt-Rock to Pop/Hip-Hop** : Originally conceived in 1990 as a touring festival showcasing "left-of-center and alternative bands" like Red Hot Chili Peppers, Soundgarden, Rage Against The Machine, Pearl Jam, Tool, Green Day, Metallica, Hole, Smashing Pumpkin, Speedy Boy, Sonic Youth, and Remones, Lollapalooza was an essential part of 90s music culture. After its original concept "ran out of steam" in the late 90s, it was reborn as a single weekend event in Chicago. However, its original alt-rock focus is now largely gone, with the festival primarily featuring pop and hip-hop acts, reflecting a cultural shift in America. "When La La Palosa was first conceived in 1990, it was a full-blown touring festival of Weirdos, featuring the best in left-of-center and alternative bands." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZzIqIydZTlA
**Evolution from Alt-Rock to Pop/Hip-Hop (Post-2008)** : Coachella, which began in the desert in 1999, initially focused on "Lollapalooza-type bands" such as Tool, Rage Against The Machine, Jane's Addiction, Oasis, Red Hot Chili Peppers, The Cure, Radiohead, Nine Inch Nails, Depeche Mode, Coldplay, and The Killers. However, starting around 2008, the festival's focus shifted towards pop and hip-hop, mirroring a cultural change in America where these genres became dominant among festival-goers. While still featuring some rock acts, its overall complexion is now vastly different from its early years. **Maintaining Physical Locations** : Rough Trade Records was highlighted as one of the few music retailers that continued to operate multiple physical locations, with two in London, one in Bristol, one in Nottingham, one in Liverpool, and two in New York, contrasting with the general mass extinction of chain stores.
