**Marketing Strategy** : Fender employed a marketing strategy of naming its guitars after popular contemporary technologies or concepts to piggyback on their public attention. Examples include the Telecaster, named after television, and the Stratocaster, named after Stratocaster airplanes. "The name Telecaster was deliberately chosen as an of the moment marketing move because television was the biggest thing in home appliances in 1951. The offender was hoping to piggyback on the phenomenon and it wasn't the first time he would do that. ... In the 1950s, there were airplanes called Stratocasters that were capturing the public's attention. So just as the Telecaster's name was from television, another new thing, so did the name Stratocaster." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=E6cXyaHALBQ
**Post-Telecaster Innovations** : Following the success of the Telecaster, Fender continued to introduce more instruments, some of which achieved iconic status while others did not. The 1950s and early 1960s were characterized by significant innovation from the company. "Fender introduced more instruments, some that failed, and others that went on to become iconic.
