# label - DGC

## curiosities
**"Unicorn Label" Reputation** : DGC/Geffen Records was considered a "unicorn label" among major labels in the late 1980s and early 1990s due to its somewhat independent approach and the presence of staff who came from college radio backgrounds, aiming to sign bands like Sonic Youth and Husker Dew. "DGC, Geffen Records was kind of at that time was considered to be sort of a unicorn label amongst all the major labels in there. They were kind of somewhat independent in a way." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xdn31jMOOA4
**Geffen's Alternative Imprint** : DGC was established as a brand new subsidiary of Geffen Records, intended to be Geffen's alternative label. It was designed to maintain an indie label attitude and aesthetic while benefiting from Geffen's distribution network. DGC successfully targeted and signed Sonic Youth as its first major act, offering complete creative control, and subsequently influenced Nirvana to sign a similar deal. "Now DGC was to be the Geffen alternative label. Records on DGC would be distributed by Geffen, but it would be run with the same sort of attitude and aesthetic as an indie label." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AymMybE33zU
**Nirvana's Choice Influenced by Sonic Youth** : Nirvana signed with DGC on January 4, 1991, largely because Sonic Youth had chosen the label. Kurt Cobain reportedly felt that if DGC was good enough for Sonic Youth, it was good enough for him. "So on January 4th, 1991, it was announced that Nirvana had signed a contract with DGC. Now why DGC? Well, like Thurston Moore said, it was because Sonic Youth had chosen that company as their major label home." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xdn31jMOOA4
**Nirvana's Label** : DGC was the record label for Nirvana, and later released Sweet 75's debut album. "Their debut album, which was a cell title thing, was issued in 1997 on DGC, Nirvana's label" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cS8k3PYU_Eo
**Nirvana's Major Label Home** : DGC was the major label that Nirvana signed with. This signing later led to a legal problem for Dave Grohl, as he was sued by someone associated with his previous band, Scream, who claimed he was still under contract to them. "When he joined Nirvana and they were signed to DGC, Dave was immediately sued by someone associated with Scream for $40,000." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_AY5lS-FmlU
**Sonic Youth's Major Label Home** : DGC, a subsidiary of Universal Records, became Sonic Youth's major label home in 1990. The band chose DGC for the security and healthcare benefits, not a large paycheck, marking an "experiment" for major labels on whether indie bands could do business. "And so it came to pass that Sonic Youth signed with DGC, a subsidiary of Universal Records, much to the shock and horror of their indie constituency." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xdn31jMOOA4
