# label - Geffen

## curiosities
**Artist-Friendly Contract Release** : Despite a six-album contract, Geffen Records ultimately chose to release Sloan from their contractual obligations, which the band found to be an "artist friendly and cool" decision, especially as Geffen was financially secure and "didn't really need us." This allowed Sloan to pursue other labels and eventually regain their music rights. "But in the end, Geffen was just cool. Like, first of all, they don't need, they made tons of money so they didn't really need us. But they were really artist friendly and cool. And they said, just go, we're not going to hold you to contract." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
**Nirvana's Label** : Geffen Records was the label for the iconic grunge band Nirvana. Sloan signed with Geffen in 1992 for their "Smeared" album, at the height of the grunge era, in an attempt to break into the American market. "They were signed to Nirvana's label, the height of the whole Grunge thing, but eventually that whole thing fell apart largely due to some label politics and they were dropped." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QB3nyFH_tRU
**Released Death to False Metal** : Weezer returned to Geffen for the release of "Death to False Metal," after "Hurley" was released on Epitaph Records. "For the next record, they were back on Geffen." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LYRPxtP61JM
**Skepticism Towards "Twice Removed"** : Geffen Records expressed significant concern about Sloan's album "Twice Removed" due to its stylistic departure from "Smeared," viewing it as a completely different band and suggesting they "redo the whole thing" because it would be difficult to market. Despite this, Sloan stood their ground, a move that later became part of their "sticking it to the man" narrative. "It is true that Geffen, when they heard it, they were like, what are you serious? Like, this is a different band. It's going to be hard for us to market you. Please, you know, consider redoing the whole thing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
**Sloan's First Album Remix** : Geffen Records picked up Sloan's first record (later known as "Smeared") and remixed it for distribution. This remix cost $1,200. "The first record we made, which was on Gephin, we made with the aspirations of trying to make a thousand CDs or five hundred. And then we got picked up and remixed by Gephin." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QB3nyFH_tRU
**Sloan's Major Label Deal** : Geffen Records (also referred to as DGC) signed Sloan after A&R representative Todd Sullivan was captivated by their song "Underwhelmed." The signing was a rapid and exciting event for the Halifax band. "He set it to a friend of his Todd Sullivanic Geffen who loved the song underwhelmed and then came to see his play and then he was like, do you guys want to sign to Geffen?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbic-70ZhpE
