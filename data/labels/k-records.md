# label - K Records

## curiosities
**Champion of Tweet Punk and Counterbalance** : K Records not only released material by aggressive bands such as Fugazi, Bikini Kill, and L7 but also notably championed "Tweet Punk" acts like Heavenly. These groups served as a vital counterbalance to the intense masculinity prevalent in hardcore and the heavy messaging of Riot Grrrl groups. Many were openly queer and actively vocal against homophobic, sexist, and racist elements in mainstream rock, all while maintaining a sweet, childlike, naive, romantic, and sentimental musical style. "While the label did issue material by Fugazi, Bikini Kill, and L7, K also had acts like Heavenly." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jhgZrwtYE2g
**Evolution of Releases from Cassette to Vinyl** : K Records initially focused predominantly on releasing music on cassette tapes. However, in 1987, the label made a strategic shift, transitioning its primary release format to vinyl records, adapting to the changing landscape of independent music distribution. "At first, most of their releases were on cassette." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jhgZrwtYE2g
**International Pop Underground (1990s)** : In the 1990s, K Records spearheaded the "International Pop Underground," an initiative encompassing a series of records and a significant concert event in 1991. The International Pop Underground Convention featured more than 50 indie acts, drawing from diverse scenes including the new Riot Grrrl movement, the hardcore community, straight-edge bands, and other anti-corporate, like-minded groups. "In the 1990s, K had something called the International Pop Underground, which applied to a series of records and a concert in 1991." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jhgZrwtYE2g
**Olympia, Washington DIY Operation (1982)** : K Records, established in Olympia, Washington, in 1982, was a highly independent and "DIY" operation. It was founded by Calvin Johnson, the frontman for Beat Happening, with its initial headquarters located at his kitchen table. Johnson later partnered with Candace Peterson, who received $20 a week and earned academic credit from Evergreen State College for her contributions to the label. "K was founded by Kelvin Johnson, the frontman for a group called Beat Happening." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jhgZrwtYE2g
**Olympia-based Indie Label** : K Records is an indie label based out of Olympia, Washington, identified as one of the more important labels in the American Pacific Northwest that released records by bands like Mecca Normal. It was a key part of the DIY and indie scene. "There was K records out of Olympia, Washington" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Qss5kC2FUx0
