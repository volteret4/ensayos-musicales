# label - Epiphone

## curiosities
**Acquisition by Gibson's Parent Company** : In 1958, Epiphone was acquired by Gibson's parent company, leading to the merger of the two distinct lines. While Epiphone continued as a brand, its ownership and production decisions became intertwined with Gibson. "But then in 1958, it was bought by Gibson's parent company and the two lines were merged, so it's an epiphone but it's owned by Gibson." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Founding and Early Focus** : Epiphone was established as a standalone company in Izmir, Turkey, in 1873, and its primary business during its early years was largely centered on the manufacture of banjos. "Epiphone was a standalone company formed in Izmir, Turkey in 1873, and their business was largely devoted to making banjos." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Post-1969 Production Complexity** : Following 1969, Gibson relocated production of Epiphone models to various locations including Korea, China, and Indonesia. This geographical shift resulted in a complex manufacturing situation where some Epiphone 355s were produced by Gibson, while other models were made by different companies under license. "Things get a little murky after 1969 when Gibson moved production to Korea and then China and then Indonesia." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
