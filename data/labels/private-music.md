# label - Private Music

## curiosities
**BMG Subsidiary** : Private Music was a subsidiary of BMG, based in Los Angeles. The Matthew Good Band signed a two-record deal with them for the "Underdogs" album. "Well, Underdogs, I signed a record deal with Private Music, which was a BMG subsidiary in Los Angeles." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-s0gwXY_CZQ
**Financial Payout** : Due to a two-record contract, Matthew Good was able to legally negotiate a payout from Private Music for both records without having to record anything. This money was then used to self-fund the "Underdogs" album. "So basically, through some legal finangling, I got them to pay me out on what they owed me for both records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-s0gwXY_CZQ
**Record Deal Collapse** : The record deal between The Matthew Good Band and Private Music collapsed unexpectedly. Private Music folded on the very day recording was scheduled to begin for the "Underdogs" album, causing the record to be pulled. "So what happened with that was his lives came over and private folded, and they pulled the record the day we were supposed to start recording." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-s0gwXY_CZQ
