# label - Factory Records

## curiosities
**Aesthetic Focus** : Distinguished itself by a strong emphasis on aesthetic, meticulous attention to album artwork and packaging, and a unique, quirky cataloging system. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Business Failures** : Notorious for its unconventional business practices, including never asking bands to sign formal contracts, dubious accounting, and a general disregard for cash flow, which ultimately led to its spectacular bankruptcy. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Co-ownership of The Haçienda** : Factory Records, along with New Order, owned the infamous Manchester club, The Haçienda. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**Failed London Records Acquisition** : An attempt to sell assets to the more financially solid London Records for 4 million pounds collapsed when it was discovered that Factory Records had no legally binding contracts with its artists, as no signatures were ever obtained. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RKvFJMkZdBE
**Financial Collapse (Early 1990s)** : By the early 1990s, Factory Records was under severe financial stress due to a miserable recession, falling property values, and high interest rates, causing its asset value to plummet from 3.5 million pounds to 900,000 pounds, with 2.5 million pounds borrowed. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RKvFJMkZdBE
**First Release (December 24, 1978)** : Issued its very first release on December 24, 1978, comprising two seven-inch singles bundled together, featuring samples from four bands on its roster, including Joy Division. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Great British Indie Label** : Grew to become one of the most significant British indie labels of all time, known for the influential music that flowed from its roster. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Haçienda Nightclub (1980s)** : Established the Haçienda nightclub, which became "ground zero" for a large part of British dance culture throughout the 1980s. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Indie Label in Manchester** : Factory Records was described as a "very cool, new indie label" in Manchester. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**Origin of Name** : Named after a local nightclub night. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Rob Gretton's Role** : Manager Rob Gretton worked for Factory Records and was instrumental in Joy Division signing with the label, as well as bringing in other clients like Happy Mondays and OMD. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**Role of Happy Mondays in Demise** : The extreme drug-related expenses, property damage, and destructive behavior of the Happy Mondays were cited as a primary factor in the label's ultimate downfall. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RKvFJMkZdBE
**Tony Wilson's Manchester Vision (1978)** : Founded by Manchester TV host Tony Wilson, who was inspired by the Sex Pistols' 1976 gig, with the aim of creating an indie label for the city. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
