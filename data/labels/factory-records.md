# label - Factory Records

## curiosities
**Aesthetic Focus** : Distinguished itself by a strong emphasis on aesthetic, meticulous attention to album artwork and packaging, and a unique, quirky cataloging system.
**Business Failures** : Notorious for its unconventional business practices, including never asking bands to sign formal contracts, dubious accounting, and a general disregard for cash flow, which ultimately led to its spectacular bankruptcy.
**First Release (December 24, 1978)** : Issued its very first release on December 24, 1978, comprising two seven-inch singles bundled together, featuring samples from four bands on its roster, including Joy Division.
**Great British Indie Label** : Grew to become one of the most significant British indie labels of all time, known for the influential music that flowed from its roster.
**Haçienda Nightclub (1980s)** : Established the Haçienda nightclub, which became "ground zero" for a large part of British dance culture throughout the 1980s.
**Origin of Name** : Named after a local nightclub night.
**Tony Wilson's Manchester Vision (1978)** : Founded by Manchester TV host Tony Wilson, who was inspired by the Sex Pistols' 1976 gig, with the aim of creating an indie label for the city.
