# label - Capitol Records Canada

## curiosities
**Initial Low Sales (1963)** : An executive for Capitol Records Canada reported very low initial sales for The Beatles' early singles in Canada: "Love Me Do" sold 170 copies, "Please Please Me" sold 280, and "From Me to You" sold 300, indicating a distinct lack of initial Canadian interest in the band. "All white, the executive for Capital Records Canada said the Beatles only sold 170 copies in total." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2IDbX5rNT1E
**Manufacturing for US Export (1963-1964)** : Before The Beatles achieved widespread fame in the United States, Capitol Records Canada took on the responsibility of manufacturing their records, including songs like "All My Love," "Roll Over Bay, Tovon," and "Twist and Shout," north of the 49th parallel. These records were then shipped to the United States, with exports estimated to be at least 100,000 copies, and possibly as high as 350,000 copies each. "In fact, Capital Records Canada had to manufacture their records north of 49 and ship them to the United States." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2IDbX5rNT1E
