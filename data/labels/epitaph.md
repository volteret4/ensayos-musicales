# label - Epitaph

## curiosities
**Biggest Selling Indie Album** : Epitaph Records is the independent label responsible for releasing The Offspring's "Smash" on August 23, 1994. This album went on to become the biggest-selling indie rock record of all time, demonstrating significant success for an independent label in terms of worldwide sales, exceeding 18 million copies globally. "Released on the epitaph label on August 23rd, 1994. It's the offspring and smash." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T1XSJIm0Nh4
**Founded by Bad Religion** : Epitaph was established by the band Bad Religion as a necessity, since no other record label was willing to release their music. Initially, it served as a vehicle exclusively for their own records, with their self-titled EP in 1981 being its first release. "No one wanted to release their stuff either, so they had to establish their own label, and they called theirs Epitaph." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Founding by Bad Religion** : Epitaph Records was founded by Bad Religion in 1980 to release their debut EP after they were unable to secure a record deal. "And no one would release their debut EP, they formed their own indie label, and they called it Epitaph." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PEHkKfmmDiI
**Global Significance and The Offspring** : Epitaph later grew to become one of the most important independent labels worldwide, notably taking a chance on the band The Offspring, which led to their significant success. "Now Epitaph, of course, would later become one of the most important indie labels in the entire world. They were the ones who took a chance on a band called The Offspring," ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PEHkKfmmDiI
**Growth Beyond Hardcore** : The company eventually expanded significantly beyond its initial focus on hardcore punk, although the transcript notes that one of its bands later became the biggest-selling indie band in history. "The company eventually grew beyond just hardcore punk, and in fact, at one point, one of their bands was the biggest selling indie band in the history of the universe..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Indie Label Success and Influence** : Epitaph was an indie label originally founded and run by Bad Religion. It played a crucial role in the 1990s punk revival by signing bands like Rancid, NoFX, and The Offspring. Its success, particularly with The Offspring's "Smash" album (the biggest selling indie album of all time), demonstrated that indie labels could foster massive commercial hits and directly influenced other bands to sign with major labels for wider distribution. "Remember that they ran their own record label. If epitaph could produce a band like them, who else on this roster might be worth checking out." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gkLMwrthZ9s
**Pioneering Independent Label** : Epitaph was among the small independent labels that played a role in the rise of indie music by operating outside the established major label system. "that included rough trade for a D, beggars banquet, epitaph, SST, and many others." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4
**The Offspring's Smash Release** : The Offspring's seminal album "Smash" (1994) was issued through Epitaph, which was the label associated with Bad Religion. "It was a complete indie thing and issued through Bad Religions Epitaph label." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Unconventional Marketing & Success** : Epitaph, an independent label, experienced an unusual stroke of luck when The Offspring's first single was picked up by an LA radio station, an event noted as something that "almost never happened to Epitaph bands." Recognizing the potential, the label "dug deep" to fund a modest $5,000 for The Offspring to produce a music video, which subsequently gained significant airplay on MTV and dramatically boosted their album sales. "the first single was picked up by an LA radio station which was pretty cool because that almost never happened to Epitaph bands." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8UIBWyUt38g
