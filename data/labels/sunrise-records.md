# label - Sunrise Records

## curiosities
**Acquisition by Doug Putnam** : Sunrise Records was acquired by Canadian entrepreneur Doug Putnam, who also oversaw the resurrection of HMV, positioning these as rare successes in the struggling physical music retail landscape. "The only real bright spot was the resurrection of HMV by Canadian entrepreneur Doug Putnam in addition to his sunrise Records Chain, which you also bought." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZzIqIydZTlA
**Acquisition of HMV Canada and UK (2017)** : Following HMV Canada's bankruptcy, Doug Putnam's Sunrise Records acquired the leases for 70 HMV locations across Canada, converting them into Sunrise Records stores. Later, when HMV UK faced bankruptcy, Putnam also purchased Hillcoe's interest, thereby maintaining the HMV brand. Some acquired HMV stores were closed, while others were reopened, including a new flagship HMV Vault store in Birmingham in 2019. "However, Canada's Sunrise Records, a chain formerly with just a handful of locations, moved in and snapped at the leases for 70 HMV locations across Canada." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
**Decline and Acquisition by Doug Putnam (2014)** : The chain faced challenging times, and by the fall of 2014, its presence had diminished to just a few locations. Subsequently, Doug Putnam, who also owns a toy company, acquired Sunrise Records. Putnam entered record retailing with a new business concept and a willingness to take risks. "But then the company was purchased by Doug Putnam, who runs a toy company." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
**Founding and Early Growth (1977 - 1980s)** : Sunrise Records was established in 1977 with its inaugural location on Yonge Street in Toronto. Over approximately a decade, it evolved into one of Canada's five principal record chains, alongside HMV, Sam the Record Man, Music World, and A&M Records. At its peak, Sunrise operated 85 stores nationwide. "The company started in 1977 with a single location on Young Street in Toronto." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
**US Expansion and Global Leadership (2020)** : In 2020, Sunrise Records expanded its operations into the United States by acquiring "five entertainment" (FYE Entertainment), which encompassed 206 record stores operating under various brand names. With this acquisition, Sunrise Records, based in Ancaster, Ontario, solidified its position as the world's largest operator of bricks-and-mortar record stores. "In 2020, they bought five entertainment, which owns 206 record stores, under various brand names across the United States." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
