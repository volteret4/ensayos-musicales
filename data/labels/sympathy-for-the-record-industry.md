# label - Sympathy for the Record Industry

## curiosities
**Esteemed Indie Label** : Sympathy for the Record Industry was a label held in high esteem by the indie community. They signed The White Stripes within two years of their formation and later also signed The Von Bondis, highlighting their role in the early 2000s Garage Rock revival. "Within two years, the White Stripes had a deal with a label called Sympathy for the Record Industry, which was held in very high esteem by members of the indie community." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UpWz3X33vSY ← sympathy-for-the-record-industry
**Long Gone John's Label** : This label, located in Olympia, Washington, was run by Long Gone John and had been dedicated to Garage Rock and Punk since 1988. It was the label through which The White Stripes' third album sold a million copies in the US and another million globally. "a company from Olympia, Washington run by a guy called Long Gone John, that had been dedicated to Garage Rock and Punk since 1988. Things took off with them. The third white stripes album for the label sold a million copies in the US and another million globally." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q4dEV1yn3OY ← sympathy-for-the-record-industry ← sympathy-for-the-record-industry
**Overwhelmed by Demand** : As an independent label, Sympathy for the Record Industry was completely unprepared for and unable to fulfill the immense demand generated by The White Stripes' "White Blood Cells" album upon its release in 2001. This inability to meet the album's unexpected popularity necessitated its re-release by a larger, more capable label.
