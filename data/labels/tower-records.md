# label - Tower Records

## curiosities
**Canadian Operations (1995 - 2001)** : Tower Records established two stores in Canada. The first opened in December 1995 at the south end of the Eaton Centre at Yonge and Queen in Toronto, which operated until 2001. A second location north of downtown Toronto also ceased operations in 2001. "The first opened at the south end of the Eaton Center at Young and Queen in December 1995." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
**Expansion and Famous Locations (1967 - Peak)** : Seven years after its inception, Tower Records expanded to San Francisco, initiating a rapid growth that eventually led to a peak of 200 locations. Many of these stores were standalone establishments rather than mall-based. Its most renowned location was on the Sunset Strip, at the corner of Sunset Boulevard and Horn Avenue, a popular destination for major stars like Elton John, Michael Jackson, and Bruce Springsteen, known for its exceptional selection of imports and a prominent rooftop billboard promoting new releases. "The most famous location of the mall was on the Sunset Strip, the building at the corner of Sunset Boulevard and Horn Avenue." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
**Founder's Death and Online Revival (2018 - 2020)** : Russell Solomon, the founder of Tower Records, passed away in 2018 at the age of 92. In 2020, the brand was revived, transitioning into an online retail format. "Russ Solomon died at the age of 92 and 2018." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
**Founding and Naming (1960)** : Tower Records, recognized as the largest American record chain, was established in Sacramento, California, in 1960 by Russell Solomon. Solomon named the store after his father's drug store, which was located in the same building as the Tower Theatre on Broadway in downtown Sacramento. "It was established in Sacramento, California in 1960 by Russell Solomon." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
**International Reach** : Tower Records achieved an international presence, operating in 18 countries, with Japan serving as a particularly crucial market. Other international locations included Malaysia, Hong Kong, Singapore, Thailand, the Philippines, South Korea, Taiwan, Ireland, Israel, and the UK. "Japan was a very important market for Tower." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
**Mass Extinction Event (2006)** : Tower Records experienced a significant decline, closing almost all of its stores worldwide except for one or two in Japan by 2006, as part of a larger "mass extinction event" for big chain record stores. "Tower Records closed every single store except one or two in Japan in 2006." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZzIqIydZTlA
**Peak Sales and Decline (Early 21st Century)** : During its most successful period, Tower Records achieved impressive sales, independently selling a billion dollars worth of CDs. However, the company encountered severe difficulties in the early 21st century, leading to the closure of all its stores except for the Tokyo location by 2006. "The best years saw Tower Records sell a billion dollars worth of CDs, all on its own." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
