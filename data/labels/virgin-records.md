# label - Virgin Records

## curiosities
**Deal Review and Reunion Suggestion** : Iggy Pop's deal with Virgin Records was under review, and his next record needed to generate significant buzz to prevent him from being dropped. It was "quietly suggested" that Iggy round up guest musicians, specifically hinting at the "Ashton Boys," for an album which eventually became *Skull Ring*. "Iggy's deal with Virgin Records was under review. The next record had to generate some buzz, or he would be dropped." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-rihv544I8
**Missing Royalties – After Nancy Spungen's Death** : Following Nancy Spungen's death, a significant sum of money, estimated by some at $20,000 in royalties from Virgin Records, was reported missing from her and Sid's hotel room. This led to speculation about a robbery as a motive for her murder. "Others say $20,000 in royalties from Virgin Records, was missing for the room." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OcylgiXkDP8
**Refusal of Tour Support for PiL (1992)** : Virgin Records refused to provide further tour support for Public Image Limited, which directly led to the band's last gig on September 18, 1992, and subsequent breakup. "It became their last show when their label, Virgin Records, refused to pay for any more tour support, so that was it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJGFUlQTUhQ
**Sex Pistols Contract (1977)** : The Sex Pistols signed their contract with Virgin Records on March 10, 1977, in front of Buckingham Palace, an event that "kicked punk into high gear in the UK." "The sex pistol signing their Virgin Records contract in front of Buckingham Palace on March 10th, 1977." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Sex Pistols Single Release** : Virgin Records released the Sex Pistols' 7-inch single "Pretty Vacant" in 1977, carrying the serial number VS184. "It's the original release on Virgin Records, 1977, and carries the serial number of VS184." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zUQ2vZzmRiI
**Sex Pistols' Enduring Deal** : The Sex Pistols' third record deal was with Virgin Records, and this particular contract proved to be successful, allowing the band to achieve significant sensation and disrupt the British establishment. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**Signing of Krautrock Bands** : Virgin Records was among British labels that actively signed Krautrock bands, perceiving their music as another form of progressive rock. This move played a crucial role in introducing Krautrock to the UK and its subsequent influence on post-punk music. "Virgin Records and one or two other labels signed up pretty much every crop rock band that they found, considering it to be another form of proc rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ePZDkqLM_gw
**Simple Minds and "Don't You (Forget About Me)"** : When composer Keith Forsey was struggling to find an artist for "Don't You (Forget About Me)" for *The Breakfast Club* soundtrack, he turned to Virgin Records for suggestions. Virgin suggested Simple Minds, despite the band's initial reluctance to record an outsider's song, ultimately leading to their biggest hit. "So in desperation, Keith Forsy turned to Virgin Records. Have you got anyone you think could do this on justice? Virgin had a listen, gave it a thing and said, well, how about simple minds?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM
**Support for Online Music (1996)** : Virgin Records supported David Bowie's decision to make his song "Telling Lies" available for download in 1996, seeing him as the perfect test bed for this new digital distribution method. "In 1996, he made a song available for download with the support of his label Virgin Records, who also saw where things were going." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Yq2A_o9-ulM
