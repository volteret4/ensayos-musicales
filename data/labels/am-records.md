# label - A&M Records

## curiosities
**Acquisition by Polygram (1989)** : A&M Records was bought by Polygram in 1989, marking the end of its independent status. "By the time they were bought by Polygram in 1989, they were the biggest indie label in the world." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
**Brief Sex Pistols Signing (1977)** : In early 1977, A&M Records signed the Sex Pistols after EMI dropped them, but the deal lasted only five days due to rebellion from other A&M artists who refused to be on the same label as the "Yabos," leading management to drop the band. "After being dropped by EMI records because of their bad behavior, they were picked up by A&M records. And that deal lasted for a total of five days." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tbmssCZ05ro
**Catalyst for OMD's US Success (1980s)** : OMD's move to A&M Records in the United States marked a turning point in their North American career. Unlike their previous label which showed little interest, A&M actively promoted them, leading to increased exposure and success. "We got, um, the biggest change for us actually happened just before that when we changed record labels because the record label we were on in the States couldn't get us arrested and didn't care. We moved to A&M and that's when they started to push us." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0fQQubt9UTI
**Co-founder Herb Alpert** : The independent label was co-founded by Herb Alpert, who was also known for his work with the Tijuana Brass. "the, at the time, indie label co-founded by Herb Elpert. Yes, the guy from the Tijuana brass." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sEZ13v1LNYQ
**Connection to "Never Say Never Again" Theme (1983)** : A&M Records is mentioned in the context of Lani Hall, the singer of the "Never Say Never Again" theme, being married to Herb Alpert, who was associated with the label. This highlights a link between a major music label and a specific, albeit less remembered, Bond theme. "She was married to her Belpert, the A and A and M records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K0pgJ7XAlpo
**Destruction Order for "God Save the Queen" (1977)** : Following the termination of their contract with the Sex Pistols in 1977, A&M Records ordered the pressing plant to destroy all 25,000 copies of the "God Save the Queen" 7-inch single that had been pressed on the A&M label. "When that contract was terminated, A&M ordered the pressing plant to destroy any and all copies of the God saved the queen seven inch that had been pressed up on A&M records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tbmssCZ05ro
**Embrace of Experimental Artists** : During the late 1960s and early 1970s, A&M Records, headquartered in Los Angeles, was notable for its progressive stance, openly embracing "hippie ideals" and readily signing artists deemed weird or experimental, such as Captain Beefheart. "It was headquartered in Los Angeles and had no trouble with the hippie ideals of the day. You're weird, you're experimental, great, what you got, maybe we'll get you a deal." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sEZ13v1LNYQ
**Embrace of Hippie Ideals and Experimentation (Late 60s/Early 70s)** : Headquartered in Los Angeles, A&M Records was known for its openness to the prevailing hippie ideals of the late 1960s and early 1970s. The label showed a willingness to sign and indulge experimental and unconventional artists, exemplified by their long-term support for Captain Beefheart despite his eccentricities. "It was headquartered in Los Angeles and had no trouble with the hippie ideals of the day." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7_ZmZxnSOnE
**Founding (1962)** : A&M Records was formed in 1962 by Herb Alpert (the 'A') and Jerry Moss (the 'M'). "They were formed by Herb Albert, the A, and his partner Jerry Moss, who was the M. This was 1962." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
**Largest Indie Label (Pre-1989)** : By the time it was acquired by Polygram in 1989, A&M Records had grown to become the biggest independent record label in the world. "By the time they were bought by Polygram in 1989, they were the biggest indie label in the world." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
**Mainstream & Alt Rock Roster** : While known for mainstream and "middle of the road" acts like The Carpenters, A&M Records also contributed to the world of Alt Rock by signing artists such as OMD, Human League, and The Police. They notably dropped the Sex Pistols after only five days. "Most of their acts were pretty mainstream and pretty middle of the road. This was a label of the Carpenters after all. And the company that dropped the Sex Bistles after just five days." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
**Sex Pistols "God Save the Queen" Release and Destruction (1977)** : A&M Records signed the Sex Pistols for a mere five days in 1977, during which time approximately 25,000 copies of their "God Save the Queen" 7-inch single were pressed. Following the band's dismissal due to other artists' objections, these singles were ordered destroyed, though several dozen were "liberated" and became highly valuable collectibles. "But in that five days, A&M had pressed up something like 25,000 copies of God Save the Queen." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zWNzlSxrj6s
**Sex Pistols Signing Incident – March 10, 1977** : The Sex Pistols signed their record deal with A&M Records outside Buckingham Palace on March 10, 1977. This event was a significant moment in the band's history before their notoriously brief and tumultuous career. "Sid really got into being a sex pistol. He was there when the pistol signed their record deal with A&M records outside of Buckingham Palace in March 10th, 1977." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OcylgiXkDP8
**Sex Pistols' Brief Contract** : A&M Records signed the Sex Pistols to a contract, but this agreement lasted a mere five days before the band was once again paid to leave. This reflected the band's contentious reputation and the labels' reluctance to manage them. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
