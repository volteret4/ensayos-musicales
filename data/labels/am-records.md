# label - A&M Records

## curiosities
**Acquisition by Polygram (1989)** : A&M Records was bought by Polygram in 1989, marking the end of its independent status. "By the time they were bought by Polygram in 1989, they were the biggest indie label in the world." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk ← am-records
**Brief Sex Pistols Contract (1977)** : Two months after the Sex Pistols were dropped by EMI, A&M Records decided to take a chance and signed them. However, a party at the label's offices following the signing resulted in significant damage, and complaints from other A&M artists like The Carpenters, along with questions from head office in Los Angeles, led to the band being dropped after just six days, with a £75,000 buyout. "Two months later, an M records decided to take a chance and signed the pistols to another deal." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wYuNolSDLFM ← am-records ← am-records
**Brief Sex Pistols Signing (1977)** : In early 1977, A&M Records signed the Sex Pistols after EMI dropped them, but the deal lasted only five days due to rebellion from other A&M artists who refused to be on the same label as the "Yabos," leading management to drop the band. "After being dropped by EMI records because of their bad behavior, they were picked up by A&M records. And that deal lasted for a total of five days." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tbmssCZ05ro ← am-records
**Catalyst for OMD's US Success (1980s)** : OMD's move to A&M Records in the United States marked a turning point in their North American career. Unlike their previous label which showed little interest, A&M actively promoted them, leading to increased exposure and success. "We got, um, the biggest change for us actually happened just before that when we changed record labels because the record label we were on in the States couldn't get us arrested and didn't care. We moved to A&M and that's when they started to push us." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0fQQubt9UTI ← am-records
**Challenges for Canadian A&M in US Market** : Having the same label name, such as A&M Records, across both Canada and the United States did not provide a Canadian act with an inherent advantage in the American market. In some cases, it was even a "detriment" as the US counterpart might "take it for granted" that the Canadian branch was "part of the family," potentially reducing the urgency or effort to promote their artists. "The fact that your name is A&M Records and their name is A&M Records is not an edge in any way. In fact in some cases it's probably a detriment because they take it for granted, you're part of the family." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 45 - Oh, Canada! **Embrace of Experimental Artists** : During the late 1960s and early 1970s, A&M Records, headquartered in Los Angeles, was notable for its progressive stance, openly embracing "hippie ideals" and readily signing artists deemed weird or experimental, such as Captain Beefheart. **Embrace of Hippie Ideals and Experimentation (Late 60s/Early 70s)** : Headquartered in Los Angeles, A&M Records was known for its openness to the prevailing hippie ideals of the late 1960s and early 1970s. The label showed a willingness to sign and indulge experimental and unconventional artists, exemplified by their long-term support for Captain Beefheart despite his eccentricities. **Mainstream & Alt Rock Roster** : While known for mainstream and "middle of the road" acts like The Carpenters, A&M Records also contributed to the world of Alt Rock by signing artists such as OMD, Human League, and The Police. They notably dropped the Sex Pistols after only five days. "Most of their acts were pretty mainstream and pretty middle of the road. This was a label of the Carpenters after all. And the company that dropped the Sex Bistles after just five days." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk ← am-records
**Post-Hit Pressure** : Following the success of Ozark Mountain Daredevils' "Jackie Blue," A&M Records pressured the band to replicate the hit with similar "Jackie Red," "Jackie Green," and "Jackie Brown" songs. The label also pushed for the band to move to Los Angeles for greater TV exposure, requests the band ultimately refused to protect their artistic integrity and family-centric lifestyle in Missouri. "A and M that's our label said to us Jackie Blue was great now give us Jackie red." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 46 - The Chaos and Music of the Ozark Music Festival of 1974 | https://www.ftr70.com/ ← am-records
**Sex Pistols "God Save the Queen" Release and Destruction (1977)** : A&M Records signed the Sex Pistols for a mere five days in 1977, during which time approximately 25,000 copies of their "God Save the Queen" 7-inch single were pressed. Following the band's dismissal due to other artists' objections, these singles were ordered destroyed, though several dozen were "liberated" and became highly valuable collectibles. "But in that five days, A&M had pressed up something like 25,000 copies of God Save the Queen." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zWNzlSxrj6s ← am-records

This event was a significant moment in the band's history before their notoriously brief and tumultuous career. "Sid really got into being a sex pistol.
