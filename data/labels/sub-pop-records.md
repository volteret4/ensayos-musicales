# label - Sub Pop Records

## curiosities
**Financial State in 1989** : In 1989, when Nirvana recorded their debut album "Bleach," Sub Pop Records out of Seattle was described as "perpetually broke." "The band was a completely non-entity at the time, recording for the perpetually broke subpop records out of Seattle." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tbmssCZ05ro
**Nirvana's First Single Release (1988)** : Sub Pop Records was the label responsible for issuing Nirvana's very first single in October 1988, a release that is now identifiable by unique dead wax inscriptions. "issued on subpop records in October of 1988" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IIJySSo276k
**Singles Club (1989)** : In 1989, Sub Pop Records ran a "Singles Club" that mailed subscribers a random 7-inch single every month. The very first release through this club was Nirvana's "Bleach" single, mailed out on November 1, 1989, with 1,000 copies hand-numbered in red pen on the white center label, becoming highly valuable collectibles. "This was a club where the label mailed you a seven inch single every month, a random seven inch single." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tbmssCZ05ro
