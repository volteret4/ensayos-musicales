**"Next Elvis" Strategy and Production Flaws** : RCA Records signed Roy Orbison, envisioning him as "the next Elvis," and used many of the same session musicians from Elvis's big hits. However, the label's producers failed to understand Orbison's unique voice and style, pushing him to sing pop-country ballads not of his choosing, leading to singles performing worse than his Sun records. **Chet Atkins' Self-Blame** : Chet Atkins, involved in Orbison's RCA recordings, later expressed regret and self-blame for not recognizing Orbison's true potential. He characterized the records as "typical Nashville at that time" and admitted they failed to "reach out and try to do something different" or see "the greatness in him and the quality of his voice." "Chet Atkins, like Sam Phillips, just didn't get Roy Orbison. He would later say, we did some pretty good records, but they were typical Nashville at that time, and we didn't reach out and try to do something different. I blame myself for that. I should have seen the greatness in him and the quality of his voice." ← Episode 42： ＂Ooby Dooby＂ by Roy Orbison and the Teen Kings | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1zvzpXqr-k8
