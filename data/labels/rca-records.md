# label - RCA Records

## curiosities
**"Space Oddity" Demo and Release** : David Bowie demoed "Space Oddity" for executives at RCA Records after the song had been rejected multiple times. RCA ultimately released the song, which became Bowie's first hit. "But then he had a chance to demo it for some people at RCA Records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sPCrATqBdeA
**David Bowie Deal (Early 1970s)** : RCA Records secured a new deal with David Bowie in the early 1970s, approximately 18 months after Tony DeFries signed on as Bowie's manager. This deal was pivotal in launching Bowie as an international star. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**David Bowie's Early Career Label** : David Bowie was with RCA Records during the period when he entered into a problematic management arrangement with Tony DeFrees, and he remained with them even after parting ways with DeFrees in 1975. "He was still with RCA records, but he had left DeFrees behind in 1975." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rw0uXf5UJvo
**Early Recognition of Million-Selling Singles (1956)** : In 1956, RCA Records presented Elvis Presley with a gold record for selling one million copies of his single "Don't Be Cruel." This act by a record label preceded the formalization of sales awards by the RIAA. "In 1956, RCA presented one to Elvis Presley for selling one million copies of his single Don't Be Cruel." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gP3oQeYEvso
**Establishment of Nashville Studio** : Ethel Gabriel worked with RCA to establish their renowned studio in Nashville, playing a key role in developing the label's presence in a vital music hub. Her involvement extended beyond production into infrastructure development and the label's strategic growth. "She worked with RCA to establish their famous studio as a Nashville." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
**Introduction of the 45 RPM Single (1949)** : RCA Records unveiled the 7-inch, 45 RPM single in March 1949, serving as its direct response to Columbia Records' introduction of the LP. "Texarkana Baby" by Eddie Arnold was among the initial 45s released. "RCA Records responded to Columbia's LP with the 7 inch 45 RPM single." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gP3oQeYEvso
**Long-standing Female Producer** : For nearly four decades, Ethel Gabriel was the only woman producer at RCA Records, a period spanning from the late 1950s. She produced over 2,500 albums and earned at least 15 gold records during her extensive career with the label, making her a formidable figure in music production. "And for nearly four decades, she was the only woman producer at RCA Records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
**Pioneering Stereo and Disco Releases** : RCA Records was at the forefront of technical innovation with Ethel Gabriel's involvement in the first stereophonic recordings, including supervising Bing Crosby's first stereo session. The label also embraced new genres, releasing its first disco records with Gabriel's contributions, demonstrating forward-thinking in both technology and genre. "She even supervised the first-ever stereo recording session for Bing Crosby, and she was involved with RCA's first-ever disco releases." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
**Reissue of The Shags' *Philosophy of the World*** : RCA Records reissued The Shags' album *Philosophy of the World* in 1999 and has kept it in print ever since. This action reflects the album's legendary status and enduring cult following within outsider music circles, making it widely accessible to new listeners. "The cult of the shags is now so big that RCA records reissued Philosophy of the World in 1999, and have kept it in print ever since." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c8CD_FcWcg8
**Response to David Bowie's Album Cover Controversy (1971)** : RCA Records had to intervene when *The Man Who Sold the World* album, featuring David Bowie in a dress on its original cover, was refused by many American record stores in 1971. To address this sales impediment, the label supplied an alternative cover. "And many record stores refused to stock the album until RCA records supplied them with a different cover." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2RKchFW42Jc
**Scrapped Album Project** : In early 1978, Joy Division attempted to record an album for RCA Records, but the project "didn't work out" and was entirely scrapped before any significant material could be completed or released. "They tried recording an album for RCA Records in early 1978, but that didn't work out, and the whole thing was scrapped before anything significant was accomplished." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1VWJ0uCQx8w
