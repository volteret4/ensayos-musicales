# label - Sire Records

## curiosities
**"Don't Call It Punk" Campaign** : Recognizing that punk carried too much negative baggage in North America, Sire Records launched a marketing campaign for its new signings under the banner "Don't Call It Punk." They consciously rebranded these acts as "New Wave" to make them more acceptable to American audiences. "Sire launched a marketing campaign for these new signings under the banner, don't call it punk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
**Champion of New Music** : Seymour Stein and Sire Records were pivotal in supporting new music, particularly post-punk and new wave, in North America. Stein loved the new sound and signed bands like The Ramones, The Dead Boys, and The Talking Heads. "Which is where we meet Seymour Stein and his people at Sire Records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
**Founded by Seymour Stein** : Sire Records was founded by Seymour Stein, a legendary record executive. Under his leadership, the label signed highly influential artists and was responsible for bringing significant international talent to North America. "He's the founder of Sire Records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
**Key Signings and Imports** : Seymour Stein, as founder of Sire Records, was responsible for signing seminal acts like The Ramones and The Talking Heads. He also gave Madonna her "first proper record deal" and imported "unknown English bands" such as The Smiths, The Pretenders, and The English Beat into the North American market. "He's the guy who signed the Ramones and the Talking Heds. He's the guy who gave Madonna her first proper record deal. He's the guy that imported unknown English bands like The Smiths and The Pretenders and The English Beat into North America." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
**Marketing New Wave (1976)** : Facing radio resistance to "punk rock," Sire Records, under Seymour Stein, initiated a marketing campaign around Christmas 1976 urging the use of "New Wave" to describe their new signings, like the Ramones. This strategy successfully removed the punk stigma and propelled the term into mainstream acceptance. "So Sire launched a NAD campaign called Don't Call It Punk, urging people to call this new music New Wave." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**North American Distributor for The Smiths** : Sire Records was responsible for distributing The Smiths' music in North America, playing an important role in the band's reach beyond the UK. "The Smiths, with this charming man released on Rough Trade in the UK and distributed North America by Sire Records, a very important part of our history of indie music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Signing New Wave Talent** : Sire Records played a crucial role in the early New Wave scene, with its head, Seymour Stein, being an ardent supporter of the bands emerging from CBGB, leading to signings like The Ramones and The Pretenders. "The head of the company, a guy named Seymour Stein, was a fan of what was happening at CBGB and had signed a bunch of these new bands, including the Ramones." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**Signing of The Ramones** : Seymour Stein, the head of Sire Records, was interested in the Ramones after hearing their demos and seeing them perform at Mothers, a gay bar on 23rd Street, in October 1975. This led to a record deal where the band signed with Sire for $6,000. "Negotiations for a record deal resulted in the band signing to Sire for $6,000." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=j8uk7BnbXwU
