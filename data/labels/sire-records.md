# label - Sire Records

## curiosities
**"Don't Call It Punk" Marketing Campaign (Late 1970s)** : Launched a deliberate marketing campaign to promote its new signings under the banner "Don't Call It Punk," explicitly defining them as American New Wave to circumvent punk's negative baggage in North America. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
**Founded by Seymour Stein** : Sire Records was founded by Seymour Stein, who was married to Linda Stein (co-manager of the Ramones) in the late 1970s. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**Key Artist Signings** : Seymour Stein, as the founder of Sire Records, was responsible for signing numerous influential artists including the Ramones, Talking Heads, The Pretenders, and Madonna. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**Marketing Strategy** : Headed by Seymour Stein, the label struggled to get punk acts like The Ramones on American radio because the term "punk" was perceived as dangerous and violent. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**North American Distribution** : Served as the distributor for The Smiths' music in North America. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Seminal Signings** : Sire Records is known for signing influential artists such as The Ramones and Talking Heads, and for giving Madonna her first proper record deal. It also played a role in importing English bands like The Smiths, The Pretenders, and The English Beat to North America. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
**Seymour Stein's Vision** : Label head Seymour Stein loved the new music emerging from punk and post-punk scenes, actively signing acts like The Ramones, The Dead Boys, The Talking Heads, The Pretenders, The Undertones, and Echo & The Bunnymen. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
**The "Don't Call It Punk" Campaign** : Around Christmas 1976, Sire launched an advertising campaign to rebrand their artists as "New Wave" to wipe away the stigma of punk and gain mainstream radio access. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
