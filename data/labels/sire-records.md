**"Don't Call It Punk" Campaign** : Recognizing that punk carried too much negative baggage in North America, Sire Records launched a marketing campaign for its new signings under the banner "Don't Call It Punk." They consciously rebranded these acts as "New Wave" to make them more acceptable to American audiences. **Marketing New Wave (1976)** : Facing radio resistance to "punk rock," Sire Records, under Seymour Stein, initiated a marketing campaign around Christmas 1976 urging the use of "New Wave" to describe their new signings, like the Ramones. This strategy successfully removed the punk stigma and propelled the term into mainstream acceptance. "So Sire launched a NAD campaign called Don't Call It Punk, urging people to call this new music New Wave." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**North American Distributor for The Smiths** : Sire Records was responsible for distributing The Smiths' music in North America, playing an important role in the band's reach beyond the UK. "The Smiths, with this charming man released on Rough Trade in the UK and distributed North America by Sire Records, a very important part of our history of indie music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Pioneering "New Wave" Marketing (Early 1977)** : Sire Records invented the term "New Wave" as a marketing strategy in early 1977, launching a campaign called "Don't Call it Punk." This aimed to re-brand British punk rock into a more palatable and commercially viable genre for the North American market, fostering new opportunities for bands like The Pretenders, Depeche Mode, Elvis Costello, Echo & the Bunnymen, The Cure, and The Smiths. "And before the company was bought by Warner and ceased to be an indie label, someone in the office invented a new term." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
**Signing New Wave Talent** : Sire Records played a crucial role in the early New Wave scene, with its head, Seymour Stein, being an ardent supporter of the bands emerging from CBGB, leading to signings like The Ramones and The Pretenders. "The head of the company, a guy named Seymour Stein, was a fan of what was happening at CBGB and had signed a bunch of these new bands, including the Ramones." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**Signing of The Ramones** : Seymour Stein, the head of Sire Records, was interested in the Ramones after hearing their demos and seeing them perform at Mothers, a gay bar on 23rd Street, in October 1975.
