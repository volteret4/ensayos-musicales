They consciously rebranded these acts as "New Wave" to make them more acceptable to American audiences. This strategic rebranding proved successful in "wiping away" the stigmas and suspicions attached to punk, thereby allowing bands like Talking Heads and Blondie to gain radio play and public acceptance. "And so it came to pass that at a marketing meeting at Sire Records, someone suggested that they use the term new wave in place of punk to describe their new signings.
