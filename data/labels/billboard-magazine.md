# label - Billboard magazine

## curiosities
**Continued Relevance and Name Retention** : Billboard magazine remains one of the most crucial and influential sources for music and industry information globally today. It retained its original name throughout its evolution, reflecting its historical roots in covering "billboards" as a primary advertising medium. "Billboard still exists today and is considered to be one of the most important and influential sources for music and industry information in the world. And through it all, it kept its original name because back in the day, Billboard was really all about Billboard." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TsiYLGGSJ2U
**Evolution into Entertainment and Music (Early 1900s)** : The magazine's coverage expanded from general advertising to county fairs, circuses, vaudeville, and the gossip generated by these entertainment industries, thereby entering the entertainment sector. In the early 1900s, with the rise of sales of photographs and pre-recorded discs, "Billboard Advertising" began tracking which records were selling most, covering the jukebox industry, and later, radio airplay. "From there, the magazine moved on to covering county fairs, circuses, vaudeville, and Superlux show, and the kind of advertising those things did and the gossip those industries generated. This got the magazine into the entertainment industry. But then in the early 1900s, when sales of photographs and pre-recorded discs and records began to take off, Billboard Advertising started taking an interest in that." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TsiYLGGSJ2U
**First Music Chart and Industry Leadership (1936-1950s)** : By the time of the Great Depression, Billboard was predominantly focused on music. It published its first music chart on January 4, 1936, followed by additional charts ranking America's best-selling records. By the 1950s, Billboard had become the definitive industry leader in tracking music sales. "And then on January 4th, 1936, the magazine published its first ever music chart. That was followed by more charts which ranked the best selling records in America. And by the 1950s, Billboard was the industry leader when it came to tracking the sales of music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TsiYLGGSJ2U
**Origin of Name and Founding (1894)** : Billboard magazine was established in 1894, years before the modern music industry, originally named "Billboard Advertising." Its initial purpose was to cover outdoor advertising, including the posting of bills, flyers, posters, and billboards, which were the primary means of advertising before radio and television. "Its original name was Billboard Advertising, and its first job was covering advertising and the posting of bills." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TsiYLGGSJ2U
