**Benchmark for Electronic Sound** : Warp Records is referenced as a benchmark for classic British electronic music, particularly in the IDM genre. John Frusciante's 2013 electronic album, "Outsides," was described as sounding almost like a Warp release, aligning him with the label's aesthetic and influential artists such as Autechre, Aphex Twin, and Clark. **Central to IDM Development** : Warp Records was the central label in the development of the EDM (IDM) genre. It continued to expand the "Artificial Intelligence" series by releasing new compilations and albums by key artists like Black Dog Productions, Aotecre, and Affextwing (under his Polygon Window alias), among others. **New Beginning with "Artificial Intelligence" (1992)** : The release of the "Artificial Intelligence" compilation in 1992 marked a significant new beginning for Warp Records and for post-rave electronic music as a whole, pivoting towards a more contemplative and intellectual listening experience. "Pero no sería hasta el recoplatorio artificial Intelligence, donde marcaría a un nuevo comienzo para ellos, y para la música electrónica post-Reif." ← Qué fue el IDM y por qué cambió la música electrónica | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RTwn3hP1XTg
**Pioneering Deep Listening** : Warp Records played a pivotal role in elevating electronic music, which originated in the rave scenes, into more experimental and profound listening territories. It transformed the perception of this music beyond its purely functional role for dancing.
