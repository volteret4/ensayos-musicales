# instrument - Mixing Console

## curiosities
**Dedicated RCA Inputs** : Mixing consoles typically feature distinct RCA inputs on their rear panel to accommodate different audio sources. One set of inputs is designated for vinyl turntables, while another is provided for various other formats like CDJs, computers, synthesizers, or samplers. This distinction highlights the unique preamplification requirement of turntable signals. "si os fijáis en las mesas de mezclas detrás para conectar los cables se reciá de mes dos entradas una es la entrada de vinilo y la otra es la entrada cualquier otro formato un cdj, un ordenador, un sintetizador, una corplo que queráis." ← Music Radar Clan > Preamplificador： toda la información | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aMllStNDBWg&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**Ground Connection Point** : Alongside the dedicated inputs for turntables, mixing consoles commonly include a grounding post or "roda de citas para tomar de tierra." This connection is essential for minimizing hum and noise that can arise from grounding issues when connecting turntables. **Integral to Experimental Scratching** : The mixing console is used in conjunction with turntables for experimental scratching techniques. Yeop Barrow, for instance, demonstrated his ability to create complex and innovative sounds using only a turntable and a mixing console during live performances. **Integrated Turntable Preamps** : Many mixing consoles are equipped with built-in preamplifiers specifically for turntables. When turntables are connected to such a mixer, the mixer itself provides the necessary preamplification for the audio signal. This means an external preamplifier is not required in such setups.
