# instrument - Guitar

## curiosities
**Alex Lifeson's Influence on Dave Grohl** : Dave Grohl admits to "ripping off" Alex Lifeson's guitar playing from Rush, particularly "sustained notes that sort of ring through a riff pattern," in many Foo Fighters songs.
**Current Underrepresentation in Alt-Rock** : some argue that there are "too few big guitar bands" in alt-rock today, indicating a shift away from its past dominance.
**Dominance in Post-Grunge Alt-Rock** : after grunge, alt-rock began to coalesce around "hard guitar rock," with "big guitars" becoming a primary focus.
**Drop-Tuning in Grunge** : many grunge and "grunge-ish" guitar-based bands utilized drop-tuning.
**Jangly Style of C86** : Jangly guitar-based songs were a defining characteristic of the sound represented on the C86 cassette collection.
**Jeff Buckley's Early Start** : Jeff Buckley picked up a guitar at age five.
**Jeff Buckley's First Electric Guitar** : Jeff Buckley got his first electric guitar at age 12, influenced by prog rock bands.
**Johnny Marr's Style** : Johnny Marr's guitar playing for The Smiths was noted for being both fresh and familiar.
**My Bloody Valentine's Noise** : My Bloody Valentine innovated by pushing guitar noise and "walls of sound" to new and extreme levels, influencing many subsequent bands.
**Punk Rock Accessibility** : The simplicity of three-chord guitar playing in punk rock made it accessible to aspiring musicians who might not have had the virtuosic skills of figures like Jimmy Page or Peter Frampton.
**Scott Ian's Damaged Pedals (Anthrax, 1980s)** : During an Anthrax show in Denver, stage divers messed up guitarist Scott Ian's effects pedals, an incident that contributed to the inspiration for the song "Caught in a Mosh."
**Tim Buckley's Possession at Death** : Tim Buckley owned only his clothes, a guitar, and an amplifier at the time of his death.
