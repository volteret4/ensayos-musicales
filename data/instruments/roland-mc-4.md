# instrument - Roland MC-4

## curiosities
**Early Useful Sequencer (1978)** : Roland introduced the MC-4 in 1978, which was one of the first truly useful sequencers. It could store approximately 12,000 notes, though they had to be entered one by one. Once programmed, the data was stored on a cassette tape. Despite being "absolute hell to operate," it allowed for the sequencing of synthesizer events without further human intervention once started, enabling groups like The Human League to perform live outside the studio. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
