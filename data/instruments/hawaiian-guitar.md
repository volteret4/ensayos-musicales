# instrument - Hawaiian guitar

## curiosities
**Motivation for Amplification** : Early Hawaiian guitars, made by Adolf Rickenbacher and George Beauchamp, were too quiet for wider audiences. This lack of volume spurred Rickenbacher and Beauchamp to seek methods of amplification, leading to the development of the electric guitar. "The problem though was that they were too quiet. If anyone was going to hear these new fangal things, they had to be louder." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V7WjNR1ujhk
**Origins from Spanish Guitar (Early 1900s)** : The Hawaiian guitar evolved from the Spanish guitar, which was introduced to Hawaii by Spanish and Mexican sailors before the US annexation in 1893. Native Hawaiians repurposed it, preferring to play it vertically rather than flat on the lap. By 1915, the "Hawaiian guitar" was gaining popularity in America. "The native Hawaiians repurposed the Spanish guitar, preferring to play it vertically as we do today, rather than laying flat in your lap. By 1915, the now renamed Hawaiian guitar was all the rage in certain sectors of America." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V7WjNR1ujhk
