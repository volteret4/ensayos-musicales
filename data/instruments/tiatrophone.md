# instrument - Tiatrophone

## curiosities
**Decline due to Radio** : The Tiatrophone service lasted until 1931, but its eventual demise was caused by the advent of radio (referred to as "the wireless" in Britain). Radio offered music transmission without the need for extensive wiring to individual homes, making it a more convenient and accessible technology. "This lasted a long time, too, all the way to 1931, in fact, and the thing that killed it, radio, which required no wires running all over the place." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VnXBJqKwkrw
**Early Wired Music Streaming (1881)** : Invented by Clement Adler and demonstrated in 1881, the Tiatrophone was a system that used 80 telephone transmitters placed around Parisian theater stages to transmit live symphony and opera performances via telephone lines to private homes for a subscription fee. It even offered a form of stereo sound, 70 years before stereo was widely adopted. "In 1881, a French engineer named Clement Adler demonstrated a system called the Tiatrophone at the International Exhibition of Electricity in Paris." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VnXBJqKwkrw
**Expansion and Commercial Use** : Following its debut, Tiatrophone services expanded across Europe, appearing in the UK as the "Electrophone" and even demonstrated in New York. It was also installed in public venues like restaurants and clubs, where for 50 centimes (50 cents), listeners could enjoy five minutes of music through ear tubes. "Tiatrophones were also installed in restaurants and lounges and private clubs, where for 50 times, which was around 50 cents, you got five minutes of listening through a couple of tubes that you stuck in your ears." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VnXBJqKwkrw
