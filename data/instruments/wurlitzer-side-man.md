# instrument - Wurlitzer Side Man

## curiosities
**First Promising Rhythm Device (1950s)** : The Wurlitzer Side Man, introduced in the 1950s, was a standalone rhythm box that utilized vacuum tubes and a clock device to produce bass drum, snare, tom-tom, and cymbal sounds, with tempos ranging from 34 to 150 beats per minute. Priced at $400 (approximately $4,000 today), it was considered the first rhythm-making device to demonstrate significant potential. "But it was the first rhythm-making device that showed potential." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dablAKDOOV0
**Influence on Home Organs** : The Side Man's concept heavily influenced home organs, which often incorporated similar internal rhythm sections with pre-programmed rhythms like Bossa Nova, Foxtrot, Tango, Waltz, Swing, Mambo, Cha-Cha, Samba, and sometimes a "rock and roll" button.
