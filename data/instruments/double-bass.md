# instrument - Double Bass

## curiosities
**Acoustic Amplification Challenge** : While double basses added a desirable "thud" to early rock and roll songs, they remained acoustic instruments. Amplifying them effectively was tricky with the technology available at the time, posing a significant hurdle for musicians. "Double basses added some nice thud to early rock and roll songs, but they were still acoustic. They needed to be amplified somehow, which was tricky with the technology of the day." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Disappearance in the 1950s** : The large double bass largely disappeared throughout the 1950s, being replaced by the relatively tiny Fender Precision Bass. This shift was driven by the Precision Bass's superior ease of transport, playability, amplification, and modification. "Throughout the 1950s, the big double bass disappeared, and it was replaced with a relatively tiny Fender Precision, which was easier to transport, play, amplify, and modify." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Predecessor to Electric Bass** : The Double Bass, a large classical instrument dating back hundreds of years and often played with a bow, began replacing the tuba in bands in the 1920s to provide low-end frequencies. It offered greater carrying power and blended more effectively than the tuba. "In the 1920s, a very large classical instrument called the Double Base, an instrument that dates back hundreds of years and was most often played with a bow, started to be used in place of the tuba." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Superiority over Tuba (1926)** : According to a Billboard magazine article from April 1926, the string bass, also known as bull fiddle or doghouse, was replacing the tuba in prominent New York bands. Leaders agreed it had far greater carrying power and blended more effectively, despite the inconvenience of larger instrument cases. "All over New York, it has been noticed that with most of the prominent bands, the string bass, alias bull fiddle, alias doghouse, is replacing the tuba. Leaders agree that the string bass has a far greater carrying power than the tuba, and that it blends more effectively." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
