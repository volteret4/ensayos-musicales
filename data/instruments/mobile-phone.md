**Nokia 2110 (1994) - Ringtone Pioneer** : In 1994, the Nokia 2110 introduced a revolutionary feature: several selectable melodies as phone tones, including "Type 7" (an extract of Francisco Tárrega's "Gran Vals"), which would later become the "Nokia Tune." This marked the beginning of technological personalization and transformed the phone from an anonymous tool into an extension of personality. **Nokia 3210 (1999) - Integrated Composer** : Launched in 1999, the Nokia 3210, along with the later Nokia 3310 (2000), included a crucial personalization tool: an integrated composer. This allowed users to create their own ringtones, utilizing a protocol that enabled the exchange of small data packets, such as musical scores, via SMS. **Nokia 3310 (2000) - Integrated Composer** : The "indestructible" Nokia 3310, launched in 2000, like its predecessor the 3210, featured an integrated composer that allowed users to create custom ringtones. **Polyphonic Sound Enhancement - Mid-2000s** : With the advent of polyphonic ringtones, mobile phones significantly improved their sound chips. Unlike earlier monophonic phones that could only play one channel, these new devices could play several channels simultaneously, making music sound "something more real" and greatly amplifying the ringtone phenomenon.
