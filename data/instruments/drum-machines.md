# instrument - Drum Machines

## curiosities
**Early Basic Models (Early Days)** : The very first drum machines were simple, non-programmable units, often integrated with home organs. They offered basic preset rhythms like "bossa nova" or "tango" but lacked versatility. "We used to have an old drum machine right in the early days that was just the sort of thing that you used to have with your home organ and it just repressed my shit and it bossaed over one, bossaed over two, tango, etc." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0fQQubt9UTI
**Programmable Models and Distinctive Sounds (Late 1970s)** : The Roland CR-78 was an early programmable drum machine, though complicated to operate. It allowed for the creation of distinctive intro patterns, such as the one famously heard in OMD's "Enola Gay." "But the first programmable drum machine we got is most famously probably known for the intro to Enola Gay. The roll and CR78. You could program that." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0fQQubt9UTI
**Technological Advancements (Early 1980s)** : Drum machines were among the new electronic technologies that emerged in the early 1980s, contributing to the expanded sonic palette of post-punk and genres like Dreampop, enabling more complex music production. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFLMkvJAaUY
