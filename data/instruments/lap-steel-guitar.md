# instrument - Lap Steel Guitar

## curiosities
**Hawaiian Origin and Development** : In the late 1800s, Spanish guitars brought to Hawaii by European and Mexican sailors were repurposed by locals. They re-tuned the guitars to produce a major chord with a simple strum, replaced cat-gut strings with metal ones, and used a metal bar or pipe slid along the strings. This playing style, difficult with a vertical guitar, led players to lay the instruments flat on their laps, giving rise to what became known as Hawaiian guitars and, later, lap steel guitars. Interest in these instruments significantly grew into the 1920s, attracting companies like George Beechum's. "By the late 1800s, European and Mexican sailors had brought Spanish guitars to Hawaii. The locals repurposed that guitar, tuning it so that a simple strum across the strings would produce a major chord without the need for messing around on the fretboard. And instead of using cat-cut, they used metal strings. The changed sounds, a metal bar or a piece of pipe, was slid along the strings. And since this was hard to do while holding the guitar vertically, players laid these Hawaiian guitars flatter on their lap.
