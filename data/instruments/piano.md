## curiosities
**Elvis Presley's Unexpected Skill (1956)** : During the Million Dollar Quartet session, Elvis Presley played the piano "perfectly decent[ly]" for the first part of the session, despite claiming not to be able to play the instrument. His casual noodling initially prompted Jerry Lee Lewis to try and displace him. **Jerry Lee Lewis's Boogie Style (1956)** : Jerry Lee Lewis, known as a Louisiana piano player influenced by Moon Mulligan, immediately launched into a boogie piano part upon hearing Carl Perkins sing the lines for "Matchbox." This spontaneous contribution significantly shaped the song's sound, making it sound "exactly like a Jerry Lee Lewis record" until the guitar solo.
