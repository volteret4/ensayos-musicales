# instrument - Record (Flat Rotating Disc)

## curiosities
**Early Capacity Limitations** : In the nascent days of recorded music, flat discs could hold only about four minutes of audio per side. This limitation meant that longer performances, such as symphonies, had to be fragmented across multiple discs, which ultimately led to the development of the "record album" concept. "Back in the early days of recorded music, discs could only hold about 4 minutes per side and that was pushing it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gP3oQeYEvso
**Invention and First Creation (1889)** : Emil Berliner, the inventor behind the concept of a flat rotating disc, created the very first record of this type in 1889. He recorded himself reciting a German ballad, and although the physical record was later destroyed, its audio was recreated in 1890 by scanning a picture found in a magazine. "Emil Berliner, the inventor that came up with the idea of a flat rotating disc in the first place, was the guy that made the first record as well." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gP3oQeYEvso
