# instrument - Audio amplifier

## curiosities
**DeForest's Invention (1914)** : Lee DeForest invented the audio amplifier, stemming from his earlier work on the triode vacuum tube. By 1912, he had a working triode that could amplify electrical signals, and by 1914, he produced his first prototype of an audio amplifier. This invention became fundamental for boosting signals in telephones, telegraphs, radios, movies, televisions, audio recording, and public address systems. **Early Public Use (December 24, 1915)** : Audio amplifiers were an integral part of the Magnavox public address system first demonstrated at San Francisco City Hall on December 24, 1915. This system, which included a microphone, an amplifier, and a large 34-inch speaker, was used to amplify Christmas music and speeches for a crowd of 100,000 people, showcasing its potential for large-scale sound reinforcement.
