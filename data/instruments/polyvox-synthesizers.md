# instrument - Polyvox Synthesizers

## curiosities
**Soviet Reverse Engineering** : Polyvox synthesizers are examples of old Soviet technology, designed through reverse engineering to approximate Western instruments like the MOOG, without the engineers ever having seen the original. An electronic engineer in Kiev, inspired by the sound of the MOOG, built the Polyvox based solely on theory. The resulting instrument, while "kind of wrong," produced a "kind of good" and cool sound, characterized by its extreme and aggressive filter, and an appearance resembling a Soviet tank's control panel. "But there was an electronic engineer in Kiev I think he was. And he wanted to build a MOOG. I loved the sound of the MOOG. He knew the theory of how a MOOG was built. Had heard what they sound like but had never seen one. And so he built this thing and it looks like the control panel of a Soviet tank." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RWbKqqW78yg
**Used by Franz Ferdinand on "Tonight"** : Franz Ferdinand notably experimented with and incorporated Polyvox synthesizers into the recording of their third album, Tonight, particularly on the song "Live Alone," highlighting its distinct sound.
