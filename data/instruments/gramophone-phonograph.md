# instrument - Gramophone / Phonograph

## curiosities
**Early 20th Century "Evil" (Turn of 20th century)** : Gramophones and phonographs, as new "talking machines" at the turn of the 20th century that played recordings, were denounced by John Philip Sousa as "nothing short of evil." He believed they would lead to the deterioration of American music and taste, and the elimination of the "vocal court." "The new talking machines of the turn of the 20th century, gramophones and phonographs that played recordings were nothing short of evil." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ak6OuOUkRrQ
**Widespread Availability (By 1932)** : By the time John Philip Sousa died in 1932, recorded music, played on gramophones and phonographs, had become ubiquitous. Records were affordable, costing "as little as 30 cents," making music accessible to millions of people who could now experience a song that once only a few hundred might hear live. "But by the time he died in 1932, recorded music was everywhere. You could buy a record for his littleest 30 cents." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ak6OuOUkRrQ
