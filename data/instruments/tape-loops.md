# instrument - Tape loops

## curiosities
**Early Electronic Music Composition Technique** : Tape loops were a composition technique used in early electronic music, notably within Carlheinz Stockhausen's avant-garde studio near Cologne in 1953, contributing to experimental soundscapes. "Oscillators, wave generators, tape loops, white noise composition." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE
**Used by Adrian Sherwood** : Producer Adrian Sherwood incorporated tape loops into his "radical remixes" for Depeche Mode, creating a heavy, distorted, electronically rhythmic sound that deeply influenced Al Jourgensen of Ministry. "He loved the onslaught of electronic rhythm and the use of distortion and tape loops." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE
**Used by Cabaret Voltaire (Early 1970s)** : Cabaret Voltaire began experimenting with tape loops, alongside noise and samples, as early as the beginning of the 1970s in Sheffield, England, demonstrating their pioneering use of these techniques. "Back in England, a group from Sheffield called Cabaret Voltaire had been experimenting with noise, tape loops, and samples since the early 1970s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE
