## curiosities
**Evolution of Live Performance Practice** : Backing tracks, which are pre-recorded audio elements used to supplement live performances, have evolved from an accepted practice for "filling in the sound" (as exemplified by Muse) to a widespread and often controversial phenomenon in pop, hip-hop, and even classic rock shows. Historically viewed as "cheating" and dishonest (e.g., Milli Vanilli, Ashlee Simpson), their modern acceptance is frequently attributed to audience expectations for "perfect studio quality" sound for their concert investment. Artists use them to reduce the need for large numbers of musicians, ensure consistency, synchronize with complex stage effects, and mask vocal deterioration or enable strenuous dancing.
