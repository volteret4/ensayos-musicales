# instrument - Shellac Records

## curiosities
**Brittle Material for Early Records** : Records released around 1930, such as Tommy Johnson's "Alcohol and Jake Blues" on the Paramount label, were made using shellac, a material so brittle that they could "smash like China, even if he just looked at it wrong." "When this was released on the Paramount label in 1930, we were still using Shalak to make records. They were very brittle and it could smash like China, even if he just looked at it wrong." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tbmssCZ05ro
