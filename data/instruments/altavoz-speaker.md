**Non-Symmetrical Cabinet Design for Resonance Control** : High-quality speakers frequently feature non-symmetrical cabinet designs with inclined sides and irregular internal shapes. This construction is vital for minimizing internal sound resonances, as it prevents sound waves from repeatedly reflecting between parallel surfaces (a "ping-pong" effect). This design improves sound dispersion and reduces unwanted coloration, especially in the bass frequencies. **Speaker Power Measurement (RMS)** : Speaker power is commonly quantified in three ways: power per individual speaker (combining bass and treble power), total power of the entire system, and RMS (Root Mean Square). RMS indicates the average power output during real-world use, accounting for the non-uniform power emission across various frequencies and music types. An RMS value exceeding 50 watts is generally considered adequate for home audio use. **Wooden Cabinet Importance for Sound Quality** : The material of the speaker cabinet significantly impacts sound quality, with wooden cabinets being superior to plastic. Wood offers better resonance characteristics, preventing the hollow, "egg-shell" sound often associated with plastic enclosures. Even more affordable agglomerated wood cabinets are preferable to plastic, while higher-end options use veneered or multi-layered wood for enhanced performance.
