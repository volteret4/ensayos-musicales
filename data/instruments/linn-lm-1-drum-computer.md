# instrument - Linn LM-1 Drum Computer

## curiosities
**Accidental Quantization Feature** : Through "accidental ghosts in the machine," the LM-1 unintentionally introduced the concept of quantization, where beats are automatically corrected to be in perfect time. "And through some accidental ghosts in the machine, the LM1 introduced the concept of quantization, where a beat is automatically corrected to be in perfect time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dablAKDOOV0
**First Digital Sample-Based Drum Machine (1980)** : Released in 1980 by Roger Linn, the LM-1 Drum Computer was groundbreaking as the first drum machine to use digital samples of real drums, and it was fully programmable. It initially sold for $5,000, which is equivalent to more than $20,000 today. "In 1980, he released the Lynn LM1 drum computer. It sold for $5,000. The equivalent to more than $20,000 today. But it was the first drum machine to use digital samples of real drums. Plus, it was programmable." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dablAKDOOV0
**Included Sounds and Features** : The LM-1 offered a dozen sampled drum and percussion sounds, including snare, bass, tom-toms, cowbells, and handclaps provided by members of Tom Petty and the Heartbreakers. Operators could change the pitch of each sound and delay notes by a fraction of a second to mimic human drumming nuances. "A dozen drum sounds were selected, snare, bass, tom-toms, cowbells, and a few other percussion sounds, and handclaps. They came courtesy of the guys in Tom Petty and the heartbreakers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dablAKDOOV0
**Limited Production and High Demand** : Only 525 units of the LM-1 were built, with demand far exceeding the supply. "Only 525 of these things were built." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dablAKDOOV0
**Sampling Process** : Studio drummer Art Wood was recorded repeatedly hitting his drum kit in a closet at Linn's house to create the samples. An old Slingerland snare drum from the 1930s was used for one of the snare sounds. Due to memory limitations, closed hi-hats were favored over cymbals because of their shorter decay. "Lynn put wood in a closet at his house and had him hit the various parts of his real drum kit over and over and over again. One of the snare sounds, for example, was an old slinger Lynn from the 1930s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dablAKDOOV0
