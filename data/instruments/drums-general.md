# instrument - Drums (General)

## curiosities
**Historical Consolidation from Multiple Players** : In the "modern era" prior to the late 19th century, various drum-related percussion instruments were played by different individuals; for example, one person played the bass drum, another the cymbals, and a separate player handled the snare drum. This multi-person approach is still visible in today's marching bands. The desire to consolidate these roles into a single player drove early innovation in drum kit design. "For the modern era, the various drum-related percussion instruments were played by different people." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WL3zEWJJNuw
**Oldest Instrument and Basic Construction** : Technically classified as membranophones, drums are believed to be humankind's oldest instrument, requiring only a stick and a rock to produce rhythm. The first proper drums emerged around 6,000 BC and were used for rituals, religious ceremonies, marching armies, and music. Drums fundamentally consist of a membrane (skin) stretched over an open-ended cylinder (shell), which is then struck with a hand or stick. "Technically, drums are known as membrane of phones and are probably humankind's oldest instrument." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WL3zEWJJNuw
**Post-Mid-60s Incremental Evolution** : Since the mid-1960s, the evolution of the modern drum kit has been primarily incremental. Changes have largely focused on cosmetic alterations, the introduction of new materials, and different arrangements of standard components, rather than fundamental design shifts. "Everything that's happened since the mid 60s has been incremental in the way drums are made and played." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WL3zEWJJNuw
