# instrument - Flute

## curiosities
**Ancient Origin of Music** : In 2009, archaeologists discovered four flutes made out of animal bones in a cave in Germany. These instruments, estimated to be 40,000 years old, were of different sizes and produced distinct tones, suggesting they were tuned differently and likely intended for playing together. This discovery highlights the ancient presence of music in human history. "Back in 2009, archaeologists searching through a cave in Germany found four flutes made out of animal bones." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1HMW9rGtJHI
**Neanderthal Bone Flute Discovery** : An ancient flute made from the femur of a bear was discovered in Slovenia. This flute, featuring four holes corresponding to the notes Do, Re, Mi, and Fa of the modern diatonic scale, was crafted by a Neanderthal and could be up to 82,000 years old. This discovery suggests music creation predates Homo sapiens. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BHN6db7m3DM
**Sample Source for "Sure Shot"** : A flute sample from Jeremy Steig's 1970 instrumental "Howlin' For Judy" became the central element of the Beastie Boys' 1994 song "Sure Shot." The unauthorized use of this sample led to significant legal issues for the Beastie Boys. "The Beastie Boys got into trouble with Sure Shot from the Il Communication album in 1994, and the issue is the flute." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fS8V3kYXT6w
