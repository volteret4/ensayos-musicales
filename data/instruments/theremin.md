## curiosities
**Early Electronic Music Device** : Invented by Bolshevik scientist Leon Theremin during the Russian Revolution, this device utilized electric currents and magnetic fields to generate sounds, marking an early milestone in electronically generated music. "The roots of electronically generated music goes far back as the Russian Revolution, when a full-shevik scientist named Leo Thereman introduced a device that used electric currents and magnetic fields to generate sounds." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
**Rhythmicon Development (1930s)** : In the 1930s, Leon Thereman also contributed to the development of the Rhythmicon, an early device designed to keep the beat. Although considered "pretty cool," it was ultimately deemed "way too complicated" for practical use. "This is an issue that goes back to at least the 1930s when Leon Thereman, the same guy, helped develop a device called the Rhythmicon." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**Touchless Electronic Instrument (1919)** : Invented 20 years after the Singing Arc by Soviet physicist Leon Thereman, the Theremin is a unique electronic musical instrument played without physical touch. Coils within the box create an electrical field, and waving one hand through this invisible field produces a tone, while waving the other hand in a different spot changes its pitch. When played skillfully, it produces a warbling sound reminiscent of a female opera singer, famously featured in the sci-fi film *The Day the Earth Stood Still*. "Then, 20 years later, a Soviet physicist named Leon Thereman invented an electronic musical instrument that could be played without being touched." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
