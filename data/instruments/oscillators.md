# instrument - Oscillators

## curiosities
**Component of Early Electronic Music** : Oscillators were a key component used in early electronic music studios, such as Carlheinz Stockhausen's studio near Cologne in 1953, where they were integral to the avant-garde scene's experimentation with sound generation. "Oscillators, wave generators, tape loops, white noise composition." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE
**Exploration of Sonic Possibilities (Late 1960s)** : In the late 1960s, artists like CAN and Tangerine Dream began to explore the "sonic possibilities of oscillators and the other fiddly bits of electronics," using them to generate otherworldly sounds. "And other people began to explore the sonic possibilities of oscillators and the other fiddly bits of electronics that generated other worldly sounds." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE
**Wonky Behavior of Mini-Moog Oscillators** : The Mini-Moog's distinctive sound was partly due to its three oscillators, devices that control voltage in sound, not syncing up properly. This "wonky" behavior, while a technical imperfection, serendipitously enabled the Mini-Moog to produce wild and crazy sounds. "It wasn't very reliable when it came to staying in tune and holding settings because engineers couldn't get the things three oscillators, the devices that controlled voltage in the sound to sync up properly." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
