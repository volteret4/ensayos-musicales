# instrument - Turntables

## curiosities
**DJ and Turntablist Preference** : DJs and turntableist performers preferred vinyl for its tactile nature, finding it easier to mix, scratch, and sample compared to other formats. "DJ culture and turntableist performers preferred vinyl because of its tactile nature." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GES0Ms_kaCI
**Musical Instruments for DJs** : In the 1970s, quick-handed DJs on the streets of New York transformed turntables and mixers into genuine musical instruments. Through a technique called turntableism, they could chop records into sounds and manipulate them to create entirely new music from spare parts, circumventing the need for expensive machines like the Fairlight. "Some DJs became so good that in their hands turntables and mixers were genuine musical instruments that were used to create brand new music out of a series of spare parts without having to spend $30,000 on a fair light machine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**Outselling Guitars (Late 1990s - Early 2000s)** : During the late 1990s and early 2000s, turntables experienced such a surge in popularity, driven by the dominance of hip-hop, dance, and electronica, that they were, at one point, outselling guitars. "turntables were outselling guitars." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q4dEV1yn3OY
**Preservation of Vinyl** : Turntables played a crucial role in keeping vinyl, an otherwise "dead format," alive because DJs preferred them over CDs for their ability to spin, scratch, and beat mix. "Vinyl, which was an otherwise dead format, was kept alive really only by DJs who wanted something other than CDs to spin and scratch and beat mix." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Rise in Sales (Late 90s)** : In the late 1990s, sales of turntables significantly increased and for "a year or two when turntable sales threatened to exceed that of guitars," driven by the explosion of DJ culture and the desire to become a DJ. "Sales of turntables went up. In fact there was a year or two when turntable sales threatened to exceed that of guitars because everybody wanted to be a DJ." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Sales Turnaround** : The sales of turntables, along with vinyl records, began to turn around in the latter half of the first decade of the 21st century. "Bottom line though is that sales of both vinyl records and turntables began to turn around." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GES0Ms_kaCI
**Technology Improvement (Post-2000s)** : Turntable technology has significantly improved, with a good turntable purchased today for around $500 being far superior to an equivalently priced one from 1980. "Turntable technology. It's improved. A good turntable bought today for, say, $500 is far, far superior to a turntable of equivalent value bought in 1980." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GES0Ms_kaCI
**Use in Blink 182 Recording** : For their self-titled 2003 album, Blink 182 incorporated turntables into their recording process for scratching and looping. "They used Polynesian Camel and Bals, turntables for scratching and looping, and old organs and cellos." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tkLRJiOBu0
**Use in Early Hip-hop Sampling** : Turntables became essential tools for DJs in the early days of hip-hop, who used them to loop drum breaks from funk and soul records. This technique was a precursor to digital sampling and was crucial in shaping the genre's sound. "DJs were looking for drum breaks that they could loop on their turntables, the funkier or the break, the better." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VnXBJqKwkrw
