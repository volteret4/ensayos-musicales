**Declining Sales in 2010s** : An alarming drop in electric guitar sales occurred during the 2010s, with a Washington Post report in 2017 stating that sales in the US had fallen by a third to approximately one million per year. This decline led to financial difficulties for major manufacturers like Gibson and Fender and contributed to Guitar Center's bankruptcy. **Feedback Challenges** : Early electric guitars, particularly hollow-body designs, presented challenges with feedback when played at higher volumes. This issue was a driving force behind the development of solid-body and semi-hollow body designs to control unwanted resonant frequencies. "Like I said earlier, making electric guitars had their challenges with feedback." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Invention of the Electric Guitar (1890s-1950s)** : The invention of the electric guitar is attributed to several individuals. George Breed, a US naval officer, laid the groundwork for the guitar pickup in 1890. Lloyd Law, an acoustic guitar designer, patented a "quasi-electric" in 1934. Adolf Rickenbacker's company introduced an electric lap guitar popular with country performers in the mid-1930s. George Bochamp designed the "frying pan," the first electric guitar built in significant numbers. Les Paul created his experimental "log" by cannibalizing guitars in 1941 but couldn't find a manufacturer. Fender began working on a solid-body electric guitar in 1949, releasing the Telecaster two years later, while Gibson issued a Les Paul-endorsed model a year after that. Without this instrument, "rock and roll would have never had a chance." "So who really invented the electric guitar? Like I said, it depends on who you ask, but this much we know. Without it, rock and roll would have never had a chance." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MTDY6qT11Dc
**Post-War Electrification** : After World War II, there was a significant focus on electrifying the guitar due to evolving playing styles and a growing demand for increased volume. This era marked a pivotal shift in how guitars were designed and played. "After the war, there was a fixation on electrifying the guitar. Playing styles had changed, and more importantly, there was now a need for volume." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Standard Six Strings** : A common characteristic of many iconic electric guitars, including the Gibson Les Paul, Gibson ES-335, and Fender Stratocaster, is that they all have six strings. This traditional string configuration is foundational to their design and playability. "The one thing that they have in common is that they each have six strings." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
