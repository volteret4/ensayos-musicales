**Invention of the Electric Guitar (1890s-1950s)** : The invention of the electric guitar is attributed to several individuals. George Breed, a US naval officer, laid the groundwork for the guitar pickup in 1890. Lloyd Law, an acoustic guitar designer, patented a "quasi-electric" in 1934. Adolf Rickenbacker's company introduced an electric lap guitar popular with country performers in the mid-1930s. George Bochamp designed the "frying pan," the first electric guitar built in significant numbers. Les Paul created his experimental "log" by cannibalizing guitars in 1941 but couldn't find a manufacturer. Fender began working on a solid-body electric guitar in 1949, releasing the Telecaster two years later, while Gibson issued a Les Paul-endorsed model a year after that. Without this instrument, "rock and roll would have never had a chance." "So who really invented the electric guitar? Like I said, it depends on who you ask, but this much we know. Without it, rock and roll would have never had a chance." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MTDY6qT11Dc
**Invention of the First True Electric Guitar (1931)** : The Rickenbacker electro guitar, invented in 1931 by George Beauchamp and engineer Adolf Rickenbacker, is recognized as the first true electric guitar. "It was invented by a guy named George Bochamp and an engineer called Adolf Rickenbacker in 1931." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gP3oQeYEvso

**Technological Prerequisites for Amplification** : The development of the electric guitar depended on three key technological advancements becoming widespread and acceptable: widespread availability of electricity by the 1930s, the invention of the vacuum tube by Lee DeForest in 1911 (forming the basis for amplifiers), and the co-development of the first speaker by General Electric and AT&T in 1921. "A couple of things had to happen before George Beachham could make his guitars louder. First of all, electricity had to become both widely available and acceptable everywhere. That happened by the 1930s. Second, someone had to invent some electronics that could take a quiet electrical signal and make it louder. That happened in 1911 when Lee DeForest unveiled a vacuum tube, and that became the foundation for something soon known as an amplifier. And third, someone had to invent something that could turn an electric signal back into sound. And that happened in 1921, when a co-development project between General Electric and AT&T resulted in the first speaker, a paper call that reproduced sound." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=E6cXyaHALBQ
