# instrument - Monitor Speakers

## curiosities
**Disappearance of Traditional Monitors (Post-2000)** : Traditional wedge-shaped monitors and side-fill speakers have "largely disappeared" from concert stages, particularly since around 2000. They have been replaced by wireless in-ear monitors, which offer a more controlled, quieter, and personalized listening experience for performers. "Remember those wedge-shaped monitors and side-fill speakers for performers? They've largely disappeared and have been replaced by what are essentially earbuds." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qrrwqdpCjpQ
**Improvised Stage Monitors (1965)** : During The Beatles' notoriously poor-sounding Shea Stadium concert in 1965, the band couldn't hear themselves. In a desperate, last-minute solution, one of the road crew members "flipped over some of their amplifiers and pointed them directly at the band," effectively creating the earliest form of on-stage monitoring. "As a last second fix, one of the road crew flipped over some of their amplifiers and pointed them directly at the band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qrrwqdpCjpQ
**Loud Stage and Hearing Loss** : The necessity for traditional stage monitors to "overpower the sound of the main PA" meant that the stage itself became an "extremely loud place." This environment significantly contributed to the hearing loss experienced by many musicians throughout their careers. "No wonder so many musicians went deaf." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qrrwqdpCjpQ
**Side-Fill Monitors** : In addition to wedge-shaped floor monitors, some stage setups included "side-fill monitors," which were large speakers positioned on either side of the stage and pointed towards the middle. These ensured that performers "would be no place you could go on stage without hearing yourself," providing comprehensive stage coverage. "Some setups also had side-fill monitors, big speakers and either side of the stage pointing towards the middle so there would be no place you could go on stage without hearing yourself." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qrrwqdpCjpQ
**Wedge-Shaped Stage Monitors (Bill Hanley)** : Bill Hanley was the first to employ "wedge-shaped monitor speakers that fired the music back at the performers." This innovation, inspired by The Beatles' improvised solution at Shea Stadium, allowed musicians to "actually hear what was happening on stage" and avoid confusion from main PA echoes, significantly improving performance quality. "Hanley was the first to employ those wedge-shaped monitor speakers that fired the music back at the performers so they could actually hear what was happening on stage and not get confused by any echoes from the main PA coming back at them." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qrrwqdpCjpQ
