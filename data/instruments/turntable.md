**Technics SL-1200 Introduction (1972)** : Introduced in 1972, the Technics SL-1200 was the successor to the groundbreaking SP-10 direct drive turntable. It quickly became renowned for its power, ease of use, versatility, and "indestructible" nature. The SL-1200, particularly after the introduction of its Mark II version around 1979 which featured a beefier motor and the now-familiar slide-pitch control, became the turntable of choice for DJs worldwide. Its widespread adoption was foundational for the growth of club culture, DJ culture, beat mixing, and the popularization of 12-inch records and extended remixes. Even in the post-turntable digital age, thousands of SL-1200 units are still sold annually, all hand-made in a factory in Osaka, Japan. The introduction of this turntable is considered a pivotal moment, without which figures like Fat Boy Slim might not have emerged. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Somq2MoRi5Q
**Technics SP-10 Introduction (1970)** : The Technics SP-10, introduced in 1970 by Technics (a division of Japan's Panasonic), was the world's first direct drive turntable. Developed by a "shadowy group of engineers and scientists" known as the Special Products Group, this innovation eliminated belts and pulleys, directly driving the platter with a motor. This design significantly reduced "rumble" (low frequency noise) and allowed the platter to reach operating speed much more quickly. It utilized a linear frequency generator to ensure stable speed, which could also be pitched up or down by as much as 8%. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Somq2MoRi5Q
