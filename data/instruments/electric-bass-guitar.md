**Revolutionizing Bass in Rock and Roll (1950s)** : The electric bass guitar, particularly the Precision Bass introduced by Fender in 1951, transformed music by offering a fat, heavy bottom end in a portable, amplified format. As rock and roll gained traction in the 1950s, the electric bass became increasingly popular, gradually pushing the upright double bass out of rock music by 1957 because, as music grew louder, the demand for electric instrumentation increased. By the 1960s, many companies, including those offering six-string models, were producing electric bass guitars, making the instrument indispensable to modern music.
