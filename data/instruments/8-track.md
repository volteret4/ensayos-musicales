# instrument - 8-track

## curiosities
**Invention and Early Adoption (1964-1965)** : The 8-track format was invented in 1964, based on continuous loop magnetic tape technology used in radio stations. It consisted of a continuous loop of magnetic tape housed in a plastic cartridge, allowing for fast forwarding but not rewinding. Ford was the first to integrate 8-track players into their vehicles for the 1965 model year, providing people with the novel ability to enjoy their own music while driving. "The 8-track was invented in 1964 and was based on a technology used in radio stations for playing back songs and commercials and so on. A continuous loop of magnetic tape was placed inside a plastic cartridge." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Epz5Sn151bk
**Last Album Release (1988)** : Fleetwood Mac's "Greatest Hits," released in November 1988, is considered the last album put out by a record company on the 8-track format. "The best answer seems to be Fleetwood Max Greatest Hits, which came out in November of 1988." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Epz5Sn151bk
**Market Penetration and Decline** : Following its introduction, 8-track players and recorders became available for home stereos and as portable units. Record labels rapidly adopted the format, leading to hundreds of millions of 8-tracks being sold. However, it was eventually superseded by the cassette and later the compact disc. While phased out of general record stores in the US and Canada by the end of 1982, 8-tracks remained popular with specific demographics like long-haul truckers, found in truck stops until at least the late 1980s, and maintained by record and tape clubs. "But it was eventually eclipsed first by the cassette and then by the compact disc." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Epz5Sn151bk
