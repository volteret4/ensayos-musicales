# instrument - LP record

## curiosities
**Impact on Soundtrack Industry (1948)** : The introduction of the 33 1/3 RPM LP in the summer of 1948 revolutionized the recorded music industry by allowing up to 22 minutes of audio per side. Movie studios readily adopted this new format, leading to a flood of movie soundtracks and original cast recordings of Broadway shows throughout the late 1940s, 1950s, and into the 1960s. "But all that changed in the summer of 1948, when the 33 and a third RPM LP allowed up to 22 minutes of audio per side." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM
**Introduction and Purpose (June 1948)** : The LP record was introduced in June 1948, developed at least partially at the behest of movie studios and Broadway show producers. They sought a better listening experience than the prevailing 78 RPM records, which were limited to about four minutes per side and required frequent flipping or changing.
