# instrument - Keyboard

## curiosities
**Goth's Primary Instrument** : Goth music primarily employs a keyboard-based approach, drawing influence from early 1980s technopop and smoother modern electronic sounds. "On the other hand, Goth has a keyboard based approach that is more in common with the techno popper of the early 1980s and the smoother flavors of modern electronic than it does with any hard techno." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Industrial Use** : Industrial music utilizes "sharp keyboard sounds of techno" over heavy beats. "industrial relies on guitars and the sharp keyboard sounds of techno over ultra heavy beats." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=evlGhfUe6QQ
**Ostinato in "Dark Horse" Lawsuit** : A keyboard figure, specifically an ostinato (a repeating phrase), was at the center of the plagiarism lawsuit between Katy Perry's "Dark Horse" and Flame's "Joyful Noise." Flame claimed Perry's keyboard ostinato infringed on his, despite differences in tempo and pitch. "He claimed that a keyboard figure, and we call keyboard figures Austinatos, which is a repeating phrase in the musical work, he claimed that this figure in Katie's song infringed on the Austinato in his 2008 Grammy winning track Joyful Noise." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fS8V3kYXT6w
**Traditional Instrument for Learning** : The keyboard is listed as a traditional instrument, alongside guitar and drums, that requires significant time and dedication to learn proficiently, often taking years. This contrasts with modern electronic instruments and software that offer easier ways to create music. "Learning to play a traditional instrument like the guitar or keyboard or drums or whatever takes time and dedication." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Y6nHw5xx88
