# instrument - Wah pedal

## curiosities
**Introduction and Early Adopters** : The wah pedal was introduced to the music market in the mid-1960s. Henry Goldrich, proprietor of Manny's Music in New York, played a pivotal role in its adoption by convincing two prominent guitarists, Jimi Hendrix and Eric Clapton, to purchase the new gizmo. Their enthusiastic reception and subsequent use of the pedal profoundly influenced their playing styles and the overall direction of guitar-based rock and roll. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
**Widespread Musical Impact** : The distinctive sound of the wah pedal quickly became ubiquitous in popular music. It can be heard across countless songs and genres. Notable examples include Kirk Hammett's use throughout Metallica's "Enter Sandman," its presence in many Bob Marley songs, David Gilmour's incorporation into Pink Floyd's material, and Tom Morello's specific application with his wah pedal in full treble position for the riffs in Rage Against the Machine's "Bulls on Parade." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
