# instrument - Multi-track Recording

## curiosities
**Invention and Annoyance to Older Generation (Late 1940s/Early 1950s)** : The invention of multi-track recording, alongside the adoption of magnetic tape, was a significant technological innovation in the late 1940s and early 1950s. This development, which made all modern music possible, greatly annoyed the older generation due to its perceived artificiality and departure from "pure" recording. "It was the adoption of magnetic tape and the invention of multi-track recording." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L0j9iDuHQVA
**Modern Prevalence (200+ Tracks)** : Today, multi-tracking is a ubiquitous method for recording music, with sessions often involving far more than just two or four tracks. For example, a song by Evanescence reportedly utilized over 200 tracks, highlighting the dramatic increase in complexity and layering enabled by this technology. "Now today, multi-tracking and overdubbing is how all music is recorded, and it's not just two or four tracks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L0j9iDuHQVA
