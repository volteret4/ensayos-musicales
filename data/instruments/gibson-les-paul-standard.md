# instrument - Gibson Les Paul Standard

## curiosities
**Humbucking Pickup Innovation** : The Les Paul Standard includes humbucking pickups, which consist of two coils wrapped around a magnet. This design effectively cancels out noisy interference, such as buzz from nearby light fixtures or other electrical devices. "They also include something called a humbucking pickup, which is two coils wrapped around a magnet. This construction cancels out noisy interference, like picking up a buzz from a nearby light fixture or some other electric device." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d9HNqb9gJSU
**Initial Commercial Failure and Resurgence (1960, 1961)** : Despite praise from musicians, the Les Paul Standard was a commercial failure and Gibson ceased production by 1960. However, as rock music gained interest throughout the 1960s, guitarists sought out the standards made between 1958 and 1960, leading Gibson to resume production in late 1961. "But despite the praise from many musicians, the Les Paul Standard was a commercial failure, and by 1960, Gibson stopped making them." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d9HNqb9gJSU
**Legendary Status and Features (1957)** : Introduced in 1957, the Les Paul Standard is where the guitar's legend truly began. These guitars were heavy but produced an "heavenly" sound and featured fantastic "burst" finishes such as sunburst, cherry burst, and tobacco burst. "The next change came in 1957 with the Les Paul Standard. This is where the legend really begins." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d9HNqb9gJSU
**Rarity and Collectibility (1958-1960 models)** : Only about 1700 Les Paul Standards were manufactured between 1958 and 1960, with approximately 700 known to still exist today, making them highly sought after by collectors and extremely expensive. "only about 1700 were ever manufactured, and today only about 700 are known to still exist." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d9HNqb9gJSU
