# instrument - Tape Recorder

## curiosities
**Discovery of Nazi Magnetophones** : At the end of World War II, Major Jack Mullin of the US Army signal corps discovered Nazi-perfected real-to-reel tape recorders, known as Magnetophones, at Radio Frankfurt. These machines were used in radio broadcasts to confuse Allied intelligence about Hitler's location, allowing him to appear to deliver hours-long speeches in multiple cities simultaneously. Mullin was astonished by their high fidelity, which eliminated background noise and made it impossible to discern live sound from playback. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
**Innovations Enabled by Tape Recording** : The adoption of real-to-reel tape recording technology, pioneered by Jack Mullin and enabled by Bing Crosby's investment in Ampex, revolutionized recording. It allowed for performances to be started, stopped, and restarted, with easy editing using razor blades. Mistakes could be excised, and segments could be reordered. This new flexibility laid the groundwork for all modern recording studio techniques. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
**Multi-track Recording Invention** : Les Paul received an early production unit of the tape recording machine from Ampex. He modified these machines to allow for multi-track recording, which made overdubs possible. This innovation meant that individual parts of a recording could be redone without affecting other tracks, and the volume of each track could be controlled separately. Special effects like reverb and echo could also be added after the initial recording. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
**Tape's Broader Impact Beyond Music** : The same tape recording technology that revolutionized music production also made videotape possible. This extended its transformative impact to television and, subsequently, to home entertainment, fundamentally changing how visual and audio content was created and consumed. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
