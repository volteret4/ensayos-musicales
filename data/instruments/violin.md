# instrument - Violin

## curiosities
**Max Matthews' Use with IBM 704 (1957)** : In 1957, acoustics researcher Max Matthews at Bell Labs hooked up his violin to an IBM 704 mainframe to capture its tones. This experiment was instrumental in the development of his "Music" synthesis program, which became foundational for computer-generated music. "He hooked up his violin and got the 704 to capture some of its tones." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QFtS8fl553Y
**Mention in Screaking Weasel Song** : The instrument "violin" is mentioned in a song by Screaking Weasel about compact discs, highlighting how the CD was the most technologically advanced and commercially available option for music listening. "It could commercially buy a violin. The thing you listen to is called a compact disk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BHkJFA0LMro
**Role in IMS's "Hello World" (N/A)** : The classical composition "Hello World," a pioneering piece entirely created and notated by the IMS computer program, was scored for violin, clarinet, and piano. This demonstrates early AI's ability to produce music for specific string and wind instruments. "It was a classical composition for clarinet, violin, and piano." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QFtS8fl553Y
