# instrument - Accordion

## curiosities
**David Botrell's Childhood Lessons** : David Botrell had "seven years of accordion lessons" during his youth, but he did not feel this made him musically adept enough to pursue music production schooling. "I had seven years of accordion lessons, but that didn't seem to cut it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vWvYe1a-2Aw
**Geek Rock Instrument** : The accordion was notably used by the Geek Rock band They Might Be Giants, highlighting the genre's tendency to incorporate "weird instruments" into its sound. "They might be giants, who like to sing about astronomy and physics while busting out an accordion." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Prominence in 2010s Alt Rock** : Accordions were prominently featured in the music of a new generation of individual singer-songwriters, duos, or small combos in the 2010s, particularly in the softer side of alt rock. "What kind of music was big? Well, acoustic guitars, mandalins, banjos, ukulele's, accordions, played by a new generation of individual singer-songwriters, duos, or small combos." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Role in Celtic Rock** : The accordion is a traditional instrument commonly found in Celtic rock, enriching the genre's sound by blending with contemporary rock instruments. "But Celtic rock also brings in the bagpipes, the accordion..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cuUF4MByxyo
**Symbol of Unconventionality (2008)** : The mention of an "accordion player" in an early 2008 Island Records meeting was met with derision, with an executive equating it to a band of "dustbin merchants," highlighting the instrument's perceived oddity and lack of commercial appeal in the contemporary music landscape. "Next thing you'll tell me is that they have an accordion player." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P1RyOchn7pk
**The Space Lady's Early Instrument** : The Space Lady (Susan Dietrich) initially played the accordion as a street musician before transitioning to a synthesizer in the 1980s. "She played the accordion." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HcK4M7-02ik
**They Might Be Giants' Early Instrumentation** : In the early years of They Might Be Giants, John Linnell provided backup music on the accordion. "while the other John would provide backup on a accordion." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9_3EbwKPnU
**Unexpected Use in Rock Music** : The accordion was notably played by Sheryl Crow as a guest musician on Scott Weiland's solo album, 12 Bar Blues. Its inclusion was considered unusual and interesting for a rock record, diverging from what would typically be heard in Weiland's band, Stone Temple Pilots. "She was brought in to play of all things the accordion. This wasn't STP, but I'm sure as hell was interesting." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-f9jhqAHkQo
