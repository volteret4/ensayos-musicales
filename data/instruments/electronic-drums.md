# instrument - Electronic drums

## curiosities
**Distinction from Drum Machines** : Electronic drums are distinct from drum machines; they are electronic pads that drummers hit with sticks, designed to be played physically rather than programmed. "These aren't drum machines, they're drums, electronic things, pads that you hit with a stick." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WL3zEWJJNuw
**Early Limitations and Decline in Style** : Early electronic drums, such as those by Simmons, had limitations; they couldn't store sounds and required constant programming and reprogramming. Although initially fun and futuristic, this distinctive early electronic drum sound quickly fell out of style, much like other 1980s aesthetics such as "big hair, shoulder pads, and white sport coats," becoming a hallmark that instantly dates music from that era. "But they couldn't store sounds and needed to be programmed and reprogrammed." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WL3zEWJJNuw
**First Commercial Release: Pollard's "the syndrome" (1976)** : The first commercially marketed electronic drum was released by a company called Pollard in 1976 and was named "the syndrome." Two years later, Simmons released their own models, which were quickly embraced by new wave and synth pop bands for their futuristic "lightsaber" sounds. "The first commercially marketed electronic drum was released by a company called Pollard in 1976." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WL3zEWJJNuw
**Late 1990s Resurgence with Realism Focus** : Despite their earlier decline in popularity, electronic drums did not die out. By the end of the 1990s, companies like Roland and Yamaha began producing electronic kits with a strong emphasis on achieving a feel and sound indistinguishable from acoustic kits. Electronic hi-hats and cymbals also evolved to sound remarkably real. These modern electronic kits became excellent practice sets, especially for musicians living in condos and apartments. "By the end of the 90s companies like Roland and Yamaha were making electronic kits with emphasis on making them feel and sound indistinguishable from acoustic kits." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WL3zEWJJNuw
**Modern Adoption and Versatility** : Today's advanced electronic drums have been embraced by numerous prominent drummers, including Danny Carey, Neil Peart, Will Champion of Coldplay, and Rob Borden of Lincoln Park. These drummers utilize electronic kits to trigger a wide array of sounds, tones, patterns, and samples, demonstrating their versatility and integration into modern performances. "Today's electronic drums have been embraced by players such as Danny Carey, Neil Peart, Will Champion of Coldplay, Rob Borden of Lincoln Park, and many, many others." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WL3zEWJJNuw
