# instrument - Gibson Explorer

## curiosities
**1976 Resuscitation Due to Copycat Designs** : Gibson resurrected the Explorer model in 1976 after another guitar manufacturer began producing guitars with a similar distinctive shape, indicating a renewed market interest in its unique design. "But when another guitar manufacturer started putting out guitars with a similar shape, Gibson resurrected the model in 1976." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d9HNqb9gJSU
**Radical Modernist Design and Initial Failure (1958)** : Released concurrently with the Flying V, the Explorer also featured a radical, angular, and "too weird" modern design. It sold even worse than the Flying V, with only about 50 units sold before its discontinuation. "It also had a radical modern design with the body jutting out at all sorts of weird angles. Too weird, so it was discontinued after selling maybe 50 versions of the type." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d9HNqb9gJSU
**The Edge's Signature Guitar** : The Gibson Explorer is one of The Edge's favorite guitars, with his 1976 model being the first guitar he ever bought at age 17 in New York. It became a visual signature for U2 and was used on every tour until its retirement around ten years ago. "The Explorer is one of the edges favorite guitars of all time. His 1976 model, one of the first after Gibson started making them again, appeared during every single U2 tour until about 10 years ago when it was finally retired." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d9HNqb9gJSU
**Tom Morello's Key Early Guitar** : Tom Morello acquired a Gibson Explorer around age 20, which he took with him to Harvard and still owns, marking the period when he began seriously studying the instrument. "And this Gibson Explorer was the guitar he took with him when he enrolled in Harvard. He still has him." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nVIswnZYFkU
