# instrument - Roland MC4

## curiosities
**Early Useful Sequencer (1978)** : In 1978, Roland introduced the MC4, one of the first truly useful sequencers. It could store approximately 12,000 notes, though they had to be entered one by one, which was a "hell to operate." Once programmed, data was stored on cassette tape, and hitting the start button would launch a series of synthesizer events automatically, enabling bands like Human League to perform live.
