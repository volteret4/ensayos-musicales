# instrument - Semi-acoustic Semi-hollow Body Guitar

## curiosities
**Addressing Tone and Feedback Issues** : The Gibson ES-335, introduced in 1958, was a semi-acoustic, semi-hollow body guitar designed to address the challenge of achieving warmer tones while eliminating feedback. It offered the best of both worlds between solid and hollow bodies, being lighter than a solid body but still amplifyable without feedback. "Basically, it offered the best of both worlds between the solid bodies and the hollow bodies. You can amplify the hell out of it with no feedback." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Warm, Round Tones** : The hollow parts of the ES-335's design allowed it to produce warm, round tones. These qualities were reminiscent of the sounds typically associated with 100% hollow acoustic guitars, providing a rich sonic palette. "And the hollow parts give it warm round tones, reminiscent of what we get from acoustic guitars, which are 100% hollow, of course." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
