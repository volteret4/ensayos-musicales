# instrument - Microphone

## curiosities
**Bell's Invention (March 10, 1876)** : Alexander Graham Bell's early experiments with sound transmission, spurred by his mother's deteriorating hearing and observations from teaching his dog Truve to "speak," led him to conceive of turning sound into electrical waves. He eventually invented a device on March 10, 1876, that transformed sound vibrations into electrical signals and then back again, serving as both a microphone and a speaker, forming the basis of the first practical telephone. "It was a microphone and a speaker, and this became the basis of the first practical working telephone. It was March 10th, 1876." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
**Berliner's Improvement (March 4, 1877)** : Emil Berliner, the future inventor of the gramophone, significantly improved upon Alexander Graham Bell's microphone design on March 4, 1877. Berliner's version offered much better sound quality, advancing the technology necessary for both telecommunications and future audio recording. "On March 4th, 1877, Emil Berliner, the man who would invent the gramophone, featuring music on a rotating disk played by a stylus, invented a new kind of microphone." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
**Edison's Further Improvement (1886)** : Thomas Edison further improved microphone technology in 1886, creating a device that was "even better" than previous designs. This microphone was notable for being the one used in the first ever radio broadcast, an experimental transmission from the Metropolitan Opera in New York in 1910. "Thomas Edison, who came up with something even better in 1886. And this was the first mic used at the first ever radio broadcast, an experimental thing, at the Metropolitan Opera in New York in 1910." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
**Facilitator of Electrical Recording** : In electrical recording, microphones replaced the acoustic horn method, picking up audio and electrically moving the diaphragm and stylus. This innovation allowed engineers to manipulate volume, freeing singers from the necessity of loud projection. Artists could now perform with more nuance, phrasing, and dynamic range, giving rise to "microphone singers" and "crooners" like Frank Sinatra and Bing Crosby. "Instead of everything being shouted down this big funnel, microphones were used to pick up the audio and electrically move the diaphragm and the stylus." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
**Revolutionary Impact on Singing (Early 20th Century)** : Before the microphone, singers had to possess naturally loud voices to project over an orchestra. Its introduction allowed for a new, more intimate vocal style, enabling artists like Bing Crosby to create a "close and personal" mood, fundamentally altering vocal performance. "Before it came along, singers had to be naturally louder than the orchestra behind them. They needed to have a voice that could reach the back rows of the theater. But when the microphone came along, certain singers like Bing Crosby realized that you could use it to create a whole new mood for singing by getting a close and personal." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0fQQubt9UTI
**Revolutionizing Performance and Recording** : The microphone fundamentally revolutionized musical performances and recordings in the 20th century. It enabled the capture of subtle performance elements, such as the intimate vocals and delicate instrumental nuances heard in Chet Baker's 1954 recordings, a sound previously impossible to achieve. This expanded the expressive possibilities for musicians and listeners alike. "The microphone allowed for the subtlest bits of a performance to be picked up and stored and enjoyed later. This completely revolutionized musical performances and recordings in the 20th century." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
