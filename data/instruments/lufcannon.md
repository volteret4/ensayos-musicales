**Nazi World War II Sonic Weapon** : The Lufcannon, literally an "air gun," was the first sonic weapon ever developed, a Nazi device from World War II. It used the combustion of methane and oxygen, creating 800 to 1500 explosions per second. These explosions generated sonic shockwaves that were amplified and roughly aimed by a parabolic sound mirror, approximately three meters in diameter. Experiments suggested it could kill a man at 60 meters in about 30 seconds, though it never entered the battlefield and was never tested on humans as far as is known.
