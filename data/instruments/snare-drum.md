# instrument - Snare drum

## curiosities
**Origins and Military Utility** : The snare drum first appeared in the 14th century, initially serving as a time-keeping device for flute players. It features two heads—a top and a bottom—with a series of metal wires running along the bottom head, which produce its characteristic staccato crack when struck. This distinct sound made the snare drum highly effective for military use, and it subsequently became the de facto rhythm-keeping machine. "There's a special drum called the snare, which first appeared sometime in the 14th century as a way to keep time for flute players." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WL3zEWJJNuw
**Standard Dimensions** : Today's snare drums typically measure 14 inches in diameter. Their depth can vary significantly, ranging from 4 inches to more than 8 inches, with deeper snares commonly used in marching bands.
