# instrument - Bass

## curiosities
**"Walk on the Wild Side" Bassline** : The famous sliding bass line in Lou Reed's "Walk on the Wild Side" was a creation of session player Herbie Flowers. It ingeniously combined two distinct bass lines: one from a stand-up double bass and another from a fretless electric, contributing to the song's smoky, sleazy nightclub feel. "Actually, there are two bass lines here. One is from a stand-up double bass and the other is from a fretless electric." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
**Adam Clayton's Tech Filling In** : During U2's 1993 Zoo TV tour, Adam Clayton's bass tech, Edward Morgan, had to fill in for him on stage when Clayton was unable to perform. This highlights the critical role of instrument technicians in live shows. "He was up to a Rody Adam's base text to Edward Morgan to fill in." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZVGnSVfed9s
**Boris Baker's Role** : Boris Baker is the bass player for the Canadian band Cassadour. "the bass player is Boris Baker." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MmXDbXI6Sqk
**Bridge Between Rhythm and Melody** : The bass guitar serves as a critical bridge between a band's rhythmic foundation and its melodic elements. It functions percussively, augmenting the drummer's beat, while also providing the melodic backbone for lead instruments. "The bass is that bridge between rhythm and melody." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CVPPymc_qrk
**Catalyst for Personal Engagement** : For some individuals, the bass guitar can be a powerful motivator, sparking engagement and passion, as it did for Adam Clayton who, despite being a "lazy troublemaker" in school, was deeply dedicated to the instrument. "The only thing that got him to care about anything was that bass guitar." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CVPPymc_qrk
**Chris Novoselic's Role in The Stiff Woody's** : In Nirvana's first band, The Stiff Woody's, Chris Novoselic played the bass. "Kurt was the drummer, Chris was on bass." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CyTI00SIrLE
**Destruction for Art** : Paul Simonon of The Clash famously trashed his bass guitar during a performance at the Palladium in New York on September 21, 1979. This moment was captured in an iconic photograph by Penny Smith, which became the cover of "London Calling," demonstrating how the instrument's destruction could become a central artistic statement. "So towards the end of the set, he spun around and started taking it out on his bass." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pwebM3TfSdg
**Distinctive Grunge Playing** : Bassists in the grunge era adopted a different playing style, characterized by lines that were often "slinky and burbly," sometimes even playing power chords as exemplified by Ben Shepherd of Soundgarden. "And people started playing the bass differently. The lines were often, I don't know, slinky and burbly." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tFBSrKMEZxI
**Dual Influences on Matthew Good's Style** : Matthew Good's approach to the bass was shaped by two contrasting influences: the melodic bass lines common in 60s and 70s music, and the "straight A instrument" style that moves in steps, as exemplified by bands like The Pixies, The Replacements, and Afghan Whigs. This dual perspective allowed him to command its role effectively in his solo songwriting. "the bass to me was always a very melodic instrument. But also someone that was massively influenced by bands like The Pixies and the Replacements and Afghan Wigs and that kind of things. It was a straight A instrument." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=--g0gugy3ZE
**Evolution of Style and Technique** : The bass can be a versatile instrument for developing unique styles, such as Flea's slap technique, which, while widely copied, reflects an innovative approach. "If you listen to albums like Blood Sugar Sex Magic, you'll hear Flea doing the slap thing with his strings." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CVPPymc_qrk
**Flea's Evolving Playing Style with Frusciante** : Under the influence of John Frusciante, Flea's bass playing style evolved significantly, becoming "more melodic" and less characterized by his previous "finger picking or popping and slapping" techniques. "Flea's bass playing evolved too. He wasn't finger picking or popping and slapping as much. He was becoming more melodic too, and that's John's influence." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TESrEzVHLCc
**Foundation for Grunge Bands** : Bass playing was crucial for holding grunge bands together and providing a cool foundation for their sound, as demonstrated by Chris Novoselic in Nirvana and Mike Starr in Alice in Chains. "Listen to how Chris Nova Celich's low end held Nirvana together. Go back and listen to some of those Allison Chains records and hear how Mike Star provided a cool foundation for everything." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tFBSrKMEZxI
**Gord Sinclair's Production Role** : Gord Sinclair, known for playing bass in The Tragically Hip, also provided production assistance for Cassadour's album *Brude and Bloom*. "hip bass player Gord Sinclair." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MmXDbXI6Sqk
**Isaac Brock's First Instrument** : Isaac Brock of Modest Mouse first picked up a bass guitar when he was 15 years old before switching to guitar. "Isaac first picked up a base when he was 15 and then switched to guitar after boring one from a friend." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CyTI00SIrLE
**Jacob Danger Armstrong's Temporary Role** : Jacob Danger Armstrong temporarily played bass for his older brother Joey's band, Emily's Army. "including the temporary appearance of little brother Jacob Armstrong on bass." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MmXDbXI6Sqk
**John Paul Jones's Spontaneous Playing** : During an interview at Pink Duck Studios, John Paul Jones, known for his work with Led Zeppelin, spontaneously picked up a bass guitar sitting on a stand and plucked "vaguely Zeppelin-esque notes," demonstrating his ongoing connection to the instrument. "John Paul Jones wanders in. Hello, he says, picking up a base, sitting on a stand and plucking some vaguely Zeppelin-esque notes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
**Melodic Role in Goth Music** : In early Goth music, bass players often played high on the fretboard and frequently carried much of the melody, rather than solely providing a rhythmic foundation. "Bass players played high on the fretboard and sometimes carried much of the melody instead of just providing the rhythmic foundation." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gd9sO7c3N1s
**Not a "Lady's Instrument" in 1963** : When Carol Kaye switched from guitar to bass in 1963 to pursue session work, it was uncommon for women to play the instrument, as it was generally not considered "a lady's instrument." Her decision defied gender norms of the time, paving the way for other female instrumentalists. "She started working in studios in 1957, switching from guitar to bass in 1963. And let's say no woman played bass in 1963. She was just not a lady's instrument, but she didn't care if there were gigs to be had as a session bass player." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
**Post-Technopop Resurgence** : In the mid-1980s, as electronic music's dominance waned, the bass guitar, alongside guitar and drums, experienced renewed prominence. Bands like The Smiths notably developed new methods for incorporating these instruments, opposing the artificiality of synthesized sounds. "Few groups fought back with new approaches to the guitar bass and drums lineup." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
**Ramones' Initial Purchase** : The Ramones, specifically John Cummings and Douglas Colvin, spent $250 combined to purchase their first guitar, bass, and an amp, marking the initial investment for their band's formation. "So after spending $250 between them on a guitar, a bass, and an amp, they held their first rehearsal four days later." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4
**Traditional Rock Instrument** : Bass guitars were a core component of traditional rock music, alongside vocals, guitars, drums, piano, and organ, with their sounds often manipulated through various effects and studio techniques. "For the longest time, the sounds of rock were made with voice guitars, bass, drums and keyboards like piano and organ." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
**Unusual Use in Post-Rock** : In post-rock, bass guitars, along with other traditional rock instruments, are employed in unusual ways to produce non-rock outcomes, contributing to the genre's experimental philosophy. "What we're doing is producing non-rock outcomes. That means compositions without the usual verse chorus, verse structure, and more atmospheric, some more experimentation, and more of a cinematic approach to audio." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9-v4qnOO8wY
**Walking Bass Line in Ska** : The bass line is highly important in ska, characterized by "steady quarter notes along a scale or something similar," known as a walking bass line. This contributes significantly to the genre's infectious and danceable sound. "The bass line is very important too; steady quarter notes along a scale or something similar. We call that a walking bass line, and the result is a very infectious sound that's really easy to dance to." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zNIVqKqAlnk
**Wolfgang Van Halen's Role** : Wolfgang Van Halen played bass in his father and uncle's band, Van Halen, for years. "Wolfgang Van Halen has been playing bass in his dad and uncle's band for years." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MmXDbXI6Sqk
