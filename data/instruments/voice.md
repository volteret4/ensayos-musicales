## curiosities
**Complex Processing as an Instrument** : The human voice, particularly in James Blake's music, is subjected to complex processing, often layered multiple times with varying treatments. This technique transforms the voice into something resembling other instruments, female choirs, or sounds manipulated by different playback speeds, effectively making the voice a highly versatile and processed instrument. **Frankie Lymon's Falsetto Innovation** : Frankie Lymon's unique falsetto voice, largely due to his voice not yet having broken, introduced a groundbreaking new timbre into rock and roll lead vocals. This marked a departure from the previous doo-wop norm where falsetto was a minor vocal blend element and lead vocals were typically a smooth high tenor. His vocal style profoundly influenced numerous subsequent vocal groups. **Natural Vocal Pitch Nuance** : For a human voice to sound natural and not artificial, it should not consistently hit perfect pitch. Instead, slight deviations, often described as being "a bit below the perfect pitch," are preferred, as perfect pitch can make a voice sound like a machine or a musical instrument rather than a human voice.
