## curiosities
**Analog Recording Mechanism** : The analog system fundamentally involves the literal transfer of an audio wave directly onto a physical support, such as vinyl, where the wave's shape is precisely reproduced as a physical groove. **Classic Analog Format** : Vinyl is presented as the quintessential example of a classic analog physical support. In this format, actual sound waves are literally transferred and reproduced as grooves on the record's surface. "Tenemos un formato clásico analógico que es el vinilo." ← Music Radar Clan > El cambio de Musica analogica a musica digital. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aMuIwP-mVUo&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc ← vinilo
**Durability and Degradation Profile** : While considered an "eternal" format in terms of archival storage—meaning it doesn't degrade merely from being stored and can play centuries later—vinyl records do experience degradation through repeated use. "Si yo cojo un vinilo lo guardo y lo mistatará niitos en dos cintos años lo cogen, eso vuelve a sonar perfectamente. Se degrada con el uso." ← Music Radar Clan > El cambio de Musica analogica a musica digital. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aMuIwP-mVUo&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc ← vinilo
**Intrinsic Noise Generation** : A small, inherent background noise, known as intrinsic noise, is generated by the needle as it traverses the grooves of a vinyl record during playback. This noise cannot be entirely eliminated.
