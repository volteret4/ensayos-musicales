# instrument - Pro Tools

## curiosities
**Drum Beat Production by Tim** : Drummer Tim utilizes Pro Tools to process and loop drum beats for the band's songwriting. He records practice sessions using various tools like mics, phones, or Zoom recorders, then cuts these recordings into loops and organizes them into folders to share with the other band members for creative inspiration. "I'll take that and I'll just cut up, like, you know, make loops out of these beats and then I'll just dump them all in a folder and send them to these guys so they can, you know, if they're ever sitting around holding a guitar or whatever they're doing, they can pull them up and just start going through them." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKNn01M8GG4
**High-End Recording Software (2010s)** : Pro Tools continued to be a professional, high-end recording software option in the 2010s, requiring significant investment for a fully loaded rig. However, its existence highlighted the growing accessibility of "pro-sounding" alternatives like GarageBand for personal computers. "Oh sure, you can splash out for something like a fully loaded Pro Tools, rig." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mNqqLDpUuZY
**Modern Audio Production** : In the current digital era, the speaker records his voice track into Pro Tools at his home studio, streamlining the production process compared to earlier analog methods. "Now I have a studio at home. I have Pro Tools. I record my voice track into Pro Tools." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4xxgPX-aR90
**Non-Destructive Digital Recording** : Pro Tools is highlighted as a modern digital audio workstation that offers non-destructive editing, a significant advantage over older analog methods where recording over a track would permanently erase previous content. "It's not like Pro Tools where it's non-destructive. Because you're recording over what you've done." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mjQBaX_3oL4
**Producer's Delegation** : While David Botrell is proficient with Pro Tools, he increasingly delegates its operation to dedicated engineers, such as Darren at Union Sound in Toronto, who he finds quicker. This allows Botrell to focus primarily on coaching musicians and performance. "I can use pro tools. I do it all the time. He's sitting there doing it already. Why do I need to go over and do that?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vWvYe1a-2Aw
**Steve Lilywhite's Approach** : Renowned producer Steve Lilywhite prefers to have his "own Pro Tools guy" in the studio, allowing him to concentrate entirely on "coaching and suggesting and cheering on the performances" rather than operating the equipment himself. "I've sat with Steve Lily White in the studio and he wants his own pro tools guy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vWvYe1a-2Aw
**Unlimited Tracks** : Unlike analog multi-track tape systems with fixed track limits, Pro Tools offers virtually unlimited tracks, facilitating complex arrangements and mixing. "Nowadays it's Pro Tools, and there's no limit to tracks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mjQBaX_3oL4
