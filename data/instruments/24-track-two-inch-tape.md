# instrument - 24-track, two-inch tape

## curiosities
**Analog Recording Medium** : This was a common multi-track recording system in the past, allowing for 24 separate audio tracks to be recorded on a two-inch wide magnetic tape. "when I was doing this stuff, it was a two-inch tape. You only had 24 tracks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mjQBaX_3oL4
**Limited Usable Tracks** : In practice, only 23 tracks were usable for audio, as one track was often reserved for a code to drive or sync another machine. "And one of them had a code on it to drive another machine. So you could only use 23 tracks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mjQBaX_3oL4
**Tape Wear Issue** : Analog tape, specifically 24-track, two-inch tape, could wear out with repeated playbacks and transfers, leading to degradation of the recorded sound. This necessitated transferring the content to new tapes, as seen during the production of Def Leppard's "Pyromania." "They transferred 24 track onto another new one because it was getting thin. The tape was wearing out." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mjQBaX_3oL4
