## curiosities
**First Songs Ripped for Piracy (1996)** : In 1996, a group known as Compressed to Audio (CDA) began ripping CDs and making them available for free online for illegal file sharing purposes. Metallica's "Until It Sleeps" is believed to be the first song ripped and uploaded, closely followed by Rage Against the Machine's "Bulls on Parade" and Soundgarden's "Black Hole Sun." "It was 1996 and a group of geeks calling themselves Compressed to Audio or CDA for short. Started ripping CDs and making them available for free online." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gP3oQeYEvso
**Foundation for Podcasting** : The invention of the MP3 in the late 1980s was a critical technological precursor for the emergence of podcasting. This digital audio compression format made it practical to efficiently store and distribute audio files online, thereby laying the groundwork for on-demand audio content. "This is something that would have never been born, had it not been for the invention of the MP3 in the late 1980s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uEe_HLUay5M
**Popularity and Fidelity Trade-off (Late 1990s)** : MP3s began to appear online in large quantities in the late 1990s, offering an "amazing" combination of free access to virtually any song in seconds and the portability of thousands of tracks on devices like the iPod. However, MP3s and similar file formats were compressed, removing up to 90% of the original music data based on psychoacoustics. This meant that the audio quality was inferior to CDs, vinyl, or even FM radio, but the public largely prioritized convenience and access over high fidelity, often adopting the attitude of "eh, sounds good enough." "MP3's first started appearing online in bulk in the late 1990s, they were amazing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GlcOoINI7EQ
