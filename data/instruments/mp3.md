**Popularity and Fidelity Trade-off (Late 1990s)** : MP3s began to appear online in large quantities in the late 1990s, offering an "amazing" combination of free access to virtually any song in seconds and the portability of thousands of tracks on devices like the iPod. However, MP3s and similar file formats were compressed, removing up to 90% of the original music data based on psychoacoustics. This meant that the audio quality was inferior to CDs, vinyl, or even FM radio, but the public largely prioritized convenience and access over high fidelity, often adopting the attitude of "eh, sounds good enough."
