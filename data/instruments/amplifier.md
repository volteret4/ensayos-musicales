# instrument - Amplifier

## curiosities
**Early Fragility and Distortion** : Early amplifiers were generally fragile and not very good, easily malfunctioning to produce sounds that were not clean or pure. This inherent flaw inadvertently led to the discovery of desirable distorted sounds. "Early amplifiers were fragile things, and frankly not very good." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w
**Foundation in Vacuum Tube** : Amplifiers, designed to boost quiet electrical signals, found their foundation in the vacuum tube, which was unveiled by Lee DeForest in 1911. This invention was critical for enabling sounds to be made louder. "That happened in 1911 when Lee DeForest unveiled a vacuum tube, and that became the foundation for something soon known as an amplifier." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=E6cXyaHALBQ
**Marshall Amplifier Innovation – Responding to Rock and Roll Demand** : The renowned Marshall amplifier was conceived, invented, manufactured, and marketed by Jim Marshall, a drummer and engineer. In 1963, in response to customer complaints that American amps like Fender were insufficient for the "new-ish thing called rock and roll," Marshall and his staff created the "loudest, cleanest, most rugged amplifier they could make." This invention, along with associated speaker cabinets, became standard gear for rock guitarists. "This amp and the speaker cabinets with 12 inch and 19 inch speakers became a big hit with the new generation of British musicians and Jim stuff is still standard gear for guitar players who want to go big or go home." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsSTJWN6cUo
**Overdrive Mechanism** : Distortion, also known as overdrive, occurs when an electrical signal is pushed beyond the capacity of an amplifier's circuitry. This excessive power causes the signal to "clip," cutting off its peaks and troughs and adding new characteristics like overtones, sustain, and compression. Extreme overdrive results in distortion. "Distortion is also known as overdrive. Basically it means you've pushed the electrical signal past what the circuitry and the amplifier can handle." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w
**Pre-amplifier Sections** : To allow for powerful distortion at lower, more manageable volumes, pre-amplifier sections were added to standard amplifiers. Turning up the pre-amp creates distortion, while a master volume knob controls the overall output volume. This innovation enabled guitarists to achieve "big amp" sounds even in small spaces or at low volumes. "This was solved by adding a pre-amplifier section to a standard amplifier. Turn that up and you get distortion." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w
