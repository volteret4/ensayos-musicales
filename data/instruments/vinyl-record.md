**Context for Early CD Releases** : During the 1980s and early 1990s, music released on Compact Disc often had vinyl or cassette as its primary format of diffusion. CDs from this period are therefore less likely to hold significant collector's value, partly because their audio quality was not exceptional. **Limitations for Classical Music** : Despite having a great frequency range, vinyl records were disliked by classical music fans and conductors due to issues like channel contamination, intrinsic noise, and complex processes. These problems significantly affected the clarity (nicidez) of classical music. "eran grandes enemigos de un gran desproblemas que tenía el disco de vinilo, sobre todo en la contaminación de canales, son el ruido intrínseco, en ciertos procesos un poco complejos, que afectaban bastante a la nicidez de la música clásica." ← Music Radar Clan > ESPECIAL FORMATOS： El CD | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7iRO7Hwg9V0&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc

**Vinyl Revival and Fidelity (21st Century)** : The ongoing vinyl revival has contributed to the resurgence of high-fidelity music listening. Consumers who purchase vinyl records to actually play them have invested in proper two-channel stereo systems, akin to those used in earlier decades. This trend indicates a conscious choice to prioritize sound quality over the convenience offered by digital formats. "Meanwhile, the vinyl revival is contributed to this.
