**British Chart Fad (1964)** : In early 1964, the harmonica was experiencing a popular fad in the British music charts. Ernest Ranglin capitalized on this trend by replacing the original saxophone solo with a harmonica solo for Millie Small's "My Boy Lollipop" to enhance its commercial appeal. "Wranglin replaced the saxophone solo from the original record, with a harmonica solo, to fit the current fad for the harmonica in the British chart." ← Episode 114： ＂My Boy Lollipop＂ by Millie | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kf-DtqoOsRI
**First Musical Instrument Played in Space (1965)** : On December 7, 1965, astronaut Wally Schirra used a harmonica to perform "Jingle Bells" aboard Gemini 6. This marked the first instance of a musical instrument being played in space. "On December 7, 1965, Wally Shira and Thomas Stafford pulled out a harmonica and some hand bells that they'd stowed away aboard Gemini 6 and performed jingle bells for the planet." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aSk1XQHNkd8 ← harmonica
**Prominence in Quincy Jones's Sound** : The prominent use of harmonica in Quincy Jones's 1971 version of Carole King's "Smackwater Jack" played a crucial role in developing a sound that served as a "dress rehearsal" for one of his most recognized pieces of music: the theme to TV's *Sanford & Son*, "The Street Beater." "And the sound of that track, built around prominent harmonica, turned out to be a dress rehearsal for one of Q's most heard pieces of music, the theme to TV's Sanford & Sun." ← Hit Parade Music History and Music Trivia > I Wanna Rock with Q.
