# instrument - Sampler

## curiosities
**Akai S1000 (1988)** : Following the early innovations of samplers like the Fairlight CMI, Emulator, and Sinclair Vier, the Japanese company Akai introduced a line of rack-mounted samplers that became an industry standard. The Akai S1000, released in 1988, was particularly notable for its affordability. Its accessibility and capabilities made it a crucial tool for musicians, with figures like Trent Reznor being among the first to fully utilize its potential for their music. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Somq2MoRi5Q
**Early Sound Manipulation (Pre-1980)** : Before the advent of digital samplers in 1980, manipulating sound in non-traditional ways required extensive use of magnetic tape. Engineers would meticulously measure out reels of tape with timers or rulers, cut desired sound bites with razor blades, and then piece them together using special splicing tape. This "old school, literally cut and paste form of sound manipulation" was a time-consuming and "mind numbing" process, exemplified by the cash register sequence in Pink Floyd's 1973 song "Money." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Somq2MoRi5Q
**Fairlight Computer Musical Instrument (1980)** : Developed by the Australian company Fairlight throughout the 1970s, the Fairlight Computer Musical Instrument (CMI) made its debut in 1980 as the world's first keyboard-based digital sampler. This revolutionary device allowed operators to digitally record any acoustic sound, store it on a floppy disk, visualize its waveform on a monitor, manipulate the waveform using a light pen, and then play it back via a standard 88-note keyboard. This enabled musicians to take any sound, "shape it, mold it, and manipulate it into something totally new." The first ever digitally sampled sound in history was a barking dog. Despite its groundbreaking capabilities, the Fairlight CMI was extremely expensive, costing between $25,000 and $36,000, and could only store sound files up to 20 kilobytes in size. Over 300 studios and record labels acquired this cutting-edge technology. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Somq2MoRi5Q
