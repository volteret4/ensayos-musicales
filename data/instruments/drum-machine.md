## curiosities
**Evolution from Rhythm Machines (1960s-1970s)** : Rhythm machines, which initially offered pre-programmed standard beats like waltzes and fox-trots, evolved into drum machines in the 1960s and 1970s. Companies like Roland, Multivox, and Bentley began selling increasingly sophisticated devices that were particularly popular with new German electronic bands. "In the 60s and 70s, rhythm machines evolved into drum machines. **Threat to Drummers (1980s)** : The widespread adoption and advancement of drum machines in the 1980s, especially after Roland unveiled the TR808, made "flesh and blood drummers really got nervous." These analog drum machines had unique, inspiring sounds that challenged traditional percussionists.
