# instrument - Synthesizers

## curiosities
**1980s Tribute in "Always"** : Blink 182's song "Always" from their self-titled album prominently featured synth sounds as part of its tribute to 1980s music. "The song was their tribute to the 80s, with its synth sounds." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tkLRJiOBu0
**Central to Nine Inch Nails' Early Sound** : In the early days of Nine Inch Nails, Trent Reznor heavily relied on synthesizers and computers due to his limited guitar experience. This allowed him a "pretty big palette of sounds" to simulate various instruments and create the desired textures for "Pretty Hate Machine." "It wasn't really based on, can I play the guitar because I can simulate that through the computer." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M3nUQtja0qc
**Early Limitations and Polyphony (Pre-FM Synthesis)** : In their early development, keyboard synthesizers were monophonic, capable of playing only one note at a time, which restricted them from playing chords. The subsequent introduction of polyphonic synthesizers allowed for multiple notes, but the sounds produced were often artificial and robotic, thereby limiting their practical applications. "In the early days of keyboard synthesizers, you could only play one note at a time. You couldn't play chords. Then came polyphonic synthesizers, which allowed you to play, you know, two, three, four, five, six, whatever, as many notes as you want. Press the keys, you get sounds. But since sounded very artificial and very robotic, which limited their use." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TsiYLGGSJ2U
**Evolution from Novelty to Standard** : Synthesizers, similar to effects pedals, Marshall stacks, and drum machines, were once innovative sources of new sounds in music. They have since become "part of the standard arsenal of musical tools," indicating their common use in modern music production. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m7MbV-eCLyE
**Frequency Modulation (FM) Synthesis Breakthrough** : John Chowning at Stanford University pioneered Frequency Modulation (FM) Synthesis, a technology that revolutionized the synthesizer industry. By manipulating electronically generated sine waves, Chowning discovered a method to make synthesizers produce sounds resembling real instruments, significantly enhancing their utility and appeal. "The big breakthrough came from a guy named John Chaoning at Stanford University. He was fiddling with some electronically generated sine waves, and he figured out a way to make them behave so that they began to sound like real instruments. Long story short, he came up with a technology known as Frequency Modulation Synthesis, or FM Synthesis for short." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TsiYLGGSJ2U
**New Pop Characteristics** : Synthesizers played a significant role in the sound of New Pop bands like The Human League, contributing to their new wavy approach and the overall slick pop sheen of the genre in the early 1980s. "thanks to music videos and the use of synthesizers, they were labeled as a new pop band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4
**New Romantic Sound of the Future** : Synthesizers were highly favored by New Romantics, who regarded them as the "sound of the future" and themselves as futurists. Their perceived ease of play, allowing for experimental manipulation of knobs and keys to create unprecedented sounds, aligned with a punk-like ethos that prioritized creativity and guts over traditional musical virtuosity. "As for music, New Romantics really like synthesizers. They fancy themselves as futurists, and at the time synths were the sound of the future. Besides, they could be very easy to play." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mAlc-N2wn5s
**Technological Advancements (Early 1980s)** : In the early 1980s, synthesizers became more powerful and more affordable. This, alongside other electronic technologies, facilitated new sounds in the post-punk era and played a crucial role in the development of genres like Dreampop. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFLMkvJAaUY
**Theme Song Composition** : Synthesizers were used by imaging producer Amin Batia in 1993 for the composition of the ongoing history show's theme song. "The theme song is a combo of me doing stuff with my synthesizers, some production library stuff, some sound effects." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4xxgPX-aR90
**Trent Reznor's Early Fascination** : Synthesizers were a profound source of inspiration for Trent Reznor from a young age. He found them, along with drum machines from bands like The Human League, OMD, and Depeche Mode, "really cool," to the extent that he once considered a job working for a company that made them. "But the synthesizers and drum machines of the human league and OMD and Depeche mode. Now that was really cool." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M3nUQtja0qc
