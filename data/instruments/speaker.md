# instrument - Speaker

## curiosities
**General Electric and AT&T Development** : The first speaker, a paper coil device capable of reproducing sound, was the result of a co-development project between General Electric and AT&T in 1921. This invention was essential for converting amplified electrical signals back into audible sound. "And that happened in 1921, when a co-development project between General Electric and AT&T resulted in the first speaker, a paper call that reproduced sound." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=E6cXyaHALBQ
**Integration into Electric Guitar Design** : Speakers were the final component in the signal chain that enabled the electric guitar. An amplified electrical signal from the guitar, boosted by vacuum tubes, would be sent to a speaker, which then transformed these signals back into sound. "creating an electrical signal, which traveled down a wire to an amplifier filled with vacuum tubes, which in turn made the signal louder and transmitted everything to a speaker, which turned those signals back into sound." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V7WjNR1ujhk
**Invention and Early Use (1921)** : In 1921, General Electric, alongside AT&T, developed the speaker, a device necessary to convert an amplified electric signal back into audible sound. The speaker, electricity, and the vacuum tube first converged with the introduction of the radio. "And in 1921, General Electric, NAT and T created the speaker." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V7WjNR1ujhk
**Part of Bell's Original Device (March 10, 1876)** : Alexander Graham Bell's invention on March 10, 1876, which transformed sound vibrations into electrical signals and back again, functioned as both a microphone and a speaker. This dual-purpose device formed the foundational technology for the first practical working telephone. "It was a microphone and a speaker, and this became the basis of the first practical working telephone. It was March 10th, 1876." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
