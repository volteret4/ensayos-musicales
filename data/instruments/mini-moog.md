# instrument - Mini-Moog

## curiosities
**Catalyst for Gary Numan's Electronic Shift** : In the spring of 1979, Gary Numan's discovery and impromptu playing of a Mini-Moog synthesizer left in a studio dramatically changed his musical direction. This encounter inspired him to transition from guitar-based punk to electronic punk, leading to the creation of his massive hit "Are 'Friends' Electric?". **Portable and Affordable Synthesizer (1970)** : Introduced on January 24, 1970, by Dr. Moog, the Mini-Moog (or Mini-Mogue) was a small, portable, and powerful synthesizer. Crucially, it was relatively cheap at $1,200. Its accessibility led to 300 units being sold monthly within three years, offering rock musicians an alternative to traditional instruments like guitars, pianos, and drums. "January 24, 1970. Very important date.
