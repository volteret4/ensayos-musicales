# instrument - Drums

## curiosities
**Alan Cross's Personal Connection** : Alan Cross, the podcast host, plays the drums and bought his first kit with money earned from teaching lessons at a drum shop.
**Establishes and Holds the Beat** : The drummer is responsible for establishing and holding the beat of a band.
**Gord Downey's Performance on "Pasco Submarine" (2003)** : Gord Downey notably played the drums himself on his song "Pasco Submarine," featured on his album "The Battle of the Nudes."
**Motown Hired Local Drummers** : Many Motown acts in the 1960s touring live would hire local drummers to fill out their band, rehearsing quickly before shows.
**Neil Peart's Influence on Taylor Hawkins** : Neil Peart of Rush was Taylor Hawkins' drumming idol ("my guy on the wall"). Taylor admits to "ripping off" Peart's drum fills for Foo Fighters songs, specifically "crazy fills" and sections where the drums become a "big part of the song" (e.g., in "Winnebago" and "Times Like These").
**Perceived as a "Man's Job"** : Prior to Maureen Tucker joining The Velvet Underground in 1965, playing drums was largely considered a man's job, making her a pioneer.
**Prominence in Rush's "2112"** : Taylor Hawkins noted that Rush's "2112" was the first time he heard music where drums were so prominently featured.
**Stereotypes and Contributions** : Drummers often receive a "bad rap" and are the subject of jokes, but they have made very significant, often overlooked, contributions to music, altering the course of rock music.
**Zach Starky's First Kit** : Zach Starky received his first drum kit from his godfather, Keith Moon.
**Zach Starky's Only Lesson** : Ringo Starr gave his son Zach Starky exactly one drum lesson.
