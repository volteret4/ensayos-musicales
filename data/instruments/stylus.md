**Acoustic Stylus System (Homemade)** : This simple system operates through direct mechanical vibration. A small needle, in contact with a vinyl record, vibrates with the record's sound wave. This vibration is then transmitted to a membrane, typically a paper leaf, which is shaped into a funnel or connected to an external funnel to amplify the sound signal. The required materials for a homemade version include a small needle, a paper leaf, and adhesive tape. **Electromagnetic Induction Stylus System (Homemade)** : This more complex, yet fundamentally simple, system mimics modern stylus technology. It requires a support structure, two small tubes to form coils, copper wires, and a needle with a small magnet at its other end. As the acute tip of the needle traces the vinyl groove, it vibrates. This vibration causes the attached magnet to also vibrate, generating an alternating current within the surrounding copper coils. This induced current precisely represents the sound wave. **Signal Conversion in Induction Stylus** : The core function of an induction stylus involves a chain of vibration and signal conversion. The vibration originating from the vinyl record's groove is transferred to the stylus needle, then to the attached magnet. The magnet's movement, in turn, induces a corresponding electrical current in the surrounding copper wires. This electrical current is an accurate representation of the original sound wave.
