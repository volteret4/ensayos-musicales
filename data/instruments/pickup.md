# instrument - Pickup

## curiosities
**Function and Design** : A pickup is a device designed to convert the vibrations of a guitar's strings into electrical energy for amplification. It fundamentally consists of a wire coiled around a magnet. While research into pickups began in the 1890s, George Beechum was the first to successfully adapt the technology for use with the guitar. "The answer was a device known as a pickup, which essentially is a wire coiled around a magnet. While work on developing pickups went as far back as the 1890s, no one had properly adapted for the guitar until George Beachham." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=E6cXyaHALBQ
**Major Breakthrough for Electric Guitar** : The pickup was identified as the key invention for the electric guitar, consisting of a magnet wrapped in copper wire. When metal strings were strummed, their vibrations created an electrical field through the pickup, generating an electrical signal that could then be amplified and converted into sound.
