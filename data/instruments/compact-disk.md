# instrument - Compact Disk

## curiosities
**Conceptualization and Prototype (1969-1970)** : In 1969, Dutch physicist Klaus Compaan conceived the idea of breaking sound into binary bits (zeros and ones) that could be read and converted back into sound. By 1970, working for Phillips with Peter Kramer, he developed a prototype of the compact disk, initially made of glass, and determined that a laser was the optimal method for reading the encoded information. "In 1969, a Dutch physicist by the name of Klaus Kompon had a thought, what if you could break sound down into tiny binary bits, you know, zeros and ones?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AymMybE33zU
**Impact on Music Industry (1980s)** : Upon its introduction, the compact disk rapidly transformed the music industry. Vinyl records and pre-recorded cassettes, which collectively sold nearly a billion copies annually, largely disappeared by the end of the 1980s. Furthermore, the advent of the CD pulled the entire music industry out of a severe recession that had followed the end of the disco era. "And not only that, the introduction to the CD brought the entire music industry out of a terrible recession following the end of disco." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AymMybE33zU
**Standardization and Global Rollout (1979-1982)** : Phillips and its subsidiaries experimented with the CD for years, with Sony joining development in 1979, leading to the establishment of a worldwide standard. The final specifications included a polycarbonate disk (replacing glass), a diameter of 120 millimeters, laser reading from the inside spiraling outwards (opposite of vinyl), and a maximum length of 74 minutes, reportedly to accommodate the Sony chairman's favorite Beethoven symphony. In 1982, major electronics manufacturers were on board, and the technology rolled out in Europe and Japan that fall, followed by North America the next spring. "A worldwide standard is set. The disk will be made from polycarbonate instead of glass. They will be exactly 120 millimeters in diameter." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AymMybE33zU
