# instrument - TONTO (The Original New Timbral Orchestra)

## curiosities
**Cultural Appearance in "Phantom of the Paradise"** : TONTO made a visual appearance in the movie "Phantom of the Paradise." The actual TONTO synthesizer can be seen in the background of the studio where Winslow Leech works on his cantata, solidifying its place in popular culture beyond its musical impact. "Hey, have you ever seen the movie Phantom of the Paradise? You know the studio in which poor, winstlow leech slaves away on his cantata? That's what we see in the background. That is the actual Tonto." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
**Decline in Usage and Restoration** : Despite its initial impact, TONTO's immense size became a problem as smaller, cheaper, and more powerful synthesizers emerged daily. It was shuffled between studios and gradually became unwanted. In 2013, after being housed at Mark Mothersbaugh of Devo's studio in Los Angeles, it was sold and subsequently purchased and restored by the National Music Center in Calgary. It now stands as a major part of the Center's keyboard collection. "After a while though, Tonto's size was a problem, especially with smaller, cheaper, and more powerful synths coming out every day." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
**World's Largest Modular Synthesizer** : TONTO, short for "The Original New Timbral Orchestra," was constructed by Malcolm Cecil and Robert Margouleff in the late 1960s. It was designed as the world's largest modular synth setup, integrating gear from Moog, Oberheim, ARP, EMS, Roland, and Yamaha, along with custom analog synth modules, keyboards, patch bays, and joysticks. Its immense size (25 feet in diameter, 6 feet tall, weighing one ton) allowed for unprecedented sound creation, enabling simultaneous drum tracks, bass lines, and multiple melodies, which was brand new at the time. "God, this thing was huge. It featured gear from Mogue, Oberheim, ARP, EMS, Roland, and Yamaha." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
