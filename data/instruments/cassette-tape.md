# instrument - Cassette Tape

## curiosities
**Decades of Storage and Degradation** : After the child's recording, the cassette was put back into its "hard little plastic box and tidied away," remaining unheard for many years. The recording itself contained "hiss and drop out," indicating natural audio degradation over time, further challenging the retrieval of its embedded clues. "And buried somewhere in the hiss and drop out of the recording are the clues to who this boy is and how he got here." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vFVnhksrFao
**Medium for Lost Memories** : A cassette tape, with its magnetic coating, became the repository for a child's last words in his mother tongue in 1979, preserving "grainy flecks and wisps" of his memory. This tape held scratched sounds and muffled phrases that, at the time, made sense to him but were later lost to his understanding. "imprinted onto the magnetic coating of the tape are the last grainy flecks and wisps of the child's memory." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vFVnhksrFao
