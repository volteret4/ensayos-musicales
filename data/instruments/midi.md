# instrument - MIDI

## curiosities
**Universal Synthesizer Interface Standard (1981-82)** : MIDI, or Musical Instrument Digital Interface, originated from an engineering paper called "Universal Synthesizer Interface" and became a crucial worldwide standard for connecting electronic instruments. Developed around 1981-82, a single MIDI cable could carry 16 channels of information, enabling up to 16 devices to communicate. This meant playing one note on a lead unit could trigger events on up to 15 other devices, effectively turning one musician into a "one-person orchestra" if programmed correctly, vastly expanding creative possibilities in professional studios. "MIDI stands for Musical Instrument Digital Interface. It originated in an engineering paper called Universal Synthesizer Interface and became the new worldwide standard for allowing all these new electronic instruments to talk to each other." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
