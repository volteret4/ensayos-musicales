**Origin and Impact on Vocals** : Invented in 1997 by Andy Hildebrand of Antares Audio Technologies, who adapted his oil-searching technology to correct musical pitches, Auto-Tune was initially intended for subtle studio vocal corrections. Its use was radically transformed by Cher's "Believe" (1998), which maximized the effect to create a distinctive "robotic sound." This popularized Auto-Tune as an overt artistic tool, widely adopted by artists like Kanye, T-Pain, and Travis Scott for exaggerated effects. Its ubiquitous presence in modern recording has sparked debates about "fakery," the "humanity" being "squeezed out of music," and the value of intentional flaws, exemplified by Nirvana deliberately leaving a Kurt Cobain vocal error in a song.
