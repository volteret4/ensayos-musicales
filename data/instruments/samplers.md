## curiosities
**Creative Use by New Synthesizer Technology (Post-1987)** : By 1987, new synthesizer technology facilitated the increasingly creative use of samples in industrial music, contributing to the rapidly evolving scene. "They had drum machines and they had loud guitars. Perhaps more than any other band from the early 80s, Skinny Puppy was more important to the development of the modern industrial sound than anyone else." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSFhhEaiYwE
**Pivotal in Alt Rock Dance Scene (Mid-1980s)** : Samplers, alongside drum machines and 12-inch remixes, were key instruments used by artists like New Order, Depeche Mode, and The Cure in the mid-1980s. Their experimentation with these tools was crucial in the birth of the Alt Rock dance scene. "thanks to artists like New Order and Depeche Mode and the Cure experimenting with drum machines, samplers and 12 inch remixes, an Alt Rock dance scene was born." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mNqqLDpUuZY
**Technological Advancements (Early 1980s)** : Samplers, alongside synthesizers and drum machines, became available in the early 1980s.
