## curiosities
**Commonplace for Major Artists** : Today, shooting music videos on a smartphone has become commonplace, with major artists like Lady Gaga and Olivia Rodrigo adopting this method. This trend underscores how accessible and high-quality smartphone video technology has become, enabling creativity without reliance on traditional, expensive production setups. "Today shooting a video on a smartphone is commonplace. Artists biggest Lady Gaga and Olivia Rodrigo have shot videos this way." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7IDbqdIw3WU
**Revolutionized Video Production (Post-2007)** : The smartphone revolution, ignited by the iPhone in 2007, made consumer-grade video gear as good as or better than professional equipment once costing millions. This cheap and powerful technology empowered musicians and video artists to create "crazy conceptual stuff" for minimal cost, freeing them from punishing budgets. "This cheap and powerful technology allowed musicians and video artists to run wild, freed from punishing budgets and empowered by gadgets already in their pockets." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7IDbqdIw3WU
**Smartphone's Impact on Music Consumption** : Smartphones transformed music consumption by enabling users to be "untethered from computers," consolidating multiple gadgets (phone, MP3 player, camera) into a single device, and increasingly becoming the primary source for daily music listening as onboard storage expanded. "Instead of having all these separate gadgets, you know, a phone, you had an MP3 player, you had a digital camera, maybe you had something else, we had just one device." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XM1RvL7Kdts
**iPhone's Redefinition of Smartphones** : Although not the first smartphone, the iPhone redefined the device's capabilities, prompting other companies to invest billions in R&D to compete. "The iPhone wasn't the first smartphone, but it redefined what a smartphone should be." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XM1RvL7Kdts
