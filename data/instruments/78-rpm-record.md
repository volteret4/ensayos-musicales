# instrument - 78 RPM record

## curiosities
**Extended Use Globally (Until ~1963 and beyond)** : Continued to be released in parts of the world, especially South America (Columbia, Uruguay, Argentina, Brazil) perhaps as late as 1963, with subsequent releases in South Africa, India, and the Philippines, though documentation is scarce. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Epz5Sn151bk
**Last Official Release in UK (1960)** : The last 78 RPM record released by HMV Records in the UK was a classic by Ray Charles (catalog number POP 742) in November 1960. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Epz5Sn151bk
**Last Official Releases in US (1960)** : The last 78 RPM records officially released by a record company in America were "Fanny May" by Buster Brown and "Something On Your Mind" by Bobby Marshan, both on Fire Records in June 1960. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Epz5Sn151bk
**Modern Usage with Historical Speed Settings (1990)** : Turntables sold through the 1960s, 70s, and even 80s often included a speed setting to play 78s. Dave Edmonds released a 10-inch single in 1990 with a side designed to be played at 78 RPM. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Epz5Sn151bk
**Replaced by LP and Single (1950s-1960s)** : Eventually superseded by the 12-inch vinyl LP and the 7-inch single. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Epz5Sn151bk
**Special Promotional Releases (Ongoing)** : Special promotional 78s were produced "just for fun." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Epz5Sn151bk
**Standard Format for Decades (Before 1900-1950s)** : Was the primary format for recorded music for more than 50 years, starting sometime before 1900 and adopted as the standard global format for decades. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Epz5Sn151bk
