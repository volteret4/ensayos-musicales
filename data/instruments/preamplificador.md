## curiosities
**Connection Inputs** : Amplifiers that include preamplification feature distinct audio inputs for turntables, typically labeled "fono" (phono) and "line." The "fono" input is designed to pass through preamplification, which is necessary for turntables, whereas the "line" input does not. It is critical to connect the turntable to the correct "fono" input for proper functioning. "Los amplificadores que llevan preamplificación tienen una entrada distinta para los tocatiscos. **Cost-Effectiveness for Quality** : Despite being considered by some as the most "dispensable" part of the sound chain, acquiring a good external preamplifier is recommended for those seeking true sound quality. They are quite economical, with good models available for around 30-40 euros, and help prevent quality loss during the initial amplification stage. **Role in Sound Chain** : The preamplifier is a necessary component in the sound system chain, specifically for turntables, and cannot be omitted. It performs an initial amplification step before the main amplifier.
