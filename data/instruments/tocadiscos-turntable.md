**Mechanical vs. Electronic Systems** : Older gramophones operated mechanically, using a membrane system where the needle was connected to a vibrating membrane, and sound was mechanically amplified via a funnel. Modern turntables, established in the late 1950s and early 1960s, replaced this mechanical process with an electrical induction system where a magnet receptor generates an induction current, electrically amplified to speakers.
