# instrument - compact disc (CD)

## curiosities
**Industry Game Changer and Retailer Resistance (Early 1980s)** : Introduced in the early 1980s, the compact disc (CD) initially faced resistance from record stores due to the need for new shelving investments after prior expenditures on 45s, albums, 8-tracks, and cassettes. However, the industry, in a deep recession, pushed the format, highlighting its space efficiency (two CDs could fit in the space of one 12-inch album). The CD proved to be the needed catalyst for the industry's recovery, with Tower Records alone selling a billion dollars worth of CDs in its best years.
