# instrument - Compact Disc (CD)

## curiosities
**Digital Storage and Contactless Playback Concept (Unspecified Year)** : The foundational idea behind the compact disk originated from Jim Russell's understanding of digital binary code, where information is stored as zeros and ones. He envisioned a system that could digitize music and play it back without any physical contact between parts, thereby eliminating the wear inherent in traditional vinyl records. "What if, Jim thought, I could digitize music and then play it back using a system that didn't involve any physical contact between any of the parts?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
**Patented as First CD (1970)** : By 1970, Jim Russell had fully developed his concept for an optical recording and playback device and received the first patent for it. This invention is recognized as, "for all intents and purposes," the very first compact disk, laying the groundwork for its subsequent commercial development by Sony and Phillips. "By 1970, he had it all figured out and received the first patent for an optical recording and playback device. This, for all intents and purposes, was the very first compact disk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
**True Inventor Jim Russell (1970)** : Jim Russell is identified as the true inventor of the compact disk, predating the well-known collaborations between Phillips and Sony in the 1970s. By 1970, Russell, a scientist frustrated by the wear of vinyl records, had patented the first optical recording and playback device.
