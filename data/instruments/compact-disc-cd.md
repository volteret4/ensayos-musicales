# instrument - Compact Disc (CD)

## curiosities
**Digital Storage and Contactless Playback Concept (Unspecified Year)** : The foundational idea behind the compact disk originated from Jim Russell's understanding of digital binary code, where information is stored as zeros and ones. He envisioned a system that could digitize music and play it back without any physical contact between parts, thereby eliminating the wear inherent in traditional vinyl records. "What if, Jim thought, I could digitize music and then play it back using a system that didn't involve any physical contact between any of the parts?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
**Disputed First Commercial Release** : The identity of the very first commercially available CD is a matter of dispute. Leading contenders include a classical recording of Strauss's "Alpine Symphony" conducted by Herbert von Karajan, ABBA's "The Visitors," and Billy Joel's "52nd Street," which went on sale in Japan in October 1982. "Well, some say it was a classical recording of Strauss's and Alpine Symphony, a performance conducted by Herbert von Karien. Others insist it was The Visitors by Ava. Maybe it's Billy Joel's 52nd Street." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gP3oQeYEvso
**First Commercial Production Date (August 17, 1982)** : The first Compact Discs intended for consumers rolled off the production line on August 17, 1982, from a factory located just outside of Hanover, in what was then West Germany. This date marks the beginning of mass commercial CD availability. "Whatever they were, we do know the date they were made. August 17th, 1982." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gP3oQeYEvso
**First Demonstration (1981)** : The Bee Gees' album "Living Eyes" was the first album to be transferred to CD for demonstration purposes in 1981. It was used extensively on television and radio to introduce the world to the new Compact Disc technology. "In 1981 their album Living Eyes was used on TV and radio as a way of introducing the world to Compact Discs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gP3oQeYEvso
**First North American Pressing (1984)** : Bruce Springsteen's "Born in the USA" was apparently the first Compact Disc to be pressed in North America, a milestone achieved in 1984. This occurred as new CD factories began coming online globally, expanding production beyond Germany and Japan. "And apparently the first CD pressed in North America was Bruce Springsteen's Born in the USA." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gP3oQeYEvso
**First to Sell Over a Million Copies (1985)** : Dire Straits' "Brothers in Arms" was the first Compact Disc to sell more than one million copies, reaching this significant sales milestone in 1985. This marked a pivotal moment for the commercial success and widespread adoption of the CD format. "The first compact disc to sell more than a million copies was Brothers and Arms by Dyer Straits that happened in 1985." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gP3oQeYEvso
**Initial Global Manufacturing Hubs** : Initially, all Compact Discs were produced exclusively in either Germany or Japan, as these were the only two manufacturing plants in the entire world. This concentration meant that all early CDs originated from these two countries. "At first, all CDs came out of either Germany or Japan because there were only two manufacturing plants in the entire world." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gP3oQeYEvso
**Integration with Personal Computers** : The requirement for CD-ROM drives in new personal computers, driven by software installations like Windows, inadvertently led to CDs becoming a format for digital music transfer. Users discovered they could play music CDs on their computers and, with free software, transfer digital files to their hard drives, paving the way for digital music consumption outside physical media. "Somewhere along the line, someone discovered that a computer's CD-ROM drive was just like the compact disk player they had on the shelf." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r8AazBVoS7g
**Patented as First CD (1970)** : By 1970, Jim Russell had fully developed his concept for an optical recording and playback device and received the first patent for it. This invention is recognized as, "for all intents and purposes," the very first compact disk, laying the groundwork for its subsequent commercial development by Sony and Phillips. "By 1970, he had it all figured out and received the first patent for an optical recording and playback device. This, for all intents and purposes, was the very first compact disk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
**Profit Margins and Consumer Cost (1990s)** : The compact disc became a major driver of record industry revenue in the 1990s, offering significantly higher profit margins for record labels compared to vinyl and cassettes. Despite initial promises that prices would drop with increased manufacturing, CDs retailed above $15 throughout the decade, with some, like Led Zeppelin's fourth album, selling for $24.99 ($41 in 2020s dollars), long after production costs were recouped. "As vinyl and pre-recorded cassettes fell out of favor, people began collecting CDs, which were much more expensive, and offered higher profit margins for record labels." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r8AazBVoS7g
**Shift in Music Consumption and Industry Strategy** : As CDs gained popularity, people not only bought new releases on disc but also replaced their existing vinyl and cassette collections. This coincided with the industry phasing out singles, forcing consumers to purchase full $20 albums even if they only wanted one song, thereby boosting album sales and label profits. "And not only were people buying CDs, but they were buying discs to replace their old vinyl and cassettes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r8AazBVoS7g
**True Inventor Jim Russell (1970)** : Jim Russell is identified as the true inventor of the compact disk, predating the well-known collaborations between Phillips and Sony in the 1970s. By 1970, Russell, a scientist frustrated by the wear of vinyl records, had patented the first optical recording and playback device. "Jim is probably the true inventor of the compact disk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
