## curiosities
**Introduction of Distorted Strings in *Gear of the Sneech*** : The album *Gear of the Sneech* features a surprising and significant presence of distorted string sounds, an element that was previously not dominant in Dead Grips' musical repertoire. While "Hothead" from *Buttonlesspeak* had some guitar, the extensive and distorted use of strings in the new album marks a notable sonic evolution for the band, heavily influenced by the involvement of De Chambelor. **Introduction to Rockabilly** : The introduction of a string section to Roy Orbison's music with the Nashville A-team on "Uptown" was a novel concept in Nashville at the time, particularly for a track that was otherwise considered rockabilly. "the introduction of a string section along with the Nashville A team. This was something that was only just starting to be done in Nashville, and it made little sense to most people involved that Orbison would want strings and would otherwise be a rockabilly track, but they went ahead." ← Episode 83： ＂Only the Lonely＂ by Roy Orbison | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uDvkjXa1ALk
**Philadelphia Orchestra Members in MFSP** : The house band for Philadelphia International Records, MFSP, included members of the Philadelphia Orchestra, who contributed to the prominent and distinctive string arrangements of the Philly Sound, enjoying the opportunity to play "a little funky" music as a change from classical repertoire. **Remote Recording for "One Tree Hill"** : The string parts for U2's "One Tree Hill" were recorded by Toronto musicians Dick Paul and Adele Armin at Grand Avenue Studios in Hamilton. The recording session was directed remotely by Daniel N. Waw and The Edge through a six-hour trans-Atlantic phone call.
