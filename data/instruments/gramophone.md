# instrument - Gramophone

## curiosities
**Berliner's Flat Disk Invention (1888)** : The gramophone, invented by Emil Berliner, was the first device to store music on a flat rotating disk. He demonstrated it in 1888 and secured patents (number 564,586) in 1896. This design quickly gained popularity over Edison's phonograph cylinders because it sounded better, disks lasted longer, and offered double the capacity with two sides. "The guy who came up with the idea of storing music on a flat rotating disk was Emil Berliner. He first demonstrated his device, which he called the gramophone, in 1888." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VlIErdSSVOM
**Early Sound Capture Device (1887)** : Emil Berliner's gramophone, introduced in 1887, was an early machine designed to capture sound. Like its predecessor, it had a three-minute recording limit, which inadvertently standardized the length of popular music for decades. "Emil Berliner's gramophone, which debuted ten years later, were the first machines able to capture sound up to three minutes at a time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mNqqLDpUuZY
**Spreading "Dirty Lyrics" (c. 1900)** : Around 1900, with the rise of the gramophone and phonograph, people quickly began recording songs with "dirty lyrics." This new technology facilitated the widespread dissemination of such content, leading to considerable public outrage about the perceived foulness music was bringing. "When the gramophone and phonograph first took off around 1900, it didn't take long for people to start recording songs with dirty lyrics, and there was plenty of outrage." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L0j9iDuHQVA
**Superior Side-to-Side Tracking** : Berliner's gramophone records used a superior technology where the stylus moved from side to side in the grooves. This design resulted in fewer skips and increased the record's lifespan compared to Edison's up-and-down tracking system. "Berliner's stylus moved from side to side. This meant that the record skipped last and lasted longer. Gramophone records used superior technology." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VlIErdSSVOM
