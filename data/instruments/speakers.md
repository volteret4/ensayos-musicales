**Nazi Concentration Camp Control** : In Nazi concentration camps, if no live orchestra was present, music was played over loudspeakers. Prisoners were forced to walk and work endlessly in time to the music, which was often the same songs repeated, and inmates were forced to sing along loudly to prevent communication. "If there was no orchestra, then the music was played over loudspeakers. **Operation Just Cause (1989)** : During the US invasion of Panama in December 1989, General Maxwell "Mad Max" Thurman ordered speakers to be placed around the perimeter of the Vatican Embassy in Panama City. Music was then pumped through at full volume for 24 hours a day to psychologically pressure Manuel Noriega to surrender. "General Maxwell Mad Max Thurman ordered that speakers be placed around the perimeter of the embassy and they returned to full volume and music was pumped through 24 hours a day." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=R1wdsGt07Gg

This was a countermeasure to drown out propaganda announcements from North Korea, whose weaker speakers were overwhelmed. "In South Korea, speakers were set up along the south side of the demilitarized zone, and K-pop songs were blasted to the north." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=R1wdsGt07Gg
