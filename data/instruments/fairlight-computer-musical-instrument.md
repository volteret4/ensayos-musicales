# instrument - Fairlight Computer Musical Instrument

## curiosities
**Comparison to Akai S-1000** : By 1988, the Akai S-1000 was 100 times more powerful than the original Fairlight and one-tenth of its price. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**High Cost and Studio Use ($30,000+)** : Initially selling for $30,000 and up, the Fairlight was primarily affordable only for high-end recording studios. Its capabilities were crucial for creating sounds like those in Frankie Goes to Hollywood's "Relax." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**Pioneering Digital Sampler and Synthesizer (Australia)** : An Australian company developed the Fairlight CMI, a device capable of synthesizing sounds, sequencing events, and, significantly, digitally sampling sounds for the first time. It allowed users to record, isolate, and manipulate any sound. Samples appeared as waveforms on a black and white monitor and could be manipulated with a light pen. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
