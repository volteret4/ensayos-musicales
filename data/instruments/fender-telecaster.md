# instrument - Fender Telecaster

## curiosities
**Name Origin (Marketing)** : The name "Telecaster" was a deliberate marketing choice in 1951, intended to capitalize on the public fascination with television, which was then the biggest new home appliance. Fender aimed to associate its new guitar with this popular phenomenon. "The name Telecaster was deliberately chosen as an of the moment marketing move because television was the biggest thing in home appliances in 1951. The offender was hoping to piggyback on the phenomenon and it wasn't the first time he would do that." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=E6cXyaHALBQ
**Notable Players** : Many prominent musicians have played the Telecaster, including Billy Joe Armstrong, Bob Dylan, Eric Clapton, Graham Coxon, George Harrison, Chris Yhine, Johnny Mar, Alex Lifeson, Tom Morello, and Bruce Springsteen. "Billy Joe Armstrong, Bob Dylan, Eric Clapton, Graham Coxon, George Harrison, Chris Yhine, Johnny Mar, Alex Lifeson, Tom Morello, Bruce Springsteen, they all play Telecasters. And there were dozens and dozens and dozens more big names." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=E6cXyaHALBQ
**Official Release and Impact** : The Fender Telecaster officially went on sale in 1951. It is often compared to the Ford Model T for its role in making the electric guitar mainstream and widely accessible to the masses. The instrument achieved and maintains immense popularity due to its sound, appearance, feel, and versatility. "And officially went on sale in 1951. The Telly has been compared to the Ford Model T. Just as that car brought motoring to the masses, the Telecaster made the electric guitar mainstream. It's really hard to overstate how popular this electric guitar became and how popular it still is today. The sound and the look and the feel and the versatility of the Telly made it incredibly popular." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=E6cXyaHALBQ
**Precursor to the Stratocaster** : The Fender Telecaster is recognized as the precursor to the Stratocaster. It was a one-pickup electric guitar and holds the distinction of being the world's first mass-produced and commercially successful electric guitar. "This is one of the all-time classic amps introduced in 1952 between the introduction of the Fender Telecaster, a one pickup electric, which was also the world's first mass produced and commercially successful electric guitar, and the unveiling of the Stratocaster in 1954." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
