## curiosities
**LP Introduction and Impact (June 1948)** : The introduction of the 12-inch long-playing (LP) vinyl album by Columbia Records in June 1948 marked a significant shift in the music industry. Its large 12x12 inch double-sided packaging created a need for more textual content, particularly as LPs initially catered to "high-brow" music genres like classical and jazz, leading to the commissioning of informative essays and the popularization of the term "liner notes."
