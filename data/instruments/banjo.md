# instrument - Banjo

## curiosities
**African Origin** : The banjo is an instrument with African origins, indicating a direct link between African culture and the foundational elements of country music. **African Origin and Hillbilly Music** : The banjo is an instrument that came from Africa, and its rhythmic influences, combined with storytelling in song, were integral to the development of "hillbilly music," an ancestor of country music. "mix in the rhythmic influences and instruments like the banjo which came from Africa and you have hillbilly music which is an ancestor of country." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 23 - Women of Country Music in the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/023-country-women-72820-2-45-pm
**Challenging Perceptions (2008)** : In early 2008, the banjo was considered an unconventional instrument in the mainstream music industry, with a "banjo band" like Mumford & Sons facing skepticism from record label executives who questioned if they could be "the next big alternative band." "Yes, sir. They play a banjo. Good Lord Smith. This is 2008. We need the next big alternative band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P1RyOchn7pk ← banjo ← banjo
**Ditching in Country Music (Early 1960s)** : From the early 1960s, a decision was made in country radio to move away from traditional country instrumentation like banjos and fiddles, opting for a smoother, more pop-oriented sound to attract a broader audience. "But here, I will just say that this had been going on since the early 60s when someone in radio decided that country in Western should ditch the banjos and the fiddles." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 40 - Honestly Loving Olivia Newton-John | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-40-honestly-loving-olivia-newton-john
**Experimental Inclusion in Alt Rock (Late 2000s)** : In the late 2000s, the banjo was proposed as a new and experimental instrument to integrate into alt-rock, suggesting a form of folk and bluegrass-influenced music to broaden the genre's appeal during a period of innovation. "Well, how about banjos? Those haven't been tried much in alt-rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_yslM5oanRo ← banjo
**Incongruous with Motown Sound** : The banjo was featured in The Supremes' song "The Man with the Rock and Roll Banjo Band," which aimed to replicate the feel of "Buenos Aires Dance with the Guitar Man." Despite its virtues, the instrument was considered ill-fitting for the characteristic Motown sound, contributing to the song's perceived poor quality. "the banjo, and instruments which has many virtues, but which does not really fit into the Motown sound." ← Episode 116： ＂Where Did Our Love Go？＂ by The Supremes | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g2PetxEXQC4
**Off-beat Emphasis in Mento** : In Mento music, the banjo often played a crucial role in stressing the off-beats in a 4/4 rhythm, a technique that Edward Seaga later applied to the guitar in Ska's development. "in mental, you'd often have a banjo stress the off beats." ← Episode 114： ＂My Boy Lollipop＂ by Millie | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kf-DtqoOsRI
**Pop Song Feature** : The banjo was featured in pop songs of the early 1970s, such as The Stampeders' "Sweet City Woman" and BJ Thomas's "Raindrops Keep Fallin' on My Head" from the *Butch Cassidy and Sundance Kid* soundtrack. In "Sweet City Woman," its use was clever, serving as a reminder of The Stampeders' origins in Calgary, a western city, contrasting with their move to Toronto. "Now this is not the only pop song of that era to feature a little bit of banjo." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 60 - One-Hit Wonders of the 70s | https://www.ftr70.com
**Prominence in 2010s Alt Rock** : Banjos were prominently featured in the music of a new generation of individual singer-songwriters, duos, or small combos in the 2010s, particularly in the softer side of alt rock. "What kind of music was big? **Rural Mento Instrument** : The banjo was a core instrument in Rural Mento, one of the two main variants of Jamaican Mento music, contributing to its resemblance to American country music and British Skiffle. "Rural Mentor, was based around instruments like the Banjo." ← Episode 114： ＂My Boy Lollipop＂ by Millie | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kf-DtqoOsRI
**Traditional Country Element** : Banjos are regarded as an essential component of traditional country music, but country radio in the 1970s deliberately moved away from featuring them. "Fiddles and banjos that really are an essential piece of traditional country music." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 2 - Countryish Music of the 1970s (Encore) | https://www.ftr70.com/
**Traditional Trag Jazz Instrument** : The banjo is a traditional instrument used in Trag Jazz, a revival of Dixieland Jazz. Trag Jazz music was explicitly made with banjos, among other brass and rhythm instruments. "That meant music made with trumpets, the trombone, the clarinet, the banjo, upright bass and drums." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zNIVqKqAlnk ← banjo
