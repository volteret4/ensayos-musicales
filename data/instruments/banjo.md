# instrument - Banjo

## curiosities
**Challenging Perceptions (2008)** : In early 2008, the banjo was considered an unconventional instrument in the mainstream music industry, with a "banjo band" like Mumford & Sons facing skepticism from record label executives who questioned if they could be "the next big alternative band." "Yes, sir. They play a banjo. Good Lord Smith. This is 2008. We need the next big alternative band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P1RyOchn7pk
**Experimental Inclusion in Alt Rock (Late 2000s)** : In the late 2000s, the banjo was proposed as a new and experimental instrument to integrate into alt-rock, suggesting a form of folk and bluegrass-influenced music to broaden the genre's appeal during a period of innovation. "Well, how about banjos? Those haven't been tried much in alt-rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_yslM5oanRo
**Prominence in 2010s Alt Rock** : Banjos were prominently featured in the music of a new generation of individual singer-songwriters, duos, or small combos in the 2010s, particularly in the softer side of alt rock. "What kind of music was big? Well, acoustic guitars, mandalins, banjos, ukulele's, accordions, played by a new generation of individual singer-songwriters, duos, or small combos." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Traditional Trag Jazz Instrument** : The banjo is a traditional instrument used in Trag Jazz, a revival of Dixieland Jazz. Trag Jazz music was explicitly made with banjos, among other brass and rhythm instruments. "That meant music made with trumpets, the trombone, the clarinet, the banjo, upright bass and drums." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zNIVqKqAlnk
