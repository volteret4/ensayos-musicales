# instrument - Streaming platforms

## curiosities
**Dominance in Music Consumption (2017 onwards)** : Streaming overtook physical formats as the primary method of music consumption in 2017. By the close of the 2010s, streaming accounted for 80% of all music consumption, marking a profound shift in how people access and listen to music. "Streaming overtook physical formats as the main way people got their music in 2017. By the end of the decade, 80% of all music consumption was done via streaming." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mNqqLDpUuZY
**Impact on Album Sales** : Streaming significantly contributed to the decline of album sales in the 2010s. By promoting individual songs on playlists, often compiled by algorithms based on mood or activity, streaming services de-emphasized the album as a cohesive listening experience, leading to a focus on singles and shorter EPs. "It did its very best to kill the album. We've already talked about how sales of both CDs and digital albums cratered during the 2010s. Listening to albums was largely replaced by listening to individual songs on playlists." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mNqqLDpUuZY
**Reduced Music Piracy** : Streaming services dramatically reduced the problem of music piracy. The convenience and low cost of accessing complete, proper songs from platforms like Spotify made the effort of torrenting, seeding, and cataloging illegal files largely unnecessary for many users. "Music piracy wasn't exactly wiped out, but it sure became less of a problem because of streaming." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mNqqLDpUuZY
**Rise and Consolidation (2001-2010s)** : Legal music streaming began with Rhapsody in December 2001, later rebranding as a legal Napster. Competitors like Amazon and Deezer (2007) and Spotify (2008) followed. By 2010, rights issues were resolved, leading to a flood of companies, many of which (Video, Groove Shark, Songza, etc.) subsequently shut down or merged. By the end of the decade, about 40 Digital Streaming Providers (DSPs) were active globally, with Spotify, Apple Music, and Amazon Music dominating. "Legal streaming, as we know, it really begins with a platform called Rhapsody, which debuted on December 3rd, 2001. It was then rebranded as a legal version of Napster... A ton of competitors followed, most notably Amazon and the Paris-based diesel, both debuting in 2007, and then Spotify in 2008." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mNqqLDpUuZY
