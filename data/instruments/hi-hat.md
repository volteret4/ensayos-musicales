# instrument - Hi-hat

## curiosities
**Barney Walberg, Inventor in 1926** : The invention of the modern hi-hat is attributed to Barney Walberg in 1926, while he was working for a small company specializing in drum accessories. These new time-keeping devices quickly became popular and were widely adopted by jazz players. "The guy who invented the modern hi-hat seems to be a dude named Barney Walberg, this was 1926, when he was working with a small company that made drum accessories." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WL3zEWJJNuw
**Post-1927 Development and Function** : The hi-hat emerged as a new development after 1927, designed as two cymbals positioned facing each other and sitting one on top of the other. Mounted on a hi-hat stand, a foot pedal allows these cymbals to be moved apart or clanged together. Drummers can also strike hi-hats with sticks, creating various sounds depending on whether they are open, closed, or a combination. "A new development was the hi-hat." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WL3zEWJJNuw
**Standardization of Diameter** : Hi-hat cymbals were soon standardized to a diameter of 14 inches. While various sizes are available today, 14 inches remains the most common and widely used size. "Hi-hat symbols were soon standardized at 14 inches in diameter." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WL3zEWJJNuw
