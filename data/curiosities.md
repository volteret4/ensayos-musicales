**Record Labels' Pivot to Alt-Rock** : Following the revelation of Nirvana's Nevermind selling 300,000 copies a week after SoundScan, record labels initiated a "Grunge Gold Rush" to find more alt-rock; this later expanded to include other alt-rock bands like Jane's Addiction, Red Hot Chili Peppers, Sonic Youth, and R.E.M.
**Revival of Pressing Plants** : old, mothballed pressing plants came back online, and new manufacturers started up with state-of-the-art equipment due to demand.
**Rise of Female-Fronted Bands in Punk** : Punk rock brought "new egalitarian values," leading fans to embrace groups fronted by, or entirely consisting of, women, including Blondie, The Pretenders, The Slits, Patti Smith, The Raincoats, Siouxsie and the Banshees, and The Plasmatics.

**Ronald Reagan's Influence on Hardcore Punk** : President Ronald Reagan's deeply conservative and hawkish political policies were met with strong disapproval by many young people, leading to the emergence of hardcore punk as a form of rebellion. This political climate fostered the need, desire, and demand for a new generation of independent record labels in the early 1980s, which quickly expanded beyond the hardcore genre itself.

**Sexism in Early Rock Music** : Prevailing sexist attitudes in the late 1960s and early 1970s perpetuated the idea that female-fronted rock bands were "silly" and "could not rock," contributing to the breakup of The Ace of Cups by 1972. This changed significantly with the emergence of punk rock.

**Slam Dancing Emergence (1977, Southern California)** : An aggressive form of dancing that emerged in the hardcore punk scene, involving deliberately crashing or slamming into other dancers. It was seen as a response to hippie culture and disco, often driven by a desire for aggression and "testosterone."

**SoundScan Implementation (1991)** : A new system introduced on March 1, 1991, which counted album sales by scanning barcodes at checkout, providing a "much, much, much more accurate picture" of sales compared to previous methods.
**SoundScan's Impact on Genre Perception** : The first SoundScan report revealed that country music and "left of center rock music" (alt-rock) were selling significantly better than previously known, leading record labels to rethink their strategies.
**Spin Magazine "California Dreaming" (1992)** : An article published in the summer of 1992 that highlighted various melodic punk bands, including those on Lookout Records like Green Day, signaling a turning point for pop punk's wider recognition.
**Stage Diving History** : The act of audience members getting onto the stage and immediately diving back on top of the audience. Documented instances date back to The Rolling Stones in Germany (1964), Iggy Pop with The Stooges (1969), and Jim Morrison with The Doors (around 1969). Dee Snider of Twisted Sister also claims to have invented it in the 1970s.
