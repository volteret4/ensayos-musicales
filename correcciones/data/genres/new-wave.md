# genre - New Wave

## curiosities
**"Punk Only Intelligent"** : New Wave was frequently characterized as "like punk only intelligent," a description that highlighted its experimental and artistic dimensions while retaining the raw energy and attitude of its punk predecessor. This broad definition allowed for significant "wiggle room" and fostered diverse musical approaches. "People described new wave as like punk only intelligent." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iL9KpQFqH4o
**1978 Emergence and Characteristics** : New Wave began to appear in 1978 as punk was peaking and burning out. It resulted from a cross-pollination between punk's attitude and pop's melody, featuring twitchy, spiky, choppy, and nervous sounds with experimental structures, geeky vocals, artsy intellectualism, and a suburban vibe, contrasting with traditional rock's blues roots. "Pop and punk had a baby, and they called it New Wave." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**1978-1984 Era and Evolution** : The New Wave scene, roughly from 1978 to 1984, can be divided into two periods. The first saw a proliferation of guitar-driven alternative pop bands, liberated by punk to experiment and emerging from cities beyond New York and London, such as Toronto, Chicago, Los Angeles, and Akron, Ohio. "And as these new groups started to come to our attention, many of them came from cities other than New York and London, the two main centers of punk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**1980s New Wave Hairstyling Trends** : The 1980s New Wave hairstyles were characterized by a focus on cutting-edge styles, including dramatic "swoops," "wings" on the sides, small ponytails, and significant height achieved through heavy use of products like Aqua Net hairspray, mousse, and industrial-strength hair gels. "Height was important. We had to destroy a lot of ozone with cans and cans of aquennet to get the right amount of loft and stiffness." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q3zkJX8S4Xc
**American Marketing Strategy** : In New York City, Sire Records faced a marketing challenge as mainstream radio stations refused to play their punk signings like The Ramones. During a marketing meeting, the term "new wave" was proposed as a substitute for "punk" to describe new artists. This strategic re-branding successfully "wiped away" the stigmas associated with punk, facilitating radio play and broader acceptance. "At a marketing meeting at Sire Records, someone suggested that they use the term new wave in place of punk to describe their new signings. And it worked, just like that." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iL9KpQFqH4o
**American Radio Reluctance and MTV Breakthrough** : Despite commercial success for some acts, American FM Rock Radio remained hesitant to play New Wave, often banning the term "New Wave" on air, which hampered many bands. This resistance persisted until August 1, 1981, when MTV launched, breaking the genre wide open. "Thanks to the reluctance of American radio to play New Wave music, the whole thing seemed to be on its way out." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**American Version (Late 1970s)** : In North America, punk was deemed too political, crass, and violent. Sire Records, led by Seymour Stein, deliberately co-opted "New Wave" for its new signings (like The Ramones and Talking Heads) through a "Don't Call It Punk" marketing campaign, to make the music more palatable to the American audience. "Sire launched a marketing campaign for these new signings under the banner, don't call it punk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
**Antidote to Punk Nihilism and Recession** : Emerging after the "nihilism of punk" and during the "brutal recession of the early 80s," New Wave offered an antidote. It embraced "fun and romanticism," took elements from the past with a "modern sheen," and sang about "love in the future" rather than present-day despair, providing an escape and optimism. "After the nihilism of punk and all of this talk about burning down the past and rebuilding the present, people were actually ready for something new." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjTWcy8RHEs
**Appeal During Economic Downturn** : The "gloss and the fun and the escape" offered by New Wave music were precisely what people needed during the early 1980s when the economy was struggling. It provided a much-needed diversion from difficult times. "The gloss and the fun and the escape they offered was just what everyone needed when the economy was in the toilet." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjTWcy8RHEs
