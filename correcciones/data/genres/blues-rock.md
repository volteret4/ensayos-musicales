# genre - Blues Rock

## curiosities
**Blues Rock → Hard Rock → Heavy Metal; Heavy Metal Term Popularized by Steppenwolf** : Blues Rock became the progenitor of Hard Rock, a term that began appearing around 1968, which led into Heavy Metal by the end of the decade. Heavy metal was largely a British invention — Led Zeppelin, Black Sabbath, Deep Purple — though the term "Heavy Metal" itself was popularized by the Canadian singer John Kay of Steppenwolf. "Blues Rock became known as the progenitor of Hard Rock, a term that began showing up around 1968, which led us into heavy metal by the end of the decade." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M11CC71FSWE
**Early Sound of The Tragically Hip** : The Tragically Hip's early performances, particularly in Eastern Ontario venues like the Tarapin Tavern and Lakeview Manor, largely featured blues rock standards. Their setlists included material from influential artists such as The Rolling Stones, The Doors, The Yardbirds, Marvin Gaye, Otis Redding, and Van Morrison. "Their set consisted largely of blues rock standards. Early stuff from the stones, material from the doors, the yard birds, Marvin Gaye, Otis Redding, Ben Morrison, that kind of thing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dnz_Ycd_q7s
**Post-1978 Rock Diversification** : Blues rock was a genre that became a specific point of discussion within mainstream rock after 1978, reflecting the new complexity in music categorization. "People were now talking about metal and Prague and folk rock, and soft rock, and country rock, and blues rock, and of course, punk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
