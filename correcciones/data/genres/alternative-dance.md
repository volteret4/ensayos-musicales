# genre - Alternative Dance

## curiosities
**British Rave Scene Descendant** : Most of the better-known Alternative Dance came from the UK, being a "direct descendant of the British rave and dance and DJ scene of the 1980s." "Most of the better known Alternative Dance we got came from the UK. It's a direct descendant of the British rave and dance and DJ scene of the 1980s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Continuum with Pure Dance Music** : Alternative Dance formed a "vast continuum linking Alternative Music proper with pure dance music," blurring the lines between these genres and expanding the possibilities of electronic music in the mainstream. "It was a vast continuum linking Alternative Music proper with pure dance music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Electronic Basis** : Alternative Dance is rooted in electronics, heavily utilizing "lots of keyboards and drum machines and loops and samples" to create its distinct sound. "Again, this was based in electronics. Lots of keyboards and drum machines and loops and samples." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Evolution of 80s Technopop** : Essentially, Alternative Dance was described as "souped up 80s Technopop," infused with indie rock sensibilities, indie attitudes, and DJ techniques, including "Turntableism." "Basically, Alternative Dance is souped up 80s Technopop, dressed with Indy Rock and Indy Attitudes, infused together with DJ moves and something that became known as Turntableism." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Explosion of Flavors in the 90s** : As the 90s progressed, the number of artists under the Alternative Dance umbrella "exploded," leading to "a million flavors" such as Post disco, electrical clash, new rave, and big beat. "As the 90s progressed, the number of artists that fell under the umbrella of Alternative Dance exploded. A million flavors appeared." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Post-Punk Subgenre** : Alternative dance music emerged from Technopop, forming part of the diverse subgenres that arose during the post-punk era. "and a form of alternative dance" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
