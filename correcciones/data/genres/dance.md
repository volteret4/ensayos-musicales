# genre - Dance

## curiosities
**Blending with Alt Rock in 2000s** : In the 2000s, acts successfully blended alt rock sensibilities with "a million different types of dance influences," ranging from old school raves and disco to house, R&B, and hip hop, contributing to the genre's fragmentation. "These acts blended alt rock sensibilities with a million different types of dance influences, from old school raves to disco to house to R&B to certain elements of hip hop." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_yslM5oanRo
**D:Ream's Genre** : The band D:Ream (referred to as "Dream" in the transcript), featuring Brian Cox, is described as a dance act. "Brian Cox was a member of a dance act called Dream." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jMfH9xAOpI
**Electronic Elements in 80s Alt Rock** : Prior to the dominance of grunge guitars, the 1980s alt rock scene featured significant "dancey electronic elements," with alternative kids dancing to keyboard-bass bands like Depeche Mode, New Order, and Duran Duran. This attitude saw a revival in the 2000s. "Like the 80s, the odds alt rock scene had its dancey electronic elements." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_yslM5oanRo
**Key Genre for Faithless** : Faithless, the British band fronted by Maxi Jazz, is characterized as an electronic/hip-hop/dance band, indicating dance music as a core component of their sound. "Faithless, the British electronic slash hip-hop, slash dance, slash whatever band, tormented by a guy named Maxi Jazz." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_1NNvxgx8uA
**Sampling "Walk on the Wild Side" Bassline** : The distinctive sliding bass line from Lou Reed's "Walk on the Wild Side," created by Herbie Flowers, has been sampled countless times in both hip-hop and dance songs around the world. This highlights its enduring impact on global music production. "It has since been sampled countless times in hip-hop and dance songs around the world." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0xd0Hf5REg4
