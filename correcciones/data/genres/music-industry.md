# genre - Music Industry

## curiosities
**Canadian Content Policy (1971)** : In 1971, Canada lacked a developed music industry. A government policy mandating that 30% of radio broadcasts feature Canadian-origin music spurred the creation of a national music infrastructure, leading to the establishment of record labels and recording studios. "In the year 1971, there really wasn't any kind of music industry in Canada." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N5kSZOl-5UY
**Crisis in Pro-Recording Studios (Post-Millennium)** : Since the beginning of the millennium, professional recording studios have faced a crisis due to high maintenance costs, the continuous need for technology upgrades, and significantly reduced recording budgets. Advances in home recording technology further contribute to this challenge. "Pro-Recording studios have been in crisis since the beginning of the millennium." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N5kSZOl-5UY
**Current Use Cases for Big Studios** : Presently, large recording studios are primarily utilized for specific requirements, such as recording a distinctive live drum sound that demands a particular room acoustic, or for film scores that necessitate a large orchestra. Otherwise, artists increasingly opt not to use big studios. "Unless you need a room to record a specific live drum sound, or unless you're doing a movie score that requires a large orchestra, people are opting not to use big studios." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N5kSZOl-5UY
**Evolution of Studio Business Models (Education)** : To ensure financial viability, many recording studios have diversified their operations by establishing trade schools. These institutions teach subjects ranging from recording engineering to sound reinforcement and music industry law, with the educational component subsidizing their core recording business. "A facility like Metalworks can afford to stay in business because it's now more than just a recording studio." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N5kSZOl-5UY
**Label Funding Model (Pre-Millennium)** : Historically, the music industry was highly profitable, enabling record labels to readily advance funds for recording costs, which were then recouped from the profits generated by future album sales. "Your label would happily advance you the money to cover your recording costs because they were just going to take it out of the profits derived from the future sales of that album." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N5kSZOl-5UY
**Ongoing Relevance of Professional Studios** : Despite the prevalence of home recording, large-scale professional recording studios maintain their relevance for specific sounds, particular needs, and recording environments that cannot be replicated at home. "There are some things, some sounds, and some needs that require a dedicated recording studio environment." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N5kSZOl-5UY
**Recording Studio Building Boom** : The significant profitability of the music industry in earlier eras spurred the construction of numerous large, expensive recording studios. These facilities were established in major centers like New York, Los Angeles, and London, as well as in more secluded locales such as countryside chateaus or exotic islands. "Because there was so much money to be made, a lot of big, expensive recording studios were built." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N5kSZOl-5UY
**Shift to Home Recording** : Contemporary technological advancements, including laptops and even smartphones, now enable musicians to produce high-quality, professional-sounding albums in home environments. "These days, though, it's possible to make a very good sounding album on a laptop in your bedroom." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N5kSZOl-5UY
**Studio Closures Due to Industry Changes** : Many recording studios have been forced to close down due to profound shifts in the music industry and the high operational costs associated with maintaining a studio, particularly when booking rates decline. "But then there were those facilities that have been forced to shut down, killed by the massive changes to the music industry and the high cost of maintaining a studio when bookings are down." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N5kSZOl-5UY
**Traditional Recording Studio Costs (Pre-Millennium)** : In earlier decades, making an album necessitated renting expensive recording studios, incurring hourly, daily, weekly, or monthly rates. Additional costs included producers, engineers, recording tape, and catering, making the process very expensive. "In addition to paying an hourly, daily, weekly, or monthly rate, you needed to pay for a producer, an engineer or two, all the recording tape you used, and any catering that was required." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N5kSZOl-5UY
