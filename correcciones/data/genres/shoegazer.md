# genre - Shoegazer

## curiosities
**"Loveless" as Epitome (1991)** : My Bloody Valentine's album "Loveless" released in 1991, is identified as the epitome of the shoegazer sound, which subsequently influenced many other artists. "After releasing the Lovelace album in 1991, that groundbreaking noise pop album that was the epitome of the shoegazer sound and everything that descended from it, not a lot happened." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJGFUlQTUhQ
**Outskirts of Britpop Universe** : Shoegazer bands like My Bloody Valentine, Ride, Lush, Swerve Driver, and Slow Dive existed "circling the outskirts" of the Britpop universe, indicating a distinct but co-existing musical scene. "Circling the outskirts of this universe were shoe-gazer bands like My Bloody Valentine and Ride and Lush and Swerve Driver and Slow Dive." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DSvoppf1dGY
