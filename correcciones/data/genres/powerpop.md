# genre - Powerpop

## curiosities
**1980s Categorization Confusion** : In the early 1980s, there was a tendency to broadly categorize all short, sharp rock songs as Powerpop, often blurring the lines with New Wave or alternative artists like Tom Petty, Rick Springfield, and Greg Kihn. This period of confusion gradually gave way to a resurgence of big guitar sounds with pop melodies by the mid-decade, influencing bands like R.E.M. "There was a tendency to categorize all short, sharp rock songs as PowerPop. And if they were PowerPop, then they were New Wave, right?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hstJ8M2laho
**Defining Characteristics** : Powerpop is described as a hooky, spiky, sometimes angular sounding, and always fun sort of rock and roll. It typically consists of short, sharp songs performed with a basic lineup of guitars, bass, drums, and vocals. "Powerpop, a hooky, spiky, sometimes angular sounding, and always fun sort of rock and roll, consisting of short, sharp songs using the same basic lineup. Guitars, bass, drums, and vocals." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hstJ8M2laho
**Integration into New Wave** : The late 1970s saw Powerpop integrate into the world of alternative rock, with new wave bands enthusiastically embracing its characteristics. While distinct from punk, the influence of punk was evident in the new wave brand of Powerpop, creating a fusion of spiky, angular, and fun sing-along and dance-friendly songs. "The Nack sat at this nexus of rock pop and this new thing called New Wave. And this is where Powerpop began its integration into the world of alternative rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hstJ8M2laho
**Origins and Evolution** : Powerpop's roots trace back to The Beatles in 1963, extending through British Invasion bands like The Kinks and The Who, and American bands such as The Beach Boys and The Byrds. It began taking definite shape in the early 1970s with bands like The Razberries and Big Star, evolving from a blend of charged-up old-school Top 40 music, bubblegum pop, and garage rock. "Powerpop, as we know it, began taking shape in the early 1970s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hstJ8M2laho
**Timeless Quality** : Powerpop is considered a timeless genre of rock, a style that rarely sounds dated. It's often difficult to determine if a Powerpop song was recorded in, for example, 1972 or 2012, highlighting its enduring appeal and consistent aesthetic. "Powerpop is a timeless sort of rock. It's a style that never seems to sound dated." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hstJ8M2laho
