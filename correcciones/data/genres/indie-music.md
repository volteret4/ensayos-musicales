# genre - Indie Music

## curiosities
**DIY Ethic** : A crucial aspect of being indie is the "do it yourself" (DIY) ethic. Without the extensive infrastructure of a massive label, artists and those committed to distributing their music often have to handle much of the business and marketing themselves, acting as independent business people. "Another aspect of being Indy is the do it yourself, Ethic." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
**Definition of Independent (Indie)** : "Indie" is short for independent, referring to music from artists not directly signed to one of the major multinational record labels (Universal, Sony, Warner). Artists recording for any other label, or operating independently of the three majors, are considered indie. The term describes a type of record company, its signed artists, and the music they create, having nothing to do with a band's sound or record sales. "Indy is, of course, short for independent. This means we're dealing with music from artists who are not signed directly to one of the major multinational record labels." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
**Distribution Deals with Majors** : Many indie acts that gained prominence in the 2010s came from indie labels that maintained distribution deals with major labels. "In many cases, these acts came from the world of indie labels who had distribution deals with the major labels." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Evolution into Mainstream Influence** : By the early 1990s, indie and alternative music began to be taken very seriously. As the number of major labels decreased, the power and importance of independent labels gained more attention, shifting the dynamic of the music industry. "For the early 90s, people had begun to take Indie and alternative music very, very seriously. And as the number of major label shrunk, more attention was paid to the power and importance of independent labels." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Evolution of Labels (Historical Context)** : Initially, all record labels were considered indie, with thousands existing in America alone in the early 1960s (at least 3,000). Over time, larger labels acquired smaller ones, merged, or went out of business, consolidating the industry. By the 1970s, there were six or seven major labels, which has since reduced to just three (Universal, Sony, Warner) today. "Now once upon a time, all record labels were Indy. They were some that were bigger than others like Deca and London and RCA and EMI, but there were literally thousands of small labels that did their own thing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
**Historical Perception and Growth** : Not long ago, indie music was widely ignored, with the belief that any good music would be picked up by a major label. It was often seen as the domain of "weirdos and outliers," not good enough for broad enjoyment. However, this allowed it to grow in its "Petri dish" without capitalistic interference, to the point where it could no longer be ignored by the early 1990s. "There was a time, not that long ago, when indie music was ignored by most people. The thinking was that if the music was any good, then it would have been picked up and released by a major label, right?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Importance in the 2010s** : Indie music, and material from artists not directly signed to major labels, constituted an important aspect of the music of the 2010s. Without the sheer volume of new music and crafty distribution by indie-friendly companies, the decade would have been "pretty empty." "Indie music, or at least material from artists not directly signed to one of the three majors, was an important aspect to the music of the 2010s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Influence on Mainstream Rock** : The styles and trends introduced by independent musicians significantly influenced major label mainstream rock, which would have been "much different" without their contributions. "And without the influence of independent musicians and the styles and trends that they brought along with them, major label mainstream rock would have been much different." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Major Label Subsidiaries** : Many subsidiary labels of the major corporations often adopt the appearance and feel of indie labels, frequently by design, but they are still considered part of the major label machine. This allows for a perceived independent image while still being part of a larger corporate structure. "Many of those subsidiaries have the look and feel of an Indy label, and often that's very much by design, but they are still considered to be part of the major label machine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
**Major Part of 2010s Soundtrack** : Indie music played a major role in the soundtrack of the 2010s, characterized by interesting trends and commonalities. "Indie music was a major part of our soundtrack for the 2010s, and there were some interesting trends and commonalities." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Market Dominance in Streaming Era** : Despite traditional wisdom, independent labels and do-it-yourself musicians account for approximately 96% of all new music uploaded to streaming services daily, a significant shift from the 4% contributed by major labels. "96% of all new music comes from independent musicians." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Rising Market Share** : The market share for indie labels has been increasing by double digits for nearly 25 years. "The market share for indie labels has been rising by double digits for almost 25 years now." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**UK Dominance in Early 1990s** : At the beginning of the 1990s, The Stone Roses were recognized as the biggest Indie band in the UK, signaling the genre's significant popularity and influence during that specific period. "At the beginning of the 1990s the biggest Indie band in the UK was the Stone Roses." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qC0Ee4-dpnI
