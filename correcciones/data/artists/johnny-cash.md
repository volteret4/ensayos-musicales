## albums
**American IV: The Man Comes Around (2002) – 87th Studio Album** : Released in the early 2000s, this was Johnny Cash's 87th studio album, produced by Rick Rubin. It featured Cash covering contemporary songs in his distinctive style, with material written by artists such as Paul Simon, Depeche Mode, Sting, The Beatles, The Eagles, and included a duet with Nick Cave. "The result was American 4, the man comes around. Johnny Cash's 87th studio album." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw
**At San Quentin (1969)** : Released in 1969, this live album contained the unedited version of "A Boy Named Sue," which included a deliberate swear word that the record label initially considered too strong for his fanbase. The album was primarily bought by adults. "One of the very first records I ever owned was Johnny Cash at San Quentin, which was released in 1969." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yPok3MemDmE
**Ride This Train (1960) – Train Song Collection** : Johnny Cash released this album in 1960, featuring a collection of songs all centered around the theme of trains. "Maybe it was Johnny Cash. He released an album in 1960 entitled Ride This Train, featuring a bunch of train songs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Dm5N6yDRxXQ

## songs
**A Boy Named Sue (1969)** : Featured on the album "At San Quentin," this song includes the deliberate use of the phrase "son of a bitch" in its original album version. The record label initially bleeped the word in some contexts due to concerns about Johnny Cash's audience. "But you ought to think me before I die for the gravel any guts in the spittier, because I'm the son of a bitch that named you Sue." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yPok3MemDmE
**Cry Cry Cry** : Johnny Cash performed this song live at the Overton Park Shell concert in Memphis. "There'll be Johnny Cash in the Tennessee too with Cry Cry Cry." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8q2bRZyHnHE
**Folsom Prison Blues** : Johnny Cash performed this song live at the Overton Park Shell concert in Memphis. "There'll be Johnny Cash in the Tennessee too with Cry Cry Cry and Folsom Prison Blues." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8q2bRZyHnHE
**Hurt (2002)** : Cash's rendition of the Nine Inch Nails song is described as "one of the most painfully honest recordings of all time." The accompanying video, which intersperses shots of Cash in his prime with footage of him as an older man, brought Trent Reznor to tears. This cover sold nearly 3 million downloads, accumulated tens of millions of YouTube views, and earned numerous music awards from both the rock and country establishments, becoming the biggest hit of his career. "The result was one of the most painfully honest recordings of all time, especially when you see the video which intersperses shots of Cash and is primed with the older man that he become." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw
**Ring of Fire (1963)** : In 2004, the makers of Preparation H hemorrhoid ointment offered a significant sum of money to Johnny Cash's estate for the use of "Ring of Fire" in a commercial. The estate, however, declined the offer. "In 2004, the people who make preparation H approached the estate of Johnny Cash with bags of money asking, pretty please, could they use a song Ring of Fire in a commercial for their hemorrhoid ointment? They said no." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aSk1XQHNkd8

## curiosities
**Army Service Intercepting Soviet Messages** : As a 19-year-old Air Force recruit in the army, Johnny Cash spent three years stationed in Germany. During this time, his duties involved intercepting Soviet messages that were transmitted in Morse code to their combat aircraft. "he spent three years in Germany intercepting Soviet messages sent in Morse code to their combat aircraft." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HoBx6s3JmVQ
**Backstage Rider – American Flag Requirement** : Johnny Cash, known as "the man in black" and a simple individual, had one non-negotiable item in his rider: an American flag that had to be displayed in full view of the audience during his performances. "The one thing he would not skip on was an American flag that had to be displayed in full view of the audience." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z2hvUDtBxho
**Career Resurgence with Rick Rubin (2000s)** : In the early 2000s, Johnny Cash's career had stagnated, but a connection with producer Rick Rubin led to the concept of Cash covering contemporary songs in his unique style. This collaboration revitalized his career, turning him into an "alternative hero" and solidifying his image as "the original man in black." "But then he connected with producer Rick Rubin and came up with the idea of Cash covering contemporary songs in his own way." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw
**Cash Home and "Hurt" Video Props** : The house featured in the "Hurt" video was Johnny Cash's home for 30 years and had become a museum in Hendersonville, Tennessee, before it tragically burned to the ground in 2007. The chair in which Cash is seen sitting in the video is on permanent display behind glass at the Jody Cash Museum in Nashville. "The house in the video, the Cash home for 30 years in the museum in Hendersonville, Tennessee, was in really rough shape. It burned to the ground in 2007." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw
