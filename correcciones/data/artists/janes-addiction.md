**Jane Says (1988) – Breakthrough for an LA Band** : Recorded over six months in 1987 and 1988 and released on August 23, 1988, "Jane Says" became Jane's Addiction's big breakthrough song. Despite its meandering lyrics, simple two-chord structure, and lack of a traditional chorus (featuring only three verses and a bridge), the song successfully established the band as part of a new wave of LA rock bands in the late 1980s. The song is a direct reflection of the life and struggles of Jane Bainter, Perry Farrell's ex-housemate and muse. "Jane says, have you seen my wig around? I can make it without it. She knows they all want it to go." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk
**Mountain Song** : Kevin Lyman, the founder of the Warped Tour, was inspired to create his own touring festival after watching Perry Farrell's performance of "Mountain Song" during the first Lollapalooza road trip in 1991, which he was stage managing. "Jane's performance of this song on that tour. There was just something about Perry Farrell building up Mountain Song that had Kevin Lyman thinking about his own tour." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Superhero** : This song was licensed for use as the theme for the TV series "Entourage," providing ongoing exposure and revenue for the band through its repeated play at the beginning of each episode. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sfie4YYncxk

"Perry Farrell of Jane's Addiction saw the reaction they got at Reading on August 26th, 1990. He thought to himself, why can't we have this kind of thing in America?
