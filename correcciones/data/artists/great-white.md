## curiosities
**Station Nightclub Fire (February 20, 2003)** : On February 20, 2003, during a Great White performance at the Station Nightclub in Warwick, Rhode Island, pyrotechnics ignited flammable acoustic foam on the walls and ceilings. Within approximately one minute, the club was engulfed in flames, and within two minutes, visibility was lost due to thick black smoke. The subsequent rush to escape led to 100 deaths and 230 injuries. **The Station Nightclub Fire (2003)** : The band was tragically involved in the Station Nightclub fire in February 2003 in West Warwick, Rhode Island. The incident resulted in 100 fatalities, including the band's guitar player, when pyro at the start of their set ignited insulation, causing the venue to go up in flames. "He was the singer of the band Great White, the group that was involved in the horrible station nightclub fire in February 2003, where 100 people, including Great White's guitar player, died.
