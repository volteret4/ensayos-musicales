**Death by Cocaine Overdose (October 21, 1995)** : Shannon Hoon died on October 21, 1995, at the age of 28, from a heart attack brought on by a cocaine overdose. Despite Blind Melon's debut album going platinum four times over and a hit single, Shannon struggled with drug and alcohol issues, attempting rehab at least twice without success, against the advice of his drug counselor. The band had just finished recording their second album, "Soup," and had begun an 18-month tour on September 19, 1995. Following a disappointing show in Houston on October 20, Shannon went on an all-night drug binge. The band arrived in New Orleans early on October 21 to play Tipitina's. Shannon called his girlfriend for 45 minutes, explaining how "messed up" he was, then visited Smoothie King on Canal Street and spent 45 minutes talking to someone on a psychic hotline. He went back to the tour bus instead of the hotel, laid down in bandmate Christopher Thorn's bunk, and was found unresponsive by a sound technician at 1:30 PM. EMS declared him dead. "He was twenty-eight. He's buried in Dayton Cemetery in Dayton, Indiana, and the epitaph is from a blind million song called Change." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sP75_gbZzIU
