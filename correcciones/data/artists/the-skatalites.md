## curiosities
**First Wave Ska Originator** : The Skatalites were an originator of the "first wave" of Ska, a style born in Jamaica in the 1950s. Along with Desmond Dekker and Toots and the Maytals, these artists comprised the initial pioneers of the genre. "Those originators, Desmond Decker, Tutson the Maytaws, the Scott lights and so on, all from Jamaica comprised that first wave." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HSvgRyGiOpE
**The Skatalites (Jamaica, 1963–1967) – Trombone-Led Studio Band; Played on Virtually Every Jamaican Record; Don Drummond Murdered His Girlfriend; Died in Asylum 1969** : Don Drummond was a trombone player who gave Jamaican ska a slightly darker feel. He first called his group The Satellites, then changed the name to The Skatalites. The Skatalites played on virtually every record made in Jamaica between 1963 and 1967, functioning as the equivalent of the studio bands at Motown and Stax in America. Their choppy sound became hugely influential and may have given ska its name. Unfortunately, Don Drummond never saw what became of his invention — in 1964 he murdered his girlfriend and died in an insane asylum in 1969, some say of natural causes, others say he was murdered by gangsters avenging his girlfriend's death, and some claim it was a government plot to suppress the powerful Jamaican music scene.
