## songs
**Holidays in the Sun** : This song was recorded at Wessex Studios with a lineup reduced to Johnny Rotten, Steve Jones (who also played bass after Glenn Matlock was fired), and Paul Cook. Producer Chris Thomas implemented his "mono deluxe" technique, involving layered, pitch-shifted guitar parts and a duplicated bass line one octave lower, to create the band's signature studio sound. "The sex bestows with holiday in the sun recorded at Westc studios in the old church hall behind St. Augustine's church in North London." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QEkGSpxeuMk

"You too, the cure, Marilyn Manson, Roxy Music, the Sex Pistols, Blur, they all get it without going over the top, mainly because each one of them either A went to art school, or B studied art, or C collect art." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RWbKqqW78yg

"Eddie Cochrane, who songs about teenage boredom were later covered by sedentions of the sex pistols." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U
**Disastrous American Tour and Final Performance (1978)** : The Sex Pistols' American tour culminated in their final performance at the Winterland Ballroom in San Francisco on January 14, 1978. The tour was marred by hostile audiences, Sid Vicious's extreme intoxication and altercations, Malcolm McLaren's difficult management, and deep internal animosity, all of which were captured in the film "The Great Rock and Roll Swindle." This show marked the last time the original lineup would perform together for nearly 18 years, and the final appearance with Sid Vicious before his death 13 months later. "It shows a band at the end of their rope and literally ready to blow apart." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1y2UuU-me_s
**Early Fan Base** : Siouxsie Sioux was noted as one of the Sex Pistols' first fans and was present in the audience during their scandalous TV appearance where they swore at the host. "Susie Su is another person with connections to the sex pistols. She was one of their first fans." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oPgeSCy93bo
**Failed Saturday Night Live Debut (1977)** : The Sex Pistols were scheduled to appear on Saturday Night Live on December 17, 1977, intended as the inaugural event of their first North American tour. However, their appearance was canceled when they encountered issues at immigration, facing scrutiny for improper paperwork and questions related to various criminal charges. "The sex pistols have been booked to appear on Saturday Night Live that week. It was supposed to be the kickoff to their first ever North American tour. But then they got nailed to immigration for improper paperwork and with questions about various criminal charges." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1y2UuU-me_s
**First Gig (November 1975)** : The Sex Pistols played their first gig in November 1975, the same month Patti Smith released her influential debut album "Horses." They were contemporaries of The Slits and a foundational band of the punk movement. "That was the same month the sex pistols played their first gig" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Qss5kC2FUx0
**First Live Gig – St. Martins (November 6, 1975)** : The band's debut performance took place at an art school in London, St. Martins, as an unexpected opening act for Bizzouca Joe. They reportedly lasted only about two songs before the school's social programmer halted their performance by pulling the plug. This event, despite its unceremonious ending, was considered a pivotal moment that changed music. "music was never to be quite the same." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oPgeSCy93bo
**Impact on UK Music Scene (1975)** : The Sex Pistols were identified as one of three bands, alongside The Clash and The Saints, that "changed the music forever in the UK," following the media frenzy over The Saints' 1976 single. Their emergence in England coincided with the germination of punk rock in New York by 1975. "Because of that media freak out, three bands changed the music forever in the UK, the sex pistols, the clash, and the Saints." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4
**Lesser Free Trade Hall Gig (1976)** : On Friday, June 4, 1976, just after 7:30 PM, the Sex Pistols played a pivotal gig at the Lesser Free Trade Hall in Manchester. Despite selling tickets for only 50 pence, fewer than 50 people attended, making it a "failed money losing gig." Nonetheless, this concert proved to be a transformative moment for English rock, as the small audience included future members of influential bands such as the Buzzcocks, Pet Shop Boys, Joy Division, and New Order. "Didn't seem like much of the time, but this failed money losing gig became a turning point for English rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1y2UuU-me_s
**Manchester Gig Inspiration (Spring 1976)** : A performance by the Sex Pistols in Manchester, though described as "terrible" by attendees Bernie and Peter, was nonetheless found to be "great" in its raw energy. This concert directly inspired Bernie and Peter to form their own band, which would eventually become Joy Division. "He says, they were terrible. I thought they were great. I wanted to get up there and be terrible too." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oPgeSCy93bo
**Opening Act for The 101ers – Nashville Club** : The band opened for The 101ers at a club called Nashville. Despite their "awful" musicianship, their attitude and energy profoundly impressed Joe, the guitarist for The 101ers, motivating him to leave his band and form The Clash. "It was their attitude and their energy that grabbed his attention." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oPgeSCy93bo
**Perceived Uncompromising Ideology (1976-1977)** : In the nascent stages of British punk, particularly around 1976 and 1977, The Sex Pistols were widely regarded as the most uncompromising and ideologically pure of all the bands in the movement. This perception stood in stark contrast to other bands like The Clash, who faced criticism for signing major label deals. "The clash was seen as the most uncompromising and the most ideologically pure of all the British punk bands." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1y2UuU-me_s
**Rolling Stone Magazine Cover (1977)** : The Sex Pistols were featured on the cover of the October 20th, 1977, edition of Rolling Stone magazine, which carried the headline, "Rock is sick and living in London." This highlights punk's impact and controversy in the late 70s. "The October 20th, 1977 edition of Rolling Stone magazine had the sex pistols on the cover, but the headline, Rock is sick and living in London." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
**TV Show Scandal (December 1, 1976)** : The Sex Pistols appeared on a popular television program where they used "bad words," leading to a significant scandal across Britain. This incident was widely discussed and served as a catalyst for future musicians, inspiring Declan McManus (later Elvis Costello) to pursue a career in music. "All of Britain was talking about it. Terrible, terrible. These young men on TV." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oPgeSCy93bo
**Three-Member Lineup for Recording** : For the recording of "Holidays in the Sun," The Sex Pistols were a three-piece band consisting of Johnny Rotten, Steve Jones, and Paul Cook, as bassist Glenn Matlock had been fired, requiring Steve Jones to perform the bass parts. "When the pistols recorded this next song, they were down to just three members. There was Johnny Rotten, guitarist Steve Jones and drummer Paul Cook. Bass player Glenn Matlock had been fired, so it was up to Steve to play the bass parts." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QEkGSpxeuMk
