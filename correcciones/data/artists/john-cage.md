# artist - John Cage

## albums
**Four Minutes and 33 Seconds (1952) – Silence as Music** : This piece, debuted in 1952, consists of four minutes and 33 seconds of silence. During live performances, musicians sit at their instruments but play nothing, allowing the audience to focus on environmental sounds as an exercise in mindfulness. "When performed live, the musicians just sit at their instruments and play nothing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=72Lei7uug8c

## songs
**4 minutes and 33 seconds (1952)** : This avant-garde composition famously involves a pianist sitting at a keyboard and performing nothing for precisely four minutes and thirty-three seconds. Despite its conceptual nature, a box set of "covers" by 50 different artists, including Depeche Mode, New Order, and Moby, has been released. "John Cage's avant-garde composition, four minutes and thirty three seconds, which in case you didn't know, involves a pianist sitting down at the keyboard and playing exactly nothing for four minutes and thirty three seconds." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HoBx6s3JmVQ

## curiosities
**Box Set of "Covers" for Silent Composition** : John Cage's avant-garde composition, "4 minutes and 33 seconds," which consists of a pianist playing nothing for its duration, has inspired a peculiar box set. This collection features "covers" of the silent piece by 50 different artists, including notable acts like Depeche Mode, New Order, and Moby. "The weirdest box set of all time has to be a collection of covers of John Cage's avant-garde composition, four minutes and thirty three seconds... There's a covers box set, Depeche Mode, New Order, Moby, a total of fifty artists and all participated." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HoBx6s3JmVQ
**Covers Compilation Box Set (2019)** : In 2019, Mute Records released a box set featuring various artists covering John Cage's "Four Minutes and 33 Seconds." The compilation included more than 50 artists, such as Depeche Mode, New Order, Moby, Erasure, and Afghan Whigs, all performing a piece consisting of silence. "More than 50 artists playing nothing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=72Lei7uug8c
