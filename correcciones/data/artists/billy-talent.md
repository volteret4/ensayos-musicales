## albums
**666 (Live Album/DVD)** : This live album and DVD features material from the Rock am Ring Festival, a large event held at the Nürburgring in Germany, which attracts over 50,000 people. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ocMHRLQMQY0
**Afraid of Heights (2016)** : This fifth album was released after a four-year break, during a period (2012-2013) when rock bands were struggling to make waves amidst the dominance of indie rock, characterized by bands like Mumford & Sons. The band expressed initial uncertainty about how the record would be received by Canadian rock stations. Its themes touch upon global political and social realities like Brexit, the refugee crisis, the rise of the right, and Donald Trump. The band views it not as a "political record" but a reflection of current global realities affecting people they know. Drummer Aaron was unable to perform on this album due to an MS relapse, with Jordan Hastings filling in. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ocMHRLQMQY0
**Billy Talent (2003) - Self-Titled Major Label Debut** : This album marked the beginning of the band's major label career in the fall of 2003. It was recorded in Vancouver with local Toronto producer Gavin Brown. The album featured songs that had been worked on for "years and years," though some, like "River Below," were written more quickly. The band's distinctive sound, particularly the singing and guitar playing, made it stand out on Canadian radio. Singles like "Try Honesty" and "River Below" were released. The album's success in Canada was boosted by high rotation on MuchMusic and an increase in the Canadian content requirement for radio to 35%. However, in the US, after "Try Honesty" did well, the label chose not to release another single, marking "the beginning of our open battle with America." "Billy Town is nothing to lose from their self-tapped major label debut album." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GT_u0eFDlk8
**Billy Talent 2 (2006) - Second Studio Album** : This album was released on June 27, 2006, three years after their debut. The band initially had a different title but ultimately settled on "Billy Talent 2" at Ian's suggestion, who thought two bars next to the name would look cool graphically. It was recorded in Vancouver at The Warehouse at Brown Island Studio with Gavin Brown producing, and Ian also became more involved in the production. The album aimed to move beyond the "screamy thing" of the first album, incorporating more melody and expanding the band's sound with songs like "Surrender" and "Pins and Needles." Despite an A&R representative from Atlantic Records stating they didn't hear a "Green Day single" on it, the album became a "fan favorite" and produced some of their biggest singles, including "Devil in a Midnight Mass," "Red Flag," and "Fallen Leaves." The album's success came after intense touring following the first album, driven by a desire to "outdo yourself" and prove themselves on the critical second record. "Devil and a midnight mass, the first single from that second album, cleverly entitled, Billy Talent 2." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GT_u0eFDlk8
**Billy Talent 3 (2009) - Third Studio Album** : Released in 2009, this album featured "Rusted from the Rain" as one of its singles, which was the band's first charting number one. The album was produced by Brendan O'Brien, recorded in Atlanta and Toronto, and took 40 days for recording and mixing in Atlanta. The label (Warner US) initially sought an "A-level" producer like Butch Vig or Brendan O'Brien for the album. The band felt it was a "rock record" compared to their first two "punk" records. Despite the band's confidence, the album was leaked three months before its release and Warner US decided not to release it two months before, forcing them to quickly sign with Roadrunner Records. This last-minute change and the leak "sabotaged" the record's release, though it still performed well in Canada and Germany. It's described as a "polarizing record" for fans, often considered a "grower." "Billy Talent was arrested for the rain, one of the singles from their 2009 album, Billy Talent 3." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GT_u0eFDlk8
**Billy Talent III (2009)** : This was the band's third studio album, produced by Brendan O'Brien. The production involved spontaneous additions like xylophones and background vocals, which O'Brien would improvise and tuck into the mix. This approach taught the band that not everything needs to be perfectly calculated during recording. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ocMHRLQMQY0
**Dead Silence (2012)** : The band's fourth album, released on September 11, 2012. It marked a departure from the band's previous numbered album titles, a decision made due to a desire to shift interview focus from numbering to the music itself. The album debuted at number two on the charts, behind all of Michael Jackson's albums, who had passed away that same week. For this album, the band collaborated with artist Kent Taylor for the artwork, reflecting their commitment to physical art accompanying their releases. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ocMHRLQMQY0
**First Live Album (2006)** : In 2006, Billy Talent released their first live album, a two-CD set recorded during two performances in England: London on September 8th and Manchester on September 16th. These raw, 75-minute sets were sold at concerts and online. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ocMHRLQMQY0

**"Billy Talent 3" Label Rejection and Leak** : The release of "Billy Talent 3" was marred by significant issues. The album was leaked online three months before its scheduled release. Two months prior, Warner US, who the band had signed with after leaving Atlantic, decided they no longer wanted to release it, with Tom Wally pulling the plug. This left them without a US label just before a worldwide release. They ultimately signed with Roadrunner Records last minute, but the leak and delayed US release "sabotaged the record," leading labels to "pull back on the whole release." "it got leaked and then two months before it was supposed to come out Warner US decided that they didn't want to release it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GT_u0eFDlk8
**Aaron Solowoniuk's MS Diagnosis (1998)** : Drummer Aaron Solowoniuk was diagnosed with Multiple Sclerosis (MS) in 1998 at the age of 24. His initial symptoms included buzzing legs, difficulty walking to his job at Chrysler, optic neuritis in his left eye (leading to vision loss), and confusion. He managed the disease for over a decade with medication, needle therapy, and a positive outlook. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ocMHRLQMQY0
**Aaron Solowoniuk's MS Relapse and Album Absence (2016)** : On January 15, 2016, Aaron announced on YouTube that he was experiencing an MS relapse, which had been slowing him down for six to seven months. The symptoms made it difficult to perform daily tasks and play drums. He decided to step away from recording drums for the "Afraid of Heights" album to focus on his health, as he was unsure how long his recovery would take. The band fully supported his decision and brought in Jordan Hastings as a fill-in drummer. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ocMHRLQMQY0
**Aaron Solowoniuk's Treatment and Recovery** : After his relapse, Aaron decided to stop taking his MS medication for three months and explore alternative therapies, focusing on healthy eating and exercise. He expressed a goal to come back stronger, listening to his body more carefully, after having previously pushed himself too hard, to the point of walking with a cane. In 2017, he made a return to the stage, playing one song in Poland, his first performance in a year. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ocMHRLQMQY0

**Unique Guitar Playing Style** : Ian, the band's guitarist, developed a unique, self-taught playing style that combines rhythm and lead parts due to being the sole guitarist. He achieves this by playing in drop D tuning, which allows him to play bar chords with one finger while doing leads with the others, describing it as "almost like piano playing." He was influenced by clean players like Andy Summers and Mark Knopfler, as well as Tom Morello and Kim Thayil, and consciously avoided traditional playing. His style is considered "the most unique in the world." "I ended up trying to do both when I became the sole guitar player in this band. And by doing that, I found like dropping to D, I could play, I could play bar chords with one finger and then do a bunch of leads with the other." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GT_u0eFDlk8
**Vocal Style Development** : Ben, the vocalist, initially spent the first five years of the band trying to emulate other singers like Eddie Vedder, Scott Weiland, and Zack de la Rocha. He eventually realized he couldn't sound like them and developed his own distinctive style. This unique vocal approach, combined with Ian's guitar playing, helped the band stand out from other Canadian bands of the era like Moist, Treble Charger, and The Tea Party.
