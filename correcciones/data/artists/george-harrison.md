**My Sweet Lord (1971)** : George Harrison's song was involved in a long-running plagiarism case against The Chiffons' "He's So Fine" (1963). The judge ultimately ruled that Harrison was guilty of "subconscious plagiarism" or cryptomnesia, meaning he unconsciously replicated the melody, believing it to be an original composition, despite differences in key, arrangement, and instrumentation. The case, which started in 1971, was resolved in 1993, primarily over the ownership of the central melody and back royalties. "George Harrison got caught with this. Because it sounded way too much like this chiffon song from 1963." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fS8V3kYXT6w
