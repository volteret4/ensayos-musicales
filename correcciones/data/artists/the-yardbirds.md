**Early R&B Exploration** : The Yardbirds were among the British bands in the 1960s who attempted to play R&B, which aligned with the musical tastes of the Mod subculture. "Then British bands trying their handed R&B, like the Rolling Stones and the Yard Birds and the Kinks, and especially the Who." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mAlc-N2wn5s
**Influence on Early Tragically Hip** : The Tragically Hip's early setlists largely consisted of blues rock standards, including material from The Yardbirds. "Early stuff from the stones, material from the doors, the yard birds, Marvin Gaye, Otis Redding, Ben Morrison, that kind of thing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dnz_Ycd_q7s
**Jimmy Page Hearing "Dazed and Confused"** : While Jimmy Page was a member of The Yardbirds, he heard Jake Holmes perform his song "Dazed and Confused" in 1968, which he later reworked for Led Zeppelin, leading to a plagiarism dispute. "Jimmy Page heard that version when he was still with the Artbirds." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fS8V3kYXT6w
