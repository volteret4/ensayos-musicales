# artist - Mark Hoppas

## member of
- Blink-182

## curiosities
**"Adam's Song" Inspiration** : He composed "Adam's Song" based on his experiences of loneliness while touring, particularly as a single individual among bandmates with girlfriends. The lyrical themes of depression and suicide were significantly influenced by a suicide letter he had encountered in a teen magazine. "Mark Hopper's remembered how lonely he got on tour." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eljIIqCu5gU
**Construction Job** : While Blink was in its formative years, Mark maintained a day job at a construction site, where he spent his days hauling bags of cement and sand. "Mark, for example, had a gig where he hauled bags of cement and sand at a construction site all day." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eljIIqCu5gU
**Early Life and Family Trauma** : Born in Washington, D.C., his family relocated to San Diego when his father, a Navy member, was transferred to a base there. This move coincided with his parents' divorce, a "broken family thing" that scarred him and led him to move around with his dad while his sister remained in San Diego with their mother. "Mark's father was in the Navy. He was born in Washington, DC, but ended up in San Diego when his father was transferred to the base there. That's when there was a divorce." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eljIIqCu5gU
**Musical Discoveries** : As a teenager, he found solace in music, discovering and becoming influenced by groups such as The Cure and The Smiths. He dedicated a significant portion of his earnings from working at Little Caesar's pizza to purchasing CDs. "He was scarred by the whole broken family thing, which explains why he discovered groups like the cure and the Smiths as a teenager." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eljIIqCu5gU
**Previous Bands and Connection to Tom** : Before joining Blink, Mark, who was a few years older than Tom DeLong and Scott Rainer, had already been a member of "a bunch of bands." He connected with Tom through his sister, who was dating one of Tom's friends, leading to their collaboration in songwriting. "Mark's sister was going out with one of Tom's friends, a connection was made and they started writing songs together." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eljIIqCu5gU
**School Expulsion** : During eighth grade, he was expelled from his school after showing up drunk to a basketball game. This expulsion serendipitously led him to register at another school for a semester, a key event that ultimately connected him with Tom DeLong. "One night he showed up at his school for a basketball game. He was in grade eight and he was drunk. That got him expelled, which meant he had to register at another school for one semester." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eljIIqCu5gU
**Voice Strain during Dude Ranch** : During the recording sessions for "Dude Ranch," Mark severely strained his voice by attempting to sing the album's first single, which was outside his vocal range. This injury accounts for his distinct vocal quality on the song "Dammit" and why Tom DeLong often sang the chorus when the band performed it live. "Mark had really screwed up his voice by trying to sing the first single from the song which was outside his range." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eljIIqCu5gU
