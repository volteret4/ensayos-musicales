## curiosities
**John Hughes Film Exposure (1980s)** : Spandau Ballet was among the artists whose music was prominently featured on the meticulously curated soundtracks of John Hughes' 1980s movies. These film soundtracks were instrumental in exposing numerous North American audiences to various alternative rock acts, especially those from the UK. "So many people learned of bands like The Specials and Spandovalet and Billy Idle and General Public and Wang Chung and Yellow and New Order and the Psychedelic Furs and the Smiths and Echo and the Bunyman, Susanne Vagan in excess the Jesus and Mary chain, Flesh for Luluo and so many others from those soundtrack albums." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM
**New Romantic Descendant** : Spandau Ballet (referred to as "Spana-Belle") is identified as a band that emerged from the New Romantic movement, directly influenced by the legacy of glam rock, particularly David Bowie and Roxy Music. "Spana-Belle." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw
**New Romantic Example** : Listed as one of the bands associated with the New Romanticism movement. "Then there was Visage, Adam in the Ants, Spandovalet." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KF5MwJVYng
**Pioneers of New Romanticism** : Spandau Ballet emerged from the vibrant British club scene of Billies and The Blitz Club, which served as breeding grounds for groups associated with the New Romantic movement. This offshoot of synth-based music was characterized by its distinctive style and electronic sound. "One such offshoot was known as New Romantic, which had its roots in a couple of British clubs dedicated to new music. One was called Billies, and the other was the Blitz Club. This was the breeding site of groups like Spandovalet, Visage, and Culture Club." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
