## songs
**Doctor Who Theme (1963) - Electronic Arrangement** : Delia Derbyshire is renowned for her electronic arrangement of Ron Grainer's "Doctor Who Theme." Joining the BBC Radiophonic Workshop in 1962, she transformed the original composition into an entirely electronic piece, which was heard by viewers from 1963 through 1980 and became one of the most famous pieces of sci-fi music. ## curiosities
**BBC Radiophonic Workshop Career** : Delia Derbyshire joined the BBC Radiophonic Workshop in 1962 and remained there for 11 years, contributing to electronic music for nearly 200 radio and television programs, employing tape machines, oscillators, and sine wave generators. She continued her experimental compositions until 1975, making significant contributions to the history of electronic music. "She stayed for 11 years, working on electronic music for close to 200 radio and TV programs using tape machines and oscillators and sine wave generators." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjQiZqpmQlE
**Electronic Music Pioneer at BBC Radiofonc Workshop** : Delia Derbyshire worked at the BBC Radiofonc Workshop for 11 years, starting in 1962. During her tenure, she composed electronic music for nearly 200 radio and TV programs, utilizing tape machines, oscillators, and sine wave generators in her experimental compositions until 1975, making significant contributions to the field. "She stayed there for 11 years, working on electronic music for close to 200 radio and TV programs, using tape machines and oscillators and sine wave generators, whatever she could get her hands on." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
**Iconic "Doctor Who" Theme Arrangement** : Her most famous work is the electronic arrangement of the theme song for the BBC show "Dr. Who." Although the music was written by composer Ron Grainer, Delia Derbyshire transformed it into its familiar electronic sound, which was heard by viewers at the start of every episode from 1963 to 1980, making it one of the most famous TV theme songs ever. "But she is the one who gave it that familiar electronic arrangement." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
