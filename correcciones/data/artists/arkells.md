## albums
**Deadlines EP (2008) – Self-Released and Re-issued** : This EP was initially self-released by the band, featuring songs recorded around 2005-2006, some in Tim Oxford's basement. It was later re-issued by Dino Lone Records as both a physical CD and digital release. The re-issue included a flyer inserted into the City and Colour record that was distributed across Canada. "It was 2005, 2006 songs? Yeah, yeah, fine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WSe0wdljSGA
**High Noon** : This album is mentioned as a future topic for discussion in part two of the interview. "On part two, we'll cover the recording and release of the Michigan Left album, High Noon, a strange little EP called Study Music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WSe0wdljSGA
**High Noon (2014)** : Recorded over a month in Los Angeles in October 2013, this album was the first to feature Anthony as keyboardist. The band secured Tony Hoffer, known for producing Phoenix, M83, and Beck, after he unexpectedly contacted them via MySpace in 2013, despite earlier attempts to reach him. They rehearsed for two months in Hamilton at Spice Factory, treating it like a "day job," before the L.A. sessions where synthesizers played a prominent role; Anthony subsequently purchased Hoffer's Korg GX3P and Roland D50 keyboards used on the record. "It is the first record with, uh, with Tony in the mix." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKNn01M8GG4
**Jackson Square** : This debut album was recorded quickly, in roughly two weeks, due to budget constraints, with bed tracks recorded in five days and overdubs done in a cheap studio. It was described as a concise encapsulation of four years of live songs, refined through performance, and characterized by a loud, fast rock sound with edgy guitars and super loud drums, reflecting a less open approach to diverse influences at the time. "We recorded Jackson Square in basically, you know, two weeks or a little bit over two weeks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKNn01M8GG4
**Jackson Square (2008) – Debut Full Album** : This album is named after a somewhat dated, brutalist-architecture mall in downtown Hamilton, which for the band symbolized the city's core and its inspiring "fight." The album reflects the city's atmosphere before widespread gentrification, capturing stories that might take place there, such as the one in the song "Abigail." "It felt... I don't know. It was the core of Hamilton and I think for us, there was a part of it that we found to be really like inspiring about Hamilton, sort of like the fight in the city and around that time as well in 2008, I feel like the city was... There was like this light in the distance that we could sort of see that it was changing, that there was this creative community." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WSe0wdljSGA
**Michigan Left** : This album took three years to release after "Jackson Square," recorded at The Bathhouse studio in Bath, Ontario, which is owned by The Tragically Hip. Initial demos were made at the Tokyo Police Club space and in Dan's basement. It was the band's first time self-producing a record with Dan, leading to a somewhat unfocused but experimental month-long process where they tried new arrangements, instrumentation, and effects like delays and gated reverb, and became more open to synth sounds ("Hollow notes"). The album was completed before they signed with Universal, so it was not conceived as a major label record. "This was also your first major label album." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKNn01M8GG4
**Michigan Left (2011)** : This album is referenced as a significant later work, from which the live version of "Whistleblower" was taken. "Arkels in a live version of Whistleblower, a track from their 2011 album Michigan Left." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WSe0wdljSGA
**Morning Report** : This album includes significant songs such as "My Heart's All Yours," "Private School," and "Drake's Dad." During this period, the band began incorporating horn sections and backup singers into their live shows, a practice that later influenced the arrangements on subsequent recordings like "Knockin' at the Door." "The next album is, is Morning Report." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKNn01M8GG4
**Rally Cry (2018)** : Released two months before early 2019, this album is described as having significant energy and a communal feeling, effectively integrating elements and lessons learned from the band's previous records. The spontaneous and open studio approach taken during the creation of the single "Knockin' at the Door" served as an inspiration for the recording sessions of this album. "How do you describe it? Yeah, I think being so close to it is hard to totally describe it, but I can tell you the feeling of it because we're playing a lot live. I think that it has a lot of energy and I think there's a certain kind of like communal energy to the record and I think it brings together a lot of the elements from all the records we've discussed in a really great way." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKNn01M8GG4
**Study Music (EP)** : This EP is mentioned as a future topic for discussion in part two of the interview, described as "a strange little EP." "On part two, we'll cover the recording and release of the Michigan Left album, High Noon, a strange little EP called Study Music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WSe0wdljSGA
**Study Music EP** : An acoustic EP featuring acoustic versions of songs from "High Noon," created in response to fan demand for recorded acoustic performances seen on YouTube. Tracks like "1111" and "My Heart's All Yours" from this EP are frequently used for first dances at weddings. The EP was partly a competitive effort to showcase that their songs, when played acoustically, could serve as effective "soothing music" for studying. "It was just a little acoustic thing that we did. That was, uh, acoustic songs from high new." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKNn01M8GG4

## songs
**1111 (2014)** : The third single from "High Noon," this song's lyrics draw inspiration from the common practice of making a wish at 11:11, an observation frequently made by a band member in bars. The lyrical motif "you made a wish at 1111" is paired with "I held your hips at 1234," creating a narrative around these specific times. The acoustic version of this song has become a popular choice for first dances at weddings. "I would just see like groups of girls or just bear say, well, it's 1111, make a wish." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKNn01M8GG4
**Abigail** : This song is mentioned as an example of a story that, in the band's mind, could have taken place within the Jackson Square mall, reflecting the atmosphere of downtown Hamilton at the time. "There's a song called Abigail that in my mind, I don't know, it didn't take place in Jackson Square mall but it seemed like the sort of story that would take place there." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WSe0wdljSGA
**Come to Light** : This track served as the first single released from the "High Noon" album. "Come to late was the first single." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKNn01M8GG4
**Drake's Dad** : This single from the "Morning Report" album was inspired by an encounter with Drake's father in a Memphis bar during a band road trip in America. The song broadly captures the experience of exploring a new city with friends, with Drake's dad making a brief appearance in the first verse and starring in the accompanying music video. The title was chosen purely for its humorous appeal. "I was on a road trip in America and we ran into Drake's dad in a bar in Memphis." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKNn01M8GG4
**John Lennon (2008)** : This song is focused on the Westdale area around McMaster University in Hamilton, painting a picture of summer life there. The title refers to "John Lennon '67," a period when Lennon was miserable despite being at the peak of his career, with the lyricist using this as a self-deprecating comparison to his own youthful confusion and self-pity, despite having no real reason for it. The song also features a line inspired by a friend's joke about getting Frank McCourt to write a foreword for a girl's book about her charmed life, contrasting it with McCourt's memoir "Angela's Ashes." "I'm kind of poking fun at myself, I think, in that song where I'm laughing about sort of just being young and confused and having no real, no real reason to feel sorry for yourself, but feeling that way." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WSe0wdljSGA
**Knockin' at the Door** : Released as a standalone single, this song was directly inspired by the night of Trump's inauguration and the subsequent Women's March in Washington D.C., which the band witnessed while touring with Frank Turner. The lyrics reflect themes of demanding change and reclaiming narrative. Drummer Tim's "military marching" beats served as the initial musical foundation, resonating with the concept of street marches. Max, Nick, and Mike created a demo in Hamilton, and producer Eric Ratz later helped speed up the tempo and maintain spontaneity during studio recording. The track incorporates horns and backup singers, influenced by the live performances of Kanye West's "Jesus Walks" and the band's use of horns on their "Morning Report" tour. The decision to release it as a single outside of an album cycle was described as a "hip-hop move." "We were in DC the night before he was inaugurated. So we were kind of taking in the vibes of America. And the next day was the women's march and I was like, oh, you know what? This is a depressing time for a lot of people, but I am inspired by saying these people take the streets and sort of take back the narrative of who we are as people." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKNn01M8GG4
**Leather Jacket** : This track was released as the second single from the "High Noon" album. "leather jacket, second single." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKNn01M8GG4
**My Heart's All Yours** : A significant track from the "Morning Report" album, this song is a popular choice for weddings, with its acoustic version frequently used for first dances. The lyrics were written about a band member's "special lady," and the band often receives videos from couples featuring the song at their weddings. "Yeah, it is. Is it about anybody in particular? Yeah, it's about my special lady." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKNn01M8GG4
**Private School** : Featured on the "Morning Report" album, this song explores the persona of affluent, cool, and intelligent students. While acknowledging their attractive qualities, the lyrics also convey a subtle critique of an "insufferable" lack of self-awareness regarding their inherent privilege and good fortune. Coming from "five white dudes" who recognize their own privilege, the song highlights the comedic aspect of this top echelon not fully grasping their luck. "I want to like you, but like, you don't quite get it, do you?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKNn01M8GG4
**Pub Crawl (2020) – Amazon Music Exclusive** : This 2020 Christmas song was commissioned as an exclusive for Amazon Music. It is based on the band's tradition of meeting friends at pubs during Christmas breaks, where credit cards are drawn to determine who pays the tab at each location. The song was leaked to bars across Canada and some in the US in November, encouraging fans to request it from bartenders. "This song is actually a commission for Amazon Music. It's one of their exclusives." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nYPGQiDL5bw
**The Ballad of Hugo Chavez (2008)** : This song was written about Hugo Chavez during a period when he was seen by many on the left as a hopeful leader, although the band acknowledges that history has since been less kind to his reputation. The song specifically focuses on his time in jail in the 1990s, where he learned about socialist revolutions, an experience that shaped the rest of his life. The band sought to write about interesting, complicated characters. "At the time, he was a bit of a darling of people on the left because I think people in Venezuela and around the world saw him as a real hopeful leader." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WSe0wdljSGA
**The Boss Is Coming (2008)** : This song's title stemmed from the band members' experiences working "terrible jobs" during university summers, including Mike DeAngeles picking up needles at Jackson Square, working in a distribution warehouse, and being fired from East Side Marios. Musically, it reflected the guitar-driven indie rock of bands like the Constantine's and Winter Sleep that the band was listening to at the time. "Yeah, I mean, we all were working terrible jobs when we were either through the summers in university like Mike picked up needles on the top of Jackson Square. I'd run a real bad ones." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WSe0wdljSGA
**Whistleblower** : One of two singles from "Michigan Left," this song was first performed live at the Olympics, where its initial rendition differed significantly from its final recorded version. It was considered the most aggressive track on the album, sharing the loud, fast rock characteristics of "Jackson Square." "I remember we played it live at the Olympics was the first time we ever played it live." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKNn01M8GG4
**Whistleblower (2011)** : This track is from their 2011 album *Michigan Left* and was performed live at 102.1 The Edge in Toronto. "Arkels in a live version of Whistleblower, a track from their 2011 album Michigan Left." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WSe0wdljSGA

## curiosities
**Adoption of Hamilton as Home City** : Although the members are originally from different parts of Ontario (Newmarket, London, Mississauga, Guelph, and Toronto), the band adopted Hamilton as its official home. This is because the band was "born" there, all its formative experiences, first sold-out shows, and connections to the music scene happened in Hamilton, making it their true musical home. "But as a band all our formative experiences happened in Hamilton, you know, like we're never going to know the clubs in any other city the way we know the clubs in Hamilton or you know, our connections to the music scene and all those really important things for us happened in Hamilton, you know." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WSe0wdljSGA
**Anthony's Baby Born Before Morton's Field Show** : Anthony's baby was born approximately 36 to 48 hours before the band's "The Rally" show at Morton's Field. His wife was due June 10th, and the show fell within a two-week window. Anthony did soundcheck the day before after his baby was born at 8 AM, then slept upright in a hospital chair, and drove his wife and newborn home the morning of the show, performing on "fumes." "My wife was due June 10th. And this was like the one show that was slotted in kind of like a two week before, two week after a period." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKNn01M8GG4
**Band Formation at McMaster University (2004)** : The band's core began forming on the first and second days Max Kermann attended McMaster University in Hamilton in 2004. Max, who was primarily interested in finding bandmates, met Mike DeAngeles on day one by asking about his favorite bands (The Weakerthans) and if he played guitar. On day two, Nick Dica introduced himself to Max because Max was wearing a Sam Roberts Band T-shirt, and upon learning Nick played bass, he was also invited to join. "You're literally the first and second day that you were in university." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WSe0wdljSGA
**Canadian Band Prolificacy** : The Arkells are highlighted as one of the most prolific Canadian bands of the past decade, consistently writing, recording, and releasing new material. "Arkels have been one of the most prolific Canadian bands of the last decade." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nYPGQiDL5bw
**Collaborative Songwriting Process** : While Max Kermann typically initiates the songs and is the chief lyricist, the songwriting process is highly collaborative. The other band members provide significant input through editing and questioning, ensuring clarity and improving the lyrics. All members share in the songwriting split, reflecting an "all for one, one for all" approach. "Yeah, I mean, I start the songs, but the more we do it, the more there's a pretty good heavy dose of editing going on from the guys where I go, is there a better way to say this? Or is this part clear? So it is very collaborative in that sense." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WSe0wdljSGA
**Continuous Audience Engagement** : The band prioritizes consistent engagement with their listeners, understanding the importance of continuously supplying new songs, shows, and special online content. They believe that for most bands, showing earnest care and being visible is crucial, as audiences expect to be engaged constantly. "working consistently and being loyal to your listeners and, and providing them with, you know, the service of new songs and shows and all those things, I think, uh, is really important to remember that." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKNn01M8GG4
**Continuous Evolution of Songs Post-Release** : The band acknowledges that they have no control over how songs evolve in people's lives after release. They find it interesting to see where their music lands, citing examples like "Knockin' at the Door" being used at the Olympics and "11-11" at weddings, appreciating that songs "can land in interesting places." "You put someone in the world and you go, oh, like knock at the door, I ended up being played at the Olympics. And like 11-11 was played at people's weddings. You know, it's funny how, you know, you have no control over that, but they can land in interesting places." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKNn01M8GG4
**Embrace of "Hollow Notes" Sound** : During the production of "Michigan Left," the band underwent a "turning point" where they became open to more influences, particularly synth sounds referred to as "Hollow notes." Previously, they "hated Hollow notes" and focused on a guitar-driven, edgy sound for "Jackson Square," but on "Michigan Left," they found these nuanced synth sounds "actually kind of sound interesting," leading to a slightly more nuanced record. "I hated Hollow notes before we made Michigan left. And then I was sort of, those synths actually kind of sound interesting." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKNn01M8GG4
**Extensive Touring and Performances** : The band has a reputation for playing a vast number of shows, appearing "anywhere and everywhere" asked by their label or radio stations, including pop-up gigs and spontaneous busking. They estimate they've played hundreds of shows per year for over a decade, working between 175 and 200 days annually on musically directed activities. This volume of performance is suggested to have potentially surpassed the Ramones' lifetime total of 2250 shows. "We do say we do like a hundred a year for, we've been doing this for 12 years. 1200 shows." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WSe0wdljSGA
**Featured in "In Their Own Words" Series** : Arkells was featured in the "In Their Own Words" series, where band members provide their personal accounts of the band's history. "USS Arkels, Alexis on Fire..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bI6TTA-fn7c
**Free Olympics Trip via Tweet** : The band leveraged their public profile and a "statue" (likely a metaphorical reference to their influence or popularity) to secure free airline tickets to the Olympics simply by sending a tweet. "They have a statue that allowed them to blag free airline tickets to the Olympics after doing nothing more than a quick tweet." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WSe0wdljSGA
**Initial Band Name: Charlemagne (2004-2007/2008)** : The band initially performed under the name Charlemagne from their first gig at the McMaster Battle of the Bands until late 2007 or early 2008. They were forced to change their name after another band named Charlemagne on MySpace threatened to sue them. "We call ourselves Charlemagne as our first band name." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WSe0wdljSGA
**Morton's Field "The Rally" Stadium Show** : In 2017, the band curated a massive show at Morton's Field in Hamilton for 24,000 people, aiming to surpass their previous large shows. Collaborating with the Tiger-Cats, they designed the event from the ground up, as the facility hadn't hosted many concerts in decades since a Pink Floyd issue in the 70s. The event featured a curated bill, custom poster, a bike ride from downtown to the stadium, an artist flea market, and local businesses creating Arkells-themed products like beer, sausages, and cupcakes. A portion of proceeds went to Refuge, a healthcare organization for new Canadians, making it a "real communal event." "We got to curate the bill. We got to design the poster. We got to, we organized a bike ride along the Canis Street bike lanes from downtown to the stadium. We had an artist flea market beforehand." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKNn01M8GG4
**MySpace Contact from Producer Tony Hoffer (2013)** : Despite previous unsuccessful attempts to contact producer Tony Hoffer through his manager, Hoffer personally reached out to Arkells via MySpace in 2013. He had discovered them after watching a live session of theirs, thinking they were a "cool band" and asking if they were "thinking of recording anytime soon." "he reached out to us on MySpace. On MySpace saying like, hey, you guys are, wait, in 2013, he reaches out on MySpace." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKNn01M8GG4
**Name Change to Arkells (Late 2007/Early 2008)** : The name change from Charlemagne to Arkells occurred under extreme pressure when Dino Lone Records needed a new name in 45 minutes for an EP flyer to be inserted into the City and Colour record. Max Kermann and Mike DeAngeles, who lived on Arkell Street at the time, proposed the name in a last-minute effort, noting its 60s Motown-esque sound. "So then we frantically got on the phone. I was at the library and McMaster, I still remember. And we called everyone, we got to change the name now because we've been sitting on this kind of decision for a long time and we just hadn't got around to it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WSe0wdljSGA
**Pub Crawl Tradition** : The band has a tradition when they return home for Christmas after a tour, where they gather with friends at a pub, throw credit cards into a hat, and draw them to determine who pays the tab. The last two people drawn pay, and the process repeats at the next pub, excluding those who already paid, until everyone has contributed. "The song is based on how the band catches up with friends when they come home for Christmas after a long tour." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nYPGQiDL5bw
**Pyeongchang 2018 Winter Olympics "Scam Trip"** : The band "drifted" into performing at the Canada House during the 2018 Pyeongchang Winter Olympics. The opportunity arose after they jokingly tweeted to Art Canada and Canada House (who were using "Knockin' at the Door" as a theme song) about covering their expenses for a trip. Within three days, they were on a plane, flying coach for a 14.5-hour flight without bringing instruments due to last-minute arrangements. They played three house-party style shows over the last five days of the Olympics, largely for athletes who were "ready to party" having finished their events, playing on the ground because the stage was only big enough for drums. "Yeah, that's a 14 and a half hour flight. Yeah. Did they, at least spring for premium economy? No, we were absolutely not. We were in coach." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKNn01M8GG4
**Scarlet Fever during Michigan Left Recording** : One of the band members contracted scarlet fever during the recording sessions for "Michigan Left" at The Bathhouse studio, requiring them to be quarantined and out of commission for a couple of days. "I got scarlet fever actually during that, uh, okay. During that recording session. You had to be quarantined." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKNn01M8GG4
**Stadium-Filling Status** : Arkells has achieved significant popularity, capable of filling stadiums for their performances. "Not only can they fill a stadium, more on that in a bit, they have a statue that allowed them to blag free airline tickets to the Olympics after doing nothing more than a quick tweet." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WSe0wdljSGA
**Value of a Professional Producer** : After self-producing "Michigan Left," the band learned that hiring a professional producer is "actually a very helpful thing." They found it challenging as "kids doing something for the first time" to work through all the questions and decisions involved in making a record. They view a producer as a "part psychologist," "social worker," and "translator" who can communicate effectively with each musician, ensuring good vibes and progress. "hiring a producer is actually a very helpful thing because I do remember it being tough as a bunch of like kids doing something for the first time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKNn01M8GG4
**Wedding Song Popularity** : Several acoustic versions of Arkells' songs, including "1111," "My Heart's All Yours," and "And Then Some," have become popular choices for first dances at weddings. The band regularly receives emails and videos from couples using their music for these special occasions, considering it a "very cool" and precious recognition. "Like that 1111 acoustic version has been used at like first dances for weddings, like countless times. Same thing with our acoustic versions of my heart, so it's yours. And, and then some, those three songs like we get in the summertime, like a couple of emails every week with videos from someone's wedding." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kKNn01M8GG4
