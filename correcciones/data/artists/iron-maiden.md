## curiosities
**Backmasking Use (70s/80s)** : Iron Maiden was among the many artists who utilized backmasking effects during the 1970s and 1980s, a period considered the peak for the practice. "Tons of artists used back masking effects in the 70s and 80s. That seems to be when things reached their peak. Iron Maiden, Pink Floyd, Sticks, Prince B-52s, Weird Al, they were all into it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_YjwRApoJhA
**Bruce Dickinson's Commercial Aviation Career** : Bruce Dickinson, the singer for the metal band Iron Maiden, is a full-fledged pilot for a UK charter airline, flying 757s. He was originally taught to fly by a British Airways pilot. In 2008, during a 7-week World Tour, Bruce personally flew the plane that transported the band and crew between continents and gigs. "Bruce flies there's 757s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WIFxAaReLn0
**Crashing and Playing a Wedding in Poland** : In 1984, while on their "Powerslave" tour through Poland, Iron Maiden discovered a wedding taking place at their hotel. The band unexpectedly crashed the event, took over from the wedding band, and performed a set for the guests. "In nineteen eighty four, Iron Maiden was on their power slave tour through Poland when they discovered that there was a wedding at their hotel. They crashed it, took over from the wedding band and played a set." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HoBx6s3JmVQ
**Donington Festival Performance (August 28, 1988)** : Iron Maiden was part of the lineup at the Donington outdoor rock show in England on August 28, 1988, which tragically experienced a crowd collapse that resulted in two deaths during a different band's performance. "Guns and roses, kiss iron maiden, and others were on the bill." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pgchw2h87aw

**Seven Lead Singers; Paul Di'Anno on First Two Albums; Bruce Dickinson Returned After Blaze Bayley's Vocal Issues** : Iron Maiden's singer history begins with Paul Day (about two years), then Dennis Wilcock (a performer who would put fake blood capsules in his mouth and draw a sword across his face to "bleed" on stage), then Paul Di'Anno — who recorded the band's first two studio albums before being fired for excessive drinking and drug use. Bruce Dickinson joined in 1981 and was with the band through 1993 before leaving to focus on solo work. Blaze Bayley replaced him but ran into serious vocal difficulties; when Dickinson indicated he wanted to return in 1999, Bayley was out and Dickinson returned, remaining to the present. "Dennis Wilcock had a thing about where he'd put capsules of fake blood in his mouth." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l0F22NTnQzQ
