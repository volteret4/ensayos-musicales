# artist - Def Leppard

## genres
- Glitter Rock

## instruments

## albums
**Pyromania (1983)** : This album, produced by Mutt Lange, was incredibly expensive to make, costing around $3 million. Chris Berkett was responsible for programming all the drums on a Fairlight CMI, a process that took six weeks of meticulous typing in musical code for every single event. The drums had to be re-recorded from the original floppy disks three times because the analog master tapes wore out from repeated transfers and overdubs. "not wonder that album cost something like $3 million to make." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mjQBaX_3oL4

## curiosities

"They also played the homage to guns and rouses, NACDC and Led Zeppelin and Van Halen and Ozzy and Sabbath and Death Lepred and Jimmy Hendrix." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Drum Programming Challenges on "Pyromania"** : For the album "Pyromania," producer Mutt Lange insisted on the latest technology for drums. Chris Berkett spent six weeks programming all the drums on a Fairlight CMI. This pre-MIDI process required converting the drummer's demo tapes into musical score and then typing every single event into the monophonic Fairlight using a mathematical music composition language. "And I had to write all that stuff out in score. Because it had to be converted to mathematical music language." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mjQBaX_3oL4
**First Client of Q Prime (1982)** : Def Leppard became the very first, and initially only, client of Q Prime when Cliff Bernstein and Peter Mensch founded the company in 1982. Given Def Leppard's peak popularity at the time, they quickly expanded their client roster beyond just this band. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**Glam/Glitter Rock Influence** : Def Leppard incorporated elements of glitter rock, a heavier variant of glam, into their sound. "Death Leopard had a piece of this." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw
**Meticulous Drum Production on "Pyromania"** : The drum programming for "Pyromania" was an incredibly meticulous process. Mutt Lange would spend half a day listening to a single hi-hat sound against a click track, checking if it was "a bit ahead, a bit behind," before approving it. "We would listen literally to a hi hat against the click for half a day." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mjQBaX_3oL4
**Repeated Drum Recording Due to Tape Wear on "Pyromania"** : The drums for "Pyromania" had to be re-recorded three separate times from the original Fairlight floppy disks. This was because the analog 24-track master tapes, on which guitars and vocals were overdubbed, wore out through repeated transfers to new tapes, forcing the producer to request "first generation drums" again. "We've worn out three tapes doing all the guitars and the vocals. And they transferred... It wasn't digital in those days. It was analog. They transferred 24 track onto another new one because it was getting thin." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mjQBaX_3oL4
