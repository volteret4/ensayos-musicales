It saw the return of lead guitarist Dave Baksh, who had left in 2006, rejoining in 2015. Tom Thacker, Baksh's replacement, remained with the band. It also introduced new drummer Frank Zummo. **Underclass Hero (2007) - Fourth Album & Semi-Concept Record** : This album was the follow-up to "Chuck" and took nearly a year to write due to Derek Whibley's "paranoid writer's block" and lost confidence. Derek ended up producing it himself as they couldn't find anyone else. It featured a different sound, with Derek adding piano and keyboards, and was described as a semi-concept record encompassing Derek's outlook on life. Several songs are admitted by Derek to be about his relationship with Avril Lavigne, to whom he was married. ## curiosities
**"Don't Call It A Some Back Tour"** : The tour accompanying the "13 Voices" album was deliberately named "Don't Call It A Some Back Tour," humorously addressing the media's branding of the album as a comeback after a five-year gap and lineup changes. **"Still Waiting" and Italian Recording Similarity** : The Sum 41 hit "Still Waiting" was found by internet users to bear a strong resemblance to an Italian recording from the mid to late 1990s. Despite the striking likeness, this was categorized as an "unfortunate sonic coincidence" because pop songs often have simple structures, and the melody (based on two alternating notes punctuated by three or four more) could easily be independently discovered by different composers. "Sounds very, very similar, doesn't it? ... I'd simply chalk this up to unfortunate sonic coincidences and we're only aware of it because of the power and the reach of the internet." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L09R2evjyMc
**Adapting to Music Industry Changes** : Derek Whibley acknowledges the drastic changes in the music industry from CD sales to streaming but admits he doesn't fully understand the business models. **Derek Whibley's Songwriting Process** : Derek's songwriting begins by playing guitar (through a loud, "stun" amp) or piano, focusing on creating a riff that dictates the song's style. He aims for riffs that make him feel "like I want to jump around or move and be like what I'm like on stage." He then creates extensive demos using a drum machine and all possible parts, usually with placeholder "gibberish" melodies, before focusing on actual lyrics, which he often procrastinates on until recording. He collects lyrical fragments but finds full lines emerge with a melody. "Songwriting process for me is kind of how it's always been where I just pick up a guitar or I sit at a piano and I just start playing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=puFgo2ft7hQ
