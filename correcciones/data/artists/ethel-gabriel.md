## curiosities
**A&R and Talent Scouting Role** : In addition to her production work, Ethel Gabriel also served in an A&R (Artists and Repertoire) role, actively scouting for new talent. This demonstrates her multifaceted contribution to identifying and developing artists for RCA Records. "She also worked in ANR. She was a talent scout." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
**First Female Producer at RCA Records** : Ethel Gabriel was one of the very first, if not the first, female record producers, beginning her work in the late 1950s. She remained the sole woman producer at RCA Records for almost four decades, breaking significant barriers in the male-dominated music industry. "Starting in the late 1950s, she was one of the very, very first, if not the first, female producers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
**Long and Distinguished Career** : Ethel Gabriel retired from RCA in 1984 after 44 years with the company, but continued to work on various projects for another two decades. She lived to be 99, passing away in 2021, marking a remarkably long and influential career in music production. "Ethel retired from RCA in 1984 after 44 years, but continued with various projects for the next 20 years." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
**Technical Contributions and Studio Establishment** : Gabriel was deeply involved in technical advancements, working on the first stereophonic recordings and supervising Bing Crosby's first-ever stereo recording session. She also contributed to establishing RCA's famous Nashville studio and was involved with the label's initial disco releases. "She got very technical, too, working on the first stereophonic recordings." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
**Work with Iconic Artists and Gold Records** : Throughout her career at RCA, Ethel Gabriel earned at least 15 gold records and worked on hits for legendary artists such as Elvis Presley and Perry Como. Her contributions were central to the success of many major label releases. "And this included working on hits by people like Elvis Presley and Perry Como." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
