# artist - The Shins

## albums
**Self-Titled (2009)** : This record was a collaboration between James Mercer (The Shins) and producer Danger Mouse. The drums, played by Danger Mouse, intentionally feature a slight "drag" behind the beat, which Chris Berkett cites as an example of how imperfection adds emotional vibe to music. "We got together with the Shins James Mercer in 2009 to release a self-titled record." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mjQBaX_3oL4

## songs
**The High Road (2009)** : This single from The Shins' self-titled album showcases Danger Mouse's drumming, which intentionally drags slightly behind the beat. This "imperfection" is highlighted as providing crucial emotional vibe and excitement, contrasting with overly precise, quantized modern production. "Listen to this single called The High Road. Dangermas is playing drums here, and listen to how he drags ever so slightly behind the beat." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mjQBaX_3oL4
