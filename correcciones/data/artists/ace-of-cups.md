## curiosities
**All-Female San Francisco Group** : Ace of Cups was an all-female group formed in San Francisco in 1967, featuring Diane Metilige on drums. They were noted for opening for Jimi Hendrix and maintaining friendships with prominent bands like Jefferson Airplane and The Grateful Dead, marking their presence in the late 1960s psychedelic rock scene. "They were at all-female group formed in 1967 and featured die-end metallige on drums." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9GHpC0kCgHY
**Altamont Festival Incident** : During the Altamont Festival in December 1969, one of the band members suffered a fractured skull after being hit by a full beer can, requiring brain surgery. "Another issue happened when one of the band members suffered a fractured skull when she was hit by a full beer can during the awful Altamont Festival in December 1969." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjQiZqpmQlE
**Breakup Due to Sexism (1972)** : Despite their talent and early recognition, the band broke up by 1972, attributed to the re-emergence of old sexist attitudes in the music industry that dismissed the idea of female rock bands. "The Ace of Cups gave it a good shot, but by 1972, they'd broken up." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI
**Breakup and Posthumous Release** : The band broke up in 1972 after a couple of members became parents and were replaced by men. They had been poised to be the first all-female rock band to release a record, but this did not happen until a CD of their demos and live tracks was released in 2003. "It wasn't until 2003 that a CD of demos and live tracks was released." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjQiZqpmQlE
**Early Female Rock Band (1967)** : An all-female rock band from San Francisco that wrote their own songs, had a frontwoman who could "shred like a man," and a female drummer. This lineup was considered radical even in 1967, challenging prevailing norms about women in rock music. "An all-girl rock band? A bunch of women who wrote their own songs, went a checkout front that could shred like a man and a girl playing drums? Even in 1967, this was radical." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI
**Formation and Early Connections** : Formed in San Francisco in 1967, coinciding with the Summer of Love, Ace of Cups consisted of five women without any male members. They were friends with The Grateful Dead and Jefferson Airplane, and Jimi Hendrix was so impressed that he invited them to open one of his gigs at Golden Gate Park. "They were formed in San Francisco in 1967, right in time for the summer of love." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjQiZqpmQlE
**Jimmy Hendrix's Support** : Jimi Hendrix was greatly impressed by the band's musical talent and invited them to open for a couple of his shows, signaling a significant early endorsement for the all-female group. "Hendrix was so impressed by their chops that he invited them to open a couple of his shows." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI
**Sexism and Lack of Professional Releases** : Despite securing a record deal, the band experienced rampant sexism, receiving low-balled contract offers because they were women. They ultimately never made any professional recordings, and their record deals failed to materialize. "They did get a record deal despite the rampant sexism of the era. Although they were low balled on the value of these contracts because they were only women." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjQiZqpmQlE
