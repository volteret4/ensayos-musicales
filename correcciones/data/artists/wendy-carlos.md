## albums
**Impact of "Switched-On Bach"** : The album was a massive commercial success and played a crucial role in legitimizing the synthesizer. It demonstrated that synthesizers could be used for sophisticated musical compositions beyond simple sound effects, opening new possibilities for electronic music and garnering critical acclaim. "Not only was it commercially successful, but it drew attention to the capabilities of the synthesizer and the possibilities for electronic music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
**Switched On Bach (1969) – Best-Selling Classical Album** : This album was a best-selling classical record upon its release. "Switched On Bach. 1969. That's selling classical album." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KF5MwJVYng
**Switched-On Bach (1968)** : Released in 1968, this album featured 10 pieces by Johann Sebastian Bach, entirely performed on a giant modular Moog synthesizer unit. The recording process was extensive, taking five months and over a thousand studio hours on a custom-built eight-track recorder due to the synth's monophonic nature and complex sound programming. "The result was an album entitled Switched On Bach, 10 pieces by Johann Sebastian Bach, and everything was played on a giant modular synthesizer unit supplied by Bob Mogue." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE

## curiosities
**A Clockwork Orange (1972) Score** : Wendy Carlos, a classical musician who had been collaborating with Bob Moog on the development and application of his new modular synthesizers, was hired by director Stanley Kubrick to compose the score for his 1971 futuristic dystopian film, *A Clockwork Orange*. The entirely electronic score, released as a soundtrack in 1972 (with Carlos identifying as Walter Carlos at the time), introduced sounds no one had heard before and became a landmark in the widespread development of electronic music, influencing many rock musicians seeking novel sounds. "No one had ever heard such sounds. It was completely electronic." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM

Her input was instrumental in decisions like incorporating a keyboard and refining the tactile experience of individual keys, making synthesizers more user-friendly for musicians. "She worked carefully with Bob Mogue, offering a musician's perspective, and how his new electronic instruments should look and feel." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
