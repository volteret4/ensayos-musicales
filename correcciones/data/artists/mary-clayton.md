## songs
**Gimme Shelter (1969)** : Mary Clayton performed a legendary guest vocal on this Rolling Stones track, recorded on November 2, 1969, at Sunset Sound in LA. She was called late at night by producer Jack Nitzsche while almost five months pregnant, initially refusing but persuaded by her husband. She arrived in her pajamas and rollers, was greeted by Keith Richards, and completed three takes. Her voice cracked powerfully on the word "murder," eliciting an impressed "woo" from Mick Jagger, creating one of the great guest vocals in history. "That has gone down in history as one of the great guest vocals on any record." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE

## curiosities
**Life-Altering Car Crash and Continued Career** : On August 16, 2014, Mary Clayton was involved in a severe car crash in Los Angeles, which resulted in her losing both her legs, amputated below the knees. Despite these profound injuries, she has continued her career, recording both as a solo artist and as a backup singer, showcasing remarkable resilience. "Her injuries were so bad that she lost both her legs. They were amputated below the knees. But she continues to record, both as a solo artist and as a backup singer." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
**Session Vocalist for Major Artists** : Mary Clayton has appeared on plenty of other legendary recordings as a session vocalist, lending her voice to tracks by artists such as Leonard Skynyrd ("Sweet Home Alabama"), Joe Cocker ("Feeling All Right"), Tori Amos, Coldplay, and Ringo Starr. Her extensive work demonstrates her reputation as a highly sought-after backup singer across various genres. "Mary has also appeared on plenty of other legendary recordings, Sweet Home Alabama by Leonard Skinnerd, Feeling All Right by Joe Cocker. Tori Amos, Coldplay, Ringo Star." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
**The Acid Queen in "The Who's Tommy"** : Mary Clayton also ventured into musical theater, appearing on the London stage in the role of the Acid Queen in a production of The Who's "Tommy." This showcases her diverse talents beyond her prolific recording career, demonstrating her versatility as a performer. "Mary has also appeared on the London stage in the role of the acid queen in a production of The Who's Tommy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
**Tragic Miscarriage After "Gimme Shelter" Recording** : The day after her strenuous, late-night recording session for "Gimme Shelter," Mary Clayton suffered a miscarriage, losing her little girl. She believed the physical strain and late hour of the performance contributed to this tragic event, leading her to be unable to listen to the song for many years. "The next day, Mary miscarried. She and her husband lost a little girl, and Mary thinks it might have happened because of the lightness of the hour, and the strain she put on her body with that performance." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
