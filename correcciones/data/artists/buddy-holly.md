**Plane Crash Death (1957)** : On February 3, 1957, a day famously known as "the day the music died," Buddy Holly was killed when the small plane he was a passenger in went down in a cornfield outside of Mason City, Iowa. He died alongside fellow musicians Richie Valens and The Big Bopper. "February 3rd, 1957 has gone down in history as the day the music died." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hmnMG6f80Sg
**Plane Crash Death (1959)** : Buddy Holly was killed in a small plane crash in a cornfield outside Mason City, Iowa, on February 3, 1959. This tragedy, which also claimed the lives of Ritchie Valens and Big Wopper, is historically referred to as "the day the music died." "Buddy Holly, Richie Val, and Big Wopper were all killed when their small plane went down in a cornfield outside of Mason City, Iowa." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3eGBnwPWRGo
**Posthumous Hologram Tour (2018)** : Despite having passed away in 1959, Buddy Holly embarked on a hologram tour in 2018. This tour, along with similar ventures for other deceased artists, showcased the advancements in projection technology that allowed lifelike 3D images to appear on stage, marking a new era for posthumous performances. "In 2018, Buddy Holly, dead since 1959, and Roy Orbison, dead since 1988, were on stage again as holograms." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GYUuTbOvyak
**Songs Acquired by Paul McCartney (1973)** : Many of Buddy Holly's songs were part of the Norvat Jack Music company, which Paul McCartney acquired in 1973.
