## curiosities
**Admiration for Bobby Sands** : One of Richie's childhood heroes was Bobby Sands, an IRA member who died at the age of 27 after a 65-day hunger strike in prison. This admiration provides insight into Richie's fascination with extreme forms of protest and self-sacrifice. "Interestingly, one of Richie's heroes as a kid was Bobby Sans, the IRA member who killed himself in prison by going on a hunger strike." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCwgvHg86Oc
**Ambitious Rock Star with Charisma** : From the outset, Richie aspired to be a rock star whose existence "meant something." Despite minimal musical ability, his glamorous look, prominent cheekbones, and intense charisma captivated audiences, often leading them to mistakenly assume he was the band's leader. "He had zero musical ability. Oh, sure, he had a guitar on stage, but he was mostly just miming with his amp turn down." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCwgvHg86Oc
**Anorexia and Self-Control** : Richie suffered from anorexia, which appeared to be linked to a deep-seated need for self-control, possibly serving as an alternative coping mechanism to his self-harm. At 5'8", he weighed an alarming 84 pounds during his final university exams, highlighting the severity of his condition. "Alcoholism became a problem. So did anorexia, which appeared to be related to a need to exhibit self-control, perhaps as an alternative to his need to self-harm." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCwgvHg86Oc
**Beloved Cocker Spaniel, Snoopy** : Richie was devoted to his cocker spaniel, Snoopy. The dog's death at the age of 17 on January 17, 1995, left Richie inconsolable, contributing significantly to his emotional fragility in the days leading up to his disappearance. "Three days after Snoopy, his beloved cocker spaniel died at the age of 17. Richie was inconsolable, and with the band tensions and a relationship breakdown, he must have felt that the universe was crashing down around him." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCwgvHg86Oc
**Diagnoses of Borderline Personality Disorder and Asperger's** : While at Priory Hospital, Richie was diagnosed with Borderline Personality Disorder, a then-newly named condition explaining his issues with identity, relationships, and occasional dissociation. A later diagnosis of Asperger's (now a form of autism) further elucidated his laser focus and insistence on strict routines. "Two weeks into his stay at the primary, Richie was diagnosed with borderline personality disorder, a newly named condition at the time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCwgvHg86Oc
**Early Life and Mental Health Trigger** : Richie experienced a happy childhood in Blackwood, South Wales, stating, "Up to the age of 13, I was ecstatically happy." However, upon moving from his nan's home and starting comprehensive school, he felt "everything started to go wrong." His self-harm behaviors reportedly began around the time of his grandmother's death. "Then I moved from my nan and started at a comprehensive school and everything started to go wrong." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCwgvHg86Oc
**Intellectual and Literary Figure** : Richie was the intellectual force behind the Manic Street Preachers, well-versed in figures like Karl Marx, Albert Camus, Sylvia Plath, Dostoevsky, JD Salinger, Jack Kerouac, and Joseph Conrad, as well as the Beat Poets. He meticulously rehearsed philosophical and literary quotes for interviews, solidifying his role as the band's literary expert. "Richie knew about Karl Marx, Albert Kamu, Sylvia Plath, Dostoevsky, JD Salinger, Jack Kerawak, Joseph Conrad, and all the beat poets." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCwgvHg86Oc
**Nickname "Richie Manic"** : Richie Edwards was known by the nickname "Richie Manic," a name he preferred to be called. This moniker reflected his stage persona and identity within the band. "The band featured Richie James Edwards or Richie Manic, as he'd like to be called." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCwgvHg86Oc
**Obsession with Fictional Disappearances** : Richie frequently discussed the 1970s TV show *The Rise and Fall of Reginald Parent*, about an executive who fakes his own death during a midlife crisis. He was also obsessed with Francis Ford Coppola's *Apocalypse Now*, particularly Marlon Brando's mysterious disappearance to a South Pacific island after the film wrapped, suggesting a fascination with vanishing without a trace. "Richie would often talk to visitors of an old 1970s TV show called The Rise and Fall of Reginald Parent. Reginald Parent was a successful executive who faked his own death during a midlife crisis." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCwgvHg86Oc
**Struggles with Mental Health and Self-Harm** : Richie openly discussed his battle with depression and frequent self-harm, which started in high school and included cutting himself and stubbing cigarettes on his arms. He found cutting to be a logical outlet for emotional pain, stating, "When I cut myself, I feel so much better. All the little things that might have been annoying me certainly seemed so trivial because I'm concentrating on the pain." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCwgvHg86Oc
