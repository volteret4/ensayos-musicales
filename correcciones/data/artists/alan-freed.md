## curiosities
**Coining the Term "Rock and Roll"** : Freed famously started referring to the music he played on his radio show as "Rock and Roll." This change in terminology occurred after a New York street musician, who claimed prior usage of the name "Moondog," threatened legal action. One theory suggests Freed derived the term from the 1951 recording "60 Minute Man" by Billy Ward and the Dominoes, which included the lyric "I rock them, roll them all night long," known as African American slang for sexual intercourse, a meaning Freed was likely aware of. **Early Career and Rendezvous Records Partnership (Late 1940s - 1951)** : Alan Freed, a radio announcer at WAKR in Akron, Ohio, developed a friendship with Leo Mintz, who operated Rendezvous Records, a prominent record store in Cleveland. Mintz observed that a significant number of white teenagers were purchasing R&B records sourced from the US South and suggested Freed feature these on his radio show. When Freed moved to Cleveland for a position at WJW, Mintz bought airtime on the station for Freed's show, which premiered on July 11, 1951, playing records supplied by Rendezvous. "Fried hosted, playing R&B records supplied by rendezvous records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
**The Moondog and Pioneering Rock Concerts** : Alan Freed achieved considerable fame, adopting the moniker "the Moondog" because his radio program aired after midnight. He subsequently began promoting live shows for the artists featured on these R&B records, naming these events "Moondog Balls." These concerts are historically recognized as the first-ever rock concerts. "And looking back, these were the first ever rock concerts." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
