## curiosities
**Discovery and Early Career** : In 1929, she was discovered by a representative from Columbia Records while performing outside a barbershop, leading to a trip to New York where she recorded blues songs with her second husband, Joe McCoy. By 1935, Memphis Minnie had become an established artist, recognized as one of the very few female blues guitarists. "In 1929, she was discovered by someone from Columbia Records while playing outside of Barber Shop." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjQiZqpmQlE
**Early Life and Tough Demeanor** : Born Lizzie Douglas in Louisiana in 1897, she ran away from home at 13 to play guitar on the streets of Memphis and reportedly worked as a prostitute when necessary. Known for her toughness, she was unafraid to fight, even resorting to a knife or pistol if a situation became confrontational. "She was tough. If things got heavy, she wasn't afraid to fight, whether she needed to pull a knife or a pistol." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjQiZqpmQlE
**Retirement and Posthumous Recognition** : After decades of recording numerous songs, her declining health in the 1950s, including a series of strokes that impaired her ability to play guitar, forced her to retire. She passed away in a nursing home in 1973, but in 1996, Bonnie Raitt funded a headstone for her, inscribed with an epitaph praising her singular contribution to the blues. "A series of strokes destroyed her ability to play guitar, and she died in a nursing home in 1973." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjQiZqpmQlE
