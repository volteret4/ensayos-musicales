# artist - Raymond Scott

## songs
**Piece (1937)** : A jazz piece from 1937 by Raymond Scott was inadvertently sampled or copied by Rush in their 1978 instrumental "La Villa Strangiato." Rush believed the piece was in the public domain, but it was not, leading to a copyright infringement settlement. "Now compare that to a 1937 jazz piece by Raymond Scott." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fS8V3kYXT6w

## curiosities
**Bandito the Bong Artist (c. 1963)** : Following his rhythm synthesizer, Scott developed a drum machine named "Bandito the Bong Artist" around 1963. "That was followed by a drum machine he called Bandito the Bong Artist. This is from around 1963." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dablAKDOOV0
**Cartoon Soundtrack Composer** : Raymond Scott's compositions were extensively adapted for classic Looney Tunes and Merrie Melodies cartoons, providing the soundtracks for characters like Bugs Bunny and Porky Pig. "Hundreds of his works were adapted for old Looney tunes and Mary Melody's cartoons to soundtrack Bugs Bunny and Porkey Pig." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dablAKDOOV0
**Rhythm Synthesizer (1960)** : An inventor interested in new electronic keyboards, Scott introduced a device called the rhythm synthesizer in 1960. "He also introduced something called the rhythm synthesizer in 1960." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dablAKDOOV0
