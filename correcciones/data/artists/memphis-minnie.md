## curiosities
**"Original Punk Rocker" Persona** : Memphis Minnie earned the nickname "the original punk rocker" due to her distinctive performance style, which involved singing louder and playing her guitar harder than most of her contemporaries in the 1930s. Her formidable personality was further emphasized by her habit of carrying a pistol and a pocket knife, and her readiness to use them if provoked. "She always had her pistol and she always had her pocket knife." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
**Cutting Contest Victories** : Memphis Minnie was known for participating in and winning "cutting contests," musical duels where musicians competed to demonstrate the best licks and greatest skill, often triumphing against male competitors. "Mini won her share of those and they were all against dudes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hbNJQ6OYmd0
**Discovery and Extensive Recording** : Around 1930, Memphis Minnie and her second husband, Joe McCoy, were discovered by a Columbia Records talent scout, which launched her recording career. She went on to make hundreds of records. "Around 1930, she and her second husband, a guy named Joe McCoy, have been discovered by a Columbia Records talent scout and started making records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hbNJQ6OYmd0
**Early Life and Busking Career** : Born in 1904, Memphis Minnie received her first guitar at age seven and was performing guitar and banjo at parties by age 11. In 1910, she ran away from home, surviving by busking on Beale Street in Memphis, followed by a stint with the Ringling Brothers Circus before returning to immerse herself in the burgeoning Memphis blues scene. "In 1910, she ran away from home and survived by busking on street corners along Beale Street in Memphis." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hbNJQ6OYmd0
**Intimidating Stage Presence** : Beyond her musical aggression, Memphis Minnie was known for her tough-as-nails demeanor, threatening to shoot or cut anyone who tried anything against her. She also chewed and spit tobacco while performing, reinforcing her "total badass" image and making her a formidable figure on stage. "Try anything and she threatened to shoot you or cut you." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
**Unique Blues Style and "First Punk Rocker" Label** : Her playing style offered a distinct take on the blues, characterized by more swing, force, and bounce, coupled with confidence and swagger. Her songs were frequently described as fierce and angry, leading some to call her the "first ever punk rocker" based on her attitude. "If you listen carefully to her playing style, she took the blues in a different direction. She played with a little more swing, a little more force, maybe a little more bounce." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hbNJQ6OYmd0
