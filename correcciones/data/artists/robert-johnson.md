## curiosities
**Crossroads Legend** : In the 1930s, Robert Johnson was repeatedly told to go away by other guitarists because he wasn't good enough. When he returned just months later, he was a master, doing things no one else had thought of. This led to the story that he sold his soul to the devil at a crossroads near Clarksdale, Mississippi (highways 49 and 61) in exchange for guitar virtuosity, a site still visited by people today. "So the story became that Robert Johnson met at a crossroads in rural Mississippi, where he sold his soul to the devil in exchange for guitar virtuosity." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pmKjZzypzvg
**Inspiration for Danko Jones' Look** : This legendary blues artist served as a direct inspiration for the Toronto band Danko Jones's formal black attire and overall visual style, highlighting his enduring influence on modern musicians. "They were inspired by blues legend Robert Johnson, and a punk band called Nation of Ulysses." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mhQ36Y-c9fM
**Post-Mortem Reputation Explosion** : Robert Johnson was known by myths and legends among hardcore Delta Blues fans while he was alive, but his reputation exploded in 1961 when his records were reissued, 23 years after his death. "Not when you anything about Robert Johnson when he was alive, other than some myths and legends among hardcore fans of Delta Blues, but when his records were reissued in 1961, 23 years after he died, his reputation exploded." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1VWJ0uCQx8w
