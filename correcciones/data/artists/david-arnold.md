# artist - David Arnold

## songs
**The World Is Not Enough (1999) – Composer and Co-writer** : David Arnold was hired to compose and sort out the theme for the 1999 James Bond film "The World Is Not Enough." After writing the theme and testing demos that were approved by producers, he approached Shirley Manson and Garbage to perform the song, fulfilling Manson's long-held dream to sing a Bond theme. "Composer David Arnold was hired to sort things out. He wrote the theme, tested demos with producers and they approved." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K0pgJ7XAlpo
**You Know My Name (2006) – Co-writer** : David Arnold, the film score composer for "Casino Royale," co-wrote the theme song "You Know My Name" with Chris Cornell. Cornell wrote his parts in his Paris apartment, aiming for something new yet traditional. Arnold's collaboration helped shape the critically successful and Grammy-nominated track. "Cornell's song is a co-write with David Arnold the composer for the film score." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K0pgJ7XAlpo

## curiosities
**Rejection of Multiple Singers for "The World Is Not Enough" (1999)** : For "The World Is Not Enough" theme in 1999, David Arnold and the producers considered and rejected a list of prominent artists including Björk, Melanie C of the Spice Girls, Jamiroquai, and Robbie Williams, before ultimately offering the role to Shirley Manson and Garbage. Producers wanted an electronic sound that also retained a big orchestral arrangement. "Calls went out to Bjork, Melanie C of the Spice Girls, Jamira Quye and Robbie Williams and they were all rejected by the producers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K0pgJ7XAlpo
