## curiosities
**"Thatchergate" Hoax (1983-1984)** : Following the Falklands War, this anarchist musical collective created a tape known as "Thatchergate." They cobbled together speeches from Margaret Thatcher and Ronald Reagan to create what sounded like a fake phone call between the two leaders, including a fabricated discussion about firing missiles at allies. When the tape circulated in 1983, the US State Department suspected it was KGB disinformation, and MI6 considered it a possible Argentinian intelligence operation. Its true source was not discovered until January 1984. While not a song, its samples were used in tracks by Crass and the Dead Kennedys. Crass's motivation was to expose governments as "small-minded, petty and stupid," not just corrupt or violent, and the hoax indeed embarrassed the CIA. "The band cobbled together speeches from Margaret Thatcher and Ronald Reagan into what appeared to be a fake phone call between the two of them." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0ARYt4sYT90
**Falklands War Hoax Tape (1982)** : Crass devised a prank in 1982 by editing speeches from Ronald Reagan and Margaret Thatcher to create a fake phone call that made it sound as if they were plotting a wider war against the Soviet Union, with Thatcher also appearing to take responsibility for the sinking of an Argentine battleship during the Falklands War. This idea stemmed from classified information shared by a former skinhead sailor who had served in the Falklands. Crass secretly sent the tape to newspapers across Europe. "Based on what they heard from this sailor, Crasse, who wrote it down, they took notes, came up with the idea of cutting together some speeches by Ronald Reagan and Margaret Fatcher to make it sound like a phone call between the two of them, sometime, allegedly, in 1982." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YeNWDtN_sHE
**Intelligence Agency Investigations and Harassment** : After Crass's Falklands War hoax tape was uncovered, it triggered investigations by both the CIA and MI5, and the Pentagon also took notice. MI5 opened a secret dossier on the band, and their phones were tapped. The band was subsequently courted by various organizations, including the real KGB, Germany's Red Army Faction (Baader-Meinhof group), and the IRA, all expressing interest in their disruptive capabilities. Crass admitted to being terrified as their prank escalated into a genuinely dangerous situation involving national security. "MI5 opened a dossier on the band. It was kept secret for 30 years. The band had their phones tapped. Plus, intelligence spooks from the CIA looked at the tape and opened an investigation. The Pentagon took notice too." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YeNWDtN_sHE
**Invitation to Russian Literary Magazine (1982)** : Shortly after their Falklands War hoax was uncovered in 1982, Crass was invited to a meeting at a supposed Russian literary magazine in London. This event, combined with subsequent approaches from various intelligence and radical groups, fueled suspicion within British intelligence about potential Soviet contact. "Crasse was invited to a meeting at a supposed Russian literary magazine in London." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YeNWDtN_sHE
**KGB and Radical Group Courtship** : Following their Falklands War hoax, Crass found themselves being approached by various organizations, including the actual KGB, representatives from Germany's Baader-Meinhof group, and even the IRA. These groups, seeing their potential for destabilization, sought to offer assistance or gather further information. Crass felt terrified as their prank turned into a serious national security concern.
