This was an early, label-specific instance of recognizing a single's immense commercial success before the formalization of industry-wide awards by the RIAA. "In 1956, RCA presented one to Elvis Presley for selling one million copies of his single Don't Be Cruel." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gP3oQeYEvso

**First Recording at Sun Studios (1953)** : On July 18, 1953, a young truck driver named Elvis Presley recorded two songs, "That's When Your Heartaches Begin" and "My Happiness," at Sun Studios in Memphis as a birthday present for his mother. These were Elvis Presley's first recordings ever, and based on this session, studio head Sam Phillips helped Elvis become the iconic figure he would be. This record is argued to be the genesis of all rock and roll. Jack White later purchased this one-of-a-kind artifact for $305,000 US, subsequently digitizing and re-releasing it through Third Man Records so the world could hear it. "This is on July 18th, 1953, a young truck driver dropped into Sun Studios in Memphis to record himself singing a song for his mother." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tbmssCZ05ro
