## curiosities
**Collaboration with Dave Stewart (1990)** : By the end of 1990, Dread Zeppelin had recorded a significant amount of material with Dave Stewart, known from the Eurythmics. "and by the end of 1990, they'd recorded a bunch of stuff with Dave Stewart, the guy from the Eurythmics." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9_3EbwKPnU
**Expanded Repertoire** : The band eventually broadened its repertoire beyond just Led Zeppelin songs to include material from The Yardbirds, another band associated with Jimmy Page, and Bob Marley, which made sense given their reggae style. "But Dread Zeppelin eventually expanded their repertoire to include the Yard Birds, another Jimmy Page band, and Bob Marley, which made sense, I guess." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9_3EbwKPnU
**First Gig on Elvis' Birthday (January 8, 1989)** : Dread Zeppelin's very first gig took place on January 8th, 1989, which would have been the real Elvis Presley's 54th birthday. The concept admittedly started as a joke. "Their first gig, January 8th, 1989, on what would have been the real Elvis' 54th birthday. And yes, everybody will admit that this started as a joke." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9_3EbwKPnU
**Longevity (25+ Years)** : Dread Zeppelin is still together today and continues to play gigs more than 25 years after their formation, demonstrating their enduring appeal. "Now the group is still together today, they're still playing gigs more than 25 years later." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9_3EbwKPnU
**Original Material Development** : The band also began recording their own original material, moving beyond strictly covers. "and they also started recording original material." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9_3EbwKPnU
**Pasadena Origin (Evolved from The Prime Movers)** : The band originated in Pasadena, California, evolving out of a previous group known as The Prime Movers. "Dread Zeppelin came together in Pasadena, California, evolving out of a group called The Prime Movers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9_3EbwKPnU
**Reggae Led Zeppelin Covers** : Dread Zeppelin's core concept was playing reggae covers of Led Zeppelin songs, a pursuit they took seriously despite initial perceptions as a novelty act. "They were pretty serious about what they were doing, which was playing reggae covers of Led Zeppelin songs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9_3EbwKPnU
**Serious Business Development** : As they played more, people began to take them more seriously, transforming their initial "fun" concept into a legitimate business. "The more they played, the more people took them seriously. Not too seriously, though. Everybody understood that it was about having fun, but this fun turned into a business, a real business." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9_3EbwKPnU
**Signed to IRS Records** : Dread Zeppelin signed with IRS Records, which was R.E.M.'s former label. "They were then signed to IRS Records, REM's old label." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9_3EbwKPnU
**Stewart Adamson's Fandom** : Stewart Adamson of Big Country was a significant fan of Dread Zeppelin. "Steward Adamson, a big country, remember them? He was a big fan of these guys." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9_3EbwKPnU
**Tortelvis - Elvis Impersonator Frontman** : After The Prime Movers imploded, the remaining members reformed with an Elvis impersonator named Tortelvis, whose real name was Greg Tortelli. At the time he joined, he weighed nearly 300 pounds. "When that group imploded, they started up again, this time with an Elvis impersonator who went by the name Tort Elvis out front. His real name was Greg Tortelli, and at the time he joined the band, he was pushing 300 pounds." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9_3EbwKPnU
