## albums
**Attack & Release (2008)** : The fifth studio album, marking the band's transition to a professional studio (Summa in Painsville, Ohio) and their first collaboration with an outside producer, Danger Mouse. Danger Mouse was initially unavailable due to a commitment to Ike Turner but became available when that project fell through; he played keyboards and bass on the album, with other musicians contributing guitars, vocals, and clarinets. Patrick Carney adopted a John Bonham-inspired drum setup for this album in the summer of 2007, resulting in a bigger, deeper, tighter drum sound. "From Attack and Release, that's the album, this is called I Got Mine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PmwfC4HR3_I
**Black Rock (2009)** : A collaborative album recorded in 2009 with Damon Dash, featuring numerous R&B and hip hop guest artists. "In 2009, they recorded a record called Black Rock, which was a collaboration with a guy named Damon Dash." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
**Blackroc (2009)** : A collaboration record with Damon Dash, featuring numerous R&B and hip-hop guest artists. "In 2009, they recorded a record called BlackRock, which was a collaboration with a guy named Damon Dash." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PmwfC4HR3_I
**Brothers** : Ten tracks from this album were recorded in the abandoned Muscle Shoals Sound Studio. The band brought their own equipment and worked amidst dust and cobwebs, demonstrating their "desperate" desire to absorb the lingering vibes of the historic space. "They worked through the dust and the cobwebs and recorded ten tracks from the Brothers album there." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QEkGSpxeuMk
**Brothers (2010)** : The band's sixth album, released on May 18, 2010, after seven years and five albums of steady growth. The cover features a longer, nonsensical title as a tribute to Howlin' Wolf's 1969 album. The production involved Danger Mouse for one song and much of it was recorded at the historic Muscle Shoals Sound Studio in Alabama, known for its amazing acoustics and natural echo chambers, despite challenges with outdated in-house gear and electrical issues frying modern equipment. The recording rooms emphasized the bass on the record. The album was nominated for five Grammy Awards, winning Best Alternative Album and Best Artwork; the physical CD was designed to change color (black to white in US, white to black in Australia, silver to black in Europe) when heated by a CD player's laser. "The Brothers record was big too, with five Grammy nominations overall." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PmwfC4HR3_I

"and a tribute record called Chula Homa, which is dedicated to a blue singer named Junior Kimbrough." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
**Chulahoma (2006)** : A tribute record dedicated to blues singer Junior Kimbrough, an artist who significantly influenced Dan Auerbach in high school, inspiring him to pursue guitar full-time. **El Camino (2011)** : The seventh studio album, released just 19 months after *Brothers*, following a period of intense touring. Recorded in Nashville in spring 2011 at Dan Auerbach's Easy Eye Sound studio, with Danger Mouse producing the entire record and co-writing all 11 tracks. The album's songs were composed around short hooks inspired by favorite bands like The Beatles, The Clash, The Cramps, Elvis, and The Ramones, simplifying the creative process as they had no finished music upon entering the studio. The album cover features an old Chrysler van, similar to their "Grey Ghost" tour vehicle. It achieved huge success, with platinum sales globally and triple platinum in Canada, where it was a best-performing territory. It won Best Rock Album at the Grammys, and two other awards went to its single "Lonely Boy," while Dan Auerbach won Producer of the Year. "Huge success. Platinum sales around the planet. Triple platinum in Canada by the way, where, with about half the population of the UK, we bought almost as many copies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PmwfC4HR3_I
**Live in Austin (2003)** : An official bootleg album released with the band's approval, recorded on October 24, 2003, at Emo's in Austin, Texas, during the tour for the *Thickfreakness* album. Its initial release format was as a podcast. "An official boot leg from the block keys that's hard-ro, and early single, captured in Austin, Texas on October 24, 2003, back in a moment." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PmwfC4HR3_I
**Magic Potion (2006)** : The fourth studio album, released in 2006 on the Nonesuch label. For this record, the band returned to Patrick Carney's basement, using about $5,000 worth of inexpensive gear, preferring the "vibe and weird sounds" of that space. The band was reportedly unhappy with the album's mastering, which resulted in a lack of low end. Despite their reservations, it sold better than their previous three albums, reaching the top 100 on the American album charts and number 12 in Australia, marking encouraging progress. "On the plus side, this record sold better than the other three, reaching into the top 100 on the American album charts, and it's highest number 12 in Australia." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PmwfC4HR3_I
**Rubber Factory (2000) – Tire Warehouse Recording** : Released in 2000, this album was recorded in an abandoned tire warehouse they rented for $500 a month, using only used gear and recycled recording tape. The album's title reflects its unconventional recording location. "Their 2000 album was entitled, Surprise, Rubber Factory, makes sense." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=raRCZ0Y5up8
**Rubber Factory (2004)** : The third album, recorded over five months in the second floor of a former General Tire factory in East Akron, Ohio, rented for $500 a month. The band used a recording console bought on eBay (once owned by a Loverboy sound engineer, but deemed "crap") and recycled, baked recording tape provided by their label. Despite glitches with the space and gear, it received good reviews, secured a slot at Lollapalooza, a performance on David Letterman, and was their first album to break into the Top 200 album chart, peaking at number 143. "In the end, they called it, what else, rubber factory." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PmwfC4HR3_I
**The Big Come Up (2002)** : The Black Keys' debut album, recorded in Patrick Carney's parents' unfinished basement in Akron, Ohio, using an 8-track digi recorder between January and March 2002. Released on May 14, 2002, on CD and in ten different colors of vinyl (limited to under 1,000 copies each, now collector's items). It was their only release on the LA-based label Alive. "It was called The Big Come Up and was recorded in Patrick's parents' unfinished basement in Akron using an 8-track digi recorder between January and March of 2002." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PmwfC4HR3_I
**The Moan (2004)** : An EP release by The Black Keys. "one of which is called The Mone from 2004". ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PmwfC4HR3_I
**Thickfreakness (2003)** : The band's second album, funded by Dan Auerbach and Patrick Carney by mowing lawns. It was recorded in a single 14-hour sprint in Patrick Carney's basement after signing with indie label Fat Possum. Critically acclaimed, its Japanese release was retitled *Inezuma Rock and Blues* (Inezuma meaning lightning) because the original title didn't translate well. "This one was called Thick Freakness, and like the first record was loved by critics." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PmwfC4HR3_I
**Turn Blue (2014)** : The band's eighth studio album, released on May 12, 2014. Recorded in fits and starts over more than a year and a half, it is described as Dan Auerbach's divorce album, with the lyrics reflecting his personal struggles. The album's existence was announced via a tweet from Mike Tyson. Its title and cover art are an homage to Gullardy, a Cleveland horror movie show host from the 1960s, drawing on his interest in mind control, brainwashing, and hypnotism. Five singles were released from the album. "Turn blue, the eighth album, was recorded in fits and starts over more than a year and a half before being released on May 12, 2014." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PmwfC4HR3_I
**Turn Blue (2014) – Vinyl Release** : The Black Keys released their 2014 album Turn Blue on vinyl, with producer Danger Mouse reportedly considering the vinyl version during the studio process. "The Black Keys released their 2014 album Turn Blue on Vinyl." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GES0Ms_kaCI

## curiosities
**"Grey Ghost" Van** : In their early touring days, The Black Keys used a 1994 Chrysler van, which they affectionately named "The Grey Ghost," to travel between gigs. A similar old Chrysler van later featured prominently on the album cover of *El Camino*. "they were just scraping by and chugging from gig to gig in a 1994 Chrysler van that they called the Grey Ghost..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
**Accidental Band Formation** : The Black Keys formed when Dan Auerbach, seeking to create a demo as a singer-songwriter, asked Patrick Carney, who owned a 4-track recorder, for help. They invited musician friends to round out the sound, but only Dan and Patrick showed up on the scheduled recording day, leading them to form the band as a duo out of necessity. "On the appointed day, no one except Dan and Patrick showed up. So that left the two of them to form a band on their own." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PmwfC4HR3_I
**Akron, Ohio Roots** : Both Dan Auerbach and Patrick Carney hail from Akron, Ohio, having met around ages 8 or 9. They became friends in high school, despite contrasting personalities (Dan as a "jock" and Patrick as a "nerd"), and both attended the University of Akron. Their hometown's historical significance as a tire manufacturing hub and their attendance at Firestone High School are notable local connections. "Dan and Patrick are both from Akron, Ohio." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PmwfC4HR3_I
**Album Announcement by Mike Tyson** : The Black Keys' *Turn Blue* album was uniquely announced by boxer Mike Tyson via a tweet, creating an unusual media buzz around its release. "Its existence was announced by Mike Tyson, yes, him in a tweet." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PmwfC4HR3_I
**Band Internal Crisis (Brothers Era)** : During the period leading up to and during the *Brothers* album, Dan Auerbach and Patrick Carney faced severe interpersonal conflicts, with Patrick explicitly stating they were not "friends" outside of band work. Tensions escalated when Dan recorded a solo album without Patrick's knowledge, leading to months of silence, and further complicated by drama involving Patrick's wife, Denise, whom Dan disliked. Denise's alleged infidelity, "mind games," and financial misconduct contributed to Patrick's depression and increased drinking, bringing the band close to collapse until Patrick ended his marriage while on tour in Europe, which helped restore the band's stability. "The thing that probably kept the block keys together at this point was some stuff that initially kept them apart." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PmwfC4HR3_I
**Band Tension and Patrick Carney's Divorce (2010)** : During the period surrounding the recording of *Brothers*, significant internal tension arose between Dan Auerbach and Patrick Carney. Patrick acknowledged they were "sick of each other" and maintained separate lives outside of band work. This was compounded by Dan's solo album (made without Patrick's knowledge, which upset him), and Patrick's tumultuous divorce from his wife, Denise. Dan believed Denise was "bad news," and she was accused of cheating, playing mind games, and taking money. Patrick consequently suffered from depression and began drinking, leading the band to the brink of collapse, until he divorced his wife over the phone while the band was in Europe, after which their relationship and the band's dynamic began to improve. "Then there was the drama between Dan and Patrick's wife Denise." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ooNAHswiT5Q
