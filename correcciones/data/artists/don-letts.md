## curiosities
**Association with The Clash** : Don Letts was a confidant and friend to both the Sex Pistols and The Clash, later becoming a member of Big Audio Dynamite alongside his Clash bandmate, Mick Jones. "Don Letts, a confidant and friend of both the sex pistols and the clash... And later on, Don would become a member of Big Audio Dynamite with this clash buddy, Mick Jones." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_1NNvxgx8uA
**Pioneer DJ in Early Punk Scene** : Don Letts, a member of Big Audio Dynamite with Mick Jones of The Clash, was deeply involved in the early punk scene as a DJ. He played dub and reggae records in clubs, introducing these new genres and sounds to the pioneers of the punk era, marking a significant first in musical cross-pollination. "And he got involved in the early punk scene as a DJ spinning dub and reggae records in clubs, introducing that music to pioneers in the punk era to New genres." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
**Punk and Reggae Crossover** : In the 1970s, Don Letts was a DJ who played a crucial role in introducing reggae and dub music to a new generation of punk kids in clubs. At a time when punk records were scarce, Jamaican music, including dub and other Black music forms, became the foundational sound for these punks until their own genre's records emerged. "Don Letts, a confidant and friend of both the sex pistols and the clash. He was the DJ who introduced reggae and dub to a new generation of these punk kids in the 1970s in clubs where they hung out. See, there were no punk records yet, so that music, Jamaican music, dub music, black music became theirs until they had some punk records to listen to." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_1NNvxgx8uA
