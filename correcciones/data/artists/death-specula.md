# artist - Death Specula

## songs
**Internet Band (1994)** : Death Specula opened their August 3, 1994, live netcast from the University of California in Santa Cruz with a song they called "Internet Band." This track was a take-off of the Grand Funk song "American Band," and its recording is reportedly hard to locate. "They started their set with a song they called Internet Band, which was a take-off of the Grand Funk song American Band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gP3oQeYEvso

## curiosities
**Early Online Music Repository Artist (1992)** : Death Specula was among the first ten bands featured on the Internet Underground Music Archive, which was launched in 1992 by computer enthusiasts at the University of California at Santa Cruz. This archive is considered a very early online repository for music. "But we do know that a California band called Death Specula was one of the first ten bands on something called the Internet Underground Music Archive." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gP3oQeYEvso
**Possible First Musical Netcast (August 3, 1994)** : Death Specula performed a live netcast on August 3, 1994, from the University of California in Santa Cruz, home of the Internet Underground Music Archive. This performance, which began with their song "Internet Band," is a strong contender for the earliest live musical performance broadcast over the internet. "It might have been Death Specula. They did something live to the net on August 3rd, 1994, from the University of California in Santa Cruz" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gP3oQeYEvso
