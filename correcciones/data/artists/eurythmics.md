## curiosities
**Dread Zeppelin Producer** : Dave Stewart, known from the Eurythmics, produced a significant amount of material for Dread Zeppelin by the end of 1990. "they'd recorded a bunch of stuff with Dave Stewart, the guy from the Eurythmics." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I9_3EbwKPnU
**Featured on MTV** : Eurythmics was among the numerous New Wave groups whose music videos were regularly played on MTV, contributing to the channel's diverse programming. "They played all the groups that we've talked about in many more. Eurythmics, XTC, Soft Sale, Adamance, Human League, Bowah Wow, and The Go Go's..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYsV3ozbk4M
**Pioneering 80s Keyboard-Bass Alt Rock** : Eurythmics was identified as one of the "keyboard bass bands" from 25 years prior to the 2000s, whose music resonated with alternative kids and contributed to the dance-oriented electronic sound of 80s alt rock that later saw a resurgence. "25 years earlier, long before Grunge guitars took over, alternative kids danced to keyboard bass bands like Depeche Mode, New Order, OMD, Durand Iran, the Human League, Eurasia, Eurythmics, and so many others." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_yslM5oanRo
**Prominent Technopop Band** : Eurythmics was listed among the Technopop bands that achieved popularity, further demonstrating the genre's crossover appeal into the pop charts and its exposure on platforms like MTV. "Petchop boys, Urythmix, Erasure, More Depeche Mode." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
**Saluted on Teal Album** : Eurythmics was among the artists whose songs were saluted (covered) on Weezer's Teal Album. "Eurythmixt." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LYRPxtP61JM
**Two-Person Synthpop Model** : Listed as an example of a synthpop band that adopted the common two-person model, featuring a singer or front person alongside a composer, programmer, and operator. "Yes, E-Rature, the pet shop boys, Eurythmics. Many of them had this two-person model." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KF5MwJVYng
