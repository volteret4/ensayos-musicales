## curiosities
**'Unknown Pleasures' Cover Art** : The striking black cover design for "Unknown Pleasures" by Factory Records in-house art director Peter Saville featured minimal text and an image that appeared to be a mountain range but was actually a graphical representation (ridge line plot) of 100 consecutive pulses from CP1919, the first pulsar ever discovered, found in the Cambridge Encyclopedia of Astronomy. Saville reversed the original black-on-white diagram to white-on-black, creating an iconic image that launched countless t-shirts and a 2003 clothing line. "It was originally black on white, but Savile reversed it to be white on black. But what is this? It's a graphical representation, what's known as a ridge line plot, of 100 consecutive pulses from a pulsar known as CP1919." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1VWJ0uCQx8w
