## curiosities
**Cultural Mentions** : Fisher appeared in various media. He is mentioned in Thomas Pynchon's 1990 novel *Vineland*, was the subject of a comic book, made late-night network TV appearances, and was featured in a documentary about his life. "He's mentioned in the book Vinyland, a 1990 novel by Thomas Pinchon." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HcK4M7-02ik
**Early Life and Mental Health** : Larry Fisher, known as Wildman Fisher, was from Los Angeles. He suffered from schizophrenia and bipolar disorder, which led to much of his adult life being spent homeless or institutionalized, often heavily medicated. "Because he suffered from schizophrenia and bipolar disorder, much of his adult life was spent either homeless or institutionalized." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HcK4M7-02ik
**Early Supporters** : Fisher attracted fans, notably Frank Zappa, Alice Cooper, and later Mark Mothersbaugh of Devo. Zappa helped him record his 1969 double album. "He did have fans, including Frank Zappa, Alice Cooper, and later Mark Mother's Ball of Devo." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HcK4M7-02ik
**Estrangement from Zappa** : His relationship with Frank Zappa ended when Fisher reportedly threw a bottle at Zappa's baby daughter, leading to him being "cut off." "He was this pseudo projesse of Zappa until the day he threw a bottle at his baby daughter. He was cut off." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HcK4M7-02ik
**Godfather of Outsider Music** : Many consider Wildman Fisher to be the godfather of outsider music. "Wildman Fisher, the former homeless LA street musician, a lot of people consider to be the godfather of outsider music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HcK4M7-02ik
**Later Decline and Death** : In 2003, Fisher experienced a six-month episode of paranoia that led him back to homelessness. His aunt rescued him and helped him get back on his medication, which restored some functionality but also stifled his musical creativity, leading him to stop performing. He died in June 2011 from heart failure. "But the medications somehow snuffed out his musical creativity, and he never performed again." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HcK4M7-02ik
**Opening Acts** : He opened for various prominent artists and bands, including The Byrds, Iron Butterfly, and Bo Diddley. "He landed opening gaze with everyone from the birds to iron butterfly to bow didly" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HcK4M7-02ik
**Street Musician Career** : He worked as a street musician, frequently performing a cappella songs on the Sunset Strip. "He was from Los Angeles and worked as a street musician, often performing accapelosongs on the sunset strip." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HcK4M7-02ik
