# artist - Art Wood

## curiosities
**Drums on "Dream Weaver" (Gary Wright)** : The human drums featured on Gary Wright's song "Dream Weaver" were played by Art Wood, distinguishing them from the pure synthesizer elements found elsewhere on the record. "The else on that record is pure synthesizer, but the drums, that's a human, Art Wood." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dablAKDOOV0
**Studio Drummer for Linn LM-1 Samples** : Art Wood, a studio drummer who had played with notable artists like Tina Turner, James Brown, and Bette Midler, was hired by Roger Linn to provide the real drum samples for the Linn LM-1 drum computer. He recorded these samples by repeatedly hitting his drum kit while isolated in a closet at Linn's house. "He brought in a studio drummer named Art Wood." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dablAKDOOV0
**Ubiquitous Sampled Sound** : Through his samples being used in the Linn LM-1 and subsequent LinnDrum, Art Wood technically appeared on thousands of recordings, often without physically playing, as demonstrated by his presence in recordings by artists like The Human League. "So technically, Art Wood has appeared on thousands of recordings." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dablAKDOOV0
