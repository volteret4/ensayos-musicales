## songs
**Me and Her (1993)** : This 1993 song by Heavens to Betsy is described as "intense stuff" and directly addresses the perils that can sometimes arise in female relationships and friendships. It exemplifies the band's "short, sharp female facing punk rock" style, exploring themes relevant to women. "Here for example is a song entitled Me and Her, which talks about the perils that sometimes come with female relationships and friendships." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Qss5kC2FUx0

## curiosities
**Early Breakup** : By 1994, Heavens to Betsy had broken up. This dissolution was part of the broader end of the original Riot Grrrl scene, influenced by differing views on media exposure and the watering down of its "girl power" ideals. "By 1994, Brad Mabel, Evans De Betsy and Huggy Bear had all broken up." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Qss5kC2FUx0
**Part of IPUC Festival** : Heavens to Betsy was an early pioneering Riot Grrrl group that participated in the International Pop Underground Festival in 1991, a key formative event for the scene. The group was led by Corin Tucker and included drummer Tracy Sawyer. "Another pioneering early Riot Girl group was called Heavens to Betsy. Again they were part of the International Pop Underground Festival" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Qss5kC2FUx0
**Riot Grrrl Band** : Heavens to Betsy is mentioned as one of the bands within the Riot Grrrl movement. "And then there were bands like Sugar Baby Doll and the Pagan Babies and Brat Mobio and Heaven's De Betsy and Huggy Bear." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zZM9ajwYr9E
