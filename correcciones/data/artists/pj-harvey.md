**Diverse Musical and Image Shifts** : PJ Harvey is known for her wide musical range, transitioning from quiet piano songs to "bruising punk-like performances." She enjoys theatricality and frequently alters her image from one project to another, maintaining a dynamic and unpredictable artistic persona. "PJ loves to be theatrical and enjoys shifting her image from project to project." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=clPuV7ezrIc
**Evolving Image** : Initially a tomboy, PJ Harvey adopted an approach similar to David Bowie in constantly transforming her image for almost every album project. This involved experimenting with both her clothing and physical appearance, often collaborating with her photographer friend, Maria Monachic. "PJ was originally very much a tomboy, but once she got started in the music business, she became almost bowie-esque with the way she changes her image for almost every album project." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IxJ0X9ytGgI
**Fearless Thematic Exploration** : Throughout her career, which began in 1988, PJ Harvey has cultivated a reputation for being "unnervingly fearless" in her artistic approach. She explores difficult subjects such as religion, sex, love, and death, doing so entirely on her own terms. "And like all the other women on this list, she forged a reputation as being unnervingly fearless when it came to doing things her way, and exploring themes of religion, and sex, and love, and death, and other difficult subjects." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=clPuV7ezrIc

She was also nominated for the award two other times. Additionally, *Let England Shake* won the Ivor Novello Songwriting Award in 2012, and she has received eight Brit nominations and six Grammy nominations, highlighting her extensive critical acclaim. "But PJ remains the only person to win the Mercury Prize twice." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=clPuV7ezrIc
**Only Two-Time Mercury Music Prize Winner** : PJ Harvey holds the unique distinction of being the only artist to win the Mercury Music Prize twice. Her first win was for "Stories from the City, Stories from the Sea" in 2001, and her second was for "Let England Shake" in 2011. "PJ remains the only person to win the Mercury Prize twice." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rEawe_1Fqf0
