**Esteemed Fans and Original Songwriting** : Before their breakup in 1975, Fanny garnered a significant following that included David Bowie, John Lennon, George Harrison, and Sly Stone. They released five major label albums and notably wrote all their own songs, a rare feat for female artists at the time. "David Bowie, John and George from The Beatles, Slice Stone, and a ton of other big names became huge fans. They released five major label albums and they wrote all their own songs, which is something almost no female artists did back in those days." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjQiZqpmQlE
**Pioneering All-Female Band** : Formed in 1969, Fanny consisted of four women, two of whom were from the Philippines. The band is considered arguably the first all-female band to achieve both critical and commercial success. "And you could make a case that this was the first all female band to enjoy both critical and commercial success." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjQiZqpmQlE
**Pioneering All-Female Rock Band** : Fanny was an excellent all-female rock group from California that achieved significant milestones, including a major label deal with Warner. They were the third all-female group to secure such a deal, following Goldie and the Gingerbreads and The Pleasure Seekers. "With DeBure, they ended up with a major label deal with Warner, making them the third all-female group behind Goldie and the Gingerbreads and the Pleasure Seekers to have that kind of deal." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9GHpC0kCgHY
**Post-Breakup Careers** : After Fanny broke up, original drummer Bray Brantz, who had returned for the band's fourth album, went on to tour and perform with several major artists including Elton John, ELO, Carole King, and Jimmy Buffett. **Session Work with Barbra Streisand** : Proving their musical prowess, Fanny's members were skilled enough to work as studio musicians, even being hired by Barbra Streisand in 1971 to appear on one of her albums. "In 1971, no one less than Barbara Streisand hired them to appear on one of her albums." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjQiZqpmQlE
**Sexism and Novelty Status** : Despite their talent, the band faced considerable sexism, often being told they were "pretty good for a girl" and asked to "sex it up" for their image. Their initial record deal was secured under the premise of them being a "novelty act." "And their original record deal came about only because they were signed as a novelty act." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjQiZqpmQlE
**Touring and Influence** : Fanny toured with prominent acts such as Jethro Tull and Humble Pie, and their work was instrumental in influencing a generation of aspiring female rock musicians. "They toured with big acts like Jethro Tull and Humble Pie and they influenced a generation of wannabe female rockers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjQiZqpmQlE
**Victims of Industry Sexism** : Despite their talent and championship by figures like David Bowie, Fanny's career was hindered by the "old boys club" in the music industry. They were not given due credit as musicians and songwriters, instead being pressured to conform to expectations of being "cute" and "sexy," which they refused. "Unfortunately, Fanny became a victim of the old boys club in the recorded music industry, and weren't given their dues as musicians and songwriters." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9GHpC0kCgHY
