# artist - Elvis Costello & The Attractions

## concerts
- Saturday Night Live

## songs
**Less Than Zero (N/A) - Saturday Night Live Substitution** : Elvis Costello initially agreed to perform this British single during his December 17, 1977, appearance on Saturday Night Live, after being told he could not play "Radio Radio." However, his promise was not fully kept, as he abruptly halted "Less Than Zero" to play the banned song instead. "Elvis said, okay, mate, whatever you say, I'll do my British single less than zero." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1y2UuU-me_s
**Radio Radio (1977) - Saturday Night Live Performance** : On December 17, 1977, during a last-minute appearance on Saturday Night Live, Elvis Costello was explicitly instructed by the show's producers not to perform "Radio Radio" due to its critical stance on radio broadcasting and NBC's ownership of radio stations. Despite initially agreeing to play "Less Than Zero," he famously stopped that song midway through and launched into the forbidden "Radio Radio," defying the network's censorship. "I'm sorry, ladies and gentlemen, there's no reason to do this song here. Radio radio." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1y2UuU-me_s

## curiosities
**Saturday Night Live Defiance (1977)** : On December 17, 1977, Elvis Costello and his band, The Attractions, were booked for Saturday Night Live as a last-minute replacement for the Sex Pistols. The show's producers specifically forbade him from performing "Radio Radio." Despite his initial agreement, Costello stopped his scheduled song and played "Radio Radio," creating a memorable and rebellious moment in live television. "Great new rock moment number 59. The night Elvis Costello screwed the producers of Saturday Night Live." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1y2UuU-me_s
