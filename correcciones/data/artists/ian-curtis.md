## curiosities
**Affair with Annik Honoré** : On October 16, 1979, while playing a gig at Plan K in Brussels, Belgium, Ian met Annik Honoré, a woman described as resembling Siouxsie Sioux. This encounter quickly evolved into a full-blown affair, with Ian frequently fabricating excuses to meet Annik, who subsequently moved to London and worked at the Belgian Embassy, and even toured with the band on their European dates in early 1980. "On October 16, 1979, the band played a gig at a place called Plan K in Brussels, Belgium. This is where Ian met a woman named Anik on a ray." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1VWJ0uCQx8w
**Allergies and Mood Swings** : Ian suffered from health challenges beyond drug-related incidents, including an allergy to sunlight (polyformus-like eruption) that caused his hands to swell like "big red balloons," requiring bandaging. He also exhibited "Jekyll and Hyde mood swings," once throwing a drink at Deborah at their engagement party, and in the band, his erratic behavior was termed "alter Ian," leading to strange actions like kicking in a door or getting into fights. "For example, he was allergic to sunlight. He had something called polyformus-like eruption." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1VWJ0uCQx8w
**Dancing as a Symptom** : Ian's distinctive "herky-jerky" dancing during live performances of Joy Division was often so erratic that it was difficult to distinguish from an actual epileptic seizure, leading to confusion among audiences and even band members about his true condition. "When joint division played live, Ian would dance in a very herky jirky sort of way, almost as if he was being electrocuted or something. Was he dancing or was he suffering another seizure?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1VWJ0uCQx8w
**Darkening Lyrics and Poetry** : Ian consistently carried a plastic bag filled with notebooks, where he would write poetry and lyrics whenever inspiration struck. As these writings were adapted into Joy Division's music, the band's sound became increasingly somber, dark, and depressing, with nearly all compositions written in a minor key. "And finally, more people began to notice that Ian's lyrics and poetry were becoming darker and more depressing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1VWJ0uCQx8w
**Day Jobs and Punk Discovery** : After finding a house with Deborah (eventually settling at 77 Barton Street in Macclesfield, where he had a room for his poetry), Ian initially worked in a record store in downtown Manchester, where he discovered punk rock. He later secured more stable employment within the British Civil Service, moving between several parts of the bureaucracy, such as the Ministry of Defense and an unemployment office. "He got a day job in a record store at downtown Manchester, which is where he discovered punk rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1VWJ0uCQx8w
**Death by Suicide (May 18, 1980)** : Ian Curtis died by suicide on May 18, 1980, at the age of 23. He had been in very poor health, suffering from epileptic seizures exacerbated by a "crazy cocktail" of prescriptions, drinking, illegal drug use, poor diet, and late nights. He also faced an impending divorce, an affair with a Belgian woman, money worries, and pressure to continue performing with Joy Division despite his problems. The final Joy Division show on May 2, 1980, saw Ian faint during the last song, experiencing another grand mal seizure, leading to the cancellation of their upcoming North American tour. On the evening of May 17, Ian returned to his home at 77 Barton Street in Macclesfield, Manchester. His wife Deborah was out. He watched the 1977 film "Strozek," drank strong coffee and whiskey, and then wrote a long note to Deborah all night. As the sun rose on May 18, he put on his favorite album, "The Idiot" by Iggy Pop, went to the garden for a length of rope from the clothesline, and hanged himself. Deborah found him a few hours later. "He'd been in very poor health, suffering from epileptic seizures that were exacerbated by a crazy cocktail of prescriptions, drinking illegal drug use, poor diet late nights, an impending divorce, and a ferry was having with a woman from Belgium worries about money and pressures to keep on performing with the band despite all these problems." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sP75_gbZzIU
**Early Life and Influences** : Born in Manchester on July 15, 1956, Ian Curtis's father was a police detective, and he had a younger sister named Carol. Raised in Hudsfield, outside Manchester, he sang in the church choir and was an avid reader, delving into Dostoyevsky, Sartre, and other philosophers, as well as literature and poetry, alongside a fascination with military history, particularly Nazi Germany. "Ian was born in Manchester on July 15, 1956. His father was a police detective and he had a younger sister named Carol." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1VWJ0uCQx8w
**Early Marriage and Poetic Preoccupation** : On August 23, 1974, Ian married his girlfriend, Deborah, whom he had met when he was 16. Deborah found him mysterious and cool, and was intrigued by a box full of poetry he had written, some of which contained romantic fantasies about him dying young. "But then again, some of that poetry contained romantic fantasies involving Ian dying young." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1VWJ0uCQx8w
**Epilepsy Diagnosis** : After collapsing at a gig as a teenager from strobe lights and learning about epilepsy through his civil service job in 1977, Ian experienced his first confirmed epileptic seizure on December 27, 1978, in the band's van returning from their first London gig. He was hospitalized, and after being on a waiting list, saw a specialist on January 23, 1978 (transcribed year, likely 1979 in context), who prescribed anti-convulsive drugs. "This was Ian's first confirmed epileptic seizure." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1VWJ0uCQx8w

This incident led to the decision to cancel all upcoming shows before the band was scheduled to leave for their first-ever North American tour, which was planned for two weeks later. **Formation of the Band** : Ian's interest in music intensified after being one of a few dozen people to see the Sex Pistols play the Manchester Free Trade Hall on July 20, 1976, easily identifiable by his jacket with the word "hate" on the back. He then formed a band with Peter Hook and Bernard Albrecht, two guys he kept bumping into again and again at concerts, including that Pistols gig. "His interest in music really took off when he was one of a few dozen people in the audience, when the sex pistols played the Manchester Free Hall on July 20th, 1976." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1VWJ0uCQx8w

**Increased Seizures and Mounting Pressures** : As Joy Division's popularity increased, the more gigs they played and the harder they worked, the worse and more frequent Ian's epileptic attacks became, suffering four grand mal seizures in one night in May 1979. By April 1980, the intense pressures from recording the "Closer" album, an ongoing tour, an impending North American tour, his failing marriage, guilt over fatherhood, his demanding mistress, and his continued use of alcohol and drugs exacerbated his epilepsy, making his mood swings unpredictable and leading to a state where he couldn't fall asleep until he had a seizure. "It got to the point where he couldn't fall asleep until he had a seizure." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1VWJ0uCQx8w
**On-Stage Collapse and Overdose Attempt** : On April 4, 1980, during a show with The Stranglers at London's Rainbow venue, Ian suffered three epileptic attacks within the first five songs before completely collapsing; the band struggled to intervene earlier due to the difficulty of distinguishing his seizures from his characteristic dancing. Three days later, on April 7, Ian deliberately overdosed on his epilepsy medication, leaving a suicide note, and required a stomach pump at the hospital, yet a psychiatrist controversially declared him "not suicidal." "Three days later, Ian purposely overdosed on his epilepsy medication." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1VWJ0uCQx8w
**Pre-Suicide Activities (May 17-18, 1980)** : On the evening of May 17, 1980, Ian Curtis returned to his home at 77 Barton Street in Macclesfield, Manchester, while his wife Deborah was out. He watched the 1977 film "Strozek," which depicted a musician unable to decide between two women in his life, and consumed several cups of strong coffee and a shot of whiskey. With pictures of his wife and baby daughters in front of him, he spent the entire night writing a long note to Deborah. As the sun came up on May 18, he put on Iggy Pop's album "The Idiot," retrieved a rope from the garden clothesline, and hanged himself. "Once she was gone, Ian sat down to watch his movie, which was a 1977 film entitled Strozek, which follows a musician and formmental patient who travels to the US and is unable to decide between the two women in his life." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sP75_gbZzIU
