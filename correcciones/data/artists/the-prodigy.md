## curiosities
**Continuing Late 90s Electronica Trend** : The Prodigy was still active and relevant in the 2000s, representing the continuation of late 90s trends towards electronica that contributed to the revival of dance-oriented elements in alt rock. "The prodigy and the chemical brothers were still with us, so as Fatboy Slim." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_yslM5oanRo
**Featured on Big Shiny Toons 2** : The Prodigy was among the 100% alt-rock artists featured on Much Music's *Big Shiny Toons 2* compilation CD in 1997. This album sold over 1.2 million units in Canada, showcasing the popularity of alternative music in the Canadian market. "featuring the prodigy, blur, Marilyn Manson, Bush, Collective Soul, the Tea Party, Radiohead, Stone Temple Pilots, and more." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7IDbqdIw3WU
**Influence on Zero** : The Prodigy was one of the diverse musical inspirations collectively enjoyed by the members of Zero, the band that eventually became Linkin Park. "Allison Chains, the Roots, the Prodigy, you mix it all together and it all came out like this." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cUqdY_T7luM
**Keith Flint's Death in 2019** : Keith Flint of The Prodigy passed away in 2019, joining a growing list of deceased musicians. "Then in 2019, Keith Flynn of the Prodigy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sdPeii-8Ysw
**The Matrix Soundtrack Contribution (1999)** : The Prodigy was featured on the first *Matrix* soundtrack, released in 1999. The album was characterized by "really cool heavy stuff," including contributions from other heavy acts like Marilyn Manson, Ministry, Rammstein, and Rage Against the Machine. "It was some really cool heavy stuff, Marilyn Manson, Ministry of the Prodigy, Ramstein, Rage Against the Machine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM
**Tom Morello Guest Appearance** : Tom Morello has made guest appearances on records by various friends and artists, including The Prodigy.
