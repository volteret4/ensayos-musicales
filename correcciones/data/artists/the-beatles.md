## concerts
- **Shakespeare Stadium (August 1965)** : The Beatles performed at Shakespeare Stadium in August 1965, during which their amplification system proved insufficient for the crowd's noise. They used four All-Tech amplifiers, each generating 175 watts, through the stadium's baseball PA system. Despite this being considered "crazy, crazy loud" at the time, the crowd generated 135 decibels of noise, which was twice the volume the amps could produce, highlighting the critical need for improved concert amplification. "When The Beatles played Shakespeare Stadium in August 1965, all they had behind them was four all-tech amplifiers, each generating 175 watts of power, through the same PA system that the announcer used for baseball games."

**Yesterday and Today (1966) – Infamous Butcher Cover** : An album issued in 1966 featuring original artwork of the Beatles posing with headless baby dolls and raw meat, known as the infamous "butcher cover." This photo, part of a collection by Robert Whitaker, was conceived as a protest against the Vietnam War and possibly a "dig" at EMI for "butchering" the Beatles' UK albums for North American release. A limited number of these albums were sent to stores, causing record store owners to be appalled. Capitol Records, the band's American label, immediately recalled all 60,000 albums distributed and held back another 750,000 in warehouses. A new cover was chosen, depicting the Beatles sitting around a steamer trunk looking annoyed. Many recalled albums simply had the new cover pasted over the old one, which could be carefully steamed off to reveal the original. A sealed copy of the original butcher version in mint condition can be worth $125,000.
