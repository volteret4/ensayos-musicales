## albums

The first track, "Hallogallo," runs for approximately 10 minutes and exemplifies the gentle, unhurried improvisation and distinctive beat of this "Motorik Prog Rock." ## curiosities
**Influence on David Bowie** : David Bowie was deeply interested in the German band Neu!, and continued to follow his "Krautrock Muse" during the recording of his album "Heroes." "He was deeply interested in what was happening in Germany with groups such as Kraftwerk, Ken, New, and the rising sound of synthesizers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rw0uXf5UJvo
**Inspiration for Bowie's "Heroes" Album Title** : The title of David Bowie's album "Heroes" is attributed to a song called "Hero" that appears on an album by the German band Neu!. "In fact, the title of the album is attributed to a song called Hero that appears on an album by the German band New." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rw0uXf5UJvo
**Pioneers of Motorikbeat** : Formed in 1971, the group Neu! (German for "new") pioneered the Motorikbeat, a distinctive straight 4/4 rhythm that conveys a feeling of movement, often associated with driving on Germany's motorways. This approach became popular in the German underground and influenced artists in Britain and pre-CBGB New York. "They were pioneers of something known as the MotorRickbeat." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ePZDkqLM_gw
