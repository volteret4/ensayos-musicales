## curiosities
**Considered "Too Pop for Alternative"** : Paramore was listed among the artists considered "too pop to be alternative" by some segments of the alt-rock scene, encountering similar criticisms regarding their appeal. "and even Paramore." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Exception to Pop Punk Decline** : Paramore was noted as an exception to the general decline of pop punk in the 2010s, demonstrating continued relevance in the genre. "We might point to Paramore, but a better example is probably Fallout Boy, who actually returned from Mahayatus in 2013 with an album that they called Save Rock and Roll." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Influenced by Blink-182** : Paramore is mentioned as an artist who has been influenced by Blink-182, highlighting Blink-182's significant role in shaping the landscape of contemporary pop punk and alternative music. "Some 41 good Charlotte simple-plan panic at the disco, followed boy, headly, billy talent, you would meet six, paramour, you get the idea." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-QP7ePxkQE
**Moved Away from Emo Sound** : Paramore was cited as a band that moved away from their original emo sound by the early 2010s, along with Fall Out Boy and Panic! at the Disco, contributing to the genre's temporary decline. This shift was part of a larger trend as fans and bands evolved, though emo later saw a strong revival mid-decade. "Followed boy moved away from their original emo sound as did Paramore and Panic at the disco." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HSvgRyGiOpE
**Shift from Emo Sound** : Paramore was noted as one of the bands that moved on to explore other sounds and styles, diverging from their original emo sound after the peak of third wave emo. "Others moved on to other sounds and styles, think panic at the disco and fallout boy in Paramore." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v_am7ifh7Pk
**Twilight Soundtracks Contribution** : Paramore contributed to the various *Twilight* soundtracks that accompanied the film series. These soundtracks featured a nice selection of tunes, mostly album cuts and outtakes, from a range of artists spanning different genres. "The Paramore, Lincoln Park, Collective Soul, Death Cab for Cutie, Tom York, the Killers, Editors, Metric, Block Keys, Dead Weather, Beck, Vampire Weekend, Florence in the Machine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM
