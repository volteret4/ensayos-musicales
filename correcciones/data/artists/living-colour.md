## curiosities
**Breaking Racial Barriers in Rock** : Living Colour, composed of four black guys from New York, was a "revelation" in the mid-1980s, playing heavy hard rock in a rock scene that was largely segregated. Their presence challenged the racial divide, especially as regular performers at the famous punk club CBGB. "Four black guys from New York playing heavy, hard rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PqOJ3YiFgYE
**Groundbreaking Funk Metal Sound** : Living Colour, a band of four Black men from New York City, created a funk metal sound that laid the groundwork for many groups of the 1990s. Their influence was felt across bands like Rage Against the Machine, 311, Primus, and even Korn. "The resulting funk metal laid the groundwork for many groups of the 1990s, ranging from rage against the machine to 311 to primus to even corn." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
**Lollapalooza 1991 Lineup** : Living Colour was part of the lineup for the first Lollapalooza festival in the summer of 1991. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**Lollapalooza 1991 Performer** : Living Colour performed at the inaugural Lollapalooza tour in the summer of 1991, which proved that a large audience was willing to pay to see a lineup of decidedly non-mainstream bands. "Along with founding band Jane's addiction, the line up included Suzy and the band sheets, living color, iced tea and body count, butthole surfers, Rawens band and some weirdo group that was never going to go anywhere called Nine-inch Nails." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2wZydttn4Cs
**MTV and Grammy Success** : The band achieved considerable success, becoming "MTV heroes and ended up winning a bunch of Grammy Awards," showcasing the widespread appeal of their genre-bending music.
