## songs
**Got You Where I Want You (1998)** : The first single from The Flies' 1998 album "Holiday Man." It was simultaneously placed on the soundtrack of the film "Disturbing Behavior." The synergy between the single, album, and soundtrack created sufficient buzz to make it a top 15, top 40 single in Canada and a number five record at Alt Rock. Despite attempts to replicate this success with other soundtrack placements, nothing else happened for the band. The song was re-recorded in 2008. As of February 2026, it had 44.7 million Spotify plays and 37.6 million YouTube views, totaling 82.366,552, with a One Hit Wonder Power Rating of 4.98 out of 10. "Together, the single, the album and the soundtrack created just the right amount of synergy and buzz, turning it into a top 15, top 40 single in Canada, and a number five record at Alt Rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZcF5JMhBj2M
**Hey Jude (2008, Cover)** : The Flies attempted a comeback by covering The Beatles' song "Hey Jude" in 2008, which is noted as "probably wasn't the wisest move." "They tried to come back by covering Hey Jude by the Beatles, which probably wasn't the wisest move." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZcF5JMhBj2M

## curiosities

**Origin and Family Background** : The Flies were formed in Hollywood in 1994 by two brothers from a famous surfing family. "The Flies were formed in Hollywood in 1994 by a couple of brothers from a famous surfing family." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZcF5JMhBj2M
**Soundtrack Strategy** : The band attempted to break new acts by including their songs on movie soundtracks appealing to Gen Xers, a popular method in the 90s. They tried to replicate the success of "Got You Where I Want You" through the soundtrack route "several times," but without further hits. "Thinking that they'd crack some kind of code, the Flies tried the soundtrack route again several times." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZcF5JMhBj2M
