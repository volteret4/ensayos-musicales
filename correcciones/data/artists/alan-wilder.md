# artist - Alan Wilder

## member of
- Depeche Mode

## curiosities
**Decision to Leave and Kidney Stone Attack (1992-1994)** : Alan Wilder decided during the recording of "Songs of Faith and Devotion" that he would eventually leave Depeche Mode. During a break between the first and second legs of the Devotional Tour, while in South Africa, he suffered an excruciating kidney stone attack. "At some point during the recording process, Alan Wilder decided that when the time was right, he was going to leave the group." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jvh8SP2QKXQ
**Joining Depeche Mode and Creative Frustration (1982-1991)** : Alan Wilder was the last to join Depeche Mode in 1982, serving as a synth player and programmer. By 1991, he grew frustrated as songwriting became almost exclusively Martin Gore's domain, limiting his creative contributions. "He was the last person to join Depeche Mode, signing on in 1982 as a synth player and programmer." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jvh8SP2QKXQ
**Near-Death Experience (September 1, 1994)** : On September 1, 1994, approximately two months after the Devotional Tour concluded, Alan Wilder was driving through the Scottish countryside when an RAF jet fighter trainer almost crashed onto his car, missing him by just over 200 meters. Had his girlfriend not seen the plane approaching and alerted him to pull over into a farm road, his car would have been hit. The two pilots onboard the jet were killed. "Had his girlfriend not seen the plane coming, allowing Alan to pull over into a farm road, his car would have been hit by this plane falling out of the sky." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jvh8SP2QKXQ
**Personal Life and Outside Projects (Early 1990s)** : Alan Wilder got married and purchased a 30-acre, eight-bedroom estate in the English countryside. He also pursued projects outside Depeche Mode, working with a group called Recoyl and the English Electronic Body Music (EBM) band, Knights of Rab. "He got married and bought a 30-acre eight-bedroom estate in the English countryside." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jvh8SP2QKXQ
**Post-Depeche Mode Life (Present)** : Alan Wilder now lives quietly, having owned a 30-acre, eight-bedroom country estate with his own recording studio. He also possessed a vacation property in Vestfold, Norway. "Alan Wilder is living quietly." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jvh8SP2QKXQ
**Resignation from Depeche Mode (June 1, 1995)** : Alan Wilder officially resigned from Depeche Mode on June 1, 1995, after 13 years with the group. His official statement cited increasing dissatisfaction with internal relations, working practices, and a consistent imbalance in the distribution of workload, stating that his input "never received the respect and acknowledgement that it warrants." "On June 1, 1995, almost a year after the tour ended, Alan Wilder resigned from Depeche Mode after serving 13 years with the group." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jvh8SP2QKXQ
