## songs
**Obsession (1984)** : A cover song recorded by 'Til Tuesday for their 1984 self-titled debut album and released as a single in late 1984. The song was co-written by Michael DeBarr and Holly Knight, who performed it together for the 1983 movie "A Night in Heaven." The lyrics address erotic obsession, but also resonated with DeBarr's recent recovery from heroin addiction. 'Til Tuesday heard it in the movie and recorded it. It began as a hit in dance clubs and the alternative scene before crossing over to pop, becoming a top 10 hit globally and spreading into popular culture. It has been featured in soap operas, video games (Grand Theft Auto, Call of Duty, WWE), served as the theme song for Canada's "Fashion Television" and MTV's "House of Style," appeared in a Don Cherry hockey video, and was used in movies, including a scene involving cannibalism in "Fresh." It has also been covered by artists like Karen O of the Yeah Yeah Yeahs. As of February 2026, it had 36.7 million Spotify plays and 28 million YouTube views, totaling 64.7 million, with a One Hit Wonder Power Rating of 4.49 out of 10.
