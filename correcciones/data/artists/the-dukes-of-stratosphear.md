## albums
**25 O'Clock (1985) - Debut EP** : This was the debut EP released by The Dukes of Stratosphear in 1985. It marked XTC's foray into 60s psychedelia, a genre they explored for fun, separate from their usual serious pop songwriting. "It started with an EP called 25O Clock in 1985." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=itK5fEqnA5E
**Psonic Psunspot (1987) - Second Album** : Released in 1987, this album (spelled with silent Ps) followed their debut EP. Psonic Psunspot garnered significant positive attention, receiving great reviews and producing a single that achieved success on alternative radio in North America, indicating that the pseudonymous project gained traction and was widely appreciated. "then came Sonic Sunspots, both spelled with Silent Peas in 1987, and that second record actually gained some traction." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=itK5fEqnA5E

## curiosities
**60s Psychedelia Tribute** : The Dukes of Stratosphear was XTC's pseudonymous project, conceived in 1985 to indulge their fascination with 1960s psychedelia. They adopted fake names for all band members and their producer, allowing them to experiment with a retro sound inspired by bands like Pink Floyd, The Byrds, Jefferson Airplane, Revolver-era Beatles, and The Beach Boys, purely for enjoyment and a break from their serious pop songs. "XTC wanted to try something a little retro, stuff that was outside their normal post-punk quirkiness." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=itK5fEqnA5E
**Producer John Leckie's Pseudonym** : The producer for The Dukes of Stratosphear's albums was credited under the fake name Swami Anand Nagara. This was actually John Leckie, who later went on to produce The Stone Roses. "The producer was somebody named Swami Anan Nagara, and that was actually John Lucky, the guy that would later go on to produce the stone roses." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=itK5fEqnA5E
