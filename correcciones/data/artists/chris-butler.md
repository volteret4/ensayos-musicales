# artist - Chris Butler

## albums
**The Devil Glitch (1996) – Longest Recorded Rock Song** : Released in 1996 as a solo project, this album featured only its title track, which stretched to 68 minutes and 53 seconds. The song contained 500 non-repeating verses and no instrumental breaks. Chris Butler spent three months writing it, then recorded his part (singing and acoustic guitar) in one continuous take. He subsequently divided the song into two to five-minute segments, which were then given to musician friends to add their instrumentation and backup vocals, before Butler reassembled everything into the final single track. It was nominated for a Grammy Award in 1998 and certified by the Guinness Book of World Records as the world's longest recorded rock song. "It stretched out to 68 minutes and 53 seconds, 500 verses, none of which repeated, and there were no instrumental breaks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T1XSJIm0Nh4

## curiosities
**1980s Career** : Throughout much of the 1980s, Chris Butler focused his career on working as a record producer and producing various cable TV shows, before returning to performing as a solo artist in the mid-1990s. "Chris spent much of the 1980s as a record producer and producing a bunch of cable TV shows." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T1XSJIm0Nh4
**Kent State University Experience** : Originally from Ohio, Chris Butler attended Kent State University and was among the students present when the National Guard fired upon protesters on May 4th, 1970. This event famously inspired the Crosby, Stills, Nash & Young song "Ohio." "He was actually in the group of students that was fired upon by the National Guard on May 4th of 1970." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T1XSJIm0Nh4
**Square Pegs Theme Song** : Chris Butler wrote the theme song for the CBS comedy "Square Pegs," which aired around 1981 and featured a very young Sarah Jessica Parker. "At around the same time, he wrote the theme song for the CBS comedy called Square Pigs, which started a very young Sarah Jessica Parker." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T1XSJIm0Nh4
