# label - The Face Magazine

## curiosities
**Coining of "Britpop" (May 1994)** : *The Face* magazine is credited with the first appearance of the term "Britpop" in its May 1994 issue. This occurred during an interview with Blur, where the magazine abbreviated "British Pop" to "Brit Pop" to reflect the band's call for a renewed focus on British popular music. "The first appearance of the word seems to have been in the face... There was an interview with Blur in the May 1994 issue... The magazine then abbreviated that to Brit Pop." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg
**The Face Magazine's Role in Britpop** : *The Face*, another monthly magazine, also began to dedicate coverage to the new generation of homegrown indie bands, contributing to the rise of Britpop. "Another magazine called The Face began to do the same." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg
