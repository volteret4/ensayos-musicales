# label - NME (New Musical Express)

## curiosities
**C81 Predecessor (1981)** : In 1981, the NME had previously attempted to assert its influence with a cassette compilation called *C81*, which achieved modest success. This earlier effort set a precedent for their more impactful *C86* project, demonstrating the tabloid's ongoing commitment to discovering and championing new musical talent. "The NME had tried to seize the lead once before 1981, with a cassette called C81, and that had been a modest success." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jhgZrwtYE2g
**C86 Compilation (May 1986)** : As one of the UK's prominent weekly music tabloids, the NME offered its readers a landmark compilation cassette titled *C86* in May 1986. This cassette, whose name referenced both tape length conventions and its release year, became a pivotal moment—a "big bang"—for the British indie scene by showcasing guitar-based indie songs and playing a crucial role in the discovery and promotion of new music. "In May 1986, the NME, the new musical express, one of the UK's weekly music tabloids, hadn't offered for their readers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jhgZrwtYE2g
**Crucial Role in British Music Discovery** : During a period when the British public had limited access to new music on the state-run BBC radio, weekly music tabloids like the NME, along with three other competing publications, became essential for discovering and promoting new bands. These papers played a critical role in anointing the "next big thing" and shaping the musical landscape. "At the time, there were four music tabloids, and because the British public was stuck mainly with the state BBC, there wasn't a lot of new music played on the radio." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jhgZrwtYE2g
