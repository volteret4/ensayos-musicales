# label - Odeon

## curiosities
**First Multi-Disc Album (1909)** : Odeon, a German record company, was the first label to issue a multi-disc "album" in the early 20th century. This release was a version of Tchaikovsky's "Nutcracker Suite" in 1909. The packaging resembled a photo album, with a series of sleeves for each record, though the cover offered minimal artistic detail. "The first label to do this was a German record company called Odeon, which issued a multi-disc version of Chikovsky's Nutcracker Suite. That was in 1909." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pwebM3TfSdg
**Pioneering Record Bundles (1909)** : In 1909, Odeon, a German company, was the first to introduce the practice of bundling multiple records together. These bundles, consisting of 10-inch, 78 RPM discs packaged in bound book-like formats, served as the direct precursor to what would become known as record albums. "The first company to create such bundles of records was Odeon, a German company in 1909." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tbKuJR0cPs
