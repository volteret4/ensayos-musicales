# label - Warner US

## curiosities
**Mass Artist Purge (2004)** : Around 2004, Warner US faced financial difficulties, driven by fears of Napster, piracy, and declining CD sales. They conducted a "big cull of artists," dumping approximately 200 artists in one week. The company had a policy to drop any artist who sold less than 50,000 units in America. Billy Talent, having sold around 60,000 units of their second record, narrowly avoided being cut, though they initially "wanted to get dumped" due to lack of label support. "I remember 2004, ish, Warner had a big call of artists. They dumped a lot of people because they were so afraid of Napster." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GT_u0eFDlk8
**Rejection of Billy Talent 3 Release** : After Billy Talent left Atlantic Records to sign with Warner US for their third album, "Billy Talent 3," the label ultimately decided not to release the record two months before its scheduled worldwide release. This decision was made by President Tom Wally, who "pulled the plug" after the album had been leaked. This left them without a US label just before a worldwide release, forcing them to find another label (Roadrunner Records) last minute. "two months before it was supposed to come out Warner US decided that they didn't want to release it and we had it was scheduled all around the world to be released except now we didn't have a label in the States to release it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GT_u0eFDlk8
