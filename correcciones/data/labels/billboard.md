# label - Billboard

## curiosities
**Billboard Hot 100 Singles Chart** : The Billboard Hot 100 singles chart is a primary industry benchmark, where a song's entry into the Top 40 is frequently used as the starkest definition of a "hit." "The starkest definition of a hit is a song that makes it into the top 40 of the Billboard Hot 100 singles chart." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d_uDHds2XGQ
**Historical Chart Compilation** : In earlier periods, the Billboard Hot 100 was compiled using a combination of radio airplay data and sales figures for 45 RPM, seven-inch singles, which determined the original "one hit wonders." "Now, in the old days, the chart was put together using a combination of radio, airplay and the sales of 45 RPM, seven, and singles." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d_uDHds2XGQ
**Influence of Pop Fan Consumption** : The listening habits of pop music fans, who often listen to the same songs multiple times, can significantly skew streaming charts, which in turn impacts the positions of songs on the Billboard Hot 100. "pop fans tend to be heavy consumers of music in the sense that they'll listen to the same songs 10, 20, 30 times in a row. And that skews where songs end up on the streaming charts, which in turn impacts Billboard Hot 100." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d_uDHds2XGQ
**Modern Chart Metrics** : Contemporary methods for compiling music charts, including the Billboard Hot 100, now incorporate diverse metrics such as download sales and various streaming statistics to reflect current music consumption patterns. "Today, though, metrics include download sales and all-matter streaming stats." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d_uDHds2XGQ
**Pop Music Bias** : The Billboard Hot 100 chart has a tendency to favor pop music, a characteristic attributed to the specific methods employed in its compilation and calculation. "the Billboard Hot 100 tends to veer over towards pop music because of the way it's compiled." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d_uDHds2XGQ
