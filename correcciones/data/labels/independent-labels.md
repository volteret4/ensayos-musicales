# label - Independent Labels

## curiosities
**Physical Release of "In Rainbows"** : Following its successful digital "tip jar" release, Radiohead's "In Rainbows" album was also released in physical form through a series of independent labels. This physical release achieved an additional million sales, demonstrating the continued viability of independent distribution channels. "And when the album was released in a physical form through a series of independent labels, they sold another million copies or so." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-CT7yYXonIk
**Rise of Power in Digital Age** : The music industry in the digital age saw the number of major record labels shrink to just four, leading to a resurgence of small, nimble independent record companies. These indies could quickly capitalize on new music, bands, or trends, providing global distribution without the need for major label backing. "When small, nimble, independent record companies can quickly capitalize on a song or a band or an album or a scene or a trend, no longer did you have to be on a major label to get global distribution of your music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-CT7yYXonIk
**Use of 360 Deals** : Independent labels also extensively utilized the "360 deal" model, similar to major labels. This business model involves providing upfront financial support to artists in exchange for a share of various revenue streams, enabling both parties to potentially survive and thrive in a market with declining record sales. "Indie labels use this model a lot too." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-CT7yYXonIk
