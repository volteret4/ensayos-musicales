# label - Vox

## curiosities
**Company Founding (1957)** : Vox, a British amplifier company, was established in 1957. It quickly became known for its distinctively designed amplifiers. "The first is Vox, an amplifier company founded in 1957 with the distinctive diamond-shaped grill pattern." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fQCOj3_zy54
**Defining British Invasion Sound** : The distinctive "Vox sound" became the definitive sonic characteristic for nearly all British Invasion bands from the mid-1960s onward. Artists like The Rolling Stones, The Kinks, and The Yardbirds, among dozens of others, prominently featured Vox products in their performances. "And the Vox sound became the sound for pretty much all British invasion bands of the mid-1960s and beyond." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fQCOj3_zy54
**Enduring Impact and Notable Users** : Vox amplifiers have maintained a profound impact on guitar rock across several decades. Beyond the British Invasion, later prominent groups and guitarists such as Queen, Dire Straits, Echo and the Bunnymen, R.E.M., Tom Petty, Blink 1-2, Oasis, Muse, U2, Foo Fighters, Kurt Cobain, Slash, Arctic Monkeys, and Radiohead have all utilized Vox units. "You could not understate the impact Vox had on guitar rock through the decades." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fQCOj3_zy54
**Introduction of the AC30 (1959)** : The 30-watt Vox AC30, introduced in 1959, became the company's most crucial model. Before its advent, most electric guitarists relied on amplifiers made by Fender and other smaller American manufacturers, leading to a more uniform guitar tone. "The most important model was the 30-watt Vox AC30, first introduced in 1959." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fQCOj3_zy54
**Unique Circuitry and Sound** : The Vox AC30 revolutionized guitar amplification with its use of vacuum tubes and innovative circuitry, including a "second treble stage" known as the "top boost circuit." This design produced a brighter, more "jangly" sound that differed significantly from existing amplifiers. "First it used vacuum tubes. Secondly, its amplification circuitry was different, employing a second treble stage, something called the top boost circuit." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fQCOj3_zy54
