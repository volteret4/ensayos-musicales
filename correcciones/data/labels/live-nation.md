# label - Live Nation

## curiosities
**Adele World Temporary Venue Construction (2024)** : In 2024, Live Nation constructed a temporary pop-up stadium in Munich capable of holding 80,000 people for Adele's "Adele World" residency. This ambitious project facilitated a record-breaking non-Vegas residency, grossing $240 million USD over 10 shows and demonstrating Live Nation's innovative approach to artist engagements beyond traditional venues. "In 2024, Live Nation built a temporary pop-up stadium in Munich that held 80,000 people." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GYUuTbOvyak
**Financial Impact of Lawsuits on Safety** : Live Nation has faced substantial financial repercussions from lawsuits related to festival disasters, such as the Astroworld Festival. These significant costs, as exemplified by the experience of Live Nation and Travis Scott, serve as a major incentive for the music industry to implement and improve crowd control, security, and safety measures. "Specifically, any lawsuits that might result from something going wrong just ask live nation and Travis Scott how expensive that can be." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pgchw2h87aw
**Involvement in Downsview Park Inquest** : As the promoter for the Radiohead concert at Downsview Park, Live Nation was a key party in the extensive and complex inquest that followed the stage collapse on June 16, 2012, which resulted in the death of drum tech Scott Johnson. The inquest scrutinized the outdated stage design and safety protocols. "In the aftermath, a long and confusing inquest was held involving the Ontario Ministry of Labor, Live Nation, the promoter, and Optex, the company commissioned to build the stage." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pgchw2h87aw
**Promoter in Reunion Era** : Live Nation was identified as one of the large promoters needing to sell tickets for various venues as new acts struggled to reach superstar status in the 21st century. Along with AEG, Live Nation capitalized on the "reunion fever" by bringing back heritage performers to appeal to nostalgic fan bases with disposable income. "Meanwhile, large promoters like Live Nation and AEG needed to sell tickets for festivals, stadiums, amphitheaters and arenas." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GYUuTbOvyak
**The Police Reunion Facilitation (2007-2008)** : Arthur Fogel, Live Nation's Head of Global Touring, was instrumental in securing The Police reunion tour. He offered a financial deal so substantial that the notoriously fractious band could not refuse, leading to a tour that grossed $359 million ($508 million in today's cash). "But when live nations head of global touring, Arthur Fogel, put an offer on the table, the numbers were so big that there was just no way the guys could turn it down." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GYUuTbOvyak
