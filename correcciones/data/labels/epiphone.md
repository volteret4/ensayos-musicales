# label - Epiphone

## curiosities
**Acquisition by Gibson (1957)** : Epiphone's rivalry with Gibson in producing high-quality guitars ended in 1957 when Gibson purchased the company, although the Epiphone name was preserved. "That was all settled when Gibson bought epiphone in 1957 while still keeping the epiphone name." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d9HNqb9gJSU
**Acquisition by Gibson's Parent Company** : In 1958, Epiphone was acquired by Gibson's parent company, leading to the merger of the two distinct lines. While Epiphone continued as a brand, its ownership and production decisions became intertwined with Gibson. "But then in 1958, it was bought by Gibson's parent company and the two lines were merged, so it's an epiphone but it's owned by Gibson." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Beatles Boost (1964)** : Epiphone experienced a significant sales surge in 1964 following The Beatles' appearance on The Ed Sullivan Show, where John Lennon notably played an Epiphone Casino. "The company's biggest break came in 1964 when the Beatles broke out." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d9HNqb9gJSU
**Budget Brand Status** : After being acquired by Gibson in 1957, Epiphone became Gibson's budget brand, offering cheaper versions of popular Gibson models, such as the Epiphone Les Paul or Epiphone Casino as alternatives to their Gibson counterparts. "And for a while, epiphone became Gibson's budget brand." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d9HNqb9gJSU
**Company History and Evolution** : Epiphone was founded in Turkey in 1873, initially making fiddles and lutes. The company moved to New York in 1903, expanding its production to mandolins and banjos, and later Spanish guitars around 1928, before introducing its first electric Hawaiian guitars in 1935. "This is a musical instrument company founded in 1873 in Turkey. That started by making fiddles and lutes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d9HNqb9gJSU
**Founding and Early Focus** : Epiphone was established as a standalone company in Izmir, Turkey, in 1873, and its primary business during its early years was largely centered on the manufacture of banjos. "Epiphone was a standalone company formed in Izmir, Turkey in 1873, and their business was largely devoted to making banjos." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Post-1969 Production Complexity** : Following 1969, Gibson relocated production of Epiphone models to various locations including Korea, China, and Indonesia. This geographical shift resulted in a complex manufacturing situation where some Epiphone 355s were produced by Gibson, while other models were made by different companies under license. "Things get a little murky after 1969 when Gibson moved production to Korea and then China and then Indonesia." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
