# label - NME

## curiosities
**"And the Motorcade Sped On" Giveaway (February 1987)** : The music magazine NME gave away Steinsky and Mass Media's song "And the Motorcade Sped On" with an issue in February 1987. "It was given away with an issue of the NME in February 1987." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pmKjZzypzvg
**NME's Role in Britpop** : The weekly music paper NME, along with Melody Maker, devoted significant coverage to the emerging Britpop scene, helping to establish it as a "new and very viable local music scene." "And the weekly papers, the NME and Melodymaker, some thirtieth into what appeared to be a new and very viable local music scene." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg
**Role in British Music Press** : NME was one of the numerous British music magazines and tabloids that filled the void left by the limited legal commercial radio in Britain prior to the 1970s. These publications saw it as their duty to discover and hype new music and scenes. "The NME, sounds, melody maker, and dozens more filled the gap played by radio in North America." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zB5zxycrbnY
**Widespread Adoption of "Britpop" (January 1995)** : The term "Britpop" reached its "real tipping point" in an issue of *NME* in January 1995, after which it became widely adopted by everyone to describe the burgeoning British music scene. "But the real tipping point seemed to come in an issue of the NME in January 1995. And after that, everybody began to use Brit Pop to describe what was going on." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg
