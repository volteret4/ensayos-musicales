# label - Rickenbacker

## curiosities
**12-String Guitar Innovation (360/12)** : Rickenbacker became famous for its 12-string models, particularly the 360-12, which was a favorite of George Harrison. Its popularity led other manufacturers like Gibson and Gretsch to enter the 12-string market. "With a 12 string, you have six pairs of strings, with each string in the pair very close together and tuned to the same note, an octave apart for the four lower pairs, and to the same note for the two higher ones." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d9HNqb9gJSU
**Company Sale and Shift to Spanish Electrics (1953)** : The company was sold in 1953, coinciding with the rise of Rock and Roll. The new owner discontinued the traditional Hawaiian steel guitars and shifted the company's focus to Spanish-style electric models. "The company was sold in 1953, just as Rock and Roll was being born." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d9HNqb9gJSU
**Distinctive "Jangle" Sound** : Rickenbacker guitars are known for their very distinct sound, often described with terms like "jangle" or "chime," making them immediately recognizable. "Rickenbacher's have a very distinct sound, jangle or chime or words that people use." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d9HNqb9gJSU
**Pioneer of Electric Guitars (1932)** : Rickenbacker, through Adolf Rickenbacker, is recognized as the first company to manufacture electric guitars, beginning in 1932. "As far as we know, Adolf Rickenbacher was the first to manufacture electric guitars in 1932." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d9HNqb9gJSU
