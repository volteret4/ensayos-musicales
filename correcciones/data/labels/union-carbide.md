# label - Union Carbide

## curiosities
**Refinement of Vinylite for Records** : Union Carbide refined polyvinyl chloride in the 1930s, marketing it under the name "vinylite." This material was crucial for Columbia Records' development of the micro-groove record (LP) due to its toughness and smooth surface, enabling closer grooves and reduced noise.
