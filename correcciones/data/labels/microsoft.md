**Windows 95 and CD-ROM Adoption** : Microsoft's release of Windows 95 on August 15, 1995, was a pivotal moment that cemented the company's dominance in personal computing. The operating system's large size (nearly 60 MB, requiring 26 floppy disks for installation) prompted Bill Gates to include CD-ROMs as the installation medium. This decision led to PCs widely shipping with CD-ROM drives, inadvertently laying the groundwork for digital music ripping and file sharing by making CD-ROM drives ubiquitous and enabling users to treat music CDs as data disks.
