# label - Cream Magazine

## curiosities
**Early "Punk" Term Usage (1970-1971)** : Cream, a Detroit-based music magazine, played a role in the early usage of the term "punk" in music criticism. In December 1970, critic Lester Bangs referred to Iggy Pop as "that stooge punk." In May 1971, Dave Marsh called Question Mark and the Mysterians "purveyors of punk rock" in the magazine. **Roxy Music Album Review (1973)** : Detroit-based Cream Magazine published a critical review of Roxy Music's self-titled debut album in 1973, disparaging its "artifice" and "polished deformity" and noting the heavy use of synthesizer by Brian Eno on Side Two. Despite the harshness, such reviews inadvertently attracted fans who enjoyed music that challenged conventional tastes.
