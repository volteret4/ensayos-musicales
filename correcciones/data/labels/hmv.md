# label - HMV

## curiosities
**Endorsement of the Gramophone** : HMV, a British company and ancestor of the well-known music store, played a crucial role in the decline of Edison's phonograph cylinders. They publicly endorsed Emil Berliner's gramophone over the phonograph, contributing to the shift towards flat disc records. "The death nail for Edison cylinders came when a British company called HMV, yes, the ancestors of the music store, endorsed the gramophone over the phonograph." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VlIErdSSVOM
**Resurrection by Doug Putnam** : HMV experienced a "resurrection" under Canadian entrepreneur Doug Putnam, who also acquired his Sunrise Records chain. This made HMV one of the few bright spots in the otherwise widespread collapse of big chain record stores. "The only real bright spot was the resurrection of HMV by Canadian entrepreneur Doug Putnam in addition to his sunrise Records Chain, which you also bought." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZzIqIydZTlA
**Suitcase Gramophone (1920s-1950s)** : HMV, a British electronics and recording company, was an early pioneer in portable music, releasing a gramophone housed in a suitcase as early as the mid-1920s. This device was manufactured in India. Their Model 102, launched in 1931, was considered the best of its kind and remained on sale until the late 1950s, demonstrating remarkable longevity for an early portable music player. `"Well listen, there was HMV, the British electronics and recording company. They released a gramophone packed into its own sort of suitcase as early as the middle 1920s. They made this thing in India."` ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OuFvfZJKcpc
