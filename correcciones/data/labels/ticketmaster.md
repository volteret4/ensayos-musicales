# label - Ticketmaster

## curiosities
**Acquisition of Ticketron and Market Dominance (1991)** : In 1991, Ticketmaster acquired its struggling rival, Ticketron, after the Carlyle Group had purchased and then flipped Ticketron's assets. This acquisition effectively made Ticketmaster the undisputed market leader in computerized concert ticket selling. "And with the acquisition of Ticketron, Ticketmaster became the undisputed market leader when it came to computerized concert ticket selling." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OCkov6FTliE
**Early Business Model and Shift (1982)** : Initially, Ticketmaster licensed its ticketing software to other vendors. This model changed in 1982 when Fred Rosen became CEO, moving the company from Phoenix to Los Angeles and focusing on securing exclusive contracts as the sole ticket seller for venues in Southern California. "The first ticket master also wrote Ticketing software that it licensed to other vendors, but that had all stopped by 1982 when Fred Rosen became CEO." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OCkov6FTliE
**Founding and Naming (1976)** : Founded in Phoenix in 1976, Ticketmaster got its distinctive name from co-founder Albert Leffler's wife. She suggested "Master Tickets," inspired by a dry cleaner called Master Cleaners, which Albert then flipped to "Ticket Master." "And so the new company was founded in Phoenix in 1976." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OCkov6FTliE
**Investment from Hyatt (1980s)** : Ticketmaster received a crucial investment from the Hyatt hotel chain, run by the Pritzker family. Hyatt, needing an uncorrupted ticket seller for the Superdome in New Orleans, found Ticketmaster suitable and invested in the company, further solidifying its market position. "Another big break came when the high at hotel chain run by the Pritzker family got a contract to run the superdome in New Orleans. They talked to Ticketmaster and they liked the company enough to invest in it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OCkov6FTliE
**Major Break with US Festival (1983)** : In early 1983, Ticketmaster landed a significant contract to be the sole seller of tickets for the second US Festival in San Bernardino, California, a large event underwritten by Steve Wozniak of Apple Computers. Despite lacking its own terminals at the time, Ticketmaster successfully sold approximately 670,000 hard tickets through box offices and a dedicated phone line. "A big landmark in the history of concert tickets happened in early 1983 when Ticketmaster managed to land a massive account. It became the sole seller of tickets for the second us festival, a giant event in San Bernardino California underwritten by Steve Wozniak of Apple computers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OCkov6FTliE
