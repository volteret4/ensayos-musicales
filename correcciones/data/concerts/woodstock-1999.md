# concert - Woodstock 1999

## curiosities
**Extreme Violence and Sexual Assaults** : The 30th-anniversary Woodstock show in 1999 was marred by tremendously violent mosh pits, resulting in numerous injuries, broken bones, and concussions. Most horrifically, the event was associated with widespread sexual assaults and charges of rape within the crowds. "Part of the disaster that was the 30th anniversary Woodstock show in 1999 can be traced back to the Moshe Pit." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KMx4bmq8o2Y
**Riots and Unsanitary Conditions (July 26, 1999)** : Woodstock 1999, held on July 26th, 1999, at an old Air Force base in Rome, New York, to commemorate the original festival's 30th anniversary, devolved into widespread riots. The event was plagued by extreme heat (38 degrees Celsius), exorbitant prices for basic necessities like food and water (a small pizza cost $12), and severely unsanitary campground conditions. These factors, combined with testosterone-fueled performances, pushed the crowd to a breaking point, resulting in bonfires, destruction of equipment and fences, looting of vendor booths and a nearby hardware store, the forced evacuation of a TV crew, and the burning of 12 tractor trailers. The aftermath included charges of rape and sexual assault, marking it as "not Rock's finest hour."
