"A year later, the landscape had changed entirely. And the second Lala Palusa tour began on July 18, 1992. **Canadian Counterparts** : While Lollapalooza was a prominent US festival, Canadian music's popularity and lucrativeness in the 1990s allowed for the creation of several homegrown festivals that acted as "our own Lola Palozes." This highlights the scale of the Canadian music industry's growth during that era. "Yes, there was Lola Paloza, but Canadian music was so popular and so lucrative in the 1990s that we had several of our own Lola Palozes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6PGgNCaW4xo
**Conception and Traveling Format** : Inspired by the Pixies' 1990 Reading performance, Perry Farrell conceived Lollapalooza as a traveling festival that would move from city to city over several months, a departure from previous U.S. festivals that were rooted in one location. "Here is where we need to talk about Lala Palusa. Now, remember how I said that Perry Farrell was inspired by the pixie set at Reading in 1990. That's where the idea for Lala Palusa came from." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Cultural Phenomenon (Early 1990s)** : In January 1993, Lollapalooza was recognized as a significant cultural "thing," alongside the rising grunge movement. Its popularity influenced radio station management to pursue an alternative format. "Lola Palusa was a thing and thought, yeah, this is maybe something that we can work with." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bI6TTA-fn7c
**Current Static Format** : In contemporary times, Lollapalooza has transformed from a touring festival into a static event held annually in Chicago every August. "Lala Palusa is a static festival held in Chicago every August." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Decline and Hiatus (1997-2003)** : Enthusiasm for Lollapalooza waned after the 1997 tour, leading to a hiatus. Another attempt in 2003 was largely forgotten. "There was one more tour in 1997, but by that time enthusiasm had waned. Another attempt was made in 2003, but nobody remembers that." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Doc Martens as Essential Accessory (Early 1990s)** : In the early 1990s, Doc Martens reached the height of their popularity, becoming an indispensable fashion accessory for anyone associated with the "Lollapalooza nation." These black boots, with their distinctive yellow stitching, perfectly complemented the grunge aesthetic of flannel shirts and jeans prevalent at the festival. "Docks reached the peak of popularity in the early 1990s as they became an essential accessory for anyone who was part of the Lava Polluzination." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mhQ36Y-c9fM
**Evolution from Alt-Rock to Pop/Hip-Hop** : Originally conceived in 1990 as a touring festival showcasing "left-of-center and alternative bands" like Red Hot Chili Peppers, Soundgarden, Rage Against The Machine, Pearl Jam, Tool, Green Day, Metallica, Hole, Smashing Pumpkin, Speedy Boy, Sonic Youth, and Remones, Lollapalooza was an essential part of 90s music culture. After its original concept "ran out of steam" in the late 90s, it was reborn as a single weekend event in Chicago. However, its original alt-rock focus is now largely gone, with the festival primarily featuring pop and hip-hop acts, reflecting a cultural shift in America. "When La La Palosa was first conceived in 1990, it was a full-blown touring festival of Weirdos, featuring the best in left-of-center and alternative bands." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZzIqIydZTlA
**Expanded Format (1992)** : For its second year in 1992, Lollapalooza expanded significantly, incorporating multiple stages and a wider array of attractions. "And this time things were expanded across multiple stages and multiple attractions." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4

**Festival for the Alternative Generation** : For several years in the 1990s, Lollapalooza was recognized as the premier touring music festival catering to the alternative generation. "And for the next couple of years, Lala Palusa was the touring music festival for the alternative generation." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**First Tour (1991)** : The first Lollapalooza tour, held in the summer of 1991, featured a diverse lineup including founding band Jane's Addiction, Siouxsie and the Banshees, Living Colour, Ice-T & Body Count, Butthole Surfers, Rollins Band, and Nine Inch Nails. "Along with founding band Jane's addiction, the line up included Suzy and the band sheets, living color, iced tea and body count, butthole surfers, Rawens band and some weirdo group that was never going to go anywhere called Nine-inch Nails." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2wZydttn4Cs
**Founded by Perry Farrell (1991)** : Lollapalooza was founded in 1991 by Perry Farrell, the singer for Jane's Addiction, as a multi-act bill to support what was intended to be Jane's Addiction's final tour. Its format was reminiscent of the old caravan tours from the 1950s. "Very fair, the singer for Jane's edition put together a multi-act bill to support what would be the last ever tour for Jane's edition." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Generation-Defining Event (1991-1995)** : Lollapalooza was a defining event for a generation during the 1991-1995 music era, which was characterized by a rapid influx of new sounds and scenes, including grunge, American indie, and gangster rap. "the entire Lola Paloose generation was gangster rap." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bI6TTA-fn7c
**Glory Years (1992-1995)** : The period from 1992 through 1995 is considered the "glory years" for Lollapalooza, as it perfectly represented the state of the alternative nation during that time. "1992 through 1995 were the glory years for Lala Palusa. Nothing, and I mean nothing better represented the state of the alternative nation during that time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Initial Purpose as Farewell Tour** : Lollapalooza was originally conceived as a Jane's Addiction farewell tour, which masqueraded as a music festival. This allowed the event to get off the ground, despite being seen by some as a semantic distinction. "The main tradition was winding down and preparing for a farewell tour. And Lala Palusa was conceived as a Jane's addiction farewell tour that masqueraded as a music festival." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Initial Success & Impact (1991)** : Despite many unsold tickets, the 1991 Lollapalooza tour was a "mod of success" because upwards of 20,000 people paid to see a lineup of "decidedly non-mainstream bands," convincing promoters to make it an annual event and indicating a shift in public taste towards alternative music. "But the fact that upwards of 20,000 people paid to see all these decidedly non-mainstream bands, convinced promoters to do it again, so they did." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2wZydttn4Cs
**Inspiration for Kevin Lyman** : Kevin Lyman, future founder of the Warped Tour, worked as a stage manager on the first Lollapalooza road trip in 1991. He was inspired by Jane's Addiction's performance of "Mountain Song" and observed both the strengths and weaknesses of Lollapalooza, particularly its booking of artists that were "too mainstream and not eclectic enough," before starting his own tour. "And it was while working with Jane's addiction as a stage manager on that first Lola Blues a road trip in 1991 that he started thinking of the new possibilities of package tours." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Introduction of Second Stage (1992)** : 1992 marked the first year that Lollapalooza included a second stage on the tour, featuring bands like Stone Temple Pilots, Cypress Hill, Rage Against the Machine, and Tool. "And that was also the first year of a second stage on the tour." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Lollapalooza Generation** : The "Lala Pulosa generation" refers to a group of music fans in the early 1990s, associated with the Lollapalooza festival, who were drawn to industrial music and hip hop in addition to grunge and punk revival sounds. "the Lala Pulosa generation. They got into industrial music and hip hop." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzyMSSlD7Yg

**Missed Timing in 1991** : The first Lollapalooza tour wrapped up on August 28, 1991, the day before Nirvana released "Smells Like Teen Spirit" and Pearl Jam released their debut album. This timing meant the festival missed the explosion of the alt-rock landscape that occurred a year later. "It wrapped up on August 28, 1991. **Name Origin** : The name "Lollapalooza" was chosen from old Three Stooges clips, which derived it from a late 19th-century slang word meaning "an extraordinary or unusual thing or event." "The name Lala Palusa was picked because it was a word that came up in old three stuages clips. **Nirvana's Refusal to Headline (Circa 1993-1994)** : Kurt Cobain's decision to turn down an offer to headline Lollapalooza created significant financial and interpersonal tension within Nirvana, as it would have generated an estimated $9 million, benefiting all band members. This refusal was a source of frustration for management and his bandmates, and it was anecdotally and unconfirmedly suggested to have angered "mysterious outside forces" who sought retribution, contributing to Kurt's paranoia. "What do you mean you don't want a headline, Lollapalooza? That would have been a fast $9 million and everyone in the band would have benefited." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1Jt2fUBDusk
