## curiosities
**Fatalities and Legal Outcomes** : The Ghost Ship fire resulted in 36 deaths. The master tenant of the building and his assistant were charged with involuntary manslaughter; one was acquitted, while the other was sentenced to 12 years in jail. Lawsuits led to a $33 million payout in one series of cases, with an electrical company settling another series out of court. "36 people died. The master tenet of the building and his assistant were charged with involuntary manslaughter." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pgchw2h87aw
**Origin, Spread, and Escalation of Fire** : The Ghost Ship fire likely originated from a faulty electrical appliance, possibly a fridge, or an overloaded circuit. It rapidly consumed flammable materials within the makeshift construction, creating a maze-like environment filled with thick, dark smoke that made escape nearly impossible. The main staircase from the second floor, constructed of wooden pallets, quickly burned, trapping people and leading to the second floor's collapse 22 minutes after the arrival of fire trucks. "It looks like it may have been a bad electrical appliance, maybe a fridge, maybe something else. Maybe it was just an overloaded electrical circuit." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pgchw2h87aw
**Warehouse Fire Tragedy (Oakland, California, December 2, 2016)** : Just before midnight on December 2, 2016, an old warehouse in Oakland, California, known as the Ghost Ship and illegally converted into artist lofts, caught fire during a concert. This industrial site, not zoned for residential or entertainment use, was a "death trap" due to inadequate safety measures, including illegal construction, piles of flammable trash, and a complete lack of sprinklers, alarms, or proper exit routes. "Just before midnight, on December 2, 2016, an old warehouse in Oakland, California that had been converted into a series of artists' lobstes went up in flames during a concert in the building." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pgchw2h87aw
