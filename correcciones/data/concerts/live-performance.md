# concert - Live Performance

## curiosities
**Band Chemistry as Literal Chemistry** : When musicians play together, they can literally get on the same wavelength, with their brains generating precise amounts of specific chemicals across no fewer than 12 distinct parts of the brain. This neurological synchronicity provides a literal basis for the common phrase "band chemistry," referring to a real mix of chemicals within their beings. "Everyone literally gets on the same wavelength. Their brains generating the correct chemicals and exactly the correct amounts using no fewer than 12 distinct parts of the brain." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mTbagQE7akQ
**Neurological Synchronization and Entrainment** : The transcendent feeling experienced at an amazing live show, where performers and audience seem entirely in sync, is a neurological response stemming from mirror neurons in the right hemisphere of the brain. These neurons facilitate synchronicity with the thoughts and actions of others, making music an excellent vehicle for this state. This process, known as "entrainment," fosters a collective bond that enhances individual enjoyment, with research even suggesting it's better for well-being than practicing yoga. "That's a neurological response generated by what's called mirror neurons in the right hemisphere of your brain. They allow us to get in sync with the thoughts and actions of others." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mTbagQE7akQ
**Post-Concert Hearing Impairment** : Exposure to very loud music for extended periods at concerts can result in a temporary muffled, "underwater" sensation in hearing. This is caused by powerful sound waves damaging the cilia, tiny hairs in the inner ears that convert acoustic energy into electrical impulses for the brain. It typically takes about three days for cilia to regenerate and hearing to return to normal, but repeated damage can permanently impair this regenerative ability, leading to irreversible hearing loss. "Too much volume destroys or impairs Cilia, resulting in an inability for them to do their job. It takes about three days for them to regenerate, which is when you're hearing returns." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mTbagQE7akQ
**Time Perception Warping** : During a live music performance, if the music is compelling, the brain can readjust and recalibrate temporal perception, causing minutes or even hours to pass in a flash. This phenomenon, where time seems to lose meaning, is attributed to music-induced chemicals in our brains literally distorting our perception of time. "If the music is just right, the brain will readjust and recalibrate temporal perception. This explains why time sometimes has no meaning when you're listening to music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mTbagQE7akQ
