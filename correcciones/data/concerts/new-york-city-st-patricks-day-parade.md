# concert - New York City St. Patrick's Day Parade

## curiosities
**U2's Dilemma in 1982** : In 1982, U2 was scheduled to march in the New York City St. Patrick's Day Parade as a promotional gimmick arranged by their manager, Paul McGinnis. However, the band learned that members of the Irish Republican Army (IRA) would also be participating. U2 strongly opposed the extension of Irish conflict to other countries and decided not to march, leading to a physical altercation between McGinnis and some IRA members when he tried to explain the band's position. This incident inspired U2's song "Sunday Bloody Sunday."
