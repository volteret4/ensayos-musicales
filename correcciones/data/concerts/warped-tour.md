# concert - Warped Tour

## curiosities
**All-Ages and Wide Reach** : The Warped Tour was an all-ages event, allowing its music to reach an incredibly broad audience as it traveled across the US, Canada, Europe, Australia, New Zealand, and Japan. "And because it was all ages, the music reached an incredibly wide audience as it touched down in places throughout the US, Canada, Europe, Australia, New Zealand, and Japan." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Anthony Carone's Performance with Il Scarlett** : Anthony Carone played on the Warped Tour with the band Il Scarlett for a couple of years. He was called by Tim Oxford to join Arkells while in the middle of this tour, leading him to learn Arkells' material on the road and audition directly after returning, still in his tour attire. "I went on Wharp tour and in the middle of that Wharp tour." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WSe0wdljSGA
**Anti-Moshing Signage (2014)** : The 2014 Warped Tour prominently displayed "no moshing" signs, featuring a stark warning: "You get hurt, we get sued, no more warp tour." This demonstrated a proactive measure by organizers to address liability concerns and ensure safety amidst the ongoing issues with moshing injuries. "Anyone who went to the 2014 warp tour would have seen no moshing signs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KMx4bmq8o2Y
**Boost for Kid Rock** : Kid Rock was picked up by the Warped Tour in August 1998, which helped build momentum for his album *Devil Without A Cause*, leading to millions in sales. "He was picked up by the Warped Tour, and from there things began to build." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5bQz8R0gbXc
**Disappearance (2010s)** : The disappearance of the Warped Tour, a festival that championed pop punk and emo, contributed to the decline of the genres in the 2010s. "or that the warp tour disappeared" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Disastrous American Tour** : Alexis on Fire's participation in the Warped Tour during the "Old Crows" era was described as a "broke event" and a "disaster," contributing to a period of unfulfilling American tours that took a toll on the band. "I remember like during that war, we're doing that full warp tour was, you got a broke event. It was a disaster doing that." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nEPT8ZVZ0so
**Efficient Touring Model** : The Warped Tour crisscrossed North America and occasionally ventured into Canada, operating as a super-efficient, self-contained show that offered a maximum amount of music for a low price, featuring dozens of bands at each stop. "Starting in 1995, it crisscrossed North America, occasionally veering up into Canada, with a super-efficient self-contained operation that offered maximum music for a low, low price." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Emo-Heavy Period** : The Warped Tour, a major music festival, dedicated a significant portion of its lineup to emo bands for a period, providing opportunities for these groups to tour alongside more established artists. "The Warped Tour went emo-heavy for a while, and there were chances to tour with more established artists." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v_am7ifh7Pk
**Extensive Lineup History** : The list of artists who participated in the Warped Tour over the years is staggering, with its own organizers not having a clear idea of everyone who has played due to its extensive history. "The line of history is so extensive that even the warp people don't have a clear idea of everyone who's played on the tour." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Founding Vision** : Founded by Kevin Lyman, the Warped Tour was conceived as a Lollapalooza-like traveling music festival with a strong emphasis on punk rock, though its focus evolved over its 27-year existence. "Founder Kevin Lyman wanted a Lola Paloosa-like traveling music festival that was heavily biased towards punk rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Genre Expansion** : While primarily focused on punk music, the Warped Tour eventually branched out to include different genres like hip-hop. "Warped was primarily about punk, but eventually it branched out into different genres like hip-hop." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Global Reach** : Blackfire, a punk and alternative rock band from the Arizona Navajo Nation, played on the Warped Tour, indicating the tour's global reach and inclusion of diverse artists. "They've played all over the world, including on the Warped Tour." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YFv6Ln00XjA
**Inception and Corporate Sponsorship** : The Warped Tour commenced in 1995. By 1996, it secured a corporate sponsor, Vans, a skate shoe manufacturer, leading to its commonly known name, the Vans Warped Tour. "A lot of credit has to go to the warp tour, which began in 1995. And then in 1996, it took on a corporate sponsor, Vance, the maker of skate shoes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Initial Name (1995)** : For its first year in 1995, the festival was simply known as the Warped Tour. "For year one, and this is 1995, it was just the Warped Tour." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Lollapalooza Inspiration** : The Warped Tour took the idea of Lollapalooza and expanded on it, starting in 1995. It became the longest-running North American traveling rock show. "The Warped Tour took the idea of Lala Palusa and ran with it. Starting in 1995, it crisscrossed North America, occasionally veering up into Canada, with a super-efficient self-contained operation that offered maximum music for a low, low price." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Longevity and Diverse Acts** : The Warped Tour had an impressive run of "24 summers," consistently showcasing "dozens and dozens and dozens of acts" each year. Early performers included Bad Religion, Dance Hall Crashers, Face to Face, Bucket nine, Less Than Jake, Mighty Mighty Bosstones, No Doubt, Sublime, and Goldfinger. "The warp tour ran for 24 summers, each featuring dozens and dozens and dozens of acts." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**MXPX Participation** : MXPX, a Christian punk band that began on Tooth and Nail Records, later toured with the Warped Tour after signing with a major label, showcasing their crossover success. "Although the band has since been signed to a major label and has moved on to touring with a warp tour and can be seen in a Pepsi commercial, MXPX was one of Tooth and Nail's first big victories." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g-tBn8dsSqw
**Premier Platform for Pop Punk and Ska Punk** : The tour embraced "the pop punk thing right from the beginning," and also heavily featured ska punk acts. It catered to the music that young audiences were "clamoring for," establishing itself as a key venue for these genres. "The warp tour, no wait, sorry, it's the Vance warp tour, got into the pop punk thing right from the beginning because that's what the kids were clamoring for. That and Scott punk, which can be viewed as a subset of pop punk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Promotion of Famous Stars and Straps Clothing** : The Warped Tour served as a significant venue for people to encounter and wear clothing from Travis Barker's Famous Stars and Straps brand. Its presence at the festival contributed to the brand's wide recognition among the target skate crowd and beyond. "And if you've ever been to the band's warp tour, chances are that you have seen people wearing Famous Stars and Straps stuff." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WIFxAaReLn0
**Punk-Centric Touring Festival (1995-1996)** : Created by skateboard fan Kevin Lyman and his partner Ray Woodbury, the Warped Tour was designed as a stripped-down, self-contained, punk-centric festival that could quickly move from city to city. It featured cheap tickets, encouraged underage attendance, and fostered a communal atmosphere where bands assisted each other. The first tour in 1995 featured around 20 bands (e.g., Sublime, No Doubt), doubling in size by the second year, and its impact on punk's renewed popularity was "incalculable." "the Warp Tour, created by a skateboard fan named Kevin Lyman and his partner Ray Woodbury, was designed as a stripped-down, self-contained, punk-centric festival that could quickly and nimbly move from city to city." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gkLMwrthZ9s
**Significant Impact on Bands** : Many bands benefited greatly from the exposure gained on the Warped Tour, with some acts, like Blink-182, appearing six times, including during periods when they were selling millions of records. "One band that benefited greatly from the Warped Tour exposure was these guys who appeared six different times." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Vans Sponsorship (1996 onwards)** : From 1996, the festival became the Vans Warped Tour, with Vans, a maker of skateboard shoes, as its sponsor. This partnership helped to solidify and promote the connection between melodic punk rock and skateboard culture. "But from 1996 on, it became the Vans Warped Tour, Vans being the maker of skateboard shoes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
