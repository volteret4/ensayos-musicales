# concert - Woodstock

## curiosities
**Advances in Festival Amplification (1969)** : Woodstock in 1969 is cited as a festival that benefited from, and contributed to, advances in sound system technology for large outdoor musical events. Building on earlier efforts at Monterey Pop, Woodstock pushed the boundaries of live sound, though the Grateful Dead's "Wall of Sound" a few years later would mark an even bigger breakthrough. "Some advances were made for festivals like Monterey Pop in 1967 and Woodstock in 1969." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
**Influence on Festival Culture** : Woodstock is cited as one of the seminal festivals, directly influenced by Monterey Pop, and serving as a predecessor to other major events like Glastonbury and Roskilde. "That led to Woodstock and Glastonbury and Ross Killed and a ton of others." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Location Misconception** : None of the Woodstock festivals, including the two in the 1990s, were actually held in Woodstock, New York. "Actually, none of the Woodstocks have ever been in Woodstock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Peace and Love Ideals (1969)** : The "good vibes of Woodstock" were seen as a symbol of the peace and love ideals of the 1960s, but these ideals were metaphorically "destroyed by the violence of Altamont," contributing to a sense of disillusionment in the early 1970s. "The good vibes of Woodstock were destroyed by the violence of Altamont." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4
