# instrument - Roland TR-808 Rhythm Composer

## curiosities
**Beastie Boys Usage** : The TR-808 is extensively featured on the first Beastie Boys album, where producer Rick Rubin famously "fattened up" its sound and laid it underneath tracks. "The 808 is all over the first Beastie Boys album. Producer Rick Rubin fattened up the sound and laid it underneath songs like this." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dablAKDOOV0
**Cultural Impact** : Its cultural significance is reflected in the band 808 State being named after it, and Kanye West's album *808s & Heartbreak* drawing its title from the machine. It has also been the subject of books and documentaries. "The band 808 State is named after it. Kanye West's 808 and heartbreak album named after the TR808." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dablAKDOOV0
**Distinctive "Weird" Sounds** : The TR-808 was renowned for its "weird" and unique synthetic sounds, particularly its booming bass drum, which was achieved by intentionally using faulty transistors in its circuitry. Its wonky, sizzly, spacey, and futuristic character, including its unrealistic cowbell and handclap sounds, contributed to its distinct appeal. "What set these sounds apart was that they were really kind of weird, especially the bass drum, which could be set to really, really boom. That in part was created by using faulty transistors on purpose within the circuitry." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dablAKDOOV0
**Foundational Sound of Genres** : The TR-808 became a foundational sound for hip-hop, post-disco dance music, British rave, techno house, and acid pop, and has been famously likened to the "Stratocaster of Hip-hop and Dance Music." "It was one of the foundational sounds of hip-hop and any kind of post-disco dance music. British rave, techno house, acid pop." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dablAKDOOV0
**Full Song Programmability** : Owners of second-hand TR-808s discovered it was the first drum machine capable of being programmed to carry an entire song, complete with rolls and fills, a feature critical for its adoption in emerging genres. "The owners of these second-hand machines realized that the 808 was the first drum machine that could be programmed to carry through an entire song completely with rolls and fills." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dablAKDOOV0
**Initial Commercial Failure & Resurgence** : Initially a commercial flop upon its 1980 release, priced over $1200, the TR-808 was often traded or sold off by early adopters who preferred Linn machines. Its subsequent availability for $100-$200 made it accessible to young artists in hip-hop, dance, and electronic music, where it achieved cult status and became used on more hit records than any other drum machine. "The TR808 was initially a commercial flop. Early adopters who bought a unit didn't think it was nearly as good as a Lynn machine, so they traded them or sold them off. That made them accessible to other artists, most notably young people, getting into hip-hop, dance music and electronic music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dablAKDOOV0
**Synthetic Sounds, Not Samples (1980)** : The programmable Roland TR-808 Rhythm Composer, released in 1980, generated its sounds synthetically rather than using samples, with "TR" standing for Transistor Rhythm. "Like the Lynn, it was programmable, but it generated synthetic sounds. It did not use samples. The TR actually stands for Transistor Rhythm." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dablAKDOOV0
