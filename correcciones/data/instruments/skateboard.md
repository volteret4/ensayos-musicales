## curiosities
**"Death Traps" (1963)** : By 1963, despite dedicated shops, early skateboards were considered "death traps" due to rudimentary construction, poor design of decks, wheels, and trucks, leading to thousands of hospitalizations each year. "And they were death traps. Construction was very rudimentary and we still had a long, long way to go with the design of the deck and especially the wheels and trucks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Bill Richard's Innovation** : A surf shop owner named Bill Richard in Los Angeles made a deal with a Chicago roller skate company to produce special wheels for skateboards, which were initially attached to carved wood decks. "We do know that a surf shop in Los Angeles run by a guy named Bill Richard made a deal with the Chicago roller skate company to produce a special set of wheels that could be attached to the bottom of a deck." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Dry Swimming Pools as Bowls (1976)** : A severe drought in California in 1976 led to many swimming pools remaining dry, which were subsequently used by skaters as "bowls," providing new surfaces that facilitated new tricks and advanced freestyle skateboarding. "California suffered from a terrible drought causing a lot of swimming pools to remain dry. And a lot of those pools became bowls for skaters." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Early Design (Late 1940s/Early 1950s)** : The very earliest skateboards were rudimentary, consisting of nothing more than a plank of wood with roller skate wheels bolted to the bottom. "The very earliest skateboards were nothing more than a plank of wood with some roller skate wheels bolted to the bottom." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Global Reach and Barefoot Designs** : Skateboarding has seen a global explosion in popularity, particularly in Africa and South America, where many boards are specifically designed to be ridden barefoot. "Many of the boards used there are designed to be written barefoot." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Resurrection and Material Improvements (1975)** : The resurrection of skateboarding around 1975 brought significant design improvements. Steel roller skate wheels were replaced with polyurethane wheels, offering much better traction and fewer wipeouts. Decks, previously only wood, became available in aluminum, fiberglass, and various composites. "No more steel roller skate wheels. The new wheels were made of polyurethane and offered much bit of traction leading to many fewer wipeouts." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
