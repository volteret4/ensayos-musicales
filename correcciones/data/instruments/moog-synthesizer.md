# instrument - Moog Synthesizer

## curiosities
**BBC Demonstration of Waveforms** : Wendy Carlos provided a detailed demonstration of the Moog synthesizer for the BBC, explaining how various sounds are generated from basic waveforms. She illustrated sine, sawtooth, triangular, pulse, and square waves, emphasizing the creative process of "building every sound" from these simple elements. "Let me start and show you how we get some of these sounds." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
**Early Design Collaboration with Wendy Carlos** : Bob Moog worked closely with Wendy Carlos during the early development of his synthesizers, benefiting from her musician's perspective. Carlos's input was critical in shaping design elements such as the inclusion of a keyboard and the tactile feel of individual keys, making the instrument more appealing and intuitive for musicians. "She worked carefully with Bob Mogue, offering a musician's perspective, and how his new electronic instruments should look and feel." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
**Used for "Switched-On Bach" Album** : The Moog synthesizer was the sole instrument used by Wendy Carlos for her landmark 1968 album "Switched-On Bach." This giant modular unit, supplied by Bob Moog, demonstrated the synthesizer's capability for complex classical arrangements, despite the laborious process of programming each sound one note at a time using switches, knobs, and patch cords. "The result was an album entitled Switched On Bach, 10 pieces by Johann Sebastian Bach, and everything was played on a giant modular synthesizer unit supplied by Bob Mogue." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
