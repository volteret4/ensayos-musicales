# instrument - Reverb

## curiosities
**1930s Development** : The first reverb effects were developed in the 1930s, primarily by American producer Bill Putnam. "The first reverb effects were developed in the 1930s, largely by an American producer named Bill Putnam." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pOeChe_D1Ag
**Dreamy, Twangy Guitar Effect** : Reverb, a fast echo introduced in the recording studio, can make guitar sounds even more dreamy and twangy. "It could be made even more dreamy and twangy by adding reverb, a fast echo introduced in the recording studio." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pOeChe_D1Ag
**Live Sound Enhancement** : Reverb was extensively used to enliven many early rock and roll recordings, particularly on vocals. "Reverb was used to live up a lot of early rock and roll recordings, especially on the vocals." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pOeChe_D1Ag
**Purpose in Music** : Reverb enhances sound, making music sound better. Ancient Greeks created amphitheaters for slight delays, and great concert halls were praised for their natural reverb. Reverb adds life to otherwise dry recordings. "Now why would you want reverb on a recording? Because music can sound better with it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pOeChe_D1Ag
