# instrument - Phonograph

## curiosities
**Edison's Cylinder-Based System** : The phonograph, invented by Thomas Edison, was the first device capable of recording sound. However, it utilized rotating cylinders, which were eventually superseded by Emil Berliner's flat disk gramophone due to limitations such as shorter lifespan (worn out after about 160 plays) and single-sided capacity. "Thomas Edison may have invented how to record sound with his phonograph, but he used rotating cylinders." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VlIErdSSVOM
**Invention (1877)** : The phonograph was unveiled by Thomas Edison in 1877, representing a significant early step in the history of recorded sound. Its invention spurred other inventors, such as Oberlin Smith, to explore new methods of sound recording. "After seeing his first phonograph in 1878, the year after Thomas Edison unveiled the thing, Oberlin became fascinated with the idea of recorded sound." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V7WjNR1ujhk
**Mechanical Tracking System** : Edison's phonograph system utilized a mechanical format where the needle tracked in the grooves with an up and down motion. This was less durable and skipped more often than Berliner's side-to-side tracking system for gramophones. Edison eventually abandoned cylinders for his own incompatible disk format in 1913, but was out of the record business by the end of the 1920s. "You see, the needle on an Edison record tracked in the grooves with an up and down motion." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VlIErdSSVOM
**Spreading "Dirty Lyrics" (c. 1900)** : Around 1900, with the rise of the phonograph and gramophone, people quickly began recording songs with "dirty lyrics." This new technology facilitated the widespread dissemination of such content, leading to considerable public outrage about the perceived foulness music was bringing. "When the gramophone and phonograph first took off around 1900, it didn't long for people to start recording songs with dirty lyrics, and there was plenty of outrage." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L0j9iDuHQVA
