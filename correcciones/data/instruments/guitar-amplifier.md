# instrument - Guitar Amplifier

## curiosities
**Completing the Electric Guitar** : Guitar amplifiers are considered an essential component for electric guitars, as they make the instruments complete. Without proper amplification, the electric guitar's capabilities and sound cannot be fully realized. "When we come back, we'll segue to the thing that makes electric guitars complete, and that is the guitar amplifier." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Enduring Rock Legacy (Decades)** : Over many decades, Jim Marshall's amplifiers have become synonymous with the loud and powerful sound of rock music. They are extensively used by hundreds of musicians, both celebrated and emerging, including iconic figures like Slash, Oasis, Deftones, Rob Zombie, Paul Weller, Sum 41, and Pop Evil, solidifying their status as influential and indispensable gear. "Over the decades, Jim's amplifiers became synonymous with loud, powerful rock and today they used by slash, oasis, the death tones, rob zombie, Paul Weller, some 41, pop erosion, hundreds of others, famous and not so famous." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
**Meeting Demand for Louder Sound (Early 1960s)** : In the early 1960s, Jim Marshall, then a drum teacher running a music store in London, observed a growing need among customers for guitar amplifiers that could produce sufficient volume to be heard over increasingly larger and louder rock and roll audiences. Responding to this unmet demand, he began building custom amplifiers. "In the early 1960s, customers kept coming into the store looking for guitar amplifiers that could be heard over larger and noisier rock and roll audiences." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
**Modular Design for Performance (Unspecified Year)** : Marshall's amplifiers featured a modular design where the internal circuitry and controls were housed in one box (the head), and the speakers were contained in a separate cabinet. This innovative approach allowed musicians to stack multiple cabinets on stage, enhancing both the volume and the imposing visual aesthetic of their live performances. "The amplifiers were modular too. The guts of the amp and all the controls were housed in one box and all the speakers were in another cabinet." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
**Signature Thick Sound and Sustain (Unspecified Year)** : Jim Marshall's custom-built amplifiers became renowned for their distinctive "thicker sound," which allowed guitar chords to sustain for remarkably long durations. This unique sonic characteristic was a key factor in their appeal and contributed significantly to their widespread adoption in rock music. "Not only were his amplifiers loud, but they had a thicker sound that could sustain a chord for a long, long time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
