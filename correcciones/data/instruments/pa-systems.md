# instrument - PA systems

## curiosities
**Early Use for Speech (1915-1925)** : Public Address (PA) systems were initially developed and refined in the early 20th century, notably demonstrated by the Magnavox system in San Francisco in 1915 and used for King George V's speech at the British Empire Exhibition in 1925. These early systems were effective for amplifying voice in large venues, but their limitations for musical performance became apparent with the rise of rock and roll. "It was also used for audio recording and for public address systems." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
**Inadequacy for Rock and Roll** : With the advent of rock and roll, existing PA systems designed primarily for speech proved insufficient for musical amplification. The Beatles' 1965 concert at Shakespeare Stadium, where their 700-watt PA system was drowned out by the 135-decibel crowd, highlighted this deficiency and spurred the demand for more powerful and specialized PA systems for music.
