# instrument - Silvertone Guitars

## curiosities
**Built-in Amplifier Feature** : A notable cost-saving and convenient feature of Silvertone guitars was that each came with a built-in amplifier integrated into its carrying case. This allowed purchasers to immediately play their new guitar, making them popular gifts. "And one of the cost-saving things about the Silvertones is that each guitar case came with a built-in amp. So if you got one under the Christmas tree, you could fire it up right away." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fQCOj3_zy54
**Playability and Collectibility** : With typically only 18 frets on the neck, Silvertone guitars were lightweight and easy for young or beginner players to handle. While often entry-level, they were also used by renowned musicians like Jimi Hendrix and Bob Dylan. Today, vintage Silvertone models, particularly those from the 1960s, are highly sought after by collectors. "And because the neck had only 18 frets, a typical Silvertone guitar was light and easy for a young person to play. But they weren't just four beginners. Jimmy Hendrix had one, so did Bob Dylan. And today, vintage Silvertones are highly collectible, especially those from the 1960s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fQCOj3_zy54
**Sears Department Store Brand** : Silvertone guitars were a prominent department store brand, exclusively sold by Sears, often through their catalog. These instruments were manufactured by a variety of companies for Sears, including Harmony, K, Danclectro, Velco, and Tesco.
