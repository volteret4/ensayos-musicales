# instrument - Amplifiers

## curiosities
**100 Watt Vox Amplifiers (1965)** : During The Beatles' Shea Stadium concert in August 1965, the band utilized a couple of "100 WODVox amplifiers" as part of their attempt to project sound through the stadium's PA system. In a last-minute effort to improve monitoring, some of these amps were flipped to face the band. "using a bunch of mics and a couple of 100 WODVox amplifiers, they pumped everything through the stadium's public address system." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qrrwqdpCjpQ
**Early Primitive Units (1920s)** : Amplifiers first appeared in the 1920s but were "primitive things that really didn't have a lot of power." Initially, their use was "reserved mostly for vocalists" to assist with projecting voices in larger spaces. "Amplifiers started coming along in the 1920s, but there were primitive things that really didn't have a lot of power." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qrrwqdpCjpQ
**Improved and Cheaper (1980s)** : By the 1980s, amplifiers had become significantly "better and cheaper." This improvement was a key factor in a major shift in concert sound, enabling the widespread adoption of speaker systems hung above the audience, which freed up stage space and enhanced sound quality. "Amplifiers got better and cheaper." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qrrwqdpCjpQ
**Introduction with Electrical Recording (1925)** : Amplifiers were introduced as part of the new electrical recording technology in 1925, working in conjunction with microphones. This advancement was critical for capturing and enhancing sound beyond the limitations of purely acoustic methods. "This involved the use of these newfangled listening sticks called microphones and also things called amplifiers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L0j9iDuHQVA
**Theft from Bowie (July 1973)** : Amplifiers were among the equipment stolen from David Bowie at the Hammersmith Odeon in London in July 1973 by Steve Jones, who later used them with the Sex Pistols. "made off with a bunch of microphones and amplifiers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw
