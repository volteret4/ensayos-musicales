# instrument - The Log (Les Paul)

## curiosities
**Prototype for Solid-Body Design** : "The log" was an experimental electric guitar developed by Les Paul in 1939. It was constructed from a 4x4 piece of pine, with attached wings to mimic a Spanish guitar, a standard guitar neck, a bridge, and two crude homemade pickups. Its solid body was revolutionary at the time, departing from hollow designs. "The result was something he called the log. It was a 4x4 piece of pine with two wings attached to it to make it look like a Spanish guitar. He attached a regular guitar neck to that body, along with a bridge and two crude homemade pickups. Unlike all the other electric guitars in the market, this one wasn't hollow. Its body was solid." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=E6cXyaHALBQ
