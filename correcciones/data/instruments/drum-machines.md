# instrument - Drum machines

## curiosities
**Alternative Dance Basis** : Drum machines were a fundamental component of Alternative Dance, used extensively alongside keyboards, loops, and samples to create the genre's electronic foundation. "Lots of keyboards and drum machines and loops and samples." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Evolution from Novelty to Standard** : Drum machines, along with effects pedals, Marshall stacks, and synthesizers, introduced novel sounds to music production. Over time, they have become "part of the standard arsenal of musical tools," signifying their ubiquitous presence in contemporary music. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m7MbV-eCLyE
**Evolving Understanding of Drums** : Initially, for Trent Reznor, a "drum was a button on a machine" for early Nine Inch Nails recordings. However, after touring for a couple of years with "real drummers," he developed a deeper understanding and appreciation for the sound of actual drums, leading him to incorporate them more in later works like the "Broken" EP. "To me, drum was a button on a machine, but after touring for a couple years with real drummers, I understood, wow, it doesn't sound like that button, and I wanted to incorporate some of the sounds." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M3nUQtja0qc
**Integration in New Wave Music** : Drum machines were utilized by bassists like Peter Hook in New Order for programming bass lines, contributing to the band's distinctive sound and making it "much more than the sum of its parts." "Later, his work in programming bass lines and drum machines with new order drummer Stephen Morris made that band into something much more than the sum of its parts." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kbFfT0pKchI
**Trent Reznor's Early Fascination** : Drum machines, particularly as used by bands like The Human League, OMD, and Depeche Mode, deeply inspired Trent Reznor's musical journey. He regarded their sound as "really cool," a significant factor in his development as a musician. "But the synthesizers and drum machines of the human league and OMD and Depeche mode. Now that was really cool." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M3nUQtja0qc
