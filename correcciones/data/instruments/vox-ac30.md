# instrument - Vox AC30

## curiosities
**30-Watt Output and 1959 Introduction** : The Vox AC30 is a 30-watt amplifier model first introduced in 1959. Its release marked a significant departure from the predominantly American-made amplifiers used by most electric guitarists at the time, which had resulted in a more uniform guitar tone across the industry. "The most important model was the 30-watt Vox AC30, first introduced in 1959. Until Vox came along, the majority of electric guitars were using amps built by Fender and a few other small American manufacturers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fQCOj3_zy54
**Defining Sound of British Invasion** : The Vox AC30 became synonymous with the sound of the British Invasion bands of the mid-1960s and beyond, influencing a generation of musicians. Its characteristic tone was sought after by numerous groups, cementing its legacy in rock history. "And the Vox sound became the sound for pretty much all British invasion bands of the mid-1960s and beyond." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fQCOj3_zy54
**Vacuum Tubes and Top Boost Circuit** : The AC30's distinctive bright, jangly sound was achieved through its use of vacuum tubes and a unique amplification circuitry, which included a "second treble stage" specifically known as the "top boost circuit." This innovative design set it apart from other amplifiers on the market. "First it used vacuum tubes. Secondly, its amplification circuitry was different, employing a second treble stage, something called the top boost circuit. And the result was a brighter, jangly sort of sound." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fQCOj3_zy54
