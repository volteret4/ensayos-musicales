**Ancient Origins and Female Use** : The frame drum, resembling a large tambourine with skin stretched over a shallow circular frame, is the oldest known instrument. Archeological evidence from a shrine in Turkey dating back 9300 years shows a human figure, identified as a woman and likely a priestess, playing a frame drum. This indicates women's ancient role in drumming, particularly in ritual and religious ceremonies. "The oldest known instrument is something known as a frame drum. It's like a big tambourine, some kind of skin stretched over a shallow circular frame and a tell in one hand and then struck by the other, you know, with a stick, maybe your fingers, maybe the palm of your hand, whatever." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9GHpC0kCgHY
