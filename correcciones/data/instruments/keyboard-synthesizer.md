# instrument - Keyboard Synthesizer

## curiosities
**Democratizing Music Production** : The ease of use, with simple knob twiddling yielding brilliant sounds, meant musicians didn't need to be highly skilled. This made the technology accessible and versatile, serving as a "great equalizer" that allowed professional-sounding music to be made even in a bedroom by the late 70s. "You know, you twiddle a couple of knobs on these new machines and you sounded brilliant. It was easy to be awesome. You didn't have to be pink Floyd, some huge prog rock band to access this technology. ... And by the late 70s it was possible to make a full professional sounding song in your bedroom. ... And synthesizers became a great equalizer for a great number of one of the musicians." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KF5MwJVYng
**Development and Affordability** : These instruments, in development since the early 1940s, became significantly cheaper by the late 1970s. This made them accessible to a wider audience, moving beyond traditional studios or university labs. "At around this time, keyboard synthesizers, inventions that had been in development since the early 1940s, started to fall in price. Instead of being confined to a studio or a lab on a university campus somewhere, it was possible to pick up a reasonably powerful keyboard for a reasonable price." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KF5MwJVYng
**Rare Keyboard Synthesizer in Timbaland's Studio** : A rare keyboard synthesizer was spotted in Timbaland's personal recording studio during a November 2006 MTV documentary. This specific piece of equipment was capable of playing the same music file as a previously posted online cover of Jan Tempest Suni's "Asad Jazzed Evening," which heightened suspicions of plagiarism. "A shot of his personal recording studio reveals a piece of equipment, a rare keyboard synthesizer that is capable of playing the same music file as the cover posted by the Norwegian dude." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wYD_dIvYY5o
**Unprecedented Sound Capabilities** : Synthesizers were capable of generating entirely new and unimagined noises, including unique square waveforms, which made their sounds artificial and distinct from the curvy or spiky waveforms of traditional instruments. "These synthesizers could not only make noises that had never been heard before, but they made sounds that no one had ever even imagined before. And with a little experimenting, you could build a kind of music that was utterly unique. ... See up until this point, all musical instruments generated sounds that could be represented with curvy or spiky waveforms. synthesizers could generate sounds with square waveforms. This made them sound artificial and unreal." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KF5MwJVYng
**Vintage Revival and Value** : By the late 2000s, the price of vintage analog synthesizers had dramatically increased. These older machines produce unique sounds unattainable from modern digital ones, offering a warmth and depth despite requiring more frequent tuning. "Meanwhile, the price of vintage synthesizers has skyrocketed. These old analog machines have sounds that you just can't get from modern digital ones. ... But it makes the sounds much more depth to it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KF5MwJVYng
