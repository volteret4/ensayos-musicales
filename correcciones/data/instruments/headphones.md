## curiosities
**Baldwin's US Navy Invention** : Nathaniel Baldwin, an electrician in northern Utah, invented a superior type of telephone receiver while working on a hydroelectric dam. The US Navy, which used earpieces that were "not very good" for radio operators, placed a large order for Baldwin's invention, which he assembled on his kitchen table, effectively becoming the first significant client for his headphones. "When the Navy tested Baldwin's invention, they put in a big order for his headphones, which he assembled himself on his own kitchen table." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
**Breakthrough in Sony Soundabout (1979)** : The most significant innovation in the original Sony Soundabout (later renamed Walkman) was not its miniature cassette mechanism or tiny amplifier, but rather its "tiny little things that sounded pretty damn good" headphones, which represented a major technological breakthrough. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9T9wi0vEvc
**Evolution of Headphone Types** : Over several decades, headphones have evolved into distinct categories. Circum-aural headphones, the original stereo type, feature large pads that fully enclose the ears. Supra-aural headphones have pads that rest directly on top of the ears. Earbuds, a more recent development, are designed to be worn inside the ear canal. "The first and original stereo headphones are called Circum Oral. That means the ears fit inside two big pads. Super oral headphones have pads that fit against the ears. They just kind of ride on top of your ears. And then we have earbuds, which are worn inside the ear canal." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8BZMo41DjlY
**Facilitating Personal Music Experience** : The combination of the Walkman's portability and its tiny headphones enabled individuals to experience music in a more personal and private manner, fundamentally changing how people engaged with their audio content. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9T9wi0vEvc
**Headphone Technologies** : A diverse range of technologies are employed in modern headphones to produce sound. These include moving coil, electrostatic, electric, planar magnetic, and thermoacoustic principles, among others. Depending on a user's budget, it is possible to achieve "exquisitely good" sound quality with current headphone offerings. "There are a variety of technologies used by headphones, moving coil, electrostatic, electric, planar magnetic, thermochoustic, and a few others." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8BZMo41DjlY
**Integral to Personal Portable Music (1972)** : Headphones were an essential component of Andreas Pavel's 1972 invention, the Stereo Belt. This device was a small cassette player specifically designed to deliver music directly to the listener via headphones, marking a pivotal step towards widespread personal and portable music consumption. "It was a small cassette player that pumped music through headphones." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
**Invention by John Koss (1959)** : In the mid-1950s, John Koss, while trying to promote his portable record player, engineered a set of headphones as an afterthought to demonstrate the device privately. Although his record player failed, the headphones proved to be a "brilliant idea." In 1959, the first Koss headphones were introduced, initiating the widespread "headphone craze." `"As an afterthoughts, a way to demonstrate the device, he engineered a set of headphones so people could listen privately. His record player was a bust, but the headphones? That was a brilliant idea, and in 1959, the first ever Koss headphones were introduced, and that was the beginning of the headphone craze."` ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OuFvfZJKcpc
**Koss SP3s (1958)** : The world's first stereo headphones, invented in 1958 by jazz musician John Koss and engineer Martin Lang Jr. Originally conceived as an accessory for a portable turntable, the SP3s quickly became the star attraction at audio shows, revolutionizing personal listening by introducing stereo sound directly to the ears. "These first headphones were the Koss SP3s, and frankly they weren't supposed to be that big of a deal. They were simply an accessory for a portable turntable, but whenever one saw and heard them at an audio show, all people wanted were the headphones." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8BZMo41DjlY
**Koss SP3s – First Consumer Model (1958)** : John Koss, after developing a portable record player with a privacy switch that redirected audio to headphones, realized the potential for standalone consumer headphones. In 1958, he introduced the Koss SP3s, the first consumer-grade headphones specifically designed for private listening. This product was a "huge hit" and marked the beginning of widespread headphone adoption. "And so in 1958, the first standalone consumer grade headphones, the Koss SP3s, were introduced." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
**Lack of Patent and Industrial Use** : Nathaniel Baldwin never patented his headphone invention, which contributed to his eventual bankruptcy in 1924 and subsequent fraud conviction. Consequently, the technology was picked up by others but found limited success beyond industrial applications, such as for telephone operators, radio stations, and the military, for several decades. "Nathaniel Baldwin never patented his headphone invention." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
**Noise-Canceling Headphones Origin (1978)** : The concept for noise-canceling headphones emerged in 1978 when Amar Bose, the founder of Bose speakers, was dissatisfied with the headphones provided to him on an airline flight. Before becoming available to the public, this technology was first adopted by the Air Force, Army, and aviation professionals. "The idea for noise-canceling headphones originated in 1978 when Amar Bose, the guy behind Bose speakers, hated the headphones and airline provided him on a flight. **Stereo Experience Debate** : A debate exists regarding whether headphones and earbuds truly provide a proper stereo experience comparable to listening through external speakers. Unlike speakers, where the brain can analyze incoming audio to construct a spatial soundstage with height, depth, and distance, headphones deliver audio discretely and separately to each ear. This can lead to a diminished or absent perceived center of the stereo image, instruments appearing in only one ear, or sounds seemingly coming from behind, as the brain struggles to spatially locate the audio. "There is a debate, however, with all headphones and all earbuds. Do they offer a proper stereo experience?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8BZMo41DjlY
