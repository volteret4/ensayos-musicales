# instrument - Fairlight CMI

## curiosities
**Expensive Keyboard and Sampling Device** : The Fairlight was an "hideously expensive" keyboard and sampling device during its time. Despite its limitations compared to modern technology, it offered revolutionary sound capabilities. "This was a hideously expensive keyboard and sampling device." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjTWcy8RHEs
**First Sampling Machine** : The Fairlight was an Australian machine, recognized as the first sampling machine. "It was the first sampling machine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mjQBaX_3oL4
**High Cost** : The Fairlight CMI was extremely expensive; the one at Tapestry Studios cost 60,000 pounds, translating to almost twice that in dollars. "The one that we had at Tapestry Studio. It was cost 60,000 pounds." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mjQBaX_3oL4
**High Cost for Limited Functionality** : The Fairlight cost over $30,000, yet its capabilities were "less than what you can do with an iPhone today." This high price point made it an exclusive piece of equipment. "It could do less than what you can do with an iPhone today, but it cost over $30,000." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjTWcy8RHEs
**Limited Memory and Monophonic** : The Fairlight was monophonic, meaning it could only handle one sound at a time, and had very limited memory, possibly around 20K. Samples were stored on large floppy disks. "It was monophonic, sampler. And I think it had, what, 20K of memory?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mjQBaX_3oL4
**Music Composition Language (MCL)** : Before MIDI, the Fairlight used Music Composition Language, requiring users to write complex code to define sounds, their duration, and placement in a musical sequence. For drum programming, specific numbers in brackets would dictate where a kick drum event, for example, would occur within a bar divided into 48 segments. "in the early days, it was called music composition language. So you had to... There's a mono machine. So you can only get one sound in it at a time. You had to write a whole code for... To identify the sound and how long sound would play and where it played." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mjQBaX_3oL4
**Studio Adoption and Unique Sounds** : Despite its prohibitive cost, approximately 300 studios were persuaded to invest in Fairlight CMIs. The device produced sounds that were "unlike anything anyone had ever heard," making it a sought-after tool for creating innovative music in the early 1980s. "That 300 studios were persuaded to invest in them, and the sounds they got were unlike anything anyone had ever heard." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XjTWcy8RHEs
