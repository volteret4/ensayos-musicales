**End of Collaboration with The Rolling Stones (1967)** : Andrew Loog Oldham, who had been an instrumental producer in establishing The Rolling Stones' importance, officially ceased his collaboration with the band in late 1967. This decision was a rapid response to the commercial and critical setbacks the band experienced earlier that year. **Uncomfortable Period for The Rolling Stones (1967-1968)** : The Rolling Stones experienced a phase where they felt uncomfortable engaging with psychedelic music. This genre, combined with attempts at being overly commercial, led them to lose their essence, a situation from which they recovered with the success of "Jumpin' Jack Flash."
