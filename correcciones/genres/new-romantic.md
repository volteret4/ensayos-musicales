# genre - New Romantic

## curiosities
**Synth-Based Offshoot of Technopop** : New Romantic was a specific offshoot of synth-based music, originating in British clubs like Billies and The Blitz Club. This movement was instrumental in breeding groups such as Spandau Ballet, Visage, and Culture Club, characterized by their unique style and electronic sounds. "One such offshoot was known as New Romantic, which had its roots in a couple of British clubs dedicated to new music. One was called Billies, and the other was the Blitz Club. This was the breeding site of groups like Spandovalet, Visage, and Culture Club." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
**David Bowie's Influence (1980)** : The New Romantic movement of the early 1980s was significantly jumpstarted by David Bowie's surreal yet fashionable clown character in his groundbreaking 1980 music video for a song from his "Scary Monsters" album. This visual style, featuring elements like extras from the Blitz Club, became highly influential. "Bowie's surreal but very fashionable clown character helped jumpstart the whole new romantic movement of the early 1980s." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The Rise and Fall and Future of the Music Video - Part 1 | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lqd6IbUJ7tg&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG ← new-romantic
**Descendant of Glam** : The New Romantic movement is identified as a direct descendant of glam, particularly in its emphasis on wild, flamboyant, and eccentric fashion being as crucial as the music. It formed a clear lineage from David Bowie and Roxy Music of the 1970s to the music of the early 1980s, enabling bands like Duran Duran, Japan, Ultravox, Adam Ant, Spandau Ballet, Visage, Soft Cell, and The Human League. "the first obvious descendants of Glam was the New Romantic movement. This soundtrack to the era where wild, flamboyant and eccentric fashion was just as important as the music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw ← new-romantic
