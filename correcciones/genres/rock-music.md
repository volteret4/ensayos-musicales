This highlights the arbitrary and often debated nature of genre classification. "In which section would I find band X? Why? Why was Nine-inch nails under electronics instead of industrial? **Early Segmentation and Stratification (1960s)** : By the time rock music celebrated its 10th birthday in the mid-1960s, it had already begun to segment and stratify. This led to a division between "acceptable mainstream stuff" and music that was either not ready for or not interested in prime time, giving birth to new musical subcultures. "See, even as rock celebrated its 10th birthday, it had began to segment and stratify." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6oUkMOkEr9U

**Indie Revolution of the 2000s** : The early 21st century saw an "indie revolution" where a new generation of musicians began releasing stripped-down rock through independent labels on both sides of the Atlantic. This movement was propelled by the financial crisis of major labels, the rise of illegal file sharing, the drop in CD sales, and millennials' desire for more authentic music, along with their internet-savvy ability to discover it. "By the end of the 90s, rock music was deep in the doldrums. **Mainstream Decay and New Generation Revival** : A recurring truth in rock's history is that the mainstream often deteriorates to such an extent that a new generation takes it upon themselves to "fix it." This involves returning to basics, stripping down the music, and starting anew, a phenomenon observed at the close of the 1990s and the dawn of the 2000s. "My feeling is that music is the greatest parameter of sociological conditions. The general upheaval of society in the 1960s has passed when acid rock and electric rock were peaking. **Resilience Against "Death" Declarations** : Despite numerous predictions from pundits that rock music and its associated instruments were obsolete, the electric guitar, as a primary tool for rock, has consistently demonstrated remarkable adaptability. It has successfully withstood various challenges, particularly when wielded by innovative and unconventional musicians. "And while there have been many occasions where pundits have declared that rock and by extension the tools to make this music dead, the electric guitar has proven to be extremely adaptable and has so far been able to take on all comers, especially when placed in the hands of radicals and rule breakers." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The Story of the Electric Guitar - Part 3 | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fQCOj3_zy54&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG

"That genre that we call rock music in the 60s and into the early 70s did take on a less aggressive sound.
