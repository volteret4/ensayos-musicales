# genre - Surf music

## curiosities
**Broad Influence and Legacy** : Surf music is a significant genre with an influence and legacy that extends far beyond its beach-related origins. It can present in different forms, including punky or hardcore variations, and is associated with activities like skateboarding and snowboarding. The genre encompasses themes of guitars, amps, pedals, cars, girls, beer, and parties, and while it can feature vocals, it is often best experienced as instrumentals. "It's important and its influence and legacy goes way beyond the beach." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pOeChe_D1Ag
**Building Block of Rock** : Surf music is considered one of the foundational building blocks of punk rock, amplifier design, and recording studio innovations. "In fact, surf music could be considered one of the building blocks of punk rock, amplifier design and innovations in the recording studio." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pOeChe_D1Ag
**Persistence Through Decline** : After a decline in the mid-1960s due to the rise of new rock genres like the British Invasion and psychedelic rock, surf music did not die out but continued to return and appear in interesting and important places over subsequent decades. "Surf music went into such a decline that even the Beach Boys started to diversify. But Surf did not die. It would return again and again over the coming decades." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pOeChe_D1Ag
**Resurgence in Punk and New Wave** : The "surf sound" or "surf feel" reappeared during the punk and new wave movements of the 1970s, indicating its infectious and fun nature. "It was just too much fun to play, which is one of the reasons the surf sound, or at least the surf feel, showed up during the punk and new wave explosions of the 1970s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pOeChe_D1Ag
**Worldwide Contemporary Scene** : Contemporary surf music is a global phenomenon, with traditional and innovative groups found worldwide, including in Denmark, London, Japan, and Toronto, demonstrating its enduring appeal and adaptability. "Although it's been around since the 1950s, surf music is still very much with us. Some is still pretty traditional and sticks to the original rules of arrangement and performance. But then there are other groups who have done their best to advance the art of surf rock or bands that abuse surf as a big influence. And it's worldwide too." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pOeChe_D1Ag
