**Critic's Reaction to Crooning** : Crooning, a new style of singing that emerged with the advent of microphones and amplification, was vehemently criticized by purists and "old folks." Critics described it as a "degenerate form of singing," with terms like "winers and bleeders defiling the air." One critic stated that "No true American would practice this bass art. They are riballed and revolting to true men," while another referred to a "soft foul, crune-reobsessed Britain."
