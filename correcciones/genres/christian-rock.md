# genre - Christian Rock

## curiosities
**Development of the Christian Underground** : By the late 1990s, a sophisticated infrastructure emerged to support Christian rock, including Christian-owned clubs, record labels, and bookstores. This ecosystem was sometimes referred to as the "Christian Underground." "Christian bands played in Christian-owned clubs and signed a Christian-owned record label so sold their CDs through Christian bookstores." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g-tBn8dsSqw
**Early Stereotypes** : Throughout the 1980s, Christian rock was often stereotyped as "well scrub preaching from behind a guitar," with mainstream fans perceiving the music as weak and watered down, clashing with rock and roll's rebellious spirit. "Mainstream fans found the music weak in water down and wasn't rock and roll supposed to be about rebellion?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g-tBn8dsSqw
**Growing and Profitable Segment** : Christian Rock is highlighted as one of the few segments within the music industry that is actively experiencing growth and increasing profitability. "It's one of the few segments of the music industry that's actually growing and becoming more profitable." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g-tBn8dsSqw
**Historical Oxymoron** : For decades, the concept of "Christian rock" was considered oxymoronic, as "good Christians" were not believed to "rock," and electric guitars were deemed inappropriate for hymns. "There was no such thing as Christian rock for decades." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g-tBn8dsSqw
**INXS's Brief Venture** : The band INXS, early in its career, briefly attempted to enter the Christian rock market. This endeavor, however, was very short-lived, lasting only "about a minute." "and originally the idea was to try to break into the Christian rock market, which lasted about a minute." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gxbuajGKbko
**Mainstream Perceptions vs. Crossover Ambitions** : Christian rock bands often face a dilemma: they fear being "too wholesome" for the mainstream, which could lead to them being dismissed as "sermonizers and proselytizers" based on preconceived notions. "At the same time, these bands are worried about being too wholesome for the rest of the world." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g-tBn8dsSqw
**Mid-90s Market Growth** : The market for Christian rock experienced rapid growth in the mid-1990s, with youth groups and churches hosting concerts. These events were considered safe for parents and helped spread awareness of the bands. "The market for Christian rock grew quickly in the middle 90s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g-tBn8dsSqw
**Ongoing Condemnation** : Even with the genre's evolution, the belief that rock music itself is evil persists, with some people actively condemning Christian rock bands for "perverting Christianity" by playing such music. "Some people believe that these bands, no matter what their veneer, are perverting Christianity." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g-tBn8dsSqw
**Schisms and Fan Judgment** : The Christian underground experienced internal divisions. Bands crossing over to the mainstream worried about alienating Christian fans if their content was perceived as negative, immoral, offensive, or "anti-god." Some Christian fans are described as very judgmental, particularly concerning the "GPMs" (Gods Per Minute) in lyrics. "Some factions of Christian rock fans can be very judgmental without the requisite number of GPMs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g-tBn8dsSqw
**Shifting Perception in the Late 90s** : Towards the end of the 1990s, stereotypes associated with Christian rock began to diminish. Bands started attracting fans based on their sound rather than solely their message, offering positive, current, and less preachy music that resembled mainstream rock while notably lacking themes of sex and drugs. "Instead of weak and safe imitations of the real thing, these bands began to attract fans for their sound and not their message." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g-tBn8dsSqw
**Subtle Religious Message** : Many Christian Rock bands produce music that sounds mainstream, with listeners often unaware of the religious orientation of the artists unless explicitly told. "And here's the weird thing, unless you even know, you'd never guess that these bands were all about God." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g-tBn8dsSqw
