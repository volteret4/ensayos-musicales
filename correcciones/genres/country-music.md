# genre - Country Music

## curiosities
**Influence of Blues Slide Players** : The slide guitar techniques developed by early 20th-century Mississippi Delta blues players, who improvised with knives or beer bottle necks, influenced countless country musicians. "Like that, interned influence not only a billion country players, but rockers like Rye Kooter, Brian Jones and Keith Richards of the Rolling Stones and Duane Almond." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8GU8ZpSgY8
**Prevalence of Alcohol References** : A notable characteristic of country music is its frequent mention of alcohol; statistics show that one in five country music songs contains some reference to alcohol. "One in five country music songs have some reference to alcohol." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GO0K_N7Ys7I
**Rise of Steel Guitar** : The steel guitar, an evolution of the Hawaiian lap-style guitar where a metal slide (the "steel") is used, became a significant instrument and sound in country music. "Follow that forward a number of decades and you end up with the steel guitar, which became a big thing in country music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8GU8ZpSgY8
**Authenticity and Struggle** : For country music, the concept of authenticity is deeply tied to the "music of struggle," almost reveling in hardship, which contrasts with rock's focus on escaping struggle. This emphasis on lived experience often led to skepticism towards privileged artists or those perceived as insincere. "Some genres do not permit veering too far away from this elusive idea of authenticity. Which for country is music of struggle, almost reveling in it, and for rock is escaping from it." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 35 - Cosmic American Music - 70s Country Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-35-cosmic-american-music-70s-country-rock
**Skepticism Towards Rock Singers** : Some country singers and their fans were concerned that rock singers entering the country genre were insincere in their appreciation or were making fun of country music. The differing approaches to music-making, such as a rock artist's desire to "play what you feel" versus a country session player's preference for specific instructions, fueled this suspicion. "They were worried that the rock singers were not sincere about their appreciation for country. Their approach to making the music might have been a little different, too, which leads to questions about whether or not they were serious." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 35 - Cosmic American Music - 70s Country Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-35-cosmic-american-music-70s-country-rock
