# genre - Madchester

## curiosities
**Bridge to Britpop** : After a brief lull following Madchester's decline, Britpop emerged, with its foundations essentially built upon the Madchester scene. "then there's a bit of a law and then Brickpop comes along. But Brickpop's foundations were essentially Manchester." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KF_adyXf86Q
**Cultural Hub at The Haçienda** : The Haçienda nightclub in Manchester was a focal point for the Madchester scene, with its balconies and stairwells offering a unique vantage point for observing the rise of this genre and the emergence of DJs as prominent figures. "The balconies and stairwells were ringside seats to the explosion in rave music and mad chester in the rise of the DJ as star." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2RKchFW42Jc
**Cultural Impact and Travel** : During its peak, Madchester clubs were so popular that people would travel extensively from London via coaches and trains, arriving at Piccadilly Station on Fridays and Saturdays to spend the weekend in Manchester. This was unusual for Londoners to travel to "provincial cities." "the first time ever you'd been a club and you'd hear people would come up from London, get a coach... All these Londoners piling off to go, you know, and staying in Manchester for the weekend." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=q_Scyr2czh8
**Definition and Flourishing Period** : Madchester was a crazy and wildly popular music scene that flourished in Manchester, England, in the late 1980s and early 1990s. It was exemplified by bands like the Happy Mondays, Inspiral Carpets, and The Stone Roses, collectively known as the "Holy Trinity." "that crazy and wildly popular scene in Manchester, England that flourished in the late 80s and early 90s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KF_adyXf86Q
**Duration and Decline** : The Madchester era burned brightly and hard for about four years, primarily ending around 1992 or 1993. Its decline was attributed to bands burning out and public interest waning. "the Madchester thing comes to an end somewhere around, what he figured, 92, 93." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KF_adyXf86Q
**Era of Drugs and Debauchery (90s)** : The Madchester scene of the 1990s was notoriously associated with extensive drug and alcohol use, characterized by "stories of drugs and alcohol and debauchery," including substances like "Pot Coke, heroin, PCP crack," which led to "wreck brains, wrecked bodies, wrecked cars, wrecked careers." "Now, if you're a fan of the whole madchester thing back in the 90s, you know all about the stories of drugs and alcohol and debauchery." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
**Fashion Style** : The Madchester look was defined by "baggy pants and a bowl haircut," which, despite its retro elements, combined with the music to feel very modern. "Dressed it up with baggy pants and a bowl haircut and you were golden." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DSvoppf1dGY
**Foundations** : The foundations of Madchester music are rooted in Northern Soul and similar genres. "Madchester's foundations are originally Northern Seoul and stuff." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KF_adyXf86Q
**Golden Period and Decline** : Madchester experienced a "golden period" that lasted approximately 18 months before burning out. Factors contributing to its decline included legal hassles for The Stone Roses, drug issues for Happy Mondays, criminal gangs taking over The Haçienda, and Factory Records going out of business. "But madchester's golden period only lasted about 18 months. It just burned out." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DSvoppf1dGY
**Holy Trinity** : The scene was famously led by a "Holy Trinity" of bands: Inspiral Carpets, Happy Mondays, and The Stone Roses. "one of the bands that formed the Holy Trinity of madchester." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=q_Scyr2czh8
**Influence on British Indie Music** : Madchester music set the stage for much of the British indie music that emerged over the subsequent 15 years, with its impact still felt today. "This was music that set the stage for much of the British indie music that was to come for the next 15 years." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KF_adyXf86Q
**Londoners Traveling to Manchester** : During the Madchester era, it became a bizarre phenomenon for people from London to travel by coach or train to Manchester's clubs, particularly on Fridays and Saturdays. This was unusual for Londoners, who typically did not travel to provincial cities for entertainment. "the first time ever you'd been a club, and you'd hear people would come up from London, you'd get a coach." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KF_adyXf86Q
**Origin and Era** : Madchester was a "crazy and wildly popular scene" that emerged in Manchester, England, flourishing in the late 1980s and early 1990s. It is described as music that "set the stage for much of the British indie music that was to come for the next 15 years," with its impact still felt today. "In 1990, commercial reign for the Inspiral Carpet, one of the bands that formed the Holy Trinity of madchester, that crazy and wildly popular scene in Manchester, England that flourished in the late 80s and early 90s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=q_Scyr2czh8
**Overshadowing Dreampop (1990s)** : Madchester is listed as one of the new music genres of the 1990s that began to overshadow Dreampop, contributing to its recession to the margins of popular music. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFLMkvJAaUY
**Rise in UK (1990)** : The rise of Madchester in the UK in the spring of 1990 contributed to rock's quick resurgence, alongside the beginnings of Grunge in the US. "There was the rise of Madchester in the UK..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MK7L0eCpDHc
**Scene Over by Early 90s** : The Madchester scene, featuring bands like The Stone Roses and Happy Mondays, was "pretty much over" by the early 1990s, contributing to a "big void in British music" that Britpop later helped to fill. "The madchester scene featuring the stone roses and the happy Mondays was pretty much over." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5q5EnZj1FwE
**Sound Formation - Late 1980s** : The Madchester sound emerged from young Manchester musicians combining the attitude of acid house raves with proper guitars. This resulted in a clash of jangly guitars, swirling organs, and indie rock aesthetics set to a danceable beat, discovered by the British music press by late 1989. "And by late 1989, the British music press had discovered it and were calling the sound madchester." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DSvoppf1dGY
