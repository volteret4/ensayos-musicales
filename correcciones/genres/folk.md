# genre - Folk

## curiosities
**Bob Dylan's Mainstream Acceptance** : Bob Dylan, despite having a distinct and unusual singing voice, secured a major record deal, possibly due to his origins in the folk scene. This suggests that certain genres or artistic communities could be more accommodating to unconventional vocal styles early in a career. "Dylan had a major record deal, but maybe it was because he came from the folk scene." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7_ZmZxnSOnE
**Component of Indie Folk** : Folk music is identified as one of the genres that came together in Indie Folk, contributing to its rootsy and acoustic characteristics. "This is a corner of alt rock where folk, blues, gospel, and even a little country came together." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tdTs-4Irv8c
**Early Radio Exposure (1940s)** : Folk music gained increased airplay on radio in the 1940s when stations sought material from non-ASCAP performers during a conflict with established artists and music publishers, contributing to its growing public appeal. "This meant more country and folk and hillbilly and R&B were heard on the radio." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GES0Ms_kaCI
**Largest Growth in UK (2010s)** : Of all British music scenes, folk experienced probably the largest growth during the 2010s, expanding from artists like Mumford & Sons to include Laura Marling, Jake Bugg, Ben Howard, and Ed Sheeran. "Of all the scenes in Britain during this decade, folk probably saw the largest growth." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**New Influence in Alt Rock (Late 2000s)** : In the late 2000s, folk music, particularly its acoustic aspects, was explored as a potential new influence for alt rock, questioning if the industry could successfully market folk and bluegrass-influenced music to the alt-rock demographic. This innovation arose during a "down part of a cycle" for rock. "Could the industry maybe sell a form of folk and bluegrass influence music to the alt-rock crowd?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_yslM5oanRo
**Roskilde Representation** : Folk music is one of the genres represented at the Roskilde festival, which hosts approximately 180 different acts for up to 130,000 people. "There's rock, there's folk, there's electronic and hip hop. They're all represented in one way or the other." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Tim Buckley's Beginning** : Tim Buckley started his musical career as a folk singer in the 1960s before exploring other genres. "He began as a folk singer back in the 1960s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MmXDbXI6Sqk
**21st Century Revival Context** : The release of Björk's *Volta* album in 2007 coincided with a resurgence in folk music in the 21st century, with artists like CocoRosie and Devendra Banhart experiencing a "small peak in popularity" for this new approach to folk. *Volta* itself incorporated many world music sounds, positioning Björk within this broader musical landscape. **Influence on Glam** : Folk music, particularly its melancholic ballads, provided significant influences for glam rock. These balled themes became a cherished element within the glam sound, reminiscent of 1960s folk. "Teniendo influencias muchas influencias de la música folk, esas baladas que tanto gustaban dentro de la música glam, ese toque melancólico que tienen muchas veces algunos discos que recuerdan a veces a lo que era el foc de los años 60." ← Music Radar Clan > EL PIONERO DEL GLAM： MARC BOLAN Y T REX | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mw_HxfX8iBA&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc ← folk
**Late 1960s Market Saturation** : Folk music, which began its rise in the early 1960s, experienced absolute market saturation by the end of the decade, making it challenging for new artists like early David Bowie and T-Rex to achieve significant success within the genre. **Market Differentiation (2009)** : In 2009, Mumford & Sons, described as an "English folk band" playing accordions, banjos, and ukuleles, offered a sound "a million miles away from what anyone else was offering," setting them apart from the dominant alt-rock and pop scenes. "An English folk band who played accordians and banders in ukuleles, fighting against a sea of more normal alt-rock bands, along with pop stars like Beyonce and Katy Perry and Justin Bieber, where the hell did a band like Mumford & Sons fit within this equation?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P1RyOchn7pk ← folk
