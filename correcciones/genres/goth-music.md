# genre - Goth Music

## curiosities
**British Invention with Early American Practitioners** : Goth largely emerged in Britain in the late 1970s after punk rock declined, though early American artists like The Doors and The Velvet Underground had some sinister sounds. "goth is very much a British invention, with one tiny exception." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
**Etymology of "Goths"** : One story suggests British punk refugees who made gloomy music were called "goths" because they lived in an apartment building named Visigoth Towers in Brixton, London. Alternatively, music journalist Nick Kent may have coined the term. "One story says they became known as goths because a bunch of them all lived in the same apartment building, a place called Visigoth Towers in the Brixton area of London." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
**Evolution and Influence (Mid-80s)** : By the mid-1980s, Goth's influence extended to other genres, including new industrial bands and electronic groups, and even some metal acts, demonstrating its pervasive and lasting impact. "By the mid-80s Goth's influence was spilling over into other genres." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
**Fashion Aesthetics** : Goth fashion, often called "menacing" or "disconcerting" to outsiders, is characterized by wearing all black, except for occasional shades of purple or bright red (especially lipstick). Styles often harken back to 19th-century British artsy class attire, some androgenous, others BDSM-inspired, creating an erotic, mysterious, "dressed for a never-ending funeral" look. "This insistence on wearing black everything became part of the glue that held the new scene together." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
**Meaning of "Gothic" in Music** : The word "gothic" was first used to describe modern music in relation to The Doors' first album, due to its dark, mysterious, and violent qualities. "As far as we can tell, the first use of the word gothic to describe modern music was used in relation to the doors, first album." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
**Musical Characteristics** : Early Goth music featured tribal drums, bass players working high on the fretboard (sometimes carrying the melody), sharp, slashing, thin guitar sounds, and vocals ranging from growls to yelps to operatic. The overall effect was often described as mesmerizing. "In the beginning, there was almost a Goth sound, tribal drums, bass players working high on the fretboard, sometimes carrying the melody, which of course was just not done in proper circles." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
**Origin as a Subculture** : Goth music culture is one of the biggest musical subcultures globally, with its explosion beginning after the original punk era of the 1970s. It's speculated to be the second biggest music-related subculture after metal, encompassing music, fashion, art, lifestyle, and sometimes politics and religion. "goth music culture is one of the biggest musical subcultures the planet has ever seen." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
**Post-Punk Genesis** : After the original punk scene fragmented, darker-minded punks gravitated towards artists already making dark music. This music, often less aggressive and more introspective and artsy than punk, incorporated existentialist philosophy, romanticism, art history, nihilism, and macabre horror elements. "As the original punk scene lost its legs, it began shattering into many different pieces. The darker-minded punk slowly coalesced around artists who were already making dark music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
**Reclaiming the Term "Goth"** : Initially an insult, the term "Goth" was reclaimed by the scene by the summer of 1982, becoming a self-identifier. "At the beginning, to be called Goth was an insult. But by the summer of 1982, the scene reclaimed that word as their own." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
**Resilience and Longevity** : Goth has proven to be one of the most resilient and long-lasting alternative rock subgenres to emerge from the post-punk era, consistently remaining in the cultural background and influencing new generations of bands with a darker perspective on life. "Goth has proven to be one of the most resilient and long-lasting of the alt-rock subgenres that emerged from the post-punk era." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaLsZFLrMnI
