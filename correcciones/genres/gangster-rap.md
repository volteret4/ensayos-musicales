# genre - Gangster rap

## curiosities
**Hip Hop Generation Gap** : Gangster rap fans are identified as part of the "old schoolers" within hip hop, who are now "pitted against the Drake's and Chris Browns of today," highlighting a generational divide in the genre. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m7MbV-eCLyE
**Characterized by Delinquent Imagery and Themes** : Rapers in this subgenre were often depicted with gold chains, portraying a delinquent image, discussing shootings, women, aggression, prison, and originating from suburbs. This prototype of rap became deeply ingrained in public perception. "Los raperos van con cadenas de oro, son delincuente, se hablan de tiros, de tías buenas, son agresivos, es entranza de la cárcel, tienen todos estos problemas, vienen de suburbios, este prototipo un poco del rap que todos tenemos tan asentado." ← Music Radar Clan > Poniendo a Kanye West en su lugar | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ih5F70jcm6M&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**Dominant in the 1990s** : Gangster rap absolutely dominated the hip hop scene from the mid-1990s, becoming the "great era" of mainstream hip hop. This period brought forth major artists but also became somewhat "caricature-like" by the late 1990s, leading to a sense of exhaustion. "El rap y del hip hop de mitad de los 90, la verdad es que la época de mitad de los 90 del hip hop es una etapa un poco curiosa porque está absolutamente dominada por lo que entendemos por el gangster rap." ← Music Radar Clan > Poniendo a Kanye West en su lugar | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ih5F70jcm6M&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**Late 90s/Early 2000s Decline and Evolution** : Towards the end of the 1990s, after many years of prominence, gangster rap began to feel "relatively exhausted" and almost "caricature-like." Artists like 50 Cent and Jay-Z, while important, represented a late evolution of this style, maintaining the "almost delinquent" image with weapons and aggressive themes, although some, like Eminem, had more "evolutive" lyrics. "Empieza a estar relativamente agotado y empieza a ser un poco casi caricaturezco y cuando ya nos vamos hacia finales de los 90 que ya tenemos a 50 cent, tenemos a J7, tenemos a muchos otros artistas muy importantes, siguen siendo una evolución un poco tardía de lo que viene a ser esos restos en el gangster rap." ← Music Radar Clan > Poniendo a Kanye West en su lugar | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ih5F70jcm6M&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**Part of 1990s Musical Diversity** : Gangster rap was a significant element within the broad musical changes observed between 1991 and 1995. This era was characterized by a rapid emergence of diverse genres and scenes. "the entire Lola Paloose generation was gangster rap." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The 1000th Episode of the Ongoing History of New Music | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bI6TTA-fn7c&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Rise and Fragmentation of Music Video Culture** : The rise of gangster rap contributed significantly to the fragmentation of music video culture. Alongside alt-rock, it helped divide everything into distinct "scenes," leading to the demise of the music video monoculture and prompting video channels to develop genre-specific programming like "Yo MTV Raps" and "Raps City."
