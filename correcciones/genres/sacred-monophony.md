# genre - Sacred monophony

## curiosities
**Invention and Architectural Suitability** : Sacred monophony was a type of sacred music essentially invented by Hildegard von Bingen in the 12th century, serving as the standard for liturgical chanting in Catholic churches. Its slow, somber, and rhythm-less nature was perfectly suited for the highly resonant, echoey acoustics of the large stone cathedrals of the time, where faster or more complex music would have become an "unlistenable mess" due to the excessive reverb. "The official term for this music is sacred monophony." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NAroxFbYkRw
