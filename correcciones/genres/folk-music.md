# genre - Folk Music

## curiosities
**1950s/1960s Revival** : In the late 1950s, folk music experienced a significant revival, becoming popular with "cool kids" and later "hipsters." This boom, championed by artists like The Kingston Trio and Peter, Paul and Mary, eventually led to the emergence of Bob Dylan and a new wave of modern folk music. "In the late 1950s, all the cool kids were into folk music. But that was a generation removed. Suddenly became the thing all the hipsters were listening to." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gkLMwrthZ9s
**Early Conceptual Form** : Folk music was a significant part of early conceptual recordings, as "folkies" liked to tell stories with their songs, and collections of their music could be considered conceptual. "Folk music was a big part of it. Folkies like to tell stories with their songs and collections of their music could be considered conceptual." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Dm5N6yDRxXQ
**Various Flavors in 1950s Britain** : In mid to late 1950s Britain, folk music existed in "various flavors" alongside imported American rock and roll and the skiffle craze. This diverse musical landscape was the backdrop against which Trag Jazz emerged. "In the midst of imported American rock and roll records, the skiffle craze and various flavors of folk music, some young people rejected contemporary sounds, in favor of something known as Trag Jazz." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zNIVqKqAlnk
**Vehicle for Communist-Labeled Messages (1930s-1940s)** : In the 1930s and 1940s, folk music was utilized by artists like Woody Guthrie and Pete Seeger to disseminate messages. This led to them being labeled as communists by authorities. "In the 30s and 40s, Woody got three and Pete Seeger used folk music to spread messages that had them labeled as communists." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0ARYt4sYT90
