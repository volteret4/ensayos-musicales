# genre - Gothic Rock

## curiosities
**Gothic Rock Lineage – From Tambora's Eruption to Mary Shelley to Bauhaus** : The genealogy of gothic rock traces back to the 1815 eruption of the Tambora volcano in Indonesia — the largest in recorded history — which caused the "Year Without a Summer" in 1816. The resulting gloom and cold inspired Mary Shelley to conceive *Frankenstein*, a foundational text of Gothic literature. That Gothic tradition, running through horror cinema and theatrical glamour of the 1960s, was eventually absorbed into rock music by bands like Bauhaus, Joy Division, and The Cure in the late 1970s. **Influences** : Gothic rock draws its "DNA" from 1960s psychedelia, with influences extending back to The Velvet Underground and Leonard Cohen from the 1960s. Additionally, Alice Cooper's death-obsessed shock rock of the early 1970s and certain elements of David Bowie's work are credited as contributing to the genre's development. "Gothrock has some DNA from 60's psych, but its influences go as far back as the velvet underground, and even some Leonard Cohen from the 1960's." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The Tribes of Alt-Rock | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mAlc-N2wn5s&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Musical Characteristics** : Gothic rock is characterized by its dark and introspective nature, often utilizing minor keys. The sound frequently features sharp, brittle guitars and prominent reverb. The lyrical subject matter often incorporates religious and supernatural symbolism, described as gloomy, morbid, somber, and even vampiric, though Goths themselves are noted as being capable of happiness. "Yeah, the music is dark and introspective, often in a minor key, and can feature sharp brittle guitars and reverb." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The Tribes of Alt-Rock | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mAlc-N2wn5s&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Post-Punk Emergence (Late 1970s - Early 1980s)** : Gothic rock emerged in the period immediately following 1970s punk rock, with its distinct development truly taking hold in the very late 1970s and early 1980s. Its sound diverged from punk, yet its existence was clearly facilitated by punk's preceding impact.
