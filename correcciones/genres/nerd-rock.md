# genre - Nerd Rock

## curiosities
**1990s Explosion** : The 1990s saw a significant boom in nerd rock, driven by the broader explosion in alternative music. This era created an egalitarian environment where diverse ideas, attitudes, and sounds from the fringes could enter the mainstream, encouraging and celebrating artists who were perceived as weird. "The early 1990s saw a huge bloom in nerdy bands coming to the forefront." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h6snk0m4200
**Alternative Term for Geek Rock** : Nerd Rock is an alternative term for Geek Rock, used to describe a subgenre of alt-rock that emerged with personal computer culture, emphasizing intellectual and pop culture themes. "You can also call it Nerd Rock, or even Dork Rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Characteristics** : Nerd rock bands, particularly in the early to mid-90s, did not necessarily share a common sound but were unified by their humor, fun, self-deprecation, appreciation of the mundane, and extensive use of irony. "There wasn't really a common sound, but they were all about humor and fun and self-deprecation, appreciation of the mundane and loads and loads of irony." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h6snk0m4200
**Definition and Scope** : Nerd rock is a genre of music that aligns squarely with alternative music, often characterized by a sound, style, look, attitude, or personality of the musicians that embodies "nerdishness." It encompasses numerous subgenres, with geek rock being a prominent one. "Nerdishness is now so widespread that even nerds have their own genre of music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h6snk0m4200
**Early 80s Electronic Roots** : In the early 1980s, nerd rock became heavily rooted in electronics, utilizing keyboards, synthesizers, sequencers, and drum machines, as tech-minded people uninterested in traditional instruments were drawn to the new technology, and synthesizers were equated with the music of the future and science fiction. "For a time, nerd rock was heavily rooted in electronics, keyboards synthesizers, sequencers, drum machines, and so on." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h6snk0m4200
**Evolution from Folk to Electronic** : The music genre initially began as a form of folk music (FILK Rock), then incorporated surrealism, and later integrated elements of synth-based new wave and post-punk, especially as electronic instruments became more accessible and powerful. "The music started as a form of folk music, moved into surrealism and then parts of the synth based new wave and post punk world." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h6snk0m4200
