## curiosities
**Appearance and Impact (2009)** : This band appeared around 2009, sounding fresh and different. They achieved significant commercial success and had both cross-genre and cross-generational appeal, helping to usher in a new generation of softer and poppier alt rock, and sparking a folk scene growth in Britain. "When they appeared, they sounded fresh and different." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOU54d8mr5k
**Comparison to Fairground Attraction** : Fairground Attraction is described as "the Mumford and sons of their day," suggesting a similar folk-pop style and level of popularity in their era. "They were, in a sense, the Mumford and sons of their day." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZcF5JMhBj2M
**Era Influence** : Mumford & Sons characterized an era of indie rock that was prevalent around 2012-2013, known for its use of banjos and mandolins, and a "melodic, woe-as-me introspective" sound. This era made it challenging for traditional rock bands to gain traction. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ocMHRLQMQY0
**Pioneering Acoustic Alt Rock** : Mumford & Sons achieved significant success with their debut album and single in late 2009, which played a pivotal role in setting the stage for the rise of acoustic-based or acoustic-tinged alt-rock throughout the second decade of the 21st century. Their music demonstrated that folk and bluegrass influences could be successfully introduced to the alt-rock crowd. "Their success, and they had a lot of it, set the stage for a lot of acoustic-based or at least acoustic-ting alt-rock in the second decade of the 21st century." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_yslM5oanRo
**Streaming Success with Pop-Leaning Rock Sound** : Mumford and Sons were among the rock bands that experienced successful streams in the 2010s, with their songs reaching hundreds of millions or even billions of listens. Their music was described as leaning towards the pop end of rock, which helped them thrive amidst the shift to streaming consumption. "There was the luminaires, Lorde, Imagine Dragons, Coldplay, Mumford and Sons.
