## curiosities
**Band Name and Origins** : The name "Hanatarash" translates to "Snotnosed" or something similar. The band's history dates back to approximately 1983, though precise details are uncertain. "Their name translates as Snotnosed, or again, something close to it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5vKSjpvVQa8
**Extremely Dangerous Gigs** : Hanatarash gigs were notoriously perilous, with attendees sometimes required to sign waivers acknowledging the risk of injury. The band incorporated drills, power tools, and even heavy machinery as instruments during their performances. "You literally took your life into your hands." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5vKSjpvVQa8
**Molotov Cocktail Incident and Venue Bans** : At one show, the performance had to be halted because Yamantaka Eye was preparing to throw a Molotov cocktail into the crowd. These dangerous antics led to Hanatarash being banned from most venues across Japan. "At another show, things had to be stopped because I was all set to throw a Molotov cocktail into the crowd." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5vKSjpvVQa8
**Return to Performing (Conditional)** : The band was only permitted to perform again after Yamantaka Eye agreed to cease his self-destructive and dangerous stage behaviors. "it wasn't until I agreed to stop being so self-destructive and dangerous that they were allowed to perform again." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5vKSjpvVQa8
**Yamantaka Eye's Destructive Performances** : Frontman Yamantaka Eye engaged in extreme and destructive acts, including driving an excavator through the back wall of a venue at one show. At another early performance, he decapitated a dead cat with a machete and once almost severed his own leg by strapping a circular saw to his back. "At one show, frontman Yamantaka I drove an excavator through the back wall of the venue." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5vKSjpvVQa8
**Yamantaka Eye's Later Career** : Following his time with Hanatarash, frontman Yamantaka Eye went on to become a member of The Boredoms, another extreme band that toured as part of Lollapalooza in 1994. "Frontman Yamantaka, I would later go on to be in the Bordom's, another extreme band that toured as part of Lala Palusa in 1994." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5vKSjpvVQa8
