# artist - Buffalo Springfield

## songs
**For What It's Worth (1966) – Written About Sunset Strip Riot at Pandora's Box Club; Sampled 36+ Times** : "For What It's Worth" was written about a riot that broke out to protest the closing of a Sunset Strip club called Pandora's Box in 1966. It remains one of the most popular 1960s counterculture songs and has been sampled at least three dozen times by artists including House of Pain, Everlast, Chumbawamba, and Butthole Surfers. It has been covered by everyone from Cher to David Cassidy to Ozzy Osbourne to Rush to Public Enemy to Stevie Nicks. "It remains one of the most popular 60s counterculture songs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dCelQ8Zj_cg
**For What It's Worth** : Journalist Andrew Cobbkind identified this song as exemplary of 1960s rock, perfectly suited "to avoid the FBI" and serving as background music for the era's politically charged atmosphere, civil disobedience, and explorations. "Buffalo Springfields for what it's worth was perfect music to avoid the FBI by street fighting man was made for trashing draft boards Mr. Tamberine man was made for smoking dope up the country was for dropping out of the city let it be was for letting it be." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 51 - The Softer Side of 70s Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-51-the-softer-side-of-70s-rock
**Kind Woman (1968)** : This song featured Rusty Young on steel guitar and was later cited by Young as "really the first Poco record," highlighting its foundational role in the development of country rock. "rusty young who played the steel guitar on a Buffalo Springfield song called kind woman said that kind woman was really the first polko record that was in nineteen sixty eight." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 35 - Cosmic American Music - 70s Country Rock | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-35-cosmic-american-music-70s-country-rock
