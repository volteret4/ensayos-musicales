# artist - Screamin' Jay Hawkins

## genres
- Blues

## songs
**I Put a Spell on You (1956)** : Screamin' Jay Hawkins and his band recorded this sweet ballad after getting drunk in the studio, with Hawkins blacking out and not remembering the session. The resulting recording was far from a ballad and became infamous, leading to its ban from radio in some places due to complaints of being too sexual, evil, and deviant in 1956. "The results was not a ballad." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SIK2YFSmf9U

## curiosities
**Classical Music Background** : Prior to his rock and roll career, Screamin' Jay Hawkins studied classical music. "adopted by a tribe of blackfoot Indians and studied classical music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SIK2YFSmf9U
**Emerging from a Coffin** : Famous DJ Alan Fried offered Screamin' Jay Hawkins $300 to perform "I Put a Spell on You" live, with the condition that Hawkins emerge from a coffin. Hawkins accepted and continued this macabre act. "The only catch was that Hawkins had to emerge from a coffin to sing the song." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SIK2YFSmf9U
**Macabre Stage Display** : His live shows were characterized by crazy clothes, props like snakes and a skull, smoke bombs, and incantations, where he screamed and grunted, incorporating elements of death, voodoo, and black magic to freak out audiences. "Jay screamed and grunted his way to fame, freaking out people along the way with his macabre display of death and voodoo and black magic and whatever else." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SIK2YFSmf9U
**Post-War Career Start** : After serving in the army during World War II, Screamin' Jay Hawkins became a singer known for his very theatrical performances. "When he got out of the army after World War II, he became a singer, a very, very theatrical singer." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SIK2YFSmf9U
**Skull Prop "Henry"** : As part of his theatrical performances, Screamin' Jay Hawkins used props including a skull on top of a walking stick, which he named Henry. "He had a skull on the top of a walking stick and the skull's name was Henry, by the way." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SIK2YFSmf9U
