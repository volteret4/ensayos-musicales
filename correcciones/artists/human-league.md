## curiosities
**12-inch Remix Business** : Following the trend set by New Order's "Blue Monday," The Human League also ventured into the 12-inch remix market, which was a key aspect of 1980s club culture and extended the life and reach of their music. "And so was Depeche Mode and The Cure and The Human League and Simple Minds and The Cult and Killing Joke and Erasure and Psychedelic Furs and Madness. They all got into the 12-inch business." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**Live Performance with Sequencers** : The Human League was able to transition from the studio to live performances thanks to advancements like the Roland MC4 sequencer. This device allowed them to trigger a series of synthesizer events automatically without constant human intervention, enabling a single musician to produce complex arrangements. "It's stuff like this that allowed groups like the human league to leave the studio and hit the road." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**Moroder's Influence** : The Human League is recognized as one of the technopop bands whose emergence was directly influenced by Giorgio Moroder's groundbreaking synthesizer experiments. His distinctive robotic and futuristic sound, exemplified by "I Feel Love," provided a clear inspirational blueprint for many groups in the electronic music scene. "You can actually draw a straight line from that song through to Dopech mode and OMD and the human league and dozens and dozens of other technopop bands that would emerge in the next few years." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM
