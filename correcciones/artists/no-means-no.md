## curiosities
**Artwork Designer** : Peter Saville, chief art design guy at Factory Records, did design work for No Means No (referred to as "Noam D" in the transcript). "He did everything from gig posters to album design for Joy Division New Order, Roxas music, Swade, pulp, Martha Nemuffins, and Noam D." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pwebM3TfSdg
**Geographical Origins and Evolution** : No Means No originated in Victoria, Canada, and later relocated to Vancouver. Their sound occupied a unique space blending hardcore and metal, and they are recognized for containing elements that would later define post-hardcore. "And then there was no means no, who started in Victoria and later moved to Vancouver. They ended up occupying a space somewhere between hardcore and metal." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PEHkKfmmDiI
**Kurt Cobain's Endorsement** : The album "WRONG" was highly praised, notably by Kurt Cobain prior to Nirvana's mainstream success. "Excellent record. And won the cut the year of a pre-never-mine Kurt Cobain. He loved this record." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PEHkKfmmDiI
**Post-Hardcore Influence** : Their distinctive sound, blending hardcore and metal, showcased elements that foreshadowed what would become known as post-hardcore, influencing bands such as At The Drive-In, The Rollins Band, Alexisonfire, Billy Talent, The Deftones, and Silverstein. "If you listen carefully to what they did, you can hear elements of what would become known as post-hardcore, a sound that would later birth groups like at the drive-in, Rawlins Band, Alexis on Fire, Billy Talent, The Death Tones and Silverstein." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PEHkKfmmDiI
