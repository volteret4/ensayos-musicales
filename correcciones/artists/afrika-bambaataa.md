## songs
**Planet Rock (1982)** : This track, released by New York DJ and MC Afrika Bambaataa, is considered "Ground Zero" for a multitude of electronic dance music genres, including house, electro, garage, and Miami bass. It also had a profound impact on the future of hip hop, notably featuring a sample from Kraftwerk's "Trans-Europe Express." "In 1982, a New York DJ and MC named Africa Bumbata released a track called Planet Rock. And this one song became Ground Zero, the source of everything from house to electro, garage and Miami bass." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0

## curiosities
**Founding Father of Rap** : Afrika Bambaataa is widely recognized as one of the founding fathers of rap, particularly for his influential track "Planet Rock." This song's innovative use of electronic sounds and sampling, including a Kraftwerk sample, profoundly shaped the direction of hip hop and related genres. "And he is recognized as one of the founding fathers of rap." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
