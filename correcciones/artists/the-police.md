## songs
**Driven to Tears** : This song is cited as an example of a track featuring a drum kit recorded using the "Close Miking" technique, which was popular in the 1950s, 60s, and most of the 70s. This technique aimed to capture the pure sound of the stick striking the drum, resulting in a tight, sharp crack from the snare. "The police and driven to tears, that was a close mic drum kit." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TsiYLGGSJ2U
**Driven to Tears (1980)** : Featured on their 1980 album *Zenyatta Mondatta*, this song is highlighted for the "magic" interplay between Sting on bass and Stewart Copeland on drums, demonstrating amazing playing from everyone. **Album Art Designed by Storm Thorgerson** : The Police's album covers were among the many iconic designs created by the renowned Storm Thorgerson and his partner Aubrey Powell. **Career-Long Indie Status (Commercial Success)** : The Police were considered an indie band for their entire career because all their albums and singles were released on A&M Records, which was an independent label until its acquisition in 1989. They sold 75 million records for A&M, demonstrating that "indie" status is about label relationship, not commercial success. "Actually, since every single one of their albums and singles was on ANM Records, we can actually say that they were an indie band for their entire career." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
