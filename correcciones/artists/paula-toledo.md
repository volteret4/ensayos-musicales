## songs
**How Long Will It Take (2003)** : This song was recorded in a studio in Langley, BC in 2003 and subsequently licensed to one TV show and one made-for-TV movie, after which it was largely forgotten by the artist. It later surfaced as a looped section in the menu of a Russian bootleg DVD around 2007, intriguing a Ukrainian music fan who posted a snippet online, initiating a 16-year search. "Paula Toledo did indeed record this song in a studio in Langley, BC in 2003." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UsPoPUByjYw

## curiosities
**Charitable Proceeds** : Once "How Long Will It Take" was officially re-released on platforms like Bandcamp, Spotify, and Apple Music by May 2024, Paula Toledo directed all proceeds from the song to the Music Heels Charitable Foundation, which specializes in music therapy. "But now that the song is back, she's posted on Bandcamp and Spotify and Apple Music, directing all proceeds to the Music Heels Charitable Foundation, which works in the area of music therapy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UsPoPUByjYw
**Discovery on Russian Bootleg DVD** : The song "How Long Will It Take" (dubbed "How Long Will It Take?" or "How Long?") turned up on a Russian bootleg DVD around 2007, not as a full track but as a looped section in the DVD's menu. Shazam and SoundHound couldn't identify it, despite it being a "nice song" that many people wanted to know more about. "It wasn't even a full version of the song, just a section that was looped in the menu of the DVD that listed other illegal DVDs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UsPoPUByjYw
**Online Search and Identification** : The song's elusive nature bugged many people, leading to a widespread online search, with some fans creating tribute videos, remixes, and covers. The mystery was finally solved on December 8, 2023, when Reddit user L. Aerebaba found it in the Sokan database in Canada after searching performing rights societies, leading back to a podcast containing the full song. "Then, 16 years after the first initial post from Ukraine, a user named the Aerebara cracked it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UsPoPUByjYw
**Streaming Fraud Controversy** : After its rediscovery and release on streaming platforms, an unofficial duplicate of "How Long Will It Take" appeared, siphoning royalties to an unauthorized entity. This confusion led to Paula's official version being temporarily removed by streaming services in a "Kafka-esque streaming fraud case." "That caused so much confusion that Paula's version was taken down by the streaming music services as some kind of Kafka-esque streaming fraud case." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UsPoPUByjYw
**Unexpected Re-emergence** : Upon identification, Paula Toledo, then a 52-year-old woman in Vancouver, experienced her phone and email "blow up" with inquiries and even money being sent to her website. Her son confirmed that people had been searching for her song for 16 years, much to her surprise. "Mom! Someone is asking about one of your songs. People have been looking for you for 16 years!" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UsPoPUByjYw
