## albums
**Pet Sounds (1966)** : This album, released in 1966, is cited by some emo researchers as a starting point for emo music. This theory considers the record's lyrical themes, Brian Wilson's song construction, and his emotionally damaged state at the time of its creation as evidence for its early emo attributes. "some emo researchers, and there are such things, say that emo began with the beach boys and the pet sounds album in 1966, which is not really crazy if you look at the record's lyrical themes, how Brian Wilson constructed the songs, and the fact that he was so emotionally damaged at the time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v_am7ifh7Pk

This move reflected the growing demand for more powerful and effective concert amplification beyond standard PA systems, following issues faced by bands like The Beatles with inadequate volume for large crowds. **Pioneering Large-Scale Sound System (1963)** : In 1963, The Beach Boys were among the first artists to actively seek a solution for inadequate live sound. They contacted Sun Electronics and requested them to build what would become the first large-scale sound system designed specifically for use in a rock concert environment. "The Beach Boys were one of the first to try to do something about it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qrrwqdpCjpQ
**Shift Towards Album as Artistic Statement (Circa 1965)** : Around 1965, prominent artists such as The Beatles, Bob Dylan, and The Beach Boys began to pivot from focusing on individual singles towards conceptualizing albums as complete artistic statements, typically comprising ten, twelve, or more interconnected tracks. "But starting in around 1965, a number of artists, the Beatles, Bob Dylan, and the Beach Boys among them, began to think less in terms of individual songs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Sc9HApnyBLM
**Soviet X-Ray Records (Ribs)** : Songs by The Beach Boys were frequently sought after and pressed onto black market X-ray records, or "ribs," in the Soviet Union during the ban on Western music. "Today, people collect these ribs, especially of songs by the Beatles, the Rolling Stones, Elvis, and the Beach Boys, as well as other Western artists." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B2g4QSREzpw
