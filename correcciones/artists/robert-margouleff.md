## curiosities
**Collaboration with Stevie Wonder** : Robert Margouleff and Malcolm Cecil collaborated extensively with Stevie Wonder on three pivotal albums in the 1970s: "Talking Book," "Innervisions," and "Fulfillingness' First Finale." Wonder sought their expertise in synthesizers to realize his musical visions, resulting in some of his most iconic and electronically rich songs. "Malcolm, Cecil and their Tonto setup, worked with Stevie on three albums that make up much of the core of Stevie Wonder's greatest period." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
**Influence on Quincy Jones and Michael Jackson** : Robert Margouleff's work with TONTO, particularly its impact on Stevie Wonder's music, caught the attention of Quincy Jones. The knowledge and techniques gained from TONTO influenced Jones's production of Michael Jackson's albums, ushering in new possibilities for electronically generated music in mainstream pop. "Quincy Jones loved this thing. What he learned shaped how he produced Michael Jackson's albums and it brought all kinds of new attention to the possibilities of electronically generated music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
**Pioneering Electronic Music with TONTO** : In the late 1960s, American record producer Robert Margouleff, alongside British jazz musician Malcolm Cecil, was captivated by Bob Moog's new synthesizers. They embarked on creating TONTO (The Original New Timbral Orchestra), the world's largest modular synth setup. This colossal instrument, built with components from various manufacturers, enabled unprecedented sonic textures and multi-layered compositions, significantly impacting electronic music production. "These guys, a British jazz dude working for the BBC and an American record producer, were huge influences on the world of electronic music, but they were almost entirely behind the scenes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6SdPPsmpgJg
