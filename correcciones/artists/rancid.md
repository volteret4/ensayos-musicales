## albums
**Let's Go (1994)** : This was Rancid's second album, released on June 21, 1994. The band maintained their punk rock ethics by staying with Epitaph but agreed to a strategic compromise where Warner Brothers, a major label, would distribute and "stealthily promote" their records. "Ransett met the industry halfway. They stayed with epitaph, which pleased bad religion and preserved their punk rock ethics, but they agreed to have their epitaph records distributed and stealthily promoted by Warner Brothers, one of the majors." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gkLMwrthZ9s

## curiosities
**Accusations of Selling Out** : As Rancid gained popularity and commercial recognition, they, alongside other successful pop punk bands like Green Day and The Offspring, were accused of "selling out" by punk purists. "purists became annoyed, accusing groups like Green Dan, the offspring and Rancidov selling out." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Band Origins and Label Strategy** : Rancid evolved from the revered band Operation Ivy and were label-mates with Bad Religion on Epitaph. Their songs were known for being short, tight, and melodic, perfectly complementing the sound kids sought after Green Day's success. They balanced punk ethics with commercial reach by remaining on Epitaph while securing distribution and quiet promotion from Warner Brothers. "Not only was Ransett label-mates with bad religion, but they devolved out of a band called Operation Ivy, a group that was revered by a young Billy Joe Armstrong, who of course was in Green Day" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gkLMwrthZ9s
**Clash-like Sound** : This band was noted for sometimes sounding more like The Clash than The Clash themselves. "Ransett, who sometimes sounded more clash than the clash." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Evolution from Operation Ivy** : Rancid formed and evolved from Operation Ivy, a Bay Area band that preceded them in the punk scene. "They evolved out of a Bay Area band called Operation Ivy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Melodic Enough for Prime Time** : Rancid was identified as a "pure punk band" whose melodic qualities made them suitable for broader mainstream exposure. This characteristic helped them appeal to a wider audience beyond traditional punk circles. "Another pure punk band that was deemed melodic enough for prime time was Rancid." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pE7TaWSUa-o
**Multiple Warped Tour Appearances** : Rancid has agreed to participate in the Warped Tour at least three times. "Ransid has agreed to participate at least three times." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Tim Armstrong's Support for Famous Stars and Straps** : Tim Armstrong of Rancid is noted for publicly wearing gear from Travis Barker's clothing company, Famous Stars and Straps, helping to promote the brand. This shows support among musicians for each other's entrepreneurial ventures. "If you look carefully, you can see Travis's gear on you guys like Tim Armstrong of Ransid". ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WIFxAaReLn0
