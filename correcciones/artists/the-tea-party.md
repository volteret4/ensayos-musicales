## curiosities
**Collaboration with Bob Rock** : The Tea Party is listed among Bob Rock's clients. "We got the cult, Veruch assault, Erasmith, Motley crew, Ardady Pease, the Tea Party, Eric and High Fy, Afghan Wigs, David Lee Roth, the Conorline Crush, Cher." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QkSKxm_U9Yc
**Featured on Big Shiny Toons 2** : The Tea Party was one of the artists on the *Big Shiny Toons 2* compilation CD from Much Music in 1997. This album, featuring entirely alt-rock tracks, sold over 1.2 million units in Canada, indicating its widespread popularity. "featuring the prodigy, blur, Marilyn Manson, Bush, Collective Soul, the Tea Party, Radiohead, Stone Temple Pilots, and more." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7IDbqdIw3WU
**Jeff Burles' Role as Drummer** : Jeff Burles, drummer for The Tea Party, played drums for several Thorneley shows, including a significant gig in Edmonton, before the full reformation of Big Wreck. "Jeff Burles from the Tea Party was actually a drummer at the time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**Rise in 1993** : The Tea Party began their rise in 1993, joining a wave of new Canadian artists gaining attention both domestically and internationally. This marked a period when foreign music fans started discovering Canadian talent. "1993, Doe Boys, the Tea Party, Daniel Landwaw, More Sarah McLaughlin, More Pursuit of Happiness, More Cowboy Junkeys." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6PGgNCaW4xo
