## songs
**Upside Down and Inside Out** : Ok Go teamed with Russian airline S7 to create the first-ever Zero G music video for this song. The video was filmed aboard a plane flying a series of extreme parabolic trajectories, which produced momentary periods of zero gravity. The video is noted for achieving this feat while remaining within the Earth's atmosphere, distinguishing it from Chris Hadfield's earlier Zero G video on the International Space Station. "Ok go teamed with a Russian airline called S7 to create the first ever Zero G music video for a song called Upside Down and Inside Out." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hmnMG6f80Sg
**Here It Goes Again (2006)** : The music video for OK Go's "Here It Goes Again" became a viral sensation in 2006, famous for its intricate treadmill choreography. This video is cited as a prime early example of how artists could create "crazy conceptual stuff for really next to nothing" using readily available technology, demonstrating that concept could replace spectacle in achieving online success. **Upside Down and Inside Out (2014)** : This song is central to OK Go's groundbreaking music video, a collaboration with Russian airline S7. The video, filmed aboard a plane performing extreme parabolic trajectories, is notable for being the first-ever Zero-G music video created entirely within Earth's atmosphere, producing momentary periods of weightlessness. ## curiosities
**Wild Videos as Geek Rock Indicator** : Ok Go is considered part of the Geek Rock world primarily because of their elaborate and "wild videos," which are seen as a defining characteristic aligning them with the genre's inventive and often quirky visual approach. "And with all the wild videos they do, you can make a case for Ok Go to be part of the Geek Rock world." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h6snk0m4200
**Contemporary Powerpop** : OK Go is recognized as a modern band that carries on the Powerpop legacy, noted for its continued influence on today's artists. Their inclusion signifies the genre's longevity and adaptability through different eras of rock music. "Well we have Jimmy Eat World, OkGo, All American Rejects, Simple Plan, maybe Fallout Boy." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The History of Power Pop | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hstJ8M2laho&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**First Zero-G Music Video (2014)** : OK Go partnered with Russian airline S7 to produce the first-ever Zero-G music video for their song "Upside Down and Inside Out." The video was filmed by flying a plane through a series of extreme parabolic trajectories, creating momentary periods of zero gravity. While Commander Chris Hadfield filmed a Zero-G video in space in 2013, OK Go's video is recognized for being filmed within the Earth's atmosphere. "Ok go teamed with a Russian airline called S7 to create the first ever Zero-G Music video for a song called Upside Down and Inside Out." ← Ongoing History of New Music > Airplanes! | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3eGBnwPWRGo&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Self-Promotion and Viral Video Success** : OK Go initially achieved significant success through self-promotion, including playing gigs, direct downloads, social media, and creating viral music videos, one of which garnered over 60 million views on YouTube, before eventually signing with a label.
