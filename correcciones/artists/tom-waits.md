## albums
**Small Change (1976) - Breakthrough Album** : Released in 1976, *Small Change* marked Tom Waits's significant breakthrough album. It featured the single "The Piano Has Been Drinking," which was written during a period when Waits was a heavy drinker, reflecting his personal struggles at the time. ## curiosities
**Declined Plastic Beach Participation** : Tom Waits declined an invitation to contribute to the Gorillaz album "Plastic Beach," stating he had "better things to do." "Then again, Johnny Liden and Tom Weitz also said they had better things to do." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iVdTCqpd798
**Early Career as Doorman and Asylum Signing** : Before gaining attention from Asylum Records, Tom Waits worked as a doorman at a nightclub in San Diego known for featuring idiosyncratic performers. He caught the attention of Asylum because his early work had folk and jazzy elements, similar to other artists on the label like Joni Mitchell. David Geffen and Elliot Roberts, fans of Bob Dylan, saw Waits as potentially their own "Dylan" and were open to experimenting with him, especially given the popularity of the California singer-songwriter scene in the 1970s. "He'd been working as a door man at a nightclub in San Diego that liked to feature some, well, idiosyncratic performers of the day." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7_ZmZxnSOnE
**Vocal Evolution and Legendary Self-Harm** : Tom Waits's vocal stylings underwent a significant transformation over the years. Initially gruff and soulful, he is said to have deliberately damaged his vocal chords with excessive whiskey and cigarettes, a legendary act intended to cultivate an older, more world-weary sound. This unique approach to his voice often leads to him being listed among singers who are perceived as "unable to sing." "according to legend, he deliberately tortured his vocal chords with lots of whiskey and cigarettes to make him sound older and more world-weary." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7_ZmZxnSOnE
