## albums
**Come Find Yourself (1996)** : The debut album by Fun Lovin' Criminals, released in the summer of 1996. The album's third single, "Scooby Snacks," became their only hit in North America, but the album itself contributed to their major success in Britain, where they achieved two platinum albums and one gold album, along with two gold compilation albums. ## songs
**Scooby Snacks (1996)** : The third single from Fun Lovin' Criminals' debut album "Come Find Yourself." It was their only significant hit in North America, but became an immediate hit in the UK, selling 200,000 copies, and was popular on Alt Rock Radio in Canada and the US. The song's title refers to Valium pills, called "Scooby Stacks," handed out by bouncers at The Tunnel, a New York club, to calm patrons. It famously features samples from Quentin Tarantino's movies "Reservoir Dogs" and "Pulp Fiction." Tarantino demanded and received 37% of all royalties and a songwriting credit, making him an official co-writer. As of February 2026, it had 49.2 million Spotify plays and 5.8 million YouTube views, totaling 55 million, with a One Hit Wonder Power Rating of 4.20 out of 10. "First, the song's title is a reference to taking volume." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZcF5JMhBj2M

## curiosities
**Band Formation** : The trio, fronted by Huey Morgan, formed in New York City. They began performing together at a club where they all worked, filling in when booked acts failed to appear. "They are a trio formed in New York City, fronted by Huey Morgan." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZcF5JMhBj2M
**Discovery and Record Deal** : The band was noticed by a record executive during one of their fill-in performances at the club, leading to their signing with EMI. "On one of these occasions, they were noticed by a record guy who signed them to a deal with EMI." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZcF5JMhBj2M

**UK Stardom and Radio Career** : Fun Lovin' Criminals achieved major success in Britain with multiple platinum and gold albums and numerous top 40 hits.
