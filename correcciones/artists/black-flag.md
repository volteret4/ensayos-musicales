**Damaged (1981)** : This album was a pivotal record for Nate Mendel, who initially struggled to understand hardcore music but experienced a click halfway through listening to "Damaged." He was drawn to its energy, aggression, and "weirdness." "Well, the one that, that, where I understood what hardcore we call it, hardcore at the time was about, was, uh, black flags damaged." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mh-qg1izM98

The band had to create their own label, SST, to release it. "Released at almost exactly the same time, and I mean with the days of each other, was Black Flag's nervous breakdown." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PEHkKfmmDiI
**Nervous Breakdown (1979) – Debut EP** : Released in January 1979, almost simultaneously with The Middle Class's "Out of Vogue," this EP features 4 songs totaling 5 minutes and 13 seconds. The band, originally formed as Panic in 1977, had to create its own record label, SST, to release the EP after failing to secure a deal with other labels. SST was founded by Greg Ginn, who owned a ham radio electronics company called Solid State Tuners, and bass player Chuck Dukowski. "Released at almost exactly the same time, and I mean with the days of each other, was Black Flag's nervous breakdown. It was marginally longer, 4 songs over 5 minutes and 13 seconds, so you know. And the band also had to form its own record label to get it out." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0qigzi1j81U

"The title track of Black Flag's nervous breakdown EP released in January 1979." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0qigzi1j81U

**Wasted (1979)** : This track from the "Nervous Breakdown" EP, released in 1979, succinctly captured the typical feelings of a hardcore kid at the time with its short, sharp, and fast delivery. ## curiosities
**Association with Slam Dancing** : Black Flag is mentioned in the context of Orange County punks who popularized slam dancing in the late 1970s. Their performances at clubs like The Cuckoo's Nest are cited as part of the birth of this aggressive dance style. "Black flag, fear, bands like that. Playing at clubs like The Cookus Nest in Costa Mesa, which has gone down on history as the birthplace of slam dancing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bferkEWvJg0
**Documented Moshing in Film** : Black Flag was one of the groups featured in Penelope Spheeris's film "The Decline of Western Civilization," which documented the punk scene in D.C. and Southern California and visually showcased moshing to wider audiences. "Moshing to groups like the circle jerks and black flag." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KMx4bmq8o2Y
**Fidget Spinners as Merch (2017)** : Black Flag, among numerous other bands and artists, sold branded fidget spinners as merchandise in 2017. This reflects a trend where fidget spinners became one of the top-selling band merch items for the year. **Formation and Early Name** : The band was founded by Greg Ginn and originally came together in 1977 under the name "Panic." "Greg Ginn, the founder of the band, which had come together in 1977 under the name Panic" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0qigzi1j81U
**Frightening to Some** : Black Flag is among the hardcore bands that are considered frightening to some people. "Ramstein, Dillinger Escape Plan, Black Flag, and any number of hardcore bands were and are frightening to some people." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5vKSjpvVQa8
**Henry Rollins – Former 7-Eleven Clerk from DC Replaced Co-Founder, Took Band to New Level** : Black Flag was co-founded in 1976 with Keith Morris as singer, but Morris was gone by 1979 (creative differences, cocaine and speed). After brief stints by Ron Reyes and Dez Cadena, the band discovered Henry Rollins — a Washington, D.C. hardcore fan and former 7-Eleven clerk — who had attended their shows. Rollins took Black Flag to an entirely new level and remained their singer until they broke up in summer 1986. "His replacement was a former 7-11 clerk from Washington, D.C., named Henry Rollins." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l0F22NTnQzQ
**Independent Label Founding (SST)** : When no one would release their record, founder Greg Ginn and bass player Chuck Dukowski formed SST, an independent record label. The label's name is an abbreviation for Solid State Tuners, which was Greg Ginn's side business selling ham radio electronics by mail. "When the group couldn't get anyone to put out the record, Greg and bass player Chuck Dukowski formed SST, which is short for Solid State Tuners." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PEHkKfmmDiI
**Influence on Pacific Northwest Rock** : Black Flag was cited as a hardcore punk band from California whose sound influenced the musical landscape of the Pacific Northwest, contributing to the "petri dish" where grunge evolved. "bands like the dead Kennedys and black flag and bad religion." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tFBSrKMEZxI
**Influence on Seattle Scene** : Black Flag's hardcore punk tracks were included on cassettes distributed by Buzz Osborne in Aberdeen, Washington, influencing the emerging Seattle sound. In Seattle, it was not considered strange to be a fan of both Black Flag and Aerosmith simultaneously, leading to a unique hybrid sound. "Hardcore punk mixed easily with hard rock and Seattle. No one considered it strange to be both into black flag and air smith at the same time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Inspiration for Bad Religion** : Black Flag was explicitly cited as an inspiration for the high school students who formed Bad Religion in 1980, encouraging them to be part of the hardcore movement. "They came together in 1980, when a bunch of high school students, inspired by the likes of Black Flag, the Circle Jerks, and the germs, decided that they wanted a piece of the reaction." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0qigzi1j81U
**Intense Hardcore with Fun Songs** : Black Flag stood out as one of the most intense hardcore bands of the early 1980s. Despite their aggressive reputation, they also demonstrated a capacity for "fun dumb" songs, such as "TV Party." "Black Flag was one of the most intense hardcore bands of the early 80s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tZV1lz-obQE
**Most Important American Indie Act** : Black Flag became one of the most important American indie acts of all time, especially after Henry Rollins from Washington D.C. joined as their vocalist. The band's inability to find an existing record label willing to release their music led their guitarist, Greg Ginn, to transform his electronics company into SST Records to distribute their work. "Flag became one of the most important American index of all time, especially after a kid from Washington D.C. named Henry Rollins joined in his vocalist." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Road Incident with Mugger** : On the way to a gig in San Diego, while stuck in a traffic jam, Black Flag's van was cut off by a woman driving a large car. Mugger, a member of the entourage, was so frustrated that he hopped out of the van and dumped his yogurt through her car window. This led to the band being "busted," with Greg having to handle the conversation with the authorities to resolve the incident. "Mugger had had enough of her bolt-ish and hopped out of the van and dumped his yogurt through her window." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DgN0vBIugLc
**Stage Attack in San Diego** : During a show in San Diego, Henry Rollins was attacked on stage by a woman and her boyfriend, along with two friends, who were reportedly under the influence of Angel Dust. Rollins was dragged off stage and repeatedly hit in the face. The attacker was eventually pulled off, and Rollins finished the set before going to the men's room to straighten out his nose, which ended up slightly crooked. "Play the first few songs and see the squirrel trying to grab me. I move away from her. Her boyfriend and his two friends are apparently on Angel Dust and see this as a great opportunity to drag me off stage and beat this shit out of me." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DgN0vBIugLc
