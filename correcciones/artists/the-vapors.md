## songs
**Turning Japanese (1980)** : Released in spring 1980 from the album "New Clear Days." The song tells the story of songwriter David Fenton's infatuation with a photo of his girlfriend. John Weller, father and manager of Paul Weller of The Jam, pushed for The Vapors to record it, which happened after several rejections. It became a global hit, reaching number 36 on the Billboard Top 40, number one in Australia, number three in the UK, and number six in Canada. Its music video was one of the first 200 videos played on MTV when it launched on August 1, 1981. It has been covered by Kirsten Dunst and a lounge-style version was performed by Rick Moranis as his character Tom and Rowe on "Second City TV." As of February 2026, it had 50.7 million Spotify plays and 8.5 million YouTube views, totaling 59.2 million, with a One Hit Wonder Power Ranking of 4.33 out of 10. "It tells the story of songwriter David Fenton getting all gushy over a photo of his girlfriend." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZcF5JMhBj2M
