## curiosities
**Born Again Members** : All members of P.O.D. are born again Christians, a fact that is not immediately apparent from their appearance, which includes tattoos and long hair. "All the members of the band are born again." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g-tBn8dsSqw
**Criticism from within Christian community** : P.O.D. has been subject to condemnation by some individuals who believe that Christian bands, regardless of their message, are perverting Christianity by playing rock music. "And they spend lots of time quoting scripture to support their opinions on the evils and perversions of Creed and jars of clay and MXPX and everybody else." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g-tBn8dsSqw
**Early Career & Manager** : The band's manager previously operated a Christian rock club in San Diego. P.O.D. formed in 1992 and initially built their career by performing at youth group concerts in churches and community centers across the country. They independently sold 40,000 copies of three self-released EPs. "When they started up in 1992, POD began by playing all the youth group concerts at churches and community centers in their area and then across the country." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g-tBn8dsSqw
**Mainstream Crossover Success** : P.O.D. attracted a significant number of mainstream rock fans due to their positive but not preachy lyrics and their strong rock sound. By the late 1990s, they were winning local music awards. In summer 2000, they toured with Ozzy Osbourne on Ozzfest, leading to their signing with Atlantic Records. They are considered one of the first major crossovers from the Christian Underground to mainstream "Heathen Rock." "POD became one of the first major crossovers from the Christian Underground into the mainstream world of Heathen Rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g-tBn8dsSqw
**Post-9/11 Popularity** : The album "Satellite" was released on September 11th, 2001. It is suggested that the timing of its release, amidst the attacks on New York and Washington, may have contributed to its popularity as people sought positive messages. "Then again, maybe the attacks on New York and Washington helped POD become even more popular." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g-tBn8dsSqw
**Unaware Mainstream Audience** : Many mainstream listeners enjoyed P.O.D.'s music without knowing about the band's Christian roots, focusing instead on the quality of their rock sound. "In fact, it's possible that you just heard for the first time that POD was a Christian rock band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g-tBn8dsSqw
