## albums
**Bringing Down the Horse (1996)** : This was the second album by The Wallflowers and proved to be successful, leading to opening slots for major acts. The album's success paved the way for the band to open for bands like The Rolling Stones and Tom Petty, as well as Jacob's father, Bob Dylan. ## songs
**One Headlight (1996)** : This song, off the album *Bringing Down the Horse*, won two Grammy Awards and was a historic chart success. It was the first song to reach number one on three distinct charts: Alternative Songs, Mainstream Rock Songs, and Adult Alternative Songs. "This song won two Grammy Awards. ## curiosities
**Family Background and Early Influences** : Born in 1969, Jacob Dylan is the son of Bob Dylan and his model wife Sarah Dylan, who were married secretly in November 1965. Sarah inspired lyrics for many of Bob Dylan's songs before their 1977 divorce. Despite his father being a famous folk singer, Jacob preferred punk bands like The Clash, The Buzzcocks, and The Jam in his youth, and later had an aborted attempt at design school in New York. "Jacob Dylan is the son of Bob and his model wife Sarah Dylan. They were married in secret in November 1965. And for the next dozen years, she inspired the lyrics for a number of Dylan songs. Then they divorced in 1977. But before then, Jacob came along. That was 1969. And although he was the son of the world's most famous folk singer, he preferred punk bands like the clash and the buzz cocks as well as the jam." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MmXDbXI6Sqk
**The Wallflowers' Consistency** : Jacob Dylan has been the sole consistent member of The Wallflowers throughout its existence, a band that remains active today. The lineup has seen several changes, with notable past members including Jack Irons, formerly of the Red Hot Chili Peppers and Pearl Jam, and Rami Jaffee, keyboardist for Foo Fighters. "Jacob has been the only consistent member of The Wallflowers over all these years, which by the way is still a going concern.
