## albums
**Go Girl Crazy (1975)** : This album features a famous picture inside its CD booklet that shows the band at a White Castle hamburger stand, wearing black leather jackets. This image, reminiscent of 1950s troublemakers like Marlon Brando and James Dean, inspired the authors of "Punk" fanzine to adopt the title, thereby linking the band to the very origin of the term "punk rock" for a new form of music. "This was on the inside of their 1975 album Go Girl Crazy. It's a shot of them at a white castle hamburger stand wearing black leather jackets." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4

## curiosities
**Inspiration for "Punk" Magazine Name** : The Dictators' 1975 album "Go Girl Crazy" featured an inside picture of the band at a White Castle, dressed in black leather jackets, evoking the image of 1950s "punks" like Marlon Brando and James Dean. This image directly inspired the fanzine's authors to name their publication "Punk," thus making The Dictators historically significant as the band who inspired the word used to describe a new form of stripped-down rock and roll. "Instead, they got inspired by a picture of another New York band called The Dictators... And the Dictators? Well, they've gone down in history as the band who inspired the word, to describe a brand new form of stripped down rock and roll." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYi3r0G-ne4
**Performance During CBGB's Final Weeks** : The Dictators were among the bands that performed at CBGB during its final weeks before the club closed down in October 2006. "The final weeks featured bands like Bad Brains, The Dictators, and even Blondie." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Epz5Sn151bk
