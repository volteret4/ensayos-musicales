## albums
**AHA Shake Heartbreak (2005)** : This second album was largely influenced by the band's extensive touring in Europe and the UK between their first and second records, during which they experienced the "highs of rock and roll." The lyrical content explicitly reflected themes of excess, including boozing, drugging, and women. "AHA Shake, the second record, which was pretty much about boozing and drugging and women and all that good stuff." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KyvYpggZXiU
**Aha Shake Heartbreak (UK: ~2004, North America: 2005)** : This album, their second full-length release, adhered to the band's five-syllable album title rule. It was initially released in the UK and then in North America in February 2005, roughly five months later. This staggered release was due to the band's early success in the UK, where they were associated with a "new rock revival" that included bands like The Strokes. The album yielded three major singles in the UK, all of which made the Top 20, including "The Bucket" and "King of the Rodeo". "This record got a British release first and an North American release second." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Because of the Times (2007)** : This was Kings of Leon's third full-length album. It was released in 2007 and, like their other full albums, followed the band's self-imposed rule of having a five-syllable title. "We'll pick things up with a third album because of the Times, because of the Five Cylables, which came out in 2007." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Because of the Times (Year not specified)** : The band made this third album at Blackbird studio in Nashville, a conscious decision to avoid the numerous distractions of Los Angeles and focus intently on the music. This creative period followed a critical family intervention prompted by their mother's concern over their unhealthy appearance after a tour, leading the band to "chill out" and prioritize their well-being. The album surprisingly debuted at number one in Russia. "we really just got to focus on the music and the kind of record we wanted to make. And that was because of the times." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KyvYpggZXiU
**First Record (Year not specified)** : Jared Followell was 15 years old when the band created their first record, which contributed to the band's natural musical evolution over time. "my little brother was Jared, was 15 when we did the first record." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KyvYpggZXiU
**Holy Roller Novakaine (EP) (2003)** : This was the band's first release, recorded in Memphis a month after Nathan and Caleb signed their deal. The EP was influential, as their record label showed it to members of The Strokes, who were intrigued, leading to the band's first UK tour. It features songs like "Wicker Chair" and "Molly's Chambers", and its title track "Holy Roller Novocaine" gained significant exposure through video games and the soundtrack for the NASCAR comedy film "Talladega Nights: The Ballad of Ricky Bobby". "We were making our EP like a month later and Memphis." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Only by the Night (2008)** : This album, Kings of Leon's fourth, was the record that truly propelled the band to mainstream success, with the single "Notion" being a major hit. For some fans, it seemed the band "came out of nowhere" with this album, though the band points out it took five years, one EP, and four albums to reach this level of fame. They found it particularly satisfying to reach big venues with their fourth album, having "worked their way up" rather than achieving instant success. "One of the big singles from their 2008 album, Only by the Knights, the record that really pushed him over the top." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Only by the Night (2009)** : This album was the biggest selling rock CD in all of America in 2009, with sales of 1.4 million copies. Its commercial performance highlighted the continued decline in rock album sales, even for top-performing acts. "And they sold just 1.4 million copies of only by the night." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-CT7yYXonIk
**Youth and Young Manhood (No Year Mentioned, Released after 2003 EP)** : This was Kings of Leon's first full-length album, released approximately six to eight months after their debut EP. The title was inspired by a phrase found in the back of their preacher father's Bible, specifically from an illustration of the "tree of Moses' life" detailing stages like "Youth and Young Manhood". This album was particularly successful in the UK and Europe. It included material from their first EP, with some songs appearing as-is and others re-recorded, notably featuring "Molly's Chambers". "Youth and Young Manhood was the album that I guess really caught on the UK and Europe and then kind of went from there." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4

## curiosities
**"Socks and Shoes" Perception in England** : Upon their first arrival in England, the band observed that people seemed surprised they were wearing socks and shoes, suggesting an expectation of a more stereotypical "wild, crazy, hillbilly redneck" image. "when we first got to England, I mean, I honestly think they were surprised that we had socks and shoes on." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KyvYpggZXiU
**AM Radio Preachers in the South** : During their travels through the South, the family would listen to preachers on AM radio late at night, often finding them "funny" or "not that smart of people," leading to good laughs. "he would also listen to him. If he came across someone that was preaching on the radio, we would listen to it and there were some funny, funny preachers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gxbuajGKbko
**Album Title Naming Convention** : Inspired by phrases they found in their preacher father's Bible, such as "Youth and Young Manhood" from a "tree of Moses' life" illustration, Kings of Leon adopted a distinct naming rule for their albums: every full-length album title must consist of exactly five syllables. Their debut EP is the sole exception to this rule. "Then we got into this five syllable thing and so we just kind of kept that going. Every album title has five syllables." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Aversion to Political Career Revival** : The band intentionally avoided using political "anti-Bush" songs to revive their careers, viewing the practice as "uncool" and believing musicians should focus on music rather than politics. They later included some lyrics that "could have been taken as political" but primarily reflected a general desire for change. "that made us so sick as a band. All these bands, reviving their careers by these political anti-bush songs and anti, you know, you're a band, you're a musician. You make music. You're not a politician." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KyvYpggZXiU
**Backstory as a Novelty and Hook** : The band's unique narrative—comprising three brothers and a cousin, raised on the road by a preacher father—initially captivated audiences and sustained their interest. This allowed sufficient time for the band's musical potential to be recognized beyond their intriguing personal story, proving they were "something more than just a novelty." "the story definitely sucked them in. But then, you know, it kept their attention long enough to realize that, you know, this band might have some potential to, you know, actually make great music and be something more than just a novelty." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KyvYpggZXiU
**Band Name Honor** : The "Leon" in the band's name is in honor of their grandfather, Leon Followill, who passed away in 2014. "The Leon in their name is in honor of grandfather, Leon Falowell, who died in 2014." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gxbuajGKbko
**Band Name Origin (Grandfather Leon)** : Kings of Leon is named in honor of the band members' grandfather, Leon. Coincidentally, their father, Ivan, also shares the middle name Leon and, due to the band's success, has adopted and proudly embraced it as his own identity. "But we named it after our grandfather because we're all, you know, his grandsons." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Career-Threatening Fire on 9/11/2001** : On September 11th, 2001, while in San Diego to perform for radio and TV programmers, Kings of Leon inadvertently caused a fire that nearly destroyed a penthouse suite. After playing a requested gig to help programmers cope with the shock of the 9/11 attacks, Nathan Followill placed a cigarette butt in an ashtray on a balcony covered with flammable astroturf. A gust of wind blew the cigarette onto the turf, igniting a fire that led to the fire department breaking down the door and extensively damaging the suite. "It happened on September 11th. The September 11th. Really? Oh yes. We were in San Diego playing a singing for a bunch of radio and TV programmers and stuff like that." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Childhood Education and Residency** : For the majority of their early lives, the Followill brothers were homeschooled, adapting to their family's itinerant lifestyle. However, there was a period when they settled down in Jackson, Tennessee, where they were briefly enrolled in a parochial school. "For the most part, the brothers were homeschooled, although for a while they were able to settle down in Jackson, Tennessee where they were enrolled in a Perrokerial school." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Dream Collaboration with Radiohead** : Nathan Followell expressed a strong desire to collaborate with Radiohead, envisioning an "experimental" blend of Kings of Leon's "Southern country boy thing" with Radiohead's contrasting musical style to see what unique outcome would emerge. "Probably, uh... Radio Had a lot. I'd be fine just to get freaky, experimental with those guys." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KyvYpggZXiU
**Early Band Formation and Improvised Lineup (2001)** : In 2001, after Nathan and Caleb signed a record deal, the label sought to assemble a band for them. The brothers proposed their younger brother, Jared, then 15, play bass despite his lack of experience, and their cousin, Matthew, play guitar, based on a few lessons he had taken. The label, amused by this unconventional proposal, challenged them by providing a Led Zeppelin box set. The four then spent two weeks in their mother's garage, emerging with five songs that thoroughly impressed the label and secured their blessing to proceed. "We signed our deal in 2001, me and Caleb. Then they were trying to help us put our band together. We were like, we'll just buy a little brother a bass... We'll just, you know, we'll let him be the guitar player." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Early European Success** : Kings of Leon experienced immediate significant success in Europe and the UK, being "big from day one" there. This phenomenon was partly attributed to a fascination with their unique backstory as preacher sons engaging in rock and roll, contrasting with their slower reception in their native American South, which was "one of the last places to get it." "over there we were big from day one over there. ...I think they were kind of fascinated with the story. The way we were raised and how the whole good versus evil we grew up. Preacher sons and now we're out doing stuff that preach our sons shouldn't do." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KyvYpggZXiU
**Family Intervention for Well-being** : After returning home from a tour, the band's mother became visibly distressed by their appearance, noting they were "super skinny and pale" from excessive activities, which prompted the band to reflect and decide to "chill out." This led to a period of focusing on music and making their third album, "Because of the Times," in Nashville to avoid the distractions found in places like Los Angeles. "our mom busted out crying when she saw us. We were all super skinny and pale and been doing way too much stuff that we shouldn't have been doing and she was like, I don't even recognize my boys." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KyvYpggZXiU
**Father's Secret Rock and Roll** : When their mother was asleep during long drives to revivals, their father, Ivan, would listen to rock artists like The Rolling Stones and Neil Young, or whatever was playing on the AM radio late at night. These moments were cherished memories for Nathan, who would stay awake with his dad to keep him company and listen to music. "But dad liked to rock. He would put on the stones and kneel young and the car when mom wasn't there, right? Yeah. When she had fallen asleep, he would. And how it was actually my best memories was when everyone was asleep and I would stay up with my dad and we'd listen to music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gxbuajGKbko

**First Gig Embarrassment** : Kings of Leon's debut performance was opening for Billy Joe Shaver, playing to an audience primarily comprised of "cowboy hats" expecting Old Western music. The band performed behind a curtain that would open for their set and close upon their final chord. After their performance, they celebrated backstage with high-fives, hugs, and tears, only to discover that the curtain was see-through, meaning the entire audience had witnessed their emotional post-set reactions. "And we realized that the curtain was see through. So the whole place saw us high five and then like hugging and crying." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Five-Syllable Album Title Rule** : Kings of Leon maintain a unique and consistent rule where every album title they release must consist of exactly five syllables, a detail exemplified by their album "Because of the Times." "That's the Kings of Leon rule for every album title. It must have five syllables." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KyvYpggZXiU
**Goal for Remix and Cover Publishing** : The band aims to be the first to earn more publishing money from remixes and covers of their songs than from their original compositions. This strategy also serves a practical purpose, allowing them to play their own music at parties by using remixes rather than the original versions. "We're going to try to be the first band that makes more publishing money off of. Remixes and covers. Then we actually did the original songs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KyvYpggZXiU
**Influence from The Strokes** : Kings of Leon, a band consisting of brothers from Nashville, learned to play their instruments by "aping" The Strokes' debut album, "Is This It." This demonstrates the significant impact of The Strokes on other emerging artists. "The guys in Kings of Leon learned to play by aping that record." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jEfofyobGiI
**Label Patience and Development** : The band was afforded an unusual amount of time and freedom by their record label to develop their sound and fan base, avoiding the typical pressure placed on new acts to achieve immediate commercial success. This patience proved beneficial, allowing them to build a sustainable career and eventually play large venues on their fourth album. The label's willingness to wait was partly due to guaranteed sales of 500,000 units in the UK and Australia. "The label didn't panic or threaten them or otherwise freak out. They just let things happen. ...they didn't want to drop us from the label when they knew they were guaranteed 500,000 sold in the UK and Australia." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KyvYpggZXiU
**Lack of Formal Musical Training and Technical Knowledge** : The members of Kings of Leon openly admit to a striking lack of technical knowledge about their instruments, despite their proficiency as performers. Nathan Followill, for instance, requires his drum tech to create "cheat sheets" detailing the sizes, types, and heads of his drums for magazine interviews because he has "literally no idea." The band sees rock music as an art form where sometimes less technical knowledge can foster creativity, and they consider themselves "probably the least technical band in the world to ever reach this level." "I probably only band in the world that they do a special on him for bass magazine or guitar or drum magazine that we have to have our texts there with us that give us cheat sheets." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Members' Real Names** : The Followill brothers and their cousin all use their middle names professionally. Nathan was originally named after his father, Ivan. Caleb's first name is Anthony. Jared's official driver's license name is Michael Jared Falwell. Matthew's full name is Cameron Matthew Followill. "Nathan was actually named after Ivan, his father. Caleb's first name is Anthony. Jared's driver's license reads Michael Jared Falwell, and we'll get to Matthew in just a minute." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Musical Growth Over Time** : The band's sound evolved naturally from one record to the next, a direct result of their personal growth and changing musical influences, rather than a conscious effort for drastic change. Jared Followell, for example, grew from 15 years old on the first record to 21 on the fourth, naturally impacting their style. "It was just us growing musically. ...we've changed as people and musicians and our musical influences have changed." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KyvYpggZXiU
**Nomadic, Pentecostal Upbringing** : The Followill brothers and their cousin were raised in a strict, hardcore Pentecostal family, with their father serving as a preacher. The family, including their mother Betty Ann, lived a nomadic existence, traveling across the American South in an old Chrysler as their father delivered sermons. Due to a lack of funds for hotels, the family frequently camped out between services. The boys gained their foundational musical skills by singing gospel songs and playing drums during these church services. "The boys were brought up in a hardcore Pentecostal family. Dad was a preacher, and he, mother Betty Ann and the boys were pretty nomadic for a while." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Observation of Religious Radio Scams** : The band members, reflecting on their experiences with religious broadcasts, recounted observations of various individuals on the radio attempting to sell dubious items. Nathan Followill specifically recalled an incident where a preacher was trying to sell "the tears from Jesus that he wept on the cross" for $3,999, with people actually purchasing them. "I actually saw one guy at Hellbangeless one time that was actually trying to sell the tears from Jesus that he wept on the cross, like the most ridiculous like. And there were people that were literally for $3,999 you could actually get." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Pentecostal Minister Father and Road Revivals** : The Followill brothers' father, Ivan, was a "fire and brimstone preacher" who homeschooled his sons and took them on the road to revivals across the US South. This upbringing was a significant part of their early life. "Ivan, the dad, was a fire in Brimstone preacher who bundled his homeschooled sons in a car and took them on the road to revivals across the US south." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gxbuajGKbko
**Performance Over Technicality** : Despite their confessed lack of technical understanding of their instruments, Kings of Leon emphasizes their ability to perform live. They assert that the "magic happens" when they are on stage with instruments in hand, contrasting with their admitted status as "the least technical band in the world" to achieve their level of success. They also rarely soundcheck, with the first chord of the show often being the very first they play. "But you stick an instrument in our hands and stick us on the stage and we'll, you know, that's when the magic happens." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Pre-Music Career Paths** : Before Kings of Leon formed, the members had various paths. Nathan Followill was the only one to graduate from high school. Caleb Followill left school in grade 11 to pursue work as a construction worker, but he later quit this job to fully commit to music. Jared Followill, the youngest brother, "just tagged along" during the band's formation, becoming the bassist by default. "Nathan, being the oldest, graduated from high school first, Caleb bailed on school in grade 11 to become a construction worker. But then once he got deeper into music, he quit that too." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Preacher Father's Secret Rock Tastes** : Although their preacher father was staunchly "anti-rock and roll" and had been a "hippie pothead" who later came "full circle," he occasionally indulged in rock music. During long drives, particularly when their mother was asleep around 3 AM, he would tune into AM radio stations playing artists like The Rolling Stones or Jimi Hendrix. He sometimes shared anecdotes about his past, including stories of "smoking pot" with his friends. "Dad liked to rock. I mean, he would put on the stones and deal young and the car with mum wasn't there, right?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Preacher Father's Unique Party-Ending Tactic** : The band's preacher father, despite his conservative background, was remarkably lenient, allowing his sons to host parties at their home. However, when he decided the festivities had run their course and it was time for guests to depart, he employed an unusual method: he would load jalapeno peppers into the microwave, turn it on high, and effectively "tear gas" everyone out of the house. "He'd load up the microwave with jalapeno peppers, turn it on high and kind of tear gas everybody out of the place." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Preference for Diverse Remixers** : Kings of Leon typically select remixes that are "completely different" from the original versions and are usually created by artists who are not primarily associated with rock bands. This preference for non-rock artists ensures a fresh and unique interpretation of their music. "we usually only choose the ones that are completely different. And usually it's someone that isn't, you know, in a rock band or anything like that, we kind of want something different." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KyvYpggZXiU
**Record Label's Strategic Patience** : Kings of Leon attribute their ability to develop as a band over five years, one EP, and four albums to their record label's patience. They suggest the label allowed this development because of the band's inconsistent but notable success, achieving "surprisingly successful" sales of 500,000 units in markets like the UK and Australia, which made the label reluctant to drop them despite being "under the radar" elsewhere. "They kind of had to let us develop because they didn't want to drop us from the label when they knew they were guaranteed 500,000 sold in the UK and Australia and all this." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Surprise UK Magazine Tour** : During the recording of their first album, Kings of Leon's label abruptly booked them on a UK tour sponsored by a prominent magazine, featuring "all the hottest bands." Despite having only played ten shows as a band and being "scared to death" to perform alongside older, more experienced musicians, their first UK show and all subsequent dates were sold out. This experience provided Caleb Followill with his "first taste of the lifestyle" of touring, inspiring him to write more songs upon his return. "The tour that they had booked this on, it was like this magazine had this tour in the UK and it was all the hottest bands." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**U2's Mentorship on Tour** : While on tour with U2, Kings of Leon received invaluable guidance on the intricacies of the "business side of touring," including lessons on lighting and creating a distinctive stage show. Jared Followill noted U2's meticulous attention to detail, such as washing lights before every performance. This contrasted sharply with Kings of Leon's own practice of often foregoing soundchecks, with their first chord of a show often being their initial chord of the evening. **Unexpected Early International Success** : Contrary to their initial expectations, Kings of Leon's first significant success came not in North America, but predominantly in the UK and Europe, which was unusual for a band originating from Tennessee. The band had believed their sound would first resonate in the American South, only to find the South was "one of the last places to get it," while audiences in places like Ireland were already singing their songs. "Your first seat of success, it wasn't in America, it wasn't in North America, it was in the UK and Europe, which is unexpected for a band from Tennessee." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BbdnSjm0Wb4
**Use of Middle Names** : All members of Kings of Leon use their second names in the band; for instance, Nathan's birth name is Ivan, Caleb's is Anthony, Jared's is Michael, and Matthew's is Cameron.
