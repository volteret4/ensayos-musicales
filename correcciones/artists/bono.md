## songs
**Beautiful Day** : In recent U2 concerts (over the last 15 years), this song is often performed in a lower key due to Bono's vocal adaptations. Depending on the night, he might sing the chorus with less gusto and in a lower range, relying on The Edge's background vocals for added impact. "if you have seen you two in concert over the last, I don't know, 15 years or so, you may notice that a song like this is performed in a lower key." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_YjwRApoJhA

## curiosities
**Alleged Quit Threat Post-Live Aid (July 13, 1985)** : Following a heated argument backstage after U2's Live Aid set, Bono allegedly told the band, "fine, I quit," expressing his frustration with the situation. "Emotions got out of control, and Bono allegedly said, fine, I quit." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2RKchFW42Jc
**Character Witness for Peter Buck (April 5, 2002)** : Bono testified as a character witness for R.E.M. guitarist Peter Buck during his second trial on charges of air rage. Buck was eventually acquitted, largely due to a medical defense of "non-insane automatism." "Bono of you two testifying as a character witness." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2RKchFW42Jc
**Cited as a Rock Poet** : Bono is listed as one of the rare "poets in rock," alongside other prominent lyricists. "There's Dylan, Springsteen, Patty Smith, Jim Morrison, Bono, certainly, maybe Lou Reed." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dnz_Ycd_q7s
**Grandstanding at Live Aid (July 13, 1985)** : During U2's performance of "Bad" at Live Aid, Bono engaged in an extended moment trying to pull a dance partner from the audience, consuming many minutes of their allotted time. This "grandstanding" caused the band to miss playing their biggest hit, "Pride (In the Name of Love)," and led to a major backstage confrontation. "Donald decided he needed a dance partner and spent many long minutes trying to pull somebody out of the audience." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2RKchFW42Jc
**Jamaican Shooting Incident (1996)** : On January 16, 1996, Bono, his family, and friends including Jimmy Buffett and Island Records head Chris Blackwell, were taxing on the tarmac at Negril airport in Jamaica aboard Buffett's Grumman HU-16C plane, "Hemisphere Dancer," with Buffett at the controls. Jamaican police mistakenly believed the aircraft was a drug runner's plane and opened fire on it. Bono described the terrifying event as being "in the middle of a James Bond movie," and while everyone was safe, he and his family cut their holiday short, spending the rest of their time in Miami, with the Jamaican government issuing a full apology. "Those boys were shooting all over the place. I felt as if we were in the middle of a James Bond movie. Only this was real." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hmnMG6f80Sg
**Learjet Cargo Hatch Detachment (2014)** : On November 13, 2014, while Bono was on a two-hour flight from Dublin to Berlin's Schönefeld Airport aboard a private Learjet 60, the rear cargo hatch of the plane became detached as it approached the German coast. Passengers heard a "big thud," and at 12:15 AM, 11 minutes before touchdown, the door blew off entirely. Upon landing, two suitcases were found missing, and a piece of the door was later discovered in the Brandenburg area. "As the plane approached the German coast, the rear cargo hatch of the plane became detached." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hmnMG6f80Sg
**Rare Trapdoor Spider Species** : A trapdoor spider, *Apthostica's Bonoai*, was named after Bono. This species is exceptionally rare, with only a single male and female pair ever discovered, and it is native to the Joshua Tree National Forest, establishing a direct link to U2's famous album. "It is called Apthostica's Bonoai. After Bono, of course, in the whole Joshua Tree Island." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VMPLplhDk8I
**Recognition of "Bombast" in Arcade Fire** : Bono, lead singer of U2, recognized a similar kind of "bombast" in Arcade Fire's music and artistry that he believed had contributed to U2's own early fame. "Same with Bono, who sees in them the same kind of bombass that made you two famous back in the day." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ir2FfLQJNtY
**Reconsideration and Apology After Live Aid (July 13, 1985)** : After allegedly quitting U2 following Live Aid, Bono flew back to Dublin alone and took a long drive to calm down. During this drive, he reportedly picked up a female hitchhiker who recognized him and praised U2's performance. This conversation prompted Bono to reconsider his decision, leading him to apologize to the other band members in Dublin and ask to be allowed back into the group. "This conversation made Bono think that maybe he had been a little hasty in quitting." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2RKchFW42Jc
**Spiritual Advisor on Tour** : Bono frequently toured with his own full-time priest and spiritual advisor, Reverend Jack Heeslip. Heeslip initially met U2 when he was a teacher and counselor at Mount Temple High School in Dublin, where the band members were students. Reverend Jack passed away in 2015. "Bono often used to tour with his own full-time priest and spiritual advisor, a guy named Reverend Jack Heeslip." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aSk1XQHNkd8
**Unique Award Nominations Achievement** : Bono holds the distinction of being the only individual to have been nominated for a Grammy, a Golden Globe, an Oscar, and a Nobel Peace Prize, recognizing his wide-ranging achievements in music, film, and humanitarian efforts. "Can you name the only person to be nominated for a Grammy, a Golden Globe, an Oscar and Nobel Peace Prize? Bono." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GO0K_N7Ys7I
**Vocal Preservation** : Bono is noted for doing an excellent job maintaining his voice in shape and being very careful with his instrument. Despite this, he has adapted his live performances of U2 songs in recent years, often lowering the key of songs like "Beautiful Day" to accommodate vocal changes. "Bono has done an excellent job in keeping his voice in shape. He's very careful with his instrument." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_YjwRApoJhA
