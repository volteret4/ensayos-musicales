## curiosities
**Frontman of House of Freaks** : Brian Harvey was a musician from Virginia who started in Powerpop and became the frontman and guitarist for House of Freaks, a notable two-man band in the 1980s. After House of Freaks disbanded, he was involved in another project called Gutter Ball. "He eventually landed a role as a frontman in guitars for House of Freaks, one of the first two man bands we ever saw back in the 1980s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q6dAX6HCjgw
**Killed in Richmond Spree Murders** : Brian Harvey and his family were among the victims of the Richmond Spree murders in January 2006, perpetrated by Ricky Gray, Ray Dandridge, and Ashley Baskerville. On January 1, 2006, police found Brian, his wife Kathy, their four-year-old daughter Ruby May, and nine-year-old daughter Stella Ann, bound and gagged in the basement of their burning house. Brian and Kathy had their throats cut and were bludgeoned with a claw hammer, Ruby was stabbed to death, and Stella Ann died of smoke inhalation. "First responders found Brian Harvey and his family, his wife Kathy, four-year-old daughter Ruby May, and non-year-old daughter Stella Ann, bound and gagged with packing tape in the basement of this house that was on fire." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q6dAX6HCjgw
**Memorial Endowment and Tributes** : In the wake of the Richmond Spree murders, the Brian and Catherine Harvey Memorial Endowment was established, providing funds for study in the arts. Several songs have also been written about the Harvey family, including "Two Daughters and a Beautiful Wife" by Drive-By Truckers. "In the wake of all this, is the Brian and Catherine Harvey Memorial Endowment, which provides funds for study in the arts." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q6dAX6HCjgw
