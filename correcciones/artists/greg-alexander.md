## curiosities
**Early Musical Development and Unsuccessful Record Deal** : Raised as a Jehovah's Witness in Detroit, Greg Alexander taught himself piano by age 12 and began writing songs with his sister. By 16, he secured his first major label recording contract, though this initial venture ultimately "nothing happened." "Greg Alexander was a Jehovah's Witness kid raised new Detroit. By the time he was 12, he taught himself to play the piano and started writing songs with his sister. By 16, he had his first major label recording contract, which was nice, but nothing happened." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dJiSZ4X0F2g
**Formation of The New Radicals as a Primary Vehicle** : In 1997, Greg Alexander formed The New Radicals, which operated more as a project for him than a traditional band, with Alexander as the only permanent member alongside collaborator Daniel Brisebois. This structure allowed him to be the principal creative force. "In 1997, he formed a band he called the new Radicals, and band is a bit of a misnomer since he really was the only permanent member, along with a collaborator named Daniel Brizbois." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dJiSZ4X0F2g
**Transition to Highly Sought-After Mainstream Songwriter** : Despite The New Radicals being known for a single major hit, Greg Alexander successfully leveraged that into a long-term career as a highly in-demand and well-paid mainstream songwriter. He has penned songs for numerous prominent artists, including Santana, Enrique Iglesias, Geri Halliwell, Rod Stewart, Kaiser Chiefs, and The Struts, sometimes using pseudonyms. "Well, that's because he's gone on to write songs for Santana and Enrique Glesias and Jerry Hallwell and Rod Stewart and Kaiser Chiefs and the Struts and many others, sometimes under his own name, sometimes under a pseudonym." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dJiSZ4X0F2g
