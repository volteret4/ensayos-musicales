## songs
**Do They Know It's Christmas? (1984) – Chart Success and Sales** : Released on December 7, 1984, the charity single "Do They Know It's Christmas?" quickly achieved immense success. It entered the UK charts at number one on December 15th and held that position for five weeks, selling over three million copies in the UK alone, making it one of the biggest-selling UK singles ever. Additionally, it sold one million copies in the US. This commercial success marked the significant beginning of Band-Aid as a major charitable endeavor. "On December the 15th, it entered the charts at number one, and it stayed there for five weeks, selling more than three million copies, making it one of the biggest selling UK singles of all time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Somq2MoRi5Q
**Do They Know It's Christmas? (1984) – Songwriting Collaboration** : The lyrics for the Band-Aid charity single "Do They Know It's Christmas?" were notably written by Bob Geldof in the back of a taxi. Midge Ure of Ultravox then took one of Geldof's half-finished songs, originally titled "It's My World," and composed the music, completing the collaborative effort. ## curiosities
**Initial Idea for a Benefit Record** : The day after being deeply affected by the BBC documentary on the Ethiopian famine, Bob Geldof tentatively proposed the idea of a benefit record for Ethiopia at his record company's office. The suggestion was well-received by those present, setting the stage for the Band-Aid project. "he tentatively suggested that someone ought to put out a record for the Ethiopians. A benefit thing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Somq2MoRi5Q
**Legacy and Impact of Band-Aid** : The Band-Aid project, initiated by the "Do They Know It's Christmas?" single, generated significant funds for charity and inspired further philanthropic efforts in the music world. Its success led to the monumental Live Aid concerts the following July, as well as other charity singles from Canada and the US. More broadly, it fostered a renewed sense that rock and roll possessed a conscience and that music could genuinely effect positive change in the world. "The money raised by the band-aid single, the live-aid concerts the following July, the other charity singles from Canada and the US, and most of all the sense that Rock and Roll did have a conscience and that music could make a difference." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Somq2MoRi5Q
**Recording "Do They Know It's Christmas?" (November 25, 1984)** : Just two weeks after Bob Geldof saw the BBC documentary on the Ethiopian famine, 25 of Britain's most renowned musicians assembled at Sarm Studios in London's Notting Hill area on November 25, 1984. They gathered to record the charity single "Do They Know It's Christmas?", which marked the official beginning of the Band-Aid project. "On November the 25th, 1984, just two weeks after that BBC broadcast, 25 of Britain's most famous musicians gathered at Sarm studios in the Notting Hill area of London to crank out a song that was now called Do They Know It's Christmas?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Somq2MoRi5Q
**Recruitment of Musicians for Band-Aid** : Bob Geldof initiated the Band-Aid project by reaching out to prominent musicians to create a charity single. His first call was to Midge Ure of Ultravox, followed by Sting of The Police, and Simon Le Bon of Duran Duran. As word spread, other notable artists such as Bono, David Bowie, and Paul Weller of The Jam, enthusiastically joined the effort, forming a supergroup of British talent.
