## albums
**666 (Live Album/DVD)** : This live album and DVD features material from the Rock am Ring Festival, a large event held at the Nürburgring in Germany, which attracts over 50,000 people. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ocMHRLQMQY0
**Afraid of Heights (2016)** : This fifth album was released after a four-year break, during a period (2012-2013) when rock bands were struggling to make waves amidst the dominance of indie rock, characterized by bands like Mumford & Sons. The band expressed initial uncertainty about how the record would be received by Canadian rock stations. Its themes touch upon global political and social realities like Brexit, the refugee crisis, the rise of the right, and Donald Trump. The band views it not as a "political record" but a reflection of current global realities affecting people they know. Drummer Aaron was unable to perform on this album due to an MS relapse, with Jordan Hastings filling in. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ocMHRLQMQY0
**Billy Talent III (2009)** : This was the band's third studio album, produced by Brendan O'Brien. The production involved spontaneous additions like xylophones and background vocals, which O'Brien would improvise and tuck into the mix. This approach taught the band that not everything needs to be perfectly calculated during recording. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ocMHRLQMQY0
**Dead Silence (2012)** : The band's fourth album, released on September 11, 2012. It marked a departure from the band's previous numbered album titles, a decision made due to a desire to shift interview focus from numbering to the music itself. The album debuted at number two on the charts, behind all of Michael Jackson's albums, who had passed away that same week. For this album, the band collaborated with artist Kent Taylor for the artwork, reflecting their commitment to physical art accompanying their releases. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ocMHRLQMQY0
**First Live Album (2006)** : In 2006, Billy Talent released their first live album, a two-CD set recorded during two performances in England: London on September 8th and Manchester on September 16th. These raw, 75-minute sets were sold at concerts and online. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ocMHRLQMQY0

## curiosities
**Aaron Solowoniuk's MS Diagnosis (1998)** : Drummer Aaron Solowoniuk was diagnosed with Multiple Sclerosis (MS) in 1998 at the age of 24. His initial symptoms included buzzing legs, difficulty walking to his job at Chrysler, optic neuritis in his left eye (leading to vision loss), and confusion. He managed the disease for over a decade with medication, needle therapy, and a positive outlook. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ocMHRLQMQY0
**Aaron Solowoniuk's MS Relapse and Album Absence (2016)** : On January 15, 2016, Aaron announced on YouTube that he was experiencing an MS relapse, which had been slowing him down for six to seven months. The symptoms made it difficult to perform daily tasks and play drums. He decided to step away from recording drums for the "Afraid of Heights" album to focus on his health, as he was unsure how long his recovery would take. The band fully supported his decision and brought in Jordan Hastings as a fill-in drummer. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ocMHRLQMQY0
**Aaron Solowoniuk's Treatment and Recovery** : After his relapse, Aaron decided to stop taking his MS medication for three months and explore alternative therapies, focusing on healthy eating and exercise. He expressed a goal to come back stronger, listening to his body more carefully, after having previously pushed himself too hard, to the point of walking with a cane. In 2017, he made a return to the stage, playing one song in Poland, his first performance in a year. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ocMHRLQMQY0
**Band Formation and Longevity (1993-present)** : Billy Talent formed in 1993, with the same four core members staying together since then. This long-standing lineup is considered an exception in the music industry, comparable to bands like Radiohead, U2 (since 1978), and ZZ Top (since 1969). The band members feel their identity is deeply intertwined with the band, having started at a young age, around 17. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ocMHRLQMQY0
**Band Support for Aaron Solowoniuk and FUMS (2016)** : Billy Talent, their management, and their record label (Warner Music Canada) are 100% supportive of Aaron in his struggle with MS. They established a charity called FUMS (Fuck U MS) to raise money for MS research. At a music festival on May 29, 2016, a band member (Ben Kowalewicz) addressed the crowd, expressing the band's love, care, and commitment to Aaron, and thanking Jordan Hastings for filling in. The speaker referred to Aaron as "Mr. A. S. Lua," which appears to be a mis-transcription of his full name, Aaron Solowoniuk. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ocMHRLQMQY0
