## albums
**The Eraser (Year not specified, but likely 2006)** : This solo album was constructed from small musical ideas and sounds Yorke had collected on his laptop during various writing sessions, including moments while on tour with Radiohead in hotel rooms. He and Radiohead producer Nigel Godrich sorted these accumulated bits, arranged them into pieces, and then Yorke wrote melodies, lyrics, and added vocals, with some drums and guitar later. The album was primarily computer-generated and minimalist, but performed well commercially. "The eraser album was constructed from bits and pieces gathered off the floor during various writing sessions." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-f9jhqAHkQo

## songs
**The Eraser (Year not specified)** : This track is the title track of Thom Yorke's solo album, which he created during a sabbatical from his primary role as the singer of Radiohead. ## curiosities
**Involvement in Radiohead Website** : Yorke was heavily involved in Radiohead's official website, indicating his broader engagement beyond just musical creation. "Oh, and did I mention his heavy involvement in Radiohead's official website?" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-f9jhqAHkQo
**Restlessness and Work Ethic** : Yorke is described as a workaholic who constantly needs to make music. When Radiohead took time between albums after their record deal ended, he used this period to work on his solo material. "He's one of those guys who always has to keep making music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-f9jhqAHkQo
**Stage Fright and Writer's Block** : As a member of Radiohead, Yorke faced significant obligations and pressures which occasionally led to paralyzing cases of stage fright and writer's block. "As a member of Radiohead, he had a lot of different obligations and pressures, some of which resulted in occasionally paralyzing cases of stage fright and writer's block." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-f9jhqAHkQo
**Twilight Soundtracks Contribution** : Thom Yorke contributed to the various *Twilight* soundtracks that accompanied the film series. These soundtracks featured a nice selection of tunes, mostly album cuts and outtakes, from a range of artists spanning different genres. "The Paramore, Lincoln Park, Collective Soul, Death Cab for Cutie, Tom York, the Killers, Editors, Metric, Block Keys, Dead Weather, Beck, Vampire Weekend, Florence in the Machine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zIkjd1OjCXM
