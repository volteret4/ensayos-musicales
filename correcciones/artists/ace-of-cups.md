## curiosities
**All-Female San Francisco Group** : Ace of Cups was an all-female group formed in San Francisco in 1967, featuring Diane Metilige on drums. They were noted for opening for Jimi Hendrix and maintaining friendships with prominent bands like Jefferson Airplane and The Grateful Dead, marking their presence in the late 1960s psychedelic rock scene. "They were at all-female group formed in 1967 and featured die-end metallige on drums." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9GHpC0kCgHY
**Breakup Due to Sexism (1972)** : Despite their talent and early recognition, the band broke up by 1972, attributed to the re-emergence of old sexist attitudes in the music industry that dismissed the idea of female rock bands. "The Ace of Cups gave it a good shot, but by 1972, they'd broken up." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI
**Early Female Rock Band (1967)** : An all-female rock band from San Francisco that wrote their own songs, had a frontwoman who could "shred like a man," and a female drummer. This lineup was considered radical even in 1967, challenging prevailing norms about women in rock music. "An all-girl rock band? A bunch of women who wrote their own songs, went a checkout front that could shred like a man and a girl playing drums? Even in 1967, this was radical." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI
**Jimmy Hendrix's Support** : Jimi Hendrix was greatly impressed by the band's musical talent and invited them to open for a couple of his shows, signaling a significant early endorsement for the all-female group.
