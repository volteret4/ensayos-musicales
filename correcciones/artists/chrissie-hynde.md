**Early Punk Scene Figure and Guitar Teacher** : Chrissie Hynde was an American living in London at the heart of the burgeoning punk rock scene. She was a more skilled guitarist than many of her peers, even providing lessons to novices like Steve Jones and Sid Vicious of the Sex Pistols. Her rhythm guitar style was heavily influenced by James Brown's band. "She gave lessons to novices like Steve Jones and Sid Vicious of the Sex Pistols." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hbNJQ6OYmd0
**Founding The Pretenders – Lead Woman in Rock** : After several attempts to form a band, including discussions with Mick Jones before he formed The Clash, Chrissie Hynde founded The Pretenders in 1978. She was unique in late 1970s rock as a woman who not only sang lead and fronted the band but also played rhythm guitar and wrote most of their music, thriving in the receptive British punk environment. "Chrissy formed the pretenders in 1978. Here we have a woman singing lead, acting as front person and spokesperson and playing with in guitar. Not exactly a common thing in rock in the late 1970s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hbNJQ6OYmd0

**Signature Telecaster Model (2021)** : In 2021, Chrissie Hynde became one of the few female guitarists to receive her own signature model, an ice-blue Telecaster. This guitar was based on a 1965 version she purchased in New York City in 1981, and its characteristic chiming chords are integral to her sound. **Songwriting Philosophy** : Hynde's approach to songwriting focused on the intriguing idea of crafting the best melodies over the fewest possible chord changes, emphasizing melodic strength and simplicity. "And when it came to songwriting, she became intrigued at how some of the best melodies could be written over the top of the fewest number of chord changes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hbNJQ6OYmd0
