## songs
**Friday I'm in Love** : This song highlights Robert Smith's impressive vocal range, showcasing both very high notes, reaching a D6, and very low notes, descending to a D3. This demonstrates his experimentation with different vocal styles throughout his career, from punk to a softer approach, and later an agonized lover persona with yelping falsetto and low growls. "He reaches a very high D, a D6, if you want to get technical in Friday, I'm in love, the same song in which he goes down for a D3." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7_ZmZxnSOnE
**Friday I'm in Love (1992)** : This song became a top 40 hit in 1992, marking The Cure as part of an exclusive group of alt-rock bands that achieved commercial success. Its happy tone served as an entry point for many new listeners, who then explored the band's deeper, sadder Goth material, helping bring Goth to the forefront during the alternative 90s. "The Cure, formerly a pure goth band with her top 40 hit from 1992, Friday I'm in love, making them part of that exclusive group of alt rock bands with commercial success." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Killing an Arab (1979) – Banned Despite Anti-Racist Intent, Based on Camus Novel** : "Killing an Arab" caused riots and bans due to its title, despite the fact that the song was directly based on Albert Camus's novel *The Stranger* and carried an explicitly anti-racist message. The band received almost no financial compensation from the song due to contractual problems, meaning they suffered the controversy without any economic benefit. "Based on Camus's 'The Stranger' and conveying an anti-racist message, the band received almost no economic benefit due to contractual issues." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f4TOWLYRnGs
**Killing an Arab (1979) – French Promo with "Foxy Lady"** : A 7-inch single released in early 1979 for "Killing an Arab." While the A-side was the same as the UK release, approximately 100 French promo copies featured a cover of the Jimi Hendrix classic "Foxy Lady" on the B-side, instead of the UK's "10:15 Saturday Night." These rare promo copies are valued at around $2,000. Michael Dempsey, The Cure's original bass player, handled lead vocals on this particular "Foxy Lady" recording, marking one of the very few instances where someone other than Robert Smith sang lead. "In 1979, a new band called the Cure released a 7-inch in France for killing an Arab on the A side, just like they did in the UK." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tbmssCZ05ro
