## curiosities
**"Lady Bo" Nickname and Relationship with Bo Diddley** : Peggy Jones earned the nickname "Lady Bo" due to her close musical partnership with Bo Diddley, which led many to mistakenly assume they were a couple. In reality, she was married to the band's bass player. "Peggy was also known as Lady Bo because she and Bo were so tight that everybody just assumed that they were a couple. Not true. She was actually married to the band's bass player." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hbNJQ6OYmd0
**Departure for The Jewels** : Peggy Jones contributed to many Bo Diddley records until approximately 1961, when she left the band to dedicate her time to her own group, The Jewels. "Peggy ended up on a lot of Bo Didley records until about 1961 before she left a spend time with her own band called The Jewels." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hbNJQ6OYmd0
**One of the First Female Rock Guitarists** : Peggy Jones joined Bo Diddley's band as a guitarist in 1957 and is recognized as one of the first female rock guitarists in a famous rock band. She later became known for her experimental use of guitar synthesizers, a rare application in R&B music at the time. "This Black woman was recognized as one of the first female rock guitarists in a famous rock band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
**Pioneer of Guitar Synthesizers in R&B** : Peggy Jones distinguished herself through her experiments with guitar synthesizers, which was an uncommon practice in R&B music when she was using them. This innovative approach highlighted her forward-thinking musicality beyond her role as a pioneering female rock guitarist. "She'd later be recognized for her experiments with guitar synthesizers and nobody used those in R&B music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
**Pioneering Female Electric Guitarist in Rock** : Peggy Jones, also known as Lady Bo, joined Bo Diddley's band in 1957 as a rhythm guitarist, potentially making her the first woman to be visibly playing electric guitar in a rock band. Her recruitment came after Diddley observed her carrying a guitar case. "And as such, she might have very well been the first woman to be visible as an electric guitarist in a rock band. This was 1957." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hbNJQ6OYmd0
