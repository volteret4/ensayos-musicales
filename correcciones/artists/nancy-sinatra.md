# artist - Nancy Sinatra

## songs
**You Only Live Twice (1967) – Title Theme** : Nancy Sinatra, daughter of legendary singer Frank Sinatra, sang the title theme for the 1967 James Bond film "You Only Live Twice." While a different version of the song became a hit single, her rendition was the one featured in the movie and heard by cinema audiences. **These Boots Are Made For Walking (1966)** : Nancy Sinatra's 1966 hit was featured in one of the most famous Scopatone films. Scopatones were video jukeboxes, weighing 1,500 pounds and capable of playing three dozen 16-millimeter films, most of which were in color and shot exclusively for the machine. **These Boots Are Made for Walkin' (1966)** : This song was part of the loud music bombardment directed at the Branch Davidian cult compound in Waco, Texas, in 1993. It was one of the methods used by authorities to try and break the will of David Koresh and his followers during a 51-day standoff.
