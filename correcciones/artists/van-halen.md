Their sound engineer, Jerry Harvey, invented the technology after drummer Alex Van Halen complained that the "constant on-stage volume was killing his hearing." **Hide Your Sheep Tour Rider (1982-1983) – Brown M&M's Clause** : The band's rider for their 97-show, 10-month tour across six countries in North and South America, included a legendary clause on page 36 (specifically page 40, under "munchies") that stated "absolutely no brown ones" among the M&Ms. This was not a serious demand but a test to ensure that promoters had thoroughly read the entire 50-plus page contract, as failure to do so often led to "fractious relations" due to overlooked logistical and technical requirements. **Inspiration from Eric Clapton, Vivaldi, Bach** : Eddie Van Halen was inspired to play guitar by Eric Clapton and also drew influence from classical composers such as Vivaldi and Bach. He developed his technique by recording Clapton's solos and slowing them down to meticulously copy each note, a method demonstrated with "Crossroads." "Eddie Van Halen has said more than one time that the reason he picked up a guitar in the first place is because of Eric Clapton. Van Halen was also inspired by classical music artists such as Bivaldi and Bach but with Clapton he recorded his solos and slowed them down so he could copy them note for note." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 24 - Loud, Louder, Loudest - 70s Heavy Metal | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-24-loud-louder-loudest-70s-heavy-metal
