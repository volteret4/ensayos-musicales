# artist - Maureen "Moe" Tucker

## instruments
- Bass drum (played on its side)
- Drums
- Mallets
- Marching band drum
- Snare drum
- Timpani
- Tom-toms

## curiosities
**Joining The Velvet Underground** : Moe Tucker joined The Velvet Underground at the age of 19, immediately picking up the sticks for the "weirdo New York band." Her decision to pursue music was driven by the appeal of playing in the band over her previous job as a keypunch operator at IBM. "She didn't pick up the sticks until she was 19 and immediately joined the Vellvets. Playing in this weirdo New York band sounded way more fun than being a key punch operator at IBM." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9GHpC0kCgHY
**Unconventional Drumming Style** : Moe Tucker, the original drummer for The Velvet Underground, developed a distinctive and unconventional drumming style. She played standing up, primarily used mallets instead of sticks, and notably played a bass drum positioned on its side, employing marching band drums or a timpani. Her approach focused on keeping time and providing deep rhythmic backing, which was visually striking and subtle yet mesmerizing on recordings. "Mot wasn't exactly what you would call a conventional rock drummer. First of all, she played standing up. She played mostly with mallets, not sticks. There was a snare drum and a couple of tom-toms, but no symbols. And most striking of all, her bass drum was on its side." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9GHpC0kCgHY
