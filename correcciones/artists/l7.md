## curiosities
**All-Female Grunge Band** : L7 is described as an "all-female Grunge band." Jennifer Finch, formerly of Sugar Baby Doll, became a member of L7. The band is also considered "Riot Girl Adjacent," sharing kinship with the scene but being more polished, less political, and occasionally heavier. "Jennifer Finch ended up in L7, the all-female Grinch band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Qss5kC2FUx0
**Audience Reaction to New Niche** : When L7 formed in 1985, their blend of punky, sludgy rock was a new niche for alt-rock, often resulting in "blank faces" among audiences who weren't sure how to react to a group of fierce women playing such music. "When L7 was formed in 1985, there were a lot of blank faces in the audience. So what do you really know how to deal with a group of punky women playing sludgy rock? It really was a new niche for all rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oLC49nCf0ZM
**DeNita Sparks' On-Stage Act (The Word TV Show)** : DeNita Sparks, known for her fearlessness, performed a controversial act on the British TV show "The Word" by stripping off her clothes from the waist down during a performance, reportedly to make a point. "The most tame one involves the time L7 played a British TV show called The Word. During the performance, she stripped off all her clothes from the waist down just to make some kind of point." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI
**Early Sexism Challenges (1985-1991)** : For their first five or six years, L7, as an all-female band, faced not only the typical struggles of new groups but also heightened indignities due to pervasive sexism within the music industry. "For the next five or six years, they suffered not only the indignities, foisted on all new bands, but the extra indignities brought about by the sexism because, wow, they were an all-girl group." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI
**Fierce Reputation** : Over the years, L7 became known for being as "fierce and occasionally as gross" as any all-male band, developing a reputation that "you did not mess with L7," particularly Donita Sparks. "The word was that you did not mess with L7. Danita in particular" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oLC49nCf0ZM
**Live TV Pants Drop (1992)** : In 1992, Donita Sparks notably dropped her pants during a live television appearance in the UK, an act that added to the band's provocative image. "In that same year, she dropped her pants during a live TV appearance in the UK." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oLC49nCf0ZM
**Participant in First Warped Tour (1995)** : L7 was one of the roughly 20 up-and-coming alternative bands featured on the two stages of the very first Warped Tour in 1995. "For about 20 of them, sublime, deaf tones, L7 and a new group from Orange County called No Doubt." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jfQaUV6Bq4
**Producer Butch Vig's Involvement** : Amidst a surge in demand for aggressive-sounding bands, L7 secured producer Butch Vig, known for his work with Sonic Youth on "Goo" and Nirvana on "Nevermind," to help them develop their signature sound for their third album. "L7 was swept away into the hands of producer Butch Vig, the guy who worked with Kim Gordon and the rest of Sonic Youth on the Goo album, and the dude who had just finished helping Nirvana make never mind." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI
**Reading Festival Incident (1992)** : During the 1992 Reading Festival, L7 was involved in an incident described as "very weird," which garnered attention. "there was a particular incident at the 1992 Reading Festival. They got very weird. You can Google that." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oLC49nCf0ZM
**Rise to Prominence** : L7, an all-female grunge band, gained significant prominence around the same time as Nirvana, establishing themselves as one of the hardest working and hardest rocking female bands of that era. "the Grungi All Female band, that jumped to prominence around the same time as Nirvana. They were one of the hardest working, hardest rocking female bands of the era." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oLC49nCf0ZM
**Support from Progressive Bands** : Their participation in pro-choice events connected them with influential progressive bands like Nirvana, Pearl Jam, Rage Against the Machine, and the Red Hot Chili Peppers. These bands became fans and actively supported L7. "Playing some pro-choice events brought them into contact with progressive liberal bands like Nirvana and Pearl Jam and Rage Against the Machine and the Red Hot Chili Peppers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eo32mAZd8DI
