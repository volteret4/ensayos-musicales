## curiosities
**Influence on Grunge** : Heart was a key influence, alongside Led Zeppelin and Black Sabbath, that crossbred with punk/hardcore in the Pacific Northwest to eventually become Grunge. This unexpected cross-pollination was a result of the unique touring challenges posed by the Rocky Mountains, which made it easier for bands to play local punk shows in the Seattle area than major touring acts. "And that's how it crossbred with Led Zeppelin and Black Sabbath and Hart. To eventually become Grunge, that would not have happened without the Rocky Mountains being in the way." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0qigzi1j81U
**Ann Wilson's Experience with Sexism in Radio** : Ann Wilson recounted that many times in their 20s, they would encounter radio station personnel who would make comments like, "we love your tits." She described this as irritating and any physical touch as "icky, nauseating." She also noted that in the 70s, complaining about such behavior would likely result in her own termination. "Many times in our 20s we'd go into radio stations and they'd say, we love your tits. It was irritating." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 7 - Women Who Rocked the 70s | http://www.ftr70.com
**Breakthrough and Mainstream Success** : Heart's legitimate rock band breakthrough coincided with the late 70s evolution of rock, blowing up in the 1980s with arena rock and power ballads. Their 1985 self-titled album, `Heart`, marked their mainstream success by going to number one and producing four Top 10 hits, despite a shift towards a more sentimental sound. "Hearts breakthrough as a legitimate rock band coincided with this evolution of rock and roll that began to build up steam in the late 70s and totally blew up in the 1980s." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 7 - Women Who Rocked the 70s | http://www.ftr70.com
**Internal Turmoil and Image Makeover** : The band's situation was complicated by internal turmoil, and they felt pressure to undergo a "makeover" to sell records and get radio play in the 1980s. This led to a sound distinct from their earlier classic rock hits. "Hearts situation was not helped by some of the inner turmoil within the band, but they were also in a situation where selling records and getting on the radio, which still went hand in hand in the 80s, required a makeover." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 7 - Women Who Rocked the 70s | http://www.ftr70.com
**Nancy Wilson Collaboration** : Nancy Wilson of Heart was among the notable musicians who joined Taylor Hawkins on his Co-Tale Riders albums. Her contribution added to the project's broad musical scope and impressive roster of guest artists. "So did Nancy Wilson of Heart." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dD-VEcFpIFA ← heart
**Sexualization by Record Label** : Mushroom Records overtly sexualized sisters Ann and Nancy Wilson to promote their album `Dreamboat Annie`. The album cover showed them with bare shoulders standing back to back, and the label took out a full-page ad in Rolling Stone magazine with the caption, "It was only our first time." Ann Wilson later wrote "Barracuda" in response to this exploitation and a suggestive question from a radio promoter. "Mushroom records, which does not exist anymore, was not above completely sexualizing and enancy who are sisters to sell the record." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 7 - Women Who Rocked the 70s | http://www.ftr70.com

The song's lyrics, "You lying so low in the weeds that you're going to ambush me, you'd have me down, down, down to my knees, wouldn't you, Barakuda?" were directed at her record label. It was released on the 1977 album `Lil' Queen` but never became a top 10 hit.
