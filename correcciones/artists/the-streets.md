## albums
**A Grand Don't Come for Free (2004)** : Mike Skinner's second album, released in May 2004, chronicles the everyday activities of a regular British guy experiencing a series of unfortunate events. Each song acts as a chapter in the protagonist's quest to find a mysteriously disappeared thousand pounds. Throughout the narrative, he breaks up with his girlfriend (who is cheating with his friend), struggles with excessive drinking and drug use, quarrels with a TV repairman, and ultimately discovers the missing money hidden inside his TV set. "And it basically tells of the everyday activities of a regular British guy." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KyNY3zv6uc
**A Grand Don't Come for Free (2004) – Concept Album** : This 2004 album by The Streets had a significant impact on Alex Turner, more so than their previous work. It is described as a concept album chronicling the adventures of a protagonist who loses a thousand pounds and spends the entire record attempting to recover it. The lyrics are noted for being observational, conversational, and colloquial. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YvcnHKI4oIw
**Original Pirate Material (2003)** : This 2003 album by The Streets was discovered by Alex Turner. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YvcnHKI4oIw

## curiosities
**Mike Skinner's Lyrical Style** : Mike Skinner, the leader of The Streets, is highly regarded for his observational, conversational, and colloquial lyrical style. His work, particularly on the album "A Grand Don't Come for Free," was a significant influence on Alex Turner's songwriting. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YvcnHKI4oIw
