## songs
**Warm Leatherette (1978)** : This single was composed and recorded by Daniel Miller in his bedroom and became a substantial UK hit, demonstrating the viability of independent, electronically produced music. "Daniel Miller, the solitary member of a band known as The Normal from 1978 with Warm Leatherette, a single that became a substantial UK hit that year, and a song that was composed and recorded in Daniel's bedroom." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
**Warm Leatherette (Year Unspecified) – Bedroom-Recorded Hit** : This single was made by Daniel Miller in his bedroom using inexpensive keyboards. He initially pressed 500 copies and sold them on consignment, which eventually grew to 25,000 copies and generated enough money for him to found Mute Records. "The guy is Daniel Miller. He made that single in his bedroom with some cheap keyboards. He pressed up some singles, sold them on consignment through a bunch of record stores, 500 copies became a thousand and then 5,000 and I've heard his highest 25,000." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1KF5MwJVYng
**Warm Leatherette (single)** : This seven-inch single, released by Daniel Miller's one-man synth band project The Normal, sold 30,000 copies. The profits from this single were reinvested into Mute Records, which Miller had founded to release his own music. "He had a one-man synth band project called The Normal. And the only way he could interest anyone in what he was doing was to do it all himself. So he created mute, struck a deal with rough trade record shops, and ended up selling 30,000 copies of a seven-inch single called Warm Leatherette." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI

## curiosities
**Bedroom Recording Pioneer** : Daniel Miller, the sole member of The Normal, composed and recorded the single "Warm Leatherette" in his bedroom in 1978, showcasing the accessibility of electronic music production. "Daniel Miller, the solitary member of a band known as The Normal from 1978 with Warm Leatherette, a single that became a substantial UK hit that year, and a song that was composed and recorded in Daniel's bedroom." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
**Founding Mute Records** : Using the profits from "Warm Leatherette," Daniel Miller established his own independent record label, Mute, dedicated exclusively to guitar-less music, which later introduced artists like Depeche Mode, Erasure, and Yazoo. "He took the profits and founded his own independent record label devoted entirely to guitar less music. He called the label Mute." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
**Funding Mute Records Expansion** : The money generated from the sale of 30,000 copies of The Normal's seven-inch single, "Warm Leatherette," was reinvested by Daniel Miller into Mute Records, which eventually led to his collaboration with Depeche Mode. "Daniel took the money from that single Warm Leatherette by The Normal and plowed it back into mute, which eventually saw him cross paths with Depeche Mode." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sCtQqVBtCaI
**Independent Sales Success** : After recording "Warm Leatherette" in his bedroom, Miller sold 30,000 copies by placing it for sale on consignment in independent record stores. "And just by putting it for sale on consignment and independent record stores, Daniel sold 30,000 copies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
**Influenced by Bowie and Kraftwerk** : Miller was influenced by the electronic music of David Bowie and Kraftwerk, combining the concept of stylish, futuristic, machine-generated music with punk rock's DIY aesthetic. "Like a number of young British musicians, Miller had been exposed to craft work and the electronic musings of David Bowie." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
