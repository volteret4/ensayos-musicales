**Rumours (1977)** : In a 2007 retrospective, Patrick McKay described *Rumours* as a "safe middle ground" for pop music bands, positioned between punk rock (like Richard Hell) and conventional music. Critic Robert Christgau praised it, noting its role in reminding listeners that "rock and roll is supposed to be about pleasure as well as all the heavy stuff" during the "year of the punk." Conversely, critic Robert Hilburn found its weakest moments to be "a nutritionist nightmare 16 zillion calories and no substance," criticizing its sweet but "unfulfilling" vocals and "muted" guitar work compared to their live performances. Despite some initial critical reservations, it has become one of the best-selling albums of all time, cherished for its "near musical flawlessness" and lyrics that deeply resonated with listeners, particularly "boomers" navigating divorce in the late 1970s. "Patrick McKay wrote in a retrospective review of the album rumors in 2007.
