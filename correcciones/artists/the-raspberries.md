## albums
**Go All the Way (1972) - Gold Single and Power Pop Origin** : This song, released in the summer of 1972, became a global hit and earned the band a gold single. It is recognized as one of the earliest examples of the Power Pop sound that would later influence the world of alt-rock. "Eric Carmen and the raspberries from the summer of 1972 with Go All the Way. That was a global hit for them, and one of the first examples of what we would later call Power Pop in the world of alt-rock." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HBS24NwhrE8

## curiosities
**Distinctive Sound** : The Raspberries differentiated themselves through their unique combination of riff-heavy guitars and catchy pop melodies. "What made them different was their mating of riffig guitars and pop melodies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HBS24NwhrE8
**Formation (1970)** : The band was formed in Cleveland in 1970, initiating their influential run in the music scene. "They were formed in Cleveland in 1970." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HBS24NwhrE8
**Peak Years (1972-1974)** : The band experienced their most successful period between 1972 and 1974, marked by significant hits and critical recognition. "Their best years were between 1972 and 1974." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HBS24NwhrE8
**Power Pop Pioneers** : The Raspberries are widely regarded as a groundbreaking band in the Power Pop genre, with their name being synonymous with the sound. "Ask anyone about Power Pop, and they will talk about the raspberries." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HBS24NwhrE8
**Recording Technique - Limiter Use** : For their punchy mix, particularly on songs like "Go All the Way," the band utilized a prototype outboard device known as a limiter in 1972. This state-of-the-art equipment contributed an extra "oomph of power" to their sound. "It used a prototype outboard device called a limiter, state of the art stuff in 1972. And that gave everything an extra oomph of power." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HBS24NwhrE8
