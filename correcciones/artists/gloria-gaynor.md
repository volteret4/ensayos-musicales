# artist - Gloria Gaynor

## genres
- Disco

## songs
**I Will Survive (1978)** : This "glorious breakup disco song" became a dance hit, an anthem for female empowerment, and was widely embraced by gay culture. Despite its eventual fame, it was originally released as a B-side in 1978, with the A-side being a cover of the Righteous Brothers' "Substitute," which failed. DJs, however, discovered "I Will Survive" and began playing it in clubs, launching its success. "What people forget is that I Will Survive was a B side." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw

## curiosities
**Dislike of Cake's "I Will Survive" Cover** : Gloria Gaynor dislikes Cake's 1996 cover of "I Will Survive" because the band included a swear word in the first verse that was not present in her original recording. "Gloria Gainer, by the way, hates this version because cake decided to include a swear word in the first verse that wasn't in the original." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw
**National Recording Registry Preservation (2016)** : Due to its significant cultural impact, Gloria Gaynor's "I Will Survive" was selected for preservation in the National Recording Registry of the United States in 2016. "That song was such a big hit that in 2016, it was selected for preservation in the National Recording Registry of the United States." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yuA9Xor8vfw
