## albums
**Debut Record** : Franz Ferdinand achieved platinum success with their debut album. "built on the platinum success of their debut record" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7dXtgTkdv9w
**Franz Ferdinand (2004)** : The band's debut album, released on March 9, 2004. It achieved massive success, selling a million copies in the US and winning numerous awards, including two Brit Awards and the Mercury Prize. The album's artwork and sound were described as "geometric," "chopped," "solid," and "angular," reflecting a precise and bold aesthetic. "This debut record was a hugely successful album, selling a million copies in the US, winning awards from magazines and MTV." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RWbKqqW78yg
**Tonight (2009)** : The band's third album, released on January 26, 2009, was recorded after a significant break following their 2006 tour. The visual imagery departed from the Soviet theme, instead adopting a "nighttime record" theme based on photographic inspirations from Cindy Sherman and Weegee. The album's creation involved a lot of improvisation and the use of old Soviet technology, specifically Polyvox synthesizers, to achieve a unique sound. "The theme of this record is the night. It's a nighttime record." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RWbKqqW78yg
**You Could Have It So Much Better** : This album, their second, successfully built upon the platinum success of their debut record. "Franz Furtanand's second album, you could have had it so much better, built on the platinum success of their debut record" ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7dXtgTkdv9w
**You Could Have It So Much Better (2005)** : This second album was released on October 5, 2005, approximately 18 months after their debut. The band felt they had "unfinished business" and more ideas to explore from their first record, leading to its quick release. Its visual imagery drew heavily from the Soviet Communist era, characterized by bold, geometric, and blocky designs, even influencing the font choice, to match the music's clean lines, preciseness, and boldness, as well as the "cut and paste" song structures. "And that's why the second record came out so quickly after it was like, come on, this is unfinished business here, we need to do something well with this." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RWbKqqW78yg
**You Could Have It So Much Better (2005) – Second Album Success** : Their second album, released in October 2005, achieved significant commercial success by reaching number one on the UK charts and placing in the top ten in America. "That was Franz Ferdinand's second album, you could have it so much better." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=raRCZ0Y5up8

## curiosities
**"The Chateau" Rehearsal Space** : The band's initial rehearsal space was an abandoned warehouse they creatively named "The Chateau." This venue also hosted their gigs and art events, but it was eventually raided by the police, forcing them to relocate. "That track came together in the rehearsal space, which was an abandoned warehouse that they called the Chateau, a little artistic irony there." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RWbKqqW78yg
**Alex Kapranos's Activities During Band Break** : During the band's break after touring in late 2006, Alex Kapranos went to Vancouver to produce The Cribs' album and recorded his own album with a friend named Danny, released by Domino Records under the character name Victoria and Albert. The band regrouped in February 2007. "I went to Vancouver and produced the crib's last record. They went to New York and mixed that. I recorded an album, I hear my friend Danny, that's a character, which Domino put out this year actually." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RWbKqqW78yg
**Alex Kapranos's Food Writing** : Alex Kapranos began writing about food for The Guardian after an editor approached him. Despite initial skepticism about his qualifications as a musician, his past experience as a chef and the opportunity to travel globally, trying unusual foods like deep-fried locusts and bulls' testicles, provided a unique perspective. He appreciated the "naivety" his lack of a food critic's vocabulary brought to his writing. "The idea was my idea, I've got to be totally honest about that, it was one of the editors at the Guardian, approached me and said, what do you want to write about food and my immediate reaction was like, well of course I can't write about food, I'm a guy in a band, I don't know anything about it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RWbKqqW78yg

**Artwork Inspired by Cindy Sherman and Weegee** : For the artwork of their third album, Tonight, Franz Ferdinand's visual imagery shifted to a "nighttime record" theme, inspired by photographers Cindy Sherman and Weegee. They created a series of improvised nighttime "crime scenes," similar to Sherman's untitled film stills and Weegee's flash-bulb crime scene photography, inviting fans to imagine the narrative. "And they're based around the ideas of two photographers, Cindy Sherman and Ouija." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RWbKqqW78yg

**Band Name Origin - Archduke Franz Ferdinand** : The band's name was chosen by its artsy members, who viewed it as a "mission." It refers to Archduke Franz Ferdinand, a member of the Austro-Hungarian Royal Family whose assassination on June 28, 1914, in Sarajevo by Gavrilo Princip triggered World War I, marking the beginning of the 20th century's widespread carnage. The band found the name appealing for its sound, rhythm, and historical significance, aspiring for their music to have a similar pivotal impact. "But also the theory behind the name was that he was this guy and when he was shot everything turned on that moment, nothing was quite the same afterwards." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RWbKqqW78yg

**Early Band Formation and Delays** : The band members, Alex Kapranos, Paul Thompson, Bob Hardy, and Nick McCarthy, played in various bands in Glasgow during the 1990s before forming Franz Ferdinand. There was confusion regarding the band's exact formation and first rehearsal dates, with Alex initially suggesting January 2002 or April 2003 for the first show, while Paul insisted it was 2002, citing the recording of their first album in summer 2003 after a year of playing together. "First show, I think, was in April 2003. No, I was 2002." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RWbKqqW78yg
**Encounters with Archduke's Descendants** : The band has met descendants of Archduke Franz Ferdinand who were curious to meet them. In one instance at a show in Vienna, two children, identified by local papers as his great-great-grandchildren, were asked to pose with the band while holding an etching of the Archduke's assassination, which Alex Kapranos found morbid. "The weirdest one was when we played in Vienna one time and there were these two kids who came down to the show and the local papers there as well, they were saying, right, okay now we're going to pose to the band and one of the kids to hold this etching of the assassination of the Archduke." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RWbKqqW78yg

**Musical Influences** : All members of Franz Ferdinand admired the spiky, jittery, stop-start music of the 1970s, citing influences from Joy Division, The Fall, Blondie, The Stranglers, and Gang of Four. Their music managed to sound fresh despite being inspired by these earlier sounds. "Everybody in the band had a fondness for that spiky, jittery, stop-start stuff in the 1970s. Joy Division in the fall, Blondie, the Stranglers, Gang of Four." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9CNNsqXiL6U
**Physical Media Philosophy** : Alex Kapranos views records and CDs as containers for music, acknowledging that the music itself is transferable online. He believes that to sell physical containers, they must be desirable, appealing particularly to "vinyl nuts" who demonstrate devotion to music by purchasing and actively listening to vinyl. He differentiates sound quality: vinyl is like cinema, CD is like HDTV, and MP3 is like YouTube. "All the record is or a CD is it's a container for the music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RWbKqqW78yg
**Role in Bloc Party's Breakthrough** : Franz Ferdinand's singer, Alex Kapranos, played an unwitting role in Bloc Party's big break when Kele Okereke handed him a demo tape at a show around 2003. "Block Party got their big break when Kelly went to a France Ferdinand show and gave a tape to singer Alex Capranos. That would be about 2003." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Soviet Technology Experimentation** : For their third album, the band experimented with old Soviet technology, specifically Polyvox synthesizers, to achieve a distinctive sound. Alex Kapranos compared these instruments to Lomo cameras or Octavia microphones, noting that they were reverse-engineered versions of Western technology (like Moog synthesizers or Leica cameras), which, while "kind of wrong," produced a "kind of good" and cool sound, characterized by aggressive filters. "One of the things the band experimented with for this record was some old Soviet technology." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RWbKqqW78yg
**Three and a Half Million Records Sold** : Following the success of their first two albums, Franz Ferdinand sold a total of three and a half million records, a significant achievement that afforded them the luxury of taking more time to record their third album. "That's what happens when you sell three and a half million records. You get to take your time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RWbKqqW78yg
**Unconventional Song Structures** : Franz Ferdinand is known for experimenting with tempo changes and unconventional song structures, a characteristic Alex Kapranos attributes to a desire for bravery and experimentation in their music. He noted that their approach to "Take Me Out," with its contrasting fast verses and slow choruses, arose from a need to make both sections work effectively while maintaining a strong emphasis on melody within an experimental framework. "I guess it's being brave enough to use an unconventional song structure, you know, a laylite, sort of being a little bit experimental." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RWbKqqW78yg
**Victorian Jail Rehearsal Space** : Following a police raid on their previous rehearsal space, "The Chateau," the band moved their operations to a building that had once served as an old Victorian jail. This move continued their pattern of choosing ironically artsy locations for their creative endeavors. "The cops weren't so sympathetic though. They raided the place, necessitating a move to a building that was once an old Victorian jail." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RWbKqqW78yg
