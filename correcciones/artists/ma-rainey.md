# artist - Ma Rainey

## genres
- Blues

## albums
**Bow Wevell Blues** : This song is mentioned as an example of Ma Rainey's distinctive blues style, which sometimes involved a little moaning. It exemplifies her work as one of the pioneering women in music who not only recorded but also wrote her own material, showcasing her talent and independence. ## curiosities
**Cult Figure to Wider Recognition** : For many decades, Ma Rainey remained largely a cult figure, known mainly by blues enthusiasts and by Bob Dylan, who name-checked her in his song "Tombstone Blues." Her story gained much broader recognition with the release of the 2020 movie "Ma Rainey's Black Bottom," which was based on a 1982 theater production. "Over the decades, Ma Rainey was reduced to a cult figure, only people who knew the blues knew of her and Bob Dylan, who, name checked her in his song, Tombstone Blues." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
**One-Woman Music Industry** : Beyond her role as a recording artist, Ma Rainey was a bandleader, a promoter, and later became the owner of her own music venue. Her diverse roles in the industry established her as a comprehensive force, effectively operating as a "one-woman music industry." "She was a one woman music industry." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
**Openly Queer Identity in the 1920s** : Ma Rainey was a big, black, queer woman who was remarkably open about her preferences for women, a stance that was highly unusual and daring for the 1920s. Her distinctive appearance included gold-capped teeth and thick makeup, reminiscent of contemporary goth styles. "And she was very open about how her preferences lay with women." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
**Pioneering Self-Authored Music** : Ma Rainey was a significant figure in the 1920s and 1930s blues scene, touring the US South with her powerful voice. She was one of the first women to record music that she had actually written herself, breaking ground for female artists to control their creative output. "She was one of the first women to record music that she actually wrote herself." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oRR7N-YUcRE
