## curiosities
**Band Members' Personality** : Ian Thorneley found the members of Velvet Revolver, including Slash, to be "really, really sweet and articulate and cool and down to earth," akin to any other band members he had toured with. "They were just like real dudes. They were real normal guys like any other band that I toured with before." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**Brilliant Beginning, Debacle End** : Velvet Revolver's story was characterized by a brilliant start but ended in a "debacle," which was attributed almost entirely to Scott Weiland's issues. "The whole Velvet Revolver story, which began so brilliantly and ended in such a debacle, thanks almost entirely to Scott." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r6vVw66-gmI
**Ian Thorneley's Audition Experience** : Ian Thorneley rehearsed with Velvet Revolver in Los Angeles after Scott Weiland's departure. He found the musical aspect and chemistry to be strong, and the band members (Slash, Duff McKagan, etc.) to be "real dudes" and normal guys, contrary to any "reality show" perception. "It was really nice to see that. It wasn't, it wasn't the reality show, whatever that I had imagined it would be like." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**Inability to Replace Weiland** : After Scott Weiland's departure, Velvet Revolver was unable to find a suitable replacement for him despite numerous attempts, effectively leading to the end of the band. "In any event, that was definitely the end of Velvet Revolver because despite trying and trying and trying, they couldn't find a replacement for Wyland." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vLZla5h4xKQ
**Longevity (Three Years)** : Despite the challenges of managing "all those egos," the band managed to last for "two albums and a bunch of tours over three years." "Managing all those egos in town must have been tough and it's amazing they lasted for two albums at a bunch of tours over three years." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yR3xc3ra5tE
**Scott Weiland's Departure (March 21, 2008)** : Scott Weiland announced mid-show that this tour would be his last with Velvet Revolver, a surprise to his bandmates. "On March 21st, 2008, Wyland announced in the middle of a Velvet Revolver show that this would be his last tour with the band." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yR3xc3ra5tE
**Scott Weiland's Post-STP Project** : After Stone Temple Pilots' 2002 breakup, Scott Weiland joined Velvet Revolver as their singer from 2004 to 2008. He either quit or was fired after two albums. "Wyland went on to join Velvet Revolver as their singer between 2004 and 2008." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vLZla5h4xKQ
**Search for a New Vocalist** : Velvet Revolver sought a new lead singer on two separate occasions: once before Scott Weiland joined, and again after his departure. Ian Thorneley was considered during both searches. "Looking for a guided replace Scott W Island. And somehow your name came up. So let's go through that story because I find this is fascinating." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QIA7V4PxCdM
**Video Appearance** : Slash, Duff McKagan, and Matt Sorum, who were also members of Velvet Revolver (and Guns N' Roses), appeared in The Strokes' music video for "Someday." This collaboration was noted as a significant endorsement for the emerging band. "They even had Slash, Duff McKagan and Matt Soren from Guns and Roses and Velvet Revolver appear in their video." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jEfofyobGiI
