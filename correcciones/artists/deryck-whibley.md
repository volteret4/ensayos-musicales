**Catalog Sale to Harbor View Equity Partners** : Deryck Whibley sold his song catalog, specifically the songs he wrote for Sum 41, to Harbor View Equity Partners. The exact amount was not disclosed, but he "smiled very, very, very widely" when asked, suggesting a significant payout. "Derek Wibley's song is written for some 41. Another important development of the field of putting song catalogs to work came in 1997, when David Bowie and an investment banker named David Pullman created a financial instrument called Bowie Bonds. To use Wall Street speak, 287 songs from 25 albums recorded before 1990 became asset-backed securities. In exchange for forfeiting royalty income for a period of 10 years, Bowie received an immediate payment of $55 million.
