**Early Career - Acoustic Folk** : Marc Bolan, with his band T-Rex, began in the late 1960s with a sound far removed from glam music. Their early records were primarily acoustic folk, described as experimental and psychedelic, drawing comparisons to Neil Young, Simon & Garfunkel, and Bob Dylan. This phase had moderate success, likely due to the market's oversaturation with folk music. **Eccentric Persona and Androgynous Style (1970)** : Around the release of their self-titled album in 1970, Marc Bolan's public persona grew increasingly eccentric. His behavior and dressing style, featuring an androgynous look with colors and leopard prints, became reminiscent of David Bowie's later 1970s aesthetic. He also shifted from intellectual folk lyrics to more loving, sexually explicit, romantic, and melancholic themes, which became hallmarks of glam music. **Glam Rock Catalyst (1971)** : In March 1971, T-Rex appeared on the hugely important British TV show "Top of the Pops." During this performance, Marc Bolan, the band's frontman, wore satin clothes and was covered with glitter dust, an event that David Bowie watched at home and found "brilliant," marking it as "the beginning of glam rock" and inspiring Bowie to pursue a similar direction. **Inspiration for Future Rockers** : Marc Bolan and T-Rex, particularly through *Electric Warrior*, became a massive inspiration for numerous artists from the late 1970s onwards. He influenced figures like Rod Stewart, Kiss, Def Leppard, and the New York Dolls, making it "very difficult to understand late 70s and 80s rock" artistically and musically without their contribution. **Later Career and Tragic Death** : T-Rex continued releasing glam albums throughout 1972, 1973, and 1975. However, their popularity declined due to Marc Bolan's personal situation and issues within the band. Bolan later recovered and released more successful albums towards the end of his life. Tragically, he died around the age of 30 in a traffic accident, leaving the rock world to wonder what more he could have achieved as a "absolute father of glam music" and a major influence on subsequent rock generations. "Después, Marvolan se recuperaría y hacia el final de su vida sacaría algunos iscos que lo harían resodida. **Reinvention in 1970** : In 1970, the band T-Rex underwent a significant reinvention, with Marc Bolan restructuring the group and embracing new sounds. This period saw Bolan incorporating influences from psychedelic music, particularly from eccentric figures like Syd Barrett, whose image and theatrical stage presence inspired Bolan's evolution. ## albums
**A Beard of Stars (1970) - Transition to Electric Sound** : This album achieved significant success, surpassing previous works. While still heavily linked to acoustic folk music, it began incorporating electric guitar sounds and psychedelic influences. It is not considered a glam or proto-glam album but marked an important evolution, moving away from the saturation of the folk music scene and satisfying a public eager for new sounds. **Electric Warrior (1971)** : Released in September 1971, this album elevated the sound and attitude established by "Ride a White Swan." It was characterized as fun, funny, cartoony, fantastical, and provocatively naughty, making it a significant album that rocked with a distinct glam rock style and sound, and is considered the first glam rock album. "Electric Warrior, I think we can say, was the first glam rock album." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The Surprising History of Glam Rock | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blDNqVFheAw&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Electric Warrior (1971) - Glam Rock Landmark** : This album is considered a complete glam music album and is frequently cited as officially inaugurating the "golden age" of glam, preceding David Bowie's *Ziggy Stardust* which it heavily inspired. It fully embraced glam clichés, including Marc Bolan's iconic persona, vibrant clothing, irreverent behavior, and provocative lyrics. The album features characteristic glam ballads and retains psychedelic guitar influences. Its biggest hit, "Get It On," is on this record. It marked a significant turning point in UK music, bridging the 60s and 70s sounds. "en 1971, saca una auténtica obra maestra que sería el electric warrior. Sí que el electric warrior ya son disco completamente de música glam." ← Music Radar Clan > EL PIONERO DEL GLAM： MARC BOLAN Y T REX | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mw_HxfX8iBA&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc
**T-Rex (1970) - Reinvention and Proto-Glam** : Released only a few months after *A Beard of Stars*, this self-titled album represented a complete reinvention of the band and introduced many elements of glam music. Some consider it the album that marks the beginning of glam, while others see it as an intermediate step connecting earlier music with the glam era. It had even greater success than its predecessor.
