## curiosities

It notes that "Tollia people are acceptable, of course. It's attitude more than height that's important here. And don't forget the pointy hats." "Or I guess this is in lieu of a Bob Hope impersonator. **Backstage Rider – Guitarist's Counting Skills** : The rider humorously highlights guitarist Chris's counting abilities, stating he "will personally count on arrival" the "six knobs" on each amplifier and describing him as "the closest thing that we have in our touring party to a mathematical genius." "Chris will personally count on arrival and trust me, he's a very good counter." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z2hvUDtBxho
**Backstage Rider – Humorous and Detailed Amp Specifications** : The band's rider contains extremely detailed, yet humorously exaggerated, specifications for their amplifiers. For bass, it requests three Marshall VBA bass amplifiers, emphasizing their quality ("Please make sure they're good ones"). For guitar, it asks for three JCM 800 single channel master volume Marshall Amp Heads (100 watt, model 2203), requiring recent testing (within "living memory, preferably that of a goldfish"). It specifies "six knobs" (War, Pestilence, Famine, later corrected to Presence, Bass, Middle, Treble, Pre, and Post-Game) and two inputs (high and low impedance), explicitly forbidding dual-channel reverb heads or foot switches. "No, I mean recently within a living memory, preferably that of a goldfish." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z2hvUDtBxho
**Backstage Rider – Specific Catering Demands** : Catering requirements include dinner for Iggy and two others, available at the venue or a local restaurant, with local cuisine or steak/chicken (or humorously, "endangered species, including moths and anything really cute, snake whale or nurse shark"). It explicitly states that "Cauliflower and broccoli cut into individual florets and then thrown immediately in the garbage." For loadout, two enormous pizzas are requested, often left on the bus to be discarded later due to their unwieldy size. "Cauliflower and broccoli cut into individual florets and then thrown immediately in the garbage." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z2hvUDtBxho
**Soviet Blacklist Entry – Violence** : Referred to as "The Stages," presumably meaning Iggy Pop and The Stooges, this band was listed on the Soviet Union's 1985 "not recommended music" list due to themes of "violence." "The Stages, I think they meant Iggy Pop and the Stooges, Violence." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YeNWDtN_sHE
**Band Breakup and Legendary Status** : The proposed rematch with the Scorpions never happened, as The Stooges broke up shortly after the incident. This true story cemented their legend as "America's most dangerous rock and roll band." "That never happened, blessedly, because the Stooges then broke up." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The Most Dangerous Artists in Rock | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5vKSjpvVQa8&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Confrontation with Biker Gang (Early 1974)** : By early 1974, the band's behavior was completely out of control. During a gig in Wayne, Michigan, a biker gang called the Scorpions heckled Iggy Pop for his skimpy, skin-tight leopard costume. Iggy confronted them, resulting in eggs and bottles being thrown at the stage. "By early 1974, things had really gotten out of control." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The Most Dangerous Artists in Rock | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5vKSjpvVQa8&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Extreme Behavior and Drug Use** : The Stooges were known for their wild and intense performances, with Iggy Pop being particularly unhinged. The band extensively used drugs, including heroin and Angel Dust, leading to self-destructive behavior and an out-of-control reputation. "They were on every drug known to man all the way up to heroin and Angel Dust." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The Most Dangerous Artists in Rock | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5vKSjpvVQa8&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Iggy Pop's Beating and Challenge** : While the rest of the band fled, Iggy Pop remained to confront the bikers, jumping into the crowd and receiving a severe beating before being dragged out, possibly saving his life. Days later, Iggy appeared on the radio, promoting an upcoming show and boldly challenging the Scorpions to a rematch. "He stayed there to confront the bikers, jumping into the crowd. And he got a pretty good beating." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The Most Dangerous Artists in Rock | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5vKSjpvVQa8&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Iggy Pop's Stage Antics** : Iggy Pop was notorious for scaring audiences with his antics, which included smearing peanut butter on himself and carving his body with broken glass from bottles thrown at him on stage. "He'd smear peanut butter all over himself and often carve himself up with broken glass from the beer bottles that were thrown at him on stage." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The Most Dangerous Artists in Rock | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5vKSjpvVQa8&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Most Important Post-Garage Rock Band** : Iggy Pop and the Stooges were retrospectively identified as the most important of the post-Garage Rock bands. Known for being "drugged out, whacked out, and totally crazy," they were one of the few groups that continued to appeal to audiences after the original Garage Rock sound was thought to be "done" by the late 1960s, and they served as a key inspiration for the first Garage Rock revival (punk). "But in retrospect, the most important of the post garage rock bands, was Iggy Pop and the Stooges." ← Ongoing History of New Music > Alt Rock Revivals Part 4： Garage Rock | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UpWz3X33vSY&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**Motor City Ancestor** : Iggy Pop and the Stooges are cited as "Motor City ancestors" who influenced the Detroit and Michigan underground indie scene, especially inspiring bands like The White Stripes and Yeah Yeah Yeahs. "From there, they ingratiated themselves into the Detroit and Michigan underground indie scene, all of whom had something in common with motor city ancestors like the Stooges and the MC5." ← Ongoing History of New Music > Alt Rock Revivals Part 4： Garage Rock | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UpWz3X33vSY&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
