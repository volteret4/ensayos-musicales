## songs
**Hold On (1978)** : This was an indie single released by Street Band on Logo Records in 1978. It was described as "awful," leading DJs at the BBC to flip the record over to its B-side. "They released an indie single on logo records in 1978 called Hold On. And frankly Hold On was an awful song." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zUQ2vZzmRiI
**Toast (1978)** : This song, found on the B-side of the "Hold On" single, is a spoken-word piece with a Cockney-type accent about making a piece of toast. The band reportedly came up with it on stage to stretch for time and later decided to include it on the B-side of their single. Its release coincided with a series of national strikes in England, including one by bakers, making bread scarce. This context helped the obscure B-side climb to number 18 on the British charts that fall. The band even performed it live on "Top of the Pops." The song became both the group's biggest hit and its biggest liability, leading to audiences expecting a quirky English outfit and instead finding a "loud, fairly conventional rock band," which "destroyed their credibility completely." "Inside two of the single was a song about the simple pleasures of making a piece of toast. Apparently, the guys in Street Band had come up with a song on stage during a moment when they needed to stretch for time. And when it came time to record their single, they decided to slap this jam on the B side." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zUQ2vZzmRiI

## curiosities
**Band Breakup** : After the success and subsequent liability of "Toast," Street Band released a half dozen more "straight ahead rock and roll singles" which were "miserable failures." The band eventually broke up for good in 1979 and "descended into obscurity," save for the single song "Toast" and keyboardist Paul Young's subsequent solo career. "And Street Band broke up for good in 1979 and descended into obscurity." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zUQ2vZzmRiI
**Band Formation** : Street Band was a five-piece group formed in England in 1977. "This was a five piece formed in England back in 1977." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zUQ2vZzmRiI
**Impact of "Toast" on Band Credibility** : The unexpected success of "Toast" significantly damaged Street Band's credibility. Audiences who came to see them, expecting a quirky English outfit based on the song, were instead met with a loud, conventional rock band, leading to a disconnect that hurt their career. "When people came out to see Street Band, they expected some quirky English outfit and said they got this loud, fairly conventional rock band. So essentially, toast destroyed their credibility completely." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zUQ2vZzmRiI
