## songs
**I Shot the Sheriff (1973) – Justice vs. Contraception Debate** : Written around 1972 and released as a single in February 1973, this song appears on the surface to be about a person on the run from the law after shooting a sheriff. Bob Marley himself stated it was a metaphor for justice, and he initially wanted to say "I shot the police" but changed it to "sheriff" to avoid issues with the Jamaican government. However, his former girlfriend, Esther Anderson, maintains it's a protest against contraception, arguing that Marley viewed birth control pills—which she was seeking at the time—as an abomination to his Rastafari beliefs, and that "Sheriff Brown" is a veiled reference to a doctor who "kills a seed before it grows." "Bob sings Sheriff Brown always hated me for what I don't know. Every time I plant a seed, he said, kill it before it grow." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DAjcCk9xYWk

## curiosities
**Acral Lentiginous Melanoma Diagnosis and Refusal of Amputation (July 1977)** : In July 1977, a doctor in London diagnosed Bob Marley with acral lentiginous melanoma, a rare form of skin cancer discovered under his toenail that was not well documented in medical texts at the time. Despite the doctors' advice for toe amputation, Marley refused, citing his Rastafarian belief that removing any body part would be a sin, opting instead for a skin graft from his thigh performed by a Miami specialist. "In July, he visited a doctor in London, and they discovered he had something called Acro-leteginus melanoma, a pre-existing form of skin cancer under the nail that at the time wasn't well covered in the medical textbooks." "But Bob refused, saying that as a rastifarian, it would be a sin to have any body part removed." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZhzjZixCcK8
**Assassination Attempt (December 1976)** : On the evening of December 3, 1976, around 8:30 PM, seven or eight armed men forcibly entered Bob Marley's house at 56 Hope Road outside of Kingston, Jamaica. Marley was shot twice, and his wife, manager, and a band employee also sustained gunshot wounds, though fortunately, no one died. The attack was reportedly orchestrated by elements within the Jamaican Labor Party (JLP) who perceived Marley as too politically progressive and influential, with some even suggesting CIA involvement. "Around 830 on the evening of December 3, 1976, 7 or 8 armed men burst into his house at 56 Hope Road outside of Kingston, Jamaica. Marley was shot twice." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZhzjZixCcK8
**Birthplace and Mausoleum at Nine Miles** : Bob Marley was born in Nine Miles, a village in St. Anne Parish, Jamaica, which is now entirely owned by the Marley family. His childhood home has been converted into a museum. Adjacent to the museum is a mausoleum where Bob Marley's remains are interred, along with a guitar he was buried with, the remains of his mother (who died in 2008), and his brother (who was killed in 1990). The site is a significant shrine for Jamaican culture and for fans globally. "The house where Bob grew up is out in the country, a place called Nine Miles in St. Anne Parish." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sKHdTSfDd6E
**Bullet Retention for Guitar Playing** : Following the assassination attempt, a lead bullet remained lodged in Bob Marley's upper left arm. He chose not to have it removed, fearing that the removal surgery, due to its location, might cause damage that would make it difficult or impossible for him to play the guitar. **Central Park Collapse (September 1980)** : Before his second show at Madison Square Garden on September 20, 1980, while running with his friend Alan Cole in Central Park, Bob Marley experienced altered vision, difficulty speaking, tightened legs, and searing neck pain, culminating in a multi-minute seizure and collapse. Despite this, he insisted on checking himself out of the hospital to perform the concert that night. "But before the second show, Bob went running with his friend Alan Cole in Central Park. **Death in Miami (May 1981)** : On May 8, 1981, Bob Marley decided to return home to Jamaica and asked Chris Blackwell, head of Island Records, to fly him there. However, his vital signs worsened during the flight, forcing the plane to land in Miami, where he was taken to Cedars of Lebanon Hospital. Critically ill and in immense pain, he died on May 11, 1981, surrounded by his family and weighing only 82 pounds, at the age of 36. "He was critically ill and in a tremendous amount of pain. But there was nothing the doctors could do except offer large amounts of powerful pain killers. And then, surrounded by his family and weighing just 82 pounds, Bob Marley died. It was May 11, 1981." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZhzjZixCcK8

**Experimental Treatments with Dr. Joseph Issels (November 1980 - February 1981)** : Discharged with only weeks to live, Bob Marley sought alternative treatments in Europe from Dr. Joseph Issels, a controversial figure described as a former Nazi who worked for Josef Mengele but resigned after refusing orders to stop treating Jewish patients. In Bavaria in November 1980, Marley, though weak, underwent Issels' experimental methods, including a special macrobiotic diet and drug-induced fevers using toxins to stimulate the immune system, along with the removal of decaying teeth and tonsils. By February 1981, after his 36th birthday, Issels attempted painful ozone injections into his navel, which only produced epileptic-like seizures and proved unsuccessful. "Through one of his Rastafarian contacts in Jamaica, he'd heard of a doctor named Joseph Isles, a former Nazi apparently, a guy who used to work for Joseph Mangalup."
