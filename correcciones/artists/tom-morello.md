## songs

This serves as a fantastic example of the pedal's creative application in shaping distinctive guitar sounds. **Song Featuring Wah-Wah Pedal Use (Year not given)** : This song by Tom Morello of Rage Against the Machine is highlighted as an example of his distinctive use of the wah-wah pedal, with the effect notably appearing about 30 seconds into the track. ## curiosities
**African American Contribution to Alt-Rock** : Tom Morello, guitarist for Rage Against the Machine and Audioslave, is identified as one of the many African American artists who have made important contributions to alternative rock. "Ben Harper, rage against the machine in AudioSlave guitars Tom Morello, there's Slash." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Contemporary Wah Pedal Usage** : Tom Morello of Rage Against the Machine is cited as a contemporary guitarist of African descent who prominently uses the wah-wah pedal, demonstrating the lasting legacy of this effect, pioneered by artists like Jimi Hendrix. "He loves his wall." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hQJ3r-DCoMQ
**Family Connection to Adam Jones** : Tom Morello, guitarist for Rage Against the Machine, Audioslave, and Prophets of Rage, has a family connection to Adam Jones of Tool. Morello's mother was Adam Jones's home-room teacher. "The mother of Tom Morello, guitarist for rage against the machine, audio slave and profits of rage, was the home-room teacher of Adam Jones, guitarist with tool." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=72Lei7uug8c
**Initiated Audioslave Formation** : After Zack de la Rocha's departure from Rage Against The Machine, Tom Morello contacted Chris Cornell to see if he would be interested in joining the remaining members of the band (Timmy and Brad). "On the other end was guitarist Tom Morello from Rage Against The Machine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-f9jhqAHkQo
