# artist - Artists United Against Apartheid

## members
- Arthur Baker

## songs
**Sun City (1985) – Anti-Apartheid Protest Song; Premiered at the United Nations; Raised Over $1 Million** : Steve Van Zandt (Bruce Springsteen's guitarist) was disgusted that performers including the Beach Boys, Cher, Rod Stewart, Kenny Rogers, Elton John, and Queen were accepting money to perform at Sun City, a whites-only resort in the Bantustan of Bophuthatswana, South Africa, effectively breaking the international boycott against apartheid. Together with producer Arthur Baker (a favorite of New Order), he created "Sun City" under the name Artists United Against Apartheid. Participants from rock included Bono, Bob Dylan, Peter Gabriel, Ringo Starr, Lou Reed, Bob Geldof, Pat Benatar, Clarence Clemons, Jackson Browne, Keith Richards, Ronnie Wood, Joey Ramone, and Peter Garrett of Midnight Oil. Hip-hop contributors included Run DMC, African Bambaata, DJ Cool Herc, Grandmaster Melle Mel, Curtis Blow, and the Fat Boys. Also contributing were Jimmy Cliff, George Clinton, Herbie Hancock, Bonnie Raitt, Hall and Oates, Gil Scott-Heron, and Miles Davis. The single premiered at the United Nations, raised over a million dollars for anti-apartheid efforts, and was a big hit in Canada, Australia, the Netherlands, and the UK. In America it reached only number 38, with many radio stations refusing to play it due to its criticism of President Reagan's "constructive engagement" policy toward South Africa. **Sun City (Anthem)** : This collective created an anthem specifically protesting Sun City, which was described as a playground for wealthy white people in South Africa, yet entirely off-limits to everyone else under the apartheid system. The song drew attention to the inequalities and segregation of the regime.
