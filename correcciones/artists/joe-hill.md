## curiosities
**Joe Hill (1915) – Swedish IWW Songwriter, Coined "Pie in the Sky," Executed by Firing Squad; Father of the Modern Protest Song** : Joe Hill was a Swedish-born itinerant laborer and songwriter for the Industrial Workers of the World (IWW), a socialist union founded in Chicago in 1905 that organized logging, mining, farm, and factory workers. Hill is credited with coining the phrase "Pie in the Sky." He was arrested and charged with the murder of two people in Salt Lake City, subject to a major media trial, found guilty, and executed by firing squad on November 19, 1915. He is considered the father of the modern protest song and has many fans in the rock world, including Tom Morello of Rage Against the Machine, who has organized a memorial in Joe's name, performed his songs, and released songs about him. "You can draw a straight line from Rage Against the Machine right back to the 1910s and Joe Hill." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dCelQ8Zj_cg
**Labor Activist and Martyr (Died 1915)** : Joe Hill was a Swedish-born labor activist who traveled throughout the United States, advocating for workers' rights through song. He was convicted of murder, a charge widely believed to be a "railroading" by powerful interests, and was subsequently executed by a firing squad in 1915. His execution cemented his legacy as both a legend and a martyr in the labor movement.
