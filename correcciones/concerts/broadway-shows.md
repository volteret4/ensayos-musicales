# concert - Broadway shows

## curiosities
**Early Computerized Ticketing Target (1960s)** : Broadway shows were the initial target market for Ticket Reservation Systems Inc. (TRS), founded in 1965. The company aimed to revolutionize ticket sales by allowing remote purchases, eliminating the need to visit the theater box office. "Originally the goal was to handle the sales of tickets to Broadway shows from remote locations." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OCkov6FTliE
**Significant "ICE" Payments (Early 1960s)** : In the early 1960s, box office managers for Broadway shows in New York City alone were estimated to receive over $10 million annually in "ICE" – payoffs from scalpers for early access to the best printed tickets. "And in the early 1960s, it was estimated that in New York City alone, the amount of ICE paid for Broadway shows was more than $10 million a year." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OCkov6FTliE
