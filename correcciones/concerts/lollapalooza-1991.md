# concert - Lollapalooza 1991

## curiosities
**Crucial for Nine Inch Nails' Survival** : The offer for Nine Inch Nails to play on the first Lollapalooza tour in the summer of 1991 was vital for the band's survival. During a period when Trent Reznor was legally prohibited from making new music due to his contract with TVT, the $12,000 paid per gig helped fund legal expenses, as well as basic necessities like food and rent, keeping the band afloat. "The only thing that kept nine-inch nails alive was the offer to play on the first Lala Paloza tour in the summer of 1991." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M3nUQtja0qc
**First Edition and Lineup** : The first Lollapalooza festival took place in the summer of 1991, featuring a lineup that included founding band Jane's Addiction, Siouxsie and the Banshees, Living Colour, Ice-T and Body Count, Butthole Surfers, Rage Against the Machine, and Nine-inch Nails. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
**First Ever Tour Featuring Diverse Acts** : The 1991 Lollapalooza tour was the inaugural event of the festival, featuring diverse artists such as Living Color and Body Count as main stage performers. "first ever La La Palusatour in 1991." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PdCAjFW04Oc
**Modest Success and Unsold Tickets** : Despite its significant lineup, the inaugural Lollapalooza in 1991 was only a modest success, with "many, many, many unsold tickets." However, the fact that "upwards of 20,000 people paid to see all these decidedly non-mainstream bands" was enough to convince promoters to stage the festival again. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqTFinLk3oU
