# concert - Fyre Festival

## curiosities
**Catastrophic Failure of Luxury Festival (2017)** : The Fyre Festival, conceived by Billy McFarland and Ja Rule as an ultra-exclusive multi-day concert in the Bahamas with VIP packages up to $250,000, collapsed spectacularly upon its opening on April 28, 2017. Attendees, lured by influencer marketing, arrived to find disaster relief tents instead of luxury villas, inadequate food, no recreational facilities, and all promised big-name artists absent. "This was one of the biggest disasters in the history of concert promotion." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GYUuTbOvyak
**Cautionary Tale of Influencer Culture** : The Fyre Festival became a prominent example of the potential emptiness and phoniness of internet influencer culture, as its perceived legitimacy was largely built on social media promotion by figures like Kendall Jenner and Bella Hadid. It serves as a cautionary tale against deceptive marketing and the unchecked hype of online influence. "Today, the fire festival is considered to be an example of how internet influence culture can be empty and phony. It's also a cautionary tale that involved deceptive marketing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GYUuTbOvyak
**Stranded Attendees and Legal Fallout** : Approximately 5,000 attendees of the Fyre Festival were left stranded on the Bahamian island without proper food, water, or accommodation after return flights were cancelled. The event garnered swift media condemnation, led to lawsuits and investigations, and resulted in Billy McFarland's arrest, conviction for wire fraud, and a six-year jail sentence. "Those who did make the trip, about 5,000 people, were stranded on the island when return flights were cancelled without notice." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GYUuTbOvyak
