# concert - CBGB (New York)

## curiosities
**Closure Due to Gentrification and Rent (October 15, 2006)** : CBGB survived for decades but faced insurmountable challenges by 2005. Its monthly rent increased from $19,000 to $35,000, compounded by a $90,000 lawsuit from a local residence committee. Despite attempts to declare it a historical landmark, the ongoing gentrification of the neighborhood led to its closure on October 15, 2006, with Patti Smith performing the final gig. "But by 2005, the running was on the wall. Monthly rent was $19,000. Then there was a lawsuit by a local residence committee for $90,000." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Epz5Sn151bk
**Ground Zero for Punk Rock (Opened 1973)** : CBGB, opened by Hilly Crystal in 1973 in the economically distressed Bowery, quickly became "ground zero for so much of the punk rock of the 1970s." Its cheap rent and open musical policy fostered a scene where hundreds of groups like The Ramones, Talking Heads, Blondie, and Television developed the sensibilities that would spread globally. "CBGB, ground zero for so much of the punk rock of the 1970s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Epz5Sn151bk
