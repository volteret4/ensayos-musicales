# concert - Roskilde

## curiosities
**European Prominence** : On the European continent, Roskilde, held in a Danish town right after Glastonbury, was considered the equivalent of Glastonbury in terms of prestige and honor during the 1990s. "On the continent, that honor probably belonged to Ross Guilt, a festival held in that Danish town, pretty much right after Glastonbury." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Founding and Scale** : Started by two high school students in 1971, Roskilde has been running ever since. In modern times, it can host up to 130,000 people and features 180 different acts spanning rock, folk, electronic, and hip-hop. "It was started by two high school students in 1971 and has been going ever since. In modern times, it can host up to 130,000 people who come to experience 180 different acts." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**Roskilde 2000 Tragedy** : The worst year for Roskilde was 2000, when nine people died and 26 were injured during Pearl Jam's set in front of about 60,000 people at the Orange Stage. This accident led to the implementation of new safety protocols at festivals worldwide. "Ross Guilt's worst year was 2000. When nine people died and 26 were injured during Pearl Jam set in front of about 60,000 people at an area called the Orange Stage, that accident set new safety protocols in motion at festivals all over the world." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
**The Naked Run** : Roskilde is famous for its "Naked Run," a race around the campsite where participants wear no clothes. The top male and female runners win free tickets to the following year's event, and the event has become so popular that qualifier events are now held. "There's also something called the Naked Run, which is a race around the campsite where the participants don't wear any clothes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JP2tjJibTQ
