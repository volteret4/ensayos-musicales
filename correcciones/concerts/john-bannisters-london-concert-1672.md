# concert - John Bannister's London Concert (1672)

## curiosities
**Catalyst for Modern Concert Industry** : Bannister's pioneering paid concert in 1672 quickly inspired other musicians. By the early 1700s, the concept had spread, laying the groundwork for the modern concert industry as we know it today. "Other musicians took notice, and by the early 1700s, the modern concert industry had been a bit more difficult to find." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8GU8ZpSgY8
**Earliest Documented Paid Public Concert (1672)** : According to a 1936 article in "The Music Quarterly," John Bannister organized, promoted, hosted, and performed a concert for a paying audience in his home in London in 1672. This event, where the general public bought tickets for one shilling, is considered the earliest evidence of a modern, ticketed public concert. "This is the earliest evidence anyone has been able to find for this kind of paid event." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8GU8ZpSgY8
