# concert - Roundhouse

## curiosities
**Catalyst for British Punk** : On July 4th, 1976, while America celebrated its Bicentennial, the Ramones were second on a bill at the Roundhouse in North London. This event is credited with doing "more to kick start British punk than anything else that had happened up until that point." "On July 4th, 1976, while America was celebrating the Big Bison Tenio, the Ramones were second on a bill at the Roundhouse in North London." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=j8uk7BnbXwU
**Ramones' UK Debut (July 4, 1976)** : Located in Camden, North London, and holding about 2,000 people, the Roundhouse hosted The Ramones' pivotal July 4, 1976 gig. This explosive show, despite The Flamin Groovies being the official headliners, was England's "first proper exposure" to US-style punk rock and transformed many future British punk rock stars, including Johnny Rotten and Joe Strummer. "The roundhouse in Camden, which is on the north side of London, held about 2,000 people." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=huAvyInzz3Q
