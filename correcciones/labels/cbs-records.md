**Worldwide CD Launch (March 1983)** : CBS Records participated in the big worldwide launch of the compact disc format in March 1983, releasing 16 albums on the new format. This included major titles like Michael Jackson's "Thriller," helping to establish the CD in the market. "Then came the big worldwide launch in March of 1983, when CBS records released 16 albums on the new format, including Thriller from Michael Jackson." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BHkJFA0LMro
**CBS Columbia System** : CBS Records developed and offered its own distinct stereo system, known as the Columbia system. This proprietary solution was one of several attempts by record companies to establish a viable method for stereo record production. "CBS records came up with their Columbia system." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cbSmBrhNACg ← cbs-records
**Controversial Contract with The Clash (January 25, 1977)** : CBS Records signed The Clash to a worldwide contract for 129,000 pounds on January 25, 1977. This deal was highly controversial among punk's hardcore fans, who viewed it as a betrayal of punk's anti-establishment ethos and declared it "the day Punk died," despite The Clash's stated intention to "subvert the record industry from within." ← Ongoing History of New Music > Joe Strummer： A Remembrance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4m2Kx3ffJUQ&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG ← cbs-records
**MTV Racism Challenge (1983)** : CBS Records allegedly threatened to withdraw all their videos from MTV if the channel did not begin to feature Black artists. This action is credited with forcing MTV to cave and start showing videos from Michael Jackson's "Thriller" album, breaking the channel's unofficial color barrier. "It wasn't until CBS records allegedly threatened to pull all their videos from the channel that MTV caved and started to show videos from the thriller album." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The Rise and Fall and Future of the Music Video - Part 1 | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lqd6IbUJ7tg&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG ← cbs-records
**Refusal to Release The Clash's Debut in North America** : CBS Records initially refused to release The Clash's self-titled debut album in North America for two years after its British release. Executives believed the record was "too raw, and abrasive, and crude, and political for American tastes" during the disco era. This decision forced North American fans to buy higher-priced British import versions, which astonishingly sold 100,000 copies, making it the biggest-selling import ever at the time, before CBS finally relented in 1979 with a "watered down version." ← Ongoing History of New Music > Joe Strummer： A Remembrance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4m2Kx3ffJUQ&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG ← cbs-records
**Rejection of "Bat Out of Hell"** : Under the leadership of Clive Davis, CBS Records notably refused to sign or release Meat Loaf's debut album, *Bat Out of Hell*. Davis explicitly told songwriter Jim Steinman that he believed Steinman had "no idea how to write a rock song." "CBS Records, which was run by the legendary Clive Davis, said via Clive Davis that, no way, be making this. In fact, Clive Davis told Jim Steinman, clearly you have no idea how to write a rock song." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 42 - Rock Operas of the 70s | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-42-rock-operas-of-the-70s
**Reluctance Over Double and Triple Albums** : CBS Records expressed reluctance to release The Clash's *London Calling* as a double album and *Sandinista!* as a triple album, due to concerns about manufacturing hassle and market price. The band had to make significant financial concessions, such as accepting lower royalty rates for *London Calling* and foregoing royalties on the first 200,000 copies of *Sandinista!*, to convince the label to release these ambitious projects. ← Ongoing History of New Music > Joe Strummer： A Remembrance | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4m2Kx3ffJUQ&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG ← cbs-records
**The Clash Signing Controversy (1977)** : CBS Records signed The Clash to a worldwide recording contract for 100,000 pounds on January 25, 1977, amidst a fierce bidding war. This deal was widely condemned by the punk community as a betrayal of the genre's anti-establishment ethos, with one fanzine even declaring "punk died the day the clash signed to CBS." "And on January 25, 1977, at the very height of punk rock in the UK, they signed a worldwide recording contract with CBS records for 100,000 pounds." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DYtsWfguqGo ← cbs-records
