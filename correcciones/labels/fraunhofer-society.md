## curiosities
**Hockey Game Audio for MP3 Algorithm Testing** : To refine their MP3 compression algorithms, Fraunhofer engineers utilized raw audio recordings from hockey games as one of their two primary test sound sources. The complex and unique sounds produced in an arena, such as skates slashing ice and pucks hitting boards, presented a significant challenge for clean compression, prompting engineers to spend thousands of hours on this specific audio. "The first was, believe it or not, recording raw audio from hockey games. There was something about how hockey sounds in an arena with the skate slashing the ice and the puck booming off the boards that proved to be really tricky to compress cleanly. Frown-hofer engineers spent thousands of hours listening to the sounds of hockey and trying to compress them properly." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fhq9WVeiE_U
**MP3 Technology Development** : The Fraunhofer Society in Germany was responsible for developing the MP3 technology. Their initial goal was to find efficient ways to transmit large quantities of audio data over older copper telephone wires, which they achieved by creating an algorithm that compressed data by removing non-essential information. "This technology was developed by the frown-hofer society in Germany. Initially, they were looking for ways to send large amounts of audio data through old, telephone, copper wires. And the only way to do that was to compress the data using an algorithm that stripped out everything that was non-essential." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fhq9WVeiE_U
**MP3 Technology Licensing (1993)** : In 1993, the Fraunhofer Society licensed its MP3 compression technology broadly to various parties, contributing to its widespread adoption and impact on digital music. "Frown-hofer licensed this technology to everybody in 1993." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fhq9WVeiE_U
