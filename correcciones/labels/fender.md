# label - Fender

## curiosities
**Coveted Clean Sound** : In an era preceding distortion and effects pedals, the Fender Twin Reverb was highly sought after for its exceptionally clean sound. This clarity distinguished it, making it a favorite for guitarists desiring pristine, uncolored tones. "In an era before distortion and effects pedals and fuzz and deliberate guitar noise, the Fender Twin Reverb was coveted for its exceptionally clean sound." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Dominance of the Precision Bass** : Throughout the 1950s, the large, classical double bass was largely supplanted by the compact Fender Precision Bass. Its advantages included easier transport, playability, amplification, and modification, quickly establishing it as the industry standard. "Throughout the 1950s, the big double bass disappeared, and it was replaced with a relatively tiny Fender Precision, which was easier to transport, play, amplify, and modify." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Evolution of the Bassman** : The Bassman amplifier underwent several iterations throughout the 1950s as it was unexpectedly adopted by guitarists and harmonica players. By 1957, Fender focused on refining it exclusively for bass, resulting in the popular 5F6-A Bassman, known as one of the last "Tweed Amps." "Several iterations followed in the 1950s, because people were using the bass man for guitars and harmonicas." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Financial Struggles in 2010s** : Fender, a prominent manufacturer of electric guitars, faced significant debt in the 2010s, indicating the broader decline in electric guitar sales during that decade. **First 100-Watt Guitar Amp (1962)** : In 1962, Fender collaborated with surf guitar pioneer Dick Dale to produce the first-ever 100-watt guitar amplifier. This was a significant development, allowing guitarists to achieve powerful, overdriven sounds by simply setting the amp to its overdrive limits. "In 1962, surf guitar king Dick Dale worked with Fender to produce the first ever 100 watt guitar amp, a very big deal, setting everything to overdrive created a sound like this." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iGav9uJwA_w
**Introduction of the Stratocaster** : Fender unveiled the Stratocaster in 1954, a double-cutaway solid-body guitar co-designed by Leo Fender. Key features included three pickups, double-cutaways providing access up to the 21st fret, balanced design, and a vibrato bar on some models. "It's the Fender Stratocaster, a double-cutaway solid-body guitar unveiled in 1954." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Introduction of the Twin Reverb** : The Fender Twin Reverb, an all-time classic amplifier, was introduced in 1952, fitting chronologically between the Telecaster (1950) and the Stratocaster (1954). Leo Fender understood that his new electric guitars required proper amplification to be useful. "This is one of the all-time classic amps introduced in 1952 between the introduction of the Fender Telecaster, a one pickup electric, which was also the world's first mass produced and commercially successful electric guitar, and the unveiling of the Stratocaster in 1954." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Invention of the Bassman Amplifier** : Recognizing that the new Fender Precision Bass lacked a dedicated amplifier, Fender introduced the Bassman in 1952. This amp was specifically designed for bass guitars, featuring heavy-duty speakers, a cabinet to enhance bass response, and sufficient wattage to ensure basses could be heard with the band. "It wasn't until a year later, in 1952, when Fender introduced an amp designed just for bass guitars. They called it the bass man." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Pioneering Customization** : The construction of the Fender Stratocaster, though not its original purpose, allowed players to easily disassemble and modify the instrument in countless ways. This modularity inspired other manufacturers to incorporate customizable features into their own guitar designs. "Although this wasn't the original purpose of the Strat, the way it was constructed allowed players to take them apart and modify them in a million different ways." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**The Precision Bass Innovation** : In 1951, Leo Fender introduced the Fender Precision Bass, a solution to the long-standing problem of bass parts being inadequately heard. Based on the Telecaster design, it was small, light, amplified, and became the world's first commercially successful solid-body bass. "Leo Fender came up with a solution in 1951, and what he called the Fender Precision Base." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
**Versatile Models and Reissues** : The Fender Twin Reverb was produced in various models over time, identifiable by different sizes, shapes, finishes, and grill styles such as tweed, silver, blonde, blackface, and brownface. Fender also reissued classic versions with modern adjustments, maintaining the amp's enduring appeal for its specific clean sound. "The twin reverb came in different sizes and shapes and finishes with different grills, silver, blonde, blackface, brownface, and so on." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0HW8LQKvJw
