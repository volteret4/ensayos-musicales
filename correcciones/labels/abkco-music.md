# label - Abkco Music

## curiosities
**Controversial Licensing and Lawsuits** : Abkco Music initially licensed a snippet of "The Last Time" to The Verve for "Bittersweet Symphony" in exchange for 50% of earnings. Subsequently, Abkco sued The Verve, alleging the band used more of the sample than agreed, and ultimately forced them to surrender 100% of the song's publishing rights. Abkco then controversially licensed The Verve's now-owned recording to Nike for a TV commercial. "Abco Music sued saying that the Verve used more of the sample than was spelled out in the licensing agreement." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AcADOuQUiAQ
**Publishing and Copyright Ownership** : Abkco Music held the publishing and copyright for The Rolling Stones' song "The Last Time" and its orchestral rendition by the Andrew Loog Oldham Orchestra. The company was controlled by Allen Klein. "Abco Music, the company that owned the publishing and the copyright to that song." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AcADOuQUiAQ
