# label - Fraunhofer Institute

## curiosities
**Employer Ownership of MP3 Patents (Unspecified Year)** : As an employee of the Fraunhofer Institute, Carl Heinz Brandenburg's inventions and patents, including the highly lucrative MP3 technology, legally became the property of his employer. Consequently, the institute, not Brandenburg, accrued the revenues generated by the MP3, which surpassed $150 million. "However, since Carl Heinz Brennenberg was an employee of the Fraunhofer Institute, all his inventions and all his patents became the property of his employer." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
**Home of MP3 Development (1991-1995)** : The Fraunhofer Institute in Germany served as the research base for Carl Heinz Brandenburg and his team, where they developed the revolutionary MP3 algorithm. The mathematical foundations were established by 1991, leading to its acceptance as an international standard by 1994, and its public release in 1995, coinciding with the internet's emergence. "Brennenberg and his team at the Fraunhofer Institute in Germany took some of the existing research and created an algorithm that stripped out all this invisible sound from an audio file." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
