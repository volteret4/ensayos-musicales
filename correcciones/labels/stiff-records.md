# label - Stiff Records

## curiosities
**Early Association with a Cultural Meme** : Stiff Records released "Sex & Drugs & Rock & Roll" by Ian Dury and the Blockheads in 1977. This song is credited with widely popularizing the phrase, turning it into a significant cultural "meme." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BHN6db7m3DM
**First UK Punk Release (1976)** : Stiff Records was the first label to release any punk record in the UK. This historic release was "New Rose" by The Damned on October 22nd, 1976. "In fact, Stiff was the first label to release any kind of punk record in the UK. The date for that was October 22nd, 1976." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
**Founding (1976)** : Stiff Records was a small company founded in 1976 by Dave Robinson and Jake Riviera, completely dedicated to punk music. "This was a small company formed by Dave Robinson and Jake Riviera in 1976 and was totally devoted to punk." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
**Notable Roster & Business Challenges** : The label was known for signing influential artists like Elvis Costello, Madness, The Pogues, and Motörhead. However, Stiff Records struggled with business management, described as "terrible at conducting business," being "irresponsible," and "bleeding money." "Alva's Costello, Madness, the Pog's Motorhead, but Stiff was terrible at conducting business. They were irresponsible and they bled money and were sold off by 1983." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
**Promoter of DIY Punk** : Stiff Records was instrumental in promoting the "do it yourself" (DIY) punk ethos during the genre's formative years. "perhaps the most important label when it came to pushing DIY punk, do it yourself punk, in the infancy of that music." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
**Sale to Island Records (1983)** : Due to financial difficulties and mismanagement, Stiff Records was sold off by 1983, with Island Records acquiring it. "Stiff was terrible at conducting business. They were irresponsible and they bled money and were sold off by 1983. And the buyer? Another indie. Island Records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
