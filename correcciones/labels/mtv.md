# label - MTV

## curiosities
**"The Return of the Rock" Compilation (2000)** : On June 13th, 2000, MTV, described as the "Chief Spreaders of the Pop Plague," unexpectedly released a compilation album titled "The Return of the Rock." This was notable because at the time, MTV primarily featured pop artists like Mariah Carey, Savage Garden, and Destiny's Child, but this compilation showcased bands like System of a Down, Slipknot, and Incubus. **1994 Video Music Awards Success** : The "Sabotage" music video by the Beastie Boys won five trophies at the 1994 MTV Video Music Awards, highlighting MTV's role as a prominent platform for recognizing excellence in music video production. This cemented the channel's influence and prestige in the industry. "It won five trophies at the 1994 MTV Video Music Awards." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7IDbqdIw3WU
**Co-Premiere of "Black or White" (1991)** : MTV was one of the key channels, alongside VH1, BET, and Fox, that simultaneously premiered Michael Jackson's "Black or White" video in November 1991 to an audience of 500 million people. This event solidified MTV's status as a premier broadcaster for major music video releases. "it was seen simultaneously by 500 million people thanks to MTV, VH1, BET, and the brand new fourth TV network, Fox." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7IDbqdIw3WU
**Friction with Labels over Free Videos** : Record labels consistently "chafed" at the practice of providing music videos to MTV for free. This ongoing friction was a factor in MTV's decision to diversify its programming, as the channel recognized the need to adapt to changing dynamics and declining excitement for music videos. "Labels had always chafed at giving the channel videos for free, so that was causing friction." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7IDbqdIw3WU
**Last Global Music Video Channel Shut Down (2025)** : At 6 AM GMT on December 31, 2025, MTV shut down the last of its global music video-only channels, 44 years and 5 months after it first signed on on August 1, 1981. The channel's end was marked by "The Buggles and Video Killed The Radio Star," mirroring its iconic launch. "MTV shut down the last of its global music video-only video channels. 44 years and 5 months since it first signed on on August 1, 1981." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7IDbqdIw3WU
**Powerful Cultural Force** : In the 1980s and 1990s, MTV was a powerful musical, cultural, and even political force that extended beyond the United States. It was central to the music video's golden age, demanding videos for every single release and influencing artistic direction. "MTV was a powerful musical, cultural, and even political force that extended beyond the United States." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7IDbqdIw3WU
**Programming Shift to Reality TV (Late 1990s)** : By the late 1990s, MTV began to broaden its programming base, realizing that the music video monoculture was breaking up and videos were losing their novelty. It shifted towards reality TV, cartoons, and personality-based shows like *The Real World* and *Total Request Live*, moving uninterrupted music video flows to off-peak hours. "MTV began moving to Reality TV, cartoons, and personality-based shows." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7IDbqdIw3WU
