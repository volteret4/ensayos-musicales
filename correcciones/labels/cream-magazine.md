# label - Cream Magazine

## curiosities
**Early "Punk" Term Usage (1970-1971)** : Cream, a Detroit-based music magazine, played a role in the early usage of the term "punk" in music criticism. In December 1970, critic Lester Bangs referred to Iggy Pop as "that stooge punk." In May 1971, Dave Marsh called Question Mark and the Mysterians "purveyors of punk rock" in the magazine. **Criticism of Heavy Metal** : Cream Magazine gained notoriety for publishing one of the most vociferous criticisms of heavy metal, authored by its writer and editor, Robert Duncan. This reflected the widespread negative sentiment towards the genre among music critics in the 1970s. **Roxy Music Album Review (1973)** : Detroit-based Cream Magazine published a critical review of Roxy Music's self-titled debut album in 1973, disparaging its "artifice" and "polished deformity" and noting the heavy use of synthesizer by Brian Eno on Side Two. Despite the harshness, such reviews inadvertently attracted fans who enjoyed music that challenged conventional tastes.
