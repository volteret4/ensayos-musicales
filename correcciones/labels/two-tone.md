# label - Two-tone

## curiosities
**Culturally Significant Label** : Established by Jerry Dammers of The Specials, Two-tone evolved beyond just a record label to become a distinct sound and a cultural movement, actively promoting racial harmony. Its first release, "Gangsters" by The Specials in May 1979, sold 5,000 copies and reached number six on the British charts, an unprecedented success for an indie record. "So he started his own label and he called it Two-tone. This became not just a record label, but a sound and a cultural movement promoting racial harmony." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**British Indie Label** : A UK indie label that was a major factor in breaking new music, particularly since the punk era of the 1970s. "There was rough trade, two-tone and stiff, mute, and dozens of others." ← Ongoing History of New Music > The 00's Part 2： The Indie Revolution | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=raRCZ0Y5up8&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
**The Specials' Debut Album** : The Two-Tone label released The Specials' first album in 1979, which was produced by Elvis Costello. This album is credited with significantly propelling the ska revival into full swing. "Look at the credits on that first album from the Specials on the Two-Tone label who produced it, who showed this raw, untamed band from Coventry had a capture of their energy on tape." ← Ongoing History of New Music > What's The Big Deal About Elvis Costello | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vKRKu7O_5HA&list=PLM8bxV0E1Gse5C3n1u-mkVSTZLL5AJICG
