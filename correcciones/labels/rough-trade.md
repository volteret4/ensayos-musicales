# label - Rough Trade

## curiosities
**Community Hub** : The Rough Trade store served as a community gathering place where people bought records, promoted fanzines, exchanged gossip, and advertised for musicians. The original shop remains open near Portobello Road in Notting Hill. "It also became a hangout for people into non-mainstream music. They bought records, they pushed fanzines, they swapped gossip and advertised for musicians." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
**Early Supporter of DIY Electronic Music** : Rough Trade record shop in Notting Hill played a crucial role in the early Technopop scene by agreeing to stock Daniel Miller's self-produced "Warm Leatherette" single, which was recorded with rudimentary gear in his bedroom. This support helped the minimalist, futuristic record sell 30,000 copies, inspiring many aspiring musicians. "He started experimenting with it in his bedroom. Recorded two songs, found some money to make a test pressing and convinced the rough-trade record shop in Notting Hill to stock it." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
**Financial Struggles and Bankruptcy** : While Rough Trade found success with acts like The Smiths, most of its early signings sold records "by the dozen." The label eventually faced financial difficulties and went bankrupt by 1991, impacting other independent labels with which it had existing arrangements. "rough trade went bankrupt by 1991 and by then they had arrangements with other indie labels and they were all hurt as a result." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zB5zxycrbnY
**Founding and Evolution** : Rough Trade was founded by Geoff Travis, initially as a record shop in London in 1976, which he opened to sell his extensive record collection accumulated in Canada and the United States. By 1978, the shop had expanded into a record label focused on finding new and exciting post-punk bands. "It started when he had this giant record collection that he needed to sell off." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zB5zxycrbnY
**Founding as Record Store (1976)** : In 1976, Jeff Travis founded Rough Trade as a record store in Notting Hill, London. He started it with his personal record collection after a trip across the US where he bought many records. The store quickly became a hub for non-mainstream music enthusiasts. "In 1976, Jeff Travis took a trip across the US buying up all kinds of records and when he got home he didn't have anywhere to put them so he decided to start up a store with his record collection acting as inventory." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
**Home to The Smiths** : Rough Trade served as the UK home label for The Smiths, widely considered the biggest indie band of their era and one whose influences continue to be discussed. "As this tape was getting people excited about the possibilities of new music, rough trade was home to the biggest indie band of the era, whose influences are still being talked about today." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Pioneering Independent Label** : Rough Trade was among the small independent labels that emerged in the post-punk era, specializing in operating outside the major label system and supporting independent artists. "that included rough trade for a D, beggars banquet, epitaph, SST, and many others." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4
**Prominent on C86 Cassette** : Rough Trade was one of the indie labels whose artists were tapped to be featured on the influential C86 cassette, a compilation that reflected the state of new music in Britain in 1986. "They tapped labels like Creation and Rough Tray and a bunch of others." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VOv5mT1dJd8
**Recommended Record Store in London** : Rough Trade is recommended as a must-visit record store in London, with specific locations mentioned in Notting Hill and the East End store in Brick Lane. "I never go to London without visiting Rough Trade and Notting Hill or the East End store in Brick Lane." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
**Signed The Strokes** : Rough Trade, a UK-based label, signed The Strokes after American labels showed no interest in the band despite the buzz they had generated. Rough Trade "loved what they heard" from the band's demos, leading to their debut album "This Is It." "So they sent some demos to Rough Trade in the UK, and Rough Trade loved what they heard." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UpWz3X33vSY
**Transition to Record Label (1978)** : In 1978, Jeff Travis expanded Rough Trade into an independent record label. One of its earliest releases was "Alternative Ulster" by the Belfast band Stiff Little Fingers. "In 1978, Travis decided to jump into the indie record label game and one of the labels very earliest releases was by a bell fast band called Stiff Little Fingers." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
