# label - Sehan Information Systems

## curiosities
**Introduction of the MP3 Man (1997)** : In 1997, the South Korean company Sehan Information Systems launched the MP3 man, Model F10, which holds the distinction of being the first-ever portable MP3 player. It was available in 32MB and 64MB capacities and initially sold for prices ranging between $700 and $1000. "In 1997, a South Korean company called Sehan Information Systems introduced the first ever portable MP3 player, which they called the MP3 man, Model F10." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fhq9WVeiE_U
**MP3 Man Power and Loading Speed** : The MP3 man operated on a single AA battery, and the process of loading music onto the device was notably slow due to its reliance on parallel port connections. "And the device ran on a single AA battery. So you can just imagine how long it took before you ran out of juice. It took a while to load because we were still using parallel port connections, which were awfully slow." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fhq9WVeiE_U
**MP3 Man Technology and Capacity** : The MP3 man featured flash memory, distinguishing it as the first portable music device since the transistor radio to contain no moving parts, thereby eliminating skips during playback. Music was transferred to the device by docking it to a computer via a parallel port. Its capacity varied with bitrate: the smaller unit could hold six uncompressed WAV files (or more at lower bitrates), while the larger unit could hold twelve. At the time, an hour's worth of music was considered sufficient for most users. "It used flash memory, meaning that unlike every other portable music device since the transistor radio, there were zero moving parts. It could not skip. You loaded it by docking the MP man in a docking port that you connected to your computer. RIP tracks could then be transferred over. Now many RIP tracks depended on the bit rate. If you transferred uncompressed wave files, the smaller unit could hold just six songs, and the bigger one, 12. But if you lowered the bit rate, the more music you could take out of your computer. ... With something like this device, an hour's worth of music was just about right for most people at that time." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fhq9WVeiE_U
