# label - Island Records

## curiosities
**Acquisition by Polygram (1989)** : Island Records was bought by Polygram in 1989. Polygram eventually morphed into Universal, which is now one of the three major record labels. This acquisition transformed Island from an independent label into a major label entity. "Island was bought by Polygram in 1989, which eventually morphed into Universal, one of today's three major labels." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
**Acquisition of Stiff Records (1983)** : Island Records acquired Stiff Records in 1983. Stiff Records, known for its punk releases, was sold off due to irresponsible business practices and bleeding money. "Island Records. One more for this episode, Rough Trade. In 1976, Jeff Travis took a trip across the US buying up all kinds of records and when he got home he didn't have anywhere to put them so he decided to start up a store with his record collection acting as inventory." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
**Chris Blackwell Visit to Jamaica (1996)** : Chris Blackwell, the head of Island Records, was a passenger on Jimmy Buffett's plane, "Hemisphere Dancer," when it was mistakenly fired upon by Jamaican police at Negril airport on January 16, 1996. He was visiting his estate in Jamaica alongside Bono and his family. "Bono and his family and some friends including Jimmy Buffett and Island Records head Chris Blackwell visited Blackwell State in Jamaica." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3eGBnwPWRGo
**Chris Blackwell and Bob Marley's Final Flight** : Chris Blackwell, the head of Island Records, was asked by Bob Marley to arrange a flight home to Jamaica in his final days. "He asked his friend and head of island records Chris Blackwell to fly him home." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZhzjZixCcK8
**Distribution of Millie Small's "My Boy Lollipop"** : Island Records, a new label founded by Chris Blackwell, was responsible for distributing Millie Small's worldwide hit "My Boy Lollipop." This release was pivotal in breaking Ska music into the mainstream and reaching a global audience. "The big breakthrough was a worldwide hit by Millie Small, called My Boy Lollipop, produced by Englishman Chris Blackwell, and distributed through his brand new Idle and Records label." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V7WjNR1ujhk
**Financial Advance to My Bloody Valentine** : After the costly production of "Loveless," My Bloody Valentine signed with Island Records, which provided them with an advance of half a million dollars. This money was then used to construct a new, ultimately problematic, studio. "Then the band was signed to island records who gave them advance of half a million dollars, all of which was thrown down a rat hole constructing a new studio." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJGFUlQTUhQ
**Founding (July 4, 1959)** : Island Records was founded on July 4th, 1959, by Englishman Chris Blackwell and two partners in Jamaica. The label's name was inspired by the Harry Belafonte song "Island in the Sun," and it began with a startup capital of 100 pounds. "So on July 4th, 1959, he and two partners created Island Records, taking the name from the Harry Belafonte song Island in the Sun." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
**Independent Label Status in Early 80s** : In the early 1980s, Island Records was considered an independent label, notable for releasing music by bands such as U2, who maintained an "indiness" even as they gained popularity. "even U2, who recorded for island records, which in the early 80s was an independent label." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4
**Initial Focus (Jamaican & Caribbean Music)** : From its inception, Island Records focused on exporting Ska, Reggae, and other music from Jamaica and the Caribbean to the rest of the world. "from day one, Island Records was all about exporting Scott, Reggae and other music from Jamaica and the Caribbean." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
**Move to England & Diversification (1962)** : Although the company was based in the United States after 1962, Chris Blackwell moved back to England that same year and began signing local acts. This expanded Island's roster to include British groups like the Spencer Davis Group, Roxy Music, King Crimson, and Traffic. "That same year, Blackwell moved back to England where he started signing local acts, the Spencer Davis group, Roxy Music, King Crimson, Traffic." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dDcD8kg0wEk
**Multimedia Collaboration with Philips Media** : Island Records partnered with Philips Media to announce "Doors and Windows," a CDI (Compact Disc Interactive) disc by The Cranberries. This release was heralded as a technological breakthrough in multimedia entertainment, offering an interactive look at the band alongside music content. "Island Records and Philips Media announced a technological breakthrough in multimedia entertainment. Doors and Windows, an intimate, interactive look at one of the most successful new groups in music today." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BHkJFA0LMro
