# label - Main Man

## curiosities
**Exploitative Bowie Contract (September 1972)** : David Bowie's new contract with Main Man, signed at the end of September 1972, was later perceived as "weird" by Bowie, as it resulted in most of his album profits not reaching him despite millions in sales. This led to a bitter dispute and Bowie's desire to terminate the contract. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**Profits from Bowie Split (1975)** : As part of Bowie's 1975 split from Main Man, the company took half of Bowie's earnings from albums, dating back to *Hunky Dory* (1971) through an upcoming live album. Additionally, DeFries was guaranteed 16% of Bowie's gross earnings for seven years, through to 1982. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**Tony DeFries' Management Company** : Main Man was the management company founded by Tony DeFries. It signed several clients who orbited David Bowie, including Iggy Pop, Lou Reed, Mick Ronson, and Mott the Hoople, in addition to Bowie himself. ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
