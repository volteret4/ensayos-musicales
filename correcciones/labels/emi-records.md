# label - EMI Records

## curiosities
**"Butchering" UK Albums for North America** : The Beatles' controversial "Butcher Cover" for the 1966 album "Yesterday and Today" may have been a "dig" at EMI for how they reconfigured and edited the Beatles' UK albums for release in North America. "And third, it may have been a dig at EMI, their record label, for the way it butchered the Beatles UK albums for release in North America." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tbmssCZ05ro
**Acquisition by Terra Firma (2005)** : EMI was acquired for nearly $4 billion by Terra Firma, a private equity firm led by Guy Hans, who had no previous experience running a record label. Hans believed the label could be profitable if managed "like any other company," a philosophy that led to significant cost-cutting measures. "It's been taken over by somebody who's never owned a record company before, Guy Hans and Terra firma." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vt6DVj7S6nk
**Artist Compensation Cuts (2005)** : Under Terra Firma's ownership, EMI implemented strict cost-cutting measures, including reductions in advances paid to both new and established artists. Artists were told they would need to "work harder, and maybe for less," a policy that was poorly received by the label's creative roster. "But the belt tightening also continued to advance his pay to both new and established artists on the label. Artists were told that they had to work harder, and maybe for less." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vt6DVj7S6nk
**Company Status and Internet Impact (2005)** : In 2005, EMI Records was identified as the smallest and weakest among the major labels, struggling significantly under the disruptive influence of the internet and perceived mismanagement, which seemed intent on "driving the label into the ground." "They were the smallest and the weakest of the major labels. The internet was killing the company and management didn't seem to have a clue." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vt6DVj7S6nk
**Contract with Radiohead** : EMI Records held Radiohead's original recording contract, which concluded with the release of the album "Hail to the Thief." The label "really, really wanted to resign the band" but Radiohead was dissatisfied with their treatment, payment, and EMI's management and ownership instability. "With the release of Hail to the Thief, Radiohead's obligations to EMI came to an end." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JUvDp3_RBH0
**Copyright Stance on Back Catalog** : EMI staunchly refused to concede any portion of copyright control over Radiohead's back catalog. Under the copyright law of the day, the label insisted on maintaining control of their six Radiohead albums for the standard 50 years, contributing to an irreconcilable deadlock with the band. "Under the copyright law of the day, they insisted on maintaining control of those six radiohead albums for the standard 50 years. EMI would not hand over even a portion of the copyright to Radiohead." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vt6DVj7S6nk
**Dropping The Sex Pistols (1977)** : In early 1977, EMI Records dropped the Sex Pistols due to their "bad behavior" before A&M Records picked them up for a brief five-day period. "After being dropped by EMI records because of their bad behavior, they were picked up by A&M records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tbmssCZ05ro
**Facilitator of Abbey Road Tours** : EMI Records connections were instrumental in arranging private tours of Abbey Road Studios, allowing access to the historic recording facility before regular work hours began. "through some on connections at EMI. I managed to get a private tour of Abbey Road Studios." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hjProJd_JrM
**Fear of Success** : When a band like Oasis achieved immense success, labels and others in the industry became "afraid to do anything that might screw things up," which led to lack of creative oversight even on albums like *Be Here Now*. "When you get to be that successful, everybody, including the record label, is afraid to do anything that might screw things up." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5q5EnZj1FwE
**Major Label Support in Chart War** : Blur's record company (implicitly EMI, as a major label) and Oasis's record label (implicitly EMI, or another major label) "pulled out all the stops" during the August 1995 chart war, mobilizing significant resources for promotion and sales. "Blur's record company and oasis record label pulled out all the stops." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5q5EnZj1FwE
**Post-Radiohead Contract Expiration Actions** : After Radiohead departed to become independent, EMI asserted its contractual rights by releasing both a box set comprising all six of Radiohead's albums recorded before "In Rainbows," as well as a greatest hits CD. "EMI decided to show them who was still bossed by releasing both a box set of all six Radiohead albums before In Rainbows as well as a greatest hit CD. That was their right. It was their contract with Radiohead." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vt6DVj7S6nk
**Potential Distribution for Irish Blink** : EMI was involved in a potential distribution deal with Cargo Records to distribute the Irish band "Blink's" records in North America, a situation that contributed to the naming conflict with the San Diego band also called Blink. "And then Cargo Records, the San Diego Blinks indie label, had a chance to sign a distribution deal with EMI to distribute the Irish Blinks records in North America." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eljIIqCu5gU
**Pre-"In Rainbows" Label** : Radiohead recorded their first seven albums with EMI. The net revenue Radiohead received from their self-released, "pay-what-you-want" "In Rainbows" album was estimated to be greater than for all seven albums released through EMI combined, highlighting a significant shift in artist-label dynamics. "Radio head estimates that the net they received for In Rainbows was bigger than for the seven albums they recorded for EMI." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-CT7yYXonIk
**Sex Pistols Contract (1976)** : In 1976, EMI Records signed the Sex Pistols, placing them on the same label as established acts like Queen. This relationship facilitated the Sex Pistols' last-minute appearance on the *Today* show when Queen cancelled, as someone from EMI placed the call to get them to ITV headquarters. "At the time, the pistols were signed to EMI and the same label as Queen." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MTDY6qT11Dc
**Sex Pistols Contract and Dropping** : The Sex Pistols secured a deal with EMI Records through Malcolm McLaren, but the contract was short-lived. The band was dropped due to their controversial nature and was paid to "go away." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PzyvXmDKFUc
**State of Flux** : Around the mid-2000s, EMI was "in a great state of flux when it came to management and ownership," contributing to Radiohead's reluctance to renew their contract and influencing their decision to explore independent options. "admittedly, EMI was in a great state of flux when it came to management and ownership." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JUvDp3_RBH0
