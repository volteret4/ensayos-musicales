**Rejection of Personal Music Player (1972)** : In 1972, Gamahaw, a major manufacturer of consumer electronics, was among several companies that dismissed Andreas Pavel's invention, the Stereo Belt. They rejected the concept of a small, portable cassette player used with headphones, believing there was no market for such a device. "He took his invention to a number of people.
