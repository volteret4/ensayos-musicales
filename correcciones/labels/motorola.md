# label - Motorola

## curiosities
**Pioneering Car Radios (1930s)** : Founded by the Galvin brothers, Motorola played a pivotal role in popularizing car radios. They installed a prototype in a 1930s Studebaker. Their 5T71 model, offered as an option, cost $130 (the equivalent of about $2,000 today). Its success in enabling radio listening away from home led to rapid competition, and by the mid-1940s, 10 million cars were equipped with radios, ushering in the era of mobile music. `"It took the Galvan brothers and their new company called Motorola to really get things moving."` ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OuFvfZJKcpc
**Rocker Phone and iTunes Integration (2005)** : Motorola launched the Rocker phone in September 2005, making it the first mobile device capable of connecting directly to iTunes for downloading and playing songs via an embedded iTunes music player. While it served as a "nice proof of concept," its limited capacity (approximately 100 songs) and slow music transfer speeds rendered it largely impractical for users, leading to its eventual discontinuation by the summer of 2009. "In September of that same year, Motorola released a model called the Rocker. This was the first phone that you could hook up to iTunes and download songs, and then play the back with the iTunes music player embedded in the phone. It was a nice proof of concept, but really not all that useful. The Rocker could only hold about 100 songs, and transferring music to it was painfully slow. A number of iterations were in production as late as the summer of 2009, but not what you'd call a success." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fhq9WVeiE_U
