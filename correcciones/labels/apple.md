**iPod Naming Origin** : The name "iPod" was conceived by Vetti Chico, a freelance copywriter hired by Apple for the device's marketing. Inspired by the movie "2001: A Space Odyssey," Chico noted the player's resemblance to the white 'pods' used by astronauts and its function as a device that could connect to, yet operate independently from, a computer. "Vinnie was a fan of the movie 2001 Space Odyssey, and he thought that the all-white player looked a lot like the pods used by the astronauts of the spaceship Discovery for work outside the spacecraft. Another thing struck Vinnie. The new device was something that could be connected to and then operate independent from a computer, just like 2001's pods. And so it came to pass that this new device was christened the iPod." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fhq9WVeiE_U

**iPod Trademark Disputes** : Apple encountered several legal challenges regarding the "iPod" name.
