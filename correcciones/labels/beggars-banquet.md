# label - Beggars Banquet

## curiosities
**Founding and Longevity** : Beggars Banquet was established in 1977, inspired by the DIY ethos of the original punk rock movement. Unlike many of its contemporary indie labels, Beggars Banquet notably "never, ever went out of business," maintaining continuous operation. "The Beggars banquet provided another key ingredient to the indie record culture in Britain in the 80s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zB5zxycrbnY
**Pioneering Independent Label** : Beggars Banquet was listed as one of the small independent labels that worked outside the major label system during the rise of indie music. "that included rough trade for a D, beggars banquet, epitaph, SST, and many others." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LgJ7ww2AgF4
**Roster Strategy for Success** : Beggars Banquet implemented a carefully curated roster strategy, balancing "fringy stuff" with commercially successful groups to ensure its long-term viability. This approach meant for every artist like The Lurkers or Bauhaus, there was a Gary Numan or The Cult providing financial stability. "They maintained a carefully groomed roster, balancing out the fringy stuff by being just successful enough with a few groups." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zB5zxycrbnY
**Support for Gothic Bands** : Beggars Banquet was described as one of the "small nimble indie labels" that regularly appeared in post-punk England, signing artists like Bauhaus and releasing their debut single, "Bela Lugosi's Dead." "Bauhaus and Bella Logosi's dead, at least on beggars banquet, another one of those small nimble indie labels that popped up almost daily in post-punk England." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9uTORLOm5Yg
