# label - Mystic Records

## curiosities
**Connection to Skaters** : Moody connected with the young skaters, who often arrived at his studio on their skateboards as their primary mode of transportation, appreciating their "ratti clothes, their secondhand instruments, and angsty attitudes." "He identified with these kids and their ratti clothes, their secondhand instruments, and angsty attitudes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Formation and Mission (Early 1980s)** : Doug Moody founded Mystic Records in the early 1980s, based in California, with the goal of releasing numerous singles, albums, and compilations featuring groups closely associated with skateboard culture. "Doug Moody formed a label called Mystic Records in the early 1980s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Founder's Background** : Doug Moody, an Englishman, began his career in the music industry during the 1950s and 60s by recording R&B and Soul bands. "Moody, an Englishman who started in the industry recording R&B in Soul Bands in the 50s and 60s." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Notable Bands** : Bands that released material through Mystic Records include No FX, Black Flag, The Minutemen, Bad Religion, and Suicidal Tendencies. "Bands to pass through Mystic Records include No Effects, Black Flag, The Minutemen, Bad Religion, and here's these guys again, suicidal tendencies." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Prolific Recording** : Moody estimated that he recorded around 500 bands over a period of about six years for Mystic Records. "estimates that he recorded somewhere around 500 bands over about six years." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
**Scene Coalescence through Compilations** : The compilations released by Mystic Records were instrumental in helping the emerging skate punk scene to coalesce and gain structure. "And these compilations went a long way to helping the scene coalesce." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44dXy0StjIQ
