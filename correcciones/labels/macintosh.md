## curiosities
**Tube Amplifiers in the Wall of Sound (1973)** : Macintosh tube amplifiers were a critical power source for The Grateful Dead's "Wall of Sound." These amplifiers were responsible for generating approximately 26,000 watts of power, an "absolutely insane" amount of output for its era, enabling the system's immense volume and clarity.
