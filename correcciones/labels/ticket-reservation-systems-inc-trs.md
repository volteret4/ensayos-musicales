# label - Ticket Reservation Systems Inc. (TRS)

## curiosities
**Acquisition of Ticketron Name and Renaming (1969)** : In July 1969, TRS acquired the "Ticketron" name from another defunct computer ticketing company and officially rebranded itself as Ticketron, solidifying its position in the emerging market. "TRS, meanwhile, bought the name Ticketron from another company that crashed in burn trying to use computers to sell tickets. And in July 1969, TRS officially became Ticketron." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OCkov6FTliE
**Challenges and Prejudices** : Despite its innovative approach, TRS faced software glitches, printer malfunctions, and inventory management struggles. Additionally, many retail outlets were unwilling to sell rock concert tickets, viewing rock fans negatively and often placing terminals in inconvenient store locations to avoid direct interaction. "TRS did reasonably well, although there were software glitches, printers that wouldn't print and struggles with handling the inventory of tickets in the system." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OCkov6FTliE
**Early Distribution Model** : TRS installed its electronic box office terminals in banks, travel agencies, supermarkets, and department stores. These locations earned a commission on tickets sold, with the goal of attracting customers to their businesses. "At first they installed these terminals at a couple of banks and some travel agencies. Then came some supermarkets and department stores." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OCkov6FTliE
**Early Service Charges** : TRS implemented service charges, a key revenue stream to cover its operational costs, starting at 25 cents per ticket. Half of this charge was returned to the venue, a practice that set a precedent for future ticketing companies. "Oh, and men TRS did have service charges. It was 25 cents per ticket, although half of it was kicked back to the venue." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OCkov6FTliE
**Founding and Early Operations (1965)** : Founded on May 4, 1965, TRS was the first computer-controlled communication service for ticket sales, conceptualized by Harvey Dubner and funded by Edgar M. Brompton of Seagrams. Its initial aim was to sell tickets for Broadway shows remotely, using Control Data 1700 machines with a rewritten operating system. "It was called Ticket Reservation Systems Inc. or TRS. It was founded on May 4, 1965." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OCkov6FTliE
**Rivalry with Compute Ticket** : TRS engaged in a "bitter rivalry" with Compute Ticket, competing for clients such as theaters, arenas, and retail locations. Ultimately, TRS prevailed as Compute Ticket ceased operations by 1970. "TRS and Compute Ticket became really bitter rivals, fighting for clients like theaters, narinas, retail locations, and grocery stores." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OCkov6FTliE
