## curiosities
**Licensing of CD Technology (After 1970)** : A few years after Jim Russell secured the first patent for his optical recording and playback device in 1970 (which essentially was the first compact disk), Phillips, in partnership with Sony, acquired licenses to further develop this nascent technology into a marketable consumer product. "A few years later, Sony and Phillips purchased the licenses to develop the technology into consumer product." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
**Rejection of Personal Music Player (1972)** : In 1972, Phillips, a major manufacturer of consumer electronics, was among several companies that rejected Andreas Pavel's invention, the Stereo Belt. They dismissed the idea of a small, portable cassette player used with headphones, concluding that consumers would not purchase such a machine. "He took his invention to a number of people. Gamahaw, Phillips, Grundig, all big manufacturers of consumer electronics." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNiL63fY1Y8
