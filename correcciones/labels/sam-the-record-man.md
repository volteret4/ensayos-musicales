# label - Sam the Record Man

## curiosities
**Decline and Legacy (Early 2000s)** : Sam the Record Man faced significant challenges in the early 2000s, resulting in the closure of nearly all of its 140 stores, with one reportedly remaining in Belleville, Ontario. The iconic Yonge Street building was eventually demolished to make way for a Ryerson University student building, but its historically important spinning neon records were preserved, restored, and reinstalled overlooking nearby Dundas Square. "Sam the record band ran into big troubles in the early 2000s. All the locations closed safe for one." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
**Flagship Store and Iconic Neon (1959 - 1961)** : The chain's first flagship store opened at 259 Yonge Street in 1959. It later relocated to a more expansive site at 347 Yonge Street in 1961, which became a cultural landmark in Toronto. This larger store was distinguished by its prominent neon storefront featuring iconic spinning neon records, and it expanded to three floors by incorporating adjacent spaces, including a former bank and a vacated tavern. "The first flagship store opened a 259 young street in 1959, but then later moved to a cavernous place up the street at 347 young in 1961, marked by a big neon storefront with iconic spinning neon records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
**Founding and Early Stores (1920 - 1937)** : Sam the Record Man, considered Canada's equivalent to Tower Records, commenced operations in 1937. Its founder, Sam Snyderman, initiated record sales at his family's radio shop on College Street, which had been established in 1920. "Sam the record man, which started operations in 1937." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
**Product Range and Unique Offerings** : The flagship store offered a comprehensive selection of music formats, including LPs, 45s, 8-tracks, and open-reel tapes. Notably, it also housed a Chinese restaurant for a period. Sam's locations were a ubiquitous presence in malls across Canada.
