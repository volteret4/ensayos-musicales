# label - Sarah Records

## curiosities
**Bristol-based Indie Label (1987-1995)** : Sarah Records, based in Bristol, UK, was a significant indie label that operated from 1987 to 1995. Emerging from a vibrant fan scene, it joined the ranks of influential labels like Factory Records and Creation in helping to forge a distinctive identity for the British indie sound and style, particularly aligned with the C86 movement. "In the UK, it was Sarah Records." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jhgZrwtYE2g
**Focus on 7-inch Singles for Collectors** : During an era increasingly dominated by CDs, Sarah Records notably prioritized releasing the majority of its material on 7-inch singles. This strategic choice made much of their catalog and artist roster particularly appealing and interesting to music collectors, contributing to the label's unique identity. "In the air of the CD, most of what they released were on seven-inch singles, making a lot of their roster rather interesting to collectors." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jhgZrwtYE2g
**Politically Conscious Approach** : Sarah Records distinguished itself by being more politically engaged than many of its contemporaries, reflecting the socio-political climate of Margaret Thatcher's and John Major's eras, marked by widespread labor unrest. Beyond releasing compelling indie music, the label aimed for its output to gently and non-stridently guide listeners towards contemporary political and social issues, while its music typically maintained cute, girly, bratty, shy, and sweet characteristics. "But Sarah was quite a bit more political than their contemporaries." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jhgZrwtYE2g
