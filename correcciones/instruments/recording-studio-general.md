**Technological Evolution in the Early 1970s** : By the early 1970s, a four-track recording studio was considered "hideously antiquated." There was a strong demand for studios equipped with 16 and 24-track consoles, large tape recorders, and racks of machines capable of adding cool effects to music, reflecting a push for greater sonic sophistication.
