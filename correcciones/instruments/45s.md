# instrument - 45s

## curiosities
**Early Music Format and Retail Presence** : 45 RPM singles were an early music format that required specific shelving in record stores. They were sold alongside LPs, 8-tracks, and cassettes. Sam the Record Man, for example, sold 45s, and a particular 45 single by Devo was purchased for $1.49 in the late 1970s. "Since it's been a lot of money investing in shelving, first for 45s and albums, then they needed new shelving for 8 tracks and cassettes." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jD1dWxNLvfE
