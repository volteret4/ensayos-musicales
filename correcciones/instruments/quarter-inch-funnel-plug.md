## curiosities
**George Koi's Invention (1878, patented 1902)** : The quarter-inch funnel plug, widely recognized today for connecting guitars, microphones, and full-size headphones, was invented by George Koi. His innovation was spurred by the need for durable, quick-connect, and quick-disconnect plugs for central telephone exchanges. The design dates back to 1878, with the patent filed in 1902, and it forms the basic design lineage for all modern headphone plugs, including the 3.5 millimeter stereo connector.
