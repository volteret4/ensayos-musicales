# instrument - Slide Guitar

## curiosities
**African Origins (Didley Bow)** : The technique of using a slide on strings has ancient roots, with a form of it required for playing the Didley bow, a one-stringed instrument found in Africa. "In Africa, there was a one stringed instrument known as the Didley bow, which required a form of a slide." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8GU8ZpSgY8
**Evolution with Electric Guitar** : The introduction of the electric guitar further evolved slide playing, with prominent blues artists such as Muddy Waters and Elmore James adopting and popularizing the technique. "With the blues evolved and the electric guitar was introduced, playing slide evolved." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8GU8ZpSgY8
**Hawaiian Adaptation (19th Century)** : When European sailors introduced the guitar to Hawaii in the 19th century, local musicians adapted it. They would lay the guitar flat on their lap and use a piece of metal, known as a "steel," on the fretboard, which evolved into the steel guitar. "When the guitar was introduced to Hawaii in the 19th century by sailors from Europe, the locals adapted it to their own way of playing, which was laying it flat on the lap and using a piece of metal on the fretboard." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8GU8ZpSgY8
**Influence on Rock and Country** : Slide guitar, particularly as exemplified by Blind Willie Johnson, influenced countless country players and rock musicians including Rye Cooder, Brian Jones, Keith Richards, and Duane Allman. "Like that, interned influence not only a billion country players, but rockers like Rye Kooter, Brian Jones and Keith Richards of the Rolling Stones and Duane Almond." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8GU8ZpSgY8
**Mississippi Delta Blues Innovations (Early 20th Century)** : Early 20th-century blues players in the Mississippi Delta improvised slide instruments, using objects like knives or beer bottle necks when proper steels were unavailable. This raw approach significantly influenced the development of slide guitar in blues and rock music. "They used a knife on their strings and when a knife wasn't available, the neck of a beer bottle would do an pinch." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8GU8ZpSgY8
**Technique Definition** : Slide guitar is a technique where a hard, smooth object, known as a slide, is pressed against the strings on the fretboard with one hand, while the other hand plucks the strings. The slide is then moved up and down the fretboard to create a continuous, gliding effect called a glissando. "The slide is then moved up and down the fretboard to create a cool effect known as a glissando." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8GU8ZpSgY8
