# instrument - Piano

## curiosities
**Catalyst for Professional Songwriting** : The widespread popularity of the piano spurred the development of a professional songwriting class tasked with creating music for people to play at home. These compositions were among the first pop music "ditties" sold on sheet music, establishing the piano as "the musical instrument of the people" before World War I. "The piano craze spurred the development of a professional songwriting class whose job it was to come up with music that people could play in their homes. These were the first pop music diddies that were sold on sheet music and arranged specifically for the piano." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dJiSZ4X0F2g
**Enduring Importance in Songwriting** : While the electric guitar largely supplanted the piano in rock and roll performance, the piano remains a vital instrument for writing and performing across all areas of popular music. Many songs, even those ultimately recorded with different instruments, are initially composed on a standard piano. "But it is still a vital instrument for writing and performing in all areas of popular music. Songs that are ultimately performed and recorded using different instruments are often written on a standard piano." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dJiSZ4X0F2g
**Historical Accessibility and Mass Production** : For many decades, pianos were expensive, bespoke instruments largely out of reach for most people. However, by the mid-1800s, the Industrial Revolution made mass production possible, transforming the piano into an item for the masses and making it fashionable for every home. "For many, many decades, pianos were expensive bespoke instruments that were out of reach for most people. But by the middle 1800s, the Industrial Revolution had made mass production possible, and one of the items made for the masses was the piano." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dJiSZ4X0F2g
**Illegal in New York City Bars (until 1936)** : In New York City, it was illegal to play a piano in a bar until 1936. This was part of a broader "cabaret law" enacted in 1926, which aimed to crack down on speakeasies and prevent racial mixing, among other restrictions. "It was even illegal to play a radio or the piano in a bar until 1936." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aSk1XQHNkd8
**Integral to Popular Music Evolution and Various Venues** : The piano became an essential instrument not only in homes but also in musicals, movie theaters, bars, restaurants, juke joints, and even brothels. It served as the foundation upon which much of popular music evolved, becoming a ubiquitous presence in entertainment settings throughout the 20th century. "Because it seemed that everyone had a piano, it became an essential instrument, not only in homes, but in musicals and movie theaters and bars and restaurants and juke joints and even brothels. And it was on the piano that much of popular music evolved." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dJiSZ4X0F2g

**Mary Guibert's Classical Training** : Mary Guibert, Jeff Buckley's mother, was a classically trained musician who played piano. "Mom was a classically trained musician playing piano and cello." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MmXDbXI6Sqk
**Pre-Radio Role in Home Entertainment** : Before the advent of radio and recorded music, the piano served as the primary means to "summon music on demand" in homes, typically through live performance. This made the piano an essential center of home entertainment and cultural life. "Now, keep in mind that this was before radio and before any sort of recorded music. So the only way you could summon music on demand in your home was to have someone perform it, often on a piano." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dJiSZ4X0F2g
**Ray Kurzweil's Computer-Composed Piano Music (1967)** : In 1967, a 17-year-old Ray Kurzweil appeared on the CBS show "I've Got a Secret" to demonstrate a computer he had built and programmed. This machine composed a piece of music specifically for the piano, showcasing an early example of computer-generated instrumental music. "He demonstrated a piece of music for the piano that was composed by a computer that he built in programs." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QFtS8fl553Y
**Role in IMS's "Hello World" (N/A)** : The classical composition "Hello World," entirely conceived and notated by the IMS computer program, was written for piano, alongside clarinet and violin. This highlights the capacity of early AI music generation to produce scores for traditional ensembles. "It was a classical composition for clarinet, violin, and piano." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QFtS8fl553Y
**Role in Smaller Combos Post-WWII** : During World War II, as big dance bands were stripped of members due to drafting and economic constraints, smaller combos of three to five players emerged. The piano often played a key role in these groups, particularly in the development of bebop jazz in the 1940s. "During World War II, the big dance bands of the era were stripped of their members as many were drafted, maintaining a group of ten or twelve or more members became too expensive. And the big bands gave way to smaller combos of three, four or five players. In the 1940s, a form of jazz based on the abilities of the combo called the bebop developed, and such groups often had a piano player." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dJiSZ4X0F2g
**Traditional Keyboard in Rock** : The piano was identified as a traditional keyboard instrument commonly used in rock music for an extended period, contributing to the genre's foundational sounds. "For the longest time, the sounds of rock were made with voice guitars, bass, drums and keyboards like piano and organ." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PJRCC6EwUY
