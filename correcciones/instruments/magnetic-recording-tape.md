## curiosities
**Digital Signal on Magnetic Tape (Early Digital)** : The earliest digital recordings, made in Japan in 1971, still utilized magnetic tape for storage. However, the crucial innovation was that the signal stored on this tape was digital, rather than analog, representing an initial step in the transition from purely analog recording. "They still used magnetic tape, but the signal stored on the tape was digital, not analog." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r8AazBVoS7g
**Traditional Recording Medium (Pre-1990s)** : Prior to the widespread adoption of digital recording, recording studios relied heavily on large reel-to-reel tape machines using spools of magnetic recording tape, often up to two inches wide. This was considered solid technology for capturing sound but had inherent limitations compared to emerging digital methods. "Recording studios had been using big, real-to-real tape machine with huge spools of magnetic recording tape that could be up to two inches wide." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r8AazBVoS7g
