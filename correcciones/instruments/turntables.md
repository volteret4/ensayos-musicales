# instrument - Turntables

## curiosities
**DJ and Turntablist Preference** : DJs and turntableist performers preferred vinyl for its tactile nature, finding it easier to mix, scratch, and sample compared to other formats. "DJ culture and turntableist performers preferred vinyl because of its tactile nature." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GES0Ms_kaCI
**Musical Instruments for DJs** : In the 1970s, quick-handed DJs on the streets of New York transformed turntables and mixers into genuine musical instruments. Through a technique called turntableism, they could chop records into sounds and manipulate them to create entirely new music from spare parts, circumventing the need for expensive machines like the Fairlight. "Some DJs became so good that in their hands turntables and mixers were genuine musical instruments that were used to create brand new music out of a series of spare parts without having to spend $30,000 on a fair light machine." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OiqdZfxdBP0
**Preservation of Vinyl** : Turntables played a crucial role in keeping vinyl, an otherwise "dead format," alive because DJs preferred them over CDs for their ability to spin, scratch, and beat mix. "Vinyl, which was an otherwise dead format, was kept alive really only by DJs who wanted something other than CDs to spin and scratch and beat mix." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Rise in Sales (Late 90s)** : In the late 1990s, sales of turntables significantly increased and for "a year or two when turntable sales threatened to exceed that of guitars," driven by the explosion of DJ culture and the desire to become a DJ. "Sales of turntables went up. In fact there was a year or two when turntable sales threatened to exceed that of guitars because everybody wanted to be a DJ." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amHre9ZZFkU
**Sales Turnaround** : The sales of turntables, along with vinyl records, began to turn around in the latter half of the first decade of the 21st century. "Bottom line though is that sales of both vinyl records and turntables began to turn around." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GES0Ms_kaCI
**Technology Improvement (Post-2000s)** : Turntable technology has significantly improved, with a good turntable purchased today for around $500 being far superior to an equivalently priced one from 1980. "Turntable technology. It's improved. A good turntable bought today for, say, $500 is far, far superior to a turntable of equivalent value bought in 1980." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GES0Ms_kaCI
**Use in Blink 182 Recording** : For their self-titled 2003 album, Blink 182 incorporated turntables into their recording process for scratching and looping. "They used Polynesian Camel and Bals, turntables for scratching and looping, and old organs and cellos." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tkLRJiOBu0
**Use in Early Hip-hop Sampling** : Turntables became essential tools for DJs in the early days of hip-hop, who used them to loop drum breaks from funk and soul records. This technique was a precursor to digital sampling and was crucial in shaping the genre's sound. "DJs were looking for drum breaks that they could loop on their turntables, the funkier or the break, the better." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VnXBJqKwkrw
**Advanced Customization by DJ Cantar** : DJ Cantar's innovative use of turntables involved working with distinct needles and heads (cartridges) on each turntable. This customization allowed him to precisely adjust the sound to his creative intentions, contributing to his physically impossible-looking performances. "él trabajaba con distintas agujas y distintos cabezales en cada uno de los platos para ajustar mejor el sonido a lo que quería hacer." ← Music Radar Clan > 5 Momentos increíbles de Scratching experimental | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IBprDpCEn_k&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc ← turntables
**As a Sound Instrument** : The turntable is described as an instrument that, when manipulated with hands through scratching, can generate new sounds, evolving beyond its primary function into a tool for musical creation. "mover el plato con las manos y convertir el plato y también lo un instrumento para generar sonidos nuevos." ← Music Radar Clan > 5 Momentos increíbles de Scratching experimental | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IBprDpCEn_k&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc ← turntables
**Early Sampling Tool** : Before sophisticated sampling machines, the earliest forms of sampling, such as creating breakbeats, were performed manually using turntables and vinyl records. This involved physically manipulating vinyls to loop sections, laying the groundwork for more advanced sampling techniques. **Outselling Guitars (Late 1990s - Early 2000s)** : During the late 1990s and early 2000s, turntables experienced such a surge in popularity, driven by the dominance of hip-hop, dance, and electronica, that they were, at one point, outselling guitars. "turntables were outselling guitars." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q4dEV1yn3OY ← turntables
**Source of Sonic Imperfections** : The use of vinyl records as the source material for samples contributed to characteristic imperfections in 90s sample-based music, notably in DJ Shadow's *Endtroducing.....*. These imperfections included audible vinyl crackle and surface noise, which were a direct consequence of the technology and a part of the era's distinctive sound. "Este disco tiene un montón de ruidos de por ejemplo el rascado de los vinilos que os había diéxado." ← Music Radar Clan > DJ SHADOW - Endtroducing... **Two-Turntable Technique** : The use of two turntables was a fundamental innovation in early hip hop, borrowed from house DJs who perfected the technique in discos. Pioneers like DJ Kool Herk utilized this setup to extend instrumental "breaks" from funk and soul records, creating continuous loops for B-boys to dance to. This technique, which Herk called the "merry go round," allowed for the creation of entirely new musical experiences from existing records. "hip hop did borrow the two turntable technique and it used the break beats that house DJs perfected in discos." ← For the Record - The 70s > Ep. 6 - The 70s Roots of Hip Hop | https://seventies.libsyn.com/ep-6-the-70s-roots-of-hip-hop
**Yeop Barrow's Unorthodox Mastery** : Yeop Barrow, creative leader of PortiGer, is considered a "messiah of the plato" (turntable) for his unorthodox and completely new methods of working with scratching. He achieved complex sounds with just a turntable and a mixing console, challenging the perceived limits of the instrument. "el mesías del plato y sobre todo el mesías del plato por ortodoxo él inventa una manera completamente nueva de trabajar el Scratchin." ← Music Radar Clan > 5 Momentos increíbles de Scratching experimental | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IBprDpCEn_k&list=PLBFijugu0fhAAIR-Ibh59aaU8VWg-SkLc ← turntables
