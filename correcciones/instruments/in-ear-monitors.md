# instrument - In-Ear Monitors

## curiosities
**Components and Customization** : Jerry Harvey's initial in-ear monitors, developed for Alex Van Halen, were constructed using tiny components sourced from Japan, including a speaker originally designed for a heart pacemaker. These devices were custom-fitted to Alex Van Halen's ears, with wires leading to a transceiver on his belt. "He saw some tiny components from Japan, including a speaker that was originally designed for a heart pacemaker." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qrrwqdpCjpQ
**Functionality and Benefits** : Wireless in-ear monitors provide performers with their "personal mix of vocals and instrumentation" while on stage, effectively "shut[ting] out all the extraneous noise" and reducing overall volume. This protection prevents hearing damage and ensures clarity, enabling musicians to hear exactly what they need without blowing out their eardrums. "These wireless in-ear monitors shut out all the extraneous noise, tone down the volume, and allow them to hear exactly what they need to hear without blowing out their eardrums." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qrrwqdpCjpQ
**Initial Adoption Challenges and Cost (Early 2000s)** : In-ear monitors, which began appearing around 2000, typically cost between $700 and $900 a pair. Jerry Harvey faced initial difficulty convincing performers to adopt them, as musicians were concerned about the resemblance to hearing aids and the new technology. "Jerry had a hard time convincing performers that, no, no, it's not going to look like you're wearing hearing aids." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qrrwqdpCjpQ
**Integration of Crowd Noise (Post-2000)** : To address performers' complaints that in-ear monitors isolated them from the audience, modern systems incorporate "a couple of microphones on or near the stage pointing out towards the audience." These mics capture cheering and cycle it into the earpieces, allowing performers to feel the audience's energy. "One complaint was that these devices cut down on crowd noise, something that performers felt they needed to hear." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qrrwqdpCjpQ
**Invention by Jerry Harvey for Alex Van Halen (1990s)** : In-ear monitors were invented by sound engineer Jerry Harvey, who was working with Van Halen. He developed them specifically after drummer Alex Van Halen complained that the "constant on-stage volume was killing his hearing," leading to a breakthrough in musician monitoring. "The inventor was a guy named Jerry Harvey, a sound engineer who worked with Van Halen." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qrrwqdpCjpQ
