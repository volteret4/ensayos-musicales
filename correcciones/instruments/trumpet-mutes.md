# instrument - Trumpet Mutes

## curiosities
**Historical Usage (Earliest Reference 1511)** : Mutes have been in use for centuries, with the earliest known reference dating back to 1511. Their simple yet effective ability to alter an instrument's sound suggests they were likely used much earlier. "The earliest reference I could find to someone using a mute is from 1511." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8GU8ZpSgY8
**Musical Applications** : Mutes serve to help instruments blend with others in an orchestra or combo. They are widely used across various genres, including classical, Latin, jazz, and even rock music, as exemplified in Cake's song "The Distance." "A mute can be used to help the instrument blend in with other players in the orchestra or combo." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8GU8ZpSgY8
**Purpose and Materials** : Trumpet mutes are devices placed over or inside the bell of brass instruments (including trumpets, trombones, and saxophones) to mute, lower volume, or alter tonal quality. They can be made from various materials like metal, wood, plastic, or rubber, and even improvised items such as a toilet plunger can be used. "They can be made of metal, wood, plastic, and rubber. Even the end of a toilet plunger will do in a pinch." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8GU8ZpSgY8
