# instrument - 45 adapter

## curiosities
**Centering Device for 45 RPM Records** : To bridge the compatibility gap between 7-inch 45 RPM records (with their large center holes) and standard record player spindles (which were smaller, typically three-tenths of an inch wide), a "centering device for phonographic records" was developed. This plastic piece snapped into the large hole of the 45, providing a smaller central hole to fit the standard spindle. "Hutchison came up with something he described as a centering device for phonographic records. So a piece of plastic that snapped into the big hole of the 45 with a little hole in the center, which was then placed over the smaller spindle." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VlIErdSSVOM
**Multiple Inventors and the "Spider" Design** : While Thomas Hutchison is credited with conceiving the centering device for RCA, patents also point to Frank Jansen (who invented a similar device released in 1952) and a company in New York that issued something called the "Snap-It." The most famous design, resembling a three-arm swastika (known as the "spider"), was invented by James L.D. Morrison for the Voice of Music Corporation in Benton Harbor, Michigan, with a patent filed in 1951. By the 1960s, tens of millions of these adapters were in circulation. "Hutchison called his 45 adapter a spider. Now this may or may not be the truth. A search of patents lead just back to a guy named Frank Jansen who invented something that did exactly the same thing. We do know that it was released in 1952 before the RCA thing... But the most famous adapter is the one that looks kind of like a three-arm swastika... It was invented by James L.D. Morrison for the Voice of Music Corporation of Benton Harbor, Michigan, and that patent was filed in 1951." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VlIErdSSVOM
