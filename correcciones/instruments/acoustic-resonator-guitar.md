# instrument - Acoustic Resonator Guitar

## curiosities
**Non-Electric Amplification** : The acoustic resonator guitar was an innovation developed by John Dupira to make guitars louder without using electricity. It featured multiple aluminum cones affixed to the bridge, which amplified the instrument's sound in a manner similar to how large acoustic horns worked on gramophones and phonographs. These guitars gained considerable popularity. "Without getting too technical, the result was something called the acoustic resonator guitar. It wasn't powered by electricity, but it did feature a number of cones made out of aluminum that were affixed to the bridge, amplifying the instrument in the same way big acoustic horns were used on gramophones and photographs. These resonator guitars proved to be quite popular..." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=E6cXyaHALBQ
