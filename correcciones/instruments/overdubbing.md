# instrument - Overdubbing

## curiosities
**American Federation of Musicians Ban** : The reaction against overdubbing was so intense that the American Federation of Musicians issued an edict forbidding its members from participating in any recording session that used it. A major concern was that musicians called in to record instrumental backgrounds for a later-added singer would be sent home, losing income for the time the singer spent perfecting their part. "The reaction against overdubbing was so fierce that the American Federation of musicians actually put out an edict forbidding its members to participate in any recording session that used overdubbing." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L0j9iDuHQVA
**Benefits for Singers** : While controversial for musicians regarding compensation, overdubbing significantly benefited singers. It allowed them to repeatedly work on their vocal performance until it was perfect, without the pressure or cost of having all the instrumentalists waiting in the studio. "Now this was great for the singer because it meant that they could work on their performance over and over again until it was perfect without having all these musicians sitting around." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L0j9iDuHQVA
**Drawbacks for Musicians** : Overdubbing created a significant economic disadvantage for musicians. If they were called in to lay down an instrumental track and then sent home while a singer worked on their part, they were unable to charge for the singer's extended time, leading to lost income opportunities. "It was awful for the musicians because if they were sent home, they were unable to charge for the time the singer spent working on their part." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L0j9iDuHQVA
**Early Criticism as "Cheating"** : Overdubbing, pioneered by Les Paul's "sound-on-sound" technique, was met with fierce opposition from purists who considered it "cheating." They argued that music created this way "deceives the listener" and was "wrong, wrong, wrong" because one person could not replicate it live. This led to an edict from the American Federation of Musicians. "The purists were apoplectic. That's even worse cheating. This music deceives the listener. It's impossible for one man to play this live, so it is wrong, wrong, wrong." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L0j9iDuHQVA
**Modern Ubiquity** : Today, multi-tracking and overdubbing are standard practices in music recording, used in virtually all contemporary music production. This reflects a complete shift from the early purist opposition to these techniques. "Now today, multi-tracking and overdubbing is how all music is recorded, and it's not just two or four tracks." ← https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L0j9iDuHQVA
